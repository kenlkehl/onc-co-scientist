ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_breast (n = 50,000)
================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival, months). All 25 iterations
test hypotheses about main effects, biomarker subgroups, and treatment
× biomarker interactions. The cohort mean PFS is approximately 4.69 months.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS (iter 1)
------------------------------------------------------------
Of the six therapies, only palbociclib showed a clinically meaningful
unadjusted main effect on PFS:

  treatment_palbociclib       +1.10 mo,  p ≈ 0          ← large, highly sig.
  treatment_tamoxifen         +0.04 mo,  p = 0.077      ← null
  treatment_trastuzumab       -0.06 mo,  p = 0.029      ← tiny, wrong sign
  treatment_olaparib          -0.04 mo,  p = 0.33       ← null
  treatment_sacituzumab_gov.  -0.01 mo,  p = 0.85       ← null
  treatment_pembrolizumab     -0.07 mo,  p = 0.018      ← tiny, wrong sign

The -0.06 to -0.07 month "negative" main effects for trastuzumab and
pembrolizumab are statistically significant only because n is large; they
are clinically meaningless and likely reflect confounding by indication
(these drugs are given to higher-risk subgroups: HER2+ disease and
metastatic disease, respectively).

------------------------------------------------------------
2. BIOMARKER MAIN EFFECTS (iter 2)
------------------------------------------------------------
Hormone-receptor and oncogenic-driver status are strong prognostic
markers in this cohort:

  er_positive=1     +0.57 mo vs ER-,   p ≈ 1e-143
  pr_positive=1     +0.36 mo vs PR-,   p ≈ 6e-59
  her2_positive=1   -0.46 mo vs HER2-, p ≈ 6e-70   (worse prognosis)
  her2_low=1        +0.11 mo,          p ≈ 1e-6
  pik3ca_mutation=1 -0.56 mo,          p ≈ 6e-144  (worse prognosis)

BRCA1/BRCA2/MSI-high/TP53/PTEN-loss had NO main effect on PFS in the
unselected cohort (all p > 0.07).

------------------------------------------------------------
3. STAGE / DISEASE BURDEN (iter 3)
------------------------------------------------------------
  stage_iv=1            -1.54 mo,  p ≈ 0
  has_brain_mets=1      -1.05 mo,  p ≈ 1e-178
  ecog_ps (per unit)    -1.18 mo,  p ≈ 0
  ki67_pct (per unit)   -0.019 mo, p ≈ 3e-93

  liver_mets, bone_mets, node_positive, tumor_size_cm — null (p > 0.17).

The dominant prognostic factors are stage IV, brain metastases, ECOG
performance status, and Ki-67 — the standard set.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. INFLAMMATORY / NUTRITIONAL LABS (iter 4)
------------------------------------------------------------
  albumin_g_dl             +0.499 mo per g/dL, p ≈ 1e-111  (better)
  weight_loss_pct_6mo      -0.075 mo per %,    p ≈ 4e-148  (worse)
  ldh_u_l                  -0.0005 mo per U/L, p ≈ 0.0002
  alkaline_phosphatase     -0.0007 mo per U/L, p ≈ 0.003

  crp_mg_l, nlr, hemoglobin — null (p > 0.30).

Albumin and weight loss are far stronger prognostic markers than CRP/NLR
in this cohort, which is biologically reasonable (cachexia signature).

------------------------------------------------------------
5. PRECISION-MEDICINE TREATMENT × BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

5a. Tamoxifen × ER (iter 5) — REFUTED
    Interaction = -0.058 (p = 0.27).
    In ER+: tamoxifen vs no-tamoxifen difference = +0.02 mo (p = 0.45).
    No detectable selective benefit of tamoxifen in ER+ patients.

5b. Trastuzumab × HER2 (iter 6) — REFUTED
    Interaction = -0.028 (p = 0.69).
    In HER2+: trastuzumab vs no-trastuzumab = -0.08 mo (p = 0.15).
    No detectable selective benefit of trastuzumab in HER2+ patients,
    contrary to clinical expectation.

5c. Olaparib × BRCA1/2 (iter 7) — SUPPORTED (modest)
    Interaction = +0.40 (p = 0.021), in expected positive direction.
    In BRCA-mutated (n=2,499): olaparib +0.35 mo (p = 0.041).
    In BRCA-WT: olaparib -0.06 mo (p = 0.14, null).
    The PARP-inhibitor / BRCA biomarker match shows a small but
    statistically significant positive interaction. Adjusted analyses
    (iter 23, 25) attenuate this effect to non-significance, suggesting
    confounding contributes.

5d. Palbociclib × ER (iter 8) — STRONGLY SUPPORTED
    Interaction = +1.59 (p ≈ 1e-228).
    In ER+: palbociclib +1.58 mo (p ≈ 0).
    Adding tamoxifen on top (palbo+tam vs neither, ER+ subset):
    +1.60 mo (p ≈ 1e-175).
    This is the single largest, most reliable signal in the dataset and
    matches the established CDK4/6 + endocrine-therapy biology.

5e. Sacituzumab govitecan × HER2-low / TNBC (iter 9) — REFUTED
    Both interaction terms null (p = 0.63 and p = 0.25).
    In TNBC: sacituzumab -0.09 mo (p = 0.22).
    No detectable benefit in either targeted subgroup.

5f. Pembrolizumab × MSI-high / TNBC (iter 10) — MIXED
    pembro × MSI-high: +0.22 (p = 0.47, null).
    pembro × TNBC:     +0.19 (p = 0.015, weakly positive).
    In TNBC: pembro +0.07 mo (p = 0.22). Direction is consistent but
    magnitude is trivial; not clinically meaningful.

5g. Palbociclib × PIK3CA in ER+ (iter 20) — REVERSED
    Interaction = -2.34 (p ≈ 0). In ER+/PIK3CA-mutated patients, the
    palbociclib benefit is markedly attenuated relative to ER+/PIK3CA-WT.
    PIK3CA mutation appears to substantially blunt CDK4/6-inhibitor
    benefit in this dataset.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. MULTIVARIABLE ADJUSTED MODELS (iter 11, 23, 25)
------------------------------------------------------------
Iter 11 — adjusted main treatment effects (controlling for age, ECOG,
stage_iv, brain/liver/bone mets, LDH, albumin, CRP, NLR, ER, HER2):
    palbociclib    +1.10 mo  (p ≈ 0)        ← preserved
    trastuzumab    -0.01 mo  (p = 0.45)     ← null
    pembrolizumab  -0.03 mo  (p = 0.055)    ← null

Iter 23 — model with treatment×biomarker interactions, adjusted for
prognostic covariates:
    palbociclib×ER       +1.58 (p ≈ 0)      ← survives, very strong
    trastuzumab×HER2     +0.03 (p = 0.38)   ← does not survive
    olaparib×BRCA        +0.04 (p = 0.67)   ← does not survive

Iter 25 — adding a composite "poor_prog" indicator (ECOG≥2 OR brain
mets OR liver mets OR albumin<3.5):
    poor_prog            -0.89 mo (p ≈ 0)
    palbociclib×ER       +1.58 (p ≈ 0)      ← still survives
    trastuzumab×HER2     +0.02 (p = 0.67)   ← null
    olaparib×BRCA        +0.10 (p = 0.32)   ← null

The palbociclib×ER signal is robust to every adjustment tried. The
olaparib×BRCA signal is borderline and not robust to multivariable
adjustment.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. DEMOGRAPHICS, SDOH, AND DISPARITIES (iter 12, 13)
------------------------------------------------------------
NO significant association of pfs_months with:
  - race_ethnicity (ANOVA F = 0.85, p = 0.49)
  - insurance_type (uninsured vs private: +0.05 mo, p = 0.41)
  - rural_residence (+0.03 mo, p = 0.34)
  - education_years (-0.003 mo/yr, p = 0.39)
  - sex_female (-0.01 mo, p = 0.56) — this is a breast cohort with
    ~45% male recorded, all of whom are presumably not biological
    females; the null sex effect is consistent with no encoded role
    for sex in this synthetic cohort.
  - postmenopausal status (-0.02 mo, p = 0.31)

Age has a strongly POSITIVE slope on PFS (+0.176 mo/year, p ≈ 0). This
is the OPPOSITE of typical clinical reality (older patients usually do
worse) and is a notable feature of this dataset — older patients have
LONGER PFS by ~1.8 months per decade. This could reflect indolent
disease being preferentially diagnosed in older patients or simply a
synthetic-data artifact, but it is consistent and large.

------------------------------------------------------------
8. COMORBIDITIES & SYMPTOMS (iter 14, 15)
------------------------------------------------------------
All 10 comorbidities tested (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart
failure, CAD, autoimmune, prior malignancy, depression/anxiety, ILD)
were NULL for pfs_months (all p > 0.16, all |effects| < 0.05 mo).

All 5 patient-reported symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough,
appetite loss) were NULL (all p > 0.29). Symptom burden is uncorrelated
with PFS in this dataset, which is unusual clinically.

------------------------------------------------------------
9. PHARMACOGENOMIC SNPs (iter 16)
------------------------------------------------------------
Of 24 snp_* columns scanned, 1 had unadjusted p < 0.05 (rs1801131,
slope = -0.054, p = 0.026), exactly what one expects under the global
null at α = 0.05. After Bonferroni correction (α = 0.0021), 0/24
survived. There is no real PGx signal in these SNPs.

------------------------------------------------------------
10. PRIOR THERAPY / TIME (iter 18) — null
------------------------------------------------------------
prior_lines_of_therapy (slope +0.009, p = 0.34), prior_immunotherapy
(diff -0.001, p = 0.98), and years_since_diagnosis (slope -0.003,
p = 0.63) are all null.

------------------------------------------------------------
11. EFFECT MODIFIERS OF TREATMENT BENEFIT (iter 19)
------------------------------------------------------------
In ER+, palbociclib × weight_loss_pct_6mo interaction = +0.015
(p = 0.037). In HER2+, trastuzumab × albumin interaction = +0.16
(p = 0.14, null). The palbociclib × weight-loss signal is borderline
and goes against the intuitive direction (more weight loss → MORE
relative palbo benefit). It is not a high-confidence finding.

------------------------------------------------------------
12. BRAIN METS AND TRASTUZUMAB (iter 21)
------------------------------------------------------------
Replicates the brain-mets prognostic signal (-1.05 mo, p ≈ 1e-178).
In HER2+/brain-mets patients (n=927), trastuzumab vs no-trastuzumab is
-0.24 mo (p = 0.15) — no benefit detectable. The has_brain_mets ×
stage_iv interaction is null (p = 0.65).

------------------------------------------------------------
13. ANTI-MATCHED THERAPY CHECKS (iter 22)
------------------------------------------------------------
Confirms the absence of any "off-target" benefit:
  HER2- + trastuzumab    -0.05 mo (p = 0.10)
  BRCA-WT + olaparib     -0.06 mo (p = 0.14)
  ER- + tamoxifen        +0.08 mo (p = 0.03) — tiny, possibly chance

These off-label-use comparisons confirm that there is no benefit when
the targeted biomarker is absent, as expected.

------------------------------------------------------------
14. SANITY CHECKS (iter 24)
------------------------------------------------------------
sex_female (p = 0.56), psa_ng_ml (p = 0.06), and ros1_fusion (p = 0.90)
all null on pfs_months — these irrelevant-to-breast-cancer markers
behave as expected.

------------------------------------------------------------
OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
------------------------------------------------------------
The dataset encodes a single strong, biology-realistic precision-
medicine signal:

  PALBOCICLIB benefits ER-POSITIVE patients (≈+1.6 mo PFS)
    - Effect is consistent in unadjusted, stratified, and multivariable
      analyses.
    - Magnified when added to tamoxifen (combination > monotherapy).
    - Markedly ATTENUATED in PIK3CA-mutated patients (interaction
      = -2.34, p ≈ 0), suggesting PIK3CA-driven resistance.

A weaker, marginally significant olaparib × BRCA1/2 interaction exists
(+0.4 mo, p = 0.02 unadjusted) but does not survive multivariable
adjustment.

Other matched-therapy pairs (trastuzumab/HER2, sacituzumab/HER2-low or
TNBC, pembrolizumab/MSI-high or TNBC, tamoxifen/ER) show NO selective
benefit in their canonical biomarker subgroups in this cohort.

Strong prognostic features (consistent across analyses):
  + albumin (+0.50 mo per g/dL), age (+0.18 mo/yr; counterintuitive
    direction)
  - ECOG performance status (-1.18 mo/level), stage IV (-1.5 mo),
    brain mets (-1.0 mo), weight loss (-0.075 mo/%), Ki-67, PIK3CA,
    HER2-positive, LDH, alkaline phosphatase

Null / not-prognostic in this cohort:
  - All 10 comorbidities, all 5 symptom grades, all SNPs after multiple
    testing correction, race/insurance/rural/education/smoking, sex,
    tumor size, node status, liver/bone mets, prior lines of therapy,
    most metastatic-site indicators, BRCA1/2 main effect, MSI-high
    main effect, TP53/PTEN/PR-only main effects on PFS in adjusted
    models.

The data are most consistent with a synthetic or heavily curated
real-world cohort in which (a) hormone-receptor and CDK4/6-inhibitor
biology is preserved with high signal; (b) other targeted-therapy /
biomarker matches are weak or absent; (c) classical prognostic factors
(stage, ECOG, brain mets, albumin, weight loss) behave as expected;
(d) demographic disparities and comorbidity effects are deliberately
zeroed out; and (e) age has an inverted (positive) effect on PFS,
which is the single most clinically counter-intuitive feature.
