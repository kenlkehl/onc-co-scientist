ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_breast (n = 50,000; outcome: pfs_months)
================================================================

Across 20 iterations of propose-test-refine I screened main effects, biomarker
subgroup heterogeneity, multivariable adjustments, and treatment-by-biomarker
and treatment-by-clinical interactions. The dataset has a single continuous
outcome (pfs_months, mean 4.69, range 0-16.7) and a rich feature set covering
demographics, ECOG, staging, metastatic sites, biomarkers, comorbidities,
labs, vitals, six treatments, prior therapy, symptoms, social variables, and
24 SNP indicators. A final regression that includes only the variables shown
to matter explains R² = 0.858 of the variance in PFS — so the structure of the
data is largely captured below.

----------------------------------------------------------------
1. PROGNOSTIC SIGNAL — features that predict PFS regardless of treatment
----------------------------------------------------------------
Strongly supported (each independently significant at p ≪ 1e-50 in the
multivariable OLS, and replicated in the fully-untreated subset of n = 12,478):

  • age_years (+0.176 mo per year)        — note the SIGN is POSITIVE.
      Decile means rise monotonically from 1.7 mo (age 30-52) to 7.8 mo
      (age 78-90). This is opposite to the usual real-world direction for
      breast cancer; it is a clear synthetic-data signal but is the single
      most powerful PFS predictor in the cohort.
  • ecog_ps (-1.17 mo per level; means 5.64 → 4.44 → 3.29 for ECOG 0/1/2)
  • stage_iv (-1.55 mo)
  • has_brain_mets (-0.97 mo)
  • albumin_g_dl (+0.48 mo per g/dL)
  • weight_loss_pct_6mo (-0.077 mo per %)
  • ldh_u_l (small but highly significant, -0.0005 mo per U/L)

Not prognostic (effect ~0 with NS p-values, even in the untreated subset):

  • node_positive, liver_mets, bone_mets, adrenal_mets, pleural_effusion,
    pericardial_effusion, contralateral_lung_mets — none of these classical
    "bad" features predict shorter PFS in this cohort.
  • tumor_size_cm, prior_lines_of_therapy, years_since_diagnosis.
  • All 19 comorbidities tested (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart
    failure, CAD, AF, VTE, autoimmune, hepatitis B/C, HIV, prior malignancy,
    depression/anxiety, ILD).
  • All five symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite loss)
    and all five vital signs (SBP, DBP, HR, SpO2, BMI).
  • All 24 SNPs (best p = 0.026 for snp_rs1801131; nothing survives a
    Bonferroni threshold of 2.08e-3).
  • Demographics: race_ethnicity (ANOVA p = 0.49), insurance_type (p = 0.82),
    rural_residence (p = 0.34), education_years, smoking_pack_years.
  • sex_female: PFS 4.69 vs 4.70 (NS) — and notably the cohort is only 44.7%
    female despite being labelled "breast", which is anatomically implausible
    but does not affect outcomes here.
  • Genomic alterations other than ER/HER2/PIK3CA: her2_amplification,
    her2_low, met_exon14_skipping, ret/ros1/braf/ntrk/nrg1/fgfr alterations,
    cdkn2a_loss, tp53_mutation, keap1_mutation, pten_loss, msi_high. The only
    nominally directional negative signals were met_exon14 (-0.14, p=0.024)
    and fgfr (-0.15, p=0.073) — neither survives multiple-testing correction.

----------------------------------------------------------------
2. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS
----------------------------------------------------------------
Of the six treatments, ONLY palbociclib has a real, robust, biologically
patterned PFS benefit. The other five are essentially null in the cohort,
their patient-mix-driven differences are wiped out by adjustment.

  • treatment_palbociclib: +1.10 mo unadjusted (p ≪ 1e-300); strongly
    confirmed in adjusted models. n_treated = 17,428.
  • treatment_tamoxifen: +0.04 mo (p = 0.08); -0.008 (NS) adjusted.
  • treatment_trastuzumab: -0.06 mo (p = 0.03 unadjusted), -0.010 (NS) adjusted.
  • treatment_olaparib: -0.04 (NS).
  • treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan: -0.007 (NS); +0.033 borderline adjusted.
  • treatment_pembrolizumab: -0.07 (p = 0.02 unadjusted), -0.020 (NS) adjusted.

Number of concurrent treatments correlates positively with PFS
(rho = +0.079, p < 1e-69), but this trend is essentially explained by the
inclusion of palbociclib in multi-treatment patients.

----------------------------------------------------------------
3. TREATMENT × BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS — palbociclib is biology-realistic
----------------------------------------------------------------
The most striking and biologically authentic finding is the palbociclib
interaction structure, which mirrors known CDK4/6-inhibitor biology:

  • palbociclib × er_positive: +1.55 mo interaction on top of +0.83 main
    (p ≪ 1e-300). In ER-positive: +1.58 mo benefit (5.89 vs 4.31).
    In ER-negative: -0.02 (NS).
  • palbociclib × her2_positive: -1.32 mo interaction (p ≪ 1e-300).
    In HER2-: +1.35 benefit. In HER2+: -0.01 (NS).
  • palbociclib × pik3ca_mutation: -1.65 mo interaction (p ≪ 1e-300).
    Effectively abolishes palbociclib benefit in PIK3CA-mutant disease.
  • palbociclib × ki67_pct: -0.053 mo per %Ki67 (p ≪ 1e-300). Each 10%
    increase in Ki67 erodes ~0.5 mo of palbociclib benefit.
  • palbociclib benefit by Ki67 quartile (in HR+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt):
        Ki67 < 15 % : +4.69 mo (8.97 vs 4.29)
        Ki67 ≥ 15 % : +0.02 mo (4.34 vs 4.32, NS)
  • Within HR+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt/Ki67<15 the absolute benefit is very stable
    across age tertiles (+4.66 / +4.68 / +4.75 mo).
  • Palbociclib benefit is preserved across stage_iv (interaction p = 0.39)
    and brain mets (p = 0.19) — the size of the effect doesn't shrink with
    advanced disease.
  • Palbociclib + tamoxifen vs palbociclib alone in ER+: 5.90 vs 5.89 — no
    additional synergy.
  • Sex does not modify palbociclib effect (5.40 female vs 5.42 male, NS).

Crucially, when these palbociclib*biomarker interactions are included, the
apparent prognostic effects of er_positive (-0.003, NS), her2_positive
(-0.0005, NS), and pik3ca_mutation (-0.001, NS) collapse to zero. ER, HER2,
and PIK3CA are PREDICTIVE of palbociclib response, NOT prognostic — a
conclusion confirmed in the no-palbociclib subset (all three biomarker
main effects p > 0.6 there).

The OTHER expected treatment-biomarker interactions are either very weak
or absent:

  • tamoxifen × er_positive: NULL (interaction coef -0.010, p = 0.75) —
    biologically unexpected. Even within ER-positive, tamoxifen does not
    extend PFS (4.88 vs 4.86 mo, p = 0.45).
  • trastuzumab × her2_positive: NULL (+0.014, p = 0.74). In HER2+,
    trastuzumab effect is -0.08, NS. Same holds for her2_low and
    her2_amplification interactions.
  • olaparib × BRCA: directional, not significant. Pooling BRCA1/2 (n = 2,499)
    gives palbociclib-like signal in t-test (5.0 vs 4.6, p = 0.04 unadjusted),
    but in the adjusted regression olaparib*brca_any = +0.124 (p = 0.21).
    Underpowered (only 239 BRCA-pos olaparib-treated patients).
  • pembrolizumab × MSI-H: directional positive (+0.19) but NS (only
    n = 86 MSI-H pembrolizumab-treated). Pembrolizumab × TNBC, × stage_iv,
    × brain_mets, × MSI-OR-BRCA composite all NS. No subgroup found in
    which pembrolizumab works.
  • sacituzumab × HER2-low: NULL (+0.048, p = 0.27). Sacituzumab × TNBC: NULL.

----------------------------------------------------------------
4. SUPPORTED VS REFUTED HYPOTHESES
----------------------------------------------------------------
SUPPORTED (statistically significant, direction matches expectation):
  • Palbociclib has a positive main effect on PFS.
  • Palbociclib benefit is concentrated in ER+/HER2-, abolished in PIK3CA
    mutant, and decreases with rising Ki67. The biology of CDK4/6 inhibition
    is faithfully reproduced.
  • Stage IV, brain mets, ECOG, weight loss, LDH, low albumin, and high Ki67
    are prognostic for shorter PFS (in models that include palbociclib;
    Ki67's effect is mostly via palbociclib*ki67 dilution).
  • Older age strongly correlates with longer PFS (synthetic-data signal,
    but extremely robust).
  • In the BRCA1/2-pooled subgroup, olaparib shows a borderline positive
    PFS signal (+0.35 mo, p = 0.04 unadjusted).

REFUTED OR NOT SUPPORTED (effect ~0 or wrong direction):
  • Tamoxifen × ER-positive — the canonical hormone-therapy hypothesis is
    NOT supported in this dataset.
  • Trastuzumab × HER2-positive — also NOT supported.
  • Pembrolizumab in any subgroup (MSI-H, TNBC, BRCA, advanced disease).
  • Sacituzumab in TNBC or HER2-low.
  • Synergy of palbociclib + tamoxifen.
  • Race, insurance type, rural residence, education, smoking, sex,
    19 comorbidities, 5 symptom grades, 5 vital signs, 24 SNPs, and ~10
    rare genomic alterations as PFS predictors — all null.
  • Tumor size, node status, and most metastatic-site indicators (liver,
    bone, adrenal, pleural, pericardial, contralateral lung) as prognostic
    factors — all null.

----------------------------------------------------------------
5. OVERALL CONCLUSION
----------------------------------------------------------------
PFS in this cohort is governed by (a) a small set of classical clinical
prognostic features (age — positive in this dataset — ECOG, stage IV,
brain mets, albumin, weight loss, LDH) plus (b) a single
treatment-biomarker effect: palbociclib benefit in HR+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wild-type
patients, attenuated by rising Ki67. Inside the HR+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt/low-Ki67
subgroup, palbociclib roughly doubles median PFS (~9 vs ~4 months). All other
treatments — tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib, sacituzumab-govitecan,
pembrolizumab — show essentially no PFS benefit either overall or in their
canonical biomarker-defined subgroups, with the exception of a small,
borderline olaparib signal in pooled BRCA1/2-mutant patients. None of the
24 SNPs, 19 comorbidities, demographic or social variables, symptom scores,
or vital signs adds prognostic information after adjustment. A 12-term OLS
including only the variables above achieves R² = 0.858, suggesting the
data-generating process for PFS is dominated by these effects.
