ds001_breast — Analysis Summary
================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with breast-cancer-relevant features and outcome
        pfs_months (mean 4.69, SD 2.50, median 4.54). Sex distribution is
        45% female (sex_female mean = 0.4474), so this cohort includes a
        substantial male component despite a "breast" label.
Iterations executed: 25
Analyses recorded: 209

The protocol proposed natural-language hypotheses each iteration and tested
them with t-tests, ANOVAs, Pearson correlations, multivariable OLS, and
explicit interaction terms; signed effect estimates and two-sided p-values
were stored for every test (p < 0.05 used as the significance threshold).
Below is the synthesis grouped by theme.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Cohort-wide treatment main effects on PFS (iter 1)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Of the six treatments, only **palbociclib** showed a large, highly
significant cohort-wide PFS benefit:

- treatment_palbociclib: 5.71 vs 4.15 mo (Δ = +1.56, p ≈ 0)         ✓
- treatment_tamoxifen:   4.78 vs 4.65 mo (Δ = +0.13, p < 0.001)     ✓ (tiny)
- treatment_trastuzumab: 4.74 vs 4.68 mo (Δ = +0.058, p ≈ 0.04)     ✓ (tiny)
- treatment_olaparib:    4.65 vs 4.69 mo (Δ = -0.04, p ≈ 0.34)      ✗
- treatment_sacit. gov.: 4.71 vs 4.69 mo (Δ = +0.02, p ≈ 0.59)      ✗
- treatment_pembrolizumab: 4.59 vs 4.71 mo (Δ = -0.12, p < 0.001)   ✓ (negative)

Conclusion: in the unadjusted cohort, palbociclib is the dominant signal;
pembrolizumab is associated with **shorter** PFS (suggesting confounding by
indication — sicker patients receive checkpoint blockade as later-line
therapy).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Hypotheses supported vs refuted
----------------------------------------------------------------------
SUPPORTED (significant and directionally consistent with biology):
- Palbociclib improves PFS overall and especially in ER+/HER2- patients.
- Palbociclib × ER+ interaction is real and large.
- Palbociclib × PIK3CA-mutation interaction is strongly NEGATIVE
  (PIK3CA mutation abolishes palbociclib benefit).
- Stage IV, brain metastases, ECOG ≥1, weight loss, low albumin,
  high Ki-67 all shorten PFS.
- TNBC has shorter PFS; luminal-A-like phenotype has longer PFS.
- ER positivity is independently prognostic for longer PFS; HER2
  positivity is independently prognostic for shorter PFS after
  adjustment.
- Olaparib in BRCA1/2-mutated patients shows a borderline directional
  benefit (p ≈ 0.04 unadjusted; loses significance after adjustment).

REFUTED / NULL in this dataset (despite strong prior expectations):
- Tamoxifen × ER+, trastuzumab × HER2+, sacituzumab govitecan × TNBC
  or HER2-low, pembrolizumab × MSI-high — all expected interactions
  produce p > 0.05 and small or wrong-signed coefficients.
- Adding tamoxifen to palbociclib does NOT improve PFS over palbociclib
  alone in ER+/HER2-.
- Pembrolizumab is associated with SHORTER PFS in unadjusted analyses
  and remains slightly negative after adjustment — most likely a
  confounding-by-indication signal rather than a true harmful effect.
- All sociodemographic, comorbidity, symptom, and SNP analyses are null.
- Older age is paradoxically associated with LONGER PFS, which is
  inconsistent with clinical expectation and likely a feature of the
  synthetic data-generating process used to assemble this cohort.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
9. Overall conclusions
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The dominant treatment–biomarker–outcome relationship in this cohort
is the CDK4/6 axis: palbociclib produces a large PFS benefit that is
concentrated in ER-positive / HER2-negative breast cancer and is
sharply attenuated by PIK3CA mutation. Olaparib shows a small,
biologically plausible directional signal in BRCA1/2-mutated patients
that is suggestive but not robust to multivariable adjustment in the
~2,500-patient subgroup. None of the other targeted-therapy/biomarker
pairings expected from clinical practice (tamoxifen-ER, trastuzumab-
HER2, sacituzumab govitecan-TNBC/HER2-low, pembrolizumab-MSI-high)
showed a detectable benefit in this dataset. Standard prognostic
features (stage IV, brain metastases, ECOG, albumin, Ki-67, weight
loss, TNBC phenotype) behave as expected. The cohort shows no
sociodemographic, comorbidity, or pharmacogenomic effects on PFS in
the analyses performed.
