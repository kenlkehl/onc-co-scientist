ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_breast (50,000 patients; outcome: pfs_months)
=======================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
I ran 25 iterations exploring main effects, treatment-biomarker interactions,
prognostic factors, sociodemographic disparities, comorbidity associations,
pharmacogenomic SNPs, and multivariable / three-way interactions. The
outcome was pfs_months (mean 4.69 mo, SD 2.50, range 0–16.7). A 25-covariate
adjusted OLS achieved R^2 = 0.799, indicating that a small number of
clinical features explain most of the PFS variance in this cohort.

The headline finding is a clean three-way effect: treatment_palbociclib
produces a large PFS benefit (~+1.6 mo) only in patients who are BOTH
er_positive=1 AND pik3ca_mutation=0 — and the benefit is essentially zero
in any other cell. Several other commonly expected biology-driven effects
(trastuzumab→HER2+, tamoxifen→ER+, sacituzumab→TNBC) were NOT observed.


HYPOTHESES THAT WERE SUPPORTED
------------------------------

1. Palbociclib main effect (Iter 1, h1_palbo_main)
   • +1.10 mo overall (p<1e-300). The only treatment with a clinically
     meaningful unconditional positive effect.

2. Palbociclib × ER+ interaction (Iter 2 & 10, h2_palbo_er, h10_palbo_er_adj)
   • +1.59 mo interaction coefficient (p<1e-228, robust to adjustment).
   • Palbociclib effect = +1.58 in ER+ vs essentially zero in ER−.

3. Palbociclib × ER+ × PIK3CA-WT three-way effect (Iter 15 & 16,
   h15_pik3ca_palbo, h16_threeway)
   • Three-way coefficient = −2.37 mo (p<1e-300) on the
     palbociclib:er_positive:pik3ca_mutation term.
   • Stratified means: ER+/PIK3CA-WT/palbo-treated = 6.68 mo (n=8,034) vs
     ~4.30 mo in all 7 other cells. Mechanistically consistent with PI3K
     pathway activation conferring resistance to CDK4/6 inhibition.

4. Olaparib in BRCA1/2 carriers (Iter 25, h25_subgroup_summary)
   • Within BRCA1- or BRCA2-mutated patients, olaparib delta = +0.35 mo
     (p=0.041, n_treated=239, n_untreated=2,260). Direction matches
     biology; effect is much smaller than palbociclib in ER+.

5. ECOG performance status (Iter 5, h5_ecog)
   • −1.18 mo per ECOG unit (p<1e-300). Mean PFS: ECOG 0 = 5.64,
     ECOG 1 = 4.44, ECOG 2 = 3.29.

6. Stage IV (Iter 4, h4_stage_iv)
   • −1.55 mo (p<1e-300). Strongest single binary prognostic marker.

7. Brain metastases (Iter 4, h4_brain_mets)
   • −1.05 mo (p=1.5e-178). Independent of stage IV; the two effects are
     additive (no interaction; brain mets prevalence is identical at
     10.2% in both stage strata, suggesting brain mets is encoded
     independently of stage in the data — atypical clinically).

8. Albumin (Iter 6, h6_albumin)
   • +0.50 mo per g/dL (p=9e-112). Strong nutritional/prognostic marker.

9. Weight loss in past 6 months (Iter 6, h6_weightloss)
   • −0.077 mo per percent (p=4e-148).

10. Ki67 (Iter 5, h5_ki67)
    • −0.019 mo per percentage point (p=3e-93). Higher proliferation
      worsens PFS.

11. PIK3CA mutation main effect (Iter 11, h11_pik3ca)
    • −0.56 mo (p=6e-144). Strong negative prognostic factor — and as
      shown in #3, the locus of palbociclib resistance.

12. ER+ as positive prognostic factor, HER2+ as negative prognostic
    factor (Iter 10, h10_er_main, h10_her2_main)
    • er_positive +0.55 mo (p=2e-285); her2_positive −0.47 mo
      (p=3e-166), both independent of treatment.

13. Pembrolizumab × TNBC interaction (Iter 3, h3_pembro_tnbc)
    • Statistically significant (+0.19 mo, p=0.015) but very small in
      absolute magnitude. Direction matches expected biology
      (pembrolizumab favored in TNBC) but effect size is clinically
      negligible.

14. LDH, alkaline phosphatase, total bilirubin, calcium each show small
    significant negative associations with PFS (Iter 6, h6_other_labs).


HYPOTHESES THAT WERE REFUTED
----------------------------

A. Trastuzumab × HER2+ (Iter 2 & 10, h2_trast_her2, h10_trast_her2_adj)
   • Interaction coefficient ≈ +0.01 (p=0.69–0.74). NO HER2-specific
     trastuzumab benefit detected, despite trastuzumab being a
     paradigmatic HER2-targeted agent. Within HER2+ patients,
     trastuzumab delta = −0.08 mo (p=0.15).

B. Tamoxifen × ER+ (Iter 2 & 10, h2_tamox_er, h10_tamox_postmeno)
   • No interaction (p=0.27 for ER+, p=0.65 for postmenopausal).
     Within ER+, tamoxifen delta = +0.02 mo (p=0.45).

C. Olaparib × BRCA1/2 in adjusted models (Iter 10, h10_olap_brca_any_adj)
   • Combined BRCA1/2 interaction coef = +0.06 (p=0.51) after
     adjustment. The within-subgroup direction is positive (see #4
     above), but the formal interaction test is null in adjusted models.

D. Sacituzumab × TNBC (Iter 3, h3_sg_tnbc)
   • Coef = −0.10 (p=0.25). NO TNBC-specific sacituzumab benefit.

E. Visceral metastases other than brain (Iter 4, h4_visceral_mets)
   • Liver, bone, adrenal mets, pleural and pericardial effusions,
     contralateral lung mets, and node-positive status all show
     |delta| < 0.05 mo and p > 0.16. In a real cohort, liver mets
     would substantially shorten PFS. This is likely a synthetic-data
     limitation in which only stage_iv and has_brain_mets carry signal.

F. Sociodemographic disparities (Iter 7, h7_race, h7_insurance, h7_rural)
   • No race, insurance, or rural-residence differences in PFS
     (all p > 0.24). Treatment receipt is also identical between
     sexes (Iter 17). This cohort shows no disparities of the kind
     usually observed in real-world breast-cancer registries.

G. Comorbidities (Iter 8, h8_comorbid)
   • All 11 comorbidities tested (DM, HTN, COPD, CKD, HF, CAD, AF,
     VTE, autoimmune, prior malignancy, depression/anxiety) show
     |delta| < 0.05 mo and p > 0.17. Comorbidity burden does not
     affect PFS in this cohort — atypical clinically.

H. Symptom severity scores (Iter 12, h12_symptoms)
   • Fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite loss grades all show
     no association (all p > 0.30). Patient-reported burden does not
     track with PFS here.

I. TP53 mutation main effect (Iter 11, h11_tp53)
   • Delta = +0.007, p=0.76. Surprising given TP53 is usually a
     negative prognostic marker.

J. Tumor size (Iter 5, h5_tumor_size)
   • Coef = +0.0006 mo per cm, p=0.93. NO association.

K. Pharmacogenomic SNPs (Iter 14, h14_snps)
   • Of 24 SNPs tested individually, only snp_rs1801131 (MTHFR) reached
     nominal p=0.026; would not survive Bonferroni correction. PFS in
     this cohort is essentially independent of the included SNPs.

L. Pre-treatment burden as negative prognostic (Iter 13, h13_prior_tx)
   • Prior chemotherapy, immunotherapy, lines of therapy, years since
     diagnosis: all NS. Prior surgery shows a small POSITIVE direction
     (+0.07 mo, p=0.005) — opposite of the hypothesis.

M. Ecog × stage IV interaction (Iter 20, h20_ecog_stage)
   • Significant interaction (p=0.003) but in the OPPOSITE direction
     from hypothesized: the joint decrement is sub-additive
     (coef = +0.06).

N. Albumin × palbociclib interaction (Iter 23, h23_albumin_palbo)
   • Within ER+, the palbociclib benefit is slightly LARGER at LOWER
     albumin (coef = −0.07, p=0.034) — opposite of hypothesized.


SURPRISES AND CAVEATS
---------------------

(a) Inverted age effect. age_years coefficient is +0.176 mo per year
    (p<1e-300), and mean PFS rises monotonically from 0.55 mo at age
    <40 to 9.92 mo at age ≥90. This survives adjustment for ECOG,
    stage IV, brain mets, and albumin. In real-world breast-cancer
    cohorts, very young patients have aggressive disease while very
    elderly patients have higher mortality; both ends should be
    shorter, not the elderly being best. This is the strongest signal
    that the dataset is synthetic / stylized.

(b) Sex composition. Despite being labeled a breast-cancer cohort,
    the dataset is 44.7% female and 55.3% male, with identical
    treatment-receipt rates by sex and no PFS difference. Male
    breast cancer is real but represents <1% of cases in registries.

(c) Brain mets are randomly distributed across stage IV (10.2% in
    both strata) — clinically implausible.

(d) Visceral metastases (liver, bone, adrenal) show NO PFS effect —
    clinically implausible.

(e) The dataset's only strong pharmacology signal is palbociclib in
    ER+/PIK3CA-WT. Several other targeted therapies in their canonical
    indications (trastuzumab→HER2+, tamoxifen→ER+, sacituzumab→TNBC)
    show no benefit.


OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------

In this 50,000-patient cohort, PFS is driven primarily by:
• Performance status (ECOG)
• Disease extent (stage IV, brain mets)
• Tumor biology (ER+ favorable, HER2+ unfavorable, PIK3CA mutation
  unfavorable, ki67 unfavorable)
• Nutrition / wasting (albumin, weight loss)
• Age (positive, atypical direction)

Of six targeted therapies, only treatment_palbociclib showed a strong
PFS benefit, and it was rigorously confined to the ER-positive,
PIK3CA-wildtype subgroup (≈+2.4 mo, p<1e-300 for the three-way
interaction). Olaparib showed a weaker signal in BRCA1/2 carriers
(+0.35 mo, p=0.04). All other treatment–biomarker matches expected
from drug mechanism (trastuzumab→HER2+, tamoxifen→ER+,
sacituzumab→TNBC, pembrolizumab→TNBC) were either null or trivially
small. Sociodemographic factors, comorbidities, symptom burden, and
24 of 24 pharmacogenomic SNPs showed no clinically meaningful
association with PFS.

The dominant translational message from this cohort would be:
identify ER+/PIK3CA-wildtype patients for palbociclib, since PIK3CA
mutation completely abrogates the benefit. Several signals in the
data (inverted age effect, no visceral-mets effect, no sex/race
disparities, identical treatment receipt by sex, brain mets
independent of stage) suggest the data are partly synthetic and
should be interpreted accordingly.
