================================================================================
OVERALL CONCLUSIONS (synthesized from what the data actually showed)
================================================================================

(1) Disease burden and tumor biology dominate PFS variability.
    The strongest negative prognostic signals are stage_iv (-1.54 mo), has_brain_mets
    (-1.05 mo), and ecog_ps (-1.18 mo per unit), all p<<1e-100. ER+ (+0.57 mo) and
    PR+ (+0.36 mo) are positive prognostic; HER2+ (-0.46 mo) and PIK3CA mutation
    (-0.56 mo) are negative prognostic. HER2-low carries a small (+0.11 mo) positive
    effect. BRCA1/2, TP53, postmenopausal status, and node positivity show no
    detectable main-effect difference on pfs_months in this cohort. (Iters 3, 4, 5, 13)

(2) The host-physiology axis (albumin, weight loss, calcium, alkaline phosphatase,
    LDH, the composite inflam_score) is significantly associated with PFS in the
    expected directions, but most magnitudes are modest. Notably, CRP, NLR, hemoglobin,
    and platelets did NOT reach significance individually. Albumin (+0.50 mo per g/dL)
    is the strongest single lab predictor. (Iters 6, 21)

(3) Only one drug-biomarker interaction was robust: palbociclib x er_positive.
    Joint and adjusted models put the interaction coefficient at ~+1.58 mo with
    p essentially 0. Stratified analysis confirms this is concentrated in ER+: ER+
    patients on palbociclib gain ~1.58 mo of PFS over ER+ patients not on palbociclib;
    in ER- patients the effect is null/slightly negative (-0.018, ns). The benefit
    persists in age >=75 ER+ patients (+1.50 mo, SIG). (Iters 7, 8, 15, 16, 23, 25)

(4) Olaparib x BRCA shows a weaker but consistent positive signal. The combined-BRCA
    interaction in Iter 9 is +0.40 mo (p=0.02). In the joint model (Iter 16) the
    olaparib x brca_any interaction is +0.34 mo (p=0.04). However, after adjusting
    for prognostic covariates in Iter 25, this interaction attenuates to +0.035 mo
    (ns). So the BRCA-restricted PARP-inhibitor signal here is borderline and
    confounded by prognostic features rather than robust.

(5) The other expected interactions (trastuzumab x HER2+, tamoxifen x ER+,
    sacituzumab x HER2-low, pembrolizumab x MSI-high, pembrolizumab x TNBC) are all
    null in this dataset. The hypothesis that trastuzumab's PFS benefit is concentrated
    in HER2+ patients is REFUTED here: the interaction term is essentially zero
    (coef=-0.03, p=0.69 crude; +0.03, p=0.42 adjusted). Stratified subgroup tests
    confirm trastuzumab does not extend PFS in either HER2+ or HER2- patients.
    (Iters 7, 8, 15, 16, 17, 21, 22, 25)

(6) An apparent paradox: trastuzumab and pembrolizumab show small NEGATIVE marginal
    PFS effects (Iter 1, h1.3 and h1.6), while palbociclib shows a large positive
    marginal effect (h1.2, +1.10 mo). The negative marginals are not channeling
    artifacts: Iter 2 shows the dataset has NO meaningful channeling -- biomarker
    prevalence is essentially identical between treated and untreated patients for
    every drug-biomarker pair tested. So in this cohort, treatment assignment
    appears uncorrelated with biomarker status. The negative trastuzumab/pembrolizumab
    marginals therefore plausibly reflect modest off-target burden rather than
    selection bias.

(7) Patients receiving more concurrent treatments (n_treatments) have longer PFS,
    +0.26 mo per additional drug, robust to ECOG/stage adjustment (Iter 18). This
    is consistent with either combinatorial benefit or a 'healthier-patient'
    selection effect; the available data cannot disambiguate.

(8) Triple-negative breast cancer has lower PFS than non-TNBC (-0.49 mo, p<<1e-89).
    Within TNBC, neither pembrolizumab (+0.07 mo, ns) nor sacituzumab (-0.09 mo, ns)
    showed an isolated PFS benefit, although the pembrolizumab x TNBC interaction is
    weakly positive (+0.19, p=0.015). (Iter 10)

(9) No demographic or access disparities were detectable in PFS or in receipt of
    biomarker-matched therapy. ANOVA across race_ethnicity (p=0.49), insurance_type
    (p=0.82), rural_residence (p=0.34), and education_years (p=0.39) were all null.
    Trastuzumab receipt did not differ by race in HER2+ patients (p=0.17), nor did
    olaparib receipt by insurance in BRCA carriers (p=0.64). (Iters 11, 20)

(10) Among 27 SNPs screened, the smallest p-value (snp_rs1799853) was 0.026, which
     does NOT survive Bonferroni correction (alpha=0.0019). No SNP is robustly
     associated with PFS at the strict-multiplicity threshold. (Iter 14)

(11) Surprising findings worth flagging:
     - age_years has a POSITIVE coefficient on PFS (+0.18 mo per year, p<<1e-100).
       This is the opposite of a naive 'older is sicker' expectation; in this
       breast-cancer cohort it likely reflects more indolent biology among older
       patients (e.g., enrichment of slow-growing ER+/HER2-low disease).
     - Higher ki67_pct attenuates the palbociclib benefit in ER+ (palbociclib x ki67
       interaction -0.07 per ki67%-point, p<<1e-200), implying CDK4/6 inhibition is
       most useful in low-proliferation ER+ tumors -- somewhat counter-intuitive,
       since high ki67 tumors are typically the targeting rationale, and worth
       further investigation.
     - Brain mets do not significantly modify trastuzumab benefit (3-way ns).

BOTTOM LINE: The single robust treatment-biomarker signal in this cohort is
palbociclib x er_positive. Olaparib x BRCA is a weak/borderline signal that does
not survive full adjustment. The other guideline-anchored drug-biomarker pairings
(trastuzumab/HER2+, tamoxifen/ER+, sacituzumab/HER2-low, pembrolizumab/MSI-high)
show no detectable interaction with PFS here. Disease burden (stage IV, brain mets,
ECOG) and intrinsic tumor biology (ER, HER2, PIK3CA) carry the bulk of prognostic
information.

Limitations: pfs_months is observed without censoring information; analyses use OLS
on the continuous outcome rather than Cox proportional-hazards models. With n=50,000
and many tested terms, p-values are easy to drive small for moderate effects, so
effect sizes (signed coefficients) matter more than significance per se. Interaction
terms reported here are the substantive signals rather than incidental main-effect
p-values.
