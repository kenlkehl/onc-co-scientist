ds001_breast — Analysis Summary
================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with breast cancer features and PFS (pfs_months) as
the outcome. Mean PFS in the cohort was 4.69 months (SD 2.50, range 0–16.7).
We worked through 25 iterations of hypothesis-test-refine, beginning with
clinical first principles (performance status, stage, biomarkers) and
progressively building up to multivariable adjustment and treatment-by-
biomarker interactions, plus several SDOH/comorbidity/SNP screens.

------------------------------------------------------------
Strongly supported hypotheses (effect direction + significance match clinical priors)
------------------------------------------------------------
- ECOG performance status (h1): each one-unit increase reduces mean PFS by
  ~1.18 months (univariable; p<1e-300). Adjusted coefficient -1.17 mo.
- Stage IV (h2): -1.54 mo vs non-stage-IV (p<1e-300). Adjusted -1.55 mo.
- Brain metastases (h3): -1.05 mo vs no brain mets (p<1e-180). Adjusted
  -0.99 mo.
- Albumin (h12): +0.50 mo per g/dL (p<1e-100); robust to multivariable
  adjustment (+0.48 mo per g/dL).
- LDH (h13a): higher LDH → shorter PFS, small but significant (r=-0.016,
  p≈2.5e-4); adjusted coef -0.0005 mo per U/L.
- Weight loss in 6 mo (h14c): more weight loss → shorter PFS
  (r=-0.116, p<1e-130); adjusted -0.077 mo per 1% weight loss.
- Ki67 % (h15b): higher proliferation → shorter PFS (r=-0.091, p<1e-90);
  adjusted -0.019 mo per % point.
- ER positive (h5a): +0.57 mo vs ER- (p<1e-140). Adjusted +0.53 mo.
- PR positive (h5b): +0.36 mo vs PR- (p<1e-50); attenuated to non-significant
  in the adjusted model (likely captured by ER status).
- HER2 positive main effect (h6a): -0.46 mo vs HER2- (p<1e-60); adjusted
  -0.47 mo.
- PIK3CA mutation (h18a): -0.56 mo vs WT (p<1e-140); not present in the
  base multivariable model but the unadjusted main effect is large.

------------------------------------------------------------
The biggest signal in the dataset
------------------------------------------------------------
- Palbociclib × ER-positive interaction (h10a/b, h24c, h25c) is the dominant
  treatment–biomarker pattern in this cohort. Univariable, palbociclib looks
  beneficial overall (+1.10 mo, p<1e-300), but the effect is concentrated
  almost entirely in ER+ patients (+1.58 mo, interaction p<1e-300) and
  especially ER+/HER2- patients (+1.92 mo). After adjustment for ECOG, stage,
  brain/liver mets, albumin, LDH, NLR, and age within the ER+/HER2- subgroup,
  palbociclib still adds +1.92 mo to PFS — the only treatment whose effect
  survives both stratification AND adjustment in this dataset. In ER- patients
  palbociclib has essentially no effect (-0.02 mo, p=0.63).

------------------------------------------------------------
Refuted hypotheses (direction or magnitude not supported)
------------------------------------------------------------
- Liver mets (h4a), bone mets (h4b), pleural effusion (h4c) — no detectable
  univariable effect on PFS (all p > 0.17). Liver/bone met main effects also
  null in adjusted model. Surprising given clinical expectations.
- Trastuzumab × HER2-positive interaction (h6b): no enhanced benefit in HER2+
  subgroup (interaction p=0.69). In HER2+, trastuzumab effect is -0.08 mo
  (p=0.15); in HER2-, -0.05 mo (p=0.10). Adjusted within HER2+: +0.01 mo,
  p=0.19. The expected positive trastuzumab×HER2+ interaction is absent.
- Sacituzumab × HER2-low interaction (h7b): no enhanced benefit in HER2-low
  (interaction p=0.63). Sacituzumab main effect and HER2-low interaction both
  null in this cohort.
- Tamoxifen × ER-positive interaction (h9a) and tamoxifen × postmenopausal
  (h9b): both null. Tamoxifen has no detectable effect overall, in ER+, or in
  postmenopausal patients (all subgroup and interaction p > 0.07).
- Pembrolizumab × MSI-high interaction (h11a): in MSI-high (n=498) the point
  estimate is in the expected direction (+0.14 mo) but small and not
  significant (p=0.64); the interaction p=0.47. Power-limited but the
  expected immunotherapy-MSI signature is not captured here.
- Age (h14a): hypothesized OLDER → SHORTER PFS, found OLDER → LONGER PFS
  (r ≈ +0.70, p<1e-300; adjusted +0.18 mo per year). Direction is reversed
  from clinical expectation; this is the strongest single feature-PFS
  correlation in the dataset and is robust to adjustment for biology and
  treatment, suggesting it reflects a structural feature of how the data
  were generated (likely a longer follow-up window for older patients in the
  EHR vendor extract) rather than a true biological protective effect.
- Symptoms (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, appetite loss; h16a–d): all null.
- Routine CBC counts (Hgb, platelets, ANC, ALC; h17a–d): all null univariably.
- Tumor size (h15a), CRP (h13b), NLR (h13c), BMI (h14b): null.
- Comorbidities (h21a–d): CKD, heart failure, ILD, autoimmune disease all
  show no PFS difference.
- Prior therapy / disease duration (h19a–d): prior lines, prior chemo, prior
  immunotherapy, years since diagnosis all null.
- TP53 mutation (h18b), HER2 amplification (h18c): null univariably.

------------------------------------------------------------
Partially supported / mixed
------------------------------------------------------------
- Olaparib × BRCA interaction (h8b): the unadjusted interaction was
  significant (interaction coef +0.40 mo, p=0.02; in BRCA-mut subgroup,
  olaparib +0.35 mo, p=0.04, n_tx=239). However, after adjustment for
  prognostic covariates within the BRCA-mutated subgroup, the olaparib
  coefficient attenuated to +0.07 mo (p=0.45). The signal exists at the
  interaction level but is fragile and likely confounded by other features
  in the small BRCA-mutated subset (n≈2,500). Negative-control check
  (olaparib in BRCA-WT) was correctly null/slightly negative. The clinical
  prior (olaparib benefits BRCA-mut) is directionally supported but the
  effect size is small in this dataset.

------------------------------------------------------------
Negative / SDOH / SNP screens
------------------------------------------------------------
- No univariable association of PFS with race/ethnicity (F-test p=0.49),
  insurance type (p=0.82), rural residence (p=0.34), education years
  (p=0.39), or smoking pack-years (p=0.48). The cohort shows no obvious
  socioeconomic gradient in PFS.
- 25-SNP screen (h22): smallest p among all snp_rs* features was
  snp_rs1801131 at p=0.026, well above the Bonferroni-corrected threshold
  (0.002). No SNP is associated with PFS at corrected significance.

------------------------------------------------------------
Multivariable summary (h23, n=50,000, R²=0.78)
------------------------------------------------------------
The combined OLS model identifies the following independent predictors of
pfs_months (signed coefficients, mo):
- Strongly negative: stage_iv -1.55, ecog_ps -1.17, has_brain_mets -0.99,
  her2_positive -0.47, weight_loss_pct_6mo -0.077/%, ki67_pct -0.019/%,
  ldh_u_l -0.0005/(U/L).
- Strongly positive: age_years +0.175/yr, treatment_palbociclib +1.10
  (averaged across ER status; the ER+/HER2- effect is much larger),
  er_positive +0.53, albumin_g_dl +0.48.
- Null after adjustment: tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib, sacituzumab,
  pembrolizumab (none significant as main effects); pr_positive,
  liver_mets, bone_mets, tumor_size_cm, nlr, prior_lines_of_therapy,
  her2_low.

------------------------------------------------------------
Overall conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------
1. Conventional clinical prognostic factors behave as expected in this
   cohort: higher ECOG, advanced stage, brain mets, low albumin, weight
   loss, high Ki67 all shorten PFS; ER positivity lengthens it.

2. The biomarker-stratified treatment patterns in this dataset are SELECTIVE.
   Of the six targeted/endocrine therapies represented:
     - Palbociclib in ER+/HER2- patients is a clear, large, robust
       positive effect (+1.9 mo adjusted).
     - Olaparib in BRCA-mutated patients shows the expected directional
       benefit but it is modest and not robust to adjustment.
     - Trastuzumab in HER2+, sacituzumab govitecan in HER2-low,
       pembrolizumab in MSI-high, and tamoxifen in ER+ all do NOT show
       the canonical biomarker-driven enrichment of effect — interaction
       tests are not significant and adjusted within-subgroup effects
       are null. This would be unexpected in a real-world cohort and
       most likely reflects how the synthetic / aggregated dataset was
       generated rather than a clinical-practice finding.

3. Several conventionally important features are inert in this data:
   liver/bone mets, CRP, NLR, hemoglobin, platelets, symptom grades,
   comorbidities, and prior therapy history all fail to associate with
   PFS, despite ECOG, albumin, weight loss, and Ki67 (which are
   correlated with these in real cohorts) showing strong effects.

4. Age has the strongest single feature-PFS correlation (r≈0.70) in the
   reverse of the expected direction (older = longer PFS). This survives
   adjustment for biology and treatment, indicating it is a structural
   feature of the dataset (most plausibly truncation/follow-up tied to
   EHR enrollment age) rather than a clinical signal.

5. SDOH and SNP variables show no PFS associations at conventional
   significance.

The dataset is internally consistent for prognostic features (ECOG, stage,
brain mets, albumin, weight loss, Ki67, ER/HER2 status) and for one
treatment-biomarker pairing (palbociclib in ER+/HER2-). For other
treatment-biomarker pairings, the expected interaction signatures from
practice are largely absent, which is the most clinically notable
observation from this exploration.
