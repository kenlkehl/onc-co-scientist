ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_breast (n=50,000 breast cancer patients)
Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival in months; mean 4.69, SD 2.50)

OVERALL APPROACH
Across 25 iterations I tested 97 hypotheses spanning standard prognostic factors, tumor biomarkers, treatments, predictive treatment-biomarker interactions, lab markers, comorbidities, symptoms, sociodemographics, pharmacogenomic SNPs, and multivariable models. Each hypothesis was tested with an OLS regression on PFS with reported signed coefficient and p-value. A final multivariable model achieved R^2 = 0.834.

KEY POSITIVE FINDINGS (clinically meaningful, statistically significant)

1. Standard prognostic factors behave as expected (large effect sizes):
   - ECOG PS: each unit -1.18 months PFS (p<1e-300)
   - Stage IV disease: -1.54 months PFS (p<1e-300)
   - Brain metastases: -1.05 months PFS (p~1.8e-179)
   - Higher Ki-67%: -0.019 months/% (p~3e-93)
   - Lower albumin: -0.50 months per g/dL drop (p~9e-112)
   - 6-mo weight loss: -0.075 months/% (p~4e-148)
   - Higher LDH, alkaline phosphatase, calcium: small but significant negative effects
   These are the dominant variance-explaining covariates in the multivariable model.

2. Tumor receptor / biomarker main effects:
   - ER+: +0.57 months (p~2.2e-121)
   - PR+: +0.36 months (p~8e-55)
   - HER2+: -0.46 months (p~1.2e-56) — more aggressive biology
   - HER2-low: +0.11 months (p~7e-7) — small positive
   - PIK3CA mutation: -0.56 months (p~2.4e-126) — adverse prognostic
   - BRCA1, BRCA2, TP53, tumor size: no detectable main effects
   - Triple-negative (TNBC): -0.49 months (p~5e-72)

3. THE DOMINANT TREATMENT-BIOMARKER STORY: palbociclib in ER+/PIK3CA-WT disease
   The single most important pattern in this dataset is a three-way interaction
   between ER status, PIK3CA mutation, and treatment_palbociclib:
     - ER- patients: palbociclib has NO effect (4.30 vs 4.29 months)
     - ER+/PIK3CA-WT: palbociclib raises PFS from 4.30 to 6.68 months
       (treatment effect ~+2.4 months)
     - ER+/PIK3CA-MUT: palbociclib has NO effect (4.33 vs 4.36)
   The 3-way interaction (er_positive x pik3ca_mutation x treatment_palbociclib)
   beta = -2.43, p ~ 1e-128. Within the ER+ subgroup, the PIK3CA x palbociclib
   interaction beta = -2.34, p<1e-300. In the multivariable model:
     - er_positive x palbociclib beta = +1.55 (t=74.4, p<1e-300)
     - pik3ca_mutation x palbociclib beta = -1.66 (t=-82.6, p<1e-300)
   This is consistent with known biology of CDK4/6 inhibitors being effective
   in HR+ disease, with PIK3CA-driven PI3K-pathway activation as a known
   resistance mechanism. PIK3CA mutation acts as a strong negative predictive
   biomarker for palbociclib in ER+ disease.

4. BRCA-any x olaparib (positive interaction, modest):
   Univariate analysis showed beta = +0.40, p = 0.021. Stratified means:
     - BRCA-WT: olaparib 4.65 vs no olaparib 4.70 (no benefit / slight harm)
     - BRCA-mutated: olaparib 4.93 vs no olaparib 4.59 (+0.35 mo gain)
   However, when separated into brca1 x olaparib and brca2 x olaparib in the
   adjusted multivariable model, neither component reached significance
   (beta=+0.017 p=0.87 for BRCA1; beta=-0.068 p=0.50 for BRCA2). This suggests
   the univariate combined signal may have been a chance finding and is not
   robust to careful adjustment. Direction of the univariate signal is consistent
   with the clinical mechanism but the magnitude is small and not robust.

5. TNBC x pembrolizumab: marginal positive interaction (beta=+0.19, p=0.015).
   Pembrolizumab is slightly worse in non-TNBC patients (4.81 vs 4.70) and roughly
   neutral in TNBC (4.29 vs 4.37). The interaction is statistically significant
   but small in magnitude.

6. Prior surgery: small positive association with PFS (+0.066 mo, p=0.005).

7. UNEXPECTED finding — older age associates with LONGER PFS:
   beta = +0.176 months per year of age (p<1e-300). This is in the OPPOSITE
   direction from the conventional clinical expectation. The effect is robust:
   it persists virtually unchanged after adjusting for ECOG, stage IV, brain
   mets, albumin, weight loss, and biomarkers. Age is essentially uncorrelated
   with all major prognostic covariates (|r|<0.01), so this is a primary
   association in the data, not confounding. (In a real clinical cohort one
   would suspect treatment-selection or competing-risk artifacts; in this
   synthetic dataset the effect appears built in to the data-generating process.)

KEY NEGATIVE FINDINGS (hypotheses NOT supported)

A. Several canonical predictive treatment-biomarker pairs FAILED to show interactions:
   - HER2+ x trastuzumab: no interaction (beta=-0.028, p=0.69). Stratified means
     even show slightly LOWER PFS in HER2+ patients on trastuzumab (4.25) vs
     off (4.33). This contradicts the well-established clinical effect of
     trastuzumab in HER2+ breast cancer.
   - HER2 amplification x trastuzumab: no interaction (beta=+0.04, p=0.81).
   - ER+ x tamoxifen: no interaction (beta=-0.058, p=0.27). Also no main effect
     of tamoxifen.
   - TNBC x sacituzumab govitecan: no interaction (beta=-0.10, p=0.25). Sacituzumab
     does not differentially benefit TNBC in this cohort, contrary to the
     canonical mechanism.
   - HER2-low x sacituzumab govitecan: no interaction (beta=-0.04, p=0.63).
   - Postmenopausal x tamoxifen: no interaction.
   These null findings are notable because they conflict with established
   clinical evidence; in this dataset only palbociclib (in ER+/PIK3CA-WT) and
   to a lesser extent olaparib (in BRCA-mutated, univariate only) show
   biologically expected predictive patterns.

B. Most lab markers, comorbidities, symptoms, and sociodemographic factors
   showed NO association with PFS:
   - Comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart failure, CAD, AFib,
     autoimmune disease, prior malignancy, depression/anxiety): all NS.
   - Symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite loss): all NS.
   - Race/ethnicity, insurance type, rural residence, smoking pack-years,
     years of education: all NS — no detectable sociodemographic disparities
     in PFS.
   - Metastatic site indicators beyond brain (liver, bone, adrenal, pleural,
     pericardial effusion): all NS as main effects.
   - Hemoglobin, CRP, NLR, platelets, WBC, ANC, ALC: NS.

C. Pharmacogenomic SNPs:
   - All tested SNP main effects (rs1045642 ABCB1, rs1065852 CYP2D6, rs1799853
     CYP2C9, rs429358 / rs7412 APOE, rs1801133 MTHFR) were NULL.
   - All tested SNP x treatment interactions (CYP2D6 x tamoxifen, ABCB1 x
     palbociclib, CYP2C9 x tamoxifen, CYP2C19 x pembrolizumab, TNF x
     pembrolizumab) were NULL.
   - In this dataset, the SNP columns appear to be biologically uninformative
     noise rather than carrying predictive signal.

D. Surprising direction reversals or null main effects:
   - Trastuzumab and pembrolizumab unconditional main effects are both
     mildly NEGATIVE (small but significant). These are likely confounded by
     channeling — patients selected for these aggressive therapies tend to
     have worse-prognosis disease.
   - Tamoxifen, olaparib, sacituzumab govitecan: no detectable main effect.

MULTIVARIABLE MODEL SUMMARY
A comprehensive OLS model including age, ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, albumin,
weight loss, LDH, Ki-67, ER, HER2, HER2-low, PIK3CA, all six treatments,
ER x palbociclib, PIK3CA x palbociclib, and TNBC achieved R^2 = 0.834
(adjusted R^2 = 0.834). Variance is dominated by the standard prognostic
factors (age, ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, albumin, weight loss, Ki-67) and
the palbociclib predictive interactions. After adjustment, none of the other
five treatments showed meaningful main effects, and the only interaction with
clinically meaningful magnitude was the palbociclib x ER+/PIK3CA-WT signal.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
1. Classical breast-cancer prognostic factors (ECOG PS, stage IV, brain mets,
   tumor proliferation index Ki-67, albumin, weight loss) drive PFS in this
   cohort, with effect sizes that are large and robust.
2. The dominant pharmacology signal is palbociclib's benefit confined to the
   ER+/PIK3CA-wildtype subgroup (~+2.4 months PFS), with PIK3CA mutation as a
   strong negative predictive biomarker. This biomarker-treatment relationship
   is the most clinically actionable finding in the dataset.
3. A weak BRCA-any x olaparib univariate signal exists but does not survive
   careful adjustment when split by BRCA1 vs BRCA2.
4. Several other established breast-cancer treatment-biomarker pairings
   (trastuzumab in HER2+, tamoxifen in ER+, sacituzumab in TNBC or HER2-low)
   show NO measurable predictive effect in this dataset — a notable departure
   from real-world clinical evidence, suggesting either deliberate data design
   (only palbociclib's predictive biomarker is encoded) or unobserved channeling.
5. Demographic, socioeconomic, comorbidity, symptom, and SNP variables show
   no detectable association with PFS, suggesting these were generated as
   noise/decoys.
6. An unexplained positive age-PFS association of +0.18 months/year is present
   and robust to all adjustment, contrary to clinical expectation. In a real
   cohort this would warrant investigation of treatment selection and competing
   risks; in this synthetic dataset it appears intrinsic to the data-generating
   process.
