Analysis Summary — Dataset ds001_breast (N=50,000)
====================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0.0–16.7).

This summary synthesizes 25 iterations of propose-test-refine analysis on the
50,000-patient breast cohort, walking through main effects, biomarker
modification, multivariable adjustment, and dead-end hypotheses.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Treatment main effects (Iteration 1)
------------------------------------------------------------

Six therapies were tested for unconditional PFS effects (Welch t-tests):

  treatment_palbociclib         +1.10 mo  (p<1e-300)  *** strong positive
  treatment_tamoxifen           +0.04 mo  (p=0.08)        no effect
  treatment_trastuzumab         -0.06 mo  (p=0.03)        weakly negative
  treatment_olaparib            -0.04 mo  (p=0.33)        no effect
  treatment_sacituzumab_govit.  -0.01 mo  (p=0.85)        no effect
  treatment_pembrolizumab       -0.07 mo  (p=0.02)        weakly negative

Only palbociclib shows a clinically meaningful main effect; the other five
exhibit either null or paradoxically negative point estimates.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Treatment-biomarker interactions (Iterations 2-6, 10-12)
------------------------------------------------------------

Each agent was tested in its canonical companion biomarker with adjusted
linear models (covariates: age, ECOG, stage_iv, brain_mets, albumin,
weight_loss, ki67):

  Palbociclib × ER+              beta=+1.574  p<1e-300   *** SUPPORTED
  Palbociclib × PIK3CA-mut       beta=-1.678  p<1e-300   *** SUPPORTED (negative)
  Palbociclib × ER × PIK3CA      beta=-2.397  p<1e-300   *** three-way modifier
  Tamoxifen × ER+                beta=-0.004  p=0.88         REFUTED
  Trastuzumab × HER2+            beta=+0.012  p=0.74         REFUTED
  Trastuzumab in HER2-amp        diff=-0.02   p=0.89         REFUTED
  Olaparib × BRCA1/2             beta=+0.022  p=0.81         REFUTED (univ p=0.04)
  Sacituzumab × HER2-low         beta=+0.042  p=0.28         REFUTED
  Sacituzumab in TNBC            diff=-0.09   p=0.22         REFUTED
  Pembrolizumab × MSI-H          beta=+0.200  p=0.18         not supported
  Pembrolizumab in TNBC          diff=+0.07   p=0.22         not supported

The dominant – and essentially only – pharmacological signal in this dataset
is palbociclib in ER+/PIK3CA-wild-type disease. The four-cell stratified table
makes the interaction transparent:

  ER- / PIK3CA-wt   : palbo Δ = -0.05 mo (NS)
  ER- / PIK3CA-mut  : palbo Δ = +0.04 mo (NS)
  ER+ / PIK3CA-wt   : palbo Δ = +2.38 mo (p<1e-300)   *** the entire signal
  ER+ / PIK3CA-mut  : palbo Δ = +0.04 mo (NS)

Palbociclib benefit in ER+/PIK3CA-wt patients is preserved whether or not the
patient has brain metastases (+2.38 mo without, +2.24 mo with) and whether
postmenopausal or premenopausal (+1.05 vs +1.18). Adding tamoxifen on top of
palbociclib gives no additive PFS benefit in ER+ disease.

The remaining canonical biology pairings — tamoxifen→ER+, trastuzumab→HER2+,
olaparib→BRCA1/2, sacituzumab→HER2-low/TNBC, pembrolizumab→MSI-H/TNBC — are
all refuted in this cohort. Olaparib in BRCA carriers (n=2,499) showed a
borderline univariate +0.35 mo signal (p=0.04) but the interaction term in the
adjusted model is +0.02 (p=0.81). Pembrolizumab in MSI-H (n=498) trends
positive (+0.14 to +0.20 mo) but is statistically null at this sample size.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Prognostic main effects (Iterations 7-9, 13-16)
------------------------------------------------------------

Strong negative prognostic effects (multivariable-adjusted):

  ecog_ps          beta = -1.17 mo per point   (p<1e-300)
  stage_iv         beta = -1.55 mo             (p<1e-300)
  has_brain_mets   beta = -0.98 mo             (p<1e-300)
  her2_positive    beta = -0.47 mo             (p<1e-300)
  pik3ca_mutation  beta = -0.58 mo             (p<1e-300, ER+ only -0.80)
  weight_loss_pct  beta = -0.077 mo per %      (p<1e-300)
  ki67_pct         beta = -0.019 mo per %      (p<1e-300, ER+ only)

Strong positive prognostic effects:

  age_years        beta = +0.176 mo per year   (p<1e-300)
  er_positive      beta = +0.53 mo             (p<1e-300)
  albumin_g_dl     beta = +0.48 mo per g/dL    (p<1e-300)

The age relationship is exceptionally strong (Spearman ρ=+0.726). Mean PFS
rises monotonically by decade — 30s 0.55 mo, 50s 3.04 mo, 70s 6.31 mo,
90s 9.92 mo — and the effect size persists (≈+0.18 mo/yr) under full
multivariable adjustment. PIK3CA mutation is selectively prognostic in ER+
disease (-0.80 mo; null in ER- patients), consistent with PI3K-pathway driven
endocrine resistance. High Ki-67 only matters in ER+ disease (ρ=-0.116;
ρ≈0 in ER-).

Triple-negative phenotype (ER−/PR−/HER2−, prevalence 21%) is associated with
shorter PFS (4.30 vs 4.80 mo, p=1e-89). PR positivity adds nothing beyond ER
positivity in adjusted models.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Negative findings (Iterations 17-20, 23, 24)
------------------------------------------------------------

Variables that are NOT associated with PFS in this cohort:

  - Site-specific metastases (liver, bone, adrenal, contralateral lung,
    pleural / pericardial effusion) once stage IV is accounted for: all
    p>0.15, all effect sizes <0.05 mo.
  - node_positive: diff -0.02 mo, p=0.29.
  - Comorbidity burden: diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart failure,
    CAD, AF, VTE history, autoimmune disease, depression/anxiety, ILD —
    none reach p<0.20.
  - Demographics: race_ethnicity (range across groups <0.1 mo),
    insurance_type, rural_residence — all NS.
  - Prior therapy: prior_chemotherapy, immunotherapy, targeted therapy,
    radiation — all NS. prior_surgery is +0.07 mo (p=0.005), but tiny.
    prior_lines_of_therapy ρ=+0.005 (NS).
  - SNPs (24 genotypes including rs1045642, rs1065852, rs1799853,
    rs1800566, rs2228001, rs3813867, rs4244285, rs4986893, rs1801133,
    rs1800896, rs1800629, rs2228570, rs1801131, rs429358, rs7412, rs662,
    rs2298771, rs2032582, rs1128503, rs1800470, rs1799983, rs4880,
    rs1050828, rs4363657): smallest p=0.021 (rs2228001); none survives
    Bonferroni at 24 tests.
  - Inflammatory and tumor markers: NLR, CRP, CEA, CA-125, AST, ALT,
    creatinine, platelets, hemoglobin, alkaline phosphatase, total
    bilirubin — essentially zero correlation with PFS. LDH is the only
    weak signal (ρ=-0.013, p=0.005).
  - Symptom grades (fatigue, pain_nrs, dyspnea, cough, appetite_loss):
    no meaningful correlation.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Hypothesis verdict matrix
------------------------------------------------------------

  Supported:
    - Palbociclib improves PFS overall and especially in ER+ disease.
    - PIK3CA mutation abrogates the palbociclib benefit (treatment-by-
      biomarker interaction).
    - Stage IV, brain mets, higher ECOG, higher Ki-67, higher weight loss,
      and higher PIK3CA mutation status worsen PFS.
    - ER positivity, higher albumin, and (counter-intuitively) older age
      are associated with longer PFS.
    - HER2 positivity and triple-negative phenotype are unfavorable.

  Refuted (canonical biology that did NOT replicate):
    - Tamoxifen does not improve PFS in ER+ patients.
    - Trastuzumab does not improve PFS in HER2+ patients (even in HER2-
      amplified or stage IV HER2+ subgroups).
    - Olaparib does not improve PFS in BRCA carriers in adjusted models.
    - Sacituzumab govitecan does not improve PFS in HER2-low or TNBC.
    - Pembrolizumab does not improve PFS in MSI-H or TNBC.

  Not supported by data (size or signal too small):
    - SNP main effects on PFS.
    - Comorbidity, sociodemographic, prior-therapy effects.
    - Site-specific metastasis effects beyond stage IV.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. Overall conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------

In this 50,000-patient breast cohort the dominant clinical signal is a strong
treatment-by-biomarker interaction: palbociclib confers ~+2.4 months of PFS
benefit in ER-positive, PIK3CA-wild-type patients and is essentially neutral
elsewhere. The other five targeted therapies — tamoxifen, trastuzumab,
olaparib, sacituzumab govitecan, and pembrolizumab — do not show
companion-biomarker–conditional efficacy in their canonical subgroups
(ER+, HER2+, BRCA1/2, HER2-low/TNBC, MSI-H/TNBC respectively).

Prognostic structure is dominated by stage IV, ECOG, brain metastases,
HER2 status, ER status, age, albumin, weight loss, Ki-67, and PIK3CA
mutation (the last conditional on ER+ disease). Comorbidities, prior
therapies, sociodemographics, organ-specific metastases beyond stage IV,
and the genotyped SNP panel contribute essentially no additional
discriminative information about PFS.
