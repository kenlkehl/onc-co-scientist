Analysis summary — ds001_breast (n=50,000)
==============================================

OVERVIEW
--------
This cohort labels itself as "breast cancer" patients, with progression-free survival in
months (`pfs_months`) as the sole outcome (mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0–16.7 months).
We probed disease-burden, performance status, biomarker, treatment, lab, demographic,
mutation, symptom, comorbidity, prior-therapy, and pharmacogenomic predictors of
`pfs_months` over 25 hypothesis–analysis iterations. Hypotheses progressed from main
effects, to prespecified treatment×biomarker interactions, to multivariable models with
and without interactions, ending with a compact prespecified-interaction model.

Note on cohort: 55% of the cohort is recorded as `sex_female == 0`, an unusual male
fraction for a breast cancer cohort. We tested for sex effects on PFS and found none
(p=0.56), so the rest of the analysis proceeds without sex-stratification.


CLINICAL/DEMOGRAPHIC PREDICTORS THAT MATTER
-------------------------------------------
The strongest, most consistent independent predictors of `pfs_months` were:

 * `stage_iv == 1`: -1.54 mo (univariable p~0; multivariable -1.53, p~0). Largest
   single negative driver of PFS. Confirmed in every model that included it.
 * `has_brain_mets == 1`: -1.05 mo (univariable p=1.5e-178; multivariable -1.03,
   p~0). Second-largest negative effect.
 * `ecog_ps`: monotone, dose-responsive worsening — ECOG 1 vs 0 = -1.20 mo,
   ECOG 2 vs 0 = -2.35 mo (both p~0). Effect persists after every adjustment.
   Treatment×ECOG interactions for palbociclib and trastuzumab were null,
   meaning treatment effects do not differ meaningfully by performance status.
 * `albumin_g_dl`: r = +0.10 (p=9.4e-112); after adjusting for stage IV and ECOG,
   each +1 g/dL of albumin = +0.49 mo PFS (p~0). Robust independent positive
   prognostic marker.
 * `weight_loss_pct_6mo`: r = -0.12 (p=4.1e-148); each +1% weight loss = -0.075
   mo PFS in the multivariable compact model (p~0).
 * `ki67_pct`: r = -0.09 (p=3.0e-93). Higher proliferative index = shorter PFS.
 * `ldh_u_l`: r = -0.016 (p=2.5e-4); after adjustment -0.0004 mo per U/L
   (p<0.001). Small but independent negative effect.
 * `age_years`: r = +0.70 (p~0). Older age was associated with LONGER, not
   shorter, PFS — this directly refutes the natural prior expectation that
   older patients fare worse, and is a striking feature of this cohort. The
   correlation is strong enough that it dominates many univariable comparisons
   and is likely an artifact of how the dataset was constructed.

Predictors that were essentially NULL (failed to reach significance, or too small to be
clinically meaningful):

 * Disease burden: `liver_mets`, `bone_mets` (both p>0.15 univariable; both null in
   multivariable). Counter-intuitive — visceral metastases would normally
   predict worse PFS.
 * `node_positive`: p=0.29.
 * `nlr` and `crp_mg_l` (inflammatory markers): null after adjustment.
 * `tumor_size_cm`: p=0.93.
 * Symptom burden (`fatigue_grade`, `pain_nrs`, `dyspnea_grade`, `cough_grade`,
   `appetite_loss_grade`): all p>0.29.
 * Comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, HF, CAD, AF, VTE history,
   autoimmune, depression/anxiety, prior_malignancy): every single one was
   non-significant.
 * Prior therapy lines, prior_radiation, prior_chemotherapy, prior_immunotherapy,
   prior_targeted_therapy: all null. Only `prior_surgery` reached p=0.005 with
   a tiny +0.07 mo effect.
 * Demographic disparities: race_ethnicity, insurance_type, rural_residence —
   no detectable PFS differences. There are NO measurable PFS disparities by
   these social determinants in this dataset.
 * Pharmacogenomic SNPs (24 tested): top SNP `snp_rs1801131` p=0.026; only 1 of 24
   reached p<0.05 — fully consistent with chance after multiple testing. No
   robust pharmacogenomic signal.


RECEPTOR / BIOMARKER MAIN EFFECTS
---------------------------------
 * `er_positive == 1`: +0.57 mo (p=3.7e-143); confirmed independent positive
   effect (multivariable +0.35 mo, p~0).
 * `pr_positive == 1`: +0.36 mo (p=5.8e-59).
 * `her2_positive == 1`: -0.46 mo (p=6.1e-70); multivariable -0.56 mo. HER2+
   disease independently predicts shorter PFS.
 * `her2_low == 1`: +0.11 mo (p=1.0e-6) — small but real.
 * `pik3ca_mutation == 1`: -0.56 mo (p=6.1e-144). Strong univariable signal.
 * `tnbc` (ER-/PR-/HER2-): -0.30 mo independent multivariable effect (p~0).
 * Other mutations (`tp53`, `pten_loss`, `cdkn2a_loss`, `her2_amplification`):
   all null.


TREATMENT EFFECTS — THE MAIN STORY
----------------------------------
The cohort received six labelled "breast" therapies. Their main and biomarker-stratified
effects on PFS were:

 1. Palbociclib (CDK4/6 inhibitor): UNAMBIGUOUSLY EFFECTIVE.
    * Main effect +1.10 mo (p~0).
    * `treatment_palbociclib × er_positive` interaction = +1.59 mo (p~0).
    * In ER+ patients on palbo: +1.58 mo vs not on palbo. In ER- patients:
      -0.02 mo (no effect). Benefit is fully restricted to ER+.
    * Effect persists in multivariable + interaction models (≈ +1.60 mo for
      ER+ on palbo) and is independent of ECOG, prior lines, postmenopausal
      status, and brain mets.
    * `treatment_palbociclib × postmenopausal` = -0.13 mo (p=0.004): slightly
      smaller incremental benefit in postmenopausal patients (opposite of the
      prior hypothesis direction).
    * Conclusion: HYPOTHESIS SUPPORTED — palbociclib's PFS benefit is real,
      large, and confined to ER+ disease.

 2. Olaparib (PARP inhibitor): EFFECTIVE IN BRCA CARRIERS.
    * Main effect null (-0.04 mo, p=0.33).
    * `treatment_olaparib × brca_any` interaction = +0.40 mo (p=0.021).
    * BRCA+ on olaparib: +0.35 mo vs BRCA+ off olaparib (p=0.041). BRCA-
      patients: -0.06 mo (p=0.14).
    * In the larger multivariable adjusted model the interaction loses formal
      significance (p=0.22) — the BRCA carriers are a small sample (n=2,499
      total, 239 on olaparib), so the test is power-limited.
    * Conclusion: HYPOTHESIS SUPPORTED in unadjusted analysis, qualified
      under multivariable adjustment.

 3. Pembrolizumab (PD-1 inhibitor): WEAK TNBC SIGNAL, NULL IN MSI-HIGH.
    * Main effect -0.07 mo (p=0.018), driven by confounding (more advanced
      patients receive pembro).
    * `treatment_pembrolizumab × tnbc` interaction = +0.19 mo (p=0.015).
      Direction matches expectation: incremental benefit in TNBC.
    * `treatment_pembrolizumab × msi_high` interaction = +0.22 mo (p=0.47):
      direction matches expectation but the MSI-high subgroup has only 86
      patients on pembro and is severely underpowered.
    * Conclusion: TNBC-restricted benefit weakly supported; MSI-high
      hypothesis under-powered (effectively unverified).

 4. Trastuzumab (HER2 monoclonal antibody): NO HER2-SPECIFIC PFS BENEFIT.
    * Main effect -0.06 mo (p=0.029) — small negative, likely confounded by
      HER2+ disease being a poorer-prognosis subset.
    * `treatment_trastuzumab × her2_positive` interaction = -0.03 mo
      (p=0.70). Subgroup tests: HER2+ on/off trastuzumab diff = -0.08 mo
      (p=0.15); HER2- on/off diff = -0.05 mo (p=0.10).
    * In the compact final model, the interaction is FRANKLY NEGATIVE
      (-0.46 mo, p<0.001), indicating that adjusted PFS in HER2+ patients
      receiving trastuzumab is shorter than expected.
    * The trastuzumab × has_brain_mets interaction was also null (p=0.38).
    * Conclusion: HYPOTHESIS REFUTED in this dataset — trastuzumab is NOT
      associated with longer PFS in HER2+ patients. This contradicts the
      established clinical literature and is a notable feature of this
      cohort.

 5. Sacituzumab govitecan (TROP-2 ADC): NO BENEFIT IN ANY SUBGROUP.
    * Main effect null.
    * `treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan × her2_low` interaction p=0.74.
    * `treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan × tnbc` interaction p=0.25.
    * Combined `pembrolizumab + sacituzumab_govitecan` in TNBC also showed no
      additive benefit.
    * Conclusion: HYPOTHESIS REFUTED — no detectable PFS effect in any
      examined subgroup.

 6. Tamoxifen: NO MAIN EFFECT, NO ER+ INTERACTION.
    * Main effect +0.04 mo (p=0.077).
    * `treatment_tamoxifen × er_positive` interaction = -0.06 mo (p=0.27).
    * `treatment_tamoxifen × postmenopausal` interaction p=0.39.
    * Conclusion: HYPOTHESIS REFUTED — tamoxifen shows no measurable PFS
      benefit overall or in ER+/menopausal subgroups in this cohort.


MULTIVARIABLE SYNTHESIS (ITERATIONS 20–21, 25)
----------------------------------------------
A 21-covariate multivariable OLS model recovered the same pattern: stage_iv,
has_brain_mets, ECOG, albumin, LDH, weight loss, er_positive, her2_positive, tnbc,
and palbociclib are independent, statistically significant predictors of PFS.
Adding prespecified treatment×biomarker interaction terms revealed only
`palbociclib:er_positive` as a robust positive interaction (+1.60, p~0). The other
prespecified interactions (`tamoxifen:er_positive`, `trastuzumab:her2_positive`,
`olaparib:brca_any`, `sacituzumab_govitecan:her2_low`, `pembrolizumab:msi_high`)
are non-significant or, in the case of trastuzumab:her2_positive, frankly negative.


OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. The dataset reproduces well-established prognostic features of metastatic
   breast cancer — stage IV, brain metastases, worse ECOG, low albumin, weight
   loss, high Ki-67, HER2-positivity, PIK3CA mutation, and TNBC all predict
   shorter PFS, while ER/PR positivity and HER2-low predict longer PFS.

2. Of the six labelled treatments, only PALBOCICLIB demonstrates a
   convincingly large, biomarker-targeted PFS benefit (≈ +1.6 mo in ER+, no
   effect in ER-) that holds up across every adjustment. OLAPARIB shows the
   expected BRCA-restricted benefit but in a small sample. PEMBROLIZUMAB shows
   a weak TNBC-restricted effect.

3. TRASTUZUMAB and SACITUZUMAB GOVITECAN show NO detectable benefit in any
   biomarker subgroup. This is unusual relative to clinical evidence; these
   are notable null/discrepant findings in this synthetic cohort.

4. Inflammatory markers (NLR, CRP), most comorbidities, symptom grades, and
   24 pharmacogenomic SNPs are essentially uninformative for PFS in this
   cohort. There are NO detectable PFS disparities by race/ethnicity,
   insurance, rural residence, or sex.

5. Older age is paradoxically associated with LONGER PFS (r = +0.70), which
   is the largest univariable correlation in the dataset and likely a
   structural feature of how this cohort was generated.
