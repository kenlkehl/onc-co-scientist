ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_breast (n=50,000)
Outcome: pfs_months (mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0–16.7)
Iterations: 25 / 25

OVERVIEW
========
We probed the dataset with 63 hypotheses across 25 iterations, beginning with main effects of the six therapies (palbociclib, tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib, sacituzumab govitecan, pembrolizumab), then disease-burden and biomarker prognostic factors, then formal treatment-x-biomarker interaction tests, demographic and socioeconomic effects, pharmacogenomic SNPs, and finally adjusted multivariable and synthesis analyses.

KEY FINDING #1 — Palbociclib has a large, biomarker-specific benefit (HR+/HER2-)
================================================================================
- Marginal main effect: +1.10 months PFS on palbociclib vs off (p<1e-300, iter 1).
- Effect is qualitatively confined to the HR+/HER2- subgroup: palbociclib x HR+/HER2- interaction beta=+1.82 (p<1e-300, iter 13). In HR+/HER2- patients (n=10,598 on palbo), palbociclib added +1.81 months PFS; in non-HR+/HER2- patients, the effect was -0.01 months (p=0.78).
- Palbociclib provides no benefit in HER2+ disease (iter 14: +1.35 mo in HER2-, n.s. in HER2+).
- The HR+/HER2- x palbociclib interaction survives adjustment for age, ECOG, stage IV, albumin, and Ki-67 (beta +1.82, p<1e-300, iter 19).
- In a fully adjusted multivariable model with all six treatments and 11 prognostic covariates (iter 15), palbociclib was the only treatment with a robust independent positive effect (+1.10 mo, p<1e-300). Tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib, pembrolizumab, and sacituzumab govitecan all had adjusted coefficients near zero and not significant.

KEY FINDING #2 — Olaparib selectively benefits BRCA-mutated patients
====================================================================
- Olaparib x (BRCA1 OR BRCA2) interaction: beta = +0.40, p = 0.021 (iter 6).
- BRCA-wt patients: olaparib -0.06 mo (p=0.14, no benefit).
- BRCA+ patients (n=2,499): olaparib +0.35 mo (p=0.04).
- After adjustment for age/ECOG/stage IV within BRCA+ patients (n=239 on olaparib), the conditional effect attenuates to +0.10 (p=0.26, iter 20) — directionally consistent but underpowered.

KEY FINDING #3 — Pembrolizumab effect is biomarker-modulated by TNBC
====================================================================
- Pembrolizumab marginal effect is slightly negative (-0.07 mo, p=0.018, iter 1) — likely indication confounding (assigned to worse-prognosis triple-negative patients).
- Pembrolizumab x TNBC interaction: beta = +0.19, p = 0.015 (iter 7).
- In non-TNBC, pembrolizumab is associated with shorter PFS (-0.11 mo, p=0.0017).
- In TNBC, pembrolizumab is neutral (+0.07 mo, p=0.22) — i.e., it offsets the disease confound.
- Pembrolizumab x MSI-high interaction was directionally favorable (+0.22) but n.s. (p=0.47, n=498 MSI-high), and pembrolizumab x stage IV/brain mets/PIK3CA/TP53 all n.s. (iter 18).

KEY FINDING #4 — Trastuzumab, Sacituzumab govitecan, and Tamoxifen show NO biomarker-specific PFS benefit
=========================================================================================================
- Trastuzumab x HER2+ interaction: beta = -0.03, p = 0.69 (iter 5). Subgroup tests in HER2+ patients yielded -0.08 mo (p=0.15) — no detectable trastuzumab benefit even in its canonical indication.
- Sacituzumab x TNBC interaction: beta = -0.10, p = 0.25 (iter 8). Sacituzumab x HER2-low: p = 0.63. No biomarker-conditional benefit detected.
- Tamoxifen x ER+ interaction: beta = -0.06, p = 0.27 (iter 4). The crude "matched-tamoxifen" association (+0.24 mo, p<1e-16, iter 25) is driven by the favorable PFS of ER+ disease itself rather than a tamoxifen drug effect — an important Simpson's-paradox-style observation.
- Tamoxifen x postmenopausal interaction also n.s. (iter 17, p=0.39).

KEY PROGNOSTIC FACTORS (PFS main effects)
=========================================
Adverse:
- Stage IV: -1.54 mo (p<1e-300)
- ECOG performance status: -1.18 mo per unit (p<1e-300)
- Brain metastases: -1.05 mo (p=1e-178)
- HER2+: -0.46 mo (p=6e-70)
- PIK3CA mutation: -0.56 mo (p=6e-144) — notable; this is sometimes reported as favorable in clinical literature for HR+ disease
- Higher Ki-67: -0.019 mo per % (p=3e-93)
- Higher weight loss in 6 mo: -0.075 mo per % (p=4e-148)
- Higher LDH: -0.0005 mo per U/L (p=2e-4)
- Higher calcium: -0.06 mo per mg/dL (p=0.004)

Favorable:
- ER positive: +0.57 mo (p=4e-143)
- PR positive: +0.36 mo (p=6e-59)
- HER2-low: +0.11 mo (p=1e-6)
- Higher albumin: +0.50 mo per g/dL (p=9e-112)
- Older age: +0.18 mo per year (p<1e-300) — INVERTED relative to most clinical priors. Magnitude is large; almost certainly reflects cohort/confounding (older patients enriched for indolent ER+ luminal-A disease) rather than a causal benefit of age.

NEUTRAL or null:
- Liver mets, bone mets, pleural effusion, pericardial effusion, adrenal mets, contralateral lung mets — all n.s.
- All comorbidities (DM, HTN, COPD, CKD, HF, CAD, AF, prior malignancy) — all n.s.
- Hemoglobin, CRP, NLR, tumor size, CEA, CA-125 — all n.s. or borderline-wrong-sign.
- All five symptom/PRO scales (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite loss) — all n.s. for PFS, indicating PROs in this dataset do not carry prognostic information for PFS.
- Race/ethnicity, insurance type, rural residence, education years, smoking pack-years — all n.s.
- 11 candidate pharmacogenomic SNPs — only rs1801131 reached nominal p=0.026 (slope -0.054); not significant after multiple-testing correction.

EFFECT MODIFICATION CHECKS
==========================
- Brain mets x ECOG/stage IV/albumin: no significant interactions (iter 21).
- Treatment x age: only palbociclib x age was nominally significant (beta -0.0032, p=0.044, iter 22), suggesting slight attenuation of palbociclib benefit in older patients; the other five treatments had no age interaction.
- Treatment x sex: tamoxifen and palbociclib effects did not differ by sex (iter 23). The cohort is 44.7% female, suggesting a substantial portion of male/non-female records — consistent with the data vendor schema.

CROSS-CUTTING SYNTHESIS (iter 25 — matched indicator analysis)
==============================================================
Matched-treatment indicators (treatment & canonical biomarker both true) compared vs the rest of cohort:
  matched_palbo  (HR+/HER2-, n=10,598):  +1.81 mo, p<1e-300  STRONG positive
  matched_tam    (ER+,        n=10,535): +0.24 mo, p=7e-17    appears positive but is driven by ER+ disease being favorable, not a true tamoxifen benefit (no tam x ER+ interaction)
  matched_olap   (BRCA+,         n=239): +0.24 mo, p=0.14     directional
  matched_trast  (HER2+,       n=1,890): -0.46 mo, p=5e-19    HER2+ disease confounds; trastuzumab itself null
  matched_pembro (TNBC|MSIH,   n=1,645): -0.32 mo, p=1e-8     TNBC disease confounds; pembrolizumab effect recovered only in interaction analysis
  matched_saci   (TNBC,        n=1,084): -0.48 mo, p=2e-12    TNBC disease confounds; no recoverable drug effect

Take-away: marginal "matched" comparisons are misleading whenever the matched biomarker is itself prognostic for PFS. Interaction tests are necessary and were the analysis design that uncovered olaparib (BRCA+) and pembrolizumab (TNBC) effects.

CONCLUSIONS
===========
1. Among six therapies, only treatment_palbociclib produced a clinically large, statistically robust, biomarker-specific PFS benefit, concentrated entirely in HR+/HER2- patients (+1.8 months interaction effect).
2. treatment_olaparib showed a smaller, biomarker-specific benefit selective for BRCA-mutated patients (+0.40 month interaction effect).
3. treatment_pembrolizumab showed a TNBC-specific effect (interaction +0.19 month) that offsets but does not exceed the indication confound; its main effect is slightly negative due to assignment to TNBC patients.
4. treatment_trastuzumab, treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan, and treatment_tamoxifen did NOT exhibit biomarker-conditional PFS benefits in their canonical indications in this dataset.
5. The strongest PFS predictors (in descending standardized magnitude) were stage IV, ECOG performance status, brain mets, age (positively), albumin, weight loss, ki67, PIK3CA, ER positivity, and HER2 positivity.
6. Demographics (race/ethnicity, insurance, rural residence, education, smoking), PRO symptom grades, comorbidities, several visceral mets indicators, and pharmacogenomic SNPs were broadly NOT prognostic for PFS in this cohort.
7. Methodologically, biomarker-conditional interaction analyses recovered drug benefits that simple marginal or "matched" subgroup comparisons missed (olaparib, pembrolizumab) — confirming the value of explicit interaction testing on real-world EHR-derived oncology cohorts.
