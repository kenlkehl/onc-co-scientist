ds001_breast — Iterative Analysis Summary
==========================================

Cohort: 50,000 patient records, 128 features + pfs_months outcome.
Mean PFS 4.69 months (SD 2.50, range 0.00 – 16.66). Treatments observed in
roughly 30% (tamoxifen), 35% (palbociclib), 20% (trastuzumab), 10% (olaparib),
10% (sacituzumab govitecan) and 15% (pembrolizumab) of patients. Hormone
receptor / HER2 status is intermediate prevalence; BRCA1, BRCA2, and MSI-high
are rare (~2.5%, 2.4%, 1.0%).

Across 25 iterations I ran 147 statistical tests (t-tests, Pearson
correlations, ANOVAs, OLS regressions with interaction terms, and adjusted
multivariable models). 35 of 147 reached p < 0.05. The story below
synthesizes what was supported, what was refuted, and where the data point.

------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Treatment main effects on pfs_months (Iter 1, 9, 25)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Only one treatment shows a large main effect:

* treatment_palbociclib +1.10 mo (Welch p ≈ 0; adjusted +1.10 mo, p ≈ 0).

The other treatments either show no signal or small, statistically marginal
effects that disappear under adjustment:

* treatment_tamoxifen     +0.04 mo (p=0.08; adjusted -0.003, p=0.82)
* treatment_trastuzumab   -0.06 mo (p=0.03; adjusted -0.011, p=0.42)
* treatment_pembrolizumab -0.07 mo (p=0.02; adjusted -0.023, p=0.14)
* treatment_olaparib      -0.04 mo (p=0.33)
* treatment_sacituzumab   -0.01 mo (p=0.85)

The small "harm" signals for trastuzumab and pembrolizumab are confounding —
they go away once stage, ECOG, biomarkers and labs are adjusted for. The
palbociclib effect, in contrast, is robust and persists in every model.

------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Biomarker main effects (Iter 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Strong, highly significant main effects:

* er_positive    +0.57 mo (p≈4e-143)  — supported.
* pr_positive    +0.36 mo (p≈6e-59)   — supported.
* her2_positive  -0.46 mo (p≈6e-70)   — supported, but in the *opposite*
                                         direction to a "good biomarker":
                                         HER2-positive patients have worse
                                         PFS overall in this cohort.
* her2_low       +0.11 mo (p≈1e-6)    — supported, modest.
* pik3ca_mutation -0.56 mo (p≈6e-144) — supported, large negative.

Nulls (refuted at α=0.05):

* brca1_mutation, brca2_mutation, tp53_mutation, msi_high — no detectable
  main effect on pfs_months.

------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Disease burden, performance status, prognostic labs (Iter 3, 8, 11, 21)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Classical prognostic axes are intact:

* stage_iv       -1.54 mo (p≈0)        — supported.
* has_brain_mets -1.05 mo (p≈2e-178)   — supported.
* ecog_ps        r=-0.32 (p≈0)         — supported.
* weight_loss_pct_6mo r=-0.12 (p≈0)    — supported.
* albumin_g_dl   r=+0.10 (p≈0)         — supported.
* ki67_pct       r=-0.09 (p≈0)         — supported.
* ldh_u_l        r=-0.016 (p=2e-4)     — weak but supported.

Several other signals are essentially null: liver_mets, bone_mets,
node_positive, pleural_effusion, crp_mg_l, nlr, tumor_size_cm, hemoglobin,
ast/alt, creatinine, ca_125, cea, fatigue/pain/dyspnea/cough/appetite scores,
bmi, BP, heart rate, glucose, INR. The dataset's outcome appears to be
driven by stage / ECOG / hormone-receptor / palbociclib axes rather than by
generic lab abnormalities or symptoms.

Notable counterintuitive finding: age_years correlates *positively* with
pfs_months (r=+0.70, p≈0). This is the dominant univariable predictor in the
data and runs opposite to the usual clinical expectation that older patients
do worse. It is likely a feature of how the synthetic cohort was constructed
(or a sampling/inclusion artifact); flagged here as a surprising main-effect
pattern.

------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Treatment × biomarker interactions (the core question; Iter 4, 5, 10)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Strongly supported:

* treatment_palbociclib × er_positive
  Unadjusted interaction +1.59 mo (p≈9e-229).
  Subgroup means: (palbo+, ER+) 5.89; (palbo+, ER-) 4.29; (palbo-, ER+) 4.31;
  (palbo-, ER-) 4.27. Palbociclib's benefit is essentially confined to ER+
  disease. Adjusted interaction +1.56 mo (p≈0). ER-positive predictive
  biomarker for palbociclib is the cleanest signal in the dataset.

* treatment_palbociclib × pik3ca_mutation
  Interaction -1.62 mo (p≈4e-255).
  Subgroup means: (palbo+, PIK3CA mut) 4.35; (palbo+, PIK3CA WT) 5.97;
  (palbo-, mut) 4.32; (palbo-, WT) 4.31. Palbociclib benefit is concentrated
  in PIK3CA wildtype tumors; PIK3CA-mutant tumors show no palbociclib effect.
  This is a striking negative effect modifier.

* treatment_palbociclib × ki67_pct (continuous)
  Interaction -0.052 per Ki67% unit (p≈1e-174). Higher Ki67 attenuates the
  palbociclib benefit; consistent with the PIK3CA finding that the most
  aggressive proliferative tumors gain less.

* treatment_palbociclib × postmenopausal
  Interaction -0.13 mo (p=0.004). Palbociclib benefit is somewhat smaller in
  postmenopausal patients. Modest but real.

Refuted / null (no detectable interaction):

* treatment_trastuzumab × her2_positive   (coef -0.03, p=0.70)
* treatment_olaparib    × brca1_mutation  (+0.45, p=0.07; adjusted +0.10, p=0.39)
* treatment_olaparib    × brca2_mutation  (+0.34, p=0.16; adjusted -0.02, p=0.86)
* treatment_pembrolizumab × msi_high      (+0.22, p=0.47; adjusted +0.20, p=0.17)
* treatment_sacituzumab × her2_low        (-0.04, p=0.63)
* treatment_sacituzumab × her2_positive   (+0.10, p=0.29)
* treatment_tamoxifen   × er_positive     (-0.06, p=0.27)
* treatment_tamoxifen   × pr_positive     (-0.02, p=0.73)
* treatment_pembrolizumab × tp53_mutation (-0.05, p=0.44)

The "textbook" predictive biomarker pairings (trastuzumab/HER2+,
olaparib/BRCA, pembrolizumab/MSI-H, sacituzumab/HER2-low) do NOT replicate in
this cohort. The signals are uniformly small and not statistically
distinguishable from chance.

------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Subgroup confirmations (Iter 6, 7, 16, 17, 25)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Among biomarker-positive subgroups:

* In ER+ (n=34,886), palbociclib +1.58 mo (p≈0). Adjusted in ER+/HER2-
  (n=28,589): +1.92 mo (p≈0). Strongly supported.
* In ER+ (n=34,886), tamoxifen +0.02 mo (p=0.45). Refuted.
* In HER2+ (n=9,060), trastuzumab -0.08 mo (p=0.15). Refuted.
* In MSI-high (n=498), pembrolizumab +0.14 mo (p=0.64). Refuted.
* In BRCA1/2 carriers (n=2,499), olaparib +0.35 mo (p=0.04) — nominally
  positive but small and does not survive multivariable adjustment in BRCA+
  (adjusted +0.08, p=0.37).
* In HER2-low/HER2-negative (n=18,543), sacituzumab -0.03 mo (p=0.64).
  Refuted.
* In TNBC-like (ER-/PR-/HER2-, n=10,496), pembrolizumab +0.07 mo (p=0.22).
  Refuted.
* In has_brain_mets=1 (n=5,090), trastuzumab +0.01 mo (p=0.87). Refuted.

Among biomarker-negative subgroups (specificity check):

* BRCA wildtype: olaparib -0.06 mo (p=0.14). Consistent with no specific
  benefit but no clear harm either.
* MSI-low: pembrolizumab -0.08 mo (p=0.015). A small unfavorable signal.
* HER2-negative: trastuzumab -0.05 mo (p=0.10).
* ER-negative: palbociclib -0.02 mo (p=0.63). As expected, no benefit
  outside of ER+.
* ER-negative: tamoxifen +0.08 mo (p=0.03). A small unexpected positive
  signal — surprising and not explained by ER biology.

------------------------------------------------------------------
6. Interactions with comorbidity / demographics / other axes
   (Iter 8, 13, 14, 17, 18, 20, 22, 23, 24)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Almost everything tested is null:

* sex_female, postmenopausal, tumor_size_cm — no main effect.
* All comorbidities tested (DM, HTN, CKD, HF, CAD, autoimmune, HIV, prior
  malignancy, ILD, depression/anxiety) — no main effect (all p>0.15).
* race_ethnicity ANOVA p=0.49; insurance_type ANOVA p=0.82; rural_residence
  p=0.34; education_years p=0.39; smoking_pack_years p=0.48. No social-
  determinant signal.
* Prior_chemotherapy / prior_radiation / prior_immunotherapy /
  prior_targeted_therapy: no effect. prior_surgery +0.07 mo (p=0.005),
  weak.
* treatment × ECOG, treatment × age, treatment × stage_iv,
  treatment × has_brain_mets interactions are all null in this cohort —
  i.e., we don't see treatment-effect modification by overall illness
  severity beyond what's already captured by the ER/PIK3CA story.

------------------------------------------------------------------
7. SNP screen (Iter 15)
------------------------------------------------------------------
24 snp_* variables tested for linear association with pfs_months. 1 reached
raw p<0.05 (snp_rs1801131, r=-0.010, p=0.026); none reach Bonferroni
α=2.1e-3. The germline-SNP panel does not contribute a detectable
prognostic or predictive signal in this cohort.

------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Symptoms (PROs) (Iter 12)
------------------------------------------------------------------
fatigue_grade, pain_nrs, dyspnea_grade, cough_grade, appetite_loss_grade —
all show null associations with pfs_months (|r|<0.005, p>0.29). PRO burden
in this dataset does not correlate with PFS.

------------------------------------------------------------------
9. Bottom line
------------------------------------------------------------------
The clinically meaningful structure in ds001_breast can be summarized in
four points:

(a) Hormone-receptor biology dominates the prognostic signal. ER+ and PR+
    are favorable; HER2+ is unfavorable; PIK3CA-mutant is unfavorable; high
    Ki67 is unfavorable.

(b) Disease burden / performance status retains the expected prognostic
    direction (stage_iv worse, brain mets worse, higher ECOG worse, lower
    albumin worse, more weight loss worse).

(c) The single dominant treatment–biomarker interaction is palbociclib in
    ER-positive, PIK3CA-wildtype, lower-Ki67 disease — about 1.5–1.9 months
    of additional PFS in that subset, and essentially nothing outside it.
    This pattern survives full multivariable adjustment.

(d) The other "obvious" predictive-biomarker pairings — trastuzumab/HER2+,
    olaparib/BRCA1/2, pembrolizumab/MSI-H, sacituzumab/HER2-low — are NOT
    detectable in this cohort. Either the underlying generative model did
    not encode them, or sample sizes in the relevant biomarker-positive
    subgroups (especially MSI-high, BRCA carriers) were too small to
    surface anything beyond a marginal trend in BRCA+/olaparib (which
    itself disappears under adjustment).

(e) Demographics, comorbidities, social determinants, vital signs, common
    labs, symptoms, and the SNP panel are uninformative for pfs_months in
    this dataset — a striking lack of secondary structure that itself is a
    finding.

Surprising / flag-worthy: age_years has a strong *positive* correlation
with pfs_months (r=+0.70, p≈0), opposite to clinical intuition. This
warrants investigation of the dataset's generative process before any
clinical interpretation.
