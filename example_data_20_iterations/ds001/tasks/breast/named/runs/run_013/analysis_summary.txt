Analysis Summary — ds001_breast (n = 50,000)
=============================================

Outcome: pfs_months (mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0.0–16.7).

Across 25 iterations I tested 75 hypotheses spanning host biology, disease
burden, tumor biology, treatment effects (overall, biomarker-defined, and
adjusted), demographic disparities, lab/symptom prognostics, and SNP
associations.  All p-values are two-sided.

1. Performance status and disease burden
----------------------------------------
ECOG strongly stratifies PFS: ECOG 2 patients have mean PFS 3.29 mo vs 4.94 mo
for ECOG 0–1 (Δ = −1.65 mo, p ≈ 0); ECOG 0 patients have mean PFS 5.64 mo vs
4.18 mo for ECOG ≥1 (Δ = +1.46 mo, p ≈ 0). Stage IV patients have shorter PFS
(3.62 vs 5.15 mo, Δ = −1.54, p ≈ 0). Brain mets shorten PFS (3.75 vs 4.80,
Δ = −1.05, p ≈ 1.5e-178). Notably, liver mets (Δ = +0.018, p = 0.55), bone
mets (Δ = +0.038, p = 0.17), and pleural effusion (Δ = −0.034, p = 0.28) are
NOT associated with PFS in this dataset — suggesting brain involvement is the
dominant metastatic prognostic signal rather than visceral disease per se.

2. Tumor biology
----------------
Hormone-receptor-positive disease confers longer PFS (ER+: Δ = +0.57,
p = 4e-143; PR+: Δ = +0.36, p = 6e-59). HER2+ confers shorter PFS
(Δ = −0.46, p = 6e-70) while HER2-low gives a small positive signal
(Δ = +0.11, p = 1e-6). PIK3CA mutation is a strong negative prognostic factor
(Δ = −0.56, p = 6e-144). BRCA1 carriers show no main-effect PFS difference
(Δ = −0.024, p = 0.72); BRCA2 is borderline negative (Δ = −0.13, p = 0.08).
Postmenopausal status, by itself, has no PFS association (p = 0.31).

3. Treatment main effects
-------------------------
Treatment_palbociclib has a large unadjusted PFS benefit: 5.41 mo vs 4.31 mo
(Δ = +1.10, p ≈ 0). The other agents look weak or null marginally:
treatment_trastuzumab Δ = −0.06 (p = 0.029, but the sign is wrong),
treatment_pembrolizumab Δ = −0.07 (p = 0.018, sign also wrong),
treatment_tamoxifen Δ = +0.04 (p = 0.08), treatment_olaparib Δ = −0.04
(p = 0.33), treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan Δ = −0.01 (p = 0.85).
These confounded marginal patterns motivated the biomarker-stratified tests.

4. Treatment × biomarker interactions
-------------------------------------
The clearest result in the dataset:

  Palbociclib × ER+: interaction coefficient +1.59 (p = 9e-229).
  In ER+ patients, palbociclib raises mean PFS by +1.58 mo (5.89 vs 4.31).
  In ER- patients, palbociclib does nothing (Δ = −0.02, p = 0.63).

  Palbociclib × HER2+: interaction coefficient −1.36 (p = 2e-118).
  Palbociclib's benefit is concentrated in HER2- disease (Δ = +1.35) and
  vanishes in HER2+ disease (Δ = −0.01). Adjusted effect in ER+ subgroup is
  +1.58 (multivariable OLS, p ≈ 0). This is the canonical CDK4/6-inhibitor
  signal (effect in HR+/HER2- biology).

  Trastuzumab × HER2+: interaction coefficient −0.03 (p = 0.69) — NO
  differential benefit. Even within HER2+ patients (n=9,060), trastuzumab
  yields Δ = −0.08 (p = 0.15); adjusted coefficient in HER2+ subgroup is
  −0.002 (p = 0.89). Trastuzumab does not improve PFS in this dataset — a
  notable null result that the unadjusted negative marginal signal had
  already hinted at.

  Olaparib × BRCA: in the BRCA1- or BRCA2-mutant pooled subgroup (n = 2,499),
  olaparib yields Δ = +0.35 (p = 0.041). The BRCA1- and BRCA2-only
  interaction terms are individually borderline (+0.45 p = 0.066;
  +0.34 p = 0.16), but the pooled signal is consistent with the targeted
  hypothesis.

  Sacituzumab × HER2-low: Δ = −0.03 (p = 0.64). In TNBC: Δ = −0.09
  (p = 0.22). NO signal.

  Pembrolizumab × MSI-high (n = 498): Δ = +0.14 (p = 0.64). In TNBC:
  Δ = +0.07 (p = 0.22). NO signal.

5. Continuous prognostics
-------------------------
age_years is by far the strongest continuous correlate of PFS, with r = +0.70
(p ≈ 0). The direction is unusual for clinical oncology (older → longer PFS),
which suggests either the dataset's PFS encoding embeds age in some way
(e.g. PFS is from diagnosis to last follow-up rather than to event, or older
patients are enriched for indolent ER+ biology). Either way, age dominates
host-factor variance.

Cachexia/inflammation panel: albumin_g_dl r = +0.10 (p = 9e-112);
weight_loss_pct_6mo r = −0.12 (p = 4e-148); ki67_pct r = −0.09 (p = 3e-93);
ldh_u_l r = −0.016 (p = 2e-4). When modeled jointly, albumin and weight loss
remain strongly significant (β = +0.50 and −0.075), while crp_mg_l (p = 0.57)
and nlr (p = 0.65) become null — i.e., serum albumin and weight loss carry
the cachexia signal and CRP/NLR add no independent information.

Tumor_size_cm is essentially uncorrelated with PFS (r ≈ 0). Vital signs
(heart_rate, systolic_bp, spo2), patient-reported symptom grades (fatigue,
pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite_loss), most labs (AST, ALT, hemoglobin,
platelets, WBC, ANC, ALC, CA-125, CEA, BMI, glucose, sodium, potassium),
smoking_pack_years, education_years, prior_lines_of_therapy, and
years_since_diagnosis all show |r| < 0.02 and are non-significant — these
features carry no prognostic information in this cohort.

6. Comorbidities and prior treatments
-------------------------------------
Among diabetes_mellitus, hypertension, copd, chronic_kidney_disease,
heart_failure, coronary_artery_disease, atrial_fibrillation,
venous_thromboembolism_history, autoimmune_disease, prior_malignancy, and
depression_anxiety_diagnosis: all p > 0.15. Among prior treatments only
prior_surgery is weakly associated (Δ = +0.066, p = 0.005); prior_chemo,
prior_radiation, prior_immunotherapy, prior_targeted_therapy show no signal.

7. Demographic disparities
--------------------------
Race/ethnicity strata do not differ in unadjusted PFS (all p > 0.09 vs
others). After adjusting for age, ECOG, stage, ER, HER2, albumin,
weight_loss, and palbociclib, all race coefficients fall within ±0.02 mo of
the reference category (all p > 0.47). Insurance type strata do not differ
in adjusted PFS (all p > 0.65). Rural residence is non-significant
(Δ = +0.027, p = 0.34).

Treatment access (proportion receiving the appropriate targeted agent within
the biomarker-defined eligible population) is also flat across strata:
palbociclib receipt in ER+ patients ranges 34.4–35.4% across race groups
(<1pp spread) and 34.5–35.5% across insurance categories. Trastuzumab
receipt in HER2+ patients ranges 18.2–21.9% across race groups (~3.7pp
range, chi-square NS). No detectable disparities in either outcomes or
treatment access.

8. SNPs
-------
None of the 12 directly-tested SNPs (rs1045642, rs1065852, rs1799853,
rs1800566, rs2228001, rs3813867, rs4244285, rs4986893, rs1801133,
rs1800896, rs429358, rs7412) shows a meaningful association with PFS;
all |r| < 0.006, all p > 0.20. The remaining SNPs were similar.

9. Multivariable model and interactions
---------------------------------------
A single OLS model containing age, ECOG, stage, brain mets, ER, HER2,
PIK3CA, albumin, weight loss, ki67, and all six treatments confirmed:

  • Strong adjusted associations: age (+0.176/yr), ECOG (−1.17/level),
    stage IV (−1.55), brain mets (−0.98), ER+ (+0.54), HER2+ (−0.47),
    PIK3CA mut (−0.58), albumin (+0.48), weight_loss (−0.077),
    ki67 (−0.019), palbociclib (+1.10).
  • Null adjusted associations: trastuzumab, tamoxifen, olaparib,
    sacituzumab, pembrolizumab (all p > 0.05 in the overall cohort).

A small but significant ECOG × stage_iv interaction (+0.067, p = 0.040)
indicates the joint penalty is sub-additive. A small palbociclib × age
interaction (−0.0032/yr, p = 0.044) indicates a slight attenuation of
palbociclib benefit with age, but the absolute magnitude is trivial.

10. Synthesis (iteration 25)
----------------------------
PFS in this cohort is driven by four factors:

  (a) Host biology — age dominates (r = +0.70); ECOG (−), albumin (+),
      and weight loss (−) add independently.
  (b) Disease burden — stage IV (−1.55) and brain mets (−1.05).
  (c) Tumor biology — ER/PR positive favorable; HER2+ and PIK3CA-mut
      unfavorable; HER2-low slightly favorable.
  (d) One effective therapy — palbociclib in ER+/HER2- disease
      (+1.6 mo PFS, p ≈ 0).

Targeted-therapy hypotheses with strong biological priors had mixed support:
olaparib showed the expected (small) benefit confined to BRCA1/2 carriers,
but trastuzumab in HER2+, sacituzumab in HER2-low or TNBC, and pembrolizumab
in TNBC or MSI-high all showed no measurable PFS benefit. Routine vitals,
patient-reported symptoms, comorbidities, and the tested SNPs were
prognostically uninformative. There were no detectable race or insurance
disparities in PFS or in receipt of targeted therapy.
