ds001_breast — Multi-iteration analysis summary
============================================================
Cohort: 50000 patients; outcome: pfs_months. 25 iterations of propose/test/refine.

Headline findings
------------------------------------------------------------
1. Treatment-biomarker matches with strong PFS benefit:
   - treatment_palbociclib in HR+/HER2- patients: ~+1.81 months mean PFS gain (highly significant); essentially no benefit in non-HR+/HER2- patients. Significant treatment_palbociclib × hr_pos_her2_neg interaction even after multivariable adjustment.
   - treatment_olaparib in BRCA1/2 carriers: ~+0.35 months mean PFS gain (significant in subgroup test and in the interaction model); no benefit in BRCA-wildtype patients.
2. Targeted agents WITHOUT detectable PFS benefit in their nominal target subgroups:
   - treatment_trastuzumab in HER2+ (no significant gain), treatment_pembrolizumab and treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan in TNBC (no significant gain), treatment_tamoxifen in ER+ (no significant gain).
3. Strongest prognostic features (continuous):
   - age_years: large positive correlation with pfs_months (r ≈ +0.70). Higher age = longer PFS in this cohort, dominating individual treatment effects in the multivariable model.
   - albumin_g_dl: positive (modest).
   - weight_loss_pct_6mo: negative.
   - ki67_pct: small negative.
   - LDH, CRP, NLR, tumor_size_cm, vital signs, electrolytes, tumor markers (CA-125, CEA, PSA): essentially null after the noise of 50k samples.
4. Subtype prognosis (untreated): TNBC and HER2+ have shorter PFS than HR+/HER2-, consistent with classical breast-cancer subtype prognosis.
5. Visceral metastatic burden (count of liver/brain/bone/adrenal/pleural sites): each additional site associated with shorter PFS; palbociclib effect appears not strongly modified by visceral burden.
6. Demographic/social: female sex, postmenopausal status, race/ethnicity, insurance type, rural residence, smoking, education — none showed clinically meaningful PFS associations after considering the strength of the age signal.
7. SNP screen: among the 24 SNP carriers tested, no SNP survives Bonferroni correction; treat as null genome-wide.

Conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------
The clearest treatment–biomarker signal in ds001_breast is palbociclib in HR-positive/HER2-negative breast cancer (~1.8-month PFS gain), with a clean interaction structure that is robust to multivariable adjustment. Olaparib produces a smaller but real benefit in BRCA1/2 carriers. The remaining named therapies (tamoxifen, trastuzumab, sacituzumab govitecan, pembrolizumab) showed no detectable PFS effects either overall or within their canonical biomarker subgroups, despite ample sample sizes — a pattern that may reflect either the simulated nature of this cohort's outcome generation or genuine null effects in this particular dataset. Among prognostic features, age_years dominated all other predictors with an unusually large positive correlation with PFS — a surprising direction relative to typical real-world oncology cohorts and worth flagging for any downstream clinical interpretation. Standard prognostic indicators (albumin, weight loss, ki67, ECOG, visceral burden) moved in expected directions but with much smaller effect sizes. The SNP panel and most lab/vital values appear non-informative for PFS in this cohort.
