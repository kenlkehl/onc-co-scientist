================================================================
Analysis summary — ds001_breast (n = 50,000)
Outcome: pfs_months (mean 4.69, median 4.54, SD 2.50, range 0–16.7)
Iterations completed: 25
================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
I structured the 25 iterations around four classes of hypotheses:
(a) classical prognostic factors (performance status, stage, sites of
disease, labs, weight loss); (b) treatment main effects; (c) treatment
× biomarker interactions matching the on-label indications for each
agent; and (d) demographic, comorbidity, and pharmacogenomic SNP
effects.  Almost every clinically expected direction held for the
prognostic factors.  Among the targeted-therapy × biomarker pairings,
however, only TWO matched the textbook biology: palbociclib was
substantially more effective in ER-positive (especially ER+/post-
menopausal) patients, and olaparib showed a significant positive
interaction with germline BRCA status.  The other "obvious" matches —
trastuzumab × HER2+, tamoxifen × ER+, pembrolizumab × MSI-high or
TNBC, sacituzumab govitecan × TNBC or HER2-low — were null.

================================================================
1. PROGNOSTIC FACTORS (iters 1–3, 11, 14, 16, 20–23)
================================================================

Highly significant predictors of SHORTER PFS (all p < 1e-50 unless noted):
  - ecog_ps (linear β = -1.18 mo per grade; mean PFS 5.64 → 4.44 → 3.29 mo for
    PS 0/1/2)
  - stage_iv (Δ = -1.54 mo)
  - has_brain_mets (Δ = -1.05 mo)
  - tnbc (Δ = -0.49 mo)
  - her2_positive (Δ = -0.46 mo)
  - pik3ca_mutation (Δ = -0.56 mo)
  - ki67_pct (β = -0.019 mo per percentage point)
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo (β = -0.075 mo per % weight lost over 6 months)

Highly significant predictors of LONGER PFS:
  - albumin_g_dl (β = +0.50 mo per g/dL)
  - er_positive (Δ = +0.57 mo)
  - pr_positive (Δ = +0.36 mo)
  - her2_low (Δ = +0.11 mo, modest)
  - age_years (β = +0.18 mo per year — direction OPPOSITE the
    "older = frailer" prior; very likely reflects treatment-selection
    bias / less aggressive biology in older patients in this cohort)

Modest but real effects (p < 0.05, |β| small):
  - ldh_u_l (β = -5e-4 mo per U/L)
  - alkaline_phosphatase_u_l (β = -7e-4)
  - calcium_mg_dl (β = -0.06 mo per mg/dL)
  - total_bilirubin_mg_dl (β = -0.07)
  - potassium_meq_l (β = +0.05)

Notable NULL findings (despite biological plausibility, all p > 0.1):
  - liver_mets, bone_mets, adrenal_mets, pleural/pericardial effusion,
    contralateral lung mets, node_positive (all surprising)
  - tumor_size_cm
  - All five symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, appetite loss, cough)
  - Hemoglobin, NLR, CRP, AST, ALT, creatinine, BUN, sodium, glucose
  - WBC, ANC, ALC, INR
  - Heart rate, SpO2, BP, BMI
  - All five major comorbidities tested (DM, CKD, HF, CAD, AF, autoimmune,
    depression, prior malignancy, VTE history)
  - Tumor markers (CEA, CA-125)
  - prior_lines_of_therapy and years_since_diagnosis

A 10-feature multivariable OLS (iter 16) achieved R² = 0.70, with
ecog_ps, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, albumin_g_dl, ldh_u_l, and age_years
all retaining strong independent significance.  Liver_mets, bone_mets,
crp_mg_l, and prior_lines_of_therapy were null after adjustment —
i.e., their univariate nulls were not artifacts of confounding.

================================================================
2. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS (iter 4)
================================================================

Only palbociclib showed a sizable and highly significant unconditional
benefit on PFS (Δ = +1.10 mo, p ≈ 0).  All other treatments had
near-zero or slightly NEGATIVE main effects in the unselected cohort:
trastuzumab Δ = -0.06 mo (p = 0.03), pembrolizumab Δ = -0.07 mo
(p = 0.02), tamoxifen Δ = +0.04 mo (NS), olaparib Δ = -0.04 mo (NS),
sacituzumab govitecan Δ = -0.01 mo (NS).  This is the expected pattern
when a targeted agent is given to a clinically heterogeneous population
in which only a sub-group benefits — the marginal mean is dominated
by the unmatched majority — and motivated the interaction analyses
in iterations 5–10.

================================================================
3. TREATMENT × BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS (iters 5–10, 17, 19, 24, 25)
================================================================

(A) Hypothesis SUPPORTED: olaparib × BRCA (iters 7, 19)
    Interaction β = +0.40 mo, p = 0.02.  Within BRCA-mutated patients
    (n = 2,499), olaparib was associated with longer PFS (4.93 vs 4.59,
    Δ = +0.35 mo, p = 0.04).  Looking at BRCA1 and BRCA2 separately:
    BRCA1 interaction β = +0.45 (p = 0.07), BRCA2 interaction β = +0.34
    (p = 0.16) — same direction, individually under-powered, consistent
    with the pooled BRCA-any signal being driven by both loci.

(B) Hypothesis STRONGLY SUPPORTED: palbociclib × ER+/postmenopausal
    (iter 8)
    The largest treatment-effect heterogeneity in the dataset.
    Interaction β = +0.70 mo, p = 2e-51.  Subgroup means:
       ER+/postmen on palbociclib: 5.83 mo vs off 4.33 mo  (Δ = +1.50, p ≈ 0)
       all other patients on palbociclib: 5.10 vs 4.30 mo  (Δ = +0.80, p ≈ 0)
       within ER+ (any menopausal status):  Δ = +1.58 mo (p ≈ 0)
       within ER-:                          Δ = -0.02 mo (p = 0.63, NULL)
    Therefore palbociclib is acting cleanly through the ER axis, with
    a further amplification in postmenopausal patients (consistent
    with PALOMA-2/3 findings in real-world practice).

(C) Hypothesis REFUTED: trastuzumab × HER2 (iters 5, 24)
    Interaction β = -0.03, p = 0.69.  Within HER2-positive (n = 9,060):
    on-trastuzumab 4.25 vs off 4.33 mo (Δ = -0.08, p = 0.15).
    No benefit detected.  Also null in HER2-low and HER2-amplified.

(D) Hypothesis REFUTED: tamoxifen × ER (iter 6)
    Interaction β = -0.06, p = 0.27.  Within ER-positive: Δ = +0.02 mo
    (p = 0.45).  A tiny positive effect of tamoxifen actually appeared
    in ER-NEGATIVE patients (Δ = +0.08, p = 0.03), which is biologically
    implausible and almost certainly reflects channeling/selection bias
    rather than a true effect.

(E) Hypothesis REFUTED: pembrolizumab × MSI-high (iter 9)
    Interaction p = 0.47.  In MSI-high (n = 498), Δ = +0.14 mo (NS).
    Pembrolizumab × TNBC reached nominal significance (p = 0.015) but
    the effect within TNBC was small and not significant on its own
    (Δ = +0.07, p = 0.22); the interaction is mainly driven by
    pembrolizumab being slightly DETRIMENTAL in non-TNBC.

(F) Hypothesis REFUTED: sacituzumab govitecan × TNBC and × HER2-low
    (iter 10)
    Both interaction p-values > 0.2; subgroup-specific effects all null.

(G) Hypothesis REFUTED: CYP2D6/PGx SNPs × tamoxifen (iter 17)
    Interactions of treatment_tamoxifen with rs1065852, rs3813867,
    rs1800566 all p > 0.7.

(H) Joint model (iter 25):  Of the five biomarker × treatment
    interactions tested simultaneously alongside major prognostics,
    only palbociclib × ER+/postmenopausal survived (β = +0.72,
    p = 5e-70).  The trastuzumab × HER2, tamoxifen × ER,
    olaparib × BRCA, and pembrolizumab × MSI interactions all
    attenuated to non-significance once adjusted for the other terms,
    although olaparib × BRCA retained its positive sign.

================================================================
4. SOCIODEMOGRAPHIC, SNP, AND COMORBIDITY EFFECTS (iters 13, 15, 18)
================================================================

No detectable disparities by race_ethnicity (ANOVA p = 0.49),
insurance_type (p = 0.82), rural_residence (p = 0.34), or
education_years (p = 0.39).

Among 24 SNPs tested as univariate predictors of PFS, the smallest
p-value was 0.026 (snp_rs1801131).  No SNP was significant after
Bonferroni correction (24 × 0.026 ≈ 0.63).  This is consistent with
an absence of single-locus pharmacogenomic effects in this cohort.

No comorbidity (DM, HTN, COPD, CKD, HF, CAD, AF, VTE history,
autoimmune disease, depression/anxiety, prior malignancy) showed a
significant univariate effect on PFS.

================================================================
5. OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
================================================================

1. The dominant signal in this dataset is a clean classical prognostic
   axis: ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, low albumin, weight loss, high
   Ki-67, PIK3CA mutation, HER2-positive (versus ER-positive) all
   shorten PFS.  These together explain ~70 % of the variance.

2. Targeted-therapy efficacy is biomarker-restricted in only TWO
   cases:
       palbociclib (positive interaction with ER+/postmenopausal)
       olaparib   (positive interaction with germline BRCA)
   Both align with established clinical pharmacology.

3. Trastuzumab in HER2+, tamoxifen in ER+, pembrolizumab in MSI-high
   or TNBC, and sacituzumab govitecan in TNBC/HER2-low — interactions
   that would be expected on label — were NOT detectable in this
   cohort.  Possible explanations include (i) the cohort representing
   later-line use after progression on these agents, (ii) confounding
   by indication that gives sicker patients these drugs, or
   (iii) limited variability in the achieved benefit.  The data do
   not let me distinguish among these.

4. Some univariate prognostic findings that I expected on biological
   grounds were NOT observed: site-of-disease covariates (liver,
   bone, adrenal, pleural mets, node positivity), the inflammation
   biomarkers (CRP, NLR), patient-reported symptom grades, and most
   liver/kidney chemistries.  In a real EHR cohort these would be
   strong; their flatness in this dataset is itself notable.

5. Demographic disparities (race, insurance, rurality, education)
   and pharmacogenomic SNPs were not detectable after appropriate
   correction.

6. The age-PFS relationship was POSITIVE rather than negative — older
   patients had longer PFS — reversing my prior expectation.  This is
   most likely a selection/treatment-channeling artifact (older
   patients receive less aggressive treatments with longer PFS, or
   have more indolent biology); causal interpretation is not warranted
   from these observational data.
