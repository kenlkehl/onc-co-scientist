Analysis summary — ds001_breast (50,000 patients; outcome = pfs_months)
======================================================================

Across 25 iterations I proposed and tested 145 hypotheses on the breast-
cancer cohort, working from simple main effects to subgroup analyses,
treatment x biomarker interactions, and a fully adjusted multivariable
OLS model. Below I synthesize the evidence by theme, noting direction,
magnitude, statistical significance, and clinical plausibility.


1. PROGNOSTIC / DISEASE-BURDEN MAIN EFFECTS (strongly supported)
---------------------------------------------------------------
The classical breast-cancer prognostic features behave as expected:

* Stage IV vs. non-stage-IV: mean PFS 3.61 vs 5.15 mo, diff -1.54,
  p ~ 0 (h3_1).
* Brain metastases: -1.05 mo, p ~ 1e-178 (h3_2).
* ECOG performance status: each one-point increase associates with
  -1.18 mo PFS (p ~ 0; h3_7).
* Higher Ki-67%: continuous slope -0.019 mo per percentage point
  (p ~ 3e-93; h7_1). Ki-67 > 20 dichotomy: -0.51 mo (p ~ 2e-105;
  h19_1).
* 6-month percent weight loss: -0.075 mo per % (p ~ 4e-148; h7_3).
* Albumin (g/dL): +0.50 mo per unit (p ~ 1e-112; h6_1).
* LDH (U/L): small but highly significant slope, -0.0008 mo/U
  (p ~ 2e-4; h6_2).
* Alkaline phosphatase: -0.0006 mo/U (p ~ 0.003; h6_6).
* In the fully adjusted multivariable model (h21_*) all of these
  remained statistically independent predictors of PFS, with stage_iv
  (-1.55), ecog_ps (-1.17), albumin_g_dl (+0.48), ldh_u_l (-0.001),
  ki67_pct (-0.019), and weight_loss_pct_6mo (-0.077) carrying the
  largest standardized impact.

A composite prognostic score (albumin - LDH/200 - ECOG) is positively
associated with PFS (+0.78 mo per unit, p ~ 0; h25_5), confirming that
these laboratory and performance-status features capture coherent
clinical risk.

Notable null/contradicting findings: liver metastases, bone metastases,
node positivity, and pleural effusion did not show significant univariate
associations with PFS once examined in this cohort (h3_3..h3_6); their
absence of effect was consistent in the adjusted model. Symptom grades
(fatigue, pain NRS, dyspnea, cough, appetite-loss; iteration 8) showed
NO association with PFS — surprising given their typical prognostic
weight in real-world cohorts and worth flagging as a possible
data-generation artifact. Comorbidities (diabetes, CKD, heart failure,
COPD, autoimmune disease, depression, prior VTE; iteration 9) were
similarly null.


2. BIOMARKER MAIN EFFECTS
-------------------------
Univariate biomarker associations with PFS were highly significant:

* ER-positive: +0.57 mo (p ~ 4e-143; h2_1).
* PR-positive: +0.36 mo (p ~ 6e-59; h2_2).
* HER2-positive (overall, unadjusted for therapy): -0.46 mo
  (p ~ 6e-70; h2_3) — the expected biological "worse-prognosis"
  signal in the absence of effective HER2-directed therapy effect.
* HER2-low: small positive +0.11 mo (p ~ 1e-6; h2_4).
* PIK3CA mutation: -0.56 mo (p ~ 6e-144; h2_7).

In contrast, BRCA1, BRCA2, TP53, and MSI-high status showed no
significant overall main effect on PFS in this cohort. BRCA1
mutation rate did not differ significantly between ER- and ER+
patients (h24_1) — a deviation from the classical BRCA1/triple-negative
enrichment pattern, again worth flagging.


3. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS
-------------------------
Looking at each agent across the entire cohort (iteration 1):

* treatment_palbociclib was the standout, with +1.10 mo PFS
  (p ~ 0; h1_3). This effect persisted essentially unchanged in
  the multivariable model (+1.10 mo adjusted; h21_2 / h11_3).
* treatment_trastuzumab: small NEGATIVE -0.06 mo (p = 0.029; h1_1)
  unadjusted; null after adjustment (p = 0.42; h11_1).
* treatment_pembrolizumab: small NEGATIVE -0.07 mo (p = 0.018; h1_6)
  unadjusted; remained marginally negative after adjustment
  (-0.03; p = 0.046; h11_6).
* treatment_tamoxifen, treatment_olaparib, treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan:
  no overall effect (h1_2, h1_4, h1_5; all p > 0.05).

The naive treatment-only ranking is therefore misleading for every
agent except palbociclib unless interactions with biomarkers are
considered (next section).


4. TREATMENT x BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS (the key biology)
-------------------------------------------------------
Iterations 4, 13, 14, 15, 16, 22, 24, 25 explored heterogeneity of
treatment effect by biomarker.

Confirmed (clinically expected) modifiers:

* Palbociclib x ER-positive: interaction +1.59 mo (p ~ 1e-228;
  h4_3). Within ER-positive patients, palbociclib confers +1.58 mo
  (h14_3, p ~ 0). This is consistent with palbociclib's
  CDK4/6-on-ER-driven mechanism.
* Olaparib x any-BRCA (BRCA1 OR BRCA2): interaction +0.40 mo
  (p = 0.021; h4_4); within BRCA-mutated patients, olaparib confers
  +0.35 mo (p = 0.041; h14_4 / h25_4). Direction matches the PARP-
  inhibitor mechanism, although the magnitude is modest. When
  separated, BRCA1 (h13_1) and BRCA2 (h13_2) interactions trended
  positive (+0.45 and +0.34 mo) but did not reach 0.05 individually.
* Pembrolizumab x TNBC (ER-/PR-/HER2-): interaction +0.19 mo
  (p = 0.015; h24_2). This is the only place pembrolizumab shows
  benefit in this cohort and aligns with the standard-of-care role
  of immunotherapy in TNBC.

Noteworthy off-target nulls (good direction but not biologically
suspicious):

* In ER-negative patients, tamoxifen has a small POSITIVE association
  (+0.08 mo, p = 0.030; h15_2) — likely chance / confounding rather
  than a real effect.
* In MSI-stable patients, pembrolizumab is associated with -0.08 mo
  (p = 0.015; h15_4), confirming the lack of expected benefit in
  non-MSI-high disease.

Surprising null where strong signal was anticipated:

* Trastuzumab x HER2-positive interaction: -0.03 mo, p = 0.69 (h4_1).
* Within HER2-positive patients (n ~ 9000): trastuzumab effect on
  PFS is essentially null (-0.08 mo, p = 0.15; h14_1).
* Trastuzumab x HER2-amplification interaction: -0.04, p = 0.81
  (h16_1); within HER2-amplified patients no significant effect of
  trastuzumab (h22_1).
* her2_positive and her2_amplification are NOT statistically
  associated in this cohort (chi2 p = 0.55; h22_3) — the two columns
  appear to encode independent random binary indicators rather than
  the biologically near-equivalent concepts they suggest. This is a
  data anomaly worth flagging.
* Sacituzumab govitecan x HER2-low interaction: -0.04, p = 0.63
  (h4_5). Within HER2-low patients no signal (h14_5). Within TNBC,
  the interaction also fails to reach 0.05 (h24_3, p = 0.25).
  Expected biology: HER2-low or TNBC ought to enrich for benefit.

Notable PIK3CA finding:

* Palbociclib x PIK3CA mutation interaction is strongly NEGATIVE
  (-1.62 mo, p ~ 4e-255; h20_3). Combined with the palbociclib x
  ER+ positive interaction, the biology recovered is "palbociclib
  helps ER+/PIK3CA-wildtype patients more than ER+/PIK3CA-mutant
  patients" — plausibly reflecting PI3K-pathway-driven resistance
  to CDK4/6 inhibition.

Other interactions tested (palbociclib x CDKN2A loss, tamoxifen x
postmenopausal, ECOG x stage IV moderation of treatment benefits in
iterations 16/19/23): mostly null, with only ecog_ps x stage_iv
showing a small positive interaction (+0.07; p = 0.040; h19_2),
indicating ECOG's prognostic information saturates somewhat once
disease is stage IV.


5. AGE, DEMOGRAPHICS, AND SOCIOECONOMIC EFFECTS
------------------------------------------------
* Age: each year of age associates with +0.18 mo longer PFS, p ~ 0
  (h5_1). This is OPPOSITE the typical direction in real-world
  oncology data and likely reflects either (a) a synthetic-data
  artifact or (b) a survivor / selection bias whereby older patients
  in this cohort have more indolent biology.
* Sex female: only 44.7% of the cohort is female (h5_3) — anomalous
  for a "breast cancer" dataset and a likely synthetic-data artifact.
* Postmenopausal status: no PFS difference (h5_2).
* Race/ethnicity (ANOVA, h5_4): p = 0.49 — no overall PFS difference.
* Insurance type (h5_5): p = 0.82 — null.
* Disadvantaged insurance (medicaid/uninsured, h17_1): null.
* Rural residence (h17_2), education years (h17_3), Black vs White
  PFS (h17_4), Hispanic vs White PFS (h17_5): all null.

So the well-documented real-world disparities in breast-cancer
outcomes are NOT present in this cohort.


6. PHARMACOGENOMICS / SNPs
--------------------------
A panel of twelve SNPs commonly studied as drug-metabolism modifiers
(rs1045642 [ABCB1], rs1065852/rs1799853 [CYP2D6/2C9], rs1800566 [NQO1],
rs2228001 [XPC], rs4244285 [CYP2C19], rs1801133 [MTHFR], rs429358/rs7412
[APOE], rs1800629 [TNF-alpha], rs4880 [SOD2], rs1050828 [G6PD]) was
tested as PFS dosage main effects (iteration 12). All 12 had p > 0.05,
slopes < 0.04 mo per allele (range -0.034 to +0.034). No
pharmacogenomic-PFS signal was detected.


7. ADDITIONAL LAB / COMORBIDITY / PRIOR-THERAPY MAIN EFFECTS
------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 18 explored additional laboratory variables; only calcium
(-0.06 mo per mg/dL, p = 0.004; h18_5) and total bilirubin (-0.07
mo per mg/dL, p = 0.046; h18_9) reached significance. Platelets,
WBC, ANC, ALC, CA-125, CEA, and creatinine were null. Comorbidities
were uniformly null (iteration 9). Prior therapies were almost all
null (iteration 10), with only prior_surgery showing a tiny positive
+0.07 mo (p = 0.005; h10_3) — likely a marker of less-advanced disease
selected for surgical resection. Prior lines of therapy did not
predict PFS univariate.


8. WHAT WAS REFUTED VS. SUPPORTED
---------------------------------
Supported (effect direction matches expectation, p < 0.05):
- Worse PFS with stage IV, brain mets, ECOG, Ki-67 high, weight loss
- Better PFS with ER+, PR+, HER2-low, albumin, prior surgery
- Worse PFS with PIK3CA mutation
- Palbociclib benefit overall and in ER+ subgroup
- Olaparib benefit in BRCA-mutated subgroup (modest)
- Pembrolizumab benefit in TNBC subgroup

Refuted / null (no significant effect where one was hypothesized):
- Trastuzumab benefit in HER2+ (and the her2_positive / her2_amp
  decoupling itself)
- Sacituzumab govitecan benefit in HER2-low or TNBC
- Tamoxifen benefit in ER+ subgroup (no significant interaction or
  within-subgroup difference; h4_2, h14_2)
- Symptom-grade and comorbidity prognostic information
- Pharmacogenomic SNP main effects on PFS
- Race/ethnicity, insurance, rural residence, education disparities
- Age direction (older patients fared BETTER, not worse — anomalous)


9. OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
----------------------
The strongest signals in this cohort are the prognostic ones (stage,
ECOG, brain mets, albumin, LDH, Ki-67, weight loss) and the palbociclib
x ER-positive treatment-effect interaction, which together account
for most of the explainable variance in PFS. The PIK3CA-mutation
modifier of palbociclib benefit is a striking secondary finding.

Olaparib in BRCA carriers and pembrolizumab in TNBC reproduce known
biology albeit at modest effect sizes. Trastuzumab and sacituzumab
govitecan do NOT reproduce their expected biomarker-driven effects in
this dataset, and the her2_positive / her2_amplification columns
appear statistically independent — suggestive of a data quality
issue or synthetic encoding mismatch worth verifying before drawing
clinical inferences.

The conspicuous null findings for symptoms, comorbidities, demographic
disparities, and pharmacogenomics — combined with a 44.7% female rate
in a "breast" cohort and a positive age/PFS association — suggest
that this dataset reproduces the canonical biomarker-driven treatment
effects of breast oncology faithfully but does NOT recreate many of
the social, comorbidity, and symptom-driven gradients seen in real
electronic-health-record cohorts.
