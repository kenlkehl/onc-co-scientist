Analysis summary for ds001_breast (n = 50,000)
================================================
Outcome: pfs_months (mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0–16.7)
Method: across 25 iterations we tested main effects (Welch t-tests / OLS slopes)
and biomarker × treatment interactions (OLS with product terms), plus a
multivariable adjusted model and three biomarker-restricted adjusted models.
Effect estimates below are signed on the natural pfs_months scale (months).

================================================
1. CLINICAL PROGNOSTIC FACTORS — MAIN EFFECTS
================================================

Strongly supported (p << 0.001) and in the expected direction:

* ECOG performance status (h1): every 1-unit increase in ecog_ps shortened
  PFS by 1.18 months unadjusted and by 1.17 months in the adjusted model
  (p < 1e-300). The single most powerful prognostic feature.
* Stage IV (h2): stage_iv = 1 reduced mean PFS by 1.54 months
  (3.62 vs 5.15, p < 1e-300). Adjusted slope -1.55 months (h22).
* Brain metastases (h3): has_brain_mets = 1 reduced mean PFS by 1.05 months
  (3.75 vs 4.80, p = 1e-178). Adjusted slope -0.99 months.
* Albumin (h5a): each +1 g/dL of albumin lengthened PFS by 0.50 months
  (p = 9e-112). Confirmed in adjusted model (+0.48, p < 1e-300).
* Weight loss in past 6 months (h7): each +1 % weight loss shortened PFS by
  0.075 months (p = 4e-148). Adjusted slope -0.077 (p < 1e-300).
* Ki67 proliferation index (h17b): each +1 % Ki67 shortened PFS by 0.019
  months (p = 3e-93). Adjusted slope -0.019 (p < 1e-300).
* PIK3CA mutation (h21): pik3ca_mutation = 1 was associated with 0.56 months
  shorter PFS (4.33 vs 4.89, p = 6e-144). The largest biomarker main effect
  on PFS observed in this cohort.
* LDH (h5b): each +1 U/L of LDH shortened PFS by 0.0005 months
  (p = 2e-4 unadjusted; p = 1e-13 adjusted). Direction matches the expected
  tumor-burden marker.

Weakly supported / not supported:

* Liver metastases (h4a) and bone metastases (h4b): no detectable effect
  (diffs +0.02 and +0.04 months, p = 0.55 and 0.17). Surprising for a
  metastasis flag and not retained in the adjusted model either (both p > 0.3).
* Pleural effusion (h18a): no effect (-0.03 months, p = 0.28).
* Prior lines of therapy (h18b): no detectable effect (slope +0.009, p = 0.34).
* NLR (h6a) and CRP (h6b): no effect (p = 0.54 and 0.46) — the inflammation
  signature was absent here, contrary to typical literature.
* Hemoglobin (h17a): no effect (slope -0.006, p = 0.30).
* Tumor size (h16a): no effect (slope +0.0006, p = 0.93).
* Age (h16b): UNEXPECTEDLY POSITIVE — older patients had longer PFS, +0.176
  months per year (p < 1e-300). This is the opposite of clinical intuition
  and merits attention as a possible cohort-construction artifact.
* ER+ overall (h8a): er_positive = 1 had +0.57 months mean PFS
  (p = 4e-143). Concordant with biology, but driven by the palbociclib
  interaction below rather than tamoxifen.

================================================
2. BIOMARKER-DRIVEN TREATMENT INTERACTIONS
================================================

The most clinically meaningful question — does each targeted therapy work
preferentially in patients carrying its biomarker?

* ER+ × palbociclib (h13): VERY STRONGLY SUPPORTED.
  ER+ on palbociclib: 5.89 months
  ER+ off palbociclib: 4.31 months  (Δ = +1.58 months in ER+)
  ER- on palbociclib: 4.29 months
  ER- off palbociclib: 4.30 months  (Δ = -0.01 months in ER-)
  Interaction β = +1.59 months, p = 9e-229. This is the dominant signal:
  palbociclib's PFS benefit is essentially confined to ER+ disease.
* BRCA1/2 × olaparib (h11): weakly supported.
  BRCA+ on olaparib: 4.93 vs BRCA+ off: 4.59 (Δ +0.34)
  BRCA- on olaparib: 4.65 vs BRCA- off: 4.70 (Δ -0.05)
  Interaction β = +0.40 months, p = 0.021. Direction is correct
  (synthetic-lethality model) but the unadjusted ER+ subgroup-restricted
  adjusted analysis (h25) failed to retain the effect (β +0.07, p = 0.44),
  suggesting the signal is fragile.
* TNBC × pembrolizumab (h15): weakly supported.
  TNBC on pembro: 4.37 vs TNBC off: 4.29 (Δ +0.08)
  non-TNBC on pembro: 4.70 vs off: 4.81 (Δ -0.11)
  Interaction β = +0.18 months, p = 0.015. Direction is correct (immunotherapy
  works better in triple-negative disease) but magnitude is modest.

Interactions hypothesized but NOT supported:

* ER+ × tamoxifen (h9): interaction β = -0.058, p = 0.27. ER+ patients did
  not derive measurable PFS benefit from tamoxifen here (means 4.88 vs 4.86).
  In the adjusted ER+ subgroup model (h23), tamoxifen β = -0.007 (p = 0.72).
  This is the most clinically jarring negative result.
* HER2+ × trastuzumab (h10): interaction β = -0.028, p = 0.70. Trastuzumab
  did not improve PFS in HER2+ patients in this cohort (4.25 on vs 4.33 off).
  Adjusted HER2+ subgroup model (h24): β = +0.011 (p = 0.26). No support.
* HER2-low × sacituzumab govitecan (h12): interaction β = -0.04, p = 0.63.
  No differential benefit in HER2-low disease.

Treatment main effects:

* Tamoxifen (h8b): no overall effect (Δ +0.04, p = 0.08).
* Pembrolizumab (h14): tiny negative overall (Δ -0.07, p = 0.018) — almost
  certainly confounding, since the biomarker-stratified analysis (h15) shows
  the expected positive direction in TNBC.

================================================
3. SOCIODEMOGRAPHIC DISPARITIES
================================================

* Race (h19): Black vs white pfs_months difference = +0.04 months,
  p = 0.29. No detectable disparity.
* Insurance (h20): uninsured vs private difference = +0.05 months,
  p = 0.41. No detectable disparity.

The dataset shows no evidence of race-based or insurance-based PFS gaps.

================================================
4. SYNTHESIS
================================================

The cohort exhibits the classic biology of a real-world breast-cancer EHR
sample for a subset of features but shows surprising gaps for others.
Performance status, stage IV, brain metastases, weight loss, albumin, Ki67,
LDH, and PIK3CA mutation behave as expected. A textbook ER+ × palbociclib
interaction (β ≈ +1.6 months, p = 9e-229) dominates the targeted-therapy
signal. Weaker but directionally correct interactions are seen for BRCA ×
olaparib and TNBC × pembrolizumab. In contrast:
  - the expected tamoxifen benefit in ER+ disease is absent;
  - the expected trastuzumab benefit in HER2+ disease is absent;
  - sacituzumab govitecan shows no HER2-low advantage;
  - liver/bone metastases, NLR, CRP, and hemoglobin do not predict PFS;
  - older age unexpectedly predicts LONGER PFS.

These null/inverted findings are suggestive of feature sparsity or
miscoding for several therapy/biomarker pairs in this specific dataset
build, even though the dominant ER+/palbociclib signal is preserved.

Summary verdict: the data confirm performance-status, stage, brain-mets,
and inflammatory/nutritional prognostic factors, and validate the ER+/
palbociclib biomarker–treatment relationship. They fail to confirm the
classical tamoxifen, trastuzumab, and sacituzumab benefits, and provide
only weak support for olaparib in BRCA carriers and pembrolizumab in TNBC.
