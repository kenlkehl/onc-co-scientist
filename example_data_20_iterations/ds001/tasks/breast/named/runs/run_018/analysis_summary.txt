ds001_breast — Analysis Summary
================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with breast cancer outcomes assembled from EHR. Outcome
variable: pfs_months (continuous, mean 4.69, SD 2.50). The cohort is 45% female
(suggesting either a heterogeneously coded "sex" field or inclusion of male
breast cancer); 60% postmenopausal, 70% ER+, 64% PR+, 18% HER2+, 37% HER2-low,
2.6% BRCA1, 2.4% BRCA2, 35% PIK3CA, 1% MSI-high. Treatment exposure rates:
tamoxifen 30%, palbociclib 35%, trastuzumab 20%, olaparib 10%, sacituzumab 10%,
pembrolizumab 15%.

A 25-iteration propose-test-refine workflow probed (i) treatment main effects,
(ii) biomarker prognostics, (iii) clinical/lab prognostics, (iv) treatment x
biomarker predictive interactions, (v) demographic and access disparities, (vi)
SNP main and interaction effects, (vii) multivariable adjustment, and (viii)
stratified subgroup analyses. Statistical tools: Welch t-tests, OLS with
interactions, one-way ANOVA, chi-square. Significance defined at p < 0.05.

KEY FINDINGS
============

1. Palbociclib has the largest, most consistent treatment effect on PFS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Unadjusted: mean PFS 5.41 months on palbociclib vs 4.31 off (+1.10 months,
p~0). After multivariable adjustment for age, ECOG, stage, metastases, all
biomarkers, and labs, palbociclib still adds +1.10 months (p~0). The effect is
overwhelmingly driven by an ER+ x palbociclib interaction: in ER+ patients,
palbociclib adds +1.58 months (5.89 vs 4.31), while in ER- patients, the
"effect" is essentially zero (-0.02 months). The interaction coefficient
(+1.59 unadjusted, +1.57 adjusted) is highly significant (p ~ 1e-228), and the
effect persists in both postmenopausal ER+ (+1.50, p=4e-283) and premenopausal
ER+ (+1.69, p=5e-230) strata. This is by far the strongest predictive
biomarker-treatment relationship in the cohort and is consistent with the known
indication for CDK4/6 inhibition in HR+ breast cancer.

2. PARP-inhibitor (olaparib) has a small but real BRCA-restricted signal
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Olaparib has no overall main effect on PFS (-0.04 months, p=0.33). However, in
the unadjusted interaction model, olaparib x BRCA-any (BRCA1 or BRCA2) yields
a positive interaction coefficient of +0.40 (p=0.021). Stratified analysis
within BRCA-any=1 shows olaparib adds +0.35 months (p=0.041). The signal is
directionally consistent in BRCA1-only (+0.40, p=0.12) and BRCA2-only (+0.30,
p=0.19) strata but underpowered. Within BRCA-wild-type, olaparib trends toward
shorter PFS (-0.06, p=0.14). After adjustment for prognostic covariates, the
BRCA x olaparib interaction loses significance (coef +0.06, p=0.51), suggesting
imbalance in baseline characteristics partially confounded the unadjusted
signal. Conclusion: weak signal consistent with the expected indication, but
not statistically robust to adjustment.

3. Trastuzumab, pembrolizumab, sacituzumab, and tamoxifen do NOT show their
   expected indication-specific effects in this cohort
----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Trastuzumab: unadjusted mean PFS 4.65 on vs 4.71 off (-0.06, p=0.029, slight
  negative). No HER2+ x trastuzumab interaction (coef -0.03, p=0.69). Within
  HER2+, trastuzumab is non-beneficial (-0.08, p=0.15). Within
  her2_amplification=1 (a separate column, only 2.9% of cohort, weakly
  concordant with her2_positive — chi2 p=0.55), trastuzumab still shows no
  benefit (-0.02, p=0.89).
* Pembrolizumab: small unadjusted negative main effect (-0.07, p=0.018).
  Pembrolizumab x MSI-high interaction coef +0.22 (p=0.47, NS). Within MSI-high
  (n=498), pembrolizumab adds +0.14 months (p=0.64). Within TNBC (n=10,496),
  pembrolizumab adds +0.07 months (p=0.22).
* Sacituzumab govitecan: no main effect (-0.01, p=0.85). No her2_low or TNBC
  interaction (both NS). Within her2_low=1, saci -0.03 months (p=0.64); within
  TNBC, saci -0.09 (p=0.22).
* Tamoxifen: no overall effect (+0.04, p=0.077). No ER+ x tamoxifen interaction
  (coef -0.06, p=0.27). Within ER+, tamoxifen adds only +0.02 months (p=0.45).
  Surprisingly, within ER- patients, tamoxifen is associated with slightly
  longer PFS (+0.08, p=0.030) — likely chance/confounding given no biological
  rationale.
* CYP2D6 pharmacogenomic interaction: tamoxifen x snp_rs1065852 interaction
  coef -0.002 (p=0.97); restricted to ER+ patients, coef +0.026 (p=0.78). No
  evidence that the CYP2D6*10 reduced-metabolizer variant attenuates tamoxifen
  benefit in this dataset.

Net conclusion: only the palbociclib x ER+ relationship and a weaker
olaparib x BRCA signal recapitulate canonical breast-cancer pharmacology in this
cohort. Trastuzumab, pembrolizumab, sacituzumab, and tamoxifen do not show
their textbook biomarker-driven benefit.

4. Strong and robust prognostic factors
---------------------------------------
The full multivariable model (R^2 = 0.80) identifies a tight, clinically
familiar set of prognostic predictors of pfs_months (all with p < 1e-15):
  * stage_iv:           -1.55 months adjusted (-1.54 unadjusted)
  * ecog_ps:            -1.17 months per unit ECOG (-1.18 unadjusted)
  * has_brain_mets:     -0.98 months adjusted (-1.05 unadjusted)
  * albumin_g_dl:       +0.48 months per g/dL (+0.50 unadjusted)
  * weight_loss_pct_6mo:-0.077 per percent (-0.075 unadjusted)
  * ki67_pct:           -0.019 per % (-0.019 unadjusted)
  * age_years:          +0.18 per year (positive, surprising; persists after
                        adjustment for menopausal status, ECOG, stage)
  * ldh_u_l:            -0.0005 per U/L (-0.0005 unadjusted, p=2e-18)
  * alkaline_phosphatase_u_l: -0.0007 per U/L unadjusted, p=0.003

5. Biomarker prognostics independent of treatment
--------------------------------------------------
* er_positive: +0.57 months (p=4e-143) — better prognosis
* pr_positive: +0.36 months (p=6e-59)  — better prognosis
* her2_positive: -0.46 months (p=6e-70) — worse natural history
* pik3ca_mutation: -0.56 months (p=6e-144) — worse natural history
* TNBC composite (er=0, pr=0, her2=0): -0.49 months (p=1e-89)
* tp53_mutation, brca1, brca2, msi_high: no prognostic main effect (all NS).
* her2_low and her2_amplification: no prognostic main effect.

6. Null findings (no association detected with pfs_months)
----------------------------------------------------------
* Demographics & access: race_ethnicity (ANOVA p=0.49), insurance_type (p=0.82),
  rural_residence (p=0.34), education_years (p=0.39), sex_female (p=0.56),
  postmenopausal status (p=0.31), bmi (p=0.11). No evidence of disparity in
  PFS in this synthetic-style cohort.
* Comorbidities: diabetes_mellitus, hypertension, chronic_kidney_disease,
  heart_failure, prior_malignancy — all NS.
* Prior therapy: prior_chemotherapy, prior_targeted_therapy,
  prior_lines_of_therapy, years_since_diagnosis — all NS.
* Symptom grades: fatigue_grade, pain_nrs, dyspnea_grade, appetite_loss_grade
  — all NS (small magnitudes, p > 0.30 each), unusual given typical expectation
  that symptom burden tracks disease severity.
* Tumor markers other than alkaline phosphatase and Ki67: ca_125, cea,
  tumor_size_cm — all NS.
* SNPs tested (rs429358 APOE, rs1801133 MTHFR, rs1045642 ABCB1, rs1065852
  CYP2D6, rs4244285 CYP2C19) — all NS for main effects; rs1065852 also NS for
  tamoxifen interaction.
* Liver, bone, node-positive metastases — surprisingly NS at the unadjusted
  level (p > 0.17), although stage_iv and brain mets dominate.

HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED VS REFUTED (summary)
=========================================
SUPPORTED:
  - Palbociclib improves pfs_months overall (h1_palbociclib).
  - ER+ has longer PFS (h2_er); PR+ longer (h2_pr); HER2+ shorter (h2_her2);
    PIK3CA shorter (h2_pik3ca).
  - Stage IV, brain mets, higher ECOG predict shorter PFS (h3_stage4,
    h3_brain, h3_ecog).
  - Higher albumin → longer PFS (h5_albumin); higher LDH, weight loss → shorter
    (h5_ldh, h5_wtloss).
  - palbociclib x ER+ predictive interaction (h6_palb_er, h18_full_model,
    h24_adj_palb_er, h19_palb_postmen, h23_palb_premen).
  - olaparib x BRCA-any interaction in unadjusted analysis (h7_olap_brca,
    h14_olap_in_brca) — weakened after adjustment.
  - TNBC has shorter PFS (h21_tnbc_pfs).
  - Higher Ki67, alkaline phosphatase predict shorter PFS (h13_ki67,
    h13_alkphos).
  - has_brain_mets remains negative after adjustment (h20_brain_pfs_short).

REFUTED / NOT SUPPORTED:
  - Tamoxifen overall benefit (h1_tamoxifen) and tamoxifen x ER interaction
    (h6_tam_er, h14_tam_in_er, h19_tam_postmen).
  - Trastuzumab benefit overall (direction was opposite) and trastuzumab x
    HER2 interaction (h6_tras_her2, h14_tras_in_her2pos, h17_tras_her2amp,
    h24_adj_tras_her2).
  - Pembrolizumab benefit overall (direction opposite) and pembrolizumab x
    MSI-high interaction (h7_pembro_msi, h14_pembro_in_msi, h21_pembro_tnbc,
    h24_adj_pembro_msi).
  - Sacituzumab govitecan benefit overall and her2_low/TNBC interactions
    (h7_saci_her2low, h7_saci_tnbc, h21_saci_tnbc_strat, h23_saci_her2low_strat).
  - Tamoxifen x CYP2D6 (rs1065852) pharmacogenomic interaction
    (h16_tam_cyp2d6, h16_tam_cyp2d6_in_erpos).
  - BRCA1, BRCA2, TP53, MSI-high prognostic main effects (h2_brca1, h2_brca2,
    h2_tp53, h2_msi).
  - Race/ethnicity and insurance disparities (h9_race, h9_insurance, h9_rural).
  - Comorbidity associations (h10_*); prior-therapy associations (h11_*);
    symptom-burden associations (h12_*).
  - Liver/bone metastases and node-positivity main effects unadjusted (h3_liver,
    h3_bone, h3_node).

OVERALL CONCLUSION
==================
The cohort behaves like a clean, biology-driven dataset for palbociclib (the
prototypical CDK4/6 inhibitor) in the ER+ subgroup, where a +1.5 to +1.7 month
PFS benefit is reproducible, stratum-stable, and robust to multivariable
adjustment, with R^2 of the full model ~0.80. A weaker, directionally correct
olaparib x BRCA signal is also present but does not survive covariate
adjustment. Other targeted/immune therapies in this cohort — trastuzumab,
pembrolizumab, sacituzumab govitecan, and tamoxifen — do NOT exhibit their
canonical biomarker-driven benefits, suggesting either treatment assignment
patterns that confound the simple comparisons, or that these arms in the
underlying data-generating process were specified without an indication-specific
PFS advantage. Prognostic factors recapitulate the standard breast-cancer
playbook (stage IV, brain metastases, ECOG, low albumin, weight loss, high
Ki67, high LDH, high alkaline phosphatase, ER/PR/HER2 status, PIK3CA), with a
notable absence of any sociodemographic, comorbidity, prior-therapy, or
symptom-burden association — and SNP analyses (including a CYP2D6 x tamoxifen
pharmacogenomic test) returned uniformly null. The single most actionable
takeaway is the dominance of the ER+ x palbociclib predictive interaction.
