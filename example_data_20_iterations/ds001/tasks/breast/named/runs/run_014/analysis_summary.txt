Analysis Summary — ds001_breast (n=50,000)
============================================

Outcome: pfs_months (mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0.0–16.7).
The protocol explored 25 iterations of hypotheses on prognostic markers,
treatment main effects, biomarker–treatment interactions, demographics,
labs, comorbidities, and a multivariable synthesis.

1. Treatment main effects (Iteration 1)
---------------------------------------
- treatment_palbociclib: SUPPORTED — strong positive main effect.
  Mean PFS 5.41 mo on vs 4.31 mo off (diff +1.10 mo, p ≈ 0). The dominant
  treatment signal in the cohort.
- treatment_tamoxifen: small positive trend (+0.04 mo) but not significant
  in unadjusted comparison (p=0.077).
- treatment_olaparib, treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan: no significant
  unadjusted main effect on PFS.
- treatment_trastuzumab and treatment_pembrolizumab: small significantly
  *negative* unadjusted differences (–0.06 mo, p=0.029; –0.07 mo, p=0.018).
  These reverse direction once biomarker context is considered (see below),
  consistent with confounding by indication: trastuzumab is preferentially
  used in HER2+ (worse-prognosis) disease, and pembrolizumab in TNBC.

2. Hormone-receptor and HER2 status (Iteration 2)
-------------------------------------------------
- er_positive: SUPPORTED — +0.57 mo (p ≈ 4×10⁻¹⁴³).
- pr_positive: SUPPORTED — +0.36 mo (p ≈ 6×10⁻⁵⁹).
- her2_positive: SUPPORTED — −0.46 mo (p ≈ 6×10⁻⁷⁰); HER2+ disease is
  shorter PFS overall, which lines up with biology when HER2-targeted
  therapy is not always given.
- her2_low: small but significant +0.11 mo (p ≈ 10⁻⁶).

3. Disease burden and host status (Iteration 3)
-----------------------------------------------
- stage_iv: SUPPORTED — −1.54 mo (p ≈ 0).
- has_brain_mets: SUPPORTED — −1.05 mo (p ≈ 10⁻¹⁷⁸).
- ecog_ps: SUPPORTED — −1.18 mo per ECOG point (p ≈ 0); the largest
  individual prognostic gradient seen in the dataset.
- age_years: REFUTED in expected direction — slope was *positive*
  (+0.18 mo per year, p ≈ 0). In this cohort older age is associated
  with longer (not shorter) PFS, likely because older patients here are
  enriched for ER+/postmenopausal disease and slower-growing tumors.

4. Biomarker × treatment interactions (Iterations 4–9, 17, 22, 23)
------------------------------------------------------------------
- treatment_palbociclib × er_positive: STRONGLY SUPPORTED.
  Interaction +1.59 mo (p ≈ 9×10⁻²²⁹). In ER+ patients palbo gives
  +1.58 mo (5.89 vs 4.31, p ≈ 0); in ER− patients palbo has essentially
  no effect (−0.02 mo, p=0.63). The cohort's largest treatment–biomarker
  signal and the driver of the palbociclib main effect.
- treatment_olaparib × brca_any (BRCA1 OR BRCA2): SUPPORTED.
  Interaction +0.40 mo (p=0.021). Among BRCA carriers olaparib gives
  +0.35 mo (4.93 vs 4.59, p=0.041); in BRCA wild-type olaparib has no
  benefit (−0.06 mo, p=0.14).
- treatment_pembrolizumab × tnbc: weakly SUPPORTED.
  Interaction +0.19 mo (p=0.015), but the within-TNBC subgroup
  difference itself was not significant (+0.07 mo, p=0.22).
- treatment_pembrolizumab × msi_high: NOT SUPPORTED (interaction +0.22,
  p=0.47; only 498 MSI-H patients limits power).
- treatment_trastuzumab × her2_positive: NOT SUPPORTED in this dataset
  (interaction −0.03, p=0.69; within HER2+ trastuzumab non-significant).
  This is a notable null given biological expectation.
- treatment_tamoxifen × er_positive: NOT SUPPORTED (interaction −0.06,
  p=0.27); within ER+ tamoxifen showed no benefit (+0.02 mo, p=0.45).
  Tamoxifen × postmenopausal interaction was also non-significant.
- treatment_palbociclib × pik3ca_mutation: NOT SUPPORTED (interaction
  near zero, NS).
- treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan × tnbc and × her2_low: both NS.
- treatment_palbociclib + treatment_tamoxifen combination: combination
  arm did not show synergy; combo vs palbo-alone difference was small
  and the multiplicative interaction was NS.

5. Lab and tumor-biology continuous prognostic markers (Iterations 10, 14, 18)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- albumin_g_dl: SUPPORTED — slope +0.50 per g/dL (p ≈ 10⁻¹¹²).
- ldh_u_l: SUPPORTED — slope −5×10⁻⁴ per U/L (p=2×10⁻⁴).
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: SUPPORTED — slope −0.075 mo per percent
  (p ≈ 4×10⁻¹⁴⁸); a strong host-decline signal.
- ki67_pct: SUPPORTED — slope −0.019 mo per % (p ≈ 3×10⁻⁹³).
- pik3ca_mutation: SUPPORTED — −0.56 mo (p ≈ 6×10⁻¹⁴⁴).
- total_bilirubin_mg_dl: weakly significant (slope −0.07, p=0.046).
- nlr, crp_mg_l, hemoglobin_g_dl, tumor_size_cm: NOT supported as
  prognostic in this cohort (all p > 0.1).
- tp53_mutation, pten_loss: NOT supported as prognostic.
- chronic_kidney_disease, heart_failure, diabetes_mellitus,
  creatinine_mg_dl: NOT supported.
- liver_mets, bone_mets, pleural_effusion, adrenal_mets: NOT
  associated with PFS in this cohort (all p > 0.15) — somewhat
  surprising and suggests these flags may carry less independent
  prognostic information than ECOG/stage_iv/has_brain_mets in this
  data-generating process.

6. Symptoms and prior therapy (Iterations 15–16)
-----------------------------------------------
- fatigue_grade, pain_nrs, dyspnea_grade, appetite_loss_grade: none
  significantly associated with PFS (all p > 0.29).
- prior_lines_of_therapy, prior_chemotherapy, prior_radiation,
  prior_immunotherapy: none significantly associated with PFS.

7. Demographic and socioeconomic disparities (Iterations 11–12)
---------------------------------------------------------------
- race_ethnicity: NO significant differences in PFS across white,
  black, hispanic, asian, other (ANOVA p=0.49; black vs white +0.04
  mo, p=0.29; hispanic vs white +0.03 mo, p=0.38).
- insurance_type: NO significant differences (ANOVA p=0.82).
- rural_residence: NO significant difference (p=0.34).
The expected social-determinant disparities do NOT appear in this
synthesized cohort, which is itself an important descriptive finding.

8. SNP scan (Iteration 21)
-------------------------
- 24 SNP carrier indicators scanned with unadjusted t-tests vs PFS.
  Number of raw p<0.05 hits = 1 (about what is expected by chance for
  24 tests). Smallest p-value did not survive Bonferroni
  (α = 0.05/24 ≈ 0.0021). No SNP carries credible PFS signal.

9. Multivariable model (Iterations 19–20, 24)
---------------------------------------------
A single OLS on pfs_months with 30+ covariates (R² = 0.78) confirmed:
- ecog_ps: adj coef −1.17 (p ≈ 0).
- albumin_g_dl: adj coef +0.48 (p ≈ 0).
- treatment_palbociclib: adj coef +1.10 (p ≈ 0) — robust to adjustment.
- Other treatment main effects (trastuzumab, pembrolizumab, tamoxifen,
  sacituzumab) were either NS or marginally negative once biomarker
  adjustment is included; treatment_olaparib was marginal (−0.04,
  p=0.045) without an interaction term, but turns to a clear positive
  effect concentrated in BRCA carriers when the interaction is added.

A joint interaction model (key covariates plus six biomarker×drug
interactions) showed the same overall pattern:
- palbociclib × er_positive interaction adj +1.60 (p ≈ 0): supported.
- olaparib × brca_any adj +0.43 (p ≈ 0.014): supported.
- trastuzumab × her2_positive, tamoxifen × er_positive, pembrolizumab
  × msi_high, sacituzumab_govitecan × tnbc: NS in the joint model.

10. Subgroup-matched checks (Iteration 25)
------------------------------------------
- Within ER+: palbo vs no palbo +1.58 mo (p ≈ 0). SUPPORTED.
- Within BRCA+: olaparib vs no olaparib +0.35 mo (p=0.041). SUPPORTED.
- Within HER2+: trastuzumab vs no trastuzumab −0.08 mo (p=0.15). NOT
  supported.
- Within MSI-H: pembrolizumab vs no pembrolizumab +0.14 mo (p=0.64).
  Not supported (small n).
- Within ER+: tamoxifen vs no tamoxifen +0.02 mo (p=0.45). Not
  supported.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
The dominant features of this cohort are:
(a) ECOG performance status, stage IV, brain metastases, albumin,
    weight loss, ki67, pik3ca_mutation, ER/PR/HER2 status, and
    palbociclib treatment account for almost the entire explainable
    variation in PFS (R² ≈ 0.78 with these and a few other covariates).
(b) Palbociclib has a large, ER-restricted benefit (~+1.6 mo within
    ER+, none in ER−), consistent with the known biology of CDK4/6
    inhibition in HR+ disease.
(c) Olaparib has a modest, BRCA-restricted benefit, also consistent
    with known biology.
(d) The classical pairings trastuzumab→HER2+ and tamoxifen→ER+ did NOT
    show treatment benefits in this dataset — either by interaction
    test or by within-stratum comparison. Either the data-generation
    process in this synthetic cohort did not encode those benefits, or
    confounding by indication exactly offsets them; in either case the
    evidence in this dataset does not support efficacy for these two
    pairings.
(e) Pembrolizumab shows a weak TNBC-favored interaction but no
    convincing within-subgroup benefit (and no MSI-H signal, limited
    by sample size).
(f) Sacituzumab govitecan has no detected effect, even within TNBC or
    HER2-low subgroups.
(g) Race, insurance type, rural residence, and ECOG-aside symptom
    grades show no association with PFS in this cohort. SNP indicators
    do not carry credible signal after multiple-testing correction.
(h) Several "expected" prognostic features (visceral metastases such
    as liver/bone/pleural, comorbidities, NLR, CRP, hemoglobin,
    tp53_mutation, pten_loss, prior lines of therapy) were null in
    this dataset — a useful negative-finding map for future analyses.

Net: the strongest, most reproducible signals here are
ECOG → PFS (negative), albumin/ER/PR → PFS (positive), HER2+/stage
IV/brain mets/weight loss/ki67/pik3ca_mutation → PFS (negative), and
two biomarker-restricted treatment effects (palbociclib in ER+,
olaparib in BRCA+).
