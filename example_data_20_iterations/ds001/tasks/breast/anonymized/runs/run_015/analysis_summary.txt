ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_breast (50,000 patients, 124 anonymized features, outcome pfs_months)
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
This summary synthesizes 25 iterations of hypothesis-driven analysis on a
breast oncology cohort with progression-free survival in months (`pfs_months`,
mean 4.69 months, IQR 2.97-6.17, range 0-16.66) as the outcome. Hypotheses
were tested with univariate t-tests / ANOVA / Pearson correlation, stratified
subgroup tests, and multivariable OLS models with interaction terms. The work
proceeded from broad univariate scans toward focused multivariable
adjustments and interaction probes.

PROGNOSTIC SIGNAL: WHAT MOVES PFS
----------------------------------
A small subset of features carry essentially all the prognostic information.
The full multivariable model (all 125 numeric features entered jointly)
explains R² = 0.79 of the variance in PFS, but only ~12 features retain
p<0.001 after mutual adjustment.

Strong continuous / ordinal predictors (h1, h2, h11-h13):
  * feature_080 (range 30-90, mean 65) — STRONGEST predictor of any kind:
    Pearson r = +0.70, OLS slope ~+0.18 months PFS per unit. Quintile means
    rise monotonically from 2.28 to 7.21 months (Q1->Q5). Approximately
    linear over its full range.
  * feature_063 (3-level ordinal, 0/1/2; consistent with a stage-like
    variable) — strong negative trend (r = -0.32, p<1e-300; level 0 mean
    PFS 5.64, level 2 mean 3.29; per-level decrement ~1.17 months).
  * feature_067 (continuous): r = -0.116 (slope -0.077/unit), p ~ 4e-148.
  * feature_019 (continuous, range 1.5-5.5): r = +0.100, slope +0.48/unit,
    p ~ 9e-112.
  * feature_101 (continuous, range 1-100): r = -0.091, slope -0.019/unit,
    p ~ 3e-93.

Strong binary biomarkers (h3-h10):
  * feature_056 (prevalence 30%): mean PFS 3.62 vs 5.15 in feature_056=0
    (diff -1.54 months, p≈0). Largest negative binary effect.
  * feature_042 (prevalence 35%): mean PFS 5.41 vs 4.31 (diff +1.10 months,
    p≈0). Largest positive binary effect.
  * feature_048 (10%): -1.05 months (p~1e-178).
  * feature_015 (35%): -0.56 months (p~6e-144).
  * feature_111 (70%): +0.57 months (p~4e-143).
  * feature_040 (18%): -0.46 months (p~6e-70).

Univariate signals that DROP OUT after mutual adjustment (h25, h26, h28):
  * feature_039 (univariate +0.36 months) is highly correlated with
    feature_111 (phi=0.67). After adjusting for feature_111, feature_039
    coefficient = +0.014 (p=0.32) — no independent contribution.
  * feature_034 (univariate +0.11 months) is mutually exclusive with
    feature_040 (feature_040=1 implies feature_034=0). In multivariable
    model feature_034 coefficient ~ 0 (p=0.88).
  * feature_077's small univariate signal (+0.07, p=0.005) disappears after
    adjustment (p=0.58). feature_055 had a small but borderline effect
    (-0.14 months, p=0.024) of unclear independent importance.

DEMOGRAPHIC DISPARITY ANALYSIS (h14-h19, h30-h32)
--------------------------------------------------
A central concern in EHR-derived oncology cohorts is whether outcomes or
treatment allocation differ by race/ethnicity (`feature_011`) or insurance
(`feature_089`). The cohort shows striking absence of disparity at every
checkpoint:

  * Raw PFS by race: white 4.68, black 4.72, hispanic 4.71, asian 4.74,
    other 4.76 months. ANOVA F=0.85, p=0.49 (h14, REFUTED).
  * Raw PFS by insurance: medicaid 4.71, medicare 4.69, private 4.68,
    uninsured 4.73 months. ANOVA F=0.31, p=0.82 (h15, REFUTED).
  * Treatment/biomarker allocation by race (h16): all 8 top binary features
    showed no race-based prevalence differences (every chi-square p>0.05;
    prevalence within ~3 percentage points across racial groups for every
    feature). Stage feature_063 distribution by race is also flat
    (h32, p~0.7).
  * Treatment/biomarker allocation by insurance (h17): same null pattern
    across all 8 top features (every chi-square p>0.18).
  * feature_080 distribution by race (h30): ANOVA F=0.81, p=0.52, mean
    range <0.5 units across race groups. By insurance (h31): mean range
    <0.1 units, all p>0.5.
  * Adjusted race effect (h18): in OLS adjusting for feature_080,
    feature_063, feature_067, feature_019, feature_101, and the 8 top
    binary biomarkers, every race coefficient was non-significant (|coef|
    <= 0.03 months; all p > 0.28).
  * Adjusted insurance effect (h19): same pattern; all insurance
    coefficients non-significant (|coef| <= 0.02; all p > 0.59).

Interpretation: in this synthetic/de-identified cohort, both raw and
risk-adjusted PFS are essentially identical across demographic strata, and
there is no measurable allocation imbalance in the major
treatments/biomarkers by race or insurance. Any disparity attribution to
this cohort is unsupported.

INTERACTION / SUBGROUP HETEROGENEITY (h20-h24, h33, h34, h36)
--------------------------------------------------------------
Most main effects appear approximately additive on the natural pfs_months
scale; a few sharp interactions stand out:

  * feature_042 × feature_111 (h21): SYNERGISTIC. Interaction coef ~+1.58,
    p≈0. feature_042 confers ~+0.36 months in feature_111=0 patients but
    ~+1.94 months in feature_111=1 patients.
  * feature_042 × feature_015 (h22): ANTAGONISTIC. Interaction coef ~-1.67,
    p≈0. The favorable effect of feature_042 (+~1.93 months in
    feature_015=0) shrinks to near zero (+0.26 months) in feature_015=1.
  * feature_015 × feature_111 (h23): ANTAGONISTIC. Interaction coef
    ~-0.84, p~5e-171.
  * feature_042 × feature_040 (h24): ANTAGONISTIC. Interaction coef ~-1.38,
    p≈0.
  * feature_080 × feature_063 (h20): small but significant negative
    interaction (coef ~-0.006/unit, p=5.6e-08); the per-level decrement
    from feature_063 grows by ~0.006 months for each additional unit of
    feature_080.

Effects with NO meaningful interaction (additivity confirmed):
  * feature_056 × feature_063 (h33): effect of feature_056 is ~-1.5 to
    -1.6 months at every level of feature_063. Strictly additive.
  * feature_042 × feature_063 (h34): effect of feature_042 is +1.09 to
    +1.15 months at every level of feature_063. Strictly additive.

Behavior in extreme strata (h36): in the high-risk subgroup
(feature_056=1 AND feature_048=1, n=1528), mean PFS = 2.65 months. Even
here, feature_042=1 raises PFS by ~1.01 months (3.31 vs 2.30, p≈1.5e-15),
showing that feature_042's benefit is preserved even in the worst-risk
stratum.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. PFS is well-predicted by a compact set of clinical features
   (~12 predictors capture R²~0.79). The strongest single predictor is
   continuous feature_080, with stage-like ordinal feature_063 and binary
   feature_056/feature_042 each accounting for ~1.0-1.5 months PFS
   difference.
2. Several binary "treatment-like" features (feature_039, feature_034,
   feature_077) carry no independent prognostic value once their highly
   correlated partners (feature_111 and feature_040) are accounted for.
3. The cohort shows no demographic disparities in PFS, in major
   biomarker prevalence, or in stage distribution by race or insurance.
   Adjusted race/insurance effects on PFS are ~0 with very tight
   confidence intervals — the absence of disparity is robust.
4. A small number of biologically suggestive interactions exist
   (feature_042 × feature_111 synergy; feature_042 × feature_015,
   feature_015 × feature_111, feature_042 × feature_040 antagonisms;
   small feature_080 × feature_063 modification). The remaining
   prognostic effects are essentially additive across stratifications
   tested. Notably, the largest binary effects (feature_056, feature_042)
   are preserved across stage strata and even in extreme high-risk
   subgroups, indicating that they reflect prognostic axes that operate
   independently of clinical staging.
5. The remaining ~110 features carry no detectable signal beyond noise
   in the multivariable model — typical of large EHR-derived feature
   sets with many sparse comorbidity flags.
