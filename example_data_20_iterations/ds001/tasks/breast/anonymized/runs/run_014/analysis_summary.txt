ds001_breast — Analysis Summary
==================================
Cohort: 50,000 patients with breast oncology records, single outcome pfs_months
(mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0–16.7). 127 anonymized features: 82 binary, 7
multilevel ordinal (3–11 levels), 36 continuous, and 2 categorical (race in
feature_011, insurance in feature_089). The protocol ran 25 iterations of the
propose–test–refine loop.


1. Strongly prognostic main effects (independent of treatment)
--------------------------------------------------------------
The dataset contains a small number of features with very large, robust
prognostic effects on pfs_months that survive multivariable adjustment:

  feature_080 (continuous, 30–90):  +0.176 mo per unit (Pearson r=+0.70,
        p≈0). This is the dominant single predictor; alone it explains
        ~49% of variance in PFS, and the predicted PFS difference between
        the lowest and highest values of feature_080 is ~10.6 months.
        Its sign and magnitude are consistent with a functional-status /
        performance-style score.

  feature_063 (3-level ordinal):  monotonic dose-response. Mean PFS at
        levels 0/1/2 = 5.64 / 4.44 / 3.29 (ANOVA p≈0; ordinal trend
        r=-0.32). Adjusted decrement vs level 0: -1.19 (level 1),
        -2.35 (level 2). This pattern is consistent with an
        ordinal stage marker.

  feature_056 (binary, 30% prev):  -1.55 mo (p≈0). Largest single binary
        decrement. Effect is identical in treated and untreated patients
        (interaction p=0.51) — pure prognostic, not predictive. The
        decrement is slightly attenuated at higher feature_063 stage
        (interaction p=7e-5) and slightly amplified at higher feature_080
        (interaction coef -0.011/unit, p=2e-21), but is strongly negative
        in every subgroup.

  feature_048 (binary, 10% prev):  -0.97 mo (p≈0). Acts purely
        prognostically; no interaction with feature_042 (p=0.66).

  feature_067 (continuous, 0–24.6):  -0.075 mo per unit (p=4e-148).
  feature_019 (continuous, 1.5–5.5):  +0.499 mo per unit (p=9e-112).

Together these six factors capture most of the prognostic signal in PFS
and define the patient-risk backbone of the cohort.


2. The predictive biomarker / treatment system
----------------------------------------------
feature_042 (binary, 35% prev) carries the largest treatment-like positive
main effect (+1.10 mo, p≈0). Its association with PFS, however, is not
uniform — it is governed by a tightly structured biomarker / resistance
system that the iterative analysis uncovered:

  feature_111 (binary, 70% prev) is the NECESSARY positive biomarker.
        feature_111 has NO main effect on PFS in untreated patients
        (raw difference +0.011 mo; adjusted β=-0.001, p=0.69), but
        confers a +1.53-mo advantage in treated patients (p≈0).
        Conditional on treatment, feature_111 is a purely predictive
        marker: feature_042 only helps when feature_111=1.

  feature_015 (35% prev) is a NEGATIVE PREDICTIVE marker. Among the 111+
        responder population, feature_015=0 patients gain +2.38 mo from
        treatment, while feature_015=1 patients gain +0.04 mo
        (interaction coef -1.65, p≈0). feature_015 has no main effect
        when feature_042=0.

  feature_040 (18% prev) is a second NEGATIVE PREDICTIVE marker. Among
        111+ patients, 040=0 → +1.92 mo benefit; 040=1 → +0.02 mo
        (interaction coef -1.32, p≈0). No main effect when 042=0.

  feature_101 (continuous, 1–100) is a CONTINUOUS NEGATIVE PREDICTIVE
        gradient ON TOP of these binary markers. Within the
        (042=1, 111=1, 015=0, 040=0) responder subgroup,
        r(feature_101, PFS) = -0.52, p≈0. In the untreated arm and in
        111-negative patients the correlation is essentially zero. The
        three-way interaction feature_042:feature_111:feature_101
        coefficient is -0.075 mo per unit (p≈0).

  feature_048 looked like a candidate predictive marker because of its
        strong negative main effect, but stratified analyses (042 effect:
        +1.58 vs +1.53 by feature_048 status) and the adjusted
        interaction p=0.66 confirmed it is purely prognostic.

  feature_039 had a deceptively large 042-interaction in the marginal
        scan (coef +1.03, p≈0), but feature_039 is heavily correlated
        with feature_111: 92.9% of feature_039=1 patients are 111+, vs
        28.9% of feature_039=0 patients (chi² p≈0). After stratifying
        on feature_111, feature_039 confers no additional treatment
        effect (3-way interaction p=0.14). It is best understood as a
        partial surrogate for feature_111.

The full cell decomposition of the treatment effect (Δ PFS for 042=1 vs
042=0):

      feature_111  feature_015  feature_040     ΔPFS    n
            0          *           *           ~0.00    15,114
            1          0           0          +2.91    16,785  (largest)
            1          1           *          +0.04    ~12,000
            1          0           1          +0.02     ~3,500

Within the maximal-benefit subgroup, the benefit further decreases
roughly linearly with feature_101.


3. Multivariable consolidation
------------------------------
A single linear model that incorporates the prognostic backbone plus the
predictive interactions accounts for R²=0.867 (N=50,000):

  pfs_months ~ feature_080 + feature_067 + feature_019 + feature_101
              + C(feature_063) + feature_056 + feature_048
              + feature_042 + feature_111 + feature_015 + feature_040
              + feature_042:feature_111
              + feature_042:feature_015
              + feature_042:feature_040
              + feature_042:feature_101
              + feature_042:feature_111:feature_101

In this model the main coefficients for feature_111, feature_015, and
feature_040 are essentially zero (p > 0.7 for each), confirming that
those features have no impact on PFS independent of feature_042. The
predictive interactions, by contrast, are all extraordinarily significant
and their magnitudes are clinically meaningful.


4. Demographic disparities — none detected
------------------------------------------
The dataset includes race (feature_011: white, black, hispanic, asian,
other) and insurance (feature_089: private, medicare, medicaid,
uninsured). Across multiple analyses, neither variable shows any of the
usual disparity signals:

  Outcome:  PFS by race ranges 4.68–4.76 mo (ANOVA p=0.49). PFS by
            insurance ranges 4.69–4.73 mo (ANOVA p=0.82).

  Treatment receipt:  feature_042 prevalence is 0.337–0.351 across all
            races and 0.346–0.350 across all insurance categories.

  Biomarker prevalence:  feature_111 rate is 0.687–0.708 across races,
            0.694–0.701 across insurances. Other strong markers
            (056, 048, 015, 040, 063 stage) show no race or insurance
            patterning either.

  Treatment effect:  Adjusted β(feature_042) by race is
            white +1.10, asian +1.15, black +1.10, hispanic +1.11,
            other +1.09; by insurance: private +1.09, medicare +1.11,
            medicaid +1.11, uninsured +1.14. Within feature_111=1
            patients, treatment benefit by race is +1.58 (white),
            +1.58 (black), +1.58 (hispanic), +1.49 (asian), +1.56
            (other) — all interaction terms p > 0.20.

  Adjusted main effects:  In the full multivariable model, race and
            insurance coefficients are all within ±0.02 of zero with
            p ≥ 0.39.

These uniformly null findings should be reported as such: in this
cohort, race and insurance are not associated with PFS, treatment
allocation, biomarker frequency, or treatment efficacy.


5. Hypotheses that were proposed but NOT supported
--------------------------------------------------
- feature_039 main effect after adjustment (h4.2): attenuated to 0.014
  (p=0.32); not independently prognostic.
- feature_034 main effect after adjustment (h4.3): attenuated to 0.002
  (p=0.87).
- feature_077 independent association (h20.1): adjusted β=+0.009
  (p=0.33), not independent.
- feature_042 × feature_063 interaction (h7.1): no stage modification.
- feature_042 × race (h6.1) and × insurance (h6.2): both null.
- feature_080 × feature_042 interaction (h15.2, h22.1): no modification
  of treatment effect by patient functional status.
- feature_042 × feature_056 (h5.4): no interaction; 056 is purely
  prognostic.
- feature_042 × feature_048 (h10.3): no interaction; 048 is purely
  prognostic.
- feature_039 enhances treatment beyond its 111-correlation (h8.1, h9.1):
  the apparent enhancement disappears after conditioning on 111.
- feature_019 × feature_042 (h23.1): borderline (p=0.07), small magnitude
  (-0.039 per unit), not retained as a robust finding.


6. Hypotheses that WERE supported
---------------------------------
- Strong prognostic main effects: feature_080, feature_063, feature_056,
  feature_048, feature_067, feature_019, feature_101 (h2.1–h2.4, h3.1,
  h1.2, h1.3).
- feature_042 has a robust positive treatment-like main effect that
  survives multivariable adjustment (h4.1).
- feature_042 × feature_111 synergy (h5.1).
- feature_042 × feature_015 antagonism (h5.2, h10.1).
- feature_042 × feature_040 antagonism (h5.3, h10.2).
- feature_111 has no prognostic effect when 042=0 (purely predictive)
  (h11.1, h11.2).
- feature_056 is purely prognostic (h12.1).
- feature_056 × stage modest attenuation (h13.1).
- feature_056 × feature_080 amplification (h14.1, h21.1).
- feature_101 modifies the 042 effect; the modification is concentrated
  in 111-positive treated patients (h15.1, h16.1).
- Maximal treatment subgroup (042=1, 111=1, 015=0, 040=0) gains
  +2.91 mo (h10.4, h25.1).
- Comprehensive model R² = 0.867 (h17.1).
- feature_080 dominates the prognostic landscape with a 10.6-mo
  difference across its range (h24.1, h24.2).
- No demographic disparities (h6.1, h6.2, h6.3, h18.1, h19.1, h25.2).


7. Overall conclusions
----------------------
The data describe a well-structured biomarker-driven treatment landscape
for a single intervention (feature_042) in this 50,000-patient breast
cohort. Treatment efficacy is governed by a 4-marker rule:

  Patients who are feature_111-positive AND feature_015-negative AND
  feature_040-negative gain a meaningful PFS benefit from feature_042;
  the magnitude of that benefit decreases linearly with higher
  feature_101 values. All other patients gain essentially nothing.

Independent of treatment, prognosis is dominated by feature_080
(continuous performance-style score), feature_063 (ordinal stage),
feature_056 and feature_048 (binary risk markers), and feature_067 /
feature_019 / feature_101 (continuous risk markers).

The dataset shows no association between race or insurance and any of
the prognostic, predictive, treatment-allocation, or treatment-efficacy
quantities tested. Reports of this cohort should not invoke disparities
that are not present in the data.
