ds001_breast — Analysis Summary
================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients, 127 anonymized features, outcome = pfs_months
        (mean 4.69 mo, median 4.54 mo, SD 2.50 mo, range 0–16.66).

Across 25 propose-analyze-refine iterations the analysis screened all 82
binary, 36 continuous, and 7 multilevel/categorical features for univariable
association with `pfs_months`, then built a multivariable OLS model to test
which signals were independent and to probe interactions.

1.  Top binary main effects (univariable t-tests on pfs_months):
       feature_056=1: ΔPFS = -1.54 mo (p≈0)  -- strongest negative binary
       feature_042=1: ΔPFS = +1.10 mo (p≈0)  -- strongest positive binary
       feature_048=1: ΔPFS = -1.05 mo (p≈1e-178)
       feature_015=1: ΔPFS = -0.56 mo (p≈1e-144)
       feature_111=1: ΔPFS = +0.57 mo (p≈1e-143)
       feature_040=1: ΔPFS = -0.46 mo (p≈1e-70)
       feature_039=1: ΔPFS = +0.36 mo (p≈1e-59)  (loses signal in MV adjustment)
       feature_034=1: ΔPFS = +0.11 mo (p≈1e-6)   (loses signal in MV adjustment)
   Roughly 33 of the 82 binaries showed no main effect (p>0.05); race
   (feature_011) and insurance (feature_089) were among the null group.

2.  Top continuous main effects (Pearson r vs pfs_months):
       feature_080: r = +0.70 (p≈0)  -- by far the dominant single predictor;
            mean PFS rises monotonically from 0.56 mo (range 30–40) to
            8.20 mo (range 80–90). Behaves like a positive prognostic axis,
            consistent with patient age in years (older → longer PFS, the
            pattern expected if older patients have more indolent
            ER-positive disease).
       feature_067: r = -0.12 (p≈4e-148)  negative
       feature_019: r = +0.10 (p≈9e-112)  positive
       feature_101: r = -0.09 (p≈3e-93)   negative
       Other continuous features had |r|<0.02 (most non-significant).

3.  Multilevel / ordinal categoricals:
       feature_063 (3-level, 0/1/2): clear monotone gradient -- mean PFS
            5.64 / 4.44 / 3.29 mo (ANOVA p≈0). Slope ≈ -1.18 mo per level
            in adjusted models. Behaves like a stage- or grade-like axis.
       feature_021, feature_024, feature_127, feature_001, feature_073,
            feature_016: ALL ANOVA p > 0.27. No main-effect signal.
       feature_011 (race) and feature_089 (insurance): ALL ANOVA p > 0.45.
            No main-effect signal, even unadjusted.

4.  Multivariable OLS (13 leading univariable predictors): R² = 0.792.
       After mutual adjustment, every predictor retained its direction and
       statistical significance EXCEPT feature_039 and feature_034, both of
       which collapsed to p > 0.3 -- their univariable signals were entirely
       confounded by the other features. The 11 surviving predictors are:
            feature_080  +0.176 mo / unit  (≈+1.76 mo per 10-unit increment)
            feature_063  -1.17 mo per ordinal level
            feature_056  -1.55 mo (binary)
            feature_042  +1.10 mo (binary)
            feature_048  -0.98 mo (binary)
            feature_015  -0.58 mo (binary)
            feature_111  +0.54 mo (binary)
            feature_040  -0.47 mo (binary)
            feature_019  +0.48 mo / unit
            feature_067  -0.077 mo / unit
            feature_101  -0.019 mo / unit
       Adding race (feature_011) and insurance (feature_089) as adjusters
       leaves the R² unchanged (0.792) and produces no race/insurance terms
       with p < 0.18.

5.  Subgroup and interaction findings:
       * The negative effect of feature_056 was clinically uniform across
         races (range -1.34 to -1.63 mo), insurance categories (range
         -1.43 to -1.65 mo), feature_063 levels (-1.60 / -1.51 / -1.48), and
         feature_080 quartiles (-1.43 / -1.57 / -1.61 / -1.61).
       * feature_056 × feature_063 interaction: small but statistically
         significant attenuation of feature_056's harm at higher stage
         levels (~+0.07 to +0.12 mo per stage step; p=0.030 / 0.006).
       * feature_080 × feature_056: significant negative interaction
         (-0.011 mo/unit, p≈3e-12). The age-related PFS benefit is slightly
         attenuated in feature_056=1 patients.
       * feature_080 × feature_063: significant negative interaction at
         level 2 (-0.013 mo/unit, p≈4e-9); borderline at level 1.
       * NO interaction: feature_056 × feature_042 (p=0.36),
         feature_056 × feature_111 (p=0.75),
         feature_042 × feature_063 (both terms p>0.10),
         feature_063 × feature_089 (smallest p=0.27, all NS),
         feature_011 × feature_063 within-stratum ANOVA (all p>0.4),
         feature_056 × feature_089 (smallest p=0.17).
       The dominant pattern is therefore additivity: most predictors
       contribute independently, with only modest effect-modification by
       feature_080 and feature_063.

6.  Disparities check (race, insurance):
       Both univariable and fully-adjusted analyses found NO association of
       feature_011 (white / hispanic / black / asian / other) or feature_089
       (medicare / private / medicaid / uninsured) with pfs_months. Within
       each feature_063 stratum, race-stratified mean PFS spans only
       ~0.07 mo. Insurance × stage interaction p=0.27. Race × feature_056
       interaction p=0.027 (white) but the size of the modification is
       only ~0.18 mo, well below clinical significance and likely a Type-I
       artefact given multiple testing.

Conclusions
-----------
The cohort's progression-free survival is dominated by a single, very strong
positive predictor (feature_080, behaviorally consistent with age, r≈+0.70)
together with a stage-like ordinal (feature_063) and a small set of binary
prognostic markers led by feature_056 (negative) and feature_042 (positive).
Eleven features explain ~79% of variance in pfs_months in additive form;
interactions among them are statistically detectable but clinically modest
(at most ~0.1–0.2 mo of effect-modification per stratum). Race and insurance
are NOT prognostic in this cohort -- their absence of effect persists in
unadjusted, age/stage-adjusted, and stratified analyses, and within the
final 13-predictor model. The signals from the early univariable screen for
feature_039 and feature_034 turned out to be confounder-driven and did not
survive multivariable adjustment.
