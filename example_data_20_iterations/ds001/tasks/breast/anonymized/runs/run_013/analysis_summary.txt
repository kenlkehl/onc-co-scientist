Analysis summary — ds001_breast (n=50,000, outcome: pfs_months)
================================================================

Overview
--------
The cohort comprises 50,000 breast cancer patients with 127 anonymized features
(82 binary, 43 continuous/ordinal, 2 multi-category: feature_011 race-like,
feature_089 insurance-like) and a single continuous outcome, pfs_months
(progression-free survival in months; mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0–16.7). Across
25 iterations I screened main effects, demographic disparities, treatment-
biomarker interactions, and assembled a final multivariable model. Below I walk
through the hypotheses tested in order, what the data showed, and the overall
clinical interpretation.

Iteration 1–3: univariate screening
-----------------------------------
A pass over all 82 binary features and 43 continuous features identified the
strongest candidates for inclusion in later models.

Binary features with the largest mean PFS differences:
  feature_056 (n=14,969 positive): -1.54 months (p~0).            [refuted: H1=supported]
  feature_042 (n=17,428 positive): +1.10 months (p~0).            [H2 supported]
  feature_048 (n=5,090):            -1.05 months (p=1.5e-178).    [H3 supported]
  feature_015 (n=17,365):           -0.56 months (p=6e-144).      [H4 supported]
  feature_111 (n=34,886):           +0.57 months (p=4e-143).      [H5 supported]
  feature_040 (n=9,060):            -0.46 months (p=6e-70).       [H6 supported]
  feature_039 (n=31,954):           +0.36 months (p=6e-59).       [H7 supported]

Continuous features:
  feature_080 (range 30–90, mean 65): r=+0.70, p~0 (an enormous correlation).
  feature_063 (levels 0/1/2, mean 0.80): r=-0.32, p~0.
  feature_067 (range 0–24.6): r=-0.115, p=4e-148.
  feature_019 (range 1.5–5.5): r=+0.10, p=9e-112.
  feature_101 (range 1–100): r=-0.091, p=3e-93.

All five hypotheses (H8–H12) about these continuous predictors were supported
with the predicted sign. In the early multivariable model (H13, R^2=0.79),
feature_080 (+0.176/unit), feature_063 (-1.17/level), feature_056 (-1.55),
feature_042 (+1.10), feature_048 (-0.98), feature_015 (-0.58), feature_111
(+0.53), feature_040 (-0.47) and the continuous biomarkers all kept the
expected sign and high significance. feature_039 (p=0.32) and feature_034
(p=0.88) were the only top-univariate features to lose significance after
adjustment, which already foreshadowed that their univariate effects came
from confounding rather than independent prognostic value.

Iteration 5–7, 16, 21–22: demographic equity
--------------------------------------------
I tested whether race (feature_011) or insurance (feature_089) is associated
with PFS, with treatment access (feature_042 receipt), or with treatment
efficacy. All five demographic hypotheses (H14, H15, H16, H17, H18, H19, H33,
H34, H40, H41) failed to reject the null:

  • PFS by race: ANOVA F=0.85, p=0.49; means span only 4.68–4.76 months.
  • PFS by insurance: ANOVA F=0.31, p=0.82; means span 4.69–4.73.
  • feature_042 receipt by race: 0.337–0.351 (chi-square NS).
  • feature_042 receipt by insurance: 0.346–0.350 (chi-square NS).
  • feature_042 efficacy by race within feature_111=1: +1.49 to +1.58 months,
    interaction p>0.65 across all pairwise contrasts.
  • feature_042 efficacy by insurance: equivalent across groups.
  • feature_080 means do not differ by race (ANOVA p>0.4).
  • In the fully adjusted model, all race and insurance coefficients are
    < 0.04 months and non-significant. Race and insurance therefore appear
    to be conditionally independent of pfs_months given clinical features.

Bottom line: this dataset shows no measurable demographic disparity in
outcomes, treatment access, or treatment effect.

Iteration 8–10, 23–24: predictive treatment–biomarker structure
---------------------------------------------------------------
The most clinically important finding emerged from interaction testing.
feature_042 is a treatment-like binary whose effect is *entirely localized*
to a precise biomarker subgroup.

  • feature_042 × feature_111 (H20): interaction +1.59, p=9e-229. In
    feature_111=0 patients feature_042 yields no benefit (-0.01 months);
    in feature_111=1 patients it yields +1.58 months. feature_111 alone is
    not prognostic — among feature_042=0 patients its effect collapses to
    +0.011 months (H30, refuted). feature_111 is therefore a purely
    *predictive* biomarker.
  • feature_042 × feature_015 (H21): interaction -1.62, p=4e-255. feature_015
    abolishes the feature_042 benefit.
  • feature_042 × feature_040 (H22): interaction -1.36, p=2e-118. feature_040
    abolishes the feature_042 benefit.

I refined this into a single eligibility rule (H23): the responsive subgroup
is defined by feature_111 = 1 AND feature_015 = 0 AND feature_040 = 0
(n=18,681, ~37% of cohort). Stratifying by all eight cells of
feature_111 × feature_015 × feature_040:
  • Eligible (1,0,0): feature_042 off=4.30, on=7.21, diff=+2.91 months.
  • All seven other cells: feature_042 effect ranges -0.12 to +0.09 (null).

The classification rule cleanly separates responders from non-responders.
This is a textbook predictive-biomarker pattern: feature_042 looks like a
targeted therapy that requires a positive marker (feature_111) and the
absence of two resistance markers (feature_015, feature_040) — analogous to,
e.g., an HER2-positive treatment that is rendered ineffective by specific
co-occurring resistance mutations.

H24 tested whether feature_039 acts as an additional predictive modifier
beyond the eligibility triple. Within (feature_111=1, feature_042=1) the
mean PFS is 5.78 (feature_039=0) vs 5.91 (feature_039=1), diff +0.13 — a
small but real residual interaction. feature_039 is therefore a weak
secondary modifier; it is not a third major axis.

H42 (iteration 23) confirmed the three predictive features are nearly
independent (pairwise crosstab differences <1 percentage point), so they
genuinely tag different biological factors rather than a single subtype.

H43 (iteration 24) showed that all the largest pairwise binary interactions
in the cohort involve feature_042 or feature_111. Once those are accounted
for, no large higher-order structure remains.

Iteration 11–12, 18–20: prognostic structure of the cohort
----------------------------------------------------------
feature_080 dominates the prognostic landscape. Its univariate r=+0.70 with
PFS is preserved in the multivariable model (coef +0.176/unit; H38
confirmed approximate linearity, with PFS rising monotonically across
deciles from ~2.4 months at feature_080~30 to ~7.5 at feature_080~90).

feature_063 is approximately linear across its three levels (H37): means
5.64, 4.44, 3.29 — per-level decrement ~-1.18 months, fitting the linear
coefficient exactly.

Two interactions modify prognostic effects by feature_080:
  • feature_056 × feature_080 (H25): -0.011/unit, p=3e-12. The negative
    impact of feature_056 is amplified at higher feature_080.
  • feature_063 × feature_080 (H26): -0.006/unit, p<1e-7. Same pattern for
    the staging-like factor.
  • feature_048 × feature_080: -0.008/unit (also significant).

By contrast, feature_042 efficacy is *not* modified by feature_080 (H27 was
refuted): within the eligibility subgroup the treatment effect is roughly
constant across age deciles (range +2.65 to +3.17 months). Treatment
efficacy is biomarker-driven, not demographic-driven.

H39 (iteration 20) verified that the eligibility-defined feature_042
benefit holds even in the worst-prognosis strata (feature_056=1 or
feature_048=1): treated eligible mean ~5.5 vs untreated eligible ~2.7,
a ~+2.8-month benefit that stacks additively on the otherwise low baseline.

Iteration 13: ordinal severity features
---------------------------------------
H28 and H29 tested whether the 0–4 ordinal features (feature_021, _024,
_127, _001, _073) and the 0–10 ordinal feature_016 — plausibly symptom or
performance-status grades — are associated with PFS. Every Pearson r was
between -0.004 and +0.005 (all NS, p>0.29). Mean PFS varies by less than
0.06 months across all five levels of each ordinal feature. These features
carry no detectable prognostic signal in this cohort. Both hypotheses
were refuted.

Iteration 14–15, 25: building toward the final model
----------------------------------------------------
H30 and H31 refined our understanding of feature_111, feature_015,
feature_040: in patients not receiving feature_042, all three have near-
zero residual effect. Their large univariate effects came almost entirely
from their interaction with feature_042 (whether they enable or block
its benefit), not from independent prognosis.

H32 confirmed that the seven remaining variables (feature_080, feature_063,
feature_056, feature_048, feature_019, feature_067, feature_101) hold up
as independent prognostic factors with stable signs and large coefficients
when included alongside the eligible × feature_042 interaction
(R^2 = 0.876).

H44 (final, iteration 25) tested whether the parsimonious model
  pfs_months ~ feature_080 + feature_063 + feature_056 + feature_048 +
              feature_019 + feature_067 + feature_101 + eligible*feature_042
captures most explainable variance. Adding race + insurance increases R^2
by 0.0001. Adding 10 more binary features (feature_034, _077, _086, …)
raises R^2 from 0.876 to ~0.879. The eight-term model is essentially
saturating in this dataset.

Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
--------------------------------
Supported (29):  H1, H2, H3, H4, H5, H6, H7, H8, H9, H10, H11, H12, H13,
                 H20, H21, H22, H23, H24, H25, H26, H32, H35, H36, H37,
                 H38, H39, H43, H44; H30 supported as refined null.
Refuted (15):    H14, H15, H16, H17, H18, H19, H27, H28, H29, H30 (its
                 prognostic version), H31, H33, H34, H40, H41, H42 (also
                 supported as null — three features are independent).

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. The cohort has a large, almost age-like prognostic axis (feature_080,
   r=+0.70 with PFS) plus a staging-like ordinal (feature_063, ~-1.2/level)
   that together explain the bulk of between-patient variance. Several
   binary features (feature_056, feature_048) and continuous biomarkers
   (feature_019, feature_067, feature_101) make additional independent
   contributions.

2. The treatment-biomarker story is sharp and binary in nature. feature_042
   delivers an additional ~+2.9 months of PFS on top of baseline only in
   patients meeting all three of (feature_111 = 1, feature_015 = 0,
   feature_040 = 0). In every other configuration its benefit is null.
   This holds across age, race, insurance, and prognostic stratum.

3. Demographic features (race, insurance) appear neither prognostic
   independently of clinical features, nor associated with treatment
   access, nor with heterogeneity of treatment effect. There is no
   signal of bias on these axes in the dataset.

4. The 0–4/0–10 ordinal features included in the cohort do not carry
   prognostic information for PFS — a notable null worth flagging,
   since clinically these labels often look like performance-status or
   symptom grades but here they are uninformative.

5. A parsimonious 8-term model achieves R^2 ~ 0.88 on the outcome.
