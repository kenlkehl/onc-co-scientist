ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_breast (n=50,000)
==========================================

OUTCOME
-------
The dataset contains a single continuous outcome, pfs_months (mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0.0–16.7).

OVERALL STRATEGY
----------------
Across 25 iterations the analysis moved from broad univariate screening of all 127 features
through multivariable adjustment, then to interaction discovery, subgroup quantification,
and finally a consolidated multivariable model. A single OLS model with eight terms ends up
explaining 87.6% of the variance in pfs_months — i.e., this cohort has a very clean,
strongly structured signal.


PRIMARY FINDINGS
----------------

1. Age-like feature_080 is the single strongest correlate of pfs_months (Iteration 1)
   - feature_080 has range 30–90, mean ~65; almost certainly age in years.
   - Pearson r = +0.70; slope +0.176 mo per unit.
   - Mean pfs_months by decade: <=40 → 0.56, 40s → 1.52, 50s → 3.06, 60s → 4.67, 70s → 6.32,
     80s → 8.20. The relationship is monotonic, near-linear, and survives adjustment for all
     other features (Iter 22 confirms quadratic term adds <0.001 to R²).
   - Direction (older → longer pfs) is the opposite of the typical age–prognosis association
     in many cancers; consistent with breast-cancer biology in which older patients more often
     have indolent, hormone-receptor-positive disease.

2. A single dominant treatment effect: feature_042 + feature_111 in biomarker-negative
   patients (Iterations 5–11)

   The most clinically important pattern is a 3-way structure between two treatments
   (feature_042, feature_111) and two resistance markers (feature_015, feature_040):

   a. Strong treatment-by-biomarker interactions (Iter 5–6):
      - feature_042 × feature_015 interaction = -1.62 (p ~ 4e-255)
      - feature_042 × feature_040 interaction = -1.36 (p ~ 2e-118)
      - feature_111 × feature_015 interaction = -0.80 (p ~ 1e-56)
      - feature_111 × feature_040 interaction = -0.66 (p ~ 4e-26)
      Each marker fully abolishes the corresponding treatment's benefit.

   b. The benefits are restricted to the doubly-negative biomarker subgroup
      (feature_015=0 AND feature_040=0, n=26,760, 54% of cohort) (Iter 7).

   c. Within the biomarker-negative subgroup the two drugs are SYNERGISTIC, not additive
      (Iter 8). Mean pfs_months by (feature_042, feature_111):
        (0,0) 4.32   (0,1) 4.33   (1,0) 4.26   (1,1) 7.13
      Either drug alone confers essentially zero benefit. The combination provides ~+2.7 mo
      gain over no treatment.

   d. The combo benefit is uniform across age (Iter 10, p=0.51), feature_063 stage
      (Iter 11, p=0.73), feature_019/albumin (Iter 16, p=0.39), race (Iter 12), insurance
      (Iter 13). The only marginal effect modifier was feature_067 (interaction +0.024
      mo per unit, p=0.006) — clinically negligible.

   e. feature_039's apparent univariate +0.36 mo benefit was an artifact of co-administration
      with the combo (Iter 9): adjusted within combo-treated biomarker-negative patients its
      effect drops to +0.13 (p=0.14); within combo=0 patients its effect is -0.01 (p=0.69).

3. feature_015 and feature_040 are PURELY PREDICTIVE markers, not prognostic
   (Iterations 18–19)
   - They are statistically independent of each other (chi-square p=0.36).
   - Among untreated patients (combo=0), mean pfs_months is essentially identical
     (~4.30 mo) regardless of feature_015 / feature_040 status.
   - Their entire impact on outcomes operates through abolition of the combo effect.
   - feature_034 is mutually exclusive with feature_040 (zero co-occurrence) and adds no
     independent prognostic or predictive signal once feature_040 is accounted for (Iter 20).

4. No alternative treatment regimen helps biomarker-positive patients (Iteration 21)
   In the 23,240 patients with feature_015=1 OR feature_040=1, all 8 cells of the
   (feature_042, feature_111, feature_039) crosstab have mean pfs_months between 4.13 and
   4.35 — i.e., no detectable benefit from any combination. Resistance is comprehensive.

5. Independent prognostic factors (Iterations 2–4, 16–17, 23, 24)
   After adjustment, the following carry orthogonal prognostic information:
   - feature_063 (3-level ordinal, presumably stage or grade): -1.17 mo per level
     Mean pfs_months: level 0 → 5.64; level 1 → 4.44; level 2 → 3.29.
   - feature_056 (binary, prevalence 30%): -1.55 mo (the largest single binary effect).
   - feature_048 (binary, prevalence 10%): -0.97 mo.
   - feature_067 (continuous): -0.077 mo per unit.
   - feature_019 (continuous, range 1.5–5.5, mean 3.8 — pattern compatible with serum
     albumin g/dL): +0.48 mo per unit.
   - feature_101 (continuous, mean 15.5): -0.019 mo per unit.
   These prognostic markers do NOT modify the combo treatment effect; they shift baseline
   pfs_months but do not interact with treatment.

6. Demographic null effects (Iterations 12–15)
   - Race (feature_011): ANOVA p=0.49 on pfs_months main effect; treatment effects
     virtually identical across white, asian, black, hispanic, other.
   - Insurance (feature_089): ANOVA p=0.82 on pfs_months main effect; treatment effects
     virtually identical across medicaid, medicare, private, uninsured.
   - Race and insurance distributions are also balanced across the predictive
     biomarker (feature_015), reducing concern about confounding.


CONSOLIDATED MULTIVARIABLE MODEL (Iteration 24)
-----------------------------------------------
A single OLS model with 8 terms achieves R² = 0.876:

   pfs_months ~ -6.87
                + 0.176 * feature_080            (age, mo/yr)
                - 1.17  * feature_063            (stage-like, mo/level)
                - 1.55  * feature_056
                - 0.97  * feature_048
                - 0.077 * feature_067
                + 0.48  * feature_019
                - 0.019 * feature_101
                + 2.92  * combo × biom_neg       (feature_042 × feature_111
                                                  × (feature_015=0 ∧ feature_040=0))

All coefficients are highly significant (p << 0.001). The combo×biomarker interaction
captures the entire detectable treatment effect cleanly; main effects of combo, biom_neg,
and feature_039 are non-significant once this single interaction term is included.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
This breast-cancer-like dataset has a remarkably clean structure dominated by:

(1) a strong, near-linear age effect of +0.18 mo per year, monotonically across the full
    30–90 year range;
(2) several orthogonal prognostic factors (feature_063 stage-like ordinal, feature_056,
    feature_048, feature_067, feature_019, feature_101);
(3) a single high-magnitude therapeutic effect — combination of feature_042 and feature_111
    yielding ~+2.9 mo PFS — that is fully restricted to patients negative for both
    feature_015 and feature_040, in whom either drug alone provides no detectable benefit;
(4) complete absence of treatment benefit when either resistance marker is present, with no
    alternative regimen rescuing this group;
(5) no detectable disparity in either pfs_months or treatment effect across racial or
    insurance categories.

The combo+biomarker pattern is consistent with a clinically realistic
"targeted-combination + receptor-status biomarker" model of metastatic breast-cancer
treatment, in which two drugs delivered jointly produce a synergistic PFS gain in
biomarker-defined responders while neither drug alone, nor any combination, helps
biomarker-defined non-responders.
