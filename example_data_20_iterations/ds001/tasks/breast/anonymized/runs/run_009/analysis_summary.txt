ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_breast (n=50,000; outcome: pfs_months)

This is a synthesis of 25 iterations of propose-test-refine analyses on the
de-identified breast oncology cohort. The outcome pfs_months is a continuous
months-of-progression-free-survival variable (mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range
0-16.7). Below I walk through the hypotheses, what the statistical evidence
showed, and the integrated picture.

================================================================================
1. AGE-LIKE DEMOGRAPHIC (feature_080) — DOMINANT MAIN EFFECT
================================================================================
feature_080 ranges 30-90 (mean 65) and is virtually certain to be patient age.
It is by far the strongest single predictor of pfs_months: each year confers
+0.176 months of PFS (p < 1e-300; Spearman rho = 0.726). Mean PFS rises
monotonically across age strata: 1.41 (30-50) -> 3.04 (50-60) -> 4.65 (60-70)
-> 6.31 (70-80) -> 7.99 (80-90) months. A model with age alone yields
R^2 ~ 0.53. The age gradient is steepest in patients with feature_063 grade 0
and is attenuated at the highest grade (interaction p ~ 4e-9) — i.e., the
age advantage is partially blunted by aggressive disease.

================================================================================
2. ORDINAL GRADE/SEVERITY MARKER (feature_063) — STRONG INVERSE EFFECT
================================================================================
feature_063 takes 3 ordered values (0/1/2) with prevalences ~35%/50%/15%, and
shows a monotone decreasing PFS gradient: means 5.64 / 4.44 / 3.29 months
(ANOVA p < 1e-300). Adjusted contrasts: level 1 vs 0 ~ -1.19 months,
level 2 vs 0 ~ -2.33 months. This is consistent with a tumor-grade or
disease-severity covariate.

================================================================================
3. FAVORABLE-PROGNOSIS BIOMARKERS (positive on PFS)
================================================================================
* feature_042 (~35% prevalent): unadjusted mean PFS difference +1.10 months
  (p < 1e-300); adjusted coef +1.10 months. Stratified analyses show a
  similar absolute benefit across all feature_063 grades (interaction p
  not significant). Plausibly a hormone-receptor-positive or other
  favorable biomarker / treatment indicator.

* feature_111 (~70% prevalent): unadjusted +0.57 months (p < 1e-140);
  adjusted coef +0.53 months. Strongly correlated with feature_039
  (r = 0.67); when feature_039 is added, it retains an independent but
  smaller effect (+0.014 months adjusted, p ~ 0.32 — not significant in
  the full model). feature_039's univariate association (+0.36 months)
  is largely mediated by feature_111. Among feature_039=1 patients,
  feature_111=1 in ~93% of cases.

================================================================================
4. UNFAVORABLE-PROGNOSIS BIOMARKERS (negative on PFS)
================================================================================
* feature_056 (~30% prevalent): unadjusted -1.54 months, adjusted -1.55
  months (p < 1e-300). The single largest negative biomarker effect.
  Effect deepens in older patients (feature_056 x age interaction
  p ~ 3e-12) and is partially attenuated at the highest grade
  (feature_056 x grade-2 interaction p ~ 0.006).

* feature_048 (~10% prevalent): -1.05 unadjusted, -0.98 adjusted
  (p < 1e-100). Modest amplification with age (interaction p ~ 0.004).

* feature_015 (~35%): -0.56 unadjusted, -0.58 adjusted.
* feature_040 (~18%): -0.46 unadjusted, -0.47 adjusted.

feature_040 and feature_034 (n=18,543; +0.11 unadjusted) are MUTUALLY
EXCLUSIVE — no patient has both =1. After joint adjustment, feature_034's
univariate association largely vanishes (coef ~ 0.002, p ~ 0.87), so it
appears mainly to mark the absence of feature_040 (i.e., feature_040 and
feature_034 are likely two of three categories of an underlying class
variable).

================================================================================
5. CONTINUOUS BIOMARKERS
================================================================================
* feature_067 (range 0-24.6, ~25% are 0): negative; rho = -0.116; adjusted
  coef -0.077 per unit (p < 1e-300). Age x feature_067 interaction is
  significant — slope is steeper in older patients.

* feature_019 (range 1.5-5.5, mean 3.8): positive; rho = 0.102; adjusted
  coef +0.476 months per unit (p < 1e-300). Survives full adjustment.
  Range/mean is consistent with serum albumin.

* feature_101 (range 1-100, mean 15.5, right-skewed): negative; rho =
  -0.083; adjusted coef ~ -0.019 per unit (p < 1e-300). Steeper slope at
  high feature_063 grade (interaction p ~ 1e-3 with grade 2).

================================================================================
6. DEMOGRAPHIC VARIABLES — NULL FINDINGS
================================================================================
* feature_011 (white / hispanic / black / asian / other) — UNADJUSTED
  ANOVA p = 0.49; mean PFS within ~0.08 months across categories. After
  multivariable adjustment, all race contrasts have p > 0.29. NO race
  effect on PFS in this cohort.

* feature_089 (medicare / private / medicaid / uninsured) — UNADJUSTED
  ANOVA p = 0.82. After adjustment all coefficients are non-significant
  (p > 0.58). NO insurance-mediated effect on PFS.

These are clear null findings: race and insurance category are not
prognostic for pfs_months in this cohort.

================================================================================
7. RARE FEATURES
================================================================================
17 binary features have prevalence <5%. Only feature_055 (prevalence
~3%) achieved nominal significance (mean PFS difference -0.144 months,
p ~ 0.024) — a small effect that may not survive correction for the
17 comparisons. All other rare features show small, non-significant
directional trends.

================================================================================
8. TREATMENT-LIKE x GRADE INTERACTIONS — MOSTLY HOMOGENEOUS
================================================================================
For each major positive/negative binary marker (feature_042, feature_111,
feature_039, feature_034, feature_015, feature_040, feature_048), the
interaction with feature_063 grade was tested. Most were null:
* feature_042 x grade: p > 0.12 (homogeneous benefit across grades).
* feature_111 x grade: p > 0.6.
* feature_039 / feature_034 / feature_015 / feature_040 x grade: all p > 0.4.

The only meaningful interactions detected were:
* feature_056 x grade-2: p ~ 0.006 (attenuation at highest grade).
* feature_048 x grade-2: p ~ 0.044 (mild).

The takeaway: prognostic and treatment-like markers in this cohort act
roughly homogeneously across feature_063 strata.

================================================================================
9. AGE INTERACTIONS
================================================================================
The age effect was found to interact with several features:
* feature_063 x age (grade-2 vs grade-0): -0.013 months/year, p ~ 4e-9.
* feature_056 x age: -0.011 months/year, p ~ 3e-12.
* feature_048 x age: -0.008 months/year, p ~ 4e-3.
* feature_067 x age: small but significant.

Pattern: the prognostic disadvantage of poor-prognosis markers WIDENS
with age (i.e., older patients with these features lose more of the
"older-is-better" advantage).

================================================================================
10. JOINT MODEL — VARIANCE EXPLAINED
================================================================================
A model containing age + grade + the 6 binary biomarkers + the 3 continuous
markers explains R^2 = 0.792 of the variance in pfs_months. Adding race,
insurance, feature_039, feature_034, and rare features yields essentially no
gain. The age signal alone yields R^2 ~ 0.53; grade + biomarkers without age
yields R^2 ~ 0.301; the two combined are largely additive.

================================================================================
11. SUPPORTED vs REFUTED HYPOTHESES — SUMMARY
================================================================================
SUPPORTED (statistically significant in expected direction):
  * Older age (feature_080) -> longer PFS.
  * Higher feature_063 grade -> shorter PFS.
  * feature_042 / feature_111 -> longer PFS (independent of age & grade).
  * feature_056 / feature_048 / feature_015 / feature_040 -> shorter PFS.
  * Higher feature_067 / feature_101 -> shorter PFS.
  * Higher feature_019 -> longer PFS.
  * feature_055 (rare) -> shorter PFS (modest, multiple-testing caveat).
  * Age x feature_063, age x feature_056, age x feature_048 interactions.
  * feature_080 x feature_067, feature_101 x feature_063 interactions.

REFUTED / NULL:
  * feature_011 (race) effect on PFS.
  * feature_089 (insurance) effect on PFS.
  * feature_042 x feature_063 differential treatment effect (effect is
    homogeneous).
  * feature_111 x feature_063 differential treatment effect.
  * feature_042 x feature_056 synergy / interaction (effects are
    approximately additive).
  * feature_039 carrying meaningful additional prognostic information
    beyond feature_111 (its univariate signal collapses on adjustment).
  * feature_034 carrying independent prognostic information beyond
    feature_040 (univariate signal disappears on joint adjustment).

================================================================================
12. OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================
The pfs_months outcome in ds001_breast is heavily prognosticated by
clinical and biomarker features rather than demographic factors. Age
dominates, with a clean monotone gradient that is partially modified by
disease severity. A small set of binary biomarkers (feature_042,
feature_056, feature_111, feature_048, feature_015, feature_040) and
three continuous markers (feature_019, feature_067, feature_101) capture
nearly all of the explainable variance. Race and insurance are NOT
associated with PFS, either marginally or after adjustment — a clean
null finding.

The main interaction signals are between age and disease severity /
poor-prognosis biomarkers: older patients still do better, but the
relative protection conferred by age is attenuated when high-risk
biomarkers (feature_056, feature_048) or high disease severity
(feature_063 grade 2) are present. Conversely, the favorable biomarkers
(feature_042, feature_111) act with approximately constant absolute
benefit across grades and ages, suggesting a homogeneous treatment
effect or a constitutive prognostic advantage.
