Analysis summary - ds001_breast cohort (N = 50,000)
====================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival, months). Mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0-16.7.
The cohort has 127 candidate features (82 binary, 7 ordinal, 36 continuous, 2 multi-level
categorical: feature_011 = race, feature_089 = insurance), all de-identified to opaque labels.
No missing data. 25 iterations of propose-test-refine were run.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Strongest main effects (signed direction, large magnitude)
------------------------------------------------------------
The dominant predictors of pfs_months identified univariately and confirmed in a 13-term
multivariable OLS (adjusted R^2 = 0.792) are:

POSITIVE (longer PFS)
  - feature_080 (continuous, range 30-90, mean 65 - structurally an age-like variable):
    Pearson r = +0.70 with PFS; adjusted slope +0.176 months per unit (p ~ 0). Mean PFS rises
    monotonically across quartiles: 2.50 -> 4.09 -> 5.27 -> 6.96 months. The slope itself
    accelerates at higher values (significant positive quadratic, p ~ 1e-63), so the
    feature_080-PFS relationship is convex rather than strictly linear.
  - feature_019 (continuous, range 1.5-5.5, mean 3.8 - albumin-like): adjusted +0.476 mo/unit.
  - feature_042 (binary, prevalence 35%): unadjusted +1.10 mo, but see synergy section below.
  - feature_111 (binary, prevalence 70%): unadjusted +0.57 mo, but see synergy section below.

NEGATIVE (shorter PFS)
  - feature_063 (3-level ordinal, likely tumor grade/stage): mean PFS 5.64 / 4.44 / 3.29 across
    levels 0/1/2; adjusted contrasts -1.19 and -2.33 vs reference (p ~ 0).
  - feature_056 (binary, prevalence 30%): -1.55 mo adjusted (p ~ 0). Largest negative
    treatment-like signal.
  - feature_048 (binary, prevalence 10%): -0.98 mo adjusted.
  - feature_015 (binary, prevalence 35%): -0.58 mo.
  - feature_040 (binary, prevalence 18%): -0.47 mo.
  - feature_067 (continuous): -0.077 mo per unit.
  - feature_101 (continuous): -0.019 mo per unit.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Major interaction finding: feature_042 x feature_111 synergy
------------------------------------------------------------
The most striking interaction is between feature_042 and feature_111. Cell means:

                      feature_111 = 0   feature_111 = 1
  feature_042 = 0          4.30              4.31
  feature_042 = 1          4.29              5.89

Neither feature alone is associated with longer PFS; only the combination is. The interaction
coefficient is +1.56 months (t = 68.7, p ~ 0). The apparent univariate main effects of these
two binaries are entirely driven by the joint state. In a parsimonious final model that
includes the interaction, both main effects collapse to ~0 (p > 0.5) while the
feature_042 x feature_111 product retains its full +1.56 month effect, lifting model R^2 from
0.792 to 0.812. This pattern is consistent with a combination-therapy or co-receptor-required
mechanism in which both inputs are necessary for any benefit.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Other interactions tested
------------------------------------------------------------
Significant:
  - feature_056 x feature_080: -0.011 mo per feature_080-unit (p = 7e-22). The negative
    feature_056 effect is mildly amplified at higher feature_080: about -1.38 mo at
    feature_080 = 50 vs -1.71 mo at feature_080 = 80.
  - feature_056 x C(feature_063): small attenuation of the feature_056 effect at higher grades
    (interaction terms +0.06 and +0.14, p = 0.014 and 8e-5). Direction is small relative to
    main effect.

Null (i.e., effects are uniform across strata):
  - feature_042 x feature_063 (p = 0.20)
  - feature_042 x feature_080 (p = 0.10)
  - feature_042 x feature_019 (p = 0.12)
  - feature_056 x feature_042, feature_056 x feature_048, feature_015 x feature_048,
    feature_056 x feature_015 (all p > 0.5)
  - feature_056 x feature_011 (race), feature_056 x feature_089 (insurance) - see below.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Race and insurance: no PFS gap, no treatment-effect heterogeneity
------------------------------------------------------------
Mean PFS by race (feature_011): white 4.68, black 4.72, hispanic 4.71, asian 4.74, other 4.76.
ANOVA p = 0.49. After adjustment for clinical covariates, no racial pairwise contrast
approaches significance (all p > 0.29; magnitudes < 0.03 mo).

Mean PFS by insurance (feature_089): medicare 4.69, private 4.69, medicaid 4.71,
uninsured 4.73. ANOVA p = 0.82. Adjusted contrasts < 0.02 mo.

Treatment-effect heterogeneity by race (feature_056 x feature_011) and by insurance
(feature_056 x feature_089) was tested explicitly: no interaction term reached p < 0.05
(closest was feature_056 x uninsured at p = 0.054, magnitude -0.14 mo). A joint test of
race x grade (feature_011 x feature_063) returned F = 0.36, p = 0.94.

The dataset thus shows no demographic disparity in PFS, and clinical effects are uniform
across racial and insurance strata.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Null / weak findings
------------------------------------------------------------
- Multi-level ordinal features feature_021, feature_024, feature_016, feature_127,
  feature_001, feature_073: ANOVA p = 0.27-0.82 each; range across levels < 0.16 mo.
  These appear to be noise variables.
- feature_039 (binary): apparent univariate +0.36 mo effect disappears (coef +0.014, p = 0.35)
  once feature_111 is in the model. feature_039 = 1 in 85% of feature_111 = 1 patients vs
  15% of feature_111 = 0 patients - the two are highly co-occurring and feature_039's
  univariate signal is captured by feature_111.
- Several continuous features (feature_053, feature_066, feature_006, feature_065,
  feature_105) reach univariate p < 0.05 due to N = 50,000 but have |r| < 0.02 - they are
  not clinically meaningful predictors.
- Of the 127 candidate features, only 23 reach univariate p < 0.05 and 13 survive
  Bonferroni correction; the load-bearing predictors are concentrated in fewer than 10
  features.

6. Hypotheses supported vs refuted (summary)
------------------------------------------------------------
Supported:
  + Higher feature_080 -> longer PFS (h1, h22, h31, h34)
  + Higher feature_063 -> shorter PFS (h2)
  + feature_056, feature_048, feature_015, feature_040 -> shorter PFS (h3, h5, h6, h8)
  + feature_042, feature_111 univariately -> longer PFS (h4, h7), but only via synergy (h25)
  + Higher feature_067, feature_101 -> shorter PFS; higher feature_019 -> longer PFS
    (h10-h12)
  + Multivariable model with the strong predictors achieves R^2 > 0.79 (h16)
  + feature_039 redundant once feature_111 is in the model (h33)
  + feature_080 effect nonlinear (positive quadratic, h31)
  + Negative feature_056 effect modestly amplified at higher feature_080 (h24)
  + Small but significant feature_056 x feature_063 attenuation at higher grade (h17)
  + feature_042 x feature_111 super-additive synergy (h25, h38)
  + Race and insurance show no main effect on PFS after adjustment (h20, h22)
  + Most of the 127 features are noise (h35)

Not supported:
  - Race main effect on PFS (h13): null
  - Insurance main effect on PFS (h14): null
  - Multi-level ordinals feature_021/024/016/127/001/073 effects (h15): null
  - feature_042 x feature_063 heterogeneity (h18): null
  - feature_056 x feature_011 race heterogeneity (h19): null
  - feature_056 x feature_089 insurance heterogeneity (h21): null
  - feature_042 x feature_080 heterogeneity (h23): null
  - feature_056 x feature_042, feature_056 x feature_048, feature_015 x feature_048,

------------------------------------------------------------
7. Overall conclusions about treatment-biomarker-outcome relationships
------------------------------------------------------------
Approximately 80% of the variance in pfs_months in this cohort is explained by ten predictors.
The dominant prognostic axis is feature_080 (age-like, +0.18 mo per unit, accelerating) and
feature_063 (grade/stage-like, monotonic -2.3 months from level 0 to level 2). On top of these
prognostic factors, several treatment-or-biomarker-like binaries shift PFS: feature_056
(-1.55 mo), feature_048 (-0.98), feature_015 (-0.58), feature_040 (-0.47) reduce PFS, while
feature_019 (continuous, +0.48 / unit) and the joint state of feature_042 + feature_111
(+1.56 mo) extend it. The feature_042 x feature_111 finding is the most clinically interesting:
neither marker alone is informative, but their co-occurrence is associated with the
single largest positive effect on PFS in the dataset, suggesting a combination requirement.

Beyond the feature_056 x feature_080 and feature_056 x feature_063 modulations, treatment-like
effects are remarkably uniform across patient subgroups - including across all five racial
categories and all four insurance categories. There is no evidence of a demographic disparity
in either baseline PFS or in treatment effect; the patterns recovered from this dataset are
overwhelmingly clinical-biomarker-by-treatment rather than demographic-by-treatment.
