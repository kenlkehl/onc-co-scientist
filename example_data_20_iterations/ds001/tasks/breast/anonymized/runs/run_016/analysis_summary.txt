ds001_breast — Analysis Summary
================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with anonymized features (feature_001..feature_127) and a single
outcome, pfs_months (mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0–16.7). Features comprise 82 binary
indicators, 7 multi-level integer codes, 36 continuous values, and 2 categorical
strings (feature_011 with 5 race-like levels, feature_089 with 4 insurance-like levels).
No missing data.

Across 10 iterations of propose-test-refine, the analysis converged on a coherent
prognostic + treatment-biomarker structure that explains the great majority of variance
in pfs_months (R² ≈ 0.86 in the omnibus OLS).


1. Univariate prognostic features (Iterations 1–3)
--------------------------------------------------

The strongest prognostic signals fall into three families.

(a) Continuous prognostic markers. feature_080 (range 30–90, mean 65, plausibly age in
years) had Pearson r = +0.70 with pfs_months — an unusually strong, monotonic positive
association (per-unit beta ≈ +0.18 month). feature_067 (range 0–25) had r = -0.12 and
feature_019 (range 1.5–5.5, plausibly serum albumin) had r = +0.10; feature_101 (range
1–100, mean 16, plausibly tumor-burden or LDH-like) had r = -0.09. Other continuous
markers had |r| < 0.02.

(b) Multi-level integer predictors. Of the seven candidates, only feature_063 (3 levels,
0/1/2) carried prognostic information: mean PFS dropped monotonically from 5.64 → 4.44
→ 3.29 across its three levels (Spearman r = -0.33, ANOVA p ≈ 0). feature_063 behaves
like a stage- or burden-of-disease grading. feature_021, feature_024, feature_016,
feature_127, feature_001, and feature_073 all had ANOVA p > 0.27 — they appear to be
clinically inert codes.

(c) Binary prognostic markers. feature_056 = 1 was the strongest negative prognostic
(mean PFS 3.62 vs 5.15 in feature_056 = 0; Δ = -1.54 months, p ≈ 0). feature_048 = 1
was independently unfavorable (Δ = -1.05 months, p ≈ 1e-178). Their effects persisted
across feature_063 strata, so they capture risk separate from disease grade.


2. A treatment with a multi-criterion responder phenotype (Iterations 5–8)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

feature_042 had the largest positive marginal effect of any binary feature (+1.10
months, p ≈ 0). Pairwise interaction screens identified three biomarkers that gate
that effect:

  - feature_042:feature_111 = +1.59 (p ≈ 9e-229); feature_042 only helps when
    feature_111 = 1.
  - feature_042:feature_015 = -1.62 (p ≈ 4e-255); feature_042's benefit is abolished
    when feature_015 = 1.
  - feature_042:feature_040 = -1.36 (p ≈ 2e-118); feature_042's benefit is abolished
    when feature_040 = 1.

Defining a composite "responder" indicator as feature_111 = 1 AND feature_015 = 0 AND
feature_040 = 0 (37% of the cohort, n = 18,681) produced a clean treatment-by-eligibility
contrast: in responders, feature_042 added +2.91 months of PFS (treated 7.21 vs
untreated 4.30, p ≈ 0). In non-responders the effect was +0.003 months (NS, p = 0.91).
This interpretation is consistent with a targeted oncologic therapy whose label
specifies a single positive biomarker (feature_111) plus exclusionary co-features
(feature_015, feature_040).

A further continuous modifier was identified: feature_042:feature_101 = -0.052 per unit
(p ≈ 2e-192). Within responders, treatment effect by feature_101 quintile was +4.90,
+5.03, +4.42, +0.05, -0.04 months — large and significant only when feature_101 was
below ~15. Among non-responders, no level of feature_101 produced benefit. This
behaves like a tumor-burden / disease-aggressiveness threshold beyond which the
targeted therapy loses efficacy.

A spurious-looking signal in the marginal screen — feature_039 = 1 carrying +0.36
months — was traced to feature_039's strong correlation with feature_111 (phi = 0.67).
After conditioning on feature_111, feature_039 had no independent association
(stratified |Δ| ≤ 0.15 mo, p > 0.08 in every cell). Interpretation: feature_039
co-occurs with the predictive biomarker but does not itself carry treatment-effect
information.


3. No race or insurance disparity in PFS or in treatment receipt (Iterations 4, 9, 10)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Unadjusted ANOVA across the 5 race levels gave p = 0.49; pairwise vs the white reference
all yielded |Δ| < 0.10 months and p > 0.23. Insurance ANOVA p = 0.82, with all
pairwise |Δ| < 0.05 months. After adjustment for the full prognostic + treatment-
biomarker model, every race coefficient had |β| < 0.05 mo (p > 0.19) and every insurance
coefficient |β| < 0.01 mo (p > 0.62) — race and insurance carry no residual signal.

Disparities in treatment receipt were similarly absent. Among biomarker-eligible
(responder = 1) patients, the probability of receiving feature_042 was: white 0.353,
black 0.343, hispanic 0.357, asian 0.346, other 0.361 (chi-square p = 0.85); medicaid
0.343, private 0.347, medicare 0.356, uninsured 0.378 (p = 0.21). And the magnitude of
the treatment benefit, when administered to eligible patients, was uniform across race
groups (white +2.89, black +2.90, hispanic +2.96, asian +2.98, other +2.87 months). The
treatment effect was also uniform across the three feature_063 (stage-like) levels
(+2.92, +2.92, +2.97 months).

Interpreted clinically, this is a notably equitable picture: there is no signal that
self-reported race or insurance type degrades who gets the targeted therapy, who
benefits, or how long they remain progression-free. Whether this reflects a genuinely
equitable cohort, a curated population, or selection in how the dataset was assembled
cannot be determined from these data alone.


4. Omnibus model (Iteration 10)
-------------------------------

A single OLS model containing feature_080, feature_063, feature_056, feature_048,
feature_042, feature_111, feature_015, feature_040, feature_067, feature_019,
feature_101 and the four interactions feature_042:feature_111, feature_042:feature_015,
feature_042:feature_040, feature_042:feature_101 achieved R² = 0.858 with residual SD
= 0.94 mo (compared to a raw outcome SD of 2.50 mo). Final coefficients (per-unit or
per-indicator), all p ≪ 0.001 unless noted:

  Intercept                       -7.12
  feature_080                     +0.176 / unit
  feature_063                     -1.176 / level
  feature_056                     -1.548
  feature_048                     -0.967
  feature_042 (main, biomarker-)  +1.665     (effective only at feature_101=0)
  feature_111 (main)              -0.001 (NS)
  feature_015 (main)              -0.003 (NS)
  feature_040 (main)              +0.004 (NS)
  feature_042:feature_111         +1.531
  feature_042:feature_015         -1.643
  feature_042:feature_040         -1.325
  feature_042:feature_101         -0.053 / unit
  feature_067                     -0.076 / unit
  feature_019                     +0.476 / unit
  feature_101 (main)              -0.0001 (NS)

The main-effect coefficients on feature_111, feature_015 and feature_040 are essentially
zero, confirming they act *only* as treatment-effect modifiers, not as independent
prognostic markers in this cohort.


5. Hypotheses supported, refuted, and unsupported
-------------------------------------------------

Supported (all with p << 0.001):
  - feature_056, feature_048 are independent negative prognostics
  - feature_063 is a 3-level prognostic stage-like grade with monotonic dose-response
  - feature_080 is the dominant positive prognostic continuous variable
  - feature_067 (negative), feature_019 (positive) are weaker continuous prognostics
  - feature_042 is a treatment whose benefit is gated by feature_111+, feature_015−,
    feature_040−, AND feature_101 below a threshold (~15)
  - The treatment effect within eligible patients is invariant across stages and races
  - Marginal feature_039 association is fully explained by its correlation with
    feature_111

Refuted / not supported:
  - Race disparities in PFS (h4.1, h4.3): unadjusted and adjusted models both null
  - Insurance disparities in PFS (h4.2, h4.4): unadjusted and adjusted models both null
  - feature_021, feature_024, feature_016, feature_127, feature_001, feature_073 as
    prognostic factors (h3.3): no association
  - feature_080 as a treatment-effect modifier (h7.1): no interaction
  - Disparities in treatment receipt (h9.1): receipt rates equivalent across race and
    insurance


6. Overall conclusion
---------------------

This cohort exhibits a textbook precision-oncology pattern: a single targeted therapy
(feature_042) with a clean four-criterion eligibility rule (feature_111 = 1, feature_015
= 0, feature_040 = 0, low feature_101) that delivers a clinically substantial PFS gain
(~2.9 months on average, up to ~5 months in the lowest-burden quintile of eligible
patients) and no meaningful effect outside that subgroup. Independent of the targeted
therapy, the dominant prognostic levers are feature_080 (age-like continuous), an
ordinal feature_063 (stage-like), and two binary risk factors feature_056 and
feature_048. After accounting for these, neither race nor insurance contribute residual
signal to PFS, and there is no detectable disparity in who receives or benefits from
the targeted therapy. The combined model explains 86% of PFS variance, leaving roughly
0.94 months of residual SD — a level of unexplained variation comparable to typical
biological noise in real-world breast cancer registries.
