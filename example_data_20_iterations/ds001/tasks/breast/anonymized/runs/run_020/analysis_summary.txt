Analysis Summary - ds001_breast (N=50000)
============================================================

OVERVIEW
We explored a de-identified breast oncology cohort of 50,000 patients with PFS
(months) as the outcome and 127 anonymized features. Across 25 iterations we
moved from broad univariate screens to multivariable models, subgroup analyses,
and pairwise/three-way interactions. The hierarchy of effects that emerged is
sharp and reproducible.

DOMINANT PROGNOSTIC AXES
1. feature_080 (continuous, range 30-90) is by far the strongest single predictor:
   Spearman r = 0.726 with PFS. Mean PFS rises monotonically
   from 1.70 months (lowest decile) to 7.84 months (highest decile). The
   relationship is approximately linear with mild positive curvature (squared term
   p = 6.24e-25). In the full adjusted OLS, each one-unit rise adds
   0.176 months of PFS.

2. feature_063 (3-level ordinal, likely a stage/grade marker) is the second
   most powerful axis: mean PFS 5.64 / 4.44 / 3.29 across levels 0/1/2;
   adjusted coefficient -1.171 months per level.

ADVERSE BINARY MARKERS (effect sizes from full OLS adjusted for all features)
  feature_056 (30% prev): -1.549 months, p=0.00e+00
  feature_048 (10% prev): -0.982 months, p=0.00e+00
  feature_040 (18% prev): -0.471 months, p=9.32e-239
  feature_015 (35% prev): -0.582 months, p=0.00e+00
  (feature_015's effect is mostly through interactions, see below.)

FAVORABLE BINARY MARKERS AND THE 'RESPONDER PHENOTYPE'
feature_111 (70% prev) and feature_042 (35% prev) appear individually favorable,
but the dominant signal is their POSITIVE INTERACTION:
    Cell means (feature_042, feature_111):
       (0,0): 4.303   (0,1): 4.313   (1,0): 4.285   (1,1): 5.890
The simple two-way interaction term is +1.59 months (p=9.31e-229).
Within feature_111=0, feature_042 has zero effect (diff -0.017, p=0.63).
Within feature_111=1, feature_042 adds 1.577 months (p=0.00e+00).
This pattern is the signature of a treatment that benefits only biomarker-positive patients.

feature_039 reinforces the same axis: feature_042 x feature_039 interaction +1.09
(p=2.60e-117). The full three-way interaction f042xf111xf039 is
positive but modest (coef 0.126, p=0.360) - little additional
signal beyond the two-way f042xf111.

feature_034 contributes a smaller secondary boost within the responder cohort:
within (f042=1 & f111=1 & f015=0), f034=1 adds +0.80 months (p=4.39e-26).

THE 'RESISTANCE' MARKER feature_015
feature_015 has near-zero independent effect when feature_042=0 (diff 0.008, p=0.73),
but in feature_042=1 patients it eliminates the entire benefit:
  feature_015=1 mean PFS = 4.352 vs feature_015=0 mean PFS = 5.966
  diff = -1.615 months, p = 0.00e+00.
feature_015 x feature_111 interaction = -0.800 months (p=9.90e-57).
This is the canonical pattern of an endocrine-resistance or acquired-resistance marker
that nullifies a targeted-therapy benefit.

CONTINUOUS BIOMARKERS (12-feature adjusted)
  feature_019 (range 1.5-5.5): +0.476 months/unit, p=0.00e+00
  feature_067 (range 0-25):    -0.077 months/unit, p=0.00e+00
  feature_101 (range 1-100):   -0.0189 months/unit, p=0.00e+00
A small feature_080 x feature_019 interaction is detectable (coef=0.00500, p=0.002) but negligible in magnitude.

This dataset shows NO measurable racial or insurance-related disparity in PFS,
which is itself a notable finding given how often such disparities appear in
real-world EHR-derived oncology cohorts. The hypothesis that disparities would
emerge after disease-stratification was REFUTED.

INTERACTIONS WITH feature_080
feature_080 x feature_056: coef=-0.0112 (p=3.13e-12)
  -> adverse decrement of feature_056 grows with feature_080: Q1 Delta=-1.43, Q4 Delta=-1.61.
feature_080 x feature_042: coef=-0.0032 (p=4.38e-02) - small, mostly independent.

VARIANCE EXPLAINED
  Full OLS (127 features incl. race/insurance dummies): R^2 = 0.7927
  Trimmed 12-feature OLS:                                R^2 = 0.7919
  + 5 key two-way interactions:                          R^2 = 0.8389
The trimmed-plus-interactions model captures effectively all of the detectable
signal in the dataset.

SUPPORTED HYPOTHESES
- feature_080 and feature_063 are dominant prognostic axes (h2.1, h3.1, h4.1, h4.2).
- feature_056, feature_048, feature_040, feature_015 are independent adverse markers (h1.1, h1.3, h1.5, h4.3).
- feature_042 and feature_111 are favorable, mostly via interaction (h1.2, h1.4, h4.4, h8.1).
- feature_042 x feature_111 is a strong positive interaction; feature_042 has essentially no effect when feature_111=0 (h10.1, h10.2, h15.1).
- feature_015 is an interaction-negating 'resistance' marker (h8.2, h8.4, h9.1).
- feature_080 modifies the absolute decrement of feature_056 (h6.1, h16.1).
- A small responder cohort (~16% of patients) has +2.4 months PFS over the rest (h23.1).
- feature_019, feature_067, feature_101 are independent continuous biomarkers (h14.1-h14.3).
- feature_017, feature_037, feature_093, feature_126 have small but non-zero adverse effects after adjustment (h24.1).

REFUTED HYPOTHESES
- feature_011 (race) does NOT influence PFS in any subgroup tested (h3.3, h5.1, h18.1).
- feature_089 (insurance) does NOT influence PFS in any subgroup tested (h3.4, h5.2, h19.1).
- 5-level ordinals (feature_021, feature_024, feature_127, feature_001, feature_073) do NOT independently predict PFS (h3.2, h20.1).
- The three-way f042xf111xf039 interaction adds little beyond the two-way f042xf111 interaction (h11.1).
- feature_034, feature_068, feature_028, feature_020 have no detectable adjusted effect (h24.2).

OVERALL CONCLUSION
PFS in this cohort is driven by (i) a dominant continuous prognostic axis
(feature_080), (ii) an ordinal disease-burden axis (feature_063), (iii) a 'responder
phenotype' defined by feature_042 conditional on feature_111 (modulated by
feature_015 as a resistance switch and feature_039 as a co-marker), and (iv)
several independent adverse markers (feature_056 above all). Continuous biomarkers
feature_019, feature_067, and feature_101 add secondary signal. There is no
detectable racial or insurance-related disparity in this cohort.
