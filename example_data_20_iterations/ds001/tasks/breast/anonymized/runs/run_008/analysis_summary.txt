ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_breast (n = 50,000)
Outcome: pfs_months (mean 4.69, sd 2.50, range 0–16.7)

OVERVIEW
========
Across 25 iterations I screened all 127 features against pfs_months, fitted a
multivariable OLS model with the strongest predictors, then probed for
interactions among the leading binary features and for effect modification by
the dominant continuous predictor. The final model with main effects plus a
small set of well-supported interactions explains 85.4% of the variance in
PFS — a residual standard deviation of 0.96 months versus an outcome standard
deviation of 2.50 months.

The dataset is best described as having (a) a strong continuous prognostic
gradient driven by feature_080 and one ordinal factor (feature_063), (b) two
independent negative prognostic binaries (feature_056, feature_048), and
(c) a single very strong predictive signal: feature_042 looks like a
targeted-therapy-like intervention whose benefit is essentially confined to
patients defined by a three-marker biomarker profile.

DOMINANT MAIN EFFECTS (univariate and adjusted)
================================================
* feature_080 (continuous, range 30–90, mean 65)
  Pearson r = +0.70 (p effectively 0). Mean PFS rises monotonically across
  quartiles (Q1 = 2.50, Q2 = 4.09, Q3 = 5.27, Q4 = 6.96 months). Adjusted
  slope +0.179 PFS-months per one-unit increase — across the full range that
  is a ~10.5-month spread. By far the strongest single predictor.

* feature_063 (ordinal, levels 0/1/2; prev 35%/50%/15%)
  Spearman rho = -0.33. Mean PFS by level: 5.64 / 4.44 / 3.29 months.
  Adjusted slope -1.17 months per one-level increase. Behaves like a
  performance-status-style score.

* feature_056 (binary, prevalence 30%)
  Univariate effect -1.54 months (5.15 → 3.62); adjusted main effect ~-0.92.
  Strong, consistent negative prognostic factor.

* feature_048 (binary, prevalence 10%)
  Univariate effect -1.05 months; adjusted main effect ~-0.63. Independent
  negative prognostic factor.

* feature_067 (continuous): r = -0.12; adjusted slope -0.077/unit.
* feature_019 (continuous, 1.5–5.5): r = +0.10; adjusted slope +0.48/unit.
* feature_101 (continuous, 1–100): r = -0.09; adjusted slope -0.019/unit.

THE PREDICTIVE SIGNAL: feature_042 AND ITS BIOMARKERS
======================================================
feature_042 (prevalence 35%) shows an overall positive effect of +1.10 months
on PFS (5.41 vs 4.31). Decomposing this with stratified analyses and
interaction terms reveals a striking pattern:

  feature_042 × feature_111: interaction coefficient +1.56 (p ~ 0)
  feature_042 × feature_015: interaction coefficient -1.66 (p ~ 0)
  feature_042 × feature_040: interaction coefficient -1.32 (p ~ 0)

Stratified estimates of the feature_042 effect are dramatic:
  feature_111=0:  -0.02 months (no benefit)
  feature_111=1:  +1.58 months
  feature_015=0:  +1.66 months
  feature_015=1:  +0.04 months (no benefit)
  feature_040=0:  +1.35 months
  feature_040=1:  -0.01 months (no benefit)

The three-way subgroup analysis is the cleanest expression of the pattern.
Among the eight subgroups defined by (feature_111, feature_015, feature_040):

  111=1, 015=0, 040=0  (n = 18,681): feature_042 effect = +2.91 months
  All seven other subgroups            : feature_042 effect ≤ 0.13 months in
                                          absolute value.

Interpretation: feature_042 functions like a biomarker-selected targeted
therapy. It confers a clinically meaningful benefit only in patients who are
positive for feature_111 AND negative for both feature_015 and feature_040.
In every other patient stratum, feature_042 has no detectable effect on PFS.

A smaller secondary modifier was identified: in feature_111=1 patients, a
co-positive feature_034 amplifies the feature_042 benefit further
(stratum-specific effects: +1.36 if 034=0, +1.94 if 034=1; interaction
coefficient +0.38, p < 1e-67). No other binary feature in the cohort showed
an interaction with feature_042 above 0.20 months in absolute value after
adjustment.

OTHER NOTABLE INTERACTIONS
==========================
* Among the three biomarkers themselves there is substantial cross-modulation:
    feature_111 × feature_015  : -0.83  (favorable effect of 111 attenuated)
    feature_015 × feature_040  : +0.73  (sub-additivity of two negatives)
    feature_111 × feature_040  : -0.66
* feature_080 × feature_056 = -0.011/unit (p = 1.9e-21): feature_056's
  prognostic deficit is larger at higher feature_080 values (~0.6 mo extra
  across the range).
* feature_080 × feature_048 = -0.005/unit (p = 1.6e-4): same direction,
  smaller magnitude.
* feature_063 × feature_056 = +0.067 (p = 4.6e-5): mild sub-additivity
  between the two strongest negative ordinal/binary prognostic factors.
* feature_080 does NOT modify the feature_042 benefit (interaction p = 0.13);
  feature_063 does NOT modify the feature_042 benefit (p = 0.26). The
  treatment-like signal is constant across these prognostic gradients.

CONFOUNDED / NULL FINDINGS
===========================
* feature_039 had a significant univariate effect (+0.36 mo, p = 5.8e-59), but
  this is fully explained by its strong correlation with feature_111
  (r = +0.67). Adjusted coefficient drops to +0.014 (p = 0.32).
* feature_034 had a small univariate effect (+0.11 mo) that vanishes after
  adjustment for feature_040 (with which it is correlated, r = -0.36); its
  only role in the data is as a modifier of the feature_042 benefit.
* The multi-level integer features feature_021, feature_024, feature_016,
  feature_127, feature_001, feature_073 (all 3–11 levels) carry essentially
  no signal: |Spearman rho| < 0.006 for every one of them, none significant.

DEMOGRAPHIC SUBGROUPS — NO DETECTABLE DISPARITY
=================================================
This was an explicit pre-specified question across iterations 17 and 18.

* Self-reported race (feature_011: white 65%, hispanic 15%, black 12%,
  asian 6%, other 2%) — group means span only 4.679 (white) to 4.762 (other),
  a difference of 0.083 months; ANOVA p > 0.1.

* Insurance type (feature_089: medicare 42%, private 40%, medicaid 14%,
  uninsured 4%) — group means span 4.685 (private) to 4.733 (uninsured),
  a difference of 0.048 months; ANOVA p > 0.5.

* The benefit of feature_042 is essentially identical across race subgroups
  (range +1.01 to +1.12 months) and insurance subgroups (range +1.08 to
  +1.11 months). There is no evidence that the predictive treatment-like
  signal is differentially distributed by demographic strata in this cohort.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
===================
1. Survival in this 50,000-patient cohort is largely explained by a small
   set of predictors. A multivariable OLS with eleven main effects and eight
   interactions reaches R^2 = 0.854.

2. The strongest single source of variation is the continuous feature_080
   gradient (~10-month range across its values), followed by the ordinal
   feature_063 (-1.17 months per level), then the binary prognostic factors
   feature_056 (-1.54) and feature_048 (-1.05).

3. The single most clinically informative finding is the predictive
   biomarker pattern around feature_042. Its benefit is concentrated entirely
   in the feature_111+ / feature_015- / feature_040- subgroup (+2.9 months),
   with no detectable benefit in any other subgroup. This is the dataset's
   strongest interaction signal and is consistent with a targeted-therapy
   interpretation.

4. feature_056 and feature_048 are pure prognostic factors: their effects on
   PFS are independent of feature_042 status (no significant interaction with
   the treatment-like variable).

5. Self-reported race and insurance status show no association with PFS, and
   no differential effect of feature_042 across demographic subgroups was
   observed in this cohort.
