Analysis summary — ds001_crc (n = 50,000 patients; outcome pfs_months)
======================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Across 25 iterations, the analysis profiled all 124 anonymized features against
pfs_months, identified the dominant main-effect predictors via univariate and
adjusted regression, then probed treatment-biomarker interactions and tested
whether demographic strata (race, insurance) showed disparities in outcome,
treatment receipt, or treatment benefit. A consolidated multivariable model
(R^2 ~ 0.90) at the end of the protocol reconciled all earlier findings.

DOMINANT MAIN-EFFECT PREDICTORS (iterations 1-11)
-------------------------------------------------
The four strongest pfs_months predictors are:
  - feature_078 (continuous, range 30-90, mean 65): Spearman rho = +0.785
    (p < 1e-300). Mean PFS rises monotonically across deciles from 1.33 to
    7.45 months. The relationship is essentially linear (linear R^2 0.587 vs
    quadratic R^2 0.588). Largest single predictor in the dataset.
  - feature_057 (3-level ordinal, 0/1/2): Mean PFS 5.27 / 4.05 / 2.91 months;
    one-way ANOVA F = 3528.9, p < 1e-300. Each step ~ -1.2 months. The
    pattern is consistent with a performance-status-like ordinal variable.
  - feature_051 (binary, 55% prevalence): mean PFS 3.70 vs 5.05 (Welch
    p < 1e-300; adjusted coefficient -1.36). Plausibly a stage / metastatic
    indicator.
  - feature_038 (binary, 20% prevalence): mean PFS 5.09 vs 4.12 (Welch
    p ~ 1.7e-216; adjusted coefficient +0.94). Direction is positive and
    survives heavy adjustment, but as iterations 12-16 demonstrate, the
    benefit is concentrated in a marker-defined subgroup.

Additional smaller-but-significant main effects:
  - feature_099 (continuous): rho = -0.120 (p ~ 5.6e-160); adjusted -0.076/unit.
  - feature_092 (continuous, range 1.5-5.5): rho = +0.103 (p ~ 4.4e-117);
    adjusted +0.46/unit. Range and sign are consistent with a serum-protein
    measure (e.g., albumin).
  - feature_013 (binary, 42%): adjusted -0.33 months (p < 1e-300).
  - feature_043 (binary, 35%): adjusted -0.29 months (p < 1e-300).
  - feature_109 (binary, 4.5%): adjusted -0.36 months (p ~ 1e-30).
  - feature_009 (continuous, right-skewed): rho = -0.047; adjusted
    -0.005/unit (p ~ 2.5e-75). Small but real.
  - feature_067 (binary, 3%): adjusted +0.18 months (p ~ 6e-11) — small but
    positive, opposite direction from feature_013/043/109.
A joint OLS of these ten predictors achieves R^2 = 0.858 with every
coefficient retaining its univariate sign and p < 1e-15.

TREATMENT-BIOMARKER INTERACTION PATTERN (iterations 12-16)
----------------------------------------------------------
The most striking pattern in the dataset is a classical treatment-by-biomarker
interaction centered on feature_038 (the apparent treatment) and three
"resistance" markers (feature_013, feature_043, feature_109).

  Stratified PFS gain from feature_038 = 1 vs 0:
    - feature_013 = 0:  +1.66 months   |  feature_013 = 1:  +0.03 months
    - feature_043 = 0:  +1.48 months   |  feature_043 = 1:  +0.03 months
    - feature_109 = 0:  +1.03 months   |  feature_109 = 1:  -0.16 months

  OLS interaction coefficients (adjusted for feature_078, feature_057,
  feature_051, feature_099):
    feature_038 × feature_013  = -1.78  (p < 1e-300)
    feature_038 × feature_043  = -1.47  (p < 1e-300)
    feature_038 × feature_109  = -1.76  (p ~ 1.3e-88)

In a "triple-marker-negative" subgroup (feature_013 = 0 AND feature_043 = 0
AND feature_109 = 0; n = 17,372), the feature_038 effect is +2.76 months
(6.88 vs 4.12). Among any-marker-positive patients (n = 32,628), the effect
is +0.03 months (p = 0.29). The treatment benefit is therefore essentially
confined to the marker-negative subgroup. This is the canonical signature of
a precision-oncology biomarker-treatment relationship — for example, the
abrogation of anti-EGFR benefit in colorectal cancer with RAS/BRAF
mutations.

A fourth marker, feature_067 (binary, 3%), shows the opposite pattern in
crude analysis: feature_038 benefit is +1.93 months when feature_067 = 1
versus +0.94 months when feature_067 = 0 (interaction +0.89, p ~ 3e-50).
However, after joint adjustment for feature_013/043/109, the residual
feature_038 × feature_067 interaction shrinks to +0.07 (p = 0.15),
suggesting feature_067 mainly marks patients enriched for absence of the
three resistance markers rather than independently potentiating the
treatment.

DEMOGRAPHIC STRATA (iterations 17-22)
-------------------------------------
Race (feature_064) and insurance (feature_018) showed no clinically or
statistically meaningful associations:

  - Mean PFS by race: white 4.32, hispanic 4.32, asian 4.31, black 4.27,
    other 4.41 (ANOVA p = 0.48).
  - Mean PFS by insurance: medicare 4.31, private 4.33, medicaid 4.28,
    uninsured 4.25 (ANOVA p = 0.22).
  - Probability of receiving feature_038 by race: 19.2% - 21.1% across
    five groups (chi-square p = 0.55).
  - Probability of receiving feature_038 by insurance: 19.0% - 20.2%
    across four groups (chi-square p = 0.59).
  - feature_038 × race interaction: stratified PFS gains span 0.92 - 1.22
    months across racial groups; all interaction terms p > 0.10.
  - feature_038 × insurance interaction: only the uninsured-vs-medicaid
    contrast was borderline (-0.12 months, p = 0.044); other terms null.
    The overall pattern is uniform treatment benefit.

There is no evidence of disparities in PFS, treatment receipt, or treatment
benefit by race or insurance in this cohort.

OTHER INTERACTIONS (iterations 23-24)
-------------------------------------
feature_038 effect was uniform across feature_057 (performance-status-like)
levels (gains 0.93 / 1.00 / 0.90, interaction n.s.). A small negative
feature_038 × feature_092 interaction was detected (-0.086 per unit
feature_092, p ~ 2e-5); direction and magnitude are minor relative to the
biomarker interactions above.

CONSOLIDATED MODEL (iteration 25)
---------------------------------
A final OLS of the form
  pfs_months ~ feature_078 + C(feature_057) + feature_051 + feature_092
             + feature_099 + feature_009
             + feature_038*feature_013 + feature_038*feature_043
             + feature_038*feature_109 + feature_038*feature_067
             + C(feature_064) + C(feature_018)
achieved R^2 = 0.897 (adj R^2 = 0.897), versus 0.858 for the additive
top-10 model, demonstrating that explicitly modelling the biomarker
interactions adds ~4 percentage points of explained variance. All directional
findings from iterations 1-22 reproduced. Race and insurance terms remained
null.

HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED VS REFUTED
-------------------------------
Supported (significant in the predicted direction):
  h1 (feature_078 +), h2 (feature_057 -), h3 (feature_051 -),
  h4 (feature_038 +), h5 (feature_099 -), h6 (feature_092 +),
  h7 (feature_013 -), h8 (feature_043 -), h9 (feature_109 -),
  h10 (feature_009 -), h11 (joint adjustment preserves directions),
  h12-h14 (feature_038 effect modified by feature_013/043/109),
  h15 (feature_038 × feature_067 positive in crude analysis),
  h16 (treatment benefit confined to triple-marker-negative subgroup),
  h24 (small feature_038 × feature_092 interaction),
  h25 (final consolidated multivariable model).

Refuted (no detectable association):
  h17 (race vs PFS), h18 (insurance vs PFS),
  h19 (race vs feature_038 receipt),
  h20 (insurance vs feature_038 receipt),
  h21 (race modifies feature_038 effect),
  h23 (feature_057 modifies feature_038 effect).

Marginal:
  h22 (insurance modifies feature_038 effect — only the uninsured contrast
       reached p = 0.044; effect is ~-0.12 months; not robust).

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. pfs_months in this cohort is dominated by patient-intrinsic factors:
   feature_078 (linear, +0.18 months/unit), feature_057 (ordinal,
   ~-1.2 months per step), feature_051 (-1.4 months), feature_092 / 099
   (small but consistent), and a treatment-marker effect.
2. feature_038 functions as a "treatment" with a marker-modified benefit
   pattern: ~+2.8 months in the triple-marker-negative subgroup and ~0
   elsewhere. The signature is consistent with anti-EGFR therapy in CRC
   (RAS/BRAF wild-type benefit pattern), though the dataset is anonymized
   so this assignment is suggestive, not certain.
3. Race (feature_064) and insurance (feature_018) are not associated with
   pfs_months, with feature_038 receipt, or (with one marginal exception)
   with feature_038 benefit. The dataset shows no detectable demographic
   bias in either treatment access or treatment effectiveness.
4. The consolidated multivariable model with biomarker interactions
   explains ~90% of the variance in pfs_months, suggesting the major
   structural determinants of outcome are well-captured by a small,
   interpretable set of features and one treatment-by-biomarker
   interaction.
