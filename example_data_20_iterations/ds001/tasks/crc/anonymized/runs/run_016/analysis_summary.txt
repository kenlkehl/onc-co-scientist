Analysis summary - dataset ds001_crc (n = 50,000; outcome pfs_months)
=====================================================================

Approach
--------
The cohort contains 124 de-identified features (`feature_001`-`feature_123` plus
two free-text categorical fields, `feature_064` and `feature_018`) and a single
continuous outcome, `pfs_months` (mean 4.31, sd 2.29, range 0-16.25). Across
25 iterations I (i) screened every feature univariately against the outcome,
(ii) ranked candidates by signed effect and statistical strength, (iii) built
a joint multivariable OLS, and (iv) probed pairwise interactions plus the
demographic-equity questions raised by the column inventory.

Strongest individual predictors (univariate, all p < 1e-7)
----------------------------------------------------------
1.  feature_078 (continuous, range 30-90, mean ~65). Spearman rho = +0.785
    against pfs_months; mean PFS rises monotonically across deciles from
    1.33 mo (lowest decile) to 7.45 mo (top decile). By far the dominant
    single predictor in the cohort.
2.  feature_057 (ordinal 0/1/2; ~35% / 50% / 15%). Strict monotonic
    decrease in mean PFS: 5.27 -> 4.05 -> 2.91 mo. Behaviour consistent
    with a performance-status-like variable.
3.  feature_051 (binary, 55% positive). Mean PFS 3.70 vs 5.05 mo
    (positive vs negative); difference -1.35 mo, p < 1e-300. Strongest
    binary negative predictor.
4.  feature_038 (binary, 20% positive). Mean PFS 5.09 vs 4.12 mo;
    difference +0.97 mo, p ~ 1.7e-216. Strongest binary positive
    (favorable) predictor.
5.  feature_099 (continuous, 0-24.6, ~28% zeros). Spearman rho = -0.120;
    Q1 -> Q4 means 4.59 -> 3.99 mo. Negative biomarker.
6.  feature_092 (continuous, 1.5-5.5). Spearman rho = +0.103;
    Q1 -> Q4 means 4.03 -> 4.58 mo. Positive biomarker (range
    consistent with serum albumin).
7.  feature_013 / feature_043 / feature_109 (binary, all negative
    effects of -0.23 to -0.33 mo, all p < 1e-6).
8.  feature_009 (highly skewed continuous, range 0.02-777). Modest
    negative effect, Spearman rho = -0.047 (p ~ 1e-25).
9.  feature_067 (binary, ~3% prevalence) showed +0.22 mo univariately
    (p ~ 7e-4) but collapses to the null after multivariable adjustment.

Joint multivariable model
-------------------------
A single OLS containing the ten leading clinical predictors -
feature_078, feature_051, C(feature_057), feature_038, feature_099,
feature_092, feature_009, feature_013, feature_043, feature_109 -
explains R^2 = 0.858 of the variance in pfs_months. Every predictor
retains its univariate sign with high statistical confidence:

   feature_078        +0.176 mo / unit (p < 1e-300)
   feature_051        -1.36 mo                    (p < 1e-300)
   feature_057 = 1   -1.20 mo                     (p < 1e-300)
   feature_057 = 2   -2.33 mo                     (p < 1e-300)
   feature_038        +0.94 mo                    (p < 1e-300)
   feature_099        -0.076 mo / unit            (p < 1e-300)
   feature_092        +0.47 mo / unit             (p < 1e-300)
   feature_009        -0.0050 mo / unit           (p ~ 1e-97)
   feature_013        -0.35 mo                    (p < 1e-300)
   feature_043        -0.29 mo                    (p < 1e-300)
   feature_109        -0.36 mo                    (p ~ 1e-78)

After adjustment, the marginal feature_067 effect (+0.22 mo) drops to
+0.02 mo (p = 0.37), confirming confounding rather than independent
biology.

Interaction findings
--------------------
* feature_078 x feature_051 (significant, p ~ 2e-9). The per-unit benefit
  of feature_078 is attenuated by 0.0071 mo per unit when feature_051 = 1
  (i.e., the protective slope is shallower in the high-risk subgroup).
* feature_078 x feature_057 (significant for level 2, p ~ 7e-23). The
  per-unit feature_078 slope drops from +0.180 in feature_057 = 0 to
  +0.163 in feature_057 = 2 - a ceiling on benefit at the worst PS tier.
* feature_051 x feature_057 (borderline only at level 2, p = 0.021).
  feature_051 acts largely additively across PS tiers.
* feature_013 x feature_043 (significant, p ~ 1e-25). Highly sub-additive:
  individually each removes ~0.49 mo, but together they remove only
  ~0.53 mo total (interaction +0.45 mo). The two markers tag overlapping
  biology.
* feature_038 x feature_051 (NS, p = 0.151). The favorable effect of
  feature_038 (~+0.94-+1.01 mo) is essentially identical inside both
  feature_051 strata - i.e., feature_038's benefit transports across
  the high-risk and low-risk subpopulations.
* feature_099 x feature_092 (NS, p = 0.60); feature_078 x feature_038
  (NS, p = 0.28). These pairs act additively.

Demographic equity (race feature_064, insurance feature_018)
------------------------------------------------------------
* Unadjusted ANOVAs across feature_064 (asian / black / hispanic /
  other / white): F = 0.87, p = 0.479. All five group means lie
  within 0.14 mo of each other (4.27-4.41).
* Unadjusted ANOVAs across feature_018 (medicaid / medicare / private
  / uninsured): F = 1.47, p = 0.219. Group means span 4.25-4.33 mo.
* Models that also adjust for the leading clinical features (h21, h22)
  produced no significant race or insurance coefficients (all
  |effect| < 0.04 mo, all p > 0.16).
* Race-stratified analyses within each level of feature_057 (h25)
  showed no within-stratum racial differences (ANOVA p = 0.41,
  0.37, 0.73 for levels 0, 1, 2 respectively).
* Race x feature_051 interaction (h23): all four interaction terms
  |effect| < 0.19 mo, smallest p = 0.25. Insurance x feature_051 (h24):
  no interaction (smallest p = 0.23). The strong feature_051 penalty
  is constant across both demographic dimensions.
* The omnibus model (h28) including demographics increases R^2 over
  the clinical-only model by < 1e-5 - effectively zero incremental
  explanatory power.

Hypotheses supported / refuted
------------------------------
Supported (predicted direction confirmed at p < 0.05):
  h1, h2, h3, h4, h5, h6, h7, h8, h9, h10, h11, h14, h16, h17, h18, h20.

Refuted or null:
  h12 (race main effect; p = 0.48),
  h13 (insurance main effect; p = 0.22),
  h15 (feature_067 independent of multivariable adjustment; p = 0.37,
       which is the *intended* refined claim),
  h19 (feature_038 x feature_051; p = 0.15),
  h21 (race adjusted; all p > 0.35),
  h22 (insurance adjusted; all p > 0.16),
  h23 (race x feature_051; p > 0.25),
  h24 (insurance x feature_051; p > 0.23),
  h25 (within-PS race differences; p = 0.41-0.73 - intentional negative),
  h26 (feature_099 x feature_092; p = 0.60),
  h27 (feature_078 x feature_038; p = 0.28),
  h28 (demographics adding incremental information; delta R^2 < 1e-5).

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. Progression-free survival in this colorectal cohort is governed by a
   small set of clinical and biomarker features. ~86% of the variance is
   captured by ten predictors. feature_078 (continuous, age-like) is
   dominant, followed by the ordinal feature_057 (PS-like) and the
   binary feature_051 (poor-prognosis attribute).
2. The principal favorable molecular marker, feature_038, confers
   approximately the same +0.94-+1.0-month benefit irrespective of
   feature_051 status, performance-status tier, or demographic
   subgroup - i.e., it is a transportable prognostic signal.
3. Two additive-vs-interaction patterns matter clinically:
   (a) feature_013 and feature_043 are partially redundant - they
   should not be added as if independent;
   (b) the per-unit feature_078 benefit is attenuated in patients
   with feature_051 = 1 or feature_057 = 2.
4. There is no measurable racial or insurance-based disparity in
   pfs_months in this cohort - neither unadjusted, nor after clinical
   adjustment, nor stratified inside performance-status or feature_051
   subgroups, nor as differential effect modification of the strongest
   prognostic markers. Demographics add < 1e-5 to model R^2.
