Analysis Summary for ds001_crc (n = 50,000 patients, outcome = pfs_months)
============================================================================

Overview
--------
Across 25 iterations I screened the 124 baseline features against the outcome
pfs_months, then refined hypotheses around the strongest signals. The data
display a clear and biologically plausible structure: a single dominant
prognostic variable (feature_078, plausibly age), an ECOG/stage-like ordinal
factor (feature_057), a binary disease-severity-like marker (feature_051),
and one targeted-therapy-like binary intervention (feature_038) whose
benefit is gated by a small set of mutually exclusive predictive markers
(feature_013, feature_043, feature_109, feature_067). Standard biochemical
prognostic variables (feature_092 albumin-like, feature_099 inflammation-like,
feature_009 CEA-like, feature_006 LDH-/ALP-like) contribute additional but
quantitatively smaller information.

The full multivariable summary model achieves R^2 = 0.86 on pfs_months.

Iterations 1-3: Univariate landscape
------------------------------------
- feature_078 (continuous, range 30-90, mean 65, SD 10) is monotonically
  POSITIVELY associated with pfs_months. Spearman rho = +0.785 (p < 1e-300).
  The decile means rise from 1.33 months in the youngest decile to 7.45 months
  in the oldest decile -- an extraordinarily strong association. The
  distribution and range are consistent with age-in-years; the directionality
  (older = longer PFS) is the opposite of the usual clinical intuition for
  metastatic CRC, suggesting either a synthetic-data construction or a
  selection/treatment-pattern artifact in the source EHR cohort. Either way,
  feature_078 is by far the strongest single predictor in the dataset.
  Hypothesis SUPPORTED.

- feature_057 (ordinal 0/1/2): mean PFS 5.27 / 4.05 / 2.91 months; clear
  ordinal step-down (consistent with ECOG performance status or stage).
  Hypothesis SUPPORTED.

- feature_051 (binary, prevalence 55%): mean PFS 5.05 (=0) vs 3.70 (=1),
  difference -1.35 months, p<1e-300. Hypothesis SUPPORTED. Plausibly a
  metastatic / advanced-disease indicator.

- feature_038 (binary, prevalence 20%): mean PFS 4.12 (=0) vs 5.09 (=1),
  difference +0.97 months, p<1e-300. Hypothesis SUPPORTED. Identified as
  the candidate treatment.

Iterations 4-9: feature_038 is a treatment whose benefit is biomarker-gated
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
- feature_038 x feature_013 interaction = -1.63 months (p<1e-300). In
  feature_013=0 patients, feature_038 raises mean PFS by ~+1.6 months; in
  feature_013=1 patients, that gain is essentially erased. SUPPORTED.

- feature_038 x feature_043 interaction = -1.46 months (p<1e-300). Same
  pattern -- feature_043 abolishes the feature_038 benefit. SUPPORTED.

- Mutual-exclusivity test: cross-tabulation showed 0 patients with
  feature_013=1 AND feature_109=1; 0 with feature_013=1 AND feature_067=1;
  0 with feature_109=1 AND feature_067=1. The three markers are mutually
  exclusive, suggesting they encode three distinct alterations of a single
  underlying gene (or three states of one categorical variable). SUPPORTED.

- feature_038 x feature_109 interaction = -0.97 months (p=6e-80). feature_109
  is also a resistance marker. Among feature_109=1 patients, feature_038
  appears to slightly worsen PFS (raw means 4.14 -> 3.94). SUPPORTED.

- feature_038 x feature_067 interaction = +0.91 months (p=4e-48). Unlike the
  other three, feature_067 is a SENSITIVITY marker -- patients with
  feature_067=1 derive an even larger gain from feature_038 (raw means 4.13
  untreated -> 6.05 treated, a nearly 2-month gain). SUPPORTED.

- Combining the three mutually-exclusive resistance markers, I defined a
  "wild-type-like" subgroup (feature_013=0 AND feature_043=0 AND feature_109=0;
  ~35% of cohort, n=17,372). Within this subgroup, the adjusted pfs_months
  benefit of feature_038 is +2.74 months (p<1e-300). In the complementary
  "mutant-like" subgroup, the feature_038 effect is -0.003 months (p=0.35,
  not significant). Treatment benefit is essentially restricted to the
  wild-type-like subgroup. SUPPORTED.

Iteration 10: The mutation markers are predictive, not prognostic
-----------------------------------------------------------------
- Among untreated (feature_038=0) patients, mean pfs_months is essentially
  identical (4.10-4.14) across all combinations of feature_013/043/109 status.
  Range of means = 0.04 months. These markers do NOT shorten PFS in untreated
  patients; they only abolish the response to feature_038. They are pure
  predictive markers, not prognostic ones. SUPPORTED.

Iterations 11-13: Other prognostic variables
--------------------------------------------
- feature_092 (range 1.5-5.5, plausibly albumin): adjusted coefficient
  +0.47 per unit (p<1e-300), or about +0.24 months per SD. SUPPORTED.

- feature_099 (range 0-24.6): adjusted coefficient -0.077 per unit
  (p<1e-300), about -0.30 months per SD. Inflammation- or CRP-like.
  SUPPORTED.

- feature_009 (range 0.02-777, plausibly CEA): adjusted coefficient
  -0.0048 per unit (p~4e-124), about -0.08 months per SD. SUPPORTED.

- feature_006 (range 48-830): adjusted coefficient -0.0005 per unit
  (p~2e-33), about -0.04 months per SD. SUPPORTED.

- After accounting for feature_038 and the mutation markers, NO other binary
  feature has an adjusted main effect with |b| > 0.03 months. There is no
  evidence of a second hidden treatment with a meaningful main effect.
  Hypothesis SUPPORTED -- feature_038 is the dominant therapeutic
  intervention encoded in this dataset.

Iterations 14-16: Demographic effects and disparities
------------------------------------------------------
- feature_064 (race-like, 5 levels): all adjusted coefficients |b| < 0.02
  months and all p > 0.50. No race-based difference in PFS overall. SUPPORTED
  (null hypothesis of no disparity not rejected).

- feature_018 (insurance-like, 4 levels): all |b| < 0.03 months, p > 0.21.
  No insurance-based difference in PFS overall. SUPPORTED.

- Treatment ASSIGNMENT (rate of feature_038 use among wild-type-like
  patients): 19.3-21.4% across all races (chi-square p=0.60); 19.4-20.2%
  across insurance categories (p=0.83). No disparities in access among the
  eligible subgroup. SUPPORTED.

- Race x feature_038 interaction within the wild-type-like subgroup: all
  interaction terms p > 0.08. No racial disparity in treatment benefit.
  SUPPORTED.

- Insurance x feature_038 interaction within the wild-type-like subgroup:
  uninsured patients show ~0.38 months less benefit from feature_038 vs
  medicaid baseline (p=0.0021), in a small subgroup (n=687). The other
  insurance types show no significant difference. This is a small but
  statistically significant disparity that is worth flagging, although the
  absolute magnitude is dwarfed by the +2.7-month main effect. PARTIALLY
  SUPPORTED.

Iterations 17-19: Effect modification of feature_038 by clinical covariates
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
- feature_057 (ECOG-like): treatment benefit is essentially constant across
  the three levels (+2.67, +2.77, +2.74). Even patients with the most severe
  feature_057=2 score derive the full benefit. Interaction terms not
  significant. SUPPORTED -- benefit is consistent across performance status.

- feature_078 (age-like): feature_038 x feature_078 interaction = +0.0062 per
  year (p=0.004). Older patients derive marginally more benefit, but the
  effect is small (~+0.06 months extra benefit per decade of age). SUPPORTED
  but quantitatively modest.

- feature_092 (albumin-like): feature_038 x feature_092 interaction = -0.124
  per unit (p=0.004). Patients with already-high feature_092 derive slightly
  less incremental benefit -- consistent with a ceiling effect. SUPPORTED but
  small relative to the main effect.

Iterations 20-22: Higher-order and refined patterns
---------------------------------------------------
- feature_013 x feature_043 interaction = +0.50 (p=1e-148). When both markers
  are present (~15% of cohort), the joint negative effect on PFS is
  sub-additive by ~0.5 months. Consistent with these markers acting on a
  shared resistance pathway: once one is present, the second does not add
  incremental harm. SUPPORTED.

- Multi-level features (feature_025, 075, 071, 026, 096, 033) show pfs_months
  range across categories <0.2 months and are not substantively prognostic.
  SUPPORTED -- these are likely demographic/clinical category variables with
  weak association.

- feature_038 x feature_051 interaction within wild-type-like subgroup =
  +0.166 (p=1e-4). Patients with worse-prognosis feature_051=1 derive a
  marginally larger absolute gain from feature_038. SUPPORTED but small.

Iterations 23-25: Synthesis
---------------------------
- Per-SD prognostic strength ranking on pfs_months (after full adjustment):
  feature_078 (+1.75 months/SD) >> feature_099 (-0.30) > feature_092 (+0.24)
  > feature_009 (-0.08) > feature_006 (-0.04). feature_078 dominates the
  variance. SUPPORTED.

- In the wild-type-like (sensitive) subgroup, feature_067=1 adds only ~+0.15
  months on top of the +2.7-month feature_038 main effect, since these
  patients are already in the responder population. The strong feature_067 x
  feature_038 interaction observed in the full cohort comes mostly from the
  contrast with feature_038-non-responders. PARTIALLY SUPPORTED.

- Final summary OLS model:
  pfs_months ~ feature_078 + feature_051 + C(feature_057) + feature_092 +
  feature_099 + feature_009 + feature_006 + feature_038*feature_013 +
  feature_038*feature_043 + feature_038*feature_109 + feature_038*feature_067
  achieves R^2 = 0.86. The dataset's structure is consistent with a single
  targeted treatment whose response is gated by mutually exclusive resistance
  markers (feature_013/043/109) and modestly enhanced by a sensitivity marker
  (feature_067), set against a backdrop of standard prognostic variables
  (age-like, performance status, disease severity, albumin-like, inflammation-
  like, CEA-like). SUPPORTED.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. There is a single dominant treatment-effect pattern in this cohort.
   feature_038 is a binary treatment indicator (~20% of patients receive it).
   Its benefit is essentially confined to a "wild-type-like" subgroup
   (~35% of cohort) defined by absence of three mutually exclusive markers
   (feature_013, feature_043, feature_109). Within that subgroup, feature_038
   adds ~+2.74 months of pfs_months. Outside that subgroup, feature_038 has
   no benefit. feature_067 (a fourth mutually-exclusive marker) acts as a
   sensitivity rather than resistance marker, with feature_038 retaining its
   benefit in feature_067=1 patients.

2. The mutation markers (feature_013/043/109) are predictive, not prognostic
   -- in untreated patients they have essentially no effect on PFS. This is
   the classic pattern of RAS/RAF-style mutations and anti-EGFR therapy in
   metastatic CRC.

3. feature_078 (continuous, range 30-90) is by far the strongest single
   predictor, with a Spearman rho of 0.785 and an adjusted slope of +0.176
   months per unit. The direction is unusual for clinical age in metastatic
   CRC, but the variable behaves like a single dominant prognostic factor.

4. Standard biochemical/clinical prognostics (feature_092 albumin-like,
   feature_099 inflammation-like, feature_009 CEA-like, feature_006 LDH/ALP-
   like, feature_051 disease-severity-like, feature_057 ECOG/stage-like) all
   contribute independent prognostic information in the expected directions,
   though with smaller effect sizes per SD than feature_078.

5. There are no meaningful disparities by race (feature_064) or insurance
   (feature_018) in either overall pfs_months, treatment assignment among
   eligible patients, or treatment benefit -- with a single small exception:
   uninsured patients (n=687) within the wild-type-like subgroup show ~0.38
   fewer months of feature_038 benefit than medicaid patients (p=0.002).
   This is small relative to the +2.7-month main effect but is the one
   detectable disparity in the dataset.

6. No second hidden treatment was detected: after accounting for feature_038
   and the mutation markers, no remaining binary feature has an adjusted
   main effect with |b| > 0.03 months on pfs_months.

7. The full prognostic + predictive model captures ~86% of the variance in
   pfs_months. Most of that ceiling is driven by feature_078; the
   feature_038-by-mutation interactions explain the bulk of the remaining
   structure.
