ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_crc (50,000 patients; outcome: pfs_months)
====================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Across 25 iterations I tested main effects, interactions, subgroup
heterogeneity, and a multivariable model relating 123 anonymized features
(80 binary, 7 ordinal/multi-level integer, 34 continuous, 2 categorical
strings) to progression-free survival in months. The cohort mean PFS was
4.31 months (SD 2.29; range 0–16.25). A small set of variables explains
the great majority of variance in PFS (R^2 ≈ 0.86 with eight predictors).

DOMINANT MAIN EFFECTS (Iterations 1–2)
--------------------------------------
* feature_078 (continuous, range 30–90): The strongest single predictor.
  Univariate OLS slope = +0.176 months per unit, p ≈ 0 (Pearson r = +0.77).
  Mean PFS rises monotonically across coarse bins: 30–50 → 1.08 mo,
  50–60 → 2.65, 60–70 → 4.29, 70–80 → 5.95, 80–90 → 7.78 — so a 60-unit
  span shifts mean PFS by ~6.7 months. Direction supported.

* feature_051 (binary, prevalence 55%): mean PFS 3.66 (on) vs 5.02 (off);
  difference = -1.35 months, p ≈ 0. Strongest binary risk marker.
  Direction supported.

* feature_038 (binary, prevalence 20%): mean PFS difference = +0.97 months
  (5.16 vs 4.20), p = 1.7e-216. Strongest binary positive marker.
  Direction supported.

* feature_057 (ordinal 0/1/2; n = 17592 / 24971 / 7437): step-wise PFS
  decline 5.27 → 4.05 → 2.91 months; OLS slope = -1.19 months per level,
  p ≈ 0. Direction supported.

Secondary (univariately significant) main effects:
  feature_013 (binary): diff = -0.33, p = 1.9e-58
  feature_043 (binary): diff = -0.31, p = 1.0e-49
  feature_109 (binary, ~5% prevalence): diff = -0.23, p = 3.5e-7
  feature_067 (binary, ~3%): diff = +0.22, p = 7.3e-4
  feature_099 (continuous, range 0–25): slope = -0.073, p = 2.2e-167
  feature_092 (continuous, range 1.5–5.5): slope = +0.47, p = 3.2e-115

Categorical demographics-coded variables tested in iterations 12–13:
  feature_064 ("white", "black", "hispanic", "asian", "other"):
    Unadjusted ANOVA p = 0.48; adjusted joint F-test p = 0.67 — NOT
    associated with PFS in this cohort.
  feature_018 ("medicare", "private", "uninsured", "medicaid"):
    Unadjusted p = 0.22; adjusted p = 0.22 — NOT associated with PFS.

NONLINEARITY (Iterations 3–4, 15, 25)
-------------------------------------
* feature_057 categorical vs linear (Iter 3): nested F p = 0.075 — no
  meaningful departure from linearity; the three group means are nearly
  equally spaced (step -1.22 then -1.14).
* feature_078 quadratic term (Iter 4): coefficient = +5.9e-4, p = 7e-34.
  Statistically detectable in a 50k-row cohort but practically tiny — the
  binned means in Iteration 25 (steps +1.57, +1.64, +1.66, +1.83 across
  10-unit bins) are visually linear with slope matching the global OLS.
  Refined hypothesis (slope is non-linear): supported statistically but
  trivial in magnitude.
* feature_099 quadratic (Iter 15): p = 0.59 — linear approximation
  sufficient.

INTERACTIONS (Iterations 6–11, 14–16, 18, 19)
---------------------------------------------
Significant:
* feature_078 × feature_051 (Iter 6): -0.0071 (p = 1.9e-9). The strong
  positive feature_078 slope is somewhat dampened in feature_051==1
  patients. Direction supported.
* feature_078 × feature_057 (Iter 7): -0.0072 (p = 1.3e-18). The
  feature_078 slope is reduced as feature_057 increases — equivalently,
  feature_057's harmful effect is muted at high feature_078. Direction
  supported.
* feature_038 × feature_057 (Iter 9 / Iter 19): -0.050 (p = 4e-4) in the
  full model. Slight modulation: the +1-month feature_038 advantage is
  marginally smaller at higher feature_057, but subgroup analysis
  (Iter 23) showed it is preserved across all three strata.
* feature_051 × feature_057 (Iter 10): +0.063 (p = 0.019). Modest
  attenuation of the feature_051 effect at higher feature_057.
* feature_013 × feature_043 (Iter 11): +0.45 (p = 1.7e-25). The two
  negative main effects are *sub*-additive: patients carrying both flags
  do worse than either alone, but not by the sum of the two effects.
  Direction supported.

Non-significant or null:
* feature_038 × feature_051 (Iter 8): p = 0.15. Effects of these two
  binary markers act largely additively. Hypothesized modification not
  supported.
* feature_038 × feature_078 (Iter 14): p = 0.28. feature_038 benefit is
  consistent across feature_078.
* feature_099 × feature_051 (Iter 15): p = 0.23.
* feature_092 × feature_057 (Iter 16): p = 0.86.
* 3-way feature_038 × feature_051 × feature_057 (Iter 18): p = 0.18 — no
  evidence the three combine super-additively.

SUBGROUP STRATIFIED EFFECTS (Iterations 22–23)
----------------------------------------------
* feature_038 effect within feature_051 strata: +0.94 mo (feature_051==0,
  p = 6e-95) and +1.01 mo (feature_051==1, p = 4e-140). Highly consistent.
* feature_038 effect within feature_057 strata: +0.92 (level 0), +0.99
  (level 1), +0.90 (level 2). All strongly significant; magnitude similar
  across strata. Hypothesis of cross-stratum consistency supported.

ADJUSTED EFFECTS OF RARE PREDICTORS (Iterations 20–21)
------------------------------------------------------
* feature_109: adjusted coefficient = -0.20 (p = 4.5e-19) — independent
  signal, slightly smaller than unadjusted (-0.23). Direction supported.
* feature_067: adjusted coefficient = +0.18 (p = 6.0e-11) — independent
  positive signal even after adjusting for feature_078, feature_051,
  feature_057. Direction supported.

WEAKER SIGNALS LOST UPON ADJUSTMENT (Iteration 24)
--------------------------------------------------
* feature_089 (univariate p = 0.017): adjusted p = 0.029, marginal.
* feature_102, feature_112, feature_079: all non-significant after
  adjustment. The weak univariate signals appear to reflect confounding
  with the dominant predictors rather than independent biology.

VARIANCE STRUCTURE (Iteration 17)
---------------------------------
Levene test on residuals from the main-effects model, grouped by
feature_057, gave W = 3.66, p = 0.026. Residual SDs were 0.984 / 0.959 /
0.977 — statistically detectable but very mild heterogeneity in absolute
terms.

MULTIVARIABLE MODELS (Iterations 5 & 19)
----------------------------------------
* 8-predictor main-effects model (feature_078 + feature_051 + feature_038
  + feature_057 + feature_099 + feature_092 + feature_013 + feature_043):
  R^2 = 0.856; every coefficient retains its univariate sign and remains
  highly significant. The dominant predictors are not confounded by each
  other.
* Full model (10 main effects + 3 interactions): R^2 = 0.857. Adding
  three of the strongest interactions plus the two rare flags
  (feature_109, feature_067) buys only ~0.001 incremental R^2 — the
  bulk of explained variance comes from the additive model.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. PFS in this cohort is dominated by a small set of features:
   feature_078 (the strongest, continuous and near-linear), feature_051
   (binary, large negative), feature_038 (binary, large positive), and
   feature_057 (ordinal, monotone negative). Together with feature_092,
   feature_099, feature_013, and feature_043, they explain ~86% of
   PFS variance.

2. Most relationships are well captured by an additive model. Of the
   pairwise interactions tested, only those involving feature_078 with
   feature_051 / feature_057, plus feature_013 × feature_043, are
   convincingly non-zero — and even these are small relative to the main
   effects (changing slopes by a few percent or sub-additive negative
   stacking, not effect reversal).

3. The rare flags feature_109 (negative) and feature_067 (positive)
   carry independent prognostic signal even after adjusting for the
   strongest predictors.

4. Demographic-coded variables (feature_064 race, feature_018
   insurance) show no detectable association with PFS in this cohort,
   either unadjusted or after adjusting for the dominant clinical
   predictors.

5. No evidence was found for clinically important non-linearities (the
   feature_078 quadratic term is statistically significant only because
   of the cohort size; binned means are essentially linear).

6. The benefit of feature_038 is consistent across feature_051 strata
   and across all three feature_057 strata, indicating it functions as
   a broadly applicable positive prognostic marker rather than acting
   in a particular subgroup.
