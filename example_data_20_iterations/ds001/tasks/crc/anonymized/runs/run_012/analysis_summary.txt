Analysis summary — ds001_crc (n = 50,000; outcome = pfs_months)
================================================================

Goal. Surface clinically meaningful patterns linking the de-identified
features to progression-free survival (pfs_months), focusing on main
effects, subgroup heterogeneity, and biologically plausible
treatment–biomarker interactions. Twenty-five iterations were run.

Outcome distribution. pfs_months is approximately positive and
right-skewed (mean 4.31, median 4.21, sd 2.29, range 0–16.25).

Main-effect findings (iterations 1–10)
--------------------------------------

Six features dominate the univariate landscape and are also dominant
after multivariable adjustment:

  feature_078 (continuous, range 30–90, mean 65). Strongest single
  predictor. Each unit is worth +0.18 month of PFS (p < 1e-300; r =
  +0.766). Mean PFS rises monotonically from 1.33 mo in the lowest
  decile to 7.45 mo in the highest. Behaviorally consistent with a
  performance-status-like fitness scale (e.g., Karnofsky).

  feature_057 (3-level ordinal, 0/1/2). Strong negative ordinal
  effect: mean PFS = 5.27 / 4.05 / 2.91 across levels 0/1/2 (β =
  -1.19/level, p < 1e-300). Behaviorally consistent with an
  ECOG-like score.

  feature_051 (binary, 55%). The largest binary main effect:
  -1.35 months (3.70 vs 5.06; p < 1e-300). Behaves like a major
  prognostic factor (e.g., presence of metastatic disease, line of
  therapy, or visceral involvement).

  feature_038 (binary, 20%). The largest positive binary main
  effect: +0.97 months (5.09 vs 4.12; p = 1.7e-216). On main effect
  alone this looks like a beneficial treatment or favorable
  biomarker — but the interaction analyses (below) show this average
  is the blend of a large benefit in the right subgroup and
  essentially no effect outside it.

  feature_092 (continuous, range 1.5–5.5, mean 3.80). Positive
  effect on PFS (β = +0.47/unit, p = 3e-115; multivariable +0.47).
  Range and direction are consistent with a serum-protein–like
  marker (e.g., albumin).

  feature_099 (continuous, range 0–24.6, mean 3.84). Negative
  effect (β = -0.073/unit; p = 2.2e-167). Behaviorally consistent
  with an inflammatory or tumor-burden marker (e.g., LDH, CRP, or a
  tumor-marker proxy).

Two additional binary features have small but statistically clear
main effects: feature_013 (-0.33 mo, p = 1.9e-58, prevalence 42%),
feature_043 (-0.31 mo, p = 1.0e-49, prevalence 35%). Two rare
binaries also show modest but reproducible effects: feature_109
(-0.23 mo, p = 3.5e-7, prevalence 4.5%) and feature_067 (+0.22 mo,
p = 7.3e-4, prevalence 3.0%).

A multivariable model on the ten main effects above achieves
R² = 0.857; every coefficient retains its sign and statistical
significance after mutual adjustment (feature_067 alone falls to
p = 0.36 once interactions are considered; see below).

The central finding: feature_038 benefit is biomarker-conditional
-----------------------------------------------------------------

Iterations 11–16 establish the most clinically interesting pattern
in the dataset: feature_038's positive PFS effect is governed by a
small mutation/biomarker-like axis composed of feature_013,
feature_043, feature_067, and feature_109.

  feature_038 × feature_013. Interaction β = -1.63, p < 1e-300.
  When feature_013 = 1, feature_038 = 1 vs 0 yields essentially no
  PFS difference (4.14 vs 4.12). When feature_013 = 0, feature_038
  yields +1.66 months.

  feature_038 × feature_043. Interaction β = -1.47, p < 1e-300.
  Direction and magnitude mirror the feature_013 interaction. In a
  joint model both interactions remain large, indicating that
  feature_013 and feature_043 each independently abolish the
  feature_038 benefit. Either positive marker alone is sufficient
  to negate the effect.

  Double-negative subgroup (feature_013 = 0 AND feature_043 = 0,
  n = 18,853). Mean PFS = 6.64 (feature_038 = 1) vs 4.12
  (feature_038 = 0); raw difference +2.52 months, p < 1e-300.
  Multivariable-adjusted feature_038 effect in this subgroup =
  +2.52, p < 1e-300. Outside the double-negative subgroup the
  feature_038 effect is statistically indistinguishable from zero
  (deltas of -0.04 to +0.07 mo).

  feature_038 × feature_067. Interaction β = +0.89, p = 1.6e-56.
  Within feature_038 = 1 patients, feature_067 = 1 adds an
  additional ~+1.0 mo of PFS (6.05 vs 5.06). In feature_038 = 0
  patients feature_067 has no effect. feature_067 is therefore an
  enhancer of the feature_038 response.

  feature_038 × feature_109. Interaction β = -0.99, p = 2.1e-102.
  Within feature_038 = 1 patients, feature_109 = 1 reverses the
  benefit, yielding shorter PFS than feature_038 = 0 (3.97 vs 5.14;
  -1.17 mo within the stratum). In feature_038 = 0 patients
  feature_109 has no effect. feature_109 is a negative effect
  modifier — a candidate marker of acquired or intrinsic
  resistance.

A structural observation (iteration 16) explains the molecular
shape of this axis: feature_013 = 1 is mutually exclusive with
both feature_067 = 1 and feature_109 = 1. When feature_013 = 1
(n = 20,898), feature_067 = 0 and feature_109 = 0 in every patient.
When feature_013 = 0, feature_067 occurs in 5.2% and feature_109
in 7.8%. This pattern is consistent with a single biomarker axis
in which feature_013 codes one alteration class (resistance type
A) and feature_067 / feature_109 code two alternative states of
the 'feature_013-negative' background (one enhancing benefit, one
abolishing it). The differential impact on the same therapy is
exactly what is expected from a panel of distinct, mutually
exclusive driver alterations.

This entire pattern — a treatment that helps only in patients
without two prevalent resistance markers, plus a rare positive
predictive marker that augments benefit and a rare negative marker
that reverses it — is structurally identical to the well-known
RAS / BRAF biology in metastatic colorectal cancer (e.g., anti-EGFR
benefit confined to RAS wild-type, with BRAF V600E as a negative
modifier). The 'crc' label on the dataset is consistent with that
interpretation, although feature labels remain de-identified.

Other tested patterns (iterations 17–24)
----------------------------------------

  feature_078 quadratic (iter 17). Adding I(feature_078²) yields
  β = +6.8e-4, p = 1.4e-123: a small but highly significant upward
  curvature. Each additional unit is worth slightly more PFS at
  high values than at low values. Practically minor relative to
  the linear effect.

  feature_038 × feature_057 (iter 18). Interaction β = -0.052,
  p = 3e-4. Statistically significant but very small; the
  feature_038 benefit is essentially preserved across performance-
  status strata (deltas of +0.93, +0.99, +0.90 across levels
  0/1/2).

  feature_038 × feature_078 (iter 19). β = +0.0012, p = 0.20.
  Not significant. The treatment effect does not vary along the
  fitness continuum.

  feature_064 (race) main effect (iter 20). ANOVA p = 0.48; means
  span 4.27–4.41 across white/black/hispanic/asian/other. After
  multivariable adjustment all coefficients p > 0.35. There is no
  evidence of a race main effect on pfs_months.

  feature_064 × feature_038 (iter 20). Stratified feature_038
  benefit ranges from +0.92 mo (white) to +1.22 mo (other) — small
  variation that is not significantly different across race
  categories. No evidence of disparities in feature_038 response
  by race.

  feature_018 (insurance) main effect (iter 21). ANOVA p = 0.22;
  multivariable coefficients all p > 0.16. No detectable insurance
  effect on pfs_months.

  feature_007 and feature_122 (binary, ~45% prevalence each;
  iter 22). p = 0.77 and ~0.45. No association.

  Other ordinals (feature_071, feature_086, feature_025,
  feature_075, feature_026, feature_096, feature_033; iter 23).
  All Spearman rhos with pfs_months are |r| ≤ 0.01 with p > 0.10.
  These features are not associated with pfs_months at the
  univariate level and are not part of the dominant prognostic /
  predictive axis.

  feature_051 × feature_057 (iter 24). β = +0.048, p = 2.9e-5.
  Significant but very small; the joint effect of advanced disease
  and poor performance status is essentially additive (the
  feature_051 deficit is slightly smaller in patients who already
  have a worse feature_057).

Final integrative model (iteration 25)
--------------------------------------

A consolidated OLS model with the six dominant prognostic factors
(feature_078, I(feature_078²), feature_057, feature_051,
feature_092, feature_099) plus feature_038 and its four
biomarker interactions (× feature_013, × feature_043, × feature_067,
× feature_109) achieves R² = 0.898 vs 0.857 for main effects only
(ΔR² = 0.041; F-test highly significant).

  Interpretation in the natural scale.
    - Baseline (all biomarkers zero, feature_038 = 0): PFS is
      governed by the prognostic block.
    - feature_038 net effect (over baseline) ≈
        +2.15 mo if feature_013 = feature_043 = 0 and no enhancer
                   / silencer is present;
        +3.04 mo if additionally feature_067 = 1 (rare
                   enhancer);
        +1.16 mo if feature_109 = 1 (rare reverser, partial
                   reversal of benefit);
        ≈ 0 mo if feature_013 = 1 or feature_043 = 1.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------

Supported:
  - h1 (feature_078 +ve), h2 (feature_057 -ve ordinal), h3
    (feature_051 -ve), h4 (feature_038 +ve overall), h5
    (feature_013 -ve), h6 (feature_043 -ve), h7 (feature_092 +ve),
    h8 (feature_099 -ve), h9 (feature_109 -ve), h10 (feature_067
    +ve), h11 (multivariable model, R² = 0.857), h12 (feature_038
    × feature_013 interaction), h13 (feature_038 × feature_043
    interaction), h14 (large feature_038 benefit confined to
    double-negative subgroup), h15 (feature_038 × feature_067
    enhancer), h16 (feature_038 × feature_109 reverser), h17
    (feature_013 / feature_067 / feature_109 mutually exclusive
    biomarker axis), h18 (feature_078 quadratic), h19 (feature_038
    × feature_057 — very small but significant), h27
    (feature_051 × feature_057 significant but tiny), h28 (final
    integrative model).

Refuted (no detectable effect under the alpha = 0.05 threshold,
or magnitude clinically negligible):
  - h20 (feature_038 × feature_078 interaction; p = 0.20 — uniform
    across the fitness continuum), h21 / h22 (no race main effect,
    no race-modified feature_038 effect), h23 (no insurance
    effect), h24 / h25 (feature_007 and feature_122 not associated
    with pfs_months), h26 (the other ordinal features tested do
    not predict pfs_months).

Overall conclusion
------------------

Pfs_months in this colorectal-cancer-labeled cohort is well
explained (R² ≈ 0.90) by a small set of variables: a strong
fitness-like continuous score (feature_078) with a mild upward
curvature, a 3-level performance-status-like ordinal (feature_057),
a major binary prognostic factor (feature_051), two continuous
laboratory-like markers (feature_092 positive, feature_099
negative), and a single 'treatment' indicator (feature_038) whose
benefit is biomarker-conditional. The biomarker axis comprises four
mutually-related binary features: feature_013 and feature_043
abolish the benefit when present (each independently and
sufficiently); feature_067 enhances the benefit when present; and
feature_109 reverses it. No race or insurance disparity in either
overall PFS or feature_038 response was detected. The classical
shape of the result — a targeted-therapy benefit confined to a
'double wild-type' subgroup with additional positive and negative
predictive markers — is consistent with anti-EGFR / RAS-pathway
biology in metastatic colorectal cancer, although feature
identities remain anonymized.
