Analysis summary — dataset ds001_crc (n = 50,000)
===================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (mean 4.31, SD 2.29, range 0.0 to 16.25).

The analysis explored 124 features (80 binary, 35 continuous, 7 multi-level
ordinal/categorical, 2 string demographic) against pfs_months across 25
iterations. The protocol began with univariate screens, moved through
multivariable adjustment, and probed pre-specified interactions and subgroup
contrasts (especially the demographic axes that are usual targets of bias
audits: race, insurance).

------------------------------------------------------------------
1. The dominant prognostic axis: feature_078, feature_057, feature_051
------------------------------------------------------------------

Three variables drive most of the variance in pfs_months. In a multivariable
OLS model that uses feature_078, feature_057, feature_051, feature_038,
feature_092, feature_099, feature_013, and feature_043, R² = 0.856.

feature_078 (continuous, mean 65, range 0–90): Pearson r = +0.766 with
pfs_months; OLS slope +0.176 mo per unit (p effectively 0). Adjusted
slope +0.176 (p ≈ 0). Partial ΔR² when removed from the full model = 0.583,
the single largest contribution. A quadratic term for feature_078 was also
significant (β = +0.00059, p = 7.0e-34), indicating a mildly convex
relationship with pfs_months on top of the dominant linear effect. By far
the strongest predictor in the cohort.

feature_057 (ordinal 0/1/2; counts 17,592 / 24,971 / 7,437): mean PFS
5.27 / 4.05 / 2.91 — perfectly monotone. Linear slope -1.19 mo per level
(r = -0.35, p ≈ 0); adjusted β = -1.17 (p ≈ 0); partial ΔR² = 0.120. The
0-to-2 spread is 2.36 months. Pattern is consistent with a 3-level
performance status / global functioning summary.

feature_051 (binary; 27,481 carriers, 55%): mean PFS 3.70 vs 5.05 (Δ = -1.35,
p ≈ 0). Adjusted β = -1.36 (p ≈ 0); partial ΔR² = 0.087. The single most
impactful binary feature in the dataset.

feature_038 (binary; 10,022 carriers, 20%): mean PFS 5.09 vs 4.12 (Δ = +0.97,
p = 1.7e-216). Adjusted β = +0.94 (p ≈ 0); partial ΔR² = 0.027. A protective
factor that is preserved within both feature_051=0 (Δ = +0.94) and
feature_051=1 (Δ = +1.01) strata, with no significant feature_051 × feature_038
interaction (β = +0.07, p = 0.15).

------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Continuous biomarker contributions
------------------------------------------------------------------

feature_092 (mean 3.80, SD 0.50, plausibly serum albumin): r = +0.10 with
pfs_months; adjusted β = +0.47 mo per unit, p ≈ 0. Quartile means rise
monotonically (Q1→Q4: 4.03, 4.28, 4.44, 4.58). Partial ΔR² = 0.011.

feature_099 (continuous, mean 3.84): r = -0.12, adjusted β = -0.076 mo per
unit (p ≈ 0); partial ΔR² = 0.016.

feature_009 (continuous, range to 777): adjusted β = -0.0051 (p = 2.6e-93).

feature_006 (continuous, mean 224, plausibly platelet count): adjusted β
= -0.00041 (p = 7.6e-17); small but reliable.

feature_028 and feature_070, although significant in unadjusted screens at the
α = 0.05 level for feature_028, did not retain explanatory value once the
other top predictors entered the model (feature_028 adj. p = 0.27,
feature_070 adj. p = 0.94). Confounded by stronger correlates.

------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Secondary binary features
------------------------------------------------------------------

feature_013 (n=20,898): Δ = -0.33 mo unadjusted; adjusted β = -0.33 (p ≈ 0);
partial ΔR² = 0.005. No interaction with feature_051 (p = 0.56).
feature_043 (n=17,657): Δ = -0.31 mo unadjusted; adjusted β = -0.29
(p = 6.1e-268); partial ΔR² = 0.004. No interaction with feature_051
(p = 0.15).
feature_109 (n=2,272, rare): Δ = -0.23 mo (p = 3.5e-7).
feature_067 (n=1,506, rare): Δ = +0.22 mo (p = 7.3e-4).

The 70+ remaining binary features did not produce a meaningful univariate
signal (no other binary reached |Δ| ≥ 0.10 mo with multiplicity-corrected
significance).

------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Demographic features (race, insurance) — the bias-audit axes
------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the central null result of the analysis.

Race (feature_064; white 65%, hispanic 15%, black 12%, asian 6%, other 2%):
- Univariate ANOVA on pfs_months: F = 0.87, p = 0.479. Range across the five
  groups was 0.13 mo (4.27 black to 4.41 other).
- After adjustment for the eight prognostic features above (and insurance),
  joint F-test for the four race indicator coefficients: F = 0.48,
  p = 0.752. Largest adjusted race β = +0.019 (other vs reference).
- Race did not modify the prognostic gradient of feature_057 (joint
  interaction p = 0.45) and did not modify the effect of feature_051
  (joint interaction p = 0.69; stratified Δ ranged from -1.21 in black to
  -1.39 in hispanic, all highly significant within strata, all clinically
  similar in magnitude).
- Race × feature_038 interaction reached marginal significance at the joint
  level (p = 0.032). Stratified feature_038 effects: white +0.92, black
  +0.97, hispanic +1.11, asian +1.09, other +1.21. Direction of benefit was
  consistent and large across all groups; the marginal joint p reflects mild
  variation in magnitude rather than any reversal of effect.

Insurance (feature_018; medicare 42%, private 40%, medicaid 14%, uninsured
4%):
- Univariate ANOVA: F = 1.47, p = 0.219. Range 0.08 mo across groups.
- Adjusted joint F-test: F = 0.60, p = 0.616.
- feature_051 × insurance: joint p = 0.69 (stratified Δ = -1.33 to -1.40).
- feature_038 × insurance: joint p = 0.16 (stratified Δ = +0.93 to +1.11).

The clear take-home: in this cohort, prognostic and (apparently) treatment
or biomarker effects on PFS are essentially constant across race and
insurance categories. The only rule with a hint of differential magnitude
is feature_038 × race at p = 0.032 — and even there, every racial group
benefited substantially (Δ ≥ +0.92 mo) in the same direction.

------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Interactions tested
------------------------------------------------------------------

Tested 12 interaction terms.

Significant:
- feature_078 × feature_057: β = -0.0072, p = 1.3e-18. Older patients gain
  less from being feature_057=0 vs 2 (or equivalently, the negative slope
  on feature_057 attenuates at high feature_078). Small in magnitude.
- feature_078 × feature_051: β = -0.0071, p = 1.9e-9. Slope of pfs_months on
  feature_078 is +0.180 in feature_051=0 and +0.173 in feature_051=1 — the
  feature_078 benefit is essentially preserved in both strata, just slightly
  smaller in feature_051=1. Statistically firm but clinically trivial.
- feature_051 × feature_057: β = +0.063, p = 0.019. Mildly antagonistic; the
  combined burden is slightly less than additive.
- feature_038 × race (joint): p = 0.032 (described above; no reversal).
- Quadratic feature_078: β = +0.00059, p = 7.0e-34 (described above).

Not significant:
- feature_051 × feature_038 (p = 0.15) — additive
- feature_038 × feature_057 (p = 0.89) — fully additive
- feature_038 × feature_078 (p = 0.28)
- feature_092 × feature_057 (p = 0.86)
- feature_051 × feature_013 (p = 0.56)
- feature_051 × feature_043 (p = 0.15)
- feature_051 × race (joint p = 0.69)
- feature_051 × insurance (joint p = 0.69)
- feature_057 × race (joint p = 0.45)
- feature_038 × insurance (joint p = 0.16)

------------------------------------------------------------------
6. Joint risk stratification
------------------------------------------------------------------

Cross-classifying patients by feature_057 (0/1/2) × feature_051 (0/1) yields
six prognostic cells, with mean PFS:
  (0, 0) 6.04 mo (n = 7,888) — best
  (0, 1) 4.64 mo (n = 9,704)
  (1, 0) 4.79 mo (n = 11,295)
  (1, 1) 3.44 mo (n = 13,676)
  (2, 0) 3.61 mo (n = 3,329)
  (2, 1) 2.34 mo (n = ~4,108) — worst
The 3.7-month range across this 2 × 3 grid alone identifies a clinically
useful stratification. Adding feature_078 to this grid (continuous) would
sharpen it further given feature_078's large slope.

------------------------------------------------------------------
7. Hypotheses supported vs refuted
------------------------------------------------------------------

Supported: every specific prognostic hypothesis with a signed direction
named at the top of this analysis (h1.1–h1.4, h2.1–h2.4, h3.1–h3.8,
h10.1, h16.1, h16.2, h22.1, h23.1, h24.1–h24.4, h25.1, plus the modest
significant interactions h6.1, h8.1, h19.1, h20.1).

Refuted (failed to reject the null with meaningful effect size, supporting
the *absence* of an effect):
- h4.1, h4.2: race and insurance main effects on pfs_months — no.
- h17.1, h17.2: race and insurance retain non-zero adjusted associations —
  no, both clearly null after adjustment.
- h11.1, h12.1, h14.1: feature_051 and feature_057 effects are not modified
  by race or insurance.
- h5.1, h7.1, h9.1, h15.1, h15.2, h21.1: most additional interactions
  involving feature_038, feature_013, feature_043, feature_092 with the
  major axes — no detectable interaction.
- h24.5, h24.6: feature_028 and feature_070 lose adjusted significance.
- h13.1, h18.1: feature_038 effect is essentially constant across race
  (joint p = 0.032 with no reversal of direction) and across insurance
  (joint p = 0.156).

------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Overall conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------------

(a) pfs_months in this cohort is dominated by three axes: feature_078
    (continuous, partial ΔR² 0.58), feature_057 (3-level ordinal, ΔR² 0.12),
    and feature_051 (binary, ΔR² 0.09). Together with feature_038 and a few
    biomarkers, they explain ≈86% of variance in pfs_months — far higher
    than typical real-world EHR cohorts, suggesting a noise-light or
    structurally regular dataset.

(b) feature_038 looks treatment-like or biomarker-positive-like: a 20%-
    prevalence binary that adds nearly a month of PFS, consistently across
    race, insurance, the high-risk feature_051 stratum, and across all
    levels of feature_057 (no interaction). If this is a treatment, its
    benefit is uniform.

(c) feature_051 looks adverse-prognostic-marker-like: a 55%-prevalence
    binary that subtracts ~1.35 months. It interacts only weakly with
    feature_057 and feature_078, both at clinically negligible magnitude.
    Its effect is uniform across demographic strata (race, insurance).

(d) Demographic disparities in pfs_months are absent in this cohort —
    both unadjusted and adjusted — and demographic groups do not modify
    the prognostic or treatment-like effects studied. The single hint of
    heterogeneity (feature_038 × race, p = 0.032) shows preserved
    direction and large benefit in every group. From a bias-audit
    perspective, the data describe an outcome process that is independent
    of race and insurance once the medical features are accounted for.

(e) The relationship of feature_078 to pfs_months is positive and convex,
    with a small but reliable interaction with both feature_057 and
    feature_051 (older patients gain marginally less benefit when also
    high-risk on those axes). The dominant pattern is monotone and
    near-linear.
