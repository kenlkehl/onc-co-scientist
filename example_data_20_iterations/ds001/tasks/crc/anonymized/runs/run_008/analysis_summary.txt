Oncology dataset ds001_crc — exploratory analysis summary
==========================================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients, 124 anonymized features (80 binary, 34 continuous,
7 ordinal, 2 categorical: feature_064 ~ race with 5 levels, feature_018 ~
insurance with 4 levels). Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival,
range 0–16.25, mean 4.31, sd 2.29). 25 propose-test-refine iterations were
run. All analyses use linear regression on pfs_months (signed effect on the
natural scale of the outcome) with Welch t-tests, Pearson/Spearman
correlations, chi-square tests, and joint Wald F-tests where appropriate.


Hypotheses explored across iterations
-------------------------------------

Univariate main effects (iters 1–5).

H1 — feature_078 (continuous, range 30–90, mean ~65) is a positive predictor
of pfs_months. SUPPORTED: Pearson r = +0.766; mean feature_078 by pfs
quintile rises monotonically 53.4 → 75.5. This single covariate explains
roughly 59% of the variance in pfs_months and is by far the dominant
predictor in the cohort. Higher feature_078 → longer pfs.

H2 — feature_051 reduces pfs_months. SUPPORTED: prevalence 55%, mean
difference -1.352 months (5.02 → 3.67), Welch p ~ 0. Largest binary main
effect in the dataset.

H3 — feature_038 lengthens pfs_months. SUPPORTED: prevalence 20%, mean
difference +0.972 months, p = 1.7e-216.

H4 — feature_057 (ordinal 0/1/2) is monotonically negative. SUPPORTED: mean
pfs 5.27 / 4.05 / 2.91 across the three levels; Spearman rho = -0.360,
p ~ 0. Behaves like a stage- or risk-tier variable.

H5 — race (feature_064) main effect on pfs. NOT SUPPORTED: ANOVA F=0.87,
p=0.479. Means are essentially identical (white 4.32, black 4.27, hispanic
4.31, asian 4.31, other 4.41).

H6 — insurance (feature_018) main effect on pfs. NOT SUPPORTED: F=1.48,
p=0.219.

Multivariable model (iter 6).

H7 — combined model. SUPPORTED, with R² = 0.857. Adjusted signed effects
(months on pfs): feature_078 +0.176/unit; feature_051 -1.361; feature_038
+0.943; feature_013 -0.354; feature_043 -0.287; feature_057 -1.171/level;
feature_109 -0.357; feature_099 -0.076/unit; feature_092 +0.470/unit;
feature_067 +0.021 (NS, p=0.357). All race and insurance coefficients are
non-significant after adjustment.

Interaction analyses (iters 7–15).

H8 — feature_078 × feature_051 negative interaction. SUPPORTED:
interaction = -0.0070 per unit (p=2.0e-15). The pfs decrement from
feature_051 widens with age — about -1.11 at feature_078=30 vs about -1.53
at feature_078=90.

H9 — feature_078 × feature_038. NOT SUPPORTED: +0.0012/unit, p=0.281.
The +0.94-month benefit of feature_038 is uniform across age.

H10 — feature_051 × feature_038. NOT SUPPORTED: +0.029, p=0.179. The two
effects are statistically additive; patients with both have ~ -0.42 months
relative to neither.

H11 — feature_078 × feature_057 negative interaction. SUPPORTED:
-0.0074/unit (p=4.4e-31). Per-level effect of feature_057 grows in
magnitude from ~ -1.06 at feature_078=50 to ~ -1.28 at feature_078=80.

H12 — feature_051 × feature_057 interaction. SUPPORTED but small:
+0.045/level (p=5.2e-04). A modest attenuation of the feature_051 effect at
higher feature_057 levels (likely a floor on pfs at the most severe end).

H13 — feature_038 × feature_057 negative interaction. SUPPORTED but small:
-0.058/level (p=2.5e-04). The feature_038 benefit is mildly weaker at higher
feature_057, although stratified differences (+0.93, +1.00, +0.90 across
levels 0/1/2) remain large and clinically consistent.

H14 — feature_013 × feature_043 large positive interaction. STRONGLY
SUPPORTED: main effects -0.507 and -0.494, interaction +0.510 (p=1.8e-178).
Conditional means relative to neither: 13-only -0.51; 43-only -0.49; both
-0.49. The two features are essentially redundant — having both is no worse
than having either alone, suggesting a shared underlying mechanism or
collinear clinical entity.

H15 — feature_078 × feature_099 negative interaction. SUPPORTED:
-0.00044 per unit-pair (p=3.9e-05). The deleterious effect of feature_099
amplifies modestly with age.

H16 — feature_078 × feature_092 positive interaction. SUPPORTED:
+0.0035 per unit-pair (p=3.1e-05). The protective effect of feature_092
grows with age.

Equity / heterogeneity analyses (iters 16–17, 20–23).

H17 — race-based heterogeneity in feature_051 effect. NOT SUPPORTED:
stratified differences range -1.33 to -1.52 with overlapping CIs; joint
F=1.29, p=0.271. The exposure penalty is uniform across race.

H18 — insurance-based heterogeneity in feature_051 effect. NOT SUPPORTED:
joint F=2.00, p=0.112.

H21 — race-based utilization disparity in major treatment-like binaries.
NOT SUPPORTED: chi-square p-values for feature_051 0.382, feature_038
0.550, feature_013 0.304, feature_043 0.632, feature_109 0.652, feature_067
0.756.

H22 — insurance-based utilization disparity. NOT SUPPORTED: chi-square
p-values 0.505, 0.591, 0.900, 0.692, 0.758, 0.128 across the same six
features.

H23 — race jointly predicts pfs after clinical adjustment. NOT SUPPORTED:
joint F=0.55, p=0.700.

H24 — insurance jointly predicts pfs after clinical adjustment. NOT
SUPPORTED: joint F=0.55, p=0.647.

Stratified confirmations (iters 18–19).

H19 — feature_051 reduces pfs in every age stratum, more strongly in older
patients. SUPPORTED: differences -1.214 (<55), -1.364 (55–70), -1.390 (≥70),
all p ~ 0.

H20 — feature_038 confers benefit in every feature_057 stratum. SUPPORTED:
+0.93 / +1.00 / +0.90 months across levels 0 / 1 / 2, all p < 1e-33.

Functional form and noise control (iters 24–25).

H25 — feature_078 has a positive quadratic component. SUPPORTED: linear
+0.091, quadratic +0.00065 (t=20.5, p<1e-90). Marginal slope rises from
~0.16 at feature_078=50 to ~0.21 at feature_078=85 — the relationship is
convex (accelerating).

H26 — most of the 80 binary features are not independently predictive.
SUPPORTED: a model with all 80 binaries plus the four strongest non-binary
predictors achieves R² = 0.857, identical to the parsimonious model. Only
five binaries survive p<0.001: feature_051, feature_013, feature_038,
feature_043, feature_109.


Overall conclusions
-------------------

1. PFS in this cohort is dominated by feature_078 (a continuous variable
   strongly resembling age, but positively associated with PFS — older
   patients have longer pfs, with a convex/accelerating relationship). It
   alone explains ~59% of variance.

2. A compact set of binary indicators captures essentially all remaining
   binary signal: feature_051 (-1.36 months), feature_038 (+0.94),
   feature_013 (-0.35), feature_043 (-0.29), feature_109 (-0.36). The
   ordinal feature_057 contributes -1.17 per level and behaves like a
   stage/risk variable. Together with feature_092 (+0.47/unit) and
   feature_099 (-0.08/unit) these reach R² = 0.857.

3. Two clinically interesting non-additivities are present:
   • feature_013 × feature_043: a near-perfect canceling positive
     interaction (+0.51 vs main effects of -0.51 and -0.49) — the two
     features behave as redundant proxies for the same underlying
     condition.
   • feature_078 × {feature_051, feature_057, feature_099, feature_092}:
     consistent sign-aligned interactions in which the magnitudes of these
     effects on pfs grow modestly with feature_078.

4. Demographics (feature_064 race, feature_018 insurance) show no main
   effects on pfs (p=0.48 and p=0.22), no joint effect after adjustment
   (p=0.70 and p=0.65), no race- or insurance-based heterogeneity in the
   feature_051 effect (p=0.27 and p=0.11), and no race- or insurance-based
   utilization disparities for the six most influential clinical features
   (all chi-square p > 0.12). On every dimension tested, the dataset
   appears demographically balanced with respect to outcome and exposure.

5. feature_038 represents a robust positive signal: roughly +0.94 to +1.00
   months across all subgroups (every level of feature_057, every age
   stratum, every race, every insurance category), with no significant
   modification by feature_078, feature_051, race, or insurance. It looks
   like a candidate beneficial treatment or biomarker that performs
   uniformly well in this population.

6. Conversely, feature_051 is a robust negative signal (~ -1.35 months)
   that is uniform across demographic subgroups but is amplified by age
   (feature_078) and accompanied by an ordinal severity (feature_057),
   consistent with a marker of more advanced or aggressive disease rather
   than a protected/preferential treatment.
