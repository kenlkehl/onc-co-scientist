ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_crc (n = 50,000; outcome: pfs_months)
================================================================

Approach
--------
The dataset contains 50,000 patient records with 124 de-identified
features (80 binary, 6 multinary, 35 continuous, 3 categorical
strings: patient_id, feature_064, feature_018) and one outcome,
pfs_months (progression-free survival in months; mean 4.31, std 2.29,
range 0-16.25). I worked through 25 iterations beginning with a
univariate screen of every feature, then layered multivariable
regression, planned interactions, subgroup analyses, and a search
across all binary features for treatment-effect heterogeneity.


Strongest prognostic main effects (Iter 1-7, 10, 23)
-----------------------------------------------------
feature_078 (continuous, mean 65, range 30-90) was by far the
single strongest predictor: Pearson r = 0.766 (p ~ 0); per-unit
adjusted slope +0.176 mo per unit. PFS rises monotonically from
1.86 mo in the lowest quintile to 6.83 mo in the highest. The
range, scale, and sign together strongly suggest feature_078
indexes a continuous physiologic measure aligned with health
reserve (it appears more like a fitness/laboratory composite
than chronological age, since older age is usually a negative
predictor in CRC; nevertheless the column-level claim of a
positive linear association is well supported regardless of
mechanism).

feature_057 (3-level ordinal) showed a strong dose-response:
mean PFS = 5.27, 4.05, 2.91 mo across levels 0, 1, 2 (one-way
ANOVA F = 3528.9, p ~ 0). The ordering and magnitude are
consistent with a stage-of-disease or performance-status
indicator.

feature_051 (binary, prevalence 55%) was negatively associated
with PFS by 1.35 mo unadjusted (-1.36 mo adjusted, p ~ 0).

feature_038 (binary, prevalence 20%) was positively associated
with PFS by +0.97 mo unadjusted (+0.94 mo adjusted, p ~ 0). Its
prevalence and the size and direction of its effect are
consistent with a treatment indicator rather than a passive
prognostic factor; subsequent analyses confirmed predictive
biomarker structure around it (see below).

feature_092 (continuous, range 1.5-5.5, mean 3.8 - consistent
with a serum albumin-like marker) was positively associated
(adjusted +0.47 mo per unit, p ~ 0).

feature_099 (continuous, range 0-25, right-skewed) was negatively
associated (adjusted -0.076 mo per unit, p ~ 0); the distribution
is consistent with a tumor-burden / tumor-marker analyte.

feature_013 and feature_043 (binary; prevalences ~42% and ~35%)
were each modestly negative as main effects (-0.33 and -0.31 mo
unadjusted respectively, both p < 1e-49). Their main effects
turned out to be downstream of treatment-effect modification
(see below).

feature_109 (binary, prevalence 4.5%) was negative (adjusted
-0.36 mo, p < 1e-77).

feature_067 (binary, prevalence 3%) was nominally positive
(+0.22 mo, p ~ 7e-4) but lost significance entirely once
feature_038 was in the model (adjusted coef = +0.02, p = 0.37);
its real role is as a treatment-effect modifier.

A single multivariable model with the leading predictors
(feature_078, feature_051, feature_057, feature_038, feature_099,
feature_092, feature_013, feature_043, feature_009, feature_109,
feature_067) achieved adjusted R^2 = 0.858, indicating these few
variables carry the bulk of the explainable PFS variance. After
residualizing out this base model, only ~8 additional features
reached p < 1e-7 against the residual; the rest of the 124 features
showed no detectable adjusted association with PFS. The signal in
this dataset is therefore concentrated, not diffuse.


No demographic disparity after adjustment (Iter 8-9)
----------------------------------------------------
feature_064 (race/ethnicity: white, black, hispanic, asian, other)
showed only a 0.13 mo unadjusted spread across categories, and ALL
four contrasts had |coef| < 0.05 mo and p > 0.37 after adjustment
for feature_078, feature_051, feature_057, and feature_038. The
hypothesis of a race effect on PFS was REFUTED at the column level.

feature_018 (insurance: medicaid, medicare, private, uninsured)
similarly showed no significant adjusted association (largest
absolute contrast -0.04 mo, p > 0.11).

Neither demographic variable modified the feature_038 treatment
effect either: all feature_038 x feature_064 interaction p-values
were > 0.14 (Iter 21).


Treatment-effect heterogeneity: predictive biomarkers (Iter 11-18)
------------------------------------------------------------------
The clearest clinical story in this dataset is treatment-effect
modification of feature_038 by several binary features:

  feature_043 (resistance, prevalence ~35%):
    feature_043 = 0 -> feature_038 effect = +1.44 mo (p ~ 0)
    feature_043 = 1 -> feature_038 effect = +0.002 mo (p = 0.82)
    Interaction: -1.43 mo, p ~ 0.

  feature_013 (resistance, prevalence ~42%):
    feature_013 = 0 -> feature_038 effect = +1.62 mo (p ~ 0)
    feature_013 = 1 -> feature_038 effect = -0.008 mo (p = 0.30)
    Interaction: -1.62 mo, p ~ 0.

  feature_109 (resistance, prevalence ~4.5%):
    feature_109 = 0 -> feature_038 effect = +0.98 mo (p ~ 0)
    feature_109 = 1 -> feature_038 effect = +0.006 mo (p = 0.78)
    Interaction: -0.97 mo, p ~ 6e-80.

  feature_067 (sensitizer, prevalence ~3%):
    feature_067 = 0 -> feature_038 effect = +0.91 mo (p ~ 0)
    feature_067 = 1 -> feature_038 effect = +1.82 mo (p ~ 4e-109)
    Interaction: +0.91 mo, p ~ 1e-39.

Hypotheses h15, h16, h17, h18 were all SUPPORTED with very
large effect sizes and decisive p-values. The pattern is
unambiguous: feature_043, feature_013, and feature_109 each
abolish the feature_038 PFS benefit when present, whereas
feature_067 roughly doubles it.

Joint biomarker (Iter 16): defining any_resistance =
feature_043=1 OR feature_013=1 OR feature_109=1 (~65% of cohort)
yields adjusted feature_038 effect = -0.003 mo (p = 0.65) in the
positive group vs. +2.71 mo (p ~ 0) in the negative group. The
cohort splits cleanly into responders and non-responders by these
three markers.

Iter 17: feature_043 and feature_013 are statistically independent
(chi^2 p = 0.60); the joint 2x2 stratification shows that EITHER
marker alone is sufficient to abolish benefit (cells where exactly
one is positive both have ~0 mo feature_038 effect).

Iter 18: feature_109 and feature_013 are MUTUALLY EXCLUSIVE in
this cohort (zero co-occurring patients vs. ~948 expected under
independence; p ~ 0). This is consistent with their representing
mutually exclusive molecular alterations.


Severity / age-effect modulation (Iter 11, 19, 22)
--------------------------------------------------
A modest negative interaction feature_038 x feature_057 was
detected (-0.066, p = 0.006 at level 1; -0.112, p = 0.001 at
level 2): the feature_038 PFS gain shrinks slightly with worse
feature_057. Stratified estimates of the feature_038 effect were
0.99, 0.92, 0.88 mo at feature_057 = 0, 1, 2.

A small but significant feature_078 x feature_051 interaction
(-0.0070, p ~ 2e-15) means the per-unit feature_078 PFS gain
is slightly attenuated in feature_051=1 patients (+0.173 vs
+0.180 mo per unit).

A three-way feature_038 x feature_051 x feature_057 interaction
was significant only between feature_057 levels 0 and 1 (+0.172,
p ~ 3e-4); the corresponding contrast at level 2 was non-
significant. The two-way feature_038 x feature_051 alone is
NOT significant (p = 0.19).

A small feature_038 x feature_092 interaction was detected
(-0.091, p ~ 2e-5; feature_038 effect 0.98 vs 0.89 mo across
median split of feature_092). Direction: a higher value of the
albumin-like feature_092 modestly reduces incremental treatment
benefit, possibly because the absolute prognosis is already
better.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. The dataset contains a clinically intelligible structure
   centered on one likely treatment indicator (feature_038) with
   roughly 1 month adjusted PFS benefit on average.

2. That benefit is profoundly heterogeneous and is governed
   chiefly by three resistance markers (feature_043, feature_013,
   feature_109) and one sensitizer (feature_067). Roughly 35% of
   patients (resistance-marker negative) gain ~2.7 mo from
   feature_038; the remaining 65% gain essentially nothing. This
   is the textbook predictive-biomarker pattern.

3. Prognostic backbone (independent of feature_038): feature_078
   (large positive), feature_057 (large negative dose-response),
   feature_051 (large negative), feature_092 (positive),
   feature_099 (negative), with smaller contributions from
   feature_009 and feature_006.

4. Demographic variables in this dataset (feature_064 race,
   feature_018 insurance) show no PFS effect after adjustment
   for clinical/biomarker variables, and they do not modify the
   feature_038 treatment effect.

5. Of 124 features tested, the explanatory signal concentrates
   in roughly 10-12 columns; the multivariable model built from
   them captures ~86% of PFS variance. The remaining columns
   appear largely uninformative for this outcome in this cohort.

6. Three-way and multi-feature interactions exist (e.g.,
   feature_038 x feature_051 x feature_057) but are smaller in
   magnitude than the dominant two-way treatment-by-biomarker
   effects, which should be the primary subgroup story for any
   downstream clinical interpretation.
