Analysis summary — ds001_crc (50,000 patients; outcome pfs_months)
================================================================

Outcome distribution
--------------------
pfs_months ranges 0 to 16.25 with mean 4.31 and median 4.21. The dataset has 123
de-identified patient features (80 binaries, 7 ordinal integers, 35 continuous
numerics, 2 demographic categoricals — feature_064 race, feature_018 insurance —
plus patient_id). No values are missing.

Strategy
--------
I screened every feature univariately against pfs_months, prioritized the
strongest signals, then probed pairwise and three-way interactions to look for
treatment–biomarker effect modification, and finally fit a consolidated
multivariable OLS model. 25 iterations were used.

Iterations 1-3 — demographic / context
--------------------------------------
* feature_078 (continuous, range 30-90, mean 65; plausibly age) had the largest
  univariate signal of any continuous feature, Pearson r = +0.77, p ≈ 0. In an
  OLS frame each unit of feature_078 ≈ +0.18 months PFS. Hypothesis SUPPORTED.
* feature_064 (race) showed group means within ~0.13 months of each other and
  the ANOVA was not significant. Hypothesis REFUTED.
* feature_018 (insurance) similarly showed no significant raw difference in PFS.
  Hypothesis REFUTED.

Iterations 4-12 — single-feature main effects
---------------------------------------------
* feature_051 (binary, prevalence 0.55): mean PFS 3.50 vs 4.85 months
  (Δ = -1.35, p ≈ 0). Largest binary effect in the dataset. Hypothesis
  SUPPORTED — feature_051 = 1 carries a large adverse signal.
* feature_038 (binary, prevalence 0.20): +0.97 months (p ≈ 1.7e-216) overall.
  Hypothesis SUPPORTED — feature_038 = 1 is associated with longer PFS, the
  pattern expected of an effective intervention.
* feature_057 (ordinal, levels 0/1/2): Spearman ρ = -0.36, p ≈ 0; group means
  4.94 / 3.82 / 2.91 months — a clean monotone decrease ≈ 1.17 months/level.
  Hypothesis SUPPORTED.
* feature_013 (binary, prevalence 0.42): -0.33 months (p ≈ 1.9e-58).
  feature_043 (binary, prevalence 0.35): -0.31 months (p ≈ 1.0e-49). Both
  modestly adverse. Hypotheses SUPPORTED.
* feature_092 (continuous, 1.5-5.5): r = +0.10, p ≈ 3e-115; feature_099
  (continuous): r = -0.12, p ≈ 2e-167; feature_009: r = -0.04, p ≈ 3e-17.
  Hypotheses SUPPORTED — feature_092 is favorable, feature_099 and feature_009
  are unfavorable.
* feature_109 (rare binary, prevalence 0.05): -0.23 months, p ≈ 3e-7. Hypothesis
  SUPPORTED.
* feature_067 (rare binary, prevalence 0.03): +0.22 months, p ≈ 7e-4. Hypothesis
  SUPPORTED.

Iterations 13-17 — treatment x biomarker interactions for feature_038
---------------------------------------------------------------------
The univariate +0.97 month effect of feature_038 obscures very strong subgroup
heterogeneity. Three binary biomarkers each abrogate the feature_038 benefit:

  feature_038 effect, stratified
  ------------------------------
  feature_013 = 0  → +1.66 months (p ≈ 6e-293)
  feature_013 = 1  → +0.03 months (p = 0.46)        interaction p = 7.7e-226
  feature_043 = 0  → +1.48 months (p ≈ 4e-274)
  feature_043 = 1  → +0.03 months (p = 0.52)        interaction p = 4.1e-168
  feature_109 = 0  → +1.03 months (p ≈ 5e-226)
  feature_109 = 1  → -0.16 months (p = 0.13)        interaction p = 6.1e-23

Combining the three: the "triple-negative" wild-type subgroup
(feature_013 = 0 AND feature_043 = 0 AND feature_109 = 0) contains 17,372
patients (~35%). Within this subgroup, feature_038 produces +2.76 months PFS
(6.88 vs 4.12 months, p ≈ 0). Outside it, feature_038 produces only
+0.03 months (p = 0.16). Hypothesis SUPPORTED.

A fourth candidate, feature_067, appeared to enhance the feature_038 effect
univariately (+1.93 vs +0.94 months, interaction p ≈ 1.2e-11). After conditioning
on the wild-type composite the modification disappears (feature_067 = 0 → +2.75
months, feature_067 = 1 → +2.91 months). The univariate enhancement was
confounded by feature_067 over-representing the wild-type state. Hypothesis
REFINED — the apparent positive predictor is not a true effect modifier.

Iterations 18-20 — feature_051 interactions
-------------------------------------------
* feature_051 × feature_078 (age-like): interaction β = -0.0067 per unit, p ≈
  2e-9. Statistically detectable but small — the deficit attributed to
  feature_051 is roughly -1.28 months at low feature_078 and -1.39 months at
  high feature_078. Hypothesis SUPPORTED but clinically modest.
* feature_051 × feature_057: interaction β = +0.063, p = 0.019. Across the three
  feature_057 strata the feature_051 deficit is -1.40 / -1.35 / -1.27 months —
  detectable but minor. Hypothesis WEAKLY SUPPORTED.
* feature_051 × feature_038: interaction β = +0.07, p = 0.15 (not significant).
  Hypothesis SUPPORTED — these two effects appear additive and independent.

Iterations 21-23 — robustness across demographics and continuous biomarkers
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Within the triple-negative wild-type subgroup, the feature_038 benefit is
remarkably consistent:
  by feature_064 race → white +2.63, black +2.79, hispanic +3.21, asian +2.88,
                       other +3.07 (all p < 1e-10).
  by feature_018 insurance → medicare +2.80, private +2.60, medicaid +3.09,
                             uninsured +2.75 (all p < 1e-15).
No evidence of disparate treatment benefit by race or insurance once the
biomarker subgroup is held fixed. Hypotheses SUPPORTED.

feature_092 × feature_038 interaction: β = -0.077 per unit, p = 0.12 (not
significant). feature_092 contributes additively, not as an effect modifier.
Hypothesis REFUTED.

Iteration 24 — consolidated multivariable model
-----------------------------------------------
OLS for pfs_months on feature_051, feature_078, feature_057, feature_038,
feature_013, feature_043, feature_109, feature_067, feature_092, feature_099,
plus interactions feature_038*feature_013, feature_038*feature_043,
feature_038*feature_109, feature_051*feature_078:

  R² = 0.896
  feature_051     -0.918  (p ≈ 0)
  feature_078     +0.180/unit (p ≈ 0)
  feature_057     -1.166/level (p ≈ 0)
  feature_038     +2.285  (p ≈ 0; this is the wild-type-subgroup effect)
  feature_013     +0.000  (p = 0.90)
  feature_043     +0.003  (p = 0.68)
  feature_109     +0.003  (p = 0.85)
  feature_067     -0.003  (p = 0.89)
  feature_092     +0.473/unit (p ≈ 0)
  feature_099     -0.077/unit (p ≈ 0)
  f038 × f013     -1.782   (p ≈ 0)
  f038 × f043     -1.471   (p ≈ 0)
  f038 × f109     -1.766   (p ≈ 0)
  f051 × age      -0.007   (p ≈ 7e-24)

After accounting for the interactions, the main effects of feature_013,
feature_043, feature_109, and feature_067 collapse to ≈0 — these features only
operate as effect modifiers of feature_038, not as independent prognostic
markers. This is the signature of a "predictive biomarker," not a "prognostic
biomarker."

Iteration 25 — therapeutic-window refinement
--------------------------------------------
Within the wild-type subgroup, splitting by median feature_078 shows the
feature_038 benefit is +2.32 months in the lower half and +2.84 months in the
upper half (both p < 1e-100). Older feature_078 patients in the wild-type
subgroup derive at least as much benefit as younger ones — there is no signal
to age-restrict eligibility for feature_038.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. Strong, independent prognostic factors for pfs_months: feature_051 (-1.35),
   feature_057 (-1.17/level), feature_092 (+0.47/unit), feature_099 (-0.08/unit),
   feature_078 (+0.18/unit). These act roughly additively.
2. feature_038 functions like a targeted intervention: a +2.76-month PFS
   benefit but only in patients who are simultaneously feature_013 = 0,
   feature_043 = 0, and feature_109 = 0. Patients positive for any of those
   three markers should not be expected to derive any benefit (effect 0.03
   months, NS). The pattern is highly reminiscent of EGFR-pathway therapy
   in colorectal cancer where activating mutations downstream of EGFR
   eliminate the response.
3. feature_067, which superficially looked like a positive treatment-effect
   modifier, is in fact a passive marker of the wild-type state — once
   conditioned on feature_013/043/109 status, it contributes nothing
   independently.
4. The feature_038 benefit is consistent across feature_064 (race) and
   feature_018 (insurance) categories within the wild-type subgroup, providing
   no evidence of differential treatment efficacy along sociodemographic axes.
5. The combined multivariable model explains roughly 90% of the variance in
   pfs_months (R² = 0.896), suggesting these few features capture nearly all
   of the systematic structure in this outcome and that further hypothesis
   refinement (additional covariates, three-way interactions) would yield
   diminishing returns.
