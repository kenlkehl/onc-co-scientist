Analysis summary — ds001_crc (50,000 patients, outcome pfs_months)

Headline finding
================

In this cohort, progression-free survival (pfs_months) is overwhelmingly
governed by a compact set of prognostic factors plus one striking
treatment-by-tumor-biology interaction:

  treatment_regorafenib confers a large PFS benefit, but ONLY in
  left-sided (right_sided_primary = 0) tumors that are wild-type for
  KRAS, NRAS, and BRAF V600E.

In that subgroup (n=3,264 regorafenib-treated of 16,443 left-sided
RAS/RAF-wildtype patients), mean PFS is 6.87 months on regorafenib
versus 4.12 months off — an absolute benefit of about +2.7 months.
In every other side x molecular stratum the regorafenib effect is
near zero. The three-way interaction
treatment_regorafenib x left_sided x ras_raf_wildtype is +2.55 with
p < 1e-300 (R^2 of the 3-way model = 0.906).

This is the inverse of the textbook profile for cetuximab (which is
the agent typically given preferentially in left-sided RAS/RAF-WT
disease). In this dataset, cetuximab itself has no effect — neither
overall nor in the canonical eligible subgroup.

Iteration-by-iteration narrative
================================

Iter 1 — Canonical biomarker-targeted treatments. I tested four classical
CRC biomarker-targeted drug pairings as interactions:
  * cetuximab x RAS/RAF-wildtype: interaction p=0.79 (refuted).
  * pembrolizumab x msi_high:    interaction p=0.99 (refuted).
  * encorafenib x braf_v600e:    interaction p=0.38, point estimate
    in the wrong direction (refuted).
  * trast/tucatinib x her2_amplified: interaction p=0.78 (refuted).
None of the textbook biomarker-targeted treatment effects materialized.

Iter 2 — Major prognostic clinical features.
  * ecog_ps:   adjusted -1.18 months per ECOG point (p~0).
  * stage_iv:  adjusted -1.36 months (p~0).
  * albumin_g_dl: adjusted +0.46 months per g/dL (p~0).
  * weight_loss_pct_6mo: adjusted -0.076 months per percentage point
    of 6-month weight loss (p~0).
All four supported with very strong magnitude.

Iter 3 — Tumor side and core mutations.
  * right_sided_primary: -0.31 months (p≈5e-46), supported.
  * kras_mutation:       -0.33 months (p≈9e-56), supported.
  * braf_v600e:          -0.23 months univariate, -0.36 adjusted, supported.
  * nras_mutation:       +0.22 univariate (p≈2e-4); attenuates to null
    after adjustment for KRAS/BRAF/ECOG/stage. The univariate signal is
    confounded — NRAS mutants here are usually KRAS-WT/BRAF-WT.

Iter 4 — Other prognostic continuous features.
  * age_years: +0.18 months per year, robust to adjustment (p~0).
    The positive direction is opposite to typical clinical intuition
    (older patients in this cohort have LONGER PFS) but is large and
    reproducible.
  * cea_ng_ml: -0.005 per ng/mL (p≈3e-17), supported.
  * ldh_u_l:   -0.0004 per U/L (p≈1e-15), supported.

Iter 5 — Main effects of all six treatments. In a single multivariable
model adjusting for prognostic features (R^2 = 0.86), the only treatment
with a substantial main effect was treatment_regorafenib:
  * regorafenib: +0.94 months (p~0).
  * cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, trast/tucatinib:
    all null (|coef|<0.03, p>0.06).

Iter 6 — Heterogeneity of the regorafenib effect.
  * regorafenib x kras_mutation:       interaction -1.63 (p~0). KRAS-WT
    benefit +1.66 months; KRAS-mutant +0.03.
  * regorafenib x braf_v600e:          interaction -0.99 (p≈5e-104).
    BRAF-WT benefit +1.03; BRAF-V600E -0.16.
  * regorafenib x right_sided_primary: interaction -1.46 (p~0).
    Left-sided benefit +1.48; right-sided +0.03.
All three supported; regorafenib benefit is concentrated in the
left-sided / RAS-WT / BRAF-WT slice.

Iter 7 — Combining the modifiers. The 3-way interaction
regorafenib x left_sided x ras_raf_wildtype = +2.55 (p<1e-300).
Stratified within-cell PFS deltas:
  * left/WT:   +2.75 months
  * left/mut:  +0.21
  * right/WT:  +0.07
  * right/mut: -0.02
The clinically meaningful regorafenib benefit is essentially confined
to the left-sided RAS/RAF-WT cell.

Iter 8 — Other treatments WITHIN the left-sided RAS/RAF-WT cell. Even
inside this most-favorable subgroup, regorafenib is the only treatment
with a substantive effect (+2.73, p<1e-300); cetuximab is actually
slightly negative (-0.03, p=0.10); bevacizumab, pembrolizumab,
encorafenib, and trast/tucatinib are essentially null. So the
regorafenib effect is not just contrast against an unrelated
comparator — it is unique to that drug.

Iter 9 — Other treatment-biomarker interactions. After adjustment:
  * bevacizumab x msi_high:                +0.07 (p=0.08, marginal)
  * encorafenib x nras_mutation:           -0.15 (p=0.08, marginal)
  * trast/tucatinib x kras_mutation:       -0.06 (p=0.08, marginal)
None robustly supported.

Iter 10 — Demographics. Race/ethnicity, insurance type, rural residence,
and sex_female: all null associations with pfs_months (omnibus p > 0.30
for race/insurance; |coef|<0.03 with p>0.13 for rural and sex). Refuted.

Iter 11 — Comorbidities. None of 15 comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension,
COPD, CKD, heart failure, CAD, AF, VTE, autoimmune, hepatitis B/C, HIV,
prior malignancy, ILD) shows a significant univariate association.
Depression/anxiety borderline (-0.05, p=0.053). Refuted.

Iter 12 — Lab values. Of 24 labs screened, only alkaline phosphatase
(-0.0006 per U/L, p=0.004) and total bilirubin (-0.065 per mg/dL,
p=0.044) showed univariate signal. Hemoglobin and calcium were
borderline; the remaining labs were null. Partial support.

Iter 13 — SNPs. 3 of 28 SNPs (rs1801131, rs1050828, rs1801197) crossed
p<0.05 univariately — close to chance under multiple testing
(28 * 0.05 ≈ 1.4 expected). After adjustment for the major prognostic
factors, those particular SNPs no longer reach significance, while
three different SNPs (rs1045642, rs1800629, rs2070744) reach borderline
significance with very small effect sizes (|coef|<0.03). No SNP shows
a robust, large, replicated association. Effectively refuted.

Iter 14 — Metastatic patterns. Only pericardial effusion (-0.05, p=0.03)
showed an adjusted negative effect; bone mets had a small positive
adjusted effect (+0.024, p=0.03), opposite to expectation. Liver mets,
adrenal mets, and pleural effusion were null. Largely refuted.

Iter 15 — Interactions among prognostic factors.
  * stage_iv x ecog_ps interaction = +0.05 (p<1e-4): direction OPPOSITE
    to expected synergy; modest sub-additivity (each marker already
    captures severe disease).
  * albumin x weight_loss interaction: null (p=0.63). Effects are
    additive.
  * age x regorafenib: null (p=0.28).

Iter 16 — Effect modification of regorafenib.
  * regorafenib x ecog_ps: -0.05 per ECOG point (p=0.0004). Small
    attenuation of regorafenib benefit at higher ECOG.
  * bevacizumab x right_sided_primary: null (p=0.65).

Iter 17 — Other biomarker main effects.
  * msi_high:        coef -0.02 (p=0.68), refuted.
  * her2_amplified:  coef -0.06 (p=0.35), refuted.
  * ntrk_fusion:     coef +0.04 (p=0.78), refuted.
The standard "MSI-H is favorable" intuition does not hold; in this
cohort MSI status is essentially neutral.

Iter 18 — Prior treatment history and symptom burden.
prior_lines_of_therapy, years_since_diagnosis, fatigue_grade, pain_nrs:
all null. Refuted.

Iter 19 — Lifestyle/SES. Smoking pack-years and education years: both
null. Refuted.

Iter 20 — Within left-sided RAS/RAF-WT subset, the regorafenib benefit
of +2.73 months is preserved across stage and ECOG strata. The only
modest modifier is ECOG (interaction -0.05/point), too small to be
clinically actionable. Supported.

Iter 21 — regorafenib x nras_mutation. NRAS-mutated subgroup
(n=1,506) shows an even larger regorafenib effect (+1.93 vs +0.94 in
NRAS-WT) with adjusted interaction +0.89 (p≈1.5e-57). Almost all NRAS
mutants in this dataset are KRAS-WT and BRAF-WT, placing them inside
the broader RAS/RAF-WT regorafenib-responsive class — so this is
consistent with, not separate from, the main 3-way interaction.

Iter 22 — Inflammation markers (crp_mg_l, nlr): both null after
adjustment. Refuted.

Iter 23 — Prior chemotherapy/radiation/surgery/immunotherapy/targeted
therapy: none predicts subsequent PFS. Refuted.

Iter 24 — Sex- and age-based modification of regorafenib effect: null in
both cases. The regorafenib benefit applies uniformly across these
demographic strata.

Iter 25 — Synthesis model. A linear model containing age_years, ecog_ps,
stage_iv, right_sided_primary, kras_mutation, braf_v600e, albumin_g_dl,
weight_loss_pct_6mo, cea_ng_ml, plus treatment_regorafenib and the
3-way regorafenib x left_sided x ras_raf_WT interaction explains 90.6%
of pfs_months variance.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
===============================

Supported (positive direction matches data):
  * H5  ECOG_PS shortens PFS (-1.18/pt)
  * H6  stage_iv shortens PFS (-1.36)
  * H7  albumin lengthens PFS (+0.46/g/dL)
  * H8  weight loss shortens PFS (-0.076/pp)
  * H9  right-sided primary shortens PFS (-0.30)
  * H10 KRAS mutation shortens PFS (-0.33)
  * H11 BRAF V600E shortens PFS (-0.36 adjusted)
  * H12 NRAS positive univariate (but confounded)
  * H13 Older age associated with LONGER PFS in this cohort (+0.18/yr)
  * H14 Higher CEA shortens PFS (small)
  * H15 Higher LDH shortens PFS (small)
  * H16 treatment_regorafenib improves PFS overall (+0.94)
  * H17/H18 cetuximab and bevacizumab have null main effects
  * H19/H20/H21 regorafenib benefit concentrated in KRAS-WT, BRAF-WT,
    left-sided
  * H22/H23/H49 regorafenib benefit is essentially confined to
    left-sided RAS/RAF-WT and is not shared by other treatments in
    that subgroup
  * H32 alkaline phosphatase shortens PFS (small)
  * H39 small attenuation of regorafenib benefit at higher ECOG
  * H50 regorafenib x NRAS positive (consistent with H22)
  * H56 the synthesis hypothesis

Refuted:
  * H1  cetuximab x RAS/RAF-WT (no anti-EGFR-like effect found)
  * H2  pembrolizumab x MSI-high (no IO benefit found)
  * H3  encorafenib x BRAF-V600E
  * H4  trast/tucatinib x HER2-amplified
  * H24/H25/H26 marginal interactions (not robust)
  * H27/H28/H29/H30 race, insurance, rural residence, sex
  * H31 comorbidities
  * H35 specific metastatic patterns
  * H36 ECOG x stage synergy (direction was opposite)
  * H37 albumin x weight loss synergy
  * H38/H55 age x regorafenib
  * H40 bevacizumab x right-sided
  * H41/H42/H43 MSI-high, HER2-amplified, NTRK-fusion main effects
  * H44/H45 prior therapy lines, years since diagnosis
  * H46 fatigue/pain
  * H47/H48 smoking, education
  * H51/H52 CRP, NLR
  * H53 prior chemo/RT/surgery/IO/targeted

Overall conclusions
===================

1) Pfs_months in this colorectal cohort is dominated by classic
   prognostic features: ECOG performance status, stage IV, albumin,
   weight loss, sided-ness, and KRAS/BRAF status — directionally
   consistent with prior literature, except that **older age tracks
   with LONGER PFS** here, which is unusual and worth flagging.

2) The defining treatment effect is regorafenib's benefit, which is
   essentially confined to the left-sided, RAS/RAF wild-type subgroup
   (~+2.7 months). This is a dramatic finding because:
     - Regorafenib is normally an "agnostic" later-line agent in CRC.
     - The left-sided RAS/RAF-WT slice is the canonical anti-EGFR
       (cetuximab) target population.
     - In this dataset, cetuximab has no effect there, and regorafenib
       takes its place.

3) The other "signature" CRC biomarker-targeted regimens
   (pembrolizumab in MSI-H, encorafenib in BRAF V600E, trastuzumab/
   tucatinib in HER2-amplified, cetuximab in RAS-WT) all show null
   effects in this cohort — neither in main effect nor in the
   biomarker-defined subgroup.

4) Demographics, comorbidities, prior therapy, symptom grades,
   inflammation markers, and SNPs do not contribute meaningful
   prognostic information beyond what ECOG, stage, albumin, weight
   loss, side, KRAS, BRAF, age, CEA, and LDH already capture. The
   handful of marginal SNP hits are consistent with chance.

5) The bottom-line clinical inference, taken at face value, would be:
   in this cohort, treat left-sided RAS/RAF wild-type colorectal
   cancer with regorafenib and expect ~2.7 additional months of PFS;
   do not expect benefit from regorafenib in any other side x
   molecular slice; and do not expect benefit from cetuximab,
   pembrolizumab, encorafenib, or trastuzumab/tucatinib in their
   classical biomarker-defined eligible populations.
