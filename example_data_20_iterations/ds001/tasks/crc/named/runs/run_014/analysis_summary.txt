ds001_crc — Analysis Summary
============================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with colorectal-cancer-relevant features and progression-free
survival (pfs_months) as the sole outcome. Mean PFS 4.31 months, SD 2.29, range 0–16.25.
Treatments queried: cetuximab (30.2%), bevacizumab (44.8%), pembrolizumab (15.3%),
encorafenib (10.2%), trastuzumab/tucatinib (8.1%), regorafenib (20.0%). All analyses
were ordinary-least-squares regressions on pfs_months, with stratified t-tests for
descriptive subgroup contrasts. Bonferroni correction applied to the SNP scan.

I. Prognostic main effects (iterations 1–5)
-------------------------------------------
The dominant predictors of pfs_months are clinical-severity terms. ECOG performance
status (slope -1.190 months per unit, p<1e-300), stage_iv (-1.352 months, p<1e-300),
weight_loss_pct_6mo (-0.073 months per %, p=2e-167), and albumin_g_dl (+0.465 months
per g/dL, p=3e-115) are the four largest. CEA (-0.005/ng/mL, p=3e-17) and LDH
(-0.0004/U/L, p=0.013) are independently prognostic at smaller magnitudes.

Tumor biology contributes additional but smaller signals: kras_mutation (-0.327
months, p=2e-58), braf_v600e (-0.228, p=3e-7), right_sided_primary (-0.305, p=1e-49).
NRAS mutation (h9) showed a paradoxical +0.221-month univariate signal (p=7e-4) that
disappeared on adjustment (β=+0.030, p=0.23), consistent with confounding rather than
a true protective effect. msi_high, her2_amplified, and ntrk_fusion had no detectable
main effect on PFS (p=0.68, 0.36, 0.79).

Age (h14): direction was opposite to expectation. Older age was associated with
LONGER PFS (linear slope +0.176 months/year, p<1e-300; quadratic term also positive,
p=7e-34), and the effect persisted after adjustment. This counterintuitive result
likely reflects a synthetic-data construction or selection by fitness, not real
clinical biology. Sex showed no PFS difference (p=0.77).

II. Multivariable model (iteration 6)
-------------------------------------
A 24-covariate OLS model achieved R²=0.859. The signed coefficient ordering of
prognostic features was preserved from univariate analysis. Of the six treatments,
only regorafenib had a non-trivial coefficient (+0.943 months, t=97.9, p<<0.001).
Cetuximab β=-0.011, bevacizumab β=+0.003, pembrolizumab β=+0.0001, encorafenib
β=+0.006, trastuzumab/tucatinib β=+0.026 — all NS at α=0.05.

III. Treatment main effects and "expected" biomarker interactions (iterations 7–11)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Refuted hypotheses (one each, all in the direction predicted by standard CRC biology):
  - cetuximab × KRAS-WT (h20): no benefit in KRAS-WT (diff=-0.048, p=0.12);
    interaction p=0.27.
  - cetuximab × left-sided in triple-WT (h21): no benefit in left-sided KRAS-/NRAS-/
    BRAF-WT subgroup (diff=-0.065, p=0.11); interaction p=0.27.
  - pembrolizumab × MSI-high (h22): in MSI-high (n=2,513), pembrolizumab diff=+0.007,
    p=0.96; interaction p=0.28.
  - encorafenib × BRAF V600E (h23): in BRAF-mut (n=2,272), encorafenib diff=-0.129,
    p=0.33; interaction p=0.999.
  - trastuzumab/tucatinib × HER2-amplified (h24): in HER2-amp (n=1,504), diff=+0.017,
    p=0.93; interaction p=0.94.

In short, none of the five canonical biomarker-targeted treatment indications
reproduces in this dataset.

IV. Regorafenib subgroup heterogeneity — the principal finding (iterations 12–15, 24)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Once the biomarker-targeted hypotheses had been refuted, attention turned to
heterogeneity within the only treatment that did show a main effect: regorafenib.
A series of formal interaction tests (h25–h28) revealed that regorafenib's PFS
benefit is highly localized.

  Two-way regorafenib × kras_mutation: interaction β=-1.635, p<<0.001.
    Stratified: KRAS-WT diff=+1.656 (p=6e-293, n=29,102); KRAS-mut diff=+0.027 (p=0.46).
  Two-way regorafenib × braf_v600e: interaction β=-0.987, p=2e-102.
    Stratified: BRAF-WT diff=+1.027 (p=5e-226); BRAF-mut diff=-0.156 (p=0.13).
  Two-way regorafenib × right_sided_primary: interaction β=-1.461, p<<0.001.
    Stratified: left-sided diff=+1.480 (p=4e-274); right-sided diff=+0.026 (p=0.52).

The three-way regorafenib × KRAS × right_sided interaction is significant (β=+2.510,
p<<0.001), and the cell-wise estimates are clean:
    KRAS=0 & left-sided     diff = +2.520  p<<0.001  n=18,853
    KRAS=0 & right-sided    diff = +0.071  p=0.16
    KRAS=1 & left-sided     diff = +0.061  p=0.17
    KRAS=1 & right-sided    diff = -0.040  p=0.52

Restricting further to triple-WT (KRAS-/NRAS-/BRAF-WT) AND left-sided (n=16,443),
the unadjusted regorafenib PFS gain is +2.749 months. Within KRAS-WT/left-sided,
fully adjusted for age, sex, ECOG, stage, albumin, weight loss, CEA, LDH, all other
biomarkers, and the five other treatments, regorafenib β=+2.516 months, p<<0.001,
R²=0.818.

This is the cohort's standout result and is biologically counterintuitive: regorafenib
in real practice is a multikinase salvage TKI used regardless of biomarker status, yet
in this dataset its benefit pattern matches the eligibility profile of anti-EGFR
therapy (cetuximab/panitumumab) — which itself shows no benefit. The pattern is
consistent with a synthetically constructed treatment-effect rule.

V. Demographic and access disparities (iterations 16–18)
--------------------------------------------------------
Refuted: race ANOVA p=0.48, all adjusted race coefficients |β|<0.02 with p>0.5;
insurance ANOVA p=0.22, adjusted coefficients NS; rural residence p=0.34;
education years slope +0.0033/year, p=0.34; sex difference -0.006 months, p=0.77.
No demographic disparity in PFS.

Treatment-receipt access disparities also refuted: 30 chi-square tests of
biomarker × treatment receipt, only bevacizumab × BRAF V600E nominally significant
(p=0.040, 42.6% vs 44.9% — within multiple-testing noise). Treatment-by-insurance
chi-square p-values 0.41–0.90; treatment-by-race p-values 0.078–0.95. Treatment
receipt rates are within ±2 percentage points across all demographic and biomarker
strata, indicating that treatments behave as if randomized.

VI. Pharmacogenomic SNP scan (iteration 19)
-------------------------------------------
None of the 27 SNPs reached the Bonferroni threshold of p=1.85e-3. Best signals were
snp_rs1050828 (diff=+0.092, p=0.017), snp_rs1801131 (-0.049, p=0.027), and
snp_rs1801197 (-0.049, p=0.046) — none survive correction.

VII. Symptom and comorbidity adjustment (iterations 20–21)
----------------------------------------------------------
Symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite loss) carry no independent
PFS information once ECOG, stage, albumin, and weight loss are in the model
(all |β|<0.005, p>0.24). Of nine comorbidities tested, only COPD reaches nominal
significance (β=-0.027, p=0.0045) — a small effect that should not be over-interpreted
given multiple comparisons.

VIII. Other interactions (iterations 22–23)
-------------------------------------------
Bevacizumab × BRAF V600E (h38): nominally significant in raw stratified analysis
(BRAF-mut bev diff=-0.237, p=0.006) but does not survive multivariable adjustment
(interaction β=-0.032, p=0.43); confounding by KRAS/sidedness is the most likely
explanation.

Age × ECOG interaction (h39): significant (β=-0.0069, p=1.2e-26) but in the
direction OPPOSITE to the "older patients pre-selected for fitness" hypothesis —
each additional year of age makes the per-unit ECOG penalty slightly more negative.

ECOG × stage IV interaction (h40): small positive interaction (β=+0.049, p=1e-4)
indicating that the ECOG penalty is slightly less steep in stage-IV patients — likely
a floor effect (PFS already lower in stage IV).

IX. Overall conclusions
-----------------------
1. The four dominant predictors of pfs_months are ECOG (-1.17 / unit), stage IV
   (-1.36), albumin (+0.47 / g/dL), and weight loss (-0.076 / %). KRAS, BRAF, CEA,
   LDH, and right-sidedness contribute additional smaller decrements. Age has a
   counterintuitive POSITIVE association with PFS that persists on adjustment.
2. Five of the six treatments — cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib,
   trastuzumab/tucatinib — show no main or biomarker-defined interaction effect on
   PFS, refuting the canonical CRC biomarker-targeted indications.
3. Regorafenib is the only treatment with a meaningful PFS effect (+0.94 months
   adjusted overall), and that effect is highly localized. Within the KRAS-WT,
   BRAF-WT, left-sided subgroup it confers ~+2.5 months PFS; in any other cell the
   effect is essentially zero. Three-way and stratified analyses converge on this
   conclusion.
4. The treatment-effect pattern is biologically counterintuitive: regorafenib's
   "indication" in this cohort matches the anti-EGFR-eligible subgroup, while
   cetuximab itself does nothing. Together with the absence of any race/insurance/
   rural disparity in either PFS or treatment receipt, and the apparent random
   assignment of treatments with respect to biomarkers, the data structure is
   consistent with a synthetic / simulated cohort built around one programmed
   treatment-effect rule (regorafenib helps when KRAS-WT, BRAF-WT, and left-sided)
   rather than a real-world biomarker-driven oncology population.
5. There is no detectable pharmacogenomic SNP signal at Bonferroni-corrected
   significance, and individual symptom grades carry no independent prognostic
   information beyond standard severity covariates.
