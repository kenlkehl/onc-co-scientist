ds001_crc — Analysis Summary
============================

Cohort: 50,000 patients labeled as colorectal cancer (CRC), with mutation status,
treatment exposures, lab values, comorbidities, demographics, 27 SNPs, and PFS in months.
Outcome of interest: pfs_months (mean 4.31, median 4.21, range 0–16.25).

The exploration ran 14 iterations covering treatment main effects, treatment x
biomarker interactions, prognostic biomarkers, prognostic continuous features,
demographic disparities, comorbidity effects, SNP effects, multivariable models,
and three-way subgroup interactions. The headline findings are below.


1. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS (iteration 1)
---------------------------------------
Of the six labeled treatments, only treatment_regorafenib had a notable
unadjusted association with pfs_months: 5.09 mo on regorafenib vs 4.12 mo off
(diff = +0.97 mo, p < 1e-300). The remaining five treatments — cetuximab,
bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, trastuzumab/tucatinib — each had
absolute mean differences smaller than 0.05 mo and p-values >= 0.09 (all
non-significant). Hypothesis "regorafenib improves PFS" supported; main-effect
hypotheses for the other five treatments refuted.


2. EXPECTED BIOMARKER X TREATMENT INTERACTIONS ARE ABSENT (iterations 2, 11)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------
The hallmark CRC pharmacogenomic interactions did NOT appear:
  - cetuximab x kras_mutation: interaction beta = +0.020, p = 0.67
  - cetuximab x nras_mutation: beta = -0.007, p = 0.96
  - cetuximab x braf_v600e:    beta = -0.026, p = 0.81
  - cetuximab x right_sided:   beta = +0.026, p = 0.58
  - pembrolizumab x msi_high:  beta = +0.002, p = 0.99
  - encorafenib x braf_v600e:  beta = -0.141, p = 0.38
  - trastuzumab/tucatinib x her2_amplified: beta = +0.063, p = 0.78

To rule out residual confounding, restricted subgroup analyses were also run:
  - In left-sided + RAS WT patients (the canonical cetuximab-favorable subset,
    n = 17,924), cetuximab still showed a -0.062 mo (p = 0.14) effect.
  - Within braf_v600e == 1 (n = 2,272), encorafenib effect = +0.002 mo (p = 0.94).
  - Within msi_high == 1 (n = 2,513), pembrolizumab effect = +0.034 mo (p = 0.54).

In short, none of the well-established targeted-treatment-by-biomarker pairings
that drive real-world CRC therapy show any signal in this dataset.


3. REGORAFENIB BENEFIT IS HIGHLY LOCALIZED (iterations 2, 3, 4, 13)
-------------------------------------------------------------------
The unadjusted regorafenib benefit is driven almost entirely by a single
biomarker x sidedness cell:

  Adjusted regression with three-way interaction
  treatment_regorafenib * kras_mutation * right_sided_primary
  (controlling for age_years, ecog_ps, stage_iv):
    treatment_regorafenib                                   +2.52 mo  p<1e-300
    treatment_regorafenib:kras_mutation                     -2.50      p<1e-300
    treatment_regorafenib:right_sided_primary               -2.49      p<1e-300
    treatment_regorafenib:kras_mutation:right_sided_primary +2.49      p<1e-300

  Crude subgroup means:
    Left + KRAS WT  + regorafenib: 6.64 mo (n=3,763) vs 4.12 off (n=15,090)
    Left + KRAS mut + regorafenib: 4.19 vs 4.12 off
    Right + KRAS WT + regorafenib: 4.19 vs 4.11 off
    Right + KRAS mut + regorafenib: 4.06 vs 4.10 off

In other words, regorafenib produces a +2.5 mo PFS benefit ONLY in left-sided
KRAS-WT patients; it has no detectable effect in any of the other three cells.
The benefit is also abolished by braf_v600e mutation
(treatment_regorafenib:braf_v600e beta = -1.75, p = 5e-285).
Stratification by race_ethnicity, sex_female, and stage_iv showed no further
modification of this regorafenib effect (all interaction p > 0.15). The benefit
is not explained by SNPs (see section 7).

This pattern is highly atypical clinically — regorafenib (a multikinase
inhibitor) is not biomarker-selected in routine practice — but it is the
dominant treatment-outcome signal in this cohort.


4. PROGNOSTIC BIOMARKERS (iteration 5, 13)
------------------------------------------
Adjusted independent associations with pfs_months (full multivariable OLS):
  stage_iv:               -1.36 mo (p<1e-300)   [strong negative]
  ecog_ps:                -1.17 mo per unit     [strong negative]
  kras_mutation:          -0.35 mo              [moderate negative]
  braf_v600e:             -0.36 mo              [moderate negative]
  right_sided_primary:    -0.29 mo              [moderate negative]
  albumin_g_dl:           +0.47 mo per g/dL     [moderate positive]
  weight_loss_pct_6mo:    -0.076 mo per %       [small negative]
  cea_ng_ml:              -0.005 mo per ng/mL   [very small negative]
  ldh_u_l:                -0.0004 mo per U/L    [tiny negative; p<0.001]

Null/inconsistent associations:
  msi_high, her2_amplified, nras_mutation (slightly positive crude diff +0.22
  but not significant in adjusted model), liver_mets, bone_mets, tp53_mutation,
  pik3ca_mutation, pten_loss, cdkn2a_loss — none statistically meaningful.


5. AGE_YEARS — ANOMALOUSLY POSITIVE (iterations 6, 7)
-----------------------------------------------------
Age in years has a striking POSITIVE Pearson correlation with pfs_months
(r = +0.766, p < 1e-300). The adjusted age coefficient is +0.176 mo per year
(p < 1e-300) and persists in every model tried. This is the OPPOSITE of the
typical clinical expectation, where older oncology patients accrue worse PFS.
Two interaction effects qualify the trend:
  age_years x ecog_ps interaction: -0.007 (p = 1.5e-23) — high age + high
    ECOG worsens PFS more than additive expectations would predict.
  age_years x stage_iv interaction: -0.007 (p = 2.7e-12) — stage-IV penalty
    is greater at older ages.
Even with these negative interactions, the dominant age main effect on PFS
remains strongly positive across all subgroups. This suggests either an
unusual data-generating process (e.g., synthetic data), or a confounder not
captured in observable covariates.


6. NO DEMOGRAPHIC DISPARITIES (iteration 8)
-------------------------------------------
sex_female (p = 0.77), race_ethnicity (ANOVA p = 0.48), insurance_type (all
adjusted contrasts p > 0.08), rural_residence (p = 0.34), smoking_pack_years
(p = 0.91), and education_years (p = 0.34) all showed no clinically or
statistically meaningful associations with pfs_months. Stratifying the
regorafenib benefit by race_ethnicity also produced essentially identical
adjusted effects across all five categories (white +1.59, hispanic +1.64,
asian +1.60, black +1.72, other +1.76; all interaction p > 0.15) — i.e.,
no equity gap in treatment effect.


7. COMORBIDITIES, PRIOR THERAPIES, SNPs (iterations 9, 10)
----------------------------------------------------------
Comorbidities: every binary comorbidity (diabetes_mellitus, hypertension,
copd, chronic_kidney_disease, heart_failure, coronary_artery_disease,
atrial_fibrillation, autoimmune_disease, hepatitis B/C, HIV, prior_malignancy,
depression_anxiety_diagnosis, interstitial_lung_disease_history,
venous_thromboembolism_history) had |mean diff| < 0.08 mo and p >= 0.05.
None is a meaningful prognostic factor.

Prior anticancer therapies (chemotherapy, radiation, surgery, immunotherapy,
targeted therapy) similarly all had |diff| < 0.05 mo and p >= 0.16.

SNPs: no SNP main effect survives Bonferroni (alpha = 0.0019). Three SNPs
do show statistically robust SNP x regorafenib interactions at Bonferroni
threshold:
  snp_rs1800629 x regorafenib  beta = +0.126  p = 7.7e-06
  snp_rs2070744 x regorafenib  beta = -0.070  p = 1.5e-03
  snp_rs1801197 x regorafenib  beta = -0.097  p = 2.6e-04
However, the absolute magnitudes are small (~ 0.1 mo) compared to the
~2.5 mo regorafenib benefit in the favorable subgroup, and they should be
interpreted as exploratory.


8. OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
----------------------
- The dominant outcome signal is that treatment_regorafenib substantially
  prolongs pfs_months — by roughly 2.5 months — but ONLY in patients who
  are simultaneously left-sided primary, KRAS wild-type, and BRAF wild-type.
  Outside of this cell, regorafenib's effect on PFS is essentially zero.
- None of the other five treatments has a credible PFS effect at the cohort
  level OR within their canonical biomarker-defined subgroups, including the
  high-stakes pairings cetuximab/RAS-WT, pembrolizumab/MSI-high,
  encorafenib/BRAF-V600E, and trastuzumab-tucatinib/HER2-amplified.
- Stage IV disease (-1.36 mo), worse ECOG performance status (-1.17 mo per
  unit), KRAS mutation (-0.35), BRAF V600E (-0.36), right-sided primary
  (-0.29), low albumin, and 6-month weight loss are the only clinically
  meaningful prognostic features after adjustment.
- Age_years is strongly POSITIVELY associated with pfs_months (r = +0.77,
  +0.18 mo per year of age in the adjusted model) — an inversion of the
  usual clinical pattern that drives most of the explained variance in
  pfs_months and is robust to all attempted adjustments.
- There are no significant disparities in baseline PFS or in regorafenib
  benefit across sex, race/ethnicity, insurance type, or rural residence.
- The combined picture (sharp, narrowly-localized treatment effects;
  reversed age effect; absent canonical biomarker pairings) strongly
  suggests this dataset is synthetic or has a non-standard generating
  process; clinicians should NOT draw real-world treatment-decision
  conclusions from it without additional context.
