OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
==============================================================================

Prognostic main effects supported by the data:
  - ECOG performance status: -1.19 mo PFS per unit (p<1e-300). Robust in adjusted
    model (-1.17 mo per unit).
  - Stage IV: -1.35 mo PFS vs non-stage IV (p<1e-300). Robust in adjusted model.
  - Albumin: +0.47 mo PFS per g/dL (p~3e-115); preserved in adjusted model.
  - 6-month weight loss percent: -0.073 mo PFS per percent (p~2e-167).
  - KRAS mutation: -0.33 mo PFS (p~2e-58).
  - Right-sided primary: -0.31 mo PFS (p~1e-49).
  - BRAF V600E: -0.23 mo PFS unadjusted; -0.36 mo adjusted (p~5e-79).
  - CEA: -0.005 mo PFS per ng/mL (p~3e-17).
  - LDH: small negative association unadjusted (p=0.013), highly significant in
    adjusted model (p~7e-19).
  - Age: small POSITIVE association (older patients had slightly longer PFS,
    +0.18 mo per year, p<1e-300), opposite to the hypothesized direction.

Hypotheses refuted or unsupported:
  - Sex (female vs male): essentially identical mean PFS (diff ~0 mo, p=0.77).
  - MSI-high: not associated with longer PFS (diff -0.02 mo, p=0.68); refutes
    the prognostic-favorable expectation for MSI-H in this cohort.
  - NRAS mutation: associated with LONGER PFS (+0.22 mo, p=0.0007), opposite to
    the hypothesized poor-prognosis direction.
  - Liver metastases, bone metastases: no significant association with PFS.
  - NLR, CRP, fatigue/pain/appetite-loss grades, comorbidity count,
    venous-thromboembolism history: no significant unadjusted association with PFS.
  - Race/ethnicity, insurance type, rural residence: no significant differences
    in PFS in this cohort (ANOVA p>0.2 for race; uninsured-vs-private p=0.13).
  - SNP panel (27 candidate SNPs): no SNP reached Bonferroni-corrected
    significance; smallest unadjusted p was 0.015 (snp_rs1050828).

Treatment effects (overall and biomarker-modified) — striking findings:
  - Cetuximab: no significant overall effect (-0.04 mo, p=0.09). The classic
    cetuximab × KRAS, × NRAS, × BRAF V600E, and × right-sided interactions were
    ALL non-significant (interaction p-values 0.30–0.96). Subgroup analyses
    within KRAS-WT, RAS/RAF-WT, and left-sided primaries failed to show longer
    PFS on cetuximab. The canonical anti-EGFR biomarker selection signal is not
    detectable in this dataset.
  - Bevacizumab: no significant overall effect (p=0.35) and no significant
    interactions with KRAS, right-sidedness, age, or ECOG.
  - Pembrolizumab: no significant overall effect; pembrolizumab × MSI-high
    interaction null (p=0.99). Within MSI-high (n=2513), pembrolizumab vs no
    pembrolizumab differed by only 0.007 mo (p=0.96). The expected MSI-driven
    immunotherapy benefit was not observed.
  - Encorafenib: no significant overall effect; encorafenib × BRAF V600E
    interaction non-significant and in the opposite direction (-0.14 mo,
    p=0.38). Within BRAF-V600E mutants (n=2272), encorafenib was associated
    with numerically shorter PFS (-0.13 mo, p=0.33).
  - Trastuzumab + tucatinib: no significant overall effect; tras_tuc × HER2
    interaction null (p=0.78). Within HER2-amplified patients (n=1504),
    tras_tuc made no difference (+0.02 mo, p=0.93).
  - Regorafenib: significantly LONGER PFS on regorafenib than off (+0.97 mo,
    p<1e-216). This was the largest treatment-associated effect in the cohort
    and is the opposite direction from the originally hypothesized later-line
    indication; in this dataset regorafenib appears as a positive predictor of
    PFS, possibly reflecting selection of patients who received it.
  - Combined targeted regimens within BRAF-V600E (encorafenib + cetuximab) and
    within HER2+ (tras_tuc × KRAS) showed no significant interactions.

Adjusted multivariable model (Iteration 13/22) confirmed ECOG, stage IV, BRAF
V600E, albumin, LDH, weight-loss percent, and CEA as independent prognostic
features for PFS, while neither MSI-high nor any treatment indicator (after
adjustment) carried an independent association of clinical magnitude. The
final joint model with four biomarker × treatment interactions (Iteration 25)
kept all four interactions at p>0.2, while ECOG and stage IV remained the
dominant prognostic features.

Bottom line: This CRC cohort behaves as a strongly prognostic but largely
treatment-null and biomarker-matching-null dataset for the standard CRC
precision-oncology hypotheses. Performance status, stage IV, BRAF V600E,
albumin, weight loss, KRAS mutation, primary-side, CEA, LDH, and (paradoxically)
older age and regorafenib exposure are the variables that move PFS in this
dataset; the canonical cetuximab × RAS, pembrolizumab × MSI-H, encorafenib ×
BRAF V600E, and trastuzumab/tucatinib × HER2 effects are not present at
detectable magnitudes despite ample sample size in each biomarker subgroup.
