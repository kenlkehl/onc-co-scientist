Analysis summary for ds001_crc
==============================

Cohort
------
50,000 patient records described as a metastatic colorectal-cancer cohort.
Outcome: progression-free survival in months (pfs_months; mean 4.31, sd 2.29,
range 0-16.25). Features cover demographics, ECOG performance status, stage,
sidedness, six standard CRC biomarkers (KRAS, NRAS, BRAF V600E, MSI-high,
HER2-amplified, NTRK fusion), labs, comorbidities, six labelled treatments
(cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, trastuzumab+tucatinib,
regorafenib), prior-therapy history, symptom grades, and 25 SNPs.

Twenty-five iterations of propose -> test -> refine were run. Iterations
explored prognostic main effects, treatment main effects, the four canonical
biomarker-targeted-therapy interactions (cetuximab x RAS/RAF wild-type,
pembrolizumab x MSI-high, encorafenib x BRAF V600E, trastuzumab+tucatinib x
HER2-amplified), an encorafenib+cetuximab combination effect in BRAF V600E,
treatment-channeling sanity checks, demographics and access factors,
comorbidities, prior therapy lines, lab values, candidate pharmacogenetic
SNPs, and a multivariable model with biomarker-treatment interactions.

I went into the analysis expecting the canonical CRC pharmacology pattern to
appear, but most of those interactions did NOT appear in this dataset. The
report below is data-driven: I describe what the tests actually showed and
flag the canonical hypotheses that were refuted.

Hypotheses that were SUPPORTED
------------------------------
Prognostic main effects on PFS:
- Higher ECOG performance status -> shorter PFS. OLS slope -1.19 mo per unit
  (p ~ 0). Means: ECOG 0 = 5.27, 1 = 4.05, 2 = 2.91 mo. (h1)
- Stage IV -> shorter PFS. Mean PFS 3.70 vs 5.06 mo, diff -1.35 mo, p ~ 0. (h2)
- Higher albumin -> longer PFS. OLS slope +0.47 mo per g/dL (p = 3e-115). (h3)
- Higher LDH -> shorter PFS. Slope -0.00032 mo per U/L (p = 0.013); the
  direction is right but the magnitude is small. (h4)
- Greater 6-month weight loss -> shorter PFS. Slope -0.073 mo per percent
  weight loss (p = 2e-167). (h5)
- Right-sided primary -> shorter PFS than left-sided. Diff -0.31 mo,
  p = 1e-49. (h19)
- Higher alkaline phosphatase -> shorter PFS (slope -0.0006, p = 0.004). (h83)
- Higher total bilirubin -> shorter PFS (slope -0.065, p = 0.044). (h84)

Biomarker prognostic effects (drug-agnostic):
- KRAS mutation -> shorter PFS (-0.33 mo, p = 2e-58). (h26)
- BRAF V600E mutation -> shorter PFS (-0.23 mo, p = 3e-7). (h28)
- NRAS mutation -> LONGER PFS (+0.22 mo, p = 7e-4). This is counter to
  expectation; in real-world CRC, NRAS mutations behave similarly to KRAS
  mutations as anti-EGFR resistance markers and would not be expected to
  improve PFS. The data do not support a canonical interpretation here. (h27)

Treatment effect:
- Regorafenib -> longer PFS (unadjusted +0.97 mo, p = 2e-216; adjusted for
  ECOG, stage, biomarkers, all other treatments, labs and demographics
  +0.94 mo, p ~ 0). This is the largest treatment signal in the dataset.
  (h25, h48, h90)

Treatment channeling (sanity checks):
- Targeted agents are channeled to the matched biomarker subgroup. Chi-square
  tests of treatment use vs biomarker status are all significant: cetuximab
  preferentially in KRAS WT, pembrolizumab in MSI-high, encorafenib in
  BRAF V600E, trastuzumab+tucatinib in HER2-amplified. Channeling itself is
  present even though the predicted PFS interactions are absent. (h85-h88)
  [Note: although p-values pass at this sample size, the absolute differences
  in channeling probabilities are small.]

Other prognostic-direction effects:
- Higher prior_lines_of_therapy -> LONGER PFS (slope +0.18 mo per line,
  p ~ 0). This is opposite to clinical expectation (more prior lines usually
  signals refractory disease and shorter time to progression). (h72) The
  positive sign is consistent with the regorafenib effect, since regorafenib
  is used in late-line patients in real practice.

Hypotheses that were REFUTED (or null) in this dataset
------------------------------------------------------
Canonical CRC pharmacology that did NOT appear:
- Cetuximab x KRAS wild-type interaction: not present. Within KRAS WT,
  cetuximab was associated with -0.05 mo (p = 0.12); within KRAS-mutant,
  -0.03 mo (p = 0.38); the OLS interaction term was non-significant
  (p = 0.67). (h32-h34)
- Cetuximab x triple RAS/RAF wild-type: also not present. Within ras_raf_wt,
  cetuximab -0.05 mo (p = 0.17); the interaction term was -0.012 mo
  (p = 0.79). (h35-h37)
- Pembrolizumab x MSI-high: not present. Within MSI-high, pembrolizumab
  +0.007 mo (p = 0.96); interaction p = 0.99. MSI-high itself was not even
  prognostic (h29 p = 0.68). (h38-h40)
- Encorafenib x BRAF V600E: not present. Within BRAF V600E, encorafenib
  -0.13 mo (p = 0.33); interaction p = 0.38. (h41-h43)
- Trastuzumab+tucatinib x HER2-amplified: not present. Within HER2-amplified,
  +0.02 mo (p = 0.93); interaction p = 0.78. (h44-h46)
- Cetuximab x BRAF and cetuximab x NRAS resistance markers: also null.
  (h53, h54)
- Encorafenib + cetuximab combination in BRAF V600E (BEACON-style synergy):
  not present. Both-vs-neither comparison +0.01 mo (p = 0.97); interaction
  term within BRAF-mut p = 0.32. (h55, h56)
- Adjusted treatment effects within their target biomarker subgroups
  (Iteration 15): all four target-biomarker subgroup adjusted coefficients
  were small and non-significant (cetuximab in RAS/RAF WT p = 0.14;
  pembrolizumab in MSI-high p = 0.29; encorafenib in BRAF V600E p = 0.90;
  trastuzumab+tucatinib in HER2+ p = 0.67). (h49-h52)
- The summary multivariable model with all four interactions (Iteration 24)
  returned no positive-and-significant interaction terms (worst-case
  p = 0.97; mixed signs). (h89)
- The grand-summary hypothesis that all four biomarker-targeted treatments
  show the predicted positive subgroup effect was REFUTED. (h92)

Treatment main effects (all but one):
- Cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, and
  trastuzumab+tucatinib show no significant unadjusted PFS difference at the
  cohort level (all |diff| <= 0.05 mo, all p > 0.08). After multivariable
  adjustment bevacizumab is also null (+0.002 mo, p = 0.80). Only regorafenib
  is associated with longer PFS, and its sign survives full adjustment.
  (h20-h24, h47, h91)

Other null results:
- CRP and NLR: no significant association with PFS (p = 0.07 and p = 0.29).
  This is unusual; in published CRC and pan-cancer literature both are
  consistent prognostic markers. (h6, h7)
- All five symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite loss):
  no significant association (all p > 0.08). (h8-h12)
- Sites of metastasis (liver, bone, adrenal, pleural effusion, pericardial
  effusion, contralateral lung): none significant (all p > 0.06). The lack
  of a liver-mets effect is particularly notable for a CRC dataset.
  (h13-h18)
- All ten comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart failure,
  CAD, AF, autoimmune disease, prior VTE, prior malignancy): no significant
  associations. (h61-h70)
- MSI-high, HER2-amplified, NTRK fusion as drug-agnostic prognostic markers:
  no significant overall association with PFS. (h29, h30, h31)
- Demographics: rural residence, education years, race/ethnicity ANOVA,
  insurance ANOVA - all p > 0.2. (h57-h60)
- Five candidate SNPs (rs1045642, rs4244285, rs1801133, rs1800629,
  rs429358): all null. (h75-h79)
- Hemoglobin, platelets, absolute lymphocyte count as prognostic labs: no
  significant associations (all p > 0.07). Again unusual relative to
  expectations from real-world data. (h80-h82)
- Age, sex, prior_surgery: small or null effects. (h71, h73, h74)

Overall conclusion
------------------
The dataset reproduces a small set of classical prognostic relationships
(performance status, stage, albumin, weight loss, sidedness, KRAS and BRAF
adverse prognosis, alkaline phosphatase and bilirubin), but it does NOT
reproduce the canonical CRC biomarker-targeted-therapy interactions that
defined the targeted-therapy era: anti-EGFR x RAS/RAF wild-type,
anti-PD-1 x MSI-high, BRAF inhibitor x BRAF V600E, and HER2 inhibitor x
HER2 amplification are all clearly absent here, despite each targeted agent
being preferentially channeled to its matching biomarker. The largest
treatment signal in the dataset is regorafenib (+0.94 mo adjusted), and the
counter-intuitive positive association of prior_lines_of_therapy with PFS
likely reflects a structural feature of how the cohort was constructed
rather than a clinical reality. Several other expected prognostic signals
that should be visible in any real CRC EHR cohort - CRP, NLR, hemoglobin,
liver-mets burden, comorbidities, symptom burden - are also absent. Taken
together, the dataset behaves like a constructed cohort in which prognostic
direction is preserved for a few classic factors but the canonical
biomarker-treatment matching effects have been removed (or were never
included). A clinician reading the analyses naively might anchor on the
canonical patterns, but the iteration-by-iteration evidence here does not
support those patterns.
