Oncology Dataset Analysis — ds001_crc
=====================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with metastatic colorectal cancer (CRC).
Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival in months; mean 4.31, SD 2.29, range 0–16.3).
Approach: 25 iterations of hypothesis generation and statistical testing covering main
effects, biomarker × treatment interactions, subgroup analyses, and multivariable
adjustment. 73 analyses were run.

KEY HEADLINES
-------------
1. Classical clinical/laboratory prognostic factors behave as expected and are highly
   significant — strong negative effects on PFS for ecog_ps, stage_iv, weight loss,
   CEA, age, alkaline phosphatase, total bilirubin, right-sided primary, BRAF V600E,
   and a strong positive effect for albumin.
2. The canonical molecular-biomarker × matched-targeted-therapy interactions that
   define modern CRC therapy were NOT recovered in this cohort:
     - cetuximab × KRAS / NRAS / BRAF V600E: no significant interaction
     - pembrolizumab × MSI-high: no significant interaction
     - encorafenib × BRAF V600E: no significant interaction
     - trastuzumab/tucatinib × HER2: no significant interaction
   Effect estimates were small in magnitude and statistically null.
3. The only treatment with a significant overall main effect on PFS was regorafenib
   (+0.97 months, p ≈ 1.7e-216), which is counterintuitive given its real-world role
   as a salvage agent and likely reflects how treatment was assigned in the cohort.

ITERATION-BY-ITERATION DETAIL
-----------------------------

Iteration 1 — Performance status. ECOG ≥ 2 vs <2 had a mean PFS difference of
−1.65 months (Welch t, p≈0). Continuous OLS gave −1.19 months per ECOG point,
p≈0. Strongly supported.

Iteration 2 — Stage and albumin. Stage IV: −1.35 mo (p≈0). Albumin: +0.47 mo
per g/dL (p=3e-115). Both supported, large magnitudes.

Iteration 3 — Tumor burden / nutritional markers. Higher log1p(CEA): −0.15 mo per
log unit (p=5e-44). Higher LDH: small but significant (−3e-4 per U/L, p=0.013).
Greater 6-month weight loss: −0.073 mo per percent (p=2e-167). All supported.

Iteration 4 — BRAF V600E and MSI-H main effects. BRAF V600E had a small but
significant negative main effect on PFS (−0.23 mo, p=3.5e-7). MSI-H had no
significant main effect on PFS (−0.02 mo, p=0.68) — refuted as a prognostic
biomarker in this cohort.

Iteration 5 — Cetuximab × KRAS interaction. The classical EGFR-resistance
hypothesis (cetuximab benefit only in KRAS wild-type) was NOT supported:
interaction p=0.66; cetuximab effect in KRAS-wt patients was −0.05 mo (p=0.12);
cetuximab effect in KRAS-mut patients was −0.03 mo (p=0.38). Refuted.

Iteration 6 — Cetuximab × NRAS / BRAF / triple-WT subgroup. None of these
canonical resistance markers showed significant interactions or a cetuximab
benefit even in the triple-wild-type subgroup (cetuximab effect there:
−0.05 mo, p=0.17). The expected RAS/BRAF-wild-type benefit signal is absent.

Iteration 7 — Pembrolizumab × MSI-H. No interaction (p=0.99). Within MSI-H,
pembrolizumab effect was +0.007 mo (p=0.96); within MSS, +0.005 mo (p=0.86).
The canonical immunotherapy-in-MSI-H benefit was NOT detectable here. Refuted.

Iteration 8 — Encorafenib × BRAF V600E. No positive interaction (p=0.38);
within BRAF V600E patients, encorafenib effect was −0.13 mo (p=0.33). Refuted.

Iteration 9 — Trastuzumab/tucatinib × HER2 amplification. No interaction
(p=0.78); within HER2-amplified, drug effect was +0.02 mo (p=0.93). Refuted.

Iteration 10 — Bevacizumab effects. No significant overall main effect
(−0.02 mo, p=0.35). No interaction with KRAS (p=0.92) or with VTE history
(p=0.10). Bevacizumab is essentially flat on PFS in this cohort.

Iteration 11 — Sidedness. Right-sided primary tumors had shorter PFS
(−0.31 mo, p=1e-49) — supported and consistent with CRC literature. However,
the expected stronger negative interaction between cetuximab and right-sided
primary was NOT seen (interaction p=0.58); even within left-sided RAS/BRAF
WT patients (the canonical cetuximab indication), cetuximab provided no
benefit (−0.07 mo, p=0.13).

Iteration 12 — Demographics. Sex had no effect on PFS (p=0.77). Age had a
counterintuitive positive coefficient (+0.18 mo per year, p≈0) which warrants
caution — likely reflects confounding (older patients in this cohort may have
indolent disease). Race/ethnicity had no significant overall difference
(ANOVA p=0.48).

Iteration 13 — Sites of disease and multivariable model. Liver mets (p=0.52)
and bone mets (p=0.23) had no significant univariate effects, suggesting
they are already captured by other variables. In a multivariable model
(R²=0.35), ECOG remained strongly independent (−1.18 mo per point, p≈0).

Iteration 14 — Inflammation. CRP showed only a borderline effect (p=0.065).
NLR was not significant (p=0.29). Hemoglobin was borderline (p=0.078). The
inflammation/anemia axis is weak in this cohort.

Iteration 15 — Liver function tests. Alkaline phosphatase was significantly
negative (p=0.004). Total bilirubin was negative (p=0.044). AST was not
significant (p=0.32).

Iteration 16 — Patient-reported symptoms. Fatigue, pain, and appetite-loss
grades all had non-significant effects on PFS (p > 0.5). Either symptoms are
collinear with stronger covariates (ECOG, weight loss) or they were generated
independently of outcome.

Iteration 17 — Comorbidities. Heart failure, CKD, and diabetes had no
significant association with PFS in this cohort.

Iteration 18 — Treatment history. Prior lines of therapy (p=0.39), prior
chemotherapy (p=0.82), and years since diagnosis (p=0.14) all non-significant.

Iteration 19 — Pharmacogenomic SNPs. Univariate screen of all 32 SNPs found
3 with nominal p<0.05 (consistent with chance under the null at α=0.05 for 32
tests, but the top hit had eff=+0.09 mo, p=0.015 — does not survive Bonferroni).
No clear pharmacogenomic signal.

Iteration 20 — Sociodemographics. Insurance type, rural residence, and
education years all non-significant.

Iteration 21 — Smoking, effusions. Smoking pack-years p=0.91. Pleural
effusion borderline (p=0.069), pericardial effusion non-significant (p=0.12).

Iteration 22 — Other treatments. Regorafenib showed a strongly positive main
effect on PFS in the overall cohort: +0.97 mo (p=1.7e-216). This is the largest
treatment effect observed and is opposite to the typical clinical experience of
regorafenib as a modestly active later-line agent. No significant interaction
with prior lines of therapy (p=0.48). Pembrolizumab × TP53 also non-significant.

Iteration 23 — Multivariable model with full interaction panel. The joint Wald
test of all six biomarker × matched-treatment interactions was NOT significant
(p=0.74). After adjustment, neither cetuximab×KRAS (p=0.23) nor
pembrolizumab×MSI (p=0.27) became significant.

Iteration 24 — Treatment-effect heterogeneity by demographics. Pembrolizumab×sex
(p=0.52), bevacizumab×age (p=0.99), and cetuximab×ECOG (p=0.25) — all null.

Iteration 25 — Adjusted within-biomarker subgroup checks. After adjustment for
age, sex, ECOG, stage IV, albumin, log-CEA, LDH, and side, there was no
significant cetuximab effect in RAS/BRAF wild-type (−0.02 mo, p=0.17), no
pembrolizumab effect in MSI-H (+0.03 mo, p=0.52), no encorafenib effect in
BRAF V600E (−0.02 mo, p=0.45), and no trastuzumab/tucatinib effect in HER2
amplified (+0.01 mo, p=0.94). All four canonical "matched-targeted-therapy"
hypotheses are refuted in this cohort.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
- The dataset cleanly recapitulates the dominant CLINICAL prognostic axis in
  metastatic CRC: PFS is shorter in patients with higher ECOG performance
  status, stage IV disease, lower albumin, higher CEA, more weight loss,
  higher alkaline phosphatase / bilirubin, BRAF V600E mutation, and right-sided
  primary tumors.
- The dataset does NOT recapitulate the canonical PREDICTIVE biomarker–drug
  pairings that govern modern CRC treatment: no detectable cetuximab benefit
  in RAS/BRAF wild-type tumors (or in left-sided RAS/BRAF wt), no pembrolizumab
  benefit in MSI-high disease, no encorafenib benefit in BRAF V600E, and no
  trastuzumab/tucatinib benefit in HER2-amplified disease. These null
  predictive findings are the most striking departure from real-world CRC
  oncology in this cohort.
- The single significant overall treatment main effect is regorafenib (+0.97 mo,
  p ≈ 1.7e-216), which is biologically implausible at that magnitude and
  suggests that treatment assignment in this dataset is not modeled on
  real-world prescribing patterns or that regorafenib carries an unmeasured
  positive selection signal.
- Pharmacogenomic SNPs and sociodemographic factors had no clear association
  with PFS beyond what would be expected by chance.

Hypotheses supported (with direction): poor ECOG → shorter PFS; stage IV →
shorter; higher albumin → longer; higher CEA → shorter; higher LDH → shorter;
greater weight loss → shorter; BRAF V600E → shorter; older age → longer
(unexpected direction); right-sided primary → shorter; higher ALP → shorter;
higher bilirubin → shorter; regorafenib → longer.

Hypotheses refuted: cetuximab × KRAS / NRAS / BRAF / sidedness interactions;
cetuximab benefit in RAS/BRAF wt; pembrolizumab × MSI-H interaction and
within-MSI-H benefit; encorafenib × BRAF V600E interaction and within-mutant
benefit; trastuzumab/tucatinib × HER2 amp interaction and within-amplified
benefit; sex differences; race/ethnicity differences; insurance/rural/education
differences; comorbidity main effects (HF, CKD, DM); symptom main effects
(fatigue, pain, appetite); smoking pack-year effect; bevacizumab main effect.
