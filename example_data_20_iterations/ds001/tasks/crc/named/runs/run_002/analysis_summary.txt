Oncology dataset ds001_crc — Analysis summary
==============================================

Cohort: 50,000 metastatic colorectal cancer patients with 124 features and one outcome (pfs_months, mean 4.31, sd 2.29, range 0–16.25). All values complete; no missingness.

Approach
--------
Across 19 propose-test-refine iterations I (a) screened all main effects of clinical features, biomarkers, treatments, comorbidities, demographics, and 27 pharmacogenomic SNPs on pfs_months; (b) tested all canonical biomarker-matched treatment-effect interactions; (c) built multivariable OLS models; (d) refined to subgroup and interaction structure that explained heterogeneity.

Iteration-by-iteration synthesis
--------------------------------

Iteration 1 (clinical baseline). Standard prognostic factors behaved as expected: each unit higher ECOG-PS shortened PFS by 1.19 months (p<<0.001), stage_iv by 1.35 months (p<<0.001), each 1 g/dL of albumin lengthened PFS by 0.47 months (p<<0.001), and CEA was modestly negative (-0.005/ng/mL, p=3e-17). LDH was weakly negative (-0.0003/U/L, p=0.013). Liver_mets had no main effect on PFS (p=0.52) — first hint of unusual structure. msi_high alone had no PFS effect (p=0.68).

Iteration 2 (treatment main effects). Of six treatments, only treatment_regorafenib had a significant unadjusted PFS effect (+0.97 months, p<<0.001). Cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, and trastuzumab/tucatinib were all null or trivially negative.

Iteration 3 (textbook treatment × biomarker interactions). All four canonical interactions tested null:
  • pembrolizumab × msi_high: beta=0.002, p=0.99 (expected positive synergy)
  • encorafenib × braf_v600e: beta=-0.14, p=0.38 (expected positive synergy)
  • trastuzumab/tucatinib × her2_amplified: beta=0.06, p=0.78 (expected positive synergy)
  • cetuximab × kras_mutation: beta=0.02, p=0.66 (expected resistance)
  • cetuximab × right_sided_primary: beta=0.026, p=0.58 (expected attenuation)
  • cetuximab × braf_v600e: beta=-0.026, p=0.81

Iteration 4 (biomarker prognostic signal). Univariate prognostic associations: KRAS mutation -0.33 months (p=6e-41), BRAF V600E -0.22 (p=0.0002), right_sided_primary -0.31 (p<<0.001). NRAS mutation, surprisingly, was POSITIVE (+0.22, p=0.002). age_years showed a strong, unexpected POSITIVE effect on PFS (+0.18 months/year, p<<0.001) — opposite the typical real-world direction.

Iteration 5 (demographics/social). race_ethnicity, insurance_type, rural_residence, sex_female, and education_years were all null on PFS. No demographic disparities detected.

Iteration 6 (age robustness). The +0.18 months-per-year age effect persisted after multivariable adjustment for ECOG, stage, albumin, weight loss, and was nearly identical in regorafenib responders (+0.177) and non-responders (+0.176). A significant positive quadratic term (p<<0.001) shows the relationship accelerates at older ages: among ECOG-0/non-stage-IV patients, mean PFS rises monotonically from 2.59 months (age ≤50) to 9.49 months (age >80). The age effect is robust, large, and biologically counterintuitive for real CRC.

Iteration 7 (full multivariable). A 30-covariate OLS model achieved R²=0.86. Independent predictors: stage_iv -1.36, ecog_ps -1.17, treatment_regorafenib +0.94, albumin +0.47, BRAF V600E -0.36, KRAS -0.35, right_sided -0.29, age +0.176, weight_loss_pct_6mo -0.076/pct, CEA -0.005, LDH -0.0004. All other treatments, msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion, NRAS, liver_mets, bone_mets, comorbidities, prior therapy, hemoglobin, liver enzymes, and vitals were null after adjustment.

Iteration 8 (regorafenib subgroup heterogeneity). Highly heterogeneous regorafenib effect:
  • rego × KRAS interaction beta=-1.625, p<<0.001 (works in WT, not mutant)
  • rego × BRAF V600E interaction beta=-0.97, p=8e-80 (works in WT, not mutant)
  • rego × right_sided interaction beta=-1.43, p<<0.001 (works left-sided only)
  • rego × MSI-H: no significant interaction
  • rego × liver_mets: small attenuation (beta=-0.08, p=0.007)

Iteration 9 (joint biomarker subgroup). In left-sided AND KRAS-WT AND BRAF-WT patients (n=17,372): regorafenib gives +2.71 months PFS (p<<0.001); mean PFS jumps from 4.12 → 6.88 months. In every other subgroup combination: rego effect ≈ 0 (p=0.66). Including a treatment_regorafenib × responsive_group interaction term raises model R² from 0.86 to 0.91.

Iteration 10 (textbook biomarker-matched stratified analyses). Even when restricted to the indicated biomarker subgroup, the textbook-matched targeted therapies failed:
  • pembrolizumab in MSI-H (n=2,513): beta=+0.04, p=0.52 — no benefit
  • encorafenib in BRAF V600E (n=2,272): beta=+0.005, p=0.88 — no benefit
  • trastuzumab/tucatinib in HER2-amplified (n=1,504): beta=+0.02, p=0.83 — no benefit
  • cetuximab in left-sided RAS/BRAF-WT (n=16,443): beta=-0.04, p=0.19 — no benefit; regorafenib in the same subset: +2.70 (p<<0.001)

Iteration 11 (SNP main effects). Of 27 SNPs, three reached nominal p<0.05 (rs1050828 +0.092 p=0.015; rs1801131 -0.049 p=0.027; rs1801197 -0.049 p=0.047) — within chance expectation; none survive Bonferroni at α=0.0019.

Iteration 12 (treatment × SNP screen, 162 tests). Only regorafenib showed multiple SNP interactions: × rs1800629 (TNF) beta=+0.13, p=8e-6; × rs1801197 (CALCR) beta=-0.10, p=0.0003 — both survive Bonferroni at α=3e-4. Smaller signals × rs2070744 (NOS3, p=0.002), × rs1045642 (ABCB1, p=0.005). No pembrolizumab × SNP interaction at p<0.01. The signal is weak and effect sizes (0.06–0.13 months) are small relative to the regorafenib main interaction.

Iteration 13 (comorbidity/prior therapy/lab screen). Of 15 comorbidities, only COPD reached nominal p<0.01 (-0.027, p=0.009; not Bonferroni-significant). All five prior-therapy variables were null. Liver enzymes, renal labs, vitals, BMI, symptom grades, and metastatic-site indicators were all null after adjustment for age/ECOG/stage/albumin. Only albumin and weight_loss_pct_6mo carried independent prognostic information among labs.

Iteration 14 (continuous biomarker × treatment). Negative regorafenib × CEA interaction (-0.025, p<<0.001) and × albumin interaction (-0.090, p=2e-5): regorafenib benefit is largest in lower-albumin/higher-tumor-burden patients, plausibly because the responsive subgroup itself has a wider PFS dynamic range. Within MSI-H, bevacizumab showed a marginal positive effect (+0.10, p=0.019) that does not survive multiple testing.

Iteration 15 (other prognostic confirmations). weight_loss_pct_6mo (-0.076/pct, p<<0.001) and ldh_u_l (-0.0004/U/L, p<<0.001) are independent prognostic factors. Albumin and ECOG share variance but each retains a large independent coefficient.

Iteration 16 (collinearity diagnosis). Adding the regorafenib × responsive_group interaction nullifies the apparent independent prognostic effects of right_sided_primary (p=0.96), kras_mutation (p=0.77), and braf_v600e (p=0.98). Their univariate negative-prognostic appearance is fully explained by encoding which patients can or cannot benefit from regorafenib — not by any direct tumor-biology-of-the-mutant effect.

Iteration 17 (age effect robustness). Age's positive PFS coefficient (+0.176) is uniform across treatment-responsive and non-responsive patients, across all individual treatment groups, and across baseline-fit (ECOG=0, non-stage-IV) patients. It is a global, treatment-independent feature of the dataset.

Iteration 18 (synthesis of dataset anomalies). Three notable departures from real-world CRC biology emerge:
  (a) Older age is associated with LONGER PFS (opposite the real-world expectation).
  (b) Every canonical biomarker-matched targeted therapy (pembro/MSI-H, encorafenib/BRAF V600E, tras-tuca/HER2-amplified, cetuximab/left-sided RAS/BRAF-WT) fails to improve PFS even within its indicated subgroup.
  (c) Regorafenib (in real life a modestly active multikinase salvage agent) shows a +2.7 month benefit and a left-sided RAS/BRAF-wild-type selectivity that, in actual oncology, characterizes anti-EGFR therapy (cetuximab/panitumumab).

Iteration 19 (final ranked predictors). Dominant independent predictors of pfs_months in this cohort, in approximate magnitude order:
  • stage_iv: -1.36 months
  • ecog_ps: -1.17 months per unit
  • treatment_regorafenib × left-sided RAS/BRAF-WT subgroup: +2.74 months interaction
  • albumin_g_dl: +0.47 months per g/dL
  • age_years: +0.176 months per year (positive — counterintuitive)
  • weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.076 months per pct
  • cea_ng_ml: -0.005 months per ng/mL
  • ldh_u_l: -0.0004 months per U/L
  • regorafenib × rs1800629 (TNF): +0.13 (small pharmacogenomic signal)

Null/near-null in adjusted models: sex, race/ethnicity, insurance, rural residence, education, all comorbidities, prior therapy lines, hemoglobin, liver enzymes, renal labs, vitals, BMI, symptom grades, most metastatic-site indicators, msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion, nras_mutation, and the remaining 26 SNPs.

Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
--------------------------------

Supported by the data:
  • Higher ECOG, stage IV, KRAS mutation (univariate), BRAF V600E (univariate), right-sided primary (univariate), CEA, LDH, weight loss → shorter PFS.
  • Higher albumin → longer PFS.
  • Regorafenib improves PFS overall and especially in left-sided RAS/BRAF-WT patients.
  • Older age in this cohort is associated with LONGER PFS (significant but biologically anomalous).
  • Two regorafenib × SNP pharmacogenomic interactions (rs1800629, rs1801197).

Refuted by the data:
  • Pembrolizumab benefit in MSI-H (the textbook strongest indication).
  • Encorafenib benefit in BRAF V600E.
  • Trastuzumab/tucatinib benefit in HER2 amplified.
  • Cetuximab benefit overall and within the canonical left-sided RAS/BRAF-WT subset.
  • Bevacizumab benefit overall.
  • KRAS, BRAF V600E, and right-sided being independently negative-prognostic of tumor biology — these effects are entirely explained by their use as predictors of regorafenib non-response.
  • Strong demographic disparities (race, sex, insurance, rural, education) in PFS.
  • Most comorbidity, prior-therapy, lab, vital, symptom, and SNP main effects.

Overall conclusion
------------------
In ds001_crc, only one of six treatments — treatment_regorafenib — produced a measurable PFS benefit, and that benefit is concentrated almost entirely in left-sided KRAS-wild-type BRAF-wild-type tumors (≈+2.7 months), where it lifts mean PFS from 4.12 to 6.88 months. The classical biomarker-matched targeted regimens (anti-PD-1 in MSI-H, BRAF inhibition in V600E, HER2-targeted therapy in HER2-amplified, anti-EGFR in left-sided RAS-WT) all produced null results — a marked departure from real-world CRC oncology. Most patient features that would conventionally drive clinical decisions — demographics, comorbidities, organ-function labs, vitals, symptoms, prior therapy lines, and pharmacogenomic SNPs — carry little independent prognostic signal in this cohort. Two features dominate prognosis (ECOG, stage), one dominates treatment effect (regorafenib in the responsive biomarker subgroup), and one dominates baseline PFS variance in a counterintuitive direction (age, with a positive coefficient). Together these results imply the dataset's treatment-effect and age-PFS structure differ systematically from real-world CRC populations.
