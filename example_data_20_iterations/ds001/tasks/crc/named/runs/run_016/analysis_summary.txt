ds001_crc — Analysis Summary
============================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with metastatic colorectal cancer features. Outcome: progression-free survival in months (pfs_months; mean 4.31, SD 2.29). Twenty-five iterations of hypothesis-driven analyses were run, covering main effects, biomarker–treatment interactions, multivariable adjustment, and a SNP scan.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Prognostic main effects — strong and clinically coherent
------------------------------------------------------------
The cohort behaves as expected on classic prognostic axes:

- ECOG performance status: each 1-point increase reduces mean PFS by ~1.19 months (p ≈ 0; iter 1, h1.1). The effect size is unchanged after multivariable adjustment (-1.18 mo, iter 9, h9.1).
- Stage IV disease: PFS 3.70 mo vs 5.05 mo for non-stage-IV (diff −1.35, p ≈ 0; iter 1, h1.2).
- Weight loss in prior 6 months: −0.073 mo of PFS per percent (p ≈ 2e-167; iter 2, h2.2), retained in the adjusted model (−0.076, iter 9, h9.3).
- Albumin: each 1 g/dL higher albumin adds 0.47 mo of PFS (p ≈ 3e-115; iter 2, h2.1; adjusted +0.46, iter 9, h9.2).
- CEA, LDH, alkaline phosphatase, total bilirubin: all show small but statistically significant negative associations with PFS (iter 2 and 13).
- Right-sided primary: PFS 4.11 vs 4.42 mo (diff −0.31, p ≈ 1e-49; iter 8, h8.1).
- BRAF V600E: PFS 4.09 vs 4.32 mo (diff −0.23, p ≈ 3e-7; iter 23, h23.1).

A surprising main-effect finding is that older age was associated with LONGER PFS (+0.18 mo per year, p ≈ 0; iter 1, h1.3) — opposite to the usual clinical prior. This likely reflects selection / reverse confounding in how the cohort was assembled (e.g. older patients may only be enrolled when functionally well), but readers should not over-interpret it as a true biological effect.

CRP and NLR (the inflammatory markers) showed only weak/null associations with PFS in this cohort (iter 2, h2.4–h2.5), as did fatigue, pain, dyspnea, and appetite-loss grades (iter 10), heart failure, CKD, COPD, diabetes (iter 11), and the metastatic-site indicators bone, adrenal, and pleural effusion (iter 15). Liver mets was likewise null (iter 8, h8.3).

Demographic equity check (iter 14): ANOVA across race/ethnicity (p=0.48), insurance type (p=0.22), and rural residence (p=0.34) found no PFS differences — the cohort does not appear to encode socioeconomic gradients in PFS.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Treatment main effects
------------------------------------------------------------
The most striking result of the entire analysis is that the targeted/immunotherapy main effects (iter 3) are essentially null:

- Cetuximab: −0.04 mo (p=0.09)
- Bevacizumab: −0.02 mo (p=0.35)
- Pembrolizumab: +0.005 mo (p=0.86)
- Encorafenib: +0.005 mo (p=0.87)
- Trastuzumab + tucatinib: −0.04 mo (p=0.24)

Only regorafenib showed a large, highly significant POSITIVE main effect: +0.97 mo (5.09 vs 4.12, p ≈ 2e-216; iter 3, h3.6). This is clinically counterintuitive — regorafenib is typically a later-line agent with modest benefit. Adjustment for prior_lines_of_therapy, ECOG, and stage_iv did not attenuate it (iter 19, h19.1: +0.96 mo, p ≈ 0); in heavily pretreated patients (≥2 prior lines), the regorafenib effect remained +0.99 mo (iter 19, h19.2). In a fully adjusted model (iter 24, h24.3) regorafenib retained +0.94 mo while bevacizumab dropped to ~0. The regorafenib signal is robust within this dataset but does not match real-world expectations, suggesting a synthetic or aggregation-level mechanism rather than a biological one.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Biomarker–treatment interactions (the central CRC questions) — all null
------------------------------------------------------------
The clinically established matched-biomarker hypotheses were tested directly and were NOT supported:

- Cetuximab × RAS/RAF wild-type (iter 4): interaction +/-0.01 mo (p=0.79); cetuximab effect in WT was actually negative (−0.05, p=0.17) and in mutant was −0.03 (p=0.25). The KRAS-mutant subgroup also showed no harm (iter 4, h4.3). Adjusted analysis in the WT subset (iter 20, h20.1): −0.02 mo (p=0.29).
- Cetuximab × sidedness within RAS/RAF WT (iter 4 h4.2 / iter 21 / iter 25 h25.2): interaction +0.04 mo (p=0.56); WT-left effect −0.07 (p=0.12); WT-right −0.03 (p=0.59). The expected left-sided WT cetuximab benefit is absent.
- Pembrolizumab × MSI-high (iter 5, iter 22): interaction +0.002 mo (p=0.99); MSI-high pembrolizumab effect +0.01 (p=0.96); MSS effect +0.005 (p=0.86). Adjusted analysis in the MSI-high subset (iter 20, h20.2): +0.06 mo (p=0.23). Conversely, in pembrolizumab-treated patients, MSI-high vs MSS PFS difference was −0.02 (p=0.89; iter 22, h22.1) — i.e., MSI-high status confers no PFS advantage even for ICB-treated patients, contrary to KEYNOTE-177 findings in real-world data.
- Encorafenib × BRAF V600E (iter 6, iter 20 h20.3): interaction −0.14 mo (p=0.38); BRAF-mut encorafenib effect −0.13 (p=0.33); adjusted −0.002 (p=0.87). The expected positive interaction for BRAF inhibition in V600E disease is absent.
- Trastuzumab+tucatinib × HER2 amplified (iter 7, iter 25 h25.1): interaction +0.06 mo (p=0.78); HER2-amp effect +0.02 (p=0.93); adjusted +0.01 (p=0.88). The expected HER2-targeted benefit in HER2-amplified disease is absent.
- Bevacizumab × KRAS / sidedness / stage (iter 16, iter 24 h24.1): all interaction terms ~0 (p > 0.6) and bevacizumab is null in all subgroups — consistent with no benefit anywhere in this dataset, not just biomarker-specific lack.

In summary: the dataset exhibits realistic prognostic biology (ECOG, stage, albumin, weight loss, sidedness, BRAF V600E) but does NOT encode the canonical CRC drug–biomarker interactions that drive real-world treatment decisions.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Mutation status (other than BRAF V600E)
------------------------------------------------------------
TP53 mutation, PIK3CA mutation, and MSI-high status all showed null associations with PFS at the cohort level (iter 23). MSI-high in particular had effectively no main effect on PFS (−0.02 mo, p=0.68; iter 23, h23.2), which is consistent with the absent pembrolizumab × MSI interaction.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Pharmacogenomic SNP scan
------------------------------------------------------------
None of the 27 SNPs achieved Bonferroni significance (alpha = 1.85e-3); the strongest signal, snp_rs1050828, was nominal at p=0.015 (iter 17, h17.1). Pre-specified pharmacologically motivated SNPs — rs1801133 (MTHFR C677T), rs1065852 (CYP2D6), rs4244285 (CYP2C19) — were all null at p > 0.3 (iter 18). The SNP panel does not appear to drive PFS in this cohort.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. Multivariable synthesis
------------------------------------------------------------
A multivariable OLS containing all six treatments, all major biomarkers, performance status, stage, age, albumin, LDH, weight loss, CRP, prior lines of therapy, sidedness, and liver mets (iter 24, h24.2/24.3) produced these notable adjusted coefficients:

- Bevacizumab: ~0 (p=0.79)
- Regorafenib: +0.94 mo (p ≈ 0)
- Other treatments: small/null
- Albumin and ECOG retain their dominant prognostic roles.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. Hypothesis verdict summary
------------------------------------------------------------
Supported:
- Higher ECOG → shorter PFS (h1.1, h9.1)
- Stage IV → shorter PFS (h1.2)
- Lower albumin → shorter PFS (h2.1, h9.2)
- Higher weight loss → shorter PFS (h2.2, h9.3)
- Higher LDH/CEA/AlkP/bilirubin → shorter PFS (h2.3, h2.6, h13.2, h13.3)
- Right-sided primary → shorter PFS (h8.1)
- BRAF V600E → shorter PFS (h23.1)
- Regorafenib → LONGER PFS overall and in heavily pretreated patients (h3.6, h19.1, h19.2, h24.3)

Refuted (null):
- Cetuximab × RAS/RAF WT, × sidedness within WT (h4.1, h4.2, h21.1, h25.2)
- Pembrolizumab × MSI-high (h5.1, h5.2, h20.2, h22.1)
- Encorafenib × BRAF V600E (h6.1, h6.2, h20.3)
- Trastuzumab+tucatinib × HER2 amplified (h7.1, h7.2, h25.1)
- Bevacizumab main effect and all interactions tested (h3.2, h16.x, h24.1, h24.2)
- Pembrolizumab, encorafenib, trastuzumab+tucatinib, cetuximab main effects (h3.1, h3.3, h3.4, h3.5)
- Liver/bone/adrenal/pleural metastasis main effects (h8.3, h15.x)
- Symptom grades (h10.x)
- Comorbidities (h11.x)
- Prior therapy main effects (h12.x)
- Race/ethnicity, insurance, rural residence (h14.x)
- MSI-high, TP53, PIK3CA main effects (h23.2–h23.4)
- All 27 SNPs after Bonferroni (h17.1, h18.x)

Surprising:
- Older age → LONGER PFS (h1.3)
- Regorafenib → LONGER PFS unconditionally (h3.6, h19.x)

------------------------------------------------------------
8. Overall conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------
The ds001_crc dataset reproduces the standard CRC prognostic landscape — performance status, stage, nutritional/inflammatory status, sidedness, and BRAF V600E all behave as in clinical literature — but it does NOT reproduce the matched biomarker-treatment effects that define modern targeted CRC therapy: cetuximab in RAS/RAF wild-type left-sided disease, pembrolizumab in MSI-high disease, encorafenib in BRAF V600E, and HER2-targeted therapy in HER2-amplified disease are all absent. The dominant treatment signal is a large, robust positive effect for regorafenib that survives adjustment but defies clinical expectation. Combined with the unexpectedly positive age coefficient and the absent SNP signal, the most likely interpretation is that the underlying data-generating process encodes a coherent prognostic model but a non-clinical (or simplified) treatment-effect structure. Clinically meaningful conclusions about treatment efficacy should NOT be drawn from this cohort; the prognostic findings are robust and can be reasoned about more confidently.
