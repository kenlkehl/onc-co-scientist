ANALYSIS SUMMARY -- ds001_crc cohort (n=50000)
======================================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival, months; mean ~4.31, SD ~2.29).
Approach: 25 iterations of propose-test-refine. We tested 106 hypotheses
covering classic prognostic factors, tumor biology main effects, treatment
main effects and treatment-biomarker interactions, lab and symptom predictors,
metastatic patterns, comorbidities, demographics, SNPs, and adjusted
multivariable models.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. CLASSIC PROGNOSTIC FACTORS -- strong, directionally expected, highly
   significant (iterations 1, 9-13; re-confirmed in adjusted OLS in
   iteration 14)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

 * ecog_ps: each unit increase reduces pfs_months by ~1.19 mo (p<<0.001).
   Survives multivariable adjustment with negative coefficient.
 * stage_iv=1: pfs ~1.35 mo shorter than stage_iv=0 (p<<0.001).
 * albumin_g_dl: positive association, +0.47 mo per g/dL (p~10^-115);
   remains positive in adjusted model.
 * ldh_u_l: negative correlation with pfs (highly significant); negative
   coefficient remains after adjustment.
 * crp_mg_l, nlr: small negative correlations with pfs (significant in
   the very large sample).
 * hemoglobin_g_dl: positive correlation with pfs (significant).
 * weight_loss_pct_6mo, cea_ng_ml, pain_nrs, fatigue_grade,
   appetite_loss_grade: all negatively correlated with pfs (Spearman,
   significant).
 * liver_mets=1: clearly shorter pfs (significant); robust to adjustment.
 * bone_mets, adrenal_mets, pleural_effusion: shorter pfs (each
   significant in this large cohort).
 * Comorbidities: chronic_kidney_disease, heart_failure, prior_malignancy
   were associated with shorter pfs; diabetes_mellitus also showed a
   small negative association.
 * age_years: small negative association with pfs (significant).
 * right_sided_primary=1: ~0.31 mo shorter pfs (p~10^-49) -- recapitulates
   the known CRC sidedness prognostic effect.
 * kras_mutation=1, braf_v600e=1: each associated with shorter pfs in
   univariable analysis (significant, modest magnitude).
 * msi_high (alone): no overall pfs difference (NOT significant) -- i.e.,
   in a cohort that mixes MSI-high responders to immunotherapy with
   non-responders, the prognostic signal cancels out.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
2. TREATMENT-BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS -- TESTED BUT NOT OBSERVED
----------------------------------------------------------------------

A central goal was to test the well-known CRC pharmacogenomic
relationships. In this cohort, NONE of the canonical biomarker-targeted
matching effects achieved statistical significance:

 * treatment_cetuximab x kras_mutation interaction: coef +0.020, p=0.66 (NS).
   Stratified subgroups: no benefit of cetuximab in either KRAS wild-type
   (diff=-0.05, p=0.12) or KRAS mutant (diff=-0.03, p=0.38) patients.
   Hypothesis NOT supported by this dataset.
 * treatment_pembrolizumab x msi_high interaction: coef +0.002, p=0.99 (NS).
   In MSI-high subgroup: pembrolizumab vs no pembrolizumab pfs diff=+0.007
   (p=0.96). Hypothesis NOT supported.
 * treatment_encorafenib x braf_v600e interaction: coef -0.14, p=0.38 (NS).
   In BRAF V600E subgroup the encorafenib subgroup difference was actually
   slightly negative (diff=-0.13, p=0.33). Hypothesis NOT supported.
 * treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib x her2_amplified interaction:
   coef +0.063, p=0.78 (NS). Hypothesis NOT supported.
 * treatment_cetuximab x right_sided_primary interaction: coef +0.026,
   p=0.58 (NS).
 * treatment_bevacizumab x kras_mutation interaction: NS.

The adjusted multivariable model (iteration 22) including age, ECOG,
stage, albumin, LDH, liver_mets, key biomarkers, and all four matched
interaction terms confirmed: each of the four targeted-therapy x
biomarker interaction coefficients was small and non-significant.
Stratified, multivariable-adjusted within-biomarker subgroup tests
(iteration 25) likewise did not show positive treatment effects in the
matched subgroups (e.g., adjusted cetuximab coef among KRAS wild-type
was small and non-significant; adjusted pembrolizumab coef among MSI-high
was small and non-significant).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
3. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

 * treatment_regorafenib=1: SHORTER pfs, large effect (~0.97 mo shorter,
   p~10^-216). Almost certainly driven by indication bias -- regorafenib
   is used in heavily pretreated patients with exhausted standard
   options.
 * treatment_cetuximab, treatment_bevacizumab, treatment_pembrolizumab,
   treatment_encorafenib, treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib: NO
   statistically significant unadjusted main effect on pfs (all p>0.05).
 * higher prior_lines_of_therapy: associated with shorter pfs (negative
   correlation, significant).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
4. DEMOGRAPHICS / SOCIODEMOGRAPHIC
----------------------------------------------------------------------

 * Age: small negative association with pfs (significant).
 * Sex: small or no association.
 * rural_residence: small association.
 * Insurance (uninsured vs private), Race (black vs white): small/no
   significant difference in unadjusted pfs.
None of these effects were of large magnitude.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
5. SYMPTOM BURDEN / COMPOSITE INFLAMMATORY STATE (iteration 23)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

 * Composite "poor systemic state" (high NLR + low albumin) had shorter
   pfs than the "good" quadrant (significant) -- consistent with the
   individual marker analyses.
 * Total symptom burden (sum of fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough,
   appetite loss): negatively correlated with pfs (significant Spearman).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
6. SNP SCREEN (iterations 18-20)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

We screened 27 SNPs. At nominal p<0.05 without multiple-testing
correction, three SNPs reached nominal significance:
 * snp_rs1801131 (carrier=1): pfs ~ -0.05 mo (p=0.027).
 * snp_rs1050828 (carrier=1): pfs ~ +0.09 mo (p=0.017).
 * snp_rs1801197 (carrier=1): pfs ~ -0.05 mo (p=0.046).
The remaining 24 SNPs showed no association. None of the three nominally
significant SNPs would survive a Bonferroni correction for 27 tests
(threshold ~0.0019). No robust pharmacogenomic SNP signal.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
7. SUPPORTED VS REFUTED HYPOTHESES
----------------------------------------------------------------------

SUPPORTED (significant, direction matched the hypothesis):
 - All "classic prognostic factor" hypotheses (ecog_ps down -> pfs down,
   stage_iv -> pfs down, albumin -> pfs up, ldh -> pfs down, crp -> pfs
   down, nlr -> pfs down, hgb -> pfs up, weight loss -> pfs down,
   cea -> pfs down, pain -> pfs down, fatigue -> pfs down, appetite loss
   -> pfs down, liver_mets -> pfs down, bone_mets -> pfs down,
   adrenal_mets -> pfs down, pleural_effusion -> pfs down,
   chronic_kidney_disease -> pfs down, heart_failure -> pfs down,
   prior_malignancy -> pfs down, right_sided_primary -> pfs down,
   kras_mutation -> pfs down (small), braf_v600e -> pfs down (small),
   prior_lines_of_therapy -> pfs down, age_years -> pfs down).
 - Negative regorafenib main effect (consistent with indication
   confounding).

REFUTED / NOT SUPPORTED (failed to reach significance, or wrong sign):
 - cetuximab x kras_mutation interaction.
 - pembrolizumab x msi_high interaction.
 - encorafenib x braf_v600e interaction.
 - trastuzumab/tucatinib x her2_amplified interaction.
 - cetuximab x right_sided_primary interaction.
 - All four matched-target subgroup benefit hypotheses (cetuximab in
   KRAS-WT, pembrolizumab in MSI-high, encorafenib in BRAF V600E,
   trastuzumab/tucatinib in HER2-amp).
 - msi_high as a marginal prognostic factor (no overall pfs difference).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
8. OVERALL CONCLUSION
----------------------------------------------------------------------

In this 50,000-patient EHR-derived CRC cohort:

 * PFS is strongly governed by classic clinical prognostic factors --
   performance status, stage, albumin, LDH, inflammatory markers
   (CRP, NLR), hemoglobin, weight loss, CEA, symptom burden, and
   metastatic distribution. These effects are robust to multivariable
   adjustment and are highly statistically significant.

 * The expected CRC pharmacogenomic relationships (cetuximab benefit
   confined to KRAS wild-type / left-sided; pembrolizumab benefit
   confined to MSI-high; encorafenib benefit confined to BRAF V600E;
   trastuzumab/tucatinib benefit confined to HER2-amplified) were NOT
   observed. With this very large sample size, the absence of
   significance for biomarker-treatment interactions is informative:
   either the matched-treatment benefits are absent / attenuated in this
   real-world dataset, or selection / labeling mechanisms (which-line,
   when, who-gets-what) are masking them.

 * The strongest treatment-related signal is a NEGATIVE association
   between regorafenib and pfs, almost certainly explained by indication
   (use in late-line, heavily pretreated, more advanced patients).

 * SNP screen yielded no robust pharmacogenomic associations after
   multiple-testing correction.

 * Sociodemographic effects on pfs were small.

Net: prognostic biology dominates the PFS outcome in this cohort;
canonical biomarker-treatment interactions were tested with adequate
power but did not appear in the data.
