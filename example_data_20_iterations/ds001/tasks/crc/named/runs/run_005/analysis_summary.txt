Analysis Summary -- ds001_crc (50,000 patients, outcome: pfs_months)
================================================================================

Across 25 iterations of propose-test-refine, we examined main effects of treatments,
clinical covariates, biomarkers, comorbidities, sociodemographics, symptom burden,
treatment-by-biomarker interactions, molecular co-alterations, a SNP screen, and
adjusted multivariable models. Each test reported a signed effect on pfs_months
(months) and a p-value; significance below uses p < 0.05.

ITERATION 1 -- Treatment main effects on PFS (Welch t-tests)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
- treatment_cetuximab: diff -0.04 months, p=0.088 -- NOT significant.
- treatment_bevacizumab: diff -0.02, p=0.354 -- NOT significant.
- treatment_pembrolizumab: diff +0.005, p=0.86 -- NOT significant.
- treatment_encorafenib: diff +0.018, p=0.61 -- NOT significant.

  effect: regorafenib-treated patients had ~1 month longer mean PFS than untreated.

================================================================================
OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

1) The dominant treatment-effect signal in this 50,000-patient CRC cohort is a
   POSITIVE ~1-month PFS advantage for treatment_regorafenib (p~10^-216), present
   both overall and within the heavily pretreated (>=2 prior lines) subgroup.
   This is large and somewhat surprising relative to typical real-world
   experience, where regorafenib in late-line use produces only modest PFS gains.
   Either treatment assignment in this dataset correlates with favorable
   prognostic factors (a confounder pattern), or the data-generating process
   embedded a true protective effect.

2) The canonical biomarker-targeted therapy effect modifications expected from
   CRC oncology -- pembrolizumab benefiting MSI-H, encorafenib benefiting BRAF
   V600E, trastuzumab/tucatinib benefiting HER2-amplified, cetuximab benefiting
   KRAS WT (and especially left-sided KRAS WT) -- are ALL ABSENT in this dataset.
   Every relevant biomarker x treatment interaction is non-significant, both
   unadjusted and after adjustment for ECOG/stage/age/albumin/LDH/sex/sidedness.
   None of the within-biomarker subgroup contrasts achieved significance.

3) Strong prognostic main effects (independent of treatment) are present and
   match clinical expectation in direction:
     ECOG PS         -1.19 months / unit       (negative)
     Stage IV        -1.35 months              (negative)
     Albumin         +0.47 months / g/dL       (positive)
     Weight loss %   -0.073 months / %         (negative)
     CEA             -0.005 months / ng/mL     (negative, small)
     LDH             -0.0003 months / U/L      (negative, very small)
     KRAS mutation   -0.33 months              (negative)
     BRAF V600E      -0.23 months              (negative)
     Right-sided     -0.30 months              (negative)

4) Several findings are clinically counterintuitive in direction and warrant
   note:
     - age_years: +0.18 months per year of age (older -> longer PFS).
     - nras_mutation=1: +0.22 months longer PFS than NRAS WT.
   These suggest the data-generating process embedded a non-canonical pattern
   for these factors.

5) Comorbidity burden, metastatic site indicators (liver/bone/adrenal/effusions),
   sociodemographic factors (race, insurance, rural, education), symptom-grade
   PROs, smoking, BMI, prior therapy lines, and most molecular co-alterations
   (TP53, PIK3CA, PTEN loss, CDKN2A loss, FGFR alteration) showed no detectable
   association with pfs_months at the population level in this cohort.

6) The SNP screen (28 SNPs) yielded 3 nominally significant hits at alpha=0.05
   (rs1801131, rs1050828, rs1801197), consistent with chance expectation under
   the null; none survived multiple-testing correction.

NET TAKEAWAY: Across 25 iterations and ~130 statistical tests, the cohort
displays well-behaved, classically-signed prognostic relationships for
performance status, stage, albumin, weight loss, KRAS, BRAF and sidedness,
but the canonical biomarker-targeted-therapy interactions that define modern
metastatic-CRC management are NOT detectable in these data. Regorafenib is
the only treatment with a robust population-level PFS association, and that
association is large, positive, and surprising.
