Analysis summary — ds001_crc (n = 50,000)
=========================================

Outcome: pfs_months (median 4.21, IQR 2.72–5.74, range 0.0–16.3).
Approach: 25 iterations of hypothesis-driven OLS regression (and Welch t-tests for
subgroup contrasts), with each iteration refining toward biomarker × treatment
interactions, multivariable adjustment, and composite phenotypes.

1) Robust baseline prognostic main effects
-------------------------------------------
The following features had clear, large, and statistically significant
associations with progression-free survival in the expected direction:

  - ECOG performance status: each +1 unit -> -1.19 months PFS (p ≈ 0).
  - Stage IV: -1.35 months versus non-stage IV (p ≈ 0).
  - Albumin: each +1 g/dL -> +0.47 months (p < 1e-100).
  - Weight loss (% in 6 months): each +1% -> -0.073 months (p < 1e-150).
  - LDH: each +1 U/L -> -0.00032 months (p = 0.013).
  - CEA: each +1 ng/mL -> -0.0053 months (p ≈ 1e-17).
  - Alkaline phosphatase: each +1 U/L -> -0.0006 months (p = 0.004).
  - Right-sided primary: -0.31 months versus left (p ≈ 5e-46), the textbook
    sidedness signal in CRC.
  - KRAS mutation: -0.33 months (p ≈ 9e-56), classic poor-prognostic effect.
  - BRAF V600E: -0.23 months unadjusted (p ≈ 4e-6); after adjustment for ECOG,
    albumin, LDH, stage, the adjusted BRAF V600E effect is -0.17 months
    (p = 2.7e-4) — robustly worse PFS, again consistent with real-world CRC.

A surprise direction-of-effect: age_years had a positive coefficient
(+0.18 months/year, p ≈ 0). In this synthetic cohort, older patients have
LONGER PFS — opposite to typical clinical expectation. This was confirmed in
both univariate and the full multivariable model.

NRAS mutation also moved opposite the conventional direction: NRAS-mut
patients had +0.22 months PFS (p = 2.3e-4). Both age and NRAS findings should
be treated as cohort-specific quirks rather than confirming canonical prognostic
biology.

2) The single dominant treatment effect: regorafenib
----------------------------------------------------
Regorafenib was the only treatment with a clear main effect on PFS:
+0.97 months (p ≈ 0). The effect persisted essentially unchanged after
adjustment for prior_lines_of_therapy (+0.97, p ≈ 0) and in the full
multivariable model (+0.94, p ≈ 0). It did NOT depend on prior_lines depth
(interaction p = 0.61), so the benefit appears uniform across pretreatment.

3) Canonical biomarker × treatment interactions were NOT recovered
------------------------------------------------------------------
A central goal was to test the four predictive-biomarker hypotheses that
define modern CRC therapy:

  - Cetuximab × RAS-WT (canonical: cetuximab works only in RAS-WT,
    especially left-sided RAS-WT): NS. Two-way interaction p = 0.71;
    three-way cet × right_sided × ras_wt p = 0.94; even in the canonical
    favorable subgroup (left-sided RAS-WT), Δpfs = -0.06 mo (p = 0.13).
    After multivariable adjustment, cet:ras_wt:left_sided still NS (p = 0.62).
    No selective benefit was detectable.

  - Pembrolizumab × MSI-high (canonical: large PFS gain in MSI-H, the FDA-label
    population): NS. Two-way interaction p = 0.99; pembrolizumab in the
    MSI-high subgroup Δpfs = +0.007 mo (p = 0.96); adjusted interaction
    p = 0.45. The MSI-high main effect itself was also NS (-0.02, p = 0.68),
    further suggesting MSI status does not stratify outcomes here.

  - Encorafenib × BRAF V600E (canonical: encorafenib targeted at BRAF V600E):
    Two-way interaction p = 0.38 unadjusted; after multivariable adjustment
    the interaction strengthened to p = 0.073 with the expected sign
    (encorafenib coef in BRAF-mut vs BRAF-WT differs by -0.25 months).
    Suggestive but not significant.

  - Trastuzumab+tucatinib × HER2-amplified (canonical: targeted to HER2-amp):
    Interaction p = 0.78 unadjusted, p = 0.38 adjusted. Direction matched the
    expectation (HER2-amp-only benefit), but the trial-arm size (~8% T+T
    treated, ~3% HER2-amp) limits power and the signal is not detectable.

Bevacizumab also showed no PFS effect, alone (p = 0.35), by tumor side
(interaction p = 0.61), or after multivariable adjustment (p = 0.49).

4) Several conventional prognosticators were absent or null
-----------------------------------------------------------
Despite >50,000-patient power, a number of variables that are typically
prognostic in real CRC populations did NOT show measurable associations:

  - Liver metastases (p = 0.52), bone metastases (p = 0.22), pleural,
    pericardial, contralateral-lung, and adrenal mets — all NS.
  - Symptom grades (fatigue, pain NRS, dyspnea, cough, appetite loss) —
    all NS, all coefficients essentially zero.
  - Comorbidities (CKD, heart failure, COPD, AF, autoimmune disease,
    HIV-positive, hepatitis B/C, prior malignancy, depression/anxiety,
    interstitial lung disease) — all NS.
  - Hemoglobin (p = 0.08), calcium (p = 0.11), platelets (p = 0.73), NLR
    alone (p = 0.29), CRP alone (p = 0.06) — all NS at α = 0.05.
  - Sex_female: NS (p = 0.77).
  - BMI (p = 0.16), smoking pack-years (p = 0.91), education years (p = 0.34) — NS.

5) No race / insurance / rural disparities detected
----------------------------------------------------
With 50,000 patients, all four race/ethnicity coefficients (vs white reference)
were NS (smallest p = 0.19 for Black). All three insurance categories
(vs private) were NS (smallest p = 0.09 for Medicaid, trend toward shorter
PFS but not significant). Rural residence was NS. The dataset shows no
detectable demographic disparity in PFS.

6) SNPs behave as expected under the global null
-------------------------------------------------
Of 27 candidate SNPs screened individually, 3 reached unadjusted p < 0.05
(snp_rs1801131, snp_rs1050828, snp_rs1801197). After Bonferroni correction
for 27 tests (alpha = 0.0019), NONE survive. Number of nominal hits (3/27 ≈ 11%)
is only marginally above the 5% expected by chance and consistent with the
SNP panel being noise.

7) Composite phenotypes can amplify weak signals
-------------------------------------------------
A pre-specified composite "high systemic inflammation" phenotype
(NLR > 3 AND albumin < 3.5 g/dL) — combining two markers that were
individually weak (NLR p = 0.29; albumin highly significant alone) — produced
a strong signal: -0.34 months PFS (p ≈ 1.8e-27). This shows that the
inflammation × nutrition synergy carries information that is hidden when
either marker is regressed alone.

8) Final multivariable model
----------------------------
A full multivariable OLS combining the prognostic main effects, treatments,
and the four biomarker × treatment interactions explained R² = 0.86 of
variance in pfs_months. Significant terms were: ECOG (-1.17), stage IV
(-1.36), albumin (+0.47), LDH (-0.00042), weight loss (-0.076), age (+0.18),
CEA (-0.005), right-sided primary (-0.29), BRAF V600E (-0.36), KRAS
mutation (-0.36), regorafenib (+0.94). All four biomarker × treatment
interaction terms were NS in the adjusted model, confirming Section 3.

Bottom-line conclusions about treatment-biomarker-outcome relationships
=======================================================================
- This cohort is dominated by clinical/laboratory prognostic signal:
  ECOG, stage, albumin, weight loss, age, KRAS, BRAF, side, CEA, LDH together
  explain the bulk of PFS variance.
- The only treatment with detectable PFS impact is regorafenib (+~1 month,
  uniform across subgroups).
- The four canonical predictive biomarker–drug pairs that drive modern CRC
  therapy in the real world (RAS-WT cetuximab, MSI-H pembrolizumab,
  BRAF-V600E encorafenib, HER2-amp trastuzumab+tucatinib) were NOT recovered
  here. Encorafenib × BRAF V600E is the closest miss (adjusted p ≈ 0.07,
  expected direction); the others have either null or wrong-sign effects.
  This is a notable limitation of this synthetic cohort relative to known
  oncology pharmacology.
- Composite biomarker scores (e.g., NLR > 3 AND albumin < 3.5) can surface
  signal that single markers miss.
- Sociodemographic features and most comorbidities show no detectable
  PFS association in this dataset.

Caveats
-------
All p-values are unadjusted within iterations; iterations 6 (8 mutations),
16 (12 comorbidities), 17 (8 sociodemographic terms), and 18 (27 SNPs)
involve multiple testing not corrected here. Direction-of-effect anomalies
(age, NRAS, hemoglobin, regorafenib magnitude, missing canonical
predictive interactions) suggest the dataset was either generated from a
simplified model that emphasises baseline severity over modern targeted
therapy, or includes unobserved confounding by indication.
