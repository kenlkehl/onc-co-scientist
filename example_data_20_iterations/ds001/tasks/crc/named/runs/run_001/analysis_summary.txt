Analysis summary — ds001_crc (n = 50,000)
==========================================

Outcome: progression-free survival in months (pfs_months); cohort mean ≈ 4.31 mo
(SD 2.29). All analyses used ordinary t-tests, chi-square tests, or
ordinary-least-squares regression with optional interaction terms. The 25
iterations below moved from prognostic main effects, to canonical predictive
biomarker–treatment interactions, to subgroup and adjusted models, and finally
to a comprehensive multivariable model.

1. Prognostic main effects (iterations 1, 2, 7, 8, 10)
------------------------------------------------------
The strongest prognostic signals were entirely consistent with general
oncology biology:

* stage_iv = 1 vs 0: PFS −1.35 mo (p≈0; supports h1_stageiv).
* ecog_ps higher: −1.19 mo per unit (p≈0; supports h1_ecog).
* albumin_g_dl higher: +0.47 mo per g/dL (p=3e-115; supports h2_alb).
* weight_loss_pct_6mo higher: −0.073 mo per % (p=2e-167; supports h2_wl).
* cea_ng_ml higher: −0.0053 mo per ng/mL (p=3e-17).
* ldh_u_l higher: −0.0003 mo per U/L (p=0.013).
* age_years: +0.18 mo per year (p≈0). The DIRECTION is opposite to the
  conventional clinical expectation — older patients had longer PFS in this
  cohort. Refutes h8_age (which posited a negative age effect).
* No effect: liver_mets, bone_mets, hemoglobin, CRP, NLR, fatigue/pain/dyspnea/
  appetite/cough grades, diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart failure, CAD,
  AFib, VTE, prior chemo/radiation/surgery/immuno/targeted, prior_lines_of_therapy,
  years_since_diagnosis, prior_malignancy, sex, BMI, smoking pack-years, vitals
  (SBP, HR, SpO2), rural residence, education years.

2. Genomic biomarker prognostic effects (iteration 3)
------------------------------------------------------
* kras_mutation = 1 vs 0: PFS −0.33 mo (p=2e-58). Supports h3_kras.
* braf_v600e = 1 vs 0: PFS −0.23 mo (p=3e-7). Supports h3_braf.
* right_sided_primary = 1 vs 0: PFS −0.31 mo (p=1e-49). Supports h3_right.
* nras_mutation = 1 vs 0: PFS +0.22 mo (p=7e-4). DIRECTION OPPOSITE the
  hypothesized prognostic harm (h3_nras posited shorter PFS; observed longer).
  Note small N (1,506) and small effect; possibly noise.
* msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion: no detectable main effect on PFS.

3. Treatment main effects, raw (iteration 4)
--------------------------------------------
* treatment_regorafenib: +0.97 mo (p=2e-216). The ONLY treatment with a clear
  raw main effect. Supports h4_rego.
* No raw effect for cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, or
  trastuzumab+tucatinib (all |Δ| < 0.05 mo, p > 0.08).

4. Canonical biomarker × treatment interactions — ABSENT (iterations 5, 6)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
This was the most striking negative finding. None of the canonical CRC
predictive biomarker–treatment relationships were detectable:

* cetuximab × kras_mutation: interaction p = 0.665 (not significant).
  Refutes h5_cetux_kras.
* cetuximab × nras_mutation: p = 0.96 (NS). Refutes h5_cetux_nras.
* cetuximab × braf_v600e: p = 0.81 (NS). Refutes h5_cetux_braf.
* pembrolizumab × msi_high: p = 0.99 (NS). Refutes h5_pembro_msi.
* encorafenib × braf_v600e: p = 0.38 (NS). Refutes h5_enc_braf.
* trastuzumab_tucatinib × her2_amplified: p = 0.78 (NS). Refutes h5_tt_her2.

Stratified subgroup analyses (iteration 6) gave the same answer. In particular:
  - Cetuximab in KRAS-WT: Δ = −0.05 mo, p = 0.12 (no benefit).
  - Pembrolizumab in MSI-H: Δ = +0.007 mo (n=386 vs 2,127), p = 0.96.
  - Encorafenib in BRAF V600E: Δ = −0.13 mo, p = 0.33.
  - Trastuzumab+tucatinib in HER2-amplified: Δ = +0.02 mo, p = 0.93.

This was probed further in iteration 13 (cetuximab in KRAS/NRAS/BRAF triple
wild-type: Δ = −0.05 mo, p = 0.17), iteration 12 (cetuximab × right-sided
primary interaction p = 0.58; in left-sided RAS-WT Δ = −0.06, p = 0.13),
iteration 15 (encorafenib × cetuximab interaction within BRAF V600E: p = 0.32;
combo arm n=58, mean 4.15 mo vs neither n=1,390, mean 4.14 mo), and iteration
21 (BRAF V600E combo enc+cetux vs neither: Δ = +0.012, p = 0.97).

Take-away: in this cohort, no combination of stratification or interaction
modeling rescued the predictive-biomarker hypothesis for any of the four
"matched" therapy–biomarker pairs.

5. Covariate-adjusted treatment effects (iteration 11)
------------------------------------------------------
After adjusting for age, ECOG, stage_iv, albumin, LDH, and liver_mets:

* treatment_regorafenib: +0.94 mo (p≈0). The only adjusted treatment effect
  that remains. Supports h11_rego_adj.
* All other treatments: |β| ≤ 0.02 mo, p > 0.29. The earlier near-zero raw
  effects for cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, and TT survive
  adjustment. Refutes h11_cetux_adj/h11_bev_adj/h11_pembro_adj/h11_enc_adj/h11_tt_adj.

6. Tumor-laterality, MSI-H adjusted, HER2-A adjusted (iterations 12, 18, 19)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
* MSI-H, adjusted for age/ECOG/stage/albumin/LDH: pembro β = +0.04, p = 0.43
  (no benefit). Refutes h18_pembro_msi_adj.
* HER2-amplified, adjusted: TT β = +0.005, p = 0.96. Refutes h19_tt_her2_adj.
* Cetuximab × right_sided_primary: interaction p = 0.58. Refutes h12_cetux_lat.

7. Targeted-treatment delivery audit (iteration 24)
---------------------------------------------------
None of the four "matched" treatments are channelled to their biomarker-positive
subgroups any more than to their biomarker-negative counterparts:

* pembrolizumab in MSI-H vs MSI-L: 15.8% vs 15.3%, chi² p = 0.55.
* encorafenib in BRAF V600E vs WT: 10.7% vs 10.2%, p = 0.51.
* trastuzumab+tucatinib in HER2-amp vs not: 8.0% vs 8.1%, p = 0.94.
* cetuximab in KRAS-mut vs WT: 29.9% vs 30.3% (Δ = −0.4 pp), p = 0.38.

This pattern is internally consistent with the absence of any biomarker–treatment
interaction in iterations 5/6/12/15/18/19/21: treatments were assigned essentially
independently of biomarker status. Refutes h24_pembro_audit, h24_enc_audit,
h24_tt_audit, h24_cetux_audit.

Likewise, pembrolizumab use in MSI-H did not differ across insurance categories
(p = 0.34, iteration 20). Refutes h20_pembro_ins.

8. SNP scan (iteration 14)
--------------------------
Across 27 SNPs entered as 0/1/2 dose into single-marker OLS, three reached
nominal p < 0.05 (rs1801131 β = −0.05, p = 0.027; rs1050828 β = +0.09,
p = 0.015; rs1801197 β = −0.05, p = 0.047). After Bonferroni correction
(α = 0.05/27 ≈ 0.00185), none survive. Most consistent with chance findings.

9. Higher-order interactions and the comprehensive model (iterations 22, 23)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* ecog_ps × stage_iv: interaction p = 0.019, β = +0.06 mo. The negative ECOG
  effect is slightly attenuated in stage IV. Nominally supports h22_ecog_stage.
* Multivariable OLS on 24 covariates (iteration 23): R² = 0.857, n = 50,000.
  The significant terms (ranked by p) are the same prognostic features
  identified in iterations 1–4 plus regorafenib: ecog_ps (β=−1.17), stage_iv
  (β=−1.36), albumin (β=+0.47), weight_loss (β=−0.076), age_years (β=+0.18),
  kras_mutation (β=−0.36), right_sided_primary (β=−0.29), braf_v600e (β=−0.36),
  ldh (β=−0.0004), and treatment_regorafenib (β=+0.94). All other treatments
  have |β| < 0.03 mo and p > 0.05. Supports h23_multivar.

10. Other negative findings worth flagging
------------------------------------------
* bevacizumab × right-sided (h16_bev_lat) and × kras_mutation (h16_bev_kras):
  both NS.
* regorafenib × prior_lines_of_therapy (h17): NS — regorafenib's effect is not
  stronger or weaker as a function of line of therapy.
* pembrolizumab × prior_immunotherapy within MSI-H (h25): NS.

Overall conclusions
===================
1. The prognostic structure of this cohort is conventional. Stage IV, higher
   ECOG, lower albumin, more weight loss, KRAS or BRAF V600E mutation,
   right-sided primary, and higher LDH/CEA all independently shorten PFS, with
   effect sizes and signs matching the published literature. The age effect,
   however, is positive (older = longer PFS), which is unusual.

2. Of the six administered therapies, only treatment_regorafenib has a real
   PFS effect (≈ +0.94 mo, robust to adjustment, p ≈ 0).

3. None of the four canonical predictive biomarker–treatment relationships
   (cetuximab benefit confined to RAS/RAF wild-type, pembrolizumab benefit in
   MSI-high, encorafenib benefit in BRAF V600E, trastuzumab+tucatinib benefit
   in HER2-amplified) were detectable, whether tested as interaction terms,
   stratified subgroups, or covariate-adjusted within-subgroup models. There
   is no detectable enrichment of these therapies among biomarker-positive
   patients either, which is consistent with that absence: in this cohort the
   five biomarker-targeted agents appear to have been distributed
   approximately at random with respect to their indications, and they do not
   move PFS in either direction. The cohort therefore behaves like one in
   which targeted therapies are clinically inert and only regorafenib carries
   a measurable on-treatment benefit.

4. SNP single-marker associations were unremarkable (no Bonferroni-significant
   hits across 27 candidates).

5. A comprehensive multivariable OLS achieves R² ≈ 0.86 using only the
   prognostic features and regorafenib treatment status — confirming that
   essentially all explainable PFS variance in this dataset is captured by
   patient/disease prognostic factors plus regorafenib.
