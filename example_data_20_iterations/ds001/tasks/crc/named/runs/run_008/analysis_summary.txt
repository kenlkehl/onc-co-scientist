Analysis Summary — ds001_crc
=================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with colorectal cancer (CRC) drawn from a commercial EHR aggregator. Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival, months). Twenty-five iterations of hypothesis generation and statistical testing were run; 120 hypotheses were proposed and 120 individual analyses executed.

Overall framing
---------------
Across the cohort, mean pfs_months is 4.31 (SD 2.29). I systematically tested (a) demographic and prognostic main effects, (b) tumor-biology main effects, (c) treatment main effects, (d) the canonical CRC biomarker–treatment predictive interactions, (e) lab/symptom/comorbidity main effects, (f) heterogeneity of treatment effects across age, ECOG, sidedness, and biomarkers, and (g) demographic equity questions. The biggest, most reproducible findings concentrate in three buckets: (1) classic prognostic factors behave as expected with one striking exception (age), (2) only one of the six listed treatments shows a population-level efficacy signal, and (3) that one treatment’s benefit is dramatically heterogeneous across tumor biology.

Iterations 1–4 (foundation: demographics, prognosis, biomarkers, treatments)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 1 examined demographics. sex_female, race_ethnicity, insurance_type, and rural_residence all showed no meaningful association with pfs_months (all p > 0.2; race spread <0.15 mo across 5 groups). The single striking finding was age_years: Pearson r = +0.766 with pfs_months (p ≈ 0). Older patients had MUCH longer PFS. Iteration 2 confirmed expected prognostic effects: ecog_ps drops PFS by ~2.4 mo from 0→2 (p≈0); stage_iv reduces PFS by 1.35 mo (p≈0); prior_lines_of_therapy and years_since_diagnosis had null effects. Iteration 3 tested primary biomarkers: right_sided_primary, kras_mutation, and braf_v600e were each significantly associated with shorter PFS (−0.31, −0.33, −0.23 mo respectively, all p<1e-6). nras_mutation was paradoxically associated with LONGER PFS (+0.22 mo, p=7e-4) on univariate testing. msi_high, her2_amplified, and ntrk_fusion were null. Iteration 4 tested unadjusted treatment main effects on PFS: cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, and trastuzumab_tucatinib all showed essentially zero unadjusted effect (|diff| < 0.05 mo, all p>0.08). Only treatment_regorafenib stood out, with a +0.97 mo unadjusted PFS difference (p ≈ 1.7e-216). The combination of these four iterations sets the stage: regorafenib looks real, the other targeted therapies do not show their expected efficacy.

Iterations 5–9 (canonical CRC biomarker–treatment predictive interactions)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
I tested all four canonical CRC predictive pairings:
- Cetuximab × KRAS (iteration 5): interaction coef = +0.023, p = 0.26 (NS). Within KRAS-WT, cetuximab vs none: −0.05 mo (p=0.12). Within KRAS-mutant, similar (−0.03, p=0.38). The biology that defines anti-EGFR use in CRC is NOT recapitulated here.
- Cetuximab × BRAF and Cetuximab × sidedness (iteration 6): both interactions NS (p=0.96 and p=0.58). Within left-sided KRAS-WT (the canonical anti-EGFR-responsive subset), cetuximab still showed no benefit (−0.06 mo, p=0.11).
- Pembrolizumab × MSI-high (iteration 7): interaction coef = +0.031, p = 0.60. Within MSI-H patients (n=2,513), pembrolizumab vs none: +0.007 mo (p=0.96). The single biggest established CRC biomarker-driven indication is essentially flat in this cohort.
- Encorafenib × BRAF V600E (iteration 8): interaction coef = +0.003, p = 0.97. Within BRAF V600E-mutant patients, encorafenib was numerically WORSE (−0.13 mo, p=0.33).
- Trastuzumab/tucatinib × HER2 amplification (iteration 9): interaction coef = +0.007, p = 0.94. Within HER2-amplified patients, the regimen showed no benefit.

The clean pattern across iterations 5–9: **none** of the four canonical biomarker-driven predictive interactions in CRC are detectable in this dataset, despite 50,000 patients providing extreme power to detect even small effects. Iteration 25 quantified this formally: 0 of 4 predictive interactions reach p<0.05.

Iterations 10–13 (laboratory and clinical biology main effects)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 10 (labs): albumin_g_dl was strongly positive (r=+0.10, p≈3e-115); weight_loss_pct_6mo strongly negative (r=−0.12, p≈2e-167); cea_ng_ml mildly negative (r=−0.038, p≈3e-17); ldh_u_l (r=−0.011, p=0.013) and alkaline_phosphatase (r=−0.013, p=0.004) significant but tiny; nlr, crp_mg_l, and hemoglobin_g_dl were all NS. Iteration 11 (comorbidities — diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart failure, CAD, AF, VTE, autoimmune, depression/anxiety, prior malignancy): every single comorbidity tested was non-significant (all p>0.05; depression/anxiety came closest at p=0.053). Iteration 12 (symptoms): all five PRO grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite loss) showed null Pearson r with PFS — somewhat surprising given the strong albumin/weight-loss signal. Iteration 13 (metastatic site burden — liver, bone, adrenal, pleural effusion, pericardial effusion, contralateral lung mets): every site indicator was non-significant. The clean message: among biological/clinical features, only ECOG, stage IV, age, albumin, and weight loss carry signal; symptom grades, comorbidities, and met-site burden look like noise variables in this cohort.

Iteration 14 (SNP scan)
-----------------------
Of ~30 snp_* variables tested as ordinal genotype dose by Pearson correlation against pfs_months, ~1 reached nominal p<0.05 — exactly what would be expected by chance under the null. There is no evidence of a germline genetic prognostic signal in these data.

Iteration 15 (multivariable adjusted treatment effects)
-------------------------------------------------------
I adjusted treatment main effects for ecog_ps, stage_iv, and age_years simultaneously. Treatment-regorafenib coefficient = +0.94 mo (p ≈ 0). Every other treatment coefficient |coef|<0.02 mo with p>0.27. Adjustment confirms the unadjusted pattern: only regorafenib has a population-level association with PFS.

Iterations 16–18 (treatment-effect heterogeneity for the only “working” drug, plus age/ECOG)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 16: regorafenib’s benefit interacts strongly with kras_mutation (interaction coef = −1.62, p≈0; i.e., the benefit is much smaller — essentially absent — in KRAS-mutant patients) and modestly with ecog_ps (coef = −0.06, p=2.5e-4). It does not significantly interact with stage_iv or age_years. In ECOG-2 patients alone, regorafenib still gives +0.90 mo (p<1e-33). Iteration 17 (age × treatment) and Iteration 18 (ECOG × treatment) interactions for cetuximab, bevacizumab, and pembrolizumab were all NS (every p>0.25). So among treatments, only regorafenib shows meaningful effect-modification, and it is driven by KRAS status (and to a lesser extent ECOG) — the opposite biological direction from cetuximab’s expected RAS-WT benefit.

Iteration 19 (refining the NRAS direction-of-effect anomaly)
------------------------------------------------------------
The univariate NRAS+→longer-PFS finding from iteration 3 disappeared after multivariable adjustment for kras, braf, ecog, stage, and age (adjusted coef = +0.019, p=0.49). Within KRAS-WT patients only, NRAS+ vs NRAS− differed by +0.08 mo, p=0.21. The NRAS univariate signal was confounding (collinearity with KRAS-WT) and is not a real prognostic effect. By contrast, the adjusted negative coefficients for kras_mutation (−0.35, p≈3e-297), braf_v600e (−0.35, p≈6e-57), and right_sided_primary (−0.28, p≈9e-195) are robust real prognostic effects.

Iteration 20 (equity / treatment receipt)
-----------------------------------------
Receipt of regorafenib and pembrolizumab did not differ significantly across race_ethnicity (chi-sq p=0.55, p=0.45) or insurance_type (p=0.59 for regorafenib). Adjusted analysis of insurance_type vs PFS showed no meaningful differences across groups (uninsured vs medicaid: −0.046 mo, p=0.084). I did not find evidence of disparate treatment access or disparate outcomes by race or insurance in this dataset.

Iteration 21 (comprehensive multivariable model)
------------------------------------------------
A single OLS with ~25 predictors confirmed: age_years coef = +0.18 mo per year (p≈0); albumin_g_dl coef = +0.47 (p≈0); weight_loss_pct_6mo coef = −0.08 per percentage point (p≈0); treatment_regorafenib coef = +0.94 (p≈0). Other treatment coefficients remained near zero.

Iteration 22 (bevacizumab subgroup hypotheses)
----------------------------------------------
Bevacizumab × KRAS, × BRAF, × sidedness interactions all NS. Bevacizumab in KRAS+ subgroup or in right-sided subgroup: NS (coefs essentially zero). Bevacizumab is uninformative throughout.

Iteration 23 (regorafenib subgroup characterization)
----------------------------------------------------
The most biologically interesting finding of the whole exploration. Within KRAS-WT patients (n≈29,000), regorafenib gives +1.66 mo PFS (p≈6e-293). Within left-sided primary patients, +1.48 mo (p≈4e-274). Within MSI-H, +0.95 mo (p≈8e-12). Within HER2-amp, +0.87 mo (p≈7e-7). But within KRAS-mutant patients, +0.027 mo (p=0.46) — essentially nothing. Within right-sided primary patients, +0.026 mo (p=0.52) — essentially nothing. So while regorafenib is the only drug with a measurable population-level PFS signal, its benefit is concentrated in the KRAS-wild-type AND/OR left-sided population — biologically opposite to where regorafenib is typically indicated in clinical practice (it is generally a refractory-line salvage agent without selection by sidedness/RAS).

Iteration 24 (biomarker prognostic effect within and outside relevant treatment arms)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
KRAS+ shortens PFS in both cetuximab-treated (−0.31 mo, p≈1e-17) and untreated (−0.33 mo, p≈1e-42) patients — same magnitude in both arms, confirming KRAS is purely prognostic, not predictive of cetuximab response. BRAF+ shortens PFS in encorafenib-untreated patients (−0.21, p≈8e-6). MSI-H has no effect on PFS in pembrolizumab-untreated patients (−0.02, p=0.70). This further supports that KRAS, BRAF, and sidedness are real PROGNOSTIC factors, while MSI-H here is neither prognostic nor predictive.

Iteration 25 (final synthesis)
------------------------------
Five formal synthesis hypotheses, each tested:
1. Only regorafenib has a significant adjusted treatment effect — SUPPORTED (sig_treats = ['treatment_regorafenib']; coef +0.935, p≈0).
2. None of the four canonical biomarker-treatment predictive interactions reach p<0.05 — SUPPORTED (0 of 4; worst p=0.97).
3. age_years has the largest standardized beta among continuous predictors — SUPPORTED (|beta|=1.75 vs next predictor 0.62).
4. KRAS, BRAF, right-sidedness retain negative prognostic associations after adjustment — SUPPORTED (all p<1e-50).
5. sex, race_ethnicity, insurance_type, rural_residence have no meaningful adjusted associations — SUPPORTED (all p>0.05; worst NS).

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
SUPPORTED (consistent with statistical evidence):
- Older age associated with longer PFS (h1_age) — direction is opposite to typical clinical reality but the data say so unambiguously
- Higher ECOG, Stage IV, more weight loss, lower albumin associated with shorter PFS (h2_ecog, h2_stage, h10_alb, h10_wl)
- Right-sided primary, KRAS, BRAF associated with shorter PFS as prognostic factors (h3_side, h3_kras, h3_braf, h19_kras_adj, h19_braf_adj, h19_side_adj, h24_kras_in_no_cetux, h24_braf_in_no_enco, h25_kras_braf_side_prognostic)
- Regorafenib has a positive PFS association overall and in the multivariable model (h4_treatment_regorafenib, h15_adj_reg, h21_reg_full, h25_only_reg_works)
- Regorafenib’s benefit is entirely concentrated in KRAS-WT and left-sided subgroups (h16_reg_kras_int, h23_reg_in_left, h23_reg_in_kraswt; null in h23_reg_in_right and h23_reg_in_kras)

REFUTED (no statistical support for the hypothesized effect):
- Sex, race, insurance, rural residence — no PFS association (h1_sex, h1_race, h1_ins, h1_rural, h25_demographics_null)
- Cetuximab × KRAS predictive interaction (h5_cetux_kras_int and the krasWT/krasMUT subgroup pair)
- Cetuximab × BRAF and Cetuximab × sidedness interactions (h6_cetux_braf_int, h6_cetux_side_int, h6_cetux_in_left, h6_cetux_in_right, h6_cetux_rasWT_leftsided)
- Pembrolizumab × MSI-high interaction; pembro benefit in MSI-H (h7_pembro_msi_int, h7_pembro_in_msih)
- Encorafenib × BRAF interaction; enco benefit in BRAF+ (h8_enco_braf_int, h8_enco_in_braf)
- Trastuzumab/tucatinib × HER2 interaction (h9_her2tx_int, h9_her2tx_in_her2)
- Bevacizumab efficacy and any of its interactions (h22_*)
- Comorbidities and PRO symptom grades vs PFS (h11_*, h12_*)
- Mets sites vs PFS (h13_*)
- SNP main effects (h14_any_snp)
- NRAS positive direction after adjustment (h19_nras_adj — original h3_nras univariate finding was confounded)
- MSI-H prognostic effect (h24_msi_in_no_pembro)

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. **Prognostic structure is mostly canonical** — high ECOG, Stage IV, low albumin, weight loss, KRAS mutation, BRAF V600E, right-sided primary all correctly identify worse PFS, with very large signal-to-noise given n=50,000. The single anomaly is age, which carries an extraordinary positive association (older = longer PFS).
2. **Predictive biomarker–treatment relationships are absent**. The four headline indications in modern CRC drug development — cetuximab in RAS-WT/left-sided, pembrolizumab in MSI-H, encorafenib in BRAF V600E, trastuzumab/tucatinib in HER2-amplified — are NOT detectable in this dataset. With 50,000 patients, the absence of even tiny signals is striking. This pattern is consistent with either (a) outcomes that were generated independently of the molecular indication–treatment matching (e.g., synthetic data or a population so heavily confounded by indication that on-label benefit is invisible) or (b) a real-world cohort in which treatment was assigned without molecular guidance.
3. **Regorafenib is the one drug with a measurable PFS signal**, and the signal is biologically heterogeneous in an unexpected direction: present in KRAS-WT and left-sided patients, absent in KRAS-mutant and right-sided patients. In real-world CRC practice, regorafenib is a salvage agent used regardless of RAS status; the heterogeneity here is therefore a notable finding for the cohort.
4. **No demographic disparity** in receipt of any treatment or in adjusted PFS by race/insurance was detected, suggesting either equitable access in this cohort or a dataset with demographic features uncoupled from treatment assignment.
5. **Most other variables (comorbidities, symptom PROs, met sites, SNPs) appear to be noise** with respect to PFS in this cohort — a pattern compatible with a synthetic data-generation process that did not encode these dependencies.
