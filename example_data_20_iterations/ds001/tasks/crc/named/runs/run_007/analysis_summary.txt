Analysis summary — ds001_crc (n=50,000 patients; outcome: pfs_months)
=====================================================================

Across 25 iterations I proposed 75 hypotheses and ran 83 statistical
analyses. The picture that emerged is dominated by classical clinical
prognostic factors, while the canonical CRC biomarker–treatment
interactions (cetuximab×KRAS/NRAS/BRAF/sidedness, pembrolizumab×MSI-high,
encorafenib×BRAF V600E, trastuzumab/tucatinib×HER2-amplified) were NOT
detectable as PFS-modifying effects in this cohort. I report what the
data show, not what I expected clinically.

1. Disease burden and performance status (strongly supported)
-------------------------------------------------------------
- stage_iv=1 vs 0: PFS 3.57 vs 4.92 mo, OLS-adjusted beta = −1.35 mo,
  p ≪ 1e-200 (very large effect). Hypothesis h1 strongly supported.
- ecog_ps: each one-point increase reduces PFS by ~1.18 mo,
  p ≪ 1e-200 (h2 supported).
- age_years: surprisingly, beta = +0.176 mo per year, p ≪ 1e-200 — older
  patients have *longer* PFS in this cohort (h3 was REFUTED in
  direction; the sign is opposite to the clinically expected).
- sex_female: beta ≈ 0.006 mo, p = 0.55 — no sex difference (h4
  refuted).

2. Anti-EGFR (cetuximab) × biomarker interactions (NOT supported)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
The clinically classical pattern is anti-EGFR benefit confined to
RAS/RAF wild-type and left-sided tumors. None of these interactions
reached significance:
- cetuximab × kras_mutation (h5–h7): cetuximab effect in KRAS-WT
  diff = −0.05 mo (p = 0.12); in KRAS-mut diff = −0.03 mo (p = 0.38);
  interaction beta = +0.023 mo, p = 0.26 — refuted.
- cetuximab × nras_mutation (h8): interaction beta = −0.043,
  p = 0.48 — refuted.
- cetuximab × braf_v600e (h9): interaction beta = +0.002,
  p = 0.96 — refuted.
- cetuximab × right_sided_primary (h10): left-sided cetuximab effect
  = −0.05 mo (p = 0.09), right-sided = −0.02 (p = 0.49); interaction
  beta = +0.014, p = 0.50 — refuted.
- KRAS-WT subgroup × right-sidedness (h85, refined): same null
  result; cetuximab provided no benefit in either side among KRAS-WT.
- 3-way cetux × KRAS × right-sided (h95): beta = −0.021, p = 0.62 —
  refuted. Cell-mean diffs are uniformly tiny (≤ 0.06 mo).

3. Other biomarker-targeted therapies (NOT supported)
-----------------------------------------------------
- treatment_pembrolizumab × msi_high (h11–h13): in MSS, pembro effect
  = +0.005 mo (p = 0.86); in MSI-H (n = 2,492), effect = +0.007 mo
  (p = 0.96). Interaction beta = 0.031, p = 0.59. The 3-way
  pembro×MSI×stage_iv (h98) was also non-significant. h11 and h13
  refuted; h12 (no MSS benefit) trivially supported.
- treatment_encorafenib × braf_v600e (h14–h16): in BRAF V600E
  (n = 2,253), encorafenib effect = −0.13 mo, p = 0.33; in BRAF-WT,
  +0.01, p = 0.72; interaction beta = −0.135, p = 0.33. h14 and h16
  refuted.
- treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib × her2_amplified (h17–h19): in
  HER2+ (n = 1,486), T/T effect = +0.02 mo, p = 0.93; interaction
  beta = +0.007, p = 0.94 — refuted.

4. Treatment main effects
-------------------------
- treatment_bevacizumab (h20): beta = +0.007 mo, p = 0.43 — null;
  refuted.
- treatment_bevacizumab × kras_mutation (h86): interaction beta =
  −0.023, p = 0.23 — refuted.
- treatment_regorafenib (h21): SUPPORTED IN MAGNITUDE, OPPOSITE IN
  SIGN. Adjusting for prior_lines_of_therapy and core covariates,
  regorafenib carries beta = +0.94 mo, p ≪ 1e-200 — a large positive
  PFS association. Even in the comprehensive multivariable model
  (R² = 0.86), regorafenib retains beta = +0.94. This is the largest
  treatment effect in the cohort and runs counter to the typical
  late-line, marginal-benefit profile of regorafenib in real-world
  CRC. The most likely explanations are confounding by indication
  not captured by prior_lines_of_therapy, or a feature of how this
  dataset was assembled.

5. Lab and inflammatory markers
-------------------------------
- albumin_g_dl (h22): beta = +0.46 mo per g/dL, p ≪ 1e-200 —
  supported.
- weight_loss_pct_6mo (h25): beta = −0.076 mo per %, p ≪ 1e-200 —
  supported.
- ldh_u_l (h26): beta = −4.2e-4 per U/L, p = 1.4e-13 — supported.
- cea_ng_ml (h27): beta = −5.0e-3 per ng/mL, p = 1.3e-67 —
  supported.
- crp_mg_l (h23): beta = −7e-4, p = 0.16 — refuted.
- nlr (h24): beta = +0.003, p = 0.14 — refuted (and sign opposite to
  hypothesis).
- hemoglobin_g_dl (h28): beta = +0.001, p = 0.70 — refuted.
- alkaline_phosphatase, AST, ALT, total bilirubin (h29 etc.): all
  null after adjustment.

6. Metastatic sites
-------------------
After adjustment for stage_iv, age, sex, and ECOG, none of liver,
bone, adrenal, pleural-effusion, or pericardial-effusion variables
predicted PFS at p < 0.05 (h30–h34 all refuted). The stage_iv
indicator absorbs most of the burden-related signal.

7. Comorbidities, symptoms, prior therapy
-----------------------------------------
- Of eight comorbidities, only copd reached p < 0.05 (beta = −0.027,
  p = 9e-3). diabetes_mellitus, hypertension, chronic_kidney_disease,
  heart_failure, coronary_artery_disease, atrial_fibrillation,
  autoimmune_disease all null.
- All five PRO symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough,
  appetite-loss) were null at p < 0.05 (h50–h54 refuted).
- All prior-therapy variables (prior_lines_of_therapy,
  prior_chemotherapy/radiation/surgery/immunotherapy/targeted, and
  years_since_diagnosis) were null after adjustment (h70–h73
  refuted). This is striking: heavily pre-treated patients did not
  have shorter PFS.

8. Demographics and access
--------------------------
- race_ethnicity: hispanic, black, asian, "other" all non-significant
  vs white reference (h60–h62 refuted). Group-mean PFS span 4.27–
  4.41 mo.
- insurance_type: only insurance_type=uninsured vs private was
  borderline (beta = −0.051, p = 0.035; h64 weakly supported).
  Medicare and Medicaid coefficients were null (h63 refuted).
- rural_residence (h65) and education_years (h66): both null.

9. Tumor signaling alterations
------------------------------
tp53_mutation, pik3ca_mutation, pten_loss, cdkn2a_loss (h81–h84):
all non-significant (cdkn2a_loss borderline beta = +0.023, p = 0.08;
sign opposite to hypothesis). ntrk_fusion (n = 251, h80): null.
her2_amplification (the second column with this prefix), met_exon14,
RET/ROS1/NRG1, FGFR alterations were not separately tested but did
not appear as material drivers in the comprehensive model.

10. Pharmacogenomic SNPs
------------------------
Across the 27 snp_rs* variables, screening each as an additive term
in an ECOG/age/sex/stage-adjusted OLS:
- 3 SNPs reached raw p < 0.05 (snp_rs1800629 +0.030 mo, p = 0.014;
  snp_rs1050828 +0.041, p = 0.017; snp_rs2070744 −0.019, p = 0.046).
- 0 SNPs survived Bonferroni correction at α = 0.05/27 ≈ 1.85e-3.
- Hypotheses h90–h94 supported only at the nominal level; none of
  the top hits reached genome-wide-style significance.

11. Albumin × treatment efficacy
--------------------------------
- cetuximab × low_albumin (<3.5 g/dL) (h96): beta = +0.037,
  p = 0.11 — refuted.
- bevacizumab × low_albumin (h97): beta = −0.003, p = 0.87 —
  refuted.

12. Comprehensive multivariable model (iteration 25)
----------------------------------------------------
A model with all six treatments, the seven canonical biomarker–
treatment interactions, biomarkers, lab markers, mets, ECOG, stage,
age, sex, prior_lines_of_therapy, and selected PRO terms attained
R² = 0.859 on n = 50,000. In this fully adjusted model (h99):
- treatment_regorafenib remained the dominant treatment term
  (beta = +0.94 mo, p ≪ 1e-200).
- treatment_cetuximab beta = −0.028 mo, p = 0.035 (small, opposite
  the expected anti-EGFR direction).
- All seven canonical interaction terms (cetux×KRAS, cetux×NRAS,
  cetux×BRAF, cetux×right-sided, pembro×MSI, encora×BRAF,
  T/T×HER2) had p > 0.05 with effect estimates ≤ 0.06 mo.

Overall conclusions
===================
1. Outcome variation in this CRC cohort is overwhelmingly explained
   by stage_iv, ecog_ps, age_years, albumin_g_dl, weight_loss_pct_6mo,
   ldh_u_l, and cea_ng_ml. These are the classical clinical
   prognosticators, and they show large, robust, highly significant
   effects in the expected direction (with the notable exception of
   age, where older patients have longer PFS).
2. The canonical biomarker × treatment interactions on which CRC
   precision oncology is built (anti-EGFR by RAS/RAF/sidedness;
   pembrolizumab by MSI-H; encorafenib by BRAF V600E;
   trastuzumab/tucatinib by HER2-amplification) were not detected
   in this dataset, despite strong statistical power
   (n = 50,000; minimum biomarker subgroup sizes from 1,486 to
   ~5,000+). All seven interaction terms are statistically null,
   with effect-estimate magnitudes mostly < 0.05 months. I treat
   this as the empirical finding: in this synthesized cohort,
   biomarker-targeted therapies do not show the discriminating
   benefit they show in landmark trials.
3. treatment_regorafenib carries an unusually large positive PFS
   association (~+0.94 mo) that is unlikely to reflect a true
   superiority over other lines and most plausibly reflects
   confounding by indication or feature-construction artefact in
   the dataset.
4. Demographic, insurance-status, prior-therapy, comorbidity,
   PRO-symptom, mets-site (after stage adjustment), and SNP
   variables contributed minimally; only insurance=uninsured and
   COPD reached even nominal significance, with very small effect
   sizes.
