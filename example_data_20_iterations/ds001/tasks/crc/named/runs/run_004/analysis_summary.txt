ds001_crc — Hypothesis-Driven Analysis Summary
================================================

Dataset: 50,000 patient records with 124 patient features (clinical, lab, comorbidity,
metastasis sites, gene panel, pharmacogenomic SNPs, demographics, prior therapy, and six
treatment indicators) and a single continuous outcome, pfs_months. The cohort and
analyses below were generated across 25 iterations of propose-test-refine.

Outcome distribution (sanity check): mean pfs_months ~ 4.31 (sd ~ 2.29; range 0–16.25).

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A. Clinical / prognostic main effects (very strong signal)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The dominant prognostic axis in this cohort is performance status / disease burden /
nutrition / age, not specific genomic alterations or treatments.

Iteration 3 — clinical features:
- ecog_ps: r = -0.352, slope -1.19 mo per unit ECOG, p ≈ 0 (very large effect).
- stage_iv: stage-IV mean PFS 3.70 vs non-IV 5.06 (delta -1.35 mo, p ≈ 0).
- age_years: r = +0.766, slope +0.18 mo/year (p ≈ 0; large, positive — older patients
  show LONGER pfs_months in this synthetic cohort, an unusual direction relative to
  real-world expectation).
- sex_female: no detectable effect (delta -0.006 mo, p = 0.77).

Iteration 4 — labs:
- albumin_g_dl: r = +0.102, slope +0.47 mo/g·dL (p ≈ 3e-115). Strongest lab signal.
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: r = -0.123, slope -0.073 mo per percent loss (p ≈ 2e-167).
- cea_ng_ml: weak negative (r = -0.038, p = 3e-17).
- ldh_u_l, crp_mg_l, nlr, hemoglobin_g_dl: weak to null (|r| < 0.012; p ranging 0.01–0.29).

Iteration 19 — composite prognosis: above-median CEA AND below-median albumin (n =
11,433) had mean PFS 3.96 vs 4.42 in the rest of the cohort (delta -0.46 mo, p ≈ 2e-87) —
confirming that the CEA/albumin axis is a clinically meaningful summary marker.

Iteration 14 — adjusted multivariable model (R² = 0.859): ecog_ps (β = -1.17, p ≈ 0)
and albumin_g_dl (β = +0.47, p ≈ 0) remained the dominant independent prognostic
covariates. Age and stage_iv (already in the model) drove most of the remaining
variance, explaining the very high R².

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
B. Genomic / biomarker main effects
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 2 — colorectal-relevant alterations:
- kras_mutation: -0.33 mo (mut vs WT; p ≈ 2e-58) — significant, expected direction.
- braf_v600e: -0.23 mo (mut vs WT; p ≈ 3e-7) — significant, expected direction.
- right_sided_primary: -0.30 mo (right vs left; p ≈ 1e-49) — significant, expected.
- nras_mutation: +0.22 mo (mut vs WT; p = 7e-4) — significant but in the OPPOSITE
  direction to typical clinical expectation. Likely a synthetic-data quirk; flagging it.
- msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion: null (p > 0.36 for each).

Iteration 17 — broader genomic panel (tp53, pik3ca, pten_loss, cdkn2a_loss,
her2_amplification, fgfr_alteration): no significant association with pfs_months
(all p > 0.24).

Iteration 13 — common pharmacogenomic SNPs (n ≈ 28 SNPs): only three were nominally
significant (snp_rs1050828 r = +0.011 p = 0.015; snp_rs1801131 r = -0.010 p = 0.027;
snp_rs1801197 r = -0.009 p = 0.047). None survive Bonferroni correction. Interpretation:
no robust SNP–PFS association in this cohort.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
C. Treatment main effects
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 1 — six treatment indicators tested individually against pfs_months:
- treatment_regorafenib: +0.97 mo (p ≈ 2e-216) — large, robust, positive effect.
- treatment_cetuximab, _bevacizumab, _pembrolizumab, _encorafenib, _trastuzumab_tucatinib:
  all null (delta within ±0.05 mo; p > 0.08 for each).

Iteration 14 — adjusted multivariable model: regorafenib retained an essentially
unchanged effect (β = +0.94 mo, p ≈ 0); the other five treatments remained null
after adjustment (|β| < 0.03 mo, p > 0.06).

Therefore, regorafenib is the only treatment in the dataset with detectable direct
PFS benefit on average.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
D. Treatment–biomarker interactions (the key hypothesis-tests)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Iterations 5–8, 15, 18, 20, 21, 24 — testing the canonical treatment-biomarker
interactions one would expect in colorectal cancer:

1. Cetuximab × KRAS (anti-EGFR works in KRAS-WT only): NULL.
   - Iteration 5: interaction β = +0.020 (p = 0.66).
   - Iteration 18 subgroup: in KRAS-WT, cetuximab delta = -0.05 mo (p = 0.12); in
     KRAS-mut, delta = -0.03 mo (p = 0.38).
   - Iteration 24 (adjusted): β = +0.018 (p = 0.29).
   No evidence the dataset encodes the expected KRAS-restricted cetuximab benefit.

2. Cetuximab × right_sided_primary (anti-EGFR works in left-sided): NULL.
   - Iteration 5: interaction β = +0.026 (p = 0.58).
   - Iteration 18 (LS, RAS/RAF-WT): cetuximab delta = -0.07 mo (p = 0.12).
   No sidedness modulation of cetuximab effect.

3. Cetuximab × NRAS, × BRAF, × (KRAS × right_sided): all null (Iter 5, 15).

4. Pembrolizumab × MSI-high (immunotherapy works in MSI-H): NULL.
   - Iteration 6: interaction β = +0.002 (p = 0.99). MSI-H+pembro mean PFS 4.30 vs
     MSI-H no-pembro 4.29 (delta +0.007 mo, p = 0.96; n_treated = 396).
   - Iteration 24 (adjusted): β = +0.059 (p = 0.23).
   The expected MSI-restricted ICI benefit is absent.

5. Encorafenib × BRAF V600E (BRAF inhibitor in BRAF-mut): NULL / SLIGHTLY OPPOSITE.
   - Iteration 7: in BRAF-V600E patients (n = 2,272), encorafenib-treated had a
     slightly LOWER mean PFS (3.98 vs 4.11; delta -0.13 mo, p = 0.33). Interaction
     β = -0.14 (p = 0.38). Direction of point estimate is opposite to expectation but
     not statistically significant.

6. Trastuzumab/tucatinib × HER2-amplified: NULL.
   - Iteration 8: HER2-amp+T/T mean PFS 4.27 vs HER2-amp no-T/T 4.26 (delta +0.02,
     p = 0.93). Interaction β = +0.06 (p = 0.78).

7. Bevacizumab × right_sided / × KRAS / × liver_mets: all null (Iter 21; |β| < 0.025,
   p > 0.6).

Iterations 16, 21 — heterogeneity of treatment effects across stage IV vs non-IV, or
across other clinical strata: no significant interactions detected.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
E. Other null/secondary domains
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Iter 9 (PROs: fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite_loss): all null (p > 0.08).
- Iter 10 (15 comorbidities): all null at α = 0.05; depression/anxiety came closest
  (delta -0.05 mo, p = 0.053).
- Iter 11 (sites of metastasis: liver, bone, adrenal, pleural, pericardial,
  contralateral lung): all null (p > 0.06).
- Iter 12 (rural residence, education_years, smoking_pack_years, race_ethnicity,
  insurance_type): all null (rural delta +0.02 mo p = 0.34; race ANOVA F = 0.87
  p = 0.48; insurance ANOVA F = 1.47 p = 0.22).
- Iter 22 (race & insurance after adjustment for ECOG/stage/albumin/CEA/MSI/treatments):
  race remains null (F = 1.21, p = 0.30); uninsured β = -0.09 mo vs private
  (p = 0.06, borderline) — no robust disparity signal in this cohort.
- Iter 23 (prior therapies, prior_lines_of_therapy): all null.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
F. Hypotheses supported vs refuted
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Strongly supported:
- Higher ECOG → shorter PFS (h3_1).
- Stage IV → shorter PFS (h3_2).
- Older age → LONGER PFS in this cohort (h3_3 — direction strongly positive,
  unusual relative to clinical expectation).
- Higher albumin → longer PFS (h4_2).
- Higher weight loss → shorter PFS (h4_7).
- Higher CEA → shorter PFS (h4_1, weak but very significant due to N).
- KRAS mutation → shorter PFS (h2_1).
- BRAF V600E → shorter PFS (h2_3).
- Right-sided primary → shorter PFS (h2_7).
- Composite high-CEA + low-albumin → markedly shorter PFS (h19_2).
- Treatment_regorafenib → longer PFS, both unadjusted and adjusted (h1_6, h14_6).
- The full multivariable interaction model explains substantial variance
  (adj R² = 0.859, h25_4) — but this is dominated by clinical/prognostic terms.

Refuted (null) — these were proposed because the columns are present and the
biology is well-known, but the dataset does not encode the expected effects:
- Cetuximab benefit (overall and in KRAS-WT, NRAS-WT, BRAF-WT, left-sided, RAS/RAF-WT
  subgroups) — h1_1, h5_1–h5_4, h15_1, h18_1, h18_3, h20_1, h20_2, h24_1.
- Pembrolizumab benefit overall and in MSI-high — h1_3, h6_1–h6_3, h24_2.
- Encorafenib benefit in BRAF V600E — h1_4, h7_1, h7_2, h24_3.
- Trastuzumab/tucatinib benefit in HER2-amplified — h1_5, h8_1, h8_2, h24_4.
- Bevacizumab benefit overall and in clinical subgroups — h1_2, h21_1–h21_3.
- MSI-high, HER2-amplified, NTRK fusion main effects on PFS — h2_4, h2_5, h2_6.
- Comorbidity, metastasis-site, PRO, demographic, insurance, race, prior-therapy
  associations — h9_*, h10_*, h11_*, h12_*, h22_*, h23_*.

Refuted with reverse direction (point-estimate opposite to expectation):
- NRAS mutation → +0.22 mo vs WT (h2_2; statistically significant; opposite to
  typical clinical biology).

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
G. Overall conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PFS in this synthetic CRC cohort is dominated by a small set of standard
   prognostic factors — ECOG, stage_iv, albumin, age, weight_loss, plus the
   tumor-side and tumor-genotype prognostic markers KRAS, BRAF, and right_sided_primary.
   These five-to-eight variables together yield a multivariable R² of ~0.86,
   leaving very little residual variance for additional features to explain.

2. Of the six listed treatments, only treatment_regorafenib has a detectable PFS
   effect — a uniform, substantial ~+0.95 mo benefit that does not interact with any
   tested biomarker or stage stratum. Adjusted and unadjusted estimates agree.

3. The canonical CRC treatment–biomarker pairings are NOT present in this dataset:
   - cetuximab → KRAS-WT / left-sided
   - pembrolizumab → MSI-high
   - encorafenib → BRAF V600E
   - trastuzumab/tucatinib → HER2-amplified
   For each, both the multiplicative interaction term AND the within-subgroup
   point estimate are null (and in some cases trend opposite). The well-powered
   sample size (50,000) makes "no detected effect" close to "no effect": for an
   interaction such as pembrolizumab × MSI-high we have ~400 treated MSI-H patients
   and the observed delta is +0.007 mo with a 95% CI tightly around zero.

4. Among the 28 pharmacogenomic SNPs tested, three reach nominal p < 0.05 but none
   survive multiplicity correction — consistent with the global null.

5. No demographic disparity (race, rural residence, education, insurance) was
   detectable, even after adjustment.

In short: this cohort behaves like one in which the treatment columns are largely
random (with regorafenib the lone exception), the genomic markers KRAS / BRAF /
sidedness carry their expected prognostic but not predictive signal, and the bulk
of pfs_months variation is captured by ECOG, stage, age, albumin, and weight loss.
