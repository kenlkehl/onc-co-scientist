Analysis Summary — ds001_crc
============================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with colorectal cancer; outcome of interest is
progression-free survival (pfs_months, mean 4.31, SD 2.29). I worked through
25 iterations of hypothesis-test-refine, starting from the canonical
biomarker-treatment pairings used in metastatic CRC and broadening into
prognostic features, demographics, comorbidities, symptoms, prior therapy,
SNPs, and multi-way interactions.

1. Canonical biomarker-targeted treatment pairings (iters 1-5)
--------------------------------------------------------------
I tested each established biomarker-drug pairing in CRC:
  * cetuximab x KRAS wild-type / left-sided: interaction coefficients +0.020
    (p=0.67) and +0.026 (p=0.58); cetuximab main effect even in KRAS WT and
    left-sided was -0.05 mo (p~0.09-0.12). REFUTED.
  * pembrolizumab x MSI-high: interaction +0.002 (p=0.99); pembro effect in
    MSI-high (n=2513) was +0.007 mo (p=0.95). REFUTED.
  * encorafenib x BRAF V600E: interaction -0.141 (p=0.38); in BRAF V600E
    patients (n=2272) encorafenib effect was actually -0.13 mo (p=0.36).
    REFUTED.
  * trastuzumab+tucatinib x HER2-amplified: interaction +0.063 (p=0.78);
    HER2+ effect +0.017 mo (p=0.94). REFUTED.

None of the textbook biomarker-driven matched-treatment effects appear in
this dataset.

2. Prognostic main effects (iters 7-12, 15-19)
----------------------------------------------
Strong, expected prognostic features:
  * ecog_ps: -1.19 mo per unit (p~0). Dominant predictor.
  * stage_iv: -1.35 mo (p~0).
  * albumin_g_dl: +0.47 mo per g/dL (p=3e-115).
  * weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.073 mo per percent (p=2e-167).
  * cea_ng_ml: -0.0053 mo per ng/mL (p=3e-17).
  * ldh_u_l: -0.0004 mo per U/L (p=0.013).
  * BRAF V600E: -0.228 mo (p=4e-6) — the expected poor prognostic.
  * KRAS mutation: -0.333 mo univariate (p<<0.001), but see Section 4.
  * right_sided_primary: -0.313 mo univariate (p<<0.001), but see Section 4.

Surprising / null:
  * age_years: +0.176 mo per year (p~0), positive direction, robust to
    adjustment for ECOG and stage. Older patients have LONGER PFS in this
    cohort, opposite of typical CRC biology — likely a structural artifact
    of how the dataset was generated.
  * MSI-high, sex, NTRK fusion: all null on PFS.
  * NRAS mutation: +0.221 mo BETTER PFS (p=2e-4), opposite of expected.
  * liver_mets: -0.019 mo (p=0.52), null — surprising in CRC.
  * Comorbidities (11 tested): all null (|diff| <= 0.06 mo, p >= 0.05).
  * Patient-reported symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough,
    appetite loss): all null.
  * Prior therapy variables (5 binaries + lines + years since dx): all null.
  * Hemoglobin, BMI, smoking, education: null (hemoglobin even pointed
    "wrong way" at p=0.08).
  * race_ethnicity (ANOVA p=0.48) and insurance_type (p=0.22): no detectable
    PFS disparities. Insurance ranking did directionally match expectation
    (private > medicare > medicaid > uninsured) but was not significant.
  * 27 SNPs tested univariately: 3 reached nominal p<0.05 (rs1050828,
    rs1801131, rs1801197), about what is expected by chance; none survives
    multiplicity correction.

3. Treatment effects in adjusted models (iters 6, 14)
-----------------------------------------------------
Adjusting for age, ECOG, stage, albumin, CEA, LDH, weight loss, CRP, NLR,
and biomarkers, only ONE treatment retains a meaningful main effect:
  * regorafenib: +0.943 mo (p<1e-300).
  * cetuximab -0.013 (p=0.16); bevacizumab +0.003 (p=0.70);
    pembrolizumab 0.000 (p=1.0); encorafenib -0.001 (p=0.92);
    trastuzumab/tucatinib +0.024 (p=0.11).

So the dataset shows a single drug — regorafenib — with a clean and large
average PFS benefit, while the other five drugs have effectively zero
average effect.

4. The regorafenib biomarker pattern (iters 22-24, the most striking finding)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Stratified analyses revealed that regorafenib's benefit is sharply
concentrated:
  * KRAS wild-type: regorafenib +1.66 mo (p<1e-300)
  * KRAS mutant: regorafenib +0.03 mo (p=0.45)
  * BRAF wild-type: regorafenib +1.03 mo (p<1e-300)
  * BRAF V600E: regorafenib -0.16 mo (p=0.15)
  * Left-sided: regorafenib +1.48 mo (p<1e-300)
  * Right-sided: regorafenib +0.03 mo (p=0.52)
Triple-WT/left-sided subgroup (n=17,372): regorafenib +2.76 mo (p<1e-300).

Interaction coefficients in linear models:
  * regorafenib x kras_mutation = -1.63 (p=8e-226)
  * regorafenib x braf_v600e   = -1.18 (p=6e-23)
  * regorafenib x right_sided  = -1.45 (p=4e-168)
  * regorafenib x msi_high, x stage_iv, x ecog_ps, x age_years: all null.

When regorafenib and its three interactions are entered jointly along with
ECOG, stage, age, albumin, the apparent prognostic main effects of
kras_mutation, braf_v600e, and right_sided_primary all collapse to ~0
(coefficients -0.003, 0.000, 0.000; all p>0.7). The univariate
"poor-prognostic" appearance of these three biomarkers in this cohort is
ENTIRELY mediated by differential regorafenib response, not intrinsic
disease biology.

Clinically, the pattern (RAS WT, BRAF WT, left-sided => big benefit)
is the canonical EGFR-inhibitor signature, not the established
regorafenib biology — in real metastatic CRC, regorafenib is a
multikinase inhibitor used in chemorefractory disease without these
biomarker selectors. The dataset behaves as if its "regorafenib" treatment
column has been generated with anti-EGFR-like response logic, while the
labeled cetuximab column shows no signal.

5. Other interactions explored
------------------------------
  * right_sided x KRAS mutation interaction = +0.452 (p=2e-25): a
    sub-additive pattern (joint penalty smaller than sum of marginals).
    This too can be reinterpreted as overlap in their explanation through
    regorafenib response.
  * Stage IV x each treatment: all null (p>=0.15).
  * Bevacizumab x liver_mets: null.
  * Regorafenib x ECOG, x age, x MSI, x sex, x stage IV: all null.

Bottom line
-----------
* Established prognostic features (ECOG, stage, albumin, weight loss, CEA,
  LDH, CRP-trend) behave largely as expected and dominate PFS variance.
* Of six oncologic drugs included, FIVE have no detectable average effect
  on PFS in any subgroup at clinically plausible biomarker selectors:
  cetuximab (any), bevacizumab (any), pembrolizumab even in MSI-high,
  encorafenib in BRAF V600E, trastuzumab/tucatinib in HER2 amplified.
  The expected biomarker-treatment pairings of metastatic CRC do NOT
  manifest here.
* The single, very large signal in the dataset is the regorafenib
  treatment column, which delivers ~+1 mo PFS overall and ~+2.8 mo PFS
  in the canonical anti-EGFR-eligible subgroup (RAS WT, BRAF WT,
  left-sided primary). The biomarker dependency of "regorafenib" matches
  EGFR-inhibitor (cetuximab) biology rather than published regorafenib
  biology.
* Demographic / socioeconomic / comorbidity signals are essentially absent.
* Age has a positive effect on PFS in this cohort, opposite of typical
  CRC observational data; combined with the regorafenib re-labeling
  pattern, this suggests the dataset may be a synthetic / simulated cohort
  whose response structure differs in places from real-world CRC.
