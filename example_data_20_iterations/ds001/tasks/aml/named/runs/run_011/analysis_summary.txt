ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_aml (n=50,000)
========================================
Outcome: objective_response (overall rate ≈ 16.9%, 8,452/50,000).
Iterations executed: 25. Hypotheses tested: 83. Analyses: 83.
Significance threshold: p < 0.05 (two-sided).

OVERVIEW
--------
Across 25 iterations we walked from main effects of AML driver mutations and
clinical features, through treatment main effects, treatment×biomarker
interactions, comorbidity / demographic / SES factors, pharmacogenomic SNPs,
prior therapy, symptom burden, lab markers, multivariable adjustment, and
finally three-way interactions. The strongest signals in the dataset are
clinical/host factors (performance status, cachexia/weight loss, marrow
blast burden, WBC, albumin, CRP). Biomarker–treatment matching produced
mixed signals: the venetoclax+azacitidine relationships behaved as
clinically expected, but the FLT3-inhibitor and IDH-inhibitor matched
interactions were largely null or in unexpected directions.

ITERATION-BY-ITERATION FINDINGS
--------------------------------

Iter 1 — AML driver mutations (main effects):
  flt3_itd, npm1_mutation, tp53_mutation, complex_karyotype: none of these
  individual driver mutations showed a significant main-effect difference in
  objective_response rate (all p > 0.05). The crude associations are weak;
  sample sizes are large, so the absence of effect is informative.

Iter 2 — Other drivers / fitness markers:
  idh1_mutation: +5.6 percentage points higher response in IDH1-mutant
    patients (22.5% vs 16.5%, p ≈ 3.5e-18) — supported.
  flt3_tkd, idh2_mutation, secondary_aml, unfit_for_intensive: not
    significant on a crude basis (each compared to its complement), refuting
    the simple main-effect hypothesis for each.

Iter 3 — Demographics / performance status:
  ecog_ps: very strong negative association with response (logit coef per SD
    ≈ -0.26, p ≈ 2e-97) — strongly supported.
  age_years: not significant on logistic regression.
  sex_female: not significant.

Iter 4 — Treatment main effects:
  Only treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine reached significance, and only
  marginally (Δ ≈ +0.7 pp, p ≈ 0.031). All single-agent and combination
  treatments otherwise looked similar to non-recipients, consistent with
  treatment effects being concentrated in matched biomarker subgroups
  rather than in the overall population.

Iter 5 — midostaurin × FLT3-ITD:
  Interaction coefficient was small and non-significant; within FLT3-ITD+
  patients, midostaurin recipients did not show a significantly different
  response than non-recipients. The expected biologic interaction was NOT
  detected — refuting H19/H20.

Iter 6 — gilteritinib × FLT3-ITD:
  Same result as Iter 5: no significant interaction or within-FLT3-ITD+
  treatment effect. Refutes H21/H22.

Iter 7 — ivosidenib × IDH1:
  Surprising direction: the interaction was statistically NEGATIVE
  (coef ≈ -0.36, p ≈ 0.027) — i.e., ivosidenib's odds-ratio for response was
  numerically lower in IDH1-mutant patients than in IDH1-wildtype patients.
  This contradicts the prior expectation. Within-IDH1+ within-treatment
  comparison was not itself significant. Refutes the directional H23 (which
  predicted a positive interaction).

Iter 8 — enasidenib × IDH2:
  Interaction was not significant; within-IDH2+ enasidenib comparison was
  not significant. Refutes H25/H26.

Iter 9 — venetoclax+azacitidine × unfit_for_intensive:
  Interaction was POSITIVE and significant (coef ≈ +0.10, p ≈ 0.036).
  Within unfit patients, ven+aza recipients had a higher response rate
  (Δ ≈ +1.6 pp, p ≈ 0.003). This is the expected biology and supports
  H27/H28.

Iter 10 — 7+3 × unfit_for_intensive:
  No significant interaction or within-fit treatment effect. Refutes H29/H30.

Iter 11 — TP53 × 7+3 and ven+aza:
  Neither interaction nor within-TP53+ ven+aza comparison reached
  significance.

Iter 12 — FLT3-TKD interactions with midostaurin and gilteritinib:
  Neither significant.

Iter 13 — Lab markers (host fitness / disease burden):
  albumin_g_dl: positive (coef per SD ≈ +0.058, p ≈ 1e-6) — supported.
  ldh_u_l: not significant (refutes H36).
  wbc_k_per_ul: negative (coef per SD ≈ -0.056, p ≈ 2e-5) — supported.
  blast_pct_marrow: negative (coef per SD ≈ -0.11, p ≈ 1e-19) — strongly
    supported. Marrow blast burden is a robust predictor of non-response.

Iter 14 — Inflammation / cachexia / cytopenia:
  crp_mg_l: negative (per SD ≈ -0.064, p ≈ 3e-6) — supported.
  weight_loss_pct_6mo: strongly negative (per SD ≈ -0.155, p ≈ 7e-35) —
    one of the strongest predictors in the dataset; supported.
  nlr, hemoglobin_g_dl, platelets_k_ul: not significant; refutes H40/H42/H43.

Iter 15 — Comorbidities:
  None of CKD, heart failure, diabetes, prior malignancy, hypertension
  reached significance. Refutes H44–H48.

Iter 16 — Race/ethnicity, insurance, rural residence:
  race_ethnicity == black: small but significant lower response
    (Δ ≈ -1.3 pp, p ≈ 0.012) — supported (potential disparity signal,
    though absolute magnitude is small).
  Hispanic, Medicaid, uninsured, rural: not significant.

Iter 17 — Pharmacogenomic SNPs (5 markers):
  None of rs1045642 (ABCB1), rs1065852 (CYP2D6*10), rs1799853 (CYP2C9*2),
  rs4244285 (CYP2C19*2), rs1801133 (MTHFR C677T) were significantly
  associated with response. Refutes H54–H58.

Iter 18 — Prior therapy and disease tenure:
  prior_lines_of_therapy, prior_chemotherapy, prior_radiation,
  years_since_diagnosis: all non-significant. Refutes H59–H62.

Iter 19 — NPM1 × treatments:
  treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine × npm1_mutation: strongly POSITIVE
    interaction (coef ≈ +0.26, p ≈ 7e-7) — supported. Ven+aza is
    differentially more effective in NPM1-mutated AML.
  treatment_7plus3 × npm1_mutation: not significant.

Iter 20 — Complex karyotype × treatments:
  Within complex_karyotype: ven+aza vs not — not significant.
  treatment_7plus3 × complex_karyotype: NEGATIVE interaction
    (coef ≈ -0.21, p ≈ 0.0016) — supported. 7+3's relative odds-ratio for
    response is reduced in patients with complex karyotype, in line with
    the known cytogenetic adverse risk profile.

Iter 21 — Older patients (age ≥ 75) × treatments:
  Within age ≥ 75: neither ven+aza nor 7+3 produced a significantly
  different response rate compared to non-recipients. Refutes H67/H68 in
  this cohort.

Iter 22 — Multivariable adjusted interactions:
  After adjusting for age, ECOG, secondary AML, unfit-for-intensive,
  complex karyotype, FLT3-ITD, NPM1, TP53, IDH1, IDH2, and several other
  treatment×biomarker interactions:
    midostaurin × FLT3-ITD: not significant.
    ivosidenib × IDH1: significant NEGATIVE (coef ≈ -0.38, p ≈ 0.022) —
      same direction as the unadjusted Iter-7 finding; the adjusted model
      confirms an unexpected qualitative pattern.
    enasidenib × IDH2: not significant.

Iter 23 — Symptom-burden grades:
  fatigue_grade, pain_nrs, dyspnea_grade: not significant.
  appetite_loss_grade: marginally negative (per SD ≈ -0.025, p ≈ 0.038) —
    weakly supported.

Iter 24 — Liver/kidney function and other labs:
  total_bilirubin, creatinine, inr, bmi: not significant (refutes
  H76–H79 directionally).
  alkaline_phosphatase_u_l: weakly POSITIVE (per SD ≈ +0.023, p ≈ 0.048).
    Direction unexpected; magnitude small.

Iter 25 — Three-way interaction and education:
  Within unfit & TP53+ subgroup: ven+aza vs not — not significant
    (sample size shrinks substantially after the joint subset).
  Three-way interaction ven+aza × unfit × TP53: not significant.
  education_years: not significant.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1) Host-fitness signals dominate response prediction in this cohort:
   ECOG performance status, 6-month weight loss, marrow blast %, WBC,
   serum albumin, and CRP all carry the expected directions with strong
   statistical support. These appear to be the most reliable predictors
   of objective response.

2) Among targeted-therapy × biomarker pairings, only the
   venetoclax+azacitidine relationships behaved as clinically expected:
     - Positive ven+aza × unfit_for_intensive interaction.
     - Strongly positive ven+aza × NPM1 interaction.
   These are consistent with established AML biology (ven+aza efficacy in
   unfit patients and in NPM1-mutated AML).

3) FLT3-inhibitor (midostaurin, gilteritinib) interactions with FLT3-ITD
   and FLT3-TKD were null in this dataset — both at the interaction-term
   level and in within-FLT3-mutated subgroups. This either reflects a
   genuine absence of biomarker-matched benefit in this synthetic/observed
   cohort, or that response is driven dominantly by host factors that
   wash out targeted-treatment signals.

4) Ivosidenib × IDH1 produced a paradoxical NEGATIVE interaction that
   persisted after multivariable adjustment, with the effect appearing
   numerically larger in IDH1-wildtype patients. This is biologically
   counter-intuitive and warrants caution.

5) Cytogenetic adverse risk: 7+3 × complex_karyotype showed the expected
   negative interaction — chemotherapy is relatively less effective in
   complex-karyotype disease.

6) IDH1 mutation main effect was positive on response (~+5.6 pp), while
   FLT3-ITD, FLT3-TKD, NPM1, TP53, complex karyotype, secondary AML, and
   unfit_for_intensive showed no significant unadjusted main effects.

7) Pharmacogenomic SNPs, comorbidities, prior-therapy variables, and most
   demographics did not show response associations. A small but
   significant lower response rate was observed in patients of Black
   race/ethnicity (Δ ≈ -1.3 pp); the magnitude is small but the signal
   is statistically present in n = 5,889.

8) The large null fields (most demographics, most comorbidities, all
   tested SNPs, prior therapy variables, several symptom grades) suggest
   the dataset was generated with response driven primarily by clinical
   fitness markers and a subset of biomarker–treatment matches, rather
   than broadly across patient features.

LIMITATIONS
-----------
- Multiple testing was not formally adjusted (83 analyses); the marginal
  findings (e.g., alkaline_phosphatase, appetite_loss_grade,
  Black race ethnicity, ivosidenib×IDH1 unadjusted) should be interpreted
  cautiously.
- Treatments are not mutually exclusive in the data; observational
  treatment-effect estimates are subject to confounding by indication.
- "Objective response" is binary; magnitude of benefit and durability
  cannot be assessed from this outcome alone.
