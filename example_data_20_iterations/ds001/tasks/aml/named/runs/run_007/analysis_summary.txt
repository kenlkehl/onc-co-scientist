ds001_aml — Analysis Summary
============================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with AML, binary outcome objective_response (overall
rate 16.9%). The dataset contains demographics, ECOG performance status, AML-
specific genetics (FLT3-ITD/TKD, IDH1/IDH2, NPM1, TP53, complex karyotype,
secondary AML), six AML treatments (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib,
enasidenib, venetoclax-azacitidine, 7+3), routine labs, comorbidities, prior
therapy, patient-reported symptom grades, social determinants, a 24-SNP panel,
and a set of clearly off-disease "distractor" features (HER2 amplification,
solid-tumor fusions like RET/ROS1/NTRK, MSI-high, sites of metastasis, CA-125,
CEA, PSA, etc.). Treatments are not mutually exclusive: ~40% of patients
received >=2 of the listed regimens.

I ran 25 iterations of propose-test-refine, beginning with main effects of
the most plausible AML predictors and progressively focusing on
treatment x mutation interactions, then sweeping demographics, comorbidities,
SNPs, and distractor variables, and finally consolidating the findings in a
multivariable model.

Findings supported by the data (p < 0.05; magnitudes signed on the
log-odds scale of objective_response unless noted):

1. ECOG performance status is by far the dominant prognostic factor.
   Univariable log OR = -0.384 per 1-point increase (OR 0.68), p ~ 1.5e-97.
   The effect remains essentially unchanged after multivariable adjustment.

2. IDH1 mutation has a strong unadjusted positive association with response
   (log OR +0.36, p = 4.1e-18; absolute response rate 22.1% vs 16.5%), but
   ALL of this main effect is mediated by an interaction with venetoclax-
   azacitidine (see #3) — IDH1's adjusted main effect is not significant
   (p = 0.43).

3. IDH1 x venetoclax-azacitidine interaction is the largest single signal
   in the dataset: interaction log OR = +0.73 (p = 7.7e-18 univariable;
   +0.77, p = 4.4e-19 in the multivariable model). Concretely:
       IDH1-/ven-aza-: RR = 16.6%
       IDH1-/ven-aza+: RR = 16.4%
       IDH1+/ven-aza-: RR = 17.1%
       IDH1+/ven-aza+: RR = 29.8%
   Adding ven-aza to IDH1-mutated patients raises objective response by
   ~13 percentage points absolute, while it does essentially nothing in
   IDH1-wildtype patients.

4. NPM1 x venetoclax-azacitidine interaction is also positive and
   significant (log OR +0.26, p = 6.7e-7 univariable; +0.27, p = 3.6e-7
   adjusted). NPM1-mutated patients gain a meaningful additional benefit
   from ven-aza relative to NPM1-wildtype.

5. Complex karyotype x 7+3 interaction is negative and significant
   (log OR -0.21, p = 0.0016). Patients with complex karyotype respond
   worse to 7+3 than to non-7+3 regimens, consistent with the long-
   standing clinical observation that intensive cytotoxic chemotherapy
   does poorly in this cytogenetic subgroup.

6. Unfit-for-intensive x venetoclax-azacitidine interaction is positive
   (log OR +0.10, p = 0.036). Within the unfit subgroup, ven-aza raises
   RR from 16.3% to 17.9%, while 7+3 has no effect (16.9% vs 17.0%).
   Modest in magnitude but directionally consistent with the standard-
   of-care framework.

7. Disease burden / nutrition / inflammation main effects are robust and
   independent of ECOG:
       weight_loss_pct_6mo: log OR -0.040 per 1%, p = 7.4e-35
       blast_pct_marrow:    log OR -0.0058 per 1%, p = 1.2e-19
       albumin_g_dl:        log OR +0.115 per 1 g/dL, p = 1.4e-6
       crp_mg_l:            log OR -0.007 per mg/L, p = 2.8e-6
       wbc_k_per_ul:        log OR -0.0022 per k/uL, p = 1.9e-5
   All persist in the multivariable model with little attenuation.

8. Three smaller, but significant, signals:
       atrial_fibrillation main effect: log OR -0.10, p = 0.024
       appetite_loss_grade main effect: log OR -0.023 per grade, p = 0.038
       race_ethnicity 'white' (vs all others): log OR +0.08, p = 0.002
       race_ethnicity 'black' (vs all others): log OR -0.10, p = 0.011
       snp_rs1050828:                   log OR +0.118, p = 0.005
   The race signal is unadjusted, and could reflect unmeasured confounding
   (e.g. by genetic background, regimen access, or comorbidity panel
   not exhaustively captured); I have not attempted to disentangle it.

Surprising / counter-intuitive findings:

9. IDH1 x ivosidenib interaction is NEGATIVE in this dataset (log OR
   -0.36, p = 0.027 univariable; persists as -0.35, p = 0.037 adjusted).
   Ivosidenib is supposed to be the matched-pair targeted therapy for
   IDH1, but here IDH1-mutated patients on ivosidenib have a LOWER
   response rate (17.5%) than IDH1-mutated patients off ivosidenib
   (22.6%). In IDH1-wildtype, ivosidenib has a small positive association.
   This refutes the matched-pair targeted-therapy expectation.

10. IDH2 x enasidenib interaction is null (p = 0.45). The corresponding
    matched-pair benefit is not observed.

11. FLT3-ITD x midostaurin interaction (p = 0.22) and FLT3-ITD x
    gilteritinib interaction (p = 0.69) are both null. The clinically
    expected FLT3-inhibitor-in-FLT3-ITD benefit is not evident in this
    dataset.

12. NPM1 x ivosidenib interaction is NEGATIVE (log OR -0.23, p = 0.024) —
    NPM1-mutated patients respond worse to ivosidenib than NPM1-wildtype
    do.

Refuted / null hypotheses (effects that classical AML literature would
have led one to expect):

  - TP53 mutation: no main effect on response (p = 0.51). No
    treatment-specific subgroup effect detected.
  - Secondary AML: no main effect (p = 0.90).
  - Complex karyotype main effect: not significant (p = 0.81); only the
    interaction with 7+3 emerges.
  - FLT3-ITD or FLT3-TKD as main effects, in either direction: null.
  - Age: null at the response endpoint (p = 0.72) and remains null after
    multivariable adjustment. The "older AML patient does worse" pattern
    in this dataset is captured entirely by ECOG, weight loss, and lab
    markers.
  - Sex, BMI, smoking pack-years, education, prior lines of therapy,
    years since diagnosis: all null.
  - Insurance type and rural residence: null (no socioeconomic-disparity
    signal at the response endpoint).

Distractor variables (deliberately off-disease features that should not
matter for AML response): all null, exactly as expected.
  - HER2 amplification, MET exon 14 skipping, RET/ROS1/NTRK/NRG1 fusions,
    BRAF V600E, FGFR alteration, MSI-high, PIK3CA, PTEN loss, CDKN2A loss,
    KEAP1 mutation: all p > 0.13.
  - Liver/bone/adrenal mets, pleural/pericardial effusion, contralateral
    lung mets: all p > 0.20.
  - CA-125, CEA, PSA, TSH: all p > 0.10.
  - Hepatitis B/C history, HIV: all p > 0.10.
  - 23 of 24 SNPs (excluding rs1050828) have p > 0.05; the next-smallest
    (rs2032582) has p = 0.049, consistent with chance given multiple
    testing.

Overall conclusions:

  * Performance status (ECOG), disease-burden labs (blast%, WBC, weight
    loss), nutritional/inflammatory markers (albumin, CRP) drive most of
    the prognostic signal in this AML cohort.

  * The clinically meaningful treatment-biomarker interaction in the
    dataset is IDH1 mutation x venetoclax-azacitidine, with NPM1 x
    venetoclax-azacitidine and complex karyotype x 7+3 as supporting
    second-tier signals.

  * The classical "matched-pair" targeted-therapy hypotheses (FLT3-ITD x
    FLT3 inhibitors; IDH1 x ivosidenib; IDH2 x enasidenib) do NOT bear
    out at the objective_response endpoint here. In particular,
    ivosidenib appears to be detrimental, not beneficial, in
    IDH1-mutated patients in this dataset.

  * The off-disease "distractor" features and the SNP panel are
    essentially uninformative for objective response, which is the
    expected behavior in an AML cohort.

  * After full multivariable adjustment, age, sex, secondary AML, TP53,
    FLT3-ITD/TKD, IDH2, midostaurin/gilteritinib/enasidenib main
    effects, and rural / insurance demographics are not independent
    predictors of response. The dominant signals come from ECOG,
    blast %, weight loss, albumin, CRP, WBC, and the IDH1/NPM1 x
    venetoclax-azacitidine and complex-karyotype x 7+3 interactions.
