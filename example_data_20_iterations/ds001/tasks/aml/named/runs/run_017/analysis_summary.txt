Analysis Summary — ds001_aml (50,000 patients, single binary outcome: objective_response)
==========================================================================================

Cohort and outcome
------------------
Overall objective response rate (ORR) was 16.90% (n=50,000). Performance status was
distributed as ECOG 0: 35.2%, 1: 49.9%, 2: 14.9%. Common molecular markers: NPM1 30.0%,
FLT3-ITD 20.0%, complex karyotype 14.9%, TP53 10.0%, IDH2 9.4%, IDH1 7.2%, FLT3-TKD 5.6%.
Treatment exposures: 7+3 in 45.2%, venetoclax/azacitidine in 40.0%, midostaurin 15.1%,
gilteritinib 15.1%, ivosidenib 7.9%, enasidenib 7.9%. Race/ethnicity: White 65.0%,
Hispanic 15.3%, Black 11.8%, Asian 5.9%, Other 2.0%.

Iterations 1-6 — main effects (clinical, lab, single-treatment)
---------------------------------------------------------------
* ECOG performance status was the single strongest predictor: ORR fell monotonically
  21.3% → 15.5% → 11.2% across ECOG 0/1/2 (point-biserial r=-0.094, p=6.4e-99).
* Marrow disease burden adversely predicted response: blast_pct_marrow r=-0.041
  (p=1.0e-19); peripheral wbc_k_per_ul r=-0.019 (p=1.8e-5).
* Nutrition/cachexia signals: albumin_g_dl positively associated (r=+0.022, p=1.3e-6);
  weight_loss_pct_6mo strongly negative (r=-0.055, p=4.6e-35).
* Inflammation: crp_mg_l negatively associated (r=-0.021, p=2.9e-6).
* Treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine had a small but statistically significant positive
  main effect (17.35% vs 16.61%, p=0.031, +0.74 pp).
* idh1_mutation showed a strikingly favorable main effect: 22.13% vs 16.50%
  (chi-square p=3.5e-18, +5.63 pp).

Iterations 7-14 — biomarker × treatment interactions (the central clinical question)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The targeted-therapy hypotheses (the textbook AML rationale) largely did NOT replicate
in this cohort:
* FLT3-ITD × midostaurin: interaction coef -0.105 (p=0.22); FLT3-ITD × gilteritinib:
  +0.034 (p=0.69). No FLT3-inhibitor benefit specific to FLT3-ITD patients.
* FLT3-TKD × midostaurin/gilteritinib: both non-significant.
* IDH2 × enasidenib: interaction -0.124 (p=0.45). Numerically lower ORR in
  IDH2+/enasidenib+ stratum; no targeted benefit.
* IDH1 × ivosidenib: interaction -0.364 (p=0.027) — statistically significant but in
  the OPPOSITE direction from the textbook expectation. IDH1+ patients NOT given
  ivosidenib had ORR 22.5%; IDH1+ patients given ivosidenib had ORR 17.4%. This persists
  after adjustment (iter 25). Most plausibly reflects selection bias — sicker IDH1+
  patients being channeled to ivosidenib monotherapy — rather than a harmful effect of
  the drug.
* TP53 × venetoclax/azacitidine: interaction -0.087 (p=0.29). Direction-consistent with
  TP53 attenuating the ven/aza benefit, but not significant.
The two interactions that DID emerge as positive and statistically significant:
* NPM1 × venetoclax/azacitidine: interaction +0.261 (p=4.7e-5). NPM1+ patients on
  ven/aza had ORR 19.3% vs 16.0% off ven/aza; NPM1- patients showed essentially no
  benefit. This is the clearest predictive treatment-by-biomarker interaction in the
  data, consistent with published AML literature.
* unfit_for_intensive × venetoclax/azacitidine: interaction +0.104 (p=0.036). VenAza
  appears to deliver more benefit in patients flagged as unfit for intensive therapy.
And one significant negative interaction:
* complex_karyotype × 7+3: interaction -0.213 (p=0.0016). 7+3 was associated with
  numerically lower ORR in complex-karyotype patients than in non-CK patients,
  consistent with the well-known cytogenetics-resistance pattern.

Iterations 15-19 — sociodemographic / SNP / comorbidity effects
---------------------------------------------------------------
* Race: significant disparity. Black ORR 15.74% vs White ORR 17.28% (chi2 p=0.004,
  diff -1.54 pp). Overall race × ORR test p=0.0096. After adjustment for age, sex,
  ECOG, IDH1, NPM1, WBC, blast %, albumin, weight loss, CRP, and ven/aza, the
  Black-vs-White difference persisted (coef=-0.109, p=0.005), as did the Other category
  (coef=-0.196, p=0.031). Asian and Hispanic differences were not significant.
* Insurance type: no significant association (p~0.55).
* Rural residence: no association (p~0.99).
* Sex: no significant difference (p=0.33).
* Of 24 SNPs tested, snp_rs1050828 carriage was associated with higher ORR (18.48% vs
  16.77%, p=0.0056). This is the only SNP main effect with p<0.01; all others are
  non-significant under any reasonable multiple-comparison correction.
* Comorbidities: atrial_fibrillation modestly negative (-1.38 pp, p=0.025);
  heart_failure no association (p=0.72); diabetes/hypertension/COPD/CKD/HIV/MSI-high
  all non-significant.

Iterations 20-25 — multivariable and refinement analyses
--------------------------------------------------------
A logistic-regression model jointly including age, sex, ECOG, secondary AML, fitness
flag, complex karyotype, the four cytogenetic/molecular markers, baseline labs (WBC,
blast %, albumin, LDH, weight loss, CRP, NLR), and all six treatment indicators yielded
the following independently significant predictors of objective_response:
  ecog_ps                            -0.384   (p<1e-90)
  idh1_mutation                      +0.360   (p<1e-16)
  albumin_g_dl                       +0.118   (p=4.9e-7)
  weight_loss_pct_6mo                -0.040   (p<1e-33)
  blast_pct_marrow                   -0.0058  (p<1e-19)
  crp_mg_l                           -0.0073  (p<1e-5)
  wbc_k_per_ul                       -0.0022  (p<1e-4)
  treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine   +0.059   (p=0.016)
All other features (age, sex, secondary AML, FLT3 markers, IDH2, NPM1 main effect,
TP53, complex karyotype, individual targeted therapies) were non-significant in the
adjusted model.

Adding race indicators to a parsimonious adjusted model yielded race_black coef=-0.109
(p=0.005) and race_other -0.196 (p=0.031), Asian and Hispanic non-significant. The
unadjusted Black-White ORR gap therefore is not explained by the measured clinical or
laboratory variables.

Pre-specified ECOG × ven/aza and age × 7+3 interactions were not significant
(p=0.48 and p=0.61). A TP53 × complex_karyotype synergy hypothesis was also rejected
(p=0.70). A direct test of treatment_7plus3 main effect was non-significant (p=0.50);
treatment_midostaurin had no main effect and no FLT3-ITD-specific effect.

Final refinement: the IDH1 favorable prognostic effect is robust to inclusion of
treatment indicators, comorbidities, and the IDH1 × ivosidenib interaction term, with
coefficient ~+0.36 (p<1e-15). The paradoxical negative IDH1 × ivosidenib interaction
also persists after age/sex/ECOG adjustment (coef ~-0.36, p~0.03), strongly suggesting
selection bias as the driver rather than a true drug-induced harm.

Synthesis and conclusions
-------------------------
1. Response in this cohort is driven primarily by patient fitness and disease-burden
   indicators (ECOG, weight loss, marrow blast %, WBC, albumin, CRP) rather than by
   most molecular markers.
2. Among molecular markers, only IDH1 mutation acts as a strong favorable prognostic
   biomarker; FLT3, IDH2, NPM1 (main effect), TP53, and complex karyotype are not
   independently significant predictors after adjustment.
3. Treatment effects are modest. Venetoclax/azacitidine has a small positive overall
   benefit and is the only treatment with a significant main effect after adjustment.
4. The two clinically interpretable predictive interactions are NPM1 × ven/aza
   (positive — confirms biological hypothesis) and complex karyotype × 7+3 (negative —
   confirms cytogenetic resistance pattern). The unfit × ven/aza positive interaction
   is consistent with low-intensity therapy being well matched to less fit patients.
5. The expected FLT3-ITD × FLT3-inhibitor and IDH1/IDH2 × IDH-inhibitor synergies were
   NOT detected; the IDH1 × ivosidenib interaction was significantly negative,
   plausibly reflecting selection bias rather than drug effect.
6. There is a clinically meaningful and statistically significant racial disparity:
   Black patients have ~1.5 pp lower ORR than White patients overall and remain
   significantly worse off after multivariable adjustment for clinical variables. Other
   sociodemographic factors (insurance, rural residence, sex) showed no association
   with response.
7. Of 24 SNPs surveyed, only snp_rs1050828 showed a significant main effect; this
   should be considered hypothesis-generating given the multiple-comparisons context.
