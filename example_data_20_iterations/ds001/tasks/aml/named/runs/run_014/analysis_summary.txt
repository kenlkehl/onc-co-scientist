Analysis Summary - ds001_aml (n=50,000; objective_response rate ~16.9%)
========================================================================

Approach
--------
Across 25 propose-test-refine iterations, I tested hypotheses about the
relationships between patient features (genomics, labs, performance status,
comorbidities, demographics, SNPs), the six AML treatments captured in this
dataset (treatment_midostaurin, treatment_gilteritinib, treatment_ivosidenib,
treatment_enasidenib, treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine, treatment_7plus3) and
the binary outcome objective_response. Tests used chi-square for binary
contrasts, univariate logistic regression for continuous predictors, and
logistic regression with multiplicative interaction terms for treatment-by-
biomarker hypotheses; multivariable logistic regressions were used to assess
independence of effects. Significance threshold p<0.05.

Iteration walkthrough
---------------------
Iter 1 (adverse cytogenetic/clinical markers - tp53, complex karyotype,
secondary AML, unfit_for_intensive): every association with ORR was
non-significant (all p>0.5). The classical "adverse risk" labels did not
discriminate response in this cohort.

Iter 2 (favorable AML markers - npm1, idh1, idh2, flt3_itd): only
idh1_mutation was significant. IDH1+ patients had ORR 0.221 vs 0.165 in
IDH1- (diff +0.056, p=3.5e-18). NPM1, IDH2 and FLT3-ITD main effects were
null.

Iter 3 (treatment main effects): only treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine
showed a small positive main effect on ORR (0.173 vs 0.166 off, p=0.03).
The five other treatments had no detectable main effect.

Iter 4-5 (targeted-therapy x biomarker interactions): the canonical AML
biomarker-drug pairings did NOT show the expected positive interactions in
this cohort. Specifically:
  - flt3_itd x midostaurin: ns interaction (p=0.62)
  - flt3_itd x gilteritinib: ns interaction (p=0.91)
  - flt3_tkd x gilteritinib: ns interaction (p=0.55)
  - idh2_mutation x enasidenib: ns interaction (p=0.43)
  - idh1_mutation x ivosidenib: SIGNIFICANT but in the *opposite* direction
    (interaction logOR -0.364, p=0.027): IDH1+ patients receiving
    ivosidenib had ORR 0.174 vs 0.225 in IDH1+ patients NOT receiving it.
    This was confirmed in iter 18 after adjustment for age, ECOG and
    fitness status (logOR -0.382, p=0.021). Most plausibly explained by
    confounding by indication (sickest IDH1+ patients are channeled to
    ivosidenib in real-world practice) rather than a true negative drug
    effect.

Iter 6 (treatment x fitness): treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine showed a
positive interaction with unfit_for_intensive (logOR +0.10, p=0.036): in
unfit patients its ORR was 0.179 vs 0.163 off-drug (diff +0.016).
Treatment_7plus3 showed no detectable interaction with fitness.

Iter 7 (TP53 x venetoclax+aza; TP53+complex composite): the predicted
TP53-attenuates-venetoclax interaction was not significant. The dual
TP53/complex-karyotype composite did not differ from neither, contrary
to canonical AML biology.

Iter 8 (continuous lab/inflammation markers): albumin_g_dl SIG positive
(logOR +0.115, p=1.4e-6), crp_mg_l SIG negative (logOR -0.007 per mg/L,
p=2.8e-6), blast_pct_marrow SIG negative (logOR -0.0057, p=1.2e-19); LDH
and NLR were not significant.

Iter 9 (performance status & age): ecog_ps was the strongest single
predictor in the dataset (logOR -0.384 per point, p=1.8e-97).
weight_loss_pct_6mo was strongly negative (logOR -0.040, p=7.4e-35).
age_years was NOT significantly associated with response (p=0.72) -
contrary to the typical AML expectation.

Iter 10 (sex/SES): sex_female and rural_residence did not predict response
(both p>0.3). smoking_pack_years was non-significant.

Iter 11 (race, insurance): race_ethnicity showed a significant difference
across categories (chi2 p=0.0096; ORR white 0.173, hispanic 0.167, asian
0.163, black 0.157, other 0.147). insurance_type was not associated
overall, and uninsured-vs-private comparison was non-significant.

Iter 12 (chronic comorbidities): heart failure, CKD, diabetes, prior
malignancy each non-significant.

Iter 13 (PROs): of the four symptom grades, only appetite_loss_grade was
weakly significant (logOR -0.023, p=0.038); fatigue, pain, dyspnea were ns.

Iter 14 (cytopenias): wbc_k_per_ul was significant negative (logOR
-0.0022, p=1.9e-5); hemoglobin, platelets, ANC were ns.

Iter 15 (treatment effects after adjusting for age, ECOG, secondary AML,
complex karyotype, TP53, fitness): treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine
adjusted logOR +0.056, p=0.022, retains a positive association.
Treatment_7plus3 adjusted logOR was non-significant.

Iter 16 (hepatic/renal): creatinine, total bilirubin, INR all ns.

Iter 17 (SNPs spot-checks - MTHFR rs1801133, APOE rs429358, ABCB1
rs1045642): all ns; consistent with a screen of all 24 SNPs separately
(none survive Bonferroni 0.05/24).

Iter 18 (adjusted treatment-by-biomarker interactions): flt3_itd x
midostaurin and idh2_mutation x enasidenib interactions remain
non-significant after adjustment for age, ECOG and fitness; the
idh1_mutation x ivosidenib interaction stays significantly *negative*
after adjustment.

Iter 19 (race/insurance after adjustment): race_black-vs-white logOR
remains -0.113 (p=0.0035) after adjusting for age, ECOG, TP53, complex
karyotype, secondary AML, fitness. Insurance type non-significant after
adjustment.

Iter 20 (three-way ven+aza x unfit x age): in unfit patients aged <=75,
ven+aza diff was +0.021 (p<0.001); in unfit patients aged >75, diff was
small (+0.003, p=0.83) - i.e., ven+aza benefit was larger in younger-unfit
than older-unfit patients, contrary to the hypothesized direction.
Treatment_7plus3 in fit & young patients: ns.

Iter 21 (composites): adverse composite (TP53 OR complex karyo OR
secondary AML) showed no difference from neither composite. Favorable
composite (NPM1+/FLT3-ITD-) was also ns - the favorable molecular profile
expected from ELN risk stratification does not meaningfully separate ORR
in this cohort.

Iter 22 (inflammation/nutrition composite): low albumin (<3.5 g/dL) AND
high CRP (>10 mg/L) had ORR 0.134 vs 0.177 in clean patients (diff
-0.043, p=1e-6) - a strong joint signal of cachexia/inflammation.
High-LDH (above median) was ns.

Iter 23 (combination & comparative-effectiveness): in FLT3-ITD+ patients
already getting 7+3, adding midostaurin did not improve ORR. In unfit
patients receiving exactly one of (ven+aza vs 7+3), ven+aza ORR was 0.182
vs 0.165 for 7+3 (diff +0.017, p=0.025) - supporting ven+aza preference
over 7+3 in unfit patients.

Iter 24 (treatment receipt by SES): receipt of ven+aza differed
significantly across insurance_type categories; receipt of 7+3 differed
significantly across race_ethnicity. Both are channeling patterns rather
than outcome effects per se but matter for interpretation.

Iter 25 (final multivariable model): adjusted logOR for ecog_ps -0.384,
p=1.2e-97 (dominant predictor). NPM1 mutation: ns adjusted; TP53 mutation:
ns adjusted. Confirmed that ECOG PS is the single most important
predictor.

Hypotheses supported (significant in the predicted direction)
-------------------------------------------------------------
- IDH1 mutation -> higher ORR (h6).
- Higher ECOG PS -> lower ORR (h29, replicated in adjusted model h75).
- Higher blast_pct_marrow -> lower ORR (h28).
- Higher albumin_g_dl -> higher ORR (h25).
- Higher CRP -> lower ORR (h26).
- Higher weight_loss_pct_6mo -> lower ORR (h31).
- Higher WBC -> lower ORR (h48).
- Higher appetite_loss_grade -> lower ORR (h45, marginal).
- Treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine -> small positive main effect (h13)
  and survives adjustment for prognostic factors (h50).
- Treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine x unfit_for_intensive -> positive
  interaction (h20).
- Among unfit patients, ven+aza > 7+3 head-to-head (h70).
- Low-albumin + high-CRP composite -> lower ORR (h67).
- Race_ethnicity differences in ORR with black<white persisting after
  clinical adjustment (h35, h61) - a possible disparities signal.
- Differential receipt of treatments by insurance and race (h71, h72) -
  channeling/access patterns.

Hypotheses refuted (no signal or wrong direction)
-------------------------------------------------
- TP53 mutation, complex_karyotype, secondary_aml, unfit_for_intensive,
  age_years - none of these "classical adverse" markers had a significant
  univariate or adjusted association with ORR (h1-h4, h30, h73).
- NPM1 main effect (h5) and FLT3-ITD main effect (h8) were null.
- IDH2 main effect (h7) was null.
- The four canonical biomarker-targeted-drug pairings (FLT3-ITD x
  midostaurin/gilteritinib, FLT3-TKD x gilteritinib, IDH2 x enasidenib)
  were all null (h15, h16, h17, h19, h60).
- IDH1 x ivosidenib was significantly negative (h18, h59) - opposite of
  the hypothesized positive interaction; most plausibly explained by
  channeling/confounding rather than a true treatment harm.
- TP53 x ven+aza interaction (h22) was not significant.
- 24 germline SNPs screened - no signals survive multiple-testing
  correction (h55-h57 individually ns).
- LDH, hemoglobin, platelets, ANC, NLR, creatinine, bilirubin, INR -
  none significantly predicted ORR in univariate models.
- TP53/complex composite (h23), NPM1/FLT3 favorable composite (h66) -
  no detectable effect.
- ven+aza benefit was *not* greater in older-unfit than younger-unfit
  patients (h63 reversed).

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. Functional status and disease burden dominate response prediction.
   ECOG PS is by far the strongest single predictor (logOR -0.38,
   p~1e-97), followed by blast %, CRP, albumin and 6-month weight loss.
   Together these define a "fit, lower-burden, non-cachectic" phenotype
   that responds.
2. Among molecular markers, only IDH1 mutation showed a robust favorable
   main effect on ORR. Other classical AML risk variants (TP53, complex
   karyotype, secondary AML, FLT3-ITD, NPM1) did NOT separate response.
3. Targeted therapies did not show predicted biomarker-driven benefits in
   this binary objective_response endpoint. The IDH1 x ivosidenib result
   was paradoxically negative, consistent with confounding by indication
   in observational data rather than a true negative effect.
4. The strongest treatment signal was venetoclax+azacitidine, with a
   small positive main effect (OR ~1.05) that was modestly amplified in
   unfit patients and exceeded 7+3 in unfit patients head-to-head.
5. Demographic/SES variables suggest disparities: black-vs-white ORR
   gap persists after adjusting for clinical features (-0.11 logOR,
   p=0.0035), and access to ven+aza differs by insurance and 7+3 use
   differs by race. These warrant further investigation.
6. Age was not a predictor of response on its own - performance status
   captures the relevant fitness axis.
