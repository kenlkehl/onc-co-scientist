Analysis Summary — ds001_aml
============================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with AML, real-world EHR-derived dataset.
Outcome: objective_response (binary; overall rate 16.9%).
Method: 25 iterations of propose-test-refine. Tested with chi-square (binary
predictors), logistic regression (continuous predictors and adjusted models),
and logistic regression with multiplicative interaction terms (treatment ×
biomarker). Significance threshold p < 0.05; multivariable models additionally
reported.

================================================================================
HEADLINE FINDINGS
================================================================================

The dominant predictors of objective_response in this cohort were not the
canonical AML cytogenetic / molecular risk groups, but rather **patient
condition and disease-burden markers**: ECOG performance status, weight loss,
serum albumin, CRP, blast burden, and presenting WBC. Several classical AML
risk markers (TP53, NPM1, complex karyotype, FLT3-ITD, secondary AML, age)
showed essentially **no main-effect association** with response. Most matched
biomarker-targeted therapies failed to show predictive interactions with
their target lesion. Two non-clinical signals deserve attention: a residual
disparity by race after adjustment (lower response among Black patients), and
the only mechanistically plausible drug-class signal — venetoclax/azacitidine
in unfit patients.

================================================================================
WHAT IS ASSOCIATED WITH RESPONSE (signals)
================================================================================

Patient condition / functional status (strongest signals)
  - ECOG performance status (ecog_ps): adjusted log-OR -0.384 per unit,
    p = 1.2 × 10⁻⁹⁷.  By far the dominant predictor; effect survives every
    multivariable adjustment.
  - Weight loss in last 6 months (weight_loss_pct_6mo): log-OR -0.040 per
    percentage point, p = 7.4 × 10⁻³⁵.
  - Appetite-loss grade: log-OR -0.023 per grade, p = 0.038 (weaker; other
    PRO grades — fatigue, pain, dyspnea — were null).

Disease burden
  - Blast percentage marrow (blast_pct_marrow): log-OR -0.0057 per percent,
    p = 1.2 × 10⁻¹⁹.
  - Presenting WBC (wbc_k_per_ul): log-OR -0.0022 per unit, p = 1.9 × 10⁻⁵.

Inflammation / nutrition
  - Albumin (albumin_g_dl): log-OR +0.115 per g/dL, p = 1.4 × 10⁻⁶.
  - CRP (crp_mg_l): log-OR -0.0070 per mg/L, p = 2.8 × 10⁻⁶.
  - Composite "low albumin (<3.5) AND high CRP (>10)" vs neither: response
    13.4% vs 17.7%, diff -4.3 percentage points, p = 9.6 × 10⁻⁷ — the largest
    binary-feature effect size observed in the cohort.

Treatments
  - Venetoclax + azacitidine (treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine): unadjusted
    response 17.3% vs 16.6% off, diff +0.7 pp, p = 0.032; adjusted log-OR
    +0.056, p = 0.022. Effect is real but modest.
  - 7+3, midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib: NO significant
    main effect on response; some directionally negative.

Treatment-effect modification (interactions)
  - Venetoclax/azacitidine × unfit_for_intensive (interaction term):
    log-OR +0.104, p = 0.036. Within unfit patients (n ≈ 20,056), VEN/AZA
    raised response from 16.3% to 17.9% (diff +1.6 pp); within fit patients
    the same drug had no effect. This is the only treatment × clinical-feature
    interaction that was directionally consistent with established AML
    practice (VIALE-A).
  - Within unfit patients, head-to-head VEN/AZA vs 7+3 (mutually exclusive):
    response 18.2% vs 16.5%, diff +1.7 pp, p = 0.025 favoring VEN/AZA.

Demographics
  - IDH1 mutation (idh1_mutation): unadjusted response 22.1% vs 16.5%,
    diff +5.6 pp, p = 3.5 × 10⁻¹⁸. A clear positive prognostic signal — but
    in this dataset it is NOT modified favorably by ivosidenib (see below).
  - Race / ethnicity: chi-square across 5 categories p = 0.0096; after
    adjusting for age, ECOG, TP53, complex karyotype, secondary AML, and
    unfit_for_intensive, Black-vs-white log-OR = -0.113, p = 0.0035.
    Insurance type was not associated with response (chi-square p = 0.54),
    and uninsured-vs-private had no detectable difference.

================================================================================
WHAT IS NOT ASSOCIATED WITH RESPONSE (refuted hypotheses)
================================================================================

Classical AML cytogenetic / molecular risk markers (all NULL on response):
  - TP53 mutation: 16.6% vs 16.9%, p = 0.52. No interaction with VEN/AZA
    (p = 0.29) or 7+3.
  - Complex karyotype: 16.8% vs 16.9%, p = 0.82.
  - Secondary AML: 16.9% vs 16.9%, p = 0.92.
  - "Unfit for intensive" alone: 16.9% vs 16.9%, p = 0.84.
  - NPM1 mutation: +0.6 pp, p = 0.11 (trend, not significant).
  - FLT3-ITD: +0.4 pp, p = 0.40.
  - Age: per-year log-OR essentially zero (p = 0.72).
  - The composite "adverse risk" (TP53 OR complex karyotype OR secondary)
    was likewise null (p = 0.65), and the favorable composite "NPM1+/FLT3-ITD−"
    was non-significant (p = 0.30).

Targeted-therapy / matched-biomarker interactions (all NULL or backwards):
  - Midostaurin × FLT3-ITD: interaction log-OR -0.105, p = 0.22; within
    FLT3-ITD+, midostaurin actually trended *worse* (16.1% vs 17.4%).
  - Gilteritinib × FLT3-ITD: interaction log-OR +0.034, p = 0.69.
  - Gilteritinib × FLT3-TKD: directionally negative (p = 0.33).
  - Ivosidenib × IDH1 mutation: SIGNIFICANT but in the WRONG direction.
    Within IDH1+, response was 17.4% on ivosidenib vs 22.5% off
    (diff -5.1 pp, p = 0.027 unadjusted; adjusted interaction log-OR -0.382,
    p = 0.021). This refutes the hypothesis of a positive predictive
    biomarker × treatment interaction in this cohort.
  - Enasidenib × IDH2 mutation: log-OR -0.124, p = 0.45; within IDH2+,
    enasidenib trended toward lower response (14.4% vs 16.7%).
  - Among FLT3-ITD+ patients receiving 7+3, adding midostaurin made no
    difference (17.3% vs 17.3%, p = 1.0). The expected RATIFY-style benefit
    is absent.

Comorbidities and most labs:
  - Heart failure, CKD, diabetes, prior malignancy: all p > 0.13.
  - Hemoglobin, platelets, ANC: all non-significant.
  - LDH, creatinine, total bilirubin, INR: all non-significant.
  - Pharmacogenomic SNPs (rs1801133/MTHFR, rs429358/APOE, rs1045642/ABCB1):
    all non-significant.

Treatment-receipt equity:
  - Receipt of VEN/AZA did NOT differ significantly by insurance type
    (range 39.8–40.7%, p = 0.53).
  - Receipt of 7+3 did NOT differ significantly by race/ethnicity (range
    44.1–46.2%, p = 0.25).
  Together with the null insurance-vs-response result, this argues that the
  observed Black-vs-white response gap is NOT explained by differential
  receipt of the two main treatments tracked here.

INTEGRATED MULTIVARIABLE MODEL (Iteration 25)
================================================================================

Model: objective_response ~ age + ECOG + TP53 + complex karyotype + secondary
AML + unfit + NPM1 + FLT3-ITD + albumin + LDH + VEN/AZA.

Adjusted log-OR (selected):
  - ecog_ps:                          -0.384  (p = 1.2 × 10⁻⁹⁷)   ✓ negative
  - npm1_mutation:                    +0.043  (p = 0.10)          trend, ns
  - tp53_mutation:                    -0.028  (p = 0.49)          NOT sig
  - treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine: +0.056  (p = 0.022; adj iter 15) ✓

Hypotheses h73 (TP53 negative in adjusted model) and h74 (NPM1 positive in
adjusted model) were NOT supported. Hypothesis h75 (ECOG negative) was
strongly supported.

================================================================================
SYNTHESIS / OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

1. In this real-world AML cohort, response is driven primarily by patient
   condition (ECOG, weight loss, appetite loss) and disease/inflammation burden
   (blast %, WBC, albumin, CRP) — NOT by molecular cytogenetic risk groups.
   This is a striking departure from published prospective trial findings,
   where TP53, complex karyotype, NPM1, and FLT3-ITD have large independent
   prognostic effects.

2. The most reproducible mechanistic drug signal is venetoclax + azacitidine
   in unfit patients, with a small but statistically robust positive effect
   on response and a positive treatment × unfit interaction. This matches
   established practice and serves as a positive control.

3. Targeted FLT3 inhibitors (midostaurin, gilteritinib) and IDH inhibitors
   (ivosidenib, enasidenib) failed to show predictive benefit within their
   matched-biomarker subgroups. Ivosidenib showed a significant interaction
   in the OPPOSITE direction within IDH1-mutant patients, suggesting either
   strong confounding by indication (worse-prognosis IDH1+ patients selected
   for ivosidenib in real-world practice), data-quality issues with
   biomarker/treatment matching in EHR data, or genuine real-world effectiveness
   below trial expectations.

4. After clinical adjustment, Black race remained associated with lower
   objective response (log-OR -0.11, p = 0.004), yet receipt of the two main
   treatment classes did not differ by race/insurance, and insurance type
   was not directly associated with response. The disparity therefore is
   not explained by the variables captured here and warrants further
   investigation (treatment intensity, supportive care, residual unmeasured
   confounders).

5. The most useful clinically-actionable composite identified: low albumin
   (<3.5 g/dL) AND elevated CRP (>10 mg/L), associated with a 4.3-percentage-
   point absolute reduction in response (p < 10⁻⁶). Combined with ECOG, this
   captures the bulk of the predictable variation in this cohort.

Caveats: This is observational EHR data, so all treatment-effect estimates
are subject to confounding by indication. Many "null" findings for known
prognostic markers may reflect that prognostic signal has been absorbed
into treatment-selection patterns. The unexpectedly inverse ivosidenib ×
IDH1 interaction is the clearest example of this pattern.
