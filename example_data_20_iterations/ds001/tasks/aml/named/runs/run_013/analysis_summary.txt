ds001_aml — Analysis Summary
============================

Cohort: 50,000 AML patients, EHR-derived. Outcome: objective_response (binary, 16.9% prevalence overall).
Iterations: 25 of 25 used. All p-values are nominal (no multiplicity correction); given n=50,000, even
modest effects are easily detected, so I focus on effect size and clinical plausibility alongside p.

SECTION 1. Strong, independent prognostic factors
-------------------------------------------------

The univariate screens (iters 1–6) and multivariable logistic regression (iter 12, adjusted for age, sex,
ECOG, AML genetics, key labs, and treatment) converge on a tight short list of robust predictors:

  • ECOG performance status (h1, h12_ecog_ps) — the dominant predictor.
        Mean ECOG = 0.66 in responders vs 0.83 in non-responders (diff −0.17, p ≈ 3e-103).
        Adjusted log-OR per ECOG point = −0.38 (p ≈ 1e-96). Effect direction matches clinical intuition.

  • Weight_loss_pct_6mo (h2, h12_weight_loss_pct_6mo) — recent weight loss is strongly unfavorable.
        3.38% vs 3.95% (diff −0.57, p ≈ 7e-39); adjusted log-OR per +1% = −0.040 (p ≈ 1e-34).

  • Blast_pct_marrow (h3a, h12_blast_pct_marrow) — higher marrow disease burden is unfavorable.
        58.2% vs 60.3% (diff −2.08, p ≈ 1e-19); adjusted log-OR per +1% = −0.0058 (p ≈ 6e-20).

  • Wbc_k_per_ul (h3b, h12_wbc_k_per_ul) — higher leukocyte count is unfavorable.
        19.1 vs 20.4 (diff −1.33, p ≈ 1e-5); adjusted log-OR per +1k = −0.0022 (p ≈ 1e-5).

  • Albumin_g_dl (h4a, h12_albumin_g_dl) — higher albumin (better nutrition) is favorable.
        3.83 vs 3.80 (diff +0.029, p ≈ 1e-6); adjusted log-OR per +1 g/dL = +0.118 (p ≈ 1e-6).

  • Crp_mg_l (h4b, h12_crp_mg_l) — higher inflammation is unfavorable.
        5.65 vs 6.17 (diff −0.51, p ≈ 4e-7); adjusted log-OR per +1 mg/L = −0.0072 (p ≈ 1e-6).

  • Idh1_mutation (h5, h12_idh1_mutation) — strongly favorable, the largest molecular effect in the data.
        Response rate IDH1+ = 22.1% vs IDH1− = 16.5% (absolute Δ = +5.6 pp, p ≈ 4e-18).
        Adjusted log-OR = +0.36 (p ≈ 3e-17), i.e., OR ≈ 1.43 independent of other features.

SECTION 2. Treatment effects
----------------------------

Univariate (iter 13): of the six AML regimens, only treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine showed a significant
main-effect difference in raw response (17.4% vs 16.6%, p = 0.031). The others (midostaurin, gilteritinib,
ivosidenib, enasidenib, 7+3) had effectively flat marginal response rates (|Δ| ≤ 0.9 pp, all p > 0.13).

Multivariable (iter 12) confirmed: treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine was the only treatment indicator
independently associated with response (adjusted log-OR +0.059, p = 0.016). Other regimens were null.

Targeted-therapy/biomarker matches were tested in iters 7–10:

  • IDH1 × ivosidenib (h7) — surprising NEGATIVE interaction. Unadjusted cell rates: IDH1+/ivo− = 22.5%,
        IDH1+/ivo+ = 17.5% (interaction log-OR = −0.36, p = 0.027). After full multivariable adjustment
        (h21_int) the interaction remained negative (log-OR = −0.36, p = 0.032). The favorable effect of
        IDH1 mutation (h21_main) is fully explained by the mutation itself and is not amplified — and may
        actually be diminished — by ivosidenib in this cohort. This contradicts the canonical clinical
        expectation and is the most counterintuitive finding.

  • IDH2 × enasidenib (h8) — null. Interaction log-OR −0.12, p = 0.45.

  • FLT3-ITD × midostaurin (h9_treatment_midostaurin) — null. Interaction log-OR −0.10, p = 0.22.
  • FLT3-ITD × gilteritinib (h9_treatment_gilteritinib) — null. Interaction log-OR +0.03, p = 0.69.

  • Unfit_for_intensive × venetoclax/azacitidine (h10) — significant, in the expected direction.
        VenAza raises response in unfit patients (16.3% → 17.9%) while leaving fit patients unchanged
        (16.8% → 17.0%); interaction log-OR +0.10, p = 0.036. This matches the clinical rationale for
        VenAza in patients ineligible for intensive induction.

SECTION 3. AML genetics beyond IDH1
-----------------------------------

Iter 6 showed that in this cohort, the prognostic AML mutations one would normally expect to dominate
were null: NPM1 (Δ +0.6 pp, p = 0.11), TP53 (Δ −0.4 pp, p = 0.52), complex karyotype (Δ −0.1 pp,
p = 0.82), FLT3-ITD (univariate Δ +0.4 pp, p = 0.58; iter 13/9), FLT3-TKD (Δ −0.6 pp, p = 0.36).
Only IDH1 stood out among the molecular features. After multivariable adjustment (iter 12), npm1_mutation
showed a borderline favorable trend (log-OR +0.04, p = 0.097) but did not reach significance.

SECTION 4. Demographics, comorbidities, symptoms, SDOH
------------------------------------------------------

  • Age (h11a) and sex (h11b) — both null univariately and in the multivariable model.

  • Comorbidities (iter 14) — almost all null. Atrial_fibrillation showed a modest unfavorable signal
        (15.6% vs 17.0%, p = 0.025); the rest (CKD, HF, DM, HTN, COPD) were null.

  • Symptom burden (iter 15) — fatigue/pain/dyspnea/cough were null. Appetite_loss_grade was modestly
        unfavorable (Δ −0.026, p = 0.037), consistent with the weight-loss/albumin/CRP "cachexia"
        signal already established.

  • SDOH (iter 16) — rural_residence, education_years, and insurance_type were all null. Race_ethnicity
        showed a small but significant difference across groups (chi-square p = 0.010): response rates
        were highest in white (17.3%) and lowest in "other" (14.7%) and black (15.7%) patients. The
        absolute spread is ≈2.6 pp and would not survive adjustment for ECOG/labs in any clinically
        meaningful sense, but the disparity is detectable at this sample size.

SECTION 5. Pharmacogenomic SNPs
-------------------------------

A screen of 24 SNPs (iter 17) returned one nominally significant hit: snp_rs1050828 (G6PD variant)
with response 18.5% vs 16.8% (diff +1.7 pp, p = 0.006). All other SNPs had p > 0.05; none would survive
Bonferroni correction (threshold 0.002 for 24 tests). Treat snp_rs1050828 as a hypothesis-generating
finding rather than a confirmed pharmacogenomic effect.

SECTION 6. Other labs, vitals, and prior therapy
------------------------------------------------

Hepatic / renal panel (iter 18): alkaline phosphatase showed a borderline positive association
(diff +1.17 U/L, p = 0.050); AST, ALT, total bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, LDH were all null.
Electrolytes, glucose, platelets, ANC, ALC, hemoglobin, BP, heart rate, SpO2, BMI (iter 19) — all null.
Prior therapy lines and prior modality flags (iter 20) — all null. Years_since_diagnosis was borderline
unfavorable (diff −0.04 yr, p = 0.052).

SECTION 7. Pre-specified interaction probes
--------------------------------------------

  • Ecog × treatment_7plus3 (iter 22, h22) — interaction log-OR −0.018, p = 0.63. No evidence that
        intensive induction is differentially harmful or beneficial across ECOG strata in this cohort.
  • Albumin-low × CRP-high additivity (iter 23, h23) — interaction log-OR +0.016, p = 0.74. The two
        risk markers act additively on the log-OR scale; no synergy beyond their main effects.
  • Sex × IDH1 (iter 24, h24) — interaction log-OR −0.06, p = 0.50. The favorable IDH1 effect is
        comparable in men and women.

SECTION 8. Composite multivariable discrimination
-------------------------------------------------

Iter 25: predicted response probabilities from the iter-12 multivariable Logit, split into quartiles,
showed strong rank ordering of observed response — Q1 = 11.2%, Q2 = 14.7%, Q3 = 18.4%, Q4 = 23.3%
(chi-square p ≈ 2e-154; Q4−Q1 absolute Δ = +12.1 pp). The model captures real, clinically interpretable
signal, driven mainly by ECOG, weight loss, blast %, albumin, CRP, WBC, IDH1 mutation, and (modestly)
VenAza treatment.

SECTION 9. Hypotheses supported vs refuted
------------------------------------------

Supported:
  H1  ECOG ↑ ⇒ response ↓ (very strong)
  H2  Weight loss ↑ ⇒ response ↓ (very strong)
  H3a Blast % ↑ ⇒ response ↓
  H3b WBC ↑ ⇒ response ↓
  H4a Albumin ↑ ⇒ response ↑
  H4b CRP ↑ ⇒ response ↓
  H5  IDH1 mutation ⇒ response ↑ (strong, independent)
  H10 Unfit × VenAza positive interaction (modest, expected)
  H13_venaza  VenAza ⇒ response ↑ (small main effect)
  H14_atrial_fibrillation, H15_appetite_loss_grade, H16d (race), H17_snp_rs1050828, H18_alkaline_phosphatase
       — borderline / weak associations.
  H21_main  IDH1 favorability persists after full adjustment.
  H25  Multivariable model discriminates Q1 vs Q4 strongly.

Refuted (or null in this cohort):
  H6_npm1_mutation, H6_tp53_mutation, H6_complex_karyotype  — no main effects.
  H7  IDH1 × ivosidenib positive interaction — REFUTED (interaction is negative, p = 0.027).
  H8  IDH2 × enasidenib positive interaction — null.
  H9_midostaurin, H9_gilteritinib  FLT3-ITD × FLT3 inhibitor synergy — null.
  H11a, H11b  Age and sex effects — null.
  H13_midostaurin, H13_gilteritinib, H13_ivosidenib, H13_enasidenib, H13_7plus3 — null main effects.
  Most comorbidities, symptoms, vitals, electrolytes, hepatic/renal labs, SDOH features, prior-therapy
  flags — null.
  H22, H23, H24  ECOG×7+3, albumin×CRP, sex×IDH1 interactions — null.

SECTION 10. Overall conclusions
-------------------------------

The dataset behaves like a clinical prognostic cohort in which the dominant signal comes from "host
fitness and disease burden" features (ECOG, weight loss, marrow blast %, WBC, albumin, CRP) plus a
single molecular factor — IDH1 mutation, which is favorable. Most of the canonical AML-prognosis
mutations (NPM1, TP53, FLT3-ITD, complex karyotype) and most of the targeted-therapy/biomarker
matchings (FLT3 inhibitors for FLT3-ITD; ivosidenib for IDH1; enasidenib for IDH2) do not show the
expected enrichment in objective_response. The only treatment-by-context interaction that behaves
as the literature would predict is VenAza in unfit patients. Notably, ivosidenib appears to interact
*negatively* with IDH1 status, which is biologically counter-intuitive and worth flagging — it could
reflect a peculiarity of how the cohort was assembled or how response was ascertained, rather than a
true biological effect. Demographic/SDOH effects are essentially absent except for a small race signal,
and pharmacogenomic SNPs collectively add little. A simple Logit on the host-fitness + IDH1 + VenAza
features captures most of the predictable variance, separating Q1 from Q4 of predicted risk by ~12
absolute percentage points of observed response.
