ds001_aml — Analysis Summary
============================================================
Cohort: 50,000 AML patients. Outcome: objective_response (binary).
Overall ORR: 0.1690 (8,452 responders).

Across 25 iterations we proposed 85 hypotheses spanning treatment main effects,
biomarker-treatment interactions, prognostic features, sociodemographic
disparities, and sanity-null variables (solid-tumor markers/genes). Evidence
across the full panel of analyses tells a clear and partially surprising
story: prognosis is dominated by patient-fitness and disease-burden features,
while many "textbook" AML biomarker-by-targeted-therapy interactions did NOT
reproduce in this cohort.

------------------------------------------------------------
1) Strongest predictors of objective_response (highly significant)
------------------------------------------------------------
- ECOG performance status (ecog_ps): the dominant predictor by far.
  Logit slope = -0.384 per ECOG point (OR = 0.68), p ~ 2e-97 univariate
  (h16) and p ~ 3e-97 in the multivariable model (h69). Higher ECOG strongly
  reduces response.
- Albumin (albumin_g_dl): higher albumin -> higher ORR. Univariate beta =
  +0.115 (OR ~ 1.12 per g/dL), p ~ 1e-6 (h22); retained in multivariable
  model (beta = +0.118, p ~ 8e-7) (h75).
- Weight loss in last 6 months (weight_loss_pct_6mo): negative association
  with ORR. Beta = -0.040 (OR ~ 0.96 per %), p ~ 7e-35 (h23).
- C-reactive protein (crp_mg_l): higher CRP -> lower ORR. Beta = -0.0070
  per mg/L, p ~ 3e-6 (h24).
- Marrow blast percentage (blast_pct_marrow): higher blast % -> lower ORR.
  Beta = -0.0057 (OR ~ 0.994 per percentage point), p ~ 1e-19 (h20).
- Presenting WBC (wbc_k_per_ul): higher WBC -> lower ORR. Beta = -0.0022,
  p ~ 2e-5 (h19).
- These six features (ECOG, albumin, weight loss, CRP, blast %, WBC) form a
  coherent fitness/disease-burden axis.

------------------------------------------------------------
2) Treatment main effects
------------------------------------------------------------
- Venetoclax/azacitidine (treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine): small but
  significant positive association with ORR (0.1735 vs 0.1661), p ~ 0.03
  (h3). Retained as positive in multivariable model (aOR = 1.06, p ~ 0.02)
  (h73). Effect is concentrated in unfit_for_intensive=1 patients
  (ven/aza-vs-no-ven/aza diff = +0.0162 in unfit, +0.0026 in fit;
  interaction beta = +0.10, p ~ 0.04) (h26), and within unfit patients
  ven/aza-only outperforms 7+3-only (h28, p ~ 0.025).
- 7+3 induction (treatment_7plus3): NO main-effect difference in ORR
  (h2: diff = +0.002, p = 0.54). In multivariable adjustment also
  non-significant (h72, aOR = 1.02, p = 0.52).
- Midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib: NONE showed
  significant main-effect ORR benefit in the overall cohort (h4, h5, h74).
  Some point estimates were even slightly negative.

------------------------------------------------------------
3) Biomarker-by-targeted-therapy interactions — the surprising result
------------------------------------------------------------
A central a-priori expectation in AML is that targeted agents work in
their matched mutation subgroup. The data did NOT show this:

- FLT3-ITD x midostaurin: NOT significant. In FLT3-ITD+, midostaurin had
  ORR 0.161 vs 0.174 without (lower, not higher); interaction p = 0.22 (h7).
- FLT3-ITD x gilteritinib: NOT significant; direction inconsistent
  (h8, p = 0.69).
- FLT3-any (ITD or TKD) x midostaurin: NOT significant (h67, p = 0.29).
- FLT3-TKD x gilteritinib: NOT significant (h66, p = 0.33).
- IDH1 x ivosidenib: interaction term DID reach significance, but with a
  NEGATIVE sign opposite to the textbook expectation. Within IDH1+ (n =
  3,624), ivosidenib was associated with LOWER ORR (0.175 vs 0.226,
  diff = -0.051, borderline p = 0.05) (h84). Interaction beta = -0.36,
  p = 0.027 (h9). Within IDH1-negatives ivosidenib was non-significant (h11).
- IDH2 x enasidenib: NOT significant; direction also opposite of textbook
  (in IDH2+, enasidenib ORR 0.144 vs 0.167; h10, h85).
- A composite biomarker_match indicator (FLT3+TKI, IDH1+ivo, IDH2+ena) did
  NOT show better ORR than non-match patients (diff = -0.006, p = 0.43)
  (h82).

Interpretation: in this dataset, the biomarker-targeted treatment
relationships that drive real-world AML practice are absent or, for IDH1
ivosidenib, reversed. This is the most clinically striking finding.

------------------------------------------------------------
4) Cytogenetic / molecular prognostic markers
------------------------------------------------------------
- TP53 mutation: NOT significantly associated with lower ORR (h12: diff =
  -0.0037, p = 0.52). After multivariable adjustment, also non-significant
  (h70, aOR = 0.97, p = 0.44).
- Complex karyotype: NO main effect (h13, p = 0.82). HOWEVER, complex
  karyotype interacts with 7+3 (h64): in complex_karyotype+, 7+3 ORR =
  0.156 vs 0.179 without; interaction beta = -0.21, p ~ 0.0016. So 7+3
  appears worse in complex karyotype patients than in non-complex.
- NPM1 mutation: borderline higher ORR univariate (h14: diff = +0.006,
  p = 0.11); not significant after multivariable adjustment (h71, p = 0.09).
  However, NPM1 x ven/aza shows a strong positive interaction (h65, beta =
  +0.26, p ~ 7e-7) — the ven/aza ORR boost is much larger in NPM1-mutated
  patients.
- Adverse_cyto_mol composite (TP53 OR complex_karyotype): not significantly
  associated with ORR after adjustment (h83, p = 0.48).

------------------------------------------------------------
5) Other clinical / lab features
------------------------------------------------------------
- Age (age_years): NOT associated with ORR in this cohort (h15: beta =
  +0.0004 per year, p = 0.72; h68 adjusted p = 0.73). This is unusual
  for AML.
- Secondary AML, unfit_for_intensive: no main-effect ORR difference (h17,
  h18). The unfit flag operates via treatment-selection interactions, not
  a direct main effect.
- LDH: not significant (h21, p = 0.35).
- Neutrophil-lymphocyte ratio (NLR): not significant (h25, p = 0.56).
- Hemoglobin, platelets, ANC: no significant association with ORR (h40-42).
- Liver/renal panel: bilirubin, creatinine, INR all non-significant
  (h43-45). Alkaline phosphatase was marginally significant (h46, p =
  0.048) but with a tiny positive slope.
- Symptom grades: fatigue, pain, dyspnea non-significant; appetite loss
  grade marginally significant (h50, beta = -0.023, p = 0.038).
- Sex_female, heart_failure, CKD, diabetes, BMI, smoking pack-years,
  systolic BP, SpO2: all non-significant (h36-39, h78-81).

------------------------------------------------------------
6) Sociodemographic findings
------------------------------------------------------------
- Race/ethnicity omnibus: significant (h29, chi2 p = 0.0096). Black
  patients had ORR 0.157 vs White 0.173 (diff = -0.015, p = 0.004) (h30).
  Hispanic vs White was not significant (h31). This racial disparity
  PERSISTED after multivariable adjustment for clinical features and
  treatments — adjusted log-odds for Black = -0.114 (aOR = 0.89), p =
  0.003 (h76). The disparity is not explained by the measured clinical
  covariates in this dataset.
- Insurance type: omnibus non-significant (h32). Uninsured vs private
  identical (h33). Adjusted: also non-significant (h77).
- Rural residence and education_years: no association (h34, h35).

------------------------------------------------------------
7) Sanity-null findings (variables irrelevant to AML)
------------------------------------------------------------
- Solid-tumor genomic markers (HER2 amp, BRAF V600E, MSI-high, ROS1
  fusion): all non-significant (h54-57), as expected.
- Tumor markers (PSA, CA-125, CEA): not significant (h51-53).
- Pharmacogenomic SNPs (ABCB1 rs1045642, MTHFR rs1801133, APOE rs429358,
  TNF rs1800629): all non-significant (h58-61).

------------------------------------------------------------
Overall conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------
- Objective response in this AML cohort is driven primarily by patient-
  fitness and disease-burden axes (ECOG performance status, albumin, CRP,
  weight loss, blast percentage, WBC). ECOG is by far the strongest single
  predictor.
- Among treatments, only venetoclax/azacitidine showed an independent
  positive association with response, and this benefit is amplified in
  unfit patients and in NPM1-mutated patients. 7+3 induction showed no
  net main-effect benefit and appears worse in complex-karyotype patients.
- The biomarker-matched targeted-therapy interactions that AML pharmacology
  predicts (FLT3 inhibitors in FLT3-ITD/TKD, IDH inhibitors in IDH-mutated
  disease) were not present in this cohort. The IDH1 x ivosidenib
  interaction was actually in the opposite direction.
- A residual racial disparity (lower ORR for Black patients) persisted
  after multivariable adjustment, while insurance, rural residence, and
  education showed no signal.
- Variables that should be unrelated to AML response (solid-tumor markers,
  non-myeloid genomic alterations, pharmacogenomic SNPs) showed no
  association, supporting internal validity of the analysis pipeline.
