Analysis Summary — ds001_aml
================================================================================
Cohort: 50,000 patient records (commercial-EHR-derived AML cohort).
Outcome: `objective_response` (binary; cohort base rate 16.9%).
Method: 25 iterations of propose -> test -> refine. Tests used chi-square for
binary group comparisons, univariable logistic regression for continuous
predictors, and logistic regression with multiplicative interaction terms for
treatment-by-biomarker hypotheses. Significance threshold p<0.05.

================================================================================
1. Disease prognostic markers (Iter 1-2)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Refuted (no association with ORR):
 - tp53_mutation         (diff -0.4 pp, p=0.52)  [h1]
 - complex_karyotype     (diff -0.1 pp, p=0.82)  [h2]
 - secondary_aml         (diff -0.05 pp, p=0.91) [h3]
 - unfit_for_intensive   (diff +0.07 pp, p=0.84) [h4]
 - npm1_mutation         (diff +0.6 pp, p=0.11)  [h5]
 - idh2_mutation         (diff -0.4 pp, p=0.50)  [h7]
 - flt3_itd              (diff +0.4 pp, p=0.40)  [h8]

Supported:
 - idh1_mutation         (diff +5.6 pp, p=3.5e-18) [h6]
   IDH1-mutated patients have a substantially higher response rate (22.5%) than
   IDH1-wildtype (16.9%). This is the largest single-mutation effect in the
   cohort — but, see Iter 5/18, it is NOT explained by ivosidenib receipt.

2. Treatment main effects (Iter 3)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Refuted (no marginal ORR difference):
 - midostaurin (p=0.92), gilteritinib (p=0.13), ivosidenib (p=0.76),
   enasidenib (p=0.17), 7+3 (p=0.54)

Supported (small):
 - venetoclax_azacitidine: +0.74 pp ORR (p=0.031) [h13]

3. Treatment-by-biomarker (predictive) interactions (Iter 4-5, 7, 18)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The clinically expected predictive pairings did NOT hold:
 - midostaurin x flt3_itd:   interaction logOR -0.012, p=0.22 (refuted) [h15/h58]
 - gilteritinib x flt3_itd:  p=0.69 (refuted) [h16]
 - gilteritinib x flt3_tkd:  p=0.33 (refuted) [h17]
 - enasidenib x idh2_mutation: p=0.45 (refuted) [h19/h60]
 - venetoclax+aza x tp53:    p=0.28 (no negative effect-modification) [h22]

Notable inversion:
 - ivosidenib x idh1_mutation: interaction logOR -0.51, p=0.027 [h18/h59]
   Among IDH1-mutated patients, ORR was LOWER on ivosidenib (17.4%, n=298) than
   off ivosidenib (22.5%, n=3326). This is the opposite of the expected clinical
   pattern. Ivosidenib was assigned essentially at random with respect to IDH1
   status (8.2% receipt in IDH1+ vs 7.8% in IDH1-), so the "negative" effect
   reflects noise/confounding rather than a biologic predictive signal.

Supported (favorable):
 - venetoclax+aza x unfit_for_intensive: in unfit patients, ven+aza yields
   +1.6 pp ORR; interaction p=0.036 [h20]
 - In unfit patients receiving exactly one of {ven+aza, 7+3}, ven+aza gave
   ORR 17.9% vs 7+3 16.2% (diff +1.7 pp, p=0.025) [h70]

4. Clinical / lab predictors (Iter 8-9, 14, 16)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Strongly supported:
 - ecog_ps (logOR -0.38 per unit, p=2e-97) [h29] — by far the dominant predictor
   of response in this cohort.
 - albumin_g_dl (logOR +0.115 per g/dL, p=1.4e-6) [h25]
 - crp_mg_l (logOR -0.007 per mg/L, p=2.8e-6) [h26]
 - blast_pct_marrow (logOR -0.006 per 1%, p=1.2e-19) [h28]
 - weight_loss_pct_6mo (logOR -0.040 per 1%, p=7.4e-35) [h31]
 - wbc_k_per_ul (logOR -0.002 per K/uL, p=1.9e-5) [h48]
 - low-albumin + high-CRP composite: -4.3 pp ORR vs uninflamed
   (p=9.6e-7) [h67]

Refuted:
 - ldh_u_l (p=0.35), nlr (p=0.56), age_years (p=0.72), sex_female (p=0.33),
   creatinine, bilirubin, INR, hemoglobin, platelets, ANC, smoking pack-years,
   rural residence, fatigue/pain/dyspnea grades, comorbidities (HF, CKD,
   diabetes, prior malignancy), and the three SNPs probed (rs1801133, rs429358,
   rs1045642) — all p>0.05.

Note: appetite_loss_grade was nominally significant (logOR -0.023, p=0.038) but
small.

5. Multivariable adjusted treatment effects (Iter 15, 25)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Supported:
 - venetoclax+aza, adjusted for age, ECOG, secondary_aml, complex_karyotype,
   tp53_mutation, unfit_for_intensive: adj logOR +0.056, p=0.022 [h50]
 - In a full multivariable model (age, ECOG, TP53, complex karyotype, secondary,
   unfit, NPM1, FLT3-ITD, albumin, LDH, ven+aza), ECOG remained the dominant
   independent predictor (adj logOR -0.384, p=1e-97) [h75]
   TP53 (adj logOR -0.028, p=0.49) and NPM1 (adj logOR +0.043, p=0.10) were
   not independently significant — refuting [h73] and [h74].

Refuted:
 - 7+3 (adj p=0.46) — no incremental benefit after adjustment for clinical
   covariates [h51]

6. Demographics, equity, access (Iter 10-11, 19, 24)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Mixed:
 - Crude race_ethnicity x ORR chi-square is significant (range 14.3% to 16.9%
   across groups, chi2 p=0.010) [h35]; after adjusting for age, ECOG, TP53,
   complex karyotype, secondary AML, and unfit status, race='black' vs white
   adj logOR -0.113 (p=0.0035) [h61]. A small, statistically detectable
   adjusted residual but the absolute gap is ~1-2 pp.
 - insurance_type marginal differences NOT significant (p=0.54) [h36];
   uninsured-vs-private adj logOR -0.006 (p=0.92) [h62]. Refuted.
 - rural_residence (p=1.0), sex_female (p=0.33), smoking pack-years (p=0.51):
   refuted.
 - Receipt of ven+aza by insurance: not significant (p=0.53) [h71]; receipt of
   7+3 by race: not significant (p=0.25) [h72]. No detectable disparities in
   treatment receipt in this cohort.

7. Composite-risk and combination-therapy hypotheses (Iter 21, 23)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 - Adverse composite (TP53 OR complex karyotype OR secondary AML): refuted
   (diff -0.16 pp, p=0.65) [h65]
 - Favorable composite (NPM1+ AND FLT3-ITD-): refuted (diff +0.41 pp, p=0.30)
   [h66]
 - Among FLT3-ITD+ on 7+3, adding midostaurin: NULL (p=1.0) [h69]

8. Three-way effect-modification (Iter 20)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 - Ven+aza benefit in unfit was not modified by age (>75 vs <=75): NULL [h63]
 - 7+3 benefit in fit-and-young (age <=60): diff -0.04 pp, p=0.99 [h64] — refuted

================================================================================
Overall conclusions
================================================================================
1. The dominant determinant of response in this cohort is functional status
   (ECOG PS), accompanied by a coherent constellation of frailty/inflammation
   markers: low albumin, high CRP, larger weight loss, higher marrow blast
   percentage, and higher presenting WBC.

2. Disease genetics (TP53, complex karyotype, secondary AML, FLT3-ITD/TKD,
   IDH2, NPM1) showed essentially no marginal or adjusted association with
   ORR — except IDH1, where mutation-positive patients had a +5.6 pp higher
   response rate. The IDH1 ORR advantage was NOT mediated by ivosidenib;
   indeed, IDH1+ patients receiving ivosidenib had a LOWER response rate than
   IDH1+ patients not receiving it (17.4% vs 22.5%; interaction p=0.027).

3. Predictive treatment-by-biomarker pairings classically expected in AML
   (midostaurin/gilteritinib for FLT3, ivosidenib for IDH1, enasidenib for
   IDH2) were NOT observed. Targeted-therapy receipt was almost independent of
   the corresponding biomarker (e.g., 8% of both IDH1+ and IDH1- patients got
   ivosidenib), and conditional ORRs went the wrong direction or were null.
   This suggests the cohort either does not reflect biomarker-driven
   prescribing or that response to these targeted agents was not encoded in
   the simulated outcome.

4. Venetoclax+azacitidine showed a small but reproducible benefit overall
   (crude +0.7 pp; adjusted logOR +0.056, p=0.022) and a modest positive
   interaction with unfit_for_intensive (+1.6 pp benefit in unfit; ven+aza
   beat 7+3 by 1.7 pp head-to-head in unfit patients, p=0.025). 7+3 had no
   independent or fit-subgroup benefit after adjustment.

5. Equity findings were largely null: receipt of major treatments did not
   differ by race or insurance, and after adjustment for clinical severity
   only a small (1-2 pp) adjusted residual association between
   race='black' and lower ORR remained (p=0.0035). Insurance, sex, rural
   residence, and smoking history all had no detectable association with
   response.

6. The three pharmacogenomic SNPs we probed (rs1801133, rs429358, rs1045642)
   showed no association with response — consistent with this being EHR-style
   data with little PGx signal.

In summary: in this AML cohort the response signal is driven by host
functional/inflammatory status and (intriguingly) IDH1 mutation, with a
small ven+aza treatment benefit in unfit patients. Classic AML cytogenetic
risk and predictive biomarker-treatment interactions did not replicate.
