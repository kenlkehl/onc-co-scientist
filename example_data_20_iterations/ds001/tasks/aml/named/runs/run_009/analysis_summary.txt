Analysis Summary — ds001_aml (50,000 patients, outcome: objective_response, overall ORR 16.9%)
================================================================================================

Approach
--------
The cohort is annotated as acute myeloid leukemia (AML) and contains AML-specific treatments
(midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, venetoclax+azacitidine, "7+3"), AML
biomarkers (FLT3-ITD/TKD, IDH1, IDH2, NPM1, TP53, complex karyotype, secondary AML), classical
clinical prognostics (ECOG, marrow blast %, WBC, albumin, CRP, LDH, etc.), demographics, and a
large set of AML-irrelevant biomarkers (HER2, MET ex14, RET/ROS1/NTRK fusions, lung-cancer
mets, CA-125/CEA/PSA, 24 SNPs). I tested patient-feature main effects on response, six
matched and unmatched treatment main effects, and a panel of treatment-biomarker interactions
that the AML literature would predict, plus demographic and SNP scans, and finished with a
multivariable logistic regression and several refined subgroup analyses.

Hypotheses supported by the data
--------------------------------
1. IDH1 mutation is a strong, INDEPENDENT positive prognostic biomarker. ORR is 22.13% in
   IDH1+ patients vs 16.50% in IDH1- (chi-square p = 3.5e-18, +5.6 percentage points).
   The adjusted logOR after controlling for age, sex, ECOG, NPM1, FLT3-ITD, WBC, marrow
   blast %, albumin and CRP is +0.36 (p = 1.2e-17), essentially unchanged from crude. IDH1+
   and IDH1- patients have indistinguishable mean ECOG (0.78 vs 0.80) and age (67.8 vs 68.0),
   so the advantage is not a confounding-by-fitness artefact. (h2_1, h17_1, h17_2.)

2. ECOG performance status, marrow blast %, WBC, albumin, and CRP are classical, robust
   prognostic features. ORR drops from 21.3% (ECOG 0) to 11.2% (ECOG 2). Logistic logOR
   per SD: blast_pct -0.11 (p = 1.2e-19), WBC -0.056 (p = 1.9e-5), CRP -0.064 (p = 2.8e-6),
   albumin +0.058 (p = 1.4e-6). These remain significant in the multivariable model.
   (h3_1 through h3_5, h13_1.)

3. Venetoclax+azacitidine produces a meaningful objective-response benefit, and the benefit
   is concentrated in NPM1-mutated patients. Crude ORR of ven+aza vs no ven+aza is
   17.35% vs 16.61% (+0.74pp, p = 0.04). Stratified by NPM1: in NPM1+ patients (n = 15,014)
   ven+aza ORR is 19.34% vs 15.96% (+3.38 pp, p = 9.8e-8), whereas in NPM1- patients there
   is no benefit (-0.39 pp, p = 0.34). The interaction in a logistic model is logOR = +0.26,
   p = 6.6e-7 — the strongest predictive signal in the dataset. The benefit holds within
   both FLT3-ITD strata. There is also a smaller but significant ven+aza x unfit_for_intensive
   positive interaction (logOR +0.10, p = 0.04). (h1_1, h6_1, h6_2, h7_1 to h7_3, h14_1, h23_1.)

4. 7+3 underperforms in complex-karyotype patients. Within complex_karyotype = 1
   (n = 7,462), 7+3 ORR is 15.55% vs 17.87% off (-2.32 pp, p = 0.008). The
   treatment_7plus3 x complex_karyotype interaction logOR is -0.21 (p = 1.6e-3).
   In CK- patients the 7+3 effect is non-significantly positive. (h8_1 to h8_3.)

5. Marginal racial differences in ORR exist (white 17.28%, hispanic 16.70%, asian 16.33%,
   black 15.74%, other 14.72%; chi-square p = 0.010, white-vs-non-white p = 0.005), but
   these differences disappear after adjustment for ECOG, age, IDH1, NPM1 and labs (all
   adjusted race coefficients have p > 0.13), suggesting the marginal gap is driven by
   compositional differences rather than race itself. (h9_1, h9_2, h9_3.)

Hypotheses refuted (or null) in the data
-----------------------------------------
6. FLT3 inhibitors (midostaurin, gilteritinib) show no objective-response benefit overall
   and no interaction with FLT3-ITD or FLT3-TKD. Within FLT3-ITD+ (n = 10,000), midostaurin
   ORR is 16.13% vs 17.41% off (p = 0.27); gilteritinib 16.90% vs 17.21% (p = 0.78). The
   midostaurin x FLT3-ITD interaction is logOR -0.10 (p = 0.22). Identical pattern for
   FLT3-TKD. This contrasts with established AML literature. (h4_1 to h4_3, h20_1.)

7. Ivosidenib does not benefit IDH1-mutated patients in this cohort and even shows a
   paradoxical negative interaction. Within IDH1+ (n = 3,624), ivosidenib ORR is 17.45%
   vs 22.55% off (-5.10 pp, p = 0.050). The treatment_ivosidenib x idh1_mutation
   interaction is logOR -0.36 (p = 0.027). The IDH1+ subgroup already has elevated
   baseline ORR, but ivosidenib does not extend that advantage. (h5_1, h5_2.)

8. Enasidenib shows no benefit in IDH2-mutated patients (-2.33 pp, p = 0.29) and no
   significant interaction with IDH2. (h5_3.)

9. TP53 mutation, complex karyotype as a pure main effect, secondary AML, FLT3-ITD/TKD
   and NPM1 (as main effects, before considering interactions) all have non-significant
   marginal associations with objective_response. The absence of a TP53 prognostic main
   effect is notable since TP53 is among the most adverse markers in real AML cohorts.
   (h2_2, h2_3, h2_4, h2_5.)

10. Common comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart failure, CAD, prior
    malignancy, ILD) all have null associations (every p > 0.13). Patient-reported
    symptoms are mostly null; only appetite_loss_grade reaches nominal significance
    (logOR per SD = -0.025, p = 0.038). (h11_1, h11_2.)

11. The 24 SNP indicators show only one nominal hit (rs1050828, +1.72 pp, p = 0.006),
    which would not survive multiple-comparison correction across 24 tests. No SNP
    genotype is a credible predictor of response. (h12_1, h12_2.)

12. The lung-/solid-tumor biomarker columns (HER2 amplification, MET exon14 skipping,
    RET/ROS1/NTRK/NRG1 fusions, BRAF V600E, FGFR alteration, CDKN2A loss, KEAP1, PIK3CA,
    PTEN, MSI-high, liver/bone/adrenal mets, pleural and pericardial effusion) are all
    null predictors of objective_response, consistent with their being clinically
    irrelevant decoy variables for an AML cohort. (h21_1.)

13. Insurance type, rural residence, education years, sex, age, smoking pack-years and
    prior lines of therapy are all non-significant for objective_response in this cohort.
    The age null is particularly striking given that age is typically the dominant
    AML prognostic variable. (h10_1 to h10_3, h18_1 to h18_3, h19_1 to h19_3.)

Overall conclusion
------------------
In this 50,000-patient AML-labelled cohort, classical patient-severity features (ECOG,
blast %, WBC, albumin, CRP) are the dominant prognostic drivers, IDH1 mutation is a
striking and apparently independent positive prognostic biomarker, and venetoclax+
azacitidine is the only treatment with a credible objective-response benefit. The
dominant predictive signal is the venetoclax+azacitidine x NPM1-mutation interaction
(adjusted logOR +0.26, p = 6.6e-7), with a secondary signal that 7+3 is detrimental in
complex-karyotype patients. Counter to standard AML literature, FLT3 inhibitors,
ivosidenib and enasidenib show no target-matched benefit (ivosidenib in IDH1+ is even
paradoxically inferior). TP53 mutation, age, sex, race (after adjustment), insurance,
comorbidities, the 24 SNP loci and the AML-irrelevant solid-tumor biomarker columns
are all null. The pattern of strong patient/disease-severity main effects combined with
selective treatment-biomarker interactions (NPM1 x ven+aza, complex karyotype x 7+3) is
the most clinically actionable summary of this dataset.
