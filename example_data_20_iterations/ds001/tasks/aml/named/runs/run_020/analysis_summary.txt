Analysis Summary — ds001_aml (50,000 patients)
==================================================

Outcome
-------
Binary objective_response. Overall response rate: 16.9% (8,452 / 50,000).

Approach
--------
We progressed from broad univariate scans (binary features, continuous labs,
ordinal scales) to multivariable logistic regressions and pre-specified
biomarker × treatment interactions, then closed with a synthesis model that
combined the strongest signals.

What was supported
------------------
1. Performance status (ecog_ps) is the dominant prognostic variable. Response
   falls monotonically from 21.3% at ECOG 0, to 15.5% at ECOG 1, to 11.2% at
   ECOG 2 (Pearson r=-0.094, p~6e-99). In the multivariable model, ECOG
   carried the largest negative coefficient (beta = -0.384, OR per point ~ 0.68).

2. IDH1 mutation is the dominant favorable molecular marker. Univariate ORR
   is 22.1% in IDH1+ versus 16.5% in IDH1- (chi-square p = 3.5e-18).
   Adjusted IDH1 effect remains beta = +0.36 (p ~ 2.5e-17). The benefit
   is comparable in patients younger and older than 70.

3. Continuous baseline markers behave like a clinically coherent prognostic
   panel. Responders have lower 6-month weight loss (3.38% vs 3.95%,
   p = 4.6e-35), lower marrow blasts (58.2% vs 60.3%, p = 1.0e-19),
   higher albumin (3.83 vs 3.80 g/dL, p = 1.4e-6), lower CRP (5.65 vs
   6.17 mg/L, p = 2.9e-6) and lower disease WBC (19.1 vs 20.4 K/uL,
   p = 1.9e-5). Each survives multivariable adjustment.

4. Treatment effects are concentrated in venetoclax/azacitidine, and they
   are biomarker-driven:
   - IDH1 x venetoclax/azacitidine interaction beta = +0.764 (p = 4.6e-19).
     ORR among IDH1-mutated patients on ven/aza is 29.8% versus 17.1% off
     ven/aza (+12.6 absolute pp); among IDH1- patients the drug shows no
     net effect (16.4% vs 16.6%).
   - NPM1 x venetoclax/azacitidine interaction beta = +0.267 (p = 4.4e-7);
     +3.4 pp ORR gain in NPM1+ patients on ven/aza, no gain in NPM1-.
   - Unfit-for-intensive x ven/aza interaction beta = +0.103 (p = 0.039),
     consistent with the regimen's intended population.
   - Adjusted ven/aza main-effect coefficient is +0.059 (p = 0.016), so the
     drug is also marginally beneficial on average after biomarkers and
     prognostics are accounted for.

5. 7+3 chemotherapy interacts negatively with complex karyotype: in
   complex-karyotype AML, ORR on 7+3 is 15.5% vs 17.9% off 7+3 (interaction
   beta = -0.214, p = 0.0017). Without complex karyotype, 7+3 is roughly
   neutral.

6. Race disparity: after adjustment for age, sex, ECOG, mutations, albumin,
   weight loss, and blasts, Black race carried a beta of -0.108 (p = 0.0055)
   versus White; "Other" race -0.194 (p = 0.032). Hispanic and Asian were
   not significant. Treatment-receipt rates and ECOG distributions were
   essentially identical across racial groups, and race x treatment
   interactions were not significant, so the disparity is a main-effect
   gap that is NOT explained by the variables in the dataset (including
   treatment access, performance status, and mutational profile).

7. SNP rs1050828 was the strongest pharmacogenomic signal (adjusted
   beta = +0.122, p = 0.0044), but it does not survive a strict Bonferroni
   correction across the 24-SNP panel; the panel is otherwise null.

8. Atrial fibrillation was associated with a small reduction in ORR
   (15.6% vs 17.0%, p = 0.025). No other comorbidity, and aggregate
   comorbidity count, was significantly associated.

What was refuted (or unsupported)
---------------------------------
1. Classic targeted-therapy biomarker pairings did not behave as expected:
   - FLT3-ITD x midostaurin interaction p = 0.17, point estimate negative.
   - FLT3-ITD x gilteritinib interaction p = 0.58, near zero.
   - FLT3-TKD x midostaurin / gilteritinib both near zero (p >= 0.22).
   - IDH2 x enasidenib interaction p = 0.41.
   - IDH1 x ivosidenib interaction was statistically significant but in
     the OPPOSITE direction (beta = -0.36, p = 0.029): IDH1+ patients on
     ivosidenib had a LOWER ORR (17.4%) than IDH1+ patients off ivosidenib
     (22.5%). Within IDH1+ AML, ven/aza dominates ivosidenib for objective
     response.
   None of these four targeted therapies showed a positive main effect
   in the multivariable model either.

2. TP53 mutation x ven/aza interaction was directionally negative but did
   not reach significance (beta = -0.112, p = 0.17).

3. Patient age and patient sex have no detectable effect on ORR in either
   univariate analyses or the multivariable model.

4. Insurance type, education years, rural residence, and smoking pack-years
   were each null after adjustment.

5. Symptom-grade variables (fatigue_grade, pain_nrs, dyspnea_grade,
   cough_grade, appetite_loss_grade) were essentially uninformative for
   ORR; only appetite_loss_grade reached nominal p < 0.05.

6. Aggregate comorbidity count (sum of 8 chronic-disease flags) was not
   associated with ORR (r = -0.005, p = 0.29).

7. Treatment-effect modification by age, ECOG, and sex was non-significant
   for all major treatments (all interaction p > 0.30).

8. Three-way IDH1 x ven/aza x ECOG interaction was non-significant
   (p = 0.46), so the IDH1 x ven/aza synergy is robust across performance
   strata.

Synthesis
---------
The most parsimonious story for objective response in this cohort is:
(a) ECOG drives prognosis, (b) IDH1 mutation is the strongest favorable
biomarker, especially when combined with venetoclax/azacitidine, where
the response rate nearly doubles to ~30%, (c) NPM1 is a secondary
positive predictive marker for ven/aza benefit, (d) 7+3 is harmful
in complex karyotype, (e) the four targeted therapies (midostaurin,
gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib) do not deliver detectable
biomarker-matched benefit in this dataset, and (f) Black-vs-White race
shows a small, statistically significant ORR gap that is not explained by
ECOG, mutations, or differential treatment receipt.

Practical takeaways for trial design / treatment selection in this cohort:
prioritize ven/aza in IDH1+ (and to a lesser extent NPM1+) patients;
avoid 7+3 in complex-karyotype AML; do not expect biomarker-matched
benefit from the FLT3 or IDH targeted agents observed here; investigate
the residual Black-White ORR gap because it is not mediated by the
measured clinical variables.
