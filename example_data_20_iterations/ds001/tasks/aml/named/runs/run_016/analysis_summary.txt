========================================================================
Analysis summary — ds001_aml (50,000 AML patients)
Outcome: objective_response (overall ORR = 0.1690)
Total iterations executed: 16
========================================================================

APPROACH
------------------------------------------------------------------------
We probed the dataset across 16 iterations, ~66 hypotheses, ~66 statistical
tests. The plan moved from broad/marginal effects to biology-driven
treatment x biomarker interactions, then to clinical/lab predictors,
sociodemographics, comorbidities, an SNP screen, and finally a
comprehensive multivariable logistic-regression sanity check.

We hypothesised the standard AML-biology pattern:
  - FLT3 inhibitors (midostaurin, gilteritinib) help FLT3-ITD/TKD+
  - Ivosidenib helps IDH1-mutated patients
  - Enasidenib helps IDH2-mutated patients
  - Venetoclax/azacitidine helps especially unfit_for_intensive patients
  - TP53, complex karyotype, secondary AML carry adverse main effects
  - NPM1 carries a favourable main effect

Many of these biology-motivated hypotheses were NOT supported by the
data. Below we summarise what was actually observed.

HEADLINE FINDINGS (what the data showed)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

1) Marginal treatment effects on ORR are essentially flat, except a
   small ven/aza signal.
   - midostaurin: ORR 0.169 vs 0.169 off (p=0.92)
   - gilteritinib: ORR 0.163 vs 0.170 off (p=0.13)
   - ivosidenib: ORR 0.171 vs 0.169 off (p=0.76)
   - enasidenib: ORR 0.161 vs 0.170 off (p=0.17)
   - venetoclax+azacitidine: ORR 0.173 vs 0.166 off (p=0.032)  *** sig
   - 7+3: ORR 0.170 vs 0.168 off (p=0.54)
   Only ven/aza shows a small but reproducible main-effect benefit
   (~0.7 pp absolute).

2) Classical FLT3-inhibitor x FLT3 mutation interactions were NOT
   detected.
   - In FLT3-ITD+ (n=10,000), midostaurin: ORR 0.161 vs 0.174, p=0.25
   - In FLT3-ITD+, gilteritinib: ORR 0.169 vs 0.172, p=0.75
   - flt3_itd x midostaurin interaction logOR = -0.10, p=0.22
   - flt3_itd x gilteritinib interaction logOR = +0.03, p=0.69
   - In FLT3-TKD+, gilteritinib: ORR 0.141 vs 0.166, p=0.20
   After multivariable adjustment for age, ECOG, TP53, complex
   karyotype, secondary AML, and NPM1 (iter 12), the FLT3-ITD x
   FLT3-inhibitor interactions remained non-significant.
   Conclusion: FLT3-targeted activity is not detectable in this cohort.

3) Ivosidenib in IDH1-mutated AML showed a SIGNIFICANT but NEGATIVE
   association — opposite to biology.
   - In IDH1+ (n=3,624): ivo ORR=0.174 vs no-ivo ORR=0.225, diff=-5.1pp,
     p=0.042
   - idh1 x ivosidenib interaction logOR = -0.36, p=0.027
   - After multivariable adjustment (iter 12), the negative interaction
     persisted (logOR=-0.38, p=0.021)
   Specificity check: in IDH1-wild-type, ivo had no effect (p=0.34).
   This is most likely confounding by indication (ivosidenib is given
   to patients with worse expected response within the IDH1+ subgroup);
   either way, the data here do NOT support a positive ivo benefit
   signal in IDH1+ patients.

4) Enasidenib in IDH2-mutated AML showed no detectable benefit.
   - In IDH2+ (n=4,679): ena ORR=0.144 vs no-ena ORR=0.167, p=0.26
   - idh2 x enasidenib interaction logOR = -0.12, p=0.45 (also negative
     after adjustment, p=0.45)
   Direction was nominally negative; not statistically significant.

5) Venetoclax+azacitidine showed the strongest biology-consistent
   patterns.
   - In unfit_for_intensive (n=20,056): ven/aza ORR=0.179 vs 0.163
     (diff=+1.6pp, p=0.0027)  *** sig
   - unfit x ven/aza interaction logOR = +0.10, p=0.036  *** sig
   - In TP53-WT (n=45,015): ven/aza ORR=0.175 vs 0.166 (p=0.017)  *** sig
   - In TP53-mutated (n=4,985): ven/aza ORR=0.164 vs 0.167 (p=0.74,
     null) — consistent with the well-known TP53 resistance pattern,
     although the formal tp53 x ven/aza interaction term was not
     significant (p=0.28).
   - npm1 x ven/aza interaction: logOR=+0.26, p=6.6e-07  *** sig
     This is the strongest interaction in the dataset and is consistent
     with NPM1-mutated AML being especially venetoclax-sensitive.

6) 7+3 induction: no meaningful interaction with unfit status.
   - In unfit: 7+3 ORR=0.169 vs 0.170 (p=0.90)
   - unfit x 7+3 interaction logOR = -0.03, p=0.50

7) Adverse-biology mutations (TP53, complex karyotype, secondary AML)
   showed essentially NO main effect on ORR — a notable null finding.
   - tp53_mutation: 0.166 vs 0.169 (p=0.51)
   - complex_karyotype: 0.168 vs 0.169 (p=0.80)
   - secondary_aml: 0.169 vs 0.169 (p=0.90)
   - npm1_mutation: 0.173 vs 0.167 (p=0.11) — small positive trend, NS
   In a multivariable logistic adjusting for all four (iter 5/7), none
   was a significant predictor of ORR.

8) Clinical/lab covariates were the strongest predictors of response.
   - albumin_g_dl: logOR per unit = +0.115 (p=1.4e-06)  *** sig
     Adjusted (iter 7): logOR = +0.118 (p=8e-07)  *** sig
   - blast_pct_marrow: logOR = -0.0057 (p=1.2e-19)  *** sig
   - wbc_k_per_ul: logOR = -0.0022 (p=1.9e-05)  *** sig
   - ecog_ps: logOR = -0.384 (p=1.8e-97) *** sig — by far the largest
     effect in the dataset; each ECOG unit roughly halves response odds.
   - ldh_u_l: logOR = -0.00014 (p=0.35), null
   - age_years: logOR = +0.0004 (p=0.72), null — surprising; in this
     cohort age is not a significant ORR predictor.

9) Symptoms / weight loss.
   - weight_loss_pct_6mo: logOR = -0.040 (p=7e-35)  *** sig (very strong)
   - fatigue_grade: NS (p=0.21)
   - pain_nrs: NS (p=0.34)
   - nlr: NS (p=0.56)
   - sex_female: NS (p=0.32)

10) Sociodemographics.
    - race_ethnicity omnibus chi-square: p=0.010 *** sig (small absolute
      differences across categories; range ~0.147 'other' to ~0.172
      white).
    - insurance_type omnibus: p=0.54, null.
    - uninsured vs private: p=0.97, null.
    - rural_residence: p=0.99, null.
    The race association is statistically detectable at N=50,000 but
    differences in ORR between groups are <2.5 percentage points.

11) Comorbidities (CKD, heart failure, diabetes, prior malignancy,
    prior chemotherapy): all individually NS for ORR (p=0.13-0.70).

12) SNP panel was noise.
    - 2 of 24 SNPs (snp_rs1050828, snp_rs2032582) reached nominal
      p<0.05 but neither survived Bonferroni correction (alpha=0.0021).
    - Best p-value across the panel was p=0.0053.
    Conclusion: the 25-SNP panel does not contain detectable signal
    for objective_response in this cohort.

13) Comprehensive multivariable confirmation (iter 14).
    A logistic model with all main effects (age, ECOG, secondary AML,
    unfit, complex karyotype, TP53, NPM1, FLT3-ITD/TKD, IDH1/IDH2, all
    six treatments, albumin, LDH) and 5 interaction terms gave:
      flt3_itd x mido      : logOR=-0.10, p=0.23 (NS)
      flt3_itd x gilt      : logOR=+0.04, p=0.67 (NS)
      idh1     x ivo       : logOR=-0.38, p=0.045 (negative, sig)
      idh2     x ena       : logOR=-0.12, p=0.46 (NS)
      unfit    x ven/aza   : logOR=+0.13, p=0.022 (positive, sig)
    H14_1 (all four targeted interactions positive and significant)
    was REFUTED.

14) Matched-targeted-therapy summary (iter 16).
    Defining a "matched targeted therapy" indicator (FLT3-ITD/TKD with
    mido or gilt; IDH1+ with ivo; IDH2+ with ena), patients on a
    matched therapy actually had slightly LOWER ORR (0.163 vs 0.170,
    diff=-0.6pp, p=0.29) — null/inverted, not the expected positive
    matched-targeting effect.

CONCLUSIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
This 50,000-patient AML cohort behaves very differently from
expectations grounded in the published AML literature:

  - The classical FLT3-ITD x FLT3-inhibitor and IDH x IDH-inhibitor
    response benefits are NOT detectable; if anything, ivosidenib
    appears NEGATIVELY associated with response in IDH1-mutated
    patients (most plausibly confounding by indication, but the data
    do not contain a counter-signal).

  - Adverse cytogenetics/mutations (TP53, complex karyotype, secondary
    AML) have NO main effect on objective response in this cohort —
    another departure from biology.

  - The ONE strongly biology-consistent finding is venetoclax+
    azacitidine: it shows a small overall benefit, a positive
    interaction with unfit_for_intensive, an ORR benefit that is
    attenuated to null in TP53-mutated patients, and a strong positive
    interaction with NPM1 (NPM1 x ven/aza logOR=+0.26, p<1e-6).

  - The strongest predictors of objective response are clinical and
    laboratory: ECOG performance status (very large negative effect),
    weight loss in the prior 6 months (large negative effect), serum
    albumin (positive), bone-marrow blast percentage (negative), and
    WBC (negative).

  - Sociodemographic disparities are detectable for race/ethnicity at
    this sample size (omnibus chi-square p=0.010) but absolute
    differences are small (<2.5 percentage points). Insurance type,
    uninsured status, and rural residence show no association with
    ORR.

  - The 25-SNP panel does not predict response (no SNP survives
    Bonferroni; best p=0.0053).

Bottom line: in this cohort, response to AML therapy is driven much
more by global host fitness (ECOG, albumin, weight loss) and disease
burden (blast %, WBC) than by any specific genotype-treatment matching,
with the notable exception of venetoclax/azacitidine — particularly
in unfit and NPM1-mutated patients.
