ds001_aml — Analysis Summary
============================

Cohort: 50,000 adults with acute myeloid leukemia (AML) abstracted from
EHR-linked vendor data. Outcome: objective_response (binary, ORR overall
= 16.9%). Six treatment indicators (midostaurin, gilteritinib,
ivosidenib, enasidenib, venetoclax+azacitidine, 7+3) and the standard
AML biomarker panel (FLT3-ITD/TKD, IDH1/2, NPM1, TP53, complex
karyotype, secondary AML, fitness/ECOG).

Across 25 iterations I tested treatment main effects, biomarker main
effects, biomarker × treatment interactions (the canonical
matched-therapy hypotheses), demographic and socioeconomic factors,
comorbidities, symptom burden, hematologic and chemistry indices, SNP
genotypes, and a multivariable adjusted model.

==================================================================
1. Treatment main effects (Iter 1)
==================================================================
None of the six treatments shows a clinically large main-effect
benefit. The only nominally significant signal is venetoclax +
azacitidine, with ORR 17.3% on-treatment vs 16.6% off (chi2 p = 0.031,
diff +0.007). The other treatments' p-values range 0.13–0.92.

This is consistent with an EHR-style cohort where treatment is
assigned non-randomly (e.g. fitter patients get 7+3, biomarker-positive
patients get matched targeted therapy), so unadjusted main effects
mostly reflect indication confounding rather than causal benefit.

==================================================================
2. Biomarker / cytogenetics main effects (Iter 2, 18)
==================================================================
The biggest surprise of the cohort: idh1_mutation is a strong POSITIVE
predictor of ORR (22.1% with vs 16.5% without, diff +5.6 pp, chi2
p = 3.5e-18). After multivariable adjustment (Iter 19) idh1_mutation
remains independently associated with response (adj OR ≈ 1.43,
p = 2.1e-17).

In contrast, the canonical "favorable" NPM1 main effect is small
(+0.6 pp, p = 0.11) and the canonical "adverse" markers (TP53,
complex_karyotype, secondary_aml) show no main effect on ORR
(all p > 0.5). The composite favorable group (NPM1+ AND FLT3-ITD-) and
the adverse group (TP53+ OR complex_karyotype) likewise are null.

So in this dataset the textbook ELN-style risk stratification does NOT
translate into ORR differences — the only mutation that "behaves" as
expected is IDH1, and even that is unusually strong vs the literature.

==================================================================
3. Biomarker × treatment interactions (Iter 3–6, 10–12, 14, 17, 22–24)
==================================================================
The matched-targeted-therapy hypotheses are LARGELY UNSUPPORTED:

- FLT3-ITD × midostaurin: interaction beta = -0.105, p = 0.22.
  Direction is the wrong way (FLT3+/mido+ ORR 16.1% vs FLT3+/mido-
  17.4%).
- FLT3-ITD × gilteritinib: beta +0.034, p = 0.69. Null.
- FLT3-TKD × midostaurin: beta +0.023, p = 0.87. Null.
- FLT3-TKD × gilteritinib: beta -0.152, p = 0.33. Null.
- IDH1 × ivosidenib: beta -0.364, p = 0.027 — significant in the
  WRONG direction (IDH1+/ivo+ ORR 17.4% < IDH1+/ivo- 22.5%). This
  almost certainly reflects channelling: ivosidenib is given to the
  sicker IDH1+ patients, so within IDH1+ the ones who got ivosidenib
  fare worse than the IDH1+ who didn't.
- IDH2 × enasidenib: beta -0.124, p = 0.45. Null.

So none of the four canonical biomarker→targeted-therapy pairings
shows a positive interaction in this cohort.

Two interaction findings DO emerge clearly:

- complex_karyotype × treatment_7plus3: interaction beta = -0.213,
  p = 0.0016 (significant negative interaction). Complex-karyotype
  patients given 7+3 do measurably worse than complex-karyotype
  patients given other therapies — fitting the clinical view that
  intensive induction is poorly suited to adverse cytogenetics.
- unfit_for_intensive × treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine: interaction
  beta = +0.104, p = 0.036 (positive). Unfit patients gain
  proportionally more from venaza than fit patients (ORR 17.9%
  unfit/venaza+ vs 16.3% unfit/venaza-). This matches venaza's
  approved indication for older/unfit patients.

TP53 × 7+3 (p = 0.59), TP53 × venaza (p = 0.29), NPM1 × 7+3 (p = 0.10),
ECOG × 7+3 (p = 0.63), age × 7+3 (p = 0.61) are all null.

==================================================================
4. Performance status, age, fitness (Iter 7)
==================================================================
ECOG performance status is the dominant prognostic signal in the
entire dataset. Per 1-point higher ECOG, OR for response = 0.68
(beta = -0.384, p = 1.8e-97). It remains the strongest predictor in
the multivariable model (adj OR 0.68, p = 2.7e-97). Age_years,
remarkably, is null on ORR (p = 0.72) — counterintuitive in real-world
AML but consistent across analyses here. The unfit_for_intensive flag
is also null on ORR alone.

==================================================================
5. Disease burden, inflammation, nutrition (Iter 8, 9, 21)
==================================================================
- Higher blast_pct_marrow → lower ORR (OR/% = 0.994, p = 1.2e-19).
- Higher wbc_k_per_ul → lower ORR (p = 1.9e-5).
- ldh_u_l null (p = 0.35).
- Higher albumin_g_dl → higher ORR (OR/g/dL = 1.12, p = 1.4e-6).
- Higher CRP → lower ORR (p = 2.8e-6); NLR null.
- Hemoglobin, platelets, ANC, ALC: all null individually.

==================================================================
6. Symptom burden (Iter 20)
==================================================================
weight_loss_pct_6mo is a strong negative predictor (beta = -0.040,
p = 7.4e-35). appetite_loss_grade also nominally significant
(p = 0.038). Other symptom measures (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough)
are not.

==================================================================
7. Sociodemographic factors (Iter 13)
==================================================================
- sex_female: null (diff +0.003, p = 0.33).
- rural_residence: null (p = 1.0).
- education_years, smoking_pack_years: null.
- race_ethnicity: chi2 p = 0.0096 — small but real heterogeneity
  (white 17.3%, hispanic 16.7%, asian 16.3%, black 15.7%, other
  14.7%).
- insurance_type: null (p = 0.54).

==================================================================
8. Comorbidities (Iter 15)
==================================================================
14 comorbidities tested. Only atrial_fibrillation reached p < 0.05
(15.6% vs 17.0%, diff -1.4 pp, p = 0.025). Diabetes, hypertension,
COPD, CKD, heart failure, CAD, VTE, autoimmune, hepatitis B/C, HIV,
prior malignancy, and depression/anxiety are all null. With 14
tests, an AF p of 0.025 likely does not survive multiple-testing
correction.

==================================================================
9. SNPs (Iter 16)
==================================================================
24 candidate SNPs tested. snp_rs1050828 (G6PD) reached chi2
p = 0.0056 (carriers 18.5% vs non 16.8%). snp_rs1799853, snp_rs2032582,
snp_rs3813867 brushed against the 0.05 threshold (p = 0.05–0.07). Most
SNPs are null. With ≥24 tests these mild signals do not survive
Bonferroni correction (alpha 0.002), so I treat them as exploratory.

==================================================================
10. Multivariable model (Iter 19)
==================================================================
Adjusted model on 21 features confirms a tight set of independent
predictors (ranked by p):

1. ecog_ps         beta -0.384  OR 0.68   p 2.7e-97
2. blast_pct_marrow beta -0.0057 OR 0.994  p 1.4e-19
3. idh1_mutation   beta +0.360  OR 1.43   p 2.1e-17
4. albumin_g_dl    beta +0.118  OR 1.13   p 8.0e-7
5. wbc_k_per_ul    beta -0.0022 OR 0.998  p 1.0e-5
6. treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine beta +0.057 OR 1.06 p 0.019
   (small adjusted benefit)
- All other treatment dummies, FLT3-ITD/TKD, NPM1, TP53,
  complex_karyotype, IDH2, age, LDH, NLR, secondary_aml: NS.

==================================================================
11. Combined matched-therapy signal (Iter 25)
==================================================================
Defining matched_therapy = (FLT3+ on midostaurin or gilteritinib) OR
(IDH1+ on ivosidenib) OR (IDH2+ on enasidenib):
- Unadjusted ORR 16.3% matched vs 17.0% unmatched, diff -0.6 pp,
  p = 0.30.
- Adjusted (age, ECOG, TP53, complex karyo, secondary AML, albumin,
  NLR): beta -0.055, OR 0.95, p = 0.21.

The composite matched-therapy effect is null/slightly negative.

==================================================================
Overall conclusions
==================================================================

What is supported:
- ECOG performance status, marrow blast percentage, peripheral WBC,
  serum albumin, CRP, and 6-month weight loss are independent
  ORR predictors with the expected directions.
- IDH1 mutation is associated with a substantially higher ORR in
  this cohort, both unadjusted and adjusted (the strongest
  biomarker signal).
- Complex karyotype × 7+3 shows a real negative interaction —
  intensive induction is less effective in cytogenetically adverse
  patients.
- Unfit-for-intensive × venaza shows a real positive interaction —
  venaza's relative benefit is larger in unfit patients, matching
  its label.
- A small adjusted main-effect benefit of venetoclax + azacitidine
  (OR 1.06, p = 0.02).

What is NOT supported (despite being clinically expected):
- None of the four biomarker→targeted-therapy matched-pair
  hypotheses (FLT3+midostaurin, FLT3+gilteritinib, IDH1+ivosidenib,
  IDH2+enasidenib) shows a positive interaction. The IDH1×ivosidenib
  interaction is in fact significantly negative, almost certainly due
  to indication-channelling rather than a causal harm.
- The composite matched-therapy indicator is null/negative.
- TP53, complex karyotype, secondary_aml, and the favorable/adverse
  ELN-style composites do NOT predict ORR on their own in this
  cohort.
- Age is null on ORR.
- NPM1 favorable status is at most a trend.
- Most comorbidities, SNPs, and sociodemographic factors are null.
  Race/ethnicity shows mild heterogeneity (p = 0.01), atrial
  fibrillation (p = 0.025) and snp_rs1050828 (p = 0.006) are
  nominal but do not survive multiple-testing correction.

Interpretation:
The dataset appears to encode a strong "patient fitness/disease burden"
axis (ECOG, blast %, WBC, albumin, CRP, weight loss) that drives ORR,
plus a single very strong biomarker (IDH1). Treatment-biomarker
matched-pair effects, which are pharmacologically expected for FLT3
and IDH1/2 inhibitors, do NOT emerge in observational ORR comparisons.
This pattern is consistent with confounding by indication (sicker
biomarker-positive patients receive the targeted drug, masking its
benefit) and would normally require either causal-inference adjustment
(propensity weighting, instrumental variables) or trial data to
disentangle. With only an objective_response endpoint (no
time-to-event data, no remission depth), several treatment effects may
also simply not be detectable.
