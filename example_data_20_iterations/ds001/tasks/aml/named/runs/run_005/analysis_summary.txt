Analysis Summary — Dataset ds001_aml (n = 50,000 AML patients)
================================================================

Outcome: objective_response (binary; cohort prevalence 16.9%, 8,452 / 50,000).

Approach
--------
Across 25 iterations I (1) screened marginal effects of each treatment and each candidate biomarker / clinical feature on objective_response, (2) tested the canonical drug-target interactions (FLT3 × midostaurin/gilteritinib, IDH1 × ivosidenib, IDH2 × enasidenib, NPM1 × ven/aza, IDH1 × ven/aza, complex karyotype × 7+3, TP53 × intensive/non-intensive), (3) probed effect modification by age, ECOG, and unfit-for-intensive status, (4) ran multivariable logistic models with and without interaction terms, (5) discovered and isolated a striking three-way IDH1 × NPM1 × treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine synergy and refined the model to that effect, and (6) systematically confirmed that solid-tumor genomic flags, tumor-marker labs, vital signs, electrolytes, comorbidities, and SNPs are largely uninformative for response in this AML cohort.

What the evidence shows
-----------------------

1. Prognostic main effects (well-supported)

   • ECOG performance status — large, monotonically negative independent effect on response (adjusted log-odds slope -0.39 per ECOG unit, p < 1e-90). The single strongest predictor in the cohort.
   • Marrow blast percentage — negative (slope -0.006 per pp, p < 1e-19, adjusted).
   • Weight loss over 6 months — negative (slope -0.040 per pp, p < 1e-30, adjusted).
   • Albumin — positive (slope +0.12 per g/dL, p ≈ 1.4e-6, adjusted).
   • CRP — negative (slope -0.007 per mg/L, p ≈ 1e-6, adjusted).
   • WBC — small negative (slope -0.0022 per k/µL, p ≈ 2e-5).
   • Age, sex, secondary AML, FLT3-ITD, FLT3-TKD, IDH2, TP53, complex karyotype — none independently significant in the adjusted model. NPM1 trends positive but is borderline (p ≈ 0.10) once IDH1 interactions are excluded.
   • IDH1 mutation — strongly positive marginally (ORR 22.1% IDH1+ vs 16.5% IDH1-, p ≈ 3.5e-18; adjusted coef +0.36, p < 1e-16). Crucially, this main effect is largely driven by the ven/aza-treated subgroup (see #2).

2. Treatment effects (mostly null marginally; effects are biomarker-conditional)

   Marginal:
   • Only treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine shows a significant marginal benefit on ORR (17.4% on vs 16.6% off, p ≈ 0.031). In a multivariable adjusted model treating the six therapies as covariates, ven/aza retains coef +0.059 (p = 0.016); midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, and 7+3 all have nonsignificant adjusted main effects.

   Drug-target interactions tested:
   • FLT3-ITD × midostaurin: interaction coef -0.10, p = 0.22 (not significant).
   • FLT3-ITD × gilteritinib: coef +0.03, p = 0.69 (not significant).
   • FLT3-TKD × midostaurin/gilteritinib: not significant.
   • IDH1 × ivosidenib: coef -0.36, p = 0.027 — direction is opposite to the canonical clinical expectation. Within IDH1+ patients, ORR is 17.4% on ivosidenib vs 22.5% off; restricting to IDH1+ patients NOT on ven/aza, ORR remains 13.0% on ivosidenib vs 17.5% off (p = 0.14). Ivosidenib does not appear to improve response in this synthetic dataset.
   • IDH2 × enasidenib: coef -0.12, p = 0.45 (not significant).

   So in this cohort, the only treatment with an unconditional benefit is ven/aza, and the targeted single-agent inhibitors (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib) provide no detectable benefit even when limited to their molecularly matched populations.

3. Drug × biomarker interactions that DO matter

   • NPM1 × ven/aza: coef +0.26, p ≈ 7e-7. Ven/aza moves NPM1+ patients from 16.0% → 19.3% ORR while leaving NPM1- patients essentially unchanged (16.5% → 16.9%).
   • IDH1 × ven/aza: coef +0.73, p ≈ 8e-18. Ven/aza moves IDH1+ patients from 17.1% → 29.8% ORR.
   • Complex karyotype × treatment_7+3: coef -0.21, p = 0.0016. CK+ patients on 7+3 have ORR 15.5% vs 17.9% off, while CK- patients gain a small benefit (17.3% on vs 16.6% off). Consistent with intensive chemotherapy being relatively futile in adverse-cytogenetic AML.
   • Unfit_for_intensive × ven/aza: coef +0.10, p = 0.036. Ven/aza appears to add more benefit in patients flagged unfit for intensive therapy than in fit patients (modest signal).

4. The dominant finding — a three-way IDH1 × NPM1 × ven/aza synergy

   When the IDH1 and NPM1 main effects, all three two-way interactions, and the three-way product are entered together, the IDH1 × ven/aza and NPM1 × ven/aza two-way terms collapse and the three-way product carries the entire signal:

      Triple-product coef ≈ +2.14 (raw) / +2.20 (in fully prognostic-adjusted model), p ≈ 4e-30.

   The unconditional ORR table makes the magnitude obvious:

      IDH1+ AND NPM1+ AND ven/aza:                ORR = 0.621 (n = 427)
      IDH1+ AND NPM1+ AND off-ven/aza:            ORR = 0.168 (n = 620)
      All other 8 cells of the 2 × 2 × 2 cube:    ORR ≈ 0.16–0.17

   In other words, virtually all of the IDH1 main effect (+5.6 pp marginal) and most of the IDH1 × ven/aza interaction is concentrated in the ~2.1% of the cohort that is co-mutated for IDH1 and NPM1 and treated with ven/aza. This co-mutation × therapy combination is the strongest signal in the dataset by an order of magnitude. ECOG retains a graded negative effect within this responder-enriched subgroup (ORR 0.68 / 0.64 / 0.41 at ECOG 0 / 1 / 2).

5. Demographics

   • Race/ethnicity is associated with ORR (chi-square p = 0.0096 across 5 categories; ORR white 17.3%, hispanic 16.7%, asian 16.3%, black 15.7%, other 14.7%). After adjustment for ECOG, blasts, albumin, weight loss, CRP, WBC, age, sex, and major mutations, Black patients have an adjusted log-odds disparity of -0.110 versus white (p = 0.0048); the "other" category coef = -0.195 (p = 0.032).
   • Insurance type and rural residence are not associated with ORR.

6. Features that are NOT predictive in this AML cohort

   • Solid-tumor genomic distractors (HER2, MET exon14, RET / ROS1 / NTRK / NRG1 fusions, BRAF V600E, FGFR alteration, CDKN2A loss, KEAP1, PIK3CA, PTEN, MSI-high) — all individually nonsignificant.
   • Metastasis flags (liver / bone / adrenal / contralateral-lung mets, pleural/pericardial effusions) — all nonsignificant.
   • Tumor-marker labs (CA-125, CEA, PSA, TSH) — all nonsignificant.
   • Vital signs (BP, HR, SpO₂) and most electrolytes — all nonsignificant.
   • SNPs — 1 of 24 (snp_rs1050828) crosses uncorrected p < 0.05; none survives Bonferroni (0.05/24 ≈ 0.002).
   • Comorbidities — only atrial fibrillation reaches uncorrected p = 0.025 (small effect, -1.4 pp ORR); cardiac, pulmonary, viral, autoimmune, and CKD comorbidities otherwise nonsignificant.
   • Symptom grades — only appetite_loss_grade (continuous) reaches p = 0.038; fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough nonsignificant.
   • Prior therapy — number of prior lines and individual prior therapy flags nonsignificant; years_since_diagnosis borderline negative (p = 0.056).

Hypotheses supported, refuted, and refined
-------------------------------------------

Supported (well above chance):
   - Higher ECOG, higher blast%, higher CRP, higher WBC, greater weight loss, lower albumin → lower ORR.
   - IDH1 mutation → higher ORR (driven by ven/aza-treated subgroup).
   - Ven/aza has a small marginal positive effect on ORR.
   - NPM1 × ven/aza positive interaction.
   - IDH1 × ven/aza positive interaction.
   - Complex karyotype × 7+3 negative interaction.
   - IDH1 × NPM1 × ven/aza three-way synergy (the dominant finding; refined from the IDH1×ven/aza and NPM1×ven/aza two-ways).
   - Modest unfit × ven/aza positive interaction.
   - Adjusted Black-vs-white ORR disparity.

Refuted or null:
   - Marginal effects of midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, and 7+3 on ORR.
   - FLT3-ITD × midostaurin and FLT3-ITD × gilteritinib drug-target interactions.
   - IDH2 × enasidenib drug-target interaction.
   - IDH1 × ivosidenib hypothesized positive interaction (direction is in fact slightly negative).
   - TP53 × ven/aza and TP53 × 7+3 interactions.
   - Age × 7+3 and ECOG × 7+3 interactions.
   - Insurance type and rural residence as predictors.
   - Solid-tumor genomic alterations, metastasis flags, and tumor-marker labs as predictors.
   - SNP main effects after multiple-comparison correction.
   - Most comorbidity, symptom-grade, and prior-therapy effects.

Refined / synthesized:
   - The IDH1 main effect, IDH1 × ven/aza interaction, and NPM1 × ven/aza interaction are best summarized by a single mechanism: an IDH1+/NPM1+ co-mutation flag whose carriers respond to ven/aza at ~62% (vs ~17% in the rest of the cohort).
   - In a treatment-and-prognostics multivariable model, the only treatment main effect that remains significant is ven/aza; all other treatment effects act through biomarker interactions (or not at all).
   - ECOG remains an independent negative prognostic even within the IDH1+/NPM1+/ven-aza subgroup.

Overall conclusion
------------------
For objective_response in this AML cohort, biology dominates: classical performance-status, marrow-burden, and inflammation/nutritional markers carry the bulk of prognostic information, while treatment efficacy is highly biomarker-conditional. The single most actionable signal is that patients carrying both IDH1 and NPM1 mutations and receiving venetoclax + azacitidine respond at roughly 62%, more than three times the cohort average. Conversely, FLT3 inhibitors (midostaurin, gilteritinib), IDH1 inhibitor (ivosidenib), and IDH2 inhibitor (enasidenib) show no benefit — even within their molecularly matched subgroups — and intensive 7+3 chemotherapy is relatively futile in patients with a complex karyotype. Most other features in the dataset (solid-tumor genomic and laboratory distractors, tumor markers, vital signs, electrolytes, SNPs, demographic factors aside from a small Black-vs-white disparity) are not informative for response.
