Analysis summary — ds001_aml (50,000 AML patients; outcome = objective_response, overall rate 16.9%)
=====================================================================================================

Across 25 iterations I tested 135 self-contained hypotheses spanning AML mutations, cytogenetics,
six treatment indicators (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib,
venetoclax+azacitidine, 7+3), demographics/social-determinants, comorbidities, labs,
PROs, prior-therapy variables, and 26 SNPs.  Tests included chi-square 2x2, univariable
logistic regression, subgroup logistic regressions, two- and three-way interaction
models, and adjusted multivariable logistic regressions.  24 of 135 analyses reached
p < 0.05.  Below I walk through the main findings by domain.


1. Disease-defining mutations and cytogenetics (iterations 1, 2)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
- IDH1 mutation: STRONGLY associated with higher response.  3,624 IDH1+ patients had a
  22.1% response rate vs 16.5% in IDH1- (diff +5.6 pp, chi2 p ≈ 3.5e-18).  This was the
  largest unadjusted main effect of any feature in the dataset.
- IDH2, NPM1, FLT3-ITD, FLT3-TKD, TP53, complex karyotype, secondary AML — none of these
  showed a significant main effect on objective_response (all chi2 p > 0.05; raw rate
  differences were within ±0.7 pp).  This is somewhat surprising relative to literature
  expectations (TP53/complex karyotype typically predict worse, NPM1 typically better),
  and suggests these markers are not the dominant drivers of response in this cohort, or
  their effects are masked by treatment heterogeneity.

2. Treatment main effects (iteration 3)
---------------------------------------
- Venetoclax + azacitidine: 17.4% vs 16.6%, +0.7 pp, p = 0.031 — modest but nominally
  significant benefit.
- 7+3, midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib: no significant overall main
  effect on response (all |diff| ≤ 1 pp, p > 0.05).

3. Targeted-therapy x mutation interactions (iterations 4, 5, 17)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
The expected biology — FLT3 inhibitors helping FLT3+ patients, IDH1/IDH2 inhibitors
helping their respective mutation carriers — was NOT supported in this cohort:
- Within FLT3-ITD+ (n=10,000): midostaurin showed no benefit (16.1% vs 17.4%, p=0.31);
  gilteritinib showed no benefit (16.9% vs 17.2%, p=0.83).
- Within FLT3-TKD+ (n=2,815): both inhibitors directionally negative, neither
  significant.
- Within IDH1+ (n=3,624): ivosidenib was actually directionally WORSE (17.4% vs 22.5%,
  p=0.06).  The logistic interaction term idh1_mutation x treatment_ivosidenib was
  significantly NEGATIVE (coef = -0.364, p = 0.027), i.e., the IDH1 advantage attenuates
  in patients receiving ivosidenib rather than amplifying.
- Within IDH2+ (n=4,679): enasidenib showed no benefit (14.4% vs 16.7%, p=0.20).
- Iteration 17 follow-up confirmed the IDH1 main effect is essentially carried by the
  ivosidenib-untreated stratum (idh1+/iv- 22.6% vs idh1-/iv- 16.5%, p ≈ 1.8e-19), with
  little IDH1 effect among ivosidenib-treated patients.

4. Venetoclax+azacitidine subgroup heterogeneity (iterations 6, 18, 23)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
This is where treatment effects became most interpretable:
- Unfit-for-intensive subgroup: VEN+AZA boosted response 17.9% vs 16.3% (+1.6 pp,
  p = 0.0029).  The unfit x VEN+AZA logit interaction was positive (coef +0.104,
  p = 0.036).
- NPM1+ subgroup: VEN+AZA 19.3% vs 16.0% (+3.4 pp, p ≈ 9.8e-8).
- IDH1+ subgroup: VEN+AZA 29.8% vs 17.1% (+12.6 pp, p ≈ 5e-19) — by far the strongest
  treatment-by-biomarker signal in the dataset.
- TP53+, complex_karyotype: no significant VEN+AZA benefit.
- Three-way analysis (iter 23): VEN+AZA benefit in unfit patients is concentrated in
  TP53- patients (+1.8 pp, p = 0.002) and absent in unfit/TP53+ patients (p = 0.71).
  The three-way interaction term itself was not significant.

5. Performance status, age, BMI, sex (iteration 7)
--------------------------------------------------
- ECOG PS: very strongly negative (logit coef -0.384 per unit, p ≈ 1.8e-97).  This was
  the most statistically robust predictor in the dataset.
- Age: not significant in univariable logit (p > 0.05).
- Sex female: no significant difference.
- BMI: not significant.

6. Lab markers (iterations 8, 15, 16)
-------------------------------------
Highly significant and biologically coherent main effects:
- Albumin (g/dL): POSITIVE (coef +0.115, p ≈ 1.4e-6) — higher albumin → higher response.
- Blast % marrow: NEGATIVE (coef -0.0057, p ≈ 1.2e-19).
- WBC (k/uL): NEGATIVE (coef -0.0022, p ≈ 1.9e-5).
- CRP: NEGATIVE (coef -0.007, p ≈ 2.8e-6).
- LDH, NLR: trends in the expected direction but not significant.
- Hemoglobin, platelets, ANC, ALC, bilirubin, creatinine, INR, glucose, AST/ALT,
  systolic BP, heart rate, SpO2: none significant univariable.

7. Patient-reported outcomes / symptoms (iteration 9)
-----------------------------------------------------
- Weight loss % over 6 months: strongly NEGATIVE (coef -0.040, p ≈ 7.4e-35) — a large,
  highly significant predictor of poor response.
- Appetite-loss grade: NEGATIVE (coef -0.023, p = 0.038).
- Fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough: not significant.

8. Comorbidities (iteration 10)
-------------------------------
None of diabetes, heart failure, CKD, COPD, prior malignancy, or hypertension reached
p < 0.05 for response.

9. Demographics / social determinants (iteration 11)
----------------------------------------------------
- Race/ethnicity: chi2 p = 0.0096 (rates: white 17.3%, hispanic 16.7%, asian 16.3%,
  black 15.7%, other 14.7%).  Spread is modest (max-min ≈ 2.6 pp); could reflect
  confounding by clinical factors rather than direct effect.
- Insurance type, rural residence, education years, smoking pack-years: not significant.

10. Prior therapy / years since diagnosis (iteration 12)
--------------------------------------------------------
- Prior lines of therapy, prior chemotherapy, prior targeted therapy, years since
  diagnosis: none significant.

11. SNPs (iterations 14, 21)
-----------------------------
Of 26 SNPs scanned, only snp_rs1050828=1 reached nominal significance (18.5% vs 16.8%,
p = 0.006) — likely a chance hit given multiple testing (Bonferroni threshold ~0.002).
SNP × treatment interaction screens were uniformly null.

12. Adjusted multivariable models (iterations 13, 22, 24, 25)
-------------------------------------------------------------
The IDH1 signal is robust to adjustment.  In the iter-13 model adjusting for age, ECOG,
secondary AML, complex karyotype, TP53, and all six treatments, IDH1 remained
significantly positive (coef +0.359, p ≈ 1.7e-17, OR ≈ 1.43).  In a richer iter-24
model adding NPM1, FLT3-ITD, blast %, WBC, albumin, LDH, IDH1 coef was +0.360
(p ≈ 1.4e-17).  In the final iter-25 multivariable model with all major mutations,
treatments, and demographics:
- IDH1: coef +0.360, p ≈ 1.5e-17 (positive — confirmed).
- ECOG PS: coef -0.383, p ≈ 5e-97 (negative — confirmed).
- Age: coef close to zero, p > 0.05 — does not retain a sign in the adjusted model.

In iter-22, the adjusted VEN+AZA effect remained significant (coef +0.056, p = 0.022)
after adjusting for age, ECOG, secondary AML, complex karyotype, TP53.

13. Mutation-by-mutation interactions (iteration 20)
----------------------------------------------------
NPM1 x FLT3-ITD, NPM1 x TP53, TP53 x complex_karyotype, IDH1 x IDH2: no significant
interaction terms.

14. 7+3 subgroup analyses (iteration 19)
----------------------------------------
No significant age x 7+3, ECOG x 7+3, or 7+3-in-secondary-AML effects.  7+3 looks
prognostically neutral in this cohort.

15. Concurrent treatment count (iteration 24)
---------------------------------------------
Sum of the six treatment indicators showed no significant association with response,
either overall or within unfit patients.

Overall conclusions
===================
- Two predictors stand out as robust, large, and confirmed across multiple analyses:
  (1) ECOG performance status — strongly negative.
  (2) IDH1 mutation — strongly positive, robust to multivariable adjustment, not
      explained by ivosidenib use, and amplified further when combined with VEN+AZA.
- The most clinically actionable interaction is venetoclax+azacitidine x IDH1
  mutation: response in IDH1+ patients receiving VEN+AZA is 29.8%, ~13 pp above other
  combinations — the strongest treatment-biomarker pairing observed.
- Other historically expected biomarker signals (TP53 → worse; NPM1 → better; complex
  karyotype → worse; FLT3 inhibitors helping FLT3+ patients; IDH1/IDH2 inhibitors
  helping their target mutations) were NOT supported in this cohort.  The IDH1 x
  ivosidenib interaction was directionally negative.
- Standard fitness/inflammation labs (albumin↑, blast%↓, WBC↓, CRP↓, weight-loss%↓,
  ECOG↓, appetite-loss↓) all aligned with the expected direction and were significant.
- Among 26 SNPs and the social-determinant variables, only race/ethnicity and a single
  SNP (rs1050828) reached nominal significance, and these are most plausibly residual
  confounding or chance.
- Hypotheses that were SUPPORTED: h5 (IDH1+ → higher response), h9 (VEN+AZA modest
  benefit), h21 negative IDH1×ivosidenib interaction, h23/h24 (VEN+AZA benefit in
  unfit), h29 (ECOG → lower), h31-h35 inflammation/fitness labs, h41/h42 PRO weight/
  appetite, h52 race difference, h58/h_idh1_full/h_final_idh1 (IDH1 robust to
  adjustment), h_va_npm1, h_va_idh1, h_va_adj.
- Hypotheses that were REFUTED: h1 (TP53 main effect), h2 (NPM1 main effect),
  h3 (complex karyotype main effect), h4 (secondary AML), h6/h7/h8 (IDH2/FLT3 main
  effects), h10-h14 (treatment main effects other than VEN+AZA), h15-h18 (FLT3
  inhibitor benefit in FLT3-ITD+ patients), h19/h20 (IDH inhibitor benefit in IDH+
  patients), h25/h26 (VEN+AZA or 7+3 effect specifically in TP53+), h27 (age
  univariable), h28 (sex), h30 (BMI), comorbidity hypotheses h43-h48, h54-h57 (prior
  therapy), most SNP hypotheses, mutation-by-mutation interactions, and 7+3 subgroup
  effects.
