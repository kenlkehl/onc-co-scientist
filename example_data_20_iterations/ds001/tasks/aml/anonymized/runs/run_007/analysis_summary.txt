Analysis summary — ds001_aml (50,000 patients; outcome: objective_response, prevalence 16.9%)

OVERVIEW
This narrative summarizes 25 propose-test-refine iterations on the de-identified ds001_aml cohort. The dataset contains 125 covariates (88 integer-coded, 36 continuous, 2 nominal — race feature_005 with 5 levels, and insurance feature_087 with 4 levels), one identifier (patient_id), and a binary outcome (objective_response). All analyses were performed with logistic regression (via statsmodels) or chi-square tests (via scipy.stats); effect estimates are reported as logit coefficients (for continuous and binary inputs) or absolute response-rate differences (for categorical contrasts), with p-values from the corresponding model.

UNIVARIATE MAIN EFFECTS (iterations 1-13)
Seven features carry the dominant univariate signal on objective_response:

  - feature_057 (ordinal, range 0-2; distribution suggestive of an ECOG-like performance status):
    response 21.3% / 15.5% / 11.2% across values 0/1/2; logit -0.384, OR 0.681 per unit, p=1.8e-97.
    By far the strongest single predictor in the dataset.
  - feature_035 (binary, prevalence 7%): response 22.1% vs 16.5%, logit +0.364, OR 1.44, p=4.1e-18.
    Strongest binary marker; behaves like a favorable biomarker.
  - feature_011 (continuous, range 0-23.6): logit -0.040 per unit, p=7.4e-35. Negative.
  - feature_006 (continuous, range 20-100): logit -0.0057 per unit, p=1.2e-19. Negative.
  - feature_099 (continuous, range 1.7-5.5; near-Gaussian, distribution consistent with serum
    albumin in g/dL): logit +0.115 per unit, p=1.4e-6. Positive.
  - feature_092 (continuous, range 0.5-500, very right-skewed; consistent with a leukocyte/lab
    count): logit -0.0022 per unit, p=1.9e-5. Negative.
  - feature_063 (continuous, range 0.03-300, right-skewed; consistent with a serum lab value):
    logit -0.0070 per unit, p=2.8e-6. Negative.

Three additional binary markers were significant at lower magnitude:
  - feature_093 (logit +0.119, p=0.005);
  - feature_121 (logit -0.101, p=0.024);
  - feature_014 (logit +0.053, p=0.031).

Two prespecified demographic features behave very differently:
  - feature_078 (range 18-92, mean 68; consistent with patient age in years) showed NO main effect
    on response (logit +0.0004 per year, p=0.72), with response rates flat across age decades.
  - Race (feature_005): unadjusted 5x2 chi-square p=0.0096; white patients had a 17.3% response
    rate vs 16.0% for non-white (white-vs-non-white p=0.0021). A small but statistically
    significant racial gradient at the unadjusted level.
  - Insurance (feature_087): no main effect, 4x2 chi-square p=0.54; rates of 16.6-17.2% across
    medicare/private/medicaid/uninsured.

MULTIVARIABLE ADJUSTMENT (iterations 14-16)
A logistic regression including the eight clinical features above (feature_057, _011, _006, _092, _099, _063, _035, _078) yielded:
  feature_057  OR 0.682 (p=1.3e-96)
  feature_035  OR 1.43  (p=1.9e-17)
  feature_011  OR 0.961 per unit (p=1.3e-34)
  feature_006  OR 0.994 per unit (p=7.0e-20)
  feature_099  OR 1.13  per unit (p=9.3e-7)
  feature_063  OR 0.993 per unit (p~2e-6)
  feature_092  OR 0.998 per unit (p~1e-5)
  feature_078  not significant (p=0.75)
All clinical predictors except age remain independently and highly significant — none of the seven dominant univariate signals is explained away by the others, indicating they capture distinct prognostic information.

When race dummies were added to this model, ALL race coefficients became non-significant (white +0.079 p=0.13; hispanic +0.044 p=0.46; black -0.030 p=0.62; other -0.117 p=0.25, reference asian). The unadjusted racial gradient in response is therefore largely explained by differences in measured clinical/biomarker features across racial groups, not by an independent race effect. Insurance dummies likewise contributed no independent signal after adjustment (all p>0.55).

INTERACTIONS AND SUBGROUP ANALYSES (iterations 17-22)
Multiplicative interactions tested in logistic models:
  - feature_035 x feature_057: coef +0.027, p=0.67. Not significant.
  - feature_011 x feature_057: coef -0.0019, p=0.71. Not significant.
  - age (feature_078) x feature_057: coef +0.0022, p=0.19. Not significant; in fact slightly opposite to the hypothesized amplification.
  - race_white x feature_035: coef +0.134, p=0.14. Direction suggests a somewhat larger absolute response benefit of feature_035 in white patients (+6.5pp) than non-white (+4.0-5.4pp), but the interaction is not statistically significant.

Stratified analyses confirmed directional consistency rather than effect modification:
  - feature_035 produced higher response rates than feature_035=0 in all three feature_057 strata (PS=0: 27.0% vs 20.8%; PS=1: 20.5% vs 15.1%; PS=2: 15.3% vs 10.9%); each stratum-specific contrast was independently significant.
  - feature_057 had a similar negative log-odds effect in every racial stratum (logit coefficients -0.30 to -0.44; p<0.05 in every group, including the small "other" stratum).

NULL/UNINFORMATIVE FEATURES (iterations 23-24)
  - The multi-level integer feature_018 (range 0-10) had no detectable main effect (logit +0.0042, p=0.34); response rates were 16.3-19.0% across its 11 levels with no monotonic trend.
  - The summed count of '1' values across all 80 binary integer features (n_bin, mean 14.6, range 1-28) was unrelated to response (logit +0.0035, p=0.34). Total binary feature load is not a useful summary index — the response signal lives in a specific, small set of biologically informative columns.

INTEGRATED FINAL MODEL (iteration 25)
A 19-covariate model (top 12 clinical features + race dummies + insurance dummies, n=50,000) achieved log-likelihood -22,284 and pseudo-R^2 = 0.019 (LLR p=1.1e-171). Independently significant predictors (p<0.05) were exclusively clinical/biomarker-like features:
  feature_057 (z=-20.9), feature_035 (z=+8.5), feature_011 (z=-12.3), feature_006 (z=-9.1),
  feature_099 (z=+4.9), feature_063 (z=-4.8), feature_092 (z=-4.4), feature_093 (z=+2.9),
  feature_014 (z=+2.4), feature_121 (z=-2.0).
Age, feature_125, all four race dummies, and all three insurance dummies were non-significant.

CONCLUSIONS
1. The probability of objective_response in this cohort is dominantly driven by an ECOG-like ordinal feature (feature_057) and a favorable binary biomarker (feature_035), supplemented by several continuous lab-like features (feature_011, _006, _099, _063, _092). Each contributes independent signal in multivariable models.
2. Age has no detectable effect on response in this cohort, either alone or in adjusted models.
3. Unadjusted racial differences in response (white > non-white, p≈0.002) attenuate to non-significance after adjustment for measured clinical features, indicating the disparity is mediated by group differences in those features rather than by an independent race effect.
4. Insurance status is unrelated to response both before and after adjustment.
5. None of the prespecified two-way interactions (treatment-feature x performance-status, age x performance-status, race x feature_035) reached significance; effects of the dominant predictors are remarkably consistent across subgroups.
6. The total count of "positive" binary features and the multi-level integer feature_018 carry no signal — informativeness is concentrated in a small subset of features rather than diffuse across the full panel.
