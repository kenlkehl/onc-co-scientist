Analysis summary — ds001_aml (50,000 patient EHR-derived AML cohort)
====================================================================

Outcome
-------
The single outcome was `objective_response` (binary; 16.90% positive overall, 8,452 / 50,000).
The dataset contained 125 anonymized features: 80 binary, 9 ordinal/categorical (3-11
levels) plus two text categoricals (race-like `feature_005` with 5 levels and
insurance-like `feature_087` with 4 levels), and 36 continuous. No missing values.

Across 25 iterations I examined main effects (binary, ordinal, continuous, and
demographic), built mutually adjusted multivariable logistic models, and probed
for subgroup heterogeneity and treatment-by-biomarker interactions.


1. Main-effect prognostics (iters 1-8)
--------------------------------------
* `feature_057` (3-level ordinal, 0/1/2). Strongest single predictor in the dataset.
  Response rate fell monotonically: 21.3% (level 0) -> 15.5% (level 1) -> 11.2%
  (level 2). Univariable logistic slope -0.384 per level (chi-square p = 5.5e-98).
  This variable behaves like a stage / risk-category label.
* `feature_035` (binary, 7.2% positive). Response 22.1% with =1 vs 16.5% with =0
  (absolute +5.6%, chi-square p = 3.5e-18). Direction and pattern consistent with a
  treatment receipt indicator — and the interaction analyses below sharpen this
  interpretation.
* `feature_011` (continuous, 0-23.6). Strong inverse relationship: responders
  3.38 vs non-responders 3.95 (t-test p = 7.2e-39); univariable logit beta -0.040
  (p = 7.4e-35). When dichotomized at the median (3.0), high vs low absolute
  difference -3.3% (chi-square p = 2.4e-23).
* `feature_006` (continuous, 20-100). Inverse: responders 58.2 vs non-responders
  60.3 (logit beta -0.006, p = 1.2e-19).
* `feature_063` (continuous): inverse, beta -0.007, p = 2.8e-6.
* `feature_092` (continuous): inverse, beta -0.0022, p = 1.9e-5.
* `feature_099` (continuous, 1.7-5.5): mild positive, beta +0.118, p = 1.4e-6.
  Range and direction are consistent with serum albumin as a favorable marker.
* `feature_084`: borderline positive (beta +0.0005, p = 0.048).
* `feature_055`: not significant (p = 0.35).
* `feature_078` (continuous, 18-92, mean 68 - clearly age). NOT associated with
  response (univariable p = 0.72; multivariable p = 0.74). Notable, since age is
  classically a strong adverse prognostic in AML.
* `feature_005` (race-like). White patients: response 17.3%; hispanic 16.7%;
  asian 16.3%; black 15.7%; other 14.7%. Overall chi-square p = 0.0096; white vs
  non-white logit beta +0.078 (OR 1.081, p = 0.002).
* `feature_087` (insurance-like). NOT significant (p = 0.54).


2. Multivariable model (iter 9, 14, 20, 24)
-------------------------------------------
A logistic model containing feature_035, feature_057, feature_011, feature_006,
feature_063, feature_092, feature_099, age (feature_078), and race_white showed
that all eight clinical predictors retained essentially their univariable
magnitudes after mutual adjustment. The biggest coefficients were
feature_035 (+0.36) and feature_057 (-0.38). Age remained non-significant
(p = 0.74). McFadden pseudo-R^2 was modest (~0.024), but model discrimination
on internal predictions reached AUC = 0.597 with a clean monotonic gradient
in observed response across deciles of predicted probability (decile 1: 8.7%
-> decile 10: 25.4%). Adding feature_093, feature_018, feature_096, feature_125
to the core model did not meaningfully change the inference.


3. Negative subgroup probes (iters 10-13, 15-16, 19)
----------------------------------------------------
Pre-specified interaction tests of feature_035 with feature_057, race, age, and
feature_011 were all NOT significant on the log-odds scale (p = 0.17-0.92),
indicating the treatment effect did not vary by these axes. Stratified absolute
risk differences for feature_035 across feature_057 levels were similar
(+0.062, +0.054, +0.044 from low to high risk) and the subgroup-specific
estimate within the highest-risk stratum (feature_057 = 2) remained significant
(absolute +0.044, p = 0.0022). Several ordinal scoring variables that looked
like they might be comorbidity / disease counts (feature_018, feature_064,
feature_096, feature_042, feature_045) showed no significant graded
relationship with response. Only feature_125 was marginally inverse
(beta -0.023, p = 0.038). feature_093 (binary, 8% positive) was the only other
binary feature with a significant marginal main effect (rr 18.5% vs 16.8%,
p = 0.006), but it was small and showed no notable interactions.


4. The major interaction story (iters 20-23)
--------------------------------------------
A systematic scan of feature_035 x binary-feature interactions across all 80
binary features identified two interaction p-values that were many orders of
magnitude beyond a Bonferroni threshold (0.05/80 = 6.3e-4):

  feature_035 x feature_027  beta_int = +1.05  p = 1.1e-32
  feature_035 x feature_014  beta_int = +0.73  p = 7.7e-18

Stratified analyses confirmed striking effect modification:

  feature_027 = 0  (n = 34,986):  feature_035 effect +0.0008  (p = 0.94)
  feature_027 = 1  (n = 15,014):  feature_035 effect +0.193   (p = 1.4e-56)

  feature_014 = 0  (n = 29,980):  feature_035 effect +0.0056  (p = 0.51)
  feature_014 = 1  (n = 20,020):  feature_035 effect +0.134   (p = 8.9e-38)

Probing further with a 3-way model (feature_035 x feature_027 x feature_014)
showed that essentially all of the benefit comes from a single joint-positive
subset:

  Response rates by (feature_027, feature_014, feature_035):
    (0, 0, 0) = 16.9%   vs   (0, 0, 1) = 17.3%    diff +0.4%   (n_treated = 1,569)
    (0, 1, 0) = 16.5%   vs   (0, 1, 1) = 16.1%    diff -0.4%   (n_treated = 1,008)
    (1, 0, 0) = 15.9%   vs   (1, 0, 1) = 16.8%    diff +0.9%   (n_treated =   620)
    (1, 1, 0) = 16.1%   vs   (1, 1, 1) = 62.1%    diff +46.1%  (n_treated =   427)

In the joint-positive subgroup (feature_027 = 1 AND feature_014 = 1, ~12%
of the cohort), 427 patients received feature_035 and had a response rate of
62.1% versus a baseline of ~16% in the rest of the cohort. The 3-way
interaction term in the saturated model was beta = +2.14, z = 11.23 (p ~ 6e-29),
and notably, in that same model NONE of the lower-order main or 2-way terms was
itself significant. This is the canonical signature of a "precision medicine"
treatment that requires both biomarkers to be present in order to work.


5. Equity / race signal (iters 7, 11, 22, 25)
---------------------------------------------
The white-vs-non-white association with response (OR 1.08, p = 0.002) was
essentially unchanged after adjusting for the measured clinical covariates
(adjusted OR 1.08, p = 0.003). Hypothesis that the difference would be
explained by clinical confounding was REFUTED. Stratified estimates of the
feature_035 effect by race were directionally larger in white patients
(+6.5% absolute) than in any non-white subgroup (+4.0%-5.4%), but the
interaction term did not reach significance either unadjusted (p = 0.17) or
fully adjusted (p = 0.14). The persistent ~1% absolute residual difference
warrants further attention as a possible equity signal (unmeasured access,
follow-up intensity, supportive care, or selection effects), though by itself
it does not establish causation.

--------------------

  H3  feature_011 inversely related                     (p = 7.2e-39)
  H4  feature_006 inversely related                     (p = 9.5e-20)
  H6a feature_063 inverse                               (p = 4.4e-7)
  H6b feature_092 inverse                               (p = 1.3e-5)
  H6c feature_099 positive                              (p = 1.3e-6)
  H7  white race higher response                        (p = 0.002)
  H9  predictors independently associated               (multiple p < 0.005)
  H14 feature_057 robust to feature_035 adjustment      (p = 1e-97)
  H17 feature_035 retains benefit in highest-risk       (p = 0.0022)
  H18 feature_093 modest positive                       (p = 0.006)
  H19c feature_125 inverse                              (p = 0.038)
  H20 at least one binary modifies feature_035          (p = 1.1e-32)
  H21 feature_027 is positive predictive biomarker      (p = 1.4e-56)

  H6e feature_055 associated                            (p = 0.35)
  H8  insurance associated with response                (p = 0.54)
  H10 feature_035 x feature_057 interaction             (p = 0.67)

7. Take-home conclusions
------------------------
1. feature_057 is the dominant prognostic axis (large monotonic effect on response).
2. feature_035 is highly likely a TARGETED treatment whose clinical benefit is
   essentially confined to patients positive for both feature_027 and feature_014
   (~12% of the cohort, response 62% vs ~16%). Outside that joint-positive
   subset, feature_035 has no detectable effect, and treating biomarker-negative
   patients adds no benefit. This is the most clinically actionable finding in
   the dataset and would dramatically change number-needed-to-treat calculations.
3. Continuous laboratory markers feature_011, feature_006, feature_063,
   feature_092 (inverse) and feature_099 (positive) are independent prognostic
   predictors but with modest effect sizes individually.
4. Patient age (feature_078) does NOT predict response in this cohort - a
   useful negative finding for design of decision-support models.
5. There is a small but reproducible white-vs-non-white response difference
   (+1% absolute, OR 1.08) that is not explained by measured clinical
   covariates and merits investigation as a potential equity / unmeasured-
   confounding signal.
6. Insurance category, several ordinal "score" variables (feature_018,
   feature_064, feature_096), and most binary features outside the small set
   highlighted above carry essentially no information about response.
