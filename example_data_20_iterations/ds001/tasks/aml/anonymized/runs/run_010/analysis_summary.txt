Oncology dataset analysis — ds001_aml
=====================================

Cohort: 50,000 patient records, 125 features (3 categorical: race-like
feature_005 with 5 levels; insurance-like feature_087 with 4 levels;
patient_id), 36 continuous features, 80 binary 0/1 features, and 7
small-cardinality ordinal features (feature_018, 042, 045, 057, 064,
096, 125). Outcome: objective_response (binary; 16.9% prevalence,
8,452 / 50,000 responders). No missing values.

Across 25 propose-test-refine iterations I screened main effects,
adjusted associations, and pairwise interactions for every plausible
predictor of objective_response. The findings are summarized below.

1) Dominant prognostic risk gradient: feature_057
   feature_057 is a 3-level ordinal whose response gradient is the
   single dominant signal in the dataset. Crude rates: 21.3% (level 0,
   n=17,592), 15.5% (level 1, n=24,971), 11.2% (level 2, n=7,437).
   Logistic regression log-OR for level 1 vs 0 = -0.39 (p=2e-52);
   level 2 vs 0 = -0.76 (p=8e-77); overall p ≈ 5.5e-98. The shape and
   distribution are consistent with a cytogenetic / disease-risk score
   (favorable / intermediate / adverse). It explains roughly 70% of the
   pseudo-R^2 of the full model. The gradient is preserved within every
   subgroup we tested (race, sex-like markers, age, insurance).

2) Continuous prognostic markers
   Four continuous features had robust, signed, and biologically
   plausible associations:
   - feature_011 (range 0–23.6, mean 3.9; could be peripheral blast %
     or another count): higher → lower response. Log-OR -0.040 per unit
     (p=7e-35). Direction consistent across all feature_057 strata.
   - feature_006 (range 20–100, mean 60; performance-status-like):
     higher → lower response. Log-OR -0.0057 per unit (p=1e-19).
     Quartile response rates: 19.0% / 17.5% / 16.5% / 14.7%.
   - feature_092 (range 0.5–500, right-skewed; LDH-like): higher →
     lower response. Log-OR -0.0022 per unit (p=2e-5).
   - feature_063 (range 0.03–300, right-skewed): higher → lower
     response, especially on log scale: log1p log-OR -0.074 (p=7e-7).
   - feature_099 (range 1.7–5.5; albumin-like): higher → higher
     response. Log-OR +0.118 per unit (p=1e-6).

3) Independent biomarker for response: feature_035
   The binary feature_035 (prevalence 7.2%) is a strong positive
   predictor: 22.1% response with the marker vs 16.5% without.
   Adjusted log-OR = +0.36 (OR ≈ 1.43; p=2e-17). The benefit is
   essentially unchanged across feature_057 strata (no significant
   feature_035 × feature_057 interaction; stratum log-ORs 0.34, 0.37,
   0.39). This behaves like a favorable mutation marker (e.g. NPM1-like
   in AML) with prognostic effect that is independent of the primary
   risk score.

4) Other binary positive predictors
   After adjustment for the four continuous predictors and feature_057:
   - feature_093 (prev 8%): log-OR +0.13 (p=0.004).
   - feature_014 (prev 40%): log-OR +0.06 (p=0.017), small.
   - feature_121 (prev 8%): log-OR -0.09 (p=0.04), modestly negative.
   The feature_093 × feature_035 interaction is positive but not
   significant (log-OR +0.15, p=0.31), suggesting at most a weakly
   super-additive favorable combination.

5) Composite favorable score
   A simple composite (favorable feature_057=0 + feature_035=1) gives a
   clean monotone dose-response gradient: response 14.2% (n=30,115),
   20.6% (n=18,554), 27.0% (n=1,331) for 0, 1, 2 favorable factors
   respectively. This effectively doubles the absolute response rate
   between the worst and best composite categories.

6) Demographics: race, insurance, age
   - Race (feature_005): crude 17.3% (white), 16.7% (hispanic), 16.3%
     (asian), 15.7% (black), 14.7% (other); chi-square p=0.010. After
     adjustment for feature_057, feature_011, feature_006, and
     feature_035, only the binary white-vs-non-white contrast remains
     marginally significant (log-OR +0.08, p=0.003). The 4-way race
     contrasts attenuate to non-significance; 'other' shows the lowest
     response but its CI overlaps zero in the adjusted model. There is
     no race × feature_057 interaction (LR p=0.91); the cytogenetic
     gradient is operating uniformly across racial groups.
   - Insurance (feature_087): no association (chi-square p=0.54;
     adjusted p > 0.55 for every category).
   - Age (feature_078, range 18–92, mean 68): no main effect (adjusted
     p=0.79). However, age × feature_057=2 interaction is significant
     (log-OR +0.0085 per year, p=0.023): older patients in the adverse
     stratum recover ~ a fraction of the response disadvantage —
     possibly reflecting older patients in the adverse-risk group
     selectively receiving lower-intensity supportive regimens that
     change response definition, or selection-for-fitness within that
     stratum.

7) Features without effect
   - The other six small-cardinality ordinals (feature_018, 042, 045,
     064, 096, 125) had no significant chi-square association with
     response (smallest p=0.073 for feature_125).
   - High-prevalence binaries that look demographic (feature_007 prev
     45%, feature_002 prev 47%, feature_012 prev 42%) had no
     meaningful univariate or stratum-specific effect; we infer these
     are non-prognostic markers (most plausibly sex or another
     demographic).
   - The fully-saturated multivariable model achieves only McFadden's
     pseudo-R^2 ≈ 0.02, so although many effects are statistically
     reliable in 50k patients, the practical predictability of
     individual response remains limited.

8) Heterogeneity / interaction findings
   Most interactions tested were null. Notable results:
   - feature_035 × feature_057: NS (p=0.71)
   - feature_011 × feature_057: NS (p=0.34)
   - feature_006 × feature_057: NS (p=0.24)
   - feature_099 × feature_057: NS (p=0.80)
   - feature_092 × feature_057: NS (trend, p=0.10)
   - race × feature_057: NS (LR p=0.91)
   - feature_011 × feature_035: NS (p=0.24)
   - feature_093 × feature_035: NS (p=0.31, positive trend)
   - feature_022 × feature_057: nominally significant (LR p=0.030),
     driven by lower response with feature_022=1 specifically in the
     adverse-risk stratum (log-OR -0.24, p=0.10 within that stratum) —
     hypothesis-generating, not corrected for multiple testing across
     80 binary features.
   - age × feature_057=2: significant (p=0.023, see point 6).

Overall conclusions
-------------------
Objective response in this 50,000-patient AML-like cohort is governed
mostly by a single graded risk factor (feature_057) and a small set
of independent prognostic predictors:

  Negative (lower response):
     feature_057 (graded), feature_011, feature_006, feature_063,
     feature_092, feature_121
  Positive (higher response):
     feature_035, feature_099, feature_093, feature_014

The biomarker feature_035 acts independently of the cytogenetic-risk
score, and combining favorable status on both yields almost double
the response rate of patients positive on neither. Demographic
features (race, insurance, age) contribute negligible independent
predictive value once clinical features are accounted for, with a
small residual white-vs-non-white advantage (~1 pp) that may reflect
unmeasured access-to-treatment or biology effects. Most binary
features and small-cardinality ordinals carry essentially no
information about response. There is no strong evidence of biomarker-
or treatment-by-risk-score interactions: the favorable effect of
feature_035 and the unfavorable effect of feature_011/006 are similar
across all three feature_057 strata.
