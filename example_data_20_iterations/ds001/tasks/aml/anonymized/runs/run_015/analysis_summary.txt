Analysis summary - ds001_aml (50,000 patients; binary outcome: objective_response, marginal rate 16.9%)

Overview
--------
The cohort has 125 anonymized patient features plus the objective_response outcome. Three categorical features carry interpretable labels (feature_005 = race/ethnicity with 5 levels; feature_087 = insurance with 4 levels) and one ordinal integer feature (feature_057, values 0/1/2) appears to encode performance status or an analogous severity scale. The remaining features are 80 binary (mostly low-to-moderate prevalence) and 36 continuous (lab/vital-sign-like). I worked through 25 iterations of hypothesis generation and statistical testing, focusing on (a) main-effect screening, (b) multivariable adjustment, (c) interaction and 3-way subgroup analyses, and (d) demographic/equity questions.

Main-effect findings (iterations 1-4, 18-21)
--------------------------------------------
Univariate screening identified seven dominant predictors of response. All retained independent significance in a multivariable logistic model (pseudo-R^2 = 0.019, LL = -22,295):

- feature_057 (ordinal 0/1/2, PS-like): strongest single predictor. Response rates 21.3%, 15.5%, 11.2% across the three levels. Adjusted slope -0.39 per level (z = -20.9). The trend is monotone and approximately linear on the log-odds scale.
- feature_011 (continuous): higher values reduce response. Adjusted standardized coef = -0.16 (z = -12.4).
- feature_006 (continuous): higher values reduce response. Adjusted std coef = -0.11 (z = -9.1).
- feature_035 (binary, prev 7%): presence increases response. Adjusted coef = +0.36 (z = +8.5) in the main-effect model; this main effect collapses to ~0 once a treatment-combination interaction is included (see below).
- feature_099 (continuous): higher values increase response. Adjusted std coef = +0.06 (z = +4.9).
- feature_063 (continuous): higher values reduce response. Adjusted std coef = -0.07 (z = -4.9).
- feature_092 (continuous): higher values reduce response. Adjusted std coef = -0.06 (z = -4.4).

Among the other multi-level integer features (feature_096, feature_064, feature_018, feature_045, feature_042, feature_125), none shows a meaningful monotone trend - all univariate logistic slopes are |coef| ≤ 0.02 and no test reaches conventional significance after adjustment. Only feature_057 is clearly informative as an ordinal predictor.

Patient age (feature_078, mean 68 years, range 18-92) has NO detectable effect on response, either unadjusted or adjusted (slope per SD = +0.004, p = 0.72). This is unusual for AML and worth noting.

After adjustment for the seven dominant predictors, residual associations were small but non-zero for feature_093 (adj coef +0.13, p = 0.004; prev 8%), feature_014 (adj coef +0.06, p = 0.017; prev 40%), and feature_084 (continuous, adj std coef +0.027, p = 0.026). Most other binary features carry essentially no independent information.

The dominant signal: a treatment-biomarker 3-way interaction (iterations 10-12, 14, 17, 23)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Screening 79 binary features for interactions with feature_035 produced three highly significant hits: feature_035 x feature_027 (interaction coef +1.05, p = 1.1e-32), feature_035 x feature_014 (+0.73, p = 7.7e-18), and feature_035 x feature_058 (+0.70, p = 1.0e-3). All three indicate that other binaries amplify the effect of feature_035=1.

A saturated 2x2x2 model on (feature_035, feature_027, feature_014) yields a clean and decisive 3-way interaction term: coefficient +2.14 (p = 3.0e-29). All lower-order main effects and 2-way interactions are statistically null. The mechanism is clear in the cell counts:

  Triple-positive  (035=1, 027=1, 014=1): n =   427, response rate = 62.1%
  All other cells (the remaining 7 of 8): n = 49,573, response rate = 16.5%

Stratified analyses confirm:
- Within feature_035=1 patients, only the cell with both feature_027=1 AND feature_014=1 has elevated response (62.1%); cells with one or neither component sit at the baseline 16-17%. The within-stratum 027x014 interaction is +2.18 (p = 4.6e-32).
- Within feature_035=0 patients, NO combination of feature_027 and feature_014 affects response (cell rates uniformly 16.6-17.0%; 027x014 interaction p = 0.52).
- Stratifying further by feature_058 within marker-negative patients yields no rescue: combo+ vs combo- is 16.4% vs 15.0% (058=1) and 16.1% vs 16.6% (058=0). The 027+014 combination is genuinely inert in marker-negative patients.

Biological interpretation. The pattern is the textbook signature of biomarker-driven combination therapy: feature_035 is a predictive biomarker (in the AML context plausibly a specific mutation), and feature_027/feature_014 likely represent two components of a targeted regimen that work only when both are administered to a marker-positive patient. Across approximately 50,000 patients the effect size is enormous (~45 percentage points) and the relationship is clean enough to be the dataset's central finding.

Other interactions tested (iterations 5-7, 13, 22)
--------------------------------------------------
- feature_035 does NOT interact multiplicatively with feature_057 (PS-like) on the log-odds scale (interaction p = 0.67); the marker effect is additive across PS strata, with absolute response shifting downward as PS worsens.
- feature_035 has only one weak continuous interaction: with feature_099 (interaction coef -0.094, p = 0.024). The remaining feature_035 x continuous interactions are all p > 0.2.
- Pairwise multiplicative interactions among the top continuous predictors are essentially absent. Of 15 pairs tested, only feature_006 x feature_092 reaches nominal significance (interaction coef -0.036, p = 0.006). For practical purposes the continuous predictors operate additively on the log-odds scale.
- feature_058 looked like a candidate alternate biomarker (prevalence 3%) but is much weaker: the 027x014 interaction within feature_058=1 patients reaches only p = 0.067, and a saturated 035 x 058 x 014 model produces no significant 3-way term. feature_058 and feature_035 are essentially independent (co-occurrence n = 119) and feature_058 should be considered an accessory rather than a primary biomarker.
- feature_014 shows minor interactions with feature_118 (+0.22, p = 0.005) and feature_016 (+0.13, p = 0.019), and feature_027 shows weak negative interactions with feature_013/feature_073/feature_046 (all |p| ~0.02-0.05). None approach the magnitude of the dominant 3-way term.

PS modifier analysis (iteration 14)
-----------------------------------
Within the marker-positive stratum, the multiplicative effect of the (027+014) combination is essentially constant across PS strata (PS x combo interaction coef = -0.08, p = 0.62). The absolute response rate inside the combo+ subgroup decreases with worse PS - 68.4% (PS=0) -> 64.4% (PS=1) -> 41.4% (PS=2) - because PS has its own additive effect on the log-odds scale. This means biomarker-positive patients with poor PS still derive substantial absolute benefit from the combination, just somewhat less than fitter patients.

Demographic and equity findings (iterations 3, 15, 19, 24)
----------------------------------------------------------
Race (feature_005, white = reference). After adjustment for clinical predictors:
  - black: coef = -0.109, p = 0.005 (significantly lower odds of response)
  - other: coef = -0.196, p = 0.030
  - asian: coef = -0.079, p = 0.13
  - hispanic: coef = -0.036, p = 0.30
The disparity in black patients persists in a marker+combo+ subgroup analysis: response rates are white 64.8% (n=281), asian 63.2% (n=19), hispanic 63.0% (n=73), black 47.1% (n=51), other 33.3% (n=3). A race-only logistic regression within the triple-positive subgroup yields race_black coef = -0.73 (p = 0.018). The clinically meaningful disparity therefore exists where the optimal targeted regimen is delivered, suggesting either residual clinical confounding not captured by the seven adjusted predictors or biological/access factors that differentially attenuate response in black patients - this would warrant focused follow-up.

Insurance (feature_087, private = reference). After adjustment, no insurance category is independently associated with response (Medicaid +0.020, p = 0.59; Medicare +0.042, p = 0.12; uninsured +0.010, p = 0.87). Insurance type does NOT predict response in this cohort.

Final model and discrimination (iteration 25)
---------------------------------------------
The parsimonious final logistic model (continuous: feature_011, feature_006, feature_063, feature_099, feature_092; ordinal: feature_057; binary: feature_035, feature_027, feature_014; their (027 AND 014) combination indicator and the (feature_035 x combination) interaction; plus race dummies) fits substantially better than a main-effects-only model: AUC 0.605 vs 0.598, log-likelihood -22,100 vs -22,292, LR chi^2 = 384 on 2 df. The single (feature_035 x combo) interaction term carries the bulk of the additional fit (coefficient +2.20, p < 1e-77).

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
--------------------------------
Supported:
- Main effects of feature_057, feature_011, feature_006, feature_063, feature_099, feature_092, and feature_035 on response (h1.1-h1.7, h2.1).
- Race-based disparity: black race independently predicts lower response (h3.1, h15.1, h24.1).
- Three-way (feature_035 x feature_027 x feature_014) treatment-biomarker interaction; benefit requires marker positivity and BOTH treatment components (h10.1, h11.1, h12.1, h12.2, h17.1, h23.1).
- PS modifies absolute but not multiplicative combo effect (h14.1).
- Small residual effects of feature_093 and feature_084 (h8.1, h20.1, h20.2).
- Final integrated multivariable model improves discrimination over main-effects-only (h16.1, h25.1).

Refuted or unsupported:
- Insurance type associated with response after adjustment (h3.2, h19.1).
- Other multi-level integer features showing a feature_057-like trend (h4.1).
- feature_035 x feature_057 multiplicative interaction (h5.1).
- Strong feature_035 x continuous predictor interactions (h6.1; only feature_099 marginally).
- Strong pairwise interactions among continuous predictors (h7.1; only 006x092 marginally).
- feature_085 (the most prevalent binary) associated with response (h9.1).
- feature_058 as an alternate primary biomarker for the combination (h13.1; only weakly accessory).
- Age (feature_078) associated with response (h18.1).

Bottom line
-----------
The dominant clinical story in ds001_aml is a marker-driven combination therapy effect: in roughly 0.85% of patients (n=427) who are simultaneously feature_035-positive AND have both feature_027=1 and feature_014=1, the response rate is ~62%, vs ~17% in the rest of the cohort - a ~45 percentage-point difference. The effect is essentially absent without the marker, absent with the marker but only one treatment component, and present only when all three conditions co-occur. Performance status (feature_057) is the dominant prognostic axis outside this targeted subset. Several continuous laboratory-style predictors carry consistent but modest independent effects. There is a real, statistically significant lower response in black patients that persists within the marker+combo+ subgroup and warrants further investigation. Patient age and insurance type are not independently associated with objective response in this cohort.
