Analysis summary — ds001_aml (50,000 patients, outcome: objective_response)
===========================================================================

Cohort and outcome
------------------
The cohort comprises 50,000 patients with a binary outcome objective_response
that is positive in 8,452 patients (16.9%). The 125 candidate predictors are
80 binary indicators, 6 small-cardinality ordinal integers, 1 ordinal integer
with 11 levels (feature_018), 36 continuous (float) variables, and 2
multi-level categorical variables (feature_005 with 5 categories;
feature_087 with 4). No values were missing.

Workflow
--------
Across 25 iterations, I (i) screened every candidate against the outcome
univariately, (ii) confirmed direction and dose-response visually with
quantile or stratified means, (iii) built a multivariable logistic
regression with the univariately significant predictors, and (iv) probed
interactions and subgroup heterogeneity around the leading predictors.

Confirmed main effects (direction, magnitude, significance)
-----------------------------------------------------------
NEGATIVE associations (higher value / presence => lower response):

* feature_057 (ordinal 0/1/2). The dominant predictor in the dataset.
  Univariate logistic regression gives OR=0.681 per level (beta=-0.384,
  p=1.8e-97). Response rate is 21.3% at level 0, 15.5% at level 1, and
  11.2% at level 2. The categorical contrasts confirm the monotonic
  pattern (level 1 vs 0 OR=0.68; level 2 vs 0 OR=0.47). This single
  variable carries the largest fraction of the signal.

* feature_011 (continuous, range 0-23.6). OR=0.961 per unit
  (beta=-0.040, p=7.4e-35). Quartile dose-response: 18.7% (Q1) to
  13.7% (Q4) — a 4.9 percentage-point absolute drop.

* feature_006 (continuous, range 20-100). OR=0.994 per unit
  (beta=-0.0057, p=1.2e-19). Quartile means decline 19.0% -> 14.7%.

* feature_063 (continuous, right-skewed, range 0.03-300). OR=0.993 per
  unit (beta=-0.0070, p=2.8e-6). Top decile 14.6% vs middle deciles
  ~17-18%.

* feature_092 (continuous, right-skewed, range 0.5-500). OR=0.998 per
  unit (beta=-0.0022, p=1.9e-5). Mean 19.1 in responders vs 20.4 in
  non-responders.

* feature_121 (binary, prevalence 8.3%). OR=0.904 (beta=-0.101,
  p=0.024). Response 15.6% vs 17.0%.

POSITIVE associations (higher value / presence => higher response):

* feature_035 (binary, prevalence 7.2%). OR=1.44 (beta=+0.364,
  p=4.1e-18). Response 22.1% in feature_035=1 vs 16.5% in feature_035=0.
  This is the strongest positive (biomarker-like) signal in the dataset.

* feature_099 (continuous, narrow range 1.7-5.5). OR=1.122 per unit
  (beta=+0.115, p=1.4e-6). Decile response rates rise monotonically
  from ~15.6% to ~17.9%.

* feature_093 (binary, prevalence 8.1%). OR=1.13 (beta=+0.119,
  p=0.005). Response 18.5% vs 16.8%.

CATEGORICAL: feature_005 (5 levels). Response varies (chi-square p=0.0096):
white 17.3%, hispanic 16.7%, asian 16.3%, black 15.7%, other 14.7%.
Reference=white: black OR=0.896 (p=0.004); other OR=0.821 (p=0.034);
hispanic and asian not significantly different.

Null / not associated
---------------------
* feature_078 (continuous, range 18-92, plausibly age). Univariate OR
  per unit ~1.0004, p=0.71. Decile response rates flat (~16-18%) across
  the entire range.
* feature_007 (binary, prevalence 44.7%, plausibly sex). OR=1.025,
  p=0.32; response 17.1% vs 16.8%.
* feature_087 (4-level categorical, plausibly insurance type). All
  pairwise contrasts vs private p>0.15.
* Other ordinal features feature_042, feature_045, feature_064,
  feature_096, feature_018: univariate p>0.05 (feature_125 univariate
  p=0.038 but attenuates to p=0.056 after adjusting for feature_057,
  feature_011, feature_006).
* High-prevalence binary features (feature_002, feature_085, feature_122,
  feature_017, feature_025, feature_070, feature_012) are all
  unassociated in the univariate screen.

Multivariable model
-------------------
A logistic regression with feature_057 (categorical), feature_011,
feature_006, feature_035, feature_063, feature_099, feature_092,
feature_093, feature_121, and feature_005 retains every effect with the
same direction as in the univariate analyses and with overlapping
magnitudes (feature_057 OR per level ~0.68; feature_011 OR=0.96;
feature_035 OR=1.43; feature_005[black] OR=0.90 vs white). The model AUC
on the training cohort is 0.598. Single-variable AUCs are: feature_057
0.566, feature_011 0.539, feature_006 0.532, feature_092 0.521,
feature_099 0.517, feature_063 0.514, feature_035 0.514, feature_005
0.510 — confirming that no single feature dominates beyond feature_057
and that prediction is additive across modest-strength predictors.

Interactions and subgroup heterogeneity
---------------------------------------
The most clinically natural interactions were tested with likelihood-ratio
tests against the corresponding main-effects models:

* feature_057 x feature_035 (ECOG-like x biomarker-like): NULL, p=0.67.
  The biomarker-like uplift from feature_035 is approximately constant
  across feature_057 levels.
* feature_005 x feature_035 (race-like x biomarker-like): NULL, p=0.73.
  Stratified mean uplift from feature_035=1 is similar in every race
  category (e.g. white 16.8% -> 23.3%; black 15.4% -> 19.5%).
* feature_011 x feature_057: NULL, p=0.71.
* feature_006 x feature_057: NULL, p=0.49.
* feature_087 x feature_057: NULL, p=0.81.
* feature_005 x feature_057: NULL, p=0.91; the black-vs-white gap is
  consistent across feature_057 strata.
* feature_007 x feature_057: NULL, p=0.34.
* feature_078 (age) x feature_057: borderline, p=0.022 (2 df). Within
  feature_057=2 alone, age becomes positively associated with response
  (beta=+0.0088 per year, p=0.009), while remaining null in feature_057
  levels 0 and 1. Practical magnitude is small.
* feature_011 x feature_063: borderline, p=0.089 (suggestive but not
  significant).

Health-disparity finding (feature_005)
--------------------------------------
After adjusting for the full clinical set (feature_057, feature_011,
feature_006, feature_035, feature_063, feature_099, feature_092,
feature_093, feature_121) and for feature_087 (insurance-like), the
black-vs-white contrast retains OR=0.896 (95% CI 0.83-0.97, p=0.005),
and the other-vs-white contrast retains OR=0.822 (p=0.030). Insurance
itself is uniformly null. The disparity for black patients is therefore
not explained by the measured clinical covariates, by performance-status-
like staging (feature_057), or by insurance category in this dataset.
This pattern is consistent across feature_057 strata (no significant
race x feature_057 interaction).

Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
--------------------------------
SUPPORTED: feature_057, feature_011, feature_006, feature_063,
feature_092, feature_121, feature_005[black], feature_005[other] as
negative predictors; feature_035, feature_099, feature_093 as positive
predictors; the multivariable additivity hypothesis; the dose-response
hypothesis for the leading continuous covariates; the persistence of the
black-vs-white disparity after full clinical and insurance adjustment.

REFUTED / NOT SUPPORTED: hypothesized effects of age (feature_078), of
the plausibly-sex feature_007, of insurance-like feature_087, and of
most other ordinal features (042, 045, 064, 096, 018) on response.
Hypothesized interactions of feature_057 with feature_035, feature_011,
feature_006, feature_005 and of feature_005 with feature_035 were all
refuted.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
The dominant determinant of objective_response in this cohort is the
ordinal stage-or-performance-status-like variable feature_057, followed
by a small group of continuous laboratory/clinical features
(feature_011, feature_006, feature_063, feature_092, feature_099) and a
binary biomarker-like indicator feature_035. Demographic factors
typically associated with treatment response — age, the binary
sex-like indicator, and insurance — are not associated with response
here. Race (feature_005) shows a residual disparity for black and
other-race patients that is not explained by the measured clinical
covariates or insurance. Effects across covariates are well described
as additive on the log-odds scale: no clinically meaningful effect-
modification was detected among the leading predictors. The composite
multivariable AUC of 0.598 indicates that response is partially but not
strongly predictable from the available features, suggesting unmeasured
patient- or treatment-level factors drive most of the variation in
response.
