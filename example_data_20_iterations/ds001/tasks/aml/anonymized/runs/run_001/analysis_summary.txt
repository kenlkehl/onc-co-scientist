ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_aml (n = 50,000)
==========================================

Outcome: objective_response (binary; overall positive rate = 16.90%, n_responders = 8,452 / 50,000)

Feature inventory:
- 80 binary integer features
- 6 multi-level ordinal/integer features (3-level: feature_057; 5-level: feature_096, _064, _045, _042, _125; 11-level: feature_018)
- 36 continuous (float) features
- 2 categorical strings: feature_005 (race; 5 levels) and feature_087 (insurance; 4 levels)
- 1 patient_id

Below is a walk-through of the 25 iterations, the hypotheses each addressed, and what the statistical evidence showed.


1. UNIVARIATE BINARY-FEATURE SCREEN
   Hypothesis: At least one binary feature is associated with objective_response.
   Result: Only feature_035 carries a clinically large signal (n=3,624; response rate 22.1% if present vs 16.5% if absent; absolute Δ = +5.6 pp; chi-square p = 3.5e-18). Three other binary features cross p < 0.05 (feature_093 +1.7 pp p=0.006, feature_121 -1.4 pp p=0.025, feature_014 +0.7 pp p=0.031). The remaining 76 binary features are essentially null. SUPPORTED.

2. UNIVARIATE CONTINUOUS-FEATURE SCREEN
   Hypothesis: At least one continuous feature differs by response status; specifically feature_011 and feature_006 are lower in responders.
   Result: Five continuous features are strongly associated (Welch t-test):
     feature_011: 3.38 vs 3.95, p = 7.2e-39 (largest signal)
     feature_006: 58.23 vs 60.31, p = 9.5e-20
     feature_063: 5.65 vs 6.17, p = 4.4e-7
     feature_099: 3.82 vs 3.80, p = 1.3e-6 (HIGHER in responders)
     feature_092: 19.07 vs 20.40, p = 1.3e-5
   All directions support the hypotheses. SUPPORTED.

3. ORDINAL FEATURES — MONOTONE TREND
   Hypothesis: feature_057 (3-level) and the 5/11-level features show ordinal trends.
   Result: feature_057 is the dataset's strongest predictor — beta = -0.384 per unit, OR = 0.68, p = 1.8e-97; response rates 21.3% / 15.5% / 11.2% across levels 0/1/2. None of the 5-level features (096, 064, 045, 042) and the 11-level feature_018 reach significance for trend. feature_125 has a marginal effect (p = 0.038, beta = -0.023). SUPPORTED for feature_057; REFUTED for the others.

4. CATEGORICAL FEATURES (RACE, INSURANCE)
   Hypothesis: Response varies by race; response varies by insurance.
   Result: Race (feature_005) chi-square p = 0.0096 — white 17.28%, hispanic 16.70%, asian 16.33%, black 15.74%, other 14.72%. Black and 'other' have the lowest response rates. Insurance (feature_087) p = 0.54 — no signal. Race SUPPORTED; insurance REFUTED.

5. MULTIVARIABLE CORE MODEL
   Hypothesis: Top 7 univariate predictors all carry independent signal.
   Result: All 7 remain significant when entered together (logistic): feature_057 beta=-0.383 (p<<1e-50), feature_011_z -0.155 (p=1.4e-34), feature_006_z -0.110 (p=7e-20), feature_063_z -0.067 (p=1.3e-6), feature_099_z +0.059 (p=9e-7), feature_092_z -0.058 (p=1e-5), feature_035 +0.360 (p=2e-17). Pseudo-R² = 0.018, AIC = 44,622. SUPPORTED.

6. RACIAL DISPARITY ADJUSTED FOR CLINICAL COVARIATES
   Hypothesis: Adjusting for clinical features eliminates the black-vs-white response gap.
   Result: REFUTED. After adjusting for the 8 strongest clinical predictors, black race retains an independent negative association (beta = -0.109, OR ≈ 0.90, p = 0.005). Asian/hispanic non-significant; 'other' beta = -0.196 (p = 0.031). The unadjusted estimate (-0.112) is essentially unchanged after adjustment, indicating the disparity is NOT mediated by measured biology.

7. ADDING INSURANCE TO THE ADJUSTED RACE MODEL
   Hypothesis: Insurance partially mediates the residual racial gap.
   Result: REFUTED. Adding insurance dummies leaves the black coefficient at beta = -0.109 (p = 0.005); none of the insurance terms is significant.

8. PAIRWISE INTERACTIONS AMONG TOP CLINICAL PREDICTORS
   Hypotheses: feature_057 × feature_035; feature_057 × feature_011; feature_035 × feature_011.
   Result: All three interaction terms non-significant (p = 0.67, 0.71, 0.23). The clinical predictors act additively on the log-odds scale.

9. INTERACTIONS INVOLVING RACE
   Hypotheses: feature_057 × black, feature_035 × black, feature_011 × black.
   Result: All three interaction terms non-significant (p = 0.27, 0.51, 0.69). The racial gap is constant across clinical strata; the protective effect of feature_035 is the same in black and non-black patients.

10. STRATIFIED ANALYSIS — feature_035 EFFECT BY feature_057 LEVEL
    Hypothesis: feature_035 boosts response within each feature_057 stratum.
    Result: SUPPORTED. ORs are 1.40 (p=1.5e-7), 1.44 (p=2.4e-9), 1.48 (p=2.2e-3) for feature_057=0,1,2 respectively. The protective effect is present and nearly identical across all 3 strata.

11. STRATIFIED RESPONSE RATES BY RACE × feature_057
    Hypothesis: Within each feature_057 stratum, white > black response rate.
    Result: SUPPORTED. The white-vs-black gap is present at every level of feature_057 (~1–2 pp absolute, largest in absolute terms in the worst-prognosis stratum). This corroborates iteration 9's finding of an additive (non-multiplicative) racial effect.

12. NON-LINEARITY OF CONTINUOUS PREDICTORS
    Hypothesis: At least one continuous feature has a non-linear (quadratic) relationship to response.
    Result: feature_092 quadratic term p = 2.6e-4 (significant U-shape). Other continuous features are well-approximated as linear; notably feature_078 (age) is unrelated to response in either linear or quadratic form. SUPPORTED for feature_092.

13. LOG-TRANSFORM OF feature_092
    Hypothesis: Because feature_092 is right-skewed (range 0.5 to 500), log(feature_092) is a better functional form than the raw value.
    Result: SUPPORTED. log(feature_092) in a 4-predictor multivariable model gives beta = -0.070 (p = 4.5e-9, AIC = 44,720) vs raw standardized feature_092 (beta = -0.058, p = 1e-5, AIC = 44,734). Log fits ~14 AIC points better.

14. ADDITIONAL BINARY FEATURES BEYOND THE CORE
    Hypothesis: Some binary features add signal beyond the 7-predictor core.
    Result: feature_093 (beta = +0.125, p = 0.004), feature_014 (+0.059, p = 0.017), feature_121 (-0.088, p = 0.048) all reach p < 0.05 by likelihood-ratio test. SUPPORTED but the gains are modest.

15. ADDITIONAL CONTINUOUS FEATURES BEYOND THE CORE
    Hypothesis: feature_084, feature_028, feature_031, feature_033 add signal.
    Result: feature_084 (+0.027, p=0.026) and feature_028 (-0.024, p=0.049) marginally significant. feature_031 and feature_033 borderline (p=0.05–0.06). Most other continuous features (feature_044, feature_119, feature_086, feature_037, feature_106, feature_077) are completely null after adjustment.

16. NON-LINEARITY OF feature_018
    Hypothesis: feature_018 (0..10) has a non-linear relationship with response.
    Result: REFUTED. Linear p = 0.34; categorical encoding LR test vs linear: chi-square = 5.89, df = 9, p = 0.75. feature_018 is unrelated to response in any form.

17. QUARTILE / DOSE-RESPONSE ANALYSIS
    Hypothesis: Top continuous features show monotone dose-response.
    Result: SUPPORTED. feature_011 (Q-rates 18.6/16.8/13.6%), feature_006 (19.0/17.5/16.5/14.7), feature_092 (18.0/17.4/16.6/15.6), feature_099 (15.8/16.8/17.6/17.8 — monotone increasing) all behave as expected from the linear logistic effects.

18. log(feature_092) × feature_057 INTERACTION
    Hypothesis: log(feature_092)'s effect varies across feature_057 strata.
    Result: Interaction beta = -0.038, p = 0.038 — borderline significant negative. This is the only interaction term among any pair of clinical features that crosses p < 0.05; high feature_092 has a slightly stronger negative effect at higher feature_057 levels.

19. THREE-WAY INTERACTION feature_057 × feature_011 × black
    Hypothesis: complex effect modification by race.
    Result: REFUTED. 3-way term p = 0.62; all 2-way and 3-way terms non-significant.

20. MODEL DISCRIMINATION
    Hypothesis: An 11-predictor logistic model achieves modest discrimination (AUC > 0.55).
    Result: SUPPORTED. In-sample AUC = 0.5989, pseudo-R² = 0.019. Discrimination is reliably above chance but modest; the great majority of response variance in this cohort is unexplained by the recorded features.

21. CALIBRATION BY RACE (CRITICAL FAIRNESS FINDING)
    Hypothesis: A clinical-only (race-blind) model is well calibrated across race groups.
    Result: REFUTED. Mean observed vs predicted response by race:
      white   17.28% obs vs 16.92% pred (+0.36 pp)
      hispanic 16.70% vs 16.83% (-0.14 pp)
      asian   16.33% vs 17.03% (-0.70 pp)
      black   15.74% vs 16.85% (-1.11 pp)
      other   14.72% vs 16.89% (-2.17 pp)
    The race-blind clinical model SYSTEMATICALLY OVER-PREDICTS response for black and 'other' patients (and slightly for asian patients). This residual mis-calibration is the same race effect we documented in iterations 6–7 — i.e., black patients respond ~6% worse on a relative scale than their clinical features predict.

22. PARALLEL-SLOPES TEST FOR feature_011 BY RACE
    Hypothesis: feature_011 has the same per-SD effect in black and non-black patients.
    Result: SUPPORTED. beta_black = -0.169 (p = 6e-6), beta_non-black = -0.153 (p < 1e-29); slopes nearly identical and interaction term non-significant (iter 9.3).

23. CUMULATIVE FAVORABLE-FEATURE COUNT
    Hypothesis: A simple count of feature_035, feature_093, feature_014 shows dose-response.
    Result: PARTIAL. Response rate by count is non-linear: 0 → 16.45% (n=25,575), 1 → 16.47% (n=21,281), 2 → 23.26% (n=3,044), 3 → 33.0% (n=100). Most of the gradient is driven by feature_035; the boost is meaningful only when ≥2 favorable features are present.

24. FINAL INTEGRATED MODEL
    Hypothesis: An 11-predictor model with log(feature_092) and a black-race indicator improves on the 7-predictor core.
    Result: SUPPORTED. AIC drops from 44,622 to 44,591 (Δ = 31). All 11 coefficients remain individually significant.

25. SYNTHESIS
    The strongest signals for objective_response in ds001_aml are six biomarkers/clinical variables: feature_057 (3-level ordinal, monotone protective with lower levels), feature_011 (continuous, lower = higher response), feature_006 (continuous, lower = higher response), log(feature_092) (continuous, lower = higher response), feature_099 (continuous, higher = higher response), and feature_035 (binary, present = higher response). After full adjustment, black race retains an OR of ~0.91 (p = 0.014). Pairwise and 3-way interactions are largely absent (only log_092 × feature_057 hits p = 0.038). The full 11-predictor model AUC is 0.60. Of the 125 candidate features, only ~15 carry meaningful signal; the remainder are essentially null.


OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
===================

Treatment / biomarker — outcome relationships:
- The principal driver of objective_response is the ordinal clinical / disease-stage variable feature_057. Each step up the scale reduces the odds of response by ~32% (OR = 0.68, p = 1.8e-97).
- A small panel of continuous biomarkers/labs (feature_011, feature_006, feature_063, feature_092, feature_099) provides additional, independent prognostic signal in a multiplicative log-odds way. feature_092 acts on the log scale and shows a borderline interaction with feature_057.
- A single binary marker, feature_035, is positively predictive (OR ≈ 1.43); patients with two or more of {feature_035, feature_093, feature_014} have markedly higher response (~23–33%).
- Patient age (feature_078) is essentially unrelated to objective_response in this cohort.
- The vast majority of features (about 110 of the 125) are null or near-null for response.

Effect modification:
- Tested ~15 pairwise and 1 three-way interaction. Only one (log(feature_092) × feature_057, p = 0.038) reached conventional significance, and this is a borderline finding given the number of interactions tested. The model is well-described as additive on the log-odds scale.

Demographic findings (fairness/equity):
- Black race is associated with a ~1.5 percentage-point lower response rate vs white (unadjusted p = 0.005) and this gap is NOT explained by clinical biomarkers, age, or insurance. The adjusted black-vs-white OR is approximately 0.90.
- A race-blind clinical model is mis-calibrated: it systematically over-predicts response for black patients (-1.11 pp) and 'other' patients (-2.17 pp). This is consistent with — and a quantitative manifestation of — the residual racial disparity that survives covariate adjustment.
- Insurance category is not associated with response and does not mediate the racial gap.
- Several common AML predictors that one might expect to matter (age in particular, and most multi-level ordinal markers) carry no detectable signal here.

Reliability caveats:
- Model AUC ≈ 0.60 means a great deal of response variance is unexplained by recorded features.
- All p-values are reported uncorrected; with the large sample (n = 50,000), p < 0.05 borderline findings (e.g., feature_125 trend, log_092 × feature_057 interaction) should be interpreted cautiously.
- The residual racial disparity is small in absolute terms but statistically robust and replicates across multiple model specifications and across all clinical-risk strata, making it the most consequential finding from a fairness perspective.
