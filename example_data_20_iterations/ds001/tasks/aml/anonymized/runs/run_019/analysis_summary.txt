Analysis summary — ds001_aml (50,000 patients, binary outcome objective_response, base rate 16.9%)

Approach
--------
After cataloguing the 125 features (80 binary, 7 small-integer ordinal, 36 continuous, 2
nominal-categorical [feature_005 race/ethnicity, feature_087 insurance]), I ran an
omnibus univariate screen of every feature against objective_response using the
appropriate test (chi-square for binary/categorical, Spearman rank correlation for
continuous and ordinal). I then built a multivariable logistic model around the
strongest signals, probed pairwise interactions and stratified subgroup effects,
and audited the ~80 remaining binary features for residual independent association.

Top main effects (all p < 1e-5)
-------------------------------
1. feature_057 (ordinal 0/1/2): the single strongest predictor. Response rate falls
   monotonically 21.3% → 15.5% → 11.2% across levels 0/1/2. Spearman rho = -0.095
   (p = 7.7e-100). Treated as categorical, OR vs level 0 is 0.679 at level 1 and
   0.466 at level 2 — i.e., the gradient is roughly linear on the logit scale and
   linear ordinal coding captures the bulk of the signal. This behaves like a
   stage / risk-class indicator.

2. feature_011 (continuous, 0–23.6, mean 3.86): higher → lower response.
   Mean 3.95 in non-responders vs 3.38 in responders; rho = -0.051 (p = 3e-30).
   Adjusted OR 0.961 per unit. Effect monotonic across quartile bins
   (18.6%, 16.8%, 13.6%).

3. feature_006 (continuous, 20–100, mean 60): higher → lower response. Adjusted OR
   0.994 per unit (p = 7e-20). Strictly monotonic across quartiles
   (19.0% → 17.5% → 16.5% → 14.7%).

4. feature_035 (binary, prevalence 7.2%): the only strong positive predictor among
   the binary features. Response 22.1% in feature_035=1 vs 16.5% in =0, an absolute
   risk difference of +5.6 percentage points and adjusted OR 1.43 (p = 1.9e-17).

5. feature_092 (continuous, 0.5–500, highly skewed): higher → lower response,
   rho = -0.027 (p = 2e-9), adjusted OR 0.998 per unit. Identical Spearman with log
   scale; the effect is small but real.

6. feature_099 (continuous, 1.7–5.5, mean 3.8): higher → higher response, the only
   positive-direction continuous predictor. Adjusted OR 1.13 per unit (p = 9e-7).

7. feature_063 (continuous, very right-skewed): higher → lower response, adjusted
   OR 0.993 per unit (p = 1e-6).

Multivariable model
-------------------
A logistic regression of objective_response on the seven predictors above and
patient age (feature_078) leaves all seven significant and confirms patient age
contributes nothing (p = 0.77). Pseudo-R² ≈ 0.018, full-model AUC = 0.597. Modest
discrimination is expected: each individual effect is small, and outcome is driven
by many small contributions.

Demographic / SES audit
-----------------------
- feature_087 (insurance): no univariate or adjusted effect
  (LR p = 0.47 for the 3 dummies after adjustment).
- feature_005 (race/ethnicity): a small but real residual effect.
  Univariate response: white 17.3%, hispanic 16.7%, asian 16.3%, black 15.7%,
  other 14.7% (omnibus p = 0.0096; max-min = 2.6 percentage points). After
  adjusting for the seven leading clinical predictors and insurance, the omnibus
  test for race remains significant (LR p = 0.0091, df = 4). No single contrast
  reaches p < 0.05; the spread is concentrated in white vs other (~2-3 point gap),
  suggesting a real, clinically small disparity that is not explained by the
  measured prognostic variables in this cohort.
- Age (feature_078): no association at any level (mean essentially identical
  between responders and non-responders, no monotonic quartile trend).

Interactions
------------
I tested pairwise logit-scale interactions among the top predictors. Only one
interaction reached p < 0.05:

- feature_099 × feature_035 (LR p = 0.025, coef = -0.186): the absolute benefit
  of feature_035 = 1 attenuates as feature_099 rises. Risk differences for
  feature_035 across feature_099 quartiles: Q1 +0.069, Q2 +0.053, Q3 +0.067,
  Q4 +0.034. Replicates the negative interaction sign.

The other tested interactions were not significant:
- feature_057 × feature_035: LR p = 0.63 (the feature_035 benefit is consistent
  across feature_057 levels — risk differences +0.061 / +0.053 / +0.044 at levels
  0/1/2, each significant on its own).
- feature_057 × feature_011: LR p = 0.62 (slope of feature_011 is similar across
  feature_057 strata: -0.041, -0.037, -0.052).
- feature_011 × feature_006: LR p = 0.11 (suggestive only).
- feature_063 × feature_011: LR p = 0.08 (suggestive only).
- feature_006 × feature_035, feature_092 × feature_011, feature_092 × feature_057,
  feature_099 × feature_057: all p > 0.1.

Adjusted screen of remaining binary features
--------------------------------------------
Refitting logistic models with each of the 80 binary features added on top of
the six leading non-binary predictors:
- 4/80 reach p < 0.05; only feature_035 reaches p < 0.001.
- Secondary positive signal: feature_093 (OR 1.13, adjusted p = 0.0036) — small
  but real after controlling for feature_035.
- Small negative signals: feature_014 (OR 1.06, p = 0.018) and feature_121
  (OR 0.91, p = 0.040).
- The remaining 76 binary features look like noise.

Multi-level ordinal features
----------------------------
- feature_125 (0–4): small monotonic negative trend, rho = -0.010, p = 0.020.
- feature_018 (0–10), feature_096 (0–4), feature_064 (0–4), feature_045 (0–4),
  feature_042 (0–4): no clear monotonic trend with response.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
Supported (significant in the predicted direction):
  feature_057 negative gradient; feature_011 negative; feature_006 negative;
  feature_035 positive (overall and within every feature_057 stratum);
  feature_092 negative; feature_099 positive; feature_063 negative;
  feature_093 positive (adjusted); feature_121 negative (adjusted);
  feature_125 negative ordinal; race omnibus residual effect;
  feature_099 × feature_035 negative interaction;
  full multivariable model discriminates better than chance (AUC 0.597).

Refuted or not supported:
  Patient age (feature_078) effect; insurance (feature_087) effect;
  feature_018 ordinal effect; feature_057 × feature_035 interaction;
  feature_057 × feature_011 interaction; feature_006 × feature_035
  interaction; feature_011 × feature_006 interaction; feature_063 × feature_011
  interaction.

Overall conclusion
------------------
Response in this AML-flavored cohort is dominated by a stage/risk-like ordinal
gradient (feature_057), a handful of continuous prognostic variables that all push
in the expected direction (higher feature_011, feature_006, feature_092, feature_063
→ worse; higher feature_099 → better), and a single binary feature_035 that confers
a roughly 1.4-fold increase in odds of response that is consistent across
prognostic strata. Demographic factors are nearly null: insurance has no measured
effect, age has no measured effect, and race shows a small but significant residual
signal (~2-3 percentage point spread, white highest, "other" lowest) that is not
explained by the measured clinical variables. Among pairwise interactions tested,
only feature_099 × feature_035 reached significance: the feature_035 benefit
attenuates at higher feature_099. The remaining 70+ binary features in the panel
are largely uninformative once the leading predictors are accounted for.
