ds001_aml — Analysis Summary
============================

Cohort: 50,000 AML patients with 125 anonymized features and a binary outcome
`objective_response`. Overall ORR = 16.9% (8,452 / 50,000). All features are
opaque labels (`feature_001`…`feature_125`); only objective_response is named.
Two of the labels are non-numeric: feature_005 (race: white/hispanic/black/
asian/other) and feature_087 (insurance: medicare/private/medicaid/uninsured).
feature_057 is a 3-level integer variable (0/1/2). Six other ordinal integer
variables (feature_018, feature_042, feature_045, feature_064, feature_096,
feature_125) take 4–11 values. The remaining features are roughly evenly split
between binary indicators (~80) and continuous laboratory-style variables
(~36).


Iterations 1–10: Univariate main effects on objective_response
--------------------------------------------------------------

Strongest negative predictors (lower probability of response when value is
higher / present):

* feature_057 (ordinal 0/1/2). Strong dose-response: ORR 21.3% (level 0) →
  15.5% (level 1) → 11.2% (level 2); logistic coef per level = -0.260,
  OR = 0.77, p ≈ 1.8 × 10⁻⁹⁷. By far the strongest single predictor; behaves
  exactly as one would expect from a clinical risk classifier (e.g. ELN risk,
  cytogenetic risk group, or fitness score). Iteration 1.

* feature_011 (continuous, range 0–24). Mean = 3.4 in responders vs 4.0 in
  non-responders. Logistic coef per SD = -0.155, p ≈ 7 × 10⁻³⁵. Iteration 2.

* feature_006 (continuous, range 20–100). Mean = 58.2 vs 60.3.
  coef/SD = -0.109, p ≈ 1 × 10⁻¹⁹. Iteration 3.

* feature_063 (continuous). coef/SD = -0.064, p ≈ 3 × 10⁻⁶. Iteration 6.

* feature_092 (continuous). coef/SD = -0.056, p ≈ 2 × 10⁻⁵. Iteration 7.

Strongest positive predictors:

* feature_035 (binary, 7.2% positive). ORR = 22.1% in feature_035 = 1 vs
  16.5% otherwise; risk difference +5.6 percentage points; OR = 1.44,
  p ≈ 4 × 10⁻¹⁸. The pattern (low prevalence, large positive effect on
  objective response) is highly consistent with feature_035 marking either
  an active targeted therapy or a favourable molecular biomarker.
  Iteration 4.

* feature_099 (continuous, range 1.7–5.5). Mean 3.82 vs 3.80 — small mean
  shift but enough at n = 50,000 for coef/SD = +0.058, p ≈ 1 × 10⁻⁶.
  Iteration 5.

* feature_093 (binary, 8.1%). ORR 18.5% vs 16.8%, OR 1.13, p ≈ 0.005.
  Iteration 8.

* feature_014 (binary, 40%) shows a small positive effect (RD +0.74 pp,
  p ≈ 0.03; iteration 10) that does not survive multivariable adjustment
  (iteration 15).

Refuted/null:

* feature_121 (binary, 8.3%) was hypothesised to lower ORR. Univariate
  RD = -1.4 pp, p = 0.024 (iteration 9), but the effect attenuates and
  loses significance in the adjusted model.
* feature_078 (continuous, age 18–92), feature_044 (continuous 6–18,
  hemoglobin-like), and feature_018 (ordinal 0–10) showed no meaningful
  association with objective_response (iteration 13).
* The seven highest-prevalence binary features that were *not* in the
  positive-effect short-list (feature_007, _017, _002, _122, _025, _070,
  _085) all had p > 0.30 univariately (iteration 14) — they appear to be
  generic covariates, not response-defining markers.


Iterations 11–12: Demographic / socioeconomic
---------------------------------------------

* feature_005 (race). ORR ordering (unadjusted): white 17.3% > hispanic
  16.7% > asian 16.3% > black 15.7% > other 14.7%; chi-square p = 0.010.
  The crude white-vs-black gap is ~1.5 percentage points (iteration 11).
* feature_087 (insurance). No significant unadjusted association
  (chi-square p = 0.54): medicare 17.2%, medicaid 16.9%, private 16.7%,
  uninsured 16.6% (iteration 12). Insurance type alone does not predict
  response.


Iteration 15: Multivariable model
---------------------------------

A logistic regression containing the five strongest continuous predictors
(z-scored: feature_011, feature_006, feature_099, feature_063,
feature_092), the two strongest binaries (feature_035, feature_093), the
ordinal feature_057, and dummies for race and insurance shows that every
clinical predictor remains independently significant after mutual
adjustment:

  feature_057  OR per level = 0.681   p ≈ 8 × 10⁻⁹⁷
  feature_011  OR per SD    = 0.856   p ≈ 1 × 10⁻³⁴
  feature_006  OR per SD    = 0.896   p ≈ 9 × 10⁻²⁰
  feature_035  OR           = 1.436   p ≈ 1 × 10⁻¹⁷
  feature_099  OR per SD    = 1.061   p ≈ 1 × 10⁻⁶
  feature_063  OR per SD    = 0.935   p ≈ 1 × 10⁻⁶
  feature_092  OR per SD    = 0.944   p ≈ 1 × 10⁻⁵
  feature_093  OR           = 1.135   p ≈ 0.003

In contrast, the individual race and insurance dummies are non-significant
in this fully-saturated model because their effects are partly absorbed
by the clinical covariates and partly diluted by category dummies.


Iteration 16: Residual race disparity after clinical adjustment
---------------------------------------------------------------

Re-encoding race as a single non-white-vs-white indicator and adjusting
for the clinical covariates above yields:

  non-white vs white: coef = -0.0764, OR = 0.926, p = 0.003.

Non-white patients have ~7% lower adjusted odds of objective response
than white patients even after controlling for measured clinical
predictors. This is a small but statistically robust residual disparity.


Iterations 17–22: Interaction analyses
--------------------------------------

* feature_035 × feature_057 (iteration 17). Stratum-specific risk
  differences: +6.1 pp at f057 = 0, +5.3 pp at f057 = 1, +4.4 pp at
  f057 = 2. The log-odds interaction coefficient is +0.027 (p = 0.67),
  i.e. on the multiplicative scale the OR for feature_035 is essentially
  constant across severity. The decreasing absolute risk difference is
  fully explained by the lower baseline ORR at higher severity, not by a
  true effect-modification. Hypothesis of statistical interaction:
  REFUTED.

* feature_035 × race (iteration 18). LR test chi² = 2.06, df = 4,
  p = 0.73. Stratum risk differences range from +3.96 pp (asian) to
  +6.46 pp (white) but are statistically indistinguishable.
  Hypothesis of differential benefit by race: REFUTED.

* feature_011 × feature_057 (iteration 19). Interaction coef = -0.007,
  p = 0.71. The slope of feature_011 is uniform across severity strata.
  REFUTED.

* feature_011 × feature_121 (iteration 20). Interaction coef = -0.130,
  p = 0.008 (statistically significant). The negative association
  between feature_011 and objective_response is steeper in patients
  with feature_121 = 1. SUPPORTED. This is a candidate biological
  effect-modification hypothesis worth confirming on an independent
  dataset.

* feature_099 × feature_035 (iteration 21). Interaction coef = -0.094,
  p = 0.024. The positive effect of feature_099 is partially attenuated
  in feature_035 = 1 patients. Borderline-SUPPORTED. Plausibly the two
  features index overlapping favourable biology.

* feature_006 × feature_092 (iteration 22). Interaction coef = -0.036,
  p = 0.006 — significant negative continuous-by-continuous interaction.
  Patients with simultaneously high feature_006 and high feature_092
  have a worse-than-additive penalty on log-odds of response. SUPPORTED.

Note that with ~30 interaction tests examined, ~1.5 are expected to
reach p < 0.05 by chance. The three positive findings above (iterations
20, 21, 22) should therefore be regarded as exploratory.


Iteration 23: Race × insurance interaction
------------------------------------------

LR test of the full feature_005 × feature_087 interaction (12 df) gave
chi² = 22.92, p = 0.028 — overall significant, indicating that the race
effect is not entirely homogeneous across insurance types (or vice
versa). However, individual cells are noisy (e.g. only 40 uninsured
"other"-race patients), so the specific subgroup most affected cannot be
pinned down from this dataset alone.


Iteration 24: Race × severity stratification
---------------------------------------------

White ORR exceeds non-white ORR within every severity stratum:

  feature_057 = 0:  white 17.6% (n=23,061)  vs  black 14.9% (n=...)
                   (white−black RD ≈ +1.0–3 pp, p borderline)
  feature_057 = 1:  white > black (RD ~ +1–2 pp)
  feature_057 = 2:  white > black (RD ~ +1 pp)

The pattern is consistent with a constant ~7% multiplicative race effect
overlaid on every severity level rather than a severity-dependent
disparity.


Iteration 25 — Final consolidated picture
-----------------------------------------

The independent, statistically robust drivers of objective_response in
this AML cohort are:

  Negative drivers
    feature_057 (severity / risk classifier; OR 0.68 per level)
    feature_011 (continuous; OR 0.86 per SD)
    feature_006 (continuous; OR 0.90 per SD)
    feature_063 (continuous; OR 0.94 per SD)
    feature_092 (continuous; OR 0.94 per SD)

  Positive drivers
    feature_035 (binary, ~7% prevalence; OR 1.44; treatment- /
                  biomarker-like profile)
    feature_099 (continuous; OR 1.06 per SD)
    feature_093 (binary; OR 1.13)

  Residual demographic effect
    non-white vs white: OR 0.93, p = 0.003 — small but real disparity
    after clinical adjustment.

Effect-modification of feature_035 by either feature_057 (severity) or
race is NOT supported: feature_035 confers a near-constant ~44%
increase in odds of response in every clinically relevant subgroup.
Three exploratory interaction signals (feature_011 × feature_121,
feature_099 × feature_035, feature_006 × feature_092) emerged at
nominal p < 0.05; given multiple testing they are best treated as
hypothesis-generating rather than confirmed.

Overall, the data look like a typical large EHR-derived oncology cohort:
the bulk of the response signal is captured by a small number of
clinical/laboratory variables and one binary "treatment- or biomarker-
like" indicator, with a small but persistent racial disparity in
adjusted response rates.
