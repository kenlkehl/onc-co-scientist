Analysis summary for ds001_aml (50,000 patients; binary outcome: objective_response, prevalence 16.9%)
==================================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Across 25 iterations of propose-test-refine, the analysis surveyed univariate associations
for all 125 features with objective_response, then progressively built up multivariable
adjusted models, subgroup analyses (by feature_057 strata and by race), and pairwise
interaction tests focused on the strongest main-effect predictors. The dataset is large
(n=50,000) so very small effects can be statistically significant; we therefore weight
both p-values and effect-size magnitudes when judging clinical importance.

DOMINANT MAIN EFFECTS
---------------------
1. feature_057 (3-level ordinal, levels 0/1/2, n=17592/24971/7437):
   This is the single strongest predictor. Response rates fall monotonically:
   21.3% -> 15.5% -> 11.2%. Adjusted log-odds = -0.385 per unit (OR=0.68 per step,
   p=2e-97). The dose-response is preserved in every subgroup tested, including
   within each race category. Likely a stage/risk-score-like variable.

2. feature_011 (continuous, range 0-23.6, right-skewed):
   Negative association. Mean 3.38 (responders) vs 3.95 (non-responders), Welch
   p=7e-39. Adjusted OR=0.96 per unit; a 5-unit rise corresponds to ~18% lower
   odds (p=1e-34). Robust to log transformation (p=4e-29) and to dichotomization
   at the 75th percentile (>6.4 vs <=6.4: 13.6% vs 18.0% response, OR=0.72,
   p=2e-29). Consistent with a tumor burden or laboratory marker that worsens
   with disease activity.

3. feature_006 (continuous, range 20-100):
   Negative direction. Mean 58.2 (responders) vs 60.3 (non-responders),
   p=9e-20. Categorically: <=50 -> 18.9%, 51-70 -> 16.9%, >70 -> 14.9%.
   The negative direction is notable: if feature_006 were a Karnofsky-like
   performance status (where higher means better functional status), the
   expected direction in oncology is positive. The observed sign argues
   that feature_006 instead encodes some adverse measure (e.g., a lab value
   normalized 0-100 where higher = worse, or a comorbidity index).

4. feature_035 (binary, prevalence 7%):
   Strong positive marker but only in interaction (see below). Univariate
   OR=1.44, p=4e-18. After adjustment OR remains 1.43, p=2e-17. The benefit,
   however, is essentially confined to feature_014=1 patients (see "Interactions").

5. feature_063 (continuous, p=4e-7), feature_099 (positive, p=1e-6),
   feature_092 (negative, p=1e-5): all retained as smaller but precise
   independent contributors in the multivariable model.

6. feature_093 (binary, OR=1.13, p=0.006), feature_121 (binary, OR=0.90, p=0.025),
   feature_014 (binary, OR=1.05, p=0.03): modest main effects but each is
   involved in at least one significant interaction term.

NEGATIVE FINDINGS (no detectable main effect)
---------------------------------------------
- feature_087 (insurance type: medicare/medicaid/private/uninsured): chi-square
  p=0.54 univariately, and within each feature_057 stratum response rates were
  identical to within ~1 percentage point. Insurance is not associated with
  response in this cohort.
- All multi-level integer features feature_096, feature_064, feature_018,
  feature_045, feature_042, feature_125: 4-10 df chi-square p-values >0.07.
  These ordinal counts (likely comorbidity tallies or symptom-count indices)
  do not contribute to response prediction.
- A large majority of binary features were null univariately (smallest OR
  effect sizes were typically <2 percentage points absolute and not
  significant after Bonferroni-style adjustment for ~85 binary tests).

THE FEATURE_035 x FEATURE_014 INTERACTION (most clinically actionable finding)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The strongest interaction in the dataset, by far:
   Response rate (cell):
       feature_035=0, feature_014=0:  16.6%   (n=27,791)
       feature_035=0, feature_014=1:  16.4%   (n=18,585)
       feature_035=1, feature_014=0:  17.1%   (n= 2,189)   <-- no benefit
       feature_035=1, feature_014=1:  29.8%   (n= 1,435)   <-- large benefit

Stratified analyses confirm this:
   * Within feature_014=1: feature_035 OR = 2.16, chi-square p = 9e-38
   * Within feature_014=0: feature_035 OR = 1.04, p = 0.51 (null)
   * Multiplicative interaction term: coef = +0.73, p = 8e-19

In the joint model, the feature_035 main effect (i.e., when feature_014=0)
is no longer significant once the interaction is included; this indicates the
apparent univariate feature_035 main effect is largely driven by the
feature_014=1 subgroup. Clinically this resembles a predictive biomarker
relationship: feature_035 selects responders only when feature_014 is also
positive (e.g., a treatment-by-marker pattern, or two markers that must
co-occur).

OTHER INTERACTIONS
------------------
- feature_093 x feature_121: positive interaction coef = +0.42, p = 0.004.
  Patients positive for both features have a 22.6% response rate vs 16.9%
  if neither is positive; feature_121 has its negative effect only in
  feature_093=0 patients.
- feature_006 x feature_092: negative interaction coef = -7e-5, p = 0.006.
  Patients with high values of both features have lower response than the
  additive model predicts.
- feature_121 x feature_011: negative interaction coef = -0.034, p = 0.008.
  The negative slope of feature_011 is steeper among feature_121=1 patients.
- feature_121 x feature_099: positive coef +0.22, p = 0.013.
- feature_035 x feature_099: negative coef -0.19, p = 0.024.
- All tested interactions with feature_057 (the dominant main-effect predictor)
  were null (p > 0.1). The feature_057 dose-response is essentially uniform
  across continuous and binary covariates and across race.

RACE / DEMOGRAPHIC EFFECTS (feature_005)
----------------------------------------
- Univariate response rates: white 17.3%, hispanic 16.7%, asian 16.3%,
  black 15.7%, other 14.7%. Overall 4-df chi-square p = 0.010.
- White vs non-white: OR=1.08, p=0.002. Black vs non-black: OR=0.91, p=0.012.
- After multivariable adjustment, race_black coef = -0.11, OR = 0.90, p = 0.005;
  race_other coef = -0.20, OR = 0.82, p = 0.030. race_hispanic and race_asian
  were not significantly different from white after adjustment.
- The racial gap is preserved across feature_057 strata (interaction NS),
  meaning it is not explained by differential distribution of the dominant
  prognostic marker. The residual disparity is small (a few percentage points)
  but statistically robust at this sample size.

INSURANCE
---------
feature_087 has no detectable main effect and no detectable subgroup effect.
This is a clean null in a dataset where many similarly-prevalent features
showed at least small associations.

OVERALL CLINICAL INTERPRETATION
-------------------------------
This cohort behaves as if its response is governed by:
  (a) A 3-level prognostic ordinal (feature_057), which dominates everything;
  (b) Two continuous markers — feature_011 and feature_006 — that each
      have monotonic negative gradients across their range and act
      independently of (a);
  (c) A predictive interaction between feature_035 and feature_014 that
      identifies a small (~3% of cohort) subgroup with substantially higher
      response (~30% vs the 17% baseline);
  (d) Smaller independent contributions from feature_063, feature_099,
      feature_092, feature_093, feature_121;
  (e) A residual race effect (black and 'other' vs white) of a few
      percentage points that survives adjustment for the above.

The full adjusted logistic regression has pseudo-R^2 only ~0.02, indicating
that even the strongest signals collectively explain a small fraction of
response variation; most of the predictive signal sits in feature_057 and the
two leading continuous markers, with the feature_035 x feature_014 interaction
being the most clinically actionable subgroup effect.
