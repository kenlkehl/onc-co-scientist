ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_aml (50,000 patients, binary outcome objective_response, baseline ORR = 16.9%)
============================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The cohort was screened across 124 anonymized features for associations with objective_response,
then characterized in detail through multivariable logistic regression, dose-response stratification,
demographic-disparity adjustment, and pre-specified pairwise interaction tests across 25 iterations.
The dataset is dominated by a small number of strong predictors against a backdrop of many
non-informative features.

A. SINGLE STRONGEST PREDICTOR: feature_057 (3-LEVEL ORDINAL)
------------------------------------------------------------
* Levels 0/1/2 with prevalences 35%/50%/15% (uniform across racial groups).
* Crude ORR: 21.3% (level 0) -> 15.5% (level 1) -> 11.2% (level 2). All three pairwise
  comparisons highly significant (p ranging 1e-20 to 4e-80; overall chi-square p = 5.5e-98).
* Linear-ordinal log-odds form: each step increases level reduces odds of response by 32%
  (OR = 0.682 per level, p = 3e-96). Linear-vs-categorical LR test p = 0.99 -> the gradient is
  log-linear; level can be treated as a single ordinal score. This is the dominant feature in
  the dataset by a wide margin.

B. CONTINUOUS PREDICTORS WITH INDEPENDENT EFFECTS
-------------------------------------------------
The six top continuous predictors are mutually uncorrelated (|r| < 0.01 for all pairs), so each
contributes orthogonal information.
* feature_011 (range 0-23.6, ~28% of patients = 0): negative, OR per SD = 0.86, p = 7e-35.
  Adjusted OR per unit = 0.96. Within nonzero values the gradient is monotonic across quintiles
  (Q1 ORR 18.6% -> Q5 ORR 12.9%). The zero subgroup behaves at the upper end of nonzero quintiles
  on the raw scale; after fitting a linear term, an additional zero-indicator is borderline (p=0.058)
  with a small negative residual, indicating no separate threshold effect of consequence.
* feature_006 (range 20-100, mean 60, plausibly age in years): negative, OR per SD = 0.90,
  p = 1e-19. Monotonic across deciles (lowest decile ORR 19.4% -> top decile 14.3%, ~5 ppt drop).
  Adjusted OR per unit = 0.994.
* feature_063 (range 0.03-300): negative, OR per SD = 0.94, p = 3e-6.
* feature_092 (range 0.5-500): negative, OR per SD = 0.95, p = 2e-5.
* feature_099 (range 1.7-5.5, mean 3.8, plausibly a serum protein like albumin): positive,
  OR per SD = 1.06, p = 1e-6.
* feature_084: weak positive, OR per SD = 1.02, p = 0.05.

C. BINARY PREDICTORS
--------------------
* feature_035 (prevalence 7.2%, treatment-like indicator): the dominant binary predictor.
  Univariate ORR 0.221 (=1) vs 0.165 (=0), diff +5.6 ppt, p=3.5e-18. Adjusted OR = 1.43,
  p = 2e-17. Effect is positive and consistent across age tertiles, racial groups,
  and feature_057 strata (all interaction tests p > 0.25).
* Three additional binary features retained adjusted significance after biology adjustment:
  feature_093 (OR 1.13, p = 4e-3, positive), feature_014 (OR 1.06, p = 0.02, positive),
  feature_121 (OR 0.91, p = 0.05, negative). The remaining 76 binary features had no
  detectable adjusted association.
* feature_125 (5-level ordinal): weak negative trend, coef -0.023 per level, p = 0.038.

D. DEMOGRAPHIC DISPARITIES (RACE AND INSURANCE)
-----------------------------------------------
* Insurance (feature_087: medicare/medicaid/private/uninsured) showed no association with
  objective_response unadjusted (p = 0.54) or after biology adjustment (all p > 0.55). ORR
  differences across insurance categories were < 0.5 ppt.
* Race (feature_005): weak overall association in univariate analysis (chi-square p = 0.0096),
  driven by white patients having ~1 ppt higher ORR than other groups (white 17.3%, hispanic
  16.7%, asian 16.3%, black 15.7%, other 14.7%). Crucially, the distribution of feature_057
  is essentially identical across racial groups (all within 2 ppt of each other), so the
  univariate gap is *not* mediated by differential biology in this measured set. After
  adjustment for feature_057, feature_011, feature_006, and feature_035, all racial coefficients
  fall to non-significance (largest p = 0.13 for white). The unadjusted gap is small in absolute
  terms and explained by other measured covariates; race contributes no detectable independent
  signal here.
* No statistically significant race x feature_057 interaction (LR p = 0.91): the strong
  feature_057 gradient applies uniformly to all racial groups.
* No statistically significant race x feature_035 interaction (LR p = 0.73): the +4 to +7 ppt
  feature_035 benefit is consistent across racial groups.

E. INTERACTIONS / EFFECT MODIFICATION
-------------------------------------
With feature_011, feature_006, feature_063, feature_092, feature_099, feature_035 as candidate
modifier pairs, eight of nine pairwise interaction tests were non-significant (p ranging
0.08 to 0.82). The single nominal exception was feature_099 x feature_035 (LR p = 0.025,
negative): the +5 to +6 ppt feature_035 benefit was 1.4 ppt larger in patients with below-median
feature_099 (+6.4 ppt) than above-median (+5.0 ppt). Given the multiplicity of tests performed,
this finding should be regarded as hypothesis-generating rather than definitive.

Stratified analyses confirmed homogeneity:
* feature_035 effect across feature_057 strata: +6.2/+5.4/+4.4 ppt (LR p = 0.92).
* feature_035 effect across feature_006 (age) tertiles: +4.2/+7.8/+4.9 ppt (interaction p = 0.39).
* feature_011 within-stratum coefficients (feature_057 = 0/1/2): -0.041/-0.037/-0.052
  (LR p = 0.39).
* feature_006 effect across feature_057 strata: LR p = 0.49.

F. FINAL MULTIVARIABLE MODEL
----------------------------
Logistic regression with feature_057 dummies, feature_011, feature_006, feature_063,
feature_092, feature_099, feature_084, feature_035, feature_093, feature_014, feature_121
(n = 50,000, McFadden pseudo-R^2 = 0.019, LLR chi-square approx 853, p < 1e-170, AIC 44623).
All listed predictors retained nominally significant adjusted associations. Discriminative
ability is modest in absolute terms (consistent with a noisy multifactorial outcome) but the
overall model fit is highly significant.

G. HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED vs REFUTED
----------------------------------
SUPPORTED:
* Existence of strong response predictors among binary features (h1, h2: feature_035).
* Existence of strong response predictors among continuous features (h3, h4, h5).
* Strong ordinal effect of feature_057 with monotonic ORR gradient (h6, h9, h22).
* Independent multivariable contributions of biology, age-like, and treatment-like signals (h10).
* Monotonic dose-response in feature_006 (h11) and feature_011 (h12 partially).
* Mutual orthogonality of continuous predictors (h20, h21).
* Race-based ORR gap explained away by other covariates (h15).
* Insurance unrelated to response (h8 adjusted, h14).
* Adjusted screen leaves only four significant binary features (h24).

REFUTED / NULL:
* feature_011 == 0 as a separate threshold category beyond the linear effect (h12 partially, h26).
* Race x feature_057 interaction (h16): null.
* Race x feature_035 interaction (h28): null.
* feature_035 x feature_006 interaction (h17): null.
* feature_011 x feature_057 (h18) and feature_006 x feature_057 (h19) interactions: null.
* feature_035 x feature_057 interaction (h30): null.

NOMINAL / WEAK:
* feature_099 x feature_035 negative interaction (h25, h27): borderline at p = 0.025.
* feature_125 ordinal effect (h29): borderline at p = 0.038.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
The objective_response outcome in this AML cohort is shaped predominantly by a single 3-level
biology stratification (feature_057), independently modified by several uncorrelated continuous
biomarkers (feature_011, feature_006, feature_063, feature_092, feature_099) and a binary
treatment-like indicator (feature_035, OR 1.43, present in 7% of patients). The biology and
treatment signals are essentially additive on the log-odds scale: no clinically meaningful
interaction was identified, and effects are consistent across age, race, and biology strata.
Demographic disparities by race are small (~1 ppt) and fully attenuated after adjustment for
the measured covariates; insurance type is unrelated to response. Most binary features in the
dataset (76 of 80) carry no detectable adjusted association with response. The full multivariable
model explains a modest 1.9% of pseudo-R^2 variance, reflecting a multifactorial outcome where
the measured features collectively shift response probability by a meaningful but limited amount.
