Analysis Summary — ds001_aml (50,000 patients, outcome: objective_response)
==========================================================================

Overview
--------
The cohort comprises 50,000 de-identified AML patients with 124 anonymized
features (80 binary, 7 ordinal/multi-level integer, 36 continuous, 2
free-text categorical: race-like feature_005 and insurance-like feature_087)
and a single binary outcome, objective_response (overall rate 16.9%; 8,452
responders).  Across 25 iterations I screened every feature for an unadjusted
association with objective_response, then drilled into subgroups,
interactions, and a final multivariable model.

Headline findings
-----------------
1.  feature_057 (a 3-level ordinal: 17,592/24,971/7,437 patients with response
    rates 21.3% / 15.5% / 11.2%) is by far the strongest predictor.
    Each one-unit increase reduces the odds of response by ~32%
    (OR 0.681, 95% CI ~0.66-0.71, p = 1.84e-97).  A linear-vs-categorical
    likelihood-ratio test (LRT chi2 = 0.03, p = 0.86) shows the dose-response
    is essentially linear, supporting an ordinal/risk-strata interpretation
    (consistent with a performance-status- or risk-tier-like variable).

2.  feature_035 (a low-prevalence binary marker; 7.2% positive) is associated
    with higher response: 22.1% vs 16.5%, OR 1.44, p = 4.1e-18.  But the
    effect is highly conditional (see #3).

3.  Two large, replicable interactions modify feature_035's effect:
        feature_027 (interaction p = 1.1e-32; stratified OR 1.01 vs 2.86)
        feature_014 (interaction p = 7.7e-18; stratified OR 1.04 vs 2.16)
    A composite "qualifying" indicator (feature_027 = 1 OR feature_014 = 1)
    cleanly separates the patients in whom feature_035 matters (n = 29,009;
    OR 1.80, p = 2e-28) from those in whom it does not (n = 20,991; OR 1.03,
    p = 0.67).  This is the classical biomarker-defined responder pattern.

4.  Five continuous biomarkers carry independent prognostic signal in the
    full multivariable model:
        feature_011  (range 0-23.6)        OR/SD 0.856  p = 1.2e-34   (lower=better)
        feature_006  (range 20-100)        OR/SD 0.895  p = 3.0e-20   (lower=better)
        feature_099  (range 1.7-5.5)       OR/SD 1.060  p = 1.1e-06   (higher=better)
        feature_092  (range 0.5-500)       OR/SD 0.943  p = 8.8e-06   (lower=better)
        feature_063  (range 0.03-300)      OR/SD 0.936  p = 1.7e-06   (lower=better)
    Log transforms confirm direction and significance for the right-skewed
    features (feature_092 log-OR 0.92 p = 4e-9; feature_063 log-OR 0.93 p = 1e-6).

5.  Joint stratification by feature_057 x feature_035 x feature_027 yields a
    response gradient from 11.1% (feature_057 = 2, feature_035 = 0,
    feature_027 = 0) up to 38.4% (feature_057 = 0, feature_035 = 1,
    feature_027 = 1, n = 375).  This 3-fold gradient is the largest
    discrimination in the dataset and identifies a clinically meaningful
    super-responder subgroup.

What was NOT associated
-----------------------
* Patient age (feature_078; mean 68 yr, range 18-92): OR 1.0004 / year,
  p = 0.72 — flat across age quartiles (16.7% / 17.4% / 16.6% / 16.9%).
* Insurance type (feature_087; medicare/private/medicaid/uninsured): all
  response rates within 0.55 percentage points of each other; chi-square
  p ~ 0.7.
* Race/ethnicity (feature_005): unadjusted chi-square p = 0.0095 with white
  marginally higher (17.3%) and "other" lowest (14.7%), but the difference
  vanishes after adjusting for feature_057 + feature_035 + feature_011 +
  feature_006 + age (all race coefficients p > 0.13).  The marginal
  univariate disparity is fully explained by clinical case-mix.
* All 15 binary features near 50/50 prevalence (the "sex-likely" candidates,
  including feature_002, feature_122, feature_070, feature_017, feature_085,
  feature_007, feature_012, etc.): max absolute response-rate difference
  is ~+0.0074 (feature_014, p = 0.031, and that is the same feature that
  acts as an interaction modifier of feature_035, not a main effect).
* The 5-level ordinal features feature_096, feature_064 and the 11-level
  count-like feature_018 — all unassociated (p > 0.5).
* The vast majority of binary features: only ~14 of 124 reach unadjusted
  p < 0.05; only 7 cross the Bonferroni threshold (alpha = 4.0e-04).  This
  is consistent with most candidate features being uninformative noise.

Heterogeneity tests for the dominant predictors
-----------------------------------------------
* feature_057 is homogeneous: its effect is similar across strata of every
  continuous biomarker (interaction p > 0.13 for f057 x f011, f057 x f006,
  f057 x f099, f057 x f092, f057 x f063, f057 x f078) and across all five
  race groups (race-stratified ORs 0.64-0.74, all p < 0.05).
* feature_057 x feature_035 interaction is null (p = 0.67); feature_035's
  benefit is preserved at every feature_057 level.

Final multivariable model
-------------------------
A 12-parameter logistic model — feature_057 + feature_035 + feature_027 +
feature_014 + (feature_035 x feature_027) + (feature_035 x feature_014) +
five standardized continuous biomarkers — captures the principal predictable
signal.  Adjusted ORs:
    feature_057                    0.679  (p = 1e-97)
    feature_035 (with f027 or f014) ~1.8-2.9 conditional
    feature_011                    0.855 / SD  (p = 1e-34)
    feature_006                    0.895 / SD  (p = 3e-20)
    feature_099                    1.063 / SD  (p = 4e-7)
    feature_092                    0.944 / SD  (p = 1.2e-5)
    feature_063                    0.935 / SD  (p = 1e-6)
Adding race or insurance dummies does not improve fit and leaves no
demographic coefficient significant.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
SUPPORTED
  H2.1  feature_057 -> negative monotone effect on response.  (p < 1e-97)
  H3.1  Linear ordinal coding suffices.                       (LRT p = 0.86)
  H4.1  feature_035 -> higher response.                       (p = 4e-18)
  H5.1/5.2  feature_011, feature_006 -> negative continuous effects.
  H6.1/6.2/6.3  feature_099 +, feature_092 -, feature_063 -.
  H8.1  Marginal race association exists (p = 0.0095).
  H12.1  feature_035 x feature_027 interaction.               (p = 1e-32)
  H13.1  feature_035 x feature_014 interaction.               (p = 8e-19)
  H14.1  Composite qualifying biomarker explains modification.
  H15.1  3-way subgroup yields ~38% super-responder cell.
  H17.1  feature_093 weak positive predictor.                 (p = 0.005)
  H18.1/18.2  feature_125 weak negative; feature_084 weak positive.
  H20.1  All five continuous biomarkers retain effect when adjusted jointly.
  H24.1  feature_121 weak negative.                           (p = 0.024)
  H25.1  Final parsimonious 12-parameter model captures dominant signal.

REFUTED / NULL
  H1.2  feature_078 represents age but does NOT predict response (p = 0.72).
  H7.1  Age effect on response.                               (NOT significant)
  H9.1  Race remains independent after adjustment.            (NOT significant)
  H10.1  Insurance affects response.                          (NOT significant)
  H11.1  feature_057 x feature_035 interaction.               (NOT significant)
  H21.1  A 50/50 binary acts as a sex predictor of response.  (NOT significant)
  H22.1  Demographic adjustment finds independent demographic signal.   (refuted; no demographic effect)
  H23.1  5-level/11-level ordinals carry signal.              (NOT significant)
  H24.2  feature_111 negative.                                (BORDERLINE p = 0.054)

Overall conclusion
------------------
Objective response in this AML cohort is driven by a small set of biological
predictors organized in a clean structure:
  (a) a strong ordinal risk variable (feature_057) that linearly halves the
      odds of response across its range,
  (b) a positive marker (feature_035) whose benefit is conditional on the
      presence of at least one of two qualifying co-markers (feature_027 or
      feature_014) — together yielding the largest subgroup gradient in the
      dataset (11% to 38% response),
  (c) five independent continuous biomarkers (feature_011, feature_006,
      feature_099, feature_092, feature_063) that each contribute additional,
      non-redundant prognostic information.
Patient age, insurance, and race do not independently affect response after
adjustment for case mix, and the bulk of the remaining 100+ candidate
features behave as noise.  The findings are consistent with a clinical
picture in which a single strong risk-strata variable plus a small
biomarker-defined responder subset organize most of the predictable
treatment-response heterogeneity in this cohort.
