Analysis Summary — ds001_aml (50,000-patient AML cohort)
=========================================================

Outcome: objective_response (binary; overall rate 16.9%, n=8452/50000).
Predictors: 80 binary features, 36 continuous features, race (feature_005, 5 categories), insurance (feature_087, 4 categories).

Iterations 1-2: Univariate screening
------------------------------------
Among binary features, the strongest single predictor of objective_response was feature_035 (response rate 22.13% in feature_035=1 vs 16.50% in feature_035=0; absolute difference +5.63 pp; chi-square p=3.5e-18). Other significant binary hits included feature_093 (+1.7 pp, p=5.6e-3), feature_121 (-1.4 pp, p=2.5e-2), feature_014 (+0.7 pp, p=3.2e-2). Among continuous features, feature_011 was strongly negatively correlated with response (mean 3.38 in responders vs 3.95 in non-responders; r=-0.055, p=4.6e-35). feature_006 also showed a clear negative gradient (mean 58.2 vs 60.3; r=-0.041, p=1.0e-19), with smaller effects from feature_099 (positive, p=1.4e-6), feature_063 (negative, p=2.9e-6), and feature_092 (negative, p=1.9e-5). Quintile analyses confirmed monotonic gradients for feature_011 (Q1 18.6% to Q5 13.3%) and feature_006 (Q1 18.7% to Q5 14.9%).

Iteration 3: Demographics
-------------------------
Race showed a small but significant univariate association (chi-square p=9.6e-3): white 17.28%, hispanic 16.70%, asian 16.33%, black 15.74%, other 14.72%. Insurance type was not associated (p=0.54).

Iteration 4: Age (feature_078)
------------------------------
Although feature_078 (mean 68, range 18-92) is presumptively patient age, it had essentially no association with objective_response: r=-0.001, p=0.83, with flat quintile rates (16.7-17.0% across all age strata). This is unusual for AML, where age typically predicts worse outcomes — suggesting the cohort either represents a setting where age is heavily confounded with selection, or the response-defining therapy is age-agnostic.

Iteration 5: Multivariable adjustment
-------------------------------------
A multivariable logistic regression including the top binary, continuous, race and insurance variables found:
- feature_035: coef +0.366 (OR 1.44, p=3.8e-18)
- feature_011: coef -0.041 (p<1e-30)
- feature_006: coef -0.006 (p<1e-19)
- feature_063: coef -0.007 (p<1e-5)
- feature_092: coef -0.002 (p<1e-4)
- feature_099: coef +0.117 (p<1e-5)
- feature_093: coef +0.118 (p=5e-3)
- feature_078 (age): p=0.77 (no effect)
- All race indicators: p>0.15 (white p=0.16)
- All insurance indicators: p>0.5
The univariate race association attenuated to non-significance after adjusting for clinical features, indicating the small race effect was confounded by the joint distribution of clinical predictors.

Iterations 6-8: Robustness and demographic subgroup checks
----------------------------------------------------------
An adjusted screen of every binary feature (controlling for top continuous predictors) confirmed the same set of significant predictors as in univariate analysis (feature_035, feature_093, feature_121, feature_014, feature_111, feature_012). Tests for feature_035 × race interaction (p>0.4 across all race strata; effect 4-6 pp in each race) and feature_011 × race interaction (all p>0.37) showed no heterogeneity by race.

Iterations 9-12: Discovery of the central interaction structure
---------------------------------------------------------------
A scan of feature_035 × continuous interactions revealed only feature_035 × feature_099 was marginal (p=0.024). However, a scan of pairwise binary × binary interactions revealed two highly significant signals:
- feature_035 × feature_014: interaction coef +0.730, p=7.7e-18
- feature_093 × feature_121: interaction coef +0.421, p=3.7e-3
A broader scan of feature_035 × all binaries identified feature_027 as an even stronger modifier: feature_035 × feature_027 interaction coef +1.046, p=1.1e-32. Together this suggested that feature_035's efficacy depends on the joint presence of both feature_014 and feature_027.

A saturated 2×2×2 logistic model on feature_014 × feature_027 × feature_035 confirmed a striking three-way interaction (coef +2.144, p=1.4e-29). The 8 cells of this 3-way table revealed that response rate is essentially baseline (~16-17%) in seven of the eight cells, but jumps to 62.06% in the cell where all three are positive (n=427):

  feature_014=0, feature_027=0, feature_035=0: 16.85% (n=19422)
  feature_014=0, feature_027=0, feature_035=1: 17.27% (n=1569)
  feature_014=0, feature_027=1, feature_035=0: 15.90% (n=8369)
  feature_014=0, feature_027=1, feature_035=1: 16.77% (n=620)
  feature_014=1, feature_027=0, feature_035=0: 16.52% (n=12987)
  feature_014=1, feature_027=0, feature_035=1: 16.07% (n=1008)
  feature_014=1, feature_027=1, feature_035=0: 16.08% (n=5598)
  feature_014=1, feature_027=1, feature_035=1: 62.06% (n=427)

This pattern is highly characteristic of a targeted-therapy effect: feature_035 acts like a treatment that is efficacious only in the dual-biomarker-positive subgroup defined by feature_014=1 AND feature_027=1.

Iteration 13: Confirmation in adjusted within-subgroup model
------------------------------------------------------------
Within the biomarker-pair subgroup (n=6025), an adjusted logistic regression including the top continuous prognostic markers gave: feature_035 coef +2.180 (OR 8.85, 95% CI 7.17-10.93, p=2.2e-91). The continuous prognostic predictors retained their direction and significance within this subgroup.

Iterations 14-16: Bias and equity checks
----------------------------------------
We tested whether the apparent feature_035 benefit was unevenly distributed by race or insurance, which would suggest treatment-access bias.
- Within the biomarker-pair subgroup, feature_035 prevalence by race: white 7.25%, hispanic 7.68%, black 7.02%, asian 5.46%, other 2.44%. Chi-square p=0.19 — not significant. In the full cohort, prevalence by race ranged 7.18-7.51% (p=0.94).
- feature_035 prevalence by insurance within biomarker-pair: medicare 7.43%, medicaid 7.13%, private 6.84%, uninsured 6.02%. Chi-square p=0.78.
- feature_035 efficacy (absolute increase in response) within biomarker-pair, by race: white +48.4 pp, hispanic +45.7 pp, asian +46.7 pp, black +33.6 pp, other +20.0 pp. Black/other groups have numerically smaller benefit but small N (51 and 3 treated respectively); logit interaction terms feature_035 × race not significant (all p>0.4).
Conclusion: no statistically supported evidence of differential treatment access or differential efficacy by race or insurance.

Iterations 17-18: Effect modifiers within the biomarker-pair subgroup
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Within biomarker-pair patients, scans of feature_035 × continuous interactions surfaced several modest effect modifiers:
- feature_011: -0.064 (p=2.3e-2; drug works less well at high feature_011, consistent with feature_011 being a general adverse prognostic marker)
- feature_078 (age): +0.026 (p=9.8e-3; the older the patient, the larger the absolute benefit -- noteworthy because age has no main effect on response)
- feature_086: +0.139 (p=7.1e-3)
- feature_094: -0.085 (p=1.7e-2)
- feature_037: +0.092 (p=1.9e-2)

Iteration 19: A second smaller pairwise interaction
---------------------------------------------------
After clinical adjustment, feature_093 × feature_121 retained a positive interaction (coef +0.433, p=3.0e-3). Cell rates: both 0 = 16.92%; only feature_093=1 = 18.11%; only feature_121=1 = 15.03%; both =1 = 22.62%. feature_121 alone is associated with lower response (a negative main effect), but the combination of feature_093 and feature_121 elevates response above baseline. This is a much smaller-magnitude pattern than the f014/f027/f035 triad.

Iteration 20: Continuous-continuous interactions
------------------------------------------------
Among the top continuous predictors, only feature_006 × feature_092 showed a marginal non-additive effect (interaction coef -1.4e-4, p=5.6e-3). feature_011 × feature_006 was nominal (p=9.3e-2). A median-split 2×2 of feature_011 × feature_006 showed approximately additive joint effects (both-low cell 20.1%, both-high cell 14.1%).

Iteration 21: Search for additional treatment-marker triads
-----------------------------------------------------------
A scan of three-way interactions excluding feature_035 found weak signals: (feature_093, feature_058, feature_124) coef +1.291, p=2.6e-3; (feature_093, feature_012, feature_058) p=7.2e-3; (feature_012, feature_027, feature_058) p=8.5e-3. None approach the magnitude of the feature_014 × feature_027 × feature_035 triad.

Iterations 22-24: Robustness of prognostic predictors
-----------------------------------------------------
feature_011 quintile response rates: Q1 18.61%, Q2 17.62%, Q3 16.31%, Q4 13.27% (clean monotonic). feature_006 quintile rates: Q1 18.74%, Q2 18.45%, Q3 16.55%, Q4 15.88%, Q5 14.87% (clean monotonic). After excluding the triple-positive responder cell, the multivariable continuous predictors (feature_011, feature_006, feature_063, feature_092, feature_099) retained essentially identical effect sizes -- they are genuine prognostic markers, not confounders of feature_035.

Iteration 25: Final synthesis
-----------------------------
The dominant clinically-meaningful pattern in this AML cohort is a targeted-therapy effect:
1) Patients with the dual-biomarker pattern feature_014=1 AND feature_027=1 (n=6025; 12.0% of cohort) who receive feature_035 (n=427 within biomarker-pair) have a ~46 percentage-point absolute increase in objective_response (62.1% vs 16.1% within the biomarker-defined population), corresponding to an adjusted OR of 8.85.
2) Outside the biomarker-defined subgroup, feature_035 has essentially no effect on response.
3) Independent prognostic markers of response (across the full cohort) are: feature_011 (lower response with higher values; OR 0.96 per unit, p<1e-30), feature_006 (lower with higher; OR 0.994 per unit, p<1e-19), feature_063 (lower with higher; OR 0.993 per unit, p<1e-5), feature_092 (lower with higher; OR 0.998 per unit, p<1e-4), and feature_099 (higher with higher; OR 1.12 per unit, p<1e-5).
4) A second, smaller positive interaction involves feature_093 × feature_121 (within-cell response 22.6% when both =1; adjusted interaction p=3e-3).
5) Patient age (feature_078), race (feature_005), and insurance type (feature_087) have negligible main effects on objective_response after adjustment for clinical features. There is no statistically supported evidence of differential treatment access (feature_035 receipt) or differential treatment efficacy (feature_035 × race or × insurance interaction) within the biomarker-defined subgroup.

Hypotheses supported (key examples): h1, h2, h6, h7, h11, h12, h13, h14, h15, h19, h20, h21, h22, h24, h25, h26, h27. Hypotheses refuted/not supported: h3 (race effect attenuates after adjustment), h5 (no age effect), h9 (no feature_035 × race interaction), h10 (no feature_011 × race interaction), h16 (no within-subgroup race interaction), h17 (no race-based access disparity), h18 (no insurance-based access disparity).
