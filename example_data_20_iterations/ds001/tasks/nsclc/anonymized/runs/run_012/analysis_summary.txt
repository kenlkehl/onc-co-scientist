Analysis Summary — ds001_nsclc (50,000 patients, binary outcome objective_response, 16.9% positive)
====================================================================================================

Approach
--------
The dataset contains 123 anonymized features and one binary outcome. Across 25 iterations,
I ran a propose-test-refine loop: a univariate screen of binary, categorical, and continuous
features (iters 1-3), a multivariable logistic regression of the top predictors (iter 4),
pairwise and stratified interaction tests (iters 5-9, 16-19, 22-23), subgroup-stratified
models (iter 8, 12, 13, 18, 22), an adjusted screen for additional binary predictors
(iter 15, 24), insurance-disparity testing (iter 20), and a final synthesis multivariable
model with biomarker x treatment interactions (iter 25).

Strongest predictors of objective_response
-------------------------------------------
1.  feature_051 (3-level ordinal 0/1/2). Univariate logistic OR=0.687 per unit, p=1.7e-93;
    adjusted OR=0.68. By far the strongest predictor in the dataset. The ordinal scale and
    direction are consistent with a performance-status-like clinical variable in NSCLC.

2.  feature_011 (count, 0-28). Univariate OR=0.962 per unit, p=1.8e-32; adjusted OR=0.96.
    Categorically the response rate falls monotonically: 19.3% (=0), 18.0% (1-2), 16.8%
    (3-5), 15.1% (6-10), 12.8% (11+). Consistent with a tumor-burden/comorbidity count.

3.  feature_013 (binary). Univariate response 15.4% (=1) vs 19.6% (=0); OR=0.744, p<1e-300.
    Adjusted OR=0.74. Strong independent negative predictor.

4.  feature_067 (binary). Univariate 14.4% vs 17.7%; OR=0.777, p<1e-300. Adjusted OR=0.77.
    feature_013 and feature_067 act independently (interaction p=0.51).

5.  feature_006, feature_007, feature_039 (binary; modest positive main effects).
    Univariate ORs 1.14, 1.09, 1.07 (all p<0.005). When summed into a regimen_count
    variable (0-3), response rate climbs from 16.1% (0 components) to 23.0% (all 3).

6.  feature_099 (continuous, 1.7-5.5). Adjusted OR=1.10 per unit, p=1e-4. Quartile rates
    rise from 16.0% (Q1) to 17.7% (Q3-Q4) and plateau.

7.  feature_063 (continuous, range 0-297). Small per-unit OR=0.995, p=7e-4 univariate;
    retained as significant in multivariable model.

8.  feature_092 (continuous, 0-0.8). Marginal univariate OR=1.18 per unit, p=3.2e-3 — a
    weak overall main effect. The interpretation changes dramatically once interactions
    are tested (see below).

Predictive biomarker–treatment interaction (the key finding)
------------------------------------------------------------
The most striking pattern in the cohort is a strong predictive interaction between
feature_092 (continuous) and the trio feature_006 / feature_007 / feature_039 (each
binary). Each of the three has its own significant interaction with feature_092
(interaction-term ORs 1.81-1.85, p between 1.3e-6 and 6.1e-8 in iter 5; jointly
significant in iter 16).

Aggregating the three into regimen_count (0-3) makes the structure transparent:
- regimen_count*feature_092 interaction: coef +0.604, p=4e-19 (iter 9).
- Within regimen_count=0 patients (n≈10,000): feature_092 has OR=0.89 per unit
  (p=0.37, NS).
- Within regimen_count=3 patients (n≈3,200): feature_092 has OR=16.05 per unit
  (p=2e-39).
- Joint table (iter 17/23): in the top quintile of feature_092 the response rate
  is 15.4%, 16.1%, 17.7%, 46.2% for regimen_count = 0, 1, 2, 3 respectively. The
  46% rate in the (regimen=3, top-quintile) cell is not approached by any other
  cell in the table.
- Within-tertile regression (iter 18): regimen_count's OR is 1.02 (p=0.43) in the
  bottom tertile of feature_092, 1.00 (p=0.87) in the middle tertile, and 1.28
  (p=4.7e-24) in the top tertile.

Interpretation: feature_092 looks like a continuous predictive biomarker (PD-L1-like
in this oncology context) and the three correlated binary features look like
components of a treatment regimen. The full regimen produces a large response only
when the biomarker is high; in biomarker-low patients, treatment provides essentially
no benefit. This is the textbook structure of a predictive biomarker-by-treatment
interaction.

Effect modification that was tested and found absent
----------------------------------------------------
- feature_051 (PS-like) effect did not differ across binary covariates (iter 6) or
  by feature_011 (iter 19): all interaction p > 0.13.
- feature_011 effect did not differ by histology (feature_043, p=0.79) or race
  (feature_123, all p > 0.40) — iter 7.
- Top predictors had similar adjusted ORs in adenocarcinoma vs squamous strata
  (iter 8): feature_013 OR 0.73 vs 0.77, feature_067 0.78 vs 0.75, feature_051
  0.69 vs 0.66, feature_011 0.96 vs 0.96. No meaningful effect modification by
  histology.
- Smoking status x histology interaction was null (all p > 0.80; iter 13).
- feature_013 x feature_067 interaction was null (p=0.51; iter 11) — they act
  multiplicatively.

Sociodemographic associations
-----------------------------
- Race (feature_123): unadjusted chi-square p=0.045 with rates 16.5-18.5% across the
  five categories, but after adjustment for clinical predictors all category effects
  attenuate to NS (all p > 0.13; iter 12). The marginal race difference is fully
  explained by clinical features.
- Insurance (feature_005): private vs medicaid OR=1.08 unadjusted (p=0.040),
  rising slightly to OR=1.09 after adjustment (p=0.019). A small disparity that
  persists after clinical adjustment (iter 20).
- Smoking status (feature_057): NS marginally (p=0.27) and in interactions (iter
  13). Histology (feature_043) NS marginally (p=0.078) and in interactions.

Additional independent predictors found in the adjusted screen
--------------------------------------------------------------
After adjusting for the top 9 predictors (and their interactions), two extra
binary features remain independently associated with the outcome:
- feature_076: adjusted OR=1.10, p=0.021. Modest positive effect.
- feature_112: adjusted OR=0.90, p=0.031. Modest negative effect.
Both effect sizes are small and would need replication.

Final synthesis model (iter 25)
-------------------------------
A 19-variable logistic regression including main effects (feature_051, feature_011,
feature_013, feature_067, feature_006, feature_007, feature_039, feature_092,
feature_099, feature_063, feature_076, feature_112, and insurance) plus the three
feature_006/007/039 x feature_092 interactions:
- Pseudo R^2 = 0.022, AIC = 44,467, log-likelihood = -22,214.
- Confirms all main effects (feature_051, feature_011, feature_013, feature_067,
  feature_099, feature_063, feature_076, feature_112, private-insurance) and all
  three biomarker*treatment interactions remain individually significant.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
Supported: H1.1-H1.5 (binary main effects), H3.1-H3.5 (continuous main effects),
H4.1-H4.2 (independent multivariable signals), H5.1-H5.3 (biomarker x treatment
interactions), H9.1 / H17.1 / H18.1 / H22.1 / H23.1 (the regimen-count x feature_092
interaction structure), H14.1 (feature_011 dose-response), H15.1-H15.2 (feature_076,
feature_112 in adjusted screen), H20.1 (insurance disparity), H21.1 (feature_099 dose
response with plateau), H24.1 (robustness of feature_076/112 in final model), H25.1
(final synthesis model).

Refuted: H2.2-H2.4 (no marginal histology / smoking / insurance effect at the chi-
square level for some), H6.1-H6.2 (no feature_051 effect modification), H7.1-H7.2
(no feature_011 effect modification by histology or race), H8.1 marginal (effects
similar across histology — note this is the supported direction), H11.1 (no
feature_013 x feature_067 synergy), H12.1 (race difference attenuated to null after
adjustment — supported in the sense that the refined hypothesis predicted attenuation),
H13.1 (no smoking x histology), H19.1 (no feature_051 x feature_011 interaction).

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. The strongest predictors of objective_response are feature_051 (likely a
   performance-status-like ordinal), feature_011 (likely tumor burden /
   comorbidity count), feature_013 and feature_067 (binary clinical features).
2. The three correlated binary features feature_006, feature_007, feature_039
   appear to be components of a treatment regimen; their joint presence
   (regimen_count=3) plus a high value of the biomarker feature_092 produces a
   response rate of 46% — far higher than any other patient subset. This is the
   clinical signal of a predictive biomarker-by-treatment interaction.
3. The biomarker (feature_092) is essentially uninformative on its own and is
   uninformative in patients not receiving the full regimen, but is a powerful
   predictor of response when the full regimen is given.
4. Race-related differences in objective_response are explained by clinical
   features. A modest insurance-related difference (private > medicaid)
   persists after clinical adjustment.
5. No major effect modification by histology, smoking, or sociodemographic
   variables was detected for any of the strongest predictors.
