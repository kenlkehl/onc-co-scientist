Analysis summary for ds001_nsclc (50,000 patients, anonymized features, outcome = objective_response, ORR = 16.9%)
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Across 25 propose-test-refine iterations, this dataset's signal structure resolved into
three clear classes of variables:

  (1) one treatment-regimen indicator that is the joint event (feature_006 = feature_007 =
      feature_039 = 1), called "triplet_111" below (prevalence 6.4%, n=3216);
  (2) a continuous biomarker, feature_092 (range 0-0.8), and a binary biomarker,
      feature_027 (prevalence 14.9%), that together govern *whether* triplet_111
      produces a response benefit; and
  (3) a small panel of independent prognostic covariates (feature_051, feature_013,
      feature_067, feature_011, feature_099, feature_063) with consistent main-effect
      signs and no meaningful interaction with treatment.

Race (feature_123), insurance type (feature_005), histology (feature_043), and
smoking status (feature_057) did not modify treatment effect or contribute meaningful
adjusted main effects.

ITERATION-BY-ITERATION NARRATIVE
--------------------------------
Iteration 1 - Univariate screen.  Of 122 features, the strongest marginal signals were
feature_051 (r=-0.092, p=8e-95), feature_013 (binary, ORR 0.196 vs 0.154, p=1e-33),
feature_011 (r=-0.053, p=1e-32), feature_067 (binary, 0.177 vs 0.144, p=2e-18),
feature_006 (binary, +1.8 pp, p=1e-6), feature_099 (r=+0.017, p=1.4e-4), feature_007
(+1.2 pp, p=6e-4), feature_063 (r=-0.015, p=7e-4), feature_092 (r=+0.013, p=3e-3),
feature_039 (+1.0 pp, p=3e-3).  Most other features were noise.

Iteration 2 - Multivariable adjustment.  All ten of the above retained independent
signals after mutual adjustment with race, insurance, histology, and smoking dummies
(all p<=0.02).  Race and insurance categorical dummies were not jointly significant.

Iteration 3 - feature_006 x feature_092 interaction.  Although feature_006 had a small
positive marginal effect, its interaction with feature_092 was strongly positive
(beta=+0.59, p=1e-6).  PD-L1-like effect was concentrated in feature_006=1: r=+0.047
(p=1.4e-8) versus r=-0.001 (p=0.79) in feature_006=0.

Iteration 4 - 3-way treatment interaction.  Cross-tabulating feature_006, feature_007,
and feature_039 revealed that ORR was elevated only when all three equaled 1 (22.97%
in cell 111; ~16-17% in the other seven cells).  The 3-way interaction term was
beta=+0.39, p=2e-4; pairwise interactions in the saturated model were not
significant.  This is consistent with the three binaries jointly tagging a single
multi-component treatment regimen (referred to here as "triplet_111").

Iteration 5 - PD-L1 x triplet_111.  Within triplet_111 the correlation between
feature_092 and ORR was r=+0.237 (p=2e-42); in every other cell |r|<0.03.
Dichotomizing PD-L1 at 0.50: triplet_111 + PD-L1>=0.5 -> ORR 44.8% (n=712);
triplet_111 + PD-L1<0.5 -> ORR 16.8% (n=2504).  Logistic interaction t111*high_pdl1
beta=+1.40 (p=3e-47).

Iteration 6 - feature_051 (PS-like).  Adjusted beta=-0.38 (p<1e-95), with monotonic
ORR gradient 0.197 -> 0.151 -> 0.108 across scores 0/1/2.  Treatment-effect
modification borderline (p=0.06) but independently very strong prognostic.

Iteration 7 - feature_013 (metastatic-like).  Adjusted beta=-0.31 (p<1e-34); ORR
0.196 vs 0.154.  Effect approximately consistent across all eight treatment cells;
interaction with triplet_111 borderline (p=0.06).  Prognostic, not predictive.

Iteration 8 - feature_067.  Adjusted beta=-0.26 (p<1e-18); ORR 0.177 vs 0.144,
independent of feature_013 and feature_051.

Iteration 9 - feature_011 (pack-years-like).  Adjusted beta=-0.0397 per unit
(p<1e-32); no triplet_111 interaction (p=0.72).

Iteration 10 - Albumin-like and CRP-like.  feature_099 adjusted beta=+0.092
(p=1e-4); feature_063 adjusted beta=-0.005 (p=4e-4).  Both consistent with serum-
chemistry prognostic markers.

Iteration 11 - Histology and smoking status.  Neither feature_043 nor feature_057
modified the triplet_111 effect (interaction p > 0.4 in each); marginal ORR
differences were small.

Iteration 12 - Demographic disparities.  Triplet_111 receipt was uniform across race
(5.8-6.6%) and insurance (6.1-6.7%).  ORR among triplet_111 recipients was uniform
across race (21.9-27.1%, all CIs overlap) and insurance.  No detectable disparity in
access or response.

Iteration 13 - PS and metastatic-status interaction with treatment.  Borderline
positive interactions on the OR scale (p~0.06 each); not strong enough to refute the
"prognostic only" interpretation.

Iteration 14 - Scan for treatment-effect modifiers.  Of all binary features, only
feature_027 had a strong interaction with triplet_111: beta=-0.87, p=1e-8.  Cross-
tab: triplet_111 + feature_027=0 ORR=24.8% (n=2751); triplet_111 + feature_027=1
ORR=12.5% (n=465); non-triplet_111 ORR ~16.5% regardless of feature_027.  In the
feature_027=1 stratum, triplet_111 was not just non-beneficial but modestly
associated with a 5.8 pp lower ORR than control (p=0.004).

Iteration 15 - 3-way triplet_111 x PD-L1 x feature_027.  The 4 strata partition the
benefit cleanly: triplet_111+PD-L1>=0.5+feature_027=0 ORR=48.9% (n=617);
triplet_111+PD-L1>=0.5+feature_027=1 ORR=17.9% (n=95);
triplet_111+PD-L1<0.5+feature_027=0 ORR=17.8% (n=2134);
triplet_111+PD-L1<0.5+feature_027=1 ORR=11.1% (n=370).  3-way interaction beta=
-0.91 (p=0.008) on top of the strong t111*high_pdl1 (+1.50) and t111*feature_027
(-0.58) terms.  feature_027 abolishes the IO biomarker benefit.

Iteration 16 - Targeted-therapy search in feature_027=1.  Within the n=7451
feature_027=1 cohort, no other single binary feature produced an ORR boost > +2.5 pp.
The dataset thus does not appear to encode a corresponding targeted-therapy
indicator that "rescues" feature_027-positive patients.

Iteration 17 - Lab-marker screen.  Only feature_099 (p=1.4e-4) and feature_063
(p=7e-4) were significant in marginal logistic regression.  All other 28 continuous
lab-like features had p>0.08.

Iteration 18 - Other treatment-like binaries.  feature_086, 115, 002, 037, 053, 093
all have univariate p>0.05 against ORR and lose any signal in adjusted models.  No
4-way or alternative multi-component treatment combination is detectable.

Iteration 19 - PD-L1 threshold shape.  Within triplet_111, ORR by PD-L1 quintile is
17.2/17.4/15.2/18.0/47.0%.  The benefit is essentially confined to the top quintile
(cutpoint near 0.5), suggesting a clinically actionable threshold rather than a
linear gradient.

Iteration 20 - Final multivariable model.  A logistic regression with triplet_111, its
interactions with feature_092 and feature_027, and the prognostic covariates
feature_051, 013, 067, 011, 099, 063 yields all key terms significant (treatment-
biomarker interactions p<0.011; prognostic main effects p<1e-4 to p<1e-95) with
pseudo R^2 = 0.026.  The treatment-effect estimate translates to an absolute ORR
difference of +33 pp in the canonical responder stratum.

Iteration 21 - Stratified treatment-effect quantification.  feature_027=0 + high
PD-L1: t111 vs control diff=+32.6 pp (p=1e-89).  feature_027=0 + low PD-L1: +1.3 pp
(p=0.12).  feature_027=1 + low PD-L1: -5.8 pp (p=0.004).  feature_027=1 + high
PD-L1: +1.4 pp (p=0.83).

Iteration 22 - 3-way absorbs pairwise.  Each 2-way interaction among feature_006/007
/039 is individually significant (p<0.005) but loses significance once the 3-way
term is added.  The 3-way interaction is the parsimonious description of treatment-
indicator structure.

Iteration 23 - Robustness in feature_027=0 majority.  Within the n=42549 feature_027=0
cohort, the t111*PD-L1 interaction remains beta~+1.62, p<1e-50, and the canonical
high-responder cell preserves ORR ~49%.  The biomarker-driven benefit is robust to
mutation-positive removal.

Iteration 24 - Treatment cells balanced on covariates.  Triplet_111 vs non-triplet_111
have very similar means/prevalences for feature_051, 013, 067, 011, 099, 092, and
feature_027.  Suggests this is a balanced cohort where treatment-effect estimates
are unlikely to be heavily confounded by measured patient mix.

Iteration 25 - Synthesis.  The data-generating story is parsimonious:
triplet_111 is a treatment regimen that confers a large response benefit (~+33 pp
ORR) only in patients with high feature_092 (PD-L1 >=0.5) and absence of feature_027
(a putative driver/resistance marker).  In all other strata, triplet_111 is at best
neutral and may slightly harm feature_027-positive low-PD-L1 patients.  Independent
prognostic effects exist for performance-status-like (feature_051), metastatic-burden
-like (feature_013), feature_067, pack-years-like (feature_011), albumin-like
(feature_099), and CRP-like (feature_063), with consistent signs.  Histology,
smoking status, race, and insurance do not modify treatment effect or contribute
substantively to ORR after adjustment.

SUPPORTED HYPOTHESES
--------------------
- Univariate signals exist for >=10 features (h1.1).
- feature_051 inversely associated with ORR, dose-response (h1.2, h6.1).
- feature_006 marks treatment whose effect is enhanced by feature_092 (h3.1, h3.2).
- feature_006/007/039 jointly identify a regimen via 3-way interaction (h4.1, h4.2,
  h22.1).
- PD-L1 effect is concentrated in triplet_111, with apparent threshold near 0.5
  (h5.1, h5.2, h19.1).
- feature_013, feature_067, feature_011, feature_099, feature_063 are independent
  prognostic factors (h7.1, h8.1, h9.1, h10.1, h10.2, h17.1).
- feature_027 is a strong predictive biomarker that abolishes the IO benefit (h14.1,
  h14.2, h15.1, h15.2).
- Treatment effect is concentrated in feature_027=0 + high PD-L1 stratum (h21.1).
- Final model captures the dominant signal (h20.1, h23.1, h24.1, h25.1).

REFUTED OR NULL HYPOTHESES
--------------------------
- Race and insurance do not modify treatment effect or have independent effects
  after adjustment (h2.2, h12.1, h12.2).
- Histology and smoking status do not modify treatment effect (h11.1, h11.2).
- Performance status and feature_013 only borderline modify treatment effect on the
  OR scale (h13.1, h13.2 - effectively null).
- No targeted-therapy-like indicator rescues feature_027=1 patients (h16.1).
- No additional treatment-like binary or multi-component regimen exists beyond the
  feature_006/007/039 triplet (h18.1).

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
This cohort behaves like a NSCLC treatment outcomes dataset whose central
clinical-trial-like signal is a chemoimmunotherapy (or pembrolizumab-based) regimen
benefit that is biomarker-defined: large in PD-L1-high, mutation/resistance-marker-
negative patients, and absent (or harmful) elsewhere.  The remaining ORR variation
is explained by a small set of established prognostic factors (performance status,
disease-burden indicator, smoking exposure, albumin, inflammation-marker).
Demographic and histologic features do not contribute meaningfully.  The strongest,
most actionable inference is the triplet_111 x PD-L1 x feature_027 stratification,
which selects a high-benefit subgroup (~10% of the cohort, ORR ~49%) from a
population whose otherwise-uniform ORR is ~16-17%.
