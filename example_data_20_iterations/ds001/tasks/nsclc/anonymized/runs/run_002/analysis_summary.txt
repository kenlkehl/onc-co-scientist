Analysis summary - ds001_nsclc (50,000 patients, anonymized features feature_001..feature_123, outcome objective_response with overall positive rate 16.89%)

Across 14 iterations I tested 30 hypotheses spanning main effects (binary, categorical, ordinal, and continuous predictors), demographic and SES contrasts, and 2- and 3-way interactions.

1. Strongest predictor: feature_051 (ordinal 0/1/2, prevalences 35%/50%/15%). Response rate falls monotonically 21.08% -> 15.70% -> 11.05% across the three levels. Logistic per-unit OR = 0.687 (coef -0.375, p ~ 3e-93). This is by far the largest signed effect in the cohort and is consistent with a performance-status-like clinical severity ladder.

2. Continuous biomarker feature_011 (median 3.0, 0-28 range) shows a strong inverse association with response: Q1 18.5% -> Q4 14.2%, logit coef -0.0385 per unit (p ~ 3e-35). Effect remains essentially unchanged across feature_013 strata (no interaction; iter 13).

3. Three additional clinical-biomarker continuous variables behave consistently with their univariate direction in adjusted models: feature_063 (right-skewed; negative, log-transform coef -0.071, p~4e-4); feature_099 (mean 3.8 around 3.5-4.1; positive, p~1e-4 univariate, p<0.001 adjusted); feature_092 (positive, p~4e-3 univariate, p~0.002 adjusted).

4. Of 77 binary features screened, only five reached p < 0.01 against objective_response: feature_013 (negative, RR1=15.40% vs RR0=19.64%, p=1.1e-33), feature_067 (negative, 14.36% vs 17.75%, p=2.0e-18), feature_006 (positive, 18.19% vs 16.38%, p=1.0e-6), feature_007 (positive, 17.54% vs 16.37%, p=5.9e-4), and feature_039 (positive, 17.39% vs 16.40%, p=3.4e-3). All five retained statistical significance in the full multivariable model with similar magnitudes, confirming that they capture independent signal.

5. Demographics and SES.
- Race (feature_123): unadjusted chi-square across 5 levels p=0.045. White patients had the lowest response rate (16.55%) versus 17.15-18.54% for non-white groups; white-vs-non-white logit coef = -0.070 (p=0.005). After adjustment for feature_051, feature_011, and feature_013, the coefficient attenuated only modestly to -0.065 (p=0.010), and in the full multivariable model none of the four race contrasts reached p<0.05 (p range 0.13-0.94). The unadjusted association is therefore real but small (~1 percentage point) and largely robust to clinical adjustment, with point estimates losing significance only when many additional covariates are included.
- Insurance (feature_005): no overall association (chi-square p=0.23); medicaid-vs-others marginal (p=0.066). In the multivariable model, only private vs medicaid reached p<0.05 (coef +0.089, p=0.019), with medicare and uninsured indistinguishable from medicaid.
- Smoking (feature_057): no overall association (p=0.27); response 17.23% current / 16.85% former / 16.38% never. Smoking does not modify the effect of feature_006 (3-way interaction iter 13, all p>0.34) nor of feature_007 (iter 12, p=0.23).
- Histology (feature_043): adenocarcinoma 16.71% vs squamous 17.37%, p=0.078 unadjusted; non-significant after adjustment. No detectable interaction with feature_006 (p=0.62) or feature_007 (p=0.57).
- Age (feature_078, range 30-90): no association (mean 65.0 in both responders and non-responders, p=0.78).

6. Interaction structure. I explicitly tested ten 2-way and 3-way interactions among the strongest features (feature_051 x feature_011, feature_013 x feature_051, feature_006 x feature_043, feature_006 x feature_051, feature_007 x feature_057, feature_007 x feature_043, feature_011 x feature_013, plus the 3-way feature_006 x feature_043 x feature_057). All p > 0.23. Within strata of feature_051, the favorable effect of feature_006 was directionally consistent (+1.6 / +2.1 / +1.6 percentage points across levels 0/1/2). The data are well described as additive on the log-odds scale.

7. Final multivariable summary. Adjusted log-odds (95% CI) for objective_response, holding all other tested covariates fixed:
- feature_051: -0.382 per unit (OR 0.683)
- feature_013: -0.302 (OR 0.739)
- feature_067: -0.259 (OR 0.772)
- feature_011: -0.039 per unit (OR 0.961)
- feature_063: -0.005 per unit
- feature_006: +0.130 (OR 1.139)
- feature_092: +0.174 (OR 1.189)
- feature_099: +0.093 (OR 1.097)
- feature_007: +0.081 (OR 1.084)
- feature_039: +0.077 (OR 1.080)
Race, smoking, histology, and age all non-significant in this adjusted model. Pseudo-R^2 = 0.020 (substantial residual variability is unexplained by the measured features).

Hypotheses supported: h1, h2, h3, h4, h5, h6, h7, h8, h9 (direction), h14, h15, h16, h17, h18, h22, h23, h24, h28, h29, h30.
Hypotheses refuted or not supported: h10 (medicaid not significant), h11 (smoking), h12 (histology), h13 (age), h19, h20, h21, h25, h26, h27 (no interactions).

Overall conclusion. Objective response in this NSCLC cohort is driven primarily by an ordinal severity-like feature (feature_051), a continuous biomarker (feature_011), and a small set of binary clinical/biomarker indicators acting independently and additively. Apparent demographic disparities (white vs non-white, medicaid vs private) are real but small at the unadjusted level and largely subsumed by clinical case-mix. No interaction or subgroup heterogeneity reached statistical significance among the variables tested.
