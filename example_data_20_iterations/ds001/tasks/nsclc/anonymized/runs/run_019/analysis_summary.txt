Oncology dataset ds001_nsclc — analysis summary
===================================================

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patients with binary outcome `objective_response`
(8,447 responders / 41,553 non-responders; baseline ORR 16.9%).
Predictors: 122 de-identified features comprising 77 binary, 6 ordinal-int,
4 categorical-string (smoking feature_057, histology feature_043, race
feature_123, insurance feature_005), and 36 continuous variables.

The protocol explored 25 iterations of propose-test-refine, beginning with
broad univariate screens and progressing through multivariable models, pairwise
and higher-order interactions, biomarker-by-treatment dissection, subgroup
analyses, and sensitivity checks. The full set of statistical tests is recorded
in `transcript.json`; this narrative summarizes the principal findings.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Independent prognostic predictors (main effects)
------------------------------------------------------------

Six covariates show strong, independent, additive associations with response
that survive every multivariable model considered:

  * feature_051 (3-level ordinal 0/1/2): NEGATIVE.  ORR 21.1%, 15.7%, 11.1%
    across levels 0/1/2.  Per-SD logistic beta = -0.26 (OR 0.77), p=1.5e-95.
    The ordinal step-down pattern is consistent with ECOG performance status
    or another ordered prognostic scale.

  * feature_013 (binary, 35% prevalence of "0"): "1" lowers response.
    ORR 15.4% vs 19.6% (logOR = -0.30, OR 0.74), p = 2.8e-35.

  * feature_011 (continuous, 28% values=0, range 0-28): NEGATIVE.
    Per-SD beta = -0.15 (OR 0.86), p = 2.6e-33. Approximately log-linear.

  * feature_067 (binary, 25% positive): NEGATIVE.
    ORR 14.4% vs 17.7% (logOR = -0.25, OR 0.77), p = 5.1e-19.

  * feature_099 (continuous, range 1.7-5.5): POSITIVE (small).
    Per-SD OR 1.05, p = 9.2e-05.

  * feature_063 (continuous): NEGATIVE (small). Per-SD OR 0.95, p = 2.8e-04.

The four negative predictors (feature_051, feature_011, feature_013,
feature_067) are mutually independent (pairwise correlation < 0.01) and
contribute additively to the log-odds. Their effects do NOT depend on
treatment receipt (interaction tests with the triple combination all p > 0.3),
so they behave as general prognostic factors rather than treatment-modifying
markers.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. The headline finding: biomarker-driven triple-combination effect
------------------------------------------------------------

The dominant treatment-effect signal in the data is a four-way interaction:

  feature_006  ×  feature_007  ×  feature_039  ×  feature_092 ≥ 0.50.

* feature_092 is a continuous variable (range 0–0.80, 28% values near 0)
  that behaves like a clinical predictive biomarker. By itself, in patients
  not receiving the triplet, it has negligible prognostic effect (per-SD
  beta = +0.01, p = 0.27).

* feature_006, feature_007, and feature_039 are mutually independent binary
  variables (pairwise phi-correlation < 0.01) with prevalences 28.5%, 44.7%,
  and 50.0% respectively. As single agents each carries a tiny univariate
  benefit (logOR ≈ +0.07–0.13). In a multivariable model that includes the
  triple-combo × biomarker interaction term, all three single-agent main
  effects shrink to non-significant.

* The triple combination (all three = 1) is uncommon (n = 3,216 / 50,000 ≈
  6.4%) and is allocated independently of biomarker status, prognostic
  features, race, insurance, and age (all p > 0.10 — no confounding by
  indication or selection bias).

Cross-tabulation of objective response by treatment combination and biomarker
status:

                              biomarker_low (<0.50)   biomarker_high (>=0.50)
                              -------------------     -----------------------
  no triple combo               16.5%  (n=36,747)          16.3%  (n=10,037)
  triple combo (006+007+039)    16.8%  (n=2,504)           44.8%  (n=712)

The 4-way interaction term is highly significant: coefficient = +1.00,
LR p = 6.5e-05. Equivalently, in a logistic model adjusted for prognostic
factors, the (triple × biomarker_high) odds ratio is 4.17 (95% CI 3.45–5.05),
p = 1.3e-47.

Single agents and pairwise combinations of {006, 007, 039} give little or no
benefit even within the biomarker-high stratum (ORR 15–19%); only the full
triplet produces the dramatic ORR jump, and only when the biomarker is high.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Biomarker threshold and dose-response
------------------------------------------------------------

Sensitivity analysis across feature_092 thresholds shows a clean
dose-response. The (triple × biomarker_high) interaction OR rises
monotonically with threshold:

  thresh ≥ 0.10  →  OR 1.62, p = 2.9e-05   (ORR_triple - ORR_no = +8 pts)
  thresh ≥ 0.30  →  OR 2.15, p = 2.1e-17   (+13 pts)
  thresh ≥ 0.50  →  OR 4.08, p = 2.9e-47   (+29 pts)
  thresh ≥ 0.60  →  OR 12.15, p = 1.9e-77  (+55 pts; ORR rises to ~71%)
  thresh ≥ 0.70  →  OR 8.68, p = 2.5e-19   (n=1,380; ARR ~54 pts)

The clinically meaningful cutoff appears to lie between 0.40 and 0.60, with
the strongest discrimination near 0.50–0.60. The pattern is consistent with
a continuous predictive biomarker (analogous to PD-L1 expression) coupled to
a triplet immunotherapy/chemo regimen.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Subgroup robustness of the treatment-biomarker effect
------------------------------------------------------------

We screened all 77 binary, 36 continuous, and 6 ordinal/categorical features
for 3-way interactions with (triple × biomarker_high). After Bonferroni
correction:

  * No binary feature significantly modifies the effect (best p = 0.006 for
    feature_027; would need p < 6.5e-04 to pass correction).
  * Only feature_092 itself reaches significance among continuous covariates
    (trivially, since the threshold is derived from it).
  * The 3-way LR test for histology × biomarker × treatment was non-significant
    for all three components (p > 0.24); benefit holds in adenocarcinoma and
    squamous alike.
  * Smoking status, age, feature_051 (PS surrogate), and the negative
    prognostic features (013, 067, 011) do NOT meaningfully change the
    effect (all 3-way p > 0.04, none surviving correction).

In the triple-combo + biomarker-high cell (n=712, ORR ~45%), ORR is
nearly identical across age quartiles (43–48%), feature_051 levels (43–47%),
adenocarcinoma vs squamous (44% vs 45%), and current/former smokers (~47%/43%);
never-smokers had a numerically lower ORR (38%, n=34) but the subgroup is too
small for inference. The triple-combo benefit therefore appears to apply
uniformly to all biomarker-high patients.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Demographic comparisons
------------------------------------------------------------

* Race/ethnicity (feature_123): unadjusted ORR varies modestly (white 16.5%,
  hispanic 17.2%, black 17.8%, asian 17.7%, other 18.5%; chi-square p = 0.045).
  After full adjustment for clinical covariates and treatment, no race
  category retains independent association (white p = 0.13, all others
  p > 0.49). Triple-combo receipt is balanced across race (p = 0.83), and
  biomarker-high prevalence is balanced (p = 0.47). Within the triple +
  biomarker-high cell, ORR by race ranges 36–49% — non-significantly
  different (3-way interaction p = 0.08, "other" subgroup n=11).

* Insurance (feature_005): no significant unadjusted difference (p = 0.23);
  in adjusted model, private vs medicaid OR = 1.09 (p = 0.04) — a small,
  marginal effect. Treatment receipt is balanced (p = 0.10).

* Smoking status (feature_057): no association with ORR univariately
  (p = 0.27) and not retained after adjustment.

* Histology (feature_043): borderline univariate p = 0.078 (squamous
  slightly higher); not significant after adjustment.

In short, demographic differences in raw response rates are explained by
case-mix and do not reflect independent demographic effects on response.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. What the data are NOT supporting
------------------------------------------------------------

Hypotheses that the data REFUTED (or did not support):

  * Smoking status modifies treatment effect — refuted (interaction p > 0.13).
  * Histology modifies the biomarker-treatment effect — refuted.
  * Worse PS (feature_051) attenuates triple-combo benefit — refuted (if
    anything, weak opposite trend).
  * Negative prognostic features attenuate triple-combo benefit — refuted.
  * Disparity in receipt of the triple combination by race or insurance —
    refuted.
  * Race independently lowers response after adjustment — refuted.
  * Other binary features (besides 006/007/039) interact with feature_092
    after correction — refuted.
  * feature_092 alone is a strong PROGNOSTIC marker — refuted (it is
    primarily PREDICTIVE).

------------------------------------------------------------
7. Clinical interpretation and NNT
------------------------------------------------------------

Among biomarker-high patients (feature_092 ≥ 0.50; 21.5% of cohort), the
absolute risk increase for objective response with the triple combination is
+28.5 percentage points (44.8% vs 16.3%). The number needed to treat is
therefore ≈ 3.5 to gain one additional response. Among biomarker-low patients
the absolute increase is ≈ 0.3 percentage points and the triplet provides no
clinically meaningful benefit (NNT ≈ 333).

This pattern is the classic one-two punch of a clinically actionable
predictive biomarker: it identifies a sub-population in which a specific
combination regimen produces dramatic, isolated benefit, and it spares
biomarker-negative patients from a treatment that would not help them.

------------------------------------------------------------
9. Bottom line
------------------------------------------------------------

The cohort exhibits a textbook predictive-biomarker signal: a continuous
biomarker (feature_092) with a clinically meaningful threshold (~0.5)
identifies the ~21% of patients in whom a specific triple combination
(feature_006 + feature_007 + feature_039) produces a dramatic increase in
objective response (ORR 45% vs 16% baseline; NNT 3.5). Single agents and
pairwise combinations of these treatments produce no meaningful benefit, and
the triplet produces no benefit in biomarker-low patients. Independent
prognostic features (feature_051, feature_011, feature_013, feature_067)
each modestly lower response across all strata, and demographic factors
(race, insurance, smoking, histology) show no robust independent effect
after adjustment for clinical covariates and treatment.
