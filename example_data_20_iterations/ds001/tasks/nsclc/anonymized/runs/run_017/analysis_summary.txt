ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (50,000 patients, objective_response binary outcome, baseline ORR 16.89%)
================================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The cohort contains 124 de-identified features (77 binary, 7 ordinal 0-4 or 0-10, 35 continuous, 4
categorical strings: smoking, histology, race, insurance) and one binary outcome (objective_response).
Across 10 iterations I screened every feature for an association with response, built a multivariable
logistic regression, probed pairwise and three-way interactions among the strongest predictors, tested
demographic disparities, and assessed model calibration / discrimination.

PRIMARY PROGNOSTIC FEATURES (large independent effects)
-------------------------------------------------------
Two features behave like canonical clinical prognostic markers:

* feature_051 (ordinal 0/1/2, mean 0.80) — almost certainly an ECOG-style performance status. ORR
  drops monotonically from 21.1% (level 0) → 15.7% (level 1) → 11.0% (level 2). Univariate logistic
  coefficient -0.375 per unit (p=1.7e-93). After full multivariable adjustment, OR = 0.77 per SD
  (p<1e-30). Largest single effect in the dataset.
* feature_011 (continuous 0-28, right-skewed, plausibly a tumor-burden / sum-of-diameters proxy) —
  ORR drops from 18.9% in the lowest quintile to 13.7% in the highest. Univariate logistic coefficient
  -0.039 per unit (p=1.8e-32). Adjusted OR = 0.86 per SD (p<1e-30).

Both effects survive every adjustment I tested. The two features act ADDITIVELY on the log-odds
scale (no interaction, p=0.50), and the PS effect does not differ by histology (p=0.22). These are
the "performance status / disease burden" axis of the prognostic story.

BINARY BIOMARKER SIGNALS
------------------------
Five binary features carry independent associations with response after multivariable adjustment;
they cluster into two opposite-direction groups:

NEGATIVE (response-suppressing), additive on log-odds:
  * feature_013 (prev 64.7%): adjusted OR 0.74 (p<1e-30). Univariate ORR 15.4% vs 19.6%.
  * feature_067 (prev 25.2%): adjusted OR 0.77 (p<1e-30). Univariate ORR 14.4% vs 17.8%.
  Joint stratification: both 0 → ORR 20.7%; both 1 → ORR 13.2%. Pairwise interaction OR 1.04
  (p=0.51) — the two markers act independently.

POSITIVE (response-enhancing), with three-way SYNERGY:
  * feature_006 (prev 28.5%): univariate OR 1.14 (p=1.0e-6).
  * feature_007 (prev 44.7%): univariate OR 1.09 (p=5.9e-4).
  * feature_039 (prev 50.0%): univariate OR 1.07 (p=3.4e-3).

The most striking interaction in the dataset is among these three. ORR by sum:
    0/3 trio = 16.1% (n=9,882)   1/3 = 16.4%   2/3 = 16.8%   3/3 = 23.0% (n=3,216)
A model with all main effects, all pairwise products, and the THREE-WAY product reveals that
once the f006*f007*f039 term is included (OR 1.48, p=2e-4) the pairwise and main-effect terms
all drop to non-significance — a textbook signature of pure third-order synergy. The synergy
persists after full adjustment for prognostic variables (adjusted OR 1.45, p=2.7e-11). The
absolute boost from being trio-positive is uniform across PS strata (~6-8 percentage points
in PS=0, 1, and 2), suggesting the interaction acts on the absolute-risk scale.

The pairwise interactions that initially looked significant in iteration 5 (006x039 p=4e-4,
007x039 p=2e-3, 006x007 p=4e-3) were entirely artifacts of the underlying three-way structure;
each pairwise product captures part of the conditional dependence implied by the synergy.

OTHER CONTINUOUS PREDICTORS (small but significant)
---------------------------------------------------
* feature_063 (range 0.03-296.88, right-skewed; plausibly a serum biomarker like LDH): adjusted
  per-SD OR 0.95 (p=5e-4). Higher → lower ORR.
* feature_099 (range 1.7-5.5): adjusted per-SD OR 1.05 (p=1e-4). Higher → higher ORR.
* feature_092 (range 0-0.798, fractional): adjusted per-SD OR 1.04 (p=2e-3). Higher → higher ORR.

DEMOGRAPHIC, HISTOLOGIC, AND LIFESTYLE FEATURES
-----------------------------------------------
Most of the "expected" demographic axes are NOT prognostic in this cohort:

* feature_078 (age, 30-90, mean 65): ORR is flat across age quartiles (~16.5-17.5%); logistic
  p=0.78 univariate and 0.78 adjusted. NO age effect.
* feature_057 (smoking — current/former/never): chi-square p=0.27. ORR 17.2% / 16.9% / 16.4%.
  Not significant; smoking does not predict response in this cohort.
* feature_043 (histology — adenocarcinoma/squamous): chi-square p=0.078. Squamous ORR 17.4%
  vs adenocarcinoma 16.7%. Direction tilts toward squamous but does not reach significance,
  and disappears entirely after adjustment.

The smoking-x-histology cross-tab confirms expected epidemiology (only 28 of 1,000 squamous
patients are never-smokers vs 196 of 1,000 adenocarcinoma patients), but neither smoking nor
histology nor their interaction modifies response: never-smoker squamous ORR (16.4%) equals
never-smoker adenocarcinoma ORR (16.4%); interaction p=0.77.

DEMOGRAPHIC DISPARITIES
-----------------------
Two small but statistically significant demographic signals emerged:

* feature_123 (race): overall chi-square p=0.045. Pairwise vs white, only Black-vs-white was
  significant: black ORR 17.76% vs white ORR 16.55% (Δ=+0.012, z-test p=0.023). After
  multivariable adjustment for prognostic features, the Black indicator retained an OR of
  1.085 (p=0.029) — a small ORR advantage rather than the typical disparity direction. Asian,
  Hispanic, and "other" categories were not significantly different from white.
* feature_005 (insurance): overall chi-square p=0.232. Medicaid ORR 16.13% vs private 17.20%
  (Δ=-0.011, p=0.037). Adjusted OR for Medicaid 0.915 (p=0.019). Medicare and uninsured were
  not significantly different from private.

Importantly, the negative association of feature_013 with ORR is consistent across racial
strata (white -0.044, black -0.047, hispanic -0.036, asian -0.023, other -0.058; interaction
p=0.32). There is no Black-x-Medicaid interaction (p=0.91), so the two demographic signals act
independently rather than compounding.

NULL / REFUTED SIGNALS
----------------------
* Six ordinal 0-4 / 0-10 features (018, 026, 033, 042, 045, 064) — none had a meaningful
  monotonic ORR association; ORR varies by <2 ppts across levels of each. feature_033 had a
  marginal univariate p=0.04 but a tiny coef (-0.022) and disappeared in multivariable models.
* Thirteen rare binary features (prevalence <2%) — smallest p was 0.116. None contribute
  detectable signal to response.
* Age, smoking, histology, the binary features 074/076/100/112 (no effect after adjustment).

MODEL PERFORMANCE
-----------------
The final multivariable logistic model (intercept + standardized continuous predictors
[078, 011, 063, 099, 092, 051] + binary predictors [013, 067, 006, 007, 039] + the 006*007*039
trio interaction; race and insurance dropped from the prediction-focused model) achieves:
    AIC = 44,505     pseudo-R^2 = 0.021     rank AUC = 0.604
    LLR p < 1e-180

Although the AUC is modest (typical for response prediction in heterogeneous oncology cohorts),
the model produces meaningful absolute-risk stratification. ORR by predicted-risk decile rises
monotonically from 9.3% in decile 1 to 26.2% in decile 10, a 2.8x ratio between extremes
(z=22.7, p<<1e-30). This indicates the predictors capture clinically relevant heterogeneity
in response probability, not merely statistical noise.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. Performance status (feature_051) and tumor-burden proxy (feature_011) are the dominant
   prognostic axes; both are independent and additive on log-odds.
2. Two binary biomarkers (feature_013, feature_067) are independent NEGATIVE predictors;
   they act additively, not synergistically.
3. Three binary features (feature_006, feature_007, feature_039) form a SYNERGISTIC POSITIVE
   triple — the three-way product is the key driver, with pairwise interactions appearing only
   as shadows of the deeper structure. Patients with all three positive show ORR ~23% vs ~16%
   in any other combination; the absolute boost is approximately uniform across PS levels.
4. Three continuous biomarkers (063, 099, 092) contribute small independent effects that are
   biologically plausible (one inversely, two positively) and survive adjustment.
5. Age, smoking status, histology have no detectable effect on response in this cohort.
6. Demographic disparities are SMALL but real: Black race confers a small ORR advantage and
   Medicaid insurance a small ORR disadvantage, both surviving prognostic adjustment with
   p ~ 0.02-0.03; the two effects are independent (no compounding interaction).
7. Most ordinal subscale features and rare-event binary features carry no detectable response
   signal in this cohort.
