ds001_nsclc — Analysis Summary
================================

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patients with binary objective_response (overall RR = 16.89%, n = 8,447 responders).

Across 25 iterations, the analysis moved from broad univariate scans of all 122 features through multivariable adjustment, into stratified/interaction analyses focused on subgroup heterogeneity. Below I synthesize the hypotheses explored, the statistical evidence, and the overall picture.

1. UNIVARIATE LANDSCAPE (iterations 1-4)
----------------------------------------

Among 77 binary features, two stood out by univariate chi-square: feature_013 (RR 15.40% when=1 vs 19.64% when=0, p=1.1e-33) and feature_067 (RR 14.36% vs 17.75%, p=2.0e-18). Both were associated with LOWER response — clinically suggestive of advanced-disease / disease-burden indicators.

Among 35 continuous features, the strongest signals were feature_011 (continuous 0-28; OR 0.962/unit, p=1.8e-32), feature_099 (range 1.7-5.5, albumin-like; OR 1.10, p=1.4e-04), and feature_063 (right-skewed lab; OR 0.995, p=6.9e-04). feature_092 (range 0-0.798, PD-L1-TPS-like) had only a modest main effect (p=3e-3) — but this turned out to be the single most important predictive biomarker once interactions were tested (see below).

Among multi-level integers, feature_051 (3 levels: 0, 1, 2) emerged as the SINGLE STRONGEST PREDICTOR in the dataset, with monotonically declining response across levels (RR 21.08% / 15.70% / 11.05%; logit coef -0.375 per level, p=1.7e-93). Its profile (3 ordered levels, very strong negative association, large n in each level) is consistent with an ECOG performance-status proxy.

Among the four categorical (string) features, only feature_123 (race) reached nominal significance (chi-square p=0.045), with white patients having the lowest crude RR (16.55% vs 17.2-18.5% in other groups). Smoking (feature_057) and insurance (feature_005) showed no significant overall heterogeneity. Histology (feature_043: adenocarcinoma vs squamous) was borderline (p=0.078) and not significant after adjustment.

2. MULTIVARIABLE INDEPENDENT PREDICTORS (iteration 5)
-----------------------------------------------------

A logistic regression with all 129 predictors (including one-hot encoded categoricals) identified ten independent predictors at p<0.01: feature_051 (p=8e-96), feature_013 (p=1e-34), feature_011 (p=2e-33), feature_067 (p=3e-19), feature_006 (p=2e-6), feature_099 (p=1e-4), feature_063 (p=5e-4), feature_007 (p=9e-4), feature_039 (p=1e-3), feature_092 (p=2e-3). Of these, the directionally NEGATIVE prognostic markers (feature_051, feature_013, feature_011, feature_067, feature_063) point to disease-burden / poor-prognosis phenotypes; the POSITIVE markers (feature_006, feature_092, feature_099, feature_007, feature_039) point to favorable disease biology or active treatment.

3. THE feature_006 TREATMENT-LIKE EFFECT (iterations 6-11)
----------------------------------------------------------

feature_006 (binary, prevalence 28.5%) had a small but highly significant main effect: RR 18.19% when=1 vs 16.38% when=0 (delta +1.82 pp, OR 1.14, p=1e-6). The modest size of the main effect raised the question of whether feature_006 acts predominantly through interaction with a marker — the classic immunotherapy story.

Testing feature_006 x feature_092 interaction: coefficient +0.593, p=1.2e-06. Stratification confirmed the pattern is essentially a textbook predictive-biomarker:
  - Within feature_006 = 0 (no treatment-like): feature_092 has zero relationship with response (coef -0.018, p=0.79).
  - Within feature_006 = 1 (treatment-like): feature_092 is strongly positively associated (coef +0.576, p=1.5e-08).
  - Three-level binning (mimicking PD-L1 TPS <1% / 1-49% / >=50%): in feature_006 recipients, RRs are 18.0% / 16.8% / 22.5%; in non-recipients, RRs are flat at ~16.5%.
This is the signature pattern of an immune checkpoint inhibitor whose benefit is concentrated in PD-L1-high tumors. feature_006 is, with high probability, an indicator of immune checkpoint inhibitor receipt; feature_092 is the corresponding PD-L1 expression marker.

A surprising further structure emerged: feature_007 (prevalence 44.7%) and feature_039 (prevalence 50.0%) — neither of which is correlated with feature_006 (phi ~ 0) — also had small main effects (each ~+1 pp on RR, p~0.001) that turned out to be CONFINED TO feature_006 = 1 patients:
  - Within feature_006=0, feature_007 effect coef = +0.034 (p=0.23); feature_039 coef = +0.023 (p=0.42).
  - Within feature_006=1, feature_007 = +0.191 (p=1.5e-05); feature_039 = +0.196 (p=8.8e-06).
  - Pairwise interaction tests: feature_007 x feature_006 p=0.0043; feature_039 x feature_006 p=4.1e-04.

The structure further deepens when stratified jointly by feature_092: within feature_006=1 AND feature_092 high (>=0.5), the response rate by feature_007 is 15.8% vs 30.7% (n=1731 vs 1419); and the four-way cell {feature_092 >= 0.5, feature_006 = 1, feature_007 = 1, feature_039 = 1} has RR = 44.80% (n=712) — vs <19% in every other cell of the 4D table. The triple interaction feature_006 x feature_007 x feature_039 is significant (coef +0.421, p=6.9e-05) after adjusting for main effects and lower-order interactions. feature_007 and feature_039 thus appear to be additional treatment / regimen indicators (e.g., chemotherapy backbone or specific regimen flags) that, in combination with the immunotherapy-like feature_006 and PD-L1-high feature_092, identify a small but well-defined ~1.4% super-responder phenotype.

4. PROGNOSTIC vs PREDICTIVE MARKERS (iterations 12-13, 19-23)
------------------------------------------------------------

Several predictors are unambiguously PROGNOSTIC (i.e., associated with response regardless of feature_006 status):
  - feature_051 (performance-status-like): identical effect in both feature_006 strata (level 0 -> 1 -> 2 RRs: 20.6/15.1/10.6 if f006=0, 22.2/17.2/12.2 if f006=1). Interaction with f006 p=0.31.
  - feature_013: independent OR 0.74 (p=1e-34); f013 x f006 interaction p=0.15 (n.s.).
  - feature_067: independent OR 0.77 (p=3e-19).
  - feature_011: identical slope in both f006 strata; interaction p=0.13.
  - feature_099 (albumin-like): prognostic in f006=0 (p=2e-4) but null in f006=1 (p=0.21) — possibly because performance/burden dominates in untreated patients while treatment markers dominate in treated patients.
  - feature_063: independent prognostic effect (p=5e-4); no f006 interaction.

PREDICTIVE markers (i.e., effect ONLY in feature_006=1):
  - feature_092 (PD-L1-like): null in f006=0; large positive effect in f006=1.
  - feature_007 and feature_039: null in f006=0; positive effects in f006=1, with strong joint dependence on feature_092.

5. NULL FINDINGS (iterations 14-17)
-----------------------------------

Several pre-specified clinical hypotheses were not supported:
  - Age (feature_078): no main effect on response (p=0.67) and no age-by-feature_006 interaction (p=0.75). Treatment-like benefit is similar across age strata (<50, 50-60, 60-70, 70-80, 80+).
  - Histology (feature_043): no significant main effect (p=0.15 adjusted) and no histology-by-feature_006 interaction (p=0.62).
  - Smoking (feature_057): no main effect (p=0.27) and no smoking-by-feature_006 interaction (current x f006 p=0.59; never x f006 p=0.46). Treatment-like benefit is uniform across smoking strata, including in never-smokers (delta +2.8 pp).
  - Race (feature_123): borderline univariate heterogeneity (p=0.045) is fully attenuated after multivariable adjustment for clinical predictors (all race contrasts p>0.13). No race-by-feature_006 interaction (all p>0.18). The 'other' group (n=1014) shows the largest numerical f006 delta (+5.5 pp) but is small; not a robust signal.
  - Multi-level integers (feature_026, 033, 018, 045, 042, 064): only feature_033 reaches nominal univariate significance (p=0.04), and even that is below most other predictors. Not retained as meaningful.

6. INSURANCE — A SMALL DISPARITY THAT SURVIVES ADJUSTMENT (iteration 18)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Univariate, insurance-type shows no significant overall heterogeneity (chi-square p=0.23). After multivariable adjustment for clinical predictors, however, private insurance vs medicaid shows a small, statistically significant advantage: coef +0.088, OR ~1.09, p=0.020. Medicare vs medicaid: p=0.097 (borderline). Uninsured: n.s. The magnitude is small (about 1 pp on the absolute response scale) but the result is consistent with a residual disparity not captured by the recorded clinical variables.

7. EXTREMES OF RESPONSE (iteration 25)
--------------------------------------

Putting it together:
  - SUPER-RESPONDER PHENOTYPE: {feature_092 >= 0.5 & feature_006 = 1 & feature_007 = 1 & feature_039 = 1}: n=712 (1.42% of cohort), RR=44.80% — about 2.6x the cohort average. OR vs rest of cohort approximately 4.0.
  - LOW-RESPONSE PHENOTYPE: stacking the four independent prognostic risk markers (feature_051=2 + feature_013=1 + feature_011 high + feature_067=1) yields RR <8% on a subset of ~800-900 patients.

8. OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
----------------------

The dataset is well-suited to characterizing a treatment-biomarker-outcome triangle that mirrors first-line metastatic NSCLC immunotherapy: feature_006 is plausibly an immune checkpoint inhibitor (or chemo-IO regimen) indicator; feature_092 plausibly represents PD-L1 tumor proportion score; feature_007 and feature_039 plausibly identify additional regimen components or eligibility flags whose value is conditional on feature_006.

  - The DOMINANT prognostic signal is feature_051 (performance status) — a 2-fold range in response (21% -> 11%) across its three levels.
  - The DOMINANT predictive signal is feature_006 x feature_092 — a 6 percentage-point treatment benefit limited to PD-L1-high patients.
  - A more refined predictive interaction (feature_006 x feature_007 x feature_039, especially when feature_092 is high) defines a small super-responder subgroup (RR ~45%).
  - Demographic/social variables (age, sex-like binaries embedded among feature_007/039 candidates, race, smoking, histology) do NOT modify the treatment effect; only insurance shows a small adjusted disparity.

The findings are consistent with established NSCLC immunotherapy clinical evidence: PD-L1 as a predictive biomarker for ICI response, performance status as a strong prognostic factor, and disease burden / advanced-stage indicators as additional independent prognostic markers.
