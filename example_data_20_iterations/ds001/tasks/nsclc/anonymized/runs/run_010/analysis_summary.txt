Analysis Summary — ds001_nsclc (50,000-patient NSCLC cohort, anonymized features)
=================================================================================

Outcome: objective_response (binary). Cohort response rate: 16.9% (8,447/50,000).
Iterations: 25. Tools: logistic regression (statsmodels), chi-square, stratified
analyses, pairwise and three-way interaction terms.


1. Univariate main-effect screen (iterations 1-3)
-------------------------------------------------
Of 77 binary features, 8 reached p<0.05 univariately. Four reached p<0.001:

  feature_013   negative   RR 15.4% vs 19.6%   OR 0.74   p = 1.3e-33
  feature_067   negative   RR 14.4% vs 17.7%   OR 0.78   p = 2.0e-18
  feature_006   positive   RR 18.2% vs 16.4%   OR 1.14   p = 9.8e-7
  feature_007   positive   RR 17.5% vs 16.4%   OR 1.09   p = 5.7e-4
  (feature_039  positive   RR 17.4% vs 16.4%   OR 1.07   p = 3.3e-3 — also significant)

Of 35 continuous features, four reached p<0.01:

  feature_011   negative   mean 3.38 vs 3.93 (responders vs non)   OR/SD 0.86   p = 1.8e-32
  feature_099   positive   marginally higher in responders         OR/SD 1.05   p = 1.4e-4
  feature_063   negative   mean 5.78 vs 6.15                       OR/SD 0.96   p = 6.9e-4
  feature_092   positive   marginally higher in responders         OR/SD 1.04   p = 3.2e-3

Of the ordinal/integer features, feature_051 (a 0/1/2 ordinal — almost certainly
performance status given the response gradient) was by far the strongest single
predictor in the entire dataset:

  feature_051 = 0  (n=17,557)   RR = 21.1%
  feature_051 = 1  (n=24,962)   RR = 15.7%
  feature_051 = 2  (n=7,481)    RR = 11.1%
  Per-level OR 0.69, p = 1.7e-93 (level 2 vs 0: OR ≈ 0.46)

Categoricals largely unrevealing:
  Histology (feature_043): squamous 17.4% vs adeno 16.7% (p=0.078, NS)
  Smoking (feature_057):   current 17.2% / former 16.9% / never 16.4% (chi p=0.27)
  Race (feature_123):      heterogeneity p=0.045 (white 16.6% slightly below
                           non-white groups 17.2-18.5%)
  Insurance (feature_005): chi p=0.23, NS overall


2. Multivariable adjustment (iteration 4)
-----------------------------------------
A 22-df logistic model with all 10 strong predictors and the four categoricals
showed each of the 10 retained independent significance (p<0.001 for 9, p=0.002
for feature_092). Pseudo-R² = 0.020. Insurance: private vs medicaid OR = 1.09
(p=0.019); medicare and uninsured both NS. Race contrasts all NS after
adjustment (white vs asian beta=-0.077, p=0.13). Histology, smoking, age (median
65 yr) had no adjusted effect.


3. Subgroup heterogeneity for top binary effects (iterations 5-6)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
For each of feature_013, feature_067, feature_006, feature_007, feature_039 the
effect sizes were essentially homogeneous across:
  - histology (interaction p-values 0.40-0.66)
  - smoking status (min interaction p 0.23-0.79)

Notably, feature_007 OR was 1.10 in current/former smokers but 1.01 in
never-smokers (NS, n=7,459 — underpowered).


4. Pairwise interactions among top binaries (iteration 7)
---------------------------------------------------------
Three pairwise interactions among feature_006, feature_007, feature_039 reached
significance:

  feature_006 × feature_039   beta +0.184   p = 4.2e-4
  feature_007 × feature_039   beta +0.146   p = 2.4e-3
  feature_006 × feature_007   beta +0.149   p = 4.3e-3

All three are supraadditive — when both members are positive the response rate
exceeds what additive main effects predict.


5. Three-way interaction — KEY FINDING (iteration 8)
----------------------------------------------------
Fitting feature_006 × feature_007 × feature_039 saturated:

   006   007   039     RR        n
    0     0     0    16.12%    9,882
    0     0     1    16.18%    9,920
    0     1     0    16.46%    7,963
    0     1     1    16.85%    7,977
    1     0     0    16.94%    3,932
    1     0     1    16.94%    3,895
    1     1     0    16.45%    3,215
    1     1     1    22.98%    3,216    ← triple-positive subgroup

Three-way interaction beta = +0.392, p = 1.8e-4. After re-fitting in the
multivariable model alongside clinical covariates, the THREE-way term remains
strongly significant (beta=+0.42, p=7.5e-5) while ALL six main and pairwise
terms drop to non-significance (p>0.17). This is a true higher-order pattern,
not an artifact of pairwise structure.


6. The triplet-positive composite (iterations 9-11)
---------------------------------------------------
Defining triplet_pos = (feature_006=1 AND feature_007=1 AND feature_039=1):

  Crude:                 OR 1.51, p = 3.0e-21   (23.0% vs 16.5%)
  Adjusted for prognostics (feature_013, _067, _011, _051):
                         OR 1.53, p = 1.2e-21
  Adjusted for everything (full multivariable model):
                         OR 1.53, p = 1.7e-21

Counts of positive markers (0/1/2/3): RRs 16.1% / 16.4% / 16.8% / 23.0%. Almost
all of the "lift" lives in the all-three-positive cell, supporting a
threshold/synergy interpretation rather than additive marker burden.

Triplet effect by subgroup:
  Histology:   adeno OR 1.49, squamous OR 1.55 (homogeneous)
  Smoking:     current 1.56, former 1.49, never 1.24 (NS, n=139 in never)
  Race:        white 1.52, black 1.64, hispanic 1.38, asian 1.46, other 1.69
               (no significant heterogeneity)
  Insurance:   medicaid 1.57, medicare 1.43, private 1.57, uninsured 1.75 (NS)
  Performance status (feature_051):
               0: OR 1.42 (p=4e-7)
               1: OR 1.50 (p=2e-10)
               2: OR 1.95 (p=9e-8)
               Interaction triplet_pos × feature_051[T.2] beta=+0.32, p=0.026
               — TRIPLET BENEFIT IS AMPLIFIED IN POOR PS PATIENTS.


7. Other interaction probes (iterations 12-17)
----------------------------------------------
- feature_051 × histology: trend toward steeper PS gradient in squamous (level 1
  interaction p=0.12), not significant.
- feature_051 × race: all eight interaction terms p>0.4. PS gradient is racially
  homogeneous.
- feature_011 quadratic term: p=0.46. Linearity confirmed; tertile RRs
  19.1% / 17.0% / 14.5% (high-vs-low OR 0.72, p<1e-29).
- Age (feature_078) × triplet: all interaction p>0.5; triplet effect is
  age-invariant.
- feature_013 × feature_067 (both negative prognostic): interaction beta=+0.04,
  p=0.51 — additive, no synergy.
- triplet_pos × feature_013: borderline interaction p=0.064, beta +0.17. If
  anything, triplet benefit may be slightly larger in feature_013-positive
  patients (opposite of the hypothesized direction).
- triplet_pos × feature_067: interaction p=0.61, no modification.
- feature_063 × feature_092: nominal interaction p=0.040, small magnitude.
- feature_011 continuous-by-binary interactions: all p>0.5 — homogeneous.


8. Other binary and ordinal screens (iterations 18-19)
-----------------------------------------------------
Several additional binaries had nominal p<0.05 univariately (feature_076,
feature_112, feature_021) but none would survive Bonferroni correction across
the 77-binary screen (threshold ~6.5e-4). Five of the six ordinal/integer
"score" variables (feature_026, _018, _045, _042, _064) showed no monotone
trend. feature_033 reached nominal p=0.04. None replicate the steep gradient
seen in feature_051; feature_051 alone captures performance status.


9. Demographic disparity check (iterations 22, 24)
-------------------------------------------------
- feature_086 (binary, prevalence 55%, the closest to 50% of any binary; a
  candidate sex marker) has zero association with response (OR 0.99, p=0.71).
  Distribution against histology, smoking, race shows it is essentially
  independent of those axes.
- After full clinical adjustment: no significant race contrast (all p>0.13). No
  significant smoking contrast (p>0.7). No histology effect (p=0.16). No age
  effect (p=0.80). The only residual non-clinical effect is private-vs-medicaid
  insurance OR = 1.09 (p=0.019), modest in magnitude — possibly residual
  confounding by access-to-care or geographic factors not captured by the
  measured features.


10. Synthesis (iteration 25)
----------------------------
Drivers of objective_response in this 50,000-patient anonymized NSCLC cohort:

INDEPENDENTLY POSITIVE (adjusted):
  triplet_pos     (feature_006 ∧ feature_007 ∧ feature_039)   OR 1.53   p ≈ 1e-21
  feature_099     (continuous, OR 1.10/unit)                            p = 1e-4
  feature_092     (continuous fractional, OR 1.19/unit)                 p = 2e-3
  insurance: private vs medicaid                              OR 1.09   p = 0.019

INDEPENDENTLY NEGATIVE (adjusted):
  feature_051 = 1  vs 0    OR 0.69                                      p < 1e-30
  feature_051 = 2  vs 0    OR 0.46                                      p < 1e-100
  feature_013     (binary)                                    OR 0.74   p = 1e-34
  feature_067     (binary)                                    OR 0.77   p = 3e-19
  feature_011     (continuous, OR 0.96/unit)                            p < 1e-32
  feature_063     (continuous, OR 0.995/unit)                           p = 4e-4

NULL (after adjustment):
  Histology, smoking status, race, age, feature_086, and the other 70+ binaries
  contribute no meaningful additional effect.

KEY CLINICAL TAKEAWAYS:
- The single largest predictor (any direction) is performance status
  (feature_051): the level-2 vs level-0 OR of 0.46 is by far the dominant
  prognostic gradient.
- The most clinically interesting hypothesis-generating finding is the three-way
  feature_006 × feature_007 × feature_039 synergy. Patients positive for all
  three (~6.4% of the cohort, n=3,216) have a 23.0% response rate vs 16.5% in
  everyone else. This is a true higher-order pattern (the three pairwise terms
  drop out when the three-way term is included). It is consistent with what one
  would expect from a multi-component biomarker signature or a multi-component
  treatment regimen indicator that requires all three flags to be present.
- Triplet benefit is greatest in poor performance status patients (feature_051=2:
  OR 1.95) — the only significant effect-modifying interaction we identified.
- This cohort shows minimal demographic disparity in response after clinical
  adjustment. Race effects disappear; only a small private-vs-medicaid insurance
  advantage (OR 1.09) persists.

UNSUPPORTED HYPOTHESES (refuted across iterations):
  - Histology heterogeneity in any binary marker effect
  - Smoking-status heterogeneity in feature_006 or feature_007 (nominal trend
    but underpowered in never-smokers)
  - feature_011 nonlinearity
  - Synergy between the two negative prognostic markers feature_013 and
    feature_067
  - Reduced triplet benefit in patients with feature_013 or feature_067 positive
  - Sex-marker hypothesis for feature_086
  - Age effect on response
  - Race disparity in triplet benefit
