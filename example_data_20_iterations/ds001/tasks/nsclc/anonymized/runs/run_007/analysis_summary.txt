ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (50,000 patients, 122 anonymized features, binary
outcome `objective_response`, overall ORR = 0.169 / 16.9%)

Approach
--------
Across 25 iterations I (1) screened all 77 binary, 42 continuous, and 4
categorical features for univariate association with ORR; (2) characterized
the sign, magnitude, and dose-response shape of the strongest predictors; (3)
formally tested clinically motivated interactions (smoking × biomarker, race ×
insurance, ECOG-like × demographics, treatment-component × biomarker, and
others); (4) verified that race differences disappear after clinical
adjustment while insurance differences persist; and (5) fit a final
multivariable logistic regression with the key interaction terms.

Each iteration proposed self-contained, signed hypotheses naming the actual
column(s) and tested them with chi-square, t-tests, logistic regression
coefficients, and stratified subgroup analyses.

Strongest main-effect predictors (all confirmed in multivariable model)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. feature_051 (integer 0/1/2, mean 0.80; structurally consistent with ECOG
   performance status) — STRONGEST predictor. ORR 21.1% at 0, 15.7% at 1,
   11.1% at 2. Logit slope -0.38 per unit, p ≈ 1e-96. Monotonic dose-response.
2. feature_013 (binary, prevalence 65%) — ORR 15.4% if present vs 19.6% if
   absent. OR = 0.74, adjusted coef -0.30, p = 1.5e-34. Effect is uniform
   across histology and ECOG (no interaction).
3. feature_067 (binary, prevalence 25%) — ORR 14.4% vs 17.7%. OR = 0.77,
   adjusted coef -0.26, p = 3e-19. Effect is uniform across smoking strata.
4. feature_011 (continuous, range 0-28, mean 3.84; consistent with number of
   metastatic sites or therapy lines) — strict monotonic decline across
   quintiles (18.9% → 13.7%). Coef -0.039 per unit, p = 2e-33.
5. feature_006 / feature_007 / feature_039 (binary, prevalence 29%/45%/50%)
   — each individually weakly positive (OR 1.07–1.14, all p<0.005), but their
   joint distribution shows pronounced super-additivity (see interactions).
6. feature_099 (continuous, range 1.7-5.5, mean 3.80; consistent with serum
   albumin) — positive, monotonic across tertiles (16.1%→17.6%). Coef +0.094,
   p = 1.3e-4. Independent of ECOG (no interaction).
7. feature_063 (continuous, right-skewed, range 0.03-296.88) — modest
   negative; quintile ORR declines 18.2% → 15.7%. Coef -0.005 per unit,
   p = 4.2e-4.
8. feature_092 (continuous, range 0-0.80, mean 0.31; structurally consistent
   with PD-L1 TPS) — small positive main effect (OR per unit ≈ 1.19, p=0.002)
   but its real role is interactive (see below).

Null findings (hypotheses REFUTED)
----------------------------------
- feature_078 (clearly age, range 30-90) is essentially independent of ORR
  (slope p = 0.67; quintile ORRs vary only 16.4-17.5%). Notable absence of
  the expected negative age effect.
- feature_014 (range 6-18, mean 12.5; consistent with hemoglobin), p = 0.60.
- feature_066 (range 0.29-30.4; consistent with NLR), p = 0.38.
- feature_044 (mean ~28; possibly BMI), borderline negative p = 0.08, NS.
- Smoking status (feature_057), histology (feature_043), and insurance
  (feature_005) are NOT significant in unadjusted univariate testing.

Interaction findings (the most clinically informative results)
--------------------------------------------------------------
1. **Triple-component regimen × PD-L1 (largest synergy in the dataset).**
   When patients have all three of feature_006, feature_007, and feature_039
   AND feature_092 ≥ 0.4 (top tertile), ORR is 33.2% (n = 1148). When they
   have all three but low PD-L1, ORR is 17.3%. Without all three, PD-L1 is
   irrelevant (16.5% vs 16.4%). Interaction coefficient +0.876, p < 1e-22.
   The three pairwise component:feature_092 interactions in the adjusted
   model are each ≈ +0.6 (each p < 1e-5). Pattern is exactly that of
   biomarker-selected combination immunotherapy: neither the regimen alone
   nor the biomarker alone is sufficient.
2. **PD-L1 × smoking (direction reversal).** Stratified slopes for feature_092
   on ORR: current smokers OR 1.21 (p = 0.06); former smokers OR 1.33
   (p = 2e-4); never smokers OR 0.72 (p = 0.024). Interaction p = 0.004.
   PD-L1 expression predicts response in smokers but predicts NON-response
   in never-smokers — biologically consistent with driver-mutation–enriched
   never-smoker NSCLC.
3. **Triple-combo benefit by smoking.** Absolute ORR benefit of all-3
   regimen is +7 pp in current/former smokers (16.5% → 23-24%) but only
   +3 pp in never-smokers, mirroring the smoking × PD-L1 pattern.
4. **Pairwise super-additivity within f006/f007/f039.** Each main effect is
   essentially zero (~0 to -0.05) but each pairwise interaction is ≈ +0.15
   (p<0.005). Strongly suggestive of a multi-component therapy where benefit
   accrues only with co-administration.
5. **Pairwise super-additivity within feature_006:feature_092.** In the
   adjusted model the three pairs feature_006:f092, feature_007:f092,
   feature_039:f092 are all ≈ +0.6 (p < 1e-5 each). The unconditional
   feature_092 coefficient is NEGATIVE (-0.60), reflecting that PD-L1
   expression without the regimen confers no benefit (and possibly a small
   liability) in this cohort.

Demographic / health-disparity findings
---------------------------------------
- **Insurance disparity persists adjusted.** Private vs Medicaid ORR
  difference (17.2% vs 16.1%) remains after adjusting for ECOG, feature_013,
  feature_011: adjusted coef +0.087, OR 1.09, p = 0.022. Medicare vs
  Medicaid p = 0.10. Private-insured patients have a small but real adjusted
  ORR advantage.
- **Race × insurance interaction is significant.** Several interaction
  terms reach p<0.05 (e.g., White:Private -0.46, p = 0.009). The pattern is
  driven in part by:
  - Asian × Medicaid: ORR 12.6% (n = 388) vs 17-19% in Asian patients with
    other insurance. Coef -0.45, OR 0.64, p = 0.005. Asian-Medicaid is the
    single most disadvantaged race × insurance subgroup despite Asian race
    being above-average overall.
- **Race main effect is fully confounded.** Adjusted for ECOG/f013/f011,
  all race coefficients shrink to non-significance (p > 0.11), suggesting
  the marginal racial differences reflect different distributions of
  clinical severity rather than direct race effects.
- **Age is null.** No monotonic age effect on ORR.
- **ECOG penalty is invariant.** ECOG × race and ECOG × insurance
  interactions are NS (all p > 0.30); the negative effect of high
  feature_051 is constant across demographic strata.

Independence findings (hypothesized interactions that were absent)
------------------------------------------------------------------
- feature_013 × feature_051: additive only (interaction p = 0.73).
- feature_011 × histology: additive only (p = 0.74).
- feature_092 × histology: additive only (p = 0.93).
- feature_099 × feature_051: additive only (p = 0.73).
- feature_067 × smoking: homogeneous OR ≈ 0.78 across all smoking strata.
- Smoking × histology: NS (p = 0.73).

Final adjusted multivariable model
----------------------------------
Including main effects for feature_051, feature_013, feature_067, feature_011,
feature_006, feature_007, feature_039, feature_092, feature_099, feature_063,
plus the three feature_006/007/039 × feature_092 interactions, yields these
significant (p < 0.001) effects:
  feature_051   coef -0.38   (ECOG-like, monotonic)
  feature_013   coef -0.30   (top binary risk feature)
  feature_067   coef -0.26   (second binary risk feature)
  feature_011   coef -0.039  (per unit; mets/lines-like)
  feature_099   coef +0.092  (per unit; albumin-like)
  feature_063   coef -0.005  (per unit; inflammatory/burden-like)
  feature_092           coef -0.60   (PD-L1 alone)
  f006:feature_092 coef +0.60   (biomarker × component)
  f007:feature_092 coef +0.62   (biomarker × component)
  f039:feature_092 coef +0.59   (biomarker × component)
Smoking, histology, race, age, and BMI-like feature_044 fall out as
non-significant after adjustment.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
- The dataset behaves like a real-world NSCLC cohort treated with a mixture
  of regimens. Performance status (feature_051), tumor burden / lines
  (feature_011), albumin-like nutritional status (feature_099), and two
  binary risk features (feature_013, feature_067) dominate prognosis with
  the directions one would expect.
- The signature finding is a clean, replicable biomarker × treatment
  interaction: feature_092 (PD-L1-like) is only beneficial in the presence
  of the three-component regimen indicated by feature_006/007/039. Without
  the regimen, PD-L1 has no association with ORR. With the regimen and
  high PD-L1, ORR doubles relative to baseline. This pattern is the
  hallmark of biomarker-selected combination immunotherapy.
- The PD-L1 effect reverses sign in never-smokers, consistent with the
  enrichment of driver-mutation–positive disease in that subgroup.
- Insurance status (Medicaid disadvantage) shows a small but adjustment-
  resistant effect on ORR, while racial differences are explained by
  underlying differences in clinical severity. The Asian × Medicaid
  intersection is the single most disadvantaged subgroup.
- Age (feature_078), hemoglobin-like (feature_014), and NLR-like
  (feature_066) features are not associated with ORR in this cohort.
