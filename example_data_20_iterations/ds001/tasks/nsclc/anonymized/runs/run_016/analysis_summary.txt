ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (50,000 NSCLC patients)
========================================================

Outcome: objective_response (binary; baseline rate 16.9%, 8,447 / 50,000).
Features: 123 de-identified columns — 77 binary indicators, 7 ordinal counts,
35 continuous measurements, and 4 categorical strings (smoking feature_057;
histology feature_043; race feature_123; insurance feature_005). Age is
plausibly feature_078 (range 30-90, mean 65). No missing values.

Across 25 iterations I screened all features against objective_response,
built a multivariable logistic model, and progressively probed pairwise,
three-way and four-way interactions, treatment-biomarker effects, subgroup
stratification, and demographic disparities. The principal findings are
below; every reported direction was verified against the data.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. UNIVARIATE SIGNALS (iterations 1-6)
------------------------------------------------------------
Binary features (chi-square):
  - feature_013 (prevalence 65%): negatively associated, OR 0.74,
    p = 1.1e-33 (the single strongest binary predictor in the cohort).
  - feature_067 (25%): negative, OR 0.78, p = 2.0e-18.
  - feature_006 (28%): positive, OR 1.14, p = 1.0e-6.
  - feature_007 (45%): positive, OR 1.09, p = 5.9e-4.
  - feature_039 (50%): positive, OR 1.07, p = 3.4e-3.
  - 72 other binary features: not individually significant.

Continuous features (Welch t):
  - feature_011 (range 0-28, mean 3.84): negative, mean diff -0.55,
    p = 2.8e-35. Dose-response over deciles (response 19.2% in the
    lowest octile, 13.2% in the highest).
  - feature_099 (range 1.7-5.5): positive, p = 1.3e-4. Range and sign
    are biologically consistent with serum albumin.
  - feature_063 (range 0.03-296, skewed): negative, p = 4.2e-4.
    Plausibly an inflammatory or tumor-burden marker.
  - feature_092 (range 0-0.8): small positive main effect, p = 3.8e-3
    — but the more interesting story is its interaction structure
    (see section 3).
  - All other continuous features (including feature_078/age) had
    p > 0.07 in univariate testing.

Ordinal features:
  - feature_051 (levels 0/1/2): strong monotone NEGATIVE trend
    (response 21.1% / 15.7% / 11.1%; chi-sq p = 2.9e-93). Range and
    behaviour are consistent with ECOG performance status.
  - The other six ordinal features (026, 033, 064, 018, 045, 042)
    showed no monotone gradient (all p > 0.10).

Categorical features:
  - Histology (feature_043): adeno 16.7% vs squamous 17.4%, p = 0.078
    — not significant.
  - Smoking (feature_057): no overall difference (p = 0.27).
  - Race (feature_123): borderline overall (p = 0.045); white 16.5%
    vs non-white 17.2-18.5%.
  - Insurance (feature_005): no overall difference (p = 0.23) but
    a tendency for medicaid (16.1%) to be lowest.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. MULTIVARIABLE MODEL (iteration 7)
------------------------------------------------------------
A logistic model containing all the screened predictors plus age,
histology, smoking, race and insurance reproduced every univariate
direction:

  Adjusted log-odds coefficients (all p < 0.005 except where noted)
    feature_051[T.1]            -0.369
    feature_051[T.2]            -0.777
    feature_013                 -0.302
    feature_067                 -0.259
    feature_006                 +0.131
    feature_007                 +0.081
    feature_039                 +0.077
    feature_011 (per unit)      -0.039
    feature_099 (per unit)      +0.094
    feature_063 (per unit)      -0.005
    feature_092 (per unit)      +0.173
    feature_005[T.private]      +0.089 (vs medicaid)
    age, histology, smoking, race: not individually significant

Pseudo-R^2 = 0.020; LLR p = 8e-179. Performance status (feature_051)
and feature_013 are the dominant prognostic signals; the remaining
binary features each carry a smaller but reproducible direction.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. THE TRIPLE-COMBINATION SYNERGY (iterations 8-9)
------------------------------------------------------------
Pairwise interactions among the three positive-direction binary
features were all positive and significant:
    feature_006 x feature_039  +0.184  p = 4.2e-4
    feature_007 x feature_039  +0.146  p = 2.4e-3
    feature_006 x feature_007  +0.149  p = 4.3e-3

Fitting a saturated 2x2x2 logistic model collapsed essentially all
of this signal onto a single THREE-WAY interaction term:
    feature_006:feature_007:feature_039  +0.392, z=3.74, p = 1.8e-4
None of the lower-order main or two-way terms remained significant
in the saturated model. The cell with all three features = 1
(n = 3,216) had a 23.0% response rate; every other 2x2x2 cell sat
at 16-17%. This is most parsimoniously interpreted as a triple
combination treatment whose components are nearly inert individually
or in pairs but produce a substantial response benefit when all
three are administered together.

The triple-combo synergy survives adjustment for feature_013 and is
NOT modulated by feature_013 — the four-way interaction
feature_013:feature_006:feature_007:feature_039 is non-significant
(p = 0.43, iteration 22). Likewise, the triple effect is additive
with respect to feature_011 (interaction p = 0.72, iteration 23).

------------------------------------------------------------
4. PREDICTIVE BIOMARKER (iterations 10-12)
------------------------------------------------------------
The continuous feature_092 has only a weak main effect on response,
but it interacts strongly with each of feature_006, feature_007 and
feature_039:
    feature_092:feature_007  +0.61  p = 7.9e-8
    feature_092:feature_006  +0.59  p = 1.2e-6
    feature_092:feature_039  +0.59  p = 1.6e-7

Stratified analysis confirms the pattern: among feature_007 = 0
patients (n = 27,629), feature_092 has no association with response
(coef -0.12, p = 0.13); among feature_007 = 1 patients (n = 22,371)
feature_092 has a strong positive association (coef +0.49, p = 3.1e-9).
This is the classic signature of a TREATMENT-PREDICTIVE BIOMARKER: a
continuous feature that is uninformative in the absence of a
particular intervention but strongly identifies responders when that
intervention is given. Range, scale (0-0.8) and direction are all
compatible with a PD-L1-like measurement.

The biomarker effect is concentrated where the triple combo is
delivered. Cross-tabulating feature_092 quartiles by triple-combo
status:
    no triple-combo:  16.7% / 16.6% / 16.4% / 16.2% (essentially flat)
    triple-combo:     16.9% / 16.1% / 18.2% / 40.1%   (n = 776/816/797/827)

The Q4-feature_092 + triple-combo cell (n = 827) at 40.1% response is
by far the highest-responding cell anywhere in the cohort.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. SUBGROUP HETEROGENEITY (iterations 13-16)
------------------------------------------------------------
- Histology: NO interactions detected. The treatment-like binary
  features and continuous predictors had identical effects in
  adenocarcinoma and squamous patients (smallest interaction p = 0.40).
- Smoking: feature_092 x never-smoker interaction = -0.525, p = 3.5e-3.
  The positive feature_092 / feature_007 interaction effect is
  attenuated (and possibly reversed) in never smokers — also
  biologically consistent with a PD-L1 / immunotherapy signature, since
  PD-L1 driven benefit tracks tobacco-induced tumor mutational burden.
  Other smoking-by-treatment interactions were null.
- Performance status (feature_051): feature_007 x feature_051[T.2]
  interaction +0.18, p = 0.030 — the response advantage of feature_007
  is somewhat larger in patients with the worst performance status,
  although the absolute response rate in feature_051 = 2 remains low.
- Age (feature_078): only feature_067 x age reached p < 0.05
  (+0.0071, p = 0.014); the negative feature_067 effect attenuates
  with increasing age. Age main effect is null after adjustment.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. DEMOGRAPHIC DISPARITIES (iterations 17-20)
------------------------------------------------------------
- Insurance x treatment: NO significant interactions (smallest
  treatment x insurance interaction p = 0.16). However, after adjusting
  for clinical factors, the privately-insured group has higher response
  odds than medicaid (+0.087, p = 0.022); medicare and uninsured trend
  positive vs medicaid but are NS.
- Race adjusted: black race vs white +0.079 (p = 0.034) AFTER adjusting
  for clinical predictors. Asian, hispanic and "other" trend positive
  but are individually NS. The adjusted disparity runs OPPOSITE to the
  conventional direction (white patients have the lowest adjusted
  odds), which is consistent with selection effects in this
  community-vendor cohort.
- Treatment-allocation disparity check: receipt of the triple-combo
  did NOT differ by race (p = 0.83) or by insurance (p = 0.097).
  The race / insurance signal is therefore unlikely to be driven by
  unequal access to the most effective regimen.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. REFINED FINAL MODEL (iteration 24)
------------------------------------------------------------
A model containing main effects for feature_013, feature_067,
feature_011, feature_099, feature_063 and feature_051, plus the
saturated feature_006*feature_007*feature_039 term and the
feature_092*feature_007 interaction, fits significantly better than
the same model without interactions (LRT chi-square = 73.1 on 5 df,
p = 2.3e-14).

Key coefficients in the refined model:
    feature_006:feature_007:feature_039   +0.415  p = 8.5e-5
    feature_092:feature_007               +0.611  p = 8.5e-8
    feature_051[T.2]                      -0.777
    feature_013                           -0.302
    feature_067                           -0.260
    feature_099 (per unit)                +0.094
    feature_011 (per unit)                -0.040
    feature_063 (per unit)                -0.005

------------------------------------------------------------
8. EXTREME-RESPONDER SUBGROUP (iteration 25)
------------------------------------------------------------
Combining the favorable signals: patients with feature_051 = 0
(best performance status), feature_013 = 0, all three
treatment-component features = 1, AND feature_092 in the top
quartile (n = 104) had a 42.3% response rate. Within this same
"otherwise favorable" subgroup, feature_092 quartile produced a
steep gradient: 19.2% / 18.8% / 30.3% / 42.3%. This biomarker
gradient is essentially absent outside the triple-combo subgroup.

------------------------------------------------------------
OVERALL CONCLUSION
------------------------------------------------------------
Three signals dominate response variation in this NSCLC cohort:
(1) clinical condition — feature_051 (performance-status-like) and
the negative-direction binary indicators feature_013 and feature_067;
(2) a triple combination of feature_006 + feature_007 + feature_039
that is essentially inert as a pair or single agent but produces a
clear response benefit when all three are administered together; and
(3) a continuous biomarker, feature_092, that is uninformative on
its own but predicts dramatically higher response (Q4 = 40%) when the
triple combination is given. The combination of these signals can
identify a small (~0.2% of the cohort) subgroup with response rates
above 40% — about 2.5x the cohort baseline. After adjusting for these
clinical signals, the only residual demographic associations are a
modestly higher response rate in black patients vs white and in
privately-insured patients vs medicaid, and these do NOT appear to
be explained by differential access to the triple combo.
