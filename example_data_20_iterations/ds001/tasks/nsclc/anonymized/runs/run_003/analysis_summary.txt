ds001_nsclc — Analysis Summary
==============================

Cohort
------
The dataset comprises 50,000 NSCLC patient records with 123 anonymized features and
a single binary outcome, objective_response. The overall response rate is 16.89%, a
realistic base rate for advanced/metastatic NSCLC across mixed treatment lines.
Feature inventory: 77 binary indicator variables, 35 continuous numeric variables,
7 multi-level integer variables (3-11 levels), and 4 free-text categorical fields
(smoking status feature_057; histology feature_043; race/ethnicity feature_123;
insurance feature_005). No missing values.

Approach
--------
Across 25 iterations, hypotheses were proposed and tested with progressively richer
models: chi-square / Mann-Whitney univariate screens, multivariable logistic
regression with main effects, and 2-way / 3-way interaction terms; subgroup analyses
were used to verify effect homogeneity across clinically relevant strata
(race, histology, smoking, performance-status surrogate).

Findings — main effects supported by the evidence
-------------------------------------------------
1. feature_051 (3-level ordinal, 0/1/2; almost certainly an ECOG-PS surrogate) is
   by far the strongest single predictor. Response rates fall monotonically with
   level: 21.1% (n=17,557) -> 15.7% (n=24,962) -> 11.1% (n=7,481). chi-square
   p = 2.9e-93. Per-level adjusted log-odds = -0.38 (z = -20.8). Direction
   negative (worse PS -> lower response).

2. feature_013 (binary, prevalence 64.7%) is a strong NEGATIVE predictor of
   response: rr 19.6% (=0) vs 15.4% (=1), risk difference -4.25 pp,
   chi-square p = 1.1e-33. Adjusted log-odds = -0.30 (independent of feature_067
   and feature_051). Behaves like a resistance biomarker.

3. feature_067 (binary, prevalence 25.2%) is a second, independent NEGATIVE
   predictor: rr 17.7% (=0) vs 14.4% (=1), p = 2.0e-18. Adjusted log-odds = -0.26.
   feature_013 and feature_067 act ADDITIVELY: lowest response is in the
   doubly-positive cell (rr = 13.2%); no significant 2-way interaction (p = 0.50).

4. feature_011 (continuous, range 0-28, median 3.0) is strongly NEGATIVELY
   associated with response. Mean 3.38 (responders) vs 3.93 (non-responders),
   Mann-Whitney p = 1.4e-32. Per-SD adjusted log-odds = -0.15. Effect is
   independent of feature_013 and additive on the log-odds scale (no significant
   feature_011 x feature_013 interaction, p = 0.50). Plausibly a marker of
   tumor burden / inflammation.

5. feature_063 (continuous) — modest NEGATIVE effect (per-SD adjusted log-odds
   -0.046, p = 4e-4). feature_099 (continuous) — modest POSITIVE effect
   (per-SD +0.047, p = 1e-4). feature_092 (continuous) — modest POSITIVE
   effect (per-SD +0.037, p = 0.002). All retained after mutual adjustment.

6. Race (feature_123): a small but real difference in unadjusted response —
   white 16.5% vs non-white 17.5%, chi-square p = 0.005. Magnitude ~1 pp; race
   does NOT confound the main biomarker effects (feature_013 prevalence is
   uniform across racial groups, range 64-66%).

Findings — interaction
----------------------
7. A POSITIVE 3-WAY INTERACTION among feature_006, feature_007, and feature_039
   is the most clinically interesting non-additive pattern in the dataset. Each
   pairwise interaction is positive and nominally significant (p = 0.0004,
   0.0024, 0.0043). However, when fit jointly, only the 3-way term is
   significant (coef = +0.42, p = 7.3e-5); pairwise terms collapse to noise.

   The cell-level response rates make the pattern obvious: response is at
   baseline (~16%) in all 7 cells where any of the three features is 0, but
   jumps to 22.98% in the single cell where all three are 1 (n = 3,216, 6.4%
   of the cohort). This is the signature of a combination-regimen indicator
   (e.g., a drug administered only when three component flags are simultaneously
   true). The triple-positive group has +6.5 pp absolute response benefit
   versus the rest (p = 5.4e-20).

   The triple-positive benefit is preserved across all 5 racial groups
   (delta +5 to +9 pp) and all 3 performance-status levels (delta +6 to +8 pp,
   borderline interaction p = 0.056 suggesting modestly larger relative benefit
   at higher PS). Direction is uniform.

Findings — refuted / null hypotheses
------------------------------------
- Age (feature_078): mean 65 in both responders and non-responders, t-test
  p = 0.67. NO age effect on response.
- Smoking status (feature_057): chi-square p = 0.27 across current/former/never;
  no main effect, and no interaction with any of the principal markers
  (all interaction p > 0.17).
- Insurance (feature_005): chi-square p = 0.23 across medicaid/medicare/private/
  uninsured; no main effect.
- Histology (feature_043, adeno vs squamous): borderline main effect
  (p = 0.078); NO interaction with feature_013, feature_067, feature_011,
  feature_051, or any of feature_006/007/039 (all interaction p > 0.12).
- Multi-level integer features feature_026, feature_033, feature_018,
  feature_045, feature_042, feature_064 all have flat response across their
  levels (all chi-square p >= 0.10).
- The bottom-tier 10 binary features (feature_001, feature_002, feature_037,
  feature_102, feature_115, feature_122, feature_034, feature_040, feature_050,
  feature_025) all have risk differences < +/- 0.5 pp and p > 0.84 — consistent
  with no real association.
- No effect modification of feature_013 by performance status: biomarker effect
  is roughly constant (~5 pp drop) across feature_051 levels; interaction
  p = 0.73.
- feature_013 effect is QUALITATIVELY HOMOGENEOUS across all 5 racial subgroups
  (negative in every group; magnitude similar).

Final integrated model
----------------------
A multivariable logistic regression with feature_013, feature_067, feature_011,
feature_051 (ordinal), feature_006, feature_007, feature_039, feature_063,
feature_099, feature_092 plus all 2-way and the 3-way interaction among
feature_006/007/039 yields LLR p = 1e-191 and pseudo-R^2 = 0.021. Independent
contributions:

  feature_013:                  log-odds = -0.30  (z = -12.3,  p < 1e-30)
  feature_067:                  log-odds = -0.26  (z = -9.0,   p < 1e-15)
  feature_011 (per SD):         log-odds = -0.15  (z = -12.1,  p < 1e-30)
  feature_051 (per level):      log-odds = -0.38  (z = -20.7,  p < 1e-90)
  feature_063 (per SD):         log-odds = -0.05  (z = -3.5,   p = 4e-4)
  feature_099 (per SD):         log-odds = +0.05  (z = +3.9,   p = 1e-4)
  feature_092 (per SD):         log-odds = +0.04  (z = +3.0,   p = 0.002)
  feature_006*feature_007*feature_039 (3-way): +0.42  (z = +4.0,   p = 7e-5)

The pseudo-R^2 of ~2% is small in absolute terms but consistent with the typical
explainable fraction of response variance in real-world oncology cohorts.

Bottom line
-----------
- Treatment-biomarker-outcome structure in ds001_nsclc is dominated by ONE
  performance-status-like ordinal feature, TWO independent binary resistance
  markers, ONE continuous tumor-burden/inflammation-like marker (feature_011),
  and a SINGLE 3-way combination-therapy indicator (feature_006 & feature_007
  & feature_039) that delivers a ~6.5 pp absolute response benefit.
- Demographic variables (age, race, insurance, smoking) and most binary/
  continuous features show NO independent effect on response.
- Effects are remarkably homogeneous across race, histology, smoking, and
  performance status; no clinically meaningful effect modification was
  identified beyond the triple-feature combination signal.
