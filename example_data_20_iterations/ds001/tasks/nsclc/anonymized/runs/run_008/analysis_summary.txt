ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (50,000 patients; outcome `objective_response`)
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The cohort consists of 50,000 patient records with 122 features (78 binary,
35 continuous, 4 categorical: feature_057 smoking, feature_043 histology,
feature_123 race, feature_005 insurance) and a single binary outcome,
`objective_response`. The marginal response rate is 16.89% (8,447/50,000).
There are no missing values. Features are de-identified to opaque labels.

I worked iteratively (20 iterations) through five conceptual stages:
(1) marginal/univariate screening of all features, (2) multivariable
modeling, (3) confounding checks vs demographic/clinical strata,
(4) interaction and effect-modification testing, and (5) synthesis and
profiling of high/low-response subgroups.

================================================================================
STAGE 1 — UNIVARIATE SIGNALS
================================================================================

CATEGORICAL FEATURES (chi-square)
- feature_057 (smoking — current/former/never): response 17.23/16.85/16.38%,
  chi-square p=0.27 (NOT significant). Hypothesis that smokers respond better
  was UNSUPPORTED.
- feature_043 (histology — adenocarcinoma vs squamous): 16.71% vs 17.37%,
  p=0.078 (NOT significant). Histology hypothesis UNSUPPORTED.
- feature_123 (race): 17.72/17.76/17.15/18.54/16.55% across asian/black/
  hispanic/other/white, chi-square p=0.045 — MARGINALLY SIGNIFICANT
  univariately, mainly driven by a slightly lower rate in white patients.
- feature_005 (insurance): p=0.23 (NOT significant).

BINARY FEATURES (78 tested; key hits, with sign)
- feature_013 = 1 (n=32,365): response 15.40% vs 19.64% — log OR -0.295,
  p=1.1e-33. STRONGEST SINGLE PREDICTOR (negative). Direction confirmed.
- feature_067 = 1 (n=12,584): 14.36% vs 17.75% — log OR -0.252, p=2e-18
  (negative). Confirmed.
- feature_006 = 1 (n=14,258): 18.19% vs 16.38% — log OR +0.127, p=1e-6
  (positive). Confirmed.
- feature_007 = 1: log OR +0.083, p=6e-4 (positive). Confirmed.
- feature_039 = 1: log OR +0.070, p=3e-3 (positive). Confirmed.
- Two additional weak hits: feature_076 (+, p=0.019), feature_112 (-, p=0.033).
- The remaining ~70 binary features did not reach p<0.05.

CONTINUOUS FEATURES (35 tested; key hits, with sign of mean difference
                     responders vs non-responders)
- feature_011: mean 3.38 (responders) vs 3.93 (non-responders), Welch t = -12.4,
  p=2.8e-35. STRONG NEGATIVE association (higher value -> lower response).
- feature_099: mean 3.821 vs 3.798, p=1.3e-4 (positive, small).
- feature_063: mean 5.78 vs 6.15, p=4.2e-4 (negative).
- feature_092: mean 0.313 vs 0.306, p=3.8e-3 (positive, small).
- feature_078 (likely age): NOT associated (means 65.0 vs 65.0, p=0.67).
- All other 30 continuous features p>=0.08.

================================================================================
STAGE 2 — MULTIVARIABLE LOGISTIC MODEL
================================================================================

A logit model with the top five binary and four continuous predictors
preserved all signs and approximate magnitudes:

  feature_013    -0.298   (p<1e-30)
  feature_067    -0.253   (p<1e-17)
  feature_006    +0.130   (p=7e-7)
  feature_007    +0.081   (p=0.001)
  feature_039    +0.072   (p=0.003)
  feature_011    -0.039   (per unit, p<1e-30)
  feature_099    +0.092   (p=1e-4)
  feature_063    -0.005   (per unit, p<0.001)
  feature_092    +0.164   (p=0.004)

Pseudo R^2 ≈ 0.0105; full-model AUC ≈ 0.572. Discrimination is modest
overall (consistent with many small independent effects rather than a single
dominant marker), but the signal is highly significant (LLR p < 1e-87).

================================================================================
STAGE 3 — DEMOGRAPHIC / CLINICAL CONFOUNDING
================================================================================

After adjustment for the nine biomarkers:
- Race (feature_123) is NO LONGER significant (each dummy p>0.10; "white"
  coefficient -0.081, p=0.11 — the only one approaching significance). The
  univariate race signal (p=0.045) appears to reflect biomarker-distribution
  confounding rather than a true racial difference in response.
- Smoking (feature_057): all dummies p>0.7 — no residual effect.
- Histology (feature_043 squamous vs adeno): adjusted coef +0.039, p=0.16 —
  no residual effect.
- Insurance (feature_005): only "private vs medicaid" reaches p=0.025
  (coef +0.085); medicare p=0.13, uninsured p=0.31. No coherent gradient and
  the block of insurance dummies is jointly non-significant.

CONCLUSION: demographic and clinical features do NOT independently predict
`objective_response` once the biomarker model is in place.

================================================================================
STAGE 4 — INTERACTIONS AND EFFECT MODIFICATION
================================================================================

Stratified estimates of the effect of feature_013 by every demographic
stratum:

  By histology:    adeno -0.31, squamous -0.26 (consistent)
  By smoking:      current -0.31, former -0.30, never -0.24 (consistent)
  By race:         white -0.31, black -0.31, hispanic -0.25, other -0.37,
                   asian -0.16 (uniformly negative; asian smallest n)
  By insurance:    medicaid -0.28, medicare -0.34, private -0.26,
                   uninsured -0.25 (consistent)

The dominant negative effect of feature_013 is REMARKABLY UNIFORM across
demographic strata. Hypotheses of effect modification by smoking, histology,
race, or insurance were UNSUPPORTED.

Pairwise binary-binary interactions (Wald p in logit):
  feature_013 x feature_067     p=0.50  (NS)
  feature_013 x feature_006     p=0.15  (NS)
  feature_013 x feature_007     p=0.16  (NS)
  feature_067 x feature_006     p=0.54  (NS)
  feature_006 x feature_007     p=0.004 (POSITIVE SYNERGY)
    Cell rates: 006=0,007=0 -> 16.15%; 006=0,007=1 -> 16.66%;
                006=1,007=0 -> 16.94%; 006=1,007=1 -> 19.72%.

Continuous-binary interactions:
  feature_011 x feature_013/067/006/007  all NS  (effect of feature_011 is
                                                  uniform across these strata)
  feature_092 x feature_013     p=0.037 (POSITIVE)
    Within feature_013=0: response is flat across feature_092 quartiles
                          (~19-20%).
    Within feature_013=1: response rises from ~15.0% to 16.5% across
                          feature_092 quartiles. Effect of feature_092 is
                          concentrated in feature_013=1 patients.

CONCLUSION: effects are largely additive on the logit scale. Two robust
synergistic interactions:
  - feature_006 + feature_007 (binary)
  - feature_092 within feature_013=1 (continuous)

================================================================================
STAGE 5 — DOSE-RESPONSE AND SUBGROUP PROFILING
================================================================================

feature_011 dose-response (by tertile of non-zero values):
  Q1 (lowest):  18.48% response
  Q2:           16.34%
  Q3 (highest): 14.21%
Monotonic decline of ~4.3 percentage points top vs bottom. A quadratic term
on feature_011 is non-significant (p=0.46) — the relationship is essentially
linear on the logit scale.

feature_063 dose-response (by quartile):
  18.06% / 16.80% / 16.71% / 16.00% — monotonic but small (~2 pp top-bottom).

feature_092 dose-response (full cohort): no clean monotonic gradient
(17.10/16.83/16.74/17.74) — the marginal main effect arises within the
feature_013=1 subgroup (see Stage 4).

JOINT SUBGROUPS:
- feature_013=1 AND feature_067=1: response 13.2% (vs 20.7% for double-
  negative; absolute gap 7.5 pp). Lowest 2x2 cell.
- feature_013=1 AND feature_067=1 AND high feature_011: ~11.7% — clearly
  identifies the lowest-response subgroup.
- feature_013=0 AND feature_006=1 AND feature_007=1 AND low feature_011:
  ~22.5% — highest-response subgroup, well above the 16.9% baseline.

Predicted-probability deciles from the 9-biomarker model:
  Top decile observed response ~24% (cohort 16.9%; ratio 1.42).
  Bottom decile observed response ~11% (~65% of cohort average).
The model separates patients into clinically meaningful response strata,
albeit with modest discrimination overall (AUC 0.57).

================================================================================
OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

1. `objective_response` in this NSCLC cohort is jointly determined by a small
   set of biomarker features acting roughly additively on the log-odds scale.
   The dominant predictors are feature_013 (negative), feature_067
   (negative), feature_011 (negative continuous), feature_006 (positive),
   feature_092 (positive), and feature_099 (positive).

2. Demographic and clinical descriptors (race, smoking, histology, insurance,
   age-like feature_078) carry NO independent prognostic information once
   biomarkers are accounted for. The marginal race signal is fully explained
   by between-race differences in biomarker distribution.

3. Effects of the dominant biomarkers are CONSISTENT across all demographic
   strata — there is no evidence of population-level heterogeneity that
   would call for stratum-specific decision rules.

4. Two synergistic interactions stand out: feature_006 x feature_007
   (joint-positive cell shows ~3 percentage point excess), and
   feature_092 x feature_013 (positive feature_092 effect concentrated in
   feature_013=1 patients).

5. Discrimination is modest (AUC ≈ 0.57). The biomarker panel reliably
   separates extremes — top and bottom deciles differ by ~13 percentage
   points in observed response — but mid-risk patients are not strongly
   resolved by available features.

End of analysis summary.
