Analysis summary — ds001_nsclc (n=50,000; 17% objective response rate)
================================================================================

Overview
--------
The dataset is a real-world NSCLC cohort with 123 anonymized features and a
binary `objective_response` outcome. Across 25 iterations I screened all
features univariately, then drilled into the strongest signals with stratified,
interaction, and multivariable models. The clearest story to emerge is a
classic NSCLC immunotherapy biology pattern: a small set of treatment-like
features synergize with a likely PD-L1 expression measure, on top of several
strong but generic prognostic factors.

Feature-class identification (inferred from value distributions)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
The five named categoricals declare themselves: feature_057 = smoking
(current/former/never), feature_043 = histology (adenocarcinoma/squamous),
feature_123 = race, feature_005 = insurance, feature_078 = age in years
(30-90, mean 65). Among numeric features the most clinically interpretable are
feature_092 (range 0-0.8, mean 0.31, behaves like PD-L1 expression fraction),
feature_011 (range 0-28, zero-inflated; behaves like prior-lines-of-therapy or
pack-years), and the ternary feature_051 (0/1/2; behaves like ECOG performance
status). Most other binary features are unlabeled but split into clear
prognostic vs. predictive (treatment-effect-modifying) classes by their
interaction patterns.

What was supported
------------------
1. PROGNOSTIC: feature_051 has a steep, monotonic dose-response (ORR
   21.1%/15.7%/11.1% at levels 0/1/2; chi-square p=2.9e-93; multivariable
   adjusted log-OR -0.78 at level 2). Direction and shape match ECOG.

2. PROGNOSTIC: feature_013 (prevalence 65%) reduces ORR by ~5pp at every level
   of feature_051 (univariate OR 0.74, p=1e-33; adjusted -0.30, p<1e-13). Effect
   is essentially uniform across PD-L1 bins, smoking strata, and treatment
   classes — generic negative prognostic.

3. PROGNOSTIC: feature_067 (prevalence 25%) is independent of feature_013
   (Pearson r=0.003) and adds an additional ~5pp ORR penalty (univariate OR
   0.78, p=2e-18; adjusted -0.26).

4. PROGNOSTIC: feature_011 has both a zero-vs-nonzero jump (ORR 19.3% if 0,
   16.0% if >0) and a monotonic decline within nonzero values (Q1 17.8% → Q4
   13.5%). The per-unit log-OR penalty (-0.039) is identical in patients who
   received triple-ICI vs. those who did not, so it acts as a baseline modifier
   rather than a treatment-effect modifier.

5. PREDICTIVE: feature_006, feature_007, and feature_039 are pairwise
   uncorrelated (|r|<0.005) and each shows a small main-effect ORR boost
   together with a strong positive interaction with feature_092 (interaction
   p-values 2e-5 to 8e-8). All three behave like immune-checkpoint inhibitors.

6. PREDICTIVE — strongest single finding: the combination of all three (the
   "triple ICI", prevalence 6.4%) interacts with PD-L1 with coefficient +2.81
   (p~1e-37). In the PD-L1 high (>=0.5) subgroup the absolute ORR jumps from
   16.3% (no triple ICI) to 44.8% (with triple ICI, n=712) — about three-fold
   the cohort baseline. In the PD-L1 low/negative subgroup the triple ICI is at
   best neutral (and slightly negative on its own coefficient), confirming that
   benefit is restricted to PD-L1-expressing tumors.

7. PREDICTIVE: feature_092 (PD-L1) has NO main effect on ORR in patients
   without any of the three ICI markers (quartile ORRs in n_ici=0:
   16.3/16.5/16.5/15.2, p=0.59). It is a true predictive biomarker, not a
   prognostic one — the textbook immunotherapy signature.

8. PREDICTIVE: PD-L1 benefit is attenuated in never-smokers (PD-L1 ×
   never-smoker interaction coef -0.53, p=0.004). Within-stratum
   correlations: never -0.026, former +0.023, current +0.015 — matches the
   well-known clinical observation that never-smokers respond less to
   PD-(L)1 blockade.

9. PREDICTIVE / HISTOLOGY-SPECIFIC: feature_104 improves ORR only in
   squamous tumors (squamous: +2.4pp, p=0.005; adenocarcinoma: no effect,
   p=0.57). Interaction p=0.008. Plausibly a squamous-targeted regimen
   (EGFR antibody, taxane combination, etc.).

10. PREDICTIVE / RESISTANCE: feature_027 attenuates the benefit of feature_006
    and feature_039 (interactions -0.27 and -0.21, p<0.005). Plausibly an
    immune-resistance phenotype.

11. EQUITY / DISPARITY: triple-ICI benefit is consistent across racial groups
    (5–9pp absolute ORR improvement in white, black, hispanic, asian, "other")
    and across insurance categories (5–10pp). No evidence in this dataset that
    these treatments deliver differential benefit by demographics.

12. EFFECT MODIFICATION: triple ICI benefit is preserved (and slightly
    amplified in absolute terms) at poor performance status — interaction
    triple_ici × feature_051==2 +0.318, p=0.026. Even PS=2 patients get a
    meaningful absolute response gain.

What was refuted (or not supported)
-----------------------------------
- Smoking marginally: feature_057 is not significantly associated with ORR
  overall (p=0.27); the smoking signal lives entirely in its interaction with
  PD-L1 and treatment.
- Histology marginally: squamous vs. adenocarcinoma do not differ
  significantly in marginal ORR (p=0.078).
- Insurance: no marginal ORR difference (p=0.23) and no insurance × triple
  ICI interaction.
- Age: no main effect of feature_078 on ORR (p=0.67), no interaction with
  triple ICI (p=0.78).
- Sex (feature_086): no main effect (p=0.72), no interaction with
  triple ICI.
- Comorbidity-like ordinal feature_064 (0-10): essentially no association
  with ORR, even within treatment subgroups.

Final multivariable model (n=50,000, AIC=44,355)
------------------------------------------------
objective_response ~ feature_013 + feature_067 + triple_ici*feature_092 +
                     feature_011 + C(feature_051) + C(feature_043) +
                     ever_smoker + feature_078

Coefficients (log-OR):
- feature_051==1: -0.37 (p<1e-13)         [ECOG-like]
- feature_051==2: -0.78 (p<1e-13)         [ECOG-like]
- feature_013:   -0.31 (p<1e-13)
- feature_067:   -0.26 (p<1e-13)
- triple_ici:    -0.56 (p<1e-9)           [main effect negative when PD-L1 low]
- feature_092:   -0.05 (p=0.39)           [no main effect]
- triple_ici × feature_092: +2.85 (p<1e-37)
- feature_011:   -0.040 per unit (p<1e-13)
- ever_smoker, squamous, age: not significant after adjustment

Overall conclusions
-------------------
This NSCLC cohort recapitulates the canonical immunotherapy paradigm:
(1) a few independent prognostic factors (a likely-ECOG ordinal, two
binary risk markers, and a likely-prior-therapy continuous marker) drive
baseline response probability; (2) a small group of immune-checkpoint-like
treatment indicators have only modest individual main effects but
synergize strongly with PD-L1 expression; (3) the strongest single
predictor in the dataset is the joint phenotype of triple-ICI plus
high PD-L1, which yields a near-three-fold ORR; (4) PD-L1 is purely
predictive (no prognostic effect in untreated patients) and its benefit
is attenuated in never-smokers; (5) treatment benefit appears uniform
across race, insurance, sex, age, and histology — no demographic
disparity in efficacy was detected, though prevalence of treatment use
across these groups was not the focus of this analysis. A
histology-specific signal (feature_104 in squamous) and a candidate
resistance marker (feature_027 attenuating ICI benefit) round out the
treatment-relevant findings.
