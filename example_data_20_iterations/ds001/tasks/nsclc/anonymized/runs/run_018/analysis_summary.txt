Analysis summary: ds001_nsclc (50,000 patients, binary outcome objective_response, baseline rate 16.9%)
=====================================================================================================

Approach
--------
Across 25 iterations, I (a) screened all 122 features against objective_response, (b) fit a multivariable logistic
regression on the most informative predictors, (c) tested a battery of pre-specified subgroup and interaction
contrasts motivated by the screen, and (d) checked for racial / insurance / age / histology / smoking-related
disparities in both the outcome and in receipt of the strongest positive predictor (feature_006). All p-values are
two-sided. Effect estimates are signed log-odds (beta) on the natural scale of the logistic model except where the
quoted estimate is a proportion or proportion difference.

Strongest main-effect predictors (univariate logistic, all in the same direction after multivariable adjustment)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Negative (lower probability of response):
  * feature_051 (ordinal 0/1/2, plausible performance-status-like): beta=-0.375 per level, OR=0.69, p=1.7e-93.
    Response 21.1% / 15.7% / 11.1% across levels 0/1/2 -- a clean monotonic gradient.
  * feature_013 (binary, 65% prevalent): beta=-0.295, OR=0.74, p=1.3e-33.
    Response 19.6% (=0) vs 15.4% (=1).
  * feature_011 (continuous 0-28): beta=-0.039 per unit, p=1.8e-32.
    Quintile rates: 18.9% -> 17.0% -> 15.8% -> 13.7% (monotonic).
  * feature_067 (binary, 25% prevalent): beta=-0.252, OR=0.78, p=2.0e-18.
  * feature_063 (continuous, right-skewed): adjusted beta=-0.005 per unit, p=5e-4.

Positive (higher probability of response):
  * feature_006 (binary, 28% prevalent): beta=+0.127 unadjusted, +0.130 adjusted, OR ~1.14, p=1e-6.
    Response 18.2% (=1) vs 16.4% (=0).
  * feature_099 (continuous): adjusted beta=+0.093, OR=1.10 per unit, p=1e-4.
  * feature_092 (continuous, range 0-0.8): unadjusted beta=+0.166, p=3e-3 (linear). The relationship is
    threshold-like: quartile rates 16.7% / 16.5% / 16.5% / 17.8% -- almost the entire signal sits in the top
    quartile/quintile rather than smoothly increasing.
  * feature_007 (binary): adjusted beta=+0.080, p=1e-3.
  * feature_039 (binary): adjusted beta=+0.077, p=1.5e-3.

Multivariable model with the top 18 features confirmed 7 features as independently significant (the ones above);
LLR p-value vs null was <1e-180 with pseudo-R^2 ~0.020 -- modest but well-defined explanatory signal at n=50,000.

Interactions and effect modification
------------------------------------
The most striking pattern was that feature_092 (continuous biomarker-like variable) consistently amplified the
positive effect of multiple binary predictors:
  * feature_006:feature_092  beta=+0.59, OR=1.81, p=1.6e-6
  * feature_007:feature_092  beta=+0.61, OR=1.85, p=7.3e-8
  * feature_039:feature_092  beta=+0.60, OR=1.81, p=1.7e-7
  * feature_013:feature_092  beta=+0.25, p=0.034 (smaller, weaker)
This is the kind of pattern expected if feature_092 is a biomarker that potentiates the benefit of certain
treatment-like exposures. The clinically interpretable strata: in the joint upper subgroup
(feature_006=1 AND feature_092 in the top quintile, n=2,927), response was 23.2% vs the cohort baseline of 16.9%,
a +6.3 percentage-point absolute gain (p<1e-15).

Several pre-specified interactions were *not* supported, despite the strong main effects of the components:
  * feature_006 x feature_051 (ECOG-like): interaction p=0.32. The relative effect of feature_006 is uniform
    across performance-status strata.
  * feature_006 x feature_013: p=0.19. No effect modification.
  * feature_006 x feature_011: p=0.17. No effect modification.
  * feature_006 x C(feature_043) (histology): p=0.71.
  * feature_006 x C(feature_057) (smoking): p>=0.46 for both contrasts.
The four binary 'treatment-like' features (006, 007, 013, 039) are pairwise independent (all chi-square p>=0.19),
so they appear to capture distinct, orthogonal exposures rather than overlapping receipts.

Demographic and access analyses (disparity checks)
--------------------------------------------------
* Race (feature_123, 5 categories): crude chi-square p=0.045 across all categories (rates ranged 16.5%-18.5%),
  but after adjustment for feature_051/013/011/067/006/092 no single race coefficient was significant (largest
  |z|=1.5, p=0.13). No racial disparity in objective_response after adjusting for clinical features.
* Insurance (feature_005): private vs medicaid beta=+0.088, p=0.020 (borderline) after adjustment; medicare
  and uninsured did not differ from medicaid. Magnitude is small (OR ~1.09).
* Age (feature_078): adjusted beta=+0.0003 per year, p=0.78 -- no association.
* Smoking (feature_057, never/former vs current): all contrasts p>=0.65. No association.
* Histology (feature_043, squamous vs adenocarcinoma): chi-square p=0.078 unadjusted; adjusted p=0.16.
* Receipt of feature_006 by race: chi-square p=0.67 (all groups 28%-30%). No disparity.
* Receipt of feature_006 by insurance: chi-square p=0.26. No disparity.

Hypotheses that were specifically refuted by the data
-----------------------------------------------------
* Age, smoking status, histology and race do not have independent effects on objective_response after adjustment.
* feature_064 (ordinal 0-10, plausible comorbidity-burden index) shows no monotonic association (univariate p=0.78;
  rates flat across the 0-10 range).
* feature_006 effect is not modified by performance status (feature_051), histology, smoking, or by feature_011.
* No racial or insurance-related disparities in receipt of the positive predictor feature_006.
* The four 'treatment-like' binary features (006, 007, 013, 039) are not collinear -- they capture independent
  exposures.

Bottom line
-----------
ds001_nsclc has a clear two-axis signal driving objective_response. A negative axis -- led by feature_051
(performance-status-like ordinal), feature_013 (a common binary), feature_011 (continuous count) and feature_067
-- depresses response, with tight monotonic dose-response on the ordinal/continuous variables. A positive axis --
led by feature_006, feature_092, feature_007, feature_039 and feature_099 -- elevates response, and feature_092
acts as a continuous biomarker that synergistically amplifies the binary positive predictors (feature_006,
feature_007, feature_039) with interaction p-values down to 7e-8. The combined feature_006=1 / high-feature_092
subgroup achieves a 23.2% response rate against the 16.9% cohort baseline. Demographic factors -- race, insurance,
age, smoking, histology -- do not retain independent associations with response and there is no evidence of
disparity in receipt of the strongest positive predictor.
