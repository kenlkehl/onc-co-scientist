Analysis Summary — ds001_nsclc (NSCLC anonymized cohort, n=50,000)
===================================================================

Outcome
-------
The single outcome examined is objective_response (binary: 1 = response, 0 = no response). The overall response rate in the cohort is 16.89% (8,447 / 50,000).

Approach
--------
Across 25 iterations I (a) screened every binary, ordinal, continuous, and categorical feature for univariate association with objective_response, (b) fit a full multivariable logistic regression to identify independent predictors, and (c) tested the most promising interactions and stratified subgroups. Tests were chi-square / logistic regression as appropriate; "significant" is reported at the conventional p < 0.05 threshold and direction is reported on the natural scale of the outcome.

Dominant predictors (large effects, p << 1e-15)
-----------------------------------------------
1. feature_051 (3-level ordinal, 0/1/2): the strongest predictor in the dataset.
   - Response rates by level: 21.08% (level 0, n=17,557), 15.70% (level 1, n=24,962), 11.05% (level 2, n=7,481).
   - Per-unit log-OR = -0.375 (OR 0.687), p = 1.7e-93. Hypothesis h1 strongly supported.
   - Behavior is consistent across smoking strata (no f051 x smoking interaction, p = 0.58, h23 supported) and across all 3 levels feature_011 retains its negative effect (h22 supported).

2. feature_013 (binary, prevalence 65%): negative.
   - RR 15.40% (==1) vs 19.64% (==0). OR 0.744, p = 1.1e-33 (h2). Effect is consistent across histologies (interaction p = 0.40, h20 supported).

3. feature_011 (continuous, range 0-28, median 3): negative — looks like a tumor-burden / count-of-sites variable.
   - Quartile RRs 18.5% / 16.3% / 14.2%. Univariate log-OR = -0.0385 per unit, p = 1.8e-32 (h4). Negative effect persists within every level of feature_051 (h22).

4. feature_067 (binary, prevalence 25%): negative.
   - RR 14.36% vs 17.75%. OR 0.777, p = 2.0e-18 (h3).

Smaller but independent predictors
----------------------------------
5. feature_006 (binary, prevalence 28.5%): positive. RR 18.19% vs 16.38% (OR 1.136, p = 1.0e-6, h5). Positive direction holds in every feature_051 stratum (h21 supported).

6. feature_007 (binary, prevalence ~45%): positive. RR 17.54% vs 16.37% (OR 1.086, p = 5.9e-4, h6).

7. feature_039 (binary, prevalence ~50%): positive. RR 17.39% vs 16.40% (OR 1.073, p = 3.4e-3, h7).

8. feature_099 (continuous): positive. log-OR = +0.091 per unit, p = 1.4e-4 (h8).

9. feature_063 (continuous): negative. log-OR = -0.0049 per unit, p = 6.9e-4 (h9).

10. feature_092 (continuous): positive. Univariate log-OR = +0.166, p = 3.2e-3; remains significant in fully adjusted model (p = 1.9e-3, h24).

All ten of these effects retain their direction and significance in the full multivariable logistic regression that includes all 125 features (h15 supported in detail).

Treatment-/biomarker-like synergy among feature_006, feature_007, feature_039
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
The three positive predictors do not act independently. Pairwise logistic interaction tests show super-additive joint effects:
- feature_006 x feature_039: interaction coef +0.184, p = 4.1e-4 (h16). Joint cell RR 19.67% versus 16.27% in the all-zero cell. The synergy survives adjustment for feature_051, feature_013, feature_067, feature_011, and feature_007 (adjusted interaction coef +0.176, LRT p = 7.9e-4).
- feature_007 x feature_039: interaction coef +0.146, p = 2.4e-3 (h17 supported).
- feature_006 x feature_007: interaction coef +0.149, p = 4.3e-3 (h18 supported).

Counting how many of the three are positive yields a clean dose-response that is not explained by a single feature:
0 markers RR 16.12%, 1 marker 16.42%, 2 markers 16.79%, 3 markers 22.98%.
Triple-positive vs triple-negative risk difference = +6.86 pp, chi-square p = 1.3e-18 (h19 supported). The triple-positive subgroup is the highest-responding non-feature_051-stratified subgroup in the cohort.

This pattern is consistent with a treatment-combination story (e.g. each of the three reflecting a different anti-cancer therapy or therapy class, with the joint effect of receiving all three exceeding the simple sum of effects).

Demographic and clinical-context features that do NOT predict response
----------------------------------------------------------------------
- Age (feature_078): log-OR per year +0.0005, p = 0.67 — null (h14 refuted).
- Smoking status (feature_057, never/former/current): chi-square p = 0.27 — null (h10 refuted).
- Insurance type (feature_005): chi-square p = 0.23 — null (h13 refuted).
- Histology (feature_043, adenocarcinoma vs squamous): borderline (p = 0.078, squamous +0.66 pp); in adjusted models, p = 0.17 (h11 not supported).
- Self-identified race/ethnicity (feature_123): unadjusted chi-square p = 0.045 with non-white groups slightly higher RR than white (16.5-18.5% range), but in the fully adjusted multivariable model no race dummy reaches p < 0.05. h12 weakly supported in unadjusted analysis only; the residual signal disappears once clinical features are controlled.

The summary multivariable model (h25) confirms: after adjusting for feature_051, feature_013, feature_067, feature_011, and the f006/f007/f039 triad, none of the demographic features (race, smoking, age, insurance, histology) reach p < 0.01. The favorable insurance signal (private vs. medicaid, p = 0.018) is the strongest remaining demographic association and is borderline.

Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
--------------------------------
Supported (effect direction and significance match prediction): h1, h2, h3, h4, h5, h6, h7, h8, h9, h15, h16, h17, h18, h19, h20 (no-interaction), h21, h22, h23 (no-interaction), h24, h25.
Refuted / null: h10 (smoking main effect), h13 (insurance main effect), h14 (age main effect).
Borderline / partial: h11 (histology direction matches but not significant), h12 (race overall p = 0.045 unadjusted, null after adjustment).

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. Response in this NSCLC cohort is dominated by a small set of clinical features. feature_051 (a 3-level ordinal that behaves like PD-L1 expression band, line of therapy, or ECOG performance status) is by far the strongest single predictor; higher levels carry roughly half the response rate of level 0.
2. feature_013 and feature_067 are strong negative binary predictors (likely poor-prognostic disease markers or unfavorable treatment indicators); feature_011 is a strong continuous negative predictor that behaves like tumor burden / number of metastatic sites.
3. The trio feature_006 + feature_007 + feature_039 act as positive predictors with statistically robust positive interactions; the triple-positive subgroup has a response rate of 23%, almost 7 pp above the triple-negative baseline. This pattern is consistent with combined treatment exposure rather than independent prognostic markers.
4. Demographic features (age, smoking, insurance, race, histology) contribute essentially no independent predictive signal once the clinical variables are accounted for. The cohort thus shows little evidence of differential response across demographic strata, but does show clear and reproducible signals for clinical/biomarker variables.
5. The favorable effects of feature_006 hold within every level of feature_051, and the unfavorable effects of feature_011 and feature_013 also operate consistently across subgroups, supporting the view that these are general predictors rather than subgroup-specific.
