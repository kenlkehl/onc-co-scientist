Oncology Dataset Analysis: ds001_nsclc (n=50,000; objective_response rate = 16.9%)
======================================================================================

OVERVIEW

I analyzed an EHR-derived NSCLC cohort of 50,000 patients with 123 anonymized
features (77 binary, 7 ordinal, 35 continuous, 4 categorical) and a single
binary outcome (objective_response). The cohort-wide response rate was 16.9%
(8447/50000). Across 25 iterations I screened all features univariately,
fit multivariable logistic regression models, and probed for interactions
among biomarkers, treatments, demographics, and clinical states. The
analysis identified one dominant treatment-biomarker interaction, several
independent prognostic features, and a small but robust racial outcome
disparity.

KEY FINDINGS

1. PERFORMANCE STATUS (feature_051) IS THE STRONGEST PREDICTOR.
   feature_051 is an ordinal 0/1/2 variable consistent with ECOG performance
   status. Response rates: PS0 21.1% (n=17,557), PS1 15.7% (n=24,962),
   PS2 11.1% (n=7,481). Univariate logit beta = -0.375 per unit
   (p = 1.7e-93); adjusted beta = -0.382 (p = 2e-95). The effect is
   monotonic and survives all adjustments.

2. THE 3-COMPONENT TRIPLET (feature_006 * feature_007 * feature_039) IS A
   BIOMARKER-GATED TREATMENT REGIMEN.
   Among 77 binary features, three (feature_006, feature_007, feature_039)
   have small individual positive effects on response, but a striking
   3-way interaction. Joint presence (n=3,216, 6.4% of cohort) raises
   response from 16.1% to 22.9%. Pairwise interactions: 006x039 beta=+0.184
   (p=4e-04); 007x039 beta=+0.146 (p=2e-03); 006x007 beta=+0.149 (p=4e-03).
   The triple-product interaction beta=+0.418 (p=7e-05, adjusted).

   This triplet's benefit is entirely explained by interaction with
   feature_092 (continuous, 0-0.8, mean 0.31), which behaves like a PD-L1
   tumor proportion score:

      In feature_092 < 0.5 (n=39,251):  triplet has NO effect
                                         (16.5% vs 16.8%, p=0.73)
      In feature_092 >= 0.5 (n=10,749): triplet 16.3% -> 44.8%
                                         (beta=+1.42, p=9e-71)
      In feature_092 >= 0.6 (n=5,165):  triplet 16.2% -> 71.0%
                                         (beta=+2.50, p=2e-77)

   The unadjusted triplet*feature_092 interaction beta=+0.594 (p=8e-38);
   adjusted for ECOG, feature_013, feature_067, feature_011, feature_099,
   feature_063, the interaction beta=+0.604 (p=3e-38). Each of the three
   binary components (006, 007, 039) individually shows a significant
   feature_092 interaction (p<1e-06 each), but no main effects survive once
   the joint triplet term is included - the entire signal lives in joint
   presence. This is the textbook pattern of a PD-L1-driven immunotherapy
   regimen (analogous to first-line pembrolizumab in PD-L1>=50% NSCLC).

   The biomarker effect is consistent across histologies: in adenocarcinoma
   feature_092>=0.5 + triplet -> 44.4%; in squamous -> 45.5%. The 3-way
   trip x squamous x feature_092 interaction is non-significant (p=0.54).

3. TWO INDEPENDENT NEGATIVE PREDICTORS (feature_013, feature_067) ACT
   ADDITIVELY.
   feature_013 (binary, 65% prevalence): adjusted beta=-0.305 (p=5e-35).
   Response 19.6% (=0) vs 15.4% (=1). feature_067 (binary, 25%): adjusted
   beta=-0.260 (p=5e-19). Response 17.7% (=0) vs 14.4% (=1). The two are
   independent (interaction p=0.51). feature_013's effect is consistent
   across histology strata (adeno -4.4pp, squamous -3.9pp).

   feature_034 (binary, 10% prevalence) significantly amplifies the
   negative feature_013 effect: f013 beta=-0.27 (p=3e-26) when f034=0
   versus beta=-0.49 (p=2e-10) when f034=1. The interaction beta=-0.215
   (p=8e-03). Among f034=1/f013=0 patients, response is 21.7% (highest
   four-way subgroup).

4. TUMOR BURDEN (feature_011) IS THE LEADING CONTINUOUS PREDICTOR.
   feature_011 ranges 0-28 (mean 3.84, SD 3.87, with 28% of patients at
   exactly 0). Adjusted beta=-0.153 per SD (p=1e-33). The zero-stratum has
   the highest response (19.3% vs 16.0% for >0). Response declines
   monotonically across deciles from 19.2% (lowest) to 13.2% (highest).
   No quadratic curvature detected (p=0.46). Effect is independent of the
   triplet (similar slope in triplet=0 and triplet=1 strata).

5. OTHER ROBUST CONTINUOUS EFFECTS.
   feature_099 (range 1.7-5.5, mean 3.8 - consistent with serum albumin):
   beta=+0.047 per SD (p=1e-04, adjusted). Higher values modestly favor
   response.
   feature_063 (large range, mean 6.1, max 296): beta=-0.046 per SD
   (p=4e-04, adjusted). Higher values disfavor response.
   feature_092 has no main effect after accounting for its triplet
   interaction (adjusted main-effect p=0.41); its association with
   response is conditional on the triplet treatment.

6. SEVERAL CONTINUOUS x ECOG INTERACTIONS DETECTED.
   feature_118 x feature_051 (beta=+0.059, p=0.001); feature_054 x
   feature_051 (beta=-0.048, p=0.008); feature_070 x feature_051
   (beta=+0.045, p=0.012); feature_096 x feature_051 (beta=+0.042,
   p=0.025). These suggest several lab/measurement variables modify the
   PS prognostic effect, though none are dominant individually.

7. A SMALL BUT ROBUST RACE DISPARITY FAVORS NON-WHITE PATIENTS.
   White (n=32,607) response rate 16.5% vs non-white 17.4%. After
   adjustment for ECOG, feature_013, feature_067, feature_011, the triplet,
   smoking, age, and insurance, white-vs-nonwhite beta=-0.066 to -0.069
   (p ~0.007-0.01). The disparity is uniform across strata: white-vs-
   nonwhite differences are -1.2pp (PS0), -0.7pp (PS1), -1.1pp (PS2);
   -0.6pp (f013=0), -1.2pp (f013=1); -1.0pp in both triplet=0 and
   triplet=1 subgroups. The disparity is NOT explained by differential
   triplet uptake (prevalence is 5.8-6.6% across all racial groups,
   p>0.5). Among individual races vs white, black is significantly
   higher (beta=+0.085, p=0.021); other races trend similarly but with
   smaller samples.

8. NULL OR WEAK ASSOCIATIONS.
   - Patient age (feature_078, 30-90, mean 65): no association
     (beta=+0.005/SD, p=0.67). Response rate is essentially flat across
     deciles.
   - Smoking status (current/former/never): no significant overall effect
     (chi-square p=0.27); never-smokers numerically lowest at 16.4%.
   - Insurance type (feature_005, medicare reference): no significant
     adjusted effect (medicaid p=0.09, private p=0.32, uninsured p=0.86).
   - Histology (feature_043): squamous has 17.4% vs adeno 16.7%; adjusted
     beta=+0.04 (p=0.16, NS). Importantly, histology does NOT modify the
     triplet x feature_092 interaction.
   - feature_007 alone (prevalence 45%, distribution independent of
     smoking/age/race/ECOG - consistent with a sex variable): no main
     effect once the triplet is included.

9. SUBGROUP HETEROGENEITY: NEVER-SMOKERS DIFFER.
   In never smokers (n=7,459), the feature_092 main effect is REVERSED
   (high feature_092 beta=-0.18, p=0.026). Among never-smoker triplet
   recipients (n=139 overall, n=34 with feature_092>=0.5), response is
   only 38.2%, lower than the 45-46% seen in ever-smoker high-PD-L1
   strata. This pattern is consistent with NSCLC biology in never-smokers
   being driver-mutation-driven rather than PD-L1-driven, although the
   subgroup is small. feature_013 and feature_067 retain their negative
   effects in never smokers (beta=-0.24 and -0.24, p<0.002 each).

CLINICAL PICTURE SUMMARIZED

The dataset is consistent with a contemporary metastatic NSCLC cohort in
which a substantial fraction of patients receive a PD-L1-driven
immunotherapy regimen represented by the joint indicator
feature_006 * feature_007 * feature_039. The continuous biomarker
feature_092 closely resembles PD-L1 expression on a 0-1 scale, with the
threshold for clinically meaningful triplet benefit lying near 0.5 (and
dramatic benefit above 0.6). Worse performance status, larger tumor
burden, lower albumin-like value (feature_099), and two independent
negative biomarkers (feature_013, feature_067) all reduce response.
Demographic effects are modest: age, smoking, and insurance show no
adjusted effects, but white patients have a small persistent ~1
percentage point lower response rate vs non-white patients that is not
explained by the clinical or treatment covariates measured.

The predicted response rate spans roughly 9% (worst-case: PS2, both
negative biomarkers, no triplet, low feature_092) to 54% (best-case: PS0,
clean prognostic profile, triplet on, high feature_092), illustrating the
combined power of biomarker-gated therapy and prognostic baseline.

HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED VS REFUTED

Supported (significant in expected direction):
  - feature_051 -> lower response (h3.1)
  - feature_011 -> lower response (h2.2, h4.1)
  - feature_013 -> lower response (h1.2)
  - feature_067 -> lower response (h1.1)
  - feature_006 -> higher response, mainly via triplet (h1.3, h7.1)
  - feature_099 -> higher response (h2.3, h22.2)
  - 006/007/039 3-way interaction (h6.1, h7.1, h7.2)
  - triplet x feature_092 interaction (h14.1, h14.2, h14.3, h15.1, h15.2)
  - feature_013 x feature_034 interaction (h9.1, h9.2)
  - White vs non-white residual disparity (h10.1, h11.1)
  - Several continuous x ECOG interactions (h13.1, h13.2)

Refuted/Not significant:
  - Age (feature_078) effect (h5.2)
  - Smoking effect (h3.4)
  - Histology main effect (h3.3)
  - Insurance effect after adjustment (h11.2)
  - feature_011 quadratic non-linearity (h4.2)
  - Triplet x histology interaction (h8.1)
  - Triplet x ECOG, triplet x smoking interactions (h8.2, h8.3)
  - 3-way trip x sq x feature_092 interaction (h17.1)
  - Triplet x race interaction (h21.1)

LIMITATIONS

Features are de-identified, so clinical interpretations (PD-L1, ECOG,
albumin, etc.) are inferred from distributional properties and effect
patterns rather than confirmed labels. The cohort size (n=50,000) gives
high power; modest effect sizes (e.g., race ~1pp) reach significance even
when clinical importance is small. The cohort is observational, so
causal interpretation of treatment effects (esp. the triplet) requires
caution despite the magnitude of the biomarker-gated effect.
