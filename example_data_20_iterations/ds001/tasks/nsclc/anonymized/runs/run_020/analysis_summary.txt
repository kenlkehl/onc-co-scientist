Oncology dataset ds001_nsclc — analysis summary
===================================================

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC-like patient records, 125 columns (1 patient_id, 1 outcome
"objective_response", 123 anonymized features). Outcome prevalence: 16.89%
(8,447/50,000). No missing values.

Variable types after inspection:
  - 77 binary integer features (one had only 286 patients positive; most are
    well populated)
  - 7 multi-level integer features (3-11 levels)
  - 35 continuous floats (incl. distributions consistent with age, BMI/weight,
    biomarkers, lab values)
  - 4 object/categorical: feature_005 (insurance), feature_043 (histology:
    adenocarcinoma vs squamous), feature_057 (smoking: current/former/never),
    feature_123 (race: white/black/hispanic/asian/other).
  - patient_id (string, 50k unique).

I worked through 25 iterations of propose -> test -> refine. The headline
findings are the dominant prognostic main effects (feature_051, feature_013,
feature_067, feature_011) and a striking treatment-biomarker interaction in
which features 006/007/039 jointly modify the effect of feature_092 on
response.


1. Strong main effects on objective_response
--------------------------------------------

Three-level ordinal feature_051 (response 0.211 / 0.157 / 0.111 across levels
0/1/2) shows the strongest single signal in the dataset. Linear-in-level
logistic slope beta = -0.375 per level, p = 1.7e-93. The unadjusted PS0 vs
PS2 chi-square is 3.1e-79. The shape (monotone decline, three levels with
the largest stratum at level 1) is consistent with an ECOG performance-status
covariate.

feature_013 (binary, prevalence 65%): unadjusted response 15.4% vs 19.6%,
chi-square p = 1.1e-33; multivariable beta = -0.302 (OR ~ 0.74), p = 1.9e-34.
A common, strongly negative covariate.

feature_067 (binary, prevalence 25%): unadjusted response 14.4% vs 17.7%;
multivariable beta = -0.259 (OR ~ 0.77), p = 5.0e-19. Plausibly an adverse
metastatic-site or comorbidity indicator.

feature_011 (continuous, range 0-28, ~28% with value 0): higher values reduce
response. Standardized logistic beta = -0.149 per SD, p = 1.8e-32. Patients
with feature_011 = 0 have response 0.193 vs 0.160 for feature_011 > 0
(p = 3.3e-19). The mass at zero plus the long right tail is characteristic
of a count-like covariate (e.g. lines of prior therapy, number of metastatic
sites, or pack-years).

feature_063 (continuous, right-skewed, mean 6, max 297): negative.
log1p-transformed beta = -0.071, p = 1.8e-6. The strong skew suggests an
inflammatory marker (LDH ratio, CRP, NLR).

feature_099 (continuous, ~normal, range 1.7-5.5, mean 3.8): positive,
beta = +0.046 per SD, p = 1.2e-4. Distribution is consistent with serum
albumin (g/dL).

feature_006, feature_007, feature_039 (binary, prevalences 28%, 45%, 50%):
each with a positive adjusted main effect. Coefficients +0.130, +0.080,
+0.079 respectively (p = 1.9e-6, 9.1e-4, 1.1e-3). Hovering near OR = 1.08-1.14.

feature_092 (continuous, range 0-0.80 with a spike at 0): positive main
effect (beta = +0.037 per SD, p = 2.3e-3) but the main effect is small;
its real importance shows up in interactions (next section).


2. The dominant interaction: combo (006 & 007 & 039) x feature_092
------------------------------------------------------------------

Pairwise scans of interactions among the top ten covariates revealed three
unusually strong signals: feature_007 x feature_092 (p = 6.9e-8),
feature_039 x feature_092 (p = 1.6e-7), and feature_006 x feature_092
(p = 1.6e-6). All three carried the same sign (positive) and similar
magnitude (beta ~ +0.13 per SD).

Cross-tabs showed feature_006/007/039 are statistically independent: the
observed all-three-on prevalence (6.43%) matches the product of the marginals
(6.37%). So they are three separate features rather than aliases of the
same construct.

A model with the full 3-way 006*007*039 product term yielded a strong
positive triple-way coefficient (beta = +0.42, p = 7.3e-5) on top of small
non-significant pairwise terms.

Defining combo = (feature_006=1) & (feature_007=1) & (feature_039=1), the
combo x feature_092 interaction is enormous: beta = +0.605 per SD,
p = 2.9e-38. The 2x2 stratification on combo and feature_092>0.6 makes the
interaction concrete:

   combo  f092>0.6   n        response rate
   0       0         41,971   0.165
   0       1          4,813   0.162
   1       0          2,864   0.171
   1       1            352   0.710

Restricting further to combo=1, f092>0.6, feature_013=0 (n=124) the response
rate is 0.726. Outside the combo=1 & f092-high cell, response barely budges
from baseline. This is a textbook "predictive biomarker x multi-component
treatment" pattern: the treatment indicator (006/007/039 together) is only
useful when the biomarker (feature_092) is sufficiently expressed.

By contrast, feature_051 (PS-like) does NOT modify these treatment/biomarker
effects: f051 x f006, f051 x f007, f051 x f039, and f051 x f092 interaction
p-values are 0.29, 0.14, 0.34, 0.84 respectively. PS is a strong main effect
but not an effect modifier.

A weaker but real interaction was detected for feature_013 x feature_092
(beta = +0.052, p = 0.034). It is at least an order of magnitude smaller
than the combo x f092 effect.


3. Variables with no meaningful adjusted effect
-----------------------------------------------

Several covariates plausibly representing demographics or simple disease
descriptors did NOT have a meaningful adjusted relationship with response:

  * feature_078 (continuous, 30-90, mean 65; almost certainly age in years):
    adjusted beta = +0.003 per SD, p = 0.80.
  * feature_123 (race; 5 levels with white the largest): unadjusted LRT
    p = 0.047 but every adjusted coefficient is non-significant
    (white -0.078 p=0.13; hispanic -0.041 p=0.47; black +0.004 p=0.94;
    other +0.045 p=0.63). Whatever apparent racial difference exists
    unadjusted is explained by the clinical covariates.
  * feature_057 (smoking status): current 0.172, former 0.168, never 0.164;
    adjusted p > 0.85 for every contrast. No effect.
  * feature_043 (squamous vs adenocarcinoma): adjusted beta +0.037, p=0.17.
    No effect.
  * feature_005 (insurance): only private vs medicaid is significant
    (beta = +0.089, p = 0.019), and the magnitude (OR ~ 1.09) is small.
    Most of the unadjusted insurance gradient disappears after adjustment.


4. Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
-----------------------------------

Supported (significant in the predicted direction):
  - h2: feature_051 monotone negative ordinal effect.
  - h3: feature_013 negative main effect.
  - h4: feature_067 negative main effect.
  - h5: feature_011 negative continuous effect.
  - h6/h7/h8: feature_006/007/039 positive main effects.
  - h9: feature_099 positive (likely albumin).
  - h10: feature_063 negative (likely inflammatory marker).
  - h11: feature_092 positive (small main effect; large in interactions).
  - h12/h13/h14: each of feature_006/007/039 amplifies the feature_092
    response slope.
  - h15: features 006/007/039 are mutually independent (so they describe
    three real, distinct features).
  - h16: positive 3-way 006*007*039 interaction.
  - h17/h18: combo (all three) x feature_092 interaction is enormous; the
    high-response subgroup is concentrated in combo=1 + f092 high.
  - h20: feature_013 x feature_092 (small but nominally significant).
  - h24: feature_005 private-vs-medicaid (small).

Refuted / not supported:
  - h19: feature_051 modifying treatment/biomarker effects (not significant).
  - h21: feature_043 (histology) main effect.
  - h22: feature_057 (smoking) main effect.
  - h23: feature_123 (race), feature_078 (age) effects.

Conclusion: This NSCLC-like cohort behaves like a "treatment plus predictive
biomarker" benchmark, layered on top of a conventional prognostic model
(performance status feature_051, plus the negative covariates
feature_013/067/011/063 and the positive covariates feature_099/006/007/039).
The single most clinically useful signal is the combo = (006 & 007 & 039)
indicator together with feature_092: when combo is on and feature_092 is in
the top decile, the response rate quadruples (0.17 -> 0.71). Demographic and
historical variables (age, race, smoking, histology, insurance) carry little
or no independent signal once the clinical covariates are included.
