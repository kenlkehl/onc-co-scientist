ds001_nsclc — Analysis Summary
==============================

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patient records. Outcome: objective_response (binary; overall ORR 16.9%).
Four study treatments — treatment_pembrolizumab (50.0% exposed), treatment_sotorasib (35.1%),
treatment_olaparib (30.0%), treatment_osimertinib (30.0%) — overlap freely (most patients receive
1–2; some receive 0 or all 4).

Iterations 1–25 explored the propose-test-refine loop. The headline finding is that, of the four
treatments, only pembrolizumab has a detectable effect on ORR, and that effect concentrates in
canonical immunotherapy-favorable subgroups; the three nominally targeted agents show no benefit
even inside their canonical biomarker indications.

A. PROGNOSTIC MAIN EFFECTS (all confirmed at p << 0.001)
--------------------------------------------------------
- ecog_ps:           higher ECOG → lower ORR (mean 0.66 in responders vs 0.83 in non-responders;
                     p=7.9e-95). Strongest single ORR predictor in the dataset.
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: higher weight loss → lower ORR (mean diff -0.55%, p=1.2e-32).
- stage_iv:          stage IV → lower ORR (15.4% vs 19.6%; RD=-0.042, p=1.1e-33).
- has_brain_mets:    brain mets → lower ORR (14.4% vs 17.7%; RD=-0.034, p=2.0e-18).
- albumin_g_dl:      higher albumin → higher ORR (small, p=1.4e-04).
- crp_mg_l:          higher CRP → lower ORR (small, p=6.8e-04).
- tmb_high:          TMB-high → higher ORR (18.2% vs 16.4%; RD=+0.018, p=1.0e-06).
- sex_female:        female sex → higher ORR (17.5% vs 16.4%; RD=+0.012, p=5.9e-04).
- pdl1_tps:          continuous PD-L1 → higher ORR (logistic coef +0.17, p=2e-03 adjusted).

In a multivariable logistic model (n=50,000), the independent predictors with p<0.01 are:
ecog_ps, stage_iv, weight_loss_pct_6mo, has_brain_mets, tmb_high, albumin_g_dl, crp_mg_l,
sex_female, treatment_pembrolizumab, and pdl1_tps. Notably, egfr_mutation, kras_g12c,
stk11_mutation, keap1_mutation, tp53_mutation, alk_fusion, and the non-pembrolizumab treatments
are all NOT significant after adjustment.

B. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS
-------------------------
- treatment_pembrolizumab: ORR 17.4% on vs 16.4% off; RD=+0.010, p=3.4e-03 (overall, unadjusted).
                           Adjusted logistic coef +0.076 (OR≈1.08), p=1.6e-03.
- treatment_sotorasib:     ORR 16.9% vs 16.9%; RD=+0.001, p=0.91. NULL.
- treatment_olaparib:      ORR 17.0% vs 16.9%; RD=+0.001, p=0.83. NULL.
- treatment_osimertinib:   ORR 16.5% vs 17.0%; RD=-0.005, p=0.18. NULL (slightly negative).

C. CANONICAL TREATMENT–BIOMARKER PAIRINGS — REFUTED for three of four
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The expected real-world pattern (osimertinib in EGFR+, sotorasib in KRAS G12C+, olaparib in
BRCA2+) is NOT reproduced in this dataset:
  - Osimertinib | EGFR+: ORR 16.4% vs 16.6%, p=0.87. Even within ECOG 0-1, p=0.78. Within
                          EGFR+ never-smokers, p=0.36. Inert in EGFR+.
  - Sotorasib  | KRAS G12C+: ORR 17.5% vs 16.6%, p=0.43. Within ECOG 0-1, p=0.41. Inert.
  - Olaparib   | BRCA2+: ORR 14.6% vs 16.8%, p=0.35. Within ECOG 0-1, p=0.29. Inert (trend to
                          worse ORR).
  - Pembrolizumab | TMB-high: ORR 19.7% vs 16.7%, p=5.4e-06. CONFIRMED — the canonical pairing
                               that does work in this dataset.

D. PEMBROLIZUMAB EFFECT MODIFIERS (where the action is)
-------------------------------------------------------
Stratified analyses and formal logistic interactions converge on a coherent ICI-biology pattern:
  - pdl1_tps >= 0.5: pembro RD=+0.052 (20.7% vs 15.5%, p=9.3e-13). pdl1_tps<0.01: RD=-0.002
    (p=0.85). Logistic interaction coef +0.378, p=5.7e-11.
  - smoking_status:  ever-smokers benefit (current RD=+0.012 p=0.048; former RD=+0.011 p=0.017);
    never-smokers do not (RD=+0.001, p=0.91).
  - stk11_mutation:  WT benefits (RD=+0.014, p=9.7e-05); mutant trends toward harm
    (RD=-0.015, p=0.084). Interaction coef -0.202, p=3.0e-03.
  - sex_female:      women benefit (RD=+0.021, p=2.6e-05); men null (RD=+0.000, p=0.94).
    Interaction coef +0.145, p=2.6e-03. (An unusual finding worth flagging.)
  - ecog_ps:         benefit in ECOG 0 (+0.016, p=0.010) and ECOG 1 (+0.009, p=0.046); none
    in ECOG 2 (RD=+0.001, p=0.91).
  - keap1_mutation:  consistent direction (WT +0.011 p=2.4e-03 vs mut +0.003 p=0.73), but the
    formal interaction is not significant (p=0.47).
  - histology, age, brain mets, liver mets, stage: no significant interactions.

Composite favorable subgroup (pdl1_tps>=0.5 AND stk11_mutation=0 AND smoking_status!='never'):
ORR 22.6% on pembrolizumab (n=3,886) vs 15.8% off (n=3,927); RD=+0.068, p=2.3e-14. The largest
treatment effect identified.

Three-way structure (PD-L1 × STK11 × pembrolizumab):
  - PD-L1 high & STK11 wt:  RD=+0.064, p=5.6e-15  ← strong benefit
  - PD-L1 high & STK11 mut: RD=-0.005, p=0.86     ← no benefit (STK11 abrogates the effect)
  - PD-L1 low  & STK11 wt:  RD=+0.001, p=0.87     ← no benefit
  - PD-L1 low  & STK11 mut: RD=-0.018, p=0.07     ← borderline harm
Pembrolizumab is essentially active only in the PD-L1-high, STK11 wild-type quadrant.

E. RARE BIOMARKERS AND SNPs (essentially null)
----------------------------------------------
- Rare driver biomarkers (braf_v600e, met_exon14_skipping, ret_fusion, ros1_fusion,
  her2_amplification, msi_high, ntrk_fusion, nrg1_fusion, fgfr_alteration) show no main effect
  on ORR and no significant treatment-by-biomarker interaction. The single nominally significant
  signal — osimertinib in BRAF V600E (RD=-0.058, p=0.04) — is most plausibly chance among ~24
  treatment-by-rare-biomarker comparisons.
- 23 SNPs (snp_rs*): only snp_rs7412 (p=0.018) and snp_rs1050828 (p=0.031) reach nominal p<0.05;
  none survive Bonferroni correction (alpha 0.0022). SNPs are not informative for ORR here.

F. DEMOGRAPHICS / SOCIAL DETERMINANTS
-------------------------------------
- race_ethnicity: unadjusted chi-sq p=0.045 (white 16.5% lowest; other 18.5% highest), but no
  individual race coefficient is significant (smallest p≈0.11) after adjusting for ECOG, stage,
  albumin, weight loss, PD-L1, TMB, and pembrolizumab. The unadjusted association is driven by
  confounding with clinical risk profile.
- insurance_type: overall p=0.23 unadjusted; private vs medicaid coef +0.087 (p=0.022) after
  adjustment. Modest effect at most.
- rural_residence: not significant.
- pembrolizumab × race: trend toward larger benefit in non-white groups but no formal subgroup
  reached significance individually.

G. COMORBIDITIES & SYMPTOMS (largely null)
------------------------------------------
- Ten cardiometabolic / pulmonary / autoimmune / psych comorbidities: all p>0.05 except a
  borderline coronary_artery_disease (RD=-0.009, p=0.051).
- Patient-reported symptoms: only fatigue_grade is borderline (p=0.04); pain, dyspnea, cough,
  appetite-loss grades are all null. Most of the "symptom" prognostic information is captured by
  ecog_ps.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1) The dataset reproduces classical NSCLC prognostic markers for ORR very crisply — performance
   status, weight loss, stage IV, brain mets, albumin, CRP, TMB-high, sex, and PD-L1 are the
   independent ORR predictors after adjustment.

2) Of the four treatments, only pembrolizumab has a detectable effect on objective response, and
   that effect is concentrated in canonical immunotherapy-favorable strata: PD-L1 high
   (especially TPS≥50%), TMB-high, ever-smokers, STK11 wild-type, ECOG 0–1, and (less expectedly)
   in women rather than men. The single most predictive composite is PD-L1 high × STK11 wt × ever
   smoker, where ORR rises from ~15.8% to ~22.6% with pembrolizumab.

3) The three "targeted" agents (osimertinib, sotorasib, olaparib) show no benefit even within
   their canonical biomarker indications (EGFR, KRAS G12C, BRCA2 respectively), and their main
   effects are null after multivariable adjustment. This is a striking contrast to real-world
   NSCLC pharmacology and suggests the dataset was generated with predictive activity assigned
   selectively to pembrolizumab while the other three agents act as null/decoy treatments.

4) Demographic, comorbidity, symptom, and SNP variables contribute little after adjusting for
   the core clinical/biomarker predictors. Apparent race-ORR differences are confounded by
   clinical risk profile.

5) Refuted hypotheses (worth recording): osimertinib benefit in EGFR mutation; sotorasib benefit
   in KRAS G12C; olaparib benefit in BRCA2; SNP main effects; comorbidity main effects; race
   independent ORR effects; pembrolizumab-by-age modification; pembrolizumab-by-brain-mets
   modification.
