Analysis Summary — ds001_nsclc (50,000 patients, binary outcome objective_response; baseline ORR ≈ 16.9%)
Harness: claude-opus-4-7 / claude-code, 25 iterations.

================================================================
OVERVIEW
================================================================
The cohort comprises 50,000 NSCLC patients with rich clinico-genomic features and a single binary outcome (objective_response). Approximately half receive each of four therapies (pembrolizumab, sotorasib, olaparib, osimertinib), allowing main-effect and treatment×biomarker interaction analyses. Across 25 iterations I tested treatment main effects, classical NSCLC biomarker–therapy pairings, immunotherapy modifier mutations (STK11, KEAP1), prognostic clinical/laboratory variables, demographic/SES variables, SNPs, and a multivariable adjusted model. Findings concentrate strongly around the pembrolizumab–biomarker axis and the canonical prognostic markers; the matched targeted-therapy interactions that one would expect from clinical trials (osimertinib×EGFR, sotorasib×KRAS_G12C, olaparib×BRCA2) were NOT detected in this cohort.

================================================================
TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS (Iteration 1)
================================================================
- treatment_pembrolizumab: small but significant absolute ORR increase (+1.0 percentage point; 17.4% vs 16.4%; chi2 p = 0.0034). Supports h1.1.
- treatment_sotorasib, treatment_olaparib, treatment_osimertinib: no significant main effect on ORR (all p > 0.18). h1.2–h1.4 NOT supported by main effects.

The lack of main effect for the targeted agents is expected if benefit is concentrated only within the biomarker-matched subpopulation. This motivated the interaction tests in iterations 2–6.

================================================================
PEMBROLIZUMAB × IMMUNE BIOMARKERS (Iterations 2–3, 24)
================================================================
- pdl1_tps: continuous positive interaction with pembrolizumab (logit interaction coef +0.591, p = 1.6e-7). Within PDL1 ≥ 0.5: ORR with pembro 20.9% vs 15.6% without (diff +5.4 pp, p = 7.7e-13). Strongly supports h2.1, h2.2.
- tmb_high: positive interaction with pembrolizumab (interaction coef +0.184, p = 4e-4). In TMB-high: pembro 19.7% vs 16.7%, diff +3.0 pp (p = 5e-6). Supports h3.1.
- Combined enrichment (PDL1 ≥ 0.5 AND tmb_high = 1): pembro raises ORR from 15.5% to 29.4% — a +13.9 pp absolute gain (p = 1.9e-20). Strongly supports h24.1; the additive PDL1+TMB stratum identifies a subgroup with markedly amplified pembrolizumab benefit.

These results are highly consistent with the established immunotherapy literature: PD-L1 expression and TMB are independent positive predictive biomarkers of response to PD-1 blockade.

================================================================
PEMBROLIZUMAB × IMMUNE-RESISTANCE MUTATIONS (Iterations 7–8, 25)
================================================================
- stk11_mutation: NEGATIVE interaction with pembrolizumab (logit interaction coef −0.211, p = 1.8e-3). In STK11+ patients pembrolizumab does not increase ORR (15.8% vs 17.3%, diff −1.5 pp, p = 0.084), whereas in STK11– patients pembrolizumab confers a clear benefit. Supports h7.1. The negative interaction persists in the multivariable adjusted model (coef −0.205, p = 0.0026).
- keap1_mutation: nominal trend toward negative interaction with pembrolizumab (coef −0.058, p = 0.36 unadjusted; coef ≈ −0.054, p = 0.40 adjusted). h8.1 NOT supported in this dataset. The biological signal exists in literature but did not separate from noise in the present cohort.

================================================================
TARGETED THERAPY × MATCHED BIOMARKER (Iterations 4–6)
================================================================
A surprising negative result for the dataset:
- osimertinib × egfr_mutation: no interaction (coef +0.024, p = 0.76). Within EGFR+ patients, osimertinib ORR 16.4% vs 16.6% without (p = 0.87). h4.1, h4.2 NOT supported.
- sotorasib × kras_g12c: no significant interaction (coef +0.059, p = 0.43). Within KRAS_G12C+ patients, sotorasib ORR 17.5% vs 16.6% without (p = 0.43). h5.1, h5.2 NOT supported.
- olaparib × brca2_mutation: no positive interaction; trend in opposite direction (coef −0.177, p = 0.27). Within BRCA2+ patients, olaparib ORR 14.6% vs 16.8% (p = 0.35). h6.1, h6.2 NOT supported.

Either the data-generating process for this cohort does not encode the canonical drug–biomarker matching effects (a feature of the synthesis rather than real clinical biology), or the biomarker prevalences/randomization mask the effect. Either way, the empirical evidence in *this* dataset is null for these matched-pair hypotheses.

================================================================
PROGNOSTIC CLINICAL/LABORATORY VARIABLES (Iterations 9–13, 25)
================================================================
- ecog_ps (Iteration 9): strong inverse association (coef −0.375 per unit, p = 1.7e-93). ORR by ECOG: 0 → 21.1%, 1 → 15.7%, 2 → 11.1%. Strongly supports h9.1.
- albumin_g_dl (Iteration 10): positive (+0.091 per g/dL, p = 1.4e-4). Supports h10.1.
- ldh_u_l (Iteration 10): no significant association (p = 0.51). h10.2 NOT supported.
- crp_mg_l (Iteration 10): negative (−0.0049 per mg/L, p = 6.9e-4). Supports h10.3.
- nlr (Iteration 11): no significant association (p = 0.38). h11.1 NOT supported.
- weight_loss_pct_6mo (Iteration 12): strongly negative (−0.039 per percentage point, p = 1.8e-32). Supports h12.1.
- has_brain_mets (Iteration 13): negative (−3.4 pp ORR, p = 2.0e-18). Supports h13.1.
- liver_mets, bone_mets (Iteration 13): no association. h13.2, h13.3 NOT supported.
- stage_iv (Iteration 25): negative (−4.2 pp ORR, p = 1.1e-33). Supports h25.1.
- fatigue_grade (Iteration 25): borderline negative (coef −0.022, p = 0.040). Marginally supports h25.2.

The classical "prognostic quartet" of ECOG, albumin, CRP, and weight loss are all detected with very large statistical significance and the expected direction; they likely act as confounders for any observed treatment effects.

================================================================
DEMOGRAPHICS, HISTOLOGY, OTHER MUTATIONS (Iterations 14–18, 20–22)
================================================================
- smoking_status (Iteration 14): no overall association (chi2 p = 0.27); within pembrolizumab-treated patients, ever- vs never-smoker non-significant trend (+1.1 pp, p = 0.10). h14.1, h14.2 NOT supported.
- histology (Iteration 15): squamous vs adeno NS (+0.7 pp, p = 0.078). h15.1 NOT supported (trend only).
- sex_female (Iteration 16): female ORR 17.5% vs male 16.4% (p = 5.9e-4); positive sex × pembro interaction (coef +0.146, p = 0.0024). Supports h16.1, h16.2 — females respond better, especially on pembrolizumab.
- age_years (Iteration 17): no association (p = 0.67). h17.1 NOT supported.
- tp53_mutation (Iteration 18): no association (p = 0.88). h18.1 NOT supported.
- race_ethnicity (Iteration 20): borderline overall difference (chi2 p = 0.045); white patients have somewhat lower ORR (16.5%) than black (17.8%) and asian (17.7%). Marginally supports h20.1.
- insurance_type (Iteration 21): no association (p = 0.23). h21.1 NOT supported.
- rural_residence (Iteration 21): no association (p = 0.50). h21.2 NOT supported.
- alk_fusion within pembrolizumab (Iteration 22): no difference (p = 0.71). h22.1 NOT supported.

================================================================
SNP SCAN (Iteration 19)
================================================================
Tested 23 SNP loci individually. Two SNPs were nominally significant at p < 0.05 (minimum p = 0.018 for snp_rs7412), but ZERO survived the Bonferroni threshold of 0.05/23 = 0.00217. h19.1 (no SNP signal after multiple-comparison correction) is supported.

The hypothesis that the matched targeted-therapy interactions (osimertinib×EGFR, sotorasib×KRAS_G12C, olaparib×BRCA2) would survive adjustment (h23.1) is REFUTED in this dataset. The pembrolizumab biomarker signature, however, is highly robust to adjustment.

================================================================
SYNTHESIS
================================================================
The cohort behaves as a relatively classical immunotherapy-predictive-biomarker dataset:

1. Pembrolizumab efficacy is concentrated in PD-L1-high and TMB-high tumors and is attenuated in STK11-mutant tumors. This recapitulates well-known clinical observations and yields the largest absolute ORR differences in the dataset (up to +14 pp in PDL1≥0.5 + TMB-high).
2. Female sex independently predicts higher ORR and amplifies pembrolizumab benefit in this cohort.
3. Classical clinical prognostics dominate: poor performance status (ECOG), low albumin, elevated CRP, weight loss, brain metastases, and stage IV all reduce response, with very large effect sizes / vanishingly small p-values.
4. Targeted therapies (osimertinib, sotorasib, olaparib) show no biomarker-matched benefit in this dataset. This is a notable discrepancy from real-world NSCLC oncology.
5. Demographic features (race, insurance, rural residence) and SNPs do not meaningfully predict response after correction.

Refuted/unsupported hypotheses (selected): h1.2–h1.4 (sotorasib/olaparib/osimertinib main effects), h4.1–h4.2 (osi×EGFR), h5.1–h5.2 (soto×KRAS_G12C), h6.1–h6.2 (ola×BRCA2), h8.1 (KEAP1×pembro), h10.2 (LDH), h11.1 (NLR), h13.2–h13.3 (liver/bone mets), h14.1–h14.2 (smoking), h15.1 (histology), h17.1 (age), h18.1 (TP53), h21.1–h21.2 (insurance, rural), h22.1 (ALK in pembro), h23.1 partially (matched targeted interactions did not survive adjustment).

Supported hypotheses (selected): h1.1 (pembro main), h2.1–h2.2 (PDL1 modifier), h3.1 (TMB modifier), h7.1 (STK11 negative modifier), h9.1 (ECOG), h10.1 (albumin), h10.3 (CRP), h12.1 (weight loss), h13.1 (brain mets), h16.1–h16.2 (sex effects), h19.1 (no SNP signal after correction), h20.1 (marginal race difference), h24.1 (combined PDL1+TMB enrichment in pembro), h25.1 (stage IV), h25.2 (fatigue, borderline), h25.3 (pembro×STK11 and pembro×PDL1 robust to adjustment).

In short: the dataset's strongest, most reproducible signal is the pembrolizumab biomarker triad — PD-L1 (positive), TMB (positive), STK11 (negative) — combined with the universal NSCLC prognostic quartet (ECOG, albumin, CRP, weight loss), with no detectable benefit from biomarker-matched targeted therapies in this cohort.
