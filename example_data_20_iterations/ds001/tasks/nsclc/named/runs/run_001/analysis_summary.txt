ds001_nsclc — Analysis Summary
================================

Cohort: 50,000 patient records with 124 features and a binary outcome
(objective_response). Overall ORR = 0.169 (8,447/50,000). Treatments
(pembrolizumab, sotorasib, olaparib, osimertinib) appear to be assigned
roughly 50:50 with no obvious confounding by biomarker (e.g., osimertinib
prevalence is ~30% regardless of EGFR status), so this is best interpreted
as randomized-style assignment within the dataset; the treatment-effect
analyses below are unbiased to that approximation.

==============================
HYPOTHESES THAT WERE SUPPORTED
==============================

1) Pembrolizumab benefits objective response in PD-L1-high NSCLC and the
   benefit scales with PD-L1 TPS.
   - Univariate ORR: pembro 17.4% vs no-pembro 16.4% (p=3.4e-3, +1.0 pp).
   - Stratified by PD-L1 TPS:
       PD-L1=0       (n=5,791) : on 17.0% vs off 17.2%, p=0.85 (null)
       PD-L1 1-49%  (n=32,797): on 16.3% vs off 16.6%, p=0.56 (null)
       PD-L1 >=50%  (n=11,412): on 20.7% vs off 15.5%, p=9.3e-13 (+5.2 pp)
   - Logistic interaction pembro x pdl1_tps beta=+0.591, p=1.6e-7
     (adjusted multivariable beta=+0.595, p=1.7e-7). The benefit is
     entirely confined to the PD-L1>=50% stratum, mirroring real-world
     KEYNOTE-024 / KEYNOTE-042 behavior.

2) Pembrolizumab benefit is amplified by TMB-high status.
   - In TMB-high patients (n=14,258): on 19.7% vs off 16.7%, p=5.4e-6.
   - In TMB-low patients: no effect (p=0.61).
   - Interaction beta=+0.184, p=4.2e-4 (multivariable beta=+0.173, p~1e-3).

3) Joint biomarker selection produces near-multiplicative benefit.
   - In PD-L1>=50% AND TMB-high (n=3,150): pembro on 29.4% vs off 15.5%,
     p=1.9e-20, absolute gain +13.9 percentage points — substantially
     larger than either marker alone (+5.2 pp PD-L1 high, +3.0 pp TMB-high).

4) STK11 mutation attenuates pembrolizumab benefit.
   - Interaction beta=-0.211, p=1.8e-3 (multivariable -0.204, p=2.8e-3).
   - Direction matches published immunotherapy-resistance literature.

5) Female sex is associated with higher ORR overall and with greater
   pembrolizumab benefit.
   - Univariate: female 17.5% vs male 16.4%, p=5.9e-4.
   - Pembro x sex_female interaction beta=+0.146, p=2.4e-3.

6) Established prognostic clinical features predict ORR in the
   expected directions.
   - ecog_ps: ORR 21.1% / 15.7% / 11.0% across grades 0/1/2; logistic
     beta=-0.375 per unit, p=1.7e-93.
   - stage_iv: ORR 19.6% (no) vs 15.4% (yes), beta=-0.295, p=1.3e-33.
   - has_brain_mets: ORR 17.8% vs 14.4%, beta=-0.252, p=2.0e-18.
   - albumin_g_dl: beta=+0.091/g-dL, p=1.4e-4 (higher = better ORR).
   - crp_mg_l: beta=-0.0049, p=6.9e-4 (higher inflammation = lower ORR).
   - weight_loss_pct_6mo: beta=-0.0385 per percent, p=1.8e-32 (strong).
   All retain independence in a multivariable logistic model.

7) Pembrolizumab benefit is concentrated in ever-smokers.
   - Stratified pembro effect on ORR: current +1.24 pp (p=0.048),
     former +1.08 pp (p=0.017), never +0.12 pp (p=0.91). Consistent
     with low-TMB never-smoker NSCLC being less immunotherapy-sensitive.

8) NLR and CRP combine non-additively as inflammatory biomarkers.
   - In a model with NLR + CRP + NLR:CRP, both main effects are negative
     (NLR beta=-0.016, p=0.019; CRP beta=-0.012, p=2.2e-5) and the
     interaction is positive (+0.0019, p=2.4e-3), indicating diminishing
     marginal harm at very high values of either marker.

9) Univariate ORR is marginally heterogeneous across race/ethnicity
   (p=0.045) but with a small absolute spread (16.6%-18.5%) and not robust
   to multiple comparisons. Modest signal at best.

10) Higher fatigue_grade is weakly associated with lower ORR
    (beta=-0.022 per grade, p=0.040). Other symptoms (pain, dyspnea,
    cough, appetite loss) showed no detectable effect.

==============================
HYPOTHESES THAT WERE REFUTED
==============================

A) The classical biomarker-matched targeted therapy effects are NOT
   detectable in this dataset:
   - osimertinib in EGFR-mutant: ORR 16.4% on vs 16.6% off (p=0.87);
     interaction p=0.76.
   - sotorasib in KRAS G12C+: ORR 17.5% on vs 16.6% off (p=0.43);
     interaction p=0.43.
   - olaparib in BRCA2-mutant: ORR 14.6% on vs 16.8% off (p=0.35);
     interaction p=0.27 with sign opposite to expected.
   These are striking null findings given that the dataset's drug-target
   pairs reflect well-established NSCLC pharmacology. The pattern
   suggests the synthetic data only encodes the immunotherapy axis
   (pembrolizumab + PD-L1 / TMB / STK11), not the targeted-therapy axes.

B) No SNP in the rs* panel is robustly associated with ORR. Two reach
   nominal significance (rs7412 p=0.018, rs1050828 p=0.031) but neither
   survives Bonferroni at alpha=0.05/23=0.0022. Consistent with chance.

C) Most baseline comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, copd, CKD, heart
   failure, atrial fibrillation, autoimmune disease, hepatitis B/C, HIV,
   prior malignancy, depression/anxiety, ILD history) show no
   association with ORR. Only coronary_artery_disease is marginally
   negative (beta=-0.062, p=0.049) and would not survive correction.

D) Insurance type and rural residence have no detectable effect on ORR
   (insurance chi-square p=0.23; rural p=0.50). Treatment-assignment
   probability is also flat across insurance and race strata
   (within ~1 pp), suggesting no allocation-bias signal in this cohort.

E) Site of metastasis beyond brain is uninformative — liver, bone,
   adrenal, pleural, pericardial, and contralateral-lung metastases all
   yield p>0.18 for ORR. Only brain metastases carry independent
   prognostic weight.

F) Most additional genomic alterations (her2_amp, met_exon14_skipping,
   ret_fusion, ros1_fusion, braf_v600e, ntrk_fusion, nrg1_fusion,
   fgfr_alteration, cdkn2a_loss, tp53_mutation, pik3ca_mutation,
   pten_loss, msi_high, alk_fusion) show no univariate association.
   pik3ca is the closest miss (p=0.086).

G) Prior therapies (chemotherapy, radiation, surgery, immunotherapy,
   targeted therapy, prior lines of therapy) do not predict ORR
   (smallest p=0.065 for prior_chemotherapy).

H) Brain metastases do not differentially attenuate pembrolizumab effect
   (interaction p=0.57); ECOG performance status does not differentially
   attenuate it either (interaction p=0.32). KEAP1 mutation, despite
   being a published candidate immunotherapy resistance marker, shows
   no significant interaction with pembrolizumab here (p=0.36).

==============================
OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
==============================

The clearest signal in this 50,000-patient NSCLC cohort is that
pembrolizumab benefits objective_response, and that this benefit is
strongly conditional on PD-L1 TPS, TMB-high status, and the absence of
STK11 mutation. The pembrolizumab x PD-L1 interaction is the largest
single effect (multivariable beta ~ +0.60 with p<1e-7), and PD-L1 high
plus TMB high together produces an absolute ORR gain of ~14 percentage
points. Female sex and ever-smoking status independently amplify the
pembrolizumab effect.

Independent of treatment, the strongest prognostic predictors of
objective_response are ecog_ps, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, weight_loss
pct_6mo, albumin_g_dl, and crp_mg_l, all in clinically expected
directions and all retaining independence in multivariable logistic
regression.

The targeted-therapy axes — osimertinib in EGFR, sotorasib in KRAS G12C,
olaparib in BRCA2 — show no detectable benefit and no biomarker x
treatment interaction in this dataset. Likewise, the SNP panel,
comorbidities (other than a marginal CAD signal), insurance type, rural
residence, and most genomic alterations beyond PD-L1/TMB/STK11 are
uninformative for objective_response.

In short: the data behave as if a faithful pembrolizumab-PD-L1-TMB-STK11
biology were simulated against a backbone of standard NSCLC prognostic
features (ECOG, stage, brain mets, albumin, CRP, weight loss), with the
remaining drug-target relationships and the SNP panel essentially
uncorrelated noise relative to the outcome.
