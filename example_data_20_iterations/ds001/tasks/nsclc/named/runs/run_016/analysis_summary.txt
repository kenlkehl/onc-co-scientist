Analysis Summary — ds001_nsclc (n = 50,000)
============================================

Outcome
-------
Binary `objective_response`. Overall response rate in the cohort = 16.9% (n = 8,447 / 50,000).

Approach
--------
Across 25 iterations I proposed and tested 100+ hypotheses, moving from
simple main-effect comparisons (treatment, single biomarker, single
laboratory value) to interaction and multivariable models. Logistic
regressions and chi-square tests were used; the signed effect estimate
in the transcript is the log-odds-ratio coefficient or the absolute risk
difference, depending on the test (described per analysis).

Headline findings
-----------------

1. Pembrolizumab (PD-1 inhibitor) has a small but real main effect on ORR.
   ORR 17.4% on pembro vs 16.4% off (diff = +1.0 percentage points, chi2
   p = 0.0034). After multivariable adjustment for ECOG, albumin, LDH,
   weight loss, stage, brain metastases, age and sex the adjusted log-OR
   for pembro is +0.076 (p = 0.0015). The small absolute effect is
   consistent with averaging across heterogeneous biomarker subgroups
   (see point 2).

2. Pembrolizumab benefit is concentrated in PD-L1-high and TMB-high
   tumors. The pembro × pdl1_tps interaction has beta = +0.591
   (p = 1.6e-7); the pembro × tmb_high interaction has beta = +0.184
   (p = 4.1e-4). Within the pembrolizumab arm, every unit increase in
   pdl1_tps raises the log-odds of response by 0.45 (p = 7.6e-9), and
   ORR is 19.7% in TMB-high vs 16.5% in TMB-low pembro recipients. The
   pembro × pdl1 interaction remains significant (p = 1.7e-7) after full
   multivariable adjustment.

3. STK11 mutation attenuates pembrolizumab benefit. In the pembro arm,
   ORR is 15.8% in STK11+ vs 17.7% in STK11- (p = 0.005). The
   pembro × stk11_mutation interaction is negative (beta = -0.211,
   p = 0.0018) and survives adjustment (beta = -0.204, p = 0.003).
   KEAP1 mutation, often grouped with STK11, did NOT show a significant
   interaction or main effect on ORR within the pembro arm in this
   cohort (p = 0.36).

4. The three "matched" targeted-therapy/biomarker pairs (sotorasib×KRAS
   G12C, osimertinib×EGFR, olaparib×BRCA2) all showed no benefit in
   their matched biomarker-positive subgroup. Stratified ORRs:
     - sotorasib in KRAS G12C+: 17.5% on vs 16.6% off (p = 0.43)
     - osimertinib in EGFR+: 16.4% on vs 16.6% off (p = 0.87)
     - olaparib in BRCA2+: 14.6% on vs 16.8% off (p = 0.35)
   None of the corresponding interaction terms reached significance,
   either unadjusted or in a multivariable model. This is the strongest
   negative finding in the cohort and contradicts the textbook
   expectation that these biomarker-matched drugs would be highly
   selective for their molecular targets.

5. Negative-control interactions behaved as expected: sotorasib × EGFR,
   osimertinib × KRAS G12C, and olaparib × EGFR all returned
   non-significant interactions (p = 0.27 - 0.96), which is reassuring
   that the absence of matched-pair effects (point 4) is not an artefact
   of the modelling.

Prognostic factors
------------------

Performance status, nutrition, and inflammation had the largest and
most reliable associations with ORR:

  - ecog_ps                 beta = -0.375 (p = 1.7e-93)
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo     beta = -0.039 (p = 1.8e-32)
  - albumin_g_dl            beta = +0.091 (p = 1.4e-4)
  - crp_mg_l                beta = -0.005 (p = 6.9e-4)
  - stage_iv                ORR diff = -4.2 ppts (p = 1.1e-33)
  - has_brain_mets          ORR diff = -3.4 ppts (p = 2.0e-18)

LDH and NLR — both classic blood-based prognostic markers — were NOT
significant in this dataset (p = 0.51 and 0.38), which is unusual.

Sex showed a small but significant effect (female ORR 17.5% vs male
16.4%, p = 6e-4). Age, smoking status, smoking pack-years, histology,
BMI, vital signs, and most comorbidities (diabetes, COPD, CKD, autoimmune,
ILD) were not associated with response.

Other findings
--------------

  - Black vs white race had a small positive ORR difference (17.8 vs
    16.5%, p = 0.022), and Medicaid vs private insurance a small
    negative difference (16.1 vs 17.2%, p = 0.042). These could
    represent unmeasured confounding by treatment selection.
  - Liver, bone, adrenal mets and pleural effusion were NOT individually
    associated with ORR — only brain mets and stage IV were.
  - Among other oncogenic drivers (ALK, BRAF V600E, MET ex14, ROS1, RET,
    NTRK, HER2 amplification, TP53, PIK3CA, PTEN loss, CDKN2A loss, MSI-H,
    NRG1, FGFR), none had a significant association with ORR in this
    cohort.
  - Symptom burden: only fatigue_grade reached p < 0.05 (beta = -0.022,
    p = 0.04); pain, dyspnea, cough, and appetite-loss grades did not.
  - Hematology / chemistry continuous markers (hemoglobin, platelets,
    CEA, ALP, lymphocytes, AST, ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, sodium,
    calcium) showed no significant associations.
  - Across 23 pharmacogenomic SNPs, only 2 reached p < 0.05 vs ORR;
    expected under the null is ~1.2, so there is no evidence of a
    germline pharmacogenomic signal.

Hypotheses supported
--------------------
  - Pembrolizumab main effect (h1.1)
  - Pembro × PD-L1 interaction (h2.1, h2.3, h22.1)
  - Pembro × TMB-high interaction (h2.2, h2.4)
  - STK11+ reduces pembro benefit, including interaction (h5.1, h5.3, h22.2)
  - ECOG, albumin, weight loss, CRP prognostic effects (h4.1, h4.2, h4.3, h4.6)
  - Stage IV and brain mets reduce ORR (h6.1, h6.2)
  - Female sex slightly higher ORR (h7.4)
  - Pembro retains effect after adjustment (h8.1)
  - Adjusted ECOG remains strongly negative (h8.2)
  - Fatigue grade negatively associated with ORR (h17.1)
  - Race and insurance disparities (h15.1, h15.2)
  - Adjusted pembro × PD-L1 and pembro × STK11 interactions (h22.1, h22.2)
  - SNP burden of significant tests close to null expectation (h21.4 — supported as a null)

Hypotheses refuted
------------------
  - Sotorasib × KRAS G12C synergy (h3.1, h13.1, h24.1) — no signal
  - Osimertinib × EGFR synergy (h3.2, h13.2, h24.2) — no signal
  - Olaparib × BRCA2 synergy (h3.3, h13.3, h24.3) — point estimate
    actually negative
  - KEAP1 attenuation of pembro benefit (h5.2, h5.4) — non-significant
  - Pembro × ECOG and Pembro × age interactions (h9.1, h9.2)
  - LDH and NLR as prognostic markers (h4.4, h4.5)
  - Pembro × EGFR and Pembro × ALK negative interactions (h11.1, h11.2)
  - Symptom burden other than fatigue (h17.2 - h17.5)
  - Standalone metastatic-site effects (liver, bone, adrenal, pleural)
    (h6.3 - h6.6)

Overall interpretation
----------------------

This cohort behaves like a real-world NSCLC cohort with respect to PD-L1,
TMB, and STK11 modulating immunotherapy response — but, surprisingly,
shows no benefit for KRAS-G12C, EGFR, and BRCA2 patients receiving the
drugs that target their alterations. Either treatment assignment in
this dataset is uncoupled from biomarker status and the targeted drugs
were given without selection (in which case the absence of matched
benefit is genuine), or the simulation underlying the dataset captured
ICI biology more faithfully than targeted-therapy biology. The dominant
predictors of response in the cohort are performance status, nutritional
state, and disease burden — which match clinical experience. Pharmacogenomic
SNPs and most laboratory values added no predictive signal beyond noise.
