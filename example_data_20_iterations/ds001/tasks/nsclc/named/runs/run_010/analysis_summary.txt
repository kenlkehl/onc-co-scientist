ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (n = 50,000)
============================================

Outcome: objective_response (binary; overall ORR = 16.9%, n = 8,447 responders).
Strategy: 25 iterations of propose -> test -> refine, working from treatment main
effects to treatment-biomarker interactions, prognostic factors, demographics,
comorbidities, SNPs, and an integrated multivariable model.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
KEY POSITIVE FINDINGS (clinically meaningful, statistically supported)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

1) Pembrolizumab efficacy is concentrated in PD-L1-high disease.
   Iter 3. The interaction term treatment_pembrolizumab x pdl1_tps was
   strongly positive (logit beta = 0.59, p = 1.6e-7). Stratified ORR among
   patients with pdl1_tps >= 0.5 (TPS >= 50%) was 20.9% on pembrolizumab
   versus 15.6% off (RD = +0.054, p = 7.7e-13). Among patients with
   pdl1_tps < 0.5 the difference was essentially zero (RD = -0.002,
   p = 0.59). This recapitulates a well-established clinical pattern.

2) TMB-high status amplifies pembrolizumab benefit.
   Iter 4. The treatment_pembrolizumab x tmb_high interaction was
   positive (beta = 0.18, p = 4e-4). Among tmb_high = 1 patients,
   pembrolizumab raised ORR by 3.0 percentage points (p = 5e-6); among
   tmb_high = 0 patients there was no detectable benefit (RD = +0.002,
   p = 0.61). The marginal main effect of tmb_high (RD = +0.018, p = 1e-6)
   is consistent with the pembrolizumab subgroup driving the signal.

3) Joint PD-L1-high AND TMB-high subgroup shows the largest IO benefit.
   Iter 24. In the 3,150 patients with pdl1_tps >= 0.5 AND tmb_high = 1,
   pembrolizumab raised ORR by 13.9 percentage points (p = 2e-20), versus
   an essentially null 0.1 pp difference (p = 0.75) in the rest of the
   cohort. The three-way interaction (pembro x pdl1_tps x tmb_high) was
   positive (beta = 1.26, p = 3e-7), confirming super-additive synergy.

4) STK11 mutation attenuates pembrolizumab benefit.
   Iter 8. The treatment_pembrolizumab x stk11_mutation interaction was
   negative (beta = -0.21, p = 0.002). Pembrolizumab raised ORR by 1.4 pp
   in STK11-wild-type (p = 1e-4) but actually trended lower in STK11-mutant
   (RD = -0.015, p = 0.08). This matches the literature in which STK11
   loss confers IO resistance.

5) Performance status, stage and CNS metastases are dominant prognostic
   factors.
   - Iter 11. ecog_ps logit beta = -0.37, p = 1.7e-93. ORR drops monotonically:
     0 -> 21.1%, 1 -> 15.7%, 2 -> 11.1%.
   - Iter 12. stage_iv: RD = -4.3 pp, p = 1e-33. has_brain_mets: RD = -3.4 pp,
     p = 2e-18.

6) Standard inflammation/nutrition labs predict response.
   Iter 13. Higher albumin_g_dl associates with higher ORR (beta = +0.091,
   p = 0.00014); higher crp_mg_l associates with lower ORR (beta = -0.005,
   p = 0.0007). LDH, NLR and hemoglobin were not independently
   significant in univariable models.

7) Composite biomarker-matched-treatment indicator predicts response
   above and beyond covariates.
   Iter 25. Defining any_match = 1 if a patient was on osimertinib AND
   EGFR+, OR sotorasib AND KRAS G12C, OR olaparib AND BRCA2, OR
   pembrolizumab AND PD-L1 >= 50%, raised crude ORR from 16.4% (unmatched)
   to 18.9% (matched), RD = +0.025, p = 6e-9. After multivariable
   adjustment (treatments, biomarkers, ECOG, stage, mets, labs, age,
   smoking, histology) the adjusted logit beta on any_match was +0.20,
   p = 4e-8 (pseudo-R-squared = 0.017). Note: as shown below, the matched
   signal in this cohort is driven almost entirely by the pembrolizumab/
   PD-L1 component.

8) Modest demographic effect: female sex.
   Iter 14. ORR was 17.5% in women vs 16.4% in men (RD = +1.16 pp,
   p = 6e-4). Age was flat (p = 0.67); never-smoker status was not
   significant (p = 0.21).

9) Fatigue grade tracks lower response.
   Iter 17. Higher fatigue_grade -> lower ORR (beta = -0.022, p = 0.04).
   Other symptom grades (pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite loss) were null.

10) Race/ethnicity heterogeneity is borderline.
    Iter 19. Omnibus chi-square across race_ethnicity strata gave
    p = 0.045, with white patients having the lowest ORR (16.5%) and
    "other" the highest (18.5%). The asian-EGFR enrichment hypothesis
    was refuted (EGFR prevalence 13.2% asian vs 13.0% non-asian, p = 0.81).


------------------------------------------------------------------------
HYPOTHESES THAT WERE NOT SUPPORTED (refuted)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

A striking pattern in this cohort is that the targeted-therapy/biomarker
matchings that are textbook clinical truths fail to show signal:

- Sotorasib x KRAS G12C (Iter 5). No main effect, no interaction
  (interaction beta = 0.06, p = 0.43). Among KRAS G12C+ patients,
  sotorasib raised ORR by only 0.8 pp (p = 0.43). Refuted.
- Osimertinib x EGFR (Iter 6). No main effect of osimertinib in either
  EGFR+ or EGFR- patients; interaction p = 0.76. Refuted.
- Olaparib x BRCA2 (Iter 7). Numerically negative interaction
  (beta = -0.18, p = 0.28), null in both BRCA2+ and BRCA2- strata.
  Refuted.
- Pembrolizumab x KEAP1 (Iter 9). Interaction p = 0.36; pembrolizumab
  benefit in KEAP1-mutant was numerically smaller but not significantly
  so. Not supported.
- Pembrolizumab x EGFR (Iter 10). Interaction p = 0.63; the standard
  finding that EGFR-mutant patients respond poorly to IO did not
  reproduce here. Not supported.
- Other targetable drivers (Iter 16). alk_fusion, ros1_fusion,
  ret_fusion, met_exon14_skipping, ntrk_fusion, nrg1_fusion,
  her2_amplification, braf_v600e: all null on objective_response
  (smallest p = 0.12 for braf_v600e), reflecting that no driver-matched
  TKIs other than the four named are present in this dataset.
- Squamous histology (Iter 15). ORR 17.4% vs 16.7% adeno (p = 0.08);
  no pembro x histology interaction (p = 0.58). Not supported.
- Socioeconomic status (Iter 18). uninsured, rural, education_years
  all null. Not supported.
- Comorbidities (Iter 21). autoimmune_disease, ILD, CKD, HF, COPD,
  HIV+ all individually null on response. Not supported.
- Prior treatment history (Iter 22). prior_lines_of_therapy,
  prior_immunotherapy, prior_chemotherapy all null (smallest
  p = 0.07 for prior_chemotherapy). Not supported.
- SNP panel (Iter 20). Across 23 candidate SNPs, no SNP cleared
  Bonferroni (alpha = 0.05/23 = 0.0022). Smallest p = 0.018 for
  snp_rs7412. Refuted.

Iteration 23 (multivariable model adjusting for ECOG, stage IV, age,
albumin, LDH, NLR) replicated the same pattern: pembro x pdl1_tps
remained strongly significant (beta = 0.61, p = 9e-8), while
sotorasib x KRAS G12C, osimertinib x EGFR, and olaparib x BRCA2 were
all null in the same multivariable model. This is internally consistent
with the unadjusted single-interaction tests.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

In this 50,000-patient NSCLC cohort, the dominant treatment-biomarker
relationship driving objective_response is between pembrolizumab and
PD-L1 expression, with TMB-high status amplifying the benefit and STK11
mutation blunting it. The largest absolute response gain (~14 percentage
points) is in the PD-L1-high AND TMB-high subgroup. None of the three
tested targeted-therapy/biomarker pairs (sotorasib/KRAS G12C,
osimertinib/EGFR, olaparib/BRCA2) showed the expected enrichment of
benefit in their molecularly matched subgroup. Beyond treatment effects,
the strongest prognostic signals were ECOG performance status, stage IV,
brain metastases, albumin, and CRP - the standard clinical-prognostic
backbone. Demographic, socioeconomic, comorbidity and SNP variables
contributed little after accounting for these clinical covariates.

The key actionable message: among these four treatments, only
pembrolizumab shows a biomarker-driven benefit signal, and it is well
captured by PD-L1 TPS plus TMB-high, with STK11 mutation status as a
candidate negative modifier.
