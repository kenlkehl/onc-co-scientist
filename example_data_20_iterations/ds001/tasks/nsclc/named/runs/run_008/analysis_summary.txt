ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (n=50,000; ORR overall 16.9%)
=============================================================

Across 25 iterations we examined patient features and outcomes in a 50,000-patient
NSCLC cohort. The single binary outcome was `objective_response`. We probed main
effects, biomarker–treatment matching interactions, prognostic clinical/laboratory
signals, demographic and access factors, comorbidities, symptom burden, prior
therapy, somatic-mutation profiles, and a panel of 23 germline SNPs.

----- Treatment main effects (Iter 1) -----
Of the four treatment indicators, only `treatment_pembrolizumab` was associated
with a higher ORR (17.4% vs 16.4%; OR=1.073, p=0.003). `treatment_sotorasib`,
`treatment_olaparib`, and `treatment_osimertinib` showed no overall benefit
(all p>0.17). This is a surprising but informative pattern: in this cohort,
unconditional rates of response to the targeted agents are no different from
the rates seen in patients not receiving them, presumably because the patients
who receive them are not enriched for the drugs’ molecular targets.

----- Biomarker × targeted therapy (Iter 2–4, 11, 16, 22, 23) -----
Despite extensive testing, the canonical biomarker–targeted-therapy
interactions were NOT supported in this cohort:
  • osimertinib × egfr_mutation: ORR 16.4% (osimertinib) vs 16.6% (none) in
    EGFR+; interaction log-OR=+0.024, LR p=0.76. After multivariable
    adjustment, log-OR=+0.029, p=0.73.
  • sotorasib × kras_g12c: ORR 17.5% vs 16.6% in G12C+; interaction log-OR=+0.06,
    LR p=0.43; adjusted p=0.40.
  • olaparib × brca2_mutation: ORR 14.6% (olaparib) vs 16.8% (none) in BRCA2+
    (point estimate inverted); interaction log-OR=−0.18, LR p=0.27; adjusted p=0.34.
None of these matched-therapy interactions reached significance, and none
showed a meaningful positive direction. In contrast, the immunotherapy
biomarker interactions were strongly supported (below).

----- Pembrolizumab × PD-L1 TPS (Iter 5, 16, 22) -----
A clear, consistent positive interaction was identified:
  • Among pembrolizumab-treated patients, higher pdl1_tps was associated with
    higher ORR (logit slope=+0.45, p=7.6e-9).
  • Pembrolizumab × pdl1_tps interaction: log-OR=+0.59 per unit pdl1_tps,
    LR p=1.5e-7.
  • In high-PD-L1 (pdl1_tps≥0.5) patients, pembrolizumab raised ORR from 15.6%
    to 20.9% (OR=1.43, p=7.0e-13).
  • The interaction remained essentially unchanged after multivariable
    adjustment (log-OR=+0.60, p=1.3e-7) and within the comprehensive joint model
    (log-OR=+0.59, p=1.9e-7).
  • Patients with pdl1_tps<0.01 derived no benefit from pembrolizumab
    (ORR 17.0% vs 17.2%, p=0.83), consistent with mechanism.

----- Pembrolizumab × TMB / STK11 / KEAP1 (Iter 6, 17) -----
  • tmb_high × pembrolizumab: stratified ORR 19.7% vs 16.7% in TMB-high
    (OR=1.22, p=5.1e-6) and 16.5% vs 16.3% in TMB-low (p=0.61). Interaction
    log-OR=+0.18, LR p=4.1e-4. SUPPORTED.
  • stk11_mutation × pembrolizumab: in STK11+, pembrolizumab ORR 15.8% vs 17.3%
    (no benefit); in STK11-, 17.7% vs 16.3% (benefit). Interaction log-OR=−0.21,
    LR p=1.8e-3. SUPPORTED — STK11 mutation attenuates pembrolizumab benefit.
  • keap1_mutation × pembrolizumab: interaction log-OR=−0.06, LR p=0.36. NOT
    supported in this cohort.
  • 3-way pembrolizumab × tmb_high × pdl1_tps: positive coefficient +1.26,
    LR p=3.0e-7. SUPPORTED — pembrolizumab benefit is amplified when both
    biomarkers are favorable simultaneously.

----- ECOG, stage, and metastatic burden (Iter 7, 10, 17, 21) -----
Strong prognostic effects emerged:
  • ecog_ps: log-OR=−0.375 per unit, p=1.7e-93 (huge effect; ORR 11.0% in
    ECOG=2 vs 21.9% in ECOG=0).
  • stage_iv: ORR 15.4% vs 19.6% (OR=0.74, p=3.4e-33).
  • has_brain_mets: ORR 14.4% vs 17.7% (OR=0.78, p=6.1e-19).
  • In contrast, individual metastatic sites (`liver_mets`, `bone_mets`,
    `adrenal_mets`, `pleural_effusion`) and pembrolizumab interactions with
    those sites were null.
  • In the ECOG=2 stratum, pembrolizumab benefit disappeared (ORR 11.1% vs
    11.0%, p=0.88), whereas ECOG=0 patients gained roughly 1.6 percentage
    points (p=0.01).

----- Laboratory and prognostic markers (Iter 8, 14, 19) -----
  • albumin_g_dl: positive (log-OR=+0.091/unit, p=1.4e-4).
  • crp_mg_l: negative (log-OR=−0.0049/unit, p=6.9e-4).
  • weight_loss_pct_6mo: strongly negative (log-OR=−0.039/%, p=1.8e-32).
  • fatigue_grade: weak negative (log-OR=−0.022/unit, p=0.04).
  • ldh_u_l, nlr, pain_nrs, dyspnea_grade, appetite_loss_grade, cea_ng_ml,
    ca_125_u_ml, psa_ng_ml: NOT associated.

----- Demographics and access (Iter 12, 25) -----
  • age_years: NOT associated (p=0.67).
  • sex_female: female ORR 17.5% vs male 16.4% (OR=1.09, p=5.7e-4).
  • insurance_type: NOT associated (p=0.23).
  • rural_residence: NOT associated (p=0.49).
  • race_ethnicity: borderline univariable difference (chi2 p=0.045) driven by
    higher ORR in Black, Asian, and Other groups vs White; no longer
    significant after biomarker/clinical adjustment (LR p=0.069).
  • Asian race not enriched for EGFR mutation in this cohort (13.2% vs 13.0%,
    p=0.78), in contrast to the well-known epidemiological pattern.
  • education_years: NOT associated.

----- Comorbidities, prior therapy, and other molecular markers (Iter 13, 15, 18, 20) -----
  • All tested comorbidities (ILD history, autoimmune disease, CKD, depression/
    anxiety, COPD): no association with ORR.
  • Prior chemotherapy, immunotherapy, targeted therapy, prior_lines_of_therapy:
    no association.
  • Other actionable mutations (alk_fusion, ros1_fusion, braf_v600e,
    met_exon14_skipping, ret_fusion, ntrk_fusion, her2_amplification,
    tp53_mutation, msi_high): no significant univariable association with ORR.
  • SNP panel (23 markers): no SNP survived Bonferroni correction; minimum
    raw p=0.018 vs threshold 0.0022. Specific lookups for snp_rs1045642 and
    snp_rs429358 were null.

----- Comprehensive multivariable synthesis (Iter 22) -----
A joint model including all four treatments, their key biomarker interactions
(pembro×pdl1_tps, pembro×tmb_high, pembro×stk11_mutation, pembro×keap1_mutation,
osimertinib×egfr, sotorasib×krasg12c, olaparib×brca2), and clinical covariates
(ecog_ps, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, age, sex, albumin, LDH, NLR, CRP,
liver_mets, bone_mets, tp53_mutation) confirmed:
  • pembrolizumab × pdl1_tps: log-OR=+0.59, p=1.9e-7 (supported).
  • osimertinib × egfr_mutation: log-OR=+0.027, p=0.73 (NOT supported).
  • sotorasib × kras_g12c: log-OR=+0.067, p=0.37 (NOT supported).
  • olaparib × brca2_mutation: log-OR=−0.16, p=0.33 (NOT supported; sign
    inverted).
  • ecog_ps: log-OR=−0.38, p=3.7e-95.
  • stage_iv: log-OR=−0.30, p=4.0e-34.

----- Overall conclusions -----
1. The strongest, most reproducible, and biologically coherent signal in this
   dataset is the immunotherapy axis: PD-L1 TPS, TMB-high, and STK11 status
   modulate pembrolizumab response in the directions one would expect from
   the published literature. The 3-way TMB × PD-L1 × pembrolizumab interaction
   suggests these biomarkers are at least partly complementary.
2. The targeted-therapy biomarker matches that are clinically established
   (EGFR↔osimertinib, KRAS G12C↔sotorasib, BRCA2↔olaparib) are NOT supported
   in this dataset. Within the EGFR+ subset (n=6,517) osimertinib was not
   associated with higher ORR; within KRAS G12C+ (n=6,368) sotorasib was not;
   within BRCA2+ (n=1,519) olaparib’s point estimate was actually inverse.
   Because these interactions are biologically and clinically expected, their
   absence here is itself a notable finding — possibly a feature of how the
   dataset was assembled or sampled, or of unmeasured treatment indication
   confounding.
3. Performance status (ECOG), stage, brain mets, weight loss, albumin, and
   CRP are the dominant clinical/laboratory drivers of response. ECOG status
   is the single largest predictor in the cohort.
4. Demographic and SES variables (age, insurance, rurality, education) and
   the SNP panel show no meaningful association with ORR. Sex shows a small
   but significant female-favoring difference; race-ethnicity differences
   become non-significant after biomarker adjustment.
5. Pembrolizumab benefit is concentrated in fitter patients (ECOG 0–1) with
   PD-L1 expression and high TMB, and is attenuated by STK11 mutation —
   a profile that mirrors what has been reported clinically.
