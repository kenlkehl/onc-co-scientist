Analysis summary — ds001_nsclc (n = 50,000 patients)
=====================================================

Outcome studied: objective_response (binary; overall rate 16.9%).

The protocol below walks through 25 iterations of propose-test-refine. Effect
estimates are signed on a natural scale: rate differences for chi-square tests
on 2x2 tables, log-odds for logistic-regression coefficients. Statistical
significance is declared at p < 0.05 (uncorrected) unless noted.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 1 — Treatment main effects
------------------------------------------------------------
Pembrolizumab raised the overall response rate by ~1 percentage point (17.4%
vs 16.4%, p = 0.003). The other three on-label treatments — sotorasib,
olaparib, osimertinib — showed no detectable main effect on overall response
(all p > 0.18). This was a first hint that the targeted-therapy benefits, if
present, are likely confined to biomarker-defined subgroups (so they are
diluted in unselected populations) and motivated the subgroup work below.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 2 — Biomarker main effects
------------------------------------------------------------
TMB-high was the only biomarker with a significant main effect on overall
response (+1.8 percentage points, p = 1e-6). EGFR mutation, KRAS G12C, ALK
fusion, BRCA2 mutation, and STK11 mutation each had no detectable overall
association with response (all p > 0.37). Again, this is consistent with the
prior that biomarkers act primarily as treatment-effect modifiers rather than
prognostic markers in this cohort.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 3 — Pembrolizumab x biomarker interactions
------------------------------------------------------------
This is the most striking finding of the analysis.
  - treatment_pembrolizumab x pdl1_tps interaction: +0.59 log-odds (p = 1.6e-7).
    Pembrolizumab benefit grows with PD-L1 expression.
  - treatment_pembrolizumab x tmb_high interaction: +0.18 log-odds (p = 4e-4).
    TMB-high adds further to pembrolizumab benefit.
  - treatment_pembrolizumab x stk11_mutation interaction: -0.21 log-odds
    (p = 0.002). STK11 mutation attenuates pembrolizumab benefit.
All three directions match the canonical clinical literature.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 4 — Targeted therapy x cognate biomarker interactions
------------------------------------------------------------
Tested whether each targeted therapy's effect is modified by its cognate
biomarker:
  - osimertinib x egfr_mutation: +0.024 log-odds (p = 0.76)
  - sotorasib x kras_g12c:       +0.059 log-odds (p = 0.43)
  - olaparib  x brca2_mutation:  -0.18  log-odds (p = 0.28)
None reached statistical significance. This was unexpected on clinical
priors; it raises the possibility that this dataset does not simulate large
biomarker-restricted treatment benefits for these drugs, or that the
benefits are too small for the available subgroup sizes.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iterations 5-6 — Subgroup analyses for targeted therapies
------------------------------------------------------------
In biomarker-positive subgroups, no significant treatment-vs-no-treatment
difference was detected for any of osimertinib (in EGFR+), sotorasib (in
KRAS G12C+), or olaparib (in BRCA2+); all subgroup p-values were > 0.4. The
same was true in biomarker-negative subgroups. Net: in this cohort, the
three targeted agents do not show measurable response benefit in their
expected sensitive subgroups, contrary to clinical expectation.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 7 — Stage / performance status / brain mets
------------------------------------------------------------
All three classic prognostic factors had strong, expected effects on response:
  - ECOG PS:        log-odds -0.38 per unit (p ~ 1e-94). Worse PS, much
                    lower probability of response.
  - stage_iv:       log-odds -0.30 (p ~ 3e-34).
  - has_brain_mets: log-odds -0.26 (p ~ 5e-19).

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 8 — Lab markers
------------------------------------------------------------
Albumin was a strong positive prognostic marker (log-odds +0.094 per g/dL,
p = 9e-6). LDH, neutrophil-to-lymphocyte ratio, CRP, and 6-month weight loss
percentage did not reach significance individually in this dataset.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 9 — Demographics
------------------------------------------------------------
Female sex was associated with higher response (+0.083 log-odds, p = 6e-4).
Age (per year) was not associated with response (p = 0.67). Ever-smoker vs
never-smoker showed a non-significant trend toward higher response in
ever-smokers (p = 0.20).

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 10 — Pembrolizumab x histology / smoking interactions
------------------------------------------------------------
Neither the histology (squamous vs adenocarcinoma) nor smoking-status
interaction with pembrolizumab reached significance (both p > 0.28). The
pembrolizumab effect modifiers in this dataset are PD-L1, TMB, and STK11 —
not histology or smoking status.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 11 — Mismatched targeted-therapy / biomarker subgroups
------------------------------------------------------------
As expected, osimertinib in KRAS-G12C-positive patients and sotorasib in
EGFR-mutant patients showed no detectable response benefit (both p > 0.69).

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 12 — PD-L1 main effect
------------------------------------------------------------
Higher PD-L1 TPS was associated with higher overall response (+0.17 log-odds
per unit TPS, p = 0.003). This is partially driven by the pembrolizumab x
PD-L1 interaction in the treated subset.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 13 — Pembrolizumab benefit by PD-L1 stratum
------------------------------------------------------------
Splitting at the PD-L1 TPS median, pembrolizumab raised response by +2.1
percentage points in the high-PD-L1 stratum (p = 7e-6) but had no detectable
benefit in the low stratum (-0.2 pp, p = 0.71). This dose-response
relationship is the practical implication of the iteration-3 interaction.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 14 — Multivariable model for objective response
------------------------------------------------------------
Adjusting for biomarkers, prognostics, and demographics simultaneously, the
following retained independent significance:
  - ecog_ps        -0.38   (p = 4e-95)
  - stage_iv       -0.30   (p = 3e-34)
  - has_brain_mets -0.26   (p = 4e-19)
  - tmb_high       +0.13   (p = 1e-6)
  - albumin_g_dl   +0.093  (p = 1e-4)
  - sex_female     +0.081  (p = 0.001)
  - pembrolizumab  +0.077  (p = 0.001)
  - pdl1_tps       +0.17   (p = 0.003)
LDH, age, and stk11_mutation did not retain independent significance.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 15 — Three-way interaction (pembrolizumab x PD-L1 x STK11)
------------------------------------------------------------
The three-way interaction term was negative but not significant (-0.39,
p = 0.22). The pembrolizumab x PD-L1 and pembrolizumab x STK11 modifiers
appear to operate roughly additively rather than synergistically.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iterations 16-18 — Race, insurance, comorbidities, mets sites
------------------------------------------------------------
A weak association of response rate with race/ethnicity (chi-square p = 0.045)
was found (max-min difference ~2 percentage points across groups). No
significant differences by insurance type. None of the tested comorbidities
(diabetes, COPD, heart failure, CKD, autoimmune disease, ILD history) or
metastatic-site features (liver, bone, adrenal, pleural effusion) were
individually significant.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 19 — SNP screen
------------------------------------------------------------
A per-SNP screen across the 24 SNP columns uncovered two SNPs at uncorrected
p < 0.05 (rs1045642: +0.10, p = 0.018; rs1065852: -0.10, p = 0.031). Given
multiple testing across 24 SNPs, the family-wise expected number of
false-positives is ~1.2, so these are best regarded as suggestive only.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 20 — Comprehensive multivariable model with interactions
------------------------------------------------------------
Adjusted for prognostics and all six pre-specified treatment-biomarker
interactions, only the three pembrolizumab interactions persisted:
  - pembrolizumab x pdl1_tps        +0.59 (p = 2e-7)
  - pembrolizumab x tmb_high        +0.17 (p = 1e-3)
  - pembrolizumab x stk11_mutation  -0.20 (p = 0.003)
The osimertinib x EGFR, sotorasib x KRAS, and olaparib x BRCA2 interactions
remained non-significant after adjustment, confirming the iteration-4
finding.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 21 — Biomarker co-occurrence
------------------------------------------------------------
EGFR mutation and TMB-high were strongly negatively associated (log OR =
-0.79, p ~ 1e-117). EGFR-mutant cancers in this cohort are predominantly
TMB-low, mirroring real-world NSCLC epidemiology.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 22 — Pembrolizumab in classically resistant subgroups
------------------------------------------------------------
In EGFR-mutant patients (n = 6,517) and in ALK-fusion patients (n = 2,464),
pembrolizumab did not show a significant response benefit (both diff < 0.012,
p > 0.47). This is consistent with clinical experience that ICI works less
well in oncogene-driven NSCLC, but the effect sizes here are smaller than the
classical literature reports.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 23 — Symptom severity
------------------------------------------------------------
Higher fatigue grade was associated with lower response (-0.022 log-odds per
unit, p = 0.04); pain, dyspnea, and appetite-loss grades were not
individually significant.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 24 — Prior lines of therapy and prior immunotherapy
------------------------------------------------------------
Prior lines of therapy were not associated with response. The prior_immuno
x pembrolizumab interaction was positive and just significant (+0.135,
p = 0.044), but should be interpreted cautiously given the multiplicity of
interactions tested.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 25 — Final adjudicated subgroup tests
------------------------------------------------------------
Adjusting for ECOG, stage, brain mets, and albumin within each cognate
biomarker subgroup did not surface targeted-therapy benefits:
  - osimertinib in EGFR-mutant (n=6,517): adj log-odds -0.008 (p = 0.92)
  - sotorasib  in KRAS-G12C  (n=6,368): adj log-odds +0.057 (p = 0.42)
  - olaparib   in BRCA2-mutant (n=1,519): adj log-odds -0.13  (p = 0.43)
By contrast, in the PD-L1-high (TPS >= 0.5), STK11-wildtype subgroup
(n ~ 14,000), pembrolizumab raised response by 6.4 percentage points
(p = 6e-15) — the largest treatment-effect estimate observed in the
analysis.

============================================================
Overall conclusions
============================================================
1. The dominant treatment-effect signal in this cohort is for pembrolizumab.
   Its benefit is concentrated in PD-L1-high and TMB-high tumors, and is
   diminished in STK11-mutant tumors. The PD-L1 modifier is by far the
   strongest, with a near-zero effect at low TPS and a clear positive
   effect at high TPS.

2. Targeted-therapy effects in their cognate biomarker subgroups
   (osimertinib in EGFR+, sotorasib in KRAS-G12C+, olaparib in BRCA2+) were
   not detectable in this dataset, neither as main effects, subgroup-
   specific contrasts, nor as treatment-by-biomarker interactions. This
   contrasts with the substantial benefits these drugs produce in real
   clinical practice, suggesting either that the simulated effect sizes are
   small relative to noise, or that these treatments were not designed into
   the data-generating process for response.

3. Standard prognostic factors behaved as clinically expected: better
   performance status, non-stage-IV disease, no brain metastases, higher
   albumin, female sex, and higher TMB were all independently associated
   with higher probability of response.

4. EGFR mutations and TMB-high are strongly mutually exclusive, consistent
   with real-world molecular epidemiology of NSCLC.

5. The PD-L1-high, STK11-wildtype subset was identified as the population
   in whom pembrolizumab response benefit is largest. This is the most
   actionable finding from this analysis and aligns with the published
   ICI biomarker literature.

6. Limitations: all p-values are uncorrected for multiplicity. Some
   borderline-significant findings (race/ethnicity, prior_immuno x
   pembrolizumab, individual SNPs, fatigue grade) should be regarded as
   hypothesis-generating only.
