Analysis summary — ds001_nsclc (n=50,000)
=========================================

Outcome and exposures
---------------------
Outcome was binary objective_response (overall rate ≈ 16.9 %). Four anti-cancer
treatments were studied as 0/1 exposure flags: treatment_pembrolizumab,
treatment_sotorasib, treatment_olaparib, treatment_osimertinib. Patients
typically received 1–2 of these (some 3–4), so all comparisons are within the
same heterogeneous cohort and the four treatments are not mutually exclusive.

Iteration 1 — Treatment main effects
------------------------------------
Hypothesis: each of the four treatments raises overall objective_response.
- treatment_pembrolizumab: response 17.4 % vs 16.4 % off-treatment, OR 1.07,
  p=0.003 — small but real overall benefit (h1.1 supported).
- treatment_sotorasib (h1.2), treatment_olaparib (h1.3), treatment_osimertinib
  (h1.4): no detectable main effect (all p>0.17). The targeted agents do NOT
  raise the average response rate of an unselected NSCLC patient — strongly
  suggesting their effects are biomarker-restricted, refuting their unconditional
  forms.

Iteration 2 — Clinical / demographic prognosticators
----------------------------------------------------
Strong, classical prognostic signals:
- ecog_ps: each unit reduces response odds by ~31 % (OR 0.69, p≈10⁻⁹³, h2.1
  supported).
- stage_iv: OR 0.74, p≈10⁻³³ (h2.2 supported).
- has_brain_mets: OR 0.78, p≈10⁻¹⁸ (h2.3 supported).
- age_years: null (OR 1.0005, p=0.67) — h2.4 refuted.
- sex_female: OR 1.09, p=5.7×10⁻⁴ — women have slightly higher response (h2.5
  supported, direction = positive).

Iteration 3 — Biomarker main effects
------------------------------------
- tmb_high: OR 1.14, p=10⁻⁶ — supported (h3.1).
- pdl1_tps continuous: OR 1.18 per 0→1 unit, p=0.003 — supported (h3.2).
- egfr_mutation, kras_g12c, alk_fusion, brca2_mutation, stk11_mutation,
  keap1_mutation, tp53_mutation: all main effects null (p≥0.37) — h3.3 refuted
  for all seven. Driver mutations alone do not predict overall response in a
  mixed-treatment cohort; their importance is via interaction with treatment.

Iterations 4–6 — Targeted-agent × cognate-biomarker interactions
----------------------------------------------------------------
A central a priori expectation in NSCLC is that osimertinib helps EGFR-mutant
tumors, sotorasib helps KRAS G12C tumors, and olaparib helps BRCA2-mutant
tumors. None of these expectations was met:
- treatment_osimertinib × egfr_mutation: interaction OR 1.02, p=0.76. Cell
  response rates are essentially flat (~16–17 % in all four cells). h4.1 refuted.
- treatment_sotorasib × kras_g12c: interaction OR 1.06, p=0.43. h5.1 refuted.
- treatment_olaparib × brca2_mutation: interaction OR 0.84, p=0.27 (and the
  point estimate is in the opposite direction). h6.1 refuted.

This is the most striking pattern in the dataset: the three small-molecule
targeted therapies have neither marginal nor biomarker-conditional effects on
objective_response.

Iterations 7–9 — Pembrolizumab response biology
-----------------------------------------------
By contrast, pembrolizumab biology is robust:
- treatment_pembrolizumab × pdl1_high (TPS≥0.5): interaction OR 1.46, p=5×10⁻¹¹.
  Pembro lifts response from ~16 % to ~21 % in PD-L1 high tumors, with little
  effect in PD-L1 low. h7.1 strongly supported.
- Continuous treatment_pembrolizumab × pdl1_tps: interaction beta=+0.59,
  p=2×10⁻⁷. Same signal on the continuous scale (h7.2 supported).
- treatment_pembrolizumab × tmb_high: interaction OR 1.20, p=4×10⁻⁴. Pembro
  raises response from 16.5 % to 19.7 % in TMB-high but not in TMB-low. h8.1
  supported.
- treatment_pembrolizumab × stk11_mutation: interaction OR 0.81, p=0.002.
  Pembro benefit is abrogated in STK11-mutant tumors, consistent with the
  published immune-cold biology. h9.1 supported.
- treatment_pembrolizumab × keap1_mutation: interaction OR 0.94, p=0.36 — null.
  h9.2 refuted.

Iteration 10 — ALK fusions
--------------------------
- treatment_osimertinib × alk_fusion: null (interaction OR 1.03, p=0.84). As
  expected, since osimertinib targets EGFR not ALK. h10.1 refuted (no off-target
  benefit).
- treatment_pembrolizumab × alk_fusion: null (p=0.90). h10.2 refuted; no
  pembrolizumab penalty for ALK-driven tumors in this cohort.

Iteration 11 — Inflammatory / nutritional labs
----------------------------------------------
- albumin_g_dl: OR 1.09 per g/dL, p=1.4×10⁻⁴ — higher albumin → more response
  (h11.1 supported).
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: OR 0.96 per 1 % loss, p=10⁻³² — heavy weight loss
  predicts non-response (h11.5 supported).
- crp_mg_l: OR 0.995 per mg/L, p=7×10⁻⁴ — h11.4 supported (small but
  significant).
- ldh_u_l (h11.2) and nlr (h11.3): both null.

Iteration 12 — Metastasis sites
-------------------------------
None of liver_mets, bone_mets, adrenal_mets, pleural_effusion, or
pericardial_effusion was associated with response (all p≥0.18); h12.1–h12.5
refuted. Only has_brain_mets carried a strong negative signal (iter 2). The
visceral-mets effect typically seen in trial cohorts does not surface here once
ECOG is implicitly held constant.

Iteration 13 — Histology / smoking_status
-----------------------------------------
- squamous histology vs adenocarcinoma: trend toward higher response in
  squamous (17.4 % vs 16.7 %, OR 1.05, p=0.08) — h13.1 not significant.
- smoking_status: response rates 17.2 % current, 16.8 % former, 16.4 % never;
  individual indicator p-values 0.19–0.77. h13.2 refuted.

Iteration 14 — Smoking × treatment interactions
-----------------------------------------------
- smoking_pack_years × pembrolizumab: null (p=0.27, h14.1 refuted).
- never × pembrolizumab: null (p=0.29, h14.2 refuted).
- never × osimertinib: borderline negative (interaction OR 0.88, p=0.09). The
  expected "EGFR-enriched never-smoker benefits from osimertinib" pattern is
  absent — actually trending in the opposite direction (h14.3 refuted).

Iteration 15 — Sex × treatment
------------------------------
- sex_female × pembrolizumab: interaction OR 1.16, p=0.0024. Women on pembro
  reach 18.6 % response vs ~16 % in men on pembro and women off pembro —
  h15.1 supported.
- sex_female × osimertinib (h15.2), × sotorasib (h15.3), × olaparib (h15.4):
  all null (p≥0.30).

Iteration 16 — Race / ethnicity
-------------------------------
- race=white: lower response than other groups (16.5 % vs 17.5 %, OR 0.93,
  p=0.005); race=black borderline higher (17.8 % vs 16.8 %, p=0.06). Other
  individual indicators not significant. h16.1 partially supported.
- Asian × osimertinib (h16.2): null (p=0.87). The classic "Asian/EGFR/TKI"
  enrichment is not detectable.
- Black × pembrolizumab (h16.3): null (p=0.51).

Iteration 17 — Insurance / SES
------------------------------
No individual insurance category, rural_residence, or education_years was
associated with objective_response (all p>0.06). h17.1–h17.3 refuted.

Iteration 18 — Comorbidities
----------------------------
None of autoimmune_disease, interstitial_lung_disease_history, copd, chronic
kidney disease, heart_failure, diabetes_mellitus, or hypertension had a
significant main effect (all p≥0.26); h18.1 refuted. autoimmune × pembrolizumab
(h18.2) and ILD × pembrolizumab (h18.3) interactions both null.

Iteration 19 — Prior therapy history
------------------------------------
- prior_chemotherapy: borderline lower response (OR 0.96, p=0.07) — h19.1 not
  significant.
- prior_lines_of_therapy: null (p=0.24, h19.2 refuted).
- prior_immunotherapy × pembrolizumab: interaction OR 1.14, p=0.044, in the
  positive direction. The expected "previously-treated-with-IO patients benefit
  less from re-IO" pattern is not present; h19.3 refuted.
- All other prior-therapy main effects null.

Iteration 20 — Symptom grades
-----------------------------
- fatigue_grade: small negative effect (OR 0.98, p=0.04, h20.1 supported).
- pain_nrs, dyspnea_grade, cough_grade, appetite_loss_grade: null
  (h20.2–h20.5 refuted).

Iteration 21 — SNP main effects (n=23 candidate drug-metabolism SNPs)
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Only 2 of 23 SNPs (snp_rs7412, snp_rs1050828) reached p<0.05 univariate, both
with modest ORs (~1.10 and 0.91). After multiple-testing correction nothing
survives; h21.1 not robustly supported. Most of the "drug metabolism" panel
behaves like noise.

Iteration 22 — SNP × treatment interactions
-------------------------------------------
24 SNP × treatment interaction tests (6 SNPs × 4 treatments). Three reached
nominal p<0.05 (rs1065852 × sotorasib, rs1799853 × pembrolizumab, rs1799853 ×
olaparib), each consistent with chance after 24 tests. h22.1 not robustly
supported.

Iteration 23 — ECOG, brain mets × treatment
-------------------------------------------
- ecog_high (≥2) × pembrolizumab: interaction p=0.38 (null). ECOG ≥2 patients
  respond at ~11 % regardless of pembrolizumab (vs ~17 % in ECOG 0–1). h23.1
  refuted at the interaction level — ECOG is purely prognostic, not predictive
  for pembrolizumab response.
- Same for ECOG × osimertinib (p=0.31, h23.2 refuted), brain mets × pembro
  (p=0.57, h23.3 refuted), brain mets × osimertinib (p=0.42, h23.4 refuted).

Iteration 24 — Multivariable model
----------------------------------
Logistic regression of objective_response on all four treatments, key
biomarkers, key labs, ECOG, stage, brain mets, age, sex, plus the four pre-
specified treatment × biomarker interactions and the pembrolizumab × stk11
interaction:
- Adjusted treatment_pembrolizumab × pdl1_tps interaction beta=+0.595,
  OR 1.81, p=1.6×10⁻⁷. Survives covariate adjustment. h24.1 supported.
- Adjusted treatment_pembrolizumab × stk11_mutation interaction beta=−0.204,
  OR 0.82, p=0.003. Survives adjustment. h24.2 supported.
- Adjusted ecog_ps OR 0.68 (p≈10⁻⁹⁵), stage_iv OR 0.74 (p≈10⁻³⁴), brain_mets
  OR 0.77 (p≈10⁻¹⁹), albumin_g_dl OR 1.10 (p≈10⁻⁴), tmb_high OR 1.13
  (p≈10⁻⁶) — all in the predicted direction. h24.3 supported.
- Adjusted treatment_osimertinib × egfr_mutation: beta=+0.028, p=0.72.
- Adjusted treatment_sotorasib × kras_g12c: beta=+0.062, p=0.41.
- Adjusted treatment_olaparib × brca2_mutation: beta=−0.155, p=0.34.
  All three remain non-significant. h24.4 supported.

Iteration 25 — Refining the TMB × pembro signal
-----------------------------------------------
Stratified by PD-L1 status:
- pdl1_high=0 subgroup (n=39,251): pembrolizumab × tmb_high interaction
  beta=+0.003, p=0.96 — no effect.
- pdl1_high=1 subgroup (n=10,749): pembrolizumab × tmb_high interaction
  beta=+0.690, p=2×10⁻¹⁰.
The TMB signal for pembrolizumab is essentially confined to PD-L1 high
tumors. h25.1 supported. This refines the simpler TMB main story: TMB
predicts pembro response only on top of high PD-L1.

Overall conclusions
===================
1. Pembrolizumab is the only treatment with a real average response benefit,
   but it is small and almost entirely driven by the PD-L1-high subset
   (response 21 % on pembro vs 16 % off, vs no benefit when PD-L1 is low).
2. Tumor mutational burden and PD-L1 act multiplicatively for pembrolizumab:
   the TMB-high effect is concentrated in PD-L1 high patients (Iter 25).
3. STK11 mutation strongly attenuates pembrolizumab response (interaction
   OR ~0.81, p=0.003 univariate; p=0.003 adjusted). KEAP1 mutation does not
   show the same effect in this cohort.
4. Female sex is associated with higher overall response (~9 % higher odds)
   and amplifies pembrolizumab benefit (interaction OR 1.16, p=0.002).
5. The three targeted small molecules (osimertinib, sotorasib, olaparib) show
   no main effect and no significant interaction with their canonical driver
   (egfr_mutation, kras_g12c, brca2_mutation respectively). This is the most
   surprising signal in the cohort and would warrant scrutiny of treatment
   assignment, dose, or response-assessment encoding before drawing biological
   conclusions.
6. Classical poor-prognosis features (high ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, weight
   loss, low albumin, high CRP) all reduce response in the expected
   directions and remain independent in adjusted analysis. ECOG and brain
   mets are purely prognostic — not predictive for any specific treatment.
7. Race/ethnicity, insurance, education, rural residence, comorbidities, prior
   therapy history (with the marginal exception of prior chemotherapy), and
   the 23-SNP drug-metabolism panel show no consistent association with
   objective response after multiple-testing scrutiny.
