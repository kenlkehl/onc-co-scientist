ds001_nsclc — Analysis summary
================================

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patients with binary objective_response (overall ORR
0.169). Four treatments are recorded (pembrolizumab, sotorasib, olaparib,
osimertinib), each independently administered to roughly 30–50% of the cohort.
The dataset has no missing values. All effect estimates below are logistic
regression coefficients on the log-odds scale unless otherwise noted; "OR" is
the corresponding odds ratio.


1. Treatment main effects (Iter 1)
----------------------------------
Pembrolizumab is the only treatment with a significant unadjusted main effect
on ORR (ORR 0.174 on vs 0.164 off; coef +0.070, OR 1.07, p=0.003). Sotorasib,
olaparib, and osimertinib had small, non-significant main effects in the
overall (unselected) cohort (p=0.82, 0.71, 0.18 respectively). This is the
expected pattern for biomarker-targeted agents whose benefit should be
concentrated in their respective marker-positive subgroups.


2. Strong baseline prognostic markers (Iter 2–6)
------------------------------------------------
The classic NSCLC prognostic markers behaved as expected:
  - ecog_ps:                coef −0.375, OR 0.69, p=2e-93   (very strong)
  - albumin_g_dl:           coef +0.091, OR 1.10, p=1.4e-4
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo:    coef −0.038, OR 0.96, p=2e-32   (very strong)
  - crp_mg_l:               coef −0.005,         p=6.9e-4
  - has_brain_mets:         coef −0.252, OR 0.78, p=2e-18
  - stage_iv:               coef −0.295, OR 0.74, p=1.3e-33
  - sex_female:             coef +0.083, OR 1.09, p=5.7e-4   (slightly higher ORR in women)
  - fatigue_grade:          coef −0.022, p=0.04 (small but consistent direction)

Markers that were NOT significant despite biological plausibility:
  - ldh_u_l (p=0.51), nlr (p=0.38) — neither showed a univariate ORR signal
    in this cohort. AST/ALT/total bilirubin/alkaline phosphatase, age, smoking
    pack-years, current/never smoking status, and prior chemo/IO/targeted
    exposure were also non-significant.

Histology (squamous vs adenocarcinoma) showed only a marginal trend (p=0.08).
Liver mets, bone mets, adrenal mets, and pleural effusion did not associate
with ORR univariately, suggesting that, conditional on stage IV and brain
mets, additional sites added little discriminative information.


3. Treatment × biomarker interactions (Iter 7–13)
-------------------------------------------------
Pembrolizumab showed clear, biologically plausible response heterogeneity:

  - Pembrolizumab × pdl1_tps:   coef +0.591, p=1.6e-7  (strongly positive)
        Within PDL1≥0.5 patients: ORR pembro 0.209 vs no-pembro 0.156.
        Within PDL1<0.5 patients: ORR pembro 0.164 vs no-pembro 0.166.
  - Pembrolizumab × tmb_high:   coef +0.184, p=4.2e-4  (positive)
        ORR pembro/TMB-hi 0.197 vs no-pembro/TMB-hi 0.167; pembro/TMB-lo 0.165
        vs no-pembro/TMB-lo 0.163.
  - Pembrolizumab × stk11_mutation: coef −0.211, p=1.8e-3 (negative)
        Pembrolizumab benefit is abolished or reversed in STK11-mutant
        patients: ORR pembro/STK11-mut 0.158 vs no-pembro/STK11-mut 0.173;
        pembro/STK11-wt 0.177 vs no-pembro/STK11-wt 0.162. This is consistent
        with the known role of STK11 mutations as a marker of immune-cold,
        checkpoint-resistant biology.
  - Pembrolizumab × keap1_mutation: not significant (p=0.36).

By contrast, the targeted-therapy × cognate-biomarker interactions were
ALL non-significant in this dataset:

  - Osimertinib × egfr_mutation:   coef +0.024, p=0.76
  - Sotorasib × kras_g12c:         coef +0.059, p=0.43
  - Olaparib × brca2_mutation:     coef −0.178, p=0.28

In a real-world EHR cohort one would expect strong positive interactions for
each of these (osi works selectively in EGFR-mutant disease, sot in KRAS-G12C,
ola in BRCA-deficient tumors). The absence of these interactions here is one
of the most striking features of this dataset and suggests that targeted-agent
exposure in the data was not selectively allocated to (or did not differentially
benefit) marker-positive patients in the way real-world prescribing would predict.

A composite immune-positive indicator (PDL1≥0.5 OR TMB-high) interacted
positively with pembrolizumab (coef +0.191, p=6.8e-5; ORR pembro/immune+ 0.189
vs no-pembro/immune+ 0.163; pembro/immune− 0.162 vs no-pembro/immune− 0.165),
re-confirming that pembrolizumab benefit is concentrated in immune-favorable
patients.


4. Sociodemographic disparities (Iter 14)
-----------------------------------------
Univariate disparities in ORR by race/ethnicity, insurance type, and rural
residence were not significant at α=0.05:
  - race_black:        coef +0.069, p=0.056 (borderline, slightly higher ORR)
  - race_hispanic:     p=0.52
  - race_asian:        p=0.21
  - ins_medicaid:      coef −0.064, p=0.066 (borderline lower ORR)
  - ins_uninsured:     p=0.90
  - rural_residence:   p=0.49

The Medicaid trend is in the expected direction (worse outcomes among
Medicaid-insured patients) but does not reach significance.


5. SNP screen (Iter 15)
-----------------------
Of 23 candidate SNPs screened univariately for an association with ORR, only
two reached p<0.05 (uncorrected for multiple testing):
  - snp_rs7412:    coef +0.100, p=0.018
  - snp_rs1050828: coef −0.099, p=0.031
After Bonferroni correction (α=0.05/23=0.0022) neither survives, so the SNP
panel does not yield convincing pharmacogenomic signal in this cohort.


6. Multivariable adjusted treatment effects (Iter 16)
-----------------------------------------------------
After adjustment for biomarkers, ECOG, age, sex, histology, stage, and
brain/liver mets, the conclusions from Iter 1 strengthened only modestly:
  - treatment_pembrolizumab: adj coef +0.077, OR 1.08, p=1.3e-3  (sig)
  - treatment_sotorasib:     adj coef +0.003, p=0.90             (ns)
  - treatment_olaparib:      adj coef +0.013, p=0.62             (ns)
  - treatment_osimertinib:   adj coef −0.034, p=0.19             (ns)

Pembrolizumab retains a small but significant adjusted main effect; the other
three treatments do not have a population-average ORR benefit in the
unselected cohort.


7. Treatment × demographic interactions (Iter 17–18)
----------------------------------------------------
Age × treatment interactions were all non-significant (p=0.22–0.80), so
treatment-effect heterogeneity does not appear to follow age in this cohort.

Sex × treatment interactions: only pembrolizumab showed sex-related
heterogeneity (sex_female × pembrolizumab coef +0.146, p=2.4e-3 — pembro
benefit appears larger in women). The other treatments showed no sex
interaction.


8. Stage / prior-therapy main effects (Iter 19) and symptom burden (Iter 20)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Prior lines of therapy (p=0.24), years_since_diagnosis (p=0.32), prior_chemo
(p=0.07, marginal lower ORR), prior_immunotherapy (p=0.38), and
prior_targeted_therapy (p=0.30) were not significantly associated with ORR
once each was tested alone. Symptom burden showed only fatigue_grade reaching
significance (p=0.04, negative) — pain, dyspnea, cough, and appetite-loss
grades did not.


9. Liver function and other oncogenic alterations (Iter 21–22)
-------------------------------------------------------------
None of AST, ALT, total bilirubin, or alkaline phosphatase reached
significance. None of TP53, PIK3CA, PTEN loss, MSI-high, HER2 amplification,
MET ex14, RET/ROS1/NTRK fusions, or BRAF V600E showed a significant
univariate association with ORR (BRAF V600E coef +0.13, p=0.11, was the only
trend).


10. Histology × treatment interactions (Iter 23)
------------------------------------------------
Squamous histology did not modify the effect of pembrolizumab (p=0.58) or
osimertinib (p=0.42). NSCLC histology, in this dataset, is not a treatment
effect modifier for these agents.


11. Multivariable model with biomarker interactions (Iter 24)
-------------------------------------------------------------
A single logistic model that included all four treatments, all major
biomarkers, all clinical covariates, and the six treatment × biomarker
interaction terms confirmed the univariate findings:
  - pembro × pdl1_tps:        adj coef +0.594, p=1.7e-7  (sig, positive)
  - pembro × tmb_high:        adj coef +0.174, p=9.5e-4  (sig, positive)
  - pembro × stk11_mutation:  adj coef −0.205, p=2.6e-3  (sig, negative)
  - osi × egfr_mutation:      adj coef +0.029, p=0.71    (ns)
  - sot × kras_g12c:          adj coef +0.062, p=0.41    (ns)
  - ola × brca2_mutation:     adj coef −0.155, p=0.34    (ns)

The pembrolizumab-related interactions are robust to multivariable adjustment;
the targeted-therapy × biomarker interactions remain absent.


12. Composite immune signature × pembrolizumab (Iter 25)
--------------------------------------------------------
A simple PDL1≥0.5 OR TMB-high indicator yielded a clean and interpretable
positive interaction with pembrolizumab (coef +0.191, p=6.8e-5), supporting
the use of a combined immunogenicity gate for selecting pembrolizumab
candidates in this cohort.


Overall conclusions
===================
1. Pembrolizumab is the only treatment with a significant overall ORR benefit,
   and that benefit is concentrated in patients with high PDL1 expression
   and/or high TMB. STK11 mutations strongly attenuate pembrolizumab benefit,
   consistent with established immune-cold biology.
2. Sotorasib, olaparib, and osimertinib showed no overall ORR benefit and,
   importantly, no significant interaction with their canonical predictive
   biomarkers (KRAS-G12C, BRCA2, EGFR mutation respectively). This is
   biologically inconsistent with how these agents work in real practice and
   is the most surprising single finding of the analysis.
3. Strong prognostic markers (worse ECOG, low albumin, weight loss, brain
   mets, stage IV) are consistently and significantly negatively associated
   with ORR, while several conventionally cited markers (LDH, NLR) were not.
4. Sociodemographic disparities (race, insurance, rural residence) did not
   reach significance, with only Medicaid status showing a borderline negative
   trend.
5. The SNP panel did not yield robust pharmacogenomic signal once multiple
   testing is considered.
6. Pembrolizumab benefit appears larger in women than in men in this cohort
   (sex_female × pembrolizumab interaction p=0.002), an observation that would
   warrant validation in an external dataset.

Hypotheses that were SUPPORTED:
  Pembrolizumab main effect (h1.1); ECOG, albumin, weight loss, brain mets,
  stage IV, sex (h2.1, 2.2, 3.3, 4.1, 4.4, 5.2); CRP (h3.2);
  pembrolizumab × PDL1 (h7.1); pembrolizumab × TMB-high (h8.1);
  pembrolizumab × STK11 (h9.1); fatigue (h20.1);
  pembrolizumab adjusted main effect (h16.1); pembrolizumab × sex (h18.1);
  composite immune signature × pembrolizumab (h25.1); the multivariable
  reaffirmations of pembro interactions (h24.1–h24.3).

Hypotheses that were REFUTED (or not supported):
  Sotorasib, olaparib, osimertinib main effects (h1.2–h1.4) and adjusted
  versions (h16.2–h16.4); LDH, NLR, age (h2.3, 3.1, 5.1); bone/liver/adrenal
  mets, pleural effusion (h4.2, 4.3, 4.5); histology and smoking variables
  (h6.x); pembrolizumab × KEAP1 (h13.1); osimertinib × EGFR (h10.1, h24.4);
  sotorasib × KRAS-G12C (h11.1, h24.5); olaparib × BRCA2 (h12.1, h24.6);
  race/insurance/rural disparities (h14.x); SNPs after correction (h15.x);
  age × treatment interactions (h17.x); sex × treatments other than
  pembrolizumab (h18.2–18.4); prior therapy and most symptom-burden grades
  (h19.x, h20.2–20.5); liver-function tests (h21.x); other oncogenic
  alterations (h22.x); histology × treatment interactions (h23.x).
