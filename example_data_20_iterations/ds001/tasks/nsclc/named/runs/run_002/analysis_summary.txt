Synthesis and conclusions
-------------------------
Treatment main effects (Iteration 1)
- Only treatment_pembrolizumab showed a significant main-effect on objective_response in this
  cohort (ORR 0.174 on vs 0.164 off; risk diff +0.010; chi2 p=3.4e-3). The three targeted agents
  (treatment_sotorasib, treatment_osimertinib, treatment_olaparib) showed no significant
  unstratified effect on objective_response. This is the first signal that response benefit, when
  it exists, is concentrated in biomarker-defined subgroups rather than across the whole arm.

Pembrolizumab biomarker hypotheses (Iterations 2-5, 13-14, 18-20, 22)
- SUPPORTED: pembrolizumab response increases with pdl1_tps. Within-pembro pdl1_tps slope
  log-odds +0.45 (p=7.6e-9). The treatment_pembrolizumab × pdl1_tps interaction is strongly
  positive (log-odds +0.59, p=1.6e-7). Stratified: PD-L1-high (TPS>=0.5) ORR on/off pembro
  0.209/0.156 (RD +0.054, p=7.7e-13) versus PD-L1-low RD -0.002 (ns). Holds in both
  adenocarcinoma (interaction +0.48, p=3.5e-4) and squamous (interaction +0.89, p=2.9e-5).
- SUPPORTED: pembrolizumab × tmb_high positive interaction (log-odds +0.18, p=4.2e-4); ORR in
  pembro arm 0.197 if TMB-high vs 0.165 (RD +0.032, p=2e-9).
- SUPPORTED: pembrolizumab × stk11_mutation negative interaction (log-odds -0.21, p=1.8e-3);
  in pembro arm STK11-mutant ORR 0.158 vs WT 0.177 (RD -0.019, p=5.3e-3). STK11 mutation
  attenuates and likely reverses pembrolizumab benefit in this cohort.
- REFUTED: pembrolizumab × keap1_mutation interaction was not significant (log-odds -0.06,
  p=0.36). Within pembro arm KEAP1-mutant ORR 0.171 vs WT 0.174 (ns). KEAP1 mutation does not
  appear to attenuate pembrolizumab benefit in these data, contrary to a common prior.
- REFUTED: pembrolizumab × tp53_mutation interaction was not significant (log-odds +0.03,
  p=0.56). TP53 status is not a useful predictive biomarker for pembrolizumab here.
- REFUTED: oncogene-driver (egfr_mutation OR alk_fusion) status did not significantly modify
  pembrolizumab response in pembro arm (RD -0.008, p=0.24), although the directional sign was
  consistent with the prior expectation.
- REFUTED: pembrolizumab × ecog_ps interaction was not significant (log-odds -0.04, p=0.32);
  in this cohort ECOG modifies prognosis but not the relative pembrolizumab effect.

Targeted-therapy biomarker hypotheses (Iterations 6-8, 15, 23, 25) — not supported
- The expected positive interaction between treatment_sotorasib and kras_g12c was not detected
  (log-odds +0.06, p=0.43; adjusted p=0.41). In G12C+ patients sotorasib added only +0.008 to
  ORR (ns). No off-target signal in non-G12C either (RD -0.0003, p=0.95).
- The expected positive interaction between treatment_osimertinib and egfr_mutation was not
  detected (log-odds +0.02, p=0.76; adjusted p=0.69). EGFR+ patients on osimertinib actually
  had numerically lower ORR (0.164 vs 0.166, ns). No osimertinib effect in EGFR-WT either
  (RD -0.005, p=0.17).
- The expected positive interaction between treatment_olaparib and brca2_mutation was not
  detected; the directional sign was actually negative (log-odds -0.18, p=0.28; adjusted
  p=0.36). Among BRCA2-mutant patients olaparib was associated with a numerically lower ORR
  (0.146 on vs 0.168 off, n=417 on; ns).
- Within osimertinib+EGFR+ subset (n=1973), pdl1_tps did not predict objective_response
  (slope +0.04, p=0.90), as expected for oncogene-addicted biology — but moot since the
  parent treatment effect is null. Within sotorasib+G12C subset (n=2246), pdl1_tps was
  positively associated with response (slope +0.83, p=1.6e-3), an unexpected post-hoc finding
  that should be considered exploratory.
- Conclusion: this dataset does not contain a strong response signal for sotorasib in KRAS
  G12C, osimertinib in EGFR, or olaparib in BRCA2. Whether this reflects a property of the
  data-generating process or genuine real-world heterogeneity, the pre-specified
  treatment-biomarker matching hypotheses for these three agents are not supported.

Prognostic main effects (Iterations 9-11, 21)
- SUPPORTED (large effects): ecog_ps log-odds -0.375/unit (p=1.7e-93); weight_loss_pct_6mo
  log-odds -0.039/unit (p=1.8e-32); has_brain_mets RD -0.034 (p=2e-18); albumin_g_dl positive
  slope +0.091 (p=1.4e-4); crp_mg_l negative slope -0.005 (p=6.9e-4); fatigue_grade negative
  (p=4e-2).
- REFUTED in this cohort: nlr (p=0.38), ldh_u_l (p=0.51), liver_mets (p=0.34), bone_mets
  (p=0.18), pain_nrs (p=0.78), dyspnea_grade (p=0.49), appetite_loss_grade (p=0.35) all failed
  to show a significant main-effect on objective_response, despite expected clinical priors.

Demographics (Iteration 16)
- sex_female showed a small positive association (RD +0.012, p=5.9e-4). race_ethnicity reached
  nominal significance (omnibus p=0.045). insurance_type, age_years, and rural_residence were
  not associated with objective_response.

Adjusted analysis (Iterations 17, 25)
- In a multivariable logit adjusted for ECOG, albumin, LDH, NLR, brain mets, liver mets, age,
  and stage_iv, treatment_pembrolizumab retained a significant positive main effect (log-odds
  +0.077, p=1.3e-3) while sotorasib, osimertinib, and olaparib did not. The single combined
  model with all four treatment x biomarker interactions confirmed only one significant
  predictive interaction: treatment_pembrolizumab × pdl1_tps (log-odds +0.60, p=1.3e-7).

Null screen (Iteration 24)
- Across 23 candidate SNPs main-effect logits, only 2 reached nominal p<0.05 (~5% expected by
  chance). No SNP-level signal that survives the simplest multiple-comparisons consideration.

Bottom line
-----------
The dominant clinically meaningful pattern in this cohort is pembrolizumab benefit modulated by
PD-L1 TPS (strong positive predictive interaction), TMB-high (positive predictive), and
STK11 mutation (negative predictive). The expected biomarker-matched response signals for
sotorasib (KRAS G12C), osimertinib (EGFR), and olaparib (BRCA2) were NOT observed in this
dataset. Standard prognostic effects of ECOG performance status, albumin, weight loss, CRP,
and brain metastases on response were confirmed; several other clinical priors (NLR, LDH,
liver/bone mets, KEAP1 with pembro) were not supported in these data. SNP variants showed no
coordinated effect on response.
