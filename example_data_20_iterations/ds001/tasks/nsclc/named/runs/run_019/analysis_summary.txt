ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (50000 patients, binary outcome: objective_response)

Approach: 25 iterations of propose-test-refine on EHR-derived NSCLC cohort with patient,
tumor, biomarker, lab, treatment, comorbidity, symptom, demographic, and SNP features.
Statistical methods: Fisher exact for 2x2 contingency tables; logistic regression for
continuous predictors and main / interaction effects; chi-square for higher-cardinality
categorical contrasts.

Overall ORR: 0.169 (8447/50000).

KEY THEMES & CONCLUSIONS (synthesizing what the data actually showed)
1. Among the four biomarker-treatment pairings tested, ONLY pdl1_tps x
   treatment_pembrolizumab shows a strong, reproducible positive interaction:
   - Interaction beta=+0.59 (OR ~1.8 per unit pdl1_tps among pembrolizumab-treated
     patients), p ~ 1.6e-7. Within pembro+: pdl1_tps positively predicts ORR (p ~ 7.6e-9).
     Within pembro-: pdl1_tps trends negatively/null (p=0.09). At pdl1_tps>=0.5,
     pembrolizumab ORR is 0.21 vs 0.16 (OR 1.43, p ~ 7e-13).
   - The hypothesized egfr_mutation x treatment_osimertinib interaction was NOT
     supported (interaction p=0.76). EGFR-mutant patients did not show higher ORR on
     osimertinib than EGFR-mutant patients off osimertinib in this cohort.
   - The hypothesized kras_g12c x treatment_sotorasib interaction was NOT supported
     (p=0.43); the point estimate was small and positive but not significant.
   - The hypothesized brca2_mutation x treatment_olaparib interaction was NOT
     supported (interaction beta=-0.18, p=0.27).
   - Net result: as a composite, matched_therapy receipt did not improve ORR
     (matched vs not matched: ORR 0.166 vs 0.169, p=0.66; adjusted OR 0.98, p=0.67).
2. tmb_high is independently associated with higher ORR (OR ~1.14, p ~ 1e-6 main
   effect; OR ~1.20 interaction with pembrolizumab, p ~ 4e-4): TMB-high contributes
   an additional positive effect that is enhanced in the pembrolizumab-treated stratum.
3. Patient/disease severity markers are the most robust predictors of ORR:
   - higher ecog_ps lowers ORR strongly (ECOG2 ORR=0.11 vs ECOG0 ORR=0.21; per-unit
     OR 0.69, p ~ 1.7e-93). The single strongest predictor in the dataset.
   - higher weight_loss_pct_6mo lowers ORR (OR 0.96 per unit, p ~ 2e-32).
   - lower albumin_g_dl lowers ORR (OR 1.10 per g/dL higher, p ~ 1e-4).
   - higher crp_mg_l lowers ORR (p ~ 7e-4). nlr trended negative but did not reach
     significance in main effect (p=0.38).
   - stage_iv lowers ORR (OR 0.74, p ~ 3e-33), as does has_brain_mets (OR 0.78,
     p ~ 6e-19). liver_mets, bone_mets, pleural_effusion did NOT differentiate ORR.
4. STK11 mutation modulates immunotherapy: STK11 had no overall main effect, but a
   significant negative stk11_mutation x treatment_pembrolizumab interaction
   (beta=-0.21, p=0.002). Within pembrolizumab-treated, ORR is 0.158 in STK11+ vs
   0.177 in STK11- (p=0.005). KEAP1 trended in the same direction but was not
   significant.
5. Within EGFR+, prior_targeted_therapy did NOT significantly modify the osimertinib
   effect (p=0.78). The hypothesized 'resistance after prior TKI' pattern was not
   detectable in this cohort.
6. Demographics:
   - sex_female is associated with higher ORR (OR 1.09, p ~ 6e-4) overall and remains
     significant in the final adjusted model (adj OR 1.08, p ~ 8e-4).
   - race_ethnicity showed marginal global association (chi2 p=0.045) with white the
     lowest ORR group; this association was not significant after adjustment for
     biomarkers and labs.
   - age_years, insurance_type, rural_residence had no significant ORR association.
   - matched_therapy receipt did not differ significantly across race or insurance
     strata (p>0.15), so access disparities to biomarker-matched therapy are not
     evident here.
7. Symptom grades: only fatigue_grade reached p<0.05 (negative); pain_nrs,
   dyspnea_grade, cough_grade, appetite_loss_grade did not significantly predict ORR.
8. Pharmacogenomic SNPs (snp_*): 2/23 nominally significant at p<0.05 (binomial test
   p=0.32), consistent with the chance rate under the null. No systematic
   pharmacogenomic signal for ORR in this cohort.
9. Rare oncogenic drivers (alk_fusion, ros1_fusion, ret_fusion, braf_v600e,
   met_exon14_skipping, ntrk_fusion, her2_amplification): none individually reached
   significance for ORR in this cohort.
10. prior_immunotherapy x treatment_pembrolizumab interaction was significantly
    POSITIVE (beta=+0.135, p=0.044) — opposite of the prior expectation that prior IO
    would reduce pembrolizumab response, suggesting this cohort does not show the
    classic 'IO re-challenge' decrement pattern.
11. Final integrated multivariable model: positive significant predictors of ORR are
    pdl1_tps:treatment_pembrolizumab (OR 1.81), tmb_high (OR 1.14), albumin_g_dl
    (OR 1.10), sex_female (OR 1.08). Negative significant predictors are ecog_ps
    (OR 0.68), stage_iv (OR 0.74), has_brain_mets (OR 0.77), weight_loss_pct_6mo
    (OR 0.96 per unit), crp_mg_l (OR 0.995 per unit). The matched-targeted-therapy
    interactions for EGFR/KRAS-G12C/BRCA2 do NOT survive in the adjusted model.

Bottom line: in this 50,000-patient NSCLC cohort, the dominant axis of objective
response is the immunotherapy-PD-L1/TMB axis combined with patient performance status
and disease burden. Classic 'biomarker-matched targeted therapy' for EGFR, KRAS G12C,
and BRCA2 was not detectable as a positive ORR effect in this dataset, suggesting
either substantial heterogeneity, treatment-effect dilution by biomarker-discordant
use, or absence of the encoded pharmacology in the data-generating process.
