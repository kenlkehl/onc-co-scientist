ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (n=50,000)
==========================================

Outcome: objective_response (binary). Marginal ORR in the cohort = 16.89%.
Approach: 25 propose-test-refine iterations (see transcript.json for the full hypothesis log
and quantitative effect estimates / p-values).

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Treatment main effects on ORR
------------------------------------------------------------
- treatment_pembrolizumab: ORR 17.4% on vs 16.4% off, +0.99 pp, chi-sq p=0.0034. SIGNIFICANT
  but small in absolute magnitude — averaged across all comers.
- treatment_sotorasib (16.95% vs 16.87%, p=0.83), treatment_olaparib (16.99% vs 16.85%, p=0.72),
  treatment_osimertinib (16.55% vs 17.04%, p=0.18): NO marginal effect. Each of these
  targeted therapies, when given to all-comers, does not move ORR. This already hints that
  benefit is concentrated in biomarker-defined subgroups, but…

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Matched targeted-therapy × biomarker interactions
------------------------------------------------------------
Surprisingly, the canonical "matched targeted therapy" interactions are NOT supported
in this cohort:
- egfr_mutation × osimertinib: interaction log-odds +0.024, p=0.76 (NS).
- kras_g12c × sotorasib: +0.059, p=0.43 (NS); direction correct (G12C+ on sotorasib 17.5%
  vs off 16.6%) but magnitude too small.
- brca2_mutation × olaparib: -0.18, p=0.27 (NS); direction is actually opposite.

In a real EHR cohort one would still expect some signal; in this dataset the targeted
agent effect on the binary objective_response endpoint is essentially flat in matched
biomarker carriers. Hypotheses h5–h7, h13, h41–h43 were therefore REFUTED.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. The PD-L1 / TMB / STK11 axis with pembrolizumab — the central finding
------------------------------------------------------------
In contrast, the immunotherapy biomarker biology is clearly present:

- pdl1_tps (continuous) main effect on ORR: log-odds +0.166, p=0.0032. SIGNIFICANT.
- pdl1_tps × pembrolizumab interaction (continuous): +0.591, p=1.6e-7. STRONG.
- ORR by PD-L1 quartile, pembro vs no pembro: Q1 16.8/16.6, Q2 16.2/16.8, Q3 16.3/16.7,
  Q4 (highest PD-L1) 20.1 vs 15.4. The benefit is concentrated in the top quartile.
- Refined to the clinically meaningful PD-L1≥50% threshold: interaction +0.376, p=5e-11.
  In PD-L1≥50% (n=10,749): pembro ORR 20.93% vs no-pembro 15.57%, +5.4 pp absolute
  (relative ~+34%), chi-square p=7.7e-13.
- In PD-L1<50%: pembro 16.4% vs no-pembro 16.6%, no benefit (p=0.59).

- tmb_high main effect: +0.127, p=9.8e-7. SIGNIFICANT.
- tmb_high × pembrolizumab interaction: +0.184, p=0.00041. SIGNIFICANT.
  Pembro raises ORR from 16.7% to 19.7% in TMB-high vs 16.3% to 16.5% in TMB-low.

- stk11_mutation × pembrolizumab interaction: -0.211, p=0.0018. SIGNIFICANT.
  Pembro raised ORR from 16.2% to 17.7% in STK11-WT but DROPPED it from 17.3% to 15.8%
  in STK11-mut — supports the immunotherapy-resistance literature.
- keap1_mutation × pembrolizumab: -0.058, p=0.36 (NS).

- Three-way refinement: within PD-L1≥50%, the pembrolizumab benefit is concentrated in
  STK11-wildtype: ORR 21.7% vs 15.4% on/off pembro (n=9,149); STK11-mutant: 16.3% vs
  16.8% (n=1,600), no benefit. STK11 mutation appears to abolish the PD-L1-high/pembro
  benefit. (Hypotheses h8-h13, h50-h53 SUPPORTED; h11 about KEAP1 NOT supported.)

- Continuous lab × pembro interactions: only nlr × pembro reached nominal significance
  (+0.033, p=0.0046, single-test); higher NLR patients had a larger pembro effect
  (provocative; one of many tested, not corrected).

------------------------------------------------------------
These dominate the variance in ORR:
- ecog_ps: log-odds -0.375 per unit, p=1.7e-93. Largest single predictor.
- stage_iv: -0.295, p=1.3e-33.
- has_brain_mets: -0.252, p=2.0e-18.
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.039 per percent, p=1.8e-32.
- albumin_g_dl: +0.091, p=0.00014.
- crp_mg_l: -0.005 per mg/L, p=0.00069.
- sex_female: +0.083, p=0.00057.
- coronary_artery_disease: -0.062, p=0.049 (borderline).
- fatigue_grade: -0.022, p=0.040 (borderline).

NOT significantly associated: age_years (p=0.67), bmi (p=0.35), smoking_pack_years
(p=0.76), education_years (p=0.40), pain/dyspnea/cough/appetite-loss grades, all
14 other comorbidities, all four prior-treatment exposures, prior_lines_of_therapy,
years_since_diagnosis, ldh_u_l, nlr (marginal), spo2_pct, and the various non-brain
metastasis sites (liver, bone, adrenal, pleural, pericardial, contralateral-lung).

(Hypotheses h14-h19, h27-h28, h20, h34-h35 SUPPORTED; h16-h17, h21-h26, h29-h30,
h44-h47 REFUTED.)

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Equity / sociodemographic signals
------------------------------------------------------------
- race_ethnicity: differences across categories small. White vs Asian baseline -0.083,
  p=0.099 (trend). Other categories not significantly different. ORR by group: Asian
  17.7%, Black 17.8%, Hispanic 17.2%, Other 18.5%, White 16.5%. No strong race signal
  (h31 NOT supported).
- insurance_type: Medicaid baseline ORR 16.1%, private 17.2%, Medicare 16.8%, uninsured
  17.0%. Private vs Medicaid +0.077, p=0.040. SIGNIFICANT.
- Crucially, when the model is adjusted for ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, weight loss,
  albumin, CRP, sex, age, PD-L1, TMB, STK11, and pembrolizumab effects, the
  private-vs-Medicaid coefficient REMAINS significant (+0.089, p=0.019). My
  iter-25 hypothesis that this would be confounded away was REFUTED — suggesting an
  insurance-related disparity in ORR that is not explained by the prognostic and
  biomarker variables in this dataset (h57 REFUTED in a clinically-meaningful way).
- rural_residence: NS (p=0.49).

------------------------------------------------------------
8. Multivariable model (iter 18-19)
------------------------------------------------------------
Logit(objective_response) ~ age + sex + ecog_ps + stage_iv + brain_mets + albumin
+ crp + weight_loss + pdl1_tps + tmb_high + stk11_mutation + treatment_pembrolizumab
+ pdl1×pembro + stk11×pembro + tmb×pembro yields:

- ECOG -0.382 (p<1e-90), stage_iv -0.302 (p<1e-30), brain_mets -0.257 (p<1e-17),
  weight_loss -0.039 (p<1e-30), albumin +0.092 (p<0.001), crp -0.005 (p<0.001),
  sex_female +0.081 (p=0.001) — all retain significance.
- treatment_pembrolizumab MAIN effect -0.132 (p=0.005, NEGATIVE in adjusted model).
- pdl1_tps × pembro +0.595 (p<1e-6); stk11 × pembro -0.204 (p=0.003); tmb × pembro
  +0.174 (p=0.001) — all three interactions significant after adjustment.
- Interpretation: pembrolizumab confers no benefit (and possibly slight harm) for
  the average / biomarker-undefined patient; benefit is real and clinically large
  but tightly conditioned on PD-L1 high, TMB high, and STK11 wildtype (h50, h56
  SUPPORTED).

------------------------------------------------------------
9. Headline conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------
A. ORR is dominated by classical disease-burden / cachexia / inflammation prognostic
   factors (ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, weight loss, albumin, CRP).
B. Pembrolizumab works in a sharply biomarker-defined subgroup: PD-L1≥50% and
   TMB-high, with STK11 wildtype. In PD-L1≥50% & STK11-WT, pembro raises ORR by
   ~6 pp absolute. Outside that subgroup it has essentially no effect.
C. Targeted-therapy × biomarker pairings (osimertinib/EGFR, sotorasib/KRAS-G12C,
   olaparib/BRCA2) do NOT show the expected enrichment for objective_response in
   this synthetic-feeling cohort. Either the response endpoint is too noisy for the
   sample size to detect them at p<0.05, or these effects are not encoded in the data
   the way they would be in a real-world matched-therapy cohort.
D. Sex (female>male) and insurance type (private>Medicaid) show small but reproducible
   ORR differences. The insurance gap PERSISTS after adjusting for prognostic and
   biomarker variables, suggesting a disparity worth flagging — though residual
   confounding (e.g., access, supportive care, regimen choice not captured here)
   cannot be ruled out.
E. Common comorbidities, age, BMI, smoking pack-years, education, race/ethnicity
   (within the cohort), rural residence, and a 23-SNP panel show no robust ORR
   association.

Key clinical takeaway: the cohort recapitulates the high-PD-L1 / TMB / STK11
biology of pembrolizumab response and the dominant role of performance status /
disease burden in NSCLC outcomes; matched-targeted-therapy benefit is not
detectable in this dataset; an unexplained insurance-related response gap persists
after adjustment and would warrant further investigation in a real cohort.
