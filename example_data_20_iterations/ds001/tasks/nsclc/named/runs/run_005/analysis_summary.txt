ds001_nsclc -- Analysis Summary
================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with NSCLC. Outcome: objective_response (binary; overall
rate 16.9%). Across 25 iterations we tested main effects, biomarker x treatment
interactions, multi-way interactions, and multivariable adjusted models, with
particular focus on the four observed therapies (pembrolizumab, sotorasib,
olaparib, osimertinib) and their canonical biomarkers (PD-L1 TPS, TMB, KRAS
G12C, EGFR, BRCA2, STK11/KEAP1).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Treatment main effects (iter 1)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Of the four therapies, only pembrolizumab showed a positive main effect on
objective_response in the unadjusted comparison (17.4% on vs 16.4% off, OR 1.07,
chi2 p = 3.4e-3). Sotorasib (p = 0.83), olaparib (p = 0.72), and osimertinib
(p = 0.18, point estimate slightly negative) all had null overall effects. This
matches a clinical reality in which the targeted drugs only help the matched
biomarker subgroup, so an unselected average is diluted to ~0.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Biomarker-matched targeted-therapy interactions (iter 2)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Surprisingly, none of the three matched biomarker-by-treatment interactions
reached significance in this dataset:
  * EGFR x osimertinib:  interaction p = 0.76; in EGFR+ patients osimertinib
    actually had a tiny negative point estimate (-0.20 pp); in EGFR- patients
    -0.54 pp. Hypothesis h2a (positive EGFR x osimertinib interaction) was
    not supported.
  * KRAS_G12C x sotorasib: interaction p = 0.43; KRAS_G12C+ patients had a
    +0.81 pp point estimate on sotorasib but the interaction was not
    significant. h2b not supported.
  * BRCA2 x olaparib: interaction p = 0.27; BRCA2+ patients on olaparib had a
    NEGATIVE point estimate (-2.16 pp), the opposite of expectation. h2c not
    supported.
Interpretation: the targeted-therapy efficacy signals expected from clinical
trial literature are not encoded in this dataset - either because the dataset
does not simulate a benefit, or because confounding (channeling) cancels it.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Pembrolizumab x PD-L1 TPS interaction (iter 3, 17, 19, 23, 24)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
This is the strongest and most consistently reproduced finding in the cohort.
  * Continuous interaction (logistic): pdl1_tps:treatment_pembrolizumab
    coef = +0.591, p = 1.6e-7 (h3a strongly supported).
  * Dichotomized PD-L1 high (TPS >= 0.5): pembrolizumab raises response from
    15.6% to 20.9% (delta +5.4 pp) in PD-L1 high, but PD-L1 low patients
    show essentially no benefit (-0.2 pp). Interaction p = 5.0e-11
    (h3b strongly supported).
  * The interaction holds within both histologies (adenocarcinoma p = 3.5e-4;
    squamous p = 2.9e-5; h17a, h17b supported), within ecog_ps <= 1
    (p = 2.1e-5, h23a), and above the albumin median (p = 4.9e-5, h23b).
  * In a multivariable logistic model adjusting for ecog_ps, stage_iv, age,
    albumin, ldh, tmb_high, stk11_mutation, pdl1_tps, the pembrolizumab
    main effect remains positive (coef +0.076, p = 1.5e-3, h19a) and the
    pdl1_tps:pembrolizumab interaction stays at +0.601 (p = 1.2e-7, h19b).
  * Adding race_ethnicity, insurance_type, sex, NLR, CRP and weight loss as
    additional covariates does not weaken the interaction (coef +0.604,
    p = 1.1e-7, h24a).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Pembrolizumab x TMB high interaction (iter 4)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Supported. tmb_high=1 patients gain +2.95 pp from pembrolizumab (16.7% -> 19.7%),
while tmb_high=0 patients gain +0.20 pp. Interaction logit coef = +0.184,
p = 4.1e-4 (h4a supported).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Pembrolizumab x STK11 / KEAP1 (iter 4, 18)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Supported as a NEGATIVE interaction. STK11+ patients lose 1.5 pp on
pembrolizumab (15.8% on vs 17.3% off), while STK11- patients gain 1.4 pp
(17.7% vs 16.2%). Interaction logit coef = -0.211, p = 1.8e-3 (h4b supported).
The STK11+KEAP1 co-mutated subgroup showed an even more negative point estimate
(-2.84 pp on pembro), but the formal three-way interaction did not reach
significance (p = 0.063, h18a borderline).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
6. Three-way PD-L1 x TMB x pembrolizumab (iter 21)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Patients positive for both PD-L1 high AND TMB high have a dramatically larger
pembro benefit. Stratifying by bio_score (sum of pdl1_high and tmb_high):
  * bio_score = 0 (n=28,143): pembro effect -0.21 pp.
  * bio_score = 1 (n=18,707): pembro effect +0.61 pp.
  * bio_score = 2 (n=3,150):  pembro effect +14.07 pp (29.4% vs 15.5%).
Three-way interaction coef = +1.42, p = 3.0e-8 (h21a supported). Within pembro
recipients, PD-L1 high & TMB high patients had OR 2.09 vs all other strata
(p = 1.3e-38, h21b strongly supported).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
7. Biomarker mismatch (iter 22)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Among biomarker-negative patients, receiving the matched targeted therapy did
NOT significantly reduce response (osimertinib in EGFR-: p = 0.17; sotorasib in
KRAS-: p = 0.95; olaparib in BRCA2-: p = 0.60). h22a-c not supported. The
data simply do not encode a strong "wrong drug hurts" signal.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Prognostic main effects (iter 5, 6, 7, 13, 16, 25)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Strongly supported, classically directional:
  * ecog_ps: response 21.1% / 15.7% / 11.1% across PS 0/1/2. Logit coef
    -0.375, p = 1.7e-93 (h5a). In the global multivariable model the
    coefficient is essentially unchanged (-0.382, p = 6.0e-96, h25a).
  * stage_iv = 1 reduces response from 19.6% to 15.4% (OR 0.74, p = 1.1e-33;
    h6a).
  * has_brain_mets reduces response 17.8% -> 14.4% (OR 0.78, p = 2.0e-18;
    h6b).
  * weight_loss_pct_6mo: logit coef -0.0385, p = 1.8e-32 (h7e).
  * albumin_g_dl: logit coef +0.091, p = 1.4e-4 (h7a, also positive in the
    multivariable model with p = 1.0e-4, h25b).
  * crp_mg_l: logit coef -0.0049, p = 6.9e-4 (h7d).
  * fatigue_grade: logit coef -0.022, p = 0.040 (h5b, marginal).

Null findings (point estimates close to zero, p > 0.1):
  * ldh_u_l (h7b NULL despite expectation), nlr (h7c NULL),
    liver_mets (h6c), bone_mets (h6d), hemoglobin, alkaline phosphatase,
    bmi, spo2, cea, age_years, all symptom grades except fatigue,
    all comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, HF, AID, ILD,
    HIV+), all prior therapy exposures, prior_lines_of_therapy.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
9. Demographics, sex, smoking, histology (iter 8, 9, 20)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  * sex_female has a small but significant positive effect: 17.5% in women
    vs 16.4% in men (OR 1.09, p = 5.9e-4, h8b). The sex x pembrolizumab
    interaction is also positive (coef +0.146, p = 2.4e-3, h20a) - women
    appear to gain more from pembrolizumab than men.
  * Age, smoking_status main effects, histology (squamous vs adeno;
    p = 0.078), rural_residence, education_years, insurance_type were all
    null (h8a, h8c, h8d, h9b, h9c, h9d not supported).
  * race_ethnicity reached marginal significance unadjusted (chi2 p = 0.045,
    range 16.5-18.5%) but in the multivariable adjusted model none of the
    race coefficients was significant (min p = 0.12; h24b - "no signal" -
    supported in the adjusted analysis).
  * Age x pembrolizumab interaction null (h20b). Never-smoker x pembro null
    (h20c).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
10. Driver mutations and co-alterations (iter 10, 11)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Other rare drivers and co-mutations had null univariate associations with
objective_response: ALK, MET ex14, RET, ROS1, BRAF V600E, NTRK, HER2 amp,
NRG1, FGFR, TP53, KEAP1, PIK3CA, PTEN loss, CDKN2A loss, MSI-high (all p > 0.05;
h10a-i, h11a-f not supported individually).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
11. SNP screen (iter 14)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Across 23 single-nucleotide polymorphisms tested individually, only two reached
nominal p < 0.05 - snp_rs7412 (OR 1.11, p = 0.019) and snp_rs1050828 (OR 0.91,
p = 0.033). Both fall above any sensible Bonferroni threshold and should be
interpreted as plausibly null after correction for multiple testing
(approximately one or two false positives expected by chance over 23 tests).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
12. Final multivariable synthesis (iter 25)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
A logistic regression of objective_response on all four treatments, the major
biomarkers, prognostic labs, demographics, and prior-therapy variables yields:
  * ecog_ps: -0.382 (p = 6e-96) - by far the dominant predictor.
  * albumin_g_dl: +0.094 (p = 1e-4) positive (canonical).
  * ldh_u_l: +9e-5, p = 0.56 (NOT directional, h25b half-supported).
  * treatment_pembrolizumab: +0.077 (p = 1.4e-3) positive (h25c supported).
  * treatment_osimertinib / sotorasib / olaparib: all non-significant
    (p = 0.19, 0.82, ...; h25d supported - unselected use shows no benefit).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Conclusions
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The strongest, most reproducible signals in ds001_nsclc are:

  (a) PD-L1 TPS x pembrolizumab interaction (positive, consistent across
      histology and prognostic subgroups, robust to multivariable adjustment).
  (b) TMB-high x pembrolizumab interaction (positive, smaller magnitude).
  (c) STK11 x pembrolizumab interaction (negative; ICI resistance signature).
  (d) The PD-L1-high & TMB-high subgroup is where pembrolizumab delivers its
      large, clinically meaningful response advantage (~14 pp absolute).
  (e) Classical prognostic factors - ECOG performance status, stage IV /
      brain metastases, albumin, CRP, weight loss - all behave as expected.

Hypotheses NOT supported include the matched-biomarker efficacy signals for
the three targeted drugs (EGFR-osimertinib, KRAS_G12C-sotorasib,
BRCA2-olaparib), most non-IO drivers, comorbidities, and SES variables. After
adjustment, race_ethnicity has no detectable association with response.

Overall picture: the cohort behaves like a real-world NSCLC dataset in which
the central "live" treatment-biomarker effect is the immune checkpoint
inhibitor signal driven by PD-L1 expression, TMB, and STK11 status, while
prognostic biology (ECOG, stage, albumin, CRP, weight loss) dominates baseline
response. The targeted-therapy arms in this dataset do not show evidence of a
matched-biomarker efficacy boost.
