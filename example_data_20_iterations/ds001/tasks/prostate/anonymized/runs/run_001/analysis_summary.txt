Analysis summary — ds001_prostate (50,000 patients, 122 anonymized features + pfs_months)
============================================================================================

Goal: identify clinically meaningful patterns in pfs_months across features, including main
effects, subgroup heterogeneity, and multivariable interactions, refining hypotheses across
up to 25 iterations.

OVERALL HEADLINE
----------------
A compact 5-predictor main-effects model (feature_078 + feature_057 + log(feature_013) +
feature_006 + feature_009) plus a single biomarker-by-treatment interaction
(feature_109 × feature_039) modified by two further variables (Gleason-like feature_067 and
feature_043) explains 98% of the variance in pfs_months. Race (feature_018) and insurance
(feature_045) have essentially no direct effect on pfs_months and only marginal interaction
with the responder benefit. The 76 remaining binary features and the 5-level ordinal/
11-level categorical features carry little to no incremental prognostic information.

MAIN EFFECTS (iterations 1-7)
-----------------------------
1) feature_078 (continuous, range 30-90, mean 65; plausibly age in years).
   Univariate R^2 = 0.732, slope +0.174 months per unit. Strong, positive, monotone:
   mean PFS climbs from 0.09 mo (feature_078 in 30-40) to 7.20 mo (80-90). This single
   variable dominates the signal.

2) feature_057 (3-level ordinal, 0/1/2). Mean PFS 4.68 / 3.49 / 2.38 mo at levels 0/1/2.
   After adjustment, level 1 vs 0 = -1.18 mo, level 2 vs 0 = -2.29 mo. Acts like a
   stage- or performance-status-like ordinal predictor.

3) log(feature_013) (skewed continuous, 0.12-3622; plausibly a lab value such as PSA or
   LDH). Adjusted slope -0.349 mo per log-unit. Strong negative association.

4) feature_006 (continuous, 0-24.6, mean 3.8). Adjusted slope -0.077 mo per unit; small
   but extremely robust negative association.

5) feature_009 (continuous, 1.5-5.5, mean 3.8; plausibly serum albumin). Adjusted slope
   +0.479 mo per unit; positive association consistent with a "good prognosis" lab.

6) feature_018 (race) — REFUTED. Univariate mean PFS by race differs by <0.07 mo and
   none of the dummy coefficients are significant (p>0.14). After full adjustment
   essentially zero.

7) feature_045 (insurance) — REFUTED. All adjusted dummy coefficients have |est|<0.001
   and p>0.9. No PFS difference between medicaid, medicare, private, uninsured.

8) feature_067 (5-level ordinal taking 6/7/8/9/10; almost certainly Gleason score) — the
   expected strong negative MAIN effect is essentially absent. Univariate mean PFS is
   3.70-3.76 across all levels. After full adjustment, Gleason 8/9/10 vs 6 are only
   -0.025/-0.024/-0.020 mo (significant only because N=50k). Clinically, the gross main
   effect of Gleason is missing — and the analysis of iteration 11 explains why:
   Gleason's true role here is as a modifier of treatment response, not a baseline
   prognostic factor.

THE TREATMENT–BIOMARKER INTERACTION (iterations 8-13)
-----------------------------------------------------
A systematic screen of all 76 binary features for residual signal after the 5-predictor
adjustment surfaced two outliers, feature_109 and feature_039, each with a residual mean
difference of about +0.15 mo (p<1e-180) and each with prevalence ~10%. Their main effects
are not independent:

- When both features are entered jointly with their interaction, both main effects
  collapse to ~0 and the interaction term feature_109:feature_039 = +1.44 mo (p~0).
- Stratified analysis: among feature_109=1 patients (n=4,996) the effect of feature_039
  is +1.44 mo (p<1e-300); among feature_109=0 patients (n=45,004) the effect of
  feature_039 is essentially exactly zero (-0.0002, p=0.96).

This is the canonical pattern of a biomarker-defined responder population: only patients
who carry both feature_109=1 AND feature_039=1 (n=529, ~1% of cohort) derive PFS
benefit. We label this composite indicator "responder."

This benefit is not uniform. Two further variables modify it:

- Gleason-like feature_067. Within the responder subgroup, dummies for Gleason 8/9/10 vs
  6 are -2.90/-2.87/-2.84 mo. Formally the 3-way feature_109 × feature_039 × Gleason-high
  interaction = -2.80 mo (p~0). The +2.82-mo benefit observed in low-grade tumors
  (Gleason 6-7) is essentially eliminated in high-grade tumors (Gleason >=8).

- feature_043 (binary, prevalence ~20%). 3-way feature_109 × feature_039 × feature_043
  = -1.72 mo (p~0). In responders, feature_043=1 attenuates the benefit by about 1.7 mo
  (~60% of the Gleason-low benefit). The 2-way interactions of feature_043 with either
  feature_109 or feature_039 alone are near zero — feature_043's role is specifically to
  modulate the responder effect.

When both modifiers are entered together (iteration 13), the fully integrated model is:
   pfs_months ~ feature_078 + C(feature_057) + log(feature_013) + feature_006 +
                feature_009 + responder + responder:gleason_high + responder:feature_043
giving responder = +3.09 mo, responder:gleason_high = -2.76 mo, responder:feature_043 =
-1.62 mo. Net benefit is therefore concentrated in responders with Gleason 6-7 AND
feature_043 = 0 (~+3.1 mo); the other responder strata see negligible or even zero net
benefit. Adjusted R^2 = 0.980.

EQUITY ANALYSES (iterations 14-16, 22)
--------------------------------------
- Treatment receipt (feature_109=1) by race: 9.4-10.7% across asian/black/hispanic/
  other/white. No meaningful access disparity.
- Biomarker positivity (feature_039=1) by race: 8.6-10.5%, similarly equitable.
- Combined responder fraction by race: ~1.0% across all groups.
- Stratified responder benefit by race (iteration 15): asian +1.61, black +1.36,
  white +1.43, hispanic +1.39, other +1.94. Interaction terms vs asian reference are
  individually significant (p=0.003-0.027), but absolute differences (~0.2 mo on a
  ~1.5-mo benefit) are small and the small responder subgroup sizes (asian n=30,
  other n=8) make these estimates unstable. We do not interpret these as clinically
  meaningful disparities.
- Stratified responder benefit by insurance (iteration 16): medicaid +1.59, medicare
  +1.47, private +1.32, uninsured +1.56. Private-vs-medicaid difference is statistically
  significant (-0.26 mo, p=1e-9) but again small in magnitude.

OTHER NULL OR NEGLIGIBLE FINDINGS (iterations 17-21, 23-24)
-----------------------------------------------------------
- feature_051 (binary, prevalence ~55%): apparent univariate r=-0.13 with PFS is fully
  explained by other adjusted covariates; adjusted coefficient -0.0018 (p=0.64).
- feature_109 × feature_039 × feature_057 (stage): responder benefit is essentially
  identical at all 3 stages (1.39-1.52 mo); stage does not modify the responder effect
  meaningfully.
- 5-level ordinal features feature_016, feature_071, feature_096, feature_033,
  feature_064 contribute nothing after adjustment (all |est|<=0.013, mostly p>0.1).
- feature_026 (11-level integer 0-10) contributes nothing after adjustment.
- After accounting for feature_109 × feature_039 and its modifiers, no additional binary
  feature has a residual main effect larger than ~0.025 mo. The largest are feature_027
  (+0.024), feature_043 (-0.016 — already handled via 3-way interaction), feature_121
  (+0.015) — none clinically meaningful.
- No remaining continuous lab/vital-sign feature (feature_092, feature_014, feature_070,
  feature_044, feature_084, feature_028, feature_120, feature_054, feature_062,
  feature_031, feature_082) has |partial r|>0.05 after adjustment.
- feature_007 is constant (=0 for all patients) and carries no information.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. PFS in this cohort is overwhelmingly driven by an age-like variable (feature_078) and
   a 3-level stage/performance-like variable (feature_057). Together these two variables
   alone explain ~85% of variance.

2. Adding three biochemistry-like features — log(feature_013), feature_006 (small
   negative), and feature_009 (likely albumin, positive) — pushes adjusted R^2 to ~0.97.

3. The dataset contains a clear biomarker-targeted-treatment signal: feature_109 and
   feature_039 individually have no effect, but jointly produce a substantial PFS
   benefit. This is the prototypical pattern of a precision oncology pairing — a therapy
   indicator and an eligibility biomarker.

4. The responder benefit is highly heterogeneous: it is essentially confined to the
   ~3-month gain seen in Gleason 6-7, feature_043=0 patients. High-grade tumors
   (Gleason >=8) eliminate the benefit; feature_043=1 substantially attenuates it. Both
   sources of heterogeneity are statistically very strong (p~0).

5. There is no evidence of meaningful disparity by race (feature_018) or insurance
   (feature_045) in either pfs_months itself, in receipt of feature_109, in
   biomarker-positivity (feature_039), or in the magnitude of the responder benefit
   beyond very small differences that are detectable only because of N=50,000.

6. The clinically expected Gleason-score MAIN effect is absent in this cohort; Gleason
   instead operates as a modifier of treatment response. This is an unusual finding but
   internally consistent given the strong feature_109 × feature_039 × Gleason-high
   3-way interaction.

7. The bulk of the 122 anonymized features carry no meaningful prognostic information
   for pfs_months once the small set of dominant predictors above is accounted for.
