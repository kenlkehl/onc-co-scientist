Analysis Summary — ds001_prostate (n = 50,000)
=================================================

Outcome
-------
pfs_months is approximately right-skewed but reasonably symmetric in this cohort
(mean 3.74, SD 2.02, range 0–14.6). All analyses below treat it as a continuous
outcome via OLS regression, t-tests, and Spearman correlations.

Search strategy
---------------
The dataset contains 122 anonymized features: 1 constant column (feature_007),
2 string-categorical columns (feature_018 with 5 levels — race-like; feature_045
with 4 levels — insurance-like), 8 multi-valued ordinals, 77 binary indicators,
and 34 continuous features. I worked through five waves of analysis:
(1) univariate screening of every feature class against pfs_months;
(2) construction of a multivariable OLS backbone capturing the dominant signal;
(3) testing each apparent univariate signal for confounding once the backbone
was controlled;
(4) interaction and subgroup tests for race, insurance, age, stage, and PSA-like
features against the candidate "treatment" indicators feature_109, feature_039,
and feature_051;
(5) sweeps of the remaining lower-prevalence binary, ordinal, and lab-like
features for any independent signal.

What the data say
-----------------

A. The dominant predictors of pfs_months are five variables that, together,
explain 96.6% of the variance:

  - feature_078 (range 30–90, mean 65 — almost certainly age):
      Spearman ρ = +0.853 with pfs_months; OLS coefficient +0.174 months PFS
      per unit. This is the largest single signal in the dataset.
  - feature_057 (3 ordered levels 0/1/2 — almost certainly stage or risk
      group): mean PFS 4.68/3.49/2.38 months across levels; OLS coefficient
      −1.152 months per level (the largest stepwise decrement).
  - feature_013 (right-skewed 0.1–3622, mean 43 — almost certainly PSA):
      Spearman ρ = −0.223; on the log scale, OLS coefficient −0.382 per unit
      log feature_013.
  - feature_006 (right-skewed 0–25, mean 3.84): OLS coefficient −0.302 per
      unit log feature_006.
  - feature_009 (range 1.5–5.5, mean 3.8 — plausibly serum albumin):
      OLS coefficient +0.478 per unit, the largest positive continuous
      coefficient.

  All five remain massively significant in the joint model (every p < 10⁻¹⁵⁰).

B. Two binary features behave like beneficial treatments:

  - feature_109 (prevalence 10%): univariate diff +0.10 months (p = 2×10⁻³),
      adjusted coefficient +0.152 months (p ≈ 10⁻¹⁶⁹). Adjusted effect is
      LARGER than the univariate, indicating it was negatively confounded
      by the core covariates.
  - feature_039 (prevalence 10%): univariate diff +0.09 months (p = 3×10⁻³),
      adjusted coefficient +0.147 months (p ≈ 10⁻¹⁶⁰). Same pattern.

  The two act ADDITIVELY: their interaction term is near zero, and including
  both at full magnitude does not require a synergy term to fit the data.

C. The most striking confounding finding involves feature_051:

  feature_051 (prevalence 55%) shows a huge univariate negative effect
  (−0.521 months PFS, p = 1.6×10⁻¹⁸¹) and that effect persists across
  every stratum of feature_057 stage (−0.56 / −0.51 / −0.47 months in
  stages 0/1/2). It is NOT correlated with feature_078 (mean age 65.0 in
  both groups), feature_057, or feature_009. It IS strongly correlated
  with feature_013: mean log(feature_013) is 2.18 when feature_051=0 vs
  3.55 when feature_051=1 (p ≈ 0). After adjusting for log(feature_013),
  the feature_051 coefficient collapses to +0.011 (p = 0.30), and to
  −0.0005 (p = 0.90) once all five core covariates are included. So
  feature_051 carries no independent information about pfs_months — it
  is a near-perfect proxy for high feature_013, and the apparent harm
  attributed to it in the marginal analysis is entirely confounded.

D. A handful of small additional independent signals survive adjustment:

  - feature_043 (prevalence 20%): adjusted coefficient −0.017 (p = 6×10⁻⁵).
      Real but tiny — about 1/70 the magnitude of a feature_057 step.
  - feature_067 (5-level ordinal, range 6–10): adjusted slope −0.010 per
      step (p = 2×10⁻¹⁰). Also tiny.
  - feature_031 (continuous): marginal adjusted slope −3×10⁻⁴ (p = 0.018);
      not robust to multiple-comparison considerations across 34 lab
      sweeps and not retained.

E. Several univariate signals fail to replicate after adjustment:

  - feature_105, feature_115, feature_104, feature_032, feature_023 all
    showed marginal univariate associations and all become non-significant
    in adjusted models (every p > 0.10).

F. Demographic / payer features show NO main or moderating effect:

  - feature_018 (race-like, 5 levels): univariate ANOVA p = 0.689; mean PFS
    differs by less than 0.06 months across the five categories. After
    adjustment for the core covariates, every level coefficient is < 0.006
    months with p > 0.69.
  - feature_045 (insurance-like, 4 levels): univariate ANOVA p = 0.794;
    adjusted level coefficients all < 0.001 months.
  - feature_109 × race interaction p > 0.5 across all comparisons.
  - feature_039 × race interaction p > 0.24 across all comparisons.
  - feature_051 × race interaction p > 0.17 across all comparisons.
    No evidence whatsoever that the apparent benefit of feature_109 or
    feature_039, or the apparent (confounded) harm of feature_051, varies
    by race or insurance category.

G. Other interaction and subgroup tests:

  - feature_109 × feature_057 (stage): interaction +0.007, p = 0.36 — null.
  - feature_109 × feature_078 (age):    interaction +0.0004, p = 0.50 — null.
  - feature_109 × log(feature_013):     interaction −0.009, p = 0.043 —
      marginal: slight attenuation of feature_109 benefit at higher
      feature_013, but the effect is small.
  - feature_051 × feature_057:          interaction +0.027, p = 4×10⁻⁸ —
      statistically significant only because of the huge sample size; the
      magnitude (~0.03 months PFS per stage step) is clinically negligible.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
SUPPORTED:
  - Older patients (feature_078) have substantially longer PFS.
  - Higher feature_057 (stage-like) levels have markedly shorter PFS.
  - Higher feature_013 (PSA-like) is associated with shorter PFS.
  - feature_009 (albumin-like) is positively associated with PFS.
  - feature_006 is negatively associated with PFS.
  - feature_109 and feature_039 each independently extend PFS by ~0.15 months.
  - feature_109 and feature_039 act additively, not synergistically.
  - feature_043 has a small (−0.017 month) but robust negative effect.
  - feature_067 has a small (−0.010 month per step) negative effect.

REFUTED:
  - feature_051 is independently harmful (entirely confounded by feature_013).
  - Race-like feature_018 affects PFS, alone or as a moderator of any
    treatment-like feature.
  - Insurance-like feature_045 affects PFS, alone or as a moderator.
  - Lab-like continuous features beyond the five-variable backbone provide
    meaningful incremental information.
  - The marginal univariate signals from feature_105, feature_115, feature_104,
    feature_032, and feature_023 represent independent biology.

Overall conclusion
------------------
This cohort is overwhelmingly driven by five biological/disease covariates —
age, stage, PSA, feature_006, and albumin — which together explain 97% of PFS
variance. Two binary features (feature_109 and feature_039) behave as modest,
robustly beneficial interventions, conferring an additional ~0.15 months PFS
each, additively. The variable feature_051, which dominates univariate analysis
with a half-month deficit, is a confounded proxy for high feature_013 (PSA) and
contributes no independent information. There is no evidence in this dataset
of differential treatment effect, or any direct outcome effect, by race-like or
insurance-like demographics. A handful of further small signals (feature_043,
feature_067) survive adjustment but at clinically trivial magnitude.
