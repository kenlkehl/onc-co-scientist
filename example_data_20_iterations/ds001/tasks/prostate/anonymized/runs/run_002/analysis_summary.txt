ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_prostate (50,000 patients, anonymized features)
=========================================================================

OUTCOME
-------
The single outcome is pfs_months (progression-free survival in months),
treated as a continuous variable (no censoring indicator was provided).
Empirical distribution: mean 3.74, median 3.72, sd 2.02, range 0-14.6.

OVERVIEW OF THE EXPLORATORY PROTOCOL
------------------------------------
Across 16 propose-test-refine iterations I worked from broad univariate
screens of all 121 features, through a strong main-effects multivariable
model, into adjusted residual screens, and finally into interaction and
disparity analyses. The cohort turned out to be remarkably structured: a
small set of clinical-feeling covariates explains ~95% of the variance in
pfs_months, and the residual signal lives mostly in a few well-defined
biomarker interactions.

DOMINANT MAIN EFFECTS (iterations 1-4)
--------------------------------------
1. feature_078 (continuous, range 30-90, mean 65 — almost certainly an
   age-like variable). Spearman rho = +0.85 with pfs_months, by far the
   strongest single predictor. Mean pfs rises monotonically across age
   bins from 0.09 months (age 30-40) to 7.20 months (age 80-90). After
   full multivariable adjustment, +0.174 months of pfs per unit
   (t=822). This positive sign is unusual for prostate-cancer
   intuition, but the signal is unambiguous.

2. feature_057 (3-level categorical: 0/1/2, plausibly stage or risk
   group). Mean pfs by level: 4.68 / 3.49 / 2.38. Adjusted coefficients
   for levels 1 and 2 (vs level 0): -1.18 and -2.29 months, both p<<1e-300.

3. feature_051 (binary, prevalence ~55%). Adjusted effect = -0.38
   months, p<<1e-300. Univariate effect -0.52. This behaves like a
   line-of-therapy or treatment indicator: present in roughly half of
   patients and uniformly associated with worse pfs.

4. feature_013 (continuous, range 0.12-3622, very heavy right tail —
   PSA-like). Adjusted coef = -0.0027 per unit, p<<1e-100. Higher values
   shorten pfs.

5. feature_006 (range 0-24.6). Adjusted coef = -0.076 per unit,
   p<<1e-100.

6. feature_009 (range 1.5-5.5, 39 unique values — perhaps albumin or
   another small-range lab). Adjusted coef = +0.479 per unit, p<<1e-100.

A 7-predictor OLS using just feature_078, feature_051, feature_057
(2 dummies), feature_013, feature_006, and feature_009 already explains
R^2 = 0.946 of the variance in pfs_months.

ADDITIONAL ADJUSTED SIGNALS (iterations 5-7)
--------------------------------------------
After the seven main effects, residual screening surfaced:
- feature_109 (binary, prevalence 10%): +0.156 months adjusted
  (p=7e-110). Univariately near-null.
- feature_039 (binary, prevalence 10%): +0.147 months adjusted
  (p=2e-99). Mirror image of feature_109.
- feature_027 (binary, prevalence 60%): small +0.022 months (p=4e-7).
- feature_043 (binary): small -0.022 months (p=4e-5).
- feature_092 (continuous, range 48-830): -0.0005 per unit (p=3e-81).
- feature_067 (5-level categorical, values 6-10 — Gleason-like): joint
  F p=3.5e-9, with grade 8/9/10 carrying small (~ -0.02 month) decrements
  vs grade 6.
- Race (feature_018) and insurance (feature_045) main effects were null
  both univariately (ANOVA p=0.69 and p=0.79) and after full adjustment
  (all coefficients within +/- 0.01 months, all p > 0.2).

THE KEY INTERACTION: feature_109 x feature_039 SYNERGY (iter 8-9)
------------------------------------------------------------------
The most clinically interesting structure in this dataset is a paired
predictive-biomarker pattern. Raw cell means of pfs_months by
(feature_109, feature_039) are:

    109=0, 039=0   3.742   (n=40,435)
    109=0, 039=1   3.668   (n= 4,569)
    109=1, 039=0   3.671   (n= 4,467)
    109=1, 039=1   5.216   (n=   529)

Single-positive patients are not better than double-negatives. The
benefit appears only in the ~1% of patients with both biomarkers
positive. In the multivariable model, the main effects of feature_109
and feature_039 collapse to zero once the multiplicative term is
included; the interaction coefficient is +1.44 months (t=66.6,
p<<1e-300).

This synergy is itself amplified by feature_027 (iteration 9):
- (109=1, 039=1, 027=0): pfs = 3.83 (n=199)
- (109=1, 039=1, 027=1): pfs = 6.05 (n=330)

In a model with full 2-way and 3-way interactions, the triple
interaction term carries +2.26 months (p<<1e-300) while the 2-way
interactions become non-significant — i.e., the entire synergy lives in
the (very small) triple-positive subgroup. Model R^2 rises to 0.955.

TREATMENT EFFECT MODIFICATION (iter 10-11, 15)
----------------------------------------------
- feature_051 x feature_057 (treatment x stage): the negative feature_051
  effect attenuates from -0.40 months at level 0 to -0.34 months at
  level 2. Significant interactions (p=0.006 and p=2e-5) but small
  relative to the -0.4 main effect.
- feature_051 x feature_013: interaction coef +0.0153 per unit
  feature_013 (p<<1e-300). High feature_013 attenuates the deleterious
  effect of feature_051. This is one of the strongest interaction
  signals in the dataset and is a natural candidate for a predictive
  biomarker hypothesis (treatment more "necessary" in high-burden
  patients).
- feature_051 x feature_078: interaction coef -0.0043 per year (p=3e-24).
  The feature_051 decrement grows by ~0.04 months per decade — small but
  highly significant.
- feature_051 x feature_009: -0.017 per unit (p=0.04). Marginal.
- feature_051 x (feature_109*feature_039) synergy: triple interaction
  coef -0.11 months (p=0.004). The predictive synergy is modestly
  blunted in feature_051=1 patients (5.0 vs 5.6 months gain), but is
  still strong in both strata.
- No statistically credible feature_051 interactions with race or
  insurance.

DISPARITIES: TREATMENT ALLOCATION AND OUTCOMES (iter 12-14)
-----------------------------------------------------------
- Treatment allocation is balanced. feature_051 prevalence is 0.544-0.554
  across racial groups and 0.546-0.562 across insurance categories.
  Chi-square p ~ 0.5-0.9. No allocation disparity detectable.
- Adjusted outcome: race coefficients (vs white reference) are all within
  +/- 0.005 months with p > 0.5. Insurance coefficients are within +/-
  0.008 months with p > 0.2. Both null in the full cohort and within
  feature_051=0 and feature_051=1 strata separately.
- Race x feature_051 interaction: stratified per-race feature_051 coefs
  are -0.36 (asian, black, white, other) and -0.43 (hispanic). Formal
  interaction tests give p=0.08 (black) and p=0.10 (hispanic); neither
  reaches conventional significance. There is at most a hint that
  hispanic patients see a slightly larger feature_051 decrement.

WHAT WAS REFUTED
----------------
- Race main effects on pfs_months (refuted; all coefficients tiny and
  non-significant, both univariately and adjusted, both within and
  across feature_051 strata).
- Insurance main effects on pfs_months (refuted, same pattern).
- Differential treatment allocation by race or insurance (refuted).
- A simple additive interpretation of feature_109 and feature_039
  (refuted — the effect is purely synergistic).
- A simple feature_109 x feature_039 2-way interaction as the full story
  (refined — the benefit concentrates in the triple-positive
  feature_109/039/027 subgroup).
- A meaningful race x feature_051 interaction (refuted at p < 0.05).

WHAT WAS SUPPORTED
------------------
- The feature_078 / feature_057 / feature_051 / feature_013 / feature_006
  / feature_009 main-effects backbone.
- Independent positive adjusted effects of feature_109 and feature_039.
- The feature_109 x feature_039 synergistic interaction.
- The feature_109 x feature_039 x feature_027 triple-positive predictive
  pattern.
- Small but reliable adjusted effects of feature_092, feature_043, and
  feature_067 high grades.
- Several modest treatment-effect-modification signals: feature_051
  decrement is smaller at higher feature_057 stage, smaller at high
  feature_013, and slightly larger in older patients.

OVERALL CONCLUSION
------------------
The pfs_months outcome in this cohort is highly predictable from a
small set of clinical-style covariates (R^2 ~ 0.95 with 7-10
predictors). The clearest treatment-biomarker structure is a paired
biomarker pattern: feature_109 and feature_039 each appear inert alone
but together produce ~1.5 months of additional pfs, a benefit further
amplified to ~2.3 months in patients also positive for feature_027.
feature_051 behaves like a treatment indicator that on average shortens
pfs but whose effect is modulated mostly by clinical severity (stage
feature_057, marker feature_013, age feature_078) rather than by race or
insurance. Across multiple framings I could not detect any race or
insurance disparity, in either treatment allocation or pfs_months
outcomes, with a sample of 50,000 patients — making this dataset's
demographic effects strikingly null.
