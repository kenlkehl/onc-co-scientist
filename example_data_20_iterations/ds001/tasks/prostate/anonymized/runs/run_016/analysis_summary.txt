Analysis summary - dataset ds001_prostate (50,000 patients, 122 features, outcome pfs_months)
===============================================================================================

Approach
--------
Across 15 iterations I moved from broad univariate scans to a parsimonious multivariable
model, then targeted interaction tests against the dominant predictor and the protected-
attribute features (race-like feature_018; insurance-like feature_045). Methods: Spearman
correlations, Welch t-tests, one-way ANOVA, chi-square tests, OLS regression with main,
quadratic and interaction terms, joint F-tests, and stratified subgroup slopes. With
n=50,000 the analyses were extremely well-powered, so where I report null results I do so
with confidence that any clinically meaningful effect would have been detected.

Outcome
-------
pfs_months: mean 3.74 (SD 2.02), range 0-14.6, roughly bell-shaped with a slight right tail.
No missing data anywhere.

1. Univariate continuous-feature scan (iteration 1-2)
-----------------------------------------------------
Of 35 continuous features, four had |Spearman rho| > 0.10 with pfs_months:
  - feature_078 (range 30-90, median 65): rho = +0.853, p ~ 0; linear slope +0.174
    months PFS per unit. By far the dominant signal in the cohort.
  - feature_013 (range 0.1-3622, highly right-skewed): rho = -0.223, p ~ 0; the log
    transform performs much better in linear models. Direction: HIGHER feature_013
    -> SHORTER PFS.
  - feature_006 (range 0-25): rho = -0.134, slope -0.075/unit. Higher -> shorter PFS.
  - feature_009 (range 1.5-5.5): rho = +0.118, slope +0.495/unit. Higher -> longer PFS.
The remaining 31 continuous features had |rho| < 0.02 with pfs_months.

2. Univariate binary-feature scan (iteration 3)
-----------------------------------------------
Of 77 binary features tested, only feature_051 was strongly associated with pfs_months
on its own: mean PFS 3.51 with feature_051=1 vs 4.03 with feature_051=0 (Welch t=-28.9,
p=2e-181, difference -0.52 months). Four others reached nominal p<0.05 (feature_109,
feature_039, feature_105, feature_043) but none came near Bonferroni significance
(threshold p<6.5e-4).

3. Race and insurance - marginal effects (iteration 4)
------------------------------------------------------
ANOVA of pfs_months across the five race-like categories of feature_018 yielded F=0.564,
p=0.689; group means ranged from 3.737 (hispanic) to 3.797 (asian). Pairwise Welch
t-tests vs white reference were all non-significant (smallest p=0.14 for asian).

ANOVA across the four insurance-like categories of feature_045 yielded F=0.344, p=0.794.
Means ranged from 3.726 (uninsured) to 3.754 (medicare). Pairwise comparisons vs private
reference: medicare diff +0.018 (p=0.36), medicaid diff +0.005 (p=0.87), uninsured diff
-0.010 (p=0.83).

The two specific equity hypotheses - that black patients have shorter PFS than white,
and that medicaid/uninsured patients have shorter PFS than privately insured patients -
were both refuted (point estimates within rounding of zero).

4. Multivariable adjustment and confounding (iteration 5)
---------------------------------------------------------
A 9-covariate OLS model (top continuous + top binary + race + insurance dummies) achieved
R^2 = 0.822. Key findings:
  - feature_078 dominates (t=453, beta=+0.174 per unit, p~0).
  - log feature_013 (t=-101, -0.382 per log-unit), feature_006 (t=-78), feature_009
    (t=+62) all remain highly significant.
  - The strong univariate feature_051 effect (-0.52 months) collapses to +0.001
    (p=0.92): feature_051 is fully confounded by the four core continuous predictors.
  - All four race coefficients have p >= 0.37 and |coef| <= 0.018; all three insurance
    coefficients have p >= 0.29 and |coef| <= 0.009.

5. Full multivariable model with all 122 features (iteration 6)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Including every feature simultaneously raised R^2 only marginally to 0.8226 (vs 0.822
for the 9-covariate model). Nine features pass Bonferroni:
  feature_078 (+0.174, p~0), log feature_013 (-0.191, p~0), feature_006 (-0.077, p~0),
  feature_009 (+0.477, p~0), feature_092 (-5.5e-4, p=7e-31), feature_109 (+0.137,
  p=7e-27), feature_039 (+0.130, p=6e-25), feature_027 (+0.036, p=5e-6), feature_043
  (-0.042, p=1e-5).
Remaining 113 features collectively contribute < 0.4 percentage points of R^2.

6. Demographic balance and effect modification (iterations 7-9)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Predictor balance: all eight key predictors (feature_078, log feature_013, feature_006,
feature_009, feature_092, feature_109, feature_039, feature_051) are balanced across
both feature_018 and feature_045 (every ANOVA / chi-square p >= 0.18).

Effect modification by race / insurance:
  - feature_078 x race: joint F=0.39, p=0.82. Race-stratified Spearman rho with PFS
    is essentially identical (0.847-0.858) and slopes are 0.171-0.175 per unit.
  - feature_078 x insurance: joint F=0.30, p=0.82.
  - For each of log feature_013, feature_006, feature_009, feature_109, feature_039:
    the joint F-test of interactions with race fails to reject (smallest p=0.23) and
    with insurance fails to reject (smallest p=0.34).
  - Residual analysis: fitting the multivariable model WITHOUT race/insurance and then
    asking whether residuals differ by demographic group, ANOVA across race p=0.853 and
    across insurance p=0.730. Demographic groups are not systematically over- or
    under-predicted.

7. Predictor-by-predictor interactions and non-linearities (iterations 10-11)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Three feature_078 interactions are strongly significant in the multivariable context:
  - feature_078 x log feature_013: coef -0.00354 per unit (p=9e-30). The negative
    effect of higher feature_013 is amplified at higher feature_078: log_feature_013
    slope goes from approximately -0.30 at feature_078=30 to approximately -0.51 at
    feature_078=90.
  - feature_078 x feature_006: coef -0.000651 per unit (p=6e-11). Negative feature_006
    effect amplifies at higher feature_078.
  - feature_078 x feature_009: coef +0.00403 per unit (p=1e-7). Positive feature_009
    effect amplifies at higher feature_078.

Non-linear shapes:
  - feature_078^2 coef +7.8e-4 (p=4e-172): concave-up; per-unit slope rises from
    ~0.12 at feature_078=30 to ~0.21 at feature_078=90.
  - feature_092^2 coef -2.4e-6 (p=1e-11): concave-down (inverse-U); approximate vertex
    near feature_092 ~ 168.

8. Stratified mean PFS by feature_078 quintile (iteration 12)
-------------------------------------------------------------
  Q1 [30.0-56.5]:  mean PFS 1.35 (n=10,006)
  Q2 [56.5-62.4]:  mean PFS 2.75 (n=10,029)
  Q3 [62.4-67.6]:  mean PFS 3.72 (n=10,101)
  Q4 [67.6-73.5]:  mean PFS 4.70 (n=10,009)
  Q5 [73.5-90.0]:  mean PFS 6.24 (n=9,855)
A 4.9-month spread across quintiles, almost monotone.

9. Binary-by-feature_078 interaction scan (iteration 13)
--------------------------------------------------------
Of 77 binary features tested for interaction with feature_078 in pairwise models, only
feature_051 survives Bonferroni (interaction coef -0.00469, p=3e-7) - a small relative
effect (~3% of the main feature_078 slope) but statistically robust. No other binary
feature modifies feature_078's effect at Bonferroni-corrected levels.

10. Final parsimonious model (iteration 14)
-------------------------------------------
A 14-term model (feature_078, feature_078^2, log feature_013, feature_006, feature_009,
feature_092, feature_092^2, feature_109, feature_039, feature_027, feature_043,
feature_078:log_feature_013, feature_078:feature_006, feature_078:feature_009) achieves
R^2 = 0.8258 / Adj R^2 = 0.8258, slightly outperforming the all-features OLS (0.8226).
All 14 terms have p < 1e-4. This is essentially the best-explainable signal in the cohort.

Hypotheses supported, refuted, and refined
------------------------------------------
SUPPORTED:
  - feature_078 is the dominant predictor of pfs_months (large positive linear effect
    with mild concave-up curvature).
  - log feature_013 negatively predicts pfs_months.
  - feature_006 negatively predicts pfs_months; feature_009 positively predicts it.
  - feature_092 has an inverse-U relationship with pfs_months.
  - feature_109 (+0.14 months), feature_039 (+0.13 months), and feature_027 (+0.04
    months) are independently protective; feature_043 (-0.04 months) is independently
    deleterious.
  - feature_078 interacts with log feature_013, feature_006, and feature_009 (all in
    the direction of amplifying their effects at higher feature_078).
  - feature_051 has a univariate effect that is fully confounded by the four core
    continuous predictors.

REFUTED:
  - The hypothesis that race (feature_018) is associated with pfs_months at any level -
    marginal main effect, adjusted main effect, residual mean, or interaction with any
    other predictor.
  - The hypothesis that insurance (feature_045) is associated with pfs_months at any
    level - marginal, adjusted, residual, or interaction.
  - The specific health-disparities priors that black-vs-white patients or
    medicaid/uninsured-vs-private patients would have shorter PFS.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
This cohort behaves like an essentially demographics-neutral cohort: the strongest
predictor (feature_078) explains by itself ~73% of the variance in pfs_months and a
small set of nine additional features captures the remainder. Race and insurance are
not associated with pfs_months marginally, are not associated after adjustment, do not
modify the effect of any clinical predictor, and the model's residual error is balanced
across both. The dataset shows neither confounding by demographics (the eight key
predictors are balanced across race and insurance) nor effect modification by them.

The clinically interpretable signal: pfs_months scales strongly and approximately
linearly (with mild concave-up curvature) with feature_078; is shortened by higher
feature_013 (after log transform), higher feature_006, and higher or lower feature_092
(inverse-U around ~168); and is lengthened by higher feature_009 and the protective
binary markers feature_109, feature_039, and feature_027. The interactions imply that
the harmful effects of feature_013 and feature_006 are larger in patients with higher
feature_078, while the benefit from feature_009 is also larger there - i.e., higher-
feature_078 patients are more responsive (positively or negatively) to other clinical
inputs.
