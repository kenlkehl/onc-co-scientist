Analysis Summary — ds001_prostate (50,000 patients, 122 candidate features, outcome = pfs_months)
================================================================================================

OVERALL STRUCTURE
-----------------
The cohort comprises 50,000 patient records with one continuous outcome (pfs_months,
range 0.0 - 14.6, mean 3.74). Features are de-identified to 121 numeric labels plus
two categorical labels (feature_018 = self-reported race with 5 levels;
feature_045 = insurance type with 4 levels). Across 15 iterations of hypothesis
generation and statistical testing, I screened 122 candidate features individually
against the outcome, then progressively narrowed to a small set of dominant
predictors and tested treatment-biomarker, treatment-stage, and treatment-demographic
interactions.

DOMINANT PROGNOSTIC STRUCTURE
-----------------------------
A six-variable OLS model already explains 94.6% of variance in pfs_months
(R² = 0.9461). The full 120+ feature model adds only 0.002 in R², so the
prognostic signal is concentrated in a handful of features:

  - feature_078 (continuous, range 30-90, plausibly an age-like variable):
    Univariate Pearson r = +0.856 (p ≈ 0). Adjusted coefficient = +0.174 mo per unit.
    A quadratic term is highly significant (p ≈ 0), revealing an accelerating
    positive relationship: mean pfs_months rises monotonically from 0.85 in the
    lowest age decile to 6.86 in the highest. The strength of this association
    is unusual for an age variable; it may reflect time-on-study or a
    construction artifact of this synthetic-feeling dataset, but I have only the
    feature_078 column and no metadata to be sure.

  - feature_057 (3-level ordinal, values 0/1/2; plausibly a stage or risk strata):
    Strong monotone negative relationship — mean pfs_months 4.68 → 3.49 → 2.38
    moving from level 0 → 1 → 2 (Spearman ρ = -0.386, p ≈ 0). Adjusted coefficients
    are -1.18 mo (level 1) and -2.29 mo (level 2) relative to level 0.

  - feature_013 (continuous, 0.12-3622, right-skewed; plausibly PSA):
    Negative association as expected (r = -0.153 univariate, adjusted coefficient
    -0.0027 mo/unit, p ≈ 0). The width of the per-patient range produces large
    cumulative effects at the high end.

  - feature_009 (continuous, 1.5-5.5, 0.1 increments): Positive association
    (r = +0.123 univariate; adjusted coef +0.479 mo per unit, p ≈ 0). Could be
    albumin or another lab biomarker.

  - feature_006 (continuous, 0-24.6): Negative association (adjusted coef -0.077,
    p ≈ 0).

  - feature_051 (binary, prevalence 55%): Behaves like the dataset's principal
    treatment marker. Patients with feature_051 = 1 have mean pfs_months 3.51 vs
    4.03 in feature_051 = 0 — a -0.52 mo unadjusted difference (p = 1.6e-181) and
    -0.38 mo adjusted (p ≈ 0).

  - feature_109 and feature_039 (binary, ~10% prevalence each): Each
    independently associated with longer pfs_months by ~+0.15 mo
    (p < 1e-100 each).

  - feature_092 (continuous, 48-830): Tiny per-unit effect (-0.000528 mo/unit) but
    statistically robust (p = 4.3e-82) given large sd.

Most of the remaining ~110 features (including the five 0–4-coded ordinal features
feature_016/071/096/033/064, the 11-level feature_026, the Gleason-like
feature_067, and most of the 77 binary features) showed essentially no
prognostic signal in adjusted analysis (68 of 155 model parameters had p > 0.5).
feature_007 is a constant column (all zeros) and provides no information.

TREATMENT-BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS
--------------------------------
1. feature_051 × feature_013 (treatment × PSA-like): The detrimental association of
   feature_051 with pfs_months is concentrated in patients with high feature_013.
   Stratified by feature_013 quartile, the adjusted feature_051 coefficient is
   -0.04 mo in Q1–Q3 (mean f013 = 3.6, 10.5, 26.1) but -0.149 mo in Q4 (mean
   f013 = 130.5) — a roughly 3-fold larger detriment in patients with the highest
   feature_013 values. Formal interaction term feature_013:feature_051 = +0.0153
   (p ≈ 0), reflecting that each unit increase of feature_013 partially offsets
   the per-unit hazard once feature_051 = 1.

2. feature_051 × feature_057 (treatment × stage): Negative association is
   consistent across all three feature_057 strata (Δ ≈ -0.55, -0.51, -0.47 mo
   respectively in unadjusted strata). Formal interaction terms are small
   (+0.026 and +0.056) but statistically significant — modest attenuation of the
   feature_051 detriment at higher stage levels.

3. feature_051 × feature_078 (treatment × age-like): The detrimental association
   becomes more negative as feature_078 increases (interaction coef = -0.0043,
   p ≈ 0). Adding a quadratic age × treatment term shows the relationship is
   slightly U-shaped.

4. No other clinically meaningful interactions: feature_051 × feature_109,
   feature_051 × feature_039, feature_051 × race, and feature_051 × insurance
   were all statistically null.

DEMOGRAPHIC DISPARITIES
-----------------------
This was a deliberate focus given the ample sample size. The findings were
consistently null:

  - Outcome by race: ANOVA across asian/black/hispanic/white/other gives mean
    pfs_months 3.797, 3.741, 3.737, 3.749, 3.741 — span of 0.06 mo, all
    pairwise ns. After adjustment for the dominant prognostic features, all four
    race coefficients remain ns (p ≥ 0.54).

  - Outcome by insurance: medicare 3.754, private 3.736, medicaid 3.741,
    uninsured 3.726 — span 0.03 mo, all ns adjusted (p > 0.16).

  - Treatment receipt by race: feature_051 prevalence ranges 0.544-0.554 across
    races; chi-square p = 0.82.

  - Treatment receipt by insurance: prevalence 0.546-0.562; chi-square p = 0.37.

  - Treatment receipt is, however, predicted by feature_013 (PSA-like) — log-odds
    coefficient +0.061 (p ≈ 0) — confirming feature_051 receipt tracks disease
    severity (not demographics).

  - Biomarkers by race: feature_013, feature_009, feature_006, feature_092,
    feature_038, feature_054, feature_062, feature_120, feature_118, feature_056
    all have ANOVA p > 0.3 across racial groups.

  - feature_057 distribution by race: chi-square p = 0.84; identical
    proportions across races.

  - Race × stage interaction on outcome: all 8 interaction coefficients ns.

  - Treatment × race, treatment × insurance interactions on outcome: all ns.

The total absence of demographic gradients across 50,000 patients is striking —
in real-world prostate cancer cohorts, racial differences in PSA, stage at
presentation, treatment receipt, and outcomes are typically detectable at this
sample size. The cohort appears to be balanced or constructed in a way that
removes such disparities.

REFUTED HYPOTHESES
------------------
- Hypothesis that feature_067 (likely Gleason 6-10) is monotonically associated
  with pfs_months: REFUTED. Mean pfs_months by Gleason is essentially flat
  (3.71-3.76 across 6-10), and adjusted effects are tiny (< 0.03 mo).

- Hypothesis that the five 0–4 ordinal features are each prognostic: REFUTED.
  None shows a meaningful monotonic gradient.

- Hypothesis that race or insurance type causes disparities in outcome,
  treatment receipt, biomarkers, or stage distribution: REFUTED for every test
  conducted (~15 separate analyses).

SUPPORTED HYPOTHESES
--------------------
- feature_078, feature_057, feature_013, feature_006, feature_009, feature_051,
  feature_109, feature_039, feature_092 are independent predictors of pfs_months
  in the directions described above.

- feature_051 × feature_013 interaction (treatment effect modified by PSA-like
  biomarker): SUPPORTED.

- feature_051 × feature_078 interaction: SUPPORTED.

- Quadratic age effect: SUPPORTED.

- Demographic balance / absence of disparities: SUPPORTED for every axis tested.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. PFS in this cohort is overwhelmingly determined by a single feature_078
   (age-like, r = 0.86) plus a 3-level stage-like variable (feature_057) and a
   PSA-like biomarker (feature_013). One binary feature (feature_051) behaves as
   a treatment marker with a small but consistent detrimental association.
2. Treatment-biomarker heterogeneity is concentrated in feature_051 ×
   feature_013: feature_051 is associated with substantially worse pfs_months in
   patients with high feature_013 — a clinically suggestive pattern that, in a
   real dataset, would prompt investigation of treatment selection or
   confounding by indication.
3. No racial or insurance disparities are detectable on any axis (outcome,
   treatment receipt, biomarkers, stage). This contrasts with real-world
   prostate cancer cohorts and likely reflects the constructed nature of this
   anonymized dataset.
4. Of 122 candidate features, only ~9 carry meaningful prognostic information
   for pfs_months; the remainder are statistically null even with N = 50,000
   power. feature_007 is a constant column.
