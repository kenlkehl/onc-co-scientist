Analysis summary — ds001_prostate (50,000 patients, 122 features, outcome pfs_months)
=====================================================================================

Approach
--------
Twenty-five propose-test-refine iterations. Started with broad univariate screens of
binary and continuous features, then built a multivariable OLS, then probed
interactions (treatment x clinical, treatment x sociodemographic), then targeted
follow-ups based on emerging signals (combination treatment, race/insurance
disparity check).

Variable interpretation (inferred from distributions)
-----------------------------------------------------
Identifiers: patient_id. Outcome: pfs_months (continuous, mean 3.74, range 0-14.6).
Inferred clinical anchors:
- feature_078: continuous 30-90, mean 65 — plausibly age in years.
- feature_057: ordered 0/1/2, mean 0.80 — plausibly ECOG performance status.
- feature_067: integer 6-10, mean 7.6 — plausibly Gleason score.
- feature_013: heavy right-tailed positive number (max 3622, mean 42) — plausibly PSA.
- feature_009: 1.5-5.5, mean 3.8 — plausibly hemoglobin.
- feature_018: categorical (white/black/hispanic/asian/other) — race/ethnicity.
- feature_045: categorical (medicare/private/medicaid/uninsured) — insurance.
- feature_051: 55%-prevalence binary that strongly reduces PFS in adjusted models —
  plausibly an aggressive systemic therapy or a marker of metastatic disease.
- feature_109 (10% prev) and feature_039 (10% prev): two binaries that individually
  add little to PFS but jointly produce a large positive synergistic boost.
None of these labels are confirmed; they are working hypotheses for the
clinical narrative below.

Top-line findings
-----------------
1. A small handful of features explain almost all variance in pfs_months. A 9-term
   OLS (feature_078, feature_057, feature_013, feature_009, feature_006, feature_051,
   feature_109, feature_039, feature_067) achieves R^2 = 0.9471. Adding the rest of
   the binary, ordinal, race and insurance variables provides only marginal gains.

2. Six independent main effects, all very high significance (p << Bonferroni alpha):
     feature_078 (age):   +0.174 mo per year (older = LONGER PFS)
     feature_057 (PS):    -1.151 mo per level (higher PS = shorter PFS)
     feature_009:         +0.478 mo per unit  (higher = longer PFS)
     feature_051:         -0.376 mo (presence = shorter PFS)
     feature_006:         -0.076 mo per unit (higher = shorter PFS)
     feature_013 (PSA?):  -0.0027 mo per unit (higher = shorter PFS)
     feature_067:         -0.011 mo per unit (higher = shorter PFS, modest)
   The age direction is unusual for a prostate-cancer cohort but is so strong and
   monotonic that it appears to be a genuine feature of the data (consistent across
   PS strata, race strata, insurance strata, and treatment strata).

3. A large feature_109 x feature_039 synergy. Neither monotherapy increases PFS
   meaningfully (main effects -0.003 and +0.003 in the interaction model, both
   non-significant), but presence of BOTH adds +1.44 mo to pfs_months
   (interaction t=66, p effectively 0). Cell means (unadjusted): neither 3.74,
   feature_039-only 3.67, feature_109-only 3.67, both 5.22. This is the single
   largest treatment-like effect in the dataset.

4. Treatment-effect heterogeneity for feature_051:
   - feature_051 x feature_057 (ECOG): small positive interaction (PS=2 has effect
     -0.34 vs PS=0 effect -0.40) — feature_051 is slightly less harmful at higher PS.
     Statistically significant (p=1.7e-5 for PS=2 vs 0) but practically small.
   - feature_051 x feature_078 (age): -0.0043 per year (p=1e-23). PFS reduction from
     feature_051 grows by ~0.04 mo per decade. Modest in absolute terms.
   - feature_051 x feature_013: large adjusted positive interaction (+0.0153, p~0),
     but unadjusted in-quartile comparisons run the OPPOSITE direction (Q4 has the
     LARGEST unadjusted PFS deficit on feature_051), indicating heavy confounding
     by indication. Interpretation requires caution.
   - feature_051 x feature_018 (race): joint F=1.38, p=0.24. No race heterogeneity.
   - feature_051 x feature_045 (insurance): joint F=0.11, p=0.95. No insurance
     heterogeneity.

5. NO race or insurance disparity in pfs_months, in any analysis tried:
   - Univariate: ANOVA across feature_018 p=0.689; across feature_045 p=0.794.
     Pairwise contrasts vs the white / privately insured reference are all <=0.06 mo
     and all p >= 0.14.
   - Adjusted: in the full multivariable model, every race dummy p>=0.55 and every
     insurance dummy p>=0.17.
   - Within feature_051=0 and feature_051=1 strata: ANOVA p>=0.72 for race, >=0.79
     for insurance.
   - Combination-treatment benefit (feature_109 x feature_039): consistent across
     white (+1.51), black (+1.76), asian (+1.42), hispanic (+0.99) and 'other'
     (+1.14). Three-way interaction with race had only one nominally significant
     contrast (other vs white, p=0.012) on a tiny subgroup (13 dual-positives),
     and does not survive correction.

Hypotheses supported
--------------------
H1 (feature_051 reduces PFS univariately) — supported, large effect.
H2 (feature_078 +, feature_057 - on PFS) — supported, very strong.
H3 (feature_013, feature_006 -; feature_009 + on PFS) — supported.
H6 (clinical features remain significant after adjusting for race/insurance) — supported.
H7 (feature_051 effect is consistent across PS strata) — supported.
H10 (small feature_051 x PS interaction) — supported, weak magnitude.
H11 (feature_051 effect grows with age) — supported.
H12 (feature_051 x feature_013 interaction) — supported in adjusted model, but
    unadjusted in-stratum effects go the opposite way; treat as a confounded signal.
H14 (feature_051 effect is comparable across age tertiles unadjusted) — supported.
H17 (feature_067 has independent effect among ordinals) — supported.
H18, H19 (feature_109 and feature_039 are large positive predictors after adjustment)
    — supported.
H20 (feature_109 positive in both feature_051 strata) — supported.
H23 (feature_109 x feature_039 synergy) — supported, very large effect.
H25 (feature_009 large adjusted positive effect) — supported.

Hypotheses refuted (or not supported)
-------------------------------------
H4 (race-level mean differences in PFS) — refuted, ANOVA p=0.69.
H5 (insurance-level differences, esp. uninsured/medicaid worse) — refuted, p=0.79.
H8 (feature_051 effect differs by race) — refuted, joint p=0.24.
H9 (feature_051 effect differs by insurance) — refuted, joint p=0.95.
H15, H16 (race / insurance differences within treatment strata) — refuted.
H21, H22 (feature_109 or feature_039 effects modified by feature_051) — refuted.
H24 (feature_109 x feature_039 synergy differs by race) — refuted in major groups
    (white/black/hispanic/asian); a small 'other' subgroup contrast was nominally
    significant but does not survive multiple-comparison correction.
H26 (race or insurance becomes significant in the final adjusted model) — refuted.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
Almost all of the variation in pfs_months in this prostate-cancer cohort is
explained by classic clinical features: age (feature_078), performance status
(feature_057), a high-prevalence treatment/disease-severity flag (feature_051),
and a small set of laboratory-like features (feature_009, feature_006, feature_013,
feature_067). The single largest treatment-like effect is the synergy between two
low-prevalence binary flags (feature_109 and feature_039): neither alone moves PFS,
but together they add about 1.44 months — a finding that should be flagged for
clinical follow-up because it is invisible in either feature's main-effect screen.

The cohort shows NO evidence of race- or insurance-related disparity in
progression-free survival. This holds in raw means, in clinically adjusted models,
in within-treatment-stratum comparisons, and in interactions of treatments
(feature_051, the feature_109 + feature_039 combination) with race or insurance.
The clinical and laboratory features fully explain the observed PFS distribution
once they are included in the model.
