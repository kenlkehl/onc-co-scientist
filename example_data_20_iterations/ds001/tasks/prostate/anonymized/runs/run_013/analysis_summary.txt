ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_prostate (n = 50,000 patients, outcome = pfs_months)
==========================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The dataset is a 50,000-patient prostate-cancer EHR cohort with 122 anonymized
features and progression-free survival in months (pfs_months) as the sole
outcome. Cohort-mean pfs_months = 3.74 (SD 2.02; range 0.0–14.6). Three
columns are categorical strings (race = feature_018; insurance = feature_045;
patient_id), seventy-seven are binary 0/1, eight are small ordinal
integers (3–11 levels), and 35 are continuous floats. Across 25 iterations I
proposed 27 hypotheses spanning main effects, confounding, subgroup analyses,
and interactions, and tested each statistically.

DOMINANT MAIN-EFFECT PROGNOSTIC SIGNALS (Iter 1–6)
--------------------------------------------------
1) feature_078 (continuous, range 30–90, mean 65; plausibly age) shows by far
   the strongest single association with pfs_months: Pearson r = 0.856
   (p ≈ 0). Per-unit linear slope = +0.174 months. Hypothesis SUPPORTED with
   very large effect.

2) feature_057 (3-level ordinal 0/1/2, prevalences 35/50/15%) has a strong
   monotonic negative gradient: mean pfs_months drops from 4.68 (level 0) →
   3.49 (level 1) → 2.38 (level 2). ANOVA F = 4446, p ≈ 0; Spearman ρ =
   −0.386. Each step ≈ −1.15 months. SUPPORTED.

3) log(feature_013) (continuous, heavy right tail to 3622; plausibly a serum
   biomarker such as PSA) has Pearson r = −0.231 with pfs_months on the log
   scale (raw r = −0.153). Negative direction confirmed; log transform is the
   cleaner relation. SUPPORTED.

4) feature_006 (continuous, 0–24.6) negatively associated, r = −0.143
   (p = 9.9e-226). SUPPORTED.

5) feature_009 (continuous, 1.5–5.5, mean 3.80; plausibly serum albumin)
   positively associated, r = +0.123 (p = 1.5e-166). SUPPORTED.

6) feature_051 (binary, prev 55%) is associated with shorter pfs_months in
   unadjusted analysis: 3.51 vs 4.03, diff = −0.52 month (p = 1.6e-181).
   SUPPORTED unadjusted.

DEMOGRAPHIC EQUITY MAIN EFFECTS (Iter 6–7, 11–12)
-------------------------------------------------
- Race (feature_018, 5 categories) — ANOVA F = 0.56, p = 0.69. Mean
  pfs_months by race ranges 3.74–3.80 across all groups. NULL.
- Insurance (feature_045, 4 categories) — ANOVA F = 0.34, p = 0.79.
  Means range 3.73–3.75. NULL.
- After adjustment for the prognostic block (feature_078 + C(feature_057) +
  log_feature_013 + feature_006 + feature_009): partial F for race =
  0.10 (p = 0.98); partial F for insurance = 0.013 (p = 0.998). Race and
  insurance remain NULL conditional on disease severity. Hypotheses about
  racial/insurance disparities in pfs_months were REFUTED in this cohort.

CONFOUNDING ANALYSIS — feature_051 (Iter 8)
-------------------------------------------
After adjusting for feature_078, feature_057, log(feature_013), feature_006,
and feature_009, the feature_051 coefficient collapsed from −0.52 to
−0.0018 (p = 0.64). Investigation showed feature_051 is balanced across
race, insurance, feature_078, and feature_057, but is strongly correlated
with log(feature_013): point-biserial r = +0.558; mean log(feature_013) =
1.99 in feature_051==0 vs 3.50 in feature_051==1. The univariate
−0.52-month deficit is therefore fully explained by feature_051 marking
patients with high biomarker burden (h9 SUPPORTED: feature_051 is a
confounded indicator, not an independent predictor).

ADDITIONAL ADJUSTED MAIN EFFECTS (Iter 9–10)
--------------------------------------------
- feature_109 (binary, prev 10%): adjusted coef = +0.152 month
  (p = 5.9e-181). Independent positive predictor. SUPPORTED.
- feature_039 (binary, prev 10%): adjusted coef = +0.149 (p = 6.7e-177).
  SUPPORTED.
- feature_105 (binary, prev 6%): univariate p = 0.015 but adjusted
  coef = +0.003 (p = 0.65). NULL after adjustment.
- feature_115, feature_104 (h27): both non-significant after adjustment
  (p = 0.54 and p = 0.12), consistent with chance / marginal confounding
  rather than true independent effects.

INTERACTIONS / EFFECT MODIFICATION (Iter 13–22)
-----------------------------------------------
Demographic interactions (race / insurance with treatment-marker or
prognostic features) were uniformly NULL or borderline:
- feature_051 × race interaction: F(4) = 1.38, p = 0.24 — REFUTED.
- feature_051 × insurance interaction: F(3) = 0.85, p = 0.47 — REFUTED.
- feature_078 × race interaction: F(4) = 1.72, p = 0.14 — REFUTED.
- feature_057 × race interaction: F(8) = 0.67, p = 0.72 — REFUTED.
- feature_057 × insurance interaction: F(6) = 2.03, p = 0.0585 —
  borderline, does not pass α = 0.05.
- Within the worst feature_057 stratum (level 2, n = 7437) racial means
  ranged 2.31–2.46 with ANOVA p = 0.47 — NULL.

Substantive prognostic-feature interactions WERE detected:
- feature_078 × feature_057: F = 983.6, p ≈ 0. Per-unit feature_078 slope
  is +0.180 in feature_057=0, +0.174 in =1, +0.159 in =2 — the
  age/feature_078 gradient is attenuated in worse-prognosis strata.
- log(feature_013) × feature_057: F = 80.2, p = 1.7e-35 — biomarker–PFS
  relationship is heterogeneous across feature_057 levels.
- feature_006 × feature_057: F = 23.6, p = 5.5e-11 — significant
  heterogeneity.
- feature_051 × feature_057: F = 18.9, p = 6.4e-9 — significant but very
  small (interaction terms ≤ 0.06 month); residual feature_051 deficit
  attenuates as feature_057 worsens.

NONLINEARITY (Iter 23)
----------------------
Adding a quadratic feature_078 term to the prognostic-block model produced
F = 5604.6 (p ≈ 0), with the quadratic coefficient = +0.000834 (positive
convex curvature). The age/feature_078 dose–response accelerates at higher
values; a linear-only specification materially underfits.

OVERALL MODEL FIT (Iter 24)
---------------------------
A multivariable OLS combining feature_078 + C(feature_057) +
log(feature_013) + feature_006 + feature_009 + feature_109 + feature_039 +
feature_051 yields R² = 0.9699 on n = 50,000. Eight features explain ~97%
of pfs_months variation; pfs_months in this cohort is essentially a
deterministic function of these prognostics with the residual being
small/random noise.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1) PFS in this prostate-cancer cohort is dominated by a small handful of
   prognostic features: feature_078 (likely age, r = 0.86), feature_057 (a
   3-level severity tier), log(feature_013) (a heavy-tailed biomarker;
   plausibly PSA), feature_006, and feature_009. Together with feature_109
   and feature_039 they explain ~97% of outcome variance.

2) feature_051's apparent unadjusted treatment-like effect (−0.52 month) is
   entirely confounded by its association with high feature_013; it has no
   independent prognostic signal.

3) Demographic features (race feature_018, insurance feature_045) show NO
   marginal or adjusted association with pfs_months and NO interaction with
   feature_051 or with feature_078. There is no evidence in this dataset of
   racial- or insurance-based disparity in PFS, either as a main effect or
   as effect modification of treatment-like or severity markers.

4) Several large prognostic interactions exist among the disease/biomarker
   features themselves: the slope of feature_078, log(feature_013), and
   feature_006 against PFS varies across feature_057 strata. The age/PFS
   curve is also nonlinear (positive convex).

5) Borderline univariate hits among low-prevalence binary features
   (feature_115, feature_104, feature_105) do not survive multivariable
   adjustment.
