Analysis summary - ds001_prostate (50,000 patients, outcome pfs_months)
========================================================================

Cohort and approach
-------------------
The dataset contains 50,000 de-identified prostate-cancer-style patient records
with 122 anonymized features and one outcome (pfs_months, "progression-free
survival" in months). Features come in three flavors: 77 binary (encoded 0/1),
9 ordinal/count integers, 34 continuous floats, plus two object-typed
demographic variables (feature_018: race - white, black, hispanic, asian,
other; feature_045: insurance - medicare, private, medicaid, uninsured).

I ran 25 iterations of propose-test-refine, starting with univariate
associations of the strongest candidate features, building a multivariable
adjusted model, and then probing demographic main effects, demographic x
clinical interactions, and biomarker x biomarker synergies.

Variables that look clinical
----------------------------
Inspection of distributions made several features clinically interpretable:
* feature_078: continuous 30-90, median 65 -> patient age in years
* feature_057: 3-level ordinal 0/1/2 -> stage / disease extent
* feature_067: ordinal 6-10 -> Gleason sum
* feature_013: right-skewed 0.12-3622, median 15.86 -> serum PSA (ng/mL)
* feature_006: continuous 0-24, ~25% zeros -> tumor-burden / symptom score
* feature_009: continuous 1.5-5.5, median 3.8 -> protective lab (e.g. albumin)

Main-effect findings (iterations 1-7)
-------------------------------------
* feature_078 (age): r = +0.856 with pfs_months. Mean PFS rises from 0.01 mo
  in ages 30-36 to 7.82 mo in ages 84-90. Adjusted slope = +0.174 mo per year
  of age. Dominant predictor. Supported.
* feature_057 (stage): r = -0.388. Mean PFS = 4.68/3.49/2.38 mo for levels
  0/1/2. Adjusted contrasts -1.18 mo (stage 1) and -2.29 mo (stage 2) vs
  stage 0. Supported.
* feature_013 (PSA): r = -0.153 raw; on log1p scale adjusted slope = -0.383
  mo per log unit. Supported.
* feature_006: r = -0.143. Adjusted slope = -0.077 mo per unit. Supported.
* feature_009: r = +0.123. Adjusted slope = +0.479 mo per unit. Supported.
* feature_067 (Gleason): r = -0.003, p = 0.49. Mean PFS is essentially flat
  across Gleason 6/7/8/9/10. Surprisingly NOT a meaningful predictor in this
  cohort. Refuted.
* feature_051 (binary, prev 0.55): univariate mean diff = -0.52 mo, p = 1.6e-181.
  Strong univariate negative association. Supported.

Mediation: feature_051 vs PSA (iteration 8)
-------------------------------------------
The strong univariate effect of feature_051 disappears after adjustment for
log feature_013 (adjusted coef = +0.0007 mo, p = 0.86). Patients with
feature_051 = 1 have a much higher mean log PSA (3.55 vs 2.17), so feature_051
is best read as a marker of high PSA rather than an independent prognostic
covariate. Refined hypothesis supported.

Demographic main effects (iterations 9-12)
------------------------------------------
* Race (feature_018) main effect on raw PFS: ANOVA F = 0.564, p = 0.689.
  Largest pairwise gap (asian 3.797 vs hispanic 3.737) is 0.06 mo. Refuted.
* Insurance (feature_045) main effect: ANOVA F = 0.344, p = 0.794. Uninsured
  vs medicare gap = 0.028 mo. Refuted.
* Adjusted (multivariable OLS controlling for clinical covariates): all four
  race contrasts and all three insurance contrasts have |coef| < 0.003 mo
  with p > 0.6. Adjusted refutation.

Variance explained (iteration 13)
---------------------------------
A 7-feature parsimonious model {age, stage, Gleason, log-PSA, feature_006,
feature_009, feature_051} achieves R^2 = 0.969 - 97% of pfs_months variance
is captured by clinical features alone. This synthetic cohort has an
extraordinarily deterministic outcome model.

Race within advanced stage (iteration 14)
-----------------------------------------
Even restricted to feature_057 = 2 (n = 7,437 advanced-stage patients),
ANOVA across racial groups gives F = 0.895, p = 0.466. Disparities are not
hidden in the highest-risk subgroup either. Refuted.

Synergistic biomarker pair feature_109 x feature_039 (iterations 15-17)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Each marker alone has only a tiny univariate positive association
(feature_109 +0.10 mo, p = 0.002; feature_039 +0.094 mo, p = 0.003), and
both lose their main effect almost entirely (|coef| < 0.001) when an
interaction term is added. The interaction term feature_109 x feature_039,
however, has coefficient +1.434 mo (95% CI 1.40-1.47, p effectively 0):

  feature_109  feature_039   mean PFS (raw)      n
       0           0            3.74           40,435
       0           1            3.67            4,569
       1           0            3.67            4,467
       1           1            5.22              529

Including this interaction raises model R^2 from 0.969 to 0.974. The
biomarker pair behaves like a "predictive companion-marker" - benefit accrues
only when BOTH are positive (~2% of patients).

feature_051 x stage interaction (iteration 18)
----------------------------------------------
Stratified t-tests show feature_051's univariate negative association is
similar in size across stages (-0.56 / -0.51 / -0.47 mo for stages 0/1/2),
slightly LARGER in early stage. The original hypothesis (largest gap at
advanced stage) is refuted.

Combo effect across demographics (iterations 19-21)
---------------------------------------------------
Within-race within-insurance estimates of the (109=1 & 039=1) vs others
PFS difference are all in the +1.0 to +2.8 mo range:
* asian +1.44, black +1.75, hispanic +0.99, white +1.53, other +2.81 mo
* medicare +1.41, private +1.52, medicaid +1.78, uninsured (n too small)

Prevalences of feature_109 and feature_039 are balanced across race
(chi-square p = 0.43 and p = 0.26, respectively) and insurance (p = 0.40 and
p = 0.63). Combo prevalence is 0.8-1.1% in every demographic stratum. This
"predictive marker" treatment-equivalent has no demographic disparity in
prevalence or effect.

feature_051 x demographics and Gleason interactions (iterations 22-24)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Race x feature_051: all four interaction coefficients |<= 0.023|, p >= 0.14.
Insurance x feature_051: all three interaction coefficients |<= 0.011|,
p >= 0.27. Gleason x feature_051: all four interactions |<= 0.016|,
p >= 0.35. No subgroup heterogeneity. Refuted.

Final nested model F-test (iteration 25)
----------------------------------------
Base model: pfs_months ~ feature_078 + C(feature_057) + C(feature_067)
            + log_psa + feature_006 + feature_009 + feature_051
            + feature_109*feature_039
Full model: base + C(feature_018) + C(feature_045)

R^2 base = 0.974451, R^2 full = 0.974451 (RSS drop 0.031 over 7 added df).
Joint F-test for race + insurance: F = 0.042, p = 0.99991. Refuted -
demographics carry zero residual signal.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. pfs_months in this cohort is essentially a deterministic function of
   clinical / biomarker variables: age (feature_078), stage (feature_057),
   log-PSA (feature_013), tumor-burden (feature_006), a protective lab
   (feature_009), and the synergistic pair (feature_109 x feature_039).
   These together explain ~97.4% of variance.
2. The Gleason-like ordinal feature_067 is NOT prognostic in this cohort
   (raw r = -0.003, p = 0.49) - a clinical surprise.
3. feature_051 is a marker of high PSA, not an independent driver of PFS.
   Its univariate effect (-0.52 mo) is fully mediated by log feature_013.
4. The biomarker pair feature_109 + feature_039 displays a strong
   multiplicative (synergistic) interaction: each alone is essentially null;
   together they add ~1.4 mo of PFS in the ~2% of patients who carry both.
5. There is NO demographic disparity in this cohort, by any metric tested:
   - No raw race or insurance main effect on pfs_months
   - No adjusted race or insurance effect after controlling for clinical
     covariates (joint F-test p ~ 1.0)
   - No racial / insurance disparity in marker (109, 039, combo) prevalence
   - No race x treatment-marker interaction
   - No insurance x treatment-marker interaction
   - No racial heterogeneity even within the worst-prognosis stage 2
     subgroup
   The benefit of the synergistic feature_109*feature_039 marker is in the
   same 1-2 month range across all racial and insurance subgroups.

In a real-world dataset this pattern - 97% R^2 from clinical variables and
an exact null on demographics - would suggest a synthetic / simulated
data-generating process designed to be fair and clinically interpretable.
