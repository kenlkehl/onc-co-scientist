Analysis Summary: ds001_prostate (50,000 patients; outcome = pfs_months)
=======================================================================

Approach
--------
We took a propose-test-refine path through 25 iterations: a screen of all
numeric features against pfs_months by Spearman correlation, univariable
linear-regression confirmation of every hypothesis-implicating feature,
then a multivariable model, then planned interaction and subgroup tests
to look for effect-modification by stage-like, biomarker-like, racial,
and socioeconomic variables. All 25 iterations produced a hypothesis with
matching analysis (signed effect estimate, p-value, significance flag) in
the transcript.

Headline findings
-----------------
1. feature_078 (continuous, range 30-90, mean ~65 - plausibly age in years)
   is the dominant predictor of pfs_months. Spearman rho = +0.853; OLS
   slope = +0.174 pfs_months per unit, p ~ 0; alone explains R^2 = 0.732
   of variance. The relationship is monotonically POSITIVE - older
   feature_078 corresponds to longer pfs_months. (Iterations 1, 2.)

2. feature_057 (ordinal 0/1/2; counts ~17.6k / 25.0k / 7.4k - plausibly a
   stage-like or treatment-line variable) drops pfs_months by ~1.16 mo
   per level (4.68 -> 3.49 -> 2.38 mo). p ~ 0, R^2 = 0.151. (Iteration 3.)

3. feature_013 (right-skewed continuous, plausibly a tumor marker) is
   negatively associated on a log scale: log10(feature_013) beta = -0.801,
   p ~ 0, R^2 = 0.053 (vs R^2 = 0.024 on the raw scale, confirming
   log-linear shape is the right specification). (Iteration 4.)

4. feature_006 (range 0-24.6, mean 3.84): negatively associated, beta =
   -0.075, p = 9.9e-226. (Iteration 5.)

5. feature_009 (range 1.5-5.5, mean 3.80, plausibly a serum-protein-like
   lab): positively associated, beta = +0.495, p = 1.5e-166. (Iteration 6.)

6. Multivariable model on the five predictors above yielded:
   feature_078 +0.174, feature_057 -1.150, log10(feature_013) -0.804,
   feature_006 -0.077, feature_009 +0.478 - all retained univariable sign
   and significance, R^2 = 0.969. (Iteration 11.)

Hypotheses that were proposed and refuted
-----------------------------------------
- feature_051 (binary): a striking univariable association (beta = -0.521,
  p = 9.8e-182) DISAPPEARED after adjustment for the iteration-11 set
  (adjusted beta = -0.0018, p = 0.645) - consistent with confounding by
  feature_078. Within strata of feature_057 alone, however, the negative
  direction is preserved (betas -0.56, -0.51, -0.47), so the simple
  marginal direction is real but not independent of feature_078. (Iter 7,
  19.)

- feature_018 (race / ethnicity, with categories asian, black, hispanic,
  other, white): NO association with pfs_months. Mean pfs_months across
  all five groups falls within a 0.06-month band (3.74-3.80); ANOVA F =
  0.564, p = 0.689. (Iteration 8.)

- feature_045 (insurance: medicare, medicaid, private, uninsured): NO
  association with pfs_months. Means span 3.726-3.754; ANOVA F = 0.344,
  p = 0.794. (Iteration 9.)

- feature_067 (range 6-10 with peak at 7 - looks like a Gleason-grade
  ordinal in a prostate cohort) showed NO main-effect association with
  pfs_months: linear beta = -0.006, p = 0.486; means 3.70-3.76 across
  values 6-10. No interaction with feature_057, feature_078, or
  log10(feature_013). This is unexpected if it really is a Gleason-like
  variable; the most plausible explanation is that the column is a noise
  feature in this anonymized release rather than a true Gleason grade.
  (Iteration 10.)

Interaction structure
---------------------
Five clinically relevant interactions on the iteration-11 predictor set
were tested. All had highly significant interaction terms with
interpretable directions:

  feature_078 x feature_057:    beta = -0.0097, p ~ 0  (older patients
       gain less from being at lower feature_057 levels - the slope of
       pfs_months on feature_078 falls from +0.180 at feature_057=0 to
       +0.159 at feature_057=2). (Iteration 14.)

  feature_078 x log10(feature_013): beta = -0.0070, p = 3.9e-145
       (the negative biomarker effect is amplified at older feature_078).
       (Iteration 15.)

  feature_057 x log10(feature_013): beta = +0.0473, p = 4.5e-32 (the
       biomarker effect attenuates from -0.84 at feature_057=0 to -0.73
       at feature_057=2). (Iteration 16.)

  feature_078 x feature_009:    beta = +0.0043, p = 5.9e-41 (feature_009
       is more protective at older feature_078). (Iteration 17.)

  feature_057 x feature_009:    beta = -0.0258, p = 3.2e-08 (feature_009
       is less protective at higher feature_057 - the protective slope
       drops from +0.49 to +0.44 across stage-like levels). (Iteration
       18.)

We additionally added a quadratic term feature_078^2 to confirm the
shape of the dominant effect: beta_quadratic = +7.8e-04, p = 1.9e-112,
indicating mild convex (upward-curving) growth of pfs_months with
feature_078 - i.e., gains in pfs_months accelerate at older ages.
(Iteration 24.)

Equity / disparity assessment
-----------------------------
Subgroup tests for differential prognostic effects by race and insurance
were performed for feature_078, feature_057, log10(feature_013), and
feature_009. NONE of the race-x-predictor F-tests produced an
interpretable disparity signal:
  feature_018 x feature_078:  F = 0.323, p = 0.863
  feature_018 x feature_057:  F = 0.221, p = 0.927
  feature_018 x log_f013:     F = 0.530, p = 0.714
  feature_018 x feature_009:  F = 2.730, p = 0.0275 (marginal, would not
       survive correction for the four predictor x race tests)
None of the four insurance-x-predictor F-tests reached p < 0.05 (smallest
p = 0.452). In a final adjusted multivariable model that included
feature_078, feature_057, log_f013, feature_006, feature_009, feature_109,
feature_039 plus race and insurance dummies, every race and insurance
dummy coefficient was |beta| <= 0.005 with p > 0.5; the model R^2 was
0.970. (Iterations 12, 13, 20, 25.)

Two further independent prognostic features
-------------------------------------------
A whole-binary-feature adjusted screen identified two binary columns whose
adjusted associations with pfs_months were 5+ times larger in absolute
value than every other binary feature in the dataset:
  feature_109 (n=4,996 with value 1): adjusted beta = +0.152, p = 2.5e-182
  feature_039 (n=5,098 with value 1): adjusted beta = +0.148, p = 2.6e-177
Each had a small but significant univariable effect (~+0.10 mo) which
GREW after adjustment - consistent with these being modestly protective
features whose marginal signal is masked by negative confounders captured
in the iteration-11 set. (Iterations 21, 22, 23.)

Bottom-line conclusions
-----------------------
- Five continuous/ordinal features (feature_078, feature_057,
  feature_013 [log10], feature_006, feature_009) drive nearly all of the
  variance in pfs_months in this cohort (multivariable R^2 = 0.969).
- Adding feature_109 and feature_039 raises R^2 only marginally to 0.970
  but adds two highly significant independent positive effects.
- feature_067 (Gleason-like by face value) and feature_051 (binary, large
  marginal effect) carry essentially NO independent signal once the five
  drivers are held fixed.
- Several biologically reasonable interactions are present
  (feature_078 with feature_057, feature_013 and feature_009;
  feature_057 with feature_013 and feature_009), with directions and
  magnitudes that fit the storyline of a stage-like axis interacting
  with biomarker and lab axes.
- We found NO evidence of differential pfs_months by self-reported race
  (feature_018) or insurance (feature_045), and NO meaningful evidence
  that the prognostic effects of the dominant predictors vary by race or
  insurance. Whatever processes generated this dataset, race and
  insurance do not modify pfs_months independently of clinical features.
