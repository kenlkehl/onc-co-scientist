Analysis Summary — ds001_prostate (n=50,000)
==============================================

OUTCOME
-------
The outcome is pfs_months (progression-free survival, months): mean 3.74, SD 2.02,
range 0–14.6.

OVERVIEW OF THE EXPLORATION
---------------------------
Across 25 iterations I worked from broad univariable screens through stratified and
interaction analyses to a final parsimonious multivariable model. The dataset turns
out to have a small number of strong predictors and a large number of features that
are simply noise. A single 8-feature model (one of those features being a binary
× binary synergy term) explains R² = 0.95 of the variance in pfs_months — adding
the other ~115 features only nudges R² to ~0.952. The discussion below walks
through what was supported, what was refuted, and what is clinically interesting.


PRIMARY PREDICTORS (all p < 1e-100 in the final adjusted model)
---------------------------------------------------------------
1. feature_078 (continuous, range 30–90, mean 65). Strongest single predictor by
   far. Pearson r = +0.856 with pfs_months. Quintile means: 1.35, 2.75, 3.72,
   4.70, 6.24 months — a near-monotonic, approximately linear gradient. Adjusted
   slope = +0.174 mo per unit. The relationship is consistent across feature_057
   strata (within-stratum r = 0.83–0.85), so feature_078 is informative across the
   full disease spectrum. Although the variable's scale is consistent with age in
   years, the direction (higher value → longer PFS) is the opposite of what one
   would expect for age in real-world prostate cancer; this may reflect the
   synthetic/anonymised structure of the dataset rather than clinical biology.

2. feature_057 (3-level ordinal, 0/1/2; behaves like a stage variable).
   Adjusted slope = -1.152 mo per level. Group means: 4.68 → 3.49 → 2.38 mo at
   levels 0/1/2. This is the dominant disease-severity feature.

3. feature_009 (continuous, 1.5–5.5, mean 3.8 — pattern resembles a serum
   protein such as albumin). Adjusted slope = +0.479 mo per unit. Consistent
   across strata, with a small attenuation in higher feature_057 strata
   (interaction coefficient -0.022, p=2.6e-4).

4. feature_013 (continuous, heavily right-skewed 0.12–3622 — pattern resembles
   PSA). Adjusted slope = -0.0027 mo per unit; relationship is linear-ish on
   raw scale and stronger after log-transform (r = -0.231 vs -0.153 raw). No
   evidence of a feature_013 × feature_057 interaction (p=0.56) — the slope is
   uniform across stage strata.

5. feature_006 (continuous, 0–24.6, mean 3.84). Adjusted slope = -0.076 mo per
   unit. Consistently negative prognostic.


TREATMENT-LIKE BINARY SIGNALS
-----------------------------
6. feature_051 (binary, ~55% prevalent). Strongly NEGATIVE for PFS:
   adjusted coefficient -0.376 mo (p≈0). The unadjusted difference is -0.521
   mo (4.03 → 3.51). The effect is consistent across:
     - all three feature_057 strata (-0.56, -0.51, -0.47 mo)
     - all 5 self-reported races (range -0.37 to -0.54; nested-F interaction
       p = 0.80 — no race × treatment heterogeneity)
     - all 4 insurance categories (range -0.51 to -0.60).
   Whether feature_051 represents a harmful exposure, an indicator of
   higher-risk patients selected for it, or a less effective therapy line cannot
   be determined from this dataset alone, but the negative sign is robust.

7. feature_109 + feature_039 (each binary, ~10% prevalent each, ~1% with
   both). This is the most interesting pattern in the dataset:
     - feature_109 alone: adjusted coef ≈ +0.003 (NS)
     - feature_039 alone: adjusted coef ≈ -0.003 (NS)
     - feature_109 × feature_039 (both = 1): coef = +1.440 (p≈0).
   The 2×2 cell means make the synergy explicit:
     (0,0) 3.74 (n=40435); (0,1) 3.67 (n=4569); (1,0) 3.67 (n=4467);
     (1,1) 5.22 (n=529).
   Expected if additive: 3.60. Observed combination: 5.22. Excess over
   additivity: +1.62 months. Either feature alone provides no benefit; the
   combination provides a large benefit. Stratified analysis suggests the
   combination benefit is qualitatively present across stage strata, with no
   significant three-way interaction. The effect is also independent of
   feature_051: in patients with feature_051=1 the combo still adds ~1.5 mo on
   top of the -0.4 mo feature_051 penalty.


WHAT IS NOT A PREDICTOR
-----------------------
Several features that *look* like they should be predictors are not:

  - feature_067 (5-level, 6–10, pattern resembling a Gleason score):
    unadjusted slope ≈ 0 (p = 0.49). Adjusted, the slope is -0.011 mo per
    level (p = 5e-8 because n=50,000), but moving from "Gleason 6" to "Gleason
    10" changes pfs_months by only ~0.04 mo — clinically negligible.
  - feature_071 (0–4 ordinal, ECOG-pattern): no monotonic trend; means
    differ within ±0.06 mo across all five levels.
  - feature_016, feature_096, feature_033, feature_064 (each 5-level 0–4):
    flat across levels, |Δ| < 0.08 mo, no monotonic trend.
  - feature_026 (11-level 0–10): non-monotonic 3.69–3.84, indistinguishable
    from noise.
  - Self-reported race (feature_018): F-test p = 0.69; group means within
    ±0.06 mo of each other.
  - Insurance category (feature_045): F-test p = 0.79.
  - Among the 77 binary features tested in adjusted models, only three
    (feature_051, feature_109, feature_039) had |β| > 0.10. The rest had
    |β| < 0.05 with mostly p > 0.05.
  - Among the 35 numeric continuous features, only feature_078, feature_013,
    feature_006, and feature_009 have |r| > 0.10 with pfs_months. The
    median |r| of the remaining numeric features is ~0.007.


INTERACTION SUMMARY
-------------------
  - feature_078 × feature_057: significant negative interaction
    (β = -0.0094, p = 6e-208) — the protective effect of higher feature_078
    is modestly attenuated at higher stage. Clinically small (~5% relative
    attenuation per stage) given a main slope of +0.174.
  - feature_009 × feature_057: significant negative interaction
    (β = -0.022, p = 2.6e-4); same direction.
  - feature_013 × feature_057: no interaction (p = 0.56).
  - feature_109 × feature_039: dominant synergistic interaction
    (β = +1.44, p ≈ 0).
  - feature_051 × feature_057: tiny but detectable (β = +0.026, p = 2e-5);
    direction is clinically minor.
  - feature_051 × race: no interaction (nested F p = 0.80).


SUBGROUPS WITH HETEROGENEITY OF EFFECT
--------------------------------------
The headline finding from subgroup analyses is *consistency*: the effects of
feature_051 and the feature_109+feature_039 combination are approximately
homogeneous across self-reported race, insurance, and disease-severity
strata. There is no evidence of a race-related disparity in either the
prognosis (race main effect p = 0.69) or the treatment-effect direction.
Disease severity (feature_057) does modify the slope of feature_078 and
feature_009 modestly, but does not alter the qualitative direction of any
predictor.


PARSIMONIOUS MODEL
------------------
Final OLS model:
  pfs_months ~ feature_078 + feature_057 + feature_013 + feature_009
              + feature_006 + feature_051 + feature_109 + feature_039
              + feature_109:feature_039
R² = 0.9514, residual SD ≈ 0.45 months.

Coefficients:
  intercept           -7.84
  feature_078         +0.174
  feature_057         -1.152
  feature_013         -0.0027
  feature_009         +0.479
  feature_006         -0.076
  feature_051         -0.376
  feature_109         +0.003 (NS — all signal in interaction)
  feature_039         -0.003 (NS — all signal in interaction)
  feature_109×feature_039  +1.440

Adding the remaining ~115 features improves R² by less than 0.001.


CONCLUSIONS
-----------
1. Variance in pfs_months in this prostate cohort is driven almost entirely
   by feature_078 (a continuous 30–90 variable) plus a 3-level severity
   variable (feature_057), three labs (feature_009, feature_013, feature_006),
   one harmful binary (feature_051), and a synergistic binary pair
   (feature_109 with feature_039).

2. The most clinically interesting pattern is the strong synergy between
   feature_109 and feature_039: each is null on its own (~1% of patients
   have both), but together they add ~1.5 months of PFS. This pattern
   would be missed by a main-effects-only screen and is a textbook example
   of why interaction testing is valuable.

3. Self-reported race and insurance show neither prognostic effect nor
   treatment-effect modification. The treatment-relevant signals
   (feature_051 and the feature_109+feature_039 combination) are
   homogeneous across demographic subgroups.

4. Several "named-looking" features (Gleason-like feature_067, ECOG-like
   feature_071, the five-level ordinals feature_016/096/033/064) are
   essentially noise in this cohort. Many lab-pattern continuous features
   are also uninformative. This concentration of signal into a small
   feature set is consistent with a synthetic or heavily-anonymised
   cohort and is itself an important methodologic note.
