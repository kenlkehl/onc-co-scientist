Analysis Summary — ds001_prostate (n=50,000)
=============================================

Outcome of interest: pfs_months (mean 3.74, SD 2.02, range 0–14.6).
Cohort: prostate cancer (sex_female=0 for all). 55% mCRPC, 20% visceral mets,
20% bone mets, 15% liver mets, 60% PSMA-high, 10% BRCA2-mutated, 3% MSI-high,
20% AR-V7+. Treatment exposure: enzalutamide 40%, abiraterone 30%, docetaxel
30%, lu177_psma 15%, olaparib 10%, pembrolizumab 5%. ECOG PS distribution
0/1/2 = 35/50/15%. Across 25 iterations I tested treatment main effects,
prognostic clinical/lab features, biomarker-treatment subgroup interactions,
multivariable adjustment, and demographic disparities.

------------------------------------------------------------
1) Treatment main effects (Iteration 1)
------------------------------------------------------------
Almost no unconditional treatment effect on PFS. Enzalutamide (-0.03, p=0.15),
abiraterone (-0.01, p=0.78), docetaxel (-0.02, p=0.38), lu177_psma (0.00,
p=0.87), and pembrolizumab (+0.06, p=0.14) were all non-significant overall.
Olaparib showed a small but statistically significant positive main effect
(+0.09 months, p=0.003) — a hint that pooled analyses miss the heterogeneity
in who benefits from targeted therapy.

------------------------------------------------------------
2) Prognostic clinical features (Iterations 2, 3, 18, 19)
------------------------------------------------------------
The dataset shows the expected biology of prostate cancer prognosis:
- mcrpc: PFS 3.51 vs 4.03 in non-CRPC, diff -0.52 months, p≈10^-181 (strong
  negative prognostic factor — supported).
- ecog_ps: r = -0.39, p≈0 (worse PS → markedly shorter PFS — supported).
- visceral_mets: small negative effect, -0.05, p=0.035 (supported, weak).
- liver_mets, bone_mets, gleason_score: not significantly associated with
  PFS in this cohort (refuted).
- psa_ng_ml: r = -0.15, p≈10^-260 (higher PSA → shorter PFS — supported).
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: r = -0.14, p≈10^-226 (cachexia → shorter PFS — supported).
- albumin_g_dl: r = +0.12, p≈10^-166 (higher albumin → longer PFS — supported).
- ldh_u_l: r = -0.017, p=0.0002 (significant but tiny — supported).
- alkaline_phosphatase_u_l: r = -0.012, p=0.007 (small, supported).
- crp_mg_l, nlr, hemoglobin_g_dl, calcium_mg_dl, creatinine, platelets, wbc:
  effectively null (most refuted, hemoglobin showed a paradoxical -0.01
  weak inverse correlation, total_bilirubin showed a tiny -0.01 negative).
- bmi, vitals (HR, BP, SpO2): no associations with PFS.

The dominant prognostic axis is therefore ECOG PS, mCRPC status, PSA,
albumin, and weight loss — a clinically intuitive set.

------------------------------------------------------------
3) Biomarker main effects on PFS (Iteration 4)
------------------------------------------------------------
- brca2_mutation: BRCA2+ patients had +0.10 months PFS overall (p=0.002).
  This pooled effect is driven by olaparib responders (see below).
- ar_v7_positive, msi_high, psma_high, tp53_mutation, pten_loss: no
  significant unconditional main effects on PFS.
This confirms biomarkers act mostly as treatment-effect modifiers, not
free-standing prognosticators in this cohort.

------------------------------------------------------------
4) Targeted-therapy × biomarker interactions (Iterations 5–7, 20)
------------------------------------------------------------
The strongest, most clinically credible signal is olaparib × BRCA2:
- BRCA2+ olaparib recipients: PFS 5.22 mo (n=529) vs 3.67 mo without
  olaparib (n=4,467); diff +1.55 months, p≈10^-27.
- BRCA2-WT olaparib recipients: -0.07 months, p=0.02 (small inverse).
- Interaction coef (olaparib × BRCA2): +1.62 months, p≈10^-61, robust to
  multivariable adjustment for ecog/mcrpc/ldh/albumin (p≈10^-76) and
  prior_lines_of_therapy (p≈10^-61). Hypothesis strongly supported — this
  is a textbook PARP-inhibitor synthetic-lethality signal.

The other two prespecified biomarker-matched effects did not replicate:
- Pembrolizumab × MSI-high: BSI-high pembrolizumab recipients had numerically
  *lower* PFS (3.53 vs 3.79, diff -0.27, p=0.24, n_pem=79). Interaction coef
  -0.34, p=0.15. Refuted in this cohort, though the small N (79 MSI-H
  pembrolizumab patients) limits power.
- Lu177-PSMA × PSMA-high: PFS 3.74 vs 3.75 in PSMA-high group (diff -0.01,
  p=0.74). Interaction coef -0.02, p=0.74. Refuted — the data carry no
  signal that PSMA-targeted radioligand therapy benefits PSMA-high patients
  in this cohort.

------------------------------------------------------------
5) AR-pathway inhibitors × AR-V7 (Iteration 8)
------------------------------------------------------------
Enzalutamide and abiraterone showed no differential benefit by AR-V7 status.
Interactions were not significant (enzalutamide × AR-V7: 0.002, p=0.96;
abiraterone × AR-V7: 0.05, p=0.33). The classical clinical hypothesis
(less benefit in AR-V7+) was not detected in this dataset.

------------------------------------------------------------
6) Multivariable confirmatory model (Iterations 9, 23)
------------------------------------------------------------
Adjusted for ecog_ps, mcrpc, visceral_mets, ldh_u_l, albumin_g_dl,
gleason_score, age, psa, and all six treatments:
- ECOG PS: coef -1.15, p≈0 (each unit ECOG cuts ~1.15 months — dominant).
- albumin_g_dl: coef +0.48, p≈0.
- mcrpc: coef -0.37, p≈0.
- ldh_u_l: coef -0.0005, p≈10^-61 (small per-unit, consistent with broad LDH range).
- olaparib (adjusted): +0.11 months, p=3×10^-5 (overall benefit even without
  BRCA2 stratification, again driven by responders).
- Pembrolizumab, lu177_psma, docetaxel: adjusted effects null (≤0.03 months).

------------------------------------------------------------
7) ECOG- and burden-stratified docetaxel (Iterations 14, 17)
------------------------------------------------------------
Docetaxel showed no PFS benefit in any subgroup tested — neither in ECOG=0,
ECOG=2, visceral_mets+, nor liver_mets+. The docetaxel × ecog_ps and
docetaxel × visceral_mets interactions were both non-significant (p=0.66
and 0.29). The dataset thus does not show selective benefit of taxane
chemotherapy in any prognostic stratum probed.

------------------------------------------------------------
8) Demographics & disparities (Iterations 10, 11, 13)
------------------------------------------------------------
- age_years vs PFS: r = +0.86, p≈0. This is an extraordinarily strong
  *positive* correlation — older patients had longer PFS. This contradicts
  the conventional clinical expectation and is almost certainly a feature
  of how the synthetic dataset was generated (or a strong selection / lead-
  time effect baked into the cohort). It is by far the largest correlation
  with PFS in the dataset.
- insurance_type (medicaid vs private), rural_residence, race_ethnicity
  (black vs white), education_years: all NULL. No demographic disparity in
  PFS detected.
- Comorbidities (DM, CKD, HF, COPD, prior malignancy, HIV): all NULL.
- Prior treatment indicators (prior_chemotherapy, prior_immunotherapy,
  prior_lines_of_therapy, years_since_diagnosis): all NULL.
- Symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite_loss): all NULL.
This dataset's variation in PFS is concentrated in objective biology
(ECOG, mCRPC, PSA, albumin, weight loss, age) rather than reported
symptoms or social determinants.

------------------------------------------------------------
9) SNPs (Iteration 16)
------------------------------------------------------------
None of the five tested pharmacogenomic SNPs (rs1045642 ABCB1, rs1065852
CYP2D6*10, rs1799853 CYP2C9*2, rs429358 APOE, rs1801133 MTHFR) had
p < 0.05 with PFS. No detectable PGx signal at the screening level.

------------------------------------------------------------
10) Three-way and refined biomarker-matched analyses
    (Iterations 21, 22, 24, 25)
------------------------------------------------------------
- The enzalutamide × AR-V7 × prior_chemotherapy three-way interaction was
  not significant (coef -0.04, p=0.67). Even within the AR-V7-/chemo-naive
  subgroup enzalutamide showed no benefit (-0.04, p=0.12).
- BRCA2+/chemo-naive olaparib subgroup confirmed the signal: PFS 5.03 vs
  3.61, diff +1.42 months, p≈10^-14.
- The "biomarker-matched" composite analysis (olap+BRCA2 OR pem+MSI OR
  lu177+PSMA-high) gave matched mean PFS 3.87 vs mismatched 3.72, diff
  +0.16 months, p=3×10^-5, robust to ecog_ps and mcrpc adjustment
  (+0.15, p=10^-5). The signal is essentially carried by the BRCA2/olap
  pair; the MSI/pembrolizumab and PSMA/lu177 components add noise rather
  than signal.

------------------------------------------------------------
Overall conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------
1. The single robust treatment–biomarker relationship is olaparib in
   BRCA2-mutated patients (≈+1.5 month PFS gain, interaction p ≪ 10^-50).
2. Of the prespecified targeted-therapy hypotheses, pembrolizumab × MSI-high
   and lu177_psma × PSMA-high were not supported by the data.
3. The dominant prognostic factors are ECOG PS (largest), mCRPC, albumin,
   PSA, weight loss, visceral mets, and the highly anomalous positive age
   correlation (r=+0.86 — likely a synthetic-data artifact).
4. AR-V7 status did not modify enzalutamide or abiraterone benefit.
5. No demographic, social-determinant, comorbidity, symptom, or SNP
   signal of meaningful magnitude emerged.
6. Most pooled treatment main effects were null because the population is
   heavily diluted with biomarker-mismatched recipients; biomarker-matched
   analyses are required to surface the true treatment effect, as
   illustrated by the olaparib/BRCA2 case.
