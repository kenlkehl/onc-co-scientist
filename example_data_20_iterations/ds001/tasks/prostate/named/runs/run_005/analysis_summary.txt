ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_prostate (N = 50,000)
=================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (mean 3.74, SD 2.02, range 0.00–14.60).

Across 25 iterations I tested 177 hypotheses spanning unadjusted main effects,
multivariable adjustment, treatment × biomarker interactions, stratified
subgroup analyses, and SNP screening. Below I synthesize what held up.

------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Dominant prognostic features (large, robust signal)
------------------------------------------------------------------

* age_years (r = +0.856, p ≈ 0). Age is by far the strongest single
  correlate of pfs_months in this cohort, and the direction is *positive*:
  mean PFS climbs monotonically across age strata (1.2 mo at ≤55 yr → 8.0 mo
  at >85 yr). This is a striking finding because it is the opposite of the
  usual clinical expectation; it dominates the variance and remains highly
  significant after adjustment.
* ecog_ps (r = -0.388, p ≈ 0; multivariable β = -1.15 mo per unit). ECOG
  performance status is the strongest *negative* prognostic feature, with
  each unit costing about 1.15 months of PFS independent of other covariates.
* mcrpc (-0.52 mo unadjusted, p ≈ 1.6e-181; multivariable β = -0.37). Castration-
  resistant disease is the largest binary adverse prognostic factor.
* albumin_g_dl (r = +0.123, p ≈ 1.5e-166; multivariable β = +0.48). Higher
  serum albumin is independently protective.
* weight_loss_pct_6mo (r = -0.143, p ≈ 1e-225). Recent weight loss is
  consistently negative.
* psa_ng_ml (r = -0.153, p ≈ 3e-260). Higher PSA modestly negative.
* visceral_mets adverse but small (-0.05 mo, p ≈ 0.035).
* ldh, alkaline_phosphatase, hemoglobin: small but statistically significant
  associations in the expected direction.

A combined linear model (age + ECOG + mCRPC + visceral_mets + albumin + LDH +
alk-phos + hemoglobin + NLR + prior_lines + Gleason + PSA) reaches an
adjusted R² of 0.925 — these classical prognostic features explain almost
all of the explainable PFS variance in this cohort.

------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Treatment main effects (mostly null)
------------------------------------------------------------------

Unadjusted univariate t-tests of pfs_months by each of the six treatments:

  enzalutamide   diff -0.026, p = 0.15  (NS)
  abiraterone    diff -0.006, p = 0.78  (NS)
  docetaxel      diff -0.017, p = 0.38  (NS)
  olaparib       diff +0.094, p = 0.003 (significant, small)
  Lu177-PSMA     diff -0.004, p = 0.87  (NS)
  pembrolizumab  diff +0.064, p = 0.14  (NS)

Only olaparib showed a significant overall effect, and as shown below this
was driven entirely by the BRCA2-mutated subgroup. None of the AR-targeted
therapies, taxane, radioligand, or checkpoint inhibitor showed a measurable
average effect on pfs_months across the whole cohort.

------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Treatment × biomarker interactions (the headline finding)
------------------------------------------------------------------

I tested the standard precision-medicine pairings as both OLS interactions
and stratified subgroup t-tests:

  *** treatment_olaparib × brca2_mutation: STRONGLY POSITIVE ***
       Interaction coef +1.619 mo, p ≈ 4e-61.
       - In BRCA2+ patients (n = 4,996): PFS 5.22 mo on olaparib (n = 529)
         vs 3.67 mo off (n = 4,467); diff +1.55 mo, p ≈ 4e-27.
       - In BRCA2- patients (n = 45,004): PFS 3.67 mo on olaparib (n = 4,569)
         vs 3.74 mo off (n = 40,435); diff -0.07 mo, p = 0.019.
       In a fully adjusted multivariable model the interaction estimate is
       +1.456 mo (p ≈ 0). This pattern — large positive PFS gain restricted
       to the biomarker-positive subgroup, modest *negative* signal in the
       biomarker-negative subgroup — is exactly the predicted
       synthetic-lethal pattern for olaparib in BRCA2-mutant disease.

  treatment_pembrolizumab × msi_high: NOT SIGNIFICANT.
       Interaction coef -0.34 mo, p = 0.15.
       In MSI-high patients (n = 79 on pembrolizumab vs n = 1,449 off),
       PFS was 3.53 vs 3.79 (diff -0.27, p = 0.24). No signal of MSI-driven
       benefit was detectable, though the on-treatment cell is small.

  treatment_lu177_psma × psma_high: NOT SIGNIFICANT.
       Interaction coef -0.017 mo, p = 0.74.
       In PSMA-high patients PFS was 3.74 on Lu177 vs 3.75 off (diff -0.01).
       The cohort does not show the expected radioligand benefit in
       PSMA-high disease.

  treatment_enzalutamide × ar_v7_positive and
  treatment_abiraterone × ar_v7_positive: NOT SIGNIFICANT.
       Both interactions p > 0.3. AR-V7 status did not modulate the
       effect of either AR-targeted agent in this dataset.

A composite biomarker-matched-therapy indicator (any of olaparib×BRCA2 OR
pembrolizumab×MSI-high OR Lu177×PSMA-high) was associated with a +0.14 mo
PFS gain (p = 7.8e-6, adjusted β = +0.14, p ≈ 3e-53), but this signal is
entirely driven by the olaparib×BRCA2 pair.

------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Other interactions explored
------------------------------------------------------------------

  - treatment × ECOG (six tests): no significant interactions.
  - treatment × visceral_mets: only treatment_olaparib × visceral_mets
    was nominally significant (-0.18 mo, p = 0.017); olaparib benefit
    is somewhat blunted in visceral disease.
  - treatment × mcrpc: only treatment_docetaxel × mcrpc was nominally
    significant (+0.080, p = 0.042) — i.e., a slight relative gain for
    docetaxel in castration-resistant disease.
  - treatment × tp53_mutation, treatment × pten_loss: all NS.
  - treatment × treatment combinations (enza+abi, doce+abi, doce+enza,
    olaparib+enza): all NS, no synergy/antagonism detected.
  - Lu177 × bone_mets and Lu177 × alk-phos: NS.

------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Comorbidities, demographics, SES, prior therapy
------------------------------------------------------------------

  - 14 comorbidities tested (DM, HTN, COPD, CKD, HF, CAD, AF, VTE,
    autoimmune, HBV, HCV, HIV, prior malignancy, depression/anxiety):
    NONE showed a significant association with pfs_months (all p > 0.05).
  - Symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite loss): all NS.
  - Demographics: race_ethnicity ANOVA p = 0.69; insurance_type ANOVA
    p = 0.79; rural_residence p = 0.50; education_years p = 0.40;
    smoking_pack_years p = 0.51. After adjustment for age and ECOG, no
    individual race or insurance category was significant.
  - Prior therapy indicators (chemo, radiation, surgery, immunotherapy,
    targeted) and prior_lines_of_therapy: all NS univariately. In the
    multivariable model prior_lines_of_therapy was also negligible.

------------------------------------------------------------------
6. SNP screening (25 markers)
------------------------------------------------------------------

A naïve Pearson screen of all 25 snp_ markers against pfs_months yielded
one marginal hit (snp_rs4986893, r = -0.011, p = 0.014) — well within the
expected false-positive rate for 25 simultaneous tests at α = 0.05. No SNP
remained convincing after multiple-testing correction.

------------------------------------------------------------------
7. Other molecular biomarkers (univariate)
------------------------------------------------------------------

  brca2_mutation  +0.10 mo, p = 0.002 (driven by olaparib subgroup)
  ar_v7_positive  -0.01 mo, p = 0.58
  msi_high        +0.04 mo, p = 0.52
  psma_high       +0.02 mo, p = 0.27
  tp53_mutation    0.00 mo, p = 0.87
  pten_loss       +0.02 mo, p = 0.62
  her2_amplification, braf_v600e: NS.

Without the matched targeted agent, none of these biomarkers carries
prognostic weight on its own.

------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Hypotheses supported vs refuted
------------------------------------------------------------------

SUPPORTED:
  - Age, ECOG, mCRPC, albumin, weight loss, PSA, LDH, alk-phos are
    independent predictors of PFS in the expected direction (except age,
    which is unexpectedly *positive*).
  - Olaparib provides large, biomarker-restricted PFS benefit in BRCA2-
    mutated patients (+1.5 months); this is the dominant precision-
    medicine signal in the cohort.
  - In BRCA2-wildtype patients, olaparib is slightly *worse* than no
    olaparib — consistent with off-target toxicity without the synthetic-
    lethal mechanism.
  - A composite "matched-therapy" indicator is positive after adjustment.

REFUTED / NOT DETECTED:
  - Pembrolizumab benefit in MSI-high disease.
  - Lu177-PSMA benefit in PSMA-high disease.
  - AR-V7 attenuation of enzalutamide or abiraterone benefit.
  - tp53 / pten loss as adverse modifiers of AR-targeted or chemo benefit.
  - Comorbidity, demographic, SES, smoking, education effects on PFS.
  - Symptom-grade effects on PFS.
  - SNP main effects (no marker survived multiple-testing thresholds).

------------------------------------------------------------------
9. Overall conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------------

In this 50,000-patient prostate cohort, PFS is governed primarily by a
small set of classical prognostic features (age, ECOG, mCRPC status,
albumin, weight loss, PSA, LDH, alkaline phosphatase) that together
explain ~92% of variance. The only treatment with a clinically meaningful
effect on PFS is olaparib, and that effect is concentrated entirely in
BRCA2-mutated patients (+1.5 months, p < 1e-26), confirming the
synthetic-lethal precision-medicine hypothesis. Other expected
treatment-biomarker pairings — pembrolizumab × MSI-high, Lu177-PSMA ×
PSMA-high, AR-V7 × AR-targeted therapy — were not borne out in this
dataset. Demographic, socioeconomic, comorbidity, symptom, and SNP
variables added essentially nothing once classical prognostics were
accounted for.
