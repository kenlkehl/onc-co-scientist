Analysis summary - ds001_prostate (n = 50,000)
===============================================

Outcome: pfs_months (mean 3.74, SD 2.02, range 0-14.6).
The cohort is uniformly male (sex_female = 0 in all 50,000 records),
consistent with prostate cancer.

I ran 25 iterations of propose -> test -> refine, generating 63
hypotheses and 63 statistical analyses.  Findings are organized below
into (1) the dominant pharmacogenomic signal, (2) prognostic main
effects, (3) negative findings on other targeted-therapy biomarker
axes, and (4) null variables.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. The dominant therapy signal: BRCA2 x olaparib synergy
------------------------------------------------------------

The single largest treatment-related effect in the dataset is a
biomarker-by-treatment interaction between brca2_mutation and
treatment_olaparib.

  - Univariate olaparib effect: mean PFS 3.84 vs 3.74 months
    (t-test p = 0.0033, +0.094 months overall).  This modest main
    effect was suspicious given the small olaparib-treated subgroup
    (n=5,098) and prompted an interaction test.

  - OLS pfs ~ brca2 + olap + brca2:olap returned an interaction
    coefficient of +1.619 months (p = 4.3e-61). Both single-term
    coefficients were ~0.

  - Stratified subgroups confirmed this:
        BRCA2+ x olap=1 (n=529):    mean PFS 5.22
        BRCA2+ x olap=0 (n=4,467):  mean PFS 3.67     diff +1.55, p = 4e-27
        BRCA2- x olap=1 (n=4,569):  mean PFS 3.67
        BRCA2- x olap=0 (n=40,435): mean PFS 3.74     diff -0.07, p = 0.019

    All of olaparib's apparent benefit lives in the BRCA2-mutated cell;
    in BRCA2-wildtype patients olaparib is null-to-slightly-harmful.

  - Robustness: the interaction beta is +1.439 (p << 1e-300) after
    adjusting for age, ECOG, mCRPC, visceral mets, albumin, weight loss
    and PSA in a multivariable model that explains 95% of PFS variance.

  - The interaction is essentially identical inside the mCRPC=1
    subgroup (BRCA2+ olaparib effect +1.55 months, p = 7.9e-16) and the
    non-mCRPC subgroup (+1.56, p = 2.4e-13). A 3-way
    BRCA2:olap:mCRPC term was effectively zero (beta +0.003, p = 0.99),
    so mCRPC modifies prognosis additively, not the synergy.

  - Even within frail (ECOG=2) patients olaparib still adds +0.15
    months on average (p = 0.044), a downstream signature of the
    BRCA2+ subset that is also present at ECOG=2.

This is the only therapy-related effect in the dataset that combines
clinical magnitude (>1 month) with overwhelming statistical support.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Prognostic main effects
------------------------------------------------------------

Variables strongly associated with pfs_months:
  - age_years            r = +0.856, p < 1e-300 (mean PFS rises from
                         0.85 mo in the youngest decile to 6.86 in the
                         oldest; direction is opposite to typical
                         clinical expectation and survives extensive
                         adjustment - likely a synthetic-data artifact
                         worth flagging rather than a biological pattern)
  - ecog_ps              r = -0.388, p < 1e-300 (multivariable beta
                         -1.15 per ECOG point)
  - mcrpc                t-test diff = -0.521 mo, p = 1.6e-181
                         (multivariable beta -0.38)
  - albumin_g_dl         r = +0.123, p = 1.5e-166 (adjusted beta +0.48)
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo  r = -0.143, p = 9.9e-226 (adjusted beta -0.075)
  - psa_ng_ml            r = -0.153, p = 3.5e-260
  - ldh_u_l              univariate r = -0.017 (p = 2e-4); after
                         adjustment for age/ECOG/mCRPC/albumin
                         beta = -5e-4 per U/L, p = 3.8e-51
  - gleason_score        univariate p = 0.49 (null), but adjusted
                         beta = -0.0084 per point, p = 0.0016
                         (very small)
  - visceral_mets        diff = -0.048 mo, p = 0.034 (small)

Variables that did not reach significance: liver_mets, bone_mets,
adrenal_mets, pleural_effusion, pericardial_effusion, hemoglobin_g_dl
(univariate r=-0.01, adjusted ns), crp_mg_l, nlr, alkaline_phosphatase,
prior_lines_of_therapy, pain_nrs, fatigue_grade, the composite symptom
burden, smoking_pack_years, education_years, and years_since_diagnosis.

A comprehensive multivariable OLS using age, ECOG, mCRPC, visceral mets,
PSA, albumin, weight loss, BRCA2, AR-V7, MSI, PSMA, six treatments and
the BRCA2:olap / MSI:pembro / PSMA:lu interactions reaches R^2 = 0.951.
Beyond the BRCA2:olap interaction the model is dominated by ECOG,
albumin, mCRPC, age, PSA, weight loss and LDH.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Other targeted-therapy biomarker pairs - all null
------------------------------------------------------------

The dataset contains the four canonical pharmacogenomic biomarker /
treatment pairings for prostate oncology.  Only one (BRCA2 / olaparib)
showed an interaction.

  - msi_high x treatment_pembrolizumab
        OLS interaction beta = -0.34, p = 0.15
        Adjusted (age/ECOG/mCRPC/albumin) interaction p = 0.87
        Within MSI-high (n=1,528): pembro diff = -0.27 mo, p = 0.24
        Subgroup is small (n=79 MSI-high on pembro) but the direction
        is opposite the hypothesis.

  - psma_high x treatment_lu177_psma
        OLS interaction beta = -0.017, p = 0.74
        PSMA-high (n=29,962): Lu177 diff = -0.011 mo (ns)
        PSMA-low  (n=20,038): Lu177 diff = +0.006 mo (ns)
        No differential benefit by PSMA expression.

  - ar_v7_positive x treatment_enzalutamide / treatment_abiraterone
        AR-V7:enza interaction beta = +0.002, p = 0.96
        AR-V7:abi  interaction beta = +0.048, p = 0.33
        Within AR-V7+ (n=10,038): enza diff = -0.024 (ns), abi diff
        = +0.032 (ns).  No resistance signal detected.

ECOG-by-treatment, visceral-mets-by-docetaxel, and bone-mets-by-Lu177
interactions were all non-significant.  An albumin-by-weight-loss
interaction (cachexia synergy hypothesis) was likewise null
(beta = -0.0004, p = 0.75) - the two cachexia markers act additively.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Variables with no detectable PFS signal
------------------------------------------------------------

  - Demographics / SDOH: race_ethnicity (black, hispanic, asian, other
    all p > 0.13 vs others), insurance_type (medicare, private,
    medicaid, uninsured all p > 0.3), rural_residence (p = 0.50),
    education_years (p = 0.40), smoking_pack_years (p = 0.51).
    No disparities in PFS were observed in this cohort.

  - Comorbidities: diabetes, hypertension, COPD, chronic kidney
    disease, heart failure, coronary artery disease, atrial
    fibrillation, autoimmune disease, VTE history, depression /
    anxiety - all p > 0.06.

  - Symptom burden: pain_nrs, fatigue_grade, dyspnea_grade,
    cough_grade, appetite_loss_grade individually and as a composite -
    all p > 0.65.

  - 28 catalogued SNPs (snp_rs*): minimum univariate p = 0.0145
    (snp_rs4986893), well above a Bonferroni threshold of 0.0018
    across 28 tests.  Effectively no signal.

  - years_since_diagnosis (r ~ 0), prior_lines_of_therapy (ns),
    sum of concurrent treatments (3.7-3.9 mo across counts 0-5).

------------------------------------------------------------
Overall conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------

This 50,000-patient prostate cancer cohort encodes one strong
pharmacogenomic interaction - BRCA2 mutation status modifies the
effect of olaparib by ~+1.6 months, with the entire benefit
concentrated in BRCA2-mutated patients.  All other treatment-biomarker
pairings tested (MSI / pembrolizumab, PSMA / Lu177-PSMA,
AR-V7 / ARSI, ECOG / various) are null.

PFS is highly explainable (R^2 ~ 0.95) from a small set of clinical
variables: age (positive direction here, an unusual pattern likely due
to synthetic data generation), ECOG, mCRPC status, albumin, weight
loss, PSA, and LDH.  Race, insurance, rurality, comorbidities,
symptom burden, and pharmacogenomic SNPs add no detectable
prognostic information in this cohort.

For prospective treatment decisions in a population that resembles
this dataset, the implication is unambiguous: olaparib should be
restricted to BRCA2-mutated patients - in everyone else it is null
or marginally harmful - and clinicians should not rely on MSI-high,
PSMA-high or AR-V7 status to select between the other available
agents based on this evidence base.
