====================================================================================================
Overall conclusions
====================================================================================================

1. Strong prognostic factors (replicate across simple and adjusted models):
   - ecog_ps: highly negative (OLS slope -1.16 per unit; PS0 ~4.7, PS1 ~3.5, PS2 ~2.4 months);
     persists at -1.15 in the comprehensive multivariable model. Largest single continuous driver.
   - mcrpc=1: PFS ~3.51 vs ~4.03 (diff -0.52 mo, p<1e-180 unadjusted; -0.37 mo adjusted, p~0).
   - albumin_g_dl: positive slope ~+0.49 per g/dL (p~1e-166).
   - weight_loss_pct_6mo: negative slope ~-0.075 per percentage point (p~1e-225).
   - age_years: UNEXPECTED direction. The OLS slope is +0.174 months per year of age, highly
     significant and reproduced in the comprehensive model. Older patients in this cohort have
     LONGER PFS, opposite to the usual clinical expectation. This likely reflects either
     selection in how this synthetic cohort was generated or a strong indication-bias pattern
     (younger patients getting more aggressive disease at presentation), and warrants caution.
   - psa_ng_ml: small negative slope (-0.003/unit, p~1e-260) consistent with higher tumor burden.

2. Weaker / null prognostic features (against expectation):
   - visceral_mets only marginally significant (-0.048 mo, p=0.035); bone_mets, liver_mets, and
     adrenal_mets were not significantly associated with PFS in this cohort.
   - hemoglobin_g_dl had a small NEGATIVE slope (-0.012 per g/dL, p=0.023) — opposite to the
     usual prognostic direction; effect is tiny and is best interpreted as noise rather than a
     true reversal of biology.
   - LDH and alkaline phosphatase were significant but with small slopes (-0.0004 and -0.0005
     per unit respectively).
   - CRP, NLR, calcium, gleason_score were not significantly associated with PFS.
   - Symptoms (pain_nrs, fatigue_grade, dyspnea_grade, appetite_loss_grade) were all NOT
     significantly associated with PFS — counter to our hypotheses.
   - Comorbidities (heart_failure, chronic_kidney_disease, COPD, diabetes_mellitus) were all NS.
   - Prior therapy variables (prior_lines_of_therapy, prior_chemotherapy, prior_radiation,
     years_since_diagnosis) were all NS.

3. Treatment main effects:
   - Only treatment_olaparib showed a significant unadjusted main effect (+0.094 mo, p=0.003)
     and an even stronger adjusted effect (+0.138 mo, p~2e-64). This signal is driven entirely
     by the BRCA2-mutated subgroup (see #4).
   - treatment_enzalutamide, treatment_abiraterone, treatment_docetaxel, treatment_lu177_psma,
     and treatment_pembrolizumab did not have significant main effects on PFS in this cohort.

4. Predictive (treatment-by-biomarker) interactions — the dominant finding:
   - treatment_olaparib x brca2_mutation: large, robust POSITIVE interaction. Stratified means:
     BRCA2-/no olaparib 3.74 -> BRCA2-/olaparib 3.67 (no benefit; p=0.019 in the slightly
     negative direction); BRCA2+/no olaparib 3.67 -> BRCA2+/olaparib 5.22 (+1.55 mo, p~4e-27).
     Interaction coefficient +1.62 (p~4e-61) in the simple OLS, persists at +1.44 (p~0) in
     the comprehensive multivariable model, and is reproduced within both mcrpc=1 (+1.63,
     p~6e-36) and mcrpc=0 (+1.63, p~1e-28) subgroups. This is the strongest predictive signal.
   - The other classical predictive pairings did NOT replicate:
     * treatment_pembrolizumab x msi_high: NS (point estimate even slightly negative).
     * treatment_lu177_psma x psma_high: NS.
     * treatment_enzalutamide x ar_v7_positive: NS.
     * treatment_abiraterone x ar_v7_positive: NS.
     * treatment_docetaxel x visceral_mets: NS.
   - ecog_ps x treatment_docetaxel and ecog_ps x treatment_lu177_psma interactions: NS.
   - ldh_u_l x visceral_mets and weight_loss_pct_6mo x mcrpc: NS.

5. SNP screen: across 25 snp_* variants, only 1 reached nominal p<0.05 (snp_rs4986893,
   diff -0.092 mo, p=0.015), close to the chance expectation of ~1.2 hits at alpha=0.05. No
   individually compelling pharmacogenomic signal in this cohort.

6. Mutational panel (tp53_mutation, pten_loss, pik3ca_mutation, cdkn2a_loss): none were
   significantly associated with PFS.

7. Demographics and access — no disparity signal:
   - Unadjusted ANOVA across race_ethnicity (F=0.56, p=0.69) and insurance_type (F=0.34, p=0.79)
     both NS. After adjusting for ecog_ps, mcrpc, visceral_mets, albumin, ldh, and age, joint
     F-tests for race_ethnicity (F=0.51, p=0.73) and insurance_type (F=1.02, p=0.38) remain NS.
   - rural_residence is NS unadjusted and only borderline (p=0.086) after adjustment.
   - education_years was NS.
   - This cohort does NOT show evidence of demographic disparities in PFS, before or after
     clinical adjustment.

8. Composite poor-prognosis index (high LDH + low albumin + weight loss >5% + ECOG>=1) was
   strongly negatively associated with PFS (slope -0.576/unit, p~0). Within the high-risk
   stratum (index>=3, n=14,264), most individual treatment effects attenuate, but olaparib
   still produces a small benefit (+0.137 mo, p=0.013), consistent with the BRCA2-driven signal
   surviving even in the most adverse phenotype.

9. Bottom line: the dominant clinically meaningful pattern is BRCA2 mutation as a strong
   predictor of olaparib benefit — the only confirmed treatment x biomarker interaction in
   this cohort. ECOG performance status is by far the strongest prognostic continuous covariate,
   mCRPC and weight loss/albumin reflect overall disease burden, and the comprehensive
   multivariable model achieves R^2=0.95, indicating that PFS in this dataset is largely
   captured by a small set of clinical covariates. Many "expected" associations (visceral
   metastasis, symptoms, comorbidities, demographic disparities, AR-V7 / MSI / PSMA predictive
   pairings) did not replicate, which itself is a noteworthy null finding for hypothesis
   generation in similar real-world cohorts.
