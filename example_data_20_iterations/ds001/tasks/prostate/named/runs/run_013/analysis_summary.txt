ds001_prostate -- Analysis Summary
==================================

Cohort: 50,000 patient records from a prostate-cancer-flavored dataset.
Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival, months).
Iterations: 25 (cap reached).

Approach
--------
We worked iteratively through (a) main effects of disease severity, lab
chemistries, treatments, and biomarkers; (b) treatment x biomarker
interactions corresponding to clinically motivated targeted-therapy
hypotheses (olaparib x BRCA2, pembrolizumab x MSI-high, Lu177-PSMA x
PSMA-high, enzalutamide / abiraterone x AR-V7); (c) demographics and
disparity probes; (d) a SNP screen; and (e) an integrated multivariable
OLS combining the strongest signals.

Iterations 1-6: Prognostic main effects
---------------------------------------
- ECOG performance status was the single strongest prognostic feature: a
  one-step increase reduced pfs_months by ~1.16 months (OLS coef -1.16,
  p<<1e-300, R^2 = 0.15 on its own).
- mCRPC status was associated with shorter PFS (3.51 vs 4.03 months,
  diff = -0.52, p ~ 1e-181).
- Visceral metastases gave a small but significant decrement (-0.05 mo,
  p = 0.034). Bone, liver, adrenal mets and metastatic-site count were
  *not* materially associated with PFS in unadjusted analyses, suggesting
  the dominant prognostic axis is performance status / mCRPC, not raw
  site count.
- Tumor markers: log(PSA) had a clear negative correlation with PFS
  (r = -0.23, p ~ 0). Raw PSA correlated similarly (r = -0.15). Gleason
  score showed no detectable linear association (p = 0.49).
- Lab chemistries: albumin was positively associated with PFS (r = 0.12,
  p ~ 1e-166). LDH, alkaline phosphatase, and hemoglobin were
  statistically detectable but minute in magnitude (|r| <= 0.02).
- Cachexia / inflammation: greater 6-month weight loss tracked with
  shorter PFS (r = -0.14, p ~ 1e-225). CRP and NLR were NOT associated
  with PFS in this dataset (p > 0.5 each), which is somewhat at odds
  with the broader oncology literature.
- Symptom burden (pain, fatigue, appetite loss) showed essentially zero
  association with PFS (all p > 0.65), again counter to clinical
  expectation that symptomatic burden tracks tumor burden.

Iteration 7: Treatment main effects (unadjusted)
------------------------------------------------
None of the six therapies was associated with longer PFS in marginal
two-sample tests, with one exception: treatment_olaparib (3.83 vs 3.74,
diff = +0.09, p = 0.003). The other five (enzalutamide, abiraterone,
docetaxel, Lu177-PSMA, pembrolizumab) had small, non-significant
differences. This is the expected pattern when therapies are
biomarker-targeted and biomarker prevalence is moderate -- the marginal
average dilutes the matched effect.

Iteration 8: Biomarker main effects
-----------------------------------
BRCA2 carriers had slightly longer PFS overall (3.84 vs 3.73, p = 0.002),
likely reflecting the olaparib effect concentrated in this subgroup.
AR-V7 positivity, MSI-high, and PSMA-high biomarkers had no detectable
PFS difference on their own (all p > 0.27).

Iterations 9-13: Targeted-therapy interactions (the central question)
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Olaparib x BRCA2 (PARP inhibitor x homologous-recombination defect):
  STRONG, ROBUST EFFECT.
  - In BRCA2 carriers, olaparib raised PFS from 3.67 to 5.22 months
    (diff = +1.55, p ~ 4e-27, n = ~5,000 carriers).
  - In BRCA2 wild-type, olaparib was NOT beneficial (diff = -0.07,
    p = 0.02 in the harmful direction).
  - The interaction term was coef = +1.62, p ~ 4e-61.
  - Adjusted for ECOG, mCRPC, visceral mets, log(PSA), LDH, albumin,
    age, the adjusted interaction = +1.45, p ~ 0.
  Conclusion: clear biomarker-driven benefit, magnitude ~1.5 months PFS.

Pembrolizumab x MSI-high: NOT supported.
  - In MSI-high (n = 1,528), pembrolizumab patients (n = 79) had a
    non-significant decrement in PFS (3.53 vs 3.79, diff = -0.27,
    p = 0.24).
  - In MSI-stable, pembro had a small non-significant uptick.
  - Interaction p = 0.15 (point estimate negative). Adjusted
    interaction p = 0.59.
  Conclusion: the canonical immunotherapy signature is *absent* in
  this cohort -- a clinically surprising negative finding.

Lu177-PSMA x PSMA-high: NOT supported.
  - Within PSMA-high (n = 29,962), Lu177-PSMA had no effect (3.74 vs
    3.75, diff = -0.01, p = 0.74).
  - Interaction p = 0.74. Adjusted interaction p = 0.51.
  Conclusion: no detectable biomarker-matched benefit for the
  radioligand therapy in this dataset.

Enzalutamide / abiraterone x AR-V7: NOT supported as resistance signal.
  - The classic clinical expectation that AR-V7 positivity predicts
    enzalutamide / abiraterone resistance was not realized.
  - All four interaction p-values were > 0.3 in both unadjusted and
    adjusted models. Within AR-V7+, enza had no effect (diff = -0.02,
    p = 0.56); within AR-V7-, likewise (diff = -0.03, p = 0.19).

Docetaxel x visceral mets: no interaction (p = 0.29). Within mCRPC,
docetaxel showed no PFS benefit (diff = +0.02, p = 0.43).

Iteration 14: Demographics
--------------------------
- Age was *positively* correlated with PFS (r = +0.86, p ~ 0). This is
  the dominant linear predictor in the dataset and is the opposite of
  the usual clinical pattern (older patients tend to do worse). The
  signal is so strong it implies the data were generated with age as a
  primary positive driver of PFS -- worth flagging to any reader.
- race_ethnicity NOT associated with PFS (ANOVA p = 0.69; group means
  3.74 - 3.80).
- insurance_type NOT associated with PFS (ANOVA p = 0.79).
- Rural residence NOT associated with PFS (p = 0.50).

Iterations 15-16: Comorbidities and prior therapy
-------------------------------------------------
None of CKD, heart failure, diabetes, prior malignancy, prior chemo,
prior lines of therapy, or years since diagnosis were significantly
associated with PFS (all p > 0.12). Either these are well-controlled
in the cohort or the data-generating process did not encode their
effects.

Iterations 17-19: Adjusted treatment effects
--------------------------------------------
Using OLS with ecog_ps + mcrpc + visceral_mets + log_psa + ldh +
albumin + age (R^2 ~ 0.95):
- Olaparib (main effect, in everyone) coef = +0.14, p ~ 1e-94, but
  this is dominated by BRCA2-positive subgroup -- with the interaction
  in the model, the main effect of olaparib in BRCA2 wild-type is
  small.
- Lu177-PSMA coef = -0.007, p = 0.20 -- no benefit on average.
- Pembrolizumab coef = +0.003, p = 0.79 -- no benefit on average.
- AR-V7 x enza and AR-V7 x abi interactions remained non-significant
  in adjusted models.

Iterations 20-21: Equity / access
---------------------------------
- Olaparib receipt among BRCA2 carriers was uniform across race
  (8.4-10.9%, p = 0.81) and insurance (10.4-11.0%, p = 0.98).
- Pembrolizumab receipt among MSI-high patients was similar across
  race (4.8-5.9%; the small 'other' bucket had zero recipients out of
  ~17 patients) and insurance.
- Lu177-PSMA receipt among PSMA-high patients was uniform across
  insurance (14.9-15.3%, p = 0.94).
- After prognostic adjustment, neither race_ethnicity nor
  insurance_type was associated with PFS (min p = 0.32 and 0.52).
Conclusion: no detectable disparity signal in either treatment access
or outcomes within this cohort.

Iteration 22: SNPs
------------------
We screened 25 SNP columns as continuous predictors of PFS in
univariate OLS. 4.0% (1/25) had p < 0.05, consistent with the 5%
nominal type-I rate -- the SNPs behave as null predictors.
snp_rs429358 (APOE) was borderline (p = 0.064); snp_rs1045642 (ABCB1)
was not (p = 0.28).

Iteration 23: Difference-in-differences magnitudes
--------------------------------------------------
- Olaparib x BRCA2: DiD = +1.62 months, interaction p ~ 4e-61. The
  only clinically meaningful biomarker-matched magnitude in the
  dataset, comfortably above the 1-month threshold.
- Pembrolizumab x MSI-high: DiD = -0.34 months, interaction p = 0.15
  (wrong direction).
- Lu177-PSMA x PSMA-high: DiD = -0.02 months, interaction p = 0.74.

Iteration 24: Biomarker-matched receipt synthesis
-------------------------------------------------
- Pooling matched recipients (olaparib+BRCA2+, pembro+MSI+, Lu177+PSMA+,
  n = 5,046) vs unmatched recipients (n = 9,297) gave 3.87 vs 3.72
  months (diff = +0.15, p ~ 4e-05). However this pooled effect is
  carried entirely by olaparib+BRCA2+ (5.22 vs 3.67, diff = +1.55,
  p ~ 3e-27); matched-vs-unmatched within pembro and within Lu177-PSMA
  showed no meaningful difference.

Iteration 25: Integrated model
------------------------------
A single OLS containing disease severity, all six treatments, the four
key biomarkers, and the four targeted interactions (R^2 = 0.95,
n = 50,000) confirms the picture:
- ecog_ps coef = -1.15, p ~ 0 (most negative effect).
- albumin_g_dl coef = +0.48, p ~ 0 (largest positive lab effect).
- treatment_olaparib x brca2_mutation coef = +1.45, p ~ 0
  (only biomarker-matched interaction that survives).
- treatment_pembrolizumab x msi_high coef = -0.02, p = 0.76 (null).
- treatment_lu177_psma x psma_high coef = -0.003, p = 0.81 (null).
- treatment_enzalutamide x ar_v7_positive coef = -0.0001, p = 0.99
  (null -- AR-V7 resistance signal absent).

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. The dominant prognostic axis in ds001_prostate is functional status
   (ECOG), disease state (mCRPC), bulk markers (log PSA), and
   nutritional status (albumin, weight loss). Age in this dataset is
   *positively* and very strongly correlated with PFS, an unusual /
   non-clinical pattern that should be acknowledged.
2. Among four biomarker-targeted hypotheses tested, only olaparib's
   benefit in BRCA2 carriers was supported -- convincingly: ~1.5-month
   PFS gain, robust to adjustment, with a clear absence of benefit
   (and possibly mild detriment) in BRCA2 wild-type. This recapitulates
   the real-world PARP-inhibitor / homologous-recombination biology.
3. Pembrolizumab in MSI-high, Lu177-PSMA in PSMA-high, and the AR-V7
   resistance pattern for enzalutamide / abiraterone -- all canonical
   clinical signals -- were ABSENT in this cohort. Notable because the
   dataset *contains* the matching biomarker columns yet does not
   encode the corresponding clinical effects.
4. There were no detectable disparities in PFS or in receipt of
   biomarker-matched therapy across race or insurance categories,
   after prognostic adjustment.
5. The 25 SNPs screened behave as null predictors of PFS, and the
   borderline rs429358 result is the kind of signal expected by chance
   in a screen of this size.
