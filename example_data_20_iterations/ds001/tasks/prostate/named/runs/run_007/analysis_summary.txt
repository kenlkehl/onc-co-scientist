ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_prostate (50,000 patients, prostate cancer cohort)
Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival, in months; cohort mean 3.74, SD 2.02)
Iterations completed: 25

================================================================
HEADLINE FINDING
================================================================
The single most striking — and statistically dominant — pattern in this
cohort is a robust *predictive* interaction between BRCA2 mutation status
and olaparib therapy:

  In brca2_mutation+ patients (n=4,996), olaparib increases mean PFS by
  +1.55 months (5.22 vs 3.67 months; p≈4e-27).

  In brca2_mutation- patients (n=45,004), olaparib produces a slight
  decrement of -0.07 months (3.67 vs 3.74; p=0.019) — i.e., no benefit
  and possibly minor harm.

  Formal interaction test (treatment_olaparib × brca2_mutation) on
  PFS: +1.62 months (p=4.3e-61). The interaction term remains highly
  significant after multivariable adjustment for ecog_ps, age_years,
  log(1+psa), albumin, ldh, mcrpc, visceral_mets, and weight_loss
  (interaction coefficient +1.43, p≈0; full model R²=0.975).

  This interaction is virtually identical in magnitude in mCRPC vs
  non-mCRPC strata (+1.63 vs +1.63 months; iteration 14, three-way
  interaction NS, iteration 24), consistent across race_ethnicity
  groups (white +1.61, black +1.67, hispanic +1.02, asian +1.54;
  iteration 20), across insurance_type strata (medicaid +1.89,
  medicare +1.41, private +1.63; iteration 21), and across ECOG strata
  (ECOG 0/1/2: +1.69 / +1.43 / +1.66; iteration 22). It is therefore
  the only treatment–biomarker pair in this dataset showing a clear
  predictive signal — and that signal is broad-spectrum, not driven
  by a particular subgroup.

================================================================
PROGNOSTIC MAIN EFFECTS ON PFS
================================================================
Iterations 3–5 evaluated univariable predictors. The features that
robustly track PFS in this cohort, in approximate order of magnitude
of effect, are:

  ecog_ps                (β = -1.16 per unit;     p < 1e-300, supported)
  age_years              (β = +0.174 per year;    p < 1e-300, supported but counterintuitive — see below)
  log(1+psa_ng_ml)       (β = -0.382 per log unit; p < 1e-300, supported)
  albumin_g_dl           (β = +0.495 per g/dL;    p ≈ 1e-166, supported)
  mcrpc (univariable)    (β = -0.521;             p ≈ 1e-182, supported)
  weight_loss_pct_6mo    (β = -0.075 per 1%;      p ≈ 1e-225, supported)
  ldh_u_l                (β = -0.0004 per U/L;    p ≈ 1.8e-04, supported)
  psa_ng_ml (raw)        (β = -0.0033 per ng/mL;  p ≈ 3e-260, supported)
  brca2_mutation         (β = +0.10 univariable;  p ≈ 9e-04, supported — driven by the olaparib-treated subset)
  visceral_mets          (β = -0.048;             p = 0.035, supported)
  alkaline_phosphatase   (β = -0.0005 per U/L;    p = 0.007, supported)
  total_bilirubin_mg_dl  (β = -0.062;             p = 0.032, supported)
  hemoglobin_g_dl        (β = -0.011 per g/dL;    p = 0.022, weakly supported but counterintuitive direction)

The composite-risk-score analysis (iteration 17) confirms the joint
prognostic strength of these features: patients with all five
high-risk features (ECOG≥1, albumin<3.5, LDH>250, weight loss>5%,
mCRPC) have mean PFS of 2.34 months (n=449), whereas patients with
all five favorable features have mean PFS of 5.58 months (n=561) —
a 3.2-month gap (p=2e-131).

================================================================
THE AGE PARADOX
================================================================
The most clinically surprising main effect is the strong POSITIVE
relationship between age and PFS. Mean PFS is 1.17 months for
patients <55, 2.89 for 55–65, 4.56 for 65–75, and 6.46 for ≥75
(monotonic, +0.17 months per year). The effect persists unchanged
after multivariable adjustment for ECOG, mCRPC, albumin, LDH, PSA,
and weight loss (β=+0.174, p<1e-300; iteration 16). This direction
is opposite to clinical expectations and likely reflects either
(a) selection effects in how this cohort was assembled (younger
patients selected for more aggressive disease or earlier-line trials)
or (b) the data-generating process of this particular synthetic /
de-identified dataset. The signal is real within the data but the
clinical interpretation is uncertain — the analyst would flag this
finding for source-data verification before acting on it.

================================================================
HYPOTHESES THAT WERE REFUTED
================================================================
A number of mechanistically plausible predictive interactions did
NOT replicate in this cohort:

  • PSMA-high × treatment_lu177_psma (p=0.74, then 0.87 in mCRPC):
    no enrichment of Lu177-PSMA benefit in PSMA-high patients.
  • MSI-high × treatment_pembrolizumab (p=0.15, then 0.72 in mCRPC):
    no enrichment of pembrolizumab benefit in MSI-high patients.
    Note MSI-high is rare (3.1%) and pembrolizumab use rare (4.8%),
    so the interaction sample is small (~80 doubly-positive).
  • AR-V7 × treatment_enzalutamide (p=0.96): no resistance signal.
  • AR-V7 × treatment_abiraterone (p=0.33): no resistance signal.
  • Visceral_mets × treatment_docetaxel (p=0.29): no enrichment.
  • TP53 × treatment_olaparib or × treatment_pembrolizumab: no
    modification (p=0.50, 0.63).
  • Bone_mets × treatment_lu177_psma (p=0.39): no enrichment.

Treatment main effects beyond olaparib were broadly null:

  • treatment_enzalutamide  (–0.026 mo, p=0.15)
  • treatment_abiraterone   (–0.006 mo, p=0.78)
  • treatment_docetaxel     (–0.017 mo, p=0.38)
  • treatment_lu177_psma    (–0.004 mo, p=0.87)
  • treatment_pembrolizumab (+0.064 mo, p=0.14)

A few small significant subgroup effects emerged (docetaxel slightly
worse in non-mCRPC, p=0.04; enzalutamide slightly worse in mCRPC,
p=0.05) but with effect sizes <0.06 months and consistent with chance
across many tests; we did NOT interpret these as substantive.

ECOG × treatment interactions for all six therapies were null
(iteration 11; smallest p=0.29). Performance status modifies overall
prognosis strongly but does not differentially modify any treatment.

Concomitant treatment in the BRCA2+ olaparib subgroup (iteration 25):
  • Adding docetaxel to olaparib in BRCA2+ patients: -0.63 months
    vs olaparib alone (p=0.027) — possible attenuation of olaparib
    benefit by chemotherapy.
  • Adding enzalutamide to olaparib in BRCA2+ patients: NS difference
    (p=0.63) — combination compatible.

================================================================
LAB MARKERS, COMORBIDITIES, AND DEMOGRAPHICS
================================================================
Routine laboratory markers beyond albumin/LDH/PSA and inflammatory
markers (CRP, NLR) were largely unrelated to PFS, and the few
nominally significant lab effects (alkaline phosphatase, bilirubin)
became NS after adjustment for ECOG and albumin (iteration 23).

No comorbidity (DM, HTN, CKD, HF, COPD, AF, VTE, autoimmune disease,
hepatitis, HIV, prior malignancy, ILD, or depression/anxiety) was
significantly associated with PFS at α=0.05 (depression/anxiety
borderline, p=0.059).

No demographic factor — race_ethnicity, insurance_type, or
rural_residence — showed a difference in PFS (race ANOVA p=0.69,
insurance ANOVA p=0.79, rural-vs-urban p=0.50). Within BRCA2+ olaparib
users, treatment benefit was also broadly preserved across race and
insurance strata, indicating no measurable disparity either in
baseline PFS or in olaparib responsiveness.

================================================================
GENETICS (SNP SCAN)
================================================================
Of 25 candidate SNPs (iteration 7), only snp_rs4986893 reached
unadjusted p<0.05 (β=-0.092, p=0.014); this does not survive
Bonferroni correction (α=0.002). No treatment × snp_rs4986893
interaction reached significance (iteration 15; smallest p=0.097
for docetaxel × rs4986893). We found no convincing pharmacogenomic
or prognostic SNP signal in this cohort.

================================================================
SYMPTOMS
================================================================
Patient-reported symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough,
appetite loss) showed no significant association with PFS, with or
without ECOG adjustment. The variance these symptoms explain is
fully captured by ECOG performance status.

================================================================
OVERALL CONCLUSION
================================================================
Across 25 iterations, this cohort is dominated by a small number of
strong prognostic factors (ECOG, age, albumin, log-PSA, weight loss,
LDH, mCRPC) and a single, very strong predictive treatment–biomarker
interaction: BRCA2 mutation × olaparib (~+1.5–1.6 months PFS,
p<1e-26). That interaction is robust to multivariable adjustment, is
of similar magnitude across mCRPC status, ECOG strata, race, and
insurance, and is the only signal among the candidate
biomarker-targeted pairings (PSMA-high × Lu177, MSI-high × pembro,
AR-V7 × ARSI) that actually replicates in this cohort. No other
treatment shows a convincing PFS effect either as a main effect or
in any tested subgroup. There is no evidence of demographic
disparity in PFS, no signal from comorbidities or candidate SNPs
beyond what would be expected by chance, and the symptom burden
captured in this dataset adds nothing to ECOG performance status as
a prognostic feature. The most clinically actionable summary is:
in this cohort olaparib should be used selectively in BRCA2-positive
patients, where its benefit is large and broadly generalizable, and
should not be expected to confer PFS benefit in BRCA2-negative
patients.
