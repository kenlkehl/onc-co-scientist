Analysis Summary — ds001_prostate (n = 50,000 patients)
========================================================

Outcome
-------
Progression-free survival, pfs_months: mean 3.74 mo (SD 2.02; range 0–14.6).
The cohort is entirely male (sex_female = 0 for all rows), consistent with
the prostate cancer label.

Across 25 iterations I probed (a) baseline prognostic factors, (b) marginal
treatment effects for the six systemic therapies, (c) treatment × biomarker
interactions corresponding to canonical precision-oncology pairings,
(d) sociodemographic factors, (e) somatic mutations and germline SNPs,
(f) comorbidity and symptom burden, and (g) multivariable confirmation. The
key signals are summarized below.

Headline findings
-----------------

1. Age is the dominant univariate predictor of PFS — and it points in the
   OPPOSITE direction from the conventional clinical expectation.
   Pearson r(age, PFS) = +0.856, p effectively 0. PFS rises monotonically
   from 1.35 months in the youngest age quintile (<56.5 y) to 6.24 months
   in the oldest (>73.5 y). A univariate OLS estimates +0.174 month of PFS
   per additional year of age. This was tested as h7 (the standard "older
   = worse" hypothesis was REFUTED) and the refined positive-direction
   hypothesis h8 was strongly supported.

2. ECOG performance status is a strong negative prognostic factor.
   Mean PFS is 4.68 / 3.49 / 2.38 mo for ECOG 0 / 1 / 2 respectively;
   univariate slope –1.16 mo per unit (p ≪ 1e-300). Adjusted slope is
   essentially identical (–1.15 mo per unit).

3. Other classical prognostic biomarkers behave as expected:
     - Higher serum albumin → longer PFS (β = +0.48 mo per g/dL adjusted).
     - Greater 6-month weight loss → shorter PFS (β = –0.077 per percentage
       point).
     - Higher log(PSA) → shorter PFS (β = –0.35 per log unit).
     - Higher LDH → modestly shorter PFS (β = –0.0005 per U/L).
     - Visceral metastases → mildly shorter PFS (–0.017 mo, p = 3e-5
       adjusted).

4. The univariate effect of mCRPC (–0.52 mo, p = 1.6e-181) is fully
   confounded by the prognostic factors above. After adjustment for age,
   ECOG, albumin, weight loss, log(PSA), LDH, and visceral mets, the
   mCRPC coefficient collapses to –0.002 mo (p = 0.66). h25 was supported.

Treatment effects
-----------------

Marginal (unadjusted) treatment effects on PFS were uniformly small:
    enzalutamide  Δ = –0.026 mo, p = 0.15
    abiraterone   Δ = –0.006 mo, p = 0.78
    docetaxel     Δ = –0.017 mo, p = 0.38
    olaparib      Δ = +0.094 mo, p = 0.0033 (significant)
    Lu-177 PSMA   Δ = –0.004 mo, p = 0.87
    pembrolizumab Δ = +0.064 mo, p = 0.14

5. The single robust drug effect is olaparib in BRCA2-mutant patients
   (the canonical PARP-inhibitor / homologous-recombination-deficiency
   pairing). Tests h15–h17:

     - Interaction olap × BRCA2 (univariate OLS): β = +1.62 mo,
       p = 4.3e-61.
     - BRCA2-mutant subgroup: olaparib raises PFS from 3.67 mo (n = 4,467,
       no olaparib) to 5.22 mo (n = 529, olaparib); difference +1.55 mo,
       p = 4.0e-27.
     - BRCA2 wild-type subgroup: olaparib does not help and is slightly
       harmful (Δ = –0.07 mo, p = 0.02), so the marginal positive olaparib
       effect is entirely driven by the BRCA2-mutant cell.
     - Interaction is unchanged by adjustment for age, ECOG, albumin,
       weight loss, log(PSA): adjusted β = +1.435 mo (h26 supported).
     - Interaction is also unchanged by mCRPC stratification: β = +1.63 mo
       in both mCRPC-yes and mCRPC-no subsets (h40 supported).

6. The other three precision-oncology pairings I tested were NOT supported:
     - Pembrolizumab × MSI-high (h18): interaction β = –0.34 mo,
       p = 0.15. PFS in MSI-high pembro+ cell is 3.53 mo (n = 79) — small
       and slightly lower than expected; underpowered, but no signal.
     - Lu-177 PSMA × PSMA-high (h19): interaction β = –0.017, p = 0.74.
       All four PSMA × treatment cells are within 0.02 mo of each other.
     - Enzalutamide × AR-V7 (h20) and abiraterone × AR-V7 (h21):
       interactions β = +0.0025 and +0.048 mo (p = 0.96 and 0.45).
       No detectable AR-V7-modifying effect of either AR-pathway inhibitor.

Sociodemographic, comorbid, and genomic null findings
-----------------------------------------------------

7. Race/ethnicity (h27) and insurance type (h28) show no association with
   PFS, either unadjusted (ANOVA p = 0.69 and 0.79) or after adjustment
   for age, ECOG, albumin, log(PSA): all coefficients ≤ 0.02 mo with
   p ≥ 0.24 (h31, h32).

8. Education years (h29; r = –0.004, p = 0.40) and rural residence
   (h30; Δ = +0.015 mo, p = 0.50) show no association.

9. Symptom grades (h33) — fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite-loss —
   each have |r| < 0.003 with PFS (all p > 0.65). No symptom-burden
   signal.

10. Common comorbidities (h34) — diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart
    failure — each have |Δ| < 0.04 mo and p > 0.18.

11. Common somatic alterations beyond BRCA2 (h35) — TP53, PTEN-loss,
    CDKN2A-loss, PIK3CA, HER2-amp, KEAP1, FGFR — each have |Δ| < 0.05 mo
    and p ≥ 0.10. The BRCA2 effect is therapy-conditional, not a
    free-standing prognostic effect (BRCA2 main effect = essentially 0).

12. SNP screen (h36): of 25 germline SNPs, the smallest ANOVA/Pearson
    p-value was 0.014 (snp_rs4986893), which does not survive a
    Bonferroni threshold of 0.05/25 = 0.002. No germline pharmacogenomic
    signal in this cohort.

13. Prior treatments (h37) — chemotherapy, radiation, surgery,
    immunotherapy, targeted therapy, prior_lines_of_therapy — all show
    |Δ| ≤ 0.030 mo and p ≥ 0.12.

Multivariable synthesis
-----------------------

14. A fully adjusted OLS that includes all prognostic factors plus all six
    treatments, BRCA2 status, and the olaparib × BRCA2 interaction (h39)
    isolates a single treatment-related driver of PFS. Adjusted treatment
    coefficients:
        enzalutamide  –0.006 (p = 0.05, borderline; magnitude trivial)
        abiraterone   –0.001 (p = 0.67)
        docetaxel     –0.003 (p = 0.28)
        olaparib      +0.0001 (p = 0.98)
        pembrolizumab +0.012 (p = 0.07)
        Lu-177 PSMA   –0.006 (p = 0.13)
        BRCA2 main    –0.001 (p = 0.92)
        olaparib × BRCA2  +1.435 (p ≪ 1e-300)

15. A small interaction between age and mCRPC was detected (h38):
    β = –0.0047 mo per year (p = 3.3e-7). Each year of age adds +0.177 mo
    of PFS in non-mCRPC and +0.172 mo in mCRPC. Statistically significant
    but tiny next to the age main effect (+0.174 mo/yr).

16. Model parsimony (h43): age alone explains R² = 0.732 of PFS variance.
    Adding ECOG, albumin, weight loss, log(PSA), and the olaparib × BRCA2
    interaction raises R² to 0.974 — i.e., nearly all PFS variance in this
    cohort is captured by ~6 features.

Conclusions
-----------

In this 50,000-patient prostate cancer cohort:

  • The PFS signal is dominated by demographic/laboratory prognostics
    (age, ECOG, albumin, weight loss, log(PSA)), with a paradoxically
    POSITIVE age effect that is large enough to merit comment as a
    dataset characteristic.

  • Among the six therapies, only olaparib carries a meaningful PFS
    benefit, and only in BRCA2-mutated patients (≈ +1.5 months).
    BRCA2 itself is not prognostic; its prognostic role only appears
    via the PARP-inhibitor interaction.

  • The other three canonical precision-oncology pairings — pembrolizumab
    × MSI-high, Lu-177 PSMA × PSMA-high, and (enzalutamide or abiraterone)
    × AR-V7 — show no statistical support in this dataset.

  • Race, insurance, education, rural residence, comorbidities, symptom
    grades, prior therapies, non-BRCA2 somatic alterations, and germline
    SNPs do not predict PFS in this cohort, either marginally or after
    confounder adjustment.

  • mCRPC's apparent univariate prognostic role is fully explained by
    correlated prognostic markers; conditioned on age, ECOG, albumin,
    weight loss, log(PSA), LDH, and visceral mets, it has essentially
    no residual association with PFS.
