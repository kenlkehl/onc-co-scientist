ds001_prostate — Analysis Summary
=================================

Cohort: 50,000 male patients (sex_female=0 for all) aggregated from EHR vendor
data. Outcome: pfs_months (mean 3.74, SD 2.02, range 0–14.6). Treatments tested
include enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, Lu-177-PSMA, and
pembrolizumab. Up to 25 iterations were planned; 20 substantive iterations were
executed and probed the dataset thoroughly across treatments, biomarkers,
clinical labs, comorbidities, social determinants, SNPs, and treatment-by-
biomarker interactions.

1. Prognostic baseline features (Iter 2, 3, 6, 15)
--------------------------------------------------
The strongest prognostic signals on PFS, all p<<1e-100 unless noted, were:
  • ecog_ps:                 β = -1.16 mo per ECOG unit (very strong negative)
  • mcrpc:                   −0.52 mo unadjusted, −0.38 mo adjusted (negative)
  • albumin_g_dl:            β = +0.49 mo per g/dL (strong positive)
    – Hypoalbuminemia (<3.5 g/dL) reduced PFS by 0.44 mo (p=3.2e-97)
  • weight_loss_pct_6mo:     β = -0.075 mo per % (strong negative)
  • psa_ng_ml:               β = -0.0033 mo per ng/mL
  • ldh_u_l:                 β = -0.00042 mo per U/L (negative)
  • alkaline_phosphatase:    small significant negative
  • visceral_mets:           −0.05 mo unadjusted, β = -0.023 mo adjusted (sig)
  • age_years:               β = +0.174 mo per year — POSITIVE, opposite of the
    a priori clinical expectation. This was tested in iter 3 and confirmed in
    the multivariable model (iter 6); the consistency across models suggests
    a structural feature of how the dataset was generated rather than a true
    biological age benefit.
  • gleason_score:           n.s. unadjusted; β = -0.011 in MV model.

Pain, fatigue, dyspnea, cough, and appetite-loss grades carried NO PFS
information once age and ECOG were already in the model (composite symptom
score β=+0.0008, p=0.56; iter 11). Comorbidities (DM, CKD, HF, COPD, HTN)
showed no significant univariate association with PFS (iter 8).

2. Treatment main effects (Iter 1, 9)
-------------------------------------
Unadjusted, only treatment_olaparib showed a significant main effect on PFS
(+0.094 mo, p=0.003). After adjusting for age, ECOG, mCRPC, visceral mets,
LDH, albumin, PSA, and Gleason, this remained: β = +0.138 mo (p=1.9e-64).
None of enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, Lu-177-PSMA, or pembrolizumab
had a significant adjusted main effect on PFS (all |β|<0.01, p>0.20). A
trend toward benefit for pembrolizumab in the full multivariable model
(β=+0.017, p=0.075; iter 6) was non-significant.

3. Treatment × biomarker interactions — predictive biomarker signals (Iter 4, 5, 16, 19)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This was the central scientific question. Five clinically motivated priors
were tested:

(a) treatment_olaparib × brca2_mutation: STRONGLY POSITIVE.
    Unadjusted interaction β = +1.62 mo, p = 4.3e-61.
    BRCA2-mut + olaparib subgroup mean PFS 5.22 mo vs BRCA2-mut without
    olaparib 3.67 mo (+1.55 mo, p=3.95e-27). In BRCA2-WT patients
    olaparib gave essentially no benefit (-0.07 mo). This signal survived
    adjustment for age, ECOG, mCRPC, albumin, LDH, PSA, weight loss
    (interaction β = +1.44, p effectively 0) and was reproduced in both the
    mCRPC subgroup (β = +1.63, p = 5.6e-36) and the non-mCRPC subgroup
    (β = +1.63, p = 1.3e-28). This is the single largest treatment-effect
    signal in the cohort and is the textbook PARP-inhibitor predictive
    biomarker relationship.

(b) treatment_lu177_psma × psma_high: NULL.
    Adjusted interaction β = -0.008, p = 0.55. PSMA-high status does NOT
    predict Lu-177-PSMA benefit in this cohort, contrary to the external
    biological prior.

(c) treatment_pembrolizumab × msi_high: NULL.
    Unadjusted interaction β = -0.34, p = 0.15 (trend in WRONG direction).
    Adjusted interaction β = +0.04, p = 0.53. MSI-high does NOT predict
    pembrolizumab benefit in this cohort, contrary to the external prior.

(d) treatment_enzalutamide × ar_v7_positive: NULL.
    Interaction β = +0.002, p = 0.96. AR-V7 does NOT confer enzalutamide
    resistance here (contradicting the biological prior).

(e) treatment_abiraterone × ar_v7_positive: NULL.
    Interaction β = +0.05, p = 0.33. Same null finding for abiraterone.

Other interaction tests (iter 13, 17, 19) were largely null. Within mCRPC,
any AR-targeting therapy was associated with very slightly SHORTER PFS
than no AR-targeting (-0.05 mo, p=0.032), most consistent with confounding
by indication (sicker patients more likely to receive these agents).
Olaparib × age interaction was non-significant. A small olaparib×docetaxel
combination interaction was nominally negative (β=-0.05, p=0.014); this is
consistent with confounding rather than a real combination effect.

4. Social determinants and demographics (Iter 7, 14)
----------------------------------------------------
No racial/ethnic group differed from white patients in mean PFS:
  • Black vs White:    diff -0.0005 mo, p=0.99
  • Hispanic vs White: diff -0.004 mo, p=0.89
  • Asian vs White:    diff +0.056 mo, p=0.14
  • Other vs White:    diff +0.008 mo, p=0.90
Insurance categories did not differ from private:
  • Medicaid:  diff +0.005, p=0.87
  • Uninsured: diff -0.010, p=0.83
  • Medicare:  diff +0.018, p=0.36
Rural residence: diff +0.015, p=0.50.

The benefit of olaparib (the strongest treatment effect) did not differ by
race (interaction with race_black p=0.53). A nominal olaparib × private-
insurance interaction (β = -0.044, p = 0.038) was small relative to the
+1.55-mo BRCA2-mut effect and likely chance after multiple testing.

5. Pharmacogenomic SNPs (Iter 10)
---------------------------------
Across 25 candidate SNPs, the smallest unadjusted p-value was 0.015 for
snp_rs4986893 (effect -0.092 mo). This does not survive Bonferroni
correction (α = 0.002 for 25 tests) and disappears entirely after adjusting
for age, ECOG, mCRPC, and albumin (β = +0.005, p = 0.68). No SNP shows a
robust association with PFS.

6. Multivariable model (Iter 6)
-------------------------------
A linear regression of PFS on all major clinical features, biomarkers,
treatments, and selected symptoms (n=27 covariates) achieved R² = 0.948.
Independent predictors (p<0.001) include ecog_ps, age_years, mcrpc,
visceral_mets, psa_ng_ml, gleason_score, brca2_mutation, psma_high,
albumin_g_dl, ldh_u_l, weight_loss_pct_6mo, and treatment_olaparib. None
of enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, or Lu-177-PSMA had independent
effects in the full model.

7. Subgroup robustness (Iter 12)
--------------------------------
Olaparib's PFS benefit was preserved in both ECOG 0–1 (+0.089 mo, p=0.009)
and ECOG ≥2 (+0.148 mo, p=0.044) patients, suggesting it is not driven
solely by fitter patients receiving it.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. The dominant signal in this cohort is the olaparib × BRCA2 mutation
   interaction. PARP-inhibitor therapy provides a clinically meaningful
   ~1.5-month PFS gain that is essentially confined to BRCA2-mutated
   patients. This matches the established biological prior for PARP
   inhibitors in homologous-recombination-deficient tumors.

2. Three other commonly cited predictive-biomarker hypotheses were NOT
   supported here: pembrolizumab×MSI-high, Lu-177-PSMA × PSMA-high, and
   enzalutamide/abiraterone × AR-V7 resistance. Either the synthetic data
   generator did not encode these effects, or, in a real-world cohort, the
   effects are too small to detect at n=50,000 against the strong
   prognostic signal of ECOG/mCRPC/albumin/age.

3. PFS is dominated by clinical performance status and disease burden:
   ECOG, mCRPC status, albumin, weight loss, PSA, LDH, and age together
   explain ~95% of the PFS variance (R² = 0.948). Most other features
   (symptoms, comorbidities, SDOH, SNPs) add little once these are
   accounted for.

4. There were no measurable racial, insurance, or rural disparities in PFS,
   and the olaparib benefit was equitable across racial groups in this
   cohort.

5. The age × PFS coefficient is positive (β ≈ +0.17 mo per year), which is
   the opposite of the typical clinical expectation. This is a feature
   worth noting for anyone modeling this dataset and may reflect how the
   underlying simulation/aggregation was constructed (e.g., older patients
   having inherently longer follow-up windows or being preferentially
   selected for inclusion).
