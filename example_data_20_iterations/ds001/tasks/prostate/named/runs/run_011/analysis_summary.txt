ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_prostate (n = 50,000; outcome = pfs_months)
=====================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
This cohort is entirely male (sex_female = 0 for all rows, consistent with
a prostate-cancer dataset). Mean PFS is 3.74 months (SD 2.02; range 0–14.6).
Six oncology treatments are recorded as binary indicators
(enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177_psma, pembrolizumab),
along with a battery of biomarkers, labs, comorbidities, prior therapies,
symptoms, demographics, and 25 SNP variants. We ran 25 iterations of
hypothesis–test–refine using OLS regression with continuous, indicator, and
interaction terms, plus stratified analyses where appropriate.

HEADLINE FINDING
----------------
The only large, robust treatment–biomarker interaction in the cohort is
treatment_olaparib × brca2_mutation on PFS.

  - Unadjusted OLS interaction coefficient ≈ +1.64 months (p ≈ 1.3e-79;
    iteration 6).
  - Fully adjusted model (controlling for ECOG, mCRPC, visceral mets, age,
    log PSA, log LDH, albumin, hemoglobin, log ALP, NLR, weight loss, log
    CRP, and the other five treatments): interaction coefficient = +1.43
    months (p < 1e-300; iteration 21). The olaparib main effect collapses
    to ≈ 0 (β = -0.0001, p = 0.98) and the BRCA2 main effect also collapses
    to ≈ 0 (β = +0.0003, p = 0.96), meaning the entire olaparib effect lives
    in the BRCA2-positive subgroup and the entire BRCA2 effect lives in the
    olaparib-treated subgroup — exactly the synthetic-lethality pattern.
  - Stratified means (iteration 6):
        BRCA2+ / olaparib+: PFS = 5.22 mo (n=529)
        BRCA2+ / olaparib−: PFS = 3.67 mo (n=4,467)  → +1.55 mo gain
        BRCA2− / olaparib+: PFS = 3.67 mo (n=4,567)
        BRCA2− / olaparib−: PFS = 3.74 mo (n=40,437) → essentially no gain
  - The interaction is preserved in the BRCA2+ subgroup whether patients are
    mCRPC or hormone-sensitive (olaparib main β = +1.68 in BRCA2+ non-mCRPC,
    interaction with mCRPC = -0.18, ns; iteration 16).
  - The interaction holds in both low- and high-risk prognostic-index
    tertiles (β = +1.51 and +1.46 respectively, both p≈0; iteration 24).

OTHER TREATMENT–BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS — MOSTLY NULL
----------------------------------------------------
Despite biological plausibility, the following expected interactions were
NOT supported (p > 0.05 in adjusted models):

  - treatment_pembrolizumab × msi_high: β = -0.24 (p = 0.27); stratified
    means actually trend in the wrong direction (MSI-high pembrolizumab+
    PFS = 3.53 vs MSI-high pembrolizumab− = 3.79; iteration 7).
  - treatment_lu177_psma × psma_high: β = -0.04 (p = 0.42); essentially no
    differential benefit (iteration 8).
  - treatment_enzalutamide × ar_v7_positive: β = +0.001 (p = 0.97);
    treatment_abiraterone × ar_v7_positive: β = +0.027 (p = 0.54). AR-V7
    confers no measurable resistance to either AR-axis agent here
    (iteration 9; replicated in iteration 23).
  - Other interactions tested (iteration 22–23): docetaxel × visceral mets,
    abiraterone × mCRPC, pembrolizumab × tp53, olaparib × tp53,
    enzalutamide × tp53, lu177 × bone mets, docetaxel × bone mets — all
    null. One borderline positive: olaparib × pten_loss β = +0.26
    (p = 0.029; iteration 22) — likely chance among many tests.
  - pembrolizumab × brca2_mutation came in at β = +0.092 (p = 0.0036;
    iteration 23) but the magnitude is ~15× smaller than the olaparib
    interaction and likely reflects multiple-testing noise.

TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS
----------------------
With all six treatments mutually adjusted (iteration 4), only olaparib
showed a small but significant positive main effect (β = +0.094 mo,
p = 0.0017) — driven entirely by the BRCA2-positive subgroup as shown above.
Enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, lu177_psma, and pembrolizumab had no
detectable main effect on PFS in this cohort. In the comprehensive model
(iteration 25) the main effect of every treatment is ≈ 0 once interactions
and prognostic factors are included.

CORE PROGNOSTIC FACTORS (negative coefficients = shorter PFS)
-------------------------------------------------------------
Strong, replicable prognostic signals (iterations 1, 2, 12, 25):

  - ecog_ps:                  β ≈ -1.15 mo per unit (p ≈ 0)  — largest single
                               contributor.
  - mcrpc:                    β ≈ -0.52 mo (p ≈ 0) in unadjusted model;
                               attenuates to ≈ 0 once labs are added (because
                               labs reflect the mCRPC state).
  - log_psa:                  β ≈ -0.38 mo per log unit (p ≈ 0).
  - log_ldh:                  β ≈ -0.08 to -0.10 mo per log unit (p < 1e-100).
  - albumin_g_dl:             β ≈ +0.48 mo per g/dL (p ≈ 0)  — strongest
                               positive prognostic factor.
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo:      β ≈ -0.075 mo per percent (p ≈ 1.7e-225).
  - alkaline_phosphatase_u_l: β ≈ -0.0005 mo per U/L (p = 0.011).
  - visceral_mets:            β ≈ -0.02 mo (p = 0.004) — small effect.

NOTABLE NEGATIVES (unexpectedly null)
-------------------------------------
  - Patient-reported symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough,
    appetite loss): all p > 0.3 once ECOG is in the model (iteration 11).
    Symptom burden in this cohort carries no incremental prognostic
    information beyond performance status.
  - Inflammation markers NLR and log(CRP): no association (p > 0.7;
    iteration 12), even though weight_loss in the same model is highly
    significant.
  - Comorbidities (DM, HTN, COPD, CKD, CHF, CAD, AF, autoimmune disease,
    prior malignancy): all p > 0.1 (iteration 13).
  - Prior therapy variables (chemo, RT, surgery, targeted, lines of therapy,
    years since dx): all p > 0.1 (iteration 14).
  - Demographics: race_ethnicity joint F-test p = 0.58, education_years
    p = 0.75, rural_residence p = 0.066 (iteration 17). No socio-demographic
    inequity in PFS.
  - SNPs (10 surveyed univariably, iteration 18): only snp_rs4986893
    nominally p = 0.014, expected by chance among 10 tests.
  - Site-of-disease beyond visceral_mets (liver_mets, bone_mets,
    adrenal_mets, pleural_effusion): all p > 0.08 (iteration 3).
  - Cross-treatment interactions (enza×abi, doc×abi, olap×lu177): all
    null (iteration 19).
  - Chemistry labs (Na, K, Ca, Cr, platelets, WBC): all small or null
    (iteration 20). Total bilirubin (p = 0.034) and glucose (p = 0.034)
    are nominal but tiny in magnitude.

AGE — A SUPPRESSION EFFECT
--------------------------
age_years has a positive coefficient (β ≈ +0.17 mo/year, p ≈ 0) both
univariably and adjusted (iteration 10). Pearson correlations between age
and ECOG (-0.004) and age and albumin (+0.003) are essentially zero, so
this is not classical confounding by other prognostic factors. The most
likely explanation is that the data-generating process associates older
age with longer PFS within this cohort (e.g., older patients selected for
less-aggressive disease), rather than age driving worse outcomes.

CONCLUSIONS
-----------
1. Treatment selection in this cohort is largely uninformative for PFS
   except for the olaparib–BRCA2 axis. The targeted-therapy biology that
   typically drives precision-oncology trials (PARP inhibition for BRCA2)
   is faithfully reproduced; the other targeted-therapy hypotheses (PD-1
   blockade for MSI-high, PSMA-radioligand for PSMA-high, AR-V7 resistance
   to enzalutamide/abiraterone) are NOT reproduced.
2. The prognostic spine is dominated by ECOG performance status, serum
   albumin, log PSA, log LDH, weight loss, ALP, and mCRPC status — the same
   handful of variables that drive most clinical-trial nomograms.
3. Demographic, comorbidity, symptom, inflammatory, and germline-SNP
   variables provide essentially no incremental prognostic information,
   suggesting they are noise relative to the disease-burden / metabolic
   axis.
4. The olaparib × BRCA2 interaction (~+1.5 months PFS gain restricted to
   BRCA2-mutant patients) is the single actionable treatment-selection
   signal in the dataset, robust to extensive multivariable adjustment and
   to risk-tertile stratification.
