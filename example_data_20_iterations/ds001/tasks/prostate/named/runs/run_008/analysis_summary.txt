Analysis Summary — ds001_prostate (50,000 patients)
====================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival, mean 3.74, SD 2.02, range 0–14.6).
The cohort is uniformly male (sex_female=0 for all 50,000 patients), consistent with prostate cancer.

Across 25 iterations I proposed 53 hypotheses spanning disease severity, laboratory
markers, treatments, treatment x biomarker interactions, comorbidities, metastasis
sites, symptom burden, demographics, prior therapies, vital signs, and a 25-locus
SNP screen. A final multivariable OLS model, fit at the end, achieved R^2 = 0.975
on PFS — essentially all explainable variance is accounted for by a small set of
features. The narrative below walks through which hypotheses were supported,
which were refuted, and the magnitudes/directions of the underlying effects.

1. Disease severity and demographics (iters 1–2)
------------------------------------------------
- ECOG performance status (`ecog_ps`) was the single strongest predictor:
  beta = -1.16 months per ECOG point (p ~ 0). SUPPORTED.
- mCRPC (`mcrpc`=1) showed a large univariate negative effect (-0.52 months,
  p=9.8e-182) but ATTENUATED to non-significance after adjustment for ECOG, log
  PSA, and other markers (adjusted beta=+0.0007, p=0.85). Refuted as an
  independent predictor.
- Visceral metastases gave a small, significant negative univariate effect
  (-0.05 months, p=0.035) and a small, robust negative effect after adjustment
  (beta=-0.016, p<1e-4). SUPPORTED but the magnitude is modest.
- log(1+PSA) (`log_psa`) was the second-strongest continuous predictor:
  beta=-0.38 per unit (p ~ 0). SUPPORTED.
- Surprisingly, age_years (`age_years`) was POSITIVELY associated with PFS
  (+0.174 months per year, p ~ 0). The direction is opposite to the standard
  oncology prior; the original "older = worse PFS" hypothesis is REFUTED but
  the relationship itself is real and very strong (and reproducible after
  multivariable adjustment, where the same +0.174 estimate persists).
- Gleason score had no detectable association (p=0.49). REFUTED.

2. Laboratory and clinical markers (iters 3–4, 16–17, 24)
---------------------------------------------------------
- Albumin (`albumin_g_dl`) was strongly favorable (+0.50 per g/dL, p=1.5e-166).
  SUPPORTED.
- LDH (`ldh_u_l`) was unfavorable, with a small per-unit beta (-0.0004, p=2e-4)
  that retained significance after multivariable adjustment (-0.0005, p<1e-50).
  SUPPORTED.
- Weight loss in the prior 6 months (`weight_loss_pct_6mo`) was strongly
  unfavorable (-0.075 per percentage point, p=9.9e-226; adjusted beta=-0.077,
  p<1e-100). SUPPORTED.
- Alkaline phosphatase had a small significant univariate negative effect
  (p=0.007) that DISAPPEARED after multivariable adjustment (p=0.27). The
  univariate signal is confounded by other covariates.
- Hemoglobin showed a tiny, marginal univariate negative slope (p=0.022) —
  direction OPPOSITE to standard expectation — and the effect vanished entirely
  after adjustment (p=0.92). The hypothesis that higher Hb improves PFS was
  REFUTED.
- CBC components (platelets, WBC, ANC, ALC), CRP, NLR, kidney function (BUN,
  creatinine), liver enzymes (AST, ALT), TSH, INR, electrolytes, calcium, BMI,
  and most vital signs were ALL non-significant. Only total bilirubin
  (-0.062, p=0.032) and glucose (+0.0007, p=0.032) showed nominal effects that
  do not survive multiple-testing correction. The hypotheses that these markers
  carry independent prognostic information were REFUTED.

3. Treatment main effects (iter 5)
----------------------------------
None of the six therapy main effects was clinically meaningful. Olaparib showed
a small significant overall benefit (+0.094 months, p=0.0016), but iteration 6
revealed this is entirely driven by BRCA2-mutated patients. All other agents —
enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, lu177_psma, and pembrolizumab — had
non-significant main effects on PFS (|diff| < 0.07 months, all p > 0.13).
Hypotheses that any of these treatments improves PFS overall were REFUTED.

4. Treatment x biomarker interactions (iters 6–9, 21)
-----------------------------------------------------
The single most striking finding in the dataset:

- *treatment_olaparib x brca2_mutation*: interaction beta = +1.62
  (p = 4.3e-61). Stratified analysis: in BRCA2-mutated patients (n=4,996),
  olaparib increased PFS by +1.55 months (p=9e-54); in BRCA2-wildtype patients
  (n=45,004), olaparib actually shortened PFS by 0.07 months (p=0.02). This is
  a textbook synthetic-lethality pattern. STRONGLY SUPPORTED. After
  multivariable adjustment for age, ECOG, log PSA, albumin, LDH, weight loss,
  and visceral mets, the interaction term remains beta=+1.43, p<1e-300.

The other expected biomarker-driven interactions were all REFUTED:
- pembrolizumab x msi_high: interaction beta=-0.34, p=0.15 (sign opposite,
  underpowered with only 79 MSI-high patients receiving pembrolizumab).
- lu177_psma x psma_high: interaction beta=-0.017, p=0.74.
- enzalutamide x ar_v7_positive: interaction beta=+0.0025, p=0.96.
- abiraterone x ar_v7_positive: interaction beta=+0.048, p=0.33.
- docetaxel x tp53_mutation: interaction beta=+0.046, p=0.25.
- docetaxel x visceral_mets: interaction beta=+0.052, p=0.29.
- lu177_psma x bone_mets: interaction beta=-0.054, p=0.39.

Treatment-by-ECOG interactions were also uniformly null across all six therapies
(iter 20). The dataset behaves as if olaparib + BRCA2 is the only "true"
biomarker-matched treatment relationship.

5. Other molecular drivers and SNPs (iters 9, 14, 15)
-----------------------------------------------------
- TP53 mutation, PTEN loss, HER2 amplification, MET ex14 skipping, RET/ROS1/
  NTRK/NRG1 fusions, BRAF V600E, FGFR alterations, CDKN2A loss, KEAP1,
  PIK3CA: ALL non-significant (all p > 0.10). REFUTED.
- 25-locus SNP screen: 1 of 25 nominally significant at p<0.05
  (snp_rs4986893, p=0.0145), exactly at the chance expectation of 1.25.
  Consistent with a global null for SNP main effects. REFUTED.

6. Comorbidities, mets sites, symptoms, demographics, prior therapy (iters 10–13, 22, 23)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- 14 chronic comorbidity flags: ALL non-significant. REFUTED.
- Bone, liver, adrenal, pleural, pericardial, contralateral mets (beyond the
  visceral_mets aggregate): ALL non-significant. REFUTED.
- Patient-reported symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite
  loss): ALL non-significant. The only patient-reported variable that mattered
  was the objective `weight_loss_pct_6mo`. REFUTED for grades.
- Race/ethnicity (4 contrasts vs white): ALL p > 0.14. No disparities in PFS
  detectable. REFUTED.
- Insurance type, rural residence, smoking pack-years, education years: ALL
  non-significant. REFUTED.
- Prior chemotherapy, radiation, surgery, immunotherapy, targeted therapy,
  prior lines, years since diagnosis: ALL non-significant. REFUTED.
- Depression/anxiety: borderline (p=0.059); HIV: NS.

7. Final multivariable model (iters 18–19, 25)
----------------------------------------------
A multivariable OLS with eight predictors — age_years, ecog_ps, log(1+PSA),
albumin_g_dl, ldh_u_l, weight_loss_pct_6mo, visceral_mets, and the
treatment_olaparib x brca2_mutation interaction — achieves R^2 = 0.975.
mCRPC, ALP, and hemoglobin (significant univariately) drop out. Adding any
of the other 100+ variables to this model does not meaningfully improve fit.

Adjusted coefficients (all p < 1e-4):
  age_years              = +0.174 months/year
  ecog_ps                = -1.151 months/point  (largest negative effect)
  log(1+PSA)             = -0.383 months/log unit
  albumin_g_dl           = +0.478 months/(g/dL)
  ldh_u_l                = -0.00052 months/(U/L)
  weight_loss_pct_6mo    = -0.077 months/%
  visceral_mets          = -0.015 months
  olaparib x BRCA2       = +1.433 months  (in BRCA2-mutated, olaparib-treated)
  treatment_olaparib (main) ~ 0
  brca2_mutation (main)     ~ 0

Bottom line
-----------
PFS in this cohort is essentially deterministic in the eight features above,
plus the olaparib x BRCA2 synthetic-lethality interaction. The dataset
recapitulates one well-established biomarker-matched signal (PARP-inhibitor
benefit in BRCA2-mutated prostate cancer) and one large set of standard
prognostic markers (ECOG, PSA, albumin, LDH, weight loss, visceral mets, age),
while every other treatment, biomarker, comorbidity, demographic, and symptom
feature tested fails to show an independent effect. The negative findings are
particularly notable for the AR-V7/AR-targeted-therapy axis, the
PSMA/Lu-177-PSMA axis, and the MSI/pembrolizumab axis — none of these
reproduced expected biology in this cohort, despite ample statistical power for
the first two (n thousands per cell).
