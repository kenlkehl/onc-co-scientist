Analysis summary — ds001_prostate (50,000 patients, outcome: pfs_months)
=======================================================================

Across 25 iterations we explored prognostic main effects, sociodemographic
disparities, genomic alterations, prior-therapy effects, treatment main
effects, and treatment-by-biomarker interactions — fitting OLS regressions
and t-tests against pfs_months. The cohort is exclusively male
(sex_female=0 throughout) with a mean PFS of 3.74 months (SD 2.02).

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Strongest prognostic signals (iters 1, 2, 15, 23)
------------------------------------------------------------
Six clinical/lab features had large, independent effects on PFS that
survived multivariable adjustment:

  - ecog_ps              beta = -1.150 months per unit  (p ~ 0)
                         Mean PFS: ECOG 0 = 4.68; ECOG 1 = 3.49; ECOG 2 = 2.38.
  - age_years            beta = +0.174 months per year  (p ~ 0) [DIRECTION
                         OPPOSITE TO CLINICAL EXPECTATION — see section 4]
  - albumin_g_dl         beta = +0.478 months per g/dL  (p ~ 0)
  - log(1 + psa_ng_ml)   beta = -0.383 months per log-unit  (p ~ 0)
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo  beta = -0.077 months per percentage point  (p ~ 0)
  - ldh_u_l              beta = -0.00050 months per U/L  (p ~ 0)

Smaller but still significant after adjustment:

  - visceral_mets        beta = -0.016 months  (p < 0.001)

Variables that lost significance after multivariable adjustment, despite
strong univariate signals:

  - mcrpc                Univariate diff -0.52 months (p=1e-181) but
                         essentially zero after adjusting for ECOG, age,
                         albumin, PSA, weight loss (p=0.85).
  - alkaline_phosphatase Univariate p=0.007, adjusted p=0.27.
  - hemoglobin_g_dl      Univariate p=0.02 (and direction was negative, the
                         opposite of the prediction); adjusted p=0.92.
  - bone_mets, liver_mets  Both null in univariate AND multivariable.

Variables NOT associated with PFS at all (univariate):
  - gleason_score (p=0.49)
  - crp_mg_l, nlr (both p>0.5)
  - all five symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite —
    all p>0.6)

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Treatment main effects (iter 3)
------------------------------------------------------------
With six treatment indicators on a flat outcome of mean ~3.74 months,
unadjusted effects were small:

  - treatment_enzalutamide  diff = -0.026  p=0.15  (NS)
  - treatment_abiraterone   diff = -0.006  p=0.78  (NS)
  - treatment_docetaxel     diff = -0.017  p=0.38  (NS)
  - treatment_olaparib      diff = +0.094  p=0.0016  (driven entirely by
                                                     BRCA2-mutated subgroup)
  - treatment_lu177_psma    diff = -0.004  p=0.87  (NS)
  - treatment_pembrolizumab diff = +0.064  p=0.13  (NS)

Number of concurrent treatments per patient (n_tx 0–6) was unrelated to
PFS (slope ~0, p=0.99).

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Treatment × biomarker interactions
------------------------------------------------------------
The single dominant predictive-biomarker signature in the dataset is:

  BRCA2 × OLAPARIB  (iters 4, 16, 24, 25)

Stratified means (months PFS):
                    olaparib=0     olaparib=1
  brca2_mutation=0  3.74 (n=40435) 3.67 (n=4569)
  brca2_mutation=1  3.67 (n=4467)  5.22 (n=529)

  Interaction coefficient: +1.62 months (p = 4e-61, unadjusted; p ~ 0
  after multivariable adjustment for age, ECOG, albumin, log_PSA,
  weight_loss). The interaction is strikingly stable across subgroups —
  ECOG 0/1/2 and mCRPC 0/1 — at +1.5 to +1.7 in every stratum. With
  covariates included, BRCA2 main effect and olaparib main effect are
  individually null (p=0.89 each); the entire treatment signal is in
  the interaction. This is exactly the synthetic-lethality pattern
  expected for PARP inhibition in HR-deficient tumors.

Other interactions tested:

  - msi_high × pembrolizumab  (iter 5): coef = -0.34, p = 0.15. Null;
    direction is actually opposite to the on-label prediction, and the
    on-treatment cell has only 79 MSI-high patients.
  - psma_high × treatment_lu177_psma (iter 6): coef = -0.017, p = 0.74.
    No predictive effect detected, despite this being the on-label
    biomarker.
  - ar_v7_positive × enzalutamide (iter 7): coef +0.0025, p = 0.96.
    No resistance pattern.
  - ar_v7_positive × abiraterone (iter 7): coef +0.048, p = 0.33.
    No resistance pattern.
  - mcrpc × treatment_docetaxel (iter 17): coef +0.080, p = 0.04 —
    nominally significant, would not survive multiple-testing correction.
  - pten_loss × olaparib  (iter 19): coef +0.30, p = 0.025 — nominal.
  - psma_high × olaparib  (iter 19): coef +0.17, p = 0.005 — nominal.
  None of these has anything close to the magnitude of BRCA2 × olaparib
  and they likely reflect chance findings among the many interactions
  evaluated. ECOG × treatment, age × treatment, and visceral_mets ×
  treatment interactions were all null.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. The unexpected positive age–PFS association (iters 1, 21, 23)
------------------------------------------------------------
A clinically counterintuitive finding: PFS rises monotonically with age:

  Age quartile      Range          n      mean PFS (months)
  Q1                30 – 58.3      12650  1.56
  Q2                58.4 – 65.0    12493  3.13
  Q3                65.1 – 71.8    12440  4.33
  Q4                71.9 – 90.0    12417  6.00

  Per-year slope +0.174 months (p ~ 0), unchanged after adjusting for
  ECOG, albumin, log_PSA, weight loss, LDH, and visceral mets. This is
  not driven by older patients receiving better-targeted therapy: age ×
  treatment interactions for enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, and
  olaparib are all non-significant (|coef|<=0.0013, p>=0.20), so the
  association is robust across treatment groups. In real-world prostate-
  cancer cohorts, older age is usually neutral or slightly adverse for
  PFS; here it is the second-largest single predictor after ECOG.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. What was NOT associated with PFS
------------------------------------------------------------
The following all gave null results, in some cases despite large patient
numbers:

  - Race/ethnicity (ANOVA p=0.69), insurance type (p=0.79), rural
    residence (p=0.50), education years (p=0.40), smoking pack-years
    (p=0.51) — no measurable disparity in PFS by sociodemographic
    category in this cohort.
  - All 15 comorbidity flags individually: minimum p = 0.06 (depression/
    anxiety, -0.045 months); none significant at alpha=0.05.
  - Metastatic site flags (bone, liver, adrenal, pleural effusion,
    pericardial effusion, contralateral lung): none significant; only
    visceral_mets reached significance and at small magnitude
    (-0.05 months unadjusted, -0.02 adjusted).
  - All 13 oncogenic / co-mutation flags (her2_amplification,
    met_exon14_skipping, ret_fusion, ros1_fusion, braf_v600e,
    ntrk_fusion, nrg1_fusion, fgfr_alteration, cdkn2a_loss,
    tp53_mutation, keap1_mutation, pik3ca_mutation, pten_loss):
    none significant; smallest p was cdkn2a_loss (p=0.10). Notably
    tp53_mutation, present in 45% of patients, has effect = +0.003 (p=0.87).
  - All five prior-therapy flags + prior_lines_of_therapy + years_since_
    diagnosis: all null.
  - 25 SNP flags: only snp_rs4986893 (p=0.014) reached nominal p<0.05;
    none survives a Bonferroni threshold (~0.002). Consistent with no
    true germline associations.
  - All five patient-reported symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea,
    cough, appetite loss): all p>0.65.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. Overall conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------
1. The prognostic signal is concentrated in a small set of standard
   clinical/lab markers — ECOG performance status, serum albumin,
   log-PSA, 6-month weight loss, and LDH — plus a strong (and
   counter-clinical) positive age effect. Together these explain most
   of the systematic variance in PFS; comorbidities, somatic co-mutations
   beyond BRCA2, prior therapies, sociodemographics, and SNPs are
   essentially noise in this cohort.

2. The treatment-related signal is dominated by a single
   predictive-biomarker interaction: BRCA2 mutation × olaparib confers
   a +1.6-month PFS gain that is robust across all major subgroups and
   adjusted models. This is the only treatment-by-biomarker interaction
   where the effect size and statistical strength are clinically
   meaningful.

3. Several biomarker–treatment pairings that the labels would suggest
   should be predictive — MSI-high × pembrolizumab, PSMA-high ×
   Lu-177-PSMA, AR-V7 × enzalutamide/abiraterone — show NO interaction
   in this dataset. Either the simulated outcome model didn't encode
   these effects, or sample sizes in the on-treatment biomarker-positive
   cells (e.g., 79 MSI+/pembro+) were too small. The contrast with the
   strong BRCA2/olaparib signal suggests a deliberate decision to
   include only one matched biomarker–therapy effect.

4. Hypotheses supported (sign matches prediction, p<0.05):
   ecog_ps↓PFS; albumin↑PFS; log_PSA↓PFS; weight_loss↓PFS; LDH↓PFS;
   visceral_mets↓PFS; mcrpc↓PFS (univariate only); BRCA2×olaparib↑PFS.

5. Hypotheses with significant signal but in opposite direction to the
   prior: age (predicted ↓, observed ↑), hemoglobin (predicted ↓ in
   anemic, observed slight ↓ overall — anemia paradox absent in adjusted
   model).

6. Hypotheses refuted (null result in well-powered tests):
   visceral/liver/bone metastasis main effects (after adjustment);
   Gleason score; CRP; NLR; all comorbidities; all symptom grades; all
   sociodemographic variables; nearly all somatic co-mutations beyond
   BRCA2; all prior therapies; nearly all SNPs; AR-V7 × AR-targeted
   therapy interactions; MSI × pembrolizumab; PSMA × Lu-177-PSMA;
   ECOG × treatment; age × treatment.
