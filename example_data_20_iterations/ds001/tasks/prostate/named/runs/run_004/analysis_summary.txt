Analysis Summary — ds001_prostate (n = 50,000)
================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival). Cohort is 100% male (sex_female=0)
prostate cancer patients; mCRPC prevalence ~55%, PSMA-high ~60%, BRCA2-mutated ~10%,
AR-V7+ ~20%, MSI-high ~3%. The mean PFS was ~3.74 months (SD 2.02).

Twenty-five iterations of propose-test-refine were executed across treatment main
effects, biomarker × treatment interactions, multivariable prognostics, demographic
disparities, comorbidities, prior therapy, lab markers, symptom burden, SNPs, and
genomic alterations. The highest-yield findings are summarized below.


1. Dominant signal: olaparib × BRCA2 interaction
------------------------------------------------
The strongest and most biologically coherent finding in the dataset.

* Unadjusted interaction (pfs_months ~ olaparib*brca2_mutation):
  +1.62 months, p = 4.3e-61.
* Within brca2_mutation=1 (n=4,996): olaparib effect = +1.55 months, p = 4.0e-27.
* Within brca2_mutation=0 (n=45,004): olaparib effect = -0.07 months, p = 0.019
  (a small but slightly negative point estimate — consistent with the
  drug having little/no benefit in BRCA2 wild-type).
* Adjusting for age, ECOG, mCRPC, visceral mets, LDH, hemoglobin, albumin
  inside brca2_mutation=1: olaparib effect remains +1.44 months, p = 1e-229.
* Stratifying by mCRPC: the olaparib×BRCA2 interaction is robust in both
  mcrpc=1 (+1.63, p = 6e-36) and mcrpc=0 (+1.63, p = 1e-28) — the
  PARP-inhibitor benefit is BRCA2-driven irrespective of castration state.
* The full multivariable model (six treatments + three biomarker
  interactions + key prognostics) confirms: olaparib:brca2_mutation
  coef = +1.45 months, p ≈ 0; main-effect olaparib (in brca2_mutation=0)
  coef = -0.009, p = 0.34.

CONCLUSION: olaparib produces a clinically meaningful PFS benefit (~1.5 months
absolute improvement in mean PFS) restricted to BRCA2-mutated patients. This is
the canonical PARP-inhibitor / HR-deficiency interaction.


2. Pembrolizumab × MSI-high — NOT supported in this cohort
-----------------------------------------------------------
Despite biologic expectation, no MSI-driven pembrolizumab signal was found:
* Interaction coef = -0.34, p = 0.15 (sign opposite to expected).
* In msi_high=1 (n=1,528): pembrolizumab effect = -0.27, p = 0.24.
* In msi_high=0 (n=48,472): pembrolizumab effect = +0.07, p = 0.09.
* Adjusted within msi_high=1: pembrolizumab coef = +0.01, p = 0.89.
* In the full multivariable model: pembrolizumab:msi_high coef = +0.02, p = 0.76.
HYPOTHESIS REFUTED: this dataset does not show a pembrolizumab/MSI-high benefit.


3. Lu177-PSMA × PSMA-high — NOT supported
------------------------------------------
* Interaction coef = -0.02, p = 0.74.
* In psma_high=1 (n=29,962): lu177_psma effect = -0.01, p = 0.74; adjusted
  coef = -0.01, p = 0.20.
* In psma_high=0 (n=20,038): lu177_psma effect = +0.01, p = 0.87.
* Full-model lu177_psma:psma_high coef = -0.005, p = 0.72.
HYPOTHESIS REFUTED in this cohort.


4. AR-V7 × AR-targeted therapy (enzalutamide / abiraterone) — NOT supported
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Hypothesized AR-V7-driven resistance to AR-axis blockers was not observed.
* enzalutamide:ar_v7_positive interaction = +0.002, p = 0.96.
* abiraterone:ar_v7_positive interaction = +0.05, p = 0.33.
* Subgroup enzalutamide effect within ar_v7_positive=0: -0.027 (p = 0.19);
  within ar_v7_positive=1: -0.024 (p = 0.56).
HYPOTHESIS REFUTED.


5. Treatment main effects (unconditional)
------------------------------------------
Across the full cohort, only olaparib's marginal effect was significant
(driven by the ~10% BRCA2 carriers): olaparib +0.094 months, p = 0.003.
Enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, lu177-PSMA, and pembrolizumab each had
trivial (|effect| < 0.07 months) and non-significant unconditional effects.
This is consistent with these therapies producing benefit only in unmeasured-
heterogeneity subgroups or being approximately balanced across patient mix.


6. Strong prognostic baseline features (univariate, all p < 0.05)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Negative associations with PFS:
* ecog_ps: -1.16 months per unit (p ≈ 0).
* psa_ng_ml: -0.0033 / unit (p = 3e-260).
* ldh_u_l: -0.00042 / unit (p = 1.8e-4).
* alkaline_phosphatase_u_l: -0.0005 / unit (p = 6.6e-3).
* weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.0749 / % (p = 1e-225).
* mcrpc=1: -0.52 months vs mcrpc=0 (p = 1.6e-181).
* visceral_mets=1: -0.05 months (p = 0.034).
* total_bilirubin_mg_dl: -0.062 / mg-dL (p = 0.032).

Positive associations with PFS:
* albumin_g_dl: +0.49 months / g-dL (p = 1.5e-166).
* age_years: +0.17 months / year (p ≈ 0). Note: this is opposite to the
  usual clinical expectation that older age confers worse PFS; in this
  cohort the relationship is positive, likely reflecting selection of
  fitter older patients into therapy or a structural feature of the data.
* glucose_mg_dl: +0.0007 / unit (p = 0.032; small).

Univariate hemoglobin had a very small negative slope (p = 0.022) but in the
multivariable model it became non-significant (p = 0.36) — its apparent
prognostic effect is confounded by ECOG, LDH, mCRPC, and albumin.


7. Multivariable prognostic model
----------------------------------
A 10-covariate OLS confirmed the most robust prognostics:
* ecog_ps: adjusted -1.15 months/grade (p ≈ 0).
* albumin_g_dl: adjusted +0.48 months/g-dL (p ≈ 0).
* ldh_u_l: adjusted -0.0005 / unit (p = 4.6e-60).
* hemoglobin and alkaline phosphatase lost significance after adjustment.


8. Demographics, SDoH, comorbidities, prior therapy — NULL
-----------------------------------------------------------
NO significant univariate or adjusted associations were detected for:
* race_ethnicity (ANOVA p = 0.69; adjusted min p = 0.33).
* insurance_type (ANOVA p = 0.79; adjusted min p = 0.16).
* rural_residence (raw p = 0.50; adjusted p = 0.085).
* education_years (p = 0.40).
* smoking_pack_years (p = 0.51).
* All comorbidities tested (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, HF, CAD,
  AF, autoimmune, prior malignancy) — all p > 0.1.
* All prior-therapy indicators (chemo, radiation, surgery, immuno,
  targeted) — all p > 0.1.
* prior_lines_of_therapy (p = 0.21) and years_since_diagnosis (p = 0.90).
* All five symptom grades except weight loss (fatigue, pain, dyspnea,
  cough, appetite) — all p > 0.6.

The absence of demographic/SDoH disparity is itself a notable finding given
the size of the cohort (n = 50,000); the dataset does not encode the kinds
of access-related PFS differences often seen in real-world prostate cancer
populations.


9. SNPs and genomic co-alterations — mostly null
-------------------------------------------------
Twenty-six SNP main-effect tests yielded one nominal hit:
* snp_rs4986893: -0.092 / allele, p = 0.015 (nominally significant; would
  not survive multiple-testing correction across 26 SNPs).
None of the 13 alternative genomic alterations (HER2, MET, RET, ROS1, BRAF
V600E, NTRK, NRG1, FGFR, CDKN2A, TP53, KEAP1, PIK3CA, PTEN) showed a
significant univariate PFS association — these alterations are typically
non-prostate drivers and the null result is consistent with that.


10. Treatment combinations
---------------------------
* enzalutamide × abiraterone interaction = +0.04, p = 0.37 — no synergy.
* docetaxel × enzalutamide interaction = +0.03, p = 0.53 — no synergy.
* docetaxel × prior_chemotherapy interaction = +0.03, p = 0.46 — no
  modulation of docetaxel effect by prior chemo exposure.
* ECOG did not modify docetaxel or enzalutamide effect (interaction p > 0.29).
* Visceral, liver, and bone mets did not modify their respective hypothesized
  treatment effects (all interaction p > 0.15).


Overall conclusions
-------------------
The single dominant treatment-biomarker signal in this cohort is the
olaparib × BRCA2 interaction (+1.45 to +1.62 months absolute PFS gain in
BRCA2-mutated patients, p << 0.001), which is robust to multivariable
adjustment, mCRPC stratification, and inclusion in a comprehensive model
with all six treatments and three biomarker interactions. In contrast, the
two other biomarker-targeted hypotheses — pembrolizumab in MSI-high and
Lu177-PSMA in PSMA-high — were not supported. AR-V7 did not modify
AR-axis-blocker activity. Among prognostic features, ECOG, albumin, LDH,
mCRPC status, weight loss, PSA, alkaline phosphatase, and (paradoxically)
older age were the strongest correlates of PFS, while demographic, SDoH,
comorbidity, prior-therapy, and most genetic features were null. The data
behave like a partially synthetic cohort in which only one
treatment-biomarker pair carries a clinically interpretable effect.
