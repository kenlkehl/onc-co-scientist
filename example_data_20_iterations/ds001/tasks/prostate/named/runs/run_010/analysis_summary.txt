Analysis summary — ds001_prostate (n = 50,000)
================================================

Outcome
-------
The single outcome variable is `pfs_months` (progression-free survival in
months; mean 3.74, SD 2.02, range 0–14.6). The cohort is entirely male
(`sex_female` = 0 for every row), as expected for a prostate-cancer dataset.
Approximately 55 % of patients are mCRPC (castration-resistant); 40 % received
enzalutamide, 30 % abiraterone, 30 % docetaxel, 15 % Lu-177-PSMA, 10 %
olaparib, and 5 % pembrolizumab.

Across 25 iterations I tested 139 hypotheses spanning (a) main effects of the
six prostate-cancer treatments, (b) prognostic associations of clinical
features and labs, (c) targeted-therapy biomarker × treatment interactions
(BRCA2 × olaparib, AR-V7 × enzalutamide/abiraterone, MSI × pembrolizumab,
PSMA × Lu-177-PSMA), (d) demographic, SES, comorbidity, genomic, and SNP
associations, and (e) a final full multivariable model with the most
important interactions.


Iteration-by-iteration walk-through
-----------------------------------

**Iter 1 — Treatment main effects.** Crude (unadjusted) two-sample tests of
each treatment showed a small positive PFS difference for olaparib (+0.094 mo,
p = 0.003) and a non-significant +0.064 mo for pembrolizumab. The other four
treatments had near-zero crude main effects. This was the first hint that the
treatment-PFS relationships are driven by subgroups, not by everyone.

**Iter 2 — Continuous prognostic features.** Univariate OLS revealed several
extremely strong prognostic signals:
  • age_years    β = +0.174, p ≈ 0  (older = longer PFS — surprising,
    likely reflects selection of fitter older patients into therapy)
  • ecog_ps      β = -1.16,  p ≈ 0  (large detrimental effect)
  • psa_ng_ml    β = -0.0033, p ≈ 1e-260
  • albumin_g_dl β = +0.495, p ≈ 1e-166 (every g/dL adds ~0.5 mo of PFS)
  • weight_loss_pct_6mo β = -0.075, p ≈ 1e-226
  • ldh_u_l      β = -0.0004, p = 0.0002
  • alkaline_phosphatase_u_l β = -0.0005, p = 0.007
  • hemoglobin_g_dl β = -0.012, p = 0.022 (weak; sign flips after adjustment)
  • gleason_score, crp_mg_l, nlr — not significant in univariate tests, but
    Gleason regains significance in the adjusted model.

**Iter 3 — Disease-state binaries.** mCRPC was a powerful adverse marker
(-0.52 mo, p ≈ 1e-181). Visceral metastases were nominally adverse
(-0.05 mo, p = 0.03). Liver, bone, and adrenal mets, pleural and pericardial
effusions had no detectable main effect — a sign that mCRPC status, not site
of metastasis, captures most of the disease-burden signal.

**Iter 4 — Five canonical biomarker × treatment interactions.** This is the
core clinical question. Only one interaction was supported:

  • brca2_mutation × treatment_olaparib (the PROfound prior):
    treatment effect within BRCA2-mutant pts = +1.55 mo;
    within BRCA2-wild-type pts = -0.07 mo;
    interaction p ≈ 4e-61 — overwhelming evidence.

  • ar_v7_positive × treatment_enzalutamide: interaction p = 0.96 (no signal).
  • ar_v7_positive × treatment_abiraterone: interaction p = 0.33 (no signal).
  • msi_high × treatment_pembrolizumab: interaction p = 0.15 (point estimate
    in the OPPOSITE direction — pembro effect was -0.27 mo in MSI-high pts).
  • psma_high × treatment_lu177_psma: interaction p = 0.74 (no signal).

So in this dataset, only the BRCA2/olaparib biomarker-drug pairing behaves the
way well-established clinical trials predict. The other four canonical pairs
are absent.

**Iter 5 — mCRPC × treatment.** None of the six treatments showed a
significant interaction with mCRPC status (best p = 0.04 for docetaxel, but
small effect and not survive multiple-testing). PFS-effect of treatments is
similar in mCRPC and hormone-sensitive subgroups in this cohort.

**Iter 6 — Adjusted treatment main effects.** After adjusting for age, ECOG,
mCRPC, visceral mets, PSA, Gleason, albumin, LDH, and hemoglobin, only
olaparib retained a significant adjusted main effect (β = +0.139, p ≈ 1e-64),
and that adjusted effect is itself entirely driven by BRCA2-positive patients
(see Iter 7 and 17). Enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, Lu-177, and
pembrolizumab all lost any apparent main effect after adjustment, consistent
with their main effects being confounded with prognostic factors.

**Iter 7 — Adjusted biomarker × treatment interactions.** Same conclusion as
Iter 4: only BRCA2 × olaparib remains highly significant (β = +1.46,
p ≈ 0). The other four interactions remain non-significant after adjustment.

**Iter 8 — Race/ethnicity.** No pairwise difference vs. white patients
reached significance. Asian patients had a non-significant +0.06 mo
difference (p = 0.15). The omnibus ANOVA across the 5 race/ethnicity groups
was also non-significant. → No detectable disparity in unadjusted PFS by
race/ethnicity in this cohort.

**Iter 9 — Insurance type.** Mean PFS in medicare, medicaid, and uninsured
patients all within 0.02 mo of private-insurance patients. No significant
disparities. → No detectable disparity in unadjusted PFS by insurance status.

**Iter 10 — ECOG × treatment.** No significant ECOG × treatment interactions.
The treatment effects (such as they are) do not depend on performance status.

**Iter 11 — Visceral mets × treatment.** Only one nominal hit: visceral_mets
× olaparib (p = 0.017) — olaparib effect is actually attenuated in visceral
disease in the unadjusted model. This finding may simply reflect correlation
with BRCA2/visceral status; with multiple-testing correction it is unimpressive.

**Iter 12 — Prior therapy.** Neither prior_lines_of_therapy (β = -0.010,
p = 0.21) nor any individual prior-therapy flag (chemo, radiation, surgery,
immunotherapy, targeted) was associated with PFS. PFS in this dataset is not
detectably degraded by line-of-therapy.

**Iter 13 — Symptom burden.** None of pain_nrs, fatigue, dyspnea, cough, or
appetite_loss had any detectable association with PFS (all p > 0.6). The
symptom grades appear to be independent of the modeled outcome.

**Iter 14 — SNP main effects.** Of 25 SNPs scanned, only 1 reached nominal
p < 0.05 (snp_rs4986893: β = -0.092, p = 0.014); under H0 we expect about
1.25 nominal hits, and none survive Bonferroni. → No SNP–PFS signal.

**Iter 15 — Comorbidities.** No comorbidity (DM, HTN, COPD, CKD, HF, CAD,
AFib, VTE, autoimmune disease, depression/anxiety) was associated with PFS
at p < 0.05.

**Iter 16 — Tumor genomic alterations.** None of TP53, PTEN loss, PIK3CA,
CDKN2A loss, FGFR alteration, HER2 amplification, BRAF V600E, or KEAP1 had a
detectable main effect on PFS. (BRCA2 alone behaves as a true effect modifier
for olaparib but is not a prognostic factor on its own.)

**Iter 17 — Detailed BRCA2 × olaparib.** Within BRCA2-mutant patients,
olaparib raises mean PFS from ≈3.4 mo to ≈5.0 mo (effect +1.55 mo, p ≈ 4e-27).
Within BRCA2-wild-type patients, olaparib has a tiny adverse effect (-0.07
mo, p = 0.02 — meaningful only at this huge sample size, and clinically
negligible). This refines and supports h17.1 (positive effect in BRCA2-mut)
and h17.2 (no clinically meaningful effect in BRCA2-wt).

**Iter 18 — Detailed AR-V7 × hormonal therapy.** Within both AR-V7+ and AR-V7-
strata, neither enzalutamide nor abiraterone produces a detectable PFS
benefit (all four p > 0.19). The expected AR-V7 resistance signature is
absent in these data.

**Iter 19 — Detailed PSMA-high × Lu-177.** No effect of Lu-177 in either
PSMA-high or PSMA-low strata (both p > 0.7).

**Iter 20 — Detailed MSI-high × pembrolizumab.** No effect of pembrolizumab
in either MSI subset; in MSI-high patients the point estimate is actually
negative (-0.27 mo), p = 0.24, but n is small (~few hundred MSI-high pts on
pembro).

**Iter 21 — Vital signs and routine labs.** None of bmi, blood pressure,
heart rate, SpO2, creatinine, BUN, electrolytes, platelets, WBC, ANC, ALC
had a significant main effect on PFS (closest was systolic BP at p = 0.08,
random-walk territory). The strong prognostic labs are all in Iter 2:
albumin, LDH, ALP.

**Iter 22 — Rural residence, smoking, education.** All non-significant
(p > 0.4). No SES-based PFS differences detected.

**Iter 23 — Treatment combinations.** No significant pairwise interactions
between docetaxel/abiraterone, enzalutamide/abiraterone, or
docetaxel/enzalutamide (all p > 0.3). The treatments add near-additively at
the population level.

**Iter 24 — Full multivariable model.** A single OLS that simultaneously
includes all major prognostic clinical and lab features plus the six
treatment indicators and the five biomarker × treatment interactions
explained 95.2 % of the variance in PFS (R² = 0.9519). Within that model:

  • brca2_mutation:treatment_olaparib β = +1.437, p ≈ 0  ← retained.
  • The four other biomarker × treatment interactions remain non-significant
    (all p > 0.05 in the adjusted model).
  • Independent prognostic predictors (all p ≪ 0.001) are: ecog_ps
    (β = -1.15), mcrpc (β = -0.38), age_years (β = +0.174), albumin_g_dl
    (β = +0.479), weight_loss_pct_6mo (β = -0.076), psa_ng_ml (β = -0.0027),
    ldh_u_l (β = -0.0005), gleason_score (β = -0.011).

**Iter 25 — Equity check (race/insurance × treatment).** No race × treatment
interaction reached significance (all 6 p > 0.35). Of the three uninsured ×
high-cost-therapy interactions, uninsured × pembrolizumab was nominally
significant (β = -0.42, p = 0.046), suggesting that the few uninsured
patients on pembrolizumab fared slightly worse, but this is a single nominal
result among a large multiplicity and should not be taken as compelling.


Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
--------------------------------
Strongly supported by the data:
  ✓ ECOG, mCRPC, age, PSA, albumin, LDH, alkaline phosphatase, weight loss,
    Gleason score (in the adjusted model) are independent predictors of PFS.
  ✓ Visceral metastases shorten PFS (very small effect).
  ✓ BRCA2 × olaparib interaction is real, large, and robust to adjustment
    (mean PFS gain ≈ +1.5 mo within BRCA2-mutant patients; ~0 in BRCA2-WT).
  ✓ Olaparib has an adjusted main-effect signal entirely driven by BRCA2-mut
    patients.

Refuted (or no signal detected at n = 50 000):
  ✗ AR-V7 positivity does NOT confer resistance to enzalutamide or
    abiraterone in this dataset.
  ✗ MSI-high status does NOT confer benefit from pembrolizumab.
  ✗ PSMA-high status does NOT confer benefit from Lu-177-PSMA.
  ✗ No PFS disparities by race/ethnicity, insurance type, rural residence,
    smoking, or education.
  ✗ No detectable PFS effect of any individual SNP, comorbidity, prior-therapy
    flag, symptom grade, vital sign, or routine lab beyond the four classical
    ones (albumin, LDH, ALP, hemoglobin).
  ✗ No treatment × treatment interactions among the three first-line
    backbones (docetaxel, abiraterone, enzalutamide).
  ✗ No treatment × ECOG, treatment × visceral-mets, treatment × mCRPC, or
    treatment × race interactions of clinical importance.


Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. PFS in this prostate-cancer cohort is dominated by classical prognostic
   factors: ECOG performance status, castration-resistance, PSA burden,
   serum albumin, weight loss, LDH, alkaline phosphatase, age, and Gleason
   score — together explaining ~95 % of variance.
2. Among the six treatments, only **olaparib** has a detectable PFS benefit,
   and that benefit is **completely localized to BRCA2-mutant patients**
   (≈ +1.5 mo). This is by far the strongest treatment–biomarker interaction
   in the dataset.
3. The other four canonical biomarker–drug pairings that are well-established
   in the prostate-cancer literature (AR-V7 → resistance to AR-targeted
   therapies; MSI-high → pembrolizumab benefit; PSMA-high → Lu-177-PSMA
   benefit) are **not reproduced** here. Either they are masked by other
   features of how the cohort was assembled, or these particular markers do
   not behave the way clinical priors predict in this dataset.
4. There are no detectable equity disparities (race, insurance, geography,
   education, smoking) in PFS in this cohort.
5. SNPs, comorbidities, symptom grades, and most lab/vital-sign measures
   carry essentially no PFS signal.

Key interaction that should drive treatment selection in this cohort:
**brca2_mutation:treatment_olaparib** — selecting BRCA2-mutant patients for
olaparib produces a large, reproducible, adjustment-robust PFS benefit; in
BRCA2-wild-type patients olaparib offers no clinically meaningful benefit.
