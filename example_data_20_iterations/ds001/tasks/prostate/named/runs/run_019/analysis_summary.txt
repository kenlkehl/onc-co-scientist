ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_prostate (n = 50,000)
================================================
Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival in months; mean 3.74, SD 2.02, range 0–14.6)
Cohort: All male; advanced prostate cancer cohort with 55% mCRPC, 60% PSMA-high,
20% AR-V7-positive, 10% BRCA2-mutated, 3% MSI-high.

==========================================================
HEADLINE FINDING
==========================================================
The single overwhelmingly significant treatment-biomarker interaction in this
cohort is treatment_olaparib × brca2_mutation (interaction β = +1.62 months,
p ≈ 4.3 × 10⁻⁶¹). The effect of olaparib on PFS is +1.55 months in BRCA2-mutated
patients (5.22 vs 3.67 mo, p = 4 × 10⁻²⁷ in n = 4,996 BRCA2+ subgroup) but
−0.07 months in BRCA2-wild-type patients (p = 0.019; clinically negligible
despite nominal significance from large n). The interaction is unchanged after
adjustment for age, ECOG, mCRPC, gleason, visceral mets, albumin, LDH, PSA,
and weight loss (adjusted interaction β = +1.44, p ≈ 0). The effect size is
also similar in mCRPC=1 and mCRPC=0 subgroups, indicating that synthetic-
lethality benefit is not modified by castration-resistant status. This finding
faithfully recapitulates the published mechanism of PARP-inhibitor activity in
HRD-deficient (BRCA2-mutant) prostate cancer.

==========================================================
ITERATION-BY-ITERATION NARRATIVE
==========================================================

Iter 1 — Main treatment effects (unadjusted t-tests on PFS).
Only treatment_olaparib showed an unadjusted PFS benefit (+0.094 mo,
p = 0.0033). Enzalutamide (-0.026, p = 0.15), abiraterone (-0.006, p = 0.78),
docetaxel (-0.017, p = 0.38), Lu-177-PSMA (-0.004, p = 0.87), and
pembrolizumab (+0.064, p = 0.14) showed no overall PFS effect. The olaparib
result motivated targeted subgroup work in later iterations.

Iter 2 — Disease state and burden.
mcrpc=1 was strongly associated with shorter PFS (3.51 vs 4.03 mo,
p = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁸¹), and visceral_mets=1 showed a small but significant
decrement (-0.048 mo, p = 0.035). liver_mets and bone_mets had no
unadjusted effect. ecog_ps was the most powerful single predictor:
β = -1.16 mo per ECOG point (p ≈ 0). gleason_score was not associated
unadjusted (p = 0.49) — surprising; it reappears as significant only in
multivariable models.

Iter 3 — Laboratory prognostic markers.
Strong, expected directions: albumin_g_dl (+0.49 mo per g/dL, p = 1.5 × 10⁻¹⁶⁶),
ldh_u_l (-0.0004 mo per U/L, p = 1.8 × 10⁻⁴), alkaline_phosphatase_u_l
(-0.0005 mo per U/L, p = 0.007), psa_ng_ml (-0.0033 mo per ng/mL,
p = 3.5 × 10⁻²⁶⁰), weight_loss_pct_6mo (-0.075 mo per %, p = 9.9 × 10⁻²²⁶).
Unexpected: hemoglobin_g_dl had a small NEGATIVE univariate coefficient
(-0.012 mo/g·dL⁻¹, p = 0.022) — opposite to clinical expectation. crp_mg_l
(p = 0.51) and nlr (p = 0.58) were not associated with PFS.

Iter 4 — Treatment × biomarker interactions (the key clinical hypotheses).
• olaparib × brca2_mutation: interaction β = +1.62, p = 4 × 10⁻⁶¹ (huge).
• lu177_psma × psma_high: β = -0.017, p = 0.74 (NULL — surprising).
• pembrolizumab × msi_high: β = -0.34, p = 0.15 (NULL; direction
  even slightly negative in MSI-high, opposite of expectation).
• enzalutamide × ar_v7_positive: β = +0.002, p = 0.96 (NULL).
• abiraterone × ar_v7_positive: β = +0.048, p = 0.33 (NULL).
Only the BRCA2/olaparib hypothesis is supported. Clinically established
patterns of AR-V7 resistance to enzalutamide/abiraterone, MSI-high response
to pembrolizumab, and PSMA-high response to Lu-177-PSMA were NOT recapitulated.

Iter 5 — Stratified confirmation of the BRCA2/olaparib effect.
Within BRCA2+ patients (n = 4,996), olaparib-exposed (n = 529) had mean PFS
5.22 mo vs 3.67 mo for unexposed (n = 4,467; diff +1.55 mo, p = 4 × 10⁻²⁷).
Within BRCA2- patients, olaparib effect was -0.073 mo (p = 0.019;
clinically null though nominally significant given n ≈ 45,000). Confirms
qualitative effect modification: olaparib benefit is BRCA2-restricted.

Iter 6 — Other tumor/genomic markers (main effects).
None of tp53_mutation (p = 0.87), pten_loss (p = 0.62), cdkn2a_loss (p = 0.10),
her2_amplification (p = 0.70), or pik3ca_mutation (p = 0.66) was associated
with PFS unadjusted. msi_high (p = 0.52) and psma_high (p = 0.27) also
unadjusted-null. Only BRCA2 had a positive main effect (+0.10 mo, p = 0.002),
itself driven by the olaparib subgroup.

Iter 7 — Demographics and SDOH.
No race-based, insurance-based, or rural-residence-based PFS disparities.
Black vs white: -0.0005 mo (p = 0.99). Hispanic vs white: -0.004 (p = 0.89).
Asian vs white: +0.056 (p = 0.14). Medicaid vs private: +0.005 (p = 0.87).
Uninsured vs private: -0.010 (p = 0.83). Rural vs urban: +0.015 (p = 0.50).
This dataset shows no measurable SDOH-driven outcome inequity in PFS.

Iter 8 — Comorbidities (unadjusted).
diabetes_mellitus (p = 0.19), hypertension (p = 0.18), copd (p = 0.35),
chronic_kidney_disease (p = 0.23, slightly POSITIVE direction),
heart_failure (p = 0.92), coronary_artery_disease (p = 0.99),
atrial_fibrillation (p = 0.28). All NS. The cohort is large enough that
even small effects would be detected, so absence of signal is informative.

Iter 9 — Germline SNPs (24 markers).
Only snp_rs4986893 reached nominal p < 0.05 (diff = -0.092, p = 0.015).
After Bonferroni correction (threshold p < 0.0021), no SNP was significant.
APOE rs429358 (diff = +0.047, p = 0.066) and CYP2D6 rs1065852
(diff = -0.048, p = 0.094) were trend-level only. Conclusion: no germline
pharmacogenomic signal.

Iter 10 — Symptoms and patient-reported scales.
pain_nrs (p = 0.80), fatigue_grade (p = 0.66), dyspnea_grade (p = 0.81),
cough_grade (p = 0.79), appetite_loss_grade (p = 0.91). All null
unadjusted — symptoms in this dataset are not univariately associated with
PFS, suggesting they may be acting via correlated clinical features
(weight loss, ECOG) that overshadow them.

Iter 11 — Adjusted treatment effects (multivariable OLS adjusting for age,
ECOG, mCRPC, visceral_mets, ldh, albumin, psa, gleason).
Olaparib remained significant (β = +0.138, p = 1.9 × 10⁻⁶⁴). All other
treatments NS (enza p = 0.21; abi p = 0.53; doce p = 0.30; lu177 p = 0.21;
pembro p = 0.45). Confirms the unique olaparib signal.

Iter 12 — Adjusted interaction tests.
olaparib × brca2 robust (adjusted β = +1.44, p ≈ 0). All other interactions
(pembro × msi_high p = 0.57; lu177 × psma_high p = 0.70; enza × arv7
p = 0.66) confirmed null after covariate adjustment.

Iter 13 — ECOG-stratified treatment effects.
Olaparib effect was directionally consistent (positive) across ECOG strata,
without significant heterogeneity. Docetaxel effects also similar across
strata. No evidence that ECOG modifies treatment benefit.

Iter 14 — Adjusted gene panel (17 markers in one model).
brca2_mutation: β = +0.154, p = 1.3 × 10⁻¹⁰⁹ (independently associated even
with olaparib in model).
psma_high: β = +0.022, p = 2.8 × 10⁻⁷ (small but very significant positive
prognostic association).
All other markers (tp53, pten, cdkn2a, pik3ca, her2, fgfr, keap1, msi,
ar_v7, met_ex14, ret, ros1, braf, ntrk, nrg1) NS after adjustment.

Iter 15 — Adjusted SDOH.
Race coefficients all p > 0.5 after adjusting for clinical covariates.
Insurance coefficients all p > 0.16. No disparities even after accounting
for differences in clinical case-mix.

Iter 16 — BRCA2 × olaparib stratified by mCRPC.
mCRPC=1: interaction β = +1.71 (p ≈ 0). mCRPC=0: interaction β = +1.49
(p ≈ 0). Synthetic-lethality benefit is roughly equal magnitude in both
mCRPC and non-mCRPC subsets — generalizable across disease state.

Iter 17 — Age effect and age-treatment interactions.
Univariate: β = +0.174 mo per year (p ≈ 0). Counter-intuitively, older
patients have LONGER PFS in this cohort. This is likely a feature of how
the simulator constructed the dataset (e.g., age-driven baseline survival,
or selection into sicker-younger subgroups), but it is a robust statistical
pattern. No interaction of age ≥75 with any treatment (all p > 0.5).

Iter 18 — Composite risk score and metastatic burden.
risk_score (count of low albumin, high LDH, high ALP, ECOG ≥ 1):
β = -0.395 mo per point (p ≈ 0). Each adverse factor reduces PFS ~0.4 mo.
mets_count (sum of metastatic-site indicators): β small and NS (p = 0.45).
Total number of mets sites doesn't drive PFS in this cohort.

Iter 19 — Adjusted comorbidities.
Diabetes (p = 0.50), CKD (p = 0.55), heart failure (p = 0.83), depression/
anxiety (p = 0.51), prior_malignancy (p = 0.83). All confirmed NS after
adjustment.

Iter 20 — Vital signs and other labs.
spo2_pct, bmi, calcium_mg_dl, creatinine_mg_dl, platelets_k_ul: all
non-significant in univariate analyses. PFS in this cohort is not detectably
modified by any of these.

Iter 21 — Prior therapy.
prior_lines_of_therapy: β = -0.010 (p = 0.20). years_since_diagnosis: also
small and non-significant. Surprisingly, treatment-line history doesn't
show the expected exhaustion effect.

Iter 22 — Visceral mets × docetaxel interaction.
Interaction β = +0.052, p = 0.29. NS — does not replicate the clinical lore
that docetaxel preferentially benefits visceral disease.

Iter 23 — Enzalutamide/abiraterone × mCRPC interactions.
enza × mcrpc: β = -0.049, p = 0.18 (slight negative, NS).
abi × mcrpc: β = +0.030, p = 0.45 (NS).
No evidence that AR-targeted agents preferentially benefit castration-
resistant patients.

Iter 24 — Cross-pathway hypotheses.
pembrolizumab × brca2: β = +0.22, p = 0.12 (trend toward larger benefit
in BRCA2+; not significant; effect = +0.26 mo in BRCA2+ vs +0.04 in
BRCA2-). Could hint at HRD-driven immunogenicity but doesn't replicate
robustly.
olaparib × ar_v7_positive: β = -0.017, p = 0.81. AR-V7 status does NOT
modify olaparib effect, consistent with a BRCA2-driven mechanism.

Iter 25 — Final integrated multivariable model (R² = 0.948).
The 27-feature OLS model explains nearly all (≈95%) of PFS variance.
Significant predictors and adjusted directions:
  • age_years: +0.174 mo/yr (p ≈ 0)
  • ecog_ps: -1.15 mo/level (p ≈ 0)
  • mcrpc: -0.375 mo (p ≈ 0)
  • visceral_mets: -0.021 mo (p ≈ 4 × 10⁻⁵)
  • psa_ng_ml: -0.0027 mo/ng·mL⁻¹ (p ≈ 0)
  • gleason_score: -0.011 mo/point (p ≈ 6 × 10⁻⁸)
  • brca2_mutation: +0.154 mo (p ≈ 0)
  • psma_high: +0.022 mo (p = 3 × 10⁻⁷)
  • albumin_g_dl: +0.478 mo/g·dL⁻¹ (p ≈ 0)
  • ldh_u_l: -0.0005 mo/U·L⁻¹ (p ≈ 0)
  • weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.077 mo/% (p ≈ 0)
  • crp_mg_l: +0.0005 (p = 0.026; small)
  • treatment_olaparib: +0.146 mo (p ≈ 0)
NOT significant after adjustment: ar_v7_positive, msi_high, hemoglobin,
liver_mets, bone_mets, nlr, pain_nrs, fatigue_grade, prior_lines_of_therapy,
treatment_enzalutamide, treatment_abiraterone, treatment_docetaxel,
treatment_lu177_psma, treatment_pembrolizumab.

==========================================================
HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED VS REFUTED
==========================================================

SUPPORTED (with strong evidence):
• olaparib × BRCA2 synthetic-lethality interaction (effect concentrated
  in BRCA2-mutated patients).
• mCRPC, ECOG, PSA, Gleason, albumin, LDH, ALP, weight loss as adverse
  prognostic factors.
• BRCA2 mutation as an independent positive prognostic factor.
• PSMA-high as a small but robust positive prognostic factor.
• Age positively associated with PFS (counter-intuitive but consistent).
• Composite Halabi-style risk score predicts PFS.

REFUTED / NULL (despite clinical-lore expectations):
• Lu-177-PSMA × PSMA-high interaction (NULL).
• Pembrolizumab × MSI-high interaction (NULL; direction opposite).
• Enzalutamide × AR-V7 interaction (NULL; published resistance pattern
  not seen).
• Abiraterone × AR-V7 interaction (NULL).
• Race/ethnicity, insurance, rural-residence outcome disparities (NULL).
• Comorbidities (diabetes, CKD, HF, COPD, AFib, depression, etc.) as
  PFS predictors (NULL adjusted).
• Symptoms (pain, fatigue, dyspnea, cough, appetite loss) as PFS predictors
  (NULL).
• Germline SNPs as pharmacogenomic modifiers (NULL after Bonferroni).
• Most non-BRCA2 tumor genomics (TP53, PTEN, CDKN2A, HER2, FGFR,
  PIK3CA, KEAP1, fusions) as PFS predictors.
• Hemoglobin: direction is OPPOSITE expectation (small but significant
  negative coefficient unadjusted; not significant adjusted).
• Number of metastatic sites does not predict PFS (only the specific site
  type matters: visceral and mCRPC stage).

==========================================================
OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
==========================================================

1. PFS in this cohort is driven primarily by classical clinical prognostic
   features (ECOG, mCRPC, albumin, LDH, weight loss, PSA, Gleason) plus
   a single targeted-therapy effect (olaparib in BRCA2-mutated patients).
   A 27-feature OLS model captures ~95% of variance — PFS is essentially
   deterministic given the assembled feature panel.

2. Of the six treatments, only olaparib carries a real PFS benefit, and
   that benefit is restricted to BRCA2-mutated patients. This faithfully
   recapitulates the PARP-inhibitor synthetic-lethality mechanism. The
   other targeted therapies (Lu-177-PSMA, pembrolizumab, enzalutamide,
   abiraterone, docetaxel) show no detectable overall or subgroup PFS
   effect — the simulator does not encode their published efficacy
   patterns.

3. There are NO measurable race/ethnicity, insurance, or rural-residence
   disparities in PFS in this cohort, either before or after clinical
   adjustment. The dataset appears constructed without SDOH-driven outcome
   inequity.

4. Of the tumor genomic markers, only BRCA2 (driven by olaparib) and
   PSMA-high (small independent prognostic effect) are signals. TP53,
   PTEN, CDKN2A, HER2, FGFR, PIK3CA, KEAP1 mutations and the rare
   fusions show no detectable association.

5. Age has an unexpected POSITIVE association with PFS (older = longer PFS)
   that survives multivariable adjustment. This is a property of the
   simulator and is unlikely to reflect a real-world pattern.

6. Comorbidities, symptoms, and germline SNPs collectively contribute
   little or nothing to PFS prediction in this dataset. The signal is
   carried by canonical clinical labs/staging plus the BRCA2/olaparib
   interaction.
