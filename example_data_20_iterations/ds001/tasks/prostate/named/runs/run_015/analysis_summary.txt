Analysis summary — ds001_prostate (n=50,000; outcome: pfs_months)
=================================================================

Cohort overview
---------------
All 50,000 patients are male (sex_female=0). Median age 65 (IQR 58.6–71.8;
range 47–90). Median PFS 3.7 months (IQR 2.3–5.1; range 0–14.6). Disease
state: 55% mCRPC, 20% visceral metastases, 20% bone metastases. Treatment
exposures: enzalutamide 40%, docetaxel 30%, abiraterone 30%, Lu177-PSMA 15%,
olaparib 10%, pembrolizumab 5%. Most patients receive 1–2 concurrent lines.

Iteration-by-iteration findings
-------------------------------

Iter 1 — Treatment main effects on PFS.
Hypotheses: each of the six treatments alters mean PFS. Findings: only
treatment_olaparib reached significance, +0.094 months on therapy
(p=0.003). Enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, Lu177-PSMA, and
pembrolizumab all showed effects of <0.07 months that did not reach
significance. The olaparib signal motivated a subgroup analysis next.

Iter 2 — Targeted-therapy biomarker interactions.
Hypothesized standard-of-care biomarker pairings:
  - brca2_mutation x treatment_olaparib: STRONGLY SUPPORTED.
    Cell means BRCA2+/olap+ 5.22, BRCA2+/olap- 3.67, BRCA2-/olap+ 3.67,
    BRCA2-/olap- 3.74. Interaction coefficient +1.62 months
    (p=4.3e-61). This is the dominant treatment–biomarker signal in the
    cohort.
  - psma_high x treatment_lu177_psma: REFUTED. No interaction (coef
    -0.02, p=0.74). PSMA-high patients gain nothing extra from PSMA-
    targeted radioligand therapy in this dataset.
  - msi_high x treatment_pembrolizumab: REFUTED. Point estimate is
    paradoxically negative (-0.34, p=0.15).
  - ar_v7_positive x treatment_enzalutamide and x treatment_abiraterone:
    REFUTED. Both interactions ~0 with p>0.3.
The expected clinical patterns for PSMA/Lu177, MSI/pembrolizumab, and
AR-V7/AR-targeted therapy are absent in this synthetic cohort; only
BRCA2/olaparib is encoded.

Iter 3 — Disease state and clinical staging.
Hypotheses about staging features mostly supported with two surprises:
  - mcrpc: SUPPORTED. mCRPC patients have ~0.52 months shorter PFS
    (3.51 vs 4.03, p=2e-181) — large and highly significant.
  - visceral_mets: weak effect (-0.05 months, p=0.03).
  - liver_mets, bone_mets, adrenal_mets, pleural_effusion: REFUTED. None
    have a meaningful association with PFS (all |Δ|≤0.04 months).
  - gleason_score: REFUTED (r=-0.003, p=0.49) — Gleason does not
    predict PFS in this cohort, which is unusual clinically.
  - ecog_ps: STRONGLY SUPPORTED. PFS drops monotonically with ECOG
    grade — 4.68 → 3.49 → 2.38 months for grades 0/1/2 (~1.15 months
    per grade).
  - psa_ng_ml: SUPPORTED. r=-0.15 (log scale r=-0.23, p≈0).

Iter 4 — Lab biomarker prognostics.
  - albumin_g_dl: SUPPORTED, positive prognostic (r=+0.12, p=1e-166).
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo: SUPPORTED, negative prognostic (r=-0.14,
    p=1e-225).
  - ldh_u_l: SUPPORTED but weak (r=-0.017, p=2e-4).
  - alkaline_phosphatase_u_l: very weak (r=-0.012, p=0.007), did not
    survive multivariable adjustment.
  - hemoglobin_g_dl: REFUTED in direction (r=-0.010, p=0.02 — sign
    opposite of clinical expectation; weak in any case).
  - crp_mg_l, nlr, creatinine, calcium, platelets, wbc, anc, alc,
    cea_ng_ml, ast, alt, sodium, bmi, spo2: all REFUTED (no meaningful
    correlation).
The two dominant lab predictors are clearly albumin (good) and
weight loss (bad), which together capture the cachexia/nutrition axis.

Iter 5 — Symptom grades and PROs.
Hypotheses that fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, and appetite-loss grades
predict PFS were ALL REFUTED. Every correlation was |r|<0.003 with p>0.6.
Patient-reported symptom severity carries no prognostic information in
this cohort.

Iter 6 — Demographics.
  - race_ethnicity: REFUTED (ANOVA p=0.69) — no PFS difference among
    white/black/hispanic/asian/other. Encouraging from a disparities
    standpoint, but contrary to many real-world cohorts.
  - insurance_type: REFUTED (ANOVA p=0.79).
  - rural_residence, education_years, smoking_pack_years: all REFUTED.
  - age_years: SUPPORTED in direction but with a startling magnitude
    (r=+0.86, p≈0). Older age strongly predicts LONGER PFS — opposite
    of typical expectation. Investigated next.

Iter 7 — Refining the age finding.
PFS rises monotonically across age deciles: ~0.85 months in the youngest
decile (mean age 47) to ~6.86 months in the oldest decile (mean age 82).
Linear fit: PFS = -7.57 + 0.174 × age (p≈0). Each year of age adds
~0.17 months of PFS. This is a very strong, nearly deterministic
relationship; in a real-world cohort one would expect the opposite
(older patients usually have worse outcomes), so this is a notable
feature of the synthetic data.

Iter 8 — Comorbidities.
diabetes, hypertension, copd, ckd, heart failure, coronary disease,
atrial fibrillation, VTE history, autoimmune disease, hepatitis B/C,
HIV, prior malignancy, depression/anxiety, and ILD history were ALL
REFUTED. None showed a meaningful or statistically significant
association with PFS (smallest p=0.06 for depression/anxiety, |Δ|≤0.06
months).

Iter 9 — Prior therapy and disease duration.
prior_chemotherapy, prior_radiation, prior_surgery, prior_immunotherapy,
prior_targeted_therapy, prior_lines_of_therapy, and years_since_diagnosis
were ALL REFUTED (all |Δ|<0.03 months / |r|<0.01). Treatment history
carries no prognostic signal.

Iter 10 — Germline SNP screen.
None of the 25 germline SNPs reached the Bonferroni-corrected
significance threshold of 0.002. Only snp_rs4986893 (CYP2C19*3) was
nominally significant (r=-0.011, p=0.014; mean PFS 3.66 in carriers vs
3.75 in non-carriers — a ~0.09-month difference). At the cohort level,
SNP-based pharmacogenomics has essentially no PFS effect here.

Iter 11 — Other oncogenic alterations.
tp53_mutation, pten_loss, cdkn2a_loss were REFUTED (all p>0.10). PTEN
loss and TP53 mutation in particular are well-established adverse
prognostics in real prostate cohorts; their absence here suggests they
are encoded as null. Off-target alterations more typical of other tumors
(her2_amplification, met_exon14_skipping, ret_fusion, ros1_fusion,
braf_v600e, ntrk_fusion, nrg1_fusion, fgfr_alteration, keap1_mutation,
pik3ca_mutation) were all REFUTED — no PFS effects.

Iter 12 — Multivariable model with leading predictors.
A linear model containing age, ECOG, mCRPC, PSA, albumin, weight loss,
LDH, ALP, hemoglobin, visceral mets, BRCA2, and olaparib achieved
adjusted R² = 0.9475. Independent significant effects (per unit):
age +0.174; ecog -1.152; mcrpc -0.376; psa -0.0027; albumin +0.478;
weight_loss -0.077; ldh -5e-4; visceral_mets -0.021; brca2_mutation
+0.155; olaparib +0.146. Hemoglobin and ALP fall out after adjustment.
The model explains nearly all PFS variance — consistent with a
synthetic dataset where outcome is a known function of these features.

Iter 13 — BRCA2/olaparib interaction in adjusted model.
After adjustment for the above clinical/biomarker covariates, the BRCA2
main effect collapses to 0 (+0.003, p=0.68), the olaparib main effect
collapses to 0 (-0.003, p=0.72), and the brca2_mutation × treatment_
olaparib interaction is +1.44 months (p effectively 0). All olaparib
benefit is concentrated in BRCA2-mutated patients — consistent with
PARP synthetic-lethality biology.

Iter 14 — Combination treatments.
Number of concurrent therapies (n_treatments) showed a tiny
multivariable-adjusted PFS gain of +0.011 months per treatment
(p=2e-4). The clinically discouraged enzalutamide + abiraterone doublet
showed no PFS difference (Δ -0.005 months) — there is no penalty or
benefit in this dataset for that combination.

Iter 15 — Six biomarker × treatment interactions in adjusted models.
After adjustment, all of the following remained NULL: msi_high ×
pembrolizumab (p=0.58), psma_high × Lu177-PSMA (p=0.68), ar_v7_positive
× enzalutamide (p=0.69), ar_v7_positive × abiraterone (p=0.15),
tp53_mutation × docetaxel (p=0.46), pten_loss × enzalutamide (p=0.42).
The cohort encodes only the BRCA2/olaparib pairing among classical
biomarker-targeted-therapy combinations.

Iter 16 — BRCA2/olaparib interaction stratified by mCRPC.
The interaction was virtually identical in both castration-resistance
strata: mcrpc=0 +1.63 (p=1e-28); mcrpc=1 +1.63 (p=6e-36). Olaparib
benefit in BRCA2 carriers does not depend on castration-resistance
status.

Iter 17 — ECOG × treatment interactions.
Hypotheses that ECOG modifies docetaxel, olaparib, or pembrolizumab
benefit were ALL REFUTED (all p>0.75). Treatment effect sizes do not
vary by performance status in this cohort.

Iter 18 — PSA tertile gradient.
A clean monotone gradient: low PSA tertile 4.24 months, mid 3.77, high
3.22 months (ANOVA p≈0). Hypothesis SUPPORTED.

Iter 19 — Albumin × weight-loss interaction.
Hypothesized synergy between low albumin and high weight loss was
REFUTED (interaction coef -0.001, p=0.38). The two cachexia markers act
additively.

Iter 20 — BRCA2/olaparib interaction across demographic strata.
Stratified by race_ethnicity (white +1.71, black +1.67, asian +1.57,
hispanic +1.00, other +3.57), insurance_type (medicare +1.49, private
+1.69, medicaid +1.95, uninsured +1.16), and age tertile (young +1.26,
mid +1.48, old +1.57). All strata p<0.013, most p<1e-15. The interaction
is robust across every demographic slice — this is the signature
treatment–biomarker signal of the cohort.

Iter 21 — Age × treatment interactions.
All four age × treatment interactions (enzalutamide, abiraterone,
docetaxel, olaparib) were REFUTED (|coef|≤0.001 month/year, all p>0.27).
The age effect is uniform across treatment exposure.

Iter 22 — Adjusted treatment main effects.
After full adjustment, the multivariable treatment coefficients
(months) were: enzalutamide -0.005 (p=0.26), abiraterone -0.0001
(p=0.97), docetaxel -0.007 (p=0.14), olaparib +0.146 (p=3e-100),
Lu177-PSMA -0.007 (p=0.26), pembrolizumab +0.017 (p=0.08). Only
olaparib (driven by BRCA2 carriers) shows independent benefit.

Iter 23 — Olaparib effect within BRCA2 strata.
Within BRCA2 mutation carriers, olaparib increases PFS by +1.55 months
(5.22 vs 3.67, p=4e-27, n_on=529). Within BRCA2 wildtype, olaparib
*decreases* PFS slightly by 0.07 months (p=0.02). Olaparib is a
biomarker-conditional therapy: beneficial only in BRCA2 carriers and
mildly detrimental otherwise.

Iter 24 — Disease-state × prognostic-marker interactions.
psa_ng_ml × mcrpc: STRONGLY SUPPORTED (interaction coef +0.0153 month
per ng/mL of PSA in mCRPC, p≈0). The negative impact of higher PSA on
PFS is markedly attenuated (and partly reversed) in mCRPC patients —
consistent with PSA being a less reliable disease marker in
castration-resistant disease.
ldh_u_l × mcrpc: REFUTED (interaction near 0, p=0.48).

Iter 25 — Final parsimonious model.
A model containing age, ECOG, mCRPC, PSA, albumin, weight loss, LDH,
visceral mets, all six treatments, and the brca2_mutation ×
treatment_olaparib interaction achieves R² = 0.9518. Coefficients
(months): age +0.174, ecog -1.152, mcrpc -0.376, psa -0.0027, albumin
+0.479, weight_loss -0.076, ldh -5e-4, visceral_mets -0.020, brca2_main
+0.003 (NS), olap_main -0.003 (NS), brca2:olap interaction +1.437
(p≈0). Hypothesis SUPPORTED — these features explain ~95% of PFS
variance.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. PROGNOSTIC AXIS (independent of treatment): Six features dominate
   PFS in this cohort and act roughly additively:
     - age_years           (strong positive — older = longer PFS,
                            unusually large effect that runs opposite
                            to typical clinical experience)
     - ecog_ps             (strong negative — ~1.15 months/grade)
     - mcrpc               (negative — castration-resistance shortens
                            PFS by ~0.4 months)
     - psa_ng_ml           (negative — log-PSA r≈-0.23, with a
                            mCRPC-specific attenuation)
     - albumin_g_dl        (positive — nutrition/reserve)
     - weight_loss_pct_6mo (negative — cachexia)
   Together with LDH and visceral mets these explain >95% of PFS
   variance.

2. TREATMENT-BIOMARKER SIGNAL: The only meaningful treatment effect is
   olaparib, and it is entirely conditional on BRCA2 mutation status.
   Olaparib in BRCA2 carriers extends PFS by ~1.5 months (5.22 vs
   3.67); in BRCA2 wildtype it provides no benefit (slight harm).
   This is the canonical PARP inhibitor synthetic-lethality pattern
   and is robust across mCRPC status, age tertiles, race, and
   insurance subgroups.

3. NEGATIVE / NULL FINDINGS of note (many of which contradict
   well-established prostate-cancer biology):
     - PSMA-high status does NOT modify Lu177-PSMA benefit.
     - MSI-high status does NOT modify pembrolizumab benefit.
     - AR-V7 positivity does NOT reduce enzalutamide or abiraterone
       benefit.
     - Gleason score, PTEN loss, TP53 mutation are not prognostic.
     - Bone, liver, and adrenal mets are not prognostic.
     - Comorbidities (diabetes, CKD, heart failure, etc.) have no
       PFS effect.
     - Prior treatment exposures and lines of therapy have no PFS
       effect.
     - Race, insurance, rural residence, education, and smoking
       have no PFS effect.
     - Patient-reported symptoms (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough,
       appetite loss) have no PFS effect.
     - 24/25 germline SNPs show no PFS effect; one is nominally
       significant but trivial in magnitude.

4. INTERACTIONS detected: brca2_mutation × treatment_olaparib (large,
   robust, biologically plausible) and psa_ng_ml × mcrpc (the per-unit
   prognostic value of PSA is markedly weaker in mCRPC). All other
   tested interactions (ECOG × treatment, age × treatment, MSI × pembro,
   PSMA × Lu177, AR-V7 × AR-targeted, TP53 × docetaxel, PTEN × enza,
   albumin × weight loss) were null.

In short, in this 50,000-patient prostate-cancer cohort PFS is almost
entirely a function of a small set of clinical/laboratory prognostic
factors (age, ECOG, mCRPC, PSA, albumin, weight loss, ±LDH and
visceral mets), and the only therapy that matters is olaparib in
BRCA2-mutated patients. Many other biomarker-treatment relationships
that are well established in real prostate-cancer practice are not
present in this dataset.
