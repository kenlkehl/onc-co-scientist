Analysis Summary — ds001_prostate (N=50,000)
=============================================

Outcome of interest: pfs_months (overall mean 3.74, SD 2.02). All patients are
male; the cohort is exclusively prostate cancer with metastatic and biomarker
features. Twenty-five propose-test-refine iterations were performed, exploring
main effects, subgroup heterogeneity, and biomarker × treatment interactions.

Below is a synthesis of what the data showed, organized by theme rather than
strictly by iteration number.

1. Main treatment effects (Iter 1, 9)
-------------------------------------
None of the six treatments showed a meaningful crude main effect on PFS:
  - enzalutamide:   diff = -0.026 mo, p = 0.15
  - abiraterone:    diff = -0.006 mo, p = 0.78
  - docetaxel:      diff = -0.017 mo, p = 0.38
  - lu177_psma:     diff = -0.004 mo, p = 0.87
  - pembrolizumab:  diff = +0.064 mo, p = 0.14
  - olaparib:       diff = +0.094 mo, p = 0.003 (only weakly positive)

After multivariable adjustment (ecog_ps, age, mcrpc, visceral_mets, ldh,
albumin, hemoglobin, prior_lines_of_therapy, all six treatments together), the
olaparib coefficient remained positive (β = +0.142, p < 1e-56), but the other
five treatments had near-zero adjusted main effects. The implication is that
"average" treatment effects are uninformative here — the action lives in
subgroup-specific responses.

2. Disease severity and prognostic biology (Iter 2, 3, 6, 7, 8, 19, 20)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Strong, biologically intuitive prognostic signals on PFS:
  - ecog_ps:           β = -1.16 mo per unit, p ≈ 0  (largest single driver)
  - mcrpc=1 vs 0:      diff = -0.52 mo,        p ≈ 1.6e-181
  - log10(psa_ng_ml):  β = -0.80 mo per log,   p ≈ 0
  - albumin_g_dl:      β = +0.49 mo per g/dL,  p ≈ 1.5e-166
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo: β = -0.075 mo per %,  p ≈ 1e-225 (also robust to
                         adjustment for ecog/mcrpc/visceral_mets)
  - ldh_u_l:           β = -4.2e-4 per U/L,    p ≈ 2e-4
  - alkaline_phos:     β = -5.0e-4 per U/L,    p = 0.007
  - visceral_mets:     diff = -0.05 mo,        p = 0.034 (weak)

Older age was, surprisingly, *positively* associated with PFS in univariable
OLS (β = +0.174 mo/yr, p ≈ 0). This is most plausibly a survivorship/cohort-
selection artifact: older patients reaching this dataset may have indolent
biology, and age is correlated with treatment patterns.

Several variables that frequently look prognostic in real-world prostate-
cancer cohorts were null here, suggesting either weak signal in this dataset
or noisy measurement: gleason_score (p = 0.49), bone_mets (p = 0.62),
liver_mets (p = 0.95), adrenal_mets (p = 0.17), pleural_effusion (p = 0.09),
crp_mg_l (p = 0.51), nlr (p = 0.58), bmi (p = 0.13), heart_rate (p = 0.87),
spo2 (p = 0.32). Symptom-burden grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, appetite-loss)
were all null univariably and after adjustment — except for weight loss,
which remained strongly negative. Hemoglobin had a small negative
univariable slope (counterintuitive direction; p = 0.02), likely confounded
by treatment selection — when adjusted for treatment and ECOG, it dropped
toward null.

3. Comorbidities and prior therapy (Iter 8)
-------------------------------------------
prior_lines_of_therapy, prior_chemotherapy, chronic_kidney_disease,
heart_failure, diabetes_mellitus, and years_since_diagnosis were all null on
PFS (p > 0.18 each). This dataset's PFS does not appear to encode comorbidity
burden.

4. Biomarker main effects (Iter 4)
----------------------------------
Of the binary biomarkers tested marginally:
  - brca2_mutation=1: +0.10 mo,  p = 0.002 (small but real)
  - msi_high=1:       +0.04 mo,  p = 0.52  (null)
  - psma_high=1:      +0.02 mo,  p = 0.27  (null)
  - ar_v7_positive=1: -0.01 mo,  p = 0.58  (null)
  - tp53_mutation=1:  +0.00 mo,  p = 0.87  (null)
  - pten_loss=1:      +0.02 mo,  p = 0.62  (null)

Biomarker positivity, on its own, is a poor predictor of PFS in this cohort.
The brca2 main effect almost certainly reflects the olaparib interaction
described next.

5. Biomarker × treatment interactions — the central finding
-----------------------------------------------------------
Iterations 5, 10, 11, 15, 23, 24, and 25 tested four biologically motivated
biomarker:matched-treatment pairs:

  (a) brca2_mutation × olaparib  →  STRONGLY POSITIVE
      Crude interaction: β = +1.62 mo, p ≈ 4.3e-61
      Adjusted (ecog/age/mcrpc/visceral): β = +1.44 mo, p ≈ 0
      Comprehensive multivariable: β = +1.45 mo, p ≈ 0
      Stratified means (brca2, olaparib): (1,1)=5.22, (1,0)=3.67,
                                          (0,1)=3.67, (0,0)=3.74.
      Among BRCA2-mutant patients (n=4,996), olaparib produced +1.55 mo
      additional PFS (p ≈ 4e-27, n=529 treated). Among BRCA2-wildtype
      patients (n=45,004), olaparib was actually slightly harmful (-0.07 mo,
      p = 0.019), consistent with off-target use providing no benefit. The
      effect persisted across ECOG strata (β ≈ +1.7 in both ECOG=0 and
      ECOG≥2 subgroups), so performance status does not modulate the BRCA2
      benefit of olaparib in this cohort.

  (b) psma_high × lu177_psma  →  NULL
      Crude interaction:  β = -0.017, p = 0.74
      Adjusted: β = -0.017, p = 0.30
      Comprehensive: β = -0.005, p = 0.73
      In PSMA-high patients, lu177-PSMA produced no benefit (-0.011 mo,
      p = 0.74). In PSMA-low patients, also no effect. Despite biological
      rationale, the effect is absent in this dataset.

  (c) msi_high × pembrolizumab  →  NULL
      Crude interaction:  β = -0.34, p = 0.15
      Adjusted: β = -0.08, p = 0.28
      Comprehensive: β = +0.02, p = 0.78
      In MSI-high patients (n=1,528, of whom 79 received pembrolizumab),
      mean PFS was actually 0.27 mo lower on pembrolizumab (p = 0.24, ns).
      In MSI-stable patients pembrolizumab showed a small +0.075 mo trend
      (p = 0.09). No evidence of the expected MSI-driven response.

  (d) ar_v7_positive × enzalutamide  →  NULL
      Crude interaction: β = +0.002, p = 0.96
      Adjusted: β = -0.005, p = 0.74
      Comprehensive: β = -0.008, p = 0.57
      No detectable resistance pattern in AR-V7+ patients; enzalutamide had
      ~0 effect in both AR-V7+ and AR-V7- strata.

In a comprehensive model containing all four interaction terms, the four
main treatment terms had a mean |coef| of 0.006 mo while the four
biomarker-treatment interaction terms had a mean |coef| of 0.371 mo —
roughly 60× larger. This is driven essentially entirely by the
brca2_mutation:olaparib interaction; the other three matched pairs do not
contribute.

6. Other interactions explored (Iter 12, 13, 18, 21, 22)
--------------------------------------------------------
None of these reached significance:
  - visceral_mets × docetaxel: β = +0.05, p = 0.29
  - ecog_ps × docetaxel: β = +0.012, p = 0.66
  - tp53_mutation × docetaxel, pten_loss × abiraterone, tp53 × olaparib:
    all p > 0.24
  - ecog_ps × visceral_mets × docetaxel three-way: p = 0.64
  - ar_v7_positive × abiraterone: β = +0.05, p = 0.33
  - ecog_ps × brca2_mutation × olaparib three-way: p = 0.87
ECOG status itself remains the dominant univariate prognostic variable
(ECOG ≥ 2 vs ≤ 1: -1.61 mo, p ≈ 0).

7. Demographics, SES, geography (Iter 7, 17)
--------------------------------------------
No raw or adjusted differences in PFS by:
  - race_ethnicity (ANOVA p = 0.69; adjusted F-test p = 0.64)
  - insurance_type (ANOVA p = 0.79; adjusted F-test p = 0.61)
  - rural_residence (raw p = 0.50; adjusted p = 0.08, trend toward worse)
  - smoking_pack_years (p = 0.51)
  - education_years (p = 0.40)
This dataset shows no detectable disparity signal on the PFS endpoint.

8. SNP screen (Iter 14)
-----------------------
A univariate scan of all 25 SNP genotypes found just 1/25 with p < 0.05
(snp_rs4986893, β = -0.092, p = 0.014). None survived a Bonferroni threshold
(0.002). Consistent with chance; no individual SNP carries reliable PFS
signal in this cohort.

9. Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
-----------------------------------
SUPPORTED:
  - ECOG performance status, mCRPC, log(PSA), albumin, weight loss, LDH,
    and alkaline phosphatase are robust prognostic markers for PFS.
  - BRCA2-mutant patients derive a large, reproducible PFS benefit from
    olaparib (~+1.5 mo); BRCA2-wildtype patients do not, and may even fare
    slightly worse on it.
  - In a fully adjusted multivariable model, biomarker:matched-treatment
    interaction effects dominate over main treatment effects.

REFUTED / NOT SUPPORTED in this dataset:
  - PSMA-high × lu177-PSMA benefit (despite plausible mechanism).
  - MSI-high × pembrolizumab benefit.
  - AR-V7 × enzalutamide resistance pattern.
  - Race/ethnicity, insurance, rural-residence disparities on PFS.
  - Symptom-grade prognostic value (except weight loss).
  - Comorbidity burden, prior therapy, gleason, bone/liver/adrenal mets,
    CRP, NLR, BMI, vital signs as PFS predictors.

10. Overall conclusions
-----------------------
The structure of this synthetic-EHR cohort is dominated by:
  (1) Strong, classically-known prognostic markers (ECOG, mCRPC, PSA,
      albumin, weight loss, LDH, alkaline phosphatase).
  (2) A single dramatic, biologically targeted treatment-biomarker
      interaction: olaparib in BRCA2-mutant disease.
The other three biomarker–therapy pairings that were biologically motivated
(PSMA/lutetium, MSI-H/pembrolizumab, AR-V7/enzalutamide) are not encoded as
detectable signals here, despite adequate sample sizes to detect ≈1-month
shifts. Demographic, socioeconomic, and broad mutation-panel features
contribute negligibly to PFS variance after adjustment for ECOG and disease
burden. Treatment evaluation in this cohort, therefore, must be done in
biomarker-stratified rather than overall populations — the BRCA2/olaparib
case is the textbook illustration of why.
