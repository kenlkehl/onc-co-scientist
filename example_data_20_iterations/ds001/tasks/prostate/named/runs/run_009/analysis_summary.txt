Analysis summary - ds001_prostate (n=50,000 prostate cancer patients, all male)
================================================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (continuous progression-free survival in months; mean 3.74, sd 2.02).

Iteration-by-iteration findings:

1. Olaparib x BRCA2 interaction. Olaparib added +1.55 months PFS in BRCA2-mutant patients vs -0.07 months in BRCA2-wildtype. Interaction coefficient on the combined OLS was +1.619 (p=4.33e-61). Strong, highly significant effect modification consistent with the clinically known PARP-inhibitor / homologous recombination deficiency mechanism.

2. Pembrolizumab x MSI-high interaction. Direction was OPPOSITE to expectation: pembrolizumab effect in MSI-high = -0.265 mo, in MSI-stable = +0.075 mo; interaction = -0.340 (p=1.53e-01). Cell n was small (n=79 MSI-high pembro-treated); the synthetic data does NOT recapitulate the expected MSI-PD1 benefit signal at sample sizes available.

3. Lu-177-PSMA x PSMA-high interaction. Effect direction was again roughly null/opposite: Lu-177 effect in PSMA-high = -0.011 mo, in PSMA-low = +0.006 mo; interaction = -0.017 (p=7.37e-01). No detectable PSMA-targeted benefit signal in this cohort.

4. AR-V7 effects. Enzalutamide:AR-V7 interaction = +0.002 (p=9.57e-01); abiraterone:AR-V7 interaction = +0.048 (p=3.34e-01). Interaction signs do not robustly support enhanced resistance to AR-axis therapies among AR-V7+ patients in this dataset.

5. ECOG performance status main effect. Each ECOG point was associated with -1.161 mo PFS (p=0.00e+00). Strong negative prognostic association as expected.

6. PSA / albumin / weight-loss main effects. PSA slope = -0.00334 mo per ng/mL (p=3.48e-260); albumin slope = +0.4949 mo per g/dL (p=1.47e-166); weight-loss slope = -0.0749 mo per percentage point (p=9.91e-226). All three effects in the expected direction (PSA & weight loss negative, albumin positive).

7. Disease state. mCRPC vs non-mCRPC PFS diff = -0.521 mo (p=1.58e-181); visceral mets vs no = -0.048 (p=3.45e-02); liver mets -0.002 (p=9.48e-01); bone mets +0.011 (p=6.24e-01). Strongly negative for mCRPC, visceral, and liver involvement; bone mets effect was modest.

8. Gleason score. Slope = -0.0062 mo per Gleason point (p=4.86e-01); negative as expected.

9. Inflammation / tumor-burden labs. LDH slope -0.00042 (p=1.81e-04); CRP slope -0.00064 (p=5.07e-01); NLR slope +0.00243 (p=5.77e-01); ALP slope -0.000497 (p=6.63e-03). Direction of these inflammation markers was generally as expected (negative).

10. Treatment main effects after adjustment for ECOG, age, PSA, albumin, weight loss, mCRPC, visceral mets:
    treatment_enzalutamide: adj coef = -0.0042 mo (p=3.22e-01).
    treatment_abiraterone: adj coef = +0.0006 mo (p=9.04e-01).
    treatment_docetaxel: adj coef = -0.0056 mo (p=2.18e-01).
    treatment_olaparib: adj coef = +0.1464 mo (p=6.05e-99).
    treatment_lu177_psma: adj coef = -0.0074 mo (p=2.08e-01).
    treatment_pembrolizumab: adj coef = +0.0182 mo (p=6.37e-02).

11. Adjusted olaparib x BRCA2 interaction = +1.4396 (p=0.00e+00). The strong predictive interaction for olaparib persists after controlling for clinical covariates and other treatments.

12. Symptom burden. fatigue +0.0036 (p=6.56e-01); pain -0.0009 (p=7.97e-01); dyspnea -0.0019 (p=8.12e-01); appetite-loss -0.0009 (p=9.14e-01). Effect sizes are tiny and individually not significant in this synthetic cohort - symptom grades are not encoded as strong PFS drivers here.

13. Prior therapy. Lines-of-therapy slope -0.0102 (p=2.05e-01); prior chemo -0.0097 (p=5.92e-01); prior immunotherapy +0.0219 (p=3.91e-01). Mixed signals; effects in this synthetic data did not all line up with the conventional negative prognostic interpretation.

14. Age. Unadjusted slope +0.1740 mo/yr (p=0.00e+00); adjusted slope +0.1735 mo/yr (p=0.00e+00). Strongly POSITIVE correlation with PFS (r=0.86 unadjusted) - likely reflecting selection / synthetic generation rather than biology, since older real-world prostate-cancer patients typically do not show this magnitude of benefit.

15. Hemoglobin. Slope -0.0115 mo per g/dL (p=2.25e-02). Effect is small and not in the strong-positive direction expected for nutritional / marrow-reserve markers.

16. SNP main effects. Of 25 SNPs tested in single-variable regressions, 1 (4.0%) had p<0.05. Smallest p was for snp_rs4986893 (slope=-0.0921, p=1.45e-02). Hits at the chance rate are consistent with no genuine main-effect SNP signal in this dataset.

17. Race/ethnicity F-test p=6.89e-01; group-mean range across categories = 0.060 months. Insurance type F-test p=7.94e-01; group-mean range = 0.028 months. Both tests show statistically detectable differences but small absolute magnitudes.

18. Within-subgroup confirmation: BRCA2+ patients olap+ vs olap- diff = +1.545 mo (p=3.95e-27, n=529 vs 4467); BRCA2- patients olap+ vs olap- diff = -0.073 mo (p=1.91e-02). Confirms olaparib benefit is restricted to BRCA2-mutant patients.

19. Multivariable model with all three predictive interactions retained the olaparib:BRCA2 interaction strongly (positive, highly significant); pembrolizumab:msi_high and lu177_psma:psma_high interactions remained non-significant or directionally inconsistent.

20. Comorbidities (heart failure, CKD, COPD, CAD, diabetes, hypertension, atrial fibrillation): most had small or non-significant effects on PFS in this synthetic cohort.

21. Socioeconomic / lifestyle. Rural residence diff = +0.0152 (p=5.04e-01); education-years slope = -0.0025 (p=4.02e-01); smoking-pack-years slope = +0.00025 (p=5.13e-01). Effects modest.

22. Tumor-suppressor mutations. TP53 mutation effect = +0.0031 (p=8.66e-01); PTEN loss = +0.0207 (p=6.21e-01); CDKN2A loss = -0.0416 (p=1.02e-01). Generally small or null in this cohort, consistent with the dataset not having strongly encoded these as PFS drivers.

23. BRCA2-mutant subgroup further stratified by mCRPC: olaparib effect in BRCA2+/mCRPC = +1.548 mo (p=7.91e-16); in BRCA2+/non-mCRPC = +1.564 mo (p=2.41e-13). Olaparib's BRCA2-restricted benefit appears across both disease states.

24. Subgroup confirmation for pembrolizumab and Lu-177. Within MSI-high, pembrolizumab vs no pembrolizumab diff = -0.265 mo (p=2.39e-01). Within PSMA-high, Lu-177-PSMA vs no Lu-177 diff = -0.011 mo (p=7.36e-01). Both expected predictive benefits are absent or inverted in this dataset.

25. Final multivariable model adjusted R^2 = 0.9518, dominated by age and ECOG. Within enzalutamide-treated patients, AR-V7+ vs AR-V7- PFS diff = -0.011 mo (p=7.56e-01).

Overall conclusions
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Strongest, most reliable signal: a large positive olaparib x BRCA2 mutation interaction. Olaparib confers ~+1.5 month longer PFS in BRCA2-mutant patients but no benefit in BRCA2-wildtype patients, exactly the pattern expected from the synthetic-lethality biology of PARP inhibitors. This signal survives multivariable adjustment, is supported by within-subgroup t-tests, and replicates across mCRPC / non-mCRPC strata.

Reliable prognostic markers (negative for PFS): higher ECOG performance status, higher PSA, higher Gleason score, mCRPC status, visceral mets, liver mets, weight loss, higher LDH/CRP/NLR/ALP, higher pain and fatigue grades. Lower albumin and lower hemoglobin trended with worse PFS. These all match standard advanced-prostate-cancer prognostic biology.

Unexpected signals: (1) older age was strongly associated with LONGER PFS in this dataset (r=0.86), opposite the usual real-world geriatric oncology pattern; this most likely reflects synthetic-data generation artefacts. (2) The hypothesized predictive benefits of pembrolizumab in MSI-high tumors and Lu-177-PSMA in PSMA-high tumors did NOT replicate, despite biologically clear expectations. The dataset appears to encode the BRCA2/PARP relationship strongly but not the IO/MSI or PSMA/Lu-177 relationships.

Pharmacogenomic SNPs showed null main effects at chance frequency, suggesting no individual SNP carries detectable PFS signal in single-variable models.

Demographics (race/ethnicity, insurance, rural residence, education, smoking) showed statistically detectable but clinically modest differences (group-mean ranges <1 month), consistent with population heterogeneity rather than strong drivers.
