Analysis summary: ds001_prostate (N = 50,000), outcome = pfs_months
====================================================================

Across 25 iterations I proposed 159 hypotheses and ran 159 statistical
analyses (univariate t-tests, Pearson correlations, ANOVA across
multi-level categoricals, multivariable OLS adjusting for clinical
prognostic factors, treatment x biomarker interaction terms,
biomarker-defined subgroup analyses, treatment x treatment
interactions, and an SNP screen). 33 of 159 analyses (21%) reached
nominal significance at p < 0.05. The story that emerges is that
classical prognostic features (age, ECOG, mCRPC, albumin, weight loss,
PSA, LDH, alk-phos, hemoglobin) drive most of the variance in
PFS, while the treatment landscape is overwhelmingly null in the
unselected cohort with one striking exception: olaparib confers a
large PFS benefit specifically in BRCA2-mutated patients.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Treatment main effects (Iteration 1)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Six treatments were tested individually against PFS:

  enzalutamide   diff = -0.026  p = 0.15   ns
  abiraterone    diff = -0.006  p = 0.78   ns
  docetaxel      diff = -0.017  p = 0.38   ns
  olaparib       diff = +0.094  p = 0.003  *
  Lu-177 PSMA    diff = -0.004  p = 0.87   ns
  pembrolizumab  diff = +0.064  p = 0.14   ns

Only olaparib showed a significant unselected main effect; the
absolute size (~3 days) was very small, suggesting biomarker-defined
subgroup effects.

Castration-resistant disease and ECOG performance status were the

----------------------------------------------------------------------

race_ethnicity   ANOVA F = 0.56  p = 0.69  ns
insurance_type   ANOVA F = 0.34  p = 0.79  ns

The +0.86 correlation between age and PFS is the largest single
correlation in the dataset and is unusual clinically (older patients
typically fare worse). It dominates downstream multivariable models.
Race, insurance, rural residence, education, and smoking were all
null - this cohort shows no meaningful disparity signal on PFS.

8-9. Treatment x biomarker interactions (Iterations 8-9)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
olaparib x brca2_mutation        beta = +1.62  p = 4.3e-61   *  HEADLINE
pembrolizumab x msi_high         beta = -0.34  p = 0.15      ns
lu177_psma x psma_high           beta = -0.02  p = 0.74      ns
enzalutamide x ar_v7_positive    beta = +0.00  p = 0.96      ns
abiraterone x ar_v7_positive     beta = +0.05  p = 0.33      ns

Subgroup PFS:
- olaparib in BRCA2+ (n = 4,996): +1.55 months,  p = 4e-27   *
- olaparib in BRCA2- (n = 45,004): -0.073 months, p = 0.019  (small,
  may reflect confounding because olaparib carries a small overall
  positive effect concentrated in BRCA2+)
- pembrolizumab in MSI-high (n = 1,528): -0.27, p = 0.24, ns
- Lu-177 PSMA in PSMA-high (n = 29,962): -0.01, p = 0.74, ns
- enzalutamide in AR-V7+ (n = 10,038): -0.02, p = 0.56, ns

Of the four canonical treatment x biomarker pairings, ONLY
olaparib x BRCA2 reproduces in this cohort. Pembrolizumab x MSI-high,
Lu-177 x PSMA-high, and AR-V7 x AR-targeted-therapy effects all
failed to materialize - either the synthetic data does not encode
those interactions or sample sizes for some (MSI-high) limit power.

14. SNP screen (Iteration 14)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
25 individual SNPs tested by Pearson correlation with PFS. One
(snp_rs4986893) reached nominal significance (r = -0.011,
p = 0.014). With 25 tests, this is well within chance (Bonferroni
threshold p < 0.002). The SNP panel does NOT contain a meaningful
PFS signal in this cohort.

15. Multivariable prognostic model (Iteration 15)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
OLS: pfs_months ~ age + ecog + mcrpc + visceral_mets + albumin +
ldh + psa + weight_loss + alk_phos + hemoglobin
n = 50,000, R^2 = 0.9465.

Coefficients:
  ecog_ps            -1.151  p ~ 0  *
  mcrpc              -0.376  p ~ 0  *
  age_years          +0.174  p ~ 0  *
  albumin_g_dl       +0.479  p ~ 0  *

A 10-feature classical-prognostic OLS already accounts for ~95% of
the variance in PFS - the dataset's PFS is heavily prognosis-driven
rather than treatment-driven. All four targeted hypotheses were
supported.

18. Adjusted olaparib effect within BRCA2 strata

After adjusting for age, ECOG, mCRPC, albumin, LDH, weight loss:
  In BRCA2+: adj-beta(olaparib) = +1.43,  p = 9e-273  *

  (olaparib AND BRCA2+) OR (pembrolizumab AND MSI-high) OR

diff = +0.143, p = 7.8e-06  *

matched_therapy becomes non-significant once olaparib x BRCA2 is

The matched_therapy aggregate adds nothing once the

the only treatment x biomarker pairing in this cohort that

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Synthesis and overall conclusions
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Hypotheses supported (top-tier evidence):
1. PFS is strongly determined by stage and prognostic features:
   age (+), ECOG (-), mCRPC (-), albumin (+), weight loss (-),
   PSA (-), LDH (-), alk-phos (-).
2. olaparib improves PFS in BRCA2-mutated patients only,
   with a large effect (+1.55 months unadjusted, +1.43 months
   adjusted), and this is robust across mCRPC strata and
   adjustment for prognostic confounders.

Hypotheses refuted:
1. pembrolizumab is NOT preferentially effective in MSI-high
   patients in this cohort (null interaction and null subgroup
   effect).
2. Lu-177 PSMA is NOT preferentially effective in PSMA-high
   patients (null interaction and null subgroup effect).
3. AR-V7 status does NOT modify the effect of enzalutamide or
   abiraterone (null interactions).
4. Treatment-treatment combinations (enzalutamide+abiraterone,
   docetaxel+enzalutamide, olaparib+abiraterone in BRCA2+) do
   NOT show synergy.
5. Demographics (race, insurance, rural, education, smoking) do
   NOT predict PFS.
6. Comorbidities, symptoms, vitals, and 13 genomic alterations
   (incl. TP53, PTEN-loss, PIK3CA) do NOT predict PFS.
7. The 25-SNP panel has no meaningful association with PFS
   beyond chance.
8. Gleason score, liver mets, and bone mets are NOT prognostic
   for PFS in this metastatic cohort.

Overall: this dataset is best summarized as a strongly
prognosis-driven cohort with one true precision-oncology signal
(olaparib in BRCA2+) embedded within an otherwise treatment-null
landscape. Aggregate "matched therapy" framing is misleading
because the matched-pair signal is entirely driven by
olaparib x BRCA2; the other "matched" pairs (pembro/MSI,
Lu-177/PSMA, AR-V7/AR-pathway) contribute no benefit. A
classical 10-feature prognostic OLS already explains ~95% of
PFS variance, and adding the olaparib x BRCA2 interaction lifts
this to ~95.2%.
