Analysis Summary - Dataset ds001_aml (50,000 patients, 124 features, binary outcome objective_response)
=========================================================================================================

Overall response rate: 16.9% (8,452 of 50,000 patients).

Approach
--------
Across 10 iterations I (1) screened all 124 features univariately against objective_response (chi-square / Welch t-test / Spearman rank correlation), (2) fit a fully-saturated multivariable logistic regression with continuous predictors standardized, (3) tested two-way interactions among the strongest predictors, (4) probed sociodemographic disparities (race=feature_005, insurance=feature_087) with crude and adjusted analyses, (5) examined nonlinear (quadratic) shapes for the candidate "age" variable feature_078, and (6) built a parsimonious 9-feature model and assessed discrimination.

Iteration walkthrough
---------------------

Iteration 1 - Univariate screen.
  - Binary features: 80 binary predictors tested; feature_035 strongest (response 22.1% if 1 vs 16.5% if 0; chi-square p=3.5e-18). Several others (feature_093, feature_121, feature_014) reached p<0.05 with much smaller effects.
  - Continuous features: 36 features tested. feature_011 strongest (mean 3.38 in responders vs 3.95 in non-responders, p=7.2e-39). Other strong negative associations: feature_006 (p=9.5e-20), feature_063 (p=4.4e-7), feature_092 (p=1.3e-5). One positive: feature_099 (p=1.3e-6).
  - Ordinal features: 7 multi-level predictors tested. feature_057 (3 levels: 0,1,2) had Spearman rho=-0.095, p=7.7e-100 - by an order of magnitude the strongest single signal in the dataset.

Iteration 2 - Probing the headline predictors.
  - feature_057 produces a strikingly monotonic gradient: response 21.3% (level 0) -> 15.5% (level 1) -> 11.2% (level 2). Logit slope -0.384/level (p<<0.001). Behaves as a risk-stratification variable analogous to a cytogenetic / molecular risk class.
  - feature_035 (binary, n=3,624 with value 1) carries OR ~1.44 favorable effect (logit coef +0.364, p=4.2e-18).
  - feature_011 falls monotonically across responders (Q1 18.6%, Q2 16.8%, Q4 13.6%); slope -0.040/unit.
  - feature_006 (range 20-100) inversely related (decile means falling from ~19% to ~14%); slope -0.0057/unit.

Iteration 3 - Multivariable logistic regression on all 124 features.
  - Four predictors retain very large independent effects (all p<1e-15): feature_057 (-0.385/level), feature_011 (-0.156/SD), feature_006 (-0.111/SD), feature_035 (+0.360).
  - Additional independent signal: feature_099 (+0.059/SD, p=1.1e-6), feature_063 (-0.066/SD, p=1.7e-6), feature_092 (-0.059/SD, p=8.8e-6), feature_093 (+0.131, p=2.3e-3).
  - 9-feature parsimonious model AUC = 0.598. Pseudo-R^2 of full 124-feature model = 0.021. The headline predictors capture genuine signal but most patient-level variability in objective_response is unexplained by available features.

Iteration 4 - Interactions among the top predictors.
  Six 2-way interactions tested:
  - feature_057 x feature_035: coef +0.027, p=0.67. Stratified rates: feature_035 raises response by ~6 percentage points uniformly across feature_057 strata. No effect-modification.
  - feature_057 x feature_011: coef -0.002, p=0.71.
  - feature_057 x feature_006: coef -0.0009, p=0.33.
  - feature_035 x feature_011: coef -0.014, p=0.23 (direction consistent with weak attenuation).
  - feature_006 x feature_011: p=0.09 (borderline).
  - feature_011 x feature_063: p=0.09 (borderline).
  None reach p<0.05; effects are well-described as additive on the log-odds scale.

Iteration 5 - Race and insurance disparities.
  - Crude response by race (feature_005): white 17.3%, hispanic 16.7%, asian 16.3%, black 15.7%, other 14.7%. Chi-square p=0.0096.
  - After adjustment for feature_057, feature_011, feature_006, feature_035, and feature_078: Black vs White log-odds = -0.108 (95% CI -0.186 to -0.033, p=0.005); Other vs White = -0.195 (p=0.031); Asian and Hispanic not significant.
  - Insurance (feature_087): crude rates 16.6-17.2%, chi-square p=0.54. No association.

Iteration 6 - Race x feature interactions and access disparities.
  - Race x feature_057 interactions all p>0.28 - the cytogenetic-risk gradient is essentially constant across race groups.
  - Prevalence of feature_035=1 essentially identical across races (7.2-7.5%) - no evidence of differential access in this anonymized cohort.
  - Race x feature_035: response benefit appears slightly larger in white patients (16.8 -> 23.3%) than in others, but interaction not statistically significant given small sub-cell sizes.

Iteration 7 - Continuous-feature shapes and the absence of an age effect.
  - feature_078 (range 18-92, mean 68 - the natural candidate for "age in years"): linear logit slope +0.0004/yr (p=0.72); quadratic term also non-significant (p=0.31). Crude response varies from 14.1% in the youngest band (18-40) to 16.7-17.2% in older bands - no monotone trend. Striking absence of an age effect on response.
  - feature_099 (range 1.7-5.5, mean 3.8): monotone increase across quartiles (Q1 15.8% -> Q4 17.8%); adjusted slope +0.059/SD.
  - feature_063 (heavy-tailed, range 0.03-300): rates flat across Q1-Q3, drop in Q4 (15.6%) - threshold-like inverse association.
  - feature_092 (range 0.5-500): smooth monotone decline across quartiles (18.0% -> 15.6%); adjusted slope -0.059/SD.

Iteration 8 - Additional binary features in adjusted models.
  Holding the top 5 covariates fixed:
  - feature_093 +0.124 (p=0.0037) - independent positive predictor.
  - feature_014 +0.059 (p=0.015).
  - feature_121 -0.089 (p=0.048) - modest negative.
  - feature_012 -0.048 (p=0.050).
  - Several borderline (feature_111 p=0.054, feature_030 p=0.059).
  - feature_035 x feature_093 interaction p=0.45 - effects approximately additive.

Iteration 9 - Combined risk grouping and discrimination.
  - 6-cell joint stratification by (feature_057, feature_035) yields response rates from 27.0% (best, feature_057=0 & feature_035=1) to 10.9% (worst, feature_057=2 & feature_035=0) - a 2.5-fold spread.
  - 9-feature multivariable model achieves AUC=0.598.
  - feature_011 x feature_063 interaction p=0.09 - non-significant trend toward super-additive harm.

Iteration 10 - Synthesis.
  - Four predictors dominate the multivariable picture (all p<1e-15): feature_057 (3-level risk-stratification gradient), feature_011 (continuous, inverse), feature_006 (continuous, inverse), feature_035 (binary, favorable).
  - Three further continuous biomarkers (feature_099, feature_063, feature_092) and two binary features (feature_093, feature_014) carry independent though smaller signal.
  - No 2-way interactions among the top predictors reach significance; effects are well-described as additive on the log-odds scale.
  - Residual Black-vs-White disparity (~10% lower adjusted odds, p=0.005) persists after adjustment for clinical predictors, while neither feature_078 (likely age) nor feature_087 (insurance) shows independent association.

Conclusions
-----------
1. Response in this AML cohort is dominated by a risk-stratification ordinal (feature_057) and a single favorable binary (feature_035), supplemented by continuous biomarkers feature_011 and feature_006. These four together drive the bulk of explainable variation; their effects are large (each p<1e-15 in multivariable analysis).
2. Effects are largely additive on the log-odds scale - no significant interaction among the top predictors. Joint stratification (feature_057 x feature_035) produces a clinically useful 2.5-fold spread in response rates (10.9% to 27.0%).
3. There is a small but statistically significant residual disparity in objective_response for Black patients (and patients in 'other' race category) compared with White patients that is NOT explained by measured clinical features in this dataset.
4. Notably absent: any independent effect of feature_078 (the natural "age" candidate) or insurance category on objective_response after adjustment.
5. Despite identifying many real signals, overall discrimination is modest (AUC 0.60). Most patient-level variability in objective_response remains unexplained by the available 124 features, suggesting that important biological or treatment heterogeneity is not captured in this dataset.
