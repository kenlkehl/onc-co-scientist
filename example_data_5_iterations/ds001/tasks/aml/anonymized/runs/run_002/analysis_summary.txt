Analysis summary — dataset ds001_aml (n=50,000; objective_response prevalence 16.9%)
========================================================================================

Approach
--------
We carried out 10 iterations of propose-test-refine. The first iterations focused on
ranking the 125 anonymized features by their univariate relationship with
objective_response and characterizing the top main effects. Later iterations turned to
demographic disparities, joint clinical adjustment, and finally a search for
biomarker-style interactions, which surfaced a striking three-way interaction that
dominates the cohort-wide signal of one of the strongest "main effects" we had
identified earlier.


Iteration-by-iteration findings
-------------------------------

Iteration 1 — top main effects screened from a univariate scan.
* feature_057 (3-level ordinal, plausibly a performance-status-like score): response
  rates fall monotonically with level (21.3% / 15.5% / 11.2%); OR per level = 0.68,
  p = 1.8e-97. Strongest single predictor in the dataset. SUPPORTED.
* feature_011 (continuous, 0-23.6): higher values associate with lower response
  (per-SD OR 0.86, p=7e-35). SUPPORTED.
* feature_006 (continuous, 20-100): per-SD OR 0.90, p=1e-19, with a clean monotonic
  quartile gradient (19.0% -> 14.7%). SUPPORTED.

Iteration 2 — additional main effects.
* feature_035 (binary, prevalence 7.3%): response rate 22.1% if =1 vs 16.5% if =0,
  OR = 1.44, p=4e-18. SUPPORTED.
* feature_092 (per-SD OR 0.95, p=2e-05), feature_063 (per-SD OR 0.94, p=3e-06), and
  feature_099 (per-SD OR 1.06, p=1e-06) all confirmed in the predicted directions.
  feature_099 was notable for being the only top continuous feature with a positive
  association.

Iteration 3 — demographic disparities.
* Race (feature_005): unadjusted response rates ranged from 14.7% (other) to 17.3%
  (white); overall chi-square p=0.0096 but pairwise contrasts vs. asian were not
  individually significant (max coefficient p=0.13).
* Insurance (feature_087): no detectable variation (chi-square p=0.78).
* Age (feature_078): null main effect (per-SD OR 1.00, p=0.72).

Iteration 4 — joint adjustment.
* In a multivariable logistic model containing feature_057, feature_011, feature_006,
  feature_092, feature_063, feature_099, feature_035, age, race, and insurance, all six
  named clinical features retained their univariate direction and remained highly
  significant. Race coefficients all attenuated to non-significant (p>=0.13); insurance
  coefficients were uniformly null (p>=0.55). The unadjusted univariate race signal
  was fully explained by clinical features. REFUTES a residual race-based disparity in
  objective_response in this cohort.

Iteration 5 — testing modification of the feature_035 effect.
* Across feature_057 strata: absolute risk differences for feature_035 shrink (+6.2,
  +5.4, +4.4 pp), but the OR is essentially constant. Multiplicative-scale interaction
  test p=0.71. NOT SUPPORTED.
* Across age (>=75 vs <75): OR 1.49 vs 1.29; interaction p=0.12. Directionally smaller
  effect in elderly but not statistically significant.
* Across race: OR ranges 1.31-1.50; LR test p=0.73. NOT SUPPORTED.
  These analyses argue against demographic effect modification of feature_035.

Iteration 6 — systematic interaction discovery.
* Scanning 72 binary features (prevalence 2-98%) for two-way interaction with feature_035,
  two stand out far above the rest:
  - feature_035 x feature_027: LR=139.8 on 1 df, interaction beta=+1.05.
    In feature_027=1 patients, response rate is 35.2% if feature_035=1 vs 16.0% if 0;
    in feature_027=0 patients, response rate is 16.8% if =1 vs 16.7% if =0.
  - feature_035 x feature_014: LR=74.3 on 1 df, interaction beta=+0.73.
    In feature_014=1 patients, response 29.8% if feature_035=1 vs 16.4% if 0; nearly no
    difference in feature_014=0 patients.
  These look like biomarker-treatment-style modifiers — feature_035 is essentially
  inert by itself but becomes powerful in the presence of feature_027 and/or
  feature_014.

Iteration 7 — joint two-way interaction model.
* Including both interactions simultaneously, feature_035 main effect when 027=014=0
  becomes mildly NEGATIVE (beta=-0.33, p=4e-06), confirming that the apparent positive
  main effect from Iteration 2 is entirely interaction-driven. Both interaction terms
  remain highly significant (p<1e-15).

Iteration 8 — three-way interaction.
* The saturated three-way model exposes the true structure: response rates in 7 of 8
  cells of the (feature_035, feature_027, feature_014) cube cluster between 15.9% and
  17.3%. Only the (027=1, 014=1, 035=1) cell departs: response rate = 62.1% in n=427
  patients. Three-way interaction coefficient beta=+2.14, p<1e-28. The two-way
  interactions seen in Iteration 6 collapse toward zero in this saturated model — they
  were proxies for this three-way synergy. SUPPORTED.

Iteration 9 — full final adjusted model.
* With the triple interaction plus all main clinical predictors and demographics:
    feature_057 (level 1 vs 0): OR 0.68 (p<1e-50)
    feature_057 (level 2 vs 0): OR 0.46 (p<1e-77)
    feature_011 per unit:        OR 0.96 (p<1e-30)
    feature_006 per unit:        OR 0.99 (p<1e-19)
    feature_063 per unit:        OR 0.99 (p=1e-06)
    feature_092 per unit:        OR 0.998 (p=1e-05)
    feature_099 per unit:        OR 1.13 (p=4e-07)
    feature_093:                 OR 1.14 (p=0.002)
    feature_121:                 OR 0.92 (p=0.05, borderline)
    age (feature_078):           null (p=0.78)
    race, insurance:             all null (p>=0.13).
  A residual race or insurance disparity in objective_response is NOT detectable in
  this cohort.

Iteration 10 — clinical interpretation of the triple-positive group.
* The (feature_027=1 AND feature_014=1 AND feature_035=1) subgroup is small (n=427,
  0.85%) but has 62.1% response vs 16.5% in the rest of the cohort (univariate OR
  8.30, p<1e-100, risk difference +45.6 pp). When restricted to patients NOT in the
  (feature_027=1, feature_014=1) genotype combination, feature_035 has no detectable
  effect (OR 0.96, p=0.5). The cohort-level OR of 1.44 for feature_035 (Iteration 2)
  is therefore almost entirely driven by this small triple-positive subgroup, which
  has the empirical signature of a precision-oncology biomarker-treatment match in
  AML.


Overall conclusions
-------------------

1. Performance-status-like variable (feature_057) is the dominant prognostic factor.
   Each step from level 0 to 1 to 2 reduces response odds by roughly one third (OR ~0.68
   per level) and the effect is monotonic and very precisely estimated.

2. Several continuous laboratory variables (feature_011, feature_006, feature_092,
   feature_063) are independently inversely associated with response, while feature_099
   is positively associated; effect sizes are individually modest (per-SD OR within
   0.86-1.06) but all retain significance jointly with feature_057 and the other
   markers.

3. Age (feature_078) has no detectable main effect on objective response in this
   cohort, even though feature_078 effect modification of feature_035 is directionally
   present (smaller benefit in elderly) and would warrant follow-up in a larger sample.

4. Apparent feature_035 main effect (OR 1.44) is misleading: it reflects a strongly
   synergistic three-way interaction with feature_027 and feature_014. In the
   ~0.85% of patients who are positive on all three, response rate is 62.1% vs
   ~16.5% in the rest of the cohort. This pattern is consistent with a targeted
   therapy whose efficacy depends on the simultaneous presence of two biomarker
   features (or, equivalently, a biomarker that is meaningful only in the context of
   a particular treatment combination).

5. Race and insurance disparities are not present after adjustment for clinical
   features. A small unadjusted race signal (chi-square p=0.0096; max difference 2.6
   pp) disappears under joint adjustment, suggesting that any apparent disparity in
   the marginal data reflects differences in case mix on the prognostic features
   identified above, not a direct race-level disparity in response.

6. Effect modification of feature_035 by performance status (feature_057), race, and
   age was not statistically supported on the multiplicative scale, so the
   biomarker-treatment-like interaction story is specific to feature_027 and
   feature_014 rather than to demographic strata.
