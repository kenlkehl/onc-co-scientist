Oncology Dataset Analysis Summary - ds001_aml
==============================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients, 125 features (80 binary, 35 continuous, 10
multi-category including race [feature_005] and insurance [feature_087]),
single binary outcome objective_response (overall rate 16.90%, 8,452/50,000).

The analysis proceeded in 10 iterations of propose-test-refine. The
narrative below walks through the hypotheses I explored, what the
statistical evidence showed, and which were supported.

ITERATION 1 - UNIVARIATE SCREEN
------------------------------------------------
Hypotheses: at least one binary, one continuous, and one multi-category
feature shows a univariate association with objective_response.

Findings (all supported):
- Binary: feature_035 (prevalence 7.2%) was the strongest signal, with
  response rate 22.1% in feature_035-positive vs 16.5% in feature_035-
  negative patients (chi-square p=3.5e-18). feature_093 and feature_121
  were weakly significant; most others were null.
- Continuous: feature_011 was the strongest predictor with responders
  showing a markedly lower mean (3.38) than non-responders (3.95;
  Welch t-test p=7.2e-39). feature_006 (responders 58.2 vs non-responders
  60.3, p=9.5e-20), feature_063, feature_099, and feature_092 were also
  highly significant. Higher feature_011, feature_006, feature_063, and
  feature_092 each predicted non-response; higher feature_099 predicted
  response.
- Multi-category: feature_057, a 3-level ordinal-appearing variable, had
  the dominant signal (response rates 21.3%, 15.5%, 11.2% for levels 0,
  1, 2; chi-square p=5.5e-98). Race (feature_005) had a smaller signal:
  white 17.3%, hispanic 16.7%, asian 16.3%, black 15.7%, other 14.7%,
  p=0.0096. Insurance (feature_087) and the other multi-category
  features showed no association.

ITERATION 2 - MULTIVARIABLE LOGISTIC REGRESSION
------------------------------------------------
Hypotheses: feature_057 confers an ordinal-linear effect on log-odds
of response; the candidate predictors retain independent associations
under mutual adjustment; age (feature_078) is independently prognostic.

Findings:
- feature_057 (ordinal): beta = -0.384 per level (OR 0.681), p = 1.8e-97.
  Each step up in feature_057 reduces response odds by ~32%. Supported.
- All seven candidate predictors retained independent significance in
  a joint logistic model (feature_057, feature_011, feature_006,
  feature_035, feature_099, feature_063, feature_092 all with adjusted
  p < 1e-5). Supported.
- Age (feature_078): adjusted p = 0.764. Quartile-stratified response
  rates (16.7%, 17.4%, 16.6%, 16.9%) were essentially flat. NOT
  supported - age is not independently associated with response in
  this cohort, contrary to the typical AML prior.

ITERATION 3 - INTERACTIONS BETWEEN feature_057 AND TOP PREDICTORS
------------------------------------------------
Hypotheses: feature_057's effect varies across feature_035, feature_011,
or feature_006 (i.e., interactions exist).

Findings:
- All three interaction tests were null (p = 0.674, 0.710, 0.330).
  feature_057 confers a main-effect-only prognostic gradient that is
  approximately constant across the other strong predictors. NOT
  supported - prognosis from feature_057 generalizes uniformly.

ITERATION 4 - feature_035 BIOMARKER VALIDATION
------------------------------------------------
Hypotheses: feature_035's positive effect appears within every level of
feature_057 (consistent direction); after adjusting for the dominant
clinical predictors, feature_035 retains independent positive effect.

Findings (both supported):
- feature_035 lifts response rate by approximately 5 percentage points
  within every feature_057 stratum (level 0: 27.0% vs 20.8%; level 1:
  20.5% vs 15.1%; level 2: 15.3% vs 10.9%). Direction and magnitude
  consistent.
- Adjusted feature_035: beta = +0.357, OR = 1.43, p = 2.9e-17.
  Independent positive association robust to clinical confounders.

ITERATION 5 - DEMOGRAPHIC SUBGROUP ANALYSES
------------------------------------------------
Hypotheses: race (feature_005) is associated unadjusted; the gap is
explained by clinical confounding; insurance (feature_087) is associated;
feature_035's effect differs by race.

Findings:
- Unadjusted race effect was small but real (chi-square p = 0.0096),
  with the white-black gap = +1.54 pp.
- After adjustment for clinical predictors (feature_057, feature_011,
  feature_006, feature_035, feature_099, feature_063, feature_092), no
  race coefficient reached p < 0.05 (white p = 0.13, black p = 0.63,
  hispanic p = 0.45, other p = 0.25). The unadjusted gap is explained
  by clinical confounding - e.g., black patients have higher mean
  feature_011 (3.97 vs 3.82 in white), and feature_011 is the strongest
  negative continuous predictor. SUPPORTED.
- Insurance: chi-square p = 0.54 unadjusted, all coefficients p > 0.55
  adjusted. NOT supported.
- feature_035 effect within white (beta = +0.41, p = 2.6e-15) vs black
  (beta = +0.29, p = 0.025): direction concordant; the formal race x
  feature_035 interaction was not significant. NOT supported - no
  reliable race differential in feature_035's effect.

ITERATION 6 - NONLINEARITY OF CONTINUOUS PREDICTORS
------------------------------------------------
Hypotheses: feature_011 and feature_092 have non-linear (quadratic)
relationships; the lowest feature_011 quintile has > 50% higher response
rate than the highest.

Findings:
- feature_011: LR p = 0.070 - borderline non-significant; linear model
  adequate.
- feature_092: LR p = 8.3e-4 for quadratic term - mild U-shape, but
  the linear negative trend dominates.
- feature_011 quintiles spanned 18.6% (low) -> 13.3% (high) response,
  a 1.40x ratio - directionally confirmed but smaller than the
  predicted 1.50x.

ITERATION 7 - PAIRWISE INTERACTIONS AMONG TOP PREDICTORS
------------------------------------------------
Hypotheses: at least one pairwise interaction among top-10 predictors
reaches p < 0.001; specifically, feature_035 x feature_014 is positive.

Findings (both supported):
- feature_035 x feature_014 had the strongest interaction signal:
  beta = +0.730, p = 7.7e-18.
- 2x2 cell rates: feature_014 had no effect when feature_035 = 0 (16.4%
  vs 16.6%, p = 0.62) but doubled response rate in feature_035-positive
  patients (29.8% vs 17.1%, OR = 2.05, p = 9e-19). A clean qualitative
  interaction, not just a magnitude shift.

ITERATION 8 - COMPREHENSIVE BINARY x BINARY INTERACTION SCREEN
------------------------------------------------
3,038 binary x binary interaction tests run.

Findings:
- Only THREE interaction pairs reached the Bonferroni threshold of
  1.6e-5, and ALL THREE involved the same triplet:
  feature_035 x feature_027 (beta = +1.046, p = 1.1e-32);
  feature_035 x feature_014 (beta = +0.730, p = 7.7e-18);
  feature_027 x feature_014 (beta = +0.261, p = 6.6e-7).
- The 2x2x2 cross-tab over feature_014 / feature_027 / feature_035
  showed seven cells with response rates between 15.9% and 17.3%, but
  the cell with feature_014 = 1 AND feature_027 = 1 AND feature_035 = 1
  had response rate 62.06% (n = 427, 265 responders). The 3-way
  interaction term in a fully decomposed logistic model: beta = +2.196,
  z = +11.4, p < 1e-29. Likelihood ratio test: chi-square = 137.4,
  p < 1e-31. STRONGLY SUPPORTED - this is the dominant finding in the
  dataset.
- After full adjustment for the dominant prognostic predictors
  (feature_057, feature_011, feature_006, feature_099, feature_063,
  feature_092), the 3-way coefficient was essentially unchanged.

ITERATION 9 - VALIDATION IN FINAL ADJUSTED MODEL
------------------------------------------------
Final model included: 6 main-effect prognostic predictors, the three
biomarker / treatment binaries (feature_014, feature_027, feature_035)
and all their 2-way + 3-way products, age (feature_078), feature_007,
feature_093, feature_121, race dummies, and insurance dummies (25 terms
total, pseudo-R^2 = 0.0275).

Findings:
- Prognostic predictors retained: feature_057 p < 1e-95, feature_011
  p < 1e-34, feature_006 p < 1e-19, feature_099 p = 3e-7, feature_063
  p = 7e-7, feature_092 p = 2e-5. SUPPORTED.
- No demographic covariate (race, insurance, age, feature_007) reached
  p < 0.05 after adjustment. SUPPORTED.
- Triple-positive (feature_014 = feature_027 = feature_035 = 1) RR =
  62.06% (n = 427); rest of cohort RR = 16.51% (n = 49,573); absolute
  difference = +45.55 pp. The triple-positive subgroup constitutes 0.85%
  of the cohort.

ITERATION 10 - SYNTHESIS
------------------------------------------------
Pulling the findings together yields a clean clinical narrative
consistent with a real AML cohort:

(1) feature_057 is a prognostic risk-stratification variable with three
    ordered levels and a near-monotonic ~5 pp drop in response rate per
    level (level 0 -> 1 -> 2: 21.3% -> 15.5% -> 11.2%). The effect is
    additive in log-odds across all subgroups tested.

(2) feature_011 is the strongest continuous prognostic marker in the
    dataset (responders 3.38 vs non-responders 3.95). The relationship
    is negative-monotonic and approximately linear in log-odds.

(3) Several other continuous markers contribute independently:
    feature_006 (negative), feature_099 (positive), feature_063
    (negative), feature_092 (negative).

(4) feature_035 functions as a predictive biomarker (prevalence 7.2%).
    Alone it provides modest benefit (~5 pp absolute), but it interacts
    strongly with feature_014 and feature_027.

(5) The dominant signal in the dataset is a three-way synergy:
    feature_014, feature_027, and feature_035 must all be simultaneously
    positive to drive a dramatic response benefit (62.1% vs ~17%
    elsewhere). A natural reading is that feature_014 and feature_027
    are two treatment components whose joint efficacy is unlocked by
    biomarker feature_035 - consistent with a targeted-therapy + sensitizer
    combination requiring a specific molecular subtype.

(6) Demographic factors (race, insurance, age, feature_007) show no
    independent association with response after clinical adjustment.
    A small unadjusted race difference (white-black gap +1.54 pp) is
    fully explained by differential distribution of clinical predictors,
    primarily feature_011.

(7) None of the multi-category features beyond feature_057 (feature_086,
    feature_096, feature_018, feature_045, feature_042, feature_064,
    feature_125) showed a meaningful association.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
------------------------------------------------
- Treatment-biomarker relationship: the most actionable finding is the
  triple synergy of feature_014 x feature_027 x feature_035. Patients
  positive for all three have response rates ~3.7x the cohort baseline;
  patients lacking any one of the three components do not benefit.
- Outcome heterogeneity is dominated by feature_057 risk strata and a
  handful of continuous markers (feature_011, feature_006, feature_099,
  feature_063, feature_092). Together with the triple synergy, these
  account for essentially all of the explainable variance (LLR p-value
  for the full model is < 1e-249, pseudo-R^2 = 0.028).
- Demographic disparities in raw response are NOT independent of
  clinical confounders. After adjusting for the prognostic markers,
  no race, insurance, age, or feature_007 effect remains.
- Refuted hypotheses: age effect; insurance effect; race x feature_035
  effect modification; feature_057 interaction with other top
  predictors; meaningful nonlinearity in feature_011.
- Supported hypotheses: feature_057 ordinal trend; feature_035 main
  effect; feature_011, feature_006, feature_063, feature_092 negative
  continuous effects; feature_099 positive continuous effect;
  feature_035 x feature_014 two-way interaction; the three-way
  feature_014 x feature_027 x feature_035 synergy.
