Analysis summary for ds001_aml (50,000 patients, objective_response outcome)

Overall objective_response rate (ORR) in the cohort was 16.9%.

Iteration 1 — main effects screen.
We screened the six listed treatments, the major AML-associated mutations and disease features, and basic demographics for marginal association with objective_response. Two main effects were unambiguous: ECOG performance status of 2 was strongly associated with lower ORR (11.2% vs 17.9%, chi-square p = 5.2e-46), and idh1_mutation was strongly associated with higher ORR (22.1% vs 16.5%, p = 3.5e-18). A small positive main effect was seen for treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine (17.3% on vs 16.6% off, p = 0.031). Notably, the FLT3 inhibitors (midostaurin, gilteritinib), the IDH inhibitors (ivosidenib, enasidenib) and 7+3 chemotherapy showed no significant overall main effect on ORR, and tp53_mutation, complex_karyotype, secondary_aml, npm1_mutation, age, and sex also had no significant main effect. These null treatment main effects motivated subgroup-interaction testing.

Iteration 2 — targeted treatment x mutation interactions.
The strongest finding was that treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine x idh1_mutation showed a large positive interaction: ORR was 29.8% in idh1+/venAza+ patients (n=1,435) versus 17.1% in idh1+/venAza- patients (n=2,189), a +12.6-percentage-point absolute benefit (interaction log-OR = +0.730, p = 7.7e-18). A weaker but significant positive interaction was seen for treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine x npm1_mutation (interaction log-OR = +0.261, p = 6.7e-7). treatment_7plus3 x complex_karyotype showed a significant negative interaction (interaction log-OR = -0.213, p = 1.6e-3): in CK+ patients, 7+3 was associated with a 2.3-pp lower ORR. Surprisingly, treatment_ivosidenib x idh1_mutation was negative (p = 0.027) — ivosidenib was associated with lower ORR within idh1+ patients than no ivosidenib (17.4% vs 22.5%) — and no FLT3-inhibitor x flt3 mutation interaction was significant. These findings did not match the prior expectation that ivosidenib and the FLT3 inhibitors would be selectively beneficial in their molecular target subgroups.

Iteration 3 — adverse-marker, fitness, and ECOG x treatment interactions.
TP53 mutation, complex_karyotype, secondary_aml, and ECOG status did not modify the effects of most treatments. Two interactions were nominally supported: treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine x unfit_for_intensive (+0.104, p = 0.036; ven+aza benefit larger in unfit patients) and treatment_gilteritinib x unfit_for_intensive (+0.135, p = 0.048). A nominally significant negative interaction was observed for treatment_ivosidenib x npm1_mutation (-0.225, p = 0.024).

Iteration 4 — continuous lab and clinical predictors of response.
Strong negative continuous predictors of ORR (per-SD log-OR shown):
  weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.155 (p = 7.4e-35)
  blast_pct_marrow:    -0.109 (p = 1.2e-19)
  crp_mg_l:            -0.064 (p = 2.8e-6)
  wbc_k_per_ul:        -0.056 (p = 1.9e-5)
Strong positive continuous predictor:
  albumin_g_dl:        +0.058 (p = 1.4e-6)
ldh_u_l, age_years, hemoglobin_g_dl, and most other labs were not significantly associated with ORR. The pattern recapitulates classic markers of disease burden, systemic inflammation, and nutritional status.

Iteration 5 — continuous x treatment interactions.
We found no significant age x treatment, ECOG x treatment, or lab x treatment interactions. The benefit of venetoclax_azacitidine and 7+3 did not vary meaningfully with age, ECOG, blast %, weight loss, albumin, CRP, or WBC. Treatment selection therefore appears to be driven by mutation status rather than continuous biomarkers in this cohort.

Iteration 6 — demographics, social determinants, comorbidities.
ORR differed significantly across race_ethnicity (chi-square p = 9.6e-3): white 17.3%, hispanic 16.7%, asian 16.3%, black 15.7%, "other" 14.7%. Insurance type and rural_residence had no association with ORR. Among comorbidities, only atrial_fibrillation reached nominal significance (15.6% vs 17.0%, p = 0.025); HIV positivity showed a non-significant trend toward lower ORR (14.1% vs 16.9%, p = 0.11). Solid-tumor markers (her2_amplification, met_exon14_skipping, ros1_fusion, braf_v600e, msi_high, liver_mets, bone_mets, etc.) showed no association with ORR — consistent with their lack of biological relevance in AML.

Iteration 7 — multivariable logistic model.
Adjusting jointly for age, ECOG, lab markers, mutations, and treatments, the dominant prognostic signals were: ecog_2 (adjusted log-OR -0.554, p = 1.1e-45), weight_loss_pct_6mo per SD (-0.157, p = 1.8e-35), blast_pct_marrow per SD (-0.111, p = 3.3e-20), idh1_mutation (+0.365, p = 8.2e-18), albumin per SD (+0.059, p = 8.5e-7), crp per SD (-0.066, p = 1.5e-6), wbc per SD (-0.057, p = 1.6e-5), and the only significant treatment main effect was treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine (+0.058, p = 0.017). When the venetoclax_azacitidine x idh1, x npm1, and 7+3 x complex_karyotype interaction terms were added, all three were significant (log-ORs +0.760, +0.270, -0.211; p = 6.1e-19, 3.3e-7, 2.0e-3 respectively), confirming the interaction findings remain after multivariable adjustment.

Iteration 8 — SNP scan.
Across the 24 single-nucleotide polymorphisms, no SNP survived a Bonferroni threshold of 0.05/24 = 2.08e-3. The strongest individual signal was snp_rs1050828 (per-SD log-OR +0.032, p = 5.3e-3). snp_rs2032582 reached nominal p = 0.049. No genetic predisposition signal of practical importance was identified.

Iteration 9 — refined / three-way interactions.
The single most striking finding of the entire analysis emerged here: the three-way interaction treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine x idh1_mutation x npm1_mutation. ORR in patients with idh1=1, npm1=1, and on venetoclax_azacitidine was 62.1% (n=427), versus 16.8% in idh1+/npm1+ patients off venetoclax_azacitidine (n=620), and ~16-17% across all other strata. The interaction log-OR was +2.144 (p = 3.0e-29). Importantly, in idh1+/npm1- patients, venetoclax_azacitidine showed no benefit (16.1% vs 17.3%), and in npm1+/idh1- patients it showed only a small benefit, indicating the response is concentrated in the co-mutated genotype rather than attributable to either mutation alone. By contrast, race_ethnicity x venetoclax_azacitidine interaction was not significant (LR p = 0.15) and sex x venetoclax_azacitidine was null (p = 0.68). The negative 7+3 x complex_karyotype effect was similar across age strata (-2.2 pp in age >=65 and -2.5 pp in age <65 in CK+).

Iteration 10 — confirmatory final model.
A final multivariable logistic model including the three-way veno_idh1_npm1 interaction, the two two-way veno_idh1 and veno_npm1 interactions, the 7+3 x complex_karyotype interaction, and main effects of mutations, treatments and key labs confirmed:
  veno_idh1_npm1 log-OR = +2.199 (OR = 9.01, p = 3.0e-30)
  After conditioning on the three-way term, the two-way veno_idh1 (-0.053, p = 0.64) and veno_npm1 (+0.038, p = 0.50) interactions became non-significant — consistent with the response benefit residing specifically in the idh1+/npm1+ co-mutated subgroup.
  s7p3_ck log-OR = -0.207 (OR = 0.81, p = 2.5e-3) — a residual harm of 7+3 in complex karyotype.
  ecog_2 (-0.565), weight_loss_pct_6mo per SD (-0.160), blast_pct_marrow per SD (-0.113), albumin per SD (+0.062), crp per SD (-0.069), wbc per SD (-0.055) all remained strongly significant.
  McFadden pseudo-R^2 was 0.022, indicating that even with these very strong effects, the model explains only a small fraction of variance in objective_response — consistent with substantial unexplained heterogeneity in this outcome.

Overall conclusions.

1. Strong prognostic markers that depress objective_response are ECOG performance status of 2, higher weight_loss_pct_6mo, higher blast_pct_marrow, higher crp_mg_l, higher wbc_k_per_ul, and lower albumin_g_dl. These are classical markers of disease burden, systemic inflammation, and nutritional status.

2. The single most important predictive (treatment-by-biomarker) finding is that venetoclax + azacitidine produces a dramatic response benefit in the specific co-mutated subgroup of patients carrying both idh1_mutation and npm1_mutation, with ORR rising to ~62% from a baseline near 17%. Neither idh1 nor npm1 alone, nor venetoclax+azacitidine alone, captures this effect; the three-way interaction is essential.

3. A secondary, smaller benefit of venetoclax_azacitidine is seen in unfit_for_intensive patients.

4. 7+3 chemotherapy is associated with a small but significant decrement in response in patients with complex_karyotype, in line with prior expectations that intensive chemotherapy underperforms in cytogenetically adverse disease.

5. Several "expected" subgroup effects were not observed in this dataset: midostaurin/gilteritinib showed no enhanced benefit in flt3_itd or flt3_tkd patients; ivosidenib was actually associated with lower response within idh1+ patients (a paradoxical signal); enasidenib showed no benefit in idh2+ patients; tp53_mutation, complex_karyotype, secondary_aml, and age had no main effect on response.

6. Demographic / social-determinant findings: white patients had modestly higher ORR than other racial groups (overall p = 0.0096), but insurance type, rural residence, and education years were not associated with response. No race x treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine disparity was detected.

7. None of the 24 measured SNPs survived multiple-testing correction, suggesting these germline variants are not strong determinants of objective response in this cohort.

8. Solid-tumor-relevant markers (her2_amplification, met_exon14_skipping, ros1_fusion, braf_v600e, msi_high, organ-specific metastases) had no association with response, as expected for an AML cohort.

9. Even with these multiple highly significant signals, the multivariable model explains only ~2% of the deviance in objective_response, indicating that response is driven by additional factors not captured in these features (or contains substantial irreducible noise).

The clearest practical takeaway is the IDH1+/NPM1+ co-mutation as a marker of dramatic benefit from venetoclax+azacitidine in this synthetic dataset, and the harm of 7+3 in complex karyotype.
