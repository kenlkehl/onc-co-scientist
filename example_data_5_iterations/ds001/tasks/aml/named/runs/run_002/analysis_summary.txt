ds001_aml — Analysis Summary
============================

Cohort: 50,000 AML patient records. Outcome: objective_response (overall ORR ≈ 16.9%).
Approach: 10 iterations of propose-test-refine, starting from main effects of biomarkers and demographics, moving through treatment×biomarker interactions, lab/symptom predictors, and ending in a unified multivariable model with the key interactions.


1. Main-effect biomarkers (iteration 1)
---------------------------------------
- Most "classical" AML prognostic markers had NO detectable effect on ORR:
  TP53 mutation (diff −0.4 pp, p=0.52), complex karyotype (−0.1 pp, p=0.82), secondary AML (≈0, p=0.92), FLT3-ITD (+0.4 pp, p=0.40), unfit_for_intensive (≈0, p=0.84), age (β≈0, p=0.72).
- Two biomarkers had strong main effects:
  • IDH1 mutation +5.6 pp (ORR 0.221 vs 0.165, p=3.5e-18) — UNEXPECTEDLY POSITIVE.
  • ECOG performance status: ORR 0.213 → 0.155 → 0.112 across ECOG 0/1/2 (β=−0.384/point, p=2e-97).
- Conclusion: in this cohort, performance status dominates the AML genetic risk markers as a predictor of response, and IDH1 mutation is a strong PROGNOSTIC marker (independent of ivosidenib).


2. Targeted-therapy biomarker × treatment interactions (iterations 2 & 7)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
We tested whether each biomarker-targeted therapy raised ORR in its biomarker-positive subgroup:
- FLT3-ITD + midostaurin:    in FLT3-ITD+, ORR 0.161 on midostaurin vs 0.174 off (diff −1.2 pp, p=0.27); interaction β=−0.10, p=0.22. NO BENEFIT.
- FLT3-ITD + gilteritinib:   ORR 0.169 vs 0.172 (diff −0.3 pp, p=0.78); interaction β=+0.03, p=0.69. NO BENEFIT.
- FLT3-TKD + gilteritinib:   ORR 0.141 vs 0.166 (diff −2.5 pp, p=0.22). NO BENEFIT.
- IDH1 + ivosidenib:         in IDH1+, ORR 0.174 on ivosidenib vs 0.225 off (diff −5.1 pp, p=0.05); formal interaction β=−0.36, p=0.027. NEGATIVE INTERACTION — IDH1+ patients did WORSE on ivosidenib than IDH1+ patients off ivosidenib.
- IDH2 + enasidenib:         in IDH2+, ORR 0.144 vs 0.167 (diff −2.3 pp, p=0.29); interaction β=−0.12, p=0.45. NO BENEFIT.

Synthesis: none of the four FDA-approved AML targeted therapies (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib) showed any measurable improvement in objective response inside its nominal target population. The IDH1×ivosidenib interaction was significantly NEGATIVE. This contradicts published AML pharmacology and is a structural feature of this dataset.


3. Treatments by patient fitness (iterations 3 & 8 & 9)
-------------------------------------------------------
- venetoclax/azacitidine × unfit_for_intensive: in unfit patients, ORR 0.179 on ven/aza vs 0.163 off (diff +1.6 pp, p=2.9e-3); formal interaction β=+0.10, p=0.036. SUPPORTED — ven/aza preferentially benefits the unfit subgroup, matching its real-world indication.
- venetoclax/azacitidine × NPM1 mutation: in NPM1+ patients, ORR 0.193 on ven/aza vs 0.160 off (diff +3.4 pp, p=9.8e-8); interaction β=+0.26, p=6.6e-7. STRONGLY SUPPORTED — the largest predictive effect in the dataset, consistent with the well-known NPM1 sensitivity to venetoclax-based regimens.
- venetoclax/azacitidine × TP53: interaction β=−0.09, NS (no resistance signal in this cohort).
- 7+3 × complex karyotype: in CK+ patients, ORR 0.155 on 7+3 vs 0.179 off (diff −2.3 pp, p=8.4e-3); interaction β=−0.22, p=1.6e-3. SUPPORTED — intensive chemotherapy underperforms in adverse cytogenetics.
- 7+3 × age≥75 / 7+3 × ECOG / 7+3 × secondary_aml: all NS.
- venetoclax/azacitidine × age≥75: NS.

Net: venetoclax/aza is the only treatment with a clear positive main effect (β≈+0.06, p=0.02 adjusted), and its benefit concentrates in the unfit and NPM1-mutant subgroups. 7+3 has no overall main effect but is harmful specifically in complex-karyotype patients.


4. Continuous predictors (iteration 4)
--------------------------------------
Univariable logistic regressions of labs/symptoms onto ORR ranked by p:
  weight_loss_pct_6mo:    β=−0.040 per percent  (p=7e-35)  STRONGLY NEGATIVE
  blast_pct_marrow:       β=−0.006 per percent  (p=1e-19)  NEGATIVE
  albumin_g_dl:           β=+0.115 per g/dL     (p=1e-6)   POSITIVE
  crp_mg_l:               β=−0.007 per mg/L     (p=3e-6)   NEGATIVE
  wbc_k_per_ul:           β=−0.002 per K/uL     (p=2e-5)   NEGATIVE
  appetite_loss_grade:    β=−0.023              (p=0.04)   small negative
  nlr, ldh, hemoglobin, total_bilirubin, sodium, creatinine, fatigue_grade, age_years: all NS.

The combined picture is a nutritional / systemic-inflammation signature (low albumin, high CRP, high blast burden, high WBC, high weight loss) — these dominate over classical AML genetic risk in this dataset.


5. Demographics and disparity (iteration 5 & 6)
-----------------------------------------------
- White vs non-white: ORR 0.173 vs 0.162 (diff +1.1 pp, p=2.1e-3). Adjusted for age, sex, ECOG, albumin, CRP, weight loss, blast %, and WBC: white-race coefficient β=+0.076, p=3.0e-3. The white-vs-non-white disparity is small but statistically robust to clinical/biomarker confounding.
- Insurance type, rural residence, sex, and most comorbidities (HTN, DM, COPD, CKD, CHF, CAD, prior malignancy, autoimmune, depression): all NS.
- atrial_fibrillation: ORR 0.156 vs 0.170 (p=0.025) — small negative association.


6. SNP scan (iteration 8)
-------------------------
Across ~25 candidate SNPs, the best signal was snp_rs1050828 (carriers ORR 0.185 vs non-carriers 0.168, p=5.6e-3). After Bonferroni correction (α=0.002 across the panel) it does not survive. No SNP is robustly associated with ORR.


7. Final unified multivariable model (iteration 10)
---------------------------------------------------
Logit(objective_response) on demographics + fitness + biomarkers + key labs + all treatments + four interaction terms.

Top coefficients (signed, ranked by p):
  ecog_ps             −0.384  p=6e-97        (dominant negative)
  weight_loss_pct_6mo −0.040  p=1e-34
  blast_pct_marrow    −0.006  p=5e-20
  idh1_mutation       +0.387  p=2e-18        (independent of ivosidenib)
  ven_aza × npm1      +0.267  p=4.5e-7       (predictive interaction)
  albumin_g_dl        +0.117  p=1e-6
  crp_mg_l            −0.007  p=1e-6
  wbc_k_per_ul        −0.002  p=1e-5
  7plus3 × CK         −0.219  p=1.4e-3       (predictive interaction, harm)
  idh1 × ivosidenib   −0.356  p=0.032        (negative predictive interaction)
  ven_aza × unfit     +0.104  p=0.038

Treatment main effects: midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, and 7+3 are all NS. Only venetoclax/aza shows a residual borderline effect.


8. Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
-----------------------------------
SUPPORTED:
  • IDH1 mutation is a strong positive prognostic marker for ORR (h8/h35/h42).
  • Higher ECOG strongly reduces ORR (h7/h36).
  • Lower albumin, higher CRP, higher WBC, higher blast %, higher weight loss all reduce ORR (h20–h24).
  • Venetoclax/aza preferentially benefits unfit patients (h16/h44).
  • Venetoclax/aza preferentially benefits NPM1-mutant patients (h47).
  • 7+3 specifically underperforms in complex-karyotype patients (h49).
  • White race is associated with modestly higher ORR after clinical adjustment (h27/h34).

REFUTED / NULL:
  • TP53, complex karyotype, secondary AML, FLT3-ITD, age, unfitness flag have no main effect on ORR (h1, h3, h4, h5, h6, h10).
  • Midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib do not improve response in their nominal target subgroups (h11, h12, h13, h14, h15; interaction tests h38–h41).
  • TP53 does not confer venetoclax resistance in this cohort (h48).
  • 7+3 is not specifically harmful for older patients, high-ECOG patients, or secondary-AML patients (h17, h46, h50, h51).
  • LDH, NLR, fatigue, sex, insurance, rural residence, most comorbidities, and the SNP panel: no association with ORR.

UNEXPECTED / OPPOSITE-DIRECTION:
  • IDH1 × ivosidenib interaction is significantly NEGATIVE (h13, h38): IDH1-mutant patients respond LESS often when given ivosidenib than IDH1-mutant patients not given ivosidenib.


9. Overall conclusions about treatment–biomarker–outcome relationships
----------------------------------------------------------------------
In this AML cohort, the strongest determinants of objective response are performance status and systemic-illness markers (ECOG, weight loss, blast burden, albumin, CRP, WBC). Among genetic markers, IDH1 mutation is uniquely prognostic — IDH1-mutant patients respond more often regardless of treatment received — while TP53, complex karyotype, secondary AML, and FLT3-ITD show no main effect.

The treatment landscape in the dataset diverges sharply from real-world AML pharmacology: none of the targeted therapies (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib) produces a measurable benefit in its biomarker-defined target subgroup, and the IDH1/ivosidenib pairing actually shows a significantly negative interaction. Only venetoclax/azacitidine demonstrates clinically coherent treatment behavior, with a small overall benefit that concentrates in the unfit and NPM1-mutant subgroups; intensive 7+3 chemotherapy specifically underperforms in patients with complex karyotype.

A small but statistically robust racial disparity in response (white > non-white) persists after adjustment for clinical and biomarker covariates, suggesting either residual confounding or a real differential treatment-effect/access pattern that the available variables do not fully explain.
