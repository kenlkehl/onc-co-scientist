Analysis Summary - ds001_prostate (n=50,000)
=============================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The cohort is exclusively male (sex_female=0 for all 50,000 records), consistent with a prostate
cancer dataset. Outcome of interest is pfs_months (mean 3.74, SD 2.02, range 0-14.6). I ran 10
iterations of hypothesis generation and testing, beginning with main effects of treatments,
biomarkers, and prognostic variables, then probing biologically motivated interactions, multivariable
adjustment, subgroup analyses (mCRPC, ECOG, age tertiles), and finally socioeconomic and SNP
associations.

DOMINANT FINDINGS (large, replicable signals)
---------------------------------------------
1. BRCA2 x olaparib interaction (the headline result).
   - In BRCA2-mutated patients (10% prevalence), olaparib adds approximately +1.55 months of PFS
     compared with no olaparib (p < 1e-26 in subgroup; interaction p = 4.3e-61 in unadjusted model;
     adjusted interaction coefficient +1.456, p approx 0 in the multivariable OLS).
   - Among BRCA2-negative patients, olaparib has essentially no benefit (-0.07 months, p = 0.02 in
     the adverse direction but trivial in magnitude).
   - The effect replicates across every ECOG stratum (effects +1.69, +1.43, +1.66 in ECOG 0/1/2)
     and across age tertiles (+1.26, +1.42, +1.57). It is robust to mCRPC status.
   This recapitulates the canonical clinical pattern (PARP inhibitor benefit concentrated in
   homologous-recombination-deficient tumors).

2. Strong prognostic axis on PFS.
   In the final multivariable OLS (R^2 = 0.931), the dominant predictors are:
     - age_years:        +0.174 months PFS per year (positive!)   p ~ 0
     - ecog_ps:          -1.152 months per ECOG unit              p ~ 0
     - mcrpc:            -0.371 months                            p ~ 0
     - albumin_g_dl:     +0.481 months per g/dL                   p ~ 0
     - ldh_u_l:          -0.0005 months per U/L (significant)     p = 4e-62
     - psa_ng_ml:        -0.0027 months per ng/mL                 p ~ 0
     - visceral_mets:    -0.021 months                            p = 6e-4
   The age effect is unusual: in a univariate sense, age is the single strongest correlate with
   PFS (r = +0.856), and the positive sign persists within every ECOG stratum (r ~ +0.93 in each).
   It is therefore not explained by performance-status confounding. This is opposite to typical
   clinical experience and likely reflects a structural feature of the synthetic cohort.

NULL FINDINGS (clinically expected interactions absent)
------------------------------------------------------
3. MSI-high x pembrolizumab: NO interaction (p = 0.15; effect estimate even nominally negative).
4. AR-V7+ x enzalutamide: NO interaction (interaction coefficient +0.002, p = 0.96).
5. AR-V7+ x abiraterone: NO interaction (interaction coefficient +0.048, p = 0.33).
6. PSMA-high x lu177-PSMA: NO interaction (interaction coefficient -0.017, p = 0.74).
7. Targeted-therapy main effects (enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, lu177_psma, pembrolizumab)
   are uniformly null after adjustment for the prognostic factors above (|beta| < 0.011, all p > 0.17).

These null findings are notable because three of them (MSI/pembrolizumab, PSMA/lu177, BRCA2/olaparib)
are the canonical biomarker-stratified targeted therapies in advanced prostate cancer; only the
BRCA2/olaparib axis behaves as expected.

OTHER NULL OR TRIVIAL FINDINGS
------------------------------
8. Comorbidities. None of 15 tested comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart failure,
   CAD, AF, VTE, autoimmune, hepatitis B/C, HIV, prior malignancy, depression/anxiety, ILD) showed
   a significant association with PFS (smallest p = 0.06 for depression/anxiety).
9. Lab values beyond albumin and LDH are mostly null. Alkaline phosphatase (r = -0.012, p = 0.007)
   and total bilirubin (r = -0.010, p = 0.03) reach nominal significance with trivial magnitude;
   creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, glucose, platelets, WBC, ANC, ALC, BMI, vitals,
   CA-125, CEA, TSH, INR all null.
10. Symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite loss): all r approx 0, all p > 0.6.
11. Prior therapy history (chemotherapy, radiation, surgery, immunotherapy, targeted therapy,
    prior_lines_of_therapy, years_since_diagnosis): all null.
12. Demographics / socioeconomics. ANOVA across race_ethnicity p = 0.69; ANOVA across insurance_type
    p = 0.79; rural_residence diff +0.015 (p = 0.50); education_years r = -0.004 (p = 0.40);
    smoking_pack_years r = +0.003 (p = 0.51).
13. Gene alterations beyond BRCA2 (her2, met_ex14, ret, ros1, braf, ntrk, nrg1, fgfr, cdkn2a, tp53,
    keap1, pik3ca, pten): none reaches significance.
14. Pharmacogenomic SNPs. Of the 25 snp_* variables, exactly 1 (rs4986893) reached p < 0.05; this
    matches the chance expectation (1.25 expected). None survives Bonferroni correction
    (p < 0.002). All |r| < 0.011.
15. Number of concomitant treatments per patient is uncorrelated with PFS (r = -0.0001, p = 0.99).

REFINED / SECONDARY OBSERVATIONS
--------------------------------
- AR-V7+ has a small residual negative prognostic effect after adjustment (-0.013 months,
  p = 0.026), but it is not modified by any of the antiandrogen treatments tested.
- PSMA-high has a small positive prognostic effect after adjustment (+0.018 months, p = 3e-4),
  again not modified by lu177-PSMA exposure.
- mCRPC by treatment interactions are nearly all null; docetaxel produced a nominal positive
  interaction (+0.080, p = 0.04) which I would not interpret as robust given the multiple
  comparisons performed.
- Visceral mets matter slightly more in non-mCRPC patients (-0.078, p = 0.02) than in mCRPC
  patients (-0.020, p = 0.51) - a small effect-modification.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
The dataset contains one large, biologically meaningful treatment-biomarker interaction:
BRCA2-mutated patients gain approximately 1.5 months of PFS from olaparib, while BRCA2-wildtype
patients gain nothing - the canonical PARP inhibitor pattern.

PFS in this cohort is otherwise dominated by classical prognostic variables: age (positive in this
synthetic cohort), ECOG performance status, mCRPC status, albumin, LDH, PSA, and to a lesser
extent visceral metastases. Together these account for over 93% of the variance in PFS, leaving
little room for additional treatment effects.

Multiple biomarker-treatment interactions that would be expected from prostate cancer biology -
MSI-high with pembrolizumab, AR-V7 positivity with enzalutamide or abiraterone, and PSMA-high
status with lu177-PSMA radioligand therapy - are absent in this dataset. Demographic variables
(race/ethnicity, insurance, rurality), pharmacogenomic SNPs, comorbidities, prior-therapy history,
and patient-reported symptom grades show no clinically meaningful relationship with PFS.

The most actionable clinical signal in the data is therefore the BRCA2 x olaparib interaction;
the most striking statistical anomaly is the strong positive association between age and PFS,
which appears to be a structural feature of the cohort rather than a confounded one.
