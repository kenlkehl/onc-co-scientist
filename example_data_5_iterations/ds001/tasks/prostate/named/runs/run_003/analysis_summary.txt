ds001_prostate — Analysis summary (10 iterations, n = 50,000 patients)
======================================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival in months; mean 3.74 ± 2.02,
range 0–14.6). All patients are male (sex_female = 0 throughout). Distribution
of mCRPC = 27,481 (55%), HSPC = 22,519 (45%); ECOG 0/1/2 = 17,592 / 24,971 / 7,437.

This narrative walks through the hypotheses we explored across iterations,
the statistical evidence for each, and what supports vs. refutes them.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 1 — Classical prognostic factors (univariate)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
We tested whether canonical prostate-cancer prognostic factors track PFS:

  - SUPPORTED: ecog_ps (r = -0.39, p≈0), psa_ng_ml (r = -0.15, p≈0),
    albumin_g_dl (r = +0.12, p≈0), mcrpc (mean diff -0.52 mo, p≈0).
  - SUPPORTED but very small effect: ldh_u_l (r = -0.017, p=2e-4),
    alkaline_phosphatase_u_l (r = -0.012, p=0.007), visceral_mets
    (-0.05 mo, p=0.034).
  - REFUTED in this cohort: gleason_score (r = -0.003, p=0.49 univariate;
    becomes -0.011, p<1e-12 in the adjusted model from iter 9), bone_mets
    (p=0.62), liver_mets (p=0.95).
  - HEMOGLOBIN went the WRONG WAY: r = -0.010 (p=0.022). Higher Hgb tracked
    very slightly LOWER PFS, opposite to clinical expectation.
  - AGE was the most striking finding of iter 1: age_years correlated
    POSITIVELY with PFS at r = +0.86, p≈0 — completely opposite to the
    clinical prior that older patients fare worse. This is so strong (and
    monotonic in the multivariable model below at +0.17 mo / year) that we
    treated age as a positive prognostic factor for the rest of the
    analysis. It almost certainly reflects how this synthetic outcome was
    generated rather than a real biological signal, but it is by far the
    largest single predictor in the dataset.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 2 — Treatment main effects (univariate)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
We tested whether each of the six treatments was associated with longer PFS
across the whole cohort:

  - treatment_olaparib: +0.094 mo, p=0.003 — SUPPORTED (significant overall).
  - treatment_enzalutamide: -0.026 mo, p=0.15 — REFUTED.
  - treatment_abiraterone: -0.006 mo, p=0.78 — REFUTED.
  - treatment_docetaxel: -0.017 mo, p=0.38 — REFUTED.
  - treatment_lu177_psma: -0.004 mo, p=0.87 — REFUTED.
  - treatment_pembrolizumab: +0.064 mo, p=0.14 — REFUTED (numerically
    positive but not significant).

Crude treatment effects are tiny. The next iterations probed whether
treatment benefit is concentrated in biomarker-defined subgroups.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 3 — Treatment × biomarker interactions
----------------------------------------------------------------------
This iteration delivered the largest signal in the dataset.

  - olaparib × brca2_mutation: STRONGLY SUPPORTED. In BRCA2-positive
    patients (n≈5,000), olaparib added +1.55 mo of PFS (5.22 mo vs 3.67 mo,
    p = 4e-27). In BRCA2-negative patients olaparib was actually slightly
    harmful (-0.07 mo, p=0.019). The OLS interaction term was +1.62 mo
    (p = 4e-61). This is the canonical PARP-inhibitor / HRR-deficiency
    signal and is faithfully represented in the data.

  - lu177_psma × psma_high: REFUTED. Within PSMA-high disease, Lu177-PSMA
    diff = -0.011 mo (p=0.74); interaction p=0.74. No detectable benefit
    even where the biomarker says it should work.

  - pembrolizumab × msi_high: REFUTED. Within MSI-high (n=1,528), pembro
    diff = -0.27 mo (p=0.24, small subgroup); interaction p=0.15. No
    significant interaction.

  - enzalutamide × ar_v7_positive: REFUTED. Interaction p=0.96; no
    AR-V7-driven resistance signal.

  - abiraterone × ar_v7_positive: REFUTED. Interaction p=0.33.

So the only treatment-biomarker pair that "fires" in this dataset is the
PARP-inhibitor / BRCA2 axis.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 4 — Subgroup heterogeneity by clinical state
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  - PFS by ECOG: 0 = 4.68 mo, 1 = 3.49 mo, 2 = 2.38 mo. Roughly 1.15 mo
    decrement per ECOG grade — the strongest non-age clinical predictor.
    SUPPORTED.
  - Treatment effects within mCRPC vs HSPC mostly washed out (all
    differences within ±0.06 mo and largely non-significant). One nominal
    finding: enzalutamide in mCRPC diff = -0.048, p=0.049 (sign opposite
    to expectation; NOT supportive of a benefit). Docetaxel in HSPC
    diff = -0.06 mo, p=0.04 — also wrong sign (CHAARTED-style benefit
    REFUTED here).
  - Lu177-PSMA in mCRPC: p=0.76 — REFUTED.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 5 — Inflammatory / nutritional / symptomatic markers
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo: r = -0.143, p≈0 — SUPPORTED, large effect (one of
    the top continuous prognostic variables).
  - total_bilirubin_mg_dl: r = -0.010, p=0.032 — borderline supported.
  - nlr (p=0.58), crp_mg_l (p=0.51), pain_nrs (p=0.80), fatigue_grade
    (p=0.66), appetite_loss_grade (p=0.91), dyspnea_grade (p=0.81),
    bmi (p=0.13), creatinine_mg_dl (p=0.14), bun, sodium, potassium,
    calcium, ALT, AST, platelets, WBC, SpO₂ — all REFUTED.
    The symptomatic-burden hypothesis (that a "frailer" symptom profile
    predicts shorter PFS) is not supported except via weight loss.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 6 — Comorbidities and demographics
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Every comorbidity tested (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart
failure, CAD, atrial fib, VTE, autoimmune disease, depression/anxiety)
returned p > 0.05 for any PFS difference. Likewise:

  - race_ethnicity ANOVA: F=0.56, p=0.69 — REFUTED.
  - insurance_type ANOVA: F=0.34, p=0.79 — REFUTED.
  - rural_residence: p=0.50 — REFUTED.
  - smoking_pack_years: r=0.003, p=0.51 — REFUTED.
  - education_years: r=-0.004, p=0.40 — REFUTED.

The data show no demographic/social-determinants disparity in PFS, and no
comorbidity main effect.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 7 — Prior-therapy history
----------------------------------------------------------------------
prior_lines_of_therapy (r=-0.006, p=0.20), years_since_diagnosis (p=0.90),
prior_chemotherapy / radiation / surgery / immunotherapy / targeted_therapy
all returned p > 0.10 — all REFUTED. There is no detectable "heavily
pretreated patients fare worse" signal.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 8 — Genomic alterations and pharmacogenomic SNPs
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  - tp53_mutation: p=0.87 (univariate) — REFUTED.
  - pten_loss: p=0.62 univariate (REFUTED at this stage), but turned
    nominally significant +0.018 mo (p=0.010) in the iter-9 multivariable
    model — counter-clinical sign.
  - cdkn2a_loss, pik3ca_mutation, fgfr_alteration, her2_amplification,
    ntrk_fusion, braf_v600e, ros1_fusion, ret_fusion, met_exon14_skipping,
    nrg1_fusion, keap1_mutation: all REFUTED.
  - SNPs (25 markers): only snp_rs4986893 reached nominal p=0.015. After
    Bonferroni correction (α=0.002), NO SNP remained significant. The
    pharmacogenomic-SNP hypothesis (h8.4) is REFUTED.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 9 — Multivariable OLS (R² = 0.97)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
A single OLS regressing pfs_months on age, ECOG, mCRPC, visceral/bone/liver
mets, log(PSA), Gleason, albumin, log(LDH), Hgb, log(ALP), NLR, CRP,
weight loss, the six treatments, BRCA2/AR-V7/MSI/PSMA/TP53/PTEN, and prior
lines achieved R² = 0.9705, indicating that the synthetic outcome is
nearly fully determined by these inputs.

Significant independent predictors (signs as in adjusted model):
  age_years            +0.174 mo / year     p≈0  (dominant)
  ecog_ps              -1.151 mo / grade    p≈0
  log1p(psa_ng_ml)     -0.383 mo            p≈0
  albumin_g_dl         +0.477 mo / g/dL     p≈0
  weight_loss_pct_6mo  -0.077 mo / %        p≈0
  log1p(ldh_u_l)       -0.101 mo            p=9e-112
  gleason_score        -0.011 mo / unit     p=8e-13  (only emerges
                                                     after adjustment;
                                                     refines h1.5)
  visceral_mets        -0.016 mo            p=3e-5
  treatment_olaparib   +0.148 mo            p=4e-180
  brca2_mutation       +0.151 mo            p=1e-184  (main effect for
                                                      BRCA2 separate from
                                                      the olaparib-BRCA2
                                                      interaction)
  psma_high            +0.025 mo            p=4e-15  (positive sign!)
  pten_loss            +0.018 mo            p=0.010  (counter-clinical)

NOT significant after adjustment: mcrpc, bone_mets, liver_mets, hemoglobin,
log(ALP), NLR, CRP, treatment_enzalutamide / abiraterone / docetaxel /
lu177_psma / pembrolizumab, ar_v7_positive, msi_high, tp53_mutation,
prior_lines_of_therapy.

The disappearance of the mCRPC main effect after adjustment indicates that
its raw -0.52 mo univariate signal was confounded by ECOG, PSA, albumin,
weight loss, and age (all of which cluster with castration-resistant
status).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 10 — Adjusted interactions among top predictors
----------------------------------------------------------------------
We re-tested the interactions from iteration 3 with adjustment for ECOG,
mCRPC, visceral mets, albumin and log(LDH), and added a few new
interactions motivated by the iter-9 model.

  - olaparib × brca2_mutation (adjusted): +1.64 mo, p=4e-76 — STILL
    HIGHLY SUPPORTED. Adjustment did not attenuate the effect.
  - lu177_psma × psma_high (adjusted): -0.04, p=0.40 — still REFUTED.
  - pembrolizumab × msi_high (adjusted): -0.23, p=0.28 — still REFUTED.
  - enzalutamide × ar_v7_positive (adjusted): -0.006, p=0.88 — REFUTED.
  - docetaxel × visceral_mets: +0.034, p=0.45 — REFUTED.
  - docetaxel × ecog_ps: +0.009, p=0.74 — REFUTED.
  - albumin × mcrpc: +0.013, p=0.70 — REFUTED. Albumin is uniformly
    prognostic regardless of castration state.
  - nlr × crp: -0.0007, p=0.12 — REFUTED. No combined inflammation signal.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Overall conclusions about treatment–biomarker–outcome relationships
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. The dataset is highly deterministic: a 28-variable OLS captures 97% of
   PFS variance. The signal-to-noise ratio is therefore much better than
   in real EHR cohorts.

2. The dominant predictors of pfs_months are (in order of impact):
   age_years (positive — counter-intuitive), ecog_ps (negative),
   albumin_g_dl (positive), log(psa_ng_ml) (negative),
   weight_loss_pct_6mo (negative), log(ldh_u_l) (negative). These six
   alone reproduce most of the explained variance.

3. The only credible treatment-biomarker interaction in the data is
   olaparib × BRCA2 mutation. PARP-inhibitor benefit is concentrated
   almost entirely in BRCA2-positive patients (+1.55 mo PFS univariate,
   +1.64 mo adjusted). Olaparib's apparent overall main effect (+0.094
   mo) is driven entirely by this subgroup.

4. The other commonly-hypothesised pairings — Lu177-PSMA × PSMA-high,
   pembrolizumab × MSI-high, AR-V7 × enzalutamide/abiraterone resistance —
   are NOT present in this synthetic cohort. PSMA-high status is a
   modest positive prognostic factor (+0.025 mo, p=4e-15) regardless of
   whether Lu177-PSMA is administered.

5. BRCA2 mutation has an independent positive prognostic effect
   (+0.15 mo, p=1e-184) on top of the olaparib interaction; in part this
   may reflect the model's inability to fully separate the marginal
   effect from the interaction.

6. No demographic, social-determinant, comorbidity, or non-BRCA2 genomic
   feature showed any meaningful PFS signal. The dataset shows zero
   detectable race/ethnicity or insurance disparity in PFS.

7. Two findings run counter to clinical priors and likely reflect the
   data-generating process rather than biology: (a) older age is
   associated with LONGER PFS (the largest single effect); (b) PTEN
   loss is associated with LONGER (not shorter) PFS in the adjusted
   model. These should be flagged when interpreting the cohort.

8. Out of the 25 pharmacogenomic SNPs, none survived Bonferroni
   correction. This dataset does not embed pharmacogenomic structure.

In sum, the supported hypotheses across iterations are h1.1, h1.2, h1.3,
h1.4, h1.6, h1.7, h1.9 (weak), h1.12 (with reversed sign), h2.4
(driven by h3.1/h3.3), h3.1, h3.3, h4.1, h5.3, h5.9 (weak), h9.1–h9.10,
and h10.1. The hypotheses that were tested and refuted include almost
every other treatment main effect and biomarker-treatment interaction
(h2.1, h2.2, h2.3, h2.5, h2.6; h3.4–h3.10; h4.2–h4.5; h5.1, h5.2,
h5.4–h5.8; all of h6 and h7; h8.1, h8.3–h8.5; h10.2–h10.8). The
single most clinically actionable signal in the cohort is the
olaparib × BRCA2 interaction.
