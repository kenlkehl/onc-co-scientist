Analysis Summary - ds001_prostate (n=50,000)
=================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
This analysis explored treatment-biomarker-outcome relationships in a 50,000-patient
prostate cancer cohort with progression-free survival (pfs_months) as the outcome.
Across 10 iterations I tested 36 hypotheses with 36 statistical analyses, including
unadjusted comparisons, multivariable ordinary-least-squares regressions with prognostic
covariates, biomarker-stratified subgroup analyses, and treatment-by-biomarker interaction
models.

HEADLINE FINDINGS
-----------------
1. The single dominant treatment-biomarker signal in this dataset is olaparib x BRCA2.
   - In BRCA2-mutated patients (n=4996), treatment_olaparib increases mean PFS by
     approximately +1.5 months unadjusted (5.22 vs 3.67, p~4e-27) and +1.44 months
     after multivariable adjustment for age, ECOG, PSA, albumin, weight loss, and mCRPC
     (p<1e-300 in interaction model with t-statistic >66).
   - In BRCA2-wildtype patients, the adjusted olaparib effect is essentially zero
     (-0.003 months, p=0.59). The slight univariate negative drift was confounding.
   - The benefit is consistent in direction across race/ethnicity strata (white +1.61,
     black +1.67, asian +1.54, hispanic +1.02, other +2.96), and is preserved in both
     mCRPC=0 (+0.10) and mCRPC=1 (+0.09) strata.
   - This recapitulates the established clinical biology of PARP inhibition in
     homologous-recombination-deficient prostate cancer.

2. No other matched treatment-biomarker pair shows a significant interaction.
   - pembrolizumab x msi_high: interaction coef +0.04, p=0.40. Subgroup analysis in
     MSI-high patients (n=1528) gave adjusted pembrolizumab effect of +0.05 months
     (p=0.36). The biological expectation of immunotherapy benefit in MSI-high tumors
     is NOT borne out in this synthetic cohort. Sample size in the on-treatment MSI-high
     cell is small (n=79).
   - lu177_psma x psma_high: interaction coef +0.0003, p=0.98. No PSMA-targeted benefit.
   - enzalutamide x ar_v7_positive: interaction coef +0.004, p=0.71.
   - abiraterone x ar_v7_positive: interaction coef +0.014, p=0.21. AR-V7 status does
     not modify androgen-receptor-targeted therapy effects in this dataset.
   - docetaxel x visceral_mets: interaction coef +0.007, p=0.55. Chemotherapy is not
     selectively beneficial in visceral disease.

3. Marginal treatment main effects (without biomarker context) are clinically negligible.
   - Olaparib alone: +0.094 months (p=0.0023, dilute reflection of the BRCA2-mutated
     subgroup signal).
   - Pembrolizumab alone: +0.064 months (p=0.13).
   - Enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, lu177-psma: all |effect|<0.03 months and
     non-significant in both unadjusted and adjusted models.
   - In the comprehensive multivariable model (R^2=0.95), every treatment main-effect
     coefficient has p>=0.07. Treatments appear to have been assigned roughly randomly
     with respect to outcome-relevant features at the population level (e.g., olaparib
     prevalence 10.2% in BRCA2- vs 10.6% in BRCA2+, suggesting no biomarker-driven
     selection).

PROGNOSTIC FACTORS
------------------
The comprehensive model (R^2 = 0.95) is dominated by clinical/demographic predictors:
   - age_years: +0.174 months PFS per year of age (t=869). UNEXPECTED DIRECTION:
     in this dataset, older patients have substantially LONGER PFS. Mean PFS rises
     monotonically from 0.62 months (age <=50) to 7.20 months (age >80). This is the
     opposite of typical clinical experience and suggests a synthetic data-generating
     process in which age is positively correlated with the outcome.
   - ecog_ps: -1.152 months per ECOG point (t=-392). Unadjusted means: 4.69 (ECOG 0),
     3.49 (ECOG 1), 2.38 (ECOG 2). Strongly negative as expected.
   - mcrpc: -0.38 months adjusted (-0.52 unadjusted). Castration-resistant disease
     associates with shorter PFS as expected.
   - albumin_g_dl: +0.48 months per g/dL (positive prognostic, expected).
   - weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.076 months per percent (negative, expected).
   - psa_ng_ml: -0.0027 months per ng/mL (negative, expected).
   - ldh_u_l: -0.0005 months per U/L (negative, expected, independent of other markers).
   - visceral_mets: -0.02 months adjusted (modest).
   - psma_high: weak positive prognostic effect (+0.02, p<0.001) independent of
     lu177-psma treatment.
   - alkaline_phosphatase, hemoglobin: no independent association after adjustment.

NULL FINDINGS (refuted or unsupported hypotheses)
-------------------------------------------------
   - Higher age associated with shorter PFS: REFUTED in direction (correlation +0.86).
   - pembrolizumab benefits MSI-high patients: NOT SUPPORTED.
   - lu177-psma benefits PSMA-high patients: NOT SUPPORTED.
   - AR-V7 modifies AR-targeted therapy effects (enzalutamide/abiraterone): NOT SUPPORTED.
   - Docetaxel selectively benefits patients with visceral mets: NOT SUPPORTED.
   - Docetaxel less effective at high ECOG: NOT SUPPORTED (null in both ECOG strata).
   - Race/ethnicity associated with PFS: NOT SUPPORTED (ANOVA p=0.69).
   - Insurance type associated with PFS: NOT SUPPORTED (ANOVA p=0.79).
   - Rural residence associated with PFS: NOT SUPPORTED (p=0.50).
   - Comorbidity burden associated with PFS: NOT SUPPORTED (r=-0.005, p=0.25).
   - Any of 28 SNPs associated with PFS at Bonferroni-corrected level: NOT SUPPORTED
     (most extreme p=0.014, threshold 0.0018).
   - BRCA2 mutation has independent prognostic effect on PFS off-treatment: NOT SUPPORTED
     (adjusted main effect +0.003, p=0.63; its impact is essentially via olaparib).

WEAKER / SUGGESTIVE SIGNALS
---------------------------
   - abiraterone x age interaction: coef -0.001 months/year, p=0.036. Marginally
     significant; in older patients the (already null) abiraterone effect drifts
     slightly more negative. Likely not clinically meaningful.
   - In mCRPC=1 patients, enzalutamide unadjusted diff -0.048 (p=0.049) and in
     mCRPC=0 docetaxel diff -0.059 (p=0.043). These small effects vanish in
     multivariable adjustment and likely reflect confounding-by-indication.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
This dataset shows a clean and biologically coherent precision-oncology signal:
treatment_olaparib provides a substantial PFS benefit (~1.4 months) confined to
BRCA2-mutated patients, with no off-target effect in BRCA2-wildtype patients. The
five other treatments have null marginal effects and no significant biomarker-matched
interactions, including the clinically expected pembrolizumab/MSI-high and
lu177-psma/PSMA-high pairings. PFS is overwhelmingly determined by clinical/host
factors - age (in an unexpectedly positive direction), ECOG performance status, mCRPC
status, albumin, weight loss, PSA, and LDH - which together explain ~95% of PFS
variance in a multivariable OLS. Sociodemographic and genomic-SNP variables show no
credible association with PFS. The cohort appears free of confounding by treatment
selection on biomarkers (e.g., olaparib usage is similar in BRCA2+ and BRCA2-
patients), which made the matched-interaction signal especially clean.
