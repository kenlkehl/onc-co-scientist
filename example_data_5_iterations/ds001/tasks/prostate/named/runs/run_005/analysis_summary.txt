ds001_prostate — Iterative Analysis Summary
============================================

Cohort: 50,000 patient records aggregated from EHR data, all male
(prostate cancer cohort: sex_female == 0 for every record).
Outcome: pfs_months (mean 3.74, median 3.72, range 0–14.6).

Analyses were performed across 10 iterations, working from main effects
to subgroup heterogeneity, biomarker-defined predictive interactions,
and a final multivariable adjusted model. Two-sided p < 0.05 was used
as the per-test threshold; SNP screen used a Bonferroni threshold of
0.05/25 = 0.002.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 1 — Main effects of the six treatments on PFS
----------------------------------------------------------------------
None of the cytotoxic / hormonal / radioligand / IO agents showed a
clinically meaningful main effect on PFS in the unselected cohort:

  enzalutamide   Δ = -0.026 mo, p = 0.15  (n.s.)
  abiraterone    Δ = -0.006 mo, p = 0.78  (n.s.)
  docetaxel      Δ = -0.017 mo, p = 0.38  (n.s.)
  Lu177-PSMA     Δ = -0.004 mo, p = 0.87  (n.s.)
  pembrolizumab  Δ = +0.064 mo, p = 0.14  (n.s.)
  olaparib       Δ = +0.094 mo, p = 0.003 (significant)

The small but significant olaparib main effect already hinted at a
biomarker-driven subgroup, motivating iteration 3.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 2 — Prognostic / clinical features
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Strong, expected prognostic signals dominated this iteration:

  ECOG PS 1 vs 0          Δ = -1.19 mo, p ~ 0          (supported)
  ECOG PS 2 vs 0          Δ = -2.30 mo, p ~ 0          (supported)
  mCRPC vs HSPC           Δ = -0.52 mo, p = 1.6e-181   (supported)
  Albumin (per g/dL)      β = +0.495 mo, p = 1e-166    (supported)
  PSA (per ng/mL)         β = -0.0033 mo, p = 3e-260   (supported)
  Weight loss (per %)     β = -0.075 mo, p = 1e-225    (supported)
  LDH (per U/L)           β = -0.0004 mo, p = 1.8e-04  (supported)
  ALP (per U/L)           β = -0.0005 mo, p = 0.007    (supported)
  Visceral mets           Δ = -0.048 mo, p = 0.035     (small, supported)
  Age (per year)          β = +0.174 mo, p ~ 0         (REVERSED direction)
  Hemoglobin (per g/dL)   β = -0.012 mo, p = 0.022     (REVERSED direction, small)
  Liver mets              Δ = -0.002 mo, p = 0.95      (refuted)
  Bone mets               Δ = +0.011 mo, p = 0.62      (refuted)
  Gleason (univariate)    β = -0.006 mo, p = 0.49      (refuted univariately;
                                                       see iter 10)
  CRP                     β = -0.001 mo, p = 0.51      (refuted univariately)
  NLR                     β = +0.002 mo, p = 0.58      (refuted)

Notes:
- Age direction is opposite the textbook "older = worse" expectation.
  In a registry/EHR cohort, older patients are likely selected for less
  aggressive disease and less intensive treatment, biasing the marginal
  age effect upward.
- Hemoglobin's small negative coefficient is also opposite the
  conventional direction; it disappears (becomes n.s.) after adjusting
  for the dominant prognostic features (iter 10).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 3 — Biomarker × treatment predictive interactions
----------------------------------------------------------------------
This is the iteration with the single most striking finding:

  olaparib × BRCA2-mutation interaction:
      β_int = +1.619 mo,  p = 4.3e-61  (STRONGLY supported)
      Subgroup means:
          BRCA2- / olaparib-  : 3.74 mo (n=40,435)
          BRCA2+ / olaparib-  : 3.67 mo (n=4,467)
          BRCA2- / olaparib+  : 3.67 mo (n=4,569)
          BRCA2+ / olaparib+  : 5.22 mo (n=529)

  This is the canonical PARP-inhibitor synthetic-lethality pattern:
  no benefit in BRCA2-wild-type patients, ~1.5 mo PFS benefit in
  BRCA2-mutated patients. The effect persists in the full
  multivariable model (β_int = +1.44, p < 1e-300; iter 10).

  Lu177-PSMA × PSMA-high interaction:
      β_int = -0.017 mo, p = 0.74 (REFUTED)
      All four cells have nearly identical PFS (3.73–3.75). No
      predictive enrichment is detectable in this dataset.

  pembrolizumab × MSI-high interaction:
      β_int = -0.34 mo, p = 0.15 (REFUTED, but underpowered)
      Only n=79 patients are MSI+/pembro+. Direction is opposite the
      hypothesized benefit. Even with the underpowered cell, no
      signal supports an MSI-high-driven response.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 4 — AR-V7 as a predictive biomarker for ARSI resistance
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  enzalutamide × AR-V7+:  β_int = +0.003, p = 0.96   (REFUTED)
  abiraterone  × AR-V7+:  β_int = +0.048, p = 0.33   (REFUTED)

Subgroup PFS values cluster within 3.72–3.76 across all four cells of
each 2x2. Despite the well-known biology of AR-V7 mediating resistance
to AR-pathway inhibitors, this dataset shows no detectable predictive
interaction.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 5 — Treatment effects modified by mCRPC status / Gleason
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  enzalutamide × mCRPC:  β_int = -0.049, p = 0.18   (n.s.)
  abiraterone  × mCRPC:  β_int = +0.030, p = 0.45   (n.s.)
  docetaxel    × mCRPC:  β_int = +0.080, p = 0.042  (borderline, supported)
  enzalutamide × gleason: β_int =  0.000, p = 0.997 (n.s.)
  abiraterone  × gleason: β_int = -0.031, p = 0.115 (n.s.)
  docetaxel    × gleason: β_int = +0.002, p = 0.921 (n.s.)

The marginal docetaxel × mCRPC interaction is small (~0.08 mo) and
loses interest after adjustment. No Gleason interactions emerge.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 6 — Inflammation markers; chemo × site-of-disease
----------------------------------------------------------------------
In a multivariable model adjusted for ECOG, albumin, LDH and all six
treatments:

  CRP (per mg/L)      β = +0.0003, p = 0.71  (refuted as adj. prognostic)
  NLR                 β = +0.003,  p = 0.51  (refuted as adj. prognostic)
  Albumin (per g/dL)  β = +0.495,  p ~ 0     (supported, robust)
  LDH (per U/L)       β = -0.0004, p = 2e-4  (supported)
  ECOG PS             β = -1.16,   p ~ 0     (supported, dominant)

  docetaxel × visceral_mets: β_int = +0.052, p = 0.29  (n.s.)
  docetaxel × liver_mets:    β_int = -0.025, p = 0.66  (n.s.)
  docetaxel × bone_mets:     β_int = +0.012, p = 0.81  (n.s.)

CRP and NLR carry no information on top of ECOG/albumin/LDH.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 7 — Comorbidities
----------------------------------------------------------------------
None of 11 baseline comorbidities (DM, HTN, COPD, CKD, CHF, CAD,
atrial fibrillation, VTE history, autoimmune disease, depression/
anxiety, prior malignancy) reached p<0.05. The largest absolute
effect was depression/anxiety at -0.045 mo (p=0.061). Comorbidities
in this dataset do not appear to drive PFS.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 8 — Demographic / socioeconomic disparities
----------------------------------------------------------------------
No racial/ethnic group, insurance category, or rural-residence
status showed a significant difference in PFS (all |Δ| < 0.06 mo,
all p > 0.13). Education years and smoking pack-years also showed
no association. No measurable disparity signal is present in this
dataset.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 9 — SNP screen (25 candidate variants)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Best per-allele association: snp_rs4986893 (β = -0.092 mo, p = 0.014).
This does NOT survive Bonferroni correction (threshold 0.002).
APOE-tag SNP rs429358 was borderline (β = +0.047, p = 0.064). All
other SNPs had p > 0.09. The candidate-SNP screen is null.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 10 — Full multivariable adjusted model
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Formula: pfs_months ~ age + ECOG + mCRPC + visceral/liver/bone mets +
albumin + LDH + hemoglobin + CRP + NLR + ALP + PSA + Gleason +
weight loss + 6 treatments + 4 biomarker:treatment interactions
(olaparib×BRCA2, Lu177×PSMA-high, pembro×MSI-high, enza×AR-V7).

Model fit: R² = 0.952, n = 50,000.

Key adjusted coefficients:
  ECOG PS              -1.152 mo   (p ~ 0)         supported
  mCRPC                -0.376 mo   (p ~ 0)         supported
  Albumin              +0.479 mo/g/dL  (p ~ 0)     supported
  LDH                  -5e-4 mo/U/L (p = 1e-86)    supported
  PSA                  -0.0027 mo/ng/mL (p ~ 0)    supported
  Weight loss          -0.076 mo/% (p ~ 0)         supported
  Gleason score        -0.011 mo/pt (p = 2e-08)    supported (emerges
                                                    only after adjustment)
  Visceral mets        -0.020 mo   (p = 6.5e-05)   supported (small)
  CRP                  +0.0005 mo/mg/L (p = 0.029) marginal/wrong sign
  Hemoglobin, NLR, liver mets:  not significant

Predictive interactions in adjusted model:
  olaparib × BRCA2     +1.437 mo  (p < 1e-300)     STRONGLY supported
  Lu177    × PSMA-high +0.0005    (p = 0.96)       refuted
  pembro   × MSI-high  +0.045     (p = 0.39)       refuted
  enza     × AR-V7+    +0.003     (p = 0.80)       refuted

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Overall conclusions
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Prognostic axis. A coherent prognostic panel — performance status
   (ECOG), castration resistance (mCRPC), nutritional/inflammatory
   status (albumin, weight loss), tumor burden (PSA, LDH, ALP,
   visceral mets) and tumor grade (Gleason, after adjustment) —
   explains the great majority of the variance in PFS (R² ~ 0.95
   in the full model). ECOG and albumin are the two largest single
   contributors.

2. Predictive biomarker–treatment interactions. Of four pre-specified
   precision-oncology hypotheses, only ONE was supported:

     * Olaparib × BRCA2 mutation: clear, large positive interaction
       (~+1.5 mo PFS gain restricted to BRCA2-mutated patients), the
       textbook PARP-inhibitor / homologous-recombination-deficiency
       synthetic-lethality pattern, and the strongest non-prognostic
       effect in the dataset.

     * Lu177-PSMA × PSMA-high: NULL — PSMA-high patients did not
       derive differential PFS benefit.
     * Pembrolizumab × MSI-high: NULL (and underpowered, n=79 in the
       relevant cell).
     * Enzalutamide × AR-V7+ (and abiraterone × AR-V7+): NULL — no
       evidence that AR-V7 confers ARSI resistance in this cohort.

3. Treatment main effects are essentially flat in the unselected
   cohort. Olaparib's small main effect is entirely explained by the
   BRCA2-mutated subgroup.

4. Comorbidities, demographics, insurance, residence and the
   25-SNP panel show no detectable association with PFS (all
   non-significant after appropriate testing). The dataset is
   notable for the absence of racial/socioeconomic disparities in
   the outcome.

5. Counter-intuitive findings to flag: the marginal age effect is
   positive (older → longer PFS, likely reflecting selection bias in
   treatment intensity), the marginal hemoglobin coefficient is
   slightly negative (and disappears with adjustment), and CRP/NLR
   carry no independent prognostic information after adjusting for
   ECOG and albumin.

The single actionable, clinically meaningful predictive
interaction in this dataset is olaparib × BRCA2 mutation; the other
biomarker-defined precision-oncology hypotheses were not supported.
