Analysis Summary -- ds001_prostate (50,000 patients, anonymized features)
=========================================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival in months; mean 3.74, sd 2.02, range 0.0-14.6).

Across ten iterations the harness moved from broad demographic and disease-severity hypotheses to interaction-focused refinements, finishing with a multivariable summary. The narrative below walks through what was tested, what the evidence showed, and what survived.


Iteration 1 -- Demographics
---------------------------
Three hypotheses were tested: that feature_078 (continuous, range 30-90, plausibly age) is associated with pfs_months; and that race (feature_018: white/hispanic/black/asian/other) and insurance (feature_045: medicare/private/medicaid/uninsured) differ in PFS.

- feature_078 was the dominant univariate signal in the entire dataset: Pearson r = +0.856 with pfs_months (p effectively 0). Unusually for an age-like variable, the relationship is positive: mean PFS rises monotonically from 1.35 months in the lowest age quintile (30-56.5) to 6.24 months in the highest (73.5-90). This single feature explains roughly 73% of pfs_months variance.
- Race showed essentially identical mean PFS across all five groups (3.74, 3.74, 3.74, 3.80, 3.75 for white/black/hispanic/asian/other). After adjusting for feature_078, ANOVA p=0.77 -- no racial disparity in PFS detectable.
- Insurance similarly showed no detectable effect: means 3.75/3.74/3.74/3.73 across medicare/private/medicaid/uninsured; adjusted ANOVA p=0.45.


Iteration 2 -- Disease severity
-------------------------------
Two hypotheses: feature_057 (3-level integer 0/1/2) is monotonically associated with worse PFS at higher levels (a stage- or performance-status-like variable); feature_067 (5-level integer 6-10, plausibly Gleason score) is negatively associated with PFS.

- feature_057 showed a strong, ordered effect: mean PFS 4.68 / 3.49 / 2.38 for levels 0/1/2 (n = 17592 / 24971 / 7437). After adjustment for feature_078, level 1 reduces PFS by 1.18 months (p<1e-200) and level 2 by 2.29 months (p<1e-300). This is the strongest categorical predictor in the dataset after feature_078.
- feature_067 (Gleason candidate) was essentially flat: 3.71 / 3.76 / 3.74 / 3.74 / 3.70 for levels 6/7/8/9/10. Adjusted ANOVA p=0.038 was driven by tiny coefficients (-0.02 to -0.023) for Gleason 8/9/10 versus 6 -- statistically marginal but clinically negligible. The hypothesis of a meaningful Gleason gradient was REFUTED.


Iteration 3 -- Top binary feature; broader binary scan
-------------------------------------------------------
feature_051 (binary, prevalence 55%) was associated with a 0.52-month REDUCTION in pfs_months when positive (univariate t-test p<1e-180; adjusted for feature_078 the effect is -0.518 months, p<1e-300) -- the largest binary main effect in the dataset.

A scan of the 16 binary features with prevalence 35-60% (the candidate "treatment-like" indicators) showed that beyond feature_051, only feature_027 (beta=+0.031, p=1e-3) reached significance after age adjustment. All other plausible treatment indicators (feature_077, feature_011, feature_087, feature_108, feature_088, feature_112, feature_047, feature_037, feature_017, feature_095, feature_012, feature_089, feature_102, feature_074) had null main effects.


Iteration 4 -- Continuous biomarkers
------------------------------------
- feature_013 (right-skewed, range 0.12-3622) was significantly NEGATIVE: beta = -0.0033 month per unit (p<1e-300; r = -0.153 univariate). Consistent with a tumor-burden marker.
- feature_006 (range 0-24.6, mean 3.84) was significantly NEGATIVE: beta = -0.077 month per unit (p<1e-300).
- feature_009 (range 1.5-5.5, mean 3.80) was significantly POSITIVE: beta = +0.484 month per unit (p<1e-300). The protective gradient is consistent across age tertiles (low +0.46, mid +0.49, high +0.51), matching the prior for an albumin-like nutritional marker.
- feature_092 (range 48-830, mean 224) was negatively associated on the log scale (beta = -0.10/log unit, p=7.5e-14) and on the linear scale (beta = -5.4e-4 month per unit, p<1e-19).


Iteration 5 -- Sparse-signal hypothesis
---------------------------------------
A scan over all 122 features adjusting for feature_078 confirmed a SPARSE signal: only nine features reached p<0.001 (feature_057, feature_051, feature_006, feature_013, feature_009, feature_092, feature_109, feature_039, feature_043), with feature_027 as a tenth at p=1e-3. All other features -- including all five race categories, all four insurance categories, all five Gleason categories, and the great majority of binary indicators -- were null.


Iteration 6 -- feature_078 x feature_057 interaction
----------------------------------------------------
Hypothesis: the protective feature_078 slope on PFS is attenuated in higher feature_057 (worse prognosis) strata.

Confirmed. The feature_078 main slope is +0.180 month/unit at feature_057=0; interaction terms are -0.0058 (level 1, p=8.6e-17) and -0.0217 (level 2, p=2e-112). The age-like protective slope falls to ~+0.158 month/unit in the worst stratum -- a clinically meaningful 12% reduction in benefit per severity level.


Iteration 7 -- feature_051 interactions with continuous markers
---------------------------------------------------------------
Three interactions were tested.

- feature_051 x feature_013: STRONG positive interaction (+0.0156, p<1e-280). Main feature_013 slope is -0.018 in feature_051=0 patients; in feature_051=1 patients the net slope collapses to roughly -0.002. The harm of high feature_013 is therefore largely confined to feature_051=0 patients -- a clinically important effect-modification finding.
- feature_051 x feature_009: NS (interaction +0.009, p=0.62). The albumin-like protection is identical across feature_051 strata.
- feature_051 x feature_006: NS (interaction +0.0002, p=0.93). The negative feature_006 slope is the same regardless of feature_051.


Iteration 8 -- Synergistic biomarker pair; feature_051 x feature_057
--------------------------------------------------------------------
- feature_109 x feature_039 (both binary, ~10% prevalence each): a striking synergy. Cell means are 3.74 / 3.67 / 3.67 / 5.22 for (109=0,039=0)/(109=0,039=1)/(109=1,039=0)/(109=1,039=1). The main effects are individually NULL (p=0.34 and 0.14) but the multiplicative interaction is +1.43 months (p=4e-178). PFS jumps dramatically only when both indicators are positive together. This is a classic synergistic-biomarker pattern and one of the most clinically interesting findings.
- feature_051 x feature_057: small but significant attenuation. The feature_051 penalty of -0.544 at feature_057=0 is reduced by +0.034 at level 1 (p=0.009) and by +0.049 at level 2 (p=0.007) -- a ~10% attenuation in the worst severity strata.


Iteration 9 -- Subgroup / disparity probes
------------------------------------------
Four hypotheses were tested specifically to look for hidden racial or insurance-status heterogeneity.

- feature_051 effect by race: essentially identical in white (-0.525), black (-0.506), hispanic (-0.529), asian (-0.535) and other (-0.366; n=977, lower precision). Race interaction REFUTED.
- feature_057 effect by race: gradients identical across races (level 2 vs 0: -2.29 white, -2.27 black, -2.27 hispanic, -2.32 asian, -2.30 other). ANOVA interaction p=0.85. REFUTED.
- Residual insurance effect after adjusting for race + age: ANOVA p=0.45. REFUTED.
- Race x insurance interaction: ANOVA p=0.35. REFUTED.

This panel of null findings is itself notable: PFS in this cohort shows no detectable disparities along race or insurance lines, neither in main effects nor in interactions with the major biomarkers, neither overall nor within the worst-prognosis stratum.


Iteration 10 -- Final multivariable summary
-------------------------------------------
A multivariable OLS containing feature_078, feature_057, feature_051, feature_006, feature_013, feature_009, feature_092, feature_109, feature_039, feature_043, feature_027 plus race, insurance, and Gleason achieves R-squared = 0.9476. Every retained continuous/binary predictor is highly significant (|t|>4 for every term). Race, insurance, and Gleason categories contribute no meaningful incremental information (|coef|<=0.023; mostly NS). Within feature_057=2 (the worst-prognosis subgroup, n=7437), race ANOVA p=0.96 -- still no disparity even where it would be most likely to surface.


Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. The progression-free-survival signal in this anonymized prostate cohort is dominated by feature_078 (a continuous, age-like variable; r=+0.86 with PFS), unusually with a POSITIVE direction -- higher values of feature_078 strongly predict longer PFS.

2. After feature_078, prognostic information is concentrated in a sparse panel of about ten features:
   - feature_057 (3-level severity-like categorical): ~1.15 month penalty per level
   - feature_051 (binary, prev 55%): -0.52 month penalty
   - feature_009 (continuous, plausibly an albumin-like protective marker): +0.48 month per unit
   - feature_006 (continuous): -0.077 month per unit
   - feature_013 (right-skewed continuous, plausibly a tumor-burden marker): small per-unit but large at the right tail
   - feature_092 (continuous): small log-scale effect
   - feature_109 + feature_039 (binary pair): synergistic +1.43-month boost when both positive
   - feature_043, feature_027: minor binary effects

3. Several plausible-sounding clinical features are NOT prognostic in this cohort:
   - Race (feature_018) -- no main effect, no interactions with severity, no disparity within the worst stratum.
   - Insurance (feature_045) -- no main effect, no interaction with race.
   - Gleason-score-like feature_067 -- effectively flat across levels 6 through 10.
   - Most ~50%-prevalence binary indicators that resemble treatment flags.

4. Two prognostically important interactions surfaced:
   - feature_078 x feature_057: the age-like protective slope is attenuated by ~12% per severity level.
   - feature_051 x feature_013: the harm of high feature_013 is largely confined to feature_051=0 patients.
   Plus the dramatic synergistic interaction between feature_109 and feature_039 that has no main effects.

5. Together, feature_078 plus this sparse panel explain ~95% of the variance in pfs_months. The remaining ~5% is consistent with random noise of magnitude ~1 month, given the residual standard deviation pattern observed across feature_078 quintiles.
