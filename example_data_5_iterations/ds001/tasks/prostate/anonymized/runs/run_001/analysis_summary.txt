Dataset: ds001_prostate (n = 50,000 patients, 122 features + pfs_months outcome)
Iterations completed: 10 of 10

OVERVIEW
========
This cohort uses opaque feature labels (feature_001 ... feature_122) and a single outcome,
pfs_months (range 0-14.6, mean 3.74, median 3.72). One feature, feature_007, is constant
(all zeros) and was discarded. The remaining features include 76 informative binary
indicators, 8 ordinal/integer-coded variables (most with 3-5 levels, one apparent Gleason
score with values 6-10), 34 continuous laboratory-style variables, and 2 string categoricals
(feature_018 with 5 race-like categories; feature_045 with 4 insurance-like categories).
There are no missing values.

The analysis proceeded in ten iterations: identifying the dominant prognostic features,
testing biomarker effects, evaluating two candidate "treatment" indicators and their
interaction, and probing extensively for race- and insurance-based disparities in both
outcomes and treatment receipt. The major finding is that PFS in this cohort is almost
fully explained by a small set of clinical/biomarker features and a striking two-drug
synergy, with NO detectable race or insurance signal once these clinical factors are
accounted for.

ITERATION 1 - DOMINANT MAIN EFFECTS
====================================
- feature_078 (continuous, range 30-90, mean 65 - patient age): Pearson r = +0.856 with
  pfs_months. Binned means rise monotonically from 0.01 mo at age 30-36 to 7.82 mo at
  age 84-90 (~+0.13 mo per year). The direction is opposite to the typical clinical
  expectation that older patients fare worse, but the signal is overwhelming and
  dominant in this dataset. SUPPORTED.
- feature_057 (ordinal 0/1/2 - ECOG performance status): mean PFS 4.68, 3.49, 2.38 across
  the three levels (-1.15 mo per ECOG step, p ~ 0). SUPPORTED.
- feature_007 has a single unique value across all 50,000 patients and is non-informative.

ITERATION 2 - BIOMARKER MAIN EFFECTS
=====================================
- feature_013 (continuous, mean 42.7, max 3,622, heavy right skew - PSA-like): adjusted
  coefficient -0.0027 mo per unit (p ~ 0). Higher value -> shorter PFS. SUPPORTED.
- feature_009 (continuous, mean 3.80, narrow range - albumin-like): adjusted coefficient
  +0.479 mo per unit (p ~ 0). Higher value -> longer PFS. SUPPORTED.
- feature_006 (continuous, mean 3.84, max 24.6): adjusted coefficient -0.077 mo per unit
  (p ~ 0). SUPPORTED.

ITERATION 3 - feature_051 AND GLEASON
======================================
- feature_051 (binary, prevalence 55%): patients with feature_051 = 1 have PFS shortened by
  ~0.54 mo after adjusting for age and ECOG. Mean feature_013 (PSA-like) is 11.96 in the
  feature_051 = 0 group vs 67.83 in the feature_051 = 1 group, a ~6-fold difference. This
  is consistent with feature_051 being a marker of advanced / high-tumor-burden disease.
  SUPPORTED.
- feature_067 (Gleason-like, range 6-10): adjusted coefficient is only -0.007 mo per unit
  (p = 0.018). Direction is correct, but magnitude is clinically negligible (PFS varies by
  <0.08 mo across Gleason 6-10 within each ECOG stratum). Surprisingly weak for a
  prostate-cancer cohort. SUPPORTED but weak.

ITERATION 4 - TWO-DRUG SYNERGY (KEY FINDING)
=============================================
- feature_109 (binary, ~10% prevalence) and feature_039 (binary, ~10% prevalence) each
  appear weakly protective in univariate analysis (r = +0.015, p < 0.01).
- The crucial finding is the interaction. Cell means of pfs_months were:
       neither active     = 3.74 mo (n = 40,435)
       only feature_109   = 3.67 mo (n =  4,467)
       only feature_039   = 3.67 mo (n =  4,569)
       both active        = 5.22 mo (n =    529)
- In an interaction model, the main effects of feature_109 and feature_039 are
  effectively zero (p ~ 0.7), but the feature_109 x feature_039 product term is
  +1.44 mo (t = +66.6, p ~ 0). Either feature alone provides no benefit; together they
  add ~1.5 months of PFS. This is the strongest interaction found in the dataset.
  SUPPORTED.

ITERATION 5 - RACE AND INSURANCE: NO MAIN EFFECT ON PFS
========================================================
- feature_018 (race): one-way ANOVA F = 0.20, p = 0.94. Group means range only 3.74-3.80.
  After adjustment for age, ECOG, and feature_051, every race coefficient is non-significant
  (|t| <= 0.9, p >= 0.38). NOT SUPPORTED.
- feature_045 (insurance): group means range only 3.73-3.75. Adjusted coefficients all
  within +/- 0.02 mo with p >= 0.37. NOT SUPPORTED.

ITERATION 6 - RACE AND INSURANCE: NO TREATMENT-RECEIPT DISPARITY
=================================================================
- Receipt of feature_109 across races: 9.4%-10.7% (chi-square p = 0.43). By insurance:
  9.9%-10.9% (p = 0.40). No disparities.
- Receipt of feature_039 across races: 8.6%-10.5% (p = 0.26). By insurance: 9.3%-10.3%
  (p = 0.60). No disparities.
- Receipt of the synergistic combination (both = 1) across races: 0.82%-1.10% (p = 0.78).
  By insurance: 1.02%-1.20% (p = 0.85). The valuable combination is distributed equally.
- feature_051 prevalence is 54.4%-55.4% across races (p = 0.82) and 54.6%-56.2% across
  insurance categories (p = 0.37). No demographic patterning of advanced disease.
- Mean feature_078 (age) and feature_057 (ECOG) are essentially identical across all five
  race groups, ruling out indirect demographic confounding via the dominant predictors.
- Together these four chi-square / ANOVA tests support the conclusion that this cohort
  contains no detectable race- or insurance-based disparities in either treatment receipt
  or baseline disease severity.

ITERATION 7 - INTERACTIONS WITH AGE / ECOG
===========================================
- age x ECOG interaction = -0.0098 mo per year per ECOG step (t = -21.3, p ~ 0). Each
  ECOG step blunts the per-year age contribution by ~0.01 mo: an 80- vs 70-year-old gain
  is +1.74 mo at ECOG 0 vs +1.55 mo at ECOG 2. SUPPORTED.
- feature_009 x ECOG interaction = -0.017 (p = 0.053): borderline and clinically
  negligible. The protective effect of feature_009 is essentially uniform across ECOG
  strata (within-stratum r = 0.131, 0.132, 0.141). SUPPORTED (no meaningful modification).
- feature_051 x ECOG interaction = +0.027 (p = 0.002): statistically detectable but tiny.
  The deleterious effect of feature_051 attenuates by only ~0.03 mo per ECOG step.
  SUPPORTED (effectively uniform).

ITERATION 8 - SYNERGY UNIFORMITY ACROSS DEMOGRAPHICS
=====================================================
- Combination benefit (PFS_both - PFS_neither) by race: asian +1.42, black +1.76,
  hispanic +0.99, white +1.51, other +2.74 mo. All positive and large; the spread is
  consistent with sampling noise given the small both-active cell sizes (n = 8 to 355).
  SUPPORTED.
- Combination benefit by insurance: medicare +1.40, private +1.51, medicaid +1.77,
  uninsured +0.98 mo. All four categories see a substantial benefit of similar magnitude.
  SUPPORTED.
- feature_051 x feature_109 interaction (p = 0.37) and feature_051 x feature_039
  interaction (p = 0.21) are both non-significant; the combination benefit is preserved
  in both advanced (feature_051 = 1) and non-advanced (feature_051 = 0) disease.
  SUPPORTED.

ITERATION 9 - WHAT REMAINS AFTER THE TOP PREDICTORS
====================================================
- After fitting the seven-feature core model (R^2 = 0.947), residuals were correlated
  with every other feature. The largest absolute residual r was 0.024 (feature_067), and
  only a handful crossed |r| > 0.01. Adding the feature_109 x feature_039 synergy plus
  Gleason raises R^2 from 0.947 to 0.952 (+0.005). The remaining 100+ features contribute
  essentially nothing. SUPPORTED.
- feature_013 (PSA-like) x feature_051 interaction = +0.0153 mo per unit (t = +59.1).
  Implied feature_013 slope is -0.018 mo per unit when feature_051 = 0 vs -0.003 mo per
  unit when feature_051 = 1 (~7-fold attenuation). feature_013 chiefly drives PFS in
  patients without the advanced-disease marker; once feature_051 = 1, additional
  feature_013 elevation adds little incremental prognostic information. SUPPORTED.

ITERATION 10 - FULL MODEL AND VARIANCE DECOMPOSITION
======================================================
- Final OLS containing age, ECOG, feature_051, feature_009, feature_013, feature_006,
  feature_092, feature_109, feature_039, feature_109 x feature_039, Gleason, race
  (nonwhite), uninsured, and medicaid achieves R^2 = 0.9518.
- All seven prognostic clinical/laboratory predictors and feature_051 are highly
  significant (|t| >= 90); feature_109 x feature_039 = +1.44 mo (t = 66.6); Gleason
  = -0.011 (t = -5.6).
- Race (nonwhite vs white): coefficient +0.002 mo, p = 0.57. Uninsured: -0.002, p = 0.81.
  Medicaid: -0.009, p = 0.11. None of the demographic adjustments materially affect any
  other coefficient. SUPPORTED.
- Sequential R^2:
       age alone                 0.732
       + ECOG                    0.880   (+0.148)
       + 5 biomarkers/f051       0.947   (+0.067)
       + synergy and Gleason     0.952   (+0.005)
  Age dominates (~77% of total explainable variance), with ECOG a clear second
  contributor (~16%). SUPPORTED.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
====================
1. PFS in this cohort is almost fully determined by a small handful of features. The two
   most powerful predictors are an age-like variable (feature_078, +0.17 mo per year) and
   an ECOG-like variable (feature_057, -1.15 mo per level). Together they explain 88% of
   the variance in pfs_months.
2. Five additional clinical/laboratory features add modest but highly significant
   prognostic information (feature_009 albumin-like, feature_013 PSA-like, feature_006,
   feature_092, and the binary advanced-disease marker feature_051).
3. The single most clinically interesting interaction is between feature_109 and
   feature_039: each is essentially inert on its own (PFS difference ~0), but together
   they add ~1.5 mo of PFS. This pattern is consistent with two agents that work only as
   a combination regimen (synergy).
4. There is NO evidence of bias in this cohort along racial or insurance lines. PFS does
   not differ by race or insurance unadjusted (group means within 0.06 mo) or after
   adjustment (all p >= 0.37). Receipt of either feature_109, feature_039, or the
   synergistic combination shows no chi-square difference across races or insurance
   categories. Prevalence of the advanced-disease marker feature_051 is also uniform
   across these demographic strata. Mean age and ECOG are essentially identical across
   the five race groups.
5. The age effect is mildly attenuated by worsening ECOG (significant negative
   age x ECOG interaction); the prognostic value of feature_013 is concentrated in
   patients without the feature_051 advanced-disease marker (significant positive
   feature_013 x feature_051 interaction). Neither interaction modifies the central
   conclusions about the dominant main effects or the two-drug synergy.
6. The Gleason-like variable (feature_067, range 6-10) carries surprisingly little
   prognostic signal (-0.007 mo per unit) - far smaller than would be expected from a
   real-world prostate cancer registry, suggesting feature_067 may have been constructed
   to be near-non-informative in this synthetic dataset.
