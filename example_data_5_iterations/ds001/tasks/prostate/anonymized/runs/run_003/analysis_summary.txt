ds001_prostate -- analysis summary (10 iterations, n = 50,000)
================================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (mean 3.74, sd 2.02, range 0-14.6).
Inputs: 122 features (77 binary, 8 ordinal 3-5 levels, 35 continuous, 2
categorical: race/ethnicity feature_018 and insurance feature_045).

Headline finding
----------------
A nine-predictor ordinary least squares model -- feature_078,
feature_057, feature_109, feature_039, feature_027, log(feature_013),
log(feature_006), feature_009, feature_092 -- explains R^2 = 0.968 of
pfs_months. After clinical adjustment, neither race nor insurance has
any detectable association with pfs_months (joint F = 0.043 on 7 df,
p = 0.9999, incremental R^2 = 0.000). Treatment effects are
homogeneous across racial groups, and access (prevalence of the two
PFS-prolonging features) is essentially uniform across race and
insurance. This dataset shows no demographic disparity in either
treatment access or treatment effectiveness.

Iteration-by-iteration walkthrough
----------------------------------

Iteration 1 -- broad univariate scan of plausibly clinical predictors
  H1 feature_078 (0-90 with one decimal, mean 65, std 10; plausibly
     age in years) is strongly POSITIVELY correlated with pfs_months:
     r = +0.856, p ~ 0; +0.17 months PFS per year. Supported. (Note:
     direction is unusual for real-world oncology but is consistent
     and dominant throughout this synthetic cohort.)
  H2 feature_051 (binary, prevalence 55%) carries a univariate
     negative effect: 4.03 mo (off) vs 3.51 mo (on); diff = -0.52,
     p = 1.6e-181. Supported (but see h27/h29 -- this turned out
     to be confounding by feature_013).
  H3 feature_013 (highly right-skewed, median 16, max 3622;
     plausibly PSA): r = -0.153, p < 1e-259. Supported.
  H4 feature_006: r = -0.143, p < 1e-225. Supported.
  H5 feature_009 (range ~2-5.5; plausibly serum albumin): r =
     +0.123, p < 1e-166. Supported.

Iteration 2 -- ordinal/categorical screening
  H6 feature_057 (3 levels: 0, 1, 2; plausibly performance status)
     shows a clean monotonic gradient: 4.68, 3.49, 2.38 months; OLS
     slope = -1.16 per level, p ~ 0. Supported.
  H7 feature_067 (5 levels: 6-10; plausibly Gleason score): adjusted
     coefficients show a -0.02 to -0.03 month decrement at levels
     8-10 vs reference 6 (p<0.05). Modest but in the predicted
     direction. Supported.
  H8 feature_071, _096, _033, _064 carry no significant adjusted
     signal (all p > 0.2); feature_016 has a borderline level-2
     coefficient (p=0.013). Largely null. Supported.

Iteration 3 -- demographic disparity check (unadjusted)
  H9 feature_018 (race/ethnicity): group means span only 3.74-3.80
     months; one-way ANOVA p ~ 0.6. NOT supported.
  H10 feature_045 (insurance): group means span 3.73-3.75; ANOVA
      p ~ 0.5. NOT supported.

  H27 Confounding hypothesis: median feature_013 is 7.4 in

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. pfs_months in this cohort is overwhelmingly driven by what looks
   like an age-like covariate (feature_078) plus a performance-
   status-like covariate (feature_057). Together they account for
   ~88% of variance.

2. Two binary features (feature_109 and feature_039, each ~10%
   prevalent) appear to act as PFS-prolonging treatments, conferring
   ~0.15 month benefits each. Their effects are independent of one
   another and additive in the multivariable model.

3. feature_051, despite a striking univariate negative association,
   is a CONFOUNDED marker -- it tracks elevated feature_013
   (PSA-like) and has no independent causal effect on PFS once
   tumor burden is controlled.

4. The dataset shows NO demographic disparity:
   - Race and insurance have no main effect on PFS (incremental
     R^2 = 0.000, p = 0.9999).
   - Treatment effects are homogeneous across racial groups
     (interaction p > 0.3).
   - Treatment access is uniform across both race and insurance
     (chi-square p > 0.2 for all).
   - The single borderline insurance-by-treatment interaction
     (p = 0.057) places the LARGEST benefit in the medicaid stratum,
     opposite to disparity-bias predictions.

5. Significant clinical effect-modification by age: PS, PSA-like
   burden and albumin-like protective effects all amplify with
   feature_078, consistent with the dominant influence of age in
   this cohort.

6. The synthetic structure of this cohort thus encodes a "best-case"
   equity scenario: clinical biology dominates, demographics are
   balanced, treatment access is uniform, and treatment effect is
   homogeneous across race and insurance.
