Analysis summary for ds001_prostate (n=50,000; outcome pfs_months)
====================================================================

We probed the dataset across 10 iterations, starting from univariate
associations and progressing through multivariable adjustment, interaction
testing, subgroup heterogeneity, and explicit sensitivity / equity checks.
Outcome pfs_months is continuous, mean 3.74, sd 2.02, range 0–14.6 (no
missing values). Apart from the patient identifier, all features are
opaque, so we describe them by column name. All analyses use OLS with
the natural pfs_months scale; effect_estimate signs are reported on that
scale.

ITERATION 1 — TOP UNIVARIATE PREDICTORS
The strongest associations with pfs_months were:
  • feature_078 (continuous, range 30–90): Spearman ρ = +0.853 — an
    exceptionally strong positive monotone association.
  • feature_057 (3-level ordinal 0/1/2): ρ = −0.386. Group means descend
    monotonically (0 → 4.68; 1 → 3.49; 2 → 2.38).
  • feature_013 (heavily right-skewed, max 3622): ρ = −0.223, with
    quartile means 4.31 / 3.93 / 3.62 / 3.12.
  • feature_006: ρ = −0.134 (p ~3.7e-200).
  • feature_009 (range 1.5–5.5, plausibly an albumin-like marker):
    ρ = +0.118 — higher feature_009 → longer PFS.
  • feature_051 (binary): unadjusted mean PFS 3.51 (=1) vs 4.03 (=0),
    Welch p ~1.6e-181, raw difference −0.52 months.
All directions are clinically plausible if we interpret feature_057 as a
disease-burden / stage indicator, feature_013 as a tumor-marker (PSA-like)
quantity, feature_009 as albumin, and feature_006 as another adverse
laboratory or symptom marker. feature_007 was a constant column (only
value 0).

ITERATION 2 — DEMOGRAPHICS (feature_018 RACE, feature_045 INSURANCE)
Race and insurance were tested as potential equity / disparity signals.
  • feature_018 ANOVA: F = 0.56, p = 0.69. Pairwise comparisons of
    black, hispanic, asian, and "other" against white: all |Δ| ≤ 0.06
    months, all p ≥ 0.14.
  • feature_045 ANOVA: F = 0.34, p = 0.79. Pairwise comparisons of
    medicaid, medicare, and uninsured against private: all |Δ| ≤ 0.02
    months, all p ≥ 0.36.
Hypotheses h7–h11 (any difference by race or insurance, including the
specific "black < white" and "uninsured < private" hypotheses) were not
supported.

ITERATION 3 — MULTIVARIABLE MODEL
We fit pfs_months ~ feature_078 + feature_057 + log(1+feature_013) +
feature_006 + feature_009 + feature_051 + feature_109 + feature_039 +
C(feature_018) + C(feature_045). R² = 0.970.
  • feature_078 = +0.174 / unit (≈ 0.17 months per unit).
  • feature_057 = −1.151 / level.
  • log(1+feature_013) = −0.383 / log-unit.
  • feature_006 = −0.077 / unit.
  • feature_009 = +0.477 / unit.
  • feature_051 = +0.0009 (p = 0.81): the strong univariate effect
    DISAPPEARED after adjustment.
  • feature_109 = +0.152 / 1 (p ≈ 1e-185).
  • feature_039 = +0.148 / 1 (p ≈ 3e-178).
  • Race and insurance: every coefficient |β| < 0.01, every p > 0.5.

Confounding mechanism: mean feature_013 was 11.96 in feature_051=0 vs
67.83 in feature_051=1 — nearly six-fold higher. The univariate
disadvantage of feature_051=1 is fully explained by its association with
elevated feature_013 (and to a lesser extent the other clinical
covariates). H13 and H14 supported.

ITERATION 4 — INTERACTIONS INVOLVING feature_051
Despite no main effect, feature_051 modifies the slopes of clinical
covariates:
  • feature_051 × feature_078: β = −0.0046, p ≈ 7e-46. The strong positive
    feature_078 slope is shallower in feature_051=1 patients.
  • feature_051 × feature_057: β = +0.0275, p ≈ 6e-9. The negative
    feature_057 slope is mildly attenuated in feature_051=1 patients.
  • feature_051 × log(1+feature_013): β = +0.0106, p = 0.0018. Small
    attenuation of feature_013's negative slope in feature_051=1.

ITERATION 5 — TREATMENT × DEMOGRAPHIC INTERACTIONS
We tested whether feature_051's effect varies by race or insurance, and
whether the binary feature_109 (which had a positive adjusted effect) is
differentially distributed across race / insurance — a check for
treatment-access bias.
  • feature_051 × feature_018: every interaction term p ≥ 0.14 (smallest
    for black at p = 0.14).
  • feature_051 × feature_045: every interaction term p ≥ 0.26.
  • Prevalence of feature_109=1 by race: 9.4 % – 10.7 %. By insurance:
    9.9 % – 10.9 %. Range ~1 percentage point in each axis — no
    meaningful access disparity.
H20–H23 not supported.

ITERATION 6 — INTERACTIONS AMONG TOP CONTINUOUS PREDICTORS
All four pairwise interactions tested were highly significant:
  • feature_078 × feature_057: β = −0.00965, p ≈ 0. Higher feature_057
    flattens the feature_078 slope.
  • feature_078 × log(1+feature_013): β = −0.00340, p ≈ 5e-151. High
    feature_013 attenuates the feature_078 slope.
  • feature_057 × log(1+feature_013): β = +0.0232, p ≈ 4e-34. The
    feature_013 penalty is smaller at higher feature_057.
  • feature_078 × feature_009: β = +0.00433, p ≈ 5e-42. The protective
    effect of feature_009 is amplified at higher feature_078.

ITERATION 7 — SUBGROUP HETEROGENEITY BY feature_057
Confirming the iter-6 interactions stratum-by-stratum:
  • log(1+feature_013) slope: −0.397 (lvl 0) / −0.384 (lvl 1) / −0.346
    (lvl 2). Mild attenuation upward.
  • feature_078 slope: 0.180 / 0.174 / 0.159. Mild attenuation upward.
  • Mean pfs_months: 4.68 / 3.49 / 2.38. Strict monotone decrease.
H28–H30 supported.

ITERATION 8 — NONLINEARITY IN feature_013
A quadratic term in log(1+feature_013) added F = 29.7 (1 df), p ≈ 5e-8,
β = +0.0045 — statistically significant but small (the relationship is
mostly captured by the log transform). Quartile analysis confirms strict
monotone decrease (4.31 → 3.93 → 3.62 → 3.12). H31 / H32 supported.

ITERATION 9 — feature_039 × feature_109 SYNERGY
The most striking interaction in the dataset:
  • Adjusted main effects: feature_039 = −0.0007 (NS); feature_109 =
    +0.0007 (NS).
  • feature_039 × feature_109 = +1.435 months (p ≈ 0).
Unadjusted means by combination: (0,0) 3.74 (n=40,435); (0,1) 3.67
(n=4,467); (1,0) 3.67 (n=4,569); (1,1) 5.22 (n=529). Only the
dual-positive cell shows a benefit. Either factor alone is essentially
inert; together they yield ~+1.5 months PFS. H33 / H34 strongly
supported.

ITERATION 10 — SENSITIVITY / ROBUSTNESS
  • Synergy by race: stratified feature_039 × feature_109 interaction is
    +1.43 (white), +1.39 (hispanic), +1.38 (black), +1.63 (asian),
    +1.94 (other). All p ≪ 0.001. No qualitative heterogeneity; modest
    variation is consistent with sampling noise (small "other" cell).
  • Synergy by insurance: +1.46 (medicare), +1.33 (private), +1.59
    (medicaid), +1.59 (uninsured). All p ≪ 0.001. Replicated in every
    stratum.
  • feature_009 slope by race: white +0.481, hispanic +0.477, black
    +0.475, asian +0.475, other +0.415. Very similar across groups.
  • Final fully adjusted model with feature_078×feature_057 and
    feature_078×log(1+feature_013) interactions: R² = 0.972. All four
    feature_018 and three feature_045 coefficients have |β| < 0.01 with
    p > 0.45.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
1. PFS in this cohort is largely determined by a small set of clinical
   features. feature_078, feature_057, log(1+feature_013), feature_006,
   and feature_009 together with the feature_039 × feature_109 synergy
   explain ~97 % of the variance in pfs_months.
2. Three plausible "treatment / disease-status" indicators behave very
   differently:
     – feature_051's apparent univariate harm is entirely confounding by
       feature_013 (much higher in feature_051=1 patients).
     – feature_109 and feature_039 individually are essentially inert,
       but the (1,1) combination buys ~1.5 additional months of PFS.
       This is consistent with a combination-therapy benefit that
       requires both components.
3. Continuous predictors interact: high feature_057 and high
   feature_013 each blunt the otherwise strong positive effect of
   feature_078, while feature_009's protective effect is amplified at
   higher feature_078.
4. The relationship of feature_013 to PFS is nearly fully linear on the
   log(1+x) scale; a small but statistically detectable curvature
   remains.
5. Race (feature_018) and insurance (feature_045) show NO marginal
   association with pfs_months, NO interaction with feature_051,
   essentially equal access to feature_109, and clinical effects
   (feature_009 slope, feature_039×feature_109 synergy) that replicate
   across every stratum. Within the variables captured here, this
   cohort shows no evidence of demographic disparity in PFS.

What was supported (sample): h1–h6 univariate effects; h12 multivariable
feature_078; h13/h14 confounding of feature_051; h15/h16 main effects of
feature_109 / feature_039 (later refined); h17–h19 feature_051
interactions; h24–h27 continuous interactions; h28–h32 subgroup +
nonlinearity findings; h33/h34 synergy; h35–h38 robustness.

What was refuted: h7–h11 (any race / insurance disparity in PFS);
h20–h23 (any feature_051 modification by race / insurance, any
distributional disparity of feature_109).
