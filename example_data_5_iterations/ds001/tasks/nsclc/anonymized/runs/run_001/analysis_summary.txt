ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (50,000 NSCLC patients, anonymized features)
===========================================================================

Outcome: objective_response (binary; overall rate 16.89%, 8,447/50,000).

Across 10 iterations I screened all 123 anonymized features against
objective_response, then refined on the strongest signals using subgroup,
interaction, and multivariable logistic-regression analyses.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. STRONGEST PROGNOSTIC FEATURES
------------------------------------------------------------

feature_051 (ordinal 0/1/2) — Strongest single predictor.
  Response rates 21.08% / 15.70% / 11.05% across levels 0/1/2.
  Univariate logit OR per unit = 0.687, p = 1.7e-93.
  Multivariable-adjusted OR = 0.682, p < 1e-90.
  Monotonic decrement is consistent with an ECOG-like performance-status variable.

feature_013 (binary, 65% prevalence) — Strongest binary predictor.
  Crude response 15.40% (=1) vs 19.64% (=0); chi-square p = 1.1e-33.
  Multivariable OR = 0.739, p ≈ 4e-34.
  Notably uncorrelated with histology, smoking, race, insurance, or any
  measured continuous feature (mean differences <0.01 between feature_013=1
  and =0). Independent unexplained risk factor.

feature_067 (binary, 25% prevalence)
  Crude response 14.36% vs 17.75%; p = 2.0e-18. Adjusted OR = 0.773,
  p ≈ 5e-19. Also independent of measured covariates.

feature_011 (continuous, range 0-28; ~25% of patients have value ≈0)
  Quintile response rates 18.92% → 13.73% (top quintile).
  Adjusted logit coef = -0.039 per unit (OR 0.962/unit), p = 1.8e-32.
  Acts as a continuous severity marker.

feature_099 (continuous, 1.7-5.5; resembles albumin)
  Adjusted coef +0.093 per unit, p = 1.2e-4. Independent positive prognostic.

feature_063 (continuous, 0.03-296; resembles an inflammatory marker / LDH-like)
  Adjusted coef -0.0051 per unit, p = 4.8e-4. Independent negative prognostic.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. PUTATIVE TREATMENTS — features_006, 007, 039
------------------------------------------------------------

Three roughly independent binary indicators (pairwise chi-square p ≥ 0.31)
all show modest positive crude effects on response:
  feature_006 (28% prev): rr1=18.19% vs rr0=16.38%, p = 1.0e-6
  feature_007 (45% prev): rr1=17.54% vs rr0=16.37%, p = 5.9e-4
  feature_039 (50% prev): rr1=17.39% vs rr0=16.40%, p = 3.4e-3

Their joint pattern strongly suggests treatment exposures rather than baseline
risk factors:

  • Independence across pairs is consistent with random/independent assignment.
  • Triple-positive patients (n=3,216) have response 22.98%, vs 16.12% in
    triple-negative (n=9,882). Difference 6.86 pp — much greater than
    additive of the individual univariate effects.
  • Effect is concentrated in the high-feature_092 tertile (see §3).

------------------------------------------------------------
3. PREDICTIVE BIOMARKER — feature_092 modifies treatment effects
------------------------------------------------------------

feature_092 (continuous, 0-0.8; resembles PD-L1 fraction) has only a small
overall main effect on response (mean 0.313 in responders vs 0.306 in
non-responders, p = 0.004), but it is a strong EFFECT MODIFIER for the three
putative treatments:

  Logistic interaction coefficients (multivariable model):
    feature_006 × feature_092:  +0.594, p = 1.2e-6
    feature_007 × feature_092:  +0.609, p = 7.9e-8
    feature_039 × feature_092:  +0.596, p = 1.6e-7
  LR test for adding all three interactions: chi2 = 79.3, df=3, p < 1e-16.

When all three interactions are in the model, the main effects of the three
treatments become NEGATIVE (p ≈ 0.01-0.22). Interpretation: at feature_092 = 0,
none of these "treatments" appears to help (and may slightly harm); benefit
grows linearly with feature_092, reaching OR ≈ 1.32 at feature_092 = 0.6.

Triple-positive × feature_092 tertile:
  Low tertile:  17.04%   (vs 16.55% baseline)  — null
  Mid tertile:  17.28%
  High tertile: 34.25%   (vs 16.41% in triple-negative high tertile)

The three-way pattern is consistent with PD-L1-driven immunotherapy:
benefit absent in PD-L1 low; substantial when all three exposures are
combined in PD-L1 high.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. SUBGROUP HETEROGENEITY
------------------------------------------------------------

Smoking status (feature_057: current/former/never) modifies feature_007 and
feature_039 effects:
  feature_007 effect: current +1.39 pp, former +1.33 pp, never +0.08 pp
  feature_039 effect: current +1.24 pp, former +1.08 pp, never +0.12 pp
  → benefit essentially absent in never-smokers (consistent with
    immunotherapy biology, where PD-L1-driven response depends on tumor
    mutational burden tied to smoking).

In contrast, feature_006 effect is INDEPENDENT of smoking status:
  current +1.99 pp, former +1.54 pp, never +2.79 pp
  → suggests feature_006 may represent a different treatment class
    (possibly chemotherapy or a targeted agent) than feature_007/039.

Histology (feature_043: adeno vs squamous) and ECOG-like feature_051
do NOT meaningfully modify any of the treatment effects on the absolute scale.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. NULL / NEGATIVE RESULTS
------------------------------------------------------------

Variables that did NOT show a meaningful association with objective_response
after multivariable adjustment:

  Age (feature_078):    coef 0.0003/year, p = 0.78  → essentially flat
                        across age quintiles (16.4-17.5%).

  Race (feature_123):   crude chi-square p = 0.045 (white 16.55% lowest,
                        other 18.54% highest), but ALL adjusted dummies have
                        p > 0.13. The marginal univariate signal is fully
                        explained by other clinical features.

  Insurance (feature_005): no crude association (p = 0.23). Adjusted: only
                        private vs medicaid is marginally positive (p=0.02);
                        no medicare or uninsured effect.

  Histology (feature_043) and smoking status (feature_057) are not
  independently associated with response after adjustment (all p > 0.1).

There is therefore no evidence in this cohort of demographic disparities in
objective response after accounting for clinical/biomarker features.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. RISK STRATIFICATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Combining the dominant features yields very large gradients:

  "Best" stratum (n=105): feature_006=feature_007=feature_039=1, feature_092>0.4,
   feature_051=0, feature_013=0, feature_067=0
   → response rate 39.05%

  "Worst" stratum (n=86): all three treatments=0, feature_092<0.2,
   feature_051=2, feature_013=1, feature_067=1
   → response rate 4.65%

Absolute gap = 34.4 percentage points (≈8.4× ratio). The features identified
above thus capture a great deal of the variation in objective response.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. SUPPORTED VS REFUTED HYPOTHESES
------------------------------------------------------------

SUPPORTED:
  • feature_051 strongly negative (h2)
  • feature_013, feature_067 strongly negative and largely independent of
    other measured covariates (h1, h5, h18, h26)
  • feature_011 negative (h4)
  • feature_006/007/039 individually positive (h3)
  • feature_092 modifies feature_006/007/039 effects (h13-h15, h23, h24)
  • Triple-positive boost concentrated at high feature_092 (h17)
  • Smoking modifies feature_007/039 effects (h11, h12) but NOT feature_006 (h27)
  • feature_099 independently positive, feature_063 independently negative
    (h21, h22)
  • Treatment-feature independence consistent with independent exposures (h31)

REFUTED OR WEAK:
  • Age effect (h7) — null
  • Histology modification of feature_006 effect (h10) — same direction in
    both histologies, no meaningful difference
  • Race disparity in response (h8) — significant univariately but does not
    survive multivariable adjustment (h19)
  • Insurance disparity (h9) — not significant either way
  • ECOG-like × feature_006 interaction (h28) — refuted (no modification on
    absolute scale)

------------------------------------------------------------
8. OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

In this 50,000-patient NSCLC cohort, the response signal is dominated by:

  (1) An ECOG-like performance score (feature_051) — linearly worse with
      higher values.

  (2) Two unexplained-by-anything-else binary risk factors (feature_013,
      feature_067) that act as substantial independent negatives. Both look
      like biological/clinical descriptors (perhaps stage/metastatic site
      indicators) that are not captured by demographics, histology, smoking,
      or labs.

  (3) A continuous severity-like feature (feature_011) and two prognostic
      labs (feature_099 positive, feature_063 negative).

  (4) A treatment-cum-biomarker structure: three independently-assigned
      exposures (features_006, 007, 039) that produce only modest benefit on
      average but dramatically increase response when combined and given to
      patients with high feature_092 (a likely PD-L1-style biomarker). The
      pattern — null at low biomarker, additive across the three treatments
      at high biomarker, smoking-dependence for two of the three — is
      consistent with immunotherapy in NSCLC.

Demographics (age, race, insurance) are NOT independent drivers of response
once the clinical features above are adjusted for. The data therefore do not
support disparities in response by demographic group, but they do support a
clinically meaningful predictive role for feature_092 in selecting patients
likely to benefit from feature_006/007/039.
