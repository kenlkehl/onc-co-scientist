ANALYSIS SUMMARY: ds001_nsclc (50,000 patients; outcome objective_response, baseline ORR 16.89%)
=================================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Across 10 iterations I screened all 123 features (78 binary indicators, 35 continuous
laboratory/measurement-style variables, 5 categorical demographics/disease descriptors,
and the patient_id) for univariate associations with objective_response, then escalated
to multivariable logistic regression, interaction tests, and stratified subgroup analyses.
The headline finding is a strong predictive-biomarker pattern between feature_006 (a
binary indicator with 28.5% prevalence) and feature_092 (a continuous variable bounded
in [0, 0.8]). Beyond that, a small set of clinical / lab-style predictors -- in
particular feature_051 (an ordinal 0/1/2 variable behaving like ECOG performance
status), feature_011 (continuous burden index), feature_013, feature_067, feature_099
(albumin-like), and feature_063 (right-skewed inflammatory-marker-like) -- have robust,
direction-consistent associations with response. Demographic factors (race, age,
smoking, histology) carry little to no independent signal after adjustment, although a
small private-vs-Medicaid disparity persists.

ITERATIONS 1-2: UNIVARIATE SCREEN AND TOP-FEATURE CHARACTERIZATION
------------------------------------------------------------------
A 123-feature univariate screen surfaced 14 features at p<0.05; the seven dominant
signals (all confirmed in iteration 2) were:

* feature_051 (ordinal 0/1/2): ORR 21.1% / 15.7% / 11.1% across categories. Strict
  monotonic decrease, chi-square p=2.9e-93. The categorical pattern strongly resembles
  ECOG performance status. (h2: SUPPORTED)
* feature_011 (continuous, range 0-28; mean 3.84): ORR drops monotonically across
  quintiles (18.9 -> 17.0 -> 15.8 -> 13.7%). Welch t-test p=2.8e-35. Plausibly a
  comorbidity index or pack-year burden. (h3: SUPPORTED)
* feature_013 (binary, prev 64.7%): ORR 15.4% if 1 vs 19.6% if 0 (chi-square
  p=1.1e-33). (h4: SUPPORTED)
* feature_067 (binary, prev 25.2%): ORR 14.4% if 1 vs 17.7% if 0 (p=2.0e-18).
  (h5: SUPPORTED)
* feature_006 (binary, prev 28.5%): ORR 18.2% if 1 vs 16.4% if 0 (p=1.0e-6). The
  only common binary indicator with a positive association; suggested itself as
  candidate therapy. (h6: SUPPORTED)
* feature_099 (continuous, range 1.7-5.5; mean 3.80): ORR rises monotonically across
  quintiles (15.9% -> 17.9%); Welch p=1.3e-4. Range and direction are consistent with
  serum albumin (g/dL). (h7: SUPPORTED)
* feature_063 (right-skewed continuous, range 0.03-296.88): ORR drops monotonically
  across quintiles (18.2% -> 15.7%); p=4.2e-4. Distribution and direction resemble
  LDH or CRP. The log-transformed version (h24, iteration 7) gave coef -0.056 (p=5e-7),
  i.e., a clean log-linear dose-response. (h8 / h24: SUPPORTED)

ITERATION 3: MULTIVARIABLE ADJUSTMENT
-------------------------------------
A logistic regression (LLR p=1.1e-182) with 11 numeric predictors plus histology and
smoking as categorical adjustments confirmed every above predictor in the same direction
after mutual adjustment:

* feature_051 OR 0.683 per unit (p=1.3e-95)
* feature_011 OR 0.961 per unit (p=1.9e-33)
* feature_013 OR 0.739 (p=1.0e-34)
* feature_067 OR 0.772 (p=5.1e-19)
* feature_006 OR 1.139 (p=1.9e-6)
* feature_099 OR 1.097 per unit (p=1.3e-4)
* feature_063 OR 0.995 per unit (p=4.7e-4)
* feature_007 OR 1.085 (p=7.6e-4)  -- binary, prev 44.7%; positive
* feature_039 OR 1.079 (p=1.6e-3)  -- binary, prev 50%; positive
* feature_092 OR 1.189 per unit (p=2.4e-3)  -- weak main effect; foreshadows iter 4
* feature_078 (age) OR 1.0003 per year (p=0.79; NULL)
* histology (feature_043) and smoking (feature_057) categorical effects NS.
(h9: SUPPORTED for the listed features; h10 SUPPORTED for age.)

ITERATION 4: INTERACTIONS -- THE PREDICTIVE-BIOMARKER FINDING
-------------------------------------------------------------
The most important finding came from testing interactions between feature_006 (the
positive-direction binary, candidate therapy) and other features:

* feature_006 x feature_092: interaction coef +0.595, OR 1.81 per unit of feature_092
  for treated patients, p=1.5e-6. Crucially, the main effect of feature_006 alone
  (when feature_092=0) became NS (p=0.22), and the main effect of feature_092 alone
  (when feature_006=0) was also NS (p=0.92). The full benefit of feature_006 is
  conditional on high feature_092. (h11: SUPPORTED -- this is the strongest finding
  in the dataset.)
* feature_013 x feature_092 also reached p=0.035 (coef +0.245) -- a much weaker
  echo of the same pattern; feature_013 may be a related therapy or biomarker.
  (h14: SUPPORTED weakly.)
* feature_006 x histology (feature_043): NS (coef -0.024, p=0.67). (h12: REFUTED.)
* feature_006 x performance status (feature_051): NS (coef +0.040, p=0.32).
  (h13: REFUTED.)
* feature_006 x feature_013: NS (coef -0.071, p=0.18). (h15: REFUTED.)

ITERATION 5: SUBGROUP ANALYSES (HISTOLOGY, SMOKING)
---------------------------------------------------
* Unadjusted histology (feature_043) effect: NS (p=0.078). (h16: REFUTED.)
* Unadjusted smoking (feature_057) effect: NS (p=0.27). (h17: REFUTED.)
* feature_006 ORR benefit replicates inside both adenocarcinoma (+1.9 pp, p<1e-4)
  and squamous (+1.5 pp, p=0.026) histologies. (h18: SUPPORTED.)
* feature_006 ORR benefit is positive in all three smoking categories: former +1.5 pp
  (p=0.003), current +2.0 pp (p=0.002), never +2.8 pp (p=0.089 due to small n). Direction
  consistent. (h19: SUPPORTED.)

ITERATION 6: DEMOGRAPHIC DISPARITIES
------------------------------------
* Race (feature_123) unadjusted ORR: white 16.5%, hispanic 17.2%, black 17.8%,
  asian 17.7%, other 18.5%. chi-square p=0.045 -- borderline; white patients have
  the lowest ORR. (h20: SUPPORTED, weakly.)
* After clinical adjustment, no race coefficient is significant; the borderline
  univariate signal is not independent of the clinical confounders. (h23: SUPPORTED.)
* Receipt of feature_006 is essentially flat across race (28.2-30.4%) and insurance
  (27.4-28.9%). No major treatment-access disparity in this cohort. (h22: SUPPORTED.)
* Insurance: unadjusted chi-square NS (p=0.23), but after clinical adjustment
  private vs. medicaid OR 1.092 (p=0.020); medicare vs. medicaid p=0.099; uninsured
  vs. medicaid p=0.244. A small private-vs-Medicaid ORR disparity persists.
  (h21: SUPPORTED.)

ITERATION 7: DOSE-RESPONSE CHARACTERIZATION
-------------------------------------------
* log(feature_063) gave a tighter fit than the linear form (coef -0.056, p=5e-7),
  consistent with a multiplicative inflammatory/tumor-burden marker. (h24: SUPPORTED.)
* feature_011 octile decomposition shows monotonic ORR decrease 19.2% -> 13.2% with no
  threshold; approximately log-linear. (h25: SUPPORTED.)
* Two additional binary features survived adjusted screening: feature_007 (OR 1.085,
  p=7.3e-4, positive) and feature_112 (OR 0.904, p=0.029, negative). (h26 SUPPORTED;
  h27 SUPPORTED.)

ITERATION 8: MECHANISTIC CONFIRMATION OF THE PREDICTIVE-BIOMARKER PATTERN
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
Stratified the cohort by feature_092 quartile and re-tested the feature_006 effect:

  Q1 (lowest feature_092):  ORR diff +0.5 pp, p=0.53 (NS)
  Q2:                       ORR diff +0.8 pp, p=0.31 (NS)
  Q3:                       ORR diff +1.2 pp, p=0.10 (NS)
  Q4 (highest feature_092): ORR diff +4.7 pp, p=6.4e-10 (HIGHLY SIGNIFICANT;
                            ORR rises from 16.4% (untreated) to 21.1% (treated))

The feature_006 benefit is concentrated in the top quartile of feature_092 -- a
textbook predictive-biomarker pattern. (h28: SUPPORTED.) Related interaction
hypotheses against PS were refuted (h29: NS; h30: NS), and feature_013 x
feature_067 showed no interaction (h31: NS).

ITERATION 9: FINAL MODEL
------------------------
A fully adjusted logistic regression including the feature_006 x feature_092
interaction plus all clinical, lab, demographic, and treatment covariates
(n=50000, pseudo-R^2=0.021, LLR p=1.4e-183) reproduced the predictive-biomarker
pattern. Predicted ORR (other covariates at population values; reference levels
adenocarcinoma / former smoker / Medicare / white):

  feature_092 = 0.05   untreated ORR 15.4%   treated ORR 15.0%   diff approx 0
  feature_092 = 0.75   untreated ORR 15.3%   treated ORR 21.0%   diff +5.7 pp

The interaction OR is 1.81 per unit of feature_092 (95% CI 1.42-2.30, p=1.6e-6).
The private-insurance vs. medicaid disparity persists (OR 1.093, p=0.020).
(h32 SUPPORTED; h33 SUPPORTED.)

ITERATION 10: NULL CONFIRMATION
-------------------------------
In the final adjusted model:
* age (feature_078) coef +0.0003 per year (p=0.79; null) -- (h34: SUPPORTED)
* squamous vs adenocarcinoma OR 1.04 (p=0.15; null) -- (h35: SUPPORTED)
* smoking status NS (former p=0.67, never p=0.75) -- (h36: SUPPORTED)

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. The strongest single signal in the dataset is a *predictive-biomarker* effect:
   feature_006 (a binary therapy-like indicator, prev 28.5%) yields a meaningful ORR
   benefit only in patients with high feature_092 (a continuous biomarker bounded in
   [0, 0.8] resembling a fractional expression value such as PD-L1 TPS). The feature_006
   x feature_092 interaction is OR 1.81 / unit, p=1.6e-6, with a 4.7 pp absolute ORR
   gain in the top biomarker quartile and essentially no benefit in lower quartiles.
2. A coherent set of prognostic features behaves consistently with the clinical
   literature on advanced NSCLC outcomes: worse performance status (feature_051),
   higher comorbidity/burden index (feature_011), lower albumin-like (feature_099),
   higher LDH-like (feature_063), and the binary indicators feature_013 and
   feature_067 all independently lower ORR. feature_007 and feature_039 (high-prev
   binaries) confer small positive associations.
3. Age, histology, smoking status, and -- after adjustment -- race do not have
   independent associations with ORR. A small private-vs-Medicaid insurance
   disparity persists (private OR 1.09, p=0.02).
4. No interactions of feature_006 with histology, smoking, performance status, or
   feature_013 were detected; the predictive-biomarker effect operates on top of an
   approximately additive (on the log-odds scale) prognostic background.
