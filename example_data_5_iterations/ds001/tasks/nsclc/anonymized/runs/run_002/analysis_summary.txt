Analysis summary: ds001_nsclc (50,000 NSCLC patients, anonymized features 001-123, outcome = objective_response)
=================================================================================================================

Cohort baseline: 16.9% of patients had objective_response = 1 (8,447 / 50,000).

Across 10 iterations, the analysis built up an internally consistent picture of which features predict
response, which subgroups of patients respond best, and where the data does NOT show signal that one
might naively expect.


1. Strongest single-variable signals (Iteration 1)
--------------------------------------------------
Univariable screen of 78 binary features, 9 multi-category features, and 36 continuous features:

  Largest negative associations with response (lower response when present / higher):
    - feature_013 (binary, prevalence 65%): response 15.4% (=1) vs 19.6% (=0); p = 1.1e-33
    - feature_067 (binary, prevalence 25%): response 14.4% vs 17.7%; p = 2.0e-18
    - feature_051 (ordinal 0/1/2): response 21.1% / 15.7% / 11.1% across the three levels; p = 2.9e-93
      (the single largest p-value-driven signal in the dataset, and the monotonic shape is consistent
      with an ECOG performance-status-like interpretation)
    - feature_011 (continuous, range 0-28, mean 3.84): mean 3.38 (responders) vs 3.93 (non-responders);
      p = 2.8e-35; binned response rates fall smoothly from 19.1% (0-1) to 12.8% (>10)
    - feature_063 (continuous, right-skewed): mean 5.78 (R+) vs 6.15 (R-); p = 4.2e-04

  Largest positive associations:
    - feature_006 (binary, prevalence 29%): response 18.2% vs 16.4%; p = 1.0e-06
    - feature_007 (binary, prevalence 45%): 17.5% vs 16.4%; p = 5.9e-04
    - feature_039 (binary, prevalence 50%): 17.4% vs 16.4%; p = 3.4e-03
    - feature_099 (continuous, range 1.7-5.5): small but significant (p = 1.3e-04)
    - feature_092 (continuous, range 0-0.8): tiny univariable difference (mean 0.313 vs 0.306, p = 3.8e-03)

  Most other features (smoking, race, insurance, histology, the bulk of the 100+ binary features) showed
  no meaningful univariable signal.


2. Multivariable picture (Iteration 2)
--------------------------------------
A logistic regression with all features simultaneously (N = 50,000, pseudo R^2 = 0.022, LLR p = 1.2e-134)
confirmed that the top univariable predictors remain independent of each other:

  Independently negative (p < 0.001 in the full model):
    feature_051 (OR 0.77 per +1 SD), feature_013 (OR 0.74), feature_011 (OR 0.86 per +1 SD),
    feature_067 (OR 0.77), feature_063 (OR 0.96 per +1 SD).

  Independently positive (p < 0.01):
    feature_006 (OR 1.14), feature_099 (OR 1.05/SD), feature_007 (OR 1.08), feature_039 (OR 1.08),
    feature_092 (OR 1.04/SD).

Race (feature_123 = "black": OR 1.08, p = 0.029) and insurance (feature_005 = "medicaid": OR 0.91,
p = 0.018) remained borderline-significant after adjustment for the clinical features. Other
demographic categories were not.


3. Subgroup / interaction effects
---------------------------------
A series of two- and three-way interaction tests (Iterations 3-7) showed that interactions are NOT
ubiquitous in this cohort -- many plausible candidates produced null results -- but a small number of
interactions are very strong.

Null interactions (no evidence of effect modification):
  - feature_006 / feature_013 / feature_067 with feature_051 (PS-like): all interaction p > 0.30.
    The PS-like feature acts roughly additively with treatment / biomarker features on the log-odds scale.
  - The same predictors with histology (feature_043 squamous vs adenocarcinoma): all p > 0.40.
  - Smoking-status (feature_057 never/current/former) interactions: all p > 0.35.
  - feature_011 (metastatic-burden-like) interactions with anything tested: all p > 0.13.
  - feature_013 x feature_067 interaction: p = 0.50; the two strongest negative predictors are additive.

Significant interactions:
  (a) feature_006 x feature_007: coef +0.149, p = 4.3e-03
      feature_006 x feature_039: coef +0.184, p = 4.1e-04
      feature_007 x feature_039: coef +0.146, p = 2.4e-03
      The three favorable binary predictors are mutually synergistic.

  (b) Three-way interaction of feature_006 x feature_007 x feature_039 (Iteration 5):
      The triple-positive cell (n = 3,216) has response rate 23.0%, vs ~16-17% in every other cell of
      the 2x2x2 table. The 3-way interaction coefficient is +0.39 (p = 1.8e-04). All two-way terms
      attenuate to non-significance once the triple is in the model -- the interaction is genuinely
      higher-order, not a sum of pairwise effects.

  (c) feature_092 x feature_006 (continuous-x-binary, Iteration 6): coef +0.59, p = 1.2e-06.
      In feature_006 = 0 patients (n = 35,742), feature_092 has essentially no association with
      response (rank-quintile trend p = 0.87; binned rates 0.16-0.17 across the entire feature_092
      range). In feature_006 = 1 patients (n = 14,258), response rises from ~17% at low feature_092
      to 23% in the top quintile (rank-quintile trend p = 3.9e-06).
      Threshold analysis (Iteration 8): the curve is roughly flat below ~0.5 and then accelerates --
      among feature_006 = 1 patients with feature_092 >= 0.6, response rate is 28.5% (n = 1,512); at
      >= 0.7 it reaches 30.0% (n = 404). This is the textbook signature of a *predictive* biomarker
      (feature_092 only matters within the feature_006 stratum).

  (d) Three-way interaction feature_006 x feature_092 x feature_007 (Iteration 7): coef +1.67,
      p = 1.3e-11 -- the strongest single interaction in the analysis. The favorable feature_092
      effect is gated by both feature_006 AND feature_007 being positive.

  (e) Three-way interaction feature_006 x feature_092 x feature_013 (Iteration 7): coef +0.58,
      p = 0.02 -- a smaller secondary signal indicating mild amplification of the feature_006 x
      feature_092 synergy in feature_013-positive patients (direction opposite to the original guess).


4. Demographic and socioeconomic findings (Iteration 5)
-------------------------------------------------------
After adjustment for the clinical predictors:
  - Black patients (n = 6,008) had a slightly HIGHER objective response rate than white patients
    (n = 32,607): adjusted OR 1.085, p = 0.029. The other race contrasts (asian, hispanic, other)
    pointed in the same direction but were not significant individually.
  - Medicaid-insured patients (n = 6,999) had a slightly LOWER response than privately insured patients
    (n = 19,823): adjusted OR 0.92, p = 0.020. Medicare and uninsured patients did not differ from
    private insurance after adjustment.
These are small absolute differences (~1.5 percentage points) but they persist after controlling for the
strongest clinical features, suggesting either residual confounding (e.g., access to or selection of
treatments not captured in the available features) or genuine subgroup differences in response biology
or treatment patterns.


5. Non-linearity and confirmatory model (Iterations 8-9)
--------------------------------------------------------
  - feature_011 effect is essentially linear on the log-odds (quadratic term p = 0.46).
  - feature_092 effect is highly non-linear, but ONLY within the feature_006 = 1 subgroup, with a
    threshold around 0.5-0.6.
  - In a final adjusted model carrying all the main effects, the feature_006 x feature_092
    interaction, the feature_006 x feature_007 x feature_039 triple, race and insurance, and
    histology / smoking, the following remained significant at p < 0.05:
      negative effects: feature_013, feature_067, feature_011, feature_051, feature_063, ins_medicaid
      positive effects: feature_099, race_black
      interactions: feature_006 x feature_092 (+0.59, p = 1.7e-06),
                    feature_006 x feature_007 x feature_039 (+0.37, p = 4.4e-11)
    The main effects of feature_006, feature_007, feature_039, and feature_092 themselves became
    non-significant after the interaction terms were entered, indicating that those predictors act
    almost entirely through their joint synergy rather than as independent main effects.
  - Histology (squamous vs adenocarcinoma) and smoking status (never / current / former) had no
    independent association with response after adjustment.


6. Predicted top and bottom subgroups (Iteration 10)
----------------------------------------------------
The model predicts that very specific patient profiles should respond very differently:
  - "Best" profile: feature_006 = 1, feature_007 = 1, feature_039 = 1, feature_092 above its
    median (~0.30), feature_013 = 0, feature_067 = 0, feature_051 = 0.  n = 164. Empirical
    response rate = 36.6% (about 2.2x the cohort average of 16.9%).
  - "Worst" profile: feature_013 = 1, feature_067 = 1, feature_051 = 2, feature_006 = 0.
    n = 879. Empirical response rate = 8.5% (about half the cohort average).
Both profiles validated the model in held-in subgroup checks.


7. Overall conclusions
----------------------
(i) Clinical / staging features dominate. The single largest predictor of objective_response in this
    cohort is feature_051 (an ordinal 0/1/2 variable that behaves like ECOG performance status), followed
    by two strongly negative binary features (feature_013 and feature_067) and a continuous metastatic-
    burden-like feature (feature_011). These four together account for most of the explainable variance.

(ii) Biomarker-treatment synergy. There is a striking *predictive-biomarker* pattern: feature_092
     (continuous, range 0-0.8, ~10% exact zeros) has near-zero average effect overall, but a strong
     threshold-like effect *only* in patients with feature_006 = 1. In that subgroup, response climbs
     from ~17% to ~28-30% as feature_092 crosses ~0.5-0.6. This is the analytic signature one expects
     when feature_006 represents a class of treatment whose mechanism depends on a high biomarker
     reading -- analogous to PD-L1 high tumors and immune-checkpoint inhibitors in NSCLC.

(iii) Higher-order favorable synergy. The combination feature_006 + feature_007 + feature_039 (all
      three positive together) drives response rate to 23%, a clearly super-additive effect. Layering
      high feature_092 on top of that combination pushes response to ~29% (n ~1,600). These combinations
      are where the predictable benefit lives.

(iv) Demographic disparities are small but real. After controlling for the strongest clinical
     predictors, Black patients responded slightly more often (~1.4 percentage points absolute,
     adjusted OR 1.08) and Medicaid-insured patients slightly less often (~1.6 points, adjusted
     OR 0.92) than their reference groups. Histology, smoking status, and other insurance categories
     did not differ.

(v) Many plausible interactions are absent. The performance-status-like feature_051, histology, and
    smoking did NOT modify any of the main treatment / biomarker effects. The two largest negative
    predictors (feature_013, feature_067) act additively. The metastatic-burden-like feature_011 acts
    independently of every other feature tested. This "no interaction" finding is itself useful: in this
    cohort, the negative prognostic factors lower response uniformly across treatment subgroups.

(vi) Most of the 124 features carry no signal. Only ~10 features account for essentially all the
     statistically detectable variation in objective_response. The remaining 100+ features showed no
     univariable association and no detectable role in interactions.
