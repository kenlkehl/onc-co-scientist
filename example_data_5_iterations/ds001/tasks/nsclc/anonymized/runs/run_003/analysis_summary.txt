ds001_nsclc — Analysis Summary
================================================================
Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patient records, 123 anonymized features and a
single binary outcome (objective_response). Overall ORR = 16.89%
(8447 / 50000). Each row = one patient; no missing values.

The analysis proceeded through 10 propose-test-refine iterations,
beginning with a univariate screen and progressively layering in
multivariable adjustment, interaction probes, and equity / subgroup
analyses.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 1 — univariate screen
----------------------------------------------------------------
For each of the 123 features, an appropriate marginal test against
objective_response was run (chi-square for categorical features;
Welch t-test for continuous). 13 of 123 features were significant
at p < 0.05 and 8 at p < 0.001.

Top hits:
  feature_051 (3-level ordinal, 0/1/2): ORR 21.1% / 15.7% / 11.0%
    chi-square p = 2.9e-93, max-min absolute ORR difference 10.0
    percentage points. By far the strongest signal in the dataset.
  feature_011 (continuous, range 0-28): mean 3.38 in responders vs
    3.93 in non-responders (Welch p = 2.8e-35).
  feature_013 (binary, 65 % = 1): ORR 15.4 % vs 19.6 %
    (chi-square p = 1.1e-33).
  feature_067 (binary, 25 % = 1): ORR 14.4 % vs 17.7 %
    (p = 2.0e-18).
  feature_006 (binary, 29 % = 1): ORR 18.2 % vs 16.4 %
    (p = 1.0e-6).
  feature_099 (continuous, range 1.7-5.5): t-test p = 1.3e-4.
  feature_063 (continuous, right-skewed): t-test p = 4.2e-4.
  feature_007 (binary, ~45 % = 1): ORR 17.5 % vs 16.4 %
    (p = 5.9e-4).

----------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 2 — characterizing the strongest signals
----------------------------------------------------------------
feature_051's three levels show a clean monotone gradient
(ORR 21.1 → 15.7 → 11.0 %). In a logistic regression with level 0
as reference, the level-1 and level-2 log-odds are -0.367 and -0.776
(both p < 1e-15) — nearly equally spaced, supporting an ordinal
interpretation. This is the kind of pattern expected from a clinical
ordinal such as PD-L1 tumor proportion score (0 / 1-49 / ≥50 %),
treatment line, or stage.

feature_011 deciles produce ORRs 19.2 → 18.2 → 16.7 → 17.2 → 15.9
→ 15.7 → 14.3 → 13.2 % — approximately monotone declining across
the range. The univariate per-SD log-odds is -0.149.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 5 — demographic equity
----------------------------------------------------------------
Tests for allocation disparities and differential efficacy:

  Distribution of feature_051 by race (asian / black / hispanic /
  other / white) — chi-square p = 0.639. Level-2 share spans only
  13.6-15.3 %.
  Distribution by insurance — p = 0.248.
  Distribution by smoking — p = 0.093.
  Distribution by histology — p = 0.34.
  Race × feature_051 interaction (LR test, df = 8) p = 0.982.
  Insurance × feature_051 interaction (LR test, df = 6) p = 0.258.

Within every race subgroup, the monotone feature_051 ORR gradient is
preserved (e.g., white 20.7 / 15.4 / 10.7 %; black 21.9 / 16.4 / 12.5 %;
hispanic 21.8 / 15.7 / 11.2 %). All four equity hypotheses
(allocation by race, allocation by insurance, race-modified
efficacy, insurance-modified efficacy) were REFUTED.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 6 — five 0-4 ordinal features and other categoricals
----------------------------------------------------------------
The five 0-4 ordinal features (feature_026, _033, _018, _045, _042),
which by their structure resembled grade / stage scales, did NOT
show monotone or significant associations with ORR at conventional
alpha (univariate chi-square p ≥ 0.10 for each; level-by-level ORRs
all within ± 1.5 percentage points of the cohort mean). Smoking and
histology were also REFUTED as predictors after adjustment.

----------------------------------------------------------------
Synthesis
----------------------------------------------------------------
Hypotheses SUPPORTED across iterations:
  - feature_051 is the dominant ordinal driver of objective_response
    (monotone 21.1 → 15.7 → 11.0 % across levels 0/1/2).
  - feature_011 (continuous, 0-28): negative monotone effect.
  - feature_013, feature_067 (binary): negative effects, additive.
  - feature_006, feature_007, feature_039 (binary): modest positive.
  - feature_099 (~albumin), feature_092: positive continuous effects.
  - log1p(feature_063) (~inflammatory): negative effect.
  - feature_076 (+) and feature_112 (-): adjusted associations.
  - The dominant features' effects do NOT vary by feature_051 level
    or by self-reported race / insurance — additive log-odds model.

Hypotheses REFUTED:
  - Treatment / biomarker allocation differs by race or insurance.
  - The effect of feature_051 differs by race or insurance (no
    biological-by-demographic interaction is detectable in this
    cohort).
  - Age, smoking status, histology, and the five 0-4 ordinal
    features (feature_026, _033, _018, _045, _042) influence ORR.
  - Super-additive feature_013 × feature_067 interaction.
  - feature_011 × feature_013 / × feature_067 / × feature_051
    interactions.

Overall conclusion: response in this NSCLC cohort is largely
captured by an additive logistic model with one dominant ordinal
feature (feature_051), one continuous burden-like feature
(feature_011), two negative binary covariates (feature_013,
feature_067), several modest positive binary covariates
(feature_006, _007, _039, _076), two positive continuous lab
features (feature_099, feature_092), and one negative log-scale
inflammatory marker (log feature_063). Equity-relevant variables
(race, insurance, smoking) showed neither allocation differences
nor differential efficacy across treatment / biomarker strata,
suggesting that — within the limits of these de-identified data —
there is no detectable demographic disparity in either who is
classified into which feature_051 stratum or in how outcomes track
that stratification.
