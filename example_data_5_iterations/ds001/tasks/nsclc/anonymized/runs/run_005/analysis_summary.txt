ds001_nsclc — Analysis summary
================================

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patients with 123 de-identified features (78 binary, 10
multi-category, 36 continuous) and a single binary outcome `objective_response`.
Overall objective response rate (ORR) in the cohort = 16.9% (8,447 / 50,000).

Across 10 iterations the propose-test-refine loop went from a univariate
screen to multivariable modeling, subgroup heterogeneity testing, formal
interaction tests, dose-response by quartile of continuous markers, ordinal
trend tests on multi-level features, a sign-corrected biomarker composite, a
parsimonious adjusted model, and a final adjusted assessment of demographic
disparities.

Iteration 1 — Univariate screen
------------------------------
The strongest univariate signals against `objective_response` were:
  * feature_051 (ordinal 0/1/2): ORR 21.1% / 15.7% / 11.1% across the three
    levels; per-level beta = -0.254, p = 1.7e-93. Direction and magnitude are
    consistent with a PD-L1-style biomarker that strongly tracks response,
    although in the unexpected direction (higher level → lower ORR), so it is
    safer to label it as "an ordinal severity-like feature" without claiming
    biology.
  * feature_011 (continuous, 0-28, mean 3.84): higher values lower ORR;
    per-SD beta = -0.149, p = 1.8e-32.
  * feature_013 (binary): ORR 15.4% (=1) vs 19.6% (=0), beta = -0.295,
    p = 1.3e-33.
  * feature_067 (binary): ORR 14.4% vs 17.7%, beta = -0.252, p = 2.0e-18.
  * feature_006 (binary): positive, ORR 18.2% vs 16.4%, beta = +0.127,
    p = 9.8e-7.
Several other binary and continuous features (feature_007, feature_039,
feature_092, feature_099, feature_063) showed weaker but significant
associations.

Iteration 2 — Joint multivariable model
---------------------------------------
A logistic regression simultaneously including the ten leading univariate
features confirmed that each carries independent information: feature_051
(beta = -0.382), feature_011 (-0.039), feature_013 (-0.302), feature_067
(-0.259), feature_006 (+0.129), feature_007 (+0.080), feature_039 (+0.077),
feature_092 (+0.172), feature_099 (+0.092). Pseudo-R^2 ~0.020, LRT
p ~3e-183. All directions matched univariate findings; none flipped.

Iteration 3 — Demographic subgroup heterogeneity
------------------------------------------------
Unadjusted ORR was very flat across most demographic strata.
  * Smoking (feature_057, never/former/current): chi-square p = 0.27;
    differences <1 percentage point. Hypothesis of smoking-related
    heterogeneity NOT supported in the unadjusted comparison.
  * Race (feature_123): chi-square p = 0.045 (marginal); white patients had
    the lowest ORR (16.5%), with black 17.8%, asian 17.7%, hispanic 17.2%,
    other 18.5%.
  * Insurance (feature_005): p = 0.23; numerically lowest in medicaid
    (16.1%), highest in private (17.2%).
  * Histology (feature_043, adenocarcinoma vs squamous): p = 0.078;
    squamous slightly higher.
Conclusion: only race showed a marginal unadjusted signal worth following up.

Iteration 4 — Interactions among top binary features
-----------------------------------------------------
Three pairwise interactions were significant on the multiplicative scale:
  * feature_006 x feature_039: beta = +0.042, p = 4.1e-4
  * feature_007 x feature_039: beta = +0.036, p = 2.4e-3
  * feature_006 x feature_007: beta = +0.033, p = 4.3e-3
All three were positive (synergistic) and involved features already in the
top-tier predictor list. The next most-suggestive interaction was
feature_063 x feature_099 (p = 0.09), and all remaining pairwise tests had
p > 0.10. The hypothesis that further significant pairs exist within this
short list was not supported.

Iteration 5 — Quartile dose-response for continuous markers
-----------------------------------------------------------
Confirmed monotonic trends for the leading continuous features:
  * feature_011: ORR 18.5% / 16.3% / 14.2% across bins (only 3 quantile bins
    formed because of ties at integer values). Strongly negative.
  * feature_099: 16.0% / 16.6% / 17.7% / 17.7% across quartiles. Positive.
  * feature_063: 18.1% / 16.8% / 16.7% / 16.0%. Negative.
  * feature_092: 16.7% / 16.5% / 16.5% / 17.8%. Effect concentrated in
    Q4 rather than smoothly monotonic.
Per-SD logistic betas remain significant for all four (p ranging 3e-3 to
2e-32).

Iteration 6 — Ordinal multi-cat features (0-4 scales)
-----------------------------------------------------
Five 0-4 ordinal features (feature_026, feature_033, feature_018,
feature_045, feature_042) were tested for monotonic trend with response.
Only feature_033 reached p < 0.05 (per-SD beta = -0.025, p = 0.040), and
even there the absolute spread across categories was <2 percentage points
(17.1% at level 0 → 16.1% at level 4). The remaining four ordinal scales
showed no meaningful trend (all p > 0.24). Hypotheses h6.1, h6.3, h6.4,
h6.5 not supported; h6.2 weakly supported.

Iteration 7 — Effect modification of feature_051 by smoking / histology
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
The negative association of feature_051 with response was tested for
modification by ever-smoking and by squamous histology, and the smoking
effect itself was tested for race-modification.
  * feature_051 x ever-smoker interaction beta = -0.053, p = 0.30 (NS).
  * feature_051 x squamous interaction beta = -0.049, p = 0.22 (NS).
  * Race x ever-smoker: all interaction terms p > 0.15.
Conclusion: the dominant biomarker effect appears homogeneous across
smoking and histology subgroups in this cohort; no clinically meaningful
effect modification detected.

Iteration 8 — Composite continuous biomarker score
--------------------------------------------------
A sign-corrected z-score sum of the top five continuous features
(feature_011, feature_099, feature_063, feature_092, feature_044) gave a
composite that ordered patients monotonically by response: quartile rates
14.5% / 16.3% / 17.6% / 19.2% (a 4.7-percentage-point spread). Per-SD
logistic beta = +0.131, p = 1.8e-26. Adding feature_051 to the same model
left both terms with strong, separate effects (feature_051 beta = -0.382,
composite beta ≈ +0.13), indicating that the ordinal feature_051 and the
continuous-feature composite carry largely orthogonal predictive
information.

Iteration 9 — Refined parsimonious model
----------------------------------------
The 10-term refined model in iteration 2 was re-examined with smoking
status added. Once the clinical/biomarker features were in the model,
ever-smoking dropped to beta ≈ +0.005 (p = 0.89), strongly suggesting that
the small unadjusted differences by smoking status are confounded by other
clinical features. All other terms retained the directions reported in
iteration 1 and remained statistically significant; pseudo-R^2 = 0.019.

Iteration 10 — Disparities adjusted for clinical features
---------------------------------------------------------
Adjusting race for feature_051, ever-smoker, feature_011, feature_013,
feature_067, and feature_006:
  * Black vs white: adjusted beta = +0.080, p = 0.032. Modest but
    statistically significant: Black patients have somewhat higher adjusted
    odds of response than white patients once measured clinical features
    are controlled.
  * Asian, Hispanic, "other" vs white: all p > 0.10, not significantly
    different in the adjusted model.

Adjusting insurance for the same clinical features:
  * Medicaid vs private: adjusted beta = -0.088, p = 0.020. Small but
    statistically significant disparity not explained by the measured
    clinical features (~1.4 percentage points absolute lower response).
  * Medicare and uninsured vs private: not significantly different (both
    p > 0.30).

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. The strongest predictor of objective_response in this cohort is the
   ordinal feature_051 (per-level OR ≈ exp(-0.38) ≈ 0.68), with a clean
   monotonic dose-response pattern.
2. A small set of binary features (feature_013, feature_067 negative;
   feature_006, feature_007, feature_039 positive) and a handful of
   continuous features (feature_011, feature_063 negative; feature_092,
   feature_099 positive) each carry independent, modest signal.
3. Three pairwise binary x binary interactions among feature_006,
   feature_007, and feature_039 are positive and statistically significant,
   indicating modest super-additive effects on response.
4. Most demographic strata (smoking, insurance, histology) show no
   unadjusted heterogeneity in response. The 0-4 ordinal multi-cat features
   are mostly null.
5. After adjusting for the leading clinical/biomarker features, two
   demographic disparities reach significance: a small advantage for Black
   versus white patients (+0.080 log-odds, p = 0.032) and a small
   disadvantage for Medicaid versus private insurance (-0.088 log-odds,
   p = 0.020).
6. The ordinal biomarker feature_051 and a sign-corrected composite of the
   top continuous markers contribute largely orthogonal predictive
   information; modeling both improves discrimination beyond either alone.
7. Effect modification by smoking status, histology, and race for the
   leading biomarker effect was tested and not detected.

These conclusions reflect statistical associations only; without ground
truth on which features represent therapy assignment versus baseline
biology versus laboratory values, no causal interpretation is offered.
