Analysis summary for ds001_nsclc (N = 50,000 NSCLC patients; outcome = objective_response, overall ORR = 16.9%)
==============================================================================================================

Across 10 propose-test-refine iterations I probed (1) overall treatment effects, (2) treatment-by-biomarker
interactions for the four available agents, (3) prognostic factors and patient-reported symptoms, (4) demographic
and socioeconomic disparities, (5) multivariable adjustment, and (6) higher-order (3-way) interactions.

1) Overall treatment effects on ORR (Iter 1)
--------------------------------------------
Of the four treatments, only treatment_pembrolizumab showed a statistically significant overall ORR benefit
(17.4% on vs 16.4% off; OR 1.07; chi-square p = 3.4e-3). treatment_sotorasib (p = 0.83), treatment_olaparib
(p = 0.72), and treatment_osimertinib (p = 0.18) had no overall main effect on ORR. This is the first hint that
benefit (where it exists) is concentrated in particular subgroups rather than averaged over the whole cohort.

2) Biomarker x treatment interactions for matched targeted therapies (Iter 2, Iter 9)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The conventional NSCLC expectation is that osimertinib benefits EGFR-mutant tumors, sotorasib benefits KRAS G12C,
and olaparib benefits BRCA2-mutant tumors. In this dataset NONE of these matched-biomarker x matched-therapy
interactions was significant, either univariately or after multivariable adjustment:
  - treatment_osimertinib x egfr_mutation: interaction logOR +0.024, p = 0.76 (adjusted +0.025, p = 0.75).
  - treatment_sotorasib x kras_g12c: interaction logOR +0.059, p = 0.43 (adjusted +0.064, p = 0.39).
  - treatment_olaparib x brca2_mutation: interaction logOR -0.178, p = 0.28 (adjusted -0.178, p = 0.28); the
    point estimate is in the opposite direction (BRCA2+ patients had numerically lower ORR on olaparib: 14.6%
    vs 16.8%).
The absence of these three signals is the most striking negative finding of the analysis. Whether this reflects
features of the underlying cohort (e.g., heavily pretreated, mixed lines, off-label use) or of the data itself
cannot be determined from the dataset alone, but it materially constrains any predictive use of these
therapy-biomarker pairs in this cohort.

3) Pembrolizumab predictive biomarkers (Iter 2, Iter 3, Iter 5, Iter 9)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
In sharp contrast to the targeted therapies, multiple pembrolizumab-relevant biomarkers showed strong,
significant predictive interactions:
  - treatment_pembrolizumab x pdl1_tps (continuous): logOR +0.591, p = 1.6e-7 (adjusted +0.595, p = 1.7e-7).
  - treatment_pembrolizumab x pdl1_hi (TPS >= 0.5): logOR +0.376, p = 5.0e-11. In PD-L1-high patients,
    pembrolizumab raised ORR from 15.6% to 20.9% (+5.4 pp); in PD-L1-low patients there was no benefit
    (16.4% on vs 16.6% off).
  - treatment_pembrolizumab x tmb_high: logOR +0.184, p = 4.2e-4. TMB-high patients on pembro had ORR 19.7%
    vs 16.7% off; TMB-low patients showed essentially no benefit.
  - treatment_pembrolizumab x stk11_mutation: logOR -0.211, p = 1.8e-3. STK11 mutation attenuated pembro
    benefit (consistent with the published IO-resistance signal in NSCLC).
  - treatment_pembrolizumab x sex_female: logOR +0.146, p = 2.4e-3. Pembro benefit was larger in females.
  - treatment_pembrolizumab x histology (squamous vs adeno): NS (p = 0.58).
  - treatment_pembrolizumab x never_smoker: NS (p = 0.29).
  - treatment_pembrolizumab x ECOG / stage_iv / brain_mets: NS interactions (these are strong main-effect
    prognostics but not effect-modifiers for IO benefit here).
A 3-way interaction (pembro x pdl1_hi x stk11) was significant (logOR -0.322, p = 0.049): the PD-L1-high
benefit of pembrolizumab is essentially eliminated when STK11 is co-mutated. The 6.4 pp pembro benefit in
PD-L1-high / STK11-WT collapses to ~0 in PD-L1-high / STK11-mutant patients.

4) Prognostic factors (Iter 3, Iter 4, Iter 7, Iter 10)
-------------------------------------------------------
Strong, classical prognostic factors were present and behaved as expected:
  - ECOG performance status: per-unit logOR -0.375, p = 1.7e-93 (the largest single signal in the dataset).
    ECOG 0 ORR ~21.1% vs ECOG > 0 ~14.6%.
  - stage_iv: logOR -0.295, p = 1.3e-33 (15.4% vs 19.6%).
  - has_brain_mets: logOR -0.252, p = 2.0e-18 (14.4% vs 17.8%).
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo: logOR -0.0385/percent, p = 1.8e-32.
  - albumin_g_dl: logOR +0.091/(g/dL), p = 1.4e-4.
  - crp_mg_l: logOR -0.0049/(mg/L), p = 6.9e-4.
  - sex_female: logOR +0.083, p = 5.7e-4 (also a pembro effect-modifier; see above).
  - PD-L1 TPS (main effect): logOR +0.166, p = 3.2e-3; TMB-high (main effect): logOR +0.127, p = 9.8e-7.
Other lab values (LDH, hemoglobin, alkaline phosphatase, sodium, calcium, BMI, SpO2, NLR, WBC, ANC, ALC,
CEA, PSA, smoking pack-years) showed no significant univariate association with ORR. Among the other
metastatic sites (liver, bone, adrenal, pleural effusion, pericardial effusion, contralateral lung), none
was significantly associated with ORR. Among co-mutations beyond stk11, none of tp53, keap1, pik3ca was
significantly associated with ORR as a main effect.

5) Patient-reported symptoms and comorbidities (Iter 10)
--------------------------------------------------------
Among 5 PRO grades, only fatigue_grade was significantly associated with ORR (logOR -0.022, p = 0.040);
pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite-loss were not. Among 15 chronic comorbidities, only coronary_artery_disease
reached p < 0.05 (logOR -0.062, p = 0.049), borderline given multiple testing. Comorbidity burden was
largely uninformative for ORR in this cohort.

6) Demographics and socioeconomic factors (Iter 8)
--------------------------------------------------
  - race_ethnicity: no group differed significantly from the asian reference; the white-vs-asian contrast was
    borderline (logOR -0.083, p = 0.10) but the four-way comparison did not reach significance.
  - insurance_type: privately insured patients had a small but significant ORR advantage over medicaid
    (logOR +0.077, p = 0.040); the medicare and uninsured contrasts were not significant.
  - rural_residence (p = 0.49) and education_years (p = 0.40): no significant associations.
  - There was no meaningful disparity in pembrolizumab assignment across race or insurance categories
    (range ~2 pp) - treatments appear approximately balanced in this cohort.

7) Pharmacogenomic SNPs (Iter 7)
--------------------------------
Across 23 SNP variants tested as main effects on ORR, the smallest p-values were snp_rs7412 (logOR +0.100,
p = 0.018) and snp_rs1050828 (logOR -0.099, p = 0.031). Neither survives the Bonferroni threshold of
0.05/23 = 2.17e-3. There is no convincing evidence that any single SNP is associated with ORR in this
cohort.

8) Multivariable confirmation (Iter 9)
--------------------------------------
A single logistic model containing core prognostic factors (ECOG, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, age, sex,
albumin, weight_loss, CRP, race), plus pembrolizumab-by-(PD-L1, TMB, STK11) interactions and the three
matched targeted-therapy-by-biomarker interactions, confirmed all of the above conclusions:
  - ECOG, stage_iv, brain mets, weight loss, CRP all retained their negative prognostic signals (p < 0.001).
  - Albumin and sex_female retained positive prognostic signals.
  - Pembrolizumab x PD-L1 interaction remained the dominant predictive term (p = 1.7e-7).
  - Pembrolizumab x TMB-high (p = 1e-3) and pembrolizumab x STK11 (p = 3e-3) remained significant.
  - The matched osimertinib/sotorasib/olaparib biomarker interactions remained non-significant.
  - No race contrast was significant after adjustment.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
- The single clearest treatment story in this cohort is pembrolizumab: small overall benefit, large benefit
  in PD-L1-high (and TMB-high) tumors, attenuated by STK11 mutation, with the PD-L1 benefit collapsing in
  STK11 co-mutants - a finding that rewards subgroup analysis over reliance on the marginal main effect.
- The matched targeted therapies (osimertinib/EGFR, sotorasib/KRAS G12C, olaparib/BRCA2) showed no
  significant predictive biomarker interactions in this cohort; the absence of these expected signals is
  the most consequential negative result of the analysis.
- Classical prognostic factors (ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, weight loss, albumin, CRP, sex_female) behaved
  as expected and dominate baseline ORR variation.
- Demographic disparities in ORR were small after adjustment; pembrolizumab assignment was approximately
  balanced across race and insurance categories.
- SNPs and most comorbidities provided no useful predictive information for ORR.
