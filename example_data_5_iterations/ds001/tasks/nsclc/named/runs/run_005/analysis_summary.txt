Analysis summary - ds001_nsclc (50,000 patients, NSCLC EHR cohort)
====================================================================
Outcome: objective_response (binary), baseline ORR = 16.9%.
Treatments: pembrolizumab (50%), sotorasib (35%), olaparib (30%), osimertinib (30%).

Iteration 1 - Main treatment effects
- treatment_pembrolizumab: ORR 17.39% vs 16.40% (+0.99 pp, chi-square p=0.003). Small but
  significant marginal benefit.
- treatment_sotorasib: +0.08 pp, p=0.83. No marginal effect.
- treatment_olaparib: +0.13 pp, p=0.72. No marginal effect.
- treatment_osimertinib: -0.50 pp, p=0.18. No marginal effect (slightly negative trend).
The four targeted/IO drugs are not delivering uniform marginal benefit, motivating
biomarker-stratified analyses.

Iteration 2 - Matched-target biomarker interactions
- osimertinib x egfr_mutation interaction log-OR +0.024, p=0.76. Within EGFR-mutant patients
  (n=6517), ORR was 16.4% on osimertinib vs 16.6% off (p=0.87). NO targeted benefit detected.
- sotorasib x kras_g12c log-OR +0.059, p=0.43. Within KRAS G12C+ (n=6368): ORR 17.5% vs
  16.6% (p=0.43). NO targeted benefit detected.
- olaparib x brca2_mutation log-OR -0.177, p=0.28. Within BRCA2-mut (n=1519): ORR 14.6% on
  olaparib vs 16.8% off. Direction OPPOSITE to expected, not significant.
- pembrolizumab x tmb_high log-OR +0.184, p=4.2e-4. Within TMB-high (n=14,258): ORR 19.7%
  on pembro vs 16.7% off (+2.95 pp, p=5.4e-6). In TMB-low: no effect (+0.2 pp, p=0.61).
  CONFIRMED: pembrolizumab benefit is concentrated in TMB-high.

Iteration 3 - PD-L1 TPS and pembrolizumab
- pdl1_tps (continuous, range 0-0.80) main effect on ORR: log-OR +0.166, p=0.003.
- pembrolizumab x pdl1_tps interaction: log-OR +0.591, p=1.6e-7. Strongly positive.
- Stratified at TPS >= 0.5: PD-L1 high (21.5% of cohort) had pembrolizumab ORR 20.9%
  vs 15.6% off (+5.36 pp, p=7.7e-13). PD-L1 low: pembro 16.4% vs 16.6% (p=0.59).
  Pembrolizumab benefit scales with PD-L1 - the canonical clinical pattern.

Iteration 4 - Disease burden / prognostic factors
- ecog_ps log-OR -0.375, p=1.7e-93. Strong negative prognostic signal.
- stage_iv: ORR 15.4% vs 19.6% (-4.25 pp, p=1.1e-33).
- has_brain_mets: ORR 14.4% vs 17.7% (-3.39 pp, p=2.0e-18).
- liver_mets, bone_mets, adrenal_mets, pleural/pericardial effusion, contralateral lung
  mets: NONE significant. Notably the burden signal is concentrated in performance status,
  stage, and brain mets - not in non-brain visceral metastases.

Iteration 5 - Lab values
- albumin_g_dl: log-OR +0.091/g/dL, p=1.4e-4. Higher albumin -> higher response (confirmed).
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: log-OR -0.039/percentage point, p=1.8e-32 (very strong - cachexia).
- crp_mg_l: log-OR -0.005/mg/L, p=6.9e-4 (confirmed; inflammation predicts non-response).
- nlr: log-OR -0.005, p=0.38 (direction matches but not significant univariably).
- ldh_u_l: p=0.51 (null; opposite direction to expected).
- hemoglobin_g_dl: p=0.60 (null).
The "host fitness" axis (albumin / weight loss / CRP) is robust; classical NLR and LDH
do not move the needle in this cohort.

Iteration 6 - Demographics & socioeconomic
- sex_female: ORR 17.5% vs 16.4% (+1.16 pp, p=5.9e-4). Modest female advantage.
- age_years: log-OR +0.0005, p=0.67. NO age effect.
- smoking_status: never 16.4%, former 16.8%, current 17.2%. Differences NOT significant.
- race_ethnicity (ref white 16.5%): black 17.8% (log-OR +0.085, p=0.021, only nominally
  significant racial difference); asian 17.7% p=0.10; hispanic 17.2% p=0.21; other 18.5% p=0.09.
- insurance_type (ref private 17.2%): medicaid 16.1% (log-OR -0.077, p=0.040); medicare
  16.8% p=0.34; uninsured 17.0% p=0.82. Only medicaid-vs-private is nominally significant.
The disparities are small and only marginally significant.

Iteration 7 - Comorbidities & prior therapy
- None of diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart failure, CAD, atrial fibrillation, VTE,
  ILD, autoimmune disease, prior malignancy, or depression/anxiety significantly affects
  ORR. CAD comes closest (-0.86 pp, p=0.051). Comorbidity burden does not appear to drive
  response in this cohort.
- prior_lines_of_therapy: log-OR -0.012, p=0.24 (NS).
- prior_chemotherapy: -0.62 pp, p=0.07 (borderline). prior_immunotherapy/radiation/
  surgery/targeted: NS.

Iteration 8 - SNPs and additional somatic biomarkers
- 23 germline SNPs tested. Smallest p-values: rs7412 (+0.10, p=0.018), rs1050828 (-0.10,
  p=0.031). Bonferroni threshold 0.05/23 = 0.00217 - NO SNP survives correction. Hypothesis
  of a discoverable SNP main effect is REFUTED.
- tp53_mutation (n=22479) p=0.88, keap1_mutation p=0.86, pik3ca_mutation p=0.09,
  msi_high p=0.74. No additional somatic biomarker has a main effect on response.

Iteration 9 - Modulators of pembrolizumab benefit
- pembrolizumab x stk11_mutation log-OR -0.211, p=0.0018. Within STK11=0: pembro ORR
  17.7% vs 16.2% (+1.42 pp, p=9.7e-5). Within STK11=1 (n=7451): pembro ORR 15.8% vs 17.3%
  (-1.52 pp, p=0.084). STK11 mutation FLIPS the pembrolizumab effect direction (consistent
  with published evidence that STK11/LKB1 loss is a negative IO predictor).
- pembrolizumab x sex_female log-OR +0.146, p=0.0024. Pembrolizumab benefit appears
  GREATER in women than men.
- osimertinib x has_brain_mets log-OR +0.051, p=0.42. No CNS-specific osimertinib effect
  (which is unexpected clinically - reinforces that the targeted-therapy biomarker signal
  is largely absent from this dataset).

Iteration 10 - Multivariable confirmation
A logistic regression with ecog_ps, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, albumin_g_dl,
weight_loss_pct_6mo, crp_mg_l, sex_female, age_years, pdl1_tps, tmb_high, stk11_mutation,
treatment_pembrolizumab and three pembro interactions yielded these adjusted log-OR
estimates:
  ecog_ps                                 -0.382  (p < 1e-90)
  stage_iv                                -0.302  (p < 1e-30)
  has_brain_mets                          -0.257  (p < 1e-17)
  albumin_g_dl                            +0.092  (p = 1.3e-4)
  weight_loss_pct_6mo                     -0.039  (p < 1e-30)
  crp_mg_l                                -0.005  (p = 4e-4)
  sex_female                              +0.081  (p = 8e-4)
  treatment_pembrolizumab                 -0.132  (p = 0.005)  [main effect at PD-L1=0,
                                                                TMB-low, STK11-wt]
  treatment_pembrolizumab:tmb_high        +0.174  (p = 9.6e-4)
  treatment_pembrolizumab:pdl1_tps        +0.595  (p < 1e-6)
  treatment_pembrolizumab:stk11_mutation  -0.204  (p = 0.003)
  pdl1_tps main                           -0.133  (p = 0.10, NS)
  tmb_high main                           +0.039  (p = 0.30, NS)
  stk11_mutation main                     +0.073  (p = 0.13, NS)
  age_years                               +0.0004 (p = 0.74, NS)
PD-L1, TMB and STK11 only matter through their interaction with pembrolizumab; once those
interactions are present, their main effects shrink and lose significance.

Overall conclusions
1. Response in this NSCLC cohort is dominated by classic prognostic factors: ECOG,
   stage IV, brain mets, albumin, weight loss, and CRP. These are the largest signals.
2. Among the four therapies in the dataset, only pembrolizumab shows treatment effects,
   and that effect is concentrated in PD-L1-high (TPS >= 0.5), TMB-high, and STK11
   wild-type patients - the canonical IO-responder profile - with women appearing to
   benefit more than men.
3. The matched targeted-therapy biomarker pairings (osimertinib-EGFR, sotorasib-KRAS
   G12C, olaparib-BRCA2) show NO detectable benefit in this dataset, even within the
   biomarker-positive subgroups. This is the most surprising finding and warrants caution
   when generalizing.
4. Common comorbidities, prior-therapy flags, and the 23 SNPs tested have no detectable
   independent effect on ORR. Modest but nominally significant disparities exist for
   medicaid (lower ORR vs private) and black race (higher ORR vs white), but the absolute
   differences are small (<= 1-2 pp).
