ds001_nsclc — Analysis Summary

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patients. Outcome: binary objective_response (overall rate 16.9%; n=8,447 responders, n=41,553 non-responders). Across 10 iterations I tested treatment main effects, treatment x biomarker interactions, prognostic clinical and laboratory markers, demographic/socioeconomic disparities, symptom burden, a 23-SNP candidate panel, and a multivariable adjusted model.

================================================================================
Iteration 1 — Treatment main effects
================================================================================
Hypothesis: Each of the four agents (pembrolizumab, sotorasib, olaparib, osimertinib) increases objective_response when compared with patients not receiving that agent (pooled across the rest of the cohort).
- treatment_pembrolizumab: response 17.4% on vs 16.4% off; risk difference +0.0099, chi-square p=0.0034. SUPPORTED, but the absolute effect is small because the cohort is unselected for PD-L1.
- treatment_sotorasib: 16.95% vs 16.87%; RD +0.0008, p=0.83. NOT supported as a main effect — consistent with sotorasib being a KRAS G12C-targeted agent that benefits only the small G12C subset.
- treatment_olaparib: 17.0% vs 16.9%; RD +0.0013, p=0.72. NOT supported.
- treatment_osimertinib: 16.5% vs 17.0%; RD -0.0050, p=0.18. NOT supported.

================================================================================
Iteration 2 — Treatment x biomarker matching (the targeted-therapy story)
================================================================================
Hypothesis: Each targeted agent has a larger effect in its biomarker-defined target population.
- sotorasib x kras_g12c: among sotorasib-treated, response was 17.4% in G12C+ (n=2,246) vs 16.9% in G12C- (n=15,298); RD +0.006, p=0.51. Logistic interaction in the full cohort logOR=+0.06, p=0.43. NOT supported — the expected G12C-selective benefit was absent in this dataset.
- osimertinib x egfr_mutation: subgroup RD -0.002, p=0.85; interaction logOR=+0.02, p=0.76. NOT supported.
- olaparib x brca2_mutation: subgroup RD -0.024, p=0.22; interaction logOR=-0.18, p=0.28. Point estimate even trends in the wrong direction; NOT supported.
- pembrolizumab x tmb_high: subgroup RD +0.032, p=2e-09; interaction logOR=+0.18, p=4.2e-04. SUPPORTED — TMB-high patients on pembrolizumab respond more often than TMB-low patients on pembrolizumab, beyond what TMB does in untreated patients.

The clinically expected genotype-matched gradients for sotorasib/osimertinib/olaparib are essentially absent in these data; the only interaction that emerges from the targeted/biomarker space is pembrolizumab x TMB.

================================================================================
Iteration 3 — PD-L1 and pembrolizumab
================================================================================
Hypothesis: Within pembrolizumab-treated patients, high pdl1_tps drives higher response; in the full cohort, pdl1_tps modifies the pembrolizumab effect.
- Among pembro-treated: PD-L1 ≥ 0.5 → 20.9% response (n=5,342) vs PD-L1 < 0.5 → 16.4% (n=19,666); RD +0.045, p=1.6e-14. SUPPORTED.
- Logistic interaction pdl1_tps:treatment_pembrolizumab logOR=+0.59, p=1.6e-07. SUPPORTED. PD-L1 is, by far, the strongest immunotherapy effect-modifier in the data.

================================================================================
Iteration 4 — Performance status and inflammatory/nutritional labs
================================================================================
- ecog_ps (vs 0): ECOG 1 logOR=-0.36 (p=9e-46), ECOG 2 logOR=-0.77 (p=9e-77). STRONGLY SUPPORTED — clear monotonic decline in response with worse PS.
- albumin_g_dl: per +1 g/dL logOR=+0.091, p=1.4e-04. SUPPORTED — better nutrition associates with response.
- crp_mg_l: per +1 mg/L logOR=-0.0049, p=6.9e-04. SUPPORTED (small magnitude).
- nlr: logOR=-0.005, p=0.38. NOT supported.
- ldh_u_l: logOR=+0.0001, p=0.51. NOT supported.

================================================================================
Iteration 5 — Tumor co-mutations and immunotherapy resistance
================================================================================
- stk11_mutation x pembrolizumab: pembro-treated STK11+ rate 15.8% vs STK11- 17.7%; RD -0.019, p=0.0053. Interaction logOR=-0.21, p=0.0018. SUPPORTED — STK11 reduces pembrolizumab response, consistent with the published immunotherapy-resistance signature.
- keap1_mutation x pembrolizumab: subgroup RD -0.003, p=0.63; interaction logOR=-0.06, p=0.36. Direction is hypothesis-consistent but NOT significant.
- tp53_mutation x pembrolizumab: no effect-modification (p=0.78).

================================================================================
Iteration 6 — Disease burden / metastatic sites
================================================================================
- stage_iv: response 15.4% (IV) vs 19.6% (non-IV); RD -0.043, p=1e-33. SUPPORTED.
- has_brain_mets: 14.4% vs 17.8%; RD -0.034, p=2e-18. SUPPORTED.
- liver_mets: RD +0.005, p=0.34 — surprisingly NOT supported (despite being a classic poor prognostic feature in the immunotherapy literature).
- bone, adrenal, pleural, pericardial, contralateral-lung mets: all non-significant (p=0.18–0.98). The disease-burden signal in this dataset is concentrated in stage_iv and brain mets only.

================================================================================
Iteration 7 — Demographic and socioeconomic disparities
================================================================================
- sex_female: response 17.5% vs 16.4% in males; RD +0.012, p=5.9e-04. Female sex is associated with a small but significant higher response.
- age_years: logOR=+0.0005/yr, p=0.67. Not supported.
- race_ethnicity: white patients have the LOWEST response (16.5%); black patients 17.8% (logOR vs white +0.085, p=0.021); other groups numerically higher but not significant. The expected disadvantage for racial minorities was not observed; if anything, the gradient runs the other way.
- insurance_type: medicaid 16.1% vs private 17.2% (logOR -0.077, p=0.040, significant); medicare and uninsured not significant. Modest insurance gradient consistent with disparity hypothesis but only for medicaid.
- rural_residence: RD -0.003, p=0.50. NOT supported.
- education_years: logOR -0.003/yr, p=0.40. NOT supported.
- smoking_status: response 17.2% current / 16.8% former / 16.4% never — no significant differences.
- pembro x smoking: directionally consistent with the literature (smokers benefit more) but logOR ≈ +0.07 with p ≈ 0.31; NOT significant.

================================================================================
Iteration 8 — Symptoms, weight loss, BMI, histology
================================================================================
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: per +1% logOR=-0.0385, p=2e-32. STRONGLY SUPPORTED — one of the largest prognostic effects in the dataset (responders lost 3.4% vs non-responders 3.9% body weight).
- fatigue_grade: per +1 logOR=-0.022, p=0.040. Modestly supported.
- pain_nrs, dyspnea_grade, cough_grade, appetite_loss_grade: none significant; cough trended in the opposite direction.
- bmi: logOR +0.002/unit, p=0.35. NOT supported (no obesity paradox detected).
- histology: squamous 17.4% vs adeno 16.7%; logOR +0.05, p=0.076. Trend but not significant.

================================================================================
Iteration 9 — Pharmacogenomic / candidate-SNP panel
================================================================================
Hypothesis: At least one of the 23 snp_* variants is associated with response after multiple-testing correction; jointly the panel explains response.
- 2/23 SNPs reached uncorrected p<0.05 (snp_rs7412 logOR +0.10 p=0.018; snp_rs1050828 logOR -0.10 p=0.031), close to the 1.15 hits expected by chance. ZERO survive Bonferroni (threshold 0.0022).
- Joint LR test of all 23 SNPs: LR=19.4, df=23, p=0.68.
NOT supported. The candidate SNP panel has no detectable signal in this cohort, consistent with these polymorphisms being effectively null with respect to response.

================================================================================
Iteration 10 — Multivariable adjusted model
================================================================================
Refined hypotheses: the headline interactions (pembro x PD-L1, pembro x TMB, pembro x STK11) survive adjustment for clinical confounders, and the established prognostic markers (ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, weight loss, albumin, CRP, sex) remain independently significant.

Multivariable logistic model included: ecog_ps (categorical), age, sex_female, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, weight_loss_pct_6mo, albumin_g_dl, crp_mg_l, histology, smoking_status, plus pembrolizumab interactions with pdl1_tps, tmb_high, and stk11_mutation. Pseudo R² = 0.021; LL ratio p ≈ 1e-188.

Adjusted interactions (all SUPPORTED):
- treatment_pembrolizumab : pdl1_tps  logOR = +0.596, p = 1.6e-07. Predicted response on pembro: 0.155 at PDL1=0 vs 0.201 at PDL1=0.7 (off pembro: 0.169 → 0.157). PD-L1 reverses the direction of the pembrolizumab effect.
- treatment_pembrolizumab : tmb_high  logOR = +0.173, p = 9.8e-04.
- treatment_pembrolizumab : stk11_mutation  logOR = -0.204, p = 2.8e-03 (resistance signature confirmed under adjustment).

Independent prognostic effects:
- ECOG 1 vs 0: logOR -0.368 (p=4e-47); ECOG 2 vs 0: -0.777 (p=2e-78).
- stage_iv: -0.302 (p=1e-34); has_brain_mets: -0.257 (p=8e-19).
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.039 per % (p=2e-33).
- albumin_g_dl: +0.092 per g/dL (p=1e-04); crp_mg_l: -0.005 per mg/L (p=4e-04).
- sex_female: +0.081 (p=8e-04).
- histology, smoking_status, age: not independently significant.

================================================================================
Overall conclusions
================================================================================
1. Treatment-biomarker matching is highly selective in this cohort. The full PD-1/PD-L1 paradigm reproduces clearly: pembrolizumab response increases with continuous PD-L1 (and at the ≥0.5 cutoff), is amplified in TMB-high tumors, and is attenuated by STK11 co-mutation (with KEAP1 directionally consistent but not significant). All three pembrolizumab interactions survive multivariable adjustment.

2. The classical targeted-therapy biomarker matches expected from the literature — sotorasib in KRAS G12C, osimertinib in EGFR, olaparib in BRCA2 — are NOT detectable in this dataset. Subgroup risk differences and full-cohort interaction terms are all non-significant; the olaparib/BRCA2 estimate even trends the wrong way. The data behave as if the genotype-treatment matching mechanism for these three drugs has been broken (or randomized).

3. Performance status is the dominant prognostic axis: ECOG 1 and 2 each carry a large, highly significant decrement, monotonic, and robust to adjustment. Together with stage IV, brain metastases, weight loss, low albumin, and elevated CRP, this defines a coherent "frailty / disease-burden" signal predicting non-response.

4. Other anatomic sites of disease (liver, bone, adrenal, effusions, contralateral lung) are not associated with response in either direction — striking, given that liver metastases are usually a strong negative predictor of immunotherapy response in real-world studies.

5. Demographic disparities are modest. Female patients respond slightly more often than males (RD +1.2 percentage points). Insurance type matters only at the medicaid-vs-private boundary (medicaid lower, p=0.04). Race/ethnicity differences run AGAINST the usual disparity narrative in this cohort — white patients have the lowest response rate, with a small significant advantage for Black patients vs white. Rural residence and education show no association.

6. Symptom burden, BMI, age, smoking history, and histology have at most weak, mostly non-significant associations after accounting for the dominant ECOG / disease-burden / weight-loss signal. Fatigue is borderline; weight loss is the only patient-reported / nutritional variable that emerges as a strong independent predictor.

7. The 23-SNP candidate pharmacogenomic panel shows no detectable signal — neither individually after Bonferroni correction nor as a joint likelihood-ratio test (p=0.68). These polymorphisms appear to be null with respect to response in this dataset.

Bottom line: in this 50,000-patient NSCLC cohort, the biology that emerges most clearly is the modern PD-1/PD-L1 / TMB / STK11 immunotherapy story, layered on a strong frailty/burden background (ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, weight loss, albumin, CRP). The historically expected single-gene → matched-targeted-therapy effects (KRAS G12C → sotorasib, EGFR → osimertinib, BRCA2 → olaparib) and the candidate-SNP panel yield essentially null results.
