Analysis summary: ds001_nsclc (50,000 NSCLC patients)
Outcome: objective_response (overall rate 16.9%)

Across 10 iterations I tested 40 hypotheses with 40 analyses, working from
specific drug-biomarker pairings outward to prognostic features, demographics,
SNPs, comorbidities, and finally to a multivariable model. The picture that
emerges is dominated by pembrolizumab + PD-L1/TMB/STK11 biology and by
classical prognostic factors; targeted-therapy x matched-biomarker effects and
genetic/socioeconomic features are essentially silent.

================================================================================
1. Drug x biomarker pairings (Iteration 1)
================================================================================
The expected matched-biomarker effect was confirmed only for the immunotherapy
pairing:

  - Pembrolizumab x TMB-high: response 19.7% on vs 16.7% off in TMB-high
    patients (chi-square p=5.4e-6); strong and clinically expected.

  - Osimertinib x EGFR mutation:   on 16.4% / off 16.6%, p=0.87  (null)
  - Sotorasib   x KRAS G12C:       on 17.5% / off 16.6%, p=0.43  (null)
  - Olaparib    x BRCA2 mutation:  on 14.6% / off 16.8%, p=0.35  (null, wrong sign)

These targeted-therapy effects remained null even after multivariable
adjustment in iteration 8 (osi*egfr p=0.74; soto*kras p=0.57; olap*brca p=0.18).
Within this dataset the targeted therapies do not increase response in
biomarker-matched patients.

================================================================================
2. The pembrolizumab program: PD-L1, TMB, STK11, sex, smoking (Iter 2-3, 10)
================================================================================
PD-L1 (continuous)
  Logistic interaction pembro x pdl1_tps coef = +0.591, p=1.6e-7.
  Stratified: in pdl1_tps >= 0.5 (n=10,749) pembrolizumab raised response from
  15.6% to 20.9% (p=7.7e-13). In pdl1_tps < 0.5 (n=39,251) the difference was
  16.4% vs 16.6% (p=0.59). Pembrolizumab benefit is essentially confined to the
  PD-L1-high stratum.

TMB-high
  Pembrolizumab x tmb_high interaction = +0.174, p=9e-4. Independent of PD-L1
  in the multivariable model.

STK11 mutation
  Pembrolizumab x stk11_mutation interaction = -0.211, p=0.0018. STK11 wild-type
  patients gain from pembrolizumab (17.7% vs 16.2%, p=1e-4); STK11-mutant
  patients do not (15.8% vs 17.3%, p=0.08, with a slight numerical reversal).
  In PD-L1 high & STK11-wt patients (n=9,149) pembrolizumab response was
  21.7% vs 15.4% (p=6e-15); in PD-L1 high & STK11-mut (n=1,600) it was
  16.3% vs 16.8% (p=0.86). STK11 mutation abolishes the PD-L1-driven benefit.

KEAP1 / TP53
  No significant interactions with pembrolizumab (KEAP1 p=0.36; TP53 p=0.56).

Smoking
  Smoking_status had no main effect on response overall (p=0.27). However, the
  pembrolizumab benefit was concentrated in current (+1.2 pp, p=0.05) and
  former smokers (+1.1 pp, p=0.02) and absent in never-smokers (+0.1 pp,
  p=0.91). Even within the PD-L1 high stratum, pembrolizumab benefit was
  large in current (24.1% vs 14.0%, p=8e-13) and former (20.5% vs 17.0%,
  p=5e-4) smokers, and not significant in never-smokers (15.9% vs 13.2%,
  p=0.14).

Sex
  Females had a small overall response advantage (17.5% vs 16.4%, p=6e-4).
  Pembrolizumab x sex_female interaction = +0.146, p=0.002: pembrolizumab
  raised response in females (+2.1 pp, p=2.6e-5) but not in males (0.0 pp,
  p=0.94). This may reflect collinearity with smoking status or be an
  independent feature; further conditioning would be needed to disentangle.

Three-way pembrolizumab x PD-L1 x STK11 interaction
  Coefficient -0.388, p=0.22 (not significant). The stratified pattern is
  directionally consistent with greatest benefit in PD-L1-high/STK11-wt, but
  formal three-way interaction is not statistically supported in this sample.

================================================================================
3. Prognostic features (Iter 4-6)
================================================================================
Strongly predictive of response in expected directions:
  - ECOG performance status:  21.1% / 15.7% / 11.1% for ECOG 0/1/2;
    coef = -0.375 per unit, p=1e-93 (the strongest single predictor).
  - Weight loss (% over 6 mo): coef = -0.039 per %, p=2e-32.
  - Stage IV vs not:           15.4% vs 19.6%, p=1e-33.
  - Brain mets:                14.4% vs 17.7%, p=2e-18.
  - Albumin:                   coef +0.091 per g/dL, p=1.4e-4.
  - CRP:                       coef -0.005 per mg/L, p=7e-4.
  - Number of metastatic sites (brain+liver+bone+adrenal+contralateral):
    coef -0.057 per site, p=1e-4.

Null prognostic features (contrary to common clinical priors):
  - LDH (p=0.50), NLR (p=0.38), hemoglobin, AlkPhos, AST, ALT, total
    bilirubin, creatinine, electrolytes, blood counts: all p>0.05.
  - Liver/bone/adrenal/contralateral mets and pleural/pericardial
    effusions: each individually null. Among anatomic features, only stage IV
    and brain mets carry independent signal.
  - Symptom grades: only fatigue_grade weakly negative (p=0.04); pain_nrs,
    dyspnea_grade, cough_grade, appetite_loss_grade all null.
  - ECOG x pembrolizumab interaction not significant (p=0.32) - pembrolizumab
    benefit does not vary with ECOG within this dataset.

================================================================================
4. Demographics, SDOH, SNPs, comorbidities (Iter 7, 9)
================================================================================
Almost everything is null:
  - Race/ethnicity: chi-square p=0.045 (borderline; small reduction in white
    vs other groups).
  - Insurance type, rural residence, age, education years, BMI, pack-years:
    all p >= 0.23.
  - 23 SNPs (snp_rs*): smallest p-values 0.018 (rs7412) and 0.031 (rs1050828);
    0/23 below Bonferroni threshold (0.00217). Consistent with chance.
  - Prior chemotherapy/radiation/surgery/immunotherapy/targeted therapy and
    prior_lines_of_therapy: all null (smallest p=0.07).
  - Comorbidities (DM, HTN, COPD, CKD, HF, autoimmune, ILD, prior malignancy,
    depression/anxiety): all null.

================================================================================
5. Final multivariable model (Iter 8, 10)
================================================================================
Logit(objective_response ~ pembro + pdl1_tps + pembro:pdl1_tps + tmb_high +
                            pembro:tmb_high + stk11_mut + pembro:stk11_mut +
                            ecog + weight_loss + albumin + crp + stage_iv +
                            brain_mets + sex_female)

Pseudo R^2 = 0.021; LLR p = 3e-190.

Coefficients (95% CI omitted; all p shown):
  Pembrolizumab x PD-L1 TPS         +0.595  (p=1.6e-7)  - amplifies pembro benefit
  Pembrolizumab x TMB high          +0.174  (p=9.7e-4)  - amplifies pembro benefit
  Pembrolizumab x STK11 mutation    -0.203  (p=0.003)   - resistance
  ECOG performance status           -0.382  (p=1e-95)
  Stage IV                          -0.302  (p=1.6e-34)
  Brain mets                        -0.257  (p=9e-19)
  Weight loss % (6 mo)              -0.039  (p=2e-32)
  Albumin (g/dL)                    +0.092  (p=1.2e-4)
  CRP (mg/L)                        -0.005  (p=4.6e-4)
  Sex female                        +0.081  (p=8.4e-4)
  Pembrolizumab (off interactions)  -0.131  (p=0.005)   - main effect alone is weakly negative
  PD-L1 TPS, TMB high, STK11 mut    n.s. as main effects after interactions

Targeted therapy x matched biomarker interactions (osi*egfr, soto*kras,
olap*brca) all remained non-significant and small after adjustment.

================================================================================
6. Hypotheses supported, refuted, and surprises
================================================================================
Supported:
  - Pembrolizumab benefit increases with PD-L1 TPS (large, robust signal).
  - Pembrolizumab benefit is greater in TMB-high tumors.
  - STK11 mutation abolishes pembrolizumab benefit, including within PD-L1-high.
  - Pembrolizumab benefit is concentrated in current/former smokers.
  - Pembrolizumab benefit appears greater in females.
  - ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, weight loss, albumin, CRP, and metastatic site
    count are all independently prognostic in expected directions.

Refuted (in this cohort):
  - Osimertinib in EGFR-mutant patients - no response benefit.
  - Sotorasib in KRAS G12C patients - no response benefit.
  - Olaparib in BRCA2-mutant patients - no response benefit (numerically worse).
  - LDH and NLR as prognostic - no detectable association.
  - Most patient-reported symptom grades, comorbidities, prior treatments,
    SNPs, and SDOH variables - no association with response.

Surprises:
  - Three of four targeted therapies are genuinely null within their matched
    biomarker subgroups in this dataset, despite being the canonical use-case
    for those drugs in real-world NSCLC. This is the most clinically
    unexpected finding.
  - LDH and NLR are absent as prognostic markers, again contrary to most
    real-world cohorts.
  - The pembrolizumab "story" is fully consistent with established biology:
    PD-L1 amplifies, TMB amplifies, STK11 abrogates, never-smokers do not
    benefit, and prognostic factors layer in additively.

================================================================================
Bottom line
================================================================================
The dominant signal is the pembrolizumab response program, driven by PD-L1
and TMB and gated by STK11/never-smoker status. Standard prognostic markers
(ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, weight loss, albumin, CRP, sex) explain the rest.
Targeted-therapy biomarker matching is, surprisingly, not associated with
response in this cohort, and a broad screen across SNPs, SDOH, comorbidities,
and prior treatment history yields no further signal.
