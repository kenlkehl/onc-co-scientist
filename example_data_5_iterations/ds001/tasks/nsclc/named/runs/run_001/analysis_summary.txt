ds001_nsclc — Analysis Summary
================================

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patients (Stage IV in 64.7%; ECOG 0/1/2 = 35/50/15%; adenocarcinoma 72%;
white 65%, hispanic 15%, black 12%, asian 6%; 50% pembrolizumab, 35% sotorasib, 30% olaparib,
30% osimertinib). Outcome `objective_response` rate is 16.9% overall.

Approach
--------
Across 10 iterations I worked from broad main effects to refined biomarker-defined subgroups,
testing 49 hypotheses. Continuous variables were screened with univariable logistic regression;
binary variables with rate-difference + Fisher exact tests; biomarker-treatment hypotheses with
both stratified analyses and treatment×biomarker interaction terms in logistic regression; and
all surviving signals were re-checked in a single multivariable logistic model adjusting for
ECOG, stage, brain mets, biomarker status, albumin, CRP, and weight loss.

Headline findings
-----------------
1. PEMBROLIZUMAB IS THE ONLY TREATMENT WITH A DETECTABLE BENEFIT, AND IT IS HIGHLY
   BIOMARKER-DEPENDENT.
   - Crude main effect is small: 17.4% on vs 16.4% off (Δ +0.99 pp, p=0.003).
   - Continuous PD-L1 × pembrolizumab interaction is strongly positive
     (log-OR +0.59, p≈2×10⁻⁷). PD-L1 is predictive, not prognostic: within
     untreated patients, PD-L1 has no effect on response (p=0.09, opposite sign).
   - TMB-high also potentiates pembrolizumab (interaction log-OR +0.17, p=9.5×10⁻⁴),
     and TMB shows no main effect on response without pembrolizumab.
   - PD-L1 and TMB act multiplicatively: in PD-L1-high & TMB-high patients
     pembrolizumab raises response from 15.5% to 29.4% (Δ +13.9 pp, p=8×10⁻²¹).
   - STK11 mutation abrogates pembrolizumab benefit (interaction log-OR −0.20,
     p=0.003). In PD-L1-high/STK11-WT patients pembrolizumab adds +6.4 pp
     (p=4×10⁻¹⁵); in PD-L1-high/STK11-MUT patients it adds essentially 0.
   - The "best ICI candidate" subgroup PD-L1-high & TMB-high & STK11-WT (n=2,714)
     shows pembrolizumab response 31.2% vs 15.3% (Δ +15.96 pp, p=5×10⁻²³) — the
     largest treatment effect identified in the dataset.

2. THE TARGETED THERAPIES SHOW NO MEASURABLE BENEFIT IN THIS COHORT, INCLUDING
   WITHIN THEIR PRESUMED BIOMARKER-MATCHED SUBGROUPS.
   - Sotorasib in KRAS-G12C+ (n=6,368): adjusted log-OR +0.057, p=0.42.
   - Osimertinib in EGFR-mutant (n=6,517): adjusted log-OR −0.013, p=0.85.
   - Olaparib in BRCA2-mutant (n=1,519): adjusted log-OR −0.159, p=0.33
     (point estimate negative; small subgroup limits power).
   - Adjusted main effects across the whole cohort are also null (sotorasib
     p=0.83, olaparib p=0.64, osimertinib p=0.18). This is a notable departure
     from the published efficacy of these agents in their respective marker-defined
     populations and likely reflects how this synthetic dataset was constructed
     rather than a clinical truth.

3. ROBUST PROGNOSTIC FACTORS FOR OBJECTIVE RESPONSE
   (multivariable adjusted, all p<0.001 unless noted):
   - ECOG performance status: each unit lower → OR 0.69 for response (rates 21%/16%/11%
     for ECOG 0/1/2; univariable p=2×10⁻⁹³).
   - Stage IV vs not: −4.25 pp (adj log-OR −0.30).
   - Brain metastases: −3.39 pp (adj log-OR −0.26).
   - Weight loss in past 6 months: per 1%-pt loss, adj log-OR −0.04 (univ p=2×10⁻³²).
   - Serum albumin: per 1 g/dL, adj log-OR +0.09.
   - CRP: per mg/L, adj log-OR −0.005, p<0.001.
   - Other site-of-disease variables (liver, bone, adrenal, pleural/pericardial
     effusions, contralateral lung mets) and most labs (LDH, NLR, hemoglobin,
     calcium, platelets, WBC) were NOT associated with response.

4. SEX EFFECT
   - Female patients respond more often: 17.5% vs 16.4% in males (Δ +1.16 pp,
     p=5.7×10⁻⁴), and this persists after multivariable adjustment
     (adj log-OR +0.081, p=0.001). Modest but reproducible.

5. NO MEANINGFUL DEMOGRAPHIC DISPARITIES IN TREATMENT ALLOCATION
   - Pembrolizumab, sotorasib, and osimertinib receipt is essentially equal
     across race/ethnicity and insurance categories.
   - Olaparib receipt by race is borderline (chi² p=0.012, range 28.7%–32.2%) but
     the absolute spread is small.
   - Race × response chi² p=0.045 with white slightly lower than other groups —
     borderline and not corrected for multiple comparisons.
   - Insurance, rural residence, age, education years: no detectable effect on
     response.

6. NULL DOMAINS
   - SNPs (23 tested): top hits snp_rs7412 (p=0.020) and snp_rs1050828 (p=0.032)
     do not survive Bonferroni at α=0.0022. No reliable pharmacogenomic signal.
   - Comorbidities (15 tested): only coronary_artery_disease borderline (p=0.049,
     uncorrected); diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, HF, AF, VTE, autoimmune,
     hep B/C, HIV, prior malignancy, depression/anxiety, ILD all NS.
   - Symptom grades: only fatigue borderline (p=0.040). Pain, dyspnea, cough,
     appetite loss NS.
   - Smoking status, pack-years, histology: no main effect on response and no
     significant pembrolizumab interaction.
   - Prior therapy types and prior_lines_of_therapy: no detectable association.

Overall conclusions about treatment–biomarker–outcome relationships
-------------------------------------------------------------------
This cohort recapitulates a clean immunotherapy-biomarker pattern: pembrolizumab
benefit is concentrated in PD-L1-high, TMB-high, STK11-wild-type tumors, with the
strongest effect (≈+16 percentage points absolute response) in the joint
"triple-positive" subgroup. PD-L1 and TMB act predictively (no prognostic effect
without pembrolizumab) and combine multiplicatively. STK11 mutation eliminates
the benefit. The other three treatments (sotorasib, olaparib, osimertinib) show
no detectable effect on objective response, even after multivariable adjustment
and within their nominal biomarker-matched subgroups — a striking departure from
their established clinical efficacy that should be flagged as a property of this
dataset. Prognostic signals are conventional and clinically sensible (ECOG, stage,
brain mets, weight loss, CRP, albumin), with a small additional female-sex
advantage. Comorbidities, symptoms, prior therapy, SNPs, smoking, and most
demographic variables show no reliable signal.
