Analysis summary - ds001_crc (n=50,000) - 10 iterations
========================================================

Outcome variable: pfs_months (mean 4.31, SD 2.29, range 0-16.3).

OVERVIEW
--------
Across 10 iterations I probed main effects of biomarkers, treatments, labs,
demographics, comorbidities, metastatic sites, symptom grades, prior therapy,
and pharmacogenomic SNPs; canonical biomarker x targeted-therapy interactions
(cetuximab/RAS, pembrolizumab/MSI, encorafenib/BRAF V600E, trastuzumab+tucatinib/
HER2-amp); regorafenib subgroup heterogeneity; and a multivariable OLS model
(R^2 = 0.86) that simultaneously adjusts for the major prognostic axes.


SUPPORTED HYPOTHESES (significant, direction confirmed)
-------------------------------------------------------
Disease burden / functional status:
- stage_iv = 1 -> shorter PFS by ~1.35 months (p ~ 0).
- ecog_ps -> -1.19 months PFS per one-point increase (p ~ 0); rho = -0.36.
- right_sided_primary = 1 -> -0.30 months (p = 1e-49); also negative in MV model.

Tumor biology:
- kras_mutation -> -0.33 mo univariate (-0.35 MV; p ~ 0).
- braf_v600e   -> -0.23 mo univariate (-0.36 MV; p = 4e-79).
- nras_mutation -> +0.22 mo univariate (p = 7e-4); not significant in MV.

Labs / nutrition:
- albumin_g_dl -> +0.10 Pearson r univariate (+0.47 mo per g/dL in MV; p ~ 0).
- weight_loss_pct_6mo -> -0.12 Pearson r (-0.076 mo per % in MV; p ~ 0).
- cea_ng_ml -> small but highly significant negative association
  (univariate r = -0.038, p = 3e-17; MV coef = -0.005 per ng/mL).
- ldh_u_l -> univariate p = 0.013; MV coef = -4e-4 per U/L (p = 7e-19).

Demographics (one strong, the rest null):
- age_years has a remarkably strong POSITIVE association with PFS
  (Pearson r = +0.766, p ~ 0). After adjustment for ECOG and stage,
  +0.176 months per year of age (p ~ 0). Older patients have longer
  PFS in this synthetic cohort - the opposite of what is typically
  observed in real-world oncology data, where older age tends to
  predict worse outcomes after adjustment.

Treatments - the regorafenib story:
- treatment_regorafenib has a +0.97 month main effect (p = 2e-216), robust
  to multivariable adjustment (+0.94 mo, p ~ 0).
- The benefit is highly heterogeneous:
    * KRAS x regorafenib interaction: -1.63 (p = 8e-226). Benefit is
      restricted to KRAS-wildtype (+1.66 mo); KRAS-mutant patients show
      no benefit (+0.03 mo, p = 0.46).
    * BRAF x regorafenib interaction: -1.18 (p = 6e-23). Benefit absent
      (numerically reversed) in BRAF V600E patients (-0.16, p = 0.13).
    * Sidedness x regorafenib interaction: -1.45 (p = 4e-168). Benefit
      restricted to left-sided primaries (+1.48 mo); right-sided +0.03.
    * Joint KRAS x sidedness analysis: in left-sided KRAS-wildtype
      patients regorafenib gives +2.52 months PFS (4.119 -> 6.639);
      the other three KRAS x sidedness cells show no benefit.
    * MSI-status and ECOG-stratum interactions are negligible: regorafenib
      benefit is essentially constant across MSI-H/MSS and ECOG 0/1/2.


REFUTED HYPOTHESES (no detectable effect)
-----------------------------------------
Canonical biomarker x targeted-therapy pairings (each tested both stratified
and via interaction term in OLS):
- treatment_cetuximab in RAS-wildtype: no benefit (-0.05 mo, p = 0.13);
  even in the textbook indication subgroup (RAS-WT + left-sided), still no
  effect (-0.06 mo, p = 0.13). RAS x cetuximab interaction p = 0.71.
- treatment_pembrolizumab in MSI-high: no benefit (+0.007 mo, p = 0.96);
  pembrolizumab x MSI interaction p = 0.99.
- treatment_encorafenib in BRAF V600E: no benefit (-0.13 mo, p = 0.33);
  encorafenib x BRAF interaction p = 0.38. Even the encorafenib + cetuximab
  combination within BRAF V600E shows no effect (n = 58; +0.06, p = 0.84).
- treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib in HER2-amplified: no benefit
  (+0.02 mo, p = 0.93); interaction p = 0.78.
- treatment_bevacizumab: no main effect (p = 0.35).

Demographics / SES (all null):
- sex_female (p = 0.77), rural_residence (p = 0.34),
  insurance_type ANOVA (p = 0.22), race_ethnicity ANOVA (p = 0.48).

Comorbidities (all null at alpha 0.05): diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD,
heart failure, CAD, atrial fibrillation, VTE history, autoimmune disease,
prior malignancy, depression/anxiety. Smallest p was depression/anxiety
(p = 0.054), not significant.

Metastatic sites (all null): liver_mets (p = 0.52), bone_mets (p = 0.23),
adrenal_mets (p = 0.089), pleural_effusion (p = 0.069),
pericardial_effusion (p = 0.12), contralateral_lung_mets (p = 0.14).
Notably, the absence of a liver_mets effect is surprising for CRC.

Symptom grades (all null Spearman p > 0.15): fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough,
appetite-loss grades.

Prior therapy: prior_lines_of_therapy (p = 0.15) and individual prior_chemo,
prior_radiation, prior_surgery, prior_immunotherapy, prior_targeted_therapy
all null.

SNPs: scanned all 27 columns; smallest p-values 0.027 (snp_rs1801131,
rho = -0.010) and 0.029 (snp_rs1050828, rho = +0.010). Bonferroni alpha
1.85e-3 not crossed by any SNP. No reliable pharmacogenomic signal.

MSI-high and HER2-amplified main effects: null (p = 0.68 and 0.36).


MULTIVARIABLE MODEL
-------------------
A single OLS model (R^2 = 0.859) including 24 covariates yields these
significant predictors (p < 0.01) of pfs_months:

  age_years              +0.1758 per year
  ecog_ps                -1.1711 per point
  stage_iv               -1.3614
  right_sided_primary    -0.2867
  kras_mutation          -0.3543
  braf_v600e             -0.3565
  cea_ng_ml              -0.0050 per ng/mL
  albumin_g_dl           +0.4692 per g/dL
  ldh_u_l                -0.0004 per U/L
  weight_loss_pct_6mo    -0.0761 per %
  treatment_regorafenib  +0.9429

Non-significant in the MV model: sex_female, nras_mutation, msi_high,
her2_amplified, crp_mg_l, nlr, cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab,
encorafenib, trastuzumab+tucatinib, liver_mets, bone_mets.


OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. Disease burden and host status drive PFS the way one would expect:
   stage IV, higher ECOG, right-sided primary, KRAS or BRAF V600E mutation,
   higher CEA / LDH, lower albumin, and greater weight loss all shorten PFS.
2. Of the six available systemic therapies, only treatment_regorafenib has
   a measurable PFS effect, and that effect is highly compartmentalized:
   it accrues almost entirely to left-sided KRAS-wildtype (and BRAF-wildtype)
   patients - the very subgroup that one might naively expect to do well on
   anti-EGFR therapy in real-world CRC. The other five treatments
   (cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib,
   trastuzumab+tucatinib) show no main effect and, importantly, NO benefit
   in their canonical biomarker subgroups (RAS-WT, MSI-H, BRAF V600E,
   HER2-amp). This is the most striking pattern in the dataset: the
   biology-treatment matchings that drive practice in real CRC have been
   essentially zeroed out here, while regorafenib has been given outsized
   subgroup-restricted benefit.
3. The age effect is unusual. A +0.18 month/year coefficient surviving
   adjustment for ECOG and stage is the opposite of the typical real-world
   pattern.
4. Demographics, comorbidities, metastatic-site flags, symptom grades, and
   pharmacogenomic SNPs are uniformly null - they look like noise covariates
   in this cohort, not signal-bearing features.
