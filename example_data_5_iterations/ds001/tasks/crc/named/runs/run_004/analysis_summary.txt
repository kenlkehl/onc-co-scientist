Analysis summary for ds001_crc (50,000 patients; outcome pfs_months)
====================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
We tested 43 hypotheses across 10 iterations covering main treatment
effects, prognostic factors, canonical biomarker-treatment predictive
interactions, multivariable models, three-way interactions, and
pharmacogenomics. The dataset behaves nothing like a typical real-world
metastatic CRC cohort: most canonical drug-biomarker pairings are flat,
yet a single drug (regorafenib) shows a very large, biomarker- and
sidedness-dependent benefit, and age has a strong, monotonic, positive
relationship with PFS. The final multivariable model that includes
regorafenib x KRAS, x BRAF V600E, and x right-sided primary interactions
explains R^2 = 0.90 of the variance in pfs_months.

ITERATION 1 - Treatment main effects
------------------------------------
Hypothesised that each of the six treatments improves PFS. Five were
refuted:
  - Cetuximab: -0.04 mo, p=0.09 (NS)
  - Bevacizumab: -0.02 mo, p=0.35 (NS)
  - Pembrolizumab: +0.005 mo, p=0.86 (NS)
  - Encorafenib: +0.005 mo, p=0.87 (NS)
  - Trastuzumab+tucatinib: -0.04 mo, p=0.25 (NS)
Only treatment_regorafenib was supported: +0.97 mo, p<1e-300.

ITERATION 2 - Major prognostic factors
--------------------------------------
ECOG, stage IV, and right-sided primary all behave as expected:
  - ECOG: -1.19 mo per unit (PFS 5.27 / 4.05 / 2.91 for ECOG 0/1/2)
  - stage_iv: -1.35 mo (3.70 vs 5.05)
  - right_sided_primary: -0.31 mo (4.11 vs 4.42)
Age was hypothesised to be negative; instead the slope is +0.18 mo/year
(p<1e-300). Decile means rise monotonically from ~0.26 mo (<36 yrs) to
~8.4 mo (84-90 yrs). Hypothesis refuted; sign reversed.

ITERATION 3 - Lab biomarkers and refined age check
--------------------------------------------------
  - albumin_g_dl: +0.47 mo per g/dL (supported)
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.07 mo per percent (supported)
  - cea_ng_ml: -0.0054 mo per ng/mL (supported)
  - ldh_u_l: -0.00032 (marginal unadjusted, robust after adjustment)
  - crp_mg_l: -0.0020 (trend only, p=0.07)
  - nlr: +0.005, p=0.29 (refuted)
The reversed age slope was reconfirmed after multivariable adjustment
for ECOG, stage, albumin, CEA, and weight loss (coef +0.176, p<1e-300).

ITERATION 4 - Canonical predictive biomarker x treatment interactions
---------------------------------------------------------------------
All canonical biomarker-driven targeted therapies showed NO predictive
effect:
  - Cetuximab x KRAS WT: interaction +0.020, p=0.67 (refuted)
  - Cetuximab x NRAS: interaction -0.007, p=0.96 (refuted)
  - Cetuximab x BRAF V600E: -0.026, p=0.81 (refuted)
  - Cetuximab x sidedness: +0.026, p=0.58 (refuted)
  - Pembrolizumab x MSI-H: +0.002, p=0.99 (strongly refuted: pembro is
    completely flat in MSI-H patients in this dataset)
  - Encorafenib x BRAF V600E: -0.141, p=0.38 (refuted)
  - Trastuzumab+tucatinib x HER2 amplified: +0.063, p=0.78 (refuted)
This is striking: the canonical CRC predictive biomarkers behave as if
the dataset's drug-biomarker biology has been deliberately scrambled.

ITERATION 5 - Multivariable confirmation
----------------------------------------
A 22-feature OLS model confirmed:
  - kras_mutation -0.354 (p<1e-300) and braf_v600e -0.357 (p=1e-79)
    appear independently negative-prognostic;
  - regorafenib remains the only treatment with a significant positive
    coefficient (+0.94, p<1e-300); cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab,
    encorafenib all null; trastuzumab+tucatinib trend (+0.026, p=0.07).

ITERATION 6 - Refined: regorafenib has the predictive interactions
------------------------------------------------------------------
This was the pivotal iteration. Stratifying regorafenib by canonical
biomarkers:
  - Regorafenib x KRAS interaction = -1.629, p<1e-200.
      KRAS WT: regorafenib +1.66 mo (p<1e-300)
      KRAS mut: regorafenib +0.03 mo (NS)
  - Regorafenib x BRAF V600E interaction = -1.183, p=6e-23.
      BRAF WT: regorafenib +1.03 mo
      BRAF V600E: regorafenib -0.16 mo (NS)
  - Regorafenib x NRAS interaction = +0.99 in a univariable model
    (p=1e-11), but shrinks to +0.04 (p=0.46) once KRAS and BRAF
    interactions are controlled - the apparent NRAS effect is
    explained by the mutual-exclusivity of KRAS/NRAS/BRAF mutations.
The dataset assigns the role of "predictive biomarkers" not to the
canonical anti-EGFR/BRAF-targeted/anti-PD1 drugs, but to regorafenib.

ITERATION 7 - Combined RAS/RAF wild-type subgroup
-------------------------------------------------
Defining rasraf_wt = (KRAS WT) AND (NRAS WT) AND (BRAF WT) (50.6% of
the cohort):
  - Within rasraf_wt: regorafenib gives +1.81 mo (p<1e-300)
  - Outside rasraf_wt: regorafenib gives only +0.13 mo
  - Interaction +1.68, p<1e-240
Within rasraf_wt all five other treatments remain null
(cetuximab -0.05 p=0.17, bevacizumab -0.003 p=0.93, pembrolizumab
-0.008 p=0.84, encorafenib +0.034 p=0.50, tras+tuc -0.067 p=0.24).

ITERATION 8 - Sidedness and three-way interaction
-------------------------------------------------
Regorafenib x right-sided primary interaction = -1.45 (p<1e-160):
  - Left-sided: regorafenib +1.48 mo
  - Right-sided: regorafenib +0.03 mo (NS)
Three-way regorafenib x KRAS x right-sided interaction = +2.35
(p<1e-100); subgroup means show regorafenib benefit is concentrated
exclusively in left-sided KRAS WT patients (+2.52 mo).

In the doubly-selected subgroup left-sided AND RAS/RAF WT
(n=16,443; 3,264 regorafenib-treated), the adjusted regorafenib effect
is +2.73 mo (p<1e-300).

ITERATION 9 - The "prognostic" biomarkers are actually predictive
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Adding regorafenib x KRAS, x BRAF, and x right-sided interactions to
the multivariable model collapses the apparent prognostic effects of
those three variables:
  - kras_mutation main effect: -0.001 (p=0.92)
  - braf_v600e main effect: +0.003 (p=0.85)
  - right_sided_primary main effect: +0.003 (p=0.67)
Meanwhile the regorafenib main effect grows to +2.29 mo (p<1e-300).
Conclusion: KRAS, BRAF V600E, and right-sided primary are NOT
independently prognostic in this cohort; their univariable apparent
prognostic effects are entirely driven by regorafenib's lack of
efficacy in those subgroups. R^2 = 0.898.

We tested several rescue/synergy hypotheses and refuted each:
  - Pembrolizumab x MSI-H x stage_iv (and x ECOG, x TP53): all NS;
  - Cetuximab in left-sided RAS/RAF WT (the canonical responder
    subgroup): diff -0.07, p=0.13 (still null);
  - Encorafenib + cetuximab combination in BRAF V600E: interaction
    +0.32, p=0.32 (no synergy).

ITERATION 10 - Pharmacogenomics, comorbidities, demographics, residuals
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
  - SNP x regorafenib screen (26 SNPs): smallest interaction p=0.008
    (rs1799983, coef +0.14). None survive a Bonferroni-corrected
    threshold of 0.05/26 = 0.0019. Likely multiple-testing noise.
  - 15 comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart failure,
    AFib, VTE history, autoimmune, hepatitis B/C, HIV, prior
    malignancy, depression/anxiety, ILD): all p>0.05. Refuted.
  - Sociodemographic (race_ethnicity, insurance_type, rural_residence,
    education_years, smoking_pack_years): all NS (race F-test p=0.48,
    insurance F-test p=0.22). Refuted.
  - Other molecular features (tp53, pik3ca, pten_loss, cdkn2a_loss,
    her2_amplification, met_exon14_skipping, ret_fusion, ros1_fusion,
    nrg1_fusion, fgfr_alteration, ntrk_fusion, keap1): all p>0.15.
    Refuted.
  - Age robustness: the +0.18 mo/year age slope is unchanged across
    every multivariable specification (R^2 0.898 model still gives
    coef +0.176, p<1e-300). It is not an artefact of treatment
    selection or prognostic confounding.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. The dominant predictive signal in this cohort is regorafenib x
   (KRAS WT AND BRAF V600E WT AND left-sided primary). In that
   triple-selected subgroup regorafenib confers +2.5 to +2.7 mo of
   PFS; outside it the drug is essentially inert.
2. None of the canonical CRC predictive biomarkers behave as in the
   real-world literature: cetuximab does not benefit KRAS WT or
   left-sided RAS/RAF WT; pembrolizumab does not benefit MSI-H;
   encorafenib does not benefit BRAF V600E; trastuzumab+tucatinib
   does not benefit HER2 amplified.
3. The apparent prognostic signal from KRAS, BRAF V600E, and
   right-sided primary is fully accounted for by regorafenib's
   biomarker-restricted efficacy; once that is modelled, the three
   factors lose independent prognostic value.
4. Genuine prognostic factors are: ECOG (-1.19 mo/grade), stage IV
   (-1.36 mo), albumin (+0.47 mo per g/dL), weight loss (-0.07 mo
   per percent), CEA (-0.005 mo per ng/mL), and LDH (-0.0005 mo
   per U/L). CRP and NLR show only weak/null trends.
5. Age has a strong, monotonic POSITIVE relationship with PFS
   (+0.176 mo/year). This direction is opposite to typical clinical
   expectation and is robust to all adjustments and subgroup checks.
6. Comorbidities, sociodemographics, ancillary mutations, fusions,
   and SNPs show no detectable effects after multiple-testing
   correction.
