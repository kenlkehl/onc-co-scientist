Oncology Dataset Analysis - ds001_crc (n=50,000)
=================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The dataset contains 50,000 colorectal-cancer patient records with 124 features
plus the outcome `pfs_months` (progression-free survival in months,
mean 4.31, range 0-16.3). I ran 10 iterations of hypothesis-driven analyses
covering treatments, biomarker x treatment interactions, lab markers, demographics,
comorbidities, SNPs, and multivariable models.

DOMINANT PROGNOSTIC FACTORS (CONFIRMED)
---------------------------------------
A small set of features explains the bulk of PFS variation
(comprehensive multivariable OLS R^2 = 0.892):

1. age_years: Strong POSITIVE association with PFS
   - Spearman rho = +0.785 (p < 1e-300); adjusted coef = +0.176 mo per year.
   - Mean PFS by decade: <50 = 1.08 mo; 50-60 = 2.65; 60-70 = 4.29;
     70-80 = 5.95; 80+ = 7.78.
   - This direction is opposite to typical clinical expectation and is the
     largest single predictor in the cohort.
2. ecog_ps: Strong negative effect (adjusted coef = -1.18 per unit; p<1e-300).
   ECOG 0/1/2 mean PFS = 5.27/4.05/2.91 mo.
3. stage_iv: Negative (adjusted coef = -1.35 mo; p<1e-300).
4. albumin_g_dl: Positive (adjusted coef = +0.47 per g/dL; p<1e-300).
5. weight_loss_pct_6mo: Negative (adjusted coef = -0.077 per percentage
   point; p<1e-300).
6. cea_ng_ml: Negative (adjusted coef = -0.005 per ng/mL; p<1e-100).
7. right_sided_primary: Modest negative effect unadjusted
   (-0.30 mo, p=5e-46); attenuates to ~ -0.29 in baseline-adjusted models.

TREATMENT EFFECTS
-----------------
Treatment assignment is essentially RANDOM with respect to biomarker
status (e.g., pembrolizumab use was 15.3% in MSS vs 15.8% in MSI-high;
encorafenib 10.2% in BRAF-wt vs 10.7% in BRAF V600E; cetuximab 30.3% in
KRAS-wt vs 29.9% in KRAS-mutant; trastuzumab+tucatinib 8.1% in HER2-wt
vs 8.0% in HER2-amplified). This permits unconfounded between-group
comparisons.

Findings:

- treatment_regorafenib: STRONG PFS benefit overall (+0.97 mo unadjusted;
  +0.94 adjusted, p<1e-300). The benefit is confined to a specific
  subgroup. Stratified analyses:
    * KRAS-wt + left-sided: +2.52 mo (p<1e-300, n=3,763 treated)
    * KRAS-wt + right-sided: +0.07 mo (p=0.16)
    * KRAS-mut + left-sided: +0.06 mo (p=0.17)
    * KRAS-mut + right-sided: -0.04 mo (p=0.52)
  The two-way interactions regorafenib x right_sided_primary and
  regorafenib x kras_mutation each negate the treatment effect
  (interaction coef approx -1.5 and -1.6, both p<1e-300). The three-way
  interaction is +2.49 (p<1e-300), consistent with benefit only in the
  cell where both adverse markers are absent. Regorafenib effect was
  also numerically present in BRAF-wt (+1.03) but not in BRAF V600E
  (-0.16), and was unchanged across MSI-high/HER2 status.

- treatment_cetuximab: NO main effect (-0.038 mo, p=0.09). NO benefit in
  KRAS-wt (-0.048, p=0.12), in RAS/BRAF wild-type (-0.045, p=0.17), or
  in left-sided primaries (-0.049, p=0.09). The classical anti-EGFR
  biomarker- and sidedness-dependent benefit is NOT present here.

- treatment_pembrolizumab: NO main effect (+0.005, p=0.86). NO benefit in
  MSI-high (+0.007, p=0.96). The expected dMMR benefit is NOT present.

- treatment_encorafenib: NO main effect (+0.005, p=0.87). In BRAF V600E
  (n=242 treated), numerically negative -0.13 mo (p=0.36). Expected
  BRAF-targeted benefit NOT present.

- treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib: NO main effect (-0.044, p=0.25). NO
  benefit in HER2-amplified (+0.017, p=0.94). Expected HER2-targeted
  benefit NOT present.

- treatment_bevacizumab: NO main effect (-0.019, p=0.35). No subgroup
  benefit detected (sidedness, KRAS, liver mets, MSI all p>0.26). A
  marginal negative association in BRAF V600E patients (-0.24 mo,
  p=0.0067) is plausibly chance with multiple comparisons.

NEGATIVE / NULL FINDINGS
------------------------
- Sex (sex_female): no main effect (diff -0.006, p=0.77; adjusted +0.008,
  p=0.41).
- Race/ethnicity: ANOVA p=0.48; no group differs after adjustment for
  age/ECOG/stage (all coefs |<0.02|, p>0.34).
- Insurance type: ANOVA p=0.22.
- Rural residence: p=0.34.
- Smoking pack-years: rho=+0.003, p=0.53.
- Education years: rho=+0.006, p=0.22.
- Other labs (LDH, CRP, NLR, hemoglobin): all |rho|<0.015, no clinically
  meaningful effect.
- Comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart failure, CAD,
  AF, VTE history, autoimmune, prior malignancy, depression/anxiety):
  none significantly associated with PFS at alpha=0.05.
- Site-of-mets indicators (liver, bone, adrenal, pleural effusion):
  no independent association after age/ECOG/stage adjustment.
- BMI / vital signs (SBP, HR, SpO2): no association.
- Symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite loss): all
  p>0.15.
- Prior lines of therapy and years since diagnosis: no association.
- 27 SNP variables: lowest p across all SNPs is p=0.027 (snp_rs1801131),
  which does not survive Bonferroni correction. No SNP shows a
  meaningful main-effect association with PFS.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. PFS in this colorectal-cancer cohort is dominated by a small set of
   patient-level prognostic factors: age (strongly POSITIVE, the largest
   predictor), ECOG performance status, stage IV, albumin, weight loss,
   CEA, and (modestly) right-sided primary.
2. Treatment assignment is unrelated to the biomarkers that would
   normally guide therapy in CRC, allowing unconfounded comparisons.
3. None of the biomarker-targeted therapies (cetuximab, pembrolizumab,
   encorafenib, trastuzumab+tucatinib) show the PFS benefits in their
   biomarker-defined target subgroups that would be expected from the
   real-world clinical evidence base.
4. Bevacizumab shows no PFS effect.
5. Regorafenib stands out as the only treatment with a robust PFS
   benefit, but the benefit is restricted to patients who are
   simultaneously KRAS wild-type AND left-sided primary - a roughly +2.5
   month gain in that cell, with essentially no benefit in the other
   three KRAS x sidedness cells. The interactions are very strong and
   highly significant. There is also a numerical signal that the
   benefit is restricted to BRAF-wt (consistent with downstream MAPK
   pathway logic), though this overlaps heavily with KRAS status.
6. Demographics (sex, race/ethnicity, insurance, rural residence,
   smoking, education), comorbidities, site-of-mets, vital signs,
   symptom grades, and 27 candidate SNPs add no detectable independent
   prognostic information for PFS in this cohort.
