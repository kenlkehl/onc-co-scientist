Analysis summary for ds001_crc (n = 50,000; outcome = pfs_months)
================================================================

Overview
--------
We ran ten iterations of propose-test-refine on a 50,000-patient colorectal
cancer EHR cohort with a single continuous outcome (pfs_months). PFS in this
cohort is centered around 4.3 months (SD 2.3, range 0-16.3). The analyses
moved from canonical prognostic main effects, to single-treatment main
effects, to clinically motivated treatment-by-biomarker interactions, to a
multivariable model, and finally to refined subgroup interactions
discovered along the way.

What was supported
------------------
1. Classic prognostic factors behave as expected and are highly robust:
   - ECOG PS: each 1-point increase reduces PFS by ~1.19 months
     (p < 1e-300; PFS 5.27 / 4.05 / 2.91 for ECOG 0/1/2).
   - Stage IV: -1.35 months (p < 1e-300).
   - Higher baseline albumin lengthens PFS (+0.47 per g/dL; p = 3e-115).
   - Greater weight loss over the past 6 months shortens PFS
     (-0.073 months per 1% loss; p = 2e-167).
   - Higher baseline CEA shortens PFS (-0.0053 per ng/mL; p = 3e-17).
   - Higher LDH shortens PFS (small but significant; beta = -0.0003, p = 0.013).
   These survive multivariable adjustment with stable signs and magnitudes.

2. KRAS, BRAF V600E and right-sided primary are independent poor-prognosis
   biomarkers. After adjustment for case mix:
   - KRAS mutation:    -0.354 months (p < 1e-300).
   - BRAF V600E:       -0.350 months (p = 8e-65).
   - Right-sided primary: -0.287 months (p = 1e-272).

3. The single most striking treatment finding -- and the central
   pharmacology signal in the dataset -- is regorafenib:
   - Marginal: regorafenib-treated patients average 5.09 vs 4.12 months
     PFS, a +0.97-month difference (p = 2e-216).
   - Adjusted: still +0.94 months in a model that controls ECOG, stage,
     age, albumin, weight loss, CEA, KRAS, BRAF V600E, and sidedness.
   - Strong qualitative subgroup heterogeneity (interactions confirmed
     statistically AND in stratified estimates):
       Regorafenib x KRAS mutation:   interaction beta = -1.63 (p = 8e-226).
         KRAS-WT diff +1.66 months (p = 6e-293)
         KRAS-MUT diff +0.03 months (NS)
       Regorafenib x BRAF V600E:      interaction beta = -1.18 (p = 6e-23).
         BRAF-WT diff +1.03 months (p = 5e-226)
         BRAF V600E diff -0.16 months (NS)
       Regorafenib x right-sided primary: interaction beta = -1.45 (p = 4e-168).
         Left-sided diff +1.48 months (p = 4e-274)
         Right-sided diff +0.03 months (NS)
     Combined: in patients who are both KRAS-WT and BRAF-WT, regorafenib
     adds +1.81 months of PFS; in patients with either mutation, the
     benefit is effectively zero (+0.009).
   This pattern is unusual relative to standard clinical priors (regorafenib
   is conventionally given as a non-selective late-line agent regardless of
   genotype/sidedness) and is a noteworthy data finding.

What was refuted
----------------
1. Canonical anti-EGFR pharmacology was NOT recapitulated for cetuximab:
   - No main effect (-0.038 months, p = 0.09).
   - No interaction with KRAS, NRAS, BRAF V600E, or right-sided primary
     (all interaction p > 0.5).
   - Even restricting to the canonical anti-EGFR-sensitive subgroup
     (RAS-WT, BRAF-WT, left-sided; n = 16,443), cetuximab on PFS = 4.62
     vs off = 4.68 (diff -0.07, p = 0.13). No detectable benefit.

2. Pembrolizumab does NOT show the expected MSI-H interaction:
   - Main effect +0.005 months (p = 0.86).
   - Interaction with msi_high beta = +0.002 (p = 0.99).
   - In the MSI-H subgroup (n = 2,513; n_pembro = 396): pembrolizumab
     diff = +0.007 months (p = 0.96). No signal.

3. Encorafenib does NOT show a BRAF V600E interaction:
   - Interaction beta = -0.14 (p = 0.38). In BRAF V600E patients
     (n_enco = 242), encorafenib diff = -0.13 (p = 0.33), trending the
     wrong way. BRAF V600E remains poor-prognosis but encorafenib does
     not appear to rescue these patients in this dataset.

4. Trastuzumab/tucatinib does NOT show a HER2 interaction. Both
   her2_amplified and her2_amplification yield null interactions
   (p = 0.78 and 0.65); the HER2-amplified subgroup with the regimen is
   small (n = 120) but the point estimate is essentially zero.

5. Bevacizumab has no detectable PFS effect (p = 0.35).

6. Several variables that we hypothesized would matter prognostically
   did NOT:
   - liver_mets, bone_mets (both NS).
   - race/ethnicity (no detectable disparity in PFS by category).
   - insurance type (private vs medicaid borderline +0.05 months,
     p = 0.09; otherwise null).
   - rural residence, sex, prior lines of therapy: all NS.
   - Comorbidity burden (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart
     failure, CAD, etc.): individually NS; depression/anxiety borderline
     (-0.05, p = 0.053).
   - 30 candidate SNPs: three reach nominal p < 0.05 (rs1050828
     +0.092 p = 0.015; rs1801131 -0.049 p = 0.027; rs1801197 -0.049
     p = 0.047), but none survive Bonferroni correction at alpha = 0.05/30.

Counter-intuitive finding
-------------------------
Age has a POSITIVE univariate and adjusted association with PFS
(beta = +0.18 months per year; p < 1e-300, robust to ECOG/stage
adjustment). This contradicts a typical clinical prior that older age
worsens cancer outcomes; in this dataset the relationship is reversed.
This deserves note when interpreting the cohort.

Multivariable summary
---------------------
The full OLS model (R^2 = 0.86) yields the following independently
significant predictors of pfs_months (signed beta):

  +  treatment_regorafenib       +0.94
  +  albumin_g_dl                +0.47
  +  age_years                   +0.18
  -  cea_ng_ml                   -0.005
  -  ldh_u_l                     -0.0004
  -  weight_loss_pct_6mo         -0.076
  -  right_sided_primary         -0.29
  -  kras_mutation               -0.35
  -  braf_v600e                  -0.36
  -  ecog_ps                     -1.17
  -  stage_iv                    -1.36

The remaining five treatments (cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab,
encorafenib, trastuzumab/tucatinib) contribute nothing detectable to
PFS in this cohort, alone or in their canonical biomarker subgroups.
Among the somatic alterations we tested, NRAS, MSI-H and HER2
amplification show no main effect or treatment interaction.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
- The standard clinical prognostics (ECOG, stage, albumin, weight loss,
  CEA, LDH) and the molecular features (KRAS, BRAF V600E, right-sided
  primary) behave as the literature would predict and dominate the
  variability in PFS.
- Of the six administered therapies, regorafenib is the only one with a
  measurable PFS effect, and its benefit is sharply concentrated in
  patients who are KRAS-wildtype, BRAF-wildtype, and left-sided. The
  triple-positive group (RAS-WT, BRAF-WT, left-sided) gains roughly
  +1.8 months while the rest gain essentially nothing.
- Several agents that would normally be expected to interact with their
  predictive biomarkers (cetuximab x KRAS/sidedness, pembrolizumab x
  MSI-H, encorafenib x BRAF V600E, trastuzumab/tucatinib x HER2)
  show null interactions in this dataset. This is a meaningful negative
  result and suggests that, whatever process produced this cohort's PFS
  values, it encoded the regorafenib-by-(KRAS/BRAF/sidedness)
  relationship rather than the canonical anti-EGFR / immunotherapy /
  targeted-therapy patterns.
- Demographic and socioeconomic features (race/ethnicity, insurance,
  rural residence, sex) and the panel of common SNPs do not show
  meaningful associations with PFS after appropriate caution about
  multiple testing.
