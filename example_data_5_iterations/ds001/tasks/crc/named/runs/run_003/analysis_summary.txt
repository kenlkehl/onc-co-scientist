Analysis summary — ds001_crc (n = 50,000 metastatic colorectal cancer patients)
================================================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival in months; mean 4.31, SD 2.29).
All effect estimates below are signed coefficients on the natural pfs_months scale
from ordinary least squares regression. "Significant" = p < 0.05.

Iteration 1 — Classical prognostic factors (main effects)
---------------------------------------------------------
All six standard prognostic features behaved as expected and were highly significant:
  - ECOG performance status: -1.190 mo per unit (p < 1e-300). [supported]
  - Stage IV vs not: -1.352 mo (p < 1e-300). [supported]
  - Serum albumin: +0.465 mo per g/dL (p = 3e-115). [supported]
  - Serum LDH: -0.000317 mo per U/L (p = 0.013). [supported, modest per unit]
  - CEA: -0.00535 mo per ng/mL (p = 3e-17). [supported]
  - 6-month weight-loss percent: -0.073 mo per % (p = 2e-167). [supported]

Iteration 2 — Treatment main effects (unadjusted)
-------------------------------------------------
Five of the six drug exposures showed no detectable main effect on PFS:
  - Cetuximab: -0.038 mo (p = 0.089). [refuted in this direction]
  - Bevacizumab: -0.019 mo (p = 0.35). [refuted]
  - Pembrolizumab: +0.005 mo (p = 0.86). [null]
  - Encorafenib: +0.005 mo (p = 0.87). [null]
  - Trastuzumab+tucatinib: -0.044 mo (p = 0.25). [refuted]
  - Regorafenib: +0.972 mo (p < 1e-300). [supported — only positive treatment signal]

Regorafenib is the single therapy with a strong, statistically robust PFS benefit in
this cohort. This is opposite to its real-world clinical role as a late-line drug
with very modest absolute benefit, suggesting the dataset's drug–outcome wiring
does not match standard CRC literature for regorafenib.

Iteration 3 — Cetuximab × RAS interaction
-----------------------------------------
Standard prior: cetuximab benefits RAS-WT only.
  - In RAS-WT (n = 28,838): cetuximab effect -0.047 mo (p = 0.13). [refuted]
  - In RAS-mut (n = 21,162): cetuximab effect -0.030 mo (p = 0.32). [null as expected]
  - Interaction term: +0.017 (p = 0.71). [no effect modification detected]
The expected RAS-WT-specific cetuximab benefit is absent.

Iteration 4 — Encorafenib × BRAF V600E interaction
--------------------------------------------------
Standard prior: encorafenib benefits BRAF V600E.
  - In BRAF V600E (n = 2,260): -0.129 mo (p = 0.36). [refuted; sign even negative]
  - In BRAF WT (n = 47,740): +0.013 mo (p = 0.72). [null as expected]
  - Interaction: -0.141 (p = 0.38). [opposite direction; not significant]
The targeted-therapy benefit is not encoded.

Iteration 5 — Pembrolizumab × MSI-high interaction
--------------------------------------------------
Standard prior: pembrolizumab benefits MSI-high markedly.
  - In MSI-H (n = 2,491): +0.007 mo (p = 0.95). [refuted]
  - In MSS (n = 47,509): +0.005 mo (p = 0.86). [null as expected]
  - Interaction: +0.002 (p = 0.99). [no modification]
The classical MSI-H × immunotherapy effect is not present.

Iteration 6 — Trastuzumab+tucatinib × HER2 amplified interaction
----------------------------------------------------------------
  - In HER2-amplified (n = 1,506): +0.017 mo (p = 0.94). [refuted]
  - In HER2-negative (n = 48,494): -0.046 mo (p = 0.23). [null]
  - Interaction: +0.063 (p = 0.78). [no modification]
The expected HER2-targeted benefit is also absent.

Iteration 7 — Sidedness × cetuximab (within RAS-WT) and sidedness main effect
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Standard prior: cetuximab benefit larger in left-sided RAS-WT.
  - Left-sided RAS-WT: cetuximab -0.062 mo (p = 0.14). [refuted]
  - Right-sided RAS-WT: cetuximab -0.035 mo (p = 0.44). [null]
  - Interaction within RAS-WT: +0.026 (p = 0.69). [no modification]
However, sidedness itself is prognostic: in the multivariable model right-sided
primary has -0.283 mo PFS (p < 1e-200). [supported]

Iteration 8 — Bevacizumab adjusted, bevacizumab × RAS, regorafenib robustness
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Bevacizumab adjusted for RAS/BRAF/MSI: -0.020 mo (p = 0.34). [null]
  - Bevacizumab × RAS interaction: +0.002 (p = 0.95). [null]
  - Regorafenib in fully adjusted multivariable model: +0.940 mo (p < 1e-300).
    [supported — independent of prognostic features and other treatments]

Iteration 9 — Inflammation/nutrition markers and age
----------------------------------------------------
  - NLR: +0.005 mo per unit (p = 0.29). Direction opposite to the inflammation
    hypothesis; not significant. [refuted]
  - CRP: -0.00202 mo per mg/L (p = 0.065). Borderline; correct direction but NS. [weak]
  - Hemoglobin: -0.010 mo per g/dL (p = 0.078). Wrong direction and NS. [refuted]
  - Age: +0.177 mo per year (p < 1e-300). Strongly positive — OPPOSITE to the prior
    that older age shortens PFS. The effect persists at +0.176 in the multivariable
    model, so it is not confounded by ECOG, stage, biomarkers, or labs as recorded.
    Most plausible explanation is selection / confounding by indication that the
    available covariates do not capture (e.g., younger patients enriched for more
    aggressive biology or for later-line salvage strategies). [refuted as stated]

Iteration 10 — Multivariable model
----------------------------------
Single OLS with age, sex, ECOG, stage, sidedness, RAS, BRAF V600E, MSI-H, HER2-amp,
albumin, LDH, CEA, NLR, and all six treatments as predictors. Key adjusted estimates:
  Negative prognostic (all p < 1e-50, supported):
    ECOG -1.172, stage IV -1.359, right-sided -0.283, RAS-mut -0.331, BRAF V600E -0.358,
    LDH -0.00043 per U/L, CEA -0.00494 per ng/mL.
  Positive prognostic (supported):
    Albumin +0.470 per g/dL, age +0.176 per year (opposite to prior), NLR +0.0045
    (small, p = 0.02).
  Null (refuted as prognostic):
    Sex (female) +0.011 (p = 0.20), MSI-H -0.005 (p = 0.77), HER2-amp -0.030 (p = 0.21).
  Treatments:
    Cetuximab -0.011 (p = 0.21), bevacizumab +0.005 (p = 0.54), pembrolizumab +0.0005
    (p = 0.96), encorafenib +0.009 (p = 0.52), trastuzumab+tucatinib +0.023 (p = 0.12)
    — all null. Regorafenib +0.940 (p < 1e-300) — the lone independent treatment effect.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. Classical prognostic biology holds: poor performance status, stage IV disease,
   right-sided primary, low albumin, elevated LDH and CEA, high RAS or BRAF V600E
   mutation status, and greater 6-month weight loss are all independent negative
   predictors of PFS in this CRC cohort, with magnitudes consistent with the
   literature.
2. Standard biomarker–treatment interactions taught as canonical CRC pharmacology
   are NOT detectable in this dataset, despite n=50,000 and adequate biomarker
   prevalences (RAS WT 58%, BRAF V600E 4.5%, MSI-H 5.0%, HER2-amp 3.0%):
     - Cetuximab × RAS WT: not significant.
     - Cetuximab × left-sided (within RAS WT): not significant.
     - Encorafenib × BRAF V600E: not significant (in fact slightly negative).
     - Pembrolizumab × MSI-high: not significant.
     - Trastuzumab+tucatinib × HER2 amplification: not significant.
3. The only treatment with a real PFS signal is regorafenib (~+0.94 months adjusted),
   which is biologically surprising — regorafenib in real-world late-line CRC has
   only modest benefit. This dataset appears to encode a strong, biomarker-agnostic
   regorafenib effect rather than the classical biomarker-targeted interactions.
4. Two findings run opposite to standard priors:
   - Older age is associated with LONGER PFS (+0.18 mo/year, robust to adjustment).
   - NLR has no negative prognostic value (in fact a small positive coefficient).
   These deviations are likely explained by selection / unmeasured confounding by
   indication rather than true reversal of biology.
5. Net: the cohort behaves like a real metastatic CRC population for prognostic
   markers but appears to have a synthetic / non-standard treatment effect
   structure (only regorafenib helps; canonical biomarker-targeted matches do not).
