ds001_crc — Analysis summary

Cohort
------
50,000 patient records, 123 anonymized features (87 binary or low-cardinality
integer; 35 continuous; 1 race-like categorical feature_064; 1 insurance-like
categorical feature_018), and one outcome `pfs_months` (range 0–16.25, mean 4.31,
median 4.21, ~3% recorded as exactly 0). All 10 iterations of the propose–test–
refine protocol were used.

Iteration 1 — universal screen
------------------------------
A univariate screen (Spearman for continuous, Welch t-test for binary, one-way
ANOVA for multi-level/categorical) identified roughly 19 of 123 features with
p < 0.05 against pfs_months, with a much smaller "head" of about 10 features
where p was effectively 0 and effect sizes were clinically large. This shaped
all subsequent iterations.

Iterations 2–5 — main effects of strong signals
-----------------------------------------------
Six features showed dominant prognostic effects on pfs_months. All directions
held under multivariable adjustment in iteration 7.

* feature_078 (continuous, range 30–90, mean 65 — pattern consistent with an
  age-like demographic): adjusted slope +0.176 months per unit (p << 1e-300).
  This is the single largest predictor in the dataset; quintile means run
  monotonically 1.86 → 3.34 → 4.30 → 5.27 → 6.83 months. Note: the direction
  is positive, i.e. higher feature_078 values are associated with longer
  pfs_months in this cohort, opposite to the usual prognostic effect of age in
  cancer; readers should treat the underlying clinical identity of feature_078
  as unconfirmed.
* feature_057 (3-level ordinal): strict monotonic decrease in pfs_months with
  level — 5.27 → 4.05 → 2.91. Adjusted contrasts: level 1 vs 0 = −1.20,
  level 2 vs 0 = −2.33 (both p << 1e-300). Pattern is consistent with a stage-
  or grade-like prognostic variable.
* feature_051 (binary, prevalence 0.55): adjusted effect −1.36 months for
  feature_051=1 vs 0 (p << 1e-300). Effect is stable across every stratum of
  feature_057 (−1.39 / −1.34 / −1.31), so feature_051 acts essentially
  additively on the pfs_months scale.
* feature_038 (binary, prevalence 0.20): adjusted effect +0.94 months for
  feature_038=1 vs 0 (p << 1e-300). Effect is highly significant in every
  stage stratum (+0.99 / +0.92 / +0.88). Pattern is consistent with a
  positive prognostic biomarker.
* feature_099 (continuous, range 0–24.6, ~28% zeros): adjusted slope
  −0.076/unit (p << 1e-300); quintile means 4.62 → 4.53 → 4.36 → 4.18 → 3.87.
* feature_092 (continuous, narrow range 1.5–5.5): adjusted slope +0.47/unit
  (p << 1e-300); quadratic term not significant after adjustment, so the
  relationship is essentially linear.

Three further features with smaller but robust adjusted effects:
feature_013 (−0.35), feature_043 (−0.29), feature_109 (−0.36); plus
feature_009 with a very small per-unit slope (−0.005) but a wide skewed range.

Refuted univariate signal
-------------------------
feature_067 (rare binary, prevalence 0.030) showed +0.22 months in univariate
testing but the effect collapsed to +0.02 (p=0.37) once feature_078 and
feature_057 were adjusted for; classified as confounded.

Iteration 6 — race and insurance: NULL
--------------------------------------
This was a deliberate equity probe, with the pre-specified expectation that
non-white patients and medicaid/uninsured patients might have shorter
pfs_months.

* Race (feature_064 — white, hispanic, black, asian, other): unadjusted means
  4.273 to 4.407 across the five categories (one-way ANOVA F=0.87, p=0.48).
  After full clinical adjustment, every race level is within ±0.031 months of
  reference at p ≥ 0.36. REFUTED.
* Insurance (feature_018 — medicare, private, medicaid, uninsured): unadjusted
  means 4.249 to 4.330 (ANOVA p=0.22). Adjusted, every level is within ±0.032
  months at p ≥ 0.16. REFUTED.
* Joint contribution of race + insurance to the adjusted model: F=0.52 on
  7 df, p=0.82, ΔR² = 0.00001.
* Race × age and insurance × stage interaction terms are all non-significant
  (max |coef|=0.077 at p=0.06).
* Stratified means are stable; e.g. young-uninsured 2.39 vs young-private 2.42;
  old-uninsured 6.21 vs old-private 6.24.
* Distribution of the positive biomarker feature_038 is balanced across race
  (0.192–0.211) and insurance (0.190–0.202); feature_051 prevalence likewise
  uniform (0.54–0.56).

The cohort therefore shows no detectable racial or insurance disparity in
pfs_months — either as a main effect, in stratified subgroups, or in
biomarker prevalence.

Iteration 7 — multivariable model
---------------------------------
A 10-term model
  pfs_months ~ feature_078 + C(feature_057) + feature_051 + feature_038
             + feature_013 + feature_043 + feature_109 + feature_099
             + feature_092 + feature_009
yields R² = 0.8584 (adj 0.8584). All 10 predictors significant at p < 1e-78,
each in the direction predicted by the univariate screen. Adding weaker
candidates (feature_028, feature_070) leaves R² unchanged (<1e-5 increment)
and they lose significance — supports treating these weak univariate signals
as confounded.

Iteration 8 — interactions among top predictors
-----------------------------------------------
* feature_038 × feature_057 (biomarker × stage): the +0.94-month biomarker
  benefit is real in every stage but shrinks modestly with stage —
  +0.985 → +0.918 → +0.875 across levels 0/1/2. Interaction coefficients
  −0.060 (p=0.005) and −0.100 (p=8e-04). Supported.
* feature_051 × feature_038 (independence test): cell means imply nearly
  identical biomarker effects in feature_051=0 (+0.94) and feature_051=1
  (+1.01); interaction coef +0.027, p=0.19. The two predictors act
  additively. Supported.
* feature_078 × feature_057 (dominant predictor × stage): the per-unit
  positive feature_078 slope is reduced at higher stages — interaction
  coefficients −0.003/year at stage 1 (p=5.7e-04) and −0.017/year at stage 2
  (p=3.8e-37). The prognostic gain from feature_078 is dampened at advanced
  stage. Supported.

Iteration 9 — qualitative effect modification
---------------------------------------------
The most striking interaction in the dataset:

* feature_013 × feature_038. Stratified by feature_038:
    - In feature_038 = 0 (n = 39,978, 80%): feature_013 coef = −0.001
      (p = 0.95) — null.
    - In feature_038 = 1 (n = 10,022, 20%): feature_013 coef = −1.630
      (p = 2.5e-182) — very large negative.
  This is a near-textbook qualitative effect-modification pattern: feature_013
  has essentially no association with pfs_months in biomarker-negative
  patients but is strongly negative in biomarker-positive patients. Pattern
  is consistent with feature_013 being a treatment or co-factor whose
  harmful association with pfs_months is restricted to feature_038-positive
  patients (analogous, in real-world oncology, to therapies that are
  contraindicated by a particular molecular feature).

Iteration 10 — synthesis
------------------------
Hypotheses supported:
  h1, h2, h3, h4, h5, h6, h7, h8, h10, h11, h12, h16, h18, h20, h21, h22,
  h23, h24.

Hypotheses refuted:
  h13 (race main effect), h14 (insurance main effect), h15 (race/insurance ×
  stage interactions), h17 (additional weak features improve fit).

Confounded univariate signal:
  h9 (feature_067 effect collapses under adjustment).

Independence confirmed:
  h19 (feature_051 and feature_038 are additive — no interaction).

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. Variation in pfs_months in this cohort is dominated by ~10 clinical
   features. A simple linear model with these features captures R² ≈ 0.86,
   leaving little room for additional structured signal.
2. The strongest predictor is feature_078 (likely an age-like demographic),
   in a direction (positive) that is unusual for an oncology cohort — its
   underlying clinical identity should be confirmed externally.
3. feature_057 behaves like a stage variable with a clean monotonic
   prognostic gradient.
4. feature_051 and feature_038 are independent, large-magnitude prognostic
   variables; feature_038 is plausibly a positive biomarker. feature_013
   shows a striking treatment-by-biomarker–style interaction with feature_038:
   feature_013 is associated with markedly shorter pfs_months only when
   feature_038 = 1.
5. Higher levels of feature_057 modestly attenuate both the feature_078 and
   the feature_038 benefits — i.e. advanced disease compresses prognostic
   gains.
6. Equity probe is robustly negative: race (feature_064) and insurance
   (feature_018) show no main-effect, stratum-specific, or biomarker-
   distribution disparity in pfs_months. The clinical predictors that drive
   pfs_months are equitably distributed across race and insurance categories,
   and conditional means of pfs_months are essentially identical across these
   groups.
