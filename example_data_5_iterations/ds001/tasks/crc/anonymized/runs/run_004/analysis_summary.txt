Analysis Summary — ds001_crc (n = 50,000; outcome = pfs_months)
================================================================

Approach
--------
Ten iterations of propose-test-refine were run against pfs_months. Iterations 1-3
established main effects for the strongest univariate predictors; iteration 4
addressed sociodemographic main effects; iteration 5 fitted a multivariable OLS
to confirm independence; iterations 6-8 tested biomarker-by-treatment, age-by-
treatment, and performance-status-by-treatment interactions; iteration 9
addressed equity (treatment receipt and treatment effect by race and insurance);
iteration 10 consolidated the findings into a final stratified three-way table
and a unified multivariable model.

Main effects on pfs_months (iterations 1-3, 5)
----------------------------------------------
The dataset is dominated by a small set of very strong prognostic factors. In
both univariate testing and a multivariable OLS that included all top features
plus race (feature_064) and insurance (feature_018), the following effects
were highly significant (all p < 1e-50 in the adjusted model):

  feature_078  — continuous (range 30-90, mean 65). Strongly POSITIVE: each
                 one-unit increase adds +0.176 months of PFS. Univariate r =
                 0.766. By far the single most predictive variable in the file.
                 (Direction is opposite to what one would expect if this were
                 chronological age in a real-world CRC cohort, so it should be
                 interpreted as an opaque strong prognostic scalar.)

  feature_051  — binary, ~55% prevalence. Strongly NEGATIVE: -1.35 months
                 unadjusted, -1.36 months adjusted. A pure prognostic factor:
                 its effect is the same in feature_038-treated and -untreated
                 patients, in marker-positive and marker-negative subgroups,
                 and across feature_057 levels.

  feature_057  — three-level ordinal (0, 1, 2; modal value 1, ~50%; level 2
                 ~15%). Strongly NEGATIVE monotone trend: -1.17 months per
                 level adjusted (means 5.27 / 4.05 / 2.91). Behaves as a
                 typical performance-status-like factor.

  feature_038  — binary, ~20% prevalence. POSITIVE: +0.97 months unadjusted,
                 +0.94 months adjusted. This factor's pattern (prevalence,
                 magnitude of effect, and behavior in interaction analyses)
                 is consistent with a targeted therapy.

  feature_092  — continuous (1.5-5.5, mean 3.8, plausibly serum albumin).
                 POSITIVE: +0.47 months per unit adjusted.

  feature_099  — continuous (0-25, mean 3.8). NEGATIVE: -0.076 months per
                 unit adjusted.

  feature_009  — continuous (0.02-777, heavy right tail; plausibly a tumor
                 marker such as CEA). NEGATIVE: -0.005 months per unit
                 adjusted (small per-unit but large dynamic range).

  feature_013  — binary, ~42%. NEGATIVE: -0.35 months adjusted; primarily
                 functions as a treatment-effect modifier (see below).

  feature_043  — binary, ~35%. NEGATIVE: -0.29 months adjusted; primarily
                 functions as a treatment-effect modifier (see below).

  feature_109  — binary, ~5%. NEGATIVE: -0.36 months adjusted.

  feature_067  — binary, ~3%. POSITIVE in unadjusted analysis (+0.22, p<0.001)
                 but NOT independent in the multivariable model (p = 0.36).

The full main-effects model achieved adjusted R² = 0.858, indicating these ~10
features explain the great majority of pfs_months variance.

Sociodemographic main effects (iteration 4)
-------------------------------------------
Neither feature_064 (race; five categories) nor feature_018 (insurance; four
categories) showed any significant association with pfs_months, in either
univariate testing (race ANOVA p = 0.48, insurance ANOVA p = 0.22) or in the
multivariable model (every race and insurance indicator p > 0.21 after
adjustment). The directional pairwise contrasts of clinical interest also did
not reach significance: white vs black p = 0.19, white vs hispanic p = 0.97,
uninsured vs private p = 0.12, medicaid vs private p = 0.10. The dataset
contains no detectable racial or insurance disparity in pfs_months.

The principal interaction finding (iterations 6-7, 10)
------------------------------------------------------
The most clinically important pattern in the dataset is a qualitative
biomarker-by-treatment interaction:

  feature_038 confers a large positive PFS benefit ONLY among patients in
  whom both feature_013 and feature_043 are zero. In patients who are
  positive for either marker, feature_038 confers no detectable benefit.

Quantitative evidence:

  - feature_013 × feature_038 interaction coefficient = -1.64 (p << 1e-300).
    Stratified: in feature_013 = 0 patients, feature_038 adds +1.66 months
    (5.77 vs 4.12, p < 1e-292); in feature_013 = 1 patients, feature_038 adds
    +0.03 months (p = 0.46).

  - feature_043 × feature_038 interaction coefficient = -1.45 (p << 1e-300).
    Stratified: in feature_043 = 0 patients, feature_038 adds +1.48 months
    (5.60 vs 4.12, p < 1e-273); in feature_043 = 1 patients, feature_038
    adds +0.03 months (p = 0.52).

  - Composite "marker-positive" flag (feature_013 = 1 OR feature_043 = 1;
    62% prevalence): feature_038 × marker_pos interaction coefficient =
    -2.52 (p << 1e-300). In marker_pos = 0 (n = 18,853), feature_038 adds
    +2.52 months (6.64 vs 4.12); in marker_pos = 1 (n = 31,147), feature_038
    adds only +0.04 months.

The two markers are statistically independent of each other in this dataset
(chi-square p ≈ 0.55), so the OR-rule does not reflect overlapping biology;
each marker independently abolishes the feature_038 benefit, and either
positive marker is sufficient to do so.

Including the feature_038 × marker_pos interaction term in a model with
feature_078, feature_057, feature_051, feature_092, and feature_099 raises
the explained variance to R² = 0.904, with the feature_038 main coefficient
= +2.52 (the treatment effect at marker_pos = 0) and the interaction
coefficient = -2.52 (cancelling that benefit at marker_pos = 1). The
three-way table by (marker_pos, feature_051, feature_038) shows that this
pattern is preserved in both feature_051 strata: feature_038 adds +2.4-2.6
months when marker_pos = 0 and adds essentially nothing when marker_pos = 1,
whether feature_051 = 0 or 1.

Effect modification by other factors (iterations 6, 8)
------------------------------------------------------
  - feature_051 × feature_038: NO interaction (p = 0.18). feature_051 is a
    pure prognostic factor that subtracts ~1.36 months in every subgroup.

  - feature_078 × feature_038: NO interaction (p = 0.28). The +0.94 month
    treatment effect of feature_038 is constant across feature_078 tertiles
    (+1.01, +0.93, +0.94 months in low/mid/high tertiles).

  - feature_057 × feature_038: STATISTICALLY significant but CLINICALLY
    trivial (interaction coef -0.06 per level, p = 2.5e-04). Stratified
    feature_038 effects: +0.93, +1.00, +0.90 months across feature_057
    levels 0, 1, 2 — the absolute treatment effect is essentially constant.

Equity analyses (iteration 9)
-----------------------------
Two equity questions were tested:

  (a) Treatment receipt. The proportion of patients receiving feature_038
      did not differ by feature_064 (race; chi-square p = 0.55; range
      19.2%-21.1%) or by feature_018 (insurance; chi-square p = 0.59; range
      19.0%-20.2%). No detectable disparity in access.

  (b) Treatment effect modification. No individual race or insurance category
      showed a significantly different feature_038 treatment effect (all
      individual interaction p-values > 0.07; per-race feature_038 effects
      0.92-1.22 months; per-insurance effects within ±0.11 of medicaid
      reference). The joint Wald test for the four race × feature_038
      interaction terms was borderline (F = 2.40 on 4 df, p = 0.048), but no
      individual contrast was significant and the practical spread is ~0.3
      months. Insurance × feature_038 joint test was not significant.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
SUPPORTED:
  - Strong main effects of feature_078, feature_051, feature_038, feature_057,
    feature_092, feature_099, feature_013, feature_043, feature_009, feature_109,
    each in the predicted direction (h1-h11).
  - Independence of those main effects after multivariable adjustment (h16).
  - Race and insurance NOT independently associated with PFS (h17).
  - Qualitative feature_013 × feature_038 interaction (h18).
  - Qualitative feature_043 × feature_038 interaction (h19).
  - feature_051 NOT a treatment-effect modifier (h20, h31).
  - Composite marker_pos × feature_038 interaction abolishes treatment
    benefit at marker_pos = 1 (h21, h29, h30).
  - feature_078 does NOT modify feature_038 treatment effect (h23).
  - No disparity in feature_038 receipt by race or insurance (h25, h26),
    no disparity in feature_038 effect by insurance (h28).

REFUTED OR NOT SUPPORTED:
  - Race-related differences in pfs_months (h12, h14): not significant.
  - Insurance-related differences in pfs_months (h13, h15): not significant.
  - feature_013 and feature_043 are correlated with each other (h22): they
    are essentially independent (p = 0.55).
  - Larger absolute attenuation of feature_038 effect at higher feature_057
    (h24): the interaction is statistically significant but the absolute
    treatment effect is essentially flat across feature_057 levels, contrary
    to the hypothesized pattern.
  - Race × treatment effect modification (h27): borderline omnibus test
    (p = 0.048) but no individual contrast significant; effectively null.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
The ds001_crc cohort is well-described by a small number of strong prognostic
features (feature_078, feature_051, feature_057, feature_092, feature_099,
feature_009) plus a single dominant treatment-biomarker pattern: feature_038
is a treatment that produces a clinically large (~2.5 month) PFS benefit only
in patients whose feature_013 AND feature_043 are both zero, and produces no
benefit in the ~62% of patients who have either marker positive. This is a
textbook qualitative biomarker-treatment interaction of the type seen, e.g.,
with EGFR-targeting therapy in RAS-wild-type colorectal cancer. The other
strong negative prognostic factors (feature_051, feature_057) act
independently of feature_038 receipt and do not modify treatment benefit.
There is no evidence in this dataset of racial or insurance-based disparities
in either receipt of feature_038 or in the PFS outcome conferred by it. A
multivariable OLS that includes only feature_078, feature_057, feature_051,
feature_092, feature_099, and the feature_038 × marker_pos interaction
explains 90.4% of the variance in pfs_months.
