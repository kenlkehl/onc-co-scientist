Analysis summary - ds001_crc cohort
====================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with anonymized features (123 numbered features, 2 named
object features feature_064 [race: white/hispanic/black/asian/other] and
feature_018 [insurance: medicare/private/medicaid/uninsured]) and one continuous
outcome pfs_months (mean 4.31, sd 2.29, range 0-16.25).
Variable typing: 80 binary, 35 continuous, 6 small-cardinality ordinal-numeric,
2 string categorical. No missing values.

Iterations 1-2: marginal screen
-------------------------------
Across all 80 binary features, two stand out by orders of magnitude in a Welch
t-test:
  - feature_051 (prevalence 55.0%) is associated with SHORTER PFS:
    mean 3.70 vs 5.06 mo, diff -1.35, p<1e-300.
  - feature_038 (prevalence 20.0%) is associated with LONGER PFS:
    mean 5.09 vs 4.12 mo, diff +0.97, p=1.7e-216.
  - Other binaries with notable but smaller effects: feature_013 (-0.33),
    feature_043 (-0.29), feature_109 (-0.23), feature_067 (+0.22).

Among continuous features, one variable dominates:
  - feature_078 (range 30-90, plausibly age in years): Spearman rho=+0.785
    with pfs_months (positive direction; older feature_078 -> longer PFS,
    which is opposite the typical age-vs-survival pattern in oncology).
  - feature_092 (range 1.5-5.5, plausibly serum albumin): rho=+0.103.
  - feature_099 (range 0-24.6): rho=-0.120.

Among ordinal/categorical features:
  - feature_057 (3 levels) is the strongest categorical predictor:
    mean PFS 5.27 / 4.05 / 2.91 mo for levels 0/1/2 (ANOVA F=3528.9,
    p<1e-300). The monotonic decrease is consistent with a stage-like
    severity variable.

Demographic variables show NO marginal main effect:
  - feature_064 (race): ANOVA F=0.87, p=0.48; means 4.27-4.41 across the
    five categories.
  - feature_018 (insurance): F=1.47, p=0.22; means 4.25-4.33.

Iteration 3: multivariable adjustment
-------------------------------------
A single OLS model with feature_078, C(feature_057), feature_051, feature_038,
feature_013, feature_043, feature_109, feature_092, feature_099 explains
R^2=0.857 of the variance in PFS. All predictors retain their univariate
direction and remain individually significant (p<1e-50). Pairwise correlations
among these predictors are <0.01, so the adjusted effects are essentially
unchanged from univariate effects. Adjusted slopes (units = months of PFS):

  feature_078        +0.176 per unit
  feature_057 = 1    -1.20 vs 0
  feature_057 = 2    -2.33 vs 0
  feature_051 (1)    -1.36
  feature_038 (1)    +0.94
  feature_092        +0.47 per unit
  feature_099        -0.076 per unit
  feature_013 (1)    -0.36
  feature_043 (1)    -0.29
  feature_109 (1)    -0.36

Adding feature_006 (continuous) leaves R^2 unchanged at 0.857; its coefficient
is tiny (-0.0004 per unit) but is statistically significant due to sample size.

Iteration 4: demographic disparities in access and outcome
-----------------------------------------------------------
Two questions: (a) is the apparent beneficial treatment feature_038 less
frequently received by minoritized racial groups or worse-insured patients?
(b) after adjustment for stage, age and the negative-prognostic feature_051,
do race or insurance still predict pfs_months?

  - feature_038 prevalence by race: asian 0.211, black 0.201, hispanic 0.198,
    other 0.192, white 0.200 (range <2 percentage points).
  - feature_038 prevalence by insurance: medicare 0.202, private 0.201,
    medicaid 0.198, uninsured 0.190.
  - feature_057 stage distribution and feature_051 prevalence are essentially
    flat across both race and insurance.

Adjusted-for-stage/age/feature_051/feature_038 effects (vs reference):
  Race vs white: asian +0.006 (p=0.75); black +0.011 (p=0.41);
  hispanic -0.003 (p=0.83); other +0.038 (p=0.23).
  Insurance vs private: medicaid -0.003 (p=0.82); medicare -0.005 (p=0.63);
  uninsured -0.042 (p=0.066).

Conclusion: in this cohort, neither race nor insurance carries a detectable
disparity in stage at presentation, treatment receipt, or adjusted PFS.

Iteration 5: feature_038 x feature_057 (stage) interaction
-----------------------------------------------------------
Stratified means show feature_038 benefit at every stage:
  feature_057=0: +0.93 mo (5.08 vs 6.01, n_treated=3,613)
  feature_057=1: +1.00 mo (3.86 vs 4.85, n_treated=4,945)
  feature_057=2: +0.90 mo (2.73 vs 3.63, n_treated=1,464)

Formal interaction (adjusted): feature_038 x C(feature_057)[1] = -0.066
(p=0.006); x [2] = -0.112 (p=0.001). Statistically significant attenuation
at advanced stage but the practical magnitude is small; feature_038 remains
beneficial at every stage.

The feature_038 x feature_051 interaction is null (coef +0.029, p=0.19).

Iteration 6: feature_038 x demographics interactions
-----------------------------------------------------
Within-race adjusted feature_038 coefficients:
  white     +0.918 (n=32,456, p<1e-300)
  hispanic  +0.931 (n=7,572,  p=8e-228)
  asian     +0.956 (n=3,003,  p=2e-94)
  black     +0.996 (n=5,996,  p=5e-202)
  other     +1.093 (n=973,    p=6e-38)

Treatment benefit is positive and highly significant in every group; spread
is narrow (0.92-1.09 mo). Interaction terms vs white: black +0.078 (p=0.022),
other +0.172 (p=0.032), asian +0.039 (p=0.39), hispanic +0.014 (p=0.66). After
Bonferroni correction across the four comparisons (alpha = 0.0125) no
interaction is significant. Insurance x feature_038 interactions are uniformly
non-significant (medicaid p=0.42, medicare p=0.45, uninsured p=0.15).

There is no robust evidence of demographic heterogeneity in treatment effect.

Iteration 7: feature_092 x feature_038 interaction; comprehensive residual screen
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
feature_092 (albumin-like) x feature_038 = -0.090, p=1.7e-5. Direction:
absolute PFS gain from feature_038 is slightly smaller in patients with high
feature_092. At feature_092=0 the main-effect estimate is +1.28 mo, declining
to roughly +0.94 at the cohort mean of 3.8.

Residualizing pfs_months on (feature_078, C(feature_057), feature_051) and
re-screening every binary identifies no additional binary feature with
adjusted-effect magnitude >0.1 month beyond feature_038, feature_013,
feature_043, feature_109, and feature_067. The remaining 75 binaries appear
to be noise relative to the outcome.

Iteration 8: feature_099 - stage and treatment interactions
-----------------------------------------------------------
feature_099 x feature_057 stage interaction is null (p=0.49 and p=0.17 for
the two contrasts): the per-unit harm of feature_099 (~ -0.076 mo) is
approximately constant across stages.

feature_099 x feature_038 interaction is +0.0066, p=0.013 - a small attenuation
of the per-unit harm under treatment (slope from -0.078 off-treatment to
about -0.071 on-treatment). Direction is consistent with a partial protective
effect of feature_038 against the high-feature_099 hazard, but the magnitude
is modest.

Iteration 9: balanced-binary screening; final model
---------------------------------------------------
feature_007 (prevalence 44.7%, balanced binary - a sex-like candidate) shows
no main effect (adj coef +0.004, p=0.68) and no significant interaction with
feature_038 (+0.028, p=0.20). None of the other 12 balanced binaries (40-60%
prevalence) shows a meaningful adjusted association.

Final model R^2 = 0.857. The signal is concentrated in a handful of variables.

Iteration 10: sensitivity - feature_071 surprise
------------------------------------------------
feature_071 (11-level ordinal 0-10, plausibly performance status) carries
ZERO independent PFS signal: adjusted coefficient -0.0007 (p=0.64). Either
feature_071 is not what its shape suggests, or its effect has been entirely
absorbed by feature_057 / feature_051 / feature_092 / feature_099 in this
particular cohort. Univariate ANOVA across feature_071 levels also shows
flat means (4.19-4.40 mo across 0-10).

Synthesis
=========
Drivers of progression-free survival in this cohort, in order of independent
contribution to the final R^2 = 0.857 model:

1. feature_078 (continuous, plausibly age):  the dominant predictor;
   each unit adds +0.176 mo of PFS. Direction is positive (older = longer
   PFS), opposite of typical real-world oncology data; this is the most
   surprising finding.

2. feature_057 (3-level ordinal stage-like): each step costs about 1.2 mo
   of PFS.

3. feature_051 (binary, 55% prevalent, negative): -1.36 mo. A risk indicator
   - possibly a metastatic / palliative-intent flag.

4. feature_038 (binary, 20% prevalent, positive): +0.94 mo. The cohort's
   apparent beneficial treatment. Benefit is preserved at every stage,
   in every race, and across every insurance category.

5. feature_092 (continuous, range 1.5-5.5, mean 3.80, plausibly serum
   albumin): +0.47 per unit; modestly buffers the apparent benefit of
   feature_038.

6. feature_099 (continuous, range 0-24.6, plausibly a tumor marker such
   as CEA): -0.076 per unit; partially attenuated under feature_038.

7. feature_013, feature_043, feature_109 (binaries, all negative,
   -0.30 to -0.36 each): smaller risk markers.

Hypotheses supported (with effect direction matching prediction):
  H1, H2, H3 (binary screen identifies strong predictors); H4 (feature_078
  monotonic), H5 (feature_057 monotonic decrease), H6 (feature_092+/feature_099-
  signs), H8 (multivariable stability), H9 (R^2>0.5), H13 (feature_038 x
  stage interaction), H17 (feature_092 x feature_038 interaction), H18 (no
  additional binary >0.1 mo after adjustment), H20 (feature_099 x feature_038
  positive), H22 (final model R^2>0.85), H23 (feature_038 benefit positive
  in every race), H24 (feature_071 null after adjustment).

Hypotheses refuted:
  H7 partially - confirmed null marginal effect of race/insurance on PFS,
  which is the absence of expected disparity.
  H10 (race disparities in feature_038 access): refuted; prevalence is flat.
  H11 (race/insurance imbalances in stage / feature_051): refuted.
  H12 (no residual disparity after adjustment): supported as null.
  H14 (no feature_038 x feature_051 interaction): supported as null.
  H15 (race x feature_038 heterogeneity): refuted (no robust interaction).
  H16 (insurance x feature_038 heterogeneity): refuted.
  H19 (feature_099 x stage flat): supported as null.
  H21 (feature_007 / sex-like effect): refuted.

Overall conclusion
==================
The cohort is dominated by a single non-clinical-direction continuous
predictor (feature_078) and a stage-like ordinal (feature_057). On top of
that backbone, one binary (feature_051) is a strong negative risk marker,
one binary (feature_038) is a strong positive treatment-like marker, and two
continuous biomarkers (feature_092 positive, feature_099 negative) add
independent signal. Together these explain ~86% of variance in PFS in a
linear additive model.

There is essentially NO evidence of demographic disparity in this cohort:
  - Stage at presentation and treatment receipt are flat across race and
    insurance categories.
  - Adjusted PFS does not differ across race or insurance categories.
  - The benefit of feature_038 is positive and highly significant in every
    race stratum, with no robust interaction effect after correction for
    multiple testing.

The clinical interactions that ARE detectable (feature_038 x stage,
feature_092 x feature_038, feature_099 x feature_038) are statistically
robust because of the large sample size but small in absolute magnitude
relative to the main effects.
