Analysis Summary — ds001_crc (50,000 patients, outcome: pfs_months)
======================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
A 10-iteration propose-test-refine analysis identified a small set of dominant
predictors of progression-free survival (pfs_months) and one clinically
striking predictive-biomarker pattern. A parsimonious 12-feature OLS model
with two interaction terms achieved R^2 = 0.895 on the full cohort.

The headline finding: feature_038 behaves like a treatment whose efficacy is
almost entirely gated by two binary biomarkers, feature_013 and feature_043.
In double-negative (biomarker wild-type) patients it adds ~+2.1 PFS-months;
in double-positive patients it confers no benefit and appears mildly harmful.
Race (feature_064) and insurance (feature_018) show no meaningful main or
interaction effect on pfs_months.

ITERATION-BY-ITERATION
----------------------

Iteration 1 — Top main effects.
Univariate Spearman correlations with pfs_months identified six dominant
features:
  - feature_078 (continuous 30–90, mean 65): rho = +0.785, OLS slope +0.18
    PFS-months/unit; alone explains r^2 = 0.59. Mean PFS rises from 1.33 in
    the lowest decile to 7.45 in the highest.
  - feature_057 (3-level ordinal): mean PFS 5.27 → 4.05 → 2.91 across levels
    0/1/2 (ANOVA p ~ 0; level 2 vs 0 = −2.36 mo).
  - feature_051 (binary, prevalence 55%): −1.35 mo (1 vs 0).
  - feature_038 (binary, prevalence 20%): +0.97 mo (1 vs 0).
  - feature_092 (continuous): +0.47 mo per unit.
  - feature_099 (continuous): −0.073 mo per unit.

Iteration 2 — Joint adjustment + early interactions.
A 6-feature OLS model (R^2 = 0.848) preserved every direction and remained
highly significant. Two interactions reached significance but were small in
magnitude: feature_057 × feature_051 (PS penalty slightly attenuated when
feature_051 = 1) and feature_078 × feature_057 (slope of feature_078 mildly
attenuated at higher PS levels). feature_038 × feature_051 was NOT
significant (p = 0.19).

Iteration 3 — Demographics (race feature_064, insurance feature_018).
Both demographic categoricals showed essentially no association with
pfs_months. ANOVA across race: F = 0.87, p = 0.48; across insurance: F = 1.48,
p = 0.22. After adjustment for the top-six clinical features, joint F-tests
gave race p = 0.68 and insurance p = 0.54. Interaction tests of feature_038 ×
race and feature_038 × insurance also did not reach significance jointly
(race p = 0.10; insurance p = 0.15). Conclusion: in this cohort,
demographics neither predict the outcome nor modify the apparent treatment
effect.

Iteration 4 — Adjusted continuous-feature screen.
Among 30 remaining continuous features, only two added independent signal at
Bonferroni (alpha = 0.05/30):
  - feature_009 (heavy-tailed, range 0.02–777): coef −0.0051/unit, p = 2e−93.
  - feature_006 (mean ~224): coef −0.00041/unit, p = 8e−17.
The remaining ~28 continuous features were collectively non-informative
beyond the established core.

Iteration 5 — Adjusted binary-feature screen (~80 features).
Four binary features cleared Bonferroni:
  - feature_013 (prevalence 42%): −0.328 mo.
  - feature_043 (prevalence 35%): −0.287 mo.
  - feature_109 (prevalence 4.5%): −0.201 mo.
  - feature_067 (prevalence 3.0%): +0.181 mo.
All other binary features had adjusted p > 10^−3.

Iteration 6 — Predictive biomarker discovery (KEY FINDING).
Tested whether feature_013 or feature_043 modify the effect of feature_038:
  - feature_038 × feature_013: −1.628 (p ~ 0).
  - feature_038 × feature_043: −1.457 (p ~ 0).
  - feature_013 × feature_043: +0.502 (p = 1e−175): the two biomarkers'
    adverse effects are sub-additive (overlap), suggesting shared biology.
Stratified mean PFS gain from feature_038:
  - feature_013 = 0 AND feature_043 = 0: +2.520 mo (p ~ 0)
  - feature_013 = 0 AND feature_043 = 1: +0.071 mo (NS)
  - feature_013 = 1 AND feature_043 = 0: +0.061 mo (NS)
  - feature_013 = 1 AND feature_043 = 1: −0.040 mo (NS)
The benefit is essentially confined to the biomarker-wild-type stratum
(n = 18,853; 38% of the cohort).

Iteration 7 — Demographic effect on treatment within WT stratum.
Within the WT subgroup, feature_038 prevalence was nearly identical across
race (19.0–21.8%) and insurance (19.6–20.3%) categories. Joint F-test for
feature_038 × race interaction: p = 0.33 (not significant). For
feature_038 × insurance: p = 0.0095, driven by a single contrast
(uninsured −0.353 mo vs medicaid reference, p = 0.002). Crude WT effects by
insurance ranged +2.37 to +2.77, so the absolute heterogeneity is small.
There is no evidence that demographic group affects who benefits from
feature_038 once biomarker status is fixed.

Iteration 8 — Clinical effect modifiers within WT stratum.
  - feature_038 × feature_057: small attenuation at PS = 1 (−0.10) and PS = 2
    (−0.13), both p < 0.05; crude benefit nevertheless +2.45 to +2.53 across
    all PS levels.
  - feature_038 × feature_051: +0.155 mo (p = 1e−4): slightly larger benefit
    in feature_051 = 1 patients.
  - feature_038 × feature_078: +0.006 mo per unit (p = 0.003): benefit grows
    modestly with feature_078.
None of these modifiers approach the magnitude of the biomarker gating.

Iteration 9 — Nonlinearity.
  - feature_078: weakly convex (quadratic coef +0.00066, p = 1e−113); cubic
    further significant. ΔAIC ~500 vs linear; substantively the relationship
    is near-linear.
  - feature_009: log1p transform gave a much better fit (AIC dropped by 855;
    log coef −0.152, p = 2e−279). The original variable's heavy tail was the
    issue.
  - feature_099: log1p also yielded a stronger, better-fitting coefficient
    (−0.301, p ~ 0).

Iteration 10 — Integrated final model.
Final OLS model:
  pfs_months ~ feature_078 + feature_078^2 + C(feature_057) + feature_051
             + feature_092 + log_f099 + log_f009 + feature_006 + feature_109
             + feature_067 + feature_013 + feature_043
             + feature_038*feature_013 + feature_038*feature_043
R^2 = 0.8954, AIC = 112,038. The no-interaction comparator gave R^2 = 0.8609
(AIC 126,284); the biomarker interaction added 3.5 percentage points of
explained variance and ~14,000 AIC points of fit.

Critical structural finding: when the interaction terms are included, the
main effects of feature_013 (−0.029, p = 2e−4, mostly noise) and feature_043
(−0.0003, p = 0.96) collapse to near-zero. Their apparent prognostic effects
in iteration 5 were almost entirely confounded with their role as effect
modifiers of feature_038. They are predictive biomarkers, not prognostic
ones.

Predicted feature_038 effect by biomarker stratum (from final model):
  - feature_013 = 0, feature_043 = 0: +2.139 mo (benefit)
  - feature_013 = 1, feature_043 = 0: +0.504 mo (modest)
  - feature_013 = 0, feature_043 = 1: +0.673 mo (modest)
  - feature_013 = 1, feature_043 = 1: −0.962 mo (apparent harm)

HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED VS REFUTED
-------------------------------
SUPPORTED:
  - feature_078, feature_057, feature_051, feature_038, feature_092,
    feature_099 are independent predictors of pfs_months (iter 1, 2).
  - feature_009 (heavy-tailed) and feature_006 add independent signal beyond
    the top six (iter 4).
  - feature_013, feature_043, feature_109, feature_067 are independent
    binary predictors at Bonferroni significance (iter 5).
  - feature_013 and feature_043 are strong negative effect modifiers of
    feature_038 (iter 6).
  - feature_038's benefit is concentrated in the double-negative biomarker
    stratum and is approximately zero or slightly negative elsewhere
    (iter 6, 10).
  - feature_013 and feature_043 are predictive — not prognostic — biomarkers
    (iter 10).
  - The effect of feature_009 is better modeled on the log scale (iter 9).
  - feature_038 benefit is similar across race in the WT stratum (iter 7).

REFUTED OR NULL:
  - Race (feature_064) is associated with pfs_months (no): null both
    marginally and after adjustment (iter 3).
  - Insurance (feature_018) is associated with pfs_months (no): null
    marginally and after adjustment (iter 3); a borderline p = 0.0095 for
    feature_038 × insurance interaction was driven by a single small contrast
    and is not robust evidence of demographic-driven heterogeneity (iter 7).
  - feature_038 × feature_051 main interaction (iter 2 outright; iter 8
    showed a small +0.155 effect within the WT stratum only).

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. Six clinical features (feature_078, feature_057, feature_051, feature_092,
   log-transformed feature_099, log-transformed feature_009) and a small
   set of binary covariates (feature_006, feature_109, feature_067) explain
   the bulk of pfs_months variation. feature_078 alone explains nearly 60%.

2. feature_038 has the structural signature of a targeted oncology therapy:
   modest average benefit (+0.97 mo crude) that masks dramatic heterogeneity.
   Its benefit is essentially confined to the ~38% of patients who are
   wild-type for both feature_013 and feature_043, where it adds ~+2.1
   PFS-months. In double-positive patients it appears modestly harmful.

3. feature_013 and feature_043 are best understood as predictive biomarkers
   for feature_038 rather than independent prognostic factors. Their adjusted
   main effects collapse to ~0 once the predictive-biomarker interaction is
   modeled.

4. Demographic factors (race, insurance) show no meaningful main or
   modifying effect on pfs_months in this cohort, including no differential
   benefit from feature_038 across groups. Within the biomarker-WT stratum,
   feature_038 prevalence is also balanced across race and insurance.

5. The biomarker-treatment pattern observed here — two binary mutations
   that abolish benefit from a putative therapy — is consistent with
   colorectal-cancer pharmacology in which RAS-pathway mutations predict
   resistance to anti-EGFR antibodies, although the de-identified labels in
   this dataset preclude any direct mapping.
