ds001_breast cohort — analysis summary
========================================

Cohort: 50,000 patient records, single PFS-style outcome (pfs_months, mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0–16.7). Although labeled "breast", only 44.7% of records have sex_female=1 and the variable set includes lung-cancer-typical alterations (met_exon14_skipping, ros1_fusion, ret_fusion, etc.); the analysis below treats every column at face value.

I ran 10 iterations of propose-test-refine. The findings cluster into five themes.

────────────────────────────────────────
1. Strong, robust prognostic main effects
────────────────────────────────────────
The largest signals on pfs_months are clinical/prognostic, not therapeutic. In a multivariable OLS (R² = 0.81 with 18 covariates; R² = 0.85 with the parsimonious model from iteration 10) every one of the following had p < 1e-200:

  • age_years          coef +0.176 mo / yr   (longer PFS at older age — see §5)
  • ECOG = 1 vs 0       coef -1.19 mo
  • ECOG = 2 vs 0       coef -2.34 mo
  • stage_iv            coef -1.55 mo  (mean 3.62 vs 5.15 mo unadjusted)
  • has_brain_mets      coef -0.98 mo  (mean 3.75 vs 4.80 mo unadjusted)
  • her2_positive       coef -0.47 mo
  • pik3ca_mutation     coef -0.58 mo  (unadjusted; main effect collapses to ~0 once the palbociclib×ER×PIK3CA interaction is included — see §3)
  • ki67_pct            coef -0.019 mo / %
  • albumin_g_dl        coef +0.48 mo / g·dL⁻¹
  • weight_loss_pct_6mo coef -0.076 mo / %

LDH, alkaline phosphatase, total bilirubin, and calcium showed nominally significant but tiny correlations (|r| < 0.02). CRP, NLR, hemoglobin, AST, ALT, sodium, glucose, BMI, INR, SpO₂, and tumor_size_cm showed no detectable association. Symptom grades (fatigue, pain, dyspnea, cough, appetite_loss) all had |r| < 0.005 and p > 0.30.

────────────────────────────────────────
2. Treatment main effects are mostly null
────────────────────────────────────────
On unadjusted Welch t-tests of pfs_months by treatment indicator:

  • treatment_palbociclib   +1.10 mo (p ≪ 1e-300) — strong, but driven entirely by ER+/PIK3CA-WT subgroup (§3)
  • treatment_tamoxifen     +0.04 mo (p = 0.077) — null
  • treatment_trastuzumab   −0.06 mo (p = 0.029) — small negative, attenuated to null after adjustment
  • treatment_olaparib      −0.04 mo (p = 0.33)  — null overall, positive in BRCA carriers (§4)
  • treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan −0.01 mo (p = 0.85) — null
  • treatment_pembrolizumab −0.07 mo (p = 0.018) — small negative, attenuated to null after adjustment

After multivariable adjustment, every treatment except palbociclib (in the ER+/PIK3CA-WT cell) is statistically indistinguishable from no effect.

────────────────────────────────────────
3. Headline finding: palbociclib × ER × PIK3CA
────────────────────────────────────────
Palbociclib's main-effect benefit is driven by a single subgroup. In stratified analysis:

           ER status   PIK3CA  n(palbo)  PFS palbo   n(no)    PFS no   Δ
           ER+        WT       8,034     6.68        14,754   4.31    +2.38**
           ER+        MUT      4,160     4.37         7,938   4.33    +0.04 ns
           ER−        WT       3,371     4.27         6,476   4.31    -0.05 ns
           ER−        MUT      1,863     4.32         3,404   4.28    +0.04 ns
                                                                      ** p ≪ 1e-300

  • Two-way: treatment_palbociclib × er_positive coef +1.59, p = 9.3e-229.
  • Three-way: treatment_palbociclib × er_positive × pik3ca_mutation coef -2.43, p = 1.6e-128.
  • Within the ER+ subgroup, treatment_palbociclib × pik3ca_mutation coef -2.34, p ≪ 1e-300.

The signal aligns with real-world oncology: CDK4/6 inhibitors are licensed for HR+/HER2− disease and PIK3CA mutations are an established mechanism of CDK4/6-resistance. Within ER+/PIK3CA-WT, palbociclib lengthens PFS by ~2.4 months; outside this stratum the effect is essentially zero. There was no further heterogeneity by stage_iv (interaction p = 0.37), age_years (p = 0.24), or postmenopausal status (interaction was nominally significant, p = 0.004, but the effect size of −0.13 mo is small and is likely confounded with ER-status overlap).

────────────────────────────────────────
4. Olaparib × BRCA: a weaker but clinically congruent signal
────────────────────────────────────────
  • brca_any (= BRCA1 OR BRCA2) × treatment_olaparib interaction coef +0.40, p = 0.021.
  • In BRCA carriers (n = 2,499): olaparib +0.35 mo (p = 0.041).
  • In non-carriers (n = 47,501): olaparib −0.06 mo (p = 0.14).
  • BRCA1-only and BRCA2-only interactions trend positive (+0.45 / +0.34) but are individually not significant due to small subgroup sizes (n with BRCA1 mutation = 1,277; BRCA2 = 1,222).

Direction and magnitude are consistent with the known PARP-inhibitor activity in BRCA-altered tumors, but the effect is much weaker than palbociclib×ER and is borderline at the 0.05 level.

────────────────────────────────────────
5. Negative findings worth noting
────────────────────────────────────────
The cohort showed no detectable signal for the following — counter to clinical priors but consistent across both univariate and adjusted analyses:

  • treatment_trastuzumab × her2_positive — interaction p = 0.70 (no preferential benefit in HER2+).
  • treatment_pembrolizumab × msi_high — interaction p = 0.47.
  • treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan × her2_low — interaction p = 0.63.
  • treatment_tamoxifen × er_positive — interaction p = 0.27.
  • Race/ethnicity (5-level): ANOVA p = 0.49.
  • Insurance type (4-level): ANOVA p = 0.82.
  • Rural residence: p = 0.34.
  • Education years, smoking pack-years: p > 0.38.
  • All 14 binary comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, CKD, heart failure, CAD, AFib, VTE, autoimmune, hep B/C, HIV, prior malignancy, depression/anxiety, ILD): p > 0.16.
  • Metastatic site indicators except brain mets (liver, bone, adrenal, pleural, pericardial, contralateral lung): p > 0.16.
  • Of 24 SNPs, only snp_rs1801131 reached uncorrected p < 0.05 (p = 0.026, Δ = −0.054 mo); none survived Bonferroni at 0.0021. No reproducible germline-genotype signal.
  • A systematic screen of all 6 treatments × 13 binary biomarkers (78 pairs) yielded only the palbociclib×ER and palbociclib×PIK3CA (within ER+) interactions at p < 0.01.

────────────────────────────────────────
6. The age effect is unusual and warrants a note
────────────────────────────────────────
age_years correlates with pfs_months at r = +0.70 (univariate) and remains +0.176 mo / yr after adjustment for ECOG, stage, brain mets, ki67, albumin, weight loss, PIK3CA, and palbociclib×ER. The mean PFS by age decade is essentially monotonic: <40 → 0.56 mo, 40–50 → 1.52, 50–60 → 3.06, 60–70 → 4.67, 70–80 → 6.32, 80+ → 8.20. This direction is opposite the usual clinical prior that older age confers worse PFS. In an actual EHR cohort one would suspect (a) selection — younger patients enrolled having more aggressive disease, (b) immortal-time bias, or (c) an artifact of how the synthetic dataset was generated. The signal is too large to ignore and too consistent across the model to be confounding by the measured covariates; readers should interpret it accordingly.

────────────────────────────────────────
Bottom line
────────────────────────────────────────
The dominant story of this 50K-patient cohort is:

  (1) Standard prognostic features (age, ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, albumin, weight loss, Ki67, HER2 status, PIK3CA status) explain the large majority of variance in pfs_months — a 9-feature parsimonious model alone yields R² ≈ 0.80, and adding the palbociclib×ER×PIK3CA term pushes R² to 0.85.
  (2) Of the six therapies, only palbociclib produced a clinically meaningful PFS gain, and only in ER-positive, PIK3CA-wild-type patients (~+2.4 months). Olaparib showed a weaker but congruent benefit in BRCA carriers.
  (3) Trastuzumab×HER2+, pembrolizumab×MSI-high, sacituzumab×HER2-low, and tamoxifen×ER+ — all clinically expected interactions — were not detectable in this cohort.
  (4) Race/ethnicity, insurance, rural residence, education, smoking, comorbidities, metastatic-site indicators (other than brain), symptom grades, and all 24 SNP genotypes had no detectable association with pfs_months.
  (5) A counterintuitive strong positive age→PFS association is the most striking single feature of the data and probably reflects the construction of the dataset rather than a clinical effect.
