Analysis summary for ds001_breast (N=50,000; outcome = pfs_months)
=================================================================

Across 10 iterations I examined treatment main effects, biomarker prognostics,
treatment-by-biomarker interactions, lab and clinical prognostics,
demographics/equity, candidate SNPs, comorbidities/symptoms, and follow-up
interactions. Below I walk through what was found, with directions, magnitudes,
and significance, and which hypotheses the data support or refute.

Iteration 1 — Treatment main effects on PFS
-------------------------------------------
Unadjusted:
  - palbociclib: +1.10 mo, p<1e-300 (large positive).
  - trastuzumab: -0.06 mo, p=0.029 (mildly negative).
  - pembrolizumab: -0.07 mo, p=0.018 (mildly negative).
  - tamoxifen: +0.04 mo, p=0.077 (n.s.).
  - olaparib: -0.04 mo, p=0.33 (n.s.).
  - sacituzumab_govitecan: -0.01 mo, p=0.85 (n.s.).
Adjusted (mutually + age, ECOG, stage_iv, brain mets, tumor size, Ki67):
palbociclib coef +1.10 (p<1e-300) is the only sizeable positive treatment
signal. pembrolizumab adjusted coef -0.033 (p=0.038) and olaparib -0.034
(p=0.074) trend negative; tamoxifen, trastuzumab, sacituzumab are flat.
Supported: h1.1 (palbociclib helps). Refuted in main-effect form: h1.2-h1.6
(no positive main effect for the other agents).

Iteration 2 — Biomarker prognostics for PFS
-------------------------------------------
Strongly significant (adjusted): er_positive +0.54 mo (p=3e-211, longer PFS),
her2_positive -0.46 mo (p=1e-171, shorter), pik3ca_mutation -0.59 mo (p<1e-300,
shorter). HER2-low +0.11 unadjusted (p=1e-6) but loses signal after
adjustment. PR+, BRCA1/2, TP53, PTEN, MSI-H, node+, postmenopausal show no
independent prognostic value once ER, HER2, PIK3CA, age, ECOG, stage and
brain-mets are in the model.
Supported: h2.1, h2.2, h2.3. Refuted: h2.4 (BRCA), h2.6 (node+).
h2.5 (HER2-low) supported only marginally.

Iteration 3 — Canonical treatment x biomarker interactions
----------------------------------------------------------
This was the most informative iteration. Tested the textbook breast-cancer
pairings:
  - tamoxifen x ER+: interaction -0.01, p=0.77. NO evidence tamoxifen
    differentially benefits ER+ patients. Refuted.
  - trastuzumab x HER2+: interaction +0.01, p=0.78. NO evidence trastuzumab
    differentially benefits HER2+ patients. Refuted.
  - olaparib x BRCA1/2: interaction +0.14, p=0.16. Direction-consistent with
    PARP-inhibitor mechanism but NOT statistically significant.
  - palbociclib x ER+/HER2-: interaction +1.93, p<1e-300. STRONGLY SUPPORTED.
    Palbociclib's ~+1 month main effect is essentially confined to the
    ER+/HER2- subgroup, mirroring the canonical CDK4/6 indication.
  - pembrolizumab x MSI-H: interaction +0.18, p=0.29. Direction-consistent but
    underpowered (only 498 MSI-H patients).
  - sacituzumab x triple-negative: interaction -0.03, p=0.52. NO differential
    benefit in TNBC.
Net: only the palbociclib x ER+/HER2- pairing matches the textbook biology in
both direction and significance; the other classical pairings are either not
present in the data or too weak to detect.

Iteration 4 — Continuous lab prognostics
----------------------------------------
Albumin (r=+0.10, p=9e-112) and weight loss% over 6 mo (r=-0.12, p=4e-148)
have the largest effect sizes and confirm classic frailty/cachexia
relationships. LDH is weakly negative (r=-0.016, p=2e-4). NLR, CRP, CEA, CA-125,
hemoglobin show no association. h4.1 and h4.2 supported; h4.3 supported with
small effect; h4.4, h4.5 refuted.

Iteration 5 — Clinical-state prognostics
----------------------------------------
ECOG (-1.17/unit), stage_iv (-1.55), brain mets (-0.99), weight loss
(-0.077/%), albumin (+0.48/g/dL) and LDH (-0.0006/U) are independently
significant in a multivariable model. Liver and bone mets do NOT add
information beyond stage_iv and ECOG. ECOG mean PFS: 0=5.64, 1=4.44, 2=3.29
(monotonic, dose-response).
Surprise (refutes h5.6): age_years has a POSITIVE adjusted coefficient
(+0.176 per year, p<1e-300). After controlling for ECOG/stage/etc., older
patients have LONGER PFS in this dataset, which is the opposite of typical
clinical experience. This is suspicious and worth noting as a potential
data-generation artifact.

Iteration 6 — Demographics & equity
-----------------------------------
Race (ANOVA p=0.49), insurance type (ANOVA p=0.82) and rural residence (t-test
p=0.34) show NO unadjusted differences in PFS. After adjustment for age, ECOG,
stage and brain mets, all race and insurance dummies have p>0.3. There is no
PFS disparity across these axes in this cohort. h6.1-h6.4 refuted.

Iteration 7 — Candidate SNPs
----------------------------
Twenty-four SNPs screened against PFS by Pearson correlation. Best uncorrected
p-value is snp_rs1801131 at p=0.026 (r=-0.010). Bonferroni threshold is 0.00208;
zero SNPs pass it. h7.1 refuted; h7.2 nominally supported but the effect is
trivial (~0.01 SD) and not credible at 24 tests.

Iteration 8 — Race x treatment interactions (equity)
----------------------------------------------------
All six tested treatment-by-black-race interaction coefficients are non-
significant (smallest p=0.25). Treatment receipt rates are essentially uniform
across race_ethnicity groups (e.g., palbociclib 0.347-0.352 across all 5
groups; pembrolizumab 0.145-0.161; trastuzumab 0.189-0.203). No equity gaps
in either treatment delivery or treatment effect were detectable. h8.1-h8.5
refuted.

Iteration 9 — Comorbidities & symptoms
--------------------------------------
None of 12 comorbidities (DM, HTN, COPD, CKD, HF, CAD, AF, VTE, autoimmune,
prior malignancy, depression/anxiety, ILD) show a significant unadjusted PFS
association (all |diff|<0.05 mo, p>0.15). None of 5 symptom grades (fatigue,
pain NRS, dyspnea, cough, appetite loss) correlate with PFS (all |r|<0.005,
p>0.29). After adjustment for age/ECOG/stage, only dyspnea_grade reaches
borderline nominal significance (p=0.048) but in the wrong (positive)
direction. Practically, comorbidities and symptoms add no PFS signal beyond
ECOG. h9.1, h9.2, h9.3 refuted.

Iteration 10 — Refined interaction probes
-----------------------------------------
- palbociclib x PIK3CA mutation: STRONG NEGATIVE interaction -1.66
  (p<1e-300). Palbociclib coefficient is +1.68 in PIK3CA-wild-type patients
  but the ~+1.7 benefit is essentially eliminated when PIK3CA is mutated.
  This is the second-most striking interaction in the entire analysis and is
  the mechanistic explanation for the absence of palbociclib benefit in the
  PIK3CA-mut subgroup. h10.1 strongly supported.
- pembrolizumab x triple-negative: interaction +0.073, p=0.092 (trend in
  predicted direction but n.s.). h10.2 not statistically supported.
- olaparib x no_brca: interaction -0.19, p=0.083; olaparib coef in BRCA
  carriers +0.15 (p=0.16). Direction matches the hypothesis that olaparib
  benefit is restricted to BRCA carriers, but neither term is significant. h10.3
  weakly/inconclusively supported.
- trastuzumab x HER2-low: interaction +0.008, p=0.84. Refuted.
- palbociclib x postmenopausal: interaction -0.069, p=0.033 — significant in
  the OPPOSITE direction; postmenopausal patients gain slightly LESS from
  palbociclib. h10.5 refuted.
- pembrolizumab x brain mets: interaction -0.03, p=0.58. Refuted.
- sex_female differences: cohort is only 44.7% female despite the "breast"
  label; PFS does not differ by sex (p=0.56) and treatment receipt is
  essentially identical between sexes for all six agents. The cohort does not
  appear to be exclusively breast-cancer patients.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. The dominant treatment story in this dataset is CDK4/6 inhibition:
   palbociclib has a large +1.1-month adjusted PFS effect that is
   biologically targeted — it is concentrated in ER+/HER2- patients
   (interaction +1.93) and is abrogated by PIK3CA mutation (interaction
   -1.66). These two interactions mirror well-known clinical findings.
2. The other "canonical" pairings expected for breast cancer were NOT
   recapitulated:
     - trastuzumab does NOT show a differential benefit in HER2+ patients;
     - tamoxifen does NOT show a differential benefit in ER+ patients;
     - sacituzumab does NOT show a differential benefit in TNBC;
     - pembrolizumab does NOT show a significant MSI-H, TNBC, or brain-mets
       interaction;
     - olaparib's BRCA interaction is direction-consistent but underpowered.
   The trastuzumab/tamoxifen results in particular are striking departures
   from real-world breast-cancer outcomes data.
3. Strong prognostic signal is concentrated in ECOG, stage_iv, brain mets,
   weight loss, albumin, LDH, ER+, HER2+, and PIK3CA. Surprisingly age has a
   POSITIVE adjusted coefficient on PFS (older = longer PFS), which is
   opposite to typical clinical patterns and may reflect a feature of the
   data-generation process rather than biology.
4. There are no detectable PFS disparities by race, insurance type, or rural
   residence in this cohort, and treatment receipt is essentially balanced
   across these strata.
5. Comorbidities, symptom grades, and the 24 candidate SNPs add essentially
   no incremental PFS information beyond ECOG/stage/labs.
6. The cohort is labeled "breast" but is 44.7% female, includes biomarkers
   not relevant to breast cancer (ROS1 fusion, MET exon 14 skipping, NTRK
   fusion, NRG1 fusion, etc.), and shows balanced treatment receipt by sex —
   suggesting the dataset is broader than a pure breast-cancer population or
   that disease coding does not constrain feature realism.

The transcript records 10 iterations, 50 hypotheses, and 49 analyses. The
analyses that received the strongest support were the palbociclib x ER+/HER2-
and palbociclib x PIK3CA interactions; the analyses that most clearly refuted
their hypotheses were the trastuzumab x HER2+ and tamoxifen x ER+
interactions and the equity-related comparisons.
