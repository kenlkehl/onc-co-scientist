Analysis summary — ds001_breast (n=50,000; outcome pfs_months)
================================================================

Approach
--------
Across 10 iterations I (1) screened main effects of the six labeled
treatments, (2) tested the canonical biomarker x treatment interactions
implied by breast-cancer pharmacology (tamoxifen x ER, trastuzumab x HER2,
palbociclib x HR/HER2, olaparib x BRCA, sacituzumab govitecan x TNBC and
HER2-low, pembrolizumab x MSI-H/TNBC), (3) characterized prognostic
clinical, laboratory and demographic features, (4) refined the strongest
signals with subgroup and three-way analyses, (5) screened 24 SNPs and
patient socio-demographics, and (6) confirmed the surviving signals in a
multivariable linear model adjusting for age, ECOG, stage, brain mets,
albumin, LDH and weight loss along with the key treatment-by-biomarker
interactions.

Headline findings
-----------------

1. Palbociclib has a large PFS benefit, but only in the canonical
   sub-population, and PIK3CA mutation abolishes it.
   - Crude main effect: +1.10 mo (p<<1e-300).
   - Restricted to HR-positive (ER+ or PR+) and HER2-negative patients,
     palbociclib added +1.81 mo (5.41 vs 4.31, p<<1e-300, n_treated≈10,600);
     the effect is essentially zero outside that subset (-0.009 mo,
     p=0.78).
   - PIK3CA mutation is itself adverse (-0.56 mo) and a strong negative
     modifier of palbociclib: in HR+/HER2- AND PIK3CA wild-type patients
     palbociclib added +2.74 mo (n_treated≈7,000), whereas in HR+/HER2-
     AND PIK3CA-mutant patients it added only +0.015 mo (p=0.73). The
     three-way palbociclib x HR+/HER2- x PIK3CA interaction
     coefficient is -2.85 (p<<1e-300) and persists in the full
     multivariable model (R²=0.835).

2. Olaparib shows a modest BRCA-restricted signal that does not
   survive adjustment.
   - Crude pooled BRCA1/2 carriers (n=2,499): +0.35 mo on olaparib
     (p=0.041), with the interaction coefficient olaparib x BRCA = +0.40
     (p=0.021). Split by gene: BRCA1+ +0.40 mo (p=0.12), BRCA2+ +0.30 mo
     (p=0.19). After multivariable adjustment the interaction collapses
     (β=-0.02, p=0.78), so this is at best a weak/inconclusive signal.

3. Pembrolizumab has a small overall negative association with PFS that
   is partially offset in triple-negative disease.
   - Main effect: -0.07 mo (p=0.018).
   - Pembrolizumab x TNBC interaction is positive and significant:
     +0.19 mo crude (p=0.015), +0.10 mo adjusted (p=0.001). Stratified,
     this is driven mainly by a worse pembrolizumab effect in non-TNBC
     patients (-0.11 mo, p=0.002) rather than a clear gain in TNBC
     (+0.07 mo, p=0.22). No interaction with MSI-high or stage IV.

4. Trastuzumab, tamoxifen, and sacituzumab govitecan show no PFS
   benefit in this dataset, even within their target subgroups.
   - Trastuzumab in HER2-positive: -0.08 mo (p=0.15);
     trastuzumab x HER2_amplification interaction n.s. (p=0.81).
   - Tamoxifen overall +0.04 mo (p=0.077); within ER-positive +0.02 mo
     (p=0.45); even ER+/premenopausal +0.04 mo (p=0.38).
   - Sacituzumab govitecan: no main effect (-0.01 mo, p=0.85), no
     interaction with TNBC, HR-negative, or HER2-low.

5. Strongly prognostic clinical/laboratory features (independent of
   treatment in the multivariable model):
   - Stage IV: -1.55 mo
   - ECOG performance status: -1.18 mo per point
   - Brain mets: -0.97 mo
   - Albumin: +0.48 mo per g/dL
   - Weight loss in 6 mo: -0.08 mo per percentage point
   - LDH: -5e-4 mo per U/L (small but highly significant)
   - ER+: +0.22 mo (adjusted); HER2+: n.s. after adjustment;
     PR+: -0.13 mo after adjusting for ER (the simple main effects of
     ER+ and HER2+ on PFS are +0.57 and -0.46 mo respectively).
   - Age: +0.18 mo per year — older patients have LONGER PFS,
     opposite to the usual prognostic direction.

6. Surprising null prognostic features:
   - liver_mets, bone_mets, node_positive, postmenopausal, sex_female,
     CRP, NLR, hemoglobin, all comorbidities (CKD, HF, ILD, COPD, DM,
     autoimmune disease, HIV), and all symptom grades (fatigue, pain,
     dyspnea, cough, appetite loss) showed |effect|<0.1 mo and p>0.15.

7. SNPs and social determinants are essentially uninformative.
   - Of 24 candidate SNPs, only rs1801131 reached p<0.05 (-0.054 mo,
     p=0.025) — well above any reasonable multiple-testing threshold.
   - race_ethnicity (5 levels), insurance_type, rural_residence,
     education_years, smoking_pack_years, prior_lines_of_therapy: all
     n.s. with point estimates within ±0.05 mo.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
Supported:
  - Palbociclib improves PFS overall and disproportionately in HR+/HER2-
    patients (h1.2, h2.3, h5.1/h5.2, h7.1).
  - PIK3CA mutation is adverse and abolishes palbociclib benefit
    (h6.6, h6.7, h7.2/h7.3, h9.2).
  - Stage IV, brain metastases, ECOG PS, weight loss, low albumin, LDH,
    alkaline phosphatase are independent adverse prognostic markers
    (h3.1-3.3, h4.1-2, h4.5-6, h9.5).
  - ER-positive patients have longer PFS; HER2-positive shorter (h4.7,
    h4.8).
  - Pembrolizumab x TNBC interaction is mildly positive (h6.3, h9.4).

Refuted or unsupported:
  - Tamoxifen x ER+ benefit (h2.1, h5.5, h7.5).
  - Trastuzumab x HER2+ benefit (h2.2, h5.3, h6.5).
  - Sacituzumab govitecan x TNBC, HR-negative, or HER2-low benefit
    (h2.5, h6.1, h6.2).
  - Pembrolizumab x MSI-high or x stage_iv interaction (h2.6, h6.4).
  - Olaparib x BRCA benefit after multivariable adjustment (h2.4, h5.4,
    h9.3, h10.3).
  - Liver mets, bone mets, node positivity, postmenopausal status,
    sex_female, comorbidity burden, symptom grades, prior lines of
    therapy as PFS prognosticators (h3.4-6, h4.9-10, h10.1-2, h10.5-6).
  - SNPs, race/ethnicity, insurance, rurality, education, smoking
    (h8.1-6).

Overall conclusion
------------------
In this 50,000-patient labeled-as-breast cohort, the dominant
treatment-biomarker signal is palbociclib's PFS benefit confined to
HR-positive / HER2-negative / PIK3CA wild-type tumors — a clinically
plausible pattern given PIK3CA-driven resistance to CDK4/6 inhibition.
The conventional companion-biomarker pairs for tamoxifen-ER+,
trastuzumab-HER2+ and sacituzumab govitecan-TNBC are NOT recapitulated:
each of those treatments shows essentially flat PFS curves in their
target populations. Olaparib in BRCA carriers shows only a weak,
multivariable-fragile signal. Pembrolizumab is mildly negative overall
with a small offset in TNBC. The strongest PFS predictors are classical
clinical-prognostic features (stage, ECOG, brain mets, weight loss,
albumin), with the notable anomalies that age is positively associated
with PFS and that several "expected" prognostic features (liver/bone
mets, node status, comorbidities, symptom grades) and the entire SNP
panel and socio-demographic block are uninformative. The cohort is also
only 44.7% female despite the breast-cancer labeling, with no PFS
difference between male and female patients and identical treatment
receipt rates — an unusual feature worth flagging.
