ds001_breast — Analysis Summary
==================================

Cohort: 50,000 de-identified breast-oncology patient records.
Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival in months; mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0–16.66).
Iterations completed: 10 of 10 allowed.


1. Main effects of binary features (Iteration 1)
------------------------------------------------
Seven binary indicators showed extremely strong univariate associations with pfs_months
(all p < 1e-50 by two-sample t-test):

   feature_042=1: +1.10 mo (longer)        n=17,428 vs 32,572
   feature_056=1: -1.54 mo (shorter)       n=14,969 vs 35,031
   feature_111=1: +0.57 mo                 n=34,886 vs 15,114
   feature_015=1: -0.56 mo                 n=17,365 vs 32,635
   feature_048=1: -1.05 mo                 n= 5,090 vs 44,910
   feature_040=1: -0.46 mo                 n= 9,060 vs 40,940
   feature_039=1: +0.36 mo                 n=31,954 vs 18,046

All hypotheses were supported: each binary feature's mean PFS difference reached
overwhelming significance with the predicted direction.


2. Continuous and categorical predictors (Iteration 2)
------------------------------------------------------
   feature_080 (range 30–90, mean 65; plausibly age in years):
       Pearson r = +0.7015 (p ≈ 0).  This is by far the strongest single predictor
       in the dataset and explains ~49% of pfs variance on its own. Mean PFS rises
       monotonically across quartiles: Q1 2.50 → Q4 6.96 mo.

   feature_067 (continuous, 0–24.6): r = -0.116, p ≈ 4e-148.
   feature_101 (continuous, 1–100):  r = -0.091, p ≈ 3e-93.
   feature_019 (39 distinct levels, 1.5–5.5): r = +0.100, p ≈ 9e-112.
   feature_063 (3-level categorical, likely an ordinal stage/grade variable):
       monotonic decline 5.64 → 4.44 → 3.29 mo across levels 0/1/2.

   Demographic features showed NO detectable association with PFS:
       feature_011 (race: white/hispanic/black/asian/other): ANOVA p = 0.49.
       feature_089 (insurance: medicare/private/medicaid/uninsured): ANOVA p = 0.82.
   Hypothesized associations for race and insurance were refuted.


3. Adjusted multivariable model (Iterations 3, 9)
-------------------------------------------------
A linear OLS model regressing pfs_months on the leading 13 features achieved R² = 0.792.
After adjustment, feature_039 (+0.014, p=0.32) and feature_034 (+0.002, p=0.87)
attenuated to null — their univariate signals were absorbed by correlated covariates.
All other top features retained large, signed effects matching their univariate direction.

Adding the three-way feature_042 × feature_111 × feature_015 interaction (see Section 5)
raised R² to 0.849, indicating the interaction structure is the largest single source
of variance not explained by additive main effects.


4. Stage-modification analyses (Iteration 4)
--------------------------------------------
Tested whether the feature_042 (treatment-like) effect varies by feature_063 (stage-like).
   Level 0: +1.115 mo    Level 1: +1.093 mo    Level 2: +1.149 mo
Interaction terms were non-significant (p ≈ 0.6); feature_042 acts approximately
additively across feature_063. Same conclusion for feature_111 × feature_063.
Hypotheses h16 and h17 were refuted.


5. Predictive biomarker structure (Iterations 5, 6, 8, 10) — KEY FINDING
------------------------------------------------------------------------
The single most important pattern in the cohort is a three-way interaction
feature_042 × feature_111 × feature_015:

   feature_042 has essentially no main effect when broken down properly.
   feature_111 has essentially no main effect when broken down properly.
   feature_015 has essentially no main effect when broken down properly.

   The benefit appears almost entirely in the cell where feature_042=1, feature_111=1,
   and feature_015=0 (n = 8,034). Mean pfs_months in this cell = 6.68;
   all other seven cells of the 2×2×2 cube cluster around 4.30–4.36 mo.

   Three-way interaction coefficient = -2.428 mo (p < 1e-100).
   feature_042 × feature_111 (within feature_015=0) = +2.42 mo.
   feature_042 × feature_111 (within feature_015=1) ≈ 0.

Interpretation:
   feature_042 acts as a treatment whose benefit is conditioned on a positive
   predictive biomarker (feature_111) and is abolished by a resistance marker
   (feature_015).

Confirmatory checks (Iteration 10):
   - feature_111 effect among feature_042=0 patients: +0.011 mo, p=0.68 → null.
     Confirms feature_111 is predictive, not prognostic.
   - feature_015 effect among feature_042=0 patients: also null (h34 supported).
   - feature_015 effect within feature_042=1/feature_111=1 stratum: -2.32 mo, p≈0 →
     resistance marker.
   - In the predicted-responder stratum (feature_111=1 & feature_015=0; n=22,788),
     the feature_042 effect is +2.376 mo — roughly double the marginal estimate
     and consistent across feature_063 stage levels (level0 +2.38, level1 +2.39,
     level2 +2.43). Stage does not modify the treatment benefit in responders.

Other pairwise interactions tested (h19, h20):
   feature_042 × feature_056 (additive, interaction p=0.90).
   feature_042 × feature_048 (additive, interaction p=0.59).
   These features act as independent prognostic factors that move PFS regardless
   of treatment assignment.


6. feature_080 (age-like) details (Iteration 7)
-----------------------------------------------
   - Approximately linear: linear-only R²=0.4922 vs quadratic R²=0.4932.
     Quadratic curvature is statistically significant but trivially small.
   - Treatment effect across age tertiles: young +1.14, mid +1.05, old +1.10.
     The 042 × feature_080 interaction is borderline (-0.0032/unit, p=0.044) and
     practically negligible.
   - feature_080 slope is slightly attenuated at feature_063 level 2 (-0.0129/unit
     vs level 0, p=4e-9). A small shrinkage of the per-unit benefit at the highest
     stage; qualitatively the relationship is preserved.


7. Equity check: race/insurance × treatment (Iteration 8)
---------------------------------------------------------
Critical for clinical interpretation: the feature_042 treatment benefit is
indistinguishable across racial and insurance categories.

   Race-stratified treatment effect (mo):
       white +1.107   hispanic +1.115   black +1.060   asian +1.062   other +1.007
       (all interaction terms p > 0.6)

   Insurance-stratified treatment effect (mo):
       medicaid +1.081   private +1.091   medicare +1.110   uninsured +1.097
       (all interaction terms p > 0.68)

   Hypotheses h27 and h28 (effect modification by race or insurance) were refuted.
   This is a reassuring null result for treatment equity in this anonymized cohort.


8. Continuous prognostic biomarkers (Iteration 9)
-------------------------------------------------
Tertile analyses confirmed monotone gradients:
   feature_019: lo 4.43 → mid 4.75 → hi 4.96  (positive, supports h11)
   feature_067: lo 4.97 → mid 4.75 → hi 4.36  (negative, supports h9)
   feature_101: lo 4.96 → mid 4.79 → hi 4.32  (negative, supports h10)
All three retain large, independent coefficients in the multivariable model.


Overall conclusions
===================
1. The cohort has a dominant prognostic axis (feature_080, likely age) explaining
   ~50% of pfs variance on its own.
2. A three-level ordinal feature (feature_063) and several binary features
   (feature_056, feature_048, feature_040; feature_067, feature_101, feature_019
   on the continuous side) act as independent prognostic factors.
3. The most clinically actionable finding is the predictive-biomarker structure:
   feature_042 (treatment-like) confers ~+2.4-month PFS benefit, but only in
   patients who are feature_111-positive AND feature_015-negative. In the
   complementary 55% of the cohort, feature_042 is essentially inert.
4. Race (feature_011) and insurance (feature_089) are unrelated to PFS and do
   not modify the treatment effect — neither prognostically nor predictively.
5. The treatment benefit is uniform across feature_063 stages and across age,
   meaning that within the feature_111+/feature_015− subgroup, all stage
   strata derive a similar absolute PFS gain.

Hypotheses supported: h1–h12, h15, h18, h21, h22, h23, h24, h29, h30, h31, h32, h34
Hypotheses refuted/null: h13 (race main effect), h14 (insurance main effect),
   h16 (feature_042 × feature_063), h17 (feature_111 × feature_063),
   h19 (feature_042 × feature_056), h20 (feature_042 × feature_048),
   h27 (feature_042 × race), h28 (feature_042 × insurance), h33 (feature_111 prognostic)
Hypotheses partially supported: h25 (treatment effect ≈ homogeneous across age,
   trivial interaction), h26 (modest feature_080 × feature_063 attenuation)
