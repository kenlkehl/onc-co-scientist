Oncology Dataset ds001_breast - Analysis Summary
==================================================

Cohort
------
50,000 de-identified patient records with 127 anonymized features (feature_001 ... feature_127) and a single continuous outcome, pfs_months (mean 4.69, sd 2.50, range 0-16.7). Two features are categorical strings (feature_011 with values white/hispanic/black/asian/other; feature_089 with values medicare/private/medicaid/uninsured); the remaining features are numeric (89 binary, ~36 continuous). No missing values.

Approach
--------
Across 10 iterations I (a) ranked features by univariate association with pfs_months, (b) tested the leading candidates singly, (c) confirmed direction and significance in mutually adjusted regression, and (d) probed clinically interesting interactions (treatment-like x severity-like, age-like x stratifier-like) and stratified analyses. Race and insurance category were also tested on the chance that the dataset encodes social-determinant disparities.

Iteration-by-iteration findings
-------------------------------

Iteration 1: Strongest univariate signals.
  - feature_080 (continuous, range 30-90, mean 65; behaves like patient age) shows a remarkably strong positive association with pfs_months: Pearson r = 0.70, slope +0.176 months per unit, p effectively 0. Univariate this single feature explains R^2 = 0.49 of outcome variance.
  - feature_042 (binary, prevalence 35%): mean pfs_months 5.41 vs 4.31, +1.10 month difference (p<<0.001). Behaves like a beneficial intervention/marker.
  - feature_056 (binary, 30%): -1.54 month decrement (p<<0.001). Strongest negative binary signal.
  - feature_048 (binary, 10%): -1.05 month decrement (p=1.5e-178).
  - feature_063 (3-level ordinal): linear dose-response, slope -1.18 per level (level 0 mean 5.64; level 1 mean 4.44; level 2 mean 3.29; ANOVA p<<0.001).
  All five hypotheses supported.

Iteration 2: Secondary binary signals confirmed: feature_111 (+0.57 m, p=3.7e-143), feature_015 (-0.56 m, p=6.1e-144), feature_040 (-0.46 m, p=6.1e-70), feature_039 (+0.36 m, p=5.8e-59).

Iteration 3: Continuous-feature signals confirmed in expected direction. feature_067 (range 0-25): r=-0.116, slope -0.075/unit, p=4e-148. feature_019 (range 1.5-5.5): r=+0.100, slope +0.50/unit, p=9e-112. feature_101 (range 1-100): r=-0.091, slope -0.019/unit, p=3e-93.

Iteration 4: Multivariable OLS with 13 candidate predictors achieves R^2 = 0.7919 (AIC 154,888). All 11 a-priori predictors retain p<1e-30 with adjusted coefficients close to crude estimates (no major confounding among them). feature_039 and feature_034 lose significance after adjustment (p=0.32 and p=0.88 respectively), indicating their univariate associations were confounded by stronger predictors (likely correlated with feature_080 or feature_042/feature_111 etc.).

Iteration 5: Interactions among top binary features.
  - feature_042 x feature_063 (p=0.367): not supported; the +1.10-month feature_042 benefit is consistent across feature_063 levels.
  - feature_080 x feature_056 (p=3.1e-12): supported. The age-like positive slope is +0.180/unit at feature_056=0 vs +0.168/unit at feature_056=1 - i.e. the favorable feature_080 effect is ~6% attenuated in the higher-risk stratum.
  - feature_042 x feature_056 (p=0.901): supported as null - the two effects are essentially additive on the pfs_months scale.

Iteration 6: Race and insurance.
  - feature_011 (race) ANOVA F=0.85, p=0.49. No raw association with pfs_months across white, black, hispanic, asian, other.
  - feature_089 (insurance) ANOVA F=0.31, p=0.82. No raw association.
  - After adjusting for feature_080, feature_063, feature_056, feature_042, race remains null (all category coefficients |estimate| ~0.02 months, all p>0.34). The dataset shows no treatment-outcome disparity along these social-determinant dimensions.

Iteration 7: feature_080 (age-like) x other-feature interactions.
  - feature_080 x feature_063: significant (p=4e-09); per-unit slope is +0.179, +0.176, +0.166 at levels 0/1/2. Mild attenuation as feature_063 worsens.
  - feature_080 x feature_042: borderline (p=0.044, magnitude -0.003). Effectively negligible compared with the +1.10-month main effect.
  - feature_080 x feature_048: significant (p=0.004). Confirms feature_080 effect is weaker among feature_048=1 patients.

Iteration 8: Functional form.
  - Quadratic feature_080 term highly significant (p=6e-25, coefficient +0.0006), with mean PFS by decade 1.43 -> 3.06 -> 4.67 -> 6.32 -> 8.20 (slight positive curvature - increments grow as feature_080 increases).
  - feature_019 monotone increasing across 5 bins (4.20 -> 4.45 -> 4.72 -> 4.90 -> 5.16).
  - feature_067 monotone decreasing across 5 bins (4.97 -> 4.78 -> 4.70 -> 4.48 -> 4.00).
  All three supported - linear approximations are reasonable, with mild useful curvature for feature_080.

Iteration 9: Stratified by feature_056.
  In both feature_056=0 and feature_056=1 strata, all 6 tested covariates retain identical signs and similar magnitudes. The feature_056 x feature_063 interaction is small but significant (+0.0611, p=0.003), indicating a slight floor effect: per-level feature_063 decrements are ~5% smaller in absolute terms among the higher-risk feature_056=1 group.

Iteration 10: Composite model and residual diagnostics.
  - 11-predictor composite OLS achieves R^2 = 0.7919.
  - Residual screen across remaining ~117 features: top residual-correlated feature is feature_053 (effect -0.037, p=1.9e-16); also weak signals for feature_126 (binary, effect -0.18, p=0.002), feature_037, feature_093, feature_017. None large enough to substantially shift the model.

Synthesis: which hypotheses were supported vs refuted
------------------------------------------------------
Supported:
  - feature_080 strongly positively associated with pfs_months, with mild positive curvature.
  - feature_042 binary associated with +1.1 m benefit, additive with feature_056.
  - feature_063 ordinal monotonic dose-response (each level approximately -1.2 m).
  - feature_056, feature_048, feature_015, feature_040, feature_067, feature_101 all confer shorter PFS (in expected directions).
  - feature_111, feature_019, feature_039 confer longer PFS.
  - 11-predictor multivariable model explains ~79% of pfs_months variance, indicating these features carry the dominant signal.
  - Mild but statistically robust interactions: feature_080 effect is attenuated in the higher-risk strata feature_056=1, feature_063=2, feature_048=1; feature_056 x feature_063 shows slight antagonism.

Refuted / null:
  - Race (feature_011) is unassociated with pfs_months, both raw and adjusted - no disparity signal in this cohort.
  - Insurance category (feature_089) is unassociated with pfs_months.
  - feature_042 x feature_063 interaction (treatment-like benefit is uniform across the ordinal stratifier).
  - feature_042 x feature_056 interaction (effects are additive).
  - Univariate associations of feature_039 and feature_034 do not survive adjustment, suggesting confounding rather than independent effect.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
The pfs_months outcome in ds001_breast is dominated by a small set of features whose patterns are consistent with prognostic markers (feature_080 acting like patient age, feature_063 like a 3-level disease stage/grade, feature_056 and feature_048 like adverse biomarkers, feature_042 and feature_111 like protective interventions or favorable biology). Effects combine close to additively on the pfs_months scale, with only modest interactions involving the strongest features. After mutual adjustment, an 11-feature OLS captures ~79% of the variance with no large unexplained signal in the remaining 116 features and no detectable disparity by race or insurance. The data are well-modelled by a parsimonious additive prognostic structure with a few small effect-modifications by the high-risk stratifiers feature_056, feature_048, and feature_063.
