Analysis Summary - ds001_breast (n = 50,000)
=============================================

Outcome: pfs_months (continuous progression-free survival in months;
mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0.00 - 16.66).

This narrative summarizes 22 hypotheses tested across 10 iterations of a
propose-test-refine loop. The model used was claude-opus-4-7 in an
interactive Claude Code harness. Effects below are signed on the
pfs_months scale (positive = longer PFS = better).


Iteration 1 - the dominant continuous and ordinal predictors
------------------------------------------------------------
H1. feature_080 (continuous, range 30-90, mean 65) is positively
correlated with pfs_months. Pearson r = +0.7015 (p < 1e-300). Quartile
means rise monotonically from 2.50 mo (Q1) to 6.96 mo (Q4) - a 4.5-month
spread driven by a single covariate. SUPPORTED, very strong.

H2. feature_063 (ordinal 0/1/2) shows monotonic decrease in PFS as the
level rises: 5.64 / 4.44 / 3.29 mo, ANOVA p < 1e-300, level-2 minus
level-0 gap = -2.35 mo. SUPPORTED.

These two variables alone account for the bulk of explained variance in
pfs_months and serve as the spine of the multivariable model below.


Iteration 2 - top binary main effects
-------------------------------------
H3. feature_042 = 1 patients (n = 17,428, prevalence ~35 percent) have
pfs_months 1.10 mo longer than feature_042 = 0 (5.41 vs 4.31, Welch
p < 1e-300). SUPPORTED.

H4. feature_056 = 1 patients (n = 14,969, ~30 percent) have pfs_months
1.54 mo SHORTER than feature_056 = 0 (3.62 vs 5.15, p < 1e-300).
SUPPORTED.

H5. feature_048 = 1 patients (n = 5,090, ~10 percent) have pfs_months
1.05 mo shorter (3.75 vs 4.80, p = 1.5e-178). SUPPORTED.


Iteration 3 - more binary effects, plus a confounding-only finding
------------------------------------------------------------------
H6. feature_015 = 1 (n = 17,365): -0.56 mo (p = 6.1e-144). SUPPORTED.
H7. feature_111 = 1 (n = 34,886): +0.57 mo (p = 3.7e-143). SUPPORTED.
H8. feature_040 = 1 (n =  9,060): -0.46 mo (p = 6.1e-70).  SUPPORTED.

H9. feature_039 = 1 has a +0.36 mo unadjusted association
(p = 5.8e-59), but the adjusted coefficient drops to +0.014 (p = 0.32)
once feature_080, feature_063, and the other top predictors are
included. The simple bivariate signal is therefore largely a
consequence of confounding rather than an independent effect. SUPPORTED
in its REFINED, adjusted form (i.e., feature_039 is NOT independently
prognostic).


Iteration 4 - continuous-feature main effects
---------------------------------------------
H10. feature_067 (range 0-24.6): Pearson r = -0.116 (p = 4.1e-148);
adjusted beta = -0.077/unit (p < 1e-300). SUPPORTED.

H11. feature_019 (range 1.5-5.5; range and mean ~3.8 are consistent
with serum albumin in g/dL): r = +0.100 (p = 9.4e-112); adjusted beta =
+0.476/unit (p < 1e-300) - a very large per-unit effect.  SUPPORTED.

H12. feature_101 (range 1-100): r = -0.091 (p = 3.0e-93); adjusted beta
= -0.019/unit (p < 1e-300). SUPPORTED.


Iteration 5 - demographic disparities
-------------------------------------
H13. Race/ethnicity (feature_011: white, hispanic, black, asian, other)
shows essentially no PFS difference. ANOVA p = 0.494; group means
range only from 4.679 (white) to 4.762 (other) - 0.08 months. NOT
SUPPORTED. This is a notable null in light of well-documented
disparities in real-world breast cancer outcomes.

H14. Insurance (feature_089: medicare, private, medicaid, uninsured)
also shows no PFS difference. ANOVA p = 0.818; group-mean spread =
0.05 months. NOT SUPPORTED.


Iteration 6 - other ordinal features
------------------------------------
H15. None of the five 0-4 ordinals (feature_021, _024, _127, _001, _073)
shows a monotonic association. All Spearman |r| <= 0.005 and all
ANOVAs are non-significant (p in 0.27-0.83). These columns are noise
with respect to PFS. NOT SUPPORTED.


Iteration 7 - multivariable model (refinement)
----------------------------------------------
H16. A single OLS model containing the 11 candidate predictors plus
feature_039 and feature_034 explains R^2 = 0.792 of pfs_months
variance. Eleven predictors retain the signs hypothesized from
univariate work and remain individually highly significant
(|t| > 30): feature_080 (+0.176/unit), feature_063 levels 1/2
(-1.19/-2.33), feature_042 (+1.10), feature_056 (-1.55), feature_048
(-0.98), feature_015 (-0.58), feature_111 (+0.53), feature_040 (-0.47),
feature_067 (-0.077/unit), feature_019 (+0.476/unit), feature_101
(-0.019/unit). Two predictors do NOT survive: feature_039 (+0.014,
p = 0.32) and feature_034 (+0.002, p = 0.87). SUPPORTED in its
REFINED form (the 11-predictor list).


Iteration 8 - tests for biomarker-treatment interaction
-------------------------------------------------------
H17. feature_042 x feature_056 interaction is essentially zero
(coef = +0.006, p = 0.90). The two binary effects are additive;
feature_042 does not differentially rescue feature_056-positive
patients. NOT SUPPORTED.

H18. feature_042 x feature_063 interaction is also null. The +1.1 mo
benefit of feature_042 is homogeneous across all three feature_063
strata (interaction p-values 0.66 and 0.60). NOT SUPPORTED.


Iteration 9 - continuous-binary interactions with feature_080
-------------------------------------------------------------
H19. feature_056 x feature_080 interaction = -0.0112/unit (p = 3e-12).
The pfs_months harm of feature_056 = 1 grows from roughly -1.16 mo at
feature_080 = 30 to -1.83 mo at feature_080 = 90. SUPPORTED.

H20. feature_042 x feature_080 interaction = -0.0032/unit (p = 0.044).
The benefit of feature_042 = 1 narrows by about 0.2 mo across the
feature_080 range. Statistically significant but small in magnitude.
WEAKLY SUPPORTED.


Iteration 10 - racial heterogeneity and a continuous-continuous interaction
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
H21. Race-stratified Welch t-tests on the feature_042 effect produce
nearly identical estimates within each race: white +1.107, asian
+1.062, black +1.060, hispanic +1.115, other +1.007 (each highly
significant on its own). The formal four-df interaction in the OLS is
non-significant (p-values 0.52 - 0.90). The treatment effect of
feature_042 is homogeneous across race. NOT SUPPORTED.

H22. feature_080 x feature_019 interaction = +0.0050 per unit-product
(p = 0.0015). At feature_019 = 5 the per-unit slope on feature_080 is
+0.182/mo vs +0.167/mo at feature_019 = 2 - higher feature_019
amplifies the feature_080 benefit. SUPPORTED, modest magnitude.


Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. The strongest single predictor of pfs_months is feature_080 (likely
   age in years given its 30-90 range), which alone explains 49 percent
   of variance (r^2 = 0.49) - and counter-intuitively higher values are
   associated with longer PFS.

2. A 3-level ordinal feature_063 is the second-strongest predictor
   (level-2 vs level-0 gap = -2.35 mo, monotone). Together with
   feature_080 it forms a large, robust prognostic backbone.

3. Eleven additional features (5 binary, 3 continuous, plus the
   ordinal) are independently prognostic in a multivariable OLS, with
   consistent signs and individual |t| > 30: protective = feature_042,
   feature_111, feature_019; harmful = feature_056, feature_048,
   feature_015, feature_040, feature_067, feature_101. The full model
   reaches R^2 = 0.792.

4. Naive bivariate signals for feature_039 and feature_034 disappear
   under adjustment - both reflect confounding with stronger
   prognostic features.

5. Demographic predictors (race/ethnicity feature_011 and insurance
   feature_089) show NO main effect on pfs_months and NO interaction
   with the strongest "treatment-like" binary feature_042. In this
   cohort there is no detectable disparity by race or insurance, and
   the effect of feature_042 is homogeneous across those groups.

6. Treatment-biomarker interactions (feature_042 x feature_056,
   feature_042 x feature_063, feature_042 x feature_080,
   feature_042 x race) are mostly null or, when significant, very
   small in magnitude. The cleanest non-zero interaction is the
   prognostic feature_056 x feature_080 effect: feature_056=1 is more
   harmful in older patients.

7. A separate continuous-continuous interaction (feature_080 x
   feature_019) is statistically significant but small: higher
   feature_019 values (consistent with serum albumin) modestly
   amplify the per-unit feature_080 benefit.

The dominant pattern is therefore one of strong, independent,
ADDITIVE main effects from a small set of clinical features, with
relatively few interactions, no detectable demographic disparities,
and noise-only behavior from the five other ordinal columns.
