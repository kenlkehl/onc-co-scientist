CRISPR dependency map analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed the 50,000-row cell-line CRISPR dependency table using only the local task bundle. The analysis used one-vs-rest mean contrasts, simple linear screens for continuous features, and a systematic heterogeneity search over categorical/binary predicates and high/low continuous-feature thresholds. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger knockout dependency.

Iteration 1 tested direct single-feature associations for the targeted dependency outcomes. Five strong main effects emerged: `feature_009 == 1` for `dependency_BRAF` (mean -0.8047 vs -0.2495; effect -0.5552; p<1e-300), `feature_022 == 1` for `dependency_EGFR` (effect -0.3486; p<1e-300), `feature_024 == 1` for `dependency_ERBB2` (effect -0.4321; p<1e-300), `feature_014 == 1` for `dependency_KRAS` (effect -0.3075; p<1e-300), and `feature_027 == 1` for `dependency_RIT1` (effect -0.0955; p<1e-300).

Iteration 2 refined those into lineage or subtype subgroups. The BRAF effect remained strong in `feature_011 == "skin" AND feature_009 == 1` (effect -0.5205; p<1e-300). The ERBB2 effect was concentrated in `feature_010 == "her2_enriched" AND feature_024 == 1` (effect -0.4581; p<1e-300). KRAS remained tied mainly to `feature_014 == 1`, including the pancreatic subgroup (effect -0.2488; p<1e-300). EGFR was slightly stronger in `feature_022 == 1 AND feature_016 >= 1.088` (effect -0.3709; p<1e-300), and RIT1 was stronger in `feature_011 == "brain" AND feature_027 == 1` (effect -0.1397; p=1.22e-13).

Iteration 3 found broad covariate-like dependency patterns. High `feature_016` was associated with more negative `dependency_POLR2A` (effect -0.1211; p<1e-300), `dependency_DHX9` (effect -0.0433; p=4.44e-16), and `dependency_TMED10` (effect -0.0407; p=1.11e-14). High `feature_003` was strongly associated with more negative `dependency_RPL11` (effect -0.1257; p<1e-300).

Iterations 4 through 6 identified and refined the strongest heterogeneity signal, for `dependency_KIF18A`. Main effects pointed to `feature_011 == "colorectal"` (effect -0.4680; p<1e-300), high `feature_028` (effect -0.3773; p<1e-300), and `feature_035 == 1` (effect -0.2921; p<1e-300). Pairwise screening showed `feature_011 == "colorectal" AND feature_028 >= 1.427` was much stronger (mean -1.0650 vs -0.2677; effect -0.7973; p<1e-300). The complete subgroup was `feature_011 == "colorectal"`, `feature_028 >= 1.427`, `feature_020 == 0`, and `feature_035 == 1`: n=1732, mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.3952 vs -0.2678 in all other lines, effect -1.1274, p<1e-300. Within colorectal high-`feature_028` lines, `feature_020 == 0` was much more dependent than `feature_020 == 1` (contrast -0.9767; p<1e-300), and `feature_035 == 1` was much more dependent than `feature_035 == 0` (contrast -0.9112; p<1e-300). The suppressor checks supported the complete definition: outside colorectal lines, high `feature_028` was not associated with stronger KIF18A dependency (effect +0.0087; p=0.2272), colorectal high-`feature_028` lines with `feature_020 == 1` were not more dependent (effect +0.0430), and those with `feature_035 == 0` were not significantly more dependent (effect +0.0221; p=0.239).

Iterations 7 through 10 completed the systematic dependency-heterogeneity search for all outcomes. For BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, and KRAS, the dominant single-feature dependencies remained the main explanations, with modest subgroup refinements. For TMED10, the best subgroup was `feature_016 >= 1.088 AND feature_026 >= 1.153` (effect -0.0893; p=1.27e-8). For DHX9, it was `feature_016 >= 1.088 AND feature_023 >= 6.330` (effect -0.0847; p=5.09e-8). For POLR2A, it was `feature_016 >= 1.088 AND feature_003 >= 1.003` (effect -0.1347; p<1e-300). For RPL11, high `feature_003` was the central signal, with `feature_003 >= 1.003 AND feature_018 == 1` yielding effect -0.1271 (p<1e-300).

Overall, the dataset contains several clear single-feature oncogene-like dependency associations and one sharply defined multivariable dependency subgroup for `dependency_KIF18A`. The strongest conclusion is that KIF18A dependency is not a general colorectal or high-`feature_028` effect; it requires the complete combination `feature_011 == "colorectal"`, `feature_028 >= 1.427`, `feature_020 == 0`, and `feature_035 == 1`. Other dependency outcomes showed statistically significant but simpler subgroup structure, most often dominated by one marker or by broad high-`feature_016`/high-`feature_003` patterns.
