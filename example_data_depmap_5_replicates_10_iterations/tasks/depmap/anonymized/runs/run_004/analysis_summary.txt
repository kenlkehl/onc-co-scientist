Dataset ds001_depmap contains 50,000 cancer cell-line records, 36 anonymized cell-line features, and 10 dependency outcomes. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger dependency after knockout. I used only the local task bundle, decoded the local Parquet file, and tested hypotheses with subgroup comparisons on the dependency-score scale. I also ran a systematic heterogeneity search for every dependency outcome over 120 single feature predicates plus pair and triple intersections built from categorical values, binary values, and continuous-feature quantile thresholds.

The clearest main-effect dependency signals were mutation- or context-like binary features. `feature_009=1` was strongly associated with more negative `dependency_BRAF` (mean -0.8047 vs -0.2495; effect -0.5552; p < 1e-300). `feature_022=1` was associated with more negative `dependency_EGFR` (effect -0.3486; p < 1e-300), `feature_024=1` with more negative `dependency_ERBB2` (effect -0.4321; p < 1e-300), `feature_014=1` with more negative `dependency_KRAS` (effect -0.3075; p < 1e-300), and `feature_027=1` with more negative `dependency_RIT1` (effect -0.0955; p < 1e-300). `feature_018=1` also supported more negative `dependency_RPL11` (effect -0.1091; p < 1e-300).

Lineage-style categories refined several of those findings. `feature_011=skin` had more negative `dependency_BRAF` than other lineages (effect -0.1783; p < 1e-300). `feature_022=1 & feature_011=brain` marked a more negative EGFR subgroup (effect -0.3377; p < 1e-300). `feature_024=1 & feature_011=breast` marked a more negative ERBB2 subgroup (effect -0.4163; p < 1e-300). `feature_014=1 & feature_011=pancreatic` also had more negative KRAS dependency (effect -0.2488; p < 1e-300), although the broad `feature_014=1` signal remained the dominant KRAS pattern.

The strongest buried heterogeneity pattern was for `dependency_KIF18A`. A main-effect screen found `feature_011=colorectal` lines had much more negative KIF18A scores (mean -0.7204 vs -0.2524; effect -0.4680; p < 1e-300), and `feature_035=1` was also associated with stronger KIF18A dependency (effect -0.2921; p < 1e-300). In contrast, `feature_020=1` shifted KIF18A scores in the less-negative direction (effect +0.0667; p < 1e-300), indicating suppression.

Refining KIF18A showed the complete high-dependency subgroup required multiple predicates. `feature_011=colorectal & feature_035=1 & feature_020=0` had mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.0922 vs -0.2527 in all other rows (effect -0.8396; p < 1e-300). Adding high `feature_028` (`feature_028>=1.427`, top decile) identified the most extreme subgroup: `feature_011=colorectal & feature_028>=1.427 & feature_035=1 & feature_020=0` had mean -1.3952 vs -0.2678 (effect -1.1274; p < 1e-300). Control subgroup checks supported the modifier logic: among high-`feature_028`, `feature_020=0` colorectal lines, `feature_035=0` abolished the signal (mean -0.2866 vs -0.3069; effect +0.0203; p=0.329), and `feature_020=1` also suppressed the high-`feature_028` colorectal dependency.

For dependency outcomes with broader essential-gene-like behavior, effects were smaller but still statistically supported. `feature_016>=1.088` was strongly associated with more negative `dependency_POLR2A` (effect -0.1211; p < 1e-300). `feature_016>=1.088 & feature_026>=1.153` marked a more negative `dependency_TMED10` subgroup (effect -0.0893; p=1.27e-8). `feature_016>=1.088 & feature_023>=6.33` marked a more negative `dependency_DHX9` subgroup (effect -0.0847; p=5.09e-8). These effects are smaller than the BRAF/EGFR/ERBB2/KRAS/KIF18A findings but consistent across systematic screens.

The systematic heterogeneity searches for final subgroup definitions produced the following best-supported patterns. For `dependency_BRAF`, the dominant subgroup remained `feature_009=1`; the best broad pair `feature_009=1 & feature_030=0` had a similar effect (-0.5514), so added predicates did not materially improve the explanation. For `dependency_EGFR`, `feature_022=1 & feature_016>=1.088` had effect -0.3709. For `dependency_ERBB2`, `feature_024=1 & feature_011=breast & feature_013>=0.519` had effect -0.4667. For `dependency_KRAS`, `feature_014=1 & feature_016>=1.088 & feature_008=1` had effect -0.3080, but the broad `feature_014=1` signal remained nearly as explanatory. For `dependency_RIT1`, `feature_027=1 & feature_011=brain & feature_008=0` had effect -0.1665. For `dependency_RPL11`, `feature_003>=1.003 & feature_016>=0.964 & feature_011=pancreatic` had effect -0.1978. For `dependency_TMED10`, `feature_016>=1.088 & feature_026>=1.153` had effect -0.0893. For `dependency_DHX9`, `feature_016>=0.964 & feature_023>=5.669 & feature_011=prostate` had effect -0.1232. For `dependency_POLR2A`, `feature_016>=1.088 & feature_010=adenocarcinoma & feature_003>=0.428` had effect -0.1819.

Overall, the dataset contains several strong, biologically plausible dependency relationships encoded in anonymized features. The most robust single-feature signals are `feature_009` for BRAF, `feature_022` for EGFR, `feature_024` for ERBB2, `feature_014` for KRAS, and `feature_027` for RIT1. The most important multivariable discovery is the KIF18A dependency concentrated in `feature_011=colorectal`, enabled by `feature_035=1` and high `feature_028`, and suppressed by `feature_020=1`. The systematic heterogeneity searches indicate that most other outcomes have either dominant single-feature drivers or smaller quantitative-feature modifier patterns rather than a KIF18A-sized buried subgroup.
