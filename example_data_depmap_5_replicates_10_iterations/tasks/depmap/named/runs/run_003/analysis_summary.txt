CRISPR dependency analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed 50,000 cancer cell-line records with CCLE-style annotations and 10 dependency outcomes. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger selective dependency after gene knockout. Tests below used signed mean differences on the dependency-score scale, with Welch large-sample tests for subgroup comparisons. Continuous-feature subgroup cutpoints were quartiles from the dataset.

Canonical driver dependencies were strongly supported. BRAF V600E lines had much more negative dependency_BRAF scores than BRAF-wild-type lines (mean -0.805 versus -0.250; effect -0.555; p<1e-300). EGFR-amplified lines had more negative dependency_EGFR scores (effect -0.349; p<1e-300), ERBB2-amplified lines had more negative dependency_ERBB2 scores (effect -0.432; p<1e-300), and KRAS-mutant lines had more negative dependency_KRAS scores (effect -0.307; p<1e-300). KRAS dependency also tracked pancreatic lineage (effect -0.113; p=2.7e-89), colorectal lineage (effect -0.050; p=5.9e-21), and SMAD4 loss (effect -0.025; p=3.4e-7), but the KRAS mutation itself was the dominant signal.

RIT1 showed a distinct RAS-pathway heterogeneity pattern: NF1-loss lines had more negative dependency_RIT1 scores than NF1-intact lines (mean -0.353 versus -0.257; effect -0.096; p=2.0e-70). The strongest refined RIT1 subgroup was NF1 loss in brain lineage with CDKN2A retained (nf1_loss=1, lineage=brain, cdkn2a_loss=0), where dependency_RIT1 averaged -0.432 versus -0.265 outside the subgroup (effect -0.166; p=3.3e-15).

The clearest noncanonical dependency was KIF18A. Colorectal lineage alone was strongly associated with dependency_KIF18A (mean -0.720 versus -0.252; effect -0.468; p<1e-300). APC mutation and high WNT activity were also strong single predictors. The complete subgroup lineage=colorectal, apc_mutation=1, and wnt_activity_score>=0.819 had mean dependency_KIF18A -1.112 versus -0.253 outside the subgroup (effect -0.859; p<1e-300). This was the strongest and most specific subgroup pattern in the dataset.

TMED10, DHX9, POLR2A, and RPL11 showed dependency patterns tied to growth or transcriptional state rather than a single oncogenic lesion. High growth (growth_rate_doublings_per_day>=0.964) predicted more negative dependency_TMED10 (effect -0.037; p=2.2e-24), dependency_DHX9 (effect -0.037; p=1.5e-24), and dependency_POLR2A (effect -0.104; p=4.3e-183). TMED10 had a small but stronger refined subgroup in high-growth, low-ASCL2, NF1-loss lines (effect -0.102; p=3.2e-8). DHX9 was more negative in high-MYC-expression lines (effect -0.029; p=2.0e-15), and the high-growth, high-MYC, prostate-lineage subgroup was stronger (effect -0.123; p=1.6e-5). POLR2A was strongest in high-growth adenocarcinoma lines with high SLFN11 expression (effect -0.127; p=6.3e-12).

RPL11 dependency was linked to MYC/8q24 biology. MYC-amplified lines had more negative dependency_RPL11 scores (mean -0.361 versus -0.252; effect -0.109; p=7.0e-144), and high copy_number_8q24 was similarly associated (effect -0.099; p=1.5e-162). The strongest refined RPL11 subgroup was MYC-amplified, high-growth pancreatic lines, with mean dependency_RPL11 -0.438 versus -0.269 outside the subgroup (effect -0.169; p=2.4e-15).

Systematic heterogeneity searches were run for every dependency outcome using binary features, categorical levels, continuous high/low quartile predicates, and pair/triple combinations of the strongest modifiers. The final best-supported subgroup definitions were: BRAF dependency in braf_v600e=1 high-growth PTEN-loss lines; EGFR dependency in EGFR-amplified high-growth high-8q24 lines; ERBB2 dependency in ERBB2-amplified breast IFN-high lines; KRAS dependency in KRAS-mutant lines, especially when NF1 and ERBB2 alterations were absent; RIT1 dependency in NF1-loss brain CDKN2A-retained lines; KIF18A dependency in colorectal APC-mutant WNT-high lines; TMED10 dependency in high-growth low-ASCL2 NF1-loss lines; DHX9 dependency in high-growth MYC-high prostate lines; POLR2A dependency in high-growth adenocarcinoma SLFN11-high lines; and RPL11 dependency in MYC-amplified high-growth pancreatic lines.
