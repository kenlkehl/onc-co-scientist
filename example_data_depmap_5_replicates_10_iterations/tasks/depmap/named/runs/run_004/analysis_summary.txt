Analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed 50,000 cell-line records from the local dataset.parquet file. Dependency scores are interpreted so that more negative values indicate stronger dependency after knockout. I used Welch-style subgroup contrasts for all reported effects; effect estimates are mean dependency score in the named subgroup minus mean score in the comparator group, so negative effects indicate stronger dependency in the subgroup. Quartile cut points used in the analyses were growth_rate_doublings_per_day >= 0.964, wnt_activity_score >= 0.819, copy_number_8q24 >= 0.428, and rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm >= 5.669.

Early screen-covariate hypotheses showed that high-growth cell lines have stronger dependency on several broadly essential or proliferation-linked outcomes. High growth was associated with more negative dependency_POLR2A (effect -0.1043, p=4.27e-183), dependency_DHX9 (effect -0.0371, p=1.50e-24), and dependency_TMED10 (effect -0.0369, p=2.22e-24). The POLR2A signal was the largest of these, while TMED10 was statistically robust but biologically small.

Canonical oncogene dependencies were strongly supported. braf_v600e = 1 cell lines had much more negative dependency_BRAF scores than braf_v600e = 0 lines (effect -0.5552, p<1e-300). A proposed skin-lineage refinement within BRAF V600E lines was not supported (within-mutant effect -0.0103, p=0.295), so the BRAF dependency is best explained by the mutation rather than an added skin predicate. egfr_amplification = 1 strongly predicted dependency_EGFR (effect -0.3486, p<1e-300), and erbb2_amplification = 1 strongly predicted dependency_ERBB2 (effect -0.4321, p<1e-300). Breast and luminal ERBB2 contrasts were significant but much smaller than the amplification contrast.

KRAS and RIT1 followed expected pathway logic. kras_mutation = 1 lines had more negative dependency_KRAS scores (effect -0.3075, p<1e-300); pancreatic and colorectal lineages also showed stronger KRAS dependency, consistent with enrichment of KRAS-mutant contexts. nf1_loss = 1 lines had more negative dependency_RIT1 scores (effect -0.0955, p=2.03e-70), making NF1 loss the clearest RIT1 subgroup signal.

The strongest non-obvious heterogeneity pattern was for dependency_KIF18A. Colorectal lineage alone (effect -0.468), apc_mutation = 1 (effect -0.2921), and high wnt_activity_score (effect -0.2168) were all individually associated with stronger KIF18A dependency. The refined complete subgroup lineage = colorectal, apc_mutation = 1, and wnt_activity_score >= 0.819 was much sharper: mean dependency_KIF18A was -1.1119 versus -0.253 outside the subgroup, for an effect of -0.8589 (p<1e-300). Even within colorectal lines only, APC-mutant/high-WNT lines were far more KIF18A-dependent than other colorectal lines (effect -0.8487, p<1e-300).

MYC-linked ribosome/helicase dependencies were also supported. dependency_RPL11 was more negative in myc_amplification = 1 and high copy_number_8q24 lines (effect -0.1123, p=3.53e-148), with myc_amplification alone also significant. dependency_DHX9 was more negative in high-growth/high-rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm lines (effect -0.0595, p=1.44e-21), supporting a proliferation/MYC-associated DHX9 dependency.

For the required systematic heterogeneity search, I screened each dependency outcome over single feature predicates, then top pair and triple subgroup definitions using lineage, subtype, culture, batch, binary molecular features, and high/low quartile thresholds for continuous molecular and screen covariates. The best-supported final subgroup definitions were: dependency_BRAF with braf_v600e = 1; dependency_EGFR with egfr_amplification = 1; dependency_ERBB2 with erbb2_amplification = 1; dependency_KRAS with kras_mutation = 1; dependency_RIT1 with nf1_loss = 1; dependency_KIF18A with colorectal/APC-mutant/high-WNT; dependency_TMED10 with high growth rate, weak magnitude; dependency_DHX9 with high growth plus high MYC RNA; dependency_POLR2A with high growth rate; and dependency_RPL11 with MYC amplification plus high 8q24 copy number.

Overall, most dependency outcomes were dominated by direct driver-feature relationships or screen/proliferation covariates. The most biologically specific multivariable finding was the colorectal, APC-mutant, high-WNT subgroup for KIF18A dependency.
