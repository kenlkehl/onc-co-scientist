Dataset ds001_depmap contained 50000 cancer cell-line records with 10 CRISPR dependency outcomes. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger dependency after knockout. I decoded the local Parquet bundle and ran Welch-style two-group comparisons, continuous-feature correlation screens, and a systematic dependency-heterogeneity search over lineage, lineage_subtype, culture_type, screen_batch, all binary molecular features, top and bottom quartiles of continuous features, and eligible pairwise subgroup intersections with at least 100 cell lines.

Canonical driver dependencies were strongly supported. BRAF-mutant lines (`braf_v600e == 1`) had much more negative `dependency_BRAF` scores than BRAF-wild-type lines (-0.8047 vs -0.2495; effect -0.5552; p<1e-300). EGFR-amplified lines had more negative `dependency_EGFR` (-0.6010 vs -0.2524; effect -0.3486; p<1e-300). ERBB2-amplified lines had more negative `dependency_ERBB2` (-0.6856 vs -0.2535; effect -0.4321; p<1e-300). KRAS-mutant lines had more negative `dependency_KRAS` (-0.5594 vs -0.2520; effect -0.3075; p<1e-300). Lineage analyses were directionally consistent: skin lines were enriched for BRAF dependency, pancreatic and colorectal lines for KRAS dependency, and breast/HER2-enriched or luminal contexts for ERBB2 dependency.

`dependency_RIT1` showed a clear NF1-loss association. Lines with `nf1_loss == 1` had mean `dependency_RIT1` -0.3527 versus -0.2572 in other lines (effect -0.0955; p<1e-300). A refined brain/NF1-loss subgroup was stronger (`lineage == "brain"` and `nf1_loss == 1`: -0.4047 vs -0.2650; effect -0.1397; p=1.22e-13), but the broad NF1-loss effect was the main reproducible signal.

The strongest non-canonical dependency pattern was for `dependency_KIF18A`. Colorectal lines were much more dependent than other lines (-0.7204 vs -0.2524; effect -0.4680; p<1e-300), APC-mutant lines were also more dependent (-0.5447 vs -0.2526; effect -0.2921; p<1e-300), and high-WNT lines (`wnt_activity_score >= 0.819`) were more dependent (-0.4694 vs -0.2526; effect -0.2168; p<1e-300). The best complete subgroup was `lineage == "colorectal"` plus `apc_mutation == 1`, with mean `dependency_KIF18A` -0.9425 versus -0.2527 in all other lines (effect -0.6899; p<1e-300). The related subgroup `lineage == "colorectal"` plus high `wnt_activity_score` was nearly as strong (-0.9288 vs -0.2527; effect -0.6761; p<1e-300). High aneuploidy alone was refuted as an explanation: top-quartile `aneuploidy_score` had almost no effect (-0.3078 vs -0.3065; effect -0.0013; p=0.775).

For `dependency_TMED10`, the evidence was weaker and mostly tied to screen/proliferation covariates. High `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` was associated with slightly more negative scores (-0.2816 vs -0.2447; effect -0.0369; p<1e-300). A colorectal hypothesis was not supported (-0.2576 vs -0.2535; effect -0.0040; p=0.412). A small luminal/STK11-loss subgroup was nominally significant but small and less robust (-0.3078 vs -0.2537; effect -0.0541; p=0.0169).

`dependency_DHX9` was modestly associated with MYC/proliferation rather than SLFN11. High `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` had a negative effect (-0.3551 vs -0.3265; effect -0.0286; p=2e-15), and the high-growth/high-MYC subgroup was stronger (-0.3892 vs -0.3297; effect -0.0595; p<1e-300). Top-quartile `rnaseq_SLFN11_log2_tpm` was refuted (effect +0.0007; p=0.848).

`dependency_POLR2A` was most strongly related to proliferation. High-growth lines had substantially more negative `dependency_POLR2A` scores (-0.4972 vs -0.3928; effect -0.1043; p<1e-300). Adding low `copy_number_9p21` to high growth still defined a negative subgroup (-0.5098 vs -0.4131; effect -0.0967; p<1e-300), but low `copy_number_9p21` alone was not supported (effect -0.0009; p=0.811).

`dependency_RPL11` was linked to MYC/8q24 biology. `myc_amplification == 1` lines had more negative `dependency_RPL11` scores (-0.3614 vs -0.2523; effect -0.1091; p<1e-300), and top-quartile `copy_number_8q24` was similarly strong (-0.3440 vs -0.2452; effect -0.0988; p<1e-300). The strongest refined subgroup combined MYC amplification with high growth rate (-0.3936 vs -0.2632; effect -0.1303; p<1e-300).

Overall, the dataset contains expected oncogene-addiction signals for BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, KRAS, and RIT1/NF1, plus a pronounced colorectal/APC/WNT-specific KIF18A dependency. TMED10, DHX9, and POLR2A were dominated by smaller growth-rate or MYC/proliferation-associated shifts rather than clean lineage-specific molecular dependencies.
