CRISPR dependency analysis summary for ds001_depmap

Dataset and approach
I analyzed 50000 cell-line records with molecular features, screen covariates, and 10 dependency outcomes. Dependency scores are interpreted on their natural scale, where more negative values indicate stronger dependency after knockout. I tested main effects, subgroup contrasts, and adjusted linear models. P-values are large-sample normal approximations from Welch contrasts or OLS coefficient tests.

Iteration 1: canonical oncogene dependency checks
The expected matched driver dependencies were strongly supported. BRAF V600E cell lines had more negative dependency_BRAF scores than BRAF-wild-type lines (mean -0.805 vs -0.250; effect -0.555; p<1e-300). EGFR-amplified lines had more negative dependency_EGFR scores (mean -0.601 vs -0.252; effect -0.349; p<1e-300). ERBB2-amplified lines had more negative dependency_ERBB2 scores (mean -0.686 vs -0.253; effect -0.432; p<1e-300). KRAS-mutant lines had more negative dependency_KRAS scores (mean -0.559 vs -0.252; effect -0.307; p<1e-300).

Iteration 2: additional driver-context dependencies
NF1 loss was associated with stronger RIT1 dependency (mean -0.353 vs -0.257; effect -0.096; p=2.03e-70). A MYC/8q24 context was associated with stronger RPL11 dependency: MYC-amplified high-copy_number_8q24 lines had mean dependency_RPL11 -0.365 vs -0.252 in the complement (effect -0.112; p=3.53e-148). MYC amplification had a much smaller but significant association with dependency_DHX9 (effect -0.018; p=1.68e-05).

Iterations 3-4: KIF18A heterogeneity and refinement
KIF18A showed the clearest noncanonical subgroup effect. Colorectal lineage alone was associated with much more negative dependency_KIF18A (mean -0.720 vs -0.252; effect -0.468; p<1e-300). APC mutation also had a large negative effect (mean -0.545 vs -0.253; effect -0.292; p<1e-300), and high WNT activity was directionally consistent (effect -0.217; p<1e-300). The complete subgroup, lineage == colorectal, apc_mutation == 1, and wnt_activity_score >= 0.819, was far stronger than any component alone: mean dependency_KIF18A -1.112 vs -0.253; effect -0.859; p<1e-300; n=3133. Within colorectal APC-mutant lines, high WNT further separated KIF18A dependency (effect -0.839; p<1e-300). Among high-WNT lines, the full colorectal/APC-mutant context was still much more dependent than high-WNT lines outside that context (effect -0.857; p<1e-300). This supports a complete subgroup definition rather than WNT activity alone.

Iteration 5: growth and screen-context effects
Fast-growing lines were more dependent on several broadly essential processes. Lines with growth_rate_doublings_per_day >= 0.964 had more negative dependency_POLR2A (effect -0.104; p=4.27e-183), dependency_TMED10 (effect -0.037; p=2.22e-24), and dependency_DHX9 (effect -0.037; p=1.50e-24). Continuous models also showed negative growth-rate slopes for POLR2A (-0.305 per doubling/day), TMED10 (-0.101), and DHX9 (-0.109).

Iterations 6-7: systematic dependency-heterogeneity search
I screened feature-defined subgroups for every dependency outcome. For BRAF, the dominant signal remained braf_v600e, with the high-growth BRAF V600E subgroup showing mean dependency_BRAF -0.829. For EGFR, the best exploratory subgroup combined egfr_amplification, high growth, and high copy_number_8q24 (effect -0.391; p=1.27e-71). For ERBB2, an ERBB2-amplified breast/high-IFN subgroup was most negative (effect -0.467; p=9.53e-108). For KRAS, KRAS-mutant high-growth STK11-loss lines were strongly dependent, although the simpler KRAS-mutant contrast remained the main supported relationship. For RIT1, NF1-loss with low aneuploidy and low mutation burden was the most negative exploratory subgroup. For KIF18A, the colorectal/APC-mutant/high-WNT subgroup was by far the strongest subgroup across the entire scan. TMED10 and DHX9 had only modest exploratory subgroup hits, while POLR2A and RPL11 were most consistent with growth and MYC-context effects respectively.

Iterations 8-9: adjusted models
The canonical driver effects persisted after adjusting for growth_rate_doublings_per_day, cas9_activity_score, and lineage: adjusted coefficients were BRAF V600E on dependency_BRAF -0.555, EGFR amplification on dependency_EGFR -0.349, ERBB2 amplification on dependency_ERBB2 -0.432, KRAS mutation on dependency_KRAS -0.309, and NF1 loss on dependency_RIT1 -0.095; all were statistically significant. For KIF18A, an OLS model containing colorectal, APC mutation, high WNT, all lower-order interaction terms, growth, and Cas9 activity still estimated a strong negative three-way coefficient for colorectal/APC-mutant/high-WNT (coefficient -0.817; p=8.77e-228). This confirms that the KIF18A pattern is not explained by any one component alone.

Overall conclusions
Supported hypotheses: BRAF V600E -> BRAF dependency, EGFR amplification -> EGFR dependency, ERBB2 amplification -> ERBB2 dependency, KRAS mutation -> KRAS dependency, NF1 loss -> RIT1 dependency, MYC/8q24 context -> RPL11 dependency, and colorectal/APC-mutant/high-WNT context -> KIF18A dependency. The KIF18A subgroup is the strongest and most biologically specific noncanonical discovery in this dataset. Weaker or more exploratory patterns for TMED10 and DHX9 appear more tied to growth or screen context than to a single robust molecular subgroup. POLR2A is most strongly associated with growth rate, consistent with a broad proliferation-linked dependency rather than a driver-genotype-specific vulnerability.
