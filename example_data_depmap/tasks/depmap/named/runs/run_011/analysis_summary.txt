Analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed 50,000 cancer cell-line CRISPR dependency records using only the local task bundle. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger selective dependency after knockout. I used two-sample subgroup contrasts for binary, categorical, and quartile-defined continuous predicates, continuous correlations/slopes, and systematic single-feature and two-feature heterogeneity screens across every dependency outcome. P-values reported as <1e-300 underflowed in the large-sample normal approximation but were decisively significant.

Iteration 1 tested canonical oncogene-driver dependencies. All four primary driver hypotheses were strongly supported. BRAF-mutant lines (`braf_v600e=1`) had much more negative `dependency_BRAF` than BRAF-wildtype lines (-0.8047 vs -0.2495; effect -0.5552; p<1e-300). `egfr_amplification=1` similarly marked stronger `dependency_EGFR` (-0.6010 vs -0.2524; effect -0.3486; p<1e-300), `erbb2_amplification=1` marked stronger `dependency_ERBB2` (-0.6856 vs -0.2535; effect -0.4321; p<1e-300), and `kras_mutation=1` marked stronger `dependency_KRAS` (-0.5594 vs -0.2520; effect -0.3075; p<1e-300).

Iteration 2 refined those driver hypotheses by lineage. Skin-lineage cell lines had more negative `dependency_BRAF` than other lineages (-0.4721 vs -0.2938; effect -0.1783), pancreatic lines had more negative `dependency_KRAS` (-0.4260 vs -0.3133; effect -0.1127), and brain lines had more negative `dependency_EGFR` (-0.3307 vs -0.2755; effect -0.0552). These effects were significant, but the alteration-level predictors remained the clearest primary explanations for the canonical dependencies.

Iteration 3 tested broad screen and proliferation effects. High `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` (top quartile >=0.964) was associated with stronger essential-gene dependencies, especially `dependency_POLR2A` (-0.4972 vs -0.3928; effect -0.1043; p<1e-300) and `dependency_TMED10` (-0.2816 vs -0.2447; effect -0.0369; p<1e-300). `myc_amplification=1` strongly marked more negative `dependency_RPL11` (-0.3614 vs -0.2523; effect -0.1091; p<1e-300). High `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` marked more negative `dependency_DHX9` (-0.3551 vs -0.3265; effect -0.0286; p=2.0e-15).

Iteration 4 tested an initial KIF18A chromosomal-instability hypothesis. A simple high-aneuploidy subgroup (`aneuploidy_score>=0.478`) was not supported: `dependency_KIF18A` was nearly identical in high-aneuploidy and other lines (-0.3078 vs -0.3065; effect -0.0013; p=0.7754). Continuous `ploidy` had a statistically significant but very small negative slope (slope -0.0266; r=-0.0341; p=2.31e-14), so ploidy alone was not a useful explanation for the large KIF18A heterogeneity.

Iteration 5 shifted KIF18A toward colorectal/WNT biology. Colorectal lineage was the strongest single-feature signal for `dependency_KIF18A` (-0.7204 vs -0.2524; effect -0.4680; p<1e-300). `apc_mutation=1` was also strong (-0.5447 vs -0.2526; effect -0.2921), as was high `wnt_activity_score` (-0.4694 vs -0.2526; effect -0.2168). High `rnaseq_ASCL2_log2_tpm` was a weaker but consistent WNT-associated marker (-0.3880 vs -0.2797; effect -0.1083).

Iteration 6 defined the complete KIF18A subgroup. The strongest and most interpretable subgroup was `lineage=colorectal`, `apc_mutation=1`, and `wnt_activity_score>=0.819`. These lines had very strong KIF18A dependency (-1.1119 vs -0.2530 in all other lines; n=3133; effect -0.8589; p<1e-300). The component checks showed that the complete definition matters: colorectal/APC-mutant but WNT-low lines were not strongly dependent (-0.2727), and colorectal/APC-wildtype/high-WNT lines were also not strongly dependent (-0.2718). Non-colorectal APC-mutant/high-WNT lines likewise lacked the effect (-0.2528). High ASCL2 further enriched the dependency (-1.1414), but lower-ASCL2 members of the same colorectal/APC/high-WNT subgroup remained strongly dependent (-1.0701), so ASCL2 is supportive rather than required.

Iteration 7 tested RIT1 and screen-quality covariates. `nf1_loss=1` marked more negative `dependency_RIT1` (-0.3527 vs -0.2572; effect -0.0955; p<1e-300), and the effect was strongest in `nf1_loss=1` brain-lineage lines (-0.4047 vs -0.2650; effect -0.1397; p=1.22e-13). High `cas9_activity_score` was associated with more negative `dependency_POLR2A` and `dependency_TMED10`, but those effects were smaller than the high-growth effects and appear best interpreted as screen/covariate sensitivity rather than primary biology.

Iterations 8 and 9 performed the required systematic dependency-heterogeneity searches. The single-feature screen recovered the same primary drivers: BRAF by `braf_v600e=1`, EGFR by `egfr_amplification=1`, ERBB2 by `erbb2_amplification=1`, KRAS by `kras_mutation=1`, RIT1 by `nf1_loss=1`, KIF18A by colorectal lineage, TMED10/POLR2A by high growth, DHX9 by high growth or high MYC expression, and RPL11 by MYC amplification or high 8q24 copy number. The two-feature screen did not overturn the canonical driver conclusions. It refined KIF18A to the colorectal/APC/high-WNT subgroup, DHX9 to high growth plus high MYC RNA, and RPL11 to MYC amplification plus high growth.

Final dependency subgroup conclusions:
- `dependency_BRAF`: best-supported subgroup is `braf_v600e=1` (effect -0.5552).
- `dependency_EGFR`: best-supported subgroup is `egfr_amplification=1` (effect -0.3486).
- `dependency_ERBB2`: best-supported subgroup is `erbb2_amplification=1` (effect -0.4321).
- `dependency_KRAS`: best-supported subgroup is `kras_mutation=1` (effect -0.3075).
- `dependency_RIT1`: best-supported broad subgroup is `nf1_loss=1` (effect -0.0955), with a stronger brain-lineage subset (effect -0.1397).
- `dependency_KIF18A`: best-supported subgroup is `lineage=colorectal`, `apc_mutation=1`, and `wnt_activity_score>=0.819` (effect -0.8589), with absence of APC mutation or high WNT suppressing the phenotype even within colorectal lines.
- `dependency_TMED10`: best-supported subgroup is high `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` >=0.964 (effect -0.0369), a modest proliferation-associated signal.
- `dependency_DHX9`: best-supported subgroup is high growth plus high `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` >=5.669 (effect -0.0595).
- `dependency_POLR2A`: best-supported subgroup is high `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` >=0.964 (effect -0.1043).
- `dependency_RPL11`: best-supported subgroup is `myc_amplification=1` plus high growth (effect -0.1303), consistent with MYC/8q24-associated ribosomal stress dependence.
