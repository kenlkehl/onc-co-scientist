CRISPR dependency analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed 50,000 cell-line records with 10 dependency outcomes. Dependency scores are interpreted on their natural scale, where more negative values indicate stronger dependency after knockout. Statistical tests used univariate linear regressions for continuous features, Welch-style group comparisons for binary and subgroup hypotheses, difference-in-differences interaction contrasts for selected modifiers, and a selected pairwise heterogeneity screen across dependency outcomes.

Iteration 1 screened broad screen covariates. Higher `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` was associated with more negative scores across outcomes, including `dependency_BRAF` slope -0.1101 (p=1.53e-36), `dependency_EGFR` slope -0.1080 (p=3.36e-41), and especially `dependency_POLR2A` slope -0.3048 (p underflow). Higher `cas9_activity_score` also shifted dependencies more negative, including `dependency_TMED10` slope -0.1040 (p=1.41e-15). These broad effects were treated as screen covariates rather than gene-specific biological subgroups.

Canonical oncogene-addiction hypotheses were strongly supported. `braf_v600e=1` was associated with much more negative `dependency_BRAF` (mean -0.8047 vs -0.2495; effect -0.5552). `egfr_amplification=1` marked stronger `dependency_EGFR` (effect -0.3486), `erbb2_amplification=1` marked stronger `dependency_ERBB2` (effect -0.4321), and `kras_mutation=1` marked stronger `dependency_KRAS` (effect -0.3075). All four p-values underflowed below machine precision.

For RAS-pathway rewiring, `nf1_loss=1` supported a RIT1 dependency hypothesis: mean `dependency_RIT1` was -0.3527 in NF1-loss lines versus -0.2572 otherwise (effect -0.0955; p=2.03e-70). Pairwise screening found only slight changes around that effect, so the final RIT1 subgroup is `nf1_loss=1`.

The strongest nontrivial heterogeneity involved `dependency_KIF18A`. Main effects pointed to colorectal lineage, APC mutation, and WNT activity: colorectal lines had effect -0.4680, `apc_mutation=1` effect -0.2921, and high `wnt_activity_score >= 0.819` effect -0.2168. Interaction tests showed these were not independent main effects: APC mutation was far more negative inside colorectal lineage (interaction -0.6822), high WNT was far more negative inside colorectal lineage (interaction -0.6691), and APC mutation combined with high WNT had interaction -0.4698. The triple subgroup `lineage=colorectal AND apc_mutation=1 AND wnt_activity_score >= 0.819` had mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.1119 versus -0.2530 in all other lines (effect -0.8589).

Refining the KIF18A subgroup revealed a suppressor/enabler pattern with SMAD4. The complete subgroup `lineage=colorectal AND apc_mutation=1 AND wnt_activity_score >= 0.819 AND smad4_loss=0` had mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.2990 versus -0.2530 otherwise (effect -1.0460; p underflow). The matched subgroup with `smad4_loss=1` had mean -0.2557 and a positive contrast versus the rest of the dataset (effect +0.0517; p=2.35e-04), indicating that SMAD4 loss suppresses or eliminates the KIF18A dependency in the colorectal/APC/high-WNT context.

MYC-linked hypotheses were partly supported. High `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm >= 5.669` was associated with more negative `dependency_DHX9` (effect -0.0286; p=1.95e-15), and `myc_amplification=1` gave a weaker DHX9 effect (effect -0.0184; p=1.68e-05). For `dependency_RPL11`, `myc_amplification=1` had a strong effect (mean -0.3614 vs -0.2523; effect -0.1091; p=7.04e-144), and high `copy_number_8q24 >= 0.428` was similarly strong (effect -0.0988; p=1.53e-162). The combined MYC-amplified/high-8q24 subgroup had effect -0.1123, but the formal interaction was not significant (p=0.080), so this appears to be overlapping MYC/8q24 signal rather than synergy.

For `dependency_TMED10` and `dependency_POLR2A`, the systematic heterogeneity search did not identify convincing molecular subgroups. `dependency_TMED10` was more negative in high-growth lines (effect -0.0369; p=2.22e-24), while the best selected molecular pair was small and only nominal (`egfr_amplification=1 AND apc_mutation=1`, effect -0.0298; p=0.044). `dependency_POLR2A` was dominated by growth rate (high-growth effect -0.1043; p=4.27e-183), while the strongest selected molecular pair was much smaller (`myc_amplification=1 AND rnaseq_ASCL2_log2_tpm >= 3.559`, effect -0.0200; p=0.013).

Final dependency-specific conclusions: `dependency_BRAF` is driven by `braf_v600e=1`; `dependency_EGFR` by `egfr_amplification=1`; `dependency_ERBB2` by `erbb2_amplification=1`; `dependency_KRAS` by `kras_mutation=1`; `dependency_RIT1` by `nf1_loss=1`; `dependency_KIF18A` by the complete colorectal/APC-mutant/high-WNT/SMAD4-wild-type subgroup; `dependency_DHX9` by high MYC expression; `dependency_RPL11` by MYC amplification/high 8q24 copy number; and `dependency_TMED10` and `dependency_POLR2A` primarily by broad screen-growth and Cas9 covariates rather than a clear molecular subgroup in the selected search.
