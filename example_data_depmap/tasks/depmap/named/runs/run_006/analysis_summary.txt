CRISPR dependency map ds001_depmap analysis summary

I analyzed the 50,000-row cell-line CRISPR dependency dataset using local parquet decoding, per-outcome univariate screens, Welch subgroup comparisons, and systematic feature-by-feature plus two-feature dependency-heterogeneity searches. Dependency scores are interpreted on their natural scale, where more negative values indicate stronger dependency after knockout.

Canonical oncogene-dependency checks were strongly supported. BRAF V600E cell lines had substantially more negative dependency_BRAF scores than other lines (mean -0.8047 vs -0.2495; effect -0.5552; p < 1e-300). EGFR-amplified lines showed stronger dependency_EGFR (mean -0.6010 vs -0.2524; effect -0.3486; p < 1e-300). ERBB2-amplified lines showed stronger dependency_ERBB2 (mean -0.6856 vs -0.2535; effect -0.4321; p < 1e-300). KRAS-mutant lines showed stronger dependency_KRAS (mean -0.5594 vs -0.2520; effect -0.3075; p < 1e-300), with the expected signal still present in KRAS-mutant pancreatic lines.

RIT1 dependency was linked to NF1 loss. NF1-loss lines had more negative dependency_RIT1 than other lines (mean -0.3527 vs -0.2572; effect -0.0955; p < 1e-300). The strongest refined subgroup added low rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm at or below 4.230, where mean dependency_RIT1 was -0.3761 versus -0.2633 outside the subgroup (effect -0.1128; p < 1e-300).

The clearest multivariable dependency was dependency_KIF18A. A first pass found colorectal lineage, APC mutation, high wnt_activity_score, and high rnaseq_ASCL2_log2_tpm as related signals. The refined complete subgroup was colorectal cell lines with apc_mutation = 1, wnt_activity_score >= 0.819, and smad4_loss = 0. This subgroup had mean dependency_KIF18A -1.2990 versus -0.2530 outside the subgroup (effect -1.0460; p < 1e-300). The suppressor checks were important: colorectal/APC-mutant lines below the high-WNT cutoff were not more dependent (effect +0.0347), and colorectal/APC-mutant/high-WNT lines with smad4_loss = 1 were not more dependent (effect +0.0517 vs outside). Within the colorectal/APC-mutant/high-WNT stratum, SMAD4-loss lines were much less negative than SMAD4-intact lines (-0.2557 vs -1.2990; effect +1.0433), indicating that SMAD4 loss suppresses the KIF18A dependency.

Several outcomes showed broader proliferation or MYC-axis patterns rather than narrow oncogene-addiction subgroups. High growth_rate_doublings_per_day (>= 0.964) was associated with stronger dependency_POLR2A (mean -0.4972 vs -0.3928; effect -0.1043; p < 1e-300) and a smaller but significant dependency_TMED10 shift (mean -0.2816 vs -0.2447; effect -0.0369; p < 1e-300). DHX9 dependency was strongest in high-growth/high-MYC-expression lines (mean -0.3892 vs -0.3297; effect -0.0595; p < 1e-300). RPL11 dependency tracked the MYC/8q24 axis: high copy_number_8q24 alone had effect -0.0988, and the MYC-amplified/high-8q24 subgroup had mean dependency_RPL11 -0.3647 versus -0.2524 outside (effect -0.1123; p < 1e-300).

Overall, the dataset contains the expected driver-matched dependency patterns for BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, and KRAS; an NF1-loss/RIT1 relationship; broad growth-associated dependency for POLR2A, TMED10, and DHX9; and a distinct buried KIF18A dependency requiring the combined predicates lineage = colorectal, apc_mutation = 1, high wnt_activity_score, and smad4_loss = 0. The KIF18A result is the most important subgroup-specific finding because WNT-high colorectal APC-mutant status enables the dependency while SMAD4 loss suppresses it.
