Dataset ds001_depmap contains 50000 cell-line records with 10 CRISPR dependency outcomes. I analyzed binary molecular features, categorical lineage/culture/batch variables, continuous molecular scores, and screen covariates. Dependency scores are interpreted on their natural scale, where more negative values mean stronger dependency.

Canonical driver dependencies were strongly recovered. `braf_v600e=1` predicted more negative `dependency_BRAF` (adjusted beta -0.5544; unadjusted means -0.8047 vs -0.2495, p approximately 1e-300). `egfr_amplification=1` predicted more negative `dependency_EGFR` (adjusted beta -0.3487; means -0.6010 vs -0.2524, p approximately 1e-300). `erbb2_amplification=1` predicted more negative `dependency_ERBB2` (adjusted beta -0.4325; means -0.6856 vs -0.2535, p approximately 1e-300). `kras_mutation=1` predicted more negative `dependency_KRAS` (adjusted beta -0.3088; means -0.5594 vs -0.2520, p approximately 1e-300).

Several lineage signals seen in the first scan were explained by driver enrichment rather than independent lineage biology. `lineage=skin` was no longer associated with `dependency_BRAF` after adjustment for `braf_v600e` (beta -0.0021, p=0.706). `lineage=brain` was null for `dependency_EGFR` after adjustment for `egfr_amplification` (beta 0.0029, p=0.635). `lineage=breast` and `lineage=gastric` were null in the adjusted ERBB2 model. The `kras_mutation` by `lineage=pancreatic` interaction was also null (beta -0.0004, p=0.971), supporting KRAS mutation itself rather than a pancreatic-specific KRAS-mutant dependency.

For RAS-pathway heterogeneity, `nf1_loss=1` was the clear RIT1-associated subgroup. `nf1_loss` lines had more negative `dependency_RIT1` scores than other lines (means -0.3527 vs -0.2572), and the association persisted after adjustment for growth and Cas9 activity (beta -0.0953, p approximately 1e-300).

The strongest noncanonical pattern was for `dependency_KIF18A`. Main-effect scans identified `lineage=colorectal`, `apc_mutation=1`, high `wnt_activity_score`, and high `rnaseq_ASCL2_log2_tpm` as related signals. Refinement showed that the complete dependency subgroup was `lineage=colorectal`, `apc_mutation=1`, high `wnt_activity_score` (`>=0.819`), and `smad4_loss=0`. This subgroup had mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.2990 versus -0.2530 in all other lines (effect -1.0460, p approximately 1e-300). Within colorectal/APC/high-WNT lines, `smad4_loss=0` had mean -1.2990 while `smad4_loss=1` had mean -0.2557 (effect -1.0433, p approximately 1e-300), indicating that SMAD4 loss suppresses the otherwise strong KIF18A dependency.

MYC and chromosome 8q24 signals separated by outcome. Higher `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` independently predicted more negative `dependency_DHX9` (adjusted beta -0.0160, p approximately 1e-300), while `myc_amplification` itself was not independent after MYC expression was included. For `dependency_RPL11`, high `copy_number_8q24` was the independent signal (adjusted beta -0.1041, p approximately 1e-300); `myc_amplification` and `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` were not independently negative once continuous 8q24 copy number was modeled.

`dependency_POLR2A` and `dependency_TMED10` were dominated by growth and screen covariates rather than a sharply defined molecular subgroup. Higher `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` strongly predicted more negative `dependency_POLR2A` (adjusted beta -0.3053, p approximately 1e-300) and more negative `dependency_TMED10` (adjusted beta -0.1010, p approximately 1e-300). `cas9_activity_score` was also negative in the TMED10 model (beta -0.1035, p=1.78e-15). `nf1_loss` had a small additional TMED10 association (beta -0.0134, p=0.013), but it was much weaker than the growth/Cas9 effects.

The systematic heterogeneity search covered every dependency outcome using binary predicates, categorical levels, upper/lower quartiles of continuous variables, and pair/triple subgroup intersections. Final best-supported subgroup definitions are: BRAF: `braf_v600e=1`; EGFR: `egfr_amplification=1`; ERBB2: `erbb2_amplification=1`; KRAS: `kras_mutation=1`; RIT1: `nf1_loss=1`; KIF18A: `lineage=colorectal`, `apc_mutation=1`, high `wnt_activity_score`, and `smad4_loss=0`; TMED10: primarily high `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` with a small `nf1_loss` component; DHX9: high `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm`; POLR2A: high `growth_rate_doublings_per_day`; RPL11: high `copy_number_8q24`, especially overlapping `myc_amplification`, with continuous 8q24 copy number carrying the independent effect.
