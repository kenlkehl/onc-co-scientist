Dataset ds001_depmap contains 50,000 cell-line CRISPR dependency records. I analyzed the dependency outcomes using direct subgroup mean comparisons, normal/Welch-style standard errors, driver-by-lineage interaction contrasts, and a systematic dependency-heterogeneity search over categorical predicates, binary molecular features, and quartile-thresholded continuous features. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger dependency.

Iteration 1 tested canonical oncogene-addiction hypotheses. These were strongly supported. `braf_v600e` lines had much more negative `dependency_BRAF` scores than other lines (mean -0.8047 vs -0.2495; effect -0.5552; p<1e-300). `egfr_amplification` lines had more negative `dependency_EGFR` (mean -0.6010 vs -0.2524; effect -0.3486; p<1e-300). `erbb2_amplification` lines had more negative `dependency_ERBB2` (mean -0.6856 vs -0.2535; effect -0.4321; p<1e-300). `kras_mutation` lines had more negative `dependency_KRAS` (mean -0.5594 vs -0.2520; effect -0.3075; p<1e-300).

Iteration 2 broadened the search. `nf1_loss` was associated with more negative `dependency_RIT1` (mean -0.3527 vs -0.2572; effect -0.0955; p<1e-300). Faster-growing lines, defined by top-quartile `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` (>=0.964), had more negative `dependency_POLR2A` (effect -0.1043), `dependency_TMED10` (effect -0.0369), and `dependency_DHX9` (effect -0.0371). These growth-rate effects were statistically strong but smaller than the direct oncogene-dependency effects.

Iteration 3 examined MYC-linked signals. `myc_amplification` and high `copy_number_8q24` both marked stronger `dependency_RPL11` (effects -0.1091 and -0.0988, respectively; p<1e-300). `myc_amplification` also had a smaller but significant association with `dependency_DHX9` (effect -0.0184; p=1.68e-5).

Iterations 4 and 5 identified and refined the dominant heterogeneous dependency pattern. `dependency_KIF18A` was much more negative in colorectal lines than non-colorectal lines (mean -0.7204 vs -0.2524; effect -0.4680; p<1e-300). Within colorectal lines, APC-mutant lines were far more dependent than APC-wildtype lines (mean -0.9425 vs -0.2592; effect -0.6833; p<1e-300). Within colorectal APC-mutant lines, top-quartile `wnt_activity_score` defined a still stronger subgroup (mean -1.1119 vs -0.2733; effect -0.8386; p<1e-300). The complete best-supported KIF18A subgroup required SMAD4 intactness: among colorectal, APC-mutant, high-WNT lines, `smad4_loss=0` had mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.2990, whereas `smad4_loss=1` had mean -0.2557 (effect -1.0433; p<1e-300). Thus `smad4_loss=1` appears to suppress the KIF18A dependency that is otherwise enabled by colorectal lineage, APC mutation, and high WNT activity.

Iteration 6 tested whether the main oncogene dependencies were restricted to plausible lineages. The BRAF V600E effect was not significantly stronger in skin lineage (interaction -0.0126; p=0.288), the EGFR amplification effect was not significantly stronger in brain lineage (interaction -0.0187; p=0.222), and the KRAS mutation effect was not stronger in pancreatic lineage (interaction -0.0028; p=0.794). ERBB2 was the exception: HER2-enriched subtype modestly strengthened the ERBB2-amplification dependency (interaction -0.0671; p=0.0398).

Iterations 7 and 8 performed the required systematic dependency-heterogeneity search for every dependency outcome. For `dependency_BRAF`, `dependency_EGFR`, `dependency_ERBB2`, `dependency_KRAS`, and `dependency_RIT1`, the greedy subgroups remained anchored on the expected driver variables (`braf_v600e`, `egfr_amplification`, `erbb2_amplification`, `kras_mutation`, and `nf1_loss`), with additional modifiers such as growth rate or culture features providing smaller refinements. For `dependency_KIF18A`, the search recovered the colorectal/APC-mutant/high-WNT subgroup, which was then improved by adding SMAD4 intactness. For `dependency_TMED10`, the strongest searched subgroup was weak and mostly growth/prostate/RB1-intact defined (effect -0.0649; p=5.3e-6). For `dependency_DHX9`, a high-growth, high-MYC-expression, low-mutation-burden subgroup showed a moderate signal (effect -0.0756; p=3.8e-9). For `dependency_POLR2A`, high proliferation was the clearest signal, with an adenocarcinoma/high-serum subgroup only modestly refining it. For `dependency_RPL11`, MYC amplification, high growth, and high stemness strengthened the dependency (searched subgroup effect -0.1512; p<1e-300).

Overall conclusions: the cleanest direct biomarker dependencies are BRAF/V600E, EGFR/amplification, ERBB2/amplification, KRAS/mutation, RIT1/NF1 loss, and RPL11/MYC amplification or 8q24 gain. Several outcomes also track proliferation-related screen biology, especially `dependency_POLR2A`, `dependency_TMED10`, and `dependency_DHX9`. The most biologically specific heterogeneous finding is `dependency_KIF18A`: it is concentrated in cell lines with `lineage=colorectal`, `apc_mutation=1`, high `wnt_activity_score`, and `smad4_loss=0`; the effect is essentially absent when `smad4_loss=1`.
