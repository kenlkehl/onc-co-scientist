Analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed 50,000 cancer cell-line CRISPR screen records with 10 dependency outcomes. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger selective dependency. For each dependency outcome I ran univariate comparisons, continuous-feature screens, and a systematic heterogeneity search over binary predicates, categorical levels, top/bottom quartile continuous predicates, compatible pairs, and triples extending the strongest pairs.

The clearest oncogene-matched dependencies were straightforward. Cell lines with braf_v600e=1 had much more negative dependency_BRAF scores than BRAF-wild-type lines (mean -0.804698 vs -0.249503; effect -0.555195; p<=1e-300). EGFR-amplified lines had more negative dependency_EGFR (mean -0.601046 vs -0.252407; effect -0.348638; p<=1e-300). ERBB2-amplified lines had more negative dependency_ERBB2 (mean -0.685595 vs -0.253473; effect -0.432122; p<=1e-300). KRAS-mutant lines had more negative dependency_KRAS (mean -0.559415 vs -0.251959; effect -0.307456; p<=1e-300).

The strongest discovered heterogeneity pattern was for dependency_KIF18A. Colorectal lineage alone was associated with stronger KIF18A dependency (mean -0.720354 vs -0.252397; effect -0.467957; p<=1e-300), but the dependency was concentrated in the complete subgroup lineage=colorectal, apc_mutation=1, and wnt_activity_score>=0.819. That subgroup had mean dependency_KIF18A -1.111866 versus -0.253014 outside it (effect -0.858852; p<=1e-300; n=3133). Partial matches to colorectal/APC/high-WNT had means near baseline, about -0.248 to -0.273, supporting the complete three-predicate definition rather than a simple main effect.

RIT1 showed a modest but consistent RAS-pathway relationship: nf1_loss=1 lines had more negative dependency_RIT1 (mean -0.352716 vs -0.257196; effect -0.095520; p<=1e-300). The strongest simple subgroup was nf1_loss=1 with lineage=brain (mean -0.404687 vs -0.264988; effect -0.139699; p=1.218e-13), suggesting lineage context amplifies the NF1/RIT1 signal.

TMED10, DHX9, and POLR2A looked more like broad fitness or screen-covariate dependencies than clean oncogene-specific effects. High growth_rate_doublings_per_day (>=0.964) was associated with more negative dependency_TMED10 (effect -0.03688; p<=1e-300) and dependency_POLR2A (effect -0.1043; p<=1e-300). The strongest TMED10 triple found by the screen, nf1_loss=1 plus high growth plus low rnaseq_ASCL2_log2_tpm, had effect -0.1022 (p=3.25e-8), but molecular main effects for TMED10 were weak. DHX9 was associated with high rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm (effect -0.02861; p=2.0e-15), and the high-growth/high-MYC subgroup had effect -0.05948 (p<=1e-300).

RPL11 showed a MYC/8q24-linked pattern. myc_amplification=1 had mean dependency_RPL11 -0.361392 versus -0.252335 in non-amplified lines (effect -0.109056; p<=1e-300), and top-quartile copy_number_8q24 had effect -0.09875. The strongest screened subgroup combined myc_amplification=1, high growth_rate_doublings_per_day, and pancreatic lineage (mean -0.4384 vs -0.2690; effect -0.1694; p=2.44e-15).

Overall, the data contain several expected gene-specific dependencies tied to their direct genomic alterations, one especially strong multivariable dependency for KIF18A in colorectal APC-mutant high-WNT cell lines, and several broader dependencies where growth rate and screen covariates explain much of the heterogeneity. The best-supported final subgroup hypothesis is the KIF18A association in lineage=colorectal, apc_mutation=1, wnt_activity_score>=0.819 cell lines.
