Dataset ds001_depmap contains 50000 cell-line records, 36 anonymized cell-line features, and 10 CRISPR dependency outcomes. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger dependency. I analyzed the local Parquet file only, using difference-in-means tests and a systematic subgroup search over categorical levels, binary features, and top/bottom quartiles of continuous features. For each dependency outcome, I also checked two-predicate subgroup definitions to look for heterogeneity beyond main effects.

The clearest driver-like main effects were large and highly significant. `feature_009 == 1` marked strong `dependency_BRAF` sensitivity: mean -0.804698 versus -0.249503 outside the group, effect -0.555195, p approximately 1e-300. `feature_022 == 1` similarly marked `dependency_EGFR` sensitivity: mean -0.601046 versus -0.252407, effect -0.348638, p approximately 1e-300. `feature_024 == 1` marked `dependency_ERBB2` sensitivity: mean -0.685595 versus -0.253473, effect -0.432122, p approximately 1e-300. `feature_014 == 1` marked `dependency_KRAS` sensitivity: mean -0.559415 versus -0.251959, effect -0.307456, p approximately 1e-300.

Lineage refinement did not improve those four driver-like dependencies as much as the main binary features. For example, `feature_009 == 1` plus skin lineage had strong `dependency_BRAF` sensitivity (effect -0.520456), but the broader `feature_009 == 1` split remained cleaner. The same pattern held for `dependency_EGFR` with brain lineage, `dependency_ERBB2` with breast or gastric lineage, and `dependency_KRAS` with pancreatic or colorectal lineage: all subgroup tests were significant, but the binary features were the main dependency-defining predicates.

`dependency_RIT1` had a weaker but clear main effect for `feature_027 == 1`: mean -0.352716 versus -0.257196, effect -0.095520, p approximately 1e-300. The heterogeneity search found stronger refined subgroups. The small `feature_027 == 1` and brain subgroup had mean -0.404687 versus -0.264988 outside, effect -0.139699, p=1.217e-13. A larger refined subgroup, `feature_027 == 1` and `feature_023 <= 4.230`, had mean -0.376141 versus -0.263323, effect -0.112818, p approximately 1e-300.

The strongest non-driver heterogeneous signal was for `dependency_KIF18A`. Colorectal lineage alone (`feature_011 == "colorectal"`) was strongly negative (mean -0.720354 versus -0.252397, effect -0.467957, p approximately 1e-300), and high `feature_028` alone was also negative (mean -0.469402 versus -0.252593, effect -0.216810, p approximately 1e-300). The two-by-two table showed the effect required both conditions: non-colorectal/low-`feature_028` mean -0.252267, non-colorectal/high-`feature_028` mean -0.252941, colorectal/low-`feature_028` mean -0.259003, and colorectal/high-`feature_028` mean -0.928802. The interaction contrast was -0.669125. Adding `feature_035 == 1` identified the strongest complete subgroup: `feature_011 == "colorectal"`, `feature_028 >= 0.819`, and `feature_035 == 1` had mean -1.111866 versus -0.253014 outside, effect -0.858852, p approximately 1e-300. Within colorectal/high-`feature_028` lines, `feature_035 == 1` had mean -1.111866 while `feature_035 == 0` had mean -0.271825, so `feature_035` appears to enable the dependency.

The broader essential-like outcomes showed smaller but reproducible dependencies. `dependency_TMED10` was modestly more negative in high-`feature_016` lines (`feature_016 >= 0.964`): mean -0.281605 versus -0.244729, effect -0.036876, p approximately 1e-300. The refined high-`feature_016` plus luminal subgroup (`feature_010 == "luminal"`) had effect -0.043545, p=8.138e-5. `dependency_DHX9` was also more negative in high-`feature_016` lines (effect -0.037084, p approximately 1e-300), with the strongest refined subgroup at high `feature_016` and high `feature_023` (`feature_023 >= 5.669`): effect -0.059477, p approximately 1e-300.

`dependency_POLR2A` had the strongest broad high-`feature_016` effect among this group: mean -0.497174 versus -0.392828, effect -0.104346, p approximately 1e-300. Pairwise subgroup refinements remained significant but did not improve meaningfully on the high-`feature_016` main effect. `dependency_RPL11` was instead most tied to high `feature_003` (`feature_003 >= 0.428`): mean -0.343967 versus -0.245214, effect -0.098753, p approximately 1e-300. The best refined subgroup combined high `feature_003` and high `feature_016`, with mean -0.376555 versus -0.261555, effect -0.114999, p approximately 1e-300.

Overall conclusions: the strongest dependency-specific patterns were `feature_009` for BRAF, `feature_022` for EGFR, `feature_024` for ERBB2, `feature_014` for KRAS, and a multivariable colorectal/high-`feature_028`/`feature_035 == 1` subgroup for KIF18A. RIT1 showed a weaker `feature_027` signal with low-`feature_023` heterogeneity. TMED10, DHX9, POLR2A, and RPL11 showed smaller but statistically robust associations with high `feature_016` or high `feature_003`, with selected two-feature refinements.
