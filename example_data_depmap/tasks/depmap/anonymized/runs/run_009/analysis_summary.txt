CRISPR dependency map analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed 50,000 cell-line records with 36 anonymized cell-line features and 10 dependency outcomes. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger dependency after knockout. I used Welch mean comparisons for categorical and quartile-defined numeric subgroups, continuous correlation checks for numeric features, and a systematic pairwise subgroup heterogeneity screen for every dependency outcome.

Strongest single-feature effects:
- dependency_BRAF was most strongly associated with feature_009 == 1: effect -0.5552, p <1e-12. feature_011 == skin was a secondary association with effect -0.1783, p <1e-12.
- dependency_EGFR was most strongly associated with feature_022 == 1: effect -0.3486, p <1e-12. feature_011 == brain was a weaker but significant association with effect -0.0552, p <1e-12.
- dependency_ERBB2 was most strongly associated with feature_024 == 1: effect -0.4321, p <1e-12. feature_011 == breast also had more negative scores, effect -0.0292, p 6.37e-9.
- dependency_KRAS was most strongly associated with feature_014 == 1: effect -0.3075, p <1e-12. feature_011 == pancreatic and feature_011 == colorectal were additional negative associations, effects -0.1127 and -0.05.

Other dependency outcomes:
- dependency_RIT1 was more negative in feature_027 == 1 lines: effect -0.0955, p <1e-12.
- dependency_KIF18A was more negative in feature_011 == colorectal lines: effect -0.468, p <1e-12.
- dependency_TMED10, dependency_DHX9, and dependency_POLR2A were more negative in the highest quartile of feature_016: effects -0.0369, -0.0371, and -0.1043, respectively.
- dependency_RPL11 was more negative in feature_018 == 1 lines: effect -0.1091, p <1e-12.

Interaction and heterogeneity findings:
- dependency_KIF18A showed the strongest interaction: feature_011 == colorectal plus feature_035 == 1 had subgroup-vs-rest effect -0.6899, interaction -0.6822, n=3925, p <1e-12.
- dependency_RIT1 was sharpened by feature_027 == 1 plus low feature_019: subgroup-vs-rest effect -0.1008, interaction -0.0103, n=1162, p <1e-12.
- dependency_DHX9 was strongest in high-feature_016 plus high-feature_023 lines: subgroup-vs-rest effect -0.0595, interaction -0.0133, n=3320, p <1e-12.
- dependency_RPL11 was strongest in feature_018 == 1 plus high-feature_016 lines: subgroup-vs-rest effect -0.1303, interaction -0.014, n=2557, p <1e-12.

Systematic dependency-heterogeneity search:
- dependency_BRAF: best two-predicate subgroup was feature_009 == 1 AND feature_030 == 0; subgroup-vs-rest effect -0.5514, interaction -0.0047, n=5200, p <1e-12. Best single predicate was feature_009 1, effect -0.5552, p <1e-12.

- dependency_KRAS: best two-predicate subgroup was feature_014 == 1 AND feature_009 == 0; subgroup-vs-rest effect -0.2988, interaction -0.0073, n=10701, p <1e-12. Best single predicate was feature_014 1, effect -0.3075, p <1e-12.
- dependency_RIT1: best two-predicate subgroup was feature_027 == 1 AND feature_019 in lowest quartile; subgroup-vs-rest effect -0.1008, interaction -0.0103, n=1162, p <1e-12. Best single predicate was feature_027 1, effect -0.0955, p <1e-12.
- dependency_KIF18A: best two-predicate subgroup was feature_011 == colorectal AND feature_035 == 1; subgroup-vs-rest effect -0.6899, interaction -0.6822, n=3925, p <1e-12. Best single predicate was feature_011 colorectal, effect -0.468, p <1e-12.

- dependency_RPL11: best two-predicate subgroup was feature_018 == 1 AND feature_016 in highest quartile; subgroup-vs-rest effect -0.1303, interaction -0.014, n=2557, p <1e-12. Best single predicate was feature_018 1, effect -0.1091, p <1e-12.

Overall conclusion:
The strongest signals are not merely broad main effects. BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, and KRAS each have a dominant binary feature association, with lineage-style feature_011 levels providing secondary context. KIF18A has a highly specific colorectal plus feature_035 == 1 dependency subgroup. RIT1, DHX9, POLR2A, TMED10, and RPL11 are tied to feature_027, feature_016, feature_018, and related quantitative feature strata, with the systematic search defining the best two-predicate subgroup for every outcome.
