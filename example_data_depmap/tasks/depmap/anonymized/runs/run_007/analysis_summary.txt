CRISPR dependency map analysis for ds001_depmap
Analyzed 50000 cell lines, 36 cell-line features, and 10 dependency outcomes. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger dependency after knockout.

Iteration 1 screened single-predicate main effects for BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, KRAS, and RIT1. The largest simple effects were all highly significant by large-sample mean comparisons.
Iteration 2 extended the single-feature scan to KIF18A, TMED10, DHX9, POLR2A, and RPL11 and paired those with continuous-feature regressions.

Iteration 10 recorded the final best-supported dependency subgroup for each outcome.

Overall conclusions: several dependency outcomes show very strong, statistically significant subgroup concentration rather than only broad main effects. The strongest rule-based subgroups are listed above; negative effects indicate stronger dependency in the subgroup. KIF18A behaves differently from the oncogene-like dependencies because its strongest simple signal is weaker and the best continuous association is modest, whereas BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, KRAS, RIT1, TMED10, DHX9, POLR2A, and RPL11 each have sharply significant subgroup or interaction structure in this synthetic DepMap-style cohort.
