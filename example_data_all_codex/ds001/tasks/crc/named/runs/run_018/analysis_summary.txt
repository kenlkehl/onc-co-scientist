Dataset ds001_crc analysis summary

Decoded 50000 records from dataset.parquet. Outcome pfs_months ranged from 0.00 to 16.25, with median 4.21 and IQR 2.72-5.74. Analyses used mean differences and linear regression coefficients on the natural month scale; p-values use large-sample normal approximations for t statistics.

Main clinical status effects: Stage IV disease and worse ECOG performance status were strongly associated with shorter PFS. Stage IV patients had lower mean PFS by 1.35 months, and each ECOG point was associated with -1.19 months. In the all-variable model, older age was positively associated with PFS (0.18 months per year), while weight loss was adverse (-0.08 months per percentage point).

Tumor biology: KRAS mutation, BRAF V600E, and right-sided primary tumors were adverse in unadjusted and adjusted analyses. MSI-high status was not meaningfully associated with PFS in this cohort. The BRAF-by-right-sided-primary interaction was significant and positive, indicating that the BRAF adverse association was attenuated rather than amplified among right-sided tumors.

Treatments: The dominant treatment finding was a positive adjusted association for treatment_regorafenib (about 0.94 months in the all-variable model). Pembrolizumab, cetuximab, bevacizumab, encorafenib, and trastuzumab/tucatinib were near-null after adjustment, and the biomarker-matched interaction hypotheses for MSI-high/pembrolizumab, BRAF/encorafenib, and HER2/trastuzumab-tucatinib were not statistically supported.

Inflammation, nutrition, and disease burden: Higher albumin was favorable, while weight loss, CEA, LDH, and alkaline phosphatase were adverse on the natural month scale. CRP was weakly negative and only borderline/weakly significant depending on adjustment, and NLR did not support the hypothesized adverse direction. Liver and bone metastasis indicators were not significant in either the unadjusted or adjusted tests performed here.

Comorbidities and treatment safety subgroups: The autoimmune-disease pembrolizumab subgroup and pembrolizumab-by-autoimmune interaction were not significant. In the all-variable scan, COPD and pericardial effusion had small negative adjusted associations, but the dominant signals remained clinical status, age, weight loss, selected tumor markers, albumin, CEA, LDH, and regorafenib.

The all-variable model included 73 predictors and achieved R-squared 0.859. The most statistically prominent adjusted coefficients included: age_years 0.18 months, ecog_ps -1.17 months, stage_iv -1.36 months, treatment_regorafenib 0.94 months, weight_loss_pct_6mo -0.08 months, albumin_g_dl 0.47 months, kras_mutation -0.35 months, right_sided_primary -0.29 months, cea_ng_ml -0.01 months, braf_v600e -0.36 months, ldh_u_l -0.00 months, copd -0.03 months.

Overall, the cohort shows strong independent associations for stage, ECOG performance status, age, cachexia/weight loss, albumin, CEA, LDH, KRAS, BRAF, right-sided primary, and treatment_regorafenib. Many clinically plausible biomarker-matched treatment interactions were refuted or remained null in this dataset, so the clearest treatment-biomarker conclusion is absence of detectable interaction rather than confirmed matched-therapy benefit.
