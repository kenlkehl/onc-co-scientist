Dataset ds001_crc, n=50000. Outcome was pfs_months (mean 4.312, SD 2.293). I used linear regression with pfs_months on its natural month scale. Main-effect models adjusted for demographics, ECOG, stage, sidedness, key biomarkers, selected labs, treatments, metastatic indicators, and comorbidities. Interaction models added the two main effects and their product to baseline clinicopathologic covariates and treatment indicators. Coefficients below are adjusted month differences.

Strong prognostic main effects emerged. Higher age_years was unexpectedly associated with longer PFS: +1.758 months per 10 years (p=<1e-99). Worse ecog_ps was strongly adverse (-1.171 months per point, p=<1e-99), and stage_iv was also strongly adverse (-1.361 months, p=<1e-99). Right-sided primary, kras_mutation, and braf_v600e were each independently associated with shorter PFS (-0.286, -0.354, and -0.356 months respectively; all significant). nras_mutation, msi_high, her2_amplified, and sex_female were not significant after adjustment.

Laboratory and clinical state markers supported a nutrition/inflammation pattern. Higher albumin_g_dl was associated with longer PFS (+0.469 months per 1 g/dL, p=<1e-99). Higher weight_loss_pct_6mo was associated with shorter PFS (-0.381 months per 5 percentage points, p=<1e-99). Higher cea_ng_ml and ldh_u_l were independently adverse (-0.050 months per 10 ng/mL CEA and -0.042 months per 100 U/L LDH). crp_mg_l was only borderline and very small (-0.008 months per 10 mg/L, p=4.818e-2).

Treatment main effects were narrow. treatment_regorafenib had a large positive adjusted association with PFS (+0.943 months, p=<1e-99). In contrast, treatment_cetuximab, treatment_bevacizumab, treatment_pembrolizumab, treatment_encorafenib, and treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib did not show statistically significant adjusted main effects.

Expected biomarker-treatment interactions were not supported. The treatment_pembrolizumab x msi_high interaction was small and nonsignificant (+0.031 months, p=5.589e-1). Cetuximab interactions with kras_mutation, nras_mutation, and right_sided_primary were nonsignificant. Encorafenib did not show a larger association in braf_v600e tumors, trastuzumab/tucatinib did not show a larger association in her2_amplified tumors, and bevacizumab did not show meaningful heterogeneity by stage_iv or liver_mets.

Some non-treatment heterogeneity was statistically detectable. The regorafenib association became smaller with worse ECOG (-0.053 months per ECOG point, p=7.125e-4), while regorafenib interactions with stage_iv and age_years were not significant at 0.05. The positive age association was attenuated in stage_iv disease (-0.068 months per decade interaction, p=2.450e-15) and at higher ecog_ps (-0.073 months per decade-by-ECOG interaction, p=1.597e-31). The ecog_ps penalty was slightly less severe in stage_iv patients (+0.043 months, p=6.797e-4). KRAS and right-sided primary showed a strong positive interaction (+0.511 months, p=<1e-99), meaning their combined decrement was less than additive. BRAF V600E's negative association was also attenuated in right-sided primaries (+0.260 months, p=1.024e-9).

Metastatic and comorbidity signals were mostly small. pericardial_effusion was associated with a modestly lower PFS (-0.045 months, p=2.246e-2), but liver_mets and bone_mets were not significant in the adjusted model. COPD had a small adverse association (-0.026 months, p=2.113e-3), while chronic_kidney_disease and heart_failure were not significant. Overall, the cohort was dominated by strong baseline prognostic effects and a large regorafenib association; most canonical treatment-biomarker predictive effects were absent or too small to detect in these data.
