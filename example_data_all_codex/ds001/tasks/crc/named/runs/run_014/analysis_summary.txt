Dataset ds001_crc contains 50000 colorectal cancer cohort records with no missing values in the supplied columns. I modeled `pfs_months` using adjusted ordinary least squares. The main-effect model included all non-identifier covariates; continuous predictors were standardized to 1-SD units except `ecog_ps`, which was modeled per point. The adjusted main-effect model had R^2=0.859 and residual SD=0.862 months. Interaction tests added the stated interaction to that same adjusted model; the regorafenib interaction results below come from a simultaneous interaction model containing all five highlighted regorafenib modifiers.

The clearest prognostic signals were not subtle. Older `age_years` was associated with longer PFS (+1.750 months (SE 0.004, p=<1e-300) per SD), while `stage_iv` (-1.361 months (SE 0.008, p=<1e-300)), higher `ecog_ps` (-1.171 months (SE 0.006, p=<1e-300) per point), greater `weight_loss_pct_6mo` (-0.293 months (SE 0.004, p=<1e-300) per SD), higher `cea_ng_ml` (-0.081 months (SE 0.004, p=6.84e-98) per SD), and higher `ldh_u_l` (-0.034 months (SE 0.004, p=5.54e-19) per SD) were associated with shorter PFS. Higher `albumin_g_dl` was favorable (+0.235 months (SE 0.004, p=<1e-300) per SD). Molecular and anatomic adverse markers were also supported: `kras_mutation` (-0.354 months (SE 0.008, p=<1e-300)), `right_sided_primary` (-0.287 months (SE 0.008, p=1.13e-275)), and `braf_v600e` (-0.355 months (SE 0.019, p=7.31e-79)) each independently predicted shorter PFS.

Treatment main effects were dominated by `treatment_regorafenib`, which had a large positive adjusted association with PFS (+0.943 months (SE 0.010, p=<1e-300)). The other treatment main-effect hypotheses were unsupported at p<0.05: treatment_cetuximab -0.011 months (SE 0.008, p=0.189); treatment_bevacizumab +0.002 months (SE 0.008, p=0.797); treatment_pembrolizumab +0.000 months (SE 0.011, p=0.992); treatment_encorafenib +0.007 months (SE 0.013, p=0.605); treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib +0.026 months (SE 0.014, p=0.0718).

The strongest heterogeneity pattern was specific to regorafenib. In the simultaneous interaction model, regorafenib-associated PFS was significantly smaller for `kras_mutation` (-1.763 months (SE 0.017, p=<1e-300)), `right_sided_primary` (-1.476 months (SE 0.017, p=<1e-300)), higher `cea_ng_ml` (-0.402 months (SE 0.008, p=<1e-300) per SD), and `braf_v600e` (-1.759 months (SE 0.039, p=<1e-300)). The `nras_mutation` interaction was positive but not statistically significant (+0.075 months (SE 0.047, p=0.110)). This suggests the headline regorafenib association is concentrated in lower-CEA, KRAS-wild-type, left-sided, BRAF-wild-type disease; evidence for a distinct NRAS-mutated enhancement was insufficient after simultaneous adjustment.

Expected biomarker-treatment interactions for other targeted or immune therapies were not supported. The adjusted interactions were nonsignificant for `treatment_pembrolizumab:msi_high` (+0.058 months (SE 0.049, p=0.232)), `treatment_encorafenib:braf_v600e` (-0.018 months (SE 0.060, p=0.770)), `treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib:her2_amplified` (-0.002 months (SE 0.083, p=0.984)), `treatment_cetuximab:kras_mutation` (+0.017 months (SE 0.017, p=0.317)), `treatment_cetuximab:right_sided_primary` (+0.024 months (SE 0.018, p=0.169)), and `treatment_bevacizumab:liver_mets` (-0.004 months (SE 0.022, p=0.840)). Overall, the supported pattern is a strong prognostic backbone plus a large, heterogeneous regorafenib association; the commonly expected MSI-pembrolizumab, BRAF-encorafenib, HER2-trastuzumab/tucatinib, KRAS/right-sided-cetuximab, and liver-metastasis-bevacizumab interaction hypotheses were refuted in these adjusted analyses.
