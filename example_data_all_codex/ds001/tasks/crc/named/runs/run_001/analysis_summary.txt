Dataset ds001_crc contained 50,000 colorectal cancer records with no missing values and the outcome `pfs_months`. I decoded the local Parquet file and used linear models and subgroup mean contrasts to test main effects, treatment-biomarker interactions, and selected feature-feature interactions across 25 iterations.

The strongest baseline prognostic signals were consistent and highly significant. `stage_iv` was associated with substantially shorter PFS (-1.361 months adjusted, p<1e-300), and each one-point increase in `ecog_ps` was associated with -1.171 months (p<1e-300). Higher `weight_loss_pct_6mo` also predicted shorter PFS (-0.076 months per percentage point, p<1e-300), while higher `albumin_g_dl` predicted longer PFS (+0.469 months per g/dL, p<1e-300). Higher tumor or disease-burden markers were adverse: `cea_ng_ml` had an adjusted slope of -0.0050 months per ng/mL (p=8.09e-98), `ldh_u_l` had a smaller negative slope (-0.00043 months per U/L, p=5.78e-19), and `crp_mg_l` was weakly but significantly negative (-0.00081 months per mg/L, p=0.0488). The age association was unusual but clear in this dataset: each additional year of `age_years` was associated with +0.176 months of PFS after adjustment (p<1e-300), with a small negative `age_years` by `ecog_ps` interaction showing that the positive age slope was weaker at worse performance status.

Several molecular and anatomic features were independently adverse. `kras_mutation` was associated with -0.354 months of PFS, `right_sided_primary` with -0.287 months, and `braf_v600e` with -0.355 months after adjustment; all were highly significant. A positive `kras_mutation` by `right_sided_primary` interaction (+0.452 months, p=1.58e-25) suggested their joint adverse effect was subadditive rather than simply the sum of two independent penalties.

Treatment analyses were dominated by `treatment_regorafenib`. In an adjusted main-effect model without interactions, regorafenib was associated with +0.943 months of PFS (p<1e-300). Follow-up interaction models showed that this average association was highly heterogeneous. In a joint model, the regorafenib association was reduced by `kras_mutation` (-1.763 months interaction), `right_sided_primary` (-1.475 months), `braf_v600e` (-1.760 months), and increasing `cea_ng_ml` (-0.0249 months per ng/mL); all were overwhelmingly significant. The initially suggested `nras_mutation` interaction did not remain significant after accounting for those modifiers (+0.0749 months, p=0.11). A refined subgroup analysis confirmed concentration of the regorafenib signal: among patients with `kras_mutation=0`, `right_sided_primary=0`, `braf_v600e=0`, and `cea_ng_ml<50`, regorafenib-treated patients had mean PFS of 6.945 months versus 4.117 months without regorafenib, a +2.828 month difference (p<1e-300). In the complementary adverse composite group, the unadjusted regorafenib difference was only +0.032 months and was not significant (p=0.257).

Other expected targeted-treatment biomarker relationships were not supported in these data. `treatment_cetuximab` had no adjusted positive main effect (-0.011 months, p=0.191), and its RAS/sidedness interactions were not significant in screening. The `treatment_pembrolizumab` by `msi_high` interaction was small and not significant (+0.057 months, p=0.237). The `treatment_encorafenib` by `braf_v600e` interaction was null to slightly negative (-0.017 months, p=0.777). The `treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib` by `her2_amplified` interaction was essentially zero (+0.0027 months, p=0.974). `treatment_bevacizumab` did not show liver-metastasis-specific heterogeneity (-0.0039 months interaction, p=0.859).

Overall, the cohort shows a strong prognostic backbone driven by stage, ECOG performance status, age, weight loss, albumin, CEA, KRAS mutation, right-sided primary location, BRAF V600E, and LDH. The most clinically meaningful multivariable treatment pattern is not a broad pan-cohort regorafenib association, but a sharply modified one: higher PFS with regorafenib is concentrated among KRAS wild-type, left-sided, BRAF V600E-negative, lower-CEA patients and is largely absent or attenuated in KRAS-mutated, right-sided, BRAF V600E-positive, or high-CEA groups. Expected pembrolizumab/MSI-high, encorafenib/BRAF, trastuzumab-tucatinib/HER2, cetuximab/RAS-sidedness, and bevacizumab/liver-metastasis patterns were not statistically supported in this dataset.
