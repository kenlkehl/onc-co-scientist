Dataset ds001_crc: 50,000 colorectal cancer records with pfs_months as the outcome.

Overall mean pfs_months was 4.31 months. I used ordinary least squares models with pfs_months on its natural month scale; continuous laboratory/tumor-burden markers were standardized when used as main-effect covariates. Interaction models included the two main effects plus their product and adjusted for age_years, sex_female, ecog_ps, stage_iv, right_sided_primary, cea_ng_ml, albumin_g_dl, ldh_u_l, weight_loss_pct_6mo, crp_mg_l, nlr, liver_mets, bone_mets, and pleural_effusion.

Supported baseline and biomarker effects:
- Higher age_years was unexpectedly associated with higher pfs_months (1.7522 months per SD, p=<1e-250), while higher ecog_ps and stage_iv were strongly adverse (-0.7977 months per SD and -1.3588 months; both p=<1e-250 or smaller).
- right_sided_primary was adverse after adjustment (-0.2903 months, p=<1e-250).
- kras_mutation and braf_v600e were independently adverse (effects -0.3533 and -0.3514 months; both p=<1e-250 or smaller).
- msi_high did not show an independent favorable association after adjustment (-0.0107 months, p=0.578).
- Albumin was favorable (0.2323 months per SD, p=<1e-250), while weight_loss_pct_6mo, cea_ng_ml, and ldh_u_l were adverse (-0.2921, -0.0816, and -0.0324 months per SD; all significant).
- The hypothesized independent adverse effects of crp_mg_l and nlr were not supported after adjustment (effects -0.0048 and 0.0064 months per SD; p=0.258 and p=0.136).

Treatment effects and interactions:
- treatment_regorafenib was the only large treatment main effect I found: it was associated with 0.9416 higher months of pfs_months (p=<1e-250).
- That regorafenib association was smaller at higher ecog_ps, with a treatment_regorafenib:ecog_ps interaction of -0.0388 months per SD increase in ECOG (p=7.71e-5).
- Main effects for treatment_cetuximab, treatment_bevacizumab, and treatment_pembrolizumab were small and not clinically meaningful in the adjusted models (-0.0116, 0.0027, and 0.0031 months).
- Biomarker-matched treatment interactions were not statistically supported: pembrolizumab:msi_high 0.0514 months (p=0.340), encorafenib:braf_v600e 0.0102 months (p=0.879), and trastuzumab/tucatinib:her2_amplified 0.0137 months (p=0.882).
- Cetuximab attenuation hypotheses were also unsupported: treatment_cetuximab:kras_mutation 0.0246 months (p=0.187) and treatment_cetuximab:right_sided_primary 0.0212 months (p=0.279).
- Safety/comorbidity interaction ideas did not pass significance: treatment_bevacizumab:venous_thromboembolism_history -0.0383 months (p=0.288) and treatment_pembrolizumab:autoimmune_disease -0.0564 months (p=0.302).

Overall conclusion: the cohort shows strong baseline prognostic structure, especially age_years, ecog_ps, stage_iv, right_sided_primary, KRAS/BRAF mutation status, albumin, weight loss, CEA, and LDH. The only large treatment signal was a positive regorafenib association, attenuated in patients with worse ECOG performance status. Most expected biomarker-matched treatment interactions were small and statistically null in this dataset.
