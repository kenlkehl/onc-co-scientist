Analysis summary for ds001_crc

I analyzed 50000 colorectal cancer records with no missing values. The outcome was `pfs_months`. Statistical tests used ordinary least-squares models; continuous laboratory variables were modeled per one standard deviation unless otherwise stated, and the primary adjusted model included `age_years`, `sex_female`, `ecog_ps`, `stage_iv`, `right_sided_primary`, KRAS/NRAS/BRAF/MSI/HER2/NTRK biomarkers, CEA, albumin, LDH, weight loss, CRP, NLR, liver/bone/adrenal/pleural/pericardial metastasis or effusion indicators, and treatment indicators. Interaction tests added a treatment-by-subgroup product term to the same covariate set. P-values are nominal two-sided normal-approximation p-values from the OLS coefficient tests.

The adjusted main-effect model explained most of the observed variation in PFS (R^2=0.859, residual SD=0.862 months). The strongest supported positive associations were older age and regorafenib treatment: a 10-year increase in `age_years` was associated with +1.758 months of PFS (p<1e-300), and `treatment_regorafenib` was associated with +0.943 months (p<1e-300). Higher `albumin_g_dl` was also favorable (+0.235 months per SD, p<1e-300).

Several baseline disease and illness-severity markers were adverse. Higher `ecog_ps` was associated with -1.171 months per point (p<1e-300), `stage_iv` with -1.361 months (p<1e-300), `right_sided_primary` with -0.287 months (p=3.77e-276), `kras_mutation` with -0.354 months (p<1e-300), and `braf_v600e` with -0.356 months (p=2.24e-79). Higher `weight_loss_pct_6mo` (-0.294 months per SD), `cea_ng_ml` (-0.081), and `ldh_u_l` (-0.034) were also significant adverse markers. `pericardial_effusion` had a small adverse association (-0.045 months, p=0.022).

Several clinically plausible hypotheses were not supported after adjustment. `msi_high` was not independently favorable (-0.009 months, p=0.627). `crp_mg_l` was borderline but not significant (-0.007 months per SD, p=0.054), and `nlr` did not show the expected adverse effect (+0.007 months per SD, p=0.083). `liver_mets` and `bone_mets` were also not significant in the multivariable model (p=0.272 and p=0.087, respectively), suggesting their unadjusted associations were largely absorbed by stage, performance status, biomarkers, and laboratory markers.

Treatment-biomarker interaction tests mostly refuted expected precision-oncology patterns in this dataset. There was no significant evidence that cetuximab benefit differed by `kras_mutation` (+0.017 months, p=0.305) or `right_sided_primary` (+0.024 months, p=0.172). Pembrolizumab did not show a statistically significant larger association in `msi_high` tumors (+0.058 months, p=0.232), encorafenib did not show larger association in `braf_v600e` tumors (-0.017 months, p=0.775), and trastuzumab/tucatinib did not show a significant HER2-amplified interaction (+0.002 months, p=0.978). Bevacizumab did not vary detectably by liver metastases (-0.004 months, p=0.850).

The main exception among interaction tests involved regorafenib. The regorafenib association was not significantly larger in stage IV disease (+0.035 months, p=0.075), but it was significantly smaller at higher albumin (-0.039 months per SD higher `albumin_g_dl`, p=5.19e-5). This is consistent with a larger regorafenib-associated PFS difference among patients with lower albumin, although the observational design means this should be interpreted as association rather than treatment efficacy.

Overall, this cohort showed strong adjusted prognostic structure: age was unexpectedly favorable, ECOG and stage IV were strongly adverse, right-sided primary and KRAS/BRAF alterations were adverse, nutritional/inflammatory tumor-burden markers mattered, and regorafenib had the dominant treatment-associated signal. Most anticipated biomarker-specific treatment interactions were not statistically supported in these data.
