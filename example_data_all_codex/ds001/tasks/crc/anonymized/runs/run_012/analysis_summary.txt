Oncology cohort ds001_crc analysis summary

Data and approach
The dataset contained 50,000 patient records. I excluded the identifier column and analyzed 73 opaque features against the continuous outcome `pfs_months` (mean 4.312 months, SD 2.293). I first screened main effects with univariate linear regressions or binary mean comparisons, then fit adjusted OLS models and targeted interaction/subgroup analyses for the strongest signals. P-values reported as 1e-300 in transcript.json indicate values below numerical precision for these sample sizes.

Main effects supported by the evidence
The clearest favorable main effect was `feature_041`: each 1-unit increase was associated with +0.1765 months of `pfs_months` univariately and +0.1758 months after adjustment, both highly significant. `feature_050` was also favorable, with an adjusted +0.4692 months per unit.

The largest adverse main effects were `feature_025` and `feature_036`. `feature_025` showed a monotone dose pattern: mean `pfs_months` fell from 5.271 at level 0 to 4.054 at level 1 and 2.908 at level 2; the adjusted coefficient was -1.1713 months per level. Patients with `feature_036 = 1` had substantially lower outcome than `feature_036 = 0`, with an adjusted -1.3615 month difference.

Several additional adverse effects were supported. `feature_056` remained negative after adjustment (-0.0761 months per unit), as did `feature_001 = 1` (-0.3539 months), `feature_015 = 1` (-0.2867 months), `feature_029` (-0.0050 months per unit), `feature_066 = 1` (-0.3565 months), and `feature_028` (-0.000425 months per unit). The individual magnitude for `feature_028` was small, but it remained statistically significant.

The main favorable binary marker was `feature_043`. Univariately, `feature_043 = 1` patients had +0.9722 months higher mean `pfs_months`; after adjustment, the coefficient remained +0.9429 months. That strong main effect motivated subgroup analyses.

Subgroup heterogeneity and interactions
The `feature_043` association was highly heterogeneous. Among `feature_001 = 0` patients, `feature_043 = 1` was associated with +1.656 months, but among `feature_001 = 1` patients it was only +0.027 months; the adjusted `feature_001 * feature_043` interaction was -1.7762 months. `feature_015` showed a similar pattern: +1.480 months when `feature_015 = 0`, +0.026 months when `feature_015 = 1`, with an adjusted interaction of -1.4726 months. `feature_066` also attenuated or reversed the `feature_043` benefit: +1.027 months when `feature_066 = 0` versus -0.156 months when `feature_066 = 1`, with an adjusted interaction of -1.7651 months.

There was also interaction among adverse markers. The joint effect of `feature_001 = 1` and `feature_015 = 1` was less adverse than additivity would predict; the adjusted interaction was +0.5005 months. For `feature_041` and `feature_036`, the positive `feature_041` slope was slightly weaker when `feature_036 = 1`: +0.1804 months/unit for `feature_036 = 0` versus +0.1733 for `feature_036 = 1`, with an adjusted interaction of -0.0070 months per unit.

Hypotheses refuted or weakened by adjustment
Some univariate signals did not survive multivariable adjustment. `feature_042 = 1` looked favorable univariately (+0.221 months), but its adjusted main-effect coefficient was only +0.0208 months with p=0.362. Its apparent amplification of the `feature_043` effect was also not robust after adjustment: the adjusted `feature_042 * feature_043` interaction was +0.0671 months with p=0.157. `feature_039` and `feature_012` were nominally negative in univariate screening, but adjusted coefficients were essentially null (`feature_039` +0.000023, p=0.768; `feature_012` -0.005166, p=0.672).

Overall conclusion
The cohort contains a strong multivariable treatment-biomarker-outcome structure despite opaque feature names. The dominant favorable signals are higher `feature_041`, higher `feature_050`, and `feature_043 = 1`. The dominant adverse signals are higher `feature_025`, `feature_036 = 1`, higher `feature_056`, and the binary markers `feature_001`, `feature_015`, and `feature_066`. The most clinically meaningful heterogeneity is that `feature_043` is strongly favorable only outside the `feature_001`, `feature_015`, and `feature_066` positive subgroups; within those subgroups, the benefit is largely absent. Smaller nominal effects should be interpreted through adjusted models because several disappeared after accounting for the dominant predictors.
