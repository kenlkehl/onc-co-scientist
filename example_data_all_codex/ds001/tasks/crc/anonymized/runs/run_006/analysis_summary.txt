Oncology dataset ds001_crc analysis summary

I analyzed 50,000 records with outcome pfs_months. The outcome mean was 4.3116 months with standard deviation 2.2930 months. All analyses used dataset.parquet in this directory only. P-values are large-sample OLS/t-test normal approximations on the natural pfs_months scale.

Main effects: the strongest favorable continuous marker was feature_041. Each one-unit increase in feature_041 corresponded to 0.1765 months higher pfs_months. feature_050 was also favorable (0.4654 months per unit), and feature_043=1 was associated with 0.9722 months higher mean pfs_months.

Adverse main effects were concentrated in feature_025, feature_036, feature_015, feature_001, feature_029, feature_056, and feature_066. The largest binary mean shift was feature_036=1 versus 0 at -1.3520 months. Each ordinal step of feature_025 was associated with -1.1897 months lower pfs_months, and each one-unit increase in feature_029 was associated with -0.0053 months lower pfs_months.

Adjustment: in a model containing the 12 strongest univariate predictors, the core signals retained their directions and remained statistically significant: feature_041, feature_025, feature_036, feature_015, feature_001, feature_029, feature_050, feature_056, feature_043, and feature_066. By contrast, feature_042 and feature_039 weakened after adjustment, suggesting their univariate associations were largely explained by other predictors.

Interactions and heterogeneity: feature_041 had weaker favorable slopes in adverse strata. The feature_041:feature_025 interaction was -0.0072 months per unit-level product, and feature_041:feature_036 was -0.0071 months per unit-product. This indicates that the favorable feature_041 association was attenuated as feature_025 increased and when feature_036=1.

The feature_015:feature_001 interaction was positive, meaning their combined adverse pattern was less negative than expected from a purely additive model. The interaction coefficient was 0.4517 months. In contrast, feature_043's favorable association was reduced by feature_015, feature_001, and feature_066, with interaction coefficients of -1.4541, -1.6288, and -1.1829 months respectively.

For continuous-by-binary heterogeneity, feature_029 was more adverse among feature_043=1 patients (feature_029:feature_043 interaction -0.0253 months per feature_029 unit) and less adverse among feature_001=1 patients (feature_001:feature_029 interaction 0.0073 months per feature_029 unit).

A total of 12 of 73 feature columns passed BH q<0.05 in univariate screening: feature_041, feature_025, feature_036, feature_015, feature_001, feature_029, feature_050, feature_056, feature_043, feature_066, feature_042, feature_039. feature_028 had a nominal p-value but BH q=0.0705, so I treated it as weak and not robust to false-discovery control. Low-ranked binary candidates feature_034, feature_023, and feature_057 were not statistically supported in univariate testing.

Overall conclusion: this cohort contains strong prognostic structure for pfs_months. Longer progression-free survival is associated most clearly with higher feature_041, higher feature_050, and feature_043=1. Shorter progression-free survival is associated with higher feature_025, feature_036=1, feature_015=1, feature_001=1, higher feature_029, higher feature_056, and feature_066=1. The clinically meaningful heterogeneity is not just main effects: adverse strata attenuate feature_041's favorable slope, and feature_043 and feature_029 show several important subgroup-dependent effects.
