Dataset `ds001_crc` contains 50,000 rows with 73 opaque patient features and the continuous outcome `pfs_months`. I analyzed `pfs_months` using marginal screens, an all-feature multivariable OLS model, adjusted one-at-a-time interaction tests, and adjusted quadratic terms. `feature_025` was modeled as indicators for levels 1 and 2 versus level 0. The cohort mean `pfs_months` was 4.31 months (SD 2.29).

The dominant adjusted main effect was `feature_041`: each 1-unit increase was associated with +0.1758 months of `pfs_months` (p<1e-300). `feature_025` showed a strong ordered negative relationship: compared with `feature_025`=0, level 1 was -1.1956 months and level 2 was -2.3291 months lower (both p<1e-300). `feature_036`=1 was also strongly unfavorable (-1.3616 months, p<1e-300), while `feature_043`=1 was favorable (+0.9428 months, p<1e-300).

Additional independent main effects were smaller but statistically clear. Higher `feature_056` was unfavorable (-0.0761 months per unit, p<1e-300), higher `feature_050` was favorable (+0.4694 months per unit, p<1e-300), `feature_001`=1 was unfavorable (-0.3544 months, p<1e-300), `feature_015`=1 was unfavorable (-0.2866 months, p=1.07e-275), higher `feature_029` was unfavorable (-0.00501 months per unit, p=5.01e-98), `feature_066`=1 was unfavorable (-0.3556 months, p=5.87e-79), and higher `feature_028` had a small unfavorable association (-0.000426 months per unit, p=5.15e-19). By contrast, the hypothesized adjusted association for `feature_037` was not supported (+0.0106 months, p=0.173).

The strongest subgroup heterogeneity centered on `feature_043`. Its favorable association was nearly absent in patients with `feature_001`=1: the adjusted `feature_001` by `feature_043` interaction was -1.6324 months (p<1e-300), and unadjusted subgroup means showed a +1.656 month `feature_043` difference when `feature_001`=0 versus +0.027 months when `feature_001`=1. A similar attenuation occurred for `feature_015`=1, with an adjusted interaction of -1.4627 months (p<1e-300). Higher `feature_029` also weakened the `feature_043` association (-0.0258 months per unit interaction, p<1e-300), as did `feature_066`=1 (-0.9887 months, p=4.03e-104). In contrast, `feature_042`=1 amplified the favorable `feature_043` association (+0.8879 months, p=1.58e-57).

Other interactions suggested nonadditive risk structure. The joint effect of `feature_015`=1 and `feature_001`=1 was less negative than expected from their separate main effects (+0.5124 month interaction, p=2.44e-219). The unfavorable association of `feature_029` was attenuated among patients with `feature_001`=1 (+0.00801 months per unit interaction, p=5.16e-62) and among patients with `feature_015`=1 (+0.00773, p=3.97e-54). The negative association of `feature_036` became stronger at higher `feature_041` (-0.00694 months per unit interaction, p=5.66e-19), while the favorable association of `feature_050` became stronger at higher `feature_041` (+0.00389, p=4.88e-7). Among patients with `feature_025`=2, the favorable `feature_041` slope was weaker than in `feature_025`=0 patients (-0.01435 interaction, p=1.33e-40).

Nonlinear checks found convex adjusted relationships for `feature_041` and `feature_029`. The centered-squared `feature_041` term was positive (+0.000671, p=1.56e-124), indicating the positive association with `pfs_months` strengthened at higher `feature_041` values. The centered-squared `feature_029` term was also positive (+0.0000186, p=2.99e-43), suggesting the negative linear association attenuated at high `feature_029` values.

Overall, this cohort has a strong multivariable outcome structure dominated by `feature_041`, `feature_025`, `feature_036`, and `feature_043`, with clinically meaningful subgroup heterogeneity around `feature_043` and modifying features `feature_001`, `feature_015`, `feature_029`, `feature_066`, and `feature_042`. Most other features were weak or nonsignificant after adjustment, and at least one plausible binary-feature hypothesis (`feature_037`) was refuted.
