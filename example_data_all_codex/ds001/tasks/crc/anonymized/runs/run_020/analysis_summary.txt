Dataset ds001_crc contained 50000 patients and one outcome, `pfs_months`. The decoded outcome distribution had mean 4.3116 months and range 0 to 16.2501 months. I used large-sample linear analyses: binary features were tested as mean differences, continuous or ordinal features as OLS slopes, multivariable effects as adjusted OLS coefficients, and interactions as centered product terms. Statistical significance was assessed at p<0.05.

The strongest marginal association was `feature_041`. Each one-unit increase in `feature_041` was associated with +0.1765 months of `pfs_months` (p<1e-300), and this remained +0.1758 months after adjustment for the other leading features. Several adverse main effects were also large and stable: `feature_025` was -1.1897 months per level marginally and -1.1715 months adjusted; `feature_036=1` was -1.3520 months marginally and -1.3614 months adjusted; `feature_056` was -0.0730 months per unit marginally and -0.0761 months adjusted. Favorable main effects included `feature_043=1` (+0.9722 months marginally, +0.9429 months adjusted) and `feature_050` (+0.4654 months per unit marginally, +0.4695 months adjusted).

Secondary main effects were directionally coherent. `feature_001=1` and `feature_015=1` were associated with lower PFS (-0.3268 and -0.3052 months marginally; -0.3545 and -0.2868 months adjusted). Higher `feature_029` was associated with lower PFS (-0.00535 months per unit marginally, -0.00500 adjusted). `feature_066=1` had a smaller adverse marginal association (-0.2279 months, p=3.24e-7) but a stronger adjusted coefficient (-0.3567 months, p<1e-300). `feature_042=1` was favorable marginally (+0.2211 months, p=0.000714), while `feature_039` and `feature_028` had statistically significant but very small negative slopes.

Interaction testing showed that the favorable `feature_043=1` subgroup was highly context-dependent. Its association with PFS was attenuated by `feature_001=1` (adjusted interaction -1.6311 months), `feature_015=1` (-1.4611 months), higher `feature_029` (-0.0258 months per `feature_029` unit), and `feature_066=1` (-0.9892 months), all with p<1e-300. In contrast, `feature_043=1` and `feature_042=1` had a positive adjusted interaction (+0.8881 months, p<1e-300), suggesting a subgroup where the favorable `feature_043` pattern was amplified.

Other interactions refined the main effects. The positive `feature_041` slope was weaker at higher `feature_025` levels (interaction -0.00729 months, p<1e-300) and among patients with `feature_036=1` (interaction -0.00690 months, p<1e-300). The combined adverse association of `feature_001=1` and `feature_015=1` was less than additive (interaction +0.5115 months, p<1e-300). The negative effects of `feature_001=1` and `feature_015=1` also weakened as `feature_029` increased (+0.00798 and +0.00778 months per unit interaction, both p<1e-300). The `feature_041` by `feature_056` interaction was statistically significant but small (-0.000455 months, p=5.97e-6), and `feature_015` by `feature_066` was positive (+0.2792 months, p=6.94e-13).

Subgroup analyses supported the main-effect conclusions. `feature_043=1` was favorable both within `feature_036=0` (+0.9411 months) and within `feature_036=1` (+1.0107 months). `feature_036=1` was adverse both within `feature_043=0` (-1.3694 months) and within `feature_043=1` (-1.2998 months). `feature_001=1` was adverse within every `feature_025` level: -0.3611 months at `feature_025=0`, -0.3167 months at `feature_025=1`, and -0.2653 months at `feature_025=2`.

Several exploratory hypotheses were not supported. `feature_031`, `feature_062`, `feature_051`, `feature_030`, `feature_063`, and `feature_022` had only weak marginal evidence, with p-values from about 0.053 to 0.116. Proposed interactions `feature_036` by `feature_043`, `feature_041` by `feature_042`, and `feature_043` by `feature_050` were also not significant in unadjusted centered interaction tests. Overall, the cohort appears dominated by a strong favorable `feature_041` gradient, adverse `feature_025` and `feature_036` patterns, a favorable but strongly modified `feature_043` subgroup, and smaller modifying roles for `feature_001`, `feature_015`, `feature_029`, `feature_042`, and `feature_066`.
