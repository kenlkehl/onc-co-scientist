Dataset ds001_crc contains 50,000 colorectal cancer records with 73 de-identified features and one outcome, pfs_months. Mean pfs_months was 4.312 months. I tested univariate associations, adjusted linear models, stratified comparisons, and interaction models. Because the cohort is large, very small effects can be statistically significant; the most important findings are the large and consistent effect sizes.

The dominant main effect was feature_041. In univariate regression, each 1-unit increase in feature_041 was associated with +0.1765 months of pfs_months (p<1e-300), and the adjusted coefficient was essentially unchanged at +0.1758 months (p<1e-300). Feature_025 showed the strongest adverse numeric pattern: each 1-unit increase was associated with -1.1897 months univariately and -1.1713 months after adjustment (both p<1e-300). Mean pfs_months fell monotonically from 5.271 at feature_025=0 to 4.054 at feature_025=1 and 2.908 at feature_025=2.

The strongest binary adverse marker was feature_036. Patients with feature_036=1 had 1.352 fewer months of mean pfs_months than patients with feature_036=0, and the adjusted coefficient was -1.3615 months (p<1e-300). Additional independent adverse binary effects were feature_015 (-0.2867 adjusted months), feature_001 (-0.3539 adjusted months), and feature_066 (-0.3565 adjusted months), all with p<1e-300. Feature_043 was the clearest favorable binary marker: feature_043=1 was associated with +0.9722 months univariately and +0.9429 months after adjustment (p<1e-300). Feature_050 was favorable (+0.4692 adjusted months per unit), while feature_056 was adverse (-0.0761 adjusted months per unit). Feature_029 and feature_028 had smaller adverse adjusted slopes (-0.00500 and -0.000425 months per unit, respectively).

Some univariate signals did not survive adjustment. Feature_042 initially appeared favorable (+0.2211 months, p=0.000714), but after adjustment its coefficient was only +0.0208 months and not significant (p=0.362). Feature_039 and feature_012 also lost evidence after adjustment. Representative null probes such as feature_061, feature_048, feature_037, feature_018, and feature_005 showed no meaningful association.

Interaction testing showed that the favorable feature_043 association was highly subgroup-dependent. The feature_043 by feature_001 interaction was -1.6288 months (p<1e-300): feature_043 was associated with +1.656 months when feature_001=0 but only +0.027 months when feature_001=1. The feature_043 by feature_015 interaction was similarly negative (-1.4541 months, p<1e-300), with feature_043 benefit present when feature_015=0 and nearly absent when feature_015=1. The feature_043 by feature_066 interaction was -1.1829 months (p<1e-300), with the feature_043 association reversing slightly in the feature_066=1 subgroup.

Feature_029 also showed heterogeneity. Its adverse slope was much stronger among feature_043=1 patients: the feature_029 by feature_043 interaction was -0.02527 months per unit (p<1e-300), with slopes of -0.00047 for feature_043=0 and -0.02574 for feature_043=1. In contrast, feature_029's negative slope was attenuated in feature_001=1 and feature_015=1 subgroups, with positive interactions of +0.00735 and +0.00750 months per unit, respectively.

Feature_041 remained favorable in all examined subgroups, but its slope varied modestly. The feature_041 by feature_036 interaction was -0.00712 months per unit (p=1.89e-9), and the slope was +0.1804 for feature_036=0 versus +0.1733 for feature_036=1. The feature_041 by feature_025 interaction was -0.00720 months per product unit (p<1e-300), with feature_041 slopes weakening across feature_025 levels. Smaller continuous-continuous interactions involving feature_056 and feature_050 were statistically significant but much smaller than the primary main effects and feature_043 subgroup effects.

Overall, the cohort contains a strong feature-outcome structure: longer pfs_months is most consistently associated with higher feature_041, feature_043=1, and higher feature_050; shorter pfs_months is most consistently associated with higher feature_025, feature_036=1, feature_001=1, feature_015=1, feature_066=1, higher feature_056, and higher feature_029. The most clinically meaningful heterogeneity pattern is that the otherwise favorable feature_043 association is nearly eliminated in the feature_001=1 and feature_015=1 subgroups and attenuated or reversed in the smaller feature_066=1 subgroup. These are statistical associations in de-identified EHR-derived data and should not be interpreted causally without additional design and validation.
