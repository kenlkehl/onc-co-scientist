Dataset ds001_crc contained 50,000 patient records, 73 opaque feature columns, and one clinical outcome, `pfs_months`. I decoded the local Parquet file directly and analyzed `pfs_months` as a continuous outcome using mean comparisons and ordinary least-squares regression. The cohort had mean `pfs_months` 4.312 months, SD 2.293 months, minimum 0.000, and maximum 16.250.

The strongest main-effect finding was `feature_041`. In univariate OLS, each 1-unit increase in `feature_041` was associated with +0.1765 months of `pfs_months` (p<1e-300). This effect remained essentially unchanged in the full 73-feature model (+0.1758 months per unit, p<1e-300), making it the dominant independent predictor in the dataset. A quadratic refinement showed a small but significant positive quadratic term (+0.000589 months per squared centered unit, p<1e-300), suggesting the positive slope becomes slightly stronger at higher `feature_041` values.

`feature_025` showed a large graded negative relationship with outcome. Treated linearly, each 1-level increase was associated with -1.1897 months of `pfs_months` (p<1e-300). Mean PFS was 5.271 months at `feature_025=0`, 4.054 months at `feature_025=1`, and 2.908 months at `feature_025=2`. In an adjusted categorical model, level 1 was -1.1975 months below level 0 and level 2 was -2.3361 months below level 0, both p<1e-300. This supports a strong dose-like adverse association.

Several additional independent main effects were supported. `feature_036=1` was associated with -1.3520 months univariately and -1.3615 months in the full multivariable model (both p<1e-300). `feature_043=1` was associated with +0.9722 months univariately and +0.9430 months in the full model (both p<1e-300). `feature_056` was negatively associated with outcome (-0.0730 months per unit univariately; adjusted -0.0761, p<1e-300), while `feature_050` was positively associated (+0.4654 months per unit univariately; adjusted +0.4694, p<1e-300). `feature_001=1`, `feature_015=1`, `feature_029`, and `feature_066=1` also retained negative adjusted effects. The hypothesized independent positive main effect of `feature_042=1` was not supported: although the univariate difference was +0.2211 months, the adjusted main-effect coefficient was only +0.0210 months (p=0.359).

Nonlinear probing supported nonlinearity for `feature_041` but not for `feature_056`. The `feature_056` quadratic term was -0.000307 months per squared centered unit and not significant (p=0.594), so the evidence supports a mostly linear negative association for `feature_056` over this range. `feature_050` showed a borderline negative quadratic term (p=0.052), and `feature_029` showed a small positive quadratic term in screening, but the clearest nonlinear result was for `feature_041`.

Interaction analyses found clinically meaningful subgroup heterogeneity. The positive `feature_041` association was weaker in higher-risk subgroups: the adjusted `feature_041` by `feature_025` interaction was -0.00728 months per product unit (p<1e-300), and unadjusted `feature_041` slopes declined from 0.1800 at `feature_025=0` to 0.1631 at `feature_025=2`. The `feature_041` by `feature_036` interaction was also negative (-0.00690, p<1e-300), with slopes 0.1804 when `feature_036=0` and 0.1733 when `feature_036=1`. Smaller but significant interactions suggested the `feature_041` slope is stronger when `feature_050` is high (+0.00388, p=5.38e-7) and weaker when `feature_056` is high (-0.000456, p=5.78e-6).

The largest interaction pattern centered on `feature_043`. Its positive association was almost absent in patients with `feature_001=1` or `feature_015=1`. For `feature_001=0`, the unadjusted `feature_043=1` vs 0 difference was +1.6557 months; for `feature_001=1`, it was only +0.0269 months. The adjusted `feature_001` by `feature_043` interaction was -1.6311 months (p<1e-300). Similarly, the `feature_043` difference was +1.4796 months when `feature_015=0` but +0.0255 months when `feature_015=1`; the adjusted interaction was -1.4610 months (p<1e-300).

`feature_043` also modified other adverse or favorable associations. The negative slope for `feature_029` was essentially absent when `feature_043=0` (-0.00047 months per unit) but substantial when `feature_043=1` (-0.02574 months per unit); the adjusted interaction was -0.02580 (p<1e-300). `feature_066=1` had no adverse unadjusted association when `feature_043=0` (+0.0098 months), but was strongly adverse when `feature_043=1` (-1.1731 months); the adjusted interaction was -0.9892 (p<1e-300). In contrast, `feature_042=1` had almost no association when `feature_043=0` (+0.0086 months) but was favorable when `feature_043=1` (+0.9966 months); the adjusted interaction was +0.8881 (p<1e-300). This explains why the adjusted main effect of `feature_042` was null despite a positive univariate signal: its association is concentrated in the `feature_043=1` subgroup.

There was also interaction between `feature_001` and `feature_015`. Both had negative main effects, but their joint association was less severe than additive. The adjusted `feature_001` by `feature_015` interaction was +0.5117 months (p<1e-300). Unadjusted, the `feature_015=1` vs 0 difference was -0.4935 months among `feature_001=0` patients but only -0.0418 months among `feature_001=1` patients. A smaller interaction between `feature_025` and `feature_036` suggested the `feature_036=1` penalty was slightly less severe as `feature_025` increased: the adjusted interaction was +0.0449 months (p=8.70e-5), and the unadjusted `feature_036` difference changed from -1.4013 months at `feature_025=0` to -1.2701 months at `feature_025=2`.

Overall, the dataset appears to contain a stable core outcome structure: `feature_041` is strongly favorable, `feature_025` and `feature_036` are strongly adverse, `feature_043` is favorable, `feature_056` is adverse, and `feature_050` is favorable. These main effects survive broad multivariable adjustment. The most important heterogeneity is not just additive: `feature_043` defines subgroups in which the effects of `feature_001`, `feature_015`, `feature_029`, `feature_066`, and `feature_042` change substantially. The supported treatment-biomarker-outcome-style conclusion, using the available opaque feature labels, is that outcome associations in this cohort are driven by both strong main effects and pronounced interactions, especially involving `feature_043` and the risk features `feature_001`, `feature_015`, `feature_029`, and `feature_066`.
