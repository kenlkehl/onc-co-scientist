Synthesis:
The strongest univariate signals were led by feature_041, feature_025, and feature_036, all with statistically significant signed associations with pfs_months. The all-feature model retained several independent associations, led by feature_041, feature_025, and feature_036, indicating that some apparent main effects were not merely proxies for the other recorded features.
Subgroup heterogeneity was most evident for feature_001 with feature_043: the joint feature_001=1/feature_043=1 subgroup differed from additivity by -1.629 months (p=<1e-300). Stratified simple-effect checks confirmed that the effect of each member of the pair changed materially across levels of the other.
Among continuous-by-binary probes, feature_029 by feature_043 showed the strongest modification of slope (-0.0253 months per unit, p=<1e-300), supporting additional heterogeneity beyond purely binary subgroups.
Three-way checks were exploratory; the largest was feature_015:feature_001:feature_043 with coefficient 2.348 months and p=<1e-300. This suggests higher-order structure may exist, but these terms should be treated as hypothesis-generating because many combinations were screened.
Overall, pfs_months in this cohort is associated with multiple feature_* columns and shows clear interaction/subgroup structure. Because the feature labels are de-identified, the findings are best interpreted as statistical treatment-biomarker-outcome patterns rather than named clinical mechanisms.
