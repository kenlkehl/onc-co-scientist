Dataset ds001_crc contains 50,000 colorectal cancer records with 73 de-identified feature columns and one continuous outcome, pfs_months. I treated pfs_months as a continuous endpoint and synthesized iterative analyses using mean comparisons, ordinary least-squares regressions, adjusted main-effect models, nonlinear terms, and interaction/subgroup models. Because the cohort is large, very small effects can be statistically significant, so the interpretation emphasizes effect size and consistency.

The strongest main-effect signal was feature_041. Each 1-unit increase in feature_041 was associated with +0.1765 months of pfs_months (p<1e-300), and prior all-feature adjustment showed the association remained essentially unchanged. A quadratic refinement was also supported: the centered feature_041 squared term was +0.000589 months (p<1e-300), suggesting the favorable slope becomes slightly stronger at higher feature_041 values.

Feature_025 showed the clearest graded adverse relationship. A 1-level increase was associated with -1.1897 months of pfs_months (p<1e-300), and the categorical pattern was monotone: mean pfs_months was 5.271 months at feature_025=0, 4.054 at feature_025=1, and 2.908 at feature_025=2. Adjusted categorical contrasts also supported this dose-like pattern, with feature_025=2 about -2.336 months below feature_025=0.

Several other main effects were stable. Feature_036=1 was associated with substantially shorter pfs_months (-1.3520 months, p<1e-300). Feature_043=1 was favorable overall (+0.9722 months, p<1e-300). Feature_056 had a negative linear association (-0.0730 months per unit, p<1e-300), while feature_050 was favorable (+0.4654 months per unit, p<1e-300). Feature_001=1 and feature_015=1 had smaller adverse univariable effects, and feature_066=1 remained adverse after adjustment for the dominant features (-0.3555 months, p<1e-300). The hypothesized independent positive main effect for feature_042=1 was not supported after adjustment (+0.0210 months, p=0.359).

Nonlinear probing supported feature_041 nonlinearity but not feature_056 nonlinearity. The feature_056 quadratic term was negative but not significant (-0.000307 months, p=0.594), so the evidence favors a mostly linear adverse association for feature_056 over the observed range.

Interaction analyses showed that the feature_041 association varied modestly by risk context. The feature_041 by feature_025 interaction was negative (-0.00728 months per product unit, p<1e-300), with feature_041 slopes weakening from 0.1800 at feature_025=0 to 0.1631 at feature_025=2. The feature_041 by feature_036 interaction was also negative (-0.00690, p<1e-300). Smaller interactions suggested feature_041 was slightly stronger with higher feature_050 (+0.00388, p=5.38e-7) and slightly weaker with higher feature_056 (-0.000456, p=5.78e-6).

The most clinically meaningful heterogeneity pattern centered on feature_043. Its overall favorable association was almost eliminated in feature_001=1 and feature_015=1 subgroups. The adjusted feature_001 by feature_043 interaction was -1.6311 months (p<1e-300): the unadjusted feature_043 difference was +1.6557 months when feature_001=0 but only +0.0269 months when feature_001=1. The feature_015 by feature_043 interaction was similarly negative (-1.4610 months, p<1e-300), with feature_043 differences of +1.4796 months when feature_015=0 and +0.0255 months when feature_015=1.

Feature_043 also modified other marker associations. Feature_029 was much more adverse when feature_043=1: the feature_029 by feature_043 interaction was -0.0258 months per product unit (p<1e-300), with slopes near zero for feature_043=0 and clearly negative for feature_043=1. Feature_066=1 was especially adverse in the feature_043=1 subgroup, with an adjusted feature_066 by feature_043 interaction of -0.9892 months (p<1e-300). Conversely, feature_042=1 had little independent adjusted main effect but was favorable specifically among feature_043=1 patients, reflected by a positive feature_042 by feature_043 interaction (+0.8881 months, p<1e-300).

There was also evidence that feature_001 and feature_015 were not simply additive. Both were adverse individually, but their joint effect was less negative than the sum of their separate effects; the adjusted feature_001 by feature_015 interaction was +0.5117 months (p<1e-300). A smaller interaction between feature_025 and feature_036 suggested the feature_036 penalty was slightly less severe as feature_025 increased (+0.0449 months, p=8.70e-5).

Overall, pfs_months in this cohort appears to be driven by strong favorable associations for feature_041, feature_043, and feature_050; strong adverse associations for feature_025 and feature_036; and additional adverse contributions from feature_056, feature_001, feature_015, feature_029, and feature_066. The largest subgroup structure involves feature_043: its favorable association is concentrated in patients without feature_001=1 or feature_015=1 and is further shaped by feature_029, feature_066, and feature_042. Because all feature labels are de-identified, these are best interpreted as statistical treatment-biomarker-outcome patterns rather than named clinical mechanisms or causal effects.
