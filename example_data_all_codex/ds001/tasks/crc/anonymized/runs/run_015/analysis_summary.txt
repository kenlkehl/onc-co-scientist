Overall synthesis
The clearest main-effect signal was feature_041: higher values were strongly associated with longer progression-free survival (about 0.176502 months per unit). Higher feature_025, feature_036=1, feature_056, feature_001=1, feature_015=1, feature_029, and feature_066=1 were adverse, while feature_043=1, feature_050, and feature_042=1 were favorable. Several smaller continuous associations (feature_039, feature_028, feature_012) were statistically significant but had very small absolute slopes and should be treated as weak unadjusted signals. The proposed feature_031 adverse main effect was not significant at p<0.05.
The strongest heterogeneity centered on feature_043. Its favorable association was markedly reduced in the presence of feature_001=1, feature_015=1, feature_066=1, or higher feature_029, but was amplified among patients with feature_042=1. Feature_041 also showed heterogeneity: its favorable association was weaker with higher feature_025 and with feature_036=1. The positive interactions for feature_001:feature_015, feature_001:feature_029, and feature_015:feature_029 indicate that some adverse main effects were less than additive in selected subgroups.
In total, 24 of 25 tested hypotheses were statistically supported and 1 was not. The most clinically meaningful pattern in this opaque-feature cohort is a dominant favorable feature_041 association, a strong adverse ordinal feature_025 and binary feature_036 pattern, and pronounced treatment-biomarker-like heterogeneity involving feature_043 with feature_001, feature_015, feature_029, feature_066, and feature_042.
