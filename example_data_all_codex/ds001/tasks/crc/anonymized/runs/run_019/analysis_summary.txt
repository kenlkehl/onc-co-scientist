Dataset ds001_crc contained 50,000 records, 73 opaque feature columns, and one continuous outcome, pfs_months. I decoded the local Parquet file, ran simple OLS/t-test style screens, a full 73-feature main-effect model, all pairwise interaction screens, selected adjusted interaction models, subgroup cell summaries, and a final joint model with dominant main effects plus selected interactions. P-values reported as 1e-300 indicate values below the numerical reporting floor.

The strongest main effects were highly consistent. Higher feature_041 was associated with longer PFS in univariate analysis (+0.1765 months per unit, p<1e-300), in the full 73-feature model (+0.1758, p<1e-300), and in the joint interaction model (+0.1739, p<1e-300). Feature_025 showed a strong monotonic adverse pattern: mean PFS was 5.271 months at level 0, 4.054 at level 1, and 2.908 at level 2, with an ordinal slope of -1.1897 months per level (p<1e-300). Feature_036 was also strongly adverse (-1.3520 months unadjusted; -1.3615 adjusted, both p<1e-300).

Feature_043 was the main favorable binary feature. Unadjusted, feature_043=1 had mean PFS 5.089 months versus 4.117 months for feature_043=0 (+0.9722 months, p<1e-300). Feature_050 was another favorable continuous feature (+0.4654 unadjusted; +0.4694 adjusted, both p<1e-300). Several adverse features were also stable: feature_056 (-0.0761 adjusted), feature_001 (-0.3544 adjusted), feature_015 (-0.2865 adjusted), feature_029 (-0.00501 adjusted), and feature_028 (-0.000425 adjusted) were all significant in the full main-effect model. Feature_042 was positive unadjusted (+0.2211 months, p=0.000228) but not independently significant after all-feature adjustment (+0.0214, p=0.350).

The dominant heterogeneity centered on feature_043. Its positive association was strongly attenuated by feature_001, feature_015, feature_029, and feature_066. Adjusted interaction estimates were feature_043:feature_001 = -1.6326, feature_043:feature_015 = -1.4628, feature_043:feature_029 = -0.02582 per product-unit, and feature_043:feature_066 = -0.9892, all p<1e-300. These interactions persisted in a joint selected-interaction model: feature_043:feature_001 = -1.7629, feature_043:feature_015 = -1.4758, feature_043:feature_029 = -0.02518, and feature_043:feature_066 = -1.7581, all p<1e-300. Raw cell means supported the same pattern: for example, the feature_043 difference was +1.656 months when feature_001=0 but only +0.027 months when feature_001=1; it was +1.480 months when feature_015=0 but only +0.026 months when feature_015=1.

Other interactions were present but secondary. Feature_001 and feature_015 had a positive interaction (+0.5121 adjusted; +0.5040 in the joint model, p<1e-300), suggesting their adverse associations were less than additive. Higher feature_041 intensified the adverse association of feature_025 (feature_041:feature_025 about -0.0073 to -0.0075, p<1e-300) and feature_036 (about -0.0066 to -0.0069, p<1e-300). Feature_001 and feature_015 each attenuated the negative slope of feature_029 through positive interactions. Feature_041 modestly strengthened the negative feature_056 association and the positive feature_050 association.

An initially positive feature_043:feature_042 interaction (+0.8891, p<1e-300 in a selected adjusted model) did not survive the final joint selected-interaction model (+0.0754, p=0.0635), so that hypothesis was not supported after accounting for the stronger feature_043 modifiers. A few non-core exploratory interactions appeared in the all-pair screen, such as feature_064:feature_060 (-0.1876, p=0.000556) and feature_035:feature_065 (+0.00563, p=0.00110), but they were much weaker than the dominant feature_043 interaction cluster and were not central to the final interpretation.

Overall, this cohort shows a strong main-effect axis for PFS, led by favorable feature_041, feature_043, and feature_050 and adverse feature_025, feature_036, feature_056, feature_001, feature_015, feature_029, and feature_028. The most clinically meaningful multivariable pattern is that the favorable feature_043 association is conditional: it is large in some subgroups but sharply reduced by feature_001, feature_015, higher feature_029, and feature_066. The evidence supports both strong main effects and substantial subgroup heterogeneity rather than a single uniform feature-outcome relationship.
