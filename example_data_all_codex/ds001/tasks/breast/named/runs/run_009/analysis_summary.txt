Analysis summary for ds001_breast

I analyzed 50,000 breast cancer records from the local dataset.parquet file. The outcome was pfs_months (mean 4.693 months, SD 2.496, range 0 to 16.66). Tests used Welch mean comparisons, univariate linear regression, multivariable ordinary least squares, and interaction models. Very small p-values are recorded as 1e-300 in transcript.json when they were below display precision.

The strongest main-effect pattern was prognostic. Higher age_years was unexpectedly associated with longer PFS (+0.1757 adjusted months per year, p < 1e-300). Worse performance status and advanced disease were strongly adverse: ecog_ps was -1.1716 months per point, stage_iv was -1.5488 months, and has_brain_mets was -0.9825 months after adjustment. Cachexia and aggressive biology also mattered: weight_loss_pct_6mo was -0.0769 months per percentage point, ki67_pct was -0.0189 months per percentage point, and ldh_u_l had a small negative effect. Higher albumin_g_dl was favorable (+0.4752 months per g/dL).

Biomarker main effects showed ER-positive disease had longer PFS (+0.5316 months adjusted), while PR positivity did not add an independent signal after ER and other covariates (+0.0136 months, p=0.34). HER2-positive disease was independently associated with shorter PFS (-0.4708 months), and pik3ca_mutation was strongly adverse (-0.5818 months).

The clearest treatment-biomarker pattern involved treatment_palbociclib. Its adjusted main effect was +1.1011 months, and subgroup tests showed much larger association in ER-positive patients (+1.5598 month interaction) and PR-positive patients (+1.0291 month interaction). In contrast, pik3ca_mutation markedly attenuated the palbociclib association (-1.6653 month interaction), and HER2-positive status also showed a negative palbociclib interaction in the four-term subgroup model (-1.3637 months).

Several clinically expected targeted-treatment hypotheses were not supported. treatment_tamoxifen did not show improved PFS in hormone-receptor-positive patients (interaction -0.0517 months, p=0.3521; adjusted main effect -0.0074, p=0.5035). treatment_trastuzumab did not show an enriched association in HER2-positive patients (interaction +0.0103 months, p=0.7736; main effect -0.0092, p=0.4705). treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan had only a tiny borderline adjusted main effect (+0.0334 months, p=0.04916) and no enrichment in triple-negative disease (interaction -0.1046, p=0.2458).

Olaparib showed a modest composite BRCA subgroup signal: patients with either brca1_mutation or brca2_mutation had a +0.4022 month interaction with treatment_olaparib (p=0.02084). However, when BRCA1 and BRCA2 were tested separately in adjusted interaction models, neither individual interaction was significant, so this finding should be treated as weaker than the palbociclib interactions.

For immunotherapy, treatment_pembrolizumab had a modest positive triple-negative interaction (+0.1853 months, p=0.01522), and an adjusted interaction using er_positive directly was negative (-0.0741 months, p=0.02882), consistent with less apparent benefit in ER-positive disease. I did not find evidence that autoimmune_disease, high nlr, or high crp_mg_l meaningfully modified pembrolizumab-associated PFS.

After stage_iv and has_brain_mets were included, other metastatic-site indicators such as liver_mets, bone_mets, and pleural_effusion were not independently significant. Overall, the cohort is dominated by strong prognostic main effects, one robust treatment-biomarker interaction cluster for palbociclib with hormone-receptor and PIK3CA/HER2 features, and several refuted expected treatment-biomarker relationships.
