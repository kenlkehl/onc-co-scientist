Oncology cohort analysis summary for ds001_breast

I analyzed 50000 breast oncology records with no missing decoded values and modeled pfs_months as a continuous outcome. The primary analyses used adjusted ordinary least-squares models with demographics, disease burden, receptor/mutation biomarkers, key labs, and all six treatment indicators included as covariates. Interaction terms were then added one at a time to test treatment-biomarker and subgroup heterogeneity.

Treatment main effects:
- tamoxifen: adjusted effect -0.008 months, p=0.4935 (not significant).
- palbociclib: adjusted effect 1.10 months, p=<1e-50 (significant).
- trastuzumab: adjusted effect -0.010 months, p=0.4543 (not significant).
- olaparib: adjusted effect -0.026 months, p=0.1202 (not significant).
- sacituzumab govitecan: adjusted effect 0.033 months, p=0.0502 (not significant).
- pembrolizumab: adjusted effect -0.019 months, p=0.1696 (not significant).

Baseline prognosis and disease burden:
- ECOG performance status: effect -1.17 months, p=<1e-50 (significant).
- stage IV: effect -1.55 months, p=<1e-50 (significant).
- brain metastases: effect -0.983 months, p=<1e-50 (significant).
- liver metastases: effect -0.018 months, p=0.1944 (not significant).
- bone metastases: effect 0.014 months, p=0.2732 (not significant).
- node-positive disease: effect -0.018 months, p=0.0810 (not significant).

Laboratory and proliferation markers:
- albumin_g_dl per 1 g/dL: effect 0.476 months, p=<1e-50 (significant).
- ldh_u_l per 100 U/L: effect -0.052 months, p=2.22e-16 (significant).
- weight_loss_pct_6mo per 5 points: effect -0.384 months, p=<1e-50 (significant).
- crp_mg_l per 10 mg/L: effect -0.001 months, p=0.8348 (not significant).
- nlr per 1 unit: effect -0.001 months, p=0.5445 (not significant).
- ki67_pct per 10 points: effect -0.189 months, p=<1e-50 (significant).

Treatment-biomarker heterogeneity:
- tamoxifen by er_positive: interaction 0.030 months, p=0.2105 (not significant).
- tamoxifen by postmenopausal: interaction -0.003 months, p=0.8957 (not significant).
- palbociclib by er_positive: interaction 1.56 months, p=<1e-50 (significant).
- palbociclib by pik3ca_mutation: interaction -1.67 months, p=<1e-50 (significant).
- trastuzumab by her2_positive: interaction 0.022 months, p=0.4951 (not significant).
- trastuzumab by her2_amplification: interaction -0.052 months, p=0.4866 (not significant).
- olaparib by any BRCA1/2 mutation: interaction 0.018 months, p=0.8241 (not significant).
- olaparib by brca1_mutation: interaction 0.079 months, p=0.4768 (not significant).
- olaparib by brca2_mutation: interaction -0.045 months, p=0.6859 (not significant).
- sacituzumab govitecan by triple-negative status: interaction 0.047 months, p=0.2497 (not significant).
- sacituzumab govitecan by visceral metastases: interaction 0.020 months, p=0.5592 (not significant).
- pembrolizumab by triple-negative status: interaction 0.032 months, p=0.3550 (not significant).
- pembrolizumab by CRP >= 10 mg/L: interaction 0.042 months, p=0.2782 (not significant).
- pembrolizumab by autoimmune disease: interaction 0.021 months, p=0.7492 (not significant).

Other multivariable interactions:
- stage IV by liver metastases: interaction 0.044 months, p=0.1514 (not significant).
- low albumin by weight loss: interaction -0.025 months, p=0.1078 (not significant).
- high Ki-67 by ER positivity: interaction -0.598 months, p=<1e-50 (significant).

Overall conclusions: the strongest treatment signal was for palbociclib, with an additional positive interaction in ER-positive disease and a negative interaction in PIK3CA-mutated disease. Tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib, sacituzumab govitecan, and pembrolizumab did not show convincing adjusted main-effect benefits in this specification. Disease burden and host-state markers were clearer: ECOG performance status, stage IV disease, brain metastases, weight loss, high LDH, and higher Ki-67 were adverse, while higher albumin and ER positivity were favorable. Several clinically plausible treatment-biomarker hypotheses were refuted or not supported, including tamoxifen by ER status, trastuzumab by HER2 status/amplification, olaparib by BRCA1/2 mutation, sacituzumab by triple-negative status, pembrolizumab by triple-negative status, and pembrolizumab by autoimmune disease. The high-Ki-67 interaction with ER positivity was significant in the opposite direction from the hypothesis: high Ki-67 was more adverse, not less adverse, among ER-positive patients.
