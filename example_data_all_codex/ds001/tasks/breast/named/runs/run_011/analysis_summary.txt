Dataset ds001_breast contained 50,000 breast oncology patient records with pfs_months as the outcome. I analyzed the Parquet data in place using large-sample linear models for adjusted effects, interaction models for subgroup heterogeneity, and Welch comparisons for treatment effects inside clinically defined subgroups. Effect estimates below are in months of PFS.

The strongest adverse clinical factors were stage IV disease, worse ECOG performance status, and brain metastases. In the multivariable model, `stage_iv` was associated with -1.549 months of PFS, each one-point increase in `ecog_ps` with -1.172 months, and `has_brain_mets` with -0.983 months; all had p < 1e-300. A clinically plausible liver-metastasis effect was not supported after adjustment (`liver_mets` coefficient -0.019 months, p = 0.191). Higher `age_years` was unexpectedly associated with longer PFS (+0.176 months per year, p < 1e-300).

Nutritional and tumor-activity markers were also important. Higher `albumin_g_dl` was favorable (+0.475 months per g/dL, p < 1e-300), while `weight_loss_pct_6mo` (-0.077 months per percentage point, p < 1e-300), `ldh_u_l` (-0.052 months per 100 U/L, p = 2.22e-16), and `ki67_pct` (-0.0189 months per percentage point, p < 1e-300) were adverse.

Biomarker main effects separated into supported and unsupported findings. `er_positive` was independently favorable (+0.532 months, p < 1e-300), whereas `pr_positive` added little after ER and other adjustment (+0.014 months, p = 0.330). `her2_positive` (-0.471 months, p < 1e-300) and `pik3ca_mutation` (-0.582 months, p < 1e-300) were adverse.

The clearest treatment signal was for `treatment_palbociclib`. Its adjusted main association was +1.101 months (p < 1e-300), and interaction analyses showed strong biomarker heterogeneity. The palbociclib association was much larger in hormone-receptor-positive/HER2-negative disease (interaction +1.796 months, p < 1e-300). It was attenuated in `pik3ca_mutation` disease (interaction -1.665 months), with `ki67_pct` >= 20 (interaction -1.445 months), in triple-negative disease (interaction -1.380 months), and in HER2-positive disease (interaction -1.337 months); all of these interaction p-values were < 1e-300. Within triple-negative patients, the palbociclib mean difference was essentially zero (+0.004 months, p = 0.928).

Several clinically plausible targeted-treatment hypotheses were not supported in this cohort. Among ER-positive patients, `treatment_tamoxifen` was associated with only +0.023 months (p = 0.450). Among HER2-positive or HER2-amplified patients, `treatment_trastuzumab` was not favorable (-0.044 months, p = 0.367). Among BRCA1/2-mutated patients, `treatment_olaparib` showed +0.016 months (p = 0.733). In triple-negative patients, `treatment_pembrolizumab` (+0.052 months, p = 0.368) and `treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan` (+0.062 months, p = 0.316) were not significant. A pembrolizumab-by-high-NLR interaction was negative (-0.067 months for `nlr` >= 4) but did not meet the 0.05 threshold (p = 0.062).

Overall, the dataset showed strong prognostic structure around stage, ECOG status, brain metastases, albumin, weight loss, LDH, Ki67, ER status, HER2 positivity, and PIK3CA mutation. Treatment heterogeneity was dominated by palbociclib: benefit was concentrated in HR-positive/HER2-negative disease and was sharply reduced in PIK3CA-mutated, high-Ki67, triple-negative, and HER2-positive subgroups. Other targeted-therapy subgroup hypotheses were not statistically supported by the analyses run here.
