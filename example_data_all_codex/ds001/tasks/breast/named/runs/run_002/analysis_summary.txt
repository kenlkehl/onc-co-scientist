Dataset ds001_breast: 50,000 records, outcome pfs_months (mean 4.69 months, SD 2.50). I used adjusted OLS models for main effects and treatment-by-subgroup interactions; continuous predictors were standardized, so those coefficients are months of PFS per 1-SD increase.

Strong baseline prognostic effects were present. Stage IV disease (-1.55 months), brain metastases (-0.98 months), worse ECOG performance status (-0.79 months per SD), greater 6-month weight loss (-0.30 months per SD), higher Ki-67 (-0.23 months per SD), and PIK3CA mutation (-0.58 months) were all associated with shorter PFS. Higher albumin (+0.24 months per SD), ER positivity (+0.53 months), and older age (+1.75 months per SD) were associated with longer PFS. HER2 positivity was independently adverse (-0.47 months). LDH was statistically significant but small (-0.04 months per SD). Tumor size, CRP, and NLR were not meaningfully associated after adjustment.

The treatment signal was dominated by palbociclib. Its adjusted main effect was +1.10 months, but interaction analyses showed the benefit was concentrated in endocrine-receptor/HER2/PIK3CA-defined subgroups: stronger in ER-positive (+1.56 interaction), PR-positive (+1.03), and HER2-low (+0.38) patients, and sharply attenuated in HER2-positive (-1.34) and PIK3CA-mutated (-1.67) patients. In the refined ER-positive, HER2-negative, PIK3CA-wildtype subgroup, palbociclib was associated with +2.92 PFS months; within ER-positive/HER2-negative patients, PIK3CA mutation reduced the palbociclib association by about 2.92 months.

Several expected treatment-biomarker hypotheses were not supported in this cohort. Tamoxifen did not show ER-specific benefit (interaction p=0.683), trastuzumab did not show HER2-positive/HER2-amplified enrichment (p=0.846), olaparib did not show BRCA1/2 enrichment (p=0.621), and pembrolizumab did not show statistically significant triple-negative enrichment (p=0.103). Sacituzumab govitecan had a modest positive interaction with brain metastases (+0.19 months, p=0.002), but that effect was much smaller than the palbociclib subgroup effects.

Overall conclusion: the clearest clinically meaningful structure in this dataset is a strong palbociclib-associated PFS advantage concentrated in ER-positive/HER2-negative/PIK3CA-wildtype disease, with major adverse baseline prognosis from metastatic burden, performance status, proliferative biology, and weight loss.
