Dataset ds001_breast contained 50,000 patients and one continuous outcome, pfs_months. I decoded dataset.parquet locally and used multivariable OLS models on pfs_months. The core adjusted model included age, sex, ECOG, stage, metastatic sites, receptor and mutation markers, key labs, treatments, and selected comorbidities. Interaction tests added one treatment-marker interaction term to that core model. Effects below are signed months of PFS.

Baseline outcome distribution: mean PFS was 4.693 months, median 4.535 months, with an SD of 2.496 months.

Clinical severity was strongly associated with shorter PFS. Stage IV disease was associated with -1.549 months (p<1e-300), each ECOG PS level with -1.172 months (p<1e-300), and brain metastases with -0.983 months (p<1e-300). Weight loss over 6 months was also adverse (-0.0768 months per percentage point, p<1e-300). Liver metastases (-0.018 months, p=0.199), bone metastases (+0.014 months, p=0.274), node positivity (-0.018 months, p=0.079), and tumor size (+0.001 months per cm, p=0.649) did not show independent adjusted effects in this model.

Laboratory and proliferation signals split into clear and null groups. Higher albumin was favorable (+0.476 months per g/dL, p<1e-300), while higher LDH (-0.000519 months per U/L, p=2.22e-16) and higher Ki-67 (-0.0189 months per percentage point, p<1e-300) were adverse. CRP (-0.000117 months per mg/L, p=0.830), NLR (-0.00138 months per unit, p=0.572), hemoglobin, and alkaline phosphatase were not independently associated with PFS after adjustment.

Biomarker main effects showed longer adjusted PFS for ER-positive disease (+0.531 months, p<1e-300), shorter PFS for HER2-positive disease (-0.471 months, p<1e-300), and shorter PFS for PIK3CA mutation (-0.582 months, p<1e-300). PR positivity (+0.014 months, p=0.321) and HER2-low status (+0.0017 months, p=0.882) were not independent. BRCA1 mutation had a small adverse association (-0.069 months, p=0.032), while BRCA2 mutation was not significant (-0.042 months, p=0.200).

Treatment findings were dominated by palbociclib. Overall, treatment_palbociclib was associated with +1.101 adjusted months of PFS (p<1e-300). This benefit was highly heterogeneous: the palbociclib by ER-positive interaction was +1.559 months (p<1e-300), and the PR-positive interaction was +1.029 months (p<1e-300). In unadjusted subgroup contrasts, palbociclib was essentially neutral in ER-negative patients (-0.017 months) but strongly favorable in ER-positive patients (+1.577 months). The benefit was markedly attenuated in PIK3CA-mutant disease: interaction -1.665 months (p<1e-300), with unadjusted differences of +1.659 months in PIK3CA-wildtype patients versus +0.036 months in PIK3CA-mutant patients. High Ki-67 also attenuated palbociclib association (interaction -1.339 months, p<1e-300).

Other treatment hypotheses were mostly unsupported. Tamoxifen had no overall adjusted association (-0.007 months, p=0.509) and no ER-positive interaction (+0.030 months, p=0.212). Trastuzumab had no overall association (-0.010 months, p=0.450) and no meaningful interaction with HER2 positivity (+0.022 months, p=0.502) or HER2 amplification (-0.052 months, p=0.487). Olaparib had no overall association (-0.027 months, p=0.116), and BRCA1/BRCA2 interactions were not significant (+0.078, p=0.478; -0.043, p=0.698). Sacituzumab govitecan had a very small borderline overall association (+0.033 months, p=0.0498), but no significant triple-negative interaction (-0.065 months, p=0.109) or HER2-low interaction (+0.048 months, p=0.176). Pembrolizumab had no overall benefit (-0.019 months, p=0.176). The triple-negative interaction was statistically significant but opposite the hypothesized benefit (-0.083 months, p=0.0145), while high NLR and autoimmune-disease interactions were not significant.

Overall conclusion: the strongest and most clinically coherent pattern in this cohort is a palbociclib-associated PFS advantage concentrated in hormone receptor positive disease and largely absent in PIK3CA-mutated or high-proliferation tumors. Independent prognosis was also strongly shaped by stage, ECOG performance status, brain metastases, weight loss, albumin, LDH, Ki-67, ER status, HER2 positivity, and PIK3CA mutation. The data did not support broad independent PFS benefit or expected biomarker-selected benefit for tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib, sacituzumab govitecan, or pembrolizumab in the tested models.
