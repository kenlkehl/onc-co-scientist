Analysis summary for ds001_breast

I analyzed 50000 patient records with progression-free survival `pfs_months` as the outcome. Mean PFS was 4.693 months (SD 2.496). Models were ordinary least squares on the natural month scale; primary adjusted models included age, ECOG performance status, stage IV status, brain/liver/bone metastases, receptor and mutation markers, Ki-67, tumor size, albumin, 6-month weight loss, and treatment indicators. Interaction models added the relevant main effects and interaction term.

Baseline clinical effects were strong and directionally coherent. Older age was associated with longer PFS (+0.176 months per year, p<1e-250), while worse ECOG performance status (-1.172 months per point, p<1e-250), stage IV disease (-1.549 months, p<1e-250), and brain metastases (-0.983 months, p<1e-250) were associated with shorter PFS. Liver and bone metastasis main effects were not independently prominent after adjustment in the exploratory model.

Tumor biology showed a clear hormone-receptor and proliferation pattern. ER positivity was independently favorable (+0.532 months, p<1e-250), whereas HER2 positivity (-0.471 months, p<1e-250), PIK3CA mutation (-0.582 months, p<1e-250), higher Ki-67 (-0.0189 months per percentage point, p<1e-250), lower albumin, and greater recent weight loss were associated with shorter PFS. PR positivity did not retain an independent main effect after ER and other markers were included.

Treatment main effects were dominated by palbociclib. Adjusted palbociclib exposure was associated with +1.102 months PFS (p<1e-250). Tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib, pembrolizumab, and sacituzumab govitecan did not show comparably strong adjusted main effects in the primary model.

The most important treatment heterogeneity was for palbociclib. Its association was much more favorable in ER-positive patients (+1.559 interaction months, p<1e-250) and PR-positive patients (+1.029 interaction months, p<1e-250), but much less favorable with PIK3CA mutation (-1.665 interaction months, p<1e-250), higher Ki-67 (-0.053 months per Ki-67 point, p<1e-250), and HER2 positivity (-1.338 interaction months, p<1e-250). This implies the strongest apparent palbociclib benefit in hormone-receptor-positive, PIK3CA-wild-type, lower-proliferation disease.

Other treatment-biomarker hypotheses were mostly unsupported. Trastuzumab did not have a meaningful HER2-positive interaction (+0.009 months, p=0.808), olaparib did not have an adjusted any-BRCA-mutation interaction (+0.045 months, p=0.606), and pembrolizumab had only a nonsignificant triple-negative interaction (+0.068 months, p=0.0836). Sacituzumab govitecan showed a small positive interaction in patients with brain metastases (+0.196 months, p=0.00180), which should be interpreted cautiously because it emerged among many exploratory interactions.

Feature-feature interactions suggested molecular subtype structure beyond main effects. The adverse PIK3CA association was stronger in ER-positive disease (-0.826 interaction months, p<1e-250), the adverse Ki-67 slope was stronger in ER-positive disease (-0.026 months per Ki-67 point, p<1e-250), and the PIK3CA adverse association was weaker in HER2-positive disease (+0.720 interaction months, p<1e-250). The positive age association was attenuated in stage IV disease (-0.0106 months per year, p<1e-250).

Overall, 21 of 25 stated hypotheses were statistically significant at p<0.05. The core conclusion is that PFS in this cohort is shaped by disease stage, performance status, brain metastases, nutritional/inflammatory reserve, and breast cancer subtype markers, with a particularly strong palbociclib signal concentrated in ER/PR-positive and PIK3CA-wild-type, lower-Ki-67 subgroups. Several biologically plausible therapy-matching hypotheses for trastuzumab, olaparib, and pembrolizumab were not supported after adjustment in this dataset.
