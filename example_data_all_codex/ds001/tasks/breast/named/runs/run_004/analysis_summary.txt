Analysis summary for ds001_breast

Cohort: 50,000 patients. Outcome was pfs_months (mean 4.693, SD 2.496, quartiles 2.972/4.535/6.174 months). I used OLS models on the natural month scale with signed coefficients; primary adjusted models included age_years, sex_female, ecog_ps, stage_iv, metastatic sites, node_positive, breast cancer biomarkers, ki67_pct, tumor_size_cm, albumin_g_dl, ldh_u_l, weight_loss_pct_6mo, crp_mg_l, nlr, and all treatment indicators.

Treatment main effects and biomarker heterogeneity:
- Palbociclib was the clearest treatment signal: adjusted main effect 1.102 months (p=<1e-300), with large positive interactions for er_positive (1.559 months, p=<1e-300) and pr_positive (1.028 months, p=<1e-300). This supported hormone-receptor subgroup heterogeneity.
- Trastuzumab hypotheses were not supported. The adjusted main coefficient was -0.009 months (p=0.4548), and interactions with her2_positive (0.022 months, p=0.4948), her2_amplification (-0.052 months, p=0.4882), and her2_low (-0.012 months, p=0.6540) were all small and non-significant.
- Tamoxifen also lacked an adjusted benefit (-0.008 months, p=0.4934), and its ER-positive interaction was small/non-significant (0.03 months, p=0.2092).
- Olaparib was not significantly associated with longer PFS after adjustment (-0.027 months, p=0.1197). BRCA1 and BRCA2 interaction hypotheses were not supported (BRCA1 0.078 months, p=0.4789; BRCA2 -0.045 months, p=0.6874).
- Pembrolizumab had no significant adjusted main association (-0.02 months, p=0.1674). Autoimmune disease and NLR did not significantly modify it (autoimmune interaction 0.021 months, p=0.7464; NLR interaction -0.014 months per SD, p=0.3316).
- The hypothesis that sacituzumab govitecan marks shorter PFS was refuted in the adjusted model: coefficient 0.033 months with borderline/non-significant p=0.0501. Stage IV and liver metastasis interactions were not meaningfully negative (stage IV 0.034 months, p=0.3623; liver metastasis 0.014 months, p=0.7696).

Disease burden and laboratory risk:
- Stage IV disease (-1.549 months, p=<1e-300), brain metastases (-0.983 months, p=<1e-300), and worse ecog_ps (-1.172 months, p=<1e-300) were strongly associated with shorter PFS. Liver metastases (-0.019 months, p=0.1919) and bone metastases (0.014 months, p=0.2749) were not significant after the broader adjustment set.
- The stage_iv-by-liver_mets interaction was 0.044 months (p=0.1510), and the brain_mets-by-pleural_effusion interaction was -0.061 months (p=0.1954). These did not show significant synergistic worsening beyond additive main effects.
- Albumin was protective (0.239 months per SD, p=<1e-300). LDH (-0.042 months per SD, p=2.22e-16), Ki-67 (-0.23 months per SD, p=<1e-300), and weight loss (-0.296 months per SD, p=<1e-300) were adverse. CRP (-0.001 months per SD, p=0.8373), NLR (-0.003 months per SD, p=0.5416), and tumor size (0.002 months per SD, p=0.6529) were not significant in the adjusted model.
- Age was an unexpectedly strong positive predictor: 0.176 months longer PFS per additional year (p=<1e-300), after adjustment for disease burden, biomarkers, treatments, and labs.

Biomarker main effects:
- ER positivity was favorable after adjustment (er_positive 0.532 months, p=<1e-300). PR positivity was not independently significant once ER and other covariates were included (pr_positive 0.014 months, p=0.3211), and the ER-by-PR interaction was also non-significant (0.023 months, p=0.4673).
- HER2-positive status was adverse in this cohort after adjustment (-0.471 months, p=<1e-300), while her2_low was essentially null (0.002 months, p=0.8642). The adjusted her2_positive-minus-her2_low contrast was -0.473 months (p=<1e-300), refuting the hypothesis that HER2-positive patients had higher PFS than HER2-low patients.
- pik3ca_mutation was a strong adverse biomarker (-0.582 months, p=<1e-300). brca1_mutation was modestly adverse (-0.068 months, p=0.0340), while brca2_mutation was not significant (-0.042 months, p=0.1985).

Overall conclusion: the strongest supported treatment pattern was palbociclib benefit concentrated in hormone-receptor-positive disease. Several clinically plausible treatment-matching hypotheses were refuted in this dataset, including trastuzumab-by-HER2, olaparib-by-BRCA, tamoxifen-by-ER, and pembrolizumab-by-NLR/autoimmune interactions. Independent of treatment, stage IV disease, brain metastases, poor ECOG performance status, high Ki-67, PIK3CA mutation, weight loss, low albumin, and higher LDH were associated with shorter progression-free survival, while age showed a large positive association. Routine comorbidity indicators were much smaller than the oncology-specific predictors in adjusted models.
