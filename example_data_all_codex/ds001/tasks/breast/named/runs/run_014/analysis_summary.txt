Dataset ds001_breast was analyzed using the local dataset.parquet file only. The outcome was pfs_months, and all signed effects below are month differences from adjusted ordinary least-squares models unless otherwise stated. The main adjustment set included age_years, sex_female, postmenopausal, stage_iv, ecog_ps, brain and other metastatic-site indicators, node_positive, tumor_size_cm, ki67_pct, albumin_g_dl, ldh_u_l, weight_loss_pct_6mo, crp_mg_l, nlr, key breast cancer biomarkers, and treatment indicators.

The first adjusted main-effect pass identified a strong prognostic backbone. Higher age_years was associated with higher pfs_months (0.176 months per year, p<1e-300). More advanced or clinically worse disease was associated with lower pfs_months: stage_iv -1.549 months (p<1e-300), ecog_ps -1.172 months per point (p<1e-300), and has_brain_mets -0.983 months (p<1e-300). Nutritional/inflammatory severity also mattered: albumin_g_dl was positive (0.476 months per g/dL, p<1e-300), while weight_loss_pct_6mo was negative (-0.077 months per percentage point, p<1e-300).

Biomarker main effects initially suggested that er_positive patients had higher PFS (0.531 months, p<1e-300), while pik3ca_mutation (-0.582 months, p<1e-300), her2_positive (-0.471 months, p<1e-300), and higher ki67_pct (-0.019 months per percentage point, p<1e-300) were associated with lower PFS. These ER, PIK3CA, and Ki-67 findings were then refined through treatment interaction models, because the strongest signal in the cohort was treatment-predictive rather than purely prognostic.

Palbociclib was the dominant treatment-associated pattern. In the adjusted main-effects model, treatment_palbociclib was associated with 1.102 additional months of pfs_months (p<1e-300). Interaction testing showed that this benefit was concentrated in ER-positive disease: the treatment_palbociclib by er_positive interaction was 1.536 months (p<1e-300), and the ER-negative subgroup effect was essentially zero (-0.003 months, p=3.66e-1). PIK3CA mutation strongly attenuated palbociclib benefit: the treatment_palbociclib by pik3ca_mutation interaction was -1.649 months (p<1e-300). In ER-positive PIK3CA-wildtype patients, palbociclib was associated with 2.395 additional months (p<1e-300), while in ER-positive PIK3CA-mutated patients the effect was 0.004 months (p=2.99e-1).

Ki-67 further separated the palbociclib-responsive subgroup. The treatment_palbociclib by centered ki67_pct interaction was -0.053 months per Ki-67 percentage point (p<1e-300), meaning higher proliferation reduced the palbociclib-associated PFS gain. The most favorable subgroup, er_positive with PIK3CA-wildtype and ki67_pct <= 25, had a 2.802 month palbociclib association (p<1e-300). In otherwise similar ER-positive PIK3CA-wildtype patients with ki67_pct > 25, the palbociclib coefficient was -0.002 months (p=7.39e-1). Stage IV status did not materially modify palbociclib effect; the palbociclib by stage_iv interaction was 0.017 months (p=4.73e-1).

Several expected targeted-therapy biomarker interactions were not supported. The treatment_tamoxifen by er_positive interaction was 0.031 months (p=2.06e-1). The treatment_trastuzumab by her2_positive interaction was 0.022 months (p=5.03e-1). The treatment_olaparib by BRCA1/2-any mutation interaction was 0.017 months (p=8.27e-1). The treatment_pembrolizumab by autoimmune_disease interaction was 0.021 months (p=7.47e-1), not the hypothesized lower effect in autoimmune disease. The treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan by her2_low interaction was 0.048 months (p=1.73e-1).

Overall, the cohort shows strong clinical severity associations and a highly specific treatment-biomarker-outcome relationship for palbociclib: benefit is concentrated in er_positive, PIK3CA-wildtype, lower-Ki-67 patients, and disappears in ER-negative, PIK3CA-mutated, or high-Ki-67 subgroups. Other examined treatment-biomarker hypotheses were refuted or statistically unsupported in this dataset.
