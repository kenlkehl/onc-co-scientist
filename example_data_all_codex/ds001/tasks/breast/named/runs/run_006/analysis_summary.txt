Dataset ds001_breast contained 50,000 breast oncology records with no missing values. The outcome `pfs_months` ranged from 0.00 to 16.66 months with mean 4.69 months. I used adjusted ordinary least squares models for `pfs_months`; coefficient tests use large-sample normal approximations. The common adjustment set included age, sex, ECOG performance status, stage, receptor and mutation features, tumor burden and metastatic sites, inflammatory/nutritional laboratory markers, and treatment indicators. Interaction models added both main effects and the specified product term.

The prognostic pass showed strong independent structure. Older age per 10 years: effect +1.757 months, p=<1e-299, significant. Higher ECOG performance status: effect -1.172 months, p=<1e-299, significant. Stage IV disease: effect -1.549 months, p=<1e-299, significant. Brain metastases: effect -0.983 months, p=<1e-299, significant. Weight loss per 5 percentage points: effect -0.384 months, p=<1e-299, significant. Albumin per 1 g/dL: effect +0.476 months, p=<1e-299, significant. Ki-67 per 10 percentage points: effect -0.189 months, p=<1e-299, significant. PIK3CA mutation: effect -0.582 months, p=<1e-299, significant. ER positivity: effect +0.531 months, p=5.09e-278, significant. HER2 positivity: effect -0.472 months, p=4.64e-279, significant. LDH per 100 U/L: effect -0.052 months, p=1.85e-16, significant.

These results support longer PFS with older age, ER positivity, and higher albumin, and shorter PFS with worse ECOG status, stage IV disease, brain metastases, higher weight loss, higher Ki-67, PIK3CA mutation, HER2 positivity, and higher LDH. The largest adjusted non-treatment effects were age, ECOG, stage IV status, and brain metastases.

Treatment analyses were dominated by palbociclib. Palbociclib main effect: effect +1.102 months, p=<1e-299, significant. Palbociclib by ER-positive interaction: effect +1.559 months, p=<1e-299, significant. Palbociclib by PR-positive interaction: effect +1.028 months, p=<1e-299, significant. Palbociclib by PIK3CA mutation interaction: effect -1.665 months, p=<1e-299, significant. Palbociclib by above-median Ki-67 interaction: effect -1.573 months, p=<1e-299, significant. Palbociclib by HER2-positive interaction: effect -1.337 months, p=<1e-299, significant. Palbociclib by triple-negative interaction: effect -1.379 months, p=<1e-299, significant. Palbociclib by HER2-low interaction: effect +0.382 months, p=3.01e-67, significant.

The palbociclib pattern is internally consistent: the average adjusted association was strongly positive, but the benefit was concentrated in hormone-receptor-positive disease and reduced in PIK3CA-mutated, high-proliferation, HER2-positive, or triple-negative subgroups. HER2-low status showed a smaller positive interaction.

The remaining treatment-biomarker probes were mixed. Sacituzumab govitecan by triple-negative interaction: effect +0.047 months, p=0.257, not significant. Sacituzumab govitecan by brain metastases interaction: effect +0.238 months, p=3.05e-5, significant. Pembrolizumab by above-median Ki-67 interaction: effect +0.073 months, p=0.009, significant. Trastuzumab by HER2-positive interaction: effect +0.022 months, p=0.503, not significant. Olaparib by any BRCA1/BRCA2 mutation interaction: effect +0.017 months, p=0.827, not significant. Tamoxifen by ER-positive interaction: effect +0.031 months, p=0.206, not significant.

Thus, the expected triple-negative sacituzumab, HER2-positive trastuzumab, BRCA-mutated olaparib, and ER-positive tamoxifen enrichment hypotheses were not supported in these adjusted models, although sacituzumab showed a positive interaction with brain metastases and pembrolizumab showed a small positive interaction with above-median Ki-67.

Finally, receptor and mutation interaction tests refined the biomarker story. PIK3CA mutation by ER-positive interaction: effect -0.826 months, p=1.97e-283, significant. Above-median Ki-67 by ER-positive interaction: effect -0.760 months, p=9.89e-270, significant. These results indicate that PIK3CA mutation and high proliferation were especially adverse in ER-positive tumors.

Overall conclusion: this cohort shows a strong prognostic axis of performance status, stage/metastatic burden, nutritional/inflammatory status, proliferation, and receptor biology. The clearest treatment-biomarker-outcome relationship is palbociclib sensitivity in hormone-receptor-positive, lower-proliferation, PIK3CA-wild-type disease, with attenuation in HER2-positive and triple-negative settings. Several clinically expected treatment enrichments were not statistically supported, so they should not be inferred from this dataset without external validation.
