Dataset ds001_breast contained 50,000 patients with no missing values. The outcome was pfs_months (mean 4.693, SD 2.496; median 4.535 months). I tested hypotheses with linear models on pfs_months; main effects were adjusted for age_years, stage_iv, ecog_ps, has_brain_mets, liver_mets, bone_mets, node_positive, albumin_g_dl, ldh_u_l, weight_loss_pct_6mo, ki67_pct, and tumor_size_cm as applicable. Interaction tests included the treatment, subgroup marker, their product, and the same clinical covariates.

Clinical severity showed the clearest main effects. stage_iv was associated with 1.551 fewer PFS months, each ECOG point with 1.166 fewer months, and has_brain_mets with 0.993 fewer months; all were highly significant. Better nutritional status was favorable: albumin_g_dl had a +0.484 month adjusted coefficient. Higher weight_loss_pct_6mo, ki67_pct, and ldh_u_l were each significantly adverse, although the LDH per-unit coefficient was small. Larger tumor_size_cm was not independently associated with PFS after adjustment.

Biomarker main effects were also present. er_positive was associated with +0.547 adjusted PFS months, while her2_positive and pik3ca_mutation were associated with -0.468 and -0.587 months, respectively. pr_positive did not remain significant after er_positive was included, and her2_low did not remain significant after accounting for her2_positive.

The dominant treatment signal was treatment_palbociclib. Its adjusted main association was +1.102 PFS months. Interaction analyses showed substantial heterogeneity: the palbociclib association was larger in er_positive patients (+1.574 month interaction) and pr_positive patients (+1.036), but smaller in pik3ca_mutation patients (-1.678), her2_positive patients (-1.346), and patients with high ki67_pct (-1.471). A simultaneous model including er_positive, pik3ca_mutation, her2_positive, and high Ki-67 interactions preserved the same pattern, with interactions of +1.532, -1.645, -1.315, and -1.455 months. Within pik3ca_mutation = 1 patients, the palbociclib mean difference was only +0.036 months and was not significant, supporting attenuation of benefit in that subgroup.

Several biologically plausible treatment-biomarker hypotheses were not supported in this cohort. Tamoxifen did not show a positive ER-specific association (interaction -0.002, p = 0.954; ER-positive subgroup difference +0.023, p = 0.4498). Trastuzumab did not show significant positive interactions with her2_positive or her2_amplification, and the HER2-positive subgroup difference was nonsignificant. Olaparib showed nonsignificant BRCA1 and BRCA2 interactions; BRCA-mutated subgroup differences were positive but imprecise and nonsignificant. Sacituzumab govitecan was not associated with higher PFS in HER2-low, triple-negative, or HER2-low triple-negative subgroups.

Pembrolizumab did not show convincing adverse heterogeneity by autoimmune_disease or high nlr. The autoimmune interaction was essentially null (-0.008 months, p = 0.9132). The high-NLR interaction was negative (-0.067 months) but did not reach significance (p = 0.07773).

Overall, this dataset points to a coherent PFS structure: advanced stage, worse performance status, brain metastases, systemic inflammation/cachexia markers, high proliferation, HER2-positive status, and PIK3CA mutation are adverse, while ER positivity and higher albumin are favorable. Treatment-biomarker heterogeneity is concentrated around palbociclib, with the strongest apparent benefit in hormone-receptor-positive biology and attenuation in PIK3CA-mutant, HER2-positive, and high-proliferation disease. Other named therapies did not show statistically supported biomarker-specific PFS advantages in these analyses.
