Analysis summary for ds001_breast

Cohort: 50,000 breast oncology records with `pfs_months` as the continuous outcome. The analyses used adjusted OLS models on the natural month scale, with covariate adjustment for demographics, disease burden, biomarkers, labs, and treatment indicators. Interaction tests added one treatment-by-subgroup or biologic interaction term at a time.

Treatment main effects: palbociclib was the only clear treatment main effect, with +1.10 months adjusted PFS (p<1e-300). Tamoxifen (-0.008 months, p=0.4935), trastuzumab (-0.010, p=0.4543), olaparib (-0.026, p=0.1202), sacituzumab govitecan (+0.033, p=0.0502), and pembrolizumab (-0.019, p=0.1696) did not show significant adjusted main-effect benefit.

Treatment-biomarker heterogeneity: the strongest supported structure was palbociclib effect modification. The palbociclib association was larger in ER-positive disease (+1.56 interaction, p<1e-300), larger in PR-positive disease (+1.03, p<1e-300), larger in HER2-low disease (+0.38, p<1e-300), and much smaller in PIK3CA-mutated (-1.67, p<1e-300) and HER2-positive disease (-1.34, p<1e-300). In the ER-positive/HER2-negative/PIK3CA-wildtype subgroup, palbociclib was associated with +2.92 months PFS; within ER-positive/HER2-negative disease, PIK3CA mutation reduced the palbociclib association by about -2.92 months.

Several clinically plausible matching hypotheses were refuted. Tamoxifen did not show ER-positive or postmenopausal enrichment. Trastuzumab did not show HER2-positive, HER2-amplified, HER2-low, or combined HER2-positive/amplified enrichment. Olaparib did not show significant BRCA1, BRCA2, or combined BRCA1/2 enrichment. Pembrolizumab did not show significant triple-negative, high-CRP, autoimmune-disease, or NLR-defined heterogeneity. Sacituzumab govitecan did not show significant triple-negative, visceral-metastasis, stage-IV, or liver-metastasis enrichment, but did show a small positive interaction with brain metastases (+0.193 months, p=0.0021).

Disease burden and performance status were strong adverse predictors. Stage IV disease was associated with -1.55 months PFS, ECOG performance status with -1.17 months per point, and brain metastases with -0.98 months, all highly significant. Liver metastases, bone metastases, and node-positive disease were not independently significant after broader adjustment. Stage-IV-by-liver-metastasis and brain-metastasis-by-pleural-effusion interactions were not significant.

Laboratory, cachexia, and proliferation signals were mixed. Higher albumin was favorable (+0.476 months per 1 g/dL), while higher LDH (-0.052 months per 100 U/L), greater 6-month weight loss (-0.384 months per 5 percentage points), and higher Ki-67 (-0.189 months per 10 points) were adverse. CRP and NLR were essentially null after adjustment, as was tumor size. Low albumin did not significantly amplify the weight-loss effect, but high Ki-67 was significantly more adverse among ER-positive patients rather than weaker.

Biomarker main effects showed ER positivity as favorable (+0.532 months), HER2-positive status as adverse (-0.471 months), PIK3CA mutation as adverse (-0.582 months), and BRCA1 mutation as a small adverse signal (-0.068 months). PR positivity, HER2-low status, and BRCA2 mutation were not independently significant. HER2-positive patients had lower adjusted PFS than HER2-low patients by about -0.473 months.

Overall conclusion: the clearest clinically meaningful treatment-biomarker-outcome relationship in this cohort is a palbociclib-associated PFS advantage concentrated in hormone-receptor-positive, HER2-negative, PIK3CA-wildtype disease. Prognosis was also strongly shaped by stage IV disease, ECOG performance status, brain metastases, Ki-67, PIK3CA mutation, weight loss, albumin, and LDH. Routine comorbidity indicators and inflammatory markers were much smaller or null compared with tumor burden, tumor biology, and palbociclib subgroup effects.
