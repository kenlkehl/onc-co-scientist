Analysis summary for ds001_breast

I analyzed 50,000 breast oncology records from dataset.parquet, using pfs_months as the outcome. Statistical tests were ordinary least-squares models on the natural month scale, with interaction terms for treatment-by-biomarker and treatment-by-subgroup hypotheses. Large-sample normal p-values were used for coefficient tests.

The strongest baseline prognostic effects were stage, ECOG performance status, brain metastases, nutritional status, proliferation, and selected biomarkers. Stage IV disease was associated with 1.55 fewer PFS months after adjustment, each ECOG point with 1.17 fewer months, and brain metastases with 0.98 fewer months. Albumin was favorable (+0.48 months per g/dL), while six-month weight loss (-0.077 months per percentage point), LDH, and Ki-67 (-0.0189 months per percentage point) were adverse. Liver and bone metastases were not independently adverse after the broader adjustment.

Biomarker main effects showed ER positivity was favorable (+0.53 months), HER2 positivity was adverse (-0.47 months), and PIK3CA mutation was adverse (-0.58 months). PR positivity and HER2-low status were not independently associated with PFS after adjustment. BRCA1 mutation had a small adverse association, while BRCA2 mutation did not. Common comorbidities and sex were not meaningful independent PFS predictors in the expanded model.

The dominant treatment pattern was palbociclib heterogeneity. The adjusted palbociclib main effect was +1.10 months, but interaction analyses showed the benefit was concentrated in hormone receptor-positive, HER2-negative, PIK3CA-wildtype disease. Palbociclib benefit was much greater in ER-positive and PR-positive patients, was attenuated in HER2-positive disease, and was nearly absent in PIK3CA-mutated disease. The derived ER-positive/HER2-negative/PIK3CA-wildtype profile had the clearest signal: adjusted palbociclib association about +2.9 PFS months. High Ki-67 (>=30%) also attenuated palbociclib benefit, whereas low albumin did not modify it.

Other treatment hypotheses were mostly refuted. Tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib, sacituzumab govitecan, and pembrolizumab did not show robust adjusted main-effect PFS benefits in this cohort. Trastuzumab did not show a significant HER2-positive interaction. Olaparib had an unadjusted BRCA-any subgroup difference, but the adjusted BRCA-any interaction and subgroup coefficient were not significant. Sacituzumab govitecan was not beneficial in derived triple-negative disease, and pembrolizumab had only a non-significant trend in triple-negative disease with no autoimmune-disease interaction. A final broad screen found one nominal exploratory result: sacituzumab govitecan had a more favorable association in patients with brain metastases (+0.16 months interaction, p=0.0158), which should be treated cautiously because it emerged from interaction exploration.

Overall, the cohort appears structured around a strong palbociclib-sensitive endocrine biology pattern: ER/PR-positive, HER2-negative, PIK3CA-wildtype tumors have markedly longer PFS with palbociclib, while HER2 positivity, PIK3CA mutation, and high Ki-67 identify reduced or absent palbociclib benefit. Baseline disease severity and host nutritional status remain major independent prognostic factors.
