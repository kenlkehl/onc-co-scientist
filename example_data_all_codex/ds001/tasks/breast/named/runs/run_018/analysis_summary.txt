Oncology cohort ds001_breast analysis summary

I analyzed 50,000 breast oncology records with pfs_months as the outcome. Tests used mean comparisons for simple subgroup contrasts and adjusted OLS models for multivariable main effects and treatment-by-biomarker interactions. Because this is observational EHR-derived data, the findings are associational rather than causal; the broad interaction screen should be interpreted as exploratory.

The strongest baseline adverse factors were metastatic and clinical severity features. stage_iv=1 was associated with 1.536 fewer months of mean PFS than stage_iv=0 (3.617 vs 5.153 months, p<1e-300). has_brain_mets=1 was associated with 1.050 fewer months (3.750 vs 4.800, p<1e-300). In adjusted OLS, each 1-point higher ecog_ps was associated with -1.17175 months of PFS (p<1e-300). Higher age_years showed an adjusted positive association (+0.17568 months/year, p<1e-300), likely reflecting cohort and disease-biology structure rather than a causal age benefit.

Systemic condition and proliferation also mattered. Higher albumin_g_dl was favorable (+0.47582 months per g/dL, p<1e-300), while higher weight_loss_pct_6mo was adverse (-0.07678 months per percentage point, p<1e-300). Higher ki67_pct was independently adverse (-0.01889 months per percentage point, p<1e-300). ldh_u_l was weakly adverse in the adjusted model, while alkaline_phosphatase_u_l did not remain significant after adjustment.

Biomarker main effects showed that er_positive=1 was independently favorable (+0.53156 months, p<1e-300), whereas pr_positive=1 added little once ER status was modeled (+0.01403 months, p=0.325). her2_positive=1 was adverse (-0.47169 months, p<1e-300), and pik3ca_mutation=1 was also adverse (-0.58166 months, p<1e-300).

Among treatment main effects, palbociclib was the dominant signal. treatment_palbociclib=1 was associated with +1.10134 adjusted months of PFS (p<1e-300). In contrast, tamoxifen (-0.00767 months, p=0.489), trastuzumab (-0.00935, p=0.462), olaparib (-0.02625, p=0.123), sacituzumab govitecan (+0.03307, p=0.052), and pembrolizumab (-0.01960, p=0.167) had no significant adjusted overall main effect.

The most clinically coherent heterogeneity was within palbociclib. In a simultaneous interaction model, palbociclib benefit was larger in ER-positive disease (interaction +1.49830 months, p<1e-300), slightly larger with PR positivity after accounting for the other modifiers (+0.05301 months, p=0.032), and markedly smaller with PIK3CA mutation (-1.64678 months, p<1e-300), HER2 positivity (-1.31829 months, p<1e-300), or ki67_pct>=20 (-1.43540 months, p<1e-300). Mean subgroup contrasts were consistent: ER+/PIK3CA-wildtype patients had a +2.376 month palbociclib difference, whereas ER+/PIK3CA-mutant patients had only +0.035 months; ER+/Ki67<20 patients had +2.076 months, whereas ER+/Ki67>=20 patients had +0.017 months. In the composite ER-positive, PIK3CA-wildtype, HER2-negative, Ki67<20 subgroup, palbociclib users had mean PFS 8.133 vs 4.288 months without palbociclib, a +3.845 month difference (p<1e-300). Triple-negative disease showed no meaningful palbociclib benefit and had a negative interaction (-1.37944 months, p<1e-300).

Expected targeted interactions for other treatments were not supported. Trastuzumab did not show a larger effect in HER2-positive patients (interaction +0.02197 months, p=0.501). Olaparib did not show a larger adjusted effect in patients with any BRCA mutation (interaction +0.01780 months, p=0.822). Pembrolizumab interactions with triple-negative status (+0.03266, p=0.349), ki67_pct>=20 (+0.05787, p=0.077), and crp_mg_l>10 (+0.04098, p=0.284) were not significant. The one non-palbociclib exploratory interaction that remained notable was a larger sacituzumab govitecan association among patients with brain metastases (interaction +0.23749 months, p=3.05e-5), which should be treated as hypothesis-generating because it came from broad screening.

Overall, this cohort appears dominated by disease burden, performance status, hormone-receptor biology, HER2/PIK3CA biology, proliferation, and nutritional status. The clearest treatment-biomarker-outcome relationship is a large palbociclib-associated PFS advantage concentrated in ER-positive, HER2-negative, PIK3CA-wildtype, lower-Ki67 disease, with little apparent benefit in PIK3CA-mutant, HER2-positive, high-Ki67, or triple-negative subgroups. Other named therapies did not show convincing adjusted biomarker-specific benefit in these analyses.
