Dataset ds001_breast contained 50000 patients and one outcome, pfs_months. I ran 25 propose-test-refine iterations using Welch mean comparisons, adjusted OLS models, interaction models, and a final nested-model F-test.

Main biomarker and disease-burden findings: ER positivity was strongly favorable in crude analysis and remained favorable after adjustment. HER2 positivity was adverse in crude and adjusted analyses. Stage IV disease and brain metastases were strongly associated with shorter PFS. The crude liver metastasis hypothesis was not supported (near-zero positive difference, p=0.555), and the adjusted stage IV by liver metastases interaction was also not supported. Higher albumin was favorable after adjustment, whereas higher LDH was unfavorable. Low hemoglobin was not supported as an adverse subgroup in the adjusted model. The age hypothesis was refuted because older age was associated with longer, not shorter, adjusted PFS. Venous thromboembolism history showed no significant main effect at stage_iv=0, but its interaction with stage_iv was significantly negative.

Treatment-biomarker findings: In ER-positive patients, palbociclib had a large positive crude association with PFS, whereas tamoxifen did not. The high-ki67 interaction for palbociclib was significantly negative, indicating substantial attenuation in high-proliferation tumors. Trastuzumab was not supported in HER2-positive or HER2-negative crude subgroup tests, nor by the HER2-positive/HER2-amplification three-way interaction or HER2-low interaction. Olaparib had a modest positive association in BRCA1/2-mutated patients and no positive association in BRCA-wildtype patients; chronic kidney disease did not significantly modify the olaparib association. Pembrolizumab did not show the hypothesized positive subgroup association, and autoimmune disease, ECOG performance status, and interstitial lung disease history did not provide significant modifying evidence at the 0.05 level. Sacituzumab govitecan was not supported in inflammatory-burden, bone-metastasis, or low-hemoglobin interaction contexts.

Across the transcript, positive effect estimates indicate longer pfs_months for the named exposure or interaction, and negative estimates indicate shorter pfs_months or attenuation. Several proposed hypotheses were refuted by either null results or statistically significant effects in the opposite direction, including liver metastases as a crude adverse factor, pembrolizumab as beneficial among patients without autoimmune disease, PIK3CA-associated benefit modification by palbociclib, and older age as adverse. The final nested-model test supported the overall value of adding biomarker-matched treatment interactions beyond main effects and severity covariates.
