Analysis summary for ds001_breast

I analyzed 50,000 records from dataset.parquet with pfs_months as a continuous outcome. Models were ordinary least-squares regressions on the natural month scale. The main adjusted model included age_years/10, sex_female, ecog_ps, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, node_positive, postmenopausal, ER/PR/HER2/BRCA/PIK3CA markers, ki67_pct/10, tumor_size_cm, albumin_g_dl, ldh_u_l/100, weight_loss_pct_6mo/5, crp_mg_l/10, nlr, and the six treatment indicators. Interaction models added the relevant treatment, subgroup marker, and product term.

The strongest clinical severity signals were clear and directionally coherent except for age. Higher age_years was strongly associated with longer PFS (+1.7569 months per 10 years, p<1e-300). Poorer performance status and advanced disease were strongly adverse: ecog_ps=1 was associated with -1.1717 months, stage_iv=1 with -1.5489 months, and has_brain_mets=1 with -0.9833 months, all p<1e-300. Node positivity was not independently significant after these adjustments (-0.0177 months, p=0.082).

Biomarker main effects showed a favorable ER-positive pattern and adverse HER2-positive/PIK3CA/high-proliferation patterns. er_positive=1 was associated with +0.5313 months (p=4.67e-278), while pr_positive was not independently informative after ER adjustment (+0.0143 months, p=0.315). her2_positive=1 was associated with -0.4708 months (p=4.71e-242), but her2_low was null (+0.0019 months, p=0.865). pik3ca_mutation was strongly adverse (-0.5816 months, p<1e-300), and ki67_pct was adverse (-0.1889 months per 10 percentage points, p<1e-300).

General health and tumor-burden variables refined the prognostic picture. albumin_g_dl was favorable (+0.4756 months per 1 g/dL, p<1e-300), while weight_loss_pct_6mo was adverse (-0.3840 months per 5 percentage points, p<1e-300) and ldh_u_l was modestly adverse (-0.0518 months per 100 U/L, p=2.06e-16). crp_mg_l and nlr were not independently significant after these covariates were included. tumor_size_cm was also null after adjustment (+0.0013 months per cm, p=0.661).

Treatment findings were dominated by palbociclib. The adjusted main association for treatment_palbociclib was +1.1014 months (p<1e-300). Interaction analyses showed this was highly heterogeneous: the palbociclib association was much larger in er_positive patients (interaction +1.5593 months, p<1e-300), decreased as ki67_pct rose (interaction -0.5316 months per 10 percentage points, p<1e-300), and was markedly smaller with pik3ca_mutation (interaction -1.6655 months, p<1e-300). These modifiers remained strong in a joint palbociclib model.

Other treatment-biomarker hypotheses were not supported. treatment_tamoxifen showed no adjusted main association (-0.0077 months, p=0.487) and no ER-positive interaction (+0.0108 months, p=0.685). treatment_trastuzumab was null overall (-0.0095 months, p=0.454) and did not interact significantly with her2_positive or her2_amplification. treatment_olaparib was null overall (-0.0264 months, p=0.121) and did not show a significant any-BRCA interaction (+0.0438 months, p=0.616). treatment_pembrolizumab did not show a significant triple-negative interaction (+0.0665 months, p=0.0886). treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan had a small nominal main association (+0.0335 months, p=0.0487), but no triple-negative interaction (-0.0217 months, p=0.638).

Additional metastatic-site and comorbidity checks were mostly negative once stage, brain metastases, biomarkers, labs, and treatments were modeled. Derived visceral_mets had no independent association (-0.0011 months, p=0.926), and diabetes_mellitus (+0.0021 months, p=0.861) and hypertension (-0.0002 months, p=0.986) were null. Overall, the dataset supports a strong prognostic structure driven by stage/performance status/brain metastases, ER/HER2/PIK3CA/Ki-67 biology, albumin/weight loss/LDH, and a pronounced palbociclib benefit concentrated in ER-positive, lower-Ki-67, PIK3CA-wildtype patients. Most other named therapy-biomarker pairings were not statistically supported in this cohort.
