Dataset ds001_breast: 50000 patients, 77 opaque features, outcome pfs_months.
The identifier column patient_id was ignored. Mean pfs_months was 4.6929 months (SD 2.4958, range 0 to 16.66).

Main effects:
The dominant univariable and adjusted main effect was feature_044. In the all-main-effects model, each 1-SD increase in feature_044 corresponded to +1.7485 months of pfs_months (p=<1e-300).
Other strong adjusted main effects were feature_027 (-0.79403 months/SD), feature_039 (-0.70933 months/SD), feature_046 (+0.52471 months/SD), feature_015 (-0.2971 months/SD), feature_037 (-0.29651 months/SD), feature_031 (-0.277 months/SD), feature_004 (+0.23827 months/SD), feature_062 (-0.22978 months/SD), feature_069 (+0.24414 months/SD), and feature_063 (-0.18134 months/SD), all with very small p-values.
A smaller independent negative effect for feature_033 was also present (-0.041874 months/SD, p=1.99e-16). Nominal small effects for feature_005, feature_032, feature_017, and feature_059 were much weaker and should be treated as secondary.

Interaction and subgroup heterogeneity:
An exhaustive pairwise screen of standardized two-feature products found a concentrated interaction pattern. After adjustment for all 77 main effects, the largest valid interactions involved feature_046 with feature_031 (-0.37782 months per SD-product), feature_069 (+0.34126), feature_062 (-0.30784), feature_063 (-0.24571), and feature_010 (+0.23542), all p<1e-300.
A joint model containing all main effects plus the top 20 interaction candidates preserved the same feature_046-centered heterogeneity for feature_031 by feature_046 (-0.37633), feature_069 by feature_046 (+0.33232), feature_062 by feature_046 (-0.30148), and feature_063 by feature_046 (-0.24549). The feature_010 by feature_046 term was positive in the single-interaction model but attenuated in the joint model (+0.0092389, p=0.0783), so it was not independently supported after accounting for the other interaction candidates.
Raw subgroup checks agreed directionally. For feature_031 and feature_046, the 2x2 difference-in-differences was -1.623 months, indicating the positive feature_046 contrast was attenuated when feature_031=1. For feature_069 and feature_046, the raw difference-in-differences was +1.5944 months, indicating the feature_046 contrast was amplified when feature_069=1.
For continuous feature_062, the unadjusted slope was -0.0049353 months/SD when feature_046=0 and -0.64213 months/SD when feature_046=1, matching the negative feature_062 by feature_046 interaction. For feature_044, the positive slope was weaker when feature_039=1 (1.6757 months/SD) than when feature_039=0 (+1.7867 months/SD).

Nonlinearity:
Quadratic probes suggested that the linear feature_044 effect had a statistically detectable but small curved component (0.08203 months for standardized z^2, p=5.94e-25). The feature_062 quadratic term was 0.19036 months (p=3.19e-32), much smaller than its linear and interaction terms.

Overall conclusion:
The cohort shows a strong multivariable signal for pfs_months. Main effects explain substantial variation (R^2=0.792505), and adding the leading interaction candidates increases R^2 to 0.878932. The most clinically interpretable pattern is not a single marker alone but heterogeneity around feature_046: its favorable association with pfs_months is reduced in feature_031=1 and feature_063=1 contexts, increased in feature_069=1 contexts, and coupled with a stronger adverse feature_062 slope. The apparent feature_010 enhancement was not robust in the joint interaction model.
