Analysis summary for ds001_breast

I analyzed 50,000 records with outcome `pfs_months` (mean 4.693 months, SD 2.496, range 0 to 16.66). Because the features are de-identified, I describe patterns by column name and avoid assigning clinical labels that are not present in the data dictionary.

The dominant main effect was `feature_044`: in the all-feature OLS model, each 1-unit increase was associated with +0.1757 months of `pfs_months` (p<1e-300). A centered quadratic term for `feature_044` was also positive (+0.000604 months per squared centered unit, p=5.94e-25), suggesting mild upward curvature on top of the very strong linear trend.

Several independent adverse features were strongly supported. Compared with `feature_027 = 0`, `feature_027 = 1` was associated with -1.188 months and `feature_027 = 2` with -2.334 months after adjustment (both p<1e-300). Other adverse adjusted effects included `feature_039 = 1` (-1.549 months), `feature_015 = 1` (-0.983 months), `feature_037` (-0.0769 months per unit), `feature_031 = 1` (-0.582 months), `feature_062` (-0.0189 months per unit), `feature_063 = 1` (-0.471 months), and `feature_033` (-0.000519 months per unit). Favorable adjusted effects included `feature_046 = 1` (+1.101 months), `feature_004` (+0.475 months per unit), and `feature_069 = 1` (+0.532 months). Marginal unadjusted comparisons also showed `feature_010 = 1` (+0.362 months) and `feature_025 = 1` (+0.114 months) associated with higher `pfs_months`.

Not every screened feature was supported. The directional hypothesis that `feature_040 = 1` would lower `pfs_months` was refuted in the all-feature model: the adjusted coefficient was small, positive, and not significant (+0.0129 months, p=0.209).

Subgroup heterogeneity was present. The positive `feature_044` slope was weaker in adverse subgroups: `feature_044 * feature_039` was -0.0108 months per `feature_044` unit (p=2.55e-15), `feature_044 * feature_015` was -0.00675 (p=0.00107), and `feature_044 * (feature_027 = 2)` was -0.0129 (p=4.02e-9). The analogous interactions for `feature_027 = 1` and `feature_046 = 1` were negative but not significant at 0.05.

The strongest binary-binary heterogeneity centered on `feature_046`. The favorable marginal association of `feature_046 = 1` was nearly absent when `feature_031 = 1` or `feature_063 = 1`: difference-in-differences estimates were -1.623 months for `feature_046 * feature_031` and -1.364 months for `feature_046 * feature_063` (both highly significant). In contrast, `feature_046 = 1` and `feature_069 = 1` together showed a strong positive interaction of +1.594 months. Overall, the cohort shows a large continuous prognostic gradient, an ordered adverse `feature_027` pattern, multiple independent main effects, and clinically meaningful subgroup interactions that change the apparent benefit of favorable binary markers.
