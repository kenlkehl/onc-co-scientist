Dataset `ds001_breast` contains 50,000 records, 77 opaque feature columns, and one continuous outcome, `pfs_months`. I treated `pfs_months` as a continuous endpoint and used ordinary least squares for main effects and interaction tests. Binary features were also summarized as raw group mean differences. Because the feature names are opaque, I do not infer clinical meanings beyond the observed feature-outcome relationships.

The dominant main-effect pattern was clear. Higher `feature_044` was associated with substantially longer `pfs_months`; the adjusted effect was +0.1757 months per one-unit increase. Several variables had strong negative adjusted associations: `feature_027` (-1.1716 months per one-level increase), `feature_039=1` (-1.5488 months), `feature_015=1` (-0.9825 months), `feature_063=1` (-0.4708 months), `feature_031=1` (-0.5818 months), continuous `feature_062` (-0.0189 months per unit), and continuous `feature_037` (-0.0769 months per unit). Strong positive adjusted associations included `feature_046=1` (+1.1011 months), `feature_069=1` (+0.5316 months), and continuous `feature_004` (+0.4752 months per unit). `feature_033` was statistically significant but very small in scale (-0.00052 months per unit after adjustment).

Some univariate findings did not survive adjustment. The clearest example was `feature_025=1`: it was associated with +0.1144 months in the raw comparison, but the adjusted coefficient was essentially zero (+0.0017 months, p=0.879). This suggests that the apparent `feature_025` main effect was explained by other covariates. A few small adjusted effects (`feature_005`, `feature_032`, `feature_017`, `feature_059`) were nominally significant but modest and should be interpreted cautiously in the context of many tested features.

The strongest heterogeneity centered on `feature_046`. The apparent benefit of `feature_046=1` depended heavily on other features. It was much larger when `feature_069=1` (adjusted interaction +1.5594 months); raw cells showed almost no `feature_046` difference when `feature_069=0` (-0.0175 months) but a large positive difference when `feature_069=1` (+1.5770 months). The `feature_046` association was also larger when `feature_010=1` (interaction +1.0286 months). Conversely, the `feature_046` association was nearly absent in the `feature_063=1` and `feature_031=1` subgroups, with adjusted interactions of -1.3387 and -1.6653 months, respectively.

Continuous `feature_062` also participated in important interactions. Its negative association with `pfs_months` was stronger in the `feature_046=1` subgroup (interaction -0.0531 months per one-unit increase), but weaker in the `feature_031=1` subgroup (interaction +0.0294 months per one-unit increase). Additional binary-binary interactions showed attenuation of the positive `feature_069` association when `feature_063=1` (interaction -0.6489 months) and attenuation of the negative `feature_031` association when `feature_063=1` (interaction +0.7184 months).

Overall, the cohort shows a structured treatment-biomarker-like pattern despite opaque feature names: `feature_046` is the strongest positive binary correlate of longer progression-free survival, but its association is concentrated in favorable subgroups marked by `feature_069=1` and `feature_010=1` and is largely neutralized in subgroups marked by `feature_063=1` or `feature_031=1`. The most robust adverse main effects are `feature_039`, `feature_027`, `feature_015`, `feature_031`, `feature_063`, `feature_062`, and `feature_037`; the most robust favorable main effects are `feature_044`, `feature_046`, `feature_069`, and `feature_004`.
