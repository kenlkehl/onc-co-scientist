Dataset ds001_breast: 50000 patients, 77 anonymized features, outcome pfs_months.
The analysis excluded patient_id, screened main effects for all features, then refined the largest findings with multivariable adjustment, quartile threshold tests, and interaction/subgroup models. P-values are large-sample OLS normal approximations.

Main-effect findings:
- The strongest positive simple association was feature_046: +1.099 months per one-unit increase (p=<1e-300).
- The strongest negative simple association was feature_039: -1.535 months per one-unit increase (p=<1e-300).
- Among binary features, feature_039=1 differed by -1.535 months versus feature_039=0 (p=<1e-300).
- Standardized continuous-feature contrasts supported feature_044 as favorable (+1.75 months per SD, p=<1e-300) and feature_027 as unfavorable (-0.799 months per SD, p=<1e-300).

Adjusted findings:
- feature_046: adjusted effect 1.101 months, p=<1e-300.
- feature_069: adjusted effect 0.53 months, p=<1e-300.
- feature_004: adjusted effect 0.235 months, p=<1e-300.
- feature_044: adjusted effect 1.749 months, p=<1e-300.
- feature_039: adjusted effect -1.549 months, p=<1e-300.
- feature_027: adjusted effect -0.795 months, p=<1e-300.
- feature_015: adjusted effect -0.985 months, p=<1e-300.
- feature_031: adjusted effect -0.583 months, p=<1e-300.

Threshold findings:
- Highest-quartile feature_044 had +3.009 months versus lower values (p=<1e-300).
- Highest-quartile feature_027 had -1.459 months versus lower values (p=<1e-300).
- feature_044 >= 71.8 showed 3.009 months difference (p=<1e-300).
- feature_044 >= 58.2 showed 2.947 months difference (p=<1e-300).
- feature_044 >= 65 showed 2.826 months difference (p=<1e-300).
- feature_027 >= 1 showed -1.459 months difference (p=<1e-300).

Final threshold probes:
- Threshold feature_044 >= 71.8 (q=0.75) had a mean difference of 3.009 months (n=12600, p=<1e-300), supporting longer pfs_months above this cutoff.
- Threshold feature_044 >= 58.2 (q=0.25) had a mean difference of 2.947 months (n=37668, p=<1e-300), supporting longer pfs_months above this cutoff.
- Threshold feature_044 >= 65 (q=0.5) had a mean difference of 2.826 months (n=25044, p=<1e-300), supporting longer pfs_months above this cutoff.
- Threshold feature_027 >= 1 (q=0.5) had a mean difference of -1.459 months (n=32404, p=<1e-300), supporting shorter pfs_months above this cutoff.
- Threshold feature_027 >= 1 (q=0.75) repeated the same tied binary cutoff and confirmed the same -1.459 month contrast.

Overall conclusion:
The cohort contains several strong feature-outcome associations with pfs_months despite anonymized predictors. The most reproducible signals were the top screened main effects that remained significant in adjusted OLS models. Several interaction terms were statistically significant, suggesting heterogeneity in feature effects across subgroups; however, because feature labels are opaque, these should be treated as pattern-discovery results rather than mechanistic clinical claims. The final threshold probes supported the same feature_044 and feature_027 directions found in continuous and adjusted models.
