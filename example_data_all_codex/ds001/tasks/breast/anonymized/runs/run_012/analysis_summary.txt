Dataset ds001_breast contains 50,000 patient records with opaque feature columns and the continuous outcome pfs_months. No missing values were encountered in the decoded Parquet data. Mean pfs_months was 4.693 months (SD 2.496; range 0 to 16.660).

The strongest main effect was feature_044: each one-unit increase was associated with about +0.176 months of PFS after adjustment for all other features (p<1e-300). Feature_027 showed a strong ordinal negative gradient: mean PFS was 5.640 months at level 0, 4.444 at level 1, and 3.289 at level 2; the adjusted linear effect was -1.172 months per level (p<1e-300).

Several binary features had large adjusted associations. Supported adverse associations included feature_039=1 (-1.549 months), feature_015=1 (-0.983 months), feature_031=1 (-0.582 months), and feature_063=1 (-0.471 months), all highly significant. Supported favorable associations included feature_046=1 (+1.101 months) and feature_069=1 (+0.532 months), also highly significant. Continuous feature_037 was adverse (-0.0769 months per unit), feature_004 was favorable (+0.475 months per unit), feature_062 was adverse (-0.0189 months per unit), and feature_033 had a small but statistically robust adverse adjusted association (-0.000518 months per unit).

Not every unadjusted signal persisted. Feature_010 had a clear unadjusted favorable difference (+0.362 months for feature_010=1), but after adjustment the effect was only +0.0136 months and not significant (p=0.340), indicating that its apparent main effect was explained by other covariates.

Interaction testing showed substantial subgroup heterogeneity centered on feature_046, feature_031, feature_069, feature_062, and feature_063. The favorable feature_046 association was strongly attenuated by feature_031 (-1.665 interaction months), feature_062 (-0.0531 per feature_062 unit), and feature_063 (-1.339), but strengthened by feature_069 (+1.559); all had p<1e-300. Feature_031 and feature_069 had a negative interaction (-0.826), while feature_031 interactions with feature_062 (+0.0294) and feature_063 (+0.718) suggested attenuation of feature_031-associated harm in those subgroups.

Additional interactions indicated that feature_062 reduced the favorable feature_069 association (-0.0263) and that feature_063 attenuated the adverse feature_062 slope (+0.0222). The positive feature_044 association was weaker when feature_039=1 (-0.0106 interaction months), at higher feature_027 levels (-0.00626), and when feature_015=1 (-0.00630). In contrast, the hypothesized feature_040 by feature_062 interaction was not supported (interaction -0.00104, p=0.216).

Overall, the cohort appears dominated by a few large main-effect gradients plus strong multivariable interactions. The most reproducible pattern was a favorable feature_044 gradient and adverse feature_027/feature_039 gradients, with treatment- or biomarker-like heterogeneity involving feature_046, feature_031, feature_069, feature_062, and feature_063.
