Analysis summary for ds001_breast

Dataset and methods
I analyzed 50,000 records with 77 opaque feature columns and the continuous outcome `pfs_months`. I used binary mean comparisons for 0/1 features, categorical contrasts for the three-level `feature_027`, univariate OLS for continuous features, full main-effects OLS with all 77 features, and selected interaction OLS models. Effect estimates are signed in months of `pfs_months`; p-values are two-sided large-sample tests.

Main effects
The strongest univariate and fully adjusted main effects were stable. Higher `feature_044` was strongly associated with longer PFS (+0.1759 months per unit univariately; +0.1757 after adjustment, p=2.0e-300). `feature_027` showed a clear ordinal negative gradient: mean PFS was 5.640 at level 0, 4.444 at level 1, and 3.289 at level 2; adjusted category effects were -1.188 months for level 1 and -2.334 months for level 2 versus level 0. Binary `feature_039`, `feature_015`, `feature_031`, and `feature_063` were adverse, while `feature_046` and `feature_069` were favorable. Continuous `feature_004` was favorable, while `feature_037` and `feature_062` were adverse. A full 77-feature main-effects model explained substantial variation (R^2=0.7925).

Some apparent univariate signals were not independent. `feature_010` was +0.362 months univariately but only +0.0136 months after adjustment (p=0.340), and `feature_025` similarly collapsed to +0.0017 months (p=0.879). Smaller adjusted-only effects included `feature_033` (-0.000518 months/unit, p=1.97e-16), `feature_005` (-0.0936 months, p=0.012), `feature_032` (-0.0689 months, p=0.0329), and `feature_059` (+0.0334 months, p=0.0492).

Nonlinearity and interactions
The association of `feature_044` was not purely linear: after controlling for all other features, the squared term was positive (+0.000611 months/unit^2, p=7.33e-60). Its positive slope was weaker among `feature_039`=1 patients and at higher `feature_027` levels.

The main interaction structure centered on `feature_046`. Its favorable association was essentially absent in several subgroups: when `feature_031`=1, the `feature_046` difference was only +0.036 months (p=0.286) versus +1.659 months when `feature_031`=0; when `feature_069`=0, it was -0.017 months (p=0.627) versus +1.577 months when `feature_069`=1; and when `feature_063`=1, it was -0.014 months (p=0.765) versus +1.350 months when `feature_063`=0. It also declined with higher `feature_062`: +1.881 months below the median of `feature_062` versus +0.319 months above it. In the selected interaction model, the corresponding adjusted interaction coefficients were large and highly significant: `feature_046:feature_031` -1.658, `feature_046:feature_069` +1.546, `feature_046:feature_062` -0.0520 per unit, and `feature_046:feature_063` -1.329.

Additional interactions showed less-than-additive or subgroup-specific adverse effects: `feature_031:feature_069` was negative (-0.830), `feature_031:feature_062` positive (+0.0291 per unit), `feature_069:feature_063` negative (-0.657), `feature_031:feature_063` positive (+0.728), and `feature_062:feature_063` positive (+0.0201 per unit). Adding these selected interactions increased model R^2 to 0.8790, indicating meaningful subgroup heterogeneity beyond main effects.

Overall conclusion
The cohort contains a strong, reproducible main-effect pattern plus clinically meaningful interaction structure among opaque features. Longer PFS is associated with higher `feature_044`, `feature_046`=1, `feature_069`=1, and higher `feature_004`; shorter PFS is associated with higher `feature_027`, `feature_039`=1, `feature_015`=1, `feature_031`=1, `feature_063`=1, higher `feature_037`, and higher `feature_062`. The strongest subgroup pattern is that the favorable `feature_046` association depends heavily on `feature_031`, `feature_069`, `feature_063`, and `feature_062`. A favorable four-feature profile (`feature_046`=1, `feature_069`=1, `feature_031`=0, `feature_063`=0) had 2.866 months longer mean PFS than the opposite profile (p=3.70e-202).
