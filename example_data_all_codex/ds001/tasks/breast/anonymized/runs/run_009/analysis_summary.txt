Dataset: ds001_breast
Rows analyzed: 50000
Outcome: pfs_months

I analyzed the local parquet file only. The cohort had mean PFS 4.693 months (SD 2.496, range 0 to 16.660). A main-effects model including all features explained a large share of outcome variation (R^2 = 0.793), and adding the strongest interactions increased explanatory fit further.

Main effects supported by both unadjusted and adjusted analyses were concentrated in a small set of features. Continuous feature_044 was the dominant positive predictor: +0.1759 months per unit unadjusted and +0.1757 months after adjustment. Ordinal feature_027 was strongly adverse, with mean PFS declining from 5.640 months at value 0 to 4.444 at value 1 and 3.289 at value 2; the adjusted linear effect was -1.172 months per category. Binary feature_039 was adverse (-1.549 adjusted months), binary feature_046 was favorable (+1.101 adjusted months), binary feature_015 was adverse (-0.983 adjusted months), continuous feature_037 was adverse (-0.0769 months per unit), binary feature_031 was adverse (-0.582 adjusted months), binary feature_069 was favorable (+0.532 adjusted months), continuous feature_004 was favorable (+0.475 months per unit), continuous feature_062 was adverse (-0.0189 months per unit), and binary feature_063 was adverse (-0.471 adjusted months).

Several unadjusted signals did not survive multivariable adjustment. Binary feature_010 appeared favorable unadjusted (+0.362 months) but was essentially null after adjustment (+0.0136 months, p=0.340). Binary feature_025 similarly dropped from an unadjusted +0.114 months to +0.0017 adjusted months (p=0.879). Smaller adjusted effects remained for feature_033 (-0.000518 months per unit, p=2.22e-16), feature_005 (-0.0936 months, p=0.012), and feature_032 (-0.0689 months, p=0.033), but these were much smaller than the primary signals.

The strongest heterogeneity centered on feature_046. The favorable feature_046 association was highly context dependent: feature_046 by feature_031 was negative (-1.665 months), feature_046 by feature_069 was positive (+1.559 months), and feature_046 by feature_063 was negative (-1.339 months), all after adjustment for all main features. Cell means supported these directions. For feature_046 and feature_031, the feature_046=1/feature_031=0 subgroup averaged 5.966 months, while the joint feature_046=1/feature_031=1 subgroup averaged only 4.352 months. For feature_046 and feature_069, the joint feature_046=1/feature_069=1 subgroup averaged 5.890 months, whereas the other cells were near 4.29 to 4.31 months. For feature_046 and feature_063, feature_046=1/feature_063=0 averaged 5.657 months, but feature_046=1/feature_063=1 averaged 4.308 months.

Feature_062 also showed marked subgroup heterogeneity. Its adverse slope was nearly absent when feature_046=0 (-0.0004) but strong when feature_046=1 (-0.0528), with an adjusted interaction of -0.0531 months per feature_062 unit. The feature_062 slope was adverse when feature_031=0 (-0.0295) but absent when feature_031=1 (+0.0003), giving a positive adjusted interaction (+0.0294). The slope was near-null when feature_069=0 (-0.0009) but adverse when feature_069=1 (-0.0263), giving a negative adjusted interaction (-0.0263). It was adverse when feature_063=0 (-0.0226) but near-null when feature_063=1 (-0.0015), giving a positive adjusted interaction (+0.0222).

Additional binary-binary interactions among the same subgroup features were supported: feature_031 by feature_069 was negative (-0.826 months), feature_031 by feature_063 was positive (+0.718 months), and feature_069 by feature_063 was negative (-0.649 months), after adjustment for all main effects. These results suggest the apparent favorable and adverse main effects are not independent; they combine in specific multi-feature patterns.

I also tested whether the feature_044 effect varied by other major predictors. The positive feature_044 slope was weaker when feature_039=1 (interaction -0.0106), weaker at higher feature_027 values (-0.00626 per feature_027 category), slightly stronger at higher feature_004 values (+0.00343), and weaker when feature_015=1 (-0.00630). These interactions were statistically significant but much smaller than the feature_046-centered interactions.

Exploratory interactions that looked plausible in lower-dimensional screens did not always persist. The feature_027 by feature_033 interaction was not significant after all-main-effect adjustment (p=0.198), and the feature_015 by feature_037 interaction was also not significant after adjustment (p=0.397).

Finally, I tested simple nonlinear terms. Feature_044 had a positive adjusted quadratic component (+0.0605 months per squared SD), and feature_062 also had a positive adjusted quadratic component (+0.0618 months per squared SD), indicating curvature around their main linear effects. In contrast, feature_037 and feature_004 did not show detectable quadratic terms in this screen.

Overall conclusion: PFS in this cohort is dominated by strong main effects for feature_044, feature_027, feature_039, feature_046, feature_015, feature_037, feature_031, feature_069, feature_004, feature_062, and feature_063, plus a coherent interaction structure involving feature_046, feature_031, feature_069, feature_063, and feature_062. Because the feature labels are de-identified, these findings should be interpreted as statistical treatment/biomarker/outcome patterns in the opaque feature space rather than as clinically named mechanisms.
