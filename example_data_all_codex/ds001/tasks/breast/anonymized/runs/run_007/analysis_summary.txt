Dataset ds001_breast contains 50,000 patients with pfs_months as the outcome. I analyzed all local columns in dataset.parquet, excluding patient_id, using large-sample mean comparisons and OLS models with interaction terms. The features are opaque, so conclusions are stated in terms of the observed feature labels rather than inferred clinical names.

The strongest main-effect pattern was feature_044: pfs_months increased by 0.176 months per 1-unit increase, with quartile means rising from 2.50 to 6.96 months (p<1e-299). Higher feature_027 category showed the opposite pattern, decreasing pfs_months by 1.180 months per category; category means were 5.64, 4.44, and 3.29 months (p<1e-299). Binary adverse markers included feature_039 (-1.536 months), feature_015 (-1.050), feature_063 (-0.459), and feature_031 (-0.559), all p<1e-299. Binary favorable markers included feature_046 (+1.098), feature_069 (+0.568), and feature_010 (+0.362) in unadjusted comparisons.

Continuous feature_062 and feature_037 were adverse, with slopes of -0.0188 and -0.0747 months per unit, while feature_004 was favorable with a +0.499 month per unit slope. Smaller unadjusted signals such as feature_025 did not hold up after multivariable adjustment: feature_025 dropped from +0.114 months unadjusted to +0.0017 months adjusted (p=0.883). feature_010 also lost its independent main effect after adjustment (+0.014 months, p=0.320), suggesting its marginal signal is explained by the stronger feature cluster and interactions.

The clearest interaction structure centered on feature_046. Its favorable association was essentially absent when feature_069=0 but large when feature_069=1; the adjusted feature_069 x feature_046 interaction was +1.559 months (p<1e-299). A similar but smaller amplification appeared with feature_010 x feature_046 (+1.030 months, p<1e-299). In contrast, feature_031 and feature_063 markedly attenuated the feature_046 benefit: adjusted interactions were -1.666 and -1.338 months, respectively, both p<1e-299.

feature_062 also showed subgroup heterogeneity. Among feature_046=0 patients, the feature_062 slope was near zero (-0.0004), but among feature_046=1 patients it was strongly negative (-0.0528); adjusted interaction -0.0532, p<1e-299. Among feature_031=0 patients, feature_062 was adverse (-0.0295), while among feature_031=1 patients it was nearly flat (+0.0003); adjusted interaction +0.0294, p<1e-299.

Additional interaction tests showed that the favorable feature_069 effect was attenuated by feature_031 and feature_063 (interactions -0.825 and -0.649 months, p<1e-299), and the favorable unadjusted feature_010 effect was attenuated by feature_031 (-0.555 months, p<1e-299). The feature_044 effect was statistically weaker in feature_039=1 patients and at higher feature_027 category, though the positive feature_044 slope remained large in all strata. A proposed positive feature_069 x feature_010 interaction was not supported after adjustment (+0.023 months, p=0.451).

Nonlinearity checks supported positive curvature for feature_044 (+0.000604 centered-square coefficient) and feature_062 (+0.000386), both p<1e-299. Overall, this cohort shows a dominant continuous favorable gradient for feature_044, a strong adverse ordinal gradient for feature_027, several independent binary main effects, and a major interaction cluster in which feature_046 benefit is amplified by feature_069/feature_010 and suppressed by feature_031/feature_063.
