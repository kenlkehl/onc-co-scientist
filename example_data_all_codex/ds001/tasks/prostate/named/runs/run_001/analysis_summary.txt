Analysis summary for ds001_prostate

I analyzed 50,000 prostate-cancer records. The overall objective_response rate was 24.0%. Effects below are signed absolute changes in objective_response probability unless otherwise stated. Main-effect models adjusted for age, ECOG performance status, mCRPC, visceral metastases, PSA, Gleason score, albumin, LDH, 6-month weight loss, CRP, NLR, all treatment flags, and key biomarker flags. Interaction models report treatment-subgroup risk-difference interactions.

The dominant treatment pattern was `treatment_enzalutamide`. It was associated with a large higher response probability overall (+20.2 percentage points, p<1e-12; unadjusted 36.1% on treatment vs 15.9% off treatment). However, this benefit was almost entirely limited to non-mCRPC patients: in `mcrpc` = 0, response was 61.0% with enzalutamide vs 16.9% without it, while in `mcrpc` = 1, response was 15.8% with enzalutamide vs 15.0% without it. The `treatment_enzalutamide:mcrpc` interaction was strongly negative (-43.2 percentage points, p<1e-12).

Several biomarkers were adverse prognostic markers after adjustment: `brca2_mutation` (-10.2 percentage points, p<1e-12), `ar_v7_positive` (-10.0 percentage points, p<1e-12), and `msi_high` (-6.3 percentage points, p=1.41e-9). These same markers also sharply attenuated the enzalutamide association: interaction effects were -24.2 percentage points for `ar_v7_positive`, -21.7 percentage points for `brca2_mutation`, and -23.2 percentage points for `msi_high`, all with p<1e-12.

Clinical disease-burden markers also mattered. `mcrpc` itself was strongly adverse (-18.8 percentage points, p<1e-12). Higher `ecog_ps`, `psa_ng_ml`, `weight_loss_pct_6mo`, and `crp_mg_l` were independently associated with lower response, while higher `albumin_g_dl` was associated with higher response. In contrast, adjusted main effects for `visceral_mets`, `psma_high`, `age_years`, and `treatment_docetaxel` were not significant.

Biologically expected precision-treatment patterns were mostly not supported. `treatment_lu177_psma:psma_high` was not positive (-0.7 percentage points, p=0.479). `treatment_pembrolizumab:msi_high` was near zero (+0.6 percentage points, p=0.898). `treatment_olaparib:brca2_mutation` was borderline significant in the opposite direction (-3.8 percentage points, p=0.049), so the expected BRCA2-specific olaparib benefit was refuted in this cohort.

Exploratory subgroup findings included a positive `treatment_pembrolizumab:her2_amplification` interaction (+11.9 percentage points, p=0.010), a small positive `treatment_abiraterone:brca2_mutation` interaction (+2.7 percentage points, p=0.034), a negative `treatment_lu177_psma:visceral_mets` interaction (-3.7 percentage points, p=0.005), and a negative `treatment_olaparib:pleural_effusion` interaction (-4.3 percentage points, p=0.015). These should be interpreted as exploratory because many interactions were screened and some positive cells were small, especially pembrolizumab plus HER2 amplification (n=80).

Overall, the clearest pattern was not a broad biomarker-matched treatment benefit. Instead, response was dominated by a large enzalutamide-associated response advantage in non-mCRPC, with loss of that advantage in mCRPC and in AR-V7-positive, BRCA2-mutated, and MSI-high subgroups. General disease severity and inflammatory/nutritional markers also tracked response in the expected directions.
