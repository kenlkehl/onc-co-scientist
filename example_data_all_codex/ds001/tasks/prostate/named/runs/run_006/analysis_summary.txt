Dataset ds001_prostate contained 50000 patients and had an objective_response rate of 0.240.

I ran 25 propose-test-update iterations focused on main treatment effects, biomarker associations, treatment-by-biomarker interactions, disease-burden features, inflammatory and nutritional markers, comorbidities, and rare genomic alterations. Analyses used two-proportion comparisons for descriptive rates and adjusted linear probability regressions with heteroskedasticity-robust standard errors for statistical tests. Effect estimates in the transcript are signed adjusted risk differences in objective_response probability, so positive values favor response and negative values indicate lower response.

Clinical synthesis: the dominant supported treatment signal was a large positive adjusted association for enzalutamide, but that benefit was sharply attenuated in AR-V7-positive disease. AR-V7 positivity itself, BRCA2 mutation, MSI-high status, mCRPC, worse ECOG performance status, higher weight loss, higher PSA, and higher CRP were associated with lower response, while higher albumin was associated with higher response. Contrary to biomarker-matched treatment expectations, olaparib-BRCA2, pembrolizumab-MSI-high, and Lu-177-PSMA-PSMA-high interactions were not statistically significant in this cohort. Docetaxel, abiraterone, pembrolizumab, olaparib, and Lu-177-PSMA also did not show significant adjusted main effects. Metastatic-site indicators, comorbidities, and rare alteration analyses were mostly weak or nonsignificant after adjustment.
