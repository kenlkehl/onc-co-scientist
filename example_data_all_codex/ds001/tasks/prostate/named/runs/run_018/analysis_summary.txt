Dataset: ds001_prostate
Rows analyzed: 50000
Outcome: objective_response (overall rate 0.24022)

Methods
I decoded the local Parquet file and analyzed objective_response on its natural probability scale. Main binary associations used two-proportion z-tests. Treatment-by-subgroup heterogeneity used four-cell risk-difference interactions, i.e. the difference in treatment risk differences between subgroup levels. Multivariable checks used HC1 robust linear probability models, so coefficients remain approximate absolute response-probability differences.

Main treatment pattern
The dominant signal was treatment_enzalutamide. Overall response was 0.361377 on enzalutamide versus 0.158936 off enzalutamide, an absolute difference of +0.202441 (p<1e-300). This effect was highly heterogeneous. In mcrpc=0 patients the enzalutamide risk difference was +0.440293, but in mcrpc=1 patients it was only +0.007655 (p=0.08565 within mCRPC); the interaction difference was -0.432638 (p<1e-300). Similar attenuation appeared for ar_v7_positive=1 and brca2_mutation=1: enzalutamide risk differences were +0.250989 in ar_v7_negative patients versus +0.008462 in ar_v7_positive patients, and +0.224572 in BRCA2-wildtype patients versus +0.006320 in BRCA2-mutated patients. The largest response concentration was in the mcrpc=0/ar_v7_positive=0/brca2_mutation=0 subgroup, where enzalutamide response was 0.780012 versus 0.173274 off treatment, an absolute difference of +0.606738 (p<1e-300).

The adjusted model confirmed that this was not just a marginal artifact. After adjustment for mcrpc, ar_v7_positive, brca2_mutation, high PSA, ECOG 2, high weight loss, high CRP, and low albumin, the reference-subgroup enzalutamide coefficient was +0.509340, while the interactions remained strongly negative: enzalutamide:mcrpc -0.431074, enzalutamide:ar_v7_positive -0.240970, and enzalutamide:brca2_mutation -0.216710, all with p<1e-300.

Other therapies and biomarkers
Abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, Lu177-PSMA, and pembrolizumab did not show positive overall main associations with objective_response. Abiraterone had a risk difference of -0.003062 (p=0.462), docetaxel -0.000494 (p=0.905), olaparib -0.001014 (p=0.872), Lu177-PSMA +0.002883 (p=0.591), and pembrolizumab -0.001623 (p=0.856).

Several clinically expected biomarker-treatment hypotheses were not supported. In brca2_mutation=1 patients, olaparib response was 0.120983 versus 0.153123 without olaparib, an absolute difference of -0.032140 (p=0.034094), opposite the hypothesized benefit; the adjusted olaparib:brca2 interaction was -0.034709 with p=0.051587. Among psma_high=1 patients, Lu177-PSMA response was essentially unchanged (-0.001146, p=0.868), and the Lu177-PSMA:psma_high interaction was not significant (-0.010007, p=0.36184). Among msi_high=1 patients, pembrolizumab response was also unchanged (+0.001232, p=0.97772), with a nonsignificant pembrolizumab:msi_high interaction (+0.002767, p=0.95102).

An exploratory exception was her2_amplification. In her2_amplification=1 patients, pembrolizumab response was 0.375000 with treatment versus 0.214535 without, a risk difference of +0.160465 (p=0.003652). The adjusted pembrolizumab:her2_amplification coefficient was +0.130957 (p=0.013768). This should be treated cautiously because the treated HER2-amplified cell contained only 80 patients.

Prognostic and clinical severity signals
mcrpc, ar_v7_positive, brca2_mutation, and msi_high were each associated with lower response in main-effect analyses. Response was lower in mcrpc=1 by -0.192495, ar_v7_positive=1 by -0.100505, brca2_mutation=1 by -0.100547, and msi_high=1 by -0.066196. In adjusted models that included treatment and other biomarkers, msi_high remained negative (-0.061422, p=1.5163e-8), while mcrpc, ar_v7_positive, and brca2_mutation remained key drivers in models without the detailed enzalutamide interaction structure.

Clinical risk markers also showed a coherent adverse pattern. ECOG 2 had a response deficit of -0.068951 unadjusted and -0.067016 adjusted. Top-quartile PSA (psa_ng_ml>=41.28) had a large unadjusted deficit of -0.116899 and a smaller but still significant adjusted deficit of -0.021099. Top-quartile weight loss (>=6.4%) remained strongly negative (-0.039408 adjusted), top-quartile CRP (>=6.9825 mg/L) remained negative (-0.026846 adjusted), and low albumin (<=3.5 g/dL) remained negative (-0.014963 adjusted). Gleason score 9-10 did not show a lower response rate than Gleason 6-8; its risk difference was +0.002049 (p=0.66969).

Subgroup and multivariable synthesis
A composite adverse-factor count using mcrpc, high PSA, ECOG 2, high weight loss, high CRP, low albumin, ar_v7_positive, and brca2_mutation showed a monotone decline in response: 0.456288 with zero factors, 0.319205 with one, 0.214513 with two, 0.151417 with three, 0.137770 with four, 0.092776 with five, and 0.063559 with six. Patients with four or more adverse factors had response 0.125983 versus 0.257245 with fewer than four factors, an absolute difference of -0.131262 (p<1e-300).

The strongest overall conclusion is that this cohort contains a large enzalutamide-associated response signal, but it is concentrated in patients without mCRPC, AR-V7 positivity, or BRCA2 mutation. Several other targeted therapy-biomarker pairs that might be expected clinically were neutral or opposite in this dataset. Prognostic severity variables, especially mCRPC, high PSA, ECOG 2, weight loss, inflammation, and low albumin, consistently marked lower objective response.
