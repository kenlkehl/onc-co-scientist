Analysis summary for ds001_prostate

The dataset contained 50000 patients and a mean objective_response rate of 0.240. I used direct Parquet decoding, two-proportion response-rate comparisons, and linear-probability regression with heteroskedasticity-robust normal-approximation p-values. Effect estimates are absolute response-probability differences.

The dominant treatment signal was enzalutamide. In the adjusted main-effects model, treatment_enzalutamide was associated with a +0.202 absolute response-probability difference (p<1e-300), with unadjusted response 0.361 on enzalutamide versus 0.159 off enzalutamide. Other treatments did not show comparable main effects in the adjusted model.

Disease state and biomarker main effects were strongly negative. mcrpc was associated with a -0.193 adjusted response-probability difference (p<1e-300). brca2_mutation and ar_v7_positive each carried about a -0.10 adjusted difference (both p<1e-300), and msi_high carried a -0.063 adjusted difference (p=2.35e-9). psma_high was not independently associated with higher response (effect -0.005, p=0.205).

General health and tumor-burden markers also mattered. A one-SD higher ecog_ps was associated with -0.034 response probability (p<1e-300), one-SD higher weight_loss_pct_6mo with -0.022 (p<1e-300), one-SD higher albumin_g_dl with +0.010 (p=1.54e-8), and one-SD higher crp_mg_l with -0.009 (p=2.79e-8). PSA showed a smaller univariable gradient: moving from the 25th to 75th percentile changed response by -0.014 (p<1e-300). NLR, liver metastases, MET exon 14 skipping, and age did not show significant response gradients in the tested contrasts.

The key heterogeneity finding was that enzalutamide benefit was concentrated outside mCRPC. The adjusted treatment_enzalutamide by mcrpc interaction was -0.430 (p=<1e-300). In mcrpc=0 patients, response was 0.610 on enzalutamide versus 0.169 off enzalutamide (RD 0.440, p=<1e-300). In mcrpc=1 patients, response was 0.158 versus 0.150 (RD 0.008, p=0.086), not statistically significant. A joint model including both enzalutamide-by-mCRPC and enzalutamide-by-AR-V7 found residual AR-V7 attenuation of enzalutamide response of -0.241 (p=<1e-300).

Several clinically plausible precision-treatment hypotheses were not supported. Abiraterone did not show AR-V7-specific attenuation (interaction 0.010, p=0.274). Olaparib did not show greater response in BRCA2-mutated patients; the interaction was negative (-0.035, p=0.050), and within BRCA2-mutated patients response was 0.121 on olaparib versus 0.153 off (RD -0.032, p=0.034). Pembrolizumab did not show MSI-high enrichment (interaction -0.011, p=0.811; MSI-high subgroup RD 0.001, p=0.978). Lu-177-PSMA did not show PSMA-high enrichment (interaction -0.008, p=0.418; PSMA-high subgroup RD -0.001, p=0.868). Docetaxel did not show a visceral-metastasis-specific advantage (interaction 0.003, p=0.736), and autoimmune disease did not significantly attenuate pembrolizumab response (interaction 0.007, p=0.851).

Overall, this cohort's response structure was driven less by expected targeted-therapy biomarker enrichment and more by a broad disease-state/prognostic axis plus a highly subgroup-dependent enzalutamide association. The strongest supported pattern was high enzalutamide-associated response in non-mCRPC patients, with marked loss of that association in mCRPC and additional attenuation with AR-V7 positivity.
