Analysis summary for ds001_prostate

I analyzed 50,000 prostate oncology records with binary objective_response as the endpoint. Effects are reported on the response-probability scale. For treatment and biomarker main effects I used adjusted linear-probability models including age, ECOG performance status, mCRPC status, visceral metastases, log PSA, key biomarkers, and the treatment indicators. For threshold and subgroup checks I used response-rate differences with two-proportion tests. Interaction hypotheses used treatment response-rate difference-in-differences, with adjusted interaction models for the main biomarker findings where applicable.

The dominant treatment main effect was for treatment_enzalutamide. After multivariable adjustment it was associated with a 20.2 percentage-point higher probability of objective_response (p<1e-300). In contrast, treatment_abiraterone (-0.45 percentage points, p=0.246), treatment_docetaxel (approximately 0, p=0.995), treatment_olaparib (+0.37 points, p=0.535), treatment_lu177_psma (+0.47 points, p=0.353), and treatment_pembrolizumab (-0.05 points, p=0.955) did not show significant overall associations.

Several disease severity and biomarker features were strongly negative. mCRPC was associated with a 17.7 percentage-point lower adjusted response probability (p<1e-300). ECOG performance status of 2 had a 6.9 point lower unadjusted response rate than ECOG below 2 (0.182 vs 0.250, p<1e-300). PSA at least 50 ng/mL had an 11.3 point lower response rate (0.151 vs 0.264, p<1e-300). BRCA2 mutation (-10.2 adjusted points, p<1e-300), AR-V7 positivity (-10.0 adjusted points, p<1e-300), and MSI-high status (-6.3 adjusted points, p=1.76e-9) were also negative. PSMA-high status was not associated with higher response after adjustment (-0.5 points, p=0.193).

Host/inflammatory markers showed smaller but directionally coherent negative associations. Albumin below 3.5 g/dL was associated with a 1.8 point lower response rate (p=5.08e-5), weight loss of at least 10% over 6 months with a 5.2 point lower response rate (p=6.66e-16), and CRP at least 10 mg/L with a 2.6 point lower response rate (p=3.74e-7).

The most important heterogeneity finding was that the enzalutamide association was concentrated outside several adverse subgroups. The enzalutamide response difference was only +0.8 points in mCRPC patients but +44.0 points in non-mCRPC patients, giving a difference-in-differences of -43.3 points (adjusted interaction -43.1 points, p<1e-300). The same attenuation appeared with AR-V7 positivity: +0.8 points among AR-V7-positive patients versus +25.1 points among AR-V7-negative patients (interaction -24.3 points, p<1e-300). Similar negative interactions were seen for BRCA2 mutation (-21.8 points, p<1e-300) and MSI-high status (-23.1 points, p<1e-300).

Expected targeted-treatment enrichment was not supported for several canonical pairs. Olaparib did not have greater benefit in BRCA2-mutated patients; the observed difference-in-differences was negative (-3.5 points, p=0.034 unadjusted; adjusted p=0.0526). Lu177-PSMA did not show greater benefit in PSMA-high patients (interaction -1.0 point, p=0.362), and pembrolizumab did not show greater benefit in MSI-high patients (interaction +0.3 points, p=0.951). An exploratory non-canonical pembrolizumab by HER2-amplification interaction was observed (+16.8 points unadjusted, p=0.00273; adjusted +12.7 points, p=0.0162), but this should be interpreted cautiously because it was found during broad subgroup probing and involves a small HER2-amplified pembrolizumab-treated subgroup. Lu177-PSMA also appeared less favorable in patients with visceral metastases (interaction -3.4 points, p=0.00865).

Overall, this cohort is dominated by a strong enzalutamide-response association and strong negative prognostic associations for mCRPC, high PSA, poor ECOG status, BRCA2 mutation, AR-V7 positivity, MSI-high status, weight loss, low albumin, and elevated CRP. The strongest treatment-biomarker pattern is not enhanced response to targeted therapies, but rather marked attenuation of enzalutamide-associated response in mCRPC and adverse biomarker subgroups, especially AR-V7-positive disease.
