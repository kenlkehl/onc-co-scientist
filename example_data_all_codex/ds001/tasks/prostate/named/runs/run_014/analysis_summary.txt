Dataset ds001_prostate contained 50000 prostate cancer patient records with no missing values in the analyzed columns. The overall objective_response rate was 0.2402.

I tested 25 hypotheses using linear probability models for adjusted main effects and interactions, plus two-proportion z tests for biomarker-defined treatment subgroups. Continuous predictors were standardized for the reported linear probability coefficients, so those effects are probability-point changes per 1-SD increase. The models are associational, not causal.

Treatment main effects: treatment_enzalutamide was the only clear overall treatment signal. Its adjusted association with objective_response was +0.20236 (p=<1e-250), with raw response 0.3614 on treatment versus 0.1589 off treatment. treatment_abiraterone (-0.00514, p=1.914e-1), treatment_docetaxel (0.00026, p=9.463e-1), treatment_olaparib (0.00294, p=6.217e-1), treatment_lu177_psma (0.00508, p=3.141e-1), and treatment_pembrolizumab (-0.00211, p=8.025e-1) did not show significant adjusted overall effects.

Clinical severity and inflammatory status: mcrpc was strongly associated with lower response in the additive adjusted model (-0.18693, p=<1e-250). Higher psa_ng_ml (-0.00952, p=4.648e-7), ecog_ps (-0.03455, p=<1e-250), weight_loss_pct_6mo (-0.02222, p=<1e-250), and crp_mg_l (-0.00908, p=4.695e-7) were each associated with lower response. Higher albumin_g_dl was associated with higher response (0.00953, p=1.233e-7). ldh_u_l and visceral_mets did not retain significant adjusted main effects in the final tests.

Biomarker main effects and refinement: additive models initially showed lower response for brca2_mutation (-0.10178, p=<1e-250), ar_v7_positive (-0.10011, p=<1e-250), and msi_high (-0.06278, p=1.982e-9). psma_high was not associated with higher response (-0.00529, p=1.500e-1). The interaction models clarified that much of the biomarker signal was heterogeneity in enzalutamide response rather than a uniform biomarker main effect.

Multivariable interactions: the enzalutamide association was sharply attenuated in mcrpc (interaction -0.43056, p=<1e-250), ar_v7_positive (-0.24132, p=<1e-250), brca2_mutation (-0.21860, p=<1e-250), and msi_high (-0.23057, p=<1e-250) subgroups. This was the most prominent multivariable pattern in the cohort.

Biomarker-directed treatment subgroup tests did not support the expected positive relationships. In brca2_mutation-positive patients, treatment_olaparib was associated with lower response rather than higher response (difference -0.03214, p=3.409e-2). In msi_high-positive patients, treatment_pembrolizumab was essentially null (difference 0.00123, p=9.777e-1). In psma_high-positive patients, treatment_lu177_psma was also null (difference -0.00115, p=8.680e-1).

Overall conclusion: objective_response in this cohort is dominated by a strong treatment_enzalutamide signal that is highly subgroup-dependent. Patients without mcrpc and without the adverse biomarker interaction profile appear to drive the enzalutamide-associated response advantage, while mcrpc, ar_v7_positive, brca2_mutation, and msi_high markedly reduce that advantage. General severity and inflammatory markers also track with worse response, whereas the other treatments and biomarker-directed subgroup hypotheses tested here did not show positive response associations.
