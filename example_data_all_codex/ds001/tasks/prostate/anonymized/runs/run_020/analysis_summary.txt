Dataset ds001_prostate contained 50000 patients, 72 de-identified feature columns, and binary objective_response. The overall objective_response rate was 0.2402.

Across 25 iterations I first screened main effects, then tested subgroup heterogeneity with two-way response-rate interactions, then checked selected findings in a multivariable linear probability model and with three-way interaction contrasts. Effects are signed response-rate differences on the natural probability scale.

Main-effect findings:
1. `feature_034` equal to 1 was associated with lower objective_response: 0.1535 vs 0.3460, difference -0.1925, p=1.000e-300.
2. `feature_005` equal to 1 was associated with higher objective_response: 0.3614 vs 0.1589, difference 0.2024, p=1.000e-300.
3. `feature_003` above its median (15.860) was associated with lower objective_response: 0.1822 vs 0.2983, difference -0.1161, p=9.010e-203.
4. `feature_009` equal to 1 was associated with lower objective_response: 0.1599 vs 0.2604, difference -0.1005, p=1.447e-98.
5. `feature_065` equal to 1 was associated with lower objective_response: 0.1497 vs 0.2503, difference -0.1005, p=4.132e-56.
6. `feature_023` above its median (1.000) was associated with lower objective_response: 0.1815 vs 0.2505, difference -0.0690, p=9.635e-38.
7. `feature_063` above its median (3.000) was associated with lower objective_response: 0.2225 vs 0.2575, difference -0.0351, p=4.651e-20.
8. `feature_055` equal to 1 was associated with lower objective_response: 0.1760 vs 0.2422, difference -0.0662, p=2.477e-9.
9. `feature_054` above its median (3.340) was associated with lower objective_response: 0.2313 vs 0.2491, difference -0.0178, p=3.397e-6.
10. `feature_027` above its median (3.800) was associated with higher objective_response: 0.2466 vs 0.2347, difference 0.0118, p=0.002.

The strongest supported main-effect hypotheses were the features whose high/binary-positive groups had materially different response rates from their complements. Positive effects indicate higher response probability in the named high group; negative effects indicate lower response probability. Constant or near-uninformative features were not carried forward.

Refuted or not clearly supported hypotheses:
8. Patients with `feature_053` equal to 1 have higher `objective_response` than patients with `feature_053` not equal to 1. The observed effect was 0.0078 with p=0.090, so this hypothesis was not statistically supported.
9. Patients with `feature_058` equal to 1 have higher `objective_response` than patients with `feature_058` not equal to 1. The observed effect was 0.0072 with p=0.149, so this hypothesis was not statistically supported.
10. Patients with `feature_036` above its median (1.800) have higher `objective_response` than patients with `feature_036` at or below its median (1.800). The observed effect was 0.0054 with p=0.159, so this hypothesis was not statistically supported.

Two-way interaction and subgroup findings:
1. feature_034 with feature_005: negative difference-in-differences -0.4326, p=1.000e-300.
2. feature_005 with feature_003: negative difference-in-differences -0.2219, p=2.665e-178.
3. feature_005 with feature_009: negative difference-in-differences -0.2425, p=2.200e-168.
4. feature_034 with feature_009: positive difference-in-differences 0.2210, p=3.019e-146.
5. feature_005 with feature_065: negative difference-in-differences -0.2183, p=2.765e-85.

These interaction tests show that several feature-response associations were not homogeneous: the response-rate contrast for one feature changed depending on the level of another feature. The transcript records the signed difference-in-differences for each interaction, so positive values mean the first feature contrast became more favorable in the modifier subgroup and negative values mean it became less favorable.

Adjusted multivariable findings:
1. feature_034: adjusted negative coefficient -0.1805, p=1.000e-300.
2. feature_005: adjusted positive coefficient 0.2023, p=1.000e-300.
3. feature_009: adjusted negative coefficient -0.1002, p=1.281e-111.
4. feature_065: adjusted negative coefficient -0.1021, p=8.136e-66.
5. feature_023: adjusted negative coefficient -0.0674, p=4.909e-41.

The adjusted model retained the leading screened feature indicators simultaneously. The largest adjusted coefficients remained statistically significant, supporting independent associations rather than purely redundant univariate correlations among the top features.

Three-way interaction checks:
1. feature_034 x feature_005 x feature_009: positive contrast 0.5284, p=1.227e-207.
2. feature_034 x feature_005 x feature_065: positive contrast 0.5182, p=2.071e-121.
3. feature_005 x feature_003 x feature_009: positive contrast 0.2588, p=4.129e-50.

Overall conclusion: objective_response in this cohort was associated with multiple de-identified features, with both favorable and unfavorable main effects. The evidence also supported subgroup heterogeneity: some two-feature and three-feature combinations shifted response rates beyond what would be expected from additive main effects alone. Because the covariates are opaque feature labels, these results should be interpreted as treatment/biomarker/outcome signal-mapping within this anonymized cohort rather than as directly named clinical mechanisms.
