Dataset ds001_prostate contained 50000 patients and 72 de-identified features plus the binary outcome objective_response. Overall response was 24.0% (12011/50000). I analyzed main effects with response-rate differences, then fitted an all-feature linear probability model and followed up with subgroup and interaction tests on the strongest signals.

The dominant positive signal was feature_005. Objective response was 36.1% with feature_005=1 versus 15.9% with feature_005=0, a +0.2024 response-rate difference (p=<1e-300). The main negative binary signals were feature_034 (risk difference -0.1925, p=<1e-300), feature_009 (-0.1005, p=<1e-300), feature_065 (-0.1005, p=<1e-300), and feature_055 (-0.0662, p=2.71e-11). These effects remained directionally consistent in the all-feature adjusted model; adjusted response-scale coefficients were 0.2025 for feature_005, -0.1881 for feature_034, -0.1000 for feature_009, and -0.1021 for feature_065.

Several non-binary features also showed response gradients. High feature_003 was adverse: above-median feature_003 had response 18.2% versus 29.8% at or below median, risk difference -0.1161 (p=<1e-300). Feature_023 showed a monotone decline from 28.2% at level 0 to 22.8% at level 1 and 18.2% at level 2; level 2 versus 0 had risk difference -0.1005. High feature_063 and feature_054 were adverse, with upper-quartile risk differences -0.0403 and -0.0257, respectively. Feature_027 was a smaller favorable signal: upper-quartile risk difference 0.0137 (p=2.21e-3).

The main clinical pattern was heterogeneity of the feature_005 association. Feature_005 was strongly favorable only in marker-negative strata. Its risk difference was 0.4403 when feature_034=0 but only 0.0077 when feature_034=1 (interaction -0.4326, p=<1e-300). Analogous attenuation occurred for feature_009 (interaction -0.2425), feature_065 (-0.2183), and feature_055 (-0.2312). When feature_034, feature_009, feature_065, and feature_055 were all absent, feature_005=1 had response 79.8% versus 17.2% for feature_005=0, a 0.6257 difference. When any of those four markers was present, the feature_005 difference shrank to 0.0077 and was not significant at 0.05 (p=5.54e-2).

A compact high-response subgroup emerged: feature_005=1, all four negative binary markers absent, and feature_003 at or below the median. This subgroup had response 80.9% compared with 17.8% for all other patients (risk difference 0.6309, p=<1e-300). Among feature_005=1 patients, any of the four negative markers reduced response from 79.8% to 16.1% (risk difference -0.6371). High feature_003 also attenuated the feature_005 effect: +0.3131 at/below median versus +0.0913 above median.

The negative binary markers also overlapped with one another rather than combining additively on the response-rate scale. Difference-in-differences were positive for feature_034 x feature_009 (0.2210), feature_034 x feature_065 (0.2098), and feature_009 x feature_065 (0.1263), all significant. As a negative-control style probe, feature_039 above the median was not meaningfully associated with response (risk difference -0.0016, p=6.73e-1).

Overall, the data support a strong treatment-biomarker-style structure: feature_005 marks a high-response subgroup, but this favorable association is largely conditional on absence of feature_034, feature_009, feature_065, and feature_055 and is attenuated by higher feature_003. Feature_023, feature_063, and feature_054 contribute additional adverse gradients, while feature_027 is a weaker favorable correlate.
