Dataset ds001_prostate contained 50,000 records and a binary outcome, objective_response, with an overall response rate of 24.0%. I tested binary and categorical contrasts as risk differences in response proportions, continuous features as linear-probability slopes, and subgroup heterogeneity as four-cell difference-in-differences tests on the response-rate scale.

The strongest main effects were concentrated in a small set of features. feature_005 = 1 was associated with much higher response (36.1% vs 15.9%, risk difference +20.24 percentage points, p < 1e-300). In the opposite direction, feature_034 = 1 was associated with much lower response (15.4% vs 34.6%, -19.25 points, p < 1e-300), as were feature_009 = 1 (-10.05 points), feature_065 = 1 (-10.05 points), and the rare feature_055 = 1 (-6.62 points, p=2.71e-11).

feature_023 showed categorical stratification: feature_023 = 0 was favorable (+6.45 points versus other values, p < 1e-300), while feature_023 = 2 was unfavorable (-6.90 points, p < 1e-300), and feature_023 = 1 was mildly unfavorable (-2.39 points, p=4.05e-10). Continuous features also carried signal: objective_response decreased with feature_003 (slope -0.000402 per unit, p < 1e-300), feature_063 (-0.00556 per unit, p < 1e-300), and feature_054 (-0.00111 per unit, p=5.19e-8), while feature_027 was positively associated (+0.0175 per unit, p=4.35e-6).

The dominant interaction pattern was that the high response associated with feature_005 = 1 collapsed in the presence of adverse markers. The feature_034 interaction was largest: the feature_034 risk difference was only -1.88 points when feature_005 = 0 but -45.15 points when feature_005 = 1, for a difference-in-differences of -43.26 points (p < 1e-300). Similar antagonistic interactions appeared for feature_009 with feature_005 (-24.25 points), feature_065 with feature_005 (-21.83 points), feature_055 with feature_005 (-23.12 points), and high feature_003 with feature_005 (-22.15 points).

A second recurring pattern was attenuation among already low-response subgroups. The negative feature_034 contrast was attenuated when feature_009 = 1 (+22.10 point interaction), feature_065 = 1 (+20.98 points), or feature_055 = 1 (+15.58 points). High feature_003 (>=15.86) was also less additionally adverse among feature_009 = 1 (+12.11 points), feature_065 = 1 (+10.32 points), and feature_055 = 1 (+9.09 points). feature_065 and feature_009 overlapped similarly: the feature_065 penalty was -12.59 points when feature_009 = 0 but approximately absent when feature_009 = 1, yielding a +12.63 point interaction.

After the dominant structure, several smaller nominal interactions remained: feature_027 >= 3.8 interacted positively with feature_043 >= 16.53 (+2.88 points, p=1.69e-4); feature_009 = 1 was more adverse with feature_072 >= 250 (-3.03 points, p=3.79e-4); feature_040 = 7 was more adverse with feature_058 = 1 (-3.35 points, p=9.26e-4); and other threshold interactions involving feature_011, feature_016, feature_039, feature_008, feature_018, and feature_023 were statistically significant but smaller.

Several plausible main-effect hypotheses were not supported. feature_040 = 7 had only a -0.52 point difference (p=0.185), feature_021 = 1 had a -0.40 point difference (p=0.301), feature_029 = 1 had a -0.57 point difference (p=0.138), and nearby binary features such as feature_067, feature_058, and feature_053 were not significant at p < 0.05 as main effects. Overall, the cohort appears dominated by strong treatment/biomarker-like heterogeneity: feature_005 identifies a high-response group only when adverse markers such as feature_034, feature_009, feature_065, feature_055, and high feature_003 are absent; once those adverse markers are present, response rates converge to a low range and additional negative markers have attenuated marginal effects.
