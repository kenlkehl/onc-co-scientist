Analysis summary for ds001_aml

The cohort contains 50000 AML patient records with no missing values in the analyzed columns and a binary outcome, objective_response. The overall objective_response rate was 18.8%. Across 25 propose-test-update iterations, I tested unadjusted response-rate differences, subgroup contrasts, and adjusted linear-probability interaction models. Effect estimates are absolute response-probability differences on the natural outcome scale.

The clearest treatment pattern was for treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine. Its unadjusted response rate was 22.0% versus 16.6% without the regimen, a +5.4 percentage-point risk difference (p=1.00e-300), and the adjusted overall association was similar at +5.4 percentage points. The effect was highly concentrated in unfit_for_intensive patients: within unfit patients, response was 30.8% with venetoclax/azacitidine versus 16.3% without it, a +14.5 point difference (p=1.00e-300), and the adjusted unfit-by-treatment interaction was +15.3 points (p=1.00e-300). This hypothesis was strongly supported. The venetoclax/azacitidine association was attenuated in tp53_mutation disease (adjusted interaction -7.2 points, p=2.90e-10) and modestly attenuated with low albumin_g_dl (interaction -1.5 points, p=0.0316). High blast_pct_marrow, high wbc_k_per_ul, high weight_loss_pct_6mo, high crp_mg_l, and age >=75 did not significantly modify the venetoclax/azacitidine association.

In contrast, treatment_7plus3 showed no meaningful overall response advantage: unadjusted risk difference was +0.3 points (p=0.458), and adjusted effect was +0.2 points (p=0.567). It also was not significantly modified by unfit_for_intensive status, npm1_mutation, or age >=75. A refined interaction did show worse association in complex_karyotype disease, with an adjusted treatment_7plus3 by complex_karyotype interaction of -3.3 points (p=0.000526).

The FLT3/IDH targeted-treatment hypotheses were mostly refuted. Any targeted-treatment use had a slightly lower, non-significant response rate than no targeted treatment (-0.4 points, p=0.274). In biomarker-positive strata, treatment_midostaurin in flt3_itd disease had a -1.5 point risk difference (p=0.167), treatment_gilteritinib in flt3_tkd disease had a -3.5 point risk difference (p=0.0691), treatment_ivosidenib in idh1_mutation disease had a -4.0 point risk difference (p=0.0703), and treatment_enasidenib in idh2_mutation disease had a +0.4 point risk difference (p=0.866). Adjusted biomarker-treatment interactions were non-significant for midostaurin by flt3_itd, gilteritinib by FLT3 alteration, and enasidenib by idh2_mutation. The one significant targeted-therapy interaction was opposite the expected direction: treatment_ivosidenib by idh1_mutation was negative, about -4.5 to -4.9 points (p about 0.043 to 0.032). Treatment-selection checks also did not show materially higher use of midostaurin in flt3_itd, ivosidenib in idh1_mutation, or enasidenib in idh2_mutation.

Core AML biology was coherent. npm1_mutation was strongly favorable: response was 23.5% with npm1_mutation versus 16.7% without it, an unadjusted +6.7 point difference (p=1.00e-300), and the adjusted association remained +6.8 points. tp53_mutation and complex_karyotype were adverse, with unadjusted effects of -2.4 and -2.3 points and adjusted effects of -2.5 and -2.4 points, respectively. Having either tp53_mutation or complex_karyotype lowered response by -2.6 points, and having both lowered response by -2.8 points, but the tp53-by-complex interaction was not significant. The favorable npm1_mutation association did not differ by flt3_itd status, but it was substantially attenuated in tp53_mutation disease (npm1-by-tp53 interaction -7.7 points, p=2.48e-10) and complex_karyotype disease (npm1-by-complex interaction -9.7 points, p=1.00e-300).

Clinical status and disease burden showed several reproducible associations. Higher ecog_ps was strongly adverse: a 1-SD increase was associated with -3.5 points unadjusted, and an adjusted 1-point increase was associated with -5.2 points (p=1.00e-300). Age_years was essentially null in both unadjusted and adjusted checks. Higher blast_pct_marrow, higher wbc_k_per_ul, lower albumin_g_dl, higher weight_loss_pct_6mo, and higher crp_mg_l predicted lower response after adjustment. The adjusted effects were -0.77 points per 10 marrow-blast percentage points, -0.26 points per 10 k/uL WBC, +1.81 points per 1 g/dL albumin, -2.60 points per 5 percentage points of weight loss, and -0.42 points per 5 mg/L CRP. nlr, ldh_u_l, hemoglobin_g_dl, platelets_k_ul, creatinine_mg_dl, common comorbidities, vital signs, and liver tests were mostly non-significant or very small.

Several non-AML or likely decoy variables were probed. Solid-tumor alteration fields such as her2_amplification, met_exon14_skipping, and ros1_fusion were non-significant. Effusion/metastasis-like fields and broad comorbidity markers were also weak or non-significant compared with treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine, ECOG status, NPM1, TP53, complex karyotype, CRP, WBC, albumin, weight loss, and marrow blast burden.

Overall, objective_response in this cohort is driven most clearly by treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine, especially in unfit_for_intensive patients, and by core AML biology. NPM1 mutation marks higher response, while TP53 mutation and complex karyotype mark lower response and blunt otherwise favorable patterns. The expected positive FLT3/IDH matched targeted-therapy effects were not supported. The most defensible treatment-biomarker-outcome conclusions are a strong venetoclax/azacitidine-fitness interaction, attenuation of venetoclax/azacitidine benefit in TP53-mutated disease, and robust molecular risk effects rather than broad matched-targeted benefit.
