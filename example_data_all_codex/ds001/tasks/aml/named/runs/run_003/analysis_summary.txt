Dataset ds001_aml contained 50,000 AML patient records and a binary outcome, objective_response. The overall response rate was 18.75%. I tested response-rate differences, adjusted linear-probability models with robust standard errors, and interaction models. Effect estimates are absolute response-probability differences.

The strongest favorable genomic main effect was npm1_mutation. Unadjusted response was 23.46% with npm1_mutation versus 16.73% without it (RD +0.0674), and the adjusted coefficient remained +0.06746. This effect was heterogeneous: the npm1_mutation by tp53_mutation interaction was -0.07697, and the npm1_mutation by complex_karyotype interaction was -0.09696, indicating that TP53-mutated or complex-karyotype disease substantially attenuated the NPM1-associated response advantage.

Adverse disease biology was also evident. tp53_mutation had an adjusted effect of -0.02628 and complex_karyotype had an adjusted effect of -0.02386. Their joint interaction was not significantly more adverse than additivity (interaction +0.02122, p=0.1649). secondary_aml was not independently associated with lower response after adjustment (effect +0.00043, p=0.9139).

Clinical status and disease burden mattered. Each 1-point increase in ecog_ps was associated with a -0.05223 lower response probability. Age was not independently associated with response (per 10 years, -0.00073, p=0.6439). Higher blast_pct_marrow, higher wbc_k_per_ul, lower albumin_g_dl, higher weight_loss_pct_6mo, and higher crp_mg_l all predicted lower response. The adjusted effects were -0.00766 per 10 blast percentage points, -0.00255 per 10 k/uL WBC, +0.01810 per 1 g/dL albumin, -0.02597 per 5 percentage points of weight loss, and -0.00424 per 5 mg/L CRP. nlr and ldh_u_l were not independently significant.

Treatment patterns were highly subgroup-specific. treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine had a strong overall adjusted association with higher response (+0.05423), but this was concentrated in unfit_for_intensive patients: the unfit by venetoclax-azacitidine interaction was +0.15298, with unadjusted treatment RD +14.51 percentage points in unfit patients versus -0.77 points in fit patients. The venetoclax-azacitidine association was attenuated by tp53_mutation (interaction -0.07178) and modestly attenuated by low albumin (interaction -0.01466). High blast burden, high WBC, high weight loss, high CRP, and age >=75 did not significantly modify the venetoclax-azacitidine association.

treatment_7plus3 did not show an overall adjusted response advantage (+0.00198, p=0.5667). Its association was worse in complex_karyotype disease (interaction -0.03281, p=0.000526), but not significantly modified by unfit_for_intensive, npm1_mutation, or age >=75.

Targeted treatment hypotheses were mostly unsupported in this cohort. FLT3-ITD did not identify a significant positive interaction with treatment_midostaurin (interaction -0.01424, p=0.2321) or treatment_gilteritinib (interaction +0.01016, p=0.3957). IDH2 mutation did not identify a benefit from treatment_enasidenib (interaction +0.00718, p=0.7435). The IDH1-ivosidenib interaction was significant but negative (interaction -0.04506, p=0.04298), opposite the hypothesized positive biomarker-treatment relationship.

Overall, the cohort showed a coherent response pattern: NPM1 mutation, better performance status, lower proliferative burden, preserved albumin, less weight loss, and lower CRP tracked with higher response. TP53 mutation and complex karyotype tracked with lower response and weakened otherwise favorable patterns. The clearest treatment-biomarker-treatment subgroup relationship was not a kinase or IDH-targeted therapy signal, but a strong venetoclax-azacitidine association concentrated among unfit_for_intensive patients and diminished in TP53-mutated disease.
