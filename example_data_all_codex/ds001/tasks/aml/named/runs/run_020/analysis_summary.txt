Analysis summary for ds001_aml

I analyzed 50,000 AML records with binary objective_response as the outcome. Effect estimates are response-probability differences. Continuous predictors were standardized, so their effects are per 1 SD increase. Interaction tests used robust linear probability models adjusted for core clinical, disease-burden, and molecular covariates.

The strongest overall treatment signal was for treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine: response was 0.2196 on treatment versus 0.1661 off treatment, a +0.0535 difference (p<1e-300). In contrast, treatment_7plus3 had no adjusted overall response advantage (+0.0020, p=0.5642).

Molecular main effects showed a large favorable association for npm1_mutation (+0.0675, p<1e-300), while tp53_mutation (-0.0263, p=2.482e-6) and complex_karyotype (-0.0239, p=4.301e-7) were adverse. However, the NPM1 signal was highly context-dependent: it was reduced by tp53_mutation (interaction -0.0772, p=2.202e-10) and complex_karyotype (interaction -0.0984, p<1e-300). Cell rates showed NPM1 advantage mainly in TP53-wild-type, non-complex disease.

Clinical disease-burden features were also important. Higher ecog_ps was associated with lower response (-0.0354 per SD, p<1e-300), as was weight_loss_pct_6mo (-0.0201 per SD, p<1e-300), blast_pct_marrow (-0.0147 per SD, p<1e-300), wbc_k_per_ul (-0.0066 per SD, p=1.044e-4), and crp_mg_l (-0.0078 per SD, p=1.115e-6). Higher albumin_g_dl was favorable (+0.0091 per SD, p=1.307e-7).

The most clinically coherent interaction was treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine with unfit_for_intensive and npm1_mutation. The venetoclax/azacitidine by unfit_for_intensive interaction was +0.1528 (p<1e-300). The venetoclax/azacitidine by npm1_mutation interaction was +0.1907 (p<1e-300). The three-way treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine by unfit_for_intensive by npm1_mutation interaction was very large (+0.4618, p<1e-300): unfit/NPM1-mutated patients had response 0.6331 on venetoclax/azacitidine versus 0.1596 without it.

Adverse genetics attenuated this venetoclax/azacitidine pattern. In unfit TP53-wild-type patients, venetoclax/azacitidine was associated with a +0.1623 response difference, but in unfit TP53-mutated patients the difference was only +0.0038; the corresponding three-way TP53 interaction was -0.1545 (p=2.364e-11). Complex karyotype also attenuated the venetoclax/azacitidine association (interaction -0.0509, p=1.436e-7).

Targeted-agent biomarker hypotheses were not generally supported in this dataset. Midostaurin did not show a positive FLT3-ITD interaction (-0.0168, p=0.1621). Gilteritinib did not show significant positive interactions with FLT3-ITD (+0.0125, p=0.3019) or FLT3-TKD (-0.0306, p=0.1250). Ivosidenib had a statistically significant IDH1 interaction in the opposite direction from a benefit hypothesis (-0.0472, p=0.03894), and enasidenib did not show an IDH2 interaction (+0.0057, p=0.7983).

For intensive therapy, treatment_7plus3 had no overall benefit and no NPM1-specific benefit (-0.0112 interaction, p=0.1578), but complex_karyotype was associated with worse 7+3 response interaction (-0.0324, p=0.0005669). Secondary_aml, age_years, and sex_female did not show meaningful independent associations after adjustment.

Overall, this cohort is dominated by a treatment-biomarker-fitness pattern: venetoclax/azacitidine response is concentrated in unfit_for_intensive patients with npm1_mutation, while tp53_mutation and complex_karyotype blunt otherwise favorable response patterns. General frailty, disease burden, inflammation, and low albumin also track with lower response, but most FLT3/IDH targeted-agent matching hypotheses were not supported by the observed response data.
