Dataset ds001_aml contains 50,000 patients and one binary outcome, objective_response. I treated effect estimates as risk differences on the response-probability scale. Crude binary comparisons used two-proportion tests, and adjusted analyses used robust linear probability models with clinical, biomarker, laboratory, and treatment covariates.

The strongest biomarker main effect was npm1_mutation. After adjustment, npm1_mutation was associated with a +0.0676 higher objective_response probability (p<1e-12), supporting a favorable response pattern. In contrast, tp53_mutation and complex_karyotype were adverse: tp53_mutation had an adjusted risk difference of -0.0263 (p=2.50e-6), and complex_karyotype had an adjusted risk difference of -0.0241 (p=3.60e-7).

The treatment signal was dominated by treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine. Its overall adjusted association with objective_response was +0.0540 (p<1e-12), but this was highly heterogeneous. The venetoclax/azacitidine association was much larger in unfit_for_intensive patients, with an adjusted interaction of +0.1527 (p<1e-12) and a crude response difference of +0.1451 within the unfit subgroup. The same treatment was attenuated in tp53_mutation patients (interaction -0.0665, p=4.47e-9) and complex_karyotype patients (interaction -0.0511, p=1.35e-7), but amplified in npm1_mutation patients (interaction +0.1907, p<1e-12).

Other targeted therapy-biomarker pairs were weak or contrary to the expected direction. In flt3_itd-positive patients, treatment_midostaurin was not beneficial (adjusted risk difference -0.0148, p=0.168; interaction -0.0155, p=0.195). In flt3_tkd-positive patients, treatment_gilteritinib trended negative but was not significant (adjusted risk difference -0.0363, p=0.061; interaction -0.0298, p=0.135). In idh1_mutation patients, treatment_ivosidenib was significantly negative rather than positive (adjusted risk difference -0.0431, p=0.0496; interaction -0.0460, p=0.0443). In idh2_mutation patients, treatment_enasidenib was near zero (adjusted risk difference +0.0029, p=0.890; interaction +0.0054, p=0.811).

Clinical and inflammatory burden features showed coherent adverse associations. Higher blast_pct_marrow, weight_loss_pct_6mo, crp_mg_l, wbc_k_per_ul, and ecog_ps were each independently associated with lower response. The adjusted risk differences per one standard deviation were -0.0146 for blast_pct_marrow, -0.0201 for weight_loss_pct_6mo, -0.0076 for crp_mg_l, -0.0067 for wbc_k_per_ul, and -0.0355 for ecog_ps. Higher albumin_g_dl was favorable (+0.0090 per SD, p=1.45e-7). Age_years and ldh_u_l did not show meaningful independent associations after adjustment.

Several plausible or noise-check hypotheses were refuted. secondary_aml had essentially no adjusted association (+0.0004, p=0.914), flt3_itd had no independent adverse association (+0.0008, p=0.855), and the non-AML feature liver_mets showed no significant crude relationship (+0.0063, p=0.203). Overall, the cohort appears structured around a favorable NPM1/venetoclax-azacitidine response pattern, adverse TP53 and complex-karyotype biology, strong response heterogeneity by fitness status, and additional negative effects of performance status, disease burden, weight loss, inflammation, and high WBC.
