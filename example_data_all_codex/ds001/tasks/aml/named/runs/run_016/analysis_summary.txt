Analysis summary for ds001_aml

Dataset and method
I analyzed 50000 patient records from dataset.parquet. The outcome was objective_response, with an overall response rate of 18.8%. Effects below are absolute response-probability differences on the natural scale. I used two-proportion z-tests for binary subgroup comparisons and linear probability models for adjusted main effects and interactions.

Main response patterns
The strongest unadjusted treatment association was treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine: response was 22.0% with the treatment vs 16.6% without it, an absolute difference of 5.4% (p=<1e-300). treatment_7plus3 did not show a meaningful overall association (0.3%, p=0.458).

Baseline disease biology mattered. npm1_mutation was associated with higher response (6.7%, p=<1e-300), while tp53_mutation (-2.4%, p=3.23e-5) and complex_karyotype (-2.3%, p=3.07e-6) were associated with lower response. ecog_ps=2 had much lower response than ecog_ps=0 (-10.2%, p=<1e-300).

Continuous severity markers were directionally coherent: per one-standard-deviation increase, weight_loss_pct_6mo was associated with -2.0% lower response, blast_pct_marrow with -1.4% lower response, wbc_k_per_ul with -0.7% lower response, and crp_mg_l with -0.8% lower response. Higher albumin_g_dl was favorable (0.9% per SD). ldh_u_l was not independently associated after adjustment (-0.2% per SD, p=0.383).

Treatment-biomarker and subgroup findings
The dominant interaction signal was treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine. Its association with response was much larger in unfit_for_intensive=1 patients than in unfit_for_intensive=0 patients (adjusted interaction 15.2%, p=<1e-300). In the unfit subgroup, response was 30.8% with venetoclax/azacitidine vs 16.3% without it (14.5%, p=<1e-300). In fit patients, the estimated difference was small and slightly negative (-0.8%, p=0.080).

npm1_mutation also strongly modified the venetoclax/azacitidine association. The adjusted interaction was 19.1% (p=<1e-300). Among npm1_mutation=1 patients, response was 34.7% with venetoclax/azacitidine vs 16.0% without (18.7%, p=<1e-300). Among npm1_mutation=0 patients, there was no positive association (-0.4%, p=0.336).

Adverse genetics attenuated venetoclax/azacitidine associations. The adjusted tp53_mutation interaction was negative (-6.8%, p=4.79e-9), and within tp53_mutation=1 patients the treatment difference was essentially null (-0.4%, p=0.736). complex_karyotype showed a similar negative interaction (-5.1%, p=2.16e-7), with only a small nonsignificant within-complex-karyotype treatment difference (1.2%, p=0.192). secondary_aml did not materially modify the venetoclax/azacitidine association (0.4%, p=0.617).

The expected targeted-agent biomarker patterns were not supported in this dataset. Among flt3_itd=1 patients, midostaurin was not associated with higher response (-1.5%, p=0.176), and the adjusted treatment_midostaurin x flt3_itd interaction was not significant (-1.6%, p=0.199). Gilteritinib did not show positive FLT3-ITD or FLT3-TKD subgroup effects; the adjusted interactions were 1.1% (p=0.374) and -2.9% (p=0.165), respectively. Ivosidenib in idh1_mutation=1 and enasidenib in idh2_mutation=1 were also not supported by statistically significant positive subgroup effects or interactions.

For treatment_7plus3, there was no evidence that benefit was meaningfully higher in fit or younger patients: interactions with unfit_for_intensive and age_years >= 70 were small and nonsignificant (-0.2%, p=0.779; -0.1%, p=0.858). complex_karyotype did attenuate the treatment_7plus3 association in the adjusted interaction model (-3.2%, p=0.001).

Overall conclusion
The clearest treatment-biomarker-outcome structure in this cohort centers on treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine: response is concentrated in unfit_for_intensive and npm1_mutation subgroups, while tp53_mutation and complex_karyotype blunt or eliminate the apparent benefit. General disease burden and functional status markers behave as expected, with worse ECOG status, marrow blasts, WBC, CRP, and weight loss associated with lower response and albumin associated with higher response. The FLT3 and IDH targeted therapy indicators did not show the anticipated positive mutation-matched response patterns in these data. Because these are observational EHR-derived records, the findings should be read as associations rather than causal treatment effects.
