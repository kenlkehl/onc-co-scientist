Dataset ds001_aml contained 50,000 patients and one binary outcome, objective_response. I used response-rate comparisons for binary predictors and linear-probability models with HC0 robust standard errors for continuous predictors, adjusted main-effect models, and interaction models. Effects below are on the objective_response probability scale.

Overall response was 18.75%. The only strong overall treatment main effect was treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine: response was 21.96% on treatment versus 16.61% off treatment, risk difference +5.35 percentage points (p<1e-300). The other treatment indicators did not show significant overall benefit: treatment_7plus3 +0.26 points (p=0.458), treatment_midostaurin -0.21 points (p=0.660), treatment_gilteritinib -0.73 points (p=0.131), treatment_ivosidenib +0.07 points (p=0.911), and treatment_enasidenib -0.18 points (p=0.774).

Genetic main effects showed a favorable NPM1 signal and adverse TP53/complex-karyotype signals. npm1_mutation was associated with +6.74 points higher response (p<1e-300). tp53_mutation was associated with -2.42 points lower response (p=1.43e-5), and complex_karyotype with -2.29 points lower response (p=1.34e-6). FLT3 and IDH mutation main effects were not significant: flt3_itd +0.21 points, flt3_tkd -0.75 points, idh1_mutation +0.37 points, and idh2_mutation +0.44 points.

Clinical status and inflammatory burden were consistent prognostic features. Per one standard deviation, ecog_ps was associated with -3.54 response points, weight_loss_pct_6mo with -2.01 points, blast_pct_marrow with -1.43 points, crp_mg_l with -0.79 points, and wbc_k_per_ul with -0.65 points. Albumin was favorable at +0.90 points per SD. These remained significant in adjusted models. LDH, NLR, age, and sex did not show meaningful main-effect associations.

The central finding was treatment-subgroup heterogeneity for venetoclax-azacitidine. The venetoclax-azacitidine by unfit_for_intensive interaction was +15.28 points unadjusted and +15.35 points adjusted (p<1e-300). In unfit patients, venetoclax-azacitidine was associated with +14.51 points higher response; in fit patients the effect was -0.77 points. The venetoclax-azacitidine by npm1_mutation interaction was +19.08 points unadjusted and +19.19 points adjusted (p<1e-300): NPM1-mutated patients had a +18.69 point treatment difference, while NPM1-wildtype patients had essentially no benefit. Conversely, TP53 mutation and complex karyotype attenuated venetoclax benefit. Adjusted interactions were -7.21 points for tp53_mutation (p=3.24e-10) and -5.19 points for complex_karyotype (p=1.20e-7).

The apparent favorable main effects of unfit_for_intensive and npm1_mutation were largely explained by those venetoclax interactions. In a main-effects model, unfit_for_intensive had a +5.62 point coefficient and npm1_mutation had a +6.75 point coefficient. After adding venetoclax interactions, the unfit main effect was -0.50 points and not significant (p=0.249), while the NPM1 main effect outside the venetoclax interaction was -0.94 points (p=0.041) with the large positive venetoclax-by-NPM1 interaction preserved.

For treatment_7plus3, there was no overall effect, but complex_karyotype modified response. The 7+3 by complex_karyotype interaction was -3.04 points unadjusted (p=0.0013) and -3.20 points adjusted (p=0.00075). In contrast, unfit_for_intensive and age at least 70 did not materially modify the 7+3 association.

Expected matched targeted-therapy interactions were not supported. Midostaurin did not show greater response in FLT3-ITD or FLT3-TKD patients; gilteritinib did not show significant positive interaction with FLT3-ITD or FLT3-TKD. Ivosidenib did not show a positive IDH1-mutated subgroup effect; the coefficient was negative and borderline but opposite the expected direction. Enasidenib did not show meaningful IDH2-mutated subgroup benefit. These null patterns persisted in the adjusted interaction model.

Exploratory scans found a few nominal treatment-by-feature interactions, including enasidenib by sex_female (+4.78 points, p=0.00024) and venetoclax-azacitidine by heart_failure (+2.96 points, p=0.0145). These were less clinically coherent than the venetoclax-by-fitness/genomics findings and should be treated as hypothesis-generating, especially given the number of exploratory comparisons. Common comorbidities and solid-tumor-oriented variables such as liver_mets, her2_amplification, and ros1_fusion did not show strong response associations.

Overall, the cohort is best summarized by a strong venetoclax-azacitidine interaction structure: higher response in unfit_for_intensive and npm1_mutation subgroups, with reduced benefit in tp53_mutation and complex_karyotype subgroups. Independent clinical response gradients were lower response with worse ECOG, weight loss, higher blast percentage, higher CRP, and higher WBC, and higher response with better albumin. Other AML targeted therapies and their nominal biomarker matches did not show statistically supported response advantages in this dataset.
