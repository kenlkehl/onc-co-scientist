Dataset ds001_aml contained 50000 patients and 9375 objective responses (18.75%). Effect estimates below are signed absolute changes in objective_response probability; positive values mean higher response. I used risk-difference z tests for binary/subgroup comparisons, univariate linear probability regressions for continuous features, and HC1-robust adjusted linear probability models for adjusted main effects and interactions.

Main treatment and biomarker findings:
- treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine showed the clearest treatment association: unadjusted response 21.96% vs 16.61%, risk difference 5.35 percentage points (p=3.69e-49); adjusted coefficient 5.42 percentage points (p=3.42e-52).
- treatment_7plus3 was not associated with materially higher response overall: risk difference 0.26 percentage points (p=0.4580).
- npm1_mutation was strongly favorable after adjustment, with a 6.76 percentage-point higher response probability (p=3.46e-66).
- tp53_mutation and complex_karyotype were adverse: adjusted coefficients -2.53 and -2.34 percentage points, respectively (p=5.66e-6 and p=7.05e-7).

Clinical burden and inflammatory/proliferative patterns:
- Higher weight_loss_pct_6mo, blast_pct_marrow, wbc_k_per_ul, and crp_mg_l were each associated with lower response; higher albumin_g_dl was associated with higher response. The largest continuous effect was weight loss: -2.61 percentage points per 5 percentage-point increase (p=7.32e-31).
- A combined low-albumin/high-CRP subgroup had lower response (-2.63 percentage points, p=1.21e-4). A high-WBC/high-LDH subgroup also had lower response (-1.77 percentage points, p=0.0108).

Subgroup heterogeneity and interactions:
- The venetoclax/azacitidine association was concentrated in patients unfit for intensive therapy: within unfit patients, response was 30.81% with treatment vs 16.30% without treatment (risk difference 14.51 points, p=9.22e-123). In fit patients, the difference was -0.77 points and was not significant at 0.05 (p=0.0792). The adjusted interaction term was strongly positive (15.25 points, p=4.70e-94).
- tp53_mutation attenuated the venetoclax/azacitidine association: adjusted interaction -6.86 points (p=1.37e-9).
- complex_karyotype reduced the treatment_7plus3 association: adjusted interaction -3.17 points (p=7.88e-4).

Targeted treatment-biomarker hypotheses were mostly not supported. Midostaurin by flt3_itd (-1.50 points, p=0.2112), gilteritinib by flt3_itd (1.17 points, p=0.3329), and enasidenib by idh2_mutation (0.49 points, p=0.8254) were null. The ivosidenib by idh1_mutation interaction was significant but negative (-4.66 points, p=0.0414), opposite the hypothesized direction. Age did not materially modify venetoclax/azacitidine or 7+3 associations.

Overall conclusion: in this cohort, objective_response was most strongly patterned by venetoclax/azacitidine use in unfit patients, favorable npm1_mutation status, adverse tp53_mutation/complex_karyotype status, and systemic disease-burden markers. Several familiar targeted therapy-biomarker pairings did not show positive response heterogeneity in these data, while TP53 and complex karyotype appeared to blunt treatment-associated response.
