Analysis summary for ds001_aml

The cohort contains 50000 AML patient records with no missing values in the analyzed columns. The overall objective_response rate was 18.8%.

Across 25 propose-test-update iterations, the clearest treatment pattern was for treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine. Its unadjusted response rate was 22.0% vs 16.6% without the regimen, a 5.4% risk difference (p=1.00e-300). In contrast, the initial hypotheses that treatment_7plus3 and the pooled FLT3/IDH targeted-treatment class would increase objective_response were not supported: treatment_7plus3 had a 0.3% risk difference (p=0.458), and any targeted treatment had a -0.4% risk difference (p=0.274).

The strongest treatment heterogeneity involved treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine and fitness. Among unfit_for_intensive patients, treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine was associated with a 14.5% higher response probability (p=1.00e-300), and the unfit-by-treatment interaction was 15.3% unadjusted and 15.3% after covariate adjustment. The regimen also had positive within-subgroup associations in secondary_aml (5.5%, p=4.91e-14) and IDH-mutated disease (5.3%, p=3.02e-9), but the secondary_aml and IDH interaction terms themselves were near zero and non-significant.

The FLT3/IDH targeted-therapy specificity hypotheses were mostly refuted in this dataset. In biomarker-positive strata, treatment_midostaurin in flt3_itd disease had a -1.5% risk difference (p=0.167), treatment_gilteritinib in flt3_tkd disease had a -3.5% risk difference (p=0.0691), treatment_ivosidenib in idh1_mutation disease had a -4.0% risk difference (p=0.0703), and treatment_enasidenib in idh2_mutation disease had a 0.4% risk difference (p=0.866). The final adjusted check found a significant negative treatment_ivosidenib by idh1_mutation interaction (-4.9%, p=0.0318), while enasidenib by idh2_mutation and gilteritinib by flt3_any were non-significant. Treatment-selection tests also did not show materially higher use of midostaurin in flt3_itd, ivosidenib in idh1_mutation, or enasidenib in idh2_mutation.

Molecular risk patterns were coherent and robust. npm1_mutation was favorable, with a 6.7% unadjusted association and 6.8% adjusted association. tp53_mutation was adverse (-2.4% unadjusted; -2.5% adjusted), as was complex_karyotype (-2.3% unadjusted; -2.4% adjusted). Having either tp53_mutation or complex_karyotype lowered response by -2.6%, and having both lowered response by -2.8%, though the tp53-by-complex interaction was not significant. The favorable npm1_mutation association did not materially differ by flt3_itd status.

Clinical and laboratory features showed a mixed pattern. unfit_for_intensive patients had a higher observed response rate (5.6%), consistent with the strong venetoclax/azacitidine enrichment rather than a pure adverse-fitness effect. Worse ecog_ps was strongly adverse (-3.5% per SD, p=1.00e-300), while age_years was essentially null. Higher albumin_g_dl was favorable (0.9% per SD), while higher crp_mg_l, wbc_k_per_ul, and blast_pct_marrow were adverse (-0.8%, -0.7%, and -1.4% per SD, respectively). nlr, ldh_u_l, hemoglobin_g_dl, platelets_k_ul, creatinine_mg_dl, common comorbidities, vital signs, and liver tests were mostly non-significant or very small.

Several non-AML or likely decoy variables were probed. Solid-tumor alteration fields such as her2_amplification, met_exon14_skipping, and ros1_fusion were non-significant. Effusion/metastasis-like fields and broad comorbidity markers were also weak or non-significant compared with treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine, ECOG status, NPM1, TP53, complex karyotype, CRP, WBC, and marrow blast burden.

Overall conclusion: objective_response in this cohort is driven most clearly by treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine, especially in unfit_for_intensive patients, and by core AML biology. NPM1 mutation marks higher response; TP53 mutation and complex karyotype mark lower response. The expected positive FLT3/IDH targeted therapy-biomarker interactions were not supported here, and one adjusted IDH1/ivosidenib interaction was significantly negative. The most defensible treatment-biomarker conclusion is therefore not broad matched-targeted benefit, but a strong venetoclax/azacitidine-fitness interaction plus robust molecular risk effects.
