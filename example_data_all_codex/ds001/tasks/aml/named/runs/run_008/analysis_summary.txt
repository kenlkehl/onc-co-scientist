Dataset: ds001_aml
Rows analyzed: 50000
Outcome: objective_response
Overall response rate: 18.75%
Maximum iterations used: 25

Methods
I analyzed binary objective response using response-rate differences for binary feature contrasts, top-versus-bottom quartile response-rate differences for continuous features, difference-in-differences for treatment-by-biomarker subgroup heterogeneity, and robust linear probability models for adjusted main effects and interactions. All effect estimates are signed on the objective_response probability scale.

Main treatment patterns
The clearest treatment main effect was for treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine. Patients receiving it had a 21.96% response rate versus 16.61% without it, a +5.35 percentage point difference (p<1e-12). This persisted in an adjusted linear probability model (+0.0542, p<1e-12).

In contrast, treatment_7plus3 showed no overall response advantage (+0.26 percentage points, p=0.458). The targeted-treatment indicators also did not show favorable main effects: treatment_midostaurin -0.21 percentage points (p=0.660), treatment_gilteritinib -0.73 points (p=0.131), treatment_ivosidenib +0.07 points (p=0.911), and treatment_enasidenib -0.18 points (p=0.774).

Molecular and cytogenetic main effects
npm1_mutation was the strongest favorable molecular main effect. Response was 23.46% with npm1_mutation=1 versus 16.73% without it, a +6.74 percentage point difference (p<1e-12), and the adjusted coefficient remained +0.0675 (p<1e-12).

tp53_mutation and complex_karyotype were adverse. tp53_mutation was associated with -2.42 percentage points lower response (p=1.431e-5), and complex_karyotype with -2.29 points lower response (p=1.343e-6). In adjusted models, the effects remained negative: tp53_mutation -0.0263 (p=2.555e-6) and complex_karyotype -0.0239 (p=3.986e-7). Combining these adverse features, patients with either tp53_mutation or complex_karyotype had a -2.60 point lower response rate than patients with neither feature (p=8.13e-11).

Other AML mutations were not strong response predictors as main effects. flt3_itd was essentially null unadjusted (+0.21 points, p=0.627) and adjusted (+0.0009, p=0.837). flt3_tkd was lower but non-significant (-0.75 points, p=0.318). idh1_mutation (+0.37 points, p=0.583 unadjusted; +0.0043, p=0.524 adjusted) and idh2_mutation (+0.44 points, p=0.465 unadjusted; +0.0024, p=0.686 adjusted) were also not significant main effects.

Clinical status and disease burden
unfit_for_intensive was unexpectedly favorable in this dataset: response was 22.10% when unfit_for_intensive=1 versus 16.51% when 0 (+5.59 points, p<1e-12), and the adjusted effect remained +0.0562 (p<1e-12). In contrast, worse ECOG performance status was strongly adverse. Patients with ecog_ps>=2 had response 12.91% versus 19.77% for ecog_ps<2, a -6.86 point difference (p<1e-12); adjusted ecog_ps was -0.0522 per point (p<1e-12).

Age and sex were not meaningful predictors. age_years>=75 had a -0.07 point difference (p=0.860), and sex_female had a +0.29 point difference (p=0.414).

High disease burden and systemic inflammation generally predicted lower response. Top-quartile blast_pct_marrow was associated with -4.01 points versus the bottom quartile (p=4.441e-16), and the adjusted effect was -0.0077 per 10 percentage points (p<1e-12). Top-quartile wbc_k_per_ul was associated with -2.29 points (p=3.336e-6). Top-quartile weight_loss_pct_6mo was associated with -4.74 points (p<1e-12), with adjusted coefficient -0.0052 per percentage point (p<1e-12). Higher albumin_g_dl was favorable: top versus bottom quartile +2.25 points (p=3.603e-7), adjusted +0.0181 per g/dL (p=1.268e-7). Higher crp_mg_l was adverse: top versus bottom quartile -2.24 points (p=4.534e-6), adjusted log1p(crp_mg_l) coefficient -0.0111 (p=7.039e-8). ldh_u_l trended adverse but was not significant in the quartile contrast (-0.78 points, p=0.114) or the adjusted model.

Key treatment-biomarker and treatment-subgroup interactions
The dominant interaction was between treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine and npm1_mutation. The venetoclax/azacitidine response difference was +18.69 percentage points in npm1_mutation=1 patients but -0.39 points in npm1_mutation=0 patients, giving a +19.08 point interaction (p<1e-12). The adjusted interaction was nearly identical (+0.1911, p<1e-12).

treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine also interacted strongly with unfit_for_intensive. The treatment effect was +14.51 points in unfit_for_intensive=1 patients versus -0.77 points in fit patients, for a +15.28 point interaction (p<1e-12); adjusted interaction +0.1522 (p<1e-12). The combined subgroup with npm1_mutation=1 and unfit_for_intensive=1 showed the largest observed response difference: 63.31% with venetoclax/azacitidine versus 15.96% without it, a +47.35 point difference (p<1e-12).

The venetoclax/azacitidine advantage was attenuated by adverse genetics. In tp53_mutation=1 patients, the venetoclax/azacitidine difference was -0.36 points versus +6.00 points in tp53_mutation=0 patients; interaction -6.36 points (p=2.161e-8), adjusted -0.0684 (p=1.505e-9). In complex_karyotype=1 patients, the treatment difference was +1.15 points versus +6.09 points without complex karyotype; interaction -4.94 points (p=3.686e-7), adjusted -0.0516 (p=9.776e-8). When patients had either tp53_mutation or complex_karyotype, venetoclax/azacitidine was not significantly beneficial (+0.65 points, p=0.359). In patients with neither feature, it remained beneficial (+6.81 points, p<1e-12).

treatment_7plus3 did not have an overall effect, but complex_karyotype modified its association. The 7+3 response difference was -2.32 points in complex_karyotype=1 patients versus +0.72 points in complex_karyotype=0 patients; interaction -3.04 points (p=0.001308), adjusted -0.0316 (p=0.000798). Interactions between treatment_7plus3 and unfit_for_intensive (-0.0021 adjusted, p=0.771) or npm1_mutation (-0.0118 unadjusted, p=0.141) were not significant.

Targeted-treatment biomarker hypotheses were mostly unsupported. treatment_midostaurin did not show greater benefit in flt3_itd=1 patients; adjusted interaction -0.0156 (p=0.194). treatment_gilteritinib also did not show a significant positive flt3_itd interaction; adjusted interaction +0.0114 (p=0.346). treatment_enasidenib did not show an idh2_mutation interaction (+0.0043, p=0.845). treatment_ivosidenib had an adjusted idh1_mutation interaction of -0.0449 (p=0.048), opposite the hypothesized positive predictive effect; this should be interpreted as a detected negative subgroup signal rather than support for targeted benefit.

Other interactions and negative controls
The favorable npm1_mutation association did not materially depend on flt3_itd status; adjusted npm1_mutation*flt3_itd interaction -0.0045 (p=0.648). tp53_mutation and complex_karyotype did not show a more-than-additive adverse interaction; adjusted interaction +0.0177 (p=0.243). secondary_aml did not amplify the tp53_mutation association; adjusted interaction +0.0041 (p=0.756).

secondary_aml itself was not adverse in this dataset: response was 18.81% with secondary_aml=1 versus 18.73% without it, a +0.08 point difference (p=0.841). Non-AML metastatic or effusion fields also looked like noise with respect to AML objective response. A composite indicator for liver_mets, bone_mets, adrenal_mets, pleural_effusion, pericardial_effusion, or contralateral_lung_mets was -0.17 points (p=0.623).

Overall conclusions
The main reproducible pattern is a strong treatment-biomarker-treatment-context structure around treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine. It is associated with higher objective_response overall, but the signal is concentrated in patients with npm1_mutation and especially those who are unfit_for_intensive. The benefit is substantially weakened or absent among patients with tp53_mutation or complex_karyotype. Independent of treatment, npm1_mutation is favorable, while tp53_mutation, complex_karyotype, high ECOG performance status, high marrow blasts, high WBC, weight loss, and CRP are adverse; higher albumin is favorable.

The data did not support broad positive main effects for treatment_7plus3 or the targeted-treatment indicators, nor did it support expected positive targeted-therapy interactions for FLT3 or IDH markers. Several demographic, comorbidity, secondary AML, and non-AML metastasis/effusion variables were null or near-null after targeted probing.
