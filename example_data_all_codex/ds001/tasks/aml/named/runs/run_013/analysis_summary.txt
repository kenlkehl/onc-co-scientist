Dataset ds001_aml contained 50000 patients and an overall objective_response rate of 18.75%. I tested 48 self-contained hypotheses over 25 iterations using response-rate contrasts, subgroup difference-in-differences, and robust linear probability models. Effect estimates in transcript.json are signed absolute response-probability differences unless noted as adjusted linear probability model coefficients.

The dominant treatment pattern was not a broad treatment main effect but a strong treatment-by-fitness interaction. treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine had a positive unadjusted main association (+0.0535), but this came from unfit_for_intensive patients: among unfit patients the treated response rate was 30.81% vs 16.30% untreated (+0.1451, p<1e-300), whereas among fit patients the treated rate was slightly lower (16.05% vs 16.81%, -0.0077, p=0.0804). The venetoclax/azacitidine-by-unfit difference-in-differences was +0.1528, and the adjusted model retained the interaction (+0.1523, p<1e-300). treatment_7plus3 showed no meaningful overall or unfit-specific advantage.

The strongest biomarker signal was npm1_mutation. Overall response was 23.46% in NPM1-mutated patients vs 16.73% in wildtype patients (+0.0674, p<1e-300). This signal was highly context-dependent: in venetoclax/azacitidine-treated patients the NPM1 response difference was +0.1816, while in patients not receiving venetoclax/azacitidine it was -0.0092. The unfit + venetoclax/azacitidine + NPM1 subgroup was especially enriched for response (63.31% vs 16.91%, +0.4640, p<1e-300), whereas unfit patients not receiving venetoclax/azacitidine had no NPM1 advantage (-0.0049, p=0.5051).

Adverse-risk biology blunted favorable NPM1 and venetoclax/azacitidine patterns. tp53_mutation lowered response overall (-0.0242, p=3.23e-5) and eliminated the NPM1 advantage: NPM1 was favorable in TP53-wildtype disease (+0.0750) but not in TP53-mutated disease (-0.0005), yielding a negative interaction of -0.0755. TP53 mutation also removed the venetoclax/azacitidine advantage (-0.0636 interaction). complex_karyotype was adverse (-0.0229 unadjusted; -0.0241 adjusted) and similarly attenuated NPM1 benefit: NPM1 was favorable in non-complex karyotype disease (+0.0819) but not in complex karyotype disease (-0.0139).

Disease burden, performance status, nutritional status, and inflammation were clinically coherent. ecog_ps >=1, ecog_ps=2, weight_loss_pct_6mo >=5, albumin_g_dl <3.5, blast_pct_marrow >=70, wbc_k_per_ul >=50, and crp_mg_l >=10 were all associated with lower response. In the adjusted model, ecog_ps (-0.0523 per ECOG step), weight_loss_pct_6mo (-0.0199 per SD), albumin_g_dl (+0.0091 per SD), blast_pct_marrow (-0.0146 per SD), and crp_mg_l (-0.0076 per SD) remained significant. NLR >=5 had a small negative estimate but was not significant.

Several clinically plausible hypotheses were refuted. FLT3-ITD, FLT3-TKD, IDH1, and IDH2 mutation status had no significant univariate response association. Within marker-positive subgroups, midostaurin for FLT3-ITD, gilteritinib for FLT3-ITD, ivosidenib for IDH1, and enasidenib for IDH2 did not show statistically significant response improvements in this cohort. secondary_aml and sex_female also were not significant. Solid-tumor-oriented markers in this AML file (her2_amplification, met_exon14_skipping, ret_fusion, ros1_fusion) had near-zero, non-significant response differences and behaved as negative controls.

Overall, the cohort's response structure is dominated by an NPM1-enriched venetoclax/azacitidine-sensitive subgroup among patients unfit for intensive therapy, counterbalanced by adverse TP53 and complex-karyotype biology and by general clinical frailty/disease-burden markers. Mutation-treatment relationships expected for FLT3 and IDH targeted agents were not supported by these observational response contrasts.
