Oncology dataset ds001_aml analysis summary

Rows analyzed: 50000. Outcome: objective_response. Overall response rate was 18.75%.

Main-effect exploration found a favorable unadjusted association for npm1_mutation: 23.5% response with mutation versus 16.7% without it, a +6.7 percentage-point difference (p < 1e-250). Adverse genetics moved in the opposite direction: tp53_mutation was associated with a -2.4 percentage-point response difference (p=3.23e-5), and complex_karyotype with a -2.3 percentage-point difference (p=3.07e-6).

Clinical burden variables were consistent with lower response. In the adjusted linear-probability model, each 1-point increase in ecog_ps was associated with -5.2 percentage points lower response (p < 1e-250), each 1% greater weight_loss_pct_6mo with -0.52 percentage points lower response (p < 1e-250), blast_pct_marrow with -0.08 percentage points per percentage point (p < 1e-250), and wbc_k_per_ul with -0.03 percentage points per k/uL (p=1.23e-4). Albumin was favorable (+1.8 percentage points per g/dL, p=1.38e-7), while CRP was unfavorable (-0.08 percentage points per mg/L, p=6.03e-6). Age and sex_female were not statistically significant after adjustment (age p=0.655, sex_female p=0.353).

Initial treatment scans showed higher unadjusted response with treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine (22.0% vs 16.6%, difference +5.4 percentage points, p < 1e-250) and in patients marked unfit_for_intensive (22.1% vs 16.5%, difference +5.6 percentage points, p < 1e-250). A main-effects adjusted model preserved positive coefficients for npm1_mutation (+0.0675), unfit_for_intensive (+0.0562), and treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine (+0.0542), but later interaction testing showed these main effects were incomplete summaries.

Targeted therapy hypotheses outside venetoclax-azacitidine were not supported. flt3_itd and flt3_tkd had no meaningful unadjusted response advantage. The adjusted treatment_midostaurin by flt3_itd interaction was -0.017 (p=0.169). treatment_gilteritinib interactions with flt3_itd and flt3_tkd were +0.012 and -0.031 (p=0.334 and p=0.135). IDH-directed hypotheses were also refuted: treatment_ivosidenib by idh1_mutation was -0.045 (p=0.0637) and treatment_enasidenib by idh2_mutation was +0.005 (p=0.826).

The dominant pattern was treatment-biomarker-fitness heterogeneity for treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine. In unfit_for_intensive patients, venetoclax-azacitidine was associated with a +14.5 percentage-point response increase (30.8% vs 16.3%, p < 1e-250), but not in fit patients (difference -0.8 percentage points, p=0.0804). In npm1_mutation patients, the venetoclax-azacitidine difference was +18.7 percentage points (p < 1e-250), but it was near zero in NPM1-wild-type patients. Adjusted two-way interactions were strongly positive for venetoclax-azacitidine with unfit_for_intensive (+0.153) and npm1_mutation (+0.191), and negative with tp53_mutation (-0.068) and complex_karyotype (-0.051).

Refinement showed the apparent two-way venetoclax-azacitidine signals were best explained by a three-way interaction. Among patients with both npm1_mutation and unfit_for_intensive, venetoclax-azacitidine response was 63.3% versus 16.0% without venetoclax-azacitidine, a +47.4 percentage-point difference (p < 1e-250). The corresponding differences were small and nonsignificant in NPM1-mutated fit patients (-0.3 percentage points, p=0.705) and NPM1-wild-type unfit patients (+0.5 percentage points, p=0.469). The explicit three-way coefficient treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine * npm1_mutation * unfit_for_intensive was +0.4622 (p < 1e-250).

Adverse-risk genetics modified those favorable NPM1/venetoclax patterns. The NPM1 advantage was present without tp53_mutation (+7.5 percentage points, p < 1e-250) but absent with tp53_mutation (-0.1 percentage points, p=0.964), with an NPM1 by TP53 interaction of -0.0755 (p=2.41e-9). Similarly, NPM1 was favorable without complex_karyotype (+8.2 percentage points, p < 1e-250) but not with complex_karyotype (-1.4 percentage points, p=0.139). Within NPM1-mutated unfit patients, venetoclax-azacitidine produced a very large benefit when TP53 was wild-type (+53.4 percentage points, p < 1e-250) or karyotype was non-complex (+55.6 percentage points, p < 1e-250), but not when tp53_mutation or complex_karyotype was present.

For treatment_7plus3, there was no adjusted overall response association (+0.0020, p=0.567), and the treatment_7plus3 by complex_karyotype interaction was negative (-0.0316, p=0.000976). Overall, the cohort's most clinically meaningful signal is a high-response subgroup defined by treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine plus npm1_mutation plus unfit_for_intensive, conditional on absence of TP53 mutation and complex karyotype. FLT3- and IDH-directed treatment-biomarker hypotheses were not statistically supported in this dataset.
