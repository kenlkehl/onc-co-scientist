Oncology dataset analysis summary for ds001_aml

I analyzed 50,000 AML patient records with binary objective_response as the endpoint. Effect estimates below are absolute response-probability differences from stratified rate comparisons or HC1-robust linear probability models; positive values mean higher objective_response.

The initial main-effect screen showed a strong association for treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine: response was 0.220 on treatment versus 0.166 off treatment (risk difference +0.0535, p=3.86e-49), and the adjusted main-effect coefficient was similar (+0.0542, p=2.51e-52). npm1_mutation also appeared strongly favorable at first (+0.0674 unadjusted, p=7.24e-64; +0.0675 adjusted main effect, p=5.17e-67). Poor-risk features tp53_mutation and complex_karyotype were initially unfavorable (-0.0263, p=2.48e-6 and -0.0239, p=4.30e-7).

Clinical disease burden behaved as expected. Each one-point increase in ecog_ps was associated with -0.0522 lower response probability (p=1.42e-97). Each 5 percentage-point increase in weight_loss_pct_6mo was associated with -0.0260 lower response (p=2.37e-34). Higher blast_pct_marrow, wbc_k_per_ul, and crp_mg_l were also adverse, while higher albumin_g_dl was favorable (+0.0181 per 1 g/dL, p=1.31e-7).

The main finding was subgroup heterogeneity around venetoclax-azacitidine. Two-way screens suggested stronger venetoclax association in unfit patients (+0.1528 interaction, p=4.29e-91) and in NPM1-mutated patients (+0.1908 interaction, p=1.07e-116). Refinement showed these were driven by a three-way subgroup: treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine=1, npm1_mutation=1, and unfit_for_intensive=1. In a multivariable interaction model, this three-way coefficient was +0.4633 (p=9.23e-185). The observed response rate in that subgroup was 0.633 versus 0.165 in all other patients.

That favorable subgroup was sharply modified by adverse genetics. Within venetoclax/NPM1-mutated/unfit patients, tp53_mutation reduced response from 0.691 to 0.188 (difference -0.5024, p=7.87e-86), and complex_karyotype reduced response from 0.717 to 0.184 (difference -0.5330, p=7.49e-126). The most refined favorable phenotype, treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine=1, npm1_mutation=1, unfit_for_intensive=1, tp53_mutation=0, and complex_karyotype=0, had response 0.786 versus 0.165 in everyone else (difference +0.6208, p<1e-300). In the refined interaction model, the lower-order venetoclax-by-NPM1 and venetoclax-by-unfit terms were no longer significant, confirming that the three-way subgroup carried the signal.

For intensive chemotherapy, treatment_7plus3 did not show a broad main-effect advantage, but complex_karyotype modified its association: the adjusted treatment_7plus3 by complex_karyotype interaction was -0.0305 (p=0.00146). By contrast, treatment_7plus3 did not meaningfully vary by unfit_for_intensive (-0.0020, p=0.782) or age >=70 (-0.0012, p=0.858).

Matched targeted-therapy hypotheses were not supported. Midostaurin and gilteritinib did not show significant positive interactions with flt3_itd or flt3_tkd. Ivosidenib did not show a positive interaction with idh1_mutation; the estimate was negative and borderline (-0.0439, p=0.053). Enasidenib showed no interaction with idh2_mutation (+0.0039, p=0.861).

Overall conclusion: the cohort contains a strong treatment-biomarker-fitness interaction. The clearest clinically meaningful pattern is high objective response among venetoclax-azacitidine treated, NPM1-mutated, intensive-unfit AML patients, but only when TP53 mutation and complex karyotype are absent. Apparent main effects for npm1_mutation, tp53_mutation, complex_karyotype, and venetoclax are misleading unless this interaction structure is modeled. General frailty and inflammatory/disease-burden markers independently track lower response.
