Dataset ds001_aml contained 50,000 patients and an overall objective_response rate of 18.75%.

I analyzed response patterns with two-proportion z-tests for binary or thresholded subgroup comparisons and robust linear probability models for adjusted main effects and interactions. Effect estimates are risk differences or adjusted probability-scale coefficients.

The clearest favorable molecular main effect was npm1_mutation. NPM1-mutated patients had a response rate of 23.46% versus 16.73% in NPM1-wild-type patients (risk difference +6.74 percentage points, p < 1e-300). This persisted after adjustment (+6.75 points, p < 1e-300).

Poorer baseline status and higher disease burden were associated with lower response. ECOG performance status >= 2 had a 12.91% response rate versus 19.77% for ECOG < 2 (risk difference -6.86 points, p < 1e-300), and each one-point higher ecog_ps remained independently negative after adjustment (-5.22 points, p < 1e-300). High WBC (wbc_k_per_ul >= 50) and high marrow blasts (blast_pct_marrow >= 70) were both lower-response groups, with risk differences of -2.23 and -2.79 points. In adjusted models, log WBC and blast percentage remained independently negative.

Inflammation, nutrition, and wasting also mattered. Albumin < 3.5 g/dL was associated with lower response (-1.84 points, p=6.11e-6), weight_loss_pct_6mo >= 10 with lower response (-5.18 points, p=4.44e-16), and CRP >= 10 with lower response (-2.78 points, p=4.84e-9). After adjustment, higher albumin remained favorable (+0.91 points per SD), while higher CRP and greater weight loss remained unfavorable.

Adverse genetics showed expected negative main effects. tp53_mutation was associated with lower response (-2.42 points, p=3.23e-5), and complex_karyotype was similarly lower (-2.29 points, p=3.07e-6). Both remained significant in adjusted models, but the tp53_mutation by complex_karyotype interaction was not significant, so the evidence supported additive adverse effects rather than a distinct synergistic interaction.

The dominant treatment-biomarker pattern was venetoclax/azacitidine heterogeneity. Overall, treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine was associated with higher response (+5.35 points), and unfit_for_intensive patients also had higher observed response (+5.59 points), which prompted interaction testing. The adjusted venetoclax/azacitidine by unfit_for_intensive interaction was strongly positive (+15.22 points, p < 1e-300). In unfit patients, response was 30.81% with venetoclax/azacitidine versus 16.30% without it; in fit patients, the comparison was slightly negative and not significant.

Venetoclax/azacitidine was also strongly modified by NPM1 and TP53. The venetoclax/azacitidine by npm1_mutation interaction was +19.12 points (p < 1e-300), and among venetoclax/azacitidine-treated patients, NPM1 mutation separated response sharply: 34.66% versus 16.49%. In contrast, the venetoclax/azacitidine by tp53_mutation interaction was negative (-6.84 points, p=1.52e-9), and TP53-mutated patients on venetoclax/azacitidine had lower response than TP53-wild-type treated patients (16.36% versus 22.60%).

Higher-order subgroup tests refined that result. The venetoclax/azacitidine by unfit_for_intensive by npm1_mutation three-way interaction was very large and positive (+46.40 points, p < 1e-300). The high-response subgroup was unfit_for_intensive = 1, npm1_mutation = 1, and tp53_mutation = 0: response was 69.08% with venetoclax/azacitidine versus 15.69% without it. TP53 mutation attenuated this pattern; venetoclax/azacitidine by unfit_for_intensive by tp53_mutation was negative (-15.86 points, p=8.31e-12), and venetoclax/azacitidine by npm1_mutation by tp53_mutation was also negative (-19.28 points, p=1.07e-14).

Other targeted-treatment hypotheses were not supported. FLT3-directed therapy did not show a significant positive interaction with flt3_itd: midostaurin by flt3_itd was -1.53 points (p=0.202), and gilteritinib by flt3_itd was +1.04 points (p=0.386). IDH-directed therapy also did not show the expected biomarker-specific signal: ivosidenib by idh1_mutation was borderline negative (-4.39 points, p=0.052), and enasidenib by idh2_mutation was near null (+0.37 points, p=0.867). Intensive 7+3 therapy did not have meaningful interactions with unfit status or age >= 75.

Several plausible demographic and comorbidity hypotheses were refuted. Age >= 75 had essentially no response difference (-0.07 points, p=0.860), sex_female was not significant (+0.29 points, p=0.414), secondary_aml was null after adjustment (+0.04 points, p=0.917), and atrial_fibrillation was negative but not significant (-1.14 points, p=0.061). Non-AML cancer-marker/metastasis fields such as liver_mets and her2_amplification showed only small, non-significant associations.

Overall, this cohort's objective_response patterns were driven by a combination of AML biology, performance/disease-burden features, and a highly specific treatment interaction: venetoclax/azacitidine benefit concentrated in unfit, NPM1-mutated, TP53-wild-type patients. TP53 mutation and complex karyotype were adverse, but TP53 was especially important because it blunted the venetoclax/azacitidine/NPM1/unfit response pattern. Other targeted-agent biomarker hypotheses were not statistically supported in these data.
