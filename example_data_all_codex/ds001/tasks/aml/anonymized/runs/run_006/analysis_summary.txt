Oncology cohort ds001_aml analysis summary

The local Parquet dataset contained 50000 AML patient records, 75 de-identified feature columns, a patient_id identifier, and the binary outcome objective_response. The overall objective_response rate was 18.75% (9375/50000). Feature labels are de-identified, so the results should be read as statistical associations with opaque columns rather than named clinical mechanisms.

Main-effect analyses first identified three favorable binary features. feature_025 was associated with response of 23.5% versus 16.7% (risk difference 6.7%, p=<1e-250). feature_015 showed 22.1% versus 16.5% (risk difference 5.6%, p=<1e-250), and feature_023 showed 22.0% versus 16.6% (risk difference 5.4%, p=<1e-250).

Adverse or lower-response main effects were also present. feature_027 level 2 had lower response than levels 0/1 (-6.9%, p=<1e-250), and the one-level feature_027 trend was -5.2% (p=<1e-250). High feature_004 and high feature_031 were associated with lower response (-4.1% and -2.8%, respectively). feature_001 and feature_032 also had negative univariate risk differences (-2.3% and -2.4%).

Interaction analyses showed that the favorable binary features were strongly non-additive. The feature_025 x feature_015 additive interaction was 19.3% (p=<1e-250), feature_025 x feature_023 was 19.1% (p=<1e-250), and feature_015 x feature_023 was 15.3% (p=<1e-250). Patients positive for all three features had response 63.3% versus 16.5% in all others (risk difference 46.8%, p=<1e-250). The formal three-way additive contrast was 46.2% (p=<1e-250), so the triple-positive subgroup exceeded the lower-order additive pattern as well.

Resistance-like heterogeneity was observed for feature_001 and feature_032. feature_001 attenuated the feature_025 association (feature_025 x feature_001 interaction -9.6%, p=<1e-250), and feature_032 attenuated the feature_025 association (-7.5%, p=6.70e-10). Similar attenuation appeared for feature_015 x feature_001 (-6.0%) and feature_023 x feature_032 (-6.4%).

A selected robust-SE linear probability model retained the three favorable pairwise interactions after adjustment: feature_025_x_feature_015 19.4% (p=<1e-250), feature_025_x_feature_023 19.2% (p=<1e-250), and feature_015_x_feature_023 15.3% (p=<1e-250). In that interaction-adjusted model, feature_001 (0.6%, p=0.320) and feature_032 (-0.2%, p=0.753) did not retain independent adverse main effects, while feature_027 and feature_004 remained negative.

Exploratory analyses separated weaker signals from the dominant pattern. feature_059 high quartile had a small positive association and feature_053 high quartile had a small negative association. In contrast, feature_005 and feature_074 univariate hypotheses were not significant at p<0.05, and the proposed feature_005 x feature_013 interaction was also not significant. Median-split feature_004 and feature_027 level 2 did not materially modify the feature_025 association.

The exhaustive binary-pair screen supported the same structure: the most significant adequate-cell interactions were dominated by feature_025, feature_015, feature_023, feature_001, and feature_032. Overall, objective response in this AML cohort appears driven by a strong treatment/biomarker-like interaction pattern among the three favorable opaque features, with adverse feature_027/feature_004 effects and attenuation by feature_001 and feature_032. Because the dataset is anonymized and observational, these findings are exploratory associations rather than causal clinical conclusions.
