Dataset ds001_aml contained 50000 AML patient records, 75 de-identified feature columns, and one binary outcome, objective_response. The overall response rate was 0.1875. Because the feature labels are opaque, the findings below are statistical treatment/biomarker-like associations with named columns, not clinical claims about known therapies or mutations.

Iterations 1-7 screened prominent main effects and a near-null comparator. feature_027 showed the strongest ordinal adverse association: response declined from 0.231 at level 0 to 0.174 at level 1 and 0.129 at level 2, with a probability-scale slope of -0.0522 per level (p=1.00e-300). Three binary features were favorable: feature_025 increased response by 0.0674 (0.235 vs 0.167, p=1.00e-300), feature_015 increased response by 0.0559 (0.221 vs 0.165, p=1.00e-300), and feature_023 increased response by 0.0535 (0.220 vs 0.166, p=1.00e-300). Two binary features were unfavorable: feature_001 had a -0.0229 response difference (p=3.07e-6), and feature_032 had a -0.0242 response difference (p=3.23e-5). By contrast, feature_044 had no meaningful main effect per 10 units (effect -0.00024, p=0.882), so that hypothesis was not supported.

Iterations 8-12 evaluated continuous or ordinal main effects. Higher feature_004 was associated with lower response (effect -0.00522 per unit, p=1.00e-300). Higher feature_031 was also adverse (effect -0.00748 per 10 units, p=2.22e-16). Higher feature_059 was favorable (effect 0.0179 per unit, p=2.82e-7). Higher feature_033 was adverse (effect -0.00086 per unit, p=6.04e-6). Higher feature_053 was adverse by linear trend (effect -0.00252 per 10 units, p=1.82e-4), and the high feature_053 subgroup (>=20) had response 0.176 versus 0.193 below 20 (difference -0.0162, p=1.78e-5).

Iteration 13 tested whether the strongest main effects persisted after adjustment for other leading signals. feature_025 remained favorable (adjusted coefficient 0.0675, p=1.00e-300), feature_027 remained adverse (-0.0522 per level, p=1.00e-300), feature_004 remained adverse (-0.00519 per unit, p=1.00e-300), and feature_059 remained favorable (0.0181 per unit, p=1.41e-7). The adjusted feature_044 effect remained nonsignificant (-0.00071 per 10 units, p=0.653), reinforcing the null main-effect result.

Iterations 14-17 found strong favorable binary-binary heterogeneity among feature_015, feature_025, and feature_023. The feature_015 by feature_025 interaction was 0.1926 (p=1.00e-300), with response 0.349 when both were present and much lower response in single-positive groups. Stratification confirmed that feature_015 had a large positive effect when feature_025=1 (effect 0.1910, p=1.00e-300) but no effect when feature_025=0 (effect -0.0016, p=0.694). Similar positive interactions were present for feature_025 by feature_023 (0.1908, p=1.00e-300) and feature_015 by feature_023 (0.1528, p=1.00e-300).

Iterations 18-21 tested whether unfavorable features attenuated favorable-feature effects. feature_001 substantially attenuated the feature_025 association, with a negative interaction of -0.0958 (p=1.00e-300). feature_032 attenuated feature_025 as well, with interaction -0.0755 (p=6.70e-10), and attenuated feature_015 with interaction -0.0458 (p=5.97e-5). The co-occurrence of feature_001 and feature_032 did not further lower response beyond additivity; the interaction was positive rather than negative (0.0181) and nonsignificant (p=0.237), so that hypothesis was refuted.

Iterations 22-23 refined the favorable interaction pattern into a three-feature subgroup. Patients with feature_015=1, feature_025=1, and feature_023=1 had response 0.633 (n=2401), compared with 0.165 among all other patients (n=47599), an absolute difference of 0.4680 (p=1.00e-300). In a model containing all three main effects, all pairwise terms, and the three-way term, the three-way interaction remained strongly positive (coefficient 0.4624, p=1.00e-300), while the feature_015 by feature_025 pairwise term became small and nonsignificant after including the three-way term (0.0079, p=0.415). This supports a high-response subgroup defined by simultaneous positivity for all three features rather than isolated positivity for one or two.

Iterations 24-25 probed additional heterogeneity. feature_027 did not significantly modify the feature_015 association (interaction -0.0044, p=0.404), so the hypothesis that feature_015 becomes weaker as feature_027 rises was not supported. Two weaker exploratory continuous-by-binary interactions were significant: the feature_044 slope was more positive among feature_066=1 than feature_066=0 (difference 0.0129 per 10 units, p=0.00398), and the adverse feature_053 slope was steeper among feature_068=1 than feature_068=0 (difference -0.00765 per 10 units, p=0.00689). These are smaller and more exploratory than the binary three-feature subgroup.

Overall, objective_response in this cohort was dominated by a strong adverse gradient across feature_027, favorable main effects for feature_025, feature_015, and feature_023, adverse main effects for feature_001, feature_032, feature_004, feature_031, feature_033, and feature_053, and a favorable continuous association for feature_059. The most clinically meaningful statistical pattern was the non-additive high-response subgroup where feature_015, feature_025, and feature_023 were all present. The strongest negative heterogeneity involved feature_001 and feature_032 attenuating otherwise favorable binary-feature associations.
