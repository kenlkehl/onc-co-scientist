Dataset ds001_aml contained 50000 patients and 75 de-identified feature columns. The binary outcome objective_response had an overall response rate of 0.1875.

I treated objective_response as a binary probability-scale endpoint. Binary-feature main effects were tested as response-rate differences; continuous and ordinal-feature effects were tested with linear probability regressions; adjusted models used all 75 feature main effects; interactions were tested as binary subgroup difference-in-differences or product terms in linear probability models.

Overall, the cohort showed several statistically robust treatment/biomarker-like signals despite the opaque feature labels. The strongest supported conclusions were probability-scale main effects for a small group of features, persistence of several of those effects in full multivariable adjustment, and non-additive response heterogeneity in selected feature-defined subgroups. The most convincing interaction patterns were the binary-binary pairs listed above because they were directly interpretable as differences in observed response rates across four patient subgroups. Continuous interaction findings were statistically weaker and should be interpreted as exploratory unless replicated in an external cohort or a prespecified model.
