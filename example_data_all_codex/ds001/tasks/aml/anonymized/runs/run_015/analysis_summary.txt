Dataset ds001_aml analysis summary

I analyzed 50000 AML patient records with binary outcome objective_response. The overall response rate was 18.8%. Effect estimates below are absolute changes in response probability unless otherwise noted; continuous-feature effects are per one standard deviation.

Main effects. The strongest univariate negative association was feature_027: each one-SD increase was associated with a -0.0354 probability change (p<1e-300), and the level-specific contrast was monotone: response was 23.1% at feature_027=0, 17.4% at feature_027=1, and 12.9% at feature_027=2. Other supported negative main effects were feature_004 (-0.0201 per SD), feature_031 (-0.0143 per SD), feature_001=1 (-0.0229), feature_032=1 (-0.0242), and feature_053 (-0.0065 per SD). feature_059 was a smaller positive main effect (+0.0090 per SD). feature_047 was tested as a positive continuous effect but was not significant at 0.05 (+0.0034 per SD, p=0.051).

The binary features feature_025, feature_015, and feature_023 initially looked like strong positive main effects. Univariate response differences were +0.0674 for feature_025=1, +0.0559 for feature_015=1, and +0.0535 for feature_023=1, all with p<1e-300. Pairwise 2x2 interaction scans also showed large positive risk-difference interactions: feature_015 with feature_025 (+0.1926), feature_025 with feature_023 (+0.1908), and feature_015 with feature_023 (+0.1528).

Refinement of that signal showed the key pattern was a three-feature subgroup. Patients with all three of feature_015=1, feature_025=1, and feature_023=1 had a 63.3% response rate (2401 patients), compared with 16.5% among patients not carrying all three. The absolute difference was +0.4680 (p<1e-300). In a saturated model containing all three main effects, all three pairwise products, and the three-way product, the three-way interaction remained very large (+0.4623, p<1e-300), while the pairwise terms were no longer independently significant. Thus the apparent pairwise synergies are best interpreted as markers of an all-three subgroup rather than separate two-feature effects.

Negative modifier analyses suggested lower response when feature_001 or feature_032 co-occurred with the initially favorable features. The strongest negative interactions were feature_025:feature_001 (-0.0958), feature_025:feature_032 (-0.0755), feature_015:feature_001 (-0.0595), feature_023:feature_032 (-0.0636), feature_023:feature_001 (-0.0494), and feature_015:feature_032 (-0.0458), all significant. These were two-feature linear probability interaction models, so they describe subgroup heterogeneity on the absolute response scale.

The final multivariable model included the saturated feature_015/feature_025/feature_023 interaction structure and the main covariates feature_027, feature_004, feature_031, feature_059, feature_001, feature_032, and feature_053. The all-three interaction remained the dominant positive association (+0.4623). Independent negative adjusted effects persisted for feature_027 (-0.0355 per SD), feature_004 (-0.0202 per SD), feature_031 (-0.0144 per SD), feature_001=1 (-0.0247), feature_032=1 (-0.0283), and feature_053 (-0.0067 per SD); feature_059 remained positively associated (+0.0090 per SD).

Additional exploratory interactions were smaller but statistically significant in the unadjusted 2x2 interaction scan: feature_019=1 with feature_047 above median (+0.0356), feature_040=1 with feature_046=1 (+0.0478), and feature_044 above median with feature_039=1 (-0.0280). These should be treated as secondary because many pairwise combinations were screened.

Overall conclusion: objective_response in this cohort is dominated by a high-response subgroup defined by concurrent feature_015=1, feature_025=1, and feature_023=1. Several additional features, especially feature_027, feature_004, feature_031, feature_001, and feature_032, identify lower-response patterns that remain after adjustment. The most defensible interaction finding is the three-way feature_015/feature_025/feature_023 relationship; isolated pairwise synergies among those three features are not supported after the three-way term is modeled.
