Dataset ds001_aml contained 50000 patients and one binary outcome, objective_response. The overall response rate was 0.1875. I decoded the local Parquet file and tested main effects, subgroup heterogeneity, and interactions using response-rate contrasts and linear probability models; p-values are exploratory and mostly unadjusted unless explicitly described as adjusted.

Main effects: feature_027 had the strongest monotone association. Response fell from 0.231 at level 0 to 0.174 at level 1 and 0.129 at level 2, with an effect of -0.0522 per level (p=1.00e-300). The strongest binary positive main effects were feature_025 (effect 0.0674, p=1.00e-300), feature_015 (effect 0.0559, p=1.00e-300), and feature_023 (effect 0.0535, p=1.00e-300). Negative binary main effects were feature_001 (effect -0.0229, p=3.07e-6) and feature_032 (effect -0.0242, p=3.23e-5). Feature_044 showed no meaningful main effect per 10 units (effect -0.00024, p=0.882).

Continuous features: feature_004, feature_031, feature_059, feature_033, and feature_053 all showed significant univariable associations. The directions were lower response with higher feature_004 (effect -0.0052 per unit), lower response with higher feature_031 (effect -0.0075 per 10 units), higher response with higher feature_059 (effect 0.0179 per unit), lower response with higher feature_033 (effect -0.00086 per unit), and lower response with higher feature_053 (effect -0.0025 per 10 units). In the extended adjusted model, feature_025, feature_027, feature_004, and feature_059 retained their directions; feature_044 stayed null.

The most important interaction pattern involved feature_015, feature_025, and feature_023. Pairwise screens showed large positive interactions among these variables, especially feature_015 x feature_025 (DID 0.1926, p=1.00e-300), feature_025 x feature_023 (DID 0.1908, p=1.00e-300), and feature_015 x feature_023 (DID 0.1528, p=1.00e-300). Subgroup testing refined this: feature_015 had almost no effect when feature_025=0 (effect -0.0016, p=0.694) but a large positive effect when feature_025=1 (effect 0.1910, p=1.00e-300).

The refined multivariable interaction model showed that the apparent pairwise effects were largely driven by a three-way pattern. Patients with feature_015=1, feature_025=1, and feature_023=1 had response 0.633 (n=2401), versus 0.165 among all other patients, an absolute difference of 0.4680. In a model containing all three main effects, all pairwise terms, the three-way term, feature_001, feature_032, and feature_027, the three-way coefficient was 0.4624 (p=1.00e-300), while the feature_015 x feature_025 pairwise term became nonsignificant. This supports a high-response subgroup defined by simultaneous positivity for all three features rather than isolated positivity for only one or two.

Negative-effect heterogeneity was also present. feature_001 attenuated the feature_025 association (interaction -0.0958, p=1.00e-300), feature_032 attenuated the feature_025 association (interaction -0.0755, p=6.70e-10), and feature_032 attenuated the feature_015 association (interaction -0.0458, p=5.97e-5). The two negative main-effect features feature_001 and feature_032 did not show evidence of an additional negative interaction with each other (interaction 0.0181, p=0.237).

Subgroup checks across feature_027 did not show statistically significant modification of the feature_015 effect by feature_027 (interaction -0.0044, p=0.404). Nominal continuous-by-binary interactions were detected for feature_044 by feature_066 (slope difference 0.0129 per 10 units, p=0.00398) and feature_053 by feature_068 (slope difference -0.0077 per 10 units, p=0.00689), but these were weaker exploratory findings.

Overall conclusion: objective_response in this cohort is dominated by a strong low-response gradient across feature_027, independent continuous adverse associations involving feature_004, feature_031, feature_033, and feature_053, a positive continuous association with feature_059, and a striking high-response subgroup where feature_015, feature_025, and feature_023 are all present. The strongest treatment-biomarker-like relationship is the three-feature interaction; isolated positivity for only one or two of those features does not reproduce the same high response rate.
