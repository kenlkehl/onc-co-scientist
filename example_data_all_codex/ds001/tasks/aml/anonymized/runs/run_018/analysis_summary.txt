Dataset ds001_aml contained 50,000 AML oncology records with binary outcome objective_response. The overall response rate was 18.75% (9,375/50,000). I decoded dataset.parquet locally and tested response-rate differences, linear probability slopes for continuous features, and interaction terms on the natural probability scale.

The strongest main-effect signal was feature_027. Patients with feature_027=2 had a 12.91% response rate versus 23.10% for feature_027=0, a -10.19 percentage-point difference (p<1e-90). Continuous feature_004 was also strongly adverse: each 1-SD increase was associated with -2.01 percentage points in response (p<1e-30). Additional continuous signals were feature_031 (-1.43 percentage points per SD, p=2.22e-16), feature_059 (+0.90 percentage points per SD, p=2.82e-7), feature_033 (-0.79 percentage points per SD, p=6.04e-6), and feature_053 (-0.65 percentage points per SD, p=1.82e-4).

Among binary features, feature_025, feature_015, and feature_023 had the clearest positive univariable associations with objective_response: +6.74, +5.59, and +5.35 percentage points, respectively. In contrast, feature_001 and feature_032 were negative, at -2.29 and -2.42 percentage points. Several candidate binary effects were refuted or weak: feature_074 was directionally negative but not significant (p=0.063), while feature_045 and feature_040 were essentially null (p=0.627 and p=0.414).

The main response pattern was not simply additive. Pairwise interactions among feature_015, feature_025, and feature_023 were large and positive: feature_015 x feature_025 was +19.26 percentage points, feature_025 x feature_023 was +19.08 percentage points, and feature_015 x feature_023 was +15.28 percentage points, all with extremely small p-values. The full three-way model showed an even sharper concentration: the feature_015=1/feature_025=1/feature_023=1 subgroup had a 63.31% response rate, while the other seven combinations ranged from 15.67% to 17.18%. The three-way interaction was +46.22 percentage points (p=9.61e-178).

Negative modifiers also appeared. feature_001 weakened the feature_025 association by -9.58 percentage points and the feature_015 association by -5.95 percentage points. feature_032 weakened the feature_025 association by -7.55 percentage points and the feature_023 association by -6.36 percentage points. These results suggest that feature_001 and feature_032 mark subgroups where otherwise favorable binary-feature patterns are substantially attenuated.

Additional subgroup heterogeneity was present for continuous-feature strata. The association of feature_019 was more favorable in the top quartile of feature_047 (interaction +3.23 percentage points, p=0.00467). The positive association of feature_023 was larger in the top quartile of feature_031 (interaction +2.18 percentage points, p=0.00756). Conversely, the positive association of feature_005 was weaker in the top quartile of feature_053 (interaction -4.78 percentage points, p=0.00904).

Overall, objective_response in this cohort is most strongly characterized by: (1) an adverse ordered feature_027 gradient, (2) adverse continuous-feature signals led by feature_004 and feature_031, (3) a highly synergistic feature_015/feature_025/feature_023 subgroup with very high response, and (4) attenuation of favorable binary-feature effects in feature_001- and feature_032-positive subgroups. Because feature labels are de-identified, these are statistical treatment-biomarker-outcome patterns rather than mechanistic clinical interpretations.
