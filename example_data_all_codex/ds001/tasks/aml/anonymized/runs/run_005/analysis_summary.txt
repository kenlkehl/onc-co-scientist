Dataset ds001_aml contains 50,000 AML patient records. The outcome objective_response was positive in 9,375 patients (18.75%). I decoded the local Parquet file and ran 25 iterative analyses using two-proportion tests, linear probability trends, additive interaction contrasts, and a robust-SE adjusted linear probability model.

The strongest main-effect signals were favorable binary features feature_025, feature_015, and feature_023. Objective response was 23.5% versus 16.7% for feature_025 (risk difference +6.7 percentage points, p<1e-10), 22.1% versus 16.5% for feature_015 (+5.6 pp, p<1e-10), and 22.0% versus 16.6% for feature_023 (+5.3 pp, p<1e-10). Adverse main effects included feature_027 level 2 versus levels 0/1 (-6.7 pp, p<1e-10), feature_001 (-2.3 pp, p=3.1e-6), feature_032 (-2.4 pp, p=3.2e-5), high feature_004, and high feature_031.

Interaction analyses changed the interpretation substantially. The three favorable binary features were not merely additive: feature_025 x feature_015 had an additive interaction of +19.3 pp, feature_025 x feature_023 +19.1 pp, and feature_015 x feature_023 +15.3 pp, all highly significant. Triple-positive patients for feature_025, feature_015, and feature_023 had a 45.3% response rate versus 17.1% for all others (+28.2 pp, p<1e-10). The formal three-way additive contrast was also strongly positive (+46.2 pp, p<1e-10), so the triple-positive subgroup exceeded expectations even after accounting for lower-order response-rate contrasts.

Resistance-like heterogeneity was also present. feature_001 and feature_032 attenuated the favorable feature_025 association: feature_025 x feature_001 had a -9.6 pp additive interaction and feature_025 x feature_032 had a -7.5 pp interaction. Similar attenuation was seen for feature_015 x feature_001 and feature_023 x feature_032. In the adjusted model, the major favorable pairwise interactions and the adverse feature_025 interactions remained strong; the independent adjusted main effects for feature_001 and feature_032 were no longer significant, while feature_027, feature_004, and feature_031 retained adverse adjusted associations.

Some weaker exploratory findings were not robust enough to treat as central. feature_005 had a positive univariate risk difference of +1.6 pp but p=0.056, and feature_074 had a negative difference of -1.2 pp but p=0.069. A feature_005 x feature_013 negative interaction was also not significant. Exploratory pairwise screens found additional smaller interactions such as feature_040 x feature_046 and feature_050 x feature_071, but the dominant and most coherent structure involved feature_025, feature_015, feature_023, feature_001, and feature_032.

Overall, objective response in this AML cohort appears driven by a strong treatment/biomarker-like interaction pattern among three favorable opaque features, with separate adverse effects from feature_027 and continuous feature_004/feature_031 and interaction-based attenuation by feature_001 and feature_032. Because the feature labels are anonymized, the findings should be interpreted as statistical patterns rather than named clinical mechanisms.
