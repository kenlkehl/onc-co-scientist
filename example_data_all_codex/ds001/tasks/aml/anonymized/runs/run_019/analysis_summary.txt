Dataset ds001_aml contains 50000 AML patient records. The overall objective_response rate was 18.8%. I ran 25 propose-test-refine iterations using response-rate comparisons, four-cell additive interaction tests on the risk-difference scale, and a selected multivariable linear probability model. All effects below are signed absolute differences in objective_response probability unless noted.

Main response-enriched features were feature_025, feature_015, feature_023, and feature_027=0. feature_025=1 had a +0.0674 higher response rate (23.5% vs 16.7%, p=4.653e-70). feature_015=1 and feature_023=1 were also favorable, with risk differences of +0.0559 and +0.0535. feature_027 separated response strongly: feature_027=0 was favorable, while feature_027=2 had a -0.1019 lower response rate than feature_027=0 (p=1.592e-75).

Several features were associated with lower response. feature_001=1 and feature_032=1 had modest but significant negative main effects, with risk differences of -0.0229 and -0.0242. Among continuous or semi-continuous features, feature_004>3 and feature_031>60 were negative (risk differences -0.0325 and -0.0254), feature_053>12.2 was modestly negative, and feature_059>3.8 was modestly positive. The plausible feature_044>68.1 split was not statistically significant (risk difference -0.0058, p=9.663e-2), so that hypothesis was not supported.

The strongest finding was subgroup synergy among feature_025, feature_015, and feature_023. Pairwise additive interactions were large and positive: feature_025 x feature_015 difference-in-differences +0.1926, feature_025 x feature_023 +0.1908, and feature_015 x feature_023 +0.1528, all highly significant. Patients with all three favorable features present had a 63.3% response rate versus 17.2% when all three were absent, a risk difference of +0.4613.

Resistance-like heterogeneity was evident. feature_001 significantly weakened the feature_025 and feature_015 associations (interaction estimates -0.0958 and -0.0595). feature_032 similarly weakened the feature_025 and feature_023 associations (interaction estimates -0.0755 and -0.0636). In contrast, the feature_027=0 by feature_025 interaction and the feature_004>3 by feature_025 interaction were not significant, suggesting those effects were more additive than synergistic.

The multivariable model supported the same interpretation. After adjustment for the major main effects and selected interactions, the feature_025 x feature_015 interaction remained strongly positive (coefficient +0.1941, p=5.572e-147), and feature_004>3 remained independently negative (coefficient -0.0332, p=9.430e-23). The final composite profile combining favorable markers (feature_025=1, feature_015=1, feature_023=1, feature_027=0, feature_001=0, feature_032=0) had an 85.9% response rate versus 11.6% in the adverse comparator group, a +0.7432 absolute difference.

Overall, objective_response in this cohort is not driven by a single feature. The dominant pattern is a synergistic favorable subgroup defined by feature_025, feature_015, and feature_023, stratified further by feature_027 and attenuated by feature_001 and feature_032. Continuous features feature_004, feature_031, feature_053, and feature_059 add smaller independent gradients. These are exploratory statistical associations in opaque de-identified variables, so they should be interpreted as cohort patterns rather than causal clinical effects.
