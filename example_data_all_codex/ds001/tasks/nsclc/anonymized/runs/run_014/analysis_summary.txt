Dataset ds001_nsclc contained 50000 patients with pfs_months as the sole outcome. I treated pfs_months as a continuous endpoint because no censoring indicator was provided. Analyses used OLS mean differences, linear models, subgroup contrasts, adjusted models, and product-term interaction tests. The cohort mean PFS was 3.432 months (SD 2.233).

The strongest pattern was feature_041. Each 1-unit increase in feature_041 was associated with +0.170 months of PFS (p<1e-300), and the top quartile had 4.340 months longer PFS than the bottom quartile. This association was monotonic and remained the dominant effect.

Several adverse main effects were large and statistically robust. Feature_036 showed a graded decrease from 4.327 months at level 0 to 2.152 months at level 2, with an ordinal coefficient of -1.099 months per level. Feature_001=1 was -1.438 months lower than feature_001=0, feature_042=1 was -0.921 months lower than feature_042=0, and feature_015=squamous was -0.857 months lower than adenocarcinoma. Feature_025=current was also adverse: -0.634 months lower than non-current patients univariately and -0.552 months lower than feature_025=former after adjustment. The apparent univariate advantage for feature_025=never did not persist after adjustment (-0.0056 months, p=0.607).

Favorable signals included feature_010=1 (+0.782 months), feature_009=1 (+0.322 months), and higher feature_056 (+0.441 months per unit; top versus bottom quartile +0.505 months). Feature_004 was adverse both linearly (-0.0733 months per unit) and by quartile (-0.691 months for top versus bottom quartile).

Adjustment separated robust predictors from confounded univariate signals. Feature_057=1 was strongly adverse univariately (-0.810 months) but was no longer significant after adjustment (-0.0278 months, p=0.0684). Feature_028 and feature_066 similarly lost independent signal after adjustment. Feature_051 retained a statistically significant but tiny adjusted effect (-0.000446 months per unit), while feature_073 was not independently significant.

The most clinically meaningful heterogeneity came from interactions. The feature_010=1 and feature_009=1 combination was supra-additive: the interaction was +2.558 months, and the double-positive cell averaged 5.766 months compared with roughly 3.2-3.3 months in the other cells. Feature_037 weakened the favorable effects of both feature_010 and feature_009, with interactions of -1.694 and -0.562 months. Feature_057 also acted mainly as an interaction marker, attenuating feature_010 (-1.210 months) and feature_009 (-0.488 months) despite its nonsignificant adjusted main effect.

Feature_041 interactions showed that adverse subgroups had a weaker positive feature_041 slope. The feature_041 slope was attenuated by higher feature_036 (-0.01472 months per feature_041 unit per feature_036 level), feature_001=1 (-0.01265), feature_015=squamous (-0.01180), and feature_042=1 (-0.01138). Favorable markers feature_010 and feature_056 modestly strengthened the feature_041 association.

Weak sparse-feature findings were less convincing. Feature_033=1 had a +0.200 month difference but p=0.0538. Feature_029 and feature_005 had nominal unadjusted differences of -0.119 and +0.097 months, respectively, but these were small compared with the major effects.

Overall, PFS in this cohort was dominated by feature_041, a cluster of adverse markers (feature_036, feature_001, feature_042, feature_015=squamous, feature_025=current, and feature_004), favorable markers (feature_010, feature_009, and feature_056), and strong interaction-defined subgroups. The clearest interaction subgroup was feature_010=1 plus feature_009=1, while feature_037 and feature_057 attenuated otherwise favorable patterns.
