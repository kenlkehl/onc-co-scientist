Dataset ds001_nsclc contains 50000 NSCLC patient records with 73 opaque feature columns, patient_id, and the outcome pfs_months. I analyzed pfs_months as a continuous outcome using unadjusted mean comparisons or simple OLS, then multivariable OLS with all available feature columns encoded as numeric terms or categorical dummies. P-values use large-sample normal approximations for coefficients and Welch-style mean differences.

The outcome had mean 3.432 months, SD 2.233 months, median 3.319, and interquartile range 1.755 to 4.896 months.

Main-effect findings:
- feature_041 showed a supported positive association: 1.699 months per 10 units unadjusted (p<1e-300) and 1.684 months per 10 units adjusted (p<1e-300).
- feature_025 showed supported favorable differences for never and former versus current: never-current 0.687 months (p<1e-300), former-current 0.620 months (p<1e-300), with adjusted never-current 0.545 months and former-current 0.552 months.
- feature_015=squamous was unfavorable versus adenocarcinoma: -0.857 months unadjusted (p<1e-300) and -0.723 months adjusted (p<1e-300).
- feature_036 showed a graded adverse pattern versus level 0: level 1 -1.141 months and level 2 -2.175 months unadjusted; adjusted coefficients were -1.147 and -2.178 months.
- feature_050 was tested as a candidate continuous factor but was not supported: 0.014 months per SD unadjusted (p=0.157) and 0.001 months per SD adjusted (p=0.677).

Several additional binary and continuous features were explored; some remained independent after adjustment while others attenuated. The strongest binary findings explored in iterations 6-10 were feature_001 (-1.438 months unadjusted, adjusted -1.417 months), feature_042 (-0.921 months unadjusted, adjusted -0.912 months), feature_010 (0.782 months unadjusted, adjusted 0.892 months), feature_057 (-0.810 months unadjusted, adjusted -0.028 months), feature_009 (0.322 months unadjusted, adjusted 0.323 months).
The additional continuous findings explored in iterations 11-14 were feature_004 (-0.284 months per SD unadjusted, adjusted -0.283 months per SD), feature_056 (0.221 months per SD unadjusted, adjusted 0.232 months per SD), feature_051 (-0.041 months per SD unadjusted, adjusted -0.036 months per SD), feature_013 (0.022 months per SD unadjusted, adjusted 0.005 months per SD).

Subgroup and interaction findings:
The strongest binary-binary interactions tested in iterations 15-19 were feature_010:feature_009 2.493 months (p<1e-300); feature_010:feature_037 -1.630 months (p<1e-300); feature_009:feature_037 -0.577 months (p<1e-300); feature_010:feature_057 -0.830 months (p<1e-300); feature_010:feature_029 -0.739 months (p<1e-300). Positive coefficients mean the joint subgroup did better than expected from adding the two main effects; negative coefficients mean worse than additivity.
The strongest continuous-binary interactions tested in iterations 20-23 were feature_041 by feature_001 -0.139 months per SD (p<1e-300); feature_041 by feature_042 -0.143 months per SD (p<1e-300); feature_041 by feature_010 0.082 months per SD (p=5.81e-8); feature_041 by feature_028 -0.043 months per SD (p=1.44e-4).

The full multivariable feature model explained R-squared 0.894 of pfs_months variation. Several hypotheses were supported after adjustment, especially feature_041, feature_025, feature_015, feature_036, and the strongest binary features. Feature_050 and the deliberate negative-control style hypothesis in iteration 25 for feature_065 were not supported, with small nonsignificant unadjusted and adjusted effects.

Because feature names are opaque, these results should be interpreted as treatment/biomarker/clinical-feature associations rather than causal effects. The strongest conclusion is that pfs_months in this cohort is heterogeneous: baseline-like variables, categorical clinical strata, multiple binary markers, and specific interactions each contribute measurable differences in progression-free survival.
