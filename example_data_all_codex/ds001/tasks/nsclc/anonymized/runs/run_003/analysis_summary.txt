Dataset: ds001_nsclc
Rows analyzed: 50,000
Outcome: pfs_months

I analyzed the local parquet file using OLS models, two-group mean comparisons, stratified analyses, and interaction models. Reported effects are signed in months of PFS. Very small p-values that underflowed numerically are represented in the transcript as 1e-300.

Main effects

The strongest univariate and adjusted favorable association was `feature_041`: +0.1699 months per unit univariately and +0.1684 months per unit after adjustment. `feature_056` was also favorable after adjustment (+0.4628 months per unit), as were `feature_010=1` (+0.8925 months) and `feature_009=1` (+0.3229 months).

Several features were independently adverse. Higher `feature_036` was associated with lower PFS (-1.1010 adjusted months per unit), `feature_001=1` with -1.4168 adjusted months, `feature_042=1` with -0.9124 adjusted months, and higher `feature_004` with -0.0732 months per unit. `feature_015="squamous"` had lower PFS than `feature_015="adenocarcinoma"` (-0.7220 adjusted months). `feature_025="current"` was worse than `feature_025="never"` (-0.5459 adjusted months), while `feature_025="former"` was not different from never (+0.0057 months, p=0.603).

Some univariate signals did not survive adjustment. `feature_057=1` attenuated to -0.0278 months (p=0.068), `feature_028=1` to -0.0053 months (p=0.481), and `feature_066=1` to -0.0004 months (p=0.967).

Interaction and subgroup findings

The dominant heterogeneity involved `feature_010`, `feature_009`, and `feature_037`. In adjusted models, `feature_010 * feature_009` was strongly positive (+2.5316 months), `feature_010 * feature_037` strongly negative (-1.6082 months), and `feature_009 * feature_037` negative (-0.5886 months). A three-way model confirmed the pattern with a -4.5787 month `feature_010 * feature_009 * feature_037` interaction. Stratified means showed that the large `feature_010=1` benefit was concentrated in `feature_009=1, feature_037=0` patients: 7.876 vs 3.315 months, a +4.5613 month difference. In the other three `feature_009/feature_037` strata, `feature_010=1` was slightly adverse (-0.10 to -0.17 months).

The favorable `feature_010=1` association was also modified by `feature_057`. When `feature_057=0`, `feature_010=1` was associated with +0.8242 months; when `feature_057=1`, it was associated with -0.3854 months (interaction -0.8459 months).

The `feature_041` slope was consistently positive but weaker in adverse or specific subgroups. Its slope declined as `feature_036` increased (interaction -0.01479), was weaker when `feature_001=1` (-0.01346 interaction), weaker when `feature_042=1` (-0.01398), weaker in squamous histology (-0.00983), and weaker in `feature_025="current"` patients (-0.00737).

Other interactions suggested partial attenuation of adverse main effects: the negative `feature_001` association weakened at higher `feature_036` (+0.0882 interaction), and the negative `feature_042` association weakened in squamous histology (+0.1100) and at higher `feature_036` (+0.0765). Apparent `feature_025="current"` interactions with `feature_009` or `feature_037` did not persist after the dominant interaction cluster was included.

Overall conclusion

This cohort contains a strong prognostic structure for PFS. The most stable main-effect pattern is: higher `feature_041` and `feature_056` are favorable, while higher `feature_036`, `feature_001=1`, `feature_042=1`, higher `feature_004`, squamous histology, and current status in `feature_025` are adverse. The most clinically notable heterogeneity is the sharply localized `feature_010` benefit in the `feature_009=1, feature_037=0` subgroup, with no comparable benefit elsewhere. These findings are exploratory and should be interpreted as statistical associations in the de-identified cohort rather than causal treatment effects.
