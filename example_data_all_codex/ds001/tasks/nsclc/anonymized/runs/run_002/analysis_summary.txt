Analysis summary for ds001_nsclc

I analyzed 50,000 NSCLC patient records with 73 de-identified features and one outcome, pfs_months. The mean pfs_months was 3.432 months (SD 2.233). I treated binary variables as two-group contrasts, multi-level variables as categorical contrasts, continuous variables with OLS slopes, and then followed the strongest main effects into adjusted and interaction models.

The strongest main-effect pattern was feature_036. Mean pfs_months was 4.327 for feature_036=0, 3.185 for feature_036=1, and 2.152 for feature_036=2; the level 0 vs level 2 contrast was +2.175 months (p<1e-15). This effect remained large after adjustment: feature_036=0 was +2.178 months and feature_036=1 was +1.031 months relative to feature_036=2 in the full main-effect model.

Several other main effects were large and robust. feature_001=1 was associated with markedly lower pfs_months (-1.438 months unadjusted, -1.417 months fully adjusted; p<1e-15). feature_042=1 was also adverse (-0.921 months unadjusted, -0.912 adjusted; p<1e-15). feature_015=adenocarcinoma had higher pfs_months than feature_015=squamous (+0.857 months unadjusted; +0.723 months adjusted; p<1e-15). For feature_025, current had the lowest mean pfs_months (2.987), while former and never were higher (3.607 and 3.675); never vs current was +0.687 months (p<1e-15), and former/never remained about +0.55 months in the full model.

Continuous feature_041 was one of the clearest positive predictors. Its adjusted slope was +0.168 months per unit (p<1e-15). Continuous feature_056 was also positive (+0.463 months per unit adjusted; p<1e-15). Continuous feature_004 was consistently adverse (-0.073 months per unit adjusted; p<1e-15). feature_051 had a statistically significant but very small negative slope. Additional smaller effects included feature_009=1 (+0.323 months adjusted; p<1e-15), feature_037=1 (-0.203 months adjusted; p<1e-15), and feature_029=1 (-0.110 months adjusted; p=7.59e-9).

Some apparent univariate effects did not survive adjustment. feature_028=1 was lower in the unadjusted screen (-0.190 months), but after adjustment the coefficient was only -0.009 months (p=0.243). feature_057=1 looked strongly adverse unadjusted (-0.810 months), but the full main-effect coefficient was -0.028 months (p=0.0629). This suggests its marginal association is largely explained by other covariates, although it remains important in interactions. feature_023 was essentially null in the univariate screen (+0.009 months; p=0.741).

The most clinically meaningful heterogeneity involved feature_010, feature_009, feature_037, and feature_057. feature_010=1 had a positive main association (+0.782 months), but this was concentrated in specific subgroups. The feature_010 by feature_009 interaction was strongly positive: patients with both features had mean pfs_months 5.766, compared with 3.213 for feature_010=1/feature_009=0, 3.329 for feature_010=0/feature_009=1, and 3.334 for neither; the difference-in-differences was +2.558 months (p<1e-15). In contrast, feature_037 strongly attenuated the feature_010 association: the interaction was -1.694 months (p<1e-15). feature_057 also attenuated or reversed the feature_010 association, with a -1.210 month interaction (p<1e-15).

feature_009 showed similar subgroup dependence. Its positive association was present when feature_037=0 but absent when feature_037=1, producing a -0.562 month interaction (p<1e-15). The feature_057 by feature_009 interaction was also negative (-0.488 months; p=4.12e-7). These patterns indicate that the favorable feature_009/feature_010 signal is not uniform across the cohort and is reduced in feature_037- or feature_057-positive subgroups.

Continuous-effect heterogeneity was smaller in magnitude but statistically clear. The positive feature_041 slope was weaker when feature_001=1 than when feature_001=0: +0.1650 vs +0.1776 months per unit, interaction -0.0126 (p<1e-15). feature_004 also weakened the feature_041 association in a centered continuous interaction model, with an interaction coefficient of -0.00074 (p=8.30e-6).

Overall, the dataset contains both strong main-effect structure and notable subgroup heterogeneity. The most robust favorable patterns were feature_036=0/1 vs 2, feature_010=1 in the right subgroup, feature_009=1 in selected subgroups, higher feature_041, higher feature_056, feature_015=adenocarcinoma, and non-current feature_025. The most robust adverse patterns were feature_001=1, feature_042=1, higher feature_004, feature_037=1, and feature_029=1. The largest interaction was the synergistic feature_010 plus feature_009 pattern, while feature_037 and feature_057 identified subgroups where otherwise favorable feature_010/feature_009 associations were markedly blunted.
