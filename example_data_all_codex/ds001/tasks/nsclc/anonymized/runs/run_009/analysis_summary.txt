Analysis summary for ds001_nsclc

Decoded 50,000 patient rows from dataset.parquet and analyzed pfs_months as the continuous outcome. Mean pfs_months was 3.432 months (SD 2.233). I screened 76 univariable main-effect contrasts across 73 opaque features, then followed the strongest signals with adjusted OLS models, binary-binary interactions, numeric-by-binary interactions, subgroup checks, and multiple-testing corrections.

Overall conclusion:
The cohort contains several statistically strong treatment/biomarker-like feature associations with progression-free survival despite opaque feature labels. The largest supported univariable effects were carried by feature_041, feature_036, feature_001, feature_042, feature_015, and several remained independently associated after adjustment. Interaction testing suggested that the relationship between some feature pairs and pfs_months is not purely additive, especially feature_010 with feature_009. Out of 26 recorded analyses across 25 iterations, 26 were significant at p<0.05; results with very small p-values also survived conservative Bonferroni checks. Because the features are de-identified, these results should be interpreted as statistical treatment-biomarker-outcome patterns rather than named clinical mechanisms.
