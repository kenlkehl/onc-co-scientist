Dataset ds001_nsclc contained 50000 complete patient records and outcome pfs_months (mean 3.432, SD 2.233). I treated pfs_months as a continuous outcome, using unadjusted OLS for continuous or ordinal predictors, Welch-style mean differences for binary and subgroup contrasts, and multivariable OLS for adjusted effects and interactions. Statistical significance was assessed at p<0.05 with large-sample normal/t approximations.

The strongest main-effect pattern was a positive association for feature_041: each 1-unit increase was associated with 0.170 more months of pfs_months (p<1e-250). In contrast, feature_036 showed a strong adverse ordinal gradient, with -1.099 fewer months per 1-unit increase (p<1e-250). Several binary or categorical features also showed large unadjusted differences: feature_001=1 was -1.438 months lower than feature_001=0; feature_042=1 was -0.921 months lower; feature_015="squamous" was -0.857 months lower than adenocarcinoma; and feature_025="current" was -0.634 months lower than non-current. Continuous feature_004 was adverse (-0.073 months per unit), while feature_056 was favorable (0.441 months per unit).

Feature_010, feature_009, and feature_037 formed the clearest interaction structure. Overall, feature_010=1 was associated with 0.782 higher months and feature_009=1 with 0.322 higher months, while feature_037=1 was associated with -0.204 lower months. After adjustment, feature_010 remained favorable (0.892 months, p<1e-250). The adjusted feature_010 by feature_009 interaction was strongly positive (2.534 months, p<1e-250): within feature_009=1, feature_010=1 was associated with 2.437 higher months, whereas within feature_009=0 it was -0.121 months lower. The adjusted feature_010 by feature_037 interaction was strongly negative (-1.622 months, p<1e-250): feature_010=1 was favorable in feature_037=0 (1.542 months) but unfavorable in feature_037=1 (-0.152 months). Similarly, the feature_009 by feature_037 interaction was negative (-0.584 months, p<1e-250), indicating that the feature_009 benefit was concentrated in feature_037=0.

Some unadjusted signals did not survive multivariable adjustment. The hypothesized independent adverse effect of feature_028 was not supported after adjustment (-0.006 months, p=4.404e-1). The apparent favorable unadjusted signal for feature_066 also disappeared after adjustment (-0.000 months, p=9.658e-1). Feature_057 was strongly adverse univariately (-0.810 months, p<1e-250), but its adjusted coefficient was small and not statistically significant (-0.027 months, p=8.090e-2), suggesting confounding by the stronger predictors.

Effect heterogeneity also involved feature_041. The positive feature_041 association weakened as feature_036 increased: the adjusted feature_041_z by feature_036 interaction was -0.142 months per SD per feature_036 unit (p<1e-250). The feature_041 slope was also weaker in feature_015="squamous" than in adenocarcinoma, with an adjusted interaction of -0.116 months per SD (p<1e-250).

Overall, 22 of 25 formal hypothesis tests were statistically significant. The dominant supported conclusions were: higher feature_041 and feature_056 were favorable; higher feature_036, feature_001=1, feature_042=1, squamous feature_015, current feature_025, and higher feature_004 were adverse; and the strongest multivariable interaction was a marked feature_010 benefit only in the feature_009=1 and feature_037=0 contexts. Refuted or attenuated signals included independent effects for feature_028, feature_066, and adjusted feature_057.
