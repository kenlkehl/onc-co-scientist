Dataset ds001_nsclc analysis summary

I analyzed 50,000 records with outcome pfs_months (mean 3.432 months, SD 2.233). Features are de-identified, so conclusions are association patterns rather than causal clinical claims.

Main effects:
- feature_041 was the dominant continuous association: each 1-unit increase corresponded to 0.1699 more months of pfs_months (p<1e-300), and this persisted after adjustment with coefficient 0.1684.
- feature_036 showed strong heterogeneity. feature_036=0 had 1.3797 higher months than all other values, while feature_036=2 had -1.5048 lower months. In the adjusted model, feature_036=0 remained positive (1.1467) and feature_036=2 remained negative (-1.0297).
- Binary/categorical adverse associations included feature_001=1 (-1.4375 months), feature_015=squamous (-0.8569 months), feature_042=1 (-0.9207 months), and feature_025=current (-0.6342 months). These remained directionally strong after adjustment except that feature_025=never did not remain independently beneficial.
- Favorable univariable associations included feature_010=1 (0.7815 months), feature_009=1 (0.322 months), feature_056 (0.4411 months per unit), and feature_066=1 (0.1889 months). feature_066 disappeared after adjustment (adjusted coefficient -0.0007, p=0.9491), suggesting confounding by stronger features.
- Some initially significant adverse signals also did not survive adjustment: feature_057=1 adjusted coefficient -0.0276 (p=0.06987) and feature_028=1 adjusted coefficient -0.0054 (p=0.4711). feature_051 retained a small independent negative slope (-0.000446 months per unit).

Interaction and subgroup patterns:
- The largest binary interaction was feature_010=1 with feature_009=1: the interaction coefficient was 2.5577 months (p<1e-300), indicating more favorable pfs_months when both indicators are present than expected additively.
- feature_037=1 attenuated favorable associations: feature_037=1 x feature_010=1 was -1.6941 months and feature_037=1 x feature_009=1 was -0.5622 months, both highly significant.
- feature_057=1 marked negative subgroup heterogeneity, with negative interactions for feature_025=current x feature_057=1 (-1.0453) and feature_010=1 x feature_057=1 (-1.2096).
- The feature_041 slope varied by subgroup: it was stronger in feature_036=0 (interaction 0.0144), weaker in feature_036=2 (-0.0276), weaker in feature_015=squamous (-0.0118), and weaker in feature_001=1 (-0.0126).

Refuted or weak hypotheses:
- feature_021 showed a small negative slope but was not significant (-0.001697 months per unit, p=0.1123).
- feature_048=1 and feature_050 were weak/non-significant in univariable screening (p=0.1387 and p=0.1571, respectively).

Overall conclusion: pfs_months in this cohort is strongly structured by a small set of opaque features, especially feature_041, feature_036, feature_001, feature_015, feature_025=current, feature_042, feature_010, feature_056, feature_004, and feature_009. Several apparent univariable signals are explained by these dominant features, while interactions involving feature_010, feature_009, feature_037, feature_057, feature_036, feature_015, and feature_001 show meaningful subgroup heterogeneity.
