Dataset ds001_nsclc: 50000 NSCLC patient records with pfs_months as the continuous outcome. I ran 25 propose-test-refine iterations using adjusted ordinary least-squares models and targeted subgroup mean comparisons. Adjusted models included demographics, stage, brain metastases, albumin, LDH, weight loss, CRP, NLR, ECOG performance status, histology, smoking, treatments, and key biomarkers unless otherwise noted.

The strongest general prognostic signals were clinical status and disease burden. Older age was unexpectedly associated with longer PFS: 1.684 months per 10-year higher age_years (p=0.000). In contrast, stage IV disease (-1.417 months, p=0.000), ECOG 2 versus 0 (-2.177 months, p=0.000), brain metastases (-0.912 months, p=0.000), squamous histology (-0.723 months, p=0.000), current versus never smoking (-0.545 months, p=0.000), and sex_female (-0.203 months, p=0.000) were all independently associated with shorter PFS.

Nutritional and tumor-burden laboratory features also tracked PFS. Each 1 percentage-point higher weight_loss_pct_6mo had a -0.073 month coefficient for PFS (p=0.000), each 1 g/dL higher albumin_g_dl had a 0.463 month coefficient (p=0.000), and each 100 U/L higher ldh_u_l had a -0.045 month coefficient (p=0.000).

The clearest treatment signal was biomarker-specific sotorasib benefit. The adjusted sotorasib main effect was 0.323 months (p=0.000), and the treatment_sotorasib by kras_g12c interaction was large and positive: 2.533 months (p=0.000). In the KRAS G12C subgroup, mean PFS was 2.552 months higher with sotorasib (p=0.000), while the KRAS G12C-negative subgroup showed no benefit (-0.005 months, p=0.799). Additional sotorasib interactions were negative for egfr_mutation (-0.208 months, p=0.000), alk_fusion (-0.343 months, p=0.000), and brca2_mutation (-0.328 months, p=0.000), with a smaller positive interaction for tmb_high (0.076 months, p=5.40e-7).

Several plausible biomarker and treatment hypotheses were not supported. stk11_mutation was only weakly adverse (-0.019 months, p=0.037), and brca2_mutation was independently adverse (-0.109 months, p=8.16e-9). However, pembrolizumab had no positive adjusted association (-0.006 months, p=0.383), pdl1_tps had no meaningful main effect (0.003 months per 0.50 higher value, p=0.668), and the pembrolizumab by pdl1_tps interaction was null (0.004 months, p=0.781). Similarly, there was no evidence of biomarker-concordant benefit for osimertinib in egfr_mutation (-0.020 interaction months, p=0.335) or olaparib in brca2_mutation (-0.004 interaction months, p=0.930).

Overall, this cohort showed strong prognostic structure and one dominant predictive treatment interaction: sotorasib benefit was concentrated in KRAS G12C-positive disease. The evidence did not support the expected pembrolizumab-PD-L1, osimertinib-EGFR, or olaparib-BRCA2 benefit patterns in this dataset. The age and sex findings are notable because their directions are not the usual clinical expectation, so they should be treated as dataset-specific associations rather than causal conclusions.
