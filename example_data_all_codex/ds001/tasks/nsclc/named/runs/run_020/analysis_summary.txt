Dataset ds001_nsclc contains 50,000 NSCLC records with `pfs_months` as the outcome. I analyzed the local `dataset.parquet` file using multivariable OLS models. Core adjusted models included age, sex, ECOG performance status, stage IV, brain metastases, histology, smoking status, albumin, LDH, weight loss, CRP, NLR, liver and bone metastases, major biomarkers, and treatments. Interaction models added the specific treatment-biomarker or clinical interaction being tested.

The dominant prognostic clinical signals were strong. Higher `ecog_ps` was associated with lower PFS by -1.101 months per ECOG point (p<1e-300). `stage_iv` was associated with -1.417 months (p<1e-300), `has_brain_mets` with -0.912 months (p<1e-300), and squamous histology with -0.723 months versus adenocarcinoma (p<1e-300). Current smoking was associated with -0.551 months versus former smoking (p<1e-300), while never smoking did not differ from former smoking (effect -0.006, p=0.595). Female sex was associated with a smaller but significant reduction in PFS (-0.203 months, p=2.85e-212).

Several continuous clinical/lab variables were also supported. Higher `albumin_g_dl` was favorable (+0.463 months per 1 g/dL, p<1e-300). Higher `weight_loss_pct_6mo` was unfavorable (-0.366 months per 5 percentage points, p<1e-300), and higher `ldh_u_l` was modestly unfavorable (-0.0446 months per 100 U/L, p=1.50e-28). In contrast, `crp_mg_l` and `nlr` were not independently associated with PFS after adjustment (CRP effect +0.002 months per 10 mg/L, p=0.559; NLR effect -0.00009 months per unit, p=0.955).

Metastatic-site effects were heterogeneous. Brain metastases were strongly adverse, but `liver_mets` and `bone_mets` were not independently significant after adjustment (`liver_mets` -0.0049 months, p=0.592; `bone_mets` -0.0075 months, p=0.356). The adverse brain-metastasis effect was slightly attenuated in stage IV disease, with a positive `stage_iv * has_brain_mets` interaction (+0.0976 months, p=4.79e-10). The ECOG penalty was also attenuated in stage IV disease (`stage_iv * ecog_ps` +0.105 months per ECOG point, p=7.69e-26).

The clearest treatment-biomarker relationship was `treatment_sotorasib` with `kras_g12c`. In a main-effects model, both `kras_g12c` (+0.892 months, p<1e-300) and `treatment_sotorasib` (+0.323 months, p<1e-300) appeared favorable. After adding the interaction, the `treatment_sotorasib * kras_g12c` coefficient was +2.535 months (p<1e-300). In the KRAS G12C-positive subgroup, sotorasib was associated with +2.532 months (p<1e-300); in KRAS G12C-negative patients, the effect was essentially absent (+0.001 months, p=0.744). Sotorasib association was attenuated in several other biomarker strata, including EGFR-mutated (-0.189 interaction, p=9.28e-21), ALK-fusion-positive (-0.327, p=3.72e-25), BRCA2-mutated (-0.331, p=7.29e-17), and STK11-mutated (-0.0596, p=0.00181) patients, while it was slightly larger in TMB-high disease (+0.0587, p=0.000107).

Other biomarker-treatment hypotheses were not supported. `treatment_pembrolizumab` had no overall adjusted association (-0.0056 months, p=0.387), and its association was not detectably modified by PD-L1 TPS (+0.627 months per 50 TPS points, p=0.690), TMB-high status (-0.0064, p=0.662), or STK11 mutation (-0.0091, p=0.625). `treatment_olaparib` had no overall association (+0.0024 months, p=0.732), and there was no BRCA2-specific olaparib signal (`treatment_olaparib * brca2_mutation` +0.00031 months, p=0.994). `treatment_osimertinib` had no overall association (+0.0012 months, p=0.867), no EGFR-specific signal (`treatment_osimertinib * egfr_mutation` -0.0179, p=0.407), and only a non-significant suggestion of stronger association in patients with brain metastases (+0.0299, p=0.074).

Among biomarker main effects, `brca2_mutation` was associated with slightly shorter PFS (-0.110 months, p=6.02e-9), and `stk11_mutation` with a very small adverse difference (-0.019 months, p=0.036). `egfr_mutation` and `tmb_high` had no independent main-effect association with PFS after adjustment. A small clinical interaction suggested the adverse squamous histology association was attenuated among current smokers (`histology_squamous * smoking_current` +0.0517 months, p=0.000817). The albumin-by-weight-loss interaction was not significant (-0.0097, p=0.246).

Overall, this cohort shows a strong clinical severity/prognostic structure, with ECOG status, stage IV disease, brain metastases, histology, smoking, albumin, LDH, and weight loss accounting for large PFS differences. The only large and clinically meaningful treatment-biomarker pattern was sotorasib benefit concentrated in KRAS G12C-positive patients. Pembrolizumab, olaparib, and osimertinib did not show overall or expected biomarker-defined PFS advantages in these analyses.
