Dataset ds001_nsclc: 50000 NSCLC records; mean pfs_months 3.43 (SD 2.23).

I ran 25 propose-test-refine iterations using linear models for pfs_months. Baseline-adjusted models controlled for age_years, sex_female, stage_iv, ecog_ps, has_brain_mets, albumin_g_dl, ldh_u_l, weight_loss_pct_6mo, crp_mg_l, nlr, smoking_status, and histology unless otherwise noted. P-values use large-sample normal approximations to the OLS coefficient tests.

General disease-status effects were partly concentrated in a few variables. Higher albumin_g_dl was strongly favorable, while higher weight_loss_pct_6mo and ldh_u_l were adverse. Stage IV disease and brain metastases had large negative adjusted associations. In contrast, crp_mg_l, nlr, liver_mets, bone_mets, pleural_effusion, adrenal_mets, and most other metastatic-site flags were weak or not significant after adjustment.

The clearest treatment-biomarker pattern was treatment_sotorasib by kras_g12c. The interaction was large and strongly positive in both focused and joint models. The other matched-treatment hypotheses were refuted: treatment_osimertinib by egfr_mutation, treatment_olaparib by brca2_mutation, and treatment_pembrolizumab by tmb_high were near zero and not significant.

Immunotherapy-specific hypotheses were mostly unsupported. Pembrolizumab interactions with tmb_high, pdl1_tps, stk11_mutation, egfr_mutation, liver_mets, histology_squamous, and current smoking were all small and not significant. The main exception was a small negative treatment_pembrolizumab by kras_g12c interaction, about -0.053 months, which was statistically significant but much smaller than the sotorasib by KRAS G12C effect.

Exploratory scans reinforced that most interaction signals were small. The top pembrolizumab scan terms were under 0.10 months in absolute value. The osimertinib scan did not identify egfr_mutation as a dominant interaction; the largest scanned osimertinib interactions were also small, with only pleural_effusion reaching nominal significance.

Overall conclusion: the dominant supported signals are a large positive sotorasib benefit in kras_g12c patients and strong baseline prognostic effects from albumin, weight loss, LDH, stage IV disease, and brain metastases. Many plausible NSCLC treatment-biomarker hypotheses were directly tested and refuted in this dataset.

Selected effect estimates:
- sotorasib x kras_g12c: 2.535 months, p=<1e-300
- osimertinib x egfr_mutation: -0.022 months, p=0.2120
- olaparib x brca2_mutation: -0.035 months, p=0.3280
- pembrolizumab x tmb_high: -0.001 months, p=0.9581
- pembrolizumab x stk11_mutation: -0.003 months, p=0.8644
- albumin_g_dl per 1 g/dL: 0.454 months, p=<1e-300
- weight_loss_pct_6mo per 5 points: -0.365 months, p=<1e-300
- stage_iv: -1.418 months, p=<1e-300
- has_brain_mets: -0.917 months, p=<1e-300
