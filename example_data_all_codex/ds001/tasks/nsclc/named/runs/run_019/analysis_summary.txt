Dataset ds001_nsclc was analyzed with 50,000 records and outcome pfs_months. Effects below are signed differences or OLS coefficients in months unless otherwise stated; p-values are two-sided large-sample tests from the executed models.

The strongest baseline prognostic findings were adverse disease burden and performance status. In the adjusted clinical model, stage_iv was associated with substantially shorter PFS (beta -1.418, p <1e-250), as were has_brain_mets (beta -0.917, p <1e-250) and each additional ECOG point (beta -1.103, p <1e-250). Higher albumin was favorable (beta 0.454 per g/dL, p <1e-250), while weight loss and LDH were unfavorable (weight loss beta -0.073 per percentage point; LDH beta -0.041 per 100 U/L; both highly significant).

Demographic and disease-subtype effects were also present. Older age was positively associated with PFS (beta 0.169 per year, p <1e-250), female sex was associated with lower PFS (beta -0.205, p <1e-250), squamous histology was lower than adenocarcinoma (beta -0.719, p <1e-250), and current smoking was lower than former smoking (beta -0.483, p <1e-250).

Several initially plausible adverse markers did not add independent signal once the major clinical factors were in the model. Liver metastases, bone metastases, and pleural effusion had coefficients near zero with p-values 0.569, 0.893, and 0.617, respectively. CRP and NLR were also null after adjustment (CRP beta 1.500e-4, p 0.702; NLR beta -9.408e-4, p 0.583).

The dominant treatment-biomarker pattern was sotorasib in KRAS G12C disease. Before interaction modeling, kras_g12c had a large positive adjusted main association (beta 0.893, p <1e-250). Explicit interaction testing showed that this was treatment-specific: treatment_sotorasib by kras_g12c was + 2.535 months (p <1e-250). In subgroup means, sotorasib was associated with +2.552 months among KRAS G12C-positive patients but -0.005 months among KRAS G12C-negative patients.

STK11 modified the sotorasib-KRAS pattern modestly but significantly. The treatment_sotorasib by kras_g12c by stk11_mutation coefficient was -0.173 months (p 4.862e-4), indicating attenuation rather than reversal of the KRAS-positive sotorasib benefit.

Other canonical treatment-biomarker hypotheses were not supported in this cohort. Pembrolizumab did not show stronger association with PFS at higher pdl1_tps (interaction beta 2.090e-5 per 0.10 PD-L1, p 0.995) or in tmb_high patients (interaction beta -0.014, p 0.385). EGFR- and ALK-driven subgroups did not show pembrolizumab resistance by interaction testing. Osimertinib by egfr_mutation was null (beta -0.017, p 0.471), and olaparib by brca2_mutation was null (beta -0.004, p 0.926), although brca2_mutation itself was associated with shorter PFS in that model (beta -0.111, p 5.656e-6).

Additional subgroup probes were largely negative. ALK fusion was not independently associated with shorter PFS after adjustment (beta -0.023, p 0.162). Autoimmune disease did not materially change the pembrolizumab association (interaction beta 0.027, p 0.422). Squamous histology did not modify sotorasib benefit after KRAS and STK11 terms were included (interaction beta 0.0012, p 0.926).

The final combined model preserved the same conclusion: the sotorasib-KRAS interaction remained very large and positive (beta 2.557, p <1e-250), STK11 attenuation remained significant (beta -0.172, p 5.127e-4), and the pembrolizumab-PD-L1, pembrolizumab-TMB, osimertinib-EGFR, and olaparib-BRCA2 interaction terms remained small and non-significant.
