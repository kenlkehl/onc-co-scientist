Oncology dataset analysis summary for ds001_nsclc

I analyzed 50000 NSCLC patient records with no missing values in the decoded Parquet file. The outcome was pfs_months, modeled as a continuous endpoint. Analyses used unadjusted mean comparisons for direct subgroup contrasts and ordinary least-squares models for multivariable main-effect and interaction tests; signed estimates are in months of progression-free survival.

Iteration narrative

- Iteration 1 tested unadjusted treatment main effects. Sotorasib showed a clear positive association; pembrolizumab, olaparib, and osimertinib did not show significant unselected benefit.
- Iteration 2 evaluated immunotherapy-adjacent biomarkers. Neither PD-L1 TPS nor TMB-high was significant in the adjusted model.
- Iteration 3 refined immunotherapy hypotheses. Neither pembrolizumab-by-PD-L1 nor pembrolizumab-by-TMB showed significant positive modification.
- Iteration 4 tested STK11 mutation as an adverse marker and pembrolizumab modifier; neither effect was significant in the adjusted model.
- Iteration 5 identified a strong KRAS G12C-specific sotorasib benefit.
- Iteration 6 tested whether STK11 modified sotorasib benefit within KRAS G12C disease.
- Iteration 7 tested EGFR-specific osimertinib benefit; neither the adjusted interaction nor the EGFR-mutant subgroup comparison supported benefit.
- Iteration 8 tested brain metastases as an osimertinib modifier within EGFR-mutant disease; there was no significant interaction.
- Iteration 9 tested BRCA2-specific olaparib benefit; neither the adjusted interaction nor BRCA2-mutant subgroup comparison supported benefit.
- Iteration 10 screened ALK and ROS1 fusions; neither reached conventional significance in the adjusted model.
- Iteration 11 screened MET exon14 skipping and HER2 amplification; neither showed a significant adjusted association.
- Iteration 12 screened RET fusion and EGFR mutation main effects; neither was independently significant in this adjusted specification.
- Iteration 13 confirmed older age as favorable, female sex as adverse, and ECOG performance status and stage IV disease as strongly adverse clinical factors.
- Iteration 14 found brain metastases were strongly adverse, while liver metastases were not significant after adjustment.
- Iteration 15 found recent weight loss was strongly adverse, while bone metastases were not significant after adjustment.
- Iteration 16 confirmed albumin was favorable while LDH was adverse.
- Iteration 17 tested CRP and NLR; neither was significant after adjustment.
- Iteration 18 found squamous histology and current smoking were adverse.
- Iteration 19 tested hemoglobin and alkaline phosphatase; neither was significant after adjustment.
- Iteration 20 tested sodium and calcium; neither was significant after adjustment.
- Iteration 21 tested COPD and chronic kidney disease; neither was significant after adjustment.
- Iteration 22 tested autoimmune disease as an immunotherapy modifier; the interaction was small and not convincingly significant.
- Iteration 23 found heart failure was adverse, while interstitial lung disease history was not significant.
- Iteration 24 retested the pembrolizumab-by-PD-L1 pattern with a median split; the interaction was not significant.
- Iteration 25 pooled biomarker-matched targeted therapy across EGFR, KRAS G12C, and BRCA2-positive patients; the matched indicator was strongly positive, largely consistent with the KRAS G12C/sotorasib signal.

Overall conclusion

The clearest treatment-biomarker pattern was KRAS G12C-specific sotorasib benefit: the adjusted interaction and the KRAS G12C subgroup comparison were both large, positive, and highly significant. The broader matched targeted-therapy indicator was also strongly positive, but the individual EGFR/osimertinib and BRCA2/olaparib analyses were not supported, so the pooled targeted-therapy result should be interpreted as mainly reflecting the KRAS G12C/sotorasib signal in this cohort. Pembrolizumab did not show significant unselected benefit or significant positive modification by PD-L1 TPS, TMB-high status, STK11 mutation, autoimmune disease, or above-median PD-L1.

The strongest prognostic patterns were clinical rather than broad targeted-therapy effects: older age was associated with longer PFS, female sex with shorter PFS, and higher ECOG performance status, stage IV disease, brain metastases, weight loss, squamous histology, current smoking, low albumin, high LDH, and heart failure were adverse. Several plausible markers were refuted or weak in adjusted models, including liver metastases, bone metastases, CRP, NLR, hemoglobin, alkaline phosphatase, sodium, calcium, COPD, chronic kidney disease, interstitial lung disease history, most rare driver alterations, and the osimertinib and olaparib biomarker interactions.
