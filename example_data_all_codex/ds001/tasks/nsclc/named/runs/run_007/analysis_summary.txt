Dataset ds001_nsclc contains 50,000 NSCLC patient records with no missing values. I analyzed pfs_months as a continuous outcome using multivariable OLS models. Core models adjusted for demographics, smoking, ECOG performance status, histology, stage, brain metastases, biomarkers, treatments, labs, comorbidities, metastatic sites, and effusions. Interaction models added the specific product terms being tested. Effect estimates are signed differences in pfs_months on the natural month scale.

The strongest adjusted prognostic patterns were age, ECOG performance status, stage IV disease, brain metastases, histology, smoking, and nutritional/tumor burden markers. Each 10-year increase in age_years was associated with higher PFS (+1.683 months, p<1e-300), while each 1-point increase in ecog_ps was strongly adverse (-1.104 months, p<1e-300). Stage IV disease (-1.435 months, p<1e-300) and brain metastases (-0.973 months, p<1e-300) were both strongly adverse. Their interaction was statistically positive (+0.080 months, p=7.72e-10), so their joint effect was slightly less adverse than a purely additive model would predict.

Histology and smoking mattered. Squamous histology was associated with lower PFS than adenocarcinoma (-0.731 months, p<1e-300). Current smoking was independently adverse versus never smoking (-0.551 months, p<1e-300), while former smoking was not different from never smoking (+0.002 months, p=0.852). Female sex had a negative main association in the primary adjusted model (-0.205 months, p<1e-300). Interaction checks showed a slightly smaller age-associated PFS increase in female patients (sex_female by age, -0.022 months per decade, p=4.56e-05) and a larger squamous-histology penalty in female patients (-0.091 months, p=3.77e-14).

Nutritional and burden markers were more informative than inflammatory indices after adjustment. Albumin was favorable (+0.460 months per g/dL, p<1e-300), weight loss was adverse (-0.730 months per 10 percentage points, p<1e-300), and LDH was modestly adverse (-0.045 months per 100 U/L, p<1e-300). CRP and NLR did not show independent negative associations after adjustment (CRP -0.002 months per 10 mg/L, p=0.419; NLR +0.0005 months, p=0.700). The albumin by weight-loss interaction was not significant (-0.014 months, p=0.294), suggesting largely additive rather than heterogeneous effects.

The dominant treatment-biomarker finding was sotorasib in KRAS G12C disease. The treatment_sotorasib by kras_g12c interaction was large and positive (+2.536 months, p<1e-300). In contrast, sotorasib had no benefit in KRAS G12C-negative patients (-0.002 months, p=0.768), and KRAS G12C itself was not independently favorable after modeling the interaction (+0.005 months, p=0.633). STK11 did not robustly blunt sotorasib benefit once the KRAS interaction was included (-0.024 months, p=0.133), and the KRAS by STK11 interaction was negative but not significant in the broader interaction model (-0.040 months, p=0.080).

Other expected treatment-biomarker pairings were not supported. Pembrolizumab had no adjusted main association with PFS (+0.0003 months, p=0.976), and neither PD-L1 TPS nor TMB-high status modified pembrolizumab benefit (pembrolizumab by pdl1_tps per 10 percentage points -0.0005 months, p=0.837; pembrolizumab by tmb_high -0.002 months, p=0.848). Olaparib had no main effect (+0.005 months, p=0.427) and no positive BRCA2 interaction (-0.032 months, p=0.365), although brca2_mutation itself was independently adverse (-0.099 months, p=8.58e-08). Osimertinib had no main effect (+0.001 months, p=0.818) and no positive EGFR interaction (-0.018 months, p=0.287). A possible osimertinib by brain metastases interaction was positive but did not meet p<0.05 (+0.026 months, p=0.060).

Among other genomic features, alk_fusion had a small negative adjusted association (-0.028 months, p=0.027). HER2 amplification, MET exon 14 skipping, RET fusion, and ROS1 fusion were not significantly associated with PFS in these adjusted models.

Most comorbidities, metastatic sites, effusions, hematologic markers, hepatic markers, renal markers, and vital/metabolic measures added little independent signal after adjustment. Heart failure was statistically adverse but tiny in magnitude (-0.026 months, p=0.0039). Diabetes, COPD, liver metastases, bone metastases, adrenal metastases, pericardial effusion, contralateral lung metastases, hemoglobin, platelets, ALC, alkaline phosphatase, AST, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, BMI, glucose, and oxygen saturation were not significant in the hypothesized directions. Pleural effusion had a very small positive adjusted coefficient (+0.018 months, p=0.015), opposite the hypothesized adverse direction and not clinically meaningful in magnitude.

Overall, the cohort shows strong prognostic structure around age, performance status, stage, brain metastases, squamous histology, current smoking, albumin, LDH, and weight loss. The only large treatment-biomarker interaction detected was sotorasib benefit in KRAS G12C-positive patients. Pembrolizumab-PD-L1/TMB, olaparib-BRCA2, and osimertinib-EGFR interaction hypotheses were not supported in this dataset.
