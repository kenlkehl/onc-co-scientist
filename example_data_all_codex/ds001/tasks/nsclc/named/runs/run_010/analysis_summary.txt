Dataset ds001_nsclc contains 50,000 NSCLC patient records with pfs_months as the continuous outcome. I evaluated 25 propose-test-refine iterations using adjusted ordinary-least-squares models and targeted interaction terms. Effect estimates are signed differences in pfs_months on the natural month scale; p < 0.05 was treated as statistically significant.

Iterations 1-3 focused on core clinical severity. A 10-year increase in age_years was unexpectedly associated with higher adjusted pfs_months (+1.683 months, p<1e-300), while each 1-point increase in ecog_ps was strongly adverse (-1.104 months, p<1e-300). Stage IV disease and brain metastases were both strongly adverse, with stage_iv associated with -1.435 months (p<1e-300) and has_brain_mets with -0.973 months (p<1e-300). The stage_iv by has_brain_mets interaction was statistically significant but positive (+0.080 months, p=7.72e-10), meaning the joint adverse effect was slightly less than additive, opposite the hypothesized more-than-additive harm.

Iterations 4-6 tested histology, smoking, nutritional status, tumor burden, and inflammatory markers. Squamous histology was independently adverse (-0.731 months, p<1e-300), as was current smoking versus never smoking (-0.551 months, p<1e-300), while former smoking was essentially null (+0.002 months, p=0.852). Albumin_g_dl was strongly favorable (+0.460 months per g/dL, p<1e-300), weight_loss_pct_6mo was strongly adverse (-0.730 months per 10 percentage points, p<1e-300), and ldh_u_l was modestly adverse (-0.045 months per 100 U/L, p<1e-300). CRP and NLR did not retain independent adverse associations after adjustment.

Iterations 7-12 examined major treatment-biomarker hypotheses. Pembrolizumab showed no adjusted main association with pfs_months (+0.0003 months, p=0.976), and neither pdl1_tps nor tmb_high positively modified pembrolizumab effect. The clearest supported treatment signal was treatment_sotorasib by kras_g12c: the interaction was large and positive (+2.536 months, p<1e-300). Among KRAS G12C-negative patients, sotorasib had no benefit (-0.002 months, p=0.768), and kras_g12c itself was not independently favorable once the interaction was modeled. STK11 did not significantly blunt sotorasib benefit, and the kras_g12c by stk11_mutation interaction was negative but not significant. Olaparib had no main benefit and no positive brca2_mutation interaction, although brca2_mutation itself was independently adverse (-0.099 months, p=8.58e-08). Osimertinib had no main benefit and no positive egfr_mutation interaction; its brain-metastasis interaction was positive but narrowly nonsignificant (+0.026 months, p=0.060).

Iterations 13-16 expanded genomic and demographic heterogeneity. ALK fusion had a very small negative adjusted association (-0.028 months, p=0.027), while HER2 amplification, MET exon14 skipping, RET fusion, and ROS1 fusion were not significant. Female sex was associated with lower adjusted PFS (-0.205 months, p<1e-300). The age-associated PFS increase was slightly smaller in female patients (-0.022 months per decade interaction, p=4.56e-05), and the squamous-histology penalty was larger in female patients (-0.091 months, p=3.77e-14). The age by ECOG interaction was negative (-0.145 months, p<1e-300), showing attenuation of the favorable age association at worse performance status. The stage_iv by ECOG interaction was positive (+0.096 months, p<1e-300), again opposite a more-than-additive harm hypothesis.

Iterations 17-23 tested comorbidities, metastatic sites, effusions, hematologic indices, liver and renal labs, and vital/metabolic features. Heart failure was statistically adverse but very small in magnitude (-0.026 months, p=0.0039). Diabetes mellitus and COPD were not significant. Liver, bone, adrenal, pericardial, and contralateral lung metastatic/effusion features were not independently adverse after adjustment; pleural_effusion had a small positive coefficient (+0.018 months, p=0.015), opposite the hypothesized direction and not clinically meaningful. Hemoglobin, platelets, ALC, alkaline phosphatase, AST, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, BMI, glucose, and oxygen saturation did not show significant effects in the hypothesized directions.

Iteration 24 tested two refinement hypotheses. The albumin by weight-loss interaction was negative but not significant (-0.014 months, p=0.294), supporting largely additive rather than heterogeneous effects. The pembrolizumab by autoimmune_disease interaction was positive and nonsignificant (+0.029 months, p=0.346), refuting the hypothesis that autoimmune disease identified a subgroup with less pembrolizumab-associated PFS.

Iteration 25 fit a final treatment-biomarker interaction comparison. It again identified treatment_sotorasib by kras_g12c as the dominant positive treatment interaction (+2.536 months, p<1e-300). In the same final model, pembrolizumab by pdl1_tps, olaparib by brca2_mutation, and osimertinib by egfr_mutation were not positive and not statistically significant.

Overall, this cohort shows strong prognostic structure around age, ECOG performance status, stage IV disease, brain metastases, squamous histology, current smoking, female sex, albumin, LDH, and weight loss. The major predictive treatment-biomarker finding is large KRAS G12C-specific sotorasib benefit. The expected pembrolizumab-PD-L1/TMB, olaparib-BRCA2, and osimertinib-EGFR patterns were directly tested and not supported. Many plausible comorbidity, metastatic-site, hematologic, renal, hepatic, inflammatory, and vital-sign hypotheses were null after adjustment, with several estimates statistically small enough to be unlikely to matter clinically even when nominally significant.
