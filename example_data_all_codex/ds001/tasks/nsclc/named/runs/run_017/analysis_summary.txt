Dataset ds001_nsclc contains 50,000 NSCLC patient records with no missing values and `pfs_months` as the continuous outcome.
Mean PFS was 3.432 months, with observed values from 0.000 to 15.33 months. I used OLS models for adjusted signed effects on the natural month scale, plus Welch mean-difference checks for selected subgroups.

The clearest baseline prognostic signals were adverse stage, brain metastases, performance status, nutrition, LDH, and cachexia. In adjusted models, `stage_iv` and `has_brain_mets` were associated with shorter PFS, `ecog_ps` worsened PFS per point, higher `albumin_g_dl` improved PFS, and higher `ldh_u_l` and `weight_loss_pct_6mo` were adverse. When additional metastatic-site terms were added, `liver_mets`, `bone_mets`, `pleural_effusion`, and `contralateral_lung_mets` did not remain statistically significant beyond the core stage/brain-metastasis/performance-status model.

Treatment-biomarker heterogeneity was concentrated in the sotorasib/KRAS pathway. `treatment_sotorasib` showed a large positive interaction with `kras_g12c` (+2.535 months, p<1e-300), and `stk11_mutation` modestly attenuated sotorasib-associated PFS (-0.059 months, p=0.00211). By contrast, pembrolizumab interactions with `pdl1_tps`, `tmb_high`, and `stk11_mutation` were not significant; olaparib did not show a BRCA2-specific PFS benefit; and osimertinib did not show an EGFR-specific PFS interaction in this dataset.

Several biologically plausible negative-control or off-target hypotheses were not supported. Osimertinib did not show a meaningful ALK-fusion-specific benefit. Exploratory off-target interactions of sotorasib with `met_exon14_skipping`, olaparib with `her2_amplification`, and osimertinib with `ros1_fusion` were also not significant. The osimertinib by `has_brain_mets` interaction was small, positive, and nominally significant, which was opposite the hypothesized smaller-benefit direction.

Broader laboratory and comorbidity checks were mostly null after adjustment: hemoglobin, alkaline phosphatase, creatinine, sodium, oxygen saturation, heart rate, COPD, interstitial lung disease history, and the PSA/sex artifact check were not significant. `heart_failure` was associated with a small but statistically significant decrement in PFS (-0.029 months, p=0.00814).

Overall, the cohort shows very strong general disease-severity prognostic effects and one dominant treatment-biomarker interaction: sotorasib benefit in KRAS G12C-positive disease, with small STK11 attenuation. Many other clinically intuitive targeted-therapy hypotheses were refuted in these adjusted tests. These are observational associations from EHR-derived data, so they should be interpreted as adjusted statistical patterns rather than causal treatment effects.
