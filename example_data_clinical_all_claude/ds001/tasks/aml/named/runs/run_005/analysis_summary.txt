Analysis summary for ds001_aml (50,000-patient AML cohort, single binary outcome objective_response)
================================================================================

Overall outcome rate: 18.75% objective response.

OVERVIEW
--------
We performed a 25-iteration propose-analyze-refine exploration of patient features, treatments, and the binary outcome objective_response. We screened (1) univariate marginal associations of every feature and treatment with response, (2) a multivariable logistic regression including all features and treatments, (3) treatment-by-feature interactions for each of the six treatments against every binary feature and every standardized continuous feature, (4) exhaustive 1-, 2-, and 3-binary-feature stratified treatment-effect subgroups for each treatment, and (5) joint logistic models with main effects and interactions to verify the structure of the leading findings.

ITERATIONS 1-3 — UNIVARIATE PROGNOSTIC FEATURES
-----------------------------------------------
- npm1_mutation: response 23.5% vs 16.7% (diff +6.7 pp, p=5.8e-70). Strongly positive.
- unfit_for_intensive: response 22.1% vs 16.5% (diff +5.6 pp, p=1.9e-55). Counterintuitive direction at face value, hinting at treatment confounding (resolved later).
- complex_karyotype: response 16.8% vs 19.1% (diff -2.3 pp, p=3.3e-6). Negative.
- tp53_mutation: response 16.6% vs 19.0% (diff -2.4 pp, p=3.5e-5). Negative.
- ECOG performance status: mean 0.67 in responders vs 0.83 in non-responders (diff -0.157, p=2.3e-95). Worse PS strongly tracks lower response.
- weight_loss_pct_6mo, blast_pct_marrow, crp_mg_l, wbc_k_per_ul: all higher in non-responders (p ranging 1e-4 to 1e-33).
- albumin_g_dl: slightly higher in responders (+0.029 g/dL, p=2.7e-7).
- age_years, sex_female, secondary_aml, flt3_itd, flt3_tkd, idh1, idh2: no marginal associations with response (all p>0.4).

ITERATION 4 — TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS
------------------------------------
Only one treatment had a meaningful unadjusted main effect on response:
- treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine: 22.0% vs 16.6% off (+5.4 pp, p=6.6e-51).
The five remaining treatments (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, 7+3) all had |response difference| < 1 pp and p > 0.13.

ITERATION 5 — MULTIVARIABLE LOGISTIC REGRESSION
-----------------------------------------------
After adjusting for all 35 features and treatments simultaneously, the strongest predictors (sorted by p) were ecog_ps (β=-0.36), npm1_mutation (β=+0.44), unfit_for_intensive (β=+0.37), treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine (β=+0.36), weight_loss_pct_6mo (β=-0.037), blast_pct_marrow (β=-0.005), albumin_g_dl (β=+0.12), complex_karyotype (β=-0.17), tp53_mutation (β=-0.19). All other treatment coefficients were near zero (|β|<0.05) and non-significant (p>0.1) — consistent with no main effect on response.

ITERATIONS 6-8 — TARGETED-AGENT × TARGET-MUTATION INTERACTIONS (REFUTED HYPOTHESES)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The canonical clinical hypotheses for the four targeted agents were tested:
- treatment_midostaurin × flt3_itd: within FLT3-ITD+ patients (n=10,000), response 17.6% on midostaurin vs 19.1% off (diff -1.5 pp, p=0.19). Interaction coefficient -0.106, p=0.20. NOT supported; trend opposite to expectation.
- treatment_gilteritinib × flt3_itd: within FLT3-ITD+ patients, response 19.1% on gilteritinib vs 18.9% off (diff +0.2 pp, p=0.86). Interaction coefficient +0.079, p=0.32. NOT supported.
- treatment_ivosidenib × idh1_mutation: within IDH1+ patients (n=3624), response 15.4% on ivosidenib vs 19.4% off (diff -4.0 pp, p=0.11). Interaction coefficient -0.304, p=0.077. Trend toward harm; NOT supported.
- treatment_enasidenib × idh2_mutation: within IDH2+ patients (n=4679), response 19.5% on enasidenib vs 19.1% off (diff +0.4 pp, p=0.92). NOT supported.

A broader screen of every targeted-agent × every binary feature (36 tests) found no interaction with p<0.001. We conclude that none of the four targeted agents in this dataset produces a clinically meaningful improvement in objective_response in its canonical biomarker subgroup.

ITERATIONS 9-11 — VEN/AZA INTERACTIONS WITH NPM1, FITNESS, TP53, AND COMPLEX KARYOTYPE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment-by-feature interactions for venetoclax_azacitidine:
- tx × npm1_mutation: β=+1.055, p=5.5e-102. Strongly positive.
- tx × unfit_for_intensive: β=+0.883, p=1.1e-78. Strongly positive.
- tx × tp53_mutation: β=-0.409, p=4.7e-7. Negative (suppressor).
- tx × complex_karyotype: β=-0.306, p=6.0e-6. Negative (suppressor).

Within tp53+ patients alone, ven/aza response is 16.4% vs 16.7% off — the entire benefit is lost in the presence of tp53 mutation. Similarly, within complex_karyotype+ patients, ven/aza adds only +1.2 pp.

ITERATIONS 12-14 — JOINT 3-WAY INTERACTION CONFIRMS THE SUBGROUP STRUCTURE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
A 2×2 stratification by unfit × npm1 reveals:
- unfit=0, npm1=0: ven/aza diff -1.0 pp (n=20,916).
- unfit=0, npm1=1: ven/aza diff -0.3 pp (n=9,028).
- unfit=1, npm1=0: ven/aza diff +0.5 pp (n=14,070).
- unfit=1, npm1=1: ven/aza diff +47.4 pp (63.3% vs 16.0%, p=6.7e-310, n=5,986).

A logistic model with all four interaction terms plus the 3-way tx*unfit*npm1 interaction yields:
- tx*unfit*npm1: β=+2.206 (p=8.1e-176)
- tx*unfit alone: β=+0.091 (NS)
- tx*npm1 alone: β=+0.027 (NS)
- tx*tp53: β=-0.534 (p=4.2e-10)
- tx*complex_karyotype: β=-0.379 (p=8.7e-8)

The benefit requires both unfit AND npm1+ jointly; tp53+ and complex_karyotype+ each independently abrogate it.

ITERATIONS 15-22 — SUBGROUP REFINEMENT FOR VEN/AZA AND OTHER TREATMENTS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Exhaustive 2- and 3-binary-feature subgroup searches across all six treatments confirmed that the largest treatment-effect subgroup in the entire dataset is venetoclax_azacitidine in unfit_for_intensive=1 AND npm1_mutation=1 AND complex_karyotype=0 patients (response 71.7% vs 16.1%, diff +55.6 pp, |z|=46.2). The next largest subgroup with the equivalent constraint tp53=0 yields diff +53.4 pp.

Combining ALL FOUR modifiers (unfit_for_intensive=1 AND npm1_mutation=1 AND tp53_mutation=0 AND complex_karyotype=0; n=4,531) gives:
- Ven/aza response: 78.6% (1,407/1,790)
- Off-treatment response: 15.8% (433/2,741)
- Absolute difference: +62.8 pp.

OUTSIDE this subgroup (n=45,469), ven/aza response is 16.4% vs 16.7% off (diff -0.3 pp, p=0.42) — essentially zero.

Adding any fifth binary feature (sex, secondary_aml, flt3_itd, flt3_tkd, idh1, idh2) does not meaningfully refine the effect (within-stratum diffs all 55-65 pp). The four-feature definition is parsimonious and complete.

Continuous-feature interactions added little: the largest tx*continuous standardized coefficient for ven/aza was tx*blast_pct_marrow at +0.068 (p=0.0035), which would not produce a clinically meaningful subgroup refinement.

Within the favorable subgroup, no co-administered treatment (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, 7+3) further altered the ven/aza response benefit (within-stratum diffs all 47-50 pp regardless of co-treatment).

ITERATIONS 16-17 — 7+3 INTERACTIONS
-----------------------------------
- treatment_7plus3 × complex_karyotype: β=-0.220, p=1.1e-3. In CK+ patients, 7+3 response 15.5% vs 17.9% off (diff -2.3 pp, p=0.008). 7+3 may slightly reduce response in complex-karyotype AML, though the effect is small.
- treatment_7plus3 × unfit: no main or interaction effect even in fit patients (diff +0.4 pp, p=0.38).

ITERATIONS 23-24 — NO OTHER MEANINGFUL HETEROGENEOUS TREATMENT EFFECTS
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Exhaustive 1- and 2-feature binary subgroup search for each non-ven/aza treatment: largest absolute response differences observed were enasidenib in flt3_itd+ & idh1+ (n=53, diff +11.0 pp, |z|=1.7, ns) and ivosidenib in idh2+ & secondary_aml (negative, n=79, diff -11.9 pp). Not robust, opposite direction to clinical expectation, and limited sample. Continuous-feature interaction screen across 5 non-ven/aza treatments × 18 continuous features (90 tests) yielded a maximum standardized |β| of 0.14 (enasidenib*hemoglobin_g_dl, p=0.0012), too small to define a clinically meaningful subgroup.

ITERATION 25 — FINAL CONCLUSIONS
--------------------------------
1. Best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis (final, confirmed):
   FINAL: treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine increases objective_response by +62.8 percentage points (78.6% vs 15.8%) in patients who are simultaneously
       unfit_for_intensive = 1
       AND npm1_mutation = 1
       AND tp53_mutation = 0
       AND complex_karyotype = 0
   Outside this subgroup, ven/aza has essentially no effect (-0.3 pp, p=0.42). The two adverse genomic features (tp53_mutation and complex_karyotype) each independently abrogate the benefit and are therefore necessary components of the subgroup definition (i.e., the favorable subgroup is defined by their absence).

2. Negative findings:
   - None of the four targeted agents (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib) shows a positive interaction with its canonical target mutation, and none produces a clinically meaningful response benefit in any tested subgroup. In fact, ivosidenib in IDH1+ patients trends toward worse response (NS).
   - 7+3 has no main effect on response and a small negative interaction with complex_karyotype.
   - No second large heterogeneous treatment effect was discovered.

3. Prognostic features (independent of treatment): higher ECOG, complex karyotype, tp53_mutation, weight_loss_pct_6mo, blast_pct_marrow, crp_mg_l, and wbc_k_per_ul are associated with worse response; npm1_mutation, unfit_for_intensive (because it concentrates ven/aza benefit), and albumin_g_dl are associated with better response. The apparent positive marginal association of unfit_for_intensive with response is entirely driven by the venetoclax_azacitidine effect within unfit patients.

OVERALL: This dataset shows one large, clinically interpretable treatment-effect heterogeneity — venetoclax+azacitidine in unfit, NPM1-mutated AML without TP53 mutation or complex karyotype — and otherwise null effects for the five remaining treatments. The subgroup structure aligns with the published clinical observation that venetoclax+azacitidine is most effective in NPM1-mutated AML and is suppressed by TP53 mutation and adverse-risk cytogenetics, while in this synthetic-EHR cohort the four targeted small molecules do not produce response benefits in their canonical biomarker subgroups.
