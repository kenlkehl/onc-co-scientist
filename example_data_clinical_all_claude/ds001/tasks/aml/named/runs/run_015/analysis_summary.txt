Analysis Summary — ds001_aml (N = 50,000 patients, outcome = objective_response, 18.75% overall)
=================================================================================================

Iterations 1–4 — Main effects of patient features and treatments
----------------------------------------------------------------
* Higher ECOG performance status was strongly associated with lower objective response
  (logit coef = -0.353, p < 1e-30; OR per unit ≈ 0.70).
* TP53 mutation lowered response (16.6% vs 19.0%, diff = -2.42pp, p ≈ 3.5e-5).
* Complex karyotype lowered response (16.8% vs 19.1%, diff = -2.29pp, p ≈ 3e-6).
* NPM1 mutation raised response substantially (23.5% vs 16.7%, diff = +6.74pp,
  p < 1e-50). NPM1 was the most prognostically favorable single feature.
* Secondary AML had no measurable effect (diff = 0.08pp, p = 0.85).
* "unfit_for_intensive" was paradoxically associated with HIGHER response
  (22.1% vs 16.5%, diff = +5.59pp), almost certainly because unfit patients are
  preferentially assigned venetoclax/azacitidine, which (as iterations 8–11 confirm)
  dominates the marginal effect.
* Sex showed no association.
* Age, considered as a continuous predictor, was not associated overall (p = 0.88) —
  again a likely indirect consequence of treatment selection by age.
* Among lab values, lower albumin and higher blast %, WBC, and CRP each correlated
  modestly with lower response (all p < 1e-3 or smaller). LDH, NLR, hemoglobin,
  liver enzymes, and electrolytes had no robust signal.
* Marginal treatment-receipt effects: only venetoclax_azacitidine showed a
  significant unadjusted main effect (22.0% vs 16.6%, diff = +5.35pp, p < 1e-30).
  Midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, and 7+3 had unadjusted
  effects within ±1pp and were not significant.

Iterations 5–7 — Targeted-therapy / biomarker alignment
-------------------------------------------------------
Tested whether each FLT3 or IDH agent has its expected effect inside the
indicated mutation-positive subgroup, and whether the effect is
heterogeneous via formal interaction terms.
* Midostaurin in FLT3-ITD+ (N = 10,000): 17.6% vs 19.1%, p = 0.19. No benefit.
* Midostaurin in FLT3-TKD+: no benefit (p = 0.85).
* Gilteritinib in FLT3-ITD+ (N = 10,000): 19.1% vs 18.9%, p = 0.86. No benefit.
* Gilteritinib in FLT3-TKD+: nominal -3.5pp (p = 0.10), not significant.
* Ivosidenib in IDH1+ (N = 3,624): nominal -4.0pp (p = 0.11).
* Enasidenib in IDH2+ (N = 4,679): no effect (p = 0.92).
* All formal treatment x biomarker interaction p-values were non-significant
  (smallest = 0.077 for ivosidenib x IDH1, in the wrong direction).
==> The four targeted agents show no measurable benefit on objective_response
in this dataset, even within their canonically indicated mutation subgroups.

Iteration 8 — Adjusted treatment effects (case-mix adjusted logit)
------------------------------------------------------------------
A single multivariable logistic regression including all six treatments and ten
case-mix adjusters (age, ECOG, TP53, CK, NPM1, secondary_AML, unfit, albumin,
WBC, blast %) showed:
* venetoclax_azacitidine: adj coef = +0.358, OR = 1.43, p < 1e-15 (independent benefit).
* All other treatments: |coef| < 0.05 and p > 0.13 (no independent effect).
This confirms that, after accounting for case-mix, only ven/aza independently
drives objective response.

Iteration 9 — Treatment-effect heterogeneity screen for venetoclax_azacitidine
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Eighteen modifier variables were tested as ven/aza x modifier interaction terms.
Significant interactions (effect of ven/aza differs by modifier):
* npm1_mutation         coef = +1.055,  p < 1e-30  (much larger benefit if NPM1+)
* unfit_for_intensive   coef = +0.883,  p < 1e-30  (much larger benefit if unfit)
* tp53_mutation         coef = -0.409,  p < 1e-7   (benefit suppressed if TP53+)
* complex_karyotype     coef = -0.306,  p ≈ 6e-6   (benefit suppressed if CK+)
* blast_pct_marrow      coef = +0.0035, p ≈ 0.004 (small positive interaction)
* All other interactions (age, ECOG, sex, FLT3-ITD, FLT3-TKD, IDH1, IDH2,
  secondary_AML, albumin, WBC, CRP, hemoglobin, LDH) p > 0.08.

Iteration 10 — Stratified ven/aza response rates
------------------------------------------------
Within each level of the strongest modifiers:
* ECOG 0/1/2: ven/aza benefit 6.0 / 5.1 / 4.9 pp (similar across ECOG).
* TP53+ (N = 4,985): ven/aza diff = -0.4pp (p = 0.77) — NO benefit.
* TP53- (N = 45,015): ven/aza diff = +6.0pp (p < 1e-30).
* CK+ (N = 7,462): ven/aza diff = +1.2pp (p = 0.20) — NO benefit.
* CK- (N = 42,538): ven/aza diff = +6.1pp (p < 1e-30).
* NPM1+ (N = 15,014): ven/aza diff = +18.7pp (p < 1e-100).
* NPM1- (N = 34,986): ven/aza diff = -0.4pp (p = 0.34) — NO benefit.
* unfit==1 (N = 20,056): ven/aza diff = +14.5pp (p < 1e-100).
* unfit==0 (N = 29,944): ven/aza diff = -0.8pp (p = 0.08) — NO benefit.
==> Both NPM1+ AND unfit appear to be NECESSARY for ven/aza benefit; both
TP53 and CK suppress the benefit completely.

Iteration 11 — Joint subgroup hypothesis (ven/aza)
--------------------------------------------------
* unfit & NPM1+ (N = 5,986):   ven/aza response 63.3% vs 16.0%, +47.4pp (p < 1e-300).
* unfit & NPM1+ & TP53- & CK- (N = 4,531): 78.6% vs 15.8%, +62.8pp (p < 1e-300).
* Complement of (unfit & NPM1+) (N = 44,014): -0.4pp (p = 0.31) — NO benefit.
* unfit & TP53+ (N = 2,076): -0.4pp (p = 0.87). TP53 wipes out benefit.
* unfit & CK+ (N = 2,979): +2.3pp (p = 0.11). CK wipes out benefit.

Iteration 12 — Heterogeneity screen for treatment_7plus3
--------------------------------------------------------
Only one significant 7+3 x modifier interaction:
* treatment_7plus3 x complex_karyotype: coef = -0.213, p = 0.0015.
  (i.e., 7+3 hurts more / helps less in CK+ patients.)
All other interactions p > 0.12.

Iterations 13–14 — Heterogeneity screens for FLT3 and IDH inhibitors
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Among 36 interaction tests across midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, and
enasidenib, four reached nominal p < 0.05:
* gilteritinib x unfit_for_intensive: +0.135, p = 0.037
* enasidenib x unfit_for_intensive: +0.187, p = 0.029
* ivosidenib x npm1_mutation: -0.200, p = 0.028 (wrong direction)
None survived as a clinically meaningful subgroup signal on follow-up
stratification, and no FLT3 or IDH inhibitor produced a positive response-rate
shift in any subgroup tested. With ~36 multiplicity, these are consistent with
chance.

Iteration 15 — Stepwise narrowing of the ven/aza subgroup
---------------------------------------------------------
Sequentially adding favorable predicates raises the within-subgroup ven/aza effect:
  unfit==1                              N=20056 diff = +14.5pp
  npm1==1                               N=15014 diff = +18.7pp
  unfit & npm1+                         N= 5986 diff = +47.4pp (resp 63.3% vs 16.0%)
  unfit & npm1+ & tp53-                 N= 5349 diff = +53.4pp (resp 69.1% vs 15.7%)
  unfit & npm1+ & tp53- & CK-           N= 4531 diff = +62.8pp (resp 78.6% vs 15.8%)
And outside this subgroup the ven/aza effect is null:
  NOT (unfit & npm1+)                   N=44014 diff = -0.4pp  p = 0.31
  unfit & tp53+ (suppressor present)    N= 2076 diff =  +0.4pp p = 0.87
  unfit & CK+ (suppressor present)      N= 2979 diff =  +2.3pp p = 0.11
  fit & npm1+ (no benefit expected)     N= 9028 diff =  -0.3pp p = 0.73

Iteration 16 — 7+3 stratified response
--------------------------------------
* CK==0 (N = 42,538):              7+3 diff = +0.7pp (p = 0.06).
* CK==1 (N = 7,462):               7+3 diff = -2.3pp (p = 0.008) — small harm.
* CK==0 & fit (N = 25,461):        7+3 diff = +0.9pp (p = 0.05). Borderline.
* CK==0 & fit & npm1+ (N = 7,664): 7+3 diff = +0.3pp (p = 0.75) — null.
* CK==0 & tp53==0 (N = 38,321):    7+3 diff = +0.8pp (p = 0.05). Borderline.
* CK==1 & tp53==1 (N = 768):       7+3 diff = -2.1pp (p = 0.49).
==> 7+3 has at most a marginal benefit (~+1pp) in the favorable cytogenetic
subgroup, and a small but consistent detriment in CK+. No subgroup shows a
clinically meaningful benefit.

Each agent x indicated-mutation subgroup with adjustment for age, ECOG, TP53,

* midostaurin in FLT3-ITD+:  adj coef = -0.10, OR = 0.90, p = 0.18.
* midostaurin in FLT3-TKD+:  adj coef = -0.07, OR = 0.93, p = 0.59.
* gilteritinib in FLT3-ITD+: adj coef = +0.02, OR = 1.02, p = 0.82.
* gilteritinib in FLT3-TKD+: adj coef = -0.27, OR = 0.76, p = 0.07.
* ivosidenib in IDH1+:       adj coef = -0.31, OR = 0.73, p = 0.07.
* enasidenib in IDH2+:       adj coef = +0.02, OR = 1.02, p = 0.90.
==> Confirms NO targeted-agent benefit on objective_response in indicated

Iteration 18 — Lab modifiers of ven/aza
----------------------------------------
Median splits of albumin, blasts, WBC, CRP, Hgb, LDH, NLR, calcium produced
ven/aza effects between +4.7 and +6.1 pp in every stratum (all highly
significant). No lab-driven heterogeneity beyond a slightly larger benefit in
high-blast or high-WBC patients (consistent with the small interaction signal
in iter 9).

Iteration 19 — Joint-subgroup search for 7+3
--------------------------------------------
* fit & CK- & tp53- & npm1+ (N=6,951): diff = +0.13pp (p = 0.91) — null.
* fit & CK- & tp53- & npm1- (N=16,042): diff = +1.35pp (p = 0.02) — small,
  marginal; survives only via large N.
* age <60 / age >=70: no benefit (p > 0.5).
* No subgroup with clinically meaningful 7+3 benefit identified.

Iteration 20 — Cross-treatment interactions for ven/aza
-------------------------------------------------------
None of the five ven/aza x other-treatment interactions reached p < 0.10
(smallest p ≈ 0.13). No evidence of synergy or antagonism.

Iteration 21 — Adjusted ven/aza effect within & outside the defining subgroup
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Within S = (unfit & npm1+ & tp53- & CK-): adj OR(ven/aza) = 20.85 (p < 1e-30).
* Outside S: adj OR(ven/aza) = 0.98 (p = 0.51).

Iteration 22 — Three-way interactions
-------------------------------------
* ven/aza x npm1 x unfit: 3-way coef = +2.13, p < 1e-30  (positive synergy
  confirms BOTH features needed).
* ven/aza x npm1 x tp53: 3-way coef = -0.99, p < 1e-7   (TP53 suppresses
  the NPM1+ benefit).

Iteration 23 — Final consolidated logit with subgroup indicator S
-----------------------------------------------------------------
* ven/aza x S interaction coef = +3.04, p < 1e-30.
* Within S: adj coef(ven/aza) = +3.03, OR ≈ 20.7, p < 1e-30.
* Outside S: adj coef(ven/aza) = -0.02, OR = 0.98, p = 0.50.
==> This is the cleanest formal demonstration that ven/aza benefit is
entirely concentrated in S.

Iteration 24 — Top-ranked treatment-by-binary-feature interactions (full scan)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The four largest interaction signals all involve ven/aza (npm1, unfit, tp53,
CK). Outside ven/aza, the only nominally significant signal at p < 0.01 was
treatment_7plus3 x complex_karyotype (-0.21, p = 0.0015) and
treatment_enasidenib x sex_female (+0.31, p = 2e-4), the latter not reflected
in stratified response-rate differences and likely a multiple-testing artifact.

Iteration 25 — Final per-treatment subgroup hypotheses (CONFIRMATORY)
---------------------------------------------------------------------
* venetoclax_azacitidine in [unfit_for_intensive==1 AND npm1_mutation==1
    AND tp53_mutation==0 AND complex_karyotype==0]: response 78.6% vs 15.8%
    (+62.8pp absolute, p < 1e-300). FINAL BEST-SUPPORTED SUBGROUP.
* 7+3 in complex_karyotype==1: response 15.5% vs 17.9% (-2.3pp, p = 0.008)
    — small harm; no positive benefit subgroup identified for 7+3.
* midostaurin / gilteritinib in FLT3-ITD+: null in indicated subgroup (p > 0.18).
* ivosidenib in IDH1+: null in indicated subgroup (p = 0.11).
* enasidenib in IDH2+: null in indicated subgroup (p = 0.92).

Overall conclusions
===================
1. Across 50,000 AML patients, the only treatment with a demonstrable effect
   on objective response is venetoclax_azacitidine, and that effect is sharply
   subgroup-dependent.

2. The defining subgroup of ven/aza response is patients who are
       unfit_for_intensive == 1
       AND npm1_mutation   == 1
       AND tp53_mutation   == 0
       AND complex_karyotype == 0.
   In that subgroup (N = 4,531), 78.6% of ven/aza recipients had an objective
   response, vs 15.8% of non-recipients (+62.8pp, adjusted OR ~ 21).
   Outside this subgroup, ven/aza provided no benefit (adjusted OR ≈ 0.98).

3. Both BIOMARKER POSITIVITY (NPM1 mutation) AND CLINICAL FRAILTY
   (unfit_for_intensive) are individually NECESSARY conditions for ven/aza
   benefit (3-way interaction p < 1e-30). TP53 and complex_karyotype each
   completely abolish the benefit when present.

4. None of the four targeted agents (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib,
   enasidenib) showed a measurable effect on objective response, including
   inside their canonically indicated mutation subgroups, even after
   case-mix adjustment.

5. 7+3 induction had no overall effect on response (diff +0.3pp, p = 0.46).
   The only consistent interaction was a small detriment in complex
   karyotype patients (-2.3pp, p = 0.008). No subgroup showed clinically
   meaningful 7+3 benefit on objective response.

6. Prognostic features that drive the baseline response rate independent of
   treatment are: ECOG performance status (lower => higher response), TP53
   mutation (lower), complex karyotype (lower), and NPM1 mutation (higher).
   Albumin (positive), blast %, WBC, and CRP (each negative) contribute
   modestly. Age, sex, secondary_AML, LDH, NLR, hemoglobin, and most other
   labs were uninformative on their own.
