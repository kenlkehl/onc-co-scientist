Analysis Summary: ds001_aml (n=50,000 AML patients; outcome = objective_response)
================================================================================

Approach
--------
We progressed across 20 propose-test-refine iterations covering: (1) main effects
of each of the 6 treatments, (2) main effects of 8 cytogenetic / mutation
features, (3) main effects of demographics and clinical features, (4) main
effects of 15 lab values, (5)-(8) hypothesized treatment x mutation/fitness
interactions, (9) full multivariable logistic regression, (10) systematic
treatment x feature interaction screen across 6 treatments and 20 features
(120 pairs), (11)-(12) joint NPM1 x unfit subgroup discovery for venetoclax/aza
plus dissection of TP53/CK suppression, (13) sex-specific enasidenib effect,
(14)-(15) heterogeneity probe for the remaining 5 treatments and adjusted
multivariable interaction models, (16)-(17) brute-force 3- and 4-feature binary
subgroup enumeration, (18)-(19) sensitivity analyses confirming the
venetoclax/aza subgroup, and (20) final per-treatment subgroup hypotheses.

Main effects on objective_response
----------------------------------
* Treatment main effects: only treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine shows a robust
  unconditional benefit (rate 0.220 vs 0.166 off, RD +0.054, p=6.6e-51). The
  other five treatments (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib,
  7+3) had no detectable main effect on response (all p>0.1).
* Mutation main effects: NPM1+ -> markedly higher response (0.235 vs 0.167,
  p=5.8e-70). TP53+ -> lower response (0.166 vs 0.190, p=3.5e-5).
  complex_karyotype -> lower response (0.168 vs 0.191, p=3.3e-6).
  FLT3-ITD, FLT3-TKD, IDH1, IDH2, secondary AML had no main effect.
* Performance status / fitness: ECOG PS strongly negative (logit coef -0.353
  per unit, p=1.6e-90); unfit_for_intensive paradoxically POSITIVE on response
  (0.221 vs 0.165, p=1.9e-55) - this is explained by the venetoclax/azacitidine
  effect heterogeneity (see below). Age had no main effect after accounting for
  these covariates. Sex had no main effect.
* Disease burden / inflammation: blast_pct_marrow negative (p=2.4e-16);
  weight_loss_pct_6mo negative (p=1.1e-30); albumin_g_dl positive (p=2.9e-7);
  crp_mg_l negative (p=6.1e-6); wbc_k_per_ul negative (p=1.9e-4). LDH, NLR,
  hemoglobin, AST, ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, electrolytes had no
  detectable univariate effect.
* Multivariable model: ECOG, NPM1, unfit_for_intensive, VEN/AZA,
  weight_loss, blast_pct_marrow, albumin, complex_karyotype, TP53, CRP, and
  WBC remained independently significant (all p<1e-4). None of the other
  five treatments was independently associated with response.

Treatment x feature interaction screen (120 pairs)
--------------------------------------------------
The dominant signals are all venetoclax/azacitidine x cytogenetic / fitness:

  VEN/AZA x npm1_mutation:           coef +1.055, p=5.5e-102  (top hit)
  VEN/AZA x unfit_for_intensive:     coef +0.883, p=1.1e-78
  VEN/AZA x tp53_mutation:           coef -0.409, p=4.7e-7    (suppression)
  VEN/AZA x complex_karyotype:       coef -0.306, p=6.0e-6    (suppression)
  enasidenib x sex_female:           coef +0.314, p=2.2e-4
  enasidenib x hemoglobin_g_dl:      coef -0.078, p=1.2e-3
  7+3 x complex_karyotype:           coef -0.213, p=1.5e-3

The strongest, by orders of magnitude, are the venetoclax/azacitidine
heterogeneities. The targeted-treatment-x-target-mutation hypotheses
(midostaurin/gilteritinib x FLT3 mutations; ivosidenib x IDH1; enasidenib x
IDH2) were NOT supported - none reached significance.

Venetoclax/azacitidine: definitive subgroup
-------------------------------------------
A three-way logit (tx + npm1 + unfit + all interactions) yielded a triple
interaction tx * npm1 * unfit coefficient of +2.13 (p=1.6e-91). Stratified
response rates within the four NPM1 x unfit cells:

   NPM1=0, unfit=0: tx 0.162 vs ctrl 0.172, RD = -0.010
   NPM1=0, unfit=1: tx 0.169 vs ctrl 0.164, RD = +0.005
   NPM1=1, unfit=0: tx 0.157 vs ctrl 0.160, RD = -0.003
   NPM1=1, unfit=1: tx 0.633 vs ctrl 0.160, RD = +0.474

The benefit of venetoclax/azacitidine is entirely concentrated in
NPM1-mutated patients who are also classified unfit_for_intensive. Either
condition alone produces no benefit.

Within the NPM1+/unfit cohort (n=5,986), TP53 and complex_karyotype each
independently abolish the venetoclax/aza effect:

   In NPM1+/unfit, TP53=0 -> RD = +0.534
   In NPM1+/unfit, TP53=1 -> RD = +0.006   (interaction tx*tp53 p=1.1e-29)
   In NPM1+/unfit, CK=0   -> RD = +0.556
   In NPM1+/unfit, CK=1   -> RD = +0.031   (interaction tx*ck p=3.0e-34)

The complete maximal-benefit subgroup is therefore:

   npm1_mutation = 1
   AND unfit_for_intensive = 1
   AND tp53_mutation = 0
   AND complex_karyotype = 0

Within this subgroup (n=4,531):
   Response on venetoclax/azacitidine: 0.786 (n=1,790)
   Response off venetoclax/azacitidine: 0.158 (n=2,741)
   Risk difference:                     +0.628
   Adjusted OR (logit, all covariates): 21.6 (p << 1e-300)

Outside this subgroup (n=45,469):
   Response on venetoclax/azacitidine: 0.164 (n=18,230)
   Response off venetoclax/azacitidine: 0.167 (n=27,239)
   Risk difference:                     -0.003 (p=0.42)
   Adjusted treatment coefficient:      -0.017 (p=0.53)

A brute-force 3- and 4-feature binary subgroup enumeration over 10
demographic/cytogenetic features identified the same conjunction as the
maximal-RD subgroup (no other combination at adequate sample size beat it).
Restricting further (e.g., adding sex, FLT3, IDH, or secondary AML
indicators) did not increase RD - the four-predicate definition is
parsimonious and sufficient.

Other treatments
----------------
1) treatment_7plus3: No main effect (RD +0.003, p=0.46). Adjusted interaction
   with complex_karyotype is significantly negative (coef -0.225, p=9.8e-4):
   in CK+ patients, 7+3 lowers response (0.156 vs 0.179 RD = -0.023; even
   larger in CK+/FLT3-ITD+/NPM1+: RD = -0.120, p=1.1e-3). 7+3 confers no
   subgroup of clear positive benefit.

2) treatment_enasidenib: Adjusted sex interaction is significant
   (tx*sex_female coef +0.32, p=2.2e-4). In females the absolute benefit is
   small (RD +0.025); in males the drug appears to slightly harm (RD -0.023).
   In the IDH2+/female/unfit subgroup the rate is 0.361 on tx vs 0.243 off
   (RD +0.118, n_tx=61) - suggestive but underpowered. No
   IDH2-mutation-specific main interaction.

3) treatment_midostaurin, treatment_gilteritinib, treatment_ivosidenib:
   No identifiable subgroup with a meaningful, robust positive risk
   difference. In particular, the canonical FLT3-targeted (midostaurin,
   gilteritinib) x FLT3-mutated and IDH1-targeted (ivosidenib) x IDH1+
   interactions were all not significant in either direction. For ivosidenib
   in IDH1+ patients, response was 0.154 on tx vs 0.194 off (RD = -0.040,
   p=0.077) - if anything the drug appears unhelpful in IDH1+ patients.

Final per-treatment subgroup hypotheses (best supported)
--------------------------------------------------------
Treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine: increases objective_response specifically
   in patients who are NPM1-mutated AND unfit_for_intensive AND
   TP53-wildtype AND lack a complex karyotype (RD ~+0.63, OR_adj ~22). No
   detectable effect outside this conjunction.

Treatment_7plus3: does NOT increase objective_response overall; reduces
   response in complex_karyotype+ patients (interaction p=9.8e-4),
   particularly in those with concurrent FLT3-ITD+ and NPM1+.

Treatment_enasidenib: small positive effect concentrated in female
   patients; no meaningful effect or possible mild harm in males (interaction
   p=2.2e-4).

Treatment_midostaurin, treatment_gilteritinib, treatment_ivosidenib: no
   identifiable subgroup with a meaningful, robust objective_response
   benefit, including in their canonical target-mutation subsets.

Confounder considerations
-------------------------
The dataset has clear non-random treatment assignment (patients flagged
unfit_for_intensive are much more likely to receive venetoclax/azacitidine
or omit 7+3, etc.). All key conclusions above were verified in multivariable
logistic regression that simultaneously adjusts for all features and all
six treatments, so the treatment-effect heterogeneity findings are unlikely
to reflect simple confounding by indication. The dramatic VEN/AZA effect in
the NPM1+/unfit/no-TP53/no-CK subgroup is the dominant pattern in the
dataset and cannot be explained by any single covariate.

Bottom line
-----------
This cohort exhibits a single, very large treatment-effect heterogeneity:
venetoclax/azacitidine produces a ~63 percentage-point absolute increase in
objective response, but only in patients who are simultaneously NPM1-mutated,
unfit for intensive chemotherapy, TP53-wildtype, and without a complex
karyotype. Outside that joint subgroup, venetoclax/azacitidine and the other
five regimens (7+3, midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib) do
not produce a clinically meaningful improvement in objective response in any
identifiable subgroup. NPM1 mutation, ECOG PS, weight loss, blast burden,
albumin, CRP, WBC, TP53 status, and complex karyotype are independent
prognostic markers for response.
