ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_aml (50,000 patients, AML cohort)
============================================================

Outcome: objective_response (binary). Overall ORR = 18.75%.

This analysis was conducted in 19 iterations covering main effects,
treatment-biomarker pairings, formal interaction tests, and systematic
heterogeneity searches culminating in a precise subgroup definition for
the only treatment that showed a clinically meaningful effect.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. PATIENT FEATURES — MAIN EFFECTS ON ORR (iterations 2-4)
------------------------------------------------------------

Negative prognostic features (lower ORR):
  - ecog_ps         OR=0.70 per unit (p=2e-90)  — strongest fitness signal
  - tp53_mutation   ORR 16.6% vs 19.0% (p=4e-5)
  - complex_karyotype ORR 16.8% vs 19.1% (p=3e-6)
  - blast_pct_marrow OR=0.995 per % (p=2e-16)
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo OR=0.965 per % (p=1e-30)
  - crp_mg_l        OR=0.994 per mg/L (p=6e-6)
  - wbc_k_per_ul    OR=0.998 per K/uL (p=2e-4)

Positive prognostic features (higher ORR):
  - npm1_mutation   ORR 23.5% vs 16.7% (p=6e-70)  — strongest molecular signal
  - albumin_g_dl    OR=1.12 per g/dL (p=3e-7)
  - unfit_for_intensive ORR 22.1% vs 16.5% (p=2e-55) — counter-intuitive direction;
      driven by treatment confounding (ven/aza is concentrated in unfit patients
      and works dramatically there).

Null / weak: age_years, sex_female, secondary_aml, flt3_itd, flt3_tkd,
idh1_mutation, idh2_mutation, AST, ALT, alk phos, total bilirubin, creatinine,
BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, hemoglobin, LDH, NLR.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS (iteration 1)
------------------------------------------------------------

Of the six treatments, ONLY treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine showed a
significant marginal effect on ORR:
  - venetoclax_azacitidine: 21.96% on vs 16.61% off, diff +5.35% (p≈7e-51)
  - 7+3:                    18.89% on vs 18.63% off (p=0.46, NS)
  - midostaurin:            18.57% on vs 18.78% off (p=0.67, NS)
  - gilteritinib:           18.13% on vs 18.86% off (p=0.14, NS)
  - ivosidenib:             18.82% on vs 18.74% off (p=0.93, NS)
  - enasidenib:             18.58% on vs 18.76% off (p=0.79, NS)

3. TREATMENT-BIOMARKER PAIRINGS (iterations 5-6)
------------------------------------------------------------

We tested the canonical clinical pairings:

  Hypothesized          Observed within target biomarker subgroup
  ----------------------------------------------------------------
  midostaurin × FLT3-ITD+:   17.6% vs 19.1% (diff -1.5%, p=0.19)   REFUTED
  gilteritinib × FLT3-ITD+:  19.1% vs 18.9% (diff +0.2%, p=0.86)   REFUTED
  gilteritinib × any FLT3:   18.2% vs 18.8% (diff -0.6%, p=0.57)   REFUTED
  ivosidenib × IDH1+:        15.4% vs 19.4% (diff -4.0%, p=0.11)   REFUTED
                             (paradoxically NEGATIVE direction)
  enasidenib × IDH2+:        19.5% vs 19.1% (diff +0.4%, p=0.92)   REFUTED
  ven/aza × unfit_for_intensive=1: 30.8% vs 16.3% (diff +14.5%, p~1e-130)  CONFIRMED
  ven/aza × unfit_for_intensive=0: 16.0% vs 16.8% (diff -0.8%, p=0.08)    NULL
  7+3 × unfit_for_intensive=0:     16.7% vs 16.3% (diff +0.4%, p=0.38)   NULL

Formal logit interaction tests (treatment × biomarker, on logit scale):
  - ven/aza × unfit_for_intensive: coef +0.88 (OR_int=2.42), p=1.1e-78  VERY LARGE
  - ivosidenib × idh1_mutation:    coef -0.30 (OR_int=0.74), p=0.077  trend wrong direction
  - all other targeted pairings: NS

CONCLUSION: Despite this dataset including the canonical FLT3 (mido,
gilt) and IDH (ivo, ena) targeted agents, none of these targeted-agent
× biomarker effects materialize in this cohort. The only positive
treatment-biomarker interaction is ven/aza × unfit_for_intensive.

  ven/aza in unfit & tp53_mutation=1:    17.7% vs 17.3% (diff +0.4%, p=0.87)   NULL

  ven/aza in unfit & complex_karyotype=1: 18.5% vs 16.2% (diff +2.3%, p=0.11)   weak/NS
  ven/aza in unfit & npm1_mutation=1:    63.3% vs 16.0% (diff +47.4%, p≈1e-310) ENORMOUS

Systematic interaction screen of ven/aza × every candidate modifier:

The dominant findings are NPM1 (+), unfit (+), TP53 (-), complex karyotype (-).

5. FINAL VEN/AZA SUBGROUP DEFINITION (iterations 15-18)
------------------------------------------------------------

The four signals combine multiplicatively. Fully cross-tabulated:

  Within unfit=1 & npm1=1:
    tp53=0, ck=0:  ORR on ven/aza = 78.6% (N=1790) vs off = 15.8% (N=2741)  diff +62.8%
    tp53=0, ck=1:  ORR on ven/aza = 18.2% (N=335)  vs off = 14.9% (N=483)   diff +3.3%
    tp53=1, ck=0:  ORR on ven/aza = 18.7% (N=235)  vs off = 18.3% (N=311)   diff +0.4%
    tp53=1, ck=1:  ORR on ven/aza = 19.5% (N=41)   vs off = 18.0% (N=50)    diff +1.5%

  Within unfit=1 & npm1=0 (any tp53/ck):  diff < 0.03 in every cell

  Within unfit=0 (any npm1/tp53/ck):       diff ≈ 0 in every cell

The treatment effect is concentrated almost entirely in the single cell
where ALL FOUR conditions are favorable simultaneously:

   unfit_for_intensive = 1
   AND npm1_mutation   = 1
   AND tp53_mutation   = 0
   AND complex_karyotype = 0

In this signature subgroup (N=4,531):
    ORR on ven/aza = 78.6% vs off = 15.8%, absolute diff = +62.8%
    Outside the signature (N=45,469):
    ORR on ven/aza = 16.4% vs off = 16.7%, diff = -0.3% (NS)

A logit interaction term (ven/aza × signature) gives OR ≈ 21.4
(p ≈ 2.8e-299), and this remains essentially unchanged after
multivariable adjustment for age, sex, ECOG, secondary_aml, FLT3, IDH,
albumin, LDH, WBC, blast%, NLR, CRP, weight loss
(adjusted interaction OR ≈ 21.4, p ≈ 0).

Drop-one sensitivity (relaxing each of the 4 components in turn):
   drop unfit:  N=11,482, on ORR 40.3% vs off 16.0%, diff +24.3%
   drop npm1:   N=15,328, on ORR 34.9% vs off 16.2%, diff +18.6%
   drop tp53:   N=5,077,  on ORR 71.7% vs off 16.1%, diff +55.6%
   drop ck:     N=5,349,  on ORR 69.1% vs off 15.7%, diff +53.4%
Every relaxation dilutes the effect, confirming all four conditions are
load-bearing components of the subgroup.

------------------------------------------------------------
8. FINAL CONCLUSIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

(a) Treatment-effect heterogeneity:
    Only treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine has a clinically meaningful
    objective_response benefit, and that benefit is essentially
    confined to a precise 4-feature subgroup:

        unfit_for_intensive = 1
        AND npm1_mutation = 1
        AND tp53_mutation = 0
        AND complex_karyotype = 0

    In this signature subgroup, ORR on ven/aza ≈ 79% vs ≈ 16% on other
    therapy. Outside this subgroup, ven/aza confers no benefit on
    objective response.

    Each of the four conditions is necessary: unfavorable values of
    tp53_mutation (=1) or complex_karyotype (=1) suppress the effect
    even within unfit & NPM1+ patients, and absence of NPM1 (=0) or
    of unfit-status (=0) likewise eliminate the response benefit.

(b) Targeted agents (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib)
    do NOT show a positive ORR effect in their canonical biomarker
    subgroups in this cohort, refuting the standard pharmacologic
    pairings as drivers of objective_response in these data. (The
    ivosidenib effect in IDH1+ is directionally negative.)

(c) treatment_7plus3 has no detectable ORR benefit in any subgroup; in
    fit patients with complex_karyotype it may be modestly harmful.

(d) Independent prognostic features for ORR (regardless of treatment):
    NPM1+ and high albumin are favorable; high ECOG, TP53 mutation,
    complex karyotype, high blast %, weight loss, high WBC, and high
    CRP are unfavorable. Age, sex, secondary AML, FLT3, IDH, and most
    routine chemistries do not independently predict ORR.
