ds001_aml — Analysis Summary
=================================================================

Cohort: 50,000 AML patients with binary outcome `objective_response`
(overall rate = 18.75%). The dataset includes baseline demographics,
ECOG PS, disease features (secondary AML, fitness flag, complex
karyotype, six AML mutations), 18 lab values, and six binary
treatment indicators (which can co-occur). 25 iterations of
hypothesis–test–refine were performed.

================================================================
1. Patient-feature main effects on response (iters 1–6)
================================================================

Strongest predictors of HIGHER objective response:
  - npm1_mutation = 1: response 23.5% vs 16.7%; chi-square p ~ 5e-70.
    Adjusted log-odds +0.43 (p ~ 3e-71). Highly favorable.
  - unfit_for_intensive = 1: response 22.1% vs 16.5%; p ~ 1e-55.
    Adjusted log-odds +0.37 (p ~ 7e-57). The favorable direction is
    counter-intuitive in isolation — driven entirely by the fact
    that "unfit" patients are the ones who receive ven/aza, the
    only highly effective drug in this cohort (see below).
  - albumin_g_dl: positive logistic beta +0.12 per g/dL (p ~ 3e-7);
    adjusted +0.12 (p ~ 1e-7).

Strongest predictors of LOWER response:
  - ecog_ps: -0.35 log-odds per 1-point increase (p ~ 1e-90);
    adjusted -0.36 (p ~ 9e-92). Largest single adverse effect.
  - blast_pct_marrow: adjusted -0.005 per % (p ~ 5e-17).
  - tp53_mutation: -2.4 percentage-point response difference
    (p ~ 3e-5); adjusted -0.18 (p ~ 7e-6).
  - complex_karyotype: -2.3 pp (p ~ 3e-6); adjusted -0.17 (p ~ 8e-7).
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo: adjusted -0.04 per % (p ~ 1e-30).
  - crp_mg_l: adjusted -0.006 per mg/L (p ~ 5e-6).
  - wbc_k_per_ul: adjusted -0.002 per K/uL (p ~ 1e-4).

NULL or borderline main effects: age_years (after multivariable
adjustment), sex_female, secondary_aml, hemoglobin, ldh, nlr, and
the entire chemistry panel (alk phos, AST/ALT, bilirubin, BUN,
creatinine, sodium, potassium, calcium). flt3_itd, flt3_tkd,
idh1_mutation, idh2_mutation each have only trivial unadjusted
effects on overall response (|diff|<0.01, p>0.3) — their effects
are gated entirely through treatment in the canonical pharmacology
narrative.

================================================================
2. Treatment main effects (iters 7–8)
================================================================

Univariable response-rate differences (treatment=1 vs treatment=0):

  treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine:  +5.4 pp  (p ~ 6e-51)  ***
  treatment_7plus3:                  +0.3 pp  (p = 0.46)
  treatment_midostaurin:             -0.2 pp  (p = 0.66)
  treatment_gilteritinib:            -0.7 pp  (p = 0.14)
  treatment_ivosidenib:              +0.1 pp  (p = 0.91)
  treatment_enasidenib:              -0.2 pp  (p = 0.78)

Adjusted (multivariable) log-odds for each treatment:

  ven/aza:        +0.358 (p ~ 7e-53)  ***
  midostaurin:    -0.012 (p = 0.71)
  gilteritinib:   -0.047 (p = 0.15)
  ivosidenib:     +0.012 (p = 0.78)
  enasidenib:     -0.012 (p = 0.79)
  7+3:            +0.015 (p = 0.51)

Conclusion: only ven/aza shows an unconditional (marginal) benefit
on objective_response. None of the targeted/cytotoxic agents has a
detectable marginal effect.

================================================================
3. Canonical mutation-targeted-therapy hypotheses (iters 9–13)
================================================================

I pre-specified the standard pharmacology subgroup hypotheses and
tested each via stratified analysis and a treatment×marker
interaction term. NONE replicated:

  - midostaurin × flt3_itd:  interaction beta = -0.106, p = 0.20.
    FLT3-ITD+ subgroup: midostaurin response 17.6% vs 19.1% (diff
    -1.5 pp, p = 0.18). FLT3-ITD- subgroup: diff +0.1 pp.
  - midostaurin × flt3_tkd:  interaction beta = -0.02, p = 0.87.
  - gilteritinib × flt3_itd: interaction beta = +0.08, p = 0.32.
    FLT3-ITD+ subgroup diff +0.2 pp, p = 0.84.
  - gilteritinib × flt3_tkd: borderline negative trend in TKD+
    subgroup; not significant.
  - ivosidenib × idh1_mutation: interaction beta = -0.30, p = 0.08
    (sign opposite to expectation). IDH1+ subgroup: ivosidenib
    response 15.4% vs 19.4% (diff -4.0 pp, p = 0.09).
  - enasidenib × idh2_mutation: interaction beta = +0.04, p = 0.79.
    IDH2+ subgroup diff +0.4 pp, p = 0.87.

Refining these with tp53=0/complex_karyotype=0 "clean" subgroups
(iters 17, 18, 21) did not rescue any of these interactions. After
adjusting for confounders in a single multivariable model with all
five canonical interactions simultaneously (iter 24), all five
interaction terms remain non-significant (smallest p = 0.06 for
ivosidenib × IDH1, sign still negative).

In short: in this cohort the four "targetable" mutations do NOT
predict benefit from their corresponding drugs.

================================================================
4. The dominant heterogeneity: ven/aza (iters 14, 16, 19, 22, 25)
================================================================

A systematic treatment × feature interaction screen across all 6
treatments × 19 candidate modifiers (iter 16) found that the
overwhelmingly strongest interactions in the dataset all involve
treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine:

  ven/aza × npm1_mutation:        beta = +1.055, p = 5e-102  ***
  ven/aza × unfit_for_intensive:  beta = +0.883, p = 1e-78   ***
  ven/aza × tp53_mutation:        beta = -0.409, p = 5e-7    ***
  ven/aza × complex_karyotype:    beta = -0.306, p = 6e-6    ***
  ven/aza × blast_pct_marrow:     beta = +0.0035, p = 0.004  *

Stratified estimates:

  - In unfit_for_intensive=1: ven/aza response 30.8% vs 16.3%
    (diff +14.5 pp, p ~ 5e-130).
  - In unfit AND tp53=0: ven/aza response 32.4% vs 16.2%
    (diff +16.2 pp, p ~ 9e-143). In unfit AND tp53=1: diff +0.4 pp,
    NS — TP53 mutation completely abolishes the ven/aza benefit.
  - In unfit AND CK=0: ven/aza response 33.0% vs 16.3% (diff +16.7
    pp, p ~ 6e-142). In unfit AND CK=1: diff +2.3 pp, NS — complex
    karyotype also abolishes the benefit.
  - In NPM1+ (any fitness): ven/aza response 34.7% vs 16.0%
    (diff +18.7 pp, p ~ 1e-154).

================================================================
5. Final, fully-specified ven/aza subgroup (iter 25)
================================================================

Combining the four modifiers gives the cleanest, largest signal:

  Subgroup: npm1_mutation=1 AND unfit_for_intensive=1 AND
            tp53_mutation=0 AND complex_karyotype=0
  n = 4,531 (9.1% of cohort).
  ven/aza response rate: 78.6%
  no-ven/aza response rate (within subgroup): 15.8%
  Absolute difference: +62.8 percentage points.
  Odds ratio: 19.5
  p-value: indistinguishable from 0.

  Outside this subgroup (n = 45,469): ven/aza response 16.4% vs
  16.7% (diff -0.3 pp, p = 0.42) — i.e., NO benefit.

A joint logistic-regression model (iter 25) including all four
ven/aza:modifier interaction terms confirmed that each contributes
independently:

  ven/aza × npm1_mutation:        beta = +1.115, p ~ 2e-109
  ven/aza × unfit_for_intensive:  beta = +0.946, p ~ 2e-86
  ven/aza × tp53_mutation:        beta = -0.485, p ~ 6e-9
  ven/aza × complex_karyotype:    beta = -0.348, p ~ 5e-7
  ven/aza main effect (when all four modifiers = 0): beta = -0.381,
    p ~ 4e-22 (i.e., in patients who are NPM1-wt AND fit AND TP53-wt
    AND CK-, ven/aza is actually slightly harmful).

The exhaustive 2-feature subgroup screen (iter 22) reached the same
conclusion: every one of the top 15 highest-effect treatment
subgroups was a ven/aza subgroup conditioned on npm1_mutation=1,
with effect sizes ranging from +18 to +47 percentage points.

================================================================
6. Final per-treatment subgroup conclusions
================================================================

ven/aza (treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine):
  STRONGLY SUPPORTED. Effect concentrated in
  [npm1_mutation=1 AND unfit_for_intensive=1 AND tp53_mutation=0
   AND complex_karyotype=0]. Outside this subgroup, no benefit.
  Both tp53_mutation=1 and complex_karyotype=1 are independent
  suppressors of the effect within the otherwise favorable
  subgroup. NPM1 mutation and unfit-for-intensive status are the
  positive-effect modifiers.

7+3 (treatment_7plus3):
  NOT SUPPORTED. No subgroup shows a clinically meaningful
  benefit, including the pre-specified favorable subgroup
  (fit/age<65/tp53=0/CK=0): diff +0.8 pp, p = 0.31. Only
  significant interaction is 7+3 × complex_karyotype (beta -0.21,
  p = 0.0015), suggesting 7+3 is somewhat worse in CK+.

midostaurin (treatment_midostaurin):
  NOT SUPPORTED in this cohort. No FLT3-defined subgroup (ITD,
  TKD, ITD-or-TKD; with or without TP53/CK refinement) shows a
  positive effect; subgroup diffs are mildly negative (e.g., -1.5
  pp in FLT3-ITD+).

gilteritinib (treatment_gilteritinib):
  NOT SUPPORTED. Even in the cleanest pharmacology subgroup
  (FLT3-ITD+ AND TP53-wt AND CK-), diff is +1.0 pp, p = 0.43.

ivosidenib (treatment_ivosidenib):
  NOT SUPPORTED, and in fact trends opposite to expectation in
  IDH1-mutated patients (diff -4.0 pp, p ~ 0.09 unadjusted; joint-
  model interaction beta -0.32, p = 0.064).

enasidenib (treatment_enasidenib):
  NOT SUPPORTED in IDH2-mutated patients (diff +0.4 pp, p = 0.87).
  The cleanest signal is a positive enasidenib × sex_female
  interaction (beta +0.31, p = 0.0002), but this lacks pharmacology
  rationale and was not pre-specified; flagged as exploratory.

================================================================
7. Hypotheses confirmed vs refuted
================================================================

CONFIRMED:
  - Higher ECOG PS lowers response (huge effect).
  - NPM1 mutation raises response.
  - Complex karyotype lowers response.
  - TP53 mutation lowers response.
  - Higher albumin raises response; higher blast %, weight loss,
    CRP, WBC each lower it.
  - ven/aza overall raises response.
  - ven/aza benefit is strongly modified (positively) by
    npm1_mutation and unfit_for_intensive, and (negatively) by
    tp53_mutation and complex_karyotype.

REFUTED in this cohort (against pre-specified pharmacology):
  - midostaurin benefit in FLT3-ITD or FLT3-TKD.
  - gilteritinib benefit in FLT3-ITD or FLT3-TKD.
  - ivosidenib benefit in IDH1-mutated patients (in fact trends
    against benefit).
  - enasidenib benefit in IDH2-mutated patients.
  - 7+3 benefit in fit/young/clean-genetics patients.
  - Hypothesis that age (per year) modifies the ven/aza benefit
    (interaction beta ~ -0.0014/year, p = 0.50): not supported —
    the modifier is fitness flag and NPM1 mutation, not age itself.
  - Most chemistry-panel labs (alk phos, AST/ALT, bilirubin, Na,
    K, Ca, BUN, creatinine) are not associated with response.

================================================================
8. Bottom line
================================================================

In this 50,000-patient AML cohort, the dominant story in the data
is venetoclax + azacitidine, and only venetoclax + azacitidine.
Its benefit is enormous but tightly confined: NPM1 mutation and
unfit-for-intensive status mark the patients who respond, while
TP53 mutation and complex karyotype each independently abolish the
benefit. The fully-specified favorable subgroup
[npm1_mutation=1 AND unfit_for_intensive=1 AND tp53_mutation=0
 AND complex_karyotype=0] (~9% of patients) shows a 78.6% vs 15.8%
response rate — a 62.8-percentage-point absolute difference. None
of the other five treatments (7+3, midostaurin, gilteritinib,
ivosidenib, enasidenib) shows a credible objective-response
benefit anywhere in the cohort, including in their canonical
mutation-defined subgroups. ECOG PS, NPM1, TP53, complex karyotype,
albumin, blast %, weight loss, CRP and WBC are the dominant
prognostic features for response, independent of treatment.
