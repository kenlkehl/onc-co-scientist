Analysis Summary - ds001_aml (n = 50,000)
============================================================

Outcome: objective_response (binary, overall rate = 18.75%).

Six therapies recorded (often co-administered):
  treatment_midostaurin            n =  7,545 (15%)
  treatment_gilteritinib           n =  7,545 (15%)
  treatment_ivosidenib             n =  3,938 ( 8%)
  treatment_enasidenib             n =  3,972 ( 8%)
  treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine n = 20,020 (40%)
  treatment_7plus3                 n = 22,580 (45%)

The propose-test-refine loop ran for the full 25-iteration cap. Below is a
narrative walk-through of what was tested, what the evidence showed, and what
hypotheses survived versus were refuted.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 1-2: Patient features and prognostic markers
------------------------------------------------------------
- h1 (older age -> lower response): REFUTED. Mean age was 68.0 in both
  responders and non-responders (Welch t p=0.88).
- h2 (higher ECOG PS -> lower response): SUPPORTED. Per-unit ECOG OR=0.703
  (p=1.6e-90).
- h3 (sex difference): not statistically meaningful.
- h4 (secondary AML lower response): REFUTED at marginal level
  (0.188 vs 0.187, p=0.85).
- h5 (unfit_for_intensive lower response): REVERSED direction; unfit patients
  had a HIGHER overall response (0.221 vs 0.165, p=1.9e-55). This is driven
  by the fact that unfit patients almost exclusively receive venetoclax-
  azacitidine, the strongest treatment.
- h6 (complex_karyotype): SUPPORTED, lower response (0.168 vs 0.191,
  OR=0.86, p=3.3e-6).
- h7 (TP53 mutation): SUPPORTED, lower response (0.166 vs 0.190, OR=0.85,
  p=3.5e-5).
- h8 (NPM1 mutation): SUPPORTED, higher response (0.235 vs 0.167, OR=1.53,
  p=5.8e-70).
- h9-h12 (FLT3-ITD, FLT3-TKD, IDH1, IDH2): individual main effects on response
  were small and largely non-significant or weak.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 3: Unadjusted treatment main effects
------------------------------------------------------------
Only treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine showed a clear unadjusted main effect:
response 0.220 vs 0.166 (OR=1.41, p=6.6e-51).

All four targeted therapies (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib,
enasidenib) and 7+3 had near-null unadjusted main effects (|delta| <= 0.01,
p > 0.1 in every case), refuting their corresponding novel hypotheses at the
unadjusted level.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 4: Continuous lab features
------------------------------------------------------------
Significant univariate associations with higher response: higher
albumin_g_dl (delta = +0.030, p=2.7e-7), lower crp_mg_l, lower
weight_loss_pct_6mo. ldh_u_l, nlr, hemoglobin_g_dl and the chemistry
panel (creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, AST, ALT, ALP,
bilirubin) had small and mostly non-significant univariate differences.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 5: Multivariable adjusted treatment effects
------------------------------------------------------------
Adjusting for age, sex, ECOG PS, fitness, all key mutations, WBC, blast %
and albumin:
  treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine: adjusted OR = 1.43, p = 4.8e-53
    (the only treatment with a significant adjusted main effect).
  midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, 7+3: all adjusted ORs
  near 1.0 (0.95-1.02), all p > 0.1. Hypotheses h36-h39, h41 were not
  supported.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 6-7: Targeted therapy x mutation interactions
------------------------------------------------------------
Hypotheses that the FLT3 inhibitors (midostaurin, gilteritinib) would
benefit FLT3-ITD/TKD patients (h42-h49), and that the IDH inhibitors
would benefit their corresponding mutated patients (h50-h57), were
broadly REFUTED in this dataset:
  - midostaurin*flt3_itd interaction OR = 0.90, p = 0.20.
  - gilteritinib*flt3_itd interaction OR = 1.08, p = 0.32.
  - ivosidenib*idh1_mutation interaction OR = 0.74, p = 0.08
    (in fact stratified analysis shows midly LOWER response on ivosidenib
    within IDH1-mut: 0.154 vs 0.194).
  - enasidenib*idh2_mutation interaction OR = 1.04, p = 0.79.

So in ds001_aml the conventional clinical-trial heterogeneity stories for
FLT3 and IDH inhibitors are NOT present at any meaningful magnitude.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 8-9: TP53 / complex karyotype / fitness modifiers
------------------------------------------------------------
- treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine x tp53_mutation: significant negative
  interaction (TP53 suppresses benefit).
- treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine x complex_karyotype: significant negative
  interaction (complex karyotype suppresses benefit).
- treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine x unfit_for_intensive: large positive
  interaction (h63 SUPPORTED) -- benefit is concentrated in the unfit
  population.
- treatment_7plus3 x unfit_for_intensive: weak (h62) - 7+3 main effect was
  too small to show meaningful HE in either direction.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 10-11: Age and ECOG strata
------------------------------------------------------------
ven-aza benefit was larger in age >= 75 and in ecog_ps >= 2 patients,
consistent with its concentration in frailer / unfit patients.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 12-15: Joint subgroup checks (mutation + fitness + cytogenetics)
------------------------------------------------------------
- gilteritinib in FLT3-mutated, fit (h71): no benefit (Δ ≈ +0.01).
- midostaurin in FLT3-ITD, fit (h73): no benefit (Δ ≈ -0.01).
- ivosidenib in IDH1-mut, TP53wt (h76): negative direction (Δ ≈ -0.05).
- enasidenib in IDH2-mut, TP53wt (h77): essentially null.
- ven-aza in unfit (h78): big benefit (Δ = +0.145, OR=2.29).
- ven-aza in unfit & TP53wt (h79): bigger benefit (Δ = +0.162, OR=2.48).
- ven-aza in unfit & non-complex (h80): Δ = +0.167, OR=2.53.
- ven-aza in unfit & TP53wt & non-complex (h81): Δ = +0.186, OR=2.76.
- 7+3 in fit & TP53wt & non-complex (h84): Δ = +0.010 (marginal).

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 16: Systematic interaction-term screen
------------------------------------------------------------
A 6-treatment by 12-modifier (binary) logistic interaction sweep produced
72 interaction tests. The strongest positive interactions all involved
treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine. None of the targeted-therapy x molecular-
target interactions reached significance. The top-ranked hits (h85-h99)
are dominated by ven-aza interactions with NPM1, fitness, complex
karyotype, TP53, age and ECOG.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 17: Best-Δ subgroup search per treatment
------------------------------------------------------------
Exhaustive 1-3 binary feature combinations (n_subgroup >= 200) ranked by
the response delta on - off:
  midostaurin best: secondary_aml=1 & flt3_itd=1 & tp53_mutation=1
    (Δ=+0.20, n=213, p=0.012) - small subgroup, plausible chance finding.
  gilteritinib best: secondary_aml=1 & unfit=1 & idh1_mutation=1
    (Δ=+0.14, n=340, p=0.047) - implausible (no FLT3 component).
  ivosidenib best: secondary_aml=1 & tp53=1 & age_ge75=0
    (Δ=+0.09, n=902, p=0.06) - not significant.
  enasidenib best: flt3_itd=1 & idh1_mutation=1 & npm1_mutation=0
    (Δ=+0.19, n=506, p=0.007) - small subgroup, opposite to expected target.
  ven-aza best: unfit=1 & non-complex & npm1=1
    (Δ=+0.556, n=5077, p<<1e-300) - DOMINANT EFFECT (response 71.7% vs
    16.1%).
  7+3 best: flt3_tkd=1 & idh2_mutation=1 & npm1=0
    (Δ=+0.16, n=208, p=0.004) - small, niche.

The ven-aza signal is by an enormous margin the most robust, largest, and
biologically coherent treatment-effect-heterogeneity finding in the
dataset.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 18-19: Refined statements + within-subgroup adjusted models
------------------------------------------------------------
Restating and adjusting (h120-h131) reproduces the same picture: every
within-target subgroup test for the targeted therapies is null or slightly
negative; ven-aza retains its large effect within unfit; 7+3 retains a
small (~+0.01 - +0.02) effect in fit, TP53wt, non-complex patients.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 20: Negative-control subgroups
------------------------------------------------------------
- gilteritinib in FLT3-wt (h132): Δ = -0.008, NS (as expected for a null
  drug in a non-target subgroup).
- midostaurin in FLT3-wt (h133): Δ = +0.002, NS.
- ivosidenib in IDH1-wt (h134): Δ = +0.004, NS.
- enasidenib in IDH2-wt (h135): Δ = -0.002, NS.
The targeted therapies behave like null drugs in both target and non-target
subgroups, confirming that ds001_aml does NOT encode the classical
mutation-selective efficacy of these drugs.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 21-22: NPM1 modifier of 7+3, treatment co-administration
------------------------------------------------------------
- 7+3 in NPM1+ fit (h136): Δ = +0.005 (NS).
- 7+3 in NPM1- fit (h137): Δ ≈ 0.
- midostaurin x 7+3 (h138), gilteritinib x 7+3 (h139): no significant

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 23: Full-model interaction terms
------------------------------------------------------------
In a fully adjusted multivariable logistic with all targeted-drug x
mutation interactions, NONE of the targeted interaction terms reached
significance. This corroborates the simpler tests and rules out
confounding-driven masking of the targeted-therapy interactions.

------------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 24: Adjusted lab predictors
------------------------------------------------------------
- albumin_g_dl: adjusted OR per +1 = 1.13 (p=2.4e-7) - SUPPORTED.
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: adjusted OR per +1 = 0.965 (p=1.7e-30) - SUPPORTED.
- crp_mg_l: adjusted OR per +1 = 0.993 (p=3.1e-6) - SUPPORTED.
- ldh_u_l, nlr, hemoglobin_g_dl: not significant after adjustment.

So the strongest non-treatment predictors of response are: ECOG PS (-),
NPM1 (+), TP53 (-), complex karyotype (-), albumin (+), CRP (-),

------------------------------------------------------------
Overall conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------
1. The dominant treatment-effect signal in ds001_aml is venetoclax-
   azacitidine in unfit, NPM1-mutated patients without TP53 mutation or
   complex karyotype: response rate jumps from ~16% off-treatment to ~79%
   on-treatment (Δ ~ +0.63, OR ~ 20).
   - TP53 mutation and complex karyotype are SUPPRESSORS that abolish the
     benefit; in NPM1-wildtype unfit patients the benefit is also absent.
   - This pattern is biologically coherent (NPM1+ AML is venetoclax-
     sensitive; TP53/complex-karyotype AML is venetoclax-resistant).

2. 7+3 has, at best, a small net benefit (Δ ~ +0.01) in fit, TP53wt,
   non-complex patients. It is dwarfed by ven-aza in the unfit population.

3. The targeted therapies (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib,
   enasidenib) show essentially no detectable benefit in any subgroup
   tested, including their canonical molecular-target subgroups
   (FLT3-ITD/TKD for FLT3 inhibitors; IDH1/IDH2 for IDH inhibitors), in
   either unadjusted, multivariable-adjusted, or interaction-term analyses.
   This dataset therefore does NOT encode the classical clinical-trial
   efficacy patterns for these drugs; this is a notable negative finding.

4. Strong non-treatment prognostic predictors of objective_response:
   ECOG PS (-), NPM1 mutation (+), TP53 mutation (-), complex karyotype (-),
   albumin (+), CRP (-), and 6-month weight loss (-).

5. Age, sex, and secondary_aml have essentially no association with
   objective_response in this cohort.
