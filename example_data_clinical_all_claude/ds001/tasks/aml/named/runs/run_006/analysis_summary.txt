ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_aml (n=50,000 AML patients, EHR-derived)
==================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The dataset contains 50,000 AML patients with 35 baseline features
(demographics, ECOG, AML mutational status, labs, treatments) and a single
binary outcome `objective_response` (overall rate 18.75%). Six treatments
are recorded: midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib,
venetoclax+azacitidine (ven/aza), and 7+3 induction. Patients can carry
multiple treatment flags (likely sequential lines).

The analytic plan walked main effects → multivariable adjustment → formal
treatment-by-modifier interaction screening → progressive refinement of the
best-supported subgroup hypothesis.

MAIN EFFECTS ON OBJECTIVE RESPONSE
-----------------------------------
Binary features (chi-square):
- npm1_mutation=1: 23.46% vs 16.73% (p=5.8e-70, Δ=+6.7%)
- unfit_for_intensive=1: 22.10% vs 16.51% (p=1.9e-55, Δ=+5.6%)
- treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine=1: 21.96% vs 16.61% (p=6.6e-51, Δ=+5.4%)
- complex_karyotype=1: 16.81% vs 19.09% (p=3.3e-6, Δ=-2.3%)
- tp53_mutation=1: 16.57% vs 18.99% (p=3.5e-5, Δ=-2.4%)
- All four other targeted therapies (midostaurin, gilteritinib,
  ivosidenib, enasidenib) and 7+3 showed no main-effect difference (all
  p>0.10). FLT3-ITD, FLT3-TKD, IDH1, IDH2, secondary_aml, and sex_female
  were also not associated with response on their own.

Continuous features (univariate logistic):
- ecog_ps: -0.353 per point (p=1.6e-90) — by far the strongest performance
  predictor.
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.036 per percent (p=1.1e-30).
- blast_pct_marrow: -0.005 per percent (p=2.4e-16).
- albumin_g_dl: +0.118 per g/dL (p=2.9e-7).
- crp_mg_l: -0.006 per mg/L (p=6.1e-6).
- wbc_k_per_ul: -0.0018 per K/uL (p=1.9e-4).
- age_years: no detectable association (p=0.88) — its contribution is
  fully captured by `unfit_for_intensive` and `ecog_ps`.

MULTIVARIABLE LOGISTIC REGRESSION
----------------------------------
Adjusting for all 35 features simultaneously preserved the directions:
positive predictors npm1_mutation (+0.44), unfit_for_intensive (+0.37),
ven/aza (+0.36), albumin (+0.12); negative predictors ecog_ps (-0.36),
weight_loss (-0.037), tp53 (-0.19), complex_karyotype (-0.17),
blast_pct_marrow (-0.005), crp (-0.007), wbc (-0.002). All other treatments
remained null after adjustment. Pseudo-R² = 0.032.

TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY (FORMAL INTERACTION SCREEN)
-----------------------------------------------------------
For each of the 6 treatments, I fit a logistic regression with each of 9
binary modifiers, recording the interaction coefficient and p-value
(54 tests). After also screening continuous-feature interactions (60 tests)
and 2-way subgroup combinations, only one treatment showed strong
heterogeneity: venetoclax+azacitidine.

  - ven/aza × npm1_mutation:    +1.055, p = 5.5e-102
  - ven/aza × unfit_for_intensive: +0.883, p = 1.1e-78
  - ven/aza × tp53_mutation:    -0.409, p = 4.7e-7  (suppressor)
  - ven/aza × complex_karyotype: -0.306, p = 6.0e-6 (suppressor)

Targeted therapies that should canonically interact with their cognate
biomarker (midostaurin/gilteritinib with FLT3, ivosidenib with IDH1,
enasidenib with IDH2) showed no such interaction in this dataset:

  - midostaurin × flt3_itd: coef -0.106, p=0.19; in FLT3-ITD+ patients,
    response 18.0% on midostaurin vs 19.4% off (TE=-0.015).
  - gilteritinib × flt3_itd: coef +0.079, p=0.32; in FLT3-ITD+, 18.7% vs
    18.6% (TE=+0.002).
  - ivosidenib × idh1_mutation: coef -0.304, p=0.077; in IDH1+, 15.4% on
    ivosidenib vs 19.5% off (point estimate goes the *wrong* direction).
  - enasidenib × idh2_mutation: coef +0.039, p=0.79; in IDH2+, 19.5% vs
    19.1% (TE=+0.004).

These cognate interactions are not detectable in the cohort; the recorded
`objective_response` outcome appears insensitive to them.

DRILLING INTO THE VEN/AZA SIGNAL
---------------------------------
A 2x2 stratification by NPM1 × unfit revealed that the ven/aza effect
lives almost entirely in a single cell:

  NPM1=0, unfit=0: TE = -0.010 (n_t=8383, n_c=12533)
  NPM1=0, unfit=1: TE = +0.005 (n_t=5612, n_c=8458)
  NPM1=1, unfit=0: TE = -0.003 (n_t=3624, n_c=5404)
  NPM1=1, unfit=1: TE = +0.474 (n_t=2401, n_c=3585; 63.3% vs 16.0%)

A logistic model with the three-way ven*npm1*unfit interaction term
yielded a coefficient of +2.13 (z=20.4, p<<1e-90) for the triple
interaction; the lower-order ven, ven:npm1, ven:unfit terms were null.
This shows that the effect is *not* additive over NPM1 and unfit — it
requires both simultaneously.

REFINING WITH SUPPRESSORS (TP53, COMPLEX KARYOTYPE)
----------------------------------------------------
Within the NPM1=1 & unfit=1 cell, further stratification by TP53 and CK
showed each independently abolishes the effect:

  TP53=0, CK=0:  TE=+0.628 (n=4531; 78.6% vs 15.8%, Fisher p≈0)
  TP53=0, CK=1:  TE=+0.033 (n=818; 18.2% vs 14.9%, p=0.21)
  TP53=1, CK=0:  TE=+0.004 (n=546; 18.7% vs 18.3%)
  TP53=1, CK=1:  TE=+0.015 (n=91)

Either an unfavorable mutation (TP53) or an unfavorable cytogenetic
finding (complex karyotype) is sufficient to neutralize the response,
even on top of an otherwise favorable NPM1+/unfit profile.

FINAL FOUR-WAY SUBGROUP DEFINITION
-----------------------------------
The final best-supported treatment-effect subgroup is:

  npm1_mutation = 1   AND
  unfit_for_intensive = 1   AND
  tp53_mutation = 0   AND
  complex_karyotype = 0

This subgroup (n=4531; 9.1% of the cohort) accounts for essentially the
entirety of the ven/aza effect. Within it, response is 78.6% on ven/aza
vs 15.8% off (Δ=+0.628, subgroup-by-treatment interaction
coefficient = +2.99, p < 1e-300). Outside it, ven/aza TE = -0.003.

Each of the four predicates is non-dispensable: violating any one drops
the treatment effect to ≈0 (NPM1=0 violation: TE=+0.002; unfit=0:
TE=-0.004; TP53=1: TE=+0.004; CK=1: TE=+0.033).

Within the favorable subgroup, none of the other five treatments
(midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, 7+3) shows any
meaningful effect (TEs between -0.019 and +0.059), so the ven/aza
signal cannot be explained by treatment confounding.

CONCLUSIONS
-----------
1. The strongest baseline predictors of objective_response are NPM1
   mutation, fitness/ECOG, performance/cachexia/inflammation markers
   (albumin, weight loss, CRP, blast %), and adverse genetics (TP53,
   complex karyotype). Age per se carries no signal once fitness is
   accounted for.

2. Of the six treatments recorded, only venetoclax+azacitidine has a
   clear effect on objective_response, and that effect is highly
   localized: it requires patients to be NPM1-mutated, unfit for
   intensive therapy, AND free of TP53 mutation and complex karyotype.
   In this 9% subgroup the response rate is approximately 5x baseline
   (~79% vs ~16%).

3. The cognate biomarker-targeted therapies (FLT3 inhibitors midostaurin
   and gilteritinib; IDH inhibitors ivosidenib and enasidenib) do not
   demonstrate enhanced response in their respective biomarker-positive
   populations in this dataset. This may reflect that
   `objective_response` is a short-term endpoint that under-captures
   their clinical benefit, or that the cohort's targeted-therapy
   exposure is insufficiently differentiated from the standard arm
   to reveal the effect.

4. 7+3 induction shows no detectable response benefit overall and a
   modestly negative interaction with complex karyotype (coef -0.21,
   p=0.0015), which is consistent with the well-known refractoriness of
   adverse-cytogenetic AML to intensive chemotherapy — though without a
   main effect of 7+3 the practical interpretation is limited.

5. The clean four-way subgroup result (NPM1+, unfit, TP53-, CK-) is
   biologically coherent: it mirrors the patient population in which
   ven/aza was originally developed (older/unfit, NPM1-mutant) and
   matches the well-described resistance phenotypes (TP53,
   complex/monosomal karyotype) reported in the trial literature.
