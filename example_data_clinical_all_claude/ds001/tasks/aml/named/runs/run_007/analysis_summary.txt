ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_aml (N=50,000; outcome: objective_response, overall ORR=18.75%)
=========================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Across 25 iterations I moved from main-effect screening (iterations 1-4) through
targeted treatment-mutation interactions (5-8), to broad subgroup heterogeneity
exploration (9-16), a systematic interaction screen (17-21), and finally to
deep characterization of the dominant treatment-effect subgroup (22-25).

The single most important finding is that the marginal benefit of
treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine on objective_response is entirely driven by
one tightly-defined four-feature subgroup — and that no other treatment in the
panel shows a robust, biologically expected responder subgroup on this outcome.


1. PROGNOSTIC MAIN EFFECTS (iterations 1-2)
-------------------------------------------
Strong, statistically robust main-effect predictors of higher ORR:
  - npm1_mutation=1: ORR 23.46% vs 16.73% (p=5.8e-70)
  - unfit_for_intensive=1: ORR 22.10% vs 16.51% (p=1.9e-55) — confounded by ven+aza assignment
  - ECOG 0 > 1 > 2: 23.10% / 17.43% / 12.91% (p=1.8e-90)
  - higher albumin_g_dl (responders 3.82 vs non 3.80; p=2.7e-7)

Strong negative predictors:
  - tp53_mutation=1: 16.57% vs 18.99% (p=3.5e-5)
  - complex_karyotype=1: 16.81% vs 19.09% (p=3.3e-6)
  - higher wbc_k_per_ul (p=1.5e-4), blast_pct_marrow (p=2e-16),
    weight_loss_pct_6mo (p=1.7e-33), crp_mg_l (p=1.6e-6)

Null on objective_response (no main effect): age_years, sex_female, secondary_aml,
ldh_u_l, nlr, hemoglobin_g_dl, AST/ALT/bilirubin, creatinine/BUN, sodium/potassium/calcium,
flt3_itd, flt3_tkd, idh1_mutation, idh2_mutation.

A multivariable logistic regression (iteration 4) confirms all of the above:
adjusted betas for npm1 (+0.44), tp53 (-0.19), complex_karyotype (-0.17), ecog_ps (-0.36),
unfit_for_intensive (+0.37), albumin (+0.12), weight_loss (-0.04), crp (-0.007),
wbc (-0.002), blast% (-0.005) all reach p<1e-4 or better.


2. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS (iteration 3)
---------------------------------------
Only treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine shows a meaningful marginal main effect:
  - ven+aza: ORR 21.96% vs 16.61% (diff +0.054, p=6.6e-51)
  - 7+3:           18.89% vs 18.63%, p=0.46
  - midostaurin:   18.57% vs 18.78%, p=0.67
  - gilteritinib:  18.13% vs 18.86%, p=0.14
  - ivosidenib:    18.82% vs 18.74%, p=0.93
  - enasidenib:    18.58% vs 18.76%, p=0.79

After multivariable adjustment the four targeted agents and 7+3 remain null
(all |beta|<0.05, p>0.13), while ven+aza adjusted beta=+0.36 (p<1e-50).


3. TARGETED TREATMENT-MUTATION INTERACTIONS (iterations 5-8)
------------------------------------------------------------
The clinically expected matches all FAIL on objective_response in this cohort:

  - midostaurin × flt3_itd: interaction beta=-0.11, p=0.19. In flt3_itd+
    patients midostaurin recipients had ORR 17.6% vs 19.1% controls (sign
    OPPOSITE to expected); not significant.
  - midostaurin × flt3_tkd: beta=-0.02, p=0.87. No effect.
  - gilteritinib × flt3_itd: beta=+0.08, p=0.32. In flt3_itd+ stratified diff
    only +0.002. No effect.
  - gilteritinib × flt3_tkd: beta=-0.21, p=0.16 (n=418/2397). Trend opposite
    to expected; not significant.
  - ivosidenib × idh1_mutation: beta=-0.30, p=0.077. In idh1+ ivosidenib
    recipients had 15.4% vs 19.4% controls — sign OPPOSITE to expected; not sig.
  - enasidenib × idh2_mutation: beta=+0.04, p=0.79. No effect.

So in this dataset the "biomarker-matched" agents do not deliver detectable
ORR benefit in their cognate mutation subgroups.


4. DISCOVERED RESPONDER SUBGROUP FOR VEN+AZA (iterations 9-16, 22-25)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The ven+aza main effect is driven by a strong ven+aza × unfit_for_intensive
interaction (beta=+0.88, p=1.1e-78):
  - In unfit: ven+aza ORR 30.81% vs 16.30% (diff +0.145, p=7e-130)
  - In fit:   ven+aza ORR 16.05% vs 16.81% (diff -0.008, p=0.08)

Within unfit_for_intensive=1, three additional features powerfully modify the
treatment effect:

  (a) npm1_mutation:
        unfit & npm1+:  ven+aza ORR 63.31% vs 15.96%   diff +0.474, p<1e-300
        unfit & npm1-:  ven+aza ORR 16.91% vs 16.45%   diff +0.005, p=0.48
      → npm1+ is essentially a NECESSARY condition for ven+aza response within
        unfit. The 3-way ven+aza × unfit × npm1 interaction is +2.16, p=2e-94.

  (b) tp53_mutation:
        unfit & tp53-:  diff +0.162, p=1.4e-142
        unfit & tp53+:  diff +0.004, p=0.87
      → tp53 mutation completely abolishes ven+aza response.

  (c) complex_karyotype:
        unfit & ck-:    diff +0.167, p=9e-142
        unfit & ck+:    diff +0.023, p=0.11
      → complex karyotype attenuates response to a small non-significant
        difference.

When all four conditions are imposed jointly:

  unfit_for_intensive=1 AND npm1_mutation=1 AND tp53_mutation=0 AND
  complex_karyotype=0  (n=4,531):

  ven+aza ORR = 78.60%   vs   control ORR = 15.83%
  Absolute diff = +0.628 (62.8 percentage points), p<1e-300
  Within-subgroup multivariable-adjusted OR for ven+aza = 21.4 (p<1e-100)

The COMPLEMENT of this subgroup (n=45,469): ven+aza ORR 16.40% vs 16.69%
control, diff -0.003, p=0.42.  → The entire marginal ven+aza benefit observed
in the cohort is concentrated in this 4-feature subgroup; outside it the drug
is null.

Within the responder core, the effect persists across ECOG strata:
  ECOG 0: diff +0.665 (n=1,542), ECOG 1: +0.619 (n=2,310), ECOG 2: +0.570 (n=679);
all p<1e-50.

Counter-checks reinforce that all four conditions are necessary:
  - fit & npm1+ (any cytogenetics):     ven+aza diff -0.003, p=0.73
  - fit & npm1+ & tp53- & ck-:           ven+aza diff -0.004, p=0.70
  - unfit & npm1+ & tp53+ (any ck):     ven+aza diff +0.006, p=0.94
  - unfit & npm1+ & ck+ (any tp53):     ven+aza diff +0.032, p=0.24

Adjusted multivariable model with the 3-way ven+aza × unfit × npm1 plus
ven+aza:tp53 and ven+aza:complex_karyotype terms (controlling for ecog,
age, sex, wbc, blast%, albumin, weight loss, crp): the 3-way interaction
remains +2.20 (p=2e-96); ven+aza:tp53 = -0.56 (p=6e-11); ven+aza:complex_karyotype
= -0.39 (p=4e-8). All three interactions are independently significant.


5. SYSTEMATIC INTERACTION SCREEN (iterations 17-21)
---------------------------------------------------
Across 60 treatment × binary-feature pairs and 108 treatment × continuous-feature
pairs:

The only interactions with |beta|>0.2 and p<1e-3 are the four ven+aza
interactions (with unfit, npm1, tp53, complex_karyotype) plus two smaller
signals:

  - enasidenib × sex_female: beta=+0.31, p=2.2e-4. Stratified: enasidenib
    OR-favorable in females (+0.025 ORR diff, p=0.012), unfavorable in males
    (-0.023, p=0.008). The signal is NOT mediated by IDH2 status (within
    idh2+ stratum the directional pattern persists but neither sex stratum
    is significant), suggesting an unexplained sex effect rather than a
    biologically driven mechanism. Treated as a hypothesis-generating
    finding given the multiple-testing burden.

  - 7+3 × complex_karyotype: beta=-0.21, p=1.5e-3. ck=0: 7+3 diff +0.007
    (p=0.06); ck=1: 7+3 diff -0.023 (p=0.008). Consistent with the clinical
    intuition that intensive chemo is less effective in adverse cytogenetics;
    effect size small.

Continuous-feature interactions (10 nominally significant; smallest p=0.001
for gilteritinib×alkphos and enasidenib×hemoglobin) do not survive a
Bonferroni adjustment over ~108 tests, and no continuous interaction has
|beta|>0.15. I interpret these as noise.

A modest negative ivosidenib × npm1 trend (beta=-0.20, p=0.028) was seen in
the screen but not robust to multiple comparisons.


6. FINAL CONCLUSIONS — TREATMENT–BIOMARKER–OUTCOME RELATIONSHIPS
----------------------------------------------------------------
Best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis (objective_response):

  treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine increases ORR ONLY in the subgroup defined
  by all four predicates simultaneously:
        unfit_for_intensive = 1
        npm1_mutation       = 1
        tp53_mutation       = 0
        complex_karyotype   = 0

  Within that subgroup the absolute ORR improvement is approximately +0.63
  (78.6% vs 15.8%); outside it the drug effect is approximately zero. The
  heterogeneity is robust across ECOG strata and survives multivariable
  adjustment for age, sex, ecog_ps, baseline labs, and other comorbidity
  markers. Both tp53 mutation and complex karyotype individually act as
  "off-switches" — their unfavorable values suppress what would otherwise be
  the very large ven+aza response in unfit, npm1-mutated patients. NPM1
  mutation appears necessary for response, and unfit-for-intensive labeling
  appears necessary as well (perhaps because fit patients are crossing over
  to/being managed with intensive chemotherapy concurrently, or because the
  dataset's "fit" patients have an underlying biology that does not respond
  to ven+aza in this cohort).

Other best-supported (weaker) findings:

  - treatment_7plus3 × complex_karyotype: 7+3 modestly underperforms in
    complex karyotype (interaction beta=-0.21, p=0.0015), with a small
    absolute decrement in ORR.

  - The four targeted agents (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib,
    enasidenib) show NO biomarker-matched ORR benefit in this dataset.
    The expected FLT3- and IDH-targeted directional effects are not
    observed; some point estimates are nominally in the unfavorable
    direction, but none reach significance after correction.

  - Strong baseline prognostic features (independent of treatment): npm1+
    (favorable), tp53/complex karyotype (unfavorable), high ecog (unfavorable),
    high blast% / wbc (unfavorable), low albumin / high weight loss / high
    crp (unfavorable, all consistent with poor performance status).

In summary: the cohort behaves as if a single drug (venetoclax+azacitidine) has a
dramatic, biology-specific effect — restricted to NPM1-mutated AML in patients
labeled unfit for intensive therapy WITHOUT TP53 mutation or complex karyotype —
while the targeted small-molecule agents are inert on objective response in
this dataset. This pattern is consistent with the ven+aza clinical profile in
real-world AML, and the suppression by TP53 and complex karyotype matches the
clinical intuition that these high-risk genomic features blunt response to
hypomethylating-agent backbones.
