Analysis summary — ds001_aml (n=50,000 AML patients, single binary outcome `objective_response`,
overall ORR = 18.75%)

================================================================================
1.  Cohort and outcome overview
================================================================================
The cohort contained 50,000 AML patients with 35 features (demographics,
performance status, fitness, cytogenetics, common AML mutations, baseline labs)
and six binary treatment indicators (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib,
enasidenib, venetoclax+azacitidine, 7+3). Treatments are not mutually exclusive
— most patients receive 1-2 treatments and 20% receive ≥3. The outcome is a
single binary `objective_response` (ORR 18.75% overall).

================================================================================
2.  Main effects on objective_response
================================================================================
Univariable and multivariable logistic regression identified the following main
effects on probability of objective response:

   Decreasing ORR
     • ecog_ps (univ. β=-0.353, p=1.6e-90; ORR 23.1% / 17.4% / 12.9% at ECOG 0/1/2)
     • complex_karyotype=1 (β=-0.155, p=3.1e-6)
     • tp53_mutation=1 (β=-0.166, p=3.3e-5)
     • blast_pct_marrow (β=-0.0049 per %, p=2.4e-16)
     • wbc_k_per_ul (β=-0.0019, p<0.001)

   Increasing ORR
     • npm1_mutation=1 (β=+0.423, p=1.8e-69)
     • albumin_g_dl (β=+0.117, p=2.9e-7)
     • unfit_for_intensive=1 (β=+0.373, p<0.001 in adjusted model — but on closer
       inspection this is entirely a downstream consequence of channeling: in
       patients NOT receiving venetoclax+azacitidine, fit and unfit groups have
       essentially identical ORR (~16-17%); the apparent "unfit advantage"
       arises only because unfit patients disproportionately receive VEN+AZA,
       which is the active subgroup-effect treatment.)

   No detectable independent main effect
     • age_years, sex_female, secondary_aml
     • flt3_itd, flt3_tkd, idh1_mutation, idh2_mutation
     • ldh_u_l, alkaline_phosphatase, AST, ALT, total bilirubin, creatinine,
       BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium

================================================================================
3.  Marginal treatment effects on objective_response
================================================================================
Of the six treatment indicators, only one had a marginal main effect on ORR:

     treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine:  ORR 22.0% on vs 16.6% off (Δ=+5.4 pp)
                                        univariable β=+0.346, p=1.0e-50

The other five had null or near-null marginal effects:

     treatment_7plus3                  Δ=+0.3 pp  (p≈0.45)
     treatment_midostaurin             Δ=-0.2 pp  (p≈0.66)
     treatment_gilteritinib            Δ=-0.7 pp  (p≈0.13)
     treatment_ivosidenib              Δ=+0.1 pp  (p≈0.91)
     treatment_enasidenib              Δ=-0.2 pp  (p≈0.74)

Importantly, this null-effect picture for the targeted agents persists in their
"natural" biomarker-positive subgroups:

     midostaurin in FLT3-ITD+:        Δ=-1.5 pp (interaction p=0.19, NS)
     gilteritinib in FLT3-ITD+:       Δ=+0.2 pp (interaction p=0.32, NS)
     enasidenib in IDH2+:             Δ=+0.4 pp (interaction p=0.79, NS)
     ivosidenib in IDH1+:             Δ=-4.0 pp (interaction p=0.077, OPPOSITE
                                                 to the prior expectation)

These hypotheses (treatment-by-target-mutation positive interactions for
midostaurin/gilteritinib×FLT3, ivosidenib×IDH1, enasidenib×IDH2) are NOT
supported by the data; ivosidenib in IDH1+ even trends in the wrong direction.

================================================================================
4.  Systematic treatment-effect heterogeneity search
================================================================================
A full screen of 6 treatments × 29 candidate effect modifiers (174 logistic
interaction tests; Bonferroni threshold p≈2.9e-4) ranked the strongest
heterogeneity signals as follows (all on outcome objective_response):

     1. VEN+AZA × npm1_mutation        β=+1.055   p=5.5e-102   ★ huge
     2. VEN+AZA × unfit_for_intensive  β=+0.883   p=1.1e-78    ★ huge
     3. VEN+AZA × tp53_mutation        β=-0.409   p=4.7e-7     suppressor
     4. VEN+AZA × complex_karyotype    β=-0.306   p=6.0e-6     suppressor
     5. enasidenib × sex_female        β=+0.314   p=2.2e-4     small/modest
     6. 7+3 × complex_karyotype        β=-0.213   p=1.5e-3     small (does not
                                                                pass Bonferroni)

All other interactions had p>0.01 and absolute effect-modification differences
<0.05.

================================================================================
5.  Refined VEN+AZA subgroup definition
================================================================================
Stratifying VEN+AZA effect across the four positive/negative modifiers
(NPM1, unfit-for-intensive, TP53, complex karyotype) revealed a sharply
multiplicative pattern:

     subgroup                                       n      Δ ORR
     ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
     all patients                                 50,000   +0.054
     NPM1+                                        15,014   +0.187
     NPM1-                                        34,986   -0.004   (null)
     unfit                                        20,056   +0.145
     fit                                          29,944   -0.008   (null)
     NPM1+ & unfit                                 5,986   +0.474
     NPM1+ & fit                                   9,028   -0.003   (null)
     NPM1- & unfit                                14,070   +0.005   (null)
     NPM1+ & unfit & TP53-                         5,349   +0.534
     NPM1+ & unfit & TP53- & complex_kar-          4,531   +0.628 ★

The four-predicate subgroup
     npm1_mutation=1  AND
     unfit_for_intensive=1  AND
     tp53_mutation=0  AND
     complex_karyotype=0
contains 4,531 patients. Within this subgroup the ORR rises from 15.8% off
VEN+AZA to 78.6% on VEN+AZA — an absolute risk difference of +62.8 percentage
points (chi-square test p<<1e-300). Outside this subgroup (n=45,469) the
on/off ORRs are 16.4% vs 16.7% (Δ=-0.3 pp, p=0.51).

In an adjusted multivariable logistic regression including age, sex, ECOG,
secondary AML, fitness, all common AML mutations, complex karyotype, and lab
values, the indicator (NPM1+ & unfit & TP53- & ck-) × VEN+AZA interaction
remains β=+3.04 (OR ≈ 21; p=3.3e-296), and the residual VEN+AZA main effect
outside this subgroup is null (β=-0.017, p=0.51). The 4-predicate definition
captures essentially the entire VEN+AZA benefit in the cohort.

An exhaustive enumeration of 3^10 = 59,049 binary-feature combinations across
the 10 binary patient features (with sample-size guards) confirmed that every
top-15 highest-effect VEN+AZA subgroup contains all four predicates
(NPM1+, unfit, TP53-, complex_karyotype-). Adding further restrictions (e.g.
sex_female=1, idh2=0, secondary_aml=0) yields slightly larger deltas (up to
+0.677) at smaller n but the parsimonious 4-predicate definition is the
dominant subgroup.

================================================================================
6.  Other treatments — heterogeneity searches
================================================================================
For each of the other five treatments the strongest modifier was:

     treatment_midostaurin       best p=0.15   (secondary_aml)   no signal
     treatment_gilteritinib      best p=0.04   (unfit_for_intensive) weak
     treatment_ivosidenib        best p=0.03   (npm1_mutation, NEG)  weak
     treatment_enasidenib        best p=2e-4   (sex_female, POS)     small
     treatment_7plus3            best p=0.0015 (complex_karyotype, NEG) small

The clinically directional hypotheses for the targeted agents (midostaurin
and gilteritinib in FLT3+, ivosidenib in IDH1+, enasidenib in IDH2+) are NOT
supported. The only non-VEN+AZA interaction that survives Bonferroni
correction (174 tests) is treatment_enasidenib × sex_female, but its absolute
magnitude is small: female patients gain about +2.5 pp in ORR on enasidenib
(21.2% vs 18.7%), male patients lose about -2.3 pp (16.5% vs 18.8%). Adjusted
interaction β=+0.31 (p<0.001). The 7+3 × complex karyotype interaction is
also directional (complex karyotype patients lose ~2.3 pp ORR on 7+3) but
the effect is small and does not pass strict multiple-testing correction.

================================================================================
7.  Hypotheses supported vs refuted
================================================================================
Supported
   • Higher ECOG, complex karyotype, TP53 mutation, marrow blast %, and WBC
     each independently reduce ORR.
   • NPM1 mutation and serum albumin each independently raise ORR.
   • VEN+AZA raises ORR overall, dominantly through a strong subgroup effect.
   • VEN+AZA effect is positively modified by NPM1+ and by unfit-for-intensive,
     and negatively modified by TP53+ and complex karyotype.
   • The full 4-predicate joint definition (NPM1+ & unfit & TP53- & ck-)
     captures essentially all of the VEN+AZA benefit in the cohort.
   • Enasidenib × sex_female interaction is real and statistically robust
     (small absolute effect; female benefit, male anti-effect).
   • 7+3 × complex_karyotype: small negative interaction.

Refuted / not supported
   • Treatment-by-mutation pairings for midostaurin × FLT3-ITD,
     gilteritinib × FLT3-ITD or FLT3-TKD, enasidenib × IDH2 — none reach
     significance and absolute effect-modification differences are <2 pp.
   • Ivosidenib × IDH1: trends in the OPPOSITE direction to the clinical
     prior; ivosidenib does not raise ORR in IDH1+ patients in this cohort.
   • Marginal main effects of midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib,
     enasidenib, and 7+3: all null.
   • Age and sex have no independent main effect on ORR.

================================================================================
8.  Final treatment-effect heterogeneity hypotheses
================================================================================
Primary final hypothesis (treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine):
   In patients who are simultaneously NPM1-mutated, unfit-for-intensive
   chemotherapy, TP53-wild-type, and lacking complex karyotype, treatment
   with venetoclax + azacitidine raises objective_response from approximately
   16% to approximately 79% (absolute risk difference ≈ +63 percentage
   points; OR ≈ 21 on the multiplicative scale). Outside this 4-predicate
   subgroup, treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine has no detectable effect on
   objective_response. The TP53- and complex_karyotype- predicates are the
   suppressor variables: their unfavorable values (TP53+ or complex karyotype
   present) abolish the otherwise large VEN+AZA benefit.

Secondary final hypothesis (treatment_enasidenib):
   Treatment with enasidenib slightly raises objective_response in female
   patients (ORR 21.2% vs 18.7%, Δ ≈ +2.5 pp) and slightly lowers it in male
   patients (ORR 16.5% vs 18.8%, Δ ≈ -2.3 pp). The effect is statistically
   significant after adjustment but small in absolute magnitude, and is not
   modified appreciably by IDH1 or IDH2 mutation status.

Tertiary final hypothesis (treatment_7plus3):
   In patients with complex karyotype, 7+3 modestly reduces objective_response
   (≈ -2.3 pp); in non-complex-karyotype patients the effect is null. This
   interaction is detectable (p≈0.0015) but does not survive strict Bonferroni
   correction across the 174-test interaction screen and is small in absolute
   terms.

For midostaurin, gilteritinib, and ivosidenib no subgroup of meaningful
treatment-effect heterogeneity was identified in this dataset.

================================================================================
9.  Overall conclusions
================================================================================
The dominant signal in this AML cohort is a sharply concentrated subgroup
benefit for venetoclax + azacitidine, defined jointly by NPM1 mutation,
clinical unfitness for intensive chemotherapy, absence of TP53 mutation, and
absence of complex karyotype. The marginal "5 percentage-point" benefit of
VEN+AZA in the whole cohort is entirely driven by a ~63 percentage-point
benefit in this 9% subgroup. The other commonly-used AML therapies in this
cohort (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, 7+3) show no
clinically meaningful main effect and only modest, mostly small heterogeneity,
including a small enasidenib-female advantage and a small 7+3 detriment in
complex-karyotype patients. The classic targeted-therapy biomarker pairings
(midostaurin/gilteritinib × FLT3, ivosidenib × IDH1, enasidenib × IDH2) are
NOT recapitulated by these data — most show null interactions and ivosidenib
× IDH1 trends in the opposite direction to the clinical prior.
