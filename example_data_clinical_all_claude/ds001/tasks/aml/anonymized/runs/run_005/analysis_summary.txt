Analysis Summary — ds001_aml (50,000 AML patients, 35 de-identified features, binary outcome objective_response)
================================================================================

Overall outcome
---------------
The cohort objective_response rate is 9,375/50,000 = 0.1875. Roughly one in five patients respond. The analysis explored which features predict response and, more importantly, whether response is concentrated in an identifiable subgroup of patients consistent with a treatment-by-biomarker interaction.

Iterations 1-4: univariate and multivariable main effects
---------------------------------------------------------
The univariate screen on all 35 features identified eleven features with strong (p < 1e-4) main-effect associations with objective_response and >20 features with no detectable association.

Strongest associations (sign indicates direction relative to higher response):
- feature_004 (3-level): rates 0.231 / 0.174 / 0.129 by level (p = 2e-90), the strongest single-feature prognostic effect; behaves like a performance-status / disease-severity index.
- feature_014 (binary, 30% positive): rate 0.235 vs 0.167 (p = 6e-70).
- feature_001 (binary, 40% positive): rate 0.221 vs 0.165 (p = 2e-55).
- feature_021 (binary, 40% positive): rate 0.220 vs 0.166 (p = 7e-51).
- feature_020 (continuous, 0-23): higher = lower response (p = 2e-33).
- feature_024 (continuous, 20-100): higher = lower response (p = 2e-16).
- feature_029 (continuous, 1.7-5.5): higher = HIGHER response (p = 3e-7).
- feature_027 (continuous): higher = lower response (p = 2e-6).
- feature_026 (binary, 15% positive): rate 0.168 vs 0.191 (p = 3e-6); =1 is unfavorable.
- feature_009 (binary, 10% positive): rate 0.166 vs 0.190 (p = 4e-5); =1 is unfavorable.
- feature_022 (continuous): higher = lower response (p = 1e-4).

A multivariable logistic regression with all 35 features confirmed independent contributions of these eleven, with pseudo-R² = 0.032 — note that this modest fit hinted that the strongest signal was NOT additive across these features, motivating the subgroup analyses below. Many other features (feature_011 likely age; feature_010 likely sex; feature_005, feature_023, feature_017, feature_002, feature_003, feature_012, feature_018, feature_032, feature_006, feature_008, feature_015, feature_016, feature_019, feature_025, feature_030, feature_034, feature_035) showed no detectable association with response.

Iterations 5-7: discovery of a three-way binary interaction
-----------------------------------------------------------
feature_001, feature_014, and feature_021 are mutually independent (pairwise correlation < 0.005), and their joint distribution exactly matches the product of marginals (40% × 30% × 40% ≈ 4.8% triple-positive; observed 2,401/50,000 = 4.8%). Despite each having a meaningful univariate effect, NONE of the three pairwise interactions among them is significant on its own.

In contrast, the three-way interaction feature_001 × feature_014 × feature_021 has logit coefficient +2.13 (OR ≈ 8.4, p = 7e-92). The cell-level structure makes this concrete:
- (0,0,0): rate 0.172
- (0,0,1): 0.162
- (0,1,0): 0.160
- (0,1,1): 0.157
- (1,0,0): 0.164
- (1,0,1): 0.169
- (1,1,0): 0.160
- (1,1,1): 0.633 (n = 2,401)

Seven of the eight cells sit at baseline (~16%); only the triple-positive cell shows a dramatic elevation to 63%. Pure higher-order, non-additive structure.

Iterations 8-10: discovery of two resistance markers
----------------------------------------------------
Treating feature_021 (or symmetrically feature_001 or feature_014) as a candidate treatment indicator, the treatment-by-feature interaction screen flagged two further significant negative modifiers:
- feature_009: interaction coef -0.41 (p = 5e-7)
- feature_026: interaction coef -0.31 (p = 6e-6)

Stratifying within the triple-positive subgroup confirmed that BOTH function as resistance markers:
- (009=0, 026=0): response 0.786 (n = 1,790)
- (009=0, 026=1): response 0.182 (n = 335)
- (009=1, 026=0): response 0.187 (n = 235)
- (009=1, 026=1): response 0.195 (n = 41)

Either resistance marker, on its own, abolishes the dramatic response benefit. This is the "missing" piece needed to define the full responder subgroup.

Iterations 11-12: the full five-feature subgroup
------------------------------------------------
The full predictive subgroup is therefore defined by:

    feature_001 = 1  AND  feature_014 = 1  AND  feature_021 = 1
    AND feature_009 = 0  AND  feature_026 = 0

This subgroup contains 1,790 patients (3.58% of the cohort) and has a response rate of 0.786 vs 0.165 in the rest of the cohort — an absolute difference of +0.621 and a crude OR of 18.5. After adjusting for the strongest prognostic continuous covariates (feature_004, feature_020, feature_024, feature_022, feature_027, feature_029), the subgroup's adjusted log-odds coefficient is +3.00 (OR ≈ 20, z = 50.2, p essentially zero).

Iterations 13-14: treatment-effect interpretation and symmetry
--------------------------------------------------------------
If feature_021 is interpreted as the treatment indicator, the treatment effect is essentially zero in the biomarker-ineligible 91% of the cohort (rate(tx=1) = 0.164 vs rate(tx=0) = 0.167; diff = -0.003) and approximately +0.628 in absolute response rate within the biomarker-eligible 9% (rate(tx=1) = 0.786 vs rate(tx=0) = 0.158). The treatment-by-subgroup interaction in a logistic model with adjustment is +3.06 (p < 1e-200), with the treatment main effect non-significant (p = 0.54).

Importantly, the structure is symmetric: re-running the interaction screen with feature_001 or feature_014 as the candidate "treatment" yields the same dominant interactions and the same predictive subgroup. The dataset alone cannot tell us which of the three is the drug indicator vs. the biomarker — but it is unambiguous that all three must be jointly in the favorable state, AND both feature_009 and feature_026 must be absent, for the response benefit to manifest.

Iterations 15-17: testing whether other features further modify the effect
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
Within the biomarker-eligible (001=1, 014=1, 009=0, 026=0) subgroup the treatment effect of feature_021 attenuates with feature_004 (from +0.665 at level 0, to +0.619 at level 1, to +0.570 at level 2) but remains very large at every level. Stratifying by feature_020 above/below median produced nearly identical effects (+0.636 vs +0.621), supporting feature_020 as a prognostic but NOT a predictive marker. The continuous-feature interaction screen produced p-values no smaller than 0.0035, none surviving Bonferroni correction. No continuous feature meaningfully modifies the treatment effect once the binary subgroup is accounted for.

Iterations 18-20: independent confirmations
-------------------------------------------
1. Exhaustive enumeration of all four-binary combinations (16 binaries × C(16,4) × 16 polarity patterns) with n ≥ 200 found that EVERY high-response subgroup (rate > 0.5) contains the (feature_001=1, feature_014=1, feature_021=1) core. Adding feature_026=0 or feature_009=0 to the core boosts rate from 0.633 to 0.69-0.72; jointly adding both pushes it to 0.786.
2. The relationship between number of "favorable" values out of (feature_001=1, feature_014=1, feature_021=1, feature_009=0, feature_026=0) and response is a pure AND-gate: rates are 0.161, 0.167, 0.170, 0.162, 0.163 for 0-4 favorable values, then jump to 0.786 for 5/5. There is no monotone dose-response; only complete co-occurrence triggers benefit.
3. Random split-half validation (25,000 / 25,000): half 1 subgroup rate 0.771 vs complement 0.167; half 2 subgroup rate 0.802 vs complement 0.164. The pattern is highly reproducible and not an artifact of one cell.

Iterations 21-25: closure and prognostic main effects
-----------------------------------------------------
After accounting for the predictive subgroup, the residual prognostic effects are: feature_004 (per-level log-OR ≈ -0.39), feature_020 (per-unit log-OR ≈ -0.04), feature_024 (-0.006), feature_022 (-0.002), feature_027 (-0.007), and feature_029 (+0.13). All independent of subgroup membership and do not change which patients benefit from treatment.

The most favorable single cell (low feature_004 = 0 within the biomarker-eligible AND treated subgroup) reaches a 0.859 response rate.

Final supported hypothesis (treatment-effect heterogeneity)
-----------------------------------------------------------
Treating feature_021 as the candidate treatment indicator, the best-supported subgroup hypothesis is:

    feature_021 increases objective_response from ~0.158 to ~0.786 (absolute difference +0.628; adjusted log-OR +3.06 with treatment-by-subgroup interaction p < 1e-200)
    SPECIFICALLY in patients satisfying ALL of:
        feature_001 = 1
        feature_014 = 1
        feature_009 = 0   (this resistance marker must be absent)
        feature_026 = 0   (this resistance marker must be absent)
    Outside this subgroup, the feature_021 treatment effect is essentially zero (-0.003, p = 0.42).

The same subgroup is symmetric in feature_001 / feature_014 / feature_021 — i.e., the data alone cannot disambiguate which of the three is "the treatment", but the responder subgroup is the same.

Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
--------------------------------
SUPPORTED:
- Strong prognostic main effects of feature_004, feature_020, feature_024, feature_022, feature_027, feature_029 (h2-h4, h7, h27).
- Strong univariate main effects of feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 (h5).
- Independence of feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 (h9).
- Three-way interaction among feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 (h11).
- feature_009 and feature_026 act as resistance markers that suppress treatment benefit (h12, h13, h14, h15).
- Five-feature subgroup confers ~+0.62 absolute response benefit (h16, h25).
- Subgroup effect survives prognostic adjustment (h17).
- Treatment effect concentrated in biomarker-eligible subgroup, ~zero outside it (h18, h26).
- AND-gate / threshold structure (h24).
- Reproducibility in split-halves (h25).
- Final adjusted treatment-by-subgroup hypothesis (h28).

REFUTED / NOT SUPPORTED:
- Pure pairwise interactions among feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 (h10) — non-significant once 3-way term included.
- Modification of treatment effect by continuous prognostic markers (h21, h22) — none survived multiple-comparison correction.
- The "treatment" feature_021 acting on biomarker-negative patients (h26) — no effect in 91% of cohort.

Overall conclusion
------------------
This AML cohort displays a pattern characteristic of a precision-oncology drug paired with a composite predictive biomarker: response is approximately 0.16 in the great majority of patients regardless of treatment status, but jumps to ~0.79 in a small (~3.6% of cohort), molecularly defined subgroup characterized by joint positivity for two biomarkers (feature_001, feature_014) plus assignment to treatment (feature_021) AND absence of two resistance markers (feature_009 = 0, feature_026 = 0). Outside this subgroup the candidate treatment indicator has no detectable effect on response. Independent prognostic effects are dominated by a 3-level severity feature (feature_004), with smaller contributions from several continuous laboratory-like features (feature_020, feature_024, feature_022, feature_027, feature_029). No additional important higher-order interactions were identified.
