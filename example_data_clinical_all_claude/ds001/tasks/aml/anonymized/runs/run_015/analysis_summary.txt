Oncology Dataset Analysis — ds001_aml (50,000 patients, single binary outcome objective_response, mean = 0.1875)
================================================================================================

OVERVIEW

Across 25 iterations, I examined main effects, multivariable adjustments, pairwise and higher-order
interactions, and treatment-effect heterogeneity for the binary outcome objective_response in this
de-identified AML cohort with 35 anonymized features (16 binary, 1 ternary, 18 continuous). The
analysis converged on a well-defined 5-way responder subgroup that accounts for essentially the
entire structured signal in this cohort beyond ordinary prognostic effects.

================================================================================================
1. UNIVARIATE MAIN EFFECTS (iterations 1-3)
================================================================================================

Binary features. Three binary features showed strong positive univariate associations:
    feature_014 (+6.7 pp; chi2 p=5.8e-70)
    feature_001 (+5.6 pp; p=1.9e-55)
    feature_021 (+5.4 pp; p=6.6e-51)
Two showed small but significant negative associations:
    feature_026 (-2.3 pp; p=3.3e-6)
    feature_009 (-2.4 pp; p=3.5e-5)
All other binary features (feature_005, feature_006, feature_008, feature_010, feature_015,
feature_016, feature_018, feature_019, feature_025, feature_030, feature_032) had NS univariate
effects. — HYPOTHESIS h1 SUPPORTED for feature_014/001/021/026/009; refuted for the rest.

Ternary feature_004. Strongly monotonic decrease in response: 0->23.1%, 1->17.4%, 2->12.9%
(p=1.8e-90). Behaves as a graded prognostic indicator. — h2 SUPPORTED.

Continuous features. Significant predictors of response (univariate logistic, std coefficient):
    feature_020 b=-0.138 (p=1e-30)   feature_024 b=-0.094 (p=2e-16)
    feature_027 b=-0.059 (p=6e-6)   feature_022 b=-0.046 (p=2e-4)
    feature_029 b=+0.059 (p=3e-7)   feature_007 b=+0.022 (p=0.05)
Other continuous features (feature_011, feature_012, feature_013, feature_017, feature_023,
feature_028, feature_031, feature_033, feature_034, feature_035, feature_002, feature_003) showed
no detectable main effect. — h3 partially SUPPORTED (only ~6 of 18 continuous features matter).

================================================================================================
2. MULTIVARIABLE ADJUSTMENT (iteration 4)
================================================================================================

Adjusted (all-features logistic regression) effects matched univariate, suggesting little
confounding among the predictors:
    feature_004=2:  -0.726  feature_014:    +0.435  feature_001: +0.373
    feature_021:    +0.362  feature_004=1:  -0.359  feature_020: -0.142
    feature_024:    -0.100  feature_026:    -0.169  feature_009: -0.188
    feature_022:    -0.048  feature_027:    -0.060  feature_029: +0.061
All other features non-significant. — h4 SUPPORTED.

================================================================================================
3. INDEPENDENCE OF THE TOP THREE BINARY FEATURES (iteration 5)
================================================================================================

feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 are mutually independent (pairwise |r|<0.01) AND
independent of feature_004 and continuous covariates (means/medians of continuous features
are essentially equal across these binary strata). This is consistent with random/independent
assignment, characteristic of either treatments or independent biomarkers, but inconsistent with
a single underlying biological factor. — h5 SUPPORTED.

================================================================================================
4. INTERACTION DISCOVERY (iterations 6-7)
================================================================================================

Pairwise binary interactions (controlling for main effects). The largest by far were among the
three "active" features:
    feature_001*feature_014  b=+1.060  p=7e-103
    feature_014*feature_021  b=+1.055  p=6e-102
    feature_001*feature_021  b=+0.883  p=1e-78
These positive synergies are larger than the corresponding main effects, indicating the
"three together" effect is genuinely multiplicative.

Negative pairwise interactions were found between {feature_026, feature_009} and the trio:
    feature_026*feature_014 b=-0.604 p=1e-16   feature_001*feature_026 b=-0.375 p=3e-8
    feature_009*feature_014 b=-0.468 p=7e-8    feature_009*feature_021 b=-0.409 p=5e-7
    feature_026*feature_021 b=-0.306 p=6e-6    feature_001*feature_009 b=-0.281 p=5e-4

Three-way interaction. The triple interaction f001*f014*f021 had b=+2.126 (p=2e-91) — and
once it was included, the lower-order terms became NS. The cell pattern was:
    7 of 8 binary combinations of (f001, f014, f021): response 0.16-0.17
    Only the (1,1,1) cell: response 0.633 (n=2401)
The signal is genuinely concentrated in the triple-positive corner. — h6 and h7 SUPPORTED.

================================================================================================
5. RESISTANCE MARKERS WITHIN THE TRIPLE (iterations 8, 9, 21)
================================================================================================

Within the triple-positive subgroup (n=2401):
    feature_026=0: response 0.717 (n=2025); feature_026=1: response 0.184 (n=376) — drop -53.3 pp
    feature_009=0: response 0.691 (n=2125); feature_009=1: response 0.188 (n=276) — drop -50.2 pp
Outside the triple-positive subgroup, neither feature_026 nor feature_009 has any effect.
Logit interaction coefficients: triple*feature_026 b=-2.439 (p=2e-62); triple*feature_009
b=-2.259 (p=4e-42). Cross-tabulation shows that either feature being 1 is sufficient to abolish
the elevated response — the two markers are independent "sufficient resistance markers", not
synergistic. — h8, h9, h21 SUPPORTED.

================================================================================================
6. THE 5-WAY RESPONDER SUBGROUP (iterations 10-13, 22)
================================================================================================

Definition: feature_001=1 AND feature_014=1 AND feature_021=1 AND feature_026=0 AND feature_009=0.
    Subgroup: n=1790 (3.6% of cohort), response 0.786
    Complement: n=48210, response 0.165
    Absolute difference: +62.1 pp
    Unadjusted OR ~ 18.5
    Adjusted OR (model with feature_004 dummies and standardized
      feature_020/024/022/027/029): 20.1, 95% CI 17.9-22.7, p ~ 0

Subgroup effect by feature_004 stratum:
    feature_004=0: 0.859 vs 0.208 (in vs out)
    feature_004=1: 0.772 vs 0.152
    feature_004=2: 0.668 vs 0.109
The subgroup x feature_004 interaction is modest (s_f4_2 p=0.056); response is uniformly very
elevated in all strata, though absolute baseline declines with feature_004.

Continuous-feature interactions with the subgroup were all NS (closest: feature_024 p=0.03, mild
positive). Within the 5-way subgroup itself, response is uniformly 0.76-0.81 across tertiles of
every continuous feature and across the remaining binary features — i.e., no further refinement
is available. — h10, h11, h12, h13, h22 SUPPORTED.

================================================================================================
7. EXHAUSTIVE 3-FEATURE SEARCH (iteration 14)
================================================================================================

I enumerated all (16-choose-3) * 8 = 4480 candidate 3-feature value cells with n>=200 and looked
for any cell with response > 0.45 or < 0.05. Only one cell matched the high end:
(feature_001=1, feature_014=1, feature_021=1) with response 0.633 (n=2401). No other 3-way
binary combination shows large response heterogeneity. — h14 SUPPORTED.

================================================================================================
8. TREATMENT-FRAMING SYMMETRY (iterations 15-17)
================================================================================================

Because feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 are mutually independent and exchangeable in the
triple-interaction model, any one of them can be regarded as the "treatment", with the other
two plus feature_026=0 and feature_009=0 as the predictive subgroup. The data are fully
consistent with all three framings:

    feature_001 as treatment: in (f014=1, f021=1, f026=0, f009=0): treatment effect +62.8 pp;
        outside: 0 pp
    feature_014 as treatment: in (f001=1, f021=1, f026=0, f009=0): treatment effect +62.0 pp;
        outside: -0.7 pp (NS)
    feature_021 as treatment: in (f001=1, f014=1, f026=0, f009=0): treatment effect +62.8 pp;
        outside: -0.3 pp (NS)

In each framing, the treatment effect is ~+62 absolute percentage points within the predictor
subgroup and ~0 elsewhere. Without external knowledge of which column was the treatment column,
the three are statistically indistinguishable. The clinical interpretation could equally be:
"a targeted therapy that works only in patients with two co-required positive biomarkers and no
resistance markers", which matches common AML treatment-biomarker patterns
(e.g., targeted-agent + biomarker-defined eligibility, with resistance mutations gating efficacy).
— h15, h16, h17 SUPPORTED in their three-way symmetric form.

================================================================================================
9. CONTINUOUS-FEATURE DOSE RESPONSE (iterations 18-20, 24)
================================================================================================

feature_020 (range 0-23.6): smooth monotonic decline of ~6 pp from low to high decile, no
threshold (h18 SUPPORTED). The sign and magnitude are preserved both inside and outside the
5-way subgroup (h24 SUPPORTED).

feature_022 (range 0.5-500, right-skewed; possibly a leukocyte-count-like variable in AML):
gentle ~4 pp decline across deciles (h19 SUPPORTED).

feature_024 (range 20-100; possibly a percentage-like variable, e.g., bone-marrow blast %):
~5 pp decline (h19 SUPPORTED).

feature_011 (range 18-92, possibly age-like): no detectable effect (h20 SUPPORTED).

================================================================================================
10. PROGNOSTIC INDEPENDENCE (iteration 23)
================================================================================================

feature_004 has a graded effect on response that is essentially independent of the 5-way subgroup
(h23 SUPPORTED). Its role is prognostic (background response rate); it does not modify the
treatment-effect heterogeneity meaningfully.

================================================================================================
11. FINAL CONSOLIDATED CONCLUSION (iteration 25)
================================================================================================

The dominant treatment-effect-heterogeneity pattern in this AML cohort is:

    Patients with feature_001=1 AND feature_014=1 AND feature_021=1
    AND feature_026=0 AND feature_009=0 (n=1,790, ~3.6% of cohort)
    have an objective response rate of 0.786, versus 0.165 in all other patients
    (absolute treatment-effect heterogeneity +62.1 percentage points).

Equivalently, taking any one of {feature_001, feature_014, feature_021} as the putative
treatment, that treatment increases response by ~62 absolute pp in the subgroup defined by the
remaining two features being 1 AND feature_026=0 AND feature_009=0, and has essentially zero
effect outside that subgroup. feature_026 and feature_009 each independently act as resistance
markers — either alone is sufficient to abolish the treatment-attributable benefit.

This pattern is robust to:
    - adjustment for feature_004 (prognostic, ternary; OR for subgroup remains 20.1)
    - adjustment for the strongest continuous prognostics (feature_020, feature_024, feature_022,
      feature_027, feature_029)
    - stratification by feature_004 level
    - choice of which of the three "trigger" features is named the treatment

It is NOT meaningfully modified by any continuous feature (within-subgroup response is uniformly
0.76-0.81) and no other 3-feature binary combination produces response above 0.45 anywhere in
the cohort.

Beyond this dominant pattern, the cohort exhibits standard prognostic gradients on feature_004
and a small set of continuous markers (feature_020, feature_024, feature_022, feature_027,
feature_029) that act roughly additively on the log-odds of response, with no further detectable
interactions of comparable magnitude.

================================================================================================
LIMITATIONS

  - All features are de-identified to opaque labels; the identity of the treatment-vs-biomarker
    distinction within the {feature_001, feature_014, feature_021} trio is unresolvable from
    these data alone. Operationally the three are exchangeable.
  - Single binary outcome (objective_response). Other clinically relevant outcomes (survival,
    duration of response, toxicity) are not in the dataset and cannot be analyzed.
  - The dataset has no missingness, so imputation/sensitivity issues do not apply, but real EHR
    data typically have substantial missingness — this dataset's clean structure may overstate
    apparent effect sizes.
  - This is observational data; subgroup inference about "treatment effect" rests on the
    independence of {feature_001, feature_014, feature_021} from prognostic covariates
    (verified empirically here) and assumes no residual confounding from unmeasured variables.
