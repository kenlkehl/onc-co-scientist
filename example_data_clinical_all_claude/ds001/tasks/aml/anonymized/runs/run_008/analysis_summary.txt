Oncology Dataset ds001_aml — Analysis Summary
==============================================

Cohort: 50,000 anonymized AML patient records, 35 features (16 binary,
3-level feature_004, 18 continuous), one binary outcome (objective_response).
Marginal response rate is 18.75% (9375/50000).

Headline finding
----------------
A single conjunctive 5-feature rule explains essentially all of the
treatment-like heterogeneity in objective_response in this cohort:

    Subgroup S =  feature_001 = 1
              AND feature_014 = 1
              AND feature_021 = 1
              AND feature_026 = 0
              AND feature_009 = 0

  - Patients in S: n = 1,790  (3.6% of cohort), response rate = 78.6%
  - All other patients:          n = 48,210, response rate = 16.5%
  - Risk difference: +62.1 percentage points
  - Unadjusted OR ~ 18.6; OR adjusted for feature_004, feature_020,
    feature_024, feature_022, feature_027, feature_029 = 20.1 (z = 50.2,
    p << 1e-300; pseudo-R^2 of full model = 0.082).
  - Pattern reproduces in two random halves (77.1% and 80.2% in S,
    16.7% and 16.4% in complements).

Path through the iterations
---------------------------
Iter 1-3 (main effects). Univariate scans identified:
  * Three positively associated binary features:
      feature_001 (prev 0.40, +5.6 pp response),
      feature_014 (prev 0.30, +6.8 pp),
      feature_021 (prev 0.40, +5.4 pp).
  * Two negatively associated binary features:
      feature_026 (-2.3 pp), feature_009 (-2.4 pp).
  * Strong negative ordinal main effect of feature_004 (response rate
    23.1% / 17.4% / 12.9% across levels 0/1/2).
  * Strong continuous "disease-burden" main effects (lower in
    responders): feature_020 (p=1.7e-33), feature_024 (p=2e-16),
    feature_027 (p=1.6e-6), feature_022 (p=1.5e-4); plus a small
    positive main effect of feature_029 (p=2.7e-7).
  * No detectable main effect of feature_011 (age) or most lab values
    (feature_007, feature_031, feature_013, feature_033, feature_003,
    feature_035, feature_023, feature_002, feature_017, feature_028,
    feature_034, feature_012).

Iter 4 (multivariable model). All-feature logistic regression confirmed
the same six binary effects (feature_001, feature_014, feature_021
positive; feature_004, feature_026, feature_009 negative) and the same
five continuous effects, with no additional independent main predictor.

Iter 5 (structural relationships). Pairwise contingency screening
revealed: feature_016 and feature_025 are perfectly mutually exclusive,
as are feature_019 and feature_015 (most likely paired alternative
levels of two underlying nominal characteristics; neither pair predicts
response). The three positive flags (feature_001, feature_014,
feature_021) are statistically INDEPENDENT at baseline (joint cell
counts match products of marginals), so any synergy among them is
genuine, not confounded by joint allocation.

Iter 6-8 (treatment-by-feature interaction screens). Treating each of
feature_001, feature_014, and feature_021 as candidate treatments,
logistic interaction screens against all other features uncovered
strikingly large two-way interactions:
  * feature_001 x feature_014:  +1.060 (p = 6.8e-103)
  * feature_001 x feature_021:  +0.883 (p = 1.1e-78)
  * feature_014 x feature_021:  +1.055 (p = 5.5e-102)
And two interactions in the opposite direction:
  * feature_001 x feature_026:  -0.375 (p = 3.3e-08)
  * feature_001 x feature_009:  -0.281 (p = 5.0e-04)
  * feature_014 x feature_026:  -0.604 (p = 1.1e-16)
  * feature_014 x feature_009:  -0.468 (p = 6.8e-08)
  * feature_021 x feature_026:  -0.306 (p = 6.0e-06)
  * feature_021 x feature_009:  -0.409 (p = 4.7e-07)

The pattern is symmetric across the three "positive" flags and across
the two "suppressor" flags.

Iter 9 (three-way joint distribution). Tabulating response rate by
(feature_001, feature_014, feature_021):
       (0,0,0)=17.2%   (0,0,1)=16.2%   (0,1,0)=16.0%   (0,1,1)=15.7%
       (1,0,0)=16.4%   (1,0,1)=16.9%   (1,1,0)=16.0%   (1,1,1)=63.3%
Response rises only when all three flags are co-present (n=2401).
Single- and double-flag combinations track baseline. The pattern is
strictly conjunctive (logical AND), not additive.

Iter 10-11 (suppressor structure). Restricting to triple-positive
patients (n=2401, response 63.3%):
  * feature_026 x feature_009 within triple+:
      (0,0)=78.6% (n=1790)   (0,1)=18.7% (n=235)
      (1,0)=18.2% (n=335)    (1,1)=19.5% (n=41)
Either suppressor alone is sufficient to abolish the benefit; presence
of both is no worse than presence of one. The suppressors thus combine
as a logical OR (i.e., RESPONSE requires BOTH be 0).

Outside the triple-positive set, neither feature_026 nor feature_009
affects response (~16.5% in all four cells), confirming that the
suppressor effect is conditional on triple+.

Iter 12-14 (refined subgroup test). The fully specified rule yields
78.6% response in S vs 16.5% in the complement. Counterfactual checks
(dropping any one of the five clauses) collapse the in-subgroup response
back to ~16-17%. Each of the five conjunction terms is necessary.

Iter 15-17 (within-subgroup modifier search). Within S:
  * No additional binary feature modifies response (all p > 0.10,
    differences <= 5 pp).
  * feature_004 monotonically lowers within-S response: 0 -> 86.0%,
    1 -> 77.2%, 2 -> 66.8%; the subgroup x feature_004 interaction is
    -0.172 (p=0.05), a mild attenuation. S-membership remains very
    favorable at every level of feature_004.
  * feature_020 modestly lowers within-S response (Q1 80.9%, Q3 72.9%;
    p=0.014). Other continuous markers (feature_011 [age],
    feature_022, feature_024, feature_027, feature_029) do not modify
    within-S response.

Iter 18 (robustness). Random 50/50 split: response in S is 77.1% (n=898)
in half 1 and 80.2% (n=892) in half 2; complement responses are 16.7%
and 16.4%. The rule is stable.

Iter 19 (formal model). A logistic model that adds a single product
term (feature_001 * feature_014 * feature_021) and that term's
interactions with feature_026 and feature_009 fits the entire
heterogeneity pattern: the triple-product log-odds is +2.86, while
abc x feature_026 is -2.66 and abc x feature_009 is -2.55 (each
roughly cancelling the boost). All lower-order main effects and pairwise
interactions among the five flags are essentially null in this model,
which is exactly what a strict conjunctive AND rule produces.

Iter 20-22 (alternative subgroups). No 1-of-3 or 2-of-3 subset of
{feature_001, feature_014, feature_021} produces a comparable response,
even when restricted to the no-suppressor stratum (~16-17% response in
each partial-flag combination vs 78.6% in the full triple+/no-suppressor
group). No other binary feature, alone or in combination with the rule,
identifies a second responsive subgroup.

Iter 23 (confounding check). The subgroup is not a hidden low-severity
stratum: feature_011 (age), feature_022, feature_024, feature_027,
feature_004, feature_020 all have nearly identical means inside and
outside S. The 78.6% response inside S is therefore an effect-
modification result, not selection on baseline severity.

Iter 24-25 (final subgroup statement and residual heterogeneity).
Outside S, residual variation in response is dominated by additive
contributions of feature_004 and the disease-burden continuous markers;
no second large subgroup exists. The conjunctive rule captures
essentially all of the treatment-effect heterogeneity present in the
dataset.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
SUPPORTED (in addition to the headline rule):
  - feature_004 monotonically lowers response (population-wide and
    within S).
  - Lower feature_020, feature_024, feature_022, feature_027 are
    associated with higher response (small-to-moderate adjusted effects).
  - feature_029 has a small positive main effect.
  - feature_026 and feature_009 are conditional suppressors of the
    triple-positive effect; outside the triple, they have no main effect.
  - feature_016/feature_025 and feature_019/feature_015 are mutually
    exclusive but irrelevant to outcome.

REFUTED:
  - Age (feature_011) does not predict response (main or as modifier).
  - Most laboratory continuous variables (feature_007, feature_031,
    feature_013, feature_033, feature_003, feature_035, feature_023,
    feature_002, feature_017, feature_028, feature_034, feature_012)
    have no detectable association.
  - No single binary feature acts as a clean "treatment" with a
    population-wide non-trivial effect; the apparent main effects of
    feature_001, feature_014, and feature_021 are entirely driven by
    the small triple-positive subgroup.
  - No alternative subgroup (any 1-of-3 or 2-of-3 combination of the
    flags, with or without suppressor conditioning) yields elevated
    response.
  - feature_011 (age), feature_010, feature_005, feature_030,
    feature_018, feature_008, feature_006, feature_032, feature_016,
    feature_025, feature_019, feature_015 do not modify the subgroup
    effect.

Final best-supported treatment-effect subgroup (per task instructions)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment-like flags: feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 (acting only
when all three are co-present).
Outcome: objective_response (positive direction).
Subgroup definition (every predicate is necessary):
    feature_001 = 1
    AND feature_014 = 1
    AND feature_021 = 1
    AND feature_026 = 0   (negative-modifier suppression absent)
    AND feature_009 = 0   (second suppression absent)
Within this subgroup the response rate is ~78.6% versus ~16.5% in the
complement; risk difference +62 percentage points, adjusted OR ~ 20.

Caveat. Because the three "treatment-like" flags are independent at
baseline and behave symmetrically with respect to each other, this
analysis does not identify which one (if any) is causally a treatment;
it only establishes that response requires all three to be present and
both suppressors to be absent. Their roles could be reversed (e.g., a
single trimodal therapy or three jointly necessary biomarker
characteristics) without changing the empirical subgroup definition.
