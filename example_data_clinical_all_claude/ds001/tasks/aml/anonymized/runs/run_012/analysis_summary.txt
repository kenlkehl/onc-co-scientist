Oncology Dataset Analysis — ds001_aml (n=50,000)
Outcome: objective_response (binary; cohort-wide ORR = 18.75%)

================================================================
HEADLINE FINDING

The dataset's response signal is dominated by a near-pure five-way
biomarker interaction. Three binary features (feature_001, feature_014,
feature_021) act through a strict AND-logic three-way interaction;
two more (feature_026, feature_009) act as suppressors that abrogate
the response only when the triple is co-positive. The clinically
interpretable subgroup definition is:

  feature_001 = 1
  AND feature_014 = 1
  AND feature_021 = 1
  AND feature_026 = 0
  AND feature_009 = 0

This 5-feature subgroup contains 1,790 patients (3.6% of the cohort)
with ORR = 78.6%, vs ORR = 16.5% in the rest of the cohort
(unadjusted OR ≈ 18.6). Adding the favorable level of feature_004
(=0) defines a 6-feature "hyper-responder" subgroup of 617 patients
with ORR = 85.9% (OR ≈ 27.9 vs the rest of the cohort).

================================================================
PROGRESSION OF HYPOTHESES AND EVIDENCE

Iteration 1–2: Univariate screen.
A chi-square / single-variable logistic screen across all 35 features
gave a clear short-list:

  Strong positive marginal associations:
    feature_001  ORR 22.1% vs 16.5% (+5.6 pp), p=1.9e-55
    feature_014  ORR 23.5% vs 16.7% (+6.7 pp), p=5.8e-70
    feature_021  ORR 22.0% vs 16.6% (+5.4 pp), p=6.6e-51

  Negative associations (small effect, large n):
    feature_026  ORR 16.8% vs 19.1% (-2.3 pp), p=3.3e-6
    feature_009  ORR 16.6% vs 19.0% (-2.4 pp), p=3.5e-5

  Ordinal prognostic:
    feature_004  ORR 23.1% / 17.4% / 12.9% across levels 0/1/2,
                 chi-square p=1.8e-90, monotonic.

  Continuous predictors (significant in joint model):
    feature_020 (β=-0.04, p=1e-30) — strong negative
    feature_024 (β=-0.005, p=2e-16) — negative
    feature_027 (β=-0.006, p=6e-6) — negative
    feature_022 (β=-0.002, p=2e-4) — negative
    feature_029 (β=+0.12, p=3e-7) — positive

  All other features (feature_010, 005, 016, 025, 019, 015, 018,
  008, 006, 032, 030, 011, 034, 028, 007, 031, 013, 033, 003, 035,
  023, 002, 017, 012) show no detectable main effect.

Iteration 3: Multivariable joint model. All of feature_001, feature_014,
feature_021, feature_004, feature_020, feature_024, feature_027,
feature_022, feature_029, feature_026, feature_009, and (marginally)
feature_007 retained independent significance, indicating the marginal
effects are not fully confounded by each other.

Iteration 3 (cont.) and 4: Are feature_001/014/021 independent in the
population? Yes — pairwise chi-square p-values all > 0.4, pairwise ORs
all near 1.00, and the triple-positive prevalence (4.8%) equals the
product of marginals (0.401 × 0.300 × 0.401 = 4.8%). So they enter the
cohort independently. Yet the joint distribution of ORR across the 8
binary cells of (feature_001, feature_014, feature_021) revealed an
extreme pattern: seven of the eight cells sit at 15.7%–17.2%, but
(1,1,1) jumps to 63.3% (n=2,401). The hypothesis that the three markers
combine pairwise was sharply REFUTED.

Iteration 5: Confirmed the structure with a logistic regression
containing all main, two-way and three-way interaction terms among
feature_001, feature_014, feature_021. Main and pairwise effects are
all near zero (p > 0.05). The three-way term carries the entire signal
(β = +2.13, p = 3.9e-92). This is a near-pure AND-interaction.

Iteration 6: Symmetry of the three. Conditioning on any two of the
three set to 1, the marginal ORR is ~16% if the third is 0 and ~63%
if the third is 1. The three biomarkers play perfectly interchangeable
roles in the AND-logic.

Iteration 7: Triple effect across feature_004 strata. Within each
feature_004 level the triple lifts ORR by ~45–49 absolute percentage
points. The triple × feature_004 interaction in logistic was
non-significant (p=0.34): on the log-odds scale the triple effect is
homogeneous across feature_004, although the absolute lift is largest
in the favorable feature_004 = 0 stratum because of the higher
baseline.

Iteration 8: Within-triple analysis. Restricting to the 2,401 triple-
positive patients and regressing objective_response on the remaining
features revealed two enormous within-triple negative coefficients:
feature_026 (β=-2.78, OR=0.062, p=5.5e-75) and feature_009 (β=-2.67,
OR=0.069, p=1.1e-54). Cell-level ORRs:

    triple=1, feature_026=0, feature_009=0  :  ORR 78.6%, n=1,790
    triple=1, feature_026=0, feature_009=1  :  ORR 18.7%, n=235
    triple=1, feature_026=1, feature_009=0  :  ORR 18.2%, n=335
    triple=1, feature_026=1, feature_009=1  :  ORR 19.5%, n=41

Either suppressor alone reduces the response down to baseline. They
behave as a logical OR of antagonists.

Iteration 9: Confirmed that feature_026 and feature_009 are PURE
effect modifiers, not main effects. Within triple-negative patients,
ORR by feature_026 differs by only 0.26 pp (16.7% vs 16.5%) and
ORR by feature_009 differs by only -0.07 pp (16.4% vs 16.5%). In a
joint logistic model with main effects + triple-by-suppressor
interactions, the main effects of feature_026 and feature_009 are
near zero (p>0.5), while the triple × feature_026 interaction has
β = -2.66 (p = 2.7e-70) and triple × feature_009 has β = -2.55
(p = 4.3e-50). The negative marginal associations of these two
features in the screen were entirely driven by their suppression of
triple-positive responders.

Iteration 10: Defined the 5-feature "quint" subgroup (triple = 1 AND
feature_026 = 0 AND feature_009 = 0). Quint-positive: n=1,790,
ORR=78.6%; quint-negative: n=48,210, ORR=16.5%. Adjusted OR =20.1
in a model that controls for feature_004, feature_020, feature_024,
feature_022, feature_027, feature_029. An exhaustive search of all
binary 3-way subgroups (n ≥ 200) outside the {001,014,021} axis
turned up no other subgroup with ORR > 0.40, so the discovered
subgroup is unique in the binary feature space.

Iteration 11: Within-quint heterogeneity. feature_004 (residual
prognostic) further stratifies the quint: ORR 86%/77%/67% across
levels 0/1/2 (within-quint p = 6.2e-11). feature_020 also adds
information: ORR 81% / 80% / 74% across low/mid/high tertiles
(within-quint p = 2.7e-4). No other feature reaches within-quint
α = 0.01.

Iteration 12: Defined the 6-feature "hyper-responder" subgroup
(quint = 1 AND feature_004 = 0). n = 617, ORR = 85.9%, vs 17.9% in
the rest of the cohort (OR = 27.9, p = 2.5e-180). Triple-by-
continuous-feature interaction screen: only feature_024 reached
interaction p < 0.05, and that effect is small and not meaningful
within the triple. All other continuous features (feature_011,
feature_022, feature_028, feature_034, feature_007, feature_031,
feature_013, feature_033, feature_003, feature_035, feature_023,
feature_002, feature_017, feature_027, feature_012) showed no
triple-modifying effect.

Iteration 13: feature_011 (likely age, 18–92) was tested explicitly
as a potential modifier — both as a triple × feature_011 interaction
and as a within-triple slope — and was non-significant in both
specifications (p > 0.38). The triple-positive phenotype responds
similarly across the age range. The cohort summary monotonically
increases through nested refinements:

   feature_001 alone   : ORR 22.1%, OR ~1.4
   triple-positive     : ORR 63.3%, OR ~8.7
   quint-positive      : ORR 78.6%, OR ~18.6
   hyper-responder     : ORR 85.9%, OR ~27.9

Iteration 14: Final synthesis (see below).

================================================================
SUPPORTED VS REFUTED HYPOTHESES

Strongly supported:
  - feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 jointly determine response
    via a near-pure AND-interaction (h6, h7, h8, h9 supported across
    multiple specifications).
  - feature_026 and feature_009 are pure interactive suppressors
    (h11, h13, h14).
  - feature_004 is a strong, ordinal, independent prognostic factor
    that does not interact with the triple on the log-odds scale
    (h2, h10).
  - feature_020 has a small but reliable independent negative
    association with response, in both the full cohort and within
    the quint (h3, h18).
  - The 5-feature quint subgroup captures essentially all of the
    treatment-effect heterogeneity in this dataset (h15, h16).
  - The hyper-responder definition (quint AND feature_004=0)
    represents the highest-ORR clinically interpretable subgroup
    (h19, h22).

Refuted:
  - Pairwise additivity of feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 (h6).
    The data require a strict three-way AND.
  - feature_026 and feature_009 as plain main effects (h13).
    They have no effect outside the triple-positive subgroup.

Not supported / null:
  - feature_011 (likely age) is not a main effect or modifier of
    the triple (h21).
  - No other continuous feature meaningfully modifies the triple
    effect (h20).

================================================================
TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY — FINAL STATEMENT (per protocol)

Treating triple-positivity (feature_001 = 1 AND feature_014 = 1 AND
feature_021 = 1) as the implicit "treatment" — the only feature
combination that produces material response heterogeneity in this
dataset — the most complete subgroup-defining heterogeneity hypothesis
for objective_response is:

  Triple-positive patients have a large positive treatment effect
  on objective_response (absolute ORR difference ≈ +47 percentage
  points; OR ≈ 8.7 unadjusted), provided that BOTH suppressors are
  absent (feature_026 = 0 AND feature_009 = 0). Either suppressor
  being present completely abrogates the treatment effect, returning
  ORR to baseline (~17%). Among the responsive subgroup, the effect
  is most pronounced in patients with the favorable feature_004 = 0
  level (ORR ~86%) and at lower values of feature_020 (~+7 pp lift
  for low vs high tertile). No other feature in the dataset modifies
  the treatment effect.

Equivalent phrased as a treatment-effect-modifier set:
   POSITIVE modifiers (required, AND): feature_001, feature_014, feature_021
   NEGATIVE modifiers (must be absent, NOT-OR): feature_026, feature_009
   Residual prognostic refinement: feature_004 = 0 (favorable level),
                                   lower feature_020.

================================================================
LIMITATIONS

1. Without column-name decoding I cannot tell whether feature_001/014/
   021 represent three biomarkers, three treatments, or some mix
   (e.g., one assigned therapy and two predictive biomarkers). The
   strict symmetry of their AND-interaction (each contributes the
   same +47 pp lift only when the others are present) is consistent
   with a multi-marker eligibility pattern — for example, a targeted
   regimen given only to patients meeting a 3-positive biomarker
   profile, or a fixed-combination therapy whose efficacy requires
   three target alterations. The dataset gives no further information
   to distinguish these scenarios.

2. The dataset has no longitudinal or survival outcomes — only the
   binary objective_response variable — so duration-of-response and
   survival-based heterogeneity searches cannot be performed.

3. Continuous features beyond the few flagged above showed no
   detectable association at this sample size. This is a real null
   given n=50,000 and a baseline ORR of 18.75% (well-powered for
   even modest effects), so we are confident the listed features are
   the operative ones.
