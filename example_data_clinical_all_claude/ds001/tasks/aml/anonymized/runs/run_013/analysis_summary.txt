ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_aml (50,000 patients, binary outcome objective_response, baseline 18.75%)
==================================================================================================

OVERVIEW

This dataset contains 35 anonymized features (16 binary, 1 ordinal with 3 levels, 18 continuous)
and a binary outcome `objective_response`. The cohort is large (n=50,000), so even small
effects achieve nominal statistical significance. The analytic strategy was: (1) screen
univariate associations, (2) fit a multivariable logistic model, (3) probe pairwise and
3-way interactions, and (4) refine the strongest treatment-effect heterogeneity finding into
a complete subgroup definition. The key finding is a single dominant treatment-by-biomarker
interaction.

ITERATIONS 1-2: UNIVARIATE AND MULTIVARIABLE SCREENING

Across the 16 binary features and the 3-level ordinal feature_004, the strongest associations
with objective_response (chi-square) were:

  feature_004 (ordinal): response 23.1% / 17.4% / 12.9% across levels 0/1/2 — strongly inverse
  feature_014 (binary):  16.7% off vs 23.5% on
  feature_001 (binary):  16.5% off vs 22.1% on
  feature_021 (binary):  16.6% off vs 22.0% on
  feature_026 (binary):  19.1% off vs 16.8% on (adverse)
  feature_009 (binary):  19.0% off vs 16.6% on (adverse)
  All other binaries: p > 0.13

Among 18 continuous features, the strongest negative associations were feature_020 (responders
3.44 vs non 3.95, p=8e-31), feature_024 (58.5 vs 60.3), feature_022 (19.3 vs 20.4) and
feature_027 (5.69 vs 6.17). feature_029 was weakly positive (3.82 vs 3.79). feature_011
(an age-like covariate ranging 18-92) showed no association with response (p=0.88).

Multivariable logistic regression on standardized features confirmed that feature_001,
feature_014, feature_021 each retain large independent positive effects (coefs +0.37, +0.44,
+0.36); feature_026 and feature_009 retain independent negative effects; and feature_004,
feature_020, feature_024, feature_022, feature_027 retain independent negative prognostic
effects, while feature_029 retains a small positive effect. Pseudo-R^2 was only 0.032 in
this main-effects-only model — a strong hint that important interactions were being missed.

ITERATIONS 3-4: PAIRWISE AND 3-WAY INTERACTIONS — DISCOVERY OF THE TRIPLE REGIMEN

Pairwise interaction screening across all 16 binary features identified extraordinarily large
synergies among feature_001, feature_014, and feature_021:

  feature_001 x feature_014: coef +1.06 (p = 7e-103)
  feature_014 x feature_021: coef +1.06 (p = 6e-102)
  feature_001 x feature_021: coef +0.88 (p = 1e-78)

These three interactions were 3+ orders of magnitude stronger than any other pairwise
binary interaction in the dataset.

Inspection of response by the joint (feature_001, feature_014, feature_021) tuple was
striking. Of the 8 cells:

  0_0_0: 17.2% (n=12,533)
  0_0_1: 16.2% (n=8,383)
  0_1_0: 16.0% (n=5,404)
  0_1_1: 15.7% (n=3,624)
  1_0_0: 16.4% (n=8,458)
  1_0_1: 16.9% (n=5,612)
  1_1_0: 16.0% (n=3,585)
  1_1_1: 63.3% (n=2,401)   <-- only this combination shows benefit

A saturated logistic regression with all 2-way and 3-way interaction terms yielded a
3-way interaction coefficient of +2.13 (p = 4e-92); after accounting for the 3-way term,
the main effects and 2-way interactions of these three features were near zero. This is a
classic super-additive (true) 3-way synergy: two of the three indicators provide essentially
no benefit, but all three together produce a dramatic increase in response. Plausible
clinical interpretation: feature_001, feature_014, and feature_021 represent three
components of a combination regimen (e.g., a backbone + targeted agent + adjunct), with
the full combination required for biological efficacy.

ITERATIONS 5-7: TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY WITHIN THE TRIPLE-POSITIVE GROUP

Within the 2,401 triple-positive patients, the response rate was 63.3% — but this average
hid striking heterogeneity. Stratifying within the triple-positive group:

  feature_026 = 0: response 71.7% (n=2,025)
  feature_026 = 1: response 18.4% (n=376)   -- collapses to baseline

  feature_009 = 0: response 69.1% (n=2,125)
  feature_009 = 1: response 18.8% (n=276)   -- collapses to baseline

Both adverse markers reduced the response rate within the triple-positive group by ~50
percentage points, all the way back to the no-treatment baseline. A formal logistic
regression with main effects + interactions (triple, feature_026, feature_009,
triple x feature_026, triple x feature_009) yielded:

  triple                : +2.90  (p < 1e-300)
  triple x feature_026  : -2.66  (p = 1e-69)
  triple x feature_009  : -2.55  (p = 9e-50)
  feature_026 main      : +0.02  (p = 0.59 — no main effect)
  feature_009 main      : -0.01  (p = 0.90 — no main effect)

Critically, in the triple-NEGATIVE population, response rates were essentially identical
across the four (feature_026, feature_009) cells: 16.5%, 16.5%, 16.8%, 15.8%. Thus
feature_026 and feature_009 are PURE EFFECT MODIFIERS of the triple regimen, not general
prognostic markers — their univariate associations seen in iteration 1 were entirely a
consequence of their action on the triple-positive subset.

A systematic interaction screen of all 32 non-treatment features against the triple
indicator confirmed that only feature_026 (p=2e-62) and feature_009 (p=4e-42) survive
multiple-testing correction as substantial treatment-effect modifiers. The next-largest
modifier was feature_024 (continuous, possibly bone-marrow blast %) with a much weaker
positive interaction coefficient (+0.15, p=6e-04) that may be a chance finding; feature_004
(performance-status-like) showed no modification of the treatment effect.

ITERATIONS 8-10: FINAL SUBGROUP DEFINITION

The complete favorable subgroup is:

  feature_001 = 1 AND feature_014 = 1 AND feature_021 = 1 AND feature_026 = 0 AND feature_009 = 0

This subgroup contains 1,790 patients (3.58% of the cohort) with an objective_response
rate of 78.6%, compared with 16.5% in all 48,210 other patients — an absolute difference
of +62 percentage points. The effect is preserved (OR ~20, coef +3.00) after multivariable
adjustment for all known main-effect prognostic features (feature_004, feature_011,
feature_020, feature_022, feature_024, feature_027, feature_029).

Within this favorable subgroup, response rate is further modulated (but not abolished) by
general prognostic factors:

  feature_004 = 0: response 86.0% (n=617)
  feature_004 = 1: response 77.2% (n=902)
  feature_004 = 2: response 66.8% (n=271)

Within-subgroup logistic regression also identified feature_020 as a continuous covariate
that further reduces response (coef -0.21 per SD, p=3e-04 within the subgroup); responders
had mean feature_020 = 3.77 vs non-responders 4.56. feature_029 was weakly positive within
the subgroup (+0.12 per SD, p=0.045).

ITERATIONS 11-13: SUBGROUP DISCOVERY — IS THERE ANYTHING ELSE?

To rule out hidden alternative treatment effects, an exhaustive search was performed
across triple-negative patients and across all binary 3-way subgroups. No other
combination of binary features yielded a response rate above ~22% (compared with the
~17% baseline), and no other 3-way interaction was positive. All other large 3-way
interactions in the dataset (e.g., feature_026 x feature_014 x feature_021, feature_001
x feature_026 x feature_014) are simply restatements of the same finding: when one of
the resistance markers (feature_026 or feature_009) is present, the corresponding pair of
treatment indicators no longer functions. The triple regimen of feature_001 + feature_014
+ feature_021 is the only therapeutic signal in this dataset.

HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED VS REFUTED

Supported:
  - Strong univariate main effects of feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 (positive),
    feature_004, feature_026, feature_009, feature_020, feature_024, feature_022,
    feature_027 (negative), feature_029 (weakly positive).
  - 3-way synergistic interaction of feature_001 x feature_014 x feature_021.
  - feature_026 and feature_009 are negative effect modifiers of the triple regimen.
  - feature_004 modulates response magnitude within the ideal subgroup.
  - feature_020 modulates response magnitude within the ideal subgroup.

Refuted (or no support):
  - Any meaningful response benefit from any binary feature in isolation in
    triple-negative patients.
  - Any meaningful main effect of feature_026 or feature_009 in triple-negative
    patients (their univariate effects are entirely driven by triple-positive cases).
  - Any modification of the treatment effect by feature_011 (age-like), or by any
    other feature beyond feature_026 / feature_009 / weakly feature_024.
  - Any alternative 2-way or 3-way binary treatment combination outside the
    feature_001 / feature_014 / feature_021 triple.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS

(1) The dataset encodes a single dominant treatment-by-biomarker interaction. The
    therapeutic regimen is the conjunction of three binary indicators (feature_001,
    feature_014, feature_021), most plausibly representing three components of a
    combination treatment (e.g., backbone agent + targeted agent + adjunct).

(2) Treatment efficacy is highly heterogeneous and well-described by two binary
    resistance markers. The complete responder phenotype is:

       feature_001 = 1 AND feature_014 = 1 AND feature_021 = 1
       AND feature_026 = 0 AND feature_009 = 0

    In this subgroup, objective_response rate is ~79% (n = 1,790). In all other
    patients, response rate is ~17% — including patients receiving the full
    triple regimen if they harbor either feature_026 or feature_009 (response
    drops to ~18% in those settings).

(3) feature_026 and feature_009 act independently as treatment-effect modifiers and
    have no main effect on response — they are pure resistance markers, not
    prognostic markers.

(4) General prognostic features (feature_004, feature_020, feature_022, feature_024,
    feature_027, feature_029) modulate the magnitude of response within the favorable
    subgroup but do not eliminate the treatment benefit.

(5) No alternative therapeutic subgroup was identified. The triple regimen of
    feature_001 + feature_014 + feature_021 is the only effective treatment combination
    encoded in this 50,000-patient AML cohort.
