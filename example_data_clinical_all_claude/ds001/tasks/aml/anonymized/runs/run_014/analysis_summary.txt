Analysis summary - dataset ds001_aml (n = 50,000)
====================================================

Outcome: objective_response (binary; overall rate 18.75%, n_responder = 9,375).

Headline finding
----------------
The data are dominated by a strict three-way treatment synergy among three
binary feature columns (feature_001, feature_014, feature_021), with effect
modification by two binary patient features (feature_026, feature_009).

* Patients receiving zero, one, or two of {feature_001, feature_014,
  feature_021} have response rates that are statistically indistinguishable
  from one another (16.2-17.2% across all such cells).
* Patients receiving all three have response rate 63.3%.
* Within the triple-positive group, response is bimodal: feature_026=0 AND
  feature_009=0 -> 78.6%; feature_026=1 OR feature_009=1 -> 18.5%.
* The "clean" predictive subgroup is therefore: feature_001=1 AND
  feature_014=1 AND feature_021=1 AND feature_026=0 AND feature_009=0
  (n=1,790, response 78.6%; absolute treatment effect ~ +62 percentage
  points vs same-stratum non-triple controls).

Iteration-by-iteration narrative
--------------------------------
1. Confirmed the outcome is binary and severely unbalanced (responder rate
   0.1875). All hypothesis testing thereafter targeted this rate.

2. Marginal binary-feature screen identified five non-null associations:
   feature_014, feature_001, feature_021 (each with positive risk
   differences of +5 to +7 pp, p < 1e-50) and feature_026, feature_009
   (small negative effects, p < 1e-4). Eleven other binary features had no
   detectable marginal association.

3. feature_004 (3-level integer 0/1/2) showed a strict monotone decline in
   response (0.231, 0.174, 0.129; ordinal logistic p < 1e-87). It behaves
   exactly like a performance-status / ECOG-like covariate.

4. Continuous-feature screen flagged feature_024 (responders lower by 1.8),
   feature_020 (responders lower by 0.5), feature_022, feature_027
   (responders lower) and feature_029 (responders higher). feature_011
   (the only feature consistent with age) showed essentially no marginal
   effect (p = 0.88).

5. A joint multivariable logistic regression on all 35 features confirmed
   the same set as independently associated: feature_004, feature_001,
   feature_014, feature_021, feature_020, feature_024, feature_029,
   feature_022, feature_027, feature_026, feature_009. Notably feature_011
   (age) is not significant in the joint model either.

6. The three "treatment-like" features (feature_001, 014, 021) are
   independent of each other and of every other feature in the dataset
   (no chi-square or t-test p < 1e-6). Their distribution is consistent
   with three independent randomized assignments. They are therefore safe
   to interpret as treatments.

7. Their marginal effects are individually significant, but this is
   misleading because the effect is concentrated in the cells where
   multiple treatments are co-administered (next iteration).

8. Pairwise treatment interactions on the logit scale are very large and
   highly significant (interaction betas +0.88 to +1.06, p < 1e-78 each),
   refuting an additive treatment model and pointing toward synergy.

9. The saturated three-way logistic model showed all single-agent main
   effects and all three pairwise interactions are essentially zero, while
   the triple interaction term feature_001 x feature_014 x feature_021 has
   coefficient +2.13 (p = 4e-92). The number-of-treatments dose-response is
   flat from 0 to 2 and jumps only at 3, refuting any monotone additive
   pattern.

10. Direct cell-by-cell comparison: seven of the eight 0/1 cells have
    response rate 0.16-0.17; the (1,1,1) cell has 0.633. Within the
    triple-positive subgroup, two binary features dramatically split the
    response distribution: feature_026 (0.184 if =1 vs 0.717 if =0) and
    feature_009 (0.188 vs 0.691). No other feature comes close.

11-12. Stratified analyses confirm that within feature_026=1 (n=7,462) and
       within feature_009=1 (n=4,985), the triple-treatment effect is null
       (p = 0.50 and 0.41 respectively). These two markers each completely
       abolish the triple-treatment benefit.

13. feature_026 and feature_009 are independent of each other (chi-square
    p ~ 0.95). They identify two disjoint resistance phenotypes, with
    overall prevalences 14.9% and 9.97%, respectively.

14. Both markers are independent of all three treatments (no chi-square
    p < 0.05 after Bonferroni correction across six tests), confirming
    they are baseline patient features rather than treatment-related.

15. Combining the two markers yields the cleanest treatment-effect
    contrast: in feature_026=0 AND feature_009=0 (n=38,321), triple-treated
    patients (n=1,790) achieve response rate 0.786 vs 0.165 in same-stratum
    non-triple controls (n=36,531) -- absolute risk difference +62 pp.

16. Within the clean triple-positive subgroup, feature_004 still
    monotonically reduces response (0:0.859, 1:0.772, 2:0.668) but does
    not eliminate it; logistic coefficient -0.576 (p < 0.001). It modifies
    rather than gates the effect.

17. Among continuous predictors, feature_020 retains a clear independent
    negative effect within the clean subgroup (beta -0.054, p < 0.001).
    feature_024, feature_022, feature_027, feature_029 trends are in the
    same direction as in the full cohort but are no longer individually
    significant once the dominant treatment heterogeneity is accounted for.

18. Multivariable logistic regression in the no-resistance stratum
    (feature_026=0 AND feature_009=0) recovers a triple-treatment
    coefficient of +3.00 on the logit scale (OR ~ 20.2, 95% CI 17.9-22.7;
    p < 1e-300) after adjusting for feature_004, feature_011, feature_020,
    feature_022, feature_024, feature_029. feature_011 (age) is again not
    significant (p = 0.75).

19. The most favorable observed cell is feature_004=0 AND feature_026=0
    AND feature_009=0 (n=13,448). Triple-treated patients here (n=617)
    achieve 0.859 response vs 0.208 in same-cell non-triple controls
    (n=12,831), a +65 pp difference. feature_004 thus refines but does
    not redefine the responder subgroup.

20. feature_026 has essentially zero main effect among non-triple-treated
    patients (response 0.166 vs 0.165, p = 0.84). It manifests only as a
    treatment-resistance marker, not a baseline prognostic factor.

21. feature_009 has a small (-2 pp) negative main effect among non-triple
    patients but a much larger (-50 pp) effect modification under triple
    therapy. Its dominant role is as a treatment-effect modifier.

22. Within the resistance subgroup (feature_026=1 OR feature_009=1,
    n=11,679, of whom 611 received triple), no further binary or 3-level
    feature split rescues the triple-treatment effect: triple-effect on
    response is +0.018 with p ~ 0.4 in this stratum, and no measured
    feature defines a sub-stratum with a triple-positive response rate
    above 25%. The resistance is essentially complete on average and is
    not modulated by any other measured covariate.

23. Exhaustive cohort-wide treatment-by-feature interaction screening
    (each of feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 against each of 34
    other features) produced no additional interactions with p < 1e-5
    beyond the three-way treatment synergy and the feature_026 /
    feature_009 modifiers. Any remaining tx-by-continuous signals
    (e.g. feature_020, feature_022) reflect baseline prognostic structure
    rather than independent effect modification.

24. Continuous-by-treatment screening using the triple indicator showed
    only one cohort-wide interaction (triple x feature_024, p = 5.6e-4)
    suggesting higher feature_024 attenuates triple effect; this signal
    disappears within the no-resistance stratum (interaction p = 0.85),
    confirming feature_024 acts only because feature_026 / feature_009
    confound the cohort-wide test.

25. FINAL UNIFIED HYPOTHESIS:
    * Triple combination feature_001 + feature_014 + feature_021 produces
      a large, clinically meaningful improvement in objective_response
      (absolute +62 to +65 pp, OR ~ 19-20) ONLY in patients with
      feature_026=0 AND feature_009=0 (~77% of the cohort).
    * No subset of one or two of the three treatments produces a
      detectable benefit in any subgroup.
    * In patients with feature_026=1 OR feature_009=1 (~23% of the
      cohort), the triple combination has no meaningful effect (delta
      ~ +1.8 pp, p ~ 0.2). These are two independent resistance markers,
      both independent of treatment assignment.
    * feature_004 (probable performance-status surrogate) acts as a
      continuous response modifier within the responder subgroup but does
      not gate the qualitative effect.

Hypotheses supported
--------------------
- Three-way treatment synergy (h9.1, h10.1)
- Two-marker resistance pattern abolishing treatment effect (h11.1, h12.1)
- Combined clean subgroup definition with very large response (h15.1, h25.1)
- Performance-status-like feature_004 as an ordered prognostic modifier
  (h3.1, h16.1, h19.1)
- Independence of treatments (h6.1) and of resistance markers from
  treatments (h14.1)

Hypotheses refuted or null
--------------------------
- Additivity of the three treatments (h8.1) - REFUTED
- Two-of-three benefit (h9.2) - REFUTED
- Age effect (h4.2) - NULL
- Recoverable triple-treatment benefit in the resistance stratum (h22.1)
  - REFUTED
- Important secondary treatment-by-feature interactions beyond the
  three-way synergy and the two resistance markers (h23.1, h24.1) - NULL
  after stratification

Overall conclusion
------------------
Among the 50,000 patients in ds001_aml the strongest, cleanest signal is a
triple-combination treatment effect (feature_001 + feature_014 +
feature_021) that confers a ~+62 percentage point increase in
objective_response, but only among patients lacking the resistance markers
feature_026 and feature_009. The two resistance markers act independently,
each one alone abolishing the treatment effect, while remaining
independent of the treatment assignments themselves. Within the responder
subgroup, increasing feature_004 (an ordinal patient feature behaving like
performance status) and elevated feature_020 reduce but do not eliminate
the response. No further treatment-effect heterogeneity beyond this
triple-treatment / two-resistance-marker structure is detectable in the
data.
