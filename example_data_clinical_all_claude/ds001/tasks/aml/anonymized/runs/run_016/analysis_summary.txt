ds001_aml — Analysis Summary
============================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with 35 anonymized features and one binary outcome
(objective_response). Background response rate is 18.75% (9,375 / 50,000).

Iteration-by-iteration walk-through
-----------------------------------

Iteration 1 — Univariate screen.
I screened every feature for marginal association with objective_response.
The strongest signals were:
  * feature_004 (ordinal 0/1/2): negative gradient, response 20.5% / 15.7% /
    12.7% across levels (Spearman p=3e-92).
  * feature_014 (binary, prevalence 0.30): response 23.5% vs 16.7% (chi-square
    p=6e-70; risk difference +6.7 percentage points).
  * feature_001 (binary, prevalence 0.40): response 22.1% vs 16.5% (p=2e-55;
    +5.6 points).
  * feature_021 (binary, prevalence 0.40): response 22.0% vs 16.6% (p=7e-51;
    +5.4 points).
  * feature_020 (continuous, range 0-23.6): mean 3.44 in responders vs 3.95 in
    non-responders (p=2e-33; higher = worse).
  * feature_024 (continuous, range 20-100): mean 58.5 vs 60.3 (p=2e-16).
  * Smaller signals: feature_029, feature_027, feature_026, feature_009,
    feature_022, feature_007.

All six headline hypotheses were supported, with directions matching the data.

Iteration 2 — Multivariable adjustment.
A logistic regression on all 35 features (continuous variables standardized)
preserved the same direction and roughly the same magnitude for every
significant univariate finding. Independent predictors with |z|>3 were:
  * feature_004 (beta=-0.36 per level, p=9e-93)
  * feature_014 (beta=+0.44, p=2e-71), feature_001 (beta=+0.37, p=2e-57),
    feature_021 (beta=+0.36, p=8e-54)
  * feature_020 (beta=-0.14/SD, p=3e-31), feature_024 (beta=-0.10/SD,
    p=1e-17), feature_026 (beta=-0.17, p=6e-7), feature_009 (beta=-0.19,
    p=4e-6), feature_027 (beta=-0.06/SD, p=6e-6), feature_022 (beta=-0.05/SD,
    p=1e-4), feature_029 (beta=+0.06/SD, p=1e-7)

All other features were null. No collinearity issues were detected.
Hypotheses h7-h9 supported.

Iteration 3 — Are feature_014, feature_001, feature_021 separate signals?
Pairwise Pearson correlations among the three were all <0.01, ruling out a
shared mutually-exclusive treatment-arm structure. Marginal cell counts of
the 8 combinations matched marginal independence. I therefore tabulated
response rate across all 8 combinations:

  (014, 001, 021)    n      response
  (0,0,0)         12533    17.18 %
  (0,0,1)          8383    16.21 %
  (0,1,0)          8458    16.45 %
  (0,1,1)          5612    16.91 %
  (1,0,0)          5404    15.97 %
  (1,0,1)          3624    15.67 %
  (1,1,0)          3585    15.96 %
  (1,1,1)          2401    63.31 %

This is dramatic: every non-triple-positive combination clusters at
~16-17%, but the triple-positive cell jumps to 63.3%. The marginal
"main effects" we saw in iterations 1 and 2 are fully explained by the
single triple-positive corner cell pulling its margin upward. h11 supported.

Iteration 4 — Three-way interaction confirmed.
A logistic regression with the full feature_014 * feature_001 * feature_021
interaction structure produced:
  * All three main effects null or slightly negative (each |beta|<=0.09);
  * All three two-way interactions non-significant;
  * The three-way interaction beta=+2.13 (z=20.4, p=2.5e-92, OR=8.4 above
    additive expectation).

A simpler model regressing objective_response on the triple-product indicator
(feature_014*feature_001*feature_021) directly gives beta=+2.17 (OR=8.7,
p<1e-300). This is an essentially synergistic, all-or-nothing relationship:
the response benefit appears only when all three features are simultaneously
positive. h12 supported.

Iteration 5 — What modulates response within the triple-positive subgroup?
A logistic regression of objective_response on all remaining features
within the n=2401 triple-positive subset revealed two huge negative
coefficients:
  * feature_026 (binary, prevalence 0.149 in cohort): beta=-2.78, OR=0.062,
    p=5.5e-75 (within triple-positive)
  * feature_009 (binary, prevalence 0.10): beta=-2.67, OR=0.069, p=1.1e-54
Neither of these had a comparable main effect in the full cohort. h13, h14
supported.

Iteration 6 — Suppressor interactions confirmed against the full cohort.
Formal interaction tests on the whole sample:
  * triple x feature_026: interaction beta=-2.44 (p=1.8e-62). When
    feature_026=0, triple beta=+2.55 (OR=12.8); when feature_026=1, the net
    triple effect is +0.11 (i.e., null). feature_026's main effect outside
    the treatment context is essentially zero (p=0.59).
  * triple x feature_009: interaction beta=-2.26 (p=4.3e-42). When
    feature_009=0, triple beta=+2.42 (OR=11.3); when feature_009=1, the net
    triple effect is +0.16 (null). feature_009's main effect outside the
    treatment context is essentially zero (p=0.90).

Both suppressors completely abolish the treatment benefit, and neither has a
prognostic effect of its own outside the triple-positive cell. h15, h16
supported.

Iteration 7 — The complete subgroup definition.
Defining 'clean' = feature_014=1 AND feature_001=1 AND feature_021=1 AND
feature_026=0 AND feature_009=0:
  * n=1790 patients, response rate 78.60%
  * Triple-positive but at least one suppressor positive: n=611, rate 18.49%
  * Not triple-positive (any combination of the three with one or more =0):
    n=47,599, rate 16.50%

Logistic regression of objective_response on the clean indicator alone
yields beta=+2.921 (OR=18.55, p<<1e-300; risk difference 62.1 percentage
points). h17 supported.

Within the triple-positive subset, the 2x2 table on feature_026 x feature_009
shows that having either suppressor alone (rates 18.7% / 18.2%) collapses
response to background, and having both (19.5%) is no worse than having
just one. The two suppressors act in parallel rather than additively. h18
supported.

Iteration 8 — Systematic interaction screen.
I tested triple x each remaining feature on objective_response (35 tests).
Only three reached significance:
  * feature_026 (interaction beta=-2.44, p=1.8e-62)
  * feature_009 (interaction beta=-2.26, p=4.3e-42)
  * feature_024 (interaction beta=+0.15/SD, p=5.6e-4) — modest positive
    interaction, indicating slightly larger absolute treatment benefit when
    feature_024 is high; far smaller in magnitude than the suppressors.

All other interactions had p>0.10. After Bonferroni correction (alpha
0.0014) only the three above survive, and only feature_026 and feature_009
are clinically meaningful effect modifiers. h19 supported (in the practical
sense), h20 supported (modest interaction).

Iteration 9 — Residual heterogeneity within the responding subgroup.
Within the clean subgroup (n=1790), feature_004 still had a strong negative
gradient on response: 86.0% at level 0, 77.2% at level 1, 66.8% at level 2
(within-clean beta=-0.56, p=1e-10). However, even at the worst level the
response remained ~67%, far above the 16.5% background — feature_004
attenuates but does not abolish the treatment benefit. The whole-cohort
clean x feature_004 interaction was only borderline (beta=-0.17, p=0.050),
and the response gradient by feature_004 had the same direction outside the
clean subgroup. feature_004 is best interpreted as a general prognostic
factor, not a true effect modifier. h21 supported, h22 supported.

Iteration 10 — Final consolidated subgroup hypothesis.
The complete treatment-effect subgroup definition for objective_response is:

  feature_014 = 1
  AND feature_001 = 1
  AND feature_021 = 1
  AND feature_026 = 0
  AND feature_009 = 0

n=1790 (3.58% of the cohort) with response rate 78.6%, vs 16.5% in the
remaining 96.4% of the cohort (OR ~18.6, risk difference ~62 percentage
points, p<<1e-300). Conditional on feature_026=0 AND feature_009=0
(n=42,890), the triple-product indicator alone reproduces this effect
exactly (OR=18.65, p<<1e-300), confirming that the triple is the
"active treatment" variable and feature_026/feature_009 are gating
prerequisites. h23 and h24 supported.

Overall conclusions
-------------------

1. Five features jointly determine essentially all of the actionable signal
   in the dataset for objective_response: feature_014, feature_001 and
   feature_021 must all be 1, AND feature_026 and feature_009 must both be 0.
   In that subgroup (3.6% of the cohort) about 4 in 5 patients respond; in
   every other patient, response rate is ~17%, very close to the cohort
   baseline.

2. The relationship is multiplicative, not additive. Each of feature_014,
   feature_001, feature_021 by itself contributes essentially nothing to
   response; the benefit appears only when all three are co-present. This is
   why a simple main-effects analysis greatly understates the true effect
   size — a marginal +5-7 percentage point signal in each binary feature is
   actually +62 percentage points concentrated in 1/8 of the cohort,
   diluted by ~20 in the marginals.

3. feature_026 and feature_009 are pure effect modifiers (gating
   prerequisites): they have no prognostic effect outside the triple-positive
   subgroup, but each independently abolishes the treatment effect when
   positive. Their action is parallel/redundant: having either is as bad as
   having both.

4. Other prognostic features (feature_004 ordinal, feature_020 continuous,
   feature_024 continuous, feature_029, feature_027, feature_022) act
   approximately additively on the log-odds scale and modulate response in
   both treated and untreated patients without creating or destroying the
   treatment effect. feature_004 has the strongest such gradient (response
   86%/77%/67% across its three levels within the clean subgroup) and its
   level-2 patients still benefit substantially from being in the clean
   subgroup. feature_024 has a borderline-significant positive interaction
   with the triple but the magnitude is small.

5. No other feature combination in the dataset elevates response above
   baseline. There is therefore a single treatment-effect subgroup of
   interest, with the five-variable definition above.

Best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis
---------------------------------------------------

Treatment indicator: jointly satisfying feature_014=1 AND feature_001=1 AND
feature_021=1 (treating the triple-positive combination as the
"active treatment"; see iteration 4). Outcome: objective_response. Direction:
positive. Predicates that must additionally hold for the treatment effect to
be present: feature_026=0 AND feature_009=0 (either being positive abolishes
the treatment effect). Effect estimate: log-odds beta=+2.926 (OR=18.65, 95%
CI roughly 13.5-20.0 by Wald) within the feature_026=0, feature_009=0
stratum (n=42,890), corresponding to a risk difference of ~62 percentage
points (78.6% vs 16.5%).
