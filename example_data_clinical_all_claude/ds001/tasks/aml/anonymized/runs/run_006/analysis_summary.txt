Oncology dataset ds001_aml — Analysis Summary
================================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients, 35 anonymized features (16 binary/categorical, 19
continuous), one binary outcome `objective_response` with overall ORR 18.75%.
Feature distributions are consistent with an AML cohort (median age ~68; lab
panels resembling WBC, BM/peripheral blast %, hemoglobin, platelets, LDH,
hepatic enzymes, electrolytes, etc.).

================================================================
ITERATIVE FINDINGS
================================================================

Iterations 1-2 (univariable scans).
A chi-square scan of all 17 binary/categorical features identified three
binary features with strikingly large positive marginal effects on
objective_response: feature_001 (RD = +5.6 pp, p ~ 1.9e-55), feature_014
(RD = +6.7 pp, p ~ 5.8e-70), and feature_021 (RD = +5.4 pp, p ~ 6.6e-51).
The 3-level feature feature_004 showed a strong monotonic gradient across
its levels (ORR 23.1% / 17.4% / 12.9% across levels 0/1/2; p ~ 1.8e-90),
consistent with a baseline prognostic risk-stratification variable. Two
binary features showed weak negative marginal effects: feature_026 (RD = -2.3 pp,
p = 3.3e-06) and feature_009 (RD = -2.4 pp, p = 3.5e-05). All other binary
features (feature_010, 005, 016, 025, 019, 015, 018, 008, 006, 032, 030)
were null (p > 0.1). For continuous features, the strongest associations
were feature_020 (-0.51 mean diff, p = 1.7e-33), feature_024 (-1.80,
p = 2.0e-16), and feature_022 (p = 1.5e-04). Notably feature_011 — the
likely-age variable, mean ~68 — has zero marginal association (AUC 0.499).

Iteration 3 (independence of treatment candidates).
Pairwise Spearman correlations among feature_001, feature_014, and
feature_021 are all between -0.003 and +0.001 (effectively zero), and
their joint frequencies match the products of their marginals to within
0.5% (e.g., all-three-positive observed 2401 vs expected 2400). This is
the signature of independent random assignment, supporting their
interpretation as three independently-assigned treatment columns.

Iteration 4 (multivariable model).
A whole-cohort logistic regression on all 35 features (continuous
standardized; feature_004 one-hot encoded) confirmed all three suspected
treatments retain large positive coefficients (feature_001 beta = +0.374,
feature_014 = +0.436, feature_021 = +0.362; each p << 1e-50), with
feature_004 dummies showing strong negative gradients (level 1 vs 0:
-0.359; level 2 vs 0: -0.726) and feature_026 (-0.169) and feature_009
(-0.188) significantly negative. Among continuous variables only feature_020,
feature_024, feature_029, feature_022, and feature_027 reached significance,
all with small per-SD effects.

Iterations 5-7 (treatment-by-feature interaction screens).
Each of the three candidate treatments was screened against all other
features for interaction. The same two modifiers — feature_026 and
feature_009 — repeatedly emerged as the strongest negative effect modifiers
for all three treatments:
  feature_014 x feature_026: in feature_026=0, RD = +8.2 pp; in
    feature_026=1, RD = -1.4 pp (interaction p = 1.1e-16).
  feature_014 x feature_009: RD changes from +7.5 pp to -0.05 pp
    (interaction p = 6.8e-08).
  feature_001 x feature_026: RD +6.5 pp -> +0.5 pp (p = 3.3e-08);
    feature_001 x feature_009: RD +6.1 pp -> +1.5 pp (p = 5.0e-04).
  feature_021 x feature_009: RD +6.0 pp -> -0.4 pp (p = 4.7e-07);
    feature_021 x feature_026: RD +6.1 pp -> +1.2 pp (p = 6.0e-06).
For each treatment, the favorable effect collapses to ~zero whenever
either feature_026=1 or feature_009=1.

Iterations 8-10 (joint modifier definition and 3-way treatment synergy).
Defining mod_neg = (feature_026=1 OR feature_009=1), the within-stratum
treatment effects are positive in mod_neg=0 (n=38,321) for all three
treatments (feature_001 RD = +7.1 pp; feature_014 +9.1 pp; feature_021
+6.8 pp; each p < 1e-60) and ~zero in mod_neg=1 (n=11,679; all
p > 0.25). However, decomposing by all 8 treatment combinations within
mod_neg=0 reveals that the entire "treatment effect" actually lives in a
single cell:
  (feature_001=0, feature_014=0, feature_021=0): ORR 17.2%
  (1,0,0): 16.4%   (0,1,0): 16.2%   (0,0,1): 16.0%
  (1,1,0): 15.8%   (1,0,1): 16.6%   (0,1,1): 15.8%
  (1,1,1): 78.6%
Single-treatment, double-treatment, and no-treatment cells all sit at
baseline ~16-17%; only the all-three cell jumps. A logistic model with
all three treatments and all 2-way and 3-way interaction terms within
mod_neg=0 yields all main effects and 2-way terms NS or marginal, and the
3-way interaction t1*t2*t3 = +2.892 (z = 23.4, p essentially 0). Within
mod_neg=1, the same model yields a null 3-way coefficient (+0.064, p = 0.78);
the synergy is fully suppressed by either modifier.

Iteration 11 (whole-cohort joint model).
A single whole-cohort logistic with triple, modifier mains, and
triple*modifier interactions: triple = +2.861, triple*feature_026 = -2.660,
triple*feature_009 = -2.550. The two modifier interactions each almost
exactly cancel the triple main effect, confirming that both modifiers
individually nullify the synergy.

Iteration 12 (intact_triple indicator).
Defining intact_triple = (feature_001=1 AND feature_014=1 AND feature_021=1
AND feature_026=0 AND feature_009=0): the cell has n=1790 (3.58% of cohort)
with ORR = 78.60%. The remaining 48,210 patients have ORR = 16.53%. Risk
difference = +62.07 percentage points; OR ~ 18.6.

Iterations 13-14 (3-way modifier model and untreated baseline).
A full triple x m26 x m9 model fits all 8 cells exactly. The triple*m26*m9
coefficient is +2.94 (positive), but this reflects only a logistic floor
effect once both modifiers have nullified the synergy — the predicted
ORR for triple=1, m26=1, m9=1 is 19.5%, matching observed 19.5% (n=41).
Critically, in untreated patients (all three treatments = 0), feature_026
and feature_009 each have *zero* main effect on ORR (17.24% vs 16.85% and
17.19% vs 17.08%, both p > 0.4). These are NOT general adverse prognostic
markers; they are specifically modifiers of the triple-treatment synergy.

Iterations 15-16 (within-subgroup heterogeneity by feature_004).
Within the intact_triple cell, ORR is monotonically graded by feature_004:
85.9% at level 0 (n=617), 77.2% at level 1 (n=902), 66.8% at level 2 (n=271).
The within-cell linear logistic on feature_004 has coef = -0.556 (p = 9.6e-11).
However, within mod_neg=0, the triple-treatment risk difference remains
massive in every feature_004 stratum (RD = +65.1 pp at level 0, +62.1 pp at
level 1, +56.0 pp at level 2; OR 17-23 in each). feature_004 modulates the
absolute response level but does not abolish the qualitative existence of
the treatment benefit.

Iteration 17 (pairwise treatment combinations within mod_neg=0).
None of the three pairwise combinations yields any benefit:
(1,1,0) ORR 15.8% (n=2741), (1,0,1) 16.6% (n=4288), (0,1,1) 15.8% (n=2789).
All three treatments are simultaneously required for the synergy.

Iterations 18-21 (further within-subgroup and exhaustive checks).
Within the intact_triple subgroup, no single binary feature outside the
already-named modifiers reaches Fisher p < 0.01 (best p = 0.10 for
feature_032). A multivariable logistic within the subgroup identifies
feature_020 as the only continuous modifier (-0.21 per SD, p = 2.7e-04);
others (feature_022 p=0.08, feature_029 p=0.05) are borderline.
An exhaustive scan of 2-binary-feature joint cells across the 12 remaining
binary features turned up zero cells with ORR > 30% or < 6% at n >= 50,
confirming the intact_triple rule is the unique strong subgroup discovery.
The other ~50%-prevalence features (feature_010, feature_030, feature_005)
have null effects both marginally and within intact_triple.

Iterations 22-24 (dose-response, baseline prognosis, miscellaneous).
ORR by number of treatments shows a strict threshold pattern at
n_tx=3 in mod_neg=0 (16-17% for 0/1/2 treatments, 78.6% at n_tx=3),
incompatible with any additive or saturating dose-response. feature_004
is confirmed as a baseline prognostic factor independent of treatment
(level 0 23.1%, level 1 17.4%, level 2 12.9%; gradient persists in
untreated and in intact_triple subsets). feature_018 and feature_008
share marginal prevalence but are only 75% concordant; neither modifies
the triple effect. Continuous laboratory variables (feature_020, 024, 022,
027) have weak negative whole-cohort main effects but are not strong
modifiers within the intact_triple cell.

================================================================
FINAL CONCLUSIONS
================================================================

1. Three independently-assigned treatment columns identified.
feature_001 (40% prevalence), feature_014 (30%), and feature_021 (40%)
behave as independently-randomized treatments — pairwise correlations
~0 and observed joint counts within 0.5% of products of marginals.

2. Triple-treatment synergy is the dominant signal.
The single 5-condition rule
   feature_001 = 1 AND feature_014 = 1 AND feature_021 = 1 AND
   feature_026 = 0 AND feature_009 = 0
defines a subgroup of 1,790 patients (3.58% of cohort) with ORR 78.60%,
versus 16.53% in the remaining 48,210 patients (RD = +62.1 percentage
points; OR ~ 18.6; chi-square p effectively 0). All marginal univariable
"treatment effects" for feature_001, feature_014, and feature_021 in
isolation arise mechanically from this single cell — within the rest of
the cohort, no individual treatment, no pair of treatments, and no
combination outside this 5-condition rule moves ORR meaningfully off the
~16-17% baseline.

3. Either feature_026=1 or feature_009=1 alone is sufficient to abolish
the synergy. Among triple-treatment patients (n=2,401), ORR is 78.6%
when both modifiers are absent (n=1790), but 18.7% when only feature_009=1
(n=235), 18.2% when only feature_026=1 (n=335), and 19.5% when both
modifiers are present (n=41). Crucially, in untreated patients these two
modifiers have no main effect on ORR — they act exclusively as
treatment-effect modifiers, not baseline prognostic factors.

4. feature_004 is a 3-level baseline prognostic gradient (likely
cytogenetic / ELN-style risk stratification) with whole-cohort ORR
23.1% / 17.4% / 12.9% at levels 0/1/2. The triple-treatment benefit is
preserved in all three strata (RD +65.1 / +62.1 / +56.0 percentage
points), so feature_004 modulates absolute response level but not the
existence of treatment benefit.

5. Other features.
feature_011 (likely age), feature_010, feature_005, feature_030, and the
remaining low-prevalence binary mutation-like features have no detectable
treatment-modifying effects. Continuous lab variables (feature_020,
feature_024, feature_022, feature_027) have weak negative main effects on
ORR but contribute little within the responsive subgroup. feature_020
(-0.21 per SD, p = 2.7e-04) is the only continuous modifier within the
intact_triple cell.

================================================================
BEST-SUPPORTED TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS
================================================================

Treatments: simultaneous administration of feature_001, feature_014, and
feature_021 (i.e., all three = 1).
Outcome: objective_response (binary, on natural risk-difference scale).
Direction: positive (responders concentrated in this cell).
Subgroup predicates required for benefit: feature_026 = 0 AND feature_009 = 0.
Optional response-rate gradient within the responsive subgroup: feature_004
(level 0 best, level 2 worst), without changing whether the triple
treatment works.

Magnitude: ORR 78.6% in qualifying patients (n = 1,790; 3.6% of cohort)
versus 16.5% in everyone else (n = 48,210); risk difference = +62.1 pp;
OR ~ 18.6.
