Oncology Dataset Analysis Summary — ds001_aml
================================================

Cohort
------
50,000 patients with one binary outcome (objective_response) and 35 de-identified
features (15 binary, 1 three-level ordinal, 19 continuous). Overall objective
response rate = 18.75% (9,375 / 50,000).

Iteration plan
--------------
The 25 iterations followed a propose-test-refine loop. Iterations 1-7
characterised baseline relationships of every feature with the response; 8-11
moved to pairwise and three-way interactions among candidate treatment/biomarker
features; 12-16 built the resistance-marker layer of the responder subgroup;
17-20 looked for further treatment-effect heterogeneity by ordinal/continuous
features and by remaining binaries; 21-23 stress-tested the final subgroup with
an exhaustive triple search and a baseline-balance check; 24-25 fitted a
saturated subgroup-interaction logistic model and stated the final subgroup
hypothesis.

Baseline / main-effect findings (iterations 1-7)
------------------------------------------------
- Age (feature_011): no effect on response (OR ~1.0/yr, p=0.88).
- Sex (feature_010): no effect (chi-square p=0.42).
- feature_004 (3-level ordinal, plausibly cytogenetic-risk or ECOG performance
  status): strong monotone negative effect — response rates 23.1% / 17.4% / 12.9%
  at levels 0/1/2 (chi2 p=1.8e-90; OR per level = 0.69).
- feature_020 (continuous, range 0-23.6, plausibly WBC/LDH-like marker):
  strong negative effect (OR = 0.87 per SD, p=1.1e-30).
- feature_024 (continuous): strong negative (OR=0.91/SD, p=2.4e-16).
- feature_022 (continuous): negative (OR=0.96/SD, p=1.9e-4).
- feature_027 (continuous): negative (OR=0.94/SD, p=6e-6).
- feature_029 (continuous, range 1.7-5.5, plausibly serum albumin or similar
  fitness marker): the only positive continuous predictor — OR=1.06/SD, p=2.9e-7.
- All other 12 continuous labs/vitals (feature_003, _007, _012, _013, _017,
  _023, _028, _031, _033, _034, _035, _002): no main effect (all p>0.05 except
  borderline feature_007 p=0.051).
- Of 15 binary features, only three (feature_001, feature_014, feature_021) had
  meaningfully positive main effects (~+5-7 pp). Two (feature_026, feature_009)
  had small negative main effects (~-2 pp). The other ten binary features
  (feature_005, _006, _008, _010, _015, _016, _018, _019, _025, _030, _032)
  showed no measurable main effect.
- A multivariable main-effects logistic regression confirmed exactly this
  signature.

Pair- and three-way interactions (iterations 8-11)
--------------------------------------------------
- Each of the three pairwise interactions (f014×f001, f014×f021, f001×f021) was
  statistically significant in a 2×2 cross-tab analysis: in each case three of
  the four cells had ~16-17% response and one cell (the doubly-positive one)
  had ~30-35% response.
- The three-way interaction f014 × f001 × f021 was very large: of the eight
  cells of the 2×2×2 table, seven had response 15.7-17.2%. The triple-positive
  cell (f014=1 AND f001=1 AND f021=1, n=2,401, 4.8% of cohort) had response
  63.31%. Saturated 2×2×2 logistic gave a triple-interaction LR-test
  chi-square = 425.7 on 1 d.f., p≈0.
- By symmetry, treating either f001 or f021 (instead of f014) as the candidate
  "treatment" variable yields the same picture: an effect of ~+47 pp limited to
  the cell where the other two features = 1. From the data alone the
  treatment vs biomarker labels among (f014, f001, f021) cannot be uniquely
  assigned; clinically, the most parsimonious reading is that one of these
  three is the treatment and the other two are required co-biomarkers.

Resistance-marker discovery (iterations 12-16)
----------------------------------------------
- Within the triple-positive subgroup, feature_026 was a dramatic resistance
  marker: response 71.7% (f026=0, n=2,025) vs 18.4% (f026=1, n=376), -53 pp,
  p=7.6e-86.
- feature_009 was a second resistance marker, also acting only inside the
  triple-positive subgroup: 69.1% (f009=0, n=2,125) vs 18.8% (f009=1, n=276),
  -50 pp, p=3.3e-59.
- Neither f026 nor f009 had any meaningful effect outside the triple-positive
  subgroup (all four cells of the f026 × f009 × non-triple combination were
  within 1 percentage point of 16.5%). They are pure conditional modifiers.
- Either resistance marker abolishes the treatment effect of f014 entirely:
  in biomarker-positive (f001=1 & f021=1) patients with f026=1, the f014
  effect is -0.2 pp (p=1.0); with f009=1 it is +1.7 pp (p=0.6).
- Defining the full responder subgroup as feature_001=1 AND feature_021=1 AND
  feature_026=0 AND feature_009=0 (n=6,078), the f014 treatment effect is
  78.60% vs 16.63% = +62.0 percentage points (n=1,790 treated vs n=4,288
  untreated), log-OR=+2.92, OR≈18.6, p≈0.

Further heterogeneity searches (iterations 17-20)
-------------------------------------------------
- Within the responder subgroup, feature_004 still attenuates response
  monotonically: treated response 86%/77%/67% at f004=0/1/2; untreated
  22%/15%/11%. The relative-OR test for f014 × f004 interaction is non-
  significant (p=0.24), i.e., on the log-odds scale the treatment OR is
  constant across f004 levels, but on the absolute-risk scale the treatment
  benefit is somewhat larger in lower-risk patients (+64 vs +56 pp).
- Age (feature_011) does not modify the treatment effect within the responder
  subgroup (response 80% vs 76% across age quartiles 55-80 y).
- Across cohort-wide quartiles of feature_020, _022, _024, _027, _011 the
  absolute f014 treatment effect varied only between +5 and +8 pp — there are
  no further important continuous-feature modifiers beyond the categorical
  responder definition.
- Within the responder subgroup, no binary feature OTHER than f026 and f009
  has a significant effect (all p > 0.13 for the remaining 11 binary features).

Specificity check (iterations 21-23)
------------------------------------
- An exhaustive scan of every C(15,3)=455 unordered triple of binary features
  identified (feature_001, feature_014, feature_021) as the UNIQUE triple
  producing >45% response in a subgroup of n>=200; no other triple comes close.
- The triple-positive subgroup is balanced with the rest of the cohort on age,
  sex, lab values, and feature_004, confirming the high response is a true
  treatment-by-biomarker interaction and not selection bias.
- Outside the biomarker-positive subgroup, the f014 treatment effect is
  ~-0.7 pp pooled (small, possibly slightly harmful, certainly not helpful).

Saturated subgroup logistic (iteration 24)
------------------------------------------
A logistic regression with the f001*f021 biomarker term, the
f001*f021*(1-f026)*(1-f009) "biomarker without resistance" term, and the
f014 × biomarker-without-resistance interaction term concentrates essentially
all of the response signal into one term: f014 × biomarker-no-resistance has
beta=+2.97, OR≈19.5, p<1e-300, while every other term in the model is small or
non-significant.

Final treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis (iteration 25)
---------------------------------------------------------
The treatment-feature feature_014 produces a large positive effect on
objective_response (+62 percentage points absolute, OR≈19) in patients meeting
the joint criteria:

    feature_001 = 1  AND  feature_021 = 1  AND  feature_026 = 0  AND  feature_009 = 0

Within this subgroup (n=6,078, 12.2% of cohort), patients receiving the
treatment (feature_014=1, n=1,790) have a 78.6% objective response rate vs
16.6% in untreated patients (n=4,288). In patients failing any of these four
predicates the f014 treatment effect is essentially zero (range -1.2 to +1.7
percentage points across the eight non-responder strata defined by f001/f021/
f026/f009).

Higher feature_004 attenuates the absolute (but not relative-OR) magnitude of
the benefit; no other continuous, ordinal, or binary feature substantially
modifies the within-subgroup treatment effect.

Final baseline-prognostic hypothesis
------------------------------------
Independent of treatment, the strongest negative prognostic factors are
feature_004 (OR 0.69 per level), feature_020 (OR 0.87/SD), feature_024 (OR
0.91/SD), feature_027 (OR 0.94/SD), feature_022 (OR 0.96/SD), feature_026
(OR 0.85), and feature_009 (OR 0.83). The only positive baseline factor besides
treatment is feature_029 (OR 1.06/SD). Age (feature_011) and sex (feature_010)
have no measurable effect on objective response in this cohort.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
Supported (with statistical evidence):
  - Lower response with higher feature_004, _020, _022, _024, _027 (all p<1e-3).
  - Higher response with higher feature_029.
  - Lower response with feature_026=1, feature_009=1 (small main effect).
  - Higher response with feature_001=1, feature_014=1, feature_021=1 (small).
  - Strong three-way interaction f014×f001×f021.
  - Within triple-positive, large additional reductions from f026 and f009.
  - Final 5-feature responder subgroup with +62 pp treatment effect.

Refuted (failed to find evidence):
  - Age effect on response.
  - Sex (feature_010) effect on response.
  - Main effects of feature_005, _006, _008, _015, _016, _018, _019, _025,
    _030, _032 (binary).
  - Main effects of 12 of 19 continuous features.
  - Treatment-effect heterogeneity by sex, age, or by quartiles of the
    continuous predictors beyond the modest f004 attenuation.
  - Existence of any other 3-binary subgroup with comparably high response.

Plain interpretation
--------------------
The dataset is consistent with a targeted-therapy AML cohort in which a single
treatment (feature_014) confers a very large response benefit in patients who
co-express two required biomarkers (feature_001 and feature_021) and lack two
resistance markers (feature_026 and feature_009). Approximately 1,790 patients
(3.6% of the cohort) appear to be in the maximal-benefit pocket, with response
rates approaching 80%. Outside this pocket the treatment is not beneficial.
Standard prognostic factors (an ECOG/cytogenetic-like ordinal score, WBC-like
and LDH-like labs, an albumin-like fitness marker) act on response largely
independently of the treatment-by-biomarker interaction.
