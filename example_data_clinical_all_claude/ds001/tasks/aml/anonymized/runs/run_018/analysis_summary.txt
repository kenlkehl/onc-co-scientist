ANALYSIS SUMMARY — Dataset ds001_aml (n=50,000; outcome: objective_response, base rate 0.1875)

OVERVIEW
========
Across 13 propose-analyze-refine iterations I screened all 35 anonymized features for marginal
associations with objective_response, fit multivariable logistic models, then targeted the
strongest signal — a striking 3-way interaction among feature_001, feature_014, and feature_021 —
and refined it into a complete 5-feature treatment-effect subgroup definition. I performed
treatment-effect heterogeneity searches around three candidate "treatment" features (those with
the largest positive marginal odds ratios) and around two plausible randomization indicators
(features with ~45% prevalence and no main effect). The dominant heterogeneity signal in this
cohort is a single, sharply defined responder subgroup that explains essentially all departure
of treatment effect from the population baseline.

ITERATION-BY-ITERATION SYNTHESIS
================================

Iter 1 — Binary main effects.
I screened all 16 binary features against objective_response. Three positively associated
features stood out — feature_001 (OR 1.43, p=1.9e-55), feature_014 (OR 1.53, p=5.8e-70),
feature_021 (OR 1.41, p=6.6e-51). Two were modestly negative — feature_026 (OR 0.86,
p=3.3e-06) and feature_009 (OR 0.85, p=3.5e-05). The remaining binary features showed no
material marginal association.

Iter 2 — Continuous and ordinal main effects.
feature_004 (3-level ordinal) showed a clean monotone gradient: response 0.231 → 0.174 → 0.129
across levels 0/1/2 (chi-square p=1.8e-90); ordinal log-odds slope −0.36 per level. Among
continuous features, the strongest negative slopes were feature_020 (OR_per_SD 0.87, p=1.1e-30)
and feature_024 (OR_per_SD 0.91, p=2.4e-16); feature_029 was modestly positive (OR_per_SD 1.06,
p=2.9e-07); feature_022 was modestly negative (OR_per_SD 0.96, p=1.9e-04). feature_011 (the
likely age variable, range 18–92) showed no detectable association (p=0.88), unusual for AML
response data and a useful clue that the dataset is biomarker- rather than demographics-driven.

Iter 3 — Joint multivariable model.
A logistic regression on all 35 features (continuous standardized) confirmed the same shortlist
of independent predictors: feature_004 (−0.36, p=9e-93), feature_014 (+0.44, p=2e-71),
feature_001 (+0.37, p=2e-57), feature_021 (+0.36, p=8e-54), feature_020 (−0.14/SD, p=3e-31),
feature_024 (−0.10/SD, p=1e-17), feature_029 (+0.06/SD, p=1e-07), feature_026 (−0.17, p=6e-07),
feature_009 (−0.19, p=4e-06), feature_027 (−0.06/SD, p=6e-06), feature_022 (−0.05/SD, p=1e-04).
All other features were non-significant after adjustment.

Iter 4 — The dominant 3-way interaction.
Cross-tabulating objective_response by (feature_001, feature_014, feature_021) revealed a
super-additive pattern: seven of eight cells clustered between 0.157 and 0.172, but the
all-positive cell (n=2,401) jumped to 0.633. Fitting a saturated logistic model in these three
features showed all main effects and 2-way interactions non-significant once the 3-way term was
included; the 3-way coefficient was +2.13 (p=1e-91). This is a classic "treatment works only in
patients with biomarker A AND biomarker B" structure — but with three exchangeable elements,
the data alone cannot tell us which element is the therapy and which two are biomarkers.

Iter 5 — Independence checks.
Pairwise chi-square tests showed feature_001, feature_014, and feature_021 are mutually
independent in the cohort (all pairwise p ≥ 0.48), so the super-additive joint effect is not a
collinearity artefact but genuine multiplicative biology.

Iter 6 — Within-subgroup analysis revealing further suppressors.
I defined triple_positive = (feature_001=1 AND feature_014=1 AND feature_021=1), n=2401, and
ran a logistic regression within this subgroup. Two features collapsed response back to
baseline: feature_026 (within-subgroup coef −2.78, OR 0.062, p=5.5e-75) and feature_009
(coef −2.67, OR 0.069, p=1.1e-54). Their effect sizes inside triple_positive are an order of
magnitude larger than their marginal effects (~OR 0.85 in the full cohort), and a global
interaction screen (triple × each binary feature) confirmed only feature_026 (p=2e-62) and
feature_009 (p=4e-42) modify the triple effect significantly.

Iter 7 — Final 5-feature subgroup definition.
favorable = (feature_001=1 AND feature_014=1 AND feature_021=1 AND feature_026=0 AND
feature_009=0). n=1,790 (3.58% of cohort). Response rate within = 0.786; outside = 0.165;
absolute gap +0.621; chi-square p << 1e-300. Within triple_positive, the split on
(feature_026=0 AND feature_009=0) vs (feature_026=1 OR feature_009=1) gives 0.786 vs 0.185,
with chi-square p=1.5e-155.

Iter 8 — Adverse modifier structure.
All five subgroup-defining features are mutually independent (all 10 pairwise chi-square
p ≥ 0.32). Within triple_positive, the four (feature_026, feature_009) cells produced rates
of 0.786, 0.187, 0.182, 0.195 — i.e., either modifier alone abolishes the favorable effect,
not just both together. The correct rule for adversity within triple_positive is therefore
OR (feature_026=1 OR feature_009=1), not AND.

Iter 9 — feature_004 within the favorable subgroup.
Within favorable, response rate by feature_004 level: 0.859 (level 0, n=617), 0.772 (level 1,
n=902), 0.668 (level 2, n=271); chi-square p=4.3e-10. The full-cohort favorable × feature_004
interaction was −0.17 per level (p=0.050) — borderline. feature_004 acts approximately
additively on the log-odds scale: a strong independent main effect that does not interact
materially with favorable status.

Iter 10 — Continuous predictors within the favorable subgroup.
feature_020 retained an independent negative slope within favorable (−0.21/SD, p=2.7e-04). No
other continuous feature reached Bonferroni significance over 35 within-subgroup tests. So
within the responder subgroup, additional gradation is governed by feature_004 and feature_020.

Iter 11 — Heterogeneity scans treating each candidate feature as the therapy.
I scanned interactions of each of feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 with every other feature.
Regardless of which was labelled the treatment, the same four modifiers emerged: the other two
of the trio (large positive interactions, p in the 1e-78 to 1e-103 range) and feature_026 and
feature_009 (large negative interactions, p in the 1e-08 to 1e-16 range). The three positive
features are exchangeable in the data: the responder rule is the same conjunction in every
labelling.

Iter 12 — Sensitivity: alternative subgroup rules and null-treatment candidates.
An exhaustive enumeration of (triple_positive AND two more binary predicates with v∈{0,1},
n_subgroup ≥ 200) confirmed that (feature_009=0, feature_026=0) is the highest-rate pair (0.786),
beating the next-best pair (feature_018=1 AND feature_026=0 → 0.750, n=280). I also tested
feature_010 and feature_030 — both have ~45% prevalence and could plausibly be randomization
indicators — but neither has a main effect (ATE +0.003 in both, p>0.4) or any heterogeneity
that survives multiple-testing correction, so they are likely null variables in this cohort.

Iter 13 — Final treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis.
The single best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis: response to the operative
therapy (whichever of feature_001 / feature_014 / feature_021 it is — the data are symmetric)
is concentrated in patients with all of {feature_001=1, feature_014=1, feature_021=1,
feature_026=0, feature_009=0}. In this subgroup objective_response = 0.786 (n=1,790) vs 0.165
elsewhere — an absolute difference of +0.621. Within the subgroup, feature_004 and feature_020
further attenuate response monotonically (response falls from 0.86 to 0.67 across feature_004
levels and from 0.81 in the lowest feature_020 quartile to 0.73 in the highest), but do not
abolish the effect. No other feature defines an additional layer of subgrouping.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
===================
1. Dominant signal is a 5-feature responder rule. The treatment-effect heterogeneity in this
   cohort is essentially captured by a single conjunction over five binary features: a triple
   positive requirement on feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 (super-additive 3-way
   interaction, +2.13 on log-odds, p=1e-91) AND absence of either suppressor feature_026 or
   feature_009 (within-triple ORs 0.062 and 0.069). About 3.6% of the cohort meets the rule;
   in that subgroup response is 0.79 versus 0.16 elsewhere.

2. The data are symmetric over which of feature_001/014/021 is the therapy. All three behave
   identically under interaction screens; assigning any one of them as treatment produces the
   same complete subgroup definition with the other two acting as positive biomarkers and
   feature_026 and feature_009 as adverse modifiers.

3. Adverse modifiers act as an OR-rule, not AND. Within triple_positive, the presence of
   either feature_026=1 or feature_009=1 alone collapses response back to ~0.18; both
   together do not produce a deeper drop.

4. Independent prognostic gradient outside the subgroup question. feature_004 (3-level)
   carries a strong, mostly additive negative effect on response everywhere; feature_020 and
   feature_024 carry independent negative continuous slopes. feature_011 (likely age) has no
   detectable effect, suggesting biomarker- and disease-feature-driven heterogeneity rather
   than demographics.

5. Hypotheses supported: h1.1, h2.1, h2.2, h2.3, h2.4, h2.5, h2.6 (negative confirmation),
   h3.1, h4.1, h5.1, h6.1, h7.1, h8.1, h8.2, h9.1, h10.1, h11.1, h11.2, h12.1, h12.2, h13.1.
   Hypotheses partially refuted/refined: h9.2 (the favorable × feature_004 interaction is
   only borderline; feature_004 is essentially additive rather than amplifying the responder
   effect on the log-odds scale), h10.2 (a few continuous variables show suggestive signals
   within the favorable subgroup but none survive multiple-testing correction).

6. Practical caveat. Without external information distinguishing therapy from biomarker among
   feature_001/014/021, the data alone cannot yield a causal estimand. The clinical
   interpretation — "this is the responder phenotype" — is what the data robustly support.
