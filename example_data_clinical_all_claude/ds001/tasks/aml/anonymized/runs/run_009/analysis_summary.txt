ds001_aml -- Analysis Summary
=============================

Cohort: 50,000 de-identified AML patient records, 35 features (mix of binary,
ordinal, and continuous clinical/laboratory values) and a binary outcome
`objective_response` (overall ORR = 18.75%).

Headline finding
----------------
Treatment-effect heterogeneity in this cohort is dominated by a single
high-order pattern. A 5-variable subgroup --

    feature_001 = 1 AND feature_021 = 1 AND feature_014 = 1
    AND feature_026 = 0 AND feature_009 = 0

-- contains 1,790 patients (3.6% of the cohort) with an objective response
rate of 78.6%, vs. 16.5% in the remaining 48,210 patients. Fisher OR = 18.55
(p < 1e-300). Three features must be present (feature_001, feature_021,
feature_014) and two must be absent (feature_026, feature_009); removing any
one constraint substantially attenuates the effect (sensitivity ORRs:
0.403, 0.406, 0.349, 0.691, 0.717 respectively when each predicate is
relaxed in turn).

Iteration-by-iteration narrative
--------------------------------
1. Univariate screening over all 35 features. feature_004 (ordinal 0/1/2,
   plausibly an ECOG-like performance status) showed a strong monotone
   negative trend (ORR 0.231 / 0.174 / 0.129; logistic per-unit
   coef -0.36, p<1e-50). feature_024 (continuous, range 20-100, plausibly a
   disease-burden marker) and feature_020 (continuous) were both
   independently negative. feature_011 (continuous, range 18-92, plausibly
   age) was essentially flat against ORR.

2. Among binary features, three stood out with similar marginal +5pp
   effects: feature_001 (ORR 0.221 vs 0.165), feature_021 (0.220 vs 0.166)
   and feature_014 (0.235 vs 0.167). All p < 1e-50 individually.

3. A multivariable additive logistic model confirmed that, of the 35
   features, only ~9 had |coef| > 0.05 with p < 0.01: feature_001 (+0.37),
   feature_021 (+0.36), feature_014 (+0.44), feature_004 (-0.36),
   feature_020 (-0.14), feature_024 (-0.10), feature_009 (-0.19),
   feature_026 (-0.17), and feature_029 (+0.06). Most other features
   were non-significant after adjustment.

4. The three "active" binary features were tested for pairwise interaction
   on objective_response. Each pair showed the same striking pattern: the
   marginal +5pp effects vanished when the other was 0, and combined to a
   +14 to +19pp absolute jump only when both =1. Difference-in-differences
   interaction estimates: feature_001 x feature_021 = +0.153,
   feature_001 x feature_014 = +0.193,
   feature_021 x feature_014 = +0.191 (all p < 1e-50).

5. The 3-way table revealed the structure exactly: among the 8 cells
   formed by feature_001, feature_021, feature_014 the ORR was at baseline
   (~0.16-0.17) in 7 of them and was 0.633 only in the all-three-positive
   cell (n=2401). The three features are statistically independent
   (observed triple-positive prevalence 0.0480 = 0.401 x 0.400 x 0.300),
   so the cell structure is not driven by feature correlation but by
   genuine 3-way joint effect modification on response.

6. A binary indicator triple_pos = (feature_001=1 AND feature_021=1 AND
   feature_014=1) added to the multivariable logit had coef +2.30
   (OR 10.0, p<1e-300) and absorbed essentially all of the individual
   feature_001 / feature_021 / feature_014 main-effect signal (which
   collapsed toward zero or even reversed sign). This confirms the
   apparent main effects in (1)-(2) above are an artifact of marginalizing
   over a true 3-way interaction.

7. Within triple_pos = 1 (n=2401), additional features were screened for
   subgroup heterogeneity. feature_026 and feature_009 each strongly
   suppressed response: ORR 0.717 (feature_026=0) vs 0.184 (feature_026=1),
   and 0.691 (feature_009=0) vs 0.188 (feature_009=1). These are dramatic
   reductions (>50 percentage points absolute) within the responsive
   subgroup.

8. Crucially, feature_026 and feature_009 have negligible main effects
   among non-triple-positive patients (ORRs 0.169 vs 0.194 and 0.167 vs
   0.190 respectively), so their adverse effect is concentrated in (and
   essentially limited to) the triple-positive subgroup. Logistic models
   with explicit interactions confirmed this: triple_pos x feature_026
   coef = -2.74 (OR 0.064, p<1e-50); triple_pos x feature_009 coef = -2.59
   (OR 0.075, p<1e-50). In the model these two features are pure
   resistance-modifier-style suppressors of the triple_pos response.

9. By contrast, feature_004 acts as a global, additive prognostic factor:
   its monotone negative gradient is present in both triple_pos = 0
   (ORR 0.208 / 0.152 / 0.108) and triple_pos = 1 (0.698 / 0.612 / 0.554),
   and the formal triple_pos x feature_004 interaction is not significant
   (p = 0.25). feature_020 behaves similarly (negative slope in both
   strata, modest interaction). These are best modeled as additive
   prognostic features rather than treatment-effect modifiers.

10. The only non-binary modifier showing a clear interaction with the
    triple_pos indicator was feature_024 (continuous, plausibly a disease
    burden percentage): triple_pos x feature_024 coef = +0.152 (p = 5.6e-4).
    feature_024 is negatively associated with ORR in non-triple patients
    (slope -0.11) but the slope is essentially flat in triple-positive
    patients (slope ~ +0.04). Other continuous-feature interactions with
    triple_pos (feature_011, feature_022, feature_028, feature_007,
    feature_034, feature_029, feature_027, feature_012, feature_020) were
    all non-significant (p > 0.18).

11. A pairwise binary-x-binary scan within the triple_pos = 0 cohort
    (n=47,599) was conducted to look for any other hidden interaction-
    driven responder cell with ORR >= 0.30 and n >= 50. None was found.
    The triple_pos / feature_001-021-014 / feature_026 / feature_009
    structure appears to be the only major treatment-effect heterogeneity
    in this dataset.

12. Final 5-variable best-supported subgroup definition (h23):
    feature_001=1 AND feature_021=1 AND feature_014=1 AND feature_026=0
    AND feature_009=0. n=1790, ORR=0.786 vs 0.165 in everyone else.
    Adding feature_004=0 on top further raises ORR to 0.859 in n=617, but
    that marginal benefit is consistent with feature_004's global
    prognostic gradient rather than a triple_pos-specific modifier, so
    feature_004 is not part of the minimal subgroup definition.

13. Treatment-vs-biomarker labeling. The three "required positive"
    features (feature_001, feature_021, feature_014) are mutually
    independent and enter the joint indicator symmetrically, so any one
    of them can be labeled as the "treatment received" indicator and the
    other two as required co-positive biomarkers without loss of fit.
    Under any such labeling the absolute treatment effect on
    objective_response is ~0 (-0.001 to +0.005) outside the
    "biomarker-positive AND no-suppressor" subgroup and ~+0.64 within it.
    For example, if feature_001 is the treatment: ORR with feature_001=1
    in (feature_021=1 AND feature_014=1 AND feature_026=0 AND
    feature_009=0) is 0.786 (n=1790) vs 0.150 in
    (feature_021=1 AND feature_014=1 AND feature_026=0 AND feature_009=0
    AND feature_001=0) (n=2654) -- absolute treatment effect +0.636. In
    the complementary "no benefit" stratum the effect is essentially zero.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
Supported:
  h2  feature_004 monotone negative on ORR (additive, global).
  h3  feature_024 negative on ORR in non-triple patients.
  h4-h6  feature_001 / 021 / 014 each marginally positive on ORR.
  h7  feature_020 negative on ORR.
  h8  Multivariable adjustment confirms the same set of significant features.
  h9-h11  Each pairwise interaction among feature_001, feature_021,
       feature_014 is positive and large.
  h12  Triple-positive subgroup has ~63% ORR vs ~16-17% in all others.
  h13  feature_001, 021, 014 are statistically independent.
  h14  triple_pos indicator absorbs the three features' main effects.
  h15-h17  feature_026 and feature_009 strongly and specifically suppress
       response within the triple-positive subgroup.
  h19  feature_020 negative within both strata.
  h20  triple_pos x feature_024 positive interaction (slope flattens in
       triple-positive).
  h23  Five-variable best subgroup ORR ~78.6% vs 16.5%.
  h24  Adding feature_004=0 yields ORR ~85.9% (additive, not modifier).
  h25  Treatment-vs-biomarker labeling is symmetric under feature
       interchangeability; absolute treatment effect ~+0.64 inside the
       defining subgroup, ~0 outside.

Refuted / null:
  h1  No effect of feature_011 on ORR.
  h18  No strong interaction of feature_004 with triple_pos (p=0.25);
       feature_004 is global, additive.
  h21  No interactions of triple_pos with feature_011, feature_022,
       feature_028, feature_007, feature_034, feature_029.
  h22  No second hidden binary-x-binary responder subgroup outside the
       triple_pos structure.

Overall conclusion
------------------
The cohort displays one large, sharply localized treatment-effect
heterogeneity. The five-feature subgroup (feature_001=1, feature_021=1,
feature_014=1, feature_026=0, feature_009=0) -- 3.6% of the cohort -- has
~4.7x the ORR of the remainder. The pattern is consistent with a
joint-positive triplet (whether interpreted as treatment + two required
biomarkers, or three co-required markers) whose response is abolished by
two distinct adverse modifiers (feature_026 and feature_009), against a
background of two global, additive negative prognostic factors
(feature_004, feature_020). All other 28 features have no, or only
modest-additive, prognostic effects on objective_response in this cohort.
