Analysis Summary — ds001_aml (n=50000, outcome: objective_response)
=====================================================================

Investigation overview
----------------------
The dataset comprises 50,000 patient records with 35 anonymized features
(feature_001 ... feature_035) and a single binary outcome,
objective_response (overall rate 18.75%). Treatment-arm assignment was not
flagged a priori; we identified the active treatment empirically. Treatment
appears to be approximately randomly allocated with respect to all candidate
biomarkers, consistent with a randomized-trial-like design.

Across 18 iterations we proposed 28 hypotheses and ran 28 statistical tests
covering univariate associations, multivariable logistic regression, two- and
three-way interaction modeling, exhaustive stratified subgroup analyses, and
an explicit treatment-effect heterogeneity search. The narrative below walks
through what we tested, what the evidence showed, and the final subgroup
conclusion.

1. Univariate associations with objective_response
--------------------------------------------------
The strongest univariate signals (sorted by p-value) were:

  Feature        Type     Direction (responder vs non-responder)         p-value
  feature_004    3-level  lower mean in responders (0.797 vs 0.954)      ~1e-95
  feature_014    binary   responders enriched (OR=1.53)                  ~1e-70
  feature_001    binary   responders enriched (OR=1.43)                  ~1e-55
  feature_021    binary   responders enriched (OR=1.41)                  ~1e-50
  feature_020    cont     lower in responders (diff=-0.51)                ~1e-33
  feature_024    cont     lower in responders (diff=-1.80)                ~1e-16
  feature_029    cont     slightly higher in responders (diff=+0.030)     ~1e-7
  feature_027    cont     lower in responders (diff=-0.48)                ~1e-6
  feature_026    binary   negative association (OR=0.86)                  ~1e-5
  feature_009    binary   negative association (OR=0.85)                  ~1e-5
  feature_022    cont     lower in responders (diff=-1.11)                ~1e-4

All other features (feature_011, feature_010, feature_005, feature_018,
feature_008, feature_006, feature_032, feature_030, etc.) showed no
significant univariate association.

2. Multivariable logistic regression
------------------------------------
Adjusting all 35 features mutually (continuous standardized) gave pseudo-R^2
= 0.032 with the same independent significant predictors as the univariate
screen, in roughly the same direction:
  Positive: feature_014 (+0.44), feature_001 (+0.37), feature_021 (+0.36),
            feature_029 (+0.06), feature_007 (+0.03)
  Negative: feature_004 (-0.36/level), feature_009 (-0.19), feature_026
            (-0.17), feature_020 (-0.14), feature_024 (-0.10), feature_027
            (-0.06), feature_022 (-0.05).
This confirmed feature_014 as the single largest binary predictor.

3. Treatment-effect heterogeneity search — three-way interaction
----------------------------------------------------------------
We identified feature_014 as the candidate treatment because of its size and
because the biomarker-like binary features feature_001 / feature_021 had
similar magnitudes and might modify treatment effect. Stratifying feature_014
by feature_001 x feature_021:

  Stratum                          tx=1 RR (n)     tx=0 RR (n)     OR
  feature_001=0, feature_021=0     0.160 (5404)    0.172 (12533)   0.92
  feature_001=0, feature_021=1     0.157 (3624)    0.162 (8383)    0.96
  feature_001=1, feature_021=0     0.160 (3585)    0.164 (8458)    0.97
  feature_001=1, feature_021=1     0.633 (2401)    0.169 (5612)    8.48

The treatment effect is concentrated almost entirely in the dual-positive
stratum. A three-way interaction term feature_014 x feature_001 x feature_021
in a logistic model has coefficient +2.13 (p ~ 1e-90) and absorbs essentially
all main and pairwise terms. This is a classic dual-biomarker signature.

4. Suppressors of the treatment effect within the dual-positive stratum
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
We then screened all remaining features for treatment-by-feature interactions
within the dual-positive stratum (n=8013). Two binary features stood out:

  feature_026: tx*feature_026 interaction = -2.55 (p ~ 1e-49)
  feature_009: tx*feature_009 interaction = -2.28 (p ~ 1e-31)

Stratifying jointly within the dual-positive group:
  feature_026=0 & feature_009=0: tx 0.786 (n=1790) vs 0.166 (n=4288), OR=18.4
  feature_026=0 & feature_009=1: tx 0.187 (n=235)  vs 0.166 (n=494),  OR=1.16
  feature_026=1 & feature_009=0: tx 0.182 (n=335)  vs 0.184 (n=728),  OR=0.99
  feature_026=1 & feature_009=1: tx 0.195 (n=41)   vs 0.196 (n=102),  OR=0.99

Either feature_026=1 or feature_009=1 is sufficient to abolish the treatment
effect.

5. The final four-feature subgroup
----------------------------------
Combining the four predicates, we defined the responsive subgroup as

   feature_001 = 1   AND   feature_021 = 1
   AND  feature_026 = 0   AND  feature_009 = 0

Within this 6,078-patient (12.2%) subgroup, treated response is 0.786 vs
0.166 untreated (OR 18.4; absolute risk difference +0.620; Fisher exact p
approaches 0). Outside this subgroup (n=43,922), treated response is 0.160
vs 0.167 (OR 0.95, p=0.06). A logistic model with a single subgroup x
feature_014 interaction explains essentially all heterogeneity:
  feature_014 (outside subgroup) coef = -0.054 (p=0.06)
  in_subgroup main coef             = -0.008 (p=0.85)
  feature_014 x in_subgroup         = +2.967 (p < 1e-300)

We confirmed that feature_026 and feature_009 are NOT prognostic outside the
dual-biomarker-positive stratum: response rates by feature_026 and feature_009
in the (feature_001!=1 OR feature_021!=1) subset are essentially equal across
0/1 levels (~16.4% in either case). Their univariate negative associations
with response were entirely an artifact of suppressing the
feature_014-driven response gain in the dual-positive group.

6. Modulation by feature_004 within the responsive subgroup
-----------------------------------------------------------
feature_004 acts as a dose-dependent prognostic modifier within the
responsive subgroup but does NOT modify the treatment effect on the OR scale:

  feature_004 level     untreated RR     treated RR     OR
  0                     0.216 (n=1526)   0.859 (n=617)  22.1
  1                     0.149 (n=2086)   0.772 (n=902)  19.3
  2                     0.107 (n=676)    0.668 (n=271)  16.9

Within the subgroup, the multivariable estimate is feature_014 +2.98 and
feature_004 -0.475 per level, with no significant feature_014 x feature_004
interaction (p=0.24). Higher feature_004 lowers the absolute response in
both arms but the relative treatment benefit is preserved.

7. Continuous prognostic features
---------------------------------
Within the untreated arm only (eliminating the treatment effect),
feature_020, feature_024, feature_027, feature_022 each show clear inverse
prognostic effects on response (lower → higher response), and feature_029
shows a small positive association (higher → higher response). These same
continuous features remain significant within the responsive subgroup (e.g.
feature_020 -0.147, feature_027 -0.085, feature_029 +0.099 per SD), and
their effects within the untreated arm closely mirror the marginal effects,
indicating they are baseline prognostic markers, not treatment-effect
modifiers.

8. Treatment balance
--------------------
feature_014 is well balanced across all four subgroup-defining biomarkers
(prevalence of biomarker=1 in feature_014=0 vs feature_014=1 differs by ≤1%
for feature_001, feature_021, feature_026, feature_009 and feature_004).
This is consistent with random allocation.

9. Other binary features
------------------------
No other binary feature (feature_010, feature_005, feature_016, feature_025,
feature_019, feature_015, feature_018, feature_008, feature_006, feature_032,
feature_030) showed a treatment-like pattern: none has a positive
interaction with the (feature_001 & feature_021) biomarker pair, and none
has a meaningful main effect on response after multivariable adjustment.
feature_014 is the unique active treatment in the cohort.

Conclusions
-----------
- The cohort behaves as a randomized trial of feature_014 vs control on a
  binary outcome (objective_response).
- feature_014 has essentially zero average effect outside a precisely
  defined four-feature subgroup, but a very large effect within it.
- Final best-supported subgroup hypothesis:
    Among patients with feature_001=1 AND feature_021=1 AND feature_026=0
    AND feature_009=0, feature_014=1 increases objective_response from
    ~17% to ~79% (absolute risk increase ~+62 percentage points; OR ~18).
    In any patient violating any of these four predicates, feature_014 has
    no effect on objective_response.
- feature_004 is a strong prognostic factor (higher level → lower response)
  in both arms but does not modify the relative treatment effect.
- feature_020, feature_024, feature_027, feature_022, feature_029 are
  baseline prognostic continuous markers, not treatment-effect markers.
- feature_026 and feature_009 are pure treatment-effect suppressors within
  the dual-biomarker-positive stratum: they have no prognostic value on
  objective_response in any other context. Failing to recognize this
  context-specific role causes them to appear as small, marginally
  significant negative prognostic markers in univariate or naïve
  multivariable analyses.

Hypotheses supported (with the evidence noted above):
  h1, h2, h3, h4, h5, h6, h7, h8, h9, h10, h11, h12, h13, h14, h15, h16,
  h17, h18, h21, h22, h23, h26, h27, h28.
Hypotheses refuted:
  h19 (no further heterogeneity outside subgroup).
  h20 (feature_026/feature_009 are NOT prognostic outside dual-positive
       — their main effect arises entirely from suppressing the treatment
       effect within dual-positive).
  h24 (treatment is balanced — no imbalance detected, hypothesis confirmed
       in the form of "no significant imbalance").
  h25 (no other binary feature behaves like a treatment).
