Dataset: ds001_prostate (n = 50,000 male prostate-cancer patients; one binary outcome: objective_response, marginal rate 0.2402; six treatment indicators; 26 baseline features). All patients have sex_female = 0 — that variable was dropped from any further consideration. No missing data.

This summary walks through the iterative analysis from main effects through multivariable adjustment to systematic treatment-effect heterogeneity. Direction, magnitude, and p-values are reported on the natural scale (absolute response-rate differences for stratified analyses; odds ratios for logistic models).

============================================================
1. Treatment marginals (Iteration 2)
============================================================
Only enzalutamide had a meaningful unadjusted effect on objective_response:
  - treatment_enzalutamide: 0.3614 vs 0.1589 (diff +0.2024, chi-square p ≈ 0)
  - treatment_abiraterone:  0.2381 vs 0.2411 (diff −0.0030, p = 0.47)
  - treatment_docetaxel:    0.2399 vs 0.2404 (diff −0.0005, p = 0.91)
  - treatment_olaparib:     0.2393 vs 0.2403 (diff −0.0010, p = 0.89)
  - treatment_lu177_psma:   0.2427 vs 0.2398 (diff +0.0029, p = 0.60)
  - treatment_pembrolizumab:0.2387 vs 0.2403 (diff −0.0016, p = 0.88)

The five non-enzalutamide treatments showed no marginal effect, motivating searches for subgroup effects later.

============================================================
2. Disease and biomarker main effects (Iterations 3–4)
============================================================
Strong negative prognostic factors for response:
  - mcrpc=1 vs 0:                0.1535 vs 0.3460  (diff −0.1925, p ≈ 0)
  - ar_v7_positive=1 vs 0:       0.1599 vs 0.2604  (diff −0.1005, p = 1.9e-98)
  - brca2_mutation=1 vs 0:       0.1497 vs 0.2503  (diff −0.1006, p = 5.4e-56)
  - ecog_ps 0/1/2:               0.282 / 0.228 / 0.182 (monotonic, p = 5.4e-72)
  - msi_high=1 vs 0:             0.1760 vs 0.2422  (diff −0.0662, p = 3.0e-9)

Null on response: visceral_mets, gleason_score, psma_high.

Note: BRCA2 mutation, AR-V7 positivity, and MSI-high all have NEGATIVE marginal associations with response. This pattern flags them as candidate effect modifiers (resistance markers) rather than independent risk factors, since their negative marginals could simply reflect that the dominant treatment driver (enzalutamide) does not work in those patients.

5. Treatment-effect heterogeneity for enzalutamide (Iterations 7–11, 14–15)
============================================================
Stratified analyses show enzalutamide benefit is concentrated in patients without specific resistance markers:

  By mcrpc: hormone-sensitive (mcrpc=0) +0.4403 (p ≈ 0); CRPC (mcrpc=1) +0.0077 (p = 0.09).
            Logistic interaction OR = 0.128, beta = −2.05, p ≈ 0.

  By ar_v7: AR-V7 negative +0.2510 (p ≈ 0); AR-V7 positive +0.0085 (p = 0.27).
            Interaction OR = 0.259 (p = 9e-104).

  By brca2: wild-type +0.2246 (p ≈ 0); mutated +0.0063 (p = 0.57).
            Interaction OR = 0.292 (p = 4e-46).

  By msi:   MSI not high +0.2092 (p ≈ 0); MSI-high −0.0221 (p = 0.30).
            Interaction OR = 0.247 (p = 2e-21).

  By psma_high: +0.2042 vs +0.1998 (interaction OR 1.04, p = 0.37) — NOT a modifier.
  By visceral_mets: +0.2030 vs +0.2001 (interaction OR 1.00, p = 0.97) — NOT a modifier.

Joint logistic with all four enz × marker interactions:
  enzalutamide main effect (in all-favorable cell)  OR 13.80 (p ≈ 0)
  enz × mcrpc                  OR 0.107 (p ≈ 0)
  enz × ar_v7_positive         OR 0.193 (p = 6e-140)
  enz × brca2_mutation         OR 0.211 (p = 4e-67)
  enz × msi_high               OR 0.189 (p = 2e-27)

Each interaction collapses the OR back near 1, consistent with an all-or-nothing threshold rule.

============================================================
6. Subgroup discovery via tree (Iteration 14)
============================================================
A two-model gradient-boosting CATE estimator (separate response models on enz=1 and enz=0; CATE = pred_t − pred_c) yielded mean CATE 0.437 in mcrpc=0 and 0.009 in mcrpc=1. A depth-4 decision tree predicting "CATE > 0.30" produced a single positive leaf with rule: mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND msi_high=0. Every other leaf is negative. The data-driven subgroup matches the interaction model exactly.

============================================================
7. The full enzalutamide-favorable subgroup (Iterations 12, 13, 20, 24)
============================================================
Joint subgroup mcrpc=0 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0 AND msi_high=0 (n = 15,681):
  enzalutamide-treated response 0.7978 (n = 6,325)
  untreated response             0.1721 (n = 9,356)
  absolute difference           +0.6257 (p ≈ 0)

Necessity check — flipping each marker individually (others held favorable):
  mcrpc=1 only unfavorable (n=19,163):  enz +0.0074, p = 0.17 — abolished.
  brca2=1 only unfavorable (n=1,775):   enz +0.0038, p = 0.88 — abolished.
  ar_v7=1 only unfavorable (n=3,940):   enz +0.0215, p = 0.086 — abolished.
  msi_high=1 only unfavorable (n=494):  enz −0.0247, p = 0.58 — abolished.

Each marker independently nullifies the enzalutamide response benefit. ECOG modulates baseline response rates within the favorable subgroup but the enzalutamide effect itself is preserved across ECOG strata (differences +0.65 / +0.62 / +0.59 at ECOG 0/1/2).

============================================================
8. Other treatments — exhaustive subgroup checks (Iterations 16–19, 21, 25)
============================================================
For each of abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA, and pembrolizumab, we searched all 12 binary subgroups (mcrpc, visceral_mets, brca2_mutation, ar_v7_positive, msi_high, psma_high — each at 0 and 1), all logistic interaction terms with each binary marker and ECOG, and the canonical biomarker-matched subgroups.

Canonical biomarker-driven subgroup checks:
  olaparib in brca2_mutation=1: −0.032 (p = 0.058) — trend toward worse response, no benefit.
  pembrolizumab in msi_high=1:  +0.001 (p ≈ 1) — no benefit.
  lu177-PSMA in psma_high=1:    −0.001 (p = 0.88) — no benefit.

In the enzalutamide-favorable subgroup (n = 15,681), none of the other five treatments changed response (all |diff| < 0.02, all p > 0.16). Within mCRPC (n = 27,481), no drug detectably improved response (all p > 0.09, all |diff| < 0.01).

Two borderline cells emerged from ~60 subgroup tests — lu177-PSMA in visceral_mets=1 (diff −0.0243, p = 0.043) and pembrolizumab in visceral_mets=1 (diff −0.0305, p = 0.135). Given multiple-testing burden these are not robustly supported and certainly do not represent benefit.

Combination check: in the favorable subgroup, adding any second treatment to enzalutamide did not move response from the ~0.80 enzalutamide-only baseline (abiraterone 0.79, docetaxel 0.80, olaparib 0.77, lu177-PSMA 0.81, pembrolizumab 0.82). No synergy or antagonism.

============================================================
9. Hypotheses supported vs refuted
============================================================
Strongly supported:
  - Enzalutamide is the only treatment that changes objective_response in this cohort (h3, h16, h22, h28, h40).
  - Enzalutamide benefit is large (≈ +0.63 absolute) only in patients defined by ALL of: mcrpc=0, brca2_mutation=0, ar_v7_positive=0, msi_high=0 (h22, h27, h28, h40).
  - Each of the four markers individually nullifies enzalutamide benefit (h17, h18, h19, h20, h23, h24, h25, h26).
  - mcrpc, ar_v7_positive, brca2_mutation, ecog_ps, msi_high, weight_loss_pct_6mo, albumin_g_dl, psa_ng_ml, crp_mg_l are independent prognostic factors for response, in the predicted directions (h5, h6, h8, h9, h10, h12, h13, h14, h38).

Refuted (no detectable effect):
  - Marginal effect of abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA, or pembrolizumab on response (h4, h16, h41).
  - Olaparib benefit in BRCA2-mutated patients (h29).
  - Pembrolizumab benefit in MSI-high patients (h30).
  - Lu177-PSMA benefit in PSMA-high patients (h31).
  - Abiraterone or docetaxel benefit in any tested subgroup (h32, h33).
  - Synergy between enzalutamide and any other drug in the favorable subgroup (h35).
  - Effect modification of enzalutamide by psma_high or visceral_mets (h21).

Null main effects: visceral_mets, gleason_score, psma_high, age, sex (constant), hemoglobin, ALP, AST, ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, Na, K, Ca, NLR, LDH (h7, h11, h15).

============================================================
Overall conclusion
============================================================
In this cohort, objective response is driven by:
  (i)  whether the patient is hormone-sensitive (mcrpc=0) without resistance markers
       (brca2_mutation=0, ar_v7_positive=0, msi_high=0) AND receives enzalutamide —
       in which case response probability ≈ 0.80;
  (ii) baseline prognosis captured by mcrpc, ECOG, AR-V7, BRCA2, MSI-high, weight loss,
       albumin, PSA, and CRP — all in the conventional directions.

The other five treatments are prognostically/predictively inert for response in this
dataset, including in their canonical biomarker-defined subgroups. The enzalutamide
treatment-effect subgroup definition (mcrpc=0 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0
AND msi_high=0) was independently recovered by stratified analyses, by a logistic model
with all four interaction terms, and by a tree-based CATE search — three orthogonal
approaches converged on the same four-predicate rule.
