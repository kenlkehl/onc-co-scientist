ds001_prostate -- Analysis summary
======================================================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients (all male, sex_female=0). Outcome: objective_response (binary, base rate 24.0%).
Six treatments are coded as non-mutually-exclusive flags. Many patients receive 1-3 treatments concurrently.
Biomarkers: brca2_mutation (10%), ar_v7_positive (20%), msi_high (3%), psma_high (60%).
Performance: ECOG 0/1/2 = 35%/50%/15%. mCRPC=1 in 55%.

ITERATION-BY-ITERATION FINDINGS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

ITER 1 -- main treatment effects on objective_response (unadjusted):
  - treatment_enzalutamide: 36.1% vs 15.9%, +20.2 pp, p<1e-300 (HUGE main effect).
  - treatment_abiraterone: -0.3 pp, p=0.46 (null).
  - treatment_docetaxel: -0.05 pp, p=0.91 (null).
  - treatment_olaparib: -0.10 pp, p=0.87 (null).
  - treatment_lu177_psma: +0.29 pp, p=0.59 (null).
  - treatment_pembrolizumab: -0.16 pp, p=0.86 (null).

ITER 2 -- biomarker main effects (each is a poor-prognosis marker except PSMA):
  - brca2_mutation+: -10.0 pp (15.0% vs 25.0%), p~4e-56.
  - ar_v7_positive+: -10.1 pp (16.0% vs 26.0%), p~1e-98.
  - msi_high+: -6.6 pp (17.6% vs 24.2%), p~2.5e-9.
  - psma_high+: -0.4 pp, p=0.30 (no main effect).

ITER 3 -- prognostic features:
  - ECOG 0/1/2: 28.2% / 22.8% / 18.2% -- monotone, p<1e-37 for the ECOG=2 vs rest contrast.
  - mCRPC=1: 15.4% vs 34.6% in mCRPC=0; -19.2 pp, p~0. mCRPC is the strongest prognostic factor.
  - visceral_mets: -0.7 pp, not significant univariately.
  - age_years: no association (coef +0.0002, p=0.82).

ITER 4 -- continuous covariates (univariate logistic):
  Negative associations (lower response): log(PSA) p~3e-240, weight_loss_pct_6mo p~3e-29, log(CRP) p~1e-9.
  Positive: albumin_g_dl p~4e-6.
  Not significant univariately: gleason_score, log(LDH), log(NLR), log(ALP), log(bili), creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, hemoglobin, AST, ALT.

ITER 5 -- canonical treatment x biomarker interactions:
  - olaparib x brca2_mutation: T_B coef -0.29, p=0.045 (NEGATIVE interaction). BRCA2+: olaparib 12.1% vs 15.3%; BRCA2-: 25.3% vs 25.0%. Olaparib is NOT helpful in BRCA2+ patients.
  - pembrolizumab x msi_high: T_B coef +0.02, p=0.96 (NULL). MSI+: pembro 17.7% vs 17.6%. No incremental benefit even in MSI-high.
  - lu177_psma x psma_high: T_B coef -0.05, p=0.36 (NULL). PSMA+: 23.8% vs 23.9%. No benefit even in PSMA-high.
  - enzalutamide x ar_v7_positive: T_B coef -1.24, p~1e-93 (STRONG NEGATIVE interaction). AR-V7-: enza 41.1% vs 16.0% (+25 pp); AR-V7+: 16.5% vs 15.7% (no benefit).
  - abiraterone x ar_v7_positive: T_B coef +0.06, p=0.33 (null).

ITER 6 -- multivariable logistic with all features:
  Strongest independent predictors of objective_response (joint model):
  - mCRPC (-1.09, p~0), ar_v7_positive, brca2_mutation, ecog_ps, weight_loss, log(PSA), albumin_g_dl, msi_high.
  Among treatments, only enzalutamide retains a strong independent main effect (coef +1.42, p~0).
  abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA, and pembrolizumab all p>0.18 (null) after adjustment.

ITER 7 -- adjusted treatment x biomarker interactions (controlling for prognostic covariates):
  Only enzalutamide x AR-V7 is unambiguously significant (TB=-1.35, p~1.5e-103). Olaparib x BRCA2 borderline negative (p=0.05).
  Pembro x MSI, Lu177 x PSMA, Abi x AR-V7 all null.

ITER 8 -- enza/abi stratified by AR-V7 status (concrete subgroup rates):
  AR-V7-: enza 41.1% vs 16.0% (n=16057 vs 23905), p~0. AR-V7+: enza 16.5% vs 15.7%, p=0.26.
  AR-V7-: abi 25.7% vs 26.2%, p=0.30. AR-V7+: abi 16.3% vs 15.8%, p=0.53. Abi null in both halves.

ITER 9 -- heterogeneity screen across 11 covariates for enzalutamide:
  Largest absolute effect: PSA-low half: enza diff +31.3 pp; PSA-high half: +9.1 pp.
  Other median-split modifiers (ECOG, LDH, albumin, gleason, hemoglobin, weight loss, etc.) all show enza diff in the +0.20 range -- effect is roughly constant in both halves.
  Conclusion: PSA acts as an apparent modifier in the marginal sense, but the dominant modifier is AR-V7 (and, as we discover next, mCRPC).

ITER 10 -- exhaustive subgroups for olaparib in BRCA2+:
  Effect within BRCA2+ overall: -3.2 pp, p=0.05.
  By modifier in BRCA2+: ECOG<=1 -4.7 pp (p=0.01); ECOG=2 +4.7 pp (p=0.18, n_t=87 only); albumin-low -5.5 pp (p=0.02); albumin-high -1.2 pp (p=0.61); visceral=0 -3.2 pp; visceral=1 -3.3 pp.
  In BRCA2- patients: 25.3% vs 25.0%, p=0.59 (null).

ITER 11 -- exhaustive subgroups for pembrolizumab in MSI-high:
  Effect within MSI+ overall: +0.1 pp, p=0.98.
  No subgroup shows pembro benefit: ECOG<=1 +0.8 pp; visceral=0 +0.9 pp; alb-high +1.4 pp -- all p>0.85.
  In MSI-low patients: pembro 24.1% vs 24.2%, p=0.87.

ITER 12 -- exhaustive subgroups for Lu177-PSMA in PSMA-high:
  Effect within PSMA+ overall: -0.1 pp, p=0.87. No favorable joint subgroup found (LDH-low+ECOG<=1 +0.1 pp, p=0.94).
  In PSMA-low patients: Lu177 25.0% vs 24.1%, p=0.30.

ITER 13 -- mCRPC interaction discovery (key finding):
  enza in mCRPC=0: 61.0% vs 16.9%, +44 pp, p~0.
  enza in mCRPC=1: 15.8% vs 15.0%, +0.8 pp, p=0.09.
  abi/docetaxel are null in BOTH mCRPC strata.

ITER 14 -- combined enza subgroup definition:
  AR-V7-/mCRPC=0: enza 71.7% vs 17.1% (+54.6 pp, n=7246 vs 10754, p~0). THE strongest subgroup-defined treatment effect in the dataset.
  AR-V7-/mCRPC=1: +0.8 pp (null). AR-V7+/mCRPC=0: +1.4 pp (null). AR-V7+/mCRPC=1: +0.5 pp (null).
  Within AR-V7-/mCRPC=0, effect is similarly large in PSA-low (+54.7 pp) and PSA-high (+54.0 pp) halves; similarly in ECOG<=1 (+25.1) and ECOG=2 (+24.9, full AR-V7- subset).

ITER 15 -- multivariable model with all five canonical interactions:
  Only enza_x_arv7 is significant (coef -1.35, p~1e-103); olaparib x BRCA2 borderline (p=0.06); pembro x MSI, Lu177 x PSMA, abi x AR-V7 all null.
  Enzalutamide main effect remains very strong (+1.42, p~0).

ITER 16 -- heterogeneity within BRCA2+ for olaparib:
  Continuous-covariate × T interactions in the BRCA2+ subset: only albumin reaches borderline (T×alb p=0.05). Stratified -- olaparib worse in low-alb half (-5.5 pp). No covariate flips the negative direction.

ITER 17 -- biomarker-biomarker interactions:
  brca2 x msi: positive interaction +0.82 (p=3e-4) -- joint cohort fares less badly than additive expectation.
  msi x psma: -0.27 (p=0.05). brca2 x psma: null (p=0.98).

ITER 18 -- docetaxel heterogeneity sweep:
  ECOG, visceral, LDH, albumin median-splits all show docetaxel diff in [-0.008, +0.006]. No subgroup shows benefit.

ITER 19 -- olaparib direction sweep across BRCA2+ joint subgroups:
  All joint BRCA2+ subgroups show olaparib diff <= 0 except BRCA2+/ECOG=2 (+0.047, n=87, p=0.18). No positive subgroup is statistically supported. Direction is uniformly null-to-negative.

ITER 20 -- final treatment-effect subgroups (per outcome):
  BEST-SUPPORTED POSITIVE SUBGROUP: enza in ar_v7_positive=0 AND mcrpc=0 (response 71.7% vs 17.1%, +54.6 pp, n=7246+10754, p~0).
  No other treatment shows a positive treatment-effect subgroup that survives this dataset's tests.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. The single positive treatment effect detectable in the cohort is enzalutamide, and it is gated by TWO conditions: AR-V7-negative AND non-mCRPC. Inside this subgroup the response rate jumps from ~17% to ~72%. Either condition alone (AR-V7- OR mCRPC=0) only partially activates the effect, because each condition's missing modifier suppresses it.
2. AR-V7 acts as a classical resistance marker for AR-pathway inhibition (consistent with prostate-cancer biology); but in this cohort, abiraterone shows no benefit even in AR-V7-negative patients, contrary to the canonical clinical pattern.
3. mCRPC is the dominant overall prognostic factor (-1.09 logistic coef, response 15% vs 35% univariately). The enzalutamide effect is concentrated in non-mCRPC (castration-sensitive) disease.
4. The other targeted therapies show NO incremental benefit in their canonical biomarker-defined subgroups: olaparib in BRCA2+ is null-to-negative (probably reflects the prognostic effect of BRCA2 swamping any therapeutic benefit), pembrolizumab in MSI-high is null, Lu177-PSMA in PSMA-high is null. This is unusual relative to published clinical trials and likely a feature of how this synthetic/aggregated EHR cohort was assembled.
5. Strong INDEPENDENT prognostic predictors (multivariable) of lower objective_response: mCRPC, AR-V7+, BRCA2+, MSI+, higher ECOG, more weight loss, higher PSA, lower albumin.
6. Continuous lab markers other than PSA, albumin, weight loss, and CRP showed no main-effect association with response.

FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS (per outcome)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Outcome objective_response, treatment_enzalutamide:
  Direction: POSITIVE.
  Subgroup definition: ar_v7_positive = 0 AND mcrpc = 0.
  Estimated effect: +54.6 percentage-point increase in objective response rate (71.7% vs 17.1%).
  Outside this subgroup, the effect is essentially zero. Either AR-V7 = 1 OR mcrpc = 1 individually wipes out the enzalutamide benefit.

Outcome objective_response, other treatments: no positive treatment-effect subgroup is supported.
