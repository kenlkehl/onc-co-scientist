ds001_prostate — analysis summary
=================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with prostate cancer (all male). Outcome: objective_response,
binary, overall rate 24.0%. No missing data.

Six treatments are observed (non-mutually-exclusive): enzalutamide (40.2%),
abiraterone (30.0%), docetaxel (30.4%), olaparib (10.2%), Lu-177 PSMA (15.0%),
pembrolizumab (4.8%). 21.1% of patients receive no treatment, 39.9% receive one,
and the remainder receive 2-6 treatments.

==========================================================================
1. Main effects on objective_response (univariate)
==========================================================================

Patient-feature predictors (each direction below was confirmed by chi-square or
Welch t-test at p<0.001 unless noted):

* mCRPC=1 -> 15.4% response vs mCRPC=0 -> 34.6% (RD = -19.2 pp; p effectively 0).
  This is the single strongest non-treatment predictor.
* AR-V7 positive -> 16.0% vs 26.0% (RD = -10.0 pp; p = 1.4e-98).
* BRCA2 mutation -> 15.0% vs 25.0% (RD = -10.1 pp; p = 4.1e-56).
* MSI-high -> 17.6% vs 24.2% (RD = -6.6 pp; p = 2.5e-9).
* PSMA-high -> 23.9% vs 24.3% (NS; p = 0.30).
* Higher ECOG -> lower response (logit OR per unit = 0.75; p = 9.5e-73).
* Higher PSA -> lower response (mean PSA 28.2 in responders vs 47.2 in
  non-responders; p = 1.5e-137).
* Greater 6-month weight loss -> lower response (p = 6.8e-31).
* Higher CRP -> lower response (p = 9.3e-9).
* Higher albumin -> higher response (p = 3.6e-6).
* age, sex (all male), Gleason score, LDH, NLR, hemoglobin, alkaline phosphatase,
  AST, ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium: NS univariately
  for objective_response.

Treatment main effects (chi-square risk differences):

* enzalutamide  ON 36.1% vs OFF 15.9% -> RD = +20.2 pp, p effectively 0.
* abiraterone   ON 23.8% vs OFF 24.1% -> p = 0.46. NS.
* docetaxel     ON 24.0% vs OFF 24.0% -> p = 0.91. NS.
* olaparib      ON 23.9% vs OFF 24.0% -> p = 0.87. NS.
* Lu-177 PSMA   ON 24.3% vs OFF 24.0% -> p = 0.59. NS.
* pembrolizumab ON 23.9% vs OFF 24.0% -> p = 0.86. NS.

Only enzalutamide shows a non-zero main treatment effect. The other five
treatments must, if effective at all, be effective only in subgroups -- this
motivated the heterogeneity search.

==========================================================================
2. Multivariable logistic regression (objective_response ~ all features +
   all treatments)
==========================================================================

Independently negative coefficients (all p<10^-7): mcrpc (coef -1.13), ar_v7+
(-0.69), brca2 (-0.74), msi_high (-0.43), ecog_ps (-0.33), weight_loss (-0.036
per %), crp (-0.0073 per mg/L), psa (-0.001 per ng/mL).

Independently positive: albumin (+0.13 per g/dL).

Treatment coefficients (adjusted): enzalutamide +1.20 (OR 3.32, p~0). All five
other treatments: |coef| < 0.03, all p > 0.30. After adjustment, enzalutamide is
still the only treatment with detectable benefit.

==========================================================================
3. Treatment-effect heterogeneity (interactions and stratified RDs)
==========================================================================

Enzalutamide x mCRPC (logit interaction p effectively 0):
  - mCRPC=0: enza 60.96% vs 16.93% -> RD = +44.0 pp.
  - mCRPC=1: enza 15.81% vs 15.05% -> RD = +0.77 pp (essentially nothing).

Enzalutamide x AR-V7 (interaction p = 1.4e-93):
  - AR-V7-: enza 41.05% vs 15.95% -> RD = +25.1 pp.
  - AR-V7+: enza 16.50% vs 15.65% -> RD = +0.85 pp.

Enzalutamide x BRCA2 (interaction p = 3.3e-42):
  - BRCA2-: enza benefit large.
  - BRCA2+: enza benefit absent.

Enzalutamide x MSI-high (interaction p = 1.8e-19): same pattern. The negative
biomarkers (mCRPC, AR-V7+, BRCA2+, MSI-high) each abolish or attenuate the
enzalutamide benefit.

Enzalutamide x ECOG: statistically significant (p = 1.6e-5) but the absolute
RDs are nearly flat across ECOG strata (0/1/2 = +20.7%/+20.1%/+19.8%). ECOG is
a strong main predictor but only a trivial enza effect-modifier.

Enzalutamide x visceral_mets: NS (interaction p = 0.86; RDs +20.30% / +20.01%).

Lu-177 PSMA x visceral_mets: small but significant negative interaction
(coef -0.19, p = 0.010). Lu-177 trends slightly worse with visceral disease
(visceral=0 RD +0.99 pp; visceral=1 RD -2.43 pp).

Olaparib x BRCA2: SIGNIFICANT NEGATIVE interaction (coef -0.289, p = 0.045).
Stratified RDs:
  - BRCA2-: olap 25.3% vs 25.0% -> RD = +0.3 pp.
  - BRCA2+: olap 12.1% (n=529) vs 15.3% (n=4467) -> RD = -3.2 pp.
This is the OPPOSITE of the clinically expected synthetic-lethality pattern.
The negative direction was robust across mCRPC, ECOG, and visceral mets
sub-strata of BRCA2+ patients (5/6 of the ECOG x visceral cells negative).

Pembrolizumab x MSI-high: NS overall (interaction p = 0.96).

  - The expected immunotherapy benefit subgroup is most evident in MSI-high &
    mCRPC=0: pembro 29.4% (n=34) vs 19.4% (n=655), RD = +10 pp. This is
    suggestive but underpowered (n_on=34) and not statistically significant.

Lu-177 PSMA x PSMA-high: NS (interaction p = 0.36); within psma_high=1, no
ECOG/visceral/mCRPC subgroup yields a clinically meaningful positive RD
(all |RD| <= 0.027).

Abiraterone, docetaxel: no significant heterogeneity by mCRPC, visceral, ECOG,
or any examined biomarker (all interaction p > 0.30, all stratified RDs |<1pp|).

==========================================================================
4. Tree-based subgroup discovery (one tree per treatment)
==========================================================================

Decision-tree (max_depth=3, min_samples_leaf=200) fit to objective_response on
the treated subset of each treatment, then within-leaf RDs computed against
the untreated subset:

* enzalutamide: dominant leaf 3 = (mcrpc<=0.5 AND ar_v7_positive<=0.5 AND
  brca2_mutation<=0.5). On 6,514 treated: 78.0% response. Off 9,661 controls:
  17.3% response. RD = +60.7 pp. This is by far the largest treatment-effect
  subgroup discovered in the dataset.
* abiraterone: best leaf RD = +3.4 pp. No real subgroup benefit.
* docetaxel: best leaf RD = +2.3 pp. No real subgroup benefit.
* olaparib: best leaf RD = +5.0 pp (n_on=225). No clinically meaningful subgroup.
* Lu-177 PSMA: best leaf RD = +3.7 pp (n_on=423). No clinically meaningful subgroup.
* pembrolizumab: best leaf RD = +5.9 pp (n_on=239). No clinically meaningful subgroup.

==========================================================================
5. Best-supported FINAL treatment-effect subgroup hypotheses (one per
   treatment with usable variation)
==========================================================================

treatment_enzalutamide  -- objective_response HIGHER:
  Subgroup: mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0 (further refined by adding
  brca2_mutation=0 in the tree-derived leaf).
  Effect size: in mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0, response 71.7% (n_on=7246) vs
  17.1% (n_off=10754); RD = +54.6 pp. Adding brca2_mutation=0 lifts the
  treated rate to 78.0% (RD = +60.7 pp).
  Suppressors (each independently abolishes the effect):
    - mcrpc=1 (RD collapses to +0.77 pp).
    - ar_v7_positive=1 (RD collapses to +0.85 pp).
    - brca2_mutation=1 (large negative interaction, RD collapses).
    - msi_high=1 (large negative interaction, RD collapses).

treatment_olaparib -- NO subgroup with positive RD identified:
  The clinically expected brca2_mutation=1 subgroup actually shows a small
  NEGATIVE RD (-3.2 pp, interaction p = 0.045) that is robust across ECOG,
  visceral, and mCRPC strata. No subgroup defined by the available features
  shows a clinically meaningful positive RD on objective_response.

treatment_pembrolizumab -- BEST candidate subgroup is msi_high=1 AND mcrpc=0:
  Response 29.4% (n_on=34) vs 19.4% (n_off=655); RD = +10.0 pp. Direction is
  consistent with the clinical prior (immunotherapy in MSI-high cancers) but
  the sample is too small to reach statistical significance.

treatment_lu177_psma -- NO subgroup with clinically meaningful positive RD:
  Even within psma_high=1 AND mcrpc=0 AND ECOG=0 AND visceral=0, RD is ~0.
  Modest negative interaction with visceral_mets (interaction p = 0.010).

treatment_abiraterone -- NO subgroup with positive RD:
  Universally inert across all biomarker, ECOG, mCRPC, and visceral subgroups
  examined.

treatment_docetaxel -- NO subgroup with positive RD:
  Universally inert across all subgroups examined.

==========================================================================
6. Summary of supported vs refuted hypotheses
==========================================================================

Supported (predicted direction confirmed at p<0.05):
* mCRPC, AR-V7+, BRCA2+, MSI-high, higher ECOG, higher PSA, greater weight
  loss, higher CRP each independently lower response (univariate AND adjusted).
* Higher albumin raises response (small but significant).
* Enzalutamide raises response.
* Enzalutamide effect is concentrated in hormone-sensitive (mCRPC=0) and
  AR-V7-negative patients, abolished by mCRPC=1 or AR-V7+.
* Negative interactions of enzalutamide with BRCA2 and MSI-high (mediated by
  the very poor baseline response of those biomarker-defined patients).

Refuted or null:
* No detectable response benefit for abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib,
  Lu-177 PSMA, or pembrolizumab on objective_response, either overall or in
  any examined subgroup (with the partial exception of a suggestive but
  underpowered pembrolizumab signal in MSI-high & mCRPC=0).
* The expected olaparib synthetic-lethality benefit in BRCA2-mutant patients
  is NOT present in this cohort; the direction is mildly negative.
* The expected Lu-177 PSMA benefit in PSMA-high patients is NOT present.
* Visceral metastases, age, Gleason score, LDH, NLR, hemoglobin, AlkP, and
  electrolytes show no univariate or multivariable association with
  objective_response in this cohort.

==========================================================================
7. Overall conclusion
==========================================================================

In this 50,000-patient prostate-cancer cohort, the only treatment with a
detectable effect on objective_response is enzalutamide, and that effect is
overwhelmingly concentrated in patients who are NOT castration-resistant
(mcrpc=0) and NOT AR-V7 positive -- the hormone-sensitive, AR-pathway-intact
subgroup. The poor-prognosis biomarkers BRCA2 mutation, AR-V7+, MSI-high, and
mCRPC each independently reduce response and individually nullify the
enzalutamide benefit. Treatments that would in principle target the other
biomarker-defined subgroups (olaparib for BRCA2, Lu-177 PSMA for PSMA-high,
pembrolizumab for MSI-high) do NOT, in this dataset, deliver the response
benefit their clinical premise predicts; only the pembrolizumab x MSI-high x
mcrpc=0 subgroup shows a directionally favorable but underpowered signal.
