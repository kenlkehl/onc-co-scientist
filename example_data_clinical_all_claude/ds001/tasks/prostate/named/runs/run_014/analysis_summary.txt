Analysis summary — ds001_prostate (n=50,000 prostate-cancer patients; outcome = objective_response, prevalence 24.0%)
=====================================================================================================================

Headline finding
----------------
The dataset contains a single dominant treatment signal: treatment_enzalutamide produces a large positive effect on
objective_response that is restricted to a clean four-criterion subgroup defined by mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0
AND brca2_mutation=0 AND msi_high=0 (31% of the cohort, n=15,681). In this subgroup the response rate is 79.8% on
enzalutamide vs 17.2% off (risk difference +0.63, adjusted odds ratio ≈ 20). Outside this subgroup the enzalutamide
effect collapses to RD ≈ +0.01 (p ≈ 0.06). None of the other five treatments — abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib,
lu177-PSMA, pembrolizumab — show a main effect or a clinically meaningful subgroup effect anywhere in the data,
including the canonical biomarker pairings (olaparib×BRCA2, pembrolizumab×MSI-high, lu177-PSMA×PSMA-high).

How the analysis evolved across the 25 iterations
-------------------------------------------------
Iterations 1–4 (main effects).  Marginal chi-square / univariate logistic regression for each of the six treatments,
the four molecular biomarkers, the two staging variables (mcrpc, visceral_mets), and the seventeen continuous clinical
features.  Treatment_enzalutamide stood out immediately (response 36.1% on vs 15.9% off, RD=+0.20, p≈0).  Every other
treatment had a marginal RD within ±0.003 of zero.  Strong negative prognostic markers: mcrpc (RD=-0.19, p≈0),
ar_v7_positive (RD=-0.10, p=2e-98), brca2_mutation (RD=-0.10, p=5e-56), msi_high (RD=-0.07, p=3e-9), and continuous
ecog_ps (OR=0.75 per unit, p=1e-72), psa_ng_ml (OR=0.995 per ng/mL, p=2e-94), weight_loss_pct_6mo (OR=0.97 per %,
p=3e-29), albumin_g_dl (OR=1.10 per g/dL, p=4e-6), and crp_mg_l (OR=0.99 per mg/L, p=6e-8).  age_years and
gleason_score were null.  Visceral_mets and psma_high had no marginal association.

Iteration 5 (adjusted treatment effects).  A logistic model with all six treatments, all binary biomarkers, and the
strongest continuous prognostic features confirmed enzalutamide remains highly significant after adjustment (OR=3.32,
p≈0); all other treatments stayed null (|adjusted log-OR|≤0.03, p>0.3 for each).

Iteration 6 (predefined biomarker→targeted-therapy hypotheses).  We tested the three textbook pairings: olaparib in
BRCA2-mutant disease (RD=-0.03, p=0.06 — direction is opposite to expected and not significant); pembrolizumab in
MSI-high disease (RD=+0.001, p=1.00); lu177-PSMA in PSMA-high disease (RD=-0.001, p=0.88).  All three are refuted.

Iterations 7–9 (enzalutamide effect modifiers).  AR-V7 splits enzalutamide patients sharply: among AR-V7-negative
patients, enzalutamide RD=+0.25; among AR-V7-positive, RD=+0.008 (interaction p=1e-93).  mCRPC has an even larger
modifying effect: among non-mCRPC, enzalutamide RD=+0.44; among mCRPC, RD=+0.008 (interaction p≈0).  Stratifying by
the joint mcrpc × ar_v7_positive yields RD=+0.546 in non-mCRPC AND non-AR-V7+ vs ~0 in every other cell.

Iterations 10–12 (heterogeneity screen for enzalutamide).  Treatment×feature interactions of enzalutamide with every
binary and median-split continuous feature were screened.  Significant negative modifiers: mcrpc, ar_v7_positive,
brca2_mutation (coef=-1.14, p=3e-42), msi_high (coef=-1.28, p=2e-19), and psa>median (driven by mCRPC confounding).
Inside the non-mCRPC AND non-AR-V7+ subgroup, BRCA2 status alone ablates the effect (RD=+0.004 in BRCA2+ within
subgroup vs +0.607 in BRCA2- within subgroup, interaction p=7e-90), and MSI-high does likewise (interaction p=1e-33).
PSMA-high has only a small interaction (coef=-0.21) and the effect remains very large in both PSMA strata, so PSMA
is NOT part of the subgroup definition.

Iterations 13–14 (final enzalutamide subgroup definition).  Combining all four negative modifiers:
mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND msi_high=0 (n=15,681).  In subgroup: enza RD=+0.626 (p≈0).
In complement: RD=+0.008 (p=0.06).  Adjusted for age, PSA, ECOG, albumin, CRP, weight loss, visceral_mets, PSMA,
Gleason, and the other five treatments, the within-subgroup enzalutamide log-odds = +3.02 (OR=20.5, p≈0).

Iterations 15–19 (heterogeneity screens for the other five treatments).  Each treatment was passed through the same
binary- and continuous-interaction battery.  Findings:
  • Abiraterone: only abi×brca2_mutation reached p<0.05 (coef=+0.19, p=0.035), but the BRCA2+ stratified RD is
    +0.012 (p=0.49) — null.  No subgroup with appreciable benefit.
  • Docetaxel: nominally significant interactions with alkaline_phosphatase>median and alt>median, but coefficients
    are small (<0.10) and stratified RDs are essentially zero.  No subgroup with appreciable benefit.
  • Olaparib: BRCA2 interaction is *negative* (coef=-0.29, p=0.045).  In BRCA2-mutant patients, olaparib RD=-0.032
    (p=0.06); in BRCA2+ AND mCRPC, RD=-0.041 (p=0.07); in BRCA2+ AND non-mCRPC, RD=-0.021 (p=0.46).  No subgroup
    with positive effect, contradicting the canonical mechanism.
  • Lu177-PSMA: visceral_mets interaction (coef=-0.19, p=0.010).  In visceral_mets=1, lu177 RD=-0.024 (p=0.04); in
    visceral_mets=0, lu177 RD=+0.010 (p=0.10).  Tiny magnitudes; no clinically meaningful positive subgroup.
  • Pembrolizumab: nominal interactions with crp>median (negative) and albumin>median (positive); none robust to
    multiple testing and no stratified subgroup including MSI-high shows benefit (MSI-high stratum RD=+0.001).

Iteration 20 (full interaction logistic per treatment).  Confirms heterogeneity is concentrated in enzalutamide:
four interaction terms with p<1e-19 each; for every other treatment, at most one nominally significant interaction
with small coefficient (|coef|≤0.30).

Iterations 21–22 (combined treatment effects within and outside the responder subgroup).  In the responder subgroup,
a logistic with all six treatments yields enza coef=+2.95 and every other treatment coefficient with |coef|<0.08
and p>0.10.  In the complement subgroup, all six coefficients are <0.10 with p>0.05.  This rigorously establishes
that adding any of the other five drugs to enzalutamide in the responder subgroup, or substituting them in the
complement subgroup, does not improve response.

Iteration 23 (tree-based subgroup discovery).  A regression tree fit to the Tian-style transformed-outcome
pseudo-response for enzalutamide independently splits on mcrpc, then ar_v7_positive, then brca2_mutation; the leaves
where all three are 0 (the only branches that reach an enza-RD > +0.5) cover n=16,175 — close to our manually defined
non-mCRPC AND non-AR-V7+ AND non-BRCA2 subgroup.  MSI-high additionally trims a small low-prevalence pocket where
the effect is null, completing the four-criterion subgroup.

Iteration 24 (necessity of all four predicates).  Stratification "any one of mcrpc, ar_v7_positive, brca2_mutation,
msi_high present" yields RD=+0.008 (p=0.06).  Stratification "all four are 0" yields RD=+0.626 (p≈0).  Each
predicate is independently sufficient to suppress the effect; the conjunction is necessary.

Iteration 25 (final consolidated hypotheses).  Two summary statements:
  (1) In male prostate-cancer patients with mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND msi_high=0,
      treatment_enzalutamide raises objective_response from ~17% to ~80% (adjusted OR ≈ 20).
  (2) None of treatment_abiraterone, treatment_docetaxel, treatment_olaparib, treatment_lu177_psma, or
      treatment_pembrolizumab shows a main effect or a clinically meaningful subgroup effect on objective_response
      anywhere in the cohort.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
Supported (significant in the predicted direction):
  • Enzalutamide raises objective_response (overall and especially in non-mCRPC, non-AR-V7+, non-BRCA2, non-MSI).
  • mCRPC, AR-V7+, BRCA2-mutant, MSI-high, high ECOG, high PSA, high CRP, high weight loss, low albumin: each
    associated with lower response.
  • Negative interactions of enzalutamide with mcrpc, ar_v7_positive, brca2_mutation, msi_high.

Refuted (null or opposite direction in this cohort):
  • Olaparib provides benefit in BRCA2-mutant patients — RD slightly negative.
  • Pembrolizumab provides benefit in MSI-high patients — RD = +0.001.
  • Lu177-PSMA provides benefit in PSMA-high patients — RD = -0.001.
  • Abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA, pembrolizumab provide ANY main or subgroup effect on response.
  • Visceral_mets is a prognostic factor (it isn't, marginally).

Best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis (per protocol)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment: treatment_enzalutamide.
Outcome:   objective_response.
Direction: positive (treatment increases response).
Subgroup predicates (ALL required): mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND msi_high=0.
Effect:    crude risk difference +0.626 (response 79.8% on vs 17.2% off, n=15,681, p<1e-300); adjusted log-OR +3.02
            (OR≈20.5).  In any patient with at least one of these four predicates failing, enzalutamide effect is
            negligible (RD=+0.008, p=0.06).

For the other five treatments there is no plausible positive treatment-effect subgroup anywhere in the data; the
final hypothesis is that they have null effects on objective_response in this cohort under any subgroup defined by
the available features.
