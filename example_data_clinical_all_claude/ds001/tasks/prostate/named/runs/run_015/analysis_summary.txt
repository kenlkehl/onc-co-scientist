ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_prostate (n=50000, all male prostate-cancer cohort)
Outcome: objective_response (binary; overall rate 24.0%)
Treatments studied: enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA, pembrolizumab
Iterations performed: 16 (cap was 25); analyses span main effects, multi-feature interactions, and systematic subgroup-discovery for every treatment.

==================================================================================
1. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS (Iter 1)
==================================================================================
Only ONE treatment shows a marginal benefit on objective_response in the cohort as a whole:

  treatment_enzalutamide     : 36.1% on tx vs 15.9% off  (diff +0.202, p~0)        ← STRONG
  treatment_abiraterone      : 23.8% vs 24.1%             (diff -0.003, p=0.46)    NS
  treatment_docetaxel        : 24.0% vs 24.0%             (diff -0.001, p=0.91)    NS
  treatment_olaparib         : 23.9% vs 24.0%             (diff -0.001, p=0.87)    NS
  treatment_lu177_psma       : 24.3% vs 24.0%             (diff +0.003, p=0.59)    NS
  treatment_pembrolizumab    : 23.9% vs 24.0%             (diff -0.002, p=0.86)    NS

Implication: the headline 20-percentage-point benefit attached to enzalutamide must contain
substantial subgroup heterogeneity (since several other "modern" therapies show no marginal
benefit at all in this cohort). All six treatments became targets for systematic
treatment-effect-heterogeneity searches.

==================================================================================
2. BIOMARKER & CLINICAL MAIN EFFECTS (Iter 2–3)
==================================================================================
Biomarkers (univariable):
  brca2_mutation = 1     → 14.97% vs 25.03%   (diff -0.10, p~0)        unfavorable
  ar_v7_positive = 1     → 15.99% vs 26.04%   (diff -0.10, p~0)        unfavorable
  msi_high = 1           → 17.60% vs 24.22%   (diff -0.066, p=2.5e-9)  unfavorable
  psma_high = 1          → 23.86% vs 24.26%   (diff -0.004, p=0.30)    NS

Clinical features:
  ecog_ps                : monotone — 0:28.2%, 1:22.8%, 2:18.2% (chi-square p=5e-72)
  mcrpc = 1              : 15.4% vs 34.6%     (diff -0.193, p~0)        STRONG unfavorable
  visceral_mets = 1      : 24.0% vs 24.0%     (diff -0.001, p=0.86)    NS
  gleason_score          : flat across categories (chi-square p=0.77)   NS
  age_years              : null (logit beta +0.0002, p=0.82)            NS

Hypotheses about ecog_ps and mcrpc as unfavorable were strongly SUPPORTED.
Hypotheses about visceral_mets and gleason_score and age were REFUTED (no main effect
on response). PSMA-high also has no marginal effect.

==================================================================================
3. CONTINUOUS LAB MAIN EFFECTS (Iter 4)
==================================================================================
Independent univariable predictors of LOWER response:
  psa_ng_ml                  : beta -0.0047, p=2e-94   (highly significant)
  weight_loss_pct_6mo        : beta -0.0315, p=3e-29
  crp_mg_l                   : beta -0.0069, p=6e-08
  albumin_g_dl               : beta +0.0959, p=4e-06   (HIGHER albumin → MORE response)
All other labs were null (ldh_u_l, hemoglobin_g_dl, alk-phos, AST, ALT, bilirubin,
creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, NLR — none significant univariably).

==================================================================================
4. TREATMENT × BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS (Iter 5)
==================================================================================
The "expected" mechanistic positive interactions were largely ABSENT in this dataset:

  Olaparib × BRCA2:
      stratum mod=1: rate diff -0.032 (p=0.05);  mod=0: -0.000 (p=0.65)
      interaction beta NEGATIVE (p=0.045) — direction OPPOSITE the canonical
      precision-oncology expectation that BRCA2-mutated patients should benefit more
      from olaparib. In this cohort olaparib is null or slightly harmful even in
      BRCA2+ patients. The companion negation analysis (Iter 13) confirms this.

  Pembrolizumab × MSI-high:
      mod=1 diff +0.001 (p=0.98);  mod=0 diff -0.002 (p=0.87)
      interaction p=0.96 — no signal whatsoever. The expected MSI-high benefit is
      not observed; pembrolizumab is null in MSI-high patients in this cohort.

  Lu-177-PSMA × PSMA-high:
      mod=1 diff -0.001 (p=0.87);  mod=0 diff +0.009 (p=0.30)
      interaction p=0.36 — null. The expected PSMA-targeted theranostic effect
      in PSMA-high patients is absent.

  Enzalutamide × AR-V7-positive:
      mod=1 diff +0.008 (p=0.26);  mod=0 diff +0.251 (p~0)
      interaction beta -1.24 (p=1.4e-93) — STRONG negative interaction. AR-V7
      positivity essentially eliminates the enzalutamide response.

  Enzalutamide × BRCA2:
      mod=1 diff +0.006 (p=0.54);  mod=0 diff +0.225 (p~0)
      interaction beta -1.14 (p=3.3e-42). BRCA2 status also abolishes the enza effect.

  Enzalutamide × MSI-high:
      mod=1 diff -0.022 (p=0.27);  mod=0 diff +0.209 (p~0)
      interaction beta -1.28 (p=1.8e-19). MSI-high also abolishes the enza effect.

==================================================================================
5. TREATMENT × CLINICAL INTERACTIONS (Iter 6)
==================================================================================
The strongest single signal in the entire dataset:

  Enzalutamide × mCRPC:
      mod=1 (mCRPC) diff +0.008 (p=0.084);  mod=0 (non-mCRPC) diff +0.440 (p~0)
      interaction beta -1.98 (p~0). In mCRPC the enzalutamide effect collapses to
      near-zero; in non-mCRPC it is enormous (44 percentage points).

Lu-177-PSMA × visceral_mets: weak (inter p=0.01, mod=1 diff -0.024 vs mod=0 +0.010);
Lu-177-PSMA × gleason_high : weak (inter p=0.036, mod=1 diff +0.026 vs mod=0 -0.003);
Pembrolizumab × gleason_high: weak (inter p=0.044, mod=1 diff +0.035 vs mod=0 -0.011).
These are marginal, multiple-comparison-suspect signals and do not reach the
robustness threshold of the enzalutamide subgroup pattern.

==================================================================================
6. TREATMENT × CONTINUOUS-LAB INTERACTIONS (Iter 7)
==================================================================================
Systematic screen surfaces only enzalutamide-related signals. The strongest is
enzalutamide × psa_ng_ml (interaction p ≈ 2e-94 on a logit basis): in the
above-median PSA stratum the enza effect drops to +0.091 vs +0.313 in below-median.
Other lab interactions involve enzalutamide × albumin, ldh, alk-phos, weight loss,
CRP — all essentially capturing the same correlated "disease-burden" axis driving the
mCRPC pattern. No usable interaction signal for the other five treatments survives.

==================================================================================
7. MULTIVARIABLE ADJUSTED MODEL (Iter 8)
==================================================================================
Full logistic regression with all features simultaneously confirms the picture:
  - treatment_enzalutamide retains a strongly positive adjusted coefficient (highly
    significant), even after controlling for all biomarkers, labs, and other treatments.
  - treatment_abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA, pembrolizumab show no
    significant independent association with objective_response.
  - mcrpc remains independently associated with LOWER response after adjustment.
  - brca2_mutation, ar_v7_positive, msi_high all retain independent NEGATIVE effects
    on response (the unfavorable main effects are real, not just marker-of-high-burden).

==================================================================================
8. SYSTEMATIC TREATMENT-EFFECT-HETEROGENEITY SEARCH (Iter 9 + 10)
==================================================================================
For each treatment we screened ~30+ binarized modifiers (biomarkers + clinical + lab
median splits) and computed both the stratified rate-difference and the logistic
interaction p-value. Then we tested two-feature joint subgroups (~40 pairs per
treatment) of the form "A=v1 AND B=v2".

Top heterogeneity signals per treatment:
  treatment_enzalutamide  : mcrpc / ar_v7_positive / brca2_mutation / msi_high
                            (all four independently kill the effect; see Iter 16)
  treatment_olaparib      : brca2_mutation suppresses the effect (opposite of expected)
                            BUN-high also marginal (interaction p=0.015) — likely chance
                            in the context of overall null effect.
  treatment_pembrolizumab : crp_mg_l-high suppresses (mod=1 diff -0.025 vs mod=0 +0.022,
                            interaction p=0.0075). Marginal and only one of many tests.
  treatment_lu177_psma    : visceral_mets suppresses (interaction p=0.01); gleason_high
                            mildly enhances (interaction p=0.036). Weak.
  treatment_abiraterone   : nothing convincing.
  treatment_docetaxel     : nothing convincing.

==================================================================================
9. NEGATION TESTS (Iter 13)
==================================================================================
For three biomarker-targeted hypotheses we tested both within and outside the
canonical biomarker-positive subgroup:
  Olaparib in BRCA2+ (n=4996):           diff -0.032 (p=0.05)
  Olaparib in BRCA2- (n=45004):          diff +0.003 (p=0.65)
  → Hypothesis "olaparib helps BRCA2+" is REFUTED in this cohort.
  Pembrolizumab in MSI-high (n=1528):    diff +0.001 (p=0.98)
  Pembrolizumab in MSI-low  (n=48472):   diff -0.002 (p=0.87)
  → Hypothesis "pembrolizumab helps MSI-high" is REFUTED.
  Lu-177-PSMA in PSMA-high (n=29962):    diff -0.001 (p=0.87)
  Lu-177-PSMA in PSMA-low  (n=20038):    diff +0.009 (p=0.30)
  → Hypothesis "Lu-177-PSMA helps PSMA-high" is REFUTED.

==================================================================================
10. ADJUSTED THREE-WAY CONFIRMATION (Iter 14)
==================================================================================
Logistic regression with biomarker × treatment interaction PLUS adjustment for age,
ECOG, mCRPC, visceral mets, Gleason, albumin, LDH, ALP, hemoglobin, PSA, NLR, CRP:
  olaparib × brca2_mutation interaction: beta -0.27 (p=0.063)  not positive
  pembrolizumab × msi_high interaction:  beta +0.07 (p=0.82)   null
  lu177-psma × psma_high interaction:    beta -0.06 (p=0.30)   null
The targeted-therapy subgroup-rescue hypotheses fail in the adjusted model too.

==================================================================================
11. COMBINATION-TREATMENT EXPLORATION (Iter 15)
==================================================================================
Patients receiving more concurrent treatments tend to show higher response rates,
but this is dominated by enzalutamide membership. Among (enzalutamide=1 & abiraterone=1)
vs (enzalutamide=1 alone) there is no additional benefit — abiraterone adds nothing
on top of enzalutamide.

==================================================================================
12. FINAL ENZALUTAMIDE RESPONDER SUBGROUP (Iter 16) — KEY DELIVERABLE
==================================================================================
Combining all suppressors into a single complete subgroup definition:

   RESPONDER SUBGROUP:
       mcrpc = 0  AND  ar_v7_positive = 0  AND  brca2_mutation = 0  AND  msi_high = 0

   In this subgroup (n=15,681; 6,325 on enza vs 9,356 controls):
       rate on enzalutamide  = 0.798
       rate off enzalutamide = 0.172
       rate diff             = +0.626  (62.6 percentage points, p ≈ 0)

   Outside this subgroup (n=34,319; 13,751 on enza vs 20,568 controls):
       rate on enzalutamide  = 0.161
       rate off enzalutamide = 0.153
       rate diff             = +0.008  (p = 0.054)

The subgroup is COMPLETE: each unfavorable value individually nullifies the
enzalutamide benefit. Within non-mCRPC patients alone, stratifying further by
each biomarker:
   non-mCRPC & ar_v7=0 : diff +0.546 (p~0)
   non-mCRPC & ar_v7=1 : diff +0.014 (p=0.23)
   non-mCRPC & brca2=0 : diff +0.491 (p~0)
   non-mCRPC & brca2=1 : diff -0.012 (p=0.43)
   non-mCRPC & msi=0   : diff +0.454 (p~0)
   non-mCRPC & msi=1   : diff -0.012 (p=0.71)
Each of ar_v7_positive, brca2_mutation, msi_high independently suppresses the
otherwise enormous treatment_enzalutamide benefit. mcRPC alone also suppresses
across all biomarker strata.

==================================================================================
13. OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
==================================================================================
A. Treatment effects: only treatment_enzalutamide produces a meaningful positive
   shift in objective_response in this cohort. The marginal effect (+20 percentage
   points) hides a striking subgroup pattern: the entire benefit is concentrated in
   ~31% of patients (n=15,681) who are simultaneously non-mCRPC, AR-V7-negative,
   BRCA2-wildtype, and MSI-low. In those patients enzalutamide raises the response
   rate from 17% to 80% (a 63-point absolute increase).

B. Targeted therapies behave UNEXPECTEDLY: the canonical mechanistic stories
   (olaparib→BRCA2+, pembrolizumab→MSI-high, Lu-177-PSMA→PSMA-high) are NOT
   reproduced in this cohort. Both crude and adjusted analyses show null or
   counterintuitive directions. We tested these explicitly and treated each as a
   refutable hypothesis; all three were refuted.

C. Independent prognostic predictors of LOWER response (treatment-agnostic):
   ecog_ps (monotone), mcrpc, brca2_mutation, ar_v7_positive, msi_high,
   higher psa_ng_ml, higher weight_loss_pct_6mo, higher crp_mg_l, lower albumin_g_dl.
   These act as a coherent "tumor-burden / aggressive-disease" axis.

D. Null variables: visceral_mets, gleason_score, age_years, hemoglobin_g_dl,
   alkaline_phosphatase, AST, ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium,
   calcium, NLR, psma_high — none are independent predictors of objective_response
   in this cohort.

E. Best-supported treatment-effect-heterogeneity claim, by treatment:
   • treatment_enzalutamide → strongly POSITIVE in {mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0
     AND brca2_mutation=0 AND msi_high=0}; near-zero outside (subgroup definition is
     complete; each unfavorable value independently abolishes the effect).
   • treatment_abiraterone → no responder subgroup found.
   • treatment_docetaxel → no responder subgroup found.
   • treatment_olaparib → no responder subgroup found; effect actually slightly
     negative in BRCA2-mutated patients.
   • treatment_lu177_psma → no robust responder subgroup; weak (and unreplicated)
     hint that high-Gleason or non-visceral patients fare slightly better.
   • treatment_pembrolizumab → no robust responder subgroup; weak (and unreplicated)
     hint that low-CRP / high-Gleason patients fare slightly better.
