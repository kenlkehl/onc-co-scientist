Analysis Summary — ds001_prostate (n = 50,000 patients; outcome: objective_response, prevalence 24.0%)
====================================================================================================

Headline finding
----------------
feature_008 is a randomized treatment indicator (40.2% prevalence; perfectly balanced across every
other feature, all balance p > 0.04). It carries by far the largest, and only positive, effect on
objective_response of any feature in the cohort: ORR 16% off-treatment vs 36% on-treatment overall
(unadjusted OR 2.99). However, this overall effect is entirely concentrated in a well-defined
subgroup of ~31% of patients — outside that subgroup treatment is essentially inert.

Final treatment-effect-positive subgroup (refined hypothesis h20.1)
-------------------------------------------------------------------
Subgroup definition: feature_013 = 0 AND feature_015 = 0 AND feature_021 = 0 AND feature_027 = 0
("all four resistance markers OFF").

Within this subgroup (n = 15,681; 31.4% of cohort):
  - ORR(feature_008 = 0) = 17.2%
  - ORR(feature_008 = 1) = 79.8%
  - Risk difference = +62.6 percentage points (bootstrap 95% CI [+61.4, +63.8])
  - Odds ratio = 19.0 (bootstrap 95% CI [17.6, 20.6])
  - NNT = 1.6

Outside this subgroup (n = 34,319; 68.6% of cohort):
  - ORR(feature_008 = 0) = 15.3%
  - ORR(feature_008 = 1) = 16.1%
  - Risk difference = +0.8 percentage points (essentially null)
  - Adjusted treatment OR collapses to ~1.06 (T x any_modifier interaction p < 1e-300)

Interaction structure
---------------------
The four modifiers (feature_013, feature_015, feature_021, feature_027) act in a SATURATION pattern,
not an additive log-OR pattern. Each modifier alone is sufficient to abolish the treatment effect:

  Number of modifiers ON | n      | RD on ORR (T1 - T0)
  -----------------------|--------|--------------------
  0                      | 15,681 | +0.626  (the responsive cell)
  1                      | 25,372 | +0.009
  2                      |  8,186 | +0.008
  3                      |    745 | -0.022
  4                      |     16 | -0.091

Formally testing this: a saturated cell-by-cell model fits ~1338 log-likelihood units better than an
additive 4-modifier-by-treatment interaction model (LRT chi-square = 1338 on 21 df, p = 1.5e-270).
A collapsed any_modifier-OR-of-the-4 indicator captures the heterogeneity essentially completely —
OR(treatment) = 19.0 when any_modifier = 0 vs OR(treatment) = 1.06 when any_modifier = 1.

Drop-one necessity check (every modifier matters):
  Definition                              | n      | RD on ORR
  ----------------------------------------|--------|----------
  All 4 modifiers = 0 (full responsive)   | 15,681 | +0.626
  Drop feature_013 from definition        | 34,844 | +0.287
  Drop feature_015 from definition        | 19,621 | +0.505
  Drop feature_021 from definition        | 17,456 | +0.563
  Drop feature_027 from definition        | 16,175 | +0.607

feature_013 is the most-prevalent (55%) and most-suppressive single modifier; feature_027 is the
rarest (3.1%) and least-individually-impactful. But all four are necessary for the maximal effect.

Predictive vs prognostic
------------------------
The four modifiers are PREDICTIVE biomarkers (treatment-effect modifiers), not PROGNOSTIC markers.

Within untreated patients (T = 0): the modifiers have small or null effects on ORR
(feature_013 RD = -1.9 pp, feature_015 RD = -0.3 pp, feature_021 RD = -1.3 pp,
feature_027 RD = +2.6 pp). Adjusted ORs in a full prognostic model (T=0 only) are all in
[0.91, 1.21] and only feature_013 is even modestly significant (p = 1e-5).

Within treated patients (T = 1): the modifiers are devastating predictors of failure
(feature_013 RD = -45.2 pp, feature_015 RD = -24.6 pp, feature_021 RD = -23.1 pp,
feature_027 RD = -20.5 pp; all p < 1e-20).

The pairwise interactions among the four modifiers are null in untreated patients (5 of 6 with
p > 0.08) but strongly positive on the log-OR scale in treated patients (interaction ORs 2.6 to
11.5, p down to 1e-141), again confirming the saturation pattern: once one resistance marker is
present, additional markers do not further suppress an already-suppressed treatment effect.

Other clinically meaningful patterns (not treatment-effect modifiers)
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Strong PROGNOSTIC predictors of objective_response (apparent in both arms; not modifying T effect):
  - feature_001 (3-level ordinal 0/1/2): per-level OR 0.69, ORR 28.2%/22.8%/18.2% across levels.
  - log(feature_022): adjusted OR 0.92 per log unit (right-skewed continuous, max 3622).
  - log(feature_020): adjusted OR 0.86 per log unit.
  - log(feature_024): adjusted OR 0.91 per log unit.
  - feature_002: adjusted OR 1.16 per unit (positive direction).

These prognostic factors have similar effect sizes within T=0 and T=1, consistent with prognostic
(baseline-risk) rather than predictive (treatment-effect-modifying) action. Quadratic terms add
nothing (all x^2 p > 0.15), so linear log-odds is sufficient.

Within the responsive subgroup, feature_001 weakly attenuates the absolute risk difference
(RD = +64.6 / +62.2 / +59.0 percentage points across feature_001 levels 0 / 1 / 2), but treatment
remains highly beneficial at every level. The interaction p with treatment within the favorable
subgroup is 0.07 (LRT) and the OR-scale interaction is null (p = 0.21 in the joint model that
includes the four binary modifiers).

Features without ORR association (refuted hypotheses)
-----------------------------------------------------
Univariate ORR analyses showed essentially no signal for:
  - feature_006 (binary, 20% prevalent, OR 1.00, p = 0.87)
  - feature_005 (binary, 60% prevalent, OR 0.98, p = 0.31)
  - feature_023 (binary, 30% prevalent, OR 0.98, p = 0.47)
  - feature_011 (binary, 30% prevalent, OR 1.00, p = 0.91)
  - feature_017 (binary, 10% prevalent, OR 0.99, p = 0.89)
  - feature_019 (binary, 15% prevalent, OR 1.02, p = 0.60)
  - feature_004 (binary, 5% prevalent, OR 0.99, p = 0.88)
  - feature_010 (5-level ordinal 6-10): chi-square p = 0.77 (essentially flat; 24% ORR at every level)
  - feature_016 (continuous, ~age): t-test p = 0.82
  - feature_018, feature_026, feature_009, feature_029, feature_003, feature_012, feature_025,
    feature_028, feature_007, feature_014, feature_032, feature_031: all univariate p > 0.05.
  - feature_030: zero variance (single value, all 0); uninformative.

These features also do NOT modify the treatment effect within the responsive subgroup (largest
median-split RD difference 0.022 absolute; smallest interaction p 0.004, not surviving Bonferroni
across 26 tests at alpha 0.0019).

Independence of features
------------------------
The five strongest binary predictors (feature_008, feature_013, feature_015, feature_021,
feature_027) are pairwise uncorrelated (|r| <= 0.008), consistent with an orthogonal design or
independent biomarker assays. The single notable pairwise correlation in the cohort is
r = 0.299 between feature_022 (continuous, right-skewed, looks PSA-like) and feature_013
(binary). This explains the apparent T x feature_022 interaction observed in the full cohort
(p = 1e-77): once feature_013 is conditioned on, feature_022 ceases to modify the T effect
(within-favorable interaction p = 0.34).

Sensitivity / robustness
------------------------
1. Bootstrap (B = 2000) of the responsive subgroup: RD = 0.6257 [0.6136, 0.6384],
   OR = 19.01 [17.57, 20.61].
2. Random 50/50 half-sample validation: half A RD = +0.624 (responsive), +0.013 (non-responsive);
   half B RD = +0.628 (responsive), +0.003 (non-responsive). Both halves replicate cleanly.
3. Exhaustive 1- and 2-feature sub-subgroup search within the unfavorable patients: NO subgroup
   of size >= 200 has a treatment risk difference > +0.10. Maximum found = +0.090
   (feature_006 = 1 & feature_004 = 1, n = 357, p = 0.017 — does not survive multiple-comparisons
   correction). There is no "rescue" subgroup hidden within the unfavorable patients.
4. Continuous quadratic effects in untreated patients: all p > 0.15 — log-linear sufficient.

Residual unexplained variation
------------------------------
Even within the responsive subgroup, treated-arm ORR plateaus at ~80%, not 100%. The 20% of
treated favorable patients who do not respond cannot be further partitioned with any feature in
the dataset (no remaining feature shows a within-subgroup interaction with treatment after
multiple-comparisons correction). This residual non-response is consistent with biological
heterogeneity not captured by the measured biomarkers.

Synthesis
---------
The cohort has the structure of a clean predictive-biomarker discovery dataset:
  - One randomized treatment indicator (feature_008).
  - One outcome (objective_response, 24% baseline rate).
  - Four binary predictive biomarkers (feature_013, 015, 021, 027) whose joint absence selects
    a clinically dramatic responsive subgroup (~31% prevalence, OR ~19, RD +62.6 pp, NNT 1.6).
  - Saturation-style interaction: any single biomarker positivity abolishes treatment efficacy.
  - Several independent prognostic features (feature_001 ordinal, log(feature_022),
    log(feature_020), log(feature_024), feature_002) that influence baseline ORR similarly in
    treated and untreated patients but do not modify the treatment effect.
  - The remaining ~15 features carry no measurable predictive or prognostic signal for ORR.

Best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis (final, complete)
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment: feature_008
Outcome:   objective_response (binary)
Direction: positive (treatment increases ORR)
Subgroup predicates (ALL must hold for the treatment effect to manifest):
   feature_013 = 0  AND
   feature_015 = 0  AND
   feature_021 = 0  AND
   feature_027 = 0
Effect within subgroup: ORR rises from ~17% to ~80% (RD +62.6 pp; OR 19.0).
Effect outside subgroup: null (RD < +0.01; OR ~1.06).
