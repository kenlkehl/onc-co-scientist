Dataset: ds001_prostate
Patients: 50,000
Outcome: objective_response (binary; 24.0% positive overall)
Iterations performed: 25 (cap)

==============================================================================
1. SETUP AND BASIC STRUCTURE
==============================================================================
The dataset has 32 anonymized patient features plus a binary outcome
objective_response. One feature (feature_030) is constant (0 for every patient)
and was discarded. There are no missing values. The cohort is imbalanced:
12,011 of 50,000 patients (24.0%) achieve an objective response.

The feature column types span binary indicators (0/1), an ordinal feature
(feature_001, levels 0/1/2), and continuous biomarkers / lab-like values
(feature_002, _016, _018, _022, _024, _029, _003, _032, _007, _014, _026, etc.).

==============================================================================
2. UNIVARIATE / MAIN-EFFECT FINDINGS
==============================================================================
Univariate associations with objective_response (chi-square for binary,
Spearman for ordinal/continuous) ranked by p-value:

  feature_013 (binary, prevalence 0.55)   r = -0.193  p ~ 0     **strong**
  feature_008 (binary, prevalence 0.40)   r = +0.202  p ~ 0     **strong**
  feature_022 (continuous)                rho = -0.152 p ~ 3e-255 **strong**
  feature_015 (binary, prevalence 0.20)   r = -0.101  p ~ 2e-98  **strong**
  feature_001 (3-level ordinal)           rho = -0.081 p ~ 2e-73 **moderate**
  feature_021 (binary, prevalence 0.10)   r = -0.101  p ~ 5e-56  **moderate**
  feature_020 (continuous)                rho = -0.049 p ~ 1e-27 small
  feature_027 (binary, prevalence 0.03)   r = -0.066  p ~ 3e-9   small
  feature_024 (continuous)                rho = -0.025 p ~ 3e-8  small
  feature_002 (continuous)                rho = +0.020 p ~ 1e-5  small
  All other features: p > 0.10 (no marginal association).

feature_008 is the only binary feature with a large *positive* main effect
(+20 percentage points), consistent with an active treatment indicator. All
other binary features with non-trivial associations are *negative*, consistent
with prognostic biomarkers / comorbidities. Subsequent treatment-effect
heterogeneity analyses therefore use feature_008 as the treatment.

Treatment allocation (feature_008) is approximately balanced with respect to
each of the candidate modifiers feature_013, feature_015, feature_021,
feature_027 (P(modifier | feature_008=1) - P(modifier | feature_008=0) is
within +/-0.003 for every modifier), so the heterogeneity findings below are
not artifacts of confounded allocation.

==============================================================================
3. SYSTEMATIC TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY SEARCH
==============================================================================
For each non-constant feature f, a logit interaction model
    objective_response ~ feature_008 + f + feature_008:f
was compared by likelihood ratio to its no-interaction counterpart. The
treatment-effect modifiers, ranked by LR statistic:

  feature_013 LR = 1885.4   df=1   p ~ 0
  feature_015 LR =  426.8   df=1   p ~ 0
  feature_022 LR =  348.1   df=1   p ~ 0
  feature_021 LR =  187.5   df=1   p ~ 0
  feature_027 LR =   85.4   df=1   p ~ 0
  feature_001 LR =   19.3   df=2   p = 6e-5
  All other features: LR < 6 (p > 0.02; none survive Bonferroni for ~30 tests).

All five top modifiers point in the same direction: each elevated value
attenuates the response benefit of feature_008. A separate decision tree
fit to the feature_008=1 arm (predicting objective_response) splits first on
feature_013, then on feature_015 and feature_021, independently corroborating
the resistance-marker structure.

Treatment effect of feature_008 stratified by each modifier (absolute risk
difference, response rate at feature_008=1 minus at feature_008=0):

  feature_013 = 0:  +0.440   (n=22,519)   feature_013 = 1:  +0.008   (n=27,481)
  feature_015 = 0:  +0.251   (n=39,962)   feature_015 = 1:  +0.008   (n=10,038)
  feature_021 = 0:  +0.225   (n=45,004)   feature_021 = 1:  +0.006   (n= 4,996)
  feature_027 = 0:  +0.209   (n=48,472)   feature_027 = 1:  -0.022   (n= 1,528)

Each marker, when set to 1, abolishes the response benefit of feature_008
to within +/-0.02 absolute. The four binary modifiers are mutually
near-independent (all pairwise correlations |r| < 0.005), so they act as
distinct, non-overlapping resistance markers.

==============================================================================
4. JOINT INTERACTION MODEL AND COMPOSITE SUBGROUP
==============================================================================
A joint logit model with all four interactions entered simultaneously
    y ~ f008 + f013 + f015 + f021 + f027
        + f008:f013 + f008:f015 + f008:f021 + f008:f027
shows that each interaction remains highly significant in the joint fit:

  feature_008                = +2.580    (p ~ 0)
  feature_008 : feature_013  = -2.199    (p ~ 0)
  feature_008 : feature_015  = -1.611    (p ~ 0)
  feature_008 : feature_021  = -1.521    (p ~ 0)
  feature_008 : feature_027  = -1.653    (p ~ 0)

i.e., presence of any one resistance marker reduces the log-odds treatment
effect by ~1.5 to 2.2 — large enough to drive the absolute treatment effect
to zero on its own.

Defining a composite "clean" indicator
    clean_no_resist = (feature_013==0) AND (feature_015==0)
                      AND (feature_021==0) AND (feature_027==0)
yields the simplest and most parsimonious description of the treatment-effect
heterogeneity:

                                feature_008=1   feature_008=0   delta (abs)
  clean_no_resist = 1 (n=15,681):  0.798           0.172          +0.626
  clean_no_resist = 0 (n=34,319):  0.161           0.153          +0.008

  Logit interaction f008:clean_no_resist = +2.885 (z=56.1, p ~ 0)
  OR for response with feature_008=1 vs 0
       in clean_no_resist = 1:  18.97  (95% CI ~17.4-20.7, p ~ 0)
       in clean_no_resist = 0:   1.06  (chi-square p = 0.056)

Treatment is therefore highly effective in the clean subgroup only, and is
essentially inert in the resistance-marker-positive subgroup.

==============================================================================
5. DOSE-RESPONSE IN MARKER COUNT
==============================================================================
Defining resist_count = sum of the four resistance markers in {0,1,2,3,4}:

  count = 0  (n=15,681):  treatment effect = +0.626  (clean subgroup)
  count = 1  (n=25,372):  treatment effect = +0.009
  count = 2  (n= 8,186):  treatment effect = +0.008
  count = 3  (n=   745):  treatment effect = -0.022
  count = 4  (n=    16):  treatment effect = -0.091

The treatment effect collapses entirely between count=0 and count=1; further
markers add no additional attenuation. The effect modification is essentially
a step function at "any marker = 1".

==============================================================================
6. RESIDUAL HETEROGENEITY AND PROGNOSTIC FEATURES
==============================================================================
After conditioning on the four resistance markers in a joint model, the
previously-significant feature_022 and feature_001 interactions with feature_008
both lose significance:

  feature_008 : log(feature_022)        = -0.007  (p = 0.74)
  feature_008 : feature_001 (level 1)   = +0.019  (p = 0.72)
  feature_008 : feature_001 (level 2)   = +0.118  (p = 0.13)

i.e., the marginal feature_022 / feature_001 modifier effects were largely an
artifact of the four binary modifiers. Within the clean_no_resist=1 subgroup
the treatment effect is essentially flat (~+0.62 to +0.65) across feature_022
quartiles and across feature_001 levels.

However, feature_022 and feature_001 remain *prognostic for objective_response
in the absence of treatment* (control-arm logit coefficients: log(feature_022)
= -0.072, p ~ 3e-7; feature_001 level 1 vs 0 = -0.36, level 2 vs 0 = -0.77,
both p ~ 0). So they predict who responds without therapy but do not by
themselves identify who benefits from feature_008.

A search within the clean subgroup for any feature whose stratification
shifts the +0.626 treatment effect by more than ~2 percentage points found
none. There is no evidence of a "super-responder" subgroup beyond
clean_no_resist=1 itself.

==============================================================================
7. SUMMARY OF HYPOTHESES (SUPPORTED vs. REFUTED)
==============================================================================
SUPPORTED:
- feature_008 has a large positive marginal effect on objective_response
  (treatment-like role; +0.20 absolute risk difference, OR ~3.0).
- feature_013, feature_015, feature_021, feature_027 are each negative
  prognostic indicators on their own.
- feature_022 (continuous) is a strong negative prognostic factor.
- feature_001 is negatively prognostic.
- The treatment effect of feature_008 is profoundly modified by each of
  feature_013, feature_015, feature_021, and feature_027 (each, when set
  to 1, reduces the absolute treatment effect to ~0).
- The composite subgroup feature_013=0 AND feature_015=0 AND feature_021=0
  AND feature_027=0 (clean_no_resist=1) captures the full treatment effect.
- feature_022 marginally appears to modify treatment, but this is fully
  explained by correlation with the four binary markers.

REFUTED OR NULL:
- feature_005, feature_006, feature_011, feature_017, feature_019, feature_023,
  feature_004, feature_010, feature_026, feature_032, feature_028, feature_007,
  feature_025, feature_018, feature_003, feature_031, feature_016, feature_009,
  feature_029, feature_012, feature_014 have no marginal main effect on
  objective_response.
- None of the continuous biomarkers (after Bonferroni correction) modifies
  the treatment effect of feature_008 within the clean subgroup.
- feature_001 and log(feature_022) lose their treatment-modifier status once
  the four resistance markers are conditioned on.
- No "super-responder" subgroup beyond clean_no_resist=1 exists.

==============================================================================
8. FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS
==============================================================================
The treatment feature_008 increases objective_response only in patients with
    feature_013 = 0 AND feature_015 = 0 AND feature_021 = 0 AND feature_027 = 0.

In this subgroup (n=15,681; ~31% of the cohort) feature_008=1 raises response
from 17.2% to 79.8% (absolute risk difference +0.626; OR 18.97; p ~ 0).
Outside this subgroup the treatment effect is approximately zero (absolute
risk difference +0.008; OR 1.06; chi-square p = 0.056). The four resistance
markers are independent of one another, each contributing additively on the
logit scale to suppress the treatment effect; presence of any one is
essentially sufficient to zero out the benefit. feature_022 and feature_001
are prognostic in the absence of treatment but do not independently modify
the treatment effect after conditioning on the four resistance markers.
