Analysis Summary: ds001_prostate (n=50,000; objective_response rate 24.0%)

OVERVIEW

The dataset contains 32 de-identified features (feature_001-feature_032; feature_030 is constant at 0
and provides no information) and one binary outcome (objective_response). Across 14 iterations of
hypothesize-test-refine, the analysis went from univariate screening, to multivariable adjustment,
to systematic treatment-by-feature interaction screening, to a complete multi-predicate subgroup
definition for treatment-effect heterogeneity. The headline finding is a clean four-modifier subgroup
that fully explains the heterogeneity of the candidate treatment.

WHICH FEATURE IS THE TREATMENT?

Iterations 1-4 established that:
- feature_008 (binary, prevalence 40.2%) carries the only strong, positive, robust adjusted effect
  on objective_response. In multivariable logistic regression of the full feature set, its
  standardized coefficient is +1.20 (z=52.6, p~0). Its raw response rates are 16% if feature_008=0
  vs 36% if feature_008=1.
- feature_013, feature_015, feature_021, feature_027, and feature_001 (3-level) all carry strong
  negative adjusted effects: feature_013 beta=-1.13, feature_021 beta=-0.74, feature_015 beta=-0.69,
  feature_027 beta=-0.43, feature_001 beta=-0.33 per level. These have skewed prevalences (10-55%)
  and behave like baseline patient characteristics (e.g., disease-severity / contraindication-type
  markers).
- The remaining binaries (feature_005, feature_006, feature_011, feature_017, feature_019,
  feature_023, feature_004) and most continuous features (feature_003, feature_007, feature_009,
  feature_012, feature_014, feature_016, feature_018, feature_025, feature_026, feature_028,
  feature_029, feature_031, feature_032) are noise covariates with |z|<2 in adjusted models and
  univariate p-values mostly >0.3.
- Two continuous features carry modest independent prognostic signal: feature_022 (highly skewed,
  range 0.1-3622, monotone negative), feature_020 (range 0-24.6, negative), feature_024 (range
  0.05-300, negative), and feature_002 (range 1.5-5.5, weakly positive).

Conclusion: feature_008 is treated as the candidate treatment; the others are baseline covariates.

MAIN-EFFECT FINDINGS

In the full cohort, feature_008 increases the objective response rate from 15.9% (n=29,924) to
36.1% (n=20,076), an absolute risk difference of +20.2 percentage points (chi-square p~0; adjusted
log-odds +1.20).

PROGNOSTIC FINDINGS (ORDERED BY STRENGTH)

- feature_013=1 (55% of cohort): response 0.154 vs 0.346 if =0; the strongest negative prognostic
  marker.
- feature_001 (3 ordinal levels): response 0.282 / 0.228 / 0.182 across levels 0/1/2.
- feature_022 quintiles in full cohort: 0.323 / 0.293 / 0.244 / 0.189 / 0.151.
- feature_021=1 (10%): response 0.150 vs 0.250.
- feature_015=1 (20%): response 0.160 vs 0.260.
- feature_027=1 (3%): response 0.176 vs 0.242.
- feature_020 (continuous): mean 3.95 in non-responders vs 3.50 in responders.
- feature_024 (continuous): mean 6.23 vs 5.69.

TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY (THE KEY FINDING)

A systematic interaction screen of feature_008 against every other feature surfaced four binary
features whose interaction with feature_008 was extreme:
- feature_008 x feature_013: beta=-2.05 (p<1e-300)
- feature_008 x feature_015: beta=-1.35 (p~9e-104)
- feature_008 x feature_021: beta=-1.23 (p~4e-46)
- feature_008 x feature_027: beta=-1.40 (p~2e-21)
All other interaction p-values are >=2e-5 (feature_001 with a small +0.14 modifier coefficient that
has no clinical relevance) or non-significant.

Stratified analysis revealed an on/off pattern: in patients with the modifier=0, the treatment
produced a large absolute response benefit (+44 pp for feature_013, +25 pp for feature_015, +22 pp
for feature_021, +21 pp for feature_027). In patients with the modifier=1, the absolute treatment
effect collapses to within ±2 pp of zero.

Crucially, the four modifiers are nearly independent of each other (pairwise phi <=0.004, all
chi-square p>0.37). They represent four distinct patient features, not a single underlying
construct.

When stratification is nested (e.g., within feature_013=0, look at the effect of feature_015):
- feature_013=0, feature_015=0: treatment effect +0.546
- feature_013=0, feature_015=1: treatment effect +0.014
- feature_013=0, feature_021=0: +0.491; feature_021=1: -0.012
- feature_013=0, feature_027=0: +0.454; feature_027=1: -0.012
- Within feature_013=1, every secondary modifier shows treatment effect within ±0.03 of zero.

Each modifier independently abolishes the treatment effect. The effects are layered: any one of the
four being =1 is sufficient to neutralize the treatment.

THE COMPLETE SUBGROUP DEFINITION

The full subgroup where feature_008 produces a treatment benefit is the conjunction
    feature_013 = 0 AND feature_015 = 0 AND feature_021 = 0 AND feature_027 = 0.
- N = 15,681 (31.4% of the cohort).
- Treatment prevalence in subgroup: 40.3% (vs 40.1% in complement; treatment is balanced -
  this is not confounding by indication).
- Inside the subgroup: feature_008=1 response rate 79.8% (5046/6325); feature_008=0 response rate
  17.2% (1610/9356); absolute risk difference +62.6 percentage points; crude odds ratio 18.98;
  chi-square p<1e-300.
- Outside the subgroup: feature_008=1 response rate 16.1%; feature_008=0 response rate 15.3%;
  absolute risk difference +0.8 pp (non-significant).

A within-subgroup interaction screen against the remaining 26 features (continuous and binary)
produced no robust further modifier: the smallest two interaction p-values were feature_005
(p=0.004) and feature_017 (p=0.033), both well above the Bonferroni threshold of 0.05/26 ~= 0.002.
The subgroup definition is effectively complete.

A confirmatory joint logistic regression with all four interactions and prognostic covariates yields
treatment main beta=+2.62 (z=70.3) and interaction betas -2.23 (feature_013), -1.64 (feature_015),
-1.56 (feature_021), -1.66 (feature_027). The pseudo R^2 rises from 0.124 (main effects only) to
0.178 once these four interactions are added.

A check on continuous prognostic markers (feature_022) confirmed that the apparent attenuation of
treatment effect across feature_022 quintiles in the full cohort (treatment effect drops from
+0.378 in Q1 to +0.025 in Q5) is fully explained by joint distribution with the four binary
modifiers: within the all-modifiers-zero subgroup, the treatment effect is uniform across feature_022
quintiles (+0.640, +0.630, +0.598, +0.647, +0.610). feature_022 is a strong prognostic marker but
not a treatment-effect modifier once the four binary modifiers are accounted for.

HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED VS REFUTED

Supported:
- feature_008 is a treatment with strong positive effect on objective_response (h7).
- feature_013, feature_015, feature_021, feature_027 each independently negatively interact with
  feature_008 (h9, h10).
- The treatment effect is essentially restricted to the all-modifiers-zero subgroup (h11, h12, h16,
  h22).
- Treatment is balanced across the subgroup, ruling out simple confounding (h21).
- feature_001 is a stage-like ordinal prognostic feature (h4).
- feature_022 is a strong continuous prognostic marker (h18).
- Within the all-modifiers-zero subgroup, no continuous feature provides further treatment-effect
  modification (h17).

Refuted / null:
- feature_030 carries no information (h2 - constant at 0).
- The four modifiers are not correlated with each other (h13).
- feature_022 is not an independent treatment-effect modifier once the four binaries are accounted
  for (h19).
- Within feature_013=1, no further patient feature predicts a usable treatment effect (h15).

OVERALL CONCLUSION

The dataset shows a textbook example of subgroup-specific treatment efficacy. A single binary
candidate treatment (feature_008) produces a very large response benefit (~63 percentage point
absolute increase, OR ~19) in the ~31% of patients who simultaneously have feature_013=0,
feature_015=0, feature_021=0, and feature_027=0; in the remaining ~69% of patients (with any one
or more of those modifiers =1), the treatment is uninformative. The four modifiers act as
independent on/off switches: any one of them being =1 is sufficient to neutralize the treatment.
This is the cleanest possible heterogeneity pattern - a compound predictive biomarker requiring all
four predicates to be satisfied.

The final treatment-effect heterogeneity hypothesis for the objective_response outcome is
therefore:

    feature_008 -> objective_response, signed effect strongly positive,
    restricted to the subgroup defined by
        feature_013 = 0 AND feature_015 = 0 AND feature_021 = 0 AND feature_027 = 0.
