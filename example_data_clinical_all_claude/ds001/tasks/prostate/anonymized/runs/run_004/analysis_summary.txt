Analysis summary — ds001_prostate (n = 50,000; outcome: objective_response, baseline 24.0%)
===========================================================================================

Overall approach
----------------
I approached the de-identified prostate-cancer cohort with three rounds of analysis: (1) feature
profiling and univariate screening, (2) a full multivariable logistic regression to identify
candidate independent main effects (and to surface a candidate "treatment" indicator), and (3) a
systematic treatment-effect heterogeneity search — first one-modifier-at-a-time stratification,
then joint subgroup analysis, then sensitivity checks dropping one modifier at a time and a
"bad-modifier count" dose-response check. Throughout, the only outcome with usable variation is
the binary objective_response.

Iteration 1 — univariate screen
-------------------------------
Hypothesis h1: several features are associated with objective_response.
Result: 10 features were strongly associated (p < 0.001). Direction & magnitude:
- feature_013 = 1 → ORR 15.4% vs 34.6% off (NEGATIVE).
- feature_008 = 1 → ORR 36.1% vs 15.9% off (POSITIVE; only large positive marker).
- feature_022 (continuous, very right-skewed, range 0.12–3622, median 15.9 — likely a tumor-
  burden / PSA-like marker): responders mean 28.2 vs non-responders 47.2 (NEGATIVE).
- feature_015 = 1 → 16.0% vs 26.0%; feature_021 = 1 → 15.0% vs 25.0%; feature_027 = 1 → 17.6% vs
  24.2% (all NEGATIVE).
- feature_001 (ordinal 0/1/2): ORR 28.2% / 22.8% / 18.2% (monotonically NEGATIVE).
- feature_020, feature_024, feature_002 also significant but small.
Many other binary features (feature_005, 011, 023, 006, 004, 017, 019), age-like feature_016,
Gleason-like feature_010, and most labs (feature_007/014/032/026/etc.) had no main effect
(p > 0.1). Hypothesis SUPPORTED for the listed features; refuted for the rest.

Iteration 2 — multivariable logistic regression
-----------------------------------------------
Hypothesis h2: in a joint model, feature_008 is positively associated, and feature_013/015/021/
027/022/020/024/001 are negatively associated with response.
Result (n = 50,000, pseudo-R² = 0.124): all listed coefficients retained their sign and
significance (z = +52.6 for feature_008; z = -45.4 for feature_013; |z| > 5 for feature_015,
021, 027, 022, 020, 024, 001, 002). All non-significant univariate features remained non-
significant in the joint model. SUPPORTED. The pattern strongly suggests feature_008 is the
"treatment" exposure and the others are prognostic factors.

Iteration 3 — treatment-by-feature interaction screen
-----------------------------------------------------
Hypothesis h3: feature_008's effect is heterogeneous, not uniform.
Result: Pairwise tx*modifier interaction tests with feature_008 as putative tx:
- feature_013: interaction beta = -1.978 (p essentially 0).
- feature_015: -1.237 (p = 1.4 × 10⁻⁹³).
- feature_021: -1.138 (p = 3.3 × 10⁻⁴²).
- feature_022 (log, standardized): -0.805 (p = 1.2 × 10⁻⁷²).
- feature_027: -1.283 (p = 1.8 × 10⁻¹⁹).
- feature_001 (ordinal): +0.141 (p = 1.6 × 10⁻⁵), small.
Each binary modifier's interaction beta is roughly equal in magnitude to the entire unmodified
tx effect — i.e., turning that modifier on essentially abolishes the treatment benefit. STRONGLY
SUPPORTED.

Iterations 4–7 — single-modifier stratified analyses
----------------------------------------------------
Hypotheses h4–h7: each of feature_013/015/021/027 = 1 abolishes the feature_008 effect.
Result (absolute ORR difference, on - off):
- feature_013 = 0: +0.440 (61.0% vs 16.9%, p < 10⁻³⁰⁰); feature_013 = 1: +0.008 (NS).
- feature_015 = 0: +0.251; feature_015 = 1: +0.008 (NS).
- feature_021 = 0: +0.225; feature_021 = 1: +0.006 (NS).
- feature_027 = 0: +0.209; feature_027 = 1: -0.022 (NS).
All four hypotheses SUPPORTED — every modifier individually switches off the treatment effect.

Iteration 8 — feature_022 (continuous) as a modifier
----------------------------------------------------
Hypotheses h8/h9: feature_022 modifies the effect on the logit scale, but NOT independently on
the absolute scale once the four binary modifiers are fixed at favorable values.
Result: Logit-scale tx*log(feature_022) interaction = -0.805 (p = 1.2 × 10⁻⁷²). SUPPORTED.
However, within the favorable subgroup (n = 15,681), absolute ORR differences across feature_022
quartiles were +0.633, +0.628, +0.624, +0.618 — essentially flat. Confirmatory regression in
that subgroup gave tx * log(feature_022) = -0.041 (p = 0.32, NS). Hypothesis h9 SUPPORTED:
feature_022 looks like a logit-scale modifier mainly because higher-feature_022 patients have
more bad modifiers; once the bad modifiers are conditioned on, feature_022 is no longer an
effect modifier.

Iteration 9 — feature_001 ordinal modifier
------------------------------------------
Hypothesis h10: feature_001 is prognostic but only mildly modifies the absolute treatment effect.
Result: Within favorable subgroup, ORR_on - ORR_off was +0.646 (level 0), +0.622 (level 1),
+0.590 (level 2). Modest attenuation (~3 pp per level) but the treatment effect remains huge at
every level. Adjusted main-effect coef -0.328 per level (very strong prognostic). SUPPORTED.

Iteration 10 — checking for hidden treatments
---------------------------------------------
Hypothesis h11: no other binary feature is a hidden treatment with strong heterogeneity.
Result: A comprehensive pairwise binary-feature interaction scan found that every interaction
passing |beta| > 0.3 and p < 10⁻⁶ involved only the set {feature_008, 013, 015, 021, 027}. None
of feature_005, 011, 023, 006, 004, 017, 019 is a hidden modifiable-treatment. SUPPORTED;
feature_008 is the unique strong treatment-like exposure in this dataset.

Iteration 11 — bad-modifier dose-response
-----------------------------------------
Hypothesis h12: the relationship is closer to step-function than dose-response.
Result: Counts of bad modifiers (feature_013 + 015 + 021 + 027):
- 0 bad (n = 15,681): ORR_on = 79.8%, ORR_off = 17.2%, diff = +62.6 pp (p < 10⁻³⁰⁰).
- 1 bad (n = 25,372): +0.9 pp (NS).
- 2 bad (n = 8,186): +0.8 pp (NS).
- 3 bad (n = 745): -2.2 pp (NS).
- 4 bad (n = 16): zero cell.
Step-function: virtually all benefit lives at bad_count = 0. SUPPORTED.

Iteration 12 — non-redundancy of the four modifiers
---------------------------------------------------
Hypothesis h13: each of the four modifiers contributes non-redundantly to the subgroup
definition.
Result: Drop-one sensitivity (the remaining three modifiers all = 0):
- Drop feature_013: within-subgroup ORR diff falls from +0.626 to +0.287 (largest drop).
- Drop feature_015: falls to +0.505.
- Drop feature_021: falls to +0.563.
- Drop feature_027: falls to +0.607.
feature_013 is the strongest single switch; feature_027 contributes the least but still
sharpens the subgroup. SUPPORTED.

Iteration 13 — final joint subgroup model
-----------------------------------------
Hypothesis h14 (FINAL): feature_008 = 1 increases objective_response, with the effect
concentrated in patients with feature_013 = 0 AND feature_015 = 0 AND feature_021 = 0 AND
feature_027 = 0. Within that subgroup the absolute response rate rises from ~17% (off) to ~80%
(on), Δ ≈ +63 pp; outside the subgroup the effect is ~0.
Result: Confirmatory adjusted logistic (with feature_001, log(feature_022), feature_020 as
covariates): tx coefficient outside favorable subgroup = +0.061 (OR ≈ 1.06; p = 0.045);
tx + tx_fav inside favorable subgroup = +3.00 (OR ≈ 20.1); interaction tx_fav z = +56.5
(p essentially 0). Pseudo-R² jumps from 0.124 (main-effects only) to 0.202 (adding the joint
interaction). STRONGLY SUPPORTED.

Iteration 14 — residual heterogeneity inside the favorable subgroup
-------------------------------------------------------------------
Hypothesis h15: continuous prognostic features (feature_020, 024, 002, 022) are NOT independent
treatment-effect modifiers within the favorable subgroup.
Result: Within favorable, all continuous-feature × tx interactions were non-significant
(feature_022 log p = 0.32; feature_020/024/002 p > 0.05). The four-binary subgroup definition
captures essentially all the treatment-effect heterogeneity. SUPPORTED.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. Among 32 features, feature_008 is the unique strong "treatment-like" exposure: it has a
   large positive adjusted association with objective_response and shows massive heterogeneity.
2. The treatment-effect heterogeneity is captured almost entirely by four binary modifiers —
   feature_013, feature_015, feature_021, and feature_027 — each acting as an off-switch.
3. Patients in the joint favorable subgroup (all four = 0; ~31% of the cohort, n = 15,681) have
   an enormous benefit from feature_008 (ORR 79.8% vs 17.2%, +62.6 pp absolute, OR ≈ 20). All
   other patients (~69%, n = 34,319) derive essentially no benefit from feature_008
   (Δ ≈ +0.8 pp, NS).
4. Many other features (feature_022, feature_020, feature_024, feature_001, feature_002) are
   prognostic main-effect predictors but do not independently modify the feature_008 effect on
   the absolute scale once the four-binary subgroup is conditioned on.
5. Several binary features (feature_005, 011, 023, 006, 004, 017, 019) and several labs
   (feature_007/014/032/026/etc.) are uninformative — neither prognostic nor treatment-effect-
   modifying.

Final treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis
------------------------------------------
For the outcome objective_response: feature_008 = 1 (vs 0) increases response, with the effect
concentrated in patients satisfying ALL of:
    feature_013 = 0 AND feature_015 = 0 AND feature_021 = 0 AND feature_027 = 0.
Direction: positive (treatment increases response). Within-subgroup magnitude: +63 pp absolute
or OR ≈ 20. Outside-subgroup magnitude: ≈ 0.
