ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (n = 50,000)
============================================

The dataset comprises 50,000 anonymized NSCLC-cohort records with 33 de-identified
features (mix of binary, ordinal, and continuous) and a single continuous outcome,
pfs_months (median 3.32, mean 3.43, range 0–15.3 months). I ran 25 iterations of
hypothesis generation and statistical testing, progressing from univariate prognostic
screens to a systematic treatment-effect heterogeneity search.


1. UNIVARIATE PROGNOSTIC LANDSCAPE  (Iterations 1–5)
----------------------------------------------------

The features with strong univariate association with pfs_months were:

   Worse PFS                                        Better PFS
   ---------------------------------                ---------------------------
   feature_023 = 1   (-1.44 months)                 feature_016 = 1   (+0.78)
   feature_014 (ordinal 0/1/2, -1.10 / unit)        feature_018 = 1   (+0.32)
   feature_011 = 1   (-0.92)                        feature_022 = 1   (+0.19)
   squamous histology vs adeno   (-0.86)            feature_015      (rho +0.77)
   feature_028 = 1   (-0.81)                        feature_025      (rho +0.10)
   current vs former/never smoker (-0.62)
   feature_031 = 1   (-0.20)
   feature_017 (rho -0.13)
   feature_005 = 1   (-0.12)

Hypotheses h1–h14 were each tested directly and supported. The thirteen lab-style
continuous features (feature_032, _024, _010, _003, _030, _008, _004, _013, _026,
_029, _007, _033, _009) all had |Spearman rho| < 0.015 with pfs_months and were
effectively noise predictors — h14 supported.

The strongest single predictor was the continuous feature_015 (range 30–90, median
65), whose univariate Spearman rho with pfs_months was +0.77 (h10). It dominates
all multivariable models as an additive prognostic factor.


2. MULTIVARIABLE STRUCTURE  (Iteration 6)
-----------------------------------------

A 33-feature OLS regression on pfs_months yielded R² = 0.894. The dominant
adjusted coefficients (months) were feature_015 +0.168/unit, feature_014 −1.10/level,
feature_023 −1.42, feature_011 −0.91, squamous −0.72, current smoker −0.55,
feature_016 +0.89, feature_018 +0.32, feature_017 −0.073/unit, feature_025 +0.46/unit.
Most lab-style features were non-significant. Hypothesis h15 supported.


3. IDENTIFYING THE ACTIVE TREATMENT  (Iteration 7)
--------------------------------------------------

Four binary features had high prevalence consistent with a treatment indicator
(feature_012 ~50 %, feature_018 ~35 %, feature_020 ~30 %, feature_027 ~30 %). Of
these, only feature_018 had a non-null marginal effect on pfs_months (+0.32, p ~ 0).
feature_012, feature_020, feature_027 were marginally null. Hypothesis h16 supported.


4. TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY OF feature_018  (Iterations 8–14)
-------------------------------------------------------------------

Step 1 — Treatment-by-feature interaction screen (h17). The largest interaction by
several orders of magnitude was feature_018 × feature_016 (coef +2.53, p ~ 0). All
other interactions were small (|coef| ≤ 0.6) or driven by sample-size artifacts.

Step 2 — Stratified verification (h18). Within feature_016 = 0 the treatment effect
was 0 (3.334 vs 3.329 months). Within feature_016 = 1 the treatment effect was
+2.55 months (3.213 vs 5.766). Therefore the +0.32-month marginal effect of
feature_018 was entirely concentrated in the ~13 % of patients with feature_016 = 1.

Step 3 — Test alternative biomarkers (h19). Continuous feature_019 (resembling
PD-L1 expression, range 0–0.8) did not modify the feature_018 effect (interaction
coef +0.015, p = 0.64; flat by quartile). This argues feature_018 is NOT an
immunotherapy.

Step 4 — Within feature_016 = 1, second-tier subgroup search (h20–h21):
   · feature_028 = 1 (n = 259) abolished the benefit:
        treated vs untreated PFS = 2.08 vs 2.43 months (-0.35).
   · feature_031 = 1 (n = 2860) also abolished the benefit:
        treated vs untreated = 3.18 vs 3.19 months (≈ 0).
   The two modifiers act independently of each other (their pairwise interaction
   inside feature_016 = 1 was non-significant once both modifiers were included).

Step 5 — Triple-interaction model (h23). With main effects and pairwise/triple
interactions among feature_018, feature_016, feature_028, feature_031 (adjusted
for the standard prognostic covariates):
        feature_018                     coef ≈ 0
        feature_018 × feature_016       +4.68 months   (p ~ 0)
        feature_018 × feature_016 × feature_028   −2.52 (p = 3 × 10⁻²⁰¹)
        feature_018 × feature_016 × feature_031   −4.61 (p ~ 0)
        feature_018 × feature_028 (alone)         ≈ 0
        feature_018 × feature_031 (alone)         ≈ 0

Conclusion: feature_018 has a near-zero average effect EXCEPT in the
biomarker-positive subgroup, and even there the benefit is wiped out by either
of two "resistance" markers.


5. THE COMPLETE TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP  (Iterations 13, 24, 25)
------------------------------------------------------------------

Active subgroup definition  (h22, h33, h34):
        feature_016 == 1   AND   feature_028 == 0   AND   feature_031 == 0

   Subgroup size:                  n = 3,370   (6.7 % of the cohort)
   Treated   (feature_018 = 1):    n = 1,191   mean pfs_months = 8.089
   Untreated (feature_018 = 0):    n = 2,179   mean pfs_months = 3.257
   Difference:                     +4.83 months   (Welch t = 58.3, p ~ 0)

Outside the subgroup:               n = 46,630
   Average treatment effect:        −0.006 months  (p = 0.75)

Adding a single indicator term  feature_018 × I(feature_016 = 1, feature_028 = 0,
feature_031 = 0)  to the additive 33-feature model raises R² from 0.894 to ≈ 0.93,
and the simple feature_018 main effect drops to ≈ 0. This single subgroup
specification fully captures the treatment-effect heterogeneity in the dataset.


6. DATA-DRIVEN VERIFICATION  (Iteration 20)
-------------------------------------------

A T-learner with gradient-boosted regressors estimated CATE per patient. Patients
with CATE > +2 months (n = 3,307) had:  feature_016 = 100 %,  feature_031 = 0 %,
feature_028 = 0.6 %.  A regression tree on CATE used feature_016 as its first split
and feature_031 as its second split — exactly recovering the algebraic subgroup.


7. OTHER TREATMENT CANDIDATES  (Iterations 15, 21)
--------------------------------------------------

A pairwise interaction screen for feature_012, feature_020, feature_027 against the
key biomarkers and prognostic features yielded no interaction with |coef| > 0.06 at
p < 1 × 10⁻⁴. They appear to be inactive baseline characteristics.

feature_022 (~13 % prevalence, +0.19 marginal main effect) had its largest
interaction with feature_031 (+0.137, p = 4e-12) — quantitatively trivial vs
feature_018. feature_022 is best characterised as a small-effect baseline marker
rather than an active treatment.


8. ADDITIONAL FINDINGS — PROGNOSTIC ADDITIVITY  (Iteration 23)
--------------------------------------------------------------

A pairwise interaction screen across the nine adverse prognostic features yielded
no |coef| > 0.15 with p < 1e-10. The model R² rose only from 0.894 to 0.896 with
all 36 pairwise interactions. The dataset's prognostic structure is essentially
additive; the only large interaction term anywhere in the data is the
feature_018 × feature_016 × {feature_028, feature_031} treatment block.


9. OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
----------------------

(i)  pfs_months in this NSCLC-cohort dataset is well explained by an additive
     model in feature_015, feature_014, feature_023, feature_011, squamous
     histology, current-smoker status, feature_017, feature_025, feature_016,
     and a small number of others (R² ≈ 0.89).

(ii) feature_018 is the only feature in the dataset that behaves like an
     active treatment with respect to pfs_months. Its action is targeted: a
     ~+4.8-month gain is delivered only when the biomarker feature_016 is
     positive AND neither of two "resistance" modifiers (feature_028 = 1 or
     feature_031 = 1) is present.

(iii) Outside that 3-predicate subgroup the average effect of feature_018 on
      pfs_months is statistically indistinguishable from zero.

(iv) feature_012, feature_020, feature_027 do not behave as active treatments
     in this dataset: neither in marginal effect nor through any subgroup.

(v)  Continuous feature_019 (PD-L1-like) is uninformative for both prognosis and
     treatment-effect modification, arguing against an immunotherapy interpretation
     of feature_018; the targetable-mutation pattern (single biomarker required
     for benefit, with disease-extent / sex-like resistance modifiers) is more
     consistent with a molecular-targeted therapy.


FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY HYPOTHESIS  (h34):

   feature_018 increases pfs_months by approximately +4.8 months
   exclusively in patients defined by

       feature_016 == 1   AND   feature_028 == 0   AND   feature_031 == 0

   (n = 3,370; treated mean PFS 8.09 vs untreated 3.26 months; p ~ 0).
   In the rest of the cohort the average treatment effect is null.
