ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc
=================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients, 33 anonymized features (mix of continuous, binary, and
small-integer variables), single outcome pfs_months (range 0-15.3, mean 3.43).

Approach: 25 iterations of propose-test-refine, beginning with univariate main
effects, then a multivariable OLS, then systematic two-way interaction screens
for the top binary features, then forward refinement of a treatment-effect
subgroup definition.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. MAIN EFFECTS THAT REPLICATED IN A MULTIVARIABLE MODEL
------------------------------------------------------------

A 34-covariate OLS achieved R^2 = 0.894 against pfs_months. The features that
retained large, highly significant adjusted coefficients (each p < 1e-9 unless
noted) were:

  • feature_015 (continuous, range 30-90, mean 65)  beta = +0.168 / unit
      Strongest single predictor (univariate r = +0.757). Mean PFS rises
      monotonically from 0.74 in the lowest decile (30-52) to 6.55 in the
      highest decile (78-90). A small but highly significant positive
      quadratic term is also present (p < 1e-135).

  • feature_014 (ordinal 0/1/2, mean 0.80 — performance-status-like)
      beta = -1.10 / level. Means: 0=4.33, 1=3.19, 2=2.15. Strict monotonic.

  • feature_023 (binary, prevalence 65 %)  beta = -1.42
      Largest negative binary effect. Effect is uniform across all ECOG
      strata (-1.53 / -1.43 / -1.31), consistent with a global adverse
      prognostic factor (e.g., disease-burden marker), not a treatment.

  • feature_011 (binary, prevalence 25 %)  beta = -0.91
      Acts additively with feature_023 (joint mean PFS 2.24 in
      f23=1 & f11=1; near-zero interaction term).

  • feature_016 (binary, prevalence 13 %)  beta = +0.89
      Significant only because of an interaction with feature_018 — see
      below.

  • feature_018 (binary, prevalence 35 %)  beta = +0.32
      Same — only meaningful as a partner to feature_016.

  • feature_017 (continuous, 0-28, inflammation-like)
      beta = -0.073 / unit (every 10 units → -0.73 months PFS).

  • feature_025 (continuous, 1.7-5.5, nutrition/protein-like)
      beta = +0.46 / unit.

  • Histology feature_006: squamous beta = -0.72 vs adenocarcinoma.

  • Smoking feature_001: current beta = -0.55 vs former; never ≈ former.

  • feature_031 (binary, prevalence 45 %, demographic-like)
      beta = -0.20 (small main effect; large EFFECT-MODIFIER role).

  • feature_032 (continuous, 50-829)  beta = -0.0004 / unit (tiny).

  • feature_005 (binary, prevalence 3 %)  beta = -0.11 main effect, but
      acts as a complete suppressor of the (16,18) treatment effect.

The remaining lab-like features (feature_003, 004, 007, 008, 009, 010, 013,
019, 024, 026, 029, 030, 033) and binaries (002, 012, 020, 021, 022, 027,
028 in adjusted model) had no incremental signal once the above were in the
model. Several with weak main effects (022, 028) turned out to be either
weakly prognostic globally (022 ≈ +0.2 months) or strongly modifying within
the (16,18) subgroup (028 abolishes benefit).

------------------------------------------------------------
2. TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY: A LARGE AND-PATTERN SUBGROUP
------------------------------------------------------------

A two-way interaction screen across all candidate-treatment binaries
(feature_023, 011, 016, 018, 022, 028, 002, 005, 021, 012, 020, 027) versus
all 33 modifiers identified one dominating interaction:

  feature_016 × feature_018:  +2.54 months (p < 1e-300)
     with both main effects ≈ 0 in the joint model.

Cell means by (feature_016, feature_018):
     (0,0) 3.33  n=28,334
     (0,1) 3.33  n=15,298
     (1,0) 3.21  n= 4,122
     (1,1) 5.77  n= 2,246    ← only this cell shows a benefit

This is a synergistic AND-pattern: the benefit requires BOTH feature_016=1
and feature_018=1; neither alone confers any benefit.

Stratifying within (1,1) revealed three additional features that fully
suppress the benefit when present:

  • feature_031: in (16=1,18=1,31=1) mean PFS = 3.18  (no benefit)
                in (16=1,18=1,31=0) mean PFS = 7.88  (large benefit)

  • feature_028: in (16=1,18=1,31=0,28=1) mean PFS = 2.50
                in (16=1,18=1,31=0,28=0) mean PFS = 8.09

  • feature_005: in (16=1,18=1,31=0,28=0,5=1) mean PFS = 3.57
                in (16=1,18=1,31=0,28=0,5=0) mean PFS = 8.23

Best-supported full subgroup definition:

   feature_016 = 1   AND
   feature_018 = 1   AND
   feature_031 = 0   AND
   feature_028 = 0   AND
   feature_005 = 0

Subgroup size: n = 1,154 (2.3 % of the cohort).
Subgroup mean pfs_months = 8.234  (sd 2.27).
Non-subgroup mean pfs_months = 3.318.
Crude difference: +4.92 months (Welch p < 1e-300).
Adjusted difference (controlling feature_015, 014, smoking, histology,
017, 025, 023, 011): +4.92 (p < 1e-300, model R^2 = 0.978).

Stability check (treatment effect within the qualifying-modifier stratum
{31=0, 28=0, 5=0}, broken out by other prognostic factors):

     f23=0 & f11=0:  +5.10 months (p < 1e-142)
     f23=0 & f11=1:  +4.76 months (p < 1e-36)
     f23=1 & f11=0:  +4.82 months (p < 1e-231)
     f23=1 & f11=1:  +4.75 months (p < 1e-76)
     ECOG 0 / 1 / 2: +4.95 / +4.96 / +4.61 months

The treatment effect is essentially constant (≈ +4.6-5.1 months) across
all combinations of other prognostic factors — feature_023, feature_011,
ECOG, histology, smoking only shift the absolute PFS level, not the
effect size of the treatment.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. WHAT WAS REFUTED OR DOWN-WEIGHTED
------------------------------------------------------------

  • Simple main effects of feature_016 alone or feature_018 alone:
    REFUTED as treatment effects in the standard sense — both are
    contingent on each other and on the modifier triplet.

  • feature_022 as a candidate treatment: REFUTED. Its small +0.19
    main effect is consistent across feature_023, feature_011,
    feature_031 strata (small uniform prognostic factor), and is
    fully erased inside the f16=1 or f18=1 sub-populations.

  • feature_023 and feature_011 as treatments with heterogeneous
    benefit: REFUTED. Their negative effects are uniform across ECOG
    and other strata; behavior consistent with global adverse
    prognostic factors. They combine roughly additively (no
    super-/sub-additive interaction beyond +0.10 month).

  • Remaining 13 lab-like continuous features (feature_003, 004, 007,
    008, 009, 010, 013, 019, 024, 026, 029, 030, 033): no significant
    independent contribution after adjustment. (feature_032 retained a
    tiny statistically-significant coefficient of -0.0004, but the
    magnitude is clinically negligible.)

  • Binary features 002, 012, 020, 027, 021: no role either as
    treatments, biomarkers, or modifiers of any other effect.

  • Search for a SECOND independent treatment-effect subgroup beyond
    (16,18,31,28,5): no additional comparable interaction emerged
    in the systematic two-way screen.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

Predictors of pfs_months in this cohort fall into three buckets:

  (a) STRONG ADDITIVE PROGNOSTIC FACTORS — feature_015 (dominant linear
      effect, 0.17 months / unit, +small positive curvature),
      feature_014 (ECOG-like, -1.1 / level), feature_023 (-1.4),
      feature_011 (-0.9), histology squamous (-0.7), current smoking
      (-0.55), feature_017 (-0.073 / unit), feature_025 (+0.46 / unit),
      feature_031 (-0.20), feature_032 (very small), feature_005
      (-0.11 main).

  (b) ONE LARGE INTERACTION-DEFINED TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP —
      Patients with (feature_016=1 AND feature_018=1 AND feature_031=0
      AND feature_028=0 AND feature_005=0) experience ~+4.9 months of
      additional PFS independent of all other factors. Both of
      feature_016 and feature_018 are required (the AND pattern); each
      of feature_031=1, feature_028=1, or feature_005=1 individually
      abolishes the benefit. This pattern is consistent with a
      treatment that acts only when a positive biomarker (one of 16/18)
      is co-present and certain unfavorable covariates (31, 28, 5) are
      absent.

  (c) NULL OR NEAR-NULL FEATURES — the bulk of the lab panel and
      several binary indicators contribute essentially nothing once the
      above structure is accounted for.

A 10-predictor model (the subgroup indicator + the 9 main prognostic
factors) explains 97.8 % of the variance in pfs_months, suggesting the
data-generating structure is well captured by these effects.

The single best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis:

  Patients with feature_016 = 1 AND feature_018 = 1 AND feature_031 = 0
  AND feature_028 = 0 AND feature_005 = 0 have approximately +4.9
  months longer pfs_months (subgroup mean 8.23 vs non-subgroup 3.32,
  p < 1e-300, adjusted for all major prognostic factors). The benefit
  requires the joint co-presence of feature_016 and feature_018 AND the
  joint absence of feature_031, feature_028, and feature_005.
