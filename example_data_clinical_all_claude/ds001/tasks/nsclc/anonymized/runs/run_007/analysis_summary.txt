Analysis Summary: ds001_nsclc (n = 50,000)
=============================================
Outcome analyzed: pfs_months (mean 3.43, median 3.32, range 0-15.33; 7.3% of
patients have pfs_months exactly 0; 95th percentile 7.3; <0.01% censored at 15).

Modeling approach
-----------------
Across 15 iterations I (1) screened every feature univariably against
pfs_months, (2) fit a fully adjusted multivariable OLS model, (3) tested
candidate prognostic factors and lab-like continuous variables, (4) ran a
pairwise interaction screen across all 13 binary features, (5) used the top
interaction signals to define a candidate treatment / biomarker pair,
(6) iteratively expanded the responder subgroup with additional treatment-
effect modifiers, (7) used a regression tree as a non-parametric confirmation,
and (8) checked whether any other binary feature behaved like a treatment.
All work was done in pandas + statsmodels + scikit-learn against
dataset.parquet.

Main effects on pfs_months
--------------------------
Adjusted OLS fit explained R^2 = 0.894 of the variance. Direction and
significance of the strongest predictors:

  Continuous:
    feature_015 (range 30-90, plausibly age in years):  +0.170 PFS-months per
        year (univariable r = +0.76, p ~ 0); the dominant continuous
        predictor. The positive direction is striking but is consistent across
        every age decile (mean PFS rises monotonically from 0.74 in the
        bottom decile to 6.55 in the top).
    feature_017 (count-like 0-28):  -0.073 / unit, p < 1e-87.
    feature_025 (range 1.7-5.5, albumin-like):  +0.46 / unit, p < 1e-71.
    feature_032 (range 50-829, LDH/platelet-like):  -4e-4 / unit, p = 2e-28.
    All other continuous lab-like features (feature_003, feature_004,
    feature_007, feature_008, feature_009, feature_010, feature_013,
    feature_026, feature_029, feature_030, feature_033) had |r| <= 0.007
    and were non-significant.  feature_019 (range 0-0.8, plausibly a
    proportion such as PD-L1 TPS) was univariably and multivariably null.

  Categorical / ordinal:
    feature_014 (3 levels, 0/1/2):  monotonic decline 4.33 -> 3.19 -> 2.15
        months; treated as ordinal severity / stage. Adjusted coefficients
        -1.15 (level 1) and -2.18 (level 2).
    feature_006 (adenocarcinoma vs squamous): squamous -0.86 mo univariably,
        -0.72 mo adjusted.
    feature_001 (smoking: former / current / never): current smokers
        -0.62 mo vs the other two; never and former are essentially
        identical (3.68 vs 3.61).

  Binary prognostic markers (always associated with worse PFS):
    feature_023 (~65% positive): -1.44 mo (-1.42 adjusted).
    feature_011 (~25% positive): -0.92 mo (-0.91 adjusted).
    feature_028 (~5% positive):  -0.81 mo univariable; small main effect
        (-0.67 outside the responder cell), but acts as a strong
        treatment-effect suppressor inside the responder cell (see below).
    feature_031 (~45% positive): -0.20 mo univariable; in adjusted model
        -0.20 (driven essentially entirely by the responder subgroup,
        see below).

  Binary noise variables (no main effect; no interaction effect):
    feature_012, feature_020, feature_027 -- all univariable |diff| < 0.04
    and all multivariable p > 0.7. These are non-prognostic indicators in
    this cohort.
    feature_002, feature_022, feature_021 -- weak / null main effects.
    feature_022 is sharply enriched in never-smokers (51% prevalence in
    never vs 3% in current), but neither it nor any of these features
    showed treatment-effect heterogeneity.

Treatment-effect heterogeneity (the central finding)
----------------------------------------------------
A pairwise interaction screen across all 13 binary features uncovered one
dominant signal, far stronger than any other:

    feature_016 x feature_018 interaction coefficient = +2.56,
        p < 1e-300.

Stratified means showed that the marginal +0.32 mo benefit of feature_018
and the +0.78 mo benefit of feature_016 are essentially entirely concentrated
in the cell where both are positive:

    (feature_016=0, feature_018=0):   3.334 mo (n = 28,334)
    (feature_016=0, feature_018=1):   3.329 mo (n = 15,298)
    (feature_016=1, feature_018=0):   3.213 mo (n =  4,122)
    (feature_016=1, feature_018=1):   5.766 mo (n =  2,246)

The PFS effect of feature_018 is -0.005 mo when feature_016 = 0 and +2.55 mo
when feature_016 = 1. Working interpretation: feature_018 is a treatment
indicator (~35% prevalence) and feature_016 is its predictive biomarker
(~13% prevalence). The roles could be swapped without changing the
statistical content.

Within the responder cell (feature_018 = 1 AND feature_016 = 1, n = 2,246,
mean PFS 5.77), three additional binary features act as resistance markers:

    feature_031 = 1: mean PFS drops from 7.88 (n=1,237) to 3.18 (n=1,009)
        -- essentially abolishes the treatment benefit.
    feature_028 = 1: mean PFS drops from 5.91 (n=2,159) to 2.08 (n=87)
        -- abolishes benefit and adds an excess deficit.
    feature_005 = 1: mean PFS drops from 5.84 (n=2,180) to 3.36 (n=66)
        -- abolishes benefit.

A critical sanity check: feature_031 and feature_005 act as PURE treatment-
effect modifiers. Outside the responder cell, the marginal effect of
feature_031 on pfs_months is +0.006 mo (95% null) and of feature_005 is
-0.007 mo (95% null), even though inside the cell each one wipes out
several months of benefit. feature_028 is a hybrid: a small adverse main
effect (-0.67 mo outside the responder cell) plus a much larger interaction
inside it.

Final, fully-specified subgroup hypothesis
------------------------------------------
The maximally responsive subgroup is:

    feature_018 = 1  AND  feature_016 = 1  AND
    feature_031 = 0  AND  feature_028 = 0  AND  feature_005 = 0

In this subgroup (n = 1,154), mean pfs_months = 8.234. The matched
biomarker-positive untreated counterfactual (same predicates but
feature_018 = 0; n = 2,112) has mean pfs_months = 3.249. The estimated
treatment effect is therefore +4.99 months (two-sample t-test p ~ 0).
Outside this subgroup, the marginal effect of feature_018 is between
-0.2 and +0.2 mo and never significant.

A regression tree fitted only on the (feature_018 = 1, feature_016 = 1)
subgroup recovered exactly this structure non-parametrically: the root
split was feature_031 <= 0.5; on the f031 = 0 branch the next splits were
age (feature_015) and feature_028, with leaf predictions of 7.0-11.6 mo;
on the f031 = 1 branch leaf predictions were 0.6-6.85 mo, almost all
below the f031 = 0 leaves. This confirms the hand-built subgroup was
not an artifact of the manual interaction search.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
Supported:
  - feature_015 (age-like) is the strongest continuous predictor of
    pfs_months, in the (counterintuitive) positive direction.
  - feature_014 acts as ordinal severity with monotonic adverse effect.
  - feature_006 squamous, feature_001 current-smoker, feature_023,
    feature_011, feature_028 are adverse prognostic factors.
  - feature_017, feature_025, feature_032 are real lab signals.
  - feature_016 x feature_018 is by far the dominant interaction.
  - feature_031, feature_005 are pure treatment-response suppressors
    (no main effect, large interaction).
  - feature_028 is a hybrid suppressor with a small main effect.

Refuted / nulls confirmed:
  - feature_012, feature_020, feature_027 have no main or interaction
    effect on pfs_months.
  - feature_019 (a 0-1 proportion) does not predict pfs_months.
  - feature_021, feature_022, feature_002 are not the treatments;
    they show no subgroup with > 1-month treatment-like effect.
  - The strong enrichment of feature_022 in never-smokers does NOT
    translate into any prognostic or treatment-modifying signal.

Overall conclusion
------------------
This cohort displays exactly one large, well-defined treatment-effect
heterogeneity signature: a treatment indicator (feature_018) confers
approximately +5 months of pfs_months, but only in patients who are
biomarker-positive (feature_016 = 1) AND free of three independent
resistance markers (feature_031 = 0, feature_028 = 0, feature_005 = 0).
In every other patient, feature_018 has essentially no effect. All other
candidate binary features act either as general prognostic factors
(uniformly adverse or uniformly favorable) or as pure noise; no second
treatment-effect-heterogeneity pattern of comparable magnitude was
detected.
