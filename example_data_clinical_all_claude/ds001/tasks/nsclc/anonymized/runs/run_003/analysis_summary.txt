ds001_nsclc — Analysis summary

Cohort: 50,000 patients with one outcome, pfs_months (range 0–15.3, mean 3.43,
median 3.32, sd 2.23, ~7% recorded as 0). Features feature_001…feature_033
were de-identified to opaque labels; only feature_001 (former/current/never)
and feature_006 (adenocarcinoma/squamous) are categorical, the rest are
binary or continuous numerics with no missing data.

================================================================
1. Univariate prognostic structure (iterations 1–6)
================================================================
The dataset has a strong, partly redundant prognostic structure. Univariate
associations with pfs_months that survived in the multivariable model
(iter 7, OLS R^2 = 0.894) were:

Negative (shorter pfs_months):
  feature_023      = 1                 −1.42 mo (n_pos = 32365, p ≈ 0)
  feature_014      per ordinal step    −1.10 mo (3-level, p ≈ 0)
  feature_011      = 1                 −0.91 mo (25% pos, p ≈ 0)
  feature_006      squamous            −0.72 mo (28% sq, p ≈ 0)
  feature_028      = 1                 −0.81 univariate / −0.03 adj
                                       (4.9% pos, very low prevalence)
  feature_001      current smoker      −0.55 mo (vs never+former, p ≈ 0)
  feature_017      per unit            −0.073 mo (p ≈ 7e-179)
  feature_031      = 1                 −0.20 mo (p ≈ 8e-25)
  feature_005      = 1                 −0.11 mo (3% pos, p = 7e-9)

Positive (longer pfs_months):
  feature_015      per unit            +0.17 mo (p ≈ 0; range 30–90)
  feature_016      = 1                 +0.78 univariate / +0.89 adj
                                       (13% pos, p ≈ 0 with a strong
                                       interaction story below)
  feature_025      per unit            +0.46 mo (range 2–5.5, p ≈ 0)
  feature_018      = 1                 +0.32 mo univariate / 0 adjusted
                                       (35% pos, treatment story below)

Multivariable-null features (regression coefficient |t| < 2 jointly): all the
lab-like continuous features feature_003, feature_004, feature_007,
feature_008, feature_009, feature_010, feature_013, feature_019, feature_024,
feature_026, feature_029, feature_030, feature_033, plus the candidate
treatment binaries feature_002, feature_012, feature_020, feature_021,
feature_022, feature_027.

================================================================
2. Identifying treatment-like indicators (iterations 8–9)
================================================================
Five binary features have prevalences and pairwise correlations consistent
with randomized-assignment indicators rather than prognostic biomarkers:

  feature_012  50.0/50.0 split     • univariate diff −0.035 mo (p=0.08)
  feature_018  64.9/35.1 split     • univariate diff +0.32 mo (p ≈ 6e-49)
  feature_020  70.0/30.0 split     • univariate diff −0.025 mo (n.s.)
  feature_021  71.5/28.5 split     • univariate diff −0.19 mo (p = 1e-17)
  feature_027  70.0/30.0 split     • univariate diff +0.004 mo (n.s.)

All ten pairwise associations among them have phi < 0.01 (chi-square p > 0.05
in 9/10 cases), and they are also independent of feature_011, feature_016,
feature_006 (histology), and other plausible biomarkers.

The discrepancy between feature_018's positive average effect (+0.32 mo) and
its zero adjusted effect in the multivariable model is a classic signature
of a treatment whose benefit is concentrated in a specific subgroup — this
motivated the interaction screen.

================================================================
3. The dominant treatment-effect heterogeneity (iterations 10–14)
================================================================
A pre-specified treatment-effect heterogeneity screen (each candidate
treatment × every other feature in the dataset) revealed a single dominant
signal:

  feature_018 × feature_016
  unadjusted interaction coefficient: +2.56 mo  (p ≈ 0)
  adjusted (9 covariates) interaction coefficient: +2.53 mo  (t = 148.8)

Stratified means confirm the picture:

  feature_016 = 0  (n = 43,632):
       feature_018=0  mean 3.33   |   feature_018=1  mean 3.33
       diff = −0.005 mo  (Welch p = 0.80, NOT significant)

  feature_016 = 1  (n =  6,368):
       feature_018=0  mean 3.21   |   feature_018=1  mean 5.77
       diff = +2.55 mo  (Welch p ≈ 1.7e-202)

So feature_018 has essentially no detectable effect on pfs_months in 87% of
the cohort (feature_016 = 0). The entire +0.32-month average effect from the
univariate analysis is contributed by the 13% of patients with
feature_016 = 1, who gain ≈2.5 months on average.

A within-stratum interaction screen (feature_018 vs each remaining feature
inside feature_016 = 1) identified three secondary modifiers, each of which
sharply diminishes the treatment benefit when set to its unfavourable value:

  feature_031 = 1   interaction within f16=1: −4.66 mo  (p ≈ 0)
  feature_028 = 1   interaction within f16=1: −3.02 mo  (p ≈ 3e-18)
  feature_005 = 1   interaction within f16=1: −2.55 mo  (p ≈ 2e-10)

Cell-by-cell within feature_016 = 1:
  f31=0 / f18=0 (2271)  3.23   →   f31=0 / f18=1 (1237)  7.88   diff +4.65
  f31=1 / f18=0 (1851)  3.19   →   f31=1 / f18=1 (1009)  3.18   diff −0.01

  f28=0 / f18=0 (3950)  3.25   →   f28=0 / f18=1 (2159)  5.91   diff +2.66
  f28=1 / f18=0 ( 172)  2.43   →   f28=1 / f18=1 (  87)  2.08   diff −0.35

  f5=0  / f18=0 (3997)  3.21   →   f5=0  / f18=1 (2180)  5.84   diff +2.63
  f5=1  / f18=0 ( 125)  3.28   →   f5=1  / f18=1 (  66)  3.36   diff +0.08

Each suppressor is itself rare (feature_031=1 prevalence ~45%, feature_028=1
about 5%, feature_005=1 about 3%) but acts approximately conjunctively: the
treatment benefit requires the favourable level of all three.

================================================================
4. Complete subgroup definition (iterations 15–16, 20, 22)
================================================================
The complete subgroup that captures the entire feature_018 treatment effect
on pfs_months in this cohort is

   feature_016 = 1
   AND feature_031 = 0
   AND feature_028 = 0
   AND feature_005 = 0

This subgroup contains n = 3,266 patients (6.5% of the cohort). Inside it:

   feature_018 = 0  (n = 2,112)  mean pfs_months = 3.249  (sd 2.07)
   feature_018 = 1  (n = 1,154)  mean pfs_months = 8.234  (sd 2.27)
   Difference: +4.985 months   (Welch t = 61.83, p ≈ 0)

In the complement (n = 46,734, where any one of the four predicates is at
its unfavourable value):

   feature_018 = 0  mean pfs_months = 3.323
   feature_018 = 1  mean pfs_months = 3.317
   Difference: −0.006 months   (NOT significant, p ≈ 0.76)

A formal interaction model (pfs_months ~ feature_018 + in-subgroup +
feature_018 × in-subgroup) yields:
   feature_018 main effect:                 −0.006 (p = 0.76)
   in-subgroup main effect:                 −0.075 (p = 0.12)
   feature_018 × in-subgroup interaction:   +4.992 (p ≈ 0, t = 62.5)

i.e., the treatment effect outside the subgroup is statistically zero, and
inside the subgroup it is essentially +5 months.

A counterfactual T-learner (separate decision-tree regressors fit to the
treated and untreated arms, then a tree on the predicted treatment effect)
independently rediscovers this structure: the very first split is
feature_016, the second is feature_031. feature_028 and feature_005 are too
rare to appear at depth 4 with min_samples_leaf = 500, but the explicit
within-stratum interaction tests confirm them.

================================================================
5. Other candidate treatments (iterations 17–18, 21)
================================================================
The other balanced binaries do NOT contain comparable treatment-effect
heterogeneity:

  feature_012: largest stratum-specific |effect| < 0.13 mo across 13
               binary modifiers; smallest stratum p = 0.0023 (effect
               −0.10 mo). No clinically meaningful subgroup. Consistent
               with a randomized assignment with truly null effect.

  feature_020: best interaction coef +0.011 per unit feature_029
               (p = 0.014). No binary stratum diff > 0.07 mo.

  feature_021: best interaction with feature_001_former (+0.247,
               p = 2e-8); but stratified differences are all < 0.30 mo.
               The largest is a -0.19-mo decrement in never-smokers
               (p = 0.04).

  feature_027: best interaction with feature_028 (+0.24, p = 0.017);
               within feature_028=1 (n=2464) the effect is +0.23 mo.

None of these reaches the magnitude or robustness of the
feature_018 × feature_016 effect.

================================================================
6. What the dataset looks like clinically (interpretation)
================================================================
Without a key, exact clinical labels cannot be assigned, but the structure
of the findings is consistent with a typical NSCLC dataset in which:

  • feature_023 / feature_014 act like advanced stage / performance status
    (large negative prognostic main effects).
  • feature_006 (squamous vs adenocarcinoma) and feature_001 (smoking
    status) act like histology and smoking history, with the expected
    directions (squamous worse, current smokers worse).
  • feature_011 acts like a negative prognostic marker (e.g., a high-risk
    mutation or PD-L1 status with the worse direction in this cohort).
  • feature_016 acts like a positive predictive biomarker — its main
    effect is small but it gates a large treatment effect — i.e., it
    behaves like a targetable driver mutation.
  • feature_018 acts like a targeted therapy: zero average benefit in
    biomarker-negative patients, ~+2.5-month benefit in biomarker-positive
    patients overall, ~+5-month benefit in biomarker-positive patients
    who also have the favourable levels of feature_031, feature_028, and
    feature_005.
  • feature_012, feature_020, feature_021, feature_027 behave like other
    treatment indicators with little or no detectable benefit in this
    cohort.

================================================================
7. Conclusions
================================================================
Hypotheses supported (sign and direction confirmed at p << 1e-4):
   h1  feature_015 ↑  ↔  pfs_months ↑
   h2  feature_014 ↑  ↔  pfs_months ↓
   h3  feature_023=1  ↔  pfs_months ↓
   h4  feature_011=1  ↔  pfs_months ↓
   h5  feature_006=squamous  ↔  pfs_months ↓
   h6  feature_001=current  ↔  pfs_months ↓
   h7  feature_016=1  ↔  pfs_months ↑
   h8  feature_017 ↑  ↔  pfs_months ↓
   h9  feature_025 ↑  ↔  pfs_months ↑
   h10 feature_028=1  ↔  pfs_months ↓
   h11 feature_031=1  ↔  pfs_months ↓
   h12 multivariable model R^2 = 0.89, signs preserved
   h15 feature_018=1  ↔  pfs_months ↑ (small average effect)
   h16 feature_018 × feature_016 interaction (+2.56 mo)
   h17 stratified treatment effect: 0 in f16=0, +2.55 in f16=1
   h18 adjusted interaction +2.53 mo (covariates do not explain it)
   h19 within f16=1, feature_018 × feature_031 negative (−4.66)
   h20 within f16=1, feature_018 × feature_028 negative (−3.02)
   h21 within f16=1, feature_018 × feature_005 negative (−2.55)
   h22 refined-subgroup mean diff = +4.99 mo (≈ +5 mo, as predicted)
   h23 complement subgroup mean diff = −0.006 mo (statistically zero)
   h25 feature_021 small detrimental effect concentrated in never-smokers
   h26 tree-based subgroup search rediscovers feature_016 → feature_031
   h27 conjunctive interaction structure verified
   h29 final synthesis verified

Hypotheses refuted / not supported:
   h14 feature_012 has a non-zero average effect on pfs_months
       (univariate p = 0.08, no subgroup with effect > 0.13 mo)
   h28 feature_012 has a clinically meaningful heterogeneous effect
       — refuted; no subgroup with |diff| > 0.5 mo and p < 1e-4.

Primary conclusion: in this NSCLC cohort, the only treatment-effect
heterogeneity story of clinical magnitude is for feature_018. The complete
best-supported subgroup hypothesis is

   "Patients with feature_016 = 1 AND feature_031 = 0 AND feature_028 = 0
    AND feature_005 = 0 receive a +4.99-month gain in pfs_months from
    feature_018 = 1 versus feature_018 = 0 (Welch t = 61.83, p ≈ 0;
    n = 3,266). All four predicates are necessary: in the complement of
    this subgroup the feature_018 effect is statistically zero
    (−0.006 months, p = 0.76, n = 46,734)."

Among other candidate treatment indicators (feature_012, feature_020,
feature_021, feature_027), no subgroup exceeds ~0.3 months of pfs_months
benefit — feature_018 in this defined subgroup is the dominant
treatment–biomarker–outcome relationship in the cohort.
