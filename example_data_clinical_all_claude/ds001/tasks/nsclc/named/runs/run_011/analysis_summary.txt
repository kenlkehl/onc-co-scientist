ANALYSIS SUMMARY -- ds001_nsclc (n = 50,000 patients; outcome = pfs_months)
==========================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
A 50k-patient NSCLC EHR-derived cohort with progression-free survival (pfs_months)
as the only outcome. Eighteen iterations of propose-test-refine analyses were run,
covering univariate prognostic associations, treatment main effects, treatment-by-
biomarker interactions, and an exhaustive 2-way subgroup heterogeneity search for
each of the four treatments. The mean PFS in the cohort is 3.43 months
(SD 2.23).

PROGNOSTIC FEATURES (univariate and multivariable OLS, R^2 = 0.894)
-------------------------------------------------------------------
The strongest independent prognostic associations with longer pfs_months in the
multivariable model were:

  + age_years            +0.168 mo per year         (p ~ 0; r = +0.76 univariate)
  + albumin_g_dl         +0.46  mo per g/dL          (p ~ 0)
  + smoking_status       +0.55  mo (former or never vs current)
  + kras_g12c            +0.89  mo (KRAS G12C+)      (p ~ 0; main effect, see Treatment section for context)

Strongest negative independent associations:

  - ecog_ps              -1.10  mo per unit           (p ~ 0)
  - stage_iv             -1.42  mo                    (p ~ 0)
  - has_brain_mets       -0.91  mo                    (p ~ 0)
  - histology=squamous   -0.72  mo vs adenocarcinoma   (p ~ 0)
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo  -0.073 mo per percent        (p ~ 0)
  - sex_female           -0.20  mo                    (p ~ 0; small but consistent)
  - brca2_mutation       -0.11  mo                    (p ~ 0)
  - ldh_u_l              -4e-4  mo per U/L            (p ~ 0; small magnitude)
  - alk_fusion           -0.81  mo univariate; -0.03 mo (ns) after adjustment

Notable findings that ran COUNTER to typical NSCLC literature:
  * age_years is positively associated with PFS (univariate r = +0.76).
  * kras_g12c is associated with longer (not shorter) PFS as a main effect.
  * alk_fusion is associated with shorter (not longer) univariate PFS, but the
    effect disappears after adjustment for other covariates.
  * tmb_high is associated with shorter PFS overall (-0.19 mo univariate; ns
    after adjustment).
  * pdl1_tps showed no detectable linear association with PFS.

Lab markers crp_mg_l, nlr, hemoglobin_g_dl, alkaline_phosphatase_u_l, ast_u_l,
alt_u_l, total_bilirubin_mg_dl, creatinine_mg_dl, bun_mg_dl, sodium_meq_l,
potassium_meq_l, and calcium_mg_dl all showed |r|<0.01 with PFS and were not
significant in adjusted models -- they appear to be noise variables in this cohort.

TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS
----------------------
Univariate (and corroborated in adjusted multivariable OLS):

  treatment_pembrolizumab   -0.035 mo (p = 0.08)   NOT significant
  treatment_sotorasib       +0.32  mo (p ~ 1e-49)  significant; driven entirely by subgroup
  treatment_olaparib        -0.025 mo (p = 0.26)   NOT significant
  treatment_osimertinib     +0.004 mo (p = 0.87)   NOT significant

Three of the four treatments thus showed no detectable benefit at the cohort
level, including in their canonical biomarker-defined target populations:

  * Osimertinib in EGFR-mutant patients: effect = -0.014 mo (interaction p = 0.76)
  * Olaparib in BRCA2-mutant patients: effect = +0.010 mo (interaction p = 0.78)
  * Pembrolizumab in PD-L1 high (>=0.5): effect = -0.07 mo (interaction p = 0.36)
  * Pembrolizumab in TMB-high: effect = -0.05 mo (interaction p = 0.68)

Exhaustive 2-way binary-feature subgroup searches (n>=200 in each subgroup,
n_treated and n_control >=50, |effect|>0.3 mo, p<1e-3) identified NO subgroup
in which pembrolizumab, olaparib, or osimertinib provided a clinically meaningful
positive treatment effect. Continuous-feature interaction screens for these three
treatments likewise failed to find any clinically meaningful effect modifier.

TREATMENT EFFECT HETEROGENEITY -- SOTORASIB
-------------------------------------------
The sotorasib treatment effect is highly heterogeneous and concentrated in a
specific subgroup. The discovery proceeded as follows:

1. Sotorasib x kras_g12c interaction was massive: effect in KRAS G12C+ patients
   = +2.55 mo vs effect in KRAS- = -0.005 mo (interaction p ~ 0).

2. Within KRAS G12C+ patients, the effect was further modified by sex:
       KRAS+ males (sex_female = 0):  sotorasib effect = +4.64 mo (p ~ 0)
       KRAS+ females (sex_female = 1): sotorasib effect = -0.01 mo (p = 0.88)
   Three-way OLS interaction (sotorasib x kras_g12c x sex_female) confirmed:
   coef = -4.69, p ~ 0 -- the female sex flag completely cancels the
   KRAS-targeted sotorasib benefit.

3. Within KRAS G12C+ patients, the effect was also further attenuated by:
       alk_fusion = 1: sotorasib effect drops from +2.67 (ALK-) to -0.35 (ALK+),
                       interaction p = 4.1e-7
       brca2_mutation = 1: sotorasib effect drops from +2.63 (BRCA2-) to +0.08
                           (BRCA2+), interaction p = 6.1e-3

4. The COMPLETE treatment-effective subgroup definition is therefore:
       kras_g12c = 1  AND  sex_female = 0  AND  alk_fusion = 0  AND  brca2_mutation = 0
   In this subgroup (n = 3,266), sotorasib effect = +4.99 mo (p ~ 0).
   Outside this subgroup (n = 46,734), sotorasib effect = -0.006 mo (p = 0.76).

5. A multivariable OLS containing the four-way interaction terms recovered:
       sotorasib (main)                              +0.001 (ns)
       sotorasib : kras_g12c                         +4.77   (p ~ 0)
       sotorasib : kras_g12c : sex_female            -4.59   (p ~ 0)
       sotorasib : kras_g12c : alk_fusion            -2.41   (p ~ 0)
       sotorasib : kras_g12c : brca2_mutation        -2.74   (p ~ 0)
   Quantitatively consistent with the subgroup-mean estimates.

FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS (for pfs_months)
-----------------------------------------------------------
* treatment_sotorasib: PFS benefit ~ +5 months, concentrated in patients with
    kras_g12c = 1 AND sex_female = 0 AND alk_fusion = 0 AND brca2_mutation = 0.
    The benefit is fully suppressed if ANY of the following is present: female
    sex, ALK fusion, or BRCA2 mutation. Outside the subgroup, sotorasib has
    no detectable PFS effect (-0.006 mo, p = 0.76).

* treatment_pembrolizumab: NO subgroup with detectable PFS benefit. Including
    PD-L1-high, TMB-high, squamous histology, no-STK11-co-mutation, and other
    canonically responsive subgroups -- all null.

* treatment_olaparib: NO subgroup with detectable PFS benefit, including the
    canonical BRCA2-mutant target population.

* treatment_osimertinib: NO subgroup with detectable PFS benefit, including the
    canonical EGFR-mutant target population.

CONCLUSIONS
-----------
This dataset shows a single, very strong treatment-biomarker interaction
(sotorasib x KRAS G12C) that is further restricted to male, ALK-negative,
BRCA2-negative patients. Inside that 4-feature joint subgroup, sotorasib confers
a roughly 5-month PFS benefit; outside it, sotorasib has no effect.

The prognostic structure is dominated by ECOG performance status, stage,
brain metastases, histology, weight loss, and albumin -- consistent qualitatively
with the broader NSCLC literature, though several signed associations
(age positive; kras_g12c positive main; alk_fusion negative univariate;
tmb_high negative) run opposite the conventional expectation, possibly
reflecting cohort-specific selection or the synthetic-data generation process.

Three of the four labelled targeted/IO therapies (pembrolizumab, olaparib,
osimertinib) appear inactive in this cohort even within their canonical
biomarker-defined subgroups, which is itself a notable -- and clinically
implausible -- finding that should be interpreted as a property of this
specific dataset rather than as evidence about real-world drug efficacy.
