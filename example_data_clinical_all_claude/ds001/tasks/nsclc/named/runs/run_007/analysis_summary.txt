ds001_nsclc — Analysis Summary
================================

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patients aggregated from EHR/commercial data, with one PFS
outcome (pfs_months) and 33 features spanning demographics, smoking, ECOG, stage,
brain metastases, histology, mutations (EGFR, KRAS G12C, ALK, STK11, BRCA2),
PD-L1 TPS, TMB, prognostic labs/inflammation markers, routine chemistries, and
four binary treatment indicators (pembrolizumab, sotorasib, olaparib,
osimertinib).

I worked the propose-test-refine loop for 25 iterations. The arc of the
investigation was: (1) main effects of features and treatments; (2) prespecified
biomarker × treatment interactions; (3) systematic heterogeneity scans for each
treatment; (4) targeted multi-feature subgroup definitions for any treatment
where the marginal effect appeared concentrated.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Main effects on PFS (iter 1–3)
------------------------------------------------------------

Strong, biologically plausible prognostic features:
- ecog_ps: β = -1.10 mo per ECOG point (p ≈ 0). Largest single prognostic.
- stage_iv: stage IV − non-stage IV = -1.44 mo (p ≈ 0).
- has_brain_mets: -0.92 mo (p ≈ 0).
- histology: adenocarcinoma − squamous = +0.86 mo (p ≈ 0).
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: β = -0.073 mo per percent (p ≈ 7e-179).
- albumin_g_dl: β = +0.44 mo per g/dL (p ≈ 3e-109).
- smoking_status: never 3.67 mo, former 3.61, current 2.99 (ANOVA p ≈ 4e-187);
  current smokers fare considerably worse.
- age_years: small positive effect on PFS, β = +0.17 mo/yr (p ≈ 0). Counter-
  intuitive at first glance; likely reflects selection of older patients toward
  histologies/treatments that, conditional on the rest of the cohort, run
  longer in this dataset.
- sex_female: female − male = -0.20 mo (p ≈ 8e-25). Females have slightly
  shorter PFS.

Mutation/biomarker main effects:
- KRAS G12C+: +0.78 mo (p ≈ 7e-94) — driven downstream by sotorasib effect.
- ALK fusion+: -0.81 mo (p ≈ 6e-66) — ALK+ patients fare worse, presumably
  because no ALK inhibitor is offered in the cohort.
- EGFR mutation+: +0.19 mo (p ≈ 6e-11) — small overall benefit.
- BRCA2+: -0.12 mo (p = 0.03) — small adverse effect.
- TMB high: -0.19 mo (p ≈ 1e-17) — counter to the typical IO-prognostic story.
- STK11 mutation: null overall (p = 0.74).
- pdl1_tps continuous: null (β = +0.067, p = 0.16).

Routine chemistries (hemoglobin, alkaline phosphatase, AST, ALT, total
bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, CRP, NLR) were all
non-significant at the univariable level. ldh_u_l was very weakly negative
(β = -5e-4, p = 4e-5).

A multivariable OLS using only clinical features (no treatments) gave R² =
0.889, dominated by stage_iv, ECOG, brain mets, histology, smoking status,
weight loss, and albumin.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Treatment main effects (iter 4)
------------------------------------------------------------

- treatment_sotorasib: +0.32 mo (p ≈ 6e-49) — significant, but driven by the
  KRAS G12C+ subgroup (see below).
- treatment_pembrolizumab: -0.035 mo (p = 0.08) — null/slightly adverse.
- treatment_olaparib: -0.025 mo (p = 0.26) — null.
- treatment_osimertinib: +0.004 mo (p = 0.87) — null.

Adding treatments and their canonical biomarker interactions to the
multivariable model raised R² to 0.927; the only significant treatment term was
sotorasib × KRAS G12C (β = +2.53, p ≈ 0).

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Pre-specified biomarker × treatment interactions (iter 5–10)
------------------------------------------------------------

- Pembro × PD-L1 TPS: interaction p = 0.22; effect in PD-L1 ≥ 0.5 stratum =
  -0.07 mo (p = 0.11). No benefit.
- Pembro × TMB high: interaction p = 0.68; TMB-high stratum -0.05 mo
  (p = 0.20). No benefit.
- Pembro × STK11: interaction p = 0.77; both strata null.
- Sotorasib × KRAS G12C: interaction β = +2.56, p ≈ 0. KRAS G12C+ stratum:
  +2.55 mo (p ≈ 2e-202; n = 2246 treated, 4122 control). KRAS-: -0.005 mo
  (p = 0.80). The entire population-level benefit lives in KRAS G12C+ patients.
- Olaparib × BRCA2: interaction p = 0.78; BRCA2+ stratum +0.010 mo (p = 0.93).
  No benefit even in BRCA2+ patients.
- Osimertinib × EGFR: interaction p = 0.76; EGFR+ stratum -0.014 mo (p = 0.81).
  No benefit even in EGFR+ patients.

Among the four treatments, sotorasib was the only one with any
biomarker-conditioned effect. Olaparib and osimertinib showed no benefit even
in their canonical biomarker-positive subgroups, an unusual but consistent
result across stratified and three-way models.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Systematic heterogeneity scans (iter 13–16)
------------------------------------------------------------

For each treatment I ran pfs_months ~ trt × modifier across 13 candidate
modifiers (sex, ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, EGFR, KRAS G12C, ALK, STK11, BRCA2,
TMB high, PD-L1 high, smoking status, histology). Highlights:

Pembrolizumab: only one nominal interaction, with stage_iv (β = +0.108, p =
0.0068). Within stage_iv = 0, pembro − control = -0.10 mo (p = 0.002); within
stage_iv = 1, +0.006 (p = 0.81). Clinically modest (slight harm in earlier
stage). No biomarker interaction reached significance.

Sotorasib: in addition to KRAS G12C, several modifiers showed strong
interactions:
  - sex_female: β = -0.56, p ≈ 5e-41 (largest non-KRAS modifier)
  - smoking_status (vs current): never β = -0.50, p = 3e-14; former β = -0.21,
    p = 7e-6
  - histology squamous: β = +0.17, p = 2e-4
  - ALK fusion: β = -0.49, p = 4e-7 (but very small subgroup)
  - BRCA2 mutation: β = -0.33, p = 0.006
  - EGFR mutation: β = -0.24, p = 1e-4
  These interactions live almost entirely inside the KRAS G12C+ subgroup
  (where the active effect is).

Olaparib: no interaction reached p < 0.05 (smallest p = 0.073 with STK11). No
heterogeneity detectable.

Osimertinib: two nominal interactions reached p < 0.05 (osi × ALK fusion p =
0.017; osi × BRCA2 p = 0.05), but stratified means in EGFR+ and EGFR− subgroups
remained null. No clinically meaningful heterogeneity.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Targeted multi-feature subgroups (iter 17–24)
------------------------------------------------------------

Pembrolizumab "best biomarker-favorable" compound subgroup
  (pdl1_tps ≥ 0.5 AND tmb_high = 1 AND stk11_mutation = 0):
  +0.06 mo, n = 1373 vs 1341, p = 0.52. Not significant. Within this PD-L1-
  high stratum the 3-way model (pembro × tmb_high × stk11_mutation) gave a
  pembro main β = -0.156 (p = 0.005) and a positive pembro:tmb β = +0.21
  (p = 0.04), consistent with no clinically useful pembro benefit.

Osimertinib × EGFR × brain mets: all four (EGFR, brain mets) cells are null;
3-way interaction p = 0.70.

Sotorasib × KRAS × STK11: KRAS+/STK11 wt +2.59 mo (p ≈ 0); KRAS+/STK11 mut
+2.33 mo (p ≈ 4e-25); 3-way β = -0.27, p = 0.13. STK11 only weakly attenuates
the sotorasib effect within KRAS+; the drug remains highly active in STK11-
mutant KRAS G12C+ patients.

Olaparib × BRCA2 × histology and other BRCA2+ refinements (sex, ECOG, adeno)
all produced null subgroup means, confirming that no sub-stratification of
BRCA2+ recovers an olaparib effect.

The decisive subgroup analysis came from sotorasib × KRAS × sex_female:

  KRAS G12C+, male:    +4.644 mo  (n_treated = 1237, n_control = 2271, p ≈ 0)
  KRAS G12C+, female:  -0.012 mo  (n_treated = 1009, n_control = 1851, p = 0.88)
  3-way interaction soto:kras:sex_female: β = -4.69, p ≈ 0.

That is, the entire population-level sotorasib effect is carried by KRAS G12C+
males. Female sex completely suppresses the effect even in KRAS G12C+ patients.
Further refinement within KRAS G12C+ males (current smoker, squamous histology,
ALK-/BRCA2-) did not change the magnitude meaningfully (e.g., +4.99 mo in the
KRAS+/male/ALK-/BRCA2- subgroup), so male sex appears to be the binding
modifier.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. Final treatment-effect-subgroup hypotheses (iter 25)
------------------------------------------------------------

Pembrolizumab: no subgroup with a clinically meaningful and statistically
  significant PFS benefit. Best-supported negative claim. (Within the canonical
  IO-favorable subgroup PD-L1 ≥ 0.5 AND TMB high AND STK11 wt, pembro effect =
  +0.06 mo, p = 0.52.)

Sotorasib: positive PFS effect of approximately +4.6 months is concentrated in
  patients with kras_g12c = 1 AND sex_female = 0 (male sex). Female sex is
  the unfavorable feature that nullifies the treatment effect within the
  KRAS G12C+ stratum. STK11 mutation only modestly attenuates the effect
  (~+2.3 mo) and is not part of the binding subgroup definition.

Olaparib: no subgroup in this cohort exhibits a significant olaparib benefit
  on PFS, including BRCA2 mutation+ (+0.01 mo, p = 0.93) and any further
  refinement (sex, ECOG, histology, STK11).

Osimertinib: no subgroup exhibits a significant osimertinib benefit on PFS,
  including EGFR mutation+ (-0.01 mo, p = 0.81) and any refinement
  (smoking, histology, stage, brain mets, sex).

------------------------------------------------------------
7. Overall conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------

Across 25 iterations the dataset's structure can be summarized as follows.

(a) Prognostic signal is large and dominated by the expected EHR features:
ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, histology, weight loss, albumin, smoking status. A
clinical-only multivariable model already explains 89% of PFS variance; adding
treatment effects buys only ~4 percentage points more.

(b) Of the four treatments in the cohort, only sotorasib has a real PFS effect,
and that effect is conditional on KRAS G12C+ AND male sex. The sex dependence
is the most striking finding: in KRAS G12C+ males the effect is +4.6 months,
which is large in absolute terms; in KRAS G12C+ females it is essentially zero.
Female sex acts as a complete suppressor of an otherwise dramatic biomarker-
matched response.

(c) Pembrolizumab, olaparib, and osimertinib fail to produce a measurable PFS
benefit even in their canonical biomarker-favorable subgroups (PD-L1 high /
TMB high / STK11 wt for pembro; BRCA2+ for olap; EGFR+ for osi). Heterogeneity
scans, three-way models, and compound subgroups all converge on the same
negative finding for these three. This pattern is unusual relative to clinical
trial data and likely reflects either the way these treatments are assigned
in this synthetic/EHR cohort (e.g., channeling toward sicker patients) or the
specific data-generating process used to create the dataset.

(d) The single most actionable refined hypothesis from this analysis is the
sotorasib × KRAS G12C × sex_female interaction. If interpreted causally it
would suggest that the data-generating process embeds a biological or
prescribing pathway in which the sotorasib benefit operates only in KRAS G12C+
males. Verifying or rejecting this would be the obvious next step.
