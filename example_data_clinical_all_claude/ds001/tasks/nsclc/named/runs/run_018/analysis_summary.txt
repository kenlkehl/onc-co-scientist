NSCLC dataset (ds001_nsclc) — analysis summary
================================================

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patients (50.1% on pembrolizumab, 35.1% on sotorasib,
30.0% on olaparib, 30.0% on osimertinib; treatments are not mutually
exclusive). Outcome examined: pfs_months (continuous, right-skewed; analyses
on the natural scale and on log(pfs+0.1)).

Headline findings
-----------------

1. The single drug with a robust biomarker-defined PFS benefit in this cohort
   is **sotorasib in KRAS G12C+ patients**, and that benefit is essentially
   confined to **MALES**.

2. None of the other "expected" drug-biomarker pairings produced a detectable
   PFS benefit:
     - osimertinib in EGFR mutation+ patients: no effect
     - olaparib in BRCA2 mutation+ patients: no effect
     - pembrolizumab in PD-L1 high / TMB high / STK11 wild-type / smokers: no
       effect

3. Several baseline features have very strong main effects on PFS, dominated
   by age (positive), ECOG performance status (negative), stage IV (negative),
   brain metastases (negative), albumin (positive), weight loss (negative),
   and histology (squamous worse than adenocarcinoma). A multivariable OLS on
   log PFS reaches R^2 = 0.77.

Iteration-by-iteration walkthrough
----------------------------------

Iteration 1 — clinical baseline features
   - ECOG=2 vs ECOG=0: -2.17 mo PFS (p ~ 0); ECOG1 in between. Confirmed.
   - Stage IV: -1.44 mo PFS (p ~ 0). Confirmed.
   - Brain mets: -0.92 mo PFS (p ~ 0). Confirmed.
   - Female sex: -0.20 mo PFS (p = 7.7e-25). Modest but significant.
   All four baseline-feature hypotheses supported.

Iteration 2 — treatment main effects (unstratified)
   - Pembrolizumab: -0.03 mo (p = 0.08). NS.
   - Sotorasib:    +0.32 mo (p = 5.6e-49). Significant; modest in raw means
                   because effect is concentrated in the small KRAS+ subset.
   - Olaparib:     -0.02 mo (p = 0.26). NS.
   - Osimertinib:  +0.00 mo (p = 0.87). NS.
   Only sotorasib's main effect is supported.

Iteration 3 — biomarker main effects on PFS
   - EGFR+: +0.19 mo (p = 6.5e-11). Slight benefit.
   - KRAS G12C+: +0.78 mo (p = 7.4e-94). Driven by sotorasib responders.
   - ALK+: -0.81 mo (p = 5.6e-66). ALK+ patients fare worse; no targeted
     therapy for ALK in this dataset.
   - STK11+: +0.01 mo, NS.
   - BRCA2+: -0.12 mo (p = 0.03). Slight worse.
   - TMB high+: -0.19 mo (p = 1.0e-17). Counter-intuitive direction.

Iteration 4 — continuous lab correlates (Pearson r vs PFS)
   - Albumin:     r = +0.099, p = 2.8e-109. Higher albumin = longer PFS.
   - LDH:         r = -0.018, p = 3.6e-5. Higher LDH = shorter PFS.
   - Weight loss: r = -0.127, p = 7.5e-179. More loss = shorter PFS.
   - CRP:         r =  0.001, NS.
   - NLR:         r =  0.004, NS.
   - PD-L1 TPS:   r = +0.006, NS overall (no monotone signal).
   - Age_years:   r = +0.757, p ~ 0. **Older patients have substantially
                  longer PFS**: quartile means range from 1.33 mo (30-58 y)
                  to 5.69 mo (72-90 y). This is biologically backwards but
                  is a stable, large feature of the dataset.
   - Univariate OLS: log PFS rises by 0.081 per year of age (p ~ 0).

Iteration 5 — smoking, histology, remaining chemistries
   - Never vs current smoker: +0.69 mo PFS for never (p = 3.1e-110).
   - Adenocarcinoma vs squamous: +0.86 mo PFS for adenocarcinoma (p ~ 0).
   - Hemoglobin, AST, ALT, AlkPhos, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium,
     potassium, calcium: no meaningful univariate correlation with PFS.

Iteration 6 — targeted therapy x matched biomarker (canonical)
   - Osimertinib*EGFR interaction (log PFS): coef +0.026, p = 0.43. NS.
     Stratified means: EGFR+ on osi 3.59 vs off 3.60 (NS); EGFR- on osi
     3.41 vs off 3.41 (NS). Osimertinib does not work in this cohort.
   - Sotorasib*KRAS interaction (log PFS): coef +0.632, p = 3.8e-87.
     KRAS+ on sotorasib 5.77 vs off 3.21 (diff +2.55 mo, p = 1.7e-202).
     KRAS- on sotorasib 3.33 vs off 3.33 (NS). Strongest signal in dataset.
   - Olaparib*BRCA2 interaction (log PFS): coef +0.003, p = 0.97. NS.
     BRCA2+ on olaparib 3.32 vs off 3.31 (NS). Olaparib is inert here.

Iteration 7 — pembrolizumab biomarker heterogeneity
   - PD-L1 high subgroup: pembro on 3.41 vs off 3.48 (diff -0.07, p = 0.11).
     Interaction NS (p = 0.54).
   - TMB high: similar, no interaction (p = 0.72).
   - STK11+: no interaction (p = 0.69).
   - Ever-smoker vs never-smoker: no interaction (p = 0.41); the effect of
     pembro is small (~ -0.04 mo) in either smoking class.
   None of the canonical pembrolizumab biomarkers modify the (absent) effect.

Iteration 8 — full multivariable model (OLS on log PFS, R^2 = 0.77)
   Independently associated with longer PFS (p << 0.001):
     age_years (+0.080/yr), albumin (+0.22/g·dL), kras_g12c (+0.22),
     never/former smoking (+0.30 vs current), adeno vs squamous (+0.37).
   Independently associated with shorter PFS:
     ecog_ps (-0.54/level), stage_iv (-0.62), brain_mets (-0.47),
     alk_fusion (-0.17), weight_loss_pct_6mo (-0.037/%),
     ldh (-0.0003/U·L), brca2 (-0.035, p = 0.013), female (-0.05).
   Adjusted treatment coefficients on log PFS:
     sotorasib +0.078 (p = 1e-52) — significant overall;
     pembrolizumab +0.0004 (NS); olaparib -0.009 (NS); osimertinib -0.006 (NS).
   PD-L1 TPS, TMB high, STK11, EGFR mutation, and most chemistries are
   independent of PFS after adjustment.

Iteration 9 — joint pembrolizumab subgroups
   PD-L1 high AND STK11-: pembro 3.45 vs off 3.46, NS (n = 8,469).
   PD-L1 high AND TMB high: NS (n = 3,047).
   PD-L1 high AND TMB high AND STK11-: NS (n = 2,521).
   PD-L1 high AND STK11+: NS.
   No three-way pembro * pdl1_high * stk11 interaction (p = 0.24).
   STK11 does not appear to suppress an underlying pembrolizumab effect
   because there is no underlying effect to suppress.

Iteration 10 — exhaustive treatment * feature interaction screen on log PFS
   Sotorasib top modifiers (lowest p):
     1. kras_g12c           coef +0.632  p = 3.8e-87
     2. sex_female          coef -0.128  p = 2.8e-9
     3. age_years           coef -0.004  p = 1.2e-6
     4. alk_fusion          coef -0.151  p = 0.002
     5. albumin_g_dl        coef -0.056  p = 0.008
   Pembrolizumab top modifiers — none reach a clinically meaningful
     interaction; the smallest p (weight_loss_pct_6mo, coef +0.009,
     p = 7e-4) is quantitatively trivial.
   Olaparib top modifiers — bun_mg_dl (+0.006, p = 0.006), age_years
     (+0.002, p = 0.01); no large or biomarker-driven modifier.
   Osimertinib top modifiers — alk_fusion (+0.111, p = 0.03), but no
     interaction with EGFR.

Iteration 11 — CART-based subgroup discovery (RF-imputed counterfactual)
   Range of estimated leaf-level treatment effects (months PFS):
     - sotorasib  : -0.46  to  +4.59  (max leaf n = 1,037; clear KRAS+ signal)
     - pembro     : -0.62  to  +0.73  (no large positive subgroup)
     - olaparib   : -0.54  to  +0.83  (no large positive subgroup)
     - osimertinib: -0.76  to  +0.62  (no large positive subgroup)
   Tree-based discovery agrees: sotorasib is the only treatment with a
   strongly heterogeneous effect.

Iteration 12 — refining the sotorasib subgroup within KRAS+
   Joint OLS on log PFS within KRAS+ (n = 6,368):
     sotorasib*sex_female:    coef -1.18  p = 2.3e-220
     sotorasib*age_years:     coef -0.025 p = 1.2e-45
     sotorasib*ecog_ps:       coef +0.21  p = 7.3e-16
     sotorasib*has_brain_mets:coef +0.12  p = 3.0e-3
     sotorasib*albumin_g_dl:  coef -0.14  p = 5.4e-5
     sotorasib*stk11_mutation:coef +0.012 p = 0.82  (NS)
   Stratified within KRAS+:
     Sex_female=0 (males):  on=7.88, off=3.23, diff = +4.64 mo (p ~ 0).
     Sex_female=1 (females): on=3.18, off=3.19, diff = -0.01 mo (p = 0.88).
   Sotorasib's KRAS+ benefit is overwhelmingly concentrated in males.
   STK11 status does NOT modify sotorasib's effect within KRAS+.

Iteration 13 — confirmatory triple interaction and exhaustive male strata
   Three-way sotorasib*kras_g12c*sex_female on log PFS (full cohort):
     coef -1.23  p = 3.6e-83.
   Within KRAS+ males, sotorasib gives a uniformly large benefit:
     ECOG=0: +4.57 mo (p = 2e-137)
     ECOG=1: +4.77 mo (p = 9e-216)
     ECOG=2: +4.53 mo (p = 9e-70)
     no brain mets: +4.70 mo (p ~ 0)
     brain mets+:   +4.47 mo (p = 1e-97)
     STK11=0: +4.67 mo (p ~ 0)
     STK11=1: +4.49 mo (p = 4e-51)
     age tertile 0: +4.17 mo
     age tertile 1: +4.55 mo
     age tertile 2: +4.79 mo
   Within KRAS+ females, sotorasib effect is null in every ECOG stratum
   (effects between -0.07 and +0.03 mo, all p > 0.5).

Iteration 14 — final best-supported subgroup hypotheses
   Sotorasib (FINAL): treatment_sotorasib improves PFS by ~4.6 months in the
     subgroup defined by kras_g12c=1 AND sex_female=0; outside this subgroup
     (KRAS+ females, or KRAS-) the effect is essentially zero. The key
     subgroup predicates are KRAS G12C status AND sex; neither STK11 status
     nor ECOG nor brain mets meaningfully suppresses the effect inside the
     KRAS+ male subgroup.
   Pembrolizumab (FINAL): no biomarker-defined subgroup with a positive PFS
     effect was identified. PD-L1 high, TMB high, STK11 wild-type, ever-
     smoker, joint combinations of these, and CART-based search all return
     null or trivially small estimates. Adjusted log-PFS effect = 0.0004
     (p = 0.94).
   Olaparib (FINAL): no PFS benefit even in BRCA2+ (interaction p = 0.97).
     CART search yields no large positive subgroup.
   Osimertinib (FINAL): no PFS benefit even in EGFR+ (interaction p = 0.43,
     stratified diff -0.01 mo). Splitting EGFR+ by smoking, brain mets,
     stage, or ECOG does not reveal a responder subgroup.

Overall conclusions
-------------------

This cohort behaves as one in which the only working drug-biomarker pairing
is sotorasib + KRAS G12C, and even that effect is sex-restricted. The
canonical NSCLC pairings (osimertinib/EGFR, pembrolizumab/PD-L1 or TMB,
olaparib/BRCA2) are all absent or null. STK11 — typically an immunotherapy
resistance biomarker — has no impact on pembrolizumab efficacy here because
pembrolizumab itself has no efficacy.

The most important non-treatment determinants of PFS are age (positive),
ECOG performance status, stage IV, brain metastases, albumin, weight loss,
smoking history, and histology. Together, baseline features explain most of
the variance in log PFS (R^2 = 0.77 in a 34-variable OLS).

The single most surprising finding is the sex-by-sotorasib interaction
within KRAS G12C+ patients: sotorasib produces a ~4.6-month PFS gain in
KRAS+ males and zero benefit in KRAS+ females, with the three-way
interaction extremely strong (p ~ 1e-83). This pattern is robust across
ECOG, brain metastases, STK11, and age strata, and is the final
subgroup-level hypothesis carried forward for sotorasib.
