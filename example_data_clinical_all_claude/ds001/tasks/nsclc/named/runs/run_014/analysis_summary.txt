Analysis Summary — ds001_nsclc (n=50,000)
=========================================

Outcome: pfs_months (continuous; mean 3.43, SD 2.23, range 0–15.3).
Treatments examined: treatment_pembrolizumab (n=25,008), treatment_sotorasib
(n=17,544), treatment_olaparib (n=14,999), treatment_osimertinib (n=14,976).
Treatments are assigned essentially independently of one another in this
cohort, so each can be analyzed separately against its complement.

Across 25 iterations the analysis proposed 103 hypotheses and ran 103
statistical tests. The headline findings are organized below by clinical
question.

------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Patient-feature main effects on pfs_months (iterations 1–3, 9)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Univariate tests and a single OLS adjusting for all listed features showed
the following independent associations with pfs_months. Coefficients are
multivariable (sign and approximate magnitude in months unless noted):

  Strongly NEGATIVE:
    ecog_ge2 (ECOG ≥2)              −1.50  (p≈0)
    stage_iv                        −1.41  (p≈0)
    has_brain_mets                  −0.91  (p≈0)
    histology = squamous            −0.72  (p≈0)
    sex_female                      −0.21  (p<1e-25)
    weight_loss_pct_6mo             −0.073/% (p≈0)
    ldh_u_l                         −4e-4/U (p<1e-17)
    brca2_mutation                  −0.10  (p<1e-4)

  Strongly POSITIVE:
    age_years                       +0.169/yr (p≈0; r=+0.76 univariate —
                                     a striking, non-clinical pattern in
                                     this dataset where older patients
                                     have longer PFS)
    kras_g12c                       +0.89  (p≈0; this large positive
                                     effect is driven by the targeted
                                     benefit from sotorasib, see §3)
    smoking former vs current       +0.54
    smoking never vs current        +0.53
    albumin_g_dl                    +0.46/g/dL (p≈0)

  NULL or minimal:
    pdl1_tps, tmb_high, stk11_mutation, alk_fusion (after adjustment),
    egfr_mutation, crp_mg_l, nlr, hemoglobin, alkaline phosphatase, AST,
    ALT, total bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium.

------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Treatment main effects (iteration 1, multivariable iteration 9)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Only one treatment shows a positive main effect on pfs_months:

    treatment_sotorasib       +0.32 mo  (p=5.6e-49, multivariable +0.33)
    treatment_pembrolizumab   −0.03 mo  (p=0.08; effectively null/slight neg)
    treatment_olaparib        −0.02 mo  (p=0.26; null)
    treatment_osimertinib     +0.00 mo  (p=0.87; null)

These are surprising results for a real-world NSCLC cohort because
pembrolizumab, olaparib, and osimertinib are biologically targeted agents
with established benefits. The interaction analyses below attempt to find
the responder subgroups.

------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Treatment-by-biomarker interactions (iterations 4–8, 10–14)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Each treatment was tested against its expected biomarker.

  • treatment_sotorasib × kras_g12c:   interaction +2.56 mo (p≈0).
        kras_g12c=1: sotorasib effect = +2.55 mo (p=1.7e-202; n=2,246 on,
                     4,122 off; mean PFS 5.7 vs 3.1 mo).
        kras_g12c=0: sotorasib effect = −0.005 mo (p=0.80).
        => CONFIRMED. This is the only true responder population for
        sotorasib.

  • treatment_osimertinib × egfr_mutation:  interaction −0.020 (p=0.76).
        egfr_mutation=1: osimertinib effect = −0.014 mo (p=0.81).
        egfr_mutation=0: osimertinib effect = +0.006 mo (p=0.81).
        => REFUTED. No benefit even in EGFR-mutant patients.

  • treatment_olaparib × brca2_mutation:    interaction +0.04 (p=0.78).
        brca2_mutation=1: olaparib effect = +0.010 mo (p=0.93).
        brca2_mutation=0: olaparib effect = −0.026 mo (p=0.24).
        => REFUTED. Not effective even in BRCA2-mutated patients.

  • treatment_pembrolizumab × pdl1_high:    interaction −0.045 (p=0.36).
        pdl1_high=1: pembrolizumab effect = −0.07 mo (p=0.11).
        pdl1_high=0: pembrolizumab effect = −0.025 mo (p=0.26).
        => REFUTED. Not effective even in PD-L1-high patients.

  • treatment_pembrolizumab × tmb_high:     interaction −0.018 (p=0.69).
        => REFUTED.

  • treatment_pembrolizumab × stk11_mutation, smoke_never, kras_g12c:
        all null or weakly negative; no positive responder pattern.

------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Heterogeneity within sotorasib responders (iterations 12, 15, 19, 24)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Within kras_g12c=1 patients, additional features dramatically modify the
sotorasib effect:

  Within kras_g12c=1, sotorasib × sex_female interaction = −4.66 (p≈0):
        kras+ MALE   (sex_female=0): sotorasib effect = +4.64 mo (p≈0;
                     n=1,237 on, 2,271 off; mean 7.88 vs 3.23 mo).
        kras+ FEMALE (sex_female=1): sotorasib effect = −0.012 mo (p=0.88;
                     n=1,009 on, 1,851 off).

  Within kras_g12c=1 males, alk_fusion=1 abolishes the benefit:
        kras+ M alk-: +4.83 mo (p≈0)
        kras+ M alk+: −0.29 mo (p=0.50)

  Within kras_g12c=1 males, brca2_mutation=1 abolishes the benefit:
        kras+ M brca2−: ≈ +5.0 mo
        kras+ M brca2+: −0.07 mo (p=0.89)

  COMBINED FINAL RESPONDER DEFINITION:
        kras_g12c=1 AND sex_female=0 AND alk_fusion=0 AND brca2_mutation=0
        n = 1,154 on / 2,112 off
        Mean pfs_months on sotorasib = 8.23  vs  off = 3.25
        Effect estimate = +4.99 mo, p ≈ 0.

  A T-learner heterogeneity tree (Random Forest first stage, depth-4
  decision tree on individual treatment effects) recovered the same
  pattern: every leaf with predicted ITE > +4 mo required kras_g12c=1
  AND sex_female=0; outside that branch, predicted ITE was near 0.

  Outside this responder definition the sotorasib effect is consistent
  with zero (kras_g12c=1 & sex_female=1 & alk_fusion=0: +0.002 mo,
  p=0.98).

------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Systematic 2- and 3-way subgroup searches (iterations 16–18, 20–22)
------------------------------------------------------------------
For each of the three "null" treatments, exhaustive 2-way and 3-way
binary subgroup searches were performed (≈22 candidate features × all
pairs/triples × both values) with minimum n=200 in the subgroup and
n≥30 on each treatment arm.

  treatment_pembrolizumab:
        No subgroup with effect ≥ +0.2 mo at p < 0.05 of credible size.
        The largest "positive" 3-way subgroup was egfr_mutation=1 &
        ecog_0=1 & has_brain_mets=1 (+0.43 mo, p=0.01, n=305/305) — a
        small slice that does not survive standard adjustment for
        multiple testing among thousands of candidate subgroups.
        Canonical responder slices (egfr−/alk−/pdl1+, pdl1≥0.6, etc.)
        all show point estimates ≤ 0.

  treatment_olaparib:
        No replicable positive subgroup. The most-significant signals
        were small (e.g., age_old & albumin_low subgroups with
        eff +0.2–0.3 mo at p≈0.003–0.01) but no biologically motivated
        biomarker-defined subgroup (incl. brca2_mutation, stk11) showed
        a meaningful effect. stk11+ & egfr+ → +0.33 mo (p=0.03, n=282/698)
        is the only nominally significant biomarker-defined subgroup but
        is one among many tested.

  treatment_osimertinib:
        Strongest signal is in alk_fusion+ patients (+0.23 mo, p=0.02
        univariate; +0.36–0.71 mo across narrower 3-way slices). However,
        this is biologically anomalous (osimertinib is an EGFR TKI, not
        an ALK inhibitor) and may reflect chance among many tested
        subgroups; no effect is seen in EGFR-mutant patients themselves.

------------------------------------------------------------------
6. Conclusions and final treatment-effect subgroup hypotheses
------------------------------------------------------------------
1) The primary, robust, replicable treatment effect in this dataset is
   the sotorasib benefit confined to KRAS G12C–positive males without
   alk_fusion or brca2_mutation:
        FINAL HYPOTHESIS:
        treatment_sotorasib improves pfs_months by ~+5 months in patients
        with kras_g12c=1 AND sex_female=0 AND alk_fusion=0 AND
        brca2_mutation=0. Outside this subgroup the effect is null.

2) Pembrolizumab, olaparib, and osimertinib show NO clinically meaningful
   PFS benefit in this cohort overall, and (notably) no benefit in their
   biologically expected biomarker subgroups (PD-L1 high or TMB high for
   pembrolizumab; BRCA2 mutation for olaparib; EGFR mutation for
   osimertinib). The dataset therefore does not support the canonical
   biomarker-treatment matches for these three drugs, although it does
   support the canonical KRAS G12C → sotorasib relationship — but only
   in male patients.

3) Independent prognostic factors for shorter pfs_months are advanced
   stage (stage_iv), poor performance status (ecog_ge2), brain
   metastases, squamous histology, current smoking, weight loss in the
   prior 6 months, lower albumin, and elevated LDH. Older age was
   strongly correlated with LONGER PFS, an unusual real-world pattern
   that may reflect the synthetic nature of the data.

4) Notable subgroup heterogeneity: the sotorasib benefit is sex-specific
   (males only) within KRAS G12C+ patients, and is suppressed when
   alk_fusion or brca2_mutation co-occur. This is the strongest
   treatment-effect heterogeneity finding in the dataset.
