ds001_nsclc — Analysis summary
===============================

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patients, single PFS outcome (pfs_months, mean 3.43,
median 3.32, range 0–15.3 months). Four treatments are coded as binary
indicators (treatment_pembrolizumab, treatment_sotorasib, treatment_olaparib,
treatment_osimertinib); patients can receive multiple treatments (max 4
recorded; 19,090 patients on a single treatment, 16,344 on two, 7,903 on
none). All baseline features are complete.

Iterations 1–6 — Prognostic main effects on pfs_months
-------------------------------------------------------
The dataset contains very strong, clinically coherent baseline prognostic
signals. Univariate OLS effect estimates (pfs_months per unit, signed):

  ECOG 1 vs 0:            -1.14 months   p ≈ 0
  ECOG 2 vs 0:            -2.17 months   p ≈ 0
  stage_iv (1 vs 0):      -1.44 months   p ≈ 0
  has_brain_mets:         -0.92 months   p ≈ 0
  albumin_g_dl:           +0.44 / g/dL   p = 2.8e-109
  weight_loss_pct_6mo:    -0.073 / %     p = 7.5e-179
  ldh_u_l:                -0.00051 / U/L p = 3.6e-5
  histology squamous:     -0.86          p ≈ 0
  smoking current vs never:-0.69         p = 7.4e-106
  smoking former vs never: -0.07         p = 0.019
  age_years:              +0.170 / yr    p ≈ 0   (counter-intuitive sign)
  sex_female:             -0.20          p = 2.4e-24 (counter-intuitive)
  alk_fusion (1 vs 0):    -0.81          p = 2.9e-69
  brca2_mutation:         -0.12          p = 0.041

Two univariate findings deserve highlighting because they run opposite to
typical clinical intuition: older age is associated with LONGER PFS
(robust to adjustment, joint-model coef +0.168/year, t≈614), and female
sex is associated with SHORTER PFS. CRP, NLR, and routine chemistries
(hemoglobin, AlkP, AST, ALT, total bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium,
potassium, calcium) have null univariate effects. tmb_high has a
univariate negative effect on PFS (-0.19, p = 9e-18) but this is fully
explained by confounding (adjusted coef -0.006, p = 0.46).

Iterations 7–8 — Treatment main effects
----------------------------------------
Unadjusted PFS difference (treated − untreated):

  treatment_pembrolizumab:  -0.035  p = 0.081  (NS, slightly negative)
  treatment_sotorasib:      +0.322  p = 1.6e-53 (positive overall)
  treatment_olaparib:       -0.025  p = 0.255  (null)
  treatment_osimertinib:    +0.004  p = 0.870  (null)

After adjustment for the full prognostic covariate set, the pembrolizumab,
olaparib, and osimertinib effects remain null (|coef|<0.01, all p > 0.37);
sotorasib retains a +0.32 month adjusted effect (p ≈ 0). The sotorasib
"main effect" is the marginalization of a strong predictive subgroup
effect (see iterations 11–13).

Iterations 9–14 — Targeted treatment-by-biomarker interactions
---------------------------------------------------------------
For each treatment we tested its canonical clinical biomarker. Only one of
the four canonical pairs replicates the expected biology in this dataset.

  treatment_sotorasib × kras_g12c:
       interaction +2.558, p ≈ 0; subgroup difference within
       kras_g12c=1: +2.55 months (p = 1.7e-202, n_on=2246, n_off=4122);
       within kras_g12c=0: -0.005 months (p = 0.80, clean null).
       This is a textbook predictive biomarker effect.

  treatment_pembrolizumab × pdl1_tps (continuous):
       interaction -0.116, p = 0.22 (NS, sign opposite to expectation).
  treatment_pembrolizumab × tmb_high:
       interaction -0.018, p = 0.69 (NS).
  treatment_pembrolizumab × stk11_mutation:
       interaction -0.016, p = 0.77 (NS).
  treatment_pembrolizumab × egfr_mutation:
       interaction +0.099, p = 0.10 (NS, and sign opposite to clinical
       expectation that EGFR-mutant disease responds less to pembro).

  treatment_osimertinib × egfr_mutation:
       interaction -0.020, p = 0.76 (NS). No osimertinib benefit
       within egfr_mutation=1 (subgroup diff -0.014, p = 0.81; n_on=1973).

  treatment_olaparib × brca2_mutation:
       interaction +0.037, p = 0.78 (NS). No olaparib benefit
       within brca2_mutation=1 (subgroup diff +0.010, p = 0.93; n_on=417).

The few nominally "significant" pembrolizumab subgroup hits all point in

Iterations 17 — Exhaustive treatment × feature interaction screen
------------------------------------------------------------------
Each treatment was crossed with all 26 features in a separate two-way
interaction OLS model with full prognostic adjustment.

  treatment_pembrolizumab: no adjusted interaction reaches p<0.005;
    strongest hit pembro×kras_g12c (coef -0.064, p = 0.0065) is small
    in magnitude and unsupported by stratified means.

  treatment_sotorasib: dominated entirely by kras_g12c (coef +2.51,
    p ≈ 0). Apparent secondary "interactions" with sex (p = 2e-256),
    alk_fusion, egfr_mutation, brca2_mutation are confounded by
    differential KRAS G12C prevalence — not independent signals.

  treatment_olaparib: no interaction significant (smallest p ≈ 0.06,
    sodium / AST / alkaline phosphatase). brca2_mutation interaction
    p = 0.78 — definitely not a top hit, refuting the clinical
    expectation in this dataset.

  treatment_osimertinib: no interaction reaches p<0.02; egfr_mutation
    interaction p = 0.76, again refuting clinical expectation.

Iterations 18–22 — Subgroup refinements
----------------------------------------
Sotorasib (KRAS G12C+ subgroup): the +2.5 month benefit is robust across
ECOG strata (ECOG 0 +2.47 months p=5.5e-70; ECOG 2 +2.49 p=2.8e-38),
across STK11 status (STK11- +2.59, STK11+ +2.33), and across albumin
strata (albumin<3.5 +2.95, p=3.2e-67). The sotorasib×kras_g12c×stk11
triple interaction is NS (p = 0.13). The simplest description of the
predictive subgroup is therefore: kras_g12c=1.

Osimertinib: tested in EGFR+ adenocarcinoma never-smokers (the
classical clinical phenotype for EGFR-targeted therapy benefit;
n_on=1114, n_off=2507): diff -0.043, p = 0.57. Tested in EGFR+ no
brain mets / EGFR+ never smoker / EGFR+ ECOG 0 — all null. No
subgroup of egfr_mutation=1 patients yields a positive osimertinib
effect.

Olaparib: tested in BRCA2+ no other driver (n=1272): diff +0.078,
p = 0.57. Tested in BRCA2 × ECOG / × PDL1 — all null. No subgroup of
brca2_mutation=1 patients yields a positive olaparib effect.

Pembrolizumab: tested in (pdl1>=0.5 & tmb_high=1 & stk11=0 & egfr=0)
— the clinically-most-favorable pembrolizumab subgroup constructible
from these features (n_on=1284, n_off=1249): diff +0.049, p = 0.59.
No detectable pembrolizumab benefit subgroup.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
Single OLS containing every treatment × canonical-biomarker interaction
plus the full prognostic adjustment set:

  treatment_sotorasib:kras_g12c:    coef +2.533  p ≈ 0    SIGNIFICANT
  treatment_pembrolizumab:pdl1_tps: coef -0.002  p = 0.94 NS
  treatment_pembrolizumab:stk11:    coef -0.002  p = 0.88 NS
  treatment_pembrolizumab:tmb_high: coef -0.000  p = 0.98 NS
  treatment_pembrolizumab:egfr:     coef +0.007  p = 0.68 NS
  treatment_osimertinib:egfr:       coef -0.021  p = 0.23 NS
  treatment_olaparib:brca2:         coef -0.032  p = 0.37 NS

Joint model R² = 0.927 — almost all explainable PFS variance is captured
by baseline prognostic features (especially ECOG, stage, brain mets,
albumin, weight loss, age, smoking, histology) plus the single
treatment_sotorasib × kras_g12c term.

Iterations 24–25 — Final treatment-effect subgroup hypotheses
--------------------------------------------------------------
1) SOTORASIB. Predictive subgroup: kras_g12c=1. Direction: positive.
   Adjusted within-stratum effect ≈ +2.53 months (p ≈ 0), unrestricted
   by STK11, ECOG, sex, smoking, or histology. Within kras_g12c=0 the
   effect is null (+0.001 month, p = 0.66). No additional refinement
   improves the subgroup definition.

2) PEMBROLIZUMAB. No detectable PFS-improving subgroup is supported by
   the data. Effect is null overall (adjusted coef -0.006, p = 0.37) and
   in every clinically-motivated subgroup including (pdl1>=0.5 & tmb_high=1
   & stk11=0 & egfr=0). The few nominally significant subgroup p-values
   point toward slight HARM and disappear after adjustment.

3) OSIMERTINIB. No detectable PFS-improving subgroup. Effect is null
   overall (adjusted coef -0.002, p = 0.83) AND in egfr_mutation=1
   (adjusted coef +0.011, p = 0.43), even when further restricted to
   adenocarcinoma never-smokers without brain metastases. This is
   contrary to the clinical reality of EGFR-mutant NSCLC and indicates
   that the data-generating process did not encode the osimertinib–EGFR
   predictive relationship.

4) OLAPARIB. No detectable PFS-improving subgroup. Effect is null
   overall (adjusted coef +0.003, p = 0.72) AND in brca2_mutation=1
   (adjusted coef +0.005, p = 0.50), even in BRCA2+ patients without
   competing oncogenic drivers. Again contrary to the expected biology.

Overall conclusion
------------------
The dataset's PFS variance is dominated by classical NSCLC prognostic
factors — ECOG, stage IV, brain metastases, albumin, weight loss,
smoking, histology, alk_fusion — together with two non-clinical sign
inversions (older age and female sex are associated with LONGER and
SHORTER PFS respectively, holding after adjustment).

Among the four treatments, only treatment_sotorasib has a predictive
biomarker that "works": kras_g12c=1 patients gain ~2.5 months of
adjusted PFS on sotorasib, with no benefit (and no harm) in
kras_g12c=0. The other three drugs — pembrolizumab, osimertinib,
olaparib — are statistically inert in this cohort, both overall and in
every clinically-motivated subgroup, including the subgroups of
patients in which they are clinically standard of care (PDL1-high
NSCLC, EGFR-mutant NSCLC, BRCA2-mutant NSCLC). This pattern, combined
with the inverted age and sex prognostic signs, strongly suggests that
the synthetic data-generating process implemented one canonical
treatment–biomarker pair (sotorasib–KRAS G12C) and left the remaining
three pairs as nulls. Any inference applied to real-world treatment
selection from this dataset alone would over-represent the sotorasib
finding and miss the standard EGFR-targeted, BRCA2-targeted, and
PD-L1-driven response patterns that exist in clinical practice.
