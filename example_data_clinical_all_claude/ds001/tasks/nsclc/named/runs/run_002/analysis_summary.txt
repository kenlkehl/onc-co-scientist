Dataset: ds001_nsclc (50,000 patient EHR-derived NSCLC cohort)
Outcome analyzed: pfs_months (continuous, range 0–15.3, mean 3.43, SD 2.23)
Iterations: 25 propose-test-refine cycles, 73 hypotheses, 68 analyses.

==============================================================================
1. HEADLINE FINDING
==============================================================================
The sole clinically meaningful targeted-treatment effect in this cohort is
treatment_sotorasib in KRAS G12C-positive males without ALK fusion or BRCA2
mutation. In that subgroup (kras_g12c=1, sex_female=0, alk_fusion=0,
brca2_mutation=0; n=3,266) sotorasib-treated patients had a mean PFS of
8.234 months vs 3.249 months in untreated patients — a gain of +4.985 months
(p ≈ 0; the largest treatment effect anywhere in the dataset).

The other three treatments (pembrolizumab, olaparib, osimertinib) showed no
clinically meaningful PFS benefit overall and no clinically meaningful
benefit in any subgroup we identified, including the canonical biomarker-
positive subgroups (PD-L1-high adeno, BRCA2-mutant, EGFR-mutant).

==============================================================================
2. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS (iteration 1)
==============================================================================
- treatment_pembrolizumab:    diff = -0.035 mo (p = 0.081)   no benefit
- treatment_sotorasib:        diff = +0.322 mo (p = 1.6e-53) marginal but
                                                              entirely driven
                                                              by G12C+ males
- treatment_olaparib:         diff = -0.025 mo (p = 0.255)   no benefit
- treatment_osimertinib:      diff = +0.004 mo (p = 0.870)   no benefit

Treatments are essentially randomly assigned with respect to biomarkers
(e.g., 35% of KRAS G12C+ vs 35% of G12C- patients receive sotorasib),
allowing clean treatment-effect estimation.

3. PROGNOSTIC MAIN EFFECTS (iterations 2, 8–10)
==============================================================================
Strong negative prognostic markers (lower PFS):
  ecog_ps           -1.10 mo per unit (p ≈ 0)
  stage_iv=1        -1.44 mo (p ≈ 0)
  has_brain_mets=1  -0.92 mo (p ≈ 0)
  histology=squamous -0.86 mo (p ≈ 0)
  weight_loss_pct_6mo -0.073 mo per pct (p ≈ 0)
  smoking_status=current vs former/never -0.55 mo (p ≈ 0)
  sex_female=1      -0.20 mo (p = 2.4e-24, but see §6)
  alk_fusion=1      -0.81 mo (p = 2.9e-69)
  ldh_u_l           -5e-4 mo per U/L (p = 3.6e-5)
  brca2_mutation=1  -0.12 mo (p = 0.04)

Strong positive prognostic markers (higher PFS):
  age_years         +0.17 mo per year (p ≈ 0; UNUSUAL DIRECTION — older →
                    higher PFS in this synthetic cohort, sustained after
                    multivariable adjustment)
  albumin_g_dl      +0.44 mo per g/dL (p = 2.8e-109)
  smoking_status=former or never +0.55 mo vs current (p ≈ 0)
  kras_g12c=1       +0.78 mo univariate, but turns NEGATIVE (-0.14 mo,
                    p=6e-6) after adjusting for sotorasib treatment effects
                    (iteration 22).

Null / non-prognostic features (univariate or after adjustment):
  pdl1_tps (continuous), tmb_high, stk11_mutation, crp_mg_l, nlr,
  hemoglobin_g_dl, AST, ALT, total_bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium,
  potassium, calcium, alkaline_phosphatase. The TMB-high "negative" effect
  in raw data (-0.19 mo, p<1e-17) is fully explained away by other features
  in joint adjustment (p=0.40; iteration 23).

==============================================================================
5. SOTORASIB SUBGROUP REFINEMENT (iterations 5–7, 15, 20)
==============================================================================
A 3-way interaction model showed sex strongly modifies the sotorasib×G12C
effect:
  treatment_sotorasib:kras_g12c                = +4.663 (p ≈ 0)
  treatment_sotorasib:kras_g12c:sex_female      = -4.688 (p ≈ 0)

Stratified within KRAS G12C+:
  Males   (n=3,508): sotorasib diff = +4.644 mo (p ≈ 0)
  Females (n=2,860): sotorasib diff = -0.012 mo (p = 0.88)  ZERO benefit.

Within G12C+ males, the benefit is fully suppressed by either of two rare
genomic co-alterations:
  alk_fusion=1     (n=138): sotorasib diff = -0.285 mo (p = 0.51)
  brca2_mutation=1 (n=107): sotorasib diff = -0.068 mo (p = 0.88)

Joint multivariable interaction model with all suppressors:
  treatment_sotorasib:kras_g12c                = +4.65   (p ≈ 0)
  treatment_sotorasib:kras_g12c:sex_female      = -4.68   (p ≈ 0)
  treatment_sotorasib:alk_fusion                = -1.05   (p = 2.2e-45)
  treatment_sotorasib:brca2_mutation            = -0.35   (p = 1.8e-4)

Best-supported sotorasib-responsive subgroup:
  kras_g12c=1 AND sex_female=0 AND alk_fusion=0 AND brca2_mutation=0
  n = 3,266 (1,154 treated, 2,112 untreated)
  PFS on sotorasib   = 8.234 mo
  PFS off sotorasib  = 3.249 mo
  Effect             = +4.985 mo (p ≈ 0)
The sotorasib effect is broadly additive across ECOG, stage, smoking,
histology, brain mets, STK11, TMB, and continuous lab features within this
subgroup (every stratum gives diff ≈ +4.5–4.8 mo).

Within KRAS G12C+ females, NO continuous lab or clinical feature recovered
any sotorasib benefit (all interaction p > 0.10; iteration 15).

==============================================================================
6. WHY DO FEMALES HAVE LOWER PFS? (iteration 24)
==============================================================================
The univariate sex_female effect (-0.20 mo, p<1e-23) is concentrated almost
entirely in the sotorasib-treated KRAS G12C+ stratum:
  G12C+, sotorasib-treated:    male 7.857 vs female 3.211 (gap = 4.65 mo)
  G12C+, sotorasib-untreated:  male 3.213 vs female 3.223 (gap ≈ 0)
  G12C-:                       gap ≈ 0.08 mo across treatment
The "female disadvantage" in PFS is therefore overwhelmingly explained by
the absence of sotorasib response in G12C+ females. This is the single most
striking sex-by-treatment heterogeneity we found.

==============================================================================
7. PEMBROLIZUMAB / OLAPARIB / OSIMERTINIB HETEROGENEITY SEARCHES
   (iterations 11–14, 17–18)
==============================================================================
Each treatment was screened for heterogeneity using:
  (a) interaction OLS over ~30 candidate moderators (all clinical/biomarker/
      continuous lab features)
  (b) exhaustive 2-feature binary subgroup search (~360 candidate subgroups
      per treatment)
  (c) targeted refinements of any nominal signal

Pembrolizumab (iterations 11–12, 17–18):
  - pembro × stage_iv:                interaction +0.108 mo (p = 0.007)
    stage_iv=0: pembro diff = -0.102 mo (p=0.002); stage_iv=1: +0.006 (NS)
    Direction implies harm in non-stage-IV; effect size <0.12 mo.
  - pembro × weight_loss_pct_6mo:     interaction +0.0184 (p = 3.2e-4)
    Low-weight-loss tertile: diff -0.111 mo (p=0.001); high tertile: +0.04 (NS)
  - pembro × pdl1_tps:                p = 0.22 (no interaction)
  - pembro × tmb_high:                p = 0.68 (no interaction)
  - 3-way pembro × pdl1_tps × tmb_high: p = 0.09 (suggestive only)
  - PD-L1-high adenocarcinoma (n=7,756): pembro diff = -0.079 mo (p=0.12)
  - Adeno + never-smoker (n=7,075):    pembro × pdl1 interaction p = 0.56
  Conclusion: NO clinically meaningful pembrolizumab subgroup. Nominal
  signals are <0.12 mo and consistent with multiple-testing noise.

Olaparib (iterations 13, 17–18):
  - olaparib × crp_mg_l: interaction +0.0055 (p=0.022) — tiny, likely noise
  - olaparib × bun_mg_dl: interaction +0.011 (p=0.014) — small, inconsistent
  - olaparib × brca2_mutation: p = 0.78
  - olaparib in BRCA2+: diff = -0.022 mo (p=0.85)
  Conclusion: NO olaparib-responsive subgroup; specifically, BRCA2 status
  does not identify a responder population.

Osimertinib (iterations 14, 17–18):
  - osimertinib × egfr_mutation: p = 0.76
  - osimertinib × alk_fusion:    interaction +0.238 (p=0.017, n=2464)
  - osimertinib × brca2_mutation: interaction -0.254 (p=0.05, n=1519)
  - osimertinib in EGFR+: diff = -0.027 mo (p=0.74)
  Conclusion: NO osimertinib-responsive subgroup; specifically, EGFR
  mutation does not identify a responder population. The nominal ALK
  interaction (off-target signal) is small and not robust to multiple
  testing.

The maximum within-subgroup effect for any non-sotorasib treatment in any
2-feature stratum was 0.22 mo (osimertinib in alk_fusion=1; p=0.06). This
is a 20-fold smaller effect than the sotorasib finding, and nothing
survives multiple-testing correction.

9. HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED vs. REFUTED
==============================================================================
SUPPORTED (representative):
  - Treatment_sotorasib × kras_g12c interaction (h12)
  - Sotorasib benefit confined to G12C+ (h17)
  - No sotorasib effect in G12C- (h18)
  - Sex modifies sotorasib×G12C; benefit is in males (h19, h20, h21)
  - ALK fusion fully suppresses sotorasib benefit in G12C+ males (h22)
  - BRCA2 mutation fully suppresses sotorasib benefit in G12C+ males (h23)
  - The full responder subgroup (kras_g12c=1 ∧ sex_female=0 ∧ alk_fusion=0
    ∧ brca2_mutation=0) gives ~+5 mo PFS (h24, h66, h73a)
  - Standard prognostic factors (ECOG, stage, brain mets, histology,
    smoking, albumin, weight loss, LDH) (h27–h34, h38)
  - KRAS G12C is itself a NEGATIVE prognostic marker conditional on
    sotorasib treatment (h69)
  - Female sex disadvantage is driven by lack of sotorasib response in
    G12C+ females (h72)

REFUTED:
  - Pembrolizumab benefits high-PD-L1 patients overall (h13)
  - Pembrolizumab benefits TMB-high patients (h16)
  - Pembrolizumab × PD-L1 in adenocarcinoma (h44, h45)
  - Pembrolizumab × PD-L1 in adeno + never-smokers (h46)
  - Olaparib benefits BRCA2-mutant patients (h14, h49)
  - Osimertinib benefits EGFR-mutant patients (h15, h51)
  - PD-L1 is independently prognostic after adjustment (h39)
  - TMB-high is independently prognostic after adjustment (h70)
  - Older age is associated with WORSE PFS (h37 — direction REVERSED in
    this dataset)
  - BRCA2 mutation is associated with HIGHER PFS (h10 — direction reversed)
  - STK11 mutation is associated with lower PFS (h11 — null)

UNSUPPORTED OR EQUIVOCAL:
  - h41/h42 pembro × stage_iv / × weight_loss interactions: nominally
    significant but small effect sizes; likely multiple-testing artifact.
  - h47/h48 olaparib × CRP / × BUN interactions: same caveat.
  - h50 osimertinib × ALK: nominal off-target signal; unlikely robust.
  - h43 pembro × pdl1 × tmb 3-way: p=0.09, suggestive but inconclusive.

==============================================================================
11. KEY TAKEAWAYS
==============================================================================
- Among the four treatments, only sotorasib shows a real biomarker-driven
  effect, and only in KRAS G12C+ patients. The effect is dramatic in males
  without ALK fusion or BRCA2 mutation (+5 months PFS) and zero in females.
- The classic biomarker-treatment pairings for pembrolizumab (PD-L1, TMB),
  olaparib (BRCA2), and osimertinib (EGFR) DO NOT manifest in this cohort
  — these treatments appear inert.
- Standard prognostic features behave largely as expected (ECOG, stage,
  brain mets, weight loss, albumin, histology, smoking, LDH), with two
  unusual reversals: age has a positive effect on PFS, and BRCA2 mutation
  has a small negative effect.
- The female PFS deficit observed marginally is fully explained by the
  absence of sotorasib response in G12C+ females rather than any general
  sex-related difference.
- KRAS G12C, on its own, is a poor prognostic marker; its apparent
  positive marginal effect is a downstream consequence of the highly
  effective targeted therapy benefiting half the carriers.
