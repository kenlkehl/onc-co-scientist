ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (n=50,000)
==========================================

Outcome: pfs_months (continuous; mean 3.43, SD 2.23, range 0.0–15.3).
Treatments: pembrolizumab, sotorasib, olaparib, osimertinib (each ~30–50%
prevalence; many patients receive 0–4 simultaneously).

The analysis proceeded in 13 iterations covering main effects, biomarker–
treatment interactions, multivariable adjustment, and a final systematic
treatment-effect heterogeneity search (T-learner causal forest + regression
tree on patient-level CATE) for each of the four treatments. The single
clinically meaningful treatment effect identified is described below.


1. PROGNOSTIC MAIN EFFECTS (iterations 1–4)
-------------------------------------------
Strong negative prognostic factors for PFS:
  • stage_iv=1                  diff -1.44 mo (p≈0)
  • ecog_ps                      slope -1.10 mo per unit (p≈0)
  • has_brain_mets=1             diff -0.92 mo (p≈0)
  • histology=squamous           diff -0.86 mo vs adenocarcinoma (p≈0)
  • smoking_status=current       diff -0.62 mo vs former/never (p=4e-187)
  • alk_fusion=1                 diff -0.81 mo (p=3e-69)
  • weight_loss_pct_6mo          r=-0.13 (p=7e-179)

Surprising positive prognostic factor:
  • age_years                    r=+0.76 with PFS (p≈0). This unusually
    strong positive correlation appears to be a property of how the
    cohort was assembled and dominates the marginal feature signal;
    after multivariable adjustment it remains positive.
  • albumin_g_dl                 r=+0.10 (p=3e-109)
  • kras_g12c=1                  diff +0.78 mo (p=5e-151) — this
    "main effect" is later shown to be entirely driven by sotorasib
    responders within KRAS G12C+ males.

Other lab values (hemoglobin, alk phos, AST, ALT, total bilirubin,
creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, CRP, NLR) showed no
clinically meaningful association with PFS (|r|<0.02; p>0.1 for most).
PD-L1 TPS as a continuous variable had essentially zero correlation with
PFS (r=+0.006, p=0.16).

Sex: females had marginally shorter PFS than males (3.32 vs 3.52, diff
-0.20, p=2e-24).


2. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS (iteration 3)
---------------------------------------
  • treatment_sotorasib          +0.32 mo (p=2e-53)   ← positive
  • treatment_pembrolizumab      -0.04 mo (p=0.08)    null
  • treatment_olaparib           -0.03 mo (p=0.25)    null
  • treatment_osimertinib        +0.00 mo (p=0.87)    null

Only sotorasib shows a positive marginal effect; the others are null at
the population level.


3. TREATMENT × INDICATED-BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS (iteration 5)
-------------------------------------------------------------
sotorasib × kras_g12c     +2.56 mo  (p≈0)        ← strong
osimertinib × egfr_mut    -0.02     (p=0.76)     null
olaparib  × brca2_mut     +0.04     (p=0.78)     null
pembro    × pdl1_tps      -0.12     (p=0.22)     null
pembro    × tmb_high      -0.02     (p=0.68)     null

Stratified within indicated subgroups:
  • Sotorasib in KRAS G12C+:    +2.55 mo  (p<1e-275, n=2246)
  • Sotorasib in KRAS G12C-:    -0.005    (p=0.80)
  • Osimertinib in EGFR+:       -0.014    (p=0.81, n=1973)
  • Olaparib in BRCA2+:         +0.010    (p=0.93, n=417)
  • Pembrolizumab in PDL1>=0.5: -0.070    (p=0.11, n=10749)
  • Pembrolizumab in TMB-high:  -0.048    (p=0.20)

Interpretation: only sotorasib's labeled biomarker (KRAS G12C) actually
predicts response in this cohort. The other three "biomarker-driven"
drugs do NOT confer benefit in their indicated subgroups.


4. SUBGROUP HETEROGENEITY OF SOTORASIB IN KRAS G12C+ (iteration 5–10)
---------------------------------------------------------------------
A systematic stratification of sotorasib's effect within KRAS G12C+
patients revealed a striking modifier:

  Stratum within KRAS G12C+      sotorasib Δ PFS    p           n
  -----------------------------  -----------------  ----------- ----
  Overall                          +2.55 mo         <1e-275     6368
  Stk11_mutation=0                 +2.59             ~0         5472
  Stk11_mutation=1                 +2.33             6e-35       896
  Histology=adenocarcinoma         +2.62             3e-193     4221
  Histology=squamous               +2.50             2e-95      2147
  Smoking=former                   +2.66             1e-152     3271
  Smoking=current                  +2.48             8e-126     2947
  Smoking=never                    +1.76             8e-5        150
  ECOG=0                           +2.47             5e-94      2257
  ECOG=1                           +2.64             3e-156     3157
  ECOG=2                           +2.49             3e-51       954
  Stage IV=0                       +2.61             4e-104     2265
  Stage IV=1                       +2.51             4e-189     4103
  Brain mets=0                     +2.63             2e-220     4817
  Brain mets=1                     +2.33             2e-63      1551
  ── sex_female=0 (male)           +4.64             ≈ 0        3508
  ── sex_female=1 (female)         -0.01             0.88       2860

Sex completely separates KRAS G12C+ patients into responders and
non-responders to sotorasib. The effect within KRAS G12C+ males is
+4.64 months PFS (a doubling-plus of median PFS), while KRAS G12C+
females derive zero benefit. The effect-modification by sex was confirmed
formally by:

  • A 3-way interaction model (iter 7):
    treatment_sotorasib × kras_g12c × sex_female = -4.69 (p≈0).
    Mean PFS by 2x2x2 cell:
       males,   KRAS G12C+, sotorasib=1: 7.88 mo
       males,   KRAS G12C+, sotorasib=0: 3.23 mo
       females, KRAS G12C+, sotorasib=1: 3.18 mo
       females, KRAS G12C+, sotorasib=0: 3.19 mo

  • Sex-stratified models (iter 10):
    Males only:    treatment_sotorasib:kras_g12c = +4.66 (p≈0)
    Females only:  treatment_sotorasib:kras_g12c = -0.02 (p=0.78)

  • Multivariable adjustment with all covariates (iter 11):
    Adding the 3-way sotorasib×kras×sex interaction raised the model R²
    from 0.894 to 0.971. The "main" sotorasib coefficient drops to ~0
    and the effect is fully captured by the male-KRAS-G12C cell.

  • A T-learner causal-forest CATE (iter 12) split first on kras_g12c
    (no effect anywhere when 0), then on sex_female within KRAS+:
       leaves for sex_female=0: CATE +4.28 to +5.48
       leaves for sex_female=1: CATE -0.56 to +0.17
    confirming the male-only nature of the effect.

  • Within KRAS G12C+ males, the +4.6 month effect was uniform across
    further substrata of stk11, tmb_high, brain mets, and stage_iv — the
    benefit is not further modified by these (all stratum-effects
    +4.5 to +4.7).

  • Within KRAS G12C+ females, no substratum (stk11, tmb_high,
    histology, smoking, brain_mets, stage_iv) recovered any sotorasib
    benefit. The null is robust.


5. SUBGROUP SEARCH FOR THE OTHER THREE TREATMENTS (iter 6, 8, 12, 13)
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Pembrolizumab: exhaustive subgroup screen across pdl1_tps>=0.5,
tmb_high, stk11_mutation, sex_female, histology, smoking, ECOG, stage,
brain_mets, and combinations thereof failed to identify any subgroup
with a significant positive effect. The best candidate (pdl1>=0.5 AND
tmb_high AND stk11=0; n=2714) yielded +0.056 mo, p=0.52.
Causal-forest CATE std for pembrolizumab was only 0.23 (vs 1.22 for
sotorasib), with leaf-level CATE between -0.25 and +0.23 — no coherent
responder subgroup.

Osimertinib: within EGFR+ (n=6517), no further subgroup of histology,
smoking, brain mets, stage, ECOG, STK11, TMB, or sex produced a
positive effect (largest stratum effect was -0.26 in TMB-high, p=0.07).
Causal-forest CATE for osimertinib also lacked a coherent positive
subgroup.

Olaparib: within BRCA2+ (n=1519), no subgroup produced a positive
effect; all p>0.09. Causal-forest CATE leaves all <|0.4|.

Conclusion: pembrolizumab, olaparib, and osimertinib appear functionally
inert in this cohort. The most likely interpretations are (a) this is
how the simulated data were generated (no real treatment signal for
those drugs), or (b) the cohort is too heterogeneous and the indicated
biomarkers as recorded fail to identify the responder subgroup. Either
way, no responder subgroup was detectable from the available features.


6. FINAL SUPPORTED TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS
-------------------------------------------------------
For the only outcome (pfs_months):

  Treatment:    treatment_sotorasib
  Outcome:      pfs_months  (longer PFS = beneficial)
  Direction:    increase by ~+4.6 months (≈ doubling of median PFS)
  Subgroup:     kras_g12c = 1  AND  sex_female = 0  (male)
  Suppressing
  variable:     sex_female = 1  (female).  When sex_female = 1, even
                in KRAS G12C+ patients, the sotorasib treatment effect
                is fully suppressed (Δ ≈ 0 mo, p=0.88).
  Evidence:     stratified mean Δ = +4.64 mo (n=3508, p<1e-300);
                3-way interaction term -4.69 (p≈0); R² jump 0.894→0.971
                with the interaction; T-learner CATE 4.3–5.5 in this
                cell vs near 0 elsewhere; effect uniform across STK11,
                TMB, stage, brain mets, ECOG within the male KRAS+
                subgroup.

For pembrolizumab, olaparib, and osimertinib, no treatment-effect
subgroup of meaningful size and direction was supported.


7. NOTABLE CAVEATS
------------------
• age_years has an implausibly strong positive marginal correlation
  with PFS (r=+0.76); this looks like a structural property of the
  simulated cohort rather than a clinical phenomenon and should not be
  over-interpreted as a real-world effect.
• The lack of any pembrolizumab benefit even at high PD-L1 / TMB is
  inconsistent with real-world NSCLC literature; in a real cohort one
  would expect a positive effect in PD-L1 high. Within this dataset,
  the signal is simply absent.
• The strong sex-mediated suppression of sotorasib efficacy in
  KRAS G12C+ females is a hypothesis the dataset clearly supports
  statistically; it does not appear in the published clinical trial
  literature for sotorasib (which was approved without sex-restricted
  labeling). It should therefore be flagged as either a data-
  generation feature (in a synthetic dataset) or, if the dataset is
  real, an unexpected finding warranting validation. Either way, it is
  the dominant heterogeneity signal in this cohort.
