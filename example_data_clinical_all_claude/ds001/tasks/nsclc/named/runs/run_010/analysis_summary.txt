Analysis Summary — ds001_nsclc (50,000 NSCLC patient records)
================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The dataset is a 50,000-row NSCLC cohort with 33 baseline features (demographics,
performance status, histology, biomarkers, lab values), four binary treatment
indicators (pembrolizumab, sotorasib, olaparib, osimertinib), and one outcome
(pfs_months, range 0–15.3, mean 3.43, median 3.32). All four treatments appear
RANDOMIZED — biomarker prevalences match within ≤0.5 percentage points on/off
each treatment — which permits causal interpretation of treatment-by-feature
interactions. Patients can carry multiple treatments (0–4 per patient).

I ran 20 iterations testing 50 hypotheses with 50 analyses, organized as:
prognostic main effects → treatment main effects → canonical
treatment×biomarker interactions → systematic heterogeneity searches per
treatment (single-feature stratifications, pairwise interaction screens, and
random-forest CATE with depth-3 decision tree) → refinement of the
best-supported subgroup hypothesis.


PROGNOSTIC MAIN EFFECTS (iterations 1–2)
----------------------------------------
Strongly supported (all p<1e-17):
- age_years: STRONG POSITIVE correlation with PFS (r=0.757, p<1e-300; quartile
  means 1.33→2.78→3.97→5.69 mo). Direction is opposite the usual clinical
  intuition — likely a property of this synthetic cohort but robust within it.
- stage_iv=1: -1.44 mo vs stage_iv=0
- ecog_ps: -1.10 mo per ECOG unit
- has_brain_mets=1: -0.92 mo
- squamous histology: -0.86 mo vs adenocarcinoma
- current smoking: ~-0.6 mo vs former/never
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: r=-0.127
- albumin_g_dl: r=+0.099
- ldh_u_l: r=-0.019 (small)
- sex_female: -0.20 mo (small but significant; this becomes important later)
- tmb_high=1: -0.19 mo (DIRECTION OPPOSITE expectation)

Null prognostic effects (p>0.1): pdl1_tps overall, nlr, crp_mg_l,
hemoglobin_g_dl, calcium_mg_dl.


TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS (iteration 3)
------------------------------------
Adjusted-model coefficients (controlling for all baseline features):
- treatment_sotorasib:    +0.323 mo  (p<1e-300)  POSITIVE
- treatment_pembrolizumab: -0.006 mo (p=0.39)    NULL
- treatment_olaparib:     +0.002 mo  (p=0.73)    NULL
- treatment_osimertinib:  +0.001 mo  (p=0.86)    NULL

Only sotorasib shows a population-level positive effect, and the next stage
shows that this entire main effect is driven by a single subgroup.

SYSTEMATIC HETEROGENEITY SEARCH (iterations 6–9, 10–13, 16, 18)
---------------------------------------------------------------
For every treatment with usable variation I ran (a) interaction screens vs all
20 candidate moderators, (b) single-stratum on/off comparisons across all
single-feature subgroups, and (c) a random-forest T-learner CATE estimation
followed by a depth-3 decision tree over the predicted CATE.

PEMBROLIZUMAB
- Interaction screen: only stage_iv (p=0.007, +0.108 mo more in stage IV) and
  weight_loss_pct_6mo (p=3e-4, +0.018 per 1%) reach p<0.05; magnitudes <0.15 mo.
  No biomarker, PD-L1, or TMB interaction.
- Single-stratum: every subgroup mean diff lies in [-0.13, +0.05]. No subgroup
  has a positive effect.
- CATE (depth-3 tree): mean -0.01, std 0.29; tree splits on age only with leaf
  range [-0.28, +0.08]. Effect essentially absent everywhere.

OLAPARIB
- Interaction screen: no moderator reaches p<0.01.
- Single-stratum: no subgroup shows |diff|>0.3 mo, none significant.
- CATE: mean +0.008, std 0.31, leaf range [-0.06, +0.20].

OSIMERTINIB
- Interaction screen: no moderator reaches p<0.01.
- Single-stratum: only ALK+ (+0.23 mo, p=0.018) and BRCA2+ (-0.24 mo, p=0.039)
  reach significance — small magnitudes; no clinically meaningful benefit.
- 3-way osimertinib×egfr×X screen: no significant 3-way interactions.
- CATE: mean +0.006, std 0.34, leaf range [-0.22, +0.45].

SOTORASIB
- Interaction screen: kras_g12c interaction is dominant (+2.56, p<1e-300).
- 3-way sotorasib×kras×stk11: NS (p=0.13). STK11 does NOT meaningfully suppress
  sotorasib benefit in this dataset.
- 3-way sotorasib×kras×sex_female: STRIKING. Within KRAS+, sotorasib effect is
  +4.64 mo in males (n=1237 on, 2271 off) but -0.01 mo in females (n=1009 on,
  1851 off). The 3-way coefficient (+4.66 main interaction, -4.69 three-way)
  exactly cancels the kras-sotorasib effect for females.
- 4-way sotorasib×kras×sex×age: positive sotorasib×kras×age coef +0.0315
  (p=5.7e-11), opposed by sotorasib×kras×sex×age coef -0.0375 (p=1.5e-7). The
  age modification of the sotorasib×kras effect is itself blunted by female sex.
- Within KRAS+ males: continuous age modifier sotorasib×age coef +0.030 mo per
  year (p=2e-9). Quartile diffs: <58.5y +4.07 → 58.5-65y +4.67 → 65-72y +4.52 →
  >72y +4.92. By histology: adeno +4.67, squamous +4.64 — equivalent.
- Within KRAS+ females: no single-feature subgroup recovers a positive effect
  (all |diff|<0.3 mo).
- CATE tree: top splits kras_g12c then sex_female. KRAS+/male leaves +4.25 to
  +5.02; KRAS+/female -0.29 to +0.29; KRAS- ≈ 0.


BEST-SUPPORTED TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS (iterations 17, 20)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment: treatment_sotorasib
Outcome:   pfs_months
Direction: POSITIVE (longer PFS on treatment)
Subgroup definition (full predicate): kras_g12c = 1 AND sex_female = 0
  (i.e., KRAS-G12C-mutant MALES)

Quantitative effect:
- n = 3,508 (7.0% of cohort) — 1,237 on sotorasib, 2,271 off
- Mean PFS on  = 7.88 mo
- Mean PFS off = 3.23 mo
- Effect      = +4.64 mo (95% CI well-bounded; t-test p<1e-300)
- Effect by histology: adenocarcinoma +4.67 mo, squamous +4.64 mo (homogeneous)
- Continuous modifier within subgroup: each additional year of age_years adds
  ~0.030 mo of treatment benefit (sotorasib×age p=2e-9)

Critical negative finding: although stk11_mutation is biologically known to
suppress KRAS-targeted therapy benefit, in this dataset stk11_mutation does
NOT meaningfully attenuate sotorasib benefit (within KRAS+: +2.59 mo if
STK11=0 vs +2.33 mo if STK11=1; 3-way p=0.13). The variable that does suppress
the effect is sex_female=1.


OVERALL CONCLUSIONS ABOUT TREATMENT–BIOMARKER–OUTCOME RELATIONSHIPS
-------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Sotorasib in KRAS-G12C-mutant MALES is the single dominant
   treatment–biomarker–outcome relationship in this cohort, contributing
   essentially the entire population-level sotorasib main effect (~7% of the
   cohort produces ~100% of the +0.32 mo overall effect). The benefit is
   independent of histology, brain mets, stage, and STK11 status, and grows
   slightly with age.

2. The osimertinib–EGFR, olaparib–BRCA2, and pembrolizumab–TMB/PDL1 pairings
   that are clinically expected from real-world NSCLC literature are NOT
   present in this dataset. Each was rigorously tested via main-effect, 2-way
   interaction, stratified-subgroup, 3-way interaction, and tree-based CATE
   analyses, and no clinically meaningful positive treatment effect was found
   in the biomarker-positive subgroup or in any other subgroup.

3. Pembrolizumab, olaparib, and osimertinib show essentially flat treatment
   effects across the entire cohort and across all subgroup definitions
   examined, with mean predicted CATE near zero and standard deviations under
   0.35 months.

4. Standard NSCLC prognostic factors behave normally in this cohort
   (stage IV, ECOG, brain mets, squamous histology, current smoking, weight
   loss, low albumin all worsen PFS; high albumin slightly improves it), with
   one notable exception: age_years is positively correlated with PFS in this
   cohort, which is unusual and a robust idiosyncrasy of this dataset.

5. The cohort and treatment structure look randomized; biomarker prevalences
   are balanced on/off treatment, supporting causal interpretation of the
   sotorasib × KRAS × sex finding.
