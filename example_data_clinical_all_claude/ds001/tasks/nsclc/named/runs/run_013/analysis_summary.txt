Analysis summary — ds001_nsclc (n=50,000)
===========================================

OVERVIEW

This dataset contains 50,000 NSCLC patients with 33 features (demographics,
ECOG performance status, histology, stage, brain metastases, six binary
biomarkers, PD-L1 TPS as a continuous score, 16 baseline labs, four binary
treatments, and weight-loss percentage) and a single continuous outcome
pfs_months. Treatments are NOT mutually exclusive — many patients receive
two or more (only 16% of patients receive zero, 38% receive one, 33%
receive two, 12% receive three, 1.5% receive all four).

The propose-test-update protocol was run for the full 25 iterations,
producing 139 hypotheses and 139 analyses. The dominant scientific finding
is one strongly-modified treatment effect (sotorasib in a narrow KRAS G12C+
male subgroup). Pembrolizumab, olaparib, and osimertinib show essentially
no effect on PFS in this cohort, even within their canonical biological
target subgroups.


ITERATIONS 1-3 — MAIN-EFFECT FEATURE-OUTCOME RELATIONSHIPS

Clinical/demographic predictors (Iter 1) of pfs_months were broadly as
expected:

  - age_years: Pearson r = +0.757, p≈0 — older patients have markedly
    LONGER PFS in this dataset (this association is unusually strong and
    consistently present after adjustment, β=+0.17 mo/year; reported as is
    although the direction is the opposite of typical clinical experience).
  - sex_female=1 vs male: -0.20 mo (Welch p=7.7e-25) — women have
    moderately shorter PFS.
  - ecog_ps: -1.10 mo per unit, p≈0 — strong negative effect (poor PS).
  - stage_iv=1: -1.44 mo, p≈0.
  - has_brain_mets=1: -0.92 mo, p≈0.
  - smoking_status: never (3.67) ≈ former (3.61) > current (2.99),
    one-way ANOVA F=433, p≈0; current minus never = -0.69 mo.
  - histology adenocarcinoma vs squamous: +0.86 mo, p≈0.

Biomarker main effects (Iter 2):

  - egfr_mutation=1: +0.19 mo (p=6e-11) — mild positive.
  - kras_g12c=1: +0.78 mo (p=7e-94) — large positive (likely confounded
    by treatment).
  - alk_fusion=1: -0.81 mo (p=6e-66) — large negative.
  - stk11_mutation=1: -0.01 mo (p=0.74) — null.
  - brca2_mutation=1: -0.12 mo (p=0.03) — small negative.
  - tmb_high=1: -0.19 mo (p=1e-17) — small negative.
  - pdl1_tps continuous: r=0.006, p=0.16 — no association.

Lab main effects (Iter 3): only a handful are non-trivial:

  - albumin_g_dl: r=+0.099, p=3e-109 — higher albumin → longer PFS.
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo: r=-0.127, p=7e-179 — more weight loss →
    shorter PFS.
  - ldh_u_l: r=-0.018, p=4e-5 — tiny negative.
  - All other 13 labs: |r|<0.01 and most are non-significant.


ITERATIONS 4-5 — TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS

Unadjusted (Iter 4) Welch t-tests:
  - treatment_pembrolizumab: -0.035 mo, p=0.08 (null).
  - treatment_sotorasib: +0.32 mo, p=6e-49 (clearly positive).
  - treatment_olaparib: -0.025 mo, p=0.26 (null).
  - treatment_osimertinib: +0.004 mo, p=0.87 (null).

Adjusted (Iter 5) — single OLS with all demographics, ECOG, stage, brain
mets, smoking, histology, all biomarkers, all 16 labs, and all four
treatments together (R²=0.894):
  - β(pembrolizumab) = -0.006, p=0.39 — null.
  - β(sotorasib)     = +0.323, p≈0   — robust positive marginal effect.
  - β(olaparib)      = +0.002, p=0.74 — null.
  - β(osimertinib)   = +0.001, p=0.88 — null.

So in the average patient, only sotorasib has any effect. The model itself
is dominated by stage_iv (-1.42 mo), ECOG (-1.10), brain mets (-0.91),
KRAS G12C (+0.89, but this is largely the confounded sotorasib benefit
folded back into the marker), histology (+0.72), and the strong age
effect (+0.17 mo/year × 90-year range).


ITERATIONS 6-9 — TX×FEATURE INTERACTION SCREENS

Pembrolizumab (Iter 6): no interaction p<0.005 reaches biological
relevance. Top hits stage_iv (β=+0.049, p=0.005) and kras_g12c
(β=-0.060, p=0.014) are both small and not consistent with any standard
predictor of immunotherapy benefit.

Sotorasib (Iter 7): a giant interaction with kras_g12c (β=+2.51, p≈0;
the residual main-effect of sotorasib in kras-WT drops to ~0). Other
strong modifiers: sex_female (β=-0.58, p<1e-235), smoke_current (+0.26),
alk_fusion (-0.34), hist_adeno (-0.14), egfr_mutation (-0.19),
brca2_mutation (-0.32), tmb_high (+0.084) — but most of these arise
because kras_g12c+ patients are non-uniformly distributed across these
features and the modifiers tag conditions where KRAS biology is
different.

Olaparib (Iter 8): no interaction stronger than β=±0.04 with p<0.05; no
canonical modifier (BRCA2, STK11) shows any signal.

Osimertinib (Iter 9): no interaction term reaches p<0.02 with a
biologically meaningful magnitude.


ITERATION 10 — STRATIFIED EFFECTS

Within kras_g12c=1 (n=6,368), unadjusted sotorasib diff = +2.55 mo
(p=2e-202). Within kras_g12c=0 the effect is null. The largest
sub-effect surfaced is in sex_female=0 (males) where sotorasib gives
+0.57 mo overall (p=4e-74) — but this is again driven by KRAS G12C+
males (see below).


ITERATION 11 — THREE-WAY INTERACTIONS (KRAS-INDEPENDENT)

Three-way interactions among the strongest sotorasib modifiers (kras_g12c,
sex_female, smoke_current, alk_fusion, hist_adeno) confirm a sex-by-kras
joint modification dominates and that no other pair adds independent
information.


ITERATIONS 12-14 — CANONICAL BIOLOGY CHECKS (ALL NULL)

  - Iter 12, osimertinib × egfr_mutation: interaction β = -0.021,
    p=0.32. Within EGFR+ (n=6,517) adjusted β(osimertinib)=-0.019
    (p=0.20). Within EGFR-WT β=+0.004 (p=0.61). EGFR-TKI biology is NOT
    reproduced in this dataset.
  - Iter 13, pembrolizumab × {pdl1_tps, tmb_high, smoke_current,
    smoke_former, hist_adeno, egfr_mutation, alk_fusion, stk11_mutation}:
    every interaction p>0.27 with effect-sizes |β|<0.04. PD-L1 / TMB
    biology is NOT reproduced.
  - Iter 14, olaparib × {brca2_mutation, stk11_mutation, egfr_mutation,
    alk_fusion, tmb_high, kras_g12c}: all interaction p>0.17, effect-sizes
    |β|<0.03. PARP-inhibitor biology is NOT reproduced.

Conclusion: pembrolizumab, olaparib, and osimertinib appear pharmacologically
inactive on PFS in this cohort regardless of subgroup.


ITERATIONS 15-17 — REFINING THE SOTORASIB SUBGROUP

Within kras_g12c=1 (Iter 15), the unadjusted sotorasib effect varies
sharply by sex_female:

  - kras_g12c=1 & sex_female=0 (males, n=3,508): diff = +4.64 mo, p≈0.
  - kras_g12c=1 & sex_female=1 (females, n=2,860): diff = -0.01 mo,
    p=0.88. Adjusted β = +0.011 (p=0.42).

Other features (smoke, histology, STK11, EGFR, TMB, stage, brain mets) do
NOT modify the kras+ effect strongly when looked at within the kras+
subset. BRCA2+ (n=191) and ALK+ (n=259) within kras+ DO appear to
abolish the effect (diff ≈ 0 with very large p), but cell sizes are
small.

Three-way interactions (Iter 16) for sotorasib × kras × {feat}:

  - sex_female: 3-way β = -4.58, p≈0 — by far the strongest modifier.
  - brca2_mutation: 3-way β = -2.70, p=8e-163.
  - alk_fusion: 3-way β = -2.57, p=2e-200.
  - All other 3-way terms |β|<0.18.

Joint subgroup model (Iter 17): the unadjusted sotorasib effect
within kras+ is +2.55 mo, but rises to +4.64 mo when restricted to
kras+ males. The OLS interaction term treatment_sotorasib × (kras+ &
male) = +4.59 (p≈0); residual sotorasib main β ≈ 0 once that joint
interaction is included.


ITERATION 18-19 — NULL-TREATMENT SUBGROUP CONFIRMATION

  - Pembrolizumab × pdl1_high (TPS≥0.5): n=10,749 in PD-L1-high,
    diff = -0.07 mo (p=0.11); interaction β=+0.004, p=0.80. No benefit
    even in the canonical PD-L1-high subgroup.
  - Olaparib × brca2_mutation: in BRCA2+ (n=1,519) diff = +0.01,
    p=0.93; interaction β=-0.007, p=0.88.


ITERATION 20 — EXHAUSTIVE 1- AND 2-FEATURE BINARY SUBGROUP SEARCH

For each treatment, all rules of the form A=v or A=v & B=w over 13
binary features (sex_female, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, the six biomarkers,
tmb_high, smoke_current, smoke_former, hist_adeno, pdl1_high) with
n_treated≥100 and n_control≥100 were evaluated.

  - Sotorasib best rule: sex_female=0 & kras_g12c=1 (n=3,508,
    diff = +4.64 mo, p≈0). Every kras+-containing rule places second.
  - Pembrolizumab best rule: stage_iv=0 & kras_g12c=1 (n=2,265,
    diff = -0.46 mo, p=1.5e-4) — i.e., a NEGATIVE association. No rule
    yields a positive PFS benefit.
  - Olaparib best rule: |diff| ≤ 0.06 mo across all rules; no
    candidate subgroup.
  - Osimertinib best rule: |diff| ≤ 0.027 mo across all rules; no
    candidate subgroup.


ITERATION 21-22 — REFINING THE STRICT SOTORASIB SUBGROUP

Within kras+ & male (Iter 21), brca2_mutation+ and alk_fusion+ each
abolish the sotorasib effect (diff ≈ 0 in n≈100-140 cells), while every
other feature (smoking, histology, STK11, EGFR, TMB, stage, brain mets)
preserves the +4.5-4.8 month benefit. The strict 4-feature subgroup
defined by

   STRICT_GRP := kras_g12c=1 AND sex_female=0
                 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND alk_fusion=0

contains n=3,266 (≈6.5% of cohort) with n_tx=1,154. Iter 22:

  - Inside STRICT_GRP: unadjusted sotorasib diff = +4.99 mo (p≈0);
    fully adjusted OLS β = +4.93, p≈0.
  - Outside STRICT_GRP (n=46,734): diff = -0.006 mo, p=0.76 — null.
  - OLS interaction treatment_sotorasib × STRICT_GRP β = +4.93, p≈0;
    residual main β(sotorasib) = +0.001 (p=0.69).

This is the best-supported treatment-effect heterogeneity hypothesis in
the dataset.


ITERATION 23 — BROADER NULL-TX SUBGROUP SEARCH

A separate exhaustive 2-binary-feature subgroup search restricted to
pembrolizumab, olaparib, and osimertinib (n_tx≥50, n_ctrl≥50) returned:

  - Pembrolizumab best subgroup: stage_iv=0 & kras_g12c=1 → diff = -0.46,
    p=1.5e-4 (a small negative association).
  - Olaparib best subgroup: stage_iv=1 & hist_adeno=0 → diff = -0.14,
    p=2e-3 (small).
  - Osimertinib best subgroup: brca2_mutation=1 & tmb_high=0 → diff =
    -0.40, p=4e-3 (small, also negative; only n=1,084).

No positive treatment subgroup of meaningful size emerges for any of
these three drugs.


ITERATION 24 — ROBUSTNESS

Within kras+ & male (n=3,508), an OLS adjusted for ECOG, stage, brain
mets, smoking, histology, all other biomarkers, all labs, and the other
three treatments yields β(sotorasib) = +4.59, p≈0. Within kras+ & female
(n=2,860) the same model returns β(sotorasib) = +0.011, p=0.43. The
sex-mediated heterogeneity is fully robust to adjustment.


ITERATION 25 — SEX SYMMETRY CHECK

  - treatment_sotorasib × sex_female: β = -0.58, p<1e-300 (the dominant
    interaction in the dataset).
  - treatment_pembrolizumab × sex_female: β = +0.011, p=0.44.
  - treatment_olaparib × sex_female: β = -0.019, p=0.21.
  - treatment_osimertinib × sex_female: β = -0.006, p=0.71.

Sex modifies sotorasib only. The other three treatments show no sex
heterogeneity.


HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED VS REFUTED — BRIEF SCORECARD

Supported:
  1. Older age, lower ECOG, non-stage-IV, no brain mets, never/former
     smoking, adenocarcinoma histology, higher albumin, lower weight loss
     all associate with longer PFS (Iters 1-3).
  2. EGFR-mutant and KRAS G12C+ patients have longer PFS; ALK-fusion+
     and TMB-high have shorter PFS (Iter 2).
  3. Sotorasib increases PFS in a narrow joint subgroup defined by
     kras_g12c=1 AND sex_female=0 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND alk_fusion=0
     (≈+4.9 mo; Iters 7, 10, 15-17, 20-22, 24).

Refuted (unexpected nulls):
  4. Pembrolizumab → PFS overall, in PD-L1-high, in TMB-high, or in any
     1-2 feature subgroup (Iters 4-6, 13, 18, 20, 23).
  5. Olaparib → PFS overall or in BRCA2+ (Iters 4-5, 8, 14, 19, 20, 23).
  6. Osimertinib → PFS overall or in EGFR+ (Iters 4-5, 9, 12, 20, 23).
  7. Sotorasib effect is NOT homogeneous across kras_g12c+ patients —
     it is suppressed in females and in those with concurrent BRCA2 or
     ALK alterations (Iters 15-17, 21).


FINAL BEST-SUPPORTED TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS

Treatment:  treatment_sotorasib
Outcome:    pfs_months
Direction:  positive (longer PFS on treatment vs control)
Subgroup:   kras_g12c=1 AND sex_female=0 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND
            alk_fusion=0
Effect size in subgroup (n=3,266, n_tx=1,154):
  - Unadjusted mean difference: +4.99 mo (p < 1e-300)
  - Adjusted OLS β: +4.93 mo (p < 1e-300)
Outside this subgroup (n=46,734) the effect is null (diff ≈ -0.006 mo,
p=0.76). No comparable positive subgroup was identified for the other
three treatments in this cohort.
