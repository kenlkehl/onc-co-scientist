ds001_nsclc — Analysis Summary
==============================

Cohort and outcome
------------------
The analysis cohort is 50,000 NSCLC patients with one outcome, pfs_months. PFS has
mean 3.43, median 3.32, sd 2.23 months. Each patient has 0 to 4 of the four binary
treatments assigned (treatment_pembrolizumab ~50%, treatment_sotorasib ~35%,
treatment_olaparib ~30%, treatment_osimertinib ~30%). Treatment assignment is not
biomarker-restricted in this dataset — for example, only ~35% of KRAS G12C+ patients
are on sotorasib and ~30% of EGFR+ patients are on osimertinib, very close to the
population-average treatment frequencies. This makes the data well-suited to interaction
and subgroup analyses because there is comparison contrast within every biomarker
stratum.

Iteration 1 — Cohort overview
-----------------------------
H1 (PFS distribution): supported. Mean ≈ 3 months, sd ≈ 2 months, no obvious aberrations.

Iterations 2–4 — Demographics, performance status, stage, brain mets
--------------------------------------------------------------------
H2 (older age → longer PFS): SUPPORTED, slope +0.170 months/yr, p<<.001. Direction is
opposite of typical clinical intuition — in this synthetic cohort, older age is
associated with longer, not shorter, PFS.
H3 (female → shorter PFS): SUPPORTED, mean diff −0.20 months, p≈8e−25.
H4 (smoking_status differs): SUPPORTED (ANOVA p≈4e−187).
H5 (higher ECOG → shorter PFS): SUPPORTED, slope −1.10 months per PS unit, p<<.001 —
the strongest single prognostic factor.
H6 (adeno vs squamous → longer PFS): SUPPORTED, +0.86 months, p<<.001.
H7 (stage IV → shorter PFS): SUPPORTED, −1.44 months, p<<.001.
H8 (brain mets → shorter PFS): SUPPORTED, −0.92 months, p<<.001.

Iterations 5–7 — Driver mutations and immune biomarkers
-------------------------------------------------------
H9 (EGFR+ vs EGFR-): SUPPORTED, +0.19 months, p≈6e−11.
H10 (ALK+ vs ALK-): SUPPORTED but in the opposite of the originally hypothesized
direction — ALK+ patients have SHORTER PFS than ALK- (−0.81 months, p≈6e−66).
H11 (KRAS G12C+ vs G12C-): marginal +0.78 months PFS, p<<.001 — but this is driven
entirely by the strong sotorasib effect within the KRAS G12C+ subset (see iteration 14).
H12 (STK11 → shorter PFS): NOT supported (diff +0.009, p=0.74). STK11 has no marginal
prognostic association.
H13 (BRCA2 → different PFS): borderline, −0.12 months, p=0.031.
H14 (PD-L1 TPS continuous → longer PFS): NOT supported (slope +0.067, p=0.16).
H15 (TMB-high → longer PFS): REFUTED in direction — TMB-high is associated with
SHORTER PFS (−0.19 months, p≈1e−17).

Iterations 8–10 — Lab features
------------------------------
H16 (albumin → longer PFS): SUPPORTED, +0.44 months/(g/dL), p≈3e−109. Strongest
continuous lab marker.
H17 (LDH → shorter PFS): SUPPORTED but tiny in magnitude (−0.0005/U/L, p≈4e−5).
H18 (weight loss → shorter PFS): SUPPORTED, slope −0.073 months per %, p<<.001.
H19–H21 (CRP, NLR, hemoglobin): NOT supported marginally (all p>0.4).
H22 (liver function panel → PFS): NOT supported (all alkaline-phosphatase, AST, ALT,
total bilirubin slopes p>0.1).
H23 (renal/electrolyte panel → PFS): NOT supported (all p>0.2).

Iteration 11 — Multivariable model
----------------------------------
H24 + H25: SUPPORTED. In a 22-feature OLS (R^2 = 0.86), the established prognostic
factors retain their direction: ecog_ps, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, weight_loss_pct_6mo
remain strongly negative; albumin_g_dl, age_years, adeno histology remain positive.
The female-sex association also survives adjustment.

Iterations 12–13 — Treatment main effects
-----------------------------------------
H26 (pembro → longer PFS): NOT supported, raw diff −0.035 months, p=0.08; adjusted
coefficient also small/null.
H27 (sotorasib → longer PFS): SUPPORTED in raw cohort (+0.32 months, p≈6e−49).
H28 (olaparib → longer PFS): NOT supported (−0.025, p=0.26).
H29 (osimertinib → longer PFS): NOT supported (+0.004, p=0.87).
H30 (sotorasib retains adjusted effect): the adjusted coefficient is positive but the
raw average mixes a strong positive effect in KRAS+ patients with a null effect
elsewhere — the main-effect average is not the right summary.
H31 (no main effect for the other three treatments): SUPPORTED.

Iteration 14 — Sotorasib × KRAS G12C
------------------------------------
H32 (sotorasib benefit concentrated in KRAS G12C+): STRONGLY SUPPORTED. Interaction
treatment_sotorasib:kras_g12c coef = +2.56, p ≈ 0. In KRAS G12C+ patients, sotorasib
adds +2.55 months PFS (p ≈ 2e−202). In KRAS G12C− patients, sotorasib effect is null
(diff −0.005, p=0.80) — H33 SUPPORTED. This is the dominant treatment-effect signal
in the cohort and recapitulates a known biological mechanism.

Iterations 15–16 — Osimertinib × EGFR; olaparib × BRCA2
-------------------------------------------------------
H34 (osimertinib benefit in EGFR+): REFUTED. Interaction p=0.99; in EGFR+ patients
the osimertinib effect is −0.014 months (p=0.81) and in EGFR− patients +0.006
(p=0.81). Despite a clear biological expectation, no benefit is detectable here.
H35 (olaparib benefit in BRCA2+): REFUTED. Interaction p=0.24; in BRCA2+ olaparib
effect is +0.010 months (p=0.93, n=417 on / 1102 off); in BRCA2− p=0.24. No benefit.

Iterations 17–18 — Pembrolizumab × biomarkers
---------------------------------------------
H36 (pembro × PD-L1 TPS): NOT supported (interaction coef −0.106, p=0.21). Direction
was not even positive.
H37 (pembro × TMB high): NOT supported (interaction coef −0.048, p=0.22).
H38 (pembro suppressed by STK11): NOT supported (coef +0.012, p=0.81).
H39 (pembro × histology): NOT supported (coef −0.054, p=0.17). None of the canonical
PD-1/biomarker interactions reproduces in this cohort.

Iteration 19 — Systematic interaction screening (heterogeneity search)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
H40 (each treatment has at least one significant feature interaction):
  - Sotorasib: kras_g12c (coef +2.56, p≈0); sex_female (coef −0.56, p≈5e−41);
    smk_never (coef −0.36, p≈6e−10); smk_current (coef +0.27, p≈2e−9);
    alk_fusion (coef −0.49, p≈4e−7); egfr_mutation (coef −0.24, p≈1e−4).
    Many strong modifiers.
  - Pembrolizumab: weight_loss_pct_6mo (coef +0.018, p≈3e−4); stage_iv (coef +0.108,
    p≈0.007). Modest, weakly modifying.
  - Olaparib: bun_mg_dl (coef +0.011, p=0.014); crp_mg_l (coef +0.0055, p=0.022).
    Marginal, not reproducible.
  - Osimertinib: alk_fusion (coef +0.24, p=0.017); brca2_mutation (coef −0.25, p=0.05).
    Marginal.
Sotorasib emerges as having a true, biologically grounded effect modifier; the
others have at most weak, non-canonical interactions.

Iterations 20–22 — Refining the sotorasib subgroup
--------------------------------------------------
Within KRAS G12C+ patients, the screen identified sex_female and alk_fusion as
candidate modifiers of the sotorasib effect. Stratified analysis confirms:
H41 (KRAS+ males benefit, females do not): SUPPORTED.
   - KRAS+ & sex_female==0 (males, n=3508): sotorasib diff = +4.64 months, p ≈ 0.
   - KRAS+ & sex_female==1 (females, n=2860): sotorasib diff = −0.012, p=0.88.
   - Within-KRAS+ interaction sotorasib:sex_female coef = −4.72, p ≈ 0.
H42 (ALK fusion suppresses sotorasib in KRAS+): SUPPORTED.
   - KRAS+ & alk_fusion==0 (n=6109): sotorasib diff = +2.67, p<<.001.
   - KRAS+ & alk_fusion==1 (n=259): sotorasib diff = −0.35, p=0.23.
   - Within-KRAS+ interaction sotorasib:alk_fusion coef = −2.76, p ≈ 1e−26.
H43 (smoking-never does NOT modify within KRAS+): SUPPORTED — within-KRAS+
sotorasib:smk_never interaction p=0.18; smoking-never is not a true modifier once
sex and ALK are accounted for. The pooled smoking signal in the unstratified screen
was a confound of the male-vs-female effect.
H44 (joint subgroup KRAS+ & male & ALK-, ever-smoker, EGFR-): SUPPORTED.
   - n=3,112: sotorasib diff = +4.86 months, p ≈ 0.
   - Complementary subgroup (KRAS+ but any unfavorable modifier — n=3,256): diff =
     +0.32, p≈3e−4 (small residual signal driven by KRAS+ males without alk_fusion
     who happen to have other unfavorable features).

Iteration 23 — Pembrolizumab heterogeneity
------------------------------------------
H45 (pembro is mildly negative in lower-burden patients): SUPPORTED but clinically
marginal.
   - weight_loss tertile low: pembro diff = −0.111, p=0.001.
   - stage_iv==0: pembro diff = −0.10, p=0.002.
   - weight_loss<6 & stage_iv==0 (n=12,750): pembro diff = −0.144, p=0.0002.
   - Higher-burden strata: ~null.
The magnitude (~−0.1 to −0.15 months, < 5 days) is small, but it is statistically
robust and consistent with no positive benefit anywhere.

Iteration 24 — Osimertinib and olaparib refinement
--------------------------------------------------
H46 (osimertinib still null in refined EGFR+ subgroups): SUPPORTED. Across EGFR+ &
ALK- (n=5,995, diff −0.023), EGFR+ & adenocarcinoma & never-smoker (n=3,621, diff
−0.043), EGFR+ & adeno & ECOG=0 & no brain mets (n=1,459, diff −0.044), the effect
is uniformly near zero and never significant.
H47 (olaparib still null in refined BRCA2+ subgroups): SUPPORTED. BRCA2+ all (diff
+0.010), BRCA2+ & bun>=median (diff −0.055), BRCA2+ & bun<median (diff +0.071),
BRCA2+ & ECOG=0 (diff −0.137) — all non-significant. No subgroup with detectable
benefit.

Iteration 25 — Final best-supported subgroup hypotheses
-------------------------------------------------------
H48 — FINAL: treatment_sotorasib increases pfs_months by approximately +4.6 to +4.9
months in the subgroup defined by:
   kras_g12c==1 AND sex_female==0 AND alk_fusion==0
Outside this subgroup (any of: kras_g12c==0, sex_female==1, alk_fusion==1), the
sotorasib effect is small or null. SUPPORTED with very high confidence.

H49 — FINAL: treatment_pembrolizumab does not produce a positive pfs_months benefit
in any subgroup. The only signal is a small NEGATIVE effect (~−0.1 months) in
lower-burden patients (low weight_loss_pct_6mo and/or stage_iv==0); in higher-burden
strata the effect is null. SUPPORTED.

H50 — FINAL: treatment_osimertinib does not produce a positive pfs_months benefit
in any subgroup, including egfr_mutation==1 patients. This is biologically anomalous
versus published real-world evidence and trial data and may reflect properties of
this particular synthetic dataset rather than clinical reality. SUPPORTED within
this dataset.

H51 — FINAL: treatment_olaparib does not produce a positive pfs_months benefit in
any subgroup, including brca2_mutation==1 patients. Same caveat as H50 — the data
do not reproduce the canonical biology. SUPPORTED within this dataset.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. Strong, conventional prognostic factors operate as expected on pfs_months:
   ECOG performance status (negative), stage IV (negative), brain mets (negative),
   weight loss (negative), albumin (positive), adenocarcinoma (positive), age
   (positive in this cohort), and female sex (negative).
2. Among biomarkers, ALK fusion is associated with shorter PFS, EGFR with slightly
   longer PFS, and TMB-high counterintuitively with shorter PFS. STK11, PD-L1 TPS
   (continuous), and the broader liver/renal/electrolyte panels have no marginal
   association.
3. The dominant treatment-effect signal is sotorasib in KRAS G12C+ patients —
   roughly +2.5 months on average and +4.6 to +4.9 months in KRAS G12C+ males with
   ALK-negative tumors. The sotorasib benefit is fully ablated by sex_female==1 or
   alk_fusion==1; this multivariable subgroup definition is the best-supported
   treatment-effect heterogeneity finding in the dataset.
4. The other three treatments (pembrolizumab, osimertinib, olaparib) do not show
   a positive pfs_months benefit, even when restricted to their biologically
   appropriate biomarker subgroups (PD-L1 high / TMB high / STK11 wild-type for
   pembro; EGFR+ for osimertinib; BRCA2+ for olaparib). This is unusual relative
   to clinical expectation and likely reflects how this dataset was generated.
