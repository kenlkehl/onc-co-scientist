ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (n=50,000)
===========================================

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patients with 33 baseline features and a single outcome,
pfs_months (progression-free survival in months; mean 3.43, SD 2.23, range
0–15.3). Four treatments are recorded as binary indicators (assigned roughly
independently): treatment_pembrolizumab (~50% prevalence),
treatment_sotorasib (~35%), treatment_olaparib (~30%),
treatment_osimertinib (~30%). Biomarker prevalence: egfr_mutation 13.0%,
kras_g12c 12.7%, alk_fusion 4.9%, stk11_mutation 14.9%, brca2_mutation 3.0%,
tmb_high 28.5%, pdl1_tps mean 0.31. Up to 25 propose-test-refine iterations
were budgeted; 18 substantive iterations were used (further iterations would
have been redundant with the question already answered).

================================================================
1. PROGNOSTIC FACTORS (Iterations 1–3)
================================================================

Strong main effects on pfs_months (all univariate; OLS or Welch t-test;
n=50,000 throughout):

  Feature                          Direction & magnitude        p-value
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  age_years (slope/yr)             +0.170 mo / yr               <1e-300
  sex_female (vs male)             −0.204 mo                    7.7e-25
  ecog_ps (per level 0/1/2)        −1.099 mo                    <1e-300
  stage_iv (vs not)                −1.438 mo                    <1e-300
  has_brain_mets (vs not)          −0.921 mo                    <1e-300
  histology = adenocarcinoma       +0.857 mo (vs squamous)      <1e-300
  smoking (current vs never)       −0.687 mo (ANOVA F)          <1e-300
  egfr_mutation                    +0.189 mo                    <1e-7
  kras_g12c                        +0.782 mo                    <1e-300
  alk_fusion                       −0.810 mo                    <1e-50
  stk11_mutation                   +0.009 mo                    0.74 (NS)
  brca2_mutation                   −0.119 mo                    0.031
  tmb_high                         −0.190 mo                    <1e-7
  pdl1_tps (per unit)              +0.067 mo / unit             0.16 (NS)
  albumin_g_dl (per g/dL)          +0.441 mo / g/dL             <1e-300
  ldh_u_l (per U/L)                −0.0005 mo / U/L             <1e-50
  weight_loss_pct_6mo (per %)      −0.073 mo / %                <1e-300

Most "frailty" labs not pre-listed above (crp, nlr, hemoglobin, alkaline
phosphatase, AST, ALT, total bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium,
calcium) were not significantly associated with pfs_months in univariate
regression — a useful negative finding indicating these are noise rather
than prognostic in this synthetic cohort.

Two findings deserve emphasis as unexpected:
  • Older age was associated with LONGER PFS (counter-clinical).
  • Female sex was associated with SHORTER PFS — opposite the typical
    real-world NSCLC pattern.
  • TMB-high was associated with SHORTER PFS in the unselected cohort
    (likely because TMB-high patients in this dataset have other
    unfavorable correlates that dominate, since pembrolizumab is not
    differentially effective; see below).

================================================================
2. MAIN TREATMENT EFFECTS (Iteration 4)
================================================================

Univariate t-tests (treated vs. not-treated) on pfs_months:

  treatment_pembrolizumab         −0.035 mo   p=0.081  NS
  treatment_sotorasib             +0.322 mo   p<1e-9   ***
  treatment_olaparib              −0.025 mo   p=0.26   NS
  treatment_osimertinib           +0.004 mo   p=0.87   NS

Only sotorasib has a statistically significant main effect — and even that
is small in the all-comers analysis because most patients are KRAS-wild-type
(see below).

Multivariable adjusted main effects (OLS with all baseline features,
biomarkers, and key labs as covariates) confirm the same pattern:

  treatment_pembrolizumab (adj)   −0.006 mo   p=0.39   NS
  treatment_sotorasib (adj)       +0.323 mo   p<1e-9   ***
  treatment_olaparib (adj)        +0.002 mo   p=0.73   NS
  treatment_osimertinib (adj)     +0.001 mo   p=0.87   NS

================================================================
3. CANONICAL TREATMENT–BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS (Iterations 6–9)
================================================================

I tested the four "expected" matches based on the labeled drug names
(osimertinib→EGFR, sotorasib→KRAS G12C, pembrolizumab→PDL1/TMB,
olaparib→BRCA2). Only one was supported:

  Sotorasib × KRAS G12C (THE STORY):
    soto in KRAS+:    +2.55 mo (p≈0)
    soto in KRAS-:    −0.005 mo (p=0.80)
    Interaction coef: +2.56 mo (p<1e-300, unadjusted)
    Adjusted interaction coef: +2.54 mo (p<1e-300)
    → Massive, robust, biomarker-restricted treatment effect.

  Osimertinib × EGFR (NOT supported):
    osi in EGFR+:     −0.014 mo (p=0.81)
    osi in EGFR-:     +0.006 mo (p=0.81)
    Interaction coef: −0.020 mo (p=0.76, unadj) / −0.011 (p=0.66, adj)
    → Osimertinib has NO efficacy in EGFR-mutant patients in this dataset.
       This is opposite to the real-world clinical literature; in this
       synthetic cohort the "expected" association does not hold.

  Pembrolizumab × TMB-high (NOT supported):
    pembro in TMB-high: −0.048 mo (p=0.20)
    pembro in TMB-low:  −0.030 mo (p=0.20)
    Interaction coef:   −0.018 (p=0.68, unadj) / −0.012 (p=0.46, adj)
    → No TMB-driven pembrolizumab benefit.

  Pembrolizumab × PDL1 (NOT supported):
    pembro in PDL1>=0.5: −0.070 mo (p=0.11)
    pembro in PDL1<0.5:  −0.025 mo (p=0.26)
    Interaction (binary PDL1):   −0.045 (p=0.36)
    Interaction (continuous PDL1): −0.116 (p=0.22)
    → No PDL1-driven pembrolizumab benefit.

  Olaparib × BRCA2 (NOT supported):
    olap in BRCA2+: +0.010 mo (p=0.93)
    olap in BRCA2-: −0.026 mo (p=0.24)
    Interaction coef: +0.037 (p=0.78, unadj) / +0.032 (p=0.51, adj)
    → No BRCA2-driven olaparib benefit.

================================================================
4. NEGATIVE-MODIFIER HYPOTHESES (Iteration 10)
================================================================

The clinically familiar hypothesis "STK11 mutation suppresses
immunotherapy response" was tested in pembrolizumab × STK11 and the 3-way
pembrolizumab × TMB × STK11 model. Both interactions were null
(pembro × STK11 coef −0.016, p=0.77; 3-way coef +0.10, p=0.40). Within
TMB-high, STK11-WT patients also showed no pembrolizumab benefit (−0.057,
p=0.17). Conclusion: STK11 does not unmask hidden pembrolizumab benefit.

================================================================
5. SYSTEMATIC TREATMENT × FEATURE INTERACTION SCREENS (Iteration 12)
================================================================

For each of the four treatments I screened OLS interactions against 11
binary and 9 continuous features. Top interactions (signed):

  Pembrolizumab: weakly significant, mostly weight_loss_pct_6mo (+0.018,
    p=0.0003) and stage_iv (+0.108, p=0.007). None translates into a
    subgroup with a positive pembrolizumab pfs_months effect on
    follow-up subgroup checks (Iteration 16) — these are noise-level
    interactions on a treatment with no main effect.

  Sotorasib: dominant signals are kras_g12c (+2.56, p≈0), sex_female
    (−0.56, p<1e-9), smoke_current (+0.27, p<1e-7), alk_fusion (−0.49,
    p<0.001), egfr_mutation (−0.24, p=0.0001). The kras_g12c interaction
    is the primary driver; the sex_female interaction is the secondary
    modifier explored in Iteration 13.

  Olaparib: top hit is crp_mg_l (+0.0055, p=0.022) — a tiny, biologically
    implausible coefficient. No subsequent subgroup analysis (Iteration
    16) revealed an actionable olaparib-responsive subgroup.

  Osimertinib: top hits are alk_fusion (+0.24, p=0.017) and brca2_mutation
    (−0.25, p=0.05). The alk_fusion interaction reflects a small benefit
    in the rare osi+alk_fusion+ subset (n_tx≈1200; +0.23 mo, p=0.018), not
    the canonical EGFR-driven response.

================================================================
6. DEEP-DIVE: SOTORASIB SUBGROUP STRUCTURE (Iterations 13–15)
================================================================

Within the KRAS G12C+ subset (n≈6,400), I screened sotorasib × every
binary feature. The dominant secondary modifier is sex_female:

  Within KRAS G12C+:
    soto × sex_female: −4.66 mo (p<1e-300)
    Sotorasib effect in KRAS+ AND male:    +4.64 mo (p≈0)
    Sotorasib effect in KRAS+ AND female:  −0.012 mo (p=0.88)

  Sanity check — Sotorasib in KRAS-wild-type males: −0.019 (p=0.50).
  Sotorasib has NO effect outside the KRAS+ AND male subgroup, in
  any sex × KRAS combination tested.

Tertiary modifiers within KRAS+ AND male:

  feature           interaction (within KRAS+ subset)
  --------------------------------------------------
  alk_fusion        −3.02 (p<1e-17)   — ALK fusion attenuates benefit
  brca2_mutation    −2.55 (p<1e-9)    — BRCA2 mutation attenuates benefit
  has_brain_mets    −0.31 (p=0.052)   — borderline attenuation
  stk11_mutation    −0.26 (p=0.19)    — NS
  smoke_*           NS (no smoking modifier)

Best-defined dual-modifier subgroups (sotorasib effect on PFS within
KRAS G12C+ AND male AND additional restriction):

  + alk_fusion=0:        +4.83 mo (n_tx=1191, n_ctrl=2179, p≈0)
  + brca2_mutation=0:    +4.80 mo (n_tx=1198, n_ctrl=2203, p≈0)
  + has_brain_mets=0:    +4.70 mo (n_tx=945,  n_ctrl=1742, p≈0)
  + stk11_mutation=0:    +4.67 mo (n_tx=1089, n_ctrl=1942, p≈0)
  + tmb_high=1:          +4.66 mo (n_tx=394,  n_ctrl=788,  p<1e-127)

After multivariable adjustment for age, ECOG, stage IV, brain mets,
histology, smoking, albumin, LDH, NLR, STK11, and ALK within the
KRAS G12C+ AND male subgroup (n≈3400), the sotorasib coefficient
remains +4.59 mo (p<1e-300).

Within KRAS G12C+ AND male AND no-ALK-fusion AND any smoking history
(former or current), sotorasib effect = +4.85 mo (n_tx=1099, n_ctrl=1971).

================================================================
7. SUBGROUP SEARCHES FOR THE OTHER THREE TREATMENTS (Iteration 16)
================================================================

I searched single-feature subgroups for each remaining treatment to make
sure I had not missed a small-but-real subgroup of responders.

  Pembrolizumab — best single-feature subgroups (smallest p):
    stage_iv=0:        −0.10 mo (p=0.002)  ← negative point estimate
    egfr_mutation=0:   −0.05 mo (p=0.026)  ← negative point estimate
    adeno=0 (squamous): −0.07 mo (p=0.043) ← negative point estimate
  No single-feature subgroup shows a positive pembrolizumab effect.

  Olaparib — best single-feature subgroups:
    adeno=0:           −0.08 mo (p=0.051)  ← negative point estimate
    stk11_mutation=0:  −0.04 mo (p=0.083)
  No single-feature subgroup shows a positive olaparib effect.

  Osimertinib — best single-feature subgroups:
    alk_fusion=1:      +0.23 mo (p=0.018)  ← only positive signal, in
                                              ALK+ patients (small effect)
    stk11_mutation=1:  +0.06 mo (p=0.32)
  The osimertinib-in-ALK+ signal is small, was not preregistered, and
  is the sole nominal hit across many tests; it is not a credible
  efficacy signal.

================================================================
8. TREATMENT-TREATMENT INTERACTIONS (Iteration 17)
================================================================

  Within KRAS G12C+: sotorasib × pembrolizumab interaction = −0.22
    (p=0.12) — pembrolizumab does not meaningfully alter the sotorasib
    benefit.

  Within EGFR-mutant: osimertinib × pembrolizumab interaction = +0.25
    (p=0.033) — nominally significant but small, and not robust to
    multiplicity correction across the four treatment-by-treatment
    interactions tested. Not a basis for clinical action.

================================================================
9. FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT-HETEROGENEITY HYPOTHESES (Iteration 18)
================================================================

Sotorasib (well-supported, large effect):
  Sotorasib substantially increases pfs_months (~+4.6 mo) in patients
  who are KRAS G12C+ AND male (sex_female=0). The benefit is completely
  abolished in KRAS G12C+ AND female patients (effect ≈ 0) and absent
  in all KRAS G12C-wild-type patients regardless of sex. Within KRAS
  G12C+ males, ALK fusion (alk_fusion=1) and BRCA2 mutation
  (brca2_mutation=1) further attenuate the benefit; the cleanest
  responders are KRAS G12C+ AND male AND alk_fusion=0 AND
  brca2_mutation=0 (effect ~+4.8 mo). The dominant subgroup-defining
  predicates are kras_g12c=1 AND sex_female=0; alk_fusion=0 and
  brca2_mutation=0 are tertiary refinements.

Pembrolizumab (no efficacy anywhere):
  No subgroup defined by available biomarkers (pdl1_tps, tmb_high,
  stk11_mutation, kras_g12c, egfr_mutation, alk_fusion, brca2_mutation,
  sex_female, smoking_status, ecog_ps, stage_iv, has_brain_mets) shows
  a positive pembrolizumab pfs_months treatment effect. Pembrolizumab
  is essentially neutral throughout the cohort, with several subgroups
  showing a small negative point estimate.

Olaparib (no efficacy anywhere):
  No subgroup defined by available features (brca2_mutation,
  stk11_mutation, tmb_high, adeno, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, ecog_ps,
  egfr_mutation) shows a positive olaparib pfs_months treatment effect.
  Olaparib is essentially neutral throughout the cohort.

Osimertinib (no clinically meaningful efficacy):
  No subgroup defined by available features (egfr_mutation,
  stk11_mutation, tmb_high, adeno, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, alk_fusion)
  shows a meaningful positive osimertinib pfs_months treatment effect.
  Osimertinib's effect is essentially null in EGFR+ and EGFR-WT
  patients alike. The only weakly significant signal is +0.23 mo
  (p=0.018) in the alk_fusion=1 subset and a within-EGFR+
  pembro × osi interaction (+0.25, p=0.033); neither constitutes a
  clinically meaningful efficacy signal.

================================================================
10. OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
================================================================

(a) The dataset's treatment-vs-biomarker structure does NOT match the
    canonical NSCLC literature. Of the four pre-specified
    treatment–biomarker matches (osimertinib–EGFR, sotorasib–KRAS G12C,
    pembrolizumab–PDL1/TMB, olaparib–BRCA2), only sotorasib–KRAS G12C
    holds.

(b) The single dominant story in the data is sotorasib efficacy in
    KRAS G12C+ males. The same KRAS G12C+ males contributed a +4.6 mo
    PFS gain across virtually every secondary stratification — an
    extremely robust signal driven by ~3,400 KRAS+ males in the cohort.

(c) Female sex is a complete suppressor of sotorasib benefit within
    KRAS G12C+ patients (effect size drops from +4.64 to ~0). This
    sex-specific effect is unexpected from the biology of KRAS G12C
    inhibition and is the most important hypothesis-generating finding.

(d) Three of the four treatments (pembrolizumab, olaparib,
    osimertinib) appear inert in this dataset — there is no biomarker
    or clinical subgroup in which they extend pfs_months by a
    clinically meaningful amount. This includes the canonical
    biomarker-defined subgroups (PDL1-high, TMB-high, BRCA2+, EGFR+)
    where each of these drugs would be expected to show benefit.

(e) Stage of disease, ECOG performance status, brain metastases,
    histology (squamous worse than adeno), albumin, LDH, and weight
    loss are the dominant prognostic factors driving baseline pfs_months
    variation. Most "noise" labs (CRP, NLR, hemoglobin, AST, ALT,
    bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium) carry no
    independent prognostic information.
