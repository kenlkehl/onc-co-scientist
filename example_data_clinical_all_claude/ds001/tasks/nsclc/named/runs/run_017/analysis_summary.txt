================================================================
NSCLC Cohort Analysis Summary (ds001_nsclc, n=50,000)
Outcome: pfs_months (continuous, mean ≈ 3.43 months, range 0–15.3)
Treatments: pembrolizumab, sotorasib, olaparib, osimertinib (overlap allowed)
================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Across 25 iterations of propose-test-refine, I evaluated marginal main effects
of each treatment, of each biomarker, of demographic / staging / lab features,
plus all four treatment-by-feature interaction screens, plus systematic
subgroup-discovery within each treatment's matched-biomarker stratum, plus a
multivariable model. The dataset shows ONE strong, well-defined treatment
effect (sotorasib in a specific subgroup) and very clear, biologically large
prognostic signals from staging and host factors. The other three treatments
(pembrolizumab, olaparib, osimertinib) show no detectable PFS benefit in any
subgroup tested.

================================================================
1.  MAIN-EFFECT FINDINGS (iterations 1–5)
================================================================

1a. Marginal treatment effects on pfs_months
- Pembrolizumab vs not: delta = -0.035 mo, p = 0.081  -> NULL
- Sotorasib    vs not: delta = +0.322 mo, p ≈ 5.6e-49 -> SIGNIFICANT, modest
- Olaparib     vs not: delta = -0.025 mo, p = 0.26    -> NULL
- Osimertinib  vs not: delta = +0.004 mo, p = 0.87    -> NULL

Only sotorasib shows a positive marginal treatment effect.  This effect is
driven entirely by the KRAS G12C+ subgroup (see §3 below).

1b. Biomarker main effects on pfs_months (univariate)
- egfr_mutation+:    delta = +0.19 mo, p ≈ 6e-11    (modestly positive)
- kras_g12c+:        delta = +0.78 mo, p ≈ 7e-94    (positive, but this is
                                                     confounded by sotorasib
                                                     being administered to
                                                     KRAS+ patients)
- alk_fusion+:       delta = -0.81 mo, p ≈ 6e-66    (negative prognostic)
- stk11_mutation+:   delta = +0.01 mo, p = 0.74     (NULL marginally)
- brca2_mutation+:   delta = -0.12 mo, p = 0.031    (weak negative)
- tmb_high:          delta = -0.19 mo, p ≈ 1e-17    (negative — counter-
                                                     intuitive for ICI use,
                                                     consistent with no ICI
                                                     benefit in this dataset)
- pdl1_tps slope:    coef = +0.07, p = 0.16          (NULL)

1c. Demographics, performance status, staging
- Age (slope):       coef = +0.17 mo per year, p ~ 0  (POSITIVE — counter-clinical)
- Female sex:        delta = -0.20 mo, p ≈ 8e-25
- Squamous histology: delta = -0.86 mo, p ≈ 0          (negative prognostic)
- Current smoker:    delta = -0.63 mo, p ~ 0           (negative prognostic)
- Never smoker:      delta = +0.29 mo, p ~ 0           (positive prognostic)
- ECOG (slope):      coef = -1.10 mo per ECOG unit, p ~ 0  (large negative)
- Stage IV:          delta = -1.44 mo, p ~ 0
- Brain metastases:  delta = -0.92 mo, p ~ 0

1d. Labs
- Albumin (slope):           coef = +0.44, p ≈ 3e-109 (strong positive)
- Weight loss % (slope):     coef = -0.073, p ≈ 7e-179 (strong negative)
- LDH (slope):               coef = -5e-4, p ≈ 4e-5    (small negative)
- CRP, NLR, Hb, ALP, AST, ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, Na, K, Ca: all p > 0.1
  on a single-predictor OLS — no marginal signal.

1e. Multivariable adjustment (R² = 0.89)
The fully-adjusted OLS confirms the same prognostic ranking. Sotorasib is the
only treatment with a non-null adjusted coefficient (+0.32, p ~ 0). Pembro,
olap, osi are all near zero (|coef|<0.01, p>0.3). Age remains positive after
adjustment (~+0.17/year, p~0), which is unusual relative to real-world NSCLC
clinical experience and is presumably an artifact of how this synthetic
cohort was generated.

================================================================
2.  INTERACTION SCREENS (iterations 6–9, 15–18)
================================================================

I screened each treatment against each of 27 candidate modifiers (binary
mutations, PDL1, TMB, demographics, staging, all labs) using OLS treatment
+ modifier + treatment × modifier on pfs_months.

2a. Pembrolizumab — top interactions by p_int:
   weight_loss_pct_6mo  coef +0.018  p = 3e-4
   stage_iv             coef +0.108  p = 0.007
   kras_g12c            coef -0.114  p = 0.057
   ldh_u_l              coef +5e-4   p = 0.063
None of these survives multiple-testing correction across 27 tests, and the
weight-loss interaction is small (sub-month). PDL1, TMB, STK11, squamous,
smoking, EGFR, ALK, BRCA2 — all non-significant. There is NO embedded
pembrolizumab benefit in any obvious biomarker subgroup.

2b. Osimertinib:
   alk_fusion       coef +0.238  p = 0.017
   brca2_mutation   coef -0.254  p = 0.050
The headline signal — osi × egfr_mutation — has coef -0.020, p = 0.76.
Osimertinib does NOT preferentially benefit EGFR+ patients in this dataset.

2c. Sotorasib (THE big signal):
   kras_g12c    coef +2.558  p ≈ 0      <-- 1° driver
   sex_female   coef -0.562  p ≈ 5e-41  <-- 2° modifier
   alk_fusion   coef -0.488  p ≈ 4e-7   (small subgroup; competing-resource)
   egfr_mutation coef -0.241 p ≈ 1e-4   (likely sample-allocation artifact)
   brca2_mutation coef -0.334 p = 0.006

2d. Olaparib:
   bun_mg_dl   coef +0.011  p = 0.014
   crp_mg_l    coef +0.006  p = 0.022
None robust. Olaparib × BRCA2: coef +0.037, p = 0.78 — NO selective benefit
in the matched biomarker.

================================================================
3.  TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY — DEEP DIVE (iterations 10–14, 19–24)
================================================================

3a. Sotorasib in KRAS_G12C+ patients
- KRAS+ overall:   delta = +2.55 mo, p < 1e-200 (n_on=2246, n_off=4122)
- KRAS-:           delta = -0.005, p = 0.80     -> null

Splitting KRAS+ by every measured covariate:
   smoking (current/former/never):  +2.48 / +2.66 / +1.76, all p < 0.001
   ecog_ps (0/1/2):                 +2.47 / +2.64 / +2.49, all p < 1e-37
   histology (adeno/squamous):      +2.62 / +2.50, both p < 1e-71
   stage_iv (1/0):                  +2.51 / +2.61, both p < 1e-75
   has_brain_mets (1/0):            +2.33 / +2.63, both p < 1e-46
   pdl1_tps high/low (≥0.5/<0.5):   +2.62 / +2.53, both p < 1e-47
   tmb_high (1/0):                  +2.51 / +2.57, both p < 1e-65
   stk11_mutation (1/0):            +2.33 / +2.59, both p < 1e-25
   albumin high/low (≥/<median):    +2.43 / +2.70, both p < 1e-98
   sex_female (0/1) [MALE/FEMALE]:  +4.64 / -0.012,  p ≈ 0  /  p = 0.88

The benefit collapses entirely along sex.  In KRAS+ MALES (n_on=1237, n_off=2271)
sotorasib adds ~+4.64 months PFS; in KRAS+ FEMALES (n_on=1009, n_off=1851) the
effect is null.

A formal interaction model within the KRAS+ stratum:
   pfs ~ sotorasib + sex_female + sotorasib:sex_female
   sotorasib coef          = +4.644  p ≈ 0
   sotorasib:sex_female    = -4.656  p ≈ 0
The interaction term is essentially the negative of the main effect, i.e. the
sotorasib effect in females equals zero. A 3-way model adding STK11 found NO
additional significant interactions (sot:stk11 p=0.44; 3-way p=0.49). STK11
status does NOT modify the sotorasib effect in this dataset, contrary to
real-world Skoulidis-style biology.

A clean subgroup definition that maximizes effect size and excludes very rare
confounding co-mutations:
   kras_g12c=1 AND sex_female=0 AND alk_fusion=0 AND brca2_mutation=0
   n_on = 1154, n_off = 2112
   mean PFS on sotorasib = 7.18, off = 2.20, delta = +4.99 months, p ≈ 0

3b. Osimertinib in EGFR_MUTATION+ patients
EGFR+ overall (n_on=1973, n_off=4544): delta = -0.014, p = 0.81 -> NULL
Stratifying EGFR+ by every covariate (sex, smoking, ECOG, brain mets,
histology, STK11): all deltas in [-0.25, +0.21], all p > 0.10. No subgroup
shows any osimertinib benefit. The dataset does NOT contain an osimertinib
treatment-effect signal.

3c. Olaparib in BRCA2_MUTATION+ patients
BRCA2+ overall (n_on=417, n_off=1102): delta = +0.010, p = 0.93 -> NULL
Stratifying BRCA2+ by sex, ECOG, histology, albumin: all deltas |Δ|<0.27,
all p > 0.1. No olaparib benefit in any subgroup.

3d. Pembrolizumab — extensive subgroup search
- pdl1_tps ≥ 0.5:                       delta = -0.07, p = 0.11
- tmb_high:                              delta = -0.05, p = 0.20
- pdl1_tps ≥ 0.5 AND tmb_high:           delta = +0.08, p = 0.30
- pdl1_tps ≥ 0.5 AND tmb_high AND adeno: delta = +0.11, p = 0.29
- No-actionable-driver (egfr-, alk-, kras-, brca2-): delta = -0.03, p = 0.18
- pdl1≥0.5 AND stk11-:                   delta = -0.10, p = 0.04 (negative direction)
- ecog 0/1/2:                            delta = +0.03 / -0.07 / -0.01
- male / female:                         delta = -0.04 / -0.02
None of these subgroups shows a meaningful positive pembrolizumab effect on
PFS. Several "candidate-positive" subgroups (PDL1-high, TMB-H, the Skoulidis
PDL1-high/STK11- subgroup) show non-significant or even mildly negative
deltas. The dataset does NOT contain a pembrolizumab treatment-effect signal.

3e. Monotherapy sanity check (single-treatment vs no-treatment)
- pure-pembro (n=8030) vs no-treat (n=7903): delta = -0.020, p = 0.54   -> NULL
- pure-sotorasib (n=4304) vs no-treat:        delta = +0.339, p ≈ 2e-14  -> +
- pure-olaparib (n=3382) vs no-treat:         delta = -0.061, p = 0.16   -> NULL
- pure-osimertinib (n=3374) vs no-treat:      delta = -0.001, p = 0.98   -> NULL

This confirms the picture above: only sotorasib has any treatment effect, and
even pure-sotorasib monotherapy averages only +0.34 months vs no treatment
(because most pure-sotorasib patients are KRAS-).

================================================================
4.  HYPOTHESIS-BY-HYPOTHESIS BOX SCORE
================================================================

Supported (signed direction confirmed at p<0.05):
+ ECOG ↑ → PFS ↓
+ Stage IV → PFS ↓
+ Brain metastases → PFS ↓
+ Squamous histology → PFS ↓
+ Current smoking → PFS ↓
+ Never smoking → PFS ↑
+ Albumin ↑ → PFS ↑
+ Weight loss % ↑ → PFS ↓
+ LDH ↑ → PFS ↓
+ Female → PFS ↓ (small)
+ KRAS G12C+ → marginally PFS ↑ (artifact of sotorasib confounding)
+ ALK fusion → PFS ↓ (negative prognostic)
+ TMB-high → PFS ↓ (counter-clinical, likely synthetic-data idiosyncrasy)
+ Sotorasib × KRAS_G12C interaction (positive)
+ Sotorasib × sex_female interaction (negative — within KRAS+ benefit confined to males)

Refuted / null (where a positive directional hypothesis was tested):
- Pembrolizumab benefit overall, in PDL1-high, in TMB-H, in PDL1-high/TMB-H,
  in PDL1-high/STK11-, in no-driver, in any ECOG / sex / histology stratum.
- Pembrolizumab × PDL1 interaction (null).
- Pembrolizumab × TMB-H interaction (null).
- Olaparib benefit overall and in BRCA2+ (null).
- Olaparib × BRCA2 interaction (null).
- Osimertinib benefit overall and in EGFR+ (null).
- Osimertinib × EGFR interaction (null).
- STK11 as a suppressor of pembrolizumab (null).
- STK11 as a suppressor of sotorasib (null — KRAS+/STK11+ still gets +2.33 mo).
- Higher age → shorter PFS (REVERSED in this dataset; age is positive).

================================================================
5.  FINAL TREATMENT–BIOMARKER–OUTCOME CONCLUSIONS
================================================================

The single replicable, large-magnitude treatment effect in this cohort is:

    *** SOTORASIB IMPROVES pfs_months BY ~+4.6 to +5.0 MONTHS ONLY IN
        PATIENTS WITH kras_g12c = 1 AND sex_female = 0. ***

  - In kras_g12c=1 AND sex_female=0:                 delta = +4.64, p ≈ 0
  - In kras_g12c=1 AND sex_female=0 AND alk_fusion=0
       AND brca2_mutation=0 (clean):                 delta = +4.99, p ≈ 0
  - In kras_g12c=1 AND sex_female=1:                 delta = -0.01, p = 0.88  (NULL)
  - In kras_g12c=0 (any sex):                        delta = -0.01, p = 0.80  (NULL)

The complete subgroup definition includes BOTH the matched biomarker
(kras_g12c=1) AND the unfavorable suppressor variable (sex_female=1) — the
sotorasib effect is suppressed entirely in females, even when the matched
biomarker is positive. STK11 mutation, ALK fusion, BRCA2 mutation, EGFR
mutation, brain metastases, ECOG, smoking, histology, stage, PDL1, TMB,
albumin, and weight loss do NOT modify the sotorasib benefit beyond what is
explained by sex.

The other three treatments (pembrolizumab, olaparib, osimertinib) showed NO
detectable PFS benefit in any subgroup tested, including their canonical
biomarker matches (PDL1/TMB-high, BRCA2, EGFR respectively). These appear to
be embedded in the cohort as "decoy" or null treatments. The strongest
prognostic features of pfs_months are ECOG performance status, stage IV,
brain metastases, squamous histology, current smoking status, weight loss,
serum albumin, and ALK fusion status.
