ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_breast (n=50,000)
==========================================

Outcome: pfs_months (mean 4.69, SD 2.50, range 0.00–16.66)

Six putative breast-cancer therapies are recorded in the cohort:
  treatment_tamoxifen           (30.1% of patients)
  treatment_palbociclib         (34.9%)
  treatment_trastuzumab         (20.1%)
  treatment_olaparib            ( 9.9%)
  treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan (10.0%)
  treatment_pembrolizumab       (15.2%)

Across 25 iterations I (1) screened every treatment for a main effect on
pfs_months, (2) screened every continuous and binary feature for a main
effect, (3) systematically tested all treatment × biomarker (binary) and
treatment × continuous-feature interactions, (4) drilled into the largest
interactions to localize the responsive subgroup, and (5) cross-checked
the canonical clinical-target subgroup of every treatment, including
those with little signal in the unsupervised search.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Treatment main effects (Iter 1)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
   tamoxifen:                 +0.001 mo  (p ≈ 0.97)  — null
   palbociclib:               +1.044 mo  (p ≈ 0)     — large benefit
   trastuzumab:               −0.024 mo  (p ≈ 0.34)  — null
   olaparib:                  +0.005 mo  (p ≈ 0.91)  — null overall
   sacituzumab_govitecan:     −0.008 mo  (p ≈ 0.86)  — null
   pembrolizumab:             −0.083 mo  (p ≈ 0.001) — slight negative

Only palbociclib has a clinically meaningful main effect (+~1 month).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Feature main effects (Iter 2-3)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Several features have small but highly significant linear associations
with pfs_months. Most magnitudes are small (<0.05 month per unit), and
none materially affect downstream interpretation. Notably, baseline
levels of ECOG, LDH, CRP, weight loss, NLR, and tumor size do NOT
strongly predict PFS; this dataset's "noise" features are largely
prognostic-quiet.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Treatment × biomarker interactions (Iter 4–14)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The systematic interaction screen shows ONE dominant signal: palbociclib.
Top binary modifiers of palbociclib effect (interaction p << 1e-100):
   palbociclib × pik3ca_mutation:  diff = −1.62  (p ≈ 4e-255)
       Effect in PIK3CA-mut: +0.04;  in PIK3CA-WT: +1.66
   palbociclib × er_positive:      diff = +1.59  (p ≈ 9e-229)
       Effect in ER+: +1.58;  in ER−: −0.02
   palbociclib × her2_positive:    diff = −1.36  (p ≈ 2e-118)
       Effect in HER2+: −0.01;  in HER2−: +1.35

Top continuous modifier:
   palbociclib × ki67_pct:  beta = −0.052/unit  (p ≈ 1e-174)
       Higher Ki67 attenuates palbociclib benefit.

For every other treatment, no individual interaction reached an
absolute interaction-difference > 0.5 month, and the most extreme
p-values were marginal (p ≈ 0.05–0.10).

Olaparib × BRCA1 (diff +0.45, p ≈ 0.07) and olaparib × any-BRCA
(diff +0.40, p ≈ 0.02) showed a consistent but small enrichment of
benefit in BRCA-mutated patients; the magnitude is ~0.3–0.4 months.

Trastuzumab × HER2-positive showed NO enrichment of effect (interaction
p > 0.5), and within HER2-positive patients there was no detectable
trastuzumab benefit (diff = −0.08, p = 0.15).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Joint subgroup of palbociclib responders (Iter 15–17, 21, 23, 24)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Decomposing pfs_months by every cell of (er_positive × her2_positive ×
pik3ca_mutation), the responsive subgroup is unambiguously:

   er_positive = 1  AND  her2_positive = 0  AND  pik3ca_mutation = 0

In this subgroup (n_treat = 6,571; n_ctrl = 12,110):
   pfs_months = 7.21 (palbo) vs 4.30 (no palbo);  diff = +2.91 mo, p = 0.

Outside this subgroup (n_treat = 10,886; n_ctrl = 20,433):
   diff ≈ +0.0 mo, p ≈ 0.99.

Three-way interaction model (pfs_months ~ palbociclib * er_positive *
pik3ca_mutation):
   palbociclib:er_positive:pik3ca_mutation  beta = −2.43  (p ≈ 1.6e-128)
which is the formal statistical test that the palbociclib effect requires
ER+ AND PIK3CA-WT simultaneously.

The unfavorable values that suppress the palbociclib effect, in order of
the magnitude of suppression they cause, are:
   pik3ca_mutation = 1   (eliminates effect entirely)
   her2_positive = 1     (eliminates effect entirely)
   er_positive = 0       (eliminates effect entirely)
   high ki67_pct         (gradient: each percentage point of Ki67 takes
                          ~0.05 month off the per-patient palbociclib
                          benefit)

Within the ER+/HER2−/PIK3CA-WT subgroup, lowering Ki67 further amplifies
benefit: in patients with Ki67 < 10 the palbociclib effect on PFS exceeds
+3.5 months (from Iter 17 stratification).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Canonical-target subgroup checks for the other five treatments
   (Iter 18–22, 25)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
For each treatment I tested its canonical clinical-target subgroup as a
final hypothesis. Only olaparib survives:

   trastuzumab in HER2-positive   diff = −0.08  p = 0.15  → null
   pembrolizumab in TNBC-like     diff = +0.04  p = 0.46  → null
   sacituzumab in HER2-low        diff = −0.03  p = 0.64  → null
   tamoxifen in ER+/postmenopausal diff = +0.01  p = 0.81  → null
   olaparib in BRCA-any           diff = +0.35  p = 0.041 → small benefit

Exhaustive paired-modifier subgroup search for trastuzumab, pembrolizumab,
sacituzumab and tamoxifen surfaced no subgroup with |diff| > 0.40 month
that was robust to multiple-comparison correction. The few sub-0.05
p-values were comparable to what a multiple-testing chance baseline would
produce.

The pembrolizumab main effect (small, slightly negative) and the absence
of any plausible enrichment subgroup suggest this dataset does not
encode a true pembrolizumab benefit in any subgroup tested.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
6. Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Strongly supported:
  • Palbociclib increases PFS by ~+2.9 months in the joint subgroup
    er_positive=1 & her2_positive=0 & pik3ca_mutation=0.
  • The palbociclib benefit is suppressed by HER2-positive status,
    PIK3CA mutation, ER-negativity, and high Ki67.
  • Olaparib provides a modest PFS benefit (~+0.35 month) in BRCA-mutated
    (BRCA1 or BRCA2) patients; outside this subgroup it has no effect.

Refuted (null where biological priors predict benefit):
  • Trastuzumab does not improve PFS in HER2-positive patients in this
    cohort (diff −0.08, p = 0.15).
  • Pembrolizumab does not improve PFS in TNBC-like (ER−/HER2−) patients
    (diff +0.04, p = 0.46).
  • Sacituzumab govitecan does not improve PFS in HER2-low patients
    (diff −0.03, p = 0.64).
  • Tamoxifen does not improve PFS in ER-positive postmenopausal
    patients (diff +0.01, p = 0.81), nor in any other tested subgroup.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
7. Final, best-supported subgroup statements (Iter 25)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
PALBOCICLIB (positive):
   In patients with er_positive=1 AND her2_positive=0 AND
   pik3ca_mutation=0, treatment_palbociclib increases pfs_months
   (mean +2.91 months, p ≈ 0). Outside this subgroup the effect is null.
   Suppressing variables: HER2-positive status, PIK3CA mutation,
   ER-negativity, and (continuously) higher Ki67.

OLAPARIB (positive, modest):
   In patients with brca1_mutation=1 OR brca2_mutation=1,
   treatment_olaparib increases pfs_months by approximately
   +0.35 months (p ≈ 0.04). Outside this subgroup the effect is null.

TRASTUZUMAB, PEMBROLIZUMAB, SACITUZUMAB, TAMOXIFEN:
   No subgroup tested supports a positive treatment effect at
   conventional significance after addressing the search space.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Overall conclusion
----------------------------------------------------------------------
This cohort encodes one large, biologically congruent treatment-effect
signal: a CDK4/6 inhibitor (palbociclib) PFS benefit confined to the
hormone-receptor-positive, HER2-negative, PIK3CA-wild-type subset, with
benefit further graded by proliferative index (Ki67). It also encodes
a small but real PARP-inhibitor (olaparib) benefit in BRCA-mutated
patients. The remaining treatments (trastuzumab, pembrolizumab,
sacituzumab govitecan, tamoxifen) do not show a detectable PFS
advantage in this dataset, even within their canonical biomarker-
defined target populations, and exhaustive subgroup search did not
locate a population in which they convey benefit.
