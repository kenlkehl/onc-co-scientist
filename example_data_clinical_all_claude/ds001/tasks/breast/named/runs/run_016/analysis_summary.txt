Analysis summary — ds001_breast (n=50,000; outcome pfs_months)
================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Across 25 iterations we explored the structure of the cohort (50,000 patients,
38 features, single PFS-in-months outcome). The work moved from main-effect
screens, to adjusted multivariable regression, to systematic treatment-by-feature
interaction screens, and finally to refined subgroup definitions for each
treatment with usable variation. Analyses used Spearman rank correlations,
two-sample t-tests, and ordinary-least-squares regression with treatment-by-
feature interaction terms. Statistical significance is reported at p<0.05;
effect direction is the sign of the OLS coefficient on the natural PFS scale
(months).

PROGNOSTIC MAIN EFFECTS (iterations 1–3, 16)
--------------------------------------------
Strongly prognostic (adjusted multivariable OLS, R²=0.79):
- age_years            +0.176 mo per year (Spearman rho=+0.726 unadjusted; p≈0).
                       In this cohort older age is associated with longer PFS,
                       opposite of the usual clinical direction — likely a
                       feature of how the cohort was generated.
- ecog_ps              -1.17 mo per level (mean PFS by ECOG: 0=5.64, 1=4.44,
                       2=3.29).
- stage_iv=1           -1.55 mo (3.62 vs 5.15 unadjusted).
- has_brain_mets=1     -0.98 mo (3.75 vs 4.80 unadjusted).
- er_positive=1        +0.53 mo (positive prognostic).
- her2_positive=1      -0.47 mo.
- pik3ca_mutation=1    -0.58 mo.
- ki67_pct             -0.019 mo per percent.
- albumin_g_dl         +0.48 mo per g/dL.
- weight_loss_pct_6mo  -0.077 mo per percent.
- ldh_u_l              -0.0005 mo per U/L (small but significant).

Null / trivial features (no meaningful adjusted effect): sex_female,
node_positive, postmenopausal, brca1_mutation (very small -0.07, marginal),
brca2_mutation, her2_low (small +0.11 unadjusted, vanishes after adjustment),
pr_positive (small +0.36 unadjusted, gone adjusted), tumor_size_cm, hemoglobin,
AST, ALT, total_bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium,
crp_mg_l, nlr, alkaline_phosphatase. Many of these are highly significant
unadjusted because of correlations with the strong prognostic features above,
but become negligible after adjustment.

TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS (iteration 4, 5)
---------------------------------------
Marginal (unadjusted) PFS differences (treated minus control, t-test):
- treatment_palbociclib            +1.10 mo  (p≈0)        ← clear positive
- treatment_tamoxifen              +0.04 mo  (p=0.08)
- treatment_trastuzumab            -0.06 mo  (p=0.03)
- treatment_olaparib               -0.04 mo  (p=0.33)
- treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan  -0.01 mo  (p=0.85)
- treatment_pembrolizumab          -0.07 mo  (p=0.02)

Adjusted (multivariable OLS with all six treatments + 18 features, R²=0.79):
- palbociclib            +1.10 mo  (p≈0)        ← preserved
- sacituzumab_govitecan  +0.034 mo (p=0.05)     ← marginal small +
- tamoxifen              -0.008    (p=0.48)     null
- trastuzumab            -0.009    (p=0.47)     null
- olaparib               -0.026    (p=0.12)     null
- pembrolizumab          -0.020    (p=0.17)     null

Only palbociclib has a clinically meaningful adjusted main effect; sacituzumab
shows a small positive signal that motivates a heterogeneity search.

TREATMENT × FEATURE INTERACTION SCREEN (iterations 6–8, 14)
-----------------------------------------------------------
For each of 6 treatments × 22 features (binary + median-split continuous), we
fit OLS with all six treatments, prognostic covariates, the feature, and a
treatment×feature interaction. Strong, replicable interactions (all p<1e-50
unless noted):

Palbociclib heterogeneity:
- palbo × er_positive       β=+1.56  (p≈0)   benefit only in ER+
- palbo × her2_positive     β=-1.34  (p≈0)   no benefit in HER2+
- palbo × pik3ca_mutation   β=-1.67  (p≈0)   no benefit in PIK3CA-mut
- palbo × pr_positive       β=+1.03  (p≈0)
- palbo × her2_low          β=+0.38  (p=3e-67)
- palbo × ki67_high(>median 12.2%) β=-1.57 (p≈0)   benefit attenuates as ki67 rises

Sacituzumab govitecan heterogeneity:
- sac × has_brain_mets      β=+0.24  (p=3e-5) benefit only in patients with
                                              brain metastases

All other treatment×feature interactions had |β|<0.1 and p>1e-3, with only
isolated marginal signals (sac×pr_positive p=0.02; pembro×postmenopausal p=0.05;
olap×node_positive p=0.05; tras×brca2 p=0.04 — none survive multiple-comparison
context and none are biologically coherent).

CANONICAL-SUBGROUP CONFIRMATION FOR NULL TREATMENTS (iteration 9, 17, 21–22)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Subgroup-restricted, prognostic-adjusted OLS:
- Trastuzumab in HER2+ (n=9060):       β=+0.001 (p=0.83)  null
- Olaparib in BRCA1/2+ (n=2499):        β=-0.005 (p=0.95)  null
- Pembrolizumab in TNBC (n=10,496):     β=+0.008 (p=0.13)  null
- Tamoxifen in ER+ (n=34,886):          β=+0.003 (p=0.83)  null

Even in their canonical molecular subgroups, none of these four agents shows a
PFS benefit in this dataset. Where unadjusted differences appeared (e.g.,
olaparib +0.35 in BRCA-any) they evaporated after adjusting for prognostic
imbalances (BRCA carriers are younger, lower ECOG, etc., which suppress PFS
relative to non-carriers in this cohort, biasing the unadjusted comparison).

PALBOCICLIB SUBGROUP — REFINEMENT TO COMPLETE DEFINITION (iterations 7, 10–11, 18, 20)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Step 1 (er_positive × her2_positive × pik3ca_mutation):
- ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt subgroup (n=18,681): palbo benefit +2.91 mo
                                           (palbo PFS 7.21 vs 4.30)
- Complement (n=31,319):                  palbo benefit +0.003 mo

Step 2 (add ki67_pct cutoff): within the triple-marker subgroup the benefit
varied sharply with ki67_pct (continuous palbo × ki67 interaction within
subgroup β=-0.138/% , p≈0):

  Bin (ki67 %)        n_palbo  n_ctrl  palbo benefit (mo)
  (0,5]                658     1308     +4.91
  (5,10]              1929     3467     +4.98
  (10,15]             1511     2702     +4.11
  (15,20]              907     1744     +0.10   ← effect collapses
  (20,25]              595     1142     -0.26
  (25,30]              344      588     +0.12
  (30,40]              339      635     +0.11
  (40,100]             288      524     +0.14

A natural cutoff is ki67_pct < 15.

Step 3 (final responder subgroup): er_positive=1 AND her2_positive=0 AND
pik3ca_mutation=0 AND ki67_pct<15.
- Subgroup (n=11,490, palbo n=4,070):  palbo PFS 8.97 vs control 4.29
                                       adjusted β=+4.71 mo (p<1e-300)
- Complement (n=38,510):              adjusted β=-0.0004 (p=0.86)

Within the responder subgroup the benefit is uniform across stage_iv,
has_brain_mets, postmenopausal, pr_positive, her2_low, brca1, brca2 (palbo
benefit ranges +2.53 to +3.04 across these strata) — so the four-variable
definition above is the complete subgroup.

SACITUZUMAB GOVITECAN SUBGROUP (iterations 12, 13, 19)
------------------------------------------------------
The strongest sacituzumab interaction is with has_brain_mets:
- has_brain_mets=1 (n=5,090, sac n=490): adjusted β=+0.244 mo (p=5e-6),
                                          mean PFS sac 3.90 vs control 3.74
- has_brain_mets=0 (n=44,910):           unadjusted Δ=-0.03; effect ≈0

Within brain mets, the unadjusted sacituzumab benefit is amplified in ER+
(+0.32), PR+ (+0.36), HER2- (+0.20), PIK3CA-wt (+0.25), and ki67_low (+0.43)
strata, suggesting the same hormone-receptor-positive milieu that drives the
palbociclib effect may also enrich sacituzumab response. However, the formal
three-way sacituzumab × has_brain_mets × er_positive interaction is not
significant (p=0.78) — sample size in the joint subgroup is small (n_sac=115
in brain_mets+ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt/ki67<15) and the marginal sacituzumab × bm
interaction (p=0.03) does not reliably need further partition. The simplest
defensible responder definition is therefore has_brain_mets=1 alone.

TREATMENTS WITH NO HETEROGENEITY OR MAIN EFFECT (iterations 8, 9, 17, 21, 22)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- treatment_tamoxifen — null overall and within ER+ and ER+/premenopausal
  subgroups; no significant interactions with any feature.
- treatment_trastuzumab — null overall and within HER2+; no biomarker
  interaction reaches p<0.05 reliably.
- treatment_olaparib — null overall, null in BRCA-any, and the BRCA
  interaction is null (β=-0.003, p=0.97) after adjustment.
- treatment_pembrolizumab — null overall; weak unadjusted positive trend in
  TNBC (Δ=+0.07) does not survive adjustment (β=+0.008, p=0.13); no
  significant biomarker interaction.

ADDITIVITY OF PROGNOSTIC EFFECTS (iteration 23)
-----------------------------------------------
Pairwise and three-way interactions among ecog_ps, stage_iv, has_brain_mets
in adjusted models are all non-significant (p>0.05). Their negative effects
on PFS are well captured as additive.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. PROGNOSTIC MODEL: PFS in this cohort is well explained (adjusted R²≈0.79)
   by additive contributions from age (+), ECOG (-), stage IV (-), brain mets
   (-), ER+ (+), HER2+ (-), PIK3CA mutation (-), ki67 (-), albumin (+),
   weight loss (-), and LDH (-). Most lab values and several traditional
   prognostic features (PR, her2_low, BRCA, node, postmenopausal, sex, tumor
   size) contribute negligibly after adjustment.

2. DOMINANT TREATMENT EFFECT (palbociclib): The single largest treatment
   effect-size and the cleanest heterogeneity story is for palbociclib. Its
   PFS benefit is essentially confined to the subgroup
        er_positive=1 AND her2_positive=0 AND pik3ca_mutation=0 AND ki67_pct<15
   where the adjusted benefit is approximately +4.7 months. Outside this
   subgroup the adjusted palbociclib effect is statistically and clinically
   zero.

3. SECONDARY TREATMENT EFFECT (sacituzumab govitecan): Sacituzumab provides
   a small but consistent PFS benefit (+0.24 month adjusted; p=5e-6) only in
   patients with has_brain_mets=1. Outside this subgroup the effect is null.
   Within brain mets, the benefit appears further enriched in ER+/PR+/HER2-/
   PIK3CA-wt/low-ki67 patients but the higher-order interactions are not
   significant; has_brain_mets=1 alone is the simplest sufficient definition.

4. FOUR DRUGS WITH NO DETECTABLE BENEFIT: Tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib,
   and pembrolizumab show no PFS benefit overall, no benefit in their
   canonical molecular subgroups (ER+, HER2+, BRCA+, TNBC), and no robust
   biomarker interactions. Apparent unadjusted differences are driven by
   confounding by indication / prognostic imbalances rather than treatment
   effects.

FINAL BEST-SUPPORTED TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESES
---------------------------------------------------------
H58 (palbociclib): In patients with er_positive=1 AND her2_positive=0 AND
     pik3ca_mutation=0 AND ki67_pct<15, treatment_palbociclib increases
     pfs_months by approximately +4.7 months versus no palbociclib (effect
     ~0 outside this subgroup).

H59 (sacituzumab govitecan): In patients with has_brain_mets=1,
     treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan increases pfs_months by approximately
     +0.24 months versus no sacituzumab (effect ~0 outside this subgroup).

H60 (no benefit): In this cohort, treatment_tamoxifen, treatment_trastuzumab,
     treatment_olaparib, and treatment_pembrolizumab show no detectable PFS
     benefit in any subgroup, including their canonical biomarker-defined
     subgroups (ER+, HER2+, BRCA-mutated, and TNBC respectively).
