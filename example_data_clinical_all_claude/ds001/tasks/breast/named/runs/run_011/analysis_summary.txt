Analysis summary for ds001_breast (n = 50,000; outcome = pfs_months)
=====================================================================

Overview
--------
Across 25 iterations I probed the cohort for (a) prognostic main effects on
pfs_months, (b) overall treatment effects, (c) treatment-by-feature interactions,
and (d) joint multi-feature subgroup definitions for each treatment. Treatments
appear to be approximately randomly assigned (treatment-vs-feature contingency
checks show <1 percentage-point imbalances on every biomarker), so unadjusted
subgroup contrasts are reasonable estimators of treatment effect. A multivariable
OLS fit of pfs_months on all features and treatments achieves R^2 = 0.79.


1. Prognostic main effects (iterations 1-6)
-------------------------------------------
The strongest independent prognostic effects on pfs_months (multivariable OLS;
all p < 1e-15) are:

  Negative (shorter PFS):
    ecog_ps                  -1.17 mo per ECOG point
    stage_iv                 -1.55 mo
    has_brain_mets           -0.98 mo
    her2_positive            -0.47 mo
    pik3ca_mutation          -0.58 mo
    weight_loss_pct_6mo      -0.077 mo per percent
    ki67_pct                 -0.019 mo per %
    ldh_u_l                  -0.0005 mo per U/L

  Positive (longer PFS):
    er_positive              +0.53 mo
    age_years                +0.176 mo per year (older patients have LONGER
                                                 PFS in this cohort, contrary
                                                 to typical expectation)
    albumin_g_dl             +0.48 mo per g/dL

Univariate-significant features that became null in the multivariable fit
(pr_positive, her2_low, postmenopausal, brca1/2 mutations, most lab chemistries)
were attenuated by their correlations with the dominant features above. Notably
brca1_mutation and brca2_mutation are not independently prognostic.


2. Overall treatment effects (iteration 7)
------------------------------------------
Univariate Welch t-tests of pfs_months by treatment status:

    treatment_palbociclib            +1.10 mo  (p ~ 0)         <-- only large effect
    treatment_tamoxifen              +0.04 mo  (p = 0.077)
    treatment_olaparib               -0.04 mo  (p = 0.33)
    treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan  -0.01 mo  (p = 0.85)
    treatment_trastuzumab            -0.06 mo  (p = 0.029)
    treatment_pembrolizumab          -0.07 mo  (p = 0.018)

Only palbociclib shows a clinically meaningful overall benefit. The others are
within ~0.1 mo of null (a few are weakly negative); this motivated heterogeneity
analyses to find subgroups in which any of the other agents might work.


3. Treatment-effect heterogeneity (iterations 8-22)
---------------------------------------------------
For each of the six treatments I screened all binary, ordinal, and continuous
features for treatment-by-feature interactions in covariate-adjusted OLS models,
then followed up the strongest signals with subgroup contrasts and exhaustive
2- and 3-feature subgroup searches.

3a. Palbociclib — strong, well-defined subgroup
   Significant interactions (all p ~ 0):
       palbociclib x er_positive       coef = +1.56
       palbociclib x pr_positive       coef = +1.03
       palbociclib x her2_positive     coef = -1.34
       palbociclib x pik3ca_mutation   coef = -1.67
       palbociclib x ki67 (per SD)     coef = -0.65

   Layered subgroup refinement of the palbociclib effect:
       overall                                            +1.10 mo
       ER+                                                 +1.58 mo
       ER+ HER2-                                           +1.92 mo
       ER+ HER2- PIK3CA-wt                                 +2.91 mo
       ER+ HER2- PIK3CA-wt PR+                             +2.95 mo
       ER+ HER2- PIK3CA-wt PR+ low Ki67 (<median ~14.6%)   +5.01 mo

   Outside this maximally refined subgroup the palbociclib effect is only
   +0.35 mo (still statistically nonzero because n is large, but ~14x smaller).
   Adjusted-interaction confirmation for the (ER+ AND HER2- AND PIK3CA-wt) joint
   modifier: interaction coefficient +2.92, p ~ 0; treatment coefficient outside
   the subgroup is essentially zero.

   This pattern is biologically coherent: palbociclib (a CDK4/6 inhibitor) is
   indicated in HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer and is known to lose efficacy
   when PIK3CA-driven escape pathways are active or when proliferation (Ki67) is
   high. The data recapitulate that pattern with very high precision.

3b. Sacituzumab govitecan — moderate effect in brain mets / low Ki67
   The strongest interaction was sacit x has_brain_mets (adjusted coef +0.24,
   p = 3e-5). Stratified subgroup contrasts:
       overall                                  -0.01 mo (null)
       has_brain_mets=1                         +0.16 mo (p=0.18; n_t=490)
       has_brain_mets=1 AND low Ki67            +0.43 mo (p=0.025; n_t=239)
       ER+ AND has_brain_mets AND low Ki67      +0.72 mo (p=0.003; n_t=169)
   Adjusted-interaction confirmation for (brain_mets AND low_ki67):
       coef = +0.30, p = 1.7e-4.
   The benefit is small in absolute terms (~0.4 mo) but reproducible across
   several adjacent subgroup definitions sharing the brain-mets + low-Ki67
   backbone.

3c. Olaparib — borderline benefit in BRCA-mutated patients
   Subgroup contrasts:
       overall                       -0.04 mo (null)
       BRCA1+                        +0.40 mo (p=0.12; n_t=120)
       BRCA2+                        +0.30 mo (p=0.19; n_t=119)
       BRCA1+ OR BRCA2+              +0.35 mo (p=0.041; n_t=239)
       BRCA wild-type                -0.06 mo (p=0.14)
   Subgroup-only contrast is borderline (p=0.041), but the covariate-adjusted
   interaction test for olaparib x (BRCA1 or BRCA2) is not significant
   (p=0.59). The clinically expected directionality is present but the
   evidence is weak — likely a small true effect masked by limited n in
   the mutated group.

3d. Trastuzumab — no detectable subgroup benefit
   Despite trastuzumab's well-known clinical activity in HER2+ disease, the
   interaction with her2_positive is null in this dataset:
       overall                          -0.06 mo
       HER2+                            -0.08 mo (p=0.15; n_t=1890)
       HER2+ AND ER-                    -0.06 mo (p=0.55)
       HER2+ AND ER+                    -0.09 mo (p=0.18)
   Exhaustive 2-/3-feature subgroup search produced no subgroup with diff
   above 0.5 mo that survived multiple-testing scrutiny. I conclude
   trastuzumab has no demonstrable PFS benefit in this cohort.

3e. Pembrolizumab — no positive subgroup; mildly harmful in frail patients
   The strongest 2-/3-feature subgroups in the exhaustive search were all
   *negative*, dominated by ECOG=2 plus low albumin / low Ki67 (frail
   patients). Examples:
       PR+ AND ECOG=2 AND low Ki67           -0.45 mo (p=0.001)
       PIK3CA-wt AND ECOG=2 AND low albumin  -0.43 mo (p=0.002)
   TNBC subgroup: +0.07 mo (p=0.22). TNBC + brain mets: -0.09 (p=0.63).
   TNBC + high Ki67 + high LDH: -0.04 (p=0.69). I conclude pembrolizumab
   has no positive PFS subgroup in this cohort and is associated with
   slightly worse outcomes in frailty-marked subgroups (which may reflect
   immune-related-adverse-event toxicity in poor-PS patients).

3f. Tamoxifen — no robust benefit; borderline BRCA1+ signal
   Overall and canonical (ER+, ER+/postmenopausal) subgroup tests are null.
   The exhaustive search highlighted several BRCA1+ subgroups with diff
   ~+0.4-0.5 mo and p ~ 0.02-0.05, but these were not robust to multiple
   testing and the directly-tested BRCA1+ ER+ subgroup gave diff +0.29
   (p=0.12). I treat tamoxifen as having no robust pfs_months benefit
   in this cohort.


4. Final subgroup hypotheses (iterations 23-25)
-----------------------------------------------
Best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypotheses by treatment:

  treatment_palbociclib        STRONG: +5.0 mo PFS in
                               er_positive=1 AND her2_positive=0 AND
                               pik3ca_mutation=0 AND pr_positive=1 AND
                               ki67_pct<median (~14.6%).
                               Effect outside this subgroup ~+0.35 mo.

  treatment_sacituzumab_govit. MODEST: +0.4 mo PFS in
                               has_brain_mets=1 AND ki67_pct<median.
                               Adjusted-interaction p=1.7e-4.

  treatment_olaparib           BORDERLINE: +0.35 mo PFS in
                               brca1_mutation=1 OR brca2_mutation=1.
                               Subgroup p=0.041; adjusted-interaction p=0.59.

  treatment_trastuzumab        NULL: no detectable PFS benefit, including
                               in HER2+ subgroups; clinically surprising
                               but consistent across all stratifications.

  treatment_pembrolizumab      NULL/HARMFUL: no positive PFS subgroup;
                               negative associations concentrated in frail
                               (ECOG=2 + low albumin / low Ki67) subgroups.

  treatment_tamoxifen          NULL: no canonical hormone-receptor benefit;
                               BRCA1+ signal borderline and not robust.


5. Other notable observations
-----------------------------
- age_years has a clearly POSITIVE association with pfs_months (slope +0.18 mo
  per year, both univariate and multivariable). This is opposite to typical
  clinical expectation and is a striking dataset feature; it persists after
  controlling for postmenopausal, ECOG, stage, biomarkers, and treatment.

- brca1_mutation and brca2_mutation are not independently prognostic in the
  multivariable model.

- The continuous lab chemistries (sodium, potassium, calcium, BUN, creatinine,
  AST, ALT, total bilirubin, hemoglobin, alkaline phosphatase, NLR, CRP) carry
  little or no independent prognostic information once the major clinical and
  biomarker variables are in the model. Only ldh_u_l survives at p<0.001.


Conclusion
----------
The cohort exhibits one dominant, biologically coherent treatment-effect
heterogeneity story: palbociclib confers a large PFS benefit (~5 months) in
HR+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt/low-Ki67 patients and essentially no benefit elsewhere.
Two additional treatments show small but plausibly real subgroup effects
(sacituzumab in brain-mets/low-Ki67 patients; olaparib in BRCA-mutated
patients). The remaining three treatments (trastuzumab, pembrolizumab,
tamoxifen) showed no demonstrable positive PFS subgroup in this cohort.
