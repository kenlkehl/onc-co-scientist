ds001_breast — Analysis Summary
================================

Dataset: 50,000 oncology patient records (breast cancer cohort) with 31 patient features,
6 treatment indicators, and one outcome (pfs_months, progression-free survival in months).
Protocol: 25 iterations of propose-test-refine, with at least one systematic
treatment-effect heterogeneity search per treatment.

Headline finding
----------------
Of the six treatments studied, only treatment_palbociclib shows a robust treatment
effect on PFS, and that effect is sharply concentrated in a 4-feature joint subgroup:

    er_positive=1  AND  her2_positive=0  AND  pik3ca_mutation=0  AND  ki67_pct < 14

Within this quad-marker subgroup (n_on ≈ 3,829; n_off ≈ 6,923), palbociclib lengthens
mean PFS by approximately +4.94 months (mean_on = 9.23 vs mean_off = 4.28, p < 1e-300).
Outside this subgroup, the unadjusted palbociclib effect on PFS is +0.016 months
(p = 0.48 — indistinguishable from zero). After multivariable adjustment (age, ECOG,
stage, brain mets, albumin, weight loss, LDH, PR, HER2-low, BRCA1/2, postmenopausal,
tumor size, CRP), the palbociclib × quad interaction coefficient is +4.97 months
(p < 1e-300), and the palbociclib main effect outside the quad is +0.008 months
(p ≈ 4e-4 — near-zero residual that is statistically detectable only because of the
large sample).

Each of the 4 markers is necessary. Leave-one-out: dropping the ER+ requirement
shrinks the diff to +3.5; dropping HER2- to +4.1; dropping PIK3CA-WT to +3.2;
dropping low-Ki67 to +2.9. The within-complement test confirms each is required:
holding the other three markers, switching the marker from 1 to 0 collapses the
palbociclib benefit to ~0 (e.g., when other 3 markers are met but ER+=0,
palbociclib diff = -0.03; when PIK3CA-WT=0, diff = -0.05; when HER2+=1, diff = +0.02).
For Ki67 the threshold is sharp around ki67 ≈ 14 — within ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT,
palbociclib gives diff = +4.94 mo for ki67 < 14 vs +0.07 mo for ki67 ≥ 14.

Walkthrough by iteration
------------------------

Iter 1 (treatment main effects).
Unadjusted, palbociclib showed a large positive PFS difference (+1.10 mo, p < 1e-300).
Trastuzumab (-0.06, p = 0.029) and pembrolizumab (-0.07, p = 0.018) showed small
negative associations consistent with confounding by indication. Tamoxifen, olaparib,
and sacituzumab govitecan were near-null in unadjusted analyses.

Iter 2 (continuous main effects).
PFS was strongly positively correlated with age_years (r = +0.70, p ≈ 0) — likely a
calendar/duration artifact in this dataset rather than a biological effect — and
with albumin (r = +0.10). PFS was negatively correlated with ki67_pct (r = -0.09)
and weight_loss_pct_6mo (r = -0.12). Most lab features had small or null univariate
correlations.

Iter 3 (binary main effects + ECOG).
Strong prognostic features (all p < 1e-50): er_positive (+0.57 mo), pr_positive
(+0.36), her2_positive (-0.46), pik3ca_mutation (-0.56), stage_iv (-1.54),
has_brain_mets (-1.05), and a smaller positive effect for her2_low (+0.11).
ECOG was strongly graded (mean PFS 5.55 / 4.46 / 3.41 for ECOG 0/1/2; F-test
p ≈ 0).

Iter 4 (multivariable OLS).
A single OLS of pfs_months on all 31 features and 6 treatments achieved R² = 0.79.
Strongest adjusted predictors matched univariate directions: age_years +0.176/yr,
ecog_ps -1.17/level, stage_iv -1.55, has_brain_mets -0.98, er_positive +0.53,
her2_positive -0.47, pik3ca_mutation -0.58, albumin +0.48, weight_loss
-0.077/percent, ki67_pct -0.019/percent, ldh -0.0005/U/L. Among treatments only
palbociclib retained a clear adjusted main effect (+1.10 mo, p < 1e-300) at this
stage, while trastuzumab/pembrolizumab/olaparib/sacituzumab/tamoxifen were null
(|coef| ≤ 0.05 mo, p > 0.05).

Iter 5 (pre-specified treatment-biomarker pairings).
The clinically expected pairings:
  - palbociclib × ER+: STRONG positive interaction (+1.59, p ≈ 1e-228) — eff in
    ER+ = +1.58, in ER- = -0.02.
  - olaparib × BRCA1: borderline positive (+0.45, p = 0.066).
  - tamoxifen × ER+, tamoxifen × postmenopausal, trastuzumab × HER2+,
    sacituzumab × HER2-low, pembrolizumab × brain mets: ALL null (interaction
    p > 0.27). The expected biomarker pairings for these treatments do NOT
    materialize in this dataset.

Iters 6-11 (per-treatment interaction screening, all 31 candidate modifiers each).
For each treatment we screened OLS interaction terms (treat × modifier).
  - Palbociclib (iter 7) lit up multiple modifiers: pik3ca_mutation (interaction
    -1.62, p ≈ 4e-255), er_positive (+1.59, p ≈ 9e-229), ki67_pct (-0.052/percent,
    p ≈ 1e-174), her2_positive (-1.36, p ≈ 2e-118), pr_positive (+1.09,
    p ≈ 3e-117), her2_low (+0.42, p ≈ 9e-19). Direction in each case was
    "palbociclib helps when the marker is favorable (ER+, HER2-, PIK3CA-WT,
    low Ki67)".
  - Olaparib (iter 9): top hits were noisy laboratory markers (creatinine
    interaction p = 0.005, ALT p = 0.024) and BRCA1 (p = 0.066). No
    overwhelming biomarker emerged.
  - Trastuzumab (iter 8): strongest interaction was tumor_size (p = 0.018) but
    no clinically interpretable modifier; HER2 status itself was NULL as a
    modifier (consistent with iter 5).
  - Sacituzumab (iter 10): albumin interaction p = 0.013 — likely confounding;
    HER2-low not a modifier.
  - Pembrolizumab (iter 11): borderline ER+ interaction (p = 0.05) in the
    "harmful in ER+" direction; ECOG p = 0.05 ("worse with higher ECOG").
    No clinically interpretable benefit subgroup.
  - Tamoxifen (iter 6): weak stage_iv interaction (p = 0.02); no consistent
    benefit subgroup.

Iter 12 (palbociclib joint subgroup ladder).
Building from the ER+, HER2-, PIK3CA-WT, low-Ki67 hits, we built the joint
subgroup ladder:
  ER+ alone:                                 +1.58 mo
  PIK3CA-WT alone:                           +1.66
  ER+ AND HER2-:                             +1.92
  ER+ AND PIK3CA-WT:                         +2.38
  ER+ AND HER2- AND PIK3CA-WT (triple):      +2.91 (n_on = 6,571)
  triple AND ki67 < median (15.0):           +4.95 (n_on = 3,271)
  triple AND ki67 ≥ median:                  +0.88
  Negative controls: ER- diff -0.02 (p = 0.63), PIK3CA-mut diff +0.04 (p = 0.29),
  HER2+ diff -0.01 (p = 0.77).

Iter 13 (joint interaction OLS).
Triple-marker (ER+ AND HER2- AND PIK3CA-WT) × palbociclib: interaction +2.91
(p ≈ 0); palbociclib effect inside triple = +2.91, outside = +0.003 (p = 0.92).
Quad-marker (triple AND ki67 < 15.0) × palbociclib: interaction +4.74 (p ≈ 0);
inside quad = +4.95, outside = +0.21.

Iter 14 (exhaustive 1-/2-/3-modifier subgroup search per treatment).
For each treatment we enumerated all 1-, 2-, and 3-modifier combinations of the
12 binary biomarker features (and their negations) requiring ≥50 patients per
arm, then ranked subgroups by t-statistic. For palbociclib the top subgroup
was "ER+ AND HER2- AND PIK3CA-WT" with diff = +2.91 (top 4 entries are minor
permutations of this combination). For the other 5 treatments, no 3-modifier
subgroup yielded an effect that even approached the magnitudes seen for
palbociclib; the largest was olaparib in "BRCA1 AND not_sex_female AND not_brain_mets"
with diff = +0.97 mo (n_on = 59, p = 0.0045) — a small subgroup with high
variance.

Iter 15 (olaparib BRCA deep-dive).
BRCA1 alone diff = +0.40 (p = 0.12); BRCA2 alone +0.30 (p = 0.19); BRCA either
+0.35 (p = 0.041); BRCA either AND TNBC +0.74 (p = 0.0097). Unadjusted BRCA
either × olaparib interaction p = 0.021.

Iter 16 (trastuzumab subgroups).
In every meaningful subgroup of HER2+ (ER+, ER-, postmenopausal, stage IV,
no brain mets, node-positive, PIK3CA-WT) trastuzumab's diff was slightly
negative (-0.05 to -0.12 mo) and not statistically significant. There is NO
subgroup in which trastuzumab provides a positive PFS benefit in this dataset.

Iter 17 (pembrolizumab and sacituzumab subgroups).
In TNBC, TNBC + high Ki67, TNBC + PIK3CA-WT, HER2-low, HER2-low + ER-, high
NLR, brain-mets, stage IV, stage IV + TNBC, PIK3CA-mut: ALL diffs were small
(|diff| < 0.20 mo) and statistically insignificant for both treatments.

Iter 18 (palbociclib leave-one-out + Ki67 sweep).
Leave-one-out (drop one marker requirement at a time): drop ER+ → +3.52;
drop HER2- → +4.08; drop PIK3CA-WT → +3.22; drop low-Ki67 → +2.91. Each
relaxation reduces the magnitude, confirming all 4 are necessary for the
maximal effect. Within-complement test (other 3 markers held): switching the
4th marker from 1 to 0 collapses the effect to ≈ 0 in 3 of the 4 cases
(ER+/-, HER2-/+, PIK3CA-WT/mut); for low-Ki67 the residual when ki67 ≥ med is
+0.88, which prompted the Ki67 threshold sweep:
  ki67 < 14 → diff +4.94 (n_on = 3,829);  ki67 ≥ 14 → diff +0.07 (n_on = 2,742)
  ki67 < 15 → diff +4.69;                   ki67 ≥ 15 → diff +0.02
  ki67 < 18 → diff +4.10;                   ki67 ≥ 18 → diff -0.05
The sharp threshold sits very close to ki67 = 14.

Iter 19 (adjusted quad interaction).
Adjusted for age, ECOG, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, albumin, weight_loss, LDH:
palbo × quad coef = +4.77 (p < 1e-300); palbo main outside quad = +0.20
(p ≈ 4e-268, but a tiny effect on the absolute scale); palbo effect inside
quad = +4.98 mo. R² = 0.95.

Iter 20 (olaparib refinement).
BRCA1+ only diff +0.40 (p = 0.12); BRCA2+ only +0.30; BRCA1+ AND BRCA2+
(very rare) +0.94 (n_on = 6); BRCA either AND ER- TNBC-leaning +0.83
(p = 0.0012); BRCA either AND no brain mets +0.36 (p = 0.040). However,
fully adjusted BRCA × olaparib interaction (with age, ECOG, stage, brain
mets, albumin, weight loss, LDH, ER, HER2, PIK3CA, Ki67) collapsed to
+0.04 (p = 0.62) — the BRCA-subgroup signal does NOT survive adjustment
and is most parsimoniously explained by prognostic-feature imbalance and/or
chance with small subgroup sizes.

Iter 21 (adjusted main effects for non-palbociclib treatments).
After full adjustment for 16 prognostic features:
  trastuzumab          coef = -0.011 (SE 0.014), p = 0.42 — null
  pembrolizumab        coef = -0.023 (SE 0.016), p = 0.14 — null
  sacituzumab          coef = +0.032 (SE 0.019), p = 0.087 — borderline-positive but small
  tamoxifen            coef = -0.003 (SE 0.012), p = 0.82 — null
None reaches statistical significance after adjustment.

Iter 22 (trastuzumab interaction screening within HER2+).
Within HER2+ (n = 9,060), the smallest interaction p-value across all 30
candidate modifiers was 0.007 for LDH (likely chance over many tests), and
the adjusted main effect of trastuzumab restricted to HER2+ was +0.001 mo
(SE 0.005, p = 0.80). Even within the indication-defined HER2+ population,
trastuzumab has no detectable PFS benefit in this dataset.

Iter 23 (final palbociclib quad with refined Ki67 threshold).
With ki67 < 12: diff = +4.96; with ki67 < 13: +4.93; with ki67 < 14: +4.94;
with ki67 < 15: +4.69. The natural threshold is ki67 = 14 (best magnitude per
patient included). Adjusted (with 14 covariates) palbo × quad14 interaction =
+4.97 (p < 1e-300); inside-quad effect +4.98; outside-quad effect +0.008
(p = 4e-4, essentially zero on the absolute scale). Outside the quad, the
unadjusted palbociclib diff is +0.016 mo (p = 0.48 — null).

Iter 24 (olaparib in TNBC + BRCA, adjusted).
TNBC + BRCA either: unadjusted diff = +0.74 (p = 0.0097, n_on = 43, n_off = 556).
TNBC + BRCA either + PR-: +0.75. BRCA1 only TNBC: +0.60 (p = 0.16). BRCA2
only TNBC: +0.85 (p = 0.031). Negative control (TNBC + not BRCA): -0.05
(p = 0.45). However, adjusted within TNBC for age, ECOG, stage, brain mets,
albumin, weight loss, LDH, PIK3CA, Ki67, PR: olaparib × brca_any coefficient
= -0.016 (p = 0.60). The BRCA × olaparib interaction in TNBC does NOT
survive adjustment, leaving olaparib without a defensible benefit subgroup.

Iter 25 (final consolidated null tests + exhaustive 4-way for palbociclib).
Adjusted main coefficients across 9 (treatment, restriction) combinations:
  trastuzumab in all                  -0.011 (p = 0.42)
  trastuzumab in HER2+                +0.001 (p = 0.80)
  pembrolizumab in all                -0.023 (p = 0.14)
  pembrolizumab in TNBC               +0.007 (p = 0.13)
  sacituzumab in all                  +0.032 (p = 0.087)
  sacituzumab in TNBC                 +0.003 (p = 0.60)
  sacituzumab in HER2-low             +0.063 (p = 0.062)
  tamoxifen in all                    -0.003 (p = 0.82)
  tamoxifen in ER+                    +0.002 (p = 0.91)
None significant. An exhaustive 4-way subgroup search for palbociclib
(including ki67_low14 as a marker) over 7,281 4-way combinations confirmed
that the top 10 combinations are all minor permutations of "ER+ AND HER2-
AND PIK3CA-WT AND ki67<14" with diffs in the range +4.0 to +5.0 months.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------

Strongly supported:
  - Palbociclib provides large PFS benefit, but only in
    ER+ AND HER2- AND PIK3CA-WT AND ki67_pct<14. Effect ~+5 months. Outside
    this subgroup the effect is null. Each of the 4 markers is necessary.
  - Stage IV, brain mets, higher ECOG, weight loss, lower albumin, higher
    Ki67, HER2-positivity, and PIK3CA-mutation are each independently
    associated with shorter PFS (multivariable OLS, p << 0.001).
  - ER-positive and PR-positive status are each independently associated with
    longer PFS, with a very large difference for ER+ (~+0.5 mo adjusted).
  - Strong positive association between age_years and pfs_months (r = +0.70).
    [Likely a dataset-level artifact rather than biological — older patients
    in this cohort have systematically longer PFS for unstated reasons.]

Refuted (or null):
  - Tamoxifen × ER-positive interaction: NULL.
  - Tamoxifen × postmenopausal interaction: NULL.
  - Trastuzumab × HER2-positive interaction (and main effect within HER2+):
    NULL — trastuzumab shows no PFS benefit anywhere in this dataset, even
    in the on-label HER2+ subgroup.
  - Olaparib × BRCA1 / BRCA2 interaction: unadjusted positive in BRCA-mutated
    (and especially in BRCA + TNBC), but the interaction does NOT survive
    multivariable adjustment for prognostic features. The most parsimonious
    interpretation is no real treatment effect for olaparib.
  - Sacituzumab govitecan × HER2-low interaction: NULL. No subgroup of
    sacituzumab shows a meaningful benefit.
  - Pembrolizumab × brain mets / TNBC / PIK3CA-WT: NULL. No subgroup of
    pembrolizumab shows a meaningful benefit.

Final best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypotheses (one per treatment)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. treatment_palbociclib (positive subgroup):
     Within the joint subgroup defined by
       er_positive=1 AND her2_positive=0 AND pik3ca_mutation=0 AND ki67_pct < 14,
     treatment_palbociclib lengthens mean pfs_months by approximately +4.94
     months (mean_on = 9.23 vs mean_off = 4.28). Outside this subgroup the
     effect is statistically indistinguishable from zero. Each of the four
     markers (ER+, HER2-, PIK3CA-WT, low Ki67) is necessary; relaxing any one
     reduces the effect, and switching any one to its complement (with the
     other three held) collapses the effect to ≈ 0.

2. treatment_olaparib:
     No subgroup with an adjustment-robust benefit. The strongest unadjusted
     subgroup ("BRCA1 or BRCA2 mutation AND ER-negative AND HER2-negative",
     i.e., BRCA-mutated TNBC) showed an unadjusted +0.74-month diff (n_on = 43)
     but this signal does not survive multivariable adjustment within TNBC
     (adjusted interaction p = 0.60). Best honest hypothesis: no real
     PFS-modifying effect of olaparib in this cohort.

3. treatment_trastuzumab:
     No subgroup with a positive PFS benefit. Even within HER2+ (the on-label
     population), the adjusted main effect is +0.001 mo (p = 0.80). Best
     hypothesis: trastuzumab has no detectable PFS effect in this dataset.

4. treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan:
     No subgroup with a positive PFS benefit. The largest (borderline)
     subgroups were HER2-low (adjusted coef +0.063, p = 0.062) and overall
     adjusted main effect (+0.032, p = 0.087) — both small and not
     statistically significant.

5. treatment_pembrolizumab:
     No subgroup with a positive PFS benefit. Adjusted main effects in all
     populations and all biomarker subgroups (TNBC, PIK3CA-WT, brain mets,
     high NLR) are null.

6. treatment_tamoxifen:
     No subgroup with a positive PFS benefit. Adjusted main effects in all
     and in ER+ are essentially zero (p > 0.8 in both).

Methodological notes
--------------------

- Outcome (pfs_months) is treated as a continuous variable; t-tests and OLS
  were used throughout. No survival censoring is encoded in the data, so we
  treated PFS as a complete-case continuous outcome.
- Multivariable adjustment used statsmodels OLS with the full prognostic
  covariate set (age, ECOG, stage, brain mets, albumin, weight loss, LDH,
  and biomarker features). Adjusted analyses change conclusions in two
  important ways:
    (a) Most apparent unadjusted negative associations of treatments
        (trastuzumab -0.06, pembrolizumab -0.07) collapse toward zero,
        consistent with confounding by indication (these treatments tend
        to be given to patients with worse prognostic profiles).
    (b) The unadjusted olaparib × BRCA signal collapses, suggesting it was
        driven by prognostic differences across BRCA-mutated patients
        (or by chance with small subgroup sizes).
- Heterogeneity search included: pre-specified treatment-biomarker pairings,
  per-treatment screening across all 31 candidate modifiers, exhaustive
  enumeration of 1-, 2-, and 3-modifier subgroups for each of the 6
  treatments, joint multivariable interaction models, ki67 threshold
  sweep, and exhaustive 4-modifier subgroup search for palbociclib.
- The palbociclib quad-marker hypothesis is robust to multiple verification
  approaches and is the dominant finding of this analysis.
