Analysis Summary — ds001_breast (n=50,000)
===========================================

Outcome: pfs_months (continuous, range 0–16.7, mean 4.69, SD 2.50).
Treatments examined: tamoxifen, palbociclib, trastuzumab, olaparib, sacituzumab-govitecan, pembrolizumab.

1. PROGNOSTIC MAIN EFFECTS
--------------------------
A multivariable OLS model on all 37 features explained 79% of PFS variance. The strongest prognostic factors were:

  Positive predictors (longer PFS):
    - age_years: +0.176 mo per year (the dominant single predictor; r=0.70 unadjusted)
    - er_positive=1: +0.53 mo (adjusted)
    - albumin_g_dl: +0.48 mo per g/dL

  Negative predictors (shorter PFS):
    - stage_iv=1: -1.55 mo
    - ecog_ps: -1.17 mo per level (ANOVA F=2861)
    - has_brain_mets=1: -0.98 mo
    - pik3ca_mutation=1: -0.58 mo
    - her2_positive=1: -0.47 mo
    - weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.077 mo per %
    - ki67_pct: -0.019 mo per %

  Null/trivial: sex_female, postmenopausal, node_positive (after adjustment), her2_low, BRCA1/2 (each), tumor_size_cm, most labs (LDH, CRP, NLR, hemoglobin, AST/ALT/bilirubin/creatinine/BUN/electrolytes).

Hormone-receptor-positive disease is favorable; HER2-positive and PIK3CA-mutant disease is unfavorable; ER+ status confers ~+0.5 mo independently of other features.

2. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS (UNCONDITIONAL)
-----------------------------------------
Only palbociclib showed a large unconditional positive PFS effect (+1.10 mo, p<1e-300). The other five treatments showed essentially null or slightly negative unconditional effects:
  - tamoxifen: +0.04 mo (p=0.08)
  - trastuzumab: -0.06 mo (p=0.029)
  - olaparib: -0.04 mo (p=0.33)
  - sacituzumab-govitecan: -0.01 mo (p=0.85)
  - pembrolizumab: -0.07 mo (p=0.018)

After full multivariable adjustment, only palbociclib remained robustly positive (+1.10 mo, p<1e-300). Sacituzumab adjusted effect was tiny (+0.03, p=0.049). All others were null in the adjusted main-effect model.

3. TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY (HIGHEST-IMPACT FINDING)
----------------------------------------------------------
Palbociclib's effect is profoundly subgroup-restricted. A systematic interaction screen and exhaustive 1–3-feature subgroup search produced an unambiguous biomarker definition:

  ** ER-positive AND HER2-negative AND PIK3CA-wildtype **
  (n=18,681; 6,571 palbociclib-treated)
  Adjusted palbociclib effect: +2.92 months PFS (p<1e-300)

The benefit:
  - is preserved across age, sex, postmenopausal, node, stage IV, brain mets, and BRCA strata (range +2.55 to +3.04 mo, all p<1e-29 in subgroup t-tests).
  - vanishes when any one of the three biomarker conditions fails:
      * ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-mut: +0.00 mo (p=0.62)
      * ER+/HER2+ (any PIK3CA): +0.02 mo (p=0.71)
      * ER-/HER2- (any PIK3CA): -0.00 mo (p=0.99)
      * ER-/HER2+ (any PIK3CA): -0.09 mo (p=0.27)
  - is attenuated by higher Ki67 (palbo×ki67_pct interaction coef -0.053, p<1e-300), so within the responsive subgroup, low-proliferation tumors derive the largest benefit.
  - shows highly significant 3-way interaction palbociclib × er_positive × pik3ca_mutation (within HER2-negative): coef -2.91, p≈0.

PIK3CA mutation is the single most important effect-modifier outside the receptor classification: it converts a +2.9 mo benefit into 0. This is the dominant treatment-effect signal in the dataset.

4. OTHER TREATMENT–BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS
-----------------------------------------
Sacituzumab-govitecan × has_brain_mets (positive interaction, p=0.009):
  - In brain_mets+ (n=5,090; 490 SG-treated): adjusted SG +0.179 mo (p=0.004); unadjusted SG +0.162 mo (p=0.18, marginal).
  - Within brain_mets+, the benefit is concentrated in er_positive=1 patients: +0.236 mo adjusted (p=0.008); brain_mets+/ER- patients show no benefit (-0.07 mo, p=0.71).
  - The 3-way interaction sacituzumab × brain_mets × er_positive borderline (coef +0.20, p=0.14) — heterogeneity within brain_mets is suggestive but not formally significant.

  Final SG subgroup definition: has_brain_mets=1 (with secondary concentration in er_positive=1).

Olaparib × BRCA mutation:
  - Unadjusted, BRCA-mut patients (n=2,499) gain +0.35 mo on olaparib (p=0.04).
  - Within BRCA-mut, the strongest unadjusted signal is BRCA-mut + ER- (n=750, 55 treated): +0.83 mo (p=0.001).
  - All BRCA-mut signals attenuate sharply after multivariable adjustment for age, ECOG, stage, brain mets, ER, HER2, albumin, ki67 (adjusted coef +0.024, p=0.78 in BRCA-mut overall; +0.033 in BRCA-mut/ER-, p=0.54).
  - The richly adjusted ola×BRCA interaction is non-significant (coef +0.045, p=0.61).
  - Net: olaparib has at most a small, possibly real BRCA-restricted benefit that is largely confounded by patient/disease factors in this cohort. Not a robust subgroup.

Pembrolizumab:
  - Unconditional effect is slightly negative (-0.07 mo, p=0.018).
  - Stratified, the direction reverses by subtype: TNBC subgroup +0.07 mo (p=0.22), non-TNBC -0.11 mo (p=0.002).
  - Within TNBC, the unadjusted effect is concentrated in premenopausal patients (TNBC/postmenopausal=0): +0.275 mo (p=0.003) — but this attenuates to +0.021 mo (p=0.16) after age/ECOG adjustment, and the 3-way interaction pembro×tnbc×postmenopausal is null (p=0.85).
  - Pembrolizumab × weight_loss_pct_6mo within TNBC: coef +0.0087 (p=0.004), suggesting larger benefit in cachectic TNBC patients (small absolute magnitude).
  - Net: NO robust adjusted pembrolizumab subgroup.

Trastuzumab:
  - HER2+/HER2- stratification yielded no benefit in either group (HER2+: -0.08 mo, p=0.15; HER2-: -0.05, p=0.10).
  - HER2+ × ER, × stage, × brain mets, × BRCA, × PIK3CA all null.
  - Adjusted trastuzumab in HER2+: +0.011 mo (p=0.27).
  - Net: trastuzumab does NOT prolong PFS in this dataset, even in the biologically expected HER2+ subgroup.

Tamoxifen:
  - No significant interaction with er_positive (interaction p=0.77; ER+ subgroup adjusted coef -0.004, p=0.84) or postmenopausal (interaction p=0.75).
  - No significant continuous-biomarker interactions in ER+.
  - Net: tamoxifen does NOT have a detectable PFS subgroup effect.

5. SYSTEMATIC SUBGROUP SEARCH SUMMARY
-------------------------------------
An enumeration of all 1-, 2-, and 3-feature binary-variable subgroups (12 features × 8 binary combinations) produced thousands of candidate subgroups per treatment. Filtering for |effect| > 0.2 mo AND p < 0.001:

  - palbociclib: many positive subgroups, ALL subsumed by ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt. Several equivalent labels (ER+/HER2-low/PIK3CA-wt, HR+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt, etc.) pick out essentially the same patients.
  - sacituzumab-govitecan: no subgroup met the threshold by raw t-test; brain-mets emerges only via formal interaction modeling (consistent direction, smaller magnitude).
  - pembrolizumab: no subgroup met the threshold.
  - olaparib: no positive subgroup met the threshold; the BRCA-mut/ER- signal is below it.
  - trastuzumab: only spurious negative subgroups.
  - tamoxifen: no subgroup met the threshold.

6. SUPPORTED vs REFUTED HYPOTHESES
----------------------------------
Supported by strong adjusted evidence:
  - Older age, ER positivity, higher albumin → longer PFS.
  - Higher ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, HER2 positivity, PIK3CA mutation, weight loss, higher Ki67 → shorter PFS.
  - Palbociclib prolongs PFS dramatically and SPECIFICALLY in ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt; PIK3CA mutation suppresses the benefit.
  - Sacituzumab-govitecan modestly prolongs PFS in patients with brain metastases (with the benefit concentrated in ER+ within brain mets).
  - Higher Ki67 reduces palbociclib benefit even within the responsive subgroup.

Refuted (or null) treatment-subgroup hypotheses:
  - Tamoxifen prolongs PFS in ER+ disease — REFUTED (adjusted +/-0.0 mo).
  - Trastuzumab prolongs PFS in HER2+ disease — REFUTED.
  - Olaparib prolongs PFS in BRCA-mutated disease — at most marginal, attenuates after adjustment.
  - Pembrolizumab prolongs PFS in TNBC — at most marginal in TNBC/premenopausal, attenuates after adjustment.

7. FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY CLAIMS
----------------------------------------------
Per outcome (pfs_months) the best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypotheses are:

  - **Palbociclib**: prolongs pfs_months by +2.9 months in patients with er_positive=1 AND her2_positive=0 AND pik3ca_mutation=0 (ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wildtype). Effect is essentially zero outside this subgroup; PIK3CA mutation is the critical effect-suppressing variable distinguishing responders from non-responders within ER+/HER2-.

  - **Sacituzumab-govitecan**: prolongs pfs_months by ~+0.18–0.24 months in patients with has_brain_mets=1, with the within-brain-mets benefit concentrated in er_positive=1 patients.

  - **Olaparib**: no robust adjusted subgroup; weakest unadjusted positive signal in brca_mut=1 + er_positive=0.

  - **Pembrolizumab**: no robust adjusted subgroup; weakest unadjusted positive signal in TNBC + postmenopausal=0.

  - **Trastuzumab**: no benefit in any subgroup including her2_positive=1.

  - **Tamoxifen**: no benefit in any subgroup.

OVERALL CONCLUSION
------------------
The dominant treatment–biomarker–outcome relationship in ds001_breast is the palbociclib ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wildtype effect. Observable PFS in this cohort is driven mostly by host/disease prognostics (age, ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, ER/HER2/PIK3CA status, albumin, weight loss, Ki67), with ER+ being protective and HER2+/PIK3CA-mut being harmful. Among the six treatments, palbociclib alone shows a large, biomarker-restricted benefit (~+2.9 months in the ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt subgroup, abolished by PIK3CA mutation or HER2 positivity). Sacituzumab-govitecan shows a small but robust positive effect in patients with brain metastases. Olaparib, pembrolizumab, trastuzumab, and tamoxifen show no robust adjusted PFS benefit in any subgroup tested.
