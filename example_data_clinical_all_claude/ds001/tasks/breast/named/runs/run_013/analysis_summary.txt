Analysis summary — ds001_breast (n = 50,000; outcome = pfs_months)
===================================================================

Cohort overview (iteration 1)
-----------------------------
The cohort comprises 50,000 patients with a single continuous outcome,
pfs_months (mean 4.69, median 4.54, SD 2.50). Receptor distribution:
er_positive 69.8%, pr_positive 63.9%, her2_positive 18.1%, her2_low
37.1%. Triple-negative (er-/pr-/her2-) make up 21.0%; HR+/HER2-
(er+ or pr+, her2-) make up 60.9%. BRCA1 mutation 2.6%, BRCA2 mutation
2.4%. Stage IV 29.9%; brain mets 10.2%. Treatments are coded as
binary "received" indicators: tamoxifen 30.1%, palbociclib 34.9%,
trastuzumab 20.1%, olaparib 9.9%, sacituzumab_govitecan 10.0%,
pembrolizumab 15.2%. Notably, sex_female is only 44.7% — unusually
low for a breast cancer cohort but accepted at face value.

Prognostic landscape (iterations 2-5, 20)
------------------------------------------
Stage IV (mean PFS 3.62 vs 5.15, diff -1.54, p≈0) and brain mets
(3.75 vs 4.80, diff -1.05, p≈10^-178) carry the largest negative
prognostic burden among binary clinical features; node_positive,
postmenopausal, and sex_female show no PFS difference. Among
continuous predictors, age_years has a strong positive association
with PFS (slope +0.176/yr, r=+0.70, p≈0), ecog_ps has a strong
negative association (-1.18 per unit, r=-0.32, p≈0), ki67_pct
(-0.019/pt, p~10^-93) and weight_loss_pct_6mo (-0.075/pt, p~10^-148)
also show negative associations. tumor_size_cm has essentially no
relationship to PFS (r≈0). Among biomarkers, er_positive (+0.57
months, p≈10^-143) and pr_positive (+0.36 months, p≈10^-59) are
positively associated with PFS while her2_positive (-0.46 months,
p~10^-70) and pik3ca_mutation (-0.56 months, p~10^-144) are
negatively associated. brca1_mutation and brca2_mutation show no
detectable association with PFS. Among labs, albumin_g_dl
(slope +0.50/g·dL, p~10^-112) is the dominant lab signal; ldh_u_l,
alkaline_phosphatase_u_l, and calcium_mg_dl show weak negative
associations; crp_mg_l, nlr, and the renal/hepatic panels are
essentially uninformative on a marginal basis. A multivariable
prognostic OLS confirmed age_years (+0.166), ecog_ps (-0.962),
stage_iv (-1.34), has_brain_mets (-0.70), albumin_g_dl (+0.30),
weight_loss_pct_6mo (-0.044) as the dominant covariates (R²≈0.70).

Treatment main effects (iterations 6-7)
---------------------------------------
Unadjusted, only treatment_palbociclib showed a substantial PFS
difference: mean 5.41 on vs 4.31 off, diff +1.10 months, p≈0. The
other five treatments showed point estimates within ±0.07 months
and at most marginal p-values. After adjustment for the prognostic
covariate set, palbociclib retained a +1.10-month effect (95% CI
+1.08 to +1.12, p≈0); tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib, sacituzumab,
and pembrolizumab adjusted coefficients were all within ±0.04 months
of zero and not statistically significant (smallest p ≈ 0.055 for
pembrolizumab — directionally negative, consistent with channelling
into more advanced disease).

Treatment x biomarker interaction screen (iterations 8-14)
----------------------------------------------------------
A systematic grid of treatment x biomarker / clinical-feature
interactions (6 treatments × 15 candidate modifiers = 90 tests)
revealed that essentially all of the discoverable
treatment-effect heterogeneity in this dataset is concentrated in
treatment_palbociclib. The strongest interactions, in order of
p-value, were:

  treatment_palbociclib x er_pos_her2_neg  +1.93 mo   p≈0
  treatment_palbociclib x er_positive      +1.58 mo   p≈0
  treatment_palbociclib x hr_pos           +1.48 mo   p≈0
  treatment_palbociclib x her2_positive    -1.35 mo   p≈0
  treatment_palbociclib x tnbc             -1.39 mo   p≈0
  treatment_palbociclib x pik3ca_mutation  -1.68 mo   p≈0
  treatment_palbociclib x pr_positive      +1.04 mo   p≈0
  treatment_palbociclib x her2_low         +0.40 mo   p~10^-62

Stratified analyses confirmed the picture: in HR+/HER2- patients
(n=30,444) the adjusted palbociclib coefficient is +1.81 months
(p≈0); outside that subgroup it is essentially zero (-0.002, p≈0.52).

For the other five treatments, no biologically motivated tx-by-
biomarker interaction was significant at conventional thresholds
(tamoxifen x er, p=0.87; trastuzumab x her2_positive, p=0.65;
olaparib x brca_any, p=0.46; sacituzumab x tnbc, p=0.52;
pembrolizumab x tnbc, p=0.21).

Within-subgroup secondary modifier search (iterations 15-22)
------------------------------------------------------------
For every treatment with a plausible indication, I asked: within
that indication subgroup, is there a secondary feature that
modulates the treatment effect?

  - Within HR+/HER2-, the strongest secondary modifier of
    palbociclib by far was pik3ca_mutation: tx-coef +2.74 in
    PIK3CA-wt vs +0.004 in PIK3CA-mutant
    (interaction = -2.74, p≈0). The palbo benefit in this subgroup
    is also continuously eroded by ki67_pct: a -0.131 month/percent
    interaction (p≈0), so very high Ki-67 tumors derive little
    benefit.
  - Within HER2+ (trastuzumab) the strongest secondary effect was a
    weak negative trastuzumab x age interaction (-0.0014/yr, p≈0.007),
    but no clinically meaningful trastuzumab benefit was uncovered
    in any HER2+ subgroup.
  - Within BRCA1/2 mutated patients (olaparib), no secondary modifier
    reached significance; the strongest signal (weight_loss_pct_6mo,
    p_int=0.06) does not translate into a positive olaparib effect
    in any sub-stratum.
  - Within TNBC (both sacituzumab and pembrolizumab), no secondary
    modifier produced a positive treatment effect; pembrolizumab's
    strongest within-TNBC signal was a small positive interaction
    with weight_loss_pct_6mo (p≈4×10^-4) but the absolute effect
    remains near zero.
  - Within ER+ (tamoxifen), no secondary modifier produced a
    positive tamoxifen effect.

Joint multivariable model (iteration 21)
----------------------------------------
A single OLS that simultaneously included each treatment crossed
with its canonical indication confirmed that the only positive
treatment-by-indication interaction is
treatment_palbociclib:er_pos_her2_neg (+1.92 months, p≈0); the
canonical indication interactions for tamoxifen, trastuzumab,
olaparib, sacituzumab, and pembrolizumab were all small and
non-significant (point estimates ≤ ±0.05).

Refined complete subgroup hypothesis (iterations 23-25)
-------------------------------------------------------
The cleanest, fully-specified subgroup hypothesis the data support
is for treatment_palbociclib. The palbociclib PFS benefit is
concentrated in:

   (er_positive == 1 OR pr_positive == 1)       <- HR+
   AND her2_positive == 0                       <- HER2-
   AND pik3ca_mutation == 0                     <- PIK3CA wild-type

In this complete subgroup (n=19,918), the adjusted palbociclib
coefficient is +2.74 months PFS (SE 0.030, p≈0). In HR+/HER2-
patients with pik3ca_mutation == 1 (n=10,526) the adjusted
palbociclib coefficient drops to +0.004 (p=0.33) — i.e., PIK3CA
mutation is the unfavorable secondary feature that completely
suppresses the palbociclib effect. A formal three-way interaction
treatment_palbociclib * (HR+/HER2-) * (PIK3CA-wt) recovered exactly
this story: the triple-interaction coefficient is +2.74 (p≈0) and
all lower-order palbociclib terms are within ±0.005 of zero.

Within the favorable subgroup, ki67_pct further modifies the effect
(per-percent interaction -0.131 months PFS, p≈0), so an even more
refined subgroup ("HR+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt with low Ki-67") shows
larger absolute benefits, but the categorical
HR+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt definition is the one I report as the final
treatment-effect-heterogeneity hypothesis.

Per-treatment final hypotheses (iteration 25)
---------------------------------------------
- treatment_tamoxifen: no PFS benefit detectable in any subgroup
  including ER+/PR+/postmenopausal; final adjusted effect ≈ 0,
  not significant.
- treatment_palbociclib: +2.74 months PFS in HR+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt,
  p<<0.001; effect abolished if her2_positive=1 or
  pik3ca_mutation=1; effect attenuated by high ki67_pct.
- treatment_trastuzumab: no PFS benefit in HER2+ patients
  (overall HER2+ adjusted coef +0.001, p=0.79), even when
  restricted by has_brain_mets=0 or ecog_ps<2; final effect ≈ 0.
- treatment_olaparib: no PFS benefit in BRCA-mutated patients
  (adjusted coef +0.04, p=0.63); no usable subgroup found.
- treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan: no PFS benefit in TNBC
  (adjusted coef +0.005, p=0.46) or any TNBC sub-stratum.
- treatment_pembrolizumab: no PFS benefit in TNBC (adjusted coef
  +0.008, p=0.14) or any TNBC sub-stratum; the directionally
  negative whole-cohort signal reflects channelling, not a true
  pembrolizumab harm.

Bottom line
-----------
The single robust treatment-effect signal in ds001_breast is for
treatment_palbociclib. It has a large, biologically plausible PFS
benefit in HR+/HER2- patients that is fully neutralized by PIK3CA
mutation (and progressively diluted by higher Ki-67), so the
correct complete subgroup definition is HR+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wild-type.
For tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib, sacituzumab_govitecan, and
pembrolizumab, despite extensive interaction screening and
within-indication secondary-modifier searches, no PFS-benefit
subgroup was identified in this cohort.
