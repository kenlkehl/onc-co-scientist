Analysis Summary — ds001_breast (50,000 patients)
=========================================================

OUTCOME
The single outcome examined was pfs_months (progression-free survival in
months). In the cohort, pfs_months had mean 4.69 (SD 2.50), median 4.54,
range 0–16.66. The marginal distribution was approximately Gaussian.

OVERALL APPROACH
Across 25 iterations I (1) characterized prognostic main effects of
patient features, (2) examined marginal treatment effects, (3) screened
for treatment-by-biomarker heterogeneity using OLS interaction terms,
(4) dissected the strongest interaction signals into subgroup-specific
effect estimates, and (5) tested whether the strongest subgroup signals
survived multivariable adjustment. The dataset contains six
treatments — tamoxifen, palbociclib, trastuzumab, olaparib,
sacituzumab_govitecan, pembrolizumab — and 36 features.

PROGNOSTIC MAIN EFFECTS (Iterations 1–3)
The dominant prognostic determinants of pfs_months were:
  * age_years: positive correlation (Pearson r=+0.702, p≈0). Older
    patients had longer PFS in this cohort.
  * ecog_ps: strong negative association (mean PFS 5.64 / 4.44 / 3.29
    months for ECOG 0/1/2; r=-0.320, p≈0).
  * stage_iv: shorter PFS by 1.54 months (p≈0).
  * has_brain_mets: shorter PFS by 1.05 months (p=1.4e-178).
  * er_positive: longer PFS by 0.57 months (p=3.7e-143).
  * pr_positive: longer PFS by 0.36 months (p=5.8e-59).
  * pik3ca_mutation: shorter PFS by 0.56 months (p=6.1e-144).
  * her2_positive: shorter PFS by 0.46 months (p=6.1e-70).
  * her2_low: longer PFS by 0.11 months (p=1.0e-6).
  * weight_loss_pct_6mo: r=-0.116, p<1e-147.
  * albumin_g_dl: r=+0.100, p<1e-111.
  * ki67_pct: r=-0.091, p<1e-92.
  * ldh_u_l, alkaline_phosphatase, calcium showed small negative
    correlations.
  * Other lab markers (CRP, NLR, hemoglobin, sodium, potassium, AST,
    ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN) showed near-null correlations
    with PFS.
  * brca1_mutation, brca2_mutation, sex_female, node_positive showed
    no significant marginal effect on PFS.

In a multivariable OLS model containing all 36 features, age_years
(beta ≈ +0.16/year, p≈0), ecog_ps (≈-1.06/unit, p≈0), and stage_iv
(≈-1.40, p≈0) remained the largest predictors. Model R^2 was high
(>0.6) driven primarily by age and ECOG.

MARGINAL TREATMENT EFFECTS (Iterations 4–5)
Marginal (cohort-wide) treatment differences in PFS:
  * treatment_palbociclib: +1.10 months (p≈0); only treatment with a
    large positive marginal signal.
  * treatment_pembrolizumab: -0.07 months (p=0.018, slightly negative).
  * treatment_trastuzumab: -0.06 months (p=0.029, very small negative).
  * treatment_olaparib: -0.04 months (p=0.33, null).
  * treatment_tamoxifen: +0.04 months (p=0.077, null).
  * treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan: not significant.

After covariate adjustment (age, ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, albumin,
LDH, CRP, NLR), only palbociclib retained a substantial positive
coefficient (+1.10 months, p≈0). All other treatments collapsed to
near-zero adjusted main effects. Pembrolizumab's adjusted main effect
was -0.04 months (p=0.031) — a small, plausibly confounding-driven
negative — suggesting that any therapeutic effect must be subgroup-
specific rather than global.

TREATMENT × BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS (Iterations 6–14)
I tested predefined interactions for each indication-relevant pair and
ran an exhaustive screen of every treatment × binary modifier pair.

* Trastuzumab × her2_positive: NOT significant (interaction beta=-0.03,
  p=0.69). Within HER2+, trastuzumab diff = -0.08 (p=0.15); within
  HER2-: -0.05 (p=0.11). Trastuzumab × her2_low: also null.
  Hypothesis of HER2-targeted benefit was REFUTED.

* Tamoxifen × er_positive: NOT significant (interaction beta=-0.06,
  p=0.27). Within ER+: tamoxifen diff = +0.02 (p=0.45); within ER-:
  +0.08 (p=0.03). No predictive subgroup. REFUTED.

* Palbociclib × er_positive: very strong positive interaction
  (beta=+1.594, p=9.3e-229). Within er_positive=1, palbociclib
  increases PFS by +1.577 months (p≈0); within er_positive=0, effect
  is -0.017 (p=0.63). STRONGLY SUPPORTED.

* Palbociclib × postmenopausal: small negative interaction
  (beta=-0.134, p=0.004). Effect within postmenopausal=1: +1.045;
  within postmenopausal=0: +1.179. Refines the picture: palbociclib
  benefit is at least as large in premenopausal patients as in
  postmenopausal patients, contradicting an a priori hypothesis that
  CDK4/6 inhibition would work better in postmenopausal disease.

* Olaparib × brca_any: significant positive interaction (beta=+0.402,
  p=0.021). Within brca_any=1: olaparib diff = +0.346 (p=0.041);
  within brca_any=0: -0.056 (p=0.15). BRCA1 alone: +0.398 (p=0.090);
  BRCA2 alone: +0.295 (p=0.225). Direction concordant in both BRCA1
  and BRCA2 carriers but small samples (n_treated=120 and 119) limit
  individual significance.

* Pembrolizumab × triple_negative (er=0 AND pr=0 AND her2=0):
  significant positive interaction (beta=+0.185, p=0.015). Within
  triple_negative=1: pembrolizumab diff = +0.072 (p=0.21); within
  triple_negative=0: -0.114 (p=1.6e-3). The interaction is significant
  but the within-TNBC effect alone does not meet conventional
  significance.

* Pembrolizumab × er_negative: borderline (interaction p=0.05).

* Sacituzumab × triple_negative: NOT significant (interaction beta=
  -0.105, p=0.25). Within TNBC: -0.085 (p=0.21). REFUTED.

The exhaustive treatment × binary-feature interaction screen
identified palbociclib × er_positive as the single dominant signal
(p≈0). The next-strongest interactions involve palbociclib with
correlated hormone-receptor markers (pr_positive, her2_low) and
olaparib × brca_any.

THREE-WAY AND REFINED SUBGROUP DEFINITIONS (Iterations 19, 22, 24)
For pembrolizumab, a three-way OLS pfs_months ~ pembrolizumab *
triple_negative * postmenopausal yielded:
  pembrolizumab:triple_negative beta=+0.422, p=5.8e-04
  pembrolizumab:triple_negative:postmenopausal beta=-0.389, p=0.013
i.e., the unadjusted pembrolizumab benefit within TNBC is concentrated
in premenopausal TNBC (+0.275 months, p=2.7e-3, n_treated=611). In
postmenopausal TNBC the effect is essentially null (-0.059, p=0.42).

For olaparib in BRCA carriers, the effect was slightly larger in
premenopausal BRCA carriers (+0.547, p=0.05) than postmenopausal BRCA
carriers (+0.226, p=0.29) and slightly larger in stage_iv=1 BRCA
carriers (+0.538) than stage_iv=0 (+0.204), but no individual sub-
strata reached significance.

WITHIN-SUBGROUP ADJUSTED EFFECTS (Iteration 16, 23)
Adjustment for age_years, ecog_ps, stage_iv, has_brain_mets,
albumin_g_dl, ldh_u_l, crp_mg_l, nlr (and where appropriate,
postmenopausal):

  Treatment x best subgroup           Adjusted beta (p)
  palbociclib in ER+ postmen          +1.553 (p≈0)        ROBUST
  palbociclib outside ER+ postmen     +0.770 (p≈0)        also large (driven by ER+ premenopausal)
  olaparib in BRCA-any                +0.090 (p=0.32)     ATTENUATED
  pembrolizumab in TNBC               +0.014 (p=0.28)     ATTENUATED
  sacituzumab in TNBC                 +0.001 (p=0.92)     null
  trastuzumab in HER2+                +0.012 (p=0.18)     null
  tamoxifen in ER+                    -0.000 (p=0.99)     null

The palbociclib-in-ER+ benefit is the only signal that remains large
and highly significant after multivariable adjustment.

KEY FINAL HYPOTHESES (Iteration 25)

(1) treatment_palbociclib improves pfs_months substantially in
    er_positive=1 patients (~+1.58 months) and is null in
    er_positive=0 patients. This is the dominant treatment-effect
    signal in the dataset, robust to multivariable adjustment.
    Postmenopausal status is NOT a required suppressor — palbociclib
    works equally (slightly more) in premenopausal ER+ patients.

(2) treatment_olaparib has a real but modest positive effect on
    pfs_months in BRCA1- or BRCA2-mutated patients (brca_any=1):
    +0.35 months unadjusted (p=0.04). The adjusted beta drops to
    +0.09 (p=0.32), so the signal is sensitive to confounding. The
    direction is consistent across BRCA1 and BRCA2 sub-strata. The
    effect is non-existent in non-carriers (brca_any=0; -0.06 mo).
    Best-supported predictive subgroup: brca1_mutation=1 OR
    brca2_mutation=1.

(3) treatment_pembrolizumab shows treatment-effect heterogeneity by
    triple_negative status (interaction p=0.015) and by a 3-way
    interaction with postmenopausal (p=0.013). Within triple_negative=1
    + postmenopausal=0 (premenopausal TNBC), unadjusted PFS gain is
    +0.275 months (p=2.7e-3, n_treated=611), but this attenuates to
    +0.022 (p=0.14) after multivariable adjustment for age, ECOG,
    stage IV, brain mets, albumin, LDH, CRP, NLR. So the apparent
    pembrolizumab benefit appears largely confounded by other
    prognostic features that co-vary with pembrolizumab assignment.
    Outside TNBC, pembrolizumab shows a small negative association
    (-0.114 mo, p=1.6e-3). Best-supported predictive subgroup:
    triple_negative=1 + postmenopausal=0 (with postmenopausal status
    acting as a suppressor of the modest effect).

(4) treatment_trastuzumab has NO predictive subgroup with a positive,
    significant effect on pfs_months in this cohort. In particular,
    her2_positive=1 — the canonical clinical indication — does NOT
    yield a positive effect (diff=-0.08, p=0.15; adjusted beta=+0.01,
    p=0.18). Tested also in HER2-low and HER2-zero strata, and within
    her2_positive=1 stratified by ECOG and stage IV: no positive
    significant subgroup found.

(5) treatment_tamoxifen has NO predictive subgroup with a clear
    positive effect on pfs_months. The canonical ER+ subgroup yields
    diff=+0.02 (p=0.45); adjusted beta=-0.0001 (p=0.99). No subgroup
    by menopausal status or PR status yielded benefit either.

(6) treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan has NO predictive subgroup with a
    positive effect. In TNBC, where the drug is clinically indicated,
    the difference was -0.085 (p=0.21); adjusted +0.001 (p=0.92).

OVERALL CONCLUSION
This breast-cancer EHR-derived cohort encodes a strong,
biology-consistent treatment-by-biomarker signal for palbociclib in
er_positive=1 patients (PFS gain ~1.5–1.7 months, robust to
adjustment). It also supports modest, BRCA-restricted benefit for
olaparib (~+0.35 months unadjusted; attenuated after adjustment) and
a borderline TNBC-restricted (especially premenopausal-TNBC) signal
for pembrolizumab that is largely confounded by patient-level
prognostic factors. Three other treatments (trastuzumab, tamoxifen,
sacituzumab_govitecan) yield no predictive subgroup with a
statistically robust positive PFS effect, despite their canonical
clinical indications being represented in the dataset. The dominant
prognostic drivers of pfs_months are age, ECOG performance status,
stage_iv, brain metastases, hormone-receptor status, PIK3CA mutation,
and HER2 status; lab markers (albumin, weight loss, Ki-67) contribute
modest additional prognostic information.
