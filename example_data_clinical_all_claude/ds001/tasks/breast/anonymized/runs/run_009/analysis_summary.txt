ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_breast (n=50,000; outcome: pfs_months)
================================================================

Cohort and approach
-------------------
The cohort consists of 50,000 patient records with 37 de-identified features
(`feature_001`..`feature_037`) and one outcome, `pfs_months` (mean 4.69 mo,
SD 2.50 mo, range 0–16.66 mo). Across 25 iterations we (1) screened
univariate associations, (2) built multivariable OLS models, (3) probed
subgroup heterogeneity, and (4) ran exhaustive treatment-effect modifier
screens. The final iterations report the most striking refined subgroup
hypothesis: feature_013 behaves as a treatment whose benefit is restricted
to a precisely-defined biomarker subgroup.

Univariate associations (Iter 1–2)
----------------------------------
Continuous features ranked by |Pearson r| with PFS:
- feature_012 (age-like, 30–90, mean 65): r = +0.70, p ≈ 0 — older patients
  have LONGER PFS, opposite of the typical age effect on overall survival.
- feature_005 (3-level ordinal, 0/1/2 with prevalences 35%/50%/15%):
  r = -0.32, p ≈ 0 — clear "stage-like" gradient (mean PFS 5.64 / 4.44 /
  3.29 across levels 0/1/2).
- feature_004 (continuous): r = -0.12, p ≈ 4e-148.
- feature_024 (continuous, range 1.5–5.5, mean 3.8): r = +0.10, p ≈ 9e-112.
- feature_033 (continuous, range 1–100, mean 15.5): r = -0.09, p ≈ 3e-93.
The remaining 14 continuous features (mostly lab-panel ranges; feature_002
sodium-like, feature_007 potassium-like, feature_010 calcium-like, etc.)
showed |r| < 0.02 and contributed essentially no incremental R^2 (Iter 11,
ΔR^2 ≈ 0.0002 on top of the strong predictors).

Binary features ranked by mean PFS difference (1 vs 0):
- feature_006 (~30% prev): -1.54 mo, p ≈ 0 — strongly UNFAVORABLE.
- feature_013 (~35% prev): +1.10 mo, p ≈ 0 — strongly FAVORABLE.
- feature_001 (~10% prev): -1.05 mo, p ≈ 1e-178 — UNFAVORABLE.
- feature_028 (~35% prev): -0.56 mo, p ≈ 6e-144.
- feature_029 (~70% prev): +0.57 mo, p ≈ 4e-143.
- feature_018 (~18% prev): -0.46 mo, p ≈ 6e-70.
- feature_023 (~64% prev): +0.36 mo, p ≈ 6e-59.
- feature_016 (~37% prev): +0.11 mo, p ≈ 1e-6.
The remaining 10 binary features (incl. feature_011, feature_030, feature_019,
feature_008, feature_009, feature_026, feature_032, feature_015, feature_022,
feature_020) showed |Δ| < 0.13 mo and weak/null p-values univariately.

Multivariable adjustment (Iter 3–4)
-----------------------------------
A full OLS of pfs_months on all features achieves R^2 = 0.79 (adj 0.79).
The strongest adjusted coefficients (in mo):
  feature_006        -1.55   (p≈0)
  feature_005        -1.17 / level (p≈0)
  feature_013        +1.10   (p≈0)
  feature_001        -0.98   (p≈0)
  feature_028        -0.58   (p≈0)
  feature_029        +0.53   (p≈3e-275)
  feature_018        -0.47   (p≈2e-239)
  feature_024        +0.48 / unit (p≈0)
  feature_012        +0.176/yr (p≈0)
  feature_004        -0.077/unit (p≈0)
  feature_033        -0.019/unit (p≈0)
The lab-panel features (feature_002/007/010/017/025/027/031/034/035/036/037/
014/021/003) were uninformative in adjusted analysis.

Stage and age (Iter 3, 5)
-------------------------
feature_005 is monotonic: per-level PFS slope -1.17 mo (p≈0); level 2 vs 0
= -2.36 mo. The unusual POSITIVE age effect (+0.18 mo/yr) persists after
adjusting for stage and the strongest binary biomarkers (feature_006,
feature_013, feature_001, feature_028), suggesting an underlying biological
gradient (older patients with more indolent disease) rather than simple
confounding.

Receptor-pair structure (Iter 6)
--------------------------------
feature_029 and feature_023 are highly co-occurring (r = +0.67) — consistent
with a paired receptor system (e.g., ER and PR). Both confer +0.4–0.6 mo PFS
univariately, but after mutual adjustment feature_029 dominates (~+0.53 mo)
while feature_023 becomes nearly null (~+0.01 mo). Hence feature_029 is the
informative member of the pair for prognosis.

Anti-correlated pair (Iter 7)
-----------------------------
feature_018 and feature_016 are negatively associated (r = -0.36), with
opposite-sign PFS effects (feature_018 -0.46 mo, feature_016 +0.11 mo) —
consistent with mutually-exclusive treatment classes or alternative biology.

Strong negative biomarkers (Iter 8) — feature_006 and feature_001
-----------------------------------------------------------------
feature_006 (~30% prevalence) carries an adjusted -1.55 mo deficit
independent of stage, age, and feature_013. feature_001 (~10% prevalence)
carries an adjusted ~-1.0 mo deficit. Both are PURE prognostic markers:
their distributions are balanced across stage levels and across feature_013
arms (~30% and ~10% in every subgroup), they have NO interaction with
feature_013 (e.g., feature_006:feature_013 p=0.40), and the deficit is
present whether or not the patient receives feature_013.

Joint feature_006 × feature_013 (Iter 15)
-----------------------------------------
The interaction is small (~+0.02, p=0.40); within feature_006=1 patients,
feature_013 still confers a positive PFS shift (+~1.1 mo, p≈0), confirming
that the feature_013 effect operates independently of feature_006 status.

Treatment-effect heterogeneity screens (Iter 17–21)
---------------------------------------------------
We ran exhaustive interaction screens: each candidate "treatment" feature
crossed with every binary modifier in `pfs_months ~ treat × mod + stage +
age`. The strongest findings, by far, all point to feature_013 as a
candidate TREATMENT with three biomarker modifiers:

  feature_013 × feature_029 :  interaction = +1.58, p ≈ 0
  feature_013 × feature_018 :  interaction = -1.38, p ≈ 0
  feature_013 × feature_028 :  interaction = -1.64, p ≈ 0

In contrast, screens centred on feature_006 yielded no significant
interactions (best p > 0.13), confirming feature_006 is a uniform negative
prognostic marker, NOT a treatment.

The KEY finding (final subgroup hypothesis, Iter 23–25)
-------------------------------------------------------
Cross-tabulating PFS by feature_013 × {feature_029, feature_018, feature_028}
reveals a textbook precision-medicine signature:

(1) Joint OLS (pfs_months ~ feature_013 × feature_029 + feature_013 × feature_018
   + feature_013 × feature_028 + feature_005 + feature_012):

    feature_013                : +0.84   (p≈0)
    feature_029                : +0.004  (p=0.81)  ← NO main effect
    feature_018                : +0.008  (p=0.69)  ← NO main effect
    feature_028                : -0.010  (p=0.53)  ← NO main effect
    feature_013:feature_029    : +1.55   (p≈0)
    feature_013:feature_018    : -1.35   (p≈0)
    feature_013:feature_028    : -1.64   (p≈0)
    R^2 = 0.72

   By themselves, feature_029, feature_018, and feature_028 have NO PFS
   effect: they only matter as predictive biomarkers for feature_013.

(2) Stratifying by these three biomarkers, the within-stratum PFS effect
   of feature_013 (verified at iteration 25):

   feature_029=1 AND feature_018=0 AND feature_028=0 (n=18,681):
        feature_013=1 mean PFS = 7.21 mo (n = 6,571)
        feature_013=0 mean PFS = 4.30 mo (n = 12,110)
        difference = +2.91 mo, p ≈ 0
        ⇒ LARGE, highly significant treatment benefit.

   COMPLEMENT (feature_029=0 OR feature_018=1 OR feature_028=1, n=31,319):
        feature_013=1 mean PFS = 4.32 mo (n = 10,857)
        feature_013=0 mean PFS = 4.32 mo (n = 20,462)
        difference = +0.003 mo, p = 0.91
        ⇒ Treatment is INERT outside the predictive subgroup.

(3) feature_013 is approximately uniformly distributed (~34–35%) across
   every combination of feature_029, feature_018, feature_028 — consistent
   with random or quasi-random treatment assignment, ruling out
   confounding-by-indication as the explanation for the subgroup result.

FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS:
  Treatment:  feature_013
  Outcome:    pfs_months
  Direction:  LONGER PFS in subgroup
  Subgroup:   feature_029 = 1  AND  feature_018 = 0  AND  feature_028 = 0
  Suppressors (any of which abolishes the benefit):
              feature_029 = 0,  feature_018 = 1,  feature_028 = 1
  Effect:     +2.91 months (p ≈ 0); ~0 months elsewhere

Prognostic extremes (Iter 25)
-----------------------------
Worst-prognosis subgroup: feature_005=2 AND feature_006=1 AND feature_001=1
  n = 247, mean PFS = 1.54 mo; difference vs rest = -3.17 mo (p ≈ 6e-72).

Best-prognosis subgroup: feature_005=0 AND feature_006=0 AND feature_001=0
  AND feature_029=1 AND feature_018=0 AND feature_028=0 AND feature_013=1
  n = 1,471, mean PFS = 8.70 mo; difference vs rest = +4.13 mo (p ≈ 0).

These two subgroups together span a ~7-month difference in mean PFS within
the same cohort, with the treatment-by-biomarker subgroup contributing the
single largest controllable component.

Summary of supported vs refuted hypotheses
------------------------------------------
SUPPORTED (significant in the expected direction):
- feature_005 monotonic stage-like effect on PFS.
- Positive age (feature_012) effect — surprising direction confirmed and
  robust to adjustment.
- feature_006 strongly negative prognostic; uniform, non-modifiable.
- feature_001 negative prognostic, ~10% prevalence.
- feature_013 strongly positive — but ONLY within the predictive subgroup.
- feature_029 / feature_018 / feature_028 are PREDICTIVE not prognostic
  biomarkers for feature_013.
- feature_024 positive, feature_033 negative, feature_004 negative
  continuous biomarkers (modest but robust).

REFUTED OR DOWNGRADED:
- feature_029, feature_018, feature_028 as prognostic main effects:
  univariate effects entirely arise via interaction with feature_013.
- feature_023 as a useful prognostic marker beyond feature_029.
- All 14 lab-panel features as meaningful incremental predictors of PFS.
- feature_006 as a treatment / treatment-effect modifier (it is a uniform
  prognostic marker only).

Caveats
-------
- All analyses are observational; treatment assignment for feature_013
  appears balanced across the three biomarkers, but unmeasured confounding
  cannot be excluded.
- The "age" interpretation of feature_012 is inferred from its 30–90 range
  and ~65 mean; if it represents another continuous covariate, the
  positive PFS slope still holds but the biological narrative shifts.
- feature_012, feature_005, feature_006, feature_001, feature_013,
  feature_029, feature_018, feature_028 together capture the bulk of
  modelable variation; remaining features add little.
