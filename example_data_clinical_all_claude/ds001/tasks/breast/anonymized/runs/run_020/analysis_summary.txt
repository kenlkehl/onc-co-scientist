ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_breast (n = 50,000; outcome = pfs_months)
=====================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The cohort contains 50,000 patient records with 37 de-identified features
and a single continuous outcome, pfs_months (mean 4.69, sd 2.50, median
4.54, range 0.0-16.7). Of the 37 features, 18 are binary, one is 3-level
ordinal (feature_005, levels 0/1/2), and 18 are continuous. There are
no missing values. Across 25 iterations of propose-test-refine I
characterized the prognostic structure of the cohort, identified the
single treatment-like variable, and converged on a 4-predicate
predictive biomarker subgroup that captures essentially all systematic
treatment-effect heterogeneity.

ITERATIONS 1-3 — UNIVARIATE AND MULTIVARIABLE PROGNOSTIC SCREEN
---------------------------------------------------------------
A pass over all features against pfs_months identified the following
independently prognostic variables (multivariable OLS, R^2 = 0.792):

  Continuous prognostic factors:
    feature_012  +0.176/unit  (Spearman r = +0.726, dominant continuous
                                prognostic; range 30-90 implies up to a
                                ~10 month spread in expected PFS)
    feature_024  +0.476/unit  (favorable, range 1.5-5.5)
    feature_033  -0.019/unit  (unfavorable, range 1-100)
    feature_004  -0.077/unit  (unfavorable, range 0-25)
    feature_031  -0.0005/unit (small unfavorable)

  Categorical prognostic factors:
    feature_005  -1.172/level (ordinal, monotonic: PFS 5.64 / 4.44 /
                               3.29 across levels 0/1/2)
    feature_006  -1.549       (binary, ~30% prevalence, large
                               unfavorable effect)
    feature_001  -0.983       (binary, ~10% prevalence, unfavorable)
    feature_018  -0.471       (binary, unfavorable)
    feature_028  -0.582       (binary, unfavorable)
    feature_023  +0.014       (NS in multivariable, marginally
                               favorable in univariate)
    feature_029  +0.532       (binary, favorable)
    feature_013  +1.101       (binary, favorable - the treatment;
                               see below)

All other features had small, non-significant, or negligible effects.
Hypotheses h1.1-h3.1 (5 univariate + 1 multivariable) were strongly
supported by the data.

ITERATION 4 — IDENTIFYING THE TREATMENT VARIABLE
------------------------------------------------
A binary x binary interaction screen across all 18 binary features
revealed a striking asymmetry: every interaction with p < 1e-100 had
feature_013 as one of the two terms. The largest interactions were:

    feature_013 x feature_028   beta = -1.62, p = 4e-255
    feature_013 x feature_029   beta = +1.59, p = 9e-229
    feature_013 x feature_018   beta = -1.36, p = 2e-118
    feature_013 x feature_023   beta = +1.09, p = 3e-117
    feature_013 x feature_016   beta = +0.42, p = 9e-19

No competing binary variable showed comparable interaction signal.
This pattern - a single variable interacting strongly with multiple
others while those others do not interact among themselves at the
same magnitude - is the signature of a treatment variable whose
effect is conditional on biomarker status. h4.1 was supported.

ITERATIONS 5-6 — DECOMPOSING THE INTERACTIONS
---------------------------------------------
Cell-mean inspection of the top three interactions established that
each modifier acts as a near-binary switch on feature_013's benefit:

    feature_013 effect:
      In feature_029=0 patients:   -0.018 months  (NS)
      In feature_029=1 patients:   +1.577 months
      In feature_028=0 patients:   +1.659 months
      In feature_028=1 patients:   +0.036 months  (NS)
      In feature_018=0 patients:   +1.350 months
      In feature_018=1 patients:  -0.014 months  (NS)

When all three favorable conditions hold (feature_029=1 AND
feature_028=0 AND feature_018=0), n=18,681 (~37% of cohort) and the
within-subgroup feature_013 effect is +2.91 months (p<1e-300, h6.1).
Outside that joint subgroup (n=31,319), the feature_013 effect is
+0.003 months (p=0.91, h6.2 - treatment effect is statistically null).

ITERATIONS 7-8 — DISCOVERING THE CONTINUOUS MODIFIER feature_033
----------------------------------------------------------------
A continuous-modifier search within the 3-binary biomarker+ subgroup
identified feature_033 as the unique strong modifier of feature_013.
The feature_013 x feature_033 interaction within the subgroup yielded
beta = -0.135 per unit (p essentially 0). A threshold scan revealed a
sharp drop in treatment benefit between feature_033 = 12.5 and
feature_033 = 17.5:

    Within biomarker-positive (3-binary) subgroup, feature_013 effect:
      feature_033 <= 5     +4.91 months (n=1966)
      feature_033 <= 7.5   +4.93 months (n=4708)
      feature_033 <= 10    +4.96 months (n=7362)
      feature_033 <= 12.5  +4.94 months (n=9639)
      feature_033 <= 15    +4.65 months (n=11575)
      feature_033 <= 17.5  +4.16 months (n=13062)
      feature_033 <= 20    +3.83 months (n=14226)

      feature_033 >  15:    +0.02 months (n=7106)
      feature_033 >  20:   -0.03 months (n=4455)

Cutoff = 15 was selected as the parsimonious threshold (h8.1, h7.1).

ITERATIONS 9-11 — FINAL 4-PREDICATE RESPONDER DEFINITION AND
                   COMPONENT NECESSITY
-------------------------------------------------------------
The full responder subgroup is:

      feature_029 = 1  AND  feature_028 = 0  AND
      feature_018 = 0  AND  feature_033 <= 15      (n = 11,575)

Within it, mean PFS rises from 4.286 months in feature_013=0 patients
(n=7,477) to 8.936 months in feature_013=1 patients (n=4,098), an
absolute gain of +4.65 months (p<1e-300). Outside it (n=38,425), the
treatment effect is +0.006 months (p=0.78). This is a textbook
predictive biomarker pattern: the responder predicate has essentially
zero prognostic effect by itself (-0.032 months in untreated patients,
p=0.26), but identifies the only patients who benefit from treatment.

Each of the four components is necessary (h10.1):
   - Drop feature_029=1: TE collapses from +2.50 to +0.04 in the
                         feature_029=0, f033<=15 cell.
   - Drop feature_028=0: TE collapses from +2.71 to +0.02 in the
                         feature_028=1, f033<=15 cell.
   - Drop feature_018=0: TE collapses from +2.17 to +0.03 in the
                         feature_018=1, f033<=15 cell.
   - Drop feature_033<=15 (within 3-binary biomarker+): TE collapses
                          from +4.65 to +0.02 in the f033>15 cell.

Other binary features that showed marginal interactions with
feature_013 (feature_023, feature_016) do NOT further refine the
responder definition (h11.1):
   feature_023=0 vs 1 within full biomarker+: TE +4.33 vs +4.71
   feature_016=0 vs 1 within full biomarker+: TE +4.58 vs +4.74
Differences <0.4 months and prevalence-trade-off is unfavorable.

ITERATION 12 — NO ADDITIONAL TREATMENT-LIKE VARIABLES
-----------------------------------------------------
Comprehensive interaction screens using each non-feature_013 binary
variable as the index revealed no other treatment candidates: the
smallest non-feature_013 interaction p-value was 4.3e-3, and that
involved feature_019 modulating feature_013 (still anchored on
feature_013), not feature_019 acting as a treatment in its own right.
feature_013 is the unique treatment variable in this dataset (h12.1).

ITERATIONS 13-16 — PROGNOSTIC vs PREDICTIVE SEPARATION
------------------------------------------------------
Several variables with large main effects on PFS are PURELY PROGNOSTIC,
not predictive of feature_013 effect:

    Variable      | Main effect  | feature_013 interaction
    --------------|--------------|------------------------
    feature_005   | -1.17/level  | p>0.6 (prognostic only)
    feature_006   | -1.55        | p=0.90 (prognostic only)
    feature_001   | -0.98        | p=0.59 (prognostic only)
    feature_012   | +0.176/unit  | p=0.044, beta=-0.003 (negligible)

Within the responder subgroup the prognostic effects of feature_005
and feature_006 persist with similar magnitudes (h16.1), confirming
they act additively on top of treatment, not multiplicatively.

Within the responder subgroup, no continuous variable other than
feature_033 modulates the treatment effect at p<0.01 (h14.1), and the
binary predicates of the responder definition are independent of
treatment assignment (correlations near 0; h15.1, h15.2 - this is
consistent with treatment being assigned independently of the
biomarkers, as in a randomized or pseudo-randomized trial-like cohort).

ITERATION 17 — EXHAUSTIVE TRIPLE-SUBGROUP SEARCH
------------------------------------------------
An exhaustive search over all triples of the top binary modifiers
(feature_029, feature_028, feature_018, feature_023, feature_016,
feature_006, feature_001, feature_011, feature_030, feature_019) for
the subgroup with the largest feature_013 treatment effect produced:

    Top subgroup TEs (n>500 in both arms):
      (feature_029=1, feature_028=0, feature_016=1)   TE=+2.92  n=8518
      (feature_029=1, feature_028=0, feature_018=0)   TE=+2.91  n=18681
      (feature_028=0, feature_018=0, feature_023=1)   TE=+2.75  n=17153
      (feature_028=0, feature_023=1, feature_016=1)   TE=+2.73  n=7801
      ...
    Largest negative-TE triple:                        TE=-0.19

Every top-ranked triple shares feature_028=0 plus at least one of
feature_029=1 or feature_018=0. The convergence supports that the
responder structure is captured by the 3-binary core (h17.1). No
"anti-responder" subgroup (TE substantially negative) was found.

ITERATIONS 18-20 — REFINEMENT AND PARSIMONY
-------------------------------------------
Adding feature_023=1 to the responder definition mildly increases TE
(+4.71 vs +4.65) but at the cost of dropping ~15% of the responder
population (n falls 11,575 -> 9,864). Not worth adding (h23.1).

The full feature_013 x biomarker_pos model achieves R^2 nearly
identical to the 37-feature model when prognostic features are also
included, confirming that the 4-predicate biomarker captures the
treatment-effect signal completely (h20.1).

ITERATION 19 — FOUR-CELL DECOMPOSITION
--------------------------------------
The cleanest summary of the entire treatment-effect structure is
the 2x2 of (3-binary biomarker_pos) x (feature_033 <= 15):

  biomarker_pos  f033_low  treatment_effect    n
  -------------  --------  ----------------  ------
       0           0         -0.003          11980
       0           1         +0.007          19339
       1           0         +0.021           7106
       1           1         +4.651          11575

>99% of the population-attributable treatment benefit is concentrated
in the single cell representing 23% of the cohort.

ITERATIONS 21-25 — FINAL HYPOTHESES
-----------------------------------
The final, best-supported treatment-effect heterogeneity hypothesis
(h25.1) is:

  Patients receiving treatment feature_013=1 will experience mean
  pfs_months approximately +4.65 months longer than identically
  biomarker-defined patients receiving feature_013=0 IF AND ONLY IF
  they meet ALL FOUR predicates:

       feature_029 = 1
       feature_028 = 0
       feature_018 = 0
       feature_033 <= 15

  Outside this subgroup the feature_013 treatment effect on
  pfs_months is statistically indistinguishable from zero (point
  estimate +0.006 months, 95% CI roughly -0.04 to +0.05).

The final prognostic factors hypothesis (h25.2):

  Independent multivariable prognostic factors for pfs_months (OLS
  R^2 = 0.792): feature_012 (+0.176/unit), feature_005 (-1.17/level),
  feature_006 (-1.55), feature_001 (-0.98), feature_029 (+0.53),
  feature_028 (-0.58), feature_018 (-0.47), feature_024
  (+0.48/unit), feature_033 (-0.019/unit, but acts mainly through
  treatment-effect modification), feature_004 (-0.077/unit),
  feature_013 (+1.10 marginal, but conditional on biomarker
  effectively zero outside the responder subgroup).

CONCLUSIONS
-----------
1. feature_013 is the treatment in this cohort. It is the only
   variable whose effect on PFS is concentrated in a biomarker-
   defined subgroup, and treatment assignment is balanced across
   biomarkers (consistent with a randomized or trial-like dataset).

2. The complete predictive biomarker signature for response is:
      feature_029=1 AND feature_028=0 AND feature_018=0 AND
      feature_033<=15
   This identifies ~23% of the cohort. Within it, treatment confers
   ~+4.65 months of PFS; outside it the treatment effect is null.

3. feature_033 is unique among continuous variables in modifying
   treatment effect; with a sharp threshold near 15. Below 15 the
   treatment benefit is large and constant; above 15 it vanishes.

4. The strongest prognostic factors (feature_012, feature_005,
   feature_006, feature_001, feature_024) are NOT predictive of
   treatment effect; they act additively on PFS regardless of
   treatment status.

5. No other binary feature behaves like a second treatment, no
   anti-responder subgroup exists (TE never substantially negative),
   and the 4-predicate responder definition is both necessary in
   each component and sufficient (no further refinement adds
   meaningful effect).
