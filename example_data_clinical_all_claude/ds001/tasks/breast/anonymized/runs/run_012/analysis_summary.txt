Analysis Summary — ds001_breast (50,000 patients; outcome pfs_months)
======================================================================

Overview
--------
The dataset contains 37 anonymized features and a single continuous outcome
(pfs_months). Across 25 iterations of propose-test-refine, I screened all
features individually, fit a multivariable model, and then performed a
systematic treatment-effect heterogeneity search. One feature -- feature_013 --
emerged unambiguously as the only feature with treatment-like behavior, and
its effect is highly heterogeneous: it is concentrated in a precisely
definable subgroup and is essentially absent outside that subgroup.

Iteration trail
---------------
Iterations 1-4 -- Univariate screen.
  * Strongest single predictor: feature_012, a continuous variable spanning
    30-90 (mean 65), with Pearson r = +0.70 vs pfs_months. Its effect is
    massive but uniform across feature_013 strata, consistent with a generic
    favorable prognostic factor.
  * Strong negatives: feature_005 (3-level ordinal; -2.35 mo top vs bottom
    level), feature_006 (-1.54 mo), feature_001 (-1.05 mo), feature_028
    (-0.56 mo), feature_018 (-0.46 mo), feature_004 (r=-0.116), feature_033
    (r=-0.091).
  * Strong positives: feature_013 (+1.10 mo), feature_029 (+0.57 mo),
    feature_023 (+0.36 mo), feature_024 (r=+0.10).
  * Many features (feature_011, feature_019, feature_030, feature_021,
    feature_003, feature_002, feature_007, feature_010, feature_027,
    feature_034, feature_014, feature_037, feature_017, feature_035,
    feature_022, feature_008, feature_032, feature_020, feature_025,
    feature_036, feature_015, feature_009, feature_026) had small or null
    associations.

Iteration 5 -- Multivariable model (OLS, 37 features) achieved R^2 = 0.792.
  Independent (adjusted) coefficients in mo per unit:
     feature_012 +0.176, feature_005 -1.17, feature_006 -1.55,
     feature_001 -0.98, feature_013 +1.10, feature_029 +0.53,
     feature_028 -0.58, feature_018 -0.47, feature_033 -0.019,
     feature_024 +0.48, feature_004 -0.077, feature_031 -0.0005.
  Several univariately-significant variables (e.g., feature_023, feature_016)
  lost independent significance once feature_013 / feature_029 were in the
  model, hinting that they were proxies for treatment-responsive subgroup
  membership.

Iterations 6-12 -- Treatment-effect heterogeneity for feature_013.
  A pairwise OLS interaction screen of feature_013 against every other
  feature surfaced very strong, qualitatively interpretable interactions:
     feature_013 x feature_029: +1.56 (p=0)
     feature_013 x feature_023: +1.03 (p=0)
     feature_013 x feature_018: -1.34 (p=0)
     feature_013 x feature_028: -1.66 (p=0)
     feature_013 x feature_033: -0.053 mo/unit (p=0)
     feature_013 x feature_016: +0.38 (p~4e-67)
  Stratified mean-difference testing showed that the feature_013 effect is
  qualitatively all-or-nothing along feature_029, feature_018, and feature_028:
     * feature_029=0 -> feature_013 effect -0.02 mo (NS)
     * feature_029=1 -> feature_013 effect +1.58 mo
     * feature_018=0 -> feature_013 effect +1.35 mo
     * feature_018=1 -> feature_013 effect -0.01 mo (NS)
     * feature_028=0 -> feature_013 effect +1.66 mo
     * feature_028=1 -> feature_013 effect +0.04 mo (NS)
  Combining the three predicates (feature_029=1 AND feature_018=0 AND
  feature_028=0) defines an 18,681-patient (37.4%) responsive subgroup in
  which feature_013 yields +2.91 mo PFS (p<<1e-300). Outside that subgroup
  (62.6%), feature_013 effect is +0.003 mo (p=0.91).

Iterations 13-16 -- Refining with feature_033.
  Within the responsive subgroup, an interaction-screen identified feature_033
  as the strongest residual modifier (interaction coef = -0.138 mo per unit,
  p~0). Tertile and threshold sweeps confirmed a near-linear dose-response:
     feature_033<=8.9 (low tertile)   -> feature_013 effect +4.94 mo
     feature_033 9.0-16.5 (mid)       -> +3.69 mo
     feature_033>=16.6 (high tertile) -> -0.02 mo
  A clean threshold at feature_033<=15 captures essentially the full benefit
  (+4.65 mo within, vs +0.02 mo above the cut), giving a final 4-predicate
  responsive subgroup of n=11,575 (23.2% of cohort) with a feature_013 effect
  of +4.65 mo (p<<1e-300).

Iterations 17-22 -- Robustness and elimination of alternatives.
  * feature_023 within the full 4-predicate subgroup: feature_013 effect is
    +4.33 mo at feature_023=0 and +4.71 mo at feature_023=1 (small
    refinement only); feature_023's apparent main effect is largely a proxy
    for responsive-subgroup membership.
  * feature_016 stratification within responsive subgroup: feature_013 effect
    is +2.90 (f016=0) vs +2.92 (f016=1); no independent modification.
  * The "main" effect of feature_013 (in the four-predicate sense) is
    essentially zero in non-responders: in all 14 cells of the
    feature_029 x feature_023 x feature_018 x feature_028 cross-tab where
    any one of feature_029=0, feature_018=1, or feature_028=1 holds, the
    feature_013 mean-difference is in the range -0.32 to +0.57 (none
    statistically significant).
  * feature_005, feature_006, feature_001, feature_004, feature_031 have no
    interaction with feature_013 and behave as additive prognostic factors.
  * feature_012 has no interaction with feature_013 and is a generic
    prognostic variable.
  * Systematic screen of every binary feature as candidate treatment finds
    that all the strongest interactions involve feature_013 and the four
    predicates already identified. The next-best non-feature_013 candidate
    (feature_006 x feature_012, p~3e-12, coef -0.011 mo/yr) is a continuous
    dampening of a prognostic effect, not a treatment story. No alternative
    treatment candidate is plausible in this cohort.

Iteration 23 -- Variance explained.
  Adding the four feature_013-by-modifier interactions to the multivariable
  model raises R^2 from 0.792 to 0.858 -- about a third of the residual
  variance is explained by the heterogeneity itself, underlining that
  feature_013 cannot be treated as having a uniform main effect.

Iterations 24-25 -- Final hypotheses.

Final treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis (h35):
  feature_013 increases pfs_months by approximately +4.7 months on average
  in the subgroup defined by:
     feature_029 = 1
     AND feature_018 = 0
     AND feature_028 = 0
     AND feature_033 <= 15
  In any patient violating any one of these four predicates, the
  feature_013 effect on pfs_months is statistically indistinguishable from
  zero. The subgroup contains ~23% of the cohort.

Companion main-effect summary (h36):
  Generic prognostic factors (no treatment-effect modification of
  feature_013), with adjusted mo-per-unit effects:
     feature_012  +0.176     (favorable, continuous)
     feature_005  -1.17      (unfavorable, ordinal 0/1/2)
     feature_006  -1.55      (unfavorable, binary)
     feature_001  -0.98      (unfavorable, binary)
     feature_018  -0.47      (unfavorable, binary; also abrogates treatment)
     feature_028  -0.58      (unfavorable, binary; also abrogates treatment)
     feature_024  +0.48      (favorable, continuous)
     feature_004  -0.077     (unfavorable, continuous)
     feature_033  -0.019     (unfavorable as main effect, on top of its
                              role as a continuous modifier of feature_013)
     feature_031  -0.0005    (slightly unfavorable, continuous)
  Features that did not retain independent significance in the full
  multivariable model and showed no interaction with feature_013:
  feature_011, feature_019, feature_030, feature_023 (proxy for subgroup),
  feature_016 (proxy for subgroup), feature_021, feature_003, feature_002,
  feature_007, feature_010, feature_027, feature_034, feature_014,
  feature_037, feature_017, feature_035, feature_022, feature_008,
  feature_032, feature_020, feature_025, feature_036, feature_015,
  feature_009, feature_026.

What the evidence supported vs. refuted
---------------------------------------
Supported:
  * feature_013 is a treatment-like variable with strongly heterogeneous
    effect on pfs_months (large positive in a defined subgroup, null
    outside).
  * The exact responsive-subgroup definition is feature_029=1 AND
    feature_018=0 AND feature_028=0 AND feature_033<=15, and the effect
    inside that subgroup is +4.65 months.
  * feature_012, feature_005, feature_006, feature_001, feature_024,
    feature_004 are robust prognostic factors with the directions stated.
  * feature_018, feature_028 are dual-role: they are unfavorable prognostic
    in their own right and they abrogate the feature_013 effect.
  * feature_033 is dual-role: small unfavorable main effect plus a strong
    continuous attenuation of the feature_013 effect.
Refuted / not supported:
  * feature_023 as an independent treatment-effect modifier (it is mostly
    a proxy for responsive-subgroup membership).
  * feature_016 as an independent treatment-effect modifier (same).
  * feature_005, feature_006, feature_001, feature_004, feature_031,
    feature_012 as treatment-effect modifiers (they are purely prognostic).
  * Any other binary feature as a comparable second treatment candidate.
  * Treating feature_013 as a uniform main effect (it adds 1.10 mo on
    average only by averaging an extreme +4.7 mo subgroup with a null
    majority; the heterogeneity is the real story).

Overall conclusion
------------------
This cohort contains a single dominant therapeutic intervention indicator
(feature_013) whose benefit on progression-free survival is sharply
restricted to ~23% of patients defined by a four-predicate biomarker
profile: feature_029 must be present, feature_018 and feature_028 must be
absent, and feature_033 must be below ~15. In that subgroup feature_013
adds ~4.7 months to pfs_months; outside it, the effect is null. All other
features either contribute additive prognostic information or correlate
with subgroup membership without independent treatment-effect modification.
