Analysis summary - ds001_breast (n=50,000)
==========================================

OVERVIEW

The dataset is a single-outcome (pfs_months: progression-free survival in
months, range 0-16.66, mean 4.69) breast-cancer cohort with 37 anonymized
features. Of those features, 17 are binary (prevalence 2.4%-55%), one
(feature_005) is a 3-level ordinal, and the remaining 18 are continuous.
We worked through 15 iterations of hypothesize-test-refine and arrived
at a clean treatment / biomarker / prognostic decomposition. The final
multivariable model attains R^2 = 0.929 on the full cohort.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 1 - MARGINAL SCREENING (iterations 1-3)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

* Largest binary marginal effects on pfs_months (signed, level 1 vs 0):
  feature_006 -1.54 (p~0); feature_013 +1.10 (p~0); feature_001 -1.05
  (p=1.5e-178); feature_029 +0.57 (p=4e-143); feature_028 -0.56
  (p=6e-144); feature_018 -0.46 (p=6e-70); feature_023 +0.36 (p=6e-59).
  All other binary features had |diff|<0.13 and most were null.

* feature_005 (3 levels) showed a strong monotone gradient:
  level 0 mean PFS 5.64; level 1 mean PFS 4.44; level 2 mean PFS 3.29
  (ANOVA F=2861, p~0). Behaves like an ordinal stage/grade marker.

* Continuous features: feature_012 (range 30-90, mean 65, plausibly age)
  was the dominant signal, r = +0.702 with PFS (older patients have
  longer PFS in this synthetic cohort). PFS rises monotonically across
  age bins from ~0.6 mo (<40 yr) to ~8.2 mo (80+ yr). Other continuous
  features with non-trivial r: feature_004 -0.116, feature_024 +0.100,
  feature_033 -0.091. The remaining 14 continuous features had |r|<0.02
  or were null.

* A 36-covariate multivariable OLS attained R^2 = 0.792. Independent
  effects (per-unit on PFS): feature_005_2 -2.33; feature_006 -1.55;
  feature_005_1 -1.19; feature_013 +1.10; feature_001 -0.98;
  feature_028 -0.58; feature_029 +0.53; feature_024 +0.48 per unit;
  feature_018 -0.47; feature_012 +0.176 per yr; feature_004 -0.077;
  feature_033 -0.019. Notably, feature_023's bivariate +0.36 collapsed
  to +0.014 (n.s.) after adjustment, foreshadowing its role as a
  surrogate marker rather than an independent driver.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 2 - IS feature_013 A TREATMENT? (iteration 4)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

A logistic regression of feature_013 on every other feature yielded NO
significant coefficient (smallest p = 0.053 for feature_027); feature_013
prevalence sat at 0.347-0.355 across every stratum of every plausible
prognostic feature (feature_005 levels, feature_006, feature_001,
feature_028, feature_018, feature_029, feature_023, feature_012 quartiles,
feature_033 quartiles). This is essentially randomized assignment at
~35% prevalence. We treat feature_013 as the cohort's intervention
arm and the remaining features as candidate prognostic / predictive
biomarkers.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 3 - HETEROGENEITY OF feature_013 EFFECT (iterations 5-7, 9-12)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Stratified by the strongest prognostic features, the +1.1-month main
effect of feature_013 was REMARKABLY UNIFORM across feature_005 strata
(+1.12/+1.09/+1.15 across levels 0/1/2), feature_006 strata (+1.10/+1.10),
and feature_001 strata (+1.10/+1.06). None of these prognostic markers
modified the treatment effect.

By contrast, an exhaustive interaction screen of feature_013 x each
binary feature found three very strong modifiers:
  feature_013 x feature_028   TM = -1.62  (p = 4e-255)
  feature_013 x feature_029   TM = +1.59  (p = 9e-229)
  feature_013 x feature_018   TM = -1.36  (p = 2e-118)
  feature_013 x feature_023   TM = +1.09  (p = 3e-117)
  feature_013 x feature_016   TM = +0.42  (p = 9e-19)

A continuous-feature interaction screen turned up one strong modifier:
  feature_013 x feature_033 (per SD)   TM = -0.64  (p = 1e-174)

When we put all candidate modifiers in one joint OLS, two findings
emerged: (a) the four binary modifiers feature_028/018/029 and the
continuous feature_033 stay strong (p~0); (b) feature_023 collapses to
+0.12 (p=0.05) and feature_016 collapses to -0.0004 (p=0.99). So the
genuine effect modifiers of feature_013 are exactly:
  feature_028 (negative biomarker), feature_018 (negative biomarker),
  feature_029 (positive biomarker), feature_033 (continuous negative).

The 8-cell crosstab of (feature_028 x feature_018 x feature_029) made
this concrete. In the (0,0,1) cell (n=18681, ~37% of cohort) the
treatment effect of feature_013 was +2.91 mo (p~0). In every other
cell (combined n=31319) the treatment effect was +0.003 mo (p=0.91).
Equivalently: any patient with feature_028=1 OR feature_018=1 (n=23240)
gets ZERO benefit from feature_013 (TE=+0.018, p=0.54). And any patient
with feature_029=0 within the otherwise-eligible (0,0,*) cell also gets
zero benefit (TE=-0.04, p=0.39).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 4 - REFINEMENT BY feature_033 (iterations 9, 12)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Within the (0,0,1) responder cell, the treatment effect varied steeply
with feature_033. By feature_033 decile within responders (with mean
feature_033 increasing from 3.7 to 43.2):
  TE = +4.9, +4.9, +5.0, +5.0, +4.9, +4.0, +0.2, -0.1, -0.2, +0.2
The effect cliff sits near feature_033 ~ 14-15. A threshold sweep
(thr=10..20) showed the optimal cut is feature_033 <= 14: in-group TE
+4.94 mo, out-of-group TE within (0,0,1) responders = +0.02 mo
(p=0.85). Importantly, in untreated patients feature_033 has zero
correlation with PFS (r=-0.002, p=0.68), confirming feature_033 is
PURELY PREDICTIVE - it does not predict outcome on its own; it only
predicts who benefits from feature_013.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 5 - PROGNOSTIC vs PREDICTIVE DECOMPOSITION (iteration 8)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Restricting to untreated (feature_013=0) patients to remove any
treatment-mixing, every modifier became null and every prognostic
marker stayed strong:

  Modifiers (predictive only - null in untreated):
    feature_028   diff = +0.008, p = 0.74
    feature_018   diff = +0.015, p = 0.64
    feature_029   diff = +0.011, p = 0.68
    feature_023   diff = -0.020, p = 0.41
    feature_033   r    = -0.002, p = 0.68

  Prognostic markers (effect persists in untreated):
    feature_005   ANOVA F = 2736, p ~ 0
    feature_006   diff = -1.538, p ~ 0
    feature_001   diff = -1.029, p = 4e-155
    feature_012   r    = +0.829, p ~ 0
    feature_004   r    = -0.144, p ~ 0
    feature_024   r    = +0.119, p ~ 0

This clean separation - feature_028/018/029/023/033 are pure
predictive biomarkers, feature_005/006/001/012/004/024 are pure
prognostic markers - is the central organizing finding of the cohort.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
STAGE 6 - NO SECOND TREATMENT (iteration 11)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

We rechecked every binary feature for hidden treatment-like behavior
in the feature_013=0 subset. None had a meaningful PFS effect there
(largest |diff| = 0.020, all p > 0.20 except for the prognostic
features feature_006 and feature_001, which retain their effects but
are not treatments). There is no second randomized treatment in the
data.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP (iteration 13)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Treatment:           feature_013
  Outcome:             pfs_months (longer = better)
  Direction:           positive
  Responder subgroup:  feature_028 = 0
                   AND feature_018 = 0
                   AND feature_029 = 1
                   AND feature_033 <= 14
  Subgroup size:       n = 10,828 (21.7% of cohort)

  Treatment effect within responder subgroup:
    PFS off-treatment   4.285 mo (n=6975)
    PFS on-treatment    9.228 mo (n=3853)
    Treatment effect   +4.943 mo  (p < 1e-300)

  Treatment effect outside responder subgroup (n=39,172, 78.3%):
    Treatment effect   +0.007 mo  (p = 0.75)

A compact final OLS that combines the responder structure with the
prognostic features (feature_012, feature_005 dummies, feature_006,
feature_001, feature_004, feature_024) yields R^2 = 0.929. In that
model, T_responder = +2.92 (intercept treatment effect inside the
responder cell), T_responder x feature_033(z) = -1.68 per SD (the
within-responder dose-response decay), and the main T term in
non-responders is +0.0004 (p = 0.96).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
SUPPORTED vs REFUTED HYPOTHESES
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Supported:
  - feature_013 is the lone (randomized) treatment.
  - feature_005, feature_006, feature_001, feature_012, feature_004,
    feature_024 are prognostic, with effects that persist in
    untreated patients and add up to most of the explained variance.
  - feature_028, feature_018, feature_029, feature_033 are predictive
    biomarkers of feature_013 response, with no prognostic role.
  - The treatment effect is biomarker-confined: ~+5 months in the
    (28=0, 18=0, 29=1, 33<=14) subgroup; ~0 elsewhere.

Refuted / clarified:
  - feature_013 effect is NOT modified by feature_005, feature_006,
    feature_001, or feature_012 (the dominant prognostic features).
  - feature_023 and feature_016 are not independent effect modifiers
    once the dominant 28/18/29/33 modifiers are accounted for; their
    bivariate signals were driven by correlation with the responder
    cell.
  - There is no second treatment hidden in the binary features.

OVERALL CONCLUSION

The cohort behaves like a randomized trial of feature_013 (~35%
treated) embedded in a heterogeneous breast-cancer population. The
intervention has no average benefit outside of a sharply defined,
biomarker-positive subgroup (~22% of patients) in which it nearly
doubles median progression-free survival (4.3 -> 9.2 months). The
biomarker definition combines two negative resistance markers
(feature_028, feature_018), one positive sensitivity marker
(feature_029), and a continuous resistance threshold on feature_033.
The variables that drive raw PFS regardless of treatment - notably
feature_012, feature_005, feature_006, and feature_001 - are
orthogonal to the treatment-effect biomarker set and do not modify
its magnitude.
