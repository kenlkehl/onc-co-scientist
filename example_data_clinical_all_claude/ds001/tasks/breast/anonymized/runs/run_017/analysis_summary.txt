Analysis Summary — ds001_breast (n=50,000; outcome: pfs_months)
================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The dataset contains 37 de-identified features (mix of binary, ordinal, and
continuous lab/clinical-style values) and a single continuous outcome,
pfs_months (mean 4.69, range 0–16.7). I ran 25 propose–test–refine
iterations covering univariate associations, full multivariable modeling,
treatment-by-feature interaction screening across all binary candidates,
joint subgroup definition, continuous effect-modifier modeling, and
verification analyses. The dataset structure ultimately collapses cleanly
to a single targeted-therapy story.


HEADLINE FINDING
----------------
feature_013 is a randomly assigned (~35% prevalence, balanced across all
covariates) treatment indicator whose effect on pfs_months is profoundly
heterogeneous:

  • In the FAVORABLE subgroup
      feature_029 = 1  AND  feature_028 = 0  AND  feature_018 = 0
      AND  feature_033 ≤ ~15
    (n = 11,575; ~23% of cohort)
    feature_013 increases pfs_months by ~4.65 months on average
    (8.94 vs 4.29 months; t-test p < 1e-300; interaction p < 1e-300).

  • Outside the favorable subgroup (n = 38,425)
    feature_013 has only a +0.21-month effect — not clinically meaningful
    despite reaching p ~ 1e-20 owing to the very large sample size.

  • Within the feature_029 = 0 stratum (all 15,114 patients)
    feature_013 has essentially no effect in any of the 8 cells defined
    by (feature_018, feature_028, lo_033) — all cell effects between
    -0.25 and +0.13 months. feature_029 = 1 is therefore a NECESSARY
    condition for the treatment to work.

  • Within the feature_029 = 1 stratum, the continuous feature_033 acts
    as a quantitative effect attenuator at -0.135 PFS-months per unit
    feature_033 (interaction p = 4.9e-91). Treatment effect crosses zero
    around feature_033 ≈ 37.


HYPOTHESES EXPLORED AND OUTCOMES
--------------------------------

(1) "Several binary features are associated with pfs_months." — SUPPORTED.
    Univariate t-tests identified strong negative effects for feature_006
    (-1.54), feature_001 (-1.05), feature_028 (-0.56), feature_018 (-0.46)
    and strong positive effects for feature_013 (+1.10), feature_029
    (+0.57), feature_023 (+0.36); ordinal feature_005 produced a clean
    monotonic decrease (5.64 → 4.44 → 3.29).

(2) "Continuous predictors correlate with pfs_months." — SUPPORTED but
    DOMINATED BY ONE VARIABLE. feature_012 had Pearson r = +0.70 (likely
    a calendar/exposure-time-like or strong host-prognostic variable);
    secondary correlations were modest (feature_004 r=-0.12, feature_024
    +0.10, feature_033 -0.09, feature_031 -0.02).

(3) "A multivariable OLS model identifies the principal main effects." —
    SUPPORTED. Full 37-feature model R² = 0.79. Largest signed t-stats:
    feature_012 +0.176/u (t=343), feature_005 -1.17/level, feature_006
    -1.55, feature_013 +1.10, feature_001 -0.98, feature_004 -0.077/u,
    feature_028 -0.58, feature_024 +0.48/u, feature_033 -0.019/u,
    feature_029 +0.53, feature_018 -0.47, feature_031 -0.0005/u.

(4) "feature_023, feature_016, feature_022, feature_020 lose
    significance in multivariable models." — SUPPORTED. All four
    p > 0.15 once correlated effect modifiers are included.

(5) "feature_013 is a randomly assigned treatment indicator." —
    SUPPORTED. feature_013 prevalence is 0.346–0.355 across every
    stratum of every other major variable; difference < 0.01 absolute.

(6) "feature_028 attenuates the feature_013 effect." — SUPPORTED.
    +1.66 in feature_028=0 vs +0.04 in feature_028=1; interaction
    p = 4.0e-255. feature_028=1 essentially abolishes the benefit.

(7) "feature_018 attenuates the feature_013 effect." — SUPPORTED.
    +1.35 in feature_018=0 vs -0.01 in feature_018=1; interaction
    p = 2.2e-118.

(8) "feature_033 (continuous) attenuates the feature_013 effect
    quantitatively." — SUPPORTED. Cross-product coefficient
    -0.052/unit (p = 1.5e-174) in full sample, -0.135/unit (p = 4.9e-91)
    within the (feature_029=1, feature_028=0, feature_018=0) stratum.

(9) "feature_029 is a predictive biomarker, not a prognostic factor."
    — SUPPORTED in the strongest possible sense. feature_029 main
    effect among feature_013=0 patients: +0.011 months (p = 0.68);
    among feature_013=1 patients: +1.605 months (p < 1e-245).
    Interaction p = 9.3e-229.

(10) "feature_005 (stage-like), feature_006, feature_001 are pure
     prognostic factors with no treatment-by-feature interaction." —
     SUPPORTED. feature_013 effect is essentially identical (~+1.1
     months) across every level of feature_005, feature_006,
     feature_001 — differences ≤ 0.06 months.

(11) "feature_012, feature_004, feature_024, feature_031 are pure
     prognostic factors." — SUPPORTED. Their per-unit
     treatment-by-feature interaction coefficients are ≤ 4% the
     magnitude of their main-effect coefficients; all have p > 0.05
     except a marginal feature_012 (p = 0.044, but coefficient is tiny
     at -0.003/unit).

(12) "feature_023 and feature_016 modifier effects are explained by
     correlation with feature_029 and feature_018 respectively." —
     SUPPORTED. feature_023~feature_029 r = +0.669; feature_016~
     feature_018 r = -0.361. After conditioning on the four-variable
     favorable subgroup, incremental treatment-by-feature_023
     interaction p = 0.068, treatment-by-feature_016 interaction
     p = 0.38.

(13) "No second hidden treatment exists in the favorable subgroup." —
     SUPPORTED. None of the other binary candidates (feature_011,
     feature_030, feature_019, feature_023, feature_016, feature_015,
     feature_022, feature_020) shows a positive significant effect on
     pfs_months within the favorable subgroup. The strongest signal
     is feature_019 in the NEGATIVE direction (-0.16, p = 0.009),
     consistent with a small selection or confounding artifact.

(14) "Searching every binary feature as a candidate treatment, only
     feature_013 has the asymmetric pattern of an effective therapy."
     — SUPPORTED. Other features that surface in the search are
     either mirror-images of the feature_013 interaction (feature_029,
     feature_023 positive; feature_028, feature_018 negative),
     prognostic markers (feature_006, feature_001), or null.

(15) "After adjusting for the favorable-subgroup interaction,
     feature_028 and feature_018 have NO independent prognostic
     effect on pfs_months." — SUPPORTED. In the joint adjusted
     R² = 0.976 model, feature_028 p = 0.10, feature_018 p = 0.93,
     and feature_033 main coef shrinks from -0.019 to -0.0013/unit.
     Their univariate negativity is essentially fully mediated by
     their suppressing role on the targeted treatment.

(16) "The single best summary multivariable model with the favorable-
     subgroup interaction explains > 97% of pfs_months variance." —
     SUPPORTED. R² = 0.9762.

(17) "feature_011 is a clinically neutral covariate." — SUPPORTED.
     Univariate diff = -0.013 (p = 0.56); multivariable p = 0.21; no
     meaningful interaction.

(18) "feature_009 and feature_026 (each ~2.5% prevalence) have no
     meaningful PFS association." — SUPPORTED.

(19) "feature_006 and feature_001 are independent strong prognostic
     factors but do not change the absolute treatment benefit from
     feature_013." — SUPPORTED. Their coefficient magnitudes (~-1.5
     and ~-1.0 PFS-months) survive the adjusted model essentially
     unchanged from the univariate estimate.


PROGNOSTIC vs PREDICTIVE — A CLEAN DECOUPLING
---------------------------------------------
The cohort cleanly partitions features into three roles:

  • PURE PROGNOSTIC (act on baseline pfs_months independent of
    treatment):
       feature_005 (-1.17/level)
       feature_006 (-1.55)
       feature_001 (-0.98)
       feature_012 (+0.176/unit)  — dominant continuous predictor
       feature_004 (-0.077/unit)
       feature_024 (+0.48/unit)
       feature_031 (-0.0005/unit)

  • TREATMENT:
       feature_013

  • PREDICTIVE BIOMARKERS / EFFECT MODIFIERS (only matter through
    feature_013):
       feature_029 (necessary positive biomarker)
       feature_028 (must be 0 — otherwise treatment fails)
       feature_018 (must be 0 — otherwise treatment fails)
       feature_033 (continuous attenuator)

  • NEUTRAL / SHADOW / RARE:
       feature_011, feature_009, feature_026, feature_030, feature_019,
       feature_022, feature_020, feature_015, feature_032, feature_008
       (no meaningful main or interaction signal)
       feature_023 (shadow of feature_029, r = +0.669)
       feature_016 (shadow of feature_018, r = -0.361)


FINAL CANONICAL TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS
----------------------------------------------------
Treatment:  feature_013 = 1
Outcome:    pfs_months
Direction:  POSITIVE  (mean PFS increase)
Subgroup that defines the effect:
            feature_029 = 1
      AND   feature_028 = 0
      AND   feature_018 = 0
      AND   feature_033 ≤ ~15  (continuous, with linear attenuation
                                -0.135 PFS-months per unit feature_033
                                even within this stratum)

Magnitude inside subgroup:    +4.65 months (n = 11,575; p < 1e-300)
Magnitude outside subgroup:   +0.21 months (no clinical meaning)

This is consistent with a targeted therapy (feature_013) that requires
a positive companion biomarker (feature_029) and whose efficacy is
abolished by adverse disease markers (feature_028, feature_018) or by
elevated values of an inflammatory/disease-burden lab (feature_033).
The clean separation between prognostic factors (which shift baseline
PFS without modifying the treatment benefit) and predictive factors
(which gate the treatment benefit but do not move baseline PFS) is the
defining structural feature of this cohort.
