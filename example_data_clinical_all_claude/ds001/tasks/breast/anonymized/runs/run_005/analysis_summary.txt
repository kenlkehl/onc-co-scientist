ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_breast (n=50,000; outcome pfs_months)
================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The dataset contains 50,000 de-identified patient records with 37 anonymized
features and a single continuous outcome, pfs_months (mean 4.69, sd 2.50,
range 0–16.66). I conducted 25 propose–test–refine iterations covering
(a) main-effect screens for every feature, (b) multivariable adjustment,
(c) randomization-balance checks to identify treatment indicators, and
(d) systematic treatment-by-feature interaction screens for every plausible
treatment candidate. The clear, dominant finding is a treatment-effect
heterogeneity story for feature_013 governed by four predictive predicates.


PRINCIPAL FINDING — Beneficial treatment with a 4-predicate biomarker subgroup
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
feature_013 is a randomized binary treatment indicator (~35% prevalence,
perfectly balanced against every other variable in N=50,000). Overall it
produces a +1.10 month PFS gain (5.41 vs 4.31; Welch p<1e-300; multivariable
β=+1.101, p<1e-300). However, that overall benefit is driven entirely by a
biomarker-defined subgroup:

  Responder subgroup:
      feature_029 = 1   AND
      feature_018 = 0   AND
      feature_028 = 0   AND
      feature_033 ≤ 15

  Inside (n = 11,575; 23.2% of cohort)
      Mean PFS treated  = 8.75 months
      Mean PFS control  = 4.10 months
      Δ = +4.65 months  (Welch p < 1e-300)

  Outside (n = 38,425; 76.8%)
      Δ ≈ -0.04 months  (NS, p > 0.1)

Each predicate is necessary. Drop-tests (flipping one predicate while
holding the other three) collapse the within-subgroup treatment effect to
within ±0.06 months of zero in every case. Formal OLS interaction terms in a
joint model are all highly significant: feature_013×feature_029 = +1.530;
feature_013×feature_018 = -1.314; feature_013×feature_028 = -1.603;
feature_013×(feature_033≤15) = +1.745 (each p < 1e-22). Thus the responder
subgroup is multiplicatively, not additively, defined.


ANTAGONIST FINDING — feature_028
--------------------------------
feature_028 (binary, ~35% prevalence, perfectly balanced) shows a -0.56
month main effect on PFS, but stratification reveals this is a pure
interaction:

  feature_013 = 0  →  feature_028 effect = +0.008 mo (p=0.73, NS)
  feature_013 = 1  →  feature_028 effect = -1.615 mo (p < 1e-300)

So feature_028 has no independent prognostic role — it functions as an
antagonist that fully cancels the feature_013 benefit. Mechanistically this
is consistent with a co-prescribed medication, an exposure, or an
alternative therapy that competes with the active treatment.


HYPOTHESES THAT WERE REFUTED
----------------------------
1. feature_011, feature_030, feature_019 (binary indicators with ~45–60%
   prevalence, all balanced like a randomized arm) showed null main effects
   (all |Δ|<0.03 mo, p>0.25) AND no detectable treatment-by-stratum
   heterogeneity in any of the >150 stratifications screened. They are
   either null indicators or treatments with no causal effect.

2. feature_023 initially looked like a +0.36 mo positive prognostic marker,
   but after multivariable adjustment its coefficient collapsed to +0.014
   (p=0.32). Most of feature_023's apparent effect — and most of its
   apparent modifier action on feature_013 — is explained by its strong
   correlation with feature_029 (phi ≈ 0.67).

3. There is no subgroup in which feature_013 produces statistically
   significant net HARM. An exhaustive 2-way binary stratification screen
   (>900 candidate subgroups) found zero subgroups meeting Δ<-0.3 with
   p<0.01. The pattern is benefit-or-null, never harm.

4. feature_005 (ordinal 0/1/2; ECOG-like dose response of -1.18 mo per
   level) and feature_006 (binary, -1.55 mo) are strong PROGNOSTIC factors
   but do NOT modify the feature_013 treatment effect (treatment effects in
   each stratum within ±0.06 mo of overall).


PROGNOSTIC PROFILE (independent of treatment, multivariable OLS, R²=0.79)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
Better PFS:
  feature_012  +0.176 mo / unit   (range 30–90; dominant continuous predictor)
  feature_024  +0.476 mo / unit   (range 1.5–5.5; lab-like, e.g. albumin-style)
  feature_029  +0.532 mo          (binary positive prognostic / predictive)

Worse PFS:
  feature_005  -1.172 mo / level  (ECOG-style 0/1/2)
  feature_006  -1.549 mo          (binary; likely metastatic-disease analogue)
  feature_001  -0.983 mo          (rare binary; ~10% prevalence)
  feature_018  -0.471 mo          (binary; also negative predictor for feature_013)
  feature_028  -0.581 mo          (driven by feature_013 interaction; see above)
  feature_033  -0.019 mo / unit   (range 1–100; negative continuous)
  feature_004  -0.077 mo / unit   (range 0–24; negative continuous)
  feature_031  -0.0005 mo / unit  (small but real)


SUMMARY OF EVIDENCE BY HYPOTHESIS CLASS
---------------------------------------
SUPPORTED:
  - Strong positive main effect of feature_012 on PFS.
  - Strong dose-response of feature_005 (worse with higher level).
  - Negative main effects of feature_006, feature_001, feature_018,
    feature_028, feature_033, feature_004 on PFS.
  - Positive main effects of feature_029, feature_024 on PFS.
  - feature_013 as a beneficial randomized treatment.
  - feature_013 effect predicated on feature_029=1, feature_018=0,
    feature_028=0, feature_033≤15 (qualitative interactions).
  - feature_028 acting as a treatment antagonist rather than a prognostic.

REFUTED / NOT SUPPORTED:
  - feature_011, feature_030, feature_019 having any causal effect.
  - feature_023 as an independent prognostic or independent modifier.
  - Existence of any subgroup with net feature_013 harm.
  - feature_005 / feature_006 / feature_001 modifying the feature_013 effect.


CLINICAL/SCIENTIFIC INTERPRETATION
----------------------------------
The architecture observed (a single drug with a four-feature predictive
signature: positive biomarker present, resistance marker absent, antagonist
absent, low value of a continuous tumour-burden-style marker) is highly
characteristic of a targeted oncology therapy in breast cancer — for
example, an HR+/HER2- selective therapy with a resistance mutation, a
co-medication that suppresses efficacy, and a tumour-burden modifier. With
~23% of the cohort meeting all four predicates and a +4.65 month PFS gain
in that group versus essentially no benefit elsewhere, the data strongly
support biomarker-directed deployment of feature_013 rather than universal
use.
