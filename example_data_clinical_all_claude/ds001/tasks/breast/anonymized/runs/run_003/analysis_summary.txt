Analysis Summary — ds001_breast (50,000 patients, anonymized features)
========================================================================

Outcome analyzed: pfs_months (continuous, mean 4.69, median 4.54, range 0–16.7).

OVERALL CONCLUSION
------------------
This cohort contains one binary treatment variable (feature_013) whose
benefit is sharply confined to a single, biologically interpretable
subgroup defined by a positive predictive biomarker, the absence of two
binary resistance markers, and a low value on one continuous resistance
biomarker. Outside that subgroup, treatment has no detectable effect on
progression-free survival. The remainder of the dataset is rich in
classical prognostic factors (age-like, stage-like, lab markers) that
operate on PFS independently of treatment.

The single best-supported treatment-effect-heterogeneity claim is:

  feature_013 prolongs pfs_months by ~+4.7 months ONLY in patients with
  feature_029=1  AND  feature_028=0  AND  feature_018=0  AND  feature_033<15.
  In all other patients (77% of the cohort) feature_013 has no effect.

This subgroup contains 11,490 patients (23.0% of the cohort). Within the
subgroup the unadjusted treatment effect is +4.687 mo (t=102.4, p<<1e-300);
after adjusting for feature_012, feature_005, feature_006, feature_001,
feature_024, feature_004, and feature_033, the effect is +4.713 mo (t=361,
p<<1e-300). Outside the subgroup the unadjusted effect is +0.005 mo
(p=0.82) and the adjusted effect is -0.0002 mo (p=0.90).


WALK-THROUGH OF HYPOTHESES TESTED
---------------------------------
Iteration 1 — Univariate screen of every feature vs PFS.
  - Strong binary main effects: feature_006 (-1.54 mo, p≈0), feature_013
    (+1.10, p≈0), feature_001 (-1.05, p=1e-178), feature_028 (-0.56,
    p=6e-144), feature_029 (+0.57, p=4e-143), feature_018 (-0.46,
    p=6e-70), feature_023 (+0.36, p=6e-59).
  - Continuous: feature_012 r=+0.70 (likely an age-like or time-like
    variable), feature_004 r=-0.115, feature_024 r=+0.100, feature_033
    r=-0.091. All other |r|<0.02.
  - feature_005 (3-level): means 5.64 → 4.44 → 3.29 across levels 0/1/2;
    monotone, ANOVA p≈0; consistent with disease stage/grade.
  - All three iter-1 hypotheses (some binary, some continuous, ordinal
    feature_005) were SUPPORTED.

Iteration 2 — Multivariable OLS containing every feature.
  - R² = 0.79. Independent predictors (all p<0.001 unless noted):
    feature_012 +0.176/unit; feature_005 levels -1.19, -2.33;
    feature_006 -1.55; feature_001 -0.98; feature_028 -0.58; feature_018
    -0.47; feature_029 +0.53; feature_013 +1.10; feature_024 +0.476/unit;
    feature_004 -0.077/unit; feature_033 -0.019/unit; feature_031
    -5e-4/unit. Most other features (~25 of them) lost any apparent
    main-effect signal after adjustment.
  - SUPPORTED.

Iteration 3 — Treatment vs biomarker assignment.
  - feature_013 (35% prev) and feature_029 (70%) are the two beneficial
    binary signals. They are uncorrelated (r=0.0008). feature_029 has a
    near-twin feature_023 (r=0.67). The asymmetric prevalences and
    structure suggested feature_013 = treatment, feature_029 = predictive
    biomarker; confirmed by the iter-4 interaction.

Iteration 4 — Interaction tests for the biomarker hypothesis.
  - feature_013 × feature_029: +1.594 mo (p=9e-229); main effects of
    each alone are NULL. Cell means: (T0,B0)=4.30; (T0,B1)=4.31;
    (T1,B0)=4.29; (T1,B1)=5.89. SUPPORTED.
  - feature_013 × feature_023: +1.093 (p=3e-117); attributable to
    feature_023 ≈ feature_029 by correlation. NOT independent.

Iteration 5 — Adjusted interaction.
  - With all major prognostics held constant, feature_013 × feature_029
    remains +1.560 (p≈0); both main effects null. The biomarker-positive
    treatment effect is not confounded by stage, age, etc. SUPPORTED.

Iteration 6 — Search for negative modifiers.
  - feature_013 × feature_028: -1.62 (p=4e-255) — strongest negative
    modifier in the entire screen.
  - feature_013 × feature_018: -1.36 (p=2e-118).
  - feature_013 × feature_033 (continuous): -0.052/unit (p=2e-174).
  - All three SUPPORTED. These behave like resistance markers.

Iteration 7 — Stratified confirmation that feature_028 and feature_018
each abolish treatment effect within the biomarker-positive (feature_029=1)
stratum.
  - Within feature_029=1, feature_013 effect is +2.38 when feature_028=0
    and essentially zero when feature_028=1 (interaction -2.34, p≈0).
  - Same for feature_018: +1.92 when feature_018=0, ~0 when feature_018=1
    (interaction -1.90, p≈0).
  - SUPPORTED.

Iteration 8 — Joint subgroup (feature_029=1, feature_028=0,
feature_018=0). Treatment effect inside = +2.91 mo (t=65.6, p≈0). Outside
= +0.003 mo (p=0.91). SUPPORTED.

Iteration 9 — Continuous modifier feature_033 within the joint subgroup.
  - feature_013 × feature_033 within subgroup: -0.135 mo per unit (p≈0).
    Each ~7-unit increase in feature_033 erases ~1 month of treatment
    benefit. SUPPORTED.

Iteration 10 — Cutpoint search for feature_033.
  - Threshold 15 (≈ subgroup median) cleanly separates a uniform large
    treatment effect (~+4.69 mo) from essentially zero (+0.015). Clean
    rectangular cutpoint. SUPPORTED.

Iteration 11 — Refined 4-feature subgroup.
  - feature_029=1, feature_028=0, feature_018=0, feature_033<15 (n=11,490;
    23.0% of cohort): treatment effect = +4.687 mo (t=102.4, p≈0).
  - Outside (n=38,510): +0.005 mo (p=0.82).
  - feature_013 prevalence is 35.4% inside vs 34.7% outside the
    subgroup: balanced, consistent with random assignment.
  - SUPPORTED.

Iterations 12–13 — Search for additional modifiers within the refined
subgroup (binary and continuous, screened separately).
  - No additional modifier survives Bonferroni correction for the number
    of tests. Closest signal: feature_023 (+0.35 mo, uncorrected p=0.005)
    — small and likely residual feature_029-correlation. NOT supported as
    independent modifiers.

Iteration 14 — Stage (feature_005) is purely prognostic.
  - Within the refined subgroup, treatment-by-stage interactions are null
    (p=0.41 and 0.70). feature_013 benefit is uniform across stage.
    SUPPORTED.

Iteration 15 — Kitchen-sink confirmation. Fit a single OLS containing
feature_013 + feature_013 × every other feature simultaneously. Top |t|
interaction terms (in absolute value): feature_028 t=-89, feature_033 t=-73,
feature_029 t=+58, feature_018 t=-54. Next strongest is feature_007 t=+3.3
— more than an order of magnitude weaker. The 4-variable subgroup is
complete.

Iteration 16 — Brute-force enumeration of all 16 cells defined by the four
modifiers (feature_029, feature_028, feature_018, feature_033>=15).
Treatment effect is statistically meaningful in exactly ONE cell:
(1,0,0,0) +4.687 mo. Effects in the other 15 cells range from -0.282 to
+0.172 mo and only one (1,1,0,1) reaches uncorrected p<0.05 (+0.169,
p=0.018) — does not survive multiple-comparison correction. The subgroup
shape is rectangular (pure AND of four predicates).

Iterations 17–18 — Adjusted treatment effect inside vs outside.
  - Inside (n=11,490, R²=0.956): +4.713 mo (p≈0).
  - Outside (n=38,510, R²=0.992): -0.0002 mo (p=0.90).

Iterations 19–22 — Characterization of the prognostic factors.
  - feature_006 -1.55 mo: largest negative prognostic, ~30% prevalence;
    plausibly visceral/metastatic disease. SUPPORTED.
  - feature_001 -0.98 mo, ~10% prevalence; plausibly a poor-prognosis
    histology marker. SUPPORTED.
  - feature_024 +0.476/unit (range 1.5–5.5; plausibly serum albumin).
    SUPPORTED.
  - feature_004 -0.077/unit (range 0–25; plausibly tumor-burden marker).
    SUPPORTED.
  - feature_005 (3-level, plausibly stage/grade): -1.19, -2.33 mo.
    Pure prognostic (no treatment interaction). SUPPORTED.
  - feature_028 and feature_018 each play DUAL roles: small negative
    main effect AND major treatment-resistance-marker effect within the
    biomarker-positive subgroup. SUPPORTED.

Iteration 20 — feature_012 (continuous, range 30-90, mean 65; plausibly
"age" by range/mean) is the strongest single predictor at +0.176 mo per
unit. The POSITIVE direction is the opposite of typical real-world
age–PFS associations; this argues either that the variable is not
biological age (could be a calendar-time index) or that the dataset is
synthetic. Supported as a strong predictor; clinical interpretation
flagged.

Iteration 23 — feature_029 is a PURE predictive biomarker (no main
effect after adjustment, +1.56 interaction with feature_013). Its
apparent +0.57 mo univariate effect is fully explained by treatment
heterogeneity, not by independent prognostic signal. SUPPORTED.

Iteration 24 — feature_033 acts mainly as a continuous treatment-
modifying biomarker (interaction coefficient -0.052/unit) with only a
small main effect (-0.019/unit). Acts more like a tumor-biology
modifier than a pure prognostic. SUPPORTED.

Iteration 25 — FINAL HYPOTHESIS (treatment-effect heterogeneity).
  - Treatment: feature_013.
  - Outcome: pfs_months.
  - Direction: positive (longer PFS).
  - Subgroup: feature_029=1 AND feature_028=0 AND feature_018=0 AND
    feature_033<15.
  - Magnitude: +4.687 mo unadjusted (+4.713 mo adjusted).
  - Significance: t=102.4, p<<1e-300.
  - Outside-subgroup effect: ≈ 0 (p=0.82 unadjusted, p=0.90 adjusted).
  - Subgroup size: 11,490 patients (23.0% of cohort).


HYPOTHESES THAT WERE REFUTED OR DOWNGRADED
-------------------------------------------
- Many univariate-significant effects (e.g., feature_023, feature_016,
  feature_020, feature_022, feature_026, feature_009 main effects) lost
  significance under multivariable adjustment, indicating they were
  confounded.
- feature_005 × feature_013 interaction (treatment-by-stage): hypothesized
  but null (p=0.41/0.70). Treatment benefit is uniform across stage.
- Within the refined subgroup, no additional binary or continuous feature
  achieves a Bonferroni-significant treatment-effect-modifier signal —
  the responsive subgroup definition is complete at 4 features.
- feature_029 as an independent prognostic factor: refuted (adjusted
  main effect = -0.0004, p=0.97). It is a pure predictive biomarker, not
  prognostic.


SUMMARY OF FEATURE ROLES (BEST INFERENCE)
-----------------------------------------
- feature_013: TREATMENT (binary, ~35% prevalence, balanced across
  subgroup membership).
- feature_029: PREDICTIVE biomarker for treatment response.
- feature_028, feature_018: RESISTANCE markers (binary; their presence
  abolishes treatment benefit within biomarker-positive patients) AND
  weak negative prognostics.
- feature_033: continuous RESISTANCE marker (cutpoint ~15) AND weak
  negative prognostic.
- feature_023: near-proxy for feature_029 (r=0.67); no independent role.
- feature_006, feature_001: PURE PROGNOSTIC (negative; large effects).
- feature_005: PURE PROGNOSTIC (3-level ordinal; stage/grade-like).
- feature_012: PURE PROGNOSTIC (continuous; +0.176/unit; direction
  opposite to typical biological age, suggesting synthetic data or a
  non-age variable).
- feature_024, feature_004, feature_031: PURE PROGNOSTIC (continuous).
- All other features (feature_011, feature_030, feature_019, feature_016,
  feature_009, feature_026, feature_015, feature_022, feature_008,
  feature_032, feature_020, feature_036, feature_021, feature_003,
  feature_025, feature_027, feature_034, feature_014, feature_037,
  feature_017, feature_035, feature_002, feature_007, feature_010): no
  reliable independent main effect or treatment interaction detectable.
