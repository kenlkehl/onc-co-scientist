ds001_breast — Analysis Summary
================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with 37 anonymized features and a single outcome,
pfs_months (mean 4.69, median 4.54, range 0.0–16.7). 1,219 patients have
pfs_months exactly 0 (immediate progression).

Below is a walkthrough of the hypotheses explored across 25 iterations,
the statistical evidence for/against each, and the final treatment–biomarker
–outcome conclusions for this cohort.

1. Main effects (iterations 1–4)
--------------------------------
Univariate screening (Welch t-tests for binary features, Pearson r for
continuous features) ranked features by their association with pfs_months.

Strongest positive associations (longer PFS):
  - feature_012 (continuous, range 30–90, mean 65; behaves like patient age):
    Pearson r=+0.70, slope +0.176 months per unit (p<1e-300).
  - feature_013 (binary, ~35%): mean pfs 5.41 vs 4.31, +1.10 months
    (p<1e-300). The single largest binary main effect on pfs_months.
  - feature_029 (binary, ~70%): +0.57 months (p~3.7e-143).
  - feature_023 (binary, ~64%): +0.36 months (p~6e-59); however
    feature_023 is highly correlated with feature_029 (Pearson r=0.67).
  - feature_024 (continuous, mean 3.8, range 1.5–5.5; behaves like a
    favorable nutritional/biochemical marker, e.g. albumin): r=+0.10.

Strongest negative associations (shorter PFS):
  - feature_006 (binary, ~30%): -1.54 months (p<1e-300).
  - feature_005 (ordinal 0/1/2): -1.18 months per level (p<1e-300).
  - feature_001 (binary, ~10%): -1.05 months (p~1e-178).
  - feature_028 (binary, ~35%): -0.56 months (p~6e-144).
  - feature_018 (binary, ~18%): -0.46 months (p~6e-70).
  - feature_004 (continuous, range 0–25, mean 3.8; behaves like a
    disease-burden count): r=-0.12.
  - feature_033 (continuous, range 1–100, mean 16, right-skewed; behaves
    like a tumor-burden biomarker): r=-0.09.

All other binaries (feature_011, 030, 019, 015, 022, 008, 032, 020, 009,
026, 016) had only marginal or non-significant univariate associations
with pfs_months.

2. Multivariable model (iteration 5)
------------------------------------
A single OLS containing all 37 features achieved R^2 = 0.79. The adjusted
coefficient for feature_013 was +1.101 months (SE 0.011, t=103, p<1e-300),
nearly identical to the univariate estimate. This independence suggests
feature_013 behaves like a randomized/exogenous treatment indicator rather
than a disease-severity proxy.

Other independently significant predictors closely tracked their univariate
directions: feature_012 (+0.176/unit), feature_005 (-1.17/level),
feature_006 (-1.55), feature_001 (-0.98), feature_028 (-0.58),
feature_029 (+0.53), feature_018 (-0.47), feature_023 (~0 after adjusting
for feature_029, confirming overlap), feature_004 (-0.077/unit),
feature_024 (+0.476/unit), feature_033 (-0.019/unit), feature_031
(-0.0005/unit).

We treat feature_013 as the candidate "treatment" for the rest of the
analysis. No other binary feature has the prevalence + main-effect +
heterogeneity profile of a treatment indicator (iteration 20).

3. Treatment × feature interaction screen (iteration 6)
-------------------------------------------------------
For each non-treatment feature f, we fit pfs ~ feature_013 + f +
feature_013:f and ranked by interaction p-value. Bonferroni-significant
interactions (p < 0.05/36) at very small p-values:

  feature_028  interaction = -1.62  (p ~ 4e-255)
  feature_029  interaction = +1.59  (p ~ 9e-229)
  feature_033  interaction = -0.052/unit (p ~ 1.5e-174)
  feature_018  interaction = -1.36  (p ~ 2e-118)
  feature_023  interaction = +1.09  (p ~ 3e-117)
  feature_016  interaction = +0.42  (p ~ 9e-19)

All other interactions were orders of magnitude weaker.

4. Stratified treatment-effect estimates (iterations 7–10)
----------------------------------------------------------
For each top modifier, we computed feature_013's effect within each level:

  feature_028=0: tx_eff = +1.659 (p<1e-300)
  feature_028=1: tx_eff = +0.036 (p=0.29, NS)

  feature_029=1: tx_eff = +1.577 (p<1e-300)
  feature_029=0: tx_eff = -0.017 (p=0.63, NS)

  feature_018=0: tx_eff = +1.350 (p<1e-300)
  feature_018=1: tx_eff = -0.014 (p=0.77, NS)

  feature_023=1: tx_eff = +1.491 (p<1e-300)
  feature_023=0: tx_eff = +0.399 (p~1e-26)

  feature_016=1: tx_eff = +1.363
  feature_016=0: tx_eff = +0.943   (modest contrast)

Interpretation: each of feature_029=1, feature_028=0, and feature_018=0
appears NECESSARY for the treatment effect; the effect collapses to
zero whenever any of the three is at the unfavorable level.
feature_023 modifies effect size but does not abolish it (consistent
with its high overlap with feature_029).

5. Joint subgroup discovery (iterations 11–13)
----------------------------------------------
The three-criterion intersection (feature_029=1 AND feature_028=0 AND
feature_018=0) defines a subgroup of n=18,681 (37.4% of the cohort).
Within this subgroup the feature_013 treatment effect is +2.908 months
(treated mean 7.21 vs untreated 4.30, p<1e-300). In the complement
(n=31,319) the effect is +0.003 months (p=0.91, fully null).

Probing each violation pattern:
  feature_029=0 only            : tx_eff = -0.04  (p=0.39, NS)
  feature_028=1 only            : tx_eff = +0.02  (p=0.62, NS)
  feature_018=1 only            : tx_eff = -0.02  (p=0.81, NS)
  feature_029=0 + feature_028=1 : tx_eff = +0.08  (p=0.25, NS)
  feature_029=0 + feature_018=1 : tx_eff = -0.08  (p=0.48, NS)
  feature_028=1 + feature_018=1 : tx_eff = +0.09  (p=0.34, NS)
  all three failed              : tx_eff = -0.12  (p=0.37, NS)

Conclusion: suppression of the treatment effect is logical-OR over the
three violations — any single one suffices to abolish the benefit.

6. Continuous modifier within the subgroup (iterations 14–16)
-------------------------------------------------------------
Inside the favorable three-criterion subgroup, feature_033 modifies the
treatment effect monotonically:

  feature_033 quartile 1 (1.0–7.5):   tx_eff = +4.93
  feature_033 quartile 2 (7.6–12.2):  tx_eff = +4.97
  feature_033 quartile 3 (12.3–19.6): tx_eff = +1.72
  feature_033 quartile 4 (19.7–100):  tx_eff = -0.05 (p=0.44, NS)

A within-subgroup OLS pfs ~ feature_013 + feature_033 +
feature_013:feature_033 estimates:
  feature_013 main = +5.008 (SE 0.059)
  feature_033 main = -0.0005 (NS — feature_033 alone is not prognostic
                              within this subgroup)
  feature_013 × feature_033 = -0.135 per unit (p<1e-300)

The implied treatment effects are +4.33 at f033=5, +2.98 at f033=15,
+1.63 at f033=25, and -1.75 at f033=50. Crucially, in the complement
of the three-criterion subgroup feature_033 has NO modifying effect
on treatment (tertile tx effects: -0.03 / +0.04 / +0.00; all NS).
feature_033 acts as a treatment-effect modifier only after the three
binary criteria are met.

7. Other potential modifiers — what does NOT modify (iterations 18–19)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Within the favorable subgroup, no binary feature beyond feature_033 has
a strongly significant interaction with feature_013. Patient age
(feature_012) does NOT modify the treatment effect: tx_eff = +2.96 in
the youngest tertile, +2.79 in the middle, +2.96 in the oldest.

Likewise, the strong negative main-effect features (feature_005,
feature_006, feature_001, feature_004) behave like additive
disease-severity markers in the multivariable OLS and do not show a
treatment-by-biomarker pattern resembling drug heterogeneity.

8. Final, best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis
-------------------------------------------------------------
TREATMENT: feature_013
OUTCOME:   pfs_months
DIRECTION: longer pfs_months in treated patients
SUBGROUP:  feature_029 = 1
       AND feature_028 = 0
       AND feature_018 = 0
       AND feature_033 ≲ 19.6 (graded continuous modifier; effect peaks
                               at low feature_033, decays linearly with
                               slope -0.135 month per unit, vanishes
                               around feature_033 ~ 37)

Within this refined subgroup (n=14,047, 28% of cohort), feature_013 is
associated with a +3.878 month increase in pfs_months (p<1e-300). With
the more aggressive cap feature_033 ≤ 12.2 (n=9,368) the effect is
+4.95 months. With no feature_033 cap (basic three-criterion definition,
n=18,681) the effect is +2.91 months. In every complement the effect is
indistinguishable from zero.

Each of the three binary criteria is necessary: dropping any one (e.g.
allowing feature_018=1 patients in) collapses the effect to zero.
feature_023 = 1 is empirically consistent with the favorable subgroup
(>87% of feature_023=1 patients are also feature_029=1) but adds
essentially no predictive value beyond feature_029.

9. Hypotheses supported vs refuted
----------------------------------
Supported:
  - feature_012 positively associated with pfs_months (very strong, r=0.70).
  - feature_013 has a large independent positive effect on pfs_months and
    behaves like a treatment indicator.
  - feature_005, 006, 001, 028, 018 are negatively associated with pfs.
  - The feature_013 treatment effect is heterogeneous across patients.
  - The treatment effect is fully concentrated in the conjunction
    feature_029=1 ∩ feature_028=0 ∩ feature_018=0 (each criterion
    necessary; OR-suppression on violations).
  - Within that subgroup, feature_033 (continuous) further modulates
    the effect with a linear gradient, near-zero benefit above ~37.
  - Patient age (feature_012) does NOT modify the treatment effect.

Refuted / not supported:
  - feature_023 as an independent driver of treatment heterogeneity
    (it is largely redundant with feature_029).
  - feature_033 as a universal modifier (it modulates only inside the
    favorable subgroup; outside, no effect).
  - Any other binary feature as a candidate treatment indicator
    (none has the heterogeneity profile of feature_013).
  - feature_005/006/001/004 as treatment indicators (they behave like
    additive severity markers).

Overall, the dataset contains a single strong treatment signal
(feature_013) whose benefit is concentrated in a clearly defined
biomarker subgroup, with one continuous modifier acting only inside
that subgroup. The remaining features partition cleanly into
disease-severity markers (negative main effects, additive) and subtype
markers (correlated, partially redundant, modify treatment effect via
the necessity of feature_029=1).
