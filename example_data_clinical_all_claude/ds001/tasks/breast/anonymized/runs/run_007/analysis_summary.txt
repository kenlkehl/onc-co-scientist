Oncology cohort `ds001_breast` (n=50,000) — analysis summary
================================================================

Goal: explore associations between 37 de-identified features and the
single outcome `pfs_months`, surface clinically meaningful subgroup and
treatment-effect patterns, and statistically test each pattern across
iterative refinement.

1. Marginal effects (Iteration 1–2)
-----------------------------------
Of the 18 binary features, the largest signed associations with
`pfs_months` were:

  * Positive (treatment-like):
      feature_013 (+1.10 mo, p<1e-300)
      feature_029 (+0.57 mo, p~3.7e-143)
      feature_023 (+0.36 mo, p~5.8e-59)
      feature_016 (+0.11 mo, p~1.0e-6)
  * Negative (poor-prognostic):
      feature_006 (−1.54 mo, p<1e-300)
      feature_001 (−1.05 mo, p~1.5e-178)
      feature_028 (−0.56 mo, p~6.1e-144)
      feature_018 (−0.46 mo, p~6.1e-70)
  * The remaining ten binaries (feature_011, feature_030, feature_019,
      feature_015, feature_022, feature_008, feature_032, feature_020,
      feature_009, feature_026) had |Δ|<0.1 month and either non-
      significant or only borderline-significant marginal differences.

The single ordinal feature, feature_005 (0/1/2), showed a strict
monotone decline in pfs_months: 5.64 → 4.44 → 3.29 (one-way ANOVA
F=2861, p≈0). This pattern is consistent with cancer stage.

Among continuous features, the strongest associations with pfs_months
were:

  * feature_012 (range 30–90, mean ~65): Pearson r = +0.70, OLS slope
      +0.176 mo per unit. The very large effect size and the variable's
      30-to-90 range suggest this column encodes patient age, although
      a positive PFS slope with age is unusual and may reflect a study-
      specific or simulation-injected pattern.
  * feature_004: r = −0.116; OLS slope −0.077 mo/unit (p<1e-50).
  * feature_033: r = −0.091; OLS slope −0.019 mo/unit (p<1e-40).
  * feature_024: r = +0.100; OLS slope +0.476 mo/unit (p<1e-50).
  * feature_031: independent OLS slope −0.0005 mo/unit (p~3e-16; very
      small but present after adjustment).
  * The remaining 13 continuous features (feature_036, feature_021,
      feature_003, feature_025, feature_027, feature_034, feature_014,
      feature_037, feature_017, feature_035, feature_002, feature_007,
      feature_010) carried no independent main effect.

2. Multivariable OLS (Iteration 3)
----------------------------------
Fitting pfs_months on all 37 features simultaneously yielded
R² = 0.79 with the same independent predictors as above. Feature_011
and feature_023 lost most of their signal after adjustment; the other
"top binaries" remained large and significant.

3. Treatment-effect heterogeneity for feature_013 (Iterations 4–8)
------------------------------------------------------------------
A systematic interaction screen of feature_013 with every other
feature produced very strong signals. The four largest effect modifiers
were feature_018, feature_028, feature_029, and feature_033.

Stratified analyses on each modifier in turn confirmed:

  * feature_018=1 abolishes the feature_013 benefit
      (effect drops from +1.35 to −0.01 mo).
  * feature_028=1 abolishes the feature_013 benefit
      (+1.66 → +0.04 mo).
  * feature_029=0 abolishes the feature_013 benefit
      (-0.02 vs +1.58 mo when feature_029=1).
  * feature_033 high abolishes the feature_013 benefit
      (within the responsive subgroup, the effect is +4.93 in the
       lowest f033 quartile and −0.05 in the highest).

A joint stratification over feature_018 × feature_028 × feature_029
produced exactly one cell with a non-trivial feature_013 effect:
feature_018=0 AND feature_028=0 AND feature_029=1, where the effect was
+2.91 months (p<1e-300). All seven other cells were null.

Adding feature_033 ≤ 12 to the predicate sharpens the effect further:
within the four-predicate subgroup the gain is +4.96 months
(mean pfs_months 9.23 treated vs 4.28 untreated, n_treated=3243,
n_untreated=5963). Outside the subgroup the gain is +0.22 months —
statistically detectable owing to the 41,000-patient sample size but
clinically negligible and partly explained by the threshold being
applied to a continuous slope.

Within the responsive subgroup, the +5-month effect is uniform across
feature_005 (stage), feature_012 (age) tertiles, feature_006,
feature_001, and feature_004 quartiles, indicating the four predicates
above fully define the responsive cohort.

4. Heterogeneity for feature_029 and feature_023 (Iterations 9–10)
------------------------------------------------------------------
The same resistance pattern recurs for two other binary features:

  * feature_029 effect on pfs_months by joint (feature_018, feature_028)
    cells: (0,0) +1.03 mo (p~2e-226); (0,1) +0.05 (p=0.24);
    (1,0) +0.02 (p=0.78); (1,1) +0.04 (p=0.59). Within (0,0,
    feature_033 ≤ 12) the effect rises to +1.72 mo (p~6e-271).
    The same three resistance markers (feature_018, feature_028, high
    feature_033) abolish the feature_029 benefit, with no requirement
    for feature_029 to "switch on" any other variable.
  * feature_023 effect by (feature_018, feature_028): (0,0) +0.67
    (p~1e-92); all other cells essentially zero. Within
    (feature_018=0, feature_028=0, feature_033 ≤ 12) the gain rises to
    +1.13 mo (p~5e-106).

5. feature_016 and minor treatment-like signal (Iteration 11)
-------------------------------------------------------------
feature_016 has a small marginal effect (+0.11 mo) but interacts
positively with feature_013 (+0.38, p~5e-67), feature_029 (+0.17,
p~5e-14), feature_023 (+0.12, p~1e-7), and negatively with feature_028
and feature_033 (same resistance pattern). Its main coefficient in the
full multivariable OLS shrinks to zero (+0.002, p=0.86), so feature_016
contributes mostly through interaction with the larger treatment-like
features rather than a stand-alone effect.

6. Stage × age and prognostic-vs-predictive markers (Iteration 13)
------------------------------------------------------------------
feature_005 × feature_012 has a small but significant negative
interaction (−0.0063 per stage·year, p~8e-17): the stage decrement on
PFS is slightly larger at older feature_012. This is small in absolute
magnitude (≈0.4 mo extra decrement at age 90 vs age 30 per stage step).

feature_006 (−1.54 mo) and feature_001 (−1.05 mo) act as constant
prognostic shifts independent of treatment status. Within the
feature_013-responsive subgroup, the +5-month treatment effect is
preserved at both feature_006=0/1 and feature_001=0/1. They are
prognostic but not predictive markers.

7. Non-informative features (Iterations 14–15)
----------------------------------------------
The ten low-effect binaries listed in §1 and the thirteen "lab-like"
continuous features (feature_036, feature_021, feature_003, feature_025,
feature_027, feature_034, feature_014, feature_037, feature_017,
feature_035, feature_002, feature_007, feature_010) showed no
independent main effects in the multivariable model and no notable
interactions with feature_013, feature_029, feature_023, or feature_016.
They are non-informative for both prognosis and treatment-effect
prediction in this cohort.

8. Summary best-supported subgroup hypotheses (Iteration 16)
------------------------------------------------------------
Final treatment-effect statements supported by the data:

  (A) feature_013 increases pfs_months by ~+5 months only when
      feature_018=0 AND feature_028=0 AND feature_029=1 AND
      feature_033 ≤ 12. Outside this subgroup the effect is
      clinically null (+0.2 months).

  (B) feature_029 increases pfs_months by ~+1.7 months only when
      feature_018=0 AND feature_028=0 AND feature_033 ≤ 12.
      Outside this subgroup the effect is null.

  (C) feature_023 increases pfs_months by ~+1.1 months only when
      feature_018=0 AND feature_028=0 AND feature_033 ≤ 12.
      Outside this subgroup the effect is null.

In every case, the resistance markers feature_018=1, feature_028=1, and
high feature_033 each independently eliminate the treatment effect; in
the case of feature_013, an additional positive predictor (feature_029
=1) is also required for the benefit to manifest.

Hypotheses supported and refuted
================================
Supported:

  * feature_013, feature_029, feature_023, feature_016 act as
    treatment-like predictors of higher pfs_months (Iter 1, 4, 9, 10,
    11).
  * feature_006, feature_001 are constant prognostic decrements that
    are independent of treatment effect (Iter 1, 13).
  * feature_005 (stage) is a stage-like ordinal with monotone PFS
    decrement; ~−1.18 mo per step (Iter 2).
  * feature_004 and feature_033 are continuous adverse predictors of
    PFS, and feature_024 is a continuous favorable predictor (Iter 2,
    3).
  * feature_018, feature_028, and high feature_033 are resistance
    markers that abolish all four treatment-like effects (Iter 5–10).
  * The four-predicate subgroup
    (feature_018=0 AND feature_028=0 AND feature_029=1 AND
     feature_033 ≤ 12) fully defines the feature_013-responsive
    population, with a robust +5 month gain (Iter 6, 8, 12, 16).

Refuted (or unsupported):

  * No important association was detected between pfs_months and any
    of feature_011, feature_030, feature_019, feature_015, feature_022,
    feature_008, feature_032, feature_020, feature_009, feature_026,
    feature_036, feature_021, feature_003, feature_025, feature_027,
    feature_034, feature_014, feature_037, feature_017, feature_035,
    feature_002, feature_007, feature_010 (Iter 14, 15).
  * feature_006 and feature_001 do not interact with feature_013/029
    /023 — they shift PFS uniformly across treatment status (Iter 13).

Clinical interpretation
=======================
The feature_013-by-(feature_018, feature_028, feature_029, feature_033)
pattern is consistent with a treatment whose efficacy depends jointly
on the presence of a positive selection biomarker (feature_029=1, akin
to a target expression) and the absence of two resistance biomarkers
(feature_018=0, feature_028=0) plus a low value of a continuous
biomarker (feature_033). The same three resistance modifiers also
suppress the smaller benefits seen for feature_029, feature_023, and
feature_016 alone, suggesting that feature_018, feature_028, and
elevated feature_033 may share an underlying biology (e.g., tumor
microenvironment or alternate-pathway activation) that confers broad
resistance to multiple lines of therapy in this cohort.
