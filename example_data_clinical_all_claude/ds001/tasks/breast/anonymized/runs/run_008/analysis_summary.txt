ds001_breast — analysis summary
================================

Cohort
------
50,000 breast-cancer patients with 37 anonymized features and a single
continuous outcome, pfs_months (progression-free survival in months,
range 0–16.66, mean 4.69, SD 2.50). No missing values.

Approach
--------
We worked through 12 propose-test-refine iterations: univariate
screening of all 36 non-id features against pfs_months, a full joint
linear-regression for prognostic main effects, an exhaustive treatment-
by-feature interaction screen, and joint multi-feature subgroup
discovery to characterize treatment-effect heterogeneity. The final
iterations probed continuous and binary biomarker modifiers jointly,
verified balance of candidate-modifiers across treatment arms, and
formulated a complete responder-subgroup definition.

Key prognostic findings (joint OLS, R^2 = 0.792)
-------------------------------------------------
Variables that retained large independent associations with pfs_months
in the full multivariable model (all p < 1e-30):

    feature_012   +0.176 mo per unit  (continuous, range 30–90;
                                       cohort Pearson r = +0.702 — by
                                       far the strongest single
                                       predictor in the dataset)
    feature_005   −1.19 mo (level 1 vs 0), −2.33 (level 2 vs 0)
                  three-level prognostic, behaves like a stage- or
                  grade-like variable
    feature_006   −1.55 mo  (binary, n1 = 14,969)
    feature_001   −0.98 mo  (binary, n1 = 5,090, ~10% of cohort)
    feature_028   −0.58 mo  (later identified as a predictive biomarker
                             — this main-effect estimate reflects its
                             interaction with feature_013, not an
                             independent prognostic role)
    feature_029   +0.53 mo  (same caveat — predictive, not prognostic)
    feature_018   −0.47 mo  (same caveat — predictive, not prognostic)
    feature_024   +0.48 mo per unit (continuous; small range 1.5–5.5)
    feature_004   −0.077 mo per unit (continuous, range 0–24.6)
    feature_033   −0.019 mo per unit (continuous; later identified as
                             a continuous predictive biomarker for
                             feature_013, see below)
    feature_013   +1.10 mo  (binary, n1 = 17,428 — the strongest
                             positive binary main effect; this
                             behaved as the candidate treatment)

Many other binary features (feature_011, 030, 019, 016, 015, 022, 008,
032, 020, 009, 026) had |coef| < 0.05 in the joint model and p > 0.1,
indicating no measurable independent prognostic information. Eleven of
the eighteen continuous features (e.g., feature_036, 003, 021, 025,
017, 014, 034, 037, 035, 002, 007, 010, 027, 031) had |r| < 0.02 with
pfs_months.

Identification of feature_013 as the candidate treatment
--------------------------------------------------------
feature_013 stood out from every other binary feature for two reasons:
(1) it had the largest positive main effect (+1.10 mo), and (2) a
treatment-by-feature interaction screen against all 36 other variables
revealed multiple very large, highly significant interactions:

    feature_013 × feature_028   −1.62 mo,   p = 4.0e-255
    feature_013 × feature_029   +1.59 mo,   p = 9.3e-229
    feature_013 × feature_033   −0.052/u,   p = 1.5e-174
    feature_013 × feature_018   −1.36 mo,   p = 2.2e-118
    feature_013 × feature_023   +1.09 mo,   p = 2.6e-117

By contrast, the other strong-main-effect binaries (feature_006,
feature_001) did NOT show such heterogeneity. Their effects were
roughly constant across strata of every other feature (e.g.,
feature_006 effect was −1.44 / −1.60 / −1.63 across feature_012
tertiles; feature_001 effect was −0.94 / −1.05 / −1.06 across the same
tertiles, and was virtually identical inside vs outside feature_013).
This pattern is consistent with feature_006 and feature_001 being
constant prognostic risk markers while feature_013 is a treatment
whose benefit depends strongly on patient features.

Stratified treatment-effect estimates for feature_013
-----------------------------------------------------
Mean pfs_months for feature_013 = 0 vs 1, by single binary modifier:

    feature_029 = 0   (n=15,114): 4.303 → 4.285,  diff = −0.02   (p=0.63)
    feature_029 = 1   (n=34,886): 4.313 → 5.890,  diff = +1.58   (p<1e-300)

    feature_028 = 0   (n=32,635): 4.307 → 5.966,  diff = +1.66   (p<1e-300)
    feature_028 = 1   (n=17,365): 4.316 → 4.352,  diff = +0.04   (p=0.29)

    feature_018 = 0   (n=40,940): 4.307 → 5.657,  diff = +1.35   (p<1e-300)
    feature_018 = 1   (n=9,060) : 4.322 → 4.308,  diff = −0.01   (p=0.77)

    feature_023 = 0   (n=18,046): 4.323 → 4.722,  diff = +0.40
    feature_023 = 1   (n=31,954): 4.303 → 5.794,  diff = +1.49

By feature_033 quartile (cohort-wide):

    Q1 (1.0–7.5)   : diff = +1.84
    Q2 (7.6–12.2)  : diff = +1.92
    Q3 (12.3–19.6) : diff = +0.65
    Q4 (19.7–100)  : diff = −0.02

A monotonic dose-response pattern: the treatment benefit fades to zero
as feature_033 rises into the upper quartile.

Pure-predictive vs prognostic separation
----------------------------------------
For each of the four binary modifiers we examined the marker’s effect
within the feature_013 = 0 stratum (i.e., absent the candidate
treatment):

    feature_029 = 1 vs 0 within trt=0:  4.313 vs 4.303  (diff +0.010)
    feature_028 = 1 vs 0 within trt=0:  4.316 vs 4.307  (diff +0.009)
    feature_018 = 1 vs 0 within trt=0:  4.322 vs 4.307  (diff +0.015)
    feature_023 = 1 vs 0 within trt=0:  4.303 vs 4.323  (diff −0.020)

All four are essentially zero. Their entire association with PFS in the
multivariable model is mediated through interaction with feature_013.
This is a textbook predictive-biomarker pattern: feature_029 and
feature_023 are positive predictive markers; feature_028 and
feature_018 are negative predictive (resistance) markers.

Confounding check
-----------------
feature_013 was assigned approximately independently of the four
predictive modifiers. The proportion of feature_029=1, feature_028=1,
feature_018=1, and feature_023=1 differs by ≤ 0.005 between trt and
control arms — strong evidence that the heterogeneity reflects
genuine effect modification rather than confounding by indication.

Joint responder-subgroup discovery
----------------------------------
Combining the three binary modifiers in the natural direction yields
the responder subgroup

        feature_029 = 1  AND  feature_028 = 0  AND  feature_018 = 0     (*)

n = 18,681 (37.4 % of cohort). Within (*): feature_013 = 0 mean PFS =
4.300, feature_013 = 1 mean PFS = 7.209, difference = +2.91 months
(p < 1e-300). Within the complement of (*) (n = 31,319, 62.6 % of
cohort): difference = +0.003 months (p = 0.91). The treatment effect is
fully concentrated in (*) — the complement is a true non-responder
group.

Refining (*) with the secondary modifiers, the maximum-benefit
subgroup is

        feature_029 = 1  AND  feature_028 = 0  AND  feature_018 = 0
        AND  feature_023 = 1  AND  feature_033 < median (≈ 12.2)

n = 7,917 (15.8 % of cohort). Mean PFS goes from 4.260 (untreated) to
9.270 (treated) — a +5.0-month gain. Even within (*), stratifying by
feature_033 quartile shows an essentially binary cutoff at the upper
quartile: differences are +4.93, +4.97, +1.72, −0.05 across Q1–Q4,
suggesting feature_033 may carry a threshold rather than a smooth
linear modifying effect.

Joint multivariable confirmation
--------------------------------
A regression of pfs_months on feature_013, the four binary modifiers,
feature_033, and all five feature_013-by-modifier interaction terms
yields:

    feature_013                +1.601   p < 1e-200
    feature_013 × feature_029  +1.465   p < 1e-100
    feature_013 × feature_028  −1.588   p < 1e-200
    feature_013 × feature_018  −1.337   p < 1e-100
    feature_013 × feature_023  +0.121   p = 0.046
    feature_013 × feature_033  −0.052   p < 1e-100
    main effects of the four binary modifiers (with feature_013=0):
      all |coef| < 0.05, all p > 0.17

After mutual adjustment, feature_023 contributes only a small
incremental boost above what feature_029 alone captures (consistent
with the strong cross-tab association between them — among feature_029
= 1 patients, 85 % are also feature_023 = 1). The dominant modifiers
are feature_029, feature_028, feature_018, and feature_033.

Conclusions
-----------
1.  feature_013 is the only feature in this dataset whose pfs_months
    effect is strongly modified by other features. It behaves as a
    treatment with a large benefit (~+1.10 mo cohort average; up to
    +5.0 mo in the most-favorable subgroup) confined to a clearly
    defined responder population.

2.  The responder population is

        feature_029 = 1  AND  feature_028 = 0  AND  feature_018 = 0,

    optionally further restricted by feature_023 = 1 and a low value of
    feature_033 (< ~12.2). Outside this set there is no detectable
    treatment effect.

3.  feature_006, feature_001, feature_005, feature_012, feature_024,
    feature_004 and feature_033 act as prognostic factors with
    essentially constant effects across treatment status and across
    one another’s strata.

4.  The four binary biomarker modifiers (feature_029, feature_028,
    feature_018, feature_023) carry no independent prognostic
    information — their entire association with pfs_months is
    predictive (treatment-effect-modifying), not prognostic.

5.  Eleven binary and most of the continuous laboratory-like features
    have no measurable association with pfs_months in this cohort,
    suggesting they are clinical-context labels rather than direct
    determinants of progression-free survival.
