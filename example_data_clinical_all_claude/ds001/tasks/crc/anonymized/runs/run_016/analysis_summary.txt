ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_crc (50,000 patients, single outcome pfs_months)
========================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The dataset contains 33 anonymized features and a single outcome,
pfs_months (progression-free survival, range 0–16.25 months, mean 4.31).
The cohort has no missing values. Across 25 iterations I screened each
feature for a main effect on pfs_months, fit a multivariable model to
identify independent effects, and then performed a systematic
treatment-effect heterogeneity search using feature_027 — the binary
variable with the strongest beneficial univariate association — as the
candidate "treatment" indicator.


MAIN EFFECTS
------------
Strong prognostic features (consistent direction in unadjusted and
multivariable models, p < 1e-50 for all):

    feature_015  +0.176 months / unit (continuous, range 30–90, r=0.766)
                  — the dominant continuous predictor; quintile means
                  rise from 1.86 (lowest) to 6.83 (highest).
    feature_001  −1.17 months per level (3-level: 5.27/4.05/2.91 for
                  levels 0/1/2) — strong dose-response.
    feature_014  −1.36 months when feature_014=1 (binary, 55% prevalence).
    feature_019  +0.47 months / unit (continuous).
    feature_025  −0.073 months / unit (continuous).
    feature_023  −0.33 months when feature_023=1.
    feature_006  −0.31 months when feature_006=1.
    feature_005  small per-unit slope (−0.005) but a clean dichotomy at
                  feature_005<5 vs ≥5 (4.47 vs 4.13 months, p≈2e-64).
    feature_022  −0.23 months when feature_022=1 (rare, 4.5%).
    feature_011  +0.22 months when feature_011=1 (rare, 3%).
    feature_021  −0.0003 months / unit (very small but significant).

Features without a detectable main effect on pfs_months (p>0.10 in the
multivariable model): feature_031, feature_016, feature_028, feature_002,
feature_024, feature_010, feature_012, feature_018, feature_020,
feature_003, feature_030, feature_008, feature_004, feature_013,
feature_026, feature_029, feature_007, feature_033, feature_009,
feature_017, feature_032 (the last two are marginal, p≈0.05–0.08).

Hypotheses h1–h13, h15, h19, h20 were supported. h16–h18 (no effect of
the rarest binaries) and parts of h7/h12/h13 were not.


CANDIDATE TREATMENT (feature_027)
---------------------------------
feature_027 is binary with 20% prevalence. Univariate effect on PFS is
+0.972 months (5.09 vs 4.12, p≈10^-216) — far larger than any other
binary variable in absolute magnitude — and the adjusted multivariable
coefficient (+0.943, p<1e-200) is virtually identical, indicating no
appreciable confounding by the measured prognostic factors. This
combination of large signed effect, prevalence near a typical
treatment-utilization rate, and indifference to adjustment is the
signature of a treatment indicator. h19 and h21 are strongly supported.


TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY (the central finding)
----------------------------------------------------
A systematic treatment×feature interaction screen (one OLS model per
modifier) flagged five highly significant effect modifiers of
feature_027:

    feature_023 × feature_027   coef −1.629  p ≈ 8e-226
    feature_006 × feature_027   coef −1.454  p ≈ 4e-168
    feature_005 × feature_027   coef −0.025/unit  p ≈ 9e-58
    feature_022 × feature_027   coef −1.183  p ≈ 6e-23
    feature_011 × feature_027   coef +0.988  p ≈ 1e-11

Negative interaction coefficients indicate that the feature_027 benefit
is concentrated in patients whose modifier value is "favorable"
(0 for the binaries; low for the continuous feature_005). Strongly
prognostic features such as feature_015, feature_001, and feature_014
were NOT effect modifiers (interaction p > 0.15) — the heterogeneity
in treatment response is not the same as heterogeneity in overall risk.

Layered subgroup analysis showed that the modifiers act in an "AND"
fashion: each of feature_023=1, feature_006=1, feature_022=1, and
feature_005≥5 by itself essentially eliminates the benefit, regardless
of the other predicates.

A cell-by-cell decomposition (treatment effect = mean PFS at f027=1
minus mean PFS at f027=0) gave:

    f023=0, f006=0, f022=0, f005<5:   +5.013 months  (n=9,326)  p ≈ 0
    f023=0, f006=0, f022=0, f005≥5:   +0.005 months  (n=8,046)  ns
    f023=0, f006=0, f022=1 (any f005):  ≈ −0.20      (n≈1,481)  ns
    f023=0, f006=1, f022=0 (any f005):  ≈ +0.08      (n≈9,458)  ns
    f023=1, f006=0, f022=0 (any f005):  ≈ +0.06      (n≈13,490) ns
    f023=1, f006=1, f022=0 (any f005):  ≈ −0.04      (n≈7,408)  ns

A sweep of cutoffs on feature_005 within the (f023=0, f006=0, f022=0)
subgroup demonstrates that the cleanest split is at feature_005=5,
where the below-cut effect is +5.0 months and the above-cut effect is
≈0 (a discontinuity rather than a smooth gradient). This is the
behavior of a clinically interpretable threshold — for example, a
CEA-like biomarker that distinguishes biologically responsive vs
nonresponsive disease, although the original feature labels are
opaque.

A formal three-way interaction model
    pfs_months ~ feature_027 * feature_023 * feature_006
returned feature_027 +2.520, feature_027:feature_023 −2.459,
feature_027:feature_006 −2.449, three-way +2.348 (all p<1e-100). The
near-cancellation pattern confirms the 2×2 "AND" structure: the
benefit is essentially confined to one cell.

A within-subgroup interaction screen (n=9,326) tested every other
feature for residual modification of the feature_027 effect inside
(f023=0, f006=0, f022=0, f005<5). The smallest p-values (feature_029
p=0.07; feature_015 p=0.11; feature_032 p=0.12) do not survive
multiple-comparison adjustment. The favorable subgroup looks
homogeneous with respect to the treatment effect; no further
restriction is supported.

Outside the proposed subgroup (n=40,674, 81% of the cohort) the
feature_027 coefficient is +0.025 months (p=0.33) — i.e., zero.


FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS
------------------------------------------
The treatment-like indicator feature_027 prolongs pfs_months by
approximately +5.0 months (mean 9.10 vs 4.09, p<1e-300) only in
patients who simultaneously satisfy

      feature_023 = 0
   AND feature_006 = 0
   AND feature_022 = 0
   AND feature_005 < 5

(~19% of the cohort, n=9,326). For patients failing any one of these
four predicates (n=40,674) the feature_027 effect on pfs_months is
indistinguishable from zero (+0.025 months, p=0.33). Each unfavorable
predicate — feature_023=1, feature_006=1, feature_022=1, or
feature_005≥5 — independently abolishes the benefit, which is why all
four conditions must be specified to define the responsive subgroup.


HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED vs REFUTED
-------------------------------
Supported (effect direction and significance as proposed):
    h1, h2, h3, h5, h6, h7, h8, h9, h10, h11, h14, h15, h19, h20, h21,
    h22, h23, h24, h25, h26, h30, h31, h32, h33, h35, h37.

Refuted or not supported:
    h4 (feature_031 has no effect),
    h12 (feature_032 only marginal),
    h13 (feature_017 has no effect),
    h16, h17, h18 (rare binaries feature_016, feature_028, feature_002
        have no effect),
    h27, h28, h29 (feature_001, feature_014, feature_015 are NOT
        effect modifiers, despite being strong prognostic factors),
    h34 (no treatment effect outside subgroup — the "null" hypothesis
        that treatment helps elsewhere is refuted),
    h36 (no further effect modifiers within the subgroup).


CONCLUSIONS
-----------
1. pfs_months is well predicted by a small set of features
   (feature_015, feature_001, feature_014, feature_019, feature_025,
   plus several binaries) under simple linear modeling. After
   adjustment, ~12 features remain independently associated and the
   rest are noise with respect to PFS.
2. feature_027 behaves like a beneficial treatment indicator: ~20%
   prevalence, +1 month average PFS gain, and an effect that is
   robust to confounding adjustment.
3. The aggregate +1-month effect of feature_027 is misleading. The
   benefit is concentrated in a single ~19% subgroup defined by the
   joint presence of four predicates (feature_023=0, feature_006=0,
   feature_022=0, feature_005<5), where the gain is ~+5 months. In the
   remaining 81% of the cohort the treatment indicator is unrelated to
   PFS. Each of the four predicates independently nullifies the
   benefit, so all four must be reported as part of the subgroup
   definition.
4. Importantly, the most powerful prognostic factors (feature_015,
   feature_001, feature_014) are not effect modifiers; conversely,
   features whose main prognostic effects are modest (feature_023,
   feature_006, feature_022, feature_005) carry the entire treatment-
   effect heterogeneity signal. This dissociation between prognostic
   and predictive features is consistent with the way biomarker-driven
   therapy selection operates in oncology.
