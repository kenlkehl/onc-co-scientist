ds001_crc — Analysis summary
=============================

Cohort: 50,000 patients, single outcome pfs_months (mean 4.31, median 4.21,
sd 2.29, max 16.25; right-skewed). 33 anonymous features comprising binary
indicators, one 3-level ordinal (feature_001), and continuous lab-like
values. No missing data.

ITERATIONS 1-3 — DESCRIPTIVES, UNIVARIATE, AND MULTIVARIABLE MAIN EFFECTS

A univariate screen identified feature_015 as the dominant single-feature
predictor (Pearson r=+0.766, p<1e-300; ~58% of pfs_months variance on its
own). Other strong univariate associations: feature_001 (PFS 5.27 -> 4.05
-> 2.91 across levels 0/1/2; behaves like a 3-level prognostic ordinal);
feature_014 (5.06 vs 3.70, large negative); feature_027 (5.09 vs 4.12, the
largest positive binary effect); feature_023, feature_006 (smaller
negative); feature_005, feature_025, feature_021 (negative continuous);
feature_019 (positive continuous); feature_022, feature_011 (rare).
Univariate-null features included feature_031, feature_010, feature_018,
feature_012, feature_002, feature_007, feature_009, feature_017,
feature_028, feature_029, feature_033.

A fully-adjusted OLS of pfs_months on all 33 features (R^2 = 0.859)
confirmed independent significance for feature_015 (+0.176 / unit, t=454),
feature_001 (-1.171 / level, t=-206), feature_014 (-1.362, t=-176),
feature_027 (+0.943, t=98), feature_019 (+0.469, t=61), feature_023
(-0.354, t=-44), feature_006 (-0.287, t=-36), feature_022 (-0.357, t=-19),
feature_025 (-0.076, t=-76), feature_005 (-0.005, t=-21), feature_021
(-0.00043, t=-9). All other features failed to reach |t|>2 in the adjusted
model. Direction and rough magnitude of effects matched the univariate
screen with no surprises (no Simpson reversals).

ITERATION 4 — CANDIDATE TREATMENT IDENTIFICATION

The protocol asked for systematic treatment-effect heterogeneity for each
outcome with usable treatment variation. I considered three classes of
candidate treatments among binary features: (a) features with a clear
positive marginal effect on pfs_months — feature_027 (the strongest), and
to a lesser extent the rare feature_011; (b) balanced (~50%) binary
features with an essentially null marginal effect, which can hide
heterogeneity that averages out — feature_031 (45%, marginal -0.006) and
feature_010 (45%, marginal -0.019); (c) other binary covariates with no
clear marginal effect — feature_018, feature_020, feature_012, feature_024,
feature_028, feature_016, feature_002.

ITERATIONS 5-8 — TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY FOR feature_027

For each candidate, I screened the treatment-by-feature interaction
against every other feature (continuous features dichotomized at the
median) using OLS on pfs_months. For feature_027 the screen was decisive
and lit up four large interactions:

  feature_027 x feature_005>median: interaction -1.67  (p=4e-244)
  feature_027 x feature_023:        interaction -1.63  (p=8e-226)
  feature_027 x feature_006:        interaction -1.45  (p=4e-168)
  feature_027 x feature_022:        interaction -1.18  (p=6e-23)

In each case the interaction roughly cancels the +1-2 month main effect of
feature_027, so each modifier alone is sufficient to extinguish the
treatment benefit. The 2x2 cross-table on feature_023 and feature_006
confirmed the pattern: among patients with feature_023=0 AND feature_006=0
the feature_027 PFS gain was +2.52 months, while it was +0.07, +0.06, and
-0.04 months in the other three cells.

I then drilled down inside the feature_023=0 & feature_006=0 cell
(n=18,853). Within that cell, the feature_027 benefit was +4.64 months
when feature_005 was below its median and only +0.27 when above. It was
+2.76 when feature_022=0 and -0.20 when feature_022=1. Joint OLS in this
subgroup with feature_027 interactions on feature_022, feature_011, and
(feature_005>median) showed the feature_022 interaction at -2.76 (p=3e-81)
and the feature_005-high interaction at -4.34 (p<1e-300); feature_011 was
not a modifier (p=0.52).

A fine cutoff search on feature_005 within feature_023=0 & feature_006=0 &
feature_022=0 (n=17,372) showed that the treatment difference is preserved
at full magnitude up to feature_005=5.0 (+5.04 to +5.01 months across cuts
4.0-5.0), then drops abruptly: cut <=5.1 gives +4.97, cut <=5.5 gives
+4.79, cut <=6.0 gives +4.55. A narrow window scan put the inflection
exactly at 5.0: feature_005 in (4.5,5.0] -> +4.75; (5.0,5.5] -> +0.22;
(5.5,6.0] -> -0.03. The cutoff is sharp and consistent with a hard
threshold on this lab.

ITERATION 8 — COMPLETE SUBGROUP DEFINITION FOR feature_027

The four resistance flags combine as a logical OR: feature_027 is
ineffective whenever ANY of the following holds: feature_023=1,
feature_006=1, feature_022=1, feature_005>5.0. I verified this by isolating
each "pure" resistance subgroup (only that flag set, the other three off)
and confirming a null treatment difference (range -0.17 to +0.11, all
p>0.4).

The complete sensitive subgroup is therefore:
  feature_023 = 0  AND  feature_006 = 0  AND  feature_022 = 0  AND
  feature_005 <= 5.

In this subgroup (n=9338, 18.7% of the cohort):
  - feature_027 = 1: n=1905, mean PFS 9.103, median 9.093
  - feature_027 = 0: n=7433, mean PFS 4.089, median 4.087
  - Mean difference +5.014 months, t-test p effectively zero
  (also confirmed in the formal interaction model: trt x sensitive
   coefficient +4.99 with t=84 unadjusted, +4.98 with t=926 after
   adjustment for feature_015, feature_001, feature_014, feature_019,
   feature_025, feature_021).

In the complement (n=40,662):
  - feature_027 = 1: n=8117, mean PFS 4.147
  - feature_027 = 0: n=32,545, mean PFS 4.123
  - Mean difference +0.024 months, p=0.357 (no benefit).

Treatment allocation is balanced across subgroups (feature_027 prevalence
0.204 in sensitive vs 0.200 in resistant), which rules out allocation
imbalance as a confounder of the heterogeneity finding.

ITERATION 9 — NEGATIVE CONTROLS (other candidate treatments)

I ran the same exhaustive treatment-by-feature interaction screen for
feature_031, feature_010, and feature_018. None produced a credible
heterogeneity signal:

  feature_031: best interaction p=0.078 (feature_033>median, +0.073),
               next p=0.091, p=0.176; nothing close to Bonferroni
               significance and no |effect|>0.2 anywhere.
  feature_010: best interaction p=0.022 (feature_022, -0.228) — the
               magnitude is small relative to other modifiers, in a rare
               (4.5%) subgroup, and is not corroborated by additional
               structure; consistent with chance among ~32 tests.
  feature_018: best interaction p=0.036 (feature_015>median, -0.111),
               again of marginal magnitude.

Tree-style leaf checks (treatment-effect-by-binary-feature with
z-statistic > 3 and |effect|>0.1) returned nothing for any of these
candidates. I also verified that the other binary features (feature_012,
feature_020, feature_024, feature_002, feature_011, feature_016,
feature_028) have no meaningful subgroup-specific PFS effects.

The only non-feature_027 candidate with a small significant marginal effect
was feature_011 (overall +0.22 months, p=2e-4), but this signal disappears
inside the feature_027-sensitive subgroup (+0.09, p=0.49) and shows no
treatment-effect modification of any other variable; it is best interpreted
as a weak prognostic marker rather than a treatment.

ITERATION 10 — ROBUSTNESS WITHIN THE SENSITIVE SUBGROUP

The +5-month feature_027 benefit is essentially constant across
demographic / functional strata within the sensitive subgroup:

  feature_001 (PS): PS0 +4.87, PS1 +5.09, PS2 +4.81
  feature_015 quartile: Q1 +4.96, Q2 +4.94, Q3 +5.01, Q4 +5.09
  feature_014: 0 +2.41 / 1 +2.60 (within feature_023=0 & feature_006=0
               cell — the 5-month effect collapses to ~+2.5 there because
               this cell still includes the feature_022 / feature_005>5
               patients).

So the treatment effect is additive on the pfs_months scale and not driven
by any single demographic stratum.

ITERATION 11 — PROGNOSTIC SUMMARY (for context)

Independent of feature_027, the dominant prognostic determinants of
pfs_months in the cohort, ranked by t-statistic in an adjusted model
including the trt-by-sensitive interaction, are:

  feature_015 +0.176 / unit  (t=2076)  — single largest effect, plausibly
                                          an age- or score-like variable
  feature_001 -1.166 / level (t=-936)  — 3-level ordinal, prognosis-like
  feature_014 -1.368          (t=-807) — binary with large negative effect
  feature_019 +0.485 / unit   (t=288)  — positive lab-like
  feature_025 -0.078 / unit   (t=-355) — negative continuous
  feature_021 -0.00050 / unit (t=-48)  — negative continuous (large scale)
  feature_023, feature_006, feature_022, feature_005 — all negative
   prognostic AND treatment-effect modifiers (see above).

ITERATION 12 — FINAL CONCLUSIONS

(1) Best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis (the "headline"
required by the protocol):

      Treatment:  feature_027
      Outcome:    pfs_months
      Direction:  feature_027 = 1 INCREASES pfs_months
      Subgroup:   feature_023 = 0  AND  feature_006 = 0  AND
                  feature_022 = 0  AND  feature_005 <= 5.0

      Effect in this subgroup:    mean PFS 9.10 vs 4.09 months,
                                  difference +5.01 months  (p<<1e-300).
      Effect in the complement:   +0.02 months  (p=0.36, null).

    Each of feature_023=1, feature_006=1, feature_022=1, or
    feature_005>5 is independently sufficient to abolish the feature_027
    benefit; the resistance criteria combine as a logical OR.

(2) Hypotheses supported:
    - feature_027 has a large positive main effect on pfs_months and a
      striking treatment-effect heterogeneity defined by the four
      resistance flags above.
    - feature_015, feature_001, feature_014, feature_019, feature_025,
      feature_021, feature_022, feature_023, feature_006, feature_005 are
      independent prognostic markers of pfs_months.
    - The four feature_027-resistance flags act independently and
      additively (any one is sufficient).

(3) Hypotheses refuted:
    - feature_031, feature_010, feature_018 are NOT treatments with
      heterogeneous effects (no surviving interaction).
    - feature_011 is not a feature_027 effect modifier (interaction n.s.
      after the f023/f006/f022/f005 stratification).
    - The cutoff for feature_005 is not at the median or some other
      arbitrary point: it is at exactly feature_005 = 5.0.

(4) No second-treatment signal is supported by the data: the only
    treatment-effect heterogeneity worth a clinical interpretation is the
    feature_027 hit described above.
