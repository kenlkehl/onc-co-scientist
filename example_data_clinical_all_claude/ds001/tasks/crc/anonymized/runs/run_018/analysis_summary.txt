Analysis summary for ds001_crc (50,000 colorectal cancer patients; outcome: pfs_months)
================================================================================

Approach
--------
Across 25 iterations I (a) characterized the dataset's main prognostic structure,
(b) identified the unique treatment indicator, and (c) systematically searched
for treatment-effect heterogeneity until the smallest stable subgroup
definition that captured all the benefit was located. All effect signs reflect
the natural pfs_months scale (months gained or lost).

Phase 1 -- Prognostic main effects (iterations 1-6)
---------------------------------------------------
Univariate Pearson screening flagged a small handful of features with
substantial pfs_months associations and a long tail of essentially null
features. A single multivariable OLS fit with all 33 features explained
R^2 = 0.86 of pfs_months variance, isolating the independent prognostic core:

  feature_015  +0.176/unit (continuous, range 30-90, mean 65; the dominant
               positive predictor; deciles span PFS 1.3 -> 7.5 months)
  feature_001  -1.17/category (3-level ordinal 0/1/2; PFS 5.27 -> 4.05 -> 2.91)
  feature_014  -1.36 (binary, prevalence 55%)
  feature_019  +0.47/unit (continuous, range 1.5-5.5, mean 3.8)
  feature_025  -0.078/unit (continuous, range 0-25)
  feature_006  -0.29; feature_023 -0.35; feature_022 -0.36 (binary)
  feature_005  -0.005/unit (continuous, right-skewed, range 0-777)
  feature_021  -0.0004/unit

The remaining ~20 features (feature_002, _003, _004, _007, _008, _009, _010,
_012, _013, _016, _017, _018, _020, _024, _026, _028, _029, _030, _031, _032,
_033) carried no independent prognostic signal in the multivariable fit --
they are noise / null variables in this cohort.

Phase 2 -- Identifying the treatment indicator (iteration 7)
------------------------------------------------------------
Among the binary features, feature_027 (prevalence 20%) stood out as the only
mid-prevalence binary with a large positive PFS effect: +0.97 unadjusted /
+0.94 adjusted PFS-months (n_trt=10,022 vs n_ctrl=39,978; p ~ 0). All other
binary features either had small effects or negative effects in the same
prognostic direction as the resistance markers above. feature_027 is thus the
natural treatment-arm candidate, and the heterogeneity search for the
remaining iterations focused on this variable.

Phase 3 -- Treatment-effect heterogeneity search (iterations 8-12)
------------------------------------------------------------------
A treatment-by-binary-feature interaction sweep was conducted for feature_027
against every binary in the dataset. Three negative interactions and one
positive interaction reached strong significance:

  feature_023 x feature_027 :  interaction coef = -1.63 (p = 7.7e-226)
  feature_006 x feature_027 :  interaction coef = -1.45 (p = 4.1e-168)
  feature_022 x feature_027 :  interaction coef = -1.18 (p = 6.1e-23)
  feature_011 x feature_027 :  interaction coef = +0.99 (p = 1.2e-11)

All other binary features had non-significant interactions with feature_027.

Stratified means showed each of feature_023, feature_006, feature_022 acts as
a complete-resistance marker -- when =1, the feature_027 PFS effect collapses
to ~ 0:

  feature_023=0: feature_027 effect = +1.66 months (p ~ 0)
  feature_023=1: feature_027 effect = +0.027 months (p = 0.45)
  feature_006=0: feature_027 effect = +1.48 months (p ~ 0)
  feature_006=1: feature_027 effect = +0.026 months (p = 0.52)
  feature_022=0: feature_027 effect = +1.03 months (p ~ 0)
  feature_022=1: feature_027 effect = -0.16 months (p = 0.15)

feature_011=1 looked like an enhancer (effect +1.93 vs +0.94 in =0), but later
analysis showed this was driven by enrichment of triple-WT/low-feature_005
patients within the feature_011=1 stratum rather than independent biomarker
activity (see iteration 24).

Phase 4 -- Joint subgroup definition (iterations 13-14)
-------------------------------------------------------
The triple-wild-type subgroup (feature_023=0 AND feature_006=0 AND
feature_022=0; n = 17,372, ~35% of cohort) showed feature_027 effect = +2.76
months -- nearly triple the overall +0.97. Within this triple-WT subgroup, a
treatment-by-continuous-feature interaction sweep identified one dominant
continuous modifier:

  feature_005 x feature_027 (within triple-WT) : interaction coef = -0.065
                                                  per unit feature_005,
                                                  p = 7.4e-136

Higher feature_005 progressively erased the treatment benefit. No other
continuous feature reached p < 0.05 with meaningful magnitude in this stratum.

Phase 5 -- Optimal cutoff for feature_005 (iterations 15-16)
------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment effect by feature_005 quintile within triple-WT was sharply
bimodal: Q1-Q3 (feature_005 < ~6) all gave ~ +5 months; Q4-Q5 (feature_005 >=
~6) gave ~ 0 months. Cutpoint sweep:

  thr  LOW-side TE     HIGH-side TE
   3   +5.03           +1.33
   4   +5.04           +0.69
   5   +5.01           +0.005
   6   +4.55           -0.02
   7   +4.13           +0.02
   8   +3.90           -0.004

feature_005 < 6 was selected as the operational cutoff: clean zero benefit on
the high side, ~+4.5 months on the low side.

Phase 6 -- Final subgroup, robustness, and refutation of competitors (iterations 17-25)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Eligibility predicate: feature_023=0 AND feature_006=0 AND feature_022=0
AND feature_005<6.

  Eligible    : n = 10,398 (20.8% of cohort)
                feature_027 effect = +4.55 months unadjusted (mean PFS 8.63 vs
                4.09; p ~ 0)
                feature_027 effect = +4.50 months adjusted (95% CI 4.47-4.53)
  Ineligible  : n = 39,602
                feature_027 effect = +0.022 months unadjusted (p = 0.41)
                feature_027 effect = -0.007 months adjusted (p = 0.18)

A joint OLS with the four-way eligibility predicate, the prognostic main
effects, and the treatment-by-eligibility interaction reached R^2 = 0.976. In
that model the feature_027:eligible interaction was +4.51 (p << 1e-300), the
feature_027 main effect was -0.004 (p = 0.37), and the eligible main effect
was -0.006 (p = 0.20). The treatment effect is essentially entirely captured
by the four-way conjunction.

Iterations 18-20 confirmed that within the eligible subgroup no further
binary or continuous feature provides additional, independent treatment-effect
modification at meaningful magnitude. Iteration 23 confirmed via pairwise
binary x binary interaction sweeping that no other binary feature in the
dataset behaves like a treatment with subgroup-specific effects -- feature_027
is the unique treatment indicator. Iteration 24 confirmed that feature_011's
apparent positive interaction is non-additive once eligibility is satisfied
(within-eligible difference between feature_011=1 vs 0 = +0.32, ns), so
feature_011 is dropped from the final subgroup definition.

Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
--------------------------------
Supported:
  * h2.1  feature_015 -> longer PFS  (+0.176/unit)
  * h3.1  feature_001 (0->1->2) -> shorter PFS (-1.17/cat)
  * h4.1  feature_014=1 -> shorter PFS (-1.35)
  * h5.1  feature_019 -> longer; feature_025 -> shorter
  * h6.1  full multivariable model R^2 > 0.8 (achieved 0.86)
  * h7.1  feature_027=1 -> longer PFS (+0.97 unadjusted)
  * h8.1, h9.1, h10.1, h11.1  feature_023, _006, _022 each abolish feature_027 benefit
  * h13.1  triple-WT subgroup amplifies treatment effect to +2.76
  * h14.1, h15.1, h16.1  feature_005 modulates treatment within triple-WT, with
                         abrupt loss of benefit above ~6
  * h17.1, h21.1, h22.1  four-way eligibility predicate captures all benefit;
                         non-eligible patients gain zero PFS from feature_027
  * h23.1  feature_027 is the unique treatment indicator
  * h25.1  FINAL: feature_027 -> +4.5 PFS-months exclusively in patients with
                  feature_023=0 AND feature_006=0 AND feature_022=0 AND
                  feature_005<6

Refuted / nuanced:
  * h12.1  feature_011 looked like an effect-enhancer in unadjusted analysis,
           but the apparent enhancement was confounded by triple-WT/low-
           feature_005 enrichment (h24.1)
  * h18.1, h19.1, h20.1  no further treatment-effect modifier exists within the
                          eligible subgroup -- the heterogeneity search
                          terminates at the four-way predicate

Final treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis (preregistered for scoring)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment    : feature_027
Outcome      : pfs_months
Direction    : positive (treatment INCREASES pfs_months)
Effect size  : +4.50 PFS-months (95% CI ~4.47 to 4.53) within the eligible
               subgroup; ~ 0 (-0.007, 95% CI -0.018 to +0.003) outside it
Subgroup     : feature_023 = 0 AND feature_006 = 0 AND feature_022 = 0
               AND feature_005 < 6
Subgroup size: 10,398 of 50,000 patients (20.8% of cohort)
Interpretation: The pattern is consistent with a targeted oncology therapy
whose response requires absence of three molecular resistance markers
(feature_023, feature_006, feature_022) and a low value of a continuous
tumor-burden / load marker (feature_005). Mechanistically this resembles, but
the dataset does not name, an EGFR-inhibitor-like profile in metastatic
colorectal cancer (resistance markers consistent with a RAS/RAF axis;
feature_005 consistent with a tumor-burden marker such as CEA or LDH).
