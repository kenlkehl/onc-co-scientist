ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_crc (n=50,000; outcome: pfs_months)
=============================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The cohort contains 50,000 patients with a single outcome (pfs_months;
mean 4.31, sd 2.29, range 0.00–16.25) and 33 candidate features
(15 binary, 1 three-level [feature_001], 17 continuous). I ran a
twenty-iteration propose–test–refine loop covering univariate associations,
joint multivariable modelling, a systematic treatment-by-feature
interaction screen, single- and multi-feature subgroup tests, and
within-subgroup heterogeneity searches. The headline finding is a clean
predictive-biomarker rule for the only treatment-like variable
(feature_027): treatment benefit is concentrated in a tightly defined
~19% of the cohort, and any single positive resistance marker abolishes
the entire effect.

PROGNOSTIC PICTURE (independent of treatment)
---------------------------------------------
The full multivariable OLS (R² = 0.859) cleanly separates two groups
of features:

(a) PROGNOSTIC features that predict baseline pfs_months:
    feature_015  +0.176 mo per unit (range 30–90; r=+0.77 marginal)
    feature_001  −1.171 mo per level (3-level: 0/1/2)
    feature_014  −1.362 mo for =1 (~55% prevalence)
    feature_019  +0.469 mo per unit (range 1.5–5.5)
    feature_025  −0.076 mo per unit (range 0–25)

(b) Several smaller-effect features (feature_005 marginal coef
    −0.005/unit, feature_021 −0.0004/unit) reach formal significance
    given n=50,000 but their clinical magnitude is negligible.

Twenty-one of the 33 features have no detectable independent main
effect on pfs_months and behave like noise — feature_002, 003, 004,
007, 008, 009, 010, 012, 013, 016, 017, 018, 020, 024, 026, 028, 029,
030, 031, 032, 033 — all with adjusted |t|<2.

TREATMENT IDENTIFICATION
------------------------
feature_027 is the candidate treatment:
  - Binary, prevalence ~20%.
  - Adjusted main coefficient is the only large positive binary effect
    (+0.94 mo, t=98) in the full OLS.
  - Baseline covariate balance: every continuous feature t-test and
    every binary feature χ² has p ≥ 0.12 (smallest is feature_001
    p=0.124). This is consistent with randomized or pseudo-randomized
    treatment assignment.

Marginal treatment effect (unadjusted): +0.972 mo (p≈0; n_T=10,022,
n_C=39,978). Adjusted estimate is essentially identical (+0.943 mo),
confirming randomization.

TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY (the key finding)
------------------------------------------------
A systematic treatment-by-feature interaction screen (controlling for
the prognostic block) flagged four large modifiers:

  feature_006  interaction = −1.46 (p≈0): TE = +1.45 at c=0 vs ≈0 at c=1
  feature_023  interaction = −1.64 (p≈0): TE = +1.63 at c=0 vs ≈0 at c=1
  feature_022  interaction = −0.99 (p=2e-102): TE = +0.99 at c=0 vs ≈0 at c=1
  feature_005  interaction = −0.026/unit (p≈0): TE shrinks across range

Direct stratified tests confirmed each acts as a near-perfect nullifier:

  feature_006=1 (alone, others 0): TE = +0.083 (p=0.12)
  feature_023=1 (alone, others 0): TE = +0.061 (p=0.17)
  feature_022=1 (alone, others 0): TE = −0.204 (p=0.13)

That is, ANY single positive marker among feature_006/023/022 fully
abolishes the treatment effect — the nullifiers are independently
sufficient.

For feature_005 (continuous), the interaction is in fact threshold-like,
not linear. Within fav3 = {feature_006=0 ∧ feature_023=0 ∧ feature_022=0},
TE by 1-unit bin of feature_005:

  (0,1] +4.96 | (1,2] +5.10 | (2,3] +5.02 | (3,4] +5.08 | (4,5] +4.83 |
  (5,6] +0.10 | (6,7] −0.24 | (7,8] +0.18 | (8,10] +0.03 | (10,15] +0.18 | (>15) −0.13

A sharp cutoff at feature_005 = 5.0: below the threshold TE ≈ +5 mo,
above it TE ≈ 0.

THE COMPLETE RESPONDER SUBGROUP (final hypothesis)
--------------------------------------------------
fav4 = {feature_006=0 AND feature_023=0 AND feature_022=0 AND feature_005 ≤ 5}

Sample: n=9,338 (18.7% of cohort), with 1,905 treated and 7,433 control.
Inside fav4:  unadjusted TE = +5.014 mo (p≈0)
              adjusted TE (controlling for feature_015, _001, _014,
                           _019, _025) = +4.978 mo (p≈0, t=1153).
Outside fav4: TE = +0.022 mo (p>0.5).

Full-cohort OLS with fav4 as the interaction term:
  T main coefficient                    = −0.004 mo   (p=0.11)
  T × fav4 coefficient                  = +4.982 mo   (p≈0)
  Main effects of feature_006, _023, _022, _005   |all p ≥ 0.24|

This last point is biologically informative: the four defining features
are PURE treatment-effect modifiers (predictive markers), not prognostic.
In untreated patients, mean pfs_months differs by less than 0.02 mo
across feature_006=0/1, feature_023=0/1, feature_022=0/1, and
feature_005≤5/>5. They do not predict baseline outcome at all — they
only modify how the patient responds to treatment.

REFUTED OR NULL HYPOTHESES
--------------------------
- feature_011 was flagged in the unconditioned interaction screen as an
  "enhancer" (TE +0.92 in 011=0 vs +1.80 in 011=1). Refuted: within fav4
  the difference is only +5.00 vs +5.24, p=0.31. The marginal signal
  came from the small set of feature_011=1 patients more often falling
  inside fav4. feature_011 is also not a competing treatment (adjusted
  main effect +0.021 mo, p=0.36).

- feature_001 (the strongest 3-level prognostic) is not a meaningful TE
  modifier within fav4: TE = +4.87 / +5.09 / +4.81 across levels 0/1/2.
  The T × feature_001 interaction within fav4 reaches p=1.7e-6 by virtue
  of n=9,338 but the interaction coefficient is only −0.030 mo per level
  — clinically negligible.

- No remaining binary feature among feature_002, 010, 012, 016, 018, 020,
  024, 028, 031 modifies TE within fav4 by more than ±0.3 mo.

- Within fav4, the largest residual T × continuous-feature interaction
  is T × feature_015 (coef +0.0053/unit, implying TE varies between
  +4.94 and +5.01 mo across the IQR). This is not clinically meaningful
  and is plausibly an artifact of left-truncation at pfs_months = 0.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. feature_027 is the treatment in this CRC cohort, with apparent
   randomized assignment.

2. The marginal treatment effect (+0.97 mo) under-states a much larger
   effect that exists in a precisely identifiable subgroup.

3. The complete responder rule is a 4-feature conjunction:
   feature_006=0 AND feature_023=0 AND feature_022=0 AND feature_005≤5.
   In this ~19% of patients, treatment adds ≈+5 months of PFS.

4. Each of the four defining markers acts independently and sufficiently
   as a resistance signal: turning ANY of them positive collapses the
   TE to zero. They have no prognostic value on their own — they are
   pure predictive (treatment-effect-modifying) biomarkers, the kind
   one would expect from molecular resistance markers rather than
   markers of disease severity.

5. The five prognostic features (feature_015, _001, _014, _019, _025)
   determine baseline PFS additively and do not modify the treatment
   effect in any clinically meaningful way.

6. No other feature in the dataset contributes either as a main
   prognostic predictor or as a treatment-effect modifier beyond the
   structure above.
