ds001_crc — Analysis Summary
============================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with progression-free survival (pfs_months) outcome and 33
de-identified clinical/biomarker features. No missing values. Median PFS = 4.21 months
(IQR 2.72-5.74), max 16.25 months, with 2.8% having PFS=0.


1. Main-effect screening (Iterations 1-3)
-----------------------------------------
Univariate associations with pfs_months identified a few strong drivers and many
features with no detectable signal:

  Strongest univariate drivers
  - feature_015 (continuous, range 30-90): r = +0.766. Mean PFS rises monotonically
    from 1.33 mo in the lowest decile (30-52) to 7.45 mo in the highest (78-90).
    Pattern is consistent with a performance-status-like score (Karnofsky-like).
  - feature_001 (ordinal 0/1/2): mean PFS 5.27 → 4.05 → 2.91 (ANOVA F=3529, p≈0).
    Behaves like disease stage / extent.
  - feature_014 (binary, 55%): -1.35 mo when =1 (p≈0). Strong prognostic.
  - feature_027 (binary, 20%): +0.97 mo when =1 (p=2e-216). Candidate treatment.
  - feature_025 (continuous, 0-25): r = -0.123, p≈0. Burden-like marker.
  - feature_019 (continuous, 1.5-5.5): r = +0.10, p≈0.
  - feature_006 / feature_023 / feature_022 (binary): smaller negative main effects.
  - feature_011 (rare 3%): small positive main effect (+0.22).

  Multivariable OLS with all features (using log transforms for highly skewed labs
  feature_005, feature_021) gave R² = 0.861. Independent significant predictors:
  feature_015, feature_001, feature_014, feature_006, feature_023, feature_022,
  log(feature_005), feature_019, log(feature_021), feature_025, feature_027. All
  other features (033, 029, 026, 013, 008, 030, 003, 004, 017, 032, 007, 009, 031,
  010, 024, 012, 018, 020, 028, 016, 002) were not independently significant at the
  multivariable level.


2. Categorical biomarker structure (Iteration 4)
------------------------------------------------
feature_023, feature_011, and feature_022 are mutually exclusive: no patient has
more than one of them =1. The cohort splits into four exhaustive categories:
  WT (all three = 0): n = 25,324 (50.6%), mean PFS = 4.48
  feature_023 only:   n = 20,898 (41.8%), mean PFS = 4.12
  feature_022 only:   n = 2,272 (4.5%),  mean PFS = 4.09
  feature_011 only:   n = 1,506 (3.0%),  mean PFS = 4.53

This pattern is consistent with a multi-state biomarker classification (e.g., a
RAS/BRAF-like mutation status grouping in colorectal cancer).


3. Treatment-effect heterogeneity discovery (Iterations 5-10)
-------------------------------------------------------------
Treating feature_027 as the candidate treatment, an interaction screen against every
other feature revealed strong, biologically-coherent heterogeneity:

  feature_027 × feature_023: interaction coef = -1.63 (p = 8e-226)
    Stratified treatment effect: feature_023=0: +1.66 mo (p≈0); feature_023=1: +0.03
    mo (p=0.46). The treatment is essentially inert in feature_023-positive patients.

  feature_027 × feature_006: interaction coef = -1.45 (p = 4e-168)
    Stratified: feature_006=0 → +1.48 mo; feature_006=1 → +0.03 mo.

  feature_027 × feature_022: interaction coef = -0.99 (p = 1e-104)
    Stratified: feature_022=0 → +1.03 mo; feature_022=1 → -0.16 mo.

The eight-cell cross of (feature_006, feature_023, feature_022) showed a clean
dichotomy: in the triple-zero subgroup (n=17,372), feature_027 produces +2.76 mo
of PFS gain (p≈0). In every other cell of the cross, the effect is null (range
-0.20 to +0.08, all p>0.10).


4. Refining within the favorable triple-zero subgroup (Iterations 11-13)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Within the favorable group (feature_006=0 AND feature_023=0 AND feature_022=0), an
interaction analysis found a fourth modifier: log(feature_005). Quintile analysis
on feature_005 within favorable patients:

  Q1 (f005 0.04-1.61):   treatment effect = +5.02 mo
  Q2 (f005 1.62-3.27):   +5.06 mo
  Q3 (f005 3.28-6.02):   +3.45 mo  (transition)
  Q4 (f005 6.03-12.18):  -0.01 mo
  Q5 (f005 12.19-442.3): -0.02 mo

A fine-grained search of cutoffs from 4.00 to 5.20 located a sharp threshold at
exactly feature_005 = 5: below 5, treatment effect = +5.01 mo (n=9,326, p≈0); at
or above 5, treatment effect = +0.005 mo (n=8,046, p=0.93).


5. Final subgroup definition and adjusted estimates (Iterations 14-24)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Define ELIGIBLE = (feature_006=0 AND feature_023=0 AND feature_022=0 AND
feature_005 < 5). This captures 9,326 of 50,000 patients (18.7%).

Necessity of each predicate (relaxing one condition at a time within the otherwise-
eligible subgroup):
  Drop feature_006=0 → effect dilutes to +3.30
  Drop feature_023=0 → +2.85
  Drop feature_022=0 → +4.62
  Drop feature_005<5 → +2.76
All four predicates are non-redundant; feature_022 contributes least but still
detectably.

Treatment is balanced across eligibility (20.4% treated within eligible vs 20.0%
within ineligible, χ² p = 0.45) — consistent with random allocation.

Definitive (fully-adjusted) treatment effects, controlling for feature_015,
feature_001, feature_014, feature_019, log(feature_021), feature_025:
  Within ELIGIBLE   (n=9,326):  feature_027 coef = +4.979 [95% CI 4.971, 4.987], p≈0
  Within INELIGIBLE (n=40,674): feature_027 coef = -0.003 [95% CI -0.008, +0.002], p=0.24

Within the eligible subgroup, the treatment benefit was uniform across all other
features tested: feature_015 quintiles, feature_001 levels, feature_011, feature_019,
feature_025, log(feature_021), feature_017, feature_032, feature_003, feature_030,
feature_004, feature_008, feature_013, feature_026, feature_029, feature_007,
feature_033, feature_009, and every other binary. No additional effect modifiers
were detected.


6. Reinterpreting the apparent main effects of f006/f023/f022/f005 (Iterations 22-23)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Once eligibility × feature_027 is in the model, the apparent prognostic effects of
feature_006, feature_023, feature_022, and log(feature_005) all collapse to ≈0:
  feature_006:        coef +0.001 (p=0.57)
  feature_023:        coef -0.003 (p=0.14)
  feature_022:        coef +0.001 (p=0.85)
  log(feature_005):   coef +0.001 (p=0.42)
Their univariate "negative" associations were entirely an artifact of the unrealized
treatment benefit in patients who received feature_027 but were ineligible — that
is, these four features are PURELY PREDICTIVE (treatment-effect modifiers), not
prognostic factors.


7. Other candidate treatments (Iterations 12, 25)
-------------------------------------------------
Each of the remaining binary features (feature_010, 024, 012, 018, 020, 028, 016,
002, 031, plus the prognostic feature_014) was examined as a candidate treatment
with possible heterogeneous effects. None showed a meaningful subgroup-specific
effect: the largest pair-restricted treatment effect for any non-feature_027 binary
was within ±0.15 months. feature_027 is the unique treatment with subgroup
heterogeneity in this cohort.


8. Final structural summary
---------------------------
The data are well-described by a linear model with R² = 0.993:

  pfs_months ≈ -6.74
              + 0.176 × feature_015
              - 1.166 × feature_001
              - 1.369 × feature_014
              + 0.485 × feature_019
              - 0.078 × feature_025
              - 0.097 × log(feature_021)
              + 4.979 × (eligible × feature_027)
              + noise

where eligible = 1 iff feature_006=0 AND feature_023=0 AND feature_022=0 AND
feature_005 < 5.

Prognostic features (independent of treatment): feature_015 (continuous +),
feature_001 (ordinal -), feature_014 (binary -), feature_019 (continuous +),
feature_025 (continuous -), log(feature_021) (continuous -).

Predictive features (treatment-effect modifiers for feature_027): feature_006,
feature_023, feature_022, feature_005 — each is required to be in its "favorable"
state for treatment to work.

Noise / no detectable independent association: feature_011, feature_002, feature_028,
feature_016, feature_010, feature_024, feature_012, feature_018, feature_020,
feature_031, feature_017, feature_032, feature_003, feature_030, feature_008,
feature_004, feature_013, feature_026, feature_029, feature_007, feature_033,
feature_009.


9. Best-supported final treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis
------------------------------------------------------------
The treatment feature_027 increases pfs_months by approximately +5.0 months
(adjusted estimate 4.979 mo, 95% CI 4.971-4.987, p≈0) in patients meeting ALL of the
following predicates:

    feature_006 = 0
  AND feature_023 = 0
  AND feature_022 = 0
  AND feature_005 < 5

In any patient outside this subgroup, the treatment has no detectable effect
(point estimate -0.003 mo, 95% CI -0.008 to +0.002, p = 0.24). The subgroup
contains 18.7% of the cohort (9,326 of 50,000 patients), and treatment is
randomly allocated with respect to it.
