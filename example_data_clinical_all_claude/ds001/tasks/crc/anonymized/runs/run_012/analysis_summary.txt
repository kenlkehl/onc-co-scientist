Analysis summary — ds001_crc (50,000 patients, 33 anonymized features, outcome = pfs_months)
================================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The dataset has a single continuous outcome (pfs_months, range 0-16.25, mean 4.31, std 2.29) and
33 anonymized features (16 binary, 1 ordinal with three levels, 16 continuous). I ran 25
iterations of hypothesis generation and statistical testing, working from univariate associations
out to a final treatment-effect heterogeneity model. A multivariable OLS using all features
explained 86% of the variance in pfs_months; adding a treatment-by-subgroup interaction term
brought R^2 to 0.93. The dominant story is (1) a very large continuous prognostic axis
(feature_015), (2) an ordinal performance-status-like variable (feature_001), (3) two binary
factors with strong universal main effects (feature_014 adverse, feature_027 favorable), and
(4) sharply concentrated treatment-effect heterogeneity for feature_027.

MAIN EFFECTS — what the evidence supports
-----------------------------------------
Feature_015 (continuous, 30-90, mean 65; iter 1-2). Spearman r = +0.785 with pfs_months. Linear
slope +0.176 PFS-months per unit. R^2 = 0.587 from this single variable. Strikingly, feature_015
is statistically independent of every other measured feature (all |Spearman r| < 0.011 with
continuous covariates, all ANOVA p>0.05 across binary feature levels). It carries an independent
prognostic axis that the other measured variables do not capture — clearly the most important
single predictor in the cohort. Hypothesis supported, very strongly (effect direction positive).

Feature_001 (ordinal 0/1/2; iter 3). After adjusting for feature_015, level 1 vs 0 = -1.20 mo
and level 2 vs 0 = -2.34 mo (both p<1e-300). Strong, monotonic dose-response — looks
performance-status-like. Hypothesis supported.

Feature_014 (binary, 55% prevalence; iter 4, 10, 23). Adjusted effect = -1.35 mo (p<1e-300). I
specifically searched for effect modifiers: across every level of every binary and ordinal
feature with adequate sample size, feature_014's stratum-specific effect ranged from -1.24
(feature_011=1, n=1,506) to -1.85 (feature_002=1, n=251). The largest interaction term in OLS
was a -0.0067 mo per-feature_015-unit slope (p=1.1e-17), indicating a very modestly stronger
adverse effect at higher feature_015. Otherwise, feature_014 is best modeled as a universal
adverse main effect with no clinically meaningful modifier. Hypothesis (adverse) supported;
heterogeneity hypothesis refuted.

Feature_027 (binary, 20% prevalence; iter 5, 11-25). Marginal adjusted effect = +0.94 mo, but
this average masks dramatic heterogeneity (see below).

Feature_023 (binary, 42%), feature_006 (binary, 35%), feature_022 (binary, 5%) (iter 6).
Adjusted effects -0.36, -0.28, -0.36 mo respectively — all small adverse main effects. The
much more important role for these three features turned out to be as effect modifiers of
feature_027 (iter 11-13, 24).

Continuous lab-like covariates (iter 7). After multivariable adjustment, three additional
features carried independent signal: feature_019 (range 1.5-5.5, albumin-like) +0.469 mo per
unit (favorable); feature_025 (range 0-24.6, neutrophil-lymphocyte-ratio-like) -0.076 mo per
unit (adverse); feature_005 (range 0-777, CEA-like) -0.005 mo per unit (modestly adverse).
feature_021 had a tiny but statistically significant adverse main effect (-0.0004/unit, p<1e-18);
feature_032 was borderline (p=0.057). All other lab features (feature_017, feature_003,
feature_030, feature_008, feature_004, feature_013, feature_026, feature_029, feature_007,
feature_033, feature_009) were null after adjustment.

Null features (iter 8-9). feature_011 (3.0% prev) was favorable in univariate analysis (+0.22 mo,
p=7e-04) but the effect collapsed to +0.01 (p=0.70) after adjustment — confounded by the
biomarker-defined subgroup it concentrates in. feature_002 (0.5% prev) had no effect.
feature_028, feature_016, feature_024, feature_010, feature_012, feature_018, feature_020,
and feature_031 (sex-like) all had |adjusted effect| < 0.04 mo and p > 0.10 — consistent with
no association.

TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY — feature_027
--------------------------------------------
This was the most striking finding of the analysis. feature_027's marginal +0.94 mo benefit is
the average of two very different worlds:

  Step 1 (iter 11-13): Stratifying by feature_006, feature_023, and feature_022 individually
  showed each one essentially abolishes the feature_027 benefit when set to 1.

      feature_006 = 0 (n=32,343):  feature_027 effect = +1.480 mo (p=3.7e-274)
      feature_006 = 1 (n=17,657):  feature_027 effect = +0.026 mo (p=0.52)
      feature_023 = 0 (n=29,102):  feature_027 effect = +1.656 mo (p=6.1e-293)
      feature_023 = 1 (n=20,898):  feature_027 effect = +0.027 mo (p=0.46)
      feature_022 = 0 (n=47,728):  feature_027 effect = +1.027 mo (p=5e-226)
      feature_022 = 1 (n=2,272):   feature_027 effect = -0.156 mo (p=0.13)

  Step 2 (iter 14): Combining the three biomarkers, the 8-cell joint stratification showed only
  the (0,0,0) cell carries a meaningful feature_027 benefit. Triple-biomarker-negative
  subgroup (n=17,372): +2.758 mo benefit. Anyone with at least one positive: +0.030 mo (null).

  Step 3 (iter 15, 17): Within the triple-negative subgroup, the feature_027 effect is further
  modulated by feature_005:

      feature_005 quartile 1 (0.04-1.96, n=4,342):   feature_027 effect = +5.030 mo
      feature_005 quartile 2 (1.97-4.44, n=4,314):   feature_027 effect = +5.037 mo
      feature_005 quartile 3 (4.45-10.07, n=4,446):  feature_027 effect = +0.674 mo
      feature_005 quartile 4 (10.08-442,  n=4,270):  feature_027 effect = -0.013 mo

  The threshold is sharp: the +5 mo effect plateau holds for any cutpoint between 1.0 and 5.0
  on feature_005, then collapses at higher values. Continuous interaction coefficient
  feature_027 x feature_005 = -0.0258 mo per unit (p<1e-300).

  Step 4 (iter 18-21): Within the refined subgroup (feature_006=0 & feature_023=0 &
  feature_022=0 & feature_005<4.44; n=8,640 = 17.3% of the cohort), the feature_027 benefit is
  essentially homogeneous: across feature_015 tertiles (4.93/5.07/5.12 mo), across feature_001
  levels (4.91/5.12/4.74 mo), and across feature_011 strata (5.02 vs 5.25 mo). feature_011's
  univariate-cohort modifier signal is therefore an artifact of feature_011 being concentrated
  in the responsive subgroup — not an independent modifier.

  Step 5 (iter 25, final): The complete subgroup definition is

      feature_006 = 0  AND  feature_023 = 0  AND  feature_022 = 0  AND  feature_005 < 4.44

  In this subgroup (n=8,640): feature_027=1 mean pfs_months = 9.12, feature_027=0 mean = 4.08,
  difference = +5.03 mo (p<1e-300). In the complement (n=41,360): feature_027 effect = +0.095
  mo (statistically significant due to n, but clinically negligible). The unfavorable values
  that suppress the feature_027 effect are: feature_006=1, feature_023=1, feature_022=1, and
  feature_005 >= ~4.44.

Adding the subgroup-by-treatment interaction term to a multivariable backbone (feature_015,
feature_001, feature_014, feature_019, feature_025, feature_027, subgroup) yields:
  feature_027 alone = +0.07 mo, subgroup main effect = -0.005 mo (n.s.), interaction = +4.91 mo
  (p<1e-300). Total in-subgroup feature_027 effect = +4.98 mo. R^2 of this interaction model = 0.93.

The pattern is highly suggestive of a targeted therapy whose efficacy depends on a wild-type
biomarker panel and a low pretreatment tumor-burden marker — feature_027 looks like a
biomarker-selected treatment, with feature_006/023/022 acting as resistance markers and
feature_005 as a tumor-burden marker that further attenuates response.

TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY — feature_014
--------------------------------------------
By contrast, feature_014 has no meaningful effect modifier. Its effect ranges narrowly between
-1.24 and -1.85 mo across every stratum tested (39 strata across all binary and ordinal
features, plus quartiles of every continuous lab feature). The strongest interaction in
multivariable OLS was feature_014 x feature_015 (-0.0067/unit, p=1.1e-17) — statistically
significant given the sample size but small in magnitude. feature_014 is best modeled as a
uniform adverse main-effect predictor.

CONCLUSIONS
-----------
1. Prognosis in this cohort is dominated by feature_015 (continuous, independent prognostic
   axis explaining ~59% of pfs_months variance) and feature_001 (ordinal, performance-status-
   like). Together with feature_014 (adverse), feature_019 (favorable), and feature_025
   (adverse), they account for almost all the explained variance from non-treatment features.

2. feature_027 is the dominant treatment-effect signal, with sharply concentrated benefit. The
   final supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis is:

       Treatment:  feature_027 = 1
       Outcome:    pfs_months
       Direction:  longer pfs_months on feature_027
       Subgroup:   feature_006 = 0 AND feature_023 = 0 AND feature_022 = 0 AND feature_005 < 4.44
       Magnitude:  ~ +5.03 months PFS (p < 1e-300; n = 8,640; 17.3% of the cohort)
       Outside subgroup: ~ +0.10 months PFS (clinically negligible)

3. feature_014 is uniformly adverse with no clinically relevant effect modifier; it should be
   modeled as a main-effect-only prognostic factor.

4. Several intermediate-strength binary and continuous features (feature_023, feature_006,
   feature_022 as resistance markers; feature_019 favorable; feature_025 and feature_005
   adverse) carry independent signal. feature_023, feature_006, and feature_022 collectively
   behave as a single resistance phenotype with respect to feature_027 efficacy.

5. Hypotheses that were refuted: any independent modifier role for feature_011, age-related
   modification of feature_014 effect (statistically detectable but trivially small), and
   non-trivial main effects of feature_002, feature_028, feature_016, feature_024, feature_010,
   feature_012, feature_018, feature_020, feature_031 (sex-like), and most lab features after
   adjustment.
