Oncology Dataset ds001_crc — Iterative Analysis Summary
=========================================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with anonymized features (feature_001 … feature_033)
Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival in months; mean 4.31, median 4.21,
range 0.00–16.25, no missing values).

I ran 25 iterations of propose-and-test, starting with main effects, advancing to
multivariable adjustment, then to systematic interaction screening, finishing with
joint subgroup definition and verification.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Main effects on pfs_months (iterations 1–3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Binary features (Welch t-tests, complete cohort):
  - feature_014 (prev=0.55):   pfs 5.05 -> 3.70  (diff -1.35, p~0)         [STRONG]
  - feature_027 (prev=0.20):   pfs 4.12 -> 5.09  (diff +0.97, p~1.7e-216)   [STRONG]
  - feature_023 (prev=0.42):   pfs 4.45 -> 4.12  (diff -0.33, p~2e-58)
  - feature_006 (prev=0.35):   pfs 4.42 -> 4.11  (diff -0.31, p~1e-49)
  - feature_022 (prev=0.045):  pfs 4.32 -> 4.09  (diff -0.23, p~3e-7)
  - feature_011 (prev=0.030):  pfs 4.30 -> 4.53  (diff +0.22, p=7.3e-4)
  - all other binary features (10/15) not significant.

Ordinal feature_001 (levels 0/1/2; 35%/50%/15%): mean PFS 5.27 / 4.05 / 2.91,
ANOVA p~0; very strong monotonic gradient — looks like a performance-status–type
prognostic.

Continuous features:
  - feature_015 (range 30–90, median 65): r=+0.766 with PFS, p~0; quartile means
    rise monotonically (2.09 -> 3.71 -> 4.90 -> 6.59). Likely an age-or-time-axis
    variable that dominates baseline variance.
  - feature_025: r=-0.123 (p~2e-167)
  - feature_019: r=+0.102 (p~3e-115)
  - feature_005: r=-0.038 (p~3e-17), but very long-tailed; non-linear effects below.
  - All other continuous features have only |r|<0.02 and are negligible alone.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Multivariable model and balance checks (iterations 4–5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A linear model with all 33 features explained R²=0.859 of variance in pfs_months.
Strongest independent predictors after adjustment (all p<1e-15):
  feature_001 -1.17, feature_014 -1.36, feature_015 +0.176, feature_019 +0.475,
  feature_025 -0.078, feature_023 -0.354, feature_006 -0.287, feature_005 -0.005,
  feature_022 -0.356, feature_027 +0.943.

All binary features showed near-balanced means of feature_001, feature_014,
feature_015, and feature_019 between their 0/1 strata, so the binary indicators
are essentially randomly distributed across measured prognostics — observed
"main effects" can be interpreted approximately as conditional-average effects
without heavy confounding.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Treatment-effect heterogeneity for feature_027 (iterations 6–13)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
feature_027 emerged as the dominant treatment-like binary in the data, with
prevalence 20% and a marginal benefit of about +0.97 months. Systematic
interaction screening (each candidate × each other feature, adjusted for the
top prognostics) identified five effect modifiers:

   feature_027 × feature_023   coef = -1.63   (p ~ 0)
   feature_027 × feature_006   coef = -1.46   (p ~ 0)
   feature_027 × feature_022   coef = -0.99   (p ~ 1e-103)
   feature_027 × feature_011   coef = +0.89   (p ~ 2e-57)
   feature_027 × feature_005   coef = -0.0258 (p ~ 0)

Critically, feature_023, feature_006, feature_022 and feature_011 are PURELY
PREDICTIVE: in untreated patients (feature_027=0) all four have null effects on
pfs_months (diff ≈ 0, p > 0.5). In treated patients they reshape the effect
dramatically:
                          untreated         treated
   feature_023  0 vs 1:  4.117 vs 4.116 (p=0.95)   5.773 vs 4.143 (p~1e-204)
   feature_006  0 vs 1:  4.121 vs 4.109 (p=0.59)   5.601 vs 4.135 (p~3e-169)
   feature_022  0 vs 1:  4.116 vs 4.126 (p=0.85)   5.144 vs 3.971 (p~1e-30)
   feature_011  0 vs 1:  4.117 vs 4.125 (p=0.89)   5.058 vs 6.054 (p~1e-7)

Stratified treatment effects:
   feature_023=0:  TE = +1.66 months;  feature_023=1:  TE = +0.03 (n.s.)
   feature_006=0:  TE = +1.48 months;  feature_006=1:  TE = +0.03 (n.s.)
   feature_022=0:  TE = +1.03 months;  feature_022=1:  TE = -0.16 (n.s.)
   feature_011=1:  TE = +1.93 months;  feature_011=0:  TE = +0.94

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Joint definition of the responsive subgroup (iterations 14–22)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Combining the three exclusionary biomarkers, I tested the triple-negative
subgroup (feature_023=0 AND feature_006=0 AND feature_022=0; n=17,372). Within
this group feature_027 produced TE = +2.76 months — substantially larger than
its marginal +0.97. Conversely, in the complement (≥1 of the three positive)
the marginal TE was only +0.03 (p=0.29).

I then layered feature_005 in as a continuous modifier. Within the
triple-negative cohort:
   tertile 1 (f005 0.04–2.69):    TE = +5.05 (n_treated=1,220)
   tertile 2 (f005 2.70–7.36):    TE = +2.98 (n_treated=1,140)
   tertile 3 (f005 7.37–442.29):  TE = -0.01 (n.s.) (n_treated=1,101)

A within-cohort interaction model gave feature_027 main = +3.35, interaction
with feature_005 = -0.065 per unit feature_005 (p~0). Treatment benefit
disappears at roughly feature_005 ≈ 5.

The final favorable subgroup is therefore:
   feature_023 = 0  AND  feature_006 = 0  AND  feature_022 = 0  AND  feature_005 < 5
   n = 9,326 (n_treated = 1,903)
   pfs(no feature_027) = 4.088 ± 2.088
   pfs(feature_027)    = 9.101 ± 2.176
   ΔPFS = +5.013 months  (p ~ 0)

In the complement (any of feature_023=1, feature_006=1, feature_022=1, or
feature_005 ≥ 5):
   n = 40,674 (n_treated = 8,119)
   ΔPFS = +0.025 months (p = 0.33, n.s.)

Each predicate is independently necessary: relaxing any single condition
materially shrinks the treatment effect (drop f023 → +2.85; drop f006 → +3.30;
drop f022 → +4.63; drop f005<5 → +2.76; all four → +5.01).

Within the favorable subgroup, feature_011=1 added only +0.24 months extra
(5.24 vs 5.00, n.s.), so it was dropped from the final definition. No other
binary or continuous feature provided incremental refinement (smallest
within-cohort interaction p = 0.073 for feature_029).

6. Joint multivariable model (iteration 23)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Final OLS combining prognostics and the feature_027 × biomarker structure
(R²=0.902):

   feature_001          -1.168   (p~0)
   feature_014          -1.355   (p~0)
   feature_015          +0.176   (p~0)
   feature_019          +0.475   (p~0)
   feature_025          -0.078   (p~0)
   feature_023, 006, 022, 011, 005 main effects:  ≈ 0 (all p > 0.69 — pure predictive)
   feature_027          +2.506   (p~0)   [base treatment effect in fully favorable case]
   feature_027:feature_023  -1.76 (p~0)
   feature_027:feature_006  -1.47 (p~0)
   feature_027:feature_022  -1.76 (p~0)
   feature_027:feature_005  -0.025 per unit (p~0)
   feature_027:feature_011  +0.076 (p=0.10, n.s.)

Interpretation: feature_027 confers about +2.5 months of PFS in a hypothetical
patient with feature_023=0, feature_006=0, feature_022=0, feature_005=0, and
feature_011=0. Each positive resistance marker subtracts roughly the entire
benefit; each unit of feature_005 erodes ~0.025–0.065 months (depending on
whether estimated marginally or within triple-negatives).

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. Hypothesis status
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SUPPORTED:
  - feature_015, feature_001, feature_014, feature_019, feature_025 are strong
    prognostic factors for pfs_months.
  - feature_027 is a beneficial treatment-like indicator with a marginal effect
    of ~+1 month, concentrated entirely in a well-defined biomarker subgroup.
  - feature_023, feature_006, feature_022 are PURE PREDICTIVE biomarkers whose
    presence (=1) abolishes feature_027 benefit individually; their combined
    presence partitions responders from non-responders cleanly.
  - feature_005 is a continuous predictive biomarker; treatment benefit
    decreases monotonically with feature_005, vanishing near feature_005 = 5.
  - feature_011 has a positive interaction with feature_027 marginally, but
    once the (f023,f006,f022,f005) subgroup is fixed it adds no incremental
    benefit.

REFUTED / NULL:
  - No second active treatment among the binary indicators: every other binary
    showed |t-stat| < 3.5 in interaction screening.
  - feature_001, feature_014, feature_015 do not modify the relative effect of
    feature_027 (no qualitative interaction).
  - feature_023, feature_006, feature_022, feature_011 have no main prognostic
    effect on pfs_months; their univariate "main effect" is purely an artifact
    of treated patients in the cohort.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Final treatment-effect-heterogeneity statement
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment:    feature_027 (received vs not received)
Outcome:      pfs_months
Direction:    Positive (longer pfs_months on feature_027)
Subgroup:     feature_023 = 0  AND  feature_006 = 0  AND  feature_022 = 0
              AND  feature_005 < 5
Magnitude:    +5.0 months (p~0; n=9,326; n_treated=1,903) within subgroup;
              +0.03 months (n.s.) outside subgroup.

Clinically the pattern is highly suggestive of anti-EGFR antibody therapy in
metastatic colorectal cancer: an effective drug whose benefit is restricted to
RAS wild-type, BRAF wild-type, left-sided primary tumors with relatively low
tumor burden. Within this dataset that mapping is the most parsimonious
explanation, and the four predicates above are necessary and (jointly)
sufficient to identify the responder subgroup.
