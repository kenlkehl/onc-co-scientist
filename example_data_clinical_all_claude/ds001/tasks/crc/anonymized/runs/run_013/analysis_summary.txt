ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_crc (n=50,000; outcome: pfs_months)
=============================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The cohort contains 33 anonymized patient features and a single continuous
outcome, pfs_months (mean 4.31, SD 2.29, range 0-16.25). Across 25 iterations
I screened each feature for prognostic and predictive associations with
pfs_months, identified the apparent randomized treatment indicator, ran a
systematic treatment-effect heterogeneity search, and refined a multi-predicate
subgroup definition that explains essentially all of the treatment-related
variance in the cohort.

KEY FINDINGS
------------

1. TREATMENT INDICATOR. feature_027 is consistent with a randomized treatment
   variable: prevalence ~20%, balanced with respect to all 32 other features
   (every covariate-vs-feature_027 test p > 0.09), unadjusted main effect on
   pfs_months = +0.972 months (Welch t-test p = 1.7e-216), and the adjusted
   effect (controlling for all features) is +0.943 — essentially unchanged,
   ruling out confounding.

2. JOINT FAVORABLE SUBGROUP (PRIMARY RESULT). The marginal +0.94 month effect
   is highly heterogeneous. A systematic interaction screen (each feature
   against feature_027) identified five strong modifiers: feature_005,
   feature_023, feature_006, feature_022, and feature_011 (all interaction
   p < 1e-10). Iteratively combining the four "negative" modifiers yields a
   clean joint favorable subgroup definition:

      FAVORABLE = (feature_006 = 0)
              AND (feature_023 = 0)
              AND (feature_005 <= 5.0)
              AND (feature_022 = 0)

   In the favorable subgroup (n = 9,338; 18.7% of cohort):
      Treated (feature_027 = 1) mean pfs_months = 9.10 (n=1,905)
      Untreated mean pfs_months                = 4.09 (n=7,433)
      Treatment effect                         = +5.014 mo (p ~ 0)

   In the complement (n = 40,662):
      Treatment effect = +0.024 mo (p = 0.36)

   Necessity check: starting from the all-favorable subgroup and flipping each
   predicate one at a time (with the other three favorable) drops the
   treatment effect to essentially zero in every case:
      flip feature_006 to 1     -> eff = +0.113 (p = 0.12)
      flip feature_023 to 1     -> eff = +0.068 (p = 0.26)
      flip feature_005 to > 5   -> eff = -0.002 (p = 0.97)
      flip feature_022 to 1     -> eff = -0.165 (p = 0.38)
   Each predicate is therefore individually necessary; no proper subset of
   the four predicates is sufficient.

3. THRESHOLD FOR feature_005. feature_005 is heavily right-skewed (median 4.44,
   max 777). A within-stratum sweep of cutoffs in the (feature_006=0,
   feature_023=0) subgroup shows a sharp threshold near feature_005 ~ 5: the
   treatment effect is +4.6 mo for feature_005 <= 5 and falls to ~0 for
   feature_005 > 5. The threshold is not a soft gradient.

4. PURE PREDICTIVE BIOMARKERS. Critically, the four effect-modifier features
   are NOT prognostic at baseline. In the untreated cohort (feature_027 = 0,
   n = 39,978), an OLS model adjusting for all candidate covariates yields:
      feature_006 coef = +0.003 (p = 0.09)
      feature_023 coef = -0.002 (p = 0.46)
      feature_022 coef = +0.002 (p = 0.74)
      feature_005 coef = -1e-5  (p = 0.82)
   None is significant. In the treated cohort the same four become highly
   significant negative predictors of PFS (e.g., feature_006 -1.47,
   feature_023 -1.76, feature_022 -1.76; all p < 1e-50). This pattern —
   no main effect in untreated, large negative effect in treated — is the
   hallmark of pure predictive (treatment-effect-modifying) biomarkers.

5. INTRINSIC PROGNOSTIC FACTORS (independent of treatment). In the untreated
   cohort, six features drive baseline pfs_months:
      feature_015 (continuous, range 30-90; plausibly age):
          slope = +0.176 mo per unit. Univariate Spearman rho with PFS = +0.785,
          accounting for ~59% of pfs variance alone — by far the largest
          single signal in the cohort.
      feature_001 (3-level ordinal; plausibly performance status):
          level 0 = reference; level 1 -> -1.18; level 2 -> -2.31.
      feature_014 (binary; plausibly extensive disease/metastases):
          coefficient -1.37.
      feature_019 (continuous, range 1.5-5.5; plausibly serum albumin):
          coefficient +0.48 per unit.
      feature_025 (continuous, range 0-24.6): coefficient -0.077.
      feature_021 (continuous, range 48-830): coefficient -0.0005 (small but
          highly significant).
   These factors retain their effects in both arms of feature_027 and do not
   modify the treatment effect (interaction p > 0.05 for each in pairwise
   tests with feature_027).

6. SECONDARY POSITIVE MODIFIER. feature_011 (rare; ~3% prevalence) shows an
   ENHANCING treatment-effect interaction: in feature_011 = 1 the treatment
   effect is +1.93 vs +0.94 baseline (interaction p = 1.2e-11). Within the
   joint favorable subgroup the additional benefit is small in absolute
   terms (+5.24 vs +4.99), so feature_011 is a minor add-on positive
   predictor rather than a primary subgroup driver.

7. NON-MODIFIERS. Despite their large prognostic effects, feature_015,
   feature_001, feature_014, and feature_019 all show interaction p > 0.05
   with feature_027. The strong prognostic gradient persists within the
   treated favorable subgroup (e.g., treated-favorable pfs_months by
   feature_015 quartile: 6.80, 8.44, 9.71, 11.50 — monotonic), but the
   incremental effect of treatment is approximately constant.

HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED VS. REFUTED
--------------------------------
SUPPORTED (significant in the predicted direction):
- feature_027 -> longer pfs_months (main effect; adjusted +0.94)
- Joint favorable subgroup definition with 4 predicates (effect +5.0; complement 0)
- Each of the 4 predicates is individually necessary
- Sharp threshold for feature_005 around 5
- feature_005, feature_006, feature_022, feature_023 are predictive, not prognostic
- feature_011 enhances the treatment effect (small additional bonus)
- feature_015 dominates baseline prognosis (rho +0.79)
- feature_001, feature_014, feature_019, feature_025, feature_021 are
  prognostic but not effect-modifying
- Treatment effect is null in the unfavorable complement

REFUTED / NULL:
- feature_031 main effect (p = 0.77) and interaction with feature_027 (p = 0.55)
- feature_014 interaction with feature_027 (p = 0.15) — strong prognostic but
  not predictive
- feature_001 interaction with feature_027 (p = 0.17) — same pattern
- Continuous features other than feature_005 do not meaningfully modify
  treatment effect within the favorable subgroup (largest residual signal:
  feature_032, magnitude 0.25 mo, marginal at p = 0.019)
- feature_002, feature_028, feature_010, feature_012, feature_018, feature_020,
  feature_016, feature_024 are not prognostic and do not modify treatment

OVERALL CONCLUSION (TREATMENT-BIOMARKER-OUTCOME)
------------------------------------------------
This dataset is best understood as a randomized treatment (feature_027)
overlaid on a strongly age-driven (feature_015) baseline-prognosis structure.
The treatment confers no clinically meaningful benefit in the broad cohort
(+0.94 mo on average) but has a large, well-defined subgroup of responders.

The complete predictive subgroup is:

    feature_006 = 0  AND  feature_023 = 0
    AND  feature_005 <= 5.0
    AND  feature_022 = 0

Within this 18.7% of the cohort, treatment increases pfs_months by ~5 months
(treated 9.10 vs untreated 4.09). Each of the four predicates is necessary;
removing any one drops the treatment effect to essentially zero. The four
predictive features are pure treatment-effect modifiers — they have no
prognostic effect in untreated patients. Independent of treatment, baseline
PFS is governed by feature_015, feature_001, feature_014, feature_019,
feature_025, and feature_021, none of which themselves modify the treatment
effect.

A clinical decision rule that follows from these analyses: administer
feature_027 only to patients meeting all four favorable predicates; in the
remaining 81.3% of patients there is no measurable PFS benefit from
feature_027.
