Analysis Summary — ds001_crc (50,000 patients, outcome: pfs_months)
====================================================================

Overview
--------
The dataset contains one outcome (pfs_months, mean 4.31, SD 2.29, range 0–16.25)
and 33 anonymized features. Features split into 16 binary/categorical variables
and 17 continuous variables. There is no missingness. The analysis below walks
through the iterative hypothesis–test–refine loop used to identify prognostic
features, candidate treatment indicators, and treatment-effect heterogeneity.

1. Univariate landscape (iterations 1–3)
----------------------------------------
Strong main effects on pfs_months:

Continuous, positive:
  feature_015 — Pearson r = 0.766 (p < 1e-300). Mean pfs_months rises monotonically
                across quintiles (1.86, 3.34, 4.30, 5.27, 6.83). This is by far
                the most influential continuous feature; range 30–90 with mean
                ~65 is consistent with an age- or duration-like variable, but in
                this cohort it is positively associated with pfs_months.
  feature_019 — r = +0.102, p = 3e-115 (modest but very significant).

Continuous, negative:
  feature_025 — r = -0.123, p = 2e-167 (modest, very significant).
  feature_005 — r = -0.038, p = 3e-17 (right-skewed, mean 9, max 777; small
                main effect but plays a major role as a treatment-effect modifier
                — see below).
  feature_021 — r = -0.011, marginal main effect (p = 0.013), retained in
                multivariable model.

Categorical, prognostic (decreasing pfs):
  feature_001 (0/1/2) — mean pfs_months 5.27 / 4.05 / 2.91 across the three
                levels; OLS slope -1.17 mo per level. Looks like an ordinal
                severity / stage / performance-status-like factor.
  feature_014 (binary, prevalence 55%) — diff -1.35 mo (p < 1e-300). Strong
                prognostic, possibly metastatic-disease indicator.
  feature_023 (binary, 42%) — diff -0.33 mo (p = 8e-56).
  feature_006 (binary, 35%) — diff -0.31 mo (p = 5e-46).
  feature_022 (binary, 4.5%) — diff -0.23 mo (p = 4e-6).

Categorical, with positive direction (unusual for prognostic features):
  feature_027 (binary, 20%) — diff +0.97 mo (p < 1e-300).
  feature_011 (binary, 3%) — diff +0.22 mo (p = 2e-4).

Several other binaries (feature_010, feature_012, feature_018, feature_020,
feature_024, feature_028, feature_031, feature_002, feature_016) showed no
detectable main effect (all p > 0.05).

2. Joint multivariable model (iteration 4)
------------------------------------------
A full OLS with all 33 features explains R² = 0.859 of pfs_months variance.
The eight largest-magnitude predictors after adjustment were exactly those
predicted: feature_015 (+0.176/unit), feature_001 (-1.17/level), feature_014
(-1.36), feature_027 (+0.94), feature_025 (-0.076/unit), feature_019
(+0.47/unit), feature_023 (-0.35), feature_006 (-0.29). feature_005 and
feature_022 also contributed. The remaining 25 features had |t| < 2 and were
not retained as main-effect prognostic factors.

3. Treatment hypothesis (iterations 5–6)
----------------------------------------
feature_027's positive direction, combined with its ~20 % prevalence and the
fact that all other significant binary features had a negative association
with pfs_months, motivated treating feature_027 as a candidate treatment
indicator. Its average effect (after adjustment) was +0.95 mo.

Stratified analyses showed feature_027's effect was nearly constant across
feature_001 levels (+0.93, +0.99, +0.90) and across feature_014 (+0.94 vs
+1.01), so the prognostic axes do not modify the treatment effect. None of
the other null-main-effect binaries (feature_010, feature_012, feature_018,
feature_020, feature_024, feature_031) showed treatment-like behavior in
stratified or interaction tests.

4. Heterogeneity screen (iteration 7)
-------------------------------------
A systematic feature_027 × every-other-feature interaction screen (OLS,
adjusting for all main effects) revealed five very strong modifiers:

  feature_027 × feature_023:  -1.63   (p < 1e-300)
  feature_027 × feature_006:  -1.46   (p < 1e-300)
  feature_027 × feature_005:  -0.026/unit (p < 1e-300)
  feature_027 × feature_022:  -0.99   (p = 4e-104)
  feature_027 × feature_011:  +0.89   (p = 2e-57; later shown to be confounded
                                       and not robust after the 4-predicate
                                       subgroup is defined)

The first four all attenuate the feature_027 benefit. This is the classic
signature of a treatment whose efficacy is restricted to a biomarker-defined
subgroup.

5. Subgroup definition refinement (iterations 8–11)
---------------------------------------------------
Joint stratification by the three binary modifiers showed:

  0 modifiers (f006=0 & f023=0 & f022=0; n=17,372): feature_027 effect = +2.76 mo
  1 modifier  (any of the three = 1; n=24,429):     feature_027 effect = +0.05 mo
  2 modifiers (n=8,199):                             feature_027 effect = -0.04 mo

Within the 0-modifiers subgroup, a residual continuous interaction with
feature_005 was identified. Stratifying feature_005 into fine bins inside
that subgroup gave a sharp threshold-like pattern:

  feature_005 in [0,1):  +4.98 mo
  feature_005 in [1,2):  +5.08 mo
  feature_005 in [2,3):  +5.01 mo
  feature_005 in [3,5):  +4.97 mo
  feature_005 in [5,10): +0.03 mo
  feature_005 in [10,20):+0.05 mo
  feature_005 in [20,50):-0.22 mo
  feature_005 in [50,inf):+0.19 mo

The drop between feature_005 ∈ [3,5) and [5,10) is consistent with a clinical
threshold at feature_005 = 5 — i.e. the treatment effect is a step function
of feature_005 with the transition right at this value.

6. Final responder subgroup (iterations 11–15)
----------------------------------------------
Combining the binary and continuous predicates yields the responder subgroup:

  feature_006 = 0   AND
  feature_023 = 0   AND
  feature_022 = 0   AND
  feature_005 < 5

This subgroup contains 9,326 patients (1,903 with feature_027 = 1, 7,423 with
feature_027 = 0). In it:

  Mean pfs_months: 4.09 (control) → 9.10 (treated), unadjusted diff = +5.01 mo,
  p < 1e-300.

Outside the subgroup (n = 40,674): mean pfs_months = 4.123 (control) vs 4.148
(treated), diff = +0.025 mo, p = 0.33 — no detectable benefit.

After multivariable adjustment for the prognostic features inside the
subgroup, the feature_027 coefficient is +2.49 mo (p < 1e-300). The unadjusted
+5 mo gap is partly explained by the favorable prognostic background of the
subgroup; the adjusted +2.5 mo confirms that direction and significance are
robust to controls.

Each predicate is independently necessary. Dropping feature_006 = 0,
feature_023 = 0, or feature_022 = 0 admits patients in whom the treatment
effect collapses to ~+0.05 mo. Dropping feature_005 < 5 admits patients in
whom the effect is also ~+0.03 mo.

7. Distinguishing prognostic from predictive features
-----------------------------------------------------
- feature_015: huge prognostic effect (+0.18/unit) but almost no
  treatment-effect modification (interaction +0.005/unit; <3 % of the main
  effect).
- feature_001 and feature_014: large prognostic effects, no meaningful
  modification of the feature_027 effect after the 4-predicate subgroup is
  defined.
- feature_019, feature_025, feature_021, feature_003, feature_030, etc.:
  prognostic only.

8. Conclusions
--------------
- pfs_months in this cohort is dominated by feature_015 (continuous, positive)
  and feature_001 / feature_014 (categorical, negative); together with the
  rest of the multivariable model these explain ~86 % of the variance.
- feature_027 is the unique candidate treatment indicator. Its main effect is
  modest (+0.95 mo), but this average masks strong heterogeneity.
- The complete responder subgroup is defined by FOUR predicates simultaneously:
  feature_006 = 0, feature_023 = 0, feature_022 = 0, AND feature_005 < 5.
  Inside this subgroup feature_027 prolongs pfs_months by approximately +5
  months (unadjusted) / +2.5 months (adjusted). Outside, the effect is
  indistinguishable from zero.
- The feature_005 cutoff at 5 is sharp enough to suggest a clinically
  established normal-range threshold (e.g. CEA < 5 ng/mL is the classical
  upper limit of normal in colorectal cancer). The three binary modifiers
  function like resistance markers: any one of them, when present, abolishes
  the treatment effect.
- No other binary in the dataset showed detectable treatment-like behavior in
  any sensible subgroup, so feature_027 appears to be the only treatment
  variable in this cohort with usable variation.
