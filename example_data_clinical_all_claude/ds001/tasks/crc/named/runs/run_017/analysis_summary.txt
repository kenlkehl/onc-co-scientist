Analysis summary — ds001_crc (n = 50,000 colorectal cancer patients)
====================================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (mean 4.31, SD 2.29, range 0.00–16.25)

Across 20 iterations I probed (a) main effects of demographics, disease
characteristics, mutations, and laboratory features on PFS; (b) main effects
of the six available treatments; (c) treatment-by-biomarker interactions; and
(d) progressively refined subgroup analyses to localize where each treatment
acts. The dataset is large enough (n=50,000) that even tiny effects become
"significant"; effect *size* and direction were therefore my primary criteria
for clinical relevance.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Patient-level main effects on PFS
------------------------------------------------------------

Strongly supported:

* age_years: PEARSON r = +0.766 with PFS (p ≈ 0). Older patients have markedly
  longer PFS in this cohort — a dominant signal that I confirmed in every
  multivariable model (adjusted slope ≈ +0.18 months per year of age).
* ecog_ps: r = -0.352, mean PFS 5.27 (ECOG 0) → 4.05 (ECOG 1) → 2.91 (ECOG 2).
  Worse performance status → much shorter PFS. Adjusted coef ≈ -1.17.
* stage_iv = 1: PFS 3.70 vs 5.05 in non-stage IV (Δ = -1.35 months, p ≈ 0).
  Adjusted ≈ -1.36.
* right_sided_primary = 1: PFS 4.11 vs 4.42 left-sided (Δ = -0.31, p < 1e-49).
* albumin_g_dl: r = +0.10 (p ≈ 0); higher albumin → longer PFS, with a large
  adjusted slope of +0.47 months per g/dL.
* weight_loss_pct_6mo: r = -0.12 (p ≈ 0); each percentage of pre-treatment
  weight loss costs ≈ 0.08 months of PFS in adjusted models.
* cea_ng_ml: small but highly significant negative association (adjusted
  -0.005 months/ng/mL, p ≈ 0).
* ldh_u_l: small negative association (adjusted -4e-4, p ≈ 0).
* sex_female: NO detectable effect on PFS (Δ = -0.006 months, p = 0.77).

Mutations (univariate):

* kras_mutation = 1: -0.33 months (p ≈ 1e-58).
* braf_v600e = 1: -0.23 months (p ≈ 3e-7).
* nras_mutation = 1: +0.22 months (p = 7e-4) — small positive, possibly
  reflecting the very small group (n=1,506) and overlap with otherwise
  favorable patients.
* msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion: NO main effect on PFS in this
  cohort (all p > 0.35).

Lab features other than albumin / weight loss / CEA / LDH (NLR, hemoglobin,
ALK-P, AST, ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, CRP):
no clinically meaningful association after adjustment; a few squeak past
p < 0.05 in univariate tests (calcium, sodium, etc.) but with |r| < 0.02 and
adjusted coefficients near zero.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Treatment main effects (univariate, before interaction)
------------------------------------------------------------

* treatment_regorafenib: +0.97 months (p < 1e-216). The ONLY treatment with a
  clinically large univariate main effect. Adjusted coef in the full
  multivariable model = +0.94 (p ≈ 0).
* treatment_cetuximab: -0.04 months (p = 0.09). Essentially null overall.
* treatment_bevacizumab: -0.02 months (p = 0.35). Null.
* treatment_pembrolizumab: +0.005 months (p = 0.86). Null.
* treatment_encorafenib: +0.005 months (p = 0.87). Null.
* treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib: -0.04 months (p = 0.24). Null.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Canonical biomarker subgroups (the obvious clinical hypotheses)
------------------------------------------------------------

Conventional CRC biology says cetuximab works in RAS/BRAF wild-type tumors,
pembrolizumab works in MSI-high, encorafenib works in BRAF V600E, and
trastuzumab/tucatinib works in HER2+. I tested each:

* cetuximab in kras=0, nras=0, braf=0 (n=25,324): coef = -0.045, p = 0.17.
  Even further restricting to LEFT-sided RAS/BRAF-WT (n=16,443) and to
  ECOG 0 within that group: coefs all negative-to-zero, all p > 0.13.
  → Refuted: cetuximab does NOT improve PFS in any expected subgroup.
* pembrolizumab in MSI-high (n=2,513): +0.007, p = 0.95. Refuted.
* encorafenib in BRAF V600E (n=2,272): -0.13, p = 0.36. Refuted.
* trastuzumab/tucatinib in HER2+ (n=1,504): +0.02, p = 0.94. Refuted.
* bevacizumab anywhere: null (only quirk was BRAF V600E subgroup, where it
  was slightly harmful, coef = -0.24, p = 0.007 — likely chance).

All canonical-target effects also remained null after covariate adjustment.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Treatment × biomarker interaction screen (the only winner: regorafenib)
------------------------------------------------------------

Across 6 treatments × 9 biomarkers (54 interaction tests), the only
clinically large, highly significant interactions involved regorafenib:

* treatment_regorafenib × kras_mutation: coef = -1.63, p ≈ 8e-226.
* treatment_regorafenib × braf_v600e:    coef = -1.18, p ≈ 6e-23.
* treatment_regorafenib × right_sided_primary: coef = -1.45, p ≈ 4e-168.
* treatment_regorafenib × nras_mutation:  coef = +0.99, p ≈ 1e-11
  (positive but tiny absolute group; possibly because NRAS+ and KRAS+
  partially exclude each other, leaving NRAS+ patients in the responsive arm).

Two minor signals (bevacizumab × BRAF, pembrolizumab × right) had |coef| < 0.25
and borderline p; not clinically meaningful.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Localizing the regorafenib-responsive subgroup
------------------------------------------------------------

Stratified analyses (single modifiers):

* KRAS WT (n=29,102): coef = +1.66 (p ≈ 0); KRAS mut (n=20,898): +0.027 (p=0.45).
* BRAF WT (n=47,728): coef = +1.03 (p ≈ 0); BRAF V600E (n=2,272): -0.16 (p=0.15).
* Left-sided (n=32,343): coef = +1.48 (p ≈ 0); right-sided (n=17,657): +0.026 (p=0.52).
* CEA tertiles: low coef = +1.78, mid +1.10, high +0.007 (p=0.87) → high CEA
  fully abolishes the benefit.

Combined modifiers:

* KRAS WT + BRAF WT (n=25,324): +1.81.
* KRAS WT + BRAF WT + LEFT (n=16,443): +2.75.
* KRAS WT + BRAF WT + LEFT + CEA-low half (n=8,202): +5.02.
* KRAS WT + BRAF WT + LEFT + CEA non-high tertile (n=11,610): +4.04
  (adjusted for age/sex/ECOG/stage IV/CEA/albumin/LDH/NLR/CRP/Hb: +3.99,
   p ≈ 0). This is the BEST regorafenib responsive subgroup.

Three-way model (regorafenib × KRAS × right-sided, adjusted for BRAF, age,
ECOG, stage IV, albumin, CEA): the rego coefficient when both KRAS=0 AND
right=0 is +2.50; both first-order interactions are ≈ -2.5 and the three-way
product is ≈ +2.5. Translation: switching ON either modifier (KRAS+ or
right-sided) drops the regorafenib effect to ~0; switching ON both keeps it
at ~0 (it doesn't go further negative). i.e., either unfavorable feature is
sufficient to abolish the benefit.

Joint multivariable model with regorafenib × {KRAS, BRAF, RIGHT, CEA-continuous}
all together: every interaction remains negative and highly significant,
confirming each independently attenuates the drug's effect.

Negative control: in the same favorable subgroup (kras=0, braf=0, left,
CEA non-high), cetuximab still has NO effect (coef = -0.10, p = 0.08), so
the regorafenib finding is not just selection bias toward better-prognosis
patients.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. Bottom line
------------------------------------------------------------

Of the six treatments in this cohort, only treatment_regorafenib has a real
treatment effect on pfs_months, and its benefit is strongly heterogeneous:

  Best-supported responsive-subgroup hypothesis (regorafenib → PFS):
    treatment_regorafenib increases pfs_months by ≈ +4.0 months (adjusted)
    in patients with kras_mutation = 0 AND braf_v600e = 0
    AND right_sided_primary = 0 AND cea_ng_ml below the upper tertile
    (~14.5 ng/mL). The benefit is abolished by ANY of: KRAS mutation,
    BRAF V600E, right-sided primary, or high CEA.

The conventional CRC targeted-therapy assignments (cetuximab in RAS-WT,
pembrolizumab in MSI-high, encorafenib in BRAF V600E, trastuzumab/tucatinib
in HER2+) all yielded null treatment effects in this dataset, even within
their expected biomarker-defined subgroups. Bevacizumab also had no
detectable subgroup benefit. Beyond treatment, the strongest predictors of
longer PFS were older age (large positive effect), good performance status,
non-stage IV disease, left-sided primary, KRAS/BRAF wild-type status, higher
albumin, and lower weight loss / CEA / LDH; sex, MSI status, HER2 status,
and NTRK fusion had no independent effect on PFS in the overall cohort.
