ANALYSIS SUMMARY: ds001_crc (50,000 colorectal-cancer patient records, single outcome pfs_months)
================================================================================================

OVERVIEW

I ran 17 propose-test-refine iterations on the cohort, working from univariate main-effect
screens through multivariable adjustment, on to systematic treatment x feature heterogeneity
search and joint subgroup definition. Below I summarize, by topic, the hypotheses I tested,
what the evidence showed (direction, magnitude, significance), and the final conclusions.


1. DEMOGRAPHIC AND CLINICAL MAIN EFFECTS ON pfs_months

  - age_years: STRONG POSITIVE association. Univariate slope +0.177 mo/yr (p<<0.001). Mean
    PFS rises monotonically from ~0.3 mo in patients aged 30-35 to ~8.6 mo at 85-90. The
    effect persists almost identically (+0.176 mo/yr) after adjustment for ECOG, stage,
    biomarkers, labs, and all six treatments. This is the opposite of the typical real-world
    pattern but it is the dominant prognostic signal in the dataset.
  - sex_female: NULL. Mean PFS 4.308 (F) vs 4.314 (M), p=0.77.
  - ecog_ps: STRONG NEGATIVE. Mean PFS 5.27 / 4.05 / 2.91 at ECOG 0/1/2; adjusted slope
    -1.17 mo per ECOG unit (p<<0.001).
  - stage_iv: STRONG NEGATIVE. Stage IV vs III: -1.35 mo unadjusted, -1.36 mo adjusted
    (p<<0.001).
  - right_sided_primary: NEGATIVE in main effect (-0.31 mo, p~1e-49). However, this main
    effect is entirely mediated by the regorafenib treatment-effect subgroup (see section 5
    below): among patients not given regorafenib, sidedness has no PFS association.


2. MUTATION / GENOMIC BIOMARKERS (UNIVARIATE)

  - kras_mutation: -0.33 mo (p~1e-58). Adjusted main effect collapses to ~0 once the
    regorafenib triple-favorable subgroup is modeled.
  - braf_v600e:  -0.23 mo (p=3e-7). Same mediation pattern as KRAS.
  - nras_mutation: +0.22 mo (p=7e-4) -- small, marginal.
  - msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion: all null (p>=0.36).


3. LAB / SERUM PREDICTORS (UNIVARIATE PEARSON r)

  Strong/meaningful: weight_loss_pct_6mo r=-0.123 (p~2e-167), albumin_g_dl r=+0.102
  (p~3e-115), cea_ng_ml r=-0.038 (p~3e-17). All retain large adjusted effects in the
  multivariable model: weight loss -0.077 mo per %, albumin +0.469 mo per g/dL, CEA
  -0.005 mo per ng/mL.

  Tiny but significant: ldh_u_l (-0.011), alkaline_phosphatase_u_l (-0.013),
  total_bilirubin_mg_dl (-0.009).
  Null: crp_mg_l, nlr, hemoglobin, AST, ALT, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium.


4. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS

  Five of the six treatments have NO cohort-wide PFS effect:
    - cetuximab        diff -0.038, p=0.088
    - bevacizumab      diff -0.019, p=0.354
    - pembrolizumab    diff +0.005, p=0.856
    - encorafenib      diff +0.005, p=0.872
    - trastuzumab/tuca diff -0.044, p=0.244

  Only treatment_regorafenib shows a clear main effect: +0.972 mo (p~1e-216), and this
  signal is entirely concentrated in one subgroup (see section 5).

  In the multivariable OLS (R^2 = 0.859), only treatment_regorafenib among the six
  treatments has a non-null adjusted effect (+0.94 mo, p<<0.001).


5. TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY: PRE-SPECIFIED CLINICAL HYPOTHESES (textbook patterns)

  I tested every standard real-world drug-biomarker pairing one by one. NONE held in this
  cohort:
    - Cetuximab x KRAS WT (expected positive): null (-0.05 mo, p=0.12) and equally null
      in KRAS mut. Also null in NRAS, BRAF, and sidedness strata.
    - Pembrolizumab x MSI-high (expected positive): null (+0.007 mo, p=0.96). Null in all
      eight 2x2 sub-strata of MSI-H by stage / sidedness / KRAS / BRAF.
    - Encorafenib x BRAF V600E (expected positive): null, slightly numerically negative
      (-0.13 mo, p=0.33). The encorafenib + cetuximab combination in BRAF V600E is also
      null (4.151 vs 4.138 mo).
    - Trastuzumab/tucatinib x HER2-amplified (expected positive): null (+0.017, p=0.93).
    - Bevacizumab x stage_iv x sidedness: null in all four 2x2 cells (|effect| <= 0.05).


6. TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY: SYSTEMATIC SCREEN AND THE REGORAFENIB SUBGROUP

  I screened each of the six treatments x each binary feature (sex, stage, sidedness, all
  six biomarkers) and each treatment x each continuous feature (in a multivariable OLS
  with an interaction term). Across the entire screen, ALL meaningful interaction signal
  was on a single drug -- treatment_regorafenib -- and was driven by three features:

    - regorafenib x kras_mutation: KRAS WT diff +1.66 vs KRAS mut diff +0.03 (qualitative).
    - regorafenib x braf_v600e:    BRAF WT diff +1.03 vs V600E diff -0.16   (qualitative).
    - regorafenib x sidedness:     left   diff +1.48 vs right  diff +0.03   (qualitative).

  Joint definition. The cleanest description of the heterogeneity is the conjunction of
  the three favorable predicates:

    JOINT SUBGROUP: kras_mutation == 0 AND braf_v600e == 0 AND right_sided_primary == 0
      (n=17,372, ~35% of the cohort)

      regorafenib effect: +2.758 mo (rego mean 6.875 vs no-rego 4.117), p<<1e-300

    COMPLEMENT (any unfavorable predicate, i.e. KRAS+ OR BRAF+ OR right-sided)
      (n=32,628, ~65% of the cohort)

      regorafenib effect: +0.030 mo, p=0.294 (null)

  The cohort-wide observed +0.97 mo effect equals the within-subgroup +2.76 mo times
  the subgroup prevalence (~0.35); the entire main effect arises from this one subgroup.

  Confirmation in multivariable OLS: I split the regorafenib indicator into rego_in_subgroup
  (rego x triple-favorable) and rego_outside (rego x complement). Adjusted betas are
  +2.736 (p<<0.001) and -0.003 (p=0.78) respectively -- exactly what the stratified
  analysis suggests. After this split, the previously significant adjusted main effects
  of kras_mutation (-0.354), braf_v600e (-0.357), and right_sided_primary (-0.287) all
  collapse to ~0 and become non-significant, indicating that the cohort-wide negative
  associations of these three predictors with PFS are entirely mediated by their
  identifying patients who do not benefit from regorafenib. Among non-regorafenib
  patients alone, KRAS, BRAF, and sidedness have NO PFS association (all |diff|<=0.012,
  p>=0.59).

  Stability of the subgroup definition: within the triple-favorable subgroup the
  regorafenib effect is approximately constant in absolute magnitude across ECOG
  (+2.67 / +2.77 / +2.74 at ECOG 0/1/2) and across NRAS status (+2.75 NRAS WT vs +2.91
  NRAS+). The interaction is genuinely with the kras/braf/right triplet, not with PS or
  the rarer biomarkers.

  Continuous effect modifiers: within the triple-favorable subgroup, higher cea_ng_ml
  attenuates the rego benefit (interaction beta -0.064 mo per ng/mL, p<<0.001), but the
  qualitative subgroup definition still holds. No interactions of regorafenib with age,
  ECOG, or other labs are clinically meaningful. No other treatment showed any continuous
  interaction worth pursuing.


7. WHAT WAS SUPPORTED VS REFUTED

  Supported:
    - Older age, lower ECOG, stage III (vs IV), higher albumin, lower weight loss, lower
      CEA, lower LDH all predict longer pfs_months (multivariable, all p<<0.001).
    - regorafenib improves pfs_months by ~+2.76 mo, but only in the (KRAS WT & BRAF WT &
      left-sided) subgroup, and is null elsewhere.

  Refuted (null in this cohort, despite real-world expectation):
    - cetuximab benefit in KRAS WT or in left-sided.
    - pembrolizumab benefit in MSI-high.
    - encorafenib (alone or with cetuximab) benefit in BRAF V600E.
    - trastuzumab/tucatinib benefit in HER2-amplified.
    - bevacizumab benefit in any stage / sidedness combination.
    - Sex effect on PFS.
    - MSI / HER2 / NTRK main effects on PFS.

  The associations of kras_mutation, braf_v600e, and right_sided_primary with shorter
  pfs_months that look real in unadjusted analysis are NOT independent prognostic effects;
  they are entirely mediated by these features identifying patients who fail to benefit
  from regorafenib.


8. FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS (for the single outcome pfs_months)

  Treatment: treatment_regorafenib
  Outcome: pfs_months
  Direction: longer pfs_months when treated (positive effect)
  Magnitude: ~+2.76 months (point estimate +2.758, multivariable-adjusted +2.736)
  Subgroup predicates (all required simultaneously):
        kras_mutation == 0
    AND braf_v600e == 0
    AND right_sided_primary == 0

  Outside this subgroup (any one or more of kras_mutation==1, braf_v600e==1, or
  right_sided_primary==1), the regorafenib effect on pfs_months is statistically and
  clinically zero (point estimate +0.030, p=0.294). No other treatment in the cohort
  has a credible pfs_months effect, overall or within any pre-specified biomarker
  subgroup, so this is the single subgroup-restricted treatment effect supported by the
  data.
