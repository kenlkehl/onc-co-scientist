ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_crc cohort (50,000 patients)
=====================================================

OUTCOME
- pfs_months: progression-free survival in months. Cohort mean 4.31 (sd 2.29; range 0–16.25).

PHASE 1 — PROGNOSTIC FEATURES (main effects)

After running univariate tests and a fully adjusted multivariable OLS model
(R^2 = 0.832), the strongest *prognostic* signals on PFS were as follows
(adjusted coefficients, all p < 1e-15):

  Stronger PFS:
    - Older age_years            +0.176 months/year
    - Higher albumin_g_dl        +0.464 months/(g/dL)

  Shorter PFS:
    - ECOG 1 vs 0                -1.20 months
    - ECOG 2 vs 0                -2.34 months
    - Stage IV (vs not)          -1.36 months
    - kras_mutation              -0.35 months
    - braf_v600e                 -0.35 months
    - right_sided_primary        -0.29 months
    - Higher cea_ng_ml           -0.005/(ng/mL)
    - Higher ldh_u_l             -0.0004/(U/L)
    - Higher weight_loss_pct_6mo -0.076/(%)

The age effect was positive (older = longer PFS), which is unusual relative
to typical real-world oncology cohorts but is robust and stable across
adjustments and strata in this dataset.

The following labs/features had NO meaningful adjusted association with
PFS: sex_female, msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion, hemoglobin_g_dl,
alkaline_phosphatase_u_l, ast_u_l, alt_u_l, total_bilirubin_mg_dl,
creatinine_mg_dl, bun_mg_dl, sodium_meq_l, potassium_meq_l, calcium_mg_dl,
crp_mg_l, nlr.

PHASE 2 — TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS

Among the six listed treatments, only treatment_regorafenib showed a
non-trivial main effect on PFS (unadjusted +0.97 months, p=2e-216; adjusted
+0.94 months, p~0). Cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, and
trastuzumab/tucatinib all had adjusted main-effect coefficients within
±0.03 months of zero (all p > 0.07).

PHASE 3 — CANONICAL BIOMARKER-TARGETED INTERACTIONS (refuted)

Hypotheses tested:
  - cetuximab × KRAS-WT (and × BRAF-WT, × left-sided)
  - pembrolizumab × MSI-high
  - encorafenib × BRAF V600E
  - trastuzumab/tucatinib × HER2 amplified

None of these canonical clinical relationships reproduced in this cohort.
Even when we restricted analyses to the canonical-indication subgroup
(e.g., pembrolizumab among MSI-high, encorafenib among BRAF V600E,
trastuzumab/tucatinib among HER2 amplified), within-subgroup PFS
differences were all within ±0.13 months and all p > 0.13.

PHASE 4 — REGORAFENIB HETEROGENEITY DISCOVERY (supported)

Systematic interaction screen of treatment_regorafenib against every
biomarker, sidedness, ECOG, age, and key lab — both at the cohort level
and stratified — recovered four highly significant effect modifiers:

  rego × kras_mutation           interaction = -1.63 (p ~ 0)
  rego × braf_v600e              interaction = -0.99 (p ~ 1e-104)
  rego × right_sided_primary     interaction = -1.46 (p ~ 0)
  rego × (cea_ng_ml > median)    interaction = -1.64 (p ~ 0)

All four modifiers act in the *negative* direction: each unfavorable
biomarker value subtracts roughly 1.5 months from the regorafenib effect.
When all four are unfavorable simultaneously, the effect is fully
suppressed.

Stratified estimates of the regorafenib effect on PFS (adjusted for
prognostic features):

  - KRAS WT only                   +1.63 months (p~0)
  - KRAS mutated                   -0.004 months (p=0.27, null)

  - KRAS WT & BRAF WT & left-sided      +2.76 months (n_treated=3461)
  - Anywhere outside that 3-way profile +0.03 months (p=0.29, null)

  - Above + cea_ng_ml < ~5 ng/mL        +5.03 months (n_treated=1782)
    [mean PFS rises from 4.08 to 9.12 months]
  - Outside the full 4-way profile      +0.10 months (clinically null)

Within the full profile, no further variable substantially modifies the
effect — small but statistically detectable interactions for age (+0.05/median
split) and ECOG-2 (-0.07) exist due to large n but neither materially
shifts the +5-month benefit.

A decision-tree T-learner provided independent confirmation: the top
quartile of estimated CATE for regorafenib is enriched for KRAS-WT
(top 8% mut vs bottom 53%), left-sided (top 14% right-sided vs 44%), and
low CEA (top median 2.20 vs bottom 5.99) — recovering the analytic
subgroup definition by an independent method.

CONCLUSIONS

(1) The cohort exhibits robust, expected prognostic relationships for
    ECOG, stage, weight loss, albumin, CEA, LDH, KRAS, BRAF, and primary
    sidedness, plus an unusual but consistent positive age effect.

(2) Among the six treatments listed, regorafenib is the only one with a
    detectable PFS benefit in this dataset. The benefit is large but
    confined to a specific biomarker-defined subgroup.

(3) The best-supported treatment-effect subgroup is:

      treatment_regorafenib improves pfs_months by ~5 months (from a
      baseline of ~4.1 to ~9.1 months) in patients who simultaneously
      satisfy ALL of:
        - kras_mutation == 0 (KRAS wild-type)
        - braf_v600e == 0  (BRAF wild-type)
        - right_sided_primary == 0  (left-sided primary)
        - cea_ng_ml < ~5 ng/mL  (low/normal CEA)

      Outside this subgroup, regorafenib has no clinically meaningful
      effect on PFS (mean difference < 0.1 months).

(4) Cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, and trastuzumab/
    tucatinib show no detectable PFS benefit in this cohort — including
    in their canonical biomarker indications. Either these agents are
    inactive in this cohort or their effects are below the resolution of
    PFS in this dataset.
