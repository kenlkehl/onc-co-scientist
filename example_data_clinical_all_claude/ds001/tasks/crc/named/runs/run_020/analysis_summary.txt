Analysis Summary — ds001_crc (50,000 patients, outcome: pfs_months)
====================================================================

Overview
--------
The cohort contains 50,000 metastatic-colorectal-cancer-style records with 33
features (demographics, ECOG performance status, stage, sidedness, six somatic
biomarkers, six investigational treatment flags, 14 laboratory analytes) and a
single continuous outcome, pfs_months (mean 4.31, SD 2.29, range 0–16.3).
Across 25 iterations, hypotheses about (a) feature main effects, (b) treatment
main effects, (c) treatment-by-feature interactions, and (d) joint multi-feature
subgroup definitions were proposed and tested with t-tests and OLS regressions
(linear and interaction terms). Significance was assessed at α = 0.05.

Iterations 1–3 — Main effects
-----------------------------
Of the six treatments tested as univariate main effects on pfs_months, only
treatment_regorafenib showed a significant association: mean pfs_months 5.09 on
regorafenib vs 4.12 off (Δ = +0.97 months, p ≈ 1.7e-216). The other five
treatments — cetuximab (Δ = -0.04, p = 0.09), bevacizumab (Δ = -0.02, p = 0.35),
pembrolizumab (Δ = +0.005, p = 0.86), encorafenib (Δ = +0.005, p = 0.87) and
trastuzumab+tucatinib (Δ = -0.04, p = 0.24) — produced no detectable PFS
difference at the cohort level.

Among clinical and biomarker features, several main effects on pfs_months were
significant and clinically directionally consistent: stage_iv (Δ = -1.35
months, p≈0), ecog_ps=2 vs not (Δ = -1.65, p≈0) and ecog_ps=0 vs not (Δ =
+1.48, p≈0), kras_mutation (Δ = -0.33, p ≈ 1.9e-58), braf_v600e (Δ = -0.23, p ≈
3.5e-7), right_sided_primary (Δ = -0.31, p ≈ 1.0e-49), higher cea_ng_ml (β =
-0.005/ng·ml, p ≈ 3.1e-17), higher weight_loss_pct_6mo (β = -0.073, p ≈
2.2e-167), higher ldh_u_l (β = -0.0003, p = 0.013) and lower albumin_g_dl (β =
+0.46/g·dl, p ≈ 3.2e-115). Surprisingly, age_years was positively associated
with pfs_months (β = +0.18 months/year, p≈0); this counter-clinical direction
holds in the multivariable model as well, suggesting an artifact of dataset
construction rather than a real biological effect. nras_mutation was
associated with slightly longer pfs_months (Δ = +0.22, p = 0.0007). msi_high,
her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion, sex_female and most chemistry analytes (Hb, ALT,
AST, ALP, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, CRP, NLR) showed no
significant univariable effect.

In a multivariable OLS, the independent predictors of shorter pfs_months
remained ecog_ps, stage_iv, right_sided_primary, cea_ng_ml, ldh_u_l, and
weight_loss_pct_6mo; albumin_g_dl and age_years remained independently
positive. CRP, NLR and hemoglobin were not independently informative once the
above were included.

Iterations 4–7 — Targeted treatment-biomarker hypotheses
--------------------------------------------------------
The four "textbook" CRC biomarker-guided hypotheses were tested directly:
  - pembrolizumab × msi_high: no main effect of pembrolizumab in MSI-H
    (Δ = +0.007, p = 0.96, n=396 vs 2117), no interaction (β = +0.03, p = 0.60).
  - cetuximab × RAS/RAF wild-type (kras=0 ∧ nras=0 ∧ braf=0): no benefit in
    wild-type (Δ = -0.05, p = 0.17, n=7691 vs 17633), and no interaction with
    RAS/RAF status. Stratifying further by left-sided primary did not rescue
    a signal.
  - encorafenib × braf_v600e: no benefit in BRAF-mut patients (Δ = -0.13,
    p = 0.33), no interaction (β = +0.003, p = 0.97).
  - trastuzumab+tucatinib × her2_amplified: no benefit in HER2+ patients
    (Δ = +0.02, p = 0.93), no interaction; stratification by KRAS status within
    HER2+ did not produce a signal either.

So the four treatment-biomarker pairings that would be canonical in real-world
CRC do not behave that way in this dataset.

Iterations 8 — Bevacizumab
--------------------------
After adjustment for age, ecog, stage, sidedness, albumin, ldh and cea, the
adjusted treatment_bevacizumab effect was essentially zero (β = +0.006, p =
0.50). A scan of bevacizumab × {kras, side, msi, stage, ecog, braf} interactions
yielded only a borderline positive interaction with msi_high (β = +0.094, p =
0.029); this does not survive multiple testing and there is no main effect to
modify, so it is most parsimoniously interpreted as noise.

Iterations 9–16 — Regorafenib heterogeneity discovery
-----------------------------------------------------
The adjusted regorafenib main effect was +0.94 months (p≈0). Treatment-by-
feature interaction screening revealed strong heterogeneity:
  - rego × kras_mutation: β = -1.62, p≈0 (KRAS-mutant patients lose the benefit)
  - rego × braf_v600e:    β = -0.97, p ≈ 7.5e-80 (BRAF V600E suppresses benefit)
  - rego × right_sided_primary: β = -1.43, p≈0 (right-sided suppresses benefit)
  - rego × ecog_ps:       β = -0.061, p = 0.002 (modestly attenuated as ECOG↑)
  - rego × her2_amplified:β = -0.13, p = 0.03 (small, marginal)
  - rego × nras_mutation: β = +0.91, p ≈ 3.2e-48 (positive, but see below)
  - rego × msi_high, × ntrk_fusion, × stage_iv, × sex_female: NS

Stratified analyses confirmed the picture:
  - In KRAS-wt patients, regorafenib Δ = +1.66 months (p≈0); in KRAS-mut, Δ =
    +0.03 (p = 0.46).
  - In BRAF-wt, Δ = +1.03 (p≈0); in BRAF-mut, Δ = -0.16 (p = 0.13).
  - In left-sided, Δ = +1.48 (p≈0); in right-sided, Δ = +0.03 (p = 0.52).

The continuous-feature interaction screen (iter 15) found several statistically
significant rego-by-lab interactions (cea, albumin, ldh, weight_loss, crp, nlr,
bun, sodium), but their magnitudes were small relative to the binary biomarker
modifiers and were judged secondary.

A joint regression including rego × kras_mutation, rego × braf_v600e and
rego × right_sided_primary (with adjustment for age, ecog, stage, albumin, cea)
produced a baseline regorafenib effect of +2.27 months in patients without any
of the three unfavorable predicates, and three significantly negative
interaction terms (β ≈ -1.78, -1.76, -1.45) — each unfavorable predicate
roughly cancels the baseline benefit on its own.

Iterations 17–21 — Confirming nulls for the other treatments
------------------------------------------------------------
Comprehensive interaction scans of cetuximab, encorafenib, trastuzumab+tucatinib,
bevacizumab and pembrolizumab against all 6 biomarkers, sidedness, stage_iv,
ecog_ps and (for some) sex_female/treatment-by-treatment combinations turned up
no convincing positive interactions. The only nominally significant one was
encorafenib × msi_high (β = -0.18, p = 0.014, negative) which goes the wrong
direction for any clinical hypothesis and most likely reflects multiple-testing
noise. Across roughly 50 interaction tests for these five treatments, the
significance rate is consistent with the null.

Iterations 22–23 — Combining the regorafenib modifiers
------------------------------------------------------
Splitting the cohort into the 2×2×2 grid of kras_mutation × braf_v600e ×
right_sided_primary (note: kras_mutation=1 ∧ braf_v600e=1 cells contain 0
patients, so KRAS and BRAF mutation appear mutually exclusive in this dataset)
revealed that the regorafenib pfs_months benefit is concentrated essentially
entirely in the (kras=0, braf=0, side=left) cell:
  - K=0,B=0,S=left: rego on Δ = +2.76 months (n=3461 on, 13911 off, p≈0)
  - K=0,B=0,S=right: Δ = +0.08 (NS)
  - K=0,B=1,S=left: Δ = -0.20 (NS)
  - K=0,B=1,S=right: Δ = -0.07 (NS)
  - K=1,B=0,S=left: Δ = +0.06 (NS)
  - K=1,B=0,S=right: Δ = -0.04 (NS)

Outside this single cell, the pooled rego effect is Δ = +0.03 months (NS).
Removing any one of the three predicates (KRAS-wt, BRAF-wt, left-sided) from
the subgroup definition reduces the estimated rego effect (drop-KRAS Δ = +1.56,
drop-BRAF Δ = +2.52, drop-side Δ = +1.81), confirming that all three predicates
contribute and that the responsive subgroup is the joint intersection.

Iteration 24 — NRAS contribution
-------------------------------
The positive rego × nras_mutation interaction observed in iteration 9 reflects
nras_mutation patients being almost entirely a subset of kras_mutation=0 (the
two RAS gene mutations are largely mutually exclusive in this cohort), so
nras_mutation status is collinear with KRAS-wt status. Within the responder
cell (K=0,B=0,left) the NRAS-mut patients show Δ = +2.91 months on rego, very
close to the cell-wide Δ = +2.76 — adding NRAS-mut as a fourth required predicate
does not buy meaningful additional separation. NRAS status is therefore not
included in the final subgroup definition.

Iteration 25 — Final subgroup confirmation
------------------------------------------
Within the regorafenib responder cell (kras_mutation=0 AND braf_v600e=0 AND
right_sided_primary=0), none of treatment_cetuximab, treatment_bevacizumab,
treatment_pembrolizumab, treatment_encorafenib or treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib
produced any significant pfs_months change (all |Δ| ≤ 0.08 months, all p ≥ 0.10).
The subgroup-specific PFS benefit is unique to regorafenib.

A final regression with rego × {kras_mutation, braf_v600e, right_sided_primary}
plus baseline adjustments confirmed the baseline (KRAS-wt + BRAF-wt + left-sided)
regorafenib effect on pfs_months at +2.27 months (p≈0).

Hypotheses supported (Final answer)
-----------------------------------
* treatment_regorafenib increases pfs_months relative to no regorafenib in the
  subgroup defined by kras_mutation = 0 AND braf_v600e = 0 AND right_sided_primary
  = 0; the magnitude is ≈ +2.7 months unadjusted (≈ +2.3 months in a model
  including the three interactions and adjustment for age, ecog and stage).
* The unfavorable value of any of the three predicates (kras_mutation = 1,
  braf_v600e = 1, or right_sided_primary = 1) suppresses the regorafenib
  benefit toward zero; combinations of unfavorable values do not restore it.
* Worse baseline pfs_months is independently associated with higher ecog_ps,
  stage_iv, right_sided_primary, kras_mutation, braf_v600e, higher cea_ng_ml,
  higher weight_loss_pct_6mo, higher ldh_u_l, and lower albumin_g_dl.

Hypotheses refuted
------------------
* treatment_pembrolizumab does NOT preferentially benefit msi_high patients
  in this dataset.
* treatment_cetuximab does NOT preferentially benefit RAS/RAF wild-type or
  RAS/RAF wild-type + left-sided patients.
* treatment_encorafenib does NOT preferentially benefit braf_v600e patients.
* treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib does NOT preferentially benefit her2_amplified
  (or her2_amplified + KRAS-wild-type) patients.
* treatment_bevacizumab has no detectable effect on pfs_months overall and
  no robust subgroup-specific effect.

Overall conclusion
------------------
Of the six treatments studied, only treatment_regorafenib is associated with a
PFS benefit, and that benefit is sharply confined to the joint subgroup
kras_mutation = 0 ∧ braf_v600e = 0 ∧ right_sided_primary = 0, where it is large
(~+2.7 months unadjusted). The five other treatments — including the four
biomarker-targeted regimens (pembrolizumab in MSI-high, cetuximab in RAS/RAF
wild-type, encorafenib in BRAF V600E, trastuzumab+tucatinib in HER2-amplified)
— are statistically inert with respect to pfs_months in this cohort, both
overall and within their nominally indicated biomarker-defined subgroups.
