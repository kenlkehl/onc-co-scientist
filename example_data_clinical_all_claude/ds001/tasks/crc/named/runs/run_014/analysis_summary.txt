ds001_crc — Analysis summary
=============================

Cohort
------
50,000 patients with metastatic colorectal cancer (CRC), each with demographics,
performance status, stage, primary tumor side, six molecular biomarkers
(KRAS, NRAS, BRAF V600E, MSI-high, HER2 amplification, NTRK fusion), 17
laboratory variables, six treatment indicators, and a single outcome:
pfs_months (progression-free survival; mean 4.31, median 4.21, range 0–16.3).
No missing values. Dataset is observational (treatment assignment is not
random) so all "treatment effect" estimates below are unadjusted comparisons
unless explicitly noted.

Iteration 1 — clinical-feature main effects on PFS
--------------------------------------------------
Linear regressions of pfs_months on individual clinical features produced
strongly negative associations for ECOG performance status (β = −1.19 mo per
unit, p ≈ 0), stage IV disease (β = −1.35 mo, p ≈ 0), 6-month percent weight
loss (β = −0.073 per %, p < 1e-167), CEA (β = −0.0053 per ng/mL, p < 1e-16),
right-sided primary (β = −0.31, p < 1e-45), and LDH (β = −3.2e-4, p ≈ 0.013),
plus a positive association for albumin (β = +0.47 per g/dL, p < 1e-115).
NLR, hemoglobin, sex, and CRP did not show significant unadjusted main
effects. Age was associated with longer PFS (β = +0.18 per year, p ≈ 0) — an
unusual sign that survives multivariable adjustment, suggesting cohort
selection (e.g., younger patients in this cohort have more aggressive
disease) rather than a true protective effect of age.

Iteration 2 — biomarker main effects
------------------------------------
KRAS mutation: β = −0.33 mo (p < 1e-55) — supported.
BRAF V600E: β = −0.23 mo (p < 1e-5) — supported.
NRAS mutation: β = +0.22 mo (p ≈ 2e-4) — small but in the OPPOSITE direction
than expected. MSI-high, HER2-amplified, and NTRK-fusion all had no detectable
main effect on PFS (p > 0.3).

Iteration 3 — treatment main effects (unadjusted)
-------------------------------------------------
Of six treatments, only treatment_regorafenib showed a meaningful main
effect: +0.97 mo (p ≈ 0; mean PFS 5.09 on regorafenib vs 4.12 off, n = 10,022
vs 39,978). Cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, and
trastuzumab/tucatinib all had effect estimates within ±0.05 mo and p > 0.08.

Iteration 4 — ECOG ordinal
--------------------------
ECOG 0 → 5.27 mo, ECOG 1 → 4.05 mo, ECOG 2 → 2.91 mo. ANOVA F ≈ 3,529, p ≈ 0.
Confirms a clean monotonic gradient with very large effect sizes.

Iterations 5–8 — testing canonical biomarker-driven treatment interactions
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
Hypotheses based on real-world CRC oncology were tested, all NEGATIVE in this
dataset:

* Pembrolizumab × MSI-high: interaction coef +0.0020, p = 0.99. MSI-high
  patients on pembro have essentially the same PFS as MSI-high patients off
  pembro (4.30 vs 4.29 mo). The expected signal of immune-checkpoint benefit
  in MSI-high mCRC is NOT present in this cohort.

* Encorafenib × BRAF V600E: interaction coef −0.14, p = 0.38. BRAF-V600E
  patients on encorafenib have 3.98 mo vs 4.11 off (point estimate the wrong
  direction). Even adding cetuximab (encorafenib + cetuximab combo) did not
  rescue an effect. Hypothesis NOT supported.

* Trastuzumab/tucatinib × HER2-amplified: interaction coef +0.063, p = 0.78.
  In HER2-amplified RAS-WT patients (n_t = 74), T+T effect = +0.05 mo, NS.
  Hypothesis NOT supported.

* Cetuximab × KRAS: interaction coef +0.020, p = 0.66. Cetuximab effect in
  KRAS-WT was −0.05 mo (NS). Hypothesis NOT supported.

Iteration 9 — cetuximab in narrowed clinical eligibility (triple WT + left)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cetuximab effect in (KRAS-WT & NRAS-WT & BRAF-WT & left-sided) = −0.067 mo
(p = 0.12, n_t = 5,067). Narrowing further to MSI-low and HER2-negative
(iterations 17, 24) did not produce a positive cetuximab signal — point
estimates remained slightly negative.

Iteration 10 — bevacizumab subgroup screen
------------------------------------------
No subgroup (right- vs left-sided, MSI status, KRAS status, stage IV, ECOG
strata, CRP/LDH strata) yielded a positive bevacizumab effect; all effect
estimates were within ±0.07 mo and either NS or marginally negative.

Iteration 11 — first regorafenib heterogeneity screen
-----------------------------------------------------
Regorafenib effect was strikingly different in the LOW-CEA half of the
cohort (+1.81 mo, p < 1e-280) vs the HIGH-CEA half (+0.13 mo, p ≈ 8e-5),
while ECOG strata and albumin strata showed similar effect sizes (~0.9 mo).
This pointed CEA as the dominant continuous heterogeneity driver and motivated
iterations 12–25.

Iteration 12 — regorafenib × CEA continuous interaction
-------------------------------------------------------
smf.ols('pfs_months ~ treatment_regorafenib * cea_ng_ml'): interaction
coefficient = −0.025 mo per ng/mL CEA, p = 8.7e-58. Across CEA tertiles, the
regorafenib effect was +1.78 mo (low), +1.10 mo (mid), and 0.007 mo (high,
p = 0.87). Strongly supports CEA as a continuous modifier.

Iteration 13 — regorafenib × biomarker screen
---------------------------------------------
* KRAS mutation: regorafenib in KRAS-WT = +1.66 mo (p ≈ 6e-293); in KRAS-mut
  = +0.027 mo (p = 0.46). Interaction coef −1.63, p = 7.7e-226.
* BRAF V600E: in BRAF-WT = +1.03 mo (p ≈ 5e-226); in BRAF-V600E = −0.16 mo
  (p = 0.13). Interaction coef −1.18, p = 6.1e-23.
* Right-sided primary: in left-sided = +1.48 mo (p ≈ 4e-274); in right-sided
  = +0.026 mo (p = 0.52). Interaction coef −1.45, p = 4.1e-168.
* MSI-high, HER2-amplified, NTRK-fusion: no significant interactions.
* NRAS mutation: interaction coef +0.99, p = 1e-11 (regorafenib effect ~1.93
  in NRAS-mutated vs 0.94 in NRAS-WT, but n_pos is small at 1,506; confounded
  with the broader RAS/BRAF/side pattern).

Iteration 14 — regorafenib × continuous feature screen
------------------------------------------------------
Of 17 continuous covariates (age, ECOG, albumin, LDH, weight loss, CRP, NLR,
hemoglobin, alkaline phosphatase, AST, ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN,
sodium, potassium, calcium), NONE produced a significant regorafenib ×
covariate interaction (all p > 0.10). CEA is the only continuous modifier.

Iteration 15 — multivariable adjustment
---------------------------------------
A single OLS model regressing pfs_months on all features and all six
treatments simultaneously confirmed (R² = 0.86) the adjusted associations:
ecog_ps (β = −1.17), stage_iv (β = −1.36), age_years (β = +0.18, again
positive), kras_mutation (β = −0.35), albumin (+0.47), weight_loss (−0.076),
right_sided_primary (−0.29), cea_ng_ml (−0.005), braf_v600e (−0.36), ldh
(−4.2e-4). Among treatments, ONLY regorafenib retained a significant
adjusted main effect: β = +0.94 mo, p ≈ 0. All five other treatments had
adjusted coefficients within ±0.03 mo (p ≥ 0.07).

Iterations 16, 22, 23 — refined regorafenib subgroups
-----------------------------------------------------
Within low-CEA, the modifiers that further enlarge regorafenib benefit are
KRAS-WT and left-sided primary; whereas within low-CEA the modifiers that
DO NOT further refine the signal are albumin level, ECOG, hemoglobin, NLR,
weight loss, and stage IV (rego effect ~1.7–1.8 mo across all of these).
Specifically:
* (cea_low & kras_wt) effect = +3.07 mo (n_t = 2,949, p ≈ 0)
* (cea_low & left_sided) effect = +2.74 mo (n_t = 3,291, p ≈ 0)
* (cea_low & kras_mut) effect = +0.017 mo (NS)
* (cea_low & right_sided) effect = +0.029 mo (NS)
* (cea_low & braf_wt) effect = +1.90 mo (p ≈ 0)
A composite "unfavorable" score (high CEA + low albumin + ECOG=2 + high
weight loss + high LDH) had a clear linear interaction with regorafenib
(β = −0.38 per added unfavorable feature, p = 2.3e-55), but most of this
gradient is driven by CEA alone.

Iteration 17 / 24 — cetuximab subgroup verification
---------------------------------------------------
Even in the most narrowly defined cetuximab-eligible subgroup (KRAS-WT,
NRAS-WT, BRAF-WT, left-sided primary, MSI-low, HER2-negative, ECOG 0/1) the
cetuximab effect estimate was −0.11 mo (n_t = 3,991, p = 0.02). The point
estimate is slightly negative and certainly not positive. Confirms no
cetuximab benefit emerges in this dataset.

Iterations 18–21 — pembrolizumab, encorafenib, T+T, bevacizumab final
---------------------------------------------------------------------
After multivariable adjustment, no subgroup-defining interaction surfaced any
positive effect for the four agents above. The only interaction term with
p < 0.05 in these screens (pembro × right_sided = −0.12, p = 0.05) implied
LESS pembro benefit in right-sided patients — opposite the canonical pattern
and very small in absolute magnitude. No biomarker rescued any of these
agents.

Iteration 25 — final, best-supported subgroup hypothesis
--------------------------------------------------------
The single refined treatment-effect hypothesis with strong support:

  In patients with right_sided_primary = 0 (left-sided), kras_mutation = 0,
  braf_v600e = 0, AND cea_ng_ml ≤ cohort median, treatment_regorafenib
  produces longer pfs_months than no regorafenib.

Supporting numbers:
  Subgroup, on regorafenib (n = 1,784): mean PFS 9.12 mo
  Subgroup, no regorafenib (n = 6,872): mean PFS 4.08 mo
  Effect: +5.03 mo, p < 1e-300
  Outside the subgroup: regorafenib effect = +0.093 mo (p = 3.2e-4)
  Interaction term (subgroup × treatment): +4.94 mo, p ≈ 0
  After adjustment for age, ECOG, stage, albumin, weight loss, LDH:
    interaction = +4.91 mo, p ≈ 0 (essentially unchanged)

The "unfavorable" values that suppress the regorafenib effect are:
  - right-sided primary tumor (right_sided_primary = 1)
  - KRAS mutation (kras_mutation = 1)
  - BRAF V600E mutation (braf_v600e = 1)
  - high CEA (cea_ng_ml > median, ~5 ng/mL in this cohort)

Each of these alone, in combination with the other three favorable values,
collapses the regorafenib effect to ~0:
  - left & kras_wt & braf_wt & CEA-HIGH: effect +0.34 mo (n_t=1,677)
  - RIGHT & kras_wt & braf_wt & cea_low: effect +0.11 mo (NS)
  - left & KRAS-MUT & braf_wt & cea_low: effect +0.056 mo (NS)
  - left & kras_wt & BRAF-V600E & cea_low: effect −0.24 mo (NS)
This rules out a simpler 1- or 2-variable subgroup definition.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
1. Clinical features predict PFS in the expected directions: ECOG
   performance status, stage IV, weight loss, low albumin, high CEA, high
   LDH, right-sided primary, KRAS mutation, and BRAF V600E mutation are all
   negative prognostic signals (with the puzzling exception that older age
   appears positively associated with PFS — a likely cohort/confounding
   artifact).

2. Among the six treatments, only regorafenib carries a meaningful
   PFS effect; cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, and
   trastuzumab/tucatinib show no detectable benefit overall or in any
   biomarker-defined subgroup. The canonical biomarker-driven benefits
   (cetuximab in RAS/BRAF WT + left-sided, pembrolizumab in MSI-high,
   encorafenib in BRAF V600E, T+T in HER2-amplified) DO NOT replicate
   in this cohort.

3. The regorafenib benefit is highly heterogeneous and concentrated in a
   four-variable subgroup: left-sided primary AND KRAS wild-type AND BRAF
   wild-type AND low CEA. In that subgroup the unadjusted effect is roughly
   +5 months of PFS (median PFS more than doubles); outside it, the effect
   collapses to essentially zero. Each of the four "unfavorable" alleles
   (right-sided, KRAS mutation, BRAF V600E, high CEA) independently
   suppresses the regorafenib effect to near-null, and the interaction
   between this composite subgroup and treatment is highly significant
   even after multivariable adjustment.
