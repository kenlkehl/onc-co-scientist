ds001_crc — Analysis Summary
=============================
Dataset: 50,000 colorectal-cancer patients, 33 features + pfs_months outcome.
Model: claude-opus-4-7. Iterations: 25.

Overview
--------
The investigation proceeded in three phases:
  1. Univariate prognostic main effects (iterations 1–5).
  2. Treatment main effects and biomarker-matched subgroup analyses (iterations 6–10).
  3. Systematic treatment-effect-heterogeneity searches and refinements (iterations 11–25).
The single most consequential finding was that the regorafenib treatment effect on PFS is entirely
concentrated in a four-predicate subgroup (KRAS-WT, BRAF V600E-WT, left-sided primary, CEA < 5 ng/mL),
where regorafenib roughly doubles median PFS (+5.0 months); outside that subgroup it is null.

1. Prognostic main effects (Iterations 1–5)
-------------------------------------------
Hypotheses about classical prognostic factors generally held:
  - ecog_ps:   slope = -1.190 mo/level, p < 1e-300 (SUPPORTED)
  - stage_iv:  slope = -1.352 mo,        p < 1e-300 (SUPPORTED)
  - right_sided_primary: -0.305 mo,      p = 5.2e-46 (SUPPORTED)
  - kras_mutation:       -0.327 mo,      p = 8.5e-56 (SUPPORTED)
  - braf_v600e:          -0.228 mo,      p = 3.7e-6  (SUPPORTED)
  - albumin_g_dl:        +0.465 mo/(g/dL), p = 3.3e-115 (SUPPORTED)
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.073 mo/%,    p = 2.2e-167 (SUPPORTED)
  - cea_ng_ml:           -0.0053 mo/(ng/mL), p = 3.1e-17 (SUPPORTED)
  - ldh_u_l:             -0.00032 mo/(U/L), p = 0.013 (weak SUPPORT)

Hypotheses that were REFUTED or directionally surprising:
  - sex_female:          slope = -0.006, p = 0.77 (no detectable effect)
  - msi_high:            slope = -0.019, p = 0.68 (no detectable effect)
  - her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion, crp_mg_l, nlr: all non-significant
  - nras_mutation:       slope = +0.221, p = 2.3e-4 — direction OPPOSITE expectation
  - age_years:           slope = +0.177 mo/yr, p < 1e-300 — direction OPPOSITE the typical
    oncology prior. Older patients have LONGER PFS in this cohort, and this persists after full
    multivariable adjustment for performance status, stage, biomarkers, labs, and treatments
    (iteration 20: adjusted age beta = +0.176, p < 1e-300). This is an unusual finding and may
    reflect cohort selection (e.g., fitter older patients eligible for treatment).

2. Treatment main effects (Iteration 6)
---------------------------------------
Of six treatments, only one showed a detectable marginal benefit:
  - treatment_regorafenib: +0.972 mo (p = 1.7e-216) — large effect.
  - cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, trastuzumab+tucatinib: all
    diff in [-0.05, +0.01] months and p > 0.08. No detectable marginal benefit for any of
    these five agents in the marginal pool.

3. Biomarker-matched subgroup tests (Iterations 7–10)
-----------------------------------------------------
We tested each non-regorafenib treatment in its canonical biomarker-matched subpopulation:
  - pembrolizumab × MSI-H (n=2513):                  diff +0.007, p=0.96 — REFUTED
  - encorafenib × BRAF V600E (n=2272):               diff -0.129, p=0.33 — REFUTED
  - encorafenib+cetuximab combo × BRAF V600E (n=58): diff +0.058, p=0.84 — REFUTED
  - trastuzumab+tucatinib × HER2-amplified (n=1504): diff +0.017, p=0.93 — REFUTED
  - cetuximab × RAS/BRAF-WT left-sided (n=16,443):   diff -0.067, p=0.13 — REFUTED
None of the canonical biomarker pairings produced a benefit in this dataset.

4. Heterogeneity search for regorafenib (Iterations 11–16)
----------------------------------------------------------
A systematic interaction screen for regorafenib was the breakthrough:
  - rego × kras_mutation:        interaction = -1.629, p = 7.7e-226
  - rego × braf_v600e:           interaction = -1.183, p = 6.1e-23
  - rego × right_sided_primary:  interaction = -1.454, p = 4.1e-168
  - rego × cea_ng_ml:            interaction = -0.025/ng/mL, p = 8.7e-58 (full sample)
                                 -0.065/ng/mL, p = 7.4e-136 (within k0/b0/r0)

Each modifier individually abolishes the regorafenib benefit. Stratified by the joint subgroup
(KRAS-WT AND BRAF-WT AND left-sided), regorafenib raised PFS by +2.76 months (n=17,372,
p<1e-300) inside vs. +0.03 months (n=32,628, p=0.29) outside.

A threshold scan within the joint subgroup pinpointed CEA < 5 ng/mL as the natural cut: below 5,
regorafenib effect = +5.01 months (n=9326); above 5, +0.005 months (n=8046, p=0.93).
NRAS status did NOT modify the regorafenib effect within k0/b0/r0 (NRAS-WT diff +2.75; NRAS-mut
diff +2.91; p_diff = 0.83), so NRAS is not part of the subgroup definition.

5. Heterogeneity search for the other five treatments (Iterations 17–18, 24)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------
We screened each non-regorafenib treatment:
  - 45 T × continuous-feature interaction tests: NONE reached p<0.01.
  - 40 T × binary-feature interaction tests: strongest were bevacizumab×braf_v600e
    (beta=-0.228, p=0.022) and pembrolizumab×right_sided_primary (beta=-0.116, p=0.050);
    nothing survives Bonferroni correction.
  - T-learner CATE std (gradient-boosted regression): all five non-regorafenib treatments
    had CATE std ≤ 0.25 months — well below the 0.5-month threshold for "meaningful"
    heterogeneity. By contrast regorafenib CATE std = 1.88 months (an order of magnitude
    larger), and the dominant tree splits on regorafenib CATE were exactly
    right_sided_primary, kras_mutation, cea_ng_ml, and braf_v600e — confirming the
    interaction-test result.
  - Fine-grained pembrolizumab probes within MSI-H by side and stage IV: all null.

6. Other multivariable findings (Iterations 19–22)
--------------------------------------------------
  - right_sided × kras_mutation interaction = +0.452, p=1.7e-25: the two negative effects are
    sub-additive, i.e., KRAS adds little additional harm in already-right-sided tumors.
  - albumin × stage_iv, weight_loss × ECOG: no detectable interactions.
  - stage_iv × ecog_ps: borderline-significant SUB-additive interaction (beta=+0.063, p=0.019).
    Best-prognosis cell (stage=0, ecog=0): mean PFS = 6.04 mo. Worst-prognosis cell
    (stage=1, ecog≥1): mean PFS = 3.19 mo (~2.85-month gap).
  - All 15 pairwise treatment-treatment interaction tests: all p ≥ 0.05. No detectable
    synergy or antagonism between treatments.
  - Full multivariable OLS (n=50,000, R^2 = 0.86):
        regorafenib +0.943 (p<1e-300)        — large
        all other treatments |beta| ≤ 0.026, p ≥ 0.07 — null
        ecog_ps -1.171, stage_iv -1.362, albumin +0.469, braf -0.357, kras -0.354,
        right-sided -0.287, age +0.176, weight_loss -0.076, cea -0.005 — all p < 1e-50.

7. Final synthesis
==================
TREATMENT-EFFECT-HETEROGENEITY CONCLUSIONS

Regorafenib (the only treatment with a detectable effect in this cohort):
  Final subgroup hypothesis (h25.1): regorafenib increases pfs_months by approximately
  +5 months EXCLUSIVELY in patients who simultaneously satisfy
        kras_mutation = 0  AND
        braf_v600e   = 0  AND
        right_sided_primary = 0  AND
        cea_ng_ml < 5.
  Inside this 4-predicate subgroup (n=9,326): mean PFS regorafenib = 9.10 vs off = 4.09,
  diff = +5.01 months (p<1e-300). Outside (n=40,674): diff = +0.025 months (p=0.33).
  This subgroup definition was supported by both interaction tests and a tree-based
  CATE search, and was robust to NRAS status.

Other treatments (cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, trastuzumab+tucatinib):
  No detectable beneficial effect on PFS in any subgroup defined by demographic, clinical,
  biomarker, or laboratory features — including the canonical biomarker-matched
  subpopulations (MSI-H for pembrolizumab; BRAF V600E for encorafenib alone or with
  cetuximab; HER2-amplified for trastuzumab+tucatinib; RAS/BRAF-WT left-sided for
  cetuximab). These five treatments behave as inert with respect to PFS in this dataset.

PROGNOSTIC CONCLUSIONS

Strongest features (sorted by |adjusted effect|): stage_iv (-1.36), ecog_ps (-1.17/level),
albumin_g_dl (+0.47/g/dL), braf_v600e (-0.36), kras_mutation (-0.35), right_sided_primary
(-0.29), age_years (+0.18/yr — opposite of typical oncology priors), weight_loss_pct_6mo
(-0.076/%), cea_ng_ml (-0.005/ng/mL). Multivariable R^2 = 0.86. Other features (sex,
msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion, crp, nlr, ldh) have no meaningful adjusted effect on
pfs_months in this cohort.

CAVEATS

The data appear to be a structured EHR-derived cohort with consistent patterns and almost no
noise on the prognostic axes. The unusual age finding and the absence of any benefit from
canonical biomarker-matched targeted therapies (e.g., pembrolizumab in MSI-H) are notable;
the regorafenib subgroup result is biologically plausible (regorafenib is a multi-kinase
inhibitor used in chemorefractory mCRC and has been hypothesized to be more effective in
KRAS/BRAF-WT, left-sided patients with low tumor burden as proxied by CEA) but the magnitude
(+5 months) is much larger than typical clinical-trial point estimates and may reflect the
synthetic / simulated nature of this dataset. All results are associational; no causal
conclusions are warranted from EHR data alone.
