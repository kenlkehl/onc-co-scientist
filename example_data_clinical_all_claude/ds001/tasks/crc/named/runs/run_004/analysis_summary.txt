ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_crc (n = 50,000)
==========================================

OUTCOME
-------
The dataset has a single time-to-event-style outcome, pfs_months, with mean 4.31
months (SD 2.29; range 0.0–16.3). All other columns are baseline features or
treatment indicators. There are 6 treatment indicators (cetuximab, bevacizumab,
pembrolizumab, encorafenib, trastuzumab+tucatinib, regorafenib), most of which
overlap (median 1, max 6 simultaneous treatments per patient). Treatment
indicators are essentially balanced across all biomarkers and demographics
(prevalence within biomarker subgroups within ±1 percentage point of marginal
prevalence), implying near-random treatment assignment.

ITERATIVE EXPLORATION (25 iterations)
-------------------------------------

Iterations 1–5 (prognostic main effects).
Univariate linear regressions of pfs_months on each candidate prognostic
feature reproduced the expected directions for the major clinical variables,
with one notable surprise:
  - ecog_ps:                    -1.190 mo per level (p<1e-300)         expected
  - stage_iv:                   -1.352 mo                              expected
  - albumin_g_dl:               +0.465 mo per g/dL                     expected
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo:        -0.073 mo per %                        expected
  - kras_mutation:              -0.327 mo                              expected
  - braf_v600e:                 -0.228 mo                              expected
  - right_sided_primary:        -0.305 mo                              expected
  - cea_ng_ml:                  -0.005 mo per ng/mL                    expected
  - ldh_u_l:                    -0.0003 mo per U/L                     expected (weak)
  - age_years:                  +0.177 mo per year                     UNEXPECTED
  - msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion, sex_female, nlr, crp:    no detectable
    main effect.

The positive age coefficient is robust: it reproduces with the same magnitude
in every ECOG×stage stratum and after full multivariable adjustment, so it is
not the usual "younger patients receive more aggressive therapy" confound.

Iteration 6 (treatment main effects).
Of the six treatments, only regorafenib showed a univariate effect on PFS:
  - cetuximab           -0.038  p=0.09
  - bevacizumab         -0.019  p=0.35
  - pembrolizumab       +0.005  p=0.86
  - encorafenib         +0.005  p=0.87
  - trastuzumab+tucat.  -0.044  p=0.24
  - regorafenib         +0.972  p=1.7e-216

Iterations 7–10 (canonical biomarker-targeted combinations).
The four canonical biomarker–drug pairings were all REFUTED in this dataset:
  - pembrolizumab in MSI-high (n=2,513): on=4.30 vs off=4.29, diff=+0.007 (p=0.96).
  - encorafenib in BRAF V600E (n=2,272): on=3.98 vs off=4.11, diff=-0.13 (p=0.33).
  - encorafenib+cetuximab combo in BRAF V600E (n=58 combo): diff=+0.06 (p=0.84).
  - trastuzumab+tucatinib in HER2-amplified (n=1,504): diff=+0.017 (p=0.93).
  - cetuximab in RAS/BRAF wild-type left-sided (n=16,443): diff=-0.07 (p=0.12).
None of the textbook biomarker-targeted treatments reproduced their expected
benefit in their target subpopulation in this cohort.

Iterations 11–15 (regorafenib heterogeneity discovery).
The regorafenib main effect is not uniform — interaction screening showed
extreme heterogeneity:
  - regorafenib × kras_mutation:        β=-1.629, p=7.7e-226
  - regorafenib × right_sided_primary:  β=-1.454, p=4.1e-168
  - regorafenib × braf_v600e:           β=-1.183, p=6.1e-23
  - regorafenib × cea_ng_ml:            β=-0.0253, p=8.7e-58 (continuous)

Stratified estimates within the joint subgroup KRAS-WT × BRAF-WT × left-sided:
  - regorafenib effect = +2.758 months PFS (n=17,372; p<1e-300).
Outside this triple subgroup: +0.030 months (p=0.29) — null.

A CEA threshold scan WITHIN the triple subgroup localized a sharp boundary at
CEA ≈ 5 ng/mL: below 5 ng/mL the effect is +5.0 months; at or above 5 ng/mL
the effect is essentially zero.

The final refined regorafenib subgroup is therefore the conjunction
  kras_mutation == 0 AND braf_v600e == 0 AND right_sided_primary == 0
  AND cea_ng_ml < 5
Inside this 4-predicate subgroup (n=9,326), regorafenib increases mean
pfs_months from 4.088 to 9.101 (diff = +5.013 months, t=90.4, p<1e-300).
Outside (n=40,674), the effect is +0.025 (p=0.33).

Iteration 16 (NRAS).
nras_mutation is mutually exclusive with KRAS and BRAF in this cohort; within
the responsive subgroup it does not modify the regorafenib effect (NRAS-WT
diff +2.75 vs NRAS-mutant diff +2.91; p=0.83 for difference). NRAS therefore
is not part of the responsive-subgroup definition.

Iterations 17–18, 22, 24 (heterogeneity searches for the other treatments).
A pre-specified scan of all 5 non-regorafenib treatments × 8 binary features
(40 tests) and × 9 continuous features (45 tests) found NO interaction
surviving Bonferroni correction. T-learner gradient-boosted models for each
non-regorafenib treatment yielded CATE distributions with std 0.14–0.22
months — within noise. Pairwise treatment×treatment interactions were all
non-significant. Fine-grained slicing of MSI-high by sidedness or stage to
look for hidden pembrolizumab benefit was also null. Conclusion:
treatment_cetuximab, treatment_bevacizumab, treatment_pembrolizumab,
treatment_encorafenib, and treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib have no
detectable beneficial effect on pfs_months in any subgroup we examined.

Iterations 19, 21 (prognostic feature × feature interactions).
  - right_sided_primary × kras_mutation:  β=+0.452, p=1.7e-25 (sub-additive
      — KRAS adds little harm in already-right-sided tumors).
  - albumin × stage_iv:                   β=+0.032, p=0.41 (no interaction).
  - weight_loss × ecog_ps:                β=+0.002, p=0.59 (no interaction).
  - stage_iv × ecog_ps:                   β=+0.040, p=0.39 (purely additive).
  Best vs worst joint clinical cell: stage=0/ecog=0 mean PFS = 5.84 mo;
    stage=1/ecog≥1 mean PFS = 3.52 mo.

Iteration 20, 23, 25 (multivariable synthesis and final).
A full multivariable OLS (all 33 features + treatments, n=50,000)
yielded R² = 0.86. Adjusted treatment coefficients confirmed regorafenib as
the only effective therapy:
   regorafenib +0.943 (p<1e-300); all others |β| ≤ 0.04 with p ≥ 0.07.
A T-learner decision tree on regorafenib CATE recovered the same four
modifiers (right_sided_primary, kras_mutation, braf_v600e, cea_ng_ml) as the
parametric interaction tests, supporting the joint subgroup definition.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------

1. PROGNOSTIC MODEL. The strongest prognostic features for longer pfs_months
   in this CRC cohort are (in descending |effect| from the multivariable
   model):
     stage_iv          -1.36
     ecog_ps           -1.17 per level
     albumin_g_dl      +0.47 per g/dL
     braf_v600e        -0.36
     kras_mutation     -0.35
     right_sided_pr.   -0.29
     age_years         +0.18 per year (POSITIVE — atypical)
     weight_loss_pct   -0.076 per %
     cea_ng_ml         -0.005 per ng/mL
     ldh_u_l           -0.0004 per U/L
   Albumin, ECOG, stage IV, and KRAS/BRAF mutational status carry the
   majority of the prognostic signal. The positive age coefficient is robust
   and reproducible across strata and is a notable feature of this dataset.

2. TREATMENT EFFECTS. Five of six treatments — cetuximab, bevacizumab,
   pembrolizumab, encorafenib, trastuzumab+tucatinib — have no detectable
   effect on pfs_months in this cohort, including in their canonical
   biomarker-matched subpopulations (MSI-H, BRAF V600E, HER2-amplified,
   RAS/BRAF wild-type left-sided). This is unusual for real-world CRC
   data and suggests either that the cohort is constructed in a way that
   masks these effects, or that these treatments are inert in this
   simulated dataset.

3. REGORAFENIB SUBGROUP — best-supported treatment-effect-heterogeneity
   hypothesis. Regorafenib produces a large, isolated benefit only in the
   conjunction:
        kras_mutation == 0
        AND braf_v600e == 0
        AND right_sided_primary == 0
        AND cea_ng_ml < 5
   Inside this subgroup (n=9,326, 18.7% of the cohort) regorafenib raises
   mean PFS from 4.09 to 9.10 months (+5.01 months, t=90.4, p<1e-300).
   Outside this subgroup (n=40,674) the regorafenib effect is +0.025 months
   (p=0.33), statistically null. Each predicate is necessary: dropping any
   one reduces the in-subgroup effect substantially (e.g. dropping
   cea_ng_ml<5 leaves effect of +2.76; dropping right_sided_primary==0
   gives +0.61 in the right-sided complement). Each modifier above
   independently abolishes the regorafenib benefit, so the subgroup
   definition lists all four predicates.

4. PROGNOSTIC FEATURE INTERACTIONS. The only notable feature×feature
   interaction is right_sided_primary × kras_mutation (β=+0.45, p=1.7e-25),
   indicating sub-additivity of these two negative prognostic factors.
   Other pairwise interactions among ECOG, stage IV, albumin, weight loss
   were not detectable.
