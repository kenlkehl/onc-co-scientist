Analysis Summary — ds001_aml (n=50,000, outcome: objective_response, base rate ~18.75%)
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The dataset contains 35 anonymized features (16 binary, 1 three-level categorical
[feature_004], and 18 continuous) and one binary outcome (objective_response).
Across 25 iterations of propose-test-refine I screened univariate associations,
fit fully adjusted multivariable models, and conducted systematic
treatment-effect-heterogeneity searches via two-way and three-way interaction
modeling, kernel-by-feature interaction screening, and within-subgroup analyses.

The dominant finding is a tightly-defined responder subgroup that explains
essentially all of the response-rate heterogeneity attributable to the binary
features.

UNIVARIATE / MAIN-EFFECT FINDINGS (Iterations 1-3)
--------------------------------------------------
Strong main effects on objective_response (signs preserved in the multivariable
model in iter 4):

  - feature_004 (3-level): response rate 23.1% / 17.4% / 12.9% across levels
    0/1/2 (p ~ 1.8e-90); monotonic decrease.
  - feature_014 (binary): 23.5% (=1) vs 16.7% (=0); log-OR = +0.42; p ~ 6e-70.
  - feature_001 (binary): 22.1% vs 16.5%; log-OR = +0.36; p ~ 2e-55.
  - feature_021 (binary): 22.0% vs 16.6%; log-OR = +0.35; p ~ 7e-51.
  - feature_020 (continuous): negative; standardized logistic coef = -0.14;
    p ~ 1e-30.
  - feature_024 (continuous): negative; coef = -0.09; p ~ 2e-16.
  - feature_029 (continuous, narrow range 1.7-5.5): positive; coef = +0.06;
    p ~ 3e-7.
  - feature_026 (binary): negative; log-OR = -0.16; p ~ 3e-6.
  - feature_009 (binary): negative; log-OR = -0.17; p ~ 4e-5.
  - feature_027 (continuous): negative; coef = -0.06; p ~ 6e-6.
  - feature_022 (continuous): negative; coef = -0.05; p ~ 2e-4.

Supported. Eleven binary features (feature_010, _005, _016, _025, _019, _015,
_018, _008, _006, _032, _030) and several continuous features (feature_007,
_031, _028, _013, _033, _034, _011, _003, _002, _012, _017, _023, _035) had no
marginal main effect on response. feature_018 and feature_008 share the same
marginal n=7545 and have a strikingly correlated joint distribution (equal
off-diagonals 6352, joint-positive 1193); they are not identical but appear
linked.

The fully adjusted multivariable logistic regression (iter 4) reproduced the
above signs and significances; no main effect direction reversed under
adjustment.

INTERACTION DISCOVERY (Iterations 5-8)
--------------------------------------
A pairwise interaction screen on all binary candidates surfaced an extreme
synergy among feature_001, feature_014, and feature_021:

  - feature_001 x feature_014: interaction logit coef = +1.06, p = 7e-103.
  - feature_014 x feature_021: +1.05, p = 5e-102.
  - feature_001 x feature_021: +0.88, p = 1e-78.

These three features are mutually independent in distribution (phi ~ 0), so the
synergy is on response, not on co-occurrence.

The saturated three-way model showed that the pairwise interactions and main
effects collapse to non-significance once the three-way term is included; the
three-way feature_001*feature_014*feature_021 interaction has coef = +2.13,
p = 4e-92. Examining the 8-cell joint table confirmed why: response is
~16-17% in 7 of the 8 cells, and 63.3% in the single triple-positive cell
(n=2401).

Hypothesis "response is dramatically higher only when feature_001 = feature_014
= feature_021 = 1" was strongly supported. Refuted: the alternative that
pairwise effects are independent-and-additive.

SUPPRESSOR DISCOVERY (Iterations 9-11)
--------------------------------------
Within the triple-positive subgroup (n=2401, response 63.3%):

  - feature_026 = 1 (n=376) drops response to 18.4%; within-subgroup logistic
    coef = -2.42, p = 4e-65.
  - feature_009 = 1 (n=276) drops response to 18.8%; within-subgroup logistic
    coef = -2.26, p = 6e-45.

A model fitting kernel = (feature_001 AND feature_014 AND feature_021) plus
kernel*feature_026 and kernel*feature_009 showed:

  - kernel coef = +2.86 (p ~ 4e-145)
  - kernel * feature_026 = -2.66 (p ~ 3e-70)
  - kernel * feature_009 = -2.55 (p ~ 4e-50)

The two suppressor*kernel terms are quantitatively offsetting: each restores the
log-odds back toward the 16-17% baseline. Both suppressors act independently
(they overlap only modestly).

FULLY-SPECIFIED RESPONDER SUBGROUP (Iteration 12)
-------------------------------------------------
  Subgroup definition:
    feature_001 = 1 AND feature_014 = 1 AND feature_021 = 1 AND
    feature_026 = 0 AND feature_009 = 0
  n = 1,790 (3.6% of the cohort)
  objective_response = 78.6% (1407 of 1790)
  Complement: n = 48,210, response 16.5% (7968 of 48210)
  Fisher OR = 18.55, p effectively 0
  Risk difference = +62.1 percentage points

WITHIN-SUBGROUP MODULATION (Iterations 13-14)
---------------------------------------------
Within the responder subgroup, additional gradients persist:

  - feature_004 logistic coef = -0.56, p = 1e-10. Response by level: 86.0%
    (level 0, n=617), 77.2% (level 1, n=902), 66.8% (level 2, n=271).
  - feature_020 standardized coef = -0.20, p = 4e-4. Response by tertile:
    81.3% (low), 80.0% (mid), 74.4% (high).

These are intensifiers, not gating predicates: the responder kernel remains
strongly elevated across all feature_004 levels (60-percentage-point gap vs
non-kernel patients persists). The kernel*feature_004 interaction is borderline
(p = 0.05), consistent with feature_004 acting mostly additively and only
weakly multiplicatively.

VERIFICATION OF ABSENCE OF OTHER TREATMENT-LIKE VARIABLES (Iter 15, 19, 22)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
A kernel-by-feature interaction screen across all 30 remaining features
identified only feature_026 (p = 2e-62) and feature_009 (p = 4e-42) as strong
modifiers of the kernel; a smaller positive interaction with feature_024
(coef = +0.15, p = 6e-4) was the only other notable modifier. No binary feature
without a marginal main effect (feature_010, feature_030, feature_005, etc.)
revealed a hidden treatment-vs-control split inside the responder subgroup
(all p > 0.1 within the n=1790 cell). This argues against any of those
zero-main-effect binaries being a treatment indicator.

ROLE INTERCHANGEABILITY OF feature_001 / feature_014 / feature_021 (Iter 16-17)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Because the three-way interaction is symmetric, any one of {feature_001,
feature_014, feature_021} can be regarded as the "treatment" with the other two
acting as the predictive biomarker pair:

  - Treating feature_014 as treatment: within feature_001 = 1 AND feature_021 = 1,
    feature_014's response gap is +47 pp (16.9% -> 63.3%); outside this
    biomarker pair, feature_014's effect is ~0 pp.
  - Treating feature_021 as treatment: gap +47 pp inside feature_001 = 1 AND
    feature_014 = 1, ~0 pp outside.
  - Treating feature_001 as treatment: gap +48 pp inside feature_014 = 1 AND
    feature_021 = 1, ~0 pp outside.

The data alone cannot distinguish which variable is the intervention; whichever
is the treatment, the heterogeneity is identical.

STABILITY (Iteration 18)
------------------------
Random 50/50 split:
  - Half 1: subgroup n=898, response 77.1%; complement 16.7%; gap 60.4 pp.
  - Half 2: subgroup n=892, response 80.2%; complement 16.4%; gap 63.8 pp.

The subgroup definition and effect size are highly reproducible.

OUTSIDE THE RESPONDER SUBGROUP (Iteration 21)
---------------------------------------------
Among the 31 non-responder strata defined by these five binary features,
response rates range only 15.2-19.6%, none reaching 20%. The five-binary mask
captures essentially all the heterogeneity those five features contribute.

FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY HYPOTHESIS (Iteration 24-25)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Best-supported subgroup statement (treating feature_014 as the candidate
"treatment" - the choice is symmetric across {feature_001, feature_014,
feature_021}):

  The effect of feature_014 on objective_response is approximately zero overall
  except in the subgroup defined by

      feature_001 = 1 AND feature_021 = 1 AND feature_026 = 0 AND feature_009 = 0,

  where it produces an absolute increase in response from ~16.6% to ~78.6%
  (+62 percentage points; OR ~ 18.5).

Within this subgroup, lower feature_004 (level 0 > level 1 > level 2) and lower
feature_020 further intensify response.

By symmetry, the same heterogeneity holds if feature_001 or feature_021 is
treated as the "treatment". feature_026 and feature_009 are dominant
suppressors: a positive value on either nullifies the responder effect even
when all three of feature_001, feature_014, and feature_021 are positive.

WHAT WAS SUPPORTED VS. REFUTED
------------------------------
Supported:
  - All eleven main-effect hypotheses in iter 1-2 (signs and significance).
  - Pairwise synergies among feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 (iter 6).
  - The three-way interaction explanation (iter 7) - meaning the response gain
    is concentrated in a single triple-positive cell, not in pairwise
    additivity.
  - Suppressor effects of feature_026 and feature_009 (iter 9-11).
  - Fully-specified five-binary responder subgroup (iter 12).
  - Within-subgroup gradients for feature_004 and feature_020 (iter 13-14).
  - Stability across split-sample halves (iter 18).

Refuted / not supported:
  - That the three-way interaction is just additive pairwise effects (iter 7;
    full model collapses pairwise terms once 3-way term is in).
  - That any other binary feature with no marginal effect hides a
    treatment-vs-control split (iter 15, 19; all kernel-by-feature interactions
    with these features were non-significant).
  - That kernel*feature_004 represents true effect-modification of the
    responder kernel (iter 20; only borderline p ~ 0.05; feature_004 acts
    largely additively within and across the kernel).

CLOSING NOTE
------------
The dataset structure points to a single dominant clinical pattern: a small
high-response subgroup (~3.6% of the cohort) defined by the simultaneous
positivity of three independent biomarkers/treatments (feature_001, feature_014,
feature_021) and the simultaneous negativity of two independent suppressors
(feature_026, feature_009). Inside that subgroup, response approaches 80%;
outside it, response is essentially flat at the cohort baseline of ~16-17%
regardless of which other features are present. feature_004 and feature_020
further refine the within-subgroup response without altering the gating
predicates.
