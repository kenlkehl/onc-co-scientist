Analysis Summary — ds001_aml (50,000 patients, objective_response outcome)
=========================================================================

Overview
--------
The cohort comprises 50,000 patients with one binary outcome
(objective_response; baseline rate 18.75%, 9,375 responders) and 35
de-identified features (16 binary, 1 ordinal 0/1/2 = feature_004, and
18 continuous). After exhaustive univariate, multivariable, and
interaction analyses across the iteration loop, the dominant signal in
the dataset is a clean three-way treatment-combination effect that is
abolished by either of two specific resistance markers. A small set of
prognostic factors operates additively on top of this picture; the
remaining ~24 features have no detectable effect.

Iteration 1 — Univariate scan
-----------------------------
Binary features:
  Strongest positive associations with response:
    feature_014: 23.5% if=1 vs 16.7% if=0 (OR=1.53, p<1e-30)
    feature_001: 22.1% vs 16.5% (OR=1.43, p<1e-30)
    feature_021: 22.0% vs 16.6% (OR=1.41, p<1e-30)
  Strongest negative associations:
    feature_026: 16.8% vs 19.1% (OR=0.86, p~3e-7)
    feature_009: 16.6% vs 19.0% (OR=0.85, p~6e-6)
  All other binary features had |OR-1|<0.05 and p>=0.13.

Continuous features (Welch t-tests, responders vs non-responders):
    feature_020: 3.44 vs 3.95 (lower in responders, t=-12.1, p<1e-30)
    feature_024: 58.5 vs 60.3 (lower, t=-8.2, p<1e-15)
    feature_029: 3.82 vs 3.79 (slightly higher, p~3e-7)
    feature_027: 5.69 vs 6.17 (lower, p~2e-6)
    feature_022: 19.27 vs 20.39 (lower, p~1.5e-4)
  All other continuous features p>0.05.

Ordinal feature_004 (0/1/2): response 23.1% / 17.4% / 12.9%
(chi-square p~1.8e-90). Monotonic decrease — a strong prognostic
gradient consistent with a performance-status/risk-stratifier
interpretation.

Iteration 2 — Multivariable adjustment
--------------------------------------
A logistic regression including all 35 features simultaneously
(continuous features standardized) confirmed independent effects of
feature_004 (β=-0.36, p~9e-93), feature_014 (+0.44, p~1.7e-71),
feature_001 (+0.37, p~2.4e-57), feature_021 (+0.36, p~8e-54),
feature_020 (-0.14, p~3e-31), feature_024 (-0.10, p~1e-17),
feature_029 (+0.06, p~1.4e-7), feature_026 (-0.17, p~6e-7),
feature_009 (-0.19, p~3.6e-6), feature_027 (-0.06, p~6e-6), and
feature_022 (-0.05, p~1.3e-4). All other features had p>0.02 and
|β|<0.05 SD.

Iteration 3 — Treatment independence (RCT-like assignment)
----------------------------------------------------------
feature_014, feature_001, and feature_021 are pairwise statistically
independent (chi-square p≥0.47, all |corr|<0.005), and each is
independent of feature_004 and of feature_026 (~15% prevalence in
treated and untreated alike). This pattern is consistent with three
independently randomized treatment indicators rather than
disease-related risk markers.

Iteration 4 — Pairwise treatment interactions
---------------------------------------------
Logistic models with two-way interaction terms revealed massive
synergy:
    feature_014 × feature_001: β=+1.06, p~6.8e-103
    feature_014 × feature_021: β=+1.05, p~5.5e-102
    feature_001 × feature_021: β=+0.88, p~1.1e-78
However, examination of the two-way cell rates showed the boost was
driven entirely by joint positivity of all three.

Iteration 5 — Three-way interaction (the central finding)
---------------------------------------------------------
Cell-level response rates across the 8 cells of (feature_014,
feature_001, feature_021):
    (0,0,0)  17.2% (n=12,533)
    (1,0,0)  16.0% (n=5,404)
    (0,1,0)  16.4% (n=8,458)
    (0,0,1)  16.2% (n=8,383)
    (1,1,0)  16.4% (n=3,585)
    (1,0,1)  15.7% (n=3,624)
    (0,1,1)  16.9% (n=5,612)
    (1,1,1)  63.3% (n=2,401)
A saturated logistic model with all main, two-way, and three-way
terms showed only the three-way product significant
(β=+2.13, z=20.4, p<1e-90); all main effects and two-way interactions
had p>=0.05 and |β|<=0.10. The signal is a pure three-way synergy:
all three treatments are required simultaneously; any singleton or
pair is no better than none.

Iterations 6–7 — Resistance markers
-----------------------------------
Within triple-treated patients (n=2,401), response rate dropped from
75.9% (n=2,025) when feature_026=0 to 18.4% (n=376) when
feature_026=1 — the triple effect is essentially erased. Likewise,
within the (T3=1, feature_026=0) subgroup (n=2,025), response was
78.6% (n=1,790) when feature_009=0 vs 18.7% (n=235) when
feature_009=1.
Logistic interaction terms:
    T3 × feature_026: β=-2.44, p~1.8e-62
    T3 × feature_009: β=-2.26, p~4.3e-42
A systematic interaction screen of all 32 non-treatment features
against T3 confirmed feature_026 and feature_009 dominate. Only
feature_024 reached p<0.05 (β=+0.15, p~5.6e-4 — small modifier);
all other features p>0.1.

Iteration 8 — feature_004 prognostic vs predictive
--------------------------------------------------
feature_004 strongly predicts response in every stratum but does not
modify the relative treatment benefit:
    feature_004=0: T3=1 -> 69.8%, T3=0 -> 20.8% (Δ=+49.0 pp)
    feature_004=1: T3=1 -> 61.2%, T3=0 -> 15.2% (Δ=+46.0 pp)
    feature_004=2: T3=1 -> 55.4%, T3=0 -> 10.8% (Δ=+44.6 pp)
The interaction T3 × feature_004 had p~0.34. feature_004 is purely
prognostic, not predictive.

Iteration 9 — Continuous biomarkers
-----------------------------------
Within the favorable subgroup (T3=1, feature_026=0, feature_009=0;
n=1,790), feature_020 remained inversely associated with response
(3.77 in responders vs 4.56 in non-responders, p~8.2e-4). In an
integrated logistic model {T3, feature_026, feature_009, T3:026,
T3:009, feature_004, feature_020, feature_024}, feature_020 (β=-0.16,
p<1e-30) and feature_024 (β=-0.11, p~1e-18) retained independent
prognostic significance with no meaningful interaction with treatment.

Iterations 10–12 — Final subgroup definition
--------------------------------------------
The complete predictive subgroup definition is:
    feature_014 = 1
    AND feature_001 = 1
    AND feature_021 = 1
    AND feature_026 = 0
    AND feature_009 = 0
n = 1,790 (3.6% of the cohort).
Response rate in this subgroup: 78.6% (1,407/1,790).
Response rate in everyone else: 16.5% (7,968/48,210).
Risk difference: +62.1 percentage points; risk ratio 4.76; odds ratio
~18.6; chi-square p effectively 0.

Within feature_026=0 & feature_009=0 — the eligible/non-resistant
population — singletons and pairs of the three treatments did not
beat baseline:
    no treatments    17.2% (n=9,626)
    feature_014 only 16.1% (n=4,162)
    feature_001 only 16.4% (n=6,509)
    feature_021 only 16.0% (n=6,416)
    014+001 only     15.8% (n=2,741)
    014+021 only     15.8% (n=2,789)
    001+021 only     16.6% (n=4,288)
    all three        78.6% (n=1,790)

Iteration 11 — Null features
----------------------------
With ~50,000 patients, the dataset has power to detect even small
effects. The following 24 features had p>0.05 and |β|<0.05 SD in the
full multivariable model and no detectable treatment-modifier effect:
feature_005, 006, 008, 010, 011, 012, 013, 015, 016, 017, 018, 019,
023, 025, 028, 030, 031, 032, 033, 034, 035, 002, 003, 007.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
Supported:
- feature_014, feature_001, feature_021 each have positive univariate
  associations with response.
- feature_026 and feature_009 each have negative univariate
  associations with response.
- feature_004 is a strong, ordinal, prognostic factor.
- A pure three-way interaction among feature_014, feature_001, and
  feature_021 drives response; singletons and pairs are inert.
- feature_026 and feature_009 each abolish the triple-treatment
  benefit (effect modification, not main effect).
- feature_020 and feature_024 are independent continuous prognostic
  factors that do not interact with treatment.
- feature_004 is prognostic but not predictive of treatment effect.

Refuted (or null):
- The remaining 24 features show no detectable effect on response
  despite ample sample size.
- feature_004 was hypothesized to potentially modify treatment effect
  — it does not (interaction p~0.34).

Final best-supported subgroup hypothesis (per-instructions)
-----------------------------------------------------------
Treatment: triple combination (feature_014 = 1 AND feature_001 = 1
AND feature_021 = 1).
Outcome: objective_response.
Direction: increased response.
Subgroup predicates required for the effect:
    feature_014 = 1 AND feature_001 = 1 AND feature_021 = 1
    AND feature_026 = 0
    AND feature_009 = 0
Outside this exact subgroup the triple combination produces no
detectable benefit (response stays at the ~17% cohort baseline).
Within this subgroup the response rate is approximately 79% (vs ~17%
baseline), a +62-percentage-point absolute increase.

Overall clinical interpretation
-------------------------------
This dataset behaves like a pragmatic AML cohort exposed to three
independently randomized treatment components, where the regimen is
only effective when all three components are co-administered, and
where two specific patient features (plausibly molecular or
cytogenetic resistance markers) eliminate the combination benefit
when present. A separate axis of risk (feature_004, an ordinal
score; together with feature_020 and feature_024 continuous
prognostic markers) shifts baseline response but does not alter the
relative treatment benefit. The remaining features carry no
prognostic or predictive signal in this cohort.
