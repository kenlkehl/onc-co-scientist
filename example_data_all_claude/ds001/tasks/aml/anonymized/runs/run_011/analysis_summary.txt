ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_aml (50,000 patient AML cohort, anonymized features)
==============================================================================

OVERALL OBJECTIVE-RESPONSE RATE: 18.75% (9,375 / 50,000)

HEADLINE FINDING
----------------
A small subgroup defined by FIVE simultaneous predicates achieves an
objective-response rate of 78.6% (n=1,790, 3.6% of the cohort), versus 16.5%
in the remaining 48,210 patients — an absolute risk difference of +0.621 and
odds ratio of 18.6 (chi-square p << 1e-300).

The complete subgroup definition is:

    feature_001 = 1   AND
    feature_014 = 1   AND
    feature_021 = 1   AND
    feature_026 = 0   AND
    feature_009 = 0

These five predicates together describe a triple-treatment combination
(features 001, 014, 021) restricted to patients lacking two resistance
markers (features 026 and 009). All five predicates are required: dropping
any one of them collapses the response rate back toward the cohort baseline.

STEP-BY-STEP NARRATIVE
----------------------

1. UNIVARIATE SCREEN (iter 1).  Across all 35 features, the strongest
   univariate associations with objective_response were:
   - Negative ordinal: feature_004 (looks like ECOG performance status,
     0/1/2), -0.10 absolute response per level, p = 1.8e-90
   - Positive binary: feature_014 (+0.067, p = 5.8e-70), feature_001
     (+0.056, p = 1.9e-55), feature_021 (+0.054, p = 6.6e-51)
   - Negative continuous: feature_020 (peripheral-blast-like), feature_024
     (BM-blast-%-like, range 20-100), feature_022 (WBC-like, very skewed),
     feature_027 (skewed continuous, bilirubin-like)

2. MULTIVARIABLE ADJUSTMENT (iter 2).  In a logistic regression with all 35
   features (skewed continuous variables log1p-transformed):
   - feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 each had positive, independent,
     similar-magnitude coefficients (OR ~ 1.4-1.5, all p < 1e-50) —
     consistent with three separate treatment-component flags.
   - feature_004 had the largest negative effect (OR per level 0.70,
     p < 1e-90) — the dominant prognostic factor.
   - feature_026 (OR 0.84, p = 5.7e-7) and feature_009 (OR 0.83, p = 3.8e-6)
     were independently negative.
   - feature_029 was the only consistently positive continuous prognostic
     marker (looks like albumin).

3. TREATMENT INDEPENDENCE (iter 3).  Cross-tabulations of feature_001,
   feature_014, feature_021 showed independent co-occurrence (no mutual
   exclusivity), consistent with three separately assigned treatment flags
   that can be co-administered.

4. NUMBER-OF-TREATMENTS ANALYSIS (iter 4).  Stratifying by the count of
   the three treatments yielded a striking pattern:
       0 treatments -> ORR 0.172 (n=12,533)
       1 treatment  -> ORR 0.162 (n=22,245)
       2 treatments -> ORR 0.163 (n=12,821)
       3 treatments -> ORR 0.633 (n= 2,401)
   No effect of any single treatment or any pair — only the full triple
   combination produced a dramatic uplift. This is a pure synergy pattern.

5. FORMAL 3-WAY INTERACTION TEST (iter 6).  Fitting the saturated 2x2x2
   logit on the three treatments confirmed: all main and 2-way interaction
   coefficients were near zero, and the 3-way product term had a coefficient
   of +2.13 (p = 2.6e-92). The effect is exactly a 3-way synergy.

6. SUBGROUP HETEROGENEITY WITHIN TRIPLE-TREATED (iter 7).  Among the 2,401
   triple-treated patients, two binary features dramatically reduced the
   response rate:
       feature_026 = 1: response 0.184 (vs 0.717 if =0), p = 7.6e-86
       feature_009 = 1: response 0.188 (vs 0.691 if =0), p = 3.3e-59
   Their joint distribution showed essentially additive abolition: either
   marker alone reduced response to baseline; both together added nothing
   further.

7. RESISTANCE-MARKER VERIFICATION (iters 8-10).  Critically, in non-triple-
   treated patients (n = 47,599), feature_026 and feature_009 had NO effect
   on response (response rates were ~0.16-0.17 across all 4 combinations).
   A formal interaction logit confirmed: triple coefficient +2.90; triple x
   feature_026 = -2.66; triple x feature_009 = -2.55; main effects of f026
   and f009 were ~0. They are pure treatment-effect modifiers, not
   independent prognostic factors.

8. SYSTEMATIC TREATMENT-BY-FEATURE INTERACTION SCREEN (iter 11).  Across
   all 32 non-treatment features, only feature_026 (p = 1.8e-62) and
   feature_009 (p = 4.3e-42) showed meaningful triple-by-feature interactions.
   feature_024 (BM blasts) showed a small positive interaction (p = 5.6e-4)
   but of trivial magnitude.

9. CLEAN-TRIPLE SUBGROUP (iter 12).  Combining the predicates yielded the
   clean-triple subgroup: triple-treatment AND f026=0 AND f009=0.
   - n = 1,790 (3.6% of the cohort)
   - ORR = 0.786 vs 0.165 elsewhere
   - Absolute difference +0.621; OR ~ 18.6

10. PROGNOSTIC FACTORS (iters 13-19).  Several features behaved as classic
    prognostic markers — affecting response within and outside the treatment
    subgroup, with no triple-by-feature interaction:
    - feature_004 (PS-like ordinal): negative, large prognostic effect
    - feature_020 (PB blasts), feature_024 (BM blasts), feature_022 (WBC):
      each negative, classic disease-burden patterns
    - feature_027 (bilirubin-like): negative
    - feature_029 (albumin-like): positive
    - feature_011 (age) and feature_010 (sex): NO independent effect on
      response in this cohort
    - Many other low-prevalence binary indicators (005, 006, 008, 015, 016,
      018, 019, 025, 030, 032): no independent effect.

11. FURTHER REFINEMENT (iter 20).  Within the clean-triple subgroup, adding
    feature_004 = 0 AND feature_020 <= 1 lifted response to 0.879 (n=394).
    Modest enrichment that operates symmetrically on both treated and
    untreated patients — consistent with prognostic refinement rather than
    additional treatment-effect modification.

12. ROBUSTNESS (iter 24).  The clean-triple effect (treated minus untreated
    response) remained large within every stratum tested: PS=0 (Δ=+0.62),
    PS=2 (Δ=+0.57), high BM blasts (Δ=+0.63), age >= 70 (Δ=+0.63).

HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED VS REFUTED
-------------------------------
SUPPORTED (significant, direction as predicted):
  - feature_001, feature_014, feature_021 are each independently associated
    with response in adjusted models, and synergize as a triple combination.
  - feature_026 and feature_009 each abolish the triple effect.
  - feature_004, feature_020, feature_024, feature_022, feature_027 are
    negative prognostic factors.
  - feature_029 is a positive prognostic factor.
  - The clean-triple subgroup (5 predicates) achieves ORR ~ 0.79.

REFUTED (or null):
  - "Each single treatment improves response on its own" — refuted; only
    the triple combination has any effect.
  - "Each pair of treatments improves response over none" — refuted;
    pairs are indistinguishable from monotherapy or no treatment.
  - "feature_026 and feature_009 are independent prognostic factors" —
    refuted; they have no effect outside the triple-treated subgroup.
  - "Age (feature_011) or sex (feature_010) independently affect response"
    — refuted in adjusted models.

FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS
------------------------------------------
TREATMENT:  feature_001 = 1 AND feature_014 = 1 AND feature_021 = 1
            (a 3-component combination regimen)
OUTCOME:    objective_response (binary)
DIRECTION:  Positive — response rate increases from ~0.17 to ~0.79
SUBGROUP PREDICATES (all required):
            - feature_001 = 1
            - feature_014 = 1
            - feature_021 = 1
            - feature_026 = 0   (resistance marker absent)
            - feature_009 = 0   (resistance marker absent)
EFFECT SIZE: Absolute risk difference +0.621; odds ratio 18.6;
             p << 1e-300 (chi-square)

The two resistance markers (feature_026 and feature_009) are the load-bearing
modifiers: each one independently abolishes the synergy of the triple
combination, but neither has any prognostic effect in the absence of the
triple combination. Clinically this resembles the pattern seen with targeted
therapies in AML, where a specific genetic/cytogenetic context is required
for a regimen to work and a single resistance-conferring lesion can wipe out
the benefit.
