Analysis Summary — ds001_aml (50,000 patients, outcome: objective_response, baseline rate 18.75%)
====================================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
We probed the dataset across 25 iterations: marginal feature-outcome associations, multivariable
logistic regression, treatment main effects, biomarker-matched treatment interactions, and an
exhaustive subgroup heterogeneity search for each treatment. The strongest signal in the cohort by
far is a single, very large treatment-by-genotype interaction for venetoclax-azacitidine; the rest
of the picture consists of modest baseline prognostic effects and several "null" findings for
targeted therapies that, biologically, would be expected to work but don't in this cohort.

1. PROGNOSTIC FEATURES (main effects on ORR)
--------------------------------------------
Confirmed univariate and multivariable-adjusted associations (all directions consistent):

  Negative prognostic factors (lower ORR):
    - ecog_ps                  (point-biserial r = -0.091, p ≈ 1e-91; adj. log-OR -0.36)
    - complex_karyotype        (ORR 16.8% vs 19.1%, p = 3.3e-6; adj. log-OR -0.17)
    - tp53_mutation            (ORR 16.6% vs 19.0%, p = 3.5e-5; adj. log-OR -0.19)
    - blast_pct_marrow         (r = -0.037, p = 2.2e-16; adj. log-OR -0.005/% blast)
    - weight_loss_pct_6mo      (r = -0.052, p = 8e-31; adj. log-OR -0.037/%)
    - crp_mg_l                 (r = -0.020, p = 6e-6; adj. log-OR -0.007/mg)
    - wbc_k_per_ul             (r = -0.017, p = 1.8e-4; adj. log-OR -0.002/k)

  Positive prognostic factors (higher ORR):
    - npm1_mutation            (ORR 23.5% vs 16.7%, p = 5.8e-70; adj. log-OR +0.44)
    - albumin_g_dl             (r = +0.023, p = 3e-7; adj. log-OR +0.12/g)

  No detectable independent association: age_years, sex_female, secondary_aml, FLT3-ITD, FLT3-TKD,
  IDH1, IDH2, LDH, NLR, hemoglobin, AST, ALT, total bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium,
  calcium. (Alkaline phosphatase showed a tiny adj. positive coefficient, p = 0.02, of negligible
  magnitude.)

2. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS
-------------------------
  - treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine: large positive marginal effect (ORR 22.0% vs 16.6%, +5.4 pp,
    p ≈ 7e-51; adj. log-OR +0.36). However, see Section 4 — this is entirely driven by one
    composite subgroup.
  - treatment_7plus3: no marginal or adjusted effect on ORR (p ~ 0.5).
  - treatment_midostaurin: no effect (ORR 18.6% vs 18.8%, p = 0.67; adj. p = 0.79).
  - treatment_gilteritinib: no effect (ORR 18.1% vs 18.9%, p = 0.14; adj. p = 0.13).
  - treatment_ivosidenib: no effect (ORR 18.8% vs 18.7%, p = 0.93; adj. p = 0.65).
  - treatment_enasidenib: no effect (ORR 18.6% vs 18.8%, p = 0.79; adj. p = 0.80).

  Treatment assignment is empirically uncorrelated with the major biomarkers (|Δ baseline rate|
  ≤ 0.016 in every treated-vs-untreated comparison), so apparent treatment effects are not
  confounded by selection.

3. BIOMARKER-MATCHED TARGETED THERAPY (the expected interactions are absent)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Biology would predict that the FLT3 inhibitors (midostaurin, gilteritinib) preferentially benefit
FLT3-ITD+ patients and that the IDH inhibitors (ivosidenib, enasidenib) preferentially benefit
IDH1+/IDH2+ patients respectively. None of these biomarker-matched interactions was observed:

  - midostaurin in FLT3-ITD+: TE = -0.015 (p = 0.18); in FLT3-ITD-: TE = +0.001
  - gilteritinib in FLT3-ITD+: TE = +0.002 (p = 0.83); in FLT3-ITD-: TE = -0.010
  - ivosidenib in IDH1+:       TE = -0.040 (p = 0.11); in IDH1-:    TE = +0.004
  - enasidenib in IDH2+:       TE = +0.004 (p = 0.89); in IDH2-:    TE = -0.002

An exhaustive 2-feature subgroup screen against each of these treatments did not surface any
positive subgroup with absolute treatment effect ≥ 0.05 and p < 0.01. The same was true for
treatment_7plus3. Conclusion: in this dataset, none of the targeted small-molecule treatments and
neither intensive chemo (7+3) produces a meaningful objective response benefit, in any subgroup.

4. THE DOMINANT FINDING — venetoclax-azacitidine effect heterogeneity
---------------------------------------------------------------------
The marginal benefit of venetoclax-azacitidine is concentrated in a single, biologically coherent
composite subgroup. The discovery path:

  Step A — Treatment × biomarker interaction screening identified four very strong moderators of
  the ven-aza effect:
    - npm1_mutation:        TE in NPM1+ = +0.187, in NPM1- = -0.004; interaction p ≈ 4e-152
    - unfit_for_intensive:  TE in unfit+ = +0.145, in unfit- = -0.008; interaction p ≈ 9e-128
    - tp53_mutation:        TE in TP53- = +0.060, in TP53+ = -0.004; interaction p ≈ ranking
    - complex_karyotype:    TE in CK- = +0.061, in CK+ = +0.012; interaction significant

  Step B — Joint stratification on the two strongest amplifiers:
    - NPM1+ AND unfit+ AND TP53-WT: TE = +0.534 (n = 5,349), p ≈ 0
    - All other 7 cells of the (NPM1, unfit, TP53) cube: TE within ±0.013 of zero
  Within the NPM1+/unfit+/TP53- cell, complex karyotype also abrogates the effect:
    - +CK: TE = +0.033 (n.s.); -CK: TE = +0.628.

  Step C — Final composite responder predicate:
        npm1_mutation = 1  AND  unfit_for_intensive = 1
        AND  tp53_mutation = 0  AND  complex_karyotype = 0
    Inside this subgroup (n = 4,531):
        ORR on venetoclax-azacitidine = 78.6% vs ORR off = 15.8%
        Treatment effect = +62.8 absolute percentage points (Fisher p ≈ 0)
    Outside this subgroup (n = 45,469):
        ORR on venetoclax-azacitidine = 16.6% vs ORR off = 16.7%
        Treatment effect = -0.3 percentage points (p = 0.42)

  Step D — Logistic regression confirmation (4-way interaction):
    treatment_venaza × NPM1 × unfit          log-OR = +2.42  (p < 1e-100)
    treatment_venaza × NPM1 × unfit × TP53   log-OR = -2.64  (p ≈ 1e-13)
  Both signs and magnitudes confirm the contingency: the joint NPM1+/unfit+ context unlocks a very
  large response, and TP53 mutation (and similarly complex karyotype) cancels it.

  Step E — The unfit_for_intensive predicate is necessary, not just adjuvant. Within the same
  molecular profile (NPM1+ AND TP53- AND CK-) but unfit_for_intensive = 0, the venetoclax-
  azacitidine TE is -0.004 (n = 6,951). The fitness label appears to mark the population in whom
  this regimen actually works in this dataset.

  Step F — The effect is unmodified by sex and age (within-subgroup TE ~ +0.63 in both halves), and
  is preserved across ECOG performance status strata (TE +0.55, +0.53, +0.51 for ECOG 0/1/2). Adding
  treatment_7plus3 on top of ven-aza inside the responder subgroup neither helps nor hurts (ORR
  79.3% with ven-aza alone vs 77.8% with ven-aza + 7+3, n.s.). Within the responder subgroup with
  ven-aza given, additional FLT3/IDH mutation status does not further raise the ~78–80% ORR
  ceiling.

  Step G — Sensitivity: removing the responder subgroup from the dataset eliminates both the
  marginal ven-aza effect and the apparent unfit_for_intensive "main effect" — the latter was
  itself a downstream consequence of carrying the responder subgroup, not an independent prognostic
  feature.

5. FINAL HYPOTHESES
-------------------
  H_final_1 (treatment effect heterogeneity, supported):
    Among AML patients meeting all of:
        npm1_mutation = 1
        AND unfit_for_intensive = 1
        AND tp53_mutation = 0
        AND complex_karyotype = 0
    treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine increases the probability of objective_response from
    approximately 16% (off treatment) to approximately 79% (on treatment), an absolute treatment
    effect of about +63 percentage points. Outside this composite subgroup, the average treatment
    effect of venetoclax-azacitidine on objective response is indistinguishable from zero.

  H_final_2 (other treatments, supported as null):
    None of treatment_midostaurin, treatment_gilteritinib, treatment_ivosidenib,
    treatment_enasidenib, or treatment_7plus3 has a clinically or statistically meaningful positive
    effect on objective_response in this cohort, either marginally, after adjustment, or in any
    biomarker-matched or systematically-searched subgroup we examined. In particular, the two FLT3
    inhibitors do not benefit FLT3-ITD-mutated patients and the two IDH inhibitors do not benefit
    IDH1- or IDH2-mutated patients in this dataset.

  Supported prognostic-only signals (not treatment-modifying): higher ECOG, complex karyotype,
  TP53 mutation, higher marrow blasts, greater 6-month weight loss, higher CRP, and higher WBC are
  each independently associated with lower ORR; NPM1 mutation and higher serum albumin are each
  independently associated with higher ORR.
