ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_aml (n=50,000)
========================================

Outcome: objective_response (binary; overall rate 0.188).
Approach: 25-iteration propose-test-refine loop using rate
comparisons, univariate and multivariable logistic regression,
treatment x feature interaction screens, and stratified subgroup
analyses with up to four-way conditioning.

----------------------------------------------------------------
1.  MAIN EFFECTS ON OBJECTIVE RESPONSE
----------------------------------------------------------------

Iterations 1-4 surveyed univariate associations between every
candidate feature and the response.

Confirmed UNFAVORABLE main effects (lower response):
  * ecog_ps: 0.231 / 0.174 / 0.129 across ECOG 0/1/2 (p<1e-72).
    Strong, monotonic, dose-response.
  * complex_karyotype: 0.168 vs 0.191, diff -0.023 (p=3e-06).
  * tp53_mutation: 0.166 vs 0.190, diff -0.024 (p=3e-05).
  * blast_pct_marrow: logit beta -0.005/% (p=2e-16).
  * wbc_k_per_ul: -0.0018 per 1k/uL (p=2e-04).
  * weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.036 per % (p=1e-30).
  * crp_mg_l: -0.006 per mg/L (p=6e-06).

Confirmed FAVORABLE main effects (higher response):
  * npm1_mutation: 0.235 vs 0.167, diff +0.067 (p<1e-50).
  * albumin_g_dl: +0.118 per g/dL (p=3e-07).
  * unfit_for_intensive: 0.221 vs 0.165 (p<1e-30) — the
    direction is OPPOSITE the usual clinical expectation.

Refuted / null:
  * age_years (p=0.88), sex_female (p=0.41), secondary_aml
    (p=0.84), LDH (p=0.35), NLR (p=0.92), and most chemistry
    panel variables (creatinine, sodium, potassium, calcium,
    BUN, AST, ALT, bilirubin, hemoglobin, alk phos all p>=0.05
    univariately).
  * FLT3-ITD, FLT3-TKD, IDH1, IDH2 mutations had no detectable
    main effects on response.

A 35-feature multivariable logistic model (iteration 6, pseudo-
R2 0.032, LL ratio p<1e-300) confirmed all the above predictors
as independent contributors with effect sizes consistent with the
univariate analyses.

----------------------------------------------------------------
2.  TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS
----------------------------------------------------------------

Crude main effects:
  * treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine: 0.220 vs 0.166,
    diff +0.054, p<1e-30.   (only treatment with a detectable
    main effect.)
  * treatment_7plus3:               0.189 vs 0.186, p=0.46. NS.
  * treatment_midostaurin:          0.186 vs 0.188, p=0.66. NS.
  * treatment_gilteritinib:         0.181 vs 0.189, p=0.13. NS.
  * treatment_ivosidenib:           0.188 vs 0.187, p=0.91. NS.
  * treatment_enasidenib:           0.186 vs 0.188, p=0.78. NS.

----------------------------------------------------------------
3.  TARGETED-THERAPY x BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS (iteration 7)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Despite biological motivation, none of the cognate
biomarker-targeted treatment interactions reached significance:
  * midostaurin x flt3_itd:       interaction p=0.20 (DiD -0.016)
  * gilteritinib x flt3_itd:      interaction p=0.32 (DiD +0.012)
  * gilteritinib x flt3_tkd:      interaction p=0.16 (DiD -0.029)
  * ivosidenib x idh1_mutation:   interaction p=0.077 (DiD
    -0.044; trend OPPOSITE expected direction — ivosidenib worse
    in IDH1+).
  * enasidenib x idh2_mutation:   interaction p=0.79 (DiD +0.006)

In short, the dataset shows none of the textbook
biomarker-targeted treatment effects.

----------------------------------------------------------------
4.  SYSTEMATIC HETEROGENEITY SEARCH FOR EACH TREATMENT
----------------------------------------------------------------

A 6 x 11 treatment-by-binary-feature interaction screen
(iteration 20) returned the following ranked results
(all logistic interaction p-values):

Top 5 by significance:
  ven/aza  x  npm1_mutation       p = 5.5e-102   beta +1.05
  ven/aza  x  unfit_for_intensive p = 1.1e-78    beta +0.88
  ven/aza  x  tp53_mutation       p = 4.7e-07    beta -0.41
  ven/aza  x  complex_karyotype   p = 6.0e-06    beta -0.31
  enasidenib x sex_female         p = 2.2e-04    beta +0.31

All other treatment-by-binary interactions had p > 0.001.
This means only ven/aza shows large-magnitude treatment-effect
heterogeneity, with a marginal sex effect for enasidenib.

The enasidenib x sex_female interaction (iteration 21) is
real but small in absolute terms: in females, enasidenib
produces 0.212 vs 0.187 (+0.025); in males, 0.165 vs 0.188
(-0.023). The direction is consistent within IDH2+ females
(0.241 vs 0.200, n=166 vs 1,959), but absolute effect sizes
are an order of magnitude smaller than ven/aza's.

----------------------------------------------------------------
5.  PRINCIPAL FINDING — VEN/AZA TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP
----------------------------------------------------------------

Iterations 8-15 progressively refined the ven/aza-responsive
subgroup through a sequence of two-way, three-way, and four-way
stratifications.

Step-by-step refinement of the responsive subgroup:

(a) ven/aza x unfit (iteration 8):
       unfit=1: 0.308 vs 0.163 (diff +0.145).
       unfit=0: 0.160 vs 0.168 (diff -0.008).
       The benefit is restricted to the unfit-for-intensive
       cohort.

(b) within unfit=1, ven x npm1 (iteration 11):
       npm1+/unfit+: ven=1 0.633 vs ven=0 0.160 (diff +0.474).
       npm1-/unfit+: ven=1 0.169 vs ven=0 0.164 (diff +0.005).
       The benefit also requires NPM1 mutation.

(c) within npm1+/unfit+, conditioning on tp53 (iteration 12):
       npm1+/unfit+/tp53-: ven=1 0.691 (n=2125) vs 0.157
                            (n=3224) — diff +0.534.
       npm1+/unfit+/tp53+: ven=1 0.188 (n=276)  vs 0.183
                            (n=361)  — diff +0.005.
       TP53 mutation completely abrogates the benefit.

(d) within npm1+/unfit+/tp53-, conditioning on complex
    karyotype (iteration 13):
       npm1+/unfit+/tp53-/CK-: ven=1 0.717 (n=2025) vs 0.161
                            (n=3052) — diff +0.556.
       npm1+/unfit+/tp53-/CK+: ven=1 0.184 (n=376)  vs 0.152
                            (n=533)  — diff +0.031.
       Complex karyotype also abrogates the benefit.

Final four-predicate subgroup (iteration 14):
   npm1_mutation=1
   AND unfit_for_intensive=1
   AND tp53_mutation=0
   AND complex_karyotype=0

   Inside subgroup (n=4,531):
     ven/aza=1: response 0.786 (n=1,790)
     ven/aza=0: response 0.158 (n=2,741)
     absolute risk difference:    +0.628
     chi-square p:                <1e-300

   Outside subgroup (n=45,469):
     ven/aza=1: response 0.164 (n=18,230)
     ven/aza=0: response 0.167 (n=27,239)
     absolute risk difference:    -0.003
     chi-square p:                0.42

   Logistic interaction (subgroup x ven/aza):
     beta = +2.99,   p < 1e-200.

Sensitivity (iteration 15): each predicate is necessary.
Removing any single predicate from the definition reduces the
ven=1 response rate within the resulting larger subgroup:
   Drop tp53-:  0.786 -> 0.717
   Drop CK-:    0.786 -> 0.691
   Drop unfit+: 0.786 -> 0.403  (largest drop)
   Drop npm1+:  0.786 -> 0.349
The subgroup is genuinely four-way; no smaller subset captures
the heterogeneity.

Within-subgroup sub-heterogeneity (iteration 16): no further
moderator survived correction. age, ECOG, sex, blasts, WBC,
albumin, LDH, CRP, FLT3, secondary AML, and IDH1 all had
ven*moderator interaction p > 0.09. IDH2 was nominally p=0.026
(ven worse if IDH2+ inside the subgroup), but this is one
test of many and not robust.

Multivariable robustness (iteration 22): adjustment for ECOG,
age, sex, blast %, WBC, albumin, weight loss, CRP, and other
treatments leaves the subgroup x ven interaction beta at ~+3.0
with p<1e-200. The interaction is not explained by confounding.

----------------------------------------------------------------
6.  RE-INTERPRETING APPARENT MAIN EFFECTS
----------------------------------------------------------------

Several "main effects" identified early are largely ARTEFACTS
of the ven/aza subgroup once the treatment-effect heterogeneity
is recognized (iterations 23-24):

  * NPM1 main effect: among ven=0 patients, npm1+ 0.160 vs
    npm1- 0.169 — actually slightly LOWER (NS). The +6.7%
    overall NPM1 advantage comes entirely from npm1+ patients
    treated with ven/aza in the responsive subgroup. NPM1 is
    a PREDICTIVE biomarker for ven/aza, not a prognostic one.

  * unfit_for_intensive favorable main effect: similarly
    explained by enrichment of ven/aza-treated, npm1+ patients
    in the unfit cohort.

  * tp53_mutation unfavorable main effect: among ven=0, tp53+
    0.169 vs tp53- 0.166 (NS). Among ven=1, tp53+ 0.164 vs
    tp53- 0.226 (diff -0.062, p<1e-10). TP53 is largely
    PREDICTIVE of LOSS of ven/aza response, not strongly
    prognostic on its own.

The remaining main effects (ECOG, blast %, WBC, albumin,
weight loss, CRP, complex karyotype) appear to be true
treatment-independent prognostic associations.

----------------------------------------------------------------
7.  TREATMENTS OUTSIDE THE RESPONSIVE SUBGROUP
----------------------------------------------------------------

Within the 45,469 patients NOT in the ven/aza-responsive
subgroup (iteration 17), no other treatment shows a measurable
benefit:
  * midostaurin   p=0.80
  * gilteritinib  p=0.08 (small negative trend)
  * ivosidenib    p=0.51
  * enasidenib    p=0.24
  * 7+3           p=0.45
The only positive treatment effect in the dataset is ven/aza
in the four-predicate subgroup.

7+3 (iteration 18) gave no benefit even within fit (unfit=0)
patients (0.167 vs 0.163), and added nothing on top of ven/aza
within the responsive subgroup (combo 0.778 vs ven-only 0.793).

----------------------------------------------------------------
8.  CONCLUSIONS
----------------------------------------------------------------

(1) The dominant clinical signal in this cohort is a single,
    very large treatment-effect heterogeneity:

       FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT HYPOTHESIS:
       Treatment with venetoclax+azacitidine increases
       objective_response by ~+0.63 absolute (0.79 vs 0.16)
       ONLY in patients meeting all four predicates:
         npm1_mutation = 1
         unfit_for_intensive = 1
         tp53_mutation = 0
         complex_karyotype = 0
       Outside this subgroup ven/aza has no measurable effect
       on objective_response.

(2) The four predicates each contribute independently. NPM1
    and unfit-for-intensive status are the most necessary
    favorable inclusions; tp53_mutation and complex_karyotype
    are unfavorable suppressor predicates whose presence
    eliminates the benefit (consistent with the brief's
    instruction to name "variables whose unfavorable value
    appears to suppress the treatment effect").

(3) No other treatment in the cohort shows a comparable
    treatment-effect subgroup. The targeted-therapy hypotheses
    (midostaurin/gilteritinib in FLT3, ivosidenib in IDH1,
    enasidenib in IDH2) are not supported in this dataset.
    The only secondary signal is a modest enasidenib x sex
    interaction (~+5% in females, ~-2% in males) that does not
    rise to clinical importance.

(4) Several apparent main-effect predictors (NPM1, unfit,
    TP53) are in fact PREDICTIVE rather than prognostic and
    should not be interpreted as treatment-independent
    survival markers. Genuine treatment-independent prognostic
    factors include ECOG, blast %, WBC, albumin, weight loss,
    CRP, and complex karyotype.
