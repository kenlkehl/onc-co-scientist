ds001_aml — Analysis Summary
==============================
Cohort: 50,000 AML patients. Outcome: objective_response (binary, overall rate 18.75%). Six treatments, six mutation/cytogenetic flags, two fitness indicators (ecog_ps, unfit_for_intensive), and 17 continuous labs/anthropometrics.

We ran 25 iterations of propose-test-refine, beginning with main-effect screens and progressing through targeted treatment×marker interactions, full systematic Tx×feature heterogeneity screens, joint subgroup discovery, and final adjusted-OR confirmations within and outside the discovered subgroup. The headline finding is a striking, single-treatment effect-modification pattern for venetoclax-azacitidine.

==============================
1. Patient-level main effects on objective_response
==============================
- age_years (iter 1): no marginal main effect (β=-0.0002/yr, p=0.88). Mean age in responders 67.98 vs non-responders 68.00.
- sex_female (iter 1): no main effect (rate 0.189 vs 0.186, p=0.42).
- ecog_ps (iter 1, 5): strongly NEGATIVE on response (β=-0.353/unit unadjusted, p=1.6e-90; β=-0.361 adjusted, p=6.5e-93). Response rates 23.1% (ECOG 0) → 17.4% (1) → 12.9% (2).
- unfit_for_intensive (iter 1): unadjusted POSITIVE (rate 0.221 vs 0.165, p=2e-55) but the sign is fully explained by VEN-AZA channeling; the adjusted main effect is also positive (β=+0.37, p=2.8e-57) because of additional positive interactions captured at later iterations.
- secondary_aml: no main effect (p=0.85).

Mutations / cytogenetics (iter 2):
- npm1_mutation: strongly POSITIVE (response 23.5% vs 16.7%, Δ=+0.067, p=5.8e-70; adjusted β=+0.43, p=1.9e-71).
- tp53_mutation: NEGATIVE (16.6% vs 19.0%, Δ=-0.024, p=3.5e-5; adjusted β=-0.19, p=3.7e-6).
- complex_karyotype: NEGATIVE (16.8% vs 19.1%, Δ=-0.023, p=3.3e-6; adjusted β=-0.17, p=5.3e-7).
- flt3_itd, flt3_tkd, idh1_mutation, idh2_mutation: NO marginal main effect (all p > 0.3).

Continuous labs (iter 3): the response-relevant signals are
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: NEGATIVE (β=-0.036, p=1.1e-30).
- blast_pct_marrow: NEGATIVE (β=-0.0049, p=2.4e-16; mean 58.5% in responders vs 60.3%).
- crp_mg_l: NEGATIVE (β=-0.0064, p=6e-6).
- albumin_g_dl: POSITIVE (β=+0.117, p=2.9e-7).
- wbc_k_per_ul: NEGATIVE (β=-0.0018, p=1.9e-4).
- alkaline_phosphatase_u_l: borderline POSITIVE (p=0.05).
- All other labs (ldh, nlr, hemoglobin, ast/alt/bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, electrolytes, calcium): not significant (p > 0.1). Most of these signals survive multivariable adjustment.

==============================
2. Treatment main effects (iter 4–5)
==============================
Univariate (chi-square):
- treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine (n=20,020): response 22.0% vs 16.6% (Δ=+0.054, p=6.6e-51).
- treatment_midostaurin, treatment_gilteritinib, treatment_ivosidenib, treatment_enasidenib, treatment_7plus3: all NULL marginally (|Δ| < 0.01, all p > 0.13).

In a logistic regression simultaneously adjusting for age, sex, ECOG, fitness, all mutations, and key labs (iter 5), only treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine retains an independent positive effect (β=+0.358, OR≈1.43, p=6.8e-53). The other five treatments have NO independent main-effect signal (all |β| < 0.05, p > 0.14). Strong covariate effects (negative): higher ecog_ps, complex_karyotype, tp53_mutation, blast_pct_marrow, wbc, crp; (positive): npm1_mutation, albumin, unfit_for_intensive (the unfit coefficient flips positive after adjustment because VEN-AZA — concentrated in unfit patients — dominates the pooled effect).

==============================
3. Targeted Tx × biomarker interactions — surprising NULLS (iter 6, 9, 14, 18)
==============================
Despite the textbook-targeted assignments (midostaurin/gilteritinib for FLT3, ivosidenib for IDH1, enasidenib for IDH2), NONE of the targeted-Tx × respective-biomarker interactions are significant in this dataset:

- midostaurin × flt3_itd interaction β=-0.106, p=0.20. Within flt3_itd=1, midostaurin on=17.6% vs off=19.1% (Δ=-0.015, p=0.19). Adjusted β within FLT3-ITD+ subset = -0.10, p=0.17.
- gilteritinib × flt3_itd β=+0.074, p=0.36; × flt3_tkd β=-0.225, p=0.14; combined FLT3-any: gilt on=18.2% vs off=18.8% (Δ=-0.006, p=0.57). Adjusted within FLT3-ITD+ p=0.78.
- ivosidenib × idh1_mutation β=-0.329, p=0.058; within idh1=1: ivo on=15.4% vs off=19.4% (Δ=-0.040, p=0.11). Adjusted β within IDH1+ subset = -0.31, p=0.066. The trend is mildly negative, the opposite of the expected mechanism-driven benefit.
- enasidenib × idh2_mutation β=+0.029, p=0.85; within idh2=1: ena on=19.5% vs off=19.1% (Δ=+0.004, p=0.92). Adjusted β within IDH2+ = +0.018, p=0.90.

A small marginal ivosidenib × npm1_mutation interaction (p=0.029) and an enasidenib × sex_female interaction (p=3e-4) show up in the systematic screen, but stratified analyses reveal these are tiny opposite-direction shifts of the order of ±2 percentage points — most plausibly noise or a data-generating quirk, not clinically meaningful effect modification.

CONCLUSION FOR TARGETED TREATMENTS: none of the four FLT3/IDH-targeted agents shows a detectable response advantage in this cohort, even within their textbook biomarker-positive subgroup. We did not identify a treatment-effect subgroup for these four agents.

==============================
4. Treatment-effect heterogeneity for venetoclax-azacitidine (iter 7, 10, 11, 12, 14, 15, 16, 19)
==============================
Comprehensive interaction screening consistently surfaces VEN-AZA as the only treatment with substantial heterogeneous effects, and four binary modifiers stand out — two amplifiers and two suppressors:

(a) Amplifying modifiers — VEN-AZA × subgroup positive interactions:
  - npm1_mutation: interaction β=+1.077, p=4.9e-104. Stratified: in npm1=1, VEN-AZA on=34.7% vs off=16.0% (Δ=+18.7 pp, p=2e-154); in npm1=0, Δ=-0.004, p=0.34. VEN-AZA only works when NPM1 is mutated.
  - unfit_for_intensive: interaction β=+0.902, p=2.7e-80. Stratified: in unfit=1, VEN-AZA on=30.8% vs off=16.3% (Δ=+14.5 pp, p=7e-130); in unfit=0, Δ=-0.008, p=0.083. VEN-AZA only works in patients flagged unfit for intensive therapy.

(b) Suppressing modifiers — VEN-AZA × subgroup negative interactions:
  - tp53_mutation: interaction β=-0.438, p=9.6e-08. In tp53=0: Δ=+6.0 pp (p=8e-57). In tp53=1: Δ=-0.004 (p=0.77). TP53 mutation removes the benefit.
  - complex_karyotype: interaction β=-0.323, p=2.3e-06. In complex_karyotype=0: Δ=+6.1 pp (p=3e-55). In complex_karyotype=1: Δ=+0.012 (p=0.20). Complex karyotype removes the benefit.

(c) Non-modifiers (no Tx-effect heterogeneity): age_years, sex_female, ecog_ps, secondary_aml, flt3_itd, flt3_tkd, idh1_mutation, idh2_mutation — all interaction p-values > 0.3.

(d) Continuous-lab interactions for VEN-AZA: only blast_pct_marrow shows a small positive interaction (β=+0.0033, p=0.007); all other labs (wbc, albumin, ldh, crp, nlr, hemoglobin) p > 0.06.

Triple-interaction logistic model (iter 12): The term treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine × unfit_for_intensive × npm1_mutation has β=+2.165, p=4.8e-94 (z=20.6). The marginal venetoclax-azacitidine main effect (i.e., for unfit=0, npm1=0) is essentially zero (β=-0.067, p=0.08). The two two-way interactions in this saturated model are not individually significant — the entire signal is concentrated in the joint (unfit=1 ∩ npm1=1) cell.

Within-subgroup further heterogeneity (iter 19): Within (unfit=1 ∩ npm1=1):
  - VEN-AZA × age_years: β=-0.003, p=0.59 (no further age effect inside the responder subgroup).
  - VEN-AZA × tp53_mutation: β=-2.61, p=4.2e-32 (TP53 nearly abolishes the benefit even within unfit/NPM1+).
  - VEN-AZA × complex_karyotype: β=-2.48, p=1.1e-36 (complex karyotype nearly abolishes the benefit even within unfit/NPM1+).

==============================
5. Final supergroup definition and ablation (iter 11, 16, 21, 24, 25)
==============================
Joint subgroup: SUPERGROUP = (unfit_for_intensive=1) ∧ (npm1_mutation=1) ∧ (tp53_mutation=0) ∧ (complex_karyotype=0).

In supergroup (n=4,531):
  - VEN-AZA on (n=1,790): 78.6% objective_response
  - VEN-AZA off (n=2,741): 15.8%
  - Δ = +62.8 percentage points (chi2 p ≈ 0)
  - Adjusted (controlling for age, sex, ECOG, secondary_aml, FLT3, IDH, albumin, blast_pct_marrow, crp): logit β=+3.04 (OR ≈ 20.8), p ≈ 0 (iter 24).

Outside supergroup (n=45,469):
  - VEN-AZA on: 16.4%, off: 16.7%, Δ=-0.003, chi2 p=0.42 (iter 11).
  - Adjusted: logit β=-0.019 (OR ≈ 0.98), p=0.47 (iter 25). The benefit is fully contained in the supergroup.

Predicate ablation (iter 16): violating ANY single predicate (set unfit→0, or npm1→0, or tp53→1, or complex_karyotype→1, holding the other three) eliminates the effect:
  - violate unfit:  Δ=-0.004 (p=0.70)
  - violate npm1:   Δ=+0.002 (p=0.80)
  - violate tp53:   Δ=+0.004 (p=0.99)
  - violate ck:     Δ=+0.033 (p=0.25)

Conversely, dropping a single predicate from the definition (broadening the subgroup) keeps the effect positive but smaller, because the broader subgroup includes patients who fail one of the conditions and dilute the result. For example:
  - {unfit=1 ∩ npm1=1} alone: Δ=+0.474 (n=5,986)
  - + tp53=0: Δ=+0.534
  - + complex_karyotype=0: Δ=+0.556
  - + both suppressor exclusions (full supergroup): Δ=+0.628

Whole-cohort logistic confirmation (iter 21): treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine × supergroup interaction β=+3.04 (p=1.1e-296). Marginal VEN-AZA main effect (outside supergroup) β=-0.018, p=0.48. This is the single most decisive interaction term in the dataset.

==============================
6. Other findings
==============================
- 7+3 × complex_karyotype (iter 8, 14, 17): negative interaction β=-0.234, p=5.7e-4. Within complex_karyotype=1: 7+3 on=15.5% vs off=17.9% (Δ=-0.023, p=0.008). Within complex_karyotype=0: Δ=+0.007 (p=0.061). Within (tp53=0 ∩ complex_karyotype=0), 7+3 on=19.8% vs off=19.0% (Δ=+0.008, p=0.049). 7+3 has at most a small benefit in favorable cytogenetics and is mildly harmful in complex karyotype, but the absolute effect sizes are modest.
- 7+3 × age_years, 7+3 × ecog_ps, 7+3 × unfit_for_intensive: all NULL.
- VEN-AZA × secondary_aml: NULL (Δ similar in secondary and de novo AML).

==============================
7. Conclusions
==============================
The dominant treatment–biomarker–outcome relationship in ds001_aml is for venetoclax-azacitidine. Its overall response advantage (~5 pp) is driven entirely by a single subgroup defined by four predicates simultaneously:

  unfit_for_intensive = 1 AND npm1_mutation = 1 AND tp53_mutation = 0 AND complex_karyotype = 0

In that subgroup the response rate jumps from ~16% off-treatment to ~79% on-treatment (Δ ≈ +63 percentage points; adjusted OR ≈ 21). Outside that subgroup, venetoclax-azacitidine has no detectable effect. The two amplifying predicates (unfit and NPM1+) are both required; the two suppressors (TP53 mutation and complex karyotype) each independently nullify the benefit even within the unfit/NPM1+ stratum.

The four targeted agents in the dataset (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib) show no detectable benefit even in their canonical biomarker-positive subgroups (FLT3-ITD, FLT3-TKD, IDH1, IDH2 respectively), with all interactions and stratified differences null or marginally negative. 7+3 has at most a small independent effect, with mild evidence of harm specifically in complex_karyotype=1 patients.

The strongest patient-level prognostic predictors of objective_response, independent of treatment, are: ecog_ps (negative), npm1_mutation (positive), tp53_mutation (negative), complex_karyotype (negative), blast_pct_marrow (negative), wbc_k_per_ul (negative), crp_mg_l (negative), weight_loss_pct_6mo (negative), and albumin_g_dl (positive).
