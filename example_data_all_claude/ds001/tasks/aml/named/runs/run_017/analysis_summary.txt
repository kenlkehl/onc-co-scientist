Analysis summary for ds001_aml (n = 50,000, single binary outcome: objective_response)
======================================================================================

Overall response rate is 18.75%. The 50k-patient cohort has six (potentially co-administered)
treatment indicators (treatment_midostaurin, treatment_gilteritinib, treatment_ivosidenib,
treatment_enasidenib, treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine, treatment_7plus3) with prevalences of
8-45%. A patient may receive 0-6 of them; 1-3 simultaneous treatments is typical.

I worked through 25 iterations: prognostic biomarker scans (iters 1-5), main treatment effects
and an explicit randomization-of-treatment check (iters 6-7), single-modifier treatment x
biomarker interactions (iters 8-11), joint multivariable interaction model (iter 12), exhaustive
treatment-by-biomarker subgroup screens for the five non-ven_aza drugs (iters 13-15, 22),
continuous moderators (iter 16), an adjusted master logistic model (iter 17), increasingly tight
multi-feature subgroup definitions for ven_aza (iters 18-21), tree/rule-based confirmation
(iter 23), and final best- and null-subgroup statements with cross-treatment closure (iters
24-25).

PROGNOSTIC FEATURES (iters 1-5)
-------------------------------
Binary biomarkers significantly associated with objective_response (chi-square):
  - npm1_mutation: 23.5% vs 16.7% (diff +6.7 pp, p=5.8e-70).  POSITIVE
  - unfit_for_intensive: 22.1% vs 16.5% (diff +5.6 pp, p=1.9e-55).  POSITIVE
    (mostly because unfit patients are the natural ven_aza population, and ven_aza works.)
  - tp53_mutation: 16.6% vs 19.0% (diff -2.4 pp, p=3.5e-5).  NEGATIVE
  - complex_karyotype: 16.8% vs 19.1% (diff -2.3 pp, p=3.3e-6).  NEGATIVE
  - sex_female, secondary_aml, FLT3-ITD/TKD, IDH1, IDH2: no detectable main effect.

Continuous features associated with objective_response (Welch t-test on means by responder
status):
  - ecog_ps: -0.157 (responders 0.67 vs non-responders 0.83; p=1e-91). NEGATIVE.
  - blast_pct_marrow: -1.80 (p=2.3e-16). NEGATIVE.
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.51 (p=8e-31). NEGATIVE.
  - albumin_g_dl: +0.029 (p=2.8e-7). POSITIVE.
  - wbc_k_per_ul: -1.11 (p=1.8e-4). NEGATIVE.
  - crp_mg_l: -0.48 (p=6e-6). NEGATIVE.
  - age, LDH, hemoglobin, NLR, ALT/AST/total bilirubin, creatinine/BUN/sodium/potassium/calcium,
    alkaline phosphatase: no clinically meaningful association (p>=0.05 or |effect| trivial).

These results were corroborated by a multivariable logistic model (iter 17) with effects that
remained significant after mutual adjustment.

TREATMENT-ASSIGNMENT CHECK (iter 7)
-----------------------------------
Across all 6 treatments x 9 binary biomarkers, the absolute differences in treatment prevalence
between biomarker-positive and biomarker-negative patients are <=1.4 percentage points -
consistent with effectively random treatment assignment with respect to baseline biomarkers. No
confounding adjustment is needed for treatment effect estimation.

MAIN TREATMENT EFFECTS (iter 6)
-------------------------------
Only treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine has a substantial main effect on objective_response:
  - venetoclax_azacitidine: 22.0% on vs 16.6% off (diff +5.4 pp, p<1e-50). SUPPORTED.
  - 7+3: 18.9% vs 18.6% (p=0.40). NULL.
  - midostaurin: 18.6% vs 18.8% (p=0.74). NULL.
  - gilteritinib: 18.1% vs 18.9% (p=0.11). NULL (slight non-significant negative trend).
  - ivosidenib: 18.8% vs 18.7% (p=0.91). NULL.
  - enasidenib: 18.6% vs 18.8% (p=0.80). NULL.

After multivariable adjustment (iter 17) the picture is identical: ven_aza beta=+0.361 (highly
significant), all other treatment betas are within +/-0.05 of zero and non-significant.

TREATMENT EFFECT HETEROGENEITY FOR VEN_AZA (iters 8-12, 16, 18-21)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Single-modifier interaction tests (logit objective_response ~ ven_aza * modifier) revealed four
strong, statistically robust modifiers, all in directions that match what the literature would
predict for venetoclax-azacitidine in AML:

  Modifier             Sign  Logistic interaction coef  p-value
  npm1_mutation        +     +1.055                     5.5e-102
  unfit_for_intensive  +     +0.883                     1.1e-78
  tp53_mutation        -     -0.409                     4.7e-7
  complex_karyotype    -     -0.306                     6.0e-6

  flt3_itd, idh1, idh2, secondary_aml, sex_female: no interaction (all p>=0.4).

When fitted jointly (iter 12), all four interactions remain significant after mutual adjustment
(npm1 p=2.6e-108, unfit p=2.5e-89, tp53 p=1.1e-8, CK p=8.4e-7), so the four modifiers act
roughly multiplicatively and independently on the log-odds scale.

Continuous moderators (iter 16): blast_pct_marrow shows a weak positive interaction with ven_aza
(coef +0.0035 per percentage point, p=0.0035) and weight_loss_pct_6mo a borderline positive one
(p=0.04). ECOG, age, albumin, LDH, hemoglobin, CRP, WBC do not modify the ven_aza effect once
the four binary modifiers are accounted for.

Subgroup-mean response rates make the heterogeneity vivid:

  Whole cohort                                          20020   29980  0.220   0.166   +0.054
  NPM1+                                                 6025    8989   0.347   0.160   +0.187
  unfit_for_intensive                                   8013    12043  0.308   0.163   +0.145
  TP53+                                                 2048    2937   0.164   0.167   -0.004
  complex_karyotype+                                    3007    4455   0.175   0.163   +0.012
  NPM1+ AND unfit                                       2401    3585   0.633   0.160   +0.474
  NPM1+ AND unfit AND TP53-                             2125    3224   0.691   0.157   +0.534

  unfit AND TP53- AND CK-                               6078    9250   0.349   0.162   +0.186

  NPM1- AND fit (neither favorable modifier)            8383    12533  0.162   0.172   -0.010

Several things stand out:
  1. The most extreme positive subgroup, NPM1+/unfit/TP53-/CK-, has an absolute treatment effect
     of +62.8 percentage points (78.6% on ven_aza vs 15.8% off). That is roughly 12x the
     population-average ven_aza effect.
  2. Each of the four predicates is necessary for the maximal effect:
       - dropping the unfit predicate (NPM1+/TP53-/CK- only): effect drops to +0.243.
       - allowing TP53+ within NPM1+/unfit: effect collapses to +0.014 (p=0.49).
       - allowing CK+ within NPM1+/unfit: effect drops to ~+0.05.
  3. TP53+ or complex_karyotype+ patients gain nothing from ven_aza (combined diff +0.006,
     p=0.36). This is the suppression subgroup.
  4. In NPM1- AND fit patients (the "neither favorable modifier" cell), there is a small negative
     trend (diff -0.010, p=0.067), consistent with ven_aza being preferentially used in fit
     patients with a default-prognosis profile not recapitulating its niche.

Tree/rule-based subgroup discovery (iter 23) re-derives the same four splits and ranks them in
the same order; no continuous variable contributes a meaningful additional split.

SUBGROUP HETEROGENEITY FOR THE OTHER FIVE TREATMENTS (iters 13-15, 22, 25)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
For each of midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, and 7+3, I screened both the
canonical "matched" biomarker subgroup (e.g., FLT3+ for midostaurin/gilteritinib, IDH1+ for
ivosidenib, IDH2+ for enasidenib) and combinations with fit/unfit, TP53, CK. Best per-treatment
positive signals:

  Treatment       Best subgroup                              n_on   n_off  diff   p
  midostaurin     FLT3+ AND fit AND TP53- AND CK-            899    4980   -0.017 0.21
  gilteritinib    FLT3+ AND unfit                            766    4428   +0.013 0.42
  ivosidenib      IDH1+ AND unfit                            137    1300   -0.059 0.12
  enasidenib      IDH2+ AND unfit                            141    1748   +0.060 0.11
  7+3             complex_karyotype+ (negative effect)       3415   4047   -0.023 0.008

None of these targeted matches reaches significance in the expected positive direction. The
nearest-to-significant positive subgroup is enasidenib in IDH2+/unfit (+0.060, p=0.108) and
gilteritinib in FLT3+/unfit (+0.013, p=0.42); both are underpowered. 7+3 is mildly harmful in
complex_karyotype+ patients (diff -0.023, p=0.008), consistent with intensive chemotherapy
underperforming in adverse-cytogenetic disease, but no positive subgroup emerges for 7+3 either.

FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT-SUBGROUP STATEMENTS (iters 24-25)
---------------------------------------------------------
Final best-supported positive subgroup hypothesis for objective_response and
treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine:
   Patients with npm1_mutation == 1 AND unfit_for_intensive == 1 AND tp53_mutation == 0 AND
   complex_karyotype == 0 derive a substantially higher objective_response rate from
   treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine than off. The treatment effect is positive (sign +) with
   absolute magnitude ~+0.628 (78.6% vs 15.8%, p<1e-300).

Final null subgroup statement for ven_aza:
   Patients with tp53_mutation == 1 OR complex_karyotype == 1 do not benefit from
   treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine (diff +0.006, p=0.357).

For midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, and 7+3, no biomarker-defined subgroup
in this dataset showed a robust positive treatment effect on objective_response.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. Of the six treatments in the cohort, only venetoclax-azacitidine is associated with a higher
   objective response rate at the population level, and the entire treatment-effect signal is
   concentrated in patients with NPM1 mutation and/or who are flagged unfit_for_intensive,
   provided they do not also carry TP53 mutation or complex karyotype.

2. The combined NPM1+/unfit/TP53-/CK- rule maximizes treatment benefit (~63 pp absolute uplift
   in response rate). This is consistent with the modern clinical understanding of
   venetoclax-azacitidine: maximal benefit in NPM1-mutated, older/unfit patients with otherwise
   favorable cytogenetics, and minimal-to-no benefit in TP53-mutated or complex-karyotype AML.

3. Targeted FLT3 (midostaurin, gilteritinib) and IDH1/2 (ivosidenib, enasidenib) treatments do
   not show measurable benefit in their canonical biomarker subgroups in this dataset; either
   the treatments lack benefit on the objective_response endpoint as encoded here, or sample
   sizes within the matched subgroups (n=137-1909 on treatment) limit power to detect modest
   effects. 7+3 has no main effect and is mildly worse in complex-karyotype patients.

4. Prognostic features for response, independent of treatment, include better performance
   status (low ECOG), lower marrow blast burden, higher albumin, lower CRP/WBC/weight loss,
   and presence of NPM1 mutation; complex karyotype and TP53 mutation are negative prognostic.
