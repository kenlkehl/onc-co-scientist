ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_aml (50,000 patients, single binary outcome: objective_response)
==========================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Overall objective_response rate in the cohort: 18.75%. Treatments recorded include
intensive induction (treatment_7plus3), the venetoclax + azacitidine regimen
(treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine), the FLT3 inhibitors midostaurin and gilteritinib,
and the IDH inhibitors ivosidenib (IDH1) and enasidenib (IDH2). Mutation features
include npm1_mutation, tp53_mutation, flt3_itd, flt3_tkd, idh1_mutation, idh2_mutation,
along with complex_karyotype, secondary_aml, and unfit_for_intensive. 25 iterations of
hypothesis-test-refine were run, ending in a systematic treatment x feature interaction
screen and a multi-feature subgroup search for each treatment.

MAIN-EFFECT FINDINGS (objective_response)
-----------------------------------------
* ECOG performance status — strong negative association: 23.10% (ECOG 0) -> 17.43%
  (ECOG 1) -> 12.91% (ECOG 2); chi-square p=1.79e-90.
* npm1_mutation+ -> +6.74 percentage point (pp) increase in response (p=5.79e-70).
* tp53_mutation+ -> -2.42 pp (p=3.51e-05).
* complex_karyotype+ -> -2.29 pp (p=3.31e-06).
* unfit_for_intensive+ -> +5.59 pp (p=1.88e-55) — paradoxical and entirely driven
  by selective ven/aza assignment (see below).
* sex_female, age_years, secondary_aml, flt3_itd, flt3_tkd, idh1_mutation, idh2_mutation
  all show no detectable main effect (p > 0.30 in each case).
* Continuous biomarkers — significant negative associations with response:
  blast_pct_marrow (beta=-0.0049/%, p=2.4e-16), weight_loss_pct_6mo
  (beta=-0.036/pt, p=1.1e-30), wbc_k_per_ul (beta=-0.0018/(K/uL), p=1.9e-04),
  crp_mg_l (beta=-0.0064/(mg/L), p=6.1e-06). Positive: albumin_g_dl
  (beta=+0.118/(g/dL), p=2.9e-07). Hemoglobin, LDH, NLR, AST, ALT, bilirubin,
  creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, alkaline phosphatase: no significant
  signal (all p > 0.05 except alk phos borderline at 0.051).

TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS
----------------------
* treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine: +5.35 pp overall (21.96% vs 16.61%),
  chi-square p=6.62e-51. ONLY treatment with a significant overall effect.
* treatment_7plus3: +0.26 pp (p=0.465). Null overall.
* treatment_midostaurin: -0.21 pp (p=0.673). Null overall.
* treatment_gilteritinib: -0.73 pp (p=0.139). Null overall.
* treatment_ivosidenib: +0.07 pp (p=0.928). Null overall.
* treatment_enasidenib: -0.18 pp (p=0.791). Null overall.

TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY (interaction screen)
---------------------------------------------------
A systematic logistic-regression interaction test was run for every pairing of the
six treatments with each of ten binary clinical/genetic features (60 tests).
After Bonferroni correction (alpha = 8.3e-04), the surviving interactions were:

  * ven/aza x npm1_mutation       beta=+1.055, p=5.50e-102  (positive: greatly enhances)
  * ven/aza x unfit_for_intensive beta=+0.883, p=1.14e-78   (positive: greatly enhances)
  * ven/aza x tp53_mutation       beta=-0.409, p=4.67e-07   (negative: abolishes)
  * ven/aza x complex_karyotype   beta=-0.306, p=6.00e-06   (negative: attenuates)
  * enasidenib x sex_female       beta=+0.315, p=2.23e-04   (positive but biologically unexplained)

The 7+3 x complex_karyotype interaction (beta=-0.213, p=1.5e-03) just misses
Bonferroni but is the largest non-ven/aza signal and is biologically plausible
(intensive chemotherapy is less effective in adverse cytogenetics).

NOTABLY ABSENT: the canonical drug-target pairings produced no benefit:
  * midostaurin x flt3_itd       interaction p=0.195, direction NEGATIVE.
  * gilteritinib x flt3_itd      interaction p=0.323, direction trivially positive.
  * midostaurin/gilteritinib x flt3_tkd: both non-significant, both directionally negative.
  * ivosidenib x idh1_mutation   interaction p=0.077, direction NEGATIVE
                                  (ivosidenib in IDH1+: response 15.4% vs 19.4%, diff -4.0 pp).
  * enasidenib x idh2_mutation   interaction p=0.788, direction trivially positive.

SUBGROUP REFINEMENT FOR VEN/AZA — THE KEY FINDING
--------------------------------------------------
The ven/aza effect is fully concentrated in patients meeting all four predicates:

   npm1_mutation==1  AND  unfit_for_intensive==1  AND  tp53_mutation==0
                      AND  complex_karyotype==0

Stratified response rates:

  Subgroup (n=4531, ~9.1% of cohort):
      ven/aza  : 78.60% (n=1790)
      no ven/aza: 15.83% (n=2741)
      Difference: +62.77 pp, chi-square p < 1e-300

  Outside this subgroup (n=45469, ~90.9% of cohort):
      ven/aza  : 16.40% (n=18230)
      no ven/aza: 16.69% (n=27239)
      Difference: -0.29 pp (essentially null)

The pattern was built up in stages:
  * Adding NPM1+ alone: ven/aza +18.69 pp inside vs -0.39 pp outside.
  * Adding NPM1+ AND unfit: +47.35 pp inside (n=2401 ven/aza-treated).
  * Adding TP53-WT: +53.39 pp inside (n=2125).
  * Adding non-CK: +62.77 pp inside (n=1790).

A multivariable logistic restricted to NPM1+ unfit (n=5986) confirms the result:
  ven/aza beta = +2.439 (OR ~ 11.5), p < 1e-300, after adjustment for age, ECOG,
  TP53, and complex_karyotype. TP53 mutation (beta=-1.39) and complex karyotype
  (beta=-1.59) act as approximately independent log-odds resistance markers within
  the responder population.

Continuous biomarker x ven/aza interactions: only marrow blast %
(beta=+0.068, p=0.0035) and (borderline) weight loss (p=0.039) modify the effect;
the categorical mutation/fitness landscape carries the heterogeneity.

VEN/AZA EFFECT BY ECOG (stratified, not used as the responder-defining variable):
  ECOG 0: +5.97 pp; ECOG 1: +5.13 pp; ECOG 2: +4.89 pp. Benefit is consistent
  across ECOG strata; ECOG was not chosen as a defining predicate because the
  unfit_for_intensive flag is the more sharply discriminating clinical variable.

OTHER TREATMENTS — FINAL SUBGROUP HYPOTHESES
--------------------------------------------
* treatment_7plus3: no responder subgroup found. Only meaningful heterogeneity is
  worse performance in complex_karyotype+ (within CK+: -2.32 pp; within CK-: +0.72 pp).
* treatment_midostaurin: null overall and within FLT3-ITD+ (-1.50 pp, ns) or
  FLT3-TKD+ (-0.52 pp, ns). No identified responder subgroup.
* treatment_gilteritinib: null overall; in FLT3-ITD+ +0.23 pp (ns); in FLT3-TKD+
  -3.49 pp (ns). No identified responder subgroup.
* treatment_ivosidenib: null overall; within IDH1+ directional effect is NEGATIVE
  (-3.99 pp, interaction p=0.077). No subgroup with positive effect identified.
* treatment_enasidenib: null overall; within IDH2+ +0.37 pp (ns). The only
  nominal interaction is enasidenib x sex_female (beta=+0.315, p=2.2e-04;
  females +2.45 pp, males -2.33 pp). With ~60 interaction tests, this is
  plausibly multiplicity-driven and lacks a biological mechanism (three-way
  enasidenib x sex x IDH2 p=0.616). Treat as exploratory.

TP53+ PATIENTS — TREATMENT-REFRACTORY GROUP
-------------------------------------------
Within the TP53-mutated population (n=4985), no treatment improved response
beyond ~2 pp in any direction:
  ven/aza -0.36, 7+3 -0.34, midostaurin +0.27, gilteritinib +0.53,
  ivosidenib +0.06, enasidenib -2.20. TP53 mutation is the strongest single
  marker of treatment refractoriness in this cohort.

CONCLUSIONS
-----------
1. The single dominant signal in this dataset is that venetoclax + azacitidine
   produces a very large response benefit (~+63 percentage points absolute) in
   the joint subgroup defined by:
        npm1_mutation==1 AND unfit_for_intensive==1 AND
        tp53_mutation==0 AND complex_karyotype==0
   and essentially no benefit anywhere else. TP53 mutation and complex karyotype
   are independent resistance markers — both are required to be absent to
   realise the full effect.

2. Adverse molecular features (TP53 mutation, complex karyotype) and worse
   clinical fitness (higher ECOG, higher marrow blast burden, lower albumin,
   greater weight loss, higher CRP/WBC) are associated with lower response
   regardless of treatment.

3. The classical targeted-therapy / mutation pairings (FLT3 inhibitors with
   FLT3-ITD/TKD, IDH inhibitors with IDH1/IDH2) do NOT exhibit the expected
   positive interactions in this cohort. Ivosidenib is directionally HARMFUL
   in IDH1+ patients in these data.

4. Best-supported final treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis (per protocol):
   FOR treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine, the responder subgroup is patients with
   simultaneously npm1_mutation==1, unfit_for_intensive==1, tp53_mutation==0,
   and complex_karyotype==0; expected absolute increase in objective_response
   is approximately +63 percentage points relative to the same subgroup not
   receiving ven/aza. For all other recorded treatments, no subgroup with a
   robustly positive effect on objective_response was identified.
