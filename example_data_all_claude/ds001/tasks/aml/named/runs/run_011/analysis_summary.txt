Analysis summary — ds001_aml (n=50,000; outcome: objective_response)
====================================================================

Cohort orientation
------------------
The cohort comprises 50,000 AML patients with one binary outcome
(objective_response) at a baseline rate of 18.75%. Six treatments are
recorded: treatment_7plus3 (45.2%), treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine
(40.0%), treatment_midostaurin (15.1%), treatment_gilteritinib (15.1%),
treatment_ivosidenib (7.9%), treatment_enasidenib (7.9%). Patients can
receive multiple treatments simultaneously (mean 1.7 per patient). No
missing values are present. Crucially, treatment assignment is
essentially independent of biomarker status (e.g., midostaurin is given
to 14.7% of FLT3-ITD+ patients vs 15.2% of FLT3-ITD- patients), so the
data behave as if treatments are randomized — interaction estimates can
be read causally rather than confounded by indication.

Iteration trajectory and findings
---------------------------------

Iterations 1-3 — Treatment main effects.
Only treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine showed a main effect on
objective_response: 21.96% on VEN/AZA vs 16.61% off (absolute +5.35 pp,
two-prop p < 1e-300). All other treatments were null at the cohort
level: treatment_7plus3 +0.26 pp (p=0.46), treatment_midostaurin
-0.21 pp (p=0.66), treatment_gilteritinib -0.73 pp (p=0.14),
treatment_ivosidenib +0.07 pp (p=0.91), treatment_enasidenib -0.18 pp
(p=0.78). VEN/AZA is therefore the only treatment with a detectable
average effect; all others appear inert in the unselected cohort.

Iterations 4-5 — Feature main effects on objective_response.
Three binary features had highly significant main effects: npm1_mutation
+6.74 pp (23.46% vs 16.73%, p<1e-300), tp53_mutation -2.42 pp
(16.57% vs 18.99%, p=3.2e-5), complex_karyotype -2.29 pp (16.81% vs
19.09%, p=3.1e-6). Strikingly, unfit_for_intensive showed a +5.59 pp
HIGHER objective_response (22.10% vs 16.51%, p<1e-300) — a direction
opposite to the clinical expectation, flagging confounding by
treatment effect. Other binary features (sex_female, secondary_aml,
flt3_itd, flt3_tkd, idh1_mutation, idh2_mutation) were null on
objective_response in the unselected cohort.

Iterations 6-7 — Continuous biomarkers.
ECOG performance status had the strongest baseline gradient on
objective_response (logit coef -0.353/unit, p=1.6e-90; rates 23.1% /
17.4% / 12.9% across ECOG 0/1/2). Higher blast_pct_marrow (coef
-0.00492, p=2.4e-16), weight_loss_pct_6mo (p=1.7e-33), wbc_k_per_ul
(p=1.5e-4), and crp_mg_l (p=1.6e-6) all predicted lower response;
higher albumin_g_dl predicted higher response (p=2.9e-7). Age, NLR,
hemoglobin, and routine organ-function labs (creatinine, bilirubin, AST,
ALT, sodium, potassium, calcium, BUN, alkaline phosphatase, LDH) were
not associated with response. These features capture baseline
prognosis but are not, by themselves, treatment effects.

Iterations 8-10 — Treatment-effect heterogeneity for VEN/AZA.
The unfit_for_intensive direction reversal flagged in iteration 4
pointed at a strong VEN/AZA × unfit interaction. Stratified rates
confirmed it: among unfit patients, OR rose from 16.30% off VEN/AZA to
30.81% on VEN/AZA (+14.5 pp); among fit patients VEN/AZA had no effect
(16.05% on vs 16.83% off). Logistic interaction coef +0.883
(p=1.1e-78). Similarly, VEN/AZA × npm1_mutation interaction coef
+1.055 (p=5.5e-102): in NPM1+ patients VEN/AZA raised OR from 16.0% to
34.7% (+18.7 pp), while in NPM1- patients VEN/AZA had no effect.
TP53 mutation and complex_karyotype each ATTENUATED the VEN/AZA
benefit: VEN/AZA × tp53_mutation coef -0.409 (p=4.7e-7), VEN/AZA ×
complex_karyotype coef -0.306 (p=6.0e-6). All four interaction
hypotheses were strongly supported in the directions predicted.

Iterations 11-12 — Other treatments' canonical biomarker interactions.
Each clinically expected pairing was tested: midostaurin × flt3_itd
coef -0.106 (p=0.19, null), gilteritinib × flt3_itd coef +0.079
(p=0.32, null), ivosidenib × idh1_mutation coef -0.304 (p=0.077, in
the wrong direction and not significant), enasidenib × idh2_mutation
coef +0.039 (p=0.79, null). 7+3 was tested against ECOG and against
unfit_for_intensive: both interactions null (p=0.66 and p=0.56).
Conclusion: the canonical targeted-therapy effects (FLT3 inhibitors in
FLT3-mutated AML, IDH inhibitors in IDH-mutated AML, intensive 7+3 in
fit patients) are NOT visible in this cohort.

Iterations 13-14 — Reinterpreting the NPM1 and unfit main effects.
Stripping VEN/AZA from each subgroup eliminated the apparent main
effects: NPM1+ off-VEN/AZA 16.00% vs NPM1- off-VEN/AZA 17.05% (NPM1
slightly underperforms in the absence of VEN/AZA). Similarly unfit
off-VEN/AZA 16.30% vs fit off-VEN/AZA 16.83%. Both 'main effects'
turned out to be entirely produced by the VEN/AZA interaction with
those features.

Iterations 15-16 — Joint subgroup discovery.
The VEN/AZA effect is concentrated in unfit AND NPM1+ patients
(absolute +37 pp in that 2-way cell). Within unfit + NPM1+, further
stratification by tp53_mutation and complex_karyotype showed the
benefit collapses entirely if EITHER tp53_mutation=1 OR
complex_karyotype=1. In unfit/NPM1+/TP53-/complex-: 78.6% on VEN/AZA
vs 15.8% off (+62.8 pp); in any cell where TP53+ or complex+, VEN/AZA
contrast is < 4 pp.

Iteration 17 — Target-subgroup indicator.
Defining target = (unfit_for_intensive=1 AND npm1_mutation=1 AND
tp53_mutation=0 AND complex_karyotype=0) yields a subgroup of n=4,531
(9.1% of cohort). Inside this group: VEN/AZA on 78.6% (n=1,790) vs off
15.8% (n=2,741). Outside: 16.6% on vs 16.7% off (effect ~0). Logit
interaction coef +2.99, p=1.2e-291.

Iterations 18-19 — Robustness within the target subgroup.
Within the target subgroup, the VEN/AZA contrast is uniform across
secondary_aml, flt3_itd, flt3_tkd, idh1_mutation, idh2_mutation, and
sex_female (all subgroups show ~75-79% on VEN/AZA vs ~14-19% off). It
is also uniform across ECOG (contrast ~57-67 pp at ECOG 0/1/2) and
across age tertiles (contrast ~60 pp in each). ECOG and other
prognostic markers shift baseline OR but do not modulate the relative
benefit, so they are not part of the subgroup definition.

Iteration 20 — Adjusted multivariable confirmation.
A logistic model with VEN/AZA, the four candidate modifiers, and all
four 2-way VEN/AZA × modifier interactions confirmed the pattern:
VEN/AZA × unfit +0.935 (p<0.001), VEN/AZA × NPM1 +1.104 (p<0.001),
VEN/AZA × TP53 -0.475 (p<0.001), VEN/AZA × complex_karyotype -0.340
(p<0.001). All four interactions were independently significant in
the predicted directions, supporting a true four-way subgroup rather
than two redundant 2-way effects.

Iterations 21-22 — Are other treatments active anywhere?
Within the target subgroup, none of the non-VEN/AZA treatments shifted
objective_response (all on/off rates within ~1 pp of the within-cell
mean). A systematic screen of all 6 treatments × 10 binary features
× 2 feature values (120 cells) found that the 16 strongest contrasts
were ALL VEN/AZA pairs; no non-VEN/AZA treatment achieved a |risk
difference| > 4 pp in any binary subgroup. After multiplicity, no
non-VEN/AZA treatment-modifier signal survives.

Iteration 23 — Continuous-feature heterogeneity in the target subgroup.
Tertiles of age_years, blast_pct_marrow, albumin_g_dl, wbc_k_per_ul,
ldh_u_l, weight_loss_pct_6mo, crp_mg_l, and hemoglobin_g_dl all
showed VEN/AZA contrast in the +56 to +66 pp range within the target
subgroup. No continuous predictor abolishes the response; the effect
is structurally tied to the four binary predicates.

Iteration 24 — Necessity of each predicate.
Dropping each predicate in turn from the subgroup definition produced
attenuation: dropping unfit gave +31 pp (diluted by no-effect fit
cells); dropping NPM1 gave +17 pp (heavy dilution); dropping the TP53
filter gave +56 pp; dropping the complex_karyotype filter gave +55 pp.
The four-predicate definition is the most parsimonious that captures
the full +63 pp effect.

Iteration 25 — Final consolidated hypotheses.
(1) treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine increases objective_response only
in patients who are unfit_for_intensive=1 AND have npm1_mutation=1 AND
do NOT have tp53_mutation AND do NOT have complex_karyotype. In this
subgroup, OR rises from ~16% to ~79% (absolute +63 pp). Outside the
subgroup, VEN/AZA has no detectable effect on OR (~0 pp). Logistic
interaction p=1.2e-291.
(2) For all other treatments (treatment_7plus3, treatment_midostaurin,
treatment_gilteritinib, treatment_ivosidenib, treatment_enasidenib) no
biomarker subgroup with a meaningful effect on objective_response was
identified.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
Supported: VEN/AZA main effect; NPM1, TP53, complex_karyotype, ECOG,
blast_pct_marrow, albumin_g_dl, weight_loss_pct_6mo, wbc_k_per_ul, and
crp_mg_l associations with response (all in predicted directions);
VEN/AZA × unfit, × NPM1, × TP53, × complex_karyotype interactions; the
four-way target subgroup hypothesis.

Refuted / null: 7+3 main effect; midostaurin, gilteritinib,
ivosidenib, enasidenib main effects; FLT3 main effect on response;
IDH main effect on response; sex/secondary_aml main effects;
midostaurin × FLT3-ITD, gilteritinib × FLT3-ITD, ivosidenib × IDH1,
enasidenib × IDH2 canonical interactions; 7+3 × ECOG and 7+3 × unfit
interactions; the 'unfit patients respond worse' clinical
expectation (it reversed in this dataset, driven by the VEN/AZA
× unfit interaction); the apparent NPM1 main effect (entirely
explained by the VEN/AZA interaction).

Overall conclusion
------------------
The dataset contains exactly one major treatment-biomarker-outcome
relationship: a large, highly localized VEN/AZA response benefit
restricted to the four-predicate subgroup (unfit_for_intensive AND
npm1_mutation AND NOT tp53_mutation AND NOT complex_karyotype), where
the absolute response rate jumps from ~16% to ~79%. The cohort-wide
appearance that "NPM1+ patients respond better" and "unfit patients
respond better" is entirely a downstream consequence of this
interaction. Targeted agents tied to canonical biomarkers
(FLT3/IDH inhibitors) and standard induction (7+3) show no detectable
treatment effect on objective_response in any subgroup of this cohort.
ECOG, blast burden, albumin, weight loss, CRP, and WBC are baseline
prognostic markers that shift response rates without interacting with
treatment.
