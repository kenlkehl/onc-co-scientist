Analysis summary — ds001_aml (n=50,000 patients, single binary outcome objective_response, overall response rate 18.75%)
====================================================================================================================

Approach
--------
The cohort was probed across 25 iterations of propose-test-refine, working from baseline-feature
main effects, to canonical treatment-biomarker pairings, to systematic interaction screening, to
exhaustive multi-feature subgroup discovery, with a final adjusted multivariable model. The
outcome is binary (objective_response). Six treatment columns are present (treatment_midostaurin,
treatment_gilteritinib, treatment_ivosidenib, treatment_enasidenib, treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine,
and treatment_7plus3); patients can have 0–6 concurrent treatments (median 1, 28% receive ≥2).
For treatment-effect heterogeneity searching, every treatment was screened with logistic
regression treatment-by-feature interactions across all 29 baseline features and with an
exhaustive 3-binary-feature subgroup scan (min n_on >= 200).

Baseline feature main effects on objective_response
---------------------------------------------------
Significant negative predictors of response (worse outcome):
  ECOG performance status (Mann-Whitney p=7.4e-92; mean 0.83 vs 0.67),
  blast_pct_marrow (p=4.4e-17; mean 60.3 vs 58.5),
  weight_loss_pct_6mo (p=7.5e-27; mean 3.95 vs 3.44),
  wbc_k_per_ul (p=2.6e-8),
  crp_mg_l (p=1.6e-6),
  complex_karyotype (chi2 p=3.3e-6; -2.3% absolute),
  tp53_mutation (chi2 p=3.5e-5; -2.4% absolute).

Significant positive predictors of response:
  npm1_mutation (chi2 p=5.8e-70; +6.7% absolute — the dominant favorable molecular marker),
  unfit_for_intensive (chi2 p=1.9e-55; +5.6% absolute — direction OPPOSITE to clinical
      expectation; explained by treatment confounding because V/A is preferentially given
      to unfit patients and V/A is the only treatment that drives response),
  albumin_g_dl (Mann-Whitney p=1.7e-7; modestly higher in responders).

Null main effects (p>0.05): age_years (flat across deciles 17.8%–19.9%, an unusual finding for
AML and a hint that age is not load-bearing in this dataset), sex_female, secondary_aml,
flt3_itd, flt3_tkd, idh1_mutation, idh2_mutation, ldh_u_l, nlr, hemoglobin_g_dl,
alkaline_phosphatase_u_l, ast_u_l, alt_u_l, total_bilirubin_mg_dl, creatinine_mg_dl, bun_mg_dl,
sodium_meq_l, potassium_meq_l, calcium_mg_dl.

Treatment main effects on objective_response (unadjusted)
---------------------------------------------------------
Only one treatment shows a significant unadjusted main effect:
  treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine: 0.220 on vs 0.166 off (+5.4%, p<1e-50).
The other five (midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, 7+3) all have absolute
differences within +/-0.7% and chi-square p>0.13. This is sustained in the multivariable
adjusted model (V/A coef +0.362, p<1e-50; all others non-significant).

Canonical treatment-biomarker pairings: did targeted therapies work in their target subgroups?
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tested every drug-mutation pair conventionally expected from AML treatment guidelines.
Results were largely null or even slightly opposite to canonical expectation:
  - Midostaurin in FLT3-ITD+ : 0.176 vs 0.191 off (diff -1.5%, p=0.19; logit interaction p=0.20).
  - Midostaurin in FLT3-TKD+ : -0.5%, p=0.85.
  - Gilteritinib in FLT3-ITD+: +0.2%, p=0.86.
  - Gilteritinib in FLT3-TKD+: -3.5%, p=0.10.
  - Ivosidenib in IDH1+      : -4.0%, p=0.11 (logit interaction coef -0.30, p=0.077; trend HARMFUL).
  - Enasidenib in IDH2+      : +0.4%, p=0.92.
None of the canonical mutation-targeted treatment effects were detectable in this dataset.
This contrasts sharply with V/A.

Systematic treatment-by-feature interaction screen
---------------------------------------------------
Strongest interactions on objective_response (logistic interaction term, p<1e-5):
  treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine × npm1_mutation        : +1.055 (p=5.5e-102)  [STRONG enhancer]
  treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine × unfit_for_intensive  : +0.883 (p=1.1e-78)   [STRONG enhancer]
  treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine × tp53_mutation        : -0.409 (p=4.7e-7)    [SUPPRESSOR]
  treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine × complex_karyotype    : -0.306 (p=6.0e-6)    [SUPPRESSOR]
  treatment_7plus3 × complex_karyotype                    : -0.213 (p=1.5e-3)    [HARM in complex]
  treatment_enasidenib × sex_female                       : +0.314 (p=2.2e-4)    [borderline]

V/A heterogeneity — pinning down the maximal subgroup
-----------------------------------------------------
Joint subgroups of V/A effect (response on V/A vs off, absolute diff):
  unfit==1                                : 0.308 vs 0.163, diff +14.5% (n_on=8013).
  npm1==1                                 : 0.347 vs 0.160, diff +18.7% (n_on=6025).
  unfit==1 & npm1==1                      : 0.633 vs 0.160, diff +47.4% (n_on=2401).
  unfit==1 & npm1==1 & tp53==0            : 0.691 vs 0.157, diff +53.4% (n_on=2125).
  unfit==1 & npm1==1 & tp53==0 & complex==0: 0.786 vs 0.158, diff +62.8% (n_on=1790, p=0).

Drop-one analysis confirms each predicate matters: dropping unfit cuts diff to +24.3%,
dropping npm1 to +18.6%, dropping the tp53wt requirement to +55.6%, dropping the
no-complex requirement to +53.4%. Every condition is a load-bearing component of the
maximal-effect subgroup.

Crucially, in the COMPLEMENT of this 4-variable subgroup (n=45,469), V/A response
diff is -0.3% (p=0.42). The V/A treatment effect is therefore essentially fully
concentrated in the unfit + NPM1+ + TP53wt + no-complex-karyotype subgroup.

Adjusted multivariable model with V/A interactions confirms the structure:
  V/A main effect            = -0.379 (p<1e-20; harmful in the off-target group)
  V/A × unfit_for_intensive  = +0.950 (p<1e-50)
  V/A × npm1_mutation        = +1.128 (p<1e-50)
  V/A × tp53_mutation        = -0.505 (p=2e-9)
  V/A × complex_karyotype    = -0.351 (p=5e-7)
Net V/A log-odds in the maximal subgroup ≈ -0.38 + 0.95 + 1.13 = +1.70 (≈5.5× odds).
Adding 7+3 to V/A in this subgroup does not change the response rate (79.3% with vs
77.8% without 7+3), suggesting V/A is the load-bearing treatment.

7+3 heterogeneity
-----------------
7+3 has no overall response effect (+0.3%, p=0.47). The only meaningful subgroup
signal is HARM in complex_karyotype: -2.3% absolute (p=0.008), with the worst
sub-subgroup being complex_karyotype + flt3_itd + npm1_mutation at -12.0% (p=0.001).
In the negative-control test 7+3 × unfit, no differential effect was seen
(interaction p=0.56). In favorable-risk subgroups (fit + tp53wt + no complex),
7+3 produces a marginal +0.7%-1.0% response uplift (p~0.05) — too small to be
clinically meaningful.

FLT3-targeted, IDH-targeted treatments
--------------------------------------
Across midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, no subgroup of objective
response benefit was identified that survived the same scrutiny as V/A:
- Their mutation-targeted subgroups are flat or slightly negative.
- Best 3-feature subgroups for each had p>0.01 with small absolute diffs.
- Adjusted multivariable coefficients are null (|β|<=0.05, p>0.13).

The one borderline secondary signal: enasidenib in NPM1+ female patients shows
+5.0% (p=0.010), driven primarily in IDH2-WT NPM1+ females (i.e., not IDH2-driven).
The three-way interaction enasidenib × sex_female × npm1_mutation has p=0.18, so
this is best regarded as suggestive only, not a confirmed subgroup.

Final treatment-effect subgroup hypotheses
==========================================

PRIMARY (h52). For treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine on objective_response, the maximal
heterogeneous treatment effect is in patients who simultaneously satisfy:
  unfit_for_intensive == 1
  npm1_mutation == 1
  tp53_mutation == 0
  complex_karyotype == 0
In this subgroup, V/A increases response from ~16% (off) to ~79% (on). Outside this
4-predicate subgroup, V/A has no detectable effect. The unfavorable predicates that
suppress the V/A treatment effect are tp53_mutation==1 and complex_karyotype==1; the
necessary favorable predicates are unfit_for_intensive==1 and npm1_mutation==1.
Direction: positive. p-value: effectively 0 (<1e-300 chi-square in the subgroup;
all four interaction terms p<1e-6 in the multivariable model).

SECONDARY (h53). For treatment_7plus3 on objective_response, the only detectable
heterogeneous treatment effect is HARM in complex_karyotype==1 patients
(diff -2.3%, p=0.008; -12.0% in the complex_karyotype + flt3_itd + npm1_mutation
sub-subgroup, p=0.001). No favorable subgroup of 7+3 benefit was identified.

NULL (h54). For treatment_midostaurin, treatment_gilteritinib, treatment_ivosidenib,
and treatment_enasidenib, no statistically significant or clinically meaningful
subgroup of objective_response benefit was identified — including the canonical
mutation-defined target subgroups (FLT3-ITD/TKD for midostaurin and gilteritinib;
IDH1 for ivosidenib; IDH2 for enasidenib). Enasidenib in NPM1-mutated females
shows a borderline +5.0% (p=0.010) but the three-way interaction
enasidenib × sex_female × npm1_mutation is not significant (p=0.18) and is
flagged here as suggestive rather than confirmed.

Overall interpretation
======================
This cohort behaves as if a single targeted intervention — venetoclax + azacitidine —
carries the entire treatment-effect signal on objective response, and only within a
well-defined favorable-biology subgroup (unfit, NPM1-mutated, TP53 wild-type, no
complex karyotype). Baseline-prognosis variables (ECOG, NPM1, TP53, complex
karyotype, blast burden, weight loss, CRP, WBC, albumin) are independently
associated with response in the expected directions. Age has no effect — an
unusual finding for AML and a clue to the dataset's structure. The remaining
canonical AML targeted therapies do not produce detectable benefit in this
cohort, including in their mutation-defined target subgroups.
