Analysis Summary — ds001_aml (n = 50,000 AML patients)
========================================================

Outcome explored: objective_response (overall rate 18.75%).
Iterations: 25 (within the 25-iteration cap).

1. Baseline / univariate prognostic landscape
---------------------------------------------
Across simple bivariate tests, several baseline features showed strong
associations with objective_response:

* npm1_mutation: response 23.5% vs 16.7% (diff +6.7 pp, chi-square p ≈ 6e-70)
  — strongest positive marginal feature.
* unfit_for_intensive: 22.1% vs 16.5% (diff +5.6 pp, p ≈ 2e-55) — positive
  in the marginal pool, but ultimately driven by treatment selection
  (see below).
* ecog_ps: monotone gradient — 23.1% (0), 17.4% (1), 12.9% (2),
  p ≈ 2e-90.
* complex_karyotype: 16.8% vs 19.1% (diff −2.3 pp, p ≈ 3e-06).
* tp53_mutation: 16.6% vs 19.0% (diff −2.4 pp, p ≈ 4e-05).
* Lower blast_pct_marrow (−1.8 pp difference between responders and
  non-responders, p ≈ 2e-16), lower wbc_k_per_ul (p ≈ 1e-04), lower
  weight_loss_pct_6mo (p ≈ 2e-33), lower crp_mg_l (p ≈ 2e-06), and
  higher albumin_g_dl (p ≈ 3e-07) were all associated with response.
* age_years, sex_female, secondary_aml, ldh_u_l, nlr, hemoglobin and
  the basic chemistries (sodium, potassium, calcium, creatinine, BUN,
  bilirubin, AST, ALT, alkaline phosphatase) showed no meaningful
  univariate association with response.
* flt3_itd, flt3_tkd, idh1_mutation, and idh2_mutation had essentially
  no marginal effect on response — a useful counterpoint to npm1/tp53.

2. Treatment main effects
-------------------------
Among the six treatments, only treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine showed a
statistically meaningful main effect on objective_response (22.0% vs
16.7%, +5.3 pp). Treatment_7plus3, treatment_midostaurin,
treatment_gilteritinib, treatment_ivosidenib, and treatment_enasidenib
were all near-null on the marginal scale.

A full multivariable logistic regression (n = 50,000) confirmed this:
ven/aza coefficient +0.361 (p << 1e-30) was the only treatment with an
independent positive effect after adjustment for age, sex, ECOG,
mutations, labs, and the other five treatments. The other treatments
were not significant. Independent baseline correlates of response in
the full model were npm1_mutation (+0.436), tp53_mutation (−0.188),
complex_karyotype (−0.169), ecog_ps (−0.361), unfit_for_intensive
(+0.373, partly capturing ven/aza eligibility), albumin (+0.123),
blast_pct_marrow (−0.005 per percent), weight_loss_pct_6mo (−0.037),
crp_mg_l (−0.007), and wbc_k_per_ul (−0.002).

3. Targeted-therapy / mutation interactions
-------------------------------------------
Despite the existence of FLT3- and IDH-targeted treatments in this
cohort, the expected mutation-by-treatment synergies were not present:

* treatment_midostaurin × flt3_itd: −1.5 pp absolute diff in FLT3-ITD+,
  +0.1 pp in FLT3-ITD−; interaction p = 0.19. No benefit.
* treatment_gilteritinib × flt3_itd: +0.2 pp in FLT3-ITD+, −1.0 pp in
  FLT3-ITD−; interaction p = 0.32. No benefit.
* treatment_ivosidenib × idh1_mutation: −4.0 pp diff in IDH1+, +0.4 pp
  in IDH1−; interaction logit −0.304, p = 0.077. Direction is opposite
  of the expected enrichment — ivosidenib is at most null and possibly
  numerically harmful in IDH1+.
* treatment_enasidenib × idh2_mutation: +0.4 pp in IDH2+, −0.2 pp in
  IDH2−; interaction p = 0.79. No benefit in IDH2 carriers.

The expected biomarker-driven heterogeneity for these targeted agents
was therefore refuted in this dataset.

4. Venetoclax/azacitidine heterogeneity (the dominant signal)
-------------------------------------------------------------
Single-feature interaction screen ranked the four largest
treatment-by-feature interactions all as ven/aza interactions:

* ven/aza × npm1_mutation: NPM1+ patients gained +18.7 pp (34.7% vs
  16.0%); NPM1− gained −0.4 pp. Logit interaction coef +1.055
  (p ≈ 5e-102).
* ven/aza × unfit_for_intensive: unfit patients gained +14.5 pp
  (30.8% vs 16.3%); fit patients gained −0.8 pp. Logit interaction
  coef +0.883 (p ≈ 1e-78).
* ven/aza × tp53_mutation: TP53− gained +6.0 pp; TP53+ gained −0.4 pp.
  Logit interaction coef −0.409 (p ≈ 5e-07). TP53 mutation removes the
  benefit.
* ven/aza × complex_karyotype: CK− gained +6.1 pp; CK+ gained +1.2 pp.
  Logit interaction coef −0.306 (p ≈ 6e-06). Complex karyotype removes
  most of the benefit.

5. Refining the subgroup
------------------------
Joint subgroups dramatically concentrate the effect:

* NPM1+ AND unfit:           ven/aza diff +47.4 pp (n_t = 2,401).
* NPM1+ AND unfit AND TP53−: ven/aza diff +53.4 pp (n_t = 2,125).
* NPM1+ AND unfit AND TP53− AND complex_karyotype−:
                             ven/aza diff +62.8 pp
                             (response 78.6% on ven/aza, n = 1,790,
                             vs 15.8% off, n = 2,741).

Outside the four-predicate subgroup (n = 45,469), the ven/aza effect on
objective_response was −0.3 pp (16.4% on, 16.7% off) — i.e. essentially
zero on average. A logistic model with a (ven/aza × subgroup-indicator)
interaction returned coef +2.993 (z = 36.5, p « 1e-200).

A three-way logistic ven/aza × npm1 × unfit interaction gave coef
+2.126 (p ≈ 2e-92) and a four-way ven/aza × npm1 × unfit × tp53
interaction gave coef −2.640 (p ≈ 1e-13), both supporting the joint
modifier structure: the ven/aza benefit only emerges when NPM1 and
unfit are both present, and is then suppressed by TP53 mutation, with
a further suppression from complex karyotype.

Stepwise sensitivity analyses showed that each of the four predicates
contributes monotonically: dropping any one of them (e.g. allowing
TP53+, or allowing complex_karyotype+, or allowing fit patients, or
allowing NPM1−) drops the ven/aza effect from +62.8 pp toward
1–10 pp. No additional binary feature among flt3_itd, flt3_tkd,
idh1_mutation, idh2_mutation, secondary_aml, or sex_female further
modified the in-subgroup effect (treatment differences within
sub-strata were all 47–58 pp). Stratification by ECOG within the
subgroup showed the absolute response was higher with better ECOG
(74.8% / 67.8% / 59.9% on ven/aza vs 19.6% / 14.9% / 9.3% off),
but the treatment delta was preserved (+55.2 / +52.9 / +50.6 pp).

Within the subgroup, an adjusted logistic regression (controlling for
age, sex, ECOG, mutations, and labs) returned a ven/aza coefficient of
+3.044 (z = 37.9, p « 1e-200). None of the other five treatments
moved the response within this subgroup (response stayed at ~40% with
or without midostaurin, gilteritinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, or
7+3) — only ven/aza separates responders from non-responders here.

6. Other secondary heterogeneity findings
-----------------------------------------
* treatment_7plus3 × complex_karyotype: 7+3 numerically harmed CK+
  patients (15.5% vs 17.9%, diff −2.3 pp; interaction p = 0.0015) and
  was consistent across TP53 strata.
* treatment_enasidenib × sex_female: enasidenib was numerically
  harmful in males (16.5% vs 18.8%, −2.3 pp) and numerically helpful
  in females (21.2% vs 18.7%, +2.5 pp); interaction p = 2.2e-04. The
  effect is small in absolute terms (~2–3 pp) and an order of magnitude
  smaller than the ven/aza signal.
* No other treatment-by-feature interaction survived multiple-testing
  scrutiny (p < 0.01) in the systematic 6-treatment × 10-feature scan.

7. Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
-----------------------------------
Supported:
- npm1_mutation positively associated with objective_response.
- tp53_mutation and complex_karyotype negatively associated with
  objective_response (in the cohort as a whole, before stratifying by
  treatment).
- worse ECOG, lower albumin, higher blast_pct_marrow, higher
  weight_loss, higher CRP, higher WBC predict lower response.
- treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine as the only treatment with an
  independent positive main effect.
- treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine benefit concentrated in NPM1+ and
  in unfit_for_intensive patients, suppressed by TP53 mutation and by
  complex_karyotype, with the four-predicate joint subgroup capturing
  essentially all of the treatment effect.
- treatment_7plus3 modest harm in complex_karyotype patients.
- treatment_enasidenib modest sex-modified effect (positive in
  females, negative in males), small in magnitude.

Refuted:
- FLT3-targeted (midostaurin, gilteritinib) benefit in FLT3-ITD or
  FLT3-TKD carriers — null/inverse in this cohort.
- IDH-targeted (ivosidenib, enasidenib) benefit specifically enriched
  in IDH1+ or IDH2+ — null or numerically inverse for ivosidenib in
  IDH1+, null for enasidenib in IDH2+.
- Sex, age, secondary_aml, ldh, nlr, basic chemistry panels as
  marginal predictors of response.

8. Final best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis
------------------------------------------------------------
Outcome: objective_response.
Treatment: treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine.
Direction: positive — ven/aza increases objective_response.
Subgroup definition (all four predicates simultaneously):
    npm1_mutation == 1
    AND unfit_for_intensive == 1
    AND tp53_mutation == 0
    AND complex_karyotype == 0

Within this subgroup (n = 4,531; ~9% of the cohort), objective_response
was 78.6% on treatment_venetoclax_azacitidine vs 15.8% off
(absolute difference +62.8 pp; adjusted logit coefficient +3.04;
p « 1e-200). Outside the subgroup the average treatment effect was
≈ 0. Each predicate is individually load-bearing: removing any one of
the four (allowing TP53+, allowing complex_karyotype+, allowing
fit-for-intensive, or allowing NPM1−) substantially attenuates the
treatment effect.

This is the dominant pattern in the dataset; no other treatment shows
a comparable subgroup-concentrated effect.
