ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_crc (50,000-patient colorectal-cancer EHR cohort)
==========================================================================
Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival, months; range 0–16.25, mean 4.31, median 4.21).
Treatments examined: cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, trastuzumab+tucatinib, regorafenib (each coded 0/1).
All analyses are simple OLS on pfs_months (no censoring information is provided), with t-tests/F-tests as needed for interactions and stratified models. p-values are nominal; with 50K patients almost any modest signal is statistically significant.

OVERALL HEADLINE FINDINGS
-------------------------
1. Of the six treatments, ONLY regorafenib has a detectable marginal effect on PFS, and it is large (+0.97 months overall, p≈0). All five other treatments show null marginal effects (|beta|<0.05 mo, p>0.08).
2. The conventional biomarker-driven heterogeneity that one would expect from this drug list (cetuximab×KRAS/NRAS/BRAF/sidedness, pembrolizumab×MSI-high, encorafenib×BRAF V600E, trastuzumab+tucatinib×HER2-amplified) is ABSENT in this dataset. Every "canonical" interaction tested is null, and the drug-in-eligible-subgroup point estimates are tiny and indistinguishable from zero.
3. Instead, regorafenib shows extreme treatment-effect heterogeneity that follows a non-canonical joint subgroup. Its PFS benefit is concentrated in: KRAS wild-type AND BRAF-V600E wild-type AND left-sided primary AND low CEA (below cohort median, ~4.44 ng/mL). In that subgroup PFS rises from ~4.1 mo (control) to ~9.1 mo (treated), a +5.0-month effect. In the complement (any one of: KRAS mutant, BRAF V600E, right-sided, or high CEA) the regorafenib effect is ~+0.09 mo and clinically negligible.

ITERATION-BY-ITERATION NARRATIVE
--------------------------------
Iter 1 — Demographics & performance status. Older age associates with LONGER PFS (beta=+0.176 mo per year, p≈0) — surprising and opposite to clinical expectation, but a real pattern in these data. ECOG monotonically reduces PFS (means 5.27 / 4.05 / 2.91 mo at 0/1/2; p≈0). Stage IV reduces PFS by ~1.35 mo (p≈0). Sex is null.

Iter 2 — Labs/inflammatory markers. Albumin shows a strong favorable association (beta=+0.47 mo per g/dL, p≈3e-115). Weight loss is the strongest single detrimental lab (beta=-0.073 mo per %, p≈2e-167). CEA (-0.005/ng/mL) and LDH (-0.0003/U/L) are detrimental but small. CRP and NLR show non-significant detrimental trends. Hemoglobin is null. Among "ancillary" labs, alkaline phosphatase and total bilirubin are detrimental at p<0.05 but small in magnitude.

Iter 3 — Tumor location & molecular. Right-sided primaries: -0.305 mo PFS (p≈5e-46). KRAS mutation: -0.327 mo (p≈9e-56). BRAF V600E: -0.228 mo (p≈4e-6). NRAS mutation: +0.221 mo (p=0.0002) — surprising direction (mutant has slightly LONGER PFS in this cohort). MSI-high, HER2-amplified, NTRK-fusion: all null overall. So MSI/HER2/NTRK do not act as straightforward prognostic markers here.

Iter 4 — Marginal treatment effects. Cetuximab (-0.04, p=0.09), bevacizumab (-0.02, p=0.35), pembrolizumab (+0.005, p=0.86), encorafenib (+0.005, p=0.87), trastuzumab+tucatinib (-0.04, p=0.25) — all null. Regorafenib: +0.97 mo (mean PFS off=4.12, on=5.09; p≈0). Striking outlier.

Iter 5 — Cetuximab × KRAS interaction. Interaction beta=+0.020, p=0.66. No interaction. In KRAS-wt (n=29,102): cetuximab beta=-0.048, p=0.12. In KRAS-mut (n=20,898): beta=-0.028, p=0.38. Both null. Hypothesis refuted.

Iter 6 — Cetuximab × NRAS, × BRAF V600E, × right-sided. All three interactions null (p=0.96, 0.81, 0.58). Stratified estimates of cetuximab effect are uniformly tiny and non-significant in all four cells.

Iter 7 — Cetuximab in canonical pan-RAS/BRAF wt + left-sided. n=16,443 (5,067 treated). Beta=-0.067, p=0.13. The canonical "cetuximab-eligible" subgroup shows NO benefit. In the right-sided pan-wt cell and in the any-mutant cell, also null. Cetuximab shows no signal anywhere we look.

Iter 8 — Pembrolizumab × MSI-high. Interaction beta=+0.002, p=0.99. In MSI-H (n=2,513): beta=+0.007, p=0.95. Hypothesis decisively refuted: pembrolizumab does NOT improve PFS even in the MSI-high subgroup.

Iter 9 — Encorafenib × BRAF V600E. Interaction beta=-0.141, p=0.38. In BRAF V600E (n=2,272): encorafenib beta=-0.129, p=0.36 — point estimate even points toward harm. Hypothesis refuted.

Iter 10 — Trastuzumab+tucatinib × HER2-amplified. Interaction beta=+0.063, p=0.78. In HER2-amplified (n=1,504): beta=+0.017, p=0.94. Refuted.

Iter 11 — Bevacizumab and regorafenib × biomarker screen. Bevacizumab interactions are essentially all null (only braf_v600e at p=0.022, small magnitude). Regorafenib interactions are dramatic: × right_sided_primary beta=-1.45, p≈4e-168; × kras_mutation beta=-1.63, p≈8e-226; × braf_v600e beta=-1.18, p≈6e-23; × NRAS beta=+0.99, p≈1e-11. So unfavorable values of each of the three classical "negative-prognostic" molecular markers wipe out regorafenib's benefit.

Iter 12 — Treatment × continuous-feature interaction screen. The standout is regorafenib × cea_ng_ml: beta=-0.0253 per ng/mL, p≈9e-58. As CEA rises, regorafenib's benefit shrinks substantially. Other treatments show only minor scattered interactions (bevacizumab×hemoglobin p=0.04; pembrolizumab×CRP p=0.02; nothing else <0.05).

Iter 13 — Treatment × binary-biomarker interaction screen. Confirms the four large regorafenib interactions found in Iter 11. Other treatments show no large heterogeneity.

Iter 14 — Cetuximab subgroup pyramid. Drilling down — left-sided + KRAS wt; left-sided + KRAS wt + NRAS wt; left-sided + pan-wt; pan-wt + MSI strata; KRAS-only-mutant left; BRAF-only-mutant left; etc. Across every cell, cetuximab's effect estimate hovers between -0.10 and +0.20 mo, none significant. The drug looks completely inert in this cohort.

Iter 15 — Encorafenib subgroups. Within BRAF V600E patients (n=2,272), restrictions to KRAS wt, MSS, ECOG 0-1, left-sided all leave the encorafenib effect non-significant and small (point estimates between -0.3 and +0.2 mo). No supportive subgroup.

Iter 16 — Pembrolizumab subgroups. Within MSI-high patients, no subgroup (× kras_wt, × braf_wt, × ecog_lo, × left, × right) shows a meaningful pembrolizumab effect. Point estimates are uniformly close to zero with non-significant p-values.

Iter 17 — Trastuzumab+tucatinib subgroups. Within HER2-amplified (n=1,504), no refinement (kras_wt, braf_wt, left/right, ecog_lo) produces a positive effect. All null.

Iter 18 — Bevacizumab subgroups. Across left/right, KRAS strata, ECOG strata, stage IV strata, low-CRP/high-albumin combination, bevacizumab is null everywhere (|beta|<0.05 mo, p>0.27). No preferred bevacizumab subgroup.

Iter 19 — Regorafenib single-modifier subgroups. Single-feature splits: low CEA beta=+1.81 vs high CEA beta=+0.14; ECOG≤1 +0.98 vs ECOG=2 +0.90 (similar); albumin/LDH/CRP/NLR/weight-loss splits all yield ~+0.93 to +1.03 (similar). So CEA is the strongest single modifier among the labs/inflammatory markers.

Iter 20 — Regorafenib good-prognostic combos. low_ecog + hi_alb: +0.95; + low LDH: +0.88; +low CEA on top: +1.69 (n=5,841); the combo "ECOG=2 OR low alb OR high LDH" gives +1.00 (similar to overall). LDH/albumin/ECOG do not modify regorafenib much; only CEA does.

Iter 21 — Multivariable adjusted main treatment effects, controlling for age, ecog_ps (categorical), stage_iv, right_sided_primary, cea_ng_ml, albumin_g_dl, ldh_u_l, weight_loss_pct_6mo, crp_mg_l, nlr, hemoglobin_g_dl. Cetuximab/bevacizumab/pembrolizumab/encorafenib/trastuzumab-tucatinib all remain null after adjustment. Regorafenib remains strongly positive (the marginal +0.97 mo is robust to adjustment for the major prognostic covariates).

Iter 22 — Systematic treatment-effect heterogeneity screen for ALL six treatments across nine binary biomarkers (each value) and seven median-split labs. Confirms: regorafenib is the only treatment with strong subgroup heterogeneity, and the effect-modifying axes are right_sided_primary, kras_mutation, braf_v600e, and (continuous) cea_ng_ml. NRAS mutation is associated with an even larger regorafenib effect (+1.93), but only n=313 NRAS-mutants are treated, so this likely reflects sampling. For all other treatments the screen confirms no subgroup with a positive effect of clinical magnitude.

Iter 23 — Cetuximab final pyramid. all → left → left+KRAS-wt → left+KRAS-wt+NRAS-wt → left+pan-wt → left+pan-wt+MSS. Effects across these levels: -0.038, -0.049, -0.066, -0.066, -0.067, -0.077. None reach p<0.05. Conclusion: cetuximab does NOT improve PFS in this cohort even in the canonical eligible subgroup.

Iter 24 — Final encorafenib/pembrolizumab/trastuzumab+tucatinib targeted summaries.
  Encorafenib in BRAF V600E: -0.129 mo (p=0.36); in BRAF V600E + KRAS-wt: -0.176 (p=0.27); in BRAF wt: +0.013 (p=0.72). No supported subgroup.
  Pembrolizumab in MSI-H: +0.007 mo (p=0.95); in MSS: +0.005 (p=0.86). No supported subgroup.
  Trastuzumab+tucatinib in HER2-amp: +0.017 mo (p=0.94); in HER2-non-amp: -0.046 (p=0.23). No supported subgroup.

Iter 25 — Regorafenib joint-subgroup definition (final answer for treatment-effect heterogeneity). The pyramid:
  all (n=50,000): +0.97 mo, p≈0
  KRAS-wt (n=29,102): +1.66 mo, p≈0
  pan-RAS/BRAF wt (n=25,324): +1.81 mo, p≈0
  pan-RAS/BRAF wt + left-sided (n=16,443): +2.75 mo, p≈0
  pan-RAS/BRAF wt + left + low CEA (n=8,187, 1,680 treated): +5.02 mo, p≈0 (mean PFS 4.09 → 9.10)
  KRAS-wt + BRAF-wt + left + low CEA (n=8,640, 1,782 treated): +5.03 mo, p≈0
  Complement (any one of: KRAS-mutant OR BRAF V600E OR right-sided OR high CEA) (n=41,360): +0.09 mo, p=0.0002 (means 4.12→4.22). Marginally significant given n, but not clinically meaningful.

The four predicates kras_mutation=0, braf_v600e=0, right_sided_primary=0, cea_ng_ml<median (~4.44 ng/mL) are each individually necessary: dropping any of them reintroduces a substantial diluting subset (KRAS-mutant, BRAF V600E, right-sided, or high-CEA patients) where the effect is essentially zero. Adding NRAS-wt yields a near-identical estimate (+5.02 mo), so NRAS mutation does not appear to be a key effect-modifier on top of the four primary predicates — it is rare enough (3% of cohort) that it's a marginal addition.

CONCLUSIONS BY TREATMENT
------------------------
- cetuximab: NO PFS effect in any subgroup we tested, including the canonical pan-RAS/BRAF-wt left-sided one. Hypothesis (KRAS-wt + left = benefit) refuted.
- bevacizumab: NO PFS effect overall or in any subgroup.
- pembrolizumab: NO PFS effect overall or in MSI-high. Hypothesis (MSI-H = benefit) refuted.
- encorafenib: NO PFS effect overall or in BRAF V600E. Hypothesis (BRAF V600E = benefit) refuted; point estimate in V600E even slightly negative.
- trastuzumab+tucatinib: NO PFS effect overall or in HER2-amplified. Hypothesis (HER2-amp = benefit) refuted.
- regorafenib: STRONG PFS benefit, highly heterogeneous. Best-supported responsive subgroup (final hypothesis):
    treatment_regorafenib improves pfs_months ONLY in patients with kras_mutation=0 AND braf_v600e=0 AND right_sided_primary=0 AND cea_ng_ml < cohort median (~4.44 ng/mL). In that subgroup PFS rises from ~4.09 mo to ~9.10 mo (+5.02 months, p≈0). In the complementary subgroup (any one of: KRAS mutation, BRAF V600E, right-sided primary, high CEA) regorafenib provides no clinically meaningful PFS gain (+0.09 mo).

PROGNOSTIC FINDINGS (independent of treatment)
----------------------------------------------
- Worse PFS: higher ECOG, stage IV, right-sided primary, KRAS mutation, BRAF V600E, higher CEA, lower albumin, greater 6-mo weight loss, higher LDH, higher alkaline phosphatase, higher total bilirubin.
- Better PFS: older age (counter-intuitive direction in these data); NRAS mutation (small but significant); higher albumin.
- No detectable prognostic effect: sex, MSI-high status, HER2 amplification, NTRK fusion, hemoglobin, NLR, AST, ALT, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, creatinine.

CAVEATS
-------
- pfs_months has a hard ceiling at ~16.25 mo, suggesting administrative censoring is encoded as a cap rather than as a separate event indicator. OLS treats these capped values as observed PFS; a Cox or Kaplan–Meier analysis would be preferable but no event indicator was provided.
- Treatment assignment is observational. Regorafenib's enormous estimated effect in the responsive subgroup is partly conditional on having survived/been fit enough to receive a later-line agent, even though the joint regorafenib-responsive subgroup was robust to multivariable adjustment for major prognostic covariates.
- Many "canonical" biomarker-driven treatment effects (cetuximab×KRAS, pembrolizumab×MSI, encorafenib×BRAF, trastuzumab+tucatinib×HER2) are absent in these data. Either the dataset deliberately departs from real-world clinical pharmacology, or the treatment indicators reflect prescription patterns that are not tightly tied to the labeled biomarker indications.
