Oncology dataset ds001_crc — Analysis summary
================================================

Cohort: 50,000 colorectal cancer patients with 33 features (demographics, ECOG,
stage, sidedness, biomarkers, labs, six treatment indicators) and a single
outcome, pfs_months. No missing values. The analysis ran an iterative
hypothesize-test-refine loop (12 substantive iterations) on this cohort.

Iteration 1 — Univariate treatment main effects on PFS
------------------------------------------------------
Welch t-tests comparing pfs_months on vs. off each treatment:

  treatment_regorafenib            : on 5.089 vs off 4.117, +0.972 mo, p=1.7e-216 ★
  treatment_cetuximab              : on 4.285 vs off 4.323, -0.038 mo, p=0.088
  treatment_bevacizumab            : on 4.301 vs off 4.320, -0.019 mo, p=0.354
  treatment_pembrolizumab          : on 4.316 vs off 4.311, +0.005 mo, p=0.856
  treatment_encorafenib            : on 4.316 vs off 4.311, +0.005 mo, p=0.872
  treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib  : on 4.271 vs off 4.315, -0.044 mo, p=0.244

Only regorafenib shows a population-level benefit (+~1 mo). The other five
treatments are flat overall, suggesting they may have biomarker- or
subgroup-restricted effects (or no effect at all).

Iteration 2 — Univariate feature main effects
---------------------------------------------
Strong main-effect predictors of pfs_months (univariate):
  age_years            r=+0.766 (older = longer PFS — likely a cohort/case-mix
                                effect rather than biology in this dataset)
  ecog_ps              r=-0.352 (worse PS = shorter PFS)
  stage_iv             diff -1.352 mo (stage IV worse), p<1e-300
  right_sided_primary  diff -0.305 mo (right-sided worse), p=1e-49
  kras_mutation        diff -0.327 mo (mut worse), p=2e-58
  braf_v600e           diff -0.228 mo (V600E worse), p=3e-7
  albumin_g_dl         r=+0.102 (higher = longer)
  weight_loss_pct_6mo  r=-0.123 (more loss = shorter)
  cea_ng_ml            r=-0.038 (higher = shorter)
  ldh_u_l              r=-0.011 (slight)

msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion, sex_female were null on PFS.
nras_mutation was nominally positive univariately (+0.22, p=7e-4) but became
non-significant after adjustment.

Iteration 3 — Multivariable adjusted main effects (OLS, all features +
treatments simultaneously)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Adjusted main effects confirmed:
  age_years           +0.176 mo/yr        p<1e-300
  ecog_ps             -1.171 mo/level     p<1e-300
  stage_iv            -1.362 mo            p<1e-300
  right_sided_primary -0.287 mo            p<1e-300
  kras_mutation       -0.354 mo            p<1e-300
  braf_v600e          -0.357 mo            p<1e-300
  cea_ng_ml           -0.005 mo/(ng/mL)    p<1e-30
  albumin_g_dl        +0.469 mo/(g/dL)     p<1e-300
  weight_loss_pct_6mo -0.076 mo/%          p<1e-300
  ldh_u_l             -0.0004 mo/(U/L)     p<1e-9

Adjusted treatment effects:
  treatment_regorafenib            +0.943 mo  p<1e-300  ★
  treatment_cetuximab              -0.011 mo  p=0.190
  treatment_bevacizumab            +0.003 mo  p=0.734
  treatment_pembrolizumab          +0.000 mo  p=0.978
  treatment_encorafenib            +0.006 mo  p=0.633
  treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib  +0.026 mo  p=0.069

So in adjusted analyses only regorafenib has an independent main effect on PFS.
The other five treatments are statistically null (and clinically near-zero) at
the population level, consistent with them being either ineffective in this
cohort or having effects confined to small subgroups.

Iteration 4 — Mechanistically motivated treatment×biomarker interactions
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tested adjusted interactions for known clinical pairings:

  cetuximab × kras_mutation    : interaction +0.024 mo, p=0.19 — null
  cetuximab × right-sided      : +0.026 mo, p=0.18 — null
  cetuximab × braf_v600e       : +0.012 mo, p=0.78 — null
  pembrolizumab × msi_high     : +0.059 mo, p=0.27 — null
  encorafenib × braf_v600e     : +0.011 mo, p=0.87 — null
  trastuzumab/tuc × her2_amp   : +0.015 mo, p=0.87 — null
  bevacizumab × stage_iv       : +0.002 mo, p=0.92 — null
  regorafenib × stage_iv       : +0.035 mo, p=0.075 — borderline (effect ~+0.94 in
                                non-stage-IV, ~+1.01 in stage IV — barely modified)
  regorafenib × ecog_ps        : -0.052 mo/level, p=3e-4 — small but significant

Strikingly, none of the canonical biomarker–targeted-therapy pairings produces a
positive interaction. Pembrolizumab does NOT preferentially benefit MSI-high
patients here, encorafenib does NOT preferentially benefit BRAF-V600E patients,
and trastuzumab/tucatinib does NOT preferentially benefit HER2-amplified
patients. Each of these biomarker-positive subgroups has small numerical effect
sizes, sometimes slightly negative, and none reach statistical significance.

Iteration 5 — Regorafenib subgroup probing (univariate stratification)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Stratifying regorafenib effect by KRAS × sidedness:

  KRAS=0, right-sided=0 (left-sided KRAS-WT)  : diff +2.520 mo, p≈0       ★
  KRAS=0, right-sided=1                        : +0.071 mo, p=0.16
  KRAS=1, right-sided=0                        : +0.061 mo, p=0.17
  KRAS=1, right-sided=1                        : -0.040 mo, p=0.52

Regorafenib's benefit is strikingly concentrated in left-sided KRAS-WT patients.

Bevacizumab: among braf_v600e=1 patients, mean PFS 3.958 on bev vs 4.195 off
(diff -0.237, p=6e-3 univariate). However, after adjustment the bev × BRAF
interaction is non-significant (p=0.49), so the apparent harm is likely
confounded.

Iteration 6 — Triple-stratification of regorafenib (KRAS × NRAS × BRAF × side)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Left + KRAS-WT + NRAS-WT + BRAF-WT  : regorafenib +2.749 mo (n=3264 vs 13179), p≈0
  Left + KRAS-WT + NRAS=1 + BRAF-WT   : regorafenib +2.906 mo (n=197 vs 732), p=7e-25
  Left + KRAS-WT + NRAS-WT + BRAF=1   : regorafenib -0.204 mo (n=302 vs 1179), p=0.12
  Left + KRAS=1 + NRAS-WT + BRAF-WT   : regorafenib +0.027 mo (n=4206 vs 16692), p=0.46
  Right-sided strata                  : regorafenib effects all near zero

Two key findings:
  1. BRAF V600E ABOLISHES the regorafenib benefit even in left-sided KRAS-WT
     patients — so BRAF-WT belongs in the qualifying subgroup definition.
  2. NRAS mutation status does NOT modify the regorafenib effect — both NRAS-WT
     and NRAS-mut benefit when KRAS-WT, BRAF-WT, left-sided. NRAS does not
     belong in the subgroup definition.

Iteration 7 — Final regorafenib subgroup verification (adjusted OLS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Defining rego_subgroup = (right_sided_primary=0 AND kras_mutation=0 AND
braf_v600e=0). Adjusted OLS with treatment_regorafenib + rego_subgroup +
treatment_regorafenib × rego_subgroup interaction:

  treatment_regorafenib (outside subgroup): -0.003 mo, p=0.77
  rego_subgroup main effect              : -0.003 mo, p=0.79
  regorafenib × rego_subgroup interaction: +2.739 mo, p≪1e-300

Stratified:
  Outside rego_subgroup: regorafenib +0.030 mo (n=6561 vs 26067), p=0.29
  Inside rego_subgroup : regorafenib +2.758 mo (n=3461 vs 13911), p≪1e-300

Within the rego_subgroup, the magnitude is essentially constant across ECOG and
across stage_iv:
  ECOG=0 +2.665, ECOG=1 +2.765, ECOG=2 +2.743
  stage_iv=0 +2.619, stage_iv=1 +2.859

Iteration 8 — Exhaustive 2-feature subgroup discovery for non-regorafenib treatments
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
For each of cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, and
trastuzumab/tucatinib, scanned all 2-feature subgroups using 18 candidate
modifiers (9 binary biomarkers + 9 continuous variables median-split: ecog_high,
age_old, cea_high, alb_low, ldh_high, wl_high, crp_high, nlr_high, hgb_low).
Top significant signals:

  Cetuximab:
    age_old=1 & wl_high=0       : -0.099 mo (p=4e-3)  — small, NS for benefit
  Bevacizumab:
    right-sided & BRAF-V600E    : -0.422 mo (p=3e-3)
    stage_iv=0 & BRAF-V600E     : -0.370 mo (p=3e-3)
    BRAF-V600E & alb_low        : -0.356 mo (p=5e-3)
    BRAF-V600E & her2_amp=0     : -0.257 mo (p=4e-3)
    Pattern: bev appears slightly harmful in BRAF-V600E patients in
    unadjusted subgroup analyses; not robust after multivariable adjustment.
  Pembrolizumab, encorafenib, trastuzumab/tuc: nothing meaningful at p<5e-3.

Iteration 9 — Adjusted treatment×modifier interaction screen (all features
adjusted, one interaction at a time)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
This is the most rigorous test: for each treatment, fit an OLS controlling for
all features, with one treatment×modifier interaction. Best p-values per
treatment:

  Cetuximab     : best p=0.13 (NRAS) — no interactions reach 0.05.
  Bevacizumab   : best p=0.08 (MSI) — no interactions reach 0.05.
  Pembrolizumab : best p=0.23 — null.
  Encorafenib   : best p=0.08 (NRAS) — null.
  Trastuzumab/tuc: best p=0.08 (KRAS) — null.

  Regorafenib   : right_sided_primary -1.46 (p<1e-300), kras_mutation -1.63
                  (p<1e-300), braf_v600e -0.99 (p=5e-104), nras_mutation
                  +0.89 (p=1e-57; reflects that NRAS=1 patients are KRAS-WT
                  by mutual exclusion, so they enrich the responsive subgroup).

The regorafenib subgroup story is extraordinarily clean and statistically
overwhelming. No other treatment shows any robust modifier signal.

Iteration 10 — Subgroup search WITHIN canonical biomarker-positive subsets
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tried to rescue the canonical biomarker-targeted therapies by looking inside
biomarker-positive patients, stratified by an additional modifier:

  Pembrolizumab × MSI-high additional modifiers: best diff +0.231 mo,
    p=0.22 — no benefit even in any MSI-high sub-stratum.
  Encorafenib × BRAF-V600E additional modifiers: strongest signal
    BRAF=1 & ecog_high=0 → -0.543 mo, p=8e-3 (apparent NUMERICAL HARM).
  Trastuzumab/tuc × HER2-amp additional modifiers: best signal
    her2=1 & stage_iv=0 → -0.557 mo, p=0.06 (numerical harm); inconsistent
    direction across strata.

Conclusion: even in canonical biomarker-positive patients, these three drugs
provide no PFS benefit in this cohort.

Iteration 11 — Cetuximab in canonical anti-EGFR-eligible patients + 3-feature scan
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Cetuximab in (KRAS-WT AND NRAS-WT AND BRAF-WT AND left-sided)
    : -0.067 mo, p=0.13 — NUMERICAL HARM, not significant.

3-feature subgroup scan (across all 18 modifiers): top signals for non-rego
treatments were all weak (|diff| ≤ 0.5 mo, best p ~ 1e-4) and far from
surviving correction for the thousands of cells tested. No new robust subgroups
of benefit identified for cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib,
or trastuzumab/tucatinib.

Iteration 12 — Final refined hypotheses
---------------------------------------
H12.1 (final regorafenib subgroup): treatment_regorafenib increases pfs_months
by approximately +2.74 months in patients with right_sided_primary=0 AND
kras_mutation=0 AND braf_v600e=0. Outside this subgroup the regorafenib effect
is statistically and clinically null (~+0.03 mo). The benefit is preserved
across NRAS status, ECOG, and stage_iv. This was confirmed by adjusted OLS
(interaction coefficient +2.74 mo, p≪1e-300) and by stratified means (inside
n=3461 on / 13911 off, +2.758 mo, p≪1e-300; outside n=6561 on / 26067 off,
+0.030 mo, p=0.29).

H12.2 (final negative result for the other treatments): adjusted main effects
of cetuximab (-0.013), bevacizumab (+0.003), pembrolizumab (~0), encorafenib
(-0.001), and trastuzumab_tucatinib (+0.024) are all within noise. No
mechanistic biomarker subgroup (KRAS/NRAS/BRAF for cetuximab; MSI-high for
pembrolizumab; BRAF-V600E for encorafenib; HER2-amplified for
trastuzumab/tucatinib) showed a benefit. Exhaustive 2- and 3-feature subgroup
searches did not surface any robust subgroup of benefit for these five
treatments.

Conclusions
-----------
1. PFS prognostic factors in this cohort are conventional: stage IV, higher
   ECOG, right-sided primary, KRAS or BRAF V600E mutation, higher CEA, lower
   albumin, higher weight loss, higher LDH all predict shorter PFS. Older age
   in this cohort correlates positively with PFS (cohort/case-mix effect).

2. Regorafenib has a large, robust, biomarker-restricted PFS benefit:
   ★★★ Final best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis ★★★
   In patients with right_sided_primary=0 AND kras_mutation=0 AND
   braf_v600e=0 (left-sided primary, KRAS wild-type, BRAF wild-type),
   treatment_regorafenib increases pfs_months by approximately +2.74 months
   (subgroup mean 6.875 on rego vs 4.117 off; p<<1e-300).
   Outside this subgroup (right-sided OR KRAS-mut OR BRAF-V600E),
   treatment_regorafenib has essentially no effect on pfs_months.
   The unfavorable values that suppress the regorafenib effect are
   right_sided_primary=1, kras_mutation=1, and braf_v600e=1; ANY of these
   abolishes the benefit. NRAS, MSI, HER2, ECOG, stage, age, sex, and labs do
   not further modify the effect within the subgroup.

3. Cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, and
   trastuzumab/tucatinib all have null adjusted PFS effects in this cohort
   — at the population level and in their canonical biomarker-positive
   subgroups. Despite the typical biology of anti-EGFR therapy in
   KRAS/NRAS-WT, anti-PD1 in MSI-high, anti-BRAF in V600E, and anti-HER2 in
   HER2-amplified disease, none of these expected subgroup effects is
   detectable here. No alternative robust subgroup of benefit was found by
   2- or 3-feature exhaustive search.

4. Bevacizumab in BRAF-V600E patients shows a small unadjusted decrement (-0.24
   mo, p=6e-3) that does not survive multivariable adjustment (interaction
   p=0.49) — most likely confounding rather than a real harmful interaction.
