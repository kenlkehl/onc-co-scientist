Oncology Dataset Analysis — ds001_crc (50,000 patients, 25 iterations)
================================================================

OUTCOME
The single outcome modeled is pfs_months (progression-free survival in months).
Across the cohort: mean 4.31, SD 2.29, median 4.21, range 0.00 to 16.25. The
distribution is right-skewed but non-degenerate, leaving plenty of variance for
features and treatments to explain.

I ran a 25-iteration propose-test-refine loop. The plan moved from main-effect
characterization (demographics, disease, biomarkers, labs, crude treatment
effects) to a multivariable adjusted treatment-effect model, then to targeted
biomarker–treatment interactions motivated by clinical priors, a systematic
treatment x feature heterogeneity screen, and finally to refinement of the one
strong heterogeneity signal that emerged.

I. PROGNOSTIC MAIN EFFECTS ON pfs_months
----------------------------------------
The dominant prognostic predictors (univariate, with adjusted multivariable
estimates that were essentially the same):
  • age_years            +0.176 mo / yr      (p<<0.001; R^2 alone = 0.59 — by
                                              far the strongest predictor)
  • ecog_ps              −1.190 mo / unit    (p<<0.001)
  • stage_iv             −1.352 mo           (p<<0.001)
  • albumin_g_dl         +0.469 mo / g/dL    (p<<0.001)
  • weight_loss_pct_6mo  −0.076 mo / %       (p<<0.001)
  • cea_ng_ml            −0.005 mo / ng/mL   (p<<0.001)
  • ldh_u_l              −0.0004 mo / U/L    (p≈0.01 unadj, p<<0.001 adj)
  • right_sided_primary  −0.305 mo (univ),
                         −0.287 mo (adj)     (p<<0.001)
  • kras_mutation        −0.354 mo (adj)     (p<<0.001)
  • braf_v600e           −0.357 mo (adj)     (p<<0.001)

Variables with no detectable main effect on pfs_months: sex_female, msi_high,
her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion, nras_mutation, crp_mg_l, nlr, hemoglobin_g_dl,
alkaline_phosphatase_u_l, ast_u_l, alt_u_l, total_bilirubin_mg_dl,
creatinine_mg_dl, bun_mg_dl, sodium_meq_l, potassium_meq_l, calcium_mg_dl.

Important nuance discovered later: the apparent "prognostic" main effects of
kras_mutation, braf_v600e, right_sided_primary, and cea_ng_ml are largely or
entirely an artifact of treatment-effect heterogeneity (see Section IV).
Patients with any of these unfavorable values do not benefit from regorafenib;
patients without them do. Once a regorafenib*responder-subgroup interaction is
included in the multivariable model, the residual main effects of those
variables drop to ~0 (kras p=0.17, braf p=0.79, right-sided p=0.84,
cea_ng_ml p=0.34). They are effect-modifying markers, not prognostic markers.

II. CRUDE TREATMENT EFFECTS ON pfs_months
-----------------------------------------
Of the six treatment indicators, only treatment_regorafenib showed a non-null
crude effect:
  • treatment_cetuximab            −0.038 mo   (p=0.09)  not significant
  • treatment_bevacizumab          −0.019 mo   (p=0.35)  not significant
  • treatment_pembrolizumab        +0.005 mo   (p=0.86)  not significant
  • treatment_encorafenib          +0.005 mo   (p=0.87)  not significant
  • treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib −0.044 mo  (p=0.25)  not significant
  • treatment_regorafenib          +0.972 mo   (p<<0.001) ★ supported

In a multivariable OLS with all features and all six treatment indicators,
regorafenib retains a +0.943 mo adjusted main effect (t=98). All five other
treatments are within ±0.03 mo of zero with non-significant p-values
(trastuzumab/tucatinib +0.026 mo, p=0.07, was the closest to a signal but
remained tiny).

III. CLINICALLY MOTIVATED BIOMARKER × TREATMENT INTERACTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------
I tested the textbook precision-oncology hypotheses for CRC and, somewhat
surprisingly, none of them held in this cohort:

  • cetuximab benefit in kras_mutation=0 vs 1 ........ interaction +0.022,
    p=0.25; eff in KRAS WT −0.020, KRAS mut +0.002. NOT supported.
  • cetuximab benefit in braf_v600e=0 vs 1 ........... interaction +0.002,
    p=0.96. NOT supported.
  • cetuximab benefit in left-sided vs right-sided ... interaction +0.022,
    p=0.25. NOT supported. (Even in RAS/RAF WT left-sided n=8866, mean pfs
    on cetuximab 4.32 vs off 4.36.)
  • pembrolizumab benefit in msi_high=1 .............. interaction +0.056,
    p=0.30; mean pfs MSI-H on pembro 4.30 vs off 4.29. NOT supported.
  • encorafenib benefit in braf_v600e=1 .............. interaction +0.004,
    p=0.95. NOT supported.
  • encorafenib + cetuximab combo within braf_v600e .. mean pfs on combo
    4.15 (n=58) vs others V600E 4.09 (n=2214). NOT supported.
  • trastuzumab/tucatinib benefit in her2_amplified=1  interaction −0.007,
    p=0.94; mean pfs HER2+ on tx 4.27 vs off 4.26. NOT supported.

Bottom line: the canonical CRC biomarker-targeted regimens have no detectable
PFS benefit in this cohort, even within their indicated biomarker subgroups.

IV. PRIMARY HETEROGENEITY FINDING — REGORAFENIB
-----------------------------------------------
The dominant treatment-effect heterogeneity in the dataset is for
treatment_regorafenib. A systematic interaction screen against every binary
modifier (sex_female, stage_iv, right_sided_primary, kras_mutation,
nras_mutation, braf_v600e, msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion) produced
three highly-significant suppressors and one continuous-modifier suppressor:

  Regorafenib × kras_mutation        : interaction −1.626 mo (p<<0.001)
    • Effect in KRAS WT (n_on=5816)   = +1.656 mo
    • Effect in KRAS mut (n_on=4206)  = +0.027 mo

  Regorafenib × braf_v600e           : interaction −0.992 mo (p≈5e-98)
    • Effect in BRAF WT (n_on=9554)   = +1.027 mo
    • Effect in BRAF V600E (n_on=468) = −0.156 mo

  Regorafenib × right_sided_primary  : interaction −1.456 mo (p<<0.001)
    • Effect in left-sided (n_on=6524)  = +1.480 mo
    • Effect in right-sided (n_on=3498) = +0.026 mo

  Regorafenib × cea_ng_ml (within KRAS WT & BRAF WT & left-sided):
    interaction −0.065 mo per ng/mL CEA (p<<0.001)
    • CEA <5  : +5.013 mo  (n_on=1903)
    • CEA 5-10: +0.028 mo
    • CEA 10+ : essentially 0 (range −0.22 to +0.97 across higher bins)

NRAS, MSI-H, HER2, NTRK, sex, age, ECOG, stage, albumin, weight loss, LDH,
CRP, NLR, and hemoglobin do NOT credibly modify the regorafenib effect.
(NRAS mutation looked like an "enhancer" — interaction +0.89, p≈0 — but this
is mechanically because most NRAS-mutant patients in the cohort happen to
satisfy the KRAS WT, BRAF WT, left-sided, low-CEA conditions; conditioning on
those four, NRAS does not modify the effect, with within-subgroup deltas of
about ±0.25 mo.)

The complete responder subgroup definition (variables whose unfavorable values
each independently ablate the effect):

    RESPONDER  =  kras_mutation = 0
              AND braf_v600e    = 0
              AND right_sided_primary = 0
              AND cea_ng_ml     < 5 (ng/mL)

Within this four-way subgroup (n = 9,326; 1,903 on regorafenib, 7,423 off):
  • mean pfs_months on regorafenib  = 9.10
  • mean pfs_months off regorafenib = 4.09
  • effect estimate                 = +5.013 months (t=92.7, p<<0.001)

Outside this subgroup (n = 40,674; 8,119 on regorafenib, 32,555 off):
  • effect estimate                 = +0.025 months (p=0.33)

In a fully adjusted OLS that includes age, ECOG, stage, albumin, weight loss,
LDH, all 10 features used as suppressors or candidate modifiers, and all six
treatment indicators, the regorafenib × responder interaction term is +4.981
months (SE 0.006, t≈893, p<<0.001), and the within-stratum main effects of
treatment_regorafenib (−0.003, p=0.28), kras_mutation (−0.003, p=0.17),
braf_v600e (+0.001, p=0.79), right_sided_primary (+0.0004, p=0.84), and
cea_ng_ml (−6e-5, p=0.34) are all null. This confirms (a) the effect is not
explained by confounding with any measured prognostic variable, and (b) those
four "unfavorable" variables are pure effect modifiers in this cohort, not
prognosticators.

Sensitivity to the CEA cutoff: thresholds of 2, 3, and 5 ng/mL all yield
within-responder regorafenib effects of ~+5.0 mo. A cutoff of 10 ng/mL
dilutes the effect to +3.6 mo (because the 5–10 ng/mL bin contributes ≈0).
The natural threshold is therefore CEA < 5 ng/mL.

Each unfavorable feature independently is sufficient to suppress the benefit
(within otherwise responder-eligible patients):
  • Otherwise eligible but CEA ≥ 5            : +0.005 mo (n_on=1558)
  • KRAS mut alone (others favorable)         : +0.073 mo (n_on=1477)
  • BRAF V600E alone (others favorable)       : −0.175 mo (n_on=154)
  • Right-sided alone (others favorable)      : +0.112 mo (n_on=1020)

Within the responder subgroup, further stratification by NRAS, MSI, HER2,
NTRK, and sex changes the regorafenib effect by ≤0.3 mo and never reverses
direction; the four-way subgroup definition is sufficient.

V. SUMMARY OF SUPPORTED VS REFUTED HYPOTHESES
---------------------------------------------
SUPPORTED:
  • age_years, ecog_ps, stage_iv, right_sided_primary, kras_mutation,
    braf_v600e, albumin_g_dl, weight_loss_pct_6mo, cea_ng_ml, ldh_u_l are
    associated with pfs_months in the expected directions (univariate).
  • treatment_regorafenib has a positive crude and adjusted main effect on
    pfs_months.
  • The regorafenib effect is concentrated in patients with kras_mutation=0
    AND braf_v600e=0 AND right_sided_primary=0 AND cea_ng_ml<5; effect within
    subgroup ≈ +5 months, outside ≈ 0.
  • Each of those four unfavorable values independently suppresses the
    regorafenib effect.
  • The "prognostic" appearance of kras_mutation, braf_v600e,
    right_sided_primary, and cea_ng_ml is largely driven by their role as
    effect modifiers of regorafenib; their pure prognostic main effect is
    near-null once the regorafenib*responder interaction is modeled.

REFUTED / NOT SUPPORTED IN THIS COHORT:
  • Cetuximab benefit in RAS/RAF WT (or left-sided) subgroup.
  • Pembrolizumab benefit in MSI-H.
  • Encorafenib (or encorafenib+cetuximab) benefit in BRAF V600E.
  • Trastuzumab/tucatinib benefit in HER2-amplified.
  • Bevacizumab × any biomarker subgroup (one marginal p=0.033 with msi_high
    treated as a chance finding given multiple testing).
  • Within-responder modifiers nras_mutation, msi_high, her2_amplified,
    ntrk_fusion, sex_female changing the regorafenib effect.

VI. FINAL CONSOLIDATED TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS
-----------------------------------------------------------
Outcome               : pfs_months
Treatment             : treatment_regorafenib
Direction of effect   : positive (longer pfs_months)
Magnitude             : +5.0 months within the responder subgroup, ~0 outside
Subgroup predicate    : kras_mutation == 0
                  AND braf_v600e == 0
                  AND right_sided_primary == 0
                  AND cea_ng_ml < 5
Suppressor variables  : kras_mutation==1; braf_v600e==1; right_sided_primary==1;
                        cea_ng_ml ≥ 5. Any single unfavorable value abolishes
                        the benefit.

No analogous treatment-effect subgroup is supported for cetuximab,
bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, or trastuzumab/tucatinib in this
cohort.
