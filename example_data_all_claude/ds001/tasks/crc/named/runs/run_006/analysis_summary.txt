ANALYSIS SUMMARY — Dataset ds001_crc (50,000 colorectal cancer patient records)
================================================================================

Outcome of interest: pfs_months (continuous; cohort mean 4.31, SD 2.29, median 4.21).

OVERVIEW OF APPROACH
--------------------
Across 25 iterations, I (1) characterised the cohort, (2) screened every feature
for a univariable association with pfs_months, (3) screened the six available
treatments for unadjusted and adjusted main effects, (4) ran a systematic
treatment-by-binary-feature and treatment-by-continuous-feature interaction
screen, and (5) refined the strongest signal — regorafenib heterogeneity — by
nested subgroup decomposition until I had a complete predicate set defining the
responding subgroup.

PROGNOSTIC FEATURES (main effects on pfs_months)
------------------------------------------------
Negative prognostic features, all highly significant in both univariable and
fully adjusted multivariable models (R^2 = 0.86):
  - ecog_ps              -1.17 mo per unit increase    (p < 1e-300)
  - stage_iv             -1.36 mo                      (p < 1e-300)
  - kras_mutation        -0.35 mo                      (p < 1e-300)
  - braf_v600e           -0.36 mo                      (p ~ 4e-79)
  - right_sided_primary  -0.29 mo                      (p ~ 9e-273)
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo  -0.076 mo per %               (p < 1e-300)
  - cea_ng_ml            -0.0050 mo per ng/mL          (p ~ 2e-97)
  - ldh_u_l              -0.00042 mo per U/L           (p ~ 7e-19)

Positive prognostic features:
  - albumin_g_dl         +0.47 mo per g/dL             (p < 1e-300)
  - age_years            +0.18 mo per year             (p < 1e-300; counterintuitive direction)

Null in adjusted model (and most univariable tests): sex_female, nras_mutation,
msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion, hemoglobin_g_dl, crp_mg_l, nlr,
alkaline_phosphatase, AST, ALT, total_bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium,
potassium, calcium. NRAS mutation showed a positive univariable coefficient
(+0.22 mo, p=2e-4) but was null after adjustment (p=0.42).

TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS
----------------------
Of the six treatments, only regorafenib has a detectable main effect:
  - treatment_regorafenib            +0.97 mo unadjusted, +0.94 mo adjusted (p < 1e-300)
  - treatment_cetuximab              null (-0.04, p=0.09 unadjusted; -0.011, p=0.19 adjusted)
  - treatment_bevacizumab            null
  - treatment_pembrolizumab          null
  - treatment_encorafenib            null
  - treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib  null (p=0.07 adjusted)

The five "targeted/IO" agents show no detectable PFS benefit, even when
restricted to their canonical biomarker-positive subgroups:
  - cetuximab in left-sided pan-RAS/BRAF wild-type (n=16,443):  -0.067 mo, p=0.12
  - pembrolizumab in MSI-high (n=2,513):                          +0.007 mo, p=0.96
  - encorafenib in BRAF V600E (n=2,272):                          -0.13 mo,  p=0.33
  - trastuzumab/tucatinib in HER2-amplified (n=1,504):            +0.017 mo, p=0.93
  - bevacizumab in any subgroup tested (KRAS, BRAF, MSI, sided, stage_iv): null

This is the key empirical finding for these five drugs — in this dataset, none
of the canonical CRC precision-oncology indications produce a measurable PFS
signal.

REGORAFENIB HETEROGENEITY — PRIMARY SIGNAL
------------------------------------------
The interaction screen identified large, biologically plausible-looking
heterogeneity for regorafenib:

  treatment_regorafenib x kras_mutation:        coef = -1.63, p ~ 7.7e-226
  treatment_regorafenib x braf_v600e:           coef = -1.18, p ~ 6e-23
  treatment_regorafenib x right_sided_primary:  coef = -1.45, p ~ 4e-168
  treatment_regorafenib x nras_mutation:        coef = +0.99, p ~ 1.2e-11   (augments)
  treatment_regorafenib x msi_high:             null (p=0.85)
  treatment_regorafenib x her2_amplified:       null (p=0.46)
  treatment_regorafenib x ECOG / stage_iv / sex: null or trivially small

Stratifying the cohort by the joint predicate left-sided AND KRAS-WT AND
BRAF-WT cleanly resolves the heterogeneity:

  Within (right_sided_primary=0 AND kras_mutation=0 AND braf_v600e=0)
    n = 17,372 patients
    Mean PFS regorafenib-treated:  6.88 months
    Mean PFS untreated:            4.12 months
    Difference:                    +2.76 months    (p effectively 0)

  Outside this subgroup (right OR KRAS-mut OR BRAF-mut)
    n = 32,628 patients
    Mean PFS regorafenib-treated:  4.15 months
    Mean PFS untreated:            4.12 months
    Difference:                    +0.030 months   (p = 0.29)

Decomposition — each of the three predicates is independently necessary:
  Right-sided + KRAS-WT + BRAF-WT  (n=9,458):  rego diff = +0.083, p=0.12  (NS)
  Left + KRAS-mut + BRAF-WT        (n=13,490): rego diff = +0.061, p=0.17  (NS)
  Left + KRAS-WT + BRAF-mut        (n=1,481):  rego diff = -0.20,  p=0.12  (NS)

A three-way interaction model (rego x kras x braf x right) confirms:
each of the three modifiers contributes ~ -1.5 to -2.0 mo to the regorafenib
coefficient independently. The KRAS-mut + BRAF-mut combination has zero patients
in the dataset, so co-occurrence cannot be tested.

NRAS mutation does NOT abolish benefit. Within the responding subgroup:
  Left + KRAS-WT + BRAF-WT + NRAS-WT (n=16,443):  rego diff = +2.75, p ~ 0
  Left + KRAS-WT + BRAF-WT + NRAS-mut (n=929):    rego diff = +2.91, p ~ 7e-25

ADDITIONAL MODIFIERS WITHIN THE RESPONDING SUBGROUP
---------------------------------------------------
Within the (left + KRAS-WT + BRAF-WT) subgroup, additional smaller-magnitude
modifiers were detected:

  rego x cea_ng_ml      coef = -0.065 mo per ng/mL    (p ~ 7e-136)
  rego x sex_female     coef = +0.31 mo               (p ~ 5e-4)
  rego x stage_iv       coef = +0.24 mo               (p ~ 5e-3)

Outside the responding subgroup, the rego-x-CEA interaction is absent
(coef = +0.0015, p = 0.41), confirming this modifier is specific to the
responding subgroup. The CEA modifier is the largest of the within-subgroup
effects — the regorafenib benefit shrinks substantially as baseline CEA rises.

The regorafenib benefit is approximately constant across ECOG strata
(ECOG 0: +0.92, ECOG 1: +0.99, ECOG 2: +0.90), and not modified by MSI status,
HER2 amplification, NTRK fusion, age, hemoglobin, LDH, CRP, NLR, or weight loss.

NULL FINDINGS WORTH RECORDING
-----------------------------
- Cetuximab + bevacizumab interaction: null (p=0.22).
- Bevacizumab x BRAF V600E: borderline (interaction coef -0.23, p=0.022) but
  bevacizumab effect is null in both the BRAF-WT and BRAF-mutated stratum.
- Pembrolizumab x right_sided_primary interaction: borderline (coef -0.12,
  p=0.05) but the absolute effect is small and the marginal p in the context
  of the full interaction screen is consistent with chance.
- Pembrolizumab x crp_mg_l: borderline (p=0.02), small effect.

FINAL HYPOTHESES (ground-truth-style summary)
---------------------------------------------
Best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis:

  Regorafenib (treatment_regorafenib=1) prolongs progression-free survival
  (pfs_months) in this CRC cohort if and only if the patient is left-sided
  primary (right_sided_primary=0) AND KRAS wild-type (kras_mutation=0) AND
  BRAF wild-type (braf_v600e=0). Within this subgroup the mean PFS gain is
  approximately +2.76 months; outside it the effect is null. The benefit is
  further attenuated by higher baseline CEA but is not modified by NRAS
  status, ECOG, MSI status, HER2 amplification, or sidedness once the
  defining left-sided predicate is met.

Best-supported null treatment hypotheses:

  In this cohort, none of treatment_cetuximab, treatment_bevacizumab,
  treatment_pembrolizumab, treatment_encorafenib, or
  treatment_trastuzumab_tucatinib produce a detectable PFS effect — overall,
  in their canonical biomarker-defined subgroups, or in any of the screened
  binary or continuous interactions. This contrasts with conventional CRC
  biology and likely reflects how this dataset was constructed.

CONCLUSIONS
-----------
1) Strong, classical prognostic signal: ECOG, stage IV, sidedness, KRAS, BRAF,
   weight loss, CEA, LDH all degrade PFS; albumin and (counterintuitively in
   this dataset) age improve it.

2) The only treatment with any detectable PFS benefit is regorafenib.

3) The regorafenib benefit is highly heterogeneous and concentrated in a
   well-defined subgroup: left-sided + KRAS-WT + BRAF-WT (the same wild-type
   profile that, in real clinical CRC practice, predicts EGFR-targeted therapy
   benefit). All three predicates are independently required.

4) The five other treatments (cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab,
   encorafenib, trastuzumab/tucatinib) show no measurable PFS benefit in any
   subgroup tested, including their canonical biomarker-positive subgroups.
