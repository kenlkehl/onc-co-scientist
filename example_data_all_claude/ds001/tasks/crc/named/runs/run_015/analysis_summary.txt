Analysis summary — ds001_crc (n=50,000; outcome: pfs_months)
===========================================================

Goal
----
Explore the cohort across 25 iterations of propose / analyze / refine and identify
clinically meaningful main effects, subgroup heterogeneity, and treatment-effect
modifiers among 6 treatments and the available clinical and molecular features.


Iterations 1-5 — clinical and molecular MAIN EFFECTS on pfs_months
------------------------------------------------------------------
Strongest prognostic features (negative = shorter PFS):
  - ecog_ps               : slope = -1.19 mo per ECOG point (p~0; 0=5.27, 1=4.05, 2=2.91 mo).
  - stage_iv              : -1.35 mo (3.70 vs 5.05; p~0).
  - right_sided_primary   : -0.31 mo (4.11 vs 4.42; p=1e-49).
  - kras_mutation         : -0.33 mo (4.12 vs 4.45; p=2e-58).
  - braf_v600e            : -0.23 mo (4.09 vs 4.32; p=3e-7).
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo   : slope -0.073 mo per % loss (p=2e-167).
  - albumin_g_dl          : slope +0.47 mo per g/dL (p=3e-115).
  - cea_ng_ml             : slope -0.005 mo per ng/mL (p=3e-17).
  - alkaline_phosphatase  : slope -0.0006 mo (p=4e-3).
  - age_years             : slope +0.18 mo per year (p~0; older patients fare slightly better here).
  - nras_mutation         : +0.22 mo (modest positive — likely confounded with downstream subgroup; see below).

Null on PFS (no main effect): sex_female, msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion, nlr,
crp_mg_l, hemoglobin, AST, ALT, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium.


Iterations 6-11 — TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS (unadjusted and covariate-adjusted)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Five of six treatments have NO main effect on pfs_months across the cohort:
  - cetuximab           : -0.04 mo (p=0.09); adjusted -0.012 (p=0.23).
  - bevacizumab         : -0.02 mo (p=0.35); adjusted +0.007 (p=0.43).
  - pembrolizumab       : +0.01 mo (p=0.86); adjusted +0.006 (p=0.64).
  - encorafenib         : +0.01 mo (p=0.87); adjusted +0.002 (p=0.91).
  - trastuzumab/tucatinib: -0.04 mo (p=0.24); adjusted +0.016 (p=0.35).
  - REGORAFENIB         : +0.97 mo (p~0); adjusted +0.94 (p~0).

Regorafenib is the only treatment with a meaningful main effect.


Iterations 12-19 — TREATMENT × BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS
-----------------------------------------------------
The "canonical" colorectal-cancer biomarker-treatment pairings are absent:
  - cetuximab × kras_mutation             : interaction p=0.66 (null).
  - cetuximab × nras_mutation             : p=0.96 (null).
  - cetuximab × braf_v600e                : p=0.81 (null).
  - cetuximab × right_sided_primary       : p=0.58 (null).
  - pembrolizumab × msi_high              : p=0.99 (null; pembro effect in MSI-high = +0.01 mo).
  - encorafenib × braf_v600e              : p=0.38 (null; in BRAF V600E pts encorafenib effect = -0.13, p=0.33).
  - trast/tuc × her2_amplified            : p=0.78 (null; in HER2-amp pts trast/tuc effect = +0.02, p=0.93).
  - bevacizumab x braf_v600e              : modest negative interaction (-0.23, p=0.02) but tiny effect.

Iteration 24 confirmed the null findings by running the head-to-head subgroup
ttests directly: in MSI-high, in BRAF V600E, in HER2-amp, and in RAS-wt+BRAF-wt+left,
the corresponding "targeted" therapy did not prolong PFS.

In short — the biomarker-treatment pairings that one would expect to be
treatment-effect modifiers in real-world colorectal cancer therapy are NOT present
in this dataset. This is an unusual feature of ds001_crc.


Iteration 17 (and 22) — REGORAFENIB heterogeneity search
--------------------------------------------------------
Single-feature interactions with treatment_regorafenib (each tested via
pfs ~ rego * marker):
  - kras_mutation         : interaction = -1.63 (p=8e-226). Effect in KRAS-mut = +0.03; in KRAS-wt = +1.66.
  - braf_v600e            : interaction = -1.18 (p=6e-23). Effect in BRAF V600E = -0.16; in BRAF-wt = +1.03.
  - right_sided_primary   : interaction = -1.45 (p=4e-168). Effect in right-sided = +0.03; in left = +1.48.
  - nras_mutation         : interaction = +0.99 (p=1e-11) — but this is largely because NRAS-mut is mutually
                            exclusive with KRAS-mut (i.e., NRAS-mut cases are KRAS-wt by construction),
                            so this is a marker of the same enriched subgroup.
  - msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion, stage_iv, sex_female, ecog_ps, NLR, CRP, LDH, albumin, weight_loss
    : all non-significant interactions individually with regorafenib.

Note: each unfavorable feature (KRAS-mut, BRAF V600E, right-sided) almost completely
suppresses the regorafenib effect on its own.


Iteration 21 — TREE-BASED HETEROGENEITY SEARCH for regorafenib
--------------------------------------------------------------
A T-learner (gradient boosting on rego-treated vs untreated) generates per-patient
treatment-effect estimates tau_hat; a depth-3 regression tree on tau_hat splits on:
  level 1: cea_ng_ml <= 4.95
  level 2: kras_mutation
  level 3: right_sided_primary
The branch with cea_ng_ml<=4.95, kras_mutation=0, right_sided_primary=0 has the
highest leaf value (~+3.97 mo predicted treatment effect). All other branches are
near zero. This independently identifies the same KRAS-wt + left-sided + low-CEA
combination, with BRAF V600E as a tertiary refinement seen in the iteration-22 grid.


Iteration 22 — EXHAUSTIVE 2-WAY SUBGROUP ENUMERATION for regorafenib
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Top regorafenib-benefit two-feature subgroups (mean PFS gain over no-rego):
  kras_mutation=0 & right_sided_primary=0 : +2.52 mo (p~0; n_rego=3,763)
  nras_mutation=1 & her2_amplified=0      : +1.96 mo (p=2e-21; n_rego=307)  -- driven by KRAS-wt enrichment
  nras_mutation=1 & ntrk_fusion=0         : +1.93 mo (p=2e-21)              -- same
  kras_mutation=0 & nras_mutation=1       : +1.93 mo (p=3e-21)              -- same
Bottom (no benefit / slight harm):
  braf_v600e=1 & right_sided_primary=0   : -0.20 mo
  braf_v600e=1 & sex_female=1            : -0.18 mo
  braf_v600e=1 & msi_high=0              : -0.18 mo
  kras_mutation=1 & msi_high=1           : -0.17 mo


Iterations 20, 23, 25 — JOINT REGORAFENIB SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS
-------------------------------------------------------------
Triple-modifier subgroup (KRAS-wt & BRAF-wt & left-sided), n=17,372:
  INSIDE  : rego mean PFS = 6.88, ctrl 4.12, effect = +2.76 mo, p~0 (n_rego=3,461).
  OUTSIDE : rego mean PFS = 4.15, ctrl 4.12, effect = +0.03 mo, p=0.29.
  Adjusted joint OLS confirms three independent negative interactions:
    rego:kras   = -1.78, rego:braf   = -1.75, rego:right_sided = -1.45  (all p~0).

Adding low CEA (cea_ng_ml <= 5) further sharpens the subgroup — within the triple-
modifier subgroup the rego effect splits cleanly by CEA:
  CEA <= 5  : rego effect = +5.01 mo (PFS 9.10 vs 4.09; p~0; n_rego=1,905).
  CEA  > 5  : rego effect = +0.00 mo (p=0.94; null).
Outside the triple-modifier subgroup, CEA does NOT modify the rego effect (still ~0).
Whole-cohort rego x cea_ng_ml interaction is highly significant (p=9e-58) but the
threshold ~5 ng/mL cleanly separates "responder" from "non-responder" in the
KRAS-wt + BRAF-wt + left-sided cohort.

Final 4-feature subgroup (KRAS-wt + BRAF-wt + left-sided + CEA<=5), n=9,338:
  INSIDE  : rego mean PFS = 9.10 vs ctrl 4.09; effect = +5.01 mo, p~0 (n_rego=1,905, n_ctrl=7,433).
  OUTSIDE : rego effect = +0.02 mo, p=0.36 (n_rego=8,117, n_ctrl=32,565).

Adjusted joint OLS (treatment_regorafenib × kras_mutation, × braf_v600e,
× right_sided_primary, × cea_low(=cea<=5), plus age/sex/ECOG/stage IV):
  rego main (KRAS-wt, BRAF-wt, left, CEA>5 baseline) = +1.32  (p~0)
  rego:kras_mutation                                  = -1.76  (p~0)
  rego:braf_v600e                                     = -1.69  (p~0)
  rego:right_sided_primary                            = -1.44  (p~0)
  rego:cea_low                                        = +1.72  (p~0)
The four modifier interactions are independently strong; the additive multivariable
estimate of rego effect inside the responder subgroup matches the unadjusted +5 mo.


Hypotheses supported / refuted across the full transcript
---------------------------------------------------------
SUPPORTED (significant in expected direction):
  - Higher ECOG, stage IV, right-sided, KRAS, BRAF V600E, weight loss, low albumin,
    high CEA, high alkaline phosphatase => shorter PFS.
  - regorafenib main effect on PFS is positive.
  - regorafenib effect is strongly modified by KRAS, BRAF V600E, side, and CEA;
    the four-feature responder subgroup KRAS-wt + BRAF-wt + left-sided + CEA<=5
    captures essentially all of the regorafenib benefit (~+5 mo PFS in subgroup;
    ~0 outside).

REFUTED (expected positive effect was null):
  - cetuximab → no effect overall, no effect in RAS-wt + BRAF-wt + left subgroup
    (the canonical anti-EGFR best-responder population), and no interaction with
    KRAS, NRAS, BRAF, or side.
  - bevacizumab → no overall effect; no clinically meaningful interactions.
  - pembrolizumab → no overall effect; no enrichment in MSI-high.
  - encorafenib → no overall effect; no benefit in BRAF V600E.
  - trastuzumab/tucatinib → no overall effect; no benefit in HER2-amplified.

NEUTRAL / NULL on prognosis: sex, MSI-high, HER2-amplified, NTRK fusion, NLR,
hemoglobin, transaminases, creatinine, BUN, electrolytes.


Bottom-line clinical narrative
------------------------------
In ds001_crc the only treatment with a real PFS benefit is regorafenib, and its
benefit is gated by FOUR features simultaneously: KRAS-wildtype, BRAF V600E-
wildtype, left-sided primary, and low CEA (<=~5 ng/mL). Patients meeting all four
criteria (~19% of the cohort) gain roughly 5 months of PFS on regorafenib (median
PFS doubles from ~4 to ~9 mo), whereas patients failing any one of the four
criteria derive essentially no PFS benefit. The "classical" molecular-agent
matchings expected from the colorectal-cancer literature (cetuximab in RAS/BRAF-
wildtype + left-sided; pembrolizumab in MSI-high; encorafenib in BRAF V600E;
trastuzumab/tucatinib in HER2-amplified) are NOT present in this dataset — they
are uniformly null both at the main-effect level and inside the relevant biomarker
subgroup, despite >100 treated patients in each marker-positive cohort. This is
the most striking and unusual structural feature of the cohort.
