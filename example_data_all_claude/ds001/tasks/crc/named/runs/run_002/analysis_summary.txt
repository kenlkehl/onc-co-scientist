ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_crc (50,000-patient colorectal cancer cohort)
========================================================================

OBJECTIVE
---------
Identify clinically meaningful patterns in a 50,000-patient colorectal cancer
EHR-derived cohort, with a focus on (a) prognostic main effects on
pfs_months, (b) treatment main effects, and (c) treatment-effect
heterogeneity that defines clinically actionable subgroups. The cohort
contains six treatment indicators (cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab,
encorafenib, trastuzumab/tucatinib, regorafenib), six molecular biomarkers
(KRAS, NRAS, BRAF V600E, MSI-high, HER2-amplified, NTRK fusion), basic
clinical features (age, sex, ECOG, stage, sidedness), and 16 routine
laboratory values. The single outcome is pfs_months.

ITERATION-BY-ITERATION SYNTHESIS
--------------------------------

Iteration 1 — clinical prognostic main effects.
ECOG performance status, stage IV, and right-sided primary were each
strongly associated with shorter PFS (slopes -1.19/level, -1.35, and -0.31
respectively, all p<<0.001). Sex was null. Age years had a striking
POSITIVE coefficient (+0.18 per year, p<<0.001) — older patients in this
cohort have longer PFS, opposite the usual clinical expectation. This is a
notable cohort feature that recurs in every multivariable model.

Iteration 2 — laboratory prognostic main effects.
Albumin was the strongest favorable prognostic lab (+0.47 months per g/dL,
p~3e-115). Weight loss was the strongest adverse prognostic lab (-0.073
months per percentage point, p~2e-167). CEA (-0.005/ng/mL) and LDH
(-0.0003/U/L) reached significance with very small slopes. Alkaline
phosphatase and total bilirubin were weakly significant. CRP, NLR,
hemoglobin, AST, ALT, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, and creatinine were
non-significant univariably.

Iteration 3 — biomarker and treatment main effects.
KRAS mutation (-0.33 months) and BRAF V600E (-0.23 months) were adverse
prognostic markers. NRAS mutation showed a small unexpected positive
coefficient (+0.22). MSI-high, HER2 amplification, and NTRK fusion had
no detectable prognostic main effect. Among the six treatments, only
regorafenib had a non-null unadjusted main effect (+0.97 months, p<1e-300).
Cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, and trastuzumab/
tucatinib all had absolute main effects below 0.05 months.

Iteration 4 — treatment x biomarker interactions for canonical pairings.
Tested cetuximab x KRAS / NRAS / BRAF / right-sided, pembrolizumab x MSI,
encorafenib x BRAF V600E, and trastuzumab/tucatinib x HER2. NONE of these
canonical pairings produced a significant interaction or any meaningful
within-subgroup treatment benefit. Most striking: in MSI-high (n=2513),
pembrolizumab on vs off PFS was 4.30 vs 4.29; in BRAF V600E (n=2272)
encorafenib was 3.98 vs 4.11; in HER2 amplified (n=1504) T+T was 4.27 vs
4.26; in left-sided RAS+BRAF WT (n=16443) cetuximab was 4.62 vs 4.68. The
biomarker-targeted therapies behave as if inert in this cohort.

Iteration 5 — multivariable adjustment.
A 24-covariate OLS confirmed regorafenib's adjusted PFS effect at +0.94
months (essentially unchanged from unadjusted), while every other treatment
adjusted coefficient was within ±0.03 months and p>=0.07. Baseline feature
distributions of regorafenib-treated vs other patients are nearly identical
(ECOG 0.79 vs 0.80, age 65.1 vs 65.0, biomarker frequencies all matched),
so the regorafenib main effect is real, not a confounding artifact.

Iteration 6 — exhaustive treatment x feature interaction screen.
For each treatment, ran 27 single-feature interaction models (each
controlling for ECOG, stage, age, albumin, weight loss). For cetuximab,
bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, and trastuzumab/tucatinib, the
smallest interaction p-values were 0.014-0.08 with coefficients of
~0.01-0.08 magnitude — no clear pattern, consistent with noise. For
regorafenib, interactions with kras_mutation (-1.63), right_sided_primary
(-1.46), cea_ng_ml (-0.026/ng/mL), braf_v600e (-0.99), and albumin (-0.085)
were all extremely significant (p < 1e-50 to < 1e-300) with large
biologically coherent effect sizes.

Iteration 7 — regorafenib joint subgroups.
Subgroup means by KRAS, BRAF, and sidedness all confirmed the screen:
regorafenib effect is +1.66 months in KRAS WT vs 0.03 in KRAS MUT;
+1.03 in BRAF WT vs -0.16 in BRAF V600E; +1.48 in left-sided vs +0.03 in
right-sided. Joint subgroups compound: KRAS+BRAF WT alone +1.81 months;
add left-sided and the effect rises to +2.76 months. The 3-way
interaction model gives main rego coefficient +2.29 with rego x KRAS-mut
-1.79, rego x BRAF V600E -1.77, rego x right-sided -1.47, all
p<1e-300. Each unfavorable predicate roughly halves the benefit.

Iteration 8 — re-test of canonical targeted-therapy subgroups with full
adjustment, plus cetux+enco BRAF combo.
Adjusted models within each agent's canonical biomarker subgroup confirmed
the iteration-4 finding: cetuximab in left-sided RAS+BRAF WT -0.026
(p=0.16); pembrolizumab in MSI-high +0.06 (p=0.26); encorafenib in BRAF
V600E -0.006 (p=0.69); T+T in HER2 amplified +0.004 (p=0.97). The
cetuximab+encorafenib combination in BRAF V600E (n=58) was also null
(+0.06 months, p=0.83). No biomarker-targeted therapy in this dataset
shows efficacy on PFS.

Iteration 9 — tree-based subgroup discovery (T-learner CATE).
A gradient-boosting T-learner generated patient-level treatment-effect
estimates (tau_i) for each treatment; a depth-3 regression tree was fit
to tau_i. For regorafenib the top leaf (mean tau = +3.97, n=10056) was
defined exactly as cea_ng_ml ≤ 4.95 AND kras_mutation = 0 AND
right_sided_primary = 0 — independently recovering the same subgroup
identified by direct interaction analysis. For all five other treatments
no leaf exceeded ±0.4 months with a coherent biological definition. The
tree-based and regression-based subgroup analyses agree.

Iteration 10 — CEA refinement.
Within the KRAS+BRAF WT, left-sided subgroup, the regorafenib x
cea_ng_ml interaction is -0.064 months per ng/mL (p<1e-300); a cutpoint
scan shows the regorafenib effect is essentially flat at +5.0 months for
CEA cutoffs of 2-5 ng/mL, then declines (CEA<=10 → +3.6, CEA<=20 →
+3.1). A clean cutpoint at ~5 ng/mL emerges. Within the favorable
subgroup, ECOG (0/1/2: +4.87/+5.09/+4.81) and stage IV (0/1: +5.03/+5.01)
do NOT modify the effect — the +5-month benefit is uniform across
performance status and stage.

Iteration 11 — final 4-predicate subgroup verification.
Defined favorable subgroup as kras_mutation=0 AND braf_v600e=0 AND
right_sided_primary=0 AND cea_ng_ml ≤ 5 (n=9338, of whom 1905 received
regorafenib). Inside subgroup: PFS on regorafenib 9.10 vs off 4.09
(diff +5.01 months, p<<1e-300). Outside subgroup: PFS 4.15 vs 4.12
(diff +0.024, p=0.36, statistically null). Necessity of each predicate
was confirmed by single-predicate violation tests, all of which knocked
the within-subgroup effect to ≤0.17 months. NRAS mutation status had no
additional modulation. Within the favorable subgroup, none of the other
five treatments interacted with regorafenib (all p>=0.22).

Iteration 12 — final hypothesis statements.
The transcript closes with three refined hypotheses summarising the
cohort:

  (a) Regorafenib increases PFS by approximately +5 months in the joint
      subgroup KRAS WT AND BRAF WT AND left-sided primary AND
      CEA ≤ 5 ng/mL; outside this subgroup the effect is null. All four
      predicates are necessary.

  (b) Cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, and
      trastuzumab/tucatinib have no detectable PFS effect in this
      cohort, including in their canonical biomarker subgroups.

  (c) The strongest independent prognostic features for PFS are ECOG
      (-1.17/level), stage IV (-1.36), albumin (+0.47/g/dL), weight
      loss (-0.08/%), age (+0.18/year, positive), right-sided primary
      (-0.29), KRAS mutation (-0.35), BRAF V600E (-0.36), CEA
      (-0.005/ng/mL), and LDH (-0.0004/U/L).

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-------------------
1. **Treatment-effect signal is concentrated in a single agent and a single
   subgroup.** Of the six treatments observed, only regorafenib produces a
   detectable PFS benefit in this cohort. That benefit is large (≈+5.0
   months) but is sharply confined to the subgroup defined by KRAS WT &
   BRAF WT & left-sided primary & CEA ≤ 5 ng/mL. Outside this subgroup
   regorafenib is effectively inert (mean effect +0.024 months, n=40,662).

2. **All four subgroup predicates are individually required.** A patient
   meeting three of the four predicates but failing on KRAS, BRAF,
   sidedness, or CEA gains essentially no benefit from regorafenib.
   Drop-one analyses show the within-subgroup effect drops to between -0.17
   and +0.11 months when any single predicate is violated.

3. **Canonical biomarker-targeted therapies are null in this cohort.**
   Pembrolizumab in MSI-high, encorafenib in BRAF V600E, trastuzumab/
   tucatinib in HER2 amplified, and cetuximab in left-sided RAS/BRAF wild-
   type each show ≤0.07-month effects (all non-significant). This is the
   most striking treatment-related finding and contradicts what would be
   expected from real-world clinical evidence — but it is internally
   consistent across direct subgroup analysis, interaction screens, and
   tree-based CATE estimation.

4. **Prognostic features behave largely as expected** with two exceptions:
   age has a positive PFS coefficient (older patients fare better here),
   and NRAS mutation has a positive univariable coefficient that vanishes
   on adjustment. These are cohort idiosyncrasies worth flagging.

5. **No surprising biomarker-prognosis interactions** were detected beyond
   the documented main effects. NTRK fusion (n=251) was too rare to power
   a useful interaction analysis.

PRINCIPAL FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS
-----------------------------------------------------
Treatment: regorafenib
Outcome:   pfs_months
Direction: positive (longer PFS on treatment)
Magnitude: ≈ +5.0 months (95% bracket roughly +4.9 to +5.1)
Subgroup predicates (ALL required):
   * kras_mutation = 0 (KRAS wild-type)
   * braf_v600e = 0 (BRAF V600E wild-type)
   * right_sided_primary = 0 (left-sided primary)
   * cea_ng_ml ≤ 5 (low baseline CEA)

For all other five treatments the final hypothesis is that they have no
detectable PFS effect in this cohort, both overall and within their
canonical biomarker subgroups.
