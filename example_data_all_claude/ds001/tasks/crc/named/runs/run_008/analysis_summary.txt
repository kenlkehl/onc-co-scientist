ds001_crc — Analysis Summary
=============================

Cohort
------
50,000 patient records with one outcome (pfs_months), six binary treatment indicators
(cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, trastuzumab+tucatinib, regorafenib),
six biomarkers (KRAS, NRAS, BRAF V600E, MSI-H, HER2 amplification, NTRK fusion), and a
range of clinical/lab features. No missingness.

Across 25 iterations I tested main effects, biomarker effects, treatment main effects,
treatment×biomarker interactions, multivariable adjustment, and exhaustive small-subgroup
search for treatment-effect heterogeneity. All p-values reported below are from t-tests
or OLS coefficients on pfs_months unless otherwise noted.

1. Prognostic main effects (iterations 1, 4, 17)
-------------------------------------------------
Stage IV: mean PFS 3.70 vs 5.06 months in non-stage IV (diff = -1.35, p << 0.001).
ECOG PS: each one-point increase reduces PFS by 1.18 months (OLS slope, p << 0.001).
Stage and ECOG remained the strongest prognostic factors after multivariable adjustment.
Sex_female showed no PFS difference (diff -0.006, p=0.77).
Right-sided primary was associated with shorter PFS (diff -0.31, p=1e-49).

Continuous prognostic correlates with PFS:
- albumin_g_dl positively correlated (r=0.10, p<<0.001) — confirmed independent (β=+0.46) in MV.
- weight_loss_pct_6mo negatively correlated (r=-0.12, p<<0.001) — independent (β=-0.076).
- cea_ng_ml negatively correlated (r=-0.038, p=3e-17) — independent (β=-0.005).
- ldh_u_l negatively correlated (small effect, p=0.013); independent (β=-0.0004).
- nlr, crp, hemoglobin, alk-phos, AST, ALT, total bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium,
  potassium, calcium had no clinically meaningful or no statistically robust univariate
  correlation with PFS.

Surprisingly, age_years had a strong POSITIVE correlation with PFS (r=+0.77, p≈0) and a
positive multivariable β. This is the opposite direction from typical real-world oncology
data and likely reflects how the cohort was assembled (older patients in this synthetic
cohort have longer recorded PFS; this finding is consistent across model adjustments).

2. Biomarker main effects on PFS (iteration 2)
-----------------------------------------------
- kras_mutation: shorter PFS (4.12 vs 4.45, diff -0.33, p=2e-58).
- braf_v600e: shorter PFS (4.09 vs 4.32, diff -0.23, p=3e-7).
- nras_mutation: slightly longer PFS (4.53 vs 4.30, diff +0.22, p=0.0007) — small and
  opposite direction to KRAS.
- msi_high, her2_amplified, ntrk_fusion: no detectable PFS difference (all p > 0.3).

3. Treatment main effects on PFS (iteration 3)
-----------------------------------------------
Only ONE treatment showed a positive main effect on PFS:
- treatment_regorafenib: 5.09 vs 4.12 months (diff +0.97, p=2e-216) — large.
The other five treatments showed null overall effects:
- cetuximab: diff -0.04, p=0.09
- bevacizumab: diff -0.02, p=0.35
- pembrolizumab: diff +0.005, p=0.86
- encorafenib: diff +0.005, p=0.87
- trastuzumab+tucatinib: diff -0.04, p=0.24

4. Canonical biomarker–treatment matches were REFUTED (iterations 5, 7, 12–14, 18, 24)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The clinically expected matches were tested directly and did not hold in this cohort:
- Cetuximab × KRAS interaction: β=+0.02, p=0.67. In KRAS WT cetuximab effect was
  -0.05 (n=8821 treated), in KRAS mut -0.03 (n=6257 treated). Even in the canonical
  responder population (RAS/RAF WT and left-sided, n=16,443), cetuximab effect was
  -0.067 (p=0.125) — directionally negative, not the expected positive benefit.
- Pembrolizumab × MSI-H: interaction β=+0.002, p=0.99. Within MSI-H (n=2513),
  pembrolizumab effect was +0.007, p=0.96.
- Encorafenib × BRAF V600E: interaction β=-0.14, p=0.38. Within BRAF V600E (n=2272),
  encorafenib effect was -0.13, p=0.33.
- Trastuzumab+tucatinib × HER2 amplification: interaction β=+0.06, p=0.78. Within HER2+
  (n=1504), the treatment effect was +0.017, p=0.93.

In short: none of the four "canonical" biomarker-matched targeted treatments shows a
detectable PFS benefit in their canonical responder subgroups in this cohort.

5. Treatment-effect heterogeneity is concentrated in REGORAFENIB (iterations 8, 16, 23–25)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The systematic treatment×feature interaction screen (iter 8) flagged regorafenib as the
treatment with by far the largest interactions:
- regorafenib × right_sided_primary: β = -1.45 (p=4e-168)
- regorafenib × kras_mutation:        β = -1.63 (p=8e-226)
- regorafenib × braf_v600e:           β = -1.18 (p=6e-23)
- regorafenib × nras_mutation:        β = +0.99 (p=1e-11) — small subgroup, NRAS-mut
  patients also benefit
Stage_iv, ecog_ps, msi_high, her2_amplified, sex did NOT modify regorafenib effect.

Single-feature stratifications:
- KRAS WT:      regorafenib effect +1.656 (p≈0); KRAS mut: +0.027 (p=0.46)
- left-sided:   regorafenib effect +1.480 (p≈0); right-sided: +0.026 (p=0.52)
- BRAF WT:      regorafenib effect +1.027 (p<<0.001); BRAF V600E: -0.156 (p=0.13)

Multi-feature subgroups (regorafenib effect in months of PFS gained):
- KRAS WT & BRAF WT:                  +1.81 (n=26,830)
- KRAS WT & left-sided:               +2.52 (n=18,853)
- RAS/RAF WT (no KRAS/NRAS/BRAF mut): +1.81 (n=25,324)
- RAS WT & left-sided:                +2.50 (n=17,924)
- RAS/RAF WT & left-sided:            +2.75 (n=16,443) <- maximal effect
- left-sided KRAS WT BRAF WT:         +2.76 (n=17,372)

Predicate-drop sensitivity around the maximal subgroup (RAS/RAF WT & left-sided):
- drop NRAS-WT  → +2.76 (no change; NRAS WT is NOT necessary)
- drop BRAF-WT  → +2.50 (small drop; BRAF WT contributes only modestly)
- drop KRAS-WT  → +1.52 (large drop; KRAS WT IS necessary)
- drop left-sided → +1.81 (large drop; left-sided IS necessary)

The complete responder subgroup definition for regorafenib is therefore:
   kras_mutation = 0  AND  right_sided_primary = 0  AND  braf_v600e = 0
NRAS status is irrelevant. Within this subgroup (n ≈ 17,372), treatment_regorafenib =
1 increases PFS by ≈ +2.76 months (mean 6.88 treated vs 4.12 untreated, p ≈ 0).

Critically, OUTSIDE this subgroup (i.e., KRAS-mutant OR right-sided OR BRAF V600E,
n=32,628), the regorafenib effect collapses to +0.03 months (p=0.29) — completely null.
This is a textbook responder/non-responder dichotomy.

6. Bevacizumab — null overall (iterations 6, 8, 15, 21, 22)
-----------------------------------------------------------
Adjusted multivariable β = +0.006 (p=0.51). Stratified by stage_iv, sidedness, KRAS, MSI,
HER2 — no subgroup with meaningful or significant benefit. The only nominally significant
treatment×feature interaction was bevacizumab × braf_v600e (β=-0.23, p=0.02), but the
absolute magnitude is modest and would not survive multiple-comparison correction.

7. Cetuximab joint-model artifact (iteration 22)
-------------------------------------------------
In the full multivariable model with treatment×biomarker interactions, the cetuximab
main coefficient is +0.073 (p=8e-8) and cetuximab × right_sided_primary is -0.27
(p=1e-64). These coefficients are likely picking up shared variance with regorafenib
and the strongly predictive sidedness × treatment terms; in raw subgroup analyses
cetuximab is null in EVERY stratification examined (all diffs |x|<0.07, all p>0.10).
The clean reading is: cetuximab has no PFS benefit in this cohort.

8. Final best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis
-------------------------------------------------------------
There is one outcome (pfs_months) and one treatment with usable variation in this dataset.
The best-supported responder hypothesis is:

    Treatment:  treatment_regorafenib
    Outcome:    pfs_months
    Direction:  positive (longer PFS when treated)
    Subgroup:   kras_mutation = 0  AND  right_sided_primary = 0  AND  braf_v600e = 0
                (left-sided, KRAS wild-type, BRAF wild-type primary CRC)
    Magnitude:  +2.76 months mean PFS gained (treated 6.88 vs untreated 4.12, n=17,372,
                p ≈ 0)
    Outside subgroup: regorafenib effect = +0.03 months (p=0.29) — null

Each of the three predicates (KRAS-WT, BRAF-WT, left-sided) is necessary for the maximal
effect: dropping any one substantially attenuates the subgroup-specific benefit, while
dropping the NRAS-WT requirement does not.

For each of the other treatments (cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib,
trastuzumab+tucatinib), no responder subgroup with statistically supported PFS benefit
was identified — including the canonical biomarker-matched subgroups (RAS/RAF-WT
left-sided for cetuximab, MSI-H for pembrolizumab, BRAF V600E for encorafenib, HER2-
amplified for trastuzumab+tucatinib). These canonical hypotheses were tested directly
and refuted by the data.

9. Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-----------------------------------
Supported:
- Stage IV, higher ECOG PS, lower albumin, higher CEA, greater weight loss are
  independently associated with shorter PFS.
- Right-sided primaries have shorter PFS than left-sided.
- KRAS mutation and BRAF V600E are negative prognostic factors.
- Regorafenib has a positive overall effect on PFS.
- Regorafenib's effect is heterogeneous: large in left-sided KRAS-WT BRAF-WT patients,
  null elsewhere.

Refuted (canonical hypotheses that did NOT hold in this cohort):
- Cetuximab benefit in KRAS WT, RAS WT, RAS/RAF WT, or left-sided RAS/RAF WT — all null.
- Pembrolizumab benefit in MSI-H — null.
- Encorafenib benefit in BRAF V600E — null (slightly negative, ns).
- Trastuzumab+tucatinib benefit in HER2-amplified — null.
- Bevacizumab benefit overall or in any subgroup — null.
- Older age associated with shorter PFS (the actual direction is positive in this
  cohort, contrary to clinical expectation).

10. Conclusions
----------------
In this 50,000-patient CRC cohort, treatment-effect heterogeneity is dominated by a
single, very clear signal: regorafenib produces a large PFS benefit (~ +2.8 months) in
left-sided, KRAS-wild-type, BRAF-wild-type tumors and essentially no benefit in any
patient outside that triad. The canonical biomarker–targeted-therapy relationships
familiar from real-world CRC data (cetuximab/RAS-WT, pembrolizumab/MSI-H,
encorafenib/BRAF V600E, trastuzumab+tucatinib/HER2) do NOT hold in this cohort: those
treatments show null effects even in their biomarker-defined responder populations.
Standard prognostic factors (stage, performance status, albumin, weight loss, CEA, KRAS
and BRAF mutation status) behave as expected, with the surprising exception of
age_years, whose association with PFS is positive rather than negative.
