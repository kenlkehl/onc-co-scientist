ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_crc (n=50,000)
=========================================

Outcome examined: pfs_months (the only outcome in this dataset).

OVERALL APPROACH
----------------
Across 25 iterations I scanned univariate associations between every baseline
feature and PFS, fit a fully-adjusted multivariable OLS model, then conducted
systematic treatment-effect heterogeneity searches for each of the six
systemic-therapy variables. For the one treatment that showed clear
heterogeneity (regorafenib) I refined the responder subgroup definition with
joint-feature subgroup tests and a treatment x continuous-modifier scan,
identifying the complete set of predicates that define the responder subgroup
(including the variables whose unfavorable values suppress the treatment
effect). I also ran a T-learner shallow-tree heterogeneity check across all
six treatments to confirm no other treatment-effect subgroup was missed.


PROGNOSTIC MAIN EFFECTS (Iters 1-5)
-----------------------------------
A multivariable OLS with all 27 baseline features and all 6 treatments fit PFS
with R^2 = 0.859. The features with the strongest independent associations
with longer PFS were:

  + age_years            : +0.176 mo per year (huge effect; p<<1e-300; range
                           contributes ~10 months across the cohort age span).
                           Verified that this is not confounded — mean ECOG,
                           stage, biomarkers, and labs are nearly identical
                           across age tertiles, and the coefficient survives
                           full adjustment.
  + albumin_g_dl         : +0.47 mo per g/dL (p<1e-100)
  + treatment_regorafenib: +0.94 mo main effect (heterogeneous; see below)

The strongest independent associations with shorter PFS were:

  - stage_iv             : -1.36 mo (p<<1e-300)
  - ecog_ps              : -1.17 mo per ECOG level (p<<1e-300)
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo  : -0.076 mo per % (p<1e-150)
  - braf_v600e           : -0.36 mo (p<1e-70)
  - kras_mutation        : -0.35 mo (p<1e-50)
  - right_sided_primary  : -0.29 mo (p<1e-200)
  - cea_ng_ml            : -0.005 mo per ng/mL
  - ldh_u_l              : -0.0004 mo per U/L

Hypotheses about negative prognostic effects of MSI-high, HER2 amplification,
NTRK fusion, and most lab features (AST, ALT, creatinine, BUN, electrolytes,
hemoglobin, CRP, NLR) were NOT supported — adjusted p-values were all >0.05
for these features. NRAS mutation showed a marginally significant POSITIVE
univariate association (+0.22 mo, p=7e-4) that did not survive full
adjustment (adjusted coef +0.020, p=0.37) and is best interpreted as null.

Among treatments, only regorafenib had a non-zero adjusted main effect
(+0.94 mo). Cetuximab, bevacizumab, pembrolizumab, encorafenib, and
trastuzumab+tucatinib all had adjusted effects within ±0.03 mo with p>0.05,
suggesting that any benefit (if it exists) is concentrated in subgroups.


TREATMENT x BIOMARKER INTERACTION SCREENING (Iters 6-11)
--------------------------------------------------------
I tested the canonical biomarker x targeted-therapy hypotheses that one would
expect from the published mCRC literature. NONE were supported in this
dataset:

  - cetuximab x kras_mutation        interaction p = 0.20  (NS)
  - cetuximab x braf_v600e           interaction p = 0.78  (NS)
  - cetuximab x right_sided_primary  interaction p = 0.18  (NS)
  - pembrolizumab x msi_high         interaction p = 0.27  (direction +,
                                     consistent with the on-label
                                     hypothesis but underpowered or null)
  - encorafenib x braf_v600e         interaction p = 0.87  (NS, strikingly so)
  - trastuzumab+tucatinib x her2_amplified  interaction p = 0.86  (NS)
  - bevacizumab x kras / braf / right_sided   all NS

In contrast, regorafenib showed dramatic heterogeneity:

  + regorafenib effect in left-sided primary  : +1.46 mo
                regorafenib effect in right-sided   : -0.01 mo  (p_int <<1e-300)
  + regorafenib effect in KRAS-WT             : +1.63 mo
                regorafenib effect in KRAS-mut       : -0.01 mo  (p_int <<1e-300)
  + regorafenib effect in BRAF-WT             : +0.99 mo
                regorafenib effect in BRAF V600E     : -0.00 mo  (p_int = 5e-104)


REFINING THE REGORAFENIB RESPONDER SUBGROUP (Iters 12-15)
---------------------------------------------------------
A treatment x continuous-modifier scan identified a strong negative
regorafenib x cea_ng_ml interaction (slope = -0.42 mo per +1 SD CEA,
p<<1e-300). Within the left-sided + KRAS-WT + BRAF-WT subgroup, regorafenib
gave +5.0 mo PFS in patients with CEA <= 5 ng/mL and 0.0 mo in patients with
CEA > 5 ng/mL. Sweeping the cutoff confirmed that the threshold is sharp and
falls between CEA=4 and CEA=5:

  cea cutoff <=4 -> rego eff +5.04   (within left-sided KRAS-WT BRAF-WT)
  cea cutoff <=5 -> rego eff +5.01
  cea cutoff <=6 -> rego eff +4.54
  cea cutoff <=7 -> rego eff +4.13
  cea cutoff <=10 -> rego eff +3.61
  cea > cutoff   -> rego eff approximately 0 in all settings

Removing any single one of the four predicates (left-sided, KRAS-WT,
BRAF-WT, CEA<=5) attenuates or abolishes the treatment effect. Each
"unfavorable" value (right-sided primary, KRAS mutation, BRAF V600E
mutation, or CEA > ~5) independently suppresses the regorafenib benefit.

Within the four-way subgroup (n=9338, 1905 on regorafenib), the
multivariable-adjusted regorafenib coefficient is +4.98 mo PFS
(p<<1e-300). The effect is uniform across ECOG strata (0/1/2 all give
~+2.7 mo within the 3-way left-WT-WT subgroup), uniform across stage IV
status (+2.6 vs +2.9 mo), and uniform across the number of co-administered
treatments (0..3 other treatments all give +5.0 mo within the four-way
subgroup), so no further refinement of the subgroup is supported by the
data.


SYSTEMATIC TREE-BASED HETEROGENEITY (Iter 17, 20, 23)
-----------------------------------------------------
A T-learner with shallow decision trees (max_depth=4) was fit per treatment
to estimate CATE distributions and to compare treatment effects in the top
versus bottom CATE quintile. The top-quintile vs other actual treatment
effects were:

  cetuximab          : +0.09 vs -0.07 mo
  bevacizumab        : +0.07 vs -0.04 mo
  pembrolizumab      : +0.22 vs -0.06 mo
  encorafenib        : +0.16 vs -0.04 mo
  trastuzumab/tucat. : -0.07 vs -0.03 mo
  regorafenib        : +3.75 vs +0.26 mo  (only treatment with meaningful
                                          CATE heterogeneity)

An exhaustive single-feature single-threshold scan over 18 candidate
splitters confirmed that no other (treatment, feature, threshold) triple
produces a treatment-effect difference greater than ~0.30 mo. Only
regorafenib shows clinically meaningful heterogeneity, and the splitters
that maximize that heterogeneity are exactly cea_ng_ml (~4-5),
kras_mutation, and right_sided_primary — fully consistent with the
four-way subgroup hypothesis.


HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED vs REFUTED
-------------------------------
Supported by the evidence:

  * Older age increases PFS (+0.18 mo/yr; not confounded; very large effect).
  * ECOG, stage IV, weight loss, KRAS, BRAF, right-sided primary,
    elevated CEA, elevated LDH all decrease PFS (independent main effects).
  * Higher albumin increases PFS.
  * Regorafenib increases PFS in the responder subgroup defined by
    right_sided_primary=0 AND kras_mutation=0 AND braf_v600e=0 AND
    cea_ng_ml<=5 (effect ~+5.0 mo). Outside this subgroup, regorafenib
    has no PFS effect; each of right-sided primary, KRAS mutation,
    BRAF V600E, and CEA>~5 independently suppresses the effect.

Refuted (or not supported) by the evidence:

  * MSI-high, HER2 amplification, NTRK fusion are NOT independent
    prognostic factors for PFS in this cohort.
  * NRAS mutation does not have a meaningful adjusted prognostic effect.
  * The classic biomarker-targeted hypotheses for cetuximab (RAS-WT,
    BRAF-WT, left-sided), pembrolizumab (MSI-high), encorafenib
    (BRAF V600E), and trastuzumab+tucatinib (HER2-amplified) are NOT
    supported in this dataset; none of these treatments produced a
    statistically significant or clinically meaningful PFS benefit in
    its on-label biomarker-positive subgroup.
  * Sex_female, ECOG-by-pembrolizumab, age-by-treatment interactions are
    all null.
  * Pairwise treatment x treatment interactions are null.


FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY HYPOTHESIS (best supported)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment : regorafenib
Outcome   : pfs_months
Direction : positive (regorafenib increases PFS)
Subgroup  : right_sided_primary = 0
            AND kras_mutation = 0
            AND braf_v600e = 0
            AND cea_ng_ml <= 5
Effect    : +4.98 months PFS (multivariable-adjusted within subgroup;
            crude +5.01 months); p << 1e-300 in a subgroup of n=9338
            (n_on_regorafenib = 1905).
Outside   : Regorafenib effect approximately 0 in any patient who is
            right-sided OR KRAS-mutant OR BRAF V600E OR has CEA > 5.

No other treatment in this cohort shows a clinically meaningful
treatment-effect subgroup for PFS.
