Analysis summary — ds001_crc (50,000 patients, outcome pfs_months)
=========================================================================

OVERVIEW

The dataset comprises 50,000 colorectal-cancer-cohort patient records with 33
de-identified features and a single continuous outcome, pfs_months (range
0–16.3, median 4.21, mean 4.31, SD 2.29). I conducted 25 iterations of
hypothesis generation and statistical testing, beginning with broad univariate
screens, building a multivariable model, identifying the lone treatment-like
exposure, and then performing systematic treatment-effect heterogeneity
analyses, culminating in a four-condition responder subgroup that fully
characterizes the treatment effect.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. MAIN-EFFECT LANDSCAPE  (iterations 1–3, 21–24)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Univariate screens identified four dominant prognostic predictors and several
secondary ones:

  feature_015  (continuous, 30–90, mean 65; presumably an age-like or composite
               score):  +0.176 months PFS per unit, Pearson r = 0.766 (the
               single strongest correlate; p <<< 1e-300).
  feature_001  (ordinal 0/1/2; behaviour consistent with ECOG performance
               status):  -1.190 months PFS per level. Mean PFS by level
               0/1/2 = 5.27 / 4.05 / 2.91; every pairwise contrast highly
               significant.
  feature_014  (binary, prev. 55%; behaviour consistent with stage-IV /
               metastatic status):  -1.35 months PFS, p <<< 1e-300.
  feature_027  (binary, prev. 20%; treatment, see below):  +0.97 months PFS,
               p = 1.7e-216.

Smaller but highly significant prognostic signals were also seen for:
feature_025 (continuous, -0.076/unit, p ≈ 2e-167; many zero values, plausibly
a behavioural-exposure score), feature_019 (continuous, +0.465/unit, p ≈ 3e-115;
plausibly albumin given range 1.5–5.5 and mean 3.8), feature_023 (binary 42%,
−0.33 mo), feature_006 (binary 35%, −0.31 mo), feature_022 (binary 4.5%,
−0.23 mo), feature_005 (continuous, heavily right-skewed with max 777; -0.005
per unit on the linear scale; cutoff of 5 separates low/high cleanly,
consistent with a tumor-marker-style biomarker such as CEA), feature_011
(rare binary 3%, +0.22 mo), and feature_021 (continuous, very small slope).
The remaining 18 features (including feature_031 — the candidate sex
variable, prevalence 45% — and the bulk of biochemistry: feature_017,
feature_003, feature_030, feature_008, feature_004, feature_013, feature_026,
feature_029, feature_007, feature_033, feature_009, plus binary
feature_010, feature_012, feature_018, feature_020, feature_024, feature_002,
feature_016, feature_028) showed |Pearson r| ≤ 0.013 and were non-significant
both marginally and in the multivariable model below.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. MULTIVARIABLE PROGNOSTIC MODEL  (iteration 5)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

A linear OLS regression of pfs_months on all 33 features simultaneously
achieved R² = 0.859. The independently significant adjusted coefficients
(sorted by |t|) were:

  feature_015 +0.176 (t = 454)        feature_005 -0.005 (t = -21)
  feature_001 -1.171 (t = -206)        feature_022 -0.357 (t = -19)
  feature_014 -1.362 (t = -176)        feature_021 -0.0004 (t = -9)
  feature_027 +0.943 (t =  98)
  feature_025 -0.076 (t = -76)
  feature_019 +0.469 (t =  61)
  feature_023 -0.354 (t = -44)
  feature_006 -0.287 (t = -36)

Every other feature was non-significant after adjustment; in particular,
feature_031 (the candidate sex variable) had p = 0.19. Signs and rough
magnitudes match the univariate picture, indicating that the predictors are
largely capturing distinct sources of variation.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. IDENTIFYING THE TREATMENT  (iteration 4)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

I tested whether feature_027 was randomly distributed with respect to all
other features (the signature of randomized treatment assignment). Across all
32 contrasts (chi-square for binary/ordinal, Welch t-test for continuous),
every p-value exceeded 0.10:

   feature_015 p = 0.16, feature_001 p = 0.12, feature_014 p = 0.33,
   feature_006 p = 0.34, feature_023 p = 0.71, feature_005 p = 0.86, etc.

This perfect balance is the strongest possible evidence in observational data
that feature_027 is a randomized intervention; combined with its substantial
adjusted main effect (+0.94 months), feature_027 is best interpreted as the
study's treatment, and any treatment-by-feature interaction is interpretable
causally rather than as confounding.

I also screened the other plausible binary candidate "treatments" (feature_024
30%, feature_010 45%, feature_012 15%, feature_018 10%, feature_020 8%) for
hidden subgroup-conditional effects. The largest subgroup-conditional
treatment-style effect for any of them was 0.08 months (feature_024, p = 0.08);
no other feature behaves like a treatment.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY  (iterations 6–20)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

A systematic interaction screen — for every other feature f, fit
pfs ~ trt + f + trt:f and record the trt:f p-value — gave the following
strongest signals:

   trt × feature_023   p = 7.7e-226  (coef -1.63 — feature_023=1 abolishes effect)
   trt × feature_006   p = 4.1e-168  (coef -1.45 — feature_006=1 abolishes effect)
   trt × feature_005   p = 8.7e-58   (coef -0.025/unit — effect declines with feature_005)
   trt × feature_022   p = 6.1e-23   (coef -1.18 — feature_022=1 abolishes effect)
   trt × feature_011   p = 1.2e-11   (coef +0.99 — apparent amplifier; explained away below)

All other interactions had p > 0.10. Stratified group-mean comparisons
confirmed the picture:

  feature_023 × feature_006 cells (treatment effect of feature_027):
     (023=0, 006=0):  +2.520 mo, p < 1e-300
     (023=0, 006=1):  +0.071 mo, p = 0.16
     (023=1, 006=0):  +0.061 mo, p = 0.17
     (023=1, 006=1):  -0.040 mo, p = 0.52

  Adding feature_022 (the triple-negative subgroup):
     023=0 & 006=0 & 022=0  (n = 17372):  diff = +2.758 mo, p < 1e-300
     Complement              (n = 32628):  diff = +0.030 mo, p = 0.29

  Adding feature_005 (continuous, threshold ≈ 5; "low" vs "high"):
     Within triple-negative subgroup, treatment effect by feature_005
     quartiles: q0 +5.03, q1 +5.03, q2 +0.66, q3 -0.02. A simple cutoff at
     feature_005 = 5 cleanly separates responders (diff +5.014) from
     non-responders (diff -0.002).

  All 16 cells of the four-modifier 2×2×2×2 partition tabulated. ONLY ONE
  cell — (023=0 & 006=0 & 022=0 & feature_005 ≤ 5) — has a meaningful, large,
  significant treatment effect (+5.01 mo). The other 15 cells all have
  |effect| ≤ 0.25 months and uncorrected p ≥ 0.12.

A leave-one-predicate-out sensitivity check (iteration 13) showed each of
the four predicates is independently necessary: violating any single one
while keeping the other three intact eliminates the effect (each remainder
diff |≤| 0.17 mo, p ≥ 0.13). A full multivariable interaction model
(iteration 14) found all four trt:modifier coefficients independently
significant (p ≤ 2e-58 each).

The continuous trt × log(feature_005) interaction within the triple-negative
subgroup (iteration 20) showed the attenuation is approximately log-linear:
at feature_005 = 1 the trt effect is +5.17 months; per unit of
log(feature_005) the effect drops by 1.66 months. The crossover to zero
occurs near feature_005 ≈ exp(5.17/1.66) ≈ 22, but in practice the simple
cutoff at 5 captures essentially all of the responder population because the
distribution of feature_005 is highly right-skewed.

The marginal feature_011 amplification (iteration 8) was reanalyzed in the
full multivariable interaction model (iteration 19): once the four core
modifiers are conditioned on, the trt × feature_011 coefficient drops to
+0.16 with p = 0.27. The earlier amplification reflects feature_011's mild
prognostic effect plus its (small) overlap with the responder cell rather
than a true biomarker-positive amplification of treatment effect.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. WITHIN-SUBGROUP HOMOGENEITY  (iteration 18)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Within the responder subgroup (feature_023=0 & feature_006=0 & feature_022=0
& feature_005 ≤ 5), the treatment effect is uniform across the major
prognostic strata:

   feature_001 levels 0/1/2:    +4.87 / +5.09 / +4.81 mo
   feature_014 levels 0/1:      +5.03 / +5.01 mo
   feature_011 levels 0/1:      +5.00 / +5.24 mo
   feature_015 (continuous) interaction p = 0.11

These factors remain prognostic but do not modulate the treatment effect.
This is reassuring — it confirms the subgroup definition is complete and
not merely a proxy for a different combination of prognostic variables.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. SUPPORTED VS. REFUTED HYPOTHESES (summary)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Supported:
  • Strong prognostic effects of feature_015 (+), feature_001 (-),
    feature_014 (-), feature_019 (+), feature_025 (-), feature_023 (-),
    feature_006 (-), feature_022 (-), feature_005 (-).
  • feature_027 is a randomly assigned treatment with a marginal +0.97-month
    PFS benefit overall.
  • The treatment benefit is concentrated almost entirely in patients with
    feature_023=0 AND feature_006=0 AND feature_022=0 AND feature_005 ≤ 5
    (~+5 months PFS, n ≈ 9,300). All four predicates are independently
    necessary.
  • feature_001 is a graded, monotone prognostic ordinal (0 > 1 > 2).

Refuted / null:
  • feature_031 (candidate sex variable) has no detectable association with
    pfs_months.
  • Rare biomarkers feature_002, feature_016, feature_028 have no detectable
    main effect and no treatment-effect modification.
  • The apparent feature_011 × feature_027 amplification disappears once
    the core four modifiers are conditioned on.
  • No other binary feature beyond feature_027 functions as a treatment
    (largest subgroup-conditional effect among feature_024, feature_010,
    feature_012, feature_018, feature_020 was 0.08 months).

----------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. FINAL TREATMENT-SUBGROUP STATEMENT
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Treatment:  feature_027 (binary, prevalence 20%, randomly assigned).
Outcome:    pfs_months (longer is better).
Direction:  treatment effect is POSITIVE (longer PFS).

Responder subgroup (~18.7% of cohort, n = 9,338):

   feature_023 = 0
   AND feature_006 = 0
   AND feature_022 = 0
   AND feature_005 ≤ 5

   In this subgroup:  mean PFS on treatment = 9.10 mo,
                       off treatment        = 4.09 mo,
                       treatment effect     = +5.01 mo
                       (bootstrap 95% CI [4.91, 5.12], p < 1e-300).

Complement (~81.3%, n = 40,662):

   Treatment effect = +0.04 mo (p = 0.20) — null.

Each of the four predicates is independently necessary; relaxing any single
one drops the treatment effect to a clinically and statistically null level
(|effect| ≤ 0.17 mo, p ≥ 0.13). No additional feature further refines the
responder subgroup. This four-condition rule is the best-supported
treatment-by-biomarker hypothesis for the cohort.
