ds001_crc — Analysis Summary
============================

Cohort: 50,000 patients. Outcome: pfs_months (mean 4.31, SD 2.29, range 0–16.25).
Features: 33 anonymized (feature_001 … feature_033), of which 15 are binary, 1 is
ordinal (feature_001, levels 0/1/2), and 17 are continuous.

Approach
--------
We worked in 25 iterations of propose-test-refine. We began with univariate
screens of every feature against pfs_months, then built progressively richer
multivariable OLS models, and finally performed a systematic treatment-effect
heterogeneity search around the strongest candidate "treatment" variable, ending
with a four-predicate composite responder definition that was tested both
unadjusted and adjusted.

Main prognostic effects
-----------------------
Strong, robust prognostic features (after full multivariable adjustment, R²≈0.86):

  feature_015 (continuous, range 30–90):  +0.176 months per unit (p<1e-300).
       End-to-end shift over the observed range is roughly +10.6 months.
       Direction is positive, i.e. higher feature_015 values track LONGER
       progression-free survival. This is opposite to the conventional
       chronological-age sign and is the dominant single driver of pfs_months
       in this dataset.
  feature_001 (ordinal 0/1/2, monotone severity-like): -1.19 (level 1 vs 0),
       -2.32 (level 2 vs 0); both p<1e-300.
  feature_014 (binary, 55% prevalence): -1.37 months (p<1e-300). Behaves as a
       constant prognostic insult; no meaningful interaction with the candidate
       treatment.
  feature_023 (binary, 42%): -0.35 months adjusted; also acts as treatment-
       effect modifier (see below).
  feature_006 (binary, 35%): -0.28 months adjusted; also a treatment-effect
       modifier.
  feature_022 (binary, 4.5%): -0.36 months; also a treatment-effect modifier.
  feature_019 (continuous, 1.5–5.5):  +0.47 per unit (p<1e-300).
  feature_025 (continuous, 0–24, mean 3.8):  -0.076 per unit (p<1e-300).
  feature_005 (continuous, 0–777, heavy right-skew):  -0.005 per unit
       adjusted (p≈1e-97); also a strong treatment-effect modifier.
  feature_021 (continuous): -0.0004 per unit (small but significant, p≈1e-18).

Null after adjustment (no detectable independent effect on pfs_months):
  Binary: feature_031, feature_010, feature_012, feature_016, feature_018,
          feature_020, feature_024, feature_028, feature_002.
  Continuous: feature_032, feature_017, feature_007, feature_003, feature_008,
          feature_013, feature_026, feature_029, feature_030, feature_033,
          feature_004, feature_009.
  feature_011 (binary, 3%): a marginal positive raw effect (+0.22 months,
          p=2.3e-4) that fully attenuates after adjustment. The patients with
          feature_011=1 all have feature_023=0 and feature_022=0 — i.e., this
          feature lives entirely inside (and so partially confounds with) the
          treatment-responsive subgroup defined below.

Candidate treatment and main effect
-----------------------------------
feature_027 (binary, 20% prevalence) is the only variable that behaves like
a treatment with a clinically meaningful favorable effect: unadjusted mean
pfs_months 5.09 vs 4.12 (diff +0.97, p<1e-300); adjusted partial effect
+0.94 months. Its rare positive raw counterpart feature_011 dropped out after
adjustment, and feature_022 (which looked weakly negative on its own) turned
out to be a treatment-effect modifier rather than an independent treatment.

Heterogeneity of the feature_027 effect
---------------------------------------
A systematic interaction screen of feature_027 against every other feature
revealed the treatment effect is highly heterogeneous. The strongest
interactions (all p<1e-50, adjusted):

  feature_027 × feature_006   = -1.46
  feature_027 × feature_023   = -1.63
  feature_027 × feature_005   = -0.026 per unit
  feature_027 × feature_022   = -0.99
  feature_027 × feature_011   = +0.89

Stratified analyses then pinned down the gating structure:

  By feature_006:
    feature_006=0 (n=32,343):  feature_027 effect = +1.48 months (p<1e-300)
    feature_006=1 (n=17,657):  feature_027 effect = +0.03   (p=0.52)
  By feature_023:
    feature_023=0 (n=29,102):  feature_027 effect = +1.66 months (p<1e-300)
    feature_023=1 (n=20,898):  feature_027 effect = +0.03   (p=0.45)

The 2×2 of feature_006 × feature_023 makes the gating explicit:
    (0,0) n=18,853  feature_027 effect = +2.52 months  (p<1e-300)
    (0,1) n=13,490  feature_027 effect = +0.06         (p=0.17)
    (1,0) n=10,249  feature_027 effect = +0.07         (p=0.17)
    (1,1) n= 7,408  feature_027 effect = -0.04         (p=0.52)

So the treatment effect lives entirely in the (feature_006=0 AND feature_023=0)
cell. Within that cell, a sharp threshold appears in feature_005:
    feature_005<1.97 (Q1):  +4.64 months
    feature_005 1.97–4.49:  +4.65
    feature_005 4.49–9.95:  +0.55
    feature_005 ≥9.95   :   -0.03
A natural cut at feature_005=5 splits the responsive (n=10,100, +4.63 effect)
from the non-responsive (n=8,753, -0.02 effect).

A further refinement comes from feature_022. Within (006=0 ∧ 023=0 ∧ 005<5):
    feature_022=0 (n=9,326): feature_027 effect = +5.01 months (p<1e-300)
    feature_022=1 (n=  774): feature_027 effect = -0.17        (p=0.35)
Thus feature_022=1, although rare, completely abolishes the otherwise +5-month
benefit.

Final composite responder definition
------------------------------------
   feature_006 = 0
   feature_023 = 0
   feature_005 < 5
   feature_022 = 0

This subgroup is 18.7% of the cohort (n=9,326).

Inside the subgroup:
   feature_027=1 (n=1,903) mean pfs_months 9.10
   feature_027=0 (n=7,423) mean pfs_months 4.09
   Unadjusted diff = +5.01 months   (p<1e-300)
   Adjusted diff (final OLS with feature_015, C(feature_001), feature_014,
       feature_019, feature_025) = +4.98 months   (95% CI 4.97–4.99)

Outside the subgroup (n=40,674):
   feature_027 effect = +0.025 months  (p=0.33)

A joint interaction model "pfs_months ~ feature_027*composite + …" reproduces
this cleanly: the main feature_027 term (effect when composite=0) is -0.003
(p=0.26) and the feature_027:composite interaction is +4.98 (p<1e-300). In
other words, the composite predicate is necessary and approximately sufficient
to predict feature_027 efficacy.

Within the composite subgroup we screened for further effect-modifying signals.
The largest residual within-subgroup interactions are quantitative not
categorical: feature_027 × feature_015 ≈ +0.005 month per unit (p≈1e-36),
feature_027 × feature_001 (level 2) ≈ -0.07 (p≈1e-7), feature_027 × feature_014
≈ -0.028 (p=0.001). These shave fractions of a month off the +5-month effect
but never null it. No additional binary predicate behaves as a categorical
on/off switch.

Hypothesis status (concise)
---------------------------
Supported:
  H1  feature_014 worsens pfs_months by ~1.35 months.
  H2  feature_027 improves pfs_months on average by ~0.97 months.
  H3  feature_001 monotone severity gradient (5.27 → 4.05 → 2.91 months).
  H4  feature_015 has a strong positive correlation with pfs_months (r=0.77).
  H5  feature_023 worsens pfs_months (-0.33 month).
  H6  feature_006 worsens pfs_months (-0.31 month).
  H7  feature_022 worsens pfs_months (-0.23 month, also a TE modifier).
  H9–13  Continuous lab-like features feature_005, feature_019, feature_021,
         feature_025 retain effects after adjustment; the rest are null.
  H14–20 feature_027 effect is heterogeneous with strong feature_006, _023,
         _005, _022 modifiers; gated into the four-predicate composite.
  H21–24 feature_015, feature_001 (level 2), feature_014 modulate the
         within-composite effect quantitatively but do not turn it off.
  H26    feature_019 has a positive adjusted slope (+0.47/unit).
  H27    feature_025 has a negative adjusted slope (-0.076/unit).
  H28    feature_005 has dual prognostic + treatment-modifier role.
  H35    Final composite (feature_006=0 ∧ feature_023=0 ∧ feature_022=0 ∧
         feature_005<5) is the maximal subgroup of feature_027 responders;
         within it, feature_027 adds ~5 months of pfs_months.
  H32    Within the composite, feature_015 quantitatively scales (not gates)
         the treatment effect.

Refuted / null:
  H8   feature_011's positive marginal effect is driven entirely by overlap
       with the responsive subgroup; no independent benefit.
  H22  Confirmed: feature_011's effect collapses after conditioning on the
       composite predicates.
  H23  feature_014 does NOT modify the feature_027 effect (it is a constant
       prognostic factor only).
  H29  feature_010, _012, _016, _018, _020, _024, _028, _031, _002 are null
       after adjustment.
  H30  feature_032, _017, _007, _003, _008, _013, _026, _029, _030, _033,
       _004, _009 are null after adjustment.
  H34  No 5th categorical predicate enlarges or sharpens the composite.

Bottom line
-----------
* Strong overall prognostic structure: pfs_months is dominated by feature_015
  (positive), feature_001 (severity gradient), and feature_014 (negative);
  feature_019 is positively prognostic and feature_025 is negatively
  prognostic.
* Treatment story: feature_027 looks like a beneficial treatment, but its
  ~+0.94 month population-average effect is misleading. The benefit is fully
  concentrated in a four-predicate "responder" subgroup
  (feature_006=0 ∧ feature_023=0 ∧ feature_022=0 ∧ feature_005<5,
  ~18.7% of the cohort), in whom the effect is roughly +5 months of pfs.
  In the other 81.3% of patients, feature_027 has no detectable effect on
  pfs_months. feature_006=1, feature_023=1, feature_022=1, or
  feature_005≥5 each independently appear sufficient to abolish the benefit.
