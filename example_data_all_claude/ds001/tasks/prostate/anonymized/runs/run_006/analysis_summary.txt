Analysis Summary — ds001_prostate
=================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients, 33 anonymised features (one constant, 32 informative),
single binary outcome `objective_response` (overall rate 24.0%, 12,011 of
50,000 responders). Twenty propose-test-refine iterations were carried out.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Identifying the treatment variable
------------------------------------------------------------------------
The strongest single positive predictor of `objective_response` is the binary
variable `feature_008`. Patients with `feature_008==1` (n=20,076) have a 36.1%
response rate vs 15.9% in `feature_008==0` (n=29,924), an absolute difference of
+20.2 percentage points (chi-square p<1e-300; univariable logistic OR ~3.0).

Critically, `feature_008` is essentially perfectly balanced across every other
feature in the dataset:
  * proportion differences for binary covariates: |diff| < 0.006 in all cases
  * median differences for continuous covariates: trivial (e.g., feature_022
    median 15.82 vs 15.89; feature_016 median 64.9 vs 65.0)
This balance pattern is the signature of a randomized treatment assignment, so
`feature_008` is treated as the active treatment of interest. All subsequent
analyses focus on uncovering its true effect heterogeneity.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Other features that were initially confused with treatments
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Four binary features have large NEGATIVE marginal associations with response —
feature_013 (-19.3 pp), feature_015 (-10.0 pp), feature_021 (-10.0 pp),
feature_027 (-6.6 pp) — but cross-tabulation shows they are also balanced
across `feature_008` arms, so they are NOT competing treatment indicators. Of
these, feature_013 is balanced on every other variable except `feature_022`
(PSA-like, median 32.94 if feature_013==1 vs 7.38 if feature_013==0), suggesting
it marks a stage/biology axis tightly linked to PSA rather than an independent
randomization.

The ordinal `feature_001` (0/1/2; likely ECOG/performance-status-like) is
strongly prognostic with monotonically decreasing response rates of 28.2 -> 22.8
-> 18.2 percent (adjusted log-OR -0.33 per level, p=5e-72).

`feature_010` (integer 6-10 with mode 7-8, suggesting Gleason sum) is NOT
predictive of `objective_response` in either crude or adjusted analyses
(p=0.77 / 0.97).

Continuous predictors with significant adjusted effects on response include
log(feature_022) (PSA-like, neg), log(feature_024) (neg), feature_002 (likely
albumin, pos), and feature_020 (likely a count of metastatic sites, neg). The
full multivariable model (32 features + 4 log-transforms) reaches pseudo-R^2 =
0.125 with all the above retaining significance at p<1e-7.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Treatment-effect heterogeneity (the main finding)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
A systematic interaction screen testing every feature's interaction with
`feature_008` in a logistic model identified four binary features whose
interaction with treatment is ENORMOUS and overwhelmingly significant:

   Feature       Interaction OR    p-value         Trt effect when 0    Trt effect when 1
   feature_013   0.138             p < 1e-300      +0.440 abs           +0.008 abs
   feature_015   0.290             p = 1.4e-93     +0.251 abs           +0.009 abs
   feature_021   0.321             p = 3.3e-42     +0.225 abs           +0.006 abs
   feature_027   0.277             p = 1.8e-19     +0.209 abs          -0.022 abs

In each case, switching the modifier to 1 collapses the absolute treatment
benefit to ~zero. log(feature_022) (PSA) showed a softer interaction
(int_OR 0.45, p=1e-72), but the effect is essentially mediated by feature_013
(see point 4 below). No other feature had an interaction p < 1e-4.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Joint subgroup discovery — Boolean-OR resistance pattern
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Stratifying by all 16 combinations of the four resistance flags revealed a
striking Boolean-OR pattern:

   feature_013 / feature_015 / feature_021 / feature_027 = 0/0/0/0
       n = 15,681 (31.4% of cohort)
       treated:    79.8% response (4,506 of 5,649)
       control:    17.2% response (1,727 of 10,032)
       absolute risk reduction +0.626  ARR
       relative risk           4.64
       odds ratio              18.98
       NNT                     1.60

   ANY other combination (any single flag = 1)
       n = 34,319 (68.6% of cohort)
       treated:    16.6% response (2,393 of 14,427)
       control:    15.3% response (3,037 of 19,892)
       absolute risk reduction +0.013
       odds ratio              1.06 (p = 0.055)

A single logistic model `y ~ trt + good_subgroup + trt:good_subgroup` returns
an interaction coefficient of +2.885 (z = 56.1, p < 1e-300) and lifts the
pseudo-R^2 from 0.027 (treatment alone) to 0.190 — by far the largest single
explanatory jump in the dataset.

A necessity check confirmed each of the four flags is indispensable: holding
the other three at 0 and switching one flag to 1 shrinks the treatment effect
from +0.626 to between -0.025 and +0.022. Dropping any flag from the subgroup
definition therefore loses the predictive signal.

A within-good-subgroup interaction screen across the remaining 26 features
found NO interaction surviving Bonferroni correction (best p ≈ 0.004 for
feature_005; threshold 0.0019). PSA is similarly inert within the subgroup —
the treatment effect ranges only from +0.618 to +0.633 across PSA quartiles.
The four-flag definition is therefore essentially complete.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Prognostic vs predictive distinction
------------------------------------------------------------------------
A direct test in the control-arm-only multivariable model (n=29,924) shows the
four flags have only weak/marginal independent prognostic value (|coef| < 0.20,
p between 0.03 and 0.23). In the treatment-arm-only model (n=20,076), the same
flags have coefficients of -2.27, -1.66, -1.65, -1.48 (all p << 1e-30), an
order of magnitude larger. This pattern — small or null in controls, dominant
in treated — is the textbook signature of PURELY PREDICTIVE biomarkers (i.e.,
they identify treatment resistance, not baseline disease aggressiveness).

The classical prognostic factors (feature_001, feature_002, log feature_022,
feature_020, log feature_024) by contrast retain similar coefficients in both
arms, confirming they are prognostic but not predictive.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. Hypothesis support summary
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Supported (p < 0.001 and direction confirmed):
  h1, h3, h4, h5, h6, h7, h9, h10, h11, h12, h13, h14, h15, h16, h17, h18,
  h19, h22, h23, h24, h25, h26
Refuted / null (no effect detected):
  h2 (confirmed null — feature_030 is constant, expected)
  h8 (feature_010 / Gleason-like is not predictive after adjustment)
  h20 (PSA does not modify treatment effect within the good subgroup)
  h21 (no additional within-subgroup interaction survives correction)

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. Final conclusion
------------------------------------------------------------------------
The treatment marked by `feature_008` produces a clinically dramatic improvement
in `objective_response` (~63 percentage-point absolute increase, NNT 1.6)
EXCLUSIVELY in the ~31% of patients with feature_013==0 AND feature_015==0 AND
feature_021==0 AND feature_027==0. In every other patient subgroup the drug
does not produce any clinically meaningful benefit. The four resistance flags
operate by Boolean OR — any one of them being positive is sufficient to abolish
the treatment effect — and each is independently necessary in the subgroup
definition. These four features are predictive of treatment response rather
than prognostic of baseline outcome, and there is no evidence that PSA
(feature_022), age (feature_016), Gleason-like (feature_010), or any other
measured feature further refines the predictive subgroup.
