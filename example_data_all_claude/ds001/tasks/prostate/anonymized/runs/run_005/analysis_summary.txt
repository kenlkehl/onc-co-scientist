ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_prostate (50,000 patients, anonymized features)
========================================================================

OUTCOME
-------
Single binary outcome: objective_response.
Overall ORR = 0.2402 (12,011 / 50,000 responders).

DATASET STRUCTURE
-----------------
33 informative features (feature_030 had a single value of 0 in every row and
was dropped from all regressions). Twelve features are binary and one is
3-category (feature_001 with values 0/1/2). The remaining features are
continuous lab/biometric variables; some were highly right-skewed
(feature_022, feature_024) and were log1p-transformed for regressions.


HEADLINE FINDING
================

There is one and only one feature that behaves like an effective therapy in
this cohort: feature_008. Its effect on objective_response is highly
heterogeneous and is *entirely* concentrated in a four-criterion patient
subgroup. Outside that subgroup the treatment has essentially zero effect.

  Favorable subgroup (15,681 patients, 31.4% of cohort):
      feature_013 = 0  AND  feature_015 = 0  AND
      feature_021 = 0  AND  feature_027 = 0
      → ORR(feature_008 = 1) = 0.7978
      → ORR(feature_008 = 0) = 0.1721
      → Δ ORR = +0.626  (chi-square p ≈ 0; LPM interaction t = 84.8)

  Unfavorable subgroup (34,319 patients):
      Any one or more of feature_013/015/021/027 = 1
      → Δ ORR(feature_008) ≈ +0.008 (p ≈ 0.05–0.10 depending on adjustment;
         clinically negligible)

Each of the four "suppressor" features is independently necessary for the
subgroup definition: holding the other three at zero and toggling any one
suppressor from 0 to 1 collapses the treatment effect from +0.63 to ≤+0.025.
This was confirmed both with stratified ORR comparisons and with a model-free
T-learner gradient-boosting + regression-tree subgroup discovery, which
spontaneously selected feature_013, feature_015, and feature_021 as the top
splits driving individual treatment-effect heterogeneity (mean ITE in
favorable leaves ≈ 0.55–0.60 vs ≈ 0 elsewhere). feature_027 (3% prevalence)
was missed by the tree only because of the leaf-size constraint, but
targeted analyses unambiguously confirm it as an additional suppressor.


HYPOTHESIS TRAJECTORY ACROSS 25 ITERATIONS
==========================================

Iteration 1: Baseline. Established overall ORR (0.240) and noted feature_030
has zero variance.

Iterations 2–4: Main-effect screen.
- feature_008 = 1 raised ORR from 0.159 → 0.361 (Δ +0.202): clear positive
  signal, almost certainly an active anti-cancer therapy.
- feature_013 = 1 (55% prevalence) lowered ORR from 0.346 → 0.154 (Δ -0.192).
- Smaller adverse main effects: feature_021 (Δ -0.10), feature_015
  (Δ -0.10), feature_027 (Δ -0.07).
- feature_001 (3-category) showed a strict monotonic gradient: ORR 0.282 →
  0.228 → 0.182 (consistent with an ECOG-like performance status).
- Among continuous features, feature_022 (highly skewed, range 0.12–3622;
  consistent with PSA) was the strongest prognostic factor (mean 47.2 in
  non-responders vs 28.2 in responders, p ≈ 1e-137). feature_002
  (3.8 ± 0.5; albumin-like) was modestly positive; feature_020 and
  log(feature_024) (AST/ALT-like) were modestly negative.
- Hypothesis that feature_010 (integer 6–10; Gleason-like) predicts response
  was REFUTED: no association at all.

Iteration 5: Multivariable logistic regression confirmed the same set of
independent predictors and showed that feature_023, feature_011, feature_017,
feature_019, feature_004 (all binary, prevalences 5–30%) carry NO independent
main-effect signal.

Iterations 6–9: Heterogeneity of feature_008. The interaction
feature_008 × feature_013 was overwhelming (Δ +0.44 when feature_013 = 0
vs +0.01 when feature_013 = 1). Stratifying further within feature_013 = 0,
each of feature_021, feature_015, feature_027 individually flips the
treatment effect off when set to 1 (interaction p-values 1e-78, 1e-163,
1e-27 respectively). The four-suppressor subgroup definition was assembled,
each suppressor verified necessary by a leave-one-out toggle test, and the
joint subgroup effect quantified at Δ +0.626.

Iteration 10: Search for residual modifiers within the favorable subgroup.
Only weak signals (feature_005 and feature_017 with nominal p ≈ 0.004 and
0.033) — small magnitude (within-favorable ORR(T=1) shifts only by ~0.03–
0.04 between modifier strata). The four-suppressor definition is not
materially improved by additional refinements.

Iteration 11: Tree-based subgroup discovery (T-learner GBC + regression
tree on ITE) independently recovered feature_013, feature_015,
feature_021 as the dominant splits, supporting the finding.

Iteration 12: The five non-008 candidate-treatment binary features
(feature_023, feature_011, feature_017, feature_019, feature_004) have NO
detectable treatment effect on objective_response — neither overall, nor in
any subgroup defined by other features, nor in any pairwise combination.
At a Bonferroni-style threshold (p < 0.01) NONE of their interactions with
the modifier panel were significant.

Iterations 13–18: Confirming that other clinical features
(feature_006, feature_005, age (feature_016), sodium/potassium/calcium
analogues (feature_007/014/032), creatinine/bilirubin analogues
(feature_025/012), feature_018, feature_026 hemoglobin-like, feature_010
Gleason-like) carry no independent association with objective_response after
multivariable adjustment.

Iterations 19–22: Refinement and consolidation.
- Within the favorable subgroup, feature_001 is an additive prognostic
  factor (ORR 0.475 → 0.410 → 0.355 across categories) but does NOT modify
  the treatment effect of feature_008 (interaction p = 0.07, n.s. after
  multiple testing).
- Higher log(feature_022) and higher feature_020 lower ORR independently in
  the multivariable model, but their interactions with feature_008 are not
  significant — they are prognostic, not predictive.
- Within the unfavorable subgroup, feature_008 has at most a +0.01 absolute
  effect even with full adjustment (logit coef +0.062, z = 2.04).
- The four suppressor variables themselves are largely effect modifiers
  rather than prognostic factors: among the feature_008 = 0 control arm,
  their direct ORR effects are small (|Δ| ≤ 0.026).
- No synergistic combinations among the inactive candidate treatments.

Iteration 23: Selection-bias check. The probability of receiving feature_008
is essentially identical in the favorable subgroup (40.3%) and the
unfavorable subgroup (40.1%); chi-square p = 0.58. The dramatic interaction
is therefore not driven by differential treatment selection across
suppressor strata.

Iterations 24–25: Final consolidated subgroup hypothesis (h33) and
prognostic-adjustment sensitivity check (h34). Within the favorable subgroup,
adjusting for feature_001, feature_002, feature_020, log(feature_022), and
log(feature_024) leaves the absolute treatment effect of feature_008
essentially unchanged at +0.625 (LPM t = 100.0; logit coef +3.02, z = 70.2).
The headline finding is robust to confounding by the major prognostic
covariates.

CONCLUSIONS — TREATMENT–BIOMARKER–OUTCOME RELATIONSHIPS
=======================================================

1. **One effective therapy.** feature_008 is the only feature in the panel
   that produces a large positive shift in objective_response.

2. **Effect is fully gated by four binary clinical/biomarker features.** The
   feature_008 benefit is essentially zero unless the patient simultaneously
   has feature_013 = 0, feature_015 = 0, feature_021 = 0, AND
   feature_027 = 0. Each of these four "suppressor" features acts
   independently and at very different prevalences (55%, 20%, 10%, 3%), and
   each is individually necessary for treatment benefit. Inside the
   four-suppressor subgroup the absolute response increase from feature_008
   is +63 percentage points (17% → 80%); outside it the effect is
   indistinguishable from zero.

3. **Prognostic vs predictive.** Several features (feature_001,
   log(feature_022), feature_002, feature_020, log(feature_024)) are strong
   PROGNOSTIC factors but not PREDICTIVE: they shift baseline ORR but do not
   modify the feature_008 treatment effect.

4. **Inactive candidate therapies.** The five other binary features that
   prevalence-wise look like therapies (feature_023, feature_011, feature_017,
   feature_019, feature_004) have neither main effects nor any detectable
   subgroup or combination effects on objective_response in this cohort.

5. **Some clinical priors do not hold.** feature_010 (which by range 6–10
   resembles Gleason score) shows no association with objective_response
   here. feature_016 (age-like), feature_026 (hemoglobin-like), and the
   renal/electrolyte panel are likewise uninformative after adjustment.

The single most important, highest-quality finding from this dataset is the
four-suppressor subgroup definition of feature_008's treatment effect on
objective_response.
