Analysis Summary - ds001_prostate
==================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients, 32 anonymized features, single binary outcome (objective_response).
Overall response rate: 24.0% (12,011 / 50,000).

OVERVIEW OF THE EVIDENCE
------------------------

1. Univariate screen.
   - feature_008 (40% of cohort positive): response 36.1% vs 15.9% off (chi-square p<<1e-300, +20.2 pp). Hypothesized treatment with positive effect.
   - feature_013 (55% positive): response 15.4% vs 34.6% (-19.2 pp, p<<1e-300). Strong negative marker.
   - feature_021 (10% positive): response 15.0% vs 25.0% (-10.0 pp, p=5e-56).
   - feature_015 (20% positive): response 16.0% vs 26.0% (-10.0 pp, p=2e-98).
   - feature_027 (3% positive): response 17.6% vs 24.2% (-6.6 pp, p=3e-9).
   - feature_001 (3-level): response 28.2% / 22.8% / 18.2% across levels 0/1/2 (p=1e-72) - prognostic gradient.
   - feature_010 (5-level), feature_006, feature_005, feature_023, feature_011, feature_017, feature_019, feature_004: no marginal association (all p>=0.30).
   - Continuous: feature_022 (highly skewed, range 0.12-3622) strongly negative (p=1e-137); feature_020 negative (p=7e-31); feature_024 negative (p=9e-9); feature_002 small positive (p=4e-6). feature_016 (age-like) and most other continuous features null.
   - feature_030 was constant (single value 0) and dropped from all subsequent analyses.

2. Multivariable logistic regression with all features.
   Coefficients confirmed direction and magnitude of the univariate signals. feature_008 +1.20, feature_013 -1.13, feature_015 -0.69, feature_021 -0.74, feature_027 -0.43 (each p<<1e-8). feature_022 retained a small but highly significant negative slope. feature_020 and feature_024 also remained significant. The block of binary features with no main effect (feature_006, feature_005, feature_023, feature_011, feature_017, feature_019, feature_004) all had multivariable p>0.20.

3. Treatment-effect heterogeneity screen (the protocol's central requirement).
   Treating feature_008 as the candidate treatment:
   - Likelihood-ratio interaction tests of feature_008 with every other feature produced four enormous interactions:
     feature_008 x feature_013: LR=1885, beta_int=-1.98 (treatment effect collapses when feature_013=1).
     feature_008 x feature_015: LR=427,  beta_int=-1.24.
     feature_008 x feature_021: LR=188,  beta_int=-1.14.
     feature_008 x feature_027: LR=85,   beta_int=-1.28.
   - feature_008 x feature_022 also yielded LR=348 (beta_int=-0.0087 per unit) but this signal disappears once the four binary markers are conditioned on (see below).
   - All other interactions were below LR=10 and would not survive multiple-testing correction.

   Symmetric screen: testing each binary feature with no main effect (feature_006, _005, _023, _011, _017, _019, _004) for any strong heterogeneity ruled them out as additional treatments (no LR>=10 anywhere).

4. Joint interaction model.
   logit(P(response)) ~ feature_008 + feature_013 + feature_021 + feature_015 + feature_027
                       + feature_008:feature_013 + feature_008:feature_021
                       + feature_008:feature_015 + feature_008:feature_027
   Results: feature_008 main = +2.58 (z=70). All four interaction terms strongly negative
   (-2.20, -1.52, -1.61, -1.65), all p<1e-10. Marker main effects on the control arm were small
   (-0.14 to +0.18 logits), demonstrating that these markers act primarily as treatment-effect
   modifiers rather than as strong main prognostic factors among untreated patients.

5. The 'clean' subgroup definition.
   Define CLEAN = (feature_013==0 & feature_021==0 & feature_015==0 & feature_027==0).
   - Clean (n=15,681; 31.4% of cohort):
        feature_008=1 -> response 79.8% (5,047 / 6,325)
        feature_008=0 -> response 17.2% (1,610 / 9,356)
        Absolute risk reduction = +62.6 pp; NNT = 1.6; OR = 18.9; chi-square p<<1e-300.
   - Unclean (any of the four markers; n=34,319):
        feature_008=1 -> response 16.1% (2,210 / 13,751)
        feature_008=0 -> response 15.3% (3,147 / 20,568)
        ARR = +0.8 pp; OR = 1.06; chi-square p=0.07.

   Each marker individually is sufficient to abolish the effect: in patients with only feature_013=1
   (no other markers) ARR=+0.7 pp; only feature_021=1 ARR=+0.4 pp; only feature_015=1 ARR=+2.1 pp;
   only feature_027=1 ARR=-2.5 pp. The subgroup definition therefore requires the conjunction
   of all four 'clean' criteria; relaxing any one re-introduces a contaminated population in
   which the effect dilutes to ~0.

6. Within-subgroup checks.
   - Within clean: a fresh interaction screen produced no LR>10 modifier (best was feature_005
     LR=8.3, p=0.004 - does not survive Bonferroni). feature_022 quintiles within the clean
     subgroup gave virtually identical absolute risk differences (+0.64, +0.63, +0.60, +0.65, +0.61),
     confirming feature_022 is correlated with the four markers but adds no independent modification.
   - Within unclean: a similar screen produced no rescue feature (best: feature_005 LR=8.3,
     p=0.004 with positive interaction sign, but absolute treatment effect remains ~0).
   - feature_001 was prognostic within both arms but the absolute treatment effect remained large
     across all three feature_001 levels within the clean subgroup (+64.6, +62.0, +59.0 pp),
     i.e., prognostic but not predictive of treatment effect on the absolute risk-difference scale.

7. Confounding/randomization check.
   Standardized mean differences between feature_008=1 and feature_008=0 were |SMD|<0.02 for every
   one of the 30 baseline features. The treatment is balanced as in a randomized assignment;
   the +20 pp main effect is not a confounded estimate. This also justifies the unadjusted
   subgroup analyses above as causal-effect estimates.

8. Decision-tree subgroup discovery (T-learner ITE -> regression tree of depth 3).
   The tree top-splits on feature_013, then feature_015 within feature_013=0, then feature_021
   within feature_013=0 & feature_015=0. Largest leaf ITE = 0.55 (feature_013=0, feature_015=0,
   feature_021=0). Lowest leaves = -0.13 to -0.09 (feature_013=1 with secondary splits).
   feature_027, with only 3% prevalence, did not split at depth 3 but its role is captured by the
   joint logistic model.

9. Predictive performance.
   Full additive logistic model: AUC=0.721. Adding the four feature_008 x marker interactions:
   AUC=0.733 (+0.012). Within the clean subgroup, feature_008 alone gives AUC=0.808 for
   objective_response - treatment status is the dominant predictor in that population.

HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED VS. REFUTED
--------------------------------

Supported:
  - feature_008 has a strong positive marginal effect on objective_response and is plausibly
    a randomized treatment indicator (perfect baseline balance).
  - feature_013, feature_015, feature_021, feature_027 are negative markers for response,
    each with a strong treatment-effect-modifier role: their presence essentially nullifies
    feature_008's benefit.
  - The conjunction (feature_013=0 & feature_021=0 & feature_015=0 & feature_027=0) defines a
    well-circumscribed responder subgroup (~31% of cohort) with treatment ARR ~+63 pp and
    NNT ~1.6.
  - feature_001 is prognostic (lower response at higher levels) but not a meaningful modifier
    of feature_008's absolute effect.
  - feature_022, feature_020, feature_024 are prognostic continuous markers; feature_022 looks
    correlated with the four binary markers and its apparent interaction with treatment is
    fully absorbed once the markers are conditioned on.

Refuted / null:
  - feature_010, feature_006, feature_005, feature_023, feature_011, feature_017, feature_019,
    feature_004, feature_016, feature_018, feature_031, feature_026, feature_009, feature_029,
    feature_003, feature_012, feature_025, feature_028, feature_007, feature_014, feature_032
    are not meaningful predictors of response or modifiers of treatment effect in this dataset.
  - feature_030 is constant and uninformative.

FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS
------------------------------------------

Treatment: feature_008.
Outcome: objective_response.
Direction: positive.
Subgroup: feature_013==0 AND feature_021==0 AND feature_015==0 AND feature_027==0.
Magnitude: response rate 79.8% on treatment vs 17.2% off treatment within the subgroup
(absolute risk reduction +62.6 percentage points, NNT 1.6, OR 18.9). Outside the subgroup
the treatment effect is essentially null (ARR +0.8 pp, OR 1.06, p=0.07). The subgroup
contains 31.4% of the cohort. Each of the four 'clean' criteria is individually necessary;
the presence of any single unfavorable marker is sufficient to abolish treatment benefit.

OVERALL CONCLUSION
------------------

This dataset behaves like a randomized trial of a single treatment indicator (feature_008)
embedded in a 32-feature electronic-health-record panel. Marginally, the treatment yields
a 20 percentage-point absolute increase in objective response, but this aggregate effect
is the dilution of an extreme heterogeneity: a +63 pp benefit in the ~31% of patients
characterized by feature_013=0, feature_021=0, feature_015=0, and feature_027=0, and
essentially no benefit in the remaining ~69% in whom one or more of those four markers
is positive. No additional features modify the treatment effect within either the
favorable or unfavorable subgroup at any level of correction for multiple testing. The
four-marker rule is sufficient to define the predictive biomarker pattern.
