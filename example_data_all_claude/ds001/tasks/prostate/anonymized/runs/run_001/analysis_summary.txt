ds001_prostate — Analysis Summary
=====================================

Cohort: 50,000 prostate-cancer patients aggregated from electronic health records.
Outcome: objective_response (binary; 24.0% positive overall, 12,011/50,000).
Features: 32 de-identified covariates (one of which, feature_030, is constant and was dropped).

Approach
--------
We used a propose-test-refine loop. In each iteration we (a) named one or
more hypotheses about marginal effects, subgroup heterogeneity, or treatment
modification, (b) tested them with logistic regression, stratified
risk-difference analysis, chi-square tests, and decision-tree subgroup
discovery, and (c) used the results to refine the next iteration. After
characterising main effects we ran systematic treatment-by-feature
interaction screens for every binary covariate with a null marginal
association (the natural "candidate randomized treatment" set) plus the one
binary covariate with the largest positive marginal effect, and we
followed every signal up with stratified RDs, joint multivariable models,
and a CART tree.

Main effects
------------
* Strong positive marginal predictor of response:
  - feature_008: response 36.1% on vs 15.9% off, RD +0.20 (OR 2.99, p<1e-300).

* Strong negative marginal predictors of response (adverse prognostic flags):
  - feature_013: 15.4% vs 34.6%, RD -0.19 (OR 0.34, p<1e-300).
  - feature_015: 16.0% vs 26.0%, RD -0.10 (OR 0.54, p=2e-98).
  - feature_021: 15.0% vs 25.0%, RD -0.10 (OR 0.53, p=5e-56).
  - feature_027: 17.6% vs 24.2%, RD -0.07 (OR 0.67, p=3e-9).
  - feature_001 (ordinal 0/1/2): rates 28.2%/22.8%/18.2%, monotone trend p=1e-72.
  - feature_022 (right-skewed continuous, PSA-like): inverse, p=2e-94.
  - feature_020 (range 0-24.6): inverse, p=3e-29.
  - feature_024: inverse, p=6e-8.
  - feature_002: positive, p=4e-6.

* No marginal association: feature_005, feature_006, feature_004, feature_011,
  feature_017, feature_019, feature_023 (all binary, all RD ~ 0, p > 0.3),
  and feature_010 (Gleason-like 6-10), feature_016 (age), feature_018,
  feature_026, feature_009, feature_029, feature_003, feature_012,
  feature_025, feature_028, feature_007, feature_014, feature_032, feature_031.

Treatment identification
-----------------------
feature_008 has the largest positive marginal effect on objective_response
and was treated as the candidate treatment indicator. Its marginal effect
(OR ~ 3) is large enough that an interaction screen was warranted before any
subgroup conclusion.

Treatment-effect heterogeneity (the headline result)
---------------------------------------------------
Pairwise multiplicative-interaction logistic regression of feature_008
against every other covariate identified four binary modifiers, each
strongly significant after Bonferroni correction across ~150 tests:

  feature_008 x feature_013  beta_int = -1.98  (p effectively 0)
  feature_008 x feature_015  beta_int = -1.24  (p = 1.4e-93)
  feature_008 x feature_021  beta_int = -1.14  (p = 3.3e-42)
  feature_008 x feature_027  beta_int = -1.28  (p = 1.8e-19)

Stratified risk differences confirm the size of each modifier:

           treated  control   RD       p
  feature_013=0  61.0%   16.9%   +0.440   ~0
  feature_013=1  15.8%   15.0%   +0.008   0.09
  feature_015=0  41.1%   16.0%   +0.251   ~0
  feature_015=1  16.5%   15.7%   +0.008   0.27
  feature_021=0  38.5%   16.0%   +0.225   ~0
  feature_021=1  15.3%   14.7%   +0.006   0.57
  feature_027=0  36.7%   15.8%   +0.209   ~0
  feature_027=1  16.2%   18.4%   -0.022   0.30

The four flags are statistically INDEPENDENT of each other (all pairwise
phi correlations ~0; all chi-square p-values > 0.37), so they cannot be
explained as redundant codings of a single underlying state.

Joint subgroup
--------------
Because the four flags are independent and each individually suppresses
the feature_008 effect, we tested the joint definition

    fav := (feature_013=0 AND feature_015=0 AND feature_021=0 AND feature_027=0)

which selects 15,681 patients (31.4% of the cohort).

           N      treated  control   RD       OR
  fav=1   15,681  79.78%   17.21%   +0.626   18.98   p < 1e-300
  fav=0   34,319  16.06%   15.30%   +0.008    1.06   p = 0.10

A logistic regression with feature_008 x fav interaction gives an OR for
treatment of 18.98 in the favorable subgroup and 1.06 elsewhere. The
interaction term is the largest single explanatory term in any model we
fit. A shallow CART decision tree (max_depth=5) trained on the full feature
set independently selects exactly the same split structure: feature_008
first; then within feature_008=1, sequential splits on feature_013,
feature_015, feature_021. The only leaf predicting "response" is the path
feature_008=1, feature_013=0, feature_015=0, feature_021=0. (feature_027
is rare enough that it falls below the tree's leaf-size threshold but its
suppressive effect is captured by the regression analyses.)

Counts of flags ("any flag" governs the heterogeneity, not a graded count):
  n_flags=0: RD = +0.626  (n=15,681)
  n_flags=1: RD = +0.009  (n=25,372)
  n_flags=2: RD = +0.008  (n= 8,186)
  n_flags=3: RD = -0.022  (n=   745)
  n_flags=4: RD = -0.091  (n=    16)
The drop is essentially complete at the first flag — adding more flags does
not further attenuate the (already null) treatment effect.

Tests for additional modifiers
------------------------------
* Within the favorable subgroup the feature_008 RD is essentially constant
  across age tertiles (0.626, 0.619, 0.632), feature_010 levels 6-10
  (0.629, 0.621, 0.635, 0.615, 0.634), feature_022 quartiles (0.633,
  0.628, 0.624, 0.618), and other continuous lab features. A weak trend
  across feature_001 levels (0.646, 0.622, 0.590) does not reach
  significance (interaction p = 0.069 within the subgroup).
* The apparent modification of feature_008 by continuous feature_022 in
  the marginal data is fully mediated by feature_013 (median feature_022
  is 7.4 in feature_013=0 vs 32.9 in feature_013=1). In a joint model that
  includes the four flag interactions, feature_008 x feature_022, x
  feature_020, x feature_002, and x feature_001 are all non-significant
  (p >= 0.22).
* Pairwise interaction screens of the seven null-marginal binaries
  (feature_005, _006, _004, _011, _017, _019, _023) against the four
  flags and against each other found no significant heterogeneity after
  Bonferroni. These features appear to be true noise / neutral
  demographic indicators with respect to objective_response.

Hypotheses supported
--------------------
* feature_008 raises objective_response (large marginal effect).
* feature_013, feature_015, feature_021, feature_027 are independent
  adverse prognostic flags and independent suppressors of the feature_008
  effect.
* feature_001 has a monotone inverse association with response.
* feature_022, feature_020, feature_024 have inverse associations;
  feature_002 has a positive association.
* The favorable joint predicate (all four flags absent) fully
  characterises the responsive subgroup.

Hypotheses refuted
------------------
* The treatment effect is heterogeneous on continuous feature_022 — refuted
  after adjustment (mediated by feature_013).
* The treatment effect is heterogeneous on age, Gleason-like
  feature_010, or feature_001 — refuted (RDs essentially flat).
* feature_005, _006, _004, _011, _017, _019, _023 contain hidden
  treatment-like effects in some subgroup — refuted on systematic
  pairwise screens.
* The number of flags governs the heterogeneity in a graded fashion —
  refuted; the suppression is essentially complete at the first flag.

Final, best-supported treatment-effect-heterogeneity hypothesis
--------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment: feature_008.
Outcome: objective_response (binary).
Direction: positive (treatment raises response).
Subgroup definition (must hold in entirety): feature_013 = 0
AND feature_015 = 0 AND feature_021 = 0 AND feature_027 = 0.
In that subgroup (n=15,681; 31.4% of the cohort) the response rate
is 79.8% on feature_008 vs 17.2% off feature_008 (RD = +62.6
percentage points; OR = 18.98). In any patient with at least one
of the four flags set (n=34,319; 68.6% of the cohort) feature_008 has
no clinically meaningful effect (RD = +0.8 percentage points;
OR = 1.06). Each of the four flags suppresses the effect
independently and essentially completely.
