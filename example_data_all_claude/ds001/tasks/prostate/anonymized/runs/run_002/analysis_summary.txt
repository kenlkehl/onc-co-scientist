ds001_prostate — Analysis Summary
=================================

Cohort
------
50,000 prostate cancer patients (no missing values), 32 anonymized features
plus a binary outcome `objective_response`. Overall response rate is 0.2402
(12,011 / 50,000). One feature, `feature_030`, is constant zero and was
discarded as uninformative. `feature_010` (5-level, 6-10) is also unrelated
to response (chi-square p = 0.77).

Approach
--------
Twenty-five iterations of propose / test / refine. The design walked from
univariate associations to a treatment-by-feature interaction screen, to
nested subgroup analyses, to a model-free tree-based confirmation, ending in
a final integrated subgroup hypothesis. All p-values are unadjusted; multiple
testing is acknowledged where it changes the interpretation.

1. Identifying the treatment indicator
--------------------------------------
A univariate screen of all binary/integer features versus `objective_response`
found a single feature with a large *positive* association: `feature_008`
(response 36.14% if =1 vs 15.89% if =0; crude difference +20.25 percentage
points, p << 1e-300). After multivariable adjustment for the other prognostic
markers (feature_013, feature_021, feature_015, feature_027, feature_001,
log feature_022, log feature_024, feature_020, feature_002), `feature_008`
remained the dominant positive predictor (OR = 3.33, p ≈ 0). I treat
`feature_008` as the treatment indicator throughout.

2. Univariate prognostic landscape
----------------------------------
Negative associations with response (univariate):
  - feature_013 (binary, prevalence 55%): 15.4% vs 34.6% response, diff -19.3pp.
  - feature_015 (binary, 20%): -10.0pp.
  - feature_021 (binary, 10%): -10.0pp.
  - feature_027 (binary, 3%): -6.6pp.
  - feature_001 (3-level 0/1/2): 28.2% / 22.8% / 18.2%, ordinal.
  - feature_022 (long-tailed, log scale): r = -0.087, p ≈ 1e-137.
  - feature_020, feature_024 (continuous): smaller negative effects.
  - feature_002 (continuous): small positive effect (p = 4e-6).
Null:
  - feature_016 (age-like 30-90), feature_006, feature_005, feature_023,
    feature_011, feature_017, feature_019, feature_004, feature_010 and most
    other continuous features.

3. Treatment-effect heterogeneity screen
----------------------------------------
For each candidate modifier `x`, I fit a logistic model
`logit(P(y=1)) = a + b·t + c·x + d·t·x` with t = feature_008 and recorded the
interaction coefficient d. The largest negative interactions (treatment
benefit suppressed) were:
  - t × feature_013      d = -1.978   p ≈ 0
  - t × log(feature_022) d = -0.568   p = 1.5e-128
  - t × feature_015      d = -1.237   p = 1.4e-93
  - t × feature_021      d = -1.138   p = 3.3e-42
  - t × feature_027      d = -1.283   p = 1.8e-19
The only positive interaction of any size was t × feature_001
(d = +0.141, p = 1.6e-5), which on inspection turned out to be a prognostic
artifact rather than a true effect modifier (see point 5).

4. Stratified treatment effects (the central finding)
-----------------------------------------------------
Crude risk differences (treated minus untreated response rate):

  Stratum                       n        diff         p
  feature_013 = 0           22,519   +44.0 pp      ≈ 0
  feature_013 = 1           27,481    +0.8 pp     0.088 (n.s.)

  feature_015 = 0           39,962   +25.1 pp      ≈ 0
  feature_015 = 1           10,038    +0.9 pp     0.27 (n.s.)

  feature_021 = 0           45,004   +22.5 pp      ≈ 0
  feature_021 = 1            4,996    +0.6 pp     0.57 (n.s.)

  feature_027 = 0           48,472   +20.9 pp      ≈ 0
  feature_027 = 1            1,528    -2.2 pp     0.30 (n.s.)

For each of the four binary markers, the unfavorable (=1) level *abolishes*
the treatment effect on objective_response. The four markers are mutually
independent (all pairwise correlations < 0.005, all chi-square p > 0.37),
and their joint absence (the "all-four-favorable" subgroup, prevalence ~31.4%,
matching the product 0.45 × 0.80 × 0.90 × 0.97) defines a subgroup with a
very large treatment benefit:

  Cumulative restriction                                     diff
  feature_013 = 0                                          +44.0 pp
  feature_013 = 0 & feature_015 = 0                        +54.6 pp
  feature_013 = 0 & feature_015 = 0 & feature_021 = 0      +60.7 pp
  fav4 (above & feature_027 = 0)         (n = 15,681)      +62.6 pp
                                       (17.2% → 79.8%)

5. Apparent feature_022 and feature_001 modifications were artefacts
--------------------------------------------------------------------
At the cohort level, `feature_022` looked like a strong continuous treatment-
effect modifier (per-quartile diffs +36.7, +26.0, +14.8, +3.4 pp). However:
  (a) `feature_013 = 1` patients have mean log(feature_022) higher by
      +1.38 log-units (p ≈ 0). So feature_022 is largely a noisy reflection
      of feature_013 status.
  (b) Within the all-favorable subgroup, the treatment×log(feature_022)
      interaction is non-significant (coef -0.039, p = 0.34), and the
      treatment risk difference is essentially constant across feature_022
      quartiles (+63.3, +62.8, +62.4, +61.8 pp).
The cohort-level feature_022 interaction is therefore explained by feature_013.

`feature_001` is a strong prognostic factor (response declines with level)
but does not change the *absolute* treatment benefit: across feature_001
levels 0/1/2, the crude treatment risk difference is +20.7, +20.1, +19.8 pp.
Within the all-favorable subgroup the risk difference is +64.6, +62.2,
+59.0 pp. feature_001 modulates the response rate in both arms similarly
and is not a treatment-effect modifier.

6. Joint-interaction logistic
-----------------------------
With all four interaction terms in a single model:
  feature_008 main:                   coef +2.62  (OR 13.8)
  t × feature_013:                    coef -2.23   p ≈ 0
  t × feature_015:                    coef -1.64   p ≈ 0
  t × feature_021:                    coef -1.55   p < 1e-66
  t × feature_027:                    coef -1.67   p < 1e-26
  feature_013 main:                   coef -0.035  p = 0.33
  feature_015 main:                   coef -0.019  p = 0.63
  feature_021 main:                   coef -0.099  p = 0.07
  feature_027 main:                   coef +0.189  p = 0.028
The marker main effects collapse to near-zero once the interactions are in
the model. The four markers carry essentially no prognostic information in
the *untreated* arm; their entire univariate "negative-prognostic" signal is
induced by the treatment-arm composition. They are pure treatment-effect
modifiers, not generic prognostic markers.

7. Tree-based confirmation
--------------------------
A T-learner (random forest fit separately to T=1 and T=0, predicted ITE =
P(y|T=1) - P(y|T=0)) yields a bimodal ITE distribution (median 0.11, P75
0.46). A depth-4 decision tree on (ITE > 0.3) splits sequentially on
feature_013, feature_015, feature_021, and assigns the high-ITE class only
to the joint feature_013 = 0 & feature_015 = 0 & feature_021 = 0 leaf
(further continuous splits do not change the class). Independent
ML-driven confirmation of the rule found by stratification.

8. Residual heterogeneity within the all-favorable subgroup
-----------------------------------------------------------
Re-running the treatment×feature interaction screen *within* the
all-favorable subgroup, the smallest p-values are feature_005 (p = 0.004),
feature_017 (p = 0.033), feature_026 (p = 0.058), feature_001 (p = 0.069);
none survives a Bonferroni adjustment for ~26 tests, and none yields a
stratum risk difference more than ~5 pp away from +62.6. fav4 captures
essentially all of the heterogeneity that exists.

9. Final integrated subgroup hypothesis
---------------------------------------
Treatment (feature_008) raises objective_response by approximately
+62.6 percentage points (17.2% → 79.8%, OR ≈ 19) ONLY in patients with
feature_013 = 0 AND feature_015 = 0 AND feature_021 = 0 AND feature_027 = 0
(~31.4% of the cohort). Outside this subgroup (i.e., in patients with at
least one unfavorable marker) the treatment effect on response is +0.77 pp
(p = 0.056) and is not clinically meaningful.

Hypotheses supported
--------------------
  h2a feature_008 is the treatment indicator (very large positive marginal
       and adjusted effect).
  h2b/h3a/h3b: feature_013, feature_015, feature_021, feature_027,
       feature_001, feature_022, feature_020, feature_024 are negatively
       associated with response in the univariate sense.
  h4   treatment-feature_008 effect survives multivariable adjustment.
  h6a  treatment effect is heterogeneous (large interactions found).
  h7-h10  feature_013/015/021/027 each fully suppress the treatment effect
       at their unfavorable level.
  h13-h15  cumulative restriction yields a +62.6 pp treatment effect in the
       all-favorable subgroup.
  h16  a single binary fav4 indicator captures the dominant heterogeneity
       (interaction OR ≈ 18).
  h20  the four unfavorable markers are mutually independent.
  h21  feature_013 is correlated with feature_022, explaining the
       cohort-level feature_022 × treatment interaction.
  h22  T-learner / decision-tree confirms the same subgroup definition
       independent of the stratification path.
  h23  treatment is essentially inactive in any-unfavorable patients
       (+0.77 pp, p = 0.056).
  h24  the four markers act as treatment-effect modifiers, not prognostic
       markers — main effects collapse to ~0 once interactions are modeled.
  h25  final integrated subgroup hypothesis (above).

Hypotheses refuted / refined
----------------------------
  h11  the cohort-level feature_022 × treatment interaction looks strong
       but is largely confounded by feature_013; within fav4, feature_022
       does not modify the treatment effect (p = 0.34). Refined.
  h12  feature_001 modifies risk in both arms (a true prognostic marker)
       but does not modify the *absolute* treatment effect; not a
       treatment-effect modifier. Refined.
  h2c  feature_010 (Gleason-like 6-10) and feature_006/005/023/011/017/019/004
       are not associated with response.
  h3c  feature_016 (age-like) is not associated with response.

Overall conclusion
------------------
The data describe a setting in which a single binary treatment (feature_008)
roughly *quadruples* the response rate (from 17% to 80%) in a clearly
defined ~31% subgroup of patients (those with feature_013 = 0 &
feature_015 = 0 & feature_021 = 0 & feature_027 = 0), while doing essentially
nothing in the remaining ~69% of patients. The four binary markers are
mutually independent and act exclusively as treatment-effect modifiers (their
main effects are essentially zero in the untreated arm). feature_001
remains a meaningful baseline prognostic factor that attenuates response in
both arms but does not need to be part of the treatment-modifier definition;
feature_022's apparent modifier role at the cohort level is a confounded
reflection of feature_013 status. The most parsimonious clinically actionable
rule is the four-marker negative test, and a logistic model with one binary
fav4-by-treatment interaction captures the heterogeneity nearly as well as
the full four-way interaction model.
