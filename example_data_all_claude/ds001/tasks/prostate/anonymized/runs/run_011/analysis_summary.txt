Analysis summary — ds001_prostate (50,000 patients; objective_response prevalence 24.0%)
=========================================================================================

Headline finding
----------------
feature_008 is the active treatment indicator in this cohort, and its benefit on
objective_response is driven almost entirely by a four-binary subgroup defined by
the absence of all of feature_013, feature_015, feature_021, and feature_027. In
that subgroup (~31% of patients, n=15,681) treatment lifts the response rate from
17.2% (untreated) to 79.8% (treated), a +62.6 percentage-point absolute benefit.
In the complementary subgroup (any of those four binaries set to 1) the response
rate is essentially identical with or without treatment (15.3% vs 16.1%, a +0.8 pp
difference that does not reach statistical significance).

How the conclusion was reached
------------------------------
1. Univariate screen.  We tested every candidate feature against objective_response
   with chi-square (categorical) or point-biserial (continuous) tests. Eight
   features were strongly associated (p < 1e-8) with response:
     - feature_008  (binary, prevalence 40%)  : 15.9% -> 36.1%, +20.1 pp; the only
       large positive marginal effect among the binaries.
     - feature_013  (binary, prevalence 55%)  : 34.6% -> 15.4%, -19.2 pp; the
       largest negative effect.
     - feature_015  (binary, prevalence 20%)  : 26.0% -> 16.0%, -10.0 pp.
     - feature_021  (binary, prevalence 10%)  : 25.0% -> 15.0%, -10.0 pp.
     - feature_022  (continuous, heavy-tailed; PSA-like): r=-0.087.
     - feature_001  (3-level ordinal; stage-like): 28.2% / 22.8% / 18.2%.
     - feature_027  (binary, prevalence 3%)   : 24.2% -> 17.6%, -6.6 pp.
     - feature_020 / feature_024 / feature_002 : weak but real effects (|r| < 0.06).
   feature_010 (5-level, range 6-10; biologically suggestive of Gleason grade) was
   FLAT across response (p = 0.77). feature_016 (range 30-90; suggestive of age),
   feature_007 (sodium-like), feature_014 (potassium-like), feature_032 (calcium-like),
   feature_026 (Hb-like) and the other lab-style continuous variables had no
   detectable association. feature_030 was constant (always 0) and was dropped.

2. Treatment identification.  feature_008 was the only binary with a large positive
   marginal effect on response, and it remained strongly positive (beta = +1.20 on
   log-odds, z = 52.6) in a multivariable logistic regression including all 31
   non-constant features. We treated it as the candidate "active treatment" and
   focused all heterogeneity analyses on it.

3. Treatment-by-feature interaction screen.  We fit a separate logistic
   regression with main effects + product term for each candidate modifier.
   Four binary features showed extremely large interactions with feature_008,
   each in the same direction (treatment benefit eliminated when the modifier = 1):
     - feature_008 x feature_013: beta_int = -1.98  (p < 1e-300)
     - feature_008 x feature_015: beta_int = -1.24  (p ~ 1.4e-93)
     - feature_008 x feature_021: beta_int = -1.14  (p ~ 3.3e-42)
     - feature_008 x feature_027: beta_int = -1.28  (p ~ 1.8e-19)
   feature_022 (PSA-like) showed a continuous interaction (beta = -0.46 on
   log[PSA+1] x feature_008, p ~ 5e-128): treatment benefit decays smoothly as
   PSA rises (Q1: 17%->54%, +37 pp; Q4: 14%->17%, only +3 pp). However, once we
   conditioned on the four-binary good subgroup the residual PSA interaction
   disappeared, indicating the four binaries already capture the PSA effect on
   the treatment axis.
   feature_001 stage shows a small attenuation of the absolute benefit per stage
   level within the good subgroup (Delta of +65, +62, +59 pp at stages 0, 1, 2),
   but treatment is still strongly beneficial at every stage.
   No other binary modifier reached interaction p < 1e-2; no continuous lab
   beyond feature_022 reached p < 1e-2.

4. Stratified subgroup analysis.  Stratifying patients by combinations of the
   four suppressor variables produced a clean monotone pattern:
     suppressors all 0:                                17.3% -> 78.0% on treatment
     013, 015, 021 all 0:                              17.3% -> 78.0% (n=16,175)
     013, 015, 021, 027 all 0 (final 'good'):          17.2% -> 79.8% (n=15,681)
     within feature_013=1 only:                        15.0% -> 15.8% (no benefit)
     within feature_015=1 only:                        15.7% -> 16.5% (no benefit)
     within feature_021=1 only:                        14.7% -> 15.3% (no benefit)
     within feature_027=1 only:                        18.4% -> 16.2% (no benefit)
   Each suppressor independently nullifies the treatment effect, consistent with
   their pairwise correlations being ~0 (|r| < 0.005). Their joint prevalence
   (1-0.55)(1-0.20)(1-0.10)(1-0.03) = 0.314 matches the observed good-subgroup
   share (31.4%).

5. Joint model with all interactions.  In a single logistic regression with
   feature_008, the four suppressors, the four corresponding interaction terms,
   and the strongest covariates (feature_022, feature_001, feature_020, feature_002,
   feature_024) every interaction remained large and highly significant:
     i(feature_008 x feature_013) = -2.23 (p < 1e-300)
     i(feature_008 x feature_015) = -1.64 (p ~ 1e-139)
     i(feature_008 x feature_021) = -1.56 (p ~ 5e-67)
     i(feature_008 x feature_027) = -1.66 (p ~ 2e-27)
   Main feature_008 coefficient in this model = +2.62, indicating ~OR 14 within
   the four-binary good subgroup. Cohort-wide AUC = 0.733.

6. Independent confirmation by decision tree.  A max-depth-5 decision tree with
   min_samples_leaf = 300 built on the candidate features produced exactly the
   subgroup hierarchy implied by the interaction analysis: root split on
   feature_008, then within feature_008==1 successive splits on feature_013 (0/1),
   feature_015 (0/1), feature_021 (0/1). The single class=1 leaf (predicted
   response) was {feature_008==1 AND feature_013==0 AND feature_015==0 AND
   feature_021==0}.

Hypotheses supported and refuted
--------------------------------
Supported:
  - feature_008 is the treatment with a large positive marginal effect on response.
  - feature_013, feature_015, feature_021, feature_027 are negative-prognostic
    binaries with both a (small) main effect and a very large treatment-effect-
    suppressing interaction.
  - feature_022 (PSA-like) is a continuous negative prognostic that also dampens
    treatment effect, but its predictive contribution is absorbed by the four
    binary suppressors.
  - feature_001 is a stage-like ordinal with a small additive negative effect.
  - feature_002 is a (small) positive prognostic; feature_020 and feature_024 are
    (small) negative prognostics, none with meaningful interaction.

Refuted (i.e., found null):
  - feature_010 (Gleason-like 6-10 ordinal) does NOT predict response and does NOT
    modify treatment, despite biological plausibility.
  - feature_016 (age-like) is uninformative both as predictor and as modifier.
  - feature_005, feature_006, feature_011, feature_017, feature_019, feature_004,
    feature_023 are uninformative — neither prognostic nor predictive of treatment
    effect (interaction p>0.01 except a borderline feature_011 at p=0.023 with very
    small magnitude).
  - The "lab" continuous variables (feature_007 sodium-like, feature_014 potassium-
    like, feature_032 calcium-like, feature_026 Hb-like, feature_018 ALP-like,
    feature_009 / feature_003 / feature_012 / feature_025 / feature_028 / feature_029 /
    feature_031) are essentially uninformative.

Final treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis
------------------------------------------
TREATMENT:  feature_008
OUTCOME:    objective_response
DIRECTION:  POSITIVE
SUBGROUP:   feature_013 = 0  AND  feature_015 = 0  AND  feature_021 = 0  AND
            feature_027 = 0
EFFECT:     +62.6 percentage points absolute response (17.2% off-treatment ->
            79.8% on-treatment) inside the subgroup;  ~0 outside the subgroup
            (+0.8 pp in the complementary group, not significant).
PREVALENCE: ~31.4% of the 50,000-patient cohort lies in this subgroup.

Limitations / caveats
---------------------
- Identifications of features as "PSA", "albumin", "Gleason", "age", etc. are
  inferences from distribution shape and biology in a prostate-cancer cohort;
  the dataset uses opaque labels and we did not access any external mapping.
- This is observational data: the assignment of feature_008 (the treatment-like
  variable) is not randomized and the "good" subgroup may also reflect lower
  baseline disease burden. Confounding by indication cannot be ruled out, but
  the treatment-by-suppressor interactions are large enough that they would not
  be eliminated by realistic residual confounding.
- One outcome (objective_response) was available; survival or duration-of-response
  endpoints are not in the dataset.
