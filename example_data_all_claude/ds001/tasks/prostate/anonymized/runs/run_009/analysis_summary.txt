Analysis summary: ds001_prostate (50,000 patients; binary outcome objective_response, baseline rate 24.0%)
================================================================================

Headline finding
----------------
The treatment indicator feature_008 produces a very large, clinically dramatic absolute increase
in objective_response (from 17.21% to 79.78%, an absolute risk difference of +62.6 percentage
points; OR ~19, adjusted OR ~20.5) -- but ONLY in the four-way subgroup defined by

        feature_013 = 0  AND  feature_015 = 0  AND  feature_021 = 0  AND  feature_027 = 0.

This subgroup contains 15,681 patients (31.4% of the cohort). In its complement (any of
feature_013/015/021/027 equal to 1; n = 34,319), the feature_008 effect is essentially null
(15.30% vs 16.06%, RD = +0.76 pp, p ~= 0.07). Each of the four "blocker" features individually
nullifies the treatment effect; their joint absence is necessary and (in this dataset)
sufficient for benefit.

How the picture was built up across iterations
----------------------------------------------
Iteration 1 (univariate binaries). Among binary features, feature_008 (+20.2 pp, p~0),
feature_013 (-19.2 pp, p~0), feature_015 (-10.1 pp, p=2e-98), feature_021 (-10.1 pp, p=5e-56),
and feature_027 (-6.6 pp, p=3e-9) showed the strongest associations with objective_response.
feature_005, 006, 011, 017, 019, 023, 004 showed no meaningful main-effect signal.

Iteration 2 (ordinal). feature_001 (0/1/2) gave a clean monotone gradient (28.2% / 22.8% /
18.2%, coef -0.285 per level, p=9.5e-73), most consistent with a performance-status / stage
indicator. feature_010 (Gleason-like, 6-10) showed a flat 23-24% rate across levels and was
non-prognostic alone.

Iteration 3 (continuous). feature_022 (right-skewed lab, very high range; PSA-like) was the
strongest continuous predictor, negatively associated (coef -0.005, p=2e-94). feature_002
(albumin-like) was modestly positive; feature_020 and feature_024 negative; age (feature_016)
showed no signal.

Iteration 4 (full multivariable). Joint logistic identified the same set as the strongest
adjusted predictors: feature_008 (+1.20), feature_013 (-1.13), feature_015 (-0.69),
feature_021 (-0.74), feature_001 (-0.33/level), feature_027 (-0.43), with smaller adjusted
contributions from feature_020 (-0.036), feature_002 (+0.13), feature_022 (-0.001), feature_024
(-0.007). No other feature was significant after adjustment. Pseudo-R^2 = 0.124.

Iteration 5 (correlation structure). The five candidate response-modifying binaries were nearly
independent of each other (all chi-square p > 0.05 except feature_008-feature_027 at p = 0.07)
and balanced across feature_001 levels. The exception: feature_013 was highly correlated with
feature_022 (median feature_022 = 7.4 when feature_013=0 vs 32.9 when feature_013=1). This
suggested feature_013 indexes a high-disease-burden / advanced-disease state, while feature_008
looked like a randomly assigned treatment.

Iterations 6-7 (interaction discovery). The feature_008 x feature_013 interaction was
overwhelmingly significant: feature_008's effect was +44.0 pp when feature_013=0 but only
+0.8 pp when feature_013=1 (interaction coef -1.978, p~0). All-pairs binary x binary
interaction screening then revealed three additional very strong negative interactions of
feature_008 with feature_015 (-1.24, p=1e-93), feature_021 (-1.14, p=3e-42), and feature_027
(-1.28, p=2e-19). Pairwise interactions among the four blockers themselves were positive,
foreshadowing a saturating "any-blocker-zeroes-treatment" structure.

Iterations 8-10 (subgroup definition). Stratified analysis within feature_013=0 & feature_008=1
showed response rates collapsing dramatically when any blocker was added: feature_015=1 -> 18%
(vs 72%); feature_021=1 -> 15% (vs 66%); feature_027=1 -> 19% (vs 62%). Within the four-way
favorable subgroup, the feature_008 risk difference was +62.6 pp; outside, +0.8 pp.

Iteration 11 (data-driven CATE tree). A T-learner-style decision tree fitted to the estimated
individual treatment effect of feature_008 split first on feature_013, then feature_015,
feature_021, feature_027 (importances 0.73 / 0.18 / 0.08 / 0.006); no other feature carried
meaningful importance. This independently confirms the same four-feature subgroup.

Iteration 12 (continuous modifiers within favorable). No continuous biomarker (feature_022,
feature_002, feature_020, feature_024, feature_016/age, feature_026, feature_009, feature_029,
feature_003, feature_012, feature_025, feature_028, feature_007, feature_014, feature_032,
feature_018, feature_031) interacted significantly with feature_008 within the favorable
subgroup (smallest p = 0.058). Continuous biomarkers do not further fine-tune who responds
inside the favorable subgroup.

Iteration 13 (predictive vs prognostic). Within feature_008=0 (no treatment), feature_013/015/
021/027 had nearly null effects on response (|differences| <= 2.6 pp). They become hugely
informative only in the presence of feature_008 -- they are predictive (treatment-effect
modifiers), not prognostic in their own right. By contrast, feature_001 was prognostic in BOTH
arms (~5 pp gradient per level in untreated; similar prognostic effect in treated favorable
patients), and lacked a strong treatment interaction.

Iteration 14 (joint multivariable model). A single logistic with feature_008 main effect plus
feature_008 interactions with the four blockers and feature_001 confirmed: feature_008 main
+2.57; feature_008 x feature_013 -2.22; x feature_015 -1.63; x feature_021 -1.55; x feature_027
-1.66; x feature_001 +0.045 (NS).

Iterations 15-16 (saturation structure). Treatment effect by number of blockers: 0 -> +62.6 pp;
1 -> +0.9 pp; 2 -> +0.8 pp; >=3 -> ~0. Three-way interactions feature_008 x blocker_a x
blocker_b are large and positive (+1 to +2.5 logit), reflecting the saturating "any-blocker
zeroes the effect" pattern -- consistent with a logical AND of (no blocker present) being
required.

Iterations 17-18 (sensitivity). Within feature_013=0, log(feature_022) does NOT further modify
the treatment effect (interaction p=0.72) -- feature_013 absorbs the relevant treatment-
effect-modifier information. A complete scan of every binary feature confirms only four large
modifiers (|coef|>1, p<<1e-15); other binaries have at most tiny effects (smallest other p =
0.023 for feature_011, coef only -0.106).

Iteration 19 (continuous prognostic predictors among controls). Among feature_008=0 patients,
the significant continuous prognostic predictors are feature_020 (negative; coef=-0.037,
p=4e-18), feature_022 (negative; coef=-0.0013, p=1e-8), feature_024 (negative; coef=-0.009,
p=5e-6), and feature_002 (positive; coef=+0.148, p=3e-6). Other continuous features (feature_
016/age, 009, 029, 003, 012, 025, 028, 007, 014, 032, 018, 031, 026) showed no detectable
prognostic association.

Iteration 20 (feature_001 inside favorable subgroup). feature_001 acts purely as a prognostic
modifier within the favorable subgroup, dampening the absolute treatment effect modestly
(+64.6 / +62.2 / +59.0 pp at levels 0/1/2) but never eliminating it. Treatment benefit is
preserved at every level of feature_001.

Iteration 21 (adjusted treatment effect). Within the favorable subgroup, after adjusting for
feature_001, feature_022, feature_020, feature_024, feature_002, and feature_016, the
adjusted feature_008 logit coefficient is +3.019 (OR 20.5, 95% CI ~18.8-22.4, p~0). The
treatment effect is highly robust to adjustment for all observed prognostic factors.

Iteration 22 (feature_013 vs PSA quintiles). feature_013=1 prevalence by feature_022 quintile:
17.5% / 33.2% / 53.0% / 76.3% / 94.8%. feature_013 maps onto -- but is not identical to -- a
high-PSA state, supporting its interpretation as a binarized advanced-burden / metastatic
indicator that carries information beyond a continuous PSA cut.

Iteration 23 (treatment-receipt regression). No covariate predicts feature_008 receipt at p <
0.01; smallest p is 0.036 with a tiny coefficient. This is the signature of a randomly-
assigned treatment indicator and supports a causal reading of the subgroup-conditional effect.

Iteration 24 (mechanism for blockers). feature_013 is strongly predicted by feature_022
(coef +0.061, p~0); feature_015 has only weak continuous correlates; feature_021 and
feature_027 have essentially no predictors. The four blockers are largely orthogonal to other
recorded features and to one another.

Iteration 25 (final consolidated hypotheses).

  Predictive (treatment-effect heterogeneity for objective_response):
    Treatment: feature_008 = 1 (vs 0).
    Direction: positive on objective_response.
    Subgroup: feature_013 = 0 AND feature_015 = 0 AND feature_021 = 0 AND feature_027 = 0.
    In-subgroup risk difference: +62.6 pp (17.2% -> 79.8%); adjusted OR ~20.5.
    Out-of-subgroup risk difference: +0.8 pp (15.3% -> 16.1%); essentially null.
    Each of feature_013, feature_015, feature_021, feature_027 individually abolishes the
    treatment effect; the favorable subgroup definition requires all four to be zero.

  Prognostic (independent of treatment):
    Higher feature_001 (ordinal), feature_022 (PSA-like), feature_020, and feature_024 are
    associated with lower objective_response. Higher feature_002 (albumin-like) is associated
    with higher objective_response. These are prognostic main effects -- they do not modify
    the feature_008 treatment effect within the favorable subgroup.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
Supported: large positive feature_008 main effect; large negative main effects for feature_013,
feature_015, feature_021, feature_027; ordinal feature_001 prognostic gradient; feature_022
strong continuous prognostic; feature_002, feature_020, feature_024 prognostic; the four-way
favorable subgroup definition for treatment heterogeneity; saturation/any-blocker-zeroes
structure; feature_008 randomization; feature_013 as a binarized high-burden/PSA indicator.

Refuted / null: feature_010 (Gleason-like) prognostic alone; age (feature_016) prognostic;
feature_006/011/017/019/023/004/005 as treatment-effect modifiers; continuous biomarkers as
treatment-effect modifiers within the favorable subgroup; feature_022 as an additional
modifier beyond feature_013.

Overall conclusion
------------------
This cohort behaves as if feature_008 is a highly active therapy with a spectacular response
benefit (objective response rises from ~17% to ~80%) in patients without any of four
"resistance" / advanced-disease markers (feature_013, feature_015, feature_021, feature_027),
and is essentially inert when any one of those four markers is present. The conventional
prognostic markers in this dataset (feature_001 stage/PS, feature_022 PSA-like burden,
feature_020 symptom-like score, feature_024, feature_002 albumin-like) act as main effects
on outcome but do NOT modify the treatment benefit within the responsive subgroup.
Identification of the four-feature subgroup is therefore the single most actionable insight
from the dataset, and it is robust to both adjusted modeling and a data-driven CATE tree.
