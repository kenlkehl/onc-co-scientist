ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_prostate (n = 50,000; outcome = objective_response, overall ORR 24.0%)
============================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The cohort is all-male (sex_female=0 for all rows) prostate cancer; 55% mCRPC, 20% with
visceral metastases, 10% BRCA2 mutated, 20% AR-V7 positive, 3% MSI-high, 60% PSMA-high.
Six treatments are recorded as binary indicators; treatment groups overlap (a patient may
be on multiple agents). The single outcome is objective_response (binary).

I worked through 25 iterations: 5 iterations of main-effect screens (treatments,
biomarkers, clinical features, labs, inflammatory markers), one fully adjusted
multivariable model, followed by a systematic treatment-effect heterogeneity search and
several rounds of subgroup refinement and confirmation.

MAIN-EFFECT FINDINGS (iterations 1–6)
-------------------------------------
Treatments (univariate):
  treatment_enzalutamide   ORR 36.1% on vs 15.9% off, OR 2.99, p<1e-300  ***
  treatment_abiraterone    ORR 23.8% vs 24.1%, p=0.46                       ns
  treatment_docetaxel      ORR 24.0% vs 24.0%, p=0.91                       ns
  treatment_olaparib       ORR 23.9% vs 24.0%, p=0.87                       ns
  treatment_lu177_psma     ORR 24.3% vs 24.0%, p=0.59                       ns
  treatment_pembrolizumab  ORR 23.9% vs 24.0%, p=0.86                       ns

Biomarkers (univariate):
  brca2_mutation positive lowers ORR by 10.0 pp (p=5e-56)
  ar_v7_positive lowers ORR by 10.0 pp (p=2e-98)
  msi_high lowers ORR by 6.6 pp (p=3e-9)
  psma_high — no main effect on ORR (p=0.31)

Clinical and lab main effects (univariate):
  mcrpc=1 lowers ORR by 19.2 pp (34.6% -> 15.4%, p<1e-300)
  ecog_ps lowers ORR by ~5 pp per unit (p=1e-72)
  log10(psa) lowers ORR (OR 0.53 per log unit, p=2e-239)
  albumin_g_dl raises ORR (OR 1.10 per g/dL, p=4e-6)
  weight_loss_pct_6mo lowers ORR (OR 0.97 per pct, p=3e-29)
  crp_mg_l lowers ORR (OR 0.99 per mg/L, p=6e-8)
  age, gleason, visceral_mets, ldh, alp, hemoglobin, nlr, and routine chemistries:
    all null (p > 0.13).

Fully adjusted multivariable logistic model: only enzalutamide retained a treatment
effect; abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177_psma, pembrolizumab all had adjusted
ORs of 0.97–1.03 (none significant). All four resistance markers (mcrpc, brca2,
ar_v7, msi_high) and the unfavorable prognostic markers (ecog, log_psa, weight_loss,
crp) retained independent associations; albumin remained protective.

HETEROGENEITY SEARCH (iterations 7–9, 12–13, 16, 20)
----------------------------------------------------
Treatment-effect heterogeneity for objective_response was probed via:
  • Treatment × biomarker (4 markers × 6 treatments = 24 interactions)
  • Treatment × clinical (mcrpc, visceral, ecog × 6 treatments = 18)
  • Treatment × continuous lab (7 labs × 6 treatments = 42)
  • Treatment × full feature set (25 features × 6 treatments = 150 interactions)
  • Exhaustive 22-subgroup search for each non-enzalutamide treatment (110 tests)
  • Within-favorable interaction screen (13 features)

Findings:
  1. Enzalutamide effect modifiers — large, independent, and replicable:
     • mcrpc=1 abolishes the effect (interaction logOR -1.98, p<1e-300)
     • ar_v7_positive=1 abolishes the effect (interaction logOR -1.24, p=1e-93)
     • brca2_mutation=1 abolishes the effect (interaction logOR -1.14, p=3e-42)
     • msi_high=1 abolishes the effect (interaction logOR -1.28, p=2e-19)
     A naive log_psa interaction (p=2e-127) disappears entirely (p=0.72) when
     conditioning on mcrpc=0 — it was confounding by mCRPC, not a genuine modifier.

  2. Cascade of restrictions (iter 10):
       all-comers           +20.2 pp (ORR 36.1% on vs 15.9% off)
       non-mCRPC            +44.0 pp (61.0% vs 17.0%)
       + AR-V7-             +54.6 pp (71.7% vs 17.1%)
       + BRCA2 WT           +60.7 pp (78.0% vs 17.3%)
       + MSI stable         +62.6 pp (79.8% vs 17.2%)

  3. Knockout test (iter 14): keeping the other three conditions favorable and
     flipping each one in turn collapses the enza effect:
       knock-in mCRPC: diff +0.7 pp (p=0.17)
       knock-in AR-V7+: diff +2.1 pp (p=0.09)
       knock-in BRCA2+: diff +0.4 pp (p=0.88)
       knock-in MSI-high: diff -2.5 pp (p=0.58)
     All four conditions are necessary; none is redundant.

  4. Composite favorable indicator (iter 19): 31.4% of the cohort meets all four
     favorable conditions; the favorable × enzalutamide interaction has logOR +2.89
     (OR 17.9), p<1e-300. Within favorable, enzalutamide OR = 19.0 (p<1e-300);
     within unfavorable, enzalutamide OR = 1.06 (p=0.054, essentially null).

  5. Other treatments (iters 7, 11, 16, 20):
     • olaparib in BRCA2+ patients: ORR is LOWER on olaparib than off (12.1% vs 15.3%);
       interaction p=0.045 in the wrong direction. No subgroup makes olaparib helpful.
     • pembrolizumab in MSI-high: only 79 patients overlap; ORR 17.7% vs 17.6% off,
       no signal. The MSI-high non-mCRPC slice (n=34) is +10 pp but unstable.
     • lu177_psma in PSMA-high: ORR 23.8% vs 23.9% off, no benefit.
     • abiraterone, docetaxel: no subgroup effect anywhere.
     The exhaustive 22-subgroup search for the five non-enzalutamide treatments
     produced zero subgroups with a >=5 pp ORR increase on treatment. The few
     interactions reaching p<0.05 (5 of ~150) are scattered, small in magnitude,
     directionally inconsistent, and do not survive Bonferroni (alpha = 4e-4).

  6. Robustness within favorable subgroup (iter 23): the +63 pp enzalutamide effect
     is consistent across ECOG strata (+65, +62, +59 pp for ECOG 0/1/2), visceral
     mets strata (+63, +62 pp), and PSA tertiles (+64, +62, +63 pp). The within-
     favorable single-feature interaction screen (iter 22) produced one nominal hit
     (psma_high logOR -0.25, p=0.004) which is small in magnitude and does not
     materially attenuate the +63 pp effect.

CONCLUSIONS
-----------
The single dominant treatment-effect signal in this cohort is:

   FINAL HYPOTHESIS (h25_final_subgroup):
     treatment_enzalutamide produces a large positive objective_response benefit
     (risk-difference +63 pp, ORR ~80% on vs ~17% off, OR ~19) confined to the
     subgroup defined SIMULTANEOUSLY by:
        mcrpc = 0  AND  ar_v7_positive = 0  AND
        brca2_mutation = 0  AND  msi_high = 0
     Outside this subgroup, the enzalutamide effect is null
     (OR 1.06, p=0.054). Each of the four conditions is necessary —
     knocking in any single unfavorable marker (mCRPC, AR-V7+,
     BRCA2-mutated, or MSI-high) collapses the treatment effect to
     within ~2 pp of zero.

No other treatment in the panel (abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177_psma,
pembrolizumab) shows any clinically or statistically meaningful effect on
objective_response, in the cohort overall or in any single- or pairwise-defined
subgroup tested. Notably, the canonical clinical heterogeneity hypotheses
(olaparib in BRCA2-mutated disease, pembrolizumab in MSI-high disease, and
lu177-PSMA in PSMA-high disease) are not supported in this cohort.

Independent prognostic features for objective_response (in the absence of
treatment effect modification) include: lower ecog_ps, lower log10(psa),
higher albumin_g_dl, lower weight_loss_pct_6mo, lower crp_mg_l, and
mcrpc=0 status. Age, gleason_score, visceral_mets, ldh, alkaline phosphatase,
hemoglobin, nlr, and routine chemistry labs were not associated with response.
