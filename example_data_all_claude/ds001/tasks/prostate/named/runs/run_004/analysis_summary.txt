ds001_prostate — Analysis Summary
==================================

Cohort: 50,000 prostate-cancer patients (all male). Outcome: objective_response (binary; overall rate 24.0%). Six treatments are coded: enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA, pembrolizumab. Patients can receive multiple treatments simultaneously.

OVERALL HEADLINE
================
Only one treatment in this dataset shows any clinically meaningful efficacy signal: enzalutamide. Its effect is enormous but is concentrated entirely in a single, sharply defined subgroup. Outside that subgroup, all six treatments are statistically and practically null.

The enzalutamide-responsive subgroup is:

    mcrpc = 0  AND  ar_v7_positive = 0  AND  brca2_mutation = 0  AND  msi_high = 0

Within that subgroup (n=15,681; 31.4% of cohort) the objective response rate is 0.798 on enzalutamide vs 0.172 off enzalutamide — an absolute difference of +0.626 (chi-square p < 1e-300; logistic interaction term enza:favorable = +2.89, z = 56).

Outside the subgroup (n=34,319) the enzalutamide effect is +0.008 absolute (p = 0.056), i.e. clinically zero.

ITERATION-BY-ITERATION NARRATIVE
================================

Iter 1 — Treatment main effects.
A simple stratified comparison of OR rate by each treatment indicator found a single positive signal: enzalutamide (0.36 vs 0.16, +0.20 absolute, p~0). Abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA, and pembrolizumab all had differences <0.01 with non-significant p-values. This already foreshadowed the asymmetric finding.

Iter 2 — Disease/clinical prognostic features.
Stratified and univariate logistic models on objective_response: mcrpc was the strongest unfavorable factor (-0.193 absolute, p~0). ECOG worsened OR monotonically (logit -0.285/unit, p~1e-72). Visceral mets, age, and gleason score showed no main effect.

Iter 3 — Genomic biomarkers.
BRCA2-mutated patients had OR 0.150 vs 0.250 (-0.100 absolute, p<1e-55). AR-V7-positive patients had OR 0.160 vs 0.260 (-0.100, p<1e-97). MSI-high patients had OR 0.176 vs 0.242 (-0.066, p<1e-8). PSMA-high status was not associated with OR. This was already suggestive: the same three biomarkers (BRCA2/AR-V7/MSI) that act as resistance markers for hormone therapy also predict overall worse outcome.

Iter 4 — Soft-tissue / inflammation labs.
Albumin: positive (logit +0.096/g/dL, p<1e-5). PSA: strongly negative (-0.0047/ng/mL, p<1e-93). CRP: -0.0069/mg/L (p<1e-7). Weight loss: -0.031/%-pt (p<1e-28). NLR, hemoglobin, and LDH did NOT reach significance — atypical relative to clinical priors but consistent with this cohort's encoding.

Iter 5 — Routine chemistry panel.
Alkaline phosphatase, AST, ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium were all univariately non-significant (all p > 0.13).

Iter 6 — Enzalutamide × AR-V7 interaction.
Strong negative interaction: coef = -1.27 (p ~ 1e-93). Stratified absolute treatment effects: +0.251 in AR-V7-, +0.008 in AR-V7+. Classic AR-V7 resistance pattern.

Iter 7 — Enzalutamide × BRCA2 and × MSI interactions.
Both negative (BRCA2: coef -1.14, p ~ 3e-42; MSI: coef -1.28, p ~ 2e-19). Within BRCA2- the enza effect is +0.225 absolute; within BRCA2+ it is +0.006. Within MSI-low +0.209; within MSI-high -0.022. Enzalutamide benefit is qualitatively absent whenever any of AR-V7, BRCA2, or MSI is positive.

Iter 8 — Expected biomarker effects of the targeted therapies (PRIORS REFUTED).
Despite well-established clinical mechanisms, this dataset does NOT encode positive interactions for the targeted drugs:
  • Olaparib × BRCA2: interaction coef -0.289, p = 0.045 — directionally OPPOSITE of expected (slight harm signal).
  • Pembrolizumab × MSI-high: coef +0.017, p = 0.96 — null.
  • Lu177-PSMA × PSMA-high: coef -0.054, p = 0.36 — null.
  • Abiraterone × AR-V7: coef +0.063, p = 0.33 — null (no main effect, no AR-V7 modification).

Iter 9 — Canonical-indication subgroups.
Within BRCA2+/mcrpc=1 (n=2,724), olaparib produced 0.111 vs 0.152 OR rate (diff -0.041, p=0.07). Within MSI+/mcrpc=1 (n=839), pembrolizumab produced 0.089 vs 0.161 (diff -0.072, p=0.28). Within PSMA-high/mcrpc=1 (n=16,405), Lu177-PSMA produced 0.159 vs 0.150 (+0.009, NS). Within mcrpc=1, docetaxel +/- arms had identical OR rates. None of the targeted/cytotoxic agents showed efficacy in their canonical indication subgroups.

Iter 10 — Multivariable logistic regression (no interactions).
Adjusted estimates (n=50,000, pseudo-R² = 0.124): enzalutamide +1.20 (z=53). All other treatments NS. Significant covariates: mcrpc -1.13, ECOG -0.33, BRCA2 -0.74, AR-V7 -0.69, MSI-high -0.43, PSA -0.001, albumin +0.13, weight loss -0.036, CRP -0.0073. Visceral mets, age, gleason, LDH, NLR, hemoglobin, alkaline phosphatase, AST, ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium not significant.

Iter 11 — Systematic enzalutamide × feature interaction screen.
Across 25 candidate modifiers, the strongest interaction with enzalutamide was mcrpc (coef -1.98, p ≈ 0), exceeding even the AR-V7, BRCA2, and MSI interactions. PSA's interaction (coef -0.0087, p≈1e-72) was substantial but largely captured collinearity with mcrpc.

Iter 12 — Enzalutamide effect by mcrpc strata.
Within mcrpc=0 (hormone-sensitive prostate cancer, n=22,519): enza+ rate 0.610 vs enza- 0.169, diff +0.440. Within mcrpc=1 (CRPC, n=27,481): enza+ 0.158 vs enza- 0.151, diff +0.008. Enzalutamide effect is concentrated entirely in non-CRPC patients in this dataset.

Iter 13 — Joint enzalutamide × (mcrpc, AR-V7, BRCA2, MSI) model.
Joint logistic regression with all four interactions: enza main effect = +2.58 (in fully favorable cell), and all four enza-by-modifier interactions strongly negative and statistically significant. Pseudo-R² rose from 0.067 (main-effects-only enza model) to 0.166 with the four interactions. Each modifier contributed independently — they were not redundant.

Iter 14 — Combined favorable_for_enza indicator.
Defined favorable_for_enza := (mcrpc=0) AND (ar_v7_positive=0) AND (brca2_mutation=0) AND (msi_high=0). Covered 31.4% of cohort. Inside: enza absolute effect +0.626 (p<1e-300). Outside: +0.008 (p=0.056). Clean qualitative separation.

Iter 15 — Full 4-cell stratification.
Confirmed that the enzalutamide treatment effect is concentrated in exactly one of four cells of (mcrpc × any-resistance-marker). The other three cells all have absolute effects ≤+0.01.

Iter 16 — ECOG within favorable subgroup.
The enzalutamide benefit is preserved across ECOG levels: +0.65 at ECOG 0, +0.62 at ECOG 1, +0.59 at ECOG 2. Modest attenuation but no qualitative change. ECOG is therefore a quantitative effect modifier, not a binary qualifier for the subgroup.

Iter 17 — Continuous PSA as additional modifier.
Even after adjusting for mcrpc and the three resistance markers, log(1+PSA) further attenuates the enzalutamide effect (interaction coef -0.474, p<1e-100). PSA captures additional disease-burden information that is not fully explained by mcrpc status.

Iter 18 — Enza × Abi combination.
No synergy detected: enza-/abi- 0.160; enza-/abi+ 0.157; enza+/abi- 0.363; enza+/abi+ 0.357. Adding abiraterone provides no incremental benefit on top of enzalutamide.

Iter 19 — Systematic non-enzalutamide treatment × feature interaction screen.
For each of the five other treatments, tested interactions with all 25 candidate features (125 tests). Best p-values: abiraterone × BRCA2 p=0.031, docetaxel × sodium p=0.023, olaparib × BUN p=0.031, lu177 × visceral_mets p=0.010, pembrolizumab × gleason p=0.033. None survives Bonferroni correction (threshold 4×10⁻⁴). No reliable subgroup of efficacy for these five drugs.

Iter 20 — Other candidate enzalutamide modifiers.
Visceral mets, albumin, and PSMA-high status do not modify the enzalutamide effect in any practically meaningful way (interaction p-values 0.86, 0.058, 0.40 respectively).

Iter 21 — Negative-control consolidation for non-enzalutamide drugs.
Combining (a) main-effect estimates, (b) canonical-indication subgroup tests, and (c) two-way interaction screens, no treatment other than enzalutamide produces a clinically meaningful (≥0.05 absolute) statistically supported subgroup of efficacy in this cohort.

Iter 22 — Docetaxel subgroup verification.
Across every binary stratum (BRCA2, AR-V7, MSI, PSMA, mcrpc, visceral mets), docetaxel-treated and untreated arms differ by <0.02 absolute. No subgroup of docetaxel benefit identified.

Iter 23 — Continuous-disease-burden adjustment.
Within a model that adjusts for ECOG, visceral mets, albumin, CRP, weight loss, and log(PSA), the enza:favorable_for_enza interaction term remains +2.95 (p<1e-300). The favorable-subgroup definition is robust to adjustment for disease-burden severity markers.

Iter 24 — Search for additional necessary predicates.
No additional variable (visceral_mets, ECOG strata, age tertiles, hemoglobin, NLR, LDH, alk-phos, AST, ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium) qualitatively eliminates the +0.6 enzalutamide effect within (mcrpc=0, ar_v7-, brca2-, msi-). The 4-way subgroup definition is therefore the complete predicate.

Iter 25 — Final consolidated hypothesis (re-confirmed).
Treatment_enzalutamide raises objective_response from 0.172 to 0.798 (absolute +0.626) in patients with mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND msi_high=0, and produces no detectable benefit anywhere else (≈+0.008 absolute, p≈0.06). The unfavorable values that suppress the treatment effect are mcrpc=1, ar_v7_positive=1, brca2_mutation=1, and msi_high=1; ANY of these is sufficient to abolish enzalutamide's benefit.

KEY CONCLUSIONS
===============

1. PROGNOSTIC VARIABLES (associated with worse objective_response):
   mcrpc (largest), AR-V7+, BRCA2-mutated, MSI-high, higher ECOG, higher PSA, lower albumin, higher CRP, greater weight loss. Visceral mets, age, gleason, LDH, NLR, hemoglobin, and routine chemistry labs were NOT significantly prognostic in this cohort.

2. ENZALUTAMIDE EFFECT (the only positive treatment signal):
   • Main effect: +0.20 absolute (0.36 vs 0.16) overall.
   • Effect modifiers (each independently negative): mcrpc, AR-V7+, BRCA2 mutation, MSI-high.
   • Final enzalutamide-responsive subgroup: mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND msi_high=0.
     Within this subgroup the enza effect is +0.626 absolute (0.798 vs 0.172).
   • Outside this subgroup the enza effect is +0.008 absolute (essentially zero).
   • PSA and ECOG further quantitatively attenuate the effect within the favorable subgroup, but do not eliminate it.
   • Adding abiraterone to enzalutamide does not improve OR.

3. OTHER TREATMENTS:
   Abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, Lu177-PSMA, and pembrolizumab show no significant main effect, no positive subgroup, and no biomarker-driven heterogeneity surviving multiple-testing correction. Notably, the canonical biomarker indications expected from external clinical knowledge (olaparib in BRCA2+, pembrolizumab in MSI-high, Lu177-PSMA in PSMA-high) DID NOT replicate in this dataset; in some cases the directional signal was even mildly the opposite.

4. CLINICALLY MEANINGFUL TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY:
   The enzalutamide × (mcrpc, AR-V7, BRCA2, MSI) heterogeneity is one of the cleanest qualitative interactions identifiable: a 4-predicate subgroup that captures essentially the entire treatment effect. No comparable heterogeneity exists for any other drug in this cohort.
