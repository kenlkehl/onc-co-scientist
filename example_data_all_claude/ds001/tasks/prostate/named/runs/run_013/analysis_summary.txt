Analysis summary — ds001_prostate (n=50,000 patients; objective_response rate 24.0%)
=====================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
This dataset contains 50,000 prostate cancer patients (all male, as expected) with six binary treatment indicators (enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177_psma, pembrolizumab), a panel of biomarkers (mcrpc, brca2_mutation, ar_v7_positive, msi_high, psma_high, visceral_mets), routine labs, and a single binary outcome (objective_response). Each patient may receive multiple treatments (median 1, max 6 simultaneously). Across 18 iterations I evaluated treatment main effects, biomarker main effects, and treatment-by-feature interactions (binary + continuous), and ran a systematic heterogeneity search for each treatment.

TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS
----------------------
Of the six treatments, only enzalutamide showed a substantial main effect on objective_response: 36.14% on enz vs 15.89% off, absolute difference +20.24 pp (chi-square p<1e-300). The other five treatments showed essentially null main effects with all p>0.4: abiraterone -0.31 pp; docetaxel -0.05 pp; olaparib -0.10 pp; lu177_psma +0.29 pp; pembrolizumab -0.16 pp. After multivariable adjustment for all baseline features, enzalutamide retained a strongly significant coefficient (logit +1.20, p<1e-300) while every other treatment had coefficients near 0 with p>0.3.

BASELINE FEATURE PREDICTORS OF RESPONSE
---------------------------------------
Strong negative predictors of objective_response: mcrpc=1 (15.35% vs 34.60%, diff -19.25 pp, p<1e-300), ar_v7_positive=1 (15.99% vs 26.04%, diff -10.05 pp, p=1.9e-98), brca2_mutation=1 (14.97% vs 25.03%, diff -10.05 pp, p=5.4e-56), and msi_high=1 (17.60% vs 24.22%, diff -6.62 pp, p=3.0e-9). Higher psa_ng_ml (logistic coef -0.0047, p=1.6e-94), higher ecog_ps (-0.285 per unit, p=9.5e-73), higher weight_loss_pct_6mo (-0.032 per pp, p=3.4e-29), and higher crp_mg_l (-0.0069, p=5.6e-8) were also negative predictors. Higher albumin_g_dl was a weak positive predictor (+0.096, p=4.4e-6). Notably null predictors included age_years, gleason_score, ldh_u_l, hemoglobin_g_dl, nlr, alkaline_phosphatase_u_l, calcium_mg_dl, total_bilirubin_mg_dl, sodium_meq_l, potassium_meq_l, creatinine_mg_dl, bun_mg_dl, ast_u_l, alt_u_l, psma_high, and visceral_mets.

THE CENTRAL HETEROGENEITY FINDING
---------------------------------
The enzalutamide effect is dramatically heterogeneous and is fully explained by four binary "resistance" markers. Stratified analyses showed that each of mcrpc, ar_v7_positive, brca2_mutation, and msi_high independently nearly abolishes the enzalutamide benefit:

    - Within mcrpc=0: enz_on 60.96% vs enz_off 16.93%, diff +44.03 pp;
      Within mcrpc=1: enz_on 15.81% vs enz_off 15.05%, diff +0.77 pp.
    - Within ar_v7_positive=0: diff +25.10 pp; within ar_v7_positive=1: diff +0.85 pp.
    - Within brca2_mutation=0: diff +22.46 pp; within brca2_mutation=1: diff +0.63 pp.
    - Within msi_high=0: diff +20.92 pp; within msi_high=1: diff -2.21 pp.

Formal logistic interaction tests confirmed all four are large and highly significant (enz x mcrpc -1.978, p<1e-300; enz x ar_v7 -1.237, p=1.4e-93; enz x brca2 -1.138, p=3.3e-42; enz x msi -1.283, p=1.8e-19). In a joint model with all four interaction terms, the enzalutamide main coefficient is +2.58 (in patients with all four markers absent) and each individual interaction is large enough to wipe it out.

THE COMPLETE RESPONSIVE SUBGROUP
---------------------------------
Defining "responsive" as mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND msi_high=0 (n=15,681; 31.4% of cohort), enzalutamide produces an enormous response shift: 79.78% on (n=6,325; 95% CI 78.77-80.76) versus 17.21% off (n=9,356; 95% CI 16.45-17.99), an absolute difference of +62.57 pp. Within the complementary "non-responsive" subgroup (any of the four markers present, n=34,319), enzalutamide produces a clinically null effect (+0.77 pp). Within the responsive subgroup, the enzalutamide effect is uniform across ECOG strata (ECOG0 +64.55, ECOG1 +62.19, ECOG2 +58.97 pp) and across PSA tertiles (low +63.45, mid +61.74, high +62.48 pp), confirming that the apparent enz x psa interaction observed in the full cohort (-0.81 logit per SD, p=1.2e-72) is mediated by mcrpc status (residual enz x psa interaction p=0.52 after adjusting for enz x mcrpc).

OTHER TREATMENTS — HETEROGENEITY SEARCH
---------------------------------------
For each of the remaining five treatments, I ran (a) interaction tests with all six binary biomarkers, (b) interaction tests with 19 continuous baseline features, and (c) an exhaustive single-feature subgroup search (binary + quartile splits). Findings:

    - Olaparib in brca2_mutation=1 (the biologically expected enriched subgroup): diff -3.21 pp (p=0.034) — direction is OPPOSITE to clinical expectation; effect size is small. Best single-feature subgroup: sodium_meq_l>142 diff +4.68 pp (p=0.0015) — likely chance among many tests.
    - Pembrolizumab in msi_high=1: diff +0.12 pp (p=0.96). The biologically expected MSI-high enrichment is absent. Best single-feature subgroup: albumin_g_dl>4.10 diff +4.18 pp (p=0.032) — likely chance.
    - Lu177_psma in psma_high=1: diff -0.11 pp (p>0.5). The biologically expected PSMA-high enrichment is absent. Best single-feature subgroup: gleason_score=10 diff +3.35 pp (p=0.17). Lu177-PSMA shows a small negative interaction with visceral_mets (-0.19 logit, p=0.010).
    - Abiraterone: best subgroup brca2_mutation=1 diff +2.09 pp (p=0.062). No coherent positive subgroup.
    - Docetaxel: best subgroup gleason_score=10 diff +3.01 pp (p=0.12) and alkaline_phosphatase>128 diff +1.89 pp (p=0.025); all marginal.

No continuous-feature interaction reached p<0.01 for any of these five treatments. With dozens of tests examined, none of the marginal findings is robust to multiple-testing concerns and none reaches a clinically meaningful magnitude (>=5 pp). I conclude that this cohort does not contain an identifiable subgroup with a robust positive treatment effect for any of abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177_psma, or pembrolizumab.

HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED VS. REFUTED
--------------------------------
SUPPORTED:
    - Enzalutamide raises objective_response (h1.1).
    - mcrpc, brca2_mutation, ar_v7_positive, msi_high each lower response (h2.1-h2.4).
    - Higher ecog_ps, psa_ng_ml, weight_loss_pct_6mo, crp_mg_l lower response; higher albumin_g_dl raises it (h3.1, h3.2, h3.5, h4.1, h4.2).
    - The enzalutamide effect is concentrated in mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND msi_high=0 (h10.1, h17.1, h18.1).
    - Each of the four resistance markers independently abolishes the enzalutamide benefit (h5.1, h6.1, h7.1, h8.1).
    - Within the responsive subgroup the enzalutamide effect is uniform across ECOG and PSA (h12.1, h12.2).
    - The apparent enz x psa interaction is fully confounded by mcrpc (h13.2).

REFUTED / NOT SUPPORTED:
    - Abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177_psma, pembrolizumab have no main effect on response (h1.2-h1.6).
    - Olaparib does NOT preferentially help brca2_mutation=1 patients (h7.2 — small effect in opposite direction).
    - Pembrolizumab does NOT preferentially help msi_high patients (h8.2).
    - Lu177-PSMA does NOT preferentially help psma_high patients (h9.1).
    - psma_high and visceral_mets have no main effect on response (h2.5, h2.6).
    - Age, gleason, LDH, hemoglobin, NLR, alkaline phosphatase have no main effect (h3.3, h3.4, h4.3-h4.6).

FINAL BEST-SUPPORTED TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS
--------------------------------------------------------
Treatment: enzalutamide
Outcome: objective_response (binary)
Direction: positive (treatment_enzalutamide=1 raises objective_response)
Subgroup definition (all four predicates required, since each one alone abolishes the effect):
    mcrpc = 0  AND  ar_v7_positive = 0  AND  brca2_mutation = 0  AND  msi_high = 0
Effect size in subgroup: response 79.78% on enzalutamide vs 17.21% off; absolute difference +62.57 percentage points (p<1e-300).
Effect outside subgroup: +0.77 pp (clinically null).

For each of the other five treatments, no subgroup with a clinically meaningful and statistically robust positive effect on objective_response was identified, including the biologically expected biomarker-matched subgroups.
