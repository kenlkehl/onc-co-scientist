ds001_prostate — Analysis Summary
==================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients (all male, sex_female=0). Outcome: objective_response (overall rate
24.0%). Six available treatments (binary, often given in combination): enzalutamide (40%),
abiraterone (30%), docetaxel (30%), olaparib (10%), lu177-PSMA (15%), pembrolizumab (5%).

Across 25 iterations I (i) characterized main effects of every feature and treatment,
(ii) ran a multivariable logistic adjusted model, (iii) tested the four most clinically
expected predictive biomarker × treatment interactions, (iv) ran a systematic 6-treatment
× 7-modifier interaction screen, (v) drilled into the only treatment with substantial
heterogeneity (enzalutamide), (vi) verified the resulting subgroup with exhaustive
2- and 3-feature subgroup searches and a T-learner / decision-tree heterogeneity
analysis, and (vii) confirmed by single-component "leave-one-out" necessity checks.

1. Main effects
---------------
Treatments. Only treatment_enzalutamide had a significant marginal effect on
objective_response: rate 36.1% on enzalutamide vs 15.9% off (Δ = +20.2%, chi² p ≈ 0).
The other five treatments had differences of |Δ| < 0.005 with p > 0.4
(abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA, pembrolizumab).

Clinical / biomarker features (all significant unless noted):
  mcrpc=1: response 15.4% vs 34.6% (Δ = -19.3%, p ≈ 0).
  brca2_mutation=1: 15.0% vs 25.0% (Δ = -10.1%, p = 5.4e-56).
  ar_v7_positive=1: 16.0% vs 26.0% (Δ = -10.1%, p = 1.9e-98).
  msi_high=1: 17.6% vs 24.2% (Δ = -6.6%, p = 3.0e-9).
  ECOG PS 0/1/2: 28.2% / 22.8% / 18.2% (chi² p = 5.4e-72).
  visceral_mets and psma_high: no association (p > 0.3).
  gleason_score: flat across 6–10 (p = 0.77).
Continuous features: higher psa_ng_ml, higher crp_mg_l, and higher weight_loss_pct_6mo are
associated with lower response (p ≤ 1e-8); higher albumin_g_dl is associated with higher
response (p = 4e-6); LDH, NLR, hemoglobin, ALP, AST/ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, and
the electrolytes are not associated with response.

2. Multivariable adjusted model
-------------------------------
A logistic model regressing objective_response on every feature and treatment confirmed
the marginal picture:
  treatment_enzalutamide adjusted log-odds = +1.20 (z = 52.7).
  Other 5 treatments: |coef| ≤ 0.03, p > 0.3.
  Independently negative: mcrpc, ar_v7_positive, brca2_mutation, msi_high, ECOG, log(PSA),
    log(CRP), weight_loss; independently positive: albumin.
  Gleason, age, Hb, LDH, ALP, AST/ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium,
    calcium were not independently associated with response.

3. Predictive biomarker × treatment hypotheses
----------------------------------------------
For each of the four biologically expected predictive pairings:
  (a) olaparib × brca2_mutation: in BRCA2+, olaparib rr = 12.1% vs 15.3% control, Δ = -3.2%
      (p = 0.06). PARP-inhibitor benefit in BRCA2+ NOT observed; trend slightly negative.
  (b) pembrolizumab × msi_high: in MSI-high, pembro rr = 17.7% vs 17.6%, Δ = +0.1%, p ≈ 1.0.
      No PD-1 benefit in MSI-H subgroup.
  (c) lu177_psma × psma_high: in PSMA-high, Δ = -0.1%, p = 0.88. No PSMA-targeted benefit.
  (d) enzalutamide × ar_v7_positive: AR-V7– enzalutamide Δ = +25.1% (p ≈ 0); AR-V7+ Δ = +0.9%
      (p = 0.27). Adjusted interaction = -1.33 (p = 1.4e-101). AR-V7 abolishes the
      enzalutamide benefit, consistent with established biology.
  Abiraterone × ar_v7_positive: no interaction (p = 0.26).

4. Systematic interaction screen and enzalutamide subgroup
----------------------------------------------------------
A 6-treatment × 7-binary-modifier (+ ECOG numeric) interaction screen (42 tests) showed
that ALL strong tx × modifier interactions involve enzalutamide:
  enz × mcrpc            coef -1.98  (p ≈ 0)
  enz × ar_v7_positive   coef -1.24  (p = 1.4e-93)
  enz × brca2_mutation   coef -1.14  (p = 3.3e-42)
  enz × msi_high         coef -1.28  (p = 1.8e-19)
  enz × ECOG (per step)  coef +0.14  (p = 1.6e-5; small magnitude)
No non-enzalutamide interaction survived multiple-testing thresholds.

Stratifying enzalutamide by mcrpc:
  mcrpc=0: enz rr = 61.0% vs 16.9%  (Δ = +44.0%).
  mcrpc=1: enz rr = 15.8% vs 15.1%  (Δ = +0.8%).
Joint AR-V7– AND mcrpc=0 (n = 18,000): Δ = +54.6% (rr 71.7% vs 17.1%).

5. Drilling into the AR-V7–/mCRPC– subgroup
-------------------------------------------
Within AR-V7– AND mcrpc=0:
  brca2_mutation=0: enz Δ = +60.7%; brca2=1: Δ = +0.4% (interaction coef -2.80, p = 6.6e-90).
  msi_high=0: enz Δ = +56.3%; msi_high=1: Δ = -2.2% (interaction coef -2.74, p = 9.6e-34).
  psma_high modulates only modestly (Δ +56.8% vs +53.1%, interaction p = 0.006).
  visceral_mets and ECOG do NOT modify the within-subgroup enzalutamide effect.

Joint subgroup AR-V7=0 AND mcrpc=0 AND brca2=0 AND msi_high=0 (n = 15,681):
  enzalutamide rr = 79.8% vs 17.2%, Δ = +62.6% (p ≈ 0).
  All five other treatments inside this same subgroup show |Δ| ≤ 0.02 — no benefit.

A T-learner gradient-boosting estimator combined with a depth-3 decision-tree summary
of the per-patient enzalutamide ITE selected the same three top splits (mcrpc → ar_v7
→ brca2). Mean estimated ITE in the leaf {mcrpc=0, ar_v7=0, brca2=0} was +0.59; ITE in
all other leaves clustered at 0. Mean ITE in the full 4-negative core subgroup was +0.61.

Necessity / leave-one-out check. Adding back patients who differ from the core only by
one positive marker (other three still negative) showed enzalutamide Δ ≤ +0.02 in every
single such stratum: AR-V7+ added back +0.022 (n_t=1573); mCRPC+ added back +0.007
(n_t=7677); BRCA2+ added back +0.004 (n_t=710); MSI-high added back -0.025 (n_t=189).
Each of the four conditions is independently necessary.

PSA, which appeared to modify the enzalutamide effect on the full sample (interaction
p = 1.2e-72), no longer modifies inside the joint core subgroup (p = 0.34) — i.e. the
PSA × enz interaction was driven by PSA's correlation with mCRPC.

6. Search for non-enzalutamide subgroup effects
------------------------------------------------
Exhaustive enumeration of all 2-feature and 3-feature binary subgroups (with adequate n)
for abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA, and pembrolizumab found no subgroup in
which any of these treatments produced a statistically significant change in
objective_response. Best 3-feature absolute differences ranged from -0.10 to +0.15 with
the smallest p-value of 0.07 — none survives even uncorrected thresholds, let alone the
hundreds of comparisons performed. Within the enzalutamide-responsive 4-negative core
subgroup, adding any of the other five treatments to enzalutamide does not further
increase response (combinations all 77–82% response).

Adjusted analyses confined to each treatment's biologically preferred subgroup
(controlling for the remaining clinical/biomarker features):
  olaparib in BRCA2+:                 coef = -0.27, p = 0.055.
  pembrolizumab in MSI-high:          coef = -0.006, p = 0.99.
  lu177-PSMA in PSMA-high:            coef = -0.007, p = 0.87.
  lu177-PSMA in PSMA-high & mCRPC+:   coef = +0.064, p = 0.29.
  abiraterone in mCRPC+:              coef = +0.005, p = 0.90.
  docetaxel in mCRPC+ & visceral_mets+: coef = +0.045, p = 0.58.

7. Other findings
-----------------
visceral_mets is non-prognostic in this cohort (no main effect overall or within mcrpc
strata). Pairs of negative biomarkers (mcrpc, ar_v7, brca2, msi_high, ecog) act
approximately additively on baseline response — pairwise interaction terms in non-
enzalutamide patients are mostly null (only ar_v7×brca2 p=0.04, brca2×ecog p=0.02, both
small and not surviving multiple testing). Patients commonly receive multiple concurrent
treatments (median 1, max 6); the positive correlation between number of treatments and
response is largely explained by enzalutamide receipt.

Conclusions
-----------
1. There is exactly one treatment with a true effect on objective_response in this
   cohort: treatment_enzalutamide.
2. The enzalutamide effect is highly subgroup-dependent. The best-supported predictive
   subgroup is:

       ar_v7_positive = 0  AND  mcrpc = 0  AND  brca2_mutation = 0  AND  msi_high = 0

   (n = 15,681; ~31% of the cohort). Inside this subgroup the absolute increase in
   objective_response from enzalutamide is approximately +62.6 percentage points
   (79.8% vs 17.2%). Outside this subgroup — i.e., when ANY of those four "resistance"
   markers is positive — the enzalutamide effect is essentially zero.

3. None of treatment_abiraterone, treatment_docetaxel, treatment_olaparib,
   treatment_lu177_psma, or treatment_pembrolizumab produces a measurable benefit on
   objective_response — neither overall, nor within their biologically expected
   biomarker subgroups (BRCA2+, MSI-high, PSMA-high, mCRPC, visceral mets), nor in any
   2- or 3-feature subgroup tested, nor when added on top of enzalutamide.

4. Independently of treatment, the strongest negative prognostic factors for response
   are mcrpc, ar_v7_positive, brca2_mutation, msi_high, higher ECOG, higher PSA,
   higher CRP, and higher weight loss; albumin is the strongest positive prognostic
   factor. Visceral mets, Gleason score, age, hemoglobin, LDH, ALP, hepatic and renal
   labs, and the electrolytes are not prognostic.
