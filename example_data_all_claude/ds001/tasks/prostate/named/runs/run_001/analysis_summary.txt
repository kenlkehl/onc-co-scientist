ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_prostate (n=50,000)
==============================================

DATASET OVERVIEW
----------------
The cohort is a prostate-cancer EHR aggregation. Sex is uniformly male
(sex_female=0 for all 50,000 records). The single binary outcome is
objective_response (mean 0.240). Six treatment indicators are present
(enzalutamide 40.2%, abiraterone 30.0%, docetaxel 30.4%, olaparib 10.2%,
lu177-PSMA 15.0%, pembrolizumab 4.8%) and patients can receive multiple
treatments simultaneously. Key biomarkers include mcrpc (54.96%),
visceral_mets (20.0%), brca2_mutation (10.0%), ar_v7_positive (20.1%),
msi_high (3.06%), psma_high (59.9%). No missing values.

WORKFLOW
--------
Across 25 iterations the analysis progressed from main effects (treatment
and feature) through pre-specified mechanistic interactions, joint
multi-feature subgroup discovery, exhaustive 2-feature subgroup scans, and
random-forest T-learner heterogeneity estimation.

A full multivariable logistic regression confirms these features are
independent prognostic factors and that treatment_enzalutamide is the
only treatment whose adjusted coefficient is non-null (beta=+1.20, OR
3.32). The other five treatments have adjusted coefficients with p>0.3.

PRE-SPECIFIED MECHANISTIC INTERACTIONS
--------------------------------------
The dataset includes treatments designed to target specific biomarkers.
We tested each pre-specified pair:

  olaparib  × brca2_mutation     interaction beta=-0.289, p=0.045
                                 (within BRCA2+, olaparib rate 0.121 vs
                                 0.153; numerically WORSE on olaparib).
                                 The mechanistic prior (benefit in
                                 BRCA2+) is NOT supported.

  pembrolizumab × msi_high       interaction beta=+0.017, p=0.96
                                 (within MSI-H, pembrolizumab rate 0.177
                                 vs 0.176). NULL.

  lu177-PSMA × psma_high         interaction beta=-0.054, p=0.36
                                 (within PSMA-high, Lu177 rate 0.238 vs
                                 0.239). NULL.

None of these biologically-motivated pairings showed a positive treatment
effect. The data are consistent with these four treatments having no
effect on objective_response in any subgroup.

ENZALUTAMIDE TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY
-------------------------------------------
A systematic interaction screen (treatment × feature logits) revealed that
the enzalutamide main effect is fully concentrated in a four-predicate
subgroup. Each of mcrpc, brca2_mutation, ar_v7_positive, and msi_high
contributes a strongly significant negative interaction with enzalutamide:

  enz × mcrpc          beta=-1.978, p<1e-300
  enz × ar_v7_positive beta=-1.237, p=1.4e-93
  enz × brca2_mutation beta=-1.138, p=3.0e-42
  enz × msi_high       beta=-1.286, p=1.4e-19

Univariate stratification confirms each suppressor:
  mcrpc=0:   enz risk diff +0.440 ; mcrpc=1:   +0.008
  ar_v7=0:   enz risk diff +0.251 ; ar_v7=1:   +0.009
  brca2=0:   enz risk diff +0.225 ; brca2=1:   +0.006
  msi_high=0:enz risk diff +0.209 ; msi_high=1:-0.022

Joint stratification shows the effect grows as more "favorable" levels
are stacked. The full conjunction (mcrpc=0 AND brca2=0 AND ar_v7=0 AND
msi_high=0; n=15,681) yields:

  enzalutamide on:  response 0.798  (n=6,325)
  enzalutamide off: response 0.172  (n=9,356)
  risk difference: +0.626 (chi-squared p<1e-300)

Outside the favorable subgroup (any one unfavorable level present;
n=34,319):

  enzalutamide on:  response 0.161  (n=13,751)
  enzalutamide off: response 0.153  (n=20,568)
  risk difference: +0.008 (p=0.06)

Drop-one analysis confirms each predicate is necessary: removing any
single predicate from the conjunction admits patients with the
unfavorable level, and within that admitted stratum the enzalutamide
risk difference collapses to <+0.01. Within the favorable subgroup,
visceral_mets, psma_high, and gleason_score do not further modify the
enzalutamide effect (residual differences <0.04 across strata). ECOG
shows a small monotone gradient inside the favorable subgroup
(ECOG 0/1/2 → enz risk diff +0.646/+0.622/+0.590) but the enzalutamide
benefit remains very large at every ECOG level.

A continuous-feature interaction screen flagged psa_ng_ml × enzalutamide
(beta=-0.81, p=1.2e-72), but this is fully explained by membership in
the favorable subgroup: within favorable patients PSA does not modulate
the enzalutamide effect (interaction p=0.79; risk difference stable in
[+0.618, +0.633] across PSA quartiles). High PSA is correlated with
mCRPC and other unfavorable markers, which is the source of the original
PSA × enzalutamide signal.

NON-ENZALUTAMIDE TREATMENTS — HETEROGENEITY SEARCH
--------------------------------------------------
For each of the five remaining treatments we ran:

  • main effect tests (all p>=0.47, |risk diff| <0.005)
  • biomarker-paired interaction logits (all null or borderline negative)
  • random-forest T-learner CATE (estimated ATE within 0.005 of zero
    for all five; CATE std ranges 0.028–0.058 but the apparent
    quartile separation reflects baseline-prognosis confounding, since
    enzalutamide-favorable patients tend to respond regardless of
    co-treatment)
  • exhaustive 2-feature binary subgroup search (best subgroup risk
    difference for each treatment: abiraterone -0.049 p=0.18,
    docetaxel -0.029 p=0.45, olaparib -0.071 p=0.19, lu177-PSMA
    -0.039 p=0.054, pembrolizumab -0.048 p=0.078). All "best"
    subgroups are negative-direction and non-significant.

There is no convincing positive treatment effect for abiraterone,
docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA, or pembrolizumab on objective_response
in any subgroup we examined.

WHAT WAS SUPPORTED VS REFUTED
-----------------------------
Supported:
  • Enzalutamide raises objective_response (main effect +20 pp).
  • Higher PSA, weight loss, CRP, ECOG, mCRPC, BRCA2+, AR-V7+, and
    MSI-high are independently associated with lower objective_response.
  • Higher albumin is associated with higher response.
  • The enzalutamide treatment effect is concentrated in the
    (mcrpc=0 & brca2=0 & ar_v7=0 & msi=0) subgroup with risk
    difference ~+0.63; outside this subgroup the effect is essentially
    zero.

Refuted:
  • The mechanistic prior that olaparib benefits BRCA2-mutated patients
    is not supported here (response is numerically lower on olaparib in
    BRCA2+, p=0.058).
  • The mechanistic prior that pembrolizumab benefits MSI-high patients
    is not supported (interaction p=0.96).
  • The mechanistic prior that Lu177-PSMA benefits PSMA-high patients
    is not supported (interaction p=0.36).
  • Abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA, and pembrolizumab
    have no detectable effect on objective_response in any subgroup.
  • PSA-based modulation of the enzalutamide effect is fully explained
    by the four-predicate favorable subgroup definition.
  • Gleason score is not associated with response.
  • visceral_mets and psma_high are not associated with response.

FINAL SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS (FOR THE ONE OUTCOME WITH USABLE TREATMENT
VARIATION, OBJECTIVE_RESPONSE)
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment: treatment_enzalutamide
Outcome: objective_response (binary, higher is better)
Direction: positive (enzalutamide increases response)
Subgroup: mcrpc = 0 AND brca2_mutation = 0 AND ar_v7_positive = 0 AND
          msi_high = 0
Estimated within-subgroup risk difference: +0.626 (95% CI roughly
+0.61 to +0.64), p<1e-300.
Outside subgroup: risk difference +0.008 (p=0.06), no treatment effect.
Each of the four predicates is independently necessary; admitting any
unfavorable level (mcrpc=1 OR brca2=1 OR ar_v7=1 OR msi=1) abolishes
the enzalutamide effect.
