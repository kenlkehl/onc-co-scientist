Analysis summary for ds001_prostate (n=50,000 patients, outcome: objective_response, base rate 24.0%)
====================================================================================================

Overall approach
----------------
Across 25 iterations I (i) characterized univariate associations between every
candidate feature and objective_response, (ii) fit a multivariable logistic model
including all six treatments and the prognostic features, (iii) screened every
treatment for biomarker- and lab-feature-driven heterogeneity, (iv) iteratively
refined a joint-modifier definition for the only treatment that showed a real
effect (enzalutamide), and (v) confirmed the resulting subgroup with both
within-stratum adjusted regression and a T-learner random-forest CATE.

Iterations 1-4: main effects
-----------------------------
- Treatment main effects on objective_response are dominated by ONE drug:
  treatment_enzalutamide, which raises the response rate from 15.9% to 36.1%
  (+20.2 pp absolute, p<1e-300). The other five treatments (abiraterone,
  docetaxel, olaparib, lu177_psma, pembrolizumab) each have an absolute main
  effect under 0.5 pp and chi-square p-values from 0.47 to 0.91 — i.e., no
  detectable main effect.
- Strong negative prognostic main effects: mcrpc (-19.2 pp, p<1e-300),
  brca2_mutation (-10.1 pp, p=5e-56), ar_v7_positive (-10.1 pp, p=2e-98),
  msi_high (-6.6 pp, p=3e-9), and ecog_ps (28% → 23% → 18% across 0/1/2).
- Continuous features: psa_ng_ml (univariate logistic coef -0.0047 per ng/mL,
  p=2e-94), weight_loss_pct_6mo (-0.032, p=3e-29), crp_mg_l (-0.007, p=6e-8),
  and albumin_g_dl (+0.10, p=4e-6) all show monotone associations with response.
  age_years, gleason_score, ldh, hemoglobin, ALP, AST, ALT, bilirubin,
  creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, nlr, visceral_mets, and
  psma_high have no detectable univariate main effect.
- All patients are male (sex_female=0 throughout), so this feature drops out.
- The hypothesized "good biomarker" features (psma_high, msi_high) trend in
  the wrong direction or are null for objective_response: psma_high is null,
  msi_high is moderately negative, brca2_mutation is strongly negative — these
  predict that biology-aligned treatment matches (lu177-PSMA → PSMA-high,
  pembrolizumab → MSI-high, olaparib → BRCA2+) may NOT hold in this dataset.

Iteration 5: multivariable logistic
------------------------------------
A 31-covariate logistic model (n=50,000, pseudo-R²=0.124) confirmed that
treatment_enzalutamide is the only treatment with an independent positive log-
odds effect (+1.20, p<1e-300); abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA,
and pembrolizumab all had |coef|<0.04 and p>0.28 after adjustment. Independent
negative covariates: mcrpc -1.12, ar_v7 -0.73, brca2 -0.71, msi_high -0.23,
ecog_ps -0.32, weight_loss -0.037, crp -0.007, psa -0.001. Albumin was the
only independent positive covariate (+0.12).

Iterations 6-9: enzalutamide × biomarker pairwise interactions
---------------------------------------------------------------
All four pairwise treatment_enzalutamide × biomarker interactions are highly
significant and all four point the same way (each biomarker SUPPRESSES the
enzalutamide effect):

  enz × mcrpc          : interaction coef -1.98, p<1e-300.
                         non-mCRPC: enz 61.0% vs 16.9%, diff +44.0 pp.
                         mCRPC    : enz 15.8% vs 15.0%, diff  +0.8 pp (p=0.09).
  enz × ar_v7_positive : coef -1.24, p=1.4e-93.
                         AR-V7-: diff +25.1 pp; AR-V7+: diff +0.8 pp.
  enz × brca2_mutation : coef -1.14, p=3.3e-42.
                         BRCA2-: diff +22.5 pp; BRCA2+: diff +0.6 pp.
  enz × msi_high       : coef -1.28, p=1.8e-19.
                         MSI-low: diff +20.9 pp; MSI-high: diff -2.2 pp.

Iteration 10: enz × other features
----------------------------------
psma_high is NOT a modifier of enzalutamide (interaction p=0.40; identical
+20 pp benefit on both sides). visceral_mets is NOT a modifier (p=0.86).
psa_ng_ml shows a continuous interaction (p=1e-72) but this is collinear with
mcrpc (which strongly inflates PSA), so it does not contribute beyond mcrpc.

Iterations 11-13: are the other five treatments effective in any subgroup?
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
For treatment_olaparib in brca2_mutation+ patients (n=4,996), olaparib is
DIRECTIONALLY HARMFUL: 12.1% on-tx vs 15.3% off-tx (-3.2 pp, p=0.058);
interaction coef = -0.29 (p=0.045) — opposite of the biological expectation.

For treatment_pembrolizumab in msi_high patients (n=1,528), no benefit:
17.7% vs 17.6% (interaction p=0.96) — opposite of clinical expectation.

For treatment_lu177_psma in psma_high patients (n=29,962): 23.8% vs 23.9%,
diff -0.1 pp (interaction p=0.36) — no PSMA-targeted enrichment.

A systematic scan (5 treatments × ~25 candidate modifiers, ~125 tests) found
no interaction p-value smaller than 0.01, none surviving Bonferroni correction.
The only non-noise heterogeneity for non-enzalutamide treatments was
lu177_psma × visceral_mets (p=0.010): in visceral-mets patients lu177-PSMA
appears mildly harmful (-2.4 pp, p=0.04). The absolute effect sizes remain
small and the finding does not change the overall picture: none of these five
agents has a clinically meaningful effect on objective_response anywhere.

Iterations 14-19: defining and validating the joint enzalutamide subgroup
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
Building on the four pairwise interactions, I tested the joint-favorable
subgroup mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND msi_high=0
(n=15,681; 31% of the cohort):

  Joint-favorable    : enz on   = 79.8% (n=6,325)
                       enz off  = 17.2% (n=9,356)
                       diff     = +62.6 pp, p<1e-300.

  Joint-unfavorable  : enz on   = 16.1% (n=13,751)
                       enz off  = 15.3% (n=20,568)
                       diff     = +0.8 pp, p=0.06.

A logistic model with all four enz-by-modifier interactions retained each
interaction at very high significance (enz×mcrpc -2.20, enz×arv7 -1.61,
enz×brca2 -1.56, enz×msi -1.67, all p<<0.001), confirming each modifier is
an independent suppressor. Removing any single constraint from the joint
subgroup substantially reduces the absolute benefit (diff falls from +62.6 pp
to +28.7 pp if mcrpc is allowed; +50.5 if AR-V7+ is allowed; +56.3 if BRCA2+
is allowed; +60.7 if MSI-high is allowed) — consistent with each modifier
acting near-multiplicatively to abolish benefit.

Within the favorable subgroup, no further effect modifier was identified
(smallest interaction p was enz×psma_high at 0.004, but the absolute diffs
are nearly equal: +64.8 PSMA-low vs +61.1 PSMA-high — explained by ratio-
scale curvature, not biology). Concomitant administration of any of the
other five treatments leaves the enzalutamide effect within favorable
patients essentially unchanged (+62 to +66 pp regardless).

A 2x2 cross-tab on mcrpc × ar_v7_positive showed that EITHER suppressor on
its own is sufficient to abolish the enzalutamide effect: the +54.6 pp
benefit is restricted to the mCRPC=0 / AR-V7=0 cell.

Iteration 20: random-forest CATE confirmation
---------------------------------------------
A T-learner random-forest CATE model trained on all 25 baseline features
ranked the same four binary modifiers as the dominant drivers of
heterogeneity, in the same order: mcrpc (importance 0.665), ar_v7_positive
(0.205), brca2_mutation (0.099), msi_high (0.023). The remaining 21
features collectively contributed <1%. The top CATE quartile (Q4, n=12,500)
was 100% non-mCRPC, 100% AR-V7-, 100% BRCA2-, 100% MSI-low — exactly
matching the regression-derived favorable subgroup — and showed an observed
absolute enzalutamide benefit of +71.3 pp (84% vs 12%; the larger gap vs the
all-favorable +63 pp is because Q4 also enriches for patients with low PSA
and good ECOG, which weakly trim the most-likely-to-respond-without-tx
patients out of Q4).

Iterations 21-23: prognostic and adjusted within-stratum effects
----------------------------------------------------------------
Within the unfavorable subgroup, an adjusted logistic with all prognostic
covariates yields treatment_enzalutamide coef = +0.061 (p=0.046), translating
to ~1 pp absolute response increase — clinically negligible.

Adjusted independent prognostic signs match univariate analysis: ECOG↓,
mCRPC↓, AR-V7+↓, BRCA2+↓, MSI-high↓, weight_loss↓, CRP↓, PSA↓, albumin↑.
Visceral_mets, gleason_score, ldh, hemoglobin, ALP, AST, ALT, bilirubin,
creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, age, NLR, and PSMA-high carry
no independent signal after adjustment.

Iteration 22 also showed that on the absolute scale, enzalutamide benefit
is nearly constant across ECOG strata (+20.7 / +20.1 / +19.8 pp for ECOG
0/1/2 in the full cohort), even though the log-odds interaction term
appears significant (p=1.6e-5) — this is a classic ratio-vs-difference
scale artifact and not a real effect modification.

Iteration 24: lu177-PSMA in PSMA-high reconfirmed null
------------------------------------------------------
Even within the joint-favorable subgroup (where overall response is high),
lu177_psma has no measurable enrichment in psma_high patients. The expected
biology is not present in this dataset.

Iteration 25: final synthesis
-----------------------------
Best-supported treatment-effect subgroup (the answer for the only treatment
with usable variation that produces effect heterogeneity):

  TREATMENT      : treatment_enzalutamide
  OUTCOME        : objective_response
  DIRECTION      : positive (response rate is INCREASED on treatment)
  SUBGROUP       : mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0 AND brca2_mutation=0
                   AND msi_high=0 (n=15,681; 31% of the cohort)
  EFFECT MAGNITUDE: response rate ≈79.8% on enzalutamide vs ≈17.2% off
                   enzalutamide; absolute difference ≈+63 percentage points.
  COMPLEMENT      : in patients with ANY of mcrpc, ar_v7_positive,
                   brca2_mutation, or msi_high present (n=34,319; 69% of
                   the cohort), the absolute enzalutamide effect is ≈+1 pp
                   (clinically negligible; adjusted log-odds coef +0.061,
                   p=0.05).

Hypotheses supported
--------------------
- treatment_enzalutamide raises objective_response (h1_enz_main).
- mcrpc, ar_v7_positive, brca2_mutation, msi_high, ecog_ps, weight_loss_pct_6mo,
  crp_mg_l, psa_ng_ml are all independently negative prognostic markers
  (h2_brca2, h2_arv7, h2_msi, h2_mcrpc, h3_ecog, h3_psa, h4_wt, h4_crp).
- albumin_g_dl is independently positive (h4_alb).
- Each of the four binary modifiers individually and additively abolishes
  the enzalutamide effect (h6_enz_mcrpc, h7_enz_arv7, h8_enz_brca2, h9_enz_msi).
- The complete favorable subgroup (mcrpc=0 & AR-V7- & BRCA2- & MSI-low)
  carries essentially all of the enzalutamide effect (h14_joint_4mod,
  h15_joint_logit, h25_final_subgroup).

Hypotheses refuted
------------------
- treatment_abiraterone, treatment_docetaxel, treatment_olaparib,
  treatment_lu177_psma, treatment_pembrolizumab DO NOT raise objective_response
  in this cohort (h1_abi_main, h1_doc_main, h1_ola_main, h1_lu_main,
  h1_pem_main).
- treatment_olaparib does NOT preferentially benefit BRCA2+ patients —
  in fact directionally harmful (h11_olap_brca2 refuted, opposite direction).
- treatment_pembrolizumab does NOT preferentially benefit MSI-high patients
  (h11_pem_msi refuted).
- treatment_lu177_psma does NOT preferentially benefit PSMA-high patients
  (h11_lu_psma refuted; h24_psma_high_psma_low confirmed null).
- gleason_score, age_years, visceral_mets, ldh_u_l, nlr, hemoglobin_g_dl,
  alkaline_phosphatase_u_l do not have main effects on objective_response
  (h2_visc, h3_gleason, h3_age, h4_ldh, h4_nlr, h4_hgb, h4_alp).

Overall conclusions
-------------------
This 50,000-patient prostate-cancer cohort exhibits a single dominant
biological story: enzalutamide is a highly effective treatment, but its
benefit is concentrated almost entirely in a four-way joint-favorable
subgroup defined by absence of mCRPC, absence of AR-V7 expression, absence
of BRCA2 mutation, and MSI-low status. The dataset's other five labelled
treatments (abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA, pembrolizumab)
exhibit no meaningful response benefit, in any subgroup tested, including
the biomarker-defined subgroups where they would conventionally be
expected to work (BRCA2+ for olaparib, MSI-high for pembrolizumab,
PSMA-high for lu177-PSMA). This pattern — one effective drug with strong
biomarker-defined modifiers, and the other drugs uniformly null — is
itself an important finding: it suggests that in this aggregated EHR
cohort, response selection or capture is dominated by enzalutamide
biology and does not recapitulate trial-validated biomarker-targeted
treatment effects for the other agents.
