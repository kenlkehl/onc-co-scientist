ds001_prostate — Analysis summary
==================================

Cohort: 50,000 patients with prostate cancer (sex_female=0 in all rows). Single
binary outcome `objective_response`; baseline rate 24.0%. Six binary treatment
flags (enzalutamide, abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177_psma, pembrolizumab),
four binary biomarkers (brca2_mutation, ar_v7_positive, msi_high, psma_high), and
~25 demographic / lab / disease-status features. Treatment prevalences: enza 40%,
abi 30%, doce 30%, lu177 15%, ola 10%, pem 5%. mCRPC 55%, visceral mets 20%,
ECOG 0/1/2 = 35/50/15%.


HEADLINE FINDING
================
Of the six treatments, only **enzalutamide** has a real treatment effect on
objective_response in this dataset, and that effect is concentrated in a
specific subgroup. The other five treatments (abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib,
lu177_psma, pembrolizumab) show no detectable response benefit overall and no
biomarker subgroup with a clinically meaningful, replicable benefit emerged from
single-feature, pairwise, three-way, exhaustive 1-2-feature, or random-forest
HTE searches.

The canonical enzalutamide responder subgroup is:

    mcrpc = 0  AND  ar_v7_positive = 0   (n = 18,000; 36% of cohort)

Within this subgroup:
  ORR enzalutamide+  = 0.717
  ORR enzalutamide-  = 0.171
  Difference         = +0.546 percentage points (p ≈ 0)

Outside this subgroup (mcrpc=1, OR ar_v7_positive=1) the enzalutamide treatment
effect is essentially zero (differences of 0.005-0.014, all p > 0.08). The
three-way logistic interaction enzalutamide × mcrpc × ar_v7_positive has
coef = +2.38, p = 9.6e-84 (unadjusted) and coef = +2.45, p = 7.3e-87 after full
adjustment for clinical, lab, biomarker, and other-treatment covariates (i22).


ITERATION-BY-ITERATION WALKTHROUGH
==================================

Iter 1 — Treatment main effects on objective_response.
  enzalutamide: ORR 0.361 vs 0.159 (diff +0.202, p ≈ 0)  -- striking
  abiraterone: 0.238 vs 0.241 (NS)
  docetaxel:  0.240 vs 0.240 (NS)
  olaparib:   0.239 vs 0.240 (NS)
  lu177_psma: 0.243 vs 0.240 (NS)
  pembrolizumab: 0.239 vs 0.240 (NS)
  Conclusion: only enzalutamide has a non-zero average effect; this raised the
  question of which subgroup carries the signal and whether the other drugs
  have biomarker-specific effects.

Iter 2 — Disease-status main effects.
  Higher ECOG performance status, mCRPC status, visceral metastases, and higher
  Gleason score are all associated with lower ORR (all p < 1e-50 except
  visceral mets which is also highly significant).

Iter 3 — Continuous lab/feature univariates.
  Lower albumin, higher LDH, higher CRP, higher NLR, higher ALP, more weight
  loss, lower hemoglobin, higher PSA, and older age are each associated with
  lower ORR (all p ≪ 0.001). Sodium, potassium, calcium, creatinine, BUN,
  bilirubin, AST, ALT show smaller effects (some weakly significant).

Iter 4 — Canonical biomarker × treatment interactions.
  enzalutamide × ar_v7_positive: interaction coef = -1.24, p = 1.4e-93
    AR-V7 NEGATIVE: enza 0.411 vs control 0.160 (diff +0.25)
    AR-V7 POSITIVE: enza 0.165 vs control 0.157 (diff +0.01)
  olaparib × brca2_mutation: NEGATIVE interaction (coef -0.29, p=0.045)
    olaparib's effect is *worse* in BRCA2-mutated patients (clinically opposite
    of the FDA label).
  pembrolizumab × msi_high: no interaction (p = 0.96)
  lu177_psma × psma_high: no interaction (p = 0.36)
  abiraterone × ar_v7_positive: no interaction (p = 0.33)
  docetaxel × ar_v7_positive: no interaction (p = 0.86)

Iter 5 — Biomarker prognostic main effects.
  brca2_mutation: ORR 0.150 vs 0.250 (diff -0.10, p = 5e-56)
  ar_v7_positive:  ORR 0.160 vs 0.260 (diff -0.10, p = 2e-98)
  msi_high:        ORR 0.176 vs 0.242 (diff -0.07, p = 3e-9)
  psma_high:       ORR 0.239 vs 0.243 (NS)
  -> BRCA2, AR-V7, and MSI-high are *prognostically negative* (regardless of
     treatment), not predictive of treatment benefit.

Iter 6 — Three-way interactions for the canonical biomarker-treatment pairings,
adding mcrpc as a third axis.
  olaparib × brca2 × mcrpc: 3-way coef -0.25, p = 0.38 (NS); within
    BRCA-mut + mcrpc subgroup, olaparib trends *negative* (0.111 vs 0.152,
    p = 0.07).
  pembrolizumab × msi × mcrpc: 3-way coef -1.35, p = 0.04, BUT within MSI+mcrpc
    pembrolizumab trends LOWER ORR (0.089 vs 0.161, p = 0.28).
  lu177 × psma × mcrpc: 3-way coef +0.23, p = 0.07; within psma_high+mcrpc
    cell ORR diff is just +0.009 (p = 0.25).
  -> No 3-way subgroup salvages a positive effect for these three drugs.

Iter 7 — Single-feature stratified screens for every treatment.
  enzalutamide: largest effects in mcrpc=0 (+0.44), psa_ng_ml<=median (+0.31),
    ar_v7=0, ecog=0/1, etc. — confirming enza signal localized to those cells.
  abi, doce, ola, lu177, pem: ZERO single-feature subgroups with significant
    *positive* treatment effects under p<0.05; some weakly significant negative
    subgroups.

Iter 8 — Pairwise (biomarker=1 ∩ biomarker=1) screens for ola/pem/lu177:
  No subgroup with significant positive effect.

Iter 9 — Continuous-modifier interaction screens for enzalutamide.
  enza × ecog_ps coef = -0.71, p ≈ 0 (effect smaller as ECOG worsens)
  enza × psa_ng_ml coef ≈ -0.013, p ≈ 0 (effect smaller at high PSA)
  enza × albumin_g_dl coef = +0.17, p < 0.01 (effect larger at higher albumin)
  enza × ldh_u_l, alp, crp, nlr, hemoglobin: each p < 0.05 in the expected
  prognostic direction (better prognosis → larger enza effect), but the
  dominant modifier remains AR-V7 status across ECOG strata.
  Within ECOG=0, =1, and =2, enza × ar_v7 interaction p = 9.4e-49, 4.8e-39,
  4.2e-9 respectively — robust across ECOG.

Iter 10 — Multivariable logistic of objective_response on all features and
treatments. Significant adjusted predictors include enzalutamide (positive),
ECOG, mCRPC, brca2_mutation, ar_v7_positive, msi_high, visceral_mets, PSA,
LDH, ALP, NLR, weight loss, CRP, low albumin, low hemoglobin (all in the
prognostically expected directions). Other treatment flags remain near 0.

Iter 11 — Refining the enza responder subgroup.
  enza × mcrpc interaction coef = -1.98, p ≈ 0.
  enza × mcrpc × ar_v7 3-way coef = +2.38, p = 9.55e-84.
  Cell-level ORR (enza+ vs enza-, diff):
    mcrpc=0, arv7=0  -> 0.717 vs 0.171, +0.546 (n=18,000)  ** signal
    mcrpc=0, arv7=1  -> 0.177 vs 0.163, +0.014 (n= 4,519)  null
    mcrpc=1, arv7=0  -> 0.159 vs 0.150, +0.008 (n=21,962)  null
    mcrpc=1, arv7=1  -> 0.155 vs 0.151, +0.005 (n= 5,519)  null

Iter 12 — Triple-feature screens for olaparib/pembrolizumab/lu177 conditioning
on the canonical biomarker plus pairs of {mcrpc, visceral_mets, ar_v7_positive}.
  Best positive effects had p > 0.2 in every case. No subgroup reached
  significance in a positive direction.

Iter 13 — Interaction-coef screens (binary mods).
  olaparib's strongest binary interaction is brca2_mutation (NEGATIVE, p=0.045).
  lu177_psma's strongest interaction is visceral_mets (NEGATIVE, p=0.01) —
  i.e., lu177 trends worse in visceral mets patients (small effect).
  abiraterone × brca2 interaction is positive at p = 0.03 (modest).
  pembrolizumab and docetaxel: nothing significant.

Iter 14 — Continuous-modifier interactions for ola/pem/lu177.
  pembrolizumab × ecog_ps: p=0.07 (effect possibly smaller at higher ECOG).
  Otherwise nothing surviving p<0.05.

Iter 15 — Random-forest HTE / uplift screen, top decile.
  enzalutamide top decile (largely mcrpc=0, ar_v7=0): ORR enza+ 0.851 vs
    enza- 0.141, diff +0.71, p ≈ 0 (confirming the canonical subgroup at
    higher fidelity).
  abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177_psma, pembrolizumab: top decile shows
    a positive treated-vs-control difference (0.10-0.21 pp), but the decile is
    enriched for *good prognosis* features (low PSA, low ECOG, high albumin) and
    treated patients are a small fraction of the decile, so the difference
    largely reflects which patients receive the drug rather than a causal
    treatment effect. None of these signals replicate in any single biomarker
    subgroup, in interaction tests, or in the multivariable model with all
    treatments included (i10) — those treatment flags are NOT significant
    predictors of response after adjustment.

Iter 16 — Prognostic confirmation in fully untreated patients.
  Among the 7,872 patients with all six treatment flags = 0:
    brca2_mutation=1  ORR 0.150 vs 0.252 (diff -0.10, p = 9e-13) — prognostic
    ar_v7_positive=1  ORR 0.157 vs 0.262 (diff -0.10, p = 6e-22) — prognostic
    msi_high=1        ORR 0.166 vs 0.246 (diff -0.08, p = 0.001)  — prognostic
  -> confirms the negative biomarker associations are not artefacts of
     treatment selection.

Iter 17 — Exhaustive 1-2 feature subgroup screen (n>=100/cell).
  olaparib: 0 positive significant subgroups, 4 negative ones (BRCA2+Gleason 7
    and ECOG 1, etc. — all consistent with olaparib being null or harmful in
    these subgroups).
  pembrolizumab: 0 positive, 0 negative significant subgroups.
  lu177_psma: 5 positive significant subgroups, but absolute differences
    +0.03-+0.07 in odd cells (mcrpc=1+Gleason10, ECOG=0+Gleason9, etc.); none
    survive multiple-comparison adjustment and none correspond to PSMA-high.
  abiraterone: 3 positive subgroups all anchored on brca2_mutation=1; absolute
    effect size +0.025 to +0.05.
  docetaxel: 2 positive (small +0.04-+0.06), 2 negative subgroups; mixed
    weak signals only.

Iter 18 — Within the canonical enzalutamide responder cell, enza effect is
uniformly very large (>0.45 ORR difference) across visceral mets, brca2,
msi_high, psma_high, ECOG 0/1/2, and Gleason 6-10. No additional binary
feature reduces the enza effect to near zero, and no continuous lab modifier
substantially attenuates it.

Iter 19 — In mcrpc=0 only, the enza × ar_v7 interaction remains strongly
negative (coef ≈ -1.5, p ≪ 0.001) after adjusting for age, ECOG, visceral
mets, PSA, Gleason, albumin, LDH, hemoglobin, ALP — confirms ar_v7 modifies
the enza effect independently of confounders.

Iter 20 — Pairwise treatment-treatment interactions: none show significant
synergy or antagonism for ORR, suggesting treatments act independently.

Iter 21 — Final per-treatment hypotheses crystallized:
  enzalutamide: BENEFIT in mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0; ORR diff +0.546.
  abiraterone:  no overall main effect; weak positive trend in BRCA2-mut.
  docetaxel:    no overall main effect; no subgroup signal.
  olaparib:     no overall main effect; trend toward HARM in BRCA2-mut
                (subgroup ORR 0.121 vs 0.153, p = 0.058) — opposite of the
                clinical expectation for a PARP inhibitor.
  lu177_psma:   no overall main effect; no signal within psma_high.
  pembrolizumab: no overall main effect; no signal within msi_high.

Iter 22 — Adjusted three-way interaction enza × mcrpc × ar_v7 (with all other
treatments and clinical/lab covariates): coef = +2.45, p = 7.3e-87. Effect
remains highly robust. Within the reference cell (mcrpc=0, ar_v7=0), the
adjusted enza coef is +2.57 (huge OR ≈ 13).


PROGNOSTIC vs PREDICTIVE STRUCTURE OF THE DATASET
=================================================
Prognostically negative (lower baseline ORR) and confirmed in patients with no
treatment exposure:
  - higher ECOG, mCRPC, visceral mets, higher Gleason
  - higher PSA, lower albumin, lower hemoglobin
  - higher LDH, higher ALP, higher CRP, higher NLR, more weight loss
  - brca2_mutation, ar_v7_positive, msi_high  (each ~0.10 pp lower ORR)
  - psma_high: NOT prognostic (essentially neutral)

Predictive (modifies treatment effect):
  - mcrpc and ar_v7_positive both predictively modify enzalutamide:
      enzalutamide effect ≈ +0.55 ORR in mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7=0,
      and ≈ 0 in any other combination of those two variables.
  - No other biomarker × treatment interaction reproducibly identifies
    a positive responder subgroup in this dataset, including the clinically
    expected pairings (olaparib × BRCA2, pembrolizumab × MSI, lu177 × PSMA).
    olaparib × BRCA2 is in the *opposite* direction to the clinical
    expectation (slight harm).


HYPOTHESES SUPPORTED vs REFUTED (summary)
==========================================
SUPPORTED:
  - enzalutamide produces a large objective_response increase in
    mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0 patients (best-supported subgroup).
  - enzalutamide × mcrpc × ar_v7_positive 3-way interaction is real
    (p ~ 1e-87 adjusted).
  - brca2_mutation, ar_v7_positive, msi_high are prognostically associated
    with lower ORR.
  - Standard sickness markers (ECOG, mCRPC, visceral mets, low albumin, high
    LDH, high CRP, high NLR, low hemoglobin, weight loss, high ALP, high PSA)
    are all associated with lower ORR.

REFUTED (or not supported by the data):
  - Olaparib improves ORR in BRCA2-mutated patients — REFUTED (trend opposite).
  - Pembrolizumab improves ORR in MSI-high patients — REFUTED (no effect).
  - Lu177-PSMA improves ORR in PSMA-high patients — REFUTED (no effect).
  - Abiraterone has a major main effect or biomarker subgroup — REFUTED
    (weak BRCA-mut subgroup signal at best).
  - Docetaxel has a detectable response benefit — REFUTED (no signal).


FINAL BEST-SUPPORTED TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUPS
================================================
For each treatment with usable variation, the best-supported claim about
treatment-effect heterogeneity, naming all relevant predicates, is:

  treatment_enzalutamide :
      Treatment effect on objective_response is concentrated in patients
      with mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0.
      Within that subgroup ORR rises from 0.171 (no enzalutamide) to 0.717
      (enzalutamide), a +0.55 absolute increase (p ≈ 0).
      In any subgroup containing mcrpc=1 OR ar_v7_positive=1, the
      enzalutamide effect is statistically and clinically null.

  treatment_abiraterone :
      No subgroup with a clinically meaningful positive treatment effect
      identified. The largest signal is in brca2_mutation=1 (subgroup
      ORR 0.164 vs 0.143, diff +0.02, p = 0.06), which is small and not
      statistically supported after correction.

  treatment_docetaxel :
      No subgroup with a clinically meaningful positive treatment effect
      identified across single-feature, pairwise, three-way, exhaustive
      1-2 feature, RF-uplift, and continuous-modifier searches.

  treatment_olaparib :
      No subgroup with a positive treatment effect. Direction in the
      canonical brca2_mutation=1 subgroup is *negative* (ORR 0.121 vs 0.153,
      p = 0.06).

  treatment_lu177_psma :
      No subgroup with a clinically meaningful positive treatment effect.
      Within the canonical psma_high=1 subgroup, ORR difference is -0.001
      (p = 0.88).

  treatment_pembrolizumab :
      No subgroup with a clinically meaningful positive treatment effect.
      Within the canonical msi_high=1 subgroup, ORR difference is +0.001
      (p = 1.0).
