ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_prostate (n=50,000 patients)
=====================================================

OUTCOME ANALYZED
----------------
Single binary outcome: objective_response (overall rate 24.0%).

TREATMENTS IN THE DATASET
-------------------------
treatment_enzalutamide        (n=20,076; 40.2%)
treatment_abiraterone         (n=14,991; 30.0%)
treatment_docetaxel           (n=15,187; 30.4%)
treatment_olaparib            (n= 5,098; 10.2%)
treatment_lu177_psma          (n= 7,504; 15.0%)
treatment_pembrolizumab       (n= 2,384;  4.8%)

These treatments are not mutually exclusive (multiple drug indicators per patient possible).

OVERVIEW OF THE 25-ITERATION INVESTIGATION
==========================================
The investigation moved from simple main effects (iter 1-4) -> canonical
treatment-by-biomarker predictive interactions (iter 5-8) -> adjusted main
effects and joint multivariable models (iter 9-12) -> stratified subgroup
hypotheses for each canonical predictive pair (iter 13-17) -> three-way
interactions (iter 18) -> systematic treatment x feature screens (iter 19) ->
two- and three-feature subgroup search (iter 20-21) -> tests for "suppressor"
variables that abolish biomarker-defined treatment effects (iter 22) ->
adjusted predictive interaction models (iter 23) -> ECOG-restricted predictive
subgroup tests (iter 24) -> final confirmed predictive subgroup definitions
(iter 25).

KEY FINDINGS
============

1) Marginal treatment main effects on objective_response (iter 1)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Only enzalutamide showed any marginal main effect. The other five treatments
produced response rates indistinguishable from untreated patients:

  Treatment                   RR treated   RR untreated   diff      p
  treatment_enzalutamide        36.1%        15.9%       +0.202    <1e-100
  treatment_abiraterone         23.8%        24.1%       -0.003     0.470
  treatment_docetaxel           24.0%        24.0%       -0.000     0.914
  treatment_olaparib            23.9%        24.0%       -0.001     0.886
  treatment_lu177_psma          24.3%        24.0%       +0.003     0.600
  treatment_pembrolizumab       23.9%        24.0%       -0.002     0.876

2) Prognostic features for objective_response (iter 2-4)
--------------------------------------------------------
Worse performance status (higher ecog_ps), higher LDH, lower albumin, lower
hemoglobin, higher alkaline phosphatase, higher CRP, higher NLR, mcrpc=1, and
visceral_mets=1 are all individually associated with lower response. The
predictive biomarkers (brca2_mutation, msi_high, ar_v7_positive) and psma_high
are also associated with lower response in unadjusted analyses, largely
because they overlap with the mcrpc/poor-prognosis population.

3) Canonical predictive biomarker hypotheses are NOT supported (iter 5-7, 13-15, 22-24)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The canonical clinical priors (olaparib in BRCA2+, pembrolizumab in MSI-high,
lu177-PSMA in PSMA-high) were tested as both treatment-by-biomarker
interactions and as stratified within-biomarker treatment effects, with and
without adjustment for prognostic covariates. None were supported by the data:

  - Olaparib in BRCA2-mutated (n=529 olap+, n=4,467 olap-):
        RR 12.1% vs 15.3%; diff -0.032; OR 0.76 (95% CI 0.58-1.00); p=0.058.
        The point estimate is in the WRONG direction relative to clinical prior,
        and the adjusted interaction coefficient (-0.29, p=0.045) is also negative.

  - Pembrolizumab in MSI-high (n=79 pem+, n=1,449 pem-):
        RR 17.7% vs 17.6%; diff +0.001; OR 1.01 (95% CI 0.56-1.83); p=1.00.
        Effectively null.

  - Lu177-PSMA in PSMA-high (n=4,486 lu+, n=25,476 lu-):
        RR 23.8% vs 23.9%; diff -0.001; OR 0.99 (95% CI 0.92-1.07); p=0.88.
        Null with tight CI.

Refining these subgroups by ECOG, albumin, visceral mets, LDH, etc. (iter 13-15,
22, 24) did not surface a hidden responder population. Three-way interactions
including ECOG (iter 18) were not significant. The canonical biomarker-defined
predictive subgroups simply do not exist in this cohort.

4) AR-V7 strongly modifies the enzalutamide effect (iter 8)
-----------------------------------------------------------
Enzalutamide x AR-V7 interaction coef = -1.24 (p~1.4e-93). In AR-V7-negative
patients the treatment effect is +25.1%; in AR-V7-positive patients it is
+0.8% (null). This is consistent with AR-V7 as a known resistance mechanism
to AR-targeted therapy. The corresponding abiraterone x AR-V7 interaction was
not significant (the abiraterone main effect was already null).

5) Strong negative interactions of enzalutamide with mcrpc, brca2_mutation,
   msi_high, and high psa_ng_ml (iter 9, 12, 19)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
A systematic treatment x feature interaction screen (iter 19) revealed that
enzalutamide is by far the most modified treatment in the cohort. The
strongest interactions (all p<<0.001) were:

  enz x mcrpc                  coef = -1.98   (p ~ 0)
  enz x ar_v7_positive         coef = -1.24   (p ~ 1.4e-93)
  enz x psa_ng_ml              coef = -0.0087 (p ~ 1.2e-72)
  enz x brca2_mutation         coef = -1.14   (p ~ 3.3e-42)
  enz x msi_high               coef = -1.28   (p ~ 1.8e-19)

In a single joint adjusted logistic-regression model
  objective_response ~ enz*(mcrpc + ar_v7_positive + brca2_mutation + msi_high)
                       + ecog_ps + visceral_mets + albumin_g_dl + ldh_u_l + age_years
all four binary interaction terms remain large, negative, and highly
significant simultaneously:

  enz main coef               +2.611  (p<<0.001)
  enz x mcrpc                 -2.222  (p<<0.001)
  enz x ar_v7_positive        -1.634  (p<<0.001)
  enz x brca2_mutation        -1.554  (p<<0.001)
  enz x msi_high              -1.665  (p<<0.001)

Each unfavorable feature (mcrpc=1, ar_v7=1, brca2=1, msi=1) cancels
substantially all of the enzalutamide main effect on its own; presence of any
one of them collapses the treatment effect.

6) Final best-supported predictive subgroup for enzalutamide (iter 25)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Stratified analyses of enzalutamide effect by progressively narrower subgroups
produced a clean, monotonic increase in the treatment effect:

  Subgroup                                   RR enz      RR no enz    diff
  ALL                                        36.1%       15.9%       +0.202
  mcrpc=0                                    61.0%       16.9%       +0.440
  ar_v7_positive=0                           41.1%       16.0%       +0.251
  mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0               71.7%       17.1%       +0.546
  mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7=0 AND brca2=0            78.0%       17.3%       +0.607
  mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7=0 AND brca2=0 AND msi=0  79.8%       17.2%       +0.626 (n_enz=6,325)

Within the fully-restricted subgroup the enzalutamide-vs-no-enzalutamide
difference is +62.6% absolute and is overwhelmingly significant (p < 1e-100).

7) Treatments with no identifiable responder subgroup (iter 17, 19-21)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177-PSMA, and pembrolizumab show no
positive main effect, no positive adjusted effect in the joint model (iter 11),
no positive predictive interaction with any single feature (iter 19), and no
positive treatment effect in any 2- or 3-binary-feature subgroup constructed
from the strongest candidate modifiers (iter 20-21). The largest positive
2-feature subgroup treatment effect found for any of these five drugs was
small and statistically marginal, and none corresponds to a coherent clinical
hypothesis.

CONCLUSIONS
===========
1. Enzalutamide is the only one of the six treatments in this cohort that
   produces a clinically meaningful increase in objective response rate.

2. The enzalutamide effect is highly heterogeneous and its responder subgroup
   is precisely defined by the conjunction
        mcrpc = 0
        AND ar_v7_positive = 0
        AND brca2_mutation = 0
        AND msi_high = 0
   Within that subgroup the absolute increase in objective response rate is
   approximately 63 percentage points (80% vs 17%). Each of the four
   conditions in the conjunction is a confirmed independent suppressor — any
   one of them is sufficient to abolish most or all of the treatment effect.

3. The dataset CONTRADICTS three canonical clinical predictive-biomarker
   hypotheses: olaparib in BRCA2-mutated, pembrolizumab in MSI-high, and
   lu177-PSMA in PSMA-high. None of these biomarker-defined subgroups shows
   a positive treatment effect, even after ECOG, albumin, LDH, visceral
   metastases, and other covariates are explored as potential effect modifiers.

4. Abiraterone and docetaxel have no demonstrable benefit on objective
   response in this cohort, in any subgroup defined by the available features.

5. The strongest single signal in the dataset is the enzalutamide x mcrpc
   interaction (coef -2.22, p~0 adjusted), which alone explains a large
   fraction of the heterogeneity. AR-V7, BRCA2, and MSI-high are additional
   independent suppressors with similar effect sizes.
