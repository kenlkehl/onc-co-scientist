Analysis Summary — ds001_prostate (50,000 patients)
====================================================

Outcome: objective_response (binary; overall rate 24.0%).
Cohort note: sex_female is constant at 0 (consistent with prostate cancer
cohort). All six treatments occur with overlap; total treatment-flag sum
exceeds 1.0, so polytherapy is present in many patients.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Marginal treatment effects (Iteration 1)
------------------------------------------------------------
Among the six treatments, only treatment_enzalutamide had a substantial
marginal association with objective_response:

  treatment_enzalutamide   rr_treated 0.361 vs rr_untreated 0.159  diff = +0.202  p ≈ 0
  treatment_abiraterone    rr_treated 0.238 vs rr_untreated 0.241  diff = -0.003  p = 0.47
  treatment_docetaxel      rr_treated 0.240 vs rr_untreated 0.240  diff ≈ 0       p = 0.91
  treatment_olaparib       rr_treated 0.239 vs rr_untreated 0.240  diff ≈ 0       p = 0.89
  treatment_lu177_psma     rr_treated 0.243 vs rr_untreated 0.240  diff = +0.003  p = 0.60
  treatment_pembrolizumab  rr_treated 0.239 vs rr_untreated 0.240  diff ≈ 0       p = 0.88

So at the marginal level, five of six therapies appeared inert. This pattern
strongly motivated subgroup heterogeneity searches.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Marginal biomarker / disease-state effects (Iteration 2)
------------------------------------------------------------
Several features were associated with substantially lower response:

  mcrpc            rr 0.154 vs 0.346  diff = -0.192  p ≈ 0
  brca2_mutation   rr 0.150 vs 0.250  diff = -0.101  p = 5.4e-56
  ar_v7_positive   rr 0.160 vs 0.260  diff = -0.101  p = 1.9e-98
  msi_high         rr 0.176 vs 0.242  diff = -0.066  p = 3.0e-9
  visceral_mets    diff = -0.001 (NS)
  psma_high        diff = -0.004 (NS)

mCRPC, BRCA2 mutation, AR-V7 positivity, and MSI-high all marked
worse-prognosis subgroups; visceral mets and PSMA-high did not by themselves
predict response.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Other clinical and lab features (Iterations 3–4)
------------------------------------------------------------
- ECOG PS: each unit increase reduces log-odds of response by 0.285
  (OR = 0.752 per unit, p = 9.5e-73). Strong and clinically expected.
- Gleason score: NS marginally.
- Age: NS marginally.
- Among labs, on a per-SD basis, lower albumin, lower hemoglobin, higher
  LDH, higher CRP, higher NLR, higher weight loss, and higher
  alkaline phosphatase were each associated with lower response — i.e.,
  inflammatory / poor-functional-status biology.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Multivariable model (Iteration 5)
------------------------------------------------------------
A logistic regression of objective_response on all treatments + biomarkers +
clinical covariates + 16 labs confirmed the same picture:

  treatment_enzalutamide  coef +1.20  OR 3.32  p ≈ 0   (the only treatment significant)
  mcrpc                   coef -1.13  OR 0.32  p ≈ 0
  ar_v7_positive          coef -0.69  OR 0.50  p ≈ 1e-108
  brca2_mutation          coef -0.74  OR 0.48  p ≈ 5e-64
  msi_high                coef -0.43  OR 0.65  p = 2.5e-9
  ecog_ps                 coef -0.33  OR 0.72  p = 1.1e-82
  weight_loss_pct_6mo     coef -0.036 (per %) p = 1.7e-33
  albumin_g_dl            coef +0.13  p = 1.8e-8
  psa_ng_ml               coef -0.001 p = 3.3e-8
  crp_mg_l                coef -0.007 p = 8.6e-8

None of abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177_psma, or pembrolizumab had a
significant coefficient even after adjustment.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Canonical treatment × biomarker interactions (Iterations 6–7)
------------------------------------------------------------
We tested the textbook clinically-motivated interactions:

  treatment_enzalutamide × ar_v7_positive  int coef -1.33  p = 1.2e-101  (NEGATIVE — confirmed)
  treatment_enzalutamide × brca2_mutation  int coef -1.21  p = 4.6e-45   (NEGATIVE — novel)
  treatment_enzalutamide × msi_high        int coef -1.37  p = 4.5e-21   (NEGATIVE — novel)
  treatment_olaparib × brca2_mutation      int coef -0.28  p = 0.060     (slight NEGATIVE — opposite of textbook)
  treatment_pembrolizumab × msi_high       interaction NS
  treatment_lu177_psma × psma_high         interaction NS

Stratified within-biomarker comparisons confirmed:
- Olaparib in BRCA2+ patients: rr 0.121 vs 0.153 (diff -0.032, p = 0.058) —
  trends negative, not the expected positive benefit.
- Pembrolizumab in MSI-high (n = 79 treated): diff = +0.001, p = 1.0 — flat.
- Lu177-PSMA in PSMA-high (n = 4,486 treated): diff = -0.001, p = 0.88 — flat.
- Enzalutamide in AR-V7-negative (n = 16,057 treated): rr 0.411 vs 0.160,
  diff = +0.251, p ≈ 0 — large positive effect.
- Enzalutamide in AR-V7-positive: diff = +0.008, p = 0.27 — flat.
- Abiraterone × AR-V7: not significant — no enzalutamide-like benefit unmasked.

The canonical "biomarker-targeted" benefits expected for olaparib (BRCA2),
pembrolizumab (MSI-high), and Lu177-PSMA (PSMA-high) are NOT present in this
dataset. The only treatment showing a real benefit is enzalutamide, and it
benefits the AR-V7-NEGATIVE subgroup (consistent with AR-V7 being a known
resistance marker).

------------------------------------------------------------
6. Comprehensive treatment-by-feature heterogeneity screen (Iteration 8)
------------------------------------------------------------
A screen of every treatment × every available feature interaction surfaced
the strongest modifier candidates:

  enzalutamide:   mcrpc (p ≈ 0), ar_v7_positive (p = 1e-93),
                  brca2_mutation (p = 3e-42), msi_high (p = 2e-19),
                  ecog_ps (p = 2e-5)
  abiraterone:    only brca2_mutation slightly positive (p = 0.03), no
                  consistent modifier
  docetaxel:      no robust modifier (top p ≈ 0.02 — chance-level given many tests)
  olaparib:       brca2_mutation negative (p = 0.045 — direction is OPPOSITE of expected)
  lu177_psma:     visceral_mets negative (p = 0.01) — no positive modifier
  pembrolizumab:  no significant modifier (gleason p = 0.03)

Joint modeling with the top-3 modifiers per treatment (Iteration 9)
confirmed that for enzalutamide, mcrpc, ar_v7_positive, and brca2_mutation
all retain large, highly significant negative interactions even after
mutual adjustment. For all other treatments, no modifier survived
multiple-testing-aware scrutiny in joint models.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. Subgroup search for the other five treatments (Iteration 10)
------------------------------------------------------------
For abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177_psma, and pembrolizumab, we
exhaustively examined 15 prespecified clinical subgroups (mCRPC vs not,
visceral vs not, BRCA2 status, AR-V7 status, MSI status, PSMA status,
ECOG strata). NO subgroup produced a clinically meaningful positive
response improvement for any of these treatments. The only directional
findings are:

  - Olaparib in BRCA2+: diff = -0.032, p = 0.058 (negative trend; opposite of expected)
  - Lu177-PSMA in visceral mets: diff = -0.024, p = 0.043 (negative)

Otherwise all subgroup absolute differences are within chance variation.

------------------------------------------------------------
8. Refined enzalutamide responder subgroup (Iterations 11–12)
------------------------------------------------------------
Progressive subgroup refinement reveals an exceptionally large effect:

  All patients (overall):                                  +0.202 (rr_t 0.361 vs rr_u 0.159)
  Non-mCRPC alone:                                         +0.440 (0.610 vs 0.169)
  Non-mCRPC AND AR-V7 negative:                            +0.546 (0.717 vs 0.171)
  Non-mCRPC AND AR-V7 neg AND BRCA2 negative:              +0.607 (0.780 vs 0.173)
  Non-mCRPC AND AR-V7 neg AND BRCA2 neg AND MSI low:       +0.626 (0.798 vs 0.172)

Conversely, in any patient who has at least one of mCRPC, AR-V7+, BRCA2+,
or MSI-high (n = 34,319), the enzalutamide treatment difference collapses
to +0.008 (rr 0.161 vs 0.153, p = 0.06). Each of the four "suppressor"
features is independently necessary: removing any one from the joint
subgroup definition leaves a residual subgroup of patients who do not
benefit from enzalutamide.

A joint adjusted logistic regression with treatment_enzalutamide × each of
{mcrpc, ar_v7_positive, brca2_mutation, msi_high} as interaction terms
(adjusting for ECOG, age, albumin, LDH, hemoglobin, visceral mets, weight
loss, CRP) yielded:

  treatment_enzalutamide                       coef +2.62, OR 13.79, p ≈ 0
  treatment_enzalutamide × mcrpc               coef -2.23, OR 0.107, p ≈ 0
  treatment_enzalutamide × ar_v7_positive      coef -1.64, OR 0.193, p = 1e-140
  treatment_enzalutamide × brca2_mutation      coef -1.55, OR 0.212, p = 7e-67
  treatment_enzalutamide × msi_high            coef -1.67, OR 0.189, p = 2e-27

A second confirmatory model that defined the joint subgroup as a single
binary indicator (mcrpc=0 & ar_v7_positive=0 & brca2_mutation=0 & msi_high=0)
gave an interaction OR of 18.98 (p ≈ 0) — i.e., the multiplicative effect
of enzalutamide is roughly 19× larger inside the subgroup than outside it.

------------------------------------------------------------
9. Final conclusions (Iteration 13)
------------------------------------------------------------

(a) Hypotheses SUPPORTED by the data
    - mcrpc, brca2_mutation, ar_v7_positive, msi_high, ecog_ps,
      weight_loss_pct_6mo, ldh, crp, nlr each independently predict lower
      objective_response.
    - Higher albumin and higher hemoglobin predict higher response.
    - treatment_enzalutamide markedly increases objective_response, but the
      effect is highly conditional: it is concentrated in the joint subgroup
      defined by mcrpc=0 AND ar_v7_positive=0 AND brca2_mutation=0 AND
      msi_high=0 (≈ 31% of the cohort), where the absolute response benefit
      is +0.626 (from 0.17 to 0.80).
    - AR-V7 positivity strongly attenuates the enzalutamide effect — the
      classical AR-V7-resistance pattern.

(b) Hypotheses REFUTED (or unsupported) by the data
    - The expected positive treatment effect of olaparib in BRCA2-mutated
      patients was NOT observed; the direction was actually slightly negative.
    - The expected positive treatment effect of pembrolizumab in MSI-high
      patients was NOT observed (effectively zero, p = 1.0).
    - The expected positive treatment effect of lu177_psma in PSMA-high
      patients was NOT observed; in visceral-mets patients it was modestly
      negative.
    - Abiraterone and docetaxel had no effect on objective_response in any
      subgroup tested.

(c) Final best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis (per treatment)

    Enzalutamide:
      Patients meeting ALL of (mcrpc=0, ar_v7_positive=0, brca2_mutation=0,
      msi_high=0) gain a large positive treatment effect (absolute +0.626;
      adjusted OR ≈ 19 vs. outside the subgroup). Patients with any of
      mCRPC, AR-V7+, BRCA2+, or MSI-high gain no benefit.

    Abiraterone, docetaxel, olaparib, lu177_psma, pembrolizumab:
      No subgroup with a positive treatment effect on objective_response
      could be identified. The canonical biomarker-targeted benefits
      (olaparib×BRCA2, pembrolizumab×MSI-high, lu177×PSMA-high) are absent
      from this cohort.

End of summary.
