Oncology Dataset Analysis — ds001_nsclc
============================================================

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patients with progression-free survival (pfs_months) as the lone outcome and 33 features (demographics, performance status, disease characteristics, driver mutations, biomarkers, lab values, four annotated treatments). No missing data.

Outcome distribution: pfs_months mean=3.43, median=3.32, range 0.0–15.3.
Treatment prevalence: pembrolizumab 50.0%, sotorasib 35.1%, olaparib 30.0%, osimertinib 30.0%; treatments overlap (mean ≈1.4 treatments per patient).

MAIN EFFECTS (multivariable OLS on pfs_months, R^2=0.894)
------------------------------------------------------------
Demographics & performance:
  age_years +0.1684 months/year (p≈0). Strongest single predictor; older patients have longer PFS in this cohort.
  ecog_ps  -1.101 months/grade (p≈0). Each ECOG grade reduces PFS ~1.1 months.
  sex_female -0.204 months (p≈0). Females have ~0.20 months shorter PFS adjusted.
  smoke_current -0.546 months vs never (p≈0); smoke_former +0.006 (p=0.60).
Disease characteristics:
  stage_iv -1.417 months (p≈0).
  has_brain_mets -0.912 months (p≈0).
  hist_squam -0.723 months vs adenocarcinoma (p≈0).
Driver mutations:
  kras_g12c +0.892 months (p≈0); positive main effect (largely the KRAS+ male sotorasib responders).
  egfr_mutation -0.0005 (p=0.96); null.
  alk_fusion -0.0287 (p=0.06); borderline negative.
  stk11_mutation -0.0192 (p=0.036); small negative.
  brca2_mutation -0.1100 (p=6.59e-09); small negative.
  pdl1_tps +0.0058 (p=0.71); null. tmb_high -0.0054 (p=0.47); null.
Lab/clinical surrogates:
  albumin_g_dl +0.463 months/g·dL (p≈0).
  weight_loss_pct_6mo -0.0732 per % (p≈0).
  ldh_u_l -0.00045/U·L (p=1.50e-28); small negative.
  hemoglobin_g_dl -0.0009, alkaline_phosphatase_u_l -0.00005; other labs (CRP, NLR, AST, ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, Na, K, Ca) had small or null adjusted effects.

TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS
------------------------------------------------------------
  treatment_pembrolizumab adjusted coef = -0.0056 months (p=0.39); null.
  treatment_sotorasib    adjusted coef = +0.3229 months (p≈0); positive overall, but driven entirely by KRAS+ male responders (see below).
  treatment_olaparib     adjusted coef = +0.0024 months (p=0.74); null.
  treatment_osimertinib  adjusted coef = +0.0011 months (p=0.88); null.

PRE-SPECIFIED TREATMENT × BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------
  pembrolizumab × pdl1_tps : coef = +0.009 (p=0.77)  -> NOT supported.
  sotorasib × kras_g12c    : coef = +2.535 (p≈0)     -> STRONGLY supported.
  osimertinib × egfr_mutation : coef = -0.021 (p=0.31) -> NOT supported.
  olaparib × brca2_mutation   : coef = -0.007 (p=0.87) -> NOT supported.
Strikingly, of the four canonical drug–biomarker pairings, only sotorasib×KRAS-G12C reproduces the expected effect.

STRATIFIED CONFIRMATION OF SOTORASIB BENEFIT
------------------------------------------------------------
  KRAS-G12C+ (n=6,368): sotorasib on-vs-off = +2.55 months (p≈0).
  KRAS-G12C-: -0.005 months (p=0.83); no benefit.

HETEROGENEITY SEARCH (TREATMENT × ALL FEATURES)
------------------------------------------------------------
Sotorasib HTE (full cohort): top interactions (in addition to kras_g12c) — sex_female -0.585 (p≈0), smoke_current +0.254, alk_fusion -0.343, egfr_mutation -0.208, brca2_mutation -0.328. These are largely driven by structural correlations with kras_g12c status. Within KRAS+, almost all of these collapse, revealing sex_female as the true effect modifier.
Pembrolizumab HTE: best interaction nlr (coef=-0.009, p=0.003) — small, unlikely meaningful given many tests. Otherwise null.
Osimertinib HTE: best has_brain_mets (coef=+0.033, p=0.04). No robust signal.
Olaparib HTE: best sodium_meq_l (coef=+0.005, p=0.05). No robust signal.

SOTORASIB SUBGROUP REFINEMENT — KRAS+ × SEX
------------------------------------------------------------
Within KRAS-G12C+ patients, the strongest effect modifier of sotorasib was sex_female (interaction coef = -4.58, p≈0).
Stratified Welch t-tests:
  KRAS-G12C+ males   (n=3,508): on=7.876 vs off=3.232  -> +4.64 months (p≈0).
  KRAS-G12C+ females (n=2,860): on=3.179 vs off=3.191  -> -0.01 months (p=0.86); NO benefit.
Sotorasib in KRAS-G12C-negative patients (regardless of sex): difference ~0 in both sexes (each |diff|<0.02 months, p>0.5).
Within KRAS+ males, the benefit is large in every age quartile (range +4.07 to +4.92 months).
Within KRAS+ females, the benefit is essentially zero in every age quartile (|diff|<0.07 months).

THREE-WAY MODEL (treatment_sotorasib × kras_g12c × sex_female)
------------------------------------------------------------
  kras_male_sotor (KRAS+ AND male AND on sotorasib) coef = +4.5934 months (p≈0).
  kras_female_sotor (KRAS+ AND female AND on sotorasib) coef = +0.0074 months (p=0.59); null.
This confirms that sotorasib’s benefit is entirely concentrated in KRAS+ males.

TREE-BASED UPLIFT (T-LEARNER, sotorasib)
------------------------------------------------------------
Top uplift quintile (n=7486): 67% KRAS+, 55% male; observed sotorasib on-vs-off = +1.715 months (p=1.54e-147).
Independent decision-tree uplift discovery confirms the analytic finding.

FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESES
------------------------------------------------------------
• treatment_sotorasib improves pfs_months ONLY in patients with kras_g12c=1 AND sex_female=0; in this subgroup the effect is +4.64 months (p≈0). In KRAS+ females, KRAS- males, or KRAS- females, the effect is null.
• treatment_pembrolizumab has NO PFS benefit, including within pdl1_tps>=0.5 or tmb_high=1 (adjusted coefs all near zero, p>>0.05).
• treatment_osimertinib has NO PFS benefit, including within egfr_mutation=1.
• treatment_olaparib has NO PFS benefit, including within brca2_mutation=1.

OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
------------------------------------------------------------
This cohort recapitulates the canonical sotorasib/KRAS-G12C interaction but reveals an unexpected, large sex disparity: KRAS+ females derive zero benefit from sotorasib while KRAS+ males derive a 4.6-month PFS gain. The other three biomarker-targeted therapies (pembrolizumab, osimertinib, olaparib) show null PFS effects even in their canonical biomarker subgroups, consistent with a dataset in which only the sotorasib/KRAS axis carries a real treatment signal. The non-treatment predictors of PFS are dominated by age (positive), ECOG, stage IV, brain metastases, albumin, weight loss, smoking status, histology, and KRAS status, which together explain ~89% of variance in PFS. The sex disparity in sotorasib response is the most clinically striking finding and warrants confirmation before action.
