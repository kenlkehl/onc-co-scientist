Oncology Dataset Analysis Summary — ds001_nsclc
===============================================

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patients with 32 baseline features and a single continuous
outcome `pfs_months` (mean 3.43, SD 2.23, range 0–15.3).

Treatments: pembrolizumab (n=25,008), sotorasib (17,544), olaparib (14,999),
osimertinib (14,976). Patients can receive multiple treatments (about 32%
receive two, 12% three, 1.5% all four). Treatment assignment is essentially
balanced on baseline covariates (mean differences in age/ECOG/stage/brain
mets/sex_female all <0.02 for every treatment), supporting a causal
interpretation of within-stratum effects.

==================================================================
1. Marginal treatment main effects on pfs_months (Iteration 1)
==================================================================
Only sotorasib shows a marginal main effect: +0.32 months over no-sotorasib
(p=1.6e-53). Pembrolizumab (-0.035, p=0.08), olaparib (-0.025, p=0.25), and
osimertinib (+0.004, p=0.87) are null. The sotorasib marginal effect is driven
entirely by a strong subgroup — see section 4.

==================================================================
2. Prognostic factors (Iteration 2)
==================================================================
The strongest determinants of pfs_months are demographic and clinical:

  * age_years          r=+0.757 (Spearman r=+0.769) — STRONGLY POSITIVE.
                       Mean PFS rises monotonically and convexly from
                       0.08 mo at 30–40 y to 6.91 mo at 80–90 y. This is
                       OPPOSITE the usual clinical direction and is one of
                       the dominant signals in the dataset.
  * ecog_ps            mean PFS 4.33 / 3.18 / 2.15 for ECOG 0/1/2.
  * stage_iv           -1.44 months for stage IV.
  * has_brain_mets     -0.92 months.
  * histology          squamous -0.86 months vs adenocarcinoma.
  * smoking_status     current 2.99 / former 3.61 / never 3.67 (current
                       markedly worse).
  * albumin_g_dl       r=+0.099 (higher = better).
  * weight_loss_pct_6mo r=-0.127 (more loss = worse).
  * ldh_u_l            r=-0.019 (small but significant).
  * sex_female         females -0.20 months marginally; this difference is
                       driven entirely by the loss of sotorasib benefit in
                       KRAS-G12C+ females (see section 5) and is non-significant
                       once treatment×kras_g12c×sex_female is modeled (adjusted
                       sex_female coefficient -0.0004, p=0.92).

Notable null prognostics: pdl1_tps (r=+0.006, p=0.16), nlr, crp_mg_l, and most
hepatic/renal/electrolyte labs (all |r|<0.01 vs PFS).

==================================================================
3. Biomarker main effects (Iteration 3)
==================================================================
  * egfr_mutation     +0.19 months (p=2e-10) — small.
  * kras_g12c         +0.78 months (p=5e-151) — but this is essentially the
                      sotorasib×KRAS interaction averaged in.
  * alk_fusion        -0.81 months (p=3e-69).
  * brca2_mutation    -0.12 months (p=0.04).
  * stk11_mutation    +0.01 months (NS).
  * tmb_high          -0.19 months (p=9e-18) — direction OPPOSITE textbook
                      expectation.
  * pdl1_tps          essentially null marginally (r=+0.006).

After adjusting for prognostic factors and treatment-by-biomarker interactions
(section 6), every mutation/biomarker main effect except age, ECOG, stage,
brain mets, histology, smoking, albumin, weight loss, and LDH falls to ~0.

==================================================================
4. Classic targeted-therapy interactions (Iteration 4)
==================================================================
Among the four obvious treatment×biomarker pairings, only one is real:

  * SOTORASIB × KRAS_G12C  interaction +2.56 months (p≈4e-294). SUPPORTED.
        Stratified means: KRAS+ sotorasib=1 mean 5.77 vs sotorasib=0 mean 3.21
        (diff +2.55); KRAS- sotorasib=1 mean 3.33 vs =0 mean 3.33 (diff 0).

  * PEMBROLIZUMAB × PDL1_TPS  interaction -0.12 (p=0.22). REFUTED.
        At PDL1≥0.7 pembro is actually slightly harmful (diff -0.28, p=0.018).
  * PEMBROLIZUMAB × TMB_HIGH  interaction -0.018 (p=0.69). REFUTED.
  * OLAPARIB × BRCA2_MUTATION  interaction +0.04 (p=0.78). REFUTED.
        Within BRCA2+ (n=1,519): olaparib diff +0.010 months (p=0.93).
  * OSIMERTINIB × EGFR_MUTATION  interaction -0.02 (p=0.76). REFUTED.
        Within EGFR+ (n=6,517): osimertinib diff -0.014 months (p=0.81).
  * OSIMERTINIB × ALK_FUSION  interaction +0.24 (p=0.017). Statistically
        significant but absolute benefit within ALK+ is only +0.23 months
        (mean 2.82 vs 2.59) — not clinically meaningful.

The pattern is striking: in this synthetic-EHR cohort, three of the four
biomarker-targeted matches that would normally drive practice (PDL1→pembro,
BRCA2→olaparib, EGFR→osimertinib) produce no PFS benefit, while
KRAS-G12C→sotorasib is the only true interaction.

==================================================================
5. Sotorasib treatment-effect heterogeneity within KRAS G12C+
   (Iterations 5–7)
==================================================================
Within the n=6,368 KRAS G12C+ patients, a systematic interaction screen
(treatment_sotorasib × each candidate feature) ranked by smallest interaction
p-value yielded three "killer" modifiers that each subtract roughly the entire
sotorasib effect:

  feature           interaction coef   p-value
  --------          ----------------   --------
  sex_female              -4.66        <1e-300
  alk_fusion              -3.02         3e-18
  brca2_mutation          -2.55         2e-10

Stratified verification within KRAS G12C+:
  * Males (sex_female=0):     sotorasib diff = +4.64 (mean 7.88 vs 3.23, n=3,508)
  * Females (sex_female=1):   sotorasib diff = -0.01 (mean 3.18 vs 3.19, n=2,860)
  * Males with alk_fusion=1:  sotorasib diff = -0.29 (n=138)
  * Males with brca2=1:       sotorasib diff = -0.07 (n=107)

In the joint multivariable model within KRAS G12C+ patients (also containing
age), the treatment_sotorasib × sex_female / × alk_fusion / × brca2_mutation
interactions are simultaneously highly significant (-4.54, -2.53, -2.49, all
p<1e-10). Each "killer" feature is sufficient on its own to abolish the
sotorasib benefit.

==================================================================
6. Final actionable subgroup (Iteration 7 / 10)
==================================================================
The clean subgroup definition for sotorasib responders is:

      kras_g12c = 1
   AND sex_female = 0   (i.e., males)
   AND alk_fusion = 0
   AND brca2_mutation = 0

In this subgroup (n=3,266; sotorasib=1: 1,154; sotorasib=0: 2,112):

      mean PFS sotorasib = 1:  8.234 months (SD 2.27)
      mean PFS sotorasib = 0:  3.249 months (SD 2.07)
      Diff = +4.985 months,   t-test p < 1e-300

The +5-month effect is robust across ECOG strata (+5.00, +5.08, +4.73 for
ECOG 0/1/2) and across histology (+5.09 adenoca, +4.85 squamous). In the
complement subgroup ("any_killer = 1"; n=3,102), the sotorasib effect is
essentially zero (-0.019, NS).

==================================================================
7. Heterogeneity searches for the other treatments (Iteration 8 / 10)
==================================================================
For each of pembrolizumab, olaparib, osimertinib I ran:
  (a) the canonical treatment×biomarker interaction (section 4);
  (b) full TEH univariate screens across all candidate features;
  (c) exhaustive 2-way subgroup screens across pairs of binary features;
  (d) joint OLS within the canonical biomarker-positive subgroup with
      sex/alk/brca2/stk11 interaction terms.

For pembrolizumab the largest subgroup effect anywhere was +0.15 months
(EGFR+ females, p=0.05), and PDL1 quartile means show pembro diff -0.003,
-0.027, -0.038, -0.072 (Q1→Q4) — i.e., pembro is slightly harmful at the
highest PDL1 levels. For olaparib, the largest effect was within
{egfr_mutation=1 & stk11_mutation=1} = +0.33 months (p=0.026, n=980); within
BRCA2+ the effect is null. For osimertinib, the only signals were
{kras_g12c=1 & alk_fusion=1} +0.67 (n=259) and {stage_iv=1 & brca2=1} -0.38
(n=976), both isolated and not consistent with a target-driven mechanism.

Conclusion: across all four treatments, the only clinically meaningful and
statistically robust PFS effect is sotorasib in KRAS G12C+ males without
ALK fusion or BRCA2 mutation. The other three treatments (pembrolizumab,
olaparib, osimertinib) contribute essentially nothing to PFS in this cohort
in any reasonably sized subgroup.

==================================================================
8. Adjusted multivariable model (Iteration 9)
==================================================================
A consolidated OLS model:
   pfs_months ~ age + age² + sex_female + smoking + ECOG + histology
              + stage_iv + brain_mets + egfr + alk + stk11 + brca2 + tmb
              + pdl1 + albumin + weight_loss + ldh + nlr + crp
              + treatment_pembrolizumab + treatment_olaparib + treatment_osimertinib
              + treatment_sotorasib*kras_g12c*sex_female
              + treatment_sotorasib:alk_fusion + treatment_sotorasib:brca2_mutation
explains R²=0.977 of the variance.

Key adjusted coefficients on pfs_months:
  age_years                                   +0.014  per year (linear)
  age_years²                                  +0.0012 per year² (convex)
  ecog_ps                                     -1.104  per level
  stage_iv                                    -1.416
  has_brain_mets                              -0.908
  histology=squamous                          -0.726
  smoking_status=former                       +0.553 (vs current)
  smoking_status=never                        +0.561 (vs current)
  albumin_g_dl                                +0.462 per g/dL
  weight_loss_pct_6mo                         -0.073 per %
  ldh_u_l                                     -0.0005 per U/L
  treatment_pembrolizumab                     -0.0022 (NS)
  treatment_olaparib                          +0.0033 (NS)
  treatment_osimertinib                       +0.0017 (NS)
  treatment_sotorasib                         +0.022  (NS — base in non-KRAS male)
  treatment_sotorasib:kras_g12c               +4.588
  treatment_sotorasib:kras_g12c:sex_female    -4.586  (cancels the benefit in females)
  treatment_sotorasib:alk_fusion              -0.264
  treatment_sotorasib:brca2_mutation          -0.367
  egfr / alk / stk11 / brca2 / pdl1 / tmb     all NS after adjustment

The mutation/biomarker main effects observed univariately disappear once age,
ECOG, stage, brain mets, histology, smoking, albumin, weight loss, and LDH
are controlled, indicating they were marginal correlates rather than
independent prognostic factors. The exception is the sotorasib×KRAS×sex
interaction, which remains highly significant.

==================================================================
9. Hypotheses supported vs refuted
==================================================================
SUPPORTED:
  * sotorasib × kras_g12c improves PFS (+2.56 marginal interaction).
  * Within KRAS+, sex_female / alk_fusion / brca2_mutation each fully
    abolish the sotorasib benefit.
  * Final subgroup (KRAS+ AND male AND no ALK AND no BRCA2): sotorasib
    increases PFS by +5.0 months.
  * Strong prognostic effects of age (POSITIVE direction), ECOG, stage IV,
    brain mets, histology, smoking, albumin, weight loss, LDH.
  * Treatment assignment is balanced across covariates.

REFUTED:
  * pembrolizumab × pdl1_tps interaction (interaction is slightly negative,
    NS; high PDL1 patients do slightly worse on pembro).
  * pembrolizumab × tmb_high interaction.
  * olaparib × brca2_mutation interaction (BRCA2+ olaparib stratum diff = +0.01,
    p=0.93).
  * osimertinib × egfr_mutation interaction (EGFR+ osimertinib stratum diff
    = -0.014, p=0.81).
  * pdl1_tps as a prognostic factor.
  * tmb_high as a favorable prognostic factor (it is mildly UNFAVORABLE here).
  * Any meaningful subgroup-specific PFS benefit of pembrolizumab, olaparib,
    or osimertinib (no subgroup with effect >0.4 months passes a sensible
    multiple-comparisons threshold).

==================================================================
10. Overall conclusions
==================================================================
This cohort shows one — and only one — treatment-biomarker matching that
delivers a clinically meaningful PFS benefit:

   sotorasib in KRAS G12C+ patients, restricted to males without
   coexistent ALK fusion and without BRCA2 mutation, gain ≈ +5 months
   of mean PFS over no-sotorasib (8.23 vs 3.25 months).

The three other treatments (pembrolizumab, olaparib, osimertinib) and the
three other biomarker matchings (PDL1, BRCA2, EGFR) that would normally
drive precision-oncology decision making appear inert in this cohort.
Several "natural" prognostic patterns (age direction, TMB direction, female
disadvantage) run opposite to clinical experience, suggesting this is a
synthetic dataset whose generative mechanisms encode a single dominant
treatment-effect subgroup plus deterministic prognostic relationships
governed primarily by age, performance status, stage, brain involvement,
histology, smoking, nutritional status (albumin, weight loss), and LDH.
