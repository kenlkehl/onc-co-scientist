Analysis Summary — Dataset ds001_nsclc (50,000 NSCLC patient records)
======================================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (range 0.0 - 15.3, median 3.32, mean 3.43).
Treatments (independently assigned, may co-occur): treatment_pembrolizumab,
treatment_sotorasib, treatment_olaparib, treatment_osimertinib.

The analysis followed a 25-iteration propose-test-refine protocol. Below is a
narrative synthesis of the hypotheses explored, what the statistical evidence
showed (direction, magnitude, significance), which hypotheses were supported
vs. refuted, and the final picture of treatment-biomarker-outcome
relationships.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Treatment main effects on PFS (overall cohort)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
- treatment_pembrolizumab: mean PFS 3.41 (on) vs 3.45 (off); diff = -0.035,
  p = 0.08. NOT significant. Refuted positive main-effect hypothesis.
- treatment_sotorasib: 3.64 vs 3.32; diff = +0.32, p = 1.6e-53. SIGNIFICANT
  positive. Supported.
- treatment_olaparib: 3.41 vs 3.44; diff = -0.025, p = 0.26. Not significant.
- treatment_osimertinib: 3.43 vs 3.43; diff = +0.003, p = 0.87. Not significant.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Baseline prognostic features
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Strong negative prognostic associations (shorter PFS):
- ecog_ps: ECOG 0 mean 4.33; ECOG 1 -1.14 (p<<0.001); ECOG 2 -2.17 (p<<0.001).
- stage_iv == 1: -1.44 months (p ~ 0).
- has_brain_mets == 1: -0.92 months (p ~ 0).
- histology = squamous (vs adeno): -0.86 months (p ~ 0).
- smoking_status = current (vs former/never): ~ -0.62 months.
- sex_female == 1: -0.20 months (p = 2.4e-24).
- alk_fusion == 1: -0.81 months (p = 3e-69).
- brca2_mutation == 1: -0.12 months (p = 0.04, marginal).
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: r = -0.13 (p = 7e-179) — modest negative.

Strong positive prognostic associations (longer PFS):
- age_years: r = +0.76; +0.168 months/year in adjusted multivariable model.
  This is opposite to typical clinical expectation and is a feature of the
  cohort itself (older patients have longer PFS even after adjusting for
  ECOG, stage, brain mets, labs, and treatments).
- albumin_g_dl: r = +0.10 (p = 3e-109).
- kras_g12c == 1: +0.78 months (largely mediated by sotorasib in carriers).
- egfr_mutation == 1: +0.19 months (p = 2e-10), but no osimertinib component.

Null associations:
- pdl1_tps with PFS overall (r = 0.006, p = 0.16).
- stk11_mutation (overall): essentially null.
- A wide range of lab values (LDH, CRP, NLR, hemoglobin, ALP, AST, ALT,
  bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium): correlations
  with PFS all |r| < 0.02 — essentially no signal.
- tmb_high: -0.19 months (p = 9e-18) — slightly NEGATIVE prognostic, opposite
  to typical IO-treatment-related expectation.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Treatment-by-biomarker interactions (canonical matches)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
- Sotorasib × kras_g12c: interaction coef = +2.56 (p ~ 0). KRAS G12C+/sot
  mean PFS 5.77 vs KRAS G12C+/no-sot 3.21 (delta +2.56). KRAS WT cells:
  3.33 (off) vs 3.33 (on) — no effect. STRONGLY SUPPORTED.
- Olaparib × brca2_mutation: interaction coef = +0.04 (p = 0.78). BRCA2+
  cells: on 3.32 vs off 3.31 (essentially identical). REFUTED.
- Osimertinib × egfr_mutation: interaction coef = -0.02 (p = 0.76). EGFR+
  cells: on 3.59 vs off 3.60. REFUTED — surprising and clinically unusual.
- Pembrolizumab × pdl1_tps (continuous): interaction coef = -0.12 (p = 0.22).
  Trend is negative, not positive. REFUTED.
- Pembrolizumab × tmb_high: interaction coef = -0.02 (p = 0.69). REFUTED.
- Pembrolizumab in PD-L1 >= 50% subgroup: -0.07 (p = 0.11). No benefit.
- Pembrolizumab in PD-L1 >= 70%: -0.28 (p = 0.018). Slightly NEGATIVE.
- Pembrolizumab in adeno never-smoker high-PD-L1: -0.02 (p = 0.86). No benefit.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
4. The dominant heterogeneity signal: sotorasib × KRAS G12C × sex
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The most striking finding in the cohort is a sex-modified KRAS-specific
sotorasib effect:

Cell means in the sot × kras × sex three-way table:
  sex=0 (male),    sot=0, kras_g12c=0: 3.33 (n=15582)
  sex=0 (male),    sot=0, kras_g12c=1: 3.23 (n=2271)
  sex=0 (male),    sot=1, kras_g12c=0: 3.32 (n=8539)
  sex=0 (male),    sot=1, kras_g12c=1: 7.88 (n=1237)   <-- huge gain
  sex=1 (female),  sot=0, kras_g12c=0: 3.33 (n=12752)
  sex=1 (female),  sot=0, kras_g12c=1: 3.19 (n=1851)
  sex=1 (female),  sot=1, kras_g12c=0: 3.35 (n=6759)
  sex=1 (female),  sot=1, kras_g12c=1: 3.18 (n=1009)   <-- no effect

Triple interaction sot × kras × sex_female: coef = -4.69, p ~ 0.

In male KRAS G12C+ (n=3508): sotorasib +4.644 months (crude) / +4.584
(adjusted for age, ECOG, stage, brain mets, albumin, weight loss, histology,
smoking, all other treatments and biomarkers, plus full lab panel) (p ~ 0).

In female KRAS G12C+ (n=2860): sotorasib -0.012 months (p = 0.88).

The effect in male KRAS G12C+ is highly homogeneous across substrata:
- ECOG 0/1/2 deltas: +4.57 / +4.77 / +4.53
- stage_iv 0/1 deltas: +4.71 / +4.62
- brain_mets 0/1 deltas: +4.70 / +4.47
- adeno/squamous deltas: +4.67 / +4.64
- STK11 WT/mut deltas: +4.67 / +4.49

The effect grows mildly with age (sot × age in male KRAS+: +0.03 months/year,
p ~ 2e-9), and pembrolizumab co-administration produces a small antagonistic
effect (sot × pembro = -0.37, p = 0.023), but neither materially changes the
subgroup definition.

The two-predicate subgroup definition (kras_g12c == 1 AND sex_female == 0)
is sufficient and parsimonious — adding more predicates does not improve
the fit or reduce the residual treatment effect.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Other treatment-effect heterogeneity searches
----------------------------------------------------------------------
For each of the four treatments, we ran (a) treatment × feature interaction
screens across all 27 numeric features and 2 categorical features, (b) an
exhaustive 2-binary-feature subgroup grid search, and (c) targeted canonical-
biomarker subgroups.

Pembrolizumab:
  - Best positive 2-feature subgroup: stk11+/brca2+ diff = +0.32 (p = 0.24,
    n = 231) — NOT significant.
  - All canonical IO subgroups (PD-L1 >= 50%, >= 70%, adeno never-smoker)
    show no positive benefit; very-high PD-L1 actually shows a small
    negative effect.
  - CONCLUSION: no robust positive subgroup for pembrolizumab.

Olaparib:
  - Best positive 2-feature subgroup: egfr+/stk11+ diff = +0.33 (p = 0.026,
    n = 980); biologically implausible.
  - BRCA2+ patients on olaparib have nearly identical PFS to BRCA2+ off
    olaparib (+0.04, p = 0.78).
  - CONCLUSION: no robust positive subgroup for olaparib.

Osimertinib:
  - Only ALK fusion produces consistent (small) positive signals: ALK+
    crude diff = +0.231 (p = 0.018); adjusted within ALK+ = +0.039
    (p = 0.049). No benefit in EGFR+.
  - CONCLUSION: small (likely real but very modest) ALK-restricted effect;
    biologically unexpected since osimertinib is an EGFR TKI clinically.

Sotorasib (already detailed above):
  - Single decisive subgroup: kras_g12c == 1 AND sex_female == 0.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
6. Drug-drug interactions
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Pairwise treatment × treatment interactions in the overall cohort produced
no signal larger than 0.05 months with p < 0.1. Within the male KRAS G12C+
subgroup, pembrolizumab attenuates the sotorasib effect by ~0.37 months
(p = 0.023) — a small antagonistic interaction; olaparib and osimertinib
do not interact with sotorasib in this subgroup.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
7. Hypotheses supported vs refuted
----------------------------------------------------------------------
SUPPORTED:
- Sotorasib has a strong positive PFS effect specifically in KRAS G12C+
  patients (interaction +2.56 months, p ~ 0).
- Within KRAS G12C+, the sotorasib effect is entirely confined to males
  (sex_female == 0): +4.6 months in males vs 0 in females.
- ECOG performance status, stage IV, brain metastases, squamous histology,
  current smoking, low albumin, weight loss, and sex female are independent
  negative prognostic features.
- Older age is a positive prognostic feature in this cohort.
- A small osimertinib effect exists in ALK-fusion+ patients.

REFUTED:
- Pembrolizumab has any positive main effect or any positive subgroup
  effect (including PD-L1 high, TMB high, ECOG 0, never-smoker adeno, etc.).
- Olaparib improves PFS in BRCA2-mutant patients (or any subgroup).
- Osimertinib improves PFS in EGFR-mutant patients.
- TMB-high patients have longer PFS (they have slightly shorter PFS).
- PD-L1 expression predicts pembrolizumab benefit (it does not).
- The sotorasib × KRAS effect is uniform across sex.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
8. Best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis (per-treatment)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
- Sotorasib: PRIMARY POSITIVE SUBGROUP = `kras_g12c == 1 AND sex_female == 0`.
  Signed effect on pfs_months = +4.64 months (crude) / +4.58 months
  (multivariable adjusted), p ~ 0. The complete subgroup definition includes
  sex_female == 0 as a required predicate; without it, sotorasib has no
  effect on the female half of the KRAS G12C+ population.

- Osimertinib: secondary, small positive subgroup = `alk_fusion == 1`.
  Signed effect ~ +0.23 months (crude) / +0.04 (adjusted), p = 0.018-0.049.

- Pembrolizumab: no supported beneficial subgroup.

- Olaparib: no supported beneficial subgroup.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
9. Overall conclusions
----------------------------------------------------------------------
This NSCLC cohort exhibits exactly one major treatment-effect heterogeneity
signal: a large, robust sotorasib benefit confined to the conjunction of
two predicates — KRAS G12C mutation positivity AND male sex. The KRAS G12C
matching of sotorasib is consistent with the drug's mechanism, but the
absolute exclusion of female KRAS G12C+ patients from any benefit is
striking and not biologically expected. None of the other three drugs
(pembrolizumab, olaparib, osimertinib) shows the canonical biomarker-
matched benefit one would expect clinically; only osimertinib shows a
small, possibly real, positive effect in ALK-fusion+ patients (which is
also not biologically expected for an EGFR TKI).

Baseline prognostic features behave largely as clinically expected (ECOG,
stage, brain mets, histology, smoking, sex, albumin, weight loss, ALK
fusion are all negative; age is positive in this cohort, opposite to
typical clinical experience).
