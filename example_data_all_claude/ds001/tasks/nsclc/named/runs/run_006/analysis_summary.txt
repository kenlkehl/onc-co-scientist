ds001_nsclc — Analysis summary
==============================

Cohort: 50,000 NSCLC patients with one outcome (pfs_months) and 33 features
spanning demographics (age, sex, smoking status), disease descriptors (ECOG,
histology, stage IV, brain metastases), molecular biomarkers (egfr, kras_g12c,
alk_fusion, stk11, brca2, pdl1_tps, tmb_high), 12 routine labs (albumin, LDH,
weight loss%, CRP, NLR, hemoglobin, alkaline phosphatase, AST, ALT, bilirubin,
creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium), and four binary treatments
(pembrolizumab, sotorasib, olaparib, osimertinib). Treatments are not mutually
exclusive (mean treatment count ≈ 1.5; ~16% of patients receive none) and their
assignment is essentially uncorrelated with the canonical biomarker for each
agent (e.g. pembrolizumab is given in ~50% of every PD-L1 quartile, sotorasib
in ~35% of both KRAS G12C+ and KRAS-wt patients), so apparent treatment-by-
biomarker effects below should reflect biology, not selection.

I worked through 25 iterations: marginal main effects (iters 1-8), treatment
main effects (iter 9), single-axis biomarker interactions for each treatment
(iters 10-15), full multivariable models with and without treatment terms
(iters 16-17), exhaustive heterogeneity scans for each treatment (iters 18-20),
multi-axis compound subgroup tests (iters 21-23), ECOG-stratified treatment
effects (iter 24), and a final consolidated set of best-supported subgroup
definitions for each treatment (iter 25).

Main effects on PFS (iters 1-8)
-------------------------------
Demographics. Older age is paradoxically associated with LONGER PFS
(+0.170 mo per year, p<<10^-300) — opposite of the prior, possibly reflecting
selection for less aggressive biology in older patients. Female sex is
associated with shorter PFS (-0.204 mo, p=7.7e-25). Current smokers fare worst
(2.99 vs 3.61 former vs 3.68 never; ANOVA p=3.7e-187).

Disease severity. As expected, ECOG (-1.10 mo per point), stage IV (-1.44 mo)
and brain metastases (-0.92 mo) are the largest single predictors of shorter
PFS, all p<<10^-300. Squamous histology is associated with -0.86 mo vs
adenocarcinoma (p<<10^-300).

Mutations / biomarkers. EGFR mutation is associated with slightly longer PFS
(+0.19 mo, p=6.5e-11). KRAS G12C is associated with LONGER PFS (+0.78 mo,
p=7e-94) — the opposite direction from textbook expectation, but explained by
sotorasib (see below). ALK fusion is associated with markedly SHORTER PFS
(-0.81 mo, p=5.6e-66) — this is the opposite of natural-history expectations
and reflects that no ALK inhibitor is included among the four available
treatments. STK11 mutation has no marginal effect (p=0.74). BRCA2 mutation has
a small unfavorable marginal effect (-0.12 mo, p=0.03). TMB high is
unfavorable (-0.19 mo, p=1e-17), again opposite to expectations and reflecting
that pembrolizumab does not work in this dataset (see below). PD-L1 TPS has no
marginal effect (p=0.16).

Labs. Albumin is the dominant favorable lab (+0.44 mo per g/dL, p=2.8e-109).
Weight-loss percent is unfavorable (-0.073 mo per percentage point,
p=7.5e-179). LDH is mildly unfavorable (p=3.6e-5). CRP, NLR, hemoglobin,
alkaline phosphatase, AST, ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, Na, K, and Ca all
show no marginal association with PFS (all p>0.14).

Treatment main effects (iter 9)
-------------------------------
Pembrolizumab: -0.035 mo, p=0.08 — null overall (numerically slightly negative).
Sotorasib:    +0.322 mo, p=5.6e-49 — significant overall, but driven entirely
              by the KRAS G12C+ subgroup (next).
Olaparib:     -0.025 mo, p=0.26 — null overall.
Osimertinib:  +0.004 mo, p=0.87 — null overall.

Treatment-by-target-biomarker interactions (iters 10-15)
--------------------------------------------------------
Pembrolizumab × PD-L1 TPS:                interaction beta=-0.116, p=0.22 (NS).
                                          PD-L1 high pembro 3.41 vs 3.48 (-0.07).
Pembrolizumab × TMB high:                 beta=-0.018, p=0.68 (NS).
Pembrolizumab × STK11:                    beta=-0.016, p=0.77 (NS).
Sotorasib × KRAS G12C:                    beta=+2.558, p≈0. KRAS+: sotorasib
                                          5.77 vs control 3.21 (+2.55 mo,
                                          p=1.7e-202, n=2246 vs 4122). KRAS-:
                                          -0.005 mo, p=0.80.
Olaparib × BRCA2:                         beta=+0.037, p=0.78 (NS). BRCA2+:
                                          +0.010 mo, p=0.93.
Osimertinib × EGFR:                       beta=-0.020, p=0.76 (NS). EGFR+:
                                          -0.014 mo, p=0.81.

The single dominant finding of the dataset is the sotorasib × KRAS G12C
interaction. In contrast, the textbook biomarker pairings for pembrolizumab
(PD-L1, TMB), olaparib (BRCA2), and osimertinib (EGFR) all show null
treatment effects in their canonical subgroups in this cohort.

Multivariable models (iters 16-17)
----------------------------------
A clinical/biomarker/lab OLS (no treatment terms, 29 predictors) explains
R² = 0.889 of PFS variance. The dominant coefficients are stage_iv (-1.42),
ecog_ps (-1.10), has_brain_mets (-0.91), kras_g12c (+0.89), squamous
histology (-0.72), former-smoker (+0.55), never-smoker (+0.55), albumin
(+0.46), sex_female (-0.21), age_years (+0.168/yr), brca2 (-0.11), and
weight_loss% (-0.073). ALK fusion (-0.030, p=0.054) and STK11 (-0.020,
p=0.03) survive but with tiny coefficients.

Adding the four treatments and their canonical interactions raises R² to
0.927. The only treatment-related term that retains a meaningful effect is
treatment_sotorasib:kras_g12c (+2.535, p≈0). All pembrolizumab terms have
|beta|<0.003 and p>0.87. olaparib and osimertinib terms are similarly null
(osimertinib×has_brain_mets is borderline at p=0.055, but the marginal
benefit even in the EGFR+/brain+ stratum is +0.051 mo, p=0.64 — not
clinically meaningful).

Heterogeneity scans (iters 18-20)
---------------------------------
Sotorasib: a univariate scan over 13 modifiers found, in addition to the
dominant kras_g12c interaction, several smaller "interactions" with
sex_female (-0.56), never-smoker (-0.50), alk_fusion (-0.49), former-smoker
(-0.21), egfr_mutation (-0.24), squamous (+0.17), and brca2 (-0.33). These
are all consistent with a single underlying mechanism: in subgroups where
KRAS G12C is rare or absent, the average sotorasib effect is diluted toward
the KRAS-wt null. Within KRAS G12C+ patients alone the sotorasib effect is
uniform across these strata.

Pembrolizumab: in the same scan, no modifier reached p<0.005. The smallest
were stage_iv (+0.108, p=6.8e-3), kras_g12c (-0.113, p=0.057), and
egfr_mutation (+0.099, p=0.10). No modifier identifies a positive
pembrolizumab subgroup.

Olaparib: no interaction at p<0.05 (smallest stk11 0.073, squamous 0.106).
Osimertinib: smallest interactions are alk_fusion (+0.24, p=0.017) and
brca2 (-0.25, p=0.050) — small effects with no clinical rationale and
likely chance given multiplicity. No subgroup of olaparib or osimertinib
benefit emerges.

Compound subgroup tests (iters 21-23)
-------------------------------------
Sotorasib × KRAS × STK11: the three-way interaction is not significant
(beta=-0.27, p=0.13). KRAS+/STK11-wt: +2.59 mo (p=3.2e-179, n=1959/3513);
KRAS+/STK11-mut: +2.33 mo (p=3.6e-25, n=287/609). STK11 status does not
suppress sotorasib in this dataset; KRAS G12C+ alone is the operative
subgroup definition.

Pembrolizumab × PD-L1 high × TMB high × STK11 wt: the most plausible
"hyper-enriched" pembrolizumab subgroup. Within PD-L1 high (n≈10,750),
the model gives pembro main beta=-0.156 (p=0.005), pembro:tmb_high beta=
+0.212 (p=0.040), pembro:stk11 beta=+0.167 (p=0.24), and a 3-way of +0.025
(p=0.93). The compound subgroup PD-L1 high & TMB high & STK11-wt
(n=1373 vs 1341): pembrolizumab 3.33 vs 3.28, +0.056 mo, p=0.52. There is
NO subgroup of robust pembrolizumab benefit; the only nominally negative
effect is in PD-L1 high + TMB low patients (~ -0.10 mo).

Osimertinib × EGFR × brain mets: 3-way beta=+0.056, p=0.70. All four
EGFR×brain quadrants show null osimertinib effects (|diff|<0.06 mo,
all p>0.62). No subgroup of osimertinib benefit.

ECOG-stratified treatment effects (iter 24)
-------------------------------------------
The sotorasib effect (mostly via KRAS G12C+ patients within each stratum)
is preserved across ECOG 0/1/2 (+0.31, +0.31, +0.30 mo). Pembrolizumab,
olaparib, and osimertinib remain null or trivially negative in every ECOG
stratum. Performance status modulates baseline PFS strongly but does not
unmask treatment benefit.

Final best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypotheses (iter 25)
-------------------------------------------------------------------
Pembrolizumab: NO subgroup of benefit. The compound (pdl1_tps>=0.5 AND
  tmb_high=1 AND stk11_mutation=0) yields +0.06 mo, p=0.52. Pembrolizumab
  appears inert (and slightly numerically detrimental in PD-L1 high / TMB
  low patients) in this cohort.

Sotorasib: kras_g12c=1 is the operative subgroup. Effect = +2.55 mo
  (5.77 vs 3.21, p=1.7e-202, n=2246 treated vs 4122 controls); ~80%
  relative increase in mean PFS. Restricting further to stk11_mutation=0
  yields essentially the same effect (+2.59 mo, p=3.2e-179), so the
  minimal sufficient subgroup is KRAS G12C+ alone — additional predicates
  do not improve enrichment.

Olaparib: NO subgroup of benefit, including brca2_mutation=1
  (+0.010 mo, p=0.93). Olaparib appears inert in every subgroup
  examined.

Osimertinib: NO subgroup of benefit, including egfr_mutation=1
  (-0.014 mo, p=0.81) and egfr_mutation=1 AND has_brain_mets=1
  (+0.051 mo, p=0.64). Osimertinib appears inert in every subgroup
  examined.

Hypotheses supported vs refuted
-------------------------------
Supported. Older age → longer PFS (direction opposite to prior, but
significant). Female sex → shorter PFS. Current smoking → shorter PFS.
ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, squamous histology, weight loss → shorter PFS.
Albumin → longer PFS. LDH → shorter PFS (small per-unit). EGFR mutation
→ slightly longer marginal PFS. Sotorasib in KRAS G12C+ → much longer PFS.
The full multivariable model explains nearly 90% of PFS variance from
clinical features alone, and adding the sotorasib×KRAS interaction is
essentially the only treatment term that contributes additional variance.

Refuted. Pembrolizumab benefit overall and in every PD-L1 / TMB / STK11
subgroup tested. Olaparib benefit overall and in BRCA2+. Osimertinib
benefit overall, in EGFR+, and in EGFR+ with brain mets. KRAS G12C as a
poor-prognosis marker (it is favorable here, due to sotorasib). ALK fusion
as a favorable marker (it is unfavorable here, because no ALK inhibitor is
available). High TMB as favorable (it is unfavorable, since pembrolizumab
does not work). STK11 as a poor-prognosis marker (no marginal effect) and
as a suppressor of sotorasib (no 3-way interaction). CRP and NLR as
prognostic markers (both null in this dataset).

Overall conclusion
------------------
The dataset is dominated by a single, very strong, biomarker-defined
treatment effect: sotorasib in KRAS G12C+ patients (+2.55 months of PFS,
~80% relative increase in mean PFS). This effect is robust to ECOG, sex,
smoking, histology, and STK11 status. Pembrolizumab, olaparib, and
osimertinib show no positive PFS benefit in this cohort — including in
their canonical predictive-biomarker subgroups (PD-L1 high / TMB high,
BRCA2+, EGFR+) and in compound subgroups built from those biomarkers.
Outside of the sotorasib×KRAS effect, PFS in this cohort is essentially a
function of disease burden (stage IV, brain mets, ECOG), histology
(adenocarcinoma > squamous), nutritional/cachexia status (albumin,
weight loss%, smoking), and demographics (older > younger, men > women).
