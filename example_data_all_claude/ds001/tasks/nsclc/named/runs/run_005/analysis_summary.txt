Analysis Summary — ds001_nsclc (50,000 NSCLC patients; outcome = pfs_months)
============================================================================

OVERVIEW

The dataset contains 50,000 NSCLC patients with a single continuous outcome
(pfs_months, mean 3.43, range 0–15.3), 33 features, and four treatments
(pembrolizumab 50%, sotorasib 35%, olaparib 30%, osimertinib 30%). Patients
can carry more than one treatment indicator. Treatment assignment is
essentially uncorrelated with the canonical biomarker (P(osim|EGFR+)=0.30
≈ P(osim|EGFR-)=0.30, etc.), consistent with random/quasi-random allocation
across the cohort.

I ran 25 iterations of propose–test–refine, beginning with main effects of
each feature on PFS, then probing each treatment overall, then performing
biomarker-matched subgroup and interaction analyses, exhaustive
(treatment × feature) interaction screens, T-learner gradient-boosting ITE
estimation, regression-tree heterogeneity discovery, two-feature subgroup
sweeps for the three null treatments, and finally a fully adjusted
multivariable model that captures the entire treatment-effect structure
with R²=0.975.

KEY FINDING — SOTORASIB IS A POWERFUL TARGETED THERAPY IN A NARROW SUBGROUP

The single dominant treatment-effect signal in the data is sotorasib in
KRAS-G12C-positive males without brca2_mutation and without alk_fusion.
The effect builds up monotonically as restrictions are added:

  • Overall sotorasib effect:                     +0.32 months (p<1e-48)
  • Within KRAS G12C+ (n=6368):                   +2.55 months (p~1e-202)
  • Within KRAS G12C+, sex_female=0 (n=3508):     +4.64 months
  • Within KRAS G12C+ male, brca2_mutation=0
    (n=3401):                                     +4.73 months (adj)
  • Within KRAS G12C+ male, alk_fusion=0
    (n=3370):                                     +4.75 months (adj)
  • Final responder subgroup
    (KRAS G12C+ ∧ male ∧ brca2- ∧ alk-) (n=3266): +4.91 months (adj),
        unadjusted means 8.23 (treated) vs 3.25 (untreated), p<<1e-300

Within this responder subgroup the effect is stable across every other
feature stratum (smoking, histology, ECOG, stage, brain mets, TMB, EGFR,
STK11), with subgroup sotorasib effects all between +4.7 and +5.2 months.
Outside this subgroup, sotorasib has no measurable effect:
  - KRAS G12C-negative:           diff=-0.005 (p=0.80)
  - KRAS G12C+ female:            diff=-0.012 (p=0.88)
  - KRAS G12C+ male brca2+:       diff=-0.07  (p=0.89)
  - KRAS G12C+ male alk_fusion+:  diff=-0.29  (p=0.50)

A fully adjusted multivariable model with the four-way interaction
structure recovers the same picture in a single regression
(treatment_sotorasib:kras_g12c=+4.77, soto:kras:sex_female=-4.59,
soto:kras:brca2_mutation=-2.74, soto:kras:alk_fusion=-2.41; all p<<1e-300;
R²=0.975).

A T-learner gradient-boosting estimator produced individual treatment
effects (ITE) whose mean was +4.16 months in the canonical responder
subgroup and +0.05 months everywhere else, and a regression tree fit on
those ITE values split first on kras_g12c, then on sex_female, with all
four KRAS+/male leaves showing ITE ≈ +4 and all other leaves ≈ 0. This
non-parametric heterogeneity search converges on the same definition.

THREE NULL TREATMENTS

Pembrolizumab, olaparib, and osimertinib show no clinically meaningful
benefit anywhere in the dataset. None of these treatments produces a
positive effect overall, in their canonical biomarker subgroup, after
multivariable adjustment, or in two-feature subgroup sweeps:

  • Pembrolizumab overall:                  -0.035 months (p=0.08)
  • Pembrolizumab in TMB-high:              -0.048 (p=0.20)
  • Pembrolizumab in PD-L1 quartile 4:      -0.072 (p=0.07; if anything
                                            slightly negative)
  • Pembrolizumab × PD-L1 × TMB triple
    interaction (adjusted):                 all coefficients p>0.12
  • Olaparib overall:                       -0.025 (p=0.26)
  • Olaparib in BRCA2+:                     +0.010 (p=0.93)
  • Olaparib × BRCA2 × sex 3-way (adj):     all coefficients p>0.35
  • Osimertinib overall:                    +0.004 (p=0.87)
  • Osimertinib in EGFR+:                   -0.014 (p=0.81)
  • Osimertinib × EGFR (adj):               -0.017 (p=0.47)

In the fully adjusted final model these three treatments have main
effects within |0.01| months and p>0.1, confirming their nullity even
after removing the sotorasib heterogeneity from the residual.

An exhaustive sweep of two-feature subgroups (n≥200, |Δ|>0.15, p<0.05)
turned up only small, unreplicating cells — the strongest were
pembrolizumab in (KRAS+, stage III) at -0.46 months (p=1.5e-4), olaparib
in (EGFR+, STK11+) at +0.33 (p=0.03), and osimertinib in (BRCA2+, TMB
low) at -0.40 (p=4e-3); none survived multiple comparisons or replicated
in adjusted models, and no positive >0.5-month effect for any of these
three treatments emerged in any subgroup.

There were no significant pairwise treatment-by-treatment interactions
(all six pair coefficients within |0.012| with p>0.13), so combining
treatments did not unlock additional benefit.

PROGNOSTIC ARCHITECTURE

After full multivariable adjustment (R²=0.89 main-effects only, 0.97
with the sotorasib interaction structure), the prognostic landscape is:

  Strong negative prognostic factors
    • ecog_ps:                  -1.10 months per ECOG unit
    • stage_iv:                 -1.42 months
    • has_brain_mets:           -0.91 months
    • histology=squamous:       -0.72 months (vs adenocarcinoma)
    • smoking_status=current:   -0.39 vs never (former vs never +0.09)
    • weight_loss_pct_6mo:      -0.073 months per percentage point
    • sex_female:               -0.20 months (mostly explained by the
                                lost sotorasib benefit in KRAS+
                                females; sex coefficient drops to ~0
                                when the analysis is restricted to
                                KRAS-)
    • ldh_u_l:                  -0.0004 per U/L (small but significant)
    • brca2_mutation:           -0.11 months

  Strong positive prognostic factor
    • age_years:                +0.169 months per year. The
                                age–PFS relationship is monotone across
                                quartiles (Q1=1.33, Q2=2.78, Q3=3.97,
                                Q4=5.69) and persists with full
                                adjustment. The effect is somewhat
                                attenuated in stage IV / brain-mets /
                                higher-ECOG patients (interaction
                                coefficients ~-0.014/yr per poor-
                                prognosis feature), but the per-year
                                slope stays positive throughout.
    • albumin_g_dl:             +0.46 months per g/dL

  Non-prognostic features (after adjustment)
    • pdl1_tps (linear and quadratic, both p>0.7)
    • tmb_high (p=0.47)
    • hemoglobin, AST, ALT, total bilirubin, creatinine, BUN,
      sodium, potassium, calcium, alkaline phosphatase, CRP, NLR — all
      |coef|<0.001 in standardized form, p>0.10
    • alk_fusion (after sotorasib structure removed): -0.03 (p=0.001
      tiny) — modestly negative
    • stk11_mutation: -0.02 (p=0.04) — essentially nil
    • egfr_mutation:  ~0 (p=0.96) — no prognostic effect

A small histology × kras_g12c interaction (squamous × KRAS+ = +0.024,
p=0.027) was detected but is clinically negligible.

WHAT WAS SUPPORTED VS. REFUTED

Supported:
  • Sotorasib is highly active in KRAS G12C+ males without brca2 or
    alk_fusion alterations (refined responder subgroup identified by
    cumulative restriction; verified by adjusted OLS, T-learner ITE,
    and regression-tree heterogeneity search).
  • Strong prognostic effects of age, ECOG, stage, brain mets, albumin,
    weight loss, smoking status, histology.
  • Multivariable model (R²=0.97) cleanly captures the entire treatment
    heterogeneity within a single 4-way interaction structure for
    sotorasib and zero main effects for the other three drugs.

Refuted:
  • Osimertinib benefit in EGFR-mutated patients (no effect anywhere).
  • Olaparib benefit in BRCA2-mutated patients (no effect).
  • Pembrolizumab benefit in PD-L1-high or TMB-high subgroups (no
    effect; tiny negative drift).
  • Treatment assignment driven by biomarker status (assignment is
    independent of biomarker).
  • Any treatment-by-treatment synergy.
  • PD-L1 TPS or TMB-high carrying any prognostic information.

FINAL BEST-SUPPORTED TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS (single
outcome pfs_months)

  Treatment:        treatment_sotorasib
  Outcome:          pfs_months
  Direction:        increased pfs_months (positive effect)
  Subgroup:         kras_g12c = 1
                    AND sex_female = 0
                    AND brca2_mutation = 0
                    AND alk_fusion = 0
  Effect size:      +4.91 months (adjusted; ~+5.0 unadjusted)
  Subgroup size:    n = 3,266 (~6.5% of cohort)
  Statistical
  evidence:         p << 1e-300; consistent across all further
                    stratifications within this subgroup; effect is
                    abolished by removing any of the four predicates
                    (KRAS-G12C-status, male sex, brca2-WT, alk-WT).

No comparable treatment-effect subgroup is supported for pembrolizumab,
olaparib, or osimertinib in this dataset.
