Analysis summary for ds001_nsclc (50,000 NSCLC patients, single outcome pfs_months)
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The dataset contains 50,000 patients with 33 anonymized features (feature_001 through feature_033, mixture of binary, ordinal/categorical, and continuous) and a single continuous outcome, pfs_months (progression-free survival, mean 3.43, SD 2.23, range 0-15.3 months). Across 25 iterations I (1) characterized univariate associations, (2) built a multivariable linear model of all main effects, (3) ran systematic treatment-by-feature interaction screens for every plausible binary "treatment" candidate, and (4) iteratively refined the best-supported treatment-effect subgroup. The final saturated model with the discovered subgroup-by-treatment interaction explains R^2 = 0.979 of the variance in pfs_months.

PROGNOSTIC LANDSCAPE (main effects)
-----------------------------------
Iterations 1-5 established the additive structure of the outcome. The strongest independent predictors of pfs_months in OLS adjusting for all features simultaneously were:

  - feature_015 (continuous, range 30-90, mean 65) +0.168 months/unit (t=+512, p<1e-300). This was the single largest contributor to variance: mean pfs_months by feature_015 quartile climbed monotonically from 1.33 (Q1) to 5.69 (Q4). Its distribution was identical across histology, smoking, and feature_014 strata, so it does not act through those channels. Although its range is age-like, the strong positive direction (longer PFS at higher values) is contrary to age in NSCLC; functionally it behaves as a baseline prognostic continuous variable.
  - feature_014 (3-level ordinal 0/1/2): each step worsens PFS by -1.10 months adjusted (p<1e-300); unadjusted means 4.33, 3.19, 2.15. Behavior consistent with a performance-status-like indicator.
  - feature_023 (binary, 65% prevalence): -1.42 months adjusted (p<1e-300). Uniformly negative across every stratum of every other binary - i.e., a stage/biology marker rather than a treatment.
  - feature_011 (binary, 25% prevalence): -0.91 months adjusted (p<1e-300). Same uniformly-negative pattern as feature_023, again consistent with a prognostic/biology marker.
  - feature_006 (squamous histology vs adenocarcinoma): -0.72 months (p<1e-300).
  - feature_001 (smoking): former +0.55 and never +0.55 vs current (both p<1e-300); former and never indistinguishable.
  - feature_017 (continuous, range 0-28): -0.073 months/unit (p<1e-300), independent of smoking - plausibly a duration/burden-of-exposure variable.
  - feature_025 (continuous, range 1.7-5.5): +0.46 months/unit (p<1e-300) - positive prognostic.
  - feature_032 (continuous, range 50-828): -0.00046 months/unit (p=1.5e-28), small but detectable.
  - feature_031 (-0.20) and feature_018 (+0.32) had nominal main effects in the unrestricted model, but iteration 13 and 26 showed these are effect modifiers, not prognostic markers.

A large set of continuous lab-like features (feature_030, feature_008, feature_004, feature_013, feature_026, feature_029, feature_007, feature_033, feature_009, feature_010, feature_003, feature_024, feature_019) and several binary indicators (feature_022, feature_002, feature_021, feature_012, feature_020, feature_027) were individually non-significant after adjustment.

TREATMENT IDENTIFICATION
------------------------
Among 13 binary features, only feature_016 had a positive adjusted main effect on pfs_months (+0.89 months, p<1e-90, prevalence 13%). It is enriched in current smokers (19.7%) vs never smokers (2.0%) and in squamous (15.4%) vs adenocarcinoma (11.7%). Iteration 6 tagged feature_016 as the most plausible treatment candidate. Other candidates with large effects (feature_023, feature_011) were ruled out as treatments because their effects were uniformly negative across every stratum of every other binary feature (iteration 12).

INTERACTION SCREEN AND SUBGROUP DISCOVERY
-----------------------------------------
Iteration 7's pre-specified screen of feature_016 x covariate interactions surfaced four enormous and internally consistent interactions:

   feature_016 x feature_018  +2.534 (p<1e-300)
   feature_016 x feature_031  -1.622 (p<1e-300)
   feature_016 x feature_028  -0.914 (p=3e-78)
   feature_016 x feature_005  -0.906 (p=1e-56)

Stratified analyses (iterations 8-10) refined these into a step-wise subgroup definition:

  Step 1 (iteration 8): feature_018=1 is necessary. feature_016 effect within feature_018=0 is -0.12 (no benefit); within feature_018=1 it is +2.44 months (p~0). Symmetrically, feature_018 has no main effect when feature_016=0 (diff=-0.005, p=0.80), but +2.55 when feature_016=1.

  Step 2 (iteration 9): feature_031=0 is also necessary. Within feature_018=1, feature_016 effect is +4.56 months when feature_031=0 (n_eligible=9776) but -0.17 when feature_031=1. The four-cell table showed effect localization to a single quadrant.

  Step 3 (iteration 10): Within feature_018=1 & feature_031=0, the small subgroups feature_028=1 (n_trt=46) and feature_005=1 (n_trt=39) abrogated the benefit (effects -0.37 and +0.08 respectively, both non-significant).

The full eligibility predicate is therefore feature_018=1 AND feature_031=0 AND feature_028=0 AND feature_005=0, which captures 9,013 patients (18.0% of the cohort).

FORMAL VERIFICATION
-------------------
Iteration 11 fit two adjusted models:
  - Within eligible (n=9013, n_trt=1154): adjusted feature_016 effect = +4.922 months (p<1e-300).
  - Within ineligible (n=40987, n_trt=5214): adjusted feature_016 effect = +0.002 months (p=0.69).

Iteration 20 combined this into one saturated model with the eligible*feature_016 interaction term. R^2 rose to 0.979 (vs 0.894 for main-effects-only). The within-vs-outside contrast was the dominant remaining source of explainable variance once feature_015, feature_014, feature_023, feature_011, smoking, histology, feature_017, and feature_025 were already included.

Iteration 18 confirmed the treatment effect is broadly homogeneous within the eligible subgroup across classical clinical strata: feature_014=0 (+4.55), =1 (+4.64), =2 (+4.42); adenocarcinoma (+4.60), squamous (+4.68); former smoker (+4.80), current (+4.68), never (+3.79; small n=23). I.e., once eligibility is met, performance status, histology, and smoking do not further shape the effect size.

Iteration 17 verified that even feature_015 (the dominant continuous prognostic factor) does not strongly modulate the absolute treatment effect: feature_016 x feature_015 interaction +0.0079/unit (p=5e-16) is small relative to the +4.9-month main effect, so the additive treatment benefit is approximately constant across baseline prognosis.

Iteration 21 verified that the joint biomarker prevalence (feature_018=1 AND feature_031=0 = 19.6%) matches independent expectation (35.1% * 55.3% = 19.4%), so the two predictive biomarkers are statistically independent and combine multiplicatively.

Iterations 19, 22, 23 ruled out alternative second-treatment candidates: no other binary feature in the dataset displays a positive subgroup-specific effect of comparable magnitude. The +0.32 main-effect coefficient on feature_018 in the unrestricted model collapses to 0 once the feature_016 x feature_018 interaction is included, confirming feature_018 has no independent prognostic value.

WHAT WAS SUPPORTED VS. REFUTED
------------------------------
Supported: feature_015 dominant continuous prognostic positive (h3); feature_014 stepwise prognostic negative (h4); squamous histology negative (h5); smoking gradient (h6); feature_017 negative (h7); feature_025 positive (h8); feature_016 is the only binary treatment candidate (h9); feature_016 has a strong heterogeneous treatment effect (h10-h11); the heterogeneity is concentrated in feature_018=1 & feature_031=0 (h12); further restricted by feature_028=0 and feature_005=0 (h13-h14); feature_023/feature_011 are prognostic, not treatments (h15); feature_018 and feature_031 have no main effect outside treatment (h16); other binaries are not treatments (h17, h22); the saturated model resolves nearly all variance (h27, h28).

Refuted/Refined: the small main-effect coefficient on feature_018 in iteration 2 was reinterpreted as confounding with feature_016 x feature_018, not an independent prognostic effect (h26).

FINAL BEST-SUPPORTED TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS
--------------------------------------------------------
Treatment: feature_016 (binary).
Outcome: pfs_months (continuous, months).
Signed direction: positive (treatment increases PFS).
Subgroup eligibility (joint, all four required):
   feature_018 == 1
   feature_031 == 0
   feature_028 == 0
   feature_005 == 0
Adjusted treatment effect within eligible subgroup: +4.92 months (95% CI ~+4.90 to +4.95, p < 1e-300, n_eligible = 9013, n_treated = 1154).
Adjusted treatment effect outside eligible subgroup: +0.002 months (p = 0.69, n = 40987, n_treated = 5214).
The pattern is clinically interpretable as a precision-oncology regimen where (a) feature_018=1 is a positive predictive biomarker required for benefit, (b) feature_031=1 is a primary-resistance marker that fully blocks benefit, and (c) feature_028=1 and feature_005=1 are additional small-prevalence resistance markers that also abolish benefit. No second treatment-effect signal of comparable magnitude exists in the data.
