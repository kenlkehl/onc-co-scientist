Analysis Summary — ds001_nsclc (n=50,000; outcome pfs_months)
================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Across 15 iterations I (i) characterized the prognostic landscape of the cohort,
(ii) screened for treatment-like features whose average effect on pfs_months is
small but whose effect could be concentrated in a subgroup, and (iii) systematically
searched for treatment-effect heterogeneity at two- and three-way levels. One
treatment-like feature, feature_018, exhibited a profoundly heterogeneous effect
that is fully captured by a four-feature subgroup definition. No other binary
feature in the dataset displayed comparable subgroup heterogeneity.


PROGNOSTIC LANDSCAPE
--------------------
The dominant single predictor of pfs_months is the continuous variable
feature_015 (range 30-90, mean 65). Pearson r = 0.757 with pfs_months; mean PFS
across feature_015 quintiles climbs monotonically from 1.14 months (Q1) to 5.92
months (Q5). Each unit increase in feature_015 corresponds to ~0.17 additional
months of PFS in the multivariable model.

Beyond feature_015, the strongest adjusted prognostic predictors of pfs_months
were:
  - feature_014 (ordered 0/1/2): -1.15 (level 1) and -2.18 (level 2) months vs.
    level 0; consistent with a performance-status- or stage-like marker.
  - feature_023 (~65% prevalence): -1.42 months when =1 (largest single binary
    negative effect).
  - feature_011 (~25%): -0.91 months when =1.
  - feature_028 (~5%): -0.81 univariate, attenuates somewhat after adjustment.
  - feature_001 (smoking): current vs. former or never both ~+0.55 months (i.e.,
    current smokers do worst).
  - feature_006 (histology): squamous -0.72 months vs. adenocarcinoma.
  - feature_016 (~13%): +0.89 months when =1 (favorable feature).
  - feature_018 (~35%): +0.32 months when =1 on average (the small average masks
    a very large heterogeneous effect — see below).
  - feature_025 (continuous, range 1.7-5.5): +0.46 per unit.
  - feature_017 (continuous, range 0-28): -0.073 per unit.
  - feature_032 (continuous, range 50-829): -0.0004 per unit (small but very
    significant).

The full multivariable OLS model achieved R^2 = 0.894, indicating that the
identified covariates explain almost all variance in pfs_months. Multiple binary
features (feature_002, feature_012, feature_020, feature_021, feature_022,
feature_027) had effectively no main effect on pfs_months either crude or after
adjustment.

Two prognostic interactions with feature_015 were also detected:
  - feature_023 x feature_015: slope on feature_015 is 0.178 in feature_023=0
    patients vs. 0.165 in feature_023=1 (interaction coef -0.0126/unit, p=2e-25).
  - feature_011 x feature_015: similar pattern (interaction coef -0.0114/unit,
    p=5e-15). feature_011=1 patients gain less PFS per unit of feature_015.


TREATMENT CANDIDATES AND HETEROGENEITY SEARCH
---------------------------------------------
I treated as candidate "treatment" indicators all binary features whose average
effect on pfs_months was small or null after adjustment (feature_002,
feature_012, feature_020, feature_021, feature_022, feature_027) — and also
feature_018 (because despite its small +0.32 main effect, its prevalence (~35%)
and the existence of significant pairwise interactions made it a plausible
treatment in disguise).

A systematic interaction screen was run: for every binary candidate treatment x
each available modifier (binary, multi-level categorical, and continuous
prognostic features), an adjusted OLS interaction model was fit. The single
largest interaction was C(feature_016)[T.1]:C(feature_018)[T.1] = +2.53
(p<1e-300). Stratified verification:
  - feature_016=0 (n=43,632): feature_018=1 vs =0 mean PFS difference = -0.005
    (p=0.80). No detectable effect.
  - feature_016=1 (n=6,368): feature_018=1 vs =0 mean PFS difference = +2.55
    (p=1.7e-202). Large effect.

A subsequent triple-interaction screen across all triples of binary features
flagged only two triples with |3-way coef|>1.0 and p<1e-15, both involving
feature_018:
  - feature_016 x feature_018 x feature_031 (3-way coef = -4.69, t=-39).
  - feature_016 x feature_018 x feature_028 (3-way coef = -2.90, t=-9.4).

That is, within the feature_016=1 subgroup, both feature_031 and feature_028
fully abrogate the feature_018 treatment effect. Stratified evidence:
  - feature_016=1 & feature_031=0: feature_018 effect = +4.64 months (n=1237 vs
    2271, p<1e-300).
  - feature_016=1 & feature_031=1: feature_018 effect = -0.01 months (n=1009 vs
    1851, ns).
  - feature_016=1 & feature_031=0 & feature_028=0: +4.83 (n=1191 vs 2179).
  - feature_016=1 & feature_031=0 & feature_028=1: -0.29 (n=46 vs 92, ns).

Within the feature_016=1 & feature_031=0 & feature_028=0 stratum, an
exhaustive search for further binary suppressors flagged feature_005=1 as a
fourth abrogator:
  - feature_005=0: feature_018 effect = +4.99 months (n=1154 vs 2112).
  - feature_005=1: feature_018 effect = +0.05 months (n=37 vs 67, ns).


FINAL RESPONDER SUBGROUP
------------------------
Defining the responder indicator as

    responder = (feature_016==1) AND (feature_031==0) AND
                (feature_028==0) AND (feature_005==0)

yields prevalence 6.53% (n=3266). The model

    pfs_months ~ feature_018 * responder

produces:
  - Outside responder (n=46,734): feature_018=1 vs =0 mean PFS diff = -0.006
    (p=0.76).
  - Inside responder (n=3266):    feature_018=1 vs =0 mean PFS diff = +4.99
    (8.23 vs 3.25 months, t=61.8, p<1e-300).
  - Interaction coefficient = +4.99 (t=62.5, p<1e-300).

Within the responder subgroup, the treatment effect is essentially homogeneous
across other covariates: by histology +5.09 (adeno) vs +4.85 (squamous), by
smoking +4.76/+5.21/+4.32 (current/former/never), and across feature_015
quintiles +4.60 to +5.16. This means the four-feature responder definition
captures the heterogeneity completely.

The very small +0.32-month main effect of feature_018 in the full cohort is
therefore not a uniformly distributed modest benefit — it is an arithmetic
average of (i) a +5-month benefit in the 6.5% of patients matching the
responder definition and (ii) ~zero benefit in the remaining 93.5%.


SUPPORTED VS. REFUTED HYPOTHESES
--------------------------------
Supported (with direction matching):
  - feature_015 strongly positively predicts pfs_months.
  - feature_014 monotonically negatively predicts pfs_months.
  - feature_006 squamous shorter PFS than adenocarcinoma.
  - feature_001 current smokers shorter PFS than former/never.
  - feature_023, feature_011, feature_028 are negative prognostics.
  - feature_016 is a positive prognostic.
  - feature_018 has a positive (heterogeneous) effect on PFS.
  - feature_018 effect is concentrated in feature_016=1 patients.
  - feature_018 effect is suppressed by feature_031=1 and by feature_028=1
    (and in fine-grained search by feature_005=1) within feature_016=1.
  - feature_023 x feature_015 prognostic interaction (negative).
  - feature_011 x feature_015 prognostic interaction (negative).
  - feature_025 and feature_017 are independent continuous prognostics.

Refuted / null:
  - feature_002, feature_012, feature_020, feature_021, feature_022, feature_027
    do not exhibit clinically meaningful main effects or any subgroup with
    treatment-effect heterogeneity above the noise floor (>0.3 months spread).
    They are best regarded as inactive features with respect to pfs_months.
  - feature_019, feature_024, feature_010, feature_003, feature_030, feature_008,
    feature_004, feature_013, feature_026, feature_029, feature_007, feature_033,
    feature_009 (continuous variables) lack independent association with
    pfs_months after adjustment.
  - feature_018 effect is NOT meaningfully modified by feature_001 (smoking),
    feature_006 (histology), feature_014, feature_023, feature_011, feature_022,
    feature_002, feature_012, feature_020, feature_027, feature_021, or
    feature_015 once the four-feature responder subgroup is conditioned on.


FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS
------------------------------------------
For the outcome pfs_months, the treatment indicator feature_018 produces a
mean increase of approximately +5.0 months exclusively in patients meeting all
of the following conditions:
    feature_016 == 1
    feature_031 == 0
    feature_028 == 0
    feature_005 == 0
In all other patients (~93.5% of the cohort), feature_018 has no detectable
effect on pfs_months. Both feature_031=1 and feature_028=1 (and to a smaller
extent feature_005=1) act as suppressors that completely abrogate the treatment
effect even in the favorable-biomarker (feature_016=1) subgroup, and these
suppressor predicates are necessary components of the complete responder
definition.
