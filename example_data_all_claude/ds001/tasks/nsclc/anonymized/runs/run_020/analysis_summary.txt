ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (50,000 patients, outcome: pfs_months)
=====================================================================

Across 25 iterations of propose-test-refine, the analysis moved from broad
main-effect screening through identification of the treatment variable to a
systematic treatment-effect heterogeneity search culminating in a clean
four-condition predictive subgroup definition.

1. PROGNOSTIC LANDSCAPE (main effects)
--------------------------------------
Univariate and adjusted (multivariable linear-regression) analyses identified
the following independent prognostic factors for pfs_months. The directions
below are signed on the natural PFS scale (months):

  * feature_014 (ordinal 0/1/2; likely performance status)  : -1.10/unit, p<1e-200
  * feature_023 (binary, prev 65%)                          : -1.42, p<1e-200
  * feature_011 (binary, prev 25%)                          : -0.92, p<1e-200
  * feature_006='squamous' vs adenocarcinoma                : -0.72, p<1e-200
  * feature_001='current' vs former smoker                  : -0.55, p<1e-9
  * feature_031 (binary, prev 45%)                          : -0.20, p<1e-30
  * feature_017 (continuous, range 0-28)                    : -0.07/unit, p<1e-150
  * feature_005 (rare binary, prev 3%)                      : -0.11, p<1e-7
  * feature_028 (rare binary, prev 5%)                      : ns after adjustment

  Favorable independent factors:
  * feature_015 (continuous, range 30-90)                   : +0.17/unit, p<1e-200 (Spearman rho=+0.77)
  * feature_025 (continuous, range 1.7-5.5; likely albumin) : +0.46/unit, p<1e-200
  * feature_016 (binary, prev 13%)                          : +0.89, p<1e-200 [primarily a predictive marker; see below]
  * feature_018 (binary, prev 35%; treatment)               : +0.32, p=1.6e-53

The remaining continuous lab values (feature_003, feature_004, feature_007,
feature_008, feature_009, feature_010, feature_013, feature_019, feature_024,
feature_026, feature_029, feature_030, feature_033) and the binary features
feature_002, feature_012, feature_020, feature_021, feature_022, feature_027
showed weak or null adjusted main effects.

2. IDENTIFICATION OF THE TREATMENT VARIABLE
-------------------------------------------
Three candidate treatment indicators were considered (binary features at
moderate prevalence): feature_012 (50.0%), feature_018 (35.1%), feature_021
(28.5%).

  * feature_012: no main effect (-0.035 mo, p=0.08); no interaction with any
    biomarker. Most likely a non-treatment indicator.
  * feature_021: small uniform negative effect (-0.19 mo) that disappears after
    adjustment (p=0.47); does NOT interact with feature_016 (interaction p=0.78).
    Behaves as a weak prognostic correlate, not a treatment with predictive
    biomarker.
  * feature_018: substantial positive main effect (+0.32 mo, p=1.6e-53) AND a
    very strong predictive-biomarker interaction (see Section 3). This is the
    only treatment-like variable with usable variation in this cohort.

3. PREDICTIVE-BIOMARKER PATTERN FOR feature_018
-----------------------------------------------
The headline finding is a textbook predictive-biomarker pattern. Feature_018
benefit is concentrated in feature_016=1 patients:

  feature_016=0 (n=43,632): feature_018 effect = -0.005 mo (p=0.80)
  feature_016=1 (n=6,368) : feature_018 effect = +2.55 mo (p=2.5e-275)
  Interaction coef pfs ~ feature_018 * feature_016 = +2.558 (p<1e-300)

Importantly, feature_016 is NOT a prognostic factor on its own: among
untreated patients (feature_018=0) the mean PFS is 3.33 (f016=0) vs 3.21
(f016=1), with the +0.12 difference favoring the wild-type — i.e., the
apparent +0.78 main effect of feature_016 in the cohort comes entirely from
treated f016=1 responders.

4. SUPPRESSORS WITHIN THE BIOMARKER-POSITIVE GROUP
--------------------------------------------------
A heterogeneity search within feature_016=1 revealed three further binary
modifiers whose unfavorable values abolish the treatment effect:

  feature_031=1 : within f016=1, treatment effect collapses from +4.64 (n=3508)
                  to -0.01 (n=2860). Three-way interaction
                  pfs ~ f018*f016*f031 has interaction coef -4.69, p<1e-300.
  feature_028=1 : within f016=1 & f031=0, treatment effect collapses from
                  +4.83 (n=3370) to -0.29 (n=138, p=0.51).
  feature_005=1 : within f016=1 & f031=0, treatment effect collapses from
                  +4.80 (n=3401) to -0.07 (n=107, p=0.88).

A fourth marginally suggestive modifier (feature_022) only mildly attenuates
the effect (interaction coef -0.24, p=1e-4) and adding f022=0 to the rule
changes the within-subgroup effect by only +0.05 mo, so it was not included
in the final definition.

5. FINAL PREDICTIVE SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS
---------------------------------------
Treatment: feature_018
Outcome  : pfs_months (linear, signed difference)
Direction: positive (treatment increases PFS)
Subgroup : feature_016 = 1 AND feature_031 = 0 AND feature_028 = 0 AND feature_005 = 0

Within this subgroup (n=3,266; 6.53% of cohort):
  feature_018 = 0 -> mean pfs = 3.25 months
  feature_018 = 1 -> mean pfs = 8.23 months
  Treatment effect = +4.99 months (p<1e-300)

Within the complement (n=46,734):
  feature_018 = 0 -> mean pfs = 3.323
  feature_018 = 1 -> mean pfs = 3.317
  Treatment effect = -0.006 months (p=0.76)

The interaction term f018 * subgroup-membership has coef +4.99, p<1e-300.

The within-subgroup ~+5 month effect is preserved across performance status
(f014=0 +4.78; f014=1 +5.06; f014=2 +4.92), histology (adeno +4.91; squamous
+5.13), feature_023 (=0 +5.05; =1 +4.96) and continuous prognostic correlates
(feature_015), supporting that the predictive rule is biomarker-driven rather
than a manifestation of prognostic confounding.

6. NULL FINDINGS / REFUTED HYPOTHESES
-------------------------------------
* feature_018 effect does NOT interact with feature_014 (PS), feature_023,
  feature_011, feature_002, feature_012, feature_020, feature_021, feature_027.
* No continuous lab value modifies feature_018 effect to a clinically
  meaningful degree (largest |interaction coef|=0.0035 for feature_015,
  p=0.010; all others p>0.05 with negligible coefficients).
* feature_021 and feature_012 do not behave like treatments with predictive
  biomarkers: their effects are uniform across feature_016 status.
* The apparent absence of f018 benefit among never-smokers (overall diff
  +0.014, p=0.78) is a confounded null: within feature_016=1 & feature_031=0,
  never-smokers DO benefit (+3.98 mo, p=2.4e-5, n=84). The marginal null
  reflects that very few never-smokers fall into the responsive subgroup.

7. SUPPORTED VS. REFUTED HYPOTHESES (SUMMARY)
---------------------------------------------
SUPPORTED:
  - Strong prognostic role of feature_014, feature_023, feature_011,
    hist_squamous, smoking status, feature_015, feature_025, feature_017.
  - feature_018 as treatment with average +0.32-mo PFS effect.
  - feature_016 as a predictive (not prognostic) biomarker for feature_018.
  - feature_031, feature_028, feature_005 as treatment-effect suppressors that
    must all be in their favorable state for benefit to manifest.
  - Final 4-condition subgroup with ~+5-mo treatment benefit.

REFUTED:
  - feature_012 and feature_021 as therapies with biomarker-driven effects.
  - Continuous lab values as treatment-effect modifiers.
  - feature_022 as a co-defining requirement for the responsive subgroup.
  - Never-smoker status as an intrinsic suppressor independent of feature_016
    and feature_031.

CONCLUSION
==========
This NSCLC cohort exhibits one usable treatment variable (feature_018) whose
benefit is dramatically heterogeneous. About 6.5% of patients, defined by the
joint condition feature_016=1 AND feature_031=0 AND feature_028=0 AND
feature_005=0, derive an approximately 5-month PFS gain on treatment, while
the remaining 93.5% derive no measurable benefit. This is the canonical
pattern of a predictive biomarker (feature_016) modulated by additional
clinical/biological co-suppressors (feature_031, feature_028, feature_005),
and it was detectable cleanly because the dataset is large (n=50,000) and the
heterogeneity is concentrated in a small but well-powered subgroup.
