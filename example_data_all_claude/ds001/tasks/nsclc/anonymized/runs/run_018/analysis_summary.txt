Analysis summary — ds001_nsclc (50,000 patients; outcome pfs_months)
===================================================================

Overview
--------
This cohort is rich and well-powered (n=50,000; outcome pfs_months mean 3.43, median 3.32, sd 2.23, range 0–15.3). After 15 iterations of hypothesis testing, the dataset reduces to a small, very clean structure: a strong additive prognostic model (multivariable R² = 0.894), one large heterogeneous treatment effect, and a small set of modifiers that together define the patient population that benefits from treatment. Adding the treatment-by-subgroup indicator to the prognostic model raises R² from 0.894 to 0.979 — i.e., almost all remaining variance in pfs_months is captured.


Prognostic backbone (main effects)
----------------------------------
After multivariable OLS adjustment, the dominant baseline drivers of pfs_months are:

- feature_015 (continuous, range 30–90, mean 65). Every unit increase associates with +0.168 months of pfs (t=512). The univariate Pearson correlation is r=+0.76 — by far the largest single signal in the data.
- feature_014 (ordinal 0/1/2). Each level decreases pfs by ~1.10 months (means 4.33 / 3.19 / 2.15; t=-230).
- feature_023 (binary, prev 64.7%): adjusted effect −1.42 months (t=-208).
- feature_011 (binary, prev 25.2%): −0.91 months (t=-122).
- feature_006='squamous' vs 'adenocarcinoma' (prev 27.8% squamous): −0.72 months (t=-95).
- feature_016 (binary, prev 12.7%): +0.89 months (t=+90). This variable also functions as the principal treatment-effect biomarker (see below).
- feature_017 (continuous, range 0–28): −0.073 per unit (t=-87).
- feature_025 (continuous, range 1.7–5.5): +0.46 per unit (t=+71).
- feature_018 (binary, prev 35.1%): main effect +0.32 (t=+47), but this is an average over a hugely heterogeneous treatment effect — see below.
- feature_001='current' (smoking status; prev 30.0%): −0.55 vs 'never' (t=-44).
- feature_031 (binary, prev 44.7%): −0.20 (t=-31).
- feature_032 (continuous): −0.00045 per unit (t=-11; small but well-powered).
- feature_005 (binary, prev 3.0%): −0.11 (t=-5.8).

Many other features (feature_002, feature_004, feature_007, feature_008, feature_009, feature_010, feature_012, feature_013, feature_019, feature_020, feature_022, feature_026, feature_027, feature_029, feature_030, feature_033) had no independent additive effect (all p>0.05 in the multivariable model, or |coef| trivially small).


Treatment-effect heterogeneity (the central finding)
----------------------------------------------------
feature_018 looks like a randomly-assigned treatment: receipt rate is ~35% throughout the cohort, and a logistic regression of feature_018 on every baseline feature returns no |z|>3.4. Yet its conditional effect is enormous and confined to a narrow biomarker-defined subgroup.

Effect of feature_018=1 on pfs_months as the subgroup is built up:

  ALL                                          TE = +0.32  (n_t=17,544, n_c=32,456)
  feature_016=1                                TE = +2.55  (n_t=2,246,  n_c=4,122)
  feature_016=1 & feature_031=0                TE = +4.64  (n_t=1,237,  n_c=2,271)
  feature_016=1 & feature_031=0 & f028=0       TE = +4.83  (n_t=1,191,  n_c=2,179)
  feature_016=1 & feature_031=0 & f028=0 & f005=0   TE = +4.99  (n_t=1,154, n_c=2,112)

In the complementary cells:

  feature_016=0 (any other features)                TE = −0.005  (NS)
  feature_016=1 & feature_031=1                     TE = −0.012  (NS)
  feature_016=1 & feature_031=0 & feature_028=1     TE = −0.29   (NS, n=138)
  feature_016=1 & feature_031=0 & feature_005=1     TE = −0.07   (NS, n=107)

In other words: the entire treatment effect is concentrated in the ~6.5% of patients who are simultaneously feature_016=1, feature_031=0, feature_028=0, and feature_005=0. Each of these four conditions is necessary; relaxing any one of them collapses the treatment effect to ≈0. The four biomarkers are also pairwise independent (|r| ≤ 0.015), so the subgroup arises as a clean intersection.

Within that responder subgroup (n=3,266; 1,154 treated, 2,112 untreated), mean pfs is 8.23 months on treatment vs 3.25 months off (Δ = +4.99, t = 63.6, p < 1e-300). Adding the indicator (in_subgroup × feature_018) to the full multivariable OLS raises R² from 0.894 to 0.979 — the model essentially saturates once the heterogeneity is captured.


Within-subgroup heterogeneity
-----------------------------
Inside the responder subgroup, the treatment effect is approximately uniform. By feature_015 quartile the TE varies only between +4.68 and +5.10 (interaction coef +0.0156 per unit, t=3.24, p=0.001 — statistically real but small in magnitude relative to the +5 base). All other continuous baseline features (feature_017, feature_025, feature_032, feature_003, feature_010, feature_004, feature_008) show median-split TE differences ≤0.17 — i.e., no further effect modification.


Other candidate treatments
--------------------------
Three other binary features are roughly balanced enough to suspect treatment-style assignment:

- feature_012 (prev 50.0%): logit on baseline shows no |z|>3.4. Main effect on pfs −0.035 (p=0.08, NS). Interaction screen across all features yields only feature_017 (coef +0.018, p=3e-4) — a small modifier with no actionable subgroup. feature_012 looks like a randomly-assigned arm with no efficacy.
- feature_022 (prev 13.0%): univariate +0.19 (p=2e-10) but, stratified by smoking, every effect collapses to null (never −0.025, former −0.008, current +0.033). feature_022 is a confounded baseline correlate, not a treatment.
- feature_002 / feature_020 / feature_021 / feature_027: no meaningful main effects and no significant heterogeneity at p<0.001.


Final, best-supported subgroup hypothesis (feature_018, pfs_months)
-------------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment: feature_018 = 1
Outcome:   pfs_months
Direction: positive (longer pfs)
Subgroup:  feature_016 = 1 AND feature_031 = 0 AND feature_028 = 0 AND feature_005 = 0
Magnitude: TE ≈ +4.99 months (8.23 on treatment vs 3.25 off)
Outside subgroup: TE ≈ 0 (−0.006, p=0.76)
Subgroup prevalence: 6.53% of cohort (n=3,266)

The pattern — a single biomarker (feature_016) that defines responsiveness, plus three independent resistance modifiers (feature_031, feature_028, feature_005) each of which abolishes benefit — is consistent with the way targeted therapies behave in NSCLC: efficacy requires the activating marker to be present and known resistance/co-existing features to be absent.


Hypotheses that were supported
------------------------------
- h1–h10: prognostic main effects for feature_015, feature_014, feature_023, feature_011, squamous histology, feature_016 (positive), smoking (current worse), feature_017 (negative), feature_025 (positive), feature_028 (negative). All confirmed univariately and multivariably.
- h11/h12: feature_018 has a small positive main effect and feature_031 a small negative main effect.
- h15/h16: a parsimonious multivariable model with R²≈0.89; ~17 features have no independent additive contribution.
- h19–h24: feature_018 has a hugely heterogeneous effect; the responder subgroup is (feature_016=1 & feature_031=0 & feature_028=0 & feature_005=0).
- h27: small but real within-subgroup feature_015 modulation of TE (~+0.4 mo across the feature_015 range).
- h29: the four subgroup biomarkers are independent.
- h30: final summary subgroup hypothesis at +5.0 months.

Hypotheses refuted or refined
-----------------------------
- h13/h14: feature_021 has a small negative main effect (real) but no subgroup of large benefit. feature_022's +0.19 main effect is fully confounded by smoking — null in every smoking stratum.
- h18: feature_012 looks like a randomized treatment but has no efficacy — its overall main effect is null and there is no clinically meaningful subgroup of benefit.
- h25: no other binary feature shows a treatment-like heterogeneous effect comparable to feature_018.
- h28: within the responder subgroup, no continuous biomarker further refines the +5.0 effect (with the exception of the small feature_015 gradient noted in h27).


Overall conclusion
------------------
This cohort behaves like a clean clinical-trial-style dataset overlaid on a prognostic NSCLC backbone. There is one treatment of interest (feature_018), and its effect is fully explained by a four-way biomarker subgroup: a positive marker (feature_016) plus three orthogonal resistance modifiers (feature_031, feature_028, feature_005). The remainder of the variation in pfs_months is captured by a small set of standard prognostic factors (feature_015, feature_014, feature_023, feature_011, histology, smoking, feature_017, feature_025), with no additional treatment signals from any other feature.
