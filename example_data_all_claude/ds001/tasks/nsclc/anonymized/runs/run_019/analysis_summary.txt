ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (n=50,000; outcome pfs_months)
==============================================================

Setting
-------
The dataset contains 50,000 patient records with 33 anonymized features
(feature_001 through feature_033) and a single continuous outcome,
pfs_months (range 0.0 - 15.3 months, median 3.32, mean 3.43). Features
include 14 binary indicators, 2 multi-level categoricals (feature_001
smoking, 3 levels; feature_014 ordinal, 3 levels; feature_006 histology,
2 levels: adenocarcinoma vs squamous), and 17 continuous variables.

Across 25 iterations I (i) screened all features for main effects on
pfs_months, (ii) fit a full multivariable model, (iii) identified
candidate "treatment-like" variables based on balance across other
covariates, (iv) screened treatment-by-feature interactions, and (v)
searched systematically for the complete subgroup definition that
captures heterogeneity of any candidate treatment.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Main prognostic effects (univariate and adjusted)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
The strongest main predictors of pfs_months are:

* feature_015 (continuous 30-90, mean 65): r=+0.757 with pfs_months
  (p<1e-300). Quintile means rise monotonically from 1.14 (Q1, 30-56.5)
  to 5.92 (Q5, 73.6-90). The relationship has positive curvature
  (quadratic coef +0.0012, p=3e-136), so the per-unit benefit
  accelerates at higher levels.
* feature_014 (ordinal 0/1/2): mean pfs 4.33 / 3.19 / 2.15. Strongly
  monotone, linear coef -1.10 per level (p<1e-300, ANOVA F=3,141).
* feature_023 (binary, prevalence 65%): mean pfs 2.93 vs 4.36, diff
  -1.44 months (p<1e-300). Largest single binary main effect.
* feature_011 (binary, prev 25%): -0.91 months (p<1e-300). Effect very
  consistent across strata (range -0.81 to -1.01) - a pure prognostic.
* feature_016 (binary, prev 13%): +0.78 months marginally; this
  appears entirely driven by the treatment heterogeneity discovered
  below (see Section 3).
* feature_028 (binary, prev 5%): -0.81 months (p=3e-69).
* feature_006 (squamous vs adenocarcinoma): squamous -0.86 months
  (p<1e-300); adjusted -0.72 (p<1e-300).
* feature_001 smoking: current smokers -0.62 vs never/former
  (p=4e-187). Adjusted: former and never both ~+0.55 vs current.
* feature_017 (continuous 0-28): r=-0.127 (p=7e-179). Quartile means
  decline monotonically from 3.70 to 2.99.
* feature_025 (continuous 1.7-5.5): r=+0.099 (p=3e-109); adjusted +0.46
  per unit.
* feature_031 (binary, prev 45%): -0.20 months adjusted (p<1e-300).
* feature_032: small adjusted -0.0004 per unit (p<1e-4) but trivial
  magnitude.

Together these explain R^2=0.894 in a full main-effects multivariable
OLS. The remaining lab-like continuous features (003, 004, 007-010,
013, 024, 026, 029, 030, 033) and binary features (002, 005, 020, 021,
022, 027) have small or null adjusted main effects.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Identifying candidate treatment variables
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Five binary features have prevalence stable across feature_014 stage
(all stage 0/1/2 prevalences within ±0.01) and across feature_023
status, suggesting random/independent assignment - the signature of
randomized treatment-like variables:

  feature_012 (~50% prev), feature_018 (~35%), feature_020 (~30%),
  feature_027 (~30%), feature_021 (~28%).

Their main effects on pfs_months:
  feature_018  +0.322  (p=1.6e-53)   beneficial
  feature_021  -0.190  (p=9.1e-18)   mildly harmful
  feature_012  -0.035  (p=0.08)      null
  feature_020  -0.025  (p=0.25)      null
  feature_027  +0.004  (p=0.87)      null

------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Treatment-effect heterogeneity for feature_018
------------------------------------------------------------------------
A treatment-by-binary-modifier interaction screen on feature_018 (35
modifiers, OLS interaction p-values) flagged feature_016 as a dominant
modifier:

  ols(pfs_months ~ feature_018 * feature_016): interaction coef +2.56,
    t=41.9, p<1e-300.
  feature_016=0 (n=43,632): tx effect = -0.005 months (null)
  feature_016=1 (n=6,368):  tx effect = +2.55 months

Adding feature_031 to the interaction (3-way feature_018 x feature_016
x feature_031, p<1e-300) revealed that the benefit is restricted to
feature_031=0:

  feature_016=1 AND feature_031=0 (n=3,508): tx effect = +4.64 months
  feature_016=1 AND feature_031=1 (n=2,860): tx effect = -0.01 months
  feature_016=0 (any feature_031): tx effect ≈ 0

Within the feature_016=1 / feature_031=0 cohort, two further small-
prevalence binary indicators fully suppress the benefit:

  feature_028=1 (n=138 in this cohort): tx effect = -0.29 months
  feature_028=0 (n=3,370):              tx effect = +4.83 months
  feature_005=1 (n=107):                tx effect = -0.07 months
  feature_005=0 (n=3,401):              tx effect = +4.80 months

Combining all four predicates yields the complete responder subgroup
definition:

  feature_016=1  AND  feature_031=0  AND  feature_028=0  AND
  feature_005=0   (n=3,266; 6.5% of cohort)

Within this subgroup:
  feature_018=1: mean pfs_months = 7.88,  median = 8.21
  feature_018=0: mean pfs_months = 3.23,  median = 3.23
  Treatment effect: +4.99 months  (p<1e-300)

Outside this subgroup (n=46,734): treatment effect = -0.006 (p=0.76).
The OLS interaction term feature_018:responder = +4.99 (t=62.5,
p<1e-300). Adding all other main effects to the model brings R^2 from
0.894 to 0.971, and the 3-way interaction term remains coef -4.58
(p<1e-300), confirming the heterogeneity is not confounded.

Important: Stage (feature_014) does NOT modify the feature_018 effect
(interaction p>0.5). The marginal smoking and histology effect-modifications
seen for feature_018 are explained by differential feature_016
prevalence across smoking strata (0.024 in never, 0.119 in former,
0.197 in current smokers) and across histology (0.121 adenocarcinoma,
0.337 squamous): once stratified on feature_016=1, the per-stratum tx
effects are nearly identical (+1.76 to +2.66, with the never-smoker
estimate based on only n=150).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Other treatment heterogeneity searches
------------------------------------------------------------------------
* feature_012, feature_020, feature_027 - exhaustive interaction
  screens against all binary, ordinal, and continuous features yielded
  no interactions surviving even uncorrected p<0.005 with meaningful
  magnitude. These three "treatments" appear inert.
* feature_021 (-0.19 main effect): the only meaningful modifier is
  feature_005=1 (n=1,519), which fully eliminates the harm
  (eff +0.014, n.s. vs eff -0.196 elsewhere). feature_028=1 amplifies
  harm slightly (-0.41). The effect is otherwise homogeneous.
* All 10 pairwise treatment-by-treatment interactions (combinations of
  feature_012/_018/_020/_021/_027) had p>0.19 - no synergy or
  antagonism between candidate treatments.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Biomarker x biomarker interactions
------------------------------------------------------------------------
feature_016 x feature_028 interaction coef -1.21 (p=4e-16); feature_016
x feature_005 interaction coef -0.82 (p=3e-6). These are downstream of
the feature_018 treatment-effect heterogeneity: feature_016's apparent
positive prognostic main effect is largely produced by treated
responders, so any modifier that suppresses the feature_018 benefit
(feature_028=1, feature_005=1) also reduces the apparent feature_016
main effect.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. Hypotheses that were supported vs refuted
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Supported:
  * Strong, monotone prognostic effects of feature_015, feature_014,
    feature_023, feature_011, feature_028, smoking, histology, and
    feature_017 on pfs_months.
  * Treatment-effect heterogeneity of feature_018 concentrated in a
    sharply-defined 4-predicate subgroup (h11 -> h14 -> h25).
  * Quadratic / accelerating shape of feature_015's prognostic effect.

Refuted / null:
  * feature_012, feature_020, feature_027 as drivers of pfs_months
    (no main or interaction effect).
  * Stage as a modifier of feature_018 benefit.
  * Pairwise treatment x treatment interactions.
  * That the feature_018 marginal smoking/histology modification is a
    biological interaction (it is mediated by feature_016 prevalence).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. Best-supported final treatment subgroup hypothesis (pfs_months)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment:    feature_018=1
Outcome:      pfs_months
Direction:    increases pfs_months by approximately +5.0 months
Subgroup:     feature_016=1  AND  feature_031=0
              AND feature_028=0  AND  feature_005=0
Subgroup size: 3,266 (6.5% of cohort)
Within-subgroup effect:  Δmean = +4.99 months (median 3.23 -> 8.21)
Outside-subgroup effect: ≈ 0 (Δmean -0.006, p=0.76)
Each unfavorable predicate (feature_016=0, feature_031=1,
feature_028=1, feature_005=1) independently abolishes the benefit.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
Conclusion
------------------------------------------------------------------------
pfs_months in this NSCLC-style cohort is dominated by strong main-effect
prognostic features (feature_015, feature_014, feature_023, feature_011,
histology, smoking, feature_017, feature_025) which together explain
~89% of variance. The single therapeutic-style intervention with a
detectable benefit is feature_018, but its benefit is highly
heterogeneous: essentially confined to the ~6.5% of patients defined by
feature_016=1 AND feature_031=0 AND feature_028=0 AND feature_005=0,
where the treatment increases progression-free survival by about 5
months. Other balanced binary "treatment" candidates show no clinically
meaningful effect on this outcome.
