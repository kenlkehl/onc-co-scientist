2. CLASSIFYING FEATURES AS BIOMARKERS, TREATMENTS, OR PROGNOSTIC
----------------------------------------------------------------
By looking at how each binary feature's prevalence depends on smoking
status, histology and stage, three classes emerged:

  Likely biomarkers (prevalence varies sharply with smoking):
    feature_022   never 51.3% / former 8.1% / current 3.0%
                  (strongly never-smoker-enriched ‚Äî consistent with an
                  EGFR-mutation-like marker)
    feature_016   never 2.0%  / former 11.9% / current 19.7%
                  (smoker-enriched ‚Äî consistent with a PD-L1-high or
                  smoking-associated biomarker)
    feature_028   never 10.3% / former 4.8% / current 2.6%
                  (never-smoker-enriched ‚Äî likely another biomarker;
                  also enriched in adenocarcinoma)

  Likely treatments (prevalence flat across smoking and stage):
    feature_018, feature_023, feature_011, feature_002, feature_005, ...

  Smoking-correlated factor (‚âàCOPD-like):
    feature_021   never 7.9% / former 25.2% / current 44.9%

3. PROGNOSTIC vs HETEROGENEOUS-RESPONSE BINARIES
------------------------------------------------
For each candidate "treatment" we ran a treatment-by-feature interaction
screen against the major candidate effect modifiers.

  feature_023 (‚àí1.42 main): no interaction larger than feature_023√ófeature_015
    (‚àí0.013 per yr, p=2e-25) and feature_023√ófeature_014 (+0.11, p=6e-5).
    Stage-stratified effect was ‚àí1.53 / ‚àí1.43 / ‚àí1.31 ‚Äî essentially flat.
    Conclusion: feature_023 is a uniform NEGATIVE PROGNOSTIC factor, not a
    treatment with response heterogeneity.

  feature_011 (‚àí0.91 main): only feature_011√ófeature_015 (‚àí0.011/yr,
    p=5e-15). Stage-stratified effect ‚àí1.01 / ‚àí0.91 / ‚àí0.84. Same
    conclusion ‚Äî uniform negative prognostic.

  feature_002, feature_005, feature_021, feature_012, feature_020,
    feature_027: no interaction with any modifier produced a clinically
    meaningful (>1 mo) PFS heterogeneity.

  feature_022 (the never-smoker-enriched biomarker): main effect ~0, but
    the feature_018√ófeature_022 interaction was ‚àí0.24 (p=1e-4). In other
    words, the population-average benefit of feature_018 (+0.35 mo) is
    nearly halved (to +0.11 mo) when feature_022=1 ‚Äî pattern that
    parallels EGFR-mutant resistance to immunotherapy in real NSCLC
    cohorts.

4. THE CENTRAL TREATMENT-EFFECT HETEROGENEITY FINDING
-----------------------------------------------------
The single most important interaction in the dataset is between
feature_018 (treatment) and feature_016 (biomarker):

  OLS pfs_months ~ feature_018 + feature_016 + feature_018:feature_016
    interaction coefficient = +2.558, p ‚âà 0.

  2x2 cell means (mean pfs_months / n):
    f018=0, f016=0   3.33   (n=28,334)
    f018=0, f016=1   3.21   (n= 4,122)
    f018=1, f016=0   3.33   (n=15,298)
    f018=1, f016=1   5.77   (n= 2,246)

Neither feature_018 alone nor feature_016 alone changes PFS materially;
their combination adds ~+2.5 months. This is the classic "predictive
biomarker" pattern.

3-way interaction screening on (feature_018 √ó feature_016 √ó MOD) for
plausible modifiers gave the following large hits:

  MOD = feature_031:  3-way coef = ‚àí4.69,  p ‚âà 0
  MOD = feature_028:  3-way coef = ‚àí2.89,  p = 4e-21
  MOD = feature_015:  3-way coef = +0.021, p = 3e-8
  (feature_014, feature_023, feature_022, hist_squamous: not significant.)

Decomposing the 4-way table feature_018 √ó feature_016 √ó feature_031 √ó
feature_028 reveals one extreme cell:

  f018=1, f016=1, f031=0, f028=0:  mean PFS = 8.09  (n=1,191)
  All 15 other cells:              mean PFS ‚âà 1.8 ‚Äì 3.4

5. FINAL JOINT SUBGROUP HYPOTHESIS (the deliverable)
----------------------------------------------------
TREATMENT:        feature_018 = 1
OUTCOME:          pfs_months  (longer)
DIRECTION:        positive (treatment increases PFS)
MAGNITUDE:        ‚âà +4.8 months
SUBGROUP (all required, conjunctive predicate):
                  feature_016 = 1   AND
                  feature_031 = 0   AND
                  feature_028 = 0

Verification:
  Within subgroup (n = 3,370):
      feature_018 = 0  ‚Üí  mean PFS  3.26  (n = 2,179)
      feature_018 = 1  ‚Üí  mean PFS  8.09  (n = 1,191)
      Difference = +4.83 mo,  t = 60.95,  p ‚âà 0
      Adjusted (for feature_015, 014, 023, 011, 025, 017, smoking,
      histology) coefficient on feature_018 = +4.77 mo (p ‚âà 0).

  Outside subgroup (n = 46,630):
      feature_018 = 0  ‚Üí  mean PFS  3.32
      feature_018 = 1  ‚Üí  mean PFS  3.32
      Difference = ‚àí0.006 mo, p = 0.75.
      Adjusted coefficient = +0.001 mo, p = 0.83.

Each of the three subgroup predicates is necessary ‚Äî removing any one
collapses the effect:
  ‚Ä¢ Drop the feature_016=1 requirement ‚Üí effect dilutes from +4.8 to
    +0.0 (the f018-alone main effect outside biomarker-positive cells).
  ‚Ä¢ Allow feature_031=1 inside the biomarker-positive cell ‚Üí effect
    drops from +4.65 to ‚àí0.01.
  ‚Ä¢ Allow feature_028=1 inside the biomarker-positive feature_031=0
    cell ‚Üí mean PFS drops from 8.09 to 2.35 (n=46).

Robustness within the responder subgroup:
  By stage:    +4.79 (stage 0) / +4.93 (stage 1) / +4.67 (stage 2) ‚Äî flat.
  By smoking:  +3.99 (never) / +5.09 (former) / +4.59 (current) ‚Äî stable.
  By age (3-way with feature_015): +0.021 mo extra benefit per year of
  age (small but significant).

6. WHAT WAS REFUTED OR FOUND NULL
---------------------------------
  ‚Ä¢ Hypothesis that any of the additional binary candidates (feature_002,
    feature_005, feature_012, feature_020, feature_021, feature_027)
    defines a treatment-effect subgroup of clinical magnitude ‚Äî null.
  ‚Ä¢ Hypothesis that feature_023 or feature_011 represents a treatment
    with responder heterogeneity ‚Äî refuted; both behave as uniform
    negative prognostic factors.
  ‚Ä¢ Hypothesis that any of the 12 lab-like continuous features
    (feature_003, 004, 008, 009, 010, 013, 019, 024, 026, 029, 030,
    033) has independent prognostic value ‚Äî refuted (all near-zero
    coefficients).
  ‚Ä¢ Hypothesis that former vs never smokers differ in adjusted PFS ‚Äî
    refuted; they are indistinguishable, both better than current
    smokers.

7. CLINICAL INTERPRETATION (HYPOTHETICAL)
-----------------------------------------
The pattern is highly reminiscent of an immune-checkpoint-inhibitor
(IO)‚Äìstyle therapy with a positive predictive biomarker:

  ‚Ä¢ feature_018 behaves like a treatment indicator (e.g., IO).
  ‚Ä¢ feature_016 behaves like a positive predictive biomarker
    (e.g., PD-L1 high; smoker-enriched).
  ‚Ä¢ feature_031 behaves like a sex-like effect modifier that abolishes
    response in one half of biomarker-positive patients.
  ‚Ä¢ feature_028 behaves like a co-resistance marker (e.g., a driver
    mutation that confers IO resistance even in PD-L1-high patients).
  ‚Ä¢ feature_022 behaves like an EGFR-mutation-like marker (never-smoker-
    enriched, partial resistance to feature_018).
  ‚Ä¢ feature_023, feature_011, feature_014 behave as classical poor-
    prognostic disease characteristics.
  ‚Ä¢ feature_015 behaves like a continuous prognostic axis dominating
    overall PFS variance (the slope direction is unusual for "age" and
    may reflect a different underlying variable in this dataset).

These interpretations are tentative ‚Äî feature labels are anonymized ‚Äî
but the statistical structure of the dataset is unambiguous:
TREATMENT feature_018 yields a ~+4.8-month PFS benefit ONLY in the
joint subgroup feature_016=1 ‚à© feature_031=0 ‚à© feature_028=0, and is
inert everywhere else.
