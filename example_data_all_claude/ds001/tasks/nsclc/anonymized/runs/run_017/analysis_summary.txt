ANALYSIS SUMMARY -- ds001_nsclc (n = 50,000; outcome: pfs_months)

OVERVIEW

The dataset contains 50,000 NSCLC patient records, 33 anonymized features
(13 binary indicators, one ordinal three-level indicator feature_014, two
categorical text columns -- feature_001 [current/former/never] and feature_006
[adenocarcinoma/squamous] -- and 17 continuous features), and a single outcome,
progression-free survival in months (pfs_months; mean 3.43 mo, SD 2.23, range
0-15.33). Across 25 iterations I screened main effects, fit full-cohort
multivariable models, ran systematic pairwise and three-way interaction
searches, and converged on a single dominant treatment-effect heterogeneity
pattern. Below I walk through what was supported, what was refuted, and the
final integrated picture.


ITERATIONS 1-3 -- MAIN EFFECTS

Binary features (Welch t-tests). Strongest negative effects: feature_023
(prev 65%, diff -1.44 mo, p<1e-300), feature_011 (prev 25%, -0.92 mo, p<1e-300),
feature_028 (prev 5%, -0.81 mo, p=5.6e-66), feature_031 (prev 45%, -0.20 mo,
p=7.7e-25), feature_021 (-0.19 mo, p=1e-17). Strongest positive effects:
feature_016 (prev 13%, +0.78 mo, p=7e-94), feature_018 (prev 35%, +0.32 mo,
p=6e-49), feature_022 (+0.19 mo, p=6e-11). feature_002, feature_012, feature_020,
and feature_027 had essentially null univariate effects (p > 0.05).

Categorical/ordinal. feature_014 showed a strict monotonic decrease in PFS
across its three levels (4.33 -> 3.19 -> 2.15 mo; F-test p<1e-300), consistent
with an ordered severity score. Smoking status (feature_001): current smokers
2.99 mo vs former 3.61 mo and never 3.67 mo (ANOVA p=4e-187) -- current is
worse, former and never indistinguishable. Histology (feature_006): squamous
2.81 mo vs adenocarcinoma 3.67 mo (p<1e-300).

Continuous features. feature_015 (range 30-90, mean 65) had a large positive
correlation with PFS (Pearson r = +0.757, p<1e-300) -- the strongest single
predictor in the dataset. feature_017 r=-0.127 (p=7e-179) and feature_025
r=+0.099 (p=3e-109) were modest. feature_032 was just-significant (r=-0.018,
p=4e-5). All other continuous laboratory-like features (feature_019, 003, 004,
007, 008, 009, 010, 013, 024, 026, 029, 030, 033) were not univariately
associated with PFS (|r|<0.01, p>0.05) and were therefore deprioritized as
prognostic factors -- though they remained on the candidate list for
heterogeneity analyses.


ITERATIONS 4-6 -- MULTIVARIABLE MODEL AND INTERACTION SCREENS

A saturated OLS containing all 33 predictors (with smoking and histology
encoded as dummies) reached R^2 = 0.894. Independent (adjusted) effects:
  feature_015      +0.168 / unit  (p<1e-300)  -- dominant continuous
  feature_014      -1.10  / level (p<1e-300)
  feature_023      -1.42         (p<1e-300)
  feature_011      -0.91         (p<1e-300)
  feature_016      +0.89         (p<1e-300)
  feature_018      +0.32         (p<1e-300)
  hist_squamous    -0.72         (p<1e-300)
  smoke_current    -0.55         (p<1e-300)
  feature_017      -0.073 / unit  (p<1e-300)
  feature_025      +0.46  / unit  (p<1e-300)
  feature_005      -0.11         (p=7e-9)
  feature_031      -0.20         (p<1e-300)
  feature_032      -0.0004/ unit  (p=1.5e-28)
All other features dropped to non-significance.

The pairwise binary x binary interaction screen returned an extremely large
positive interaction term:
  feature_016 x feature_018 :  beta_inter = +2.558,  p = 0  (largest in dataset)
and an equally striking negative interaction:
  feature_016 x feature_031 :  beta_inter = -1.694,  p = 3.2e-178
Heterogeneity searches treating each binary feature as a candidate treatment
confirmed feature_018 as the standout: no other binary candidate (feature_023,
feature_011, feature_022, feature_028) showed comparable concentrated
heterogeneity (their largest interaction terms were below 0.20 in magnitude).


ITERATIONS 7-12 -- THE f016 x f018 x f031 ARCHITECTURE

The 2x2 PFS table for feature_016 by feature_018 made the synergy plain:
       f018=0   f018=1
f016=0  3.334    3.329
f016=1  3.213    5.766    <-- only this cell departs from the 3.3-mo baseline

Stratified analyses showed feature_018 had no effect within feature_016=0
(diff -0.005, p=0.80) but a +2.55-month effect within feature_016=1 (p=2e-202).
Adding feature_031 to the cross-tabulation refined the picture:

  f016=1, f031=0:  feature_018 effect = +4.64 mo (n_responder=1237, p<1e-300)
  f016=1, f031=1:  feature_018 effect = -0.01 mo (n=2860, p=0.88)

The triple-positive cell (feature_016=1 AND feature_018=1 AND feature_031=0;
n=1237) had mean PFS 7.88 mo (median 8.03) versus 3.32 mo in the rest of the
cohort. Outside this triple, the three component features each had near-null
effects on PFS (feature_018 diff +0.001, p=0.97; feature_031 diff -0.0004,
p=0.99). After full multivariable adjustment for all 33 covariates plus the
three component flags, an indicator for the triple-positive cell carried
beta = +4.59 mo (p<1e-300) while the three component main effects collapsed to
zero -- the entire feature_016/feature_018/feature_031 signal is the
interaction. Within feature_016=1, the (feature_018, feature_031) interaction
term was approximately equal in magnitude and opposite in sign to the
feature_018 main effect (-4.585 vs +4.591), implying near-complete suppression
of the feature_018 benefit by feature_031=1.


ITERATIONS 13-16 -- TWO ADDITIONAL SUPPRESSORS (f028, f005); REFINED SUBGROUP

Inside the triple-positive responder cell I screened every other binary feature
for whether it abrogated the response. Two clear additional suppressors emerged:

  feature_028=1 within triple-positive (n=46): mean PFS 2.35 vs 8.09 mo
    (diff -5.74, p=7.2e-22). Note feature_028's full-cohort main effect on PFS
    is only -0.81 mo, so its effect within this subgroup is roughly 7 times
    larger -- i.e., feature_028 is not merely additive, it is a near-complete
    suppressor of the feature_018 response.

  feature_005=1 within triple-positive (n=39): mean PFS 3.41 vs 8.02 mo
    (diff -4.61, p=2.4e-14). feature_005's full-cohort main effect is only
    -0.12 mo, so its within-subgroup effect is about 38x larger. Same
    interpretation: a within-stratum near-complete suppressor.

No other binary feature (feature_011, 022, 023, 002, smoking, histology,
feature_012, 020, 021, 027) produced more than a 0.5-month change in the
feature_018 benefit. These eight features therefore appear to act
approximately additively (consistent with their main effects in the saturated
model), not as effect modifiers of the feature_018 response.

Combining the two new suppressors with the original three-way subgroup yields
the refined "quint" responder definition:
  feature_016 = 1 AND feature_018 = 1 AND feature_031 = 0
              AND feature_028 = 0 AND feature_005 = 0
  n = 1154; mean PFS = 8.234 mo (SD 2.27, median 8.21)
  rest (n = 48846): mean PFS = 3.318 mo
  unadjusted difference  =  +4.916 mo
  multivariable-adjusted (all 33 covariates + 5 component flags + indicator):
    beta_quint = +4.923 mo, p < 1e-300, full-model R^2 = 0.979


ITERATIONS 17-18 -- CONTINUOUS EFFECT MODIFICATION

Within the (feature_016=1, feature_031=0) responder pool (n=3508), feature_015
positively modified the magnitude of the feature_018 effect:
  PFS = -7.63 + 2.566 * feature_018 + 0.167 * feature_015
        + 0.030 * (feature_018 * feature_015)        (p_interaction = 2e-9)
At feature_015 = 30, the feature_018 effect is roughly +2.57 + 0.030*30 = +3.5 mo;
at feature_015 = 90, it rises to +2.57 + 0.030*90 = +5.3 mo. So older patients
in the responder pool gain progressively more from feature_018.

Within the triple-positive cell (n=1237), feature_017 retained its full-cohort
prognostic effect (r=-0.096, p=7e-4). Other continuous labs were either
weakly significant in only the responder subgroup (feature_010 r=+0.061
p=0.033) or not significant. There is no second concentrated continuous
heterogeneity beyond feature_015 amplifying the response and feature_017
acting as a uniform negative prognostic.


ITERATIONS 19-22 -- WHAT IS NOT THERE

The candidate-treatment heterogeneity searches for feature_023 and feature_011
-- the two largest negative main effects in the cohort -- did NOT reveal a
concentrated responder/non-responder structure. Their largest binary modifiers
shifted the effect by less than 0.20 in magnitude, all small relative to the
main effects of -1.42 and -0.91 respectively. These two features are best
understood as broadly homogeneous prognostic factors, not as treatments with
biomarker-defined responder subgroups.

A systematic three-way binary subgroup search (all triples of binary features,
with n_in >= 50) returned (feature_016=1, feature_018=1, feature_031=0) as the
unique cell with maximum within-vs-rest PFS gap (+4.56 mo). The next largest
gaps were either rephrasings of the same interaction (e.g. with feature_023=0
or feature_028=0 also fixed) or small additive stacks of main effects
(e.g. feature_023=0 + feature_028=0 + smoke_current=0 -> +1.53 mo, exactly the
sum of the three main effects). No second independent treatment-effect
heterogeneity pattern was found.

Logistic models for feature_016 and feature_018 status on the other features
showed only weak associations (smallest p around 0.001), consistent with these
two indicators being approximately as-if-randomized rather than strongly
confounded by other measured features.


ITERATIONS 23-25 -- FINAL INTEGRATED MODEL AND CONCLUSIONS

A parsimonious 10-variable OLS containing only the variables that survived
the screens and their key interaction indicator -
PFS ~ 1 + feature_015 + feature_014 + feature_023 + feature_011
        + hist_squamous + smoke_current + feature_017 + feature_025
        + feature_032 + quint
- attains R^2 = 0.978, essentially matching the saturated 33-variable +
indicator model (R^2 = 0.979). All ten coefficients are highly significant
(p < 1e-30), confirming that this set captures the principal PFS signals.

FINAL TREATMENT-EFFECT SUBGROUP STATEMENT.  Treating feature_018 as the
candidate treatment, the treatment effect on pfs_months is concentrated in --
and approximately exclusive to -- the subgroup defined by:

    feature_016 = 1
  AND feature_031 = 0
  AND feature_028 = 0
  AND feature_005 = 0

In that subgroup the effect of feature_018 = 1 versus 0 on PFS is approximately
+5 months, signed positive (longer PFS = better outcome). Outside that
subgroup the feature_018 effect on PFS is approximately zero (point estimate
near 0 mo, p > 0.9 in the held-out stratum). Within the responder subgroup,
the magnitude of the feature_018 benefit additionally scales positively with
feature_015 (interaction beta +0.030 per unit; older patients gain more).

Equivalently: feature_031 (prevalence 45%), feature_028 (5%), and feature_005
(3%) each act as near-complete suppressors of the feature_018 response within
feature_016=1 patients. feature_031 abolishes ~100% of the response on its
own; feature_028 and feature_005 abolish it within their small strata.
This is a textbook biomarker-treatment interaction with a primary marker
(feature_016) and three independent resistance/abrogation flags
(feature_031 / feature_028 / feature_005).

OTHER PRIMARY DRIVERS OF PFS (UNIFORM, NOT HETEROGENEOUS).
- feature_015 (continuous 30-90, likely age or follow-up time): r = +0.76,
  +0.168 mo per unit, by far the largest single source of PFS variance.
- feature_014 (ordinal 0/1/2, likely a performance or stage score):
  -1.10 mo per level, monotonic.
- feature_023 (binary, prev 65%): -1.42 mo, broadly uniform across
  subgroups; no concentrated heterogeneity.
- feature_011 (binary, prev 25%): -0.91 mo, uniform.
- feature_006 = squamous (vs adenocarcinoma): -0.72 mo.
- feature_001 = current smoker (vs former or never): -0.55 mo.
- feature_017 (continuous, range 0-28): -0.073 mo per unit.
- feature_025 (continuous, 1.7-5.5): +0.46 mo per unit.

WHAT WAS REFUTED.  The hypothesis that feature_023 or feature_011 carries
biomarker-defined treatment-effect heterogeneity (analogous to feature_018)
was not supported. The hypothesis that the lab-like continuous features
beyond feature_015, feature_017, feature_025, and feature_032 carry
prognostic information was not supported (all univariate p > 0.05 and all
adjusted p > 0.05). The hypothesis that feature_028 acts only as an
additive negative prognostic was refuted: within the responder subgroup
its effect is roughly seven times larger than its main effect, identifying
it as a specific suppressor of the feature_018 response (and the same
applies to feature_005, with an even larger ratio).
