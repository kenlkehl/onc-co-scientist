ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_nsclc (50,000 patients)
=================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (continuous progression-free-survival in months;
mean 3.43, sd 2.23, range 0–15.3). Treated as continuous (no
censoring information given). All 50,000 records complete; no
missing values.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Univariate / main-effect screen (Iter 1–3)
------------------------------------------------------------
A multivariable OLS regression of pfs_months on all features
yielded R^2 = 0.894. Significant adjusted predictors (units = months
PFS unless stated):

  feature_015     +0.168 per unit  (very strong; r=+0.757 univariate)
  feature_014     -1.10  per level (clean monotone 4.33 -> 3.19 -> 2.15
                                    at levels 0/1/2 — ECOG-PS-like)
  feature_023     -1.42            (large negative)
  feature_011     -0.91            (moderate negative)
  feature_016     +0.89            (large positive — biomarker, see below)
  feature_025     +0.46 per unit
  feature_018     +0.32  on average (heterogeneous, see below)
  feature_017     -0.073 per unit  (continuous, plausibly tumor burden)
  feature_031     -0.20            (small but significant)
  smoking-current -0.55  vs former
  squamous hist.  -0.72  vs adenocarcinoma
  feature_005     -0.11            (rare 3% indicator)

Several other variables (feature_002, feature_020, feature_027,
feature_012, feature_022, feature_024, feature_004, feature_013,
feature_026, feature_029, feature_030, feature_007, feature_009,
feature_033, feature_010, feature_019, feature_003, feature_008,
feature_021) had small/null adjusted effects.

Smoking and histology effects were essentially additive (interaction
terms p>0.20).

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Identifying randomly-assigned (treatment-like) indicators
------------------------------------------------------------
For each binary feature, the maximum range of its prevalence across
all major strata (feature_006, feature_001, feature_014, and other
binaries) was computed. Features whose prevalence is essentially
flat across strata (max-min spread < 0.03) are most consistent with
random assignment / treatment indicators:

  feature_005 (3%)   spread 0.006
  feature_023 (65%)  spread 0.008
  feature_002 (15%)  spread 0.010
  feature_031 (45%)  spread 0.010
  feature_018 (35%)  spread 0.012
  feature_011 (25%)  spread 0.013
  feature_027 (30%)  spread 0.019
  feature_012 (50%)  spread 0.020
  feature_020 (30%)  spread 0.026

Conversely, feature_022 (spread 0.48; 51% in never-smokers vs 3% in
current), feature_016 (0.18), feature_021 (0.37), feature_028 (0.08)
are clearly NOT randomized — they correlate with histology / smoking
and are likely biomarkers or natural clinical conditions.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Treatment-effect heterogeneity for each candidate
------------------------------------------------------------
Each randomly-distributed binary was screened for interaction with
every other feature on pfs_months. Findings:

(a) feature_018 — overall +0.32 mo, but heterogeneity huge.
    feature_018 x feature_016 interaction coef = +2.56 mo
    (unadjusted; p~0).
       In feature_016 = 0 (n=43,632, 87%): feature_018 effect
         = -0.005 mo, p=0.80 — null.
       In feature_016 = 1 (n=6,368, 13%):  feature_018 effect
         = +2.55 mo, p=2.5e-275.
    The +0.32 mo overall benefit is entirely driven by the 13%
    feature_016 = 1 subgroup.

    Within feature_016 = 1, three further variables sharply
    abolish the benefit when their unfavorable value is present:

       feature_031 — interaction coef -4.66 (p~0)
         f016=1 & f031=0: tx-ctrl = +4.64 mo (p~0)
         f016=1 & f031=1: tx-ctrl = -0.01 mo (p=0.88) — ABOLISHED
       feature_028 — interaction coef -3.02 (p=3e-18)
         f016=1 & f028=0: tx-ctrl = +2.67 mo (p~0)
         f016=1 & f028=1: tx-ctrl = -0.35 mo (p=0.23) — ABOLISHED
       feature_005 — interaction coef -2.55 (p=2e-10)
         f016=1 & f005=0: tx-ctrl = +2.63 mo (p~0)
         f016=1 & f005=1: tx-ctrl = +0.08 mo (p=0.81) — ABOLISHED

    A 3-way interaction model
    pfs ~ feature_018 * feature_016 * feature_031 (and adjusted)
    confirms:
       feature_018:feature_016         = +4.59 mo
       feature_018:feature_016:feature_031 = -4.58 mo
       all other lower-order terms ~0.

    Tree-based CATE estimation (T-learner with depth-4 regression
    trees) corroborates: 100% of patients with predicted CATE > 4 mo
    are feature_016=1 AND feature_031=0; 100% of patients with CATE
    > 2 mo are feature_016=1.

(b) feature_023 — large -1.4 mo main effect, broadly homogeneous.
    Effect within strata of feature_001/006/014/016 all in
    [-1.31, -1.53] mo. The strongest interaction is feature_023 x
    feature_015 (coef -0.013/yr, p=2e-25) — modest amplification
    with higher feature_015. No biomarker-defined subgroup of
    interest.

(c) feature_011 — -0.9 mo homogeneous main effect; only sizable
    interaction is feature_011 x feature_015 (-0.011/yr, p=5e-15)
    — slightly worse impact in older patients.

(d) feature_012, feature_020, feature_027 — all balanced indicators
    with no significant adjusted main effect and no interaction
    surviving multiple-test correction. They appear to be
    treatment-like exposures with no effect on PFS.

(e) feature_005 has a modest homogeneous -0.11 mo main effect AND a
    role as feature_018-resistance modifier within feature_016=1.

(f) feature_028 has a homogeneous -0.8 mo main harm AND a role
    abolishing feature_018 benefit when feature_016=1.

(g) feature_031 has a homogeneous small -0.18 mo effect among
    feature_016=0 patients but fully abolishes the +4.6 mo
    feature_018 benefit when feature_016=1 (a "resistance"
    modifier).

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Final treatment-effect subgroup definition
------------------------------------------------------------
The complete subgroup in which feature_018 produces a clinically
meaningful PFS benefit is:

    feature_016 = 1   AND
    feature_031 = 0   AND
    feature_028 = 0   AND
    feature_005 = 0

n = 3,266 (6.5% of the cohort).
  feature_018 = 1 (n=1,154): mean PFS = 8.234 mo
  feature_018 = 0 (n=2,112): mean PFS = 3.249 mo
  Difference = +4.985 mo, p ≈ 0.

In the complement (everyone else, n=46,734):
  feature_018 = 1: 3.317 mo
  feature_018 = 0: 3.323 mo
  Difference = -0.006 mo, p = 0.76 (null).

Treatment benefit is essentially binary in the cohort: ~+5 months
PFS within this 4-feature subgroup, indistinguishable-from-zero
benefit elsewhere. This is the textbook predictive-biomarker pattern
of a targeted therapy, with feature_016 as the eligibility biomarker
and feature_031 / feature_028 / feature_005 acting as resistance /
co-mutation / unfavorable modifiers.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Hypotheses supported vs refuted
------------------------------------------------------------
Supported:
  - Strong main effects of feature_015, feature_014, feature_023,
    feature_011, feature_016, feature_017, feature_025, smoking,
    histology on pfs_months.
  - feature_014 acts as a monotone ordered factor (PS-like).
  - Feature_018 has a heterogeneous treatment effect concentrated
    almost entirely in the feature_016=1 subgroup, further refined
    by feature_031, feature_028, feature_005.
  - feature_023 is a randomly-assigned, broadly-harmful exposure.
  - feature_022 is a smoking-correlated natural feature (51% of
    never-smokers), not a predictive biomarker for feature_018.

Refuted / null:
  - feature_012, feature_020, feature_027 as treatments with PFS
    impact: no significant main effects and no clinically
    meaningful subgroup effects.
  - Smoking x histology interaction on PFS.
  - feature_002 as a clinically meaningful PFS modifier.
  - feature_022 as an effect modifier of feature_018.
  - Existence of a SECOND treatment with strong predictive
    heterogeneity beyond feature_018: thorough screens within
    feature_016=0 found no other binary with a >2-month subgroup
    treatment effect.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. Overall conclusions
------------------------------------------------------------
Across this 50,000-patient NSCLC cohort, pfs_months is well
predicted (R^2 ≈ 0.89) by a small set of patient features
(feature_015, feature_014, feature_023, feature_011, feature_017,
feature_025, smoking, histology) and a single treatment-biomarker
interaction.

There is exactly one randomized intervention (feature_018) with
genuine predictive heterogeneity. Its benefit is ~+5 months of PFS
and is restricted to patients who are
  (i)  feature_016 = 1 (~13% of cohort, the "eligibility biomarker"),
  (ii) feature_031 = 0 (the dominant resistance modifier within
       biomarker-positive patients),
  (iii)feature_028 = 0,
  (iv) feature_005 = 0.

Outside this 6.5% subgroup, feature_018 has no measurable PFS
effect. The clinical implication mirrors a targeted-therapy +
predictive-biomarker scenario (e.g., a TKI-style agent), and
emphasises the need to test treatment effects within biomarker-
defined subgroups rather than relying on the overall +0.32 month
mean effect.
