Analysis Summary - ds001_nsclc (n=50,000)
=========================================

Outcome: pfs_months (continuous, range 0-15.33, mean 3.43, no censoring indicator).
Approach: 14 iterations of propose-test-refine on a single linear-regression-friendly outcome,
combining univariate screens, multivariable adjustment, treatment-by-feature interaction
screening, and progressive subgroup refinement leading to a saturated three-way-interaction
model and validation of a fully specified responder subgroup.

1. Data structure (iteration 1)
-------------------------------
33 anonymized features split naturally into:
  - One ordinal categorical (feature_014 in {0,1,2}, mean 0.80, behaves like ECOG performance status)
  - Two unordered categoricals (feature_001 smoking with current/former/never; feature_006 histology
    adenocarcinoma 72% / squamous 28%)
  - One age-like continuous (feature_015, range 30-90, mean 65)
  - A small set of continuous labs (feature_007 sodium-like, feature_009 hemoglobin-like,
    feature_025 albumin-like, feature_033 WBC-like, plus feature_017, feature_032, feature_010,
    feature_024, feature_003, feature_030, feature_008, feature_004, feature_013, feature_026,
    feature_029)
  - Many binary indicators with prevalences ranging from 3% (feature_005) to 65% (feature_023);
    those with balanced prevalence (~30-50%) and no clear biomarker pattern (feature_012, _018,
    _020, _021, _027, _031) are candidate treatment indicators.

2. Univariate associations with pfs_months (iteration 2)
--------------------------------------------------------
Strong negative effects (ANOVA / two-sample): feature_014 (-2.18 months max-min, p<1e-300);
feature_023 (-1.44, p<1e-300); feature_011 (-0.92, p<1e-300); feature_006 squamous vs adeno
(-0.86, p<1e-300); feature_028 (-0.81, p=6e-66); feature_001 current vs never (-0.69, p<1e-187);
feature_031 (-0.20, p=8e-25).
Strong positive effects: feature_015 (Spearman rho +0.769, p~0; deciles range 0.74 -> 6.55 months);
feature_016 (+0.78, p=7e-94); feature_018 (+0.32, p=6e-49); feature_022 (+0.19, p=6e-11);
feature_025 albumin-like (rho +0.10, p=6e-111); feature_017 (rho -0.12, p=2e-172).
Most other continuous labs are not univariately related to pfs_months (Spearman rho < |0.02|,
p>0.1); they were retained in adjustment models for completeness but contributed little.

3. Multivariable adjustment (iteration 3)
-----------------------------------------
A single OLS for pfs_months on all 33 features (with categorical dummies) achieves R^2 = 0.894.
Top adjusted coefficients (descending |t|): feature_015 +0.168 / year (t=512); feature_014
-1.10 / level (t=-230); feature_023 -1.42 (t=-208); feature_011 -0.91 (t=-122); squamous histology
-0.72 (t=-95); feature_016 +0.89 (t=+90); feature_017 -0.073 / unit (t=-87); feature_025 +0.46 / unit
(t=+71); feature_018 +0.32 (t=+47); current smoking -0.55 (t=-44); feature_031 -0.20 (t=-31);
feature_032 -4.5e-4 / unit (t=-11); feature_005 -0.11 (t=-5.8). feature_012, feature_020,
feature_021, feature_022, feature_027 and most pure-noise labs are not significant after
adjustment, identifying them as candidate non-prognostic indicators.

4. Treatment identification (iteration 4)
-----------------------------------------
Among the candidate treatment indicators, only feature_018 has a non-trivial main effect
(+0.32 adjusted, p<1e-48). Its prevalence is essentially constant (~35%) across feature_016,
feature_031, feature_028 and feature_005 strata, consistent with random treatment assignment
rather than channeled prescribing. The other balanced binaries (feature_012, _020, _021, _027,
_002, _022) have null adjusted main effects and behave as either non-treatments or treatments
without average benefit. Subsequent analyses focused on feature_018 as the candidate treatment.

5. Treatment-effect heterogeneity screen (iteration 5)
------------------------------------------------------
A pairwise (treatment x binary modifier) interaction screen across the eight candidate
treatments and 15 binary/categorical modifiers (with full covariate adjustment) produced one
dominant signal: feature_018 x feature_016 = +2.535 (p<1e-300). Other strong interactions all
involved feature_018 or feature_016: feature_018 x feature_031 = -0.585 (p<1e-300); feature_028
x feature_016 = -0.919 (p=6e-76); feature_018 x feature_028 = -0.343 (p=2e-27); feature_018 x
smk_current = +0.253 (p=4e-65); feature_018 x feature_005 = -0.328 (p=2e-16). No other treatment
candidate showed an interaction within an order of magnitude of these.

6. Stratified treatment effects (iterations 6-7)
------------------------------------------------
- feature_016=0 (n=43,632, 87% of cohort): adjusted feature_018 effect = +0.001 (p=0.75) - null.
- feature_016=1 (n=6,368, 13%): adjusted feature_018 effect = +2.529 (p<1e-300).
- feature_016=1 & feature_031=0 (n=3,508): adjusted feature_018 effect = +4.586 (p<1e-300).
- feature_016=1 & feature_031=1 (n=2,860): adjusted feature_018 effect = +0.011 (p=0.44).
The entire treatment benefit lives in feature_016=1 patients, and feature_031=1 fully
suppresses it.

7. Disentangling smoking (iteration 8a)
---------------------------------------
The whole-cohort feature_018 x smk_current interaction (+0.253, p=4e-65) was driven entirely
by enrichment of smokers in the feature_016=1 stratum: within feature_016=1 the treatment
effect is +2.516 in non-current-smokers and +2.549 in current smokers; within feature_016=0
both smoking strata show ~0 effect. Smoking is therefore not an independent modifier and was
dropped from the subgroup definition.

8. Within-subgroup additional modifiers (iteration 8b)
------------------------------------------------------
Inside the feature_016=1 & feature_031=0 cell, two more binary suppressors emerged:
feature_028 (interaction -4.763, p<1e-300) and feature_005 (-4.799, p<1e-300). Each individually
abolishes the treatment benefit. Continuous feature_015 (age) shows a mild positive scaling
(+0.028 per year, p=9e-34) but the effect is large in every age tertile and so age is not an
inclusion criterion.

9. Maximal responder subgroup (iteration 9)
-------------------------------------------
Defined as feature_016=1 AND feature_031=0 AND feature_028=0 AND feature_005=0 (n=3,266,
6.5% of cohort).
  - Adjusted feature_018 effect = +4.925 months (SE 0.0095, p<1e-300).
  - Crude means: untreated 3.249 (n=2,112); treated 8.234 (n=1,154); difference +4.985 months.
  - In each of the three single-suppressor cells (feature_028=1, feature_005=1, or feature_031=1
    while feature_016=1), the adjusted effect collapses to ~0 (all p>0.4).
  - In the entire complement (n=46,734) the adjusted feature_018 effect is essentially zero.

10. Saturated three-way interaction model (iteration 10)
--------------------------------------------------------
The four-way structure was confirmed in a single saturated OLS:
  - feature_018 main effect = +0.0018 (p=0.70) - no benefit when none of the biomarkers fire.
  - feature_018 x feature_016 = +4.768 (p<1e-300)
  - feature_018 x feature_031 = -0.0018 (p=0.80) - no effect outside feature_016=1.
  - feature_018 x feature_016 x feature_031 = -4.586 (p<1e-300)
  - feature_018 x feature_016 x feature_028 = -2.402 (p<1e-300)
  - feature_018 x feature_016 x feature_005 = -2.773 (p<1e-300)
Adjusted R^2 = 0.9752. The treatment effect is fully captured by the
feature_016=1 & feature_031=0 & feature_028=0 & feature_005=0 cell.

11. Other treatment candidates (iteration 11)
---------------------------------------------
Within the maximal responder subgroup, no other candidate treatment shows a clinically
meaningful effect: feature_012 -0.004 (p=0.63), feature_020 +0.003 (p=0.77), feature_027 +0.005
(p=0.62), feature_002 -0.001 (p=0.91), feature_021 -0.033 (p=9e-4 but tiny). The full systematic
heterogeneity search across treatments yielded only one treatment with usable variation.

12. Continuous-feature interaction screen (iteration 13)
--------------------------------------------------------
Across all 17 continuous features, the only feature_018 interaction that survives at p<0.01
in the adjusted whole-cohort model is feature_015 (age), coefficient +0.0035 / year (p=3e-7).
This reflects the modest within-subgroup age modulation noted earlier and does not constitute
a separate effect-modifier axis.

13. Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
------------------------------------
Supported:
  - feature_015 (age) positively associated with pfs_months (huge, monotonic; iteration 2-3).
  - feature_014, feature_023, feature_011, feature_006 squamous, feature_017, current smoking
    independently lower pfs_months (iteration 3).
  - feature_016, feature_025 (albumin-like) independently raise pfs_months (iteration 3).
  - feature_018 is a treatment indicator; benefit is concentrated in feature_016=1 patients
    (iterations 5-6).
  - feature_031, feature_028, feature_005 each independently nullify the feature_018 benefit
    even within feature_016=1 (iterations 7-9).
  - The maximal responder subgroup of feature_016=1 & feature_031=0 & feature_028=0 &
    feature_005=0 captures the entire treatment effect (~+4.9 months PFS gain) (iterations 9-10).

Refuted:
  - feature_012, feature_020, feature_027, feature_022, feature_002 are not treatments with
    detectable effects on pfs_months (iteration 4 + iteration 11).
  - Smoking is not an independent modifier of the feature_018 effect (iteration 8a).
  - No continuous lab beyond age modifies the feature_018 treatment effect once the four-way
    biomarker structure is accounted for (iteration 13).

14. Final, best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis
--------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment: feature_018.
Outcome: pfs_months.
Direction: positive (treatment increases pfs_months).
Magnitude: +4.93 months adjusted (+4.99 crude).
Subgroup definition (all four predicates required):
  feature_016 = 1
  AND feature_031 = 0
  AND feature_028 = 0
  AND feature_005 = 0
Outside this subgroup the feature_018 effect on pfs_months is statistically and clinically
indistinguishable from zero.
