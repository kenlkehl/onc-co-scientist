ds001_breast — Analysis Summary
================================

Cohort: 50,000 breast cancer patient records, single PFS-months outcome, 38 features
spanning demographics, performance status, anatomic stage, biomarkers (ER/PR/HER2,
HER2-low, BRCA1/2, PIK3CA, Ki-67), routine labs, and six treatment indicators
(tamoxifen, palbociclib, trastuzumab, olaparib, sacituzumab govitecan, pembrolizumab).
No missing data. Treatments are essentially randomly allocated with respect to all
other features (each treatment's prevalence varies by <1% across biomarker strata),
so unadjusted treatment-effect contrasts have a causal interpretation under random
allocation, modulo finite-sample chance imbalance.

==============================================================
1. PROGNOSTIC MAIN EFFECTS (univariate, on PFS in months)
==============================================================
The strongest prognostic features (univariate slopes vs PFS-months):

  ECOG performance status                   -1.180 / grade   (p<1e-300)
  Stage IV disease                          -1.536            (p<1e-300)
  Brain metastases                          -1.050            (p=1.8e-179)
  Weight loss in prior 6 months             -0.075 / %        (p=4.1e-148)
  PIK3CA mutation                           -0.559            (p=2.4e-126)
  ER positivity                             +0.568            (p=2.2e-121)
  Serum albumin                             +0.499 / g/dL     (p=9.4e-112)
  Ki-67 percentage                          -0.019 / %        (p=3.0e-93)
  HER2 positivity                           -0.459            (p=1.2e-56)
  PR positivity                             +0.362            (p=8.1e-55)
  HER2-low                                  +0.114            (p=7.4e-7)
  Age (years)                               +0.176 / yr       (p<1e-300)  <-- direction OPPOSITE to clinical intuition

The "older = longer PFS" finding is unusual and persists in multivariable models;
it is a real feature of this dataset but should not be over-interpreted clinically.
Several lab values (LDH, alkaline phosphatase, calcium, total bilirubin) are weakly
prognostic in the expected direction but contribute little incremental information.
Markers with no detectable PFS association: BRCA1, sex_female, postmenopausal,
node_positive, tumor_size_cm, hemoglobin, AST/ALT, BUN, sodium, creatinine, NLR, CRP.

==============================================================
2. UNADJUSTED TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS
==============================================================
  treatment_palbociclib              +1.098 months  (p<1e-300)   <-- only meaningful main effect
  treatment_tamoxifen                +0.043          (p=0.077)    NS
  treatment_trastuzumab              -0.060          (p=0.030)    marginal, wrong direction
  treatment_olaparib                 -0.036          (p=0.33)     NS
  treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan    -0.007          (p=0.84)     NS
  treatment_pembrolizumab            -0.074          (p=0.017)    marginal, wrong direction

Only palbociclib shows a substantively positive main effect. The very small negative
"effects" for trastuzumab and pembrolizumab are below clinical relevance; they
become non-significant after adjustment for prognostic covariates (see §5).

==============================================================
3. PALBOCICLIB SUBGROUP DISCOVERY (the dominant signal)
==============================================================
A treatment-by-feature interaction screen on palbociclib produced an extraordinarily
strong, biologically plausible heterogeneity pattern. Refinement proceeded as:

  Step a) Palbo x HR+/HER2-:    interaction +1.82 months (p<1e-300)
          HR+/HER2-: palbo +1.81 months
          NOT HR+/HER2-: palbo -0.009 (NS)

  Step b) Within HR+/HER2-, palbo x PIK3CA mutation: interaction -2.73 (p<1e-300)
          PIK3CA WT: palbo +2.74 months
          PIK3CA mutated: palbo +0.015 (NS)
          PIK3CA mutation entirely abrogates the palbociclib benefit.

  Step c) Within HR+/HER2- + PIK3CA WT, palbo x ER+: interaction +2.82 (p=6e-71)
          ER+: palbo +2.91 months
          ER- (PR-only HR+): palbo +0.09 (NS)
          The benefit is specific to ER+ tumors (PR-only patients do not benefit).

  Step d) Within ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA WT, palbo x Ki-67_pct: -0.135 / unit (p<1e-300)
          Effect by Ki-67 strata (mean PFS palbo+ minus palbo-):
            Ki-67  0-7.5     +4.90 months
            Ki-67  7.5-12.4  +4.98
            Ki-67 12.4-14.6  +4.42-4.88
            Ki-67 14.6-15    +0.05
            Ki-67 15-22      ~0
            Ki-67 >22        ~0
          Sharp threshold at Ki-67 = 14: at and below, full +5 month benefit;
          above, no benefit.

FINAL PALBOCICLIB SUBGROUP DEFINITION:
  er_positive = 1 AND her2_positive = 0 AND pik3ca_mutation = 0 AND ki67_pct <= 14

  Within subgroup (n = 10,828, 21.7% of cohort):
      mean PFS palbo+ = 9.23 months
      mean PFS palbo- = 4.29 months
      adjusted treatment effect = +4.98 months (p<1e-300)

  Outside subgroup (n = 39,172):
      palbociclib effect = +0.007 months (p = 0.75)

  Joint OLS interaction (palbociclib x subgroup) = +4.94, p<1e-300.

This is consistent with the known biology of CDK4/6 inhibition: efficacy in
endocrine-driven (ER+/HER2-) breast cancer, with PIK3CA mutation and high
proliferation index (Ki-67) acting as resistance markers.

==============================================================
4. OTHER TREATMENTS — HETEROGENEITY SEARCHES
==============================================================
For each remaining treatment, an interaction screen across all 30+ features and an
exhaustive 2-feature subgroup search were performed.

(a) TAMOXIFEN. No detectable benefit overall (+0.043, p=0.077) or in any natural
    subgroup. ER+: +0.023 (p=0.45). ER+/HER2-: +0.025 (p=0.48). ER+/HER2-/premeno:
    +0.047 (p=0.40). ER+ Ki-67<14: -0.002 (p=0.97). The expected ER-positive
    benefit of tamoxifen is NOT reproduced in this synthetic cohort.

(b) TRASTUZUMAB. No detectable benefit overall (-0.060, p=0.030, wrong direction).
    Within HER2+: -0.079 (p=0.15). Within HER2+ stratified by stage_iv, brain mets,
    ECOG, Ki-67, LDH, node_positive: every stratum shows zero or slightly negative
    point estimate, none significant. The expected HER2-targeted benefit of
    trastuzumab is NOT reproduced.

(c) OLAPARIB. Marginally positive within BRCA1/2-mutated (n=2,499): +0.346 months
    (p=0.040). Stronger within BRCA+/HR-negative (n=629): +0.83 months (p=0.011).
    However, after adjusting for age, ECOG, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, albumin,
    and weight_loss_pct_6mo, the within-BRCA+/HR- olaparib coefficient drops to
    -0.003 (p=0.94). The unadjusted contrast is dominated by chance baseline
    imbalance among the 46 olaparib-treated BRCA+/HR- patients. Refuted.

(d) SACITUZUMAB GOVITECAN. No detectable benefit overall (-0.007, p=0.84) or in
    its canonical TNBC indication (-0.085, p=0.21). HR+/HER2-: 0.000 (p=1.0).
    Within TNBC + premenopausal: -0.28 (p=0.009) — direction OPPOSITE to indication.
    No coherent subgroup benefit.

(e) PEMBROLIZUMAB. Slightly negative overall (-0.074, p=0.017). A 3-way interaction
    suggested any benefit would concentrate in TNBC + premenopausal + low Ki-67:
       TNBC + premeno + Ki-67 <= 14 (n=2,388):
         unadjusted pembro effect +0.54 months (p = 2e-5)
         outside this subgroup pembro effect -0.10 (p = 0.001)
    However, baseline imbalance is striking: pembro+ patients in this subgroup
    are older (66.3 vs 64.5), less likely stage IV (26% vs 32%), have fewer brain
    mets (8.8% vs 11%), and less weight loss (3.5% vs 3.9%). After adjusting for
    age, ECOG, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, albumin, weight_loss:
         within-subgroup pembro coefficient = +0.016 (p = 0.12)
         outside-subgroup pembro coefficient = -0.038 (p = 0.03)
    The apparent within-subgroup benefit collapses on adjustment. Refuted.

==============================================================
5. JOINT MULTIVARIABLE MODEL
==============================================================
A single OLS containing all six treatments, the palbociclib subgroup interaction,
and the prognostic covariates (age, ECOG, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, albumin,
weight_loss, ki67_pct, plus ER/PR/HER2/PIK3CA main effects) achieves R^2 = 0.994
on the 50,000-row dataset. Materially nonzero coefficients:

  palbociclib_subgroup_indicator x treatment_palbociclib  +4.977   (p<1e-300)
  age_years                                               +0.177 / yr
  ecog_ps                                                 -1.170 / grade
  stage_iv                                                -1.557
  has_brain_mets                                          -0.971
  albumin_g_dl                                            +0.488 / g/dL
  weight_loss_pct_6mo                                     -0.078 / %

All other treatment main effects and treatment-subgroup interactions are
near-zero in the joint model, confirming that within this cohort palbociclib
is the only treatment with a detectable causal PFS effect.

==============================================================
6. CONCLUSIONS
==============================================================
1) The dominant — and arguably only — robust treatment-effect heterogeneity in
   this cohort concerns palbociclib. The complete subgroup definition is

        er_positive = 1
        AND her2_positive = 0
        AND pik3ca_mutation = 0
        AND ki67_pct <= 14

   Within this subgroup, palbociclib increases PFS by approximately +4.94-4.98
   months (unadjusted and prognostic-covariate-adjusted estimates agree closely).
   Outside this subgroup, palbociclib has no detectable effect (point estimate
   ~0, p ~ 0.75).

2) Each component of the subgroup definition is necessary: dropping ER+ (loosening
   to HR+) reduces the effect, allowing PIK3CA mutation abolishes it, allowing
   HER2+ abolishes it, and raising the Ki-67 cutoff above 14 progressively dilutes
   the benefit until it disappears at Ki-67 ~> 14-15.

3) Tamoxifen, trastuzumab, sacituzumab govitecan, and pembrolizumab show no
   reliable PFS benefit in this cohort, in any subgroup, after appropriate
   adjustment. Olaparib shows a small unadjusted positive signal in BRCA-mutated
   HR-negative patients, but this is fully explained by chance baseline imbalance
   and disappears after covariate adjustment.

4) The strongest prognostic markers (irrespective of treatment) are ECOG, stage IV,
   brain metastases, weight loss, albumin, age (positive direction in this cohort),
   PIK3CA mutation, ER status, HER2 status, and Ki-67. Together with the
   palbociclib subgroup interaction, these explain >99% of PFS variance.

5) The treatment-effect findings reproduce the canonical CDK4/6-inhibitor biology
   for palbociclib (HR+/HER2- specificity, PIK3CA resistance, dependence on
   endocrine/proliferative state) but do NOT reproduce known clinical benefits
   of tamoxifen in ER+, trastuzumab in HER2+, sacituzumab in TNBC, or
   pembrolizumab in TNBC. This is a feature of this synthetic dataset's
   data-generating process rather than a clinical finding to generalize.
