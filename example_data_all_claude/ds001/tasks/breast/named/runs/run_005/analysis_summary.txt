Analysis summary — ds001_breast (n=50,000; outcome = pfs_months)
================================================================

Goal
----
Surface clinically meaningful patterns in the cohort: prognostic main effects, treatment main effects, and — most importantly — treatment-effect heterogeneity defined by predictive biomarkers and clinical features. The single outcome of interest is progression-free survival in months (pfs_months); the six interventions are treatment_tamoxifen, treatment_palbociclib, treatment_trastuzumab, treatment_olaparib, treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan, and treatment_pembrolizumab. 25 iterations of propose–test–refine were executed.


1. Prognostic main effects on pfs_months
-----------------------------------------
Univariate t-tests / ANOVAs / Pearson correlations (Iterations 2–3) and a fully adjusted OLS (Iteration 6) consistently identified a coherent set of prognostic features:

- ecog_ps strongly negative: each unit lowers PFS by ~1.17 months in the adjusted model; unadjusted means are 5.64 / 4.44 / 3.29 mo for ECOG 0/1/2 (ANOVA F=2861, p≈0).
- stage_iv: −1.55 mo adjusted (unadjusted diff −1.54, p≈0).
- has_brain_mets: −0.98 mo adjusted (unadjusted −1.05, p=1.5e-178).
- er_positive: +0.53 mo adjusted (unadjusted +0.57, p=3.7e-143).
- pr_positive: small unadjusted +0.36 mo (p=5.8e-59); becomes near-null after adjustment for ER+ (coef +0.014, p=0.32). PR+ adds little independent prognostic information.
- her2_positive: −0.47 mo adjusted (unadjusted −0.46, p=6.1e-70).
- pik3ca_mutation: −0.58 mo adjusted (unadjusted −0.56, p=6.1e-144) — a strong negative prognostic biomarker in this cohort.
- ki67_pct: −0.019 mo per unit (r=−0.091, p=3e-93).
- albumin_g_dl: +0.48 mo per g/dL.
- weight_loss_pct_6mo: −0.077 mo per percent.
- ldh_u_l: small but significant (r=−0.016, p=2e-4).
- her2_low, brca1_mutation, brca2_mutation, node_positive, postmenopausal, sex_female, tumor_size_cm, hemoglobin_g_dl, crp_mg_l, nlr, and the remaining lab variables: either non-significant or with effect sizes too small (|r|<0.02) to be clinically meaningful once stronger covariates are included.
- age_years: a strikingly LARGE positive correlation with pfs_months (r=+0.70, p≈0; +0.176 mo per year in adjusted model). Older patients in this cohort have substantially longer PFS — opposite to the typical clinical expectation. The pattern is robust to multivariable adjustment, indicating it reflects a real structural feature of how this dataset was assembled rather than confounding by other captured variables. It is the single dominant prognostic variable.


2. Unadjusted treatment main effects
------------------------------------
Iteration 1:
- treatment_palbociclib: +1.098 mo, p≈0 (only treatment with a large unadjusted PFS benefit).
- treatment_tamoxifen: +0.043 mo, p=0.077 (NS).
- treatment_trastuzumab: −0.060 mo, p=0.029 (statistically significant but tiny and adverse).
- treatment_olaparib: −0.036 mo, p=0.33 (NS).
- treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan: −0.007 mo, p=0.85 (NS).
- treatment_pembrolizumab: −0.074 mo, p=0.018 (small adverse).

Adjusted main effects (Iterations 6, 24): only treatment_palbociclib has a meaningful coefficient (+1.10 mo, p≈0). All others are within ±0.04 mo of zero with non-significant p-values.


3. Canonical treatment × biomarker interactions
-----------------------------------------------
Iteration 4 tested classical pairings via OLS pfs_months ~ t + b + t:b:

- treatment_palbociclib × er_positive: interaction coef = +1.59, p=9e-229. Effect is +1.58 mo in ER+ vs −0.02 in ER−.
- treatment_palbociclib × her2_positive: interaction coef = −1.36, p=2e-118. Effect is +1.35 mo in HER2− vs ~0 in HER2+.
- treatment_olaparib × brca1_mutation: interaction +0.45, p=0.066 (borderline).
- treatment_olaparib × brca2_mutation: interaction +0.34, p=0.16 (NS).
- treatment_trastuzumab × her2_positive: interaction −0.03, p=0.69 (NS). No HER2-specific trastuzumab benefit.
- treatment_trastuzumab × her2_low: NS.
- treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan × her2_low / her2_positive / er_positive: all NS.
- treatment_pembrolizumab × er_positive: interaction −0.13, p=0.050 (borderline; small effect, opposite direction of any benefit hypothesis).

Refined biomarker subgroups (Iteration 5):
- HR+/HER2- (er_positive=1 OR pr_positive=1) AND her2_positive=0: palbociclib +1.81 mo, interaction p≈0.
- ER+/HER2-: palbociclib +1.92 mo, interaction p≈0.
- BRCA-any (brca1_mutation=1 OR brca2_mutation=1): olaparib +0.35 mo, p=0.041; interaction +0.40, p=0.021.
- TNBC (er_positive=0 AND pr_positive=0 AND her2_positive=0): no benefit from sacituzumab (-0.085, p=0.22) or pembrolizumab (+0.072 within TNBC, p=0.22).

The canonical biomarker–therapy associations seen in clinical practice and trials were partly recapitulated (palbociclib in ER+/HER2-, olaparib in BRCA carriers) but several were absent: trastuzumab provided NO PFS benefit in HER2+, sacituzumab none in TNBC or HER2-low, pembrolizumab none in TNBC, and tamoxifen none in any ER+ stratum.


4. Systematic interaction screening
-----------------------------------
Iteration 7 looped each treatment over all binary biomarkers and standardized continuous features, ranking by interaction p-value:

- treatment_palbociclib was dominated by interactions with pik3ca_mutation (−1.62, p=4e-255), er_positive (+1.59, p=9e-229), ki67_pct(z) (−0.64, p=1e-174), her2_positive (−1.36, p=2e-118), pr_positive (+1.09, p=3e-117), her2_low (+0.42, p=9e-19). All point to a unified subgroup definition of luminal-like, low-proliferation, PIK3CA-WT disease.
- treatment_olaparib: brca1 (+0.45, p=0.066) and brca2 (+0.34, p=0.16) borderline; nothing else.
- treatment_trastuzumab, treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan, treatment_pembrolizumab, treatment_tamoxifen: no interaction stronger than |coef|≈0.13 mo and only borderline p-values; none survive any reasonable multiple-testing correction.

An exhaustive 2-feature subgroup search (Iteration 13) over all pairs of binary modifiers (with derived ki67_high, brca_any, ecog_2 added) confirmed: no treatment other than palbociclib (and weakly olaparib for the node_positive=1 & brca_any=1 cell, p=8e-4) had any 2-feature subgroup at interaction p<0.001.


5. Locking down the palbociclib subgroup
----------------------------------------
Iterations 8, 14, 15 progressively refined the palbociclib subgroup:

- ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT (sgA, n=18,681): palbociclib +2.91 mo (p≈0), outside +0.003 (p=0.91), interaction +2.91 (p≈0).
- ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT/Ki67<20 (sgB, n=14,174): palbociclib +3.85 mo (p≈0), outside −0.002 (p=0.94), interaction +3.85 (p≈0).
- ER+/PR+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT/Ki67<20 (sgC, n=15,916): palbociclib +3.90 mo within, +0.20 (p=8e-16) outside (because PR− members of sgB still benefit). PR+ is not an independent modifier on top of ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT/Ki67<20.

A fine Ki67 cutoff scan within ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT (Iteration 15) revealed an essentially sharp threshold:

- Ki67<14: palbociclib effect = +4.94 mo (n=10,752 patients in low-Ki67 stratum); Ki67≥14: +0.07 mo (n=7,929). Interaction coef +4.93, p≈0. Cuts >=14 progressively dilute the high-Ki67 stratum with non-responders, reducing the interaction coefficient. The data are most consistent with a sharp Ki67=14 threshold for palbociclib responsiveness within ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT.

Adjusted confirmation (Iteration 22): within ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT/Ki67<14, the OLS palbociclib coefficient adjusting for age_years, ecog_ps, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, albumin_g_dl, and weight_loss_pct_6mo is +4.98 mo, p≈0. The full-cohort interaction model (Iteration 25) gives outside-subgroup palbociclib coefficient ≈ +0.008 mo (p=4.5e-4) and additional in-subgroup gain ≈ +4.97 mo (p≈0). The effect is therefore essentially confined to the four-feature subgroup.


6. Olaparib in BRCA carriers — apparent benefit, but not robust to adjustment
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Iterations 9–10 and 16 found that within BRCA+/ER- (n=750) the unadjusted olaparib effect was +0.83 mo (p=0.0012) and within BRCA+/ER-/HER2- (n=599) it was +0.74 mo (p=0.010). These looked like the cleanest non-palbociclib subgroup signals.

However, Iteration 23 showed that this signal is largely confounded:
- Within BRCA+/ER-, olaparib-treated patients are on average 3.1 years older, have a lower stage_iv proportion (0.236 vs 0.309), and lower has_brain_mets (0.036 vs 0.102) than untreated ones. Given that age contributes ~+0.176 mo per year in the adjusted prognostic model, the age difference alone would account for ~+0.55 mo, and the stage/brain-mets imbalances explain most of the rest.
- Adjusted olaparib coefficient within BRCA+/ER-: −0.003 mo, p=0.92.
- Adjusted olaparib coefficient within BRCA+ overall: +0.025, p=0.75.

Conclusion: there is NO adjusted evidence that treatment_olaparib increases pfs_months in any BRCA-defined subgroup of this cohort. The unadjusted association reflects baseline prognostic imbalance, not a true treatment effect.


7. Within-subgroup adjusted tests for the other treatments
----------------------------------------------------------
Iteration 25 fit an adjusted OLS within each canonical subgroup (covariates: age_years, ecog_ps, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, albumin_g_dl, weight_loss_pct_6mo, ki67_pct, pik3ca_mutation, plus disease-relevant biomarker indicators):

- treatment_tamoxifen in er_positive=1: coef +0.001, p=0.94.
- treatment_trastuzumab in her2_positive=1: coef −0.0004, p=0.95.
- treatment_pembrolizumab in TNBC: coef +0.008, p=0.16.
- treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan in TNBC: coef +0.006, p=0.32.

None of these four treatments shows an adjusted PFS benefit, even within its canonical biomarker subgroup. Additional refinements explored and refuted:
- Tamoxifen in ER+ AND premenopausal (Iteration 12): NS (interaction p=0.97).
- Tamoxifen in ER+/HER2- (Iteration 12): NS.
- Tamoxifen in ER+/PR+/HER2- and ER+/postmenopausal (Iteration 21): NS.
- Trastuzumab in HER2+/Ki67≥20 aggressive subset (Iteration 18): −0.063 mo, NS.
- Trastuzumab in HER2+/ER- (Iteration 9): −0.063 mo, NS.
- Pembrolizumab in TNBC/Ki67≥20 (Iteration 19): NS.
- Pembrolizumab in TNBC/NLR<3 (Iteration 19): NS.
- Pembrolizumab in TNBC/ECOG≤1 (Iteration 9): borderline +0.114 mo (p=0.063) but not robust.
- Sacituzumab in TNBC/HER2-low (Iteration 20): NS, slightly negative.
- Sacituzumab in TNBC/stage_iv=1 (Iteration 11): −0.262 mo, p=0.018 (slightly adverse, opposite of hypothesis).


8. Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
-----------------------------------
Supported:
- All major prognostic main effects (stage_iv negative, brain_mets negative, ECOG negative, ER+ positive, HER2+ negative, PIK3CA mutation negative, ki67 negative, albumin positive, weight loss negative, age positive).
- treatment_palbociclib has a large positive PFS effect (~+1.1 mo overall).
- treatment_palbociclib × er_positive interaction (positive).
- treatment_palbociclib × her2_positive interaction (negative).
- treatment_palbociclib × pik3ca_mutation interaction (negative).
- treatment_palbociclib × ki67 interaction (negative; sharp cutoff near Ki67=14).
- Final treatment-effect subgroup: within er_positive=1 AND her2_positive=0 AND pik3ca_mutation=0 AND ki67_pct<14, treatment_palbociclib increases pfs_months by approximately +5 months.

Refuted (effect not significant or wrong direction, or not robust to adjustment):
- treatment_tamoxifen has any PFS benefit anywhere (overall, in ER+, in ER+/HER2-, in ER+/PR+/HER2-, in ER+/premenopausal, in ER+/postmenopausal): all refuted.
- treatment_trastuzumab benefits HER2+ patients or any HER2+ subset: refuted.
- treatment_pembrolizumab benefits TNBC or any TNBC sub-subset (Ki67-high, NLR-low, ECOG-low): refuted.
- treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan benefits TNBC, HER2-low TNBC, or advanced TNBC: refuted (sometimes slightly adverse).
- treatment_olaparib benefits BRCA carriers, BRCA/ER-, BRCA/HER2-, or BRCA/TNBC: unadjusted effect appears positive (+0.7–0.8 mo) but completely attenuates after adjustment for prognostic covariates — refuted as a true treatment effect.


9. Final best-supported treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis
------------------------------------------------------------
Treatment: treatment_palbociclib
Outcome: pfs_months (positive direction; longer PFS)
Subgroup predicates (all required):
  • er_positive = 1
  • her2_positive = 0
  • pik3ca_mutation = 0
  • ki67_pct < 14

Magnitude: approximately +5.0 months of PFS benefit within the subgroup (adjusted coef +4.98, p≈0; unadjusted +4.94 within ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT/Ki67<14). Outside the subgroup, the palbociclib effect is essentially zero (≤0.05 mo, NS in most strata). Each of the four predicates is necessary: removing any one of them reintroduces patients in whom palbociclib has no effect and dilutes the apparent benefit.

For all other treatment–outcome pairs in this cohort there is no defensible adjusted treatment-effect subgroup. The dataset is consistent with palbociclib being the only therapy with a true predictive biomarker pattern (the canonical luminal-A-like phenotype: ER+, HER2-, PIK3CA-WT, low proliferative index), and with the other five named therapies having no detectable PFS effect — even within the biomarker subgroups in which they are clinically used.
