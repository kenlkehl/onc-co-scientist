Analysis summary ‚ÄĒ ds001_breast (N=50,000)
Outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival, months)
Model: claude-opus-4-7.  Iterations used: 25.

============================================================
1. OVERALL APPROACH
============================================================
The dataset is an EHR-derived breast-cancer-cohort table with 38 features and one
continuous outcome (pfs_months). I ran the propose-test-refine loop in five
phases: (a) main effects of demographics/staging (iter 1), tumor biology (iter 2),
labs (iter 3), and treatments (iter 4); (b) mechanism-driven treatment-by-receptor
interactions for each of the six treatments (iter 5‚Äď10); (c) a multivariable
prognostic OLS model (iter 11) and confirmation of canonical-indication
subgroups (iter 12); (d) a systematic treatment-effect heterogeneity scan over
all six treatments √ó 19 binary modifiers and 6 continuous modifiers (iter 13‚Äď14)
followed by drill-down subgroup analyses for each treatment (iter 15‚Äď20); and
(e) confirmatory regression with explicit subgroup √ó treatment interactions and
covariate adjustment for the leading subgroup hypotheses (iter 21‚Äď25).
Statistical methods: Welch t-tests for binary contrasts, Pearson correlation
for continuous main effects, one-way ANOVA for ECOG, OLS with multiplicative
interaction terms for treatment-effect heterogeneity, and stratified t-tests
for joint subgroups.

============================================================
2. PROGNOSTIC MAIN-EFFECT FINDINGS
============================================================
Strong, expected adverse prognostics for shorter PFS (all p<1e-100):
  - ECOG performance status (PFS 5.64 / 4.44 / 3.29 mo for ECOG 0/1/2; F=2861)
  - Stage IV disease (-1.54 mo)
  - Brain metastases (-1.05 mo)
  - HER2-positive (-0.46 mo)  [in absence of effective HER2-targeted treatment]
  - PIK3CA mutation (-0.56 mo)
  - Higher Ki-67% (r=-0.09)
  - Greater 6-month weight loss % (r=-0.12)

Strong favorable prognostics for longer PFS:
  - ER-positive (+0.57 mo, p=4e-143)
  - PR-positive (+0.36 mo, p=6e-59)
  - Higher serum albumin (r=+0.10, p=9e-112)

Notable surprise: AGE is STRONGLY POSITIVELY correlated with PFS (Pearson
r = +0.70, p‚Čą0). Older patients have far longer PFS than younger ones in this
cohort. This direction is the OPPOSITE of the biological default; it most
likely reflects the population mix ‚ÄĒ older breast-cancer patients are
preferentially HR-positive, slow-growing, and assigned to endocrine + CDK4/6
therapy, while younger patients in this cohort skew toward more aggressive
biology. The full multivariable model retains a +0.18 mo per year coefficient
on age after adjustment for ECOG, stage, biology, labs, and treatment.

Null or near-null main effects: sex_female, postmenopausal, node_positive,
tumor_size_cm, BRCA1, BRCA2 (alone), CRP, NLR, and most chemistry labs
(hemoglobin, AST, ALT, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium); these contribute
only trivially after adjustment.

Multivariable model R¬≤ = 0.792, dominated by ECOG, stage_iv, brain_mets,
ER, HER2, PIK3CA, age, ki67, albumin, weight_loss, and palbociclib.

A systematic interaction scan (treatment √ó 19 binary modifiers) and continuous-

  - palbociclib √ó PIK3CA mutation:  ő≤=-1.62, p=4e-255 (strongest)
  - palbociclib √ó ER-positive:      ő≤=+1.59, p=9e-229
  - palbociclib √ó Ki67-high(‚Č•20%):  ő≤=-1.47, p=6e-169
  - palbociclib √ó triple-negative:  ő≤=-1.39, p=3e-136
  - palbociclib √ó HER2-positive:    ő≤=-1.36, p=2e-118

Drill-down stratified analyses:

  ----------------------------------------------------------

  ER-                         -0.02 (NS)

tumors and shrinks toward zero by Ki67 ‚Čą 30‚Äď40%.

Other prognostic features (age, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, ECOG, albumin,
weight_loss) do NOT meaningfully modulate palbociclib effect once the core
biology subgroup is fixed (ER+/HER2-/postmeno: +1.85‚Äď1.95 mo across all ECOG

TAMOXIFEN, TRASTUZUMAB, SACITUZUMAB GOVITECAN ‚ÄĒ NULL ACROSS ALL SUBGROUPS

None of these three drugs produced a positive effect in their canonical
indication subgroup or any other tested subgroup:
  Tamoxifen in ER+/postmeno:           +0.010 mo (p=0.81)
  Trastuzumab in HER2+:                -0.079 mo (p=0.15)
  Trastuzumab in HER2+/ER+:            -0.087 mo (p=0.18)
  Trastuzumab in HER2+/non-stage-IV:   -0.042 mo (p=0.50)
  Sacituzumab in TNBC:                 -0.085 mo (p=0.22)
  Sacituzumab in HER2-low:             -0.031 mo (p=0.64)
  Sacituzumab in HER2-low/TNBC:        -0.072 mo (p=0.49)
This is unexpected versus external real-world evidence for tamoxifen in
ER+/postmeno, trastuzumab in HER2+, and sacituzumab in TNBC/HER2-low; in
this dataset, treatment assignment for these drugs appears non-informative
(consistent with a synthetic-data construction in which only palbociclib,
olaparib, and pembrolizumab carry true biology-conditional effects).

5. FINAL BEST-SUPPORTED SUBGROUP HYPOTHESES (per-treatment)
============================================================
PALBOCICLIB (best-supported, very strong):
  Patients with er_positive=1 AND her2_positive=0 AND pik3ca_mutation=0
  experience an adjusted +2.92-month longer PFS on palbociclib than controls
  (p‚Čą0, N=18,681, R¬≤-stable to adjustment for age, ECOG, stage_iv, brain_mets,
  Ki67, albumin, weight_loss, LDH). Outside this subgroup, palbociclib effect
  is +0.0002 (p=0.91).

OLAPARIB (well-supported, smaller, smaller n):
  Patients with (brca1_mutation=1 OR brca2_mutation=1) AND er_positive=0
  experience +0.83-month longer PFS on olaparib (p=0.0012, n_tx=55). Pooling
  to BRCA-any with no ER restriction yields a smaller +0.35 mo (p=0.041,
  n_tx=239); ER+ status appears to attenuate olaparib benefit (3-way
  interaction p=0.10).

PEMBROLIZUMAB (qualitative interaction, no positive sub-phenotype reaches
significance individually):
  pfs_months difference (pembro vs. no pembro) is +0.07 in TNBC (NS) and -0.11
  in ER+ (p=0.005); the TNBC interaction term is significant (p=0.015) and
  reflects a qualitative reversal rather than a strongly responsive
  sub-phenotype within TNBC.

TRASTUZUMAB / TAMOXIFEN / SACITUZUMAB GOVITECAN:
  No subgroup analysis identifies a credible positive effect on PFS in this
  dataset; all three are well-modeled as PFS-inert.

============================================================
7. CONCLUSIONS
============================================================
Of the six treatments in this cohort, only PALBOCICLIB shows a large,
statistically robust, biology-restricted PFS benefit, fully captured by
the conjunction ER+ AND HER2- AND PIK3CA-WILD-TYPE (~+2.9 months,
extremely strong adjusted effect). OLAPARIB shows a smaller but mechanistically
plausible benefit confined to BRCA-mutated/ER-negative tumors. PEMBROLIZUMAB
shows a qualitative TNBC interaction (favorable trend in TNBC, harmful in
ER+) but no sub-phenotype within TNBC reaches significance individually.
TAMOXIFEN, TRASTUZUMAB, and SACITUZUMAB GOVITECAN are PFS-inert across all
plausible subgroups examined here. The dominant prognostic axis for PFS is
ECOG / stage / brain_mets / receptor biology / PIK3CA / cachexia (R¬≤‚Čą0.79
in the full model), and age is paradoxically positively correlated with
PFS in this cohort (likely reflecting the older / HR-positive enrichment
in the palbociclib-eligible population).
