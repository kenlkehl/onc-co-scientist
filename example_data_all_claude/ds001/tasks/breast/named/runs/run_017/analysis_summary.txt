Analysis summary - dataset ds001_breast (n=50,000; outcome pfs_months)
========================================================================

Approach
--------
I worked through 18 iterations of propose-test-refine, building from
univariate main effects to multivariable adjustment, to clinically
motivated treatment-by-biomarker interaction tests, to an exhaustive
two-feature subgroup screen for every treatment. For the one treatment
that showed strong heterogeneity I then refined the subgroup definition
along a continuous modifier. For each treatment that showed an apparent
subgroup signal I checked whether the signal survived adjustment for
the major prognostic covariates.

Outcome scale
-------------
The single outcome pfs_months has mean 4.69, sd 2.50, range 0-16.7.
Effect estimates throughout are differences in mean PFS in months,
signed so that positive = longer PFS.


1. Main effects of treatments (iter 1)
--------------------------------------
Welch t-tests on pfs_months by each treatment indicator:
  treatment_palbociclib:           +1.098 mo, p<1e-300  (huge)
  treatment_tamoxifen:             +0.043 mo, p=0.077   (null)
  treatment_trastuzumab:           -0.060 mo, p=0.029   (tiny, wrong sign)
  treatment_olaparib:              -0.036 mo, p=0.33    (null)
  treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan: -0.007 mo, p=0.85    (null)
  treatment_pembrolizumab:         -0.074 mo, p=0.018   (tiny, wrong sign)

Only palbociclib has a meaningful univariate effect. The negative point
estimates for trastuzumab and pembrolizumab are clinically negligible
(-2 to -4 days) and turn null after adjustment.


2. Main effects of biomarkers and clinical features (iter 2)
------------------------------------------------------------
Strongest prognostic main effects on pfs_months (Welch / ANOVA):
  stage_iv=1:           -1.54 mo (p<1e-300)
  has_brain_mets=1:     -1.05 mo (p=1.5e-178)
  ecog_ps gradient:     5.64 -> 4.44 -> 3.29 across 0/1/2 (~ -1.18 mo per pt)
  pik3ca_mutation=1:    -0.56 mo (p=6e-144)
  er_positive=1:        +0.57 mo (p=4e-143)
  her2_positive=1:      -0.46 mo (p=6e-70)
  pr_positive=1:        +0.36 mo (p=6e-59)
  her2_low=1:           +0.11 mo (p=1e-6)
Null: brca1, brca2, postmenopausal, node_positive, sex_female.

A surprising note for a "breast cancer" cohort: only 44.7% of records
are sex_female=1, and sex has no PFS association.


3. Continuous feature correlations with PFS (iter 3)
----------------------------------------------------
The standout finding here is age_years vs pfs_months: Pearson
r = +0.7015 (p<1e-300), with monotone rise across age deciles
(decile 0 mean PFS 1.70, decile 9 mean PFS 7.84). The sign is
opposite to typical clinical expectation. The relationship is
robust within every ECOG stratum (r 0.73-0.74) and within both
stage strata (r 0.72-0.74), so it is not a simple confounding
artifact.

Other meaningful continuous prognostics:
  albumin_g_dl:         r = +0.100 (longer PFS at higher albumin)
  weight_loss_pct_6mo:  r = -0.116 (cachexia -> shorter PFS)
  ki67_pct:             r = -0.091 (high proliferation -> shorter PFS)
  ldh_u_l:              r = -0.016 (tiny but significant)

Routine labs (hemoglobin, AST, ALT, alk phos, bilirubin, creatinine,
BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium, tumor_size_cm) had |r|<0.014; CRP and
NLR had no detectable PFS signal.


4. Multivariable OLS (iter 4)
-----------------------------
A single OLS model regressing pfs_months on all 37 features achieves
R^2 = 0.792, indicating that PFS is highly determined by the recorded
features.  Adjusted coefficients (most influential):
  age_years              +0.176 / yr
  ecog_ps                -1.17  / level
  stage_iv               -1.55
  has_brain_mets         -0.98
  albumin_g_dl           +0.48  / g/dL
  weight_loss_pct_6mo    -0.077 / %
  ki67_pct               -0.019 / %
  er_positive            +0.53
  her2_positive          -0.47
  pik3ca_mutation        -0.58
  treatment_palbociclib  +1.10
All other treatment coefficients are near zero (|coef| < 0.04, all
non-significant or only marginally so). This already suggests that
five of the six treatments have no overall PFS effect once
prognostic covariates are accounted for.


5. Treatment x biomarker interactions, clinically motivated (iter 5, 8)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Interaction terms in OLS pfs_months ~ tx*biomarker + adjusters:

  treatment_palbociclib x er_positive:          +1.559   (p<1e-300)
  treatment_palbociclib x her2_positive:        -1.338   (p<1e-300)
  treatment_palbociclib x pik3ca_mutation:      -1.665   (p<1e-300)
  treatment_palbociclib x pr_positive:          +1.030   (p<1e-300)
  treatment_palbociclib x her2_low:             +0.383   (p=3e-67)
  treatment_palbociclib x postmenopausal:       -0.026   (n.s.)

  treatment_tamoxifen x er_positive:            +0.007   (n.s.)
  treatment_trastuzumab x her2_positive:        +0.009   (n.s.)
  treatment_olaparib x brca1_mutation:          +0.101   (n.s.)
  treatment_olaparib x brca2_mutation:          -0.016   (n.s.)
  treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan x her2_low:   +0.042   (n.s.)
  treatment_pembrolizumab x pik3ca_mutation:    +0.041   (n.s.)

The biology recovered for palbociclib is clean: large positive
interaction with ER+, large negative interaction with HER2+, large
negative interaction with PIK3CA mutation. None of the other five
treatments show their expected biomarker interaction in this dataset.


6. Channeling check (iter 6)
----------------------------
Treatment use rates were almost identical across biomarker strata
(within ~2 percentage points everywhere - e.g., trastuzumab 20.9% in
HER2+ vs 19.9% in HER2-, olaparib 9.4% in BRCA1+ vs 9.9% in BRCA1-).
That is, treatments are not preferentially given to biomarker-positive
patients. The dataset behaves like a randomized assignment with respect
to biomarkers, so the absence of subgroup effects for the other five
treatments cannot be blamed on confounding-by-indication.


7. Treatments tested in their natural target subgroups (iter 7)
---------------------------------------------------------------
  Tamoxifen in ER+ (n=34886):              +0.023, p=0.45
    in ER+/postmenopausal (n=21081):       +0.010, p=0.81
    in ER+/PR+ (n=29674):                  +0.031, p=0.35
    in ER+/HER2- (n=28589):                +0.025, p=0.48
  Trastuzumab in HER2+ (n=9060):           -0.079, p=0.15
    in HER2+/ER+ (n=6297):                 -0.087, p=0.18
    in HER2+/ER- (n=2763):                 -0.063, p=0.55
  Olaparib in BRCAany (n=2499):            +0.346, p=0.041
    in BRCAany/HER2- (n=2023):             +0.321, p=0.09
    in BRCAany/ER- (n=750):                +0.829, p=0.0012  (apparent)
  Sacituzumab in TNBC (n=10496):           -0.085, p=0.22
    in HER2-low (n=18543):                 -0.031, p=0.64
  Pembrolizumab in TNBC (n=10496):         +0.072, p=0.22

Only the BRCAany/ER- olaparib comparison crossed nominal significance.


8. Exhaustive 2-way subgroup screen (iter 10)
---------------------------------------------
For each non-palbociclib treatment, every pair of 12 binary features
defined four cells; cells with n>=200 and >=30 in each treatment arm
were tested. Hits required |diff|>0.3 mo and p<0.01.
  Tamoxifen, Trastuzumab, Sacituzumab govitecan: zero hits.
  Pembrolizumab: one hit (sex_female=1 & brca1_mutation=1) of opposite
                 sign, almost certainly chance.
  Olaparib: only BRCA2+/ER- looked positive (+0.880, p=0.007, ola_n=32).

So the search did not surface a hidden subgroup-level benefit for any
of the five non-palbociclib treatments.


9. Refining the olaparib signal: confounding (iter 11, 12)
----------------------------------------------------------
Within BRCAany/ER- (n=750), olaparib-treated patients were on average
3.1 years older than controls (68.1 vs 65.0 yrs). Given the strong
positive age coefficient (+0.176/yr in the multivariable model), this
imbalance alone explains roughly +0.55 mo of the +0.83 raw difference.
Fitting OLS pfs_months ~ treatment_olaparib + age_years + ecog_ps +
stage_iv + has_brain_mets + albumin_g_dl + weight_loss_pct_6mo within
BRCAany/ER- collapses the olaparib coefficient to -0.003 (p=0.92). A
triple-interaction model (olaparib*brca_any*er_positive, with full
covariate adjustment) confirmed all interaction terms null
(all p>=0.25). Olaparib has no detectable real PFS effect in this
dataset, even in its biologically plausible niche.


10. Verifying the age-PFS positive correlation (iter 13)
--------------------------------------------------------
Pearson r(age_years, pfs_months) was essentially unchanged after
stratification:
  Within ECOG 0:   r = 0.742
  Within ECOG 1:   r = 0.741
  Within ECOG 2:   r = 0.726
  Within stage_iv=0: r = 0.739
  Within stage_iv=1: r = 0.717
This is a real (though clinically counterintuitive) feature of the
data-generating process.


11. Treatment heterogeneity for palbociclib by ki67 (iter 14, 15)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Within ER+/HER2-/PIK3CAwt (n=18,681), the palbociclib effect on PFS
declines monotonically with ki67_pct:
  Q1 ki67 <= 7.5%:     palbo +4.93 mo
  Q2 ki67 7.6-12.2%:   palbo +4.97 mo
  Q3 ki67 12.3-19.6%:  palbo +1.72 mo
  Q4 ki67 >= 19.7%:    palbo -0.05 mo

The continuous-interaction OLS in this subgroup gives
  treatment_palbociclib              coef +5.06
  treatment_palbociclib : ki67_pct   coef -0.138 / % (p<1e-300)
implying a palbociclib effect of +4.37 at ki67=5, +2.99 at ki67=15,
+1.61 at ki67=25, +0.92 at ki67=30.


12. Best-supported palbociclib subgroup (iter 9, 16)
----------------------------------------------------
Refined subgroup: er_positive=1 AND her2_positive=0 AND
pik3ca_mutation=0 AND ki67_pct <= 14. (21.7% of cohort, n=10,828.)
  Inside subgroup:    palbo on=9.228 vs off=4.285, diff +4.94 mo
                      adjusted +4.98 (p<1e-300).
  Outside subgroup:   palbo on=4.324 vs off=4.317, diff +0.007 (p=0.75).

Either of the unfavorable predicates (HER2+, PIK3CA mutation, or high
ki67) suppresses the palbociclib effect to zero. ER positivity is a
necessary positive predicate.


Final treatment-by-subgroup conclusions
---------------------------------------
- treatment_palbociclib: large PFS benefit (~ +5 months) confined to
  the subgroup defined by er_positive=1 AND her2_positive=0 AND
  pik3ca_mutation=0 AND ki67_pct <= 14. Negligible effect outside.
  Direction = positive.

- treatment_tamoxifen: no PFS effect detected in any subgroup,
  including ER+, ER+/PR+, ER+/postmenopausal, ER+/HER2-.

- treatment_trastuzumab: no PFS effect detected; direction is slightly
  negative everywhere including HER2+ (where benefit would be
  biologically expected). Effect is negligible after adjustment.

- treatment_olaparib: no genuine PFS effect. The only nominally
  positive subgroup (BRCAany/ER-) was confounded by an age imbalance
  and disappeared after covariate adjustment.

- treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan: no PFS effect detected in any
  subgroup including TNBC, HER2-low, HER2-low/HR+. The marginally
  positive multivariable coefficient (+0.033, p=0.05) is at noise.

- treatment_pembrolizumab: no PFS effect detected in any subgroup
  including TNBC and PIK3CAwt subsets. Tiny overall negative effect
  (~ -2 days) is clinically meaningless.


Cohort observations
-------------------
Two features of this dataset deviate from real-world breast oncology:
- only 44.7% of patients are sex_female, with no PFS difference by sex
- age_years has a strong POSITIVE association with PFS (older = longer
  PFS), robust within ECOG and stage strata
Both observations are consistent with synthetic data rather than a
real EHR-derived breast cancer cohort. Conclusions above describe the
data-generating process embedded in this benchmark, not real-world
biology.
