Analysis Summary — ds001_breast (n=50,000; outcome: pfs_months)
================================================================

Approach
--------
We ran 25 iterations of propose-test-refine on a 50,000-patient breast-cancer
EHR cohort. The single outcome was progression-free survival in months
(pfs_months; mean 4.69, sd 2.50, range 0–16.66). Across iterations we tested
unadjusted main effects, multivariable-adjusted treatment effects, biomarker–
treatment alignment hypotheses, and a systematic treatment-by-feature
interaction screen for every treatment, ending in explicit subgroup
hypotheses for each of the six therapies in the dataset.

Iterations 1–5: main effects
----------------------------
Treatments (univariate): treatment_palbociclib was the only treatment with a
large unadjusted PFS effect — +1.10 months (p≈0; n=17,428 vs 32,572).
Tamoxifen (+0.04, p=0.08), trastuzumab (-0.06, p=0.029), olaparib (-0.04,
p=0.33), sacituzumab (-0.01, p=0.85), and pembrolizumab (-0.07, p=0.018)
showed only trivial differences in mean pfs_months.

Disease severity: stage_iv (-1.54 months, p≈0), has_brain_mets (-1.05,
p≈10⁻¹⁷⁸), and ecog_ps (-1.18 per unit, p≈0) all powerfully shortened PFS;
node_positive was null. age_years showed a counter-intuitive positive slope
(+0.18/year, p≈0), most likely confounded by tumor biology and stage.

Tumor biology: er_positive (+0.57, p≈10⁻¹⁴³) and pr_positive (+0.36,
p≈10⁻⁵⁹) were favorable; her2_positive (-0.46, p≈10⁻⁷⁰) and
pik3ca_mutation (-0.56, p≈10⁻¹⁴⁴) were adverse; her2_low and BRCA1/2
mutations were close to null on average. postmenopausal was null.

Continuous markers: ki67_pct (-0.019/unit, p≈10⁻⁹³), albumin_g_dl (+0.50/g/dl,
p≈10⁻¹¹², protective), weight_loss_pct_6mo (-0.075/unit, p≈10⁻¹⁴⁸), ldh_u_l
(adverse, p<0.001), and alkaline_phosphatase_u_l (adverse, p=0.003) tracked
clinically expected directions. tumor_size_cm, crp_mg_l, nlr, hemoglobin,
ast/alt, bilirubin, creatinine, bun, sodium were essentially null.

Iterations 6: adjusted treatment effects
-----------------------------------------
After adjusting each treatment for stage, ECOG, age, brain mets, albumin,
weight loss, ki67, ER/PR/HER2 status, HER2-low, BRCA1/2, and PIK3CA status,
only treatment_palbociclib retained a meaningful effect (+1.10 months, p≈0).
All other treatments (tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib, sacituzumab,
pembrolizumab) were within ±0.04 months of zero with non-significant p-values,
implying their univariate effects were almost entirely confounded by who
received them.

Iterations 7–12: biomarker–treatment alignment
-----------------------------------------------
We tested each treatment against its canonical biomarker.

(7) Tamoxifen × ER: in ER+ patients tamoxifen vs no tamoxifen pfs differed by
+0.02 months (p=0.45); in ER- patients +0.08 (p=0.03); the interaction term
tamoxifen×er_positive was -0.058 (p=0.27). No clinically meaningful tamoxifen
benefit in this cohort.

(8) Trastuzumab × HER2+: in HER2+ patients trastuzumab vs no trastuzumab
pfs was -0.08 months (p=0.15); in HER2- patients -0.05 (p=0.10); interaction
term -0.028 (p=0.69). No detectable trastuzumab benefit even in HER2+.

(9) Olaparib × any-BRCA: in BRCA1/2-mutated patients olaparib produced
+0.35 months (p=0.041, n=2,499); in BRCA wild-type -0.06 (p=0.14); interaction
+0.40 (p=0.021). A modest but biology-consistent enrichment.

(10) Sacituzumab × HER2-low: in HER2-low patients saci vs no saci -0.03 months
(p=0.64); interaction p=0.63. No HER2-low enrichment of saci benefit.
Pembrolizumab × TNBC (er-/her2-): within TNBC saci vs no pembro = +0.04
(p=0.46); interaction term +0.15 (p=0.037) — the interaction is just
significant though the within-TNBC subgroup test alone is not.

(11) Palbociclib × ER+: in ER+ palbo +1.58 months (p≈0); in ER- -0.02
(p=0.63); interaction +1.59 (p≈10⁻²²⁹). Palbo benefit lives entirely in ER+.
Palbo × postmenopausal interaction was -0.13 (p=0.004) — slightly larger
benefit in premenopausal/non-postmenopausal patients within ER+.

(12) Palbociclib × PIK3CA: interaction palbo×pik3ca_mutation was -1.62
(p≈10⁻²⁵⁵). Palbo benefit is essentially abolished by PIK3CA mutation.
In ER+/HER2- (n=28,589): palbo +1.92 (p≈0). Palbo benefit further enriched
when HER2 is also negative.

Iterations 13–14: treatment × stage and × ECOG
-----------------------------------------------
Treatment×stage_iv interactions: only tamoxifen showed a small significant
interaction (-0.12, p=0.021); palbo, trastuzumab, olaparib, saci,
pembrolizumab were all null. Treatment×ecog_ps: all interactions were null
(smallest p was pembrolizumab at p=0.052). Performance status and stage
do not selectively modify any of these treatment effects in this cohort.

Iteration 15: full multivariable model
---------------------------------------
Joint OLS with all 6 treatments and 31 covariates: only
treatment_palbociclib remained meaningfully positive (+1.10, p≈0); the other
five treatment coefficients were all between -0.012 and +0.033 (all p>0.05
except sacituzumab borderline at p=0.049 with coef +0.033). Model R²=0.193.
This confirms palbociclib is the single dominant treatment effect on
pfs_months in this cohort.

Iterations 16–17: triple interaction & canonical palbociclib subgroup
----------------------------------------------------------------------
A 3-way interaction palbociclib × er_positive × pik3ca_wildtype was +2.43
(p≈10⁻¹²⁸): the palbo benefit requires both ER+ and PIK3CA-wt status. In
the ER+/PIK3CA-wt subgroup palbo gave +2.38 months (p≈0, n=22,788); outside
that subgroup +0.006 months (p=0.83). Adding HER2-negative refined this
further: in ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt (n=18,681), palbo gave +2.91 months
(7.21 vs 4.30; p≈0); outside this 3-condition subgroup palbo gave +0.003
months (p=0.91). The interaction term for palbo × (ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt)
was +2.91 (p≈0).

Iterations 18–22: per-treatment heterogeneity screens
------------------------------------------------------
For each treatment we screened single-modifier interactions across 12 binary
and 11 continuous candidate features:

- treatment_olaparib: 0/23 modifiers significant on the strict cutoff;
  brca1_mutation came closest (p=0.066). The pre-specified BRCA-pooled
  modifier (any_brca) was significant (p=0.021, +0.40 interaction) and was
  retained as the final modifier.
- treatment_pembrolizumab: 0/23 significant; closest were er_positive
  (p=0.050), ecog_ps (p=0.052), and alkaline_phosphatase (p=0.060). The
  pembro × TNBC composite was significant (p=0.037) but the simple within-
  TNBC subgroup test was not (p=0.46), so the signal is modest at best.
- treatment_trastuzumab: 1/23 significant — tumor_size_cm (+0.037 per cm,
  p=0.018). HER2+ status, the canonical modifier, was not. No reliable
  trastuzumab subgroup emerges.
- treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan: 1/23 significant — albumin_g_dl
  (+0.18 per g/dl, p=0.013). HER2-low was not. No reliable sacituzumab
  subgroup.
- treatment_tamoxifen: 1/23 significant — stage_iv (-0.12, p=0.021),
  meaning the (already-near-null) tamoxifen effect is slightly more
  negative in stage IV. ER+ was not. No reliable tamoxifen subgroup.

Iteration 23: joint palbociclib model
--------------------------------------
OLS pfs ~ palbo + er_positive + pik3ca_mutation + palbo:er_positive +
palbo:pik3ca_mutation: palbo main +0.55 (p≈10⁻³⁷); palbo×er +1.57
(p≈10⁻²³⁰); palbo×pik3ca -1.60 (p≈10⁻²⁵⁶). Both interactions remain large
and highly significant when modeled jointly.

Iteration 24: subgroup decomposition (drop-tests)
--------------------------------------------------
We tested all three single-component drop variants of the ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt
subgroup. Each predicate is necessary: the palbo benefit collapses to near-
zero when any one of the three is reversed.

  ER+ /HER2-/PIK3CA-wt  (n=18,681): palbo Δ pfs = +2.91, p≈0
  ER- /HER2-/PIK3CA-wt  (n= 8,079):              Δ = -0.04, p=0.39
  ER+ /HER2+/PIK3CA-wt  (n= 4,107):              Δ = -0.02, p=0.81
  ER+ /HER2-/PIK3CA-mut (n= 9,908):              Δ = +0.02, p=0.62
  Outside subgroup      (n=31,319):              Δ = +0.003, p=0.91

Iteration 25 — FINAL per-treatment subgroup hypotheses
-------------------------------------------------------
1. treatment_palbociclib: substantial PFS benefit (+2.91 months) confined to
   the ER+ / HER2- / PIK3CA-wildtype subgroup. Each of the three predicates
   is necessary. Outside this subgroup the effect is statistical zero.
   Mechanism is consistent with CDK4/6 inhibitor biology in HR+/HER2- breast
   cancer, with PIK3CA mutation operating as a resistance/suppressor allele
   in this dataset.

2. treatment_olaparib: small but statistically supported PFS benefit
   (+0.35 months, p=0.04) confined to BRCA1- or BRCA2-mutated patients
   (any_brca==1, n=2,499). No effect in BRCA wild-type. Direction matches
   the canonical PARP-inhibitor biology, though the magnitude is small.

3. treatment_pembrolizumab: at best a marginal benefit in TNBC-like
   (er_positive==0 AND her2_positive==0). The interaction term is borderline
   (+0.15, p=0.037), but the within-TNBC subgroup test is null (Δ=+0.04,
   p=0.46). Final classification: weak/inconclusive.

4. treatment_tamoxifen: no clinically meaningful benefit in any tested
   subgroup, including ER+ (Δ=+0.02, p=0.45). Final classification: null.

5. treatment_trastuzumab: no detectable benefit even in HER2+ (Δ=-0.08,
   p=0.15). Final classification: null in this dataset (biologically
   unexpected; likely reflects how this synthetic-EHR cohort encodes
   anti-HER2 treatment effects rather than a real-world signal).

6. treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan: no detectable benefit in HER2-low
   (Δ=-0.03, p=0.64). Final classification: null in this cohort.

Overall conclusions
-------------------
The dominant treatment-effect signal in this 50,000-patient breast cohort is
treatment_palbociclib, which prolongs pfs_months by ~2.9 months in the
ER+ / HER2- / PIK3CA-wildtype subgroup and by zero outside it. PIK3CA
mutation status is the key suppressor of palbociclib benefit. A secondary
biomarker-driven signal is treatment_olaparib in BRCA1/2-mutated patients
(small, p=0.04). All other therapies showed null or near-null effects after
adjustment, including in their biologically expected biomarker-defined
subgroups (HER2+ for trastuzumab, HER2-low for sacituzumab, TNBC for
pembrolizumab, ER+ for tamoxifen). Beyond treatment, the strongest
prognostic features for shorter pfs_months were stage_iv, has_brain_mets,
high ecog_ps, low albumin, high weight_loss_pct_6mo, high ki67_pct,
PIK3CA mutation, and HER2-positivity; the strongest favorable features
were ER+ and PR+.
