Analysis Summary — ds001_breast (50,000 patients)
=====================================================

Outcome: pfs_months (continuous, range 0–~16, median 4.5).
Six treatments studied: tamoxifen, palbociclib, trastuzumab, olaparib,
sacituzumab govitecan, pembrolizumab. 25 iterations of propose-test-refine.


1. PROGNOSTIC MAIN EFFECTS (iterations 1–10)
---------------------------------------------
Strong, highly significant prognostic associations on pfs_months were
identified for the following features (all p < 1e-90 unless noted):

  Negative associations (shorter PFS as the variable rises):
    - ecog_ps                  beta = -1.18 mo per ECOG unit
    - stage_iv = 1             diff = -1.54 months
    - has_brain_mets = 1       diff = -1.05 months  (p = 1.4e-178)
    - ki67_pct                 beta = -0.019 mo per %
    - weight_loss_pct_6mo      beta = -0.075 mo per %
    - pik3ca_mutation = 1      diff = -0.56 months  (p = 6e-144)
    - her2_positive = 1        diff = -0.46 months
    - ldh_u_l                  beta = -5.1e-4 (p = 2.4e-4)
    - alkaline_phosphatase     beta = -6.7e-4 (p = 0.003)
    - calcium_mg_dl            beta = -0.064 (p = 0.004)
    - total_bilirubin          beta = -0.07 (p = 0.046)

  Positive associations (longer PFS as the variable rises):
    - er_positive = 1          diff = +0.57 months  (p = 4e-143)
    - pr_positive = 1          diff = +0.36 months  (p = 6e-59)
    - her2_low = 1             diff = +0.11 months  (p = 1e-6)
    - albumin_g_dl             beta = +0.50 mo per g/dL (p = 1e-112)
    - age_years                beta = +0.18 mo per year (counter-
        intuitive but the dataset is unambiguous; R^2 in the
        univariate model is 0.49, so age is a remarkably strong
        positive prognostic in this cohort)
    - potassium_meq_l          beta = +0.05 (p = 0.025)

  Null or trivial associations (p > 0.05):
    - sex_female, node_positive, postmenopausal, tumor_size_cm,
      brca1_mutation, brca2_mutation, crp_mg_l, nlr, hemoglobin_g_dl,
      ast_u_l, alt_u_l, creatinine_mg_dl, bun_mg_dl, sodium_meq_l.

A multivariable OLS using ECOG, stage_iv, brain_mets, age, albumin,
LDH, weight loss, CRP, NLR, Hb, Ki67, tumor size, ALP, calcium had
R^2 = 0.72; ECOG (-1.17), albumin (+0.48), and weight loss (-0.077)
all remained highly significant — i.e., main-effect prognostics survive
adjustment.


2. TREATMENT MAIN EFFECTS (iteration 11–12)
-------------------------------------------
Unadjusted differences in mean pfs_months (treated minus untreated):

    treatment_tamoxifen               +0.043 (p=0.077)        NS
    treatment_palbociclib             +1.098 (p≈0)            ****
    treatment_trastuzumab             -0.060 (p=0.029)        small ↓
    treatment_olaparib                -0.036 (p=0.330)        NS
    treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan   -0.007 (p=0.846)        NS
    treatment_pembrolizumab           -0.074 (p=0.018)        small ↓

After adjusting for the full prognostic set (24 covariates including
ECOG, stage, brain_mets, age, labs, ER/PR/HER2/HER2-low, BRCA1/2,
PIK3CA, sex, postmenopausal, node):

    palbociclib:            +1.10 mo (p≈0)   strong, robust
    every other treatment:  |effect| ≤ 0.04 mo, all NS (p > 0.08)

So among the six treatments, only PALBOCICLIB has a measurable
adjusted main effect on PFS in this cohort.


3. CANONICAL TREATMENT-BIOMARKER INTERACTIONS (iterations 13–17)
----------------------------------------------------------------
We tested the textbook breast-cancer interactions; the dataset shows
ONE strong canonical match and several null ones.

  tamoxifen × er_positive:    +0.011 (p=0.67)  -- NO interaction.
        Stratified: tamoxifen effect in ER+ = +0.003 (p=0.89);
                    tamoxifen effect in ER- = +0.002 (p=0.58).
        => REFUTED: tamoxifen does NOT show selective benefit in ER+.

  trastuzumab × her2_positive: +0.011 (p=0.77) -- NO interaction.
        In HER2+ (n=9060): +0.001 (p=0.81); HER2-: -0.013 (p=0.46).
        => REFUTED: trastuzumab shows no PFS benefit in HER2+.

  olaparib × brca1_mutation:  +0.106 (p=0.38)  -- NO interaction.
  olaparib × brca2_mutation:  -0.020 (p=0.87)  -- NO interaction.
  olaparib × brca_any:        +0.044 (p=0.61)
        BRCA1/2 carriers (n=2499): olaparib effect = +0.024 (p=0.77);
        non-carriers: -0.023 (p=0.24).
        => REFUTED: no detectable BRCA-restricted olaparib benefit.

  palbociclib × er_positive:  +1.559 (p≈0)     STRONG positive.
  palbociclib × her2_positive: -1.337 (p≈0)    STRONG negative.
  palbociclib × pik3ca_mutation: -1.665 (p≈0)  STRONG negative.
        In HR+/HER2-           : palbociclib adjusted effect = +1.91 mo (p≈0).
        Greedy 3-feature search yields the strongest subgroup:
          ER+ AND HER2- AND PIK3CA-wt (n=18,681) →  +2.92 mo (p≈0).
        => CONFIRMED, with a critical refinement: PIK3CA mutation
        suppresses the palbociclib benefit, an additional modifier
        beyond the canonical HR+/HER2- target.

  pembrolizumab × er_positive: -0.073 (p=0.031), small but consistent
        with a tilt toward benefit in ER-negative disease.
        Stratified in TNBC (ER-/PR-/HER2-) (n=10,496): adjusted effect
        = +0.008 mo (p=0.14). The sign is favorable but the magnitude
        is trivial — does not approach a clinically meaningful effect.

  sacituzumab × tn_proxy (ER- AND HER2-):   +0.010 (p=0.78), null.
        In TNBC: adjusted sacituzumab effect = +0.005 (p=0.46).
        => Sacituzumab does not show TNBC-restricted benefit.


4. SYSTEMATIC HETEROGENEITY SCREENS (iterations 18–22)
-------------------------------------------------------
Iter 18: 6 treatments × 12 binary modifier features (72 interactions
adjusted for the full prognostic set). Top hits were dominated by
palbociclib:

    palbociclib × er_positive       +1.559   p ≈ 0
    palbociclib × pr_positive       +1.029   p ≈ 0
    palbociclib × her2_positive     -1.337   p ≈ 0
    palbociclib × pik3ca_mutation   -1.665   p ≈ 0
    palbociclib × her2_low          +0.382   p = 5e-67
    sacituzumab × has_brain_mets    +0.193   p = 0.002
    palbociclib × brca2_mutation    -0.159   p = 0.020
    palbociclib × sex_female        +0.047   p = 0.030

For tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib, pembrolizumab, no interaction
with any of the 12 modifiers reached |effect| > 0.10 months at
p < 0.01, again indicating these treatments have no detectable
heterogeneity beyond noise in this cohort.

Iter 19: ECOG×treatment interactions. None were significant; the
palbociclib effect was nearly identical at ECOG=0 (+1.11) and ECOG=2
(+1.07) — meaning ECOG does NOT modify palbociclib benefit.

Iter 20: HER2-restricted trastuzumab×brain-mets interaction (NS),
BRCA-restricted olaparib×LDH interaction (NS). HR+/HER2-/ECOG≤1
palbociclib effect = +1.92 mo (basically identical to HR+/HER2- alone,
confirming ECOG is not a meaningful modifier of palbociclib).

Iter 21: Exhaustive 1- and 2-feature subgroup search ranked by adjusted
treatment effect. For palbociclib, the top subgroups all centered on
ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT (best 2-feature combo: ER+ AND PIK3CA-WT, +2.40
mo, p≈0). Sacituzumab showed an unusual signal restricted to
brain-mets / advanced-burden patients (e.g., stage_iv=1 AND
brain_mets=1 → +0.357 mo, p = 5e-4; pik3ca-WT AND brain_mets=1 →
+0.302 mo, p = 8e-4). For tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib, and
pembrolizumab, no 1- or 2-feature subgroup produced a clinically
meaningful adjusted effect: best estimates were ≤0.4 mo and unstable.

Iter 22: Greedy 3-literal conjunction search (depth 3, min n=300):
    palbociclib  : ER+ AND HER2- AND PIK3CA-WT (n=18,681) → +2.92 mo, p≈0  CONFIRMED, novel refinement.
    sacituzumab  : has_brain_mets=1 AND stage_iv=1 AND node_negative
                   (n=752) → +0.565 mo, p = 9e-5   modest effect in
                   advanced-disease subset.
    tamoxifen    : best subgroup +0.20 mo, p=0.05, n=663 — borderline.
    trastuzumab  : best +0.19 mo, p=0.10 — null.
    olaparib     : best +0.38 mo, p=0.13 in BRCA1+/node+/PIK3CA-WT — null.
    pembrolizumab: best +0.06 mo, p=0.13 in ER-/BRCA2+/HER2- — null.

7. OVERALL CONCLUSIONS
-----------------------
- Strong prognostic baseline: ECOG, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, ki67_pct,
  weight_loss_pct_6mo, albumin_g_dl, ER+, PR+, HER2+, HER2-low, and
  PIK3CA mutation are robust prognostic features for pfs_months in
  this cohort. Age has an unusual positive slope. CRP, NLR, hemoglobin,
  hepatic transaminases, creatinine, BUN, sodium are uninformative.

- Treatment effects are dominated by palbociclib. The single most
  robust treatment-effect-heterogeneity finding is:

      PALBOCICLIB benefit is concentrated in ER+ AND HER2-negative
      AND PIK3CA-wild-type patients (n ≈ 18,681), where the adjusted
      PFS effect is approximately +2.9 months (p ≈ 0). PIK3CA mutation
      strongly suppresses the palbociclib benefit, beyond the
      canonical HR+/HER2- definition.

- For tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib, and pembrolizumab the
  canonical biomarker-defined subgroup effects expected from breast
  oncology (tamoxifen→ER+, trastuzumab→HER2+, olaparib→BRCA carriers,
  pembrolizumab→TNBC) were NOT recovered: stratified treatment
  effects in those subgroups are <0.05 months and are not
  statistically significant. This is the largest discordance between
  this dataset's structure and clinical expectation.

- For sacituzumab govitecan, the dataset shows a non-canonical
  heterogeneity signal: a small but significant adjusted PFS benefit
  in patients with stage_iv = 1 AND has_brain_mets = 1 AND
  node_negative (n=752, +0.57 mo, p ≈ 9e-5). This was discovered by
  greedy subgroup search rather than predicted from biology.

Best-supported final treatment-effect subgroup hypotheses by treatment
(see iteration 25 in transcript.json):

    palbociclib    : ER+ AND HER2- AND PIK3CA-WT  → +2.9 mo PFS, p≈0
    sacituzumab    : has_brain_mets=1 AND stage_iv=1 AND node_negative
                       → +0.57 mo PFS, p≈9e-5
    tamoxifen      : no usable subgroup defined.
    trastuzumab    : no usable subgroup defined.
    olaparib       : no usable subgroup defined.
    pembrolizumab  : weak directional signal in ER-/HER2-, sub-clinical.
