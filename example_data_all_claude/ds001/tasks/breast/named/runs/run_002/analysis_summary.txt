Analysis summary for ds001_breast (50,000 patients)
====================================================

The analysis took the form of a 25-iteration propose-test-refine loop
exploring relationships between baseline features, six treatments, and the
PFS outcome (pfs_months).

1. Baseline cohort and feature main effects
-------------------------------------------
The cohort is balanced across the six treatment indicators (mean confounders
differ by <=0.22 yr in age, <=0.02 in ECOG, <=0.013 in biomarker prevalences),
suggesting a randomized-like or near-random allocation in the synthetic data.

Univariate associations with pfs_months are dominated by:
  - age_years: r = +0.70  (older patients have substantially LONGER PFS).
    This is the single strongest covariate and drives most apparent
    confounding when not adjusted for.
  - ecog_ps: r = -0.32 (ECOG 0/1/2 -> mean PFS 5.6/4.4/3.3).
  - stage_iv: -1.54 months
  - has_brain_mets: -1.05 months
  - er_positive: +0.57 months
  - her2_positive: -0.46 months
  - pik3ca_mutation: -0.56 months
  - albumin_g_dl: +0.10 (per g/dl, modest)
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo: -0.077 per %
  - ki67_pct: -0.019 per %
  - LDH, calcium, ALP showed weak negative correlations.
  - tumor_size_cm, sex_female, postmenopausal, brca1, brca2, CRP, NLR were
    not associated with PFS.

Crude treatment main effects on pfs_months (no adjustment):
  - treatment_palbociclib:           +1.10 months  (p << 0.001) - large
  - treatment_tamoxifen:             +0.04 (NS)
  - treatment_trastuzumab:           -0.06 (p=0.03, very small)
  - treatment_olaparib:              -0.04 (NS)
  - treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan: -0.01 (NS)
  - treatment_pembrolizumab:         -0.07 (p=0.02, very small)

In the full multivariable OLS, the palbociclib coefficient is unchanged
(+1.10, p~0); all other treatments have coefficients within +/- 0.03 months
and none has a clinically meaningful main effect.

2. Treatment-by-biomarker interactions
--------------------------------------
A systematic screen of all treatment x binary feature interactions
(adjusted for age, ECOG, stage, brain mets, ER, HER2, PIK3CA, Ki-67,
albumin, weight loss) showed:

  - treatment_palbociclib x er_positive: +1.56 (p~0)        SUPPORTED
  - treatment_palbociclib x hr_pos:      +1.46 (p~0)        SUPPORTED
  - treatment_palbociclib x pr_positive: +1.03 (p~0)        SUPPORTED
  - treatment_palbociclib x pik3ca_mut:  -1.67 (p~0)        SUPPORTED (negative)
  - treatment_palbociclib x her2_pos:    -1.34 (p~0)        SUPPORTED (negative)
  - treatment_palbociclib x tnbc:        -1.38 (p~0)        SUPPORTED (negative)
  - treatment_palbociclib x ki67_pct:    -0.053/% (p<1e-100) SUPPORTED (negative)

  - treatment_trastuzumab x her2_positive:  +0.009 (p=0.81) REFUTED (no benefit)
  - treatment_olaparib    x brca_any:       +0.045 (p=0.61) REFUTED
  - treatment_olaparib    x brca1_mutation: +0.10 (p=0.41)  REFUTED
  - treatment_olaparib    x brca2_mutation: -0.02 (p=0.90)  REFUTED
  - treatment_pembrolizumab x tnbc:         +0.067 (p=0.09) WEAK / borderline
  - treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan x tnbc: -0.02 (p=0.70)  REFUTED
  - treatment_tamoxifen x er_positive:      +0.007 (p=0.78) REFUTED

3. Palbociclib subgroup characterization (the dominant signal)
--------------------------------------------------------------
Stratified analyses confirmed that the palbociclib effect is essentially
binary in the joint biomarker space:

   subgroup                                 n     palbo_diff    p
   --------------------------------------   ----- ----------   ---
   ER+ / HER2- / PIK3CA-wt (the target)    18,681  +2.91        ~0
   HR+ / HER2- / PIK3CA-wt                 19,918  +2.74        ~0
   HR+ / HER2-                             30,444  +1.81        ~0
   HR+ (any HER2/PIK3CA)                   37,166  +1.48        ~0
   ER+ (any HER2/PIK3CA)                   34,886  +1.58        ~0
   ER- subgroup                            15,114  -0.02        NS
   ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-mut                     9,908  +0.02        NS
   ER+/HER2+/PIK3CA-wt                      4,107  -0.02        NS
   ER-/PR-/HER2-/PIK3CA-wt (TNBC subset)    8,079  -0.04        NS

The 2x2x2 grid of (ER, HER2, PIK3CA) cleanly localizes the entire
palbociclib effect to ONE cell: ER+ / HER2- / PIK3CA-wt. Each of the other
seven cells has |effect| <= 0.13 months and is non-significant.

PR positivity alone is insufficient: ER-/PR+/HER2-/PIK3CAwt patients
(n=1,237) show no benefit (+0.09, NS), while ER+/PR-/HER2-/PIK3CAwt
(n=2,765) do benefit (+2.69). ER positivity is the operative driver.

Within the responsive subgroup, the only secondary modifier we identified
is Ki-67: the palbociclib x ki67_pct interaction is -0.053 per %
(p < 1e-100), meaning that proliferation-high tumors derive less benefit.
HER2-low status, BRCA mutation status, age, postmenopausal status, sex,
nodal status, brain mets, and stage all left the effect roughly
unchanged within the target subgroup.

Multivariable adjustment within the target subgroup yields essentially the
same effect as crude (+2.92 vs +2.91), and confounders are extremely well
balanced across treatment_palbociclib within this subgroup, so the +2.9
month estimate is unbiased.

4. Other treatments: subgroup heterogeneity searches
----------------------------------------------------
For each remaining treatment, we ran:
  - Targeted stratified analyses in the canonical responsive subgroup;
  - Treatment x feature interaction screens (binary and continuous);
  - Exhaustive enumeration of 2- and 3-feature subgroups (>=50/arm);
  - 3-way interactions with the canonical biomarker;
  - Within-subgroup continuous interaction screens.

Results:

treatment_trastuzumab. Within HER2+ (n=9,060), adjusted effect = -0.0002
(p=0.95). No subset of HER2+ patients (by node, brain mets, ER, PR, BRCA,
PIK3CA, age, Ki-67, albumin, etc.) shows benefit. Crude effect is slightly
negative across many strata; this is a uniform, small confounded artifact
(stage IV is slightly over-represented on trastuzumab and stage IV is the
strongest negative prognostic feature). REFUTED.

treatment_olaparib. Adjusted effect in BRCA-any (n=2,499) is +0.025 (NS).
In BRCA1+ alone, adjusted +0.084 (NS); in BRCA2+, -0.033 (NS). Apparent
crude signals (e.g., BRCA1+/male/node+ +1.6 unadjusted) reflect small-cell
chance with confounding by age. No biomarker subgroup of any size with an
adjusted effect surviving scrutiny. REFUTED.

treatment_pembrolizumab. Crude TNBC effect +0.07 (NS). Premenopausal-TNBC
crude +0.275 (p=0.003, n=4,163), but adjusted is +0.015 (p=0.05) - the
crude was driven almost entirely by premenopausal-TNBC pembro patients
being slightly older (+0.7 yr) and less stage IV (-4.8 percentage points)
than the comparator. After age adjustment the effect collapses. The
pembro x age interaction within TNBC (-0.0013/yr, p=0.013) and pembro x
weight_loss (+0.005/%, p=0.001) interactions are nominally significant but
the tertile-stratified pattern is not monotonic. REFUTED as a clinically
useful signal.

treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan. Adjusted effect in TNBC (n=10,496) =
+0.006 (NS). In HER2-low (n=18,543) = -0.03 (NS). In TNBC/stage IV the
crude effect was negative (-0.26, p=0.02), but the direction is opposite
to expected and probably reflects a small selection effect; no robust
positive subgroup. REFUTED.

treatment_tamoxifen. Adjusted effect in ER+ ~0; no positive subgroup of
ER+/postmenopausal/HER2- patients shows benefit. The strongest crude
signal (+0.15 in male/HR-) is biologically nonsensical and is driven by
chance plus age confounding (the 2,115 men in HR- are slightly enriched
for older age in the tamoxifen arm). REFUTED.

5. Cross-treatment specificity
------------------------------
We tested each treatment x palbo_target_subgroup interaction. Only
treatment_palbociclib has a substantial subgroup-specific effect
(+2.91, p~0). For all other treatments the palbo_target interaction is
small (|beta| < 0.09):
   tamoxifen: 0.00 (p=0.95)
   trastuzumab: -0.02 (p=0.56)
   olaparib: -0.07 (p=0.06)
   sacituzumab: +0.06 (p=0.10)
   pembrolizumab: -0.08 (p=0.008)
The pembrolizumab finding is small and likely reflects residual
confounding rather than a genuine harm.

6. Final best-supported subgroup hypothesis
-------------------------------------------
For the only treatment with usable variation in effect (palbociclib):

   In patients with er_positive = 1 AND her2_positive = 0 AND
   pik3ca_mutation = 0, treatment_palbociclib increases pfs_months by
   approximately +2.9 months (95% CI ~+2.85 to +3.00; p < 1e-300; n =
   18,681). Outside this subgroup the adjusted effect is essentially
   zero. The unfavorable values that suppress the palbociclib effect are
   her2_positive = 1 and pik3ca_mutation = 1; absence of er_positive
   also abolishes benefit.

For every other treatment in the cohort no treatment-effect-defining
subgroup with adjusted effect on pfs_months exceeding |0.05| months
survives multivariable adjustment and multiple-comparison scrutiny.

7. Overall conclusions
----------------------
- The dominant outcome-related signal in this cohort is the very strong,
  biologically interpretable palbociclib benefit confined to an ER+/HER2-/
  PIK3CA-wt subgroup, attenuated within that subgroup by high Ki-67.
- None of the other five treatments (tamoxifen, trastuzumab, olaparib,
  sacituzumab govitecan, pembrolizumab) shows a meaningful PFS benefit in
  this dataset, including in their canonical biomarker-defined subgroups
  (ER+, HER2+, BRCA+, TNBC). Apparent crude effects in these treatments
  consistently disappear after multivariable adjustment, which is largely
  driven by age (which has an unusually strong positive association with
  pfs_months in this cohort, r = +0.70).
- Among baseline features, the strongest negative prognostic factors are
  ECOG performance status, stage IV, brain metastases, PIK3CA mutation,
  HER2 positivity, low albumin, weight loss, and high Ki-67. The strongest
  positive features are older age, ER positivity, and higher albumin.
