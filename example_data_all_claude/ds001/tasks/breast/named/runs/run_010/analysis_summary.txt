ds001_breast — analysis summary
=================================

Cohort and outcome
------------------
The dataset contains 50,000 patients with one continuous outcome, pfs_months
(progression-free survival in months). PFS is unimodal with mean 4.69, median
4.54, SD 2.50, and range 0.00–16.66 months. There is no missing data.
Treatment columns are six binary indicators: tamoxifen, palbociclib,
trastuzumab, olaparib, sacituzumab_govitecan, and pembrolizumab. Patients can
receive any combination (the columns are not mutually exclusive).

Approach
--------
I ran 25 iterations covering: (i) outcome distribution, (ii) demographic and
disease-burden main effects, (iii) receptor and mutation main effects,
(iv) lab/biomarker main effects, (v) treatment main effects (unadjusted and
adjusted), (vi) biology-driven treatment × biomarker interactions
(tamoxifen×ER, palbociclib×ER, trastuzumab×HER2+, olaparib×BRCA1/2,
sacituzumab×HER2-low, pembrolizumab×HER2+/TNBC), (vii) systematic
treatment × feature interaction screens across all 31 candidate modifiers,
(viii) targeted subgroup tests (effect-in vs effect-out vs interaction),
(ix) three-way interactions, (x) exhaustive two-binary-feature subgroup
discovery, (xi) joint multivariable models with all six target interactions,
and (xii) a final triple-defined subgroup test for palbociclib.

Prognostic main effects (all consistent with clinical expectation)
------------------------------------------------------------------
- ecog_ps: very strong negative effect, ~ −1.17 months per ECOG point in the
  joint model (univariate slope ≈ −1.18 mo/unit, p ≈ 0). PFS falls
  monotonically from ECOG 0 → 2 by more than 2 months total.
- stage_iv: −1.55 months in the joint model, p ≈ 0.
- has_brain_mets: −0.98 months in the joint model, p ≈ 0.
- albumin_g_dl: +0.48 months per g/dL (higher albumin → longer PFS, p ≈ 0).
- ldh_u_l: small negative slope, p ≈ 1e-18.
- her2_positive: −0.57 months unadjusted (worse PFS), highly significant.
- tnbc (ER−/PR−/HER2−): −0.38 months in joint model, p ≈ 1e-60.
- er_positive: small negative shift (−0.20 in joint model, p ≈ 1e-22) — note
  this becomes negative after controlling for tnbc, palbociclib, and
  interactions; the marginal/unadjusted effect of ER+ is negligible/positive.
- brca_any: small negative shift (−0.05, p = 0.034).
- Other labs (AST, ALT, bilirubin, creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium,
  calcium, hemoglobin, alkaline phosphatase, CRP, NLR, weight loss) showed
  no robust independent association with PFS in this dataset.

Treatment main effects
----------------------
Unadjusted Welch t-tests (treated vs untreated) for each agent:
  tamoxifen:    +0.043 mo, p = 0.077
  palbociclib:  +1.098 mo, p ≈ 0  (very large positive)
  trastuzumab:  −0.060 mo, p = 0.029
  olaparib:     −0.036 mo, p = 0.33
  sacituzumab:  −0.007 mo, p = 0.85
  pembrolizumab: −0.074 mo, p = 0.018

After adjusting for age, sex, ECOG, stage IV, brain mets, tumor size, albumin,
LDH, and ki67, only palbociclib retains a large, clearly significant main
effect (+1.10 mo, p ≈ 0). Pembrolizumab remains marginally negative
(−0.035, p = 0.037). The other four treatments have adjusted main effects
indistinguishable from zero.

Biology-driven treatment × biomarker interactions
-------------------------------------------------
- palbociclib × er_positive: interaction coef = +1.58, p ≈ 0. Within ER+
  patients (n = 34,886) palbociclib raises mean PFS by +1.58 months (p ≈ 0);
  within ER− patients (n = 15,114) the effect is −0.02, n.s. This is the
  cleanest treatment-effect heterogeneity in the dataset.
- olaparib × brca1_mutation: interaction coef = +0.20, p = 0.12 (not
  significant); within BRCA1-or-BRCA2 mutated patients (n = 2,499, 239
  treated) the unadjusted olaparib effect is +0.35 mo, p = 0.04 — a small,
  marginally significant signal.
- olaparib × brca2_mutation: interaction coef = −0.04, p = 0.79.
- tamoxifen × er_positive: interaction coef = +0.005, p = 0.85 (NULL).
- trastuzumab × her2_positive: interaction coef = +0.028, p = 0.47 (NULL).
- sacituzumab × her2_low: interaction coef = +0.035, p = 0.40 (NULL).
- pembrolizumab × her2_positive: interaction coef = +0.044, p = 0.31 (NULL).
- pembrolizumab × er_positive: interaction coef = −0.065, p = 0.07; within
  TNBC (ER−/PR−/HER2−, n = 10,496) pembrolizumab effect is +0.07, n.s.;
  outside TNBC the pembrolizumab effect is −0.11, p = 0.0017.

Systematic treatment × feature interaction screens
--------------------------------------------------
For each of the six treatments I screened interaction-coefficient p-values
across all 31 candidate features (binary + continuous). Highlights:
- treatment_palbociclib: top modifiers were er_positive (p≈0), pr_positive
  (p≈0), her2_positive (p≈0, negative — palbociclib loses benefit when
  HER2+), pik3ca_mutation (p≈0, negative — PIK3CA mutation suppresses the
  benefit), and ki67_pct (p≈0).
- treatment_pembrolizumab: top modifier was ecog_ps (interaction p = 0.035,
  benefit/harm gets worse as PS worsens) and er_positive (p = 0.07).
- treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan: top modifier was creatinine_mg_dl
  (interaction p = 0.011) and has_brain_mets (p = 0.030); these did not
  translate into a clinically/biologically interpretable benefit subgroup.
- treatment_olaparib: top modifier was postmenopausal (p = 0.061), then
  brca1_mutation (p = 0.12).
- treatment_tamoxifen and treatment_trastuzumab: no modifier reached
  interaction p < 0.05; the smallest p-values (~0.10) were for stage_iv and
  did not align with biology.

Refined subgroup discovery (two-binary-feature exhaustive scan)
---------------------------------------------------------------
For palbociclib, the largest-effect two-feature subgroups are
er_positive=1 & pik3ca_mutation=0 (eff = +2.38 mo, n = 22,788, p ≈ 0) and
pr_positive=1 & pik3ca_mutation=0 (eff = +2.25 mo, n = 20,891, p ≈ 0).
Drilling further, the triple-defined subgroup
er_positive=1 & her2_positive=0 & pik3ca_mutation=0 (n = 18,681; 6,571
treated) shows an unadjusted palbociclib effect of +2.91 months (p ≈ 0).
A formal three-way interaction term palbociclib × er_positive ×
(1 − pik3ca_mutation) in an adjusted OLS has coef = +2.39 and p ≈ 0
— confirming that PIK3CA mutation suppresses the palbociclib benefit even
within ER+/HER2- patients.

For olaparib, two-feature scans surfaced brca1_mutation=1 & node_positive=1
(eff = +0.87, n = 654, p = 0.035) and brca1_mutation=1 & sex_female=0
(eff = +0.84, n = 696, p = 0.012) — these are likely chance (multiple
testing) within an already small BRCA1+ subgroup.

For trastuzumab, sacituzumab, pembrolizumab, and tamoxifen the largest-effect
two-feature subgroups are not biologically aligned with the targets and are
small (n ~500–800), with effects mostly negative — consistent with no true
benefit signal in this cohort.

Joint model with all six target interactions
---------------------------------------------
A single OLS including all main effects and all six biology-driven
treatment × biomarker interaction terms (R² = 0.812, n = 50,000) gives:
  treatment_palbociclib_x_er_positive: coef = +1.561, p ≈ 0  ← only sig.
  treatment_tamoxifen_x_er_positive:   coef = +0.007, p = 0.76
  treatment_trastuzumab_x_her2_positive: coef = +0.017, p = 0.59
  treatment_olaparib_x_brca_any:       coef = +0.021, p = 0.78
  treatment_sacituzumab_x_her2_low:    coef = +0.052, p = 0.12
  treatment_pembrolizumab_x_tnbc:      coef = +0.052, p = 0.12

After accounting for these interactions, no treatment has a residual main
effect that is significant at p<0.05 except palbociclib's interaction.

Final best-supported subgroup hypotheses by treatment
-----------------------------------------------------
- palbociclib: PFS benefit is concentrated in er_positive=1 AND
  her2_positive=0 AND pik3ca_mutation=0 patients (n = 18,681), with
  unadjusted +2.9-month gain (p ≈ 0). The benefit is preserved on
  multivariable adjustment (+2.92 months, p ≈ 0). The triple interaction
  palbociclib × er_positive × (1 − pik3ca_mutation) has coef = +2.39,
  p ≈ 0. Mechanistically consistent with the known dependence of CDK4/6
  inhibition on intact ER signaling and on the absence of PI3K-pathway
  bypass.
- olaparib: small positive PFS signal in patients with brca1_mutation=1 OR
  brca2_mutation=1 (eff = +0.35, p = 0.04 unadjusted; +0.06, p = 0.49
  adjusted). The signal is present but not robust to confounder adjustment;
  it is the only directionally biology-consistent treatment effect besides
  palbociclib.
- tamoxifen, trastuzumab, sacituzumab_govitecan, pembrolizumab: no
  biology-aligned subgroup with statistically supported PFS benefit was
  found. Within their canonical target subgroups (ER+, HER2+, HER2-low, and
  TNBC respectively) the adjusted treatment effect on PFS is ≤ |0.1| month
  and not statistically significant.

Hypotheses supported vs. refuted
--------------------------------
Supported
  • Higher ECOG, stage IV, brain mets → worse PFS (large, expected).
  • Higher albumin → better PFS; higher LDH → worse PFS.
  • HER2-positivity and TNBC phenotype carry worse baseline PFS.
  • Palbociclib prolongs PFS in ER+ patients (very strong).
  • Palbociclib benefit is suppressed by PIK3CA mutation; the strongest
    enriched subgroup is ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT.
  • A small olaparib benefit is present in BRCA1- or BRCA2-mutated patients,
    weaker after adjustment.

Refuted (in this cohort)
  • Tamoxifen does NOT prolong PFS in ER+ patients (effect ≈ 0, p ≈ 0.45).
  • Trastuzumab does NOT prolong PFS in HER2+ patients (small negative
    point estimates everywhere).
  • Sacituzumab govitecan does NOT prolong PFS in HER2-low or HER2-low/ER−
    patients (effect ≈ 0).
  • Pembrolizumab does NOT prolong PFS in TNBC patients; its overall
    marginally negative effect is concentrated in non-TNBC, ECOG ≥ 1
    patients.

Caveats
-------
- This is an observational dataset; treatment effects are not randomized.
  Adjustment was made for nine prognostic variables (age, sex, ECOG, stage
  IV, brain mets, tumor size, albumin, LDH, ki67), but residual confounding
  is possible.
- The pfs_months distribution is treated as a continuous variable; no
  censoring indicator is provided.
- Only one outcome (pfs_months) had usable variation, so the
  treatment-effect heterogeneity searches required by the protocol were run
  on it for each of the six treatments.
- Multiple testing was not formally controlled in the exhaustive subgroup
  scan (iter 22), so any small-p findings outside the strong palbociclib
  and olaparib signals should be interpreted as hypothesis-generating only.
