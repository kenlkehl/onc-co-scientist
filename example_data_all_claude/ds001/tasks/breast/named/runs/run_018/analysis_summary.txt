Analysis Summary — ds001_breast (n=50,000; outcome: pfs_months)
================================================================

Approach
--------
I ran 25 iterations of propose-test-refine analyses on the dataset, working from
univariate screens of features against PFS, to multivariable adjustment, to a
systematic treatment-by-feature interaction screen, to refined subgroup
definitions for each treatment with usable variation. All analyses used OLS
regression on PFS in months (the only outcome) and unequal-variance t-tests for
stratified comparisons.

1. Prognostic features (independent associations with pfs_months)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
A multivariable OLS over all 36 features and 6 treatments achieved adjusted
R^2 = 0.79. Independent prognostic factors with strong direction-consistent
effects (all p < 1e-15 in the adjusted model):

  - age_years            +0.176 mo per year       (LONGER PFS in older patients;
                                                   univariate r = +0.70 — by far
                                                   the dominant prognostic
                                                   feature in this cohort)
  - ecog_ps              -1.171 mo per level       (ECOG 0/1/2 means 5.64/4.44/3.29 mo)
  - stage_iv             -1.549 mo
  - has_brain_mets       -0.983 mo
  - weight_loss_pct_6mo  -0.077 mo per %
  - albumin_g_dl         +0.476 mo per g/dL
  - ki67_pct             -0.019 mo per %
  - er_positive          +0.532 mo
  - her2_positive        -0.471 mo
  - pik3ca_mutation      -0.582 mo (independent of treatment)
  - ldh_u_l              -0.0005 mo per U/L

Features without an independent association: sex_female, postmenopausal,
node_positive, her2_low, brca1_mutation, brca2_mutation, pr_positive,
tumor_size_cm, crp_mg_l, nlr, hemoglobin_g_dl, ALP, AST, ALT, total bilirubin,
creatinine, BUN, sodium, potassium, calcium.

The age-PFS relationship is unusually strong and positive — older patients
have substantially longer PFS in this dataset. I cannot adjudicate whether
this reflects a biologically real, indolent-disease-of-the-elderly pattern
or a feature of how this EHR-derived cohort was assembled, but it is a
robust feature of the data.

2. Treatment main effects (unadjusted)
--------------------------------------
  - treatment_palbociclib            +1.098 mo  (p ≈ 0)        ★ STRONG SIGNAL
  - treatment_tamoxifen              +0.043 mo  (p = 0.077)    null
  - treatment_trastuzumab            -0.060 mo  (p = 0.029)    null
  - treatment_olaparib               -0.036 mo  (p = 0.33)     null
  - treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan  -0.007 mo  (p = 0.85)     null
  - treatment_pembrolizumab          -0.074 mo  (p = 0.018)    null

Only palbociclib has a meaningful average effect. The very-small significant
"effects" of trastuzumab and pembrolizumab are in the wrong direction and are
fully explained by confounding (vanish on adjustment).

3. Adjusted, canonical-subgroup checks for each treatment
---------------------------------------------------------
For each drug, I tested its effect within the canonical biomarker subgroup
where clinical literature would expect benefit, with OLS adjustment for age,
ecog_ps, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, and other relevant covariates:

  - tamoxifen   in ER+:        +0.008 mo  (p = 0.67)
  - trastuzumab in HER2+:      +0.006 mo  (p = 0.60)
  - olaparib    in BRCA1/2+:   +0.044 mo  (p = 0.62)   (unadjusted +0.346, p=0.04
                                                       — but the marginal signal
                                                       does not survive
                                                       covariate adjustment)
  - sacituzumab in TNBC:       +0.014 mo  (p = 0.32)
  - pembrolizumab in TNBC:     +0.011 mo  (p = 0.35)

So even the textbook biomarker-matched subgroups show no detectable benefit
for any of these five drugs in this cohort.

4. Systematic T x B interaction screens
---------------------------------------
I ran adjusted OLS with treatment-by-biomarker interactions for all 6 treatments
crossed with 11 binary biomarkers and 10 continuous biomarkers (medians).

Significant interactions (adjusted, |coef| ≥ 0.1 and p < 0.001) ALL involve
palbociclib:

  - palbociclib × er_positive       +1.559   (effect concentrated in ER+)
  - palbociclib × her2_positive     -1.338   (effect abolished in HER2+)
  - palbociclib × pik3ca_mutation   -1.665   (effect abolished by PIK3CA mut)
  - palbociclib × pr_positive       +1.030   (correlated with ER+)
  - palbociclib × her2_low          +0.383   (explained by ER correlation;
                                              no residual modification once
                                              ER/HER2/PIK3CA are fixed — see §6)
  - palbociclib × ki67_pct          -0.0532 per %  (effect declines with rising
                                                    proliferation index)

No other treatment showed any biomarker interaction passing the same threshold.
This means the biomarker heterogeneity in this cohort is concentrated entirely
on palbociclib's effect.

5. Refined palbociclib subgroup
-------------------------------
Stratified analyses converged on the following definition:

  Palbociclib effect in ER+/HER2- (n = 28,589):                   +1.92 mo (p ≈ 0)
  Palbociclib effect in ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT (n = 18,681):         +2.91 mo (p ≈ 0)
  Palbociclib effect in ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-mut (n = 9,908):         +0.02 mo (p = 0.62)
  Palbociclib effect in ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT, by ki67_pct tertile:
       low   (ki67 < 8.9):                                        +4.94 mo (p ≈ 0)
       mid   (8.9 ≤ ki67 ≤ 16.5):                                 +3.70 mo (p ≈ 0)
       high  (ki67 > 16.5):                                       -0.02 mo (p = 0.77)

A cutoff of ki67_pct < 14 cleanly separates "responder" and "non-responder"
zones inside ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT:
  ki67 < 14:   palb on 9.228 vs off 4.284 → +4.94 mo (p ≈ 0)
  ki67 ≥ 14:   palb on 4.388 vs off 4.322 → +0.07  (p = 0.18)

Adjusted OLS within ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT/ki67<14 (n = 10,752) for treatment
palbociclib, controlling for age, ecog_ps, stage_iv, has_brain_mets,
weight_loss_pct_6mo, albumin_g_dl, ki67_pct: coef = +4.977 mo, p ≈ 0. The
adjusted estimate is essentially identical to the raw difference, confirming
this is not driven by demographic or prognostic confounding.

Leave-one-out verification (each predicate is binding):

  Drop er_positive=1:        +3.526   (loses ~1.4 mo)
  Drop her2_positive=0:      +4.063   (loses ~0.9 mo)
  Drop pik3ca_mutation=0:    +3.222   (loses ~1.7 mo)
  Drop ki67<14:              +2.908   (loses ~2.0 mo)

Removing any predicate substantially reduces the estimated palbociclib benefit
— so all four are required to define the maximal-effect subgroup. PIK3CA
mutation and high ki67_pct are the two principal suppressors that turn an
otherwise-eligible ER+/HER2- patient into a palbociclib non-responder.

6. Modifiers ruled out
----------------------
  - Within ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT, her2_low does not further modify the palbociclib
    effect (palb +2.92 mo with her2_low=1, +2.90 with her2_low=0). The marginal
    palbociclib×her2_low interaction in §4 is driven by correlation with
    er_positive.
  - Within ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT, postmenopausal status does not bind
    (palb +2.82 postmenopausal vs +3.04 pre-menopausal).
  - Within ER+/HER2-/PIK3CA-WT, brca1/2, node_positive, has_brain_mets, and
    stage_iv did not modify the palbociclib effect.

7. Other treatments — heterogeneity searches
--------------------------------------------
For each of the other five treatments I ran the same adjusted T×B interaction
screen (11 binary + 10 continuous biomarkers) and explored canonical
subgroups (ER+ for tamoxifen; HER2+ and HER2-low for trastuzumab; BRCA1/2+
for olaparib; TNBC, high CRP, high NLR for sacituzumab and pembrolizumab):

  - Tamoxifen: no subgroup with |effect| ≥ 0.2 mo and p < 0.05 after adjustment.
  - Trastuzumab: no benefit in HER2+ (-0.079 mo, p=0.15) or HER2-low (-0.016,
    p=0.73) — surprising relative to clinical expectation but consistent across
    every cut.
  - Olaparib: marginal +0.35 mo signal in BRCA1/2+ (p=0.04 unadjusted) does not
    survive adjustment (+0.044, p=0.62) and is not biologically robust here.
  - Sacituzumab: weak suggestion in has_brain_mets=1 (within-subgroup +0.16 mo,
    p=0.18; adjusted T×B coef +0.195, p=0.0019), but the within-subgroup
    estimate is not significant — exploratory only.
  - Pembrolizumab: small significant negative interaction with er_positive
    (-0.075, p=0.028); magnitude trivial and consistent with confounding.

8. Conclusions
--------------
A. Palbociclib has a large, biologically interpretable, and adjustment-robust
   PFS benefit in a sharply defined subgroup of this cohort:

   ★ er_positive=1 AND her2_positive=0 AND pik3ca_mutation=0 AND ki67_pct<14
     → palbociclib increases pfs_months by ≈ +4.94 mo (adjusted +4.98 mo).

   PIK3CA mutation and high ki67_pct each independently suppress the
   palbociclib effect; both must be absent for the full benefit to be
   observed. The overall +1.10-mo main effect of palbociclib in the cohort
   is the diluted average over a strong responder subgroup and a much
   larger non-responder population.

B. None of treatment_tamoxifen, treatment_trastuzumab, treatment_olaparib,
   treatment_sacituzumab_govitecan, or treatment_pembrolizumab shows a
   biomarker-defined subgroup in this cohort with a meaningful (≥0.2 mo)
   and adjustment-robust improvement in pfs_months. Either these therapies
   are simply ineffective in this dataset's case mix, or any benefit is
   masked by the way the cohort was assembled.

C. The dominant prognostic predictors are age_years (positive, very
   strong), ecog_ps, stage_iv, has_brain_mets, weight_loss_pct_6mo,
   albumin_g_dl, ki67_pct, er_positive (positive), her2_positive (negative),
   pik3ca_mutation (negative, independent of treatment), and ldh_u_l. Many
   commonly-used prognostic markers (CRP, NLR, hemoglobin, liver enzymes,
   creatinine, electrolytes, tumor size, node status, menopausal status,
   sex) had no detectable independent association with PFS in this cohort.
