ds001_breast — analysis summary
=================================

Dataset: 50,000 patient records, 37 anonymized features, 1 outcome (pfs_months,
range 0–16.66, mean 4.69). No missing data. The analysis proceeded through 25
iterations of propose-and-test, beginning with univariate screens, moving to a
full multivariable regression, and then progressively narrowing in on
treatment-effect heterogeneity using pairwise and three-way subgroup analyses
followed by a continuous-modifier search.

1. Main effects (iterations 1–3)
--------------------------------
Univariate screens identified a clear hierarchy of features associated with
pfs_months. The strongest single predictor by far was feature_012 (continuous,
30–90, mean 65), Spearman rho = +0.726 against PFS, p < 1e-300. Mean PFS rose
monotonically from 1.70 months in the lowest decile to 7.84 months in the
highest. The next-strongest signals were the ordinal feature_005 (mean PFS by
level: 5.64 → 4.44 → 3.29; Δ ≈ −2.35 months), feature_006 (binary, −1.54
months), feature_001 (binary, −1.05 months), feature_013 (binary, +1.10
months), feature_029 (binary, +0.57 months), feature_028 (binary, −0.56 months)
and feature_018 (binary, −0.46 months). A handful of continuous features had
real but smaller effects: feature_033 (negative), feature_004 (negative),
feature_024 (positive), and feature_031 (slight negative).

A multivariable OLS containing every feature (R² = 0.79) confirmed the
independent significance of feature_012, feature_005, feature_006, feature_001,
feature_013, feature_029, feature_028, feature_018, feature_033, feature_024,
feature_004 and feature_031. Two features whose univariate signals appeared
modest — feature_023 (univariate +0.36) and feature_016 (+0.11) — became
non-significant after adjustment, indicating they were confounded by feature_013
and feature_029.

Iteration 3 verified that feature_012’s strong effect is largely prognostic,
not predictive: rho with PFS was +0.81 in patients with feature_029=0 and +0.69
in patients with feature_029=1 (similar magnitudes). Feature_005 also did not
modify the feature_029 treatment effect (effects across its three levels in the
responsive subgroup: +2.86, +3.00, +2.77).

2. Pairwise interaction screen (iterations 4–5)
-----------------------------------------------
A systematic pairwise interaction search across the 18 binary features yielded
a small number of very strong interactions on pfs_months (interaction
coefficients from OLS pfs ~ a + b + a*b):

  feature_029 × feature_013   +1.59  (p = 9e-229)
  feature_018 × feature_013   −1.36  (p = 2e-118)
  feature_023 × feature_013   +1.09  (p = 3e-117)
  feature_029 × feature_028   −0.80  (p = 1e-56)
  feature_018 × feature_028   +0.70  (p = 4e-31)
  feature_023 × feature_028   −0.56  (p = 1e-30)

Stratified means clarified each pattern:
  • feature_029 effect was +0.011 (NS) in feature_013=0 patients but +1.605
    (p < 1e-300) in feature_013=1 patients — i.e., the apparent main effect of
    feature_029 was entirely concentrated in the feature_013=1 subgroup.
  • feature_023 followed exactly the same pattern (effect +1.07 only in
    feature_013=1).
  • feature_018 carried no effect when feature_013=0 but a strong negative
    effect (−1.35 months, p = 3e-180) when feature_013=1, exactly nullifying
    the feature_013 favorable signal in feature_018-positive patients.
  • feature_029 effect was +0.85 in feature_028=0 but +0.05 (NS) in feature_028=1
    — feature_028=1 abolishes the feature_029 benefit. The same null effect of
    feature_023 within feature_028=1 was observed.

These three biomarker-like binaries (feature_013, feature_028, feature_018)
collectively define which patients respond to the candidate treatments
feature_029 and feature_023.

3. Joint biomarker subgroup (iterations 6–7)
--------------------------------------------
A three-way subgroup analysis over (feature_013, feature_028, feature_018)
showed that the feature_029 treatment effect is concentrated in a single one
of the eight defined cells: feature_013=1 AND feature_028=0 AND feature_018=0.
In that cell (n = 9,326) the effect was +2.94 months (p ≈ 0). All seven other
cells produced effects between −0.04 and +0.18 months, none statistically
significant. Feature_023 followed the identical pattern (+1.97 months in the
same cell, all other cells null).

Within this joint subgroup, screening every binary feature for an effect on
PFS revealed only four with p < 0.01: feature_029 (+2.94), feature_023
(+1.97), feature_006 (−1.52) and feature_001 (−1.12). Feature_006 and
feature_001 retained essentially the same negative magnitudes inside the
subgroup as in the whole cohort, identifying them as subgroup-independent
prognostic markers rather than treatment modifiers.

4. Continuous modifier search (iterations 9–11)
-----------------------------------------------
A treatment × continuous interaction screen across all 12 continuous features
within the responsive subgroup found exactly one significant continuous
modifier of feature_029: feature_033 (interaction term −0.133 per unit,
p = 1e-160). Binning feature_033 revealed a sharp threshold: the feature_029
effect was approximately +5 months in patients with feature_033 ≤ 15, then
collapsed to zero in patients above that cutoff:

  feature_033 ≤ 5     :  feature_029 effect = +5.02
  feature_033 5–10    :  +5.02
  feature_033 10–15   :  +3.94
  feature_033 15–20   :  +0.20  (NS)
  feature_033 20–30   :  +0.02  (NS)
  feature_033 30–50   :  +0.25  (NS)
  feature_033 > 50    :  +0.26  (NS)

Thus feature_033 ≤ 15 emerged as the fourth necessary condition for treatment
benefit. Sensitivity analyses across cutoffs 10–16 showed monotonic dilution
as the threshold loosens, supporting a real biological boundary near 15.

The fully refined responsive subgroup — feature_013=1 AND feature_028=0 AND
feature_018=0 AND feature_033 ≤ 15 — contained 5,756 patients (11.5% of the
cohort). Within it the feature_029 effect was +4.62 months PFS (mean PFS
8.94 on treatment vs 4.31 off, p ≈ 0); outside it the effect was +0.05
months (NS).

5. Adjunct feature_023 (iteration 12)
-------------------------------------
Within the maximal responsive subgroup the apparent +3.07-month effect of
feature_023 was almost entirely collinear with feature_029 use (the 2×2 of
treatments shows: (29=0,23=0) 4.29 → (29=1,23=0) 8.69 → (29=1,23=1) 8.98).
Stratifying on feature_029 in this subgroup, feature_023 produced a small
marginal benefit on top of feature_029 of +0.29 months (p = 0.010), but no
detectable benefit when feature_029 = 0 (+0.15, p = 0.28). Feature_023
therefore behaves as a small adjunct to feature_029, contributing little
independent treatment effect.

6. Negative controls and orphan features (iterations 13–18)
-----------------------------------------------------------
Within each non-responsive stratum (feature_028=1, feature_018=1, or
feature_013=0) no binary feature produced a positive treatment-like signal;
only the universal negative prognostic markers feature_006 and feature_001
remained significant. The feature_029 and feature_023 effects therefore do
not “hide” in any other subgroup.

A series of features had no clinically meaningful association with PFS:
feature_011, feature_030, feature_019, feature_009, feature_026, feature_032,
feature_022, feature_008, feature_020, feature_015, plus the lab-like
continuous variables feature_002, feature_007, feature_010, feature_014,
feature_017, feature_021, feature_025, feature_027, feature_034, feature_035,
feature_036 and feature_037 (all |Spearman ρ| < 0.02 in absolute terms).
feature_016, although univariately associated, lost significance in the
multivariable model and showed no effect (+0.12, p = 0.17) within the maximal
responsive subgroup.

7. Other prognostic continuous features (iteration 17)
------------------------------------------------------
feature_004 (range 0–25, mean 3.84) had a small but real negative association
(overall rho = −0.116; persisted in subgroup at rho = −0.055). feature_024
(range 1.5–5.5) had a small positive association (overall rho = +0.102;
subgroup rho = +0.065). Both contribute prognostically but neither modifies
treatment effects.

8. Confirmatory iterations (19–25)
----------------------------------
feature_005 was confirmed as a strong dose-monotonic prognostic marker
(adjusted slope −1.17 per level, p ≈ 0); feature_012’s near-uniform
prognostic effect was confirmed by the absence of meaningful interaction
terms with any binary feature. The systematic 4-way subgroup search (16
cells defined by feature_013 × feature_028 × feature_018 × feature_033 ≤/> 15)
showed feature_029 and feature_023 effects significant only in the
(1,0,0,≤15) cell. Outside the maximal responsive subgroup mean PFS was 4.31
and no feature behaved like a treatment.

9. Hypotheses supported vs refuted
----------------------------------
Supported (statistically and across confirmatory checks):
  • feature_012 is a strong, primarily prognostic, positive predictor of PFS.
  • feature_005 is a strong dose-monotonic negative prognostic marker.
  • feature_006 (-1.5) and feature_001 (-1.0) are stable negative prognostic
    markers, similar magnitude in every subgroup.
  • feature_013, feature_028, feature_018 and feature_033 jointly define
    treatment response: only patients with feature_013=1 AND feature_028=0
    AND feature_018=0 AND feature_033 ≤ 15 derive a meaningful effect from
    feature_029 (~+4.6 months PFS).
  • feature_023 acts as a small adjunct (~+0.3 months) on top of feature_029
    within the same subgroup; it has no benefit elsewhere.
  • Mild prognostic effects of feature_004 (negative) and feature_024
    (positive) are real but modest.

Refuted (or null):
  • feature_011, feature_030, feature_019 — no association with PFS.
  • feature_009, feature_026, feature_032, feature_022, feature_008,
    feature_020, feature_015 — no association.
  • Lab-like continuous features (feature_002, feature_007, feature_010,
    feature_014, feature_017, feature_021, feature_025, feature_027,
    feature_034, feature_035, feature_036, feature_037) — no clinically
    meaningful effect on PFS.
  • feature_016 — apparent univariate effect dissolves after adjustment.
  • Hypothesis that feature_023 is an independent treatment is refuted; it
    operates as an adjunct conditional on feature_029.
  • Hypothesis that any feature acts as a treatment outside the maximal
    responsive subgroup is refuted.

10. Final treatment-effect subgroup hypothesis
----------------------------------------------
For pfs_months, the best-supported treatment-effect subgroup is:

  Treatment:        feature_029 (with adjunct feature_023)
  Outcome:          pfs_months (longer is better)
  Direction:        positive
  Subgroup (all four required):
                    feature_013 = 1
                    feature_028 = 0
                    feature_018 = 0
                    feature_033 ≤ 15

  Effect size:      +4.62 months PFS in this subgroup (p ≈ 0).
                    Adjunct feature_023 adds approximately +0.3 months
                    on top of feature_029 within the same subgroup.
                    Outside the subgroup the effect is essentially zero.

  Interpretation:   feature_013 is a positive predictive biomarker; feature_028
                    and feature_018 are independent resistance-like markers
                    each capable of nullifying response; feature_033 above
                    a threshold near 15 likewise eliminates benefit. All four
                    conditions are necessary for treatment efficacy.
