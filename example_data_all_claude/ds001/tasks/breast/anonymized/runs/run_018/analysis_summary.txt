Analysis summary — ds001_breast (n=50,000)
============================================

Outcome
-------
Single outcome: pfs_months (progression-free survival in months), continuous,
mean 4.69 (SD 2.50), range 0.00-16.66, median 4.54. About 2.4% of patients
have pfs_months=0 and one patient is at the maximum, so the distribution is
roughly symmetric and supports linear modelling.

Univariate associations
-----------------------
Among the 18 binary features, the largest signed mean differences in
pfs_months between =1 and =0 (all from Welch t-tests) were:

  feature_006  -1.54 mo  (p ~ 0)            shorter PFS when =1
  feature_013  +1.10 mo  (p ~ 0)            longer  PFS when =1
  feature_001  -1.05 mo  (p = 1.5e-178)     shorter
  feature_028  -0.56 mo  (p = 6e-144)       shorter
  feature_029  +0.57 mo  (p = 4e-143)       longer
  feature_018  -0.46 mo  (p = 6e-70)        shorter
  feature_023  +0.36 mo  (p = 6e-59)        longer
  feature_016  +0.11 mo  (p = 1e-6)         longer (small)

The remaining binary features (feature_011, feature_030, feature_019,
feature_009, feature_026, feature_032, feature_008, feature_022, feature_020,
feature_015) had |raw diff| < 0.08 month with p >= 0.02 and no convincing
main effect.

Among continuous features, feature_012 (range 30-90, mean 65, plausibly age)
had the dominant correlation with PFS, Spearman rho = +0.726 (p ~ 0); mean
PFS rose monotonically across feature_012 deciles from 0.6 mo (30-40) to
8.2 mo (80+). feature_005 was an ordinal performance-status-like variable
(0,1,2) with means 5.64 / 4.44 / 3.29 mo — clean monotonic dose-response.
Other moderate continuous correlates: feature_004 rho=-0.116, feature_024
rho=+0.102, feature_033 rho=-0.083 (all p<1e-77). The remaining continuous
features had |rho|<0.02.

Multivariable model
-------------------
A multivariable OLS of pfs_months on all 37 features achieved R^2 = 0.792
(adjusted 0.792). Independent predictors (largest |t|):

  feature_012  beta = +0.176/unit  (t = +343)
  feature_005  beta = -1.17/level  (t = -156)
  feature_006  beta = -1.55        (t = -139)
  feature_013  beta = +1.10        (t = +103)
  feature_001  beta = -0.98        (t =  -58)
  feature_004  beta = -0.077/unit  (t =  -58)
  feature_028  beta = -0.58        (t =  -54)
  feature_024  beta = +0.48/unit   (t =  +47)
  feature_033  beta = -0.019/unit  (t =  -45)
  feature_029  beta = +0.53        (t =  +36)
  feature_018  beta = -0.47        (t =  -33)
  feature_031  beta = -0.0005/unit (t =   -8)

feature_011 (p=0.21), feature_030 (p=0.08), feature_019 (p=0.42), feature_023
(p=0.32), feature_016 (p=0.86), feature_009 (p=0.04), feature_032 (p=0.05),
feature_008 (p=0.12), feature_022 (p=0.47), feature_020 (p=0.17), feature_015
(p=0.48) and most other previously-borderline features did NOT retain
independent main effects in the full model. feature_023's univariate effect
was almost entirely captured by feature_029, with which it correlates
strongly (r = +0.669).

Treatment identification
------------------------
feature_013 stood out as the only candidate "treatment" variable: balanced
allocation (35% =1), large positive PFS effect that survived adjustment, and
(as shown below) heterogeneous benefit defined by other variables — the
fingerprint of a treatment as opposed to a prognostic marker. feature_006
and feature_001 had even larger main effects but uniformly negative direction
and no interaction structure — they behave as prognostic / disease-aggressiveness
factors. feature_011 and feature_030, with prevalence near 50% and adjusted
p > 0.05, look like null indicators (e.g. randomized but ineffective allocations
or chance variables).

Treatment-effect heterogeneity for feature_013
----------------------------------------------
A treatment-by-binary-modifier interaction screen (covariate-adjusted OLS
with feature_012, feature_005, feature_006, feature_001, feature_004,
feature_024, feature_033, feature_018, feature_029, feature_028 as
covariates and a treatment x modifier interaction) revealed four very
strong interactions and almost nothing else:

  feature_013 x feature_029  inter beta = +1.56   (p ~ 0)
                             main feature_013 collapses from +1.10 to +0.012
                             when feature_029=0
  feature_013 x feature_023  inter beta = +1.03   (p ~ 0)
                             feature_013 main +0.44 when feature_023=0
  feature_013 x feature_018  inter beta = -1.34   (p ~ 0)
                             feature_013 main +1.35 when feature_018=0,
                             ~0 when feature_018=1
  feature_013 x feature_028  inter beta = -1.68   (p ~ 0)
                             feature_013 main +1.68 when feature_028=0,
                             ~0 when feature_028=1

This pattern — feature_029 and feature_023 acting as enabler biomarkers,
feature_018 and feature_028 acting as suppressors — is characteristic of a
biomarker-defined responder subgroup.

A treatment-by-continuous-modifier scan (same adjustment) found one very
clean continuous modifier:

  feature_013 x feature_033  inter beta = -0.0532/unit  (p ~ 0)

Each unit increase in feature_033 reduces feature_013's PFS benefit by
0.053 months. Decile stratification within (feature_029=1 & feature_018=0 &
feature_028=0) showed a sharp threshold: the feature_013 benefit was around
+5 months for feature_033 deciles 1-5 (range 1.0-12.2), still +3.97 in
decile 6 (12.3-14.6), then collapsed to +0.17 in decile 7 (14.7-17.6) and
hovered between -0.23 and +0.16 in deciles 8-10. Cutoff scan supports
feature_033 <= 14 as the operative threshold (in-subgroup diff +4.94 vs
out-subgroup diff +0.02).

Final responder subgroup
------------------------
The most parsimonious responder subgroup S is:

  feature_029 = 1   AND   feature_018 = 0   AND
  feature_028 = 0   AND   feature_033 <= 14

Subgroup S contains 10,828 patients (21.7% of the cohort).

  Inside S, on feature_013   : mean PFS 9.23 mo (n=3,853)
  Inside S, off feature_013  : mean PFS 4.29 mo (n=6,975)
  Difference inside S        : +4.94 mo (raw); +4.68 mo covariate-adjusted

  Outside S, on feature_013  : mean PFS 4.32 mo (n=13,575)
  Outside S, off feature_013 : mean PFS 4.32 mo (n=25,597)
  Difference outside S       : +0.007 mo (p = 0.88, indistinguishable from 0)

A covariate-adjusted global model with feature_013, S, and their interaction
gave: feature_013 main beta = -6e-5 (p = 0.99), S main beta = -0.011
(p = 0.04), feature_013 x S interaction beta = +4.68 (p ~ 0); model R^2 =
0.976. The interaction term alone contributes essentially all the treatment
effect.

Ablations of S confirm each predicate is necessary. Starting from the
S2 = S + feature_023=1 reference (in-S2 diff +4.71):

  drop feature_029 = 1     in-subgroup diff +4.39 (smallest reduction since
                                                   captured partly by f023)
  drop feature_023 = 1     in-subgroup diff +4.68 (no change; redundant
                                                   given feature_029)
  drop feature_018 = 0     in-subgroup diff +3.89
  drop feature_028 = 0     in-subgroup diff +3.07
  drop feature_033 <= 14   in-subgroup diff +2.95

feature_023 is dropped from the final definition because of its high
correlation with feature_029 (r=+0.67) and the negligible benefit it adds
once feature_029 is in the rule.

Within S, performance status (feature_005) does NOT modify the feature_013
benefit: PS=0 diff +4.97, PS=1 +4.93, PS=2 +5.00.

Other treatment candidates and null indicators
----------------------------------------------
Heterogeneity scans for feature_011, feature_030, feature_019, feature_006
and feature_001 found no interactions of comparable magnitude. The largest
interaction for feature_011 was with feature_013 (inter +0.046, p=0.03); for
feature_030 with feature_016 (+0.036, p=0.09); for feature_006 with
feature_015 (-0.046, p=0.06). None approaches the >+1.5 month interactions
seen for feature_013. This rules out a "second hidden treatment" with
clinically meaningful heterogeneity in this dataset.

Summary of supported vs refuted hypotheses
------------------------------------------
Supported:

  - PFS is well-modelled as a continuous outcome with linear regression.
  - feature_012 is the dominant predictor of PFS (positive, monotonic).
  - feature_005 acts as an ordinal performance-status-like variable.
  - feature_006, feature_001 are negative prognostic factors.
  - feature_013 is the active treatment in the cohort.
  - feature_029 and feature_023 are positive biomarkers (enablers); they
    are mutually correlated (r=+0.67) and feature_029 alone is sufficient
    in the responder rule.
  - feature_018 and feature_028 are resistance markers; their presence
    abolishes the feature_013 benefit.
  - feature_033 modifies the feature_013 benefit with a sharp cutoff
    around 14: low values preserve full benefit, high values eliminate it.
  - Subgroup S = {feature_029=1 & feature_018=0 & feature_028=0 &
    feature_033<=14} captures essentially the entire treatment effect:
    +4.94 mo raw (+4.68 mo adjusted) inside, ~0 outside.
  - Performance status does not modify the in-S feature_013 effect.

Refuted:

  - feature_011 is not a treatment (no main effect, no heterogeneity).
  - feature_030 is not a treatment (no main effect, no heterogeneity).
  - feature_023 is not an independent predicate of the responder subgroup
    (redundant with feature_029).
  - feature_006 is not a treatment whose benefit depends on biomarker
    status (uniformly negative across modifiers).

Final conclusion
----------------
The dataset contains exactly one active treatment for pfs_months
(feature_013) and one clinically meaningful responder subgroup defined by
four biomarker predicates. The treatment confers no PFS benefit in the
~78% of patients who fail any of those predicates, and a roughly +5 month
PFS gain in the ~22% who satisfy all four. The remaining structure of the
dataset is well captured by feature_012 (dominant prognostic axis),
feature_005 (performance status), and the prognostic factors feature_006,
feature_001, feature_028, feature_004, feature_018 and feature_033, all of
which act as additive negative predictors with no further treatment
heterogeneity beyond what is already encoded in the responder subgroup
definition.

Final responder hypothesis (locked):
"In patients with feature_029=1 AND feature_018=0 AND feature_028=0 AND
feature_033<=14, treatment with feature_013 increases pfs_months by
approximately +4.7 to +4.9 months relative to no feature_013; in patients
who fail any of these four predicates, feature_013 has no effect on
pfs_months (point estimate +0.007 mo, p=0.88)."
