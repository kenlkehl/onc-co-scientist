Analysis Summary — ds001_breast (50,000 patients, outcome: pfs_months)
=====================================================================

Setup
-----
The dataset has 37 de-identified features (18 binary 0/1, one ordinal 0/1/2,
18 continuous) and a single continuous outcome `pfs_months` (mean 4.69, sd 2.50,
median 4.54, range 0–16.66). All features are largely independent (only one
notable pairwise correlation among binaries: feature_023 ↔ feature_029 r=0.67),
so the cohort behaves like a randomized trial-style population suitable for
direct subgroup and interaction analyses.

Iteration 1–2: Univariate prognostic screen
-------------------------------------------
A continuous feature (feature_012, range 30–90) was very strongly positively
associated with pfs_months (Pearson r = +0.70, Spearman = +0.73, both p ≪
1e-300). The shape and range strongly suggest age in years, with each year of
feature_012 adding roughly 0.13 months of PFS in untreated patients.

The ordinal feature_005 (levels 0/1/2) showed a clean negative dose-response:
mean PFS 5.64 → 4.44 → 3.29 (ANOVA F = 2861, p ≪ 1e-300). Multivariable slope
−1.17 mo per level. This looks like a stage- or grade-like prognostic
classifier.

Among the binary features, the largest negative main effects were feature_006
(−1.54 mo, prevalence 30%, p ≪ 1e-300), feature_001 (−1.05 mo, prevalence 10%,
p = 1.5e-178), feature_028 (−0.56 mo) and feature_018 (−0.46 mo). The largest
positive binary main effect was feature_013 (+1.10 mo, prevalence 35%, p ≪
1e-300), which was the most plausible candidate "treatment". feature_029
(+0.57 mo, prevalence 70%) and feature_023 (+0.36 mo, prevalence 64%) also had
positive main effects.

Iteration 3–5: Continuous feature screen and multivariable model
----------------------------------------------------------------
Among the 18 continuous features, only feature_012 (r = +0.70), feature_004
(r = −0.12), feature_024 (r = +0.10) and feature_033 (r = −0.09) showed
meaningful overall correlations with pfs_months; the other 14 continuous
features were essentially uncorrelated (|r| < 0.02). A 37-feature
multivariable OLS model gave R² = 0.792, with the largest standardized
coefficients matching the univariate ranking above. Importantly, the
univariate signals from feature_023 (+0.36 mo) and feature_016 (+0.11 mo)
disappeared after adjustment (multivariable p > 0.3), indicating those
associations were driven by collinearity (feature_023 ↔ feature_029 r = 0.67)
and/or treatment-effect modification rather than independent prognosis.

Iteration 6–7: Treatment-effect heterogeneity screen for feature_013
--------------------------------------------------------------------
A systematic interaction screen of feature_013 against every other feature
revealed five highly significant modifiers:

  feature_013 × feature_028  coef = −1.62  p = 4.0e-255
  feature_013 × feature_029  coef = +1.59  p = 9.3e-229
  feature_013 × feature_033  coef = −0.64  p = 1.5e-174  (continuous, z-scored)
  feature_013 × feature_018  coef = −1.36  p = 2.2e-118
  feature_013 × feature_023  coef = +1.09  p = 2.6e-117

Stratified analyses made the structure clear:

  feature_029 = 0 (n = 15,114): feature_013 effect = −0.02 mo (NS)
  feature_029 = 1 (n = 34,886): feature_013 effect = +1.58 mo (p ≪ 0.001)

  feature_028 = 0 (n = 32,635): feature_013 effect = +1.66 mo (p ≪ 0.001)
  feature_028 = 1 (n = 17,365): feature_013 effect = +0.04 mo (NS)

  feature_018 = 0 (n = 40,940): feature_013 effect = +1.35 mo (p ≪ 0.001)
  feature_018 = 1 (n =  9,060): feature_013 effect = −0.01 mo (NS)

That is, feature_029 is required for feature_013 efficacy, while feature_028 = 1
or feature_018 = 1 each independently abolish the benefit. Crucially, in the
control arm (feature_013 = 0), feature_028, feature_018, feature_029 and
feature_023 all have essentially zero prognostic main effect (all |Δ| < 0.03 mo,
p > 0.4). They are pure treatment-effect modifiers — their apparent main
effects in pooled analyses come entirely from acting as predictive
biomarkers for feature_013.

Iteration 8: feature_033 as a continuous suppressor of treatment effect
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Within the favorable subgroup (feature_029=1 & feature_028=0 & feature_018=0,
n = 18,681), feature_013 effect by feature_033 bin was:

  feature_033 ≤ 5     :  +4.91 mo
  5 < feature_033 ≤ 8 :  +4.98 mo
  8 < feature_033 ≤ 10:  +4.99 mo
  10 < feature_033 ≤12:  +4.92 mo
  12 < feature_033 ≤15:  +3.50 mo
  15 < feature_033 ≤18:  +0.26 mo
  18 < feature_033 ≤20:  −0.20 mo
  feature_033 > 20    :  ≈  0    mo

Treatment effect is large and stable below feature_033 ≈ 12, drops sharply
between 12 and 18, and is null above 18. feature_033 also had no main effect
in the control arm (Pearson r = −0.002, p = 0.68), confirming it acts as a
pure predictive (not prognostic) biomarker.

Iteration 9: Composite subgroup definition
------------------------------------------
Combining all four modifiers gives a clean composite subgroup:

  Favorable subgroup S = {feature_029 = 1
                          & feature_028 = 0
                          & feature_018 = 0
                          & feature_033 ≤ 12}

  Inside S (n = 9,206 = 18.4% of cohort):
    feature_013 = 0 mean PFS = 4.28 (sd 2.12, n = 5,963)
    feature_013 = 1 mean PFS = 9.24 (sd 2.23, n = 3,243)
    Δ = +4.95 months  (p ≪ 1e-300)

  Outside S (n = 40,794):
    feature_013 = 0 mean PFS = 4.32
    feature_013 = 1 mean PFS = 4.53
    Δ = +0.22 months  (p = 2.9e-20; small residual from feature_033 12–18)

A cutoff search confirmed the threshold: cutoff feature_033 ≤ 10/12/14 each
give Δ ≈ +4.95 mo, while cutoff ≤ 18 attenuates to +4.09 mo and ≤ 20 to
+3.83 mo.

Iteration 10: Pure prognostic features do NOT modify treatment effect
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Within the favorable subgroup S, the +5-month feature_013 benefit was uniform
across feature_005 levels (0 → +5.01, 1 → +4.92, 2 → +5.00) and across
feature_012 age tertiles (young → +4.87, mid → +4.94, old → +5.11), even
though both features strongly modulate baseline PFS. Stratified tests also
showed the feature_013 effect is unchanged by feature_006 (control vs treated
difference +1.10 mo regardless of feature_006 status) and by feature_001
(+1.10 vs +1.06 mo). So the cohort cleanly separates into:

  Pure prognostic features (main effect only):
    feature_012 (~age, +1.75 mo per SD)
    feature_005 (ordinal, −1.17 mo per level)
    feature_006 (binary, −1.54 mo)
    feature_001 (binary, −0.98 mo)
    feature_004 (continuous, −0.30 mo per SD)
    feature_024 (continuous, +0.24 mo per SD)
    minor: feature_031, feature_009 (small effects)

  Pure predictive (treatment-effect-modifying) features for feature_013:
    feature_029  (necessary: only =1 patients benefit)
    feature_028  (suppressor: =1 patients lose benefit)
    feature_018  (suppressor: =1 patients lose benefit)
    feature_033  (continuous suppressor: benefit lost above ~12–15)
    feature_023  (smaller enhancer; mostly captured by feature_029)

  Almost-null features:
    feature_011, feature_030, feature_019, feature_016, feature_022,
    feature_008, feature_032, feature_020, feature_026, feature_015,
    feature_036, feature_021, feature_003, feature_025, feature_027,
    feature_034, feature_014, feature_037, feature_017, feature_035,
    feature_002, feature_007, feature_010

Final hypothesis (best-supported treatment-effect subgroup)
-----------------------------------------------------------
For the outcome pfs_months in the ds001_breast cohort:

  Treatment:  feature_013
  Direction:  positive (longer PFS)
  Subgroup:   feature_029 = 1
              AND feature_028 = 0
              AND feature_018 = 0
              AND feature_033 ≤ ~12

  Inside the subgroup (n ≈ 9,200, 18% of cohort): Δ pfs_months ≈ +5.0 months
                                                  (95% concentrated benefit)
  Outside the subgroup (n ≈ 40,800): Δ pfs_months ≈ +0.2 months
                                     (essentially null)

Interpretation
--------------
This pattern — a treatment whose benefit requires one biomarker to be
positive (feature_029) and is independently abolished by two binary
"suppressor" markers (feature_028, feature_018) and by a continuous
biomarker above a cutoff (feature_033) — is consistent with a targeted
therapy in metastatic breast cancer that requires a positive target,
is rendered ineffective by acquired-resistance mutations or by visceral-
disease/poor-PS markers, and loses efficacy as a quantitative tumor-burden
biomarker rises. The cohort also contains strong but independent
prognostic features (a likely age variable feature_012, a stage-like
ordinal feature_005, and two binary unfavorable biomarkers feature_006
and feature_001) that influence baseline PFS but neither qualify nor
disqualify patients from treatment benefit.

What the analyses supported vs. refuted
---------------------------------------
Supported
  • feature_012, feature_005, feature_006, feature_001 are prognostic for PFS
    (h1, h2, h3, h4 all confirmed with effect sizes in the expected direction).
  • feature_013 is a treatment-like factor with a large overall positive PFS
    effect (h5 confirmed).
  • feature_013 effect is heterogeneous and confined to a definable subgroup
    (h13–h15 confirmed; h19 and h23 are the final composite statement).
  • feature_029, feature_028, feature_018, feature_033 are pure predictive
    biomarkers with no prognostic main effect (h16, h17, h18 confirmed).
  • Pure prognostic features do not modify the treatment effect (h20, h22
    confirmed).

Refuted / refined
  • feature_023 (h6-style positive main effect) and feature_016 (small
    univariate effect) do NOT have independent prognostic effects after
    multivariable adjustment (h12); feature_023's signal is mediated by its
    correlation with feature_029 plus a residual treatment-effect-modifier
    role.
  • feature_029, feature_028, and feature_018 are NOT prognostic on their own;
    their pooled main effects are entirely artefacts of treatment-effect
    modification (h16 — initially treated as prognostic, refined to predictive).
