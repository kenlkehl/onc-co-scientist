ANALYSIS SUMMARY — ds001_breast (n=50,000; outcome = pfs_months)
=================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The dataset contains 37 anonymized features and one outcome (pfs_months,
range 0-16.66 months). Among the features, 18 are binary, 18 are continuous,
and one (feature_005) is a 3-level ordinal. We probed the data over 15
analysis iterations: main-effect screens, multivariable adjustment,
identification of the treatment indicator, treatment-by-feature
interaction screening, and stepwise refinement of a treatment-effect
subgroup, including a final irreducibility check.


KEY MAIN EFFECTS (UNIVARIATE)
-----------------------------
Strongest predictors of pfs_months across the full cohort:

  * feature_012 (continuous, range 30-90): +0.176 mo per unit, r=+0.70,
    p~0. By far the strongest single predictor. PFS rises monotonically
    from 0.56 mo (feature_012 in 30-40) to 8.20 mo (feature_012 in
    80-90).
  * feature_005 (ordinal 0/1/2): -1.18 mo per level, ANOVA p~0.
    Means: level 0 = 5.64; level 1 = 4.44; level 2 = 3.29.
  * feature_006 (binary, 30% prev): -1.54 mo (p~0)
  * feature_001 (binary, 10% prev): -1.05 mo (p=2e-179)
  * feature_013 (binary, 35% prev): +1.10 mo (p~0) -- strongest positive
    binary effect; flagged as candidate treatment.
  * feature_028 (binary, 35% prev): -0.56 mo (p=2e-126)
  * feature_018 (binary, 18% prev): -0.46 mo (p=1e-56)
  * feature_029 (binary, 70% prev): +0.57 mo (p=2e-121)
  * feature_023 (binary, 64% prev): +0.36 mo (p=8e-55)
  * feature_004 (continuous): -0.075 mo per unit (p=4e-148)
  * feature_033 (continuous, 1-100): -0.019 mo per unit (p=3e-93)
  * feature_024 (continuous): +0.499 mo per unit (p=9e-112)

Most of the remaining 14 continuous features showed |r| < 0.02 with
pfs_months and were essentially uninformative as univariate predictors
(feature_036, 031, 021, 003, 025, 027, 034, 014, 037, 017, 035, 002,
007, 010).


MULTIVARIABLE STRUCTURE
-----------------------
A full OLS of pfs_months on all 37 features produced R^2 = 0.79.
The robust independent predictors (|t| > 8) were: feature_012,
feature_005, feature_006, feature_013, feature_001, feature_028,
feature_024, feature_004, feature_029, feature_018, feature_033, and
feature_031. Two univariate signals collapsed after adjustment:

  * feature_023's positive effect (+0.362 univariate) shrank to
    +0.014 (p=0.32) once feature_029 was in the model -- it is
    largely a proxy for feature_029 (Pearson r=+0.67 between them).
  * feature_016's positive effect (+0.114 univariate) shrank to
    +0.002 (p=0.86) once feature_018 was in the model (r=-0.36).


TREATMENT IDENTIFICATION
------------------------
A logistic regression of feature_013 on all 37 other features yielded
McFadden pseudo-R^2 = 0.00055 with no individual covariate at p<0.05.
feature_013 (prevalence 34.86%) is essentially independent of every
measured feature -- a fingerprint of randomized assignment. This
distinguished feature_013 from feature_006/001/029/etc., which all
correlated with multiple other features. We therefore designated
feature_013 as the treatment indicator and conducted treatment-effect
analyses centered on it.


TREATMENT-BY-FEATURE INTERACTION SCREEN
---------------------------------------
A systematic interaction screen (treatment * feature, adjusting for
strong prognostic covariates) yielded four highly significant
modifiers:

  * feature_029 (positive interaction +1.56): treatment effect
    +1.58 mo when feature_029=1 vs -0.02 mo when feature_029=0.
  * feature_028 (negative interaction -1.67): treatment effect
    +1.66 mo when feature_028=0 vs +0.04 mo when feature_028=1.
  * feature_018 (negative interaction -1.34): treatment effect
    +1.35 mo when feature_018=0 vs -0.01 mo when feature_018=1.
  * feature_033 (negative interaction -0.65 per SD, continuous):
    treatment effect by quartile -- Q1 +1.84, Q2 +1.92, Q3 +0.65,
    Q4 -0.02 mo. Effect drops sharply with rising feature_033.

A weaker positive interaction with feature_023 (+1.03) was found, but
feature_023 is highly correlated with feature_029 and adds little once
feature_029 is in the subgroup. feature_012 -- the single strongest
prognostic factor -- did NOT meaningfully modify the treatment effect.


STEPWISE SUBGROUP REFINEMENT
----------------------------
Stratifying on combinations of the modifiers progressively concentrated
the treatment effect:

  feature_029=1 & feature_028=0:
     n=22788, treatment effect = +2.376 mo (p~0)

  feature_029=1 & feature_028=0 & feature_018=0:
     n=18681, treatment effect = +2.908 mo (p~0)

  feature_029=1 & feature_028=0 & feature_018=0 & feature_033<14:
     n=10752 (21.5%), treatment effect = +4.944 mo (p~0), Cohen d=2.28

The threshold on feature_033 was localized via a narrow-bin scan within
the triple subgroup: treatment-arm pfs is ~9.2-9.4 mo for feature_033
in (1, 14], drops sharply to ~4.3 in (14, 15], and is flat ~4.2-4.6
for (15, 30]. Control-arm pfs is flat (~4.2-4.4) across all bins. The
relationship is best modeled as a step function with a cutpoint at
feature_033 ≈ 14 rather than a smooth gradient.


COMPLEMENT AND IRREDUCIBILITY
-----------------------------
Outside the four-way responder subgroup (n=39,248, 78.5% of cohort)
the treatment effect is null: T=1 mean=4.316, T=0 mean=4.320,
diff = -0.004 mo (p=0.49).

Each of the four conditions is individually necessary. Single-flip
tests (keeping the other three favorable):
  * feature_029=0 (others favorable): diff -0.017 mo (p=0.80)
  * feature_028=1 (others favorable): diff -0.065 mo (p=0.27)
  * feature_018=1 (others favorable): diff -0.017 mo (p=0.86)
  * feature_033>=14 (others favorable): diff +0.067 mo (p=0.18)

In every case the treatment effect collapses to zero. The subgroup
definition therefore cannot be reduced to a smaller set of conditions.

Within the responder subgroup, no further binary or continuous feature
substantially modifies the treatment effect (largest within-subgroup
modifier interaction is feature_006 at -0.043 mo per unit, and
feature_012 at +0.05 mo per SD -- both negligible against the +4.94 mo
main subgroup effect).


PROGNOSTIC vs. PREDICTIVE BIOMARKER PATTERN
-------------------------------------------
A clean split emerged when we re-ran multivariable models stratified by
treatment arm:

  Pure prognostic (similar coefficient in T=0 and T=1 arms; affect
  pfs_months independently of treatment):
     feature_012 (+0.176), feature_006 (-1.55), feature_005 (-1.17),
     feature_001 (-0.97), feature_024 (+0.49), feature_004 (-0.075).

  Pure predictive (negligible effect in control arm; large effect only
  in treated arm; gate the treatment benefit):
     feature_029 (+1.50 in treated; ~0 in control)
     feature_028 (-1.65 in treated; ~0 in control)
     feature_018 (-1.33 in treated; ~0 in control)
     feature_033 (-0.053 per unit in treated; ~0 in control)

This gives the finding mechanistic plausibility: the four modifier
variables don't change disease prognosis on their own; they only matter
because they govern whether the patient can benefit from feature_013.


CONCLUSIONS
-----------
1. feature_013 is the treatment indicator (independent of all
   baseline features, prevalence ~35%) and provides a large pfs_months
   benefit on average (+1.10 mo crude, +1.10 mo adjusted) -- but the
   benefit is highly heterogeneous.

2. The full responder subgroup is:
       feature_029=1 AND feature_028=0 AND feature_018=0 AND feature_033<14
   In this 21.5% of patients the treatment effect is +4.94 mo
   (Cohen's d 2.3, p~0). In the complementary 78.5% the treatment
   effect is essentially zero.

3. Each of the four conditions in the subgroup definition is
   individually necessary -- having any one unfavorable value
   abolishes the benefit.

4. The strongest baseline prognostic factors -- feature_012,
   feature_005, feature_006, feature_001, feature_024, feature_004 --
   determine baseline pfs but do NOT modify the treatment effect.

5. feature_023 mimics feature_029 statistically (r=+0.67) and
   feature_016 mimics 1-feature_018 (r=-0.36); neither adds
   independent information once feature_029 and feature_018 are
   conditioned on.

6. The remaining 14 continuous features (feature_036, 031, 021, 003,
   025, 027, 034, 014, 037, 017, 035, 002, 007, 010) are essentially
   noise with respect to pfs_months and the treatment effect.
