CRISPR dependency map analysis for ds001_depmap

I analyzed dataset.parquet using the local task-bundle files only. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger dependency. I used Welch-style two-group comparisons for binary/categorical predicates, linear threshold screens for continuous features, and a systematic predicate-by-predicate heterogeneity screen across all dependency outcomes.

Initial main-effect screen

The strongest main-effect dependencies were clear. dependency_BRAF was much more negative in feature_009 == 1 cell lines (mean -0.8047 vs -0.2495; effect -0.5552; p < 1e-300). dependency_EGFR was more negative in feature_022 == 1 cell lines (mean -0.6010 vs -0.2524; effect -0.3486; p < 1e-300). dependency_ERBB2 was more negative in feature_024 == 1 cell lines (mean -0.6856 vs -0.2535; effect -0.4321; p < 1e-300). dependency_KRAS was more negative in feature_014 == 1 cell lines (mean -0.5594 vs -0.2520; effect -0.3075; p < 1e-300). dependency_RIT1 was more negative in feature_027 == 1 cell lines (mean -0.3527 vs -0.2572; effect -0.0955; p < 1e-300).

Lineage and subtype refinements

Several lineage annotations had weaker but significant associations. BRAF dependency was stronger in skin lineage (feature_011 == skin; effect -0.1783). EGFR dependency was stronger in brain lineage (effect -0.0552). ERBB2 dependency was modestly stronger in breast lineage (effect -0.0292; p = 6.37e-9) and luminal subtype (effect -0.0300; p = 5.29e-6). KRAS dependency was stronger in pancreatic lineage (effect -0.1127) and colorectal lineage (effect -0.0500). These lineage effects were smaller than the dominant binary-feature signals.

KIF18A heterogeneity

dependency_KIF18A showed the main hidden interaction pattern. Individually, colorectal lineage (feature_011 == colorectal) had effect -0.4680, feature_035 == 1 had effect -0.2921, and high feature_028 (feature_028 >= 0.819) had effect -0.2168. The complete subgroup was their intersection: feature_011 == colorectal AND feature_035 == 1 AND feature_028 >= 0.819. This subgroup had n = 3133 and mean dependency_KIF18A -1.1119 vs -0.2530 for all other cell lines, effect -0.8589 (p < 1e-300). Pairwise interaction contrasts were also strongly negative: colorectal by feature_035 == 1 contrast -0.6822, and colorectal by feature_028 >= 0.819 contrast -0.6691.

The suppressor checks supported the complete definition. Colorectal cells without feature_035 were not dependency-enriched (mean -0.2592; effect +0.0495 relative to the rest), and non-colorectal cells with feature_035 == 1 were also not dependency-enriched (mean -0.2534; effect +0.0599). Very high feature_028 (>= 1.260) further amplified the complete subgroup (mean -1.1947; effect -0.9328), but the threshold where the interaction became clear was feature_028 >= 0.819.

Core dependency outcomes

feature_016 was a recurring weak-to-moderate marker for several core dependency outcomes. High feature_016 (>= 0.964) was associated with more negative dependency_POLR2A (effect -0.1043), dependency_TMED10 (effect -0.0369), dependency_DHX9 (effect -0.0371), and dependency_RPL11 (effect -0.0418). RPL11 also had stronger specific markers: feature_003 >= 1.003 had effect -0.1257, feature_018 == 1 had effect -0.1091, and feature_016 >= 1.088 plus feature_003 >= 1.003 had effect -0.1515.

Systematic heterogeneity screen

For every dependency outcome, I screened two-predicate subgroup rules and pairwise interaction contrasts. For BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, KRAS, and RIT1, the strongest two-predicate rules mostly retained the primary single-feature marker and did not reveal a new complete interaction comparable to KIF18A. BRAF's top rule, feature_009 == 1 AND feature_030 == 0, had effect -0.5514, essentially identical to the feature_009 main effect. EGFR's strongest rule, feature_016 >= 1.088 AND feature_022 == 1, had effect -0.3709, only modestly larger than the feature_022 main effect. ERBB2's feature_022 == 0 AND feature_024 == 1 rule had effect -0.4286, matching the feature_024 main effect. KRAS's feature_010 == her2_enriched AND feature_014 == 1 rule had effect -0.3099, comparable to feature_014 alone. RIT1 showed a modest refinement in feature_011 == brain AND feature_027 == 1 (effect -0.1397).

For the core dependency outcomes, subgroup screening found modest amplifications rather than large hidden interactions. TMED10 was most negative in feature_016 >= 1.088 AND feature_026 >= 1.153 (effect -0.0893). DHX9 was most negative in feature_016 >= 1.088 AND feature_023 >= 6.330 (effect -0.0847). POLR2A was most negative in feature_016 >= 1.088 AND feature_015 <= 2.083 (effect -0.1389). RPL11 had enriched subgroups combining feature_016 with feature_003 or feature_018, but its main feature_003 and feature_018 effects remained interpretable.

Overall conclusions

The supported final dependency hypotheses are: dependency_BRAF is dominated by feature_009 == 1 with a weaker skin-lineage effect; dependency_EGFR is dominated by feature_022 == 1 with a weaker brain-lineage effect; dependency_ERBB2 is dominated by feature_024 == 1 with weaker breast/luminal effects; dependency_KRAS is dominated by feature_014 == 1 with weaker pancreatic and colorectal effects; dependency_RIT1 is dominated by feature_027 == 1 with modest brain-lineage enrichment; dependency_KIF18A is concentrated in the complete subgroup feature_011 == colorectal AND feature_035 == 1 AND feature_028 >= 0.819; dependency_TMED10, dependency_DHX9, and dependency_POLR2A track high feature_016 to varying degrees; and dependency_RPL11 tracks high feature_003, feature_018 == 1, and to a lesser extent high feature_016.
