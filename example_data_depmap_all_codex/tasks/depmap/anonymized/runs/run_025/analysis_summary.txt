CRISPR dependency map analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed 50,000 cell-line records with 36 anonymized features and 10 dependency outcomes. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger dependency. The analysis used main-effect screens, adjusted OLS models, subgroup contrasts, and a systematic two-predicate heterogeneity screen over 73 binary predicates per outcome (2452 tested pairs per outcome after minimum cell-count filtering).

Iteration 1 tested primary oncogene-like main effects. The strongest supported relationships were binary feature effects matching individual dependency outcomes: `feature_009==1` for `dependency_BRAF` (adjusted coefficient -0.5546, p approximately 0), `feature_022==1` for `dependency_EGFR` (-0.3489, p approximately 0), `feature_024==1` for `dependency_ERBB2` (-0.4326, p approximately 0), `feature_014==1` for `dependency_KRAS` (-0.3096, p approximately 0), and `feature_027==1` for `dependency_RIT1` (-0.0955, p=6.03e-69). These were supported after adjusting for lineage, subtype, culture, batch, `feature_001`, `feature_016`, and `feature_026`.

Iteration 2 refined those effects by tissue or subtype. Several biologically plausible subgroups had very negative means: `feature_009==1 & feature_011==skin` for BRAF (mean -0.8114 vs -0.2909; effect -0.5205), `feature_022==1 & feature_011==brain` for EGFR (effect -0.3377), `feature_024==1 & feature_010==her2_enriched` for ERBB2 (effect -0.4581), `feature_014==1` in pancreatic and colorectal lines for KRAS (effects -0.2488 and -0.2357), and `feature_027==1 & feature_011==brain` for RIT1 (effect -0.1397). However, stratified checks showed the primary binary effects were broad across lineages, so these subgroups were enriched contexts rather than the only responsive contexts.

Iterations 3 to 5 identified the clearest higher-order dependency pattern: `dependency_KIF18A`. Main effects suggested stronger dependency in `feature_011==colorectal` lines (effect -0.4680), `feature_035==1` lines (-0.2921), and high-`feature_028` lines (`feature_028>=0.819`, effect -0.2168). `feature_020==1` moved KIF18A scores in the opposite direction (effect +0.0667), suggesting suppression. Pairwise interaction tests confirmed strong non-additivity: colorectal by `feature_035==1` had interaction -0.6822, colorectal by high `feature_028` had interaction -0.6691, and colorectal by `feature_020==1` had a positive interaction +0.5714. The final KIF18A subgroup was `feature_011==colorectal`, `feature_035==1`, high `feature_028`, and `feature_020==0`; it had mean -1.2990 vs -0.2530 in all other lines (effect -1.0460, p approximately 0). In an adjusted model including main terms and covariates, the subgroup coefficient remained -1.0309. The otherwise enabling subgroup with `feature_020==1` was not dependent (mean -0.2557), confirming `feature_020==1` as a suppressing predicate.

Iteration 6 examined the remaining outcomes with continuous/common covariates. `dependency_TMED10` had small but significant common shifts from `feature_016` (-0.0203 per SD, p=1.79e-38) and `feature_026` (-0.0124 per SD), but the model explained little variance (R^2=0.0052). `dependency_POLR2A` was most strongly associated with higher `feature_016` (-0.0612 per SD, p approximately 0). `dependency_DHX9` was modestly more negative with higher `feature_023` (-0.0170 per SD, p=1.43e-24). `dependency_RPL11` was more negative with higher `feature_003` (-0.0559 per SD, p=7.12e-121).

Iteration 7 tested whether apparent unadjusted binary effects for `feature_018` represented independent dependencies. They did not. For DHX9, `feature_018==1` was more negative unadjusted (difference -0.0184, p=1.69e-05), but after adjustment the coefficient was +0.0029 (p=0.521). For RPL11, `feature_018==1` had a large unadjusted difference (-0.1091, p=5.71e-140), but the adjusted coefficient was +0.0092 (p=0.168), indicating that `feature_003` and other covariates explained the apparent binary signal.

Iterations 8 and 9 performed the required systematic heterogeneity screen for every dependency outcome. For BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, KRAS, and RIT1, the strongest negative pairwise interactions were small and did not survive Bonferroni correction across 2452 tested predicate pairs. For KIF18A, the screen recovered the strong colorectal/`feature_035` and colorectal/high-`feature_028` interactions. For TMED10, the top screen signal was positive for `feature_010==her2_enriched` by `feature_014==1` (+0.1187) and not corrected-significant, so it did not define a dependency subgroup. For DHX9 and RPL11, candidate negative pairwise interactions were not corrected-significant. POLR2A had one corrected-significant pairwise interaction, low `feature_026` by low `feature_023` (+0.0380, adjusted p=0.0164), but the positive direction means less negative dependency scores rather than a stronger dependency subgroup.

Overall conclusions: the dependency landscape is dominated by clean binary feature effects for BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, KRAS, and RIT1; a highly specific four-predicate KIF18A dependency subgroup; and modest continuous/common covariate associations for TMED10, DHX9, POLR2A, and RPL11. The strongest and most biologically meaningful interaction is KIF18A dependency in `feature_011==colorectal`, `feature_035==1`, high-`feature_028`, `feature_020==0` lines. Other outcomes did not show robust dependency-concentrating heterogeneity after systematic pairwise screening and multiple-testing correction.
