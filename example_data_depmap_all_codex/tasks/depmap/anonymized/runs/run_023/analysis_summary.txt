CRISPR Dependency Map Analysis Summary
Dataset: ds001_depmap; n = 50000 cell-line records. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger dependency after knockout.

Iteration 1 found strong adjusted binary-feature drivers: feature_009 for dependency_BRAF (coef -0.554, p=<1e-300), feature_022 for dependency_EGFR (-0.349, p=<1e-300), feature_024 for dependency_ERBB2 (-0.433, p=<1e-300), feature_014 for dependency_KRAS (-0.310, p=<1e-300), and feature_027 for dependency_RIT1 (-0.095, p=6.30e-69).

Iteration 2 found the strongest non-oncogene main effect for dependency_KIF18A in feature_011 == colorectal lines (adjusted coef -0.371, p=<1e-300). Higher feature_016 predicted dependency_POLR2A (-0.061, p=<1e-300), dependency_TMED10 (-0.020, p=2.23e-38), and weaker shared dependency patterns. Higher feature_003 predicted dependency_RPL11 (-0.056, p=1.34e-120), and higher feature_023 predicted dependency_DHX9 (-0.017, p=1.84e-24).

Iteration 3 showed that BRAF and KRAS driver effects were broad rather than lineage-specific: feature_009-by-skin interaction -0.013 (p=0.2883), and feature_014-by-pancreatic interaction -0.003 (p=0.7948). EGFR was modestly stronger when feature_023 >= 5.669 (interaction -0.030, p=0.0280), ERBB2 was stronger in feature_010 == her2_enriched (interaction -0.067, p=0.0372), and RIT1 was stronger in feature_011 == brain (interaction -0.058, p=0.0056).

Iteration 4 identified the clearest complete subgroup: dependency_KIF18A was concentrated in feature_011 == colorectal AND feature_035 == 1 AND feature_020 == 0 lines (mean -1.092 vs -0.253; effect -0.840, p=<1e-300). Within colorectal feature_035-positive lines, feature_020 == 1 suppressed dependency (mean -0.252 vs -1.092; positive effect 0.841, p=<1e-300). Adding feature_028 >= 1.427 defined an even stronger subgroup (mean -1.395 vs -0.268; effect -1.127, p=<1e-300), with a colorectal:feature_035:high-feature_028 interaction of -0.512 (p=1.85e-67).

Iteration 5 ran the required systematic one- and two-predicate heterogeneity screen for every dependency outcome. Top rules were:
- dependency_BRAF: feature_009 == 1; n=5574; effect -0.555; raw p=<1e-300; BH q=<1e-300.
- dependency_EGFR: feature_022 == 1 AND feature_016 >= q90 (1.09); n=393; effect -0.371; raw p=1.66e-65; BH q=1.28e-63.
- dependency_ERBB2: feature_024 == 1; n=4890; effect -0.432; raw p=<1e-300; BH q=<1e-300.
- dependency_KRAS: feature_014 == 1; n=11842; effect -0.307; raw p=<1e-300; BH q=<1e-300.
- dependency_RIT1: feature_011 == brain AND feature_027 == 1; n=329; effect -0.140; raw p=1.01e-12; BH q=7.03e-11.
- dependency_KIF18A: feature_011 == colorectal AND feature_028 >= q90 (1.43); n=2454; effect -0.797; raw p=<1e-300; BH q=<1e-300.
- dependency_TMED10: feature_026 >= q90 (1.15) AND feature_016 >= q90 (1.09); n=490; effect -0.089; raw p=2.16e-08; BH q=1.62e-06.
- dependency_DHX9: feature_011 == ovarian AND feature_016 >= q90 (1.09); n=435; effect -0.085; raw p=9.36e-07; BH q=2.92e-05.
- dependency_POLR2A: feature_016 >= q90 (1.09) AND feature_015 <= q10 (2.08); n=513; effect -0.139; raw p=1.03e-17; BH q=2.54e-16.
- dependency_RPL11: feature_022 == 1 AND feature_003 >= q90 (1); n=354; effect -0.156; raw p=1.12e-14; BH q=1.86e-13.
The BRAF, ERBB2, and KRAS screen hits mainly retained the primary binary driver plus a non-essential second predicate. The best-supported complete multifeature dependency pattern was KIF18A.

Iteration 6 refined weaker/common dependencies. TMED10 was more negative in high feature_016/high feature_026 lines; DHX9 in high feature_016/high feature_023 lines; POLR2A in high feature_016/low feature_015 lines; and RPL11 in high feature_003/feature_018 == 1 lines. These shifts were statistically significant but much smaller than the oncogene and KIF18A effects.

Iteration 7 tested residual rule effects after additive adjustment for all features. The complete KIF18A rule retained a large residual coefficient -0.921 (p=<1e-300). TMED10 and POLR2A residual rules were modest (-0.033, p=0.0413; -0.032, p=0.0471). DHX9 and RPL11 pair rules did not retain independent residual effects (-0.019, p=0.2249; 0.001, p=0.8825), indicating mostly additive component effects.

Overall conclusions: BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, KRAS, and RIT1 dependencies are dominated by specific binary features, with BRAF and KRAS broad across the tested lineages. KIF18A dependency is highly subgroup-specific and best summarized by feature_011 == colorectal, feature_035 == 1, feature_020 == 0, and feature_028 >= 1.427, with feature_020 == 1 acting as a suppressor. POLR2A, RPL11, DHX9, and TMED10 show smaller statistically significant dependencies tied to continuous-feature extremes; their multifeature rules are weaker and often additive rather than strong interactions.
