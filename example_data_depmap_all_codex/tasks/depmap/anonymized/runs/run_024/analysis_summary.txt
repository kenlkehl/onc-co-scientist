CRISPR dependency map analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed 50,000 cell-line records. More negative dependency scores were treated as stronger dependency. Categorical features were tested as one-vs-rest predicates, binary features as 1 vs 0, and continuous features both as top/bottom quartile predicates and, in the adjusted confirmation step, as standardized linear terms. P-values in the transcript come from Welch subgroup contrasts, large-sample pairwise interaction tests, or full ordinary least-squares models as noted.

Iteration 1 tested the strongest driver-like binary hypotheses. The evidence was decisive for five gene-specific signals: `feature_009` marked stronger BRAF dependency (effect -0.5552, p<1e-300), `feature_022` marked stronger EGFR dependency (effect -0.3486, p<1e-300), `feature_024` marked stronger ERBB2 dependency (effect -0.4321, p<1e-300), `feature_014` marked stronger KRAS dependency (effect -0.3075, p<1e-300), and `feature_027` marked stronger RIT1 dependency (effect -0.0955, p=1.55e-68).

Iteration 2 tested lineage and subtype context. `feature_011 == skin` was strongly associated with more negative BRAF dependency (effect -0.1783, p=2.55e-151), `feature_011 == brain` with more negative EGFR dependency (effect -0.0552, p=1.14e-16), and `feature_011 == pancreatic` with more negative KRAS dependency (effect -0.1127, p=1.33e-86). For ERBB2, `feature_010 == luminal` and `feature_010 == her2_enriched` were both negative but much smaller than the `feature_024` effect.

Iteration 3 probed broad dependencies. High `feature_016` was associated with more negative POLR2A (effect -0.1043, p=1.01e-179), TMED10 (effect -0.0369, p=2.52e-24), and DHX9 (effect -0.0371, p=1.71e-24). DHX9 was also more negative with high `feature_023` (effect -0.0286, p=2.05e-15). RPL11 had two major signals: `feature_018 == 1` (effect -0.1091, p=5.71e-140) and high `feature_003` (effect -0.0988, p=7.61e-160).

Iterations 4 and 5 focused on KIF18A, which had the clearest multivariable subgroup structure. `feature_011 == colorectal` alone gave a large negative effect (effect -0.4680, p<1e-300), as did `feature_035 == 1` (effect -0.2921, p<1e-300) and high `feature_028` (effect -0.2168, p<1e-300). `feature_020 == 1` went in the opposite direction, with less negative KIF18A scores (effect +0.0667, p=1.06e-43). Pairwise tests showed strong enabling interactions for `colorectal` with `feature_035 == 1` (interaction -0.6822, p<1e-300) and with high `feature_028` (interaction -0.6691, p<1e-300), while `feature_020 == 1` suppressed the colorectal pattern (interaction +0.5714, p=2.36e-302). The complete subgroup `feature_011 == colorectal`, `feature_035 == 1`, high `feature_028`, and `feature_020 == 0` had the strongest observed dependency_KIF18A signal (n=2571, mean -1.2990 vs -0.2530 in all others; effect -1.0460, p<1e-300).

Iteration 6 tested whether lineage/subtype predicates modified the main driver effects. BRAF dependency in `skin` plus `feature_009 == 1` remained very negative as a subgroup, but the interaction itself was not significant (interaction -0.0126, p=0.288), so the BRAF signal is mainly explained by `feature_009`. KRAS behaved similarly: the `pancreatic` plus `feature_014 == 1` subgroup was dependent, but not beyond additivity (interaction -0.0028, p=0.794). EGFR showed a modest additional interaction for `feature_022 == 1` plus high `feature_023` (interaction -0.0299, p=0.0275). ERBB2 showed modest negative interactions for `feature_024 == 1` plus high `feature_025` (interaction -0.0258, p=0.0333) and for `breast` plus `feature_024 == 1` (interaction -0.0284, p=0.0285).

Iteration 7 found additional narrower interactions. RIT1 dependency was concentrated in `feature_011 == brain` and `feature_027 == 1` (joint mean -0.4047, interaction -0.0582, p=0.00451). DHX9 had two small but significant interaction-defined subgroups: `feature_010 == squamous` plus high `feature_023` (interaction -0.0522, p=0.00254) and `feature_020 == 1` plus `feature_021 == 1` (interaction -0.0705, p=0.000690). POLR2A had a small interaction for `feature_011 == breast` plus `feature_030 == 1` (interaction -0.0570, p=0.00207). RPL11 had a significant interaction for `feature_018 == 1` plus high `feature_031` (interaction -0.0352, p=0.000315).

Iteration 8 explicitly performed the required systematic dependency-heterogeneity search for every dependency outcome using pairwise predicate interactions. This recovered the KIF18A interaction as by far the largest effect, and found smaller significant interactions for BRAF (`skin` plus `feature_018 == 1`), EGFR (`feature_022 == 1` plus high `feature_023`), ERBB2 (`feature_018 == 1` plus `feature_032 == 1`), KRAS (`lung` plus `feature_027 == 1`), RIT1 (`brain` plus `feature_027 == 1`), TMED10 (`brain` plus high `feature_036`), DHX9 (`feature_020 == 1` plus `feature_021 == 1`), POLR2A (`breast` plus `feature_030 == 1`), and RPL11 (`ovarian` plus low `feature_002`). The non-KIF18A interactions were statistically significant but much smaller than the main driver effects.

Iteration 9 confirmed selected findings in full multivariable linear models including all encoded features. The main binary driver coefficients remained strongly negative for BRAF (`feature_009`, adjusted coefficient -0.5543), EGFR (`feature_022`, -0.3492), ERBB2 (`feature_024`, -0.4327), KRAS (`feature_014`, -0.3097), and RIT1 (`feature_027`, -0.0955). KIF18A retained an adjusted negative coefficient for `feature_011_colorectal` (-0.3714) and a positive coefficient for `feature_020` (+0.0939), consistent with suppression. POLR2A retained a strong negative coefficient for `feature_016` per SD (-0.0614), and RPL11 retained a negative coefficient for `feature_003` per SD (-0.0558).

Overall conclusions: the dataset contains five clean oncogene-like dependency relationships keyed by binary features (`feature_009`/BRAF, `feature_022`/EGFR, `feature_024`/ERBB2, `feature_014`/KRAS, `feature_027`/RIT1). It also contains broad dependency effects tied to continuous features, especially `feature_016` for POLR2A/TMED10/DHX9 and `feature_003` plus `feature_018` for RPL11. The most biologically structured pattern is KIF18A: dependency is concentrated in a colorectal-like subgroup enabled by `feature_035 == 1` and high `feature_028`, and suppressed by `feature_020 == 1`. The final best-supported subgroups are recorded in iteration 10 of `transcript.json` for each dependency outcome.
