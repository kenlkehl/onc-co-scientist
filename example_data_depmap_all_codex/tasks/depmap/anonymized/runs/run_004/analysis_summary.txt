Analyzed the local `dataset.parquet` for `ds001_depmap` using only the task bundle files. More negative dependency scores were treated as stronger knockout dependency. I decoded the Snappy-compressed, dictionary-encoded parquet file locally, then screened single feature predicates and cross-feature pair subgroups for all dependency outcomes, followed by targeted multi-predicate refinement for the strongest heterogeneous signal.

Iteration 1 tested `dependency_BRAF`. `feature_009=1` was strongly associated with more negative BRAF dependency (mean -0.8047 vs -0.2495; effect -0.5552; p<1e-300). `feature_011=skin` was also more negative than other lineages (mean -0.4721 vs -0.2938; effect -0.1783; p<1e-300). The skin signal was weaker than the `feature_009` signal and appears largely secondary to feature-defined dependency.

Iteration 2 tested `dependency_EGFR`. `feature_022=1` was the dominant EGFR dependency marker (mean -0.6010 vs -0.2524; effect -0.3486; p<1e-300). `feature_011=brain` showed a smaller but significant negative shift (effect -0.0552; p=1.11e-16). A subgroup with `feature_022=1` and high `feature_016` (`>=0.964`) remained highly negative (mean -0.6253 vs -0.2727; effect -0.3526; p<1e-300).

Iteration 3 tested `dependency_ERBB2`. `feature_024=1` was strongly supported (mean -0.6856 vs -0.2535; effect -0.4321; p<1e-300). Breast lineage (`feature_011=breast`) and `feature_010=her2_enriched` had smaller negative shifts. The `feature_024=1` and `feature_010=her2_enriched` subgroup was very negative (mean -0.7525 vs -0.2944; effect -0.4581; p<1e-300), though it was small (n=142).

Iteration 4 tested `dependency_KRAS`. `feature_014=1` was the main KRAS dependency marker (mean -0.5594 vs -0.2520; effect -0.3075; p<1e-300). Pancreatic lineage was also more negative (effect -0.1127; p<1e-300), and the `feature_014=1` plus pancreatic subgroup remained more negative than the rest (effect -0.2488; p<1e-300).

Iteration 5 tested `dependency_RIT1`. `feature_027=1` was significantly more negative (mean -0.3527 vs -0.2572; effect -0.0955; p<1e-300). Pairwise searches suggested stronger RIT1 dependency in `feature_011=brain` and `feature_027=1` (effect -0.1397; p=1.22e-13) and in `feature_027=1` with low `feature_023` (`<=4.230`; effect -0.1128; p<1e-300).

Iteration 6 looked for broad dependency patterns across `dependency_POLR2A`, `dependency_TMED10`, and `dependency_DHX9`. High `feature_016` (`>=0.964`) was consistently associated with more negative scores: POLR2A effect -0.1043, TMED10 effect -0.0369, and DHX9 effect -0.0371, all significant. DHX9 was further enriched when high `feature_016` coincided with high `feature_023` (`>=5.669`; effect -0.0595; p<1e-300).

Iteration 7 tested `dependency_RPL11`. `feature_018=1` (effect -0.1091; p<1e-300) and high `feature_003` (`>=0.428`; effect -0.0988; p<1e-300) were both supported. The combined `feature_018=1` and high-`feature_003` subgroup remained negative (effect -0.1123), while `feature_018=1` plus high `feature_016` was stronger (mean -0.3936 vs -0.2632; effect -0.1303; p<1e-300).

Iterations 8 and 9 focused on `dependency_KIF18A`, the strongest heterogeneous pattern. Single-predicate screens showed large negative shifts for `feature_011=colorectal` (mean -0.7204 vs -0.2524; effect -0.4680), `feature_035=1` (effect -0.2921), and high `feature_028` (`>=0.819`; effect -0.2168). Combining all three produced a much stronger subgroup (mean -1.1119 vs -0.2530; effect -0.8589; p<1e-300). Negative-control subgroups showed the dependency was not present when either `feature_035=1` or high `feature_028` was absent. Adding `feature_020=0` defined the strongest complete subgroup: `feature_011=colorectal`, `feature_035=1`, `feature_028 >= 0.819`, and `feature_020=0` had mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.2990 versus -0.2530 in all other lines (effect -1.0460; p<1e-300).

Iteration 10 completed a systematic dependency-heterogeneity search for each outcome using 88 single feature predicates and all cross-feature predicate pairs, with KIF18A extended to targeted multi-feature combinations. The final strongest more-negative subgroup summaries were: BRAF with `feature_009=1`; EGFR with `feature_022=1` and high `feature_016`; ERBB2 with `feature_024=1` and `feature_010=her2_enriched`; KRAS with `feature_014=1`; RIT1 with `feature_011=brain` and `feature_027=1`; KIF18A with `feature_011=colorectal`, `feature_035=1`, high `feature_028`, and `feature_020=0`; TMED10 with `feature_011=prostate` and high `feature_016`; DHX9 with high `feature_016` and high `feature_023`; POLR2A with high `feature_016`; and RPL11 with `feature_018=1` and high `feature_016`.

Overall, most oncogene-like dependencies were dominated by a single binary feature (`feature_009` for BRAF, `feature_022` for EGFR, `feature_024` for ERBB2, `feature_014` for KRAS, and `feature_027` for RIT1), while common-essential-like outcomes were associated with high `feature_016` and related continuous features. The clearest multivariable subgroup discovery was for `dependency_KIF18A`, where colorectal status alone was insufficient: dependency required `feature_035=1`, high `feature_028`, and was strongest when `feature_020=0`.
