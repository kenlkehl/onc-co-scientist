I analyzed the 50000-row DepMap-style CRISPR dependency dataset using the local `dataset.parquet` file and the column descriptions in this bundle. More negative dependency scores were treated as stronger dependency after knockout. I screened single-feature effects for every dependency outcome, then ran dependency-specific subgroup and interaction searches over the strongest modifiers so the analysis did not stop at main effects.

For `dependency_BRAF`, the dominant signal was `feature_009 == 1`: mean BRAF dependency was -0.8047 in that subgroup versus -0.2495 otherwise, effect -0.5552, p effectively 0. The strongest descriptive subgroup combined `feature_009 == 1` with high `feature_016 >= 0.964` and had mean -0.8286 versus -0.2965 in all other lines. Interaction screening did not find a stronger independent modifier than the primary `feature_009` signal.

For `dependency_EGFR`, `feature_022 == 1` was the main dependency marker: mean -0.6010 versus -0.2524, effect -0.3486, p effectively 0. A refined subgroup with `feature_022 == 1`, `feature_011 == brain`, and `feature_016 >= 1.088` had mean -0.6770 versus -0.2788, effect -0.3983, p=6.7e-29, but the signal was still primarily anchored by `feature_022 == 1`.

For `dependency_ERBB2`, `feature_024 == 1` was strongly associated with more negative scores: mean -0.6856 versus -0.2535, effect -0.4321, p effectively 0. The `feature_024 == 1` and `feature_010 == her2_enriched` subgroup was still more negative, mean -0.7525 versus -0.2944, effect -0.4581, p=5.4e-53. Its interaction coefficient was -0.0671, p=0.037, supporting a modest additional HER2-enriched dependency component.

For `dependency_KRAS`, `feature_014 == 1` was the main signal: mean -0.5594 versus -0.2520, effect -0.3075, p effectively 0. The `feature_014 == 1` and `feature_016 >= 1.088` subgroup had mean -0.6092 versus -0.3177, effect -0.2915, p=1.3e-181. A separate interaction between `feature_016 >= 1.088` and `feature_020 == 1` was significant but smaller, coefficient -0.0596, p=0.00029.

For `dependency_RIT1`, `feature_027 == 1` was associated with stronger dependency: mean -0.3527 versus -0.2572, effect -0.0955, p=2.0e-70. The subgroup `feature_027 == 1` and `feature_007 <= -1.097` had mean -0.3857 versus -0.2648, effect -0.1209, p=3.6e-13. The corresponding interaction was not significant, so this appears to be a descriptive enrichment rather than an independent modifier.

The most striking heterogeneity was for `dependency_KIF18A`. `feature_011 == colorectal` alone had mean -0.7204 versus -0.2524, effect -0.4680, p effectively 0. The complete subgroup `feature_011 == colorectal`, `feature_035 == 1`, and `feature_028 >= 1.427` had mean -1.1961 versus -0.2678 in all other cell lines, effect -0.9282, p effectively 0. Two-way interaction tests supported the complete subgroup definition: colorectal x `feature_035 == 1` coefficient -0.6822, colorectal x high `feature_028` coefficient -0.6050, and high `feature_028` x `feature_035 == 1` coefficient -0.4952. This is the clearest multivariable dependency pattern in the dataset.

For `dependency_TMED10`, the main signal was smaller and tied to high `feature_016`: `feature_016 >= 1.088` had mean -0.2906 versus -0.2499, effect -0.0407, p=1.1e-14. The subgroup with both `feature_016 >= 1.088` and `feature_026 >= 1.153` had mean -0.3424 versus -0.2531, effect -0.0893, p=1.3e-08. The interaction coefficient was -0.0369, p=0.035, suggesting high `feature_026` amplifies the high-`feature_016` association.

For `dependency_DHX9`, `feature_016 >= 1.088` had mean -0.3727 versus -0.3293, effect -0.0433, p=4.3e-16. Heterogeneity search found an ovarian high-`feature_016` subgroup: `feature_016 >= 1.088` and `feature_011 == ovarian` had mean -0.4183 versus -0.3329, effect -0.0854, p=6.5e-07. The interaction coefficient was -0.0424, p=0.022.

For `dependency_POLR2A`, high `feature_016` was the major dependency marker: `feature_016 >= 1.088` had mean -0.5281 versus -0.4070, effect -0.1211, p=4.5e-119. The subgroup with `feature_016 >= 1.088` and `feature_026 >= 1.153` had mean -0.5552 versus -0.4177, effect -0.1375, p=1.1e-17. Pairwise interactions were weaker than the main feature_016 signal, so this is best interpreted as a high-`feature_016` dependency with descriptive high-`feature_026` enrichment.

For `dependency_RPL11`, `feature_003 >= 1.003` was the strongest single marker: mean -0.3831 versus -0.2573, effect -0.1257, p=2.4e-125. A three-feature subgroup, `feature_003 >= 1.003`, `feature_016 >= 0.964`, and `feature_023 >= 6.33`, had mean -0.4465 versus -0.2683, effect -0.1783, p=5.2e-27. A related `feature_003 >= 0.428` by `feature_023 >= 6.33` interaction had coefficient -0.0405, p=0.00016.

Overall, most canonical dependency outcomes had one dominant binary or thresholded marker (`feature_009` for BRAF, `feature_022` for EGFR, `feature_024` for ERBB2, `feature_014` for KRAS, `feature_027` for RIT1). In contrast, `dependency_KIF18A` showed the clearest multivariable subgroup: colorectal cell lines with `feature_035 == 1` and high `feature_028` were much more dependent than any single-feature contrast alone. Several housekeeping-like dependencies (`TMED10`, `DHX9`, `POLR2A`, `RPL11`) showed smaller but statistically robust associations with high `feature_016`, with outcome-specific modifiers adding subgroup enrichment.
