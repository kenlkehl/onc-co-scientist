CRISPR dependency analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed 50,000 cell-line records using only the local task bundle. More negative dependency scores were interpreted as stronger knockout dependency. Unless stated otherwise, effect estimates are mean(subgroup) minus mean(comparison) on the dependency-score scale, so negative effects indicate stronger dependency. Interaction estimates are the product-term coefficient from OLS models of the form dependency ~ base + modifier + base:modifier.

Iterations 1-2 tested binary driver-like hypotheses for the oncogene dependency outcomes. `feature_009=1` was strongly associated with `dependency_BRAF` (effect -0.5552, p<1e-300), `feature_022=1` with `dependency_EGFR` (effect -0.3486, p<1e-300), `feature_024=1` with `dependency_ERBB2` (effect -0.4321, p<1e-300), `feature_014=1` with `dependency_KRAS` (effect -0.3075, p<1e-300), and `feature_027=1` with `dependency_RIT1` (effect -0.0955, p=1.55e-68). These main-effect hypotheses were all supported. Subgroup refinements showed the BRAF signal was strong in `feature_009=1`/`feature_011="skin"` lines, ERBB2 was further enhanced by `feature_010="her2_enriched"`, KRAS remained strong within both pancreatic and colorectal lineages, and RIT1 was enhanced in `feature_011="brain"` lines.

Iterations 3-4 focused on `dependency_KIF18A`. The initial colorectal hypothesis was supported: `feature_011="colorectal"` lines were much more negative than other lines (effect -0.4680, p<1e-300). Interaction tests then showed that this dependency was enabled by `feature_035=1` (interaction -0.6822, p<1e-300) and `feature_028 >= 0.819` (interaction -0.6691, p<1e-300), while `feature_020=1` suppressed the colorectal dependency (interaction +0.5714, p<1e-300). The complete refined subgroup `feature_011="colorectal"`, `feature_035=1`, `feature_028 >= 0.819`, and `feature_020=0` had mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.2990 versus -0.2530 in all other lines (effect -1.0460, p<1e-300). This was the strongest and most specific dependency pattern found in the dataset.

Iterations 5-6 examined broader essential-gene-like outcomes. High `feature_016` (`feature_016 >= 0.964`) was associated with stronger dependencies for `dependency_POLR2A` (effect -0.1043, p=1.01e-179), `dependency_DHX9` (effect -0.0371, p=1.71e-24), and `dependency_TMED10` (effect -0.0369, p=2.52e-24). Low `feature_016` had the opposite direction for POLR2A (effect +0.1005, p=1.20e-166). Outcome-specific refinements were supported for `dependency_DHX9` with high `feature_016` plus high `feature_023`, for `dependency_TMED10` with high `feature_016` plus high `feature_026`, and for `dependency_RPL11` with high `feature_003`. The `feature_003 >= 0.428` by `feature_030=1` interaction for RPL11 was significant but modest (interaction -0.0390, p=7.43e-03).

Iteration 7 fit adjusted OLS models that included categorical lineage/subtype/culture/batch/`feature_001`, all binary indicators, and standardized continuous features. The main binary oncogene associations persisted after adjustment: BRAF/`feature_009` coefficient -0.5543, EGFR/`feature_022` -0.3492, ERBB2/`feature_024` -0.4327, KRAS/`feature_014` -0.3097, and RIT1/`feature_027` -0.0955, all significant at p<0.05.

Iterations 8-9 performed the required systematic dependency-heterogeneity screens. For each dependency outcome, I screened 2x2 OLS interactions between a base dependency marker and all categorical levels, binary indicators, `feature_001` levels, and continuous Q1/Q3 predicates. BRAF did not show a convincing extra negative modifier (`feature_021=1` interaction -0.0351, p=0.117). EGFR showed a small high-`feature_023` enhancement in `feature_022=1` lines (interaction -0.0299, p=0.028). ERBB2 showed a `feature_010="her2_enriched"` enhancement (interaction -0.0671, p=0.037). KRAS showed a small `feature_010="her2_enriched"` enhancement (interaction -0.0693, p=0.034), but broad `feature_014=1` remained the clearer rule. RIT1 showed enhancement in brain lines (interaction -0.0582, p=0.0056). KIF18A showed the dominant colorectal/`feature_035` interaction described above. TMED10 did not show a significant negative interaction beyond high `feature_016` (`feature_011="prostate"` interaction -0.0309, p=0.063). DHX9 showed a modest high-`feature_016` by `feature_027=1` interaction (interaction -0.0323, p=0.0097). POLR2A showed a small high-`feature_016` by `feature_010="her2_enriched"` interaction (interaction -0.0551, p=0.033). RPL11 showed the high-`feature_003` by `feature_030=1` interaction noted above.

Final best-supported subgroup hypotheses by outcome:
- Final BRAF subgroup `feature_009=1`: subgroup mean -0.8047 vs comparison mean -0.2495, effect -0.5552 on dependency_BRAF; p<1e-300.
- Final EGFR subgroup `feature_022=1 & feature_023>=5.669`: subgroup mean -0.6240 vs comparison mean -0.2729, effect -0.3511 on dependency_EGFR; p=4.52e-144.
- Final ERBB2 subgroup `feature_024=1 & feature_010="her2_enriched"`: subgroup mean -0.7525 vs comparison mean -0.2944, effect -0.4581 on dependency_ERBB2; p=6.99e-32.
- Final KRAS subgroup `feature_014=1`: subgroup mean -0.5594 vs comparison mean -0.2520, effect -0.3075 on dependency_KRAS; p<1e-300.
- Final RIT1 subgroup `feature_027=1 & feature_011="brain"`: subgroup mean -0.4047 vs comparison mean -0.2650, effect -0.1397 on dependency_RIT1; p=1.01e-12.
- Final KIF18A subgroup `colorectal & feature_035=1 & feature_028>=0.819 & feature_020=0`: subgroup mean -1.2990 vs comparison mean -0.2530, effect -1.0460 on dependency_KIF18A; p<1e-300.
- Final TMED10 subgroup `feature_016>=0.964 & feature_026>=1.080`: subgroup mean -0.2931 vs comparison mean -0.2514, effect -0.0417 on dependency_TMED10; p=1.49e-10.
- Final DHX9 subgroup `feature_016>=0.964 & feature_023>=5.669`: subgroup mean -0.3892 vs comparison mean -0.3297, effect -0.0595 on dependency_DHX9; p=2.48e-21.
- Final POLR2A subgroup `feature_016>=0.964`: subgroup mean -0.4972 vs comparison mean -0.3928, effect -0.1043 on dependency_POLR2A; p=1.01e-179.
- Final RPL11 subgroup `feature_003>=0.428 & feature_030=1`: subgroup mean -0.3759 vs comparison mean -0.2681, effect -0.1078 on dependency_RPL11; p=5.35e-17.

Overall conclusion: the dataset contains five strong marker-specific oncogene dependency patterns, one highly specific multivariable KIF18A dependency subgroup, and several weaker broad essential-gene dependency axes. The strongest supported biological-style rule is the KIF18A subgroup requiring colorectal context, `feature_035=1`, high `feature_028`, and absence of `feature_020=1`. The broad essential outcomes are less sharply subgrouped; they are mainly associated with high `feature_016`, with smaller outcome-specific modifiers.
