CRISPR dependency map analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed the 50,000-row local dataset using the supplied column description only. More negative dependency scores were interpreted as stronger knockout dependency. I used Welch two-sample tests for binary and subgroup contrasts, OLS terms for interaction and adjusted main-effect models, and an exhaustive one-to-three-predicate subgroup scan for each dependency outcome. Continuous high/low subgroup predicates used empirical quartiles.

Iterations 1-2: driver-like main effects and lineage context
The strongest main-effect findings were direct and highly significant. `feature_009 == 1` marked much stronger BRAF dependency: mean `dependency_BRAF` -0.805 versus -0.250, effect -0.555, p=<1e-300. The parallel signals were `feature_022 == 1` for EGFR (effect -0.349, p=<1e-300), `feature_024 == 1` for ERBB2 (effect -0.432, p=<1e-300), and `feature_014 == 1` for KRAS (effect -0.307, p=<1e-300). Skin lineage also showed more negative BRAF scores (effect -0.178), but the more complete explanation remained `feature_009`. Pancreatic `feature_014 == 1` lines were KRAS-dependent versus all other lines, but the explicit `feature_014:pancreatic` interaction was not supported (beta -0.003, p=0.795).

Iterations 3-4: RIT1 and KIF18A heterogeneity
`feature_027 == 1` was associated with more negative RIT1 dependency (effect -0.096, p=1.55e-68). KIF18A was the most heterogeneous outcome. Colorectal lineage alone had a large effect (effect -0.468), as did `feature_035 == 1` (effect -0.292) and high `feature_028` (effect -0.217). The complete KIF18A subgroup was `feature_011 == colorectal`, `feature_035 == 1`, and `feature_028 >= 0.819`: mean -1.112 versus -0.253, effect -0.859, p=<1e-300. Within colorectal lines, this same complete subgroup was far more dependent than other colorectal lines (effect -0.849), and the saturated three-way interaction was strongly negative (beta -0.818, p=5.37e-225).

Iterations 5-6: shared process-like dependency signals
High `feature_016` (`>= 0.964`) was consistently associated with more negative scores for TMED10 (effect -0.037), DHX9 (effect -0.037), POLR2A (effect -0.104), and RPL11 (effect -0.042), with the largest of these for POLR2A. For DHX9, high `feature_023` also contributed (effect -0.029, p=2.05e-15). RPL11 initially appeared associated with both `feature_018 == 1` and high `feature_003`, but in the adjusted model the independent negative predictor was standardized `feature_003` (beta -0.056, p=1.8e-120); adjusted `feature_018` was not independently negative (beta 0.009, p=0.177).

Iterations 7-9: systematic subgroup searches for all dependency outcomes
The systematic search reinforced that BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, and KRAS are dominated by their primary flags. Top searched subgroups all contained the corresponding driver flag: BRAF contained `feature_009 == 1` (top effect -0.555), EGFR contained `feature_022 == 1` (effect -0.368), ERBB2 contained `feature_024 == 1` (effect -0.453), and KRAS contained `feature_014 == 1` (effect -0.309). These refinements changed subgroup means only modestly relative to the primary main effects. RIT1 was different: the top subgroup `feature_011 == brain` and `feature_027 == 1` had effect -0.140, and the `feature_027:brain` interaction was negative and significant (beta -0.058, p=0.00564).

For the remaining process-like outcomes, search-discovered subgroups were statistically significant but mostly weaker or less independently interactive. TMED10's best subgroup was high `feature_016`, `feature_027 == 1`, and low `feature_017`, with effect -0.102; its three-way interaction was modest but significant (beta -0.071, p=0.0125). DHX9's top subgroup was squamous, high `feature_023`, and `feature_029 == 0` (effect -0.084), but the simpler high-`feature_016` by high-`feature_023` interaction was not significant. POLR2A's top subgroup was skin with high `feature_016` and high `feature_026` (effect -0.129), but the three-way interaction was not significant; the stable signal was high `feature_016`. RPL11's top subgroup was `feature_018 == 1`, high `feature_016`, and high `feature_031` (effect -0.151), but its three-way interaction was not significant and adjusted models favored `feature_003`.

Final conclusions
The clearest feature-outcome relationships are `feature_009` to BRAF, `feature_022` to EGFR, `feature_024` to ERBB2, `feature_014` to KRAS, and `feature_027` to RIT1. KIF18A has the strongest true subgroup dependency, concentrated in colorectal lines with `feature_035 == 1` and high `feature_028`. POLR2A, DHX9, TMED10, and RPL11 show broader process-like associations, especially with high `feature_016`; RPL11 is best explained by high `feature_003` after adjustment rather than by `feature_018` alone.
