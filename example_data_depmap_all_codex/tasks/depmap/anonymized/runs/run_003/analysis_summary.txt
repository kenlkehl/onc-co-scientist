CRISPR dependency map ds001_depmap analysis summary

I analyzed the 50,000-row DepMap-style cell-line screen using only the task-bundle files. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger knockout dependency. The analysis used direct two-group contrasts with signed mean differences on the dependency-score scale, plus a systematic heterogeneity pass over 104 single-feature predicates followed by pairwise intersections among the strongest modifiers for each dependency outcome. For KIF18A, where the screen revealed a concentrated subgroup, I followed up with targeted three-way subgroup contrasts.

Early main-effect screening found strong oncogene-like dependencies. Cell lines with feature_009 == 1 were much more BRAF-dependent than feature_009 == 0 lines (mean -0.80470 vs -0.24950, effect -0.55520, p < 1e-300). EGFR dependency was concentrated in feature_022 == 1 lines (mean -0.60105 vs -0.25241, effect -0.34864, p < 1e-300). ERBB2 dependency was concentrated in feature_024 == 1 lines (mean -0.68559 vs -0.25347, effect -0.43212, p < 1e-300). KRAS dependency was concentrated in feature_014 == 1 lines (mean -0.55941 vs -0.25196, effect -0.30746, p < 1e-300). RIT1 dependency was weaker but still significant in feature_027 == 1 lines (mean -0.35272 vs -0.25720, effect -0.09552, p < 1e-300).

The broad continuous-feature screen showed that high feature_016 was associated with more negative dependencies for several housekeeping-like outcomes. For POLR2A, feature_016 >= 0.964 had mean -0.49717 versus -0.39283 in other lines (effect -0.10435, p < 1e-300). For TMED10, the high-feature_016 effect was smaller but significant (effect -0.03688, p < 1e-300), and the strongest screened pair was feature_016 >= 0.964 plus feature_026 >= 1.0 (effect -0.04323, p < 1e-300). For DHX9, high feature_016 combined with high feature_023 (feature_023 >= 5.669) defined the strongest tested subgroup (mean -0.38919 vs -0.32972, effect -0.05948, p < 1e-300). RPL11 dependency was mainly associated with feature_018 == 1 (mean -0.36139 vs -0.25234, effect -0.10906, p < 1e-300), with only modest strengthening in the feature_018 == 1 and feature_003 >= 0.428 subgroup (effect -0.11225, p < 1e-300).

The most important heterogeneity finding was for dependency_KIF18A. A simple colorectal lineage hypothesis was supported: feature_011 == "colorectal" lines had mean KIF18A dependency -0.72035 versus -0.25240 in non-colorectal lines (effect -0.46796, p < 1e-300). However, follow-up showed this was not merely a lineage main effect. The colorectal and feature_035 == 1 subgroup had effect -0.68985, and the colorectal plus high-feature_028 subgroup had effect -0.67614. The complete intersection feature_011 == "colorectal", feature_035 == 1, and feature_028 >= 0.819 was much stronger: mean -1.11187 versus -0.25301 in all other lines (effect -0.85885, p < 1e-300).

Conditional KIF18A tests confirmed that all three subgroup predicates were required. Within colorectal and feature_035 == 1 lines, high feature_028 had mean -1.11187 versus -0.27267 for lower feature_028 (effect -0.83919, p < 1e-300). Within colorectal and high-feature_028 lines, feature_035 == 1 had mean -1.11187 versus -0.27183 for feature_035 == 0 (effect -0.84004, p < 1e-300). Within feature_035 == 1 and high-feature_028 lines, colorectal lineage had mean -1.11187 versus -0.25282 in non-colorectal lines (effect -0.85905, p < 1e-300). Incomplete subgroups were not more dependent: colorectal/feature_035 == 1/feature_028 < 0.819 had a positive effect (+0.03471), colorectal/feature_035 == 0/high-feature_028 had a positive effect (+0.03563), and non-colorectal/feature_035 == 1/high-feature_028 also had a positive effect (+0.05685). Thus the best-supported KIF18A conclusion is a complete three-way dependency subgroup, not any one predictor alone.

The systematic heterogeneity pass for all dependency outcomes did not reveal comparably strong higher-order structure outside KIF18A. For BRAF, ERBB2, and KRAS, the top pairwise intersections largely recapitulated the single-feature signals and did not improve meaningfully on feature_009 == 1, feature_024 == 1, or feature_014 == 1. EGFR had a slight strengthening for feature_022 == 1 with feature_016 >= 0.828 (effect -0.35507 versus -0.34864 for feature_022 alone). RIT1 was modestly stronger in feature_027 == 1 with feature_016 >= 0.828 (effect -0.10525 versus -0.09552 for feature_027 alone). Overall, the dataset contains clear main-effect dependencies for BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, KRAS, RIT1, POLR2A, TMED10, DHX9, and RPL11, plus a biologically notable KIF18A dependency concentrated only in the complete colorectal/feature_035/high-feature_028 subgroup.
