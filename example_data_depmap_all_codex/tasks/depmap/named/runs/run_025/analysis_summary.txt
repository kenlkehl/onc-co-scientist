CRISPR dependency map analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed 50,000 cell-line records using only the local dataset bundle. Dependency scores are interpreted so that more negative values indicate stronger dependency after knockout. I used Welch group-vs-complement tests for binary and subgroup hypotheses, simple linear regressions for continuous predictors after standardization, and OLS interaction models for candidate heterogeneity. The systematic heterogeneity search evaluated 88 single predicates, 3,652 paired predicates, and targeted triplets for every dependency outcome; KIF18A received an additional four-predicate refinement because SMAD4 loss clearly suppressed the strongest APC/colorectal/WNT signal.

Iterations 1-2 established the major matched oncogene dependencies. BRAF V600E was strongly associated with BRAF dependency (mean -0.8047 versus -0.2495; effect -0.5552; p underflowed to 0). EGFR amplification predicted EGFR dependency (effect -0.3486; p=0), ERBB2 amplification predicted ERBB2 dependency (effect -0.4321; p=0), and KRAS mutation predicted KRAS dependency (effect -0.3075; p=0). NF1 loss predicted RIT1 dependency (effect -0.0955; p=1.55e-68). APC mutation predicted KIF18A dependency (effect -0.2921; p=0). MYC amplification and high copy_number_8q24 both predicted RPL11 dependency (effects -0.1091 and -0.0988; p=5.71e-140 and 7.61e-160).

Iteration 3 showed broad continuous covariate effects. Higher growth_rate_doublings_per_day was associated with more negative POLR2A dependency (slope -0.0612 per SD; p=0) and more negative TMED10 dependency (slope -0.0203 per SD; p=1.28e-38). Higher rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm was associated with more negative DHX9 dependency (slope -0.0172 per SD; p=5.41e-28). Higher wnt_activity_score was associated with more negative KIF18A dependency (slope -0.0973 per SD; p=0).

Iteration 4 tested whether obvious lineage context explained BRAF and KRAS effects. The BRAF V600E effect was not significantly stronger in skin lineage: the interaction was -0.0126 (p=0.288), so the BRAF dependency is best described by braf_v600e status itself. The KRAS mutation effect was not significantly stronger in pancreatic lineage: the interaction was -0.0028 (p=0.795), so the KRAS dependency is also best described by kras_mutation status itself.

Iteration 5 found two supported heterogeneity patterns. ERBB2-amplified HER2-enriched lines had especially negative ERBB2 dependency (mean -0.7525 versus -0.2944; effect -0.4581; p=6.99e-32), and the amplification effect was significantly stronger in HER2-enriched subtype (interaction -0.0671; p=0.037). NF1-loss brain lines had stronger RIT1 dependency (mean -0.4047 versus -0.2650; effect -0.1397; p=1.01e-12), and the NF1-loss effect was stronger in brain than outside brain (interaction -0.0582; p=0.00564).

Iteration 6 identified the strongest multivariable dependency in the dataset. APC-mutant colorectal WNT-high lines had highly negative KIF18A dependency (mean -1.1119 versus -0.2530; effect -0.8589; p=0), and the APC effect was almost entirely colorectal-specific (interaction -0.6822; p=0). SMAD4 loss suppressed this dependency: the APC-by-SMAD4-loss interaction was +0.3322 (p=6.18e-137). The final complete KIF18A subgroup was apc_mutation=1, lineage=colorectal, wnt_activity_score >= 0.819, and smad4_loss=0; this group had mean dependency_KIF18A -1.2990 versus -0.2530 in all other lines (effect -1.0460; p=0). The corresponding WNT-high APC-mutant colorectal group with smad4_loss=1 was not dependent relative to the complement (effect +0.0517; p=2.56e-04), confirming suppression.

Iteration 7 found only a modest TMED10 subgroup. The clearest exploratory pattern was nf1_loss=1, high growth_rate_doublings_per_day >= 0.964, and low rnaseq_ASCL2_log2_tpm <= 1.956. This subgroup had mean dependency_TMED10 -0.3556 versus -0.2534 in other lines (effect -0.1022; p=6.88e-08). NF1 loss had a stronger TMED10 effect in ASCL2-low lines than outside them (interaction -0.0296; p=0.0184). This result is statistically supported but weaker and more exploratory than the canonical oncogene dependencies.

Iteration 8 refined DHX9. High MYC expression plus high growth gave a robust subgroup (mean -0.3892 versus -0.3297; effect -0.0595; p=2.48e-21). The MYC-high by growth-high interaction was not significant (interaction -0.0133; p=0.107), suggesting largely additive effects. A narrower squamous, low-ploidy, high-MYC subgroup had a larger effect (effect -0.1343; p=1.82e-05) but small support, so I treat high MYC plus high growth as the more robust final DHX9 subgroup.

Iteration 9 refined POLR2A and RPL11. POLR2A dependency was primarily growth-associated: high-growth lines had mean -0.4972 versus -0.3928 (effect -0.1043; p=1.01e-179). HER2-enriched subtype modestly strengthened the growth effect (interaction -0.0551; p=0.0327), but high growth alone is the main final subgroup. For RPL11, MYC-amplified high-growth lines had mean -0.3936 versus -0.2632 (effect -0.1303; p=1.10e-68). The MYC-amplification by high-growth interaction was not significant (p=0.136), but MYC amplification interacted with high stemness (interaction -0.0352; p=4.21e-04), suggesting stemness can intensify the MYC/RPL11 relationship.

Iteration 10 consolidated final dependency-specific subgroup hypotheses. BRAF is best captured by braf_v600e=1. EGFR is best captured by egfr_amplification=1. ERBB2 is best captured by erbb2_amplification=1 plus lineage_subtype=her2_enriched. KRAS is best captured by kras_mutation=1. RIT1 is best captured by nf1_loss=1 plus lineage=brain. KIF18A is best captured by apc_mutation=1, lineage=colorectal, WNT-high state, and intact SMAD4. TMED10 has only a weaker exploratory NF1/high-growth/ASCL2-low subgroup. DHX9 is most robustly linked to high MYC expression plus high growth. POLR2A tracks high growth rate. RPL11 tracks MYC amplification with high growth, with supporting evidence from copy_number_8q24 and stemness.

Overall, the dataset contains strong matched oncogene dependencies for BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, KRAS, and RIT1; a very strong context-specific KIF18A dependency in APC-mutant colorectal WNT-high SMAD4-intact lines; and more general proliferation/MYC-linked dependency patterns for DHX9, POLR2A, and RPL11. TMED10 showed the least specific biology, with only a modest multivariable subgroup signal.
