Dataset ds001_depmap contains 50,000 cell-line CRISPR dependency records. I analyzed all 10 dependency outcomes using local-only decoding of dataset.parquet, then ran main-effect tests, subgroup comparisons, continuous-feature regressions, and a systematic heterogeneity screen over 72 feature predicates and pairwise interactions for each dependency outcome. More negative dependency scores indicate stronger knockout dependency.

The expected oncogene dependencies were strongly recovered. Cell lines with `braf_v600e=1` had much more negative `dependency_BRAF` than non-mutant lines (mean -0.8047 vs -0.2495; effect -0.5552; p underflowed to 0), and `lineage=skin` was also enriched for BRAF dependency (effect -0.1783; p=1.32e-160). For EGFR, `egfr_amplification=1` had mean `dependency_EGFR` -0.6010 versus -0.2524 in non-amplified lines (effect -0.3486; p=0). High-growth EGFR-amplified lines were similarly dependent (effect -0.3526; p=3.12e-212), while the pairwise screen suggested low `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` slightly weakens rather than strengthens the EGFR-amplified dependency.

ERBB2 and KRAS showed the same target-matched pattern. `erbb2_amplification=1` was associated with more negative `dependency_ERBB2` (mean -0.6856 vs -0.2535; effect -0.4321; p=0), including ERBB2-amplified breast lines (effect -0.4163; p=0). `kras_mutation=1` was associated with more negative `dependency_KRAS` (mean -0.5594 vs -0.2520; effect -0.3075; p=0). Pancreatic and colorectal lineages were enriched, but the KRAS mutation itself remained the clearest final subgroup.

For `dependency_RIT1`, `nf1_loss=1` was the strongest primary association. NF1-loss lines had mean `dependency_RIT1` -0.3527 versus -0.2572 for NF1-intact lines (effect -0.0955; p=2.03e-70). The most negative plausible subgroup was `lineage=brain & nf1_loss=1` (mean -0.4047 vs -0.2650; effect -0.1397; p=1.21e-13).

The main hidden interaction was for `dependency_KIF18A`. A first pass showed that `lineage=colorectal`, `apc_mutation=1`, high `wnt_activity_score`, and high `rnaseq_ASCL2_log2_tpm` were all associated with more negative KIF18A dependency. The refined subgroup analysis showed that the complete pattern is suppressor-dependent: `lineage=colorectal & apc_mutation=1 & smad4_loss=0` had mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.0922 versus -0.2527 in the complement (effect -0.8396; p=0). In contrast, `lineage=colorectal & apc_mutation=1 & smad4_loss=1` had no dependency signal and was slightly less negative than the complement (effect +0.0560; p=1.66e-5). The colorectal-by-SMAD4-loss interaction contrast was +0.5714 (p=2.36e-302), indicating that `smad4_loss=1` suppresses the colorectal APC/WNT-associated KIF18A dependency. The high-WNT version of the same result was nearly identical: colorectal high-WNT SMAD4-intact lines had effect -0.8277, while colorectal high-WNT SMAD4-loss lines had effect +0.0513.

`dependency_TMED10` and `dependency_POLR2A` were dominated by growth and screen covariates rather than a single mutation subgroup. For TMED10, `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` had slope -0.1012 (p=1.11e-38) and `cas9_activity_score` had slope -0.1040 (p=1.41e-15); high-growth, high-Cas9 lines had a modestly more negative subgroup effect of -0.0417 (p=1.32e-10). For POLR2A, growth was much stronger: the slope for `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` was -0.3048 (p=0), and top-quartile growth lines had effect -0.1043 (p=4.27e-183). Pairwise molecular interactions were smaller and did not displace growth as the best-supported explanation.

MYC/8q24 biology emerged for `dependency_DHX9` and `dependency_RPL11`. For DHX9, higher `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` predicted more negative dependency (slope -0.0161; p=5.02e-28), and `myc_amplification=1` also had a smaller negative effect (effect -0.0184; p=1.68e-5). High-growth, high-MYC-expression lines had a stronger DHX9 subgroup effect of -0.0595 (p=1.44e-21). For RPL11, `copy_number_8q24` had a strong negative slope (effect -0.1015 per unit; p=8.39e-263), and `myc_amplification=1` had effect -0.1091 (p=7.04e-144). The strongest RPL11 subgroup found was high growth plus MYC amplification, with effect -0.1303 (p=1.89e-72).

Overall, the clearest dependency-specific conclusions are: BRAF dependency tracks `braf_v600e=1`; EGFR dependency tracks `egfr_amplification=1`; ERBB2 dependency tracks `erbb2_amplification=1`; KRAS dependency tracks `kras_mutation=1`; RIT1 dependency tracks `nf1_loss=1`, especially in brain lineage; KIF18A dependency is concentrated in `lineage=colorectal & apc_mutation=1 & smad4_loss=0` and is suppressed by `smad4_loss=1`; TMED10 and POLR2A primarily track growth/Cas9 screen covariates; DHX9 and RPL11 track MYC/8q24-high states. The systematic heterogeneity search was essential for KIF18A because the main colorectal/APC/WNT signal would be incomplete without the SMAD4-loss suppressor.
