CRISPR dependency analysis summary for ds001_depmap

This task analyzed 50,000 cancer cell-line CRISPR dependency records. Dependency scores are interpreted on their natural scale, where more negative values indicate stronger dependency after knockout. The analyses covered driver-gene main effects, adjusted models, subgroup contrasts, and a systematic dependency-heterogeneity search over binary predicates, categorical levels, quantile-thresholded continuous features, and small feature intersections for every dependency outcome.

The first iteration recovered the expected canonical oncogene dependency signals. Cell lines with `braf_v600e=1` were much more BRAF-dependent than BRAF-wild-type lines, with mean `dependency_BRAF` -0.8047 versus -0.2495 and effect -0.5552 (p approximately 1e-300). `egfr_amplification=1` was associated with more negative `dependency_EGFR` (effect -0.3486), `erbb2_amplification=1` with more negative `dependency_ERBB2` (effect -0.4321), and `kras_mutation=1` with more negative `dependency_KRAS` (effect -0.3075); all were decisively significant.

The second iteration checked whether lineage patterns were independent or simply reflected driver enrichment. After adjustment for the relevant driver alteration and screen covariates, `lineage=skin` was not independently associated with `dependency_BRAF` (beta -0.0021, p=0.706), and `lineage=brain` was not independently associated with `dependency_EGFR` (beta 0.0029, p=0.635). Among KRAS-mutant lines, the pancreatic-lineage interaction was essentially zero (beta -0.0004, p=0.971), supporting `kras_mutation` itself rather than a pancreatic-specific KRAS dependency. In contrast, `nf1_loss=1` remained a clear RIT1-associated subgroup, with adjusted beta -0.0953 and unadjusted means -0.3527 versus -0.2572.

The third and fourth iterations tested broad growth, screen-quality, MYC, and 8q24 hypotheses. High `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` (>=0.964) was associated with more negative `dependency_POLR2A` (mean -0.4972 versus -0.3928; effect -0.1043) and more negative `dependency_TMED10` (mean -0.2816 versus -0.2447; effect -0.0369). High growth combined with high `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` (>=5.669) marked more negative `dependency_DHX9` (effect -0.0595). For RPL11, high `copy_number_8q24` and `myc_amplification=1` both marked stronger dependency, with effects -0.0988 and -0.1091 respectively. Continuous `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` was also associated with more negative `dependency_DHX9` (slope -0.0161 per log2 TPM).

The fifth and sixth iterations focused on KIF18A heterogeneity. The initial scan found strong KIF18A signals for `lineage='colorectal'` (effect -0.4680), `apc_mutation=1` (effect -0.2921), high `wnt_activity_score>=0.819` (effect -0.2168), and high `rnaseq_ASCL2_log2_tpm>=3.559` (effect -0.1083). Refinement showed that the complete biologically specific subgroup was `lineage='colorectal'`, `apc_mutation=1`, high `wnt_activity_score>=0.819`, and `smad4_loss=0`. This subgroup had mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.2990 versus -0.2530 in all other lines (effect -1.0460, p approximately 1e-300), and the complete subgroup indicator remained strongly negative after including its component predicates (beta -1.0335).

The SMAD4 predicate was essential to the KIF18A interpretation. Within colorectal/APC-mutant/high-WNT lines, `smad4_loss=0` lines had mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.2990, whereas `smad4_loss=1` lines had mean -0.2557 (effect -1.0433, p approximately 1e-300). This indicates that SMAD4 loss suppresses or disables the otherwise strong colorectal APC/WNT-associated KIF18A dependency. High ASCL2 expression further enriched the dependency: adding `rnaseq_ASCL2_log2_tpm>=3.559` produced mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.3320 versus -0.2749 in the complement (effect -1.0572), but the four-predicate colorectal/APC/high-WNT/SMAD4-intact subgroup was already strongly dependent.

The seventh and ninth iterations separated independent effects from correlated markers. For `dependency_DHX9`, continuous `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` retained an adjusted negative coefficient (beta -0.0160), while `myc_amplification` was not independently negative once MYC expression was included (beta 0.0028, p=0.532). For `dependency_RPL11`, continuous `copy_number_8q24` was the independent negative signal (beta -0.1041), while `myc_amplification` was not independently negative after 8q24 copy number and MYC expression adjustment (beta 0.0089, p=0.184). For `dependency_POLR2A`, `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` remained dominant (beta -0.3053), while 8q24 and MYC-amplification terms were not significant. For `dependency_TMED10`, growth (beta -0.1010) and Cas9 activity (beta -0.1035, p=1.78e-15) were the main signals; `nf1_loss` added only a small negative coefficient (beta -0.0134, p=0.013).

The required systematic heterogeneity search covered every dependency outcome. Final best-supported subgroup definitions were: `dependency_BRAF` by `braf_v600e=1` (effect -0.5552); `dependency_EGFR` by `egfr_amplification=1` (effect -0.3486); `dependency_ERBB2` by `erbb2_amplification=1` (effect -0.4321); `dependency_KRAS` by `kras_mutation=1` (effect -0.3075); `dependency_RIT1` by `nf1_loss=1` (effect -0.0955); `dependency_KIF18A` by `lineage='colorectal'`, `apc_mutation=1`, high `wnt_activity_score>=0.819`, and `smad4_loss=0` (effect -1.0460), with high `rnaseq_ASCL2_log2_tpm` as an enriching marker; `dependency_TMED10` primarily by high `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` (effect -0.0369); `dependency_DHX9` by high MYC expression, especially with high growth (effect -0.0595); `dependency_POLR2A` by high growth (effect -0.1043); and `dependency_RPL11` by high `copy_number_8q24`, especially overlapping `myc_amplification=1` (effect -0.1123), with continuous 8q24 copy number carrying the independent adjusted effect.

Overall, the supported findings are direct driver-matched dependencies for BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, KRAS, and RIT1; broad growth or screen-quality associations for POLR2A and TMED10; MYC/8q24-associated DHX9 and RPL11 dependencies; and one prominent buried interaction for KIF18A. The strongest biologically specific discovery is the colorectal APC-mutant high-WNT SMAD4-intact KIF18A dependency, where SMAD4 loss suppresses the effect and high ASCL2 expression further enriches the dependent subgroup.
