CRISPR Dependency Map Analysis Summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed 50,000 cell-line records with 10 dependency outcomes. Dependency scores are centered so more negative values indicate stronger dependency after knockout. I used Welch tests for binary/subgroup contrasts, saturated 2x2 interaction models for candidate modifiers, and adjusted OLS models for selected robustness checks with growth rate, Cas9 activity, serum, batch, and culture covariates. I also ran a systematic heterogeneity scan over 72 single predicates and all two-predicate intersections with n>=200 for every dependency outcome.

The strongest main effects were the expected oncogene addiction patterns. `braf_v600e=1` made `dependency_BRAF` much more negative (effect -0.555195, p=0), `egfr_amplification=1` made `dependency_EGFR` more negative (effect -0.348638, p=0), `erbb2_amplification=1` made `dependency_ERBB2` more negative (effect -0.432122, p=0), and `kras_mutation=1` made `dependency_KRAS` more negative (effect -0.307456, p=0). These effects remained very large after adjusting for screen and growth covariates.

Lineage modifiers were more selective. Skin lineage did not materially intensify BRAF dependency among BRAF V600E lines (effect -0.010335, p=0.2948), pancreatic lineage did not intensify KRAS dependency among KRAS-mutant lines (effect +0.000420, p=0.9553), and brain lineage did not significantly intensify EGFR dependency among EGFR-amplified lines (effect -0.013448, p=0.3260). The one supported oncogene-lineage modifier was ERBB2: among ERBB2-amplified lines, `lineage_subtype=her2_enriched` was more negative for `dependency_ERBB2` (effect -0.068928, p=0.02425; interaction -0.067107, p=0.03723).

`dependency_RIT1` showed a distinct RAS-pathway pattern. `nf1_loss=1` was associated with more negative RIT1 dependency (effect -0.095520, p=1.553e-68). The effect was stronger in brain NF1-loss lines: the `lineage=brain` by `nf1_loss=1` interaction was -0.058229 (p=0.005638), and brain/NF1-loss lines averaged -0.404687.

The clearest multifeature subgroup in the entire dataset was for `dependency_KIF18A`. Colorectal lineage, APC mutation, and high WNT activity each showed negative KIF18A effects, but the dependency was concentrated in their combination. `lineage=colorectal AND apc_mutation=1` had a strong interaction effect of -0.682214 (p=0), and the complete subgroup `lineage=colorectal AND apc_mutation=1 AND wnt_activity_score>=0.819` averaged -1.111866 versus -0.253014 in all other lines (effect -0.858852, p=0). Dropping any one predicate suppressed the signal: colorectal/APC/not-high-WNT, colorectal/noAPC/high-WNT, and non-colorectal/APC/high-WNT groups all averaged near -0.27 or -0.25.

MYC/8q biology appeared in ribosome and helicase dependencies. `myc_amplification=1` made `dependency_RPL11` more negative (effect -0.109056, p=5.712e-140), and high `copy_number_8q24` did the same (effect -0.098753, p=7.610e-160). In a joint adjusted model, both retained independent associations with RPL11 dependency: -0.047157 for MYC amplification and -0.066766 for high 8q24. High `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` was associated with more negative `dependency_DHX9` (effect -0.028610, p=2.050e-15; adjusted effect -0.027339, p=3.524e-14).

Proliferation and screen covariates were important for broadly essential dependencies. A one-SD increase in `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` made `dependency_POLR2A` more negative after covariate adjustment (effect -0.061204, p=0). Growth also had smaller negative associations with `dependency_TMED10`, `dependency_DHX9`, and `dependency_RPL11`; Cas9 activity had small negative associations as well. For TMED10, the systematic scan found only weak subgroup signals, such as `lineage_subtype=luminal AND stk11_loss=1` (effect -0.0541, p=0.018, n=243) and a small adjusted NF1-loss effect (-0.013411, p=0.01344), so I would treat TMED10 as lacking a strong, biologically clean dependency subgroup in this dataset.

Final best-supported subgroup or driver summary by outcome: `dependency_BRAF` is driven by `braf_v600e=1`; `dependency_EGFR` by `egfr_amplification=1`; `dependency_ERBB2` by `erbb2_amplification=1`, with added enrichment in `lineage_subtype=her2_enriched`; `dependency_KRAS` by `kras_mutation=1`; `dependency_RIT1` by `nf1_loss=1`, especially `lineage=brain AND nf1_loss=1`; `dependency_KIF18A` by `lineage=colorectal AND apc_mutation=1 AND wnt_activity_score>=0.819`; `dependency_TMED10` mostly by small growth/Cas9 and weak subgroup effects; `dependency_DHX9` by high MYC RNA with modest effect; `dependency_POLR2A` by faster growth rate; and `dependency_RPL11` by MYC amplification and high 8q24 copy number, especially in fast-growing lines.
