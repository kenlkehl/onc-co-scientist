CRISPR dependency map analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed the 50000-row DepMap-style CRISPR dependency dataset using only the local task bundle. Dependency scores are interpreted on their natural scale, where more negative values indicate stronger dependency. Tests used Welch mean comparisons for binary, categorical, and subgroup hypotheses; univariate linear slopes for continuous effects; and a systematic two-feature heterogeneity screen over lineage, lineage_subtype, culture_type, screen_batch, media_serum_pct, binary molecular features, and top/bottom quartile predicates for continuous features.

Iteration 1 tested canonical oncogene-dependency hypotheses. All four were strongly supported. BRAF V600E lines had much more negative dependency_BRAF scores than other lines (-0.804698 vs -0.249503; effect -0.555195; p < 1e-300). EGFR-amplified lines had more negative dependency_EGFR (-0.601046 vs -0.252407; effect -0.348638; p < 1e-300). ERBB2-amplified lines had more negative dependency_ERBB2 (-0.685595 vs -0.253473; effect -0.432122; p < 1e-300). KRAS-mutant lines had more negative dependency_KRAS (-0.559415 vs -0.251959; effect -0.307456; p < 1e-300).

Iteration 2 asked whether lineage structure explained or refined those oncogene signals. Skin lineage was more BRAF-dependent (effect -0.178251), pancreatic lineage was more KRAS-dependent (effect -0.112747), brain lineage was more EGFR-dependent (effect -0.055171), and breast lineage was slightly more ERBB2-dependent (effect -0.029180). These lineage effects were statistically significant, but they were substantially smaller than the corresponding alteration effects, especially for EGFR and ERBB2.

Iteration 3 tested RIT1. NF1-loss lines had more negative dependency_RIT1 scores (-0.352716 vs -0.257196; effect -0.095520; p < 1e-300). The strongest pairwise RIT1 subgroup was brain lineage with NF1 loss, with mean -0.404687 versus -0.264988 in all others (effect -0.139699; p = 1.22e-13). This supports NF1 loss as the main RIT1 dependency context, with brain lineage adding enrichment.

Iterations 4 and 5 identified the strongest non-canonical subgroup: dependency_KIF18A. Colorectal lineage alone had a large negative shift (-0.720354 vs -0.252397; effect -0.467957; p < 1e-300). APC mutation and high WNT activity were also strongly associated with KIF18A dependency. Refinement showed that colorectal APC-mutant lines had mean dependency_KIF18A -0.942531 versus -0.252676 in all others (effect -0.689855), and colorectal high-WNT lines had mean -0.928802 versus -0.252657 (effect -0.676145). The strongest complete subgroup found was lineage == colorectal, apc_mutation == 1, wnt_activity_score >= 0.819, and smad4_loss == 0, with mean dependency_KIF18A -1.299007 versus -0.253046 in all other lines (effect -1.045960; p < 1e-300). This suggests SMAD4-intact, WNT-active APC-mutant colorectal lines are the clearest KIF18A dependency subgroup.

Iteration 6 tested screen- and proliferation-linked essentiality. growth_rate_doublings_per_day was associated with more negative dependency_TMED10 (slope -0.101179; p < 1e-300) and especially dependency_POLR2A (slope -0.304770; p < 1e-300). Top-quartile growth lines had more negative dependency_POLR2A (-0.497174 vs -0.392828; effect -0.104346). These patterns look more like broad proliferative or screen-essentiality effects than narrow lineage-specific vulnerabilities.

Iteration 7 tested MYC-linked dependencies. Higher rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm was associated with more negative dependency_DHX9 (slope -0.016103; p < 1e-300). MYC amplification and 8q24 gain were associated with more negative dependency_RPL11: myc_amplification == 1 gave effect -0.109056, and top-quartile copy_number_8q24 gave effect -0.098753. The high-MYC/high-growth subgroup also had more negative dependency_DHX9 (effect -0.059477), and MYC-amplified/high-growth lines had more negative dependency_RPL11 (effect -0.130333).

Iteration 8 performed the required systematic dependency-heterogeneity search for every dependency outcome. For dependency_BRAF, the top pair was braf_v600e == 1 and msi_high == 0 (effect -0.5514), essentially recapitulating the BRAF V600E main effect. For dependency_EGFR, the top pair was egfr_amplification == 1 and high growth (effect -0.352601), only slightly stronger than EGFR amplification alone. For dependency_ERBB2, the top pair was erbb2_amplification == 1 and egfr_amplification == 0 (effect -0.4286), again recapitulating ERBB2 amplification. For dependency_KRAS, the top pair was kras_mutation == 1 and brca2_loss == 0 (effect -0.3014), close to KRAS mutation alone. For dependency_RIT1, the top pair was brain lineage with NF1 loss (effect -0.139699). For dependency_KIF18A, the top pair was colorectal lineage with APC mutation (effect -0.689855). For dependency_TMED10, the strongest pair was prostate lineage with high growth (effect -0.0533), but this was modest. For dependency_DHX9, the strongest pair was squamous subtype with high MYC expression (effect -0.0656). For dependency_POLR2A, the strongest pair was HER2-enriched subtype with high growth (effect -0.1174). For dependency_RPL11, the strongest pair was MYC amplification with high growth (effect -0.130333).

Iterations 9 and 10 consolidated supported versus weak or secondary hypotheses. EGFR amplification explained far more dependency_EGFR signal than brain lineage alone, and ERBB2 amplification explained far more dependency_ERBB2 signal than breast lineage alone. TMED10 did not show a compelling biological subgroup beyond growth-related dependency. Final supported dependency patterns were: BRAF with braf_v600e; EGFR with egfr_amplification; ERBB2 with erbb2_amplification; KRAS with kras_mutation; RIT1 with nf1_loss, strongest in brain/NF1-loss lines; KIF18A with colorectal APC-mutant high-WNT SMAD4-intact lines; TMED10 and POLR2A with growth rate; DHX9 with MYC expression and growth; and RPL11 with MYC amplification or 8q24 gain.
