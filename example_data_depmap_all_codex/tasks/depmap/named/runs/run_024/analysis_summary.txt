CRISPR Dependency Map analysis summary for ds001_depmap

Dataset and methods
I analyzed 50,000 cell-line records from dataset.parquet. Dependency scores are interpreted on their native scale, where more negative values indicate stronger dependency. I used Welch two-sample comparisons for binary or threshold-defined subgroups and ordinary least squares interaction models for refined hypotheses, usually adjusting for growth_rate_doublings_per_day, cas9_activity_score, and screen_batch. For continuous high-subgroup hypotheses, the relevant Q75 thresholds were: wnt_activity_score=0.819, rnaseq_ASCL2_log2_tpm=3.559, growth_rate_doublings_per_day=0.964, rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm=5.669, and copy_number_8q24=0.428.

Iteration 1: matched oncogene dependencies
The canonical matched driver hypotheses were strongly supported. braf_v600e=1 lines had much more negative dependency_BRAF than braf_v600e=0 lines (difference -0.5552, p<1e-300). egfr_amplification=1 lines had more negative dependency_EGFR (difference -0.3486, p<1e-300). erbb2_amplification=1 lines had more negative dependency_ERBB2 (difference -0.4321, p<1e-300). kras_mutation=1 lines had more negative dependency_KRAS (difference -0.3075, p<1e-300). These were the clearest first-pass main effects.

Iteration 2: lineage heterogeneity of canonical oncogene effects
I then tested whether the apparent lineage patterns represented true effect modification. The BRAF V600E dependency was not significantly stronger in skin lines after adjustment (BRAF-by-skin interaction -0.0128, p=0.281), even though skin lines were enriched for BRAF dependency overall. EGFR amplification was not significantly stronger in brain lines (interaction -0.0197, p=0.200). KRAS mutation was not stronger in pancreatic lines (interaction -0.0024, p=0.821). ERBB2 was the exception: erbb2_amplification had a modestly stronger negative effect in breast or gastric lines (interaction -0.0255, p=0.0273).

Iteration 3: KRAS/RIT1 pathway refinements
SMAD4 loss did not modify KRAS-mutant dependency: the kras_mutation-by-smad4_loss interaction was essentially zero (-0.0002, p=0.987), refuting the hypothesis that SMAD4 loss strengthens KRAS dependency beyond the main KRAS effect. RIT1 dependency, however, was clearly associated with nf1_loss: adjusted coefficient -0.0964, p=8.04e-59. The RIT1/NF1 effect was not lung-specific (nf1_loss-by-lung interaction +0.0058, p=0.688), so the best-supported RIT1 subgroup is NF1-loss broadly rather than a lung-only subgroup.

Iteration 4: KIF18A discovery
KIF18A showed a much stronger subgroup pattern than the initial driver dependencies. Colorectal lines had more negative dependency_KIF18A than non-colorectal lines (difference -0.4680, p<1e-300). apc_mutation=1 was also strongly associated with more negative dependency_KIF18A (difference -0.2921, p<1e-300). High WNT activity was strongly associated as well (wnt_activity_score >= 0.819: difference -0.2168, p<1e-300), and high ASCL2 expression was a weaker but significant marker (difference -0.1083, p=1.7e-104).

Iteration 5: complete KIF18A subgroup and suppression by SMAD4 loss
The KIF18A signal refined to a complete subgroup: lineage="colorectal", apc_mutation=1, high WNT activity, and smad4_loss=0. The WNT slope was much more negative in colorectal lines than elsewhere (WNT-by-colorectal interaction -0.3552, p<1e-300). Within colorectal APC-mutant high-WNT lines, smad4_loss=1 nearly abolished the dependency: SMAD4-loss lines averaged -0.2557 while SMAD4-intact lines averaged -1.2990, a positive difference of +1.0433 for smad4_loss=1 versus smad4_loss=0 (p<1e-300). The complete SMAD4-intact subgroup had mean dependency_KIF18A -1.2990 versus -0.2530 in all other lines (difference -1.0460, p<1e-300). This was the strongest and most specific result in the dataset.

Iteration 6: MYC-associated dependencies
RPL11 dependency tracked MYC/8q24 biology. myc_amplification=1 lines had more negative dependency_RPL11 (difference -0.1091, p=5.7e-140), and copy_number_8q24 remained a negative predictor in an adjusted model (coefficient -0.1030, p=7.8e-99). DHX9 showed a weaker MYC/growth association: high-growth, high-rnaseq_MYC lines had more negative dependency_DHX9 (difference -0.0595, p=2.5e-21). However, myc_amplification did not independently predict DHX9 after MYC expression, growth, Cas9 activity, screen batch, and the expression-by-amplification interaction were included (coefficient -0.0331, p=0.174).

Iteration 7: broad essential and screen/growth effects
POLR2A and TMED10 behaved mainly as growth-associated dependencies. High-growth lines had much more negative dependency_POLR2A (difference -0.1043, p=1.0e-179) and modestly more negative dependency_TMED10 (difference -0.0369, p=2.5e-24). A proposed POLR2A growth-by-MYC expression interaction was not supported (interaction -0.0075, p=0.304), indicating that growth rate itself was the main observed modifier rather than a strong MYC-specific POLR2A interaction.

Iterations 8 and 9: systematic heterogeneity screens for all dependency outcomes
I ran a systematic subgroup search for every dependency outcome. Candidate predicates included both states of binary molecular features, lineage/subtype/culture/batch levels with at least 500 rows, high and low quartiles of continuous features, and pair/triple combinations with at least 500 rows. For dependency_BRAF, dependency_EGFR, dependency_ERBB2, dependency_KRAS, and dependency_RIT1, the screen recapitulated the matched driver features: braf_v600e=1, egfr_amplification=1, erbb2_amplification=1, kras_mutation=1, and nf1_loss=1 respectively. For dependency_KIF18A, the refined colorectal APC-mutant high-WNT SMAD4-intact subgroup was dominant. For dependency_TMED10, the strongest found subgroup was high growth, nf1_loss=1, and stk11_loss=0, but the effect was small (difference -0.0470, p=1.0e-05). For dependency_DHX9, high growth plus high MYC expression was the best coherent subgroup (difference -0.0595, p=2.5e-21). For dependency_POLR2A, a high-growth lung high-8q24 subgroup was somewhat stronger than high growth alone (difference -0.1184, p=2.5e-16), but this still looked like growth-associated broad-essential behavior. For dependency_RPL11, the strongest coherent subgroup combined myc_amplification=1, high growth, high MYC expression, and high 8q24 copy number (difference -0.1380, p=1.22e-43).

Iteration 10: final robustness checks
The matched driver effects remained strong in adjusted models: braf_v600e for dependency_BRAF (coefficient -0.5509, p<1e-300), egfr_amplification for dependency_EGFR (coefficient -0.3452, p<1e-300), and kras_mutation for dependency_KRAS (coefficient -0.3072, p<1e-300). Adding high ASCL2 expression to the KIF18A subgroup preserved a very large negative effect (lineage="colorectal", apc_mutation=1, high WNT, smad4_loss=0, high ASCL2: difference -1.0572, p<1e-300). The RPL11 complete MYC/8q/growth subgroup was more negative than myc_amplification alone (difference -0.1380 for the complete subgroup vs -0.1091 for myc_amplification alone).

Overall conclusions
The dataset contains clear matched oncogene addiction signals for BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, and KRAS, plus a broad NF1-loss association with RIT1 dependency. Most lineage-specific refinements of these driver effects were refuted once interaction terms were tested, except for a small ERBB2 amplification enhancement in breast/gastric lines. The most biologically specific subgroup was KIF18A: colorectal, APC-mutant, high-WNT, SMAD4-intact lines were deeply dependent, while SMAD4 loss suppressed the dependency almost completely in the same APC/WNT context. RPL11 dependency was tied to MYC amplification and 8q24 copy number, especially in fast-growing high-MYC lines. DHX9 had a weaker high-growth/high-MYC expression pattern. POLR2A and TMED10 mainly reflected growth-associated broad essentiality rather than highly specific molecular dependencies.
