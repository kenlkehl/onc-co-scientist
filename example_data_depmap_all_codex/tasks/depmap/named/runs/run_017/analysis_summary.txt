CRISPR dependency map analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed the 50,000-row DepMap-style CRISPR screen using only the local dataset bundle. Dependency scores are interpreted on their natural scale, where more negative values mean stronger dependency after knockout. I used Welch mean comparisons for subgroup contrasts, adjusted linear models for main effects, and a systematic heterogeneity search over binary events, lineage/culture/batch levels, quartiled continuous features, and all adequately sized two-predicate subgroups. I then refined the strongest patterns with interaction or adjusted subgroup models.

Initial oncogene-dependency checks were strongly supported. `braf_v600e=1` was associated with substantially more negative `dependency_BRAF` after adjustment for lineage, culture type, screen batch, Cas9 activity, and growth rate (effect -0.5545, p underflowed to 0). The same driver-matching pattern held for `egfr_amplification=1` on `dependency_EGFR` (effect -0.3489, p=0), `erbb2_amplification=1` on `dependency_ERBB2` (effect -0.4325, p=0), and `kras_mutation=1` on `dependency_KRAS` (effect -0.3095, p=0). These were broad effects rather than artifacts of screen covariates.

The RAS-pathway bypass analysis supported `nf1_loss=1` as a RIT1 dependency marker. Adjusted `nf1_loss` effect on `dependency_RIT1` was -0.0954 (p=6.67e-69). A heterogeneity refinement showed that brain lineage strengthened this relationship: the `nf1_loss` by `lineage='brain'` interaction was -0.0577 (p=0.0060), and NF1-loss brain lines had mean `dependency_RIT1` -0.4047 versus -0.2650 in the complement.

The clearest multivariable dependency subgroup was for KIF18A. `lineage='colorectal'` and `apc_mutation=1` lines had mean `dependency_KIF18A` -0.9425 versus -0.2527 in all other lines (difference -0.6899, p=0). Adding high `wnt_activity_score` sharpened the effect, but the key suppressor was `smad4_loss`: in a colorectal/APC/WNT/SMAD4 interaction model, the colorectal:APC:SMAD4-loss coefficient was +0.8484 (p=9.73e-172), meaning SMAD4 loss made KIF18A dependency much less negative. The final subgroup `lineage='colorectal'`, `apc_mutation=1`, `smad4_loss=0`, and high `wnt_activity_score` had mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.2990 versus -0.2530 in the complement; adjusted subgroup effect was -1.0386 (p=0).

For ERBB2, heterogeneity was present but smaller than for KIF18A. `erbb2_amplification=1` was the dominant driver, and `lineage_subtype='her2_enriched'` further deepened the ERBB2-amplified dependency. The ERBB2 amplification by HER2-enriched interaction was -0.0645 (p=0.045). For BRAF, a hypothesized NF1-loss suppression effect was not supported: the `braf_v600e` by `nf1_loss` interaction was +0.0120 but nonsignificant (p=0.494). For KRAS, lineage interactions were small and nonsignificant; KRAS dependency was broadly tied to `kras_mutation=1` rather than requiring pancreatic or colorectal lineage.

MYC-linked dependencies separated into two patterns. For `dependency_RPL11`, both `myc_amplification=1` and high `copy_number_8q24` were independent negative predictors after adjustment (MYC amplification effect -0.0472, p=4.06e-14; high 8q24 effect -0.0667, p=1.77e-36). The best refined subgroup was `myc_amplification=1` plus high growth rate, with adjusted effect -0.1085 (p=8.22e-47). For `dependency_DHX9`, high `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` was the independent MYC marker (effect -0.0264, p=3.47e-12), while MYC amplification itself was not independent once expression was included. A high-MYC-RNA/high-growth subgroup retained a smaller but significant adjusted effect (-0.0338, p=4.05e-7).

For `dependency_POLR2A`, the dominant pattern was proliferation rather than a named oncogenic subgroup. Growth rate had a large adjusted slope of -0.3046 per doubling/day (p=0). The systematic subgroup scan found high-growth lung adenocarcinoma as an apparent subgroup, but after continuous growth adjustment the subgroup effect was not significant (-0.0166, p=0.116). This supports growth rate as the biologically and statistically relevant driver of POLR2A dependency heterogeneity.

For `dependency_TMED10`, the evidence was weaker and mostly screen/growth related. Growth rate was negatively associated with `dependency_TMED10` (slope -0.1010, p=1.65e-38), and Cas9 activity adjusted for growth was also negative (slope -0.1035, p=1.87e-15). However, the best apparent high-growth/high-Cas9 subgroup did not remain independently significant after continuous growth and Cas9 adjustment (effect +0.0033, p=0.651). I therefore do not interpret TMED10 as having a robust named molecular dependency subgroup in this dataset.

Final best-supported outcome-specific interpretations:
- `dependency_BRAF`: strongest and sufficient marker is `braf_v600e=1`, more negative by about -0.55.
- `dependency_EGFR`: strongest and sufficient marker is `egfr_amplification=1`, more negative by about -0.35.
- `dependency_ERBB2`: strongest marker is `erbb2_amplification=1`, with a modest additional dependency in `lineage_subtype='her2_enriched'`.
- `dependency_KRAS`: strongest and broad marker is `kras_mutation=1`, more negative by about -0.31, without a required lineage predicate.
- `dependency_RIT1`: strongest marker is `nf1_loss=1`, with `lineage='brain'` enabling a stronger dependency.
- `dependency_KIF18A`: strongest complete subgroup is `lineage='colorectal'` AND `apc_mutation=1` AND `smad4_loss=0` AND high `wnt_activity_score`; `smad4_loss=1` suppresses the dependency.
- `dependency_TMED10`: no robust independent molecular subgroup; observed variation is mostly growth/Cas9 associated.
- `dependency_DHX9`: high `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm`, especially with high growth rate, marks modestly stronger dependency.
- `dependency_POLR2A`: dependency is dominated by high `growth_rate_doublings_per_day`, not by an independent lineage or mutation subgroup.
- `dependency_RPL11`: `myc_amplification=1` and high 8q24/MYC dosage mark stronger dependency, with the clearest subgroup being MYC-amplified high-growth lines.
