CRISPR dependency analysis summary for ds001_depmap

Dependency scores are interpreted on their native scale: more negative values indicate stronger dependency after knockout. I ran 10 propose-test-refine iterations using only the local dataset. The analysis combined adjusted linear models, within-subgroup contrasts, interaction tests, and a systematic heterogeneity scan over 68 single predicates plus eligible pairwise predicates for every dependency outcome.

The clearest main effects were matched oncogene dependencies. After adjusting for lineage, screen batch, culture type, serum, Cas9 activity, and growth rate, `braf_v600e == 1` predicted more negative `dependency_BRAF` by -0.5545 (p underflowed to 0), `egfr_amplification == 1` predicted more negative `dependency_EGFR` by -0.3489 (p=0), `erbb2_amplification == 1` predicted more negative `dependency_ERBB2` by -0.4325 (p=0), and `kras_mutation == 1` predicted more negative `dependency_KRAS` by -0.3095 (p=0). Apparent lineage enrichments for these outcomes mostly reflected driver prevalence: among BRAF V600E lines, skin lines were not significantly more BRAF dependent than non-skin lines (effect -0.0103, p=0.295; interaction p=0.282), and among KRAS-mutant lines, pancreatic lines were not more KRAS dependent than other KRAS-mutant lines (effect +0.0004, p=0.955; interaction p=0.818). EGFR-amplified brain lines and ERBB2-amplified breast lines were strongly dependent versus all other lines, but their effects were similar in magnitude to the corresponding amplification main effects.

RIT1 showed a different RAS-pathway pattern. `nf1_loss == 1` was associated with more negative `dependency_RIT1` after adjustment (effect -0.0955, p=6.13e-69). The NF1-loss brain subgroup was stronger (adjusted subgroup effect -0.1384, p=9.58e-13), and the `nf1_loss` by `lineage == brain` interaction was significant (interaction -0.0579, p=0.00582). This supports NF1 loss as the core RIT1-dependency feature with brain lineage as an enhancer.

KIF18A had the most structured subgroup dependency. `apc_mutation == 1` was associated with more negative `dependency_KIF18A` after adjustment (effect -0.1469, p=5.85e-180), and higher `wnt_activity_score` was also associated with stronger dependency (effect -0.0440 per SD, p=1.14e-113). The colorectal/APC-mutant/high-WNT subgroup had a very large effect (adjusted -0.8596, p=0). Refinement showed that `smad4_loss == 1` suppresses this dependency: within colorectal/APC-mutant/high-WNT lines, SMAD4 loss shifted KIF18A dependency upward by +1.0433 (mean -0.2557 with SMAD4 loss vs -1.2990 with SMAD4 intact; p=0). The final best-supported KIF18A subgroup is therefore `lineage == colorectal`, `apc_mutation == 1`, `wnt_activity_score >= 0.819`, and `smad4_loss == 0` (effect -1.0460 vs all other lines, p=0).

MYC and growth-linked dependencies were also present. `myc_amplification == 1` predicted more negative `dependency_RPL11` (adjusted effect -0.1043, p=4.29e-129). The MYC-amplified/high-growth subgroup had a larger raw contrast (effect -0.1303, p=1.10e-68; adjusted subgroup effect -0.1084, p=1.04e-46), but the MYC-amplification by high-growth interaction was not significant (p=0.105). Higher `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` predicted more negative `dependency_DHX9` (effect -0.0167 per SD, p=1.45e-26). High-growth/high-MYC RNA lines were more DHX9 dependent (adjusted subgroup effect -0.0338, p=4.24e-07), but the interaction was not significant at 0.05 (p=0.0878).

The broad growth-rate signal was strongest for POLR2A. High-growth lines (`growth_rate_doublings_per_day >= 0.964`) had more negative `dependency_POLR2A` after adjusting for lineage and non-growth screen covariates (effect -0.1044, p=3.43e-180). TMED10 showed a smaller high-growth association (effect -0.0370, p=1.07e-24). The systematic screen surfaced a prostate/high-growth TMED10 subgroup (raw effect -0.0533, p=0.000112), but the prostate-by-growth interaction was borderline and not significant at 0.05 (interaction -0.0313, p=0.0586), so I treat TMED10 as a modest broad growth effect rather than a robust lineage-specific dependency.

The systematic heterogeneity search covered every dependency outcome. The top or best-supported patterns were: `braf_v600e == 1` for BRAF; `egfr_amplification == 1` for EGFR; `erbb2_amplification == 1` for ERBB2; `kras_mutation == 1` for KRAS; `nf1_loss == 1` with brain-lineage enhancement for RIT1; colorectal/APC-mutant/high-WNT/SMAD4-intact for KIF18A; high growth for TMED10; high MYC RNA plus high growth as a modest DHX9 marker; high growth for POLR2A; and MYC amplification, especially among high-growth cells, for RPL11. Overall, the dataset contains strong engineered-like matched oncogene dependencies, a highly specific APC/WNT/SMAD4-defined KIF18A subgroup, and broader proliferation/MYC-associated dependencies for general transcriptional or ribosomal genes.
