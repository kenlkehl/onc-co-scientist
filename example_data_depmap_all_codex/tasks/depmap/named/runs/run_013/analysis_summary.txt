CRISPR dependency analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed 50,000 cell-line records with ten dependency outcomes. Dependency scores are interpreted so more negative values indicate stronger dependency.

The initial oncogene-dependency checks were strongly supported. BRAF V600E cell lines were much more BRAF-dependent than BRAF-wild-type lines (mean dependency_BRAF -0.8047 versus -0.2495; effect -0.5552; p<1e-300). EGFR-amplified lines were more EGFR-dependent (effect -0.3486; p<1e-300), ERBB2-amplified lines were more ERBB2-dependent (effect -0.4321; p<1e-300), and KRAS-mutant lines were more KRAS-dependent (effect -0.3075; p<1e-300). NF1 loss also marked a smaller but clear RIT1 dependency shift (effect -0.0955; p<1e-300).

I then tested screen and proliferation-associated patterns. Faster-growing cell lines had substantially more negative POLR2A dependency, especially above the 75th percentile of growth_rate_doublings_per_day (effect -0.1043; p<1e-300) and above the 90th percentile (effect -0.1211; p<1e-300). TMED10 showed a weaker pattern tied mainly to growth and Cas9 activity: high-growth lines had mean dependency_TMED10 -0.2816 versus -0.2447 in the complement (effect -0.0369; p<1e-300), and the high-growth/high-Cas9 subgroup sharpened this to effect -0.0605 (p=3.6e-9). I did not find a strong coherent lineage or genomic-feature subgroup for TMED10.

MYC/8q24-related features were associated with DHX9 and RPL11 dependencies. Higher rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm was associated with more negative dependency_DHX9 (slope -0.0161; p<1e-300), and the high-growth/MYC-high subgroup had mean dependency_DHX9 -0.3892 versus -0.3297 (effect -0.0595; p<1e-300). RPL11 dependency tracked 8q24/MYC status: high copy_number_8q24 had effect -0.0988 (p<1e-300), MYC amplification had effect -0.1091 (p<1e-300), and the combined high-8q24/MYC-amplified subgroup had effect -0.1123 (p<1e-300).

The strongest heterogeneous dependency was KIF18A. Colorectal lineage alone had a large shift in dependency_KIF18A (mean -0.7204 versus -0.2524; effect -0.4680; p<1e-300). APC mutation and high WNT activity also pointed to the same biology: apc_mutation=1 had effect -0.2921, and wnt_activity_score>=1.427 had effect -0.3773. Systematic subgroup search refined this to colorectal, WNT-active, SMAD4-intact cell lines. The colorectal/WNT90/SMAD4-intact subgroup had mean dependency_KIF18A -1.2409 versus -0.2677 in the complement (effect -0.9733; p<1e-300). Within colorectal WNT90 lines, SMAD4-intact lines were much more KIF18A-dependent than SMAD4-loss lines (effect -0.9767; p<1e-300), showing that SMAD4 loss suppresses or disables the dependency signal.

The final best-supported buried subgroup was: lineage='colorectal', apc_mutation=1, wnt_activity_score>=0.819, smad4_loss=0, and rnaseq_ASCL2_log2_tpm>=3.559. This subgroup had mean dependency_KIF18A -1.3320 (n=1511) versus -0.2749 in all other lines (effect -1.0572; p<1e-300). In colorectal/APC-mutant/WNT75 lines, SMAD4-intact cases had mean -1.2990 versus -0.2557 for SMAD4-loss cases (effect -1.0433; p<1e-300), supporting the complete subgroup definition and identifying SMAD4 loss as a suppressive predicate.

Overall, the dataset contains expected direct oncogene dependency signals for BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, KRAS, and RIT1; proliferation/screen-quality associations for POLR2A and TMED10; MYC/8q24-associated DHX9 and RPL11 dependencies; and one prominent buried interaction in KIF18A centered on colorectal APC/WNT activity that is enabled by intact SMAD4 and further enriched by ASCL2 expression.
