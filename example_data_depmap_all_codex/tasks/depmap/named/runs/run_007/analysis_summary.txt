Conclusions:
Classical oncogene dependencies were strongly supported: BRAF dependency tracked braf_v600e, EGFR dependency tracked egfr_amplification, ERBB2 dependency tracked erbb2_amplification, and KRAS dependency tracked kras_mutation. The pairwise screens sharpened some of these into lineage or subtype contexts, although not every pair had a statistically significant interaction term beyond the strong single-driver effect.
Other dependencies showed distinct contexts in the final subgroup screen: dependency_RIT1 in `nf1_loss` equals 1 AND `lineage` is "brain"; dependency_KIF18A in `lineage` is "colorectal" AND `apc_mutation` equals 1; dependency_TMED10 in `lineage_subtype` is "luminal" AND `stk11_loss` equals 1; dependency_DHX9 in `lineage_subtype` is "her2_enriched" AND `erbb2_amplification` equals 1; dependency_POLR2A in `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` is high (>= 0.964) AND `lineage_subtype` is "her2_enriched"; dependency_RPL11 in `myc_amplification` equals 1 AND `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` is high (>= 0.964). The best-supported effects are the signed subgroup differences reported above; weak starting ideas that were not supported were not retained as final conclusions.
All dependency outcomes received at least one systematic heterogeneity screen; all final refined subgroup hypotheses state the complete pair of predicates selected by that screen.
