CRISPR dependency map analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed 50,000 cell-line records from the local Parquet dataset. Dependency scores are interpreted on their natural scale, where more negative values indicate stronger dependency. I used OLS for simple signed molecular-feature effects and large-sample Welch contrasts for subgroup searches. The systematic heterogeneity screen evaluated 72 single-feature masks for each of the 10 dependency outcomes, then tested focused pairwise subgroups among the strongest modifiers; KIF18A was additionally refined with three-feature subgroup checks.

Canonical oncogene dependencies were strongly supported. BRAF V600E lines had much stronger BRAF dependency than other lines (mean -0.805 vs -0.250, effect -0.555, p<1e-15). EGFR-amplified lines had stronger EGFR dependency (mean -0.601 vs -0.252, effect -0.349, p<1e-15). ERBB2-amplified lines had stronger ERBB2 dependency (mean -0.686 vs -0.253, effect -0.432, p<1e-15). KRAS-mutant lines had stronger KRAS dependency (mean -0.559 vs -0.252, effect -0.307, p<1e-15).

RIT1 dependency followed NF1 loss rather than KRAS mutation. In a joint OLS model, nf1_loss had a significant negative association with dependency_RIT1 (beta -0.0955, p<1e-15), while kras_mutation was essentially null after NF1 adjustment (beta -0.0009, p=0.811). The strongest focused pair for RIT1 was nf1_loss=1 and lineage=brain (mean -0.405 vs -0.265, effect -0.140, p=1.22e-13).

The clearest buried heterogeneity was for KIF18A. Single-feature screening pointed to colorectal lineage (mean -0.720 vs -0.252, effect -0.468, p<1e-15), APC mutation (mean -0.545 vs -0.253, effect -0.292, p<1e-15), and high WNT activity (mean -0.469 vs -0.253, effect -0.217, p<1e-15). Combining lineage=colorectal and apc_mutation=1 strengthened the signal (mean -0.943 vs -0.253, effect -0.690, p<1e-15). The final complete subgroup was lineage=colorectal, apc_mutation=1, and wnt_activity_score>=Q75(0.819), with mean dependency_KIF18A -1.112 versus -0.253 outside the subgroup (effect -0.859, p<1e-15). This indicates a strong KIF18A dependency concentrated in WNT-active APC-mutant colorectal cell lines and much weaker dependency outside that subgroup.

RPL11 dependency was linked to MYC amplification and high growth. myc_amplification=1 had a significant negative effect on dependency_RPL11 even after adjusting for MYC RNA expression (beta -0.110, p<1e-15), whereas standardized rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm itself was not significant in that model (p=0.489). The strongest pair was myc_amplification=1 plus growth_rate_doublings_per_day>=Q75(0.964), with mean -0.394 versus -0.263 outside (effect -0.130, p<1e-15).

Several initially plausible feature-specific hypotheses were refuted. cdkn2a_loss did not explain TMED10 dependency (beta -0.0005, p=0.899). rnaseq_SLFN11_log2_tpm did not explain DHX9 dependency (beta +0.00010, p=0.951). copy_number_9p21 did not explain POLR2A dependency in the predicted negative direction (beta +0.00136, p=0.394).

For the remaining broad essential or process-related outcomes, growth rate was a recurring modifier. High-growth lines had more negative dependency_TMED10 (effect -0.0369, p<1e-15), dependency_DHX9 (effect -0.0371, p<1e-15), and especially dependency_POLR2A (effect -0.104, p<1e-15). Focused pair searches identified modest refinements: TMED10 in high-growth luminal lines (effect -0.0435, p=8.14e-5), DHX9 in high-MYC-expression squamous lines (effect -0.0656, p=8.39e-6), and POLR2A in high-growth adenocarcinoma lines (effect -0.103, p<1e-15).

Overall conclusions: the dataset contains clear expected oncogene-addiction signals for BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, and KRAS; an NF1-loss-associated RIT1 dependency; a strong and biologically specific KIF18A dependency in WNT-active APC-mutant colorectal lines; MYC-amplification-associated RPL11 dependency; and growth-associated sensitivity for several general transcription/translation or screen-fitness outcomes. The most important subgroup result is the complete KIF18A predicate lineage=colorectal AND apc_mutation=1 AND wnt_activity_score>=Q75(0.819), because it is much stronger than any KIF18A main effect alone and explicitly defines where the dependency is enabled versus suppressed.
