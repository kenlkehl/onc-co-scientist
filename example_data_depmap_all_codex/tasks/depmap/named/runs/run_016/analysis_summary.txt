I analyzed `dataset.parquet` as a 50,000-row DepMap-style CRISPR dependency screen with 10 dependency outcomes. Dependency scores are on the natural scale where more negative values indicate stronger dependency. I used Welch subgroup comparisons, continuous-slope tests, pairwise subgroup screens, and small adjusted linear models that included `growth_rate_doublings_per_day` and `cas9_activity_score` where needed.

Iteration 1 tested direct oncogene addiction patterns. The expected matched marker-outcome relationships were all strongly supported and remained large after adjustment for growth and Cas9 activity: `braf_v600e == 1` for `dependency_BRAF` (adjusted effect -0.5549), `egfr_amplification == 1` for `dependency_EGFR` (-0.3483), `erbb2_amplification == 1` for `dependency_ERBB2` (-0.4323), and `kras_mutation == 1` for `dependency_KRAS` (-0.3072). All had p-values below the numerical reporting floor used here.

Iteration 2 found a smaller but robust `dependency_RIT1` signal in `nf1_loss == 1` cell lines. The adjusted effect was -0.0953, and the unadjusted NF1-loss mean was -0.3527 versus -0.2572 in other lines. `dependency_TMED10` did not show a comparably specific molecular subgroup; its strongest reproducible signal was fast growth, with `growth_rate_doublings_per_day >= 0.964` showing an effect of -0.0369, and `cas9_activity_score` also contributing negatively.

Iteration 3 tested `dependency_RPL11`. High `copy_number_8q24 >= 0.428` was associated with more negative RPL11 dependency (effect -0.0988). `myc_amplification == 1` was a strong marginal marker (effect -0.1091), but an adjusted model containing both `myc_amplification` and continuous `copy_number_8q24` showed that continuous 8q24 dosage carried the independent signal (beta -0.1042), while `myc_amplification` itself was not independently negative (beta +0.0101, p=0.12).

Iteration 4 examined `dependency_DHX9`. Higher `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` remained associated with more negative DHX9 dependency after accounting for growth and Cas9 activity (slope -0.0156 per log2 TPM). The strongest pairwise subgroup was high MYC expression plus fast growth: `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm >= 5.669 AND growth_rate_doublings_per_day >= 0.964`, with mean -0.3892 versus -0.3297 in the rest.

Iteration 5 examined `dependency_POLR2A`. This outcome was dominated by proliferation: `growth_rate_doublings_per_day >= 0.964` had mean -0.4972 versus -0.3928, effect -0.1043. A continuous adjusted model estimated a growth-rate coefficient of -0.3046 per doubling/day, with a smaller independent Cas9 activity effect of -0.1029.

Iterations 6 through 8 focused on the major heterogeneous signal for `dependency_KIF18A`. Marginal screens first found colorectal lineage (effect -0.4680), `apc_mutation == 1` (-0.2921), and high `wnt_activity_score >= 0.819` (-0.2168). Combining them produced a much stronger subgroup: `lineage == "colorectal" AND apc_mutation == 1 AND wnt_activity_score >= 0.819` had mean -1.1119 versus -0.2530 in all other lines, effect -0.8589.

The complete KIF18A subgroup required SMAD4 intact status. `lineage == "colorectal" AND apc_mutation == 1 AND wnt_activity_score >= 0.819 AND smad4_loss == 0` had mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.2990 versus -0.2530, effect -1.0460. The matched `smad4_loss == 1` subgroup had mean -0.2557 and a positive effect estimate versus the rest (+0.0517), indicating suppression of the KIF18A dependency rather than enhancement. High `rnaseq_ASCL2_log2_tpm >= 3.559` marked WNT-active KIF18A-dependent lines, but adding ASCL2 high expression to the SMAD4-intact complete subgroup only slightly changed the effect (-1.0572 versus -1.0460), so ASCL2 is best treated as a marker rather than a required predicate.

Iteration 9 confirmed robustness in adjusted models. The colorectal/APC/high-WNT KIF18A subgroup remained strongly negative after growth and Cas9 adjustment (beta -0.8596). Continuous `copy_number_8q24` remained independently associated with RPL11 dependency, and `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` remained independently associated with DHX9 dependency.

Iteration 10 performed a systematic heterogeneity search for each dependency outcome using single-feature and pairwise subgroup checks. The strongest supported subgroups were: `braf_v600e == 1` for `dependency_BRAF`; `egfr_amplification == 1` for `dependency_EGFR`; `erbb2_amplification == 1` for `dependency_ERBB2`; `kras_mutation == 1` for `dependency_KRAS`; `nf1_loss == 1` for `dependency_RIT1`; the complete colorectal/APC/high-WNT/SMAD4-intact subgroup for `dependency_KIF18A`; fast growth for `dependency_TMED10`; high MYC expression plus fast growth for `dependency_DHX9`; fast growth for `dependency_POLR2A`; and high 8q24 copy number for `dependency_RPL11`.

Overall, the dataset contains several clear matched oncogene dependencies, a distinct NF1/RIT1 relationship, proliferation and screen-quality effects for broad essential genes, and a strong hidden KIF18A dependency that is concentrated in colorectal APC-mutant high-WNT cell lines only when `smad4_loss == 0`.
