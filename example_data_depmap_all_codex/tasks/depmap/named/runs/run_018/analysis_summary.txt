CRISPR dependency analysis summary for ds001_depmap

I analyzed 50,000 cell-line records with 10 dependency outcomes. Dependency scores are interpreted on their natural scale, where more negative values indicate stronger dependency. All analyses used only the local parquet and column description. The workflow combined adjusted OLS models, interaction tests, direct subgroup contrasts, and a systematic one-feature heterogeneity screen over 72 candidate indicators for every dependency outcome.

Matched oncogene dependencies were the clearest main effects. After adjustment for lineage, culture type, screen batch, media serum, Cas9 activity, and growth rate, `braf_v600e=1` shifted `dependency_BRAF` by -0.555 (p=0), `egfr_amplification=1` shifted `dependency_EGFR` by -0.349 (p=0), `erbb2_amplification=1` shifted `dependency_ERBB2` by -0.433 (p=0), and `kras_mutation=1` shifted `dependency_KRAS` by -0.310 (p=0). These effects were large and all in the expected more-negative direction.

RAS-pathway analysis also supported an NF1/RIT1 relationship: `nf1_loss=1` shifted `dependency_RIT1` by -0.095 after adjustment (p=6.68e-69). Lineage interaction checks did not support stronger BRAF V600E effects in skin, stronger EGFR-amplification effects in brain, or stronger KRAS-mutation effects in pancreatic/colorectal lines. ERBB2 amplification had a small extra breast/gastric interaction (-0.025, p=0.0279), and NF1 loss had an extra brain interaction (-0.058, p=0.00596), but the main alteration effects were present broadly.

The strongest multivariable subgroup was for `dependency_KIF18A`. Initial tests showed that `apc_mutation=1` was more negative (-0.147, p=5.59e-180) and higher `wnt_activity_score` was more negative (-0.048 per unit, p=1.19e-113). Refinement showed a complete enabling subgroup: `lineage=colorectal`, `apc_mutation=1`, `wnt_activity_score >= 0.819`, and `smad4_loss=0`. This subgroup had mean `dependency_KIF18A` -1.299 versus -0.253 outside the subgroup, a contrast of -1.046 (p=0). `smad4_loss=1` suppressed the effect within colorectal/APC/WNT-high lines, shifting the mean by 1.043 toward weaker dependency (p=0); the APC x WNT x SMAD4-loss interaction was 0.994 (p=1.71e-53).

For `dependency_RPL11`, the one-feature screen highlighted `myc_amplification=1` as the most negative subgroup (contrast -0.109, p=5.71e-140), while high `copy_number_8q24` was almost as strong (contrast -0.099, p=7.61e-160). Multivariable modeling favored copy number as the explanatory feature: continuous `copy_number_8q24` remained negative (-0.104, p=7.52e-121) after adding `myc_amplification` and MYC RNA expression, whereas `myc_amplification` itself became non-significant (0.009, p=0.169).

For `dependency_DHX9`, high MYC expression was supported. Top-quartile `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` had a contrast of -0.029 (p=2.05e-15), and continuous `rnaseq_MYC_log2_tpm` remained negative in an adjusted model (-0.016, p=1.46e-24). In the same model, `myc_amplification` was not significant (-0.009, p=0.176), so the better-supported molecular descriptor is expression rather than amplification.

`dependency_POLR2A` and `dependency_TMED10` were dominated by broad growth-associated effects. For POLR2A, adjusted growth-rate slope was -0.305 (p=0), and top-quartile growth lines were -0.104 more negative than the rest (p=1.01e-179). For TMED10, adjusted growth-rate slope was -0.101 (p=1.68e-38), and top-quartile growth lines were -0.037 more negative (p=2.52e-24). `nf1_loss=1` had only a small raw TMED10 contrast (-0.014, p=0.0105) and did not survive the systematic screen FDR.

The systematic heterogeneity screen was run for every dependency outcome before finalizing. The strongest more-negative one-feature hits were: `dependency_BRAF` -> braf_v600e=1 (effect -0.555, BH q=0); `dependency_EGFR` -> egfr_amplification=1 (effect -0.349, BH q=0); `dependency_ERBB2` -> erbb2_amplification=1 (effect -0.432, BH q=0); `dependency_KRAS` -> kras_mutation=1 (effect -0.307, BH q=0); `dependency_RIT1` -> nf1_loss=1 (effect -0.096, BH q=1.12e-66); `dependency_KIF18A` -> lineage=colorectal (effect -0.468, BH q=0); `dependency_TMED10` -> growth_rate_doublings_per_day>=Q75(0.964) (effect -0.037, BH q=1.81e-22); `dependency_DHX9` -> growth_rate_doublings_per_day>=Q75(0.964) (effect -0.037, BH q=6.15e-23); `dependency_POLR2A` -> growth_rate_doublings_per_day>=Q75(0.964) (effect -0.104, BH q=7.28e-178); and `dependency_RPL11` -> myc_amplification=1 (effect -0.109, BH q=2.06e-138).

Overall, the dataset shows expected oncogene-addiction patterns for BRAF, EGFR, ERBB2, and KRAS; NF1-loss/RIT1 dependency; a very strong colorectal APC/WNT/SMAD4-intact KIF18A dependency subgroup; 8q24-copy-driven RPL11 dependency; MYC-expression-associated DHX9 dependency; and growth-associated POLR2A/TMED10 effects. The clearest suppressor/enabler relationship is the KIF18A rule, where SMAD4 intactness enables the APC/WNT colorectal dependency and SMAD4 loss largely abolishes it.
